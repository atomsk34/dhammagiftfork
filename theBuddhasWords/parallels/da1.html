DĀ 1

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 1 第一分

1 （一）初大本经

Parallel to: DN 14 / SN 12.65 (~) / EĀ 48.4

The ultimate truth is beyond words, and the sages are enlightened through silence. The profound meaning cannot be transmitted without words, and this is why Śākyamuni taught. Therefore, when the Tathāgata appeared in the world, he gave three great teachings: Regarding body and speech, he prevented them with precepts and rules; To clarify good and evil, he guided with sūtras; To expound the subtle and profound, he distinguished with dharmas. Thus, the creation of the Tripiṭaka is based on different responses. If we unite them, they have the same ultimate. Then the different paths have the same destination.

夫宗极绝于称谓，贤圣以之冲默；玄旨非言不传，释迦所以致教。是以如来出世，大教有三：约身口，则防之以禁律；明善恶，则导之以契经；演幽微，则辨之以法相。然则三藏之作也，本于殊应，会之有宗，则异途同趣矣。

Precepts and rules are the Vinaya Piṭaka, with four parts and ten recitations. Dharmas are the Abhidharma Piṭaka, with four parts and five recitations. Sūtras are the four Āgama Piṭaka: the Ekottara Āgama has four parts and eight recitations, the Madhyama Āgama has four parts and five recitations, the Saṃyuktāgama has four parts and ten recitations, and the Dīrghāgama has four parts and four recitations. These thirty sūtras together form one collection.

禁律，律藏也，四分十诵。法相，阿毗昙藏也，四分五诵。契经，四阿含藏也：《增一阿含》四分八诵，《中阿含》四分五诵，《杂阿含》四分十诵，此《长阿含》四分四诵，合三十经以为一部。

Āgama means "returning to the Dharma." Returning to the Dharma means it is the vast repository of all good, the forest garden of total retention. As a canon, it is vast, extensive, and rich, and the more it is wrapped, the more it expands. It clearly explains the traces of blessings and misfortunes, the wise and the foolish, analyzes the origins of the true and the false, the different and the same, records the numbers of success and failure in the past and present, and the categories of the two instruments and all things. The Way is not without it, the Dharma is not without it. It is like the great ocean, to which all rivers return. Therefore, it is named "returning to the Dharma." It opens up the path of cultivation and records the distant. Therefore, it is named "long." Those who play with this canon suddenly awaken from long-lasting confusion. The true and the false are difficult to distinguish, but it is as clear as day and night; the retribution and response are obscure, but it illuminates like a reflection; the kalpas are distant, but it is as close as morning and evening; the six directions are vast, but it appears like before one's eyes. This can be said to illuminate great brightness in the dark room, to bestow the five eyes on the blind, without peeking through windows, yet wisdom is all-pervasive.

阿含，秦言法归。法归者，盖是万善之渊府，总持之林苑。其为典也，渊博弘富，韫而弥广；明宣祸福贤愚之迹，剖判真伪异齐之原，历记古今成败之数，墟域二仪品物之伦。道无不由，法无不在，譬彼巨海，百川所归，故以法归为名。开析修途，所记长远，故以长为目。玩兹典者，长迷顿晓。邪正难辨，显如昼夜；报应冥昧，照若影响；劫数虽辽，近犹朝夕；六合虽旷，现若目前。斯可谓朗大明于幽室，惠五目于众瞽，不闚户牖，而智无不周矣。

The Heavenly King of the Great Qin Dynasty, having cleansed and examined the profound, his lofty style is unique. He peacefully nurtures wisdom, saving both the world and the Dharma. He is always afraid that the subtle teachings will be obscured in different customs. He appointed Yao Shuang, the Right General, the Chief of the Imperial Secretariat, and the Duke of Jin, who is honest, gentle, profound in mind, and outstanding, to respect the great Dharma and have a wonderful natural understanding. The emperor especially kept him in mind and entrusted him with Dharma affairs. In the twelfth year of Hongshi, in the year of Shangzhang Yanmao, he invited the Kashmiri Tripiṭaka Master Buddhayaśas to translate one part of the Vinaya Piṭaka, totaling forty-five fascicles, which was completed in the fourteenth year. In the fifteenth year, in the year of Zhaoyang Chifenruo, he completed the translation of this Long Āgama. The Liangzhou monk Fotudeng translated it, and the Qin state Daoist Daohang took notes. At that time, famous and distinguished monks from the capital and summer were gathered to collate and determine it in the palace. Respectfully receiving the Dharma words, they respectfully received them without error. They removed the flowery and upheld the simple, focusing on the holy intention. I, being fortunate to have participated in the listening sessions, although without the merit of assisting goodness, was present at the end of the personal reception. Therefore, I briefly recorded the events of the time to show to the wise of the future.

大秦天王，涤除玄览，高韵独迈，恬智交养，道世俱济，每惧微言翳于殊俗。以右将军使者司隶校尉晋公姚爽，质直清柔，玄心超诣，尊尚大法，妙悟自然，上特留怀，每任以法事。以弘始十二年岁次上章阉茂，请罽宾三藏沙门佛陀耶舍出律藏一分四十五卷，十四年讫。十五年岁次昭阳赤奋若，出此《长阿含》讫。凉州沙门佛念为译，秦国道士道含笔受。时，集京夏名胜沙门，于第校定，恭承法言，敬受无差，蠲华崇朴，务存圣旨。余以嘉遇猥参听次，虽无翼善之功，而预亲承之末，故略记时事，以示来贤焉。

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was at Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树花林窟，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, after begging for alms, the bhikṣus gathered in the Flower Grove Hall and discussed among themselves: "Virtuous bhikṣus! Only the unsurpassed Honored One is most extraordinary, with divine powers reaching far and wide, and great majestic power. He knows the past countless buddhas who have entered parinirvāṇa, severed all afflictions, and eliminated disputes. He also knows the number of eons of those buddhas, their names, surnames, the clans they were born into, their food and drink, the length of their lifespans, and the suffering and happiness they experienced. He also knows that those buddhas had such precepts, such Dharma, such wisdom, such understanding, and such abodes. How is it, virtuous ones? Does the Tathāgata skillfully discern the nature of phenomena and know these things, or does he know these things because the devas come and tell him?"

时，诸比丘于乞食后集花林堂，各共议言：「诸贤比丘！唯无上尊为最奇特，神通远达，威力弘大，乃知过去无数诸佛，入于涅槃，断诸结使，消灭戯论。又知彼佛劫数多少，名号、姓字，所生种族，其所饮食，寿命修短，所更苦乐。又知彼佛有如是戒，有如是法，有如是慧，有如是解，有如是住。云何，诸贤！如来为善别法性，知如是事，为诸天来语，乃知此事？」

At that time, the World-Honored One was in a secluded place. With his pure divine ear, he heard the discussion among the bhikṣus. He then rose from his seat, went to the Flower Grove Hall, and took his seat.

尔时，世尊在闲静处，天耳清净，闻诸比丘作如是议，即从座起，诣花林堂，就座而坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing this, deliberately asked, "Bhikṣus! What were you discussing when you gathered here?" The bhikṣus then fully answered with the matter.

尔时，世尊知而故问，谓：「诸比丘！汝等集此，何所语议？」时，诸比丘具以事答。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Excellent! Excellent! You have left home to cultivate the path with equal faith. There are two kinds of practices for you: one is the discussion of the Dharma by the sages, and the other is the silence of the sages. Your discussion is indeed appropriate in this way. The Tathāgata's supernatural powers and majestic power are vast. He knows the affairs of countless past kalpas because he is able to understand the nature of the Dharma well, and also because the devas come to tell him." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「善哉！善哉！汝等以平等信，出家修道，诸所应行，凡有二业：一曰贤圣讲法，二曰贤圣默然。汝等所论，正应如是。如来神通，威力弘大，尽知过去无数劫事，以能善解法性故知，亦以诸天来语故知。」佛时颂曰：

The bhikṣus gathered in the Dharma hall, discussing the teachings of the
sages. The Tathāgata, dwelling in a quiet room, heard it all with
his divine ear. The Buddha's sun shines universally, discerning the meaning of
the Dharma realm. He also knows the past events, the parinirvāṇa of
the three Buddhas. The names, surnames, and clans, and the divisions of
birth, he knows them all. Wherever they are, his pure eyes record
them all. The devas with great majestic power, of very dignified appearance
also come to inform me of the parinirvāṇa of the three Buddhas
They record the birth, names, and surnames, and he knows them all
with the sound of a sorrowful crane. The unsurpassed Honored One of
gods and humans records the past Buddhas.

「比丘集法堂，　　讲说贤圣论；如来处静室，　　天耳尽闻知。佛日光普照，　　分别法界义；亦知过去事，　　三佛般泥洹。名号、姓、种族，　　受生分亦知；随彼之处所，　　净眼皆记之。诸天大威力，　　容貌甚端严；亦来启告我，　　三佛般泥洹。记生、名号、姓，　　哀鸾音尽知；无上天人尊，　　记于过去佛。」

He further told the bhikṣus, "Do you wish to hear the Tathāgata's knowledge of past lives, knowing the causes and conditions of the past Buddhas? I shall explain it."

又告诸比丘：「汝等欲闻如来识宿命智，知于过去诸佛因缘不？我当说之。」

At that time, the bhikṣus said, "World-Honored One! Now is the right time. We wish to hear it. Excellent! World-Honored One! Please explain it at the right time. We shall uphold it."

时，诸比丘白言：「世尊！今正是时，愿乐欲闻。善哉！世尊！以时讲说，当奉行之。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Listen carefully! Listen carefully! Consider it well, and I shall explain it to you." At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

佛告诸比丘：「谛听！谛听！善思念之，吾当为汝分别解说。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "In the past, ninety-one eons ago, there was a Buddha named Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Arhat, who appeared in the world. Furthermore, bhikṣus! Thirty-one eons ago, there was a Buddha named Śikhin Tathāgata, Arhat, who appeared in the world. Furthermore, bhikṣus! In those thirty-one eons, there was a Buddha named Viśvabhū Tathāgata, Arhat, who appeared in the world. Furthermore, bhikṣus! In this Bhadrakalpa, there was a Buddha named Krakucchanda, also named Konākamuni, also named Kāśyapa. I have now also attained supreme perfect enlightenment in this Bhadrakalpa." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

佛告诸比丘：「过去九十一劫，时，世有佛名毘婆尸如来、至真，出现于世。复次，比丘！过去三十一劫，有佛名尸弃如来、至真，出现于世。复次，比丘！即彼三十一劫中，有佛名毘舍婆如来、至真，出现于世。复次，比丘！此贤劫中有佛名拘楼孙，又名拘那含，又名迦叶。我今亦于贤劫中成最正觉。」佛时颂曰：

Ninety-one eons ago, there was a Buddha named Vipaśyin
Tathāgata; Next, thirty-one eons ago, there was a Buddha
named Śikhin Tathāgata; In that very eon, the Tathāgata
Viśvabhū appeared in the world. Now, in this Bhadrakalpa
for countless nayutas of years, There were four great
sages who appeared out of compassion for sentient beings:
Krakucchanda, Konākamuni, Kāśyapa, and Śākyamuni.

「过九十一劫，　　有毘婆尸佛；次三十一劫，　　有佛名尸弃；即于彼劫中，　　毘舍如来出。今此贤劫中，　　无数那维岁；有四大仙人，　　愍众生故出：拘楼孙、那含、　　迦叶、释迦文。

"You should know that during the time of Vipaśyin Buddha, the lifespan of humans was eighty thousand years. During the time of Śikhin Buddha, the lifespan of humans was seventy thousand years. During the time of Viśvabhū Buddha, the lifespan of humans was sixty thousand years. During the time of Krakucchanda Buddha, the lifespan of humans was forty thousand years. During the time of Konākamuni Buddha, the lifespan of humans was thirty thousand years. During the time of Kāśyapa Buddha, the lifespan of humans was twenty thousand years. Now, when I have appeared in this world, the lifespan of humans is one hundred years, with some more and some less." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「汝等当知，毘婆尸佛时，人寿八万岁。尸弃佛时，人寿七万岁。毘舍婆佛时，人寿六万岁。拘楼孙佛时，人寿四万岁。拘那含佛时，人寿三万岁。迦叶佛时，人寿二万岁。我今出世，人寿百岁，少出多减。」佛时颂曰：

During the time of Vipaśyin, the lifespan of humans was eighty-four thousand years
During the time of Śikhin Buddha, the lifespan of humans was seventy thousand
years; During the time of Viśvabhū, the lifespan of humans was sixty thousand
years; During the time of Krakucchanda, the lifespan of humans was forty thousand
years; During the time of Konākamuni, the lifespan of humans was thirty thousand
years; During the time of Kāśyapa Buddha, the lifespan of humans was twenty
thousand years; Now, during my time, the lifespan of humans is no more
than one hundred years.

「毘婆尸时人，　　寿八万四千；尸弃佛时人，　　寿命七万岁；毘舍婆时人，　　寿命六万岁；拘楼孙时人，　　寿命四万岁；拘那含时人，　　寿命三万岁；迦叶佛时人，　　寿命二万岁；如我今时人，　　寿命不过百。

"Vipaśyin Buddha was born into the kṣatriya caste, with the surname Kuru; Śikhin Buddha and Viśvabhū Buddha were also born into the same caste and surname. Krakucchanda Buddha was born into the brahmin caste, with the surname Kāśyapa; Konākamuni Buddha and Kāśyapa Buddha were also born into the same caste and surname. Now, I, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, am born into the kṣatriya caste, with the surname Gautama." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毘婆尸佛，出刹利种，姓拘利若；尸弃佛、毘舍婆佛，种、姓亦尔。拘楼孙佛，出婆罗门种，姓迦叶；拘那含佛、迦叶佛，种、姓亦尔。我今如来、至真，出刹利种，姓名曰瞿昙。」佛时颂曰：

Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, and Viśvabhū - these three perfectly enlightened
ones were born into the Kuru caste. The other three
Tathāgatas were born into the Kāśyapa caste. Now, I, the
unsurpassed honored one, guide all beings; foremost among gods and
humans, I am of the courageous Gautama clan. The first
three perfectly enlightened ones were born into the kṣatriya caste
the last three Tathāgatas were born into the brahmin caste
now, I, the unsurpassed honored one, am of the courageous kṣatriya caste.

「毘婆尸如来，　　尸弃、毘舍婆，此三等正觉，　　出拘利若姓。自余三如来，　　出于迦叶姓。我今无上尊，　　导御诸众生；天人中第一，　　勇猛姓瞿昙。前三等正觉，　　出于刹利种；其后三如来，　　出婆罗门种；我今无上尊，　　勇猛出刹利。

"Vipaśyin Buddha attained perfect enlightenment sitting under a pāpāṭala tree, Śikhin Buddha attained perfect enlightenment sitting under a puna tree, Viśvabhū Buddha attained perfect enlightenment sitting under a śāla tree, Krakucchanda Buddha attained perfect enlightenment sitting under a śirīṣa tree, Konākamuni Buddha attained perfect enlightenment sitting under a uḍumbara tree, Kāśyapa Buddha attained perfect enlightenment sitting under a nyagrodha tree. Now, I, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, attain perfect enlightenment sitting under a pāṭalā tree." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毘婆尸佛坐波波罗树下成最正觉，尸弃佛坐分陀利树下成最正觉，毘舍婆佛坐娑罗树下成最正觉，拘楼孙佛坐尸利沙树下成最正觉，拘那含佛坐乌暂婆罗门树下成最正觉，迦叶佛坐尼拘律树下成最正觉。我今如来、至真，坐钵多树下成最正觉。」佛时颂曰：

Vipaśyin Tathāgata went to the pāpāṭala tree and
attained perfect enlightenment right there. Śikhin Buddha attained
enlightenment sitting under a puna tree, Viśvabhū Tathāgata
sat under a śāla tree and attained the
knowledge and vision of liberation, with unimpeded spiritual
powers. Krakucchanda Tathāgata sat under a śirīṣa tree
and attained omniscience, pure, unstained, and unattached. Konākamuni
Buddha sat under a uḍumbara tree and right
there extinguished all greed, sorrow, and distress. Kāśyapa
Tathāgata sat under a nyagrodha tree and right
there eliminated the basis of all existence. Now
I, Śākyamuni, sit under a pāṭalā tree. The
Tathāgata, the Honored One of the Ten Powers
has cut off all afflictions, subdued the hosts
of Māra, and proclaims the great light in
the assembly. The seven Buddhas' power of diligence
emits light to dispel darkness, each sitting under
a tree and attaining perfect enlightenment.

「毘婆尸如来，　　往诣波罗树；即于彼处所，　　得成最正觉。尸弃分陀树，　　成道灭有原。毘舍婆如来，　　坐娑罗树下；获解脱知见，　　神足无所碍。拘楼孙如来，　　坐尸利沙树；一切智清净，　　无染无所著。拘那含牟尼，　　坐乌暂树下；即于彼处所，　　灭诸贪忧恼。迦叶如来坐，　　尼拘楼树下；即于彼处所，　　除灭诸有本。我今释迦文，　　坐于钵多树。如来十力尊，　　断灭诸结使；摧伏众魔怨，　　在众演大明。七佛精进力，　　放光灭暗冥；各各坐诸树，　　于中成正觉。

"Vipaśyin Tathāgata held three assemblies to teach the Dharma. The first assembly had 168,000 disciples, the second assembly had 100,000 disciples, and the third assembly had 80,000 disciples. Śikhin Buddha also held three assemblies to teach the Dharma. The first assembly had 100,000 disciples, the second assembly had 80,000 disciples, and the third assembly had 70,000 disciples. Viśvabhū Tathāgata held two assemblies to teach the Dharma. The first assembly had 70,000 disciples, and the second assembly had 60,000 disciples. Krakucchanda Tathāgata held one assembly to teach the Dharma, with 40,000 disciples. Konākamuni Buddha held one assembly to teach the Dharma, with 30,000 disciples. Kāśyapa Tathāgata held one assembly to teach the Dharma, with 20,000 disciples. Now, I hold one assembly to teach the Dharma, with 1,250 disciples." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毘婆尸如来三会说法，初会弟子有十六万八千人，二会弟子有十万人，三会弟子有八万人。尸弃如来亦三会说法，初会弟子有十万人，二会弟子有八万人，三会弟子有七万人。毘舍婆如来二会说法，初会弟子有七万人，次会弟子有六万人。拘楼孙如来一会说法，弟子四万人。拘那含如来一会说法，弟子三万人。迦叶如来一会说法，弟子二万人。我今一会说法，弟子千二百五十人。」佛时颂曰：

Vipaśyin, whose name means 'Contemplation', has immeasurable wisdom and insight, and
sees everywhere without fear. He held three assemblies with his disciples
Śikhin, whose light is unmoving, can extinguish all afflictions. He has
immeasurable great majestic virtue, and none can fathom him. He also
held three assemblies with his disciples. Viśvabhū, who has cut off
afflictions, is a great sage who gathers together. His fame spreads
in all directions, and his wonderful Dharma is greatly renowned. He
held two assemblies with his disciples, expounding the profound meaning. Krakucchanda
Tathāgata held one assembly, compassionately alleviating all suffering. The guide transformed
sentient beings, with one assembly of disciples. Konākamuni Tathāgata, the unsurpassed
one, is also like this. His body is golden in color
with a perfect appearance. He held one assembly with his disciples
expounding the subtle Dharma. Kāśyapa, with each hair, has a single-minded
unconfused thought. He speaks once without repeating, with one assembly of
disciples. The Compassionate One, with a tranquil mind, is the supreme
śramaṇa of the Śākya clan. He is the most honored one
among the gods, with one assembly of disciples. I manifest the
meaning in that assembly, expounding the pure teaching. The mind is
always joyful, with the exhaustion of taints and the end of
future existence. Vipaśyin and Śikhin held three assemblies each, Viśvabhū held
two assemblies, and the four Buddhas each held one assembly, expounding
the Dharma to the gathering of sages.

「毘婆尸名观，　　智慧不可量；遍见无所畏，　　三会弟子众。尸弃光无动，　　能灭诸结使；无量大威德，　　无能测量者；彼佛亦三会，　　弟子普共集。毘舍婆断结，　　大仙人要集；名闻于诸方，　　妙法大名称；二会弟子众，　　普演深奥义。拘楼孙一会，　　哀愍疗诸苦；导师化众生，　　一会弟子众。拘那含如来，　　无上亦如是；紫磨金色身，　　容貌悉具足；一会弟子众，　　普演微妙法。迦叶一一毛，　　一心无乱想；一语不烦重，　　一会弟子众。能仁意寂灭，　　释种沙门上；天中天最尊，　　我一会弟子。彼会我现义，　　演布清净教；心常怀欢喜，　　漏尽尽后有。毘婆、尸弃三，　　毘舍婆佛二，四佛各各一，　　仙人会演说。

"At that time, Vipaśyin Buddha had two disciples: the first was named Kenda, the second was named Tisha, and they were the foremost among all disciples. Śikhin Buddha had two disciples: the first was named Abhu, the second was named Sanpupa, and they were the foremost among all disciples. Viśvabhū Buddha had two disciples: the first was named Fuyou, the second was named Uttama, and they were the foremost among all disciples. Krakucchanda Buddha had two disciples: the first was named Sani, the second was named Bilou, and they were the foremost among all disciples. Konākamuni Buddha had two disciples: the first was named Shupanna, the second was named Utta Lou, and they were the foremost among all disciples. Kāśyapa Buddha had two disciples: the first was named Tisha, the second was named Paraba, and they were the foremost among all disciples. Now, my two disciples are named Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana, and they are the foremost among all disciples." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「时，毘婆尸佛有二弟子：一名骞茶，二名提舍，诸弟子中最为第一。尸弃佛有二弟子：一名阿毘浮，二名三婆婆，诸弟子中最为第一。毘舍婆佛有二弟子，一名扶游，二名郁多摩，诸弟子中最为第一。拘楼孙佛有二弟子，一名萨尼，二名毘楼，诸弟子中最为第一。拘那含佛有二弟子：一名舒槃那，二名郁多楼，诸弟子中最为第一。迦叶佛有二弟子，一名提舍，二名婆罗婆，诸弟子中最为第一。今我二弟子，一名舍利弗，二名目揵连，诸弟子中最为第一。」佛时颂曰：

Kenda and Tisha, etc., were disciples of Vipaśyin
Buddha. Abhu and Sanpupa were disciples of Śikhin
Buddha. Fuyou and Uttama were the foremost disciples
Both subdued Māra. They were disciples of Viśvabhū
Buddha. Sani and Bilou, etc., were disciples of
Krakucchanda Buddha. Shupanna and Utta Lou were disciples
of Konākamuni Buddha. Tisha and Paraba were disciples
of Kāśyapa Buddha. Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana are my foremost disciples.

「骞茶、提舍等，　　毗婆尸弟子；阿毗浮、三婆，　　尸弃佛弟子。扶游、郁多摩，　　弟子中第一，二俱降魔怨，　　毗舍婆弟子。萨尼、毗楼等，　　拘楼孙弟子；舒槃、郁多楼，　　拘那含弟子；提舍、婆罗婆，　　迦叶佛弟子；舍利弗、目连，　　是我第一子。

"Vipaśyin Buddha had a disciple named Wuyou who served him. Śikhin Buddha had a disciple named Renxing who served him. Viśvabhū Buddha had a disciple named Jiemie who served him. Krakucchanda Buddha had a disciple named Shanjue who served him. Konākamuni Buddha had a disciple named Anhe who served him. Kāśyapa Buddha had a disciple named Shanyou who served him. My disciple is named Ānanda." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毗婆尸佛有执事弟子，名曰无忧。尸弃佛执事弟子，名曰忍行。毗舍婆佛有执事弟子，名曰寂灭。拘楼孙佛有执事弟子，名曰善觉。拘那含佛有执事弟子，名曰安和。迦叶佛有执事弟子，名曰善友。我执事弟子，名曰阿难。」佛时颂曰：

Wuyou, Renxing, Jiemie, Shanjue, Anhe, Shanyou, and Ānanda as
the seventh. These were the attendants of the Buddhas
fully endowed with all meanings. Day and night they
were not negligent, benefiting themselves and others. These seven
wise disciples served the seven Buddhas on the left
and right. Joyfully making offerings, they peacefully returned to nirvāṇa.

「无忧与忍行，　　寂灭及善觉，安和、善友等，　　阿难为第七。此为佛侍者，　　具足诸义趣；昼夜无放逸，　　自利亦利他。此七贤弟子，　　侍七佛左右；欢喜而供养，　　寂然归灭度。

"Vipaśyin Buddha had a son named Fangying. Śikhin Buddha had a son named Wuliang. Viśvabhū Buddha had a son named Miaoguo. Krakucchanda Buddha had a son named Shangsheng. Konākamuni Buddha had a son named Daoshi. Kāśyapa Buddha had a son named Jijun. Now I have a son named Rāhula." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毗婆尸佛有子，名曰方膺。尸弃佛有子，名曰无量。毗舍婆佛有子，名曰妙觉。拘楼孙佛有子，名曰上胜。拘那含佛有子，名曰导师。迦叶佛有子，名曰集军。今我有子，名曰罗睺罗。」佛时颂曰：

Fangying, Wuliang, Miaoguo, Shangsheng, Daoshi, Jijun, and Rāhula as
the seventh. These noble and wealthy sons succeeded to
the Buddha's lineage. Loving the Dharma and delighting in
giving, they had no fear in the holy Dharma.

「方膺、无量子，　　妙觉及上胜，导师、集军等，　　罗睺罗第七，此诸豪贵子，　　绍继诸佛种；爱法好施惠，　　于圣法无畏。

"The father of Vipaśyin Buddha was named Bandhu, of the kṣatriya royal lineage. His mother was named Bandhuvati, and the city where the king ruled was named Bandhuvati." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毗婆尸佛父名槃头，刹利王种，母名槃头婆提，王所治城名曰槃头婆提。」佛时颂曰：

The father of Vipaśyin was Bandhu, the mother Bandhuvati
In the city of Bandhuvati, the Buddha taught the Dharma.

「遍眼父槃头，　　母槃头婆提；槃头婆提城，　　佛于中说法。

"The father of Śikhin Buddha was named Āditya, of the kṣatriya royal lineage. His mother was named Prabhāvatī, and the city where the king ruled was named Prabhāsa." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「尸弃佛父名曰明相，刹利王种，母名光曜，王所治城名曰光相。」佛时颂曰：

The father of Śikhin was Āditya, the mother was named Prabhāvatī. In
the city of Āditya, he subdued external enemies with his majestic virtue.

「尸弃父明相，　　母名曰光曜，于明相城中，　　威德降外敌。

"The father of Viśvabhū Buddha was named Sudipti, of the kṣatriya royal lineage. His mother was named Śīlā, and the city where the king ruled was named Anupamā." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「毗舍婆佛父名善灯，刹利王种，母名称戒，王所治城名曰无喻。」佛时颂曰：

The father of Viśvabhū Buddha was Sudipti, of the kṣatriya royal
lineage. His mother was named Śīlā, and the city was named Anupamā.

「毗舍婆佛父，　　善灯刹利种；母名曰称戒，　　城名曰无喻。

"The father of Krakucchanda Buddha was named Yajñadatta, of the brahmin caste. His mother was named Subhadrā, and the king was named Śānta. The city was named Śānta, following the king's name." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「拘楼孙佛父名祀得，婆罗门种，母名善枝，王名安和，随王名故城名安和。」佛时颂曰：

The father was Yajñadatta, a brahmin, and the mother was named Subhadrā
The king was named Śānta, and he resided in the city of Śānta.

「祀得婆罗门，　　母名曰善枝；王名曰安和，　　居在安和城。

"The father of Kanakamuni Buddha was named Mahādeva, of the brahmin caste. His mother was named Subhadrā, and the king was named Viśuddha. The city was named Viśuddha, following the king's name." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「拘那含佛父名大德，婆罗门种，母名善胜，是时王名清净，随王名故城名清净。」佛时颂曰：

The father was Mahādeva, a brahmin, and the mother was named Subhadrā
The king was named Viśuddha, and he resided in the city of Viśuddha.

「大德婆罗门，　　母名曰善胜；王名曰清净，　　居在清净城。

"The father of Kāśyapa Buddha was named Brahmadatta, of the brahmin caste. His mother was named Dhanavatī. The king was named Kṣatriya, and the city he ruled was named Vārāṇasī." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「迦叶佛父名曰梵德，婆罗门种，母名曰财主，时王名汲毗，王所治城名波罗［木＊奈］。」佛时颂曰：

The father was Brahmadatta, a brahmin, and the mother was named Dhanavatī
The king was named Kṣatriya, and he resided in the city of Vārāṇasī.

「梵德婆罗门，　　母名曰财主；时王名汲毗，　　在波罗［木＊奈］城。

"My father was named Śuddhodana, of the kṣatriya royal caste. My mother was named Mahāprajāpatī. The city he ruled was named Kapilavastu." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「我父名净饭，刹利王种，母名大清净妙，王所治城名迦毗罗卫。」佛时颂曰：

My father was a kṣatriya named Śuddhodana, and my mother was named Mahāprajāpatī
The land was vast and the people were prosperous. I was born there.

「父刹利净饭，　　母名大清净，土广民丰饶，　　我从彼而生。

"This is the cause and condition, name, clan, and birthplace of the buddhas. What wise person, hearing this cause and condition, would not rejoice and give rise to a mind of love and appreciation?"

「此是诸佛因缘、名号、种族、所出生处，何有智者闻此因缘而不欢喜，起爱乐心？」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus, "I now wish to speak of the past buddhas' affairs with my knowledge of past lives. Do you wish to hear?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「吾今欲以宿命智说过去佛事，汝欲闻不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "Now is the right time. We wish to hear with joy!"

诸比丘对曰：「今正是时，愿乐欲闻！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Listen carefully! Listen carefully! Consider it well, and I shall explain it to you. Bhikṣus, you should know that it is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven into his mother's womb through her right side, he was mindful and not confused. At that time, the earth shook and a great light shone, illuminating the world, reaching places not reached by the sun and moon, where all beings in darkness could see each other and know where they were going. At that time, this light also illuminated the palace of Māra, and all the gods, Śakra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and other beings were universally illuminated, and the light of the gods naturally did not appear." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

佛告诸比丘：「谛听！谛听！善思念之，吾当为汝分别解说。比丘！当知诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨从兜率天降神母胎，从右脇入，正念不乱。当于尔时，地为震动，放大光明，普照世界，日月所不及处皆蒙大明，幽冥众生，各相覩见，知其所趣。时，此光明复照魔宫，诸天、释、梵、沙门、婆罗门及余众生普蒙大明，诸天光明自然不现。」佛时颂曰：

Dense clouds gather in the sky, lightning illuminates the world, when
Vipaśyin descends into the womb, the light illuminates likewise; where the
sun and moon do not reach, all are illuminated, dwelling in
the womb, pure and undefiled, the Dharma of all Buddhas is thus.

「密云聚虚空，　　电光照天下，毗婆尸降胎，　　光明照亦然；日月所不及，　　莫不蒙大明，处胎净无秽，　　诸佛法皆然。

"Bhikṣus, you should know that it is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was in his mother's womb, he was mindful and not confused. There were four heavenly beings who held spears and guarded him. Humans and non-humans could not harm him. This is the constant Dharma." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「诸比丘！当知诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨在母胎时，专念不乱，有四天子，执戈矛侍护其人，人与非人不得侵娆，此是常法。」佛时颂曰：

The four heavenly beings in the four directions, with fame and
virtue, sent by Śakra Devānām-Indra, well protect the Bodhisattva. Always holding
spears in their hands, guarding and not leaving, humans and non-humans
cannot harm him, this is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas
The heavenly spirits protect him, like heavenly maidens guarding the heavens
the retinue is joyful, this is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas.

「四方四天子，　　有名称威德，天帝释所遣，　　善守护菩萨。手常执戈矛，　　卫护不去离，人非人不娆，　　此诸佛常法。天神所拥护，　　如天女卫天，眷属怀欢喜，　　此诸佛常法。」

He further told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven into his mother's womb, he was mindful and not confused. His mother's body was at ease and free from distress, and her wisdom increased. The mother herself saw the fetus and saw the Bodhisattva's body with all faculties complete, like purple gold without any flaws. It was like a person with eyes looking at a clear and transparent blue-green crystal, without any obstructions. Bhikṣus, this is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

又告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨从兜率天降神母胎，专念不乱，母身安隐，无众恼患，智慧增益。母自观胎，见菩萨身诸根具足，如紫磨金，无有瑕秽。犹如有目之士观净琉璃，内外清彻，无众障翳。诸比丘！此是诸佛常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

Like a pure lapis lazuli jewel, its brightness like the sun and
moon; The Sage in his mother's womb, her body free from distress
Wisdom increases, seeing the fetus like a golden image; The mother carrying
the fetus in peace and joy, this is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas.

「如净琉璃珠，　　其明如日月；仁尊处母胎，　　其母无恼患。智慧为增益，　　观胎如金像；母怀妊安乐，　　此诸佛常法。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven into his mother's womb, he was mindful and not confused. His mother's mind was pure, without any thoughts of desire, not burned by the fire of lust. This is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「毗婆尸菩萨从兜率天降神母胎，专念不乱，母心清净，无众欲想，不为婬火之所烧然，此是诸佛常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

The Bodhisattva dwelling in the mother's womb, the heavenly beings complete their
heavenly merits; The mother's mind is pure, without any thoughts of desire
She abandons all lust and desire, not defiled, not associating with it
Not burned by the fire of desire, the mothers of all Buddhas
are always pure.

「菩萨住母胎，　　天终天福成；其母心清净，　　无有众欲想。舍离诸婬欲，　　不染不亲近；不为欲火燃，　　诸佛母常净。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven into his mother's womb, he was mindful and not confused. His mother upheld the five precepts, her conduct was pure, she was devoted to kindness and love, and accomplished all good. She was at peace and fearless. When her body perished and her life ended, she was reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. This is the constant Dharma.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨从兜率天降神母胎，专念不乱，其母奉持五戒，梵行清净，笃信仁爱，诸善成就，安乐无畏，身坏命终，生忉利天，此是常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

The one who upholds the supreme body among humans, with diligence and complete precepts
will later definitely receive a heavenly body. This is called the mother of the Buddha.

「持人中尊身，　　精进、戒具足，后必受天身，　　此缘名佛母。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was born, he emerged from the right side. The earth shook and light shone everywhere. When he first entered the womb, even in dark places, there was no darkness. This is the constant Dharma.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨当其生时，从右脇出，地为震动，光明普照。始入胎时，暗冥之处，无不蒙明，此是常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

The prince was born, the earth shook, and great light shone
everywhere, in this world and others, above, below, and in all
directions. The light bestowed pure eyes, fully endowed with a heavenly
body. With joyful and pure sounds, they praised the name of the Bodhisattva.

「太子生地动，　　大光靡不照，此界及余界，　　上下与诸方，放光施净目，　　具足于天身，以欢喜净音，　　转称菩萨名。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was born, he emerged from the right side, with single-minded mindfulness. At that time, the Bodhisattva's mother held onto a tree branch with her hand, neither sitting nor lying down. Then four heavenly beings offered scented water with their hands, standing before the mother, saying: "Yes, heavenly mother! Now you have given birth to a holy son. Do not be worried or distressed." This is the constant Dharma.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨当其生时，从右脇出，专念不乱。时，菩萨母手攀树枝，不坐不卧。时，四天子手奉香水，于母前立言：『唯然，天母！今生圣子，勿怀忧戚。』此是常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

The Buddha's mother did not sit or lie down, abiding in precepts and practicing
pure conduct. The noble one was born without laziness, attended by devas and humans.

「佛母不坐卧，　　住戒修梵行，生尊不懈怠，　　天人所奉侍。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was born, he emerged from the right side, with single-minded mindfulness. His body was pure, not defiled by filth. It is like a person with eyes placing a clear and bright pearl on white silk, the two do not defile each other, because both are pure. The Bodhisattva's birth from the womb is also like this. This is the constant Dharma.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨当其生时，从右脇出，专念不乱，其身清净，不为秽恶之所污染。犹如有目之士，以净明珠投白缯上，两不相污，二俱净故。菩萨出胎亦复如是，此是常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

Like a pure bright pearl, placed on silk without staining
When the Bodhisattva was born, he was pure and unsullied.

「犹如净明珠，　　投缯不染污；菩萨出胎时，　　清净无染污。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was born, he emerged from the right side, with single-minded mindfulness. Emerging from the right side, he took seven steps on the ground, without anyone supporting him. He looked in the four directions and said, "In heaven and on earth, I alone am honored. I will liberate beings from birth, old age, sickness, and death." This is the constant Dharma.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨当其生时，从右脇出，专念不乱。从右脇出，堕地行七步，无人扶侍，遍观四方，举手而言：『天上天下唯我为尊，要度众生生老病死。』此是常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

Like a lion's stride, looking in the four directions. Taking seven steps
on the ground, the human lion is also so. Also like a
great dragon's stride, looking in the four directions. Taking seven steps on
the ground, the human dragon is also so. When the Two-Footed Honored
One was born, he walked seven steps with ease. Looking in the
four directions, he raised his voice, "I will end the suffering of
birth and death." When he was first born, the unequaled and equal
one, looking at the basis of birth and death, this body is
the last limit.

「犹如师子步，　　遍观于四方；堕地行七步，　　人师子亦然。又如大龙行，　　遍观于四方；堕地行七步，　　人龙亦复然。两足尊生时，　　安行于七步；观四方举声，　　当尽生死苦。当其初生时，　　无等等与等，自观生死本，　　此身最后边。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'It is the constant Dharma of all Buddhas: When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was born, from the right side, with single-minded mindfulness, two springs gushed forth, one warm and one cold, to provide for bathing. This is the constant Dharma.' At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「诸佛常法：毗婆尸菩萨当其生时，从右脇出，专念不乱，二泉涌出一温一冷，以供澡浴，此是常法。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

When the Two-Footed Honored One was born, two springs gushed forth
by themselves, to provide for the Bodhisattva's use, bathing the pure
all-seeing one. Two springs gushed forth by themselves, the water was
very pure, one warm and two cool, to bathe the Omniscient One.

「两足尊生时，　　二泉自涌出；以供菩萨用，　　遍眼浴清净。二泉自涌出，　　其水甚清净；一温二清冷，　　以浴一切智。

When the prince was first born, King Bandhumat summoned astrologers and diviners to observe the prince and know his auspiciousness and inauspiciousness. At that time, the astrologers received the order and observed, then went forward to unrobe and saw the complete marks. They divined: 'One who has these marks will surely go to two places, without doubt. If he stays at home, he will become a wheel-turning sage king, ruling the four continents, with the fourfold army complete, ruling with the right Dharma, without partiality or perversion, his grace extending to the world, the seven treasures coming by themselves, a thousand sons brave and able to subdue foreign enemies, without the use of weapons and armor, the world at peace; if he leaves home to study the path, he will attain perfect enlightenment, with the ten titles complete.' At that time, the astrologers then said to the king: 'The prince born to the king has the thirty-two marks, and will surely go to two places, without doubt. If he stays at home, he will become a wheel-turning sage king; if he leaves home, he will attain perfect enlightenment, with the ten titles complete.' At that time, the Buddha spoke a verse:

「太子初生，父王槃头召集相师及诸道术，令观太子，知其吉凶。时，诸相师受命而观，即前披衣，见有具相，占曰：『有此相者，当趣二处，必然无疑。若在家者，当为转轮圣王，王四天下，四兵具足，以正法治，无有偏枉，恩及天下，七宝自至，千子勇健，能伏外敌，兵杖不用，天下太平；若出家学道，当成正觉，十号具足。』时，诸相师即白王言：『王所生子，有三十二相，当趣二处，必然无疑。在家当为转轮圣王；若其出家，当成正觉，十号具足。』」佛时颂曰：

When the prince of a hundred blessings was born, the astrologers
predicted that he would surely go to two places, as recorded
in the scriptures. If he delights in the household life, he
will become a wheel-turning king, with the seven treasures difficult to
obtain, the king's treasures coming by themselves. The true gold with
a thousand spokes, held by a golden rim all around, can
fly and travel everywhere, thus called the heavenly wheel. Well-tamed with
seven tusks, tall and broad, white like snow, able to fly
well through the sky, called the second elephant treasure. The horse
travels all under heaven, going in the morning and returning in
the evening to eat, with a red mane and a peacock
throat, called the third treasure. The pure lapis lazuli jewel, its
light illuminating one yojana, illuminating the night as if it were
day, called the fourth treasure. Form, sound, smell, taste, and touch
none equal to it, the foremost among all women, called the
fifth treasure. Offering the king lapis lazuli jewels, jade, and various
treasures, joyfully presenting them, called the sixth treasure. Like the wheel-turning
king's thoughts, the army quickly comes and goes, as swift as
the king's intention, called the seventh treasure. These are called the
seven treasures: the wheel, elephant, horse, all white, householder, jewel, and
female treasure, with the military treasure as the seventh. Observing this
without satiation, enjoying the five desires, like an elephant breaking free
from its bonds, leaving home to attain perfect enlightenment. The king
has such a son, the most honored among two-legged beings, dwelling
in the world turning the wheel of the Dharma, attaining the
Way without indolence.

「百福太子生，　　相师之所记，如典记所载，　　趣二处无疑。若其乐家者，　　当为转轮王，七宝难可获，　　为王宝自至。真金千辐具，　　周匝金辋持，转能飞遍行，　　故名为天轮。善调七牙住，　　高广白如雪，能善飞虚空，　　名第二象宝。马行周天下，　　朝去暮还食，朱髦孔雀咽，　　名为第三宝。清净琉璃珠，　　光照一由旬，照夜明如昼，　　名为第四宝。色声香味触，　　无有与等者，诸女中第一，　　名为第五宝。献王琉璃宝，　　珠玉及众珍，欢喜而贡奉，　　名为第六宝。如转轮王念，　　军众速来去，健疾如王意，　　名为第七宝。此名为七宝，　　轮、象、马纯白，居士、珠、女宝，　　典兵宝为七。观此无有厌，　　五欲自娱乐，如象断羁靽，　　出家成正觉。王有如是子，　　二足人中尊，处世转法轮，　　道成无懈怠。

At that time, the father king earnestly asked the astrologers again and again: 'You should further observe the prince's thirty-two marks. What are their names?' At that time, the astrologers then unclothed the prince and spoke of the thirty-two marks: 'First, the feet are level and stable, the soles of the feet are full and level, treading on the ground with ease. Second, the soles of the feet have the wheel marks, with a thousand spokes accomplished, shining light upon each other. Third, the hands and feet are webbed, like the king of geese. Fourth, the hands and feet are soft, like heavenly garments. Fifth, the fingers and toes are slender and long, unmatched by others. Sixth, the heels are full, pleasing to behold. Seventh, the shins are like those of a deer, straight up and down. Eighth, the bones are connected like a chain, like a chain linking them together. Ninth, the male organ is hidden. Tenth, standing upright, the hands reach below the knees. Eleventh, each pore has a single hair growing, the hair curling to the right, the color of lapis lazuli. Twelfth, the hair grows to the right, curling upward, the color of lapis lazuli. Thirteenth, the body is golden in color. Fourteenth, the skin is fine and soft, not accepting dust and dirt. Fifteenth, the shoulders are equal and full, round and good. Sixteenth, there is a thousand-spoked wheel on the chest. Seventeenth, the body is twice the height of a man. Eighteenth, the seven parts are full and level. Nineteenth, the body is as tall and broad as a nyagrodha tree. Twentieth, the cheeks are like those of a lion. Twenty-first, the chest is square and even, like a lion. Twenty-second, there are forty teeth in the mouth. Twenty-third, they are square and even. Twenty-fourth, the teeth are close together without gaps. Twenty-fifth, the teeth are white and bright. Twenty-sixth, the throat is pure, and all tastes eaten are not unpalatable. Twenty-seventh, the tongue is broad and long, able to lick the ears to the left and right. Twenty-eighth, the voice is pure and penetrating. Twenty-ninth, the eyes are blue-black in color. Thirtieth, the eyes are like those of an ox king, the upper and lower eyelids blinking together. Thirty-first, between the eyebrows there is a white tuft of hair, soft and fine, extending one fathom, curling to the right like a conch shell or pearl. Thirty-second, there is a flesh protuberance on the crown of the head. These are the thirty-two marks.' Then he spoke a verse:

「是时，父王慇懃再三，重问相师：『汝等更观太子三十二相，斯名何等？』时诸相师即披太子衣，说三十二相：『一者足安平，足下平满，蹈地安隐。二者足下相轮，千辐成就，光光相照。三者手足网缦，犹如鹅王。四者手足柔软，犹如天衣。五者手足指纤，长无能及者。六者足跟充满，观视无厌。七者鹿䏝肠，上下佣直。八者钩鎻骨，骨节相钩，犹如鎻连。九者阴马藏。十者平立垂手过膝。十一、一一孔一毛生，其毛右旋，绀琉璃色。十二、毛生右旋，绀色仰靡。十三、身黄金色。十四、皮肤细软，不受尘秽。十五、两肩齐亭，充满圆好。十六、胸有万字。十七、身长倍人。十八、七处平满。十九、身长广等，如尼拘卢树。二十、颊车如师子。二十一、胸膺方整如师子。二十二、口四十齿。二十三、方整齐平。二十四、齿密无间。二十五、齿白鲜明。二十六、咽喉清净，所食众味，无不称适。二十七、广长舌，左右舐耳。二十八、梵音清彻。二十九、眼绀青色。三十、眼如牛王，眼上下俱眴。三十一、眉间白毫柔软细泽，引长一寻，放则右旋螺如真珠。三十二、顶有肉髻，是为三十二相。』」即说颂曰：

The feet are well-placed and soft, not treading on the ground, leaving
no trace. The thousand spokes are adorned with marks, the light and
color are all complete. Like a nyagrodha tree, the height and width
are equal and balanced. The Tathāgata has never had a hidden male
organ, like a horse. The body is adorned with golden treasures, the
marks mutually reflecting each other. Although following worldly ways, dust and dirt
do not defile. The heavenly color is extremely soft, a heavenly canopy
naturally covers. The Brahma voice, the body is purple-gold, like a flower
just emerging from a pond. The king asked the astrologers, the astrologers
respectfully reported to the king. Praising the marks of the Bodhisattva, the
whole body is fully radiant. The hands, feet, and limbs are all
visible inside and out. The tastes of food are all complete, the
body is upright and not leaning. The wheel marks appear on the
soles of the feet, the voice is like a crying crane. The
thighs and shanks are fully formed, accomplished by past karma. The arms
and elbows are round and full, the eyebrows and eyes are very
dignified. The most honored among humans, the lion, has the foremost majestic
power. The cheeks are square and even, lying on the side like
a lion. The teeth are square and even, forty in number, close
together without gaps. The Brahma voice is unprecedented, reaching far and near
according to conditions. Standing upright without leaning, the two hands touch the
knees. The hair is neat and soft, the most honored among humans
with beautiful marks. One pore has one hair growing, the hands and
feet are webbed. The flesh protuberance on the head, the eyes are
blue-black, the upper and lower eyelids blink together. The two shoulders are
round and full, with the thirty-two marks complete. The heels are without
high or low, the shanks are like those of a deer, slender
and straight. The Heaven among Heavens has come here, like an elephant
freed from its bonds; liberating beings from the suffering of birth, old
age, sickness, and death. Out of compassion, he teaches the Four Noble
Truths; expounding the meaning of the Dharma phrases, causing the multitude to
venerate the Supreme One.

「善住柔软足，　　不蹈地迹现，千辐相庄严，　　光色靡不具。如尼俱类树，　　纵广正平等。如来未曾有，　　秘密马阴藏。金宝庄严身，　　众相互相暎，虽顺俗流行，　　尘土亦不污。天色极柔软，　　天盖自然覆，梵音、身紫金，　　如华始出池。王以问相师，　　相师敬报王。称赞菩萨相，　　举身光明具。手足诸支节，　　中外靡不现。食味尽具足，　　身正不倾斜。足下轮相现，　　其音如哀鸾。佣髀形相具，　　宿业之所成。臂肘圆满好，　　眉目甚端严。人中师子尊，　　威力最第一。其颊车方整，　　卧脇如师子。齿方整四十，　　齐密中无间。梵音未曾有，　　远近随缘到。平立不倾身，　　二手摩扪膝。毛齐整柔软，　　人尊美相具。一孔一毛生，　　手足网缦相。肉髻、目绀青，　　眼上下俱眴。两肩圆充满，　　三十二相具。足跟无高下，　　鹿䏝肠纤佣。天中天来此，　　如象绝羁靽；解脱众生苦，　　处生老病死。以慈悲心故，　　为说四真谛；开演法句义，　　令众奉至尊。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "When Vipaśyin Bodhisattva was born, the devas were above, holding white parasols and jeweled fans in the sky to shield against cold, heat, wind, rain, and dust." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「毗婆尸菩萨生时，诸天在上，于虚空中手执白盖宝扇，以障寒暑、风雨、尘土。」佛时颂曰：

In the human realm, there has never been one born as the most
honored among humans; the devas hold parasols and jeweled fans in respect and reverence.

「人中未曾有，　　生于二足尊；诸天怀敬养，　　奉宝盖宝扇。

"At that time, the father king provided four wet nurses: one to breastfeed, one to bathe, one to anoint with fragrance, and one to amuse. They joyfully raised and nurtured him without any weariness." Then he spoke a verse:

「尔时，父王给四乳母：一者乳哺，二者澡浴，三者涂香，四者娱乐。欢喜养育，无有懈倦。」于是颂曰：

The wet nurses had loving kindness, and as soon as the child was born, they
entrusted him for nurturing. One breastfed, one bathed, two anointed with fragrance and amused him
The most wonderful fragrance in the world was used to anoint the most honored among humans.

「乳母有慈爱，　　子生即付养；一乳哺一浴，　　二涂香娱乐。世间最妙香，　　以涂人中尊。

"When he was a young boy, the people of the whole country looked at him without ever tiring of it." Then he spoke a verse:

「为童子时，举国士女视无厌足。」于是颂曰：

Many people loved him, like a newly completed golden image, men and women
together gazed at it intently, looking at it without ever tiring of it.

「多人所敬爱，　　如金像始成，男女共谛观，　　视之无厌足。

"When he was a young boy, the people of the whole country passed him around to hold, like viewing a precious flower." Then he spoke a verse:

「为童子时，举国士女众共传抱，如观宝华。」于是颂曰：

When the Honored One of Two Legs was born, he was loved by
many people; they passed him around to hold, like viewing a fragrant precious flower.

「二足尊生时，　　多人所敬爱；展转共传抱，　　如观宝花香。

"When the Bodhisattva was born, his eyes did not blink, like those of the gods in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. Because of not blinking, he was named Vipassi." Then he spoke a verse:

「菩萨生时，其目不眴，如忉利天。以不眴故，名毗婆尸。」于是颂曰：

The Honored One of the Gods did not blink, like the gods in the
Heaven of the Thirty-three; seeing forms and correctly observing, therefore he was called Vipassi.

「天中天不眴，　　犹如忉利天；见色而正观，　　故号毗婆尸。

"When the Bodhisattva was born, his voice was clear, penetrating, soft, and harmonious, like the voice of a kalaviṅka bird." Then he spoke a verse:

「菩萨生时，其声清彻，柔软和雅，如迦罗频伽鸟声。」于是颂曰：

Like a bird in the snow mountains, drinking flower nectar and singing
the two-footed Honored One, his voice is clear and penetrating as well.

「犹如雪山鸟，　　饮华汁而鸣；其彼二足尊，　　声清彻亦然。

"When the Bodhisattva was born, his eyes could see through and see one yojana." Then he spoke a verse:

「菩萨生时，眼能彻视见一由旬。」于是颂曰：

The retribution of pure karmic practices receives the wonderful light of
heaven; the eyes of the Bodhisattva see all around for one yojana.

「清净业行报，　　受天妙光明；菩萨目所见，　　周遍一由旬。

"When the Bodhisattva was born, as he gradually grew up, in the heavenly hall, he enlightened and transformed with the Way, bestowing kindness on the common people, his fame and virtue were widely known." Then he spoke a verse:

「菩萨生时，年渐长大，在天正堂，以道开化，恩及庶民，名德远闻。」于是颂曰：

As a youth, he was in the right hall, enlightening the
world with the Way; resolving all affairs, therefore he was called
Vipassi. His pure wisdom was vast and deep, like the great
ocean; pleasing to all beings, causing their wisdom to increase and expand.

「童幼处正堂，　　以道化天下；决断众事务，　　故号毗婆尸。清净智广博，　　甚深犹大海；悦可于群生，　　使智慧增广。

"At that time, the Bodhisattva wanted to go out and look around, so he ordered the charioteer to prepare the jeweled carriage and go to the garden to inspect and look around. The charioteer then prepared it and returned, saying, 'Now is the right time.' The prince then rode in the jeweled carriage to the garden to inspect. On the way, he saw an old man with white hair, missing teeth, wrinkled face, hunched body, leaning on a cane, walking slowly, panting. The prince asked the attendant, 'Who is this?' The attendant replied, 'This is an old man.' The prince asked again, 'What is old?' The attendant replied, 'An old person is one whose lifespan is about to end, with little remaining life, and is therefore called old.' The prince asked again, 'Will I also be like this, not exempt from this suffering?' The attendant replied, 'Yes, everyone born will grow old, regardless of status.' Thereupon, the prince was disappointed and displeased. He then told the attendant to turn the carriage around and return to the palace. Quietly contemplating, he thought, 'Thinking of this suffering of old age, I will also have it.'" The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「于时，菩萨欲出游观，告勅御者严驾宝车，诣彼园林，巡行游观。御者即便严驾讫已，还白：『今正是时。』太子即乘宝车诣彼园观。于其中路见一老人，头白齿落，面皱身偻，拄杖羸步，喘息而行。太子顾问侍者：『此为何人？』答曰：『此是老人。』又问：『何如为老？』答曰：『夫老者生寿向尽，余命无几，故谓之老。』太子又问：『吾亦当尔，不免此患耶？』答曰：『然，生必有老，无有豪贱。』于是，太子怅然不悦，即告侍者回驾还宫，静默思惟：『念此老苦，吾亦当有。』」佛于是颂曰：

Seeing the old man's life about to end, leaning on a cane and
walking slowly, the Bodhisattva thought to himself, 'I am not exempt from this difficulty.

「见老命将尽，　　拄杖而羸步；菩萨自思惟，　　吾未免此难。

"At that time, the father king asked the attendant, 'Did the prince enjoy himself on his outing?' The attendant replied, 'He was not happy.' The king asked the reason, and the attendant said, 'He encountered an old man on the way, and that is why he was not happy.' At that time, the father king silently thought to himself, 'In the past, the astrologers predicted that the prince would renounce the householder's life. Now that he is unhappy, could it be so? I shall devise a means to have him dwell in the deep palace, entertained with the five desires, to delight his mind and prevent him from renouncing the householder's life.' He then adorned the palace, selected court ladies to entertain him." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「尔时，父王问彼侍者：『太子出游，欢乐不耶？』答曰：『不乐。』又问其故，答曰：『道逢老人，是以不乐。』尔时，父王默自思念：『昔日相师占相太子，言当出家，今者不悦，得无尔乎？当设方便，使处深宫，五欲娱乐，以悦其心，令不出家。』即便严饰宫馆，简择婇女以娱乐之。」佛于是颂曰：

The father king, hearing these words, devised means to adorn the palace and
increase the five desires, wishing to prevent him from renouncing the householder's life.

「父王闻此言，　　方便严宫馆；增益以五欲，　　欲使不出家。

"Later, the prince again ordered the charioteer to prepare the carriage for an outing. On the way, he encountered a sick person, emaciated with a large belly, dark face, lying alone in filth, unattended, very sick and suffering, unable to speak. He asked the charioteer, 'What kind of person is this?' The charioteer replied, 'This is a sick person.' The prince asked, 'What is sickness?' The charioteer replied, 'Sickness is when many pains press in, life and death are uncertain, thus it is called sickness.' The prince said, 'Will I also be like this, not exempt from this affliction?' The charioteer replied, 'Yes. All who are born will be sick, there is no distinction between noble and humble.' Thereupon, the prince was disappointed and displeased. He then told the charioteer to turn the carriage back to the palace. Quietly reflecting, he thought, 'Thinking of this sickness and suffering, I will also be like this.'" The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「又于后时，太子复命御者严驾出游。于其中路逢一病人，身羸腹大，面目黧黑，独卧粪除，无人瞻视，病甚苦毒，口不能言。顾问御者：『此为何人？』答曰：『此是病人。』问曰：『何如为病？』答曰：『病者，众痛迫切，存亡无期，故曰病也。』又曰：『吾亦当尔，未免此患耶？』答曰：『然。生则有病，无有贵贱。』于是，太子怅然不悦，即告御者回车还宫。静默思惟：『念此病苦，吾亦当尔。』」佛于是颂曰：

Seeing the long-term sick person, his complexion was emaciated. Quietly
reflecting, he thought, 'I am not exempt from this affliction.

「见彼久病人，　　颜色为衰损；静默自思惟，　　吾未免此患。

"At that time, the father king again asked the charioteer, 'Did the prince enjoy his outing?' The charioteer replied, 'He was not happy.' The king further asked the reason, and the charioteer replied, 'He encountered a sick person on the road, and that is why he was not happy.' Thereupon, the father king silently reflected, 'In the past, the astrologers predicted that the prince would renounce the householder's life. Today, he is displeased. Could it be so? I shall devise another means, increasing various entertainments to delight his mind, so that he will not renounce the householder's life.' He then again adorned the palace, selecting beautiful women to entertain him." The Buddha then spoke a verse:

「尔时，父王复问御者：『太子出游，欢乐不耶？』答曰：『不乐。』又问其故，答曰：『道逢病人，是以不乐。』于是父王默然思惟：『昔日相师占相太子，言当出家，今日不悦，得无尔乎？吾当更设方便，增诸伎乐，以悦其心，使不出家。』即复严饰宫馆，简择婇女以娱乐之。」佛于是颂曰：

The subtle and delightful forms, sounds, smells, tastes, and textures, are
due to the Bodhisattva's merit. Therefore, he is entertained within them.

「色声香味触，　　微妙可悦乐，菩萨福所致，　　故娱乐其中。

"Again, on another occasion, the prince ordered the charioteer to prepare the carriage for an outing. On the way, he encountered a dead person, with colorful silk banners leading the way in front and behind, and relatives and family members crying and wailing as they escorted him out of the city. The prince again asked, 'Who is this?' The charioteer replied, 'This is a dead person.' The prince asked, 'What is meant by dead?' The charioteer replied, 'Dead means exhausted. to the heaven of no-more-fabrication. The seventh great sage
subdued the two demons. I pay homage with folded
hands to the one without heat and the one

of the Śākya Master, endowed with excellent marks, reached
the heaven of good view. Like a lotus flower
not attached to water, the World-Honored One, without defilement
reached the great heaven of good view. Like the
sun rising, pure and without dust, bright like the
autumn moon, he went to the ultimate. These five
abodes are purified by sentient beings. Because of the
purity of mind, he came to the one without
afflictions. Coming with a pure mind, he became a
disciple of the Buddha, abandoning attachment and delighting in
non-attachment. Seeing the Dharma with certainty, the son of
Vipaśyin, with a pure mind and well-come, went to
the great sage. The son of Śikhin Buddha, without
defilement and without fabrication, came with a pure mind
to the one who has left existence. The son
of Viśvabhū, with complete faculties, came to me with
a pure mind, like the sun illuminating the sky
The son of Krakucchanda, abandoning all desires, came to
me with a pure mind, with wonderful light blazing
The son of Kanakamuni, without defilement and without fabrication
came to me with a pure mind, with light
like a full moon. The disciple of Kāśyapa, with
complete faculties, came to me with a pure mind
like the northern heaven, the great sage who is
not confused, foremost in supernormal powers, with a firm

The bhikṣu, in a quiet place, free from dust
and defilements, diligently and tirelessly cutting off all bonds
of existence. This is the ultimate cause and condition
of all Buddhas, proclaimed by the Tathāgata Śākyamuni.

「譬如力士，　　屈伸臂顷，我以神足，　　至无造天。第七大仙，　　降伏二魔，无热无见，　　叉手敬礼。如昼度树，　　释师远闻，相好具足，　　到善见天。犹如莲华，　　水所不著，世尊无染，　　至大善见。如日初出，　　净无尘翳，明若秋月，　　诣一究竟。此五居处，　　众生所净，心净故来，　　诣无烦恼。净心而来，　　为佛弟子，舍离染取，　　乐于无取。见法决定，　　毘婆尸子，净心善来，　　诣大仙人，尸弃佛子，　　无垢无为，以净心来，　　诣离有尊。毘沙婆子，　　诸根具足，净心诣我，　　如日照空。拘楼孙子，　　舍离诸欲，净心诣我，　　妙光焰盛。拘那含子，　　无垢无为，净心诣我，　　光如月满。迦叶弟子，　　诸根具足，净心诣我，　　如北天念，　　不乱大仙，神足第一，　　以坚固心，为佛弟子。　　净心而来，为佛弟子，　　礼敬如来，具启人尊。　　所生成道，名、姓、种族，　　知见深法，成无上道。　　比丘静处，离于尘垢，　　精勤不懈，断诸有结。　　此是诸佛，本末因缘，　　释迦如来，之所演说。」

Having spoken this great cause and condition sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard the Buddha's words rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说此大因缘经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
