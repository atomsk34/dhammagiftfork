DĀ 10

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

10 （一〇）十上经

Parallel to: DN 33 / DN 34 / MN 16 / AN 5.205 / AN 9.71 / AN 10.14 / DĀ 9 (~) / DĀ 11 (~) / MĀ 206 / EĀ 51.4

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Anga, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus. He arrived at the city of Campā and stayed overnight by the side of the Gaggarā Lake.

一时，佛游鸯伽国，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。诣瞻婆城，止宿伽伽池侧。

On the full moon day, the World-Honored One was sitting in the open, surrounded by the great assembly. He spoke the Dharma throughout the night, and told Śāriputra, "Now, the bhikṣus from the four directions have gathered. They are all diligent and have given up sleep. They wish to hear the Dharma. I am suffering from a backache and wish to rest for a while. You should now speak the Dharma for the bhikṣus."

以十五日月满时，世尊在露地坐，大众围遶，竟夜说法，告舍利弗：「今者四方诸比丘集，皆各精勤，捐除眠睡，欲闻说法，吾患背痛，欲小] cf.【丽】止息，卿今可为诸比丘说法。」

At that time, Śāriputra accepted the Buddha's instruction. Then the World-Honored One folded his saṃghāṭī robe four times, lay down on his right side like a lion, and went to sleep.

时，舍利弗受佛教已，尔时世尊即四牒僧伽梨，偃右胁卧如师子，累足而卧。

At that time, the elder Śāriputra addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "Now I will speak the Dharma. The words from beginning to end are all true and genuine, complete in meaning and flavor, and pure in the practice of the Dharma. Listen attentively! Consider it well! I will explain it for you."

尔时，耆年舍利弗告诸比丘：「今我说法，上中下言，皆悉真正，义味具足，梵行清净，汝等谛听！善思念之，当为汝说。」

At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened. Śāriputra addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "There are ten superior dharmas that eliminate the bonds of afflictions, lead to nirvāṇa, and exhaust the limit of suffering. They also fully encompass five hundred and fifty dharmas. Now I will explain them. Listen well! The bhikṣus! There is one accomplished dharma, one cultivated dharma, one awakened dharma, one extinguished dharma, one regressing dharma, one increasing dharma, one difficult to understand dharma, one born dharma, one knowing dharma, and one realized dharma. What is the one accomplished dharma? It is not being negligent in all wholesome dharmas. What is the one cultivated dharma? It is always contemplating one's own body. What is the one awakened dharma? It is defiled contact. What is the one extinguished dharma? It is the conceit of self. What is the one regressing dharma? It is not the contemplation of impurity. What is the one increasing dharma? It is the contemplation of impurity. What is the one difficult to understand dharma? It is the uninterrupted samādhi. What is the one born dharma? It is defiled liberation. What is the one knowing dharma? It is that all sentient beings depend on food for sustenance. What is the one realized dharma? It is the liberation of the mind free from hindrances.

时，诸比丘受教而听。舍利弗告诸比丘：「有十上法，除众结缚，得至泥洹，尽于苦际，又能具足五百五十法，今当分别，汝等善听。诸比丘！有一成法、一修法、一觉法、一灭法、一退法、一增法、一难解法、一生法、一知法、一证法。云何一成法？谓于诸善法能不放逸。云何一修法？谓常自念身。云何一觉法？谓有漏触。云何一灭法？谓是我慢。云何一退法？谓不恶露观。云何一增法？谓恶露观。云何一难解法？谓无间定。云何一生法？谓有漏解脱。云何一知法？谓诸众生皆仰食存。云何一证法？谓无碍心解脱。

There are also two accomplished dharmas, two cultivated dharmas, two awakened dharmas, two extinguished dharmas, two regressing dharmas, two increasing dharmas, two difficult to understand dharmas, two born dharmas, two knowing dharmas, and two realized dharmas. What are the two accomplished dharmas? They are knowing shame and knowing conscience. What are the two cultivated dharmas? They are tranquility and insight. What are the two awakened dharmas? They are name and form. What are the two extinguished dharmas? They are ignorance and craving. What are the two regressing dharmas? They are violating precepts and breaking views. What are the two increasing dharmas? They are having complete precepts and complete views. What are the two difficult to understand dharmas? There are causes and conditions for sentient beings to become defiled, and there are causes and conditions for sentient beings to become pure. What are the two born dharmas? They are the knowledge of exhaustion and the knowledge of non-arising. What are the two knowing dharmas? They are what is possible and what is impossible. What are the two realized dharmas? They are wisdom and liberation.

「又有二成法、二修法、二觉法、二灭法、二退法、二增法、二难解法、二生法、二知法、二证法。云何二成法？谓知慙、知愧。云何二修法？谓止与观。云何二觉法？谓名与色。云何二灭法？谓无明、爱。云何二退法？谓毁戒、破见。云何二增法？戒具、见具。云何二难解法？有因有缘，众生生垢；有因有缘，众生得净。云何二生法？尽智、无生智。云何二知法？谓是处、非处。云何二证法？谓明与解脱。

There are also three accomplished dharmas, three cultivated dharmas, three awakened dharmas, three extinguished dharmas, three regressing dharmas, three increasing dharmas, three difficult to understand dharmas, three born dharmas, three knowing dharmas, and three realized dharmas. What are the three accomplished dharmas? They are associating with good friends, hearing the Dharma with the ears, and accomplishing the Dharma. What are the three cultivated dharmas? They are the three samādhis: the emptiness samādhi, the signless samādhi, and the wishless samādhi. What are the three awakened dharmas? They are the three feelings: painful feeling, pleasant feeling, and neither painful nor pleasant feeling. What are the three extinguished dharmas? They are the three cravings: craving for desire, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence. What are the three regressing dharmas? They are the three unwholesome roots: the unwholesome root of greed, the unwholesome root of anger, and the unwholesome root of delusion. What are the three increasing dharmas? They are the three wholesome roots: the wholesome root of non-greed, the wholesome root of non-anger, and the wholesome root of non-delusion. What are the three difficult to understand dharmas? They are the three difficult to understand: the noble ones are difficult to understand, the Dharma is difficult to understand, and the Tathāgata is difficult to understand. What are the three born dharmas? They are the three characteristics: the characteristic of cessation, the characteristic of diligence, and the characteristic of detachment. What are the three knowing dharmas? They are the three realms of liberation: liberation from desire reaches the form realm, liberation from the form realm reaches the formless realm; abandoning all conditioned dharmas is called exhaustion. What are the three realized dharmas? They are the three knowledges: knowledge of past lives, knowledge of the divine eye, and knowledge of the exhaustion of taints. Bhikṣus! These are the thirty dharmas, which are true and not false. The Tathāgata, having known them, teaches the Dharma equally.

「又有三成法、三修法、三觉法、三灭法、三退法、三增法、三难解法、三生法、三知法、三证法。云何三成法？一者亲近善友，二者耳闻法音，三者法法成就。云何三修法？谓三三昧：空三昧、无相三昧、无作三昧。云何三觉法？谓三受：苦受、乐受、不苦不乐受。云何三灭法？谓三爱：欲爱、有爱、无有爱。云何三退法？谓三不善根：贪不善根、恚不善根、痴不善根。云何三增法？谓三善根：无贪善根、无恚善根、无痴善根。云何三难解法？谓三难解：贤圣难解、闻法难解、如来难解。云何三生法？谓三相：息止相、精进相、舍离相。云何三知法？谓三出要界：欲出要至色界，色界出要至无色界；舍离一切诸有为法，彼名为尽。云何三证法？谓三明：宿命智、天眼智、漏尽智。诸比丘！是为三十法，如实无虚，如来知已，平等说法。

There are also four accomplished dharmas, four cultivated dharmas, four awakened dharmas, four extinguished dharmas, four regressing dharmas, four increasing dharmas, four difficult to understand dharmas, four born dharmas, four knowing dharmas, and four realized dharmas. What are the four accomplished dharmas? They are the four wheel dharmas: one, dwelling in the central country; two, associating with good friends; three, being careful oneself; four, planting good roots in past lives. What are the four cultivated dharmas? They are the four foundations of mindfulness: a bhikṣu contemplates the body internally with the body, diligently and tirelessly, remembering without forgetting, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; contemplates the body externally with the body, diligently and tirelessly, remembering without forgetting, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; contemplates the body internally and externally with the body, diligently and tirelessly, remembering without forgetting, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow. Contemplating feeling, mind, and dharmas is also like this. What are the four awakened dharmas? They are the four foods: food as sustenance, food as contact, food as intention, and food as consciousness. What are the four extinguished dharmas? They are the four kinds of feeling: feeling of desire, feeling of self, feeling of precepts, and feeling of views.

「复有四成法、四修法、四觉法、四灭法、四退法、四增法、四难解法、四生法、四知法、四证法。云何四成法？谓四轮法：一者住中国，二者近善友，三者自谨慎，四者宿植善本。云何四修法？谓四念处：比丘内身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；内外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧。受、意、法观，亦复如是。云何四觉法？谓四食：抟食、触食、念食、识食。云何四灭法？谓四受：欲受、我受、戒受、见受。

What are the four regressing dharmas? They are the four fetters: fetter of desire, fetter of existence, fetter of views, and fetter of ignorance. What are the four increasing dharmas? They are the four unfetters: unfetter of desire, unfetter of existence, unfetter of views, and unfetter of ignorance. What are the four difficult to understand dharmas? There are the four noble truths: the truth of suffering, the truth of the origin of suffering, the truth of the cessation of suffering, and the truth of the path. What are the four born dharmas? They are the four knowledges: knowledge of dharmas, knowledge of the unknown, knowledge of equality, and knowledge of others' minds. What are the four knowing dharmas? They are the four kinds of eloquence: eloquence in dharmas, eloquence in meanings, eloquence in words, and eloquence in responses. What are the four realized dharmas? They are the four fruits of the śramaṇa: the fruit of stream-entry, the fruit of once-returning, the fruit of non-returning, and the fruit of arahantship. Bhikṣus! These are the forty dharmas, which are true and not false. The Tathāgata, having known them, teaches the Dharma equally.

「云何四退法？谓四扼：欲扼、有扼、见扼、无明扼。云何四增法？谓四无扼：无欲扼、无有扼、无见扼、无无明扼。云何四难解法？谓有四圣谛：苦谛、集谛、灭谛、道谛。云何四生法？谓四智：法智、未知智、等智、知他心智。云何四知法？谓四辩才：法辩、义辩、辞辩、应辩。云何四证法？谓四沙门果：须陀洹果、斯陀含果、阿那含果、阿罗汉果。诸比丘！是为四十法，如实无虚，如来知已，平等说法。

There are also five accomplished dharmas, five cultivated dharmas, five awakened dharmas, five extinguished dharmas, five regressing dharmas, five increasing dharmas, five difficult to understand dharmas, five born dharmas, five knowing dharmas, and five realized dharmas. What are the five accomplished dharmas? They are the five branches of cessation: one, believing in the Buddha, Tathāgata, Arhat, fully endowed with the ten titles; two, being free from illness, with a body always at ease; three, being straightforward without flattery, directly heading towards the Tathāgata's path to nirvāṇa; four, concentrating the mind without distraction, reciting and not forgetting; five, being good at observing the arising and ceasing of dharmas, ending the basis of suffering through the practices of the noble ones. What are the five cultivated dharmas? They are the five faculties: the faculty of faith, the faculty of diligence, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of concentration, and the faculty of wisdom. What are the five awakened dharmas? They are the five aggregates of grasping: the aggregate of form, the aggregates of feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness.

「复有五成法、五修法、五觉法、五灭法、五退法、五增法、五难解法、五生法、五知法、五证法。云何五成法？谓五灭尽枝：一者信佛、如来、至真，十号具足；二者无病，身常安隐；三者质直无有谀谄，直趣如来涅槃径路；四者专心不乱，讽诵不忘；五者善于观察法之起灭，以贤圣行尽于苦本。云何五修法？谓五根：信根、精进根、念根、定根、慧根。云何五觉法？谓五受阴：色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴。

What are the five extinguished dharmas? They are the five hindrances: the hindrance of sensual desire, the hindrance of ill-will, the hindrance of sloth and torpor, the hindrance of restlessness and remorse, and the hindrance of doubt. What are the five regressing dharmas? They are the five fetters of the mind: first, a bhikṣu has doubts about the Buddha, and having doubts, does not draw near, and not drawing near, does not pay respect. This is the first fetter of the mind. Again, a bhikṣu has holes and leaks in the Dharma, the Saṃgha, and the precepts, does not practice truly, and practices defilement. Not drawing near the precepts, he also does not pay respect. This is the fourth fetter of the mind. Again, a bhikṣu gives rise to ill-will towards those who practice the holy life, is not joyful in his mind, and reviles them with harsh words. This is the fifth fetter of the mind. What are the five increasing dharmas? They are the five bases of joy: joy, mindfulness, tranquility, bliss, and concentration.

「云何五灭法？谓五盖：贪欲盖、瞋恚盖、眠睡盖、掉戏盖、疑盖。云何五退法？谓五心碍结：一者比丘疑佛，疑佛已，则不亲近，不亲近已，则不恭敬，是为初心碍结；又比丘于法、于众、于戒，有穿漏行、不真正行、为污染行，不亲近戒，亦不恭敬，是为四心碍结；又复比丘于梵行人生恶向心，心不喜乐，以麁恶言而毁骂之，是为五心碍结。云何五增法？谓五喜本：一悦、二念、三猗、四乐、五定。

What are the five difficult to understand dharmas? They are the five entrances to liberation: if a bhikṣu diligently practices without slacking, delights in solitary places, single-mindedly contemplates, and attains understanding where there was none, attains exhaustion where there was not, and attains peace where there was not. What are the five? If a bhikṣu hears the Buddha teach the Dharma, or hears the Dharma practitioners teach, or hears the elders teach, he contemplates and observes, analyzes the meaning of the Dharma, and his mind is joyful. Having joy, he attains the love of the Dharma. Having the love of the Dharma, his body and mind are at ease. Having body and mind at ease, he attains concentration. Having attained concentration, he attains true wisdom. This is the first entrance to liberation. Then, the bhikṣu is joyful upon hearing the Dharma, joyfully upholds and recites it, is also joyful, joyfully teaches it to others, is also joyful, contemplates and analyzes it, is also joyful, and attains concentration in the Dharma, is also like this.

「云何五难解法？谓五解脱入：若比丘精勤不懈，乐闲静处，专念一心，未解得解，未尽得尽，未安得安。何谓五？若比丘闻佛说法，或闻梵行者说，或闻师长说，思惟观察，分别法义，心得欢喜，得欢喜已，便得法爱，得法爱已，身心安隐，身心安隐已，则得禅定，得禅定已，得如实智，是为初解脱入。于是，比丘闻法欢喜，受持讽诵，亦复欢喜，为他人说，亦复欢喜，思惟分别，亦复欢喜，于法得定，亦复如是。

What are the five arising dharmas? They are the five wisdoms and concentrations of the sages: first, cultivating samādhi, experiencing present happiness and future happiness, giving rise to internal and external wisdom. Second, the sages are without attachment, giving rise to internal and external wisdom. Third, what is practiced by the Buddhas and sages, giving rise to internal and external wisdom. Fourth, relying on the characteristics of tranquility and extinction, being alone without companions, giving rise to internal and external wisdom. Fifth, entering and rising from samādhi with a single mind, giving rise to internal and external wisdom. What are the five dharmas of knowledge? They are the five realms of liberation: first, a bhikṣu does not delight in desire, does not think of it, does not draw near, but only thinks of liberation, delights in seclusion, draws near without slacking, his mind is pliant, and he is liberated from desire. The taints arising from desire are also exhausted, abandoned, and extinguished, and he attains liberation. This is the liberation from desire. The liberation from anger, jealousy, form, and the view of the self is also like this. What are the five dharmas of realization? They are the five aggregates of the non-learner: the aggregate of non-learner morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation. These are the fifty dharmas. The Tathāgata, having truly and without falsehood known them, teaches the Dharma equally.

「云何五生法？谓贤圣五智定：一者修三昧现乐后乐，生内外智。二者贤圣无爱，生内外智。三者诸佛贤圣之所修行，生内外智。四者猗寂灭相，独而无侣，而生内外智。五者于三昧一心入、一心起，生内外智。云何五知法？谓五出要界：一者比丘于欲不乐、不念，亦不亲近，但念出要，乐于远离，亲近不怠，其心调柔，出要离欲，因欲起漏亦尽舍灭，而得解脱，是为欲出要，瞋恚出要、嫉妬出要、色出要、身见出要，亦复如是。云何五证法？谓五无学聚：无学戒聚、定聚、慧聚、解脱聚、解脱知见聚，是为五十法。如实无虚，如来知已，平等说法。

There are also six dharmas of accomplishment, six dharmas of cultivation, six dharmas of awakening, six dharmas of extinction, six dharmas of decline, six dharmas of increase, six dharmas of difficulty to understand, six dharmas of arising, six dharmas of knowledge, and six dharmas of realization. What are the six dharmas of accomplishment? They are the six heavy dharmas: if a bhikṣu cultivates the six heavy dharmas, he is respectable and venerable, harmonious in the assembly, without disputes, and practices alone without mixing. What are the six? Here, a bhikṣu always practices loving-kindness with the body, respects those who practice the Brahma way, abides in a mind of loving-kindness, which is called the heavy dharma, respectable and venerable, harmonious in the assembly, without disputes, and practices alone without mixing. Furthermore, a bhikṣu practices loving-kindness with the mouth and mind, obtains sustenance and alms bowl leftovers, and shares them with others without any sense of self. Furthermore, a bhikṣu practices the precepts of the sages, without transgression or defilement, praised by the wise, well-endowed and upheld, and accomplishes concentration. Furthermore, a bhikṣu accomplishes the noble way of liberation, equally ending suffering, with right view and all the Brahma practices. This is called the heavy dharma, respectable and venerable, harmonious in the assembly, without disputes, and practices alone without mixing.

「复有六成法、六修法、六觉法、六灭法、六退法、六增法、六难解法、六生法、六知法、六证法。云何六成法？谓六重法：若有比丘修六重法，可敬可重，和合于众，无有诤讼，独行无杂。云何六？于是，比丘身常行慈，敬梵行者，住仁爱心，名曰重法，可敬可重，和合于众，无有诤讼，独行无杂。复次，比丘口慈、意慈，以法得养及钵中余，与人共之，不怀彼此。复次，比丘圣所行戒，不犯不毁，无有染污，智者所称，善具足持，成就定意。复次，比丘成就贤圣出要，平等尽苦，正见及诸梵行，是名重法，可敬可重，和合于众，无有诤讼，独行不杂。

What are the six dharmas of cultivation? They are the six recollections: recollection of the Buddha, Dharma, Saṃgha, precepts, generosity, and the heavens. What are the six dharmas of awakening? They are the six internal sense bases: eye sense base, ear sense base, nose sense base, tongue sense base, body sense base, and mind sense base. What are the six dharmas of extinction? They are the six kinds of craving: craving for form, sound, smell, taste, touch, and dharmas. What are the six dharmas of decline? They are the six disrespectful dharmas: disrespect for the Buddha, Dharma, Saṃgha, precepts, concentration, and parents. What are the six dharmas of increase? They are the six respectful dharmas: respect for the Buddha, Dharma, Saṃgha, precepts, concentration, and parents. What are the six dharmas difficult to understand? They are the six unsurpassed: unsurpassed view, unsurpassed hearing, unsurpassed material gain, unsurpassed precepts, unsurpassed respect, and unsurpassed recollection. What are the six dharmas of arising? They are the six equal dharmas: here, a bhikṣu sees forms with the eyes without sorrow or joy, abiding in equanimity and mindfulness. With the ears, sounds; with the nose, smells; with the tongue, tastes; with the body, touches; with the mind, dharmas, without sorrow or joy, abiding in equanimity and mindfulness.

Having himself attained concentration, he speaks it for others and also praises those who have attained concentration. Having himself attained wisdom, he speaks it for others and also praises those who have attained wisdom.

「云何十生法？谓十称誉处：若比丘自得信已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸得信者。自持戒已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸持戒者。自少欲已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸少欲者。自知足已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸知足者。自乐闲静，为他人说，亦复称叹乐闲静者。自多闻已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸多闻者。自精进已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸精进者。自专念已，为他人说，亦复称叹诸专念者。自得禅定，为他人说，亦复称叹得禅定者。自得智慧，为他人说，亦复称叹得智慧者。

What are the ten dharmas of knowledge? They are the ten dharmas of cessation: A person of right view can extinguish wrong view, and all the countless evils arising from wrong view are also completely extinguished. All the countless wholesome dharmas arising from right view are fully accomplished, including right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration, right liberation, and right knowledge. A person of right knowledge can extinguish wrong knowledge, and all the countless evils arising from wrong knowledge are completely extinguished; all the countless wholesome dharmas arising from right knowledge are fully accomplished. What are the ten dharmas of realization? They are the ten unlearned dharmas: unlearned right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration, right liberation, and right knowledge. Bhikṣus! These are the one hundred dharmas, which are true and not false. The Tathāgata, having known them, teaches the Dharma equally.

「云何十知法？谓十灭法：正见之人能灭邪见，诸缘邪见，起无数恶，亦尽除灭。诸因正见，生无数善，尽得成就正思、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定、正解脱、正智。正智之人能灭邪智，诸因邪智，起无数恶，悉皆除灭；诸因正智，起无数善法，尽得成就。云何十证法？谓十无学法：无学正见、正思、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定、正解脱、正智。诸比丘！是为百法，如实无虚，如来知已，平等说法。」

At that time, Śāriputra was approved by the Buddha. The bhikṣus heard what Śāriputra said and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，舍利弗佛所印可，诸比丘闻舍利弗所说，欢喜奉行。
