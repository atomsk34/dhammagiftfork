DĀ 11

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

11 （一一）增一经

Parallel to: DN 34 (~) / DĀ 10 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I will explain to you the subtle Dharma, with words of the upper, middle, and lower classes, all of which are completely true, with pure meaning and flavor, and complete in the practice of purity. It is called the Dharma of One Increase. Listen carefully and contemplate it well! I will explain it to you."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我与汝等说微妙法，上中下言，皆悉真正，义味清净，梵行具足，谓一增法也。汝等谛听！善思念之！当为汝说。」

At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened. The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "The Dharma of One Increase refers to the Dharma of One Accomplishment, the Dharma of One Cultivation, the Dharma of One Awakening, the Dharma of One Cessation, and the Dharma of One Realization. What is the Dharma of One Accomplishment? It means not abandoning the good Dharma. What is the Dharma of One Cultivation? It means always contemplating one's own body. What is the Dharma of One Awakening? It means contact with the contaminated. What is the Dharma of One Cessation? It means having the conceit of self. What is the Dharma of One Realization? It means the liberation of the mind free from obstruction.

时，诸比丘受教而听。佛告比丘：「一增法者，谓一成法、一修法、一觉法、一灭法、一证法。云何一成法？谓不舍善法。云何一修法？谓常自念身。云何一觉法？谓有漏触。云何一灭法？谓有我慢。云何一证法？谓无碍心解脱。

There are also the Dharma of Two Accomplishments, the Dharma of Two Cultivations, the Dharma of Two Awakenings, the Dharma of Two Cessations, and the Dharma of Two Realizations. What are the Dharma of Two Accomplishments? They are knowing shame and knowing modesty. What are the Dharma of Two Cultivations? They are tranquility and insight. What are the Dharma of Two Awakenings? They are name and form. What are the Dharma of Two Cessations? They are ignorance and craving for existence. What are the Dharma of Two Realizations? They are wisdom and liberation.

「又有二成法、二修法、二觉法、二灭法、二证法。云何二成法？谓知惭、知愧。云何二修法？谓止与观。云何二觉法？谓名与色。云何二灭法？谓无明、有爱。云何二证法？谓明与解脱。

There are also the Dharma of Three Accomplishments, the Dharma of Three Cultivations, the Dharma of Three Awakenings, the Dharma of Three Cessations, and the Dharma of Three Realizations. What are the Dharma of Three Accomplishments? They are associating with good friends, hearing the Dharma with the ears, and accomplishing the Dharma. What are the Dharma of Three Cultivations? They are the three samadhis: the samadhi of emptiness, the samadhi of signlessness, and the samadhi of wishlessness. What are the Dharma of Three Awakenings? They are the three feelings: the feeling of suffering, the feeling of pleasure, and the feeling of neither suffering nor pleasure. What are the Dharma of Three Cessations? They are the three kinds of craving: craving for desire, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence. What are the Dharma of Three Realizations? They are the three kinds of wisdom: the wisdom of knowing past lives, the wisdom of the divine eye, and the wisdom of the exhaustion of taints.

「又有三成法、三修法、三觉法、三灭法、三证法。云何三成法？一者亲近善友，二者耳闻法音，三法法成就。云何三修法？谓三三昧：空三昧、无想三昧、无作三昧。云何三觉法？谓三受：苦受、乐受、不苦不乐受。云何三灭法？谓三爱：欲爱、有爱、无有爱。云何三证法？谓三明：宿命智、天眼智、漏尽智。

There are also the Dharma of Four Accomplishments, the Dharma of Four Cultivations, the Dharma of Four Awakenings, the Dharma of Four Cessations, and the Dharma of Four Realizations. What are the Dharma of Four Accomplishments? They are dwelling in a central land, associating with good friends, being self-restrained, and having planted good roots in the past. What are the Dharma of Four Cultivations? They are dwelling in the four foundations of mindfulness: a monk contemplates the body internally with diligence and mindfulness, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; he contemplates the body externally with diligence and mindfulness, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; he contemplates the body internally and externally with diligence and mindfulness, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow. The same is true for contemplating feelings, mind, and dharmas. What are the Dharma of Four Awakenings? They are the four kinds of food: solid food, contact food, intention food, and consciousness food. What are the Dharma of Four Cessations? They are the four kinds of feelings: the feeling of desire, the feeling of self, the feeling of precepts, and the feeling of views. What are the Dharma of Four Realizations? They are the four fruits of the religious life: the fruit of stream-entry, the fruit of once-returning, the fruit of non-returning, and the fruit of arahantship.

「又有四成法、四修法、四觉法、四灭法、四证法。云何四成法？一者住中国，二者近善友，三者自谨慎，四者宿殖善本。云何四修法？住四念处：比丘内身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；内外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧。受、意、法观，亦复如是。云何四觉法？谓四食：抟食、触食、念食、识食。云何四灭法？谓四受：欲受、我受、戒受、见受。云何四证法？谓四沙门果：须陀洹果、斯陀含果、阿那含果、阿罗汉果。

There are also the Dharma of Five Accomplishments, the Dharma of Five Cultivations, the Dharma of Five Awakenings, the Dharma of Five Cessations, and the Dharma of Five Realizations. What are the Dharma of Five Accomplishments? They are the five factors of cessation: believing in the Buddha, the Tathāgata, the Perfectly Enlightened One, endowed with the ten titles; being free from illness, with a body always at ease; being straightforward without flattery, truly following the path to the Tathāgata's nirvāṇa; concentrating the mind without distraction, reciting and not forgetting; being skilled in observing the arising and cessation of dharmas, ending the root of suffering through the practices of the sages. What are the Dharma of Five Cultivations? They are the five faculties: the faculty of faith, the faculty of diligence, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of concentration, and the faculty of wisdom. What are the Dharma of Five Awakenings? They are the five aggregates of grasping: the aggregate of form, the aggregates of feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. What are the Dharma of Five Cessations? They are the five hindrances: the hindrance of sensual desire, the hindrance of ill will, the hindrance of sloth and torpor, the hindrance of restlessness and remorse, and the hindrance of doubt. What are the Dharma of Five Realizations? They are the five aggregates of the non-learner: the aggregate of non-learner precepts, the aggregate of non-learner concentration, the aggregate of wisdom, the aggregate of liberation, and the aggregate of the knowledge and vision of liberation.

「又有五成法、五修法、五觉法、五灭法、五证法。云何五成法？谓五灭尽支：一者信佛、如来、至真，十号具足。二者无病，身常安隐。三者质直无有谀谄，真趣如来涅槃径路。四者专心不乱，讽诵不忘。五者善于观察法之起灭，以贤圣行尽于苦本。云何五修法？谓五根：信根、精进根、念根、定根、慧根。云何五觉法？谓五受阴：色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴。云何五灭法？谓五盖：贪欲盖、瞋恚盖、睡眠盖、掉戯盖、疑盖。云何五证法？谓五无学聚：无学戒聚、无学定聚、慧聚、解脱聚、解脱知见聚。

There are also the Dharma of Six Accomplishments, the Dharma of Six Cultivations, the Dharma of Six Awakenings, the Dharma of Six Cessations, and the Dharma of Six Realizations. What are the Dharma of Six Accomplishments? They are the six layers of the Dharma. If a bhikṣu cultivates the six layers of the Dharma, he is respected and honored, harmonious in the community, without disputes, and practices alone without mixing. What are the six? Here, a bhikṣu always practices loving-kindness with the body and cultivates the brahma-conduct, abiding in a loving mind. This is called the weighty Dharma, respected and honored, harmonious in the community, without disputes, and practicing alone without mixing. Furthermore, a bhikṣu practices loving-kindness with the mouth and mind, and shares his own offerings and leftovers in his bowl with others, without any discrimination. Furthermore, a bhikṣu practices the precepts of the sages, without violating or defiling them, praised by the wise, fully upholding the precepts, accomplishing the noble path of transcendence, equally ending suffering, with right view and all the brahma-conduct. This is called the weighty Dharma, respected and honored, harmonious in the community, without disputes, and practicing alone without mixing.

「复有六成法、六修法、六觉法、六灭法、六证法。云何六成法？谓六重法。若有比丘修六重法，可敬可重，和合于众，无有诤讼，独行无杂。云何六？于是，比丘身常行慈及修梵行，住仁爱心，名曰重法，可敬可重，和合于众，无有诤讼，独行无杂。复次，比丘口慈、意慈，以己供养及钵中余，与人共之，不怀彼此。复次，比丘圣所行戒，不犯不毁，无有染污，智者所称，善具足持戒，成就贤圣出要，平等尽苦，正见及诸梵行，是名重法，可敬可重，和合于众，无有诤讼，独行不杂。

What are the Dharma of Six Cultivations? They are the six recollections: recollection of the Buddha, recollection of the Dharma, recollection of the Saṃgha, recollection of precepts, recollection of generosity, and recollection of the devas. What are the Dharma of Six Awakenings? They are the six internal sense bases: the sense base of the eye, the sense base of the ear, the sense base of the nose, the sense base of the tongue, the sense base of the body, and the sense base of the mind. What are the Dharma of Six Cessations? They are the six kinds of craving: craving for form, craving for sound, craving for smell, craving for taste, craving for touch, and craving for dharmas. What are the Dharma of Six Realizations? They are the six supernormal powers: realization of the supernormal power of spiritual faculties, realization of the supernormal power of divine hearing, realization of the supernormal power of knowing others' minds, realization of the supernormal power of recollecting past lives, realization of the supernormal power of the divine eye, and realization of the supernormal power of the exhaustion of taints.

「云何六修法？谓六念：佛念、法念、僧念、戒念、施念、天念。云何六觉法？谓六内入：眼入、耳入、鼻入、舌入、身入、意入。云何六灭法？谓六爱：色爱、声爱，香、味、触、法爱。云何六证法？谓六神通：一者神足通证，二者天耳通证，三者知他心通证，四者宿命通证，五者天眼通证，六者漏尽通证。

There are also the Dharma of Seven Accomplishments, the Dharma of Seven Cultivations, the Dharma of Seven Awakenings, the Dharma of Seven Cessations, and the Dharma of Seven Realizations. What are the Dharma of Seven Accomplishments? They are the seven treasures: the treasure of faith, the treasure of precepts, the treasure of shame, the treasure of conscience, the treasure of learning, the treasure of generosity, and the treasure of wisdom. These are the seven treasures. What are the Dharma of Seven Cultivations? They are the seven factors of awakening. Here, a bhikṣu cultivates the factor of awakening of mindfulness, based on non-desire, based on tranquility, based on detachment, cultivates the factor of awakening of investigation of phenomena, cultivates the factor of awakening of energy, cultivates the factor of awakening of joy, cultivates the factor of awakening of tranquility, cultivates the factor of awakening of concentration, cultivates the factor of awakening of equanimity, based on non-desire, based on tranquility, based on detachment.

「复有七成法、七修法、七觉法、七灭法、七证法。云何七成法？谓七财：信财、戒财、慙财、愧财、闻财、施财、惠财，是为七财。云何七修法？谓七觉意。于是，比丘修念觉意，依无欲、依寂灭、依远离，修法、修精进、修喜、修猗、修定、修舍，依无欲、依寂灭、依远离。

What are the Dharma of Seven Awakenings? They are the seven abodes of consciousness: there are sentient beings with various bodies and various perceptions, such as humans and devas. This is the first abode of consciousness. There are also sentient beings with various bodies but one perception, such as the devas of the first heaven of Brahmā. This is the second abode of consciousness. There are also sentient beings with one body and various perceptions, such as the devas of the second heaven of Brahmā. This is the third abode of consciousness. There are also sentient beings with one body and one perception, such as the devas of the third heaven of Brahmā. This is the fourth abode of consciousness. There are also sentient beings abiding in the sphere of infinite space, which is the fifth abode of consciousness. Or abiding in the sphere of infinite consciousness, which is the sixth abode of consciousness. Or abiding in the sphere of nothingness, which is the seventh abode of consciousness.

「云何七觉法？谓七识住处：若有众生，若干种身若干种想，天及人，此是初识住。复有众生，若干种身而一想者，梵光音天最初生时是，是二识住。复有众生，一身若干种想，光音天是，是三识住。复有众生，一身一想，徧净天是，是四识住处。复有众生，空处住，是五识住。或识处住，是六识住。或不用处，是七识住。

What are the Dharma of Seven Cessations? They are the seven afflictions: the affliction of desire, the affliction of existence, the affliction of views, the affliction of pride, the affliction of anger, the affliction of ignorance, and the affliction of doubt. What are the Dharma of Seven Realizations? They are the seven powers of the exhaustion of taints. Here, a bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints truly knows and sees all suffering, origination, cessation, taste, danger, and exit. He views desire as a pit of fire and also as a sword. Knowing desire and seeing desire, he is not attached to desire and his mind does not dwell in desire. He further observes well and truly knows and sees. In the world, the unwholesome dharmas of desire and evil do not arise and do not leak out. He cultivates the four foundations of mindfulness, cultivates them much and practices them much, the five faculties, five powers, seven factors of awakening, and the eightfold noble path, cultivates them much and practices them much.

「云何七灭法？谓七使法：欲爱使、有爱使、见使、慢使、瞋恚使、无明使、疑使。云何七证法？为七漏尽力。于是，漏尽比丘于一切诸苦、集、灭、味、过、出要，如实知见，观欲如火坑，亦如刀剑，知欲见欲，不贪于欲，心不住欲；于中复善观察，如实得知，如实见已，世间贪婬、恶不善法不起不漏，修四念处，多修多行，五根、五力、七觉意、贤圣八道，多修多行。

There are also eight accomplished dharmas, eight cultivated dharmas, eight awakened dharmas, eight extinguished dharmas, and eight realized dharmas. What are the eight accomplished dharmas? They are the eight causes and conditions. One has not yet attained the holy life but gains wisdom, and after attaining the holy life, wisdom increases. What are the eight? Here, a bhikṣu abides in dependence on the World-Honored One, or on an elder teacher, or on a wise practitioner of the holy life. He gives rise to a sense of shame and respect, and has affection and reverence. This is the first cause and condition. One has not yet attained the holy life but gains wisdom, and after attaining the holy life, wisdom increases. Furthermore, abiding in dependence on the World-Honored One, one asks about the meaning of the Dharma at the right time: "What is the meaning of this Dharma, and what is its purport?" The elder teacher then explains the profound meaning. This is the second cause and condition. Having heard the Dharma, one delights in the tranquility of body and mind. This is the third cause and condition. Not engaging in talk that obstructs the path and is of no benefit, one goes to the assembly and either preaches the Dharma oneself or requests others to preach. One still does not abandon the noble silence. This is the fourth cause and condition. Being learned and erudite, one upholds and does not forget the profound and subtle dharmas, the good of the upper, middle, and lower levels, the true meaning and taste, and the complete holy life. Having heard, one enters the mind and sees without being moved. This is the fifth cause and condition. Cultivating diligently, one extinguishes unwholesome practices and wholesome practices increase day by day. One strives and is capable, not abandoning these dharmas. This is the sixth cause and condition. Furthermore, with wisdom, one knows the arising and ceasing dharmas, and the noble path that can exhaust the suffering of the five aggregates. This is the seventh cause and condition. Furthermore, one observes the arising and ceasing of the five aggregates. This form, the arising and ceasing of form. This feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, the arising and ceasing of consciousness. This is the eighth cause and condition. One has not yet attained the holy life but gains wisdom, and after attaining the holy life, wisdom increases.

「复有八成法、八修法、八觉法、八灭法、八证法。云何八成法？谓八因缘。未得梵行而得智，得梵行已智增多。云何为八？如是比丘依世尊住，或依师长，或依智慧梵行者住，生惭愧心，有爱有敬，是为初因缘。未得梵行而得智，得梵行已智增多。复次，依世尊住，随时请问：『此法云何义，何所趣？』尊长即为开演深义，是为二因缘。既闻法已，身心乐静，是为三因缘。不为遮道无益杂论，彼到众中，或自说法，或请他说，犹复不舍贤圣默然，是为四因缘。多闻广博，守持不忘，诸法深奥，上中下善，义味诚谛，梵行具足，闻已入心，见不流动，是为五因缘。修习精勤，灭不善行，善行日增，勉力堪任，不舍斯法，是为六因缘。又以智慧知起灭法，贤圣] cf.【丽】所趣能尽苦际，是为七因缘。又观五受阴，生想、灭想，此色，色集、色灭，此受、想、行、识，识集、识灭，是为八因缘。未得梵行而有智，已得梵行智增多。

What are the eight cultivated dharmas? They are the eightfold noble path: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. What are the eight awakened dharmas? They are the eight worldly dharmas: gain and loss, slander and praise, fame and disgrace, suffering and happiness. What are the eight extinguished dharmas? They are the eight wrongnesses: wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, and wrong concentration. What are the eight realized dharmas? They are the eight liberations: observing forms while having form, the first liberation. Internally having no form perception, externally observing forms, the second liberation. The liberation of purity, the third liberation. Transcending form perception, extinguishing ill-will perception, abiding in the space element, the fourth liberation. Transcending the space element, abiding in the consciousness element, the fifth liberation. Transcending the consciousness element, abiding in the element of nothingness, the sixth liberation. Transcending the element of nothingness, abiding in the element of neither perception nor non-perception, the seventh liberation. Transcending the element of neither perception nor non-perception, abiding in the cessation of perception and feeling, the eighth liberation.

「云何八修法？谓贤圣八道：正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定。云何八觉法？谓世八法：利、衰、毁、誉、称、讥、苦、乐。云何八灭法？谓八邪：邪见、邪志、邪语、邪业、邪命、邪方便、邪念、邪定。云何八证法？谓八解脱：色观色，一解脱。内无色想，外观色，二解脱。净解脱，三解脱。度色想，灭瞋恚想，住空处，四解脱。度空处，住识处，五解脱。度识处，住不用处，六解脱。度不用处，住有想无想处，七解脱。度有想无想处，住想知灭，八解脱。

There are also nine accomplished dharmas, nine cultivated dharmas, nine awakened dharmas, nine extinguished dharmas, and nine realized dharmas. What are the nine accomplished dharmas? They are the nine pure branches of extinction: the pure branch of extinction of precepts, the pure branch of extinction of mind, the pure branch of extinction of views, the pure branch of extinction of transcending doubt, the pure branch of extinction of discrimination, the pure branch of extinction of the path, the pure branch of extinction of elimination, the pure branch of extinction of desirelessness, and the pure branch of extinction of liberation. What are the nine cultivated dharmas? They are the nine bases of joy: joy, love, delight, pleasure, concentration, true knowledge, abandonment, desirelessness, and liberation. What are the nine awakened dharmas? They are the nine abodes of sentient beings: some sentient beings have various bodies and various perceptions, such as gods and humans, which is the first abode of sentient beings. Some sentient beings have various bodies but one perception, such as the gods of Brahma's radiance at the initial time of birth, which is the second abode of sentient beings. Some sentient beings have one body and various perceptions, such as the gods of radiant sound, which is the third abode of sentient beings. Some sentient beings have one body and one perception, such as the gods of universal purity, which is the fourth abode of sentient beings. Without thought, without perception, without knowledge, such as the gods of the formless realm of nothingness, which is the fifth abode of sentient beings. Again, there are sentient beings who abide in the sphere of space, which is the sixth abode of sentient beings. Again, there are sentient beings who abide in the sphere of consciousness, which is the seventh abode of sentient beings. Again, there are sentient beings who abide in the sphere of nothingness, which is the eighth abode of sentient beings. Again, there are sentient beings who abide in the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, which is the ninth abode of sentient beings.

「复有九成法、九修法、九觉法、九灭法、九证法。云何九成法？谓九净灭枝法：戒净灭枝、心净灭枝、见净灭枝、度疑净灭枝、分别净灭枝、道净灭枝、除净灭枝、无欲净灭枝、解脱净灭枝。云何九修法？谓九喜本：一喜，二爱，三悦，四乐，五定，六如实知，七除舍，八无欲，九解脱。云何九觉法？谓九众生居：或有众生，若干种身若干种想，天及人是，是初众生居。或有众生，若干种身而一想者，梵光音天最初生时是，是二众生居。或有众生，一身若干种想，光音天是，是三众生居。或有众生，一身一想，遍净天是，是四众生居。无想无所觉知，无想天是，是五众生居。复有众生，空处住，是六众生居。复有众生，识处住，是七众生居。复有众生，不用处住，是八众生居。复有众生，住有想无想处，是九众生居。

What are the nine extinguished dharmas? They are the nine bases of craving: due to craving there is seeking, due to seeking there is gain, due to gain there is use, due to use there is desire, due to desire there is attachment, due to attachment there is jealousy, due to jealousy there is guarding, due to guarding there is protection. What are the nine realized dharmas? They are the nine exhaustions: entering the first dhyāna, the thorn of sound is extinguished. Entering the second dhyāna, the thorn of initial and sustained thought is extinguished. Entering the third dhyāna, the thorn of joy is extinguished. Entering the fourth dhyāna, the thorn of inhalation and exhalation is extinguished. Entering the sphere of space, the thorn of form perception is extinguished. Entering the sphere of consciousness, the thorn of space perception is extinguished. Entering the sphere of nothingness, the thorn of consciousness perception is extinguished. Entering the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, the thorn of nothingness perception is extinguished. Entering the cessation of perception and feeling, the thorn of perception and feeling is extinguished.

「云何九灭法？谓九爱本：因爱有求，因求有利，因利有用，因用有欲，因欲有著，因著有嫉，因嫉有守，因守有护。云何九证法？谓九尽：若入初禅，则声刺灭。入第二禅，则觉观刺灭。入第三禅，则喜刺灭。入第四禅，则出入息刺灭。入空处，则色想刺灭。入识处，则空想刺灭。入不用处，则识想刺灭。入有想无想处，则不用想刺灭。入灭尽定，则想受刺灭。

There are also ten accomplished dharmas, ten cultivated dharmas, ten awakened dharmas, ten extinguished dharmas, and ten realized dharmas. What are the ten accomplished dharmas? They are the ten saving dharmas: first, a bhikṣu is fully endowed with the two hundred and fifty precepts and also fully endowed with deportment, seeing even minor faults he gives rise to great fear, equally studying the precepts, his mind is not inclined to evil. Second, he obtains good friends. Third, his speech is upright and he is very tolerant. Fourth, he delights in seeking good dharmas and distributes them without stinginess. Fifth, when the various virtuous practitioners have something to establish, he immediately goes to assist them and does not consider it troublesome. What is difficult to do, he is able to do, and also teaches others to do it. Sixth, he is learned, hears and is able to retain, never forgetting. Seventh, he is diligent, extinguishing unwholesome dharmas and increasing wholesome dharmas. Eighth, he always concentrates his thoughts, without any other thoughts, recollecting past good practices as if they were right before his eyes. Ninth, he is accomplished in wisdom, observing the arising and ceasing of dharmas, cutting off the root of suffering with the noble discipline. Tenth, he delights in seclusion, concentrating his thoughts and contemplating, without any playfulness in between the dhyānas.

「复有十成法、十修法、十觉法、十灭法、十证法。云何十成法？谓十救法：一者比丘二百五十戒具，威仪亦具，见有小罪，生大怖畏，平等学戒，心无倾邪。二者得善知识。三者言语中正，多所堪忍。四者好求善法，分布不恡。五者诸梵行人有所施设，輙往佐助，不以为劳，难为能为，亦教人为。六者多闻，闻便能持，未曾有忘。七者精勤，灭不善法，增长善法。八者常自专念，无有他想，忆本善行，如在目前。九者智慧成就，观法生灭，以贤圣律断于苦本。十者乐于闲居，专念思惟，于禅中间无有调戏。

"What are the ten cultivated dharmas? They are the ten right practices: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration, right liberation, and right wisdom. What are the ten awakened dharmas? They are the ten sense bases of form: the sense base of the eye, the sense base of the ear, the sense base of the nose, the sense base of the tongue, the sense base of the body, the sense base of form, the sense base of sound, the sense base of smell, the sense base of taste, and the sense base of touch. What are the ten extinguished dharmas? They are the ten wrong practices: wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, wrong concentration, wrong liberation, and wrong wisdom. What are the ten realized dharmas? They are the ten unlearned dharmas: unlearned right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration, right liberation, and right wisdom. Bhikṣus! This is called the one increase dharma. I now explain this dharma for you. I, as the Tathāgata, have done all that should be done for the disciples. I have compassionately and earnestly instructed you. You should also diligently practice it. Bhikṣus! You should dwell in seclusion, under trees, in empty places, diligently sitting in meditation, not indulging yourselves. If you do not strive now, what benefit will there be in regretting it later? This is my teaching. You should diligently uphold it."

「云何十修法？谓十正行：正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定、正解脱、正智。云何十觉法？谓十色入：眼入、耳入、鼻入、舌入、身入、色入、声入、香入、味入、触入。云何十灭法？谓十邪行：邪见、邪志、邪语、邪业、邪命、邪方便、邪念、邪定、邪解脱、邪智。云何十证法？谓十无学法：无学正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定、正解脱、正智。诸比丘！此名一增法，我今为汝等说如是法，吾为如来、为诸弟子所应作者，皆已备悉，慈愍慇懃，训诲汝等，汝等亦宜勤奉行之。诸比丘！当在闲居树下空处，精勤坐禅，勿自放恣，今不勉力，后悔何益？此是我教，勤受持之。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
