DĀ 13

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

13 （一三）大缘方便经

Parallel to: DN 15 / MĀ 97

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the country of Kuru, at the abode of Kapilavastu, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在拘流沙国劫摩沙住处，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, Ānanda was in a secluded place, and he thought to himself, "How wonderful! How extraordinary! The light of the Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination spoken by the World-Honored One is profound and difficult to understand. As I contemplate it, it is like seeing it before my eyes. What is profound about it?" Then, Ānanda rose from his quiet room and went to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the World-Honored One, "Just now, in my quiet room, I was silently reflecting, 'How wonderful! How extraordinary! The light of the Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination spoken by the World-Honored One is profound and difficult to understand. As I contemplate it, it is like seeing it before my eyes. What is profound about it?'"

尔时，阿难在闲静处，作是念言：「甚奇！甚特！世尊所说十二因缘法之光明，甚深难解，如我意观，犹如目前，以何为深？」于是，阿难即从静室起，至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐，白世尊言：「我向于静室，默自思念：『甚奇！甚特！世尊所说十二因缘法之光明，甚深难解，如我意观，如在目前，以何为深？』」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "Stop! Stop! Do not say that the light of the Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination is profound and difficult to understand. Ānanda! This twelvefold dependent origination is difficult to see and difficult to know. The gods, demons, Brahmas, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas who have not seen the conditions have, if they wish to contemplate, observe, and analyze its meaning, all become confused and are unable to see it. Ānanda! I now tell you that old age and death have conditions. If someone asks, 'What are the conditions for old age and death?' you should answer them, 'Birth is the condition for old age and death.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for birth?' you should answer them, 'Existence is the condition for birth.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for existence?' you should answer them, 'Grasping is the condition for existence.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for grasping?' you should answer them, 'Craving is the condition for grasping.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for craving?' you should answer them, 'Feeling is the condition for craving.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for feeling?' you should answer them, 'Contact is the condition for feeling.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for contact?' you should answer them, 'The six sense bases are the condition for contact.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for the six sense bases?' you should answer them, 'Name and form are the condition for the six sense bases.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for name and form?' you should answer them, 'Consciousness is the condition for name and form.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for consciousness?' you should answer them, 'Formations are the condition for consciousness.' If they further ask, 'Who is the condition for formations?' you should answer them, 'Ignorance is the condition for formations.' Ānanda! Thus, due to ignorance there are formations, due to formations there is consciousness, due to consciousness there is name and form, due to name and form there are the six sense bases, due to the six sense bases there is contact, due to contact there is feeling, due to feeling there is craving, due to craving there is grasping, due to grasping there is existence, due to existence there is birth, due to birth there is old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress, the great mass of suffering arises. This is the condition for the great mass of suffering."

尔时，世尊告阿难曰：「止！止！勿作此言：『十二因缘法之光明，甚深难解！』阿难！此十二因缘难见难知，诸天、魔、梵、沙门、婆罗门、未见缘者，若欲思量观察分别其义者，则皆荒迷，无能见者。阿难！我今语汝老死有缘，若有问言：『何等是老死缘？』应答彼言：『生是老死缘！』若复问言：『谁是生缘？』应答彼言：『有是生缘。』若复问言：『谁是有缘？』应答彼言：『取是有缘。』若复问言：『谁是取缘？』应答彼言：『爱是取缘。』若复问言：『谁是爱缘？』应答彼言：『受是爱缘。』若复问言：『谁是受缘？』应答彼言：『触是受缘。』若复问言：『谁为触缘？』应答彼言：『六入是触缘。』若复问言：『谁为六入缘？』应答彼言：『名色是六入缘。』若复问言：『谁为名色缘？』应答彼言：『识是名色缘。』若复问言：『谁为识缘？』应答彼言：『行是识缘。』若复问言：『谁为行缘？』应答彼言：『痴是行缘。』阿难！如是缘痴有行，缘行有识，缘识有名色，缘名色有六入，缘六入有触，缘触有受，缘受有爱，缘爱有取，缘取有有，缘有有生，缘生有老、死、忧、悲、苦恼，大患所集，是为此大苦阴缘。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Dependent on birth there is old age and death. What does this mean? If all sentient beings were without birth, would there be old age and death?"

佛告阿难：「缘生有老死，此为何义？若使一切众生无有生者，宁有老死不？」

Ānanda replied, "No."

阿难答曰：「无也。」

"Therefore, Ānanda, from this cause, know that old age and death are due to birth. The meaning of what I have said is in this."

「是故，阿难！以此缘，知老死由生，缘生有老死，我所说者，义在于此。」

He further said to Ānanda, "What is the meaning of 'due to existence there is birth'? If all sentient beings had no desire for existence, no form or formless existence, would there be birth?"

又告阿难：「缘有有生，此为何义？若使一切众生无有欲有、色无色有者，宁有生不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that birth is due to existence, and that due to existence there is birth. The meaning of what I have said is in this."

「阿难！我以此缘，知生由有，缘有有生，我所说者，义在于此。」

He further said to Ānanda, "What is the meaning of 'existence due to grasping'? If all sentient beings had no desire-grasping, view-grasping, precept-grasping, or self-grasping, would there be existence?"

又告阿难：「缘取有有，此为何义？若使一切众生无有欲取、见取、戒取、我取者，宁有有不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that existence is due to grasping. The meaning of what I have said is in this."

「阿难！我以此缘，知有由取，缘取有有，我所说者，义在于此。」

He further said to Ānanda, "What is the meaning of grasping due to craving? If all sentient beings had no craving for existence, craving for non-existence, or craving for neither existence nor non-existence, would there be grasping?"

又告阿难：「缘爱有取，此为何义？若使一切众生无有欲爱、有爱、无有爱者，宁有取不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无有。」

"Ānanda! I know from this that grasping is due to craving. The meaning of my statement that grasping arises from craving is in this."

「阿难！我以此缘，知取由爱，缘爱有取，我所说者，义在于此。」

He further asked Ānanda, "What is the meaning of saying that craving arises from feeling? If all sentient beings had no pleasant feeling, painful feeling, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, would there be craving?"

又告阿难：「缘受有爱，此为何义？若使一切众生无有乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受者，宁有爱不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this that craving arises from feeling, and that craving arises from feeling. This is the meaning of what I have said. Ānanda! You should know that because of craving there is seeking, because of seeking there is gain, because of gain there is use, because of use there is desire, because of desire there is attachment, because of attachment there is jealousy, because of jealousy there is guarding, because of guarding there is protection. Ānanda! Because of protection, there are weapons, disputes, and the arising of countless evils. This is the meaning of what I have said. Ānanda! What is the meaning of this? If all sentient beings had no protection, would there be weapons, disputes, and the arising of countless evils?"

「阿难！我以此缘，知爱由受，缘受有爱，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！当知因爱有求，因求有利，因利有用，因用有欲，因欲有著，因著有嫉，因嫉有守，因守有护。阿难！由有护故，有刀杖、诤讼、作无数恶，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！此为何义，若使一切众生无有护者，当有刀杖、诤讼，起无数恶不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Therefore, Ānanda! From this cause and condition, I know that weapons and disputes arise from protection, and that weapons and disputes arise from protection. This is the meaning of what I have said."

「是故，阿难！以此因缘，知刀杖、诤讼由护而起，缘护有刀杖、诤讼。阿难！我所说者，义在于此。」

He further said to Ānanda, "What is the meaning of protection arising from guarding? If all sentient beings were without guarding, would there be protection?"

又告阿难：「因守有护，此为何义？若使一切众生无有守者，宁有护不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that protection arises from guarding, and that protection is due to guarding. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of guarding arising from jealousy? If all sentient beings were without jealousy, would there be guarding?"

「阿难！我以此缘，知护由守，因守有护，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因嫉有守，此为何义？若使一切众生无有嫉者，宁有守不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that guarding arises from jealousy, and that guarding is due to jealousy. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of jealousy arising from attachment? If all sentient beings were without attachment, would there be jealousy?"

「阿难！我以此缘，知守由嫉，因嫉有守，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因著有嫉，此为何义？若使一切众生无有著者，宁有嫉不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that jealousy arises from attachment, and that jealousy is due to attachment. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of attachment arising from desire? If all sentient beings were without desire, would there be attachment?"

「阿难！我以此缘，知嫉由著，因著有嫉，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因欲有著，此为何义？若使一切众生无有欲者，宁有著不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that attachment arises from desire, and that attachment is due to desire. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of desire arising from use? If all sentient beings were without use, would there be desire?"

「阿难！我以此缘，知著由欲，因欲有著，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因用有欲，此为何义？若使一切众生无有用者，宁有欲不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this meaning that desire arises from use, and that desire is due to use. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of use arising from profit? If all sentient beings were without profit, would there be use?"

「阿难，我以此义，知欲由用，因用有欲，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因利有用，此为何义，若使一切众生无有利者，宁有用不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this meaning that use arises from profit, and that use is due to profit. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of seeking profit? If all sentient beings were without seeking, would there be profit?"

「阿难！我以此义，知用由利，因利有用，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因求有利，此为何义？若使一切众生无有求者，宁有利不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that profit is due to seeking, and that profit is due to seeking. The meaning of what I have said is in this. Ānanda! What is the meaning of seeking due to love? If all sentient beings were without love, would there be seeking?"

「阿难！我以此缘，知利由求，因求有利，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！因爱有求，此为何义？若使一切众生无有爱者，宁有求不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that seeking is due to love, and that seeking is due to love. The meaning of what I have said is in this."

「阿难！我以此缘，知求由爱，因爱有求，我所说者，义在于此。」

He also told Ānanda, "Because of love, there is seeking, and because of seeking, there is guarding. The same is true for feeling. Because of feeling, there is seeking, and because of seeking, there is guarding."

又告阿难：「因爱有求，至于守护，受亦如是，因受有求，至于守护。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Feeling arises from contact. What does this mean? Ānanda! If there were no eyes, no forms, and no eye consciousness, would there be contact?"

佛告阿难：「缘触有受，此为何义？阿难！若使无眼、无色、无眼识者，宁有触不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"If there were no ears, sounds, and ear consciousness, nose, smells, and nose consciousness, tongue, tastes, and tongue consciousness, body, tactile sensations, and body consciousness, and mind, mental objects, and mind consciousness, would there be contact?"

「若无耳、声、耳识，鼻、香、鼻识，舌、味、舌识，身、触、身识，意、法、意识者，宁有触不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! If all sentient beings were without contact, would there be feeling?"

「阿难！若使一切众生无有触者，宁有受不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know that feeling arises from contact, and that feeling arises in dependence on contact. This is the meaning of what I have said. Ānanda! Contact arises in dependence on name and form. What does this mean? If all sentient beings were without name and form, would there be mental contact?"

「阿难！我以是义，知受由触，缘触有受，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！缘名色有触，此为何义？若使一切众生无有名色者，宁有心触不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"If all sentient beings were without physical form, would there be physical contact?"

「若使一切众生无形色相貌者，宁有身触不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! If there were no name and form, would there be contact?"

「阿难！若无名色，宁有触不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I know from this cause that contact is due to name and form, and that contact arises from name and form. This is the meaning of what I have said. Ānanda! Name and form arise from consciousness. What does this mean? If consciousness does not enter the mother's womb, would there be name and form?"

「阿难！我以是缘，知触由名色，缘名色有触，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！缘识有名色，此为何义？若识不入母胎者，有名色不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"If consciousness enters the womb but does not emerge, would there be name and form?"

「若识入胎不出者，有名色不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"If consciousness emerges from the womb, and the infant decays, can name and form grow?"

「若识出胎，婴孩坏败，名色得增长不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! If there is no consciousness, can there be name and form?"

「阿难！若无识者，有名色不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! I say this because I know that name and form depend on consciousness, and consciousness is the condition for name and form. The meaning of what I say is this. Ānanda! Consciousness depends on name and form. What does this mean? If consciousness does not abide in name and form, then consciousness has no place to abide. If there is no place to abide, can there be birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress?"

「阿难！我以是缘，知名色由识，缘识有名色，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！缘名色有识，此为何义？若识不住名色，则识无住处；若无住处，宁有生、老、病、死、忧、悲、苦恼不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! If there is no name and form, can there be consciousness?"

「阿难！若无名色，宁有识不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda! I say that consciousness depends on name and form, and name and form depend on consciousness. The meaning of my words is here. Therefore, Ānanda! Name and form depend on consciousness, consciousness depends on name and form, name and form depend on the six sense bases, the six sense bases depend on contact, contact depends on feeling, feeling depends on craving, craving depends on grasping, grasping depends on becoming, becoming depends on birth, birth depends on old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair, and the great mass of suffering arises.

「阿难！我以此缘，知识由名色，缘名色有识，我所说者，义在于此。阿难！是故名色缘识，识缘名色，名色缘六入，六入缘触，触缘受，受缘爱，爱缘取，取缘有，有缘生，生缘老、死、忧、悲、苦] cf.【丽】、恼，大苦阴集。

Ānanda! This is the limit of speech, the limit of response, the limit of definition, the limit of exposition, the limit of wisdom observation, and the limit of sentient beings. Ānanda! The bhikṣus who correctly observe this Dharma with right view attain liberation of the mind without taints. Ānanda! This bhikṣu is called one liberated by wisdom. The Tathāgata knows this kind of liberation, the Tathāgata knows the end of liberation, the Tathāgata knows the end and non-end of liberation, and the Tathāgata knows neither the end nor non-end of liberation. Why? Ānanda! This is the limit of speech, the limit of response, the limit of definition, the limit of exposition, the limit of wisdom observation, and the limit of sentient beings. Having known this completely, the bhikṣu who is liberated with mind without taints does not know or see such knowledge and vision. Ānanda! Those who conceive of a self, how many are called the view of a self? Name and form together are conceived as the self. Some say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am feeling.' Some say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am not feeling, the nature of feeling is the self.' Some say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am not feeling, the nature of feeling is not the self, but craving is the self.'

「阿难！齐是为语，齐是为应，齐是为限，齐此为演说，齐是为智观，齐是为众生。阿难！诸比丘于此法中，如实正观，无漏心解脱。阿难！此比丘当名为慧解脱，如是解脱比丘如来终亦知，如来不终亦知，如来终不终亦知，如来非终非不终亦知，何以故？阿难！齐是为语，齐是为应，齐是为限，齐是为演说，齐是为智观，齐是为众生；如是尽知已，无漏心解脱比丘不知不见如是知见。阿难！夫计我者，齐几名我见，名色与受，俱计以为我。有人言：『受非我，我是受。』或有言：『受非我，我非受，受法是我。』或有言：『受非我，我非受，受法非我，但爱是我。』

Ānanda! Those who see the self as feeling should be told: 'The Tathāgata speaks of three feelings: pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. When there is pleasant feeling, there is no painful feeling or neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling; when there is painful feeling, there is no pleasant feeling or neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling; when there is neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, there is no painful feeling or pleasant feeling.' Why? Ānanda! Pleasant contact gives rise to pleasant feeling. When pleasant contact ceases, feeling also ceases. Ānanda! Painful contact gives rise to painful feeling. When painful contact ceases, feeling also ceases; neither-painful-nor-pleasant contact gives rise to neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. When neither-painful-nor-pleasant contact ceases, feeling also ceases. Ānanda! It is like two pieces of wood rubbing together to produce fire, but when they are placed separately, there is no fire. It is the same here. Because of pleasant contact as a condition, pleasant feeling arises. When pleasant contact ceases, feeling also ceases together; because of painful contact as a condition, painful feeling arises. When painful contact ceases, feeling also ceases together; because of neither-painful-nor-pleasant contact as a condition, neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling arises. When neither-painful-nor-pleasant contact ceases, feeling also ceases together. Ānanda! These three feelings are conditioned, impermanent, arising from causes and conditions, subject to cessation, subject to destruction, and subject to decay. They are not mine, I am not them, they should be seen as they really are with right wisdom. Ānanda! Those who see the self as feeling are mistaken.

「阿难！彼见我者，言受是我，当语彼言：『如来说三受，乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受。当有乐受时，无有苦受、不苦不乐受；有苦受时，无有乐受、不苦不乐受；有不苦不乐受时，无有苦受、乐受。』所以然者？阿难！乐触缘生乐受，若乐触灭受亦灭。阿难！苦触缘生苦受，若苦触灭受亦灭；不苦不乐触缘生不苦不乐受，若不苦不乐触灭受亦灭。阿难！如两木相攒则有火生，各置异处则无有火。此亦如是，因乐触缘故生乐受，若乐触灭受亦俱灭；因苦触缘故生苦受，若苦触灭受亦俱灭；因不苦不乐触缘生不苦不乐受，若不苦不乐触灭受亦俱灭。阿难！此三受有为无常，从因缘生，尽法、灭法，为朽坏法。彼非我有，我非彼有，当以正智如实观之。阿难！彼见我者，以受为我，彼则为非。

Ānanda! Those who see the self as feeling should be told: 'The Tathāgata speaks of three feelings: painful feeling, pleasant feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. If pleasant feeling were the self, when pleasant feeling ceased, there would be two selves, which is a mistake. If painful feeling were the self, when painful feeling ceased, there would be two selves, which is a mistake. If neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling were the self, when neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling ceased, there would be two selves, which is a mistake.' Ānanda! Those who see the self as feeling are mistaken. Ānanda! Those who conceive of a self say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am not feeling, the nature of feeling is the self.' They should be told: 'All is without feeling. How can you say there is the nature of feeling? Are you the nature of feeling?' They reply: 'No.' Therefore, Ānanda! Those who conceive of a self say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am not feeling, the nature of feeling is the self.' They are mistaken.

「阿难！彼见我者，言受非我，我是受者，当语彼言：『如来说三受，苦受、乐受、不苦不乐受。若乐受是我者，乐受灭时，则有二我，此则为过。若苦受是我者，苦受灭时，则有二我，此则为过。若不苦不乐受是我者，不苦不乐受灭时，则有二我，此则为过。』阿难！彼见我者，言：『受非我，我是受。』彼则为非。阿难！彼计我者，作此说：『受非我，我非受，受法是我。』当语彼言：『一切无受。汝云何言有受法？汝是受法耶？』对曰：『非是。』是故，阿难！彼计我者，言：『受非我，我非受，受法是我。』彼则为非。

Ānanda! Those who conceive of a self say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am not feeling, the nature of feeling is not the self, but craving is the self.' They should be told: 'All is without feeling. How can there be craving? Are you craving?' They reply: 'No.' Therefore, Ānanda! Those who conceive of a self say: 'Feeling is not the self, I am not feeling, the nature of feeling is not the self, craving is the self.' They are mistaken. Ānanda! This is the limit of speech, this is the limit of response, this is the limit of explanation, this is the limit of exposition, this is the limit of wisdom, this is the limit of beings. Ānanda! If bhikṣus correctly see the truth in this Dharma, they will attain liberation of mind from the contaminants. Ānanda! These bhikṣus are called 'liberated by wisdom'. Such bhikṣus, with liberated minds, know existence, know non-existence, know both existence and non-existence, and know neither existence nor non-existence. Why? Ānanda! This is the limit of speech, this is the limit of response, this is the limit of explanation, this is the limit of exposition, this is the limit of wisdom, this is the limit of beings. Having fully known this, bhikṣus with liberated minds from the contaminants do not know or see such knowledge and views.

「阿难！彼计我者，作是言：『受非我，我非受，受法非我，但爱是我。』者，当语彼言：『一切无受，云何有爱？汝是爱耶？』对曰：『非也。』是故，阿难！彼计我者，言：『受非我，我非受，受法非我，爱是我。』者，彼则为非。阿难！齐是为语，齐是为应，齐是为限，齐是为演说，齐是为智观，齐是为众生。阿难！诸比丘于此法中如实正观，于无漏心解脱。阿难！此比丘当名为慧解脱，如是解脱心比丘，有我亦知，无我亦知，有我无我亦知，非有我非无我亦知。何以故？阿难！齐是为语，齐是为应，齐是为限，齐是为演说，齐是为智观，齐是为众生；如是尽知已，无漏心解脱比丘不知不见如是知见。」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Those who conceive of a self have already determined it. Those who conceive of a self say: 'Some form is the self, some form is not the self, some formless is the self, some formless is not the self.' Ānanda! Those who say 'some form is the self' are determined that some form is the self, and what they see is true, while the rest is false. Those who say 'some form is not the self' are determined that some form is not the self, and what they see is true, while the rest is false. Those who say 'some formless is the self' are determined that some formless is the self, and what they see is true, while the rest is false. Those who say 'some formless is not the self' are determined that some formless is not the self, and what they see is true, while the rest is false.

佛语阿难：「彼计我者，齐已为定，彼计我者，或言少色是我，或言多色是我，或言少无色是我，或言多无色是我。阿难！彼言少色是我者，定少色是我，我所见是，余者为非。多色是我者，定多色是我，我所见是，余者为非。少无色是我者，定言少无色是我，我所见是，余者为非。多无色是我者，定多无色是我，我所见是，余者为非。」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "The seven abodes of consciousness, the two sense bases, some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say: 'This place is peaceful, a refuge, a protection, a shelter, a lamp, a light, a return, not false, not afflictive.' What are the seven? Some beings have various bodies and various perceptions, such as gods and humans. This is the first abode of consciousness. Some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say: 'This place is peaceful, a refuge, a protection, a shelter, a lamp, a light, a return, not false, not afflictive.' Ānanda! If a bhikṣu knows the first abode of consciousness, knows its origin, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its danger, and knows the way out, and knows it as it really is, then Ānanda! That bhikṣu says: 'That is not me, I am not that.' He knows and sees it as it really is. Some beings have various bodies but one perception, such as the gods of Brahmā's Light Sound Heaven. Some beings have one body but various perceptions, such as the gods of Light Sound Heaven. Some beings have one body and one perception, such as the gods of Pure Heaven. Some beings abide in the space element. Some beings abide in the consciousness element. Some beings abide in the element of neither perception nor non-perception. These are the seven abodes of consciousness. Some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say: 'This place is peaceful, a refuge, a protection, a shelter, a lamp, a light, a return, not false, not afflictive.' Ānanda! If a bhikṣu knows the seven abodes of consciousness, knows their origin, knows their cessation, knows their taste, knows their danger, and knows the way out, and knows them as they really are, then that bhikṣu says: 'That is not me, I am not that.' He knows and sees it as it really is. These are the seven abodes of consciousness.

佛告阿难：「七识住，二入处，诸有沙门、婆罗门言：『此处安隐，为救、为护、为舍、为灯、为明、为归，为不虚妄，为不烦恼。』云何为七？或有众生，若干种身若干种想，天及人，此是初识住处。诸沙门、婆罗门言：『此处安隐，为救、为护、为舍、为灯、为明、为归，为不虚妄，为不烦恼。』阿难！若比丘知初识住，知集、知灭、知味、知过、知出要，如实知者。阿难！彼比丘言：『彼非我，我非彼，如实知见。』或有众生，若干种身而一想，梵光音天是；或有众生，一身若干种想，光音天是；或有众生一身一想，遍净天是；或有众生，住空处；或有众生，住识处；或有众生，住不用处，是为七识住处。或有沙门、婆罗门言：『此处安隐，为救、为护、为舍、为灯、为明、为归，为不虚妄，为不烦恼。』阿难！若比丘知七识住，知集、知灭、知味、知过、知出要，如实知见，彼比丘言：『彼非我，我非彼，如实知见。』是为七识住。

What are the two sense bases? The base of non-conception and the base of neither conception nor non-conception. These, Ānanda, are the two sense bases. Some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say: 'This place is peaceful, a refuge, a protection, a shelter, a lamp, a light, a return, not false, not afflictive.' Ānanda! If a bhikṣu knows the two sense bases, knows their origin, knows their cessation, knows their taste, knows their danger, and knows the way out, and knows them as they really are, then that bhikṣu says: 'That is not me, I am not that.' He knows and sees it as it really is. These are the two sense bases.

「云何二入处？无想入、非想非无想入。是为，阿难！此二入处，或有沙门、婆罗门言：『此处安隐，为救、为护、为舍、为灯、为明、为归，为不虚妄，为不烦恼。』阿难！若比丘知二入处，知集、知灭、知味、知过、知出要，如实知见，彼比丘言：『彼非我，我非彼，如实知见。』是为二入。

Ānanda! There are also eight liberations. What are the eight? Observing forms while having form, the first liberation. Internally without form-perception, observing external forms, the second liberation. Pure liberation, the third liberation. Transcending form-perception, extinguishing opposing perceptions, not dwelling on mixed perceptions, abiding in the space element, the fourth liberation. Transcending the space element, abiding in the consciousness element, the fifth liberation. Transcending the consciousness element, abiding in the element of neither perception nor non-perception, the sixth liberation. Transcending the element of neither perception nor non-perception, abiding in the element of neither perception nor non-perception, the seventh liberation. Cessation of perception and feeling, the eighth liberation. Ānanda! Bhikṣus who can freely enter and exit these eight liberations, going against and following them, such bhikṣus attain liberation through both wisdom and concentration.

「阿难！复有八解脱。云何八？色观色，初解脱。内无色想，观外色，二解脱。净解脱，三解脱。度色想，灭有对想，不念杂想，住空处，四解脱。度空处，住识处，五解脱。度识处，住不用处，六解脱。度不用处，住有想无想处，七解脱。灭尽定，八解脱。阿难！诸比丘于此八解脱，逆顺游行，入出自在，如是比丘得俱解脱。」

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and upheld it.

尔时，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
