DĀ 14

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

14 （一四）释提桓因问经

Parallel to: DN 21 / MĀ 134

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the Indrasāra Cave on the north side of the Ambapālī Village in the country of Magadha.

一时，佛在摩竭国庵婆罗村北，毘陀山因陀娑罗窟中。

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, gave rise to a subtle and good mind, wishing to come and see the Buddha. He thought, "Now I shall go to where the World-Honored One is."

尔时，释提桓因发微妙善心，欲来见佛：「今我当往至世尊所。」

At that time, the devas of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven heard that Śakra, Lord of the Devas, had given rise to a subtle and good mind, wishing to go to where the Buddha was. They immediately went to Śakra and said, "Excellent! Śakra, Lord of the Devas, has given rise to a subtle and good mind, wishing to go to where the Tathāgata is. We too wish to attend and follow to where the World-Honored One is."

时，诸忉利天闻释提桓因发妙善心，欲诣佛所，即寻诣帝释，白言：「善哉！帝释！发妙善心，欲诣如来，我等亦乐侍从诣世尊所。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, immediately told the gandharva general Pañcaśikha, "I now wish to go to where the World-Honored One is. You may go together. These devas of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven will also go with me to where the Buddha is."

时，释提桓因即告执乐神般遮翼曰：「我今欲诣世尊所，汝可俱行，此忉利诸天亦当与我俱诣佛所。」

He replied, "Yes!" At that time, Pañcaśikha held a crystal zither and played it in front of Śakra, Lord of the Devas, and the assembly of Trāyastriṃśa devas.

对曰：「唯然！」时，般遮翼持琉璃琴，于帝释前忉利天众中鼓琴供养。

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, the Trāyastriṃśa devas, and Pañcaśikha suddenly disappeared from the Dharma Hall, like a strong man bending and stretching his arm, and arrived at the northern Vṛji Mountains in the country of Magadha.

时，释提桓因、忉利诸天及般遮翼，于法堂上忽然不现，譬如力士屈伸臂顷，至摩竭国北毘陀山中。

At that time, the World-Honored One entered the flame-like samādhi, and the Vṛji Mountains all became the same color as fire. The people of the country saw this and said to each other, "This Vṛji Mountain is all the same color as fire. It must be the power of the Tathāgata and the devas.

尔时，世尊入火焰三昧，彼毘陀山同一火色，时国人见，自相谓言：「此毘陀山同一火色，将是如来诸天之力。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, told Pañcaśikha, "The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One is very difficult to see. He has come to this quiet place, where there is silence and no sound, with birds and beasts as companions. This place is always guarded by great devas. You should go ahead and play the crystal zither to entertain the World-Honored One. I and the devas will follow behind.

时，释提桓因告般遮翼曰：「如来、至真甚难得覩，而能垂降此闲静处，寂默无声，禽兽为侣，此处常有诸大神天侍卫世尊，汝可于前鼓琉璃琴娱乐世尊，吾与诸天寻于后往。」

He replied, "Yes!" Having received the instruction, he took the crystal zither and went to the Buddha ahead of time. Not far from the Buddha, he played the crystal zither and sang in verse:

对曰：「唯然！」即受教已，持琉璃琴于先诣佛。去佛不远，鼓琉璃琴，以偈歌曰：

I pay homage to your father, who is very dignified. When you
were born, it was auspicious, and my heart was very fond of
you. Originally, for a small reason, desire arose in my mind. It
gradually increased, like making offerings to an arhat. The Śākya son is
devoted to the four dhyānas and always enjoys solitude. With right intention
he seeks immortality. I am also devoted to this. The Able One
has given rise to the mind of the path and definitely wishes
to attain perfect enlightenment. I now seek this girl and definitely wish
to unite with her. My mind is born with attachment, loving and
not letting go. I want to let go but cannot leave, like
an elephant being controlled by a hook. Like encountering a cool breeze
when hot, like a thirsty person getting cold spring water, like one
who attains nirvāṇa, like water extinguishing fire. Like a sick person getting
a good doctor, like a hungry person getting delicious food, being satisfied
gives rise to happiness, like an arhat roaming the Dharma. Like an
elephant being deeply hooked, yet still unwilling to submit, running and difficult
to restrain, unrestrained and not stopping on its own. Like a cool
pond, with various flowers covering the water. A tired and hot elephant
bathes and cools its whole body. The offerings I have made before
and after, making offerings to the arhats, those in the world who
have the retribution of blessings, I will all make offerings to them
When you die, I will die with you. Without you, I cannot
live. Rather let my body die, unable to be without you. The
lord of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, Śakra, now grants my wish. You are
praised for your propriety. You should consider it well.

「跋陀礼汝父，　　汝父甚端严；生汝时吉祥，　　我心甚爱乐。本以小因缘，　　欲心于中生；展转遂增广，　　如供养罗汉。释子专四禅，　　常乐于闲居；正意求甘露，　　我专念亦尔。能仁发道心，　　必欲成正觉；我今求彼女，　　必欲会亦尔。我心生染著，　　爱好不舍离；欲舍不能去，　　如象为钩制。如热遇凉风，　　如渴得冷泉；如取涅槃者，　　如水灭于火。如病得良医，　　饥者得美食；充足生快乐，　　如罗汉游法。如象被深钩，　　而犹不肯伏；［马＊奔］突难禁制，　　放逸不自止。犹如清凉池，　　众花覆水上；疲热象沐浴，　　举身得清凉。我前后所施，　　供养诸罗汉；世有福报者，　　尽当与彼供。汝死当共死，　　汝无我活为？宁使我身死，　　不能无汝存。忉利天之主，　　释今与我愿；称汝礼节具，　　汝善思察之。」

At that time, the World-Honored One arose from samādhi and said to Pañcaśikha, "Excellent! Excellent! Pañcaśikha! You are able to praise the Tathāgata with a pure voice and a crystal zither. The sound of the zither and your voice are neither long nor short, sad and harmonious, touching people's hearts. What you play on the zither is fully endowed with various meanings, also speaking of the bondage of desire, also speaking of the practice of the Brahma-way, also speaking of the śramaṇa, also speaking of nirvāṇa."

尔时，世尊从三昧起，告般遮翼言：「善哉！善哉！般遮翼！汝能以清净音和琉璃琴称赞如来，琴声、汝音，不长不短，悲和哀婉，感动人心。汝琴所奏，众义备有，亦说欲缚，亦说梵行，亦说沙门，亦说涅槃。」

At that time, Pañcaśikha addressed the Buddha, saying, "I remember when the World-Honored One first attained Buddhahood by the Nairañjanā River under the Ajapāla tree, the son of the great general of the Śakra Heaven and the daughter of the King of Music were together, only engaging in sexual pleasure. At that time, I saw their minds like that and composed a verse for them, speaking of the bondage of desire, also speaking of the practice of the Brahma-way, also speaking of the śramaṇa, also speaking of nirvāṇa. When the heavenly maiden heard my verse, she raised her eyes and smiled, saying to me, 'Pañcaśikha! I have not yet seen the Tathāgata. I once heard the heavenly beings praise the Tathāgata in the Dharma lecture hall of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, saying that he has such virtues and such power. You always have faith and are close to the Tathāgata. I now wish to be your friend.' World-Honored One! After I spoke those words to her, I did not speak to her again."

尔时，般遮翼白佛言：「我念世尊昔郁鞞罗尼连禅水边，阿游波陀尼俱律树下初成佛道时，有尸汉陀天大将子及执乐天王女，共于一处，但设欲乐，我于尔时见其心尔，即为作颂，颂说欲缚，亦说梵行，亦说沙门，亦说涅槃。时，彼天女闻我偈已，举目而笑语我言：『般遮翼！我未见如来，我曾于忉利天法讲堂上，闻彼诸天称赞如来，有如是德，有如是力。汝常怀信，亲近如来，我今意欲与汝共为知识。』世尊！我时与一言之后，不复与语。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, had this thought: "This Pañcaśikha has already entertained the Tathāgata. I should now think of him." At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, immediately thought of him. At that time, Pañcaśikha also had this thought: "Śakra, Lord of the Devas, is now thinking of me." He then took his crystal zither and went to where Śakra, Lord of the Devas, was. Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to him, "You should use my name and the intention of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven to inquire about the Tathāgata's well-being and whether he is walking strong."

时，释提桓因作是念：「此般遮翼已娱乐如来讫，我今宁可念于彼人。」时，天帝释即念彼人。时，般遮翼复生念言：「今天帝释乃能念我。」即持琉璃琴诣帝释所。帝释告曰：「汝以我名并称忉利天意，问讯世尊：『起居轻利，游步强耶？』」

At that time, Pañcaśikha, receiving Śakra, Lord of the Devas' instructions, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the World-Honored One, "Śakra, Lord of the Devas, and the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities have sent me to inquire about the World-Honored One's well-being and whether he is walking strong."

时，般遮翼承帝释教，即诣世尊所，头面礼足，于一面住，白世尊言：「释提桓因及忉利诸天故，遣我来问讯世尊：『起居轻利，游步强耶？』」

The World-Honored One replied, "May your Śakra, Lord of the Devas, and the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities have long lives, happiness, and no afflictions. Because all sentient beings, including the devas, humans, and asuras, all crave long life, happiness, and no afflictions."

世尊报曰：「使汝帝释及忉利天寿命延长，快乐无患。所以然者？诸天、世人及阿须轮诸众生等，皆贪寿命、安乐、无患。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, again thought to himself, "We should go to pay homage to the World-Honored One." He then went with the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. Śakra, Lord of the Devas, then said to the Buddha, "I wonder how far it is from here to the World-Honored One. May I sit down?"

尔时，帝释复自念言：「我等宜往礼觐世尊。」即与忉利诸天往诣佛所，头面礼足，却住一面。时，帝释白佛言：「不审我今去世尊远近可坐？」

The Buddha told Śakra, Lord of the Devas, "Your heavenly assembly is numerous. Just sit near me."

佛告帝释曰：「汝天众多，但近我坐。」

At that time, the cave where the World-Honored One was staying naturally became vast and unobstructed. Then Śakra, Lord of the Devas, together with the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities and the Pāñcika deities, all bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "Once, the Buddha was staying in the house of a brāhman in Śrāvastī. At that time, the World-Honored One entered the flame-like samādhi. I was then on my way to visit Vaiśravaṇa, the Guardian King of the North, on some minor matter, riding in my thousand-spoked jeweled chariot. Passing through the sky, I saw a heavenly maiden standing with joined palms in front of the World-Honored One. I then said to that maiden, 'When the World-Honored One emerges from samādhi, you should address me by name and ask the World-Honored One about his well-being and whether he is strong in his travels.' I wonder whether that maiden later conveyed my message to him or not? World-Honored One! Can you remember this matter?"

时，世尊所止因陀罗窟，自然广博，无所障碍。尔时，帝释与忉利诸天及般遮翼皆礼佛足，于一面坐，帝释白佛言：「一时，佛在舍卫国婆罗门舍，尔时世尊入火焰三昧，我时以少因缘，乘千辐宝车，诣毘楼勒天王所，于空中过，见一天女叉手在世尊前立，我寻语彼女言：『若世尊三昧起者，汝当称我名字，问讯世尊：起居轻利，游步强耶？』不审彼女后竟为我达此心不？世尊！宁能忆此事不？」

The Buddha said, "I remember it. That maiden immediately conveyed your message to me. When I emerged from samādhi, I still heard the sound of your chariot."

佛言：「忆耳！彼女寻以汝声致问于我，吾从定起，犹闻汝车声。」

Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "In the past, for some minor reason, I gathered with the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities in the Dharma Hall. Those old deities all said, 'When the Tathāgata appears in the world, it increases the number of deities and decreases the number of asuras.' Now, I personally see the World-Honored One and personally know and personally realize that when the Tathāgata, the Perfectly Enlightened One, appears in the world, it increases the number of deities and decreases the number of asuras. There is this Śākya woman named Gopā who has practiced pure conduct under the World-Honored One. When her body perished and her life ended, she was reborn in the palace of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven and became my son. The Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities all say, 'The great deity Gopā has great merit and great power.' There are also three other bhikṣus who have practiced pure conduct under the World-Honored One. When their bodies perished and their lives ended, they were reborn among the inferior bhuta spirits. They always come to serve me every day. Gopā, having seen this, teased them with a verse:

帝释白佛言：「昔者，我以少缘，与忉利诸天集在法堂，彼诸旧天皆作是言：『若如来出世，增益诸天众，减损阿须轮众。』今我躬见世尊，躬见自知，躬自作证，如来、至真出现于世，增益诸天众，减损阿须轮众。此有瞿夷释女，于世尊所净修梵行，身坏命终，生忉利天宫，即为我子。忉利诸天皆称言：『瞿夷大天子有大功德，有大威力。』复有余三比丘，于世尊所净修梵行，身坏命终，生于卑下执乐神中，常日日来为我给使，瞿夷见已，以偈触娆曰：

You are disciples of the Buddha. When I was a householder
I offered you clothing and food, paid homage to you, and
treated you with respect. What are your names? You personally received
the Buddha's instructions. The pure-eyed one spoke, but you did not
observe it. I used to pay homage to you and heard
the supreme Dharma from the Buddha. I was reborn in the
Trāyastriṃśa Heaven and became the son of Śakra. Why do you
not observe the merit I possess? I was originally a woman
but now I am the son of Śakra. You and I
originally practiced pure conduct together, but now you alone are in
a lowly position, serving us. You originally engaged in evil conduct
and now you receive this retribution. You alone are in a
lowly position, serving us. Born in this impure place, you are
touched by others. Having heard this, you should be disgusted. This
place is to be despised. From now on, be diligent and
do not serve others again. The two of you diligently practice
and contemplate the Tathāgata's Dharma. Abandon what you cling to and
observe desire as impure conduct. Desire binds and is not true
deceiving the world. Like an elephant freed from its bonds, you
transcend the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. Śakra and the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven deities gather
in the Dharma Hall. They have already surpassed the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven
with their courageous power. Śakra praises what has never been seen
before, and the deities also see it. This is the son
of Śākya, who has surpassed the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. Gopā speaks these
words out of disgust for the bonds of desire. There is
a Buddha in the country of Magadha, named Śākyamuni. His son
greatly lost his mind, but later regained his mindfulness. Among the
three, one still becomes a bhuta spirit, while the other two
see the truth and transcend the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. The Dharma spoken
by the World-Honored One, the disciples have no doubts. All together
hearing the Dharma, the two surpass the one. Having seen their
own superiority, they are all reborn in the Ābhāsvara Heaven. Having
seen them, I have come to the Buddha.

「『汝为佛弟子，　　我本在家时，以衣食供养，　　礼拜致恭恪。汝等名何人？　　躬受佛教诫；净眼之所说，　　汝不观察之。我本礼敬汝，　　从佛闻上法；生三十三天，　　为帝释作子。汝等何不观？　　我所有功德；本为女人身，　　今为帝释子。汝等本俱共，　　同修于梵行；今独处卑贱，　　为吾等给使。本为弊恶行，　　今故受此报；独处于卑贱，　　为吾等给使。生此处不净，　　为他所触娆；闻已当患厌，　　此处可厌患。从今当精勤，　　勿复为人使；二人勤精进，　　思惟如来法。舍彼所恋著，　　观欲不净行；欲缚不真实，　　诳惑于世间。如象离羁靽，　　超越忉利天；释及忉利天，　　集法讲堂上。彼已勇猛力，　　超越忉利天；释叹未曾有，　　诸天亦见过。此是释迦子，　　超越忉利天；患厌于欲缚，　　瞿夷说此言。摩竭国有佛，　　名曰释迦文；彼子大失意，　　其后还得念。三人中一人，　　故为执乐神；二人见道谛，　　超越忉利天。世尊所说法，　　弟子不怀疑；俱共同闻法，　　二人胜彼一。自见殊胜已，　　皆生光音天；我观见彼已，　　故来至佛所。』」

Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "Please grant me the leisure to resolve my doubts."

帝释白佛言：「愿开闲暇，一决我疑。」

The Buddha said, "Ask as you wish. I will explain each one for you."

佛言：「随汝所问，吾当为汝一一演说。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "All the devas, humans, gandharvas, asuras, and other beings, are all bound by what fetters, up to those who are enemies, holding weapons against each other?"

尔时，帝释即白佛言：「诸天、世人、干沓和、阿须罗及余众生等，尽与何结相应，乃至怨讐、刀杖相向？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "The arising of enmity and resentment is all due to greed and jealousy, causing the devas, humans, asuras, and other beings to attack each other with weapons."

佛告释言：「怨结之生，皆由贪嫉，故使诸天、世人、阿须罗、余众生等，刀杖相加。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! The arising of enmity and resentment is due to greed and jealousy, causing the devas, humans, asuras, and other beings to attack each other with weapons. Now, having heard the Buddha's teaching, my doubts are all removed and I have no more doubts. But I do not understand the arising of greed and jealousy. From what do they arise? What are their causes and conditions? Who is their original source? From whom do they exist? From whom do they not exist?"

尔时，帝释即白佛言：「实尔！世尊！怨结之生，由贪嫉故，使诸天、世人、阿须罗、余众生等，刀杖相加。我今闻佛所说，疑网悉除，无复疑也，但不解此贪嫉之生，何由而起？何因何缘？谁为原首？从谁而有？从谁而无？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "The arising of greed and jealousy is all due to love and hatred. Love and hatred are the cause, love and hatred are the condition, love and hatred are the source. They arise from this, and without this, they do not exist."

佛告帝释：「贪嫉之生，皆由爱憎，爱憎为因，爱憎为缘，爱憎为首，从此而有，无此则无。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! The arising of greed and jealousy is all due to love and hatred. Love and hatred are the cause, love and hatred are the condition, love and hatred are the source. They arise from this, and without this, they do not exist. Now, having heard the Buddha's teaching, my confusion is all removed and I have no more doubts. But I do not understand the arising of love and hatred. From what do they arise? What are their causes and conditions? Who is their original source? From whom do they exist? From whom do they not exist?"

尔时，帝释即白佛言：「实尔。世尊！贪嫉之生，皆由爱憎，爱憎为因，爱憎为缘，爱憎为首，从此而有，无此则无。我今闻佛所说，迷惑悉除，无复疑也。但不解爱憎复何由而生？何因何缘？谁为原首？从谁而有？从谁而无？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "The arising of love and hatred is all due to desire. Desire is the cause, desire is the condition, desire is the source. They arise from this, and without this, they do not exist."

佛告帝释：「爱憎之生，皆由于欲，因欲缘欲，欲为原首，从此而有，无此则无。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! The arising of love and hatred is all due to desire. Desire is the cause, desire is the condition, desire is the source. They arise from this, and without this, they do not exist. Now, having heard the Buddha's teaching, my confusion is all removed and I have no more doubts. But I do not understand the arising of desire. From what does it arise? What are its causes and conditions? Who is its original source? From whom does it exist? From whom does it not exist?"

尔时，帝释白佛言：「实尔！世尊！爱憎之生，皆由于欲，因欲缘欲，欲为原首，从此而有，无此则无。我今闻佛所说，迷惑悉除，无复疑也。但不知此欲复何由生？何因何缘？谁为原首？从谁而有？从谁而无？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "Love arises from conceptualization. Conceptualization is the cause, conceptualization is the condition, conceptualization is the original source. It arises from this, and without this, it does not exist."

佛告帝释：「爱由想生，因想缘想，想为原首，从此而有，无此而无。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! Love arises from conceptualization. Conceptualization is the cause, conceptualization is the condition, conceptualization is the original source. It arises from this, and without this, it does not exist. Now, having heard the Buddha's teaching, I have no more doubts. But I do not understand the arising of conceptualization. From what does it arise? What are its causes and conditions? Who is its original source? From whom does it exist? From whom does it not exist?"

尔时，帝释白佛言：「实尔！世尊！爱由想生，因想缘想，想为原首，从此而有，无此则无。我今闻佛所说，无复疑也。但不解想复何由而生？何因何缘？谁为原首？从谁而有？从谁而无？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "Conceptualization arises from playfulness. Playfulness is the cause, playfulness is the condition, playfulness is the original source. It arises from this, and without this, it does not exist. Śakra! If there were no playfulness, there would be no conceptualization. Without conceptualization, there would be no desire. Without desire, there would be no love or hatred. Without love or hatred, there would be no greed or jealousy. If there were no greed or jealousy, all beings would not harm each other. Śakra! It is only because of playfulness as the basis, playfulness as the cause, playfulness as the condition, playfulness as the original source, that there is conceptualization. From conceptualization comes desire. From desire comes love and hatred. From love and hatred comes greed and jealousy. Because of greed and jealousy, it causes all beings to harm each other."

佛告帝释：「想之所生，由于调戏，因调缘调，调为原首，从此而有，无此则无。帝释！若无调戏则无想，无想则无欲，无欲则无爱憎，无爱憎则无贪嫉，若无贪嫉，则一切众生不相伤害。帝释！但缘调为本，因调缘调，调为原首，从此有想，从想有欲，从欲有爱憎，从爱憎有贪嫉；以贪嫉故，使群生等共相伤害。」

Śakra said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! Conceptualization arises from playfulness. Playfulness is the cause, playfulness is the condition, playfulness is the original source. It arises from this, and without this, it does not exist. If there were no playfulness from the beginning, there would be no conceptualization. Without conceptualization, there would be no desire. Without desire, there would be no love or hatred. Without love or hatred, there would be no greed or jealousy. Without greed or jealousy, all beings would not harm each other. It is only because conceptualization arises from playfulness, playfulness is the cause, playfulness is the condition, playfulness is the original source, that there is conceptualization. From conceptualization comes desire. From desire comes love and hatred. From love and hatred comes greed and jealousy. From greed and jealousy, it causes all beings to harm each other. Now, having heard the Buddha's teaching, my confusion is completely removed and I have no more doubts."

帝释白佛言：「实尔！世尊！由调有想，因调缘调，调为原首，从此有想由调而有，无调则无。若本无调者则无想，无想则无欲，无欲则无爱憎，无爱憎则无贪嫉，无贪嫉则一切群生不相伤害。但想由调生，因调缘调，调为原首，从调有想，从想有欲，从欲有爱憎，从爱憎有贪嫉，从贪嫉使一切众生共相伤害。我今闻佛所说，迷惑悉除，无复疑也。」

At that time, Śakra again said to the Buddha, "Do all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas completely eliminate playfulness and abide in the traces of extinction, or do they not eliminate playfulness and abide in the traces of extinction?"

尔时，帝释复白佛言：「一切沙门、婆罗门尽除调戏在灭迹耶？为不除调戏在灭迹耶？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "All śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas do not completely eliminate playfulness and abide in the traces of extinction. Why is this so? Śakra, there are various realms in the world, and sentient beings each rely on their own realms, firmly holding onto them and unable to let go, considering them to be true while others are false. Therefore, Śakra, all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas do not completely eliminate playfulness and abide in the traces of extinction."

佛告帝释：「一切沙门、婆罗门不尽除调戏在灭迹也。所以然者？帝释，世间有种种界，众生各依己界，坚固守持，不能舍离，谓己为实，余者为虚。是故，帝释！一切沙门、婆罗门不尽除调戏而在灭迹。」

At that time, Śakra said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! There are various sentient beings in the world, each relying on their own realms, firmly holding onto them and unable to let go, considering themselves to be right while others are false. Therefore, all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas do not completely eliminate playfulness and abide in the traces of extinction. Having heard the Buddha's words, my doubts are all dispelled and I have no more doubts."

尔时，帝释白佛言：「实尔！世尊！世间有种种众生，各依己界，坚固守持，不能舍离，谓己为是，余为虚妄。是故一切沙门、婆罗门不尽除调戏而在灭迹。我闻佛言，疑惑悉除，无复疑也。」

Śakra again said to the Buddha, "How much playfulness is there in the traces of extinction?"

帝释复白佛言：「齐几调在灭迹耶？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "Playfulness is threefold: first, verbal; second, conceptual; third, seeking. What is said verbally can harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. Abandoning such speech, one does not harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. A timely monk, as he speaks, is focused and undistracted. Conceptualization can also harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. Abandoning such conceptualization, one does not harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. A timely monk, as he conceptualizes, is focused and undistracted. Śakra! Seeking can also harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. Abandoning such seeking, one does not harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. A timely monk, as he seeks, is focused and undistracted."

佛告帝释：「调戏有三：一者口，二者想，三者求。彼口所言，自害、害他，亦二俱害。舍此言已，如所言，不自害、不害他、不二俱害。知时比丘如口所言，专念不乱，想亦自害、害他，亦二俱害；舍此想已，如所想，不自害、不害他，二俱不害，知时比丘如所想，专念不乱。帝释！求亦自害、害他，亦二俱害；舍此求已，如所求，不自害、不害他，不二俱害，知时比丘如所求，专念不乱。」

At that time, Śakra Devarāja said, "I have heard the Buddha's teaching and have no more doubts."

尔时，释提桓因言：「我闻佛所说，无复狐疑。」

He further asked the Buddha, "What is considered the abandonment of the mind of the sages?"

又白佛言：「齐几名贤圣舍心？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "There are three types of abandonment of the mind: first, the body of joy, second, the body of sorrow, and third, the body of equanimity. Śakra! The body of joy harms oneself, harms others, and harms both. Abandoning this joy, one does not harm oneself, does not harm others, and does not harm both. The timely bhikṣu who is mindful and undistracted is said to have received the full precepts. Śakra! The body of sorrow harms oneself, harms others, and harms both. Abandoning this sorrow, one does not harm oneself, does not harm others, and does not harm both. The timely bhikṣu who is mindful and undistracted is said to have received the full precepts. Furthermore, Śakra! The body of equanimity harms oneself, harms others, and harms both. Abandoning this body, one does not harm oneself, does not harm others, and does not harm both. The timely bhikṣu who is mindful and undistracted is said to have received the full precepts."

佛告帝释：「舍心有三：一者喜身，二者忧身，三者舍身。帝释！彼喜身者，自害、害他，亦二俱害；舍此喜已，如所喜，不自害、害他，二俱不害，知时比丘专念不忘，即名受具足戒。帝释！彼忧身者，自害、害彼，亦二俱害；舍此忧已，如所忧，不自害、害他，二俱不害，知时比丘专念不忘，即名受具足戒。复次，帝释！彼舍身者，自害、害他，亦二俱害；舍此身已，如所舍，不自害、不害他，二俱不害，知时比丘专念不忘，是即名为受具足戒。」

Śakra said to the Buddha, "I have heard what the Buddha has said and have no more doubts."

帝释白佛言：「我闻佛所说，无复狐疑。」

He further asked the Buddha, "What is meant by the noble precepts and the complete faculties?"

又白佛言：「齐几名贤圣律诸根具足？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "The eye knows form, and I say there are two kinds: what is to be cherished and what is not to be cherished. The ear and sound, the nose and smell, the tongue and taste, the body and touch, the mind and dharmas, I say there are two kinds: what is to be cherished and what is not to be cherished."

佛告帝释：「眼知色，我说有二：可亲、不可亲。耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意法，我说有二：可亲、不可亲。」

At that time, Śakra said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! The Tathāgata has briefly explained but not extensively distinguished. I have fully understood. The eye knows form, and I say there are two kinds: what is to be cherished and what is not to be cherished. The ear and sound, the nose and smell, the tongue and taste, the body and touch, the mind and dharmas have two kinds: what is to be cherished and what is not to be cherished. World-Honored One! If the eye sees form, the good dharmas diminish and the bad dharmas increase, then I say that such eye knowing form is not to be cherished. The ear and sound, the nose and smell, the tongue and taste, the body and touch, the mind knowing dharmas, if the good dharmas diminish and the bad dharmas increase, I say they are not to be cherished. World-Honored One! If the eye sees form, the good dharmas increase and the bad dharmas diminish, then I say that such eye knowing form is to be cherished. The ear and sound, the nose and smell, the tongue and taste, the body and touch, the mind knowing dharmas, if the good dharmas increase and the bad dharmas diminish, I say they are to be cherished."

尔时，帝释白佛言：「世尊！如来略说，未广分别，我以具解。眼知色，我说有二：可亲、不可亲。耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意法有二：可亲、不可亲。世尊！如眼观色，善法损减，不善法增，如此眼知色，我说不可亲；耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意知法，善法损减，不善法增，我说不可亲。世尊！如眼见色，善法增长，不善法减，如是眼知色，我说可亲；耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意知法，善法增长，不善法减，我说可亲。」

The Buddha told Śakra, "Excellent! Excellent! This is called the noble discipline of the faculties being complete."

佛告帝释：「善哉！善哉！是名贤圣律诸根具足。」

Śakra said to the Buddha, "I have heard what the Buddha has said and have no more doubts."

帝释白佛言：「我闻佛所说，无复狐疑。」

He further asked the Buddha, "How many bhikṣus are called 'ultimate,' 'ultimate practice of the Brahma-way,' 'ultimate peace,' and 'ultimate without remainder'?"

复白佛言：「齐几比丘名为究竟、究竟梵行、究竟安隐、究竟无余？」

The Buddha told Śakra, "Those whose bodies are destroyed by suffering due to craving are called 'ultimate,' 'ultimate practice of the Brahma-way,' 'ultimate peace,' and 'ultimate without remainder.'"

佛告帝释：「为爱所苦，身得灭者，是为究竟、究竟梵行、究竟安隐、究竟无余。」

Śakra said to the Buddha, "For a long time, I have been entangled in doubts. Now the Tathāgata has resolved my doubts."

帝释白佛言：「我本长夜，所怀疑网，今者如来开发所疑。」

The Buddha said to Śakra, "Have you ever gone to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas to ask about this meaning?"

佛告帝释：「汝昔颇曾诣沙门、婆罗门所问此义不？」

Śakra said to the Buddha, "I remember that in the past, I did go to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas to inquire about this meaning. Once, I gathered in the lecture hall with the heavenly hosts to discuss whether the Tathāgata would appear in the world or not. At that time, we searched together but did not see the Tathāgata appearing in the world. We each returned to our palaces to enjoy the five desires. World-Honored One! Later, I saw the great divine devas who had indulged in the five desires and gradually passed away. At that time, World-Honored One! I was greatly terrified, and my hair stood on end. I then saw śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas dwelling in seclusion, leaving home and renouncing desires. I went to them and asked, 'What is called the ultimate?' When I asked this meaning, they were unable to answer. Since they did not know, they asked me in return, 'Who are you?' I then replied, 'I am Śakra Devānām-Indra.' They further asked, 'What kind of Śakra are you?' I then answered, 'I am Śakra Devānām-Indra. I have doubts in my mind, so I have come to ask.' At that time, I told them the meaning of Śakra according to my knowledge and understanding. They asked me, 'Become our disciple. I am now a disciple of the Buddha, having attained the path of stream-entry, not falling into other destinies, and going back and forth seven times, I will surely accomplish the fruit of the path. May the World-Honored One predict that I will become a once-returner.' Having spoken these words, he further spoke a verse:

帝释白佛言：「我自忆念：昔者，曾诣沙门、婆罗门所咨问此义。昔我一时曾集讲堂，与诸天众共论：『如来为当出世，为未出世？』时共推求，不见如来出现于世，各自还宫，五欲娱乐。世尊！我复于后时见诸大神天，自恣五欲已，渐各命终。时我，世尊！怀大恐怖，衣毛为竖。时，见沙门、婆罗门处在闲静，去家离欲，我寻至彼所问言：『云何名究竟？』我问此义，彼不能报。彼既不知，逆问我言：『汝为是谁？』我寻报言：『我是释提桓因。』彼复问言：『汝是何释？』我时答言：『我是天帝释，心有所疑，故来相问耳。』时，我与彼如所知见，说于释义。彼问我言：『更为我弟子，我今是佛弟子，得须陀洹道，不堕余趣，极七往返，必成道果，唯愿世尊记我为斯陀含。』说此语已，复作颂曰：

Because of their defiled thoughts, I gave rise to doubts. For a
long time, I and the devas searched for the Tathāgata. Seeing the
renunciants always dwelling in secluded places, I thought they were the World-Honored
Buddha. Therefore, I went to them and bowed, saying: 'I have come
to ask now, what is the ultimate?' Having asked, they were unable
to answer the path they had gone. Today's unequaled Honored One is
what I have long sought. Having already observed my own practice, my
mind has already contemplated correctly. Only the Sage has already known the
activities of my mind. The practice I have cultivated for a long
time, may the Pure-Eyed One predict it. I take refuge in the
supreme among humans, the unsurpassed Honored One in the three realms, who
can sever the thorns of affection and love. Now I pay homage
to the Honored One of the Sun.

「『由彼染秽想，　　故生我狐疑；长夜与诸天，　　推求于如来。见诸出家人，　　常在闲静处；谓是佛世尊，　　故往稽首言：我今故来问，　　云何为究竟？问已不能报，　　道迹之所趣。今日无等尊，　　是我久所求；已观察己行，　　心已正思惟。唯圣先已知，　　我心之所行；长夜所修业，　　愿净眼记之。归命人中上，　　三界无极尊；能断恩爱刺，　　今礼日光尊。』」

The Buddha said to Śakra, "Do you remember when you attained joy and pleasure, and thought of pleasure?"

佛告帝释：「汝忆本得喜乐、念乐时不？」

Śakra replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! I remember the joy and pleasure I attained in the past. World-Honored One! I once fought with the asuras, and I won, while the asuras retreated. When I returned, I attained joy and pleasure. Compared to that joy and pleasure, it is free from the joy and pleasure of swords and weapons, and the joy and pleasure of fighting. The joy and pleasure I now attain from the Buddha is free from the joy and pleasure of swords and weapons, and the joy and pleasure of disputes."

帝释答曰：「如是，世尊！忆昔所得喜乐、念乐。世尊！我昔曾与阿须轮共战，我时得胜，阿须轮退，我时则还，得欢喜、念乐，计此欢喜、念乐，离有秽恶刀杖喜乐、鬪讼喜乐。今我于佛所得喜、念乐，无有刀杖、诤讼之乐。」

The Buddha said to Śakra, "Now that you have attained joy and pleasure, what meritorious fruits do you wish to seek from it?"

佛告帝释：「汝今得喜乐、念乐，于中欲求何功德果？」

At that time, Śakra said to the Buddha, "I wish to seek five meritorious fruits from joy and pleasure. What are the five?" He then spoke a verse:

尔时，帝释白佛言：「我于喜乐、念乐中，欲求五功德果，何等五？」即说偈言：

When my life ends here, I will give up my heavenly life
May I be free from illness in the womb, and may my
mind be joyful. The Buddha liberates those who are not yet liberated
and can teach the true path. Among the three Buddha teachings, I
will surely practice the holy life. With the body of wisdom, I
will see the truth with my own mind. I will attain the
understanding of the origin, and thus attain long liberation. I should diligently
practice and cultivate the true wisdom of the Buddha. Even if I
do not attain the realization of the path, my merits will still
surpass the heavens. All the wondrous gods, such as the Akaniṣṭha gods
down to the last body, will surely be born there. I now
receive a pure heavenly body here, and also attain an increase in
lifespan. I know this with pure eyes.

「我后若命终，　　舍于天上寿；处胎不怀患，　　使我心欢喜。佛度未度者，　　能说正真道；于三佛法中，　　我要修梵行。以智慧身居，　　心自见正谛；得达本所起，　　于是长解脱。但当勤修行，　　习佛真实智；设不获道证，　　功德犹胜天。诸有神妙天，　　阿迦尼咤等；下至末后身，　　必当生彼处。我今于此处，　　受天清净身；复得增寿命，　　净眼我自知。」

Having spoken this verse, he said to the Buddha, "I wish to obtain these five meritorious fruits from joy and pleasure."

说此偈已，白佛言：「我于喜乐、念乐中，欲得如是五功德果。」

At that time, Śakra said to the gods of Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, "It is good that you pay respect and serve the Brahma-like youth in front of the gods of Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, and now you also pay respect in front of the Buddha.

尔时，帝释语忉利诸天曰：「汝于忉利天上梵童子前恭敬礼事，今于佛前复设此敬者，不亦善哉！」

Before long, the Brahma-like youth suddenly stood above the heavenly assembly in the sky and spoke a verse to Śakra, the Lord of the Devas:

其语未久，时，梵童子忽然于虚空中天众上立，向天帝释而说偈曰：

The heavenly king's holy life benefit many beings. The lord of the
devas of Magadha is able to ask the Tathāgata about the meaning.

「天王清净行，　　多利益众生；摩竭帝释主，　　能问如来义。」

When the Brahma-like youth had spoken this verse, he suddenly disappeared. At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, rose from his seat, bowed at the feet of the World-Honored One, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and then departed. The devas of Trāyastriṃśa and Pañcaśikha also bowed at the Buddha's feet and departed. Then Śakra, Lord of the Devas, went a little further and said to Pañcaśikha, "Excellent! Excellent! You were able to play the lute and entertain the Buddha before me and the devas arrived. I now know that you will succeed your father as the foremost among the Gandharvas. I will give you the daughter of King Vāta of the Gandharvas as your wife."

时，梵童子说此偈已，忽然不现。是时，帝释即从座起，礼世尊足，遶佛三匝，却行而退，忉利诸天及般遮翼亦礼佛足，却行而退。时，天帝释少复前行，顾语般遮翼曰：「善哉！善哉！汝能先于佛前鼓琴娱乐，然后我及诸天于后方到，我今知汝补汝父位，于干沓和中最为上首，当以彼拔陀干沓和王女与汝为妻。」

When the World-Honored One spoke this Dharma, eighty-four thousand devas were free from defilements and gave rise to the Dharma eye.

世尊说此法时，八万四千诸天远尘离垢，诸法法眼生。

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, the devas of Trāyastriṃśa, and Pañcaśikha, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

时，释提桓因、忉利诸天及般遮翼闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
