DĀ 15

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

15 （一五）阿 ［少／兔］ 夷经

Parallel to: DN 24

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Mīna, in the region of Ārāḍha, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在冥宁国阿［少／兔］夷土，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, the World-Honored One put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered the city of Ārāḍha to beg for alms. At that time, the World-Honored One silently thought to himself: "Now I am begging for alms, and it is still early. I should now go to the garden of the brāhman Pāvāya." The bhikṣus should wait until the time is right before begging for alms.

尔时，世尊著衣持钵，入阿［少／兔］夷城乞食。尔时，世尊默自念言：「我今乞食，于时如早，今宜往诣房伽婆梵志园观。」比丘须时至，然后乞食。

At that time, the World-Honored One went to the garden. At that time, the brāhman saw the Buddha coming from afar, immediately got up to welcome him, and exchanged greetings, saying: "Welcome, Gautama! It has been a long time since we last met. What brings you here now? Please take a seat here, Gautama!" At that time, the World-Honored One took a seat.

尔时，世尊即诣彼园，时，彼梵志遥见佛来，即起奉迎，共相问讯，言：「善来，瞿昙！不面来久，今以何缘乃能屈顾？唯愿瞿昙就此处坐！」尔时，世尊即就其坐。

At that time, the brāhman sat to one side and said to the World-Honored One: "Last night, the bhikṣu Suṣārman came to me and said: 'Master! I will not practice the holy life under the Buddha. The reason is that the Buddha has neglected me.' Although that person spoke ill of Gautama, I did not accept it.

时，彼梵志于一面坐，白世尊言：「先夜隶车子善宿比丘来至我所，语我言：『大师！我不于佛所修梵行也。所以然者？佛疎外我。』彼人见向说瞿昙过，虽有此言，我亦不受。」

The Buddha told the brāhman: "Suṣārman's words are known to you. Once, I was at the Dharma Hall by the Monkey Pond in Vaiśālī. At that time, Suṣārman came to me and said: 'The Tathāgata has neglected me. I will not practice the holy life under the Tathāgata.' I then asked him: 'Why do you say: "The Tathāgata has neglected me. I will not practice the holy life under the Tathāgata"?'" Suṣārman replied to me: 'The Tathāgata does not show me supernatural transformations.'

佛告梵志：「彼善宿所言，知汝不受耳。昔我一时在毗舍离猕猴池侧集法堂上，时，此善宿来至我所，语我言：『如来外我，我不于如来所修梵行也。』我时告曰：『汝何故言：我不于如来所修梵行，如来外我耶？』善宿报我言：『如来不为我现神足变化。』

At that time, I said to him: 'Can I invite you to practice the holy life in my Dharma, and then show you supernatural transformations?' He then said to me: 'The Tathāgata should show me supernatural transformations before I can practice the holy life.' At that time, Suṣārman replied to me: 'No, World-Honored One!' The Buddha told Suṣārman: 'I also did not tell you: "If you practice the holy life in my Dharma, I will show you supernatural transformations." You also did not say to me: "If you show me supernatural transformations, then I will practice the holy life." How is it, Suṣārman! Do you think the Tathāgata can show supernatural transformations, or not? Can the Dharma I teach lead to liberation and the end of suffering, or not?' Suṣārman said to the Buddha: 'Yes, World-Honored One! The Tathāgata can show supernatural transformations, not that he cannot. The Dharma he teaches can lead to liberation and the end of suffering, not that it cannot.' 'Therefore, Suṣārman! The Dharma I teach, if practiced, can show supernatural transformations, not that it cannot. It can lead to liberation and the end of suffering, not that it cannot. What do you seek in this Dharma?'

「时，我语言：『吾可请汝于我法中净修梵行，当为汝现神足耶？』复当语我：『如来当为我现神足变化，然后我当修梵行耶？』时，善宿报我言：『不也。世尊！』佛告善宿：『我亦不语汝言：汝于我法中净修梵行，当为汝现神足变化；汝亦不言为我现神足者，当修梵行。云何？善宿！如汝意者，谓如来能现神足、为不能现耶？我所说法，彼法能得出要，尽苦际不耶？』善宿白佛言：『如是，世尊！如来能现神足，非为不能，所可说法，能得出要，尽诸苦际，非为不尽。』『是故，善宿！我所说法修梵行者，能现神足，非为不能，出要离苦，非不能离，汝于此法欲何所求？』

Suṣārman said: 'World-Honored One! You cannot teach me in a timely manner. The World-Honored One knows all the secret techniques of my father, but you are stingy and do not teach them to me.' The Buddha said: 'Suṣārman! Did I ever say to you: "If you practice the holy life in my Dharma, I will teach you your father's techniques?" Did you ever say to me: "If you teach me my father's techniques, I will practice the holy life under the Buddha"?' He replied: 'No.' 'Therefore, Suṣārman! I never said that before, and you never said that either. Why do you say that now? How is it, Suṣārman! Do you think the Tathāgata can teach you the secret techniques of your father, or not? Can the Dharma he teaches lead to liberation and the end of suffering, or not?' Suṣārman replied: 'The Tathāgata can teach the secret techniques of my father, not that he cannot. The Dharma he teaches can lead to liberation and the end of suffering, not that it cannot.' The Buddha told Suṣārman: 'If I can teach you the secret techniques of your father, and can also teach the Dharma to lead to liberation and the end of suffering, what else do you seek in my Dharma?' He also told Suṣārman: 'You previously praised the Tathāgata, the true Dharma, and the Saṅgha in the land of Vaiśālī and Vṛji in many ways, just as a person praises a cool pond in eight ways, making people love it: cool, light, soft, clear, sweet, unpolluted, satisfying to drink, and beneficial to the body. You also did the same, praising the Tathāgata, the true Dharma, and the Saṅgha in Vaiśālī and Vṛji, making people believe and enjoy it. Suṣārman! You should know that if you now turn back, the world will say: "The monk Suṣārman had many acquaintances, was close to the World-Honored One, and was also a disciple of the World-Honored One, but he could not practice the holy life to the end, abandon the precepts, and return to the secular life." Brahmin! You should know that I have fully warned you, but you did not follow my teachings and abandoned the precepts to return to the secular life.

「善宿言：『世尊！不能随时教我，我父秘术，世尊尽知，悋不教我。』佛言：『善宿！我颇曾言：汝于我法中修梵行者，教汝父术耶？汝颇复言：教我父术者，当于佛所修梵行耶？』答曰：『不也。』『是故，善宿！我先无此言，汝亦无言，今者何故作此语耶？云何？善宿！汝谓如来能说汝父秘术，为不能说耶？所可说法，能得出要，尽苦际不耶？』善宿报言：『如来能说父之秘术，非为不能；说法出要，能尽苦际，非为不能。』佛告善宿：『若我能说汝父秘术，亦能说法出要离苦，汝于我法中复欲何求？』又告善宿：『汝先于毗舍离跋阇土地，无数方便，称叹如来，称叹正法，称叹众僧。譬如有人八种称叹彼清凉池，使人好乐：一冷，二轻，三柔，四清，五甘，六无垢，七饮无餍，八便身。汝亦如是，于毘舍离跋阇土，称叹如来，称叹正法，称叹众僧，使人信乐。善宿！当知今汝退者，世间当复有言：善宿比丘多有知识，又是世尊所亲，亦是世尊弟子，不能尽形净修梵行，舍戒就俗处，卑陋行。』梵志！当知我时备语，不顺我教，舍戒就俗。

"Brahmin! Once, I was at the Dharma Hall by the Monkey Pond. At that time, there was a Nirgrantha named Karāla who was staying there. He was respected by people, his reputation was widely known, he had many acquaintances, and his material support was complete. At that time, the monk Suṣārman put on his robe, took his alms bowl, entered the city of Vaiśālī to beg for food, and gradually came to where the Nirgrantha was. At that time, Suṣārman asked the Nirgrantha about profound and distant meanings, but he was unable to answer. He then became angry. Suṣārman thought to himself: 'Have I caused trouble for this person? Will there be a long night of suffering and affliction?' Brahmin! You should know that at that time, after begging for food, the monk Suṣārman took his robe and bowl and came to me. He bowed his head at my feet, sat to one side, and Suṣārman did not report this matter to me at that time. I told him: 'Fool! How can you call yourself a śramaṇa of the Śākya?' Suṣārman then replied to me: 'World-Honored One! Why do you call me a fool? Should I not call myself a son of the Śākya?' I told him: 'Fool! You once went to where the Nirgrantha was and asked about profound and distant meanings. He was unable to answer and became angry. At that time, you thought to yourself: 'Have I now caused trouble for this Nirgrantha? Will there be a long night of suffering and affliction?' Did you have this thought or not?"

「梵志！一时，我在猕猴池侧法讲堂上，时，有尼乾子，字伽罗楼，在彼处止，人所宗敬，名称远闻，多有知识，利养备具。时，善宿比丘著衣持钵，入毗舍离城乞食，渐渐转到尼乾子所。尔时，善宿以深远义问尼乾子，彼不能答，便生瞋恚。善宿自念：『我触娆此人，将无长夜有苦恼报耶？』梵志！当知时善宿比丘于乞食后，执持衣钵，来至我所，头面礼足，在一面坐，善宿尔时亦不以此缘告我，我语之曰：『愚人！汝宁可自称为沙门释子耶？』善宿寻报我言：『世尊！何故称我为愚？不应自称为释子耶？』我告之曰：『愚人！汝曾往至尼乾子所问深远义，彼不能报，便生瞋恚。汝时自念：我今触此尼干，将无长夜有苦恼报耶？汝有是念不？』

"Suṣārman said to the Buddha: 'He is an arhat. How could he have such jealousy and anger?' I then replied: 'Fool! How could an arhat have jealousy and anger? It is not my arhat who has jealousy and anger. You now call him an arhat. He has seven ascetic practices that he has held for a long time. What are the seven? First, to go naked for the rest of one's life. Second, to abstain from alcohol and meat, and not eat rice or flour, for the rest of one's life. Third, to not violate the precepts for the rest of one's life. Fourth, to not leave the four stone stupas in Vaiśālī - the Eastern Udyāna Stupa, the Southern Elephant Stupa, the Western Many Sons Stupa, and the Northern Seven Aggregates Stupa - for the rest of one's life. These are the four ascetic practices. He will later violate these seven ascetic practices and die outside the city of Vaiśālī. It is like a wild fox with scabies and leprosy dying in a charnel ground. That Nirgrantha is also like this; he makes his own precepts and later violates them all. He originally vowed to go naked for the rest of his life, but later he puts on clothes. He originally vowed to abstain from alcohol and meat, and not eat rice or flour, for the rest of his life, but later he eats everything. He originally vowed not to violate the precepts, but later he also violates them. He originally said not to go beyond the four stupas - the Eastern Udyāna Stupa, the Southern Elephant Stupa, the Western Many Sons Stupa, and the Northern Seven Aggregates Stupa - but now he completely avoids them and does not associate with them. After he violates these seven vows, he will leave the city of Vaiśālī and die in a charnel ground.' The Buddha told Suṣārman: 'Fool! You do not believe my words. You should go and see for yourself. You will know for yourself."

「善宿白佛言：『彼是罗汉，何缘乃有此嫉恚心？』我时答曰：『愚人！罗汉何缘有嫉恚心？非我罗汉有嫉恚心，汝今自谓彼是罗汉，彼有七苦行，长夜执持。何谓七？一尽形寿不著衣裳。二尽形寿不饮酒食肉，而不食饭及与𪎊面。三尽形寿不犯梵行。四尽形寿毗舍离有四石塔——东名忧园塔、南名象塔、西名多子塔、北名七聚塔；尽形不离四塔，为四苦行，而彼后当犯此七苦行已，于毗舍离城外命终。譬如野干疥癞衰病，死丘冢间，彼尼乾子亦复如是；自为禁法，后尽犯之。本自誓言：尽形不著衣服，后还著衣。本自誓言：尽形寿不饮酒噉肉，不食饭及𪎊面，而后尽食。本自誓言：不犯梵行，而后亦犯。本言：不越四塔——东忧园塔、南象塔、西多子塔、北七聚塔；今尽远离不复亲近。彼人自违此七誓已，出毘舍离城，冢间命终。』佛告善宿曰：『愚人！汝不信我言，汝自往观，自当知耳。』」

"The Buddha told the brahmin: "Once, the monk Suṣārman put on his robe, took his alms bowl, entered the city to beg for food, and after begging for food, returned out of the city. In an empty charnel ground, he saw the Nirgrantha who had died there. Having seen this, he came to where I was, bowed his head at my feet, sat to one side, and did not tell me about this matter. Brahmin! You should know that at that time, I told Suṣārman: 'What do you think, Suṣārman! Did I not predict the Nirgrantha as I spoke?' He replied: 'Yes, it is as the World-Honored One said.' Brahmin! You should know that I and Suṣārman manifested supernatural powers to prove it, but he said: 'The World-Honored One did not manifest for me.

佛告梵志：「一时，比丘善宿著衣持钵，入城乞食，乞食已，还出城，于空冢间见尼乾子于彼命终。见已，来至我所，头面礼足，在一面坐，不以此事而语我言。梵志！当知我尔时语善宿曰：『云何？善宿！我先所记尼乾子如我语不？』对曰：『如是，如世尊言。』梵志！当知我与善宿现神通证，而彼言：『世尊不为我现。』

Again, once, I was in the country of Madhyadeśa, in the town of Śvetavyā. At that time, there was a Nirgrantha named Kauṇḍinya, who lived in Śvetavyā, was respected by the people, and had a far-reaching reputation, receiving much support. At that time, I put on my robe, took my alms bowl, entered the city to beg for food. At that time, the monk Suṣārman followed behind me. He saw Kauṇḍinya the Nirgrantha lying on a dung heap, licking bran and dregs. Brahmin! You should know that at that time, the monk Suṣārman saw Kauṇḍinya the Nirgrantha lying on a dung heap, licking bran and dregs, and thought: 'In the world, there are arhats and those who are on the path to arahantship. None of them can match this. This Nirgrantha's path is the most supreme. Why is that? This person practices asceticism and can do this. He abandons arrogance and lies on a dung heap, licking bran and dregs.'

「又一时，我在冥宁国白土之邑，时有尼乾子，名究罗帝，在白土住，人所宗敬，名称远闻，多得利养。时，我著衣持钵，入城乞食，时善宿比丘随我后行，见究罗帝尼乾子在粪堆上伏舐糠糟。梵志！当知时善宿比丘见此尼乾子在粪堆上伏舐糠糟已，作是念言：『世间诸有阿罗汉、向阿罗汉道者无有及此，此尼乾子其道最胜，所以者何？此人苦行乃能如是，除舍憍慢，于粪堆上伏舐糠糟。』

Brahmin! At that time, I turned to the right and told Suṣārman: 'You foolish person! How can you call yourself a son of the Śākya?' Suṣārman said to the Buddha: 'World-Honored One! Why do you call me foolish? How can I not call myself a son of the Śākya?' The Buddha told Suṣārman: 'You foolish person! You saw Kauṇḍinya squatting on a dung heap, eating bran and dregs. Having seen this, you thought: "Among all the arhats and those on the path to arahantship in the world, Kauṇḍinya is the most supreme. Why is that? Now Kauṇḍinya can practice asceticism, abandon arrogance, and squat on a dung heap, licking bran and dregs." Did you have this thought?' He replied: 'Indeed!' Suṣārman said again: 'Why, World-Honored One! Do you give rise to jealousy towards the arhats?' The Buddha told the foolish person: 'I do not give rise to jealousy towards the arhats. Why would I give rise to jealousy towards the arhats? You foolish person! You think Kauṇḍinya is a true arhat. This person will die in seven days from a distended belly and be reborn as a hungry ghost, always suffering from hunger. After his death, he will be dragged to a charnel ground with a reed rope. If you do not believe it, you can go and tell him first.'

「梵志！时，我右旋告善宿曰：『汝意愚人！宁可自称为释子耶？』善宿白佛言：『世尊！何故称我为愚？不应自称为释子耶？』佛告善宿言：『汝愚人！观此究罗帝蹲粪堆上伏食糠糟，汝见已，作是念：诸世间阿罗汉及向罗汉者，此究罗帝最为尊上，所以者何？今此究罗帝乃能苦行，除舍憍慢，蹲粪堆上伏舐糠糟。汝有是念不？』答我言：『实尔。』善宿又言：『何故？世尊！于阿罗汉所生嫉妬心。』佛告愚人：『我不于罗汉所生嫉妬] cf.【丽】心，何为于罗汉所生嫉妬] cf.【丽】心？汝今愚人！谓究罗帝真阿罗汉，此人却后七日当腹胀命终，生起尸饿鬼中，常苦饥饿，其命终后，以苇索系抴于冢间。汝若不信者，可先往语之。』

At that time, Suṣārman went to Kauṇḍinya and said: 'The śramaṇa Gautama prophesied that you will die in seven days from a distended belly, be reborn as a hungry ghost, and after death, be dragged to a charnel ground with a reed rope.' Suṣārman further said: 'You should reduce your food intake, so that his words will not come true.' Brahmin! You should know that at that time, Kauṇḍinya died from a distended belly after seven days, was reborn as a hungry ghost, and after death, was dragged to a charnel ground with a reed rope. At that time, Suṣārman, having heard the Buddha's words, counted the days with his fingers. When the seventh day arrived, Suṣārman went to the village of the naked ascetics and asked the villagers: 'Good people! Where is Kauṇḍinya now?' They replied: 'He has passed away.' He asked: 'What illness caused his death?' They replied: 'A distended belly.' He asked: 'How was he buried?' They replied: 'He was dragged to a charnel ground with a reed rope.'

「时，善宿即往诣究罗帝所，说言：『彼沙门瞿昙记汝：却后七日当腹胀命终，生起尸饿鬼中，死已以苇索系抴于冢间。』善宿复白：『汝当省食，勿使彼言当也。』梵志！当知时究罗帝至满七日腹胀而死，即生起尸饿鬼中，死尸以苇索系抴于冢间。尔时，善宿闻佛语已，屈指计日，至七日已，时，善宿比丘即往至裸形村中，到已，问其村人曰：『诸贤！究罗帝今何所在？』报曰：『已取命终。』问曰：『何患命终耶？』答曰：『腹胀。』问曰：『云何殡送？』答曰：『以苇索系抴于冢间。』

Brahmin! At that time, Suṣārman, having heard these words, went to the charnel ground. Before he arrived, the corpse suddenly moved its knees and feet, and squatted. Suṣārman then went to the corpse and said: 'Kauṇḍinya! Have you passed away?' The corpse replied: 'I have passed away.' He asked: 'What illness caused your death?' The corpse replied: 'The Gautama prophesied that I would die from a distended belly after seven days. As he said, I died from a distended belly after seven days.' Suṣārman further asked: 'Where were you reborn?' The corpse immediately replied: 'The Gautama prophesied that I would be reborn as a hungry ghost. Today, I was reborn as a hungry ghost.' Suṣārman asked: 'How were you buried after your death?' The corpse replied: 'The Gautama prophesied that I would be dragged to a charnel ground with a reed rope. It is indeed as he said, I was dragged to a charnel ground with a reed rope.' At that time, the corpse said to Suṣārman: 'Although you have renounced the householder life, you have not attained any good benefits. The śramaṇa Gautama spoke of such matters, but you always do not believe it.' Having said this, the corpse lay down again.

The śramaṇa Gautama claims to have supernatural powers, and so do I. The śramaṇa Gautama has attained the path of transcendence, and so have I. I shall compete with him in displaying supernatural powers. If the śramaṇa displays one, I shall display two. If the śramaṇa displays two, I shall display four. If the śramaṇa displays eight, I shall display sixteen. If the śramaṇa displays sixteen, I shall display thirty-two. If the śramaṇa displays thirty-two, I shall display sixty-four. 佛告梵志：「我于一时在猕猴池法讲堂上，时有梵志，名曰波梨子，在彼处止，人所宗敬，名称远闻，多有利养，于毘舍离大众之中，作如是说：『沙门瞿昙自称智慧，我亦智慧；沙门瞿昙自称神足，我亦有神足；沙门瞿昙得超越道，我亦得超越道。我当与彼共现神足，沙门现一，我当现二；沙门现二，我当现四；沙门现八，我现十六；沙门现十六，我现三十二；沙门现三十二，我现六十四。随彼沙门所现多少，我尽当倍。』

Up to whatever the śramaṇa displays, I shall double it.' At that time, the bhikṣu Suṣārman, having begged for food, came to me, bowed his head at my feet, sat to one side, and said to me: 'This morning, I put on my robe, took my alms bowl, entered the city to beg for food, and heard Pārileyya of Vaiśālī say in the great assembly: "The śramaṇa Gautama has great wisdom, and so do I. The śramaṇa Gautama has supernatural powers, and so do I. If Gautama displays one, I shall display two. Up to whatever Gautama displays, I shall double it." He came to tell me this matter in detail. I told Suṣārman: 'It is impossible for Pārileyya to give up this statement, this view, and this arrogance, and come to me. If he thinks: "I will not give up this statement, this view, and this arrogance, and go to the śramaṇa Gautama," his head will immediately break into seven pieces. 「梵志！时，善宿比丘著衣持钵，入城乞食，见波梨梵志于大众中作如是说：『沙门瞿昙自称智慧，我亦智慧；沙门瞿昙自称神足，我亦有神足；沙门瞿昙得超越道，我亦得超越道。我当与彼共现神足，沙门现一，我当现二；沙门现四，我当现八；乃至随沙门所现多少，我尽能倍。』时，善宿比丘乞食已，来至我所，头面礼，一面坐，语我言：『我于晨朝著衣持钵，入城乞食，时，闻毘舍离波梨子于大众中作是说言：沙门瞿昙有大智慧，我亦有大智慧；沙门瞿昙有神足，我亦有神足；瞿昙现一，我当现二；乃至随瞿昙所现多少，我尽能倍。』具以此事而来告我，我语善宿言：『彼波梨子于大众中不舍此语，不舍此见，不舍此慢，来至我所者，终无是处。若彼作是念：我不舍此语，不舍此见，不舍此慢，而至沙门瞿昙所者，彼头即当破为七分。欲使彼人不舍此语，不舍见慢，而能来者，无有是处。』

"Suṣārman said: 'The World-Honored One protects his speech, the Tathāgata protects his speech.' The Buddha asked Suṣārman: 'Why do you say: "The World-Honored One protects his speech, the Tathāgata protects his speech"?" Suṣārman said: 'Pārileyya has great spiritual power and great virtue. If he comes, will it not be a falsehood for the World-Honored One?' The Buddha told Suṣārman: 'Does the Tathāgata's words have two meanings?' He replied: 'No.' The Buddha again told Suṣārman: 'If there are no two meanings, why do you say: "The World-Honored One protects his speech, the Tathāgata protects his speech"?" Suṣārman said to the Buddha: 'The World-Honored One knows and sees Pārileyya by himself, or the gods have come to tell you.' The Buddha said: 'I know by myself, and also because the gods have come to tell me. I know that this great general of Vaiśālī, upon the disintegration of the body and the end of life, was reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. He came to tell me: "The brahmin Pārileyya does not know shame and has violated the precepts and lied, saying in the great assembly of Vaiśālī: "The great general Āyu, upon the disintegration of the body and the end of life, was reborn as a zombie." But I was actually reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three." I knew this by myself, and also because the gods came to tell me.' The Buddha told the foolish Suṣārman: 'If you do not believe my words, enter Vaiśālī and proclaim it as you wish. I will go to the brahmin Pārileyya's place after the meal."
