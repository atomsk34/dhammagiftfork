DĀ 16

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

16 （一六）善生经

Parallel to: DN 31 / MĀ 135

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying on Vulture Peak in Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在罗阅祇耆阇崛山中，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, the World-Honored One, at the proper time, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered the city to beg for food. At that time, there was a householder's son in the city of Rājagṛha named Sudatta. In the morning, he went out of the city to visit a garden. After bathing, his whole body was wet. He paid homage to the directions, to the east, west, south, north, above, and below, in all directions.

尔时，世尊时到著衣持钵，入城乞食。时，罗阅祇城内有长者子，名曰善生，清旦出城，诣园游观，初沐浴讫，举身皆湿，向诸方礼，东、西、南、北、上、下诸方，皆悉周遍。

At that time, the World-Honored One saw the householder's son Sudatta going to the garden to visit. After bathing, his whole body was wet. He paid homage to the directions. Having seen this, the World-Honored One went to him and said to Sudatta, "For what reason are you going out of the city in the morning, visiting the garden, with your whole body wet, paying homage to the directions?"

尔时，世尊见长者子善生诣园游观，初沐浴讫，举身皆湿，向诸方礼。世尊见已，即诣其所，告善生言：「汝以何缘，清旦出城，于园林中，举身皆湿，向诸方礼？」

At that time, Sudatta said to the Buddha, "When my father was dying, he instructed me, 'If you wish to pay homage, you should first pay homage to the east, south, west, north, above, and below.' I respectfully accepted my father's instruction and did not dare disobey. Therefore, after bathing, I first joined my palms facing east and paid homage to the east, and then to the south, west, north, above, and below in all directions."

尔时，善生白佛言：「我父临命终时，遗勅我言：『汝欲礼者，当先礼东方、南方、西方、北方、上方、下方。』我奉承父教不敢违背，故澡浴讫，先叉手东面，向东方礼；南、西、北方，上、下诸方，皆悉周遍。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Sudatta, "Householder's son! There are names for these directions, but they do not exist. However, in our noble Dharma, we do not pay homage to these six directions as a form of respect."

尔时，世尊告善生曰：「长者子！有此方名耳，非为不有；然我贤圣法中，非礼此六方以为恭敬。」

Sudatta said to the Buddha, "I only wish that the World-Honored One would kindly explain the method of paying homage to the six directions according to the noble Dharma!"

善生白佛言：「唯愿世尊善为我说贤圣法中礼六方法！」

The Buddha said to the householder's son, "Listen attentively! Listen attentively! Consider it well, and I shall explain it to you."

佛告长者子：「谛听！谛听！善思念之，当为汝说。」

Sudatta replied, "Yes, I wish to hear it with great joy!"

善生对曰：「唯然，愿乐欲闻！」

The Buddha said to Sudatta, "If a householder or householder's son knows the four binding actions and does not commit evil deeds in the four places, and also knows the six ways of losing wealth, this is called, Sudatta, a householder or householder's son who avoids the four evil deeds and pays respect to the six directions. In this life, it is also good, and in the future, one will obtain good rewards. In this life, it is the foundation, and in the next life, it is the foundation. In this present life, the wise praise it, and one attains the first fruit of the world. When the body perishes and life ends, one is reborn in heaven and a good place. Sudatta, you should know the four binding actions: first, killing living beings; second, stealing; third, sexual misconduct; fourth, false speech. These are the four binding actions. What are the four places? First, desire; second, anger; third, fear; fourth, delusion. If a householder or householder's son commits evil deeds in these four places, there will be loss." Having said this, the Buddha further spoke a verse:

佛告善生：「若长者、长者子知四结业不于四处而作恶行，又复能知六损财业，是谓，善生！长者、长者子离四恶行，礼敬六方。今世亦善，后获善报，今世根基，后世根基，于现法中，智者所称，获世一果，身坏命终，生天、善处。善生！当知四结行者：一者杀生，二者盗窃，三者婬逸，四者妄语，是四结行。云何为四处？一者欲，二者恚，三者怖，四者痴。若长者、长者子于此四处而作恶者，则有损耗。」佛说是已，复作颂曰：

Desire, anger, fear, and delusion - those who have these four things
Their fame diminishes day by day, like the moon approaching the dark fortnight.

「欲瞋及怖痴，　　有此四法者；名誉日损减，　　如月向于晦。」

The Buddha said to Sudatta, "If a householder or householder's son does not commit evil deeds in these four places, there will be increase." At that time, the World-Honored One further spoke a verse:

佛告善生：「若长者、长者子于此四处不为恶者，则有增益。」尔时，世尊重作颂曰：

Those who do not commit evil deeds in desire, anger, fear, and
delusion; Their fame increases day by day, like the moon waxing full.

「于欲恚怖痴，　　不为恶行者，名誉日增广，　　如月向上满。」

The Buddha told Sudatta, "There are six ways to lose wealth: 1. Indulging in alcohol, 2. Gambling, 3. Indulging in pleasures, 4. Being fascinated by music, 5. Associating with bad friends, 6. Laziness and idleness. These are the six ways to lose wealth. Sudatta! If a householder or householder's son understands the four ways to bind and not commit evil deeds in the four places, and also knows the six ways to lose wealth, this is called, Sudatta! being free from the four places, making offerings to the six directions, good in the present and future, the foundation of the present and future, praised by the wise in this life, attaining the first fruit of the world, and upon the destruction of the body and the end of life, being reborn in heaven and a good place. Sudatta! You should know that drinking alcohol has six faults: 1. Losing wealth, 2. Becoming ill, 3. Fighting, 4. Bad reputation spreading, 5. Anger and violence arising, 6. Wisdom declining day by day. Sudatta! If a householder or householder's son drinks alcohol excessively, their family wealth will decline day by day. Sudatta! Gambling has six faults. What are the six? 1. Wealth declining day by day, 2. Even if winning, creating enmity, 3. Being criticized by the wise, 4. Not being respected by others, 5. Being shunned by others, 6. Giving rise to the mind of theft. These are the six faults of gambling. If a householder or householder's son gambles excessively, their family wealth will decline day by day. Indulging in pleasures has six faults: 1. Not protecting oneself, 2. Not protecting wealth, 3. Not protecting descendants, 4. Always being fearful, 5. Suffering and evil dharmas always entangling the body, 6. Enjoying falsehood. These are the six faults of indulging in pleasures. If a householder or householder's son indulges in pleasures excessively, their family wealth will decline day by day.

佛告善生：「六损财业者：一者耽湎于酒，二者博戯，三者放荡，四者迷于伎乐，五者恶友相得，六者懈堕，是为六损财业。善生！若长者、长者子解知四结行，不于四处而为恶行，复知六损财业，是为，善生！于四处得离，供养六方，今善后善，今世根基，后世根基，于现法中，智者所誉，获世一果，身坏命终，生天、善处。善生！当知饮酒有六失：一者失财，二者生病，三者鬪诤，四者恶名流布，五者恚怒暴生，六者智慧日损。善生！若彼长者、长者子饮酒不已，其家产业日日损减。善生！博戯有六失，云何为六？一者财产日耗，二者虽胜生怨，三者智者所责，四者人不敬信，五者为人疎外，六者生盗窃心。善生！是为博戯六失。若长者、长者子博戯不已，其家产业日日损减，放荡有六失：一者不自护身，二者不护财货，三者不护子孙，四者常自惊惧，五者诸苦恶法常自缠身，六者喜生虚妄，是为放荡六失。若长者、长者子放荡不已，其家财产日日损减。

"Sudatta! Being fascinated by music also has six faults: 1. Seeking singing, 2. Seeking dancing, 3. Seeking stringed instruments, 4. Vādyas, 5. Pāṇavas, 6. Sāman. These are the six faults of music. If a householder or householder's son is fascinated by music excessively, their family wealth will decline day by day. Associating with bad friends also has six faults: 1. Devising deception, 2. Enjoying private places, 3. Enticing others' family members, 4. Plotting others' possessions, 5. Self-directed financial gain, 6. Enjoying exposing others' faults. These are the six faults of bad friends. If a householder or householder's son associates with bad friends excessively, their family wealth will decline day by day. Laziness also has six faults: 1. Not willing to work when wealthy and comfortable, 2. Not willing to diligently cultivate when poor, 3. Not willing to diligently cultivate when cold, 4. Not willing to diligently cultivate when hot, 5. Not willing to diligently cultivate when early, 6. Not willing to diligently cultivate when late. These are the six faults of laziness. If a householder or householder's son is lazy excessively, their family wealth will decline day by day." Having spoken this, the Buddha further spoke a verse:

「善生！迷于伎乐复有六失：一者求歌，二者求舞，三者求琴瑟，四者波内早，五者多罗槃，六者首呵那，是为伎乐六失。若长者、长者子伎乐不已，其家财产日日损减，恶友相得复有六失：一者方便生欺，二者好喜屏处，三者诱他家人，四者图谋他物，五者财利自向，六者好发他过，是为恶友六失。若长者、长者子习恶友不已，其家财产日日损减，懈堕有六失：一者富乐不肯作务，二者贫穷不肯勤修，三者寒时不肯勤修，四者热时不肯勤修，五者时早不肯勤修，六者时晚不肯勤修，是为懈堕六失。若长者、长者子懈堕不已，其家财业日日损减。」佛说是已，复作颂曰：

Those who are deluded by alcohol also have companions who drink. Wealth
is gathered, but it is also scattered according to oneself. Those who
drink without moderation always enjoy singing, dancing, and playing. During the day
they visit others' homes and thus fall into ruin. Following bad friends
without changing, they slander monastics. Their wrong views are ridiculed by the
world and their conduct is despised by people. They are attached to
external appearances and only discuss matters of victory and defeat. Their close
relationships are not reciprocated and their conduct is despised by people. Deluded
by alcohol, they are poor and do not know their limits. They
lightly spend money and enjoy extravagance, destroying their families and causing calamities
Gambling and drinking together, they spy on others' prostitutes. Indulging in base
conduct, they are like the waning moon. Practicing evil and receiving evil
they associate with bad friends. In this world and the next, they
will ultimately gain nothing. During the day, they like to sleep and
at night they are full of longing. Alone and ignorant, they have
no good friends and cannot cultivate family affairs. They are unwilling to
work in the morning and evening, and are also lazy in the
cold and hot seasons. Their undertakings are not completed and their accomplishments
are also destroyed. If one does not consider the cold and heat
and diligently cultivates in the morning and evening, one's undertakings will be
accomplished and one will have no worries until the end.

「迷惑于酒者，　　还有酒伴党；财产正集聚，　　随己复散尽。饮酒无节度，　　常喜歌舞戯；昼则] cf.【丽】游他家，　　因此自陷坠] cf.【丽】。随恶友不改，　　诽谤出家人；邪见世所嗤，　　行秽人所黜。好恶著外色，　　但论胜负事；亲要无返复，　　行秽人所黜。为酒所荒迷，　　贫穷不自量；轻财好奢用，　　破家致祸患。掷博群饮酒，　　共伺他婬女；玩习卑鄙行，　　如月向于晦。行恶能受恶，　　与恶友同事；今世及后世，　　终始无所获。昼则好睡眠，　　夜觉多悕望；独昏无善友，　　不能修家务。朝夕不肯作，　　寒暑复懈堕；所为事不究，　　亦复毁成功。若不计寒暑，　　朝夕勤修务；事业无不成，　　至终无忧患。」

The Buddha told Sudatta, "There are four kinds of enemies who appear as friends. You should be aware of them. What are the four? First, those who are fearful and submissive; second, those who speak sweetly; third, those who are respectful and obedient; fourth, bad friends."

佛告善生：「有四怨如亲，汝当觉知。何谓为四？一者畏伏，二者美言，三者敬顺，四者恶友。」

The Buddha told Sudatta, "There are four things about those who are fearful and submissive. What are the four? First, giving first and then taking away; second, giving little and expecting much; third, being close out of fear; fourth, being close for the sake of profit. These are the four things about those who are fearful and submissive."

佛告善生：「畏伏有四事，云何为四？一者先与后夺，二者与少望多，三者畏故强亲，四者为利故亲，是为畏伏四事。」

The Buddha told Sudatta, "There are four things about those who are close through sweet words. What are the four? First, following good and bad; second, abandoning when there is difficulty; third, secretly stopping when there is good coming from outside; fourth, immediately rescuing when seeing danger. These are the four things about those who are close through sweet words. There are four things about those who are close through respect and obedience. What are the four? First, deceiving first; second, deceiving later; third, deceiving in the present; fourth, adding punishment when seeing minor faults. These are the four things about those who are close through respect and obedience. There are four things about those who are close through bad friends. What are the four? First, being friends when drinking; second, being friends when gambling; third, being friends when indulging in sex; fourth, being friends when singing and dancing. These are the four things about those who are close through bad friends." After the World-Honored One spoke this, he further spoke a verse:

佛告善生：「美言亲复有四事，云何为四？一者善恶斯顺，二者有难舍离，三者外有善来密止之，四者见有危事便排济之，是为美言亲四事。敬顺亲复有四事，云何为四？一者先诳，二者后诳，三者现诳，四者见有小过便加杖之，是为敬顺亲四事。恶友亲复有四事，云何为四？一者饮酒时为友，二者博戯时为友，三者婬逸时为友，四者歌舞时为友，是为恶友亲四事。」世尊说此已，复作颂曰：

Those who are fearful and submissive and yet strong in friendship, those who are close through
sweet words are also like this; those who are close through respect and obedience are deceitful
and those who are close through bad friends do evil. These friendships cannot be relied upon
the wise should be aware of them; they should quickly avoid them, as if avoiding a dangerous path.

「畏伏而强亲，　　美言亲亦尔；敬顺虚诳亲，　　恶友为恶亲。此亲不可恃，　　智者当觉知；宜速远离之，　　如避于崄道。」

The Buddha told Sudatta, "There are four kinds of close friends who are beneficial and protective. What are the four? First, stopping wrongdoing; second, compassion; third, benefiting others; fourth, working together. These are the four kinds of close friends who are beneficial and protective. One should be close to them. Sudatta! There are four things about stopping wrongdoing that are beneficial and protective. What are the four? First, seeing others doing wrong and being able to stop them; second, showing others what is upright; third, having a compassionate mind; fourth, showing the path to humans and gods. These are the four things about stopping wrongdoing that are beneficial and protective.

佛告善生：「有四亲可亲，多所饶益，为人救护。云何为四？一者止非，二者慈愍，三者利人，四者同事，是为四亲可亲，多所饶益，为人救护，当亲近之。善生！彼止非有四事，多所饶益，为人救护。云何为四？一者见人为恶则能遮止，二者示人正直，三者慈心愍念，四者示人天路。是为四止非，多所饶益，为人救护。

"Furthermore, there are four things about compassion: first, seeing benefit and rejoicing on behalf of others; second, seeing suffering and being sorrowful on behalf of others; third, praising others' virtues; fourth, seeing others speak evil and being able to restrain them. These are the four things about compassion that are beneficial and protective. There are four things about benefiting others. What are the four? First, protecting them from carelessness; second, protecting them from losing wealth due to carelessness; third, protecting them from fear; fourth, privately instructing and admonishing them. These are the four things about benefiting others that are beneficial and protective. There are four things about working together. What are the four? First, not sparing one's life and body for them; second, not sparing one's wealth and treasures for them; third, saving them from fear; fourth, privately instructing and admonishing them. These are the four things about working together that are beneficial and protective." Having spoken thus, the World-Honored One further spoke a verse:

「复次，慈愍有四事：一者见利代喜，二者见恶代忧，三者称誉人德，四者见人说恶便能抑制，是为四慈愍，多所饶益，为人救护。利益有四，云何为四？一者护彼不令放逸，二者护彼放逸失财，三者护彼使不恐怖，四者屏相教诫，是为四利人，多所饶益，为人救护。同事有四，云何为四？一者为彼不惜身命，二者为彼不惜财宝，三者为彼济其恐怖，四者为彼屏相教诫，是为四同事，多所饶益，为人救护。」世尊说是已，复作颂曰：

Controlling non-virtue and preventing evil is a close friend, Compassion and kindness are a close friend
to others, Benefiting others is a close friend to benefit them, Working together is a close
friend to be equal to oneself. These are the close friends that can be close, Approached
by the wise, Among close friends, there is no equal close friend, Like a mother's love
for her child. If one wishes to be close to the close, One should be close
to the firm close friend, The close friend who is fully virtuous, Like the light of
fire illuminating people.

「制非防恶亲，　　慈愍在他亲；利人益彼亲，　　同事齐己亲。此亲乃可亲，　　智者所附近；亲中无等亲，　　如慈母亲子。若欲亲可亲，　　当亲坚固亲；亲者戒具足，　　如火光照人。」

The Buddha told Subhadra: "You should know the six directions. What are the six directions? Parents are the east, teachers and elders are the south, wife and children are the west, relatives are the north, servants are the nadir, and śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and those who practice the Way are the zenith. Subhadra! A son should respect and obey his parents with five things. What are the five? First, providing for them so they are not lacking; second, informing them of everything he does; third, being obedient and not disobeying his parents; fourth, not daring to go against his parents' just orders; fifth, not cutting off the right practices of his parents. Subhadra! A son should respect and obey his parents with these five things. Parents also respect their children with five things. What are the five? First, restraining their children from doing evil; second, pointing out good places for them; third, loving them with bone-penetrating marrow; fourth, seeking good marriages for their children; fifth, providing for their children's needs in a timely manner. Subhadra! If a son respects and obeys his parents, then that direction will be peaceful and free from worry.

佛告善生：「当知六方，云何为六方？父母为东方，师长为南方，妻妇为西方，亲党为北方，僮仆为下方，沙门、婆罗门、诸高行者为上方。善生！夫为人子，当以五事敬顺父母，云何为五？一者供奉能使无乏，二者凡有所为先白父母，三者父母所为恭顺不逆，四者父母正令不敢违背，五者不断父母所为正业。善生！夫为人子，当以此五事敬顺父母，父母复以五事敬亲其子。云何为五？一者制子不听为恶，二者指授示其善处，三者慈爱入骨彻髓，四者为子求善婚娶，五者随时供给所须。善生！子于父母敬顺恭奉，则彼方安隐，无有忧畏。

Subhadra! Disciples should respect and serve their teachers and elders with five things. What are the five? First, attending to their needs; second, paying respect and making offerings; third, respecting and admiring them; fourth, respectfully obeying their instructions without going against them; fifth, listening to the Dharma from the teacher and upholding it well without forgetting it. Subhadra! Disciples should serve their teachers and elders with these five things. Teachers and elders also respect their disciples with five things. What are the five? First, guiding them according to the Dharma; second, teaching them what they have not heard; third, causing them to understand the meaning well according to their questions; fourth, showing them good friends; fifth, teaching them all that they know without being stingy. Subhadra! If disciples respect and serve their teachers and elders, then that direction will be peaceful and free from worry.

「善生！弟子敬奉师长复有五事，云何为五？一者给侍所须，二者礼敬供养，三者尊重戴仰，四者师有教勅敬顺无违，五者从师闻法善持不忘。善生！夫为弟子当以此五法敬事师长，师长复以五事敬视弟子，云何为五？一者顺法调御，二者诲其未闻，三者随其所问令善解义，四者示其善友，五者尽以所知诲授不悋。善生！弟子于师长敬顺恭奉，则彼方安隐，无有忧畏。

Subhadra! Husbands should respect their wives with five things. What are the five? First, treating each other with courtesy; second, being majestic and not being disrespectful; third, providing clothing and food in a timely manner; fourth, adorning them in a timely manner; fifth, entrusting them with household affairs. Subhadra! Husbands should treat their wives with these five things. Wives also respect their husbands with five things. What are the five? First, rising before them; second, sitting after them; third, speaking gently; fourth, being respectful and obedient; fifth, anticipating their intentions. Subhadra! This is how husbands should respect their wives. If they do so, then that direction will be peaceful and free from worry.

「善生！夫之敬妻亦有五事，云何为五？一者相待以礼，二者威严不媟，三者衣食随时，四者庄严以时，五者委付家内。善生！夫以此五事敬待于妻，妻复以五事恭敬于夫，云何为五？一者先起，二者后坐，三者和言，四者敬顺，五者先意承旨。善生！是为夫之于妻敬待，如是则彼方安隐，无有忧畏。

Subhadra! When being a person, one should treat one's relatives with five things. What are the five? First, giving; second, kind words; third, benefiting; fourth, sharing benefits; fifth, not deceiving. Subhadra! These are the five things to treat one's relatives with respect. Relatives also treat people with five things. What are the five? First, protecting those who are negligent; second, protecting those who lose wealth due to negligence; third, protecting those who are afraid; fourth, instructing each other in private; fifth, always praising each other. Subhadra! Treating relatives with respect in this way will bring peace and freedom from worry.

「善生！夫为人者，当以五事亲敬亲族，云何为五？一者给施，二者善言，三者利益，四者同利，五者不欺。善生！是为五事亲敬亲族，亲族亦以五事亲敬于人，云何为五？一者护放逸，二者护放逸失财，三者护恐怖者，四者屏相教诫，五者常相称叹。善生！如是敬视亲族，则彼方安隐，无有忧畏。

Subhadra! Masters should instruct their servants with five things. What are the five? First, assigning tasks according to their abilities; second, providing food and drink in a timely manner; third, giving rewards for labor in a timely manner; fourth, providing medicine for the sick; fifth, granting them rest. Subhadra! These are the five things to instruct servants with. Servants also serve their masters with five things. What are the five? First, rising early; second, being thorough in their work; third, not taking what is not given; fourth, doing tasks in order; fifth, praising the master's name. This is how masters treat their servants. It will bring peace and freedom from worry.

「善生！主于僮使以五事教授，云何为五？一者随能使役，二者饮食随时，三者赐劳随时，四者病与医药，五者纵其休假。善生！是为五事教授僮使，僮使复以五事奉事其主，云何为五？一者早起，二者为事周密，三者不与不取，四者作务以次，五者称扬主名。是为主待僮使，则彼方安隐，无有忧畏。

"Subhadra! Lay people should serve śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with five things. What are the five? First, practicing kindness with the body; second, practicing kindness with speech; third, practicing kindness with the mind; fourth, giving at the right time; fifth, not obstructing the door. Subhadra! If lay people serve śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with these five things, śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas should also instruct them with six things. What are the six? First, preventing them from doing evil; second, pointing out good places; third, teaching them to have good thoughts; fourth, causing those who have not heard to hear; fifth, causing those who have heard to understand well; sixth, revealing the path to heaven. Subhadra! If lay people respectfully serve śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in this way, it will bring peace and freedom from worry." After the World-Honored One spoke, he spoke the verse again:

「善生！檀越当以五事供奉沙门、婆罗门，云何为五？一者身行慈，二者口行慈，三者意行慈，四者以时施，五者门不制止。善生！若檀越以此五事供奉沙门、婆罗门，沙门、婆罗门当复以六事而教授之，云何为六？一者防护不令为恶，二者指授善处，三者教怀善心，四者使未闻者闻，五者已闻能使善解，六者开示天路。善生！如是檀越恭奉沙门、婆罗门，则彼方安隐，无有忧畏。」世尊说已，重说偈曰：

Parents are the east, teachers are the south, wife and
children are the west, relatives are the north, servants are
the nadir, śramaṇas are the zenith. All the sons of
elders pay respect to the directions; respectful and timely, they
all attain rebirth in heaven after death. Generosity and kind
words benefit others in many ways, benefiting them equally as
oneself. All possessions are shared with others. These four are
the great burdens, as heavy as cart wheels; without these
four in the world, there is no filial piety. These
principles exist in the world, chosen by the wise; practicing
them brings great results, with fame spreading far and wide
Decorating beds and seats, providing the best food and drink
providing what is due, with fame spreading far and wide
Close friends do not abandon each other, showing them beneficial
matters; superiors and inferiors are always harmonious, gaining good reputation
here. First learn skills, then acquire wealth and occupation; having
wealth and occupation, one should guard it well. Spending wealth
should not be excessive, one should choose the recipient; to
those who deceive and evade, it is better to beg
than to give. Accumulating wealth starts from small beginnings, like
bees gathering from many flowers; wealth and treasures grow daily
never diminishing until the end. One meal is enough, two
cultivating without laziness; three, one should first store up for
emptiness and lack; four, farming, commerce, and raising livestock in
marshy lands; five, building stupas and temples, six, building monastic
quarters. Diligent in these six occupations at home, cultivating well
without missing the time. Practicing like this, the family will
not decline; wealth and treasures grow daily, like the ocean
swallowing all rivers.

「父母为东方，　　师长名南方，妻妇为西方，　　亲族为北方，僮仆为下方，　　沙门为上方。诸有长者子，　　礼敬于诸方；敬顺不失时，　　死皆得生天。惠施及輭言，　　利人多所益，同利等彼己，　　所有与人共。此四多负荷，　　任重如车轮；世间无此四，　　则无有孝养。此法在世间，　　智者所撰择；行则获大果，　　名称远流布。严饰于床座，　　供设上饮食；供给所当得，　　名称远流布。亲旧不相遗，　　示以利益事；上下常和同，　　于此得善誉。先当习伎艺，　　然后获财业；财业既已具，　　宜当自守护。出财未至奢，　　当撰择前人；欺诳觝突者，　　宁乞未举与。积财从小起，　　如蜂集众花；财宝日滋息，　　至终无损耗。一食知止足，　　二修业勿怠，三当先储积，　　以拟于空乏，四耕田商贾，　　泽地而置牧，五当起塔庙，　　六立僧房舍，在家勤六业，　　善修勿失时。如是修业者，　　则家无损减；财宝日滋长，　　如海吞众流。」

At that time, Subhadra said to the World-Honored One, "Excellent! World-Honored One! It truly surpasses my father's teachings, enabling those who are covered to be revealed, those who are closed to be opened, those who are deluded to be enlightened, and lighting a lamp in a dark room so that those with eyes can see. The Tathāgata's teachings are also like this, using countless skillful means to enlighten the ignorant and reveal the pure and white Dharma. Why is this? Because the Buddha is the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, therefore able to reveal and guide the world. Now I take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, and take refuge in the Saṃgha. I only wish that the World-Honored One would allow me to be a lay devotee in the true Dharma. From today onward, for as long as I live, I will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol."

尔时，善生白世尊言：「甚善！世尊！实过本望，逾我父教，能使覆者得仰，闭者得开，迷者得悟，冥室燃灯，有目得视。如来所说，亦复如是，以无数方便，开悟愚冥，现清白法。所以者何？佛为如来、至真、等正觉，故能开示，为世明导。今我归依佛、归依法、归依僧，唯愿世尊听我于正法中为忧婆塞，自今日始，尽形寿不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒。」

At that time, Subhadra, having heard the Buddha's teachings, joyfully accepted and practiced them.

尔时，善生闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
