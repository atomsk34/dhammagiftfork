DĀ 17

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

17 （一七）清净经

Parallel to: DN 29 / AN 9.7 (~) / AN 9.8 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the garden of the lay devotee Mēghiya in the country of Kapilavastu, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在迦维罗卫国缅祇优婆塞林中，与大比丘众] cf.【丽】千二百五十人俱。

At that time, there was a novice monk named Cunda in the country of Pāvā. After the summer retreat, he took his robes and bowl and gradually went to the Mēghiya Garden in Kapilavastu, where he went to Ānanda, bowed his head at Ānanda's feet, stood to one side, and said to Ānanda, "In the city of Pāvā, a Nirgrantha died not long ago. His disciples split into two groups, each quarreling and insulting each other, without any respect for each other. They competed in their knowledge and views, saying, 'I can know this, but you cannot. My practice is true, but yours is wrong. I put the past at the end and the future at the beginning, upside down and confused, without any principle. What I do is wonderful, but what you say is not. If you have any doubts, ask me.' Venerable Ānanda! The people of that country who follow the Nirgranthas, hearing the quarrels, gave rise to feelings of disgust.

时，有沙弥周那在波波国，夏安居已，执持衣钵，渐诣迦维罗卫缅祇园中，至阿难所，头面礼足，于一面立，白阿难言：「波波城内有尼乾子，命终未久，其诸弟子分为二分，各共诤讼，面相毁骂，无复上下，迭相求短，竞其知见：『我能知是，汝不能知；我行真正，汝为邪见。以前著后，以后著前，颠倒错乱，无有法则。我所为妙，汝所言非，汝有所疑，当咨问我。』大德阿难！时，彼国人民事尼干者，闻诤讼已，生厌患心。」

Ānanda said to the novice monk Cunda, "We have something to tell the World-Honored One. Let us go together now and report this matter. If the World-Honored One gives any instructions, we shall all follow them."

阿难语周那沙弥曰：「我等有言欲启世尊，今共汝往，宣启此事，若世尊有所戒勅，当共奉行。」

At that time, the novice monk Cunda, having heard Ānanda's words, immediately went with him to the World-Honored One, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. Then Ānanda said to the World-Honored One, "This novice monk Cunda, having completed the summer retreat in the country of Pāvā, took his robes and bowl and gradually came here. He bowed at my feet and said to me, 'In the country of Pāvā, a Nirgrantha died not long ago. His disciples split into two groups, each quarreling and insulting each other, without any respect for each other. They competed in their knowledge and views, saying, "I can know this, but you cannot. My practice is true, but yours is wrong. I put the past at the end and the future at the beginning, upside down and confused, without any principle. What I say is right, but what you say is wrong. If you have any doubts, ask me." The people of that country who follow the Nirgranthas, hearing the quarrels, gave rise to feelings of disgust.'"

尔时，沙弥周那闻阿难语已，即共诣世尊，头面礼足，在一面立。尔时，阿难白世尊曰：「此沙弥周那在波波国夏安居已，执持衣钵，渐来至此，礼我足，语我言：『波波国有尼乾子，命终未久，其诸弟子分为二分，各共诤讼，面相毁骂，无复上下，迭相求短，竞其知见：「我能知是，汝不能知；我行真正，汝为邪见。以前著后，以后著前，颠倒错乱，无有法则。我所言是，汝所言非，汝有所疑，当咨问我。」时，彼国人民事尼干者，闻诤讼已，生厌患心。』」

The World-Honored One said to the novice monk Cunda, "Indeed, Cunda! What they say is not worth listening to. It is not spoken by the Tathāgata. It is like an old stupa that is difficult to defile. Although they have a teacher, they all have wrong views. Although they have a teaching, it is all not true. It is not worth listening to or adopting. It cannot lead to liberation. It is not spoken by the Tathāgata. It is like an old stupa that cannot be defiled. If any of their disciples do not follow their teachings and abandon their wrong views, practicing right views, Cunda! If someone comes and tells those disciples, 'Virtuous ones! Your teacher's teachings are correct. You should practice them. Why abandon them?' If those disciples believe their words, both will lose the path and incur immeasurable offenses. Why is that? Because their teachings are not true. Cunda! If the teacher does not have wrong views and the teachings are true, it is good to listen to and adopt them. They can lead to liberation. They are spoken by the Tathāgata. It is like a new stupa that is easy to defile. However, if the disciples cannot diligently practice and accomplish this teaching, abandoning the path of equality and entering wrong views, if someone comes and tells those disciples, 'Virtuous ones! Your teacher's teachings are correct. You should practice them. Why abandon them and enter wrong views?' If those disciples believe their words, both will see the truth and gain immeasurable blessings. Why is that? Because their teachings are true.

世尊告周那沙弥曰：「如是。周那！彼非法中不足听闻，此非三耶三佛所说，犹如朽塔难可污色。彼虽有师，尽怀邪见；虽复有法，尽不真正，不足听采，不能出要，非是三耶三佛所说，犹如故塔不可污也。彼诸弟子有不顺其法，舍彼异见，行于正见。周那！若有人来语彼弟子：『诸贤！汝师法正，当于中行，何以舍离？』其彼弟子信其言者，则二俱失道，获无量罪，所以者何？彼虽有法，然不真正故。周那！若师不邪见，其法真正，善可听采，能得出要，三耶三佛所说，譬如新塔易可污色。然诸弟子于此法中，不能勤修，不能成就，舍平等道，入于邪见，若有人来语彼弟子：『诸贤！汝师法正，当于中行，何以舍离，入于邪见？』其彼弟子信其言者，则二俱见真正，获无量福。所以者何？其法真正。」

The Buddha told Cunda, "Although they have a teacher, they have wrong views. Although they have teachings, they are all not true. They are not worth listening to or adopting. They cannot lead to liberation. They are not spoken by the Tathāgata. They are like an old stupa that is difficult to defile. If any of their disciples accomplish the teachings and follow their practice, but give rise to wrong views, Cunda! If someone comes and tells those disciples, 'Your teacher's teachings are correct. What you practice is right. The diligent practice you are doing now should lead to the accomplishment of the path and fruits in this very life.' If those disciples believe their words, both will lose the path and incur immeasurable offenses. Why is that? Because the teachings are not true. Cunda! If the teacher does not have wrong views and the teachings are true, it is good to listen to and adopt them. They can lead to liberation. They are spoken by the Tathāgata. They are like a new stupa that is easy to defile. If the disciples accomplish the teachings and follow the practice, giving rise to right views, if someone comes and tells those disciples, 'Your teacher's teachings are correct. What you practice is right. The diligent practice you are doing now should lead to the accomplishment of the path and fruits in this very life.' If those disciples believe their words, both will see the truth and gain immeasurable blessings. Why is that? Because the teachings are true.

佛告周那：「彼虽有师，然怀邪见，虽复有法，尽不真正，不足听采，不能出要，非三耶三佛所说，犹如朽塔不可污色。彼诸弟子法法成就，随顺其行，起诸邪见。周那！若有人来语其弟子言：『汝师法正，汝所行是，今所修行勤苦如是，应于现法成就道果。』彼诸弟子信受其言者，则二俱失道，获无量罪。所以者何？以法不真正故。周那！若师不邪见，其法真正，善可听采，能得出要，三耶三佛所说，譬如新塔易为污色。又其弟子法法成就，随顺修行而生正见，若有人来语其弟子言：『汝师法正，汝所行是，今所修行勤苦如是，应于现法成就道果。』彼诸弟子信受其言，二俱正见，获无量福。所以者何？法真正故。

"Cunda! There are teachers who appear in the world, causing their disciples to be sorrowful. There are also teachers who appear in the world, causing their disciples to be without sorrow. How do teachers appear in the world, causing their disciples to be sorrowful? Cunda! When a teacher first appears in the world and attains enlightenment, his teachings are complete and his holy life are true and essential, but he does not reveal them. Then that teacher quickly attains parinirvāṇa, and his disciples are unable to practice, all lamenting, 'The teacher first appeared in the world and attained enlightenment, his teachings are pure and his holy life are complete, but he did not reveal the true and essential. Now the teacher has quickly attained parinirvāṇa, and we disciples are unable to practice.' This is how teachers appear in the world, causing their disciples to be sorrowful. How do teachers appear in the world, causing their disciples to be without sorrow? It means that when a teacher appears in the world, his teachings are pure, his holy life are complete, and the true and essential is widely spread, and then the teacher attains parinirvāṇa, and his disciples are all able to practice, without lamenting, 'The teacher first appeared in the world and attained enlightenment, his teachings are pure and his holy life are complete, but he did not reveal the true and essential. Now the teacher has quickly attained parinirvāṇa, and we disciples are unable to practice.' Thus, Cunda! Teachers appear in the world, causing their disciples to be without sorrow."

「周那！或有导师出世，使弟子生忧；或有导师出世，使弟子无忧。云何导师出世，使弟子生忧？周那！导师新出世间，成道未久，其法具足，梵行清净，如实真要而不布现，然彼导师速取灭度，其诸弟子不得修行，皆愁忧言：『师初出世，成道未久，其法清净，梵行具足，如实真要，竟不布现，而今导师便速灭度，我等弟子不得修行。』是为导师出世，弟子愁忧。云何导师出世，弟子不忧？谓导师出世，其法清净，梵行具足，如实真要而广流布，然后导师方取灭度，其诸弟子皆得修行，不怀忧言：『师初出世，成道未久，其法清净，梵行具足，如实真要而不布现，而今导师便速灭度，使我弟子不得修行。』如是，周那！导师出世，弟子无忧。」

The Buddha told Cunda, "This is the accomplishment of the holy life, namely, when a teacher appears in the world, he has just left home and his fame has not spread far. This is called the incomplete accomplishment of the holy life. Cunda! When a teacher appears in the world, he has been out of home for a long time and his fame has spread far. This is called the complete and full accomplishment of the holy life. Cunda! When a teacher appears in the world, he has been out of home for a long time and his fame has spread far, but his disciples have not yet received instruction, have not completed the holy life, have not reached the state of peace and ease, have not attained their own benefits, have not been able to receive the Dharma and explain it in detail, and when different theories arise, they are unable to extinguish them according to the Dharma, and have not yet been able to transform and accomplish the realization of supernatural powers. This is called the incomplete accomplishment of the holy life. Cunda! When a teacher appears in the world, he has been out of home for a long time and his fame has spread far, and his disciples have all received instruction, have completed the holy life, have reached the state of peace and ease, have attained their own benefits, are able to receive the Dharma and explain it in detail, when different theories arise, they are able to extinguish them according to the Dharma, and have transformed and accomplished the realization of supernatural powers. This is called the complete and full accomplishment of the holy life.

佛告周那：「此支成就梵行，谓导师出世，出家未久，名闻未广，是谓梵行支不具足。周那！导师出世，出家既久，名闻广远，是谓梵行支具足满。周那！导师出世，出家既久，名闻亦广，而诸弟子未受训诲，未具梵行，未至安处，未获己利，未能受法分布演说，有异论起不能如法而往灭之，未能变化成神通证，是为梵行支不具足。周那！导师出世，出家既久，名闻亦广，而诸弟子尽受教训，梵行具足，至安隐处，已获己利，又能受法分别演说，有异论起能如法灭，变化具足成神通证，是为梵行支具足满。

Cunda! When a teacher appears in the world, he has been out of home for a long time and his fame has spread far, but the nuns have not yet received instruction, have not reached the state of peace and ease, have not attained their own benefits, have not been able to receive the Dharma and explain it in detail, when different theories arise, they are unable to extinguish them according to the Dharma, and have not yet been able to transform and accomplish the realization of supernatural powers. This is called the incomplete accomplishment of the holy life. Cunda! When a teacher appears in the world, he has been out of home for a long time and his fame has spread far, and the nuns have all received instruction, have completed the holy life, have reached the state of peace and ease, have attained their own benefits, are able to receive the Dharma and explain it in detail, when different theories arise, they are able to extinguish them according to the Dharma, and have transformed and accomplished the realization of supernatural powers. This is called the complete and full accomplishment of the holy life. Cunda! The male and female lay followers who extensively cultivate the holy life, up to the transformation and accomplishment of the realization of supernatural powers, are also like this.

「周那！导师出世，出家亦久，名闻亦广，诸比丘尼未受训诲，未至安处，未获己利，未能受法分布演说，有异论起不能以法如实除灭，未能变化成神通证，是为梵行支未具足。周那！导师出世，出家亦久，名闻亦广，诸比丘尼尽受教训，梵行具足，至安隐处，已获己利，复能受法分别演说，有异论起能如法灭，变化具足成神通证，是为梵行支具足满。周那！诸优婆塞、优婆夷广修梵行，乃至变化具足成神通证，亦复如是。

Cunda! If the teacher is not in the world, there is no fame, and the benefits and offerings are reduced, then the holy life are not fully accomplished. If the teacher is in the world, his fame and benefits and offerings are all fully accomplished, without reduction, then the holy life are fully accomplished. If the teacher is in the world, his fame and benefits and offerings are all fully accomplished, but the monks are unable to fully accomplish their fame and benefits and offerings, then the holy life are not fully accomplished. If the teacher is in the world, his fame and benefits and offerings are fully accomplished without loss, and the assembly of monks is also fully accomplished, then the holy life are fully accomplished. The assembly of nuns is also like this.

「周那！若导师不在世，无有名闻，利养损减，则梵行支不具足满。若导师在世，名闻利养，皆悉具足，无有损减，则梵行支为具足满。若导师在世，名闻利养，皆悉具足，而诸比丘名闻利养，不能具足，是为梵行支不具足。若导师在世，名闻利养，具足无损，诸比丘众亦复具足，则梵行支为具足满，比丘尼众亦复如是。

"Cunda! I have been out of home for a long time, my fame has spread far, my disciples have received instruction, reached the state of peace and ease, attained their own benefits, are able to receive the Dharma and explain it to others, when different theories arise, they are able to extinguish them according to the Dharma, have transformed and accomplished the realization of supernatural powers. The nuns, male and female lay followers are also like this. Cunda! I have widely spread the holy life, up to the transformation and accomplishment of the realization of supernatural powers. Cunda! In all the worlds, I have not seen any teacher who has attained fame and benefits and offerings like the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Cunda! In all the worlds, I have not seen any assembly that has attained fame and benefits and offerings like my assembly. Cunda! If one wishes to speak correctly, one should say that one sees what is invisible. How does one see what is invisible? The complete purity of all holy life is proclaimed and revealed, this is called seeing what is invisible."

「周那！我出家久，名闻广远，我诸比丘已受教诫，到安隐处，自获己利，复能受法为人说法，有异论起能如法灭，变化具足成神通证，诸比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷皆亦如是。周那！我以广流布梵行，——乃至变化具足成神通证。周那！一切世间所有导师，不见有得名闻利养如我如来、至真、等正觉者也。周那！诸世间所有徒众，不见有名闻利养如我众也。周那！若欲正说者，当言见不可见。云何见不可见？一切梵行清净具足，宣示布现，是名见不可见。」

At that time, the World Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Udraka Rāmaputra said in the assembly: 'There is seeing and not seeing. What is meant by seeing and not seeing? Like a knife, the blade is visible but not the edge.' Bhikṣus! That son actually used the words of an ignorant ordinary person as a metaphor. Thus, Cunda! If one wishes to speak correctly, one should say that one sees what is invisible. How does one see what is invisible? You should speak correctly and say: 'The complete purity of all holy life is proclaimed and revealed, this is invisible.' Cunda! The discontinuous dharmas cannot be obtained, while the continuous dharmas can be obtained. Cunda! Among all dharmas, the pure practice is like the cream in milk.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「欝头蓝子在大众中而作是说：『有见不见，云何名见不见？如刀可见，刃不可见。』诸比丘！彼子乃引凡夫无识之言以为譬喻，如是，周那！若欲正说者，当言见不见。云何见不见？汝当正欲说言：『一切梵行清净具足，宣示流布，是不可见。』周那！彼相续法不具足而可得，不相续法具足而不可得。周那！诸法中梵行，酪酥中醍醐。」

At that time, the World Honored One told the bhikṣus: "I have personally realized this Dharma, namely the four foundations of mindfulness, the four bases of supernormal power, the four right efforts, the four dhyānas, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, and the eightfold noble path. You should all be harmonious and united, without disputes. You should all receive the same teachings, be like water and milk. In the Tathāgata's true Dharma, you should all be diligent and quickly attain peace and happiness. Having attained peace and happiness, if there are bhikṣus who speak the Dharma and say: 'The words and meanings spoken by him are incorrect.' Having heard this, the bhikṣus should not say it is so or not so. They should tell those bhikṣus: 'How is it? Venerable ones! My words are like this, your words are like this; my meaning is like this, your meaning is like this. Which is superior? Which is inferior?' If those bhikṣus reply: 'My words are like this, my meaning is like this; your words are like this, your meaning is like this; your words are also superior, your meaning is also superior.' Those bhikṣus speaking this should not be said to be wrong or right. They should admonish those bhikṣus, rebuke them, stop them, and together seek to understand. In this way, all be harmonious and united, without disputes. You should all receive the same teachings, have the same teacher, be like water and milk. In the Tathāgata's true Dharma, you should all be diligent and quickly attain peace and happiness.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我于是法躬自作证，谓四念处、四神足、四意断、四禅、五根、五力、七觉意、贤圣八道，汝等尽共和合，勿生诤讼，同一师受，同一水乳；于如来正法，当自炽然，快得安乐。得安乐已，若有比丘说法中有作是言：『彼所说句不正，义理不正。』比丘闻已，不可言是，不可言非，当语彼比丘言：『云何？诸贤！我句如是，汝句如是；我义如是，汝义如是。何者为胜？何者为负？』若彼比丘报言：『我句如是，我义如是；汝句如是，汝义如是；汝句亦胜，汝义亦胜。』彼比丘说此，亦不得非，亦不得是。当谏彼比丘，当呵当止，当共推求，如是尽共和合，勿生诤讼，同一受，同一师，同一乳；于如来正法，当自炽然，快得安乐。

Having attained peace and happiness, if there are bhikṣus who speak the Dharma and say: 'The words spoken by him are incorrect, but the meaning is correct.' Having heard this, the bhikṣus should not say it is so or not so. They should tell those bhikṣus: 'How is it? Bhikṣu! My words are like this, your words are like this. Which is correct? Which is incorrect?' If those bhikṣus reply: 'My words are like this, your words are like this. Your words are also superior.' Those bhikṣus speaking this should not be said to be correct or incorrect. They should admonish those bhikṣus, rebuke them, stop them, and together seek to understand. In this way, all be harmonious and united, without disputes. You should all receive the same teachings, have the same teacher, be like water and milk. In the Tathāgata's true Dharma, you should all be diligent and quickly attain peace and happiness.

「得安乐已，若有比丘说法，中有比丘作是言：『彼所说句不正，义正。』比丘闻已，不可言是，不可言非，当语彼比丘言：『云何？比丘！我句如是，汝句如是，何者为是？何者为非？』若彼比丘报言：『我句如是，汝句如是，汝句亦胜。』彼比丘说此，亦不得言是，不得言非，当谏彼比丘，当呵当止，当共推求，如是尽共和合，勿生诤讼，同一师受，同一水乳；于如来正法，当自炽然，快得安乐。

Having attained peace and happiness, if there are bhikṣus who speak the Dharma and say: 'The words spoken by him are correct, but the meaning is incorrect.' Having heard this, the bhikṣus should not say it is so or not so. They should tell those bhikṣus: 'How is it? Bhikṣu! My meaning is like this, your meaning is like this. Which is correct? Which is incorrect?' If those bhikṣus reply: 'My meaning is like this, your meaning is like this. Your meaning is also superior.' Those bhikṣus speaking this should not be said to be correct or incorrect. They should admonish those bhikṣus, rebuke them, stop them, and together seek to understand. In this way, all be harmonious and united, without disputes. You should all receive the same teachings, have the same teacher, be like water and milk. In the Tathāgata's true Dharma, you should all be diligent and quickly attain peace and happiness.

「得安乐已，若有比丘说法，中有比丘作是言：『彼所说句正，义不正。』比丘闻已，不可言是，不可言非，当语彼比丘言：『云何？比丘！我义如是，汝义如是，何者为是？何者为非？』若彼报言：『我义如是，汝义如是，汝义亦胜。』彼比丘说此已，亦不得言是，亦不得言非，当谏彼比丘，当呵当止，当共推求，如是比丘尽共和合，勿生诤讼，同一师受，同一水乳，于如来正法，当自炽然，快得安乐。

Having attained peace and happiness, if there are bhikṣus who speak the Dharma and say: 'The words spoken by him are correct, and the meaning is correct.' Having heard this, the bhikṣus should not say it is not so. They should praise those bhikṣus, saying: 'What you say is so, what you say is so.' Therefore, bhikṣus! In the twelve divisions of the sūtras, you should personally attain realization and widely propagate them. They are: the Sūtra of the Thread, the Gāthā Sūtra, the Sūtra of Predictions, the Gāthā Sūtra, the Sūtra of Dharma Phrases, the Sūtra of Correspondences, the Sūtra of Past Causes, the Sūtra of Heavenly Origins, the Extensive Sūtra, the Sūtra of the Unprecedented, the Sūtra of Parables, and the Sūtra of the Great Teaching. You should uphold them well, weigh and contemplate them, and widely expound and distribute them.

「得安乐已，若有比丘说法，中有比丘作如是言：『彼所说句正，义正。』比丘闻已，不得言非，当称赞彼言：『汝所言是，汝所言是。』是故，比丘！于十二部经自身作证，当广流布，一曰《贯经》，二曰《祇夜经》，三曰《受记经》，四曰《偈经》，五曰《法句经》，六曰《相应经》，七曰《本缘经》，八曰《天本经》，九曰《广经》，十曰《未曾有经》，十一曰《譬喻经》，十二曰《大教经》，当善受持，称量观察，广演分布。

"Bhikṣus! The robes I prescribe are either robes from charnel grounds, or robes from householders, or coarse and inferior robes. These robes are sufficient to ward off cold and heat, mosquitoes and flies, and to cover the four limbs. Bhikṣus! The food I prescribe is either alms food, or food from householders. This food is sufficient for oneself. If the body is afflicted with suffering and many afflictions, and it is feared that it will lead to death, then this food is allowed, and one should be content with it. Bhikṣus! The dwelling places I prescribe are either under trees, in the open, in rooms, on towers, in caves, or in various dwelling places. These places are sufficient to ward off cold and heat, wind and rain, mosquitoes and flies, down to quiet places for rest. Bhikṣus! The medicines I prescribe are either feces and urine, or ghee, oil, honey, and molasses. These medicines are sufficient for oneself. If the body is afflicted with suffering and many afflictions, and it is feared that it will lead to death, then these medicines are allowed."

「诸比丘！我所制衣，若冢间衣，若长者衣、麤贱衣；此衣足障寒暑、蚊虻，足蔽四体。诸比丘！我所制食，若乞食，若居士食；此食自足，若身苦恼，众患切已，恐遂至死，故听此食，知足而已。诸比丘！我所制住处，若在树下，若在露地，若在房内，若楼阁上，若在窟内，若在种种住处；此处自足，为障寒暑、风雨、蚊虻，下至闲静懈息之处。诸比丘！我所制药，若大小便，酥油蜜、黑石蜜；此药自足，若身生苦恼，众患切已，恐遂至死，故听此药。」

The Buddha said, "There may be non-Buddhist Brahmins who come and say, 'The śramaṇa Śākyas enjoy various pleasures.' If there are such words, you should reply like this: 'You should not say that the śramaṇa Śākyas enjoy various pleasures. Why is that? There are pleasures that the Tathāgata criticizes, and there are pleasures that the Tathāgata praises.' If the non-Buddhist Brahmins ask, 'What pleasures does Gautama criticize?' If there are such words, you should reply, 'The five sense pleasures are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them. What are the five? The eyes know forms, which are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them; the ears hear sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile sensations, which are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them. Good people! These five sense pleasures give rise to joy and pleasure, and this is what the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One criticizes. It is like a person who intentionally kills sentient beings and takes it as pleasure. This is what the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One criticizes. It is like a person who secretly steals and takes it as pleasure. This is what the Tathāgata criticizes. It is like a person who violates the precepts of purity and takes it as pleasure. This is what the Tathāgata criticizes. It is like a person who intentionally speaks falsehoods and takes it as pleasure. This is what the Tathāgata criticizes. It is like a person who indulges in dissipation and self-indulgence. This is what the Tathāgata criticizes. It is like a person who practices external austerities that are not the right practices taught by the Tathāgata and takes it as pleasure. This is what the Tathāgata criticizes.'

佛言：「或有外道梵志来作是语：『沙门释子以众乐自娱。』若有此言，当如是报：『汝等莫作此言，谓沙门释子以众乐自娱。所以者何？有乐自娱，如来呵责；有乐自娱，如来称誉。』若外道梵志问言：『何乐自娱，瞿昙呵责？』设有此语，汝等当报：『五欲功德，可爱可乐，人所贪著。云何为五？眼知色，可爱可乐，人所贪著；耳闻声、鼻知香、舌知味、身知触，可爱可乐，人所贪著。诸贤！犹是五欲缘生喜乐，此是如来、至真、等正觉之所呵责也。犹如有人故杀众生，自以为乐，此是如来、至真、等正觉之所呵责。犹如有人私窃偷盗，自以为乐，此为如来之所呵责。犹如有人犯于梵行，自以为乐，此是如来之所呵责。犹如有人故作妄语，自以为乐，此是如来之所呵责。犹如有人放荡自恣，此是如来之所呵责。犹如有人行外苦行，非是如来所说正行，自以为乐，此是如来之所呵责。』

Bhikṣus! The five sense pleasures are criticized by the Tathāgata. What are the five? The eyes know forms, which are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them; the ears hear sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile sensations, which are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them. Such pleasures are not the pleasures of the śramaṇa Śākyas. It is like a person who intentionally kills sentient beings and takes it as pleasure. The śramaṇa Śākyas do not have such pleasures. It is like a person who publicly steals and takes it as pleasure. The śramaṇa Śākyas do not have such pleasures. It is like a person who violates the precepts of purity and takes it as pleasure. The śramaṇa Śākyas do not have such pleasures. It is like a person who intentionally speaks falsehoods and takes it as pleasure. The śramaṇa Śākyas do not have such pleasures. It is like a person who indulges in dissipation and self-indulgence and takes it as pleasure. The śramaṇa Śākyas do not have such pleasures. It is like a person who practices external austerities and takes it as pleasure. The śramaṇa Śākyas do not have such pleasures.

「诸比丘！呵责五欲功德，人所贪著。云何为五？眼知色，可爱可乐，人所贪著；耳闻声、鼻知香、舌知味、身知触，可爱可乐，人所贪著；如此诸乐，沙门释子无如此乐。犹如有人故杀众生，以此为乐，沙门释子无如此乐。犹如有人公为盗贼，自以为乐，沙门释子无如是乐。犹如有人犯于梵行，自以为乐，沙门释子无如是乐。犹如有人故作妄语，自以为乐，沙门释子无如是乐。犹如有人放荡自恣，自以为乐，沙门释子无如是乐。犹如有人行外苦行，自以为乐，沙门释子无如是乐。

If the non-Buddhist Brahmins ask, 'What pleasures does Gautama praise?' If there are such words, you should reply, 'Good people! There are five sense pleasures that are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them. What are the five? The eyes know forms, which are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them; the ears hear sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile sensations, which are delightful and enjoyable, and people are attached to them. Good people! The pleasures arising from the five sense pleasures should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who intentionally kills sentient beings and takes it as pleasure. There are such pleasures that should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who publicly steals and takes it as pleasure. There are such pleasures that should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who violates the precepts of purity and takes it as pleasure. There are such pleasures that should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who intentionally speaks falsehoods and takes it as pleasure. There are such pleasures that should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who indulges in dissipation and self-indulgence and takes it as pleasure. There are such pleasures that should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who practices external austerities and takes it as pleasure. There are such pleasures that should be quickly eliminated. It is like a person who abandons desire, has no evil dharmas, has initial and sustained thought, and enters the first dhyāna with joy and happiness arising from separation. Such pleasures are praised by the Buddha. It is like a person who eliminates initial and sustained thought, has internal joy, one-pointedness of mind, no initial and sustained thought, and enters the second dhyāna with joy and happiness arising from concentration. Such pleasures are praised by the Buddha. It is like a person who eliminates joy, enters equanimity, knows bodily happiness, is sought by the noble ones, and enters the third dhyāna with mindfulness and one-pointedness of mind. Such pleasures are praised by the Buddha. It is like a person who eliminates pleasure and pain, and joy and sorrow, and enters the fourth dhyāna with purity of mindfulness and equanimity. Such pleasures are praised by the Buddha.'

「若外道梵志作如是问『何乐自娱，沙门瞿昙之所称誉？』诸比丘！彼若有此言，汝等当答彼言：『诸贤！有五欲功德，可爱可乐，人所贪著。云何为五？眼知色，——乃至身知触，可爱可乐，人所贪著。诸贤！五欲因缘生乐，当速除灭。犹如有人故杀众生，自以为乐；有如此乐，应速除灭。犹如有人公为盗贼，自以为乐；有如此乐，应速除灭。犹如有人犯于梵行，自以为乐；有如此乐，应速除灭。犹如有人故为妄语，自以为乐；有如此乐，应速除灭。犹如有人放荡自恣，自以为乐；有如此乐，应速除灭。犹如有人行外苦行，自以为乐；有如是乐，应速除灭。犹如有人去离贪欲，无复恶法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，入初禅；如是乐者，佛所称誉。犹如有人灭于觉、观，内喜、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，入第二禅；如是乐者，佛所称誉。犹如有人除喜入舍，自知身乐，贤圣所求，护念一心，入第三禅；如是乐者，佛所称誉。乐尽苦尽，忧、喜先灭，不苦不乐，护念清净，入第四禅；如是乐者，佛所称誉。』

If there are non-Buddhist Brahmins who ask, 'How many fruits and merits do you seek in this pleasure?' you should reply, 'This pleasure has seven fruits and merits. What are the seven? In this present life, one attains the realization of the path. Even if one does not attain it, at the time of death, one will attain the realization of the path. If one does not attain it at the time of death, one will exhaust the five lower fetters and attain parinirvāṇa in the intermediate state, in the next life, through practice, without practice, or in the Akaniṣṭha heaven. Good people! These are the seven fruits and merits of this pleasure. Good people! If a bhikṣu on the stage of learning desires to ascend and seek a place of peace, but has not eliminated the five hindrances, what are the five? The hindrances of sensual desire, ill-will, sloth and torpor, restlessness and remorse, and doubt. If that bhikṣu on the stage of learning desires to ascend and seek a place of peace, but has not eliminated the five hindrances, he will not be able to diligently cultivate the four foundations of mindfulness and the seven factors of awakening. If he desires to attain the supramundane dharmas, the increase of noble wisdom, and to know and see, it is impossible. Good people! If a bhikṣu on the stage of learning desires to ascend and seek a place of peace, and is able to eliminate the five hindrances - the hindrances of sensual desire, ill-will, sloth and torpor, restlessness and remorse, and doubt. If he is able to diligently cultivate the four foundations of mindfulness and the seven factors of awakening, and desires to attain the supramundane dharmas, the increase of noble wisdom, and to know and see, it is possible. Good people! If there is a bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints, is an arhat, has accomplished what was to be done, has laid down the heavy burden, has attained his own benefit, has exhausted all bonds and afflictions, is liberated by right knowledge, and is free from nine things. What are the nine? First, not killing, second, not stealing, third, not engaging in sexual misconduct, fourth, not speaking falsehoods, fifth, not abandoning the path, sixth, not following desire, seventh, not following anger, eighth, not following fear, and ninth, not following delusion. Good people! This is called an arhat who has exhausted the taints, has accomplished what was to be done, has laid down the heavy burden, has attained his own benefit, has exhausted all bonds, is liberated by right knowledge, and is free from nine things.'

「若有外道梵志作如是问：『汝等于此乐中求几果功德？』应答彼言：『此乐当有七果功德。云何为七？于现法中，得成道证，正使不成，临命终时，当成道证；若临命终复不成者，当尽五下结，中间般涅槃、生彼般涅槃、行般涅槃、无行般涅槃、上流阿迦尼咤般涅槃。诸贤！是为此乐有七功德。诸贤！若比丘在学地欲上，求安隐处，未除五盖，云何为五？贪欲盖、瞋恚盖、睡眠盖、掉戯盖、疑盖。彼学比丘方欲上求，求安隐处，未灭五盖，于四念处不能精勤，于七觉意不能勤修，欲得上人法、贤圣智慧增盛，求欲知欲见者，无有是处。诸贤！学地比丘欲上求，求安隐处，能灭五盖：贪欲盖、瞋恚盖、睡眠盖、掉戯盖、疑盖。于四意处又能精勤，于七觉意如实修行，欲得上人法、贤圣智慧增上，求欲知欲见者，则有是处。诸贤！若有比丘漏尽阿罗汉，所作已办，舍于重担，自获己利，尽诸有结使，正智解脱，不为九事。云何为九？一者不杀，二者不盗，三者不婬，四者不妄语，五者不舍道，六者不随欲，七者不随恚，八者不随怖，九者不随痴。诸贤！是为漏尽阿罗汉所作已办，舍于重担，自获己利，尽诸有结，正智得解，远离九事。』

If there are non-Buddhist Brahmins who say, 'The Śramaṇa Śākyas have a Dharma of non-abiding,' you should reply, 'Good people! Do not say that the Śramaṇa Śākyas have a Dharma of non-abiding. Why is that? The Dharma of the Śramaṇa Śākyas is always abiding, immovable, like a door frame that is always abiding and immovable. The Śramaṇa Śākyas are also like that, their Dharma is always abiding, without moving.' If there are non-Buddhist Brahmins who say, 'The Śramaṇa Gautama knows all past things, but does not know future things,' 'Those bhikṣus and those non-Buddhist Brahmins have different knowledge and different views. Their words are false. The Tathāgata knows and sees all past things as if they were right before his eyes. He has the knowledge of the path regarding the future. As for past things, if they are false and unreal, not worth rejoicing in, and not beneficial, the Buddha does not speak of them. If past things are real, but not worth rejoicing in, and not beneficial, the Buddha also does not speak of them. If past things are real and worth rejoicing in, but not beneficial, the Buddha also does not speak of them. If past things are real, worth rejoicing in, and beneficial, the Tathāgata knows them all before speaking of them. The same is true for the future and present. The Tathāgata speaks at the right time, speaks the truth, speaks the meaning, speaks the benefit, speaks the Dharma, and speaks the Vinaya, without falsehood. The Buddha attained supreme perfect enlightenment in the first watch of the night, and in the last watch of the night, all that he spoke in between was true. Therefore he is called the Tathāgata. Furthermore, the Tathāgata's words accord with reality, and reality accords with his words. Therefore he is called the Tathāgata. What is the meaning of 'Perfectly Enlightened One'? The Buddha knows, sees, eliminates, and awakens to all that is to be known. The Buddha fully knows and awakens to all. Therefore he is called the Perfectly Enlightened One.'

「或有外道梵志作是说言：『沙门释子有不住法。』应报彼言：『诸贤！莫作是说：沙门释子有不住法。所以者何？沙门释子，其法常住，不可动转，譬如门阃常住不动，沙门释子亦复如是，其法常住，无有移动。』或有外道梵志作是说言：『沙门瞿昙尽知过去世事，不知未来事。』『彼比丘、彼异学梵志智异，智观亦异，所言虚妄。如来于彼过去事，若在目前，无不知见；于未来世，生于道智。过去世事虚妄不实，不足喜乐，无所利益，佛则不记；或过去事有实，无可喜乐，无所利益，佛亦不记；若过去事有实、可乐，而无利益，佛亦不记；若过去事有实、可乐，有所利益，如来尽知然后记之；未来、现在，亦复如是。如来于过去、未来、现在，应时语、实语、义语、利语、法语、律语，无有虚也。佛于初夜成最正觉，及末后夜，于其中间有所言说，尽皆如实，故名如来。复次，如来所说如事，事如所说，故名如来。以何等义，名等正觉？佛所知见、所灭、所觉，佛尽觉知，故名等正觉。』

If there are non-Buddhist Brahmins who say, 'The world is permanent, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world is impermanent, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world is both permanent and impermanent, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world is neither permanent nor impermanent, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world has an end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world has no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world has both an end and no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The world has neither an end nor no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'This life is the body, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'This life is not the body, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'Life is different from the body, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'Life is not different from the body, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The Tathāgata has an end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The Tathāgata has no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The Tathāgata has both an end and no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Or they say, 'The Tathāgata has neither an end nor no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' Those who have these views are called those who have the original views and the original birth. I now declare to you that 'the world is permanent, - up to the Tathāgata has neither an end nor no end, only this is true, the rest is false.' This is the original view and the original birth. I declare it to you.

「或有外道梵志作如是说：『世间常存，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复说言：『此世无常，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复有言：『世间有常无常，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复有言：『此世间非有常非无常，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复有言：『此世间有边，唯此为实，余者为虚妄。』或复有言：『世间无边，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复有言：『世间有边无边，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复有言：『世间非有边非无边，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』或复有言：『是命是身，此实余虚。』或复有言：『非命非身，此实余虚。』或复有言：『命异身异，此实余虚。』或复有言：『非异命非异身，此实余虚。』或复有言：『如来有终，此实余虚。』或复有言：『如来不终，此实余虚。』或复有言：『如来终不终，此实余虚。』或复有言：『如来非终非不终，此实余虚。』诸有此见，名本生本见，今为汝记，谓：『此世常存，——乃至如来非终非不终，唯此为实，余者虚妄，是为本见本生。』为汝记之。

As for what has not been seen and not yet arisen, I also declare it. What has not been seen and not yet arisen that I declare? 'Form is me, ending with thought.' 'Formless is me, ending with thought.' 'Both form and formless are me, ending with thought.' 'Neither form nor formless is me, ending with thought.' 'I have an end, I am endless.' 'I have both an end and no end.' 'I am neither finite nor infinite, ending with thought.' 'I have happiness, ending with thought.' 'I have no happiness, ending with thought.' 'I have both happiness and suffering, ending with thought.' 'I have neither happiness nor suffering, ending with thought.' 'One thought is me, ending with thought.' 'Various thoughts are me, ending with thought.' 'Few thoughts are me, ending with thought.' 'Immeasurable thoughts are me, ending with thought.' This is true, the rest is false. This is the original view and the original birth that I declare.

「所谓未见未生者，我亦记之。何者未见 未生，我所记者？色是我，从想有终，此实余虚；无色是我，从想有终；亦有色亦无色是我，从想有终；非有色非无色是我，从想有终。我有边，我无边，我有边无边，我非有边非无边，从想有终。我有乐，从想有终；我无乐，从想有终；我有苦乐，从想有终；我无苦乐，从想有终。一想是我，从想有终；种种想是我，从想有终；少想是我，从想有终；无量想是我，从想有终，此实余虚。是为邪见本见本生，我之所记。

There are some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have such theories and such views: 'This world is permanent, only this is true, the rest is false, - up to immeasurable thoughts are me, only this is true, the rest is false.' Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say and see like this: 'This is true, the rest is false.' I should tell them: 'You really make such theories. How can you say that this world is permanent? This is true, the rest is false? The Buddha does not allow this. Why? Among these views, each has afflictions. I reason that among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, there is none equal to me, let alone surpassing me. These wrong views are just words. We should not discuss them, - up to immeasurable thoughts are me, it is also like this.'

「或有沙门、婆罗门有如是论、有如是见：『此世常存，此实余虚，——乃至无量想是我，此实余虚。』彼沙门、婆罗门复作如是说、如是见：『此实，余者虚妄。』当报彼言：『汝实作此论，云何此世常存？此实余虚耶？如此语者，佛所不许。所以者何？此诸见中各有结使，我以理推，诸沙门、婆罗门中，无与我等者，况欲出过？此诸邪见但有言耳，不中共论，——乃至无量想是我，亦复如是。』

There are some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who say: 'This world is self-created.' Some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say: 'This world is created by others.' Some say: 'Self-created and created by others.' Some say: 'Not self-created, not created by others, but suddenly exists.' Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who say the world is self-created, all of those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are based on contact as a condition. If they can speak apart from the condition of contact, it is impossible. Why? Because of the six sense bases, there is contact. Because of contact, there is feeling. Because of feeling, there is craving. Because of craving, there is grasping. Because of grasping, there is becoming. Because of becoming, there is birth. Because of birth, there is old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress, the great mass of suffering. If there are no six sense bases, there is no contact. Without contact, there is no feeling. Without feeling, there is no craving. Without craving, there is no grasping. Without grasping, there is no becoming. Without becoming, there is no birth. Without birth, there is no old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress, the great mass of suffering. Also, saying that this world is created by others, also saying that this world is self-created and created by others, also saying that this world is neither self-created nor created by others, but suddenly exists, is also like this, based on contact, without contact, there is none.

「或有沙门、婆罗门作是说：『此世间自造。』复有沙门、婆罗门言：『此世间他造。』或复有言：『自造他造。』或复有言：『非自造非他造，忽然而有。』彼沙门、婆罗门言世间自造者，是沙门、婆罗门皆因触因缘，若离触因而能说者，无有是处。所以者何？由六入身故生触，由触故生受，由受故生爱，由爱故生取，由取故生有，由有故生生，由生故有老、死、忧、悲、苦恼，大患阴集。若无六入则无触，无触则无受，无受则无爱，无爱则无取，无取则无有，无有则无生，无生则无老、死、忧、悲、苦恼，大患阴集。又言此世间他造，又言此世间自造他造，又言此世间非自造非他造，忽然而有，亦复如是，因触而有，无触则无。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "If one wishes to eliminate these wrong and evil views, one should cultivate three practices in the four foundations of mindfulness. How do bhikṣus eliminate these evils and cultivate three practices in the four foundations of mindfulness? Bhikṣus, contemplating the body internally with the body, diligently and tirelessly, remembering without forgetting, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow; contemplating the body externally with the body, diligently and tirelessly, remembering without forgetting, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow; contemplating the body internally and externally with the body, remembering without forgetting, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow; contemplating feelings, mind, and dharmas, is also like this. This is eliminating the dharmas of various evils, cultivating three practices in the four foundations of mindfulness. There are eight liberations. What are the eight? Contemplating form with form is the first liberation. Internally without form-conceptions, externally contemplating form is the second liberation. Pure liberation is the third liberation. Transcending form-conceptions, extinguishing opposing conceptions, abiding in the space-pervading-cognition-base is the fourth liberation. Abandoning the space-pervading-cognition-base, abiding in the cognition-base is the fifth liberation. Abandoning the cognition-base, abiding in the non-discrimination-cognition-base is the sixth liberation. Abandoning the non-discrimination-cognition-base, abiding in the neither-discrimination-nor-non-discrimination-cognition-base is the seventh liberation. Cessation of cognition and feeling is the eighth liberation."

佛告诸比丘：「若欲灭此诸邪恶见者，于四念处当修三行。云何比丘灭此诸恶，于四念处当修三行？比丘谓内身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，除世贪忧；外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，除世贪忧；内外身身观，忆念不忘，除世贪忧；受、意、法观，亦复如是。是为灭众恶法，于四念处，三种修行。有八解脱，云何为八？色观色，初解脱。内无色想，外观色，二解脱。净解脱，三解脱。度色想灭有对想，住空处，四解脱。舍空处，住识处，五解脱。舍识处，住不用处，六解脱。舍不用处，住有想无想处，七解脱。灭尽定，八解脱。」

At that time, Ānanda was behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan to fan the Buddha. He then bared his right shoulder, touched his right knee to the ground, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha: "How wonderful, World-Honored One! This Dharma is pure and subtle, the foremost. What should it be called? How should it be upheld?"

尔时，阿难在世尊后执扇扇佛，即偏露右肩，右膝著地，叉手白佛言：「甚奇！世尊！此法清净，微妙第一。当云何名？云何奉持？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This sūtra is called 'Pure'. You should uphold it purely."

佛告阿难：「此经名为清净，汝当清净持之。」

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully upheld it.

尔时，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
