DĀ 18

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

18 （一八）自欢喜经

Parallel to: DN 28 / SN 47.12 (~) / SĀ 498 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the city of Nandiha in the Pāpālasa mango grove, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在那难陀城波波利庵婆林，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, the elder Śāriputra was in a secluded place, silently thinking to himself, "I am certain that the wisdom, supernatural powers, merits, and spiritual power of śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the past, future, and present are not equal to that of the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One." Then Śāriputra rose from his quiet room and went to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "Just now in my quiet room, I was silently reflecting: The wisdom, supernatural powers, merits, and spiritual power of śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the past, future, and present are not equal to that of the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One."

时，长老舍利弗于闲静处，默自念言：「我心决定知过去、未来、现在沙门、婆罗门智慧、神足、功德、道] cf.【丽】力，无有与如来、无所著、等正觉等者。」时，舍利弗从静室起，往至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐，白佛言：「向于静室，默自思念：过去、未来、现在沙门、婆罗门智慧、神足、功德、道力，无有与如来、无所著、等正觉等者。」

The Buddha told Śāriputra, "Excellent! Excellent! You are able to speak such words before the Buddha, to uphold them single-mindedly, and to roar the true lion's roar. No other śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa can match you. How is it, Śāriputra? Can you know the thoughts in the minds of the Buddhas of the past? Do you know that those Buddhas had such precepts, such Dharma, such wisdom, such liberation, and such liberation hall?"

佛告舍利弗：「善哉！善哉！汝能于佛前说如是语，一向受持，正师子吼，余沙门、婆罗门无及汝者。云何？舍利弗！汝能知过去诸佛心中所念，彼佛有如是戒、如是法、如是智慧、如是解脱、如是解脱堂不？」

He replied, "I do not know."

对曰：「不知。」

"How is it, Śāriputra? Can you know the thoughts in the minds of the Buddhas of the future? Do you know that those Buddhas will have such precepts, such Dharma, such wisdom, such liberation, and such liberation hall?"

「云何？舍利弗！汝能知当来诸佛心中所念，有如是戒、如是法、如是智慧、如是解脱、如是解脱堂不？」

He replied, "I do not know."

答曰：「不知。」

"How is it, Śāriputra? Can you know the thoughts in my mind as the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One? Do you know that I have such precepts, such Dharma, such wisdom, such liberation, and such liberation hall?"

「云何？舍利弗！如我今如来、至真、等正觉心中所念，如是戒、如是法、如是智、如是解脱、如是解脱堂，汝能知不？」

He replied, "I do not know."

答曰：「不知。」

He further said to Śāriputra, "You cannot know the thoughts in the minds of the Tathāgatas, Arhats, Perfectly Enlightened Ones of the past, future, and present. Why do you have such a definite thought? What is the basis for this thought? You insist on it and roar like a lion, saying, 'I definitely know that the wisdom, supernatural powers, virtues, and power of the path of the śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas of the past, future, and present are equal to the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One.' Others will not believe your words."

又告舍利弗：「过去、未来、现在如来、至真、等正觉心中所念，汝不能知，何故决定作是念？因何事生是念？一向坚持而师子吼，余沙门、婆罗门若闻汝言：『我决定知过去、未来、现在沙门、婆罗门智慧、神足、功德、道力，无有与如来、无所著、等正觉等者。』当不信汝言。」

Śāriputra said to the Buddha, "I cannot know the thoughts in the minds of the Buddhas of the past, future, and present. But I can know the general characteristics of the Dharma. The Tathāgata teaches the Dharma to me, higher and more wonderful. He teaches the black and white dharmas, the conditioned and unconditioned dharmas, the illuminating and non-illuminating dharmas. The Tathāgata's teachings are higher and more wonderful. Having heard the Dharma, I know each dharma and have reached the ultimate in the Dharma. I believe in the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One. I believe that the Tathāgata's Dharma is well distinguished. I believe that the Tathāgata has accomplished the cessation of all suffering. Among all good dharmas, this is the supreme. The World-Honored One's wisdom is unsurpassed, his supernatural powers are unsurpassed. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

舍利弗白佛言：「我于过去、未来、现在诸佛心中所念，我不能知，佛总相法我则能知。如来为我说法，转高转妙，说黑、白法，缘、无缘法，照、无照法，如来所说，转高转妙，我闻法已，知一一法，于法究竟，信如来、至真、等正觉，信如来法善可分别，信如来众苦灭成就，诸善法中，此为最上。世尊智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间所有沙门、婆罗门无有能与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of the precepts. The teaching of the precepts refers to the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernatural power, the four dhyānas, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, and the eightfold noble path. This is the unsurpassed teaching of the precepts. Wisdom is unsurpassed, supernatural powers are unsurpassed. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「世尊说法复有上者，谓制法。制法者，谓四念处、四正勤、四神足、四禅、五根、五力、七觉意、八贤圣道，是为无上制。智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间所有沙门、婆罗门皆无有与如来等者，况欲出其上者？

The World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of controlling the sense faculties. Controlling the sense faculties refers to the eyes and forms, the ears and sounds, the nose and smells, the tongue and tastes, the body and tactile objects, and the mind and dharmas. The Tathāgatas, Arhats, Perfectly Enlightened Ones of the past also controlled these sense faculties, namely, the eyes and forms, - up to the mind and dharmas; the Tathāgatas, Arhats, Perfectly Enlightened Ones of the future will also control these sense faculties, namely, the eyes and forms, - up to the mind and dharmas; now I, the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, also control these sense faculties, namely, the eyes and forms, - up to the mind and dharmas. This teaching is unsurpassed, with none able to surpass it. Wisdom is unsurpassed, supernatural powers are unsurpassed. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「世尊说法又有上者，谓制诸入。诸入者，谓眼色、耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意法，如过去如来、至真、等正觉亦制此入，所谓眼色，——乃至意法；正使未来如来、至真、等正觉亦制此入，所谓眼色，——乃至意法；今我如来、至真、等正觉亦制此入，所谓眼色，——乃至意法。此法无上，无能过者，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无能与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of entering the womb. Entering the womb refers to the first type of entering the womb, abiding, and leaving in a confused manner; the second type of entering in a non-confused manner, abiding in a confused manner, and leaving in a confused manner; the third type of entering in a non-confused manner, abiding in a non-confused manner, but leaving in a confused manner; and the fourth type of entering in a non-confused manner, abiding in a non-confused manner, and leaving in a non-confused manner. The fourth type of entering the womb, abiding, and leaving in a non-confused manner is the highest. This teaching is unsurpassed, with none able to surpass it. Wisdom is unsurpassed, supernatural powers are unsurpassed. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「世尊说法又有上者，谓识入胎。入胎者，一谓乱入胎、乱住、乱出，二者不乱入、乱住、乱出，三者不乱入、不乱住而乱出，四者不乱入、不乱住、不乱出。彼不乱入、不乱住、不乱出者，入胎之上。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无能与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of the path. The path refers to the various methods used by śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas to enter the concentration of meditative absorption and wisdom, and to cultivate the mindfulness of the mind according to the concentration. They rely on desire, detachment, cessation, and the Dharma of liberation; diligence, joy, tranquility, concentration, and equanimity. These are the highest teachings, with unsurpassed wisdom and supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，所谓道也。所谓道者，诸沙门、婆罗门以种种方便，入定慧意三昧，随三昧心修念觉意，依欲、依离、依灭尽、依出要法；精进、喜、猗、定、舍觉意，依欲、依离、依灭尽、依出要。此法最上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无能与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of cessation. Cessation refers to the slow attainment of the cessation of suffering, which is both inferior and lowly; the quick attainment of the cessation of suffering, which is only lowly; the slow attainment of the cessation of pleasure, which is only slow and lowly; and the quick attainment of the cessation of pleasure, which is not extensive and universal. Because it is not extensive and universal, it is called inferior and lowly. Now the Tathāgata quickly attains the cessation of pleasure, and it is also extensive and universal. Even gods and humans see his supernatural transformations.

「如来说法复有上者，所谓为灭。灭者，谓苦灭迟得，二俱卑陋；苦灭速得，唯苦卑陋；乐灭迟得，唯迟卑陋；乐灭速得，然不广普，以不广普，故名卑陋。如今如来乐灭速得，而复广普，乃至天人见神变化。」

Śāriputra said to the Buddha, "The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma is the most subtle and supreme. Even women can uphold it, exhaust their afflictions, attain the non-afflicted, and attain liberation of mind and liberation through wisdom in this very life. They realize for themselves that birth and death are exhausted, the practice of purity is established, what was to be done is done, and they will not undergo further rebirth. This is the Tathāgata's teaching of the unsurpassed cessation. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

舍利弗白佛言：「世尊所说微妙第一，下至女人，亦能受持，尽有漏成无漏，心解脱、慧解脱，于现法中自身作证：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不受后有，是为如来说无上灭。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无能与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of pure speech. Pure speech means that the World-Honored One, among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, does not speak meaningless and false words. His speech does not seek victory, nor does it form factions. His speech is gentle and does not miss the right time. His speech is not empty. This is called pure speech. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓言清净。言清净者，世尊于诸沙门、婆罗门，不说无益虚妄之言，言不求胜，亦不朋党，所言柔和，不失时节，言不虚发，是为言清净。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无有与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of fixed views. The fixed views are that there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who use various means to enter the concentration of the mind and, according to the mind in concentration, observe from the head to the feet, from the feet to the head, the skin and flesh inside and outside, only the impure hair, body hair, nails, liver, lungs, intestines, stomach, spleen, kidneys, five viscera, sweat, fat, marrow, brain, feces, urine, snot, and tears, the impure things that are smelly and not worth coveting. This is the first fixed view. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the concentration of the mind, according to the mind in concentration, remove the impure things outside the skin and flesh, and only observe the white bones and teeth. This is the second fixed view. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the concentration of the mind, according to the mind in concentration, remove the impure things outside the skin and flesh and the white bones, and only observe where the consciousness is located, whether in this world or the next. If it is not cut off in this world, it is not cut off in the next. If it is not liberated in this world, it is not liberated in the next. This is the third fixed view. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the concentration of the mind, according to the mind in concentration, remove the impure things outside the skin and flesh and the white bones, and again observe the consciousness. The consciousness is in the next world, not in this world. It is cut off in this world, but not cut off in the next. It is liberated in this world, but not liberated in the next. This is the fourth fixed view. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the concentration of the mind, according to the mind in concentration, remove the impure things outside the skin and flesh and the white bones, and again observe the consciousness. It is not in this world, nor in the next. Both are cut off, and both are liberated. This is the fifth fixed view. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓见定。彼见定者，谓有沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，观头至足，观足至头，皮肤内外，但有不净发、毛、爪甲，肝、肺、肠、胃、脾、肾五脏，汗、肪、髓、脑、屎、尿、涕、 、泪，臭处] cf.【丽】不净，无一可贪，是初见定。诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，除去皮肉外诸不净，唯观白骨及与牙齿，是为二见定。诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，除去皮肉外诸不净及白骨，唯观心识在何处住？为在今世？为在后世？今世不断，后世不断；今世不解脱，后世不解脱，是为三见定。诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，除去皮肉外诸不净及除白骨，复重观识；识在后世，不在今世；今世断，后世不断；今世解脱，后世不解脱，是为四见定。诸有沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，除去皮肉外诸不净及除白骨，复重观识，不在今世，不在后世；二俱断，二俱解脱，是为五见定。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of the eternal Dharma. The eternal Dharma means that there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who use various means to enter the concentration of the mind and, according to the mind in concentration, remember and recognize the twenty eons of formation and destruction in the world. They say: 'The world is eternally existent. This is true, the rest is false. Why is that? Because I remember and recognize it, I know there are these eons of formation and destruction. The past eons I do not know, and the future eons I also do not know.' This person, morning and evening, with ignorance, says: 'The world is eternally existent. Only this is true, the rest is false.' This is the first eternal Dharma. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the concentration of the mind, according to the mind in concentration, remember and recognize the forty eons of formation and destruction. They say: 'This world is eternal. This is true, the rest is false. Why is that? Because I remember and recognize it, I know the eons of formation and destruction. I can also go beyond this and know the past eons of formation and destruction. I do not know the future eons of formation and destruction.' This is said to know the beginning, not the end. This person, morning and evening, with ignorance, says: 'The world is eternally existent. Only this is true, the rest is false.' This is the second eternal Dharma. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the concentration of the mind, according to the mind in concentration, remember and recognize the eighty eons of formation and destruction. They say: 'This world is eternal. The rest is false. Why is that? Because I remember and recognize it, I know there are eons of formation and destruction. I can also go beyond this and know the past eons of formation and destruction. I also know the future eons of formation and destruction.' This person, morning and evening, with ignorance, says: 'The world is eternally existent. Only this is true, the rest is false.' This is the third eternal Dharma. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓说常法。常法者，诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，忆识世间二十成劫败劫，彼作是言：『世间常存，此为真实，余者虚妄，所以者何？由我忆识，故知有此成劫败劫，其余过去我所不知，未来成败我亦不知。』此人朝暮以无智说言：『世间常存，唯此为实，余者为虚。』是为初常法。诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，忆识四十成劫败劫，彼作是言：『此世间常，此为真实，余者虚妄。所以者何？以我忆识故知成劫败劫，我复能过是，知过去成劫败劫，我不知未来劫之成败。』此说知始，不说知终，此人朝暮以无智说言：『世间常存，唯此真实，余者虚妄。』此是二常法。诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，忆识八十成劫败劫，彼言：『此世间常，余者虚妄。所以者何？以我忆识故知有成劫败劫，复过是知过去成劫败劫，未来劫之成败我亦悉知。』此人朝暮以无智说言：『世间常存，唯此为实，余者虚妄。』是为三常存法。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无有能与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of observation. Observation means that there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who observe others' minds with thoughts, saying: 'This mind is like this, that mind is like that.' When they think like this, it may be true or false. This is the first observation. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas do not observe with thoughts, but hear the words of various gods and non-humans, and say to them: 'Your mind is like this, your mind is like that.' This may also be true or false. This is the second observation. There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not observe with thoughts, nor hear the words of various gods and non-humans, but observe their own bodies and listen to others' words, and say to others: 'Your mind is like this, your mind is like that.' This may also be true or false. This is the third observation. There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not observe with thoughts, nor hear the words of various gods and non-humans, nor observe themselves or others, but after removing thoughts and observations, attain the concentration of the mind and observe others' minds, and say to them: 'Your mind is like this, your mind is like that.' Such observation is true. This is the fourth observation. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓观察。观察者，谓有沙门、婆罗门以想观察，他心尔趣，此心尔趣，彼心作是想时，或虚或实，是为一观察。诸沙门、婆罗门不以想观察，或闻诸天及非人语，而语彼言：『汝心如是，汝心如是。』此亦或实或虚，是二观察。或有沙门、婆罗门不以想观察，亦不闻诸天及非人语，自观己身，又听他言，语彼人言：『汝心如是，汝心如是。』此亦有实有虚，是为三观察。或有沙门、婆罗门不以想观察，亦不闻诸天及非人语，又不自观、观他，除觉、观已，得定意三昧，观察他心，而语彼言：『汝心如是，汝心如是。』如是观察则为真实，是为四观察。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无有与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the teaching of instruction. Instruction means that there are people who do not violate the instructions, exhaust the contaminants and accomplish the non-contaminants, attain liberation of the mind and wisdom, and realize for themselves in this very life that birth and death are exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been accomplished, and they will not enter into any future existence. This is the first instruction. There are people who do not violate the instructions, exhaust the five lower fetters, attain parinirvāṇa there, and do not return to this world. This is the second instruction. There are people who do not violate the instructions, exhaust the three fetters, and thin out desire, anger, and delusion, attaining the state of once-returner, returning to this world and attaining parinirvāṇa. This is the third instruction. There are people who do not violate the instructions, exhaust the three fetters, attain the state of stream-enterer, and after at most seven rebirths, will definitely attain the fruit of the path, not falling into the evil destinies. This is the fourth instruction. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，所谓教诫。教诫者，或时有人不违教诫，尽有漏成无漏，心解脱、智慧解脱，于现法中自身作证：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不复受有，是为初教诫。或时有人不违教诫，尽五下结，于彼灭度，不还此世，是为二教诫。或时有人不违教诫，三结尽，薄淫、怒、痴，得斯陀含，还至此世而取灭度，是为三教诫。或时有人不违教诫，三结尽，得须陀洹，极七往返，必成道果，不堕恶趣，是为四教诫。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无有与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, teaching others the Dharma to purify their precepts. Precept purity means that there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas whose words are sincere, without double-tonguedness, always respectful and reverent, abandoning sleepiness, not harboring deceit, not speaking falsely, not predicting good or bad fortune for worldly people, not boasting about what they have obtained from others to show others, seeking more benefits for others, sitting in meditation and cultivating wisdom, with unimpeded eloquence, single-mindedly not distracted, diligently not lazy. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，为他说法，使戒清净。戒清净者，有诸沙门、婆罗门所语至诚，无有两舌，常自敬肃，捐除睡眠，不怀邪谄，口不妄言，不为世人记于吉凶，不自称说从他所得以示于人，更求他利，坐禅修智，辩才无碍，专念不乱，精勤不怠。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无有与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the wisdom of liberation. The wisdom of liberation means that the World-Honored One, due to other causes and conditions, thinks to himself: 'This person is a stream-enterer, this is a once-returner, this is a non-returner, this is an arhat.' This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓解脱智。谓解脱智者，世尊由他因缘内自思惟言：『此人是须陀洹，此是斯陀含，此是阿那含，此是阿罗汉。』此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无有与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the wisdom of self-knowledge of past lives. Śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the samādhi of concentration, and according to the mind of samādhi, they can recall their past lives in countless eons, one life, two lives, up to hundreds of thousands of lives, eons of formation and eons of destruction. They can recall countless eons of their lives in such-and-such places, with such-and-such names, such-and-such clans, such-and-such lifespans, such-and-such food and drink, such-and-such suffering and happiness, being born here and there, and being born here from there. They can recall the countless eons of their past lives in various ways. Day and night, they always remember what they have experienced. They know that this is form, this is formless, this is thought, this is formless, this is not formless, and they can recall and know all the countless eons of their past lives. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓自识宿命智证。诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，自忆往昔无数世事，一生、二生，——乃至百千生成劫败劫，如是无数我于某处生，名字如是，种、姓如是，寿命如是，饮食如是，苦乐如是；从此生彼，从彼生此，若干种相，自忆宿命无数劫事，昼夜常念本所经历。此是色，此是无色；此是想，此是无想，此是非无想，尽忆尽知。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无与如来等者，况欲出其上？

The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the wisdom of the divine eye. The wisdom of the divine eye means that śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the samādhi of concentration, and according to the mind of samādhi, they can observe all sentient beings, those who die and are born, those with good and bad appearances, those in good and bad destinies, those who are good and bad, and those who act according to their deeds. They can see and know all of this. Some sentient beings perform evil deeds with the body, speech, and mind, slander the sages, and have wrong views. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they fall into the three evil destinies. Some sentient beings perform good deeds with the body, speech, and mind, do not slander the sages, and have right views. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among humans and gods. With the pure divine eye, they can observe all sentient beings and know and see them as they truly are. This Dharma is unsurpassed, with no remaining wisdom and no remaining supernatural powers. Among all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world, none can be equal to the Tathāgata, let alone surpass him.

「如来说法复有上者，谓天眼智。天眼智者，诸沙门、婆罗门种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，观诸众生，死者、生者，善色、恶色，善趣、恶趣，若好、若丑，随其所行，尽见尽知。或有众生，成就身恶行、口恶行、意恶行，诽谤贤圣，信邪倒见，身坏命终，堕三恶道。或有众生，身行善、口言善、意念善，不谤贤圣，见正信行，身坏命终，生天人中，以天眼净，观诸众生，如实知见。此法无上，智慧无余，神通无余，诸世间沙门、婆罗门无与如来等者，况欲出其上？

"The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma has a higher level, namely, the realization of supernatural powers. The realization of supernatural powers means that śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use various means to enter the samādhi of concentration, and according to the mind of samādhi, they can perform countless supernatural powers. They can transform one body into countless bodies, and combine countless bodies into one body. They can pass through stone walls without obstruction, and sit cross-legged in the sky. They can enter and exit the ground as if in water, and walk on water as if on land. Their bodies emit smoke and fire like a burning pile of fire. They can touch the sun and moon with their hands, and stand up to reach the Brahma Heaven. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas claim to have these supernatural powers, they should be told: 'These supernatural powers exist, they are not nonexistent. These supernatural powers are inferior and inferior, practiced by ordinary people, not cultivated by the sages. If a bhikṣu is not stained by the desirable forms in the world, abandons them, and practices accordingly, this is called the supernatural power of the sages. He is not disgusted with the undesirable forms, abandons them, and practices accordingly, this is called the supernatural power of the sages. He abandons both the desirable and undesirable forms in the world, cultivates equanimity, and does not forget the practice, this is called the supernatural power of the sages. It is like the World-Honored One, who is diligent and courageous, has great wisdom, knowledge, and understanding, and has attained the highest understanding, thus called 'equally awakened'. The World-Honored One now does not delight in desires, does not delight in the inferior practices of ordinary people, and does not labor to experience suffering. If the World-Honored One wishes to eliminate the evil dharmas, with perception and contemplation, joy and pleasure arising from separation, he can dwell in the first dhyāna, thus able to eliminate the evil dhyāna, with perception and contemplation, joy and pleasure arising from separation, and dwell in the first dhyāna; the second, third, and fourth dhyānas are also like this. He is diligent and courageous, has great wisdom, knowledge, and understanding, and has attained the highest understanding, thus called 'equally awakened'."

「如来说法复有上者，谓神足证。神足证者，诸沙门、婆罗门以种种方便，入定意三昧，随三昧心，作无数神力，能变一身为无数身，以无数身合为一身，石壁无碍，于虚空中结加趺坐。犹如飞鸟，出入于地；犹如在水，履水如地；身出烟火，如火积燃；以手扪日月，立至梵天。若沙门、婆罗门称是神足者，当报彼言：『有此神足，非为不有。此神足者，卑贱下劣，凡夫所行，非是贤圣之所修习。若比丘于诸世间爱色不染，舍离此已，如所应行，斯乃名为贤圣神足。于无喜色，亦不憎恶，舍离此已，如所应行，斯乃名曰贤圣神足。于诸世间爱色、不爱色，二俱舍已，修平等护，专念不忘，斯乃名曰贤圣神足。犹如世尊精进勇猛，有大智慧，有知、有觉，得第一觉，故名等觉。世尊今亦不乐于欲，不乐卑贱凡夫所习，亦不劳勤受诸苦恼。世尊若欲除弊恶法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，游于初禅，如是便能除弊恶法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，游于初禅；二禅、三禅、四禅，亦复如是。精进勇猛，有大智慧，有知、有觉，得第一觉，故名等觉。』」

The Buddha told Śāriputra, "If a non-Buddhist ascetic comes and asks you, 'Are past śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas equal to Śramaṇa Gautama?' how would you answer? They will further ask, 'Are future śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas equal to Śramaṇa Gautama?' how would you answer? They will further ask, 'Are present śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas equal to Śramaṇa Gautama?' how would you answer? "

佛告舍利弗：「若有外道异学来问汝言：『过去沙门、婆罗门与沙门瞿昙等不？』汝当云何答？彼复问言：『未来沙门、婆罗门与沙门瞿昙等不？』汝当云何答？彼复问言现在沙门、婆罗门与沙门瞿昙等不？汝当云何答？」

At that time, Śāriputra said to the Buddha, "If someone asks, 'Are past śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas equal to the Buddha?' you should answer, 'Yes.' If they ask, 'Are future śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas equal to the Buddha?' you should answer, 'Yes.' If they ask, 'Are present śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas equal to the Buddha?' you should answer, 'No.'"

时，舍利弗白佛言：「设有是问：『过去沙门、婆罗门与佛等不？』当答言：『有。』设问：『未来沙门、婆罗门与佛] cf.【丽】等不？』当答言：『有。』设问：『现在沙门、婆罗门与佛等不？』当答言：『无。』」

The Buddha told Śāriputra, "Those non-Buddhist ascetics may further ask, 'Why do you sometimes say yes and sometimes say no?' How would you answer?"

佛告舍利弗：「彼外道梵志或复问言：『汝何故或言有？或言无？』汝当云何答？」

Śāriputra said, "I would answer them, 'The past Tathāgatas are equal to the Tathāgata, and the future Tathāgatas will be equal to the Tathāgata. I have personally heard it from the Buddha. If you want the present Tathāgatas to be equal to the Tathāgata, it is impossible.' World-Honored One! If I answer according to what I have heard, in accordance with the Dharma and following the Dharma, will there be no fault?"

舍利弗言：「我当报彼：『过去三耶三佛与如来等，未来三耶三佛与如来等，我躬从佛闻，欲使现在有三耶三佛与如来等者，无有是处。』世尊！我如所闻，依法顺法，作如是答，将无咎] cf.【丽】耶？」

The Buddha said, "Answering like that, in accordance with the Dharma and following the Dharma, is not wrong. Because the past Tathāgatas are equal to me, and the future Tathāgatas will be equal to me. If you want two Buddhas to appear in the present world, it is impossible."

佛言：「如是答，依法顺法，不违也。所以然者？过去三耶三佛与我等，未来三耶三佛与我等，欲使现在有二佛出世，无有是处。」

At that time, Venerable Uttara was behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha. The Buddha told him, "Uttara! You should observe the World-Honored One's contentment with few desires. Now I have great spiritual power and great majestic virtue, yet I am content with few desires and do not delight in desires. Uttara! If other śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas can diligently cultivate and attain even one Dharma in this teaching, they should raise a banner and proclaim to the four directions, 'The Tathāgata is now content with few desires. Now observe the Tathāgata's contentment with few desires. The Tathāgata has great spiritual power and great majestic virtue, yet does not dwell in desires.'"

尔时，尊者欝陀夷在世尊后执扇扇佛，佛告之曰：「欝陀夷！汝当观世尊少欲知足，今我有大神力，有大威德，而少欲知足，不乐在欲。欝陀夷！若余沙门、婆罗门于此法中能勤苦得一法者，彼便当竖幡，告四远言：『如来今者少欲知足，今观如来少欲知足，如来有大神力，有大威德，不用在欲。』」

At that time, Venerable Uttara adjusted his robes, bared his right shoulder, touched his right knee to the ground, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "How wonderful! World-Honored One! Few have the contentment with few desires like the World-Honored One. The World-Honored One has great spiritual power and great majestic virtue, yet does not dwell in desires. If other śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas can diligently cultivate and attain even one Dharma in this teaching, they will be able to raise a banner and proclaim to the four directions, 'The World-Honored One is now content with few desires.' Śāriputra! You should often teach this Dharma to the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās. If they have doubts about the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, or the path, hearing this teaching will remove their doubts and entanglements."

尔时，尊者欝陀夷正衣服，偏露右肩，右膝著地，叉手白佛言：「甚奇！世尊！少有少欲知足如世尊者，世尊有大神力，有大威德，不用在欲。若复有余沙门、婆罗门于此法中能勤苦得一法者，便能竖幡，告四远言：『世尊今者少欲知足。』舍利弗！当为诸比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷数说此法，彼若于佛、法、僧，于道有疑者，闻说此法，无复疑网。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra, "You should often teach this Dharma to the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās. Why is that? Those who have doubts about the Buddha, Dharma, Saṃgha, or the path, upon hearing your teaching, will attain understanding."

尔时，世尊告舍利弗：「汝当为诸比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷数说此法。所以者何？彼于佛、法、僧，于道有疑者，闻汝所说，当得开解。」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

对曰：「唯然。世尊！」

At that time, Śāriputra often taught the Dharma to the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās. It was for the sake of purifying himself that he was called the Pure Sutra.

时，舍利弗即便数数为诸比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷说法，以自清净故，故名清净经。

At that time, Śāriputra, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

尔时，舍利弗闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
