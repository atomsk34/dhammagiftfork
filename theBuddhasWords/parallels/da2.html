DĀ 2

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 1 第一分

2 （二）游行经

Parallel to: DN 16 / DN 17 (~) / SN 47.9 (~) / AN 4.180 (~) / Snp 1.5 / MĀ 68 (~) / EĀ 28.5 (~) / T 5 / T 6 / T 7 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying on Vulture Peak in the city of Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在罗阅城耆阇崛山中，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, King Ajātaśatru of Magadha wanted to attack the country of Vṛji. He thought to himself, "Although they are brave and strong, with a large and powerful population, it should not be difficult for me to conquer them." Then King Ajātaśatru ordered the brahmin minister Udayin, saying, "Go to Vulture Peak, to where the World-Honored One is, and in my name pay homage at the feet of the World-Honored One. Inquire after the World-Honored One's well-being, asking if he is light and at ease, and if his walking is strong. Also tell the World-Honored One, 'The people of the country of Vṛji, relying on their bravery and strength, and their large and powerful population, do not submit to me. I wish to attack them. I do not know what the World-Honored One would instruct me to do?' If there is any instruction, you should remember it well, without forgetting anything. Speak as you have heard. The Tathāgata's words are never false."

是时，摩竭王阿阇世欲伐跋祇，王自念言：「彼虽勇健，人众豪强，以我取彼，未足为难。」时，阿阇世王命婆罗门大臣禹舍，而告之曰：「汝诣耆阇崛山，至世尊所，持我名字，礼世尊足，问讯世尊：『起居轻利，游步强耶？』又白世尊：『跋祇国人自恃勇健，民众豪强，不顺伏我，我欲伐之，不审世尊何所诫勅？』若有教诫，汝善忆念，勿有遗漏，如所闻说。如来所言，终不虚妄。」

Minister Udayin, having received the king's instructions, immediately rode in a jeweled carriage to Vulture Peak. When he arrived at the place where the Buddha was staying, he got off the carriage and walked forward. He went to where the World-Honored One was, inquired after his well-being, and then sat to one side. He said to the World-Honored One, "King Ajātaśatru of Magadha bows at the feet of the Buddha and respectfully inquires after your well-being, asking if you are light and at ease, and if your walking is strong. He also says to the World-Honored One, 'The people of the country of Vṛji, relying on their bravery and strength, and their large and powerful population, do not submit to me. I wish to attack them. I do not know what the World-Honored One would instruct me to do?'"

大臣禹舍受王教已，即乘宝车诣耆阇崛山，到所止处，下车步进，至世尊所，问讯毕，一面坐，白世尊曰：「摩竭王阿阇世稽首佛足，敬问慇懃：『起居轻利，游步强耶？』又白世尊：『跋祇国人自恃勇健，民众豪强，不顺伏我，我欲伐之，不审世尊何所诫勅？』」

At that time, Ānanda was behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha. The Buddha said to Ānanda, "Have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji are gathering together to discuss proper matters?"

尔时，阿难在世尊后执扇扇佛，佛告阿难：「汝闻跋祇国人数相集会，讲议正事不？」

He replied, "I have heard it."

答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed. Ānanda, have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji are harmonious with their rulers and ministers, and respect each other between superiors and inferiors?"

佛告阿难：「若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增盛，其国久安，无能侵损。阿难！汝闻跋祇国人君臣和顺，上下相敬不？」

He replied, "I have heard it."

答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed. Ānanda, have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji follow the Dharma, understand taboos, and do not violate propriety and decorum?"

「阿难！若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增盛，其国久安，无能侵损。阿难！汝闻跋祇国人奉法晓忌，不违礼度不？」

He replied, "I have heard it."

答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed. Ānanda, have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji are filial to their parents and respectful to their teachers and elders?"

「阿难！若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增盛，其国久安，无能侵损。阿难！汝闻跋祇国人孝事父母，敬顺师长不？」

He replied, "I have heard it."

答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed. Ānanda, have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji are respectful to their ancestral temples and pay homage to the spirits?"

「阿难！若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增上，其国久安，无能侵损。阿难！汝闻跋祇国人恭于宗庙，致敬鬼神不？」

He replied, "I have heard it."

答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed. Ānanda, have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji are pure and clean in their homes, and even in their jokes and laughter, do not speak inappropriately?"

「阿难！若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增上，其国久安，无能侵损。阿难！汝闻跋祇国人闺门真正洁净无秽，至於戏笑，言不及邪不？」

He replied, "I have heard it."

答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed. Ānanda, have you heard that the people of the country of Vṛji respect śramaṇas and uphold the precepts, and look after them without ever being lazy?" He replied, "I have heard it."

「阿难！若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增盛，其国久安，无能侵损。阿难！汝闻跋祇国人宗事沙门，敬持戒者，瞻视护养，未甞懈惓不？」答曰：「闻之。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If they can do that, the young and old will be harmonious and prosperous, and the country will be peaceful for a long time without being harmed."

「阿难！若能尔者，长幼和顺，转更增盛，其国久安，无能侵损。」

At that time, the great minister Yasa said to the Buddha, "If the people of that country practice even one of these principles, they cannot be plotted against, let alone all seven. Because of the many affairs of state, I now ask to take leave and return home."

时，大臣禹舍白佛言：「彼国人民，若行一法，犹不可图，况复具七？国事多故，今请辞还归。」

The Buddha said, "Yes, you should know the time." At that time, Yasa immediately rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha three times, bowed in respect, and withdrew.

佛言：「可，宜知是时。」时，禹舍即从座起，遶佛三匝，揖让而退。

Not long after he left, the Buddha told Ānanda, "You should order all the bhikṣus from Rājagṛha and its surroundings to gather in the lecture hall."

其去未久，佛告阿难：「汝勅罗阅祇左右诸比丘尽集讲堂。」

He replied, "Yes." He then went to Rājagṛha City, gathered all the bhikṣus, and assembled them in the lecture hall. He then informed the World-Honored One, "The bhikṣus have already gathered. The Sage should know the time."

对曰：「唯然。」即诣罗阅祇城，集诸比丘，尽会讲堂，白世尊曰：「诸比丘已集，唯圣知时。」

At that time, the World-Honored One rose from his seat, went to the Dharma lecture hall, took his seat, and addressed the bhikṣus, "I shall now explain to you the seven non-retrogressive dharmas. Listen attentively and contemplate them well."

尔时，世尊即从座起，诣法讲堂，就座而坐，告诸比丘：「我当为汝说七不退法，谛听！谛听！善思念之。」

At that time, the bhikṣus addressed the Buddha, saying, "Yes, World-Honored One! We wish to hear it with great joy!"

时，诸比丘白佛言：「唯然，世尊！愿乐欲闻！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "The seven non-retrogressive dharmas are: First, regularly gathering together to discuss the true principles, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed. Second, the superiors and inferiors harmoniously respect and follow without contradiction, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed. Third, upholding the Dharma and understanding taboos, not contradicting the system, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed. Fourth, if there are bhikṣus who are able to protect the assembly and have many acquaintances, they should be respected and served, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed. Fifth, contemplating and protecting the mind, with filial respect as the foremost, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed. Sixth, purifying the practice of the Brahma-way, not following desires and emotions, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed. Seventh, putting others before oneself, not being greedy for fame and profit, then the young and old will be harmonious and the Dharma cannot be destroyed."

佛告诸比丘：「七不退法者：一曰数相集会，讲论正义，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。二曰上下和同，敬顺无违，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。三曰奉法晓忌，不违制度，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。四曰若有比丘力能护众，多诸知识，宜敬事之，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。五曰念护心意，孝敬为首，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。六曰净修梵行，不随欲态，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。七曰先人后己，不贪名利，则长幼和顺，法不可坏。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are also seven dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. First, being content with few affairs and not liking to do many things, then the Dharma increases and is not depleted. Second, liking quietude and not liking to speak much. Third, having little sleep and being without confusion. Fourth, not forming cliques and speaking of things that are of no benefit. Fifth, not boasting about oneself without virtue. Sixth, not associating with evil people. Seventh, liking the solitude of mountains and forests. Bhikṣus, in this way the Dharma increases and is not depleted."

佛告比丘：「复有七法，令法增长，无有损耗。一者乐于少事，不好多为，则法增长，无有损耗。二者乐于静默，不好多言。三者少于睡眠，无有昏昧。四者不为群党，言无益事。五者不以无德而自称誉。六者不与恶人而为伴党。七者乐于山林闲静独处。如是比丘！则法增长，无有损耗。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are also seven dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. What are the seven? First, having faith, believing in the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, who is endowed with the ten epithets. Second, knowing shame, being ashamed of one's own shortcomings. Third, knowing modesty, being embarrassed by evil conduct. Fourth, being learned, being able to uphold the good teachings of the upper, middle, and lower levels, with profound and wonderful meaning, pure and undefiled, and fully endowed with the practice of purity. Fifth, diligently practicing austerities, extinguishing evil and cultivating goodness, diligently practicing without abandoning. Sixth, remembering what one has learned in the past without forgetting. Seventh, cultivating wisdom, knowing the Dharma of birth and cessation, entering the essential path of the sages, and exhausting the root of all suffering. These seven dharmas cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted."

佛告比丘：「复有七法，令法增长，无有损耗。何谓为七？一者有信，信于如来、至真、正觉，十号具足。二者知惭，耻于己阙。三者知愧，羞为恶行。四者多闻，其所受持，上中下善，义味深奥，清净无秽，梵行具足。五者精勤苦行，灭恶修善，勤习不舍。六者昔所学习，忆念不忘。七者修习智慧，知生灭法，趣贤圣要，尽诸苦本。如是七法，则法增长，无有损耗。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are also seven dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. What are the seven? First, respecting the Buddha. Second, respecting the Dharma. Third, respecting the Saṃgha. Fourth, respecting the precepts. Fifth, respecting concentration. Sixth, respecting and obeying one's parents. Seventh, respecting non-negligence. These seven dharmas cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted."

佛告比丘：「复有七法，令法增长，无有损耗。何谓为七？一者敬佛，二者敬法，三者敬僧，四者敬戒，五者敬定，六者敬顺父母，七者敬不放逸。如是七法，则法增长，无有损耗。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are also seven dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. What are the seven dharmas? First, contemplating the impurity of the body. Second, contemplating the impurity of food. Third, not delighting in the world. Fourth, always contemplating the thought of death. Fifth, giving rise to the thought of impermanence. Sixth, contemplating the suffering of impermanence. Seventh, contemplating the selflessness of suffering. These seven dharmas cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted."

佛告比丘：「复有七法，则法增长，无有损耗。何谓为七法？一者观身不净，二者观食不净，三者不乐世间，四者常念死想，五者起无常想，六者无常苦想，七者苦无我想。如是七法，则法增长，无有损耗。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are also seven dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. What are the seven? First, cultivating the mindfulness awakening factor, tranquil and desireless, leading to the unconditioned. Second, cultivating the Dharma awakening factor. Third, cultivating the vigor awakening factor. Fourth, cultivating the joy awakening factor. Fifth, cultivating the ease awakening factor. Sixth, cultivating the concentration awakening factor. Seventh, cultivating the equanimity awakening factor. These seven dharmas cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted."

佛告比丘：「复有七法，则法增长，无有损耗。何谓为七？一者修念觉意，闲静无欲，出要无为。二者修法觉意。三者修精进觉意。四者修喜觉意。五者修猗觉意。六者修定觉意。七者修护觉意。如是七法，则法增长，无有损耗。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are six non-retrogressive dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. What are the six? First, always practicing kindness with the body, not harming sentient beings. Second, speaking kindly with the mouth, not uttering evil words. Third, contemplating kindness with the mind, not harboring harm. Fourth, obtaining pure offerings and sharing them equally with others. Fifth, upholding the noble precepts without any omissions or defilements, definitely unshakable. Sixth, seeing the noble path to the end of suffering. These six dharmas cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted."

佛告比丘：「有六不退法，令法增长，无有损耗。何谓为六？一者身常行慈，不害众生。二者口宣仁慈，不演恶言。三者意念慈心，不怀坏损。四者得净利养，与众共之，平等无二。五者持贤圣戒，无有阙漏，亦无垢秽，必定不动。六者见贤圣道，以尽苦际。如是六法，则法增长，无有损耗。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "There are also six non-retrogressive dharmas that cause the Dharma to increase and not be depleted. First, mindfulness of the Buddha. Second, mindfulness of the Dharma. Third, mindfulness of the Saṃgha. Fourth, mindfulness of precepts. Fifth, mindfulness of generosity. Sixth, mindfulness of the heavens. Cultivating these six mindfulnesses causes the Dharma to increase and not be depleted."

佛告比丘：「复有六不退法，令法增长，无有损耗。一者念佛，二者念法，三者念僧，四者念戒，五者念施，六者念天。修此六念，则法增长，无有损耗。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed in Rājagṛha as he wished, told Ānanda, "You should all prepare yourselves. I wish to go to the Bamboo Grove."

尔时，世尊于罗阅祇随宜住已，告阿难言：「汝等皆严，吾欲诣竹园。」

He replied, "Yes." He then put on his robe and took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, he attended the World-Honored One. Passing through Magadha, he arrived at the Bamboo Grove. He went to the hall and sat down. He spoke to the bhikṣus about precepts, concentration, and wisdom. Cultivating precepts leads to concentration, which brings great rewards. Cultivating concentration leads to wisdom, which brings great rewards. Cultivating wisdom purifies the mind, leading to liberation. One exhausts the three taints - the outflow of desire, the outflow of existence, and the outflow of ignorance. Having attained liberation, one attains the knowledge of liberation: birth and death are exhausted, the practice of the Brahma way is established, what was to be done is done, and one will not be reborn again.

对曰：「唯然。」即严衣钵，与诸大众侍从世尊，路由摩竭，次到竹园，往堂上坐，与诸比丘说戒、定、慧。修戒获定，得大果报；修定获智，得大果报；修智心净，得等解脱，尽于三漏——欲漏、有漏、无明漏。已得解脱生解脱智：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不受后有。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed in the Bamboo Grove as he wished, told Ānanda, "You should all prepare yourselves. We shall go to the city of Pāṭaliputra."

尔时，世尊于竹园随宜住已，告阿难曰：「汝等皆严，当诣巴陵弗城。」

He replied, "Yes." He then put on his robe and took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, he attended the World-Honored One. Passing through Magadha, he arrived at the city of Pāṭaliputra. He sat under a pāṭali tree.

对曰：「唯然。」即严衣钵，与诸大众侍从世尊，路由摩竭，次到巴陵弗城，巴陵树下坐。

At that time, the lay believers heard that the Buddha had come from afar with the great assembly and was sitting under a pāṭali tree. They immediately went out of the city. Seeing the World-Honored One under the pāṭali tree, with a dignified appearance, his faculties tranquil and well-tamed, like a great dragon with clear and unclouded water, adorned with the thirty-two marks and eighty minor marks, they were delighted. Having seen this, they gradually approached the Buddha, bowed their heads at his feet, and sat to one side.

时，诸清信士闻佛与诸大众远来至此巴陵树下，即共出城，遥见世尊在巴陵树下，容貌端正，诸根寂定，善调第一。譬犹大龙，以水清澄，无有尘垢；三十二相、八十种好，庄严其身。见已欢喜，渐到佛所，头面礼足，却坐一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One gradually taught the Dharma to them, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting them. The lay believers, having heard the Buddha's teaching, said to the Buddha, "We wish to take refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and the noble Sangha. We only pray that the World-Honored One will compassionately allow us to become lay followers. From now on, we will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol. We will uphold the precepts without forgetting. We wish to make offerings tomorrow. We only pray that the World-Honored One and the great assembly will deign to accept our invitation."

尔时，世尊渐为说法，示教利喜，诸清信士闻佛说法，即白佛言：「我欲归依佛、法、圣众，唯愿世尊哀愍，听许为优婆塞，自今已后，不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒，奉戒不忘。明欲设供，唯愿世尊与诸大众垂愍屈顾。」

At that time, the World-Honored One silently accepted. The lay believers, seeing the Buddha's silence, immediately rose from their seats, circumambulated the Buddha three times, bowed, and returned. They then built a great hall for the Tathāgata, leveled the ground, swept and sprinkled it, burned incense, and arranged precious seats. When the offerings were ready, they went to inform the World-Honored One, "The offerings are ready. The Sage only knows the time."

尔时，世尊默然许可。诸清信士见佛默然，即从座起，遶佛三匝，作礼而归。寻为如来起大堂舍，平治处所，扫洒烧香，严敷宝座。供设既办，往白世尊：「所设已具，唯圣知时。」

Then the World-Honored One rose from his seat, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and went with the great assembly to the lecture hall. He washed his hands and feet, and sat in the middle. At that time, the bhikṣus sat to the left, and the lay believers sat to the right. Then the World-Honored One told the lay believers, "Ordinary people who break the precepts have five kinds of decline. What are the five? First, they seek wealth but do not get what they wish. Second, even if they obtain something, it will decline day by day. Third, wherever they go, they are not respected by the people. Fourth, their bad reputation spreads throughout the world. Fifth, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they will enter hell." He also told the lay believers, "Ordinary people who uphold the precepts have five kinds of merit. What are the five? First, whatever they seek, they immediately get what they wish. Second, their wealth and possessions increase without loss. Third, wherever they go, people respect and love them. Fourth, their good reputation spreads throughout the world. Fifth, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they will definitely be reborn in heaven."

于是，世尊即从座起，著衣持钵，与大众俱诣彼讲堂，澡手洗足，处中而坐。时，诸比丘在左面坐，诸清信士在右面坐。尔时，世尊告诸清信士曰：「凡人犯戒，有五衰耗。何谓为五？一者求财，所愿不遂。二者设有所得，日当衰耗。三者在所至处，众所不敬。四者丑名恶声，流闻天下。五者身坏命终，当入地狱。」又告诸清信士：「凡人持戒，有五功德。何谓为五？一者诸有所求，輙得如愿。二者所有财产，增益无损。三者所往之处，众人敬爱。四者好名善誉，周闻天下。五者身坏命终，必生天上。」

At that time, it was already midnight. He told the believers, "You should return home." The lay believers then followed the Buddha's instruction, circumambulated the Buddha three times, bowed at his feet, and returned.

时，夜已半，告诸信士，宜各还归。诸清信士即承佛教，遶佛三匝，礼足而归。

At that time, when the night was clear, the World-Honored One went to a quiet place. With his pure and penetrating heavenly eye, he saw that the great gods each had their own land, and the middle and lower gods also had their own land. At that time, the World-Honored One returned to the lecture hall, took his seat, and the World-Honored One knew the time. He asked Ānanda, "Who built this city of Benares?"

尔时，世尊于后夜明相出时，至闲静处，天眼清彻，见诸大天神各封宅地，中神、下神亦封宅地。是时，世尊即还讲堂，就座而坐，世尊知时故问阿难：「谁造此巴陵弗城？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "This was built by the minister Yasha to defend against the Vajjians."

阿难白佛：「此是禹舍大臣所造，以防御跋祇。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "The one who built this city was in accord with the intentions of the gods. I, in the clear night, went to a quiet place and saw with my heavenly eye that the great gods each had their own land, and the middle and lower gods also had their own land. Ānanda! You should know that where the great gods have their land, if people live there, they will be prosperous and happy. The middle gods have their land, and the middle people live there; the lower gods have their land, and the lower people live there. The merit and demerit are each according to where they live. Ānanda! This place is where the virtuous live, where merchants gather, where the laws of the country are true and there is no deception. This city is the best, praised by all directions, and cannot be destroyed. This city will eventually be destroyed by three things: first, great water, second, great fire, third, the people in the middle conspiring with outsiders to destroy the city."

佛告阿难：「造此城者，正得天意，吾于后夜明相出时，至闲静处，以天眼见诸大神天各封宅地，中、下诸神亦封宅地。阿难！当知诸大神天所封宅地，有人居者，安乐炽盛。中神所封，中人所居；下神所封，下人所居。功德多少，各随所止。阿难！此处贤人所居，商贾所集，国法真实，无有欺罔，此城最胜，诸方所推，不可破坏。此城久后若欲坏时，必以三事：一者大水，二者大火，三者中人与外人谋，乃坏此城。」

At that time, the lay believers of Pāvāla prepared offerings throughout the night. When it was time, they informed the Buddha, "The offerings are ready. Please know the time." The lay believers then arranged the offerings, served the food with their own hands, and after the meal, offered water. They then took a small seat and sat in front of the Buddha.

时，巴陵弗诸清信士通夜供办，时到白佛：「食具已办，唯圣知时。」时，清信士即便施设，手自斟酌，食讫行水，别取小床敷在佛前坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One showed him, saying, "Now, this place is where the wise and intelligent live, where many uphold the precepts, cultivate holy life, and delight the good spirits." He then made a wish for him, saying, "Those who are respectable know how to respect, those who are to be served know how to serve, who are generous and inclusive, have a compassionate mind, are praised by the gods, always together with goodness, not together with evil.

尔时，世尊即示之曰：「今汝此处贤智所居，多持戒者，净修梵行，善神欢喜。」即为呪愿：「可敬知敬，可事知事，博施兼爱，有慈愍心，诸天所称，常与善俱，不与恶会。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken the Dharma, rose from his seat, surrounded by the great assembly, and was escorted back. Minister Yasha followed behind the Buddha and thought, "Now, when Shakyamuni Buddha exits this city gate, let this gate be named Gautama Gate. Also, let the place where the Tathāgata crosses the river be named Gautama River." At that time, the World-Honored One left the city of Pāvāla and arrived at the riverbank. At that time, there were many people on the riverbank, some crossing the river by boat, some by raft, and some by floating on logs. At that time, the World-Honored One and the great assembly, like a strong man bending and stretching his arm, suddenly arrived on the other shore. Having seen this meaning, the World-Honored One then spoke a verse:

尔时，世尊为说法已，即从座起，大众围遶，侍送而还。大臣禹舍从佛后行，时，作是念：「今沙门瞿昙出此城门，即名此门为瞿昙门。又观如来所渡河处，即名此处为瞿昙河。」尔时，世尊出巴陵弗城，至于水边，时，水岸上人民众多，中有乘船渡者，或有乘筏，或有乘桴而渡河者。尔时，世尊与诸大众，譬如力士屈伸臂顷，忽至彼岸。世尊观此义已，即说颂曰：

The Buddha is the ferryman of the ocean, the Dharma bridge
crossing the river; The vehicle of the Mahayana path, crossing all
gods and humans. He also unties his own knots, crosses the
shore and attains immortality; He makes all disciples untie their bonds
and attain nirvana.

「佛为海船师，　　法桥渡河津；大乘道之舆，　　一切渡天人。亦为自解结，　　渡岸得升仙；都使诸弟子，　　缚解得涅槃。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having traveled from Vārāṇasī, arrived at the village of Kuṇḍaliya and stayed under a tree. He told the bhikṣus, "There are four profound teachings: the noble precepts, the noble concentration, the noble wisdom, and the noble liberation. These teachings are subtle and difficult to understand. Because I and you did not understand them, we have been wandering in saṃsāra for a long time." At that time, the World-Honored One, having seen this meaning, spoke a verse:

尔时，世尊从跋祇游行至拘利村，在一林下告诸比丘：「有四深法：一曰圣戒，二曰圣定，三曰圣慧，四曰圣解脱。此法微妙，难可解知，我及汝等，不晓了故，久在生死，流转无穷。」尔时，世尊观此义已，即说颂曰：

The noble precepts, concentration, wisdom, and liberation are supreme; only the Buddha can discern them
He liberates them from suffering and transforms them, causing them to sever the habits of saṃsāra.

「戒、定、慧解上，　　唯佛能分别；离苦而化彼，　　令断生死习。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed in the village of Kuṇḍaliya as he wished, told Ānanda to go together to the village of Nāḍī. Ānanda accepted the instruction, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, attended and followed the World-Honored One. Passing through Vārāṇasī, he arrived at the village of Nāḍī and stayed at the place of the drum.

尔时，世尊于拘利村随宜住已、告阿难俱诣那陀村。阿难受教，即著衣持钵，与大众俱侍从世尊，路由跋祇，到那陀村，止揵椎处。

At that time, Ānanda was in a quiet place, silently reflecting, "The twelve householders of the village of Nāḍī: the first is called Gaggara, the second is called Galiṅga, the third is called Vigaṭa, the fourth is called Galiṣu, the fifth is called Chālu, the sixth is called Bhayālu, the seventh is called Bhadralu, the eighth is called Subhadralu, the ninth is called Tārīśa, the tenth is called Subāḍīśa, the eleventh is called Yasu, and the twelfth is called Yasuḍalu. When these people die, where will they be reborn? There are also fifty people who have died, and five hundred people who have died. Where will they be reborn?" Having thought this, he rose from the quiet place and went to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I was in a quiet place, silently reflecting, 'When the twelve householders of the village of Nāḍī, Gaggara and the others, die, and there are also fifty people who have died, and five hundred people who have died, where will they be reborn?' Please explain this for me."

尔时，阿难在闲静处，默自思惟：「此那陀村十二居士：一名伽伽罗，二名伽陵伽，三名毘伽陀，四名伽利输，五名遮楼，六名婆耶楼，七名婆头楼，八名薮婆头楼，九名陀梨舍［少／兔］，十名薮达利舍［少／兔］，十一名耶输，十二名耶输多楼。此诸人等，今者命终，为生何处？复有五十人命终，又复有五百人命终，斯生何处？」作是念已，从静处起至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐，白佛言：「世尊！我向静处，默自思惟：『此那陀村十二居士伽伽罗等命终，复有五十人命终，又有五百人命终，斯生何处？』唯愿解说。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "The twelve people like Gaggara and the others have severed the five lower fetters and will be reborn in the heavens, where they will attain parinirvāṇa and not return to this world. The fifty people who have died have severed the three fetters of desire, anger, and delusion, and have become srota-āpannas. They will return to this world once more and end the root of suffering. The five hundred people who have died have severed the three fetters and become srota-āpannas. They will not fall into evil destinies, will definitely attain the Way, and will be reborn seven times, ending the cycle of suffering. Ānanda! Birth is followed by death, this is the norm of the world. Why be surprised by this? If each and every person who dies comes to ask me, wouldn't that be a disturbance?"

佛告阿难：「伽伽罗等十二人，断五下分结，命终生天，于彼即般涅槃，不复还此。五十人命终者，断除三结，婬、怒、痴薄，得斯陀含，还来此世，尽于苦本。五百人命终者，断除三结，得须陀洹，不堕恶趣，必定成道，往来七生，尽于苦际。阿难！夫生有死，自世之常，此何足恠？若一一人死，来问我者，非扰乱耶？」

Ānanda replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! It would indeed be a disturbance."

阿难答曰：「信尔，世尊！实是扰乱。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Now I will explain the Dharma mirror for you, so that the noble disciples will know where they are reborn. They will end the three evil paths, attain the state of srota-āpanna, not be reborn more than seven times, and definitely end the cycle of suffering. They will also be able to explain this to others. Ānanda! The Dharma mirror means that the noble disciples attain unshakable faith, joyfully believing in the Buddha, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, who is fully endowed with the ten titles. They joyfully believe in the Dharma: it is truly subtle, freely spoken, without time constraints, and shows the path to nirvāṇa, which is practiced by the wise. They joyfully believe in the Saṃgha: they are well harmonized, their practice is straightforward, without flattery, they have accomplished the fruits of the path, and are harmonious above and below, fully endowed with the Dharma body. They aspire to the state of srota-āpanna and attain it, aspire to the state of sakṛd-āgāmin and attain it, aspire to the state of anāgāmin and attain it, aspire to the state of arhat and attain it. The four pairs and eight categories are called the noble assembly of the Tathāgata, which is very worthy of respect and is the field of merit in the world. They believe in the noble precepts: they are pure and undefiled, without any flaws or leaks, practiced by the wise, and attain samādhi concentration. Ānanda! This is the Dharma mirror, which allows the noble disciples to know where they are reborn. They will end the three evil paths, attain the state of srota-āpanna, not be reborn more than seven times, and definitely end the cycle of suffering. They will also be able to explain this to others."

佛告阿难：「今当为汝说于法镜，使圣弟子知所生处。三恶道尽，得须陀洹，不过七生，必尽苦际，亦能为他说如是事。阿难！法镜者，谓圣弟子得不坏信，欢喜信佛、如来、无所著、等正觉，十号具足。欢喜信法：真正微妙，自恣所说，无有时节，示涅槃道，智者所行。欢喜信僧，善共和同，所行质直，无有谀谄，道果成就，上下和顺，法身具足。向须陀洹、得须陀洹，向斯陀含、得斯陀含，向阿那含、得阿那含，向阿罗汉、得阿罗汉，四双八辈，是谓如来贤圣之众，甚可恭敬，世之福田。信贤圣戒：清净无秽，无有缺漏，明哲所行，获三昧定。阿难！是为法镜，使圣弟子知所生处，三恶道尽，得须陀洹，不过七生，必尽苦际，亦能为他说如是事。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed as he wished, told Ānanda to go together to the country of Vaiśālī. He then accepted the instruction, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, followed the World-Honored One. Passing through Vṛji, they arrived at Vaiśālī and sat under a tree. There was a prostitute named Ambapālī who heard that the Buddha had come to Vaiśālī with his disciples and was sitting under a tree. She immediately adorned a jeweled carriage and wanted to go to where the Buddha was to pay homage and make offerings. Before she arrived, she saw from afar the World-Honored One's face and appearance, his extraordinary faculties, and his fully endowed marks, like the moon among the stars. Seeing this, she was delighted. She got down from the carriage and walked forward, gradually approaching the Buddha. She bowed her head at his feet and sat to one side.

尔时，世尊随宜住已，告阿难俱诣毘舍离国。即受教行，著衣持钵，与大众俱侍从世尊，路由跋祇，到毘舍离，坐一树下。有一婬女，名庵婆婆梨，闻佛将诸弟子来至毘舍离，坐一树下，即严驾宝车，欲往诣佛所礼拜供养。未至之间，遥见世尊颜貌端正，诸根特异，相好备足，如星中月。见已欢喜，下车步进，渐至佛所，头面礼足，却坐一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One gradually taught the Dharma for her, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting her. Having heard the Buddha's teaching, she gave rise to a joyful mind and said to the Buddha, "From today onward, I take refuge in the Three Jewels. I only wish to be allowed to be a female lay devotee in the true Dharma, and to observe the precepts of not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, not speaking falsely, and not drinking alcohol for the rest of my life." She also said to the Buddha, "I only wish that the World-Honored One and the disciples would accept my invitation to stay in my garden for the night." At that time, the World-Honored One silently accepted. Seeing the Buddha's silent consent, she immediately rose from her seat, bowed her head at his feet, circumambulated the Buddha, and returned.

尔时，世尊渐为说法，示教利喜。闻佛所说，发欢喜心，即白佛言：「从今日始，归依三尊，唯愿听许于正法中为优婆夷，尽此形寿，不杀、不盗、不邪婬、不妄语、不饮酒。」又白佛言：「唯愿世尊及诸弟子明受我请，即于今暮止宿我园。」尔时，世尊默然受之。女见佛默然许可，即从座起，头面礼足，遶佛而归。

Not long after she left, the Buddha told Ānanda, "I will go with you to that garden to see it."

其去未久，佛告阿难：「当与汝等诣彼园观。」

He replied, "Yes." The Buddha then rose from his seat, took his robe and bowl, and went with the thousand two hundred and fifty disciples to the garden.

对曰：「唯然。」佛即从座起，摄持衣钵，与众弟子千二百五十人俱诣彼园。

At that time, the Licchavis of Vaiśālī heard that the Buddha was staying at the Āmrapālī Grove. They immediately prepared five-colored jeweled carriages, some riding blue carriages and blue horses, with blue robes, canopies, banners, and attendants, and the five-colored carriages and horses were all the same. At that time, the five hundred Licchavis were all dressed the same and wanted to go to see the Buddha. Āmrapālī took leave of the Buddha and returned home. On the way, she encountered the Licchavis. At that time, the carriages were galloping and collided with the jeweled carriages, damaging the banners and canopies, and did not avoid the road. The Licchavis reproached her, saying, "On what authority do you ride without avoiding the road, colliding with our carriages and damaging our banners and canopies?"

时，毘舍离诸隷车辈，闻佛在庵婆婆梨园中止住，即便严驾五色宝车，或乘青车青马，衣、盖、幢幡、官属皆青，五色车马，皆亦如是。时，五百隷车服色尽同，欲往诣佛，庵婆婆梨辞佛还家，中路逢诸隷车。时，车行［马＊奔］疾，与彼宝车共相钩拨，损折幢盖而不避道，隷车责曰：「汝恃何势，行不避道，冲拨我车，损折麾葢？」

They replied, "Noble ones! I have already invited the Buddha to have a meal tomorrow. I am returning home to prepare the offerings, so I am going quickly and cannot avoid you."

报曰：「诸贵！我已请佛明日设食，归家供办，是以行速，无容相避。」

The Licchavis then said to the woman, "Forget about your invitation. I will give you a hundred thousand taels of gold if you give it to me first."

诸隷车即语女曰：「且置汝请，当先与我，我当与汝百千两金？」

The woman replied, "The invitation has already been made. I cannot give it to you."

女寻答曰：「先请已定，不得相与。」

The Licchavis then said to the woman, "I will give you sixteen times a hundred thousand taels of gold. Will you let me go first?"

时，诸隷车又语女曰：「我更与汝十六倍百千两金，必使我先？」

The woman still refused, saying, "My invitation is already set. It cannot be so."

女犹不肯：「我请已定，不可尔也。」

The Licchavis then said to the woman, "I will now give you half the wealth of the country. Will you let me go first?"

时，诸隷车又语女曰：「我今与尔中分国财，可先与我？」

The woman replied, "Even if I were given all the wealth of the country, I would not accept it. The reason is that the Buddha is staying in my garden and has already accepted my invitation. This matter is already settled and cannot be changed."

女又报曰：「设使举国财宝，我犹不取；所以然者，佛住我园，先受我请，此事已了，终不相与。」

The Licchavis each shook their hands and sighed, "Now, because of this woman, we have missed out on our first merit." They then went forward and went straight to the garden.

诸隷车等各振手叹咤：「今由斯女阙我初福。」即便前进径诣彼园。

At that time, the World Honored One saw from afar the five hundred Licchavis, with their carriages and horses numbering in the tens of thousands, filling the road as they came. He told the bhikṣus, "Do you wish to know the heavenly gardens and pavilions of the Trayastriṃśa Heaven, their demeanor and adornments, which are no different from this? You bhikṣus should restrain your minds and be fully endowed with proper deportment. How do bhikṣus restrain their minds? Here, bhikṣus observe the body internally, diligently and tirelessly, mindful and undistracted, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; they observe the body externally, diligently and tirelessly, mindful and undistracted, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; they observe the body internally and externally, diligently and tirelessly, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow. The same is true for observing feelings, mind, and dharmas. How are bhikṣus fully endowed with proper deportment? Here, bhikṣus know when to walk and when to stop, they look left and right, bend and stretch, bow and raise their heads, they hold their robes and alms bowl, eat and drink medicine, not losing the proper measure, skillfully employing means to remove obstructions, they walk, stand, sit, lie down, wake up, speak, and remain silent, restraining their minds and not being distracted. This is called being fully endowed with proper deportment by bhikṣus."

尔时，世尊遥见五百隷车，车马数万，填道而来，告诸比丘：「汝等欲知忉利诸天游戏园观，威仪容饰，与此无异。汝等比丘！当自摄心，具诸威仪。云何比丘自摄其心？于是比丘内身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；内外身观，精勤不懈，舍世贪忧。受、意、法观，亦复如是。云何比丘具诸威仪？于是比丘可行知行，可止知止，左右顾视，屈伸俯仰，摄持衣钵，食饮汤药，不失宜则，善设方便，除去荫盖，行住坐卧，觉寤语默，摄心不乱，是谓比丘具诸威仪。」

At that time, the five hundred Licchavis went to the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, wanting to go to where the Buddha was. They got off their carriages and walked forward, bowing their heads to the Buddha's feet, and then sat to one side. The Tathāgata was seated, his radiant appearance uniquely prominent, overshadowing the great assembly. It was like the autumn moon, or like the clear sky and earth, pure without any dust or obstruction, with the sun shining alone in the sky. At that time, the five hundred Licchavis surrounded and attended the Buddha as he sat in the assembly, his radiant appearance uniquely bright. At that time, there was a brāhman named Pāyāsi in the assembly. He immediately rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms towards the Buddha, and praised in verse:

尔时，五百隷车往至庵婆婆梨园，欲到佛所，下车步进，头面礼足，却坐一面。如来在座，光相独显，蔽诸大众，譬如秋月，又如天地清明，净无尘翳，日在虚空，光明独照。尔时，五百隷车围遶侍坐，佛于众中，光相独明。是时，坐中有一梵志名曰并饩，即从座起，偏袒右臂，右膝著地，叉手向佛，以偈赞曰：

The kings of Magadha and Anga have obtained great benefits. They are
armored with precious jewels. The World-Honored One has appeared in their land
His majestic virtue moves the trichiliocosm. His fame is as lofty as
the snow mountains. Like a lotus flower in full bloom, his fragrance
is extremely subtle. Now seeing the Buddha's radiance, it is like the
rising sun. Like the moon traveling through the sky, without any clouds
or obstructions. The World-Honored One is also like this. His radiance illuminates
the world. Observing the Tathāgata's wisdom is like seeing a torch in
the dark. He bestows the eyes of wisdom to all, resolving all
doubts and uncertainties.

「摩竭鸯伽王，　　为快得善利，身被宝珠铠，　　世尊出其土。威德动三千，　　名显如雪山，如莲花开敷，　　香气甚微妙。今覩佛光明，　　如日之初出，如月游虚空，　　无有诸云翳。世尊亦如是，　　光照于世间，观如来智慧，　　犹暗覩锭镣，施众以明眼，　　决了诸疑惑。」

At that time, the five hundred Licchavis, having heard this verse, again said to Pāyāsi, "Please speak again."

时，五百隷车闻此偈已，复告并饩：「汝可重说。」

At that time, Pāyāsi repeated the verse in front of the Buddha. When the five hundred Licchavis heard the repeated verse, they each took off their jeweled garments and offered them to Pāyāsi. Pāyāsi then offered the jeweled garments to the Tathāgata. The Buddha accepted them out of compassion for them.

尔时，并饩即于佛前再三重说。时，五百隷车闻重说偈已，各脱宝衣，以施并饩，并饩即以宝衣奉上如来，佛愍彼故，即为纳受。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the Licchavis of Vaiśālī, "There are five treasures in the world that are very difficult to obtain. What are the five? First, the appearance of a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One in the world is very difficult to obtain. Second, those who can expound the true Dharma of the Tathāgata are rare. Third, those who can believe and understand the Dharma expounded by the Tathāgata are rare. Fourth, those who can accomplish the Dharma expounded by the Tathāgata are rare. Fifth, those who can repay kindness when rescued from danger are rare. These are called the five treasures that are difficult to obtain."

尔时，世尊告毘舍离诸隷车曰：「世有五宝甚为难得。何等为五？一者如来、至真出现于世，甚为难得。二者如来正法能演说者，此人难得。三者如来演法能信解者，此人难得。四者如来演法能成就者，此人难得。五者崄危救厄知反复者，此人难得。是谓五宝为难得也。」

At that time, the five hundred Licchavis, having heard the Buddha's instruction, exhortation, benefit, and joy, said to the Buddha, "We only wish that the World-Honored One and the disciples would accept our invitation tomorrow!"

时，五百隷车闻佛示教利喜已，即白佛言：「唯愿世尊及诸弟子明受我请！」

The Buddha told the Licchavis, "You have already invited me, and I have already received your offerings. The girl Anupamā has already invited me first."

佛告隷车：「卿已请我，我今便为得供养已，庵婆婆梨女先已请讫。」

At that time, the five hundred Licchavis, having heard that the girl Anupamā had already invited the Buddha first, each shook their hands and said, "We wanted to make offerings to the Tathāgata, but now this girl has already taken precedence over us." They then rose from their seats, bowed their heads to the Buddha, circumambulated him three times, and returned to their own homes.

时，五百隷车闻庵婆婆梨女已先请佛，各振手而言：「吾欲供养如来，而今此女已夺我先。」即从座起，头面礼佛，遶佛三匝，各自还归。

At that time, the girl Anupamā prepared various offerings that night. The next day, when the time came, the World-Honored One, together with the 1,250 bhikṣus, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and surrounded him in front and behind. He went to the place of the invitation and took his seat. At that time, the girl Anupamā immediately set out the finest food and offered it to the Buddha and the saṃgha. After the meal, the bowls were removed and the tables were cleared. Then, the girl took a golden pitcher in her hand, served the water for washing, and said to the Buddha, "Among all the gardens and parks in this city of Vaiśālī, my garden is the most excellent. I now offer this garden to the Tathāgata. Please accept it out of compassion for me."

时，庵婆婆梨女即于其夜种种供办，明日时到，世尊即与千二百五十比丘整衣持钵，前后围遶，诣彼请所，就座而坐。时，庵婆婆梨女即设上馔，供佛及僧。食讫去钵，并除机案。时，女手执金瓶，行澡水毕，前白佛言：「此毘耶离城所有园观，我园最胜，今以此园贡上如来，哀愍我故，愿垂纳受。」

The Buddha told the girl, "You can offer this garden to the Buddha and the saṃgha of the four directions. The reason is that the Tathāgata's possessions, such as gardens, forests, dwellings, robes, and the six objects, even the māras, Śakra, Brahmā, and the gods with great supernatural powers, are not able to accept these offerings." At that time, the girl accepted the instruction and immediately offered the garden to the Buddha and the saṃgha of the four directions. The Buddha, out of compassion for her, accepted it and spoke a verse:

佛告女曰：「汝可以此园施佛为首及招提僧。所以然者？如来所有园林、房舍、衣钵六物，正使诸魔、释、梵、大神力天，无有能堪受此供者。」时，女受教，即以此园施佛为首及招提僧。佛愍彼故，即为受之，而说偈言：

Building stupas and monasteries, offering gardens and fruits for coolness, building bridges
and boats to ferry people, providing water and grass in the wilderness
and offering halls and pavilions - their merit increases day and night
Those who are pure in their precepts will surely reach the good realms.

「起塔立精舍，　　园果施清凉；桥船以渡人，　　旷野施水草。及以堂阁施，　　其福日夜增；戒具清净者，　　彼必到善方。」

At that time, the girl Amrapālī took a small seat and sat in front of the Buddha. The Buddha gradually taught her the Dharma, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting her with discussions on giving, discussions on precepts, discussions on rebirth in the heavens, and discussions on how desire is a great affliction, impure and unclean, and how the upper defilements are obstructive, and how liberation is supreme. At that time, the World-Honored One knew that the girl's mind was soft, gentle, and easily influenced, and in accordance with the methods of the Buddhas, he spoke to her about the noble truth of suffering, the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering.

时，庵婆婆梨女取一小床于佛前坐，佛渐为说法，示教利喜：施论、戒论、生天之论，欲为大患，秽污不净，上漏为碍，出要为上。尔时，世尊知彼女意柔软和悦，荫盖微薄，易可开化，如诸佛法，即为彼女说苦圣谛，苦集、苦灭、苦出要谛。

At that time, the girl Amrapālī's faith was pure, like a clean white cloth that is easy to be dyed. She immediately removed dust and defilements from her seat, gave rise to the Dharma eye of the Dharma, saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, and was determined and rightly established, not falling into evil paths, and attained fearlessness. She said to the Buddha, "I now take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, and take refuge in the Sangha." She repeated this three times. "I only wish that the Tathāgata would allow me to be a female lay devotee in the true Dharma! From now on, for as long as I live, I will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol." At that time, after the girl had received the five precepts from the Buddha, she abandoned her previous practices, removed defilements, and immediately rose from her seat, paid homage to the Buddha, and departed.

时，庵婆婆梨女信心清净，譬如净洁白毡易为受色，即于座上远尘离垢，诸法法眼生，见法得法，决定正住，不堕恶道，成就无畏，而白佛言：「我今归依佛，归依法，归依僧。」如是再三。「唯愿如来听我于正法中为优婆夷！自今已后，尽寿不杀、不盗、不邪婬、不欺、不饮酒。」时，彼女从佛受五戒已，舍本所习，秽垢消除，即从座起，礼佛而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed in Vaiśālī as he wished, said to Ānanda, "You should all prepare yourselves. I wish to go to the bamboo grove forest."

尔时，世尊于毘舍离，随宜住已，告阿难言：「汝等皆严，吾欲诣竹林丛。」

He replied, "Yes." He then put on his robe and took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, he followed the World-Honored One on the road to Vṛji and arrived at the bamboo grove.

对曰：「唯然。」即严衣钵，与大众侍从世尊，路由跋祇，至彼竹林。

At that time, there was a brāhman named Viśāradha. Hearing that the Buddha and the great assembly had come to this bamboo grove, he silently thought to himself, "This Śramaṇa Gautama is renowned and his virtues are spread far and wide in the four directions. He is endowed with the ten epithets. Among the gods, Śakra, Brahmā, Māra, and Māra's followers, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas, he has personally realized and teaches the Dharma. His words, whether superior, middling, or inferior, are all truly genuine, profound in meaning, and complete in the practice of purity. Such a true person should be visited and seen.

时，有婆罗门名毘沙陀耶，闻佛与诸大众诣此竹林，默自思念：「此沙门瞿昙，名德流布，闻于四方，十号具足，于诸天、释、梵、魔、若魔、天、沙门、婆罗门中，自身作证，为他说法，上中下言，皆悉真正，义味深奥，梵行具足。如此真人，宜往瞻覩。」

At that time, the brāhman came out from the bamboo grove, went to the World-Honored One, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. The World-Honored One gradually taught the Dharma for him, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him. Having heard it, the brāhman was delighted and immediately invited the World-Honored One and the great assembly to a meal the next day. At that time, the Buddha silently accepted the invitation. The brāhman, knowing that it was approved, immediately rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha, and returned. He then prepared food and drink that very night. The next day, when the time came, the Buddha knew the time.

时，婆罗门出于竹丛，往诣世尊，问讯讫，一面坐，世尊渐为说法，示教利喜。婆罗门闻已欢喜，即请世尊及诸大众明日舍食。时，佛默然受请。婆罗门知已许可，即从座起，遶佛而归。即于其夜，供设饮食。明日时到，唯圣知时。

At that time, the World-Honored One put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and went to his house surrounded by the great assembly. He took his seat. At that time, the brāhman prepared various delicious dishes to offer to the Buddha and the Sangha. After the meal, the bowls were put away, and water was served for washing. He then took a small bench and sat in front of the Buddha. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse for the brāhman:

尔时，世尊著衣持钵，大众围遶往诣彼舍，就座而坐。时，婆罗门设种种甘馔，供佛及僧。食讫去钵，行澡水毕，取一小床于佛前坐。尔时，世尊为婆罗门而作颂曰：

If one gives food, drink, clothing, and bedding to those
who uphold the precepts, one will attain great fruits. This
is a true companion, following from beginning to end. Wherever
one goes, it is like a shadow following a form
Therefore, plant goodness as provisions for future lives. Merit is
the foundation, and beings find peace. Merit is protected by
the gods, and one's actions are not perilous. One is
not harmed in life, and at death, one ascends to heaven.

「若以饮食，　　衣服卧具，施持戒人，　　则获大果。此为真伴，　　终始相随，所至到处，　　如影随形。是故种善，　　为后世粮，福为根基，　　众生以安。福为天护，　　行不危崄，生不遭难，　　死则上天。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke subtle and wonderful Dharma to the brāhman, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him. After rising from his seat, he departed. At that time, in that country, grain was scarce and famine-stricken, and begging was difficult to obtain. The Buddha told Ānanda, "Order all the bhikṣus in this country to gather at the Dharma hall."

尔时，世尊为婆罗门说微妙法，示教利喜已，从座而去。于时彼土谷贵饥馑，乞求难得，佛告阿难：「勅此国内现诸比丘尽集讲堂。」

He replied, "Yes." He then accepted the instruction and proclaimed it far and wide, gathering at the Dharma hall.

对曰：「唯然。」即承教旨，宣令远近普集讲堂。

At that time, the great assembly of the country had all gathered. Ānanda said to the Buddha, "The great assembly has already gathered. The Sage knows the time."

是时，国内大众皆集，阿难白佛言：「大众已集，唯圣知时。」

At that time, the World-Honored One rose from his seat, went to the Dharma hall, took his seat, and addressed the bhikṣus, "This land is experiencing a famine, and it is difficult to beg for alms. You should all go to Viśālī and Vaiśālī according to your knowledge and spend the summer retreat there, so that you will not be in want. I will stay here alone with Ānanda for the summer retreat. The reason is that I fear there will be a shortage." At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the instruction and left. The Buddha stayed alone with Ānanda.

尔时，世尊即从座起，诣于讲堂，就座而坐，告诸比丘：「此土饥馑，乞求难得，汝等宜各分部，随所知识，诣毘舍离及越祇国，于彼安居，可以无乏。吾独与阿难于此安居。所以然者？恐有短乏。」是时，诸比丘受教即行，佛与阿难独留。

Later, during the summer retreat, the Buddha's body became ill, and he was in great pain. The Buddha thought to himself, "Now that I am ill and in great pain, and all my disciples are not here, if I were to enter parinirvāṇa, it would not be appropriate. I should now diligently exert myself and prolong my life."

于后夏安居中，佛身疾生，举体皆痛，佛自念言：「我今疾生，举身痛甚，而诸弟子悉皆不在，若取涅槃，则非我宜，今当精勤自力以留寿命。」

At that time, the World-Honored One emerged from the quiet room and sat in a cool place. Ānanda saw this and quickly went to him, saying, "Now, looking at your face, it seems that your illness has worsened."

尔时，世尊于静室出，坐清凉处。阿难见已，速疾往诣，而白佛言：「今观尊颜，疾如有损。」

Ānanda said again, "World-Honored One, I am worried and anxious about your illness. I am confused and do not know which direction to go. Although my breath has not yet ceased, I am still slightly aware. I silently think: 'The Tathāgata has not yet entered parinirvāṇa, the eye of the world has not yet disappeared, and the great Dharma has not yet been damaged. Why does he not now give instructions to the disciples?'"

阿难又言：「世尊有疾，我心惶惧，忧结荒迷，不识方面，气息未绝，犹少醒悟。默思：『如来未即灭度，世眼未灭，大法未损，何故今者不有教令于众弟子乎？』」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Does the Sangha have any needs from me? If someone says, 'I uphold the Sangha, I embrace the Sangha,' then that person should have instructions for the Sangha. The Tathāgata does not say, 'I uphold the Sangha, I embrace the Sangha.' How could he have instructions for the Sangha? Ānanda! The Dharma I have taught is complete, both internally and externally. I never claim to have realized and penetrated it. I am already old, nearly eighty years old. It is like an old cart that can be repaired with skillful means to reach its destination. My body is also like this. With skillful means and effort, I can prolong my life a little. I endure this pain with diligence and do not think of anything. When I enter the state of non-perception, my body is at peace and free from affliction. Therefore, Ānanda! You should burn brightly, burn brightly in the Dharma, do not burn brightly elsewhere; you should take refuge in yourself, take refuge in the Dharma, do not take refuge elsewhere. How does one burn brightly, burn brightly in the Dharma, do not burn brightly elsewhere; take refuge in oneself, take refuge in the Dharma, do not take refuge elsewhere? Ānanda! A bhikṣu observes the internal body diligently without slacking, remembering without forgetting, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow; observes the external body, observes the internal and external body, diligently without slacking, remembering without forgetting, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow. The same is true for observing feelings, mind, and dharmas. This is called, Ānanda! burning brightly, burning brightly in the Dharma, do not burn brightly elsewhere; take refuge in oneself, take refuge in the Dharma, do not take refuge elsewhere."

佛告阿难：「众僧于我有所须耶？若有自言：『我持众僧，我摄众僧。』斯人于众应有教命，如来不言：『我持于众，我摄于众。』岂当于众有教令乎？阿难！我所说法，内外已讫，终不自称所见通达。吾已老矣，年且八十。譬如故车，方便修治得有所至。吾身亦然，以方便力得少留寿，自力精进，忍此苦痛，不念一切想，入无想定，时，我身安隐，无有恼患。是故，阿难！当自炽燃，炽燃于法，勿他炽燃；当自归依，归依于法，勿他归依。云何自炽燃，炽燃于法，勿他炽燃；当自归依，归依于法，勿他归依？阿难！比丘观内身精勤无懈，忆念不忘，除世贪忧；观外身、观内外身，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，除世贪忧。受、意、法观，亦复如是。是谓，阿难！自炽燃，炽燃于法，勿他炽燃；当自归依，归依于法，勿他归依。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "After my parinirvāṇa, those who can practice this Dharma will truly be my disciples, the foremost learners."

佛告阿难：「吾灭度后，能有修行此法者，则为真我弟子第一学者。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Let us go together to the Chebāro Pagoda."

佛告阿难：「俱至遮婆罗塔。」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One."

对曰：「唯然。」

The Tathāgata then rose, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, went to the foot of a tree, and said to Ānanda, "Spread out a seat. I have a backache and wish to rest here."

如来即起，著衣持钵，诣一树下，告阿难：「敷座，吾患背痛，欲于此止。」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One." He then immediately spread out a seat.

对曰：「唯然。」寻即敷座。

The Tathāgata sat down, and Ānanda spread out a small seat in front of the Buddha and sat down. The Buddha told Ānanda, "Those who cultivate the four bases of supernormal power, cultivate them extensively, always remember them, and have them in mind, can live for a kalpa and more. Ānanda, the Buddha has already extensively cultivated the four bases of supernormal power, remembered them, and has them in mind. The Tathāgata can stay for a kalpa and more, to remove the darkness for the world, to benefit many, and to bring peace to gods and humans."

如来坐已，阿难敷一小座于佛前坐。佛告阿难：「诸有修四神足，多修习行，常念不忘，在意所欲，可得不死一劫有余。阿难！佛四神足已多修行，专念不忘，在意所欲，如来可止一劫有余，为世除冥，多所饶益，天人获安。」

At that time, Ānanda remained silent and did not respond. This happened three times, and he was still silent. At that time, Ānanda was obscured by Māra and did not understand. The Buddha showed three signs but he did not ask.

尔时，阿难默然不对，如是再三，又亦默然。是时阿难为魔所蔽，曚曚不悟，佛三现相而不知请。

The Buddha told Ānanda, "It is time for you to know." Ānanda received the Buddha's intention, immediately rose from his seat, paid homage to the Buddha, and left. Not far from the Buddha, under a tree, he quietly contemplated.

佛告阿难：「宜知是时。」阿难承佛意旨，即从座起，礼佛而去。去佛不远，在一树下静意思惟。

Not long after, Māra Pāpīyas came and said to the Buddha, "The Buddha's intention is to be free from desires. It is now the right time for parinirvāṇa. You should quickly enter nirvāṇa."

其间未久，时，魔波旬来白佛：「佛意无欲，可般涅槃，今正是时，宜速灭度。」

The Buddha told Pāpīyas, "Stop! Stop! I know the time myself. The Tathāgata has not yet entered nirvāṇa. I need to gather my bhikṣus who can self-discipline, be courageous and fearless, reach the place of peace and tranquility, attain their own benefits, become teachers for others, expound the teachings, and reveal the meaning of the phrases. If there are different arguments, they can subdue them with the true Dharma. Also, they can personally realize it through supernatural transformations. Such disciples have not yet gathered. Also, the bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās are all like this, and have not yet gathered. Now I must widely cultivate the Brahma-conduct, expound the factors of enlightenment, and let all gods and humans see the supernatural transformations."

佛告波旬：「且止！且止！我自知时。如来今者未取涅槃，须我诸比丘集，又能自调，勇捍无怯，到安隐处，逮得己利，为人导师，演布经教，显于句义。若有异论，能以正法而降伏之。又以神变，自身作证。如是弟子皆悉未集。又诸比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷，普皆如是，亦复未集。今者要当广于梵行，演布觉意，使诸天人普见神变。」

At that time, Māra Pāpīyas again said to the Buddha, "When the Buddha first attained perfect enlightenment under the Ajapāla-niagrodha tree by the Nairañjanā River in Uruvilvā, I went to the World-Honored One and requested the Tathāgata to enter parinirvāṇa, saying, 'Now is the right time, you should quickly enter nirvāṇa.' At that time, the Tathāgata replied to me, 'Stop! Stop! Pāpīyas! I know the time myself. The Tathāgata has not yet entered nirvāṇa. I need to gather my disciples, and so on, until the gods and humans see the supernatural transformations, then I will enter nirvāṇa.' Now the Buddha's disciples have gathered, and so on, until the gods and humans have seen the supernatural transformations. Now is the right time. Why not enter nirvāṇa?"

时，魔波旬复白佛言：「佛昔于郁鞞罗尼连禅水边，阿游波尼俱律树下初成正觉，我时至世尊所，劝请如来可般涅槃：『今正是时，宜速灭度。』尔时，如来即报我言：『止！止！波旬！我自知时，如来今者未取涅槃，须我诸弟子集，乃至天人见神变化乃取灭度。』佛今弟子已集，乃至天人见神变化，今正是时，何不灭度？」

The Buddha said, "Stop! Stop! Pāpīyas! The Buddha knows the time himself. He will not stay for long. After three months, he will enter parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees in the Śāla Grove in his birthplace of Kuśinagara." At that time, Māra thought, "The Buddha does not speak falsely. He will definitely enter parinirvāṇa now." He was overjoyed and suddenly disappeared.

佛言：「止！止！波旬！佛自知时不久住也，是后三月，于本生处拘尸那竭娑罗园双树间，当取灭度。」时，魔即念：「佛不虚言，今必灭度。」欢喜踊跃，忽然不现。

Not long after Māra left, the Buddha entered a state of concentration at the Caṇḍāla Shrine and extended his lifespan. At that time, the earth shook greatly, and the people of the country were all frightened, their hair standing on end. The Buddha emitted a great light that illuminated everywhere, even the dark places, so that all beings could see each other. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

魔去未久，佛即于遮婆罗塔，定意三昧，舍命住寿。当此之时，地大震动，举国人民莫不惊怖，衣毛为竖，佛放大光，彻照无穷，幽冥之处，莫不蒙明，各得相见。尔时，世尊以偈颂曰：

Between existence and non-existence, I now abandon existence; Concentrating
internally in samādhi, like a bird leaving its egg.

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda was startled and his hair stood on end. He quickly went to the Buddha, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stepped back to one side, and said to the Buddha, "Amazing, World-Honored One! The earth shook so greatly. What is the cause and condition for this?"

尔时，贤者阿难心惊毛竖，疾行诣佛，头面礼足，却住一面，白佛言：「恠哉！世尊！地动乃尔，是何因缘？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "There are eight causes and conditions for the shaking of the earth. What are the eight? The earth rests on water, the water rests on wind, and the wind rests on space. Sometimes a great wind arises in space, which disturbs the great water, and when the great water is disturbed, the earth shakes. This is the first. Furthermore, Ānanda! Sometimes a monk or nun who has attained the Way, or a great deity, observes that the nature of water is abundant and the nature of earth is scarce. Wishing to test their power, the earth shakes. This is the second. Furthermore, Ānanda! When a beginning bodhisattva descends from Tuṣita Heaven into his mother's womb, with focused mindfulness and non-disorder, the earth shakes. This is the third. Furthermore, Ānanda! When a bodhisattva is born from his mother's right side, with focused mindfulness and non-disorder, the earth shakes. This is the fourth. Furthermore, Ānanda! When a bodhisattva first attains unsurpassed perfect enlightenment, at that time the earth shakes greatly. This is the fifth. Furthermore, Ānanda! When a Buddha first attains enlightenment and turns the unsurpassed wheel of the Dharma, which cannot be turned by Māra, Māra's followers, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, gods, humans, or worldly people, the earth shakes. This is the sixth. Furthermore, Ānanda! When a Buddha's teachings are about to end, with focused mindfulness and non-disorder, wishing to abandon his life, the earth shakes. This is the seventh. Furthermore, Ānanda! When a Tathāgata enters parinirvāṇa in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder, the earth shakes greatly. This is the eighth. These are the eight causes and conditions that cause the earth to shake greatly." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

佛告阿难：「凡世地动，有八因缘。何等八？夫地在水上，水止于风，风止于空，空中大风有时自起，则大水扰，大水扰则普地动，是为一也。复次，阿难！有时得道比丘、比丘尼及大神尊天，观水性多，观地性少，欲自试力，则普地动，是为二也。复次，阿难！若始菩萨从兜率天降神母胎，专念不乱，地为大动，是为三也。复次，阿难！菩萨始出母胎，从右脇生，专念不乱，则普地动，是为四也。复次，阿难！菩萨初成无上正觉，当于此时，地大震动，是为五也。复次，阿难！佛初成道，转无上法轮，魔、若魔、天、沙门、婆罗门、诸天、世人所不能转，则普地动，是为六也。复次，阿难！佛教将毕，专念不乱，欲舍性命，则普地动，是为七也。复次，阿难！如来于无余涅槃界般涅槃时，地大振动，是为八也。以是八因缘，令地大动。」尔时，世尊即说偈言：

「无上二足尊，　　照世大沙门；阿难请天师，　　地动何因缘？如来演慈音，　　声如迦毘陵；我说汝等听，　　地动之所由。地因水而止，　　水因风而住；若虚空风起，　　则地为大动。比丘比丘尼，　　欲试神足力；山海百草木，　　大地皆震动。释梵诸尊天，　　意欲动于地；山海诸鬼神，　　大地为震动。菩萨二足尊，　　百福相已具；始入母胎时，　　地则为大动。十月处母胎，　　如龙卧茵蓐；初从右脇生，　　时地则大动。佛为童子时，　　消灭使缘缚；成道胜无量，　　地则为大动。升仙转法轮，　　于鹿野苑中；道力降伏魔，　　则地大为动。天魔频来请，　　劝佛般泥洹；佛为舍性命，　　地则为大动。人尊大导师，　　神仙尽后有；难动而取灭，　　时地则大动。净眼说诸缘，　　地动八事动；有此亦有余，　　时地皆震动。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "There are eight assemblies in the world. What are the eight? First, the assembly of kṣatriyas, second, the assembly of brāhmaṇas, third, the assembly of householders, fourth, the assembly of śramaṇas, fifth, the assembly of the Four Heavenly Kings, sixth, the assembly of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, seventh, the assembly of Māra, and eighth, the assembly of Brahmā Heaven. I remember that in the past, I have sat and spoken with the assembly of kṣatriyas countless times. With the power of concentration and diligence, I can manifest myself there. If they have good form, my form surpasses theirs; if they have wonderful sound, my sound surpasses theirs; if they retreat from me, I do not retreat from them; if they can speak, I can also speak; if they cannot, I can also speak." Ānanda! I extensively expound the Dharma for them, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting them. Then I disappear there, and they do not know whether I am a god or a human. In this way, I go to the assembly of Brahmā Heaven countless times, extensively expounding the Dharma for them, but they do not know who I am.

佛告阿难：「世有八众。何谓八？一曰刹利众，二曰婆罗门众，三曰居士众，四曰沙门众，五曰四天王众，六曰忉利天众，七曰魔众，八曰梵天众。我自忆念：『昔者，往来与刹利众坐起言语，不可称数，以精进定力，在所能现。彼有好色，我色胜彼；彼有妙声，我声胜彼；彼辞我退，我不辞彼；彼所能说，我亦能说；彼所不能，我亦能说。』阿难！我广为说法，示教利喜已，即于彼没，彼不知我是天、是人？如是至梵天众，往返无数，广为说法，而莫知我谁？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "How wonderful! World-Honored One! It is unprecedented that you are able to accomplish such things."

阿难白佛言：「甚奇！世尊！未曾有也，乃能成就如是。」

The Buddha said, "Such subtle and wonderful rare Dharmas, Ānanda! How wonderful! How extraordinary! It is unprecedented that only the Tathāgata can accomplish such Dharmas."

佛言：「如是微妙希有之法，阿难！甚奇！甚特！未曾有也，唯有如来能成此法。」

He further told Ānanda, "The Tathāgata knows the arising, abiding, and ceasing of feelings, the arising, abiding, and ceasing of thoughts, and the arising, abiding, and ceasing of perceptions. This is a very wonderful and extraordinary Dharma of the Tathāgata that you should uphold."

又告阿难：「如来能知受起、住、灭，想起、住、灭，观起、住、灭，此乃如来甚奇甚特未曾有法，汝当受持。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "Let us go together to the Fragrant Pagoda, sit under a tree, and spread out our seats."

尔时，世尊告阿难：「俱诣香塔，在一树下，敷座而坐。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "The bhikṣus who are now to the left and right of the Fragrant Pagoda, order them all to gather in the lecture hall."

佛告阿难：「香塔左右现诸比丘，普勅令集讲堂。」

Ānanda received the instruction and proclaimed it to gather them all. Ānanda said to the Buddha, "The great assembly has already gathered. The Sage knows the time."

阿难受教，宣令普集。阿难白佛：「大众已集，唯圣知时。」

At that time, the World-Honored One went to the lecture hall, took his seat, and told the bhikṣus, "You should know that I have personally realized and attained supreme perfect enlightenment through this Dharma, namely: the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernormal power, the four dhyānas, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, and the eightfold noble path. You should all be harmonious and respectful towards this Dharma, without giving rise to disputes. Receive the same teachings, be like water and milk, and diligently study and practice in my Dharma. Encourage and delight each other. Bhikṣus, you should know that I have personally realized and manifested this Dharma for others, namely: the sutra on the thread, the sutra on the verses, the sutra on the prophecies, the sutra on the verses, the sutra on the Dharma phrases, the sutra on the correspondences, the sutra on the past causes, the sutra on the heavenly origins, the sutra on the extensive, the sutra on the unprecedented, the sutra on the analogies, and the sutra on the great teachings. You should all uphold them well, weigh and distinguish them, and practice according to the circumstances. Why is this? Because the Tathāgata will enter parinirvāṇa in three months' time."

尔时，世尊即诣讲堂，就座而坐，告诸比丘：「汝等当知我以此法自身作证，成最正觉，谓：『四念处、四意断、四神足、四禅、五根、五力、七觉意、贤圣八道。』汝等宜当于此法中和同敬顺，勿生诤讼，同一师受，同一水乳，于我法中宜勤受学，共相炽然，共相娱乐。比丘当知我于此法自身作证，布现于彼，谓：『《贯经》、《祇夜经》、《受记经》、《偈经》、《法句经》、《相应经》、《本缘经》、《天本经》、《广经》、《未曾有经》、《证喻经》、《大教经》。』汝等当善受持，称量分别，随事修行。所以者何？如来不久，是后三月当般泥洹。」

When the bhikṣus heard these words, they were all startled, confused, and lost. They threw themselves to the ground, raised their voices and cried out, "How swift! The Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa. How painful! The eye of the world will be extinguished. We will be greatly afflicted by this." Some bhikṣus wept, fell down, rolled about, and wailed uncontrollably, like a snake being cut, writhing and not knowing what to do.

诸比丘闻此语已，皆悉愕然，殒绝迷荒，自投于地，举声大呼曰：「一何驶哉！佛取灭度。一何痛哉！世间眼灭。我等于此，已为长衰。」或有比丘悲泣躃踊，宛转［口＊睪］咷，不能自胜，犹如斩蛇，宛转回遑，莫知所奉。

bhikṣus, with precepts complete. Control your minds, guard your

对曰：「不也。」

He then put on his robe and took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, he followed the World-Honored One on the road to Vṛji and arrived at Ambapālī's Grove in a mountain forest. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke to the great assembly about precepts, concentration, and wisdom. Cultivating precepts leads to concentration, which brings great rewards; cultivating concentration leads to wisdom, which brings great rewards; cultivating wisdom purifies the mind, leading to liberation, and exhausting the three kinds of taints - the outflow of desire, the outflow of existence, and the outflow of ignorance. Having attained liberation, one gives rise to the wisdom of liberation, knowing that birth and death are exhausted, the practice of the Brahma way is established, what was to be done is done, and one will not be reborn again.

佛告阿难俱诣庵婆罗村，即严衣钵，与诸大众侍从世尊，路由跋祇到庵婆罗村，在一山林。尔时，世尊为诸大众说戒、定、慧。修戒获定，得大果报；修定获智，得大果报；修智心净，得等解脱，尽于三漏——欲漏、有漏、无明漏。已得解脱，生解脱智：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不受后有。

At that time, the World-Honored One stayed at Ambapālī's Grove for as long as he wished.

尔时，世尊于庵婆罗村，随宜住已。

The Buddha told Ānanda, "You should all prepare yourselves. We will go to the villages of Campa, Kanthaka, and Vārāṇasī, and also to the city of Benares." Ānanda replied, "Yes, sir!" He then put on his robe and took his alms bowl, and together with the great assembly, he followed the World-Honored One on the road to Vṛji and gradually arrived at the other city. In the north of the city of Benares, they stayed in the Śiśupāvana Forest.

佛告阿难：「汝等皆严！当诣瞻婆村、揵茶村、婆梨婆村及诣负弥城。」对曰：「唯然！」即严衣钵，与诸大众侍从世尊，路由跋祇渐至他城，于负弥城北，止尸舍婆林。

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "I will now explain to you the four great teachings. Listen carefully and keep them in mind."

佛告诸比丘：「当与汝等说四大教法，谛听！谛听！善思念之。」

The bhikṣus said, "Yes, World-Honored One! We wish to hear it."

诸比丘言：「唯然！世尊！愿乐欲闻。」

What are the four? If a bhikṣu says, 'Venerable ones! I heard it directly from the Buddha in such-and-such village, city, or country, and received this teaching directly.' Those who hear it should not doubt it, nor should they disparage it. They should examine its truthfulness in the scriptures, investigate its basis in the Vinaya and the Dharma. If what they say is not in the scriptures, not in the Vinaya, and not in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'The Buddha did not say this. Have you misunderstood?' Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier contradicts the Dharma. Venerable one! Do not uphold it, do not teach it to others, abandon it.' If what they say is in the scriptures, in the Vinaya, and in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'What you say is truly spoken by the Buddha. Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier accords with the Dharma. Venerable one! You should uphold it, teach it widely to others, and do not abandon it.' This is the first great teaching.

「何谓为四？若有比丘作如是言：『诸贤！我于彼村、彼城、彼国，躬从佛闻，躬受是教。』从其闻者，不应不信，亦不应毁，当于诸经推其虚实，依律、依法究其本末。若其所言非经、非律、非法，当语彼言：『佛不说此，汝谬受耶！所以然者？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相违。贤士！汝莫受持，莫为人说，当捐舍之。』若其所言依经、依律、依法者，当语彼言：『汝所言是真佛所说，所以然者？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相应。贤士！汝当受持，广为人说，慎勿捐舍。』此为第一大教法也。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu says, 'In such-and-such village, city, or country, I heard it directly from the harmonious assembly of monks, the elders of great learning, and received this Dharma, Vinaya, and teaching.' Those who hear it should not doubt it, nor should they disparage it. They should examine its truthfulness in the scriptures, investigate its basis in the Dharma and the Vinaya. If what they say is not in the scriptures, not in the Vinaya, and not in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'The Buddha did not say this. Did you misunderstand the assembly?' Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier contradicts the Dharma. Venerable one! Do not uphold it, do not teach it to others, abandon it.' If what they say is in the scriptures, in the Vinaya, and in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'What you say is truly spoken by the Buddha. Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier accords with the Dharma. Venerable one! You should uphold it, teach it widely to others, and do not abandon it.' This is the second great teaching.

「复次，比丘作如是言：『我于彼村、彼城、彼国，和合众僧、多闻耆旧，亲从其闻，亲受是法、是律、是教。』从其闻者，不应不信，亦不应毁，当于诸经推其虚实，依法、依律究其本末，若其所言非经、非律、非法者，当语彼言：『佛不说此，汝于彼众谬听受耶！所以然者？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相违。贤士！汝莫持此，莫为人说，当捐舍之。』若其所言依经、依律、依法者，当语彼言：『汝所言是真佛所说，所以者何？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相应。贤士！汝当受持，广为人说，慎勿捐舍。』此为第二大教法也。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu says, 'In such-and-such village, city, or country, I heard it directly from many bhikṣus who uphold the Dharma, Vinaya, and discipline, and received this Dharma, Vinaya, and teaching.' Those who hear it should not doubt it, nor should they disparage it. They should examine its truthfulness in the scriptures, investigate its basis in the Dharma and the Vinaya. If what they say is not in the scriptures, not in the Vinaya, and not in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'The Buddha did not say this. Did you misunderstand the many bhikṣus?' Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier contradicts the Dharma. Venerable one! Do not uphold it, do not teach it to others, abandon it.' If what they say is in the scriptures, in the Vinaya, and in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'What you say is truly spoken by the Buddha. Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier accords with the Dharma. Venerable one! You should uphold it, teach it widely to others, and do not abandon it.' This is the third great teaching.

「复次，比丘作如是言：『我于彼村、彼城、彼国，众多比丘持法、持律、持律仪者，亲从其闻，亲受是法、是律、是教。』从其闻者，不应不信，亦不应毁，当于诸经推其虚实，依法、依律究其本末。若其所言非经、非律、非法者，当语彼言：『佛不说此，汝于众多比丘谬听受耶！所以然者？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相违。贤士！汝莫受持，莫为人说，当捐舍之。』若其所言依经、依律、依法者，当语彼言：『汝所言是真佛所说，所以然者？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相应。贤士！汝当受持，广为人说，慎勿捐舍。』是为第三大教法也。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu says, 'In such-and-such village, city, or country, I heard it directly from a bhikṣu who upholds the Dharma, Vinaya, and discipline, and received this Dharma, Vinaya, and teaching.' Those who hear it should not doubt it, nor should they disparage it. They should examine its truthfulness in the scriptures, investigate its basis in the Dharma and the Vinaya. If what they say is not in the scriptures, not in the Vinaya, and not in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'The Buddha did not say this. Did you misunderstand the bhikṣu?' Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier contradicts the Dharma. Venerable one! Do not uphold it, do not teach it to others, abandon it.' If what they say is in the scriptures, in the Vinaya, and in the Dharma, they should say to them, 'What you say is truly spoken by the Buddha. Because I rely on the scriptures, the Vinaya, and the Dharma. What you said earlier accords with the Dharma. Venerable one! You should uphold it, teach it widely to others, and do not abandon it.' This is the fourth great teaching.

「复次，比丘作如是言：『我于彼村、彼城、彼国，一比丘持法、持律、持律仪者，亲从其闻，亲受是法、是律、是教。』从其闻者，不应不信，亦不应毁，当于诸经推其虚实，依法、依律究其本末。若所言非经、非律、非法者，当语彼言：『佛不说此，汝于一比丘所谬听受耶！所以然者？我依诸经、依法、依律，汝先所言，与法相违。贤士！汝莫受持，莫为人说，当捐舍之。』若其所言依经、依律、依法者，当语彼言：『汝所言是真佛所说，所以然者？我依诸经、依律、依法，汝先所言，与法相应。贤士！当勤受持，广为人说，慎勿捐舍。』是为第四大教法也。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed in the city of Bhamgala as he wished, told Venerable Ānanda, "Let us go to the city of Papara." Ānanda replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!" He then put on his robe and took his bowl, and together with the great assembly, he followed the World-Honored One on the road through Malla to the city of Papara, to the garden of Jatu. At that time, there was a craftsman named Cunda who heard that the Buddha was coming to the city from Malla. He immediately adorned himself and went to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. At that time, the Buddha gradually taught and transformed Cunda with the Dharma, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him. Having heard the Buddha's teaching, Cunda was filled with faith and joy. He then invited the World-Honored One to a meal the next day. The Buddha silently accepted the invitation. Cunda, knowing that the Buddha had accepted, immediately rose from his seat, paid homage to the Buddha, and returned. He then prepared the meal that night and informed the Buddha of the time the next day.

尔时，世尊于负弥城随宜住已，告贤者阿难俱诣波婆城，对曰：「唯然！」即严衣钵，与诸大众侍从世尊，路由末罗至波婆城阇头园中。时，有工师子，名曰周那，闻佛从彼末罗来至此城，即自严服，至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。时，佛渐为周那说法正化，示教利喜，周那闻佛说法，信心欢喜，即请世尊明日舍食。时，佛默然受请。周那知佛许可，即从座起，礼佛而归。寻于其夜供设饭食，明日时到，唯圣知时。

At that time, the World-Honored One put on his Dharma robe, took his alms bowl, and surrounded by the great assembly, went to his house, took his seat, and sat down. At that time, Cunda immediately prepared food and drink to offer to the Buddha and the Sangha. He separately cooked a rare delicacy of sandalwood mushrooms, which he alone offered to the World-Honored One.

尔时，世尊法服持钵，大众围遶，往诣其舍，就座而坐。是时，周那寻设饮食，供佛及僧，别煮栴檀树耳，世所奇珍，独奉世尊。

The Buddha told Cunda, "Do not give these mushrooms to the monks." Cunda accepted the instruction and did not dare to give them. At that time, there was an elder monk in the assembly who had renounced the householder's life later in life. He took the mushrooms with another vessel from the seat.

佛告周那：「勿以此耳与诸比丘。」周那受教，不敢輙与。时，彼众中有一长老比丘，晚暮出家，于其座上以余器取。

At that time, Cunda saw that the assembly had finished eating and had put away the bowls and utensils. After serving the water for washing, he then asked the Buddha in verse:

尔时，周那见众食讫，并除钵器，行澡水毕，即于佛前以偈问曰：

I dare to ask the great sage, the rightly enlightened, the honored one with
two feet, the well-tamed and well-disciplined, how many śramaṇas are there in the world?

「敢问大圣智，　　正觉二足尊，善御上调伏，　　世有几沙门？」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied in verse:

尔时，世尊以偈答曰：

As you have asked, there are four kinds of śramaṇas; their aspirations
and pursuits are each different. You should recognize and distinguish them. One
practices the path and is superior, two skillfully explain the meaning of
the path, three live by relying on the path, and four defile
the path. What is meant by being superior in the path? Skillfully
explaining the meaning of the path, relying on the path to live
and defiling the path? Being able to cross over the thorns of
affection and love, entering nirvāṇa without doubt; transcending the paths of gods
and humans, this is called being superior in the path. Skillfully understanding
the ultimate truth, explaining the path without defilements; compassionately resolving doubts for
the masses, this is called skillfully explaining the meaning of the path
Skillfully expounding the Dharma phrases, relying on the path to sustain oneself
gazing far into the undefiled field, this is called living by relying
on the path. Harboring deceit and evil within, outwardly appearing pure and
clean; deceitful and without sincerity, this is defiling the path. How can
good and evil coexist? Purity and impurity mixed together, outwardly appearing similar
and good, like copper painted to look like gold. Lay people may
see this and think it is a noble disciple. The rest are
not all like this, do not abandon pure faith. One person holds
the masses, inwardly turbid but outwardly pure; outwardly appearing to close the
traces of deceit and evil, but actually harboring unrestraint. Do not look
at outward appearances, seeing them suddenly and then becoming intimate and respectful
outwardly appearing to close the traces of deceit and evil, but actually harboring unrestraint.

「如汝所问者，　　沙门凡有四；志趣各不同，　　汝当识别之。一行道殊胜，　　二善说道义，三依道生活，　　四为道作秽。何谓道殊胜？　　善说于道义，依道而生活，　　有为道作秽？能度恩爱刺，　　入涅槃无疑；超越天人路，　　说此道殊胜。善解第一义，　　说道无垢秽；慈仁决众疑，　　是为善说道。善敷演法句，　　依道以自生；遥望无垢场，　　名依道生活。内怀于姧邪，　　外像如清白；虚诳无诚实，　　此为道作秽。云何善恶俱？　　净与不净杂，相似现外好，　　如铜为金涂。俗人遂见此，　　谓圣智弟子，余者不尽尔，　　勿舍清净信。一人持大众，　　内浊而外清；现闭姧邪迹，　　而实怀放荡。勿视外容貌，　　卒见便亲敬；现闭姧邪迹，　　而实怀放荡。」

At that time, Zhou Na took a small seat and sat in front of the Buddha. The Buddha gradually taught him the Dharma, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him. After the great assembly surrounded and escorted him back, they stopped under a tree on the way and said to Ānanda, "I have a backache. You can spread out a seat for me." Ānanda replied, "Yes!" He then spread out a seat, and the World-Honored One rested. At that time, Ānanda also spread out a small seat in front of the Buddha and sat down.

尔时，周那取一小座于佛前坐，佛渐为说法。示教利喜已，大众围遶，侍从而还。中路止一树下，告阿难言：「吾患背痛，汝可敷座。」对曰：「唯然！」寻即敷座，世尊止息。时，阿难又敷一小座于佛前坐。

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Did Zhou Na have any regrets just now? If he did, what caused it?"

佛告阿难：「向者周那无悔恨意耶？设有此意，为由何生？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "Zhou Na's offerings will not bring any blessings. Why is that? Because the Tathāgata will enter parinirvāṇa after eating his last meal at his house."

阿难白佛言：「周那设供，无有福利。所以者何？如来最后于其舍食便取涅槃。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not say that! Do not say that! Now Zhou Na has gained great benefits, gained longevity, gained complexion, gained strength, gained a good reputation, gained much wealth and treasures, and after death will be reborn in heaven, with all desires naturally fulfilled. Why is that? The one who offered food when the Buddha first attained enlightenment and the one who offered food when the Buddha was about to enter parinirvāṇa, these two merits are equal and no different. You should now go and tell Zhou Na, 'I have personally heard from the Buddha and personally received the Buddha's instruction. Zhou Na's offerings will now bring great benefits and great rewards.'"

佛告阿难：「勿作是言！勿作是言！今者周那为获大利，为得寿命，得色，得力，得善名誉，生多财宝，死得生天，所欲自然。所以者何？佛初成道能施食者，佛临灭度能施食者，此二功德正等无异。汝今可往语彼周那：『我亲从佛闻，亲受佛教，周那设食，今获大利，得大果报。』」

At that time, Ānanda accepted the Buddha's instruction and went to Zhou Na, saying, "I have personally heard from the Buddha and personally received the Buddha's instruction. Zhou Na's offerings will now bring great benefits and great rewards. Why is that? The one who offered food when the Buddha first attained enlightenment and the one who offered food when the Buddha was about to enter parinirvāṇa, these two merits are equal and no different."

时，阿难承佛教旨，即诣彼所，告周那曰：「我亲从佛闻，亲从佛受教，周那设食，今获大利，得大果报。所以然者？佛初得道能饭食者，及临灭度能饭食者，此二功德正等无异。」

After Zhou Na had finished his meal, he heard these words for the
first time: "The Tathāgata is gravely ill, and his life and practice are
now coming to an end." Although he ate sandalwood ears, his illness still
worsened. He traveled the road with his illness, gradually approaching the city of Kuśinagara.

周那舍食已，　　始闻如此言；如来患甚笃，　　寿行今将讫。虽食栴檀耳，　　而患犹更增；抱病而涉路，　　渐向拘夷城。

At that time, the World-Honored One rose from his seat, went a little further, and sat under a tree. He again told Ānanda, "My back is in great pain. Please spread out a seat for me."

尔时，世尊即从座起，小复前行，诣一树下，又告阿难：「吾背痛甚，汝可敷座。」

He replied, "Yes!" He then spread out a seat, and the Tathāgata rested. Ānanda bowed at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side.

对曰：「唯然！」寻即敷座，如来止息。阿难礼佛足已，在一面坐。

At that time, there was an arhat disciple named Fuguai. He was traveling from the city of Kuśinagara to the city of Pāpā. On the way, he saw the Buddha sitting under a tree, with an upright appearance, his faculties tranquil and still, having attained the supreme tranquility and peace. He was like a great dragon, or like clear water, pure and undefiled. Having seen this, he was delighted and gave rise to a good mind. He then went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! The renunciant who seeks a pure place and delights in solitary dwelling is truly extraordinary. There were five hundred carts passing by, but he did not hear or see them. Once, my teacher was sitting quietly under a tree on the road between the cities of Kuśinagara and Pāpā. At that time, five hundred carts passed by, with the sound of the carts rumbling. He was awake but did not hear it. Someone came and asked my teacher, 'Did you see the passing carts?' He replied, 'No.' They further asked, 'Did you hear?' He replied, 'No.' They further asked, 'Were you here or elsewhere?' He replied, 'Here.' They further asked, 'Were you awake?' He replied, 'Awake.' They further asked, 'Were you awake or asleep?' He replied, 'Not asleep.' The person silently thought, 'This is rare! The renunciant is so focused and concentrated that he did not hear the rumbling sound of the carts.' He then said to my teacher, 'There were five hundred carts passing by on this road just now, with the sound of the carts shaking and moving, but you still did not hear it. How could you hear anything else?' He then bowed to my teacher and left joyfully."

时，有阿罗汉弟子，名曰福贵，于拘夷那竭城向波婆城，中路见佛在一树下，容貌端正，诸根寂定，得上调意第一寂灭。譬如大龙，亦如澄水，清净无秽，见已欢喜，善心生焉。即到佛所，头面礼足，在一面坐，而白佛言：「世尊！出家之人在清净处，慕乐闲居，甚奇特也。有五百乘车经过其边，而不闻见，我师一时在拘夷那竭城、波婆城，二城中间道侧树下，静默而坐，时有五百乘车经过其边，车声轰轰觉而不闻。是时，有人来问我师：『向群车过，宁见不耶？』对曰：『不见！』又问：『闻耶？』对曰：『不闻！』又问：『汝在此耶？在余处耶？』答曰：『在此！』又问：『汝醒悟耶？』答曰：『醒悟！』又问：『汝为觉寐？』答曰：『不寐！』彼人默念：『是希有也！出家之人专精乃尔，车声轰轰觉而不闻。』即语我师曰：『向有五百乘车从此道过，车声振动，尚自不闻，岂他闻哉！』即为作礼，欢喜而去。」

The Buddha told Fuguai, "I will ask you now, answer as you wish. The rumbling sound of the carts woke him up but he did not hear it. The thunder shaking the heavens and earth woke him up but he did not hear it. Which is more difficult?"

佛告福贵：「我今问汝，随意所答，群车振动觉而不闻，雷动天地觉而不闻，何者为难？」

Fuguai said to the Buddha, "The sound of thousands of carts is not equal to thunder and lightning. Not hearing the sound of carts is not difficult. The thunder shaking the heavens and earth woke him up but he did not hear it. This is truly difficult."

福贵白佛言：「千万车声，岂等雷电？不闻车声未足为难，雷动天地觉而不闻，斯乃为难。」

The Buddha told Fuguai, "Once, I was wandering in the village of Avyukta, staying in a grass hut. At that time, a strange cloud suddenly rose, with thunder and lightning striking, killing four oxen and two brothers who were plowing. A large crowd gathered. At that time, I left the grass hut and walked back and forth. Among the crowd, a person came to me, bowed at my feet, and followed me as I walked. I knew and deliberately asked, 'What is the large crowd gathered for?' The person then asked, 'Where was the Buddha just now? Were you awake or asleep?' I replied, 'I was here, not asleep.' The person also marveled at the rarity of attaining concentration like the Buddha. The thunder and lightning shook the heavens and earth, but he was peacefully concentrated and did not hear it. He then said to the Buddha, 'Just now, a strange cloud suddenly rose, with thunder and lightning striking, killing four oxen and two brothers who were plowing. That is why the large crowd gathered.' The person's mind was delighted and he immediately attained the joy of the Dharma. He bowed to the Buddha and left."

佛告福贵：「我于一时游阿越村，在一草庐。时有异云暴起，雷电霹雳，杀四特牛、耕者兄弟二人，人众大聚。时，我出草庐，彷徉经行，彼大众中有一人来至我所，头面礼足，随我经行，我知而故问：『彼大众聚何所为耶？』其人即问：『佛向在何所？为觉寐耶？』答曰：『在此！时，不寐也。』其人亦叹希闻得定如佛者也，雷电霹雳，声聒天地，而独寂定觉而不闻。乃白佛言：『向有异云暴起，雷电霹雳，杀四特牛、耕者兄弟二人，彼大众聚，其正为此。』其人心悦即得法喜，礼佛而去。」

At that time, Fuguai was wearing two yellow robes worth hundreds of thousands. He immediately rose from his seat, knelt with joined palms, and said to the Buddha, "I now offer these robes to the World-Honored One. Please accept them."

尔时，福贵被二黄叠，价直百千，即从座起，长跪叉手而白佛言：「今以此叠奉上世尊，愿垂纳受。」

The Buddha told Fuguai, "You should offer one robe to me and one to Ānanda." At that time, Fuguai accepted the Buddha's instruction, offering one to the Tathāgata and one to Ānanda. The Buddha, out of compassion for him, accepted it. At that time, Fuguai bowed at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. The Buddha gradually taught him the Dharma, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him with discussions on giving, discussions on precepts, discussions on rebirth in heaven, the great suffering of desire, impurity and defilement, the hindrance of upper defilements, and the supreme way of liberation.

佛告福贵：「汝以一叠施我，一施阿难。」尔时，福贵承佛教旨，一奉如来，一施阿难。佛愍彼故，即为纳受。时，福贵礼佛足已，于一面坐，佛渐为说法，示教利喜：施论、戒论、生天之论，欲为大患、不净、秽污，上漏为碍，出要为上。

At that time, the Buddha knew that Fuguai's mind was joyful, pliant, free from hindrances and entanglements, and easy to be taught and transformed. According to the constant practice of all Buddhas, he then explained to Fuguai the noble truth of suffering, the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering. At that time, Fuguai's faith and understanding became pure, like a clean white cloth that is easy to be dyed. He immediately removed dust and defilements from his seat, generated the Dharma eye of the Dharma, saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, and was firmly established in the right Dharma, not falling into evil paths. He attained fearlessness and said to the Buddha, "I now take refuge in the Buddha! I take refuge in the Dharma! I take refuge in the Sangha! I only wish that the Tathāgata would allow me to be an upāsaka in the true Dharma. From now on, I will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol for the rest of my life. I only wish that the World-Honored One would allow me to be an upāsaka in the true Dharma."

时，佛知福贵意，欢喜柔软，无诸盖、缠，易可开化，如诸佛常法，即为福贵说苦圣谛，苦集、苦灭、苦出要谛。时，福贵信心清净，譬如净洁白叠，易为受色，即于座上远尘离垢，诸法法眼生，见法得法，决定正住，不堕恶道，成就无畏，而白佛言：「我今归依佛！归依法！归依僧！唯愿如来听我于正法中为优婆塞，自今已后，尽寿不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒，唯愿世尊听我于正法中为优婆塞。」

He also said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! If you are traveling and teaching and come to the city of Papā, I only wish that you would deign to pass by my poor village. The reason is that I wish to offer all the food, drink, bedding, clothing, and medicine in my house to the World-Honored One. After the World-Honored One accepts it, my family will attain peace."

又白佛言：「世尊！游化若诣波婆城，唯愿屈意过贫聚中。所以然者？欲尽家所有饮食、床卧、衣服、汤药，奉献世尊，世尊受已，家内获安。」

The Buddha said, "What you say is good!"

佛言：「汝所言善！」

At that time, the World-Honored One taught, guided, benefited, and delighted Bhadra with the Dharma. He then rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and joyfully departed. Not long after he left, Ānanda presented a yellow robe to the Tathāgata. Out of compassion, the Tathāgata accepted it and put it on. At that time, the World-Honored One's countenance was serene, his majestic radiance was blazing, his faculties were pure, and his complexion was harmonious and joyful. Ānanda, having seen this, silently thought to himself, "In the twenty-five years since I became his attendant, I have never seen the Buddha's complexion so radiant and clear as it is now." He then rose from his seat, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "In the twenty-five years since I became your attendant, I have never seen the Buddha's radiance and complexion as they are now. I do not know the reason. I wish to hear the meaning of it!"

尔时，世尊为福贵说法，示教利喜已。即从座起，头面礼足，欢喜而去。其去未久，阿难寻以黄叠奉上如来，如来哀愍，即为受之，被于身上。尔时，世尊颜貌从容，威光炽盛，诸根清净，面色和悦。阿难见已，默自思念：「自我得侍二十五年，未曾见佛面色光泽，发明如今。」即从座起，右膝著地，叉手合掌，前白佛言：「自我得侍二十五年，未曾见佛光色如今，不审何缘？愿闻其意！」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "There are two causes and conditions for the Tathāgata's radiance and complexion being different from usual: first, when the Buddha first attained enlightenment and became a perfect and true Buddha; second, when he is about to enter parinirvāṇa and relinquish his life. Ānanda, these two causes and conditions make the radiance and complexion different from usual." At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke a verse, saying:

佛告阿难：「有二因缘，如来光色有殊于常：一者佛初得道，成无上正真觉时；二者临欲灭度，舍于性命般涅槃时。阿难！以此二缘，光色殊常。」尔时，世尊即说颂曰：

The golden-colored robe is radiant and delightful, fine and soft, extremely pure and fresh; Bhadra
offered it to the World-Honored One, like the white tuft of hair on the forehead.

「金色衣光悦，　　细软极鲜净；福贵奉世尊，　　如雪白毫光。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "I am thirsty and wish to drink. Please fetch some water."

佛命阿难：「吾渴欲饮，汝取水来。」

Ānanda said, "Just now, five hundred chariots crossed the stream upstream. The water is turbid and not yet clear. It is suitable for washing the feet, but not for drinking."

阿难白言：「向有五百乘车于上流渡，水浊未清，可以洗足，不中饮也。」

如是三勅：「阿难！汝取水来。」

At that time, the World-Honored One went to the Kuśinagara River, drank, bathed, and left with the assembly. On the way, he stopped under a tree and said to Cunda, "Take the saṃghāṭī robe and spread it out. I have a backache and wish to rest for a while." Cunda, upon receiving the instruction, spread it out. The Buddha sat on it. Cunda, having paid homage, sat to one side and said to the Buddha, "I wish to enter parinirvāṇa! I wish to enter parinirvāṇa!"

尔时，世尊即诣拘孙河，饮已澡浴，与众而去。中路止息在一树下，告周那曰：「汝取僧伽梨四牒而敷，吾患背痛，欲暂止息。」周那受教，敷置已讫，佛坐其上。周那礼已，于一面坐，而白佛言：「我欲般涅槃！我欲般涅槃！」

The Buddha told him, "It is time." Then Cunda entered parinirvāṇa right in front of the Buddha. At that time, the Buddha spoke a verse:

佛告之曰：「宜知是时。」于是，周那即于佛前便般涅槃，佛时颂曰：

The Buddha went to the Kuśinagara River, clear and cool, free
from turbidity and defilement. The Honored One among humans entered the
water, bathed, and crossed to the other shore. The foremost of
the great assembly instructed Cunda, 'I am now exhausted. Quickly spread
out a bed for me.' Cunda promptly received the instruction and
spread out the four-fold robe. The Tathāgata rested. Cunda sat in
front and said to the World-Honored One, 'I wish to attain
parinirvāṇa, the place of no love and no hatred. Now I
shall go there.' The ocean of immeasurable merit and virtue, the
Supreme One, told him, 'What you have done is accomplished. Now
it is time.' Seeing that the Buddha had given permission, Cunda
redoubled his diligence. The cessation of activities without remainder is like
the extinction of a lamp.

「佛趣拘孙河，　　清凉无浊秽，人中尊入水，　　澡浴度彼岸。大众之原首，　　教勅于周那：吾今身疲极，　　汝速敷卧具；周那寻受教，　　四牒衣而敷；如来既止息，　　周那于前坐。即白于世尊：　　我欲取灭度，无爱无憎处，　　今当到彼方。无量功德海，　　最胜告彼曰：汝所作已办，　　今宜知是时。见佛已听许，　　周那倍精勤；灭行无有余，　　如灯尽火灭。」

At that time, Ānanda rose from his seat, went forward, and addressed the Buddha, saying, "After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, how should we perform the funeral rites?"

时，阿难即从座起，前白佛言：「佛灭度后，葬法云何？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "You should remain silent for now and consider your own duties. The faithful lay followers will gladly do it themselves."

佛告阿难：「汝且默然，思汝所业，诸清信士自乐为之。」

At that time, Ānanda again made a third request, saying, "After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, how should we perform the funeral rites?"

时，阿难复重三启：「佛灭度后，葬法云何？」

The Buddha said, "If you want to know the funeral rites, they should be like those of a wheel-turning sage king."

佛言：「欲知葬法者，当如转轮圣王。」

Ānanda again said, "What are the funeral rites for a wheel-turning sage king?"

阿难又白：「转轮圣王葬法云何？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "The funeral rites for a sage king are as follows: First, bathe the body with fragrant water, wrap it completely with new silk, and wrap it again with five hundred layers of cloth. Place the body in a golden coffin filled with sesame oil, and place the golden coffin in a second large iron coffin. Place a sandalwood coffin outside that, and pile up various fragrant woods on top of it. After the cremation, collect the relics and build a stupa at a four-way intersection, with a flagpole and banners, so that all passersby can see the stupa of the Dharma King and long for the right teachings, benefiting many. Ānanda! If you want to bury me, first bathe the body with fragrant water, wrap it completely with new silk, and wrap it again with five hundred layers of cloth. Place the body in a golden coffin filled with sesame oil, and place the golden coffin in a second large iron coffin. Place a sandalwood coffin outside that, and pile up various fragrant woods on top of it. After the cremation, collect the relics and build a stupa at a four-way intersection, with a flagpole and banners, so that all passersby can see the Buddha's stupa, long for the Dharma King's teachings, and be greatly benefited in this life and attain rebirth in the heavens." At that time, the World Honored One, deeply considering this meaning, spoke a verse:

佛告阿难：「圣王葬法，先以香汤洗浴其体，以新劫贝周遍缠身，以五百张叠次如缠之。内身金棺灌以麻油毕，举金棺置于第二大铁椁中，栴檀香椁次重于外，积众名香，厚衣其上而阇维之。讫收舍利，于四衢道起立塔庙，表刹悬缯，使国行人皆见法王塔，思慕正化，多所饶益。阿难！汝欲葬我，先以香汤洗浴，用新劫贝周遍缠身，以五百张叠次如缠之。内身金棺灌以麻油毕，举金棺置于第二大铁椁中，旃檀香椁次重于外，积众名香，厚衣其上而阇维之。讫收舍利，于四衢道起立塔庙，表刹悬缯，使诸行人皆见佛塔，思慕如来法王道化，生获福利，死得上天。」于时，世尊重观此义，而说颂曰：

Ānanda rose from his seat, knelt for a long time, and addressed the
World Honored One, saying, 'After the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, how should we perform the
funeral rites?' Ānanda, you should remain silent for now and contemplate your own
practice. The faithful in the country will naturally be happy to do it.'
Ānanda made three requests. The Buddha spoke of the funeral rites for a
wheel-turning sage king. 'If you wish to bury the Tathāgata's body, wrap it
in layers and place it in a coffin and casket. Build a stupa
at a four-way intersection for the benefit of sentient beings. Those who pay
homage will all attain immeasurable blessings.

「阿难从坐起，　　长跪白世尊，如来灭度后，　　当以何法葬？阿难汝且默，　　思惟汝所行；国内诸清信，　　自当乐为之。阿难三请已，　　佛说转轮葬，欲葬如来身，　　叠裹内棺椁；四衢起塔庙，　　为利益众生，诸有礼敬者，　　皆获无量福。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "There are four kinds of people in the world who should be given stūpas, incense, flowers, silk canopies, and music for offerings. Who are the four? First, the Tathāgata should be given a stūpa. Second, the pratyekabuddha. Third, the śrāvaka. Fourth, the wheel-turning sage king. Ānanda! These four kinds of people should be given stūpas, incense, flowers, silk canopies, and music for offerings." At that time, the World Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告阿难：「天下有四种人，应得起塔，香花缯盖，伎乐供养。何等为四？一者如来应得起塔，二者辟支佛，三者声闻人，四者转轮王。阿难！此四种人应得起塔，香华缯盖，伎乐供养。」尔时，世尊以偈颂] cf.【丽】曰：

The Buddha should be the first to have a stūpa, followed by
the pratyekabuddha and śrāvaka, and also the wheel-turning sage king who rules
over the four continents. These four should be given offerings, as recorded
by the Tathāgata. The stūpas of the Buddha, pratyekabuddha, śrāvaka, and wheel-turning sage king.

「佛应第一塔，　　辟支佛声闻，及转轮圣王，　　典领四域主。斯四应供养，　　如来之所记，佛、辟支、声闻，　　及转轮王塔。」

At that time, the World Honored One told Ānanda, "Let us go together to the city of Kuśinagara, to the twin śāla trees."

尔时，世尊告阿难：「俱诣拘尸城，末罗双树间。」

He replied, "Yes!" and together with the great assembly, he surrounded the World Honored One and proceeded on the road.

对曰：「唯然！」即与大众围遶世尊，在道而行。

There was a brahman who was traveling from Kuśinagara to Pāpā. On the way, he saw from afar the World Honored One, whose appearance was upright and proper, with tranquil faculties. Upon seeing him, he was delighted and a good mind arose within him. He went before the Buddha, greeted him, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "My village is not far from here. You have already made offerings to me now.

有一梵志从拘尸城趣波婆城，中路遥见世尊颜貌端正，诸根寂定，见已欢喜，善心自生，前至佛所，问讯讫，一面住，而白佛言：「我所居村去此不远，唯愿瞿昙于彼止宿，清旦食已，然后趣城。」佛告梵志：「且止！且止！汝今便为供养我已。」

At that time, the brahman, having heard the Buddha's instruction, went to Ānanda. After greeting him, he stood to one side and said to Ānanda, "My village is not far from here. I wish to invite Gautama to stay there for the night. After eating in the morning, he can then proceed to the city."

时，梵志闻佛教已，即诣阿难，问讯已，于一面立，白阿难言：「我所居村去此不远，欲屈瞿昙于彼止宿，清旦食已，然后趣城。」

The brahman made the invitation three times, earnestly. Ānanda replied, "It is hot now, and the village is far away. The World-Honored One is tired and should not be troubled."

尔时，世尊观此义已，即说颂曰：

The pure one advanced on the road, tired, he went to

approached and bowed his head. 'My village is nearby now, please
stay for one night out of compassion. In the morning, I
will offer a small meal, then go to the city.' The

taints. The Buddha, pratyekabuddhas, and śrāvakas, all return to extinction; impermanence

At that time, the World-Honored One entered the city of Kuśinagara, heading towards his birthplace, between the twin śāla trees, and said to Ānanda: "Please arrange a bed and seat for the Tathāgata between the twin trees, with the head facing north and the body facing west. The reason is that my Dharma will spread and remain for a long time in the north."

尔时，世尊入拘尸城，向本生处末罗双树间，告阿难曰：「汝为如来于双树间敷置床座，使头北首，面向西方。所以然者？吾法流布，当久住北方。」

He replied, "Yes!" and arranged the seat, with the head facing north.

对曰：「唯然！」即敷座，令北首。

At that time, the World-Honored One himself folded his saṃghāṭī robe into four layers, lay on his right side like a lion king, and placed his feet one on top of the other.

尔时，世尊自四牒僧伽梨，偃右脇如师子王，累足而卧。

At that time, the spirits between the twin trees who had faith in the Buddha scattered flowers out of season on the ground. 时，双树间所有鬼神笃信佛者，以非时花布散于地。尔时，世尊告阿难曰：「此双树神以非时华供养于我，此非供养如来。」

Ānanda said, "What is called making offerings to the Tathāgata?"

阿难白言：「云何名为供养如来？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "One who can receive the Dharma and practice the Dharma is called making offerings to the Tathāgata." The Buddha observed this meaning and spoke a verse:

佛语阿难：「人能受法，能行法者，斯乃名曰供养如来。」佛观此义，而说颂曰：

The Buddha was lying down between the twin trees, his mind undisturbed. The
tree spirit, with a pure mind, scattered flowers over the Buddha. Ānanda addressed
the Buddha, saying: 'What is called making offerings?' Receiving the Dharma and being
able to practice it is called making offerings with the flowers of awakening

offerings; understanding the aggregates, sense bases, and sense fields as selfless is called

「佛在双树间，　　偃卧心不乱；树神心清净，　　以花散佛上。阿难白佛言：　　云何名供养？受法而能行，　　觉华而为供。紫金华如轮，　　散佛未为供；阴、界、入无我，　　乃名第一供。」

At that time, Brahmadatta was standing in front of the Buddha, holding a fan to fan him. The Buddha said, "Step back, do not stand in front of me."

尔时，梵摩那在于佛前执扇扇佛，佛言：「汝却，勿在吾前。」

At that time, Ānanda silently thought to himself: "This Brahmadatta is always by the Buddha's side, providing for his needs. He should respect the Tathāgata, gazing without satiation. Now, at the very end, he needs his care, yet he is ordered to step back. What is the reason for this?" Then, Ānanda adjusted his robes and addressed the Buddha, saying: "This Brahmadatta is always by the Buddha's side, providing for his needs. He should respect the Tathāgata, gazing without satiation. Now, at the very end, he needs his care, yet he is ordered to step back. What is the reason for this?"

时，阿难默自思念：「此梵摩那常在佛左右，供给所须，当尊敬如来，视无厌足。今者末后须其瞻视，乃命使却，意将何因？」于是，阿难即整衣服，前白佛言：「此梵摩那常在佛左右，供给所须，当尊敬如来，视无厌足。今者末后须其瞻视，而命使却，将有何因？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Outside this city of Kuśinagara, within a distance of twelve yojanas, are the abodes of various great gods and spirits, without any empty spaces. These great gods all dislike this bhikṣu standing in front of the Buddha, thinking: 'Now, the Buddha is about to enter final nirvāṇa for the last time. We gods all hope to pay our respects, but this bhikṣu has great spiritual power and his radiance obscures us, preventing us from approaching to pay homage and make offerings.' Ānanda! It is for this reason that I ordered him to step back."

佛告阿难：「此拘尸城外有十二由旬，皆是诸大神天之所居宅，无空缺处。此诸大神皆嫌此比丘当佛前立：『今佛末后垂当灭度，吾等诸神，冀一奉觐，而此比丘有大威德，光明暎蔽，使我曹等不得亲近礼拜供养。』阿难！我以是缘，故命使却。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "This venerable bhikṣu, what merit has he accumulated in the past? What practices has he cultivated, that his spiritual power is now like this?"

阿难白佛：「此尊比丘本积何德，修何行业，今者威德乃如是乎？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Long ago, in the past, ninety-one kalpas ago, at that time there was a Buddha named Vipaśyin. At that time, this bhikṣu, with a joyful mind, held a torch of grass to illuminate that stūpa. Because of this cause and condition, it caused his majestic radiance to penetrate up to the twenty-eight heavens, surpassing the light of the gods."

佛告阿难：「乃往过去久远九十一劫，时世有佛，名毗婆尸，时此比丘以欢喜心，手执草炬，以照彼塔，由此因缘，使今威光上彻二十八天，诸天神光所不能及。」

At that time, Ānanda immediately rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, knelt on his right knee, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha: "Do not attain parinirvāṇa in this small and humble city, in this desolate and ruined land. Why is that? There are other great countries - the great country of Campā, the country of Vaiśālī, the city of Rājagṛha, the country of Vārāṇasī, the country of Śrāvastī, the country of Kapilavastu, the country of Vārāṇasī. The people in those countries are numerous, and they have faith and delight in the Buddha's teachings. After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, they will surely be able to respectfully make offerings to the relics."

尔时，阿难即从座起，偏袒右肩，长跪叉手而白佛言：「莫于此鄙陋小城荒毁之土取灭度也。所以者何？更有大国——瞻婆大国、毗舍离国、王舍城、婆祇国、舍卫国、迦维罗卫国、波罗［木＊奈］国，其土人民众多，信乐佛法，佛灭度已，必能恭敬供养舍利。」

The Buddha said: "Stop! Stop! Do not have such a view, do not think of this land as humble and small. Why is that? In the past, there was a king here named Mahāsudarśana. At that time, this city was called Kuśāvatī, the capital of the great king. It was four hundred and eighty li long and two hundred and eighty li wide. At that time, grain was abundant and cheap, and the people were prosperous. The city was surrounded by seven layers of railings, also seven layers, carved and incised, with jeweled bells hanging in between. The foundation of the city was three fathoms deep and twelve fathoms high. The towers on the city walls were twelve fathoms high, and the pillars were three fathoms in circumference. The golden city had silver gates, the silver city had golden gates; the lapis lazuli city had crystal gates, the crystal city had lapis lazuli gates.

佛言：「止！止！勿造斯观，无谓此土以为鄙陋。所以者何？昔者，此国有王名大善见，此城时名拘舍婆提，大王之都城，长四百八十里，广二百八十里。是时，谷米丰贱，人民炽盛，其城七重，遶城栏楯亦复七重，雕文刻镂，间悬宝铃。其城下基深三仞，高十二仞，城上楼观高十二仞，柱围三仞，金城银门，银城金门；琉璃城水精门，水精城琉璃门。

The city was round and adorned with the four treasures. The railings in between were also made of the four treasures. The golden towers had silver bells, the silver towers had golden bells. The moat was seven layers deep, with lotus flowers growing in it: blue lotus flowers, red lotus flowers, white lotus flowers, and pink lotus flowers. Golden sand was spread on the bottom. On both sides of the road, there were many trees. The golden trees had silver leaves and fruits, the silver trees had golden leaves and fruits; the crystal trees had lapis lazuli flowers and fruits, the lapis lazuli trees had crystal flowers and fruits. Between the trees were many bathing pools, with clear streams and deep ponds, clean and pure, without impurities. The sides were paved with bricks of the four treasures. The golden stairs had silver steps, the silver stairs had golden steps; the lapis lazuli stairs had golden steps, the lapis lazuli stairs had crystal steps. The railings surrounded them, connecting with each other. In the city, there were many trees growing everywhere. The golden trees had silver leaves and fruits, the silver trees had golden leaves and fruits, the crystal trees had lapis lazuli flowers and fruits, the lapis lazuli trees had crystal flowers and fruits. Between the trees, there were also four kinds of jeweled ponds, with four kinds of flowers growing. The streets were neat and orderly, with rows of houses. When the wind blew, the flowers scattered on the side of the road. A gentle breeze arose from the four directions, blowing the jeweled trees, producing a soft sound, like heavenly music. The people of the country, both men and women, young and old, played together among the trees, enjoying themselves. There were always ten kinds of sounds in the country: the sound of conches, drums, vīṇās, singing, dancing, blowing, elephants, horses, chariots, and the sound of eating, drinking, and laughing.

「其城周圆四宝庄严，间错栏楯亦以四宝。金楼银铃，银楼金铃，宝壍七重，中生莲花：优钵罗花、钵头摩花、俱物头花、分陀利花。下有金沙布现其底，侠道两边生多隣娑树，其金树者，银叶花实；其银树者，金叶花实；水精树者，琉璃花实；琉璃树者，水精花实。多隣树间有众浴池，清流深潭，洁净无秽，以四宝塼间砌其边。金梯银蹬，银梯金蹬，琉璃梯金蹬，琉璃梯陛水精为蹬，水精梯陛琉璃为蹬。周匝栏楯，辽遶相承，其城处处生多隣树，其金树者，银叶花实，其银树者，金叶花实，水精树者，琉] cf.【丽】璃花实，琉] cf.【丽】璃树者，水精花实。树间亦有四种宝池，生四种花。街巷齐整，行伍相当，风吹众花，纷纷路侧。微风四起，吹诸宝树，出柔软音，犹如天乐。其国人民，男女大小，共游树间，以自娱乐。其国常有十种声：贝声、鼓声、波罗声、歌声、舞声、吹声、象声、马声、车声、饮食戏笑声。

At that time, King Mahāsudarśana was endowed with the seven treasures. The king had four virtues and ruled over the four continents. What are the seven treasures? First, the golden wheel treasure, second, the white elephant treasure, third, the dark horse treasure, fourth, the divine jewel treasure, fifth, the jade woman treasure, sixth, the householder treasure, and seventh, the military commander treasure. How did King Mahāsudarśana accomplish the golden wheel treasure? The king always bathed in fragrant water on the full moon day of the fifteenth, ascended to the high palace, surrounded by court ladies, and the natural wheel treasure suddenly appeared before him. The wheel had a thousand spokes, with complete radiance and color, crafted by heavenly artisans, not of this world, made of pure gold, with a diameter of four zhang. King Mahāsudarśana silently thought to himself: 'I have heard from the elders that when a kṣatriya king of the water-sprinkled lineage bathes in fragrant water on the full moon day of the fifteenth, ascends to the jeweled palace, surrounded by court ladies, a natural golden wheel suddenly appears before him. The wheel has a thousand spokes, with complete radiance and color, crafted by heavenly artisans, not of this world, made of pure gold, with a diameter of four zhang. This is called a wheel-turning sage king. Is this wheel appearing now the same as that? Now I should test this wheel treasure.'

「尔时，大善见王七宝具足，王有四德，主四天下。何谓七宝？一、金轮宝，二、白象宝，三、绀马宝，四、神珠宝，五、玉女宝，六、居士宝，七、主兵宝。云何善见大王成就金轮宝？王常以十五日月满时，沐浴香汤，升高殿上，婇女围遶，自然轮宝忽现在前，轮有千辐，光色具足，天匠所造，非世所有，真金所成，轮径丈四。大善见王默自念言：『我曾从先宿诸旧闻如是语：刹利王水浇头种，以十五日月满时，沐浴香汤，升宝殿上，婇女围遶，自然金轮忽现在前，轮有千辐，光色具足，天匠所造，非世所有，真金所成，轮径丈四，是则名为转轮圣王。今此轮现，将无是耶？今我宁可试此轮宝。』

At that time, King Mahāsudarśana immediately summoned the four divisions of troops, exposed his right arm, touched the golden wheel treasure with his right knee on the ground, and said: 'Turn towards the east according to the Dharma, do not violate the usual principles.' The wheel then turned east. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana immediately led the four divisions of troops to follow behind. The four divine beings in front of the golden wheel treasure led the way. Wherever the wheel stopped, the king stopped his carriage. At that time, the kings of the small countries in the east saw the great king arrive. They came to the king's place with a golden bowl filled with silver rice and a silver bowl filled with golden rice, bowed their heads, and said: 'Welcome, great king! This land in the east is rich and prosperous, with a flourishing population, kind and gentle disposition, and filial and obedient nature. We only wish that the sage king would govern here. We will provide attendants and obey your wishes.' At that time, King Mahāsudarśana said to the small kings: 'Stop! Stop! All of you have already made offerings to me. You should only govern with the right Dharma, without partiality or injustice. Do not let there be any unlawful conduct in the country. This is called my governance.'

「时，大善见王即召四兵，向金轮宝偏露右臂，右膝著地，以右手摩抆金轮，语言：『汝向东方，如法而转，勿违常则。』轮即东转。时，善见王即将四兵随其后行，金轮宝前有四神引导，轮所住处，王即止驾。尔时，东方诸小国王见大王至，以金钵盛银粟，银钵盛金粟，来趣王所，拜首白言：『善来，大王！今此东方土地丰乐，人民炽盛，志性仁和，慈孝中顺。唯愿圣王于此治政，我等当给使左右，承受所宜。』当时，善见大王语小王言：『止！止！诸贤！汝等则为供养我已，但当以正法治，勿使偏枉，无令国内有非法行，此即名曰我之所治。』

At that time, the small kings, having heard this instruction, followed the great king on a tour of the countries, reaching the eastern seacoast. Then they went to the south, west, and north, wherever the wheel went. The kings of those countries each offered their lands, just like the small kings in the east. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana, having followed the golden wheel on a tour of the four seas, having enlightened and comforted the people with the Dharma, returned to his own country, the city of Kuśāvatī. At that time, the golden wheel treasure hovered in the sky above the palace gate. King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This golden wheel treasure is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' This is the accomplishment of the golden wheel treasure.

「时，诸小王闻此教已，即从大王巡行诸国，至东海表；次行南方、西方、北方，随轮所至，其诸国王各献国土，如东方诸小王。此时，善见王既随金轮，周行四海，以道开化，安慰民庶已，还本国拘舍婆城。时，金轮宝在宫门上虚空中住，大善见王踊跃而言：『此金轮宝真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』是为金轮宝成就。

What is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the white elephant treasure? At one time, King Mahāsudarśana was sitting in the main hall in the morning when the natural elephant treasure suddenly appeared before him. Its fur was pure white, standing evenly on seven places, able to fly with strength, its head was multicolored, with six slender tusks inlaid with pure gold. When the king saw it, he thought: 'This elephant is virtuous. If well-trained, it can be ridden.' He then tried to train it, and it was able to do all. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana wanted to test the elephant himself. He rode on it, left the city in the morning, traveled around the four seas, and returned at mealtime. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This white elephant treasure is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' This is the accomplishment of the elephant treasure.

「云何善见大王成就白象宝？时，善见大王清旦在正殿上坐，自然象宝忽现在前，其毛纯白，七处平住，力能飞行，其首杂色，六牙纤佣，真金间填。时，王见已，念言：『此象贤良，若善调者，可中御乘。』即试调习，诸能悉备。时，善见大王欲自试象，即乘其上，清旦出城，周行四海，食时已还。时，善见王踊跃而言：『此白象宝真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』是为象宝成就。

What is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the horse treasure? At one time, King Mahāsudarśana was sitting in the main hall in the morning when the natural horse treasure suddenly appeared before him. It was dark blue in color, with red mane and tail, and a head and neck like an elephant, able to fly with strength. When the king saw it, he thought: 'This horse is virtuous. If well-trained, it can be ridden.' He then tried to train it, and it was able to do all. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana wanted to test the horse treasure himself. He rode on it, left the city in the morning, traveled around the four seas, and returned at mealtime. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This dark blue horse treasure is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' This is the accomplishment of the dark blue horse treasure.

「云何善见大王成就马宝？时，善见大王清旦在正殿上坐，自然马宝忽现在前，绀青色，朱髦尾，头颈如象，力能飞行。时，王见已，念言：『此马贤良，若善调者，可中御乘。』即试调习，诸能悉备。时，善见王欲自试马宝，即乘其上，清旦出城，周行四海，食时已还。时，善见王踊跃而言：『此绀马宝真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』是为绀马宝成就。

What is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the divine jewel treasure? At one time, King Mahāsudarśana was sitting in the main hall in the morning when the natural divine jewel treasure suddenly appeared before him. Its substance was clear and pure, without any flaws. When the king saw it, he said: 'This jewel is wonderful. If it has light, it can illuminate the palace.' At that time, King Mahāsudarśana wanted to test this jewel. He immediately summoned the four divisions of troops, placed this jewel on a tall banner, and carried the banner out of the city at night. The light of the jewel illuminated the troops, just like daytime. Outside the troops, it could also illuminate a distance of one yojana. The people in the city all got up to work, thinking it was daytime. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This divine jewel is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' This is the accomplishment of the divine jewel treasure.

「云何善见大王神珠宝成就？时，善见大王于清旦在正殿上坐，自然神珠忽现在前，质色清彻，无有瑕秽。时，王见已，言：『此珠妙好，若有光明，可照宫内。』时，善见王欲试此珠，即召四兵，以此宝珠置高幢上，于夜冥中赍幢出城，其珠光明，照诸军众，犹如昼日。于军众外周匝，复能照一由旬。现城中人皆起作务，谓为是昼。时，王善见踊跃而言：『今此神珠真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』是为神珠宝成就。

What is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the jade woman treasure? At one time, the jade woman treasure suddenly appeared. Her complexion was composed, her facial features were upright, not tall or short, not coarse or fine, not white or black, not hard or soft. In winter, her body was warm, and in summer, her body was cool. The pores of her body emitted the fragrance of sandalwood, and her mouth emitted the fragrance of blue lotus flowers. Her speech was gentle, and her movements were calm. She rose first and sat later, never losing propriety. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana was pure and unattached, not thinking of her for a moment, let alone being intimate with her. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This jade woman treasure is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' This is the accomplishment of the jade woman treasure.

「云何善见大王成就玉女宝？时，玉女宝忽然出现，颜色从容，面貌端正，不长不短，不麤不细，不白不黑，不刚不柔，冬则身温，夏则身凉，举身毛孔出栴檀香，口出优钵罗华香，言语柔软，举动安详，先起后坐，不失宜则。时，王善见清净无著，心不暂念，况复亲近。时，王善见踊跃而言：『此玉女宝真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』是为玉女宝成就。

What is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the householder treasure? At one time, the householder treasure suddenly appeared. His treasure store was naturally inexhaustible. The householder had the merit of eyes that could see through the ground to hidden treasures, whether they belonged to someone or not. He could protect those that belonged to someone, and take those that did not belong to anyone to provide for the king. At that time, the householder treasure went to inform the king, saying: 'Great King! Do not worry about providing for me. I can provide for myself.' At that time, King Mahāsudarśana wanted to test the householder treasure. He ordered a boat to be prepared for playing in the water. He told the householder: 'I need gold and jewels. Give them to me quickly.' The householder replied: 'Great King, please wait a moment. I need to go to the shore.' The king then urged him: 'I need them now. Bring them here now.' At that time, the householder treasure, being strictly ordered by the king, knelt on the boat and put his right hand into the water. Treasure vases in the water followed his hand out, like insects climbing a tree. The householder treasure was also like this. When he put his hand in the water, treasures followed his hand out, filling the boat. He said to the king: 'What amount of treasures do you need now?' At that time, King Mahāsudarśana said to the householder: 'Stop! Stop! I have no need. I was just testing you. You have already provided for me.' When the householder heard the king's words, he immediately returned the treasures to the water. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This householder treasure is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' This is the accomplishment of the householder treasure.

「云何善见大王居士宝成就？时，居士丈夫忽然自出，宝藏自然，财富无量。居士宿福眼，能彻视地中伏藏，有主无主，皆悉见知。其有主者，能为拥护，其无主者，取给王用。时，居士宝往白王言：『大王！有所给与，不足为忧，我自能办。』时，善见王欲试居士宝，即𠡠严船于水游戏，告居士曰：『我须金宝，汝速与我。』居士报曰：『大王小待，须至岸上。』王寻逼言：『我停须用，正今得来。』时，居士宝被王严𠡠，即于船上长跪，以右手内著水中，水中宝瓶随手而出，如虫缘树。彼居士宝，亦复如是，内手水中，宝缘手出，充满船上，而白王言：『向须宝用，为须几许？』时，王善见语居士言：『止！止！吾无所须，向相试耳！汝今便为供养我已。』时，彼居士闻王语已，寻以宝物还投水中。时，善见王踊跃而言：『此居士宝真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』是为居士宝成就。

What is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the military commander treasure? At one time, the military commander treasure suddenly appeared. He was wise, brave, and had exceptional judgment. He went to the king and said: 'Great King! Do not worry about any punitive expeditions. I can handle them myself.' At that time, King Mahāsudarśana wanted to test the military commander treasure. He immediately assembled the four divisions and told him: 'Now use the troops. Assemble those who are not assembled, and dismiss those who are assembled. Equip those who are not equipped, and disband those who are equipped. March those who have not marched, and halt those who have marched.' At that time, the military commander treasure, having heard the king's words, immediately ordered the four divisions. Those who were not assembled were assembled, and those who were assembled were dismissed. Those who were not equipped were equipped, and those who were equipped were disbanded. Those who had not marched marched, and those who had marched halted. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana joyfully said: 'This military commander treasure is truly a good omen for me. I am now truly a wheel-turning sage king.' Ānanda! This is King Mahāsudarśana's accomplishment of the seven treasures.

「云何善见大王主兵宝成就？时，主兵宝忽然出现，智谋雄猛，英略独决，即诣王所白言：『大王！有所讨罚，王不足忧，我自能办。』时，善见大王欲试主兵宝，即集四兵而告之曰：『汝今用兵，未集者集，已集者放；未严者严，已严者解；未去者去，已去者住。』时，主兵宝闻王语已，即令四兵，未集者集，已集者放；未严者严，已严者解；未去者去，已去者住。时，善见王踊跃而言：『此主兵宝真为我瑞，我今真为转轮圣王。』阿难！是为善见转轮圣王成就七宝。

What are the four divine virtues? First, long life without premature death, which is unsurpassed. Second, a strong body without illness, which is unsurpassed. Third, a dignified and upright appearance, which is unsurpassed. Fourth, overflowing treasure stores, which is unsurpassed. These are the seven treasures and four virtues accomplished by the wheel-turning sage king.

「何谓四神德？一者长寿不夭，无能及者。二者身强无患，无能及者。三者颜貌端正，无能及者。四者宝藏盈溢，无能及者。是为转轮圣王成就七宝及四功德。

Ānanda! At that time, King Mahāsudarśana, after a long time, ordered the carriage to go out and tour the rear garden. He then told the charioteer: 'You should drive well and proceed slowly. The reason is that I want to observe carefully whether the people of the country are at ease and free from suffering.' At that time, the people of the country who were watching by the roadside also said to their attendants: 'You should proceed slowly. I want to observe carefully the majestic countenance of the sage king.' Ānanda! At that time, King Mahāsudarśana nurtured the people and the land with kindness, like a father loves his child. The people of the country admired the king, like a child looks up to his father. They offered all their precious treasures to the king, wishing for him to accept them as he pleased. The king replied: 'Stop! I already have treasures. You can keep them for yourselves.' At another time, the king had this thought: 'I should now build a palace.' As soon as this thought arose, the people of the country went to King Mahāsudarśana and each said to the king: 'We now want to build a palace for the king.' The king replied: 'I am now satisfied with your offerings. I have treasures of my own that are sufficient.' At that time, the people of the country again earnestly requested the king: 'We want to build a palace for the king.' The king told the people: 'Do as you wish.' At that time, the people, having received the king's order, immediately loaded gold onto 84,000 carts and brought it to the city of Kuśāvatī to build a Dharma hall. At that time, the second heavenly craftsman of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven silently thought to himself: 'Only I am capable of building a Dharma hall for King Mahāsudarśana.'

「阿难！时，善见王久乃命驾，出游后园，寻告御者：『汝当善御，安详而行。所以然者？吾欲谛观国土人民安乐无患。』时，国人民路次观者，复语侍人：『汝且徐行，吾欲谛观圣王威颜。』阿难！时，善见王慈育民物，如父爱子，国民慕王，如子仰父，所有珍奇尽以贡王，愿垂纳受，在意所与。时王报曰：『且止！诸人！吾自有宝，汝可自用。』复于异时，王作是念：『我今宁可造作宫观。』适生是意，时，国人民诣王善见，各白王言：『我今为王造作宫殿。』王报之曰：『我今以为得汝供养，我有宝物，自足成办。』时，国人民复重启王：『我欲与王造立宫殿。』王告人民：『随汝等意。』时，诸人民承王教已，即以八万四千两车，载金而来，诣拘舍婆城，造立法殿。时，第二忉利妙匠天子默自思念：『唯我能堪与善见王起正法殿。』

Ānanda! At that time, the heavenly craftsman built a Dharma hall, 60 li long and 30 li wide, adorned with the four treasures. The foundation was level, and the steps were paved with seven layers of precious bricks. The Dharma hall had 84,000 pillars, with golden pillars and silver capitals, silver pillars and golden capitals, and lapis lazuli and crystal pillars were also like this. Around the hall were four railings, all made of the four treasures, and the four steps were also made of the four treasures. Above the Dharma hall were 84,000 precious towers. The golden towers had silver windows, the silver towers had golden windows, and the crystal and lapis lazuli towers had windows like this. The golden towers had silver beds, the silver towers had golden beds, with soft and fine gold-threaded brocade spread on the beds. The crystal and lapis lazuli towers had beds like this. The light of the hall dazzled the eyes, like the bright sun, and no one could look at it. At that time, King Mahāsudarśana had this thought: 'I should now build many gardens and ponds to the left and right of this hall.' He then built gardens and ponds, one yojana long and wide.

「阿难！时，妙匠天造法殿，长六十里，广三十里，四宝庄严，下基平整，七重宝塼以砌其阶；其法殿柱有八万四千，金柱银栌，银柱金栌，琉璃、水精栌柱亦然。绕殿周匝，有四栏楯，皆四宝成，又四阶陛亦四宝成。其法殿上有八万四千宝楼，其金楼者银为户牖，其银楼者金为户牖，水精、琉璃楼] cf.【丽】户亦然。金楼银床，银楼金床，綩𫄧细輭，金缕织成，布其座上，水精、琉璃楼床亦然。其殿光明，眩曜人目，犹日盛明，无能视者。时，善见王自生念言：『我今可于是殿左右起多隣园池。』即造园池，纵广一由旬。

He also thought: 'I should build a Dharma pond in front of the Dharma hall.' He then built a pond, one yojana long and wide. The water was clear and pure, clean and free from impurities. The pond was lined with bricks made of the four treasures. Around the pond, there were railings all around, made of the four treasures of gold, silver, crystal, and lapis lazuli. In the pond grew various flowers: blue lotus flowers, red lotus flowers, white lotus flowers, and white water lily flowers, emitting a subtle fragrance that wafted in all directions. On the four sides of the pond, land grew flowers: aheya flowers, jasmine flowers, parala flowers, sumana flowers, vasika flowers, and tagara flowers. The pond keepers allowed passersby to enter and bathe, play, and cool off as they wished. They provided drinks to those who needed them, food to those who needed it, clothing, carriages, horses, incense, flowers, and treasures, not going against people's wishes.

「又复自念：『于法殿前造一法池。』寻即施造，纵广一由旬，其水清澄，洁净无秽，以四宝塼厕砌其下，绕池四边，栏楯周匝，皆以黄金、白银、水精、琉璃四宝合成。其池中水生众杂华：优钵罗华、波头摩华、俱物头华、分陀利华，出微妙香，馚馥四散。其池四面陆地生华：阿醯物多华、瞻卜华、波罗罗华、须曼陀华、婆师迦华、檀俱摩梨华。使人典池，诸行过者将入洗浴，游戏清凉，随意所欲。须浆与浆，须食与食，衣服、车马、香华、财宝，不逆人意。

Ānanda! At that time, King Mahāsudarśana had 84,000 elephants adorned with gold and silver, strung with precious pearls, with the elephant king as the foremost. 84,000 horses adorned with gold and silver, strung with precious pearls, with the horse king as the foremost. 84,000 chariots with lion-hide coverings, adorned with the four treasures, with the golden wheel treasure as the foremost. 84,000 pearls, with the divine pearl treasure as the foremost. 84,000 jade maidens, with the jade maiden treasure as the foremost. 84,000 householders, with the householder treasure as the foremost. 84,000 kṣatriyas, with the military commander treasure as the foremost. 84,000 cities, with Kuśinagara as the foremost. 84,000 palaces, with the Dharma palace as the foremost. 84,000 towers, with the great tower as the foremost. 84,000 beds, all made of gold, silver, and various treasures, with woolen blankets and soft and fine brocade spread on them. 84,000 billion garments, with the finest cotton, kāśika, and kapa garments. 84,000 kinds of food, served daily, with each taste different.

「阿难！时，善见王有八万四千象，金银校饰，络用宝珠，齐象王为第一。八万四千马，金银校饰，络用宝珠，力马王为第一。八万四千车，师子革络，四宝庄严，金轮宝为第一。八万四千珠，神珠宝为第一。八万四千玉女，玉女宝为第一。八万四千居士，居士宝为第一。八万四千刹利，主兵宝为第一。八万四千城，拘尸婆提城为第一。八万四千殿，正法殿为第一。八万四千楼，大正楼为第一。八万四千床，皆以黄金、白银、众宝所成，氍［毯－炎＋数］毾［毯－炎＋登］，綩𫄧细软，以布其上。八万四千亿衣，初摩衣、迦尸衣、劫波衣为第一。八万四千种食，日日供设，味味各异。

Ānanda! At that time, King Mahāsudarśana rode on the elephant king, and 84,000 elephants left Kuśinagara in the morning to inspect the world, circling the four seas, and returned to the city to eat in an instant. He rode on the horse king, and 84,000 horses left to inspect the world, circling the four seas, and returned to the city to eat in an instant. He rode in the golden wheel chariot, and 84,000 chariots left to inspect the world, circling the four seas, and returned to the city to eat in an instant. The divine pearl treasure illuminated the palace day and night. The jade maiden treasure, Sudarśanā, served and attended to him. The householder treasure provided for him. The military commander treasure punished him. The 84,000 cities were always governed, with Kuśinagara as the capital. The 84,000 palaces were always where the king stayed, with the Dharma palace as the main one. The 84,000 towers were always where the king stayed, with the great tower as the main one. The 84,000 seats were always where the king stayed, with the crystal seat as the main one, for the sake of meditation. The 84,000 billion garments were adorned with the finest treasures, to be worn as one wished, out of shame and modesty. The 84,000 kinds of food were always eaten by the king, with naturally arisen rice, out of contentment.

「阿难！时，善见王八万四千象，乘齐象上，清旦出拘尸城，案行天下，周遍四海，须臾之间，还入城食。八万四千马，乘力马宝，清旦出游，案行天下，周遍四海，须臾之间，还入城食。八万四千车，乘金轮车，驾力马宝，清旦出游，案行天下，周遍四海，须臾之间，还入城食。八万四千神珠，以神珠宝，照于宫内，昼夜常明。八万四千玉女，玉女宝善贤给侍左右。八万四千居士，有所给与，任居士宝。八万四千刹利，有所讨罚，任主兵宝。八万四千城，常所治都，在拘尸城。八万四千殿，王所常止，在正法殿。八万四千楼，王所常止，在大正楼。八万四千座，王所常止，在颇梨座，以安禅故。八万四千亿衣，上妙宝饰，随意所服，以惭愧故。八万四千种食，王所常食，食自然饭，以知足故。

At that time, 84,000 elephants appeared. The king stepped on and collided with them, injuring countless beings. The king thought, 'These elephants come often and cause much harm. From now on, I will allow one elephant to appear every hundred years.' In this way, one elephant appeared every hundred years in turn, going around and repeating.

「时，八万四千象来现，王时蹋蹈冲突，伤害众生，不可称数。时王念言：『此象数来，多所损伤，自今而后，百年听现一象。』如是转次百年现一，周而复始。」

At that time, the Buddha told Ānanda: "The king thought to himself: 'What merit did I accumulate in the past? What good roots did I cultivate, that I now receive such majestic rewards?' He further thought: 'It is due to three causes and conditions that I have attained this fortune. What are the three? First, giving, second, upholding precepts, and third, meditation. It is due to these causes and conditions that I now receive great rewards.' The king further thought: 'I have already received human rewards. I should further cultivate the karma for heavenly rewards. I should restrain myself, avoid busyness and noise, and dwell in seclusion to cultivate the Way.' At that time, the king then ordered the virtuous and wise queen Sudarśanā, saying: 'I have already received human rewards. I should further cultivate the karma for heavenly rewards. I should restrain myself, avoid busyness and noise, and dwell in seclusion to cultivate the Way.' The queen said: 'Yes, as the great king commands.' She then ordered the inner and outer [palace] to stop attending to him.

尔时，佛告阿难：「时王自念：『我本积何功德，修何善本，今获果报，巍巍如是？』复自思念：『以三因缘，致此福报。何谓三？一曰布施，二曰持戒，三曰禅思，以是因缘，今获大报。』王复自念：『我今已受人间福报，当复进修天福之业，宜自抑损，去离愦閙，隐处闲居，以崇道术。』时，王即命善贤宝女，而告之曰：『我今已受人间福报，当复进修天福之业，宜自抑损，去离愦閙，隐处闲居，以崇道术。』女言：『唯诺！如大王教。』即𠡠内外，绝于侍觐。

At that time, the king ascended the Dharma hall, entered the golden pavilion, sat on the silver throne, and contemplated the desire for lust, evil and unwholesome thoughts, with perception and contemplation, joy and bliss arising from detachment, attaining the first dhyāna; eliminating perception and contemplation, internally joyful and delighted, concentrating the mind, without perception or contemplation, attaining the joy and bliss born from samādhi, attaining the second dhyāna; abandoning joy and guarding, single-mindedly not distracted, knowing the bliss of the body, sought by the sages, practicing with mindfulness and bliss, attaining the third dhyāna; abandoning suffering and bliss, first eliminating joy and sorrow, neither suffering nor blissful, practicing with mindfulness and purity, attaining the fourth dhyāna. At that time, King Sudarśana rose from the silver throne, left the golden pavilion, went to the great square pavilion, sat on the lapis lazuli throne, cultivated the mind of loving-kindness, filling one direction, and the other directions were also like this, pervading everywhere, without duality or measure, eliminating all grudges and hatred, the mind without jealousy or evil, peacefully and quietly loving-kind and gentle, enjoying oneself. The minds of compassion, joy, and equanimity were also like this.

「时，王即升法殿，入金楼观，坐银御床，思惟贪婬欲、恶不善，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，得第一禅；除灭觉、观，内信欢悦，捡心专一，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，得第二禅；舍喜守护，专念不乱，自知身乐，贤圣所求，护念乐行，得第三禅；舍灭苦、乐，先除忧、喜，不苦不乐，护念清净，得第四禅。时，善见王起银御床，出金楼观，诣大正楼，坐琉璃床，修习慈心，遍满一方，余方亦尔，周遍广普无二无量，除众结恨，心无嫉恶，静默慈柔以自娱乐，悲、喜、舍心，亦复如是。

At that time, the queen Sudarśanā silently thought to herself: 'It has been a long time since I last saw the king. I should now present myself to him.' The queen Sudarśanā then told the eighty-four thousand palace ladies: 'You should all bathe in fragrant water and adorn yourselves. The reason is that it has been a long time since we last saw the king. We should now present ourselves to him.' Upon hearing this, the ladies all adorned themselves and bathed in fragrant water. The queen Sudarśanā then told the chief minister of the army to assemble the four divisions of the army: 'It has been a long time since we last saw the king. We should now present ourselves to him.' The chief minister of the army then assembled the four divisions of the army and said to the queen: 'The four divisions of the army have been assembled. Please know the time.' Thereupon, the queen Sudarśanā, together with the eighty-four thousand palace ladies, led by the four divisions of the army, went to the Golden Grove Park. The great assembly shook and the sound reached the king. Upon hearing the sound, the king looked out the window. The queen Sudarśanā then approached and stood by the door.

「时，玉女宝默自念言：『久违颜色，思一侍觐，今者宁可奉现大王。』时，宝女善贤告八万四千诸婇女曰：『汝等宜各沐浴香汤，严饰衣服，所以然者？我等久违颜色，宜一奉觐。』诸女闻已，各严衣服，沐浴澡洁。时，宝女善贤又告主兵宝臣集四种兵：『我等久违朝觐，宜一奉现。』时，主兵臣即集四兵，白宝女言：『四兵已集，宜知是时。』于是，宝女将八万四千婇女，四兵导从，诣金多隣园，大众震动，声闻于王，王闻声已，临牕而观，宝女即前，户侧而立。

At that time, the king saw the queen and said to her: 'Stop, do not come forward. I am about to go out and observe.' Then King Sudarśana rose from the crystal seat, left the great square pavilion, descended from the Dharma hall, and went with the queen Sudarśanā to the Golden Grove Park, where he took his seat. At that time, King Sudarśana's countenance was more radiant than usual. The queen Sudarśanā then thought to herself: 'Now the king's appearance is more radiant than usual. What is this strange omen?' The queen Sudarśanā then said to the king: 'Now your countenance is extraordinary. Is it not a strange omen? Do you wish to abandon your life? Now these eighty-four thousand elephants, with the white elephant as the foremost, adorned with gold and silver, and adorned with precious jewels, all belong to the king. I wish you would pay a little attention and enjoy yourself together. Do not abandon your life and abandon the people. Moreover, there are eighty-four thousand horses, with the horse king as the foremost; eighty-four thousand chariots, with the wheel treasure as the foremost; eighty-four thousand jewels, with the divine jewel as the foremost; eighty-four thousand women, with the queen as the foremost; eighty-four thousand householders, with the householder treasure as the foremost; eighty-four thousand kṣatriyas, with the chief minister of the army as the foremost; eighty-four thousand cities, with Kuśinagara as the foremost; eighty-four thousand palaces, with the Dharma hall as the foremost; eighty-four thousand pavilions, with the great square pavilion as the foremost; eighty-four thousand seats, with the jeweled seat as the foremost; eighty-four thousand kinds of clothing, with the softest as the foremost; eighty-four thousand kinds of food, with various delicacies as the foremost. All these treasures belong to the king. I wish you would pay a little attention and enjoy yourself together. Do not abandon your life and abandon the people.'

「时，王见女，寻告之曰：『汝止勿前，吾将出观。』时，善见王起颇梨座，出大正楼，下正法殿，与玉女宝诣多隣园，就座而坐。时，善见王容颜光泽有逾于常，善贤宝女即自念言：『今者大王色胜于常，是何异瑞？』时，女寻白大王：『今者颜色异常，将非异瑞，欲舍寿耶？今此八万四千象，白象宝为第一。金银交饰，珞用宝珠，自王所有，愿少留意，共相娱乐，勿便舍寿，孤弃万民。又八万四千马，力马王为第一；八万四千车，轮宝为第一；八万四千珠，神珠宝第一；八万四千女，玉女宝第一；八万四千居士，居士宝第一；八万四千刹利，主兵宝第一；八万四千城，拘尸城第一；八万四千殿，正法殿第一；八万四千楼，大正楼第一；八万四千座，宝饰第一；八万四千亿衣，柔软第一；八万四千种食，味味珍异。凡此众宝，皆王所有，愿少留意，共相娱乐，勿便舍寿，孤弃万民。』

At that time, King Sudarśana replied to the queen: 'Since you have been serving me, you have been kind, gentle, and respectful, with no harsh words. Why do you now speak like this?' The queen said to the king: 'I do not understand. What is wrong with what I said?' The king said to the queen: 'What you said just now about elephants, horses, jeweled chariots, golden wheels, palaces, famous clothes, and delicacies are all impermanent and cannot last. You are urging me to stay. How can that be respectful?' The queen said to the king: 'I do not understand. How should I speak respectfully?' The king said to the queen: 'If you can say: "Elephants, horses, jeweled chariots, golden wheels, palaces, famous clothes, and delicacies are all impermanent and cannot last. I do not wish to be attached to them and tire my mind. The reason is that the king's life is short and he will soon go to the next world. For all that is born, there is death; for all that is united, there is separation. How can there be anything born here that will have eternal life? One should cut off affection and preserve the intention of the Way. This is called respectful speech."'

「时，善见王答宝女曰：『自汝昔来恭奉于我，慈柔敬顺，言无麤漏今者何故，乃作此语？』女白王曰：『不审所白有何不顺？』王告女曰：『汝向所言：象马、宝车、金轮、宫观、名服、肴饍，斯皆无常，不可久保，而劝我留，岂是顺耶？』女白王言：『不审慈顺当何以言？』王告女曰：『汝若能言：象马、宝车、金轮、宫观、名服、肴饍，斯皆无常，不可久保，愿不恋著，以劳神思。所以然者？王命未几当就后世，夫生有死，合会有离，何有生此而永寿者？宜割恩爱以存道意，斯乃名曰敬顺言也。』

"Ānanda! At that time, the queen Sudarśanā, hearing the king's instruction, wept and cried, wiping away her tears, and said: 'Elephants, horses, jeweled chariots, golden wheels, palaces, famous clothes, and delicacies are all impermanent and cannot last. I do not wish to be attached to them and tire my mind. The reason is that the king's life is short and he will soon go to the next world. For all that is born, there is death; for all that is united, there is separation. How can there be anything born here that will have eternal life? One should cut off affection and preserve the intention of the Way.'"

「阿难！时，玉女宝闻王此教，悲泣号啼，扪泪而言：『象马、宝车、金轮、宫观、名服、肴饍，斯皆无常，不可久保，愿不恋著，以劳神思。所以然者？王寿未几当就后世，夫生有死，合会有离，何有生此而永寿者？宜割恩爱以存道意。』

"Ānanda! At that time, the queen Sudarśanā, while speaking these words, King Sudarśana suddenly passed away, just like a strong man eating a delicious meal. He had no suffering or distress. His spirit ascended to be reborn in the seventh Brahmā Heaven. Seven days after King Sudarśana's death, the wheel treasure, pearl treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, queen treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure all passed away on the same day. The city moat, Dharma hall, towers, precious decorations, and the Golden Grove Park all turned into earth and wood."

「阿难！彼玉女宝抚此言顷，时，善见王忽然命终，犹如壮士美饭一飡，无有苦恼，魂神上生第七梵天。其王善见死七日后，轮宝、珠宝自然不现，象宝、马宝、玉女宝、居士宝、主兵宝同日命终，城池、法殿、楼观、宝饰、金多隣园，皆变为土木。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "These conditioned dharmas are impermanent and changeable, and will eventually return to destruction. Craving and desire are insatiable, and they dissipate one's life. Attachment to affection and love is never enough. Only those who attain the holy wisdom and see the truth of the path can be content. Ānanda! I remember that I once became a wheel-turning sage king here six times, and my bones were buried here. Now that I have attained supreme perfect enlightenment, I have also given up my life and placed my body here. From now on, birth and death will be forever cut off, and there will be no place to place my body. This is the last limit, and I will no longer accept existence."

佛告阿难：「此有为法，无常变易，要归磨灭，贪欲无厌，消散人命，恋著恩爱，无有知足。唯得圣智，谛见道者，尔乃知足。阿难！我自忆念，曾于此处六返，作转轮圣王，终措骨于此。今我成无上正觉，复舍性命，措身于此，自今已后，生死永绝，无有方土，措吾身处，此最后边，更不受有。」

At that time, the World-Honored One was about to enter parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees in the Śāla Grove where he was born in the city of Kuśinagara. He told Ānanda, "You enter the city of Kuśinagara and tell the Mallas, 'Virtuous ones! Know that the Tathāgata will enter parinirvāṇa at midnight between the twin śāla trees in the Śāla Grove. You should go and ask your doubts and receive teachings face-to-face. Do so now, without regret later.'"

尔时，世尊在拘尸那竭城本所生处，娑罗园中双树间，临将灭度，告阿难曰：「汝入拘尸那竭城，告诸末罗：『诸贤！当知如来夜半于娑罗园双树间当般涅槃，汝等可往咨问所疑，面受教诫，宜及是时，无从后悔。』」

At that time, Ānanda accepted the Buddha's instruction, immediately rose from his seat, bowed to the Buddha, and left. He walked with a bhikṣu, shedding tears, and entered the city of Kuśinagara. He saw that the five hundred Mallas were gathered in one place for some minor reason.

是时，阿难受佛教已，即从座起，礼佛而去。与一比丘埀泪而行，入拘尸城，见五百末罗以少因缘，集在一处。

When the Mallas saw Ānanda coming, they immediately rose and bowed. They stood to one side and said to Ānanda, "I wonder why the Venerable has entered the city so late in the evening. What is the purpose?"

时，诸末罗见阿难来，即起作礼，于一面立，白阿难言：「不审尊者今入此城，何甚晚暮，欲何作为？」

Ānanda said with tears, "I have come to inform you for your benefit. You should know that the Tathāgata will enter parinirvāṇa at midnight. You should go and ask about your doubts, receive his instructions in person. Do so now, do not regret it later."

阿难垂泪言：「吾为汝等，欲相饶益，故来相告。卿等当知，如来夜半当般涅槃，汝等可往咨问所疑，面受教诫，宜及是时，无从后悔。」

When the Mallas heard this, they raised their voices in grief, rolling on the ground, fainting and then reviving. It was like a great tree with its roots uprooted, its branches and leaves collapsing. They all cried out, "How swiftly the Buddha has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the Buddha has entered parinirvāṇa! The long decline of sentient beings, the extinction of the eye of the world!"

时，诸末罗闻是言已，举声悲号，宛转躃地，绝而复苏，譬如大树根拔，枝条摧折，同举声言：「佛取灭度，何其驶哉！佛取灭度，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭。」

At that time, Ānanda consoled the Mallas, saying, "Stop! Stop! Do not grieve! All things in the world are born and then die. How can you expect the conditioned to be permanent? Did not the Buddha say that what is united will separate, and what is born will end?"

是时，阿难慰劳诸末罗言：「止！止！勿悲！天地万物，无生不终，欲使有为而常存者，无有是处。佛不云乎？合会有离，生必有尽。」

At that time, the Mallas said to each other, "Let us return to our homes, take our families, and bring five hundred white cotton cloths to the twin trees together."

时，诸末罗各相谓言：「吾等还归，将诸家属，并持五百张白叠，共诣双树。」

At that time, the Mallas returned to their homes, took their families, and brought the white cloths. They left the city of Kuśinagara and went to the twin trees. When Ānanda saw them from afar, he silently thought to himself, "There are many people. If they see the Buddha one by one, they may not be able to see him before the Buddha enters parinirvāṇa. I should let them see the Buddha at the same time during the early night." He then took the five hundred Mallas and their families to where the World-Honored One was. They bowed their heads at his feet and stood to one side. Ānanda then said to the Buddha, "So-and-so and so-and-so Mallas and their families ask about the World-Honored One's well-being and health."

时，诸末罗各归舍已，将诸家属，并持白叠，出拘尸城，诣双树间，至阿难所。阿难遥见，默自念言：「彼人众多，若一一见佛，恐未周闻，佛先灭度。我今宁可使于前夜，同时见佛。」即将五百末罗及其家属，至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面立，阿难前白佛言：「某甲某甲诸末罗等及其家属，问讯世尊起居增损。」

The Buddha replied, "It is good that you have come. I will make your lifespans long and free from illness and pain." Ānanda was then able to lead the Mallas and their families to see the World-Honored One.

佛报言：「劳汝等来，当使汝等寿命延长，无病无痛。」阿难乃能将诸末罗及其家属，使见世尊。

At that time, the Mallas bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. The World-Honored One then spoke to them about impermanence, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting them. When the Mallas heard the Dharma, they were delighted. They then offered five hundred pieces of cloth to the World-Honored One, who accepted them. The Mallas then rose from their seats, paid homage to the Buddha, and departed.

时，诸末罗头面礼足，于一面坐。尔时，世尊为说无常，示教利喜。时，诸末罗闻法欢喜，即以五百张叠，奉上世尊，佛为受之，诸末罗即从座起，礼佛而去。

At that time, in the city of Kuśinagara, there was a brahman named Subhadra, who was one hundred and twenty years old. He was wise and learned. Hearing that the śramaṇa Gautama would attain parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees that night, he thought to himself, "I have doubts about the Dharma. Only Gautama can resolve my mind. Now is the time. I will go by my own strength." That night, he left the city of Kuśinagara and went to the twin śāla trees. He went to Ānanda and asked about his well-being. Standing to one side, he said to Ānanda, "I heard that the śramaṇa Gautama will attain parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees tonight. I have come here to ask for a meeting. I have doubts about the Dharma. I wish to see Gautama to resolve my mind. Is there any spare time to meet?"

是时，拘尸城内，有一梵志，名曰须跋，年百二十，耆旧多智，闻沙门瞿昙今夜于双树间当取灭度，自念言：「吾于法有疑，唯有瞿昙能解我意，今当及时自力而行。」即于其夜，出拘尸城，诣双树间，至阿难所，问讯已，一面立，白阿难曰：「我闻瞿昙沙门今夜当取灭度，故来至此，求一相见。我于法有疑，愿见瞿昙，一决我意，宁有闲暇得相见不？」

Ānanda replied, "Stop! Stop! Subhadra! The Buddha's body is ill. Do not disturb him!"

阿难报言：「止！止！须跋！佛身有疾，无劳扰也。」

Subhadra insisted again and again, "I have heard that the Tathāgata appears in the world only once, like the udumbara flower that appears only occasionally. I have come to seek an audience to resolve my doubts. Is there any spare time to meet?"

须跋固请，乃至再三：「吾闻如来时一出世，如优昙钵花时时乃出，故来求现，欲决所疑，宁有闲暇暂相见不？」

Ānanda replied as before, "The Buddha's body is ill. Do not disturb him!"

阿难答如初：「佛身有疾，无劳扰也。」

At that time, the Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not stop him! Let him come in. He wants to resolve his doubts, and it will not disturb me. If he hears my Dharma, he will definitely gain understanding."

时，佛告阿难：「汝勿遮止！听使来入，此欲决疑，无娆乱也，设闻我法，必得开解。」

Ānanda then told Subhadra, "If you wish to see the Buddha, now is the time."

阿难乃告须跋：「汝欲觐佛，宜知是时。」

Subhadra then entered, greeted the Buddha, and sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "I have doubts about the Dharma. Is there time to resolve them?"

须跋即入，问讯已，一面坐，而白佛言：「我于法有疑，宁有闲暇一决所滞不？」

The Buddha said, "Ask as you please."

佛言：「恣汝所问。」

Subhadra then asked, "Gautama, how is it that there are various groups who call themselves teachers, such as Rāmaputra, Maskarin Gośālīputra, Ajita Keśakambalin, Kakuda Kātyāyana, Sañjayin Vairaṭṭīputra, and Nirgrantha Jñātiputra. These teachers each have different teachings. Can the Gautama Śramaṇa know them all? Or not?"

须跋即问：「云何？瞿昙！诸有别众，自称为师，不兰迦叶、末伽梨憍舍利、阿浮陀翅舍金披罗、波浮迦旃、萨若毘耶梨弗、尼揵子，此诸师等，各有异法，瞿昙沙门能尽知耶？不尽知耶？」

The Buddha said, "Stop! Stop! Why discuss this? I know them all. Now I will explain the profound and wonderful Dharma to you. Listen carefully and think about it well."

佛言：「止！止！用论此为，吾悉知耳。今当为汝说深妙法，谛听！谛听！善思念之。」

Subhadra accepted the teaching. The Buddha told him, "If there were no eightfold noble path among all dharmas, there would be no first, second, third, or fourth fruits of the śramaṇa. Subhadra! It is because there is the eightfold noble path among all dharmas that there are the first, second, third, and fourth fruits of the śramaṇa. Subhadra! Now in my Dharma there is the eightfold noble path, and there are the first, second, third, and fourth fruits of the śramaṇa. The heterodox groups do not have the fruits of the śramaṇa." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse for Subhadra:

须跋受教，佛告之曰：「若诸法中，无八圣道者，则无第一沙门果，第二、第三、第四沙门果。须跋！以诸法中有八圣道故，便有第一沙门果，第二、第三、第四沙门果。须跋！今我法中有八圣道，有第一沙门果，第二、第三、第四沙门果，外道异众无沙门果。」尔时，世尊为须跋而说颂曰：

I left home at twenty-nine, seeking the good path. Subhadra
I attained Buddhahood fifty years ago. Practicing precepts, concentration, and
wisdom, dwelling alone in contemplation, I now teach the essentials
of the Dharma. There are no śramaṇas outside of this.

「我年二十九，　　出家求善道；须跋我成佛，　　今已五十年。戒定智慧行，　　独处而思惟；今说法之要，　　此外无沙门。」

The Buddha told Subhadra, "If all bhikṣus can restrain themselves, then the world will not be empty of arhats."

佛告须跋：「若诸比丘皆能自摄者，则此世间罗汉不空。」

At that time, Subhadra said to Ānanda, "Those who have practiced the holy life under the Śramaṇa Gautama, those who are practicing now, and those who will practice in the future, all attain great benefits. Ānanda, you have also attained great benefits by practicing the holy life under the Tathāgata. I have also attained great benefits by being able to meet the Tathāgata and ask about my doubts. Now the Tathāgata has already distinguished me with the prediction of discipleship."

是时，须跋白阿难言：「诸有从沙门瞿昙已行梵行，今行、当行者，为得大利。阿难！汝于如来所修行梵行，亦得大利，我得面觐如来，咨问所疑，亦得大利。今者，如来则为以弟子莂而别我已。」

He then said to the Buddha, "May I now be allowed to go forth and receive full ordination in the Tathāgata's teachings?"

即白佛言：「我今宁得于如来法中出家受具戒不？」

The Buddha told Subhadra, "If a non-Buddhist brāhman wants to practice the holy life in my teachings, he should be tested for four months to observe his conduct and examine his intentions. If he is fully equipped with proper conduct and is free from faults, he may receive full ordination in my teachings. Subhadra, you should know that this is up to the people's conduct."

佛告须跋：「若有异学梵志于我法中修梵行者，当试四月，观其人行，察其志性，具诸威仪无漏失者，则于我法得受具戒。须跋！当知在人行耳。」

Subhadra then said, "Non-Buddhist practitioners should be tested for four months in the Buddha's teachings to observe their conduct and examine their intentions. If they are fully equipped with proper conduct and are free from faults, they may receive full ordination. Now I can serve for four years in the Buddha's true teachings, fully equipped with proper conduct, without any faults, and then receive full ordination."

须跋复白言：「外道异学于佛法中当试四月，观其人行，察其志性，具诸威仪无漏失者，乃得具戒。今我能于佛正法中四岁使役，具诸威仪，无有漏失，乃受具戒。」

The Buddha told Subhadra, "I have already said that it is up to the people's conduct."

佛告须跋：「我先已说在人行耳。」

Then Subhadra left home that night, received the precepts, and practiced pure conduct. In this very life, he personally realized the end of birth and death, established the practice of purity, accomplished what was to be done, attained true wisdom, and would no longer be subject to rebirth. Before long that night, he became an arhat, becoming the last disciple of the Tathāgata, who then passed away before the Buddha.

于是，须跋即于其夜，出家受戒，净修梵行，于现法中，自身作证：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，得如实智，更不受有。时，夜未久，即成罗汉，是为如来最后弟子，便先灭度而佛后焉。

At that time, Ānanda was standing behind the Buddha, stroking the bed and weeping uncontrollably. He lamented, "How swiftly the Tathāgata has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the great Dharma is disappearing! How long the sentient beings will suffer! The eye of the world has disappeared. Why is this? I have received the Buddha's kindness and attained the stage of learning, but my practice is not yet complete, and the Buddha has entered parinirvāṇa."

是时，阿难在佛后立，抚床悲泣，不能自胜，歔欷而言：「如来灭度，何其驶哉！世尊灭度，何其疾哉！大法沦曀，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭，所以者何？我蒙佛恩，得在学地，所业未成，而佛灭度。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing this, deliberately asked, "Where is Ānanda Bhikṣu now?"

尔时，世尊知而故问：「阿难比丘今为所在？」

At that time, the bhikṣus said to the Tathāgata, "Ānanda Bhikṣu is now behind the Buddha, stroking the bed and weeping uncontrollably. He laments, 'How swiftly the Tathāgata has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the great Dharma is disappearing! How long the sentient beings will suffer! The eye of the world has disappeared. Why is this? I have received the Buddha's kindness and attained the stage of learning, but my practice is not yet complete, and the Buddha has entered parinirvāṇa.'"

时，诸比丘白如来曰：「阿难比丘今在佛后抚床悲泣，不能自胜，歔欷而言：『如来灭度，何其驶哉！世尊灭度，何其疾哉！大法沦曀，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭，所以者何？我蒙佛恩，得在学地，所业未成，而佛灭度。』」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Stop! Stop! Do not be sorrowful and weep. Since you have served me, your physical actions have been loving, without duality or limit; your verbal actions have been loving, and your mental actions have been loving, without duality or limit. Ānanda! Your offerings to me are of great merit. If there are offerings to the devas, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas, none can compare to yours. You should only be diligent, and you will soon attain the Way."

佛告阿难：「止！止！勿忧莫悲泣也。汝侍我以来，身行有慈，无二无量；言行有慈，意行有慈，无二无量。阿难！汝供养我，功德甚大，若有供养诸天、魔、梵、沙门、婆罗门，无及汝者。汝但精进，成道不久。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "The past buddhas' attendants were also like Ānanda, and the future buddhas' attendants will also be like Ānanda. However, the past buddhas' attendants had to be told before they knew, but now my Ānanda knows just by a glance - what the Tathāgata needs, what the World-Honored One needs. This is Ānanda's unprecedented quality. You should uphold it. A wheel-turning sage king has four extraordinary and unprecedented qualities. What are the four? When the sage king walks, all the people of the country come to welcome him. Seeing him brings joy, hearing his teachings also brings joy, and gazing at his majestic countenance is never enough. When the wheel-turning sage king stands, sits, or lies down, all the ministers and people of the country come to the king. Seeing the king brings joy, hearing his teachings also brings joy, and gazing at his majestic countenance is never enough. These are the four extraordinary qualities of a wheel-turning sage king. Now my Ānanda also has these four extraordinary qualities. What are the four? Ānanda silently enters the assembly of bhikṣus, and the assembly is all joyful. When he preaches the Dharma for the assembly, hearing it also brings joy. Gazing at his demeanor and listening to his Dharma is never enough. Furthermore, when Ānanda silently goes to the assembly of bhikṣuṇīs, the assembly of upāsakas, and the assembly of upāsikās, seeing him brings joy to all. If he preaches the Dharma for them, hearing it also brings joy. Gazing at his demeanor and listening to his Dharma is never enough. These are Ānanda's four unprecedented and extraordinary qualities."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「过去诸佛给侍弟子亦如阿难，未来诸佛给侍弟子亦如阿难。然过去佛给侍弟子，语然后知；今我阿难，举目即知：如来须是，世尊须是。此是阿难未曾有法，汝等持之。转轮圣王有四奇特未曾有法，何等四？圣王行时，举国民庶皆来奉迎，见已欢喜，闻教亦喜，瞻仰威颜，无有厌足。转轮圣王若住、若坐，及与卧时，国内臣民尽来王所，见王欢喜，闻教亦喜，瞻仰威颜，无有厌足，是为转轮圣王四奇特法。今我阿难亦有此四奇特之法，何等四？阿难默然入比丘众，众皆欢喜，为众说法，闻亦欢喜，观其仪容，听其说法，无有厌足。复次，阿难默然至比丘尼众中、优婆塞众中、优婆夷众中，见俱欢喜，若与说法，闻亦欢喜，观其仪容，听其说法，无有厌足，是为阿难四未曾有奇特之法。」

At that time, Ānanda bared his right shoulder, touched his right knee to the ground, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Now, the elders of the four directions, who are śramaṇas with great wisdom, who are clear in the sutras and vinaya, and who have lofty virtue, come to pay homage to the World-Honored One. I am able to pay respects to them, see them, and ask them questions. After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, they will no longer come. Without anyone to look up to, what should I do?"

尔时，阿难偏露右肩，右膝著地，而白佛言：「世尊！现在四方沙门耆旧多智，明解经律，清德高行者来觐世尊，我因得礼敬，亲觐问讯。佛灭度后，彼不复来，无所瞻对，当如之何？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not worry. The noble sons always have four recollections. What are the four? First, recollecting the Buddha's birthplace, they joyfully wish to see it, remember it without forgetting, and give rise to a longing mind. Second, recollecting the place where the Buddha first attained the Way, they joyfully wish to see it, remember it without forgetting, and give rise to a longing mind. Third, recollecting the place where the Buddha turned the wheel of the Dharma, they joyfully wish to see it, remember it without forgetting, and give rise to a longing mind. Fourth, recollecting the place where the Buddha entered parinirvāṇa, they joyfully wish to see it, remember it without forgetting, and give rise to a longing mind. Ānanda! After my parinirvāṇa, the noble sons and daughters who recollect the Buddha's birthplace will have such merits. The Buddha's attainment of the Way, his spiritual power, turning the wheel of the Dharma, and liberating people, as well as his approaching parinirvāṇa and leaving behind the Dharma, are all like this. They should go to those places, travel and pay respects to the various stupas and temples. After death, they will all be reborn in the heavens, except for those who have attained the Way."

佛告阿难：「汝勿忧也。诸族姓子常有四念，何等四？一曰念佛生处，欢喜欲见，忆念不忘，生恋慕心。二曰念佛初得道处，欢喜欲见，忆念不忘，生恋慕心。三曰念佛转法轮处，欢喜欲见，忆念不忘，生恋慕心。四曰念佛般泥洹处，欢喜欲见，忆念不忘，生恋慕心。阿难！我般泥洹后，族姓男女念佛生时，功德如是。佛得道时，神力如是。转法轮时，度人如是。临灭度时，遗法如是。各诣其处，游行礼敬诸塔寺已，死皆生天，除得道者。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "After my parinirvāṇa, if the Śākya clansmen come and wish to become monks, allow them to renounce the householder life and receive full ordination. Do not create obstacles for them. If non-Buddhist Brahmins come and wish to become monks, also allow them to renounce the householder life and receive full ordination. Do not test them for four months. Why is that? They have different views. If they are detained for a short time, they will give rise to their original views."

佛告阿难：「我般涅槃后，诸释种来，求为道者，当听出家，授具足戒，勿使留难。诸异学梵志来求为道，亦听出家受具足戒，勿试四月。所以者何？彼有异论，若小稽留，则生本见。」

At that time, Ānanda knelt with his palms joined and said to the Buddha, "After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, what should be done about the arrogant and self-willed bhikṣus?"

尔时，阿难长跪叉手，前白佛言：「阐怒比丘虏扈自用，佛灭度后，当如之何？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "After my parinirvāṇa, if those arrogant ones do not follow the proper conduct and do not accept the instructions, you should all collectively impose the punishment of silent treatment. Order the bhikṣus not to speak with them, nor to interact with them in teaching and assisting them."

佛告阿难：「我灭度后，若彼阐怒不顺威仪，不受教诫，汝等当共行梵檀罚，勅诸比丘不得与语，亦勿往返教授从事。」

At that time, Ānanda again said to the Buddha, "After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, what should be done about the women who have not received instruction?"

是时，阿难复白佛言：「佛灭度后，诸女人辈未受诲者，当如之何？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not meet with them."

佛告阿难：「莫与相见。」

Ānanda again said, "If we do meet, what should be done?"

阿难又白：「设相见者，当如之何？」

The Buddha said, "Do not speak with them."

佛言：「莫与共语。」

Ānanda again said, "If we do speak with them, what should be done?"

阿难又白：「设与语者，当如之何？」

The Buddha said, "You should examine your own mind. Ānanda, do you think that after the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, there will be no more protection and you will lose what you uphold? Do not have such a view. The sūtras and precepts I have taught since becoming a Buddha are your protection and what you uphold. Ānanda, from today onwards, I allow the bhikṣus to abandon minor precepts. Call each other by their first names. This is the way of respect and obedience for those who have left home."

佛言：「当自捡心。阿难！汝谓佛灭度后，无复覆护，失所持耶？勿造斯观，我成佛来所说经戒，即是汝护，是汝所持。阿难！自今日始，听诸比丘舍小小戒，上下相呼，当顺礼度，斯则出家敬顺之法。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "If you have any doubts about the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, or about the path, you should ask quickly. Now is the time. Do not regret it later. While I am still here, I will explain it for you." At that time, the bhikṣus were silent and did not speak.

佛告诸比丘：「汝等若于佛、法、众有疑，于道有疑者，当速咨问，宜及是时，无从后悔，及吾现存，当为汝说。」时诸比丘默然无言。

The Buddha again said, "If you have any doubts about the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, or about the path, you should ask quickly. Now is the time. Do not regret it later. While I am still here, I will explain it for you." At that time, the bhikṣus were again silent.

佛又告曰：「汝等若于佛、法、众有疑，于道有疑，当速咨问，宜及是时，无从后悔，及吾现存，当为汝说。」时，诸比丘又复默然。

The Buddha again said, "If you are ashamed and do not dare to ask, you should rely on your friends and quickly come to ask. Now is the time. Do not regret it later." At that time, the bhikṣus were again silent.

佛复告曰：「汝等若自惭愧，不敢问者，当因知识，速来咨问，宜及是时，无从后悔。」时，诸比丘又复默然。

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "I believe that this assembly all has pure faith. There is not a single bhikṣu who doubts the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, or doubts the path.

阿难白佛言：「我信此众皆有净信，无一比丘疑佛、法、众，疑于道者。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "I also know that even the youngest bhikṣu in this assembly has seen the path and will not go to evil paths. After seven rebirths at most, they will definitely end suffering." At that time, the World-Honored One then prophesied the attainment of the fruit of the path by the 1,200 disciples.

佛告阿难：「我亦自知今此众中最小比丘皆见道迹，不趣恶道，极七往返，必尽苦际。」尔时，世尊即记莂千二百弟子所得道果。

At that time, the World-Honored One put on his uttarāsanga robe, extended his golden arm, and told the bhikṣus, "You should all observe that the Tathāgata appears in the world from time to time, like the udumbara flower appearing once in a while." At that time, the World-Honored One further observed this meaning and spoke a verse:

时，世尊披郁多罗僧，出金色臂，告诸比丘：「汝等当观如来时时出世，如优昙钵花时一现耳。」尔时，世尊重观此义，而说偈言：

The right arm is purple-golden in color, the Buddha appears like a
spiritual auspicious sign; coming and going, impermanence, appearing and disappearing, without negligence.

「右臂紫金色，　　佛现如灵瑞；去来行无常，　　现灭无放逸。

"Therefore, bhikṣus! Do not be negligent. It is because of my own non-negligence that I attained perfect enlightenment. Immeasurable good qualities are also attained through non-negligence. All things that are impermanent exist. This is the last teaching of the Tathāgata." Then the World-Honored One entered the first dhyāna concentration, arose from the first dhyāna and entered the second dhyāna; arose from the second dhyāna and entered the third dhyāna; arose from the third dhyāna and entered the fourth dhyāna; arose from the fourth dhyāna and entered the concentration of the sphere of space; arose from the concentration of the sphere of space and entered the concentration of the sphere of consciousness; arose from the concentration of the sphere of consciousness and entered the concentration of the sphere of nothingness; arose from the concentration of the sphere of nothingness and entered the concentration of neither perception nor non-perception; arose from the concentration of neither perception nor non-perception and entered the concentration of cessation of perception and feeling.

「是故，比丘！无为放逸，我以不放逸故，自致正觉，无量众善，亦由不放逸得，一切万物无常存者，此是如来末后所说。」于是，世尊即入初禅定，从初禅起，入第二禅；从第二禅起，入第三禅；从第三禅起，入第四禅；从四禅起，入空处定；从空处定起，入识处定；从识处定起，入不用定；从不用定起，入有想无想定；从有想无想定起，入灭想定。

At that time, Ānanda asked Aniruddha, "Has the World-Honored One already entered parinirvāṇa?"

是时，阿难问阿那律：「世尊已般涅槃耶？」

Aniruddha said, "Not yet, Ānanda! The World-Honored One is now in the concentration of cessation of perception and feeling. I have heard from the Buddha in person that he will enter parinirvāṇa after arising from the fourth dhyāna."

阿那律言：「未也，阿难！世尊今者在灭想定。我昔亲从佛闻，从四禅起，乃般涅槃。」

At that time, the World-Honored One arose from the concentration of cessation of perception and feeling, entered the concentration of neither perception nor non-perception; arose from the concentration of neither perception nor non-perception, entered the concentration of non-utility; arose from the concentration of non-utility, entered the concentration of the sphere of consciousness; arose from the concentration of the sphere of consciousness, entered the concentration of the sphere of space; arose from the concentration of the sphere of space, entered the fourth dhyāna; arose from the fourth dhyāna, entered the third dhyāna; arose from the third dhyāna, entered the second dhyāna; arose from the second dhyāna, entered the first dhyāna; arose from the first dhyāna, entered the second dhyāna; arose from the second dhyāna, entered the third dhyāna; arose from the third dhyāna, entered the fourth dhyāna; arose from the fourth dhyāna, the Buddha entered parinirvāṇa. At that time, the earth greatly shook, and the devas and humans were all greatly frightened. In the dark places not illuminated by the light of the sun and moon, they were all greatly illuminated, and they could see each other. They said to each other, "That person was born here! That person was born here!" The light was universal, surpassing the light of the devas.

于时，世尊从灭想定起，入有想无想定；从有想无想定起，入不用定；从不用定起，入识处定；从识处定起，入空处定；从空处定起，入第四禅；从第四禅起，入第三禅；从三禅起，入第二禅；从二禅起，入第一禅；从第一禅起，入第二禅；从二禅起，入第三禅；从三禅起，入第四禅；从四禅起，佛般涅槃。当于尔时，地大震动，诸天、世人皆大惊怖。诸有幽冥日月光明所不照处，皆蒙大明，各得相见，迭相谓言：「彼人生此！彼人生此！」其光普遍，过诸天光。

At that time, the devas of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven scattered maṇḍala flowers, utpala flowers, padma flowers, kumuda flowers, and puṇḍarīka flowers in the sky above the Tathāgata and the assembly. They also scattered powdered celestial sandalwood on the Buddha and the great assembly. After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, the Brahmā King of the Heaven of the Thirty-three spoke a verse in the sky:

时，忉利天于虚空中，以文陀罗花、优钵罗、波头摩、拘摩头、分陀利花散如来上，及散众会。又以天末栴檀而散佛上，及散大众。佛灭度已，时，梵天王于虚空中以偈颂曰：

All beings in the dark and ignorant class will all abandon the
aggregates. The Buddha is the unsurpassed Honored One, without equal in the
world. The Tathāgata, the Great Sage Hero, has the power of fearlessness
and no-fear. The World-Honored One should abide for a long time, but
now enters parinirvāṇa.

「一切昏萌类，　　皆当舍诸阴；佛为无上尊，　　世间无等伦。如来大圣雄，　　有无畏神力；世尊应久住，　　而今般涅槃。」

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, again spoke a verse:

尔时，释提桓因复作颂曰：

The aggregates and activities are impermanent, only giving rise to the Dharma of
decline and decay. The born have no immortality, the Buddha's extinction is bliss.

「阴行无有常，　　但为兴衰法；生者无不死，　　佛灭之为乐。」

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa, King of the Guardians of the World, again spoke a verse:

尔时，毘沙门王复作颂曰：

The great forest of merit trees, the unsurpassed merit śāla trees
the excellent field for receiving offerings, between the twin trees, attaining parinirvāṇa.

「福树大丛林，　　无上福娑罗；受供之良田，　　双树间灭度。」

At that time, Aniruddha again spoke a verse:

尔时，阿那律复作颂曰：

The Buddha abides in the unconditioned, not relying on inhalation and exhalation
He originally came from tranquility and extinction, and the spiritual radiance then disappeared.

「佛以无为住，　　不用出入息；本由寂灭来，　　灵曜于是没。」

At that time, the bhikṣu Brahmadatta again spoke a verse:

尔时，梵摩那比丘复作颂曰：

Do not slacken in mind, but cultivate superior wisdom for oneself
Without attachment, without defilement, free from love, the unsurpassed Honored One.

「不以懈慢心，　　约己修上慧；无著无所染，　　离爱无上尊。」

At that time, the bhikṣu Ānanda again spoke a verse:

尔时，阿难比丘复作颂曰：

The devas and humans are filled with terror, their body hairs
stand on end. All have attained perfect enlightenment and taken parinirvāṇa.

「天人怀恐怖，　　衣毛为之竖；一切皆成就，　　正觉取灭度。」

At that time, the god Kimbila again spoke a verse:

尔时，金毘罗神复作颂曰：

The world has lost its protector, sentient beings are forever blind and dark
They will no longer see the perfectly enlightened one, the lion among men.

「世间失覆护，　　群生永盲冥；不复覩正觉，　　人雄释师子。」

At that time, the Vajra-wielding General again spoke a verse:

尔时，密迹力士复作颂曰：

In this world and the next, the gods and humans of
the Brahma world will no longer see the lion among men.

「今世与后世，　　梵世诸天人；更不复覩见，　　人雄释师子。」

At that time, the Buddha's mother Maya also spoke a verse:

尔时，佛母摩耶复作颂曰：

The Buddha was born in the Lumbini Grove, and his teachings spread far
and wide. Now returning to his birthplace, he will forever abandon his impermanent body.

「佛生楼毘园，　　其道广流布；还到本生处，　　永弃无常身。」

At that time, the tree spirit of the twin trees also spoke a verse:

尔时，双树神复作颂曰：

When will we again scatter flowers out of season upon the Buddha, the
Tathāgata who has attained the ten powers and virtues, as he enters parinirvāṇa?

「何时当复以，　　非时花散佛；十力功德具，　　如来取灭度。」

At that time, the tree spirit of the Śāla Grove also spoke a verse:

尔时，娑罗园林神复作颂曰：

This place is the most wonderful and joyful, where the Buddha was born and
grew up; here he turned the wheel of the Dharma and also entered parinirvāṇa.

「此处最妙乐，　　佛于此生长；即此转法轮，　　又于此灭度。」

At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings also spoke a verse:

尔时，四天王复作颂曰：

The Tathāgata's unsurpassed wisdom always speaks of impermanence; he liberates beings
from the bonds of suffering and ultimately enters into quiescent extinction.

「如来无上智，　　常说无常论；解群生苦缚，　　究竟入寂灭。」

At that time, the Heavenly King of the Thirty-three Heavens also spoke a verse:

尔时，忉利天王复作颂曰：

For billions and millions of eons, seeking to accomplish the unsurpassed path
liberating beings from the bonds of suffering, ultimately entering into quiescent extinction.

「于亿千万劫，　　求成无上道；解群生苦缚，　　究竟入寂灭。」

At that time, the Heavenly King of the Flame Heaven also spoke a verse:

尔时，焰天王复作颂曰：

This is the last robe, wrapped around the Tathāgata's body
after the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, where should the robe be offered?

「此是最后衣，　　缠裹如来身；佛既灭度已，　　衣当何处施。」

At that time, the Heavenly King of the Tuṣita Heaven also spoke a verse:

尔时，兜率陀天王复作颂曰：

This is the last body, the aggregates and sense bases
cease here; without sorrow or joy, without old age or death.

「此是末后身，　　阴、界于此灭；无忧无喜想，　　无复老死患。」

At that time, the Heavenly King of the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven also spoke a verse:

尔时，化自在天王复作颂曰：

The Buddha will lie down on his right side this night, in
this śāla grove, the lion of the Śākya clan will attain parinirvāṇa.

「佛于今后夜，　　偃右脇而卧；于此娑罗园，　　释师子灭度。」

At that time, the Heavenly King of the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven also spoke a verse:

尔时，他化自在天王复作颂曰：

The world will be forever in darkness, the moon, the king of stars
will fall; Covered by impermanence, the sun of great wisdom will be forever obscured.

「世间永衰冥，　　星王月奄坠；无常之所覆，　　大智日永翳。」

At that time, another bhikṣu spoke a verse:

尔时，异比丘而作颂曰：

This body is like a bubble, fragile - who would enjoy it?
The Buddha attained the vajra body, yet it was still destroyed by
impermanence. The vajra bodies of all Buddhas also return to impermanence; quickly
extinguished like a little snow - what else can one hope for?

「是身如泡沫，　　危脆谁当乐？佛得金刚身，　　犹为无常坏。诸佛金刚体，　　皆亦归无常；速灭如少雪，　　其余复何冀？」

After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, the bhikṣus were grief-stricken and collapsed on the ground, rolling and wailing uncontrollably. They lamented, "The Tathāgata's nirvāṇa is so swift! The World-Honored One's nirvāṇa is so swift! The great Dharma is obscured so quickly! The long decline of sentient beings, the extinction of the eye of the world. It is like a great tree with its roots pulled out, its branches and leaves broken. It is also like cutting a snake, writhing and confused, not knowing where to go."

佛般涅槃已。时，诸比丘悲恸殒绝，自投于地，宛转号咷，不能自胜，歔欷而言：「如来灭度，何其驶哉！世尊灭度，何其疾哉！大法沦翳，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭。譬如大树根拔，枝条摧折。又如斩蛇，宛转回遑，莫知所奉。」

At that time, the bhikṣus were also like this, grief-stricken and collapsed on the ground, rolling and wailing uncontrollably. They lamented, "The Tathāgata's nirvāṇa is so swift! The World-Honored One's nirvāṇa is so swift! The great Dharma is obscured so quickly! The long decline of sentient beings, the extinction of the eye of the world."

时，诸比丘亦复如是，悲恸殒绝，自投于地，宛转号咷，不能自胜，歔欷而言：「如来灭度，何其驶哉！世尊灭度，何其疾哉！大法沦翳，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭。」

At that time, the elder Aniruddha said to the bhikṣus, "Stop! Stop! Do not grieve. The devas above may be suspicious."

尔时，长老阿那律告诸比丘：「止！止！勿悲，诸天在上，傥有恠责。」

At that time, the bhikṣus asked Aniruddha, "How many devas are above?"

时，诸比丘问阿那律：「上有几天？」

Aniruddha said, "The sky is filled with them. How can they be counted? They all hover and stir in the sky, wailing and lamenting, weeping and saying, 'How swiftly the Tathāgata has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the great Dharma is disappearing! The sentient beings will be long in decline, and the eye of the world will be extinguished. It is like a great tree with its roots uprooted and branches broken. It is also like a snake cut in two, writhing and confused, not knowing where to go.' At that time, the devas were also like this. They all hovered and stirred in the sky, wailing and lamenting, weeping and saying, 'How swiftly the Tathāgata has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the great Dharma is disappearing! The sentient beings will be long in decline, and the eye of the world will be extinguished.'"

阿那律言：「充满虚空，岂可计量？皆于空中徘徊骚扰，悲号躃踊，埀泪而言：『如来灭度，何其驶哉？世尊灭度，何其疾哉？大法沦翳，何其速哉？群生长衰，世间眼灭。譬如大树根拔，枝条摧折，又如斩蛇，宛转回遑，莫知所奉。』是时，诸天亦复如是，皆于空中徘徊骚扰，悲号躃踊，埀泪而言：『如来灭度，何其驶哉！世尊灭度，何其疾哉！大法沦翳，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭。』」

At that time, the bhikṣus discussed the Dharma throughout the night until dawn. Aniruddha then told Ānanda, "You should enter the city and tell the Mallas, 'The Buddha has already entered parinirvāṇa. You should do what you wish to do promptly.'"

时，诸比丘竟夜达晓，讲法语已，阿那律告阿难言：「汝可入城，语诸末罗：『佛已灭度，所欲施作，宜及时为。』」

At that time, Ānanda rose, paid homage at the Buddha's feet, and entered the city with a bhikṣu, weeping. He saw that the five hundred Mallas had gathered in one place for some reason. When the Mallas saw Ānanda coming, they all rose to welcome him, paid homage at his feet, and stood there, saying to Ānanda, "Why have you come so early?"

是时，阿难即起，礼佛足已，将一比丘，涕泣入城，遥见五百末罗以少因缘，集在一处。诸末罗见阿难来，皆起奉迎，礼足而立，白阿难言：「今来何早？」

Ānanda replied, "I have come here early to benefit you. You should know that the Tathāgata entered parinirvāṇa last night. You should do what you wish to do promptly."

阿难答言：「我今为欲饶益汝故，晨来至此。汝等当知：如来昨夜已取灭度，汝欲施作，宜及时为。」

When the Mallas heard these words, they all grieved and wept, saying, "How swiftly the Buddha has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the eye of the world has disappeared!"

时，诸末罗闻是语已，莫不悲恸，扪泪而言：「一何驶哉！佛般涅槃。一何疾哉！世间眼灭。」

Ānanda said, "Stop, stop! Do not grieve. It is impossible for conditioned things not to change. The Buddha has already said: 'Those who are born will die, those who come together will part; all love and affection are impermanent.'"

阿难报曰：「止！止！诸君勿为悲泣，欲使有为不变易者，无有是处。佛已先说：『生者有死，合会有离；一切恩爱，无常存者。』」

The Mallas then said to each other, "We should return home and prepare various incense, flowers, and music. Let us quickly go to the twin trees to make offerings to the relics. After a day, we will place the Buddha's relics on a bed, and let the Mallas' young boys lift the four corners of the bed, carrying banners and canopies, burning incense, scattering flowers, and making offerings with music. We will enter the eastern city gate, go through all the streets and alleys, so that the people of the country can all make offerings. Then we will leave through the western city gate, go to a high and prominent place, and cremate them." When the Mallas had discussed this, they each returned home to prepare incense, flowers, and music. They went to the twin trees and made offerings to the relics. After a day, they placed the Buddha's relics on a bed. The Mallas and others came to lift the bed, but they were all unable to do so.

时，诸末罗各相谓言：「宜各还归，办诸香花及众伎乐，速诣双树，供养舍利。竟一日已，以佛舍利置于床上，使末罗童子举床四角，擎持幡盖，烧香散华，伎乐供养，入东城门，遍诸里巷，使国人民皆得供养；然后出西城门，诣高显处而阇维之。」时，诸末罗作此论已，各自还家，供办香华及众伎乐，诣双树间，供养舍利。竟一日已，以佛舍利置于床上，诸末罗等众来举床，皆不能胜。

At that time, Aniruddha said to the Mallas, "You should stop and not tire yourselves in vain. Now the devas wish to come and lift the bed."

时，阿那律语诸末罗：「汝等且止！勿空疲劳，今者诸天欲来举床。」

The Mallas said, "For what reason do the devas wish to lift this bed?"

诸末罗曰：「天以何意，欲举此床？」

Aniruddha said, "You wish to make offerings to the relics with incense, flowers, and music for one day, then place the Buddha's relics on the bed, have the Mallas' young men lift the four corners of the bed, hold banners and canopies, burn incense, scatter flowers, and make offerings with music, enter the eastern gate of the city, go through all the streets and alleys, so that the people of the country can all make offerings; then go out the western gate of the city, to a high and visible place to cremate it. The devas wish to keep the relics for seven days, with incense, flowers, music, and respectful offerings; then place the Buddha's relics on the bed, have the Mallas' young men lift the four corners of the bed, hold banners and canopies, scatter flowers, burn incense, and make offerings with various music, enter the eastern gate of the city, go through all the streets and alleys, so that the people of the country can all make offerings; then go out the northern gate of the city, cross the Hiranyavati River, to the Tivanaka Monastery to cremate it. This is the intention of the devas, to keep the bed from moving."

阿那律曰：「汝等欲以香花伎乐供养舍利，竟一日已，以佛舍利置于床上，使末罗童子举床四角，擎持幡盖，烧香散花，伎乐供养，入东城门，遍诸里巷，使国人民皆得供养；然后出西城门，诣高显处而阇维之。而诸天意欲留舍利七日之中，香花伎乐，礼敬供养；然后以佛舍利置于床上，使末罗童子举床四角，擎持幡盖，散花烧香，作众伎乐，供养舍利。入东城门，遍诸里巷，使国人民皆得供养；然后出城北门，渡凞连禅河，到天冠寺而阇维之。是上天意，使床不动。」

The Mallas said, "Yes! This is a good idea! We will follow the devas' intention."

末罗曰：「诺！快哉斯言！随诸天意。」

At that time, the Mallas said to each other, "We should enter the city first, and level the roads street by street, sweep and sprinkle them, burn incense, and then return here to make offerings to the relics for seven days." The Mallas then entered the city together, leveled the roads street by street, swept and sprinkled them, burned incense, and then left the city. Between the twin trees, they made offerings to the relics with incense, flowers, and music. After seven days, as the sun was about to set, they raised the Buddha's relics and placed them on a bed. The Mallas' young men lifted the four corners of the bed, held banners and canopies, burned incense, scattered flowers, and made offerings with various music. They walked slowly, with the relics in front and the Mallas following behind.

时，诸末罗自相谓言：「我等宜先入城，街里街里，平治道路，扫洒烧香，还来至此，于七日中供养舍利。」时，诸末罗即共入城，街里街里，平治道路，扫洒烧香，讫已出城，于双树间，以香花伎乐供养舍利。讫七日已，时日向暮，举佛舍利置于床上，末罗童子奉举四角，擎持幡盖，烧香散花，作众伎乐，前后导从，安详而行。

At that time, the devas of the Trayastriṃśa Heaven scattered flowers such as mandārava, utpala, padma, kumuda, and puṇḍarīka, as well as heavenly sandalwood powder, over the relics, filling the streets and roads. The devas played music and the spirits sang. The Mallas said to each other, "Let us put aside human music and please arrange heavenly music to make offerings to the relics."

时，忉利诸天以文陀罗花、优钵罗花、波头摩花、拘物头花、分陀利花、天末栴檀散舍利上，充满街路。诸天作乐，鬼神歌咏。时，诸末罗自相谓言：「且置人乐，请设天乐供养舍利。」

Then the Mallas carried the bed and gradually advanced, entering the eastern city gate and stopping in the streets and alleys. They burned incense, scattered flowers, and made offerings with music. At that time, there was a Mallas' daughter named Luyi who had faith in the Buddha's teachings. She held golden flowers as big as chariot wheels and made offerings to the relics. At that time, an old woman raised her voice in praise, saying, "These Mallas have gained great benefits. The Tathāgata passed away here in the end, and the people of the whole country have had the opportunity to make offerings."

于是，末罗奉床渐进，入东城门，止诸街巷，烧香散花，伎乐供养。时，有路夷末罗女笃信佛道，手擎金花，大如车轮，供养舍利。时，有一老母举声赞曰：「此诸末罗为得大利，如来末后于此灭度，举国士民快得供养。」

At that time, after the Mallas had made offerings, they left the city through the northern gate, crossed the Nairañjanā River, and arrived at the Tiara Monastery. They placed the bed on the ground and said to Ānanda, "What other offerings should we make?"

时，诸末罗设供养已，出城北门，渡凞连禅河，到天冠寺，置床于地，告阿难曰：「我等当复以何供养？」

Ānanda replied, "I have personally heard from the Buddha and received his instructions. If you wish to bury the relics, it should be done according to the burial method of a wheel-turning sage king."

阿难报曰：「我亲从佛闻，亲受佛教，欲葬舍利者，当如转轮圣王葬法。」

He further asked Ānanda, "What is the burial method of a wheel-turning sage king?"

又问阿难：「转轮圣王葬法云何？」

He replied, "The burial method of a sage king is to first bathe the body with fragrant water, wrap it completely with new silk, then wrap it with five hundred layers of cloth, place the body in a golden coffin, fill it with sesame oil, lift the golden coffin and place it in a second large iron coffin, with a sandalwood coffin next to it on the outside, pile up various famous incense on top, and cremate it. Collect the relics and build a stupa at the crossroads, with a flagpole and streamers, so that all passersby in the country can see the king's stupa, miss the righteous teachings, and benefit greatly. Ānanda! If you wish to bury me, first bathe the body with fragrant water, wrap it completely with new silk, then wrap it with five hundred layers of cloth. Place the body in a golden coffin, fill it with sesame oil, lift the golden coffin and place it in a second large iron coffin, with a sandalwood coffin next to it on the outside, pile up various famous incense on top, and cremate it. Collect the relics and build a stupa at the crossroads, with a flagpole and streamers, so that all passersby can see the Buddha's stupa, miss the Dharma king's teachings of the Tathāgata, gain blessings in this life, and attain rebirth in heaven upon death, except for those who attain the Way."

答曰：「圣王葬法，先以香汤洗浴其身，以新劫贝周遍缠身，五百张叠次如缠之，内身金棺，灌以麻油毕，举金棺置于第二大铁椁中，栴檀香椁次重于外，积众名香，厚衣其上而阇维之。收拾舍利，于四衢道起立塔庙，表刹悬缯，使国行人皆见王塔，思慕正化，多所饶益。『阿难！汝欲葬我，先以香汤洗浴，用新劫贝周匝缠身，以五百张叠次如缠之。内身金棺，灌以麻油毕，举金棺置于第二大铁椁中，栴檀香椁次重于外，积众名香，厚衣其上而阇维之。收捡舍利，于四衢道起立塔庙，表刹悬缯，使诸行人皆见佛塔，思慕如来法王道化，生获福利，死得上天，除得道者。』」

At that time, the Mallas said to each other, "Let us return to the city to prepare funeral supplies, incense, flowers, silk, coffins, fragrant oil, and white cloth." The Mallas then entered the city together, prepared the funeral supplies, and returned to the Heaven-Crowned Temple. They bathed the Buddha's body with fragrant water, wrapped it completely with new silk, then wrapped it with five hundred layers of cloth. They placed the body in a golden coffin, filled it with fragrant oil, lifted the golden coffin and placed it in a second large iron coffin, with a sandalwood coffin wrapped around the outside, and piled various famous incense on top.

时，诸末罗各相谓言：「我等还城，供办葬具、香花、劫贝、棺椁、香油及与白叠。」时，诸末罗即共入城，供办葬具已，还到天冠寺，以净香汤洗浴佛身，以新劫贝周匝缠身，五百张叠次如缠之。内身金棺，灌以香油，奉举金棺置于第二大铁椁中，栴檀木椁重衣其外，以众名香而［卄／积］其上。

At that time, there was a Mallas minister named Rūpa who held a large torch and tried to burn the Buddha's pyre, but the fire would not burn. Another great Mallas tried to light the pyre next, but the fire would not burn. Then Aniruddha said to the Mallas, "Stop, stop, sirs! It is not something you can do. The fire is extinguished and will not burn. It is the intention of the gods.

时，有末罗大臣名曰路夷，执大炬火，欲燃佛积，而火不燃。又有大末罗次前燃其积，火又不燃。时，阿那律语诸末罗言：「止！止！诸贤！非汝所能，火灭不燃，是诸天意。」

The Mallas asked again, "Why do the gods not allow the fire to burn?"

末罗又问：「诸天何故使火不燃？」

Aniruddha said, "The gods know that Mahakasyapa is coming from the country of Vaisali with five hundred disciples. He is halfway there and has not yet cremated the Buddha's body. The gods know his intention and therefore do not allow the fire to burn."

阿那律言：「天以大迦叶将五百弟子从波婆国来，今在半道，及未阇维，欲见佛身，天知其意，故使火不燃。」

The Mallas said, "We wish to fulfill this intention!"

末罗又言：「愿遂此意！」

At that time, Mahakasyapa was leading five hundred disciples from the country of Vaisali. On the way, he encountered a Nirgrantha holding a mandara flower. Mahakasyapa saw the Nirgrantha from afar and went up to him, asking, "Where are you coming from?"

尔时，大迦叶将五百弟子从波婆国来，在道而行，遇一尼乾子手执文陀罗花。时，大迦叶遥见尼乾子，就往问言：「汝从何来？」

He replied, "I am coming from the city of Kuśinagara."

报言：「吾从拘尸城来。」

Mahakasyapa said again, "Do you know my teacher?"

迦叶又言：「汝知我师乎？」

He replied, "I know."

答曰：「知。」

He asked again, "Is my teacher still alive?"

又问：「我师存耶？」

He replied, "It has been seven days since his parinirvāṇa. I came here with these heavenly flowers." When Kāśyapa heard this, he was disappointed and unhappy. At that time, the five hundred bhikṣus heard that the Buddha had entered parinirvāṇa. They all wept bitterly, rolling on the ground and wailing, unable to control themselves. Wiping away their tears, they said, "How swiftly the Tathāgata has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa! How swiftly the great Dharma has been obscured! The sentient beings will suffer for a long time, and the eye of the world has disappeared. It is like a great tree with its roots uprooted and branches broken. It is also like a snake cut in two, writhing and confused, not knowing where to go."

答曰：「灭度已来，已经七日，吾从彼来，得此天华。」迦叶闻之，怅然不悦。时，五百比丘闻佛灭度，皆大悲泣，宛转号咷，不能自胜，扪泪而言：「如来灭度，何其驶哉！世尊灭度，何其疾哉！大法沦翳，何其速哉！群生长衰，世间眼灭。譬如大树根拔，枝条摧折，又如斩蛇，宛转回遑，莫知所奉。」

At that time, among the assembly was a Śākya youth named Bhadrapāla. He stopped the bhikṣus and said, "Do not grieve, bhikṣus. The World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa. I am now free. He always said, 'You should do this, you should not do that.' From now on, I will do as I please."

时，彼众中有释种子，字拔难陀，止诸比丘言：「汝等勿忧，世尊灭度，我得自在。彼者常言：『当应行是，不应行是。』自今已后，随我所为。」

Kāśyapa heard this and was disappointed and unhappy. He told the bhikṣus, "Quickly put on your robes and take your alms bowls. Let us go to the twin trees now, before the cremation. We may still be able to see the Buddha."

迦叶闻已，怅然不悦，告诸比丘曰：「速严衣钵，时诣双树，及未阇维，可得见佛。」

When the bhikṣus heard Mahākāśyapa's words, they immediately rose from their seats, followed Kāśyapa, went to the city of Kuśinagara, crossed the Nairañjanā River, arrived at the Heavenly Crown Temple, and went to Ānanda's place. After paying respects, they stood to one side and said to Ānanda, "We wish to pay our respects to the relics before the cremation. May we be allowed to see them?"

时，诸比丘闻大迦叶语已，即从座起，侍从迦叶，诣拘尸城，渡尼连禅河水，到天冠寺，至阿难所。问讯已，一面住，语阿难言：「我等欲一面觐舍利，及未阇维，宁可见不？」

Ānanda replied, "Although it has not been cremated, it is difficult to see now. The reason is that the Buddha's body has been washed with fragrant water and wrapped in layers of fine cloth, with five hundred layers of cloth wrapped one after another. It is placed in a golden coffin, which is then placed in an iron coffin, with a sandalwood coffin wrapped around the outside. It is difficult to see the Buddha's body again."

阿难答言：「虽未阇维，难复可见。所以然者？佛身既洗以香汤缠以劫贝，五百张叠次如缠之。藏于金棺，置铁椁中，栴檀香椁重衣其外，以为佛身难复可覩。」

Kāśyapa requested three times, and Ānanda replied as before, "It is difficult to see the Buddha's body again."

迦叶请至三，阿难答如初：「以为佛身难复得见。」

At that time, Mahākāśyapa was just about to go to the incense pile. At that time, the Buddha's body emerged from the heavy coffin with both feet, and the feet had a different color. Kāśyapa saw this and asked Ānanda in surprise, "The Buddha's body is golden in color. Why are the feet different?"

时，大迦叶适向香［卄／积］，于时佛身从重椁内双出两足，足有异色，迦叶见已，怪问阿难：「佛身金色，足何故异？」

Ānanda replied, "Just now, an old woman sadly came forward and stroked the Buddha's feet with her hand. Tears fell on them, so the color is different."

阿难报曰：「向者，有一老母悲哀而前手抚佛足，泪堕其上，故色异耳。」

Kāśyapa, having heard this, was again greatly displeased. He then went to the incense pile and paid homage to the Buddha's relics. At that time, the four groups of disciples and the heavenly beings above all paid homage at the same time. Then the Buddha's feet suddenly disappeared.

迦叶闻已，又大不悦，即向香［卄／积］，礼佛舍利。时，四部众及上诸天同时俱礼，于是佛足忽然不现。

At that time, Mahākāśyapa circumambulated the pile three times and spoke a verse:

At that time, Mahākāśyapa, with great majesty and the four kinds of eloquence, spoke this verse. When he had finished speaking, the Buddha's pyre spontaneously burned without being lit. The Mallas said to each other, "The fire is fierce and difficult to extinguish. When cremating the relics, they may be consumed. Where can we find water to extinguish it?" At that time, there was a Śāla tree spirit beside the Buddha's pyre who had a deep faith in the Buddha's path. He immediately used his spiritual power to extinguish the fire on the Buddha's pyre.

大迦叶有大威德，四辩具足，说此偈已，时，彼佛［卄／积］不烧自燃，诸末罗等各相谓言：「今火猛炽，焰盛难止，阇维舍利，或能消尽，当于何所求水灭之？」时，佛［卄／积］侧有娑罗树神，笃信佛道，寻以神力灭佛［卄／积］火。

The Mallas again said to each other, "Within twelve yojanas of this Kuśinagara, all the fragrant flowers should be gathered to offer to the Buddha's relics." They then went to the side of the city to gather fragrant flowers to make offerings.

时，诸末罗复相谓言：「此拘尸城左右十二由旬，所有香花，尽当采取，供佛舍利。」寻诣城侧，取诸香花，以用供养。

At that time, the Mallas of Pāpā country heard that the Buddha had attained parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees. They all thought to themselves, "Now we should go to request a share of the relics and build a stupa to make offerings in our own country." The Mallas of Pāpā country then assembled the four divisions of their army - elephant troops, cavalry, chariot troops, and infantry - and went to Kuśinagara. They sent a messenger to say, "We have heard that the Buddha, the Blessed One, has attained parinirvāṇa here. He was also our teacher. With a respectful and admiring heart, we come to request a share of his relics to build a stupa and make offerings in our own country.

时，波婆国末罗民众，闻佛于双树灭度，皆自念言：「今我宜往，求舍利分，自于本土，起塔供养。」时，波婆国诸末罗即下国中，严四种兵——象兵、马兵、车兵、步兵，到拘尸城，遣使者言：「闻佛众祐，止此灭度，彼亦我师，敬慕之心，来请骨分，当于本国起塔供养。」

The king of Kuśinagara replied, "Yes, yes, it is as you say. But the World-Honored One has come to this land and attained parinirvāṇa here. The people of our country will make offerings themselves. It is too much trouble for you to come from afar. The share of relics cannot be obtained.

拘尸王答曰：「如是！如是！诚如所言，但为世尊垂降此土，于兹灭度，国内士民，当自供养，远劳诸君，舍利分不可得。」

At that time, the people of the country of Śrāvastī, the people of the country of Rāmagrāma, the brāhmanas of the country of Vaiśālī, the Śākya people of the country of Kapilavastu, the Licchavi people of the country of Vaiśālī, and King Ajātaśatru of Magadha heard that the Tathāgata had attained parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees in Kuśinagara. They all thought to themselves, "Now we should go to request a share of the relics.

时，遮罗颇国诸跋离民众，及罗摩伽国拘利民众、毘留提国婆罗门众、迦维罗卫国释种民众、毘舍离国离车民众，及摩竭王阿阇世，闻如来于拘尸城双树间而取灭度，皆自念言：「今我宜往，求舍利分。」

At that time, the kings of various countries, including Ajātaśatru, immediately dispatched the four divisions of their armies - elephant troops, cavalry, chariot troops, and infantry - to cross the Ganges River. They then ordered the brāhman Fragrant Surname: "You should go into Kuśinagara on my behalf and ask the Mallas, 'Are you all well and strong? We have always respected each other as neighbors, and there has never been any dispute between us. I have heard that the Tathāgata attained parinirvāṇa in your country. The unsurpassed Honored One is truly my heavenly being. Therefore, I have come from afar to request a share of his relics, wishing to return to my own country to build a stupa and make offerings. If you give it to me, the heavy treasures of the entire country will be shared with you.'"

时，诸国王阿阇世等，即下国中，严四种兵——象兵、马兵、车兵、步兵，进渡恒水，即勅婆罗门香姓：「汝持我名，入拘尸城，致问诸末罗等：『起居轻利，游步强耶？吾于诸贤，每相宗敬，邻境义和，曾无诤讼。我闻如来于君国内而取灭度，唯无上尊，实我所天，故从远来，求请骨分，欲还本土，起塔供养。设与我者，举国重宝，与君共之。』」

At that time, the brāhman Fragrant Surname, having received the king's instructions, immediately went to the city and said to the Mallas, "The great king of Magadha asks about your well-being: 'Are you all well and strong? We have always respected each other as neighbors, and there has never been any dispute between us. I have heard that the Tathāgata attained parinirvāṇa in your country. The unsurpassed Honored One is truly my heavenly being. Therefore, I have come from afar to request a share of his relics, wishing to return to my own country to build a stupa and make offerings. If you give it to me, the heavy treasures of the entire country will be shared with you.'"

时，香姓婆罗门受王教已，即诣彼城，语诸末罗曰：「摩竭大王致问无量：『起居轻利，游步强耶？吾于诸君，每相宗敬，邻境义和，曾无诤讼。我闻如来于君国内而取灭度，唯无上尊，实我所天，故从远来，求请骨分，欲还本土，起塔供养。设与我者，举国重宝，与君共之。』」

At that time, the Mallas replied to Fragrant Surname, "Yes, yes! It is indeed as you say. But since the World-Honored One has come to this land and attained parinirvāṇa here, the people of the country will make offerings themselves. It is too much trouble for you to come from afar. The relics cannot be divided.

时，诸末罗报香姓曰：「如是！如是！诚如君言。但为世尊埀降此土，于兹灭度，国内士民自当供养，远劳诸君，舍利分不可得。」

At that time, the kings of various countries immediately gathered their ministers and discussed together, making a verse to announce:

时，诸国王即集群臣，众共立议，作颂告曰：

We have come from afar to pay homage and humbly
request a share. If it is not given, the four
armies are here, not sparing life and limb. If it
is not obtained through righteousness, we shall take it by force.

「吾等和议，　　远来拜首，逊言求分，　　如不见与，四兵在此，　　不惜身命，义而弗获，　　当以力取。」

At that time, the country of Kuśinagara immediately gathered its ministers and discussed together, answering in verse:

时，拘尸国即集群臣，众共立议，以偈答曰：

We have been greatly troubled, and you have come
to pay homage. We dare not give the remains
of the Tathāgata. If they want to raise troops
we also have them. We will fight to the
death, without fear.

「远劳诸君，　　屈辱拜首，如来遗形，　　不敢相许，彼欲举兵，　　吾斯亦有，毕命相抵，　　未之有畏。」

At that time, the brāhman of the fragrant surname said to the crowd, "Virtuous ones! For a long time you have received the Buddha's teachings, recited the Dharma words with your mouths, and accepted the benevolent transformation in your minds. You always think of wanting to bring peace to all beings. How can you fight over the Buddha's relics and harm each other? The Tathāgata's remains are meant to bring great benefit. The relics are present now, you should just divide them up and take them."

时，香姓婆罗门晓众人曰：「诸贤！长夜受佛教诫，口诵法言，心服仁化，一切众生常念欲安，宁可诤佛舍利共相残害？如来遗形欲以广益，舍利现在但当分取。」

The crowd all praised it as good. They then discussed, "Who is capable of dividing them?"

众咸称善，寻复议言：「谁堪分者？」

They all said, "The brāhman of the fragrant surname is kind, wise, and impartial. He can be made to divide them."

皆言香姓婆罗门仁智平均，可使分也。

At that time, the kings of the various countries immediately ordered the fragrant surname, "You divide the Buddha's relics for us and make them into eight equal parts."

时，诸国王即命香姓：「汝为我等分佛舍利，均作八分。」

At that time, the fragrant surname, having heard the words of the various kings, went to the place of the relics, bowed his head to pay respects, and slowly took the Buddha's upper tooth, placing it aside. He then sent a messenger to bring the Buddha's upper tooth to King Ajātaśatru, saying to the messenger, "You should speak on my behalf to the great king, asking about his light and easy rise and strong walking, whether the relics have not yet arrived, and whether he is waiting for a long time. Now I am entrusting the messenger with the Tathāgata's upper tooth, which can be used for worship to comfort his longing. When the morning star appears, the division of the relics will be completed, and I will personally send them to him."

于时，香姓闻诸王语已，即诣舍利所，头面礼毕，徐前取佛上牙，别置一面。寻遣使者，赍佛上牙，诣阿阇世王所，语使者言：「汝以我声，上白大王：『起居轻利，游步强耶？舍利未至，倾迟无量耶？今付使者如来上牙，竝可供养，以慰企望，明星出时，分舍利讫，当自奉送。』」

At that time, the messenger, having received the words of the fragrant surname, went to King Ajātaśatru and said, "The brāhman fragrant surname asks about your immeasurable well-being: 'Are you light and easy in rising and strong in walking? The relics have not yet arrived, and you are waiting for a long time. Now I am entrusting the messenger with the Tathāgata's upper tooth, which can be used for worship to comfort your longing. When the morning star appears, the division of the relics will be completed, and I will personally send them to you.'"

时，彼使者受香姓语已，即诣阿阇世王所，白言：「香姓婆罗门致问无量：『起居轻利，游步强耶？舍利未至，倾迟无量耶？今付使者如来上牙，竝可供养，以慰企望，明星出时，分舍利讫，当自奉送。』」

At that time, the fragrant surname took one stone's worth in a bottle, and then divided the relics into eight equal parts. He then said to the crowd, "I wish to be given this bottle by the assembly's agreement, so that I may build a stupa and make offerings to it in my own house."

尔时，香姓以一瓶受一石许，即分舍利，均为八分已，告众人言：「愿以此瓶，众议见与，自欲于舍起塔供养。」

They all said, "Wise! This is knowing the right time. We will all agree to give it to you."

皆言：「智哉！是为知时，即共听与。」

At that time, the people of Vipula village said to the crowd, "Please give us the burnt earth to build a stupa and make offerings." They all said, "We will give it to you."

时，有毕钵村人白众人言：「乞地燋炭，起塔供养。」皆言：「与之。」

At that time, the people of Kuśinagara obtained a portion of the relics and immediately built a stupa in their country to make offerings.

时，拘尸国人得舍利分，即于其土起塔供养。

The people of Pāvā, the people of Śrāvastī, the people of Rāmagrāma, the people of Vṛji, the people of Kapilavastu, the people of Vaiśālī, King Ajātaśatru of Magadha, and others obtained a portion of the relics and returned to their respective countries to build stupas and make offerings. The brahmin of the Fragrant Clan took the relic jar and returned to build a stupa. The people of Vipula village took the burnt earth and returned to build a stupa.

波婆国人、遮罗国、罗摩伽国、毘留提国、迦维罗卫国、毘舍离国、摩竭国阿阇世王等，得舍利分已，各归其国，起塔供养。香姓婆罗门持舍利瓶归起塔庙，毕钵村人持地燋炭归起塔庙。

At that time, eight stupas were built for the Tathāgata's relics, the ninth was the jar stupa, the tenth was the charcoal stupa, and the eleventh was the stupa for the hair at birth.

当于尔时，如来舍利起于八塔，第九瓶塔，第十炭塔，第十一生时发塔。

When was the Buddha born? When did he attain enlightenment? When did he enter parinirvāṇa? He was born when the star Rāhu was visible, he renounced the householder's life when Rāhu was visible, he attained enlightenment when Rāhu was visible, and he entered parinirvāṇa when Rāhu was visible.

何等时佛生？何等时成道？何等时灭度？沸星出时生，沸星出出家，沸星出成道，沸星出灭度。

When was the two-legged
honored one born? When
did he leave the
suffering of the forest?
When did he attain
the supreme path? When
did he enter the
city of nirvāṇa? Rāhu
was visible when the
two-legged honored one was
born, Rāhu was visible
when he left the

Rāhu was visible when

何等生二足尊？　　何等出丛林苦？何等得最上道？　　何等入涅槃城？沸星生二足尊，　　沸星出丛林苦，沸星得最上道，　　沸星入涅槃城。八日如来生，　　八日佛出家，八日成菩提，　　八日取灭度。八日生二足尊，　　八日出丛林苦，八日成最上道，　　八日入泥洹城。二月如来生，　　二月佛出家，二月成菩提，　　八日取涅槃。二月生二足尊，　　二月出丛林苦，二月得最上道，　　八日入涅槃城。娑罗花炽盛，　　种种光相照；于其本生处，　　如来取灭度。大慈般涅槃，　　多人称赞礼；尽度诸恐畏，　　决定取灭度。
