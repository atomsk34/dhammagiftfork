DĀ 20

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 3 第三分

20 （二〇）阿摩昼经

Parallel to: DN 3 / MN 36 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Kusumapura, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus. He arrived at the village of Icchaṅgala Kusumapura, a brāhman village, and stayed overnight in the Icchaṅgala Grove.

一时，佛游俱萨罗国，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱，至伊车能伽罗俱萨罗婆罗门村，即于彼伊车林中止宿。

At that time, there was a brāhman named Vīradatta who lived in the village of Ujjayinī. The village was prosperous and the people were thriving. King Prasenajit granted this village to the brāhman Vīradatta as a brahmin's share. This brāhman had been born into a family of seven generations of pure ancestry, not slighted by others. He was well-versed in the three ancient texts and was able to discern various scriptures. He was also skilled in the methods of divining the characteristics of great men and performing sacrificial rites. He had five hundred disciples whom he taught without cease. His foremost disciple was named Āmrapālī, who had been born into a family of seven generations of pure ancestry, not slighted by others. He was well-versed in the three ancient texts and was able to discern various scriptures. He was also skilled in the methods of divining the characteristics of great men and performing sacrificial rites. He also had five hundred disciples whom he taught without cease, no different from his teacher.

时，有沸伽罗娑罗婆罗门，止郁伽罗村，其村丰乐，人民炽盛，波斯匿王即封此村，与沸伽罗娑罗婆罗门，以为梵分。此婆罗门七世已来父母真正，不为他人之所轻毁，三部旧典讽诵通利，种种经书皆能分别，又能善解大人相法、祭祀仪礼，有五百弟子，教授不废。其第一摩纳弟子名阿摩昼，七世以来父母真正，不为他人之所轻毁，三部旧典讽诵通利，种种经书皆能分别，亦能善解大人相法、祭祀仪礼，亦有五百摩纳弟子，教授不废，与师无异。

At that time, the brāhman Vīradatta heard that the śramaṇa Gautama, the son of the Śākya clan, had renounced the householder's life, attained the path, and was traveling with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus. He had arrived at the brāhman village of Icchaṅgala Kusumapura and was staying in the Icchaṅgala Grove. He had a great reputation that spread throughout the world. He was a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, endowed with the ten titles. Among devas, humans, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas, he had personally realized and taught the Dharma, with its excellent meaning and flavor, and pure practice. Such a true person should be visited and seen. I should now go and see the śramaṇa Gautama to determine whether he truly has the thirty-two marks and whether his fame is true. By what means can I see the Buddha's marks? He further thought to himself: Now my disciple Āmrapālī has been born into a family of seven generations of pure ancestry, not slighted by others. He is well-versed in the three ancient texts and is able to discern various scriptures. He is also skilled in the methods of divining the characteristics of great men and performing sacrificial rites. Only this person can be sent to observe the Buddha to know whether the marks exist or not.

时，沸伽罗娑罗婆罗门闻沙门瞿昙释种子出家成道，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱，至伊车能伽罗俱萨罗婆罗门村，止伊车林中，有大名称，流闻天下，如来、至真、等正觉，十号具足，于诸天、世人、魔、若魔、天、沙门、婆罗门中，自身作证，为他说法，上中下善，义味具足，梵行清净。如此真人应往亲觐，我今宁可观沙门瞿昙，为定有三十二相，名闻流布，为称实不？当以何缘得见佛相？复作是念言：今我弟子阿摩昼，七世以来父母真正，不为他人之所轻毁，三部旧典讽诵通利，种种经书尽能分别，又能善解大人相法、祭祀仪礼，唯有此人可使观佛，知相有无。

At that time, the brāhman Vīradatta immediately ordered his disciple Āmrapālī and said to him: "Go and observe the śramaṇa Gautama. Do you see whether he truly has the thirty-two marks or not?"

时，婆罗门即命弟子阿摩昼而告之曰：「汝往观彼沙门瞿昙，为定有三十二相，为虚妄耶？」

At that time, Āmrapālī immediately replied to his teacher: "By what means can I observe the marks of Gautama to know whether they are true or false?"

时，阿摩昼寻白师言：「我以何验观瞿昙相，知其虚实？」

The teacher then replied: "I will now tell you, one who is fully endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great person will certainly go to one of two places, there is no doubt. If he stays at home, he will become a wheel-turning sage king, ruling the four continents, governing the people and subjects with the Dharma, and being fully endowed with the seven treasures: first, the golden wheel treasure, second, the white elephant treasure, third, the dark horse treasure, fourth, the divine jewel treasure, fifth, the jade woman treasure, sixth, the householder treasure, and seventh, the military commander treasure. The king will have a thousand sons, brave and wise, subduing enemies without the use of weapons and armies, bringing peace to the world. If he does not enjoy worldly life, but leaves home to seek the Way, he will become a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, fully endowed with the ten titles. By this, you can know whether Gautama is true or false."

师即报曰：「我今语汝，其有具足三十二大人相者，必趣二处，无有疑也。若在家，当为转轮圣王，王四天下，以法治化统领民物，七宝具足：一、金轮宝，二、白象宝，三、绀马宝，四、神珠宝，五、玉女宝，六、居士宝，七、典兵宝。王有千子，勇猛多智，降伏怨敌，兵杖不用，天下泰平，国内民物无所畏惧。若其不乐世间，出家求道，当成如来、至真、等正觉，十号具足。以此可知瞿昙虚实。」

At that time, Āmrapālī, having received the teacher's instruction, immediately prepared a jeweled carriage and, together with five hundred brāhman students, left the village in the morning to go to the Iśīka Grove. Upon arrival, he alighted from the carriage and walked to where the World-Honored One was. The Buddha sat while he stood, and the Buddha stood while he sat. In between, they discussed the principles of the Dharma. The Buddha said to the brāhman: "Have you ever discussed with the elder brāhman elders like this?"

时，阿摩昼受师教已，即严驾宝车，将五百摩纳弟子，清旦出村，往诣伊车林。到已下车，步进诣世尊所；佛坐彼立，佛立彼坐，于其中间共谈义理。佛告摩纳曰：「汝曾与诸耆旧长宿大婆罗门如是论耶？」

The brāhman said to the Buddha: "What does this mean?"

摩纳白佛：「此为何言？」

The Buddha said to the brāhman: "When I sit, you stand. When I stand, you sit. In between, we discuss the Dharma. Is your teacher's Dharma discussion like this?"

佛告摩纳：「我坐汝立，我立汝坐，中间共论，汝师论法当如是耶？」

The brāhman said to the Buddha: "In our brāhman Dharma discussion, when sitting, we all sit. When standing, we all stand. When lying down, we all lie down. Now these śramaṇas have ruined their appearance, are single, humble, inferior, and practice dark and obscure teachings. When discussing the Dharma with them, there is no sitting or standing."

摩纳白佛言：「我婆罗门论法，坐则俱坐，立则俱立，卧则俱卧。今诸沙门毁形鳏独，卑陋下劣，习黑冥法，我与此辈共论义时，坐起无在。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to him: "You, brāhman, are not yet tamed."

尔时，世尊即语彼言：「卿摩纳未被调伏。」

At that time, when the brāhman heard the World-Honored One address him as "you" and also heard that he was not yet tamed, he became angry and slandered the Buddha, saying: "This son of the Śākya clan is good at harboring jealousy and evil, and has no righteous Dharma."

时，摩纳闻世尊称卿，又闻未被调伏，即生忿恚，毁谤佛言：「此释种子，好怀嫉恶，无有义法。」

The Buddha said to the brāhman: "What faults do the sons of the Śākya clan have compared to you?"

佛告摩纳：「诸释种子，何过于卿？」

The brāhman said: "Once, I had a minor matter to attend to for my teacher in the country of Kapilavastu of the Śākya clan. At that time, many sons of the Śākya clan were gathered in the lecture hall for some minor reason. When they saw me coming from afar, they treated me with contempt and ridicule, not following proper etiquette and not showing respect."

摩纳言：「昔我一时为师少缘，在释迦迦维罗越国。时，有众多诸释种子，以少因缘集在讲堂，遥见我来，轻慢戏弄，不顺仪法，不相敬待。」

The Buddha said to the brāhman: "Those sons of the Śākya clan are still in their own country, enjoying themselves freely, just like birds in their own forest, coming and going freely. The sons of the Śākya clan enjoy themselves freely in their own country in the same way."

佛告摩纳：「彼诸释子还在本国，游戏自恣，犹如飞鸟自于樔林，出入自在。诸释种子自于本国，游戏自在，亦复如是。」

The brāhman said to the Buddha: "In the world, there are four castes - kṣatriyas, brāhmaṇas, vaiśyas, and śūdras. The other three castes always respect, honor, and make offerings to brāhmaṇas. Those sons of the Śākya clan should not be like that. Those Śākyas are vulgar, lowly, and inferior, yet they do not respect me, a brāhmaṇa."

摩纳白佛言：「世有四姓——刹利、婆罗门、居士、首陀罗，其彼三姓，常尊重、恭敬、供养婆罗门；彼诸释子义不应尔。彼释厮细、卑陋、下劣，而不恭敬我婆罗门。」

At that time, the World-Honored One silently thought to himself: "This brāhman son repeatedly insults me with vulgar words. Should I now speak of his past causes and conditions to subdue him?" The Buddha asked the brāhman: "What is your family name?"

尔时，世尊默自念言：「此摩纳子！数数毁骂言及厮细，我今宁可说其本缘调伏之耶！」佛告摩纳：「汝姓何等？」

The brāhman replied: "My family name is Śroṇakoṭīviṃśa."

摩纳答言：「我姓声王。」

The Buddha told the brāhman: "If your family name is that, then you are a slave of the Śākya family."

佛告摩纳：「汝姓尔者，则为是释迦奴种。」

At that time, the five hundred brāhman disciples all raised their voices and said to the Buddha: "Do not say that this brāhman is a slave of the Śākya family! Why is that? This great brāhman is a true noble son, with a handsome appearance, eloquent and responsive, widely learned and well-versed, worthy to converse with Gautama."

时，彼五百摩纳弟子，皆举大声而语佛言：「勿说此言！谓：『此摩纳为释迦奴种。』所以者何？此大摩纳，真族姓子，颜貌端正，辩才应机，广博多闻，足与瞿昙往返谈论。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the five hundred brāhman disciples: "If your teacher is not as you say, then abandon your teacher and discuss with me; but if your teacher has the above qualities as you say, then you should remain silent and discuss with your teacher."

尔时，世尊告五百摩纳：「若汝师尽不如汝言者，当舍汝师共汝论义；若汝师有如上事如汝言者，汝等宜默，当共汝师论。」

At that time, the five hundred brāhman disciples said to the Buddha: "We will all remain silent and listen to the discussion with our teacher." At that time, the five hundred brāhman disciples all remained silent.

时，五百摩纳白佛言：「我等尽默，听共师论。」时，五百摩纳尽皆默然。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Āmrapālī: "Long ago, in the past, there was a king named Śaṅkha. The king had four sons: the first was named Mukha-prabha, the second was named Hastyaśna, the third was named Rājñi, and the fourth was named Vibhūṣita. When the four sons of the king were young and committed a minor offense, the king banished them from the country to the south of the Himalayas, where they lived in a straight-tree forest. The mothers and relatives of the four sons all missed them and gathered together to discuss it. They went to King Śaṅkha and said: 'Great King! You should know that we have been separated from the four sons for a long time and want to go and see them.' The king then said: 'If you want to go, you may.' At that time, the mothers and relatives, having received the king's order, went to the straight-tree forest south of the Himalayas and arrived at the place of the four sons. The mothers said: 'My daughter will be given to your son, and your daughter will be given to my son. They will be matched in marriage and eventually become husband and wife. Later, they will give birth to a son who is handsome and upright.'

尔时，世尊告阿摩昼：「乃往过去久远世时，有王名声摩。王有四子：一名面光，二名象食，三名路指，四名庄严。其王四子少有所犯，王摈出国到雪山南，住直树林中，其四子母及诸家属，皆追念之，即共集议，诣声摩王所，白言：『大王！当知我等与四子别久，欲往看视。』王即告曰：『欲往随意。』时，母眷属闻王教已，即诣雪山南直树林中，到四子所。时诸母言：『我女与汝子，汝女与我子，即相配匹遂成夫妇，后生男子，容貌端正。』

"At that time, King Śaṅkha heard that the mothers of the four sons had married their daughters to the sons and given birth to handsome sons. The king was delighted and uttered these words: 'This is truly a son of the Śākya clan, a young man of the Śākya clan!' He was able to support himself. For this reason, he was named Śākya. King Śaṅkha was the first of the Śākya clan. The king had a blue-clothed servant named Āmrapālī, who was handsome. She had an affair with a brāhman and became pregnant. She gave birth to a brāhman boy who could speak as soon as he was born. He immediately said to his parents: 'Wash me and remove all impurities. When I grow up, I will repay your kindness.' Because he could speak as soon as he was born, he was named Śaṅkha. From now on, the brāhman clan took Śaṅkha as their surname."

「时，声摩王闻其四子诸母与女共为夫妇，生子端正，王即欢喜，而发此言：『此真释子！真释童子！』能自存立，因此名释。声摩王即释种先也。王有青衣，名曰方面，颜貌端正，与一婆罗门交通，遂便有娠，生一摩纳子，堕地能言，寻语父母：『当洗浴我，除诸秽恶；我年大已，自当报恩。』以其初生能言，故名声王。如今初生有能言者，人皆怖畏，名为可畏；彼亦如是，生便能言，故名声王。从此已来，婆罗门种遂以声王为姓。」

He also asked the brāhman: "Have you ever heard from the elders of the brāhman clan about the origin of this clan?"

又告摩纳：「汝颇从先宿耆旧大婆罗门，闻此种姓因缘已不？」

At that time, the brāhman was silent and did not reply. When asked a second time, he still did not reply. When asked a third time, the Buddha said to the brāhman: "I have asked you three times. You should quickly answer. If you do not answer, the Vajrapani Bodhisattva, who is holding a golden vajra at my side, will immediately smash your head into seven pieces."

时，彼摩纳默然不对。如是再问，又复不对。佛至三问，语摩纳言：「吾问至三，汝宜速答，设不答者，密迹力士手执金杵在吾左右，即当破汝头为七分。」

At that time, the Vajrapani Bodhisattva stood in the sky above the brāhman's head, holding a golden vajra. If the brāhman did not answer the question immediately, the Vajrapani Bodhisattva would strike the brāhman's head with the golden vajra. The Buddha said to the brāhman: "You should look up."

时，密迹力士手执金杵，当摩纳头上虚空中立，若摩纳不时答问，即下金杵碎摩纳首。佛告摩纳：「汝可仰观。」

The brāhman looked up and saw the Vajrapani Bodhisattva standing in the sky above his head, holding a golden vajra. Seeing this, he was terrified and his hair stood on end. He then got up and moved closer to sit near the World-Honored One, relying on the World-Honored One for protection and refuge. He said to the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, please ask. I will now answer."

摩纳仰观，见密迹力士手执金杵立虚空中，见已恐怖，衣毛为竖，即起移坐附近世尊，依恃世尊为救为护，白世尊言：「世尊当问，我今当答。」

The Buddha then said to the brāhman: "Have you ever heard from the elder brāhmanas about the lineage of the brāhmanas?"

佛即告摩纳：「汝曾于先宿耆旧大婆罗门，闻说如是种姓缘不？」

The brāhman replied: "I believe I have heard of this before."

摩纳答言：「我信曾闻，实有是事。」

At that time, the five hundred brāhman disciples all raised their voices and said to each other: "This Āmrapālī is indeed a slave of the Śākya clan. What the śramaṇa Gautama said is true. We were presumptuous and had disrespectful thoughts."

时，五百摩纳弟子，皆各举声自相谓言：「此阿摩昼，实是释迦奴种也。沙门瞿昙所说真实，我等无状，怀轻慢心。」

At that time, the World-Honored One had this thought: "These five hundred brāhman disciples will later harbor arrogance and call him a slave. I should now use skillful means to eliminate the name of slave." He then told the five hundred brāhman disciples: "You all should not call him a slave. Why? Because that former brāhman was a great sage with great power. King Vaśiṣṭha asked for his daughter, and the king, out of fear, gave her to him." Because of the Buddha's words, he was spared the name of slave.

尔时，世尊便作是念：「此五百摩纳后必怀慢，称彼为奴，今当方便灭其奴名。」即告五百摩纳曰：「汝等诸人！慎勿称彼为奴种也。所以者何？彼先婆罗门是大仙人，有大威力，伐声摩王索女，王以畏故，即以女与。」由佛此言得免奴名。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Āmrapālī: "What do you think, Āmrapālī? If a kṣatriya girl has parents who are genuine for seven generations and are not belittled by others, and if she marries a brāhman and gives birth to a child, Āmrapālī, if that child is handsome, can he enter the kṣatriya caste, sit and receive water, and recite the kṣatriya Dharma?"

尔时，世尊告阿摩昼曰：「云何？摩纳！若刹利女七世已来父母真正，不为他人之所轻毁，若与一婆罗门为妻生子，摩纳！容貌端正，彼入刹利种，得坐受水诵刹利法不？」

He replied: "No, he cannot."

答曰：「不得。」

"Can he inherit his father's wealth?"

「得父财业不？」

He replied: "No, he cannot."

答曰：「不得。」

"Can he succeed to his father's position?"

「得嗣父职不？」

He replied: "No, he cannot."

答曰：「不得。」

"What do you think, Mānava? If a brahmin girl has parents who are pure for seven generations, not despised by others, and she marries a kṣatriya, and they have a son who is handsome, can that son sit, stand, and receive water in the assembly of brahmins?"

「云何？摩纳！若婆罗门女七世以来父母真正，不为他人之所轻毁，与刹利为妻，生一童子，颜貌端正，彼入婆罗门众中，得坐起受水不？」

He replied: "He can."

答曰：「得。」

"Can he recite the brahmin teachings, inherit his father's property, and succeed to his father's position?"

「得诵婆罗门法，得父遗财，嗣父职不？」

He replied: "He can."

答曰：「得。」

"What do you think, Mānava? If a brahmin is expelled by the brahmins and takes refuge with the kṣatriyas, can he then sit, rise, receive water, and recite the kṣatriya teachings?"

「云何，摩纳！若婆罗门摈婆罗门投刹利种者，宁得坐起受水，诵刹利法不？」

He replied: "He cannot."

答曰：「不得。」

"Can he inherit his father's property and succeed to his father's position?"

「得父遗财，嗣父职不？」

He replied: "He cannot."

答曰：「不得。」

"If a kshatriya is expelled from the kshatriya caste and joins the brahmin caste, can he then sit, stand, and receive water, recite the brahmin teachings, inherit his father's property, and succeed to his father's position?"

「若刹利种摈刹利投婆罗门，宁得坐起受水，诵婆罗门法，得父遗财，嗣父职不？」

He replied: "He can."

答曰：「得。」

Therefore, Monastics, among women, a kshatriya woman is superior, and among men, a kshatriya man is superior, not a brahmin.

「是故，摩纳！女中刹利女胜，男中刹利男胜，非婆罗门也。」

「比丘有如是圣戒，得圣诸根，食知止足，初夜后夜，精勤觉悟，常念一心，无有错乱，乐在静处、树下、冢间。若在山窟，或在露地及粪聚间，至时乞食，还洗手足，安置衣钵，结跏趺坐，端身正意，系念在前。除去悭贪，心不与俱，灭瞋恨心，无有怨结，心住清净，常怀慈愍；除去睡眠，系想在明，念无错乱，断除掉戏，心不与俱。内行寂灭，灭掉戏心，断除疑惑，已度疑网，其心专一，在于善法。譬如僮仆，大家赐姓，安隐解脱，免于仆使，其心欢喜，无复忧畏。

They think to themselves: 'I was imprisoned before, but now I am free. I am no longer worried or afraid, and I am very happy.' It is also like a person who has traveled through a dangerous wilderness with a lot of money and valuables, and has not been robbed by thieves. They have safely passed through the wilderness and think to themselves: 'I have passed through this dangerous wilderness with my money and valuables. 「又如有人举财治生，大得利还，还本主物，余财足用，彼自念言：『我本举财，恐不如意，今得利还，还主本物，余财足用，无复忧畏，发大欢喜。』如人久病，从病得差，饮食消化，色力充足，彼作是念：『我先有病，而今得差，饮食消化，色力充足，无复忧畏，发大欢喜。』又如人久闭牢狱，安隐得出，彼自念言：『我先拘闭，今已解脱，无复忧畏，发大欢喜。』又如人多持财宝，经大旷野，不遭贼盗，安隐得过，彼自念言：『我持财宝过此崄难，无复忧畏发大欢喜，其心安乐。』

It is like driving a four-horse carriage on level ground. A good driver holds the reins and whip, preventing the horses from straying from the path. 「摩纳！比丘有五盖自覆，常怀忧畏亦复如奴。如负债人、久病在狱、行大旷野，自见未离，诸阴盖心，覆蔽暗冥，慧眼不明，彼即精勤舍欲、恶不善法，与觉、观俱，离生喜、乐，得入初禅。彼已喜乐润渍于身，周遍盈溢，无不充满。如人巧浴器盛众药，以水渍之，中外俱润，无不周遍。比丘如是得入初禅，喜乐遍身，无不充满。如是，摩纳！是为最初现身得乐，所以者何？斯由精进，念无错乱，乐静闲居之所得也。

They give rise to faith in the absence of perception and contemplation, and with single-minded concentration, they enter the second dhyāna, which is free from perception and contemplation, and gives rise to joy and happiness. The joy and happiness permeate their bodies, filling them completely, just like cool spring water on a mountain top that does not come from outside, but arises from within the pond itself, and the clear water from the pond permeates everything. In the same way, monastics, when a bhikṣu enters the second dhyāna, the joy and happiness permeate their bodies, filling them completely. This is the second kind of happiness experienced in this life. It is also like a person who applies medicine to a wound to heal it, not for decoration or pride. In the same way, monastics, bhikṣus eat to sustain their bodies, without arrogance or indulgence. It is also like oiling a cart to make it run smoothly and be able to carry things to their destination. Bhikṣus are like this, eating to sustain their bodies, to practice the path.

「彼于觉、观，便生为信，专念一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，入第二禅。彼已一心喜乐润渍于身，周遍盈溢，无不充满，犹如山顶凉泉水自中出，不从外来，即此池中出清净水，还自浸渍，无不周遍。摩纳！比丘如是入第二禅，定生喜、乐，无不充满，是为第二现身得乐。

They abandon joy and abide in protection and mindfulness, without distraction. They experience physical happiness, as described by the sages, and give rise to the happiness of protection and mindfulness, entering the third dhyāna. Their bodies are free from joy, and happiness permeates them, filling them completely, just like blue lotus, red lotus, white lotus, and white water lily flowers that have just emerged from the mud and not yet out of the water, with their roots, stems, branches, and leaves permeated by the water, filling them completely. In the same way, monastics, when a bhikṣu enters the third dhyāna, they are free from joy, and happiness permeates their bodies, filling them completely. This is the third kind of happiness experienced in this life. It is also like a person who applies medicine to a wound to heal it, not for decoration or pride. In the same way, monastics, bhikṣus eat to sustain their bodies, without arrogance or indulgence. It is also like oiling a cart to make it run smoothly and be able to carry things to their destination. Bhikṣus are like this, eating to sustain their bodies, to practice the path.

「彼舍喜、住，护念不错乱，身受快乐，如圣所说，起护念乐，入第三禅。彼身无喜，以乐润渍，周遍盈溢，无不充满，譬如优钵花、钵头摩华、拘头摩花、分陀利花，始出淤泥而未出水，根茎枝叶润渍水中，无不周遍。摩纳！比丘如是入第三禅。离喜、住乐，润渍于身，无不周遍，此是第三现身得乐。

They abandon joy and happiness, and before joy and happiness are extinguished, they experience neither suffering nor happiness, and abide in protection and mindfulness, entering the fourth dhyāna. Their minds and bodies are pure, full, and overflowing, filling them completely. It is like a person who bathes and cleanses, and then puts on a new white robe, with their entire body pure. In the same way, monastics, when a bhikṣu enters the fourth dhyāna, their mind is pure, filling their body, filling it completely. They also enter the fourth dhyāna, with their mind neither increasing nor decreasing, not wavering, abiding in a place free from love and hatred, without movement. It is like a tightly sealed room, with the walls and windows well-sealed, without wind or dust, and a lamp burning inside without being disturbed. The flame of the lamp burns steadily without wavering. In the same way, monastics, when a bhikṣu enters the fourth dhyāna, their mind neither increases nor decreases, not wavering, abiding in a place free from love and hatred, without movement. This is the fourth kind of happiness experienced in this life. Why is this? It is because of diligent practice without laziness, mindfulness without distraction, and the attainment of joyful seclusion.

「彼舍喜、乐，忧、喜先灭，不苦不乐，护念清净，入第四禅，身心清净，具满盈溢，无不周遍。犹如有人沐浴清洁，以新白叠被覆其身，举体清净。摩纳！比丘如是入第四禅，其心清净，充满于身，无不周遍，又入第四禅，心无增减，亦不倾动，住无爱恚、无动之地。譬如密室，内外涂治，坚闭户向，无有风尘，于内燃灯无触娆者，其灯焰上怗然不动。摩纳！比丘如是入第四禅，心无增减，亦不倾动，住无爱恚、无动之地，此是第四现身得乐，所以者何？斯由精勤不懈，念不错乱，乐静闲居之所得也。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and give rise to a mind of transformation within their own body, manifesting a different body with complete limbs and faculties without deficiency. They contemplate thus: 'This body is transformed into that body by the four great elements. This body is different, and that body is different. The mind arises from this body and transforms into that body, with complete faculties and limbs without deficiency.' It is like a person drawing a sword from its scabbard, thinking: 'The scabbard is different from the sword, but the sword comes from the scabbard.' It is also like a person making a rope from hemp, thinking: 'The hemp is different from the rope, but the rope comes from the hemp.' It is also like a person taking a snake out of a box, thinking: 'The box is different from the snake, but the snake comes from the box.' It is also like a person taking clothes out of a chest, thinking: 'The chest is different from the clothes, but the clothes come from the chest.' Monastics, in the same way, this is the first supreme quality attained by bhikṣus. Why is this? It is because of diligent practice without laziness, mindfulness without distraction, and the attainment of joyful seclusion.

「彼得定心，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住无动地，自于身中起变化心，化作异身，支节具足，诸根无阙，彼作是观：『此身色四大化成彼身，此身亦异，彼身亦异，从此身起心，化成彼身，诸根具足，支节无阙。』譬如有人鞘中拔刀，彼作是念：『鞘异刀异，而刀从鞘出。』又如有人合麻为绳，彼作是念：『麻异绳异，而绳从麻出。』又如有人箧中出蛇，彼作是念：『箧异蛇异，而蛇从箧出。』又如有人从簏出衣，彼作是念：『簏异衣异，而衣从簏出。』摩纳！比丘亦如是，此是最初所得胜法。所以者何？斯由精进，念不错乱，乐静闲居之所得也。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and give rise to a mind of transformation within their own body, manifesting a transformed body with complete faculties and limbs. They contemplate thus: 'This body is composed of the four great elements, and that body arises from transformation. This body is different, and that body is different. This mind abides in this body, relying on this body, and reaches the transformed body.' It is like a clear and pure beryl or mani gem, if a blue, yellow, or red thread is passed through it, a person with eyes can place it in the palm and see that the gem is different from the thread, but the thread relies on the gem and goes from gem to gem. Monastics, in the same way, bhikṣus contemplate the mind relying on this body and reaching the transformed body. This is also the same. This is the second supreme quality of bhikṣus. Why is this? It is because of diligent practice without laziness, mindfulness without distraction, and the attainment of joyful seclusion.

「彼已定心，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住无动地，从己四大色身中起心，化作化身，一切诸根、支节具足，彼作是观：『此身是四大合成，彼身从化而有，此身亦异，彼身亦异，此心在此身中，依此身住，至化身中。』譬如琉璃、摩尼，莹治甚明，清净无秽，若以青、黄、赤𫄧贯之，有目之士置掌而观，知珠异𫄧异，而𫄧依于珠，从珠至珠。摩纳！比丘观心依此身住，至彼化身亦复如是，此是比丘第二胜法。所以者何？斯由精勤，念不错乱，乐独闲居之所得也。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and cultivate the wisdom of spiritual powers with a single mind, able to transform in various ways, transforming one body into countless bodies, and combining countless bodies back into one. The body can fly, unimpeded by mountains or walls; roaming the sky like a bird, walking on water as if on land; emitting smoke and flames from the body, like a great fire heap; touching the sun and moon with the hand, standing up to reach the Brahma Heaven. It is like a potter skillfully kneading clay, able to create whatever vessel he wishes, benefiting many. It is also like a skilled carpenter skillfully working with wood, able to freely create whatever he wishes, benefiting many. It is also like an ivory carver skillfully carving ivory, and like a goldsmith skillfully refining gold, able to create whatever he wishes, benefiting many. Monastics, in the same way, bhikṣus with a concentrated mind, pure, abiding in a place without movement, able to transform at will, up to touching the sun and moon with the hand, standing up to reach the Brahma Heaven, this is also the same. This is the third supreme quality of bhikṣus.

「彼以定心，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住无动地，一心修习神通智证，能种种变化，变化一身为无数身，以无数身还合为一；身能飞行，石壁无碍；游空如鸟，履水如地；身出烟燄，如大火［卄／积］；手扪日月，立至梵天。譬如陶师善调和泥，随意所造，任作何器，多所饶益。亦如巧匠善能治木，随意所造，自在能成，多所饶益。又如牙师善治象牙，亦如金师善炼真金，随意所造，多所饶益。摩纳！比丘如是，定心清净，住无动地，随意变化，乃至手扪日月，立至梵天，此是比丘第三胜法。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and cultivate the wisdom of the divine ear with a single mind. Their divine ear is pure, surpassing the human ear, hearing two kinds of sounds: heavenly sounds and human sounds. It is like a large lecture hall in a city, high, wide, and open, with a person of sharp hearing dwelling inside. The sounds inside the hall are heard without any effort, and all kinds of sounds are heard. In the same way, bhikṣus, with a concentrated mind, their divine ear is pure, hearing two kinds of sounds. Monastics, this is the fourth supreme quality of bhikṣus.

「彼以心定，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住无动地，一心修习，证天耳智。彼天耳净，过于人耳，闻二种声：天声、人声。譬如城内有大讲堂，高广显敞，有聪听人居此堂内，堂内有声，不劳听功，种种悉闻；比丘如是，以心定故，天耳清净，闻二种声。摩纳！此是比丘第四胜法。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and cultivate the wisdom of knowing others' minds with a single mind. They know others' minds with desire or without desire, with defilements or without defilements, with delusion or without delusion, with broad minds or narrow minds, with small minds or great minds, with concentrated minds or distracted minds, with bound minds or liberated minds, with superior minds or inferior minds, up to the unsurpassed mind, all of which they know. It is like a person using clear water to reflect on themselves, carefully examining the good and bad. In the same way, bhikṣus, with a pure mind, are able to know others' minds. Monastics, this is the fifth supreme quality of bhikṣus.

「彼以定心，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住无动地，一心修习，证他心智。彼知他心有欲无欲、有垢无垢、有痴无痴、广心狭心、小心大心、定心乱心、缚心解心、上心下心，至无上心皆悉知之。譬如有人以清水自照，好恶必察；比丘如是，以心净故，能知他心。摩纳！此是比丘第五胜法。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and cultivate the wisdom of knowing past lives with a single mind. They are able to remember countless past lives, able to remember one lifetime up to countless lifetimes, the formation and destruction of eons, dying here and being born there, names, surnames, clans, food and drink, good and bad, lifespan, long and short, suffering and happiness, and physical appearance, all of which they remember. It is like a person traveling from their own village to another country, walking or staying, speaking or silent. Monastics, bhikṣus are like this, able to use a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, abiding in a place without movement, with the wisdom of knowing past lives, able to remember countless eons of past events. This is the first supreme quality of bhikṣus. Ignorance is forever extinguished, and the Dharma of great illumination arises. Darkness and obscurity are eliminated, and the Dharma of illumination arises. This is the wisdom and illumination of bhikṣus knowing past lives. Why is this? It is because of diligent practice, mindfulness without confusion, and the attainment of enjoying solitary seclusion.

「彼以心定，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住无动地，一心修习宿命智证，便能忆识宿命无数若干种事，能忆一生至无数生，劫数成败、死此生彼、名姓种族、饮食好恶、寿命长短、所受苦乐、形色相貌皆悉忆识。譬如有人，从己村落至他国邑，在于彼处，若行若住，若语若默，复从彼国至于余国，如是展转便还本土，不劳心力，尽能忆识所行诸国，从此到彼，从彼到此，行住语默，皆悉忆之。摩纳！比丘如是，能以定心清净无秽，住无动地，以宿命智能忆宿命无数劫事，此是比丘得第一胜。无明永灭，大明法生，暗冥消灭，光曜法生，此是比丘宿命智明。所以者何？斯由精勤，念无错乱，乐独闲居之所得也。

They attain a concentrated mind, pure, undefiled, pliant, subdued, abiding in a place without movement, and cultivate the wisdom of seeing birth and death with a single mind. Their heavenly eye becomes pure, seeing all beings dying here and being born there, from there being born here, with good or bad appearances, good or bad results, noble or humble, according to the causes and conditions of their karmic retribution, all of which they know. They know that this person, with evil bodily actions, evil speech, and evil thoughts, slandering the sages, believing in wrong views, will, upon the failure of their body and the end of their life, fall into the three evil paths. They know that this person, with good bodily actions, good speech, and good thoughts, not slandering the sages, seeing the right and practicing accordingly, will, upon the failure of their body and the end of their life, be born in the heavens or among humans. With their pure heavenly eye, they see all beings going and coming in the five paths according to their karmic conditions. It is like a tall and wide building in the center of a city, at the crossroads, where a person with clear eyes can look out and see all the people coming and going in the four directions, their movements and actions, all of which they can see. Monastics, bhikṣus are like this, with a concentrated mind, pure, abiding in a place without movement, seeing the wisdom of birth and death. With their pure heavenly eye, they see all beings' good and evil deeds, and according to their karma, they are born and go and come in the five paths, all of which they know. This is the second illumination of bhikṣus. Cutting off ignorance and giving rise to the illumination of wisdom, abandoning darkness and emitting the light of wisdom, this is the illumination of seeing the birth and death of beings. Why is this? It is because of diligent practice, mindfulness without confusion, and the attainment of enjoying solitary seclusion.

It is because of diligent practice, mindfulness without confusion, and the attainment of enjoying solitary seclusion. Monastics, this is the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice. What do you think? Is this illumination and practice so or not?"

「彼以定心，清净无秽，柔濡调伏，住不动地，一心修习无漏智证。彼如实知苦圣谛，如实知有漏集，如实知有漏尽，如实知趣漏尽道。彼如是知、如是见，欲漏、有漏、无明漏，心得解脱，得解脱智，生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不受后有。譬如清水中，有木石、鱼鳖水性之属东西游行，有目之士明了见之：此是木石，此是鱼鳖。摩纳！比丘如是，以定心清净，住无动地，得无漏智证，乃至不受后有，此是比丘得第三明。断除无明，生于慧明，舍离暗冥，出大智光，是为无漏智明。所以者何？斯由精勤，念不错乱，乐独闲居之所得也。摩纳！是为无上明行具足，于汝意云何？如是明行为是，为非？」

"The Buddha told Mānava, "There are people who cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice, but they practice four methods. What are the four? Mānava! Some people cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice, but they carry axes and baskets, enter the mountains to seek medicine, and eat tree roots. This, Mānava! is the first method of those who cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice. What do you think, Mānava? Do you and your teacher practice this method?

佛告摩纳：「有人不能得无上明行具足，而行四方便，云何为四？摩纳！或有人不得无上明行具足，而持斫负笼，入山求药，食树木根。是为，摩纳！不得无上明行具足，而行第一方便。云何？摩纳！此第一方便，汝及汝师行此法不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

The Buddha told Mānava, "You are lowly and ignorant of truth and falsehood. You slander and insult the Śramaṇas, planting the roots of sin and growing the basis for hell. Furthermore, Mānava! Some people cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice, but they hold a water pot in their hands, carry a staff and counting device, enter the forest, and eat naturally fallen fruit. This, Mānava! is the second method of those who cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice. What do you think, Mānava? Do you and your teacher practice this method?"

佛告摩纳：「汝自卑微，不识真伪，而便诽谤，轻骂释子，自种罪根，长地狱本。复次，摩纳！有人不能得无上明行具足，而手执澡瓶，持杖筭术，入山林中，食自落果。是为，摩纳！不得无上明行具足，而行第二方便，云何？摩纳！汝及汝师行此法不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

The Buddha told Mānava, "You are lowly and ignorant of truth and falsehood. You slander and insult the Śramaṇas, planting the roots of sin and growing the basis for hell. Furthermore, Mānava! Those who cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice, having abandoned the previous gathering of medicine and picking up fallen fruit, return to the village and rely on humans, building grass huts and eating grass and leaves. Mānava! This is the third method of those who cannot attain the fulfillment of illumination and practice. What do you think, Mānava? Do you and your teacher practice this method?"

佛告摩纳：「汝自卑微，不识真伪，而便诽谤，轻慢释子，自种罪根，长地狱本。复次，摩纳！不得无上明行具足，而舍前采药及拾落果，还来向村依附人间，起草庵舍，食草木叶。摩纳！是为不得明行具足，而行第三方便。云何？摩纳！汝及汝师行此法不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

The Buddha told Mānava, "You are lowly and ignorant of truth and falsehood. You slander and insult the Śramaṇas, planting the roots of sin and growing the basis for hell. This is the third method. Furthermore, Mānava! Those who cannot attain the unsurpassed fulfillment of illumination and practice, not eating herbs, not eating fallen fruit, not eating grass and leaves, but building large halls and pavilions in villages and cities, providing for those who pass by from the east, west, south, and north according to their ability. This is the fourth method of those who cannot attain the fulfillment of illumination and practice. What do you think, Mānava? Do you and your teacher practice this method?"

佛告摩纳：「汝自卑微，不识真伪，而便诽谤，轻慢释子，自种罪根，长地狱本，是为第三方便。复次，摩纳！不得无上明行具足，不食药草，不食落果，不食草叶，而于村城起大堂阁，诸有东西南北行人过者随力供给，是为不得无上明行具足，而行第四方便。云何？摩纳！汝及汝师行此法不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

The Buddha told Mānava, "You are lowly and ignorant of truth and falsehood. You slander and insult the Śramaṇas, planting the roots of sin and growing the basis for hell. What do you think, Mānava? The ancient Brahmins and sages had many skills, praising and reciting what they had memorized, just like the Brahmins today recite: Aṭaṃ, Bhama, Bhamadeva, Nāpāmitra, Idhāsita, Yabatika, Pappavasita, Kāśyapa, Āruṇa, Gautama, Śiva, and Sontara. These great sages and Brahmins all dug moats and built halls and pavilions, like the dwelling places of your teachers and disciples now."

佛告摩纳：「汝自卑微，不识真伪，而便诽谤，轻慢释子，自种罪根，长地狱本，云何？摩纳！诸旧婆罗门及诸仙人多诸伎术，赞叹称说本所诵习，如今婆罗门所可讽诵称说：一、阿咤摩，二、婆摩，三、婆摩提婆，四、鼻波密多，五、伊兜濑悉，六、耶婆提伽，七、婆婆婆悉咤，八、迦叶，九、阿楼那，十、瞿昙，十一、首夷婆，十二、损陀罗。如此诸大仙、婆罗门皆掘堑建立堂阁，如汝师徒今所居止不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Did those great sages build cities and encircle their dwellings, living within them, like your teachers and disciples now?"

「彼诸大仙颇起城墎，围遶舍宅，居止其中，如汝师徒今所止不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Did those great sages use high beds and heavy quilts, soft and fine, like your teachers and disciples now?"

「彼诸大仙颇处高床重褥，綩𫄧细软，如汝师徒今所止不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Did those great sages enjoy themselves with gold, silver, necklaces, colorful flower garlands, and beautiful women, like your teachers and disciples? Did those great sages ride in jeweled carriages, with a golden spear leading the way, a white parasol covering them, a hand-held jeweled whisk, wearing colorful jeweled slippers, and also wearing all-white cloth, like what your teachers and disciples wear now?"

「彼诸大仙颇以金银、璎珞、杂色花鬘、美女自娱，如汝师徒不？彼诸大仙颇驾乘宝车，持［金＊戟］导引，白盖自覆，手执宝拂，著杂色宝屣，又著全白叠，如汝师徒今所服不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Monas, you are lowly and ignorant of truth and falsehood. You slander and belittle the disciples of the Buddha, planting the roots of sin and growing the basis for hell. How is it, Monas? Those great sages and ancient Brahmins praised and spoke of what they chanted in the past, just as Brahmins now praise and chant about Atthama and others. If one transmits what they say to teach others, hoping to be born in the Brahma Heaven, it is impossible. It is like, Monas, when King Pasenadi discusses with others, or with other kings, or with ministers, Brahmins, or householders, and other ordinary people hear it and enter Savatthi City, encountering people and immediately speaking of King Pasenadi's words. How is it, Monas? Did the king discuss with these people?"

「摩纳！汝自卑微，不识真伪，而便诽谤，轻慢释子，自种罪根，长地狱本。云何？摩纳！如彼诸大仙、旧婆罗门，赞叹称说本所讽诵，如今婆罗门所可称说讽诵阿咤摩等，若传彼所说，以教他人，欲望生梵天者，无有是处。犹如，摩纳！王波斯匿与人共议，或与诸王，或与大臣、婆罗门、居士共论，余细人闻，入舍卫城，遇人便说波斯匿王有如是语。云何？摩纳！王与是人共言议不？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Monas, if this person chants the king's words and speaks them to others, can he become a minister of the king?"

「摩纳！此人讽诵王言以语余人，宁得为王作大臣不？」

He replied, "There is no such possibility."

答曰：「无有是处。」

"Monas, today you transmit the teachings of the ancient sages and old Brahmins, chanting them to others, wishing to be reborn in the Brahma heaven. There is no such possibility. How is it, Monas? Can you practice according to the Dharma while receiving offerings from others?"

「摩纳！汝等今日传先宿、大仙、旧婆罗门，讽诵教人，欲至生梵天者，无有是处。云何？摩纳！汝等受他供养，能随法行不？」

He replied, "Yes, Gautama! When receiving offerings from others, we should practice according to the Dharma."

答曰：「如是，瞿昙！受他供养，当如法行。」

"Monas, your teacher Vepacitti received a village grant from the king. When discussing with King Pasenadi, he spoke words that were not beneficial to the king. He did not use proper means to admonish and instruct each other. Now you should consider your own and your teacher's faults. Let's put that aside for now. You should just seek the reason for your coming."

「摩纳！汝师沸伽罗娑罗受王村封，而与王波斯匿共论议时，说王不要论无益之言，不以正事共相谏晓，汝今自观及汝师过，且置是事，但当求汝所来因缘。」

The youth immediately looked up at the Tathāgata's body, seeking the marks and characteristics. He saw all the other marks, but he did not see two marks. He was then filled with doubt. At that time, the World-Honored One silently thought to himself, "Now this youth does not see two marks, and because of this he has doubts." He then extended his broad and long tongue, licking his ears and covering his face. At that time, the youth still doubted one mark. The World-Honored One again thought, "Now this youth still doubts one mark." He then used his spiritual power to cause the youth to see the hidden male organ alone. At that time, when the youth had seen all the marks, he no longer had any doubts about the Tathāgata. He then rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha, and left.

摩纳即时举目观如来身，求诸相好，尽见余相，唯不见二相，心即怀疑。尔时，世尊默自念言：「今此摩纳不见二相，以此生疑。」即出广长舌相，舐耳覆面。时，彼摩纳复疑一相，世尊复念：「今此摩纳犹疑一相。」即以神力，使彼摩纳独见阴马藏。尔时，摩纳尽见相已，乃于如来无复狐疑，即从座起，遶佛而去。

At that time, the brāhman Vepacitti stood outside the gate. He looked far into the distance and saw his disciple coming. He asked him, "Did you see that the Tathāgata really has the marks? Are his merits and spiritual powers really as you have heard?"

时，沸伽罗婆罗门立于门外，遥望弟子，见其远来，逆问之言：「汝观瞿昙实具相不？功德神力实如所闻不？」

He then said to his teacher, "The Tathāgata Śramaṇa has all thirty-two marks in full. His merits and spiritual powers are truly as I have heard."

即白师言：「瞿昙沙门三十二相皆悉具足，功德神力实如所闻。」

The teacher asked again, "Did you have any conversation with the Tathāgata?"

师又问曰：「汝颇与瞿昙少语议不？」

He replied, "I did have some conversation with the Tathāgata."

答曰：「实与瞿昙言语往返。」

The teacher asked again, "What did you discuss with the Tathāgata?"

师又问曰：「汝与瞿昙共论何事？」

The teacher said, "I have gained a wise disciple who has caused this. We will soon enter hell. Why is this? You spoke of the superiority of desires and disparaged the Tathāgata, causing him displeasure and distancing us. You, with your wise disciple, have caused this, and we will soon enter hell." With a mind full of anger and resentment, the teacher kicked the student out of the cart and drove away himself. At that moment, the student was struck with leprosy.

时，摩纳如共佛论，具以白师，师言：「我遂得聪明弟子致使如是者，我等将入地狱不久，所以者何？汝语诸欲胜毁呰瞿昙，使之不悦，于我转疎，汝与聪明弟子致使如是，使我入地狱不久。」于是，其师怀忿结心，即蹴摩纳令堕，师自乘车。时，彼摩纳当堕车时，即生白癞。

At that time, the brāhmaṇa Vālmīki looked up at the sun and thought to himself, "Now is not the time to visit the śramaṇa Gautama. I shall wait until tomorrow to go and ask him questions." The next morning, he adorned his jeweled carriage and, surrounded by five hundred disciples, went to the Bamboo Grove. He got down from the carriage and walked to where the World-Honored One was. After greeting him, he sat to one side and looked up at the Tathāgata's body, seeing all the marks, except for two.

时，沸伽罗娑罗婆罗门仰观日已，然自念言：「今觐沙门瞿昙，非是时也，须待明日，当往觐问。」于明日旦，严驾宝车，从五百弟子前后围遶，诣伊车林中，下车步进，到世尊所，问讯已，一面坐，仰观如来身，具见诸相，唯不见二相。

At that time, the brāhmaṇa had doubts about two marks. The Buddha knew his thoughts and immediately extended his broad and long tongue to lick his ears and cover his face. At that time, the brāhmaṇa again had doubts about one mark. The Buddha, knowing his thoughts, used his spiritual power to make him see the hidden male organ. At that time, the brāhmaṇa fully saw the Tathāgata's thirty-two marks, and his mind was immediately enlightened, without further doubt. He then said to the Buddha, "If I am on my way and happen to meet the Buddha, and stop my carriage for a short while, know that I have already paid homage to the World-Honored One. Why is this? If I stop my carriage when I am on my way, I will lose this fief and have a bad reputation spread."

时，婆罗门疑于二相，佛知其念，即出广长舌相，舐耳覆面。时，婆罗门又疑一相，佛知其念，即以神力，使见阴马藏。时，婆罗门具见如来三十二相，心即开悟，无复狐疑，寻白佛言：「若我行时，中路遇佛，少停止乘，当知我已礼敬世尊，所以者何？我受他村封，设下乘者，当失此封，恶声流布。」

He also said to the Buddha, "If I get down from my carriage, untie my sword, lower my canopy, remove my banner and flag, wash my water bottle, and take off my shoes, know that I have already paid homage to the Tathāgata. Why is this? Because I have received a fief from others, I have these five dignified manners. If I pay homage, I will lose the fief and have a bad reputation spread."

又白佛言：「若我下乘，解剑退盖，并除幢麾，澡瓶履屣，当知我已礼敬如来，所以者何？我受他封，故有五威仪，若礼拜者，即失所封，恶名流布。」

He also said to the Buddha, "If I am in a crowd and see the Buddha rise, if I bare my right arm, and introduce myself by my surname, then know that I have already paid homage to the Tathāgata. Why is this? Because I have received a fief from others, if I pay homage, I will lose the fief and have a bad reputation spread."

又白佛言：「若我在众见佛起者，若偏露右臂，自称姓字，则知我已敬礼如来，所以者何？我受他封，若礼拜者，则失封邑，恶名流布。」

He also said to the Buddha, "I take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, take refuge in the Sangha. Please allow me to be an upāsaka in the true Dharma! From now on, I will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol. I only wish that the World-Honored One and the great assembly would accept my invitation." At that time, the World-Honored One silently accepted the invitation.

又白佛言：「我归依佛，归依法，归依僧，听我于正法中为优婆塞！自今已后不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒，唯愿世尊及诸大众当受我请。」尔时，世尊默然受请。

At that time, the brāhmaṇa saw the Buddha silently accept, knowing that he had given permission. He immediately rose from his seat and, without realizing it, paid homage to the Buddha and circumambulated him three times before leaving. He returned to prepare the meal offerings. When the food was ready, he returned to inform, "The time has come."

时，婆罗门见佛默然，知以许可，即从坐起，不觉礼佛遶三匝而去。归设饭食，供饍既办，还白：「时到。」

At that time, the World-Honored One put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and went with the great assembly of 1,250 people to his house, where he took his seat.

尔时，世尊著衣持钵，与诸大众千二百五十人往诣其舍，就坐而坐。

At that time, the brāhmaṇa personally served various delicious foods to the Buddha and the Sangha. After the meal, the bowls were put away and the water for washing was served. The brāhmaṇa took the arm of his disciple Amacca with his right hand and went to the World-Honored One. He said, "May the Tathāgata allow him to repent! May the Tathāgata allow him to repent!" He said this three times. He then said to the Buddha, "Just as a well-trained elephant or horse may stumble but still return to the right path, so it is with this person. Although he has made mistakes, may he be allowed to repent."

时，婆罗门手自斟酌，以种种甘饍供佛及僧，食讫去钵，行澡水毕，时婆罗门右手执弟子阿摩昼臂至世尊前言：「唯愿如来听其悔过！唯愿如来听其悔过！」如是至三，又白佛言：「犹如善调象马，犹有蹶倒还复正路，此人如是，虽有漏失，愿听悔过。」

The Buddha told the brāhmaṇa, "I will make your lifespan long, and you will be at peace in this life. I will make your disciple's leprosy disappear." As soon as the Buddha spoke, the leprosy of the disciple disappeared.

佛告婆罗门：「当使汝寿命延长，现世安隐，使汝弟子白癞得除。」佛言适讫，时彼弟子白癞即除。

At that time, the brāhmaṇa took a small seat and sat in front of the Buddha. The World-Honored One then spoke the Dharma for the brāhmaṇa, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him, speaking about giving, precepts, and rebirth in heaven, about the defilement of desire, the suffering of the upper realms, and the supreme importance of liberation and purity. At that time, the World-Honored One knew that the brāhmaṇa's mind was already subdued, pure, and free from defilements, and that he was capable of receiving the teachings of the path. As is the constant practice of all Buddhas, he spoke about the noble truth of suffering, the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering. At that time, the brāhmaṇa immediately removed dust and defilements from his seat, attained the purity of the Dharma eye, just as a clean white cloth is easy to be dyed; so it was with the brāhmaṇa Bhaggava. He saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, determined the fruit of the path, no longer believed in other paths, and attained fearlessness. He then said to the Buddha, "I now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha three times. Please allow me to be an upāsaka in the true Dharma. For as long as I live, I will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol. I only wish that the World-Honored One and the great assembly would have compassion on me and accept my invitation for seven days." At that time, the World-Honored One silently accepted. At that time, the brāhmaṇa made various offerings to the Buddha and the great assembly for seven days. At that time, the World-Honored One, after seven days, traveled through the human world.

时，婆罗门取一小座于佛前坐，世尊即为婆罗门说法，示教利喜，施论、戒论、生天之论，欲为秽污，上漏为患，出要为上，演布清净。尔时，世尊知婆罗门心已调柔、清净、无垢，堪受道教，如诸佛常法，说苦圣谛、苦集圣谛、苦灭圣谛、苦出要谛。时，婆罗门即于座上远尘离垢，得法眼净，犹如净洁白叠，易为受染；沸伽罗娑罗婆罗门亦复如是，见法得法，决定道果，不信余道，得无所畏，即白佛言：「我今再三归依佛、法及比丘僧，听我于正法中为优婆塞！尽形寿不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒，唯愿世尊及诸大众哀愍我故，受七日请。」尔时，世尊默然许之。时，婆罗门即于七日中，种种供养佛及大众。尔时，世尊过七日已，游行人间。

Not long after the Buddha left, the brāhmaṇa Bhaggava fell ill and died. At that time, the bhikṣus heard that this brāhmaṇa had made offerings to the Buddha for seven days and then passed away. They each thought, "Where will this one be reborn after death?" At that time, the bhikṣus went to where the World-Honored One was, paid homage to the Buddha, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "This brāhmaṇa made offerings to the Buddha for seven days and then passed away. Where will he be reborn?"

佛去未久，沸伽罗娑罗婆罗门遇病命终。时诸比丘闻此婆罗门于七日中供养佛已，便取命终，各自念：「此命终，为生何趣？」尔时，众比丘往至世尊所，礼佛已，一面坐，白佛言：「彼婆罗门于七日中供养佛已，身坏命终，当生何处？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "This noble one has accumulated all good qualities, is fully endowed with the Dharma, does not go against the practice of the Dharma, has severed the five lower fetters, and will attain parinirvāṇa there without returning to this world."

佛告比丘：「此族姓子诸善普集，法法具足，不违法行，断五下结，于彼般涅槃，不来此世。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully received and practiced it.
