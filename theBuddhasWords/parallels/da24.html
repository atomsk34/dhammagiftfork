DĀ 24

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 3 第三分

24 （二四）坚固经

Parallel to: DN 11

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the Pāvārika Forest near the city of Nālandā, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛在那难陀城波婆利掩次林中，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, there was a householder's son named Sthiramati who came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. Then the householder's son Sthiramati said to the Buddha, "Excellent! World-Honored One! I only wish that you would now command the bhikṣus to display their supernatural powers and manifest the Dharma of the superior person when brahmins, householder's sons, and householders come."

尔时，有长者子，名曰坚固，来诣佛所，头面礼足，在一面坐。时，坚固长者子白佛言：「善哉！世尊！唯愿今者𠡠诸比丘：『若有婆罗门、长者子、居士来，当为现神足显上人法。』」

The Buddha told Sthiramati, "I never teach the bhikṣus to display their supernatural powers and manifest the Dharma of the superior person for brahmins, householders, and elders. I only teach my disciples to contemplate the Way in quietude in empty and secluded places. If they have merit, they should conceal it themselves. If they have faults, they should reveal them themselves."

佛告坚固：「我终不教诸比丘为婆罗门、长者、居士而现神足上人法也。我但教弟子于空闲处静默思道，若有功德，当自覆藏，若有过失，当自发露。」

At that time, the householder's son Sthiramati again said to the Buddha, "I only wish that the World-Honored One would command the bhikṣus to display their supernatural powers and manifest the Dharma of the superior person when brahmins, householders, and elders come."

时，坚固长者子复白佛言：「唯愿世尊𠡠诸比丘：『若有婆罗门、长者、居士来，当为现神足，显上人法。』」

The Buddha again told Sthiramati, "I never teach the bhikṣus to display their supernatural powers and manifest the Dharma of the superior person for brahmins, householders, and elders. I only teach my disciples to contemplate the Way in quietude in empty and secluded places. If they have merit, they should conceal it themselves. If they have faults, they should reveal them themselves."

佛复告坚固：「我终不教诸比丘为婆罗门、长者、居士而现神足上人法也。我但教弟子于空闲处静默思道，若有功德，当自覆藏，若有过失，当自发露。」

At that time, the householder's son Sthiramati said to the Buddha, "I have no doubts about the Dharma of the superior person. But this city of Nandi is prosperous and the people are flourishing. If supernatural powers are displayed here, it will benefit many. The Buddha and the great assembly will greatly spread the teaching of the Way."

时，坚固长者子白佛言：「我于上人法无有疑也。但此那难陀城国土丰乐，人民炽盛，若于中现神足者，多所饶益，佛及大众善弘道化。」

The Buddha again told Sthiramati, "I never teach bhikṣus to display their supernatural powers and manifest the Dharma of the superior person for brahmins, householders, and elders. I only teach my disciples to contemplate the Way in quietude in empty and secluded places. If they have merit, they should conceal it themselves. If they have faults, they should reveal them themselves. Why is this? There are three supernatural powers. What are the three? First, supernatural powers; second, observing others' minds; third, instruction. What are supernatural powers? Householder's son! Bhikṣus cultivate immeasurable supernatural powers, able to transform one body into countless, and countless bodies back into one. Whether far or near, mountains, rivers, rocks, and walls are freely unobstructed, like walking in the sky; sitting cross-legged in the sky, like a flying bird; entering and exiting the earth, like in water; walking on water, like on land; emitting smoke and fire from the body, like a great fire mass; touching the sun and moon with the hands, standing to reach the Brahma Heaven. If there are faithful householders who see this bhikṣu displaying immeasurable supernatural powers, standing to reach the Brahma Heaven, they will go to other faithful householders and say, 'I saw the bhikṣu displaying immeasurable supernatural powers, standing to reach the Brahma Heaven.' Those faithful householders will say to the faithful ones, 'I have heard of the Gula spell, which can display such immeasurable transformations, up to standing to reach the Brahma Heaven.'"

佛复告坚固：「我终不教比丘为婆罗门、长者子、居士而现神足上人法也。我但教弟子于空闲处静默思道，若有功德，当自覆藏，若有过失，当自发露。所以者何？有三神足。云何为三？一曰神足，二曰观察他心，三曰教诫。云何为神足？长者子！比丘习无量神足，能以一身变成无数，以无数身还合为一，若远若近，山河石壁，自在无碍，犹如行空；于虚空中结加趺坐，犹如飞鸟；出入大地，犹如在水；若行水上；犹如履地；身出烟火，如大火聚，手扪日月，立至梵天。若有得信长者、居士见此比丘现无量神足，立至梵天，当复诣余未得信长者、居士所，而告之言：『我见比丘现无量神足，立至梵天。』彼长者、居士未得信者，语得信者言：『我闻有瞿罗呪，能现如是无量神变，乃至立至梵天。』」

The Buddha again told the householder's son Sthiramati, "Those who do not believe will say such words. Is this not slanderous speech?"

佛复告长者子坚固：「彼不信者，有如此言，岂非毁谤言耶？」

Sthiramati said to the Buddha, "This is indeed slanderous speech."

坚固白佛言：「此实是毁谤言也。」

The Buddha said, "For this reason, I do not order the monks to display supernatural transformations. I only teach my disciples to contemplate the path in quietude and seclusion. If they have merits, they should conceal them. If they have faults, they should reveal them. In this way, householder, this is the supernatural power displayed by my monks.

佛言：「我以是故，不𠡠诸比丘现神变化，但教弟子于空闲处静默思道，若有功德，当自覆藏，若有过失，当自发露。如是，长者！此即是我诸比丘所现神足。

"What is meant by the supernatural power of observing others' minds? Here, a monk displays immeasurable supernatural powers of observation, observing the mental phenomena of sentient beings and being able to recognize their hidden activities. If there are faithful householders who see a monk displaying immeasurable supernatural powers of observation, observing the mental phenomena of others and being able to recognize their hidden activities, they will go to other unfaithful householders and say, 'I have seen a monk displaying immeasurable supernatural powers of observation, observing the mental phenomena of others and being able to recognize their hidden activities.' When those unfaithful householders hear this, they will slander, saying, 'There is a Gandhara spell that can observe others' minds and recognize their hidden activities.' What do you think, householder's son? Is this not slanderous speech?"

「云何名观察他心神足？于是，比丘现无量观察神足，观诸众生心所念法，隈屏所为皆能识知。若有得信长者、居士，见比丘现无量观察神足，观他众生心所念法，隈屏所为皆悉识知，便诣余未得信长者、居士所，而告之曰：『我见比丘现无量观察神足，观他众生心所念法，隈屏所为皆悉能知。』彼不信长者、居士，闻此语已，生毁谤言：『有干陀罗呪能观察他心，隈屏所为皆悉能知。』云何？长者子！此岂非毁谤言耶？」

The householder's son said, "This is indeed slanderous speech."

坚固白佛言：「此实是毁谤言也。」

The Buddha said, "For this reason, I do not instruct the monks to display supernatural transformations. I only teach my disciples to contemplate the Way in quietude in secluded places. If they have merits, they should conceal them. If they have faults, they should reveal them. In this way, householder's son, this is my monks' display of the supernatural power of observation.

佛言：「我以是故，不勅诸比丘现神变化，但教弟子于空闲处静默思道，若有功德，当自覆藏，若有过失，当自发露。如是，长者子！此即是我比丘现观察神足。

What is the supernatural power of instruction? Householder's son! When the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One appears in the world, endowed with the ten titles, and personally realizes and teaches the Dharma to gods, humans, Māra, Māra's followers, śramaṇas, and brahmins, with words of superior, middling, and inferior meaning, all truly genuine, with pure meaning and taste, and fully endowed with the holy life. If householders and elders hear it, they gain faith in it. Having gained faith, they contemplate and think, 'It is not suitable for me to live at home. If I live at home, I will be bound by chains and unable to practice the holy life. I would rather now shave my head and beard, wear the three Dharma robes, leave home to cultivate the path, attain all merits, and accomplish the three insights, extinguishing all darkness and giving rise to great wisdom. Why is this? It is because of diligently enjoying solitary seclusion, single-mindedly not forgetting, and what is attained from that.' Householder's son! This is my monks' display of the supernatural power of instruction.

「云何为教诫神足？长者子！若如来、至真、等正觉出现于世，十号具足，于诸天、世人、魔、若魔天、沙门、婆罗门中，自身作证，为他说法，上中下言，皆悉真正，义味清净，梵行具足。若长者、居士闻已，于中得信，得信已，于中观察自念：『我不宜在家，若在家者，钩锁相连，不得清净修于梵行。我今宁可剃除须发，服三法衣，出家修道，具诸功德，乃至成就三明，灭诸暗冥，生大智明。所以者何？斯由精勤，乐独闲居，专念不忘之所得也。』长者子！此是我比丘现教诫神足。」

At that time, the householder's son Solid-Virtue addressed the Buddha, saying, "Are there any monks who have accomplished these three supernatural powers?"

尔时，坚固长者子白佛言：「颇有比丘成就此三神足耶？」

The Buddha told the householder's son, "I do not say there are many monks who have accomplished these three supernatural powers. Householder's son! I have a monk in this assembly who contemplates by himself, 'This body is composed of the four great elements: earth, water, fire, and wind. How can it be eternally extinguished?' That monk suddenly went to the heavenly realm and approached the Four Heavenly Kings, asking them, 'This body is composed of the four great elements: earth, water, fire, and wind. By what means can it be eternally extinguished?'

佛告长者子：「我不说有数，多有比丘成此三神足者。长者子！我有比丘在此众中自思念：『此身四大，地、水、火、风，何由永灭？』彼比丘倐趣天道，往至四天王所，问四天王言：『此身四大，地、水、火、风，由何永灭？』

Householder's son! The Four Heavenly Kings replied to the monk, 'We do not know by what means the four great elements are eternally extinguished. Above us is a heaven called Trayastrimsa, which is the most subtle and has great wisdom. The gods there can know by what means the four great elements are extinguished.' Having heard this, the monk suddenly went to the heavenly realm and approached the Trayastrimsa Heaven, asking the gods there, 'This body is composed of the four great elements: earth, water, fire, and wind. By what means can it be eternally extinguished?' The gods of Trayastrimsa replied to the monk, 'We do not know by what means the four great elements are extinguished. Above us is another heaven called Yama, which is the most subtle and has great wisdom. The gods there can know.' He then went to ask them, but they also said they did not know.

「长者子！彼四天王报比丘言：『我不知四大由何永灭？我上有天，名曰忉利，微妙第一，有大智慧，彼天能知四大由何而灭。』彼比丘闻已，即倐趣天道，往诣忉利天上，问诸天言：『此身四大，地、水、火、风，何由永灭？』彼忉利天报比丘言：『我不知四大何由灭，上更有天，名焰摩，微妙第一，有大智慧，彼天能知。』即往就问，又言不知。

In this way, he went up to the Tuṣita Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, and the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, and they all said, 'We do not know by what means the four great elements are extinguished. Above us is another heaven, which is the most subtle and has great wisdom. It is called Brahmakāyika. The gods there can know by what means the four great elements are eternally extinguished.' The monk then suddenly went to the Brahma realm and approached the Brahma Heaven, asking, 'This body is composed of the four great elements: earth, water, fire, and wind. By what means can it be eternally extinguished?' The Brahma gods replied to the monk, 'We do not know by what means the four great elements are eternally extinguished. Now there is a Great Brahma King who is invincible, ruling over a thousand worlds, wealthy, noble, and most powerful. He can create all things and is the parent of all beings. He can know by what means the four great elements are eternally extinguished.' Householder's son! The monk then asked, 'Where is that Great Brahma King now?' The gods replied, 'We do not know where the Great Brahma is now. In my opinion, he will appear soon.' Not long after, the Brahma King suddenly appeared. Householder! The monk went to the Brahma King and asked, 'This body is composed of the four great elements: earth, water, fire, and wind. By what means can it be eternally extinguished?' The Great Brahma King told the monk, 'I am the Brahma King, invincible, ruling over a thousand worlds, wealthy, noble, and most powerful. I can create all things and am the parent of all beings.' At that time, the monk told the Brahma King, 'I am not asking about this. I am asking about the four great elements: earth, water, fire, and wind. By what means can they be eternally extinguished?'

「如是展转，至兜率天、化自在天、他化自在天，皆言：『我不知四大何由而灭？上更有天，微妙第一，有大智慧，名梵迦夷，彼天能知四大何由永灭。』彼比丘即倐趣梵道，诣梵天上问言：『此身四大，地、水、火、风，何由永灭？』彼梵天报比丘言：『我不知四大何由永灭，今有大梵天王，无能胜者，统千世界，富贵尊豪，最得自在，能造化物，是众生父母，彼能知四大由何永灭。』长者子！彼比丘寻问：『彼大梵王今为所在？』彼天报言：『不知大梵今为所在，以我意观，出现不久。』未久，梵王忽然出现。长者！彼比丘诣梵王所问言：『此身四大，地、水、火、风，何由永灭？』彼大梵王告比丘言：『我梵天王无能胜者，统千世界，富贵尊豪，最得自在，能造万物，众生父母。』时，彼比丘告梵王曰：『我不问此事，自问四大，地、水、火、风，何由永灭？』

Householder's son! The Brahma King still replied to the monk, 'I am the Great Brahma King, invincible, up to creating all things and being the parent of all beings.' The monk again said, 'I am not asking about this. I am asking about the four great elements. By what means can they be eternally extinguished?' Householder's son! The Brahma King did this up to three times, but could not tell the monk by what means the four great elements are eternally extinguished. Then the Great Brahma King took the monk's right hand and led him to a secluded place, saying, 'Monk! Now all the Brahma Kings consider me to be the foremost in wisdom, knowing and seeing everything. Therefore, I cannot tell you that I do not know or see by what means these four great elements are eternally extinguished.' He also said to the monk, 'You are very foolish! You have abandoned the Tathāgata and are asking about this among the gods. You should ask about this matter in the presence of the World-Honored One. Remember well what the Buddha says.' He also told the monk, 'Now the Buddha is at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in Śrāvastī. You should go and ask him.'

「长者子！彼梵王犹报比丘言：『我是大梵天王，无能胜者，乃至造作万物，众生父母。』比丘又复告言：『我不问此，我自问四大何由永灭？』长者子！彼梵天王如是至三，不能报彼比丘四大何由永灭。时，大梵王即执比丘右手，将诣屏处，语言：『比丘！今诸梵王皆谓我为智慧第一，无不知见，是故我不得报汝言：「不知不见此四大何由永灭。」』又语比丘：『汝为大愚！乃舍如来于诸天中推问此事。汝当于世尊所问如此事，如佛所说，善受持之。』又告比丘：『今佛在舍卫国给孤独园，汝可往问。』

Householder's son! At that time, the monk suddenly disappeared from the Brahma Heaven. It was like the time it takes a strong man to bend and stretch his arm. He arrived at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in Śrāvastī, came to me, bowed his head at my feet, sat to one side, and said to me, 'World-Honored One! Now these four great elements, earth, water, fire, and wind, by what means are they extinguished?' I then told him, 'Monk! It is like a merchant who releases a trained hawk into the sea. The hawk flies in the four directions, and if it finds land, it will stop there. If there is no land, it will return to the ship. Monk! You are also like this. You went up to the Brahma Heaven and asked about this meaning, but it was not accomplished. Now I will make you accomplish this meaning.' Then he spoke a verse:

「长者子！时，比丘于梵天上忽然不现。譬如壮士屈申臂顷，至舍卫国祇树给孤独园，来至我所，头面礼足，一面坐，白我言：『世尊！今此四大，地、水、火、风，何由而灭？』时，我告言：『比丘！犹如商人臂鹰入海，于海中放彼鹰飞空东西南北，若得陆地则便停止，若无陆地更还归船。比丘！汝亦如是，乃至梵天问如是义，竟不成就，还来归我。今当使汝成就此义。』即说偈言：

By what means are the four great elements, earth, water, fire, and wind, extinguished?
By what means are the coarse and fine, the long and short, the beautiful
and ugly, extinguished? By what means are name and form extinguished, without remainder? The
answer is that consciousness is formless, immeasurable, and self-luminous. When this is extinguished, the
four great elements are extinguished, the coarse and fine, the beautiful and ugly, are
extinguished, and in this, name and form are extinguished, and consciousness is extinguished, and
the rest are also extinguished.

「『何由无四大，　　地水火风灭？何由无麤细，　　及长短好丑？何由无名色，　　永灭无有余？应答识无形，　　无量自有光。此灭四大灭，　　麤细好丑灭，于此名色灭，　　识灭余亦灭。』」

At that time, the householder's son Kṣemaṅkara addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! What is the name of this monk? How should we remember it?"

时，坚固长者子白佛言：「世尊！此比丘名何等？云何持之？」

The Buddha told the householder's son: "This monk is named Ārṣabha. You should remember it thus."

佛告长者子：「此比丘名阿室已，当奉持之。」

At that time, the householder's son Kṣemaṅkara, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，坚固长者子闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
