DĀ 4

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 1 第一分

4 （四）阇尼沙经

Parallel to: DN 18

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Naṭabhaṭa residence, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，佛游那提揵稚住处，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was sitting in a quiet room, silently reflecting to himself: "How wonderful! How extraordinary! The Tathāgata's bestowal of predictions brings much benefit. When the great minister Gaggarā passed away, the Tathāgata predicted that he would sever the five lower fetters, attain parinirvāṇa in the heavens, and not return to this world. The second, Kālinga, the third, Vibhanga, the fourth, Gālava, the fifth, Śālavā, the sixth, Vāyali, the seventh, Vāṭali, the eighth, Sauvāṭali, the ninth, Tārīṣa, the tenth, Sauvārīṣa, the eleventh, Yaśodhara, the twelfth, Yaśodhara-vāṭali, and the other great ministers who passed away, the Buddha also predicted that they would sever the five lower fetters, attain parinirvāṇa in the heavens, and not be reborn in this world. There were also fifty others who passed away, and the Buddha also predicted that they would sever the three fetters, weaken lust, anger, and delusion, attain the state of once-returner, and after one more return to this world, completely end suffering. There were also five hundred who passed away, and the Buddha also predicted that they would sever the three fetters, attain the state of stream-enterer, not fall into the evil destinies, and after seven more rebirths at most, definitely end suffering. There were Buddha's disciples who passed away in various places, and the Buddha predicted where they were reborn. In the countries of Aṅga, Magadha, Kāśi, Kosala, Vṛji, Malla, Caityaka, Vaṅga, Kuru, Pañcāla, Aśmaka, Avanti, Vaṅga, Suvaṇṇabhūmi, Gandhāra, and Kamboja, those who passed away in these sixteen great countries, the Buddha predicted their rebirths. The people of Magadha were all of royal lineage and were appointed by the king. Those who passed away, the Buddha did not predict their rebirths."

尔时，尊者阿难在静室坐，默自思念：「甚奇！甚特！如来授人记别，多所饶益。彼伽伽罗大臣命终，如来记之，此人命终，断五下结，即于天上而取灭度，不来此世。第二迦陵伽，三毘伽陀，四伽利输，五遮楼，六婆耶楼，七婆头楼，八薮婆头，九他梨舍［少／兔］，十薮达梨舍［少／兔］，十一耶输，十二耶输多楼，诸大臣等命终，佛亦记之，断五下结，即于天上而取灭度，不来生此。复有余五十人命终，佛亦记之，断三结，婬、怒、痴薄，得斯陀含，一来此世便尽苦际。复有五百人命终，佛亦记之，三结尽，得须陀洹，不堕恶趣，极七往返必尽苦际。有佛弟子处处命终，佛皆记之，某生某处、某生某处。鸯伽国、摩竭国、迦尸国、居萨罗国、拔祇国、末罗国、支提国、拔沙国、居楼国、般阇罗国、颇漯波国、阿般提国、婆蹉国、苏罗婆国、干陀罗国、剑洴沙国，彼十六大国有命终者，佛悉记之，摩竭国人皆是王种王所亲任，有命终者，佛不记之。」

At that time, Ānanda rose from his quiet room and went to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha: "I was just sitting in a quiet room, reflecting to myself: 'How wonderful! How extraordinary! The Buddha's bestowal of predictions brings much benefit. Those who pass away in the sixteen great countries, the Buddha predicts their rebirths. Only the people of Magadha, who are appointed by the king, when they pass away, do not receive predictions. I only wish that the World-Honored One would predict for them! I only wish that the World-Honored One would predict for them! It would benefit all, bringing peace to gods and humans. Also, the Buddha attained enlightenment in the country of Magadha. When the people there pass away, he does not give them predictions. I only wish that the World-Honored One would predict for them! I only wish that the World-Honored One would predict for them! Also, King Bimbisāra of Magadha is a lay devotee who has deep faith in the Buddha and has made many offerings. After his passing, many people have come to believe and make offerings to the Three Jewels. Yet the Tathāgata does not give him a prediction. I only wish that the World-Honored One would give him a prediction! It would benefit beings, bringing peace to gods and humans.'" At that time, Ānanda, on behalf of the people of Magadha, requested the World-Honored One. He then rose from his seat, bowed to the Buddha, and left.

尔时，阿难于静室起，至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐，而白佛言：「我向于静室默自思念：『甚奇！甚特！佛授人记，多所饶益，十六大国有命终者，佛悉记之，唯摩竭国人，王所亲任，有命终者，独不蒙记。唯愿世尊当为记之！唯愿世尊当为记之！饶益一切，天人得安。又佛于摩竭国得道，其国人命终，独不与记。唯愿世尊当为记之！唯愿世尊当为记之！又摩竭国缾沙王为优婆塞，笃信于佛，多设供养，然后命终，由此王故，多人信解，供养三宝，而今如来不为授记。唯愿世尊当与记之！饶益众生，使天人得安。』」尔时，阿难为摩竭人劝请世尊，即从座起，礼佛而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One put on his robe, took his alms bowl, entered the city of Rājagṛha to beg for food, and then went to the great forest, sat under a tree, and contemplated the rebirth of the people of Magadha after their passing. Not far from the Buddha, a ghost self-proclaimed its name and said to the World-Honored One: "I am Jālinīprabha! I am Jālinīprabha!"

尔时，世尊著衣持钵，入那伽城乞食已，至大林处坐一树下，思惟摩竭国人命终生处。时，去佛不远，有一鬼神，自称己名，白世尊曰：「我是阇尼沙！我是阇尼沙！」

The Buddha said: "For what reason do you call yourself Jālinīprabha? By what means do you call yourself one who has seen the path of the stream-enterer?"

佛言：「汝因何事，自称己名为阇尼沙？。汝因何法，自以妙言称见道迹？」

Jālinīprabha said: "It is not elsewhere. I was a human king, and in the Tathāgata's Dharma, I was an upāsaka. I single-mindedly contemplated the Buddha and took my final breath. Therefore, I was reborn as the crown prince of Vaiśravaṇa. Ever since then, I have always illuminated all dharmas, attained the stream-enterer, not fallen into evil paths, and for seven lives, I have always been named Jālinīprabha."

阇尼沙言：「非余处也。我本为人王，于如来法中为优婆塞，一心念佛而取命终，故得生为毘沙门天王太子。自从是来，常照明诸法，得须陀洹，不堕恶道，于七生中常名阇尼沙。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having stayed in the great forest as he pleased, went to the place of the nāḍa-ghantā, took his seat, and told a bhikṣu: "You go and call Ānanda in my name."

时，世尊于大林处随宜住已，诣那陀揵稚处，就座而坐，告一比丘：「汝持我声，唤阿难来。」

He replied, "Yes!" and then, following the Buddha's instruction, went to call Ānanda.

对曰：「唯然！」即承佛教，往唤阿难。

Ānanda soon came to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "Now, looking at the Tathāgata's complexion, it is more radiant than usual, and the faculties are tranquil and focused. What kind of contemplation are you dwelling in that your countenance is so?"

阿难寻来，至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面住，而白佛言：「今观如来颜色胜常，诸根寂定。住何思惟，容色乃尔？」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "Just now, because of the people of Magadha coming to me and requesting a prediction before leaving, I then put on my robe, took my alms bowl, entered the city of Rājagṛha to beg for food, and after finishing begging for food, went to the great forest and sat under a tree, contemplating where the people of Magadha would be reborn after death. At that time, not far from me, a ghost self-proclaimed its own name and said to me, 'I am Jālinīprabha! I am Jālinīprabha!' Ānanda, have you ever heard the name Jālinīprabha before?"

尔时，世尊告阿难曰：「汝向因摩竭国人来至我所，请记而去，我寻于后，著衣持钵，入那罗城乞食，乞食讫已，诣彼大林，坐一树下，思惟摩竭国人命终生处。时，去我不远，有一鬼神，自称己名，而白我言：『我是阇尼沙！我是阇尼沙！』阿难！汝曾闻彼阇尼沙名不？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "I have never heard of it before. Now hearing its name, I am even afraid and my hair stands on end. World-Honored One! This ghost must have great power and virtue, so it is called Jālinīprabha."

阿难白佛言：「未曾闻也。今闻其名，乃至生怖畏，衣毛为竖。世尊！此鬼神必有大威德，故名阇尼沙尔。」

The Buddha said, "I asked him before, 'Because of what Dharma do you call yourself a seer of the path?' Jālinīprabha said, 'I do not rely on anything else, nor on any other Dharma. I was once a king of men, a disciple of the World-Honored One, a lay devotee with deep faith, who single-mindedly thought of the Buddha before passing away. I became a son of Vaiśravaṇa, attained the stream-enterer stage, did not fall into evil destinies, and after seven rebirths, ended suffering. Among the seven names, I was always called Jālinīprabha. Once, the World-Honored One was sitting under a tree in the great forest. At that time, I was riding a heavenly thousand-spoked jeweled chariot for some minor reason, intending to go to Vaiśravaṇa. From afar, I saw the World-Honored One sitting under a tree, with a dignified appearance and tranquil faculties, like a deep pool of clear and still water. Seeing this, I thought, 'I should now go and ask the World-Honored One where the people of Magadha will be reborn after death.' Also, once, Vaiśravaṇa spoke a verse in the assembly:

佛言：「我先问彼：『汝因何法，自以妙言称见道迹？』阇尼沙言：『我不于余处，不在余法。我昔为人王，为世尊弟子，以笃信心为优婆塞，一心念佛，然后命终，为毘沙门天王作子，得须陀洹，不堕恶趣，极七往返，乃尽苦际，于七生名中，常名阇尼沙。一时，世尊在大林中一树下坐，我时乘天千辐宝车，以少因缘，欲诣毘楼勒天王，遥见世尊在一树下，颜貌端正，诸根寂定，譬如深渊澄静清明，见已念言，我今宁可往问世尊，摩竭国人有命终者，当生何所？又复一时，毘沙门王自于众中，而说偈言：

We do not remember the past events by ourselves
but now encountering the World-Honored One, our lifespans have increased.

「『我等不自忆，　　过去所更事；今遭遇世尊，　　寿命得增益。』

Also, once, the gods of Trāyastriṃśa gathered in one place for some minor reason. At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings each sat in their positions, with Dhṛtarāṣṭra sitting in the east facing west, in front of Śakra; Virūḍhaka sitting in the south facing north, in front of Śakra; Virūpākṣa sitting in the west facing east, in front of Śakra; Vaiśravaṇa sitting in the north facing south, in front of Śakra. At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings all sat first, and then I sat. There were also other great gods who had previously practiced holy life under the Buddha, and after passing away here, were reborn in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, increasing the number of gods and reducing the number of asuras. The gods of Trāyastriṃśa were all overjoyed and said, 'The number of gods has increased, and the number of asuras has decreased.' At that time, Śakra, knowing that the gods of Trāyastriṃśa had joyful minds, spoke a verse:

「又复一时，忉利诸天以少因缘，集在一处。时，四天王各当位坐，提头赖咤在东方坐，其面西向，帝释在前；毘楼勒叉天在南方坐，其面北向，帝释在前；毘楼博叉天王在西方坐，其面东向，帝释在前；毘沙门天王在北方坐，其面南向，帝释在前。时，四天王皆先坐已，然后我坐。复有余诸大神天，皆先于佛所，净修梵行，于此命终，生忉利天，增益诸天，受天五福：一者天寿，二者天色，三者天名称，四者天乐，五者天威德。时，诸忉利天皆踊跃欢喜言：『增益诸天众，减损阿须伦众。』尔时，释提桓因知忉利诸天有欢喜心，即作颂曰：

The gods of Trāyastriṃśa enjoy themselves with Śakra, paying homage
to the Tathāgata, the supreme Dharma of the Dharma. The
gods enjoy the blessings of longevity, appearance, fame, happiness, and
majesty. Having practiced holy life under the Buddha, they are
reborn here. There are also other gods, their light and
color very majestic, the disciples of the Buddha's wisdom, born
here even more distinguished. Trāyastriṃśa and Yāma, contemplating this self-enjoyment
pay homage to the Tathāgata, the supreme Dharma of the Dharma.

「『忉利诸天人，　　帝释相娱乐；礼敬于如来，　　最上法之法。诸天受影福，　　寿、色、名、乐威；于佛修梵行，　　故来生此间。复有诸天人，　　光色甚巍巍；佛智慧弟子，　　生此复殊胜。忉利及因提，　　思惟此自乐；礼敬于如来，　　最上法之法。』

The god Śakra said, 'The gods of Trāyastriṃśa gather in the Dharma hall to discuss, contemplate, observe, and measure, giving instructions. Then they order the Four Heavenly Kings, who, having received the order, each sit in their positions. Not long after sitting, there is a great strange light illuminating the four directions. At that time, the gods of Trāyastriṃśa, seeing this strange light, are all greatly startled, wondering what strange thing this light will bring. The other great gods with majesty are also startled, wondering what strange thing this light will bring. Then the great Brahmā king transforms himself into a youth with a five-peaked topknot, standing in the sky above the assembly of gods, with an upright appearance surpassing the others, his body purple-golden in color, outshining the light of the gods. At that time, the gods of Trāyastriṃśa do not rise to welcome him, do not pay respect, and do not invite him to sit. Then the Brahmā youth goes to the seat he is destined for, and the seat gives rise to joy, like when a kṣatriya water-sprinkled head-anointed king ascends the throne, leaping with joy. Not long after sitting, he again transforms his body, taking on the appearance of a youth with a five-peaked topknot, sitting in the sky above the great assembly. It is like a strong man sitting on a comfortable seat, upright and unmoving, speaking a verse:

「阇尼沙神复言：『所以忉利诸天集法堂者，共议思惟，观察称量，有所教令，然后勅四天王，四王受教已，各当位而坐。其坐未久，有大异光照于四方，时，忉利天见此异光，皆大惊愕，今此异光将有何怪？余大神天有威德者，皆亦惊恠，今此异光将有何怪？时，大梵王即化作童子，头五角髻，在天众上虚空中立，颜貌端正，与众超绝，身紫金色，蔽诸天光。时，忉利天亦不起迎，亦不恭敬，又不请坐。时，梵童子随所诣座，座生欣悦，譬如刹利水浇头种，登王位时，踊跃欢喜。其坐未久，复自变身，作童子像，头五角髻，在大众上虚空中坐。譬如力士坐于安座，嶷然不动，而作颂曰：

The Tamer, the Unsurpassed Honored One, teaches the world to be born in the
place of illumination. The Great Illuminator expounds the illuminating Dharma, with pure conduct and
no equal, causing pure beings to be born in the pure and wondrous heavens.

「『调伏无上尊，　　教世生明处；大明演明法，　　梵行无等侣，使清净众生，　　生于净妙天。』

At that time, the Brahmā youth, having spoken this verse, said to the gods of Trāyastriṃśa, 'There are five kinds of pure sounds that are called Brahmā sounds. What are the five? First, the sound is upright; second, the sound is harmonious; third, the sound is clear and penetrating; fourth, the sound is deep and full; fifth, it is heard far and wide. Possessing these five is called a Brahmā sound. I will now explain further, listen well! The disciples of the Tathāgata, the lay followers of Magadha, some attain non-return, some attain once-return, some attain stream-entry, some are born in the Heaven of the Devas Who Delight in Creating, some are born in the Heaven of the Devas Who Delight in Others' Creations, the Heaven of the Tuṣita Devas, the Heaven of the Yāma Devas, the Heaven of the Trāyastriṃśa Devas, the Four Heavenly Kings, some are born as kṣatriyas, brāhmaṇas, householders, and great families, with the five desires naturally provided.' At that time, the Brahmā youth spoke in verse:

「时，梵童子说此偈已，告忉利天曰：『其有音声，五种清净，乃名梵声。何等五？一者其音正直，二者其音和雅，三者其音清彻，四者其音深满，五者周遍远闻，具此五者，乃名梵音。我今更说，汝等善听！如来弟子摩竭优婆塞，命终有得阿那含，有得斯陀含，有得须陀洹者，有生他化自在天者，有生化自在、兜率天、焰天、忉利天、四天王者，有生刹利、婆罗门、居士大家，五欲自然者。』时，梵童子以偈颂曰：

The lay followers of Magadha, all those who have passed away
eighty-four thousand in number, I have heard, have attained the path
Accomplishing the stream-enterer, they will not fall into evil destinies, all
riding the even and correct path, attaining the path, able to
save and deliver. These groups of beings, supported by merit and
virtue, with wisdom, abandoning affection and love, with a sense of
shame and conscience, free from deceit and falsehood. Among those heavenly
beings, the Brahmā youth prophesied thus; saying they have attained the
stream-enterer, all the gods rejoice.

「『摩竭优婆塞，　　诸有命终者，八万四千人，　　吾闻俱得道。成就须陀洹，　　不复堕恶趣，俱乘平正路，　　得道能救济。此等群生类，　　功德所扶持，智慧舍恩爱，　　惭愧离欺妄。于彼诸天众，　　梵童记如是；言得须陀洹，　　诸天皆欢喜。』

"At that time, King Vaiśravaṇa, having heard this verse, joyfully said, 'The Tathāgata has appeared in the world, speaking the true Dharma. It is wonderful! It is extraordinary! It is unprecedented! I did not know that the Tathāgata had appeared in the world, speaking such a Dharma. In the future, there will be Buddhas who will speak such a Dharma, able to cause the gods of Trāyastriṃśa to give rise to a joyful mind.'"

「时，毗沙门王闻此偈已，欢喜而言：『世尊出世说真实法，甚奇！甚特！未曾有也。我本不知如来出世，说如是法。于未来世，当复有佛说如是法，能使忉利诸天发欢喜心。』

"At that time, the Brahmā youth said to King Vaiśravaṇa, 'Why do you say this? The Tathāgata has appeared in the world, speaking such a Dharma. It is wonderful! It is extraordinary! It is unprecedented! The Tathāgata, with skillful means, speaks of good and bad, fully speaking the Dharma without attaining anything, speaking of the pure Dharma of emptiness and attaining something. This Dharma is subtle, like the best of milk.'"

「时，梵童子告毗沙门王曰：『汝何故作此言？如来出世说如是法，为甚奇！甚特！未曾有也。如来以方便力说善不善，具足说法而无所得，说空净法而有所得，此法微妙，犹如醍醐。』

"At that time, the Brahmā youth again said to the gods of Trāyastriṃśa, 'You should listen attentively and contemplate well, I will further explain for you. The Tathāgata, the Arhat, is well able to distinguish and explain the four foundations of mindfulness. What are the four? First, observing one's own body internally, diligently and tirelessly, with single-minded mindfulness, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow. Observing the external body, diligently and tirelessly, with single-minded mindfulness, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow. Observing feelings, mind, and dharmas, also like this, diligently and tirelessly, with single-minded mindfulness, eliminating worldly greed and sorrow. Having observed one's own body internally, one generates knowledge of others' bodies; having observed one's own feelings internally, one generates knowledge of others' feelings; having observed one's own mind internally, one generates knowledge of others' minds; having observed one's own dharmas internally, one generates knowledge of others' dharmas. This is the Tathāgata's well-distinguishing and explaining the four foundations of mindfulness. Furthermore, gods! You should listen well! I will further explain, the Tathāgata is well able to distinguish and explain the seven factors of enlightenment. What are the seven? Right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, and right mindfulness. This is the Tathāgata's well-distinguishing and explaining the seven factors of enlightenment. Furthermore, gods! The Tathāgata is well able to distinguish and explain the four bases of supernormal power. What are the four? First, the base of supernormal power of concentration through desire and the accomplishment of its practice. Second, the base of supernormal power of concentration through effort and the accomplishment of its practice. Third, the base of supernormal power of concentration through mind and the accomplishment of its practice. Fourth, the base of supernormal power of concentration through investigation and the accomplishment of its practice. This is the Tathāgata's well-distinguishing and explaining the four bases of supernormal power.'"

「时，梵童子又告忉利天曰：『汝等谛听！善思念之，当更为汝说。如来、至真善能分别说四念处。何谓为四？一者内身观，精勤不懈，专念不忘，除世贪忧。外身观，精勤不懈，专念不忘，除世贪忧。受意法观，亦复如是，精勤不懈，专念不忘，除世贪忧。内身观已，生他身智；内观受已，生他受智；内观意已，生他意智；内观法已，生他法智，是为如来善能分别说四念处。复次，诸天！汝等善听！吾当更说，如来善能分别说七定具。何等为七？正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念，是为如来善能分别说七定具。复次，诸天！如来善能分别说四神足，何等谓四？一者欲定灭行成就修习神足。二者精进定灭行成就修习神足。三者意定灭行成就修习神足。四者思惟定灭行成就修习神足，是为如来善能分别说四神足。』

"He further told the gods, 'Past śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, with countless skillful means, manifested immeasurable supernormal powers, all arising from the four bases of supernormal power; future śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, with countless skillful means, manifesting immeasurable supernormal powers, will also arise from these four bases of supernormal power; as for the present śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, with countless skillful means, manifesting immeasurable supernormal powers, they also arise from these four bases of supernormal power.' At that time, the Brahmā youth transformed himself into thirty-three forms, sitting together with the thirty-three gods, and said to them, 'Do you now see my supernatural powers?' They replied, 'Yes, we have seen them.' The Brahmā youth said, 'I am also able to transform in countless ways because I cultivate the four bases of supernormal power.'"

「又告诸天：『过去诸沙门、婆罗门以无数方便，现无量神足，皆由四神足起；正使当来沙门、婆罗门无数方便，现无量神足，亦皆由是四神足起；如今现在沙门、婆罗门无数方便，现无量神足者，亦皆由是四神足起。』时，梵童子即自变化形为三十三身，与三十三天一一同坐，而告之曰：『汝今见我神变力不？』答曰：『唯然已见。』梵童子曰：『我亦修四神足故，能如是无数变化。』

"At that time, the thirty-three gods each thought, 'Now the Brahmā youth alone sits in my seat and speaks these words, and when one of his transformed bodies speaks, the other transformed bodies also speak; when one transformed body is silent, the other transformed bodies are also silent.' At that time, the Brahmā youth withdrew his supernatural power, sat in the seat of Śakra, and said to the gods of Trāyastriṃśa, 'I will now explain, you should listen well! The Tathāgata, the Arhat, himself by his own power opens three paths and attains perfect enlightenment. What are the three? There are sentient beings who are close to greed and desire, practicing unwholesome actions. Later, they associate with good friends, hear the Dharma, and accomplish the Dharma. Thus, they abandon desire and unwholesome practices, attain a joyful mind, and are peacefully happy. And within that happiness, they further generate great joy; it is like a person abandoning coarse food and eating a hundred-flavored meal, being satisfied and seeking something better. The practitioner is like this, abandoning unwholesome dharmas, attaining joyful happiness, and further generating great joy within that happiness. This is the Tathāgata opening the first path by his own power and attaining perfect enlightenment. There are also sentient beings who are mostly angry and do not abandon unwholesome bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Later, they encounter good friends, hear the Dharma, and accomplish the Dharma. They abandon unwholesome bodily, verbal, and mental actions, generate a joyful mind, and are peacefully happy. And within that happiness, they further generate great joy; it is like a person abandoning coarse food and eating a hundred-flavored meal, being satisfied and seeking something better. The practitioner is like this, abandoning unwholesome dharmas, attaining joyful happiness, and further generating great joy within that happiness. This is the Tathāgata opening the second path. There are also sentient beings who are ignorant and unwise, not recognizing good and evil, and unable to truly know suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Later, they encounter good friends, hear the Dharma, and accomplish the Dharma. They recognize good and evil, and truly know suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. They abandon unwholesome practices, generate a joyful mind, and are peacefully happy. And within that happiness, they further generate great joy; it is like a person abandoning coarse food and eating a hundred-flavored meal, being satisfied and seeking something better. The practitioner is like this, abandoning unwholesome dharmas, attaining joyful happiness, and further generating great joy within that happiness. This is the Tathāgata opening the third path.'"

「时，三十三天各作是念：『今梵童子独于我坐而说是语，而彼梵童一化身语，余化亦语；一化身默，余化亦默。』时，彼梵童还摄神足，处帝释坐，告忉利天曰：『我今当说，汝等善听！如来、至真自以己力开三径路，自致正觉。何谓为三？或有众生亲近贪欲，习不善行，彼人于后近善知识，得闻法言，法法成就，于是离欲舍不善行，得欢喜心，恬然快乐，又于乐中，复生大喜；如人舍于麤食，食百味饭，食已充足，复求胜者。行者如是，离不善法，得欢喜乐，又于乐中，复生大喜，是为如来自以己力开初径路，成最正觉。又有众生多于瞋恚，不舍身、口、意恶业，其人于后遇善知识，得闻法言，法法成就，离身恶行、口、意恶行，生欢喜心，恬然快乐，又于乐中，复生大喜；如人舍于麤食，食百味饭，食已充足，复求胜者。行者如是，离不善法，得欢喜乐，又于乐中，复生大喜，是为如来开第二径路。又有众生愚冥无智，不识善恶，不能如实知苦、习、尽、道，其人于后遇善知识，得闻法言，法法成就，识善不善，能如实知苦、习、尽、道，舍不善行，生欢喜心，恬然快乐，又于乐中，复生大喜；如人舍于麤食，食百味饭，食已充足，复求胜者。行者如是，离不善法，得欢喜乐，又于乐中，复生大喜，是为如来开第三径路。』

"At that time, the Brahmā youth expounded this true Dharma in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. The Heavenly King Vaiśravaṇa also expounded this true Dharma for his retinue. The god Jālinīprabha also expounded this true Dharma before the Buddha. The World-Honored One also expounded this true Dharma for Ānanda. Ānanda also expounded this true Dharma for bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās."

「时，梵童子于忉利天上说此正法，毗沙门天王复为眷属说此正法，阇尼沙神复于佛前说是正法，世尊复为阿难说此正法，阿难复为比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷说是正法。」

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and upheld it.

是时，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
