DĀ 7

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

7 （七）弊宿经

Parallel to: DN 23 / MĀ 71

At that time, the nun Kāśyapa was traveling in the country of Kosala with five hundred bhikṣus, gradually approaching the brāhman village of Sāvatthi. At that time, the nun Kāśyapa stayed in the Śiśiravana forest north of the village of Sāvatthi. There was a brāhman named Pūrvāṣāḍha who lived in the village of Sāvatthi. This village was prosperous and had many people, with abundant trees. King Prasenajit specially granted this village to the brāhman Pūrvāṣāḍha as a share of the merit of the brāhman. The brāhman Pūrvāṣāḍha always held heterodox views and told people, "There is no other world, no rebirth, and no retribution for good and evil."

尔时，童女迦叶与五百比丘游行拘萨罗国，渐诣斯波醯婆罗门村，时，童女迦叶在斯波醯村北尸舍婆林止。时，有婆罗门名曰弊宿，止斯波醯村。此村丰乐，民人众多，树木繁茂，波斯匿王别封此村与婆罗门弊宿，以为梵分。弊宿婆罗门常怀异见，为人说言：「无有他世，亦无更生，无善恶报。」

At that time, the people of the village of Sāvatthi heard that the nun Kāśyapa and five hundred bhikṣus were gradually approaching the Śiśiravana forest from the country of Kosala, and they said to each other, "This nun Kāśyapa has a great reputation. She has already attained arahantship. She is an elder, learned, erudite, intelligent, wise, eloquent, and skilled in debate. It would be good to see her now!" Then, the people of the village went to see Kāśyapa in turn every day. At that time, Pūrvāṣāḍha was on a high tower. Seeing the people of the village following each other in groups, he did not know where they were going. He asked the umbrella-bearer to his left, "Why are those people following each other in groups?"

时，斯波醯村人闻童女迦叶与五百比丘，从拘萨罗国渐至此尸舍婆林，自相谓言：「此童女迦叶有大名闻，已得罗汉，耆旧长宿，多闻广博，聪明叡智，辩才应机，善于谈论，今得见者，不亦善哉！」时，彼村人日日次第往诣迦叶。尔时，弊宿在高楼上，见其村人队队相随，不知所趣，即问左右持盖者言：「彼人何故群队相随？」

The attendant replied, "I heard that the nun Kāśyapa is traveling in the country of Kosala with five hundred bhikṣus, and has arrived at the Śiśiravana forest. I also heard that she has a great reputation. She has already attained arahantship. She is an elder, learned, erudite, intelligent, wise, eloquent, and skilled in debate. Those people are following each other in groups, wanting to go to see Kāśyapa."

侍者答曰：「我闻童女迦叶将五百比丘游拘萨罗国，至尸舍婆林，又闻其人有大名称，已得罗汉，耆旧长宿，多闻广博，聪明叡智，辩才应机，善于谈论；彼诸人等，群队相随，欲诣迦叶共相见耳。」

At that time, the brāhman Pūrvāṣāḍha immediately ordered his attendant, "Go quickly and tell the people, 'Wait! Let us go together to see them.' Why is that? Those people are foolish and deluded, deceiving the world. They say there is an afterlife, that there is rebirth, and that there are rewards for good and evil, but in reality there is no afterlife, no rebirth, and no rewards for good and evil."

时，弊宿婆罗门即勅侍者：「汝速往语诸人：『且住！当共俱行，往与相见。』所以者何？彼人愚惑，欺诳世间，说有他世，言有更生，言有善恶报，而实无他世，亦无更生，无善恶报。」

At that time, the messenger, having received the order, went to tell the people of the village of Śāṣṭha, "The brāhman says, 'You should wait! Let us go together to see them.'"

时，使者受教已，即往语彼斯婆醯村人言：「婆罗门语：『汝等且住！当共俱诣，往与相见。』」

The villagers replied, "Excellent! Excellent! If you can come, let us go together."

村人答曰「善哉！善哉！若能来者，当共俱行。」

The messenger returned and reported, "Those people are already waiting. Let us go."

使还寻白：「彼人已住，可行者行。」

At that time, the brahmin immediately descended from the high tower, ordered his attendants to prepare the carriage, and surrounded by the villagers, went to the śiṃśapā forest. Upon arrival, he alighted from the carriage and walked to where Kāśyapa was. After exchanging greetings, he sat to one side. Some of the villagers, brahmins, and householders paid homage to Kāśyapa before sitting down, some greeted him and then sat down, some introduced themselves and then sat down, some joined their palms and then sat down, and some sat down in silence. At that time, the brahmin Vāseṭṭha said to the youth Kāśyapa, "Now I wish to ask something. Is there leisure and permission to listen?"

时，婆罗门即下高楼，勅侍者严驾，与彼村人前后围遶，诣舍婆林，到已下车，步进诣迦叶所，问讯讫，一面坐；其彼村人婆罗门、居士，有礼拜迦叶然后坐者，有问讯已而坐者，有自称名已而坐者，有叉手已而坐者，有默而坐者。时，弊宿婆罗门语童女迦叶言：「今我欲有所问，宁有闲暇见听许不？」

Kāśyapa replied, "Ask as you wish. Upon hearing, you will understand."

迦叶报曰：「随汝所问，闻已当知。」

The brahmin said, "My theory is that there is no other world, no rebirth, no retribution for good or bad deeds. What is your theory?"

婆罗门言：「今我论者，无有他世，亦无更生，无罪福报，汝论云何？」

Kāśyapa replied, "I will ask you. Answer as you wish. Are the sun and moon of this world, or of another world? Are they human or divine?"

迦叶答曰：「我今问汝，随汝意答，今上日月，为此世耶？为他世耶？为人、为天耶？」

The brahmin replied, "The sun and moon are of another world, not this world. They are divine, not human."

婆罗门答曰：「日月是他世，非此世也。是天，非人。」

Kāśyapa replied, "From this we can know that there must be another world, rebirth, and retribution for good and evil."

迦叶答曰：「以此可知，必有他世，亦有更生，有善恶报。」

The brahmin said, "Although you say there is another world, rebirth, and retribution for good and evil, according to my opinion, all of these do not exist."

婆罗门言：「汝虽云有他世，有更生及善恶报，如我意者，皆悉无有。」

Kāśyapa asked, "Are there any causes and conditions by which one can know that there is no other world, no rebirth, and no retribution for good and evil?"

迦叶问曰：「颇有因缘，可知无有他世，无有更生，无善恶报耶？」

The brahmin replied, "There are conditions!"

婆罗门答曰：「有缘！」

Kāśyapa asked, "What are the causes and conditions for saying there is no other world?"

迦叶问曰：「以何因缘，言无他世？」

The brahmin said, "Kāśyapa! I have relatives and acquaintances who fell ill. I went to ask them, 'The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas each have different views, saying that those who kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, speak divisively, harshly, falsely, or frivolously, are greedy, jealous, or have wrong views, will all enter hell when their bodies perish and lives end. I initially did not believe this. The reason is that I have never seen anyone who has died return to speak of where they fell. If someone could come and speak of where they fell, I would definitely believe it. You are now my relative. You have also committed the ten evils. If it is as the śramaṇas say, then when you die, you will definitely enter the great hell. Now I believe you. I will take your word for it. If there really is a hell, you should come back and tell me so I can know. Then I will believe.' Kāśyapa! Since they died, they have not come back. They were my relatives. They should not deceive me. They promised but did not come. There must be no afterlife."

婆罗门言：「迦叶！我有亲族知识，遇患困病，我往问言：『诸沙门、婆罗门各怀异见，言诸有杀生、盗窃、邪婬、两舌、恶口、妄言、绮语、贪取、嫉妬、邪见者，身坏命终，皆入地狱。我初不信，所以然者？初未曾见死已来还，说所堕处。若有人来说所堕处，我必信受。汝今是我所亲，十恶亦备，若如沙门语者，汝死必入大地狱中，今我相信，从汝取定，若审有地狱者，汝当还来，语我使知，然后当信。』迦叶！彼命终已，至今不来，彼是我亲，不应欺我，许而不来，必无后世。」

Kāśyapa replied, "Those who are wise understand through analogies. Now I will use an analogy to explain it to you. It is like a thief who is always deceitful and violates the king's laws. When caught, he is brought before the king and says, 'This person is a thief. Please punish him.' The king then orders his attendants to arrest and bind the person, parade him through the streets, and then take him out of the city to be handed over to the executioner. At that time, the attendants take the thief and hand him over to the executioner. The thief then speaks softly to the guards, saying, 'Please release me so I can see my relatives and say goodbye. Then I will return.' What do you think, brahmin? Would the guards release him?"

迦叶报曰：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，今当为汝引喻解之。譬如盗贼，常怀姧诈，犯王禁法，伺察所得，将诣王所，白言：『此人为贼，愿王治之。』王即勅左右，收系其人，遍令街巷，然后载之，出城付刑人者；时，左右人即将彼贼，付刑人者，彼贼以柔輭言，语守卫者：『汝可放我，见诸亲里，言语辞别，然后当还。』云何？婆罗门！彼守卫者宁肯放不？」

The brahmin replied, "No!"

婆罗门答曰：「不可！」

Kāśyapa said again, "They are of the same human race, both alive in the present world, and still they would not be released. How much less so for your relatives who are fully endowed with the ten unwholesome deeds. When their bodies die and their lives end, they will definitely enter hell. The hellish beings have no compassion and are not of the same kind. They are dead and reborn in different worlds. If they were to speak softly to the hellish beings, saying, 'Please release me for a while so I can return to the world, see my relatives, say goodbye, and then return,' would they be released?"

迦叶又言：「彼同人类，俱存现世，而犹不放，况汝所亲，十恶备足，身死命终，必入地狱，狱鬼无慈，又非其类，死生异世，彼若以輭言求于狱鬼：『汝暂放我，还到世间，见亲族言语辞别，然后当还。』宁得放不？」

The brahmin replied, "No!"

婆罗门答曰：「不可！」

Kāśyapa said again, "Using this analogy, it is sufficient to know. Why persist in delusion and give rise to wrong views?"

迦叶又言：「以此相方，自足可知，何为守迷，自生邪见耶？」

The brahmin said, "Although you have given an analogy, saying there is an afterlife, I still say there is not."

婆罗门言：「汝虽引喻，谓有他世，我犹言无。」

Kāśyapa said again, "Do you have any other reasons to know there is no afterlife?"

迦叶复言：「汝颇更有余缘，可知无他世耶？」

The brahmin replied, "I have other reasons to know there is no afterlife."

婆罗门报言：「我更有余缘，知无他世。」

Kāśyapa asked, "What are your reasons?"

迦叶问曰：「以何缘知？」

The brahmin replied, "Kāśyapa! I have relatives who fell seriously ill. I went to them and said, 'The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas each have different views, saying there is an afterlife. They say that those who do not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, slander, speak harshly, lie, engage in frivolous speech, are greedy, jealous, or have wrong views, will be reborn in heaven when their bodies perish and their lives end. I do not believe this at all. The reason is that I have never seen anyone who has died and returned to speak of where they fell. If someone could come and speak of where they were reborn, I would definitely believe it. Now you are my relative and have also practiced the ten wholesome deeds. If it is as the śramaṇas say, then when you die, you will definitely be reborn in heaven. I now believe you. I will take your word for it. If there is indeed a heavenly reward, you must definitely come and tell me so that I may know. Then I will believe.' Kāśyapa! They have died and still have not come. They were my relatives and should not deceive me. They promised but did not come. There is definitely no afterlife."

答曰：「迦叶！我有亲族，遇患笃重，我往语言：『诸沙门、婆罗门各怀异见，说有他世，言不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺，不两舌、恶口、妄言、绮语、贪取、嫉妬、邪见者，身坏命终，皆生天上；我初不信，所以然者？初未曾见死已来还，说所堕处。若有人来说所堕生，我必信耳；今汝是我所亲，十善亦备，若如沙门语者，汝今命终，必生天上，今我相信，从汝取定，若审有天报者，汝当必来语我使知，然后当信。』迦叶！彼命终已，至今不来，彼是我亲，不应欺我，许而不来，必无他世。」

Kāśyapa said again, "The wise understand through parables. I will now tell you another parable. Suppose someone fell into a deep cesspit, with their head and body submerged. The king ordered his attendants to pull the person out, to scrape their body three times with a bamboo scraper, to wash them with soap and ash, and then to bathe their body with fragrant hot water. Fine powdered incense was sprinkled on their body. He ordered a barber to clean their beard and hair. He also ordered his attendants to wash them again, like this three times, bathing them with fragrant hot water and sprinkling them with powdered incense. He dressed them in fine clothes and adorned their body. He gave them a hundred kinds of delicious food to satisfy their mouth. He led them to a high hall for entertainment with the five desires. Would that person be able to return to the cesspit?"

迦叶又言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今当复为汝说喻。譬如有人，堕于深厕，身首没溺，王勅左右：『挽此人出，以竹为篦，三刮其身，澡豆净灰，次如洗之，后以香汤，沐浴其体，细末众香，坌其身上，勅除发师，净其须发。』又勅左右，重将洗沐，如是至三，洗以香汤，坌以香末，名衣上服，庄严其身，百味甘饍，以恣其口，将诣高堂，五欲娱乐，其人复能还入厕不？」

He replied, "No! That place is foul-smelling, how could one return to it?"

答曰：「不能！彼处臭恶，何可还入？」

Kāśyapa said, "The gods are also like this. This Jambudvīpa land is foul and impure. The gods above, a hundred yojanas away, can smell the human stench, worse than a cesspit. Brahmin! Your relatives and acquaintances who have perfected the ten wholesome deeds will certainly be reborn in the heavens, enjoying the five desires and boundless happiness. Would they then be willing to return and enter this Jambudvīpa cesspit?"

迦叶言：「诸天亦尔。此阎浮利地，臭秽不净，诸天在上，去此百由旬，遥闻人臭，甚于厕溷。婆罗门！汝亲族知识，十善具足，然必生天，五欲自娱，快乐无极，宁当复肯还来，入此阎浮厕不？」

He replied, "No!"

答曰：「不也！」

Kāśyapa said again, "Using this analogy, it is sufficient to know. Why persist in delusion and give rise to wrong views?"

迦叶又言：「以此相方，自足可知，何为守迷，自生邪见？」

The brāhman said, "Although you have given an analogy, saying there is an afterlife, I still say there is not."

婆罗门言：「汝虽引喻，言有他世，我犹言无。」

Kāśyapa said again, "Do you have any other reasons to know there is no afterlife?"

迦叶复言：「汝颇更有余缘，可知无他世耶？」

The brāhman replied, "I have other reasons to know there is no afterlife."

婆罗门报言：「我更有余缘，知无他世。」

Kāśyapa asked, "What are your reasons?"

迦叶问曰：「以何缘知？」

He replied, "Kāśyapa, I have relatives who are seriously ill. I went to them and said, 'The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have different views. Some say there is an afterlife, and that those who do not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol will be reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three after their bodies perish. I do not believe this. The reason is that I have never seen anyone who has died and returned to speak of where they fell. If someone could come and tell me where they were reborn, I would definitely believe it. Now you are my relative and you have fully observed the five precepts. When your body perishes and your life ends, you will definitely be reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. Make me believe it! Take a vow from me. If there really is heavenly merit, you must come back and tell me so I can know. Then I will believe.' Kāśyapa, since they died, they have not come back. They were my relatives and should not have deceived me. They promised but did not come. There is definitely no afterlife."

答曰：「迦叶！我有亲族，遇患笃重，我往语言：『沙门、婆罗门各怀异见，说有后世，言不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒者，身坏命终，皆生忉利天上；我亦不信，所以然者？初未曾见死已来还，说所堕处；若有人来说所堕生，我必信耳。今汝是我所亲，五戒具足，身坏命终，必生忉利天上，令我相信，从汝取定，若审有天福者，汝当还来，语我使知，然后当信。』迦叶！彼命终已，至今不来，彼是我亲，不应有欺，许而不来，必无他世。」

Kāśyapa replied, "Here, one hundred years is just one day and night in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. Thirty such days make a month, and twelve such months make a year. The lifespan of the gods there is one thousand such years. What do you think, brāhmaṇa? Your relatives fully observed the five precepts. When their bodies perished and their lives ended, they were definitely reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. After being reborn there, they might think, 'I have just been born here. I will enjoy myself and play for two or three days, then come down to tell you.' Would you be able to see them?"

迦叶答言：「此间百岁，正当忉利天上一日一夜耳，如是亦三十日为一月，十二月为一岁，如是彼天寿千岁。云何？婆罗门！汝亲族五戒具足，身坏命终，必生忉利天上。彼生天已，作是念言：『我初生此，当二三日中，娱乐游戯，然后来下报汝言。』者，宁得见不？」

He replied, "No, I have been dead for a long time. How could I see them?"

答曰：「不也！我死久矣，何由相见？」

The brāhmaṇa said, "I do not believe it. Who has told you about the Heaven of the Thirty-three and its lifespan?"

婆罗门言：「我不信也，谁来告汝有忉利天，寿命如是？」

Kāśyapa said, "Those who are wise understand through parables. I will now give you another example. Suppose there is a person who is born blind and does not recognize the five colors - blue, yellow, red, white, coarse, fine, long, and short. He also does not see the sun, moon, stars, hills, and ravines. Someone asks him, 'What are the five colors - blue, yellow, red, white?' The blind person replies, 'There are no five colors.' In this way, he says that coarse, fine, long, short, the sun, moon, stars, mountains, and ravines do not exist. What do you think, brāhmaṇa? Is the blind person's statement correct?"

迦叶言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今更当为汝引喻。譬如有人，从生而盲，不识五色，青、黄、赤、白，麤、细、长、短，亦不见日、月、星象、丘陵、沟壑。有人问言：『青、黄、赤、白五色云何？』盲人答曰：『无有五色。』如是麤、细、长、短、日、月、星象、山陵、沟壑，皆言无有。云何？婆罗门！彼盲人言，是正答不？」

He replied, "No!"

答曰：「不也！」

"Why? In the world, there are indeed the five colors - blue, yellow, red, white, coarse, fine, long, and short. There are the sun, moon, stars, mountains, and ravines. But he says they do not exist. Brāhmaṇa! You are also like this. The lifespan of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven is real and not false. You yourself do not see it, so you say it does not exist."

「所以者何？世间现有五色，青、黄、赤、白，麤、细、长、短，日、月、星象、山陵、沟壑，而彼言无。婆罗门！汝亦如是。忉利天寿，实有不虚，汝自不见，便言其无。」

The brāhmaṇa said, "Although you say it exists, I still do not believe it."

婆罗门言：「汝虽言有，我犹不信。」

Kāśyapa said again, "What further evidence do you have to know that it does not exist?"

迦叶又言：「汝复作何缘，而知其无？」

He replied, "Kāśyapa! In the village I govern, there was a thief. The people caught him and brought him to me, saying, 'This person is a thief. Please punish him.' I told them, 'Tie this person up and put him in a large cauldron, cover it tightly with leather and mud, making it airtight, and have people surround it to cook him.' I wanted to observe where his spirit would leave from. I took my attendants to surround the cauldron and watch, but I did not see his spirit come or go. I also opened the cauldron and did not see the spirit coming or going. For this reason, I know there is no other world."

答曰：「迦叶！我所封村人有作贼者，伺察所得，将诣我所，语我言：『此人为贼，唯愿治之。』我答言：『收缚此人，著大釜中，韦葢厚泥，使其牢密，勿令有泄，遣人围遶，以火煮之。』我时欲观知其精神所出之处，将诸侍从，遶釜而观，都不见其神去来处，又发釜看，亦不见神有往来之处。以此缘故，知无他世。」

Kāśyapa said again, "I will ask you a question. If you can answer, please do so. Brahmin! When you are resting and sleeping on a high building, have you ever dreamed of mountains, forests, rivers, parks, ponds, countries, cities, and streets?"

迦叶又言：「我今问汝，若能答者随意报之。婆罗门！汝在高楼，息寝卧时，颇曾梦见山林、江河、园观、浴池、国邑、街巷不？」

He replied, "I have dreamed!"

答曰：「梦见！」

He asked again, "Brahmin! When you were dreaming, were your family members and attendants guarding you?"

又问：「婆罗门！汝当梦时，居家眷属侍卫汝不？」

He replied, "They were guarding me!"

答曰：「侍卫！」

He asked again, "Brahmin! Did your family members see your consciousness coming in and going out?"

又问：「婆罗门！汝诸眷属见汝识神有出入不？」

He replied, "No, they did not!"

答曰：「不见！」

Kāśyapa said again, "When you are alive, the coming and going of your consciousness is still invisible. How much more so for the dead! You cannot use what is currently visible to observe sentient beings. Brahmin! There are bhikṣus who, in the early and late watches of the night, give up sleep and diligently practice without slacking, focusing on the factors of the path. With the power of samādhi, they cultivate the pure divine eye. With the power of the divine eye, they observe sentient beings, dying here and being born there, from there being born here, their long and short lifespans, their good and bad complexions, following their actions to receive retribution, the good and evil destinies, all of which they know and see. You cannot use your defiled and turbid fleshly eyes, which cannot penetrate to see the destinies of sentient beings, and say that they do not exist. Brahmin! From this, we can know that there must be a next world."

迦叶又言：「汝今生存，识神出入，尚不可见，况于死者乎？汝不可以目前现事观于众生。婆罗门！有比丘初夜、后夜捐除睡眠，精勤不懈，专念道品，以三昧力，修净天眼；以天眼力，观于众生。死此生彼，从彼生此，寿命长短，颜色好丑，随行受报，善恶之趣，皆悉知见。汝不可以秽浊肉眼，不能彻见众生所趣，便言无也。婆罗门！以此可知，必有他世。」

The brahmin said, "Although you have used analogies to speak of the existence of the next world, according to what I see, it still does not exist."

婆罗门言：「汝虽引喻说有他世，如我所见，犹无有也。」

Kāśyapa said again, "Do you have any other causes and conditions to know that there is no next world?"

迦叶又言：「汝颇更有因缘，知无他世耶？」

The brahmin said, "There is!"

婆罗门言：「有！」

Kāśyapa said, "What causes and conditions do you have to know?"

迦叶言：「以何缘知？」

The brahmin said, "I have a village that I have been granted. The people there committed theft. I had them caught and brought before me. They said, 'This person is a thief. Please punish him.' I ordered my attendants to arrest and bind this person, flay his skin, and search for his consciousness, but I did not see it. I also ordered my attendants to cut his flesh and search for the consciousness, but I still did not see it. I also ordered my attendants to cut his tendons, veins, and bones to search for the consciousness, but I still did not see it. I also ordered my attendants to break the bones and extract the marrow, and search for the consciousness in the marrow, but I still did not see it. Kāśyapa! Based on this cause and condition, I know that there is no other world."

婆罗门言：「我所封村人有作贼者，伺察所得，将诣我所，语我言：『此人为贼，唯愿治之。』我勅左右收缚此人，生剥其皮，求其识神，而都不见；又勅左右脔割其肉，以求识神，又复不见；又勅左右截其筋、脉、骨间求神，又复不见；又勅左右打骨出髓，髓中求神，又复不见。迦叶！我以此缘，知无他世。」

Kāśyapa said again, "The wise understand through analogies. I will now give you another analogy. Long ago, in a distant past, there was a country that was destroyed and had not yet been restored. At that time, there were merchants traveling through the country with five hundred carts. There was a brahman who served the fire god and always stayed in a forest. The merchants all went to stay there and left in the morning. The brahman who served the fire god thought, 'The merchants stayed in this forest last night. Now that they have left, I wonder if they left anything behind. I should go and take a look.' He went to where they were, but saw nothing. Only a small child, just one year old, was sitting there alone. The brahman thought, 'How can I bear to see this child die in front of me? I might as well take this child to where I live and raise him.' He then picked up the child and went to where he lived to raise him. The child grew up to be over ten years old.

迦叶复言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今复当为汝引喻。乃往过去久远世时，有一国坏，荒毁未复，时，有商贾五百乘车经过其土，有一梵志奉事火神，常止一林。时，诸商人皆往投宿，清旦别去，时，事火梵志作是念言：『向诸商人宿此林中，今者已去，傥有遗漏可试往看。』寻诣彼所，都无所见，唯有一小儿始年一岁，独在彼坐。梵志复念：『我今何忍见此小儿于我前死？今者宁可将此小儿至吾所止，养活之耶！』即抱小儿往所住处而养育之，其儿转大，至十余岁。

At that time, the brahman had a small matter and wanted to travel among the people. He said to the child, 'I have a small matter and want to go out for a while. You must carefully guard this fire and make sure it does not go out. If the fire goes out, you should use a drill to make fire and light it.' Having given these instructions, he left the forest to travel. After the brahman left, the child was greedy for play and did not watch the fire often. The fire then went out. The child returned from playing and saw that the fire had gone out. He was distressed and said, 'What I did is wrong. When my father left, he instructed me in detail to guard this fire and make sure it does not go out. But because I was greedy for play, I caused the fire to go out. What should I do?' At that time, the child blew on the ashes to look for fire, but could not find it. He then used an axe to split the firewood to look for fire, but still could not find it. He also cut the firewood and put it in a mortar to pound it to look for fire, but still could not find it.

「时，此梵志以少因缘欲游人间，语小儿曰：『我有少缘，欲暂出行，汝善守护此火，慎勿使灭。若火灭者，当以钻钻木，取火燃之。』具诫勅已，出林游行。梵志去后，小儿贪戏，不数视火，火遂便灭；小儿戏还，见火已灭，懊恼而言：『我所为非，我父去时，具约勅我，守护此火，慎勿令灭，而我贪戏，致使火灭，当如之何？』彼时，小儿吹灰求火，不能得已，便以斧劈薪求火，复不能得，又复斩薪置于臼中，捣以求火，又不能得。

At that time, the brahman returned from among the people and went to the forest. He asked the child, 'When I instructed you to guard the fire, did the fire not go out?' The child replied, 'I went out to play and did not watch it often. The fire has now gone out.' He further asked the child, 'What method did you use to look for fire?' The child replied, 'Fire comes from wood. I used an axe to split the wood to look for fire, but could not find it. I also cut it into pieces and put it in a mortar to pound it to look for fire, but still could not find it.' At that time, the brahman used a drill to make fire from wood, piled up firewood and lit it. He told the child, 'The method for seeking fire should be like this. One should not break it apart and pound it to seek it.'

「尔时，梵志于人间还，诣彼林所，问小儿曰：『吾先勅汝使守护火，火不灭耶？』小儿对曰：『我向出戏，不时护视，火今已灭。』复问小儿：『汝以何方便更求火耶？』小儿报曰：『火出于木，我以斧破木求火，不得火；复斩之令碎，置于臼中，杵捣求火，复不能得。』时，彼梵志以钻钻木出火，积薪而燃，告小儿曰：『夫欲求火，法应如此，不应破析杵碎而求。』

"Brahman, you are also like this, without skillful means, peeling the skin of a dead person to seek the spirit. You cannot observe sentient beings with what is currently before your eyes. Brahman, there are bhikshus who give up sleep in the early and late parts of the night, diligently and tirelessly, focusing on the factors of the path. With the power of samadhi, they cultivate the pure heavenly eye. With the power of the heavenly eye, they observe sentient beings, dying here and being born there, being born there and being born here, their long and short lifespans, their good and bad complexions, following their actions and receiving retribution, the good and evil destinies, all of which they know and see. You cannot use your defiled and turbid fleshly eyes, which cannot penetrate and see the destinies of sentient beings, and then say that there is no other world. Brahman, from this it can be known that there must be a next world."

「婆罗门！汝亦如是无有方便，皮剥死人而求识神，汝不可以目前现事观于众生。婆罗门！有比丘初夜后夜捐除睡眠，精勤不懈，专念道品，以三昧力，修净天眼，以天眼力，观于众生，死此生彼，从彼生此，寿命长短，颜色好丑，随行受报，善恶之趣，皆悉知见；汝不可以秽浊肉眼，不能彻见众生所趣，便言无也。婆罗门！以此可知，必有他世。」

The brahman said, "Although you have used analogies to speak of the existence of the next world, according to what I see, there is still none."

婆罗门言：「汝虽引喻说有他世，如我所见，犹无有也。」

Kasyapa said again, "Do you have any other reasons for knowing that there is no next world?"

迦叶复言：「汝颇更有因缘，知无他世耶？」

The brahman said, "There is!"

婆罗门言：「有！」

Kasyapa asked, "What reasons do you have for knowing?"

迦叶言：「以何缘知？」

The brahman said, "In the village I was granted, there was a thief. I had him caught and brought to me. They said, 'This person is a thief. Please punish him.' I ordered my attendants, 'Weigh this person on a scale.' The attendants received the order and weighed him. I then told the attendants, 'Carefully kill this person, without damaging the skin and flesh.' They received my instructions and killed him without damage. I again ordered my attendants, 'Weigh him again.' He was heavier than before. Kasyapa! When he was alive, his consciousness was still there, his complexion was pleasant, and he could still speak. His body was lighter. After death, when weighed again, his consciousness was gone, his complexion was gone, he could not speak, and his body was heavier. From this reason, I know there is no other world."

婆罗门言：「我所封村人有作贼者，伺察所得，将诣我所，语我言：『此人为贼，唯愿治之。』我勅左右：『将此人以称称之。』侍者受命，即以称称。又告侍者：『汝将此人安徐杀之，勿损皮肉。』即受我教，杀之无损。我复勅左右：『更重称之。』乃重于本。迦叶！生称彼人，识神犹在，颜色悦豫，犹能言语，其身乃轻；死已重称，识神已灭，无有颜色，不能语言，其身更重，我以此缘，知无他世。」

Kasyapa said to the brahman, "I will ask you now. Answer me as you wish. When weighing iron, first weigh it when cold, then weigh it when hot. How can it be lustrous, soft, and light? How can it be without luster, hard, and heavy?"

迦叶语婆罗门：「吾今问汝，随意答我。如人称铁，先冷称已，然后热称，何有光色柔輭而轻？何无光色坚［革＊卬］而重？」

The brahman said, "Hot iron has color, is soft, and is light. Cold iron has no color, is hard, and is heavy."

婆罗门言：「热] cf.【丽】铁有色，柔輭而轻，冷铁无色，刚强而重。」

Kasyapa said, "People are also like this. When alive, they have color, are soft, and are light. When dead, they have no color, are hard, and are heavy. From this, we can know that there must be an afterlife."

迦叶语言：「人亦如是，生有颜色，柔輭而轻；死无颜色，刚强而重。以此可知，必有他世。」

The brahman said, "Although you have used analogies to speak of an afterlife, according to what I see, there definitely is none."

婆罗门言：「汝虽引喻说有他世，如我所见，必无有也。」

Kasyapa said, "What further evidence do you have to know that there is no afterlife?"

迦叶言：「汝复有何缘，知无他世？」

The brahman replied, "I have relatives who fell seriously ill. I went to them and said, 'Help this patient lie on their right side.' Their gaze, stretching, and speech were as usual. When they were made to lie on their left side, they rolled back and forth, stretching, gazing, and speaking as usual, and then they died. I had people help them turn, lying on their left and right sides, and carefully observed them, but they no longer stretched, gazed, or spoke. I know from this that there definitely is no afterlife."

婆罗门答言：「我有亲族，遇患笃重。时，我到彼语言：『扶此病人，令右脇卧。』视瞻、屈伸、言语如常；又使左卧，反复宛转，屈伸、视瞻、言语如常，寻即命终。吾复使人扶转，左卧右卧，反复谛观，不复屈伸、视瞻、言语，吾以是知，必无他世。」

Kasyapa said again, "The wise understand through analogies. Now I will give you an analogy. Once there was a country that had never heard the sound of a conch. At that time, there was a person who was skilled in blowing conches. He went to that country and entered a village. He held the conch and blew it three times, then placed it on the ground. The men and women of the village were startled by the sound and all went to ask, 'What is this sound, so beautiful and clear?' That person pointed to the conch and said, 'This is the sound of this object.' Then the villagers touched the conch with their hands and said, 'Make a sound! Make a sound!' But the conch did not make a sound. The owner then took the conch and blew it three times, placing it on the ground. The villagers said, 'The beautiful sound just now was not the power of the conch. It was because of the hands, mouth, and breath that it sounded.' People are also like this. When there is life, consciousness, and the breath coming in and out, one can stretch, gaze, and speak. When there is no life, no consciousness, and no breath coming in and out, there is no stretching, gazing, or speaking."

迦叶复言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，今当为汝引喻。昔有一国不闻贝声，时，有一人善能吹贝，往到彼国，入一村中，执贝三吹，然后置地。时，村人男女闻声惊动，皆就往问：『此是何声，哀和清彻乃如是耶？』彼人指贝曰：『此物声也。』时，彼村人以手触贝曰：『汝可作声！汝可作声！』贝都不鸣，其主即取贝三吹置地。时，村人言：『向者，美声非是贝力，有手有口，有气吹之，然后乃鸣。』人亦如是，有寿有识，有息出入，则能屈伸、视瞻、语言；无寿无识，无出入息，则无屈伸、视瞻、语言。」

He also said to the brahman, "You should now abandon this evil and wrong view, so as not to increase suffering for yourself in the long night to come."

又语婆罗门：「汝今宜舍此恶邪见，勿为长夜自增苦恼。」

The brahman said, "I cannot abandon it. The reason is that I have been reciting and studying it for a long time since birth. How can I abandon it?"

婆罗门言：「我不能舍，所以然者？我自生来长夜讽诵，玩习坚固，何可舍耶？」

Kāśyapa said again, "Those who are wise understand through parables. I will now draw another parable for you. Long ago, in a certain country, the borderlands were desolate and the people were in ruins. There were two people in that country, one wise and one foolish. They said to each other, 'I am your relative. Let us leave the city together to seek wealth.' They then followed each other to an empty village. Seeing hemp on the ground, they said to the foolish one, 'Let us pick some and take it back.' The two then each took a load. Continuing on, they passed another village and saw hemp thread. The wise one said, 'Hemp thread is light and fine, it can be picked.' The other said, 'I have already picked hemp, it is tightly bound, I cannot let it go.' The wise one then took the hemp thread and went on with a heavy load. Continuing on together, they saw hemp cloth. The wise one said, 'Hemp cloth is light and fine, it can be picked.' The other said, 'I have already picked hemp, it is tightly bound, I cannot let it go.' The wise one then discarded the hemp thread and took the cloth, making it heavier. Continuing on together, they saw silk. The wise one said, 'Silk is valuable, light and fine, it can be picked.' The other said, 'I have already picked hemp, it is tightly bound, and we have come a long way, I cannot let it go.' The wise one then discarded the hemp cloth and took the silk.

迦叶复言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今当更为汝引喻。乃往久远有一国土，其土边壃，人民荒坏。彼国有二人，一智一愚，自相谓言：『我是汝亲，共汝出城，采侣求财。』即寻相随，诣一空聚，见地有麻即语愚者：『共取持归。』时，彼二人各取一担，复过前村，见有麻缕，其一智者言：『麻缕成功，轻细可取。』其一人言：『我已取麻，系缚牢固，不能舍也。』其一智者即取麻缕，重担而去；复共前进，见有麻布，其一智者言：『麻布成功，轻细可取。』彼一人言：『我以取麻，系缚牢固，不能复舍。』其一智者即舍麻缕取布自重；复共前行，见有劫贝，其一智者言：『劫贝价贵，轻细可取。』彼一人言：『我已取麻，系缚牢固，赍来道远，不能舍也。』时，一智者即舍麻布而取劫贝。

"Continuing on, they saw silk thread, then white cloth, then white copper, then white silver, then gold. The wise one said, 'If there is no gold, take white silver, if no white silver, take white copper, and so on down to hemp thread. If there is no hemp thread, take hemp. Now there is much gold in this village, above all the treasures. You should abandon the hemp and I will abandon the silver, and together take the gold, making it heavier, and return home.' The other said, 'I have picked this hemp, it is tightly bound, and we have come a long way, I cannot let it go. If you want to take it, do as you please.' The wise one then abandoned the silver and took the gold, making it heavier, and returned home. His relatives saw him from afar, greatly pleased and welcomed him. The one who had obtained the gold, seeing his relatives welcoming him, was also greatly pleased. The unwise one, carrying the hemp, returned home. His relatives, seeing him, were displeased and did not rise to welcome him. The one carrying the hemp was doubly distressed and ashamed. Brahmin! You should now abandon your evil habits and wrong views, and do not increase your suffering for a long night, like the one carrying hemp who stubbornly refused to take the gold and returned home with hemp, exhausting himself in vain, displeasing his relatives, and increasing his poverty and suffering for a long night."

「如是前行，见劫贝缕，次见白叠，次见白铜，次见白银，次见黄金，其一智者言：『若无金者，当取白银，若无白银，当取白铜，乃至麻缕，若无麻缕，当取麻耳。今者此村大有黄金，众] cf.【丽】宝之上，汝宜舍麻，我当舍银，共取黄金，自重而归。』彼一人言：『我取此麻，系缚牢固，赍来道远，不能舍也。汝欲取者，自随汝意。』其一智者舍银取金，重担而归其家，亲族遥见彼人大得金宝，欢喜奉迎。时，得金者见亲族迎，复大欢喜。其无智人负麻而归居家，亲族见之，不悦亦不起迎，其负麻者倍增忧愧。婆罗门！汝今宜舍恶习邪见，勿为长夜自增苦恼，如负麻人执意坚固，不取金宝，负麻而归，空自疲劳，亲族不悦，长夜贫穷，自增忧苦也。」

The Brahmin said, "I cannot abandon this view. Why? Because with this view I have taught and benefited many, and the kings of the four directions have all heard my name and know that I am a nihilist."

婆罗门言：「我终不能舍此见也，所以者何？我以此见多所教授，多所饶益，四方诸王皆闻我名，亦尽知我是断灭学者。」

Kāśyapa said again, "The wise understand through parables. I will now give you another parable. Long ago, in a certain country, the borderlands were desolate and the people were in decline. At that time, a caravan of a thousand carts passed through that land, and they did not have enough water, grain, firewood, and grass. The caravan leader thought, 'Our group is large, and we do not have enough water, grain, firewood, and grass. Now, should we divide into two groups, with one group setting out first?' The leader of the first group saw a man with a coarse and large body, red eyes, and a black face, smeared with mud, coming from afar. He asked, 'Where are you coming from?' The man replied, 'I am coming from the village ahead.' He further asked, 'Is there plenty of water, grain, firewood, and grass where you came from?' The man replied, 'Where I came from, there is plenty of water, grain, firewood, and grass. On the way, I encountered a heavy rainstorm, and there is plenty of water and firewood and grass there.' He also told the caravan leader, 'If your carts have grain and grass, you can all discard them. There is plenty there, no need to burden the carts.'

迦叶复言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今当更为汝引喻。乃往久远有一国土，其土边壃，人民荒坏。时，有商人，有千乘车，经过其土，水谷、薪草不自供足，时商主念言：『我等伴多，水谷、薪草不自供足，今者宁可分为二分，其一分者于前发引。』其前发导师见有一人，身体麤大，目赤面黑，泥涂其身，遥见远来，即问：『汝从何来？』报言：『我从前村来。』又问彼言：『汝所来处，多有水谷、薪草不耶？』其人报言：『我所来处，丰有水谷，薪草无乏，我于中路逢天暴雨，其处多水，亦丰薪草。』又语商主：『汝曹车上若有谷草，尽可捐弃，彼自丰有，不须重车。』

The caravan leader said to the merchants, 'When I went ahead, I saw a man with red eyes, a black face, and mud smeared on his body. I asked him from afar, "Where are you coming from?" He replied, "I am coming from the village ahead." I then asked him, "Is there plenty of water, grain, firewood, and grass where you came from?" He answered, "It is very abundant there." He also told me, "On the way, I encountered a heavy rainstorm, and there is plenty of water and firewood and grass there." He further said to me, "If your carts have grain and grass, you can all discard them. There is plenty there, no need to burden the carts." You should all discard the grain and grass, and quickly advance with light carts.' They did as he said, discarding the grain and grass and quickly advancing with light carts.

「时，彼商主语众商言：『吾向前行，见有一人，目赤面黑，泥涂其身，我遥问言：「汝从何来？」即答我言：「我从前村来。」我寻复问：「汝所来处，丰有水谷、薪草不也？」答我言：「彼大丰耳。」又语我言：「向于中路，逢天暴雨，此处多水，又丰薪草。」复语我言：「君等车上若有谷草，尽可捐弃，彼自丰有，不须重车。」汝等宜各弃诸谷草，轻车速进。』即如其言，各共捐弃谷草，轻车速进。

In this way, they did not see water and grass for one day, two days, three days, up to seven days, and still did not see any. At that time, the merchants were exhausted in the wilderness and were eaten by ghosts. The second group then continued on their way. The caravan leader went ahead and again saw a man with red eyes, a black face, and mud smeared on his body. He asked from afar, 'Where are you coming from?' The man replied, 'From the village ahead.' He further asked, 'Is there plenty of water, grain, firewood, and grass where you came from?' The man replied, 'It is very abundant there.' He also told the caravan leader, 'On the way, I encountered a heavy rainstorm, and there is plenty of water and firewood and grass there.' He further said to the caravan leader, 'If your carts have grain and grass, you can all discard them. There is plenty there, no need to burden the carts.'

「如是一日不见水草，二日、三日，乃至七日，又复不见。时，商人穷于旷泽，为鬼所食。其后一部，次复进路，商主时前复见一人，目赤面黑，泥涂其身，遥见问言：『汝从何来？』彼人答言：『从前村来。』又问：『汝所来处，丰有水谷、薪草不耶？』彼人答曰：『大丰有耳。』又语商主：『吾于中路，逢天暴雨，其处多水，亦丰薪草。』又语商主：『君等车上若有谷草，便可捐弃，彼自丰有，不须重车。』

The caravan leader returned and told the merchants, 'When I went ahead, I saw a man who said this: "If your carts have grain and grass, you can all discard them. There is plenty there, no need to burden the carts." The caravan leader said, 'You should not discard your grain and grass. You must obtain new ones before discarding them. Why is that? Only by replacing the old with the new can you then cross this wilderness.' At that time, the merchants proceeded with heavy carts. In this way, they did not see water and grass for one day, two days, three days, up to seven days, and still did not see any. They only saw the previous person being eaten by ghosts, with their bones scattered.

「时，商主还语诸商人言：『吾向前行，见有一人，道如此事：君等车上若有谷草，可尽捐弃，彼自丰有，不须重车。』时，商主言：『汝等谷草慎勿捐弃，须得新者然后当弃，所以者何？新陈相接，然后当得度此旷野。』时，彼商人重车而行，如是一日不见水草，二日、三日至于七日，又亦不见。但见前人为鬼所食，骸骨狼藉。

"Brahmin, the one with red eyes and a black face was a rākṣasa ghost. Those who followed your instructions will suffer for a long time, just like him. The merchants in the first group, due to their lack of wisdom, followed the guide's words and destroyed themselves. Brahmin, those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who are diligent and wise, if one accepts their teachings, will attain peace for a long time, just like the merchants in the second group who, due to their wisdom, escaped the danger. Brahmin, you should now abandon this evil view, so as not to increase your suffering for a long time."

「婆罗门！彼赤眼黑面者，是罗刹鬼也。诸有随汝教者，长夜受苦，亦当如彼。前部商人无智慧故，随导师语，自没其身。婆罗门！诸有沙门、婆罗门，精进智慧，有所言说，承用其教者，则长夜获安，如彼后部商人有智慧故，得免危难。婆罗门！汝今宁可舍此恶见，勿为长夜自增苦恼。」

The brahmin said, "I will never abandon my views. If someone comes to strongly admonish me, it will only give rise to my anger, and I will never abandon my views."

婆罗门言：「我终不能舍所见也，设有人来强谏我者，生我忿耳，终不舍见。」

Kāśyapa said again, "Those who are wise understand through parables. I will now further draw a parable for you. Long ago, in a certain country, the border areas were desolate and the people were in decline. At that time, there was a man who liked to raise pigs. He went to an empty village and saw some dry dung. He thought to himself, 'This place is full of dung. My pigs are hungry. I should now take some grass, wrap this dry dung, and carry it back.' He then took some grass, wrapped the dung, and carried it. On the way, he encountered heavy rain. The dung juice flowed down to his ankles. When the people saw it, they all said, 'You madman! Dung is filthy and smelly. Even if it were sunny, you shouldn't carry it, let alone carry it in the rain!' The man became angry and cursed back, 'You are all foolish. You don't know that my pigs are hungry. If you knew, you wouldn't say I'm foolish.' Brahmin, you should now abandon this evil view, do not be deluded and suffer for a long time. Like that foolish child carrying dung, when the people admonished him, he became even more angry and cursed, saying that they did not know."

迦叶又言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今当复为汝引喻。乃昔久远有一国土，其土边壃，人民荒坏。时，有一人好喜养猪，诣他空村，见有干粪，寻自念言：『此处饶粪，我猪豚饥，今当取草裹此干粪，头戴而归。』即寻取草，裹粪而戴，于其中路，逢天大雨，粪汁流下，至于足跟，众人见已，皆言：『狂人！粪除臭处，正使天晴，尚不应戴，况于雨中戴之而行！』其人方怒，逆骂詈言：『汝等自痴，不知我家猪豚饥饿；汝若知者，不言我痴。』婆罗门！汝今宁可舍此恶见，勿守迷惑，长夜受苦。如彼痴子戴粪而行，众人诃谏，逆更瞋骂，谓他不知。」

The brahmin said to Kāśyapa, "If you say that doing good leads to rebirth in heaven and death is better than life, then you should all use swords to kill yourselves, drink poison, or tie yourselves up and throw yourselves off a cliff. But now, you are greedy for life and cannot kill yourselves. This shows that death is not better than life."

婆罗门语迦叶言：「汝等若谓行善生天，死胜生者，汝等则当以刀自刎，饮毒而死，或五缚其身，自投高岸，而今贪生不能自杀者，则知死不胜生。」

Kāśyapa said again, "Those who are wise understand through parables. I will now further draw a parable for you. Long ago, in this Sāpāṇaka village, there was a brahmin who was elderly and over a hundred and twenty years old. He had two wives, one of whom had a son and the other was pregnant. Soon after, the brahmin passed away. The son of the first wife said to the second wife, 'All the wealth belongs to me. You have no share.' The second wife said, 'Wait a moment. I will give birth. If it is a boy, he will have a share of the wealth. If it is a girl, you can marry her and get wealth.' The son persistently asked for the wealth several times, but the second wife answered as before. The son pressed her further. The second wife then used a sharp knife to cut open her belly to see if it was a boy or a girl."

迦叶复言：「诸有智者，以譬喻得解，我今当更为汝引喻。昔者，此斯波醯村有一梵志，耆旧长宿，年百二十。彼有二妻，一先有子，一始有娠。时，彼梵志未久命终，其大母子语小母言：『所有财宝，尽应与我，汝无分也。』时小母言：『汝为小待，须我分娠，若生男者，应有财分；若生女者，汝自嫁娶，当得财物。』彼子慇懃再三索财，小母答如初；其子又逼不已，时，彼小母即以利刀自决其腹，知为男女。」

He said to the brahmin, "The mother now kills herself and also harms the fetus in her womb. You brahmins are also like this. You not only kill yourselves, but also want to kill others. If śramaṇas and brahmins diligently cultivate goodness, are fully endowed with precepts and virtues, and live long in this world, they will benefit many and bring peace to gods and humans. I will now draw a final parable for you to make you understand the calamity of evil views. Long ago, in this Sāpāṇaka village, there were two performers who were skilled in juggling balls. The two competed in their skills, and one won. The loser said to the winner, 'Let's stop for today. We will compete again tomorrow.' The loser then returned home, smeared the juggling balls with poison, dried them, and brought them to the winner the next day. He said, 'Let's compete again.' They started playing together. The loser first gave the poisoned ball to the winner, who swallowed it. The loser then gave another poisoned ball, which he also swallowed. The poison spread, and his whole body trembled. The loser scolded in verse:

语婆罗门言：「母今自杀，复害胎子，汝婆罗门，亦复如是；既自杀身，复欲杀人。若沙门、婆罗门，精勤修善，戒德具足，久存世者，多所饶益，天人获安。吾今末后为汝引喻，当使汝知恶见之殃。昔者，此斯波醯村有二伎人，善于弄丸，二人角伎，一人得胜。时，不如者语胜者言：『今日且停，明当更共试。』其不如者即归家中，取其戯丸，涂以毒药，暴之使干，明持此丸诣胜者所，语言：『更可角伎。』即前共戯，先以毒丸授彼胜者：『胜者即吞。』其不如者复授毒丸，得已随吞，其毒转行，举身战动。时，不如者以偈骂曰：

I smeared the balls with poison, but you swallowed them without
realizing it. You swallowed the small skill, but you will know later.

「『吾以药涂丸，　　而汝吞不觉；小伎汝为吞，　　久后自当知。』」

Kāśyapa said to the brahmin, "You should quickly abandon this evil view now. Do not be stubbornly deluded and increase your own suffering and pain, like that performer who swallowed poison without realizing it."

迦叶语婆罗门言：「汝今当速舍此恶见，勿为专迷，自增苦毒，如彼伎人，吞毒不觉。」

At that time, the brahmin said to Kāśyapa, "Venerable One, when you first used the example of the moon, I already understood. The reason I went back and forth without accepting it immediately was to see Kāśyapa's eloquence and wisdom and give rise to firm faith. I now believe and take refuge in Kāśyapa."

时，婆罗门白迦叶言：「尊者初设月喻，我时已解，所以往返，不时受者，欲见迦叶辩才智慧，生牢固信耳。我今信受，归依迦叶。」

Kāśyapa replied, "Do not take refuge in me. Take refuge in the unsurpassed Venerable One whom I take refuge in."

迦叶报言：「汝勿归我，如我所归无上尊者，汝当归依。」

The brahmin said, "I do not know where the unsurpassed Venerable One whom you take refuge in is now."

婆罗门言：「不审所归无上尊者，今为所在？」

Kāśyapa replied, "My teacher, the World-Honored One, has just passed away."

迦叶报言：「今我师世尊，灭度未久。」

The brahmin said, "If the World-Honored One were here, I would not avoid going far to see him and pay homage. Now, hearing Kāśyapa say that the Tathāgata has entered parinirvāṇa, I now take refuge in the Tathāgata, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha who have entered parinirvāṇa. Kāśyapa, allow me to be an upāsaka in the true Dharma! From now on, for as long as I live, I will not kill, steal, engage in sexual misconduct, deceive, or drink alcohol. I will now make great offerings to all."

婆罗门言：「世尊若在，不避远近，其当亲见，归依礼拜。今闻迦叶言：『如来灭度！』今即归依灭度如来及法、众僧。迦叶！听我于正法中为优婆塞！自今已后，尽寿不杀、不盗、不婬、不欺、不饮酒，我今当为一切大施。」

Kāśyapa said, "If you slaughter living beings and beat your servants to hold a gathering, this is not pure merit. It is like barren and rocky ground, where thorns grow abundantly. Planting in it will yield nothing. If you slaughter living beings and beat your servants to hold a grand gathering, giving to those with wrong views, this is not pure merit. If you make great offerings without harming living beings, without using sticks and whips on your servants, joyfully holding a gathering, giving to the pure assembly, you will attain great merit. It is like good fields, planting according to the seasons, you will surely reap the fruits."

迦叶语言：「若汝宰杀众生，挝打僮仆，而为会者，此非净福。又如硗确薄地，多生荆棘，于中种植，必无所获。汝若宰杀众生，挝打僮仆，而为大会，施邪见众，此非净福。若汝大施，不害众生，不以杖楚加于僮仆，欢喜设会，施清净众，则获大福。犹如良田，随时种植，必获果实」。

"Kāśyapa! From now on, always make pure offerings to the monastic community, do not let it be interrupted."

「迦叶！自今已后，常净施众僧，不令断绝。」

At that time, there was a young brahmin named Māṭhara standing behind Vāseṭṭha. Vāseṭṭha turned to him and said, "I wish to make a great offering to all. You should manage and arrange it for me."

时，有一年少梵志，名曰摩头在弊宿后立，弊宿顾语曰：「吾欲设一切大施，汝当为我经营处分。」

When the young brahmin heard Vāseṭṭha's words, he immediately managed and arranged it. After making the great offering, he said, "May Vāseṭṭha not obtain the rewards of merit in this world and the next."

时，年少梵志闻弊宿语已，即为经营，为大施已，而作是言：「愿使弊宿今世、后世不获福报。」

At that time, Vāseṭṭha heard that the brahmin had managed and arranged the offering, and said such words, "May Vāseṭṭha not obtain the rewards of merit in this world and the next." He then summoned the brahmin and said to him, "Did you really say that?"

时，弊宿闻彼梵志经营施已，有如是言：「愿使弊宿今世、后世不获果报。」即命梵志而告之曰：「汝当有是言耶？」

He replied, "Yes, I really said that. The reason is that the food prepared now is coarse and bad. If this is offered to the Sangha, even if shown to the king, he would not be able to touch it with his hand, let alone eat it. The present offering is not pleasant, so how can one obtain pure rewards in the next life? The king offers robes to the Sangha, all made of hemp cloth. If shown to the king, he would not be able to touch it with his foot, let alone wear it himself. The present offering is not pleasant, so how can one obtain pure rewards in the next life?"

答曰：「如是！实有是言。所以然者？今所设食，麤澁弊恶，以此施僧，若以示王，王尚不能以手暂向，况当食之？现在所设，不可喜乐，何由后世得净果报？王施僧衣纯以麻布，若以示王，王尚不能以足暂向，况能自著？现在所施，不可喜乐，何由后世得净果报？」

At that time, the brahmin again told the brahmin, "From now on, you should use the food I eat and the clothes I wear to offer to the Sangha."

时，婆罗门又告梵志：「自今已后，汝以我所食、我所著衣以施众僧。」

At that time, the brahmin immediately accepted the order and used the king's food and clothes to make offerings to the Sangha. At that time, the brahmin made this pure offering. When his body perished and his life ended, he was reborn in a lower heaven. The brahmin who organized the assembly, when his body perished and his life ended, was reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three Gods.

时，梵志即承教旨，以王所食、王所著衣供养众僧。时，婆罗门设此净施，身坏命终，生一下劣天中，梵志经营会者，身坏命终，生忉利天。

At that time, the brahmin Bheṣaka, the young brahmin, and the brahmin and householder Śāṇavāsa, having heard what the girl Kāśyapa said, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

尔时，弊宿婆罗门、年少梵志及斯婆醯婆罗门、居士等，闻童女迦叶所说，欢喜奉行。
