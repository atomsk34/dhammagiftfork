DĀ 9

Dīrghāgama 长阿含经

Chapter 2 第二分

9 （九）众集经

Parallel to: DN 33 / DN 34 (~) / DĀ 10 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Malla, together with a group of 1,250 bhikṣus. He gradually arrived at the Jambu Grove in the city of Pāpā.

一时，佛于末罗游行，与千二百五十比丘俱，渐至波婆城阇头庵婆园。

At that time, the World-Honored One, on the full moon day of the fifteenth, was sitting in the open air, surrounded by the bhikṣu saṅgha in front and behind. After speaking the Dharma for much of the night, the World-Honored One said to Śāriputra, "Now, the bhikṣus from the four directions have gathered. They are all diligently practicing, abandoning sleep. I am suffering from a backache and wish to rest for a while. You should now speak the Dharma for the bhikṣus."

尔时，世尊以十五日月满时，于露地坐，诸比丘僧前后围绕。世尊于夜多说法已，告舍利弗言：「今者四方诸比丘集，皆共精勤，捐除睡眠，吾患背痛，欲暂止息，汝今可为诸比丘说法。」

He replied, "Yes! I shall do as the Sage instructs."

对曰：「唯然！当如圣教。」

At that time, the World-Honored One folded his saṃghāṭī robe four times, lay down on his right side like a lion, and placed one foot on top of the other.

尔时，世尊即四牒僧伽梨，偃右脇如师子，累足而卧。

At that time, Śāriputra said to the bhikṣus, "In this city of Pāpā, there was a Nirgrantha who recently passed away. His disciples divided into two groups, always quarreling and seeking each other's faults, insulting each other, and criticizing each other, saying, 'I know this Dharma, you do not know this. You are in wrong views, I am in the right Dharma. Your words are confused, without sequence.' They praised their own words as true and correct, saying, 'My words are superior, your words are inferior. I am now the master of debate. If you have any questions, come ask me.'

时，舍利弗告诸比丘：「今此波婆城有尼乾子命终未久，其后弟子分为二部，常共诤讼相求长短，迭相骂詈，各相是非：『我知此法，汝不知此；汝在邪见，我在正法。言语错乱，无有前后。』自称己言，以为真正。『我所言胜，汝所言负，我今能为谈论之主，汝有所问，可来问我。』

Bhikṣus! At that time, the people of the country who followed the Nirgranthas were tired of the sound of their quarrels, all because their Dharma was not truly correct. The Dharma being not truly correct, there was no way to attain liberation. It was like an old stupa that could not be repaired. This was not spoken by the Tathāgata. Bhikṣus! Only the Dharma of the unsurpassed Śākyamuni is the most truly correct and can lead to liberation. It is like a new stupa that is easy to adorn. This was spoken by the Tathāgata. Bhikṣus! We should now gather the Dharma and Vinaya to prevent disputes, so that the holy life may endure for a long time, benefiting many, and bringing peace to gods and humans. Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata has spoken of one true Dharma: all sentient beings depend on food to survive. The Tathāgata has also spoken of one Dharma: all sentient beings go by means of walking. This is the one Dharma spoken by the Tathāgata. We should gather it together to prevent disputes, so that the holy life may endure for a long time, benefiting many, and bringing peace to gods and humans.

「诸比丘！时，国人民奉尼干者，厌患此辈鬪讼之声，皆由其法不真正故；法不真正无由出要，譬如朽] cf.【丽】【碛砂】塔不可复圬，此非三耶三佛所说。诸比丘！唯我释迦无上尊法，最为真正可得出要，譬如新塔易可严饰，此是三耶三佛之所说也。诸比丘！我等今者，宜集法、律，以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所饶益，天、人获安。诸比丘！如来说一正法，一切众生皆仰食存。如来所说复有一法，一切众生皆由行往，是为一法如来所说，当共集之，以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所] cf.【丽】饶益，天、人获安。

Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata has spoken of two true Dharmas: one is name, the other is form. There are also two Dharmas: one is delusion, the other is craving. There are also two Dharmas: one is the view of existence, the other is the view of non-existence. There are also two Dharmas: one is shamelessness, the other is non-embarrassment. There are also two Dharmas: one is shame, the other is embarrassment. There are also two Dharmas: one is the knowledge of exhaustion, the other is the knowledge of non-arising. There are also two Dharmas, two causes, and two conditions that give rise to desire and love: one is pure and wonderful form, the other is non-reflection. There are also two Dharmas, two causes, and two conditions that give rise to anger: one is resentment, the other is non-reflection. There are also two Dharmas, two causes, and two conditions that give rise to wrong views: one is hearing from others, the other is wrong reflection. There are also two Dharmas, two causes, and two conditions that give rise to right views: one is hearing from others, the other is right reflection. There are also two Dharmas, two causes, and two conditions: one is liberation through learning, the other is liberation without learning. There are also two Dharmas, two causes, and two conditions: one is the conditioned realm, the other is the unconditioned realm. Bhikṣus! This is what the Tathāgata has spoken. We should gather it together to prevent disputes, so that the holy life may endure for a long time, benefiting many, and bringing peace to gods and humans.

「诸比丘！如来说二正法：一名，二色。复有二法：一痴，二爱。复有二法：有见、无见。复有二法：一无惭，二无愧。复有二法：一有惭，二有愧。复有二法：一尽智，二无生智。复有二法，二因二缘生于欲爱：一者净妙色，二者不思惟。复有二法，二因二缘生于瞋恚：一者怨憎，二者不思惟。复有二法，二因二缘生于邪见：一者从他闻，二者邪思惟。复有二法，二因二缘生于正见：一者从他闻，二者正思惟。复有二法，二因二缘：一者学解脱，二者无学解脱。复有二法，二因二缘：一者有为界，二者无为界。诸比丘！是为如来所说，当共撰集以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所饶益，天、人获安。

Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata has spoken of three true Dharmas: namely, the three unwholesome roots: one is greed, two is anger, three is delusion. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three wholesome roots: one is non-greed, two is non-anger, three is non-delusion. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three unwholesome practices: one is unwholesome bodily practice, two is unwholesome verbal practice, three is unwholesome mental practice. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three unwholesome practices: unwholesome bodily practice, unwholesome verbal practice, unwholesome mental practice. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three unwholesome actions: unwholesome bodily action, unwholesome verbal action, unwholesome mental action. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three wholesome actions: wholesome bodily action, wholesome verbal action, wholesome mental action. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three unwholesome thoughts: thoughts of desire, thoughts of anger, thoughts of harm. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three wholesome thoughts: thoughts of non-desire, thoughts of non-anger, thoughts of non-harm. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three unwholesome intentions: intentions of desire, intentions of anger, intentions of harm. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three wholesome intentions: intentions of non-desire, intentions of non-anger, intentions of non-harm.

「诸比丘！如来说三正法，谓三不善根：一者贪欲，二者瞋恚，三者愚痴。复有三法，谓三善根：一者不贪，二者不恚，三者不痴。复有三法，谓三不善行：一者不善身行，二者不善口行，三者不善意行。复有三法，谓三不善行：身不善行、口不善行、意不善行。复有三法，谓三恶行：身恶行、口恶行、意恶行。复有三法，谓三善行：身善行、口善行、意善行。复有三法，谓三不善想：欲想、瞋想、害想。复有三法，谓三善想：无欲想、无瞋想、无害想。复有三法，谓三不善思：欲思、恚思、害思。复有三法，谓三善思：无欲思、无恚思、无害思。

There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three meritorious actions: the action of giving, the action of equality, and the action of contemplation. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three feelings: pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-pleasant-nor-painful feeling. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of craving: craving for desire, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of taints: outflow of desire, outflow of existence, and outflow of ignorance. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of fires: fire of desire, fire of anger, and fire of delusion. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of seeking: seeking for desire, seeking for existence, and seeking for the practice of purity. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of increase: increase of self, increase of the world, and increase of the Dharma. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three realms: realm of desire, realm of anger, and realm of harm. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three realms: realm of detachment, realm of non-anger, and realm of non-harm. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three realms: realm of form, realm of formlessness, and realm of exhaustion. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three groups: group of precepts, group of concentration, and group of wisdom. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of precepts: precepts of increase, intention of increase, and wisdom of increase.

「复有三法，谓三福业：施业、平等业、思惟业。复有三法，谓三受：乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受。复有三法，谓三爱：欲爱、有爱、无有爱。复有三法，谓三有漏：欲漏、有漏、无明漏。复有三法，谓三火：欲火、恚火、愚痴火。复有三法，谓三求：欲求、有求、梵行求。复有三法，谓三增盛：我增盛、世增盛、法增盛。复有三法，谓三界：欲界、恚界、害界。复有三法，谓三界：出离界、无恚界、无害界。复有三法，谓三界：色界、无色界、尽界。复有三法，谓三聚：戒聚、定聚、慧聚。复有三法，谓三戒：增盛戒、增盛意、增盛慧。

There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of samādhi: samādhi of emptiness, samādhi of signlessness, and samādhi of wishlessness. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three characteristics: characteristic of cessation, characteristic of diligence, and characteristic of abandonment. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of wisdom: wisdom of knowing one's past lives, wisdom of the divine eye, and wisdom of the exhaustion of taints. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of transformations: transformation of spiritual powers, teaching the Dharma according to others' minds, and instruction. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three bases for the arising of desire: one arises from desire, such as humans and gods; two arise from transformation, such as the Heaven of Mastery over Transformations; three arise from being transformed by others, such as the Heaven of Mastery over Transformations of Others. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of birth through pleasure: one, sentient beings naturally accomplish and give rise to a joyful mind, such as the Heaven of Brahma's Radiance and Sound; two, some sentient beings take joy in recollection and praise themselves, such as the Heaven of Radiance; three, attaining the pleasure of cessation, such as the Heaven of Universal Purity.

「复有三法，谓三三昧：空三昧、无愿三昧、无相三昧。复有三法，谓三相：止息相、精勤相、舍相。复有三法，谓三明：自识宿命智明、天眼智明、漏尽智明。复有三法，谓三变化：一者神足变化，二者知他心随意说法，三者教诫。复有三法，谓三欲生本：一者由现欲生人天，二者由化欲生化自在天，三者由他化欲生他化自在天。复有三法，谓三乐生：一者众生自然成办，生欢乐心，如梵光音天初始生时。二者有众生以念为乐，自唱善哉，如光音天。三者得止息乐，如遍净天。

There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of suffering: suffering of conditioned existence, suffering of suffering, and suffering of change. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three faculties: faculty of desire to know, faculty of knowing, and faculty of knowing and having known. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three halls: hall of sages, heavenly hall, and Brahma hall. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of arising: arising from seeing, arising from hearing, and arising from doubt. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of discussion: in the past there were such things and such discussions; in the future there will be such things and such discussions; in the present there are such things and such discussions. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three groups: group of those who are definitely good, group of those who are definitely evil, and group of those who are undetermined. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three kinds of sorrow: sorrow of the body, sorrow of speech, and sorrow of the mind. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three elders: elder in age, elder in the Dharma, and elder in deeds. There are also three Dharmas: namely, the three eyes: flesh eye, heavenly eye, and wisdom eye. Bhikṣus! This is the true Dharma spoken by the Tathāgata. You should all compile it together to prevent disputes, so that the holy life may last long, benefiting many, and bringing peace to gods and humans.

「复有三法，谓三苦：行苦、苦苦、变易苦。复有三法，谓三根：未知欲知根、知根、知已根。复有三法，谓三堂：贤圣堂、天堂、梵堂。复有三法，谓三发：见发、闻发、疑发。复有三法，谓三论：过去有如此事，有如是论；未来有如此事，有如是论；现在有如此事，有如是论。复有三法，谓三聚：正定聚、邪定聚、不定聚。复有三法，谓三忧：身忧、口忧、意忧。复有三法，谓三长老：年耆长老、法长老、作长老。复有三法，谓三眼：肉眼、天眼、慧眼。诸比丘！是为如来所说正法，当共撰集，以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所饶益，天、人获安。

Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata speaks of four correct Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of evil speech: one, lying; two, divisive speech; three, harsh speech; four, meaningless speech. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of good speech: one, truthful speech; two, gentle speech; three, meaningful speech; four, non-divisive speech.

「诸比丘！如来说四正法，谓口四恶行：一者妄语，二者两舌，三者恶口，四者绮语。复有四法，谓口四善行：一者实语，二者软语，三者不绮语，四者不两舌。

There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of unwholesome speech: not seeing and saying one sees, not hearing and saying one hears, not knowing and saying one knows. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of wholesome speech: seeing and saying one sees, hearing and saying one hears, knowing and saying one knows. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of food: food by mouthfuls, food by contact, food by thought, and food by consciousness. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of experience: experiencing suffering now and experiencing suffering later; experiencing suffering now and experiencing happiness later; experiencing happiness now and experiencing suffering later; experiencing happiness now and experiencing happiness later. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of experience: experience of desire, experience of self, experience of precepts, and experience of views. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of bondage: bondage of desire, bondage of anger, bondage of stealing precepts, and bondage of the view of self.

「复有四法，谓四不圣语：不见言见，不闻言闻，不觉言觉，不知言知。复有四法，谓四圣语：见则言见，闻则言闻，觉则言觉，知则言知。复有四法，谓四种食：抟食、触食、念食、识食。复有四法，谓四受：有现作苦行后受苦报；有现作苦行后受乐报；有现作乐行后受苦报；有现作乐行后受乐报。复有四法，谓四受：欲受、我受、戒受、见受。复有四法，谓四缚：贪欲身缚、瞋恚身缚、戒盗身缚、我见身缚。

There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of thorns: the thorn of desire, the thorn of anger, the thorn of views, and the thorn of pride. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of birth: birth from eggs, birth from wombs, birth from moisture, and birth by transformation. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four foundations of mindfulness; namely, a bhikṣu contemplates the body in the body internally, diligently and tirelessly, with mindfulness and non-forgetfulness, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; contemplates the body in the body externally, diligently and tirelessly, with mindfulness and non-forgetfulness, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow; contemplates the body in the body internally and externally, diligently and tirelessly, with mindfulness and non-forgetfulness, abandoning worldly greed and sorrow. The same is true for contemplating feelings, mind, and dharmas. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four right efforts; namely, a bhikṣu, with skillful means, prevents unarisen evil dharmas from arising; with skillful means, causes arisen evil dharmas to cease; with skillful means, causes unarisen good dharmas to arise; with skillful means, causes arisen good dharmas to increase and expand.

「复有四法，谓四刺：欲刺、恚刺、见刺、慢刺。复有四法，谓四生：卵生、胎生、湿生、化生。复有四法，谓四念处；于是，比丘内身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧；内外身身观，精勤不懈，忆念不忘，舍世贪忧。受、意、法观，亦复如是。复有四法，谓四意断；于是，比丘未起恶法，方便使不起；已起恶法，方便使灭；未起善法，方便使起；已起善法，方便思惟，使其增广。

There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four bases of spiritual power; namely, a bhikṣu contemplates the desire for concentration, the practice of cessation, accomplishment, diligence, concentration, and contemplation, all in the same way. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four dhyānas; namely, a bhikṣu, having eliminated desire, evil, and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, attains the joy and bliss of separation, and enters the first dhyāna. Having eliminated initial and sustained thought, with internal confidence and one-pointedness of mind, without initial and sustained thought, with the bliss and joy born of concentration, he enters the second dhyāna. Having eliminated joy, he cultivates equanimity, mindfulness, and diligence, and knows bodily bliss, which is sought by the sages. With mindfulness, equanimity, and bliss, he enters the third dhyāna. Having eliminated suffering and bliss, having previously eliminated joy and sorrow, with neither suffering nor bliss, equanimity, mindfulness, and purity, he enters the fourth dhyāna. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four brahmavihāras: loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four formless concentrations; namely, a bhikṣu, having transcended all perceptions of form, having previously eliminated perceptions of resistance, not thinking of other perceptions, contemplates the limitless sphere of space, having transcended the sphere of space, enters the sphere of consciousness, having transcended the sphere of consciousness, enters the sphere of nothingness, having transcended the sphere of nothingness, enters the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception.

「复有四法，谓四神足；于是，比丘思惟欲定灭行成就；精进定、意定、思惟定，亦复如是。复有四法，谓四禅；于是，比丘除欲、恶不善法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，入于初禅。灭有觉、观，内信、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，入第二禅。离喜修舍、念、进，自知身乐，诸圣所求，忆念、舍、乐，入第三禅。离苦、乐行，先灭忧、喜、不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，入第四禅。复有四法，谓四梵堂：一慈、二悲、三喜、四舍。复有四法，谓四无色定；于是，比丘越一切色想，先尽瞋恚想，不念异想，思惟无量空处，舍空处已入识处，舍识处已入不用处，舍不用处已入有想无想处。

There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four bases of spiritual power: the base of spiritual power of non-greed, the base of spiritual power of non-hatred, the base of spiritual power of right mindfulness, and the base of spiritual power of right concentration. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four noble families; namely, a bhikṣu is content with robes, not rejoicing in good ones, not grieving over bad ones, not defiled, not attached, knowing what to avoid, knowing the way out, diligently practicing in this Dharma without slacking, accomplishing the matter, without deficiency or diminution, and also able to teach others to accomplish this matter. This is called the first content and noble family. From the beginning until now, he has never been disturbed; the gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, gods, and worldly people cannot slander him; he is content with food, bedding, and medical supplies, and it is the same. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four means of embracing: generosity, kind speech, benefiting others, and equal benefit. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four limbs of the stream-enterer: a bhikṣu has unshakable faith in the Buddha, the Dharma, the Saṃgha, and the precepts. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four kinds of realization: realizing the perception of form, realizing the cessation of bodily feelings, realizing the recollection of past lives, and realizing the knowledge of the exhaustion of taints. There are also four Dharmas, namely, the four paths: the slow path of suffering, the swift path of suffering, the slow path of pleasure, and the swift path of pleasure.

「诸比丘！如来说八正法，谓世八法：利、衰、毁、誉、称、讥、苦、乐。复有八法，谓八解脱：色观色，一解脱；内无色想观外色，二解脱；净解脱，三解脱；度色想灭瞋恚想住空处解脱，四解脱；度空处住识处，五解脱；度识处住不用处，六解脱；度不用处住有想无想处，七解脱；度有想无想处住想知灭，八解脱。复有八法，谓八圣道：正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定。复有八法，谓八人：须陀洹向、须陀洹、斯陀含向、斯陀含、阿那含向、阿那含、阿罗汉向、阿罗汉。是为如来所说正法，当共撰集，以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所饶益，天、人获安。

Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata has spoken of nine right Dharmas, namely, the nine abodes of sentient beings: there are sentient beings with various bodies and various perceptions, such as humans and gods, this is the first abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings with various bodies but one perception, such as the Brahmakāyika gods when they first arise, this is the second abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings with one body and various perceptions, such as the Brahmapurohita gods, this is the third abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings with one body and one perception, such as the Mahābrahmā gods, this is the fourth abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings without thought or perception, such as the Ābhāsvara gods, this is the fifth abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings abiding in the space element, this is the sixth abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings abiding in the consciousness element, this is the seventh abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings abiding in the non-discrimination element, this is the eighth abode of sentient beings; there are sentient beings abiding in the neither discrimination nor non-discrimination element, this is the ninth abode of sentient beings. This is the true Dharma taught by the Tathāgata. You should all gather together to prevent disputes, so that the holy life may endure for a long time, benefiting many, bringing peace to gods and humans.

「诸比丘！如来说九正法，所谓九众生居：或有众生，若干种身，若干种想，天及人是，是初众生居；复有众生，若干种身而一想者，梵光音天最初生时是，是二众生居；复有众生，一身若干种想，光音天是，是三众生居；复有众生，一身一想，遍净天是，是四众生居；复有众生，无想无所觉知，无想天是，是五众生居；复有众生，空处住，是六众生居；复有众生，识处住，是七众生居；复有众生，不用处住，是八众生居；复有众生，住有想无想处，是九众生居。是为如来所说正法，当共撰集，以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所饶益，天、人获安。

Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata has spoken of ten right Dharmas, namely, the ten qualities of the non-learner: non-learner right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right mindfulness, right effort, right concentration, right knowledge, and right liberation. This is the true Dharma taught by the Tathāgata. You should all gather together to prevent disputes, so that the holy life may endure for a long time, benefiting many, bringing peace to gods and humans.

「诸比丘！如来说十正法，所谓十无学法，无学正见、正思、正语、正业、正命、正念、正方便、正定、正智、正解脱。是为如来所说正法，当共撰集，以防诤讼，使梵行久立，多所饶益，天、人获安。」

At that time, the World-Honored One approved of what Śāriputra had said. The bhikṣus, having heard what Śāriputra had said, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

尔时，世尊印可舍利弗所说。时，诸比丘闻舍利弗所说，欢喜奉行。
