EĀ² 2.17

Ekottarikāgama 2nd佛说七处三观经

EA2 17（一七）

Parallel to: AN 5.153

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. He addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "If a person's mind is in these five dharmas, even if they hear the Buddha's teachings, they will not be able to eliminate defilements and attain the Dharma eye. What are the five? First, if they disturb the one who is expounding the sūtras; second, if they seek out loopholes; third, if they seek out faults; fourth, even if they hear, their thoughts are wrongly attached to other causes and conditions; fifth, they also lack the intention to elevate themselves, causing their understanding to be biased towards good and bad. If a person's mind follows these five dharmas, even if they hear the Buddha's teachings, they will not be able to understand and eliminate defilements, nor will they be able to give rise to the Dharma eye."

一时，佛在舍卫国，行在祇树给孤独园。佛便告比丘：「若人意在五法中，设使闻佛法教，不应除尘垢，亦不得道眼。何等为五？一者若恼说经者，二者若求便，三者若求穷，四者闻亦邪念意著他因缘，五者亦无有自高意，令所闻分别好丑。若人意随是五法，设使闻佛说法，不应自解尘垢，亦不应生法眼。」

The Buddha further told the bhikṣus, "There are five dharmas. If a person's mind is in these five dharmas, then even if they hear the teachings of the Buddha, they will be able to understand and eliminate defilements, and they will also be able to attain the Dharma eye. What are the five? First, they have no ill-will towards the one who is expounding the sūtras; second, they do not seek out the length and brevity of the sūtras, but ask questions and resolve doubts when they arise; third, their mind is not attached to form, and their mind is not attached to other causes and conditions; fourth, they have their own sharp mind that is able to understand good and bad; fifth, they are able to distinguish and know for themselves. These are the five dharmas. If a person's mind follows these five dharmas, they will be able to understand and eliminate defilements. First, they do not disturb the one who is expounding the sūtras; second, they do not seek out the length and brevity of the sūtras; third, they do not seek out faults; fourth, they do not have wrong thoughts; fifth, they also have their own sharp mind that is able to distinguish good and bad."

佛复告比丘：「有五法，若人意在五法，即闻佛所教行法，为应自解尘垢，亦应得道眼。何等为五？一者无有恶意在说经者；二者亦不求经中长短，有疑问解休；三者意亦不在色，意亦不在他因缘；四者亦自有黠意能解善恶；五者分别自知；是五法。若人意随是五法，能得自解尘垢，一为不恼说经者，二为不求经中长短，三为不求穷，四者为亦不邪念，五为亦自有黠意，能分别白黑。」

The Buddha spoke thus.

佛说如是。
