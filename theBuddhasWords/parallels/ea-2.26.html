EĀ² 2.26

Ekottarikāgama 2nd佛说七处三观经

EA2 26（二六）

Parallel to: AN 4.114 / AN 5.140

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was in the country of Shravasti, staying at Anathapindika's Park in the Jeta Grove. The Buddha then addressed the bhikshus, saying, "An elephant has five characteristics that make it suitable for official use, suitable for the king's use, and acceptable to the king. It can lead the king in accordance with the Dharma. What are the five? First, it is obedient to commands. Second, it is able to stand still. Third, it is able to fight. Fourth, it is able to run. Fifth, it is able to protect itself. What is meant by a palace elephant protecting itself? If an elephant enters the army, it can fight with its front feet, and protect its hind feet, hips, back, belly, shoulders, neck, trunk, and nose. This is called self-protection. If a bhikshu possesses five causes and conditions, he will be worthy of respect and fame, worthy of receiving prostrations and folded hands from others, and his blessings will be boundless. What are the five? First, being obedient to commands. Second, being able to stand still. Third, being able to receive. Fourth, being able to practice. Fifth, being able to protect. What is meant by being able to control and not accept the flavors in the mouth, the smoothness in the body, and the thoughts in the mind? In this way, bhikshus, a bhikshu who can protect himself protects the six sense faculties. The second is for dhyana, the third is for patience, the fourth is for upholding precepts, and the fifth is for being obedient to commands. A practitioner who possesses these five things will be worthy of respect and fame, worthy of receiving prostrations and folded hands from others, and his blessings will be boundless. The disciples heard this and joyfully accepted it."

一时，佛在舍卫国，行在祇树给孤独园。佛便告比丘：「象有五相，为应官，为中王用，为可王意，为象引王随法中。何等五？一者闻受，二者能住，三者能鬪，四者能走，五能自守。何等为，比丘！宫象自守？若象入军中，前足能鬪，后足尻背腹肩项鼻能自护，如是名为自守。若比丘五因缘具，便应礼名闻，便应从人受叉手，便福地无有极。何等为五？闻受为一，能为二，受为三，行为四，守为五。何等为口中味，身中细滑，意中所念，能制不受相？如是比丘！比丘能自守，为守六衰。第二者为禅，第三受为忍辱，第四能为持戒，第五闻受为精进。行者有是五事，便应名闻，便应从人受叉手，便福地无有极，弟子闻可意受。」
