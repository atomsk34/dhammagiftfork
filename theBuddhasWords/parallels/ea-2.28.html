EĀ² 2.28

Ekottarikāgama 2nd佛说七处三观经

EA2 28（二八）

Parallel to: AN 6.23 (~) / AN 8.56

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was in the country of Shravasti, staying at Jetavana Anathapindika's Park. At that time, the Buddha told the monks, "Monks! All fears are called desire. Monks! All suffering is called desire. Monks! All illnesses are called desire. Monks! All bonds are called desire. Monks! All wounds are called desire. Monks! All mud of defilement is called desire. Monks! All attachments are called desire. Monks! All falling into the womb of a mother is called desire.

一时，佛在舍卫国，行在祇树给孤独园。彼时佛告比丘：「诸畏是谓为欲。比丘！谓诸苦是谓为欲。比丘！诸疾病是谓为欲。比丘！诸结是谓为欲。比丘！诸疮是谓为欲。比丘！诸染泥是谓为欲。比丘！诸著是谓为欲。比丘！诸堕母腹中是谓为欲。

Monks! Why are all fears called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. Monks! That is why all fears are called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸畏为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！以是故诸畏为欲。

Monks! Why are all suffering called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. Monks! That is why all suffering is called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸苦为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！以是故诸苦为欲。

Monks! Why are all illnesses called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. Monks! That is why all illnesses are called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸病为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！以是故诸病为欲。

Monks! Why are all bonds called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. That is why all bonds are called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸结为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。以是故诸结为欲。

Monks! Why are all wounds called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. That is why all wounds are called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸疮为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！以是故诸疮为欲。

Monks! Why are all muds called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. That is why all muds are called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸染泥为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！以是故诸染为欲。

Monks! Why are all attachments called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. That is why all attachments are called desire.

「比丘！何以故诸著为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！以是故诸著为欲。

"Monks! Why are all those who fall into the womb called desire? Because foolish people in the world are infatuated with desire and bound by desire, they cannot escape from all fears in this life, nor can they escape from them in the next life. That is why all those who fall into the womb are called desire." After speaking this, the Buddha continued to speak about the cessation:

「比丘！何以故诸堕母腹中为欲？用世间痴人为贪欲所泆，为贪欲所缚，用现世不得脱诸畏，后世亦不得脱。比丘！是故诸堕母腹中为欲。」佛以说是，从后说绝：

"Fear, suffering, disease, and bondage are called desire. Foolish people are bound by these. They fall into the womb because of their attachment to form. As I have explained before, monks should be mindful and not be bound by fear. For the wise, they should cross over and contemplate the cycle of birth, aging, and death in the world."

「畏苦病结疮是谓为欲，痴人为是所缚已，可色从彼堕母腹中，上头所说，比丘正意已知莫离诸畏，为深黠人度彼，当观世间生老行展转。」

At that time, the Buddha spoke thus.

时佛说如是。

"There are eight kinds of wounds: 1. The wound of doubt, 2. The wound of attachment, 3. The wound of greed, 4. The wound of anger, 5. The wound of delusion, 6. The wound of arrogance, 7. The wound of perversion, 8. The wound of birth and death."

「疮有八辈：一为疑疮，二为爱疮，三为贪疮，四为瞋恚疮，五为痴疮，六为憍慢疮，七为邪疮，八为生死疮。」

Translated from Chinese using Gemma-2-Mitra (Buddhist-NLP).

This is the classical Chinese root text of Ekottarikāgama 2nd, a parallel to the Pali sutta(s) listed above. Source: CBETA (Taishō edition).
