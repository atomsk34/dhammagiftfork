EĀ 13.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

13.4 （四）

Parallel to: SN 22.1 / SĀ 107

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Deer Park in the Ghost Forest on Mount Śiṃśapā in the country of Vṛji.

一时，佛在拔祇国尸牧摩罗山鬼林鹿园中。

At that time, the elder Nauryāga went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and after a while, withdrew his seat and said to the World-Honored One: "I am now old and sick, with many worries and afflictions. I only wish that the World-Honored One would instruct me from time to time, so that sentient beings may attain peace and happiness for a long time."

尔时，那忧罗公长者往至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。须臾，退坐白世尊曰：「我今年朽，加复抱病，多诸忧恼，唯愿世尊随时教训，使众生类长夜获安隐。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the elder: "As you say, the body is full of fears and pain, how can it be relied upon? It is only covered by thin skin. Elder, you should know that those who rely on this body can only have momentary happiness; this is the mind of fools, not what the wise value. Therefore, elder, even if the mind is sick, make the mind free from sickness. Thus, elder, you should train in this way."

尔时，世尊告长者曰：「如汝所言，身多畏痛，何可恃怙？但以薄皮而覆其上。长者当知，其有依凭此身者，正可须臾之乐；此是愚心，非智慧者所贵。是故，长者！虽心有病，令心无病。如是，长者！当作是学。」

At that time, the elder, having heard these words, rose from his seat, bowed his head at the World-Honored One's feet, and then left. At that time, the elder further thought: "I should now go to the Venerable Śāriputra and ask about this meaning. Śāriputra is not far from there, sitting under a tree." At that time, Nauryāga went to where Śāriputra was, bowed his head at his feet, and sat to one side.

尔时，长者闻说斯言，从坐起，礼世尊足，便退而去。尔时，长者复作是念：「我今可往至尊者舍利弗所问斯义。舍利弗去彼不远，在树下坐。」是时，那优罗公往至舍利弗所，头面礼足，在一面坐。

At that time, Śāriputra asked the elder: "Your countenance is harmonious and joyful, and your faculties are tranquil. There must be a reason for this. Elder! Did you hear the Dharma from the Buddha?"

是时，舍利弗问长者：「颜貌和悦，诸根寂静，必有所因。长者！故当从佛闻法耶？」

At that time, the elder said to Śāriputra: "How is it, Venerable Śāriputra! How could my countenance not be harmonious and joyful? The reason is that just now the World-Honored One has sprinkled the Dharma of nectar into my heart."

时，长者白舍利弗言：「云何，尊者舍利弗！颜貌焉得不和悦乎？所以然者，向者世尊以甘露之法，溉灌胸怀。」

Śāriputra said: "How is it, elder! The Dharma of nectar has sprinkled your heart."

舍利弗言：「云何，长者！以甘露之法，溉灌胸怀？」

The elder replied: "In this way, Śāriputra! When I went to where the World-Honored One was, I bowed my head at his feet and sat to one side. At that time, I addressed the World-Honored One, saying: 'I am old and decrepit, constantly afflicted with illness, with many sufferings that cannot be described. I only wish that the World-Honored One would explain this body, so that all sentient beings may attain this peace and tranquility!' At that time, the World-Honored One then told me: 'Indeed, elder! This body has many afflictions and sufferings, but it is only covered by thin skin. Elder, you should know that those who rely on this body can only have momentary happiness, not knowing that they will suffer endlessly. Therefore, elder! Although this body has afflictions, one should ensure that the mind is free from afflictions. Thus, elder! One should train in this way.' The World-Honored One thus sprinkled the Dharma of nectar upon me."

长者报言：「于是，舍利弗！我至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，我白世尊曰：『年朽长大，恒抱疾病，多诸苦痛，不可称计，唯愿世尊分别此身，普使众生，获此安隐！』尔时，世尊便告我言：『如是，长者！此身多诸衰苦，但以薄皮而覆其上。长者当知，其有恃怙此身者，正可有斯须之乐，不知长夜受苦无量。是故，长者！此身虽有患，当使心无患。如是，长者！当作是学。』世尊以此甘露之法，而见溉灌。」

Śāriputra said: "How is it, elder! Did you not further ask the Tathāgata about the meaning of this? How does one have afflictions in the body but not in the mind? How does one have illness in the body but not in the mind?"

舍利弗言：「云何，长者！更不重问如来此义乎？云何身有患心无患。云何身有病心无病？」

The elder said to Śāriputra: "Indeed, I did not have the eloquence to ask the World-Honored One again about the meaning of this, that the body has afflictions and the mind has afflictions, and that the body has afflictions but the mind is free from afflictions. Venerable Śāriputra must have this eloquence. Please explain it in detail."

长者白舍利弗言：「实无此辩重问世尊，身有患心有患，身有患心无患。尊者舍利弗必有此辩，愿具分别。」

Śāriputra said: "Listen carefully! Listen carefully! Consider it well, and I shall explain the meaning to you in detail."

舍利弗言：「谛听！谛听！善思念之，吾当与汝广演其义。」

He replied, "Yes, Śāriputra!" and listened to his instructions.

对曰：「如是。舍利弗！」从彼受教。

Śāriputra said to the elder, "In this way, elder, ordinary people do not see the sages, do not receive the teachings of the sages, and do not follow their instructions; they also do not see good friends and do not associate with good friends. They regard form as the self, form as belonging to the self, the self as belonging to form; form is within the self, the self is within form, their form and my form come together in one place, their form and my form gather in one place, form then decays, changes, and does not remain still, and in this they give rise to sorrow, suffering, and distress. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are all regarded as having consciousness, consciousness is within the self, the self is within consciousness, their consciousness and my consciousness come together in one place, their consciousness and my consciousness gather in one place, consciousness then decays, changes, and does not remain still, and in this they give rise to sorrow, suffering, and distress. In this way, elder, the body also has afflictions, and the mind also has afflictions."

舍利弗告长者曰：「于是，长者！凡夫之人不见圣人，不受圣教，不顺其训；亦不见善知识，不与善知识从事。彼计色为我，色是我所，我是色所；色中有我，我中有色，彼色、我色合会一处，彼色、我色以集一处，色便败坏，迁移不停，于中复起愁忧苦恼。痛、想、行、识，皆观我有识，识中有我，我中有识，彼识、我识合会一处。彼识、我识以会一处，识便败坏，迁移不停，于中复起愁忧苦恼。如是，长者！身亦有患，心亦有患。」

The elder asked Śāriputra, "How does the body have afflictions and the mind be free from afflictions?"

长者问舍利弗曰：「云何身有患、心无患耶？」

Śāriputra said, "In this way, elder, the virtuous disciples serve the sages, practice the precepts, and associate with good friends, getting close to good friends. They do not regard form as the self; they do not see form within the self, the self within form; they do not see form as belonging to the self, the self as belonging to form. When form changes and does not remain still, when form changes and shifts, they do not give rise to sorrow, suffering, and distress, or the affliction of sorrow over form. They also do not see feeling, perception, volition, or consciousness, do not see consciousness within the self, the self within consciousness; they do not see consciousness as belonging to the self, the self as belonging to consciousness. Their consciousness and my consciousness come together in one place, consciousness then decays, and in this they do not give rise to sorrow, suffering, and distress. In this way, elder, the body has afflictions, but the mind is free from afflictions. Therefore, elder, you should practice in this way, leaving behind the body and the mind, without attachment. Elder, you should train in this way."

舍利弗言：「于是，长者！贤圣弟子承事圣贤，修行禁法，与善知识从事，亲近善知识。彼亦不观我有色；不见色中有我，我中有色；不见色是我所，我是色所。彼色迁转不住，彼色以移易，不生愁忧、苦恼、忧色之患。亦复不见痛、想、行、识，不见识中有我，我中有识；亦不见识我所，亦不见我所识。彼识、我识以会一处，识便败坏，于中不起愁忧、苦恼。如是，长者！身有患而心无患。是故，长者！当作是习，遗身去心，亦无染著。长者！当作是学。」

At that time, Elder Nāgārjuna, having heard what Śāriputra said, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

尔时，那忧罗公闻舍利弗所说，欢喜奉行。
