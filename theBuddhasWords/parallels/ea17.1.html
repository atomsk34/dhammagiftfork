EĀ 17.1

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

Chapter 17 ◎增壹 阿含经安般品第十七之一

17.1 （一）罗云

Parallel to: MN 62

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One, at the proper time, put on his robe, took his bowl, and entered the city of Śrāvastī with Rāhula to beg for alms. The World-Honored One then turned to the right and said to Rāhula, "You should now observe that form is impermanent.

尔时，世尊到时，著衣持钵，将罗云入舍卫城分卫。尔时，世尊右旋顾谓罗云：「汝今当观色为无常。」

Rāhula replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! Form is impermanent.

罗云对曰：「如是，世尊！色为无常。」

The World-Honored One said, "Rāhula! Feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are all impermanent.

世尊告曰：「罗云！痛、想、行、识皆悉无常。」

Rāhula replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! Feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are all impermanent.

罗云对曰：「如是，世尊！痛、想、行、识皆为无常。」

At that time, Venerable Rāhula also thought, "What is the reason for this? Now I am going to the city to beg for alms, and I am on the road. Why did the World-Honored One face me and instruct me? Now I should return to where I am and should not enter the city to beg for alms."

是时，尊者罗云复作是念：「此有何因缘，今方向城分卫，又在道路，何故世尊而面告诲我？今宜当还归所在，不应入城乞食。」

At that time, Venerable Rāhula immediately returned halfway to the Jetavana Monastery. He took his robes and bowl, went to the foot of a tree, sat with his body and mind upright, crossed his legs, and concentrated his mind. He contemplated that form is impermanent, and he contemplated that feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are impermanent.

尔时，尊者罗云即中道还到祇桓精舍，持衣钵，诣一树下，正身正意，结跏趺坐，专精一心，念色无常，念痛、想、行、识无常。

At that time, the World-Honored One had finished begging for alms in the city of Śrāvastī. After the meal, he was walking in meditation at the Jetavana Monastery. Gradually, he arrived where Rāhula was. Having arrived, he told Rāhula, "You should practice the method of counting the breath. By practicing this method, all thoughts of sorrow and worry will be completely eliminated. You should now also practice the thought of impurity and repulsiveness. All desires and lust will be completely eliminated. You, Rāhula, should now practice the mind of loving-kindness. Having practiced the mind of loving-kindness, all anger and hatred will be completely eliminated. You, Rāhula, should now practice the mind of compassion. Having practiced the mind of compassion, all thoughts of harming will be completely eliminated. You, Rāhula, should now practice the mind of joy. Having practiced the mind of joy, all jealous thoughts will be completely eliminated. You, Rāhula, should now practice the mind of equanimity. Having practiced the mind of equanimity, all arrogant thoughts will be completely eliminated."

尔时，世尊于舍卫城乞食已，食后在祇桓精舍而自经行，渐渐至罗云所。到已，告罗云曰：「汝当修行安般之法，修行此法，所有愁忧之想皆当除尽。汝今复当修行恶露不净想，所有贪欲尽当除灭。汝今，罗云！当修行慈心，已行慈心，所有瞋恚皆当除尽。汝今，罗云！当行悲心，已行悲心，所有害心悉当除尽。汝今，罗云！当行喜心，已行喜心，所有嫉心皆当除尽。汝今，罗云！当行护心，已行护心，所有憍慢悉当除尽。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse to Rāhula:

尔时，世尊向罗云便说此偈：

Do not often give rise to thoughts of attachment, always
follow the Dharma yourself; such a wise person's reputation will
spread. Like holding a torch for others, destroying great darkness
devas and nāgas will respect and serve, respecting and serving
the venerable elders.

「莫数起著想，　　恒当自顺法；如此智之士，　　名称则流布。与人执炬明，　　坏于大暗冥；天龙戴奉敬，　　敬奉师长尊。」

At that time, the bhikṣu Rāhula also replied to the World-Honored One with this verse:

是时，罗云比丘复以此偈报世尊曰：

I do not give rise to thoughts of attachment, always following
the Dharma; such a wise person is able to serve the elders.

「我不起著想，　　恒复顺于法；如此智之士，　　则能奉师长。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having given this instruction, then left and returned to the quiet room.

尔时，世尊作是教勅已，便舍而去，还诣静室。

At that time, Venerable Rahula also thought: "Now, how should I practice mindfulness of breathing to remove sorrow and have no thoughts?" At that time, Rahula immediately rose from his seat and went to where the World-Honored One was. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the feet, sat to one side, and after a while, withdrew his seat and said to the World-Honored One: "How should one practice mindfulness of breathing to remove sorrow and have no thoughts, attain great fruit and reward, and obtain the taste of immortality?"

是时，尊者罗云复作是念：「今云何修行安般，除去愁忧，无有诸想？」是时，罗云即从坐起，便往世尊所。到已，头面礼足，在一面坐。须臾退坐，白世尊曰：「云何修行安般，除去愁忧，无有诸想，获大果报，得甘露味？」

The World-Honored One said: "Excellent! Excellent! Rahula! You are able to roar like a lion in front of the Tathāgata and ask about this meaning: 'How should one practice mindfulness of breathing to remove sorrow and have no thoughts, attain great fruit and reward, and obtain the taste of immortality?' Now, Rahula! Listen well! Listen well! Consider it well. I shall explain it in detail for you."

世尊告曰：「善哉！善哉！罗云！汝乃能于如来前而师子吼，问如此义：『云何修行安般，除去愁忧，无有诸想，获大果报，得甘露味？』汝今，罗云！谛听！谛听！善思念之。吾当为汝具分别说。」

He replied: "Yes, World-Honored One!" At that time, Venerable Rahula received the teaching from the World-Honored One.

对曰：「如是，世尊！」尔时，尊者罗云从世尊受教。

The World-Honored One said: "In this way, Rahula! If a bhikṣu enjoys a quiet place without people, he should sit with his body and mind upright, cross-legged, without other thoughts, focusing on the tip of his nose. He knows when exhaling long, he knows when inhaling long; he knows when exhaling short, he knows when inhaling short; he knows when exhaling cold, he knows when inhaling cold; he knows when exhaling warm, he knows when inhaling warm. He observes the whole body with inhalation and exhalation, knowing them all. Sometimes there is breath, he also knows it is there; sometimes there is no breath, he also knows it is not there. If the breath comes from the heart, he also knows it comes from the heart. If the breath enters the heart, he also knows it enters the heart. In this way, Rahula, one who practices mindfulness of breathing will have no thoughts of sorrow, distress, or confusion, and will attain great fruit and reward, and obtain the taste of immortality."

世尊告曰：「于是，罗云！若有比丘乐于闲静无人之处，便正身正意，结跏趺坐，无他异念，系意鼻头，出息长知息长，入息长亦知息长；出息短亦知息短，入息短亦知息短；出息冷亦知息冷，入息冷亦知息冷；出息暖亦知息暖，入息暖亦知息暖。尽观身体入息、出息，皆悉知之。有时有息亦复知有，又时无息亦复知无。若息从心出亦复知从心出。若息从心入亦复知从心入。如是，罗云，能修行安般者，则无愁忧恼乱之想，获大果报，得甘露味。」

At that time, after the World-Honored One had fully taught Rahula the subtle Dharma, Rahula rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times, and departed. He went to the Andha Grove, sat under a tree, straightened his body and mind, crossed his legs, without any other thoughts, focused on the tip of his nose. He knew when exhaling long, he knew when inhaling long; he knew when exhaling short, he knew when inhaling short; he knew when exhaling cold, he knew when inhaling cold; he knew when exhaling warm, he knew when inhaling warm. He observed the whole body with inhalation and exhalation, knowing them all. Sometimes there was breath, he also knew it was there; sometimes there was no breath, he also knew it was not there. If the breath came from the heart, he also knew it came from the heart. If the breath entered the heart, he also knew it entered the heart.

尔时，世尊具足与罗云说微妙法已，罗云即从坐起，礼佛足，遶三匝而去。往诣安陀园，在一树下，正身正意，结跏趺坐，无他余念，系心鼻头，出息长亦知息长，入息长亦知息长；出息短亦知息短，入息短亦知息短；出息冷亦知息冷，入息冷亦知息冷；出息暖亦知息暖，入息暖亦知息暖。尽观身体入息、出息，皆悉知之。有时有息亦复知有，有时无息亦复知无。若息从心出亦复知从心出。若息从心入亦复知从心入。

At that time, Rahula had this thought, and his lustful mind was immediately liberated, without any more evil. With perception and contemplation, mindfulness, joy, and ease, he dwelled in the first dhyāna. With perception and contemplation ceasing, internally joyful, with single-minded concentration, without perception and contemplation, samādhi, mindfulness, and joy, he dwelled in the second dhyāna. Without further joy and mindfulness, guarding and knowing bodily bliss, the joy and mindfulness sought by the sages, he dwelled in the third dhyāna. The suffering and bliss there ceased, without further sorrow, without suffering or bliss, guarding and mindfulness were pure, he dwelled in the fourth dhyāna.

尔时，罗云作如是思惟，欲心便得解脱，无复众恶。有觉、有观，念持喜安，游于初禅。有觉、有观息，内自欢喜，专其一心，无觉、无观，三昧念喜，游于二禅。无复喜念，自守觉知身乐，诸贤圣常所求护喜念，游于三禅。彼苦乐已灭，无复愁忧，无苦无乐，护念清净，游于四禅。

With this samādhi, his mind was pure and free from defilement, his body was supple, he knew where he came from, remembered his past deeds, and recognized his past lives for countless eons. He also knew one life, two lives, three lives, four lives, five lives, ten lives, twenty lives, thirty lives, forty lives, fifty lives, a hundred lives, a thousand lives, ten thousand lives, hundreds of thousands of lives, eons of formation, eons of destruction, countless eons of formation, countless eons of destruction, billions of incalculable eons, "I was born there, named so-and-so, with such-and-such a surname, eating such-and-such food, experiencing such-and-such suffering and happiness, with such-and-such a lifespan. I died there and was born here, and died here to be born there." With this samādhi, his mind was pure and free from defilement, and he was also free from all afflictions. He also knew the minds of sentient beings. He further used his pure divine eye free from defilement to observe the classes of sentient beings: those who are born and pass away, those with good form and bad form, those in good destinies and bad destinies, those who are good and ugly, and their actions and deeds, he knew them as they truly are.

彼以此三昧，心清净无尘秽，身体柔软，知所从来，忆本所作，自识宿命无数劫事。亦知一生、二生、三生、四生、五生、十生、二十生、三十生、四十生、五十生、百生、千生、万生、数十万生，成劫、败劫，无数成劫、无数败劫，亿载不可计，我曾生彼，名某姓某，食如此食，受如此苦乐，寿命长短，彼终生此，此终生彼。彼以此三昧，心清净无瑕秽，亦无诸结。亦知众生所起之心，彼复以天眼清净无瑕秽，观众生类：生者、逝者，善色、恶色，善趣、恶趣，若好、若丑，所行、所造，如实知之。

Some sentient beings, with evil bodily actions, evil verbal actions, and evil mental actions, slander the sages, practice wrong views, and create the actions of wrong views. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they enter the hells. Some sentient beings, with good bodily actions, good verbal actions, and good mental actions, do not slander the sages, always practice right views, and create the actions of right views. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn in the good destinies of the heavens. This is called the pure divine eye free from defilement, observing the classes of sentient beings: those who are born and pass away, those with good form and bad form, those in good destinies and bad destinies, those who are good and ugly, and their actions and deeds, knowing them as they truly are. He further directs his mind to attain the mind of ending taints. He observes this suffering and truly knows it. He further observes the origin of suffering, also knows the cessation of suffering, and also knows the path to the cessation of suffering, truly knowing it. By observing in this way, his mind is liberated from the taints of desire, existence, and ignorance. Having attained liberation, he attains the knowledge of liberation: "Birth and death are exhausted, the practice of the Brahma way is established, what was to be done is done, and I will not again experience existence." At that time, Venerable Rahula attained arahantship.

或有众生，身行恶、口行恶、意行恶，诽谤贤圣，行邪见，造邪见行，身坏命终，入地狱中。或复众生，身行善、口行善、意行善，不诽谤贤圣，恒行正见、造正见行，身坏命终，生善处天上。是谓天眼清净无瑕秽，观众生类：生者、逝者，善色、恶色，善趣、恶趣，若好、若丑，所行、所造，如实知之。复更施意，成尽漏心，彼观此苦，如实知之。复观苦习，亦知苦尽，亦知苦出要，如实知之。彼以作是观，欲漏心得解脱，有漏、无明漏心得解脱，已得解脱，便得解脱智：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受有，如实知之。是时，尊者罗云便成阿罗汉。

At that time, Venerable Rahula, having attained arahantship, immediately rose from his seat, adjusted his robes, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the World-Honored One: "What I sought has been attained, and all taints are exhausted."

是时，尊者罗云已成罗汉，便从坐起，更整衣服，往至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面住，白世尊曰：「所求已得，诸漏除尽。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Among those who have attained arahantship, none is equal to Rahula. In terms of the exhaustion of taints, he is also Rahula Bhikṣu. In terms of upholding the precepts, he is also Rahula Bhikṣu. The reason is that the past Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Perfectly Enlightened Ones also had this Rahula Bhikṣu. If we speak of the Buddha's son, he is also Rahula Bhikṣu, born directly from the Buddha, the foremost in the Dharma."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「诸得阿罗汉者，无有与罗云等也。论有漏尽，亦是罗云比丘。论持禁戒者，亦是罗云比丘。所以然者，诸过去如来、等正觉，亦有此罗云比丘。欲言佛子，亦是罗云比丘，亲从佛生，法之上者。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Among my śrāvakas, the foremost disciple who can uphold the precepts is Rahula Bhikṣu."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我声闻中第一弟子能持禁戒，所谓罗云比丘是。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

Endowed with the Dharma of precepts, the faculties are also
accomplished; gradually one will attain the exhaustion of all afflictions.

「具足禁戒法，　　诸根亦成就；渐渐当逮得，　　一切结使尽。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
