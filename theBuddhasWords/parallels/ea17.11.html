EĀ 17.11

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

17.11 （一一）

Parallel to: AN 4.70 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Karanda Bamboo Grove in the city of Rajagriha, together with a group of five hundred great bhikshus.

一时，佛在罗阅城迦兰陀竹园所，与大比丘五百人俱。

At that time, the evil person Devadatta went to Prince Ajatasatru and said, "In the past, the lifespan of the people was extremely long, but now the lifespan of people is no more than a hundred years. Prince, you should know that human life is impermanent. If you do not ascend the throne and die in the middle of your life, wouldn't that be painful? Prince, it is time to end the life of your father, the king, and rule over the people. I will now kill the śramaṇa Gautama! Becoming the unsurpassed Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, with a new king and a new Buddha in the realm of Magadha, wouldn't that be wonderful? Like the sun piercing the clouds, illuminating everywhere, like the moon shining among the stars when the clouds have cleared." At that time, Prince Ajatasatru imprisoned his father, the king, in an iron prison, and established his own ministers to rule over the people.

尔时，提婆达兜恶人便往至婆罗留支王子所，告王子言：「昔者，民氓寿命极长，如今人寿不过百年。王子当知，人命无常，备不登位，中命终者不亦痛哉！王子，时可断父王命，统领国人。我今当杀沙门瞿昙！作无上至真、等正觉，于摩竭国界，新王、新佛，不亦快哉！如日贯云，靡所不照，如月云消，众星中明。」尔时，婆罗留支王子即收父王，著铁牢中，更立臣佐，统领人民。

At that time, many bhikṣus entered the city of Rajagriha to beg for alms. They heard that Devadatta had instructed Prince Ajatasatru to imprison his father, the king, and establish his own ministers. At that time, the many bhikṣus returned to their places after begging for alms, put away their robes and alms bowls, and went to where the World-Honored One was. They bowed their heads to his feet and said to the World-Honored One, "This morning, while begging for alms in the city, we heard that the foolish Devadatta instructed Prince Ajatasatru to imprison his father, the king, in a prison, and establish his own ministers. He also instructed the prince, saying, 'You kill your father, the king, and I will harm the Tathāgata. Wouldn't it be wonderful to have a new king and a new Buddha in the realm of Magadha?'"

尔时，有众多比丘入罗阅城乞食，便闻提婆达兜教王子收父王，著铁牢中，更立臣佐。是时，众多比丘乞食，还归所在，摄举衣钵，往至世尊所。头面礼足，白世尊曰：「朝入城乞食，闻提婆达兜愚人教王子使收父王，闭著牢狱，更立臣佐。复勅王子言：『汝杀父王，我害如来，于此摩竭国界，新王、新佛，不亦快哉！』」

At that time, the ministers also practiced unrighteousness; when the ministers practiced unrighteousness, then the prince also practiced unrighteousness; when the prince practiced unrighteousness, then the ministers and officials also practiced unrighteousness; when the ministers and officials practiced unrighteousness, then the people of the country also practiced unrighteousness; when the people of the country practiced unrighteousness, then the soldiers and horses of the people also practiced unrighteousness; when the soldiers practiced unrighteousness, then the sun and moon would be in disorder, losing their proper course; when the sun and moon lost their proper course, then there would be no years; when there were no years, the sun and moon would be in error, losing their brightness; when the sun and moon lost their brightness, then the stars would appear strange; when the stars appeared strange, then violent winds would arise; when violent winds arose, the spirits would become angry; when the spirits became angry, then the wind and rain would be untimely. At that time, the grains in the ground would not grow, and the colors of the people, insects, and crawling creatures would change, and their lifespans would be extremely short.

「尔时，臣佐亦行非法；臣佐已行非法，尔时王太子亦行非法；太子已行非法，尔时群臣、长吏亦行非法；群臣、长吏已行非法，尔时国界人民亦行非法；国界人民已行非法，尔时人众兵马亦行非法；兵众已行非法，尔时日月倒错运度失时；日月已失时，便无年岁；已无年岁，日差月错，无复精光；日月已无精光，尔时星宿现怪。星宿已现变怪，便有暴风起；已有暴风起，神祇瞋恚；神祇已瞋恚，尔时风雨不时。尔时谷子在地者便不长大，人民之类、蜎飞蠕动，颜色改变，寿命极短。

If at some time the king's law is properly administered, then the ministers also practice righteousness; when the ministers practice righteousness, then the prince also practices righteousness; when the prince practices righteousness, then the officials also practice righteousness; when the officials practice righteousness, then the people of the country also practice righteousness; the sun and moon follow their proper course, the wind and rain are timely, calamities do not appear, the spirits are joyful, and the five grains flourish; the ruler and ministers are harmonious and view each other as brothers, without any increase or decrease; the colored forms of all beings are lustrous, their food is digested by itself, there are no calamities, and their lifespans are extremely long, loved and respected by all.

「若复有时，王法治正，尔时群臣亦行正法；群臣已行正法，时王太子亦行正法；王太子已行正法，尔时长吏亦行正法；长吏已行正法，国界人民亦行正法；日月顺常，风雨以时，灾怪不现，神祇欢喜，五谷炽盛；君臣和穆相视，如兄如弟，终无增损；有形之类，颜色光润，食自消化，无有灾害，寿命极长，人所爱敬。」

At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

Just as when a herd of cattle crosses a river, if the leader
is not straight, all will follow and be crooked. This is because the
leader is crooked. Beings are also like this. In a group, there must
be a leader. If the leader practices unrighteousness, how much more so the
lower and lesser people? The herd will all suffer because of the king's
unrighteous law. Knowing the practice of unrighteousness, all the people will be the
same. Just as when a herd of cattle crosses a river, if the
leader is straight, all will follow and be straight. This is because the
leader is straight. Beings are also like this. In a group, there must
be a leader. If the leader practices the right Dharma, how much more
so the lower and lesser people? The herd will all enjoy happiness because
of the king's righteous law. Knowing the practice of the right Dharma, all
the people will be the same.

「犹如牛渡水，　　导者而不正；一切皆不正，　　斯由本导故。众生亦如是，　　众中必有导；导者行非法，　　况复下细人。萠类尽受苦，　　由王法不正；以知非法行，　　一切民亦然。犹如牛渡水，　　导者而行正；从者亦皆正，　　斯由本导故。众生亦如是，　　众中必有导；导者行正法，　　况复下庶人。萠类尽受乐，　　由王法教正；以知正法行，　　一切民亦然] cf.【丽】。

"Therefore, bhikṣus, you should abandon unrighteousness and practice the right Dharma. Thus, bhikṣus, you should train in this way."

「是故，诸比丘！当舍非法而行正法。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
