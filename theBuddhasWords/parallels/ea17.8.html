EĀ 17.8

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

17.8 （八）

Parallel to: AN 5.31 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the Brāhman Jīvaka went to where the World-Honored One was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. Then the Brāhman Jīvaka said to the World-Honored One, "How should one view an evil friend?"

尔时，生漏婆罗门便往至世尊所，共相问讯，在一面坐。是时，生漏婆罗门白世尊曰：「当云何观恶知识人？」

The World-Honored One said, "One should view them as one views the moon."

世尊告曰：「当观如观月。」

The Brāhman said, "How should one view a good friend?"

婆罗门曰：「当云何观善知识？」

The World-Honored One said, "One should view them as one views the moon."

世尊告曰：「当观如观月。」

The Brāhman said, "What the Śramaṇa Gautama has just said is a brief statement of the essentials, but I do not understand the extensive meaning. I only wish that Gautama would explain the meaning extensively, so that those who do not understand may understand."

婆罗门曰：「沙门瞿昙今所说者，略说其要，未解广义。唯愿瞿昙广普说义，使未解者解。」

The World-Honored One said, "Brāhman! Listen carefully, listen carefully, and consider it well. I shall explain the meaning extensively for you."

世尊告曰：「婆罗门！谛听！谛听！善思念之，吾当与汝广演其义。」

The Brāhman replied, "Yes, Gautama!" The Brāhman Sañjaya accepted the Buddha's instruction.

婆罗门对曰：「如是，瞿昙！」生漏婆罗门从佛受教。

The World-Honored One said, "Just as, Brāhman, at the end of the month, the day and night revolve, only diminishing, not increasing. Because of the decrease, sometimes the moon does not appear, and no one sees it. It is the same, Brāhman, if one has bad friends, who spend day and night gradually losing faith, precepts, learning, generosity, and wisdom. Because of lacking faith, precepts, learning, generosity, and wisdom, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they enter hell. Therefore, Brāhman, I now say that bad friends are like the moon at the end of the month. Just as, Brāhman, when the new moon first appears, as it passes through the days and nights, its light gradually increases, gradually becoming full, and on the fifteenth day it is fully full, and all beings see it. It is the same, Brāhman, if one has good friends, who spend day and night increasing faith, precepts, learning, generosity, and wisdom. Because of increasing faith, precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn in the good places of the heavens. Therefore, Brāhman, I now say that the destination of good friends is like the full moon."

世尊告曰：「犹如，婆罗门！月末之月，昼夜周旋，但有其损，未有其盈。彼以减损，或复有时而月不现，无有见者。此亦如是，婆罗门！若恶知识，经历昼夜，渐无有信，无有戒，无有闻，无有施，无有智慧，彼以无有信、戒、闻、施、智慧，是时彼恶知识身坏命终，入地狱中。是故，婆罗门！我今说是恶知识者，犹如月末之月。犹如，婆罗门！月初生时，随所经过日夜，光明渐增，稍稍盛满，便于十五日具足盛满，一切众生靡不见者。如是，婆罗门！若善知识，经历日夜，增益信、戒、闻、施、智慧。彼以增益信、戒、施、闻、智慧，尔时善知识身坏命终，生天上善处。是故，婆罗门！我今说此善知识所趣，犹月盛满。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

If a person has not exhausted greed, anger, and
delusion, their goodness gradually diminishes, like the waning moon
If a person is free from greed, anger, and
delusion, their goodness gradually increases, like the waxing moon.

「若人有贪欲，　　瞋恚痴不尽；于善渐有减，　　犹如月向尽。若人无贪欲，　　瞋恚痴亦尽；于善渐有增，　　犹如月盛满。

Therefore, brahmin! You should learn like the waxing moon.

「是故，婆罗门！当学如月初。」

At that time, the brahmin Jīvaka said to the World-Honored One: "Excellent! Gautama! It is like one who is bent being straightened, one who is in darkness seeing light, and one who is lost finding the way in the darkness. It is like this. The Śramaṇa Gautama has explained the Dharma to me with countless skillful means. I now take refuge in the World-Honored One, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha. From now on, please allow me to be an upāsaka, and for as long as I live, I will refrain from taking life."

尔时，生漏婆罗门白世尊曰：「善哉！瞿昙！犹如屈者得伸，冥者见明，迷者见路，于暗冥然明。此亦如是，沙门瞿昙无数方便为我说法，我今自归世尊及法、众僧，自今以往，听我为优婆塞，尽形寿不杀生。」

At that time, Jīvaka heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，生漏闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
