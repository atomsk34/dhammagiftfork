EĀ 21.6

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

21.6 （六）三夜

Parallel to: AN 3.16

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If a bhikṣu accomplishes three dharmas, he will attain happiness in this very life, be diligent and energetic, and exhaust the taints. What are the three? Here, bhikṣus, the faculties are tranquil, eating and drinking are moderate, and walking meditation is not lost.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有比丘成就三法，于现法中善得快乐，勇猛精进，得尽有漏。云何为三？于是，比丘！诸根寂静，饮食知节，不失经行。

What is meant by a bhikṣu's tranquil faculties? Here, bhikṣus, if the eyes see forms, they do not give rise to attachment or thoughts, and there is no recollection. The eye faculty becomes pure, seeking liberation from it, always guarding the eye faculty. If the ears hear sounds, the nose smells fragrances, the tongue knows tastes, the body knows tactile sensations, and the mind knows dharmas, they do not give rise to attachment or thoughts, and there is no recollection. The mind faculty becomes pure, seeking liberation from it, always guarding the mind faculty. Thus, bhikṣus, the faculties are tranquil.

「云何比丘诸根寂静？于是，比丘若眼见色，不起想著，无有识念，于眼根而得清净，因彼求于解脱，恒护眼根。若耳闻声，鼻嗅香，舌知味，身知细滑，意知法，不起想著，无有识念，于意根而得清净，因彼求于解脱，恒护意根。如是，比丘诸根寂静。

What is meant by a bhikṣu's moderate eating and drinking? Here, bhikṣus, a bhikṣu contemplates the source of food and drink, not seeking to be fat or white, but to sustain the body and complete the four great elements. I should now remove old pain and prevent new pain from arising, so that the body has strength to practice the path and not let the holy life be interrupted. It is like a man or woman with a bad sore on the body, using fat ointment to treat the sore. The reason for treating the sore is to make it heal quickly. It is the same here. Bhikṣus, a bhikṣu's moderate eating and drinking. Here, a bhikṣu contemplates the source of food and drink, not seeking to be fat or white, but to sustain the body and complete the four great elements. I should now remove old pain and prevent new pain from arising, so that the body has strength to practice the path and not let the holy life be interrupted. It is like a heavily loaded cart. The reason for greasing the axle is to reach the destination with the heavy load. The same is true for bhikṣus. A bhikṣu's moderate eating and drinking. He contemplates the source of food and drink, not seeking to be fat or white, but to sustain the body and complete the four great elements. I should now remove old pain and prevent new pain from arising, so that the body has strength to practice the path and not let the holy life be interrupted. Thus, a bhikṣu's moderate eating and drinking.

「云何比丘饮食知节？于是，比丘思惟饮食所从来处，不求肥白，趣欲支形，得全四大。我今当除故痛，使新者不生，令身有力，得修行道，使梵行不绝。犹如男女身生恶疮，或用脂膏涂疮。所以涂疮者，欲使时愈故，此亦如是。诸比丘！饮食知节，于是，比丘思惟饭食所从来处，不求肥白，趣欲支形，得全四大，我今当除故痛，使新者不生，令身有力，得修行道，使梵行不绝。犹如重载之车所以膏毂者，欲致重有所至。比丘亦如是，饮食知节，思惟所从来处，不求肥白，趣欲支形，得全四大，我今当除故痛，使新者不生，令身有力，得修行道，使梵行不绝。如是，比丘饮食知节。

What is meant by a bhikṣu's not losing his walking meditation? Here, bhikṣus, a bhikṣu in the early and late watches always thinks of walking meditation, not missing the time, always contemplating and focusing his mind on the factors of the path. Whether during the day, walking or sitting, he contemplates the subtle Dharma, removing the hindrances. Again, in the first watch of the night, whether walking or sitting, he contemplates the subtle Dharma, removing the hindrances. Again, in the middle watch of the night, he lies on his right side, contemplating and focusing his mind on the bright. Again, in the last watch of the night, he gets up, walking and contemplating the profound Dharma, removing the hindrances. Thus, a bhikṣu does not lose his walking meditation.

「云何比丘不失经行？于是，比丘前夜、后夜，恒念经行，不失时节，常念系意在道品之中。若在昼日，若行、若坐，思惟妙法，除去阴盖。复于初夜，若行、若坐，思惟妙法，除去阴盖。复于中夜，右脇卧，思惟系意在明。彼复于后夜起，行思惟深法，除去阴盖。如是，比丘不失经行。

If a bhikṣu has his faculties tranquil, eats and drinks moderately, does not lose his walking meditation, and always contemplates and focuses his mind on the factors of the path, then that bhikṣu will attain two fruits, becoming a non-returner in this very life. It is like a skilled charioteer on a level road driving a four-horse chariot without any obstruction, wherever he wants to go he will surely arrive without doubt. The same is true for this bhikṣu. If his faculties are tranquil, he eats and drinks moderately, does not lose his walking meditation, and always contemplates and focuses his mind on the factors of the path, then that bhikṣu will attain two fruits, extinguishing the taints and becoming a non-returner in this very life.

「若有比丘诸根寂静，饮食知节，不失经行，常念系意在道品之中，此比丘便成二果，于现法中得阿那含。犹如善御之士，在平正道中，御四马之车，无有凝滞，所欲到处，必果不疑。此比丘亦复如是，若诸根寂静，饮食知节，不失经行，常念系意在道品之中，此比丘便成二果，于现法中漏尽，得阿那含。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
