EĀ 23.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

23.4 （四）婆提

Parallel to: SN 3.19 / SĀ 1232 / SĀ² 2.59

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the elder Bhāti in Śrāvastī became ill and passed away. The elder had no children, and all his wealth and treasures were confiscated by the state. At that time, King Prasenajit, with dust on his body, came to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

尔时，舍卫城中婆提长者遇病命终，然彼长者无有子息，所有财宝尽没入官。尔时，王波斯匿尘土坌身来至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One asked the king, "Great King! Why have you come to me with dust on your body?"

是时，世尊问王曰：「大王！何故尘土坌身来至我所？」

King Prasenajit said to the World-Honored One, "In this Śrāvastī city, there was an elder named Bhāti who passed away today. He had no children, and his wealth and treasures were confiscated by the state. There were 80,000 catties of pure gold alone, not to mention other miscellaneous items. While he was alive, the elder ate such coarse food and did not eat fine food. The clothes he wore were dirty and impure, and the carriage and mounts he rode were extremely weak."

波斯匿王白世尊曰：「此舍卫城内有长者名婆提，今日命终，彼无子姓，躬往收摄财宝，理使入官。纯金八万斤，况复余杂物乎！然彼长者存在之日，所食如此之食，极为弊恶，不食精细，所著衣服垢坌不净，所乘车骑极为瘦弱。」

The World-Honored One said, "Indeed, Great King! As you have said, 'A stingy person, having obtained this wealth, cannot eat or drink it, and does not give it to parents, wife, children, servants, slaves, friends, acquaintances, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, or elders. If a wise person obtains this wealth, they can give it away widely, not cherishing anything, and provide for śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and those of high virtue.'"

世尊告曰：「如是，大王！如王来言：『夫悭贪之人得此财货，不能食噉，不与父母、妻子、仆从、奴婢，亦复不与朋友、知识，亦复不与沙门、婆罗门、诸尊长者。若有智之士得此财宝，便能惠施广济，一切无所爱惜，供给沙门、婆罗门、诸高德者。』」

King Prasenajit said, "Where was Elder Bhāti reborn after his death?"

时，王波斯匿说曰：「此婆提长者命终，为生何处？」

The World-Honored One said, "Elder Bhāti was reborn in the great hell of weeping after his death. Because those who sever the roots of goodness are reborn in the weeping hell when their bodies perish and their lives end."

世尊告曰：「此婆提长者命终，生涕哭大地狱中。所以然者，此断善根之人，身坏命终，生涕哭地狱中。」

King Prasenajit said, "Did Elder Bhāti sever the roots of goodness?"

波斯匿王曰：「婆提长者断善根耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Yes, great king! As you have said, that elder severed the roots of goodness. However, his past merits have been exhausted and he has not created any new ones."

世尊告曰：「如是。大王！如王所说，彼长者断于善根，然彼长者故福已尽，更不造新。」

King Prasenajit said, "Does that elder have any remaining merits?"

王波斯匿曰：「彼长者颇有遗余福乎？」

The World-Honored One said, "No, great king! There is not even a tiny bit of remainder. It is like a farmer who only harvests and does not plant. Later, he becomes impoverished and gradually dies. The reason is that he only consumes past karma and does not create new karma. This elder is also like this. He only consumes past merits and does not create new merits. This elder will be in the weeping hell tonight."

世尊告曰：「无也，大王！乃无毫厘之余有存在者，如彼田家公，但收不种，后便穷困，渐以命终。所以然者，但食故业，更不造新。此长者亦复如是，但食故福，更不造新福，此长者今夜当在涕哭地狱中。」

At that time, King Prasenajit was filled with fear. He wiped away his tears and said, "What merits and virtues did this elder create in the past to be born in a wealthy family? What unwholesome roots did he create that he could not enjoy the wealth of this extreme wealth and not enjoy the five pleasures?"

尔时，波斯匿王便怀恐怖，收泪而曰：「此长者昔日作何功德福业生在富家？复作何不善根本，不得食此极富之货，不乐五乐之中？」

At that time, the World-Honored One told King Prasenajit, "Long ago, during the time of Buddha Kasyapa, this elder was a farmer's son in this city of Sravasti. At that time, after the Buddha passed away, a pratyekabuddha appeared in the world and went to this elder's house. At that time, this elder saw the pratyekabuddha outside the door. Having seen him, he immediately thought, 'It is very difficult for such a venerable one to appear in the world. I should now offer him food.' At that time, the elder then offered food to the pratyekabuddha. Having received the food, the pratyekabuddha then flew up into the sky and left. At that time, the elder saw the pratyekabuddha perform the supernormal power and made this vow, 'With this root of goodness, may I not fall into the three evil destinies in all my future lives, and may I always have abundant wealth.' Later, he had a regretful thought, 'The food I had should have been given to the servants, not given to this bald-headed monk to eat.'

尔时，世尊告波斯匿王曰：「过去久远迦叶佛时，此长者在此舍卫城中为田家子。尔时，佛去世后，有辟支佛出世，往诣此长者家。尔时，此长者见辟支佛在门外，见已，便生是念：『如此尊者出世甚难，我今可以饮食往施此人。』尔时，长者便施彼辟支佛食，辟支佛得食已，便飞在虚空而去。时，彼长者见辟支佛作神足，作是誓愿：『持此善本之愿，使世世所生之处，不堕三恶趣，常多财宝。』后有悔心：『我向所有食，应与奴仆，不与此秃头道人使食。』

"At that time, was the farmer's son elder someone else? Do not view it like that. The reason is this: the farmer's son elder at that time is none other than this elder Bapati. Having made the offering, he made this vow, holding this merit, not falling into the evil destinies in all his future lives, always having abundant wealth and treasures, being born in a wealthy and noble family, without any lack. Having made the offering, he later gave rise to a regretful thought, 'I should have given the food to the servants, not given it to this bald-headed monk to eat.' Because of this cause and condition, he could not enjoy this extremely wealthy wealth, and he also did not enjoy the five pleasures. He did not provide for himself, nor did he give to his parents, brothers, wife, children, servants, friends, acquaintances, or to the shramanas, Brahmins, and elders. He only ate the old karma and did not create new karma. Therefore, great king! If there are wise people who obtain this wealth, they should widely give it away without being stingy, and they will also obtain inexhaustible wealth. Thus, great king! You should learn in this way."

「尔时，田家长者岂异人乎？莫作是观。所以然者，尔时田家长者，今此婆提长者是。是时施已，发此誓愿，持此功德，所生之处不堕恶趣，恒多财饶宝，生富贵之家，无所渴乏。既复施已，后生悔心：『我宁与奴仆使食，不与此秃头道人使食。』以此因缘本末，不得食此极有之货，亦复不乐五乐之中，不自供养；复不与父母、兄弟、妻子、仆从、朋友、知识，不施沙门、婆罗门、诸尊长者，但食故业，不造新者。是故，大王！若有智之士得此财货，当广布施，莫有所惜，复当得无极之财。如是，大王！当作是学。」

At that time, King Prasenajit said to the World-Honored One, "From now on, I will widely give to the shramanas, Brahmins, and the four groups of followers. If any non-Buddhist practitioners come to beg, I will not be able to give to them."

尔时，波斯匿王白世尊曰：「自今以后，当广布施沙门、婆罗门四部之众，诸外道、异学来乞求者，我不堪与。」

The World-Honored One said, "Great King! Do not think like that. The reason is this: all sentient beings depend on food to survive. Without food, they will perish."

世尊告曰：「大王！莫作是念。所以然者，一切众生皆由食得存，无食便丧。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

Always remember to give widely, never cut off the mind of giving. You
will surely encounter the sages and cross over the source of birth and death.

「念当广惠施，　　终莫断施心，必当值贤圣，　　度此生死源。」

At that time, King Prasenajit said to the World-Honored One, "I am now even more joyful towards the Tathāgata. Because all sentient beings depend on food to survive, without food they cannot survive." At that time, King Prasenajit said, "From now on, I shall widely give without being stingy."

尔时，波斯匿王白世尊曰：「我今倍复欢庆向] cf.【丽】于 如来，所以然者，一切众生皆由食得存，无食不存。」尔时，波斯匿王曰：「自今以后，当广惠施，无所悋惜。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the subtle Dharma to the king. The king then rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and left.

是时，世尊与王说微妙之法。时，王即从坐起，头面礼足，便退而去。

At that time, King Prasenajit, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，王波斯匿闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
