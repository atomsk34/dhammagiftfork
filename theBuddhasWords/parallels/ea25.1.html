EĀ 25.1

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

Chapter 25 四谛品第二十五

25.1 （一）谛

Parallel to: SN 56.21 / SĀ 403

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "You should practice the Dharma of the Four Noble Truths. What are the four? Namely, the first is the truth of suffering, whose meaning is inexhaustible and whose meaning is boundless, and the Dharma is spoken without end. The second is the truth of the origin of suffering, whose meaning is inexhaustible and whose meaning is boundless, and the Dharma is spoken without end. The third is the truth of the cessation of suffering, whose meaning is inexhaustible and whose meaning is boundless, and the Dharma is spoken without end. The fourth is the truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering, whose meaning is inexhaustible and whose meaning is boundless, and the Dharma is spoken without end.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「当修行四谛之法。云何为四？所谓初苦谛，义不可尽，义不可穷，说法无尽。第二者苦习谛，义不可尽，义不可穷，说法无尽。第三者苦尽谛，义不可尽，义不可穷，说法无尽。第四者苦出要谛，义不可尽，义不可穷，说法无尽。

What is meant by the truth of suffering? The truth of suffering refers to the suffering of birth, the suffering of aging, the suffering of sickness, the suffering of death, the suffering of sorrow, grief, and distress, the suffering of being with what is disliked, the suffering of separation from what is loved, the suffering of not getting what one desires, and in brief, the suffering of the five aggregates. This is called the truth of suffering.

「彼云何名为苦谛？所谓苦谛者，生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、忧悲恼苦、怨憎会苦、恩爱别离苦、所欲不得苦，取要言之，五盛阴苦，是谓名为苦谛。

What is meant by the truth of the origin of suffering? The truth of the origin of suffering refers to craving and desire, the mind is always attached to it, this is called the truth of the origin of suffering.

「彼云何名为苦习谛？所谓习谛者，爱与欲相应，心恒染著，是谓名为苦习谛。

What is meant by the truth of the cessation of suffering? The truth of the cessation of suffering refers to the complete and permanent cessation of desire and craving, without further creation, this is called the truth of the cessation of suffering.

「彼云何名为苦尽谛？所谓尽谛者，欲爱永尽无余，不复更造，是谓名为苦尽谛。

What is meant by the truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering? The truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering refers to the eightfold noble path, namely right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration, this is called the truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering.

「彼云何名为苦出要谛？所谓苦出要谛者，谓贤圣八品道，所谓正见、正治、正语、正行、正命、正方便、正念、正三昧，是谓名为苦出要谛。

"Thus, bhikkhus, these four truths exist, they are real and not false, they are spoken by the World-Honored One, therefore they are called truths. Among all beings, whether two-legged, three-legged, or four-legged, those with desire, those with form, those without form, those with perception, or those without perception, the Tathāgata is supreme. Because he has realized these four truths, they are called the four truths. This is what is meant by the existence of these four truths. Yet, not realizing them, one remains long in saṃsāra, transmigrating through the five paths. I have now realized these four truths, and have gone from this shore to the other shore. I have accomplished this meaning, cut off the root of saṃsāra, and will no longer be reborn. I know this as it truly is."

「如是，比丘！有此四谛，实有不虚，世尊之所说，故名为谛。诸有众生，二足、三足、四足，欲者、色者、无色者，有想、无想者，如来最上。然成此四谛，故名为四谛。是谓，比丘！有此四谛。然不觉知，长处生死，轮转五道。我今以得此四谛，从此岸至彼岸，成就此义，断生死根本，更不复受有，如实知之。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

Now there is the Dharma of the Four Noble Truths, yet if one
does not truly know them, one will revolve in saṃsāra and never attain
liberation. Now that I have realized the Four Noble Truths, I have awakened
to and understood them, cut off the root of saṃsāra, and will no
longer be reborn.

「今有四谛法，　　如实而不知，轮转生死中，　　终不有解脱。如今有四谛，　　以觉以晓了，以断生死根，　　更亦不受有。

"If the fourfold assembly does not attain this truth, does not awaken to and understand it, they will follow the five paths. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should make efforts to realize these four truths. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「若有四部之众，不得此谛，不觉不知，便随五道。是故，诸比丘！当作方便，成此四谛。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
