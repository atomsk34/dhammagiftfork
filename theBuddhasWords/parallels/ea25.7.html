EĀ 25.7

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

25.7 （七）四果

Parallel to: AN 4.105

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are these four fruits. What are the four? Some fruits are born but look ripe, some fruits are ripe but look unripe, some fruits are ripe and look ripe, some fruits are born and look unripe. These are called the four fruits in the world. There are also four kinds of people in the world who are like this. What are the four? Some people are ripe but look unripe, some people are unripe but look ripe, some people are unripe and look unripe, some people are ripe and look ripe.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有此四果。云何为四？或有果生而似熟，或有果熟而似生，或有果熟而似熟，或有果生而似生。是谓，比丘！世间有此四果。世间有此四人，亦复如是。云何为四？或有人熟而像生，或有人生而像熟，或有人生而似生，或有人熟而似熟。

What kind of person is born but looks ripe? Some people walk and move without haste, their eyes always follow the teachings, they put on their robes and hold their alms bowl also following the proper way of walking, looking at the ground without glancing left or right. Yet they break the precepts and do not follow the right practice, they are not true śramaṇas but look like śramaṇas, they do not practice the holy life but claim to practice the holy life, they completely destroy the true Dharma, they are like rotten seeds. This is called a person who is born but looks ripe.

「何等人生而似熟？或有人往来行步不行卒暴，眼目视瞻恒随法教，著衣持钵亦复随法行步，视地不左右顾望；然复犯戒不随正行，实非沙门而似沙门，不行梵行而自言行梵行，尽坏败正法，根败之种，是谓此人生而像熟。

What kind of person is ripe but looks unripe? Some bhikṣus have a loose nature, their eyes do not look proper, they do not follow the proper way of walking, they like to glance left and right. Yet they are diligent, learned, practice good dharmas, always uphold the precepts and do not lose their dignified deportment, they are afraid of even a little unwholesome dharma. This is called a person who is ripe but looks unripe.

「彼人云何熟而像生？或有比丘性行似踈，视瞻不端，亦不随法行，喜左右顾视；然复精进多闻，修行善法，恒持戒律，不失威仪，见少非法，便怀恐惧，是谓此人熟而像生。

What kind of person is born and looks unripe? Some bhikṣus do not uphold the precepts, they do not know how to walk properly, they do not know how to enter or leave, they do not know how to put on robes or hold alms bowls, their faculties are confused, their minds are attached to forms, sounds, smells, tastes, and tactile objects. They break the precepts, do not practice the true Dharma, they are not true śramaṇas but look like śramaṇas, they do not practice the holy life but look like they practice the holy life, they are people with rotten faculties, who cannot be cultivated. This is called a person who is born and looks unripe.

「彼云何人生而像生？或有比丘不持禁戒，不知行步礼节，亦复不知出入行来，亦复不知著衣持钵，诸根错乱，心著色、声、香、味、细滑之法，彼犯禁戒，不行正法，不是沙门而似沙门，不行梵行而似梵行，根败之人，不可修饰，是谓此人生而似生。

What kind of person is ripe and looks ripe? Some bhikṣus uphold the precepts and limits, their entering and leaving, walking and standing do not lose the proper time, their looking does not lose dignified deportment; yet they are extremely diligent, practicing good dharmas, their dignified deportment and proper conduct are all accomplished, they are afraid of even a little unwholesome dharma, let alone a great one. This is called a person who is ripe and looks ripe.

「彼云何有人熟而似熟？或有比丘持戒禁限，出入行步不失时节，看视不失威仪；然极精进，修行善法，威仪礼节皆悉成就，见小非法，便怀恐怖，况复大者，是谓此人熟而似熟。

"This is called, bhikṣus! there are these four fruitional people in the world, one should learn to be like the ripe fruited person. Thus, bhikṣus! one should train in this way."

「是谓，比丘！世间有此四果之人，当学熟果之人。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
