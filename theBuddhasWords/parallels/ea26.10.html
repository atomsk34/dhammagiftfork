EĀ 26.10

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

26.10 （一〇）婆迦梨

Parallel to: SN 22.87 / SĀ 1265

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Vakkali was seriously ill, lying in his own excrement and urine, wanting to kill himself with a knife, but unable to sit up on his own. Then Venerable Vakkali said to his attendant, "Bring me a knife now, I want to kill myself. The reason is that among the disciples of the Buddha Śākyamuni, there is none who surpasses me in faith and liberation, yet my mind is not liberated from defilements. The reason is that even the disciples of the Tathāgata, when encountering suffering and distress, also seek a knife to kill themselves. What use is this life of mine now? I cannot go from this shore to the other shore."

尔时，尊者婆迦梨身得重患，卧在大小便上，意欲自刀杀，无此势可自坐起。是时，尊者婆迦梨告侍者：「汝今可持刀来，吾欲自杀。所以然者，如今日释迦文佛弟子之中，信解脱者无出我上，然我今日有漏心不解脱。所以然者，然如来弟子遇苦恼时，亦复求刀自杀。我今用此命为？不能从此岸至彼岸。」

At that time, Vakkali's disciple had just gone forth and did not know about this world or the next world, did not know how to go from this shore to the other shore, and did not know about dying here and being born there. He then handed the knife to him. When Vakkali took the knife, he stabbed himself with it out of firm faith.

是时，婆迦梨弟子出家未久，未知今世、后世，不知从此岸至彼岸，亦复不知死此生彼，便授刀与之。时，婆迦梨手执刀已，以信坚固，持刀自刺。

At that time, Vakkali stabbed himself with the knife and thought, "Among the disciples of the Buddha Śākyamuni, those who do what is unlawful, obtain evil benefits and not good benefits, do not attain realization in the Tathāgata's teachings and then take their own lives." At that time, Venerable Vakkali then contemplated these five aggregates of clinging: This is form, this is the origin of form, this is the cessation of form; This is feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness, this is the origin of feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness, this is the cessation of feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. Having thoroughly contemplated these five aggregates of clinging, he knew that all phenomena that arise are subject to cessation. Having understood this, he then attained liberation from the mind with taints. At that time, Venerable Vakkali attained parinirvāṇa in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder.

是时，婆迦梨以刀自刺，而作是念：「释迦文佛弟子之中，所作非法，得恶利不得善利，于如来法中，不得受证而取命终。」是时，尊者婆迦梨便思惟是五盛阴：是谓此色，是谓色习，是谓色灭尽；是谓痛、想、行、识，是谓痛、想、行、识集，是谓痛、想、行、识、灭尽。彼于此五盛阴熟思惟之，诸有生法皆是死法。知此已，便于有漏心得解脱。尔时，尊者婆迦梨于无余涅槃界而般涅槃。

At that time, the World-Honored One used his divine ear to hear Venerable Vakkali seeking a knife to kill himself. At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "All the bhikṣus in Śrāvastī should gather in one place. I wish to give instructions."

尔时，世尊以天耳听闻尊者婆迦梨求刀自杀。尔时，世尊告阿难：「诸比丘在舍卫城者，尽集一处，吾欲所勅。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda received the World-Honored One's instruction and immediately gathered the bhikṣus in the Potala Lecture Hall. He then informed the World-Honored One, "Today the bhikṣus have already gathered in one place."

是时，尊者阿难受世尊教，即集诸比丘，在普集讲堂，还白世尊曰：「今日比丘已集一处。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, surrounded by the bhikṣu saṃgha, went to the vihāra of the bhikṣu Vakkali. At that time, the evil Māra Pāpīyas wanted to know where the spirit of the Venerable Vakkali was. Was it among humans or non-humans, devas, nāgas, ghosts, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, or yakṣas? Where was this spirit now? Where was it born and wandering? He looked everywhere in the east, west, north, south, the four intermediate directions, and above and below, but he did not know where the spirit was. At that time, Māra Pāpīyas became exhausted and did not know where it was.

是时，世尊将比丘僧，前后围遶，至彼婆迦梨比丘精舍。当于尔时，弊魔波旬欲得知尊者婆迦梨神识所在，为在何处？为在人耶？为非人耶？天、龙、鬼神、干沓惒、阿须伦、迦留罗、摩休勒、阅叉？今此神识竟为所在，在何处生游？不见东西南北，四维上下，皆悉周遍而不知神识之处。是时，魔波旬身体疲极，莫知所在。

At that time, the World-Honored One, surrounded by the bhikṣu saṃgha, went to the vihāra. At that time, the World-Honored One observed that Māra Pāpīyas wanted to know where the spirit was. The World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Have you heard any loud noises in this vihāra? Are there any strange lights?"

尔时，世尊将比丘僧，前后围遶，至彼精舍。尔时，世尊观魔波旬欲得知神识所在。世尊告诸比丘：「汝等颇闻此精舍之中有大声乎？又有] cf.【丽】光怪？」

The bhikṣus replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! We have seen it."

诸比丘对曰：「如是。世尊！我等已见。」

The World-Honored One said, "This evil Māra Pāpīyas wants to know where the spirit of Bāṣkali is."

世尊告曰：「此弊魔波旬，欲得知婆迦梨神识所在。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda addressed the World-Honored One, saying, "May the World-Honored One please explain where the spirit of Bāṣkali Bhikṣu is."

是时，尊者阿难白世尊曰：「唯愿世尊说婆迦梨比丘神识为何所在？」

The World-Honored One said, "The spirit of Bāṣkali Bhikṣu is forever unattached. That noble one has attained parinirvāṇa. One should hold it like this."

世尊告曰：「婆迦梨比丘神识永无所著。彼族姓子以般涅槃，当作如是持。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda addressed the World-Honored One, saying, "On what day did Bāṣkali Bhikṣu attain these four truths?"

是时，尊者阿难白世尊曰：「此婆迦梨比丘何日得此四谛？」

The World-Honored One said, "He attained these four truths today."

世尊告曰：「今日之中得此四谛。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "This bhikṣu has been ill for a long time. He was originally an ordinary person."

阿难白佛：「此比丘抱病经久，本是凡人。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is, Ānanda! As you say, but that bhikṣu endured great suffering for a long time. Among the disciples of Śākyamuni Buddha, he was the most excellent in faith and liberation, but his defiled mind had not yet attained liberation. He thought, 'I should now seek a knife to stab myself.' At that time, as that bhikṣu was about to stab himself, he contemplated the merits of the Tathāgata. On the day he gave up his life, he contemplated the five aggregates: 'This is the origin of form, this is the cessation of form.' At that time, after that bhikṣu contemplated this, all the conditioned dharmas ceased. That bhikṣu had already attained parinirvāṇa."

世尊告曰：「如是，阿难！如汝所言，但彼比丘谦] cf.【丽】苦甚久，诸有释迦文佛弟子之中，信解脱者此人最胜，然有漏心未得解脱：『我今可求刀自刺。』是时，彼比丘临自刺时，即思惟如来功德；舍寿之日，思惟五盛阴：是谓此色习、此色灭尽。尔时，彼比丘思惟此已，诸有习之法皆悉灭尽，此比丘已般涅槃。」

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and upheld it.

尔时，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

the method of the four right efforts the four dark and
ignorant dharmas Ayi, the origin and end of the Dharma Śāriputra Vāgīśa
