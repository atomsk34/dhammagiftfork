EĀ 26.6

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

26.6 （六）老衰法

Parallel to: SN 3.3 / SĀ 1240 / SĀ² 2.67

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and after a while, rubbed the Tathāgata's feet with both hands, and then made a sound with his mouth on the Tathāgata's feet, saying, "Why is the body of the Heavenly Honored One like this? The body is extremely loose, the Tathāgata's body is not as it was before."

尔时，尊者阿难至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面住，斯须，复以两手摩如来足已，复以口鸣如来足上，而作是说：「天尊之体，何故乃尔？身极缓尔，如来之身不如本故。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is, Ānanda! As you say, the Tathāgata's skin and flesh are now loose, and today's body is not as it was before. The reason is this: all embodied beings are afflicted by illness. If one is a sick being, one is afflicted by illness; if one is a dying being, one is afflicted by death. Today the Tathāgata is already old and feeble, past eighty years of age."

世尊告曰：「如是。阿难！如汝所言，今如来身皮肉已缓，今日之体不如本故。所以然者，夫受形体，为病所逼。若应病众生，为病所困；应死众生，为死所逼。今日如来，年已衰微，年过八十。」

At that time, Ānanda, upon hearing these words, wept and choked with sobs, unable to control himself, and said, "Alas, old age has come to this."

是时，阿难闻此语已，悲泣哽噎，不能自胜，并作是语：「咄嗟，老至乃至于斯。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, at the proper time, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered the city of Śrāvastī to beg for food. At that time, the World-Honored One gradually begged for food and arrived at King Prasenajit's house. At that time, in front of King Prasenajit's gate, there were several dozen old and broken carts discarded on one side.

是时，世尊到时，著衣持钵，入舍卫城乞食。是时，世尊渐渐乞食，至王波斯匿舍。当于尔时，波斯匿门前，有故坏车数十乘，舍在一面。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda saw the carts discarded on one side, and upon seeing them, said to the World-Honored One, "These carts belong to King Prasenajit. When they were made in the past, they were extremely exquisite. If one looks at them today, they are the same color as tiles and stones."

是时，尊者阿难以见车弃在一面，见已，白世尊曰：「此车王波斯匿车，昔日作时极为精妙，如今日观之，与瓦石同色。」

The World-Honored One said, "Indeed, Ānanda! As you say, if one looks at the carts now, they were extremely exquisite in the past, made of gold and silver. Today they are broken and cannot be used again. External things are like this, so how much more so are internal ones?"

世尊告曰：「如是，阿难！如汝所言，如今观所有车，昔日之时极为精妙，金银所造，今日坏败，不可复用。如是外物尚坏败，况复内者？」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

Alas, this old age, sickness, and death destroy people's most vibrant
appearance. At first, it is very pleasing, but now it is
driven by death. Although one may live for a hundred years
all will return to death. There is no escape from this
suffering, all will return to this path. Just as what is
within the body is driven by death, the external four great
elements all return to their original non-existence. Therefore, seeking the deathless
there is only nirvāṇa. There is no death, no birth, and
no such activities at all.

「咄此老病死，　　坏人极盛色，初时甚悦意，　　今为死使逼。虽当寿百岁，　　皆当归于死，无免此患苦，　　尽当归此道。如内身所有，　　为死之所驱，外诸四大者，　　悉趣于本无。是故求无死，　　唯有涅槃耳，彼无死无生，　　都无此诸行。」

At that time, the World-Honored One sat down right where King Prasenajit was.

尔时，世尊即就波斯匿王坐。

At that time, King Prasenajit prepared various foods and drinks for the World-Honored One. After observing the World-Honored One's meal, the king took a small seat and sat in front of the Tathāgata, saying to the World-Honored One: "How is it, World-Honored One! Are the bodies of all Buddhas made of vajra, and do they also have old age, sickness, and death?"

是时，王波斯匿与世尊办种种饮食。观世尊食竟，王更取一小座，在如来前坐，白世尊曰：「云何，世尊！诸佛形体皆金刚数，亦当有老、病、死乎？」

The World-Honored One said: "Indeed, great king! As you say, the Tathāgata will also have birth, old age, sickness, and death. I am also a human being now, with a father named Suddhodana and a mother named Māyā, born into the lineage of a wheel-turning sage king."

世尊告曰：「如是，大王！如大王语，如来亦当有此生、老、病、死。我今亦是人数，父名真净，母名摩耶，出转轮圣王种。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

All Buddhas are born as humans, with a father named Suddhodana
and a mother named Māyā, born into the noble kṣatriya caste
The path of death is extremely difficult, not considering high or
low. Even the Buddhas cannot escape it, let alone other ordinary people.

「诸佛出于人，　　父名曰真净，母名极清妙，　　豪族刹利种。死径为极困，　　都不观尊卑，诸佛尚不免，　　况复余凡俗。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse to King Prasenajit:

尔时，世尊与波斯匿王而说此偈：

Sacrificing to fire is supreme, poetry and books are honored, kings are
noble among humans, and the ocean is foremost among rivers. The moon
is supreme among the stars, the sun is foremost in light, and
the eight directions, above, below, and the center, support the world. Among
the gods and people of the world, the Tathāgata is most honored
Those who wish for blessings and merit should make offerings to the Three Buddhas.

「祠祀火为上，　　诗书颂为尊，人中王为贵，　　众流海为首。众星月为上，　　光明日为先，八方上下中，　　世界之所载。天及世人民，　　如来最为尊，其欲求福禄，　　当供养三佛。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, rose from his seat and departed, returning to the Jetavana Monastery, where he took his seat.

是时，世尊说此偈已，便从座起而去，还祇洹精舍，就座而坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "There are four things that are loved and respected by people in the world. What are the four? Youth, which is loved and respected by people in the world; being free from illness, which is loved and respected by people; longevity, which is loved and respected by people; and the gathering of loved ones, which is loved and respected by people. These are called the four things that are loved and respected by people in the world.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有四法，在世间人所爱敬。云何为四？少壮之年，世间人民之所爱敬；无有病痛，人所爱敬；寿命，人所爱敬；恩爱集聚，人所爱敬。是谓，比丘！有此四法，世间人民之所爱敬。

Furthermore, bhikṣus, there are four things that are not loved and respected by people in the world. What are the four? Bhikṣus, you should know that when youth is followed by old age and illness, it is not liked by the world; when one is free from illness, but later becomes ill, it is not liked by the world; when one has attained longevity, but later dies, it is not liked by the world; when loved ones gather, but later separate, it is not liked by the world. These are called the four things that turn with the world. Bhikṣus, the gods, the people of the world, up to the wheel-turning sage kings and the buddhas of the world all share these things. These are called the four things that turn with the world.

「复次，比丘复有四法，世间人民所不爱敬。云何为四？比丘当知，少壮之年，若时老病，世人所不喜；若无病者，后便得病，世人所不喜；若有得寿命，后便命终，世人所不喜；恩爱得集，后复别离，是世人所不喜。是谓，比丘！有此四法与世回转，诸天、世人，乃至转轮圣王、诸佛世尊，共有此法，是为，比丘！世间有此四法与世回转。

If one is not aware of these four things, one will continue to transmigrate through birth and death, cycling through the five paths. What are the four? The noble precepts, noble samādhi, noble wisdom, and noble liberation. These are called the four things that, if not realized, will lead to the four things above. I and you now realize these four noble things, cut off the roots of birth and death, and no longer accept existence. Now the Tathāgata's physical form is aging and will receive this retribution of decay. Therefore, bhikṣus, you should seek this eternal nirvāṇa, which is unborn, ageless, diseaseless, deathless, and free from separation due to love. Always contemplate the changes of impermanence. Thus, bhikṣus, you should think like this.

「若不觉此四法时，便流转生死，周旋五道。云何为四？圣贤戒、贤圣三昧、贤圣智慧、贤圣解脱。是为，比丘！有此四法而不觉知者，则受上四法。我今及汝等，以觉知此贤圣四法，断生死根，不复受有。如今如来形体衰老，当受此衰耗之报。是故，诸比丘！当求此永寂涅槃，不生、不老、不病、不死，恩爱别离，常念无常之变。如是，比丘，当作是念。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
