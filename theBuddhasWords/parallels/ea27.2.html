EĀ 27.2

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

27.2 （二）

Parallel to: MN 11 / MĀ 103

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, many bhikṣus entered Śrāvastī. Then the many bhikṣus thought: "It is still early in the day for us to beg for alms. Let us go to the village of non-Buddhist ascetics to discuss the Dharma with them."

尔时，众多比丘入舍卫城。时，众多比丘便作是念：「然我等乞食日犹故早，我等可往至外道异学村与共论义。」

At that time, the many bhikṣus went to the village of non-Buddhists. Having arrived, they greeted each other and sat to one side. Then the non-Buddhists asked the bhikṣus: "The Śramaṇa Gautama teaches his disciples thus: 'You bhikṣus should all study this Dharma, understand it completely, and having understood it, should practice it together.' We also teach our disciples thus: 'You should all study this Dharma, understand it completely, and having understood it, should practice it together.' What differences are there between the Śramaṇa Gautama and us? What are the additions and subtractions? That is to say, he teaches the Dharma, and so do we. He instructs, and so do we."

是时，众多比丘便往至外道村中。到已，共相问讯，在一面坐已，在一面坐。尔时，异学问道人曰：「沙门瞿昙与诸弟子而说此法：『汝等比丘，尽当学此法，悉当了知，以了知已，当共奉行。』我等亦与诸弟子而说此法：『汝等尽当而学此法，悉当了知，以了知已，当共奉行。』沙门瞿昙与我等有何等异？有何增减？所谓彼说法，我亦说法，彼教诲，我亦教诲。」

At that time, the many bhikṣus, having heard these words, neither affirmed nor denied them. They then rose from their seats and left. At that time, the many bhikṣus said to each other: "We should go and inform the World-Honored One of this meaning!"

尔时，众多比丘闻此语已，亦不言是，复不言非，即从坐起而去。尔时，众多比丘自相谓曰：「我等当以此义，往白世尊！」

At that time, the many bhikṣus entered the city of Śrāvastī to beg for alms. After eating, they put away their robes and bowls, placed their sitting mats on their left shoulders, and went to where the World-Honored One was. They bowed their heads at his feet and sat to one side. At that time, the many bhikṣus, using this as a cause and condition, fully informed the World-Honored One.

尔时，众多比丘入舍卫城乞食，食已，收摄衣钵，以尼师檀著左肩上，往诣世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，众多比丘以此因缘，具白世尊。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "If those non-Buddhist ascetics ask this, you should answer them thus: 'Is it one ultimate, or many ultimates?' If those brāhmaṇas speak equally, you should say: 'It is one ultimate, not many ultimates.' What is that ultimate? Is it an ultimate of desire, or an ultimate of non-desire? That ultimate is an ultimate of non-desire. How is that ultimate? Is it an ultimate of anger, or an ultimate of non-anger? That ultimate is an ultimate of non-anger, not an ultimate of anger. How is that ultimate? Is it an ultimate of delusion, or an ultimate of non-delusion? That ultimate is an ultimate of non-delusion. How is that ultimate? Is it an ultimate of attachment, or an ultimate of non-attachment? That ultimate is an ultimate of non-attachment. How is that ultimate? Is it an ultimate of sensation, or an ultimate of non-sensation? That ultimate is an ultimate of non-sensation. How is that ultimate? Is it an ultimate of the wise, or an ultimate of the unwise? That ultimate is an ultimate of the wise. Is that ultimate an ultimate of the angry, or an ultimate of the non-angry? That ultimate is an ultimate of the non-angry.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若彼外道作此问者，汝等当以此语报彼曰：『为一究竟？为众多究竟乎？』或能彼梵志平等说者，应作是说：『是一究竟，非众多究竟。』彼究竟者，为是有欲究竟？为无欲究竟？所谓彼究竟者，谓无欲究竟。云何彼究竟者，有恚究竟？为无恚究竟？所谓彼究竟者，无恚究竟，非有恚究竟。云何有痴究竟？无痴究竟？所谓彼究竟者，无痴究竟。云何彼究竟者，为是有爱究竟？为无爱究竟？所谓彼究竟者，无爱究竟。云何彼究竟者，有受究竟？为无受究竟？所谓彼究竟者，无受究竟。云何彼究竟者，为是智者？为非智者？所谓智者所究竟。此究竟者，为是怒者所究竟？为非怒者所究竟？所谓此究竟，彼当作是说，非怒者所究竟。

Bhikṣus! There are these two views. What are the two views? Namely, the view of existence and the view of non-existence. Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not know the origin and end of these two views, they then have a mind of desire, a mind of anger, a mind of delusion, a mind of attachment, a mind of sensation. They are ignorant. They have an angry mind, not in accord with practice. Those people do not escape birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. They do not escape from the myriad forms of suffering. Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who truly know this, they then have no ignorant or angry mind. They are always in accord with practice, and they attain liberation from birth, old age, sickness, and death. Now I speak of the origin of suffering. Thus, bhikṣus! There is this wonderful Dharma, which is called the equal Dharma. Those who do not practice the equal Dharma fall into the five views.

「比丘！有此二见，云何为二见？所谓有见、无见。诸有沙门、婆罗门不知此二见之本末，彼便有欲心、有瞋恚心、有愚痴心、有爱心、有受心，彼是无知。彼有怒心，不与行相应，彼人不脱生、老、病、死、愁、忧、苦、恼，辛酸万端不脱于苦。诸有沙门、婆罗门如实而知之，彼便无愚痴、瞋恚之心，恒与行相应，便得脱生、老、病、死。今说苦之元本。如是，比丘！有此妙法，斯名平等之法。诸不行平等法者，则堕五见。

Now I will speak of the four kinds of feeling. What are the four kinds of feeling? Namely, the feeling of desire, the feeling of views, the feeling of precepts, and the feeling of self. These are called the four kinds of feeling. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who fully know the name of the feeling of desire, although they know the name of the feeling of desire, they are not in accord with it. They fully discern the names of all feelings, first discerning the name of the feeling of desire, but not discerning the names of the feelings of views, precepts, and self. The reason is that those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas cannot discern the names of these three feelings. Therefore, there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who fully discern all feelings, but there are also those who are incomplete. They then discern the names of the feelings of desire and views, but do not discern the names of the feelings of precepts and self. The reason is that those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas cannot discern the two feelings. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas can fully discern all feelings, or if there are those who are incomplete, they then can discern the names of the feelings of desire, views, and precepts, but not the feeling of self. The reason is that those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas cannot discern the feeling of self. Therefore, there are also śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who fully discern all feelings, but there are also those who are incomplete.

「今当说四受，云何为四受？所谓欲受、见受、戒受、我受，是谓四受。若有沙门、婆罗门尽知欲受之名，彼虽知欲受之名，复不相应者，彼尽分别诸受之名，先分别欲受之名，而不分别见受、戒受、我受之名。所以然者，以彼沙门、婆罗门不能分别此三受之名。是故，或有沙门、婆罗门尽分别此诸受，彼便分别欲受、见受，不分别戒受、我受。所以然者，以彼沙门、婆罗门不能分别二受。若使沙门、婆罗门尽能分别诸受，或复有不具者，彼便能分别欲受、见受、戒受，不分别我受。所以然者，以彼沙门、婆罗门不能分别我受故。是故，复有沙门、婆罗门尽分别诸受，然复有不具者。

What is the meaning of these four kinds of feeling? How are they discerned? Namely, the four kinds of feeling arise from craving. Thus, bhikṣus! There is this wonderful Dharma that should be discerned. If there is no practice of these feelings, this is not called equality. The reason is that the meaning of the Dharma is difficult to understand and difficult to comprehend. Such a meaning of non-Dharma is not spoken by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Bhikṣus should know that the Tathāgata fully discerns all feelings. By being able to discern all feelings, one is in accord with them, and one can discern the feelings of desire, views, self, and precepts. Therefore, the Tathāgata fully discerns all feelings, and is in accord with the Dharma, without contradiction.

「此名四受，有何等义？云何分别？所谓四受者，由爱而生。如是，比丘！有是妙法所应分别。若有不行此诸受，此不名为平等。所以然者，诸法之义难了难解。如此非法之义者，非三耶三佛之所说也。比丘当知，如来尽能分别一切诸受，以能分别一切诸受，则与相应，则能分别欲受、见受、我受、戒受。是故，如来尽分别诸受，则与法共相应，无有相违。

From what do these four kinds of feeling arise? These four kinds of feeling arise from craving, and grow from craving. By accomplishing these feelings, one is then unable to give rise to all feelings; by not giving rise to all feelings, one is not afraid; by not being afraid, one attains parinirvāṇa: the end of birth and death, the establishment of the holy life, the completion of what is to be done, no more rebirth, and the true knowledge of this. Thus, bhikṣus! There is this wonderful Dharma that should be truly known, fully endowed with all dharmas and the basis of practicing the Dharma. The reason is that this Dharma is extremely subtle, spoken by the Buddhas, and without any deficiency in all practices. Thus, bhikṣus! There are the first, second, third, and fourth śramaṇas, and there is no śramaṇa above them or who can surpass them, making such a lion's roar.

「此四受由何而生？然此四受由爱而生，由爱而长。成就此受，彼便不能起于诸受；以不起诸受，则不恐惧，以不恐惧，便般涅槃：生死已尽，梵行已立，所造已办，更不复受有，如实知之。如是，比丘！有此妙法，如实而知之，具足诸法、法行之本。所以然者，以其此法极微妙故，诸佛之所说，则于诸行无有缺漏。于是，比丘！有初沙门、第二沙门、第三沙门、第四沙门，更无复有沙门出此上者、能胜此者，作如是师子之吼。」

The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and upheld it.

诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
