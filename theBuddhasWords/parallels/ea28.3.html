EĀ 28.3

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

28.3 （三）手

Parallel to: AN 3.35

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was staying at a shrine in Ārāḍha.

一时，佛在阿罗毘祠侧。尔时，极为盛寒，树木凋落。

At that time, the elder's son Handāro came out of the city and was walking outside. He gradually came to where the World-Honored One was. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. Then the elder's son said to the World-Honored One, "I wonder, did you sleep well last night?"

尔时，手阿罗婆长者子出彼城中，在外经行，渐来至世尊所。到已，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，彼长者子白世尊言：「不审宿昔之中得善眠乎？」

The World-Honored One said, "Yes, young man! I slept very well."

世尊告曰：「如是，童子！快善眠也。」

At that time, the elder's son said to the Buddha, "Now it is the coldest day, with all things withered. Moreover, the World-Honored One is sitting on a grass mat, and the clothes you are wearing are extremely thin. How can the World-Honored One say, 'I slept very well'?"

时，长者子白佛：「今盛寒日，万物凋落，然复世尊坐用草蓐，所著衣裳，极为单薄，云何世尊作是说：『我快得善眠。』？」

The World-Honored One said, "Young man, listen carefully! I will now ask you a question, and you can answer as you wish. Suppose there is a house belonging to an elder that is well-maintained, free from wind and dust. In that house, there are beds, blankets, and woolen carpets, all complete. There are four jade maidens with beautiful faces, like peach blossoms, rare in the world, pleasing to the eye without satiation. There is a bright lamp. Would that elder sleep well?"

世尊告曰：「童子！谛听！我今还问汝，随所报之。犹如长者家牢治屋舍，无有风尘，然彼屋中有床蓐、氍氀、毾［毯－炎＋登］，事事俱具，有四玉女颜貌端政，面如桃华，世之希有，视无厌足，然好明灯，然彼长者快得善眠乎？」

The elder's son replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! With a good bed to lie on, one can sleep well."

长者子报曰：「如是。世尊！有好床卧，快得善眠。」

The World-Honored One said, "What do you think, elder's son? If that person sleeps well, would lustful thoughts arise, causing them to be unable to sleep?"

世尊告曰：「云何，长者子！若彼人快得善眠，时有欲意起，缘此欲意不得眠乎？」

The elder's son replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! If lustful thoughts arise in that person, they will not be able to sleep."

长者子对曰：「如是。世尊！若彼人欲意起者，便不得眠也。」

The World-Honored One said, "As for those intense lustful thoughts, the Tathāgata has completely extinguished them without remainder, with no root, and they will not arise again. What do you think, elder's son? If there are thoughts of anger and ignorance, could one sleep well?"

世尊告曰：「如彼欲意盛者，今如来永尽无余，无复根本，更不复兴。云何，长者子！设有瞋恚、愚痴心起者，岂得善眠乎？」

The youth replied, "One cannot sleep well. The reason is because of the three poisons in the mind."

童子报言：「不得善眠也。所以然者，由有三毒心故。」

The World-Honored One said, "The Tathāgata now has no such mind, completely extinguished without remainder, and no root. You should know, youth, I will now explain four kinds of sitting. What are the four? There is the sitting of the lowly, the sitting of the gods, the sitting of the Brahmas, and the sitting of the Buddha. You should know, youth, the sitting of the lowly is the sitting of the Wheel-Turning Sage King. The sitting of the gods is the sitting of Śakra Devānām-Indra. The sitting of the Brahmas is the sitting of the Brahma King. The sitting of the Buddha is the sitting of the Four Truths. The sitting of the lowly is the sitting of those who aspire to the stream-enterer. The sitting of the gods is the sitting of those who attain the stream-enterer. The sitting of the Brahmas is the sitting of those who aspire to the once-returner. The sitting of the Buddha is the sitting of the Four Right Efforts. The sitting of the lowly is the sitting of those who attain the once-returner. The sitting of the gods is the sitting of those who aspire to the non-returner. The sitting of the Brahmas is the sitting of those who attain the fruit of the non-returner. The sitting of the Buddha is the sitting of the Four Immeasurables. The sitting of the lowly is the sitting of the desire realm. The sitting of the gods is the sitting of the form realm. The sitting of the Brahmas is the sitting of the formless realm. The sitting of the Buddha is the sitting of the Four Bases of Spiritual Power.

世尊告曰：「如来今日无复此心，永尽无余，亦无根本。童子当知，我今当说四种之坐。云何为四？有卑坐、有天坐、有梵坐、有佛坐。童子当知，卑坐者，是转轮圣王坐也。天坐者，释提桓因坐也。梵坐者，梵天王之坐也。佛坐者，是四谛之坐也。卑坐者，向须陀洹坐也。天坐者，得须陀洹坐也。梵坐者，向斯陀含坐也。佛坐者，四意止之坐也。卑坐者，得斯陀含之坐也。天坐者，向阿那含之坐也。梵坐者，得阿那含果坐也。佛坐者，四等之坐也。卑坐者，欲界之坐也。天坐者，色界之坐也。梵坐者，无色界之坐也。佛坐者，四神足之坐也。

"Therefore, youth, the Tathāgata sits in the sitting of the Four Bases of Spiritual Power, and sleeps well. He does not arise from the minds of desire, anger, and ignorance. By not arising from these three poisonous minds, he attains parinirvāṇa in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder: the end of birth and death, the establishment of the practice of purity, the completion of what is to be done, and the knowledge that one will not again experience existence. Therefore, son of a householder, I see this meaning, and therefore say that the Tathāgata sleeps well."

「是故，童子！如来以坐四神足坐，快得善眠，于中不起婬、怒、痴，以不起此三毒之心，便于无余涅槃界而般涅槃：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受有，如实知之。是故，长者子，我观此义已，是故说如来快得善眠。」

At that time, the son of the householder spoke this verse:

尔时，长者子便说此偈：

It has been a long time since we last met, the brāhmaṇa has
attained parinirvāṇa, having attained the power of the Tathāgata, the one with clear
eyes has attained nirvāṇa. The low seat, the heavenly seat, the brāhmaṇa seat
and the Buddha seat, the Tathāgata fully discerns them, and therefore sleeps well
I take refuge in the most honored among humans, and also in the
most excellent among humans. I am not yet able to know, on what
meditation do they rely?

「相见日极久，　　梵志般涅槃，以逮如来力，　　明眼取灭度。卑坐及天坐，　　梵坐及佛坐，如来悉分别，　　是故得善眠。自归人中尊，　　亦归人中上，我今未能知，　　为依何等禅。」

Having spoken thus, the son of the householder, the World-Honored One assented. At that time, the son of the householder thought: "The World-Honored One has assented. I am extremely joyful and cannot contain myself." He then rose from his seat, bowed his head at the feet, and departed.

长者子作是语已，世尊然可之。是时，长者子便作是念：「世尊以然可，我极怀欢喜，不能自胜。」即从坐起，头面礼足，便退而去。

At that time, the young boy, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

尔时，彼童子闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
