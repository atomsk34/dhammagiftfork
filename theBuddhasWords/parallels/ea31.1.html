EĀ 31.1

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

Chapter 31 增上品第三十一

31.1 （一）增上

Parallel to: MN 4

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the Brāhman Jīvaka went to where the World-Honored One was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. The Brāhman then said to the World-Honored One, "Living in seclusion in a cave is very difficult! Being alone and walking alone is very difficult to concentrate on."

尔时，生漏婆罗门往至世尊所，共相问讯，在一面坐。尔时，婆罗门白世尊曰：「在闲居穴处，甚为苦哉！独处只步，用心甚难。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is, Brāhman! As you say, living in seclusion in a cave is very difficult! Being alone and walking alone is very difficult to concentrate on. Because in the past, before I attained Buddhahood, when I was practicing the bodhisattva path, I always thought like this: 'Living in seclusion in a quiet cave is very difficult! Being alone and walking alone is very difficult to concentrate on.'"

世尊告曰：「如是。梵志！如汝所言：『闲居穴处，甚为苦哉！独处只步，用心甚难！』所以然者，我曩昔未成佛道时，为菩萨行，恒作是念：『在闲静穴处，甚为苦哉，独处只步，用心甚难。』」

The Brāhman said to the Buddha, "If there are sons of noble families who, with firm faith, leave home to study the path, now the Śramaṇa Gautama is the foremost, benefiting many. He is a guide for those who follow him."

婆罗门白佛言：「若有族姓子，以信坚固，出家学道，今沙门瞿昙最为上首，多所饶益，为彼萠类而作奖导。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is, Brāhman! As you say, 'If there are sons of noble families who, with firm faith, leave home to study the path, I am the foremost, benefiting many. I am a guide for those who follow them.' If they see me, they all feel ashamed and go to the mountains and forests to live in secluded places. At that time, I think like this: 'There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who practice impure conduct and live in secluded places. Their impure conduct is in vain and not the true practice. They fear unwholesome dharmas. But now my conduct is not impure, and I live in secluded places. Those who practice impure conduct and live in secluded places are not like me. Because now my conduct is pure. The arhats who practice pure conduct enjoy living in secluded places. I am the foremost.' In this way, Brāhman! I observe my own pure conduct and enjoy living in secluded places, and I am even more delighted.

世尊告曰：「如是。婆罗门！如汝所言：『诸有族姓子，以信坚固，出家学道，我最为上首，多所饶益，与彼萠类而作奖导。』设彼见我皆起惭愧，诣山泽之中闲静穴处，我尔时便作是念：『诸有沙门、婆罗门身行不净，亲近闲居无人之处；身行不净，唐劳其功，不是真行，畏恶不善法；然我今日身行非为不净，亲近闲居之处；诸有身行不净，亲近闲静之处者，此非我之所有。所以然者，我今身行清净，诸阿罗汉身行清净者，乐闲居穴处，我最为上首。』如是，婆罗门！我自观身所行清净，乐闲居之处，倍复喜悦。

At that time, I thought like this: 'There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who practice impure conduct in mind and life, and live in secluded places. Although they have this practice, it is not true. They are all fully equipped with unwholesome dharmas. This is not mine. Because now my conduct in body, speech, mind, and life is pure. There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who practice pure conduct in body, speech, mind, and life, and enjoy living in secluded and pure places. That is mine. Because now my conduct in body, speech, mind, and life is pure. The arhats who practice pure conduct in body, speech, mind, and life enjoy living in secluded places. I am the foremost.' In this way, Brāhman! When my conduct in body, speech, mind, and life is pure, and I live in secluded places, I am even more delighted.

「我尔时便作是念：『诸有沙门、婆罗门，意行不清净，命不清净，亲近闲居无人之处，彼虽有此行，犹不真正，恶不善法彼皆悉备具，此非我有。所以然者，我今所行身、口、意、命清净。有沙门、婆罗门，身、口、意、命清净，乐在闲居清净之处，彼则我所有。所以然者，我今所行身、口、意、命清净，诸有阿罗汉身、口、意、命清净者，乐在闲静之处，我最为上首。』如是，婆罗门！当我身、口、意、命清净，在闲静之处时，倍增喜悦。

At that time, I thought like this: 'This is what the śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas fear. They live in secluded places and fear unwholesome dharmas. But now I am completely fearless, living in secluded places without people. Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have a fearful mind and live in secluded places are not mine. Because now I am completely fearless, living in secluded places and playing by myself. Those who have a fearful mind and live in secluded places are not mine. Because now I am free from suffering and affliction, not the same as them.' In this way, Brāhman! When I observe this meaning, I am fearless and even more delighted.

「尔时，我便作是念：『是谓沙门、婆罗门多所畏惧，处在闲静之处，尔时便畏惧恶不善法；然我今日永无所畏，在无人闲静之处，谓诸沙门、婆罗门有畏惧之心，在闲静处，谓彼非我有。所以然者，我今永无畏惧，在闲静之处而自游戏；诸有畏惧之心在闲居者，此非我也。所以然者，我今以离苦患，不与此同也。』如是，婆罗门！我观此义已，无有恐怖，增于喜悦。

There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who slander others and praise themselves. Although they live in secluded places, they still have impure thoughts. But now, Brāhman! I do not slander others or praise myself. Those who praise themselves and slander others are not mine. Because now I am without arrogance. The sages who are without arrogance are the foremost. When I observe this meaning, I am even more delighted.

「『诸有沙门、婆罗门毁彼自誉，虽在闲居之处，犹有不净之想。然我，梵志！亦非毁他，复非自誉；诸有自叹复毁他者，此非我有。所以然者，我今无有慢故，诸贤圣无有慢者，我最为上首。』我观此义已，倍复喜悦。

There are śramaṇas who seek for gain and cannot rest. But now I do not seek for gain. Because now I do not seek from others, and am also content. Among those who are content, I am the foremost. When I observe this meaning, I am even more delighted.

「『诸有沙门求于利养，不能自休，然我今日无有利养之求。所以然者，我今无求于人，亦同知足；然我知足之中，我最为上首。』我观此义已，倍复欢喜。

There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who are lazy in mind and do not diligently cultivate secluded places. They are not mine. Because now I have a courageous mind, and am not lazy in the middle. Among the sages who have a courageous mind, I am the foremost. When I observe this meaning, I am even more delighted.

「『诸有沙门、婆罗门心怀懈怠，不勤精进亲近闲静之处，彼非我有。所以然者，我今有勇猛之心，故中不懈惓，诸有贤圣勇猛之心者，我最为上首也。』我自观此义已，倍增欢喜。

At that time, I also thought like this: 'There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who are forgetful and live in secluded places, although they have this practice, they still have evil and unwholesome dharmas. But now I am not forgetful. If there are people who are forgetful, they are not mine. Among the sages who are not forgetful, I am the foremost.' Now that I observe this meaning, I am even more delighted to dwell in seclusion.

「我尔时复作是念：『诸有沙门、婆罗门多诸忘失，居在闲处，虽有此行，犹有恶不善法；然我今日无有诸忘失。设复，梵志！有忘失之人者，彼非我有，诸有贤圣之人不忘失者，我最为上首。』我今观此义已，在闲居处，倍增欢喜。

At that time, I also thought like this: 'There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas whose minds are distracted and unstable, and they have evil and unwholesome dharmas, together with evil conduct. But now my mind is never distracted, always single-minded. Those whose minds are distracted and unstable, they are not mine. Because my mind is always single-minded. If there are sages whose minds are single-minded, I am the foremost.' Now that I observe this, although dwelling in seclusion, I am even more delighted.

「尔时，我复作是念：『诸有沙门、婆罗门意乱不定，彼便有恶不善法，与恶行共并。然我今日意终不乱，恒若一心；诸有乱意心不定者，彼非我有。所以然者，我恒一心，设有贤圣心一定者，我最为上首。』我今观此已，虽居闲静之处，倍增欢喜。

At that time, I also thought like this: 'There are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who are ignorant and dark like a flock of sheep. They have evil and unwholesome dharmas. They are not mine. But now I am always wise and not ignorant, dwelling in seclusion. If there are such practitioners, they are mine. Now I have accomplished wisdom. Among the sages who have accomplished wisdom, I am the foremost.' Now that I observe this meaning, although dwelling in seclusion, I am even more delighted.

「我尔时复作是念：『诸有沙门、婆罗门，愚痴暗冥亦如群羊，彼人便有恶不善法，彼非我有；然我今日恒有智慧，无有愚痴，处在闲居。设有如此行者，彼是我有，我今智慧成就，诸有贤圣智慧成就者，我最为上首。』我今观此义已，虽在闲居，倍增欢喜。

When I was dwelling in seclusion, if trees were uprooted and birds and beasts fled, I thought at that time: 'This is a forest of great fear.' At that time, I also thought: 'If fear comes, I will seek a means to prevent it from coming again.' If fear came while I was walking, I would not sit or lie down, but would first remove the fear before sitting. If fear came while I was standing, I would neither walk nor sit, but would first remove the fear before sitting. If fear came while I was sitting, I would not walk, but would first remove the fear before sitting. If fear came while I was lying down, I would neither walk nor sit, but would first remove the fear before lying down.

「我当在闲居之中时，设使树木摧折、鸟兽驰走，尔时我作是念：『此是大畏之林。』尔时复作是念：『设使畏怖来者，当求方便，不复使来。』若我经行有畏怖来者，尔时我亦不坐卧，要除畏怖，然后乃坐。设我住时有畏怖来者，尔时我亦非经行，亦复不坐，要使除其畏怖，然后乃坐。设我坐时有畏怖来者，我不经行，要除畏怖，然后乃坐。若我卧时有畏怖来者，尔时我亦非经行，亦复不坐，要使除其畏怖，然后乃卧。

Brahmin, you should know that there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not understand the Dharma day and night. I now say that they are extremely foolish and deluded. But, brahmin, I understand the Dharma day and night, and have a courageous mind. I am not false, my mind is not confused, I am always single-minded, without thoughts of desire, with mindfulness, contemplation, joy, and happiness, dwelling in the first dhyāna. This, brahmin, is my first mind, which delights in the present life. If one removes mindfulness and contemplation, has inner joy, and has a single mind, without mindfulness or contemplation, with concentration, joy, and happiness, dwelling in the second dhyāna; this, brahmin, is my second mind, which delights in the present life. I myself observe and know that there is no desire for mindfulness within, and I experience the happiness of the body, which is hoped for by the sages. With mindfulness and happiness, dwelling in the third dhyāna; this, brahmin, is my third mind. If one further removes happiness and suffering, and has no more joy or sorrow, no suffering and no happiness, with pure mindfulness, dwelling in the fourth dhyāna; this, brahmin, is my fourth supreme mind, which I myself observe and know, dwelling in the mind.

「梵志当知，诸有沙门、婆罗门，日夜之中不解道法，我今说彼人极为愚惑。然我，梵志！日夜之中解于道法，加有勇猛之心，亦不虚妄，意不错乱，恒若一心，无贪欲想，有觉、有观，念持喜、乐，游于初禅。是谓，梵志！是我初心于现法中而自娱乐。若除有觉、有观，内有欢喜，兼有一心，无觉、无观，定念喜，游于二禅；是谓，梵志！第二之心于现法中而得欢乐。我自观知内无念欲，觉身快乐，诸贤圣所希望，护念欢乐，游于三禅；是谓，梵志！第三之心。若复苦乐已除，无复忧喜，无苦无乐，护念清净，游于四禅；是谓，梵志！第四增上之心，而自觉知游于心意。

When I was dwelling in seclusion, I had these four supreme minds. With this samādhi mind, pure and flawless, also without afflictions, I attained fearlessness and self-knowledge of the affairs of countless past lives. At that time, I remembered the affairs of my past lives, one life, two lives, three lives, four lives, five lives, ten lives, twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, a hundred lives, a thousand lives, the eons of formation and destruction, all clearly distinguished: I was once born there, with such-and-such a name, eating such-and-such food, experiencing such-and-such suffering and happiness, passing away from there and being born here, dying here and being born there. The causes and conditions, the beginning and end, were all clearly understood.

「当我在闲居之时，有此四增上之心，我以此三昧之心，清净无瑕秽，亦无结使，得无所畏，自识宿命无数劫事。尔时，我忆宿命之事，一生、二生、三生、四生、五生、十生、二十、三十、四十、五十、百生、千生，成败之劫，皆悉分别：我曾生彼，字某、名某，食如是之食，受如是苦乐，从彼终而此间生，死此生彼。因缘本末，皆悉明了。

Brahmin, you should know that in the first part of the night, I attained the first illumination, removed ignorance, and had no more darkness. My mind delighted in seclusion and I was self-aware. Again, with a samādhi mind that was flawless and also without afflictions, my mind was concentrated and I attained fearlessness. Again, I knew the birth and death of sentient beings. I further used the heavenly eye to observe the classes of sentient beings, those who are born and die, with good or bad appearances, in good or bad destinies, whether good or bad, according to their good or bad actions, all clearly distinguished. Those sentient beings who act badly with the body, speak badly, and think badly, who slander the sages, always harbor wrong views, and are associated with wrong views, upon the destruction of the body and the end of life, are born in hell. Those sentient beings who act well with the body, cultivate good actions with the mouth, and cultivate good actions with the mind, who do not slander the sages, always cultivate right views, and are associated with right views, upon the destruction of the body and the end of life, are born in good places in the heavens. Again, with the heavenly eye that is pure and flawless, I observed the classes of sentient beings, those who are born and die, with good or bad appearances, in good or bad destinies, whether good or bad, according to their original actions, all clearly knowing them.

「梵志当知，我初夜时而得初明，除其无明，无复暗冥，心乐闲居而自觉知。复以三昧心无瑕秽，亦无结使，心意在定，得无所畏。复知众生生者、死者，我复以天眼观众生类，生者、死者，善色、恶色，善趣、恶趣，若好、若丑，随行善恶，皆悉分别。诸有众生身行恶，口行恶，意行恶，诽谤贤圣，恒怀邪见，与邪见相应，身坏命终，生地狱中。诸有众生身行善行，口修善行，意修善行，不诽谤贤圣，恒修正见，与正见相应，身坏命终，生善处天上。复以天眼清净无瑕秽，观众生类，生者、死者，善色、恶色，善趣、恶趣，若好、若丑，随其行本，皆悉知之。

Brahmin, you should know that in the middle part of the night, I attained the second illumination, had no more darkness, and was self-aware, delighting in seclusion. Again, with a samādhi mind that was pure and flawless, also without afflictions, my mind was concentrated and I attained fearlessness. I attained the mind that exhausts the taints, and knew this suffering as it really is, not falsely. When I attained this mind at that time, the taints of desire, existence, and ignorance were liberated from the mind. Having attained liberation, I attained the knowledge of liberation: 'Birth and death are exhausted, the practice of the Brahma way is established, what was to be done is done, I will not again experience rebirth,' knowing it as it really is.

「梵志当知，若中夜时得第二明，无复暗冥，而自觉知乐于闲居。我复以三昧心清净无瑕秽，亦无结使，心意得定，得无所畏，得尽漏心，亦知此苦如实不虚，当我尔时得此心时，欲漏、有漏、无明漏心得解脱，以得解脱，便得解脱智：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受胎，如实知之。

"Brahmin, you should know that in the last part of the night, I attained the third illumination, had no more darkness. How is it, Brahmin? Is there any thought in you that the Tathāgata has a mind of desire, anger, or delusion, and has not yet exhausted it in a secluded place? Brahmin, do not view it like this. The reason is that the Tathāgata today has forever eliminated the taints, always delights in seclusion, and is not in the human realm. However, I today contemplate this meaning and delight in a secluded place. What are the two? Again, I roam in a secluded place and also liberate countless sentient beings."

「是谓，梵志！我后夜时得第三明，无复暗冥。云何，梵志！颇有此心：如来有欲心、瞋恚心、愚心，未尽在闲居之处？梵志！莫作是观。所以然者，如来今日诸漏永除，恒乐闲居，不在人间，然我今日观此义已，乐闲居之处。云何为二？又自游闲居之处，兼度众生，不可称计。」

At that time, the Brahmin who had given rise to taints said to the Buddha, "It is for the sake of sentient beings, to compassionately liberate all." The Brahmin again said to the Buddha, "Stop, stop! The World-Honored One's words are too much. It is like a hunchback finding relief, a lost one finding the path, a blind person finding eyesight, and being in darkness and seeing light. Thus, the Shakyamuni Buddha has spoken the Dharma with countless skillful means. I now take refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha. From now on, I will uphold the five precepts and no longer take life. I am a lay devotee."

尔时，生漏梵志白佛言：「以为众生愍度一切。」梵志复白佛言：「止！止！世尊！所说过多，犹如偻者得申，迷者得道，盲者得眼目，在暗见明。如是，沙门瞿昙无数方便而为说法，我今归佛、法、众，自今以后受持五戒，不复杀生，为优婆塞。」

At that time, the Brahmin who had given rise to taints heard the Buddha's teachings and joyfully accepted them.

尔时，生漏梵志闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
