EĀ 31.3

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

31.3 （三）行迹

Parallel to: AN 4.162 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are four kinds of practices. What are the four? There is a practice of pleasure that is deluded, this is called the first kind of practice; there is also a practice of pleasure that is swift; there is a practice of suffering that is deluded; there is also a practice of suffering that is swift.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有四事行迹。云何为四？有乐行迹所行愚惑，此名初行迹；复有乐行迹所行速疾；复有苦行迹所行愚惑；复有苦行迹所行速疾。

What is meant by the practice of pleasure that is deluded? There is someone whose desires, anger, and ignorance are intense, and their practice is very painful, not in accord with the practice of the fundamental. Their five faculties are also dull and not swift. What are the five? Namely, the faculties of faith, diligence, mindfulness, wisdom, and concentration. If one seeks the end of defilements through a deluded mind, this is called the one who attains the path through the dull faculties in the practice of pleasure.

「彼云何名为乐行迹所行愚惑？或有一人贪欲炽盛，瞋恚、愚痴炽盛，所行甚苦，不与行本相应，彼人五根愚暗亦不捷疾。云何为五？所谓信根、精进根、念根、慧根、定根。若以愚意求三昧尽有漏者，是谓名为乐行迹钝根得道者也。

What is meant by the practice of pleasure that is swift? There is someone who is without desire and without lust; yet in regard to desire, they are always partial and not diligent. For anger and ignorance, they are extremely reduced. The five faculties are swift and without negligence. What are the five? Namely, the faculties of faith, diligence, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom. These are the five faculties. Having attained the five faculties, one attains samādhi, exhausts defilements, and attains the undefiled. This is called the one who attains the path through the sharp faculties.

「彼云何名为乐根行迹速疾？或有一人无欲、无婬；然于贪欲，恒自偏少不慇懃，为瞋恚、愚痴极为减少，五根捷疾无有放逸。云何为五？所谓信根、精进根、念根、定根、慧根，是谓五根。然得五根成于三昧，尽有漏成无漏，是谓名为利根行于道迹也。

What is meant by the practice of suffering that is deluded? There is someone whose lustful thoughts are excessive, and whose anger and ignorance are intense. They enjoy this method, exhaust defilements, and attain the undefiled. This is called the one who attains the path through the dull faculties in the practice of suffering.

「彼云何名为苦行迹行于愚惑？或有一人婬意偏多，瞋恚、愚痴炽盛。彼以此法而自娱乐，尽有漏成无漏，是谓名为苦行迹钝根者也。

What is meant by the practice of suffering that is swift? There is someone who has little desire and little lust, no anger, and no thoughts. They practice these three dharmas. At that time, they have these five faculties, without any deficiency. What are the five? Namely, the faculties of faith, diligence, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom. These are called the five. They attain samādhi through this method, exhaust defilements, and attain the undefiled. This is called the one who attains the path through the sharp faculties in the practice of suffering.

「云何苦行迹行于速疾？于是，或有一人少欲少婬，无有瞋恚，亦不起想，行此三法。尔时，有此五根，无有缺漏。云何为五？所谓信根、精进根、念根、定根、慧根，是谓为五。彼以此法得三昧，尽有漏成无漏，是谓苦行迹利根者也。

This is called the four practices of the bhikṣus. They should seek means to abandon the first three practices and practice the last one together. The reason is that the samādhi of the practice of suffering is difficult to attain, but once attained, one can quickly attain the Way and remain in the world for a long time. The reason is that one cannot seek happiness through happiness, but through suffering one can then attain the Way. Therefore, bhikṣus! Always use skillful means to accomplish this practice. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way.

「是谓比丘有此四行迹，当求方便，舍前三行迹，后一行者当共奉行。所以然者，苦行迹三昧者难得，以得便成道，久存于世。所以然者，不可以乐求乐，由苦然后成道。是故，诸比丘！恒以方便，成此行迹。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
