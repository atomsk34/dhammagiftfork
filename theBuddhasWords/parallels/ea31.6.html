EĀ 31.6

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

31.6 （六）

Parallel to: SN 35.238 / SĀ 1172

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "It is like four extremely fierce and violent poisonous snakes placed in a box. If someone comes from the four directions, wishing to be alive, not seeking death, wishing for happiness, not seeking suffering, not foolish, not ignorant, with an unconfused mind, and not attached to anything,

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「犹如四大毒蛇极为凶暴，举著一函中。若有人从四方来，欲令活、不求死，欲求乐、不求苦，不愚不暗，心意不乱，无所系属。

At that time, if the king or a royal minister calls this person and tells them, 'Now there are four extremely fierce and violent poisonous snakes. You should now bathe them at the right time to keep them clean, feed them at the right time so they do not lack, and now is the right time to carry them out.' At that time, that person is fearful and does not dare go straight forward. They abandon and flee, not knowing where to go. They are deeply afraid. They are again told by that person, 'Now five people are all holding swords and following behind you. Whoever catches you will cut off your life. There is no time to delay.'

「是时，若王、若王大臣唤此人而告之曰：『今有四大毒蛇极为凶暴，汝今当随时将养沐浴令净，随时饮食无令使乏，今正是时，可往施行。』是时，彼人心怀恐惧，不敢直前，便舍，驰走莫知所凑深。复重告彼人作是语：『今使五人皆持刀剑而随汝后，其有获汝者，当断其命，不足稽迟。』

At that time, that person is afraid of the four great poisonous snakes and also afraid of the five people holding swords and staves. They run east and west, not knowing what to do. They are again told by that person, 'Now six enemies are following behind you. Whoever catches you will cut off your life. You can do what you want to do.' At that time, that person is afraid of the four great poisonous snakes, also afraid of the five people holding swords and staves, and also afraid of the six enemies. They run east and west. If they see an empty and desolate place, they want to hide there. If they encounter an empty house, a broken wall without a firm place, or an empty vessel with nothing in it, or if there is someone who is a friend of this person and wants to save them, they are told, 'This empty and desolate place is full of thieves and bandits. You can do what you want to do now.'

「是时，彼人畏四大毒蛇，复畏五人捉持刀剑者，驰走东西，不知如何？复告彼人曰：『今复使六怨家使随汝后，其有得者当断其命；欲所为者可时办之。』是时，彼人畏四大毒蛇，复畏五人持刀杖者，复畏六怨家，便驰走东西，彼人若见空墟之中，欲入中藏。若值空舍，若破墙间无坚牢处，若见空器，尽无所有，若复有人与此人亲友，欲令免济，便告之曰：『此间空闲之处多诸贼寇，欲所为者今可随意。』

At that time, that person is again afraid of the four great poisonous snakes, also afraid of the five people holding swords and staves, also afraid of the six enemies, and also afraid of the empty and desolate village. They run east and west. As that person goes forward, if they see a great body of water that is extremely deep and wide, and there are no people or bridges to cross to the other shore, and there are also many evil bandits where that person stands. At that time, that person thinks like this: 'This water is extremely deep and wide, and there are many bandits. How can I cross to the other shore? I should now gather wood and grass to make a raft, and rely on this raft to cross from this shore to the other shore.' At that time, that person then gathers wood and grass to make a raft, and is able to reach the other shore, without wavering in their resolve.

「是时，彼人复畏四大毒蛇，复畏五人持刀杖者，复畏六怨家，复畏空墟村中，便驰走东西。彼人前行，若见大水极深且广，亦无人民及桥梁可度得至彼岸，然复彼人所立之处多诸恶贼。是时，彼人作是思惟：『此水极为深广，饶诸贼寇，当云何得度彼岸？我今可集聚材木草蘘作栰，依此栰从此岸得至彼岸。』是时，彼人便集薪草作栰已，即得至彼岸，志不移动。

Bhikṣus, you should know that I will now give an analogy. When speaking of this meaning, what is the meaning? The four poisonous snakes refer to the four great elements. What are the four great elements? Namely, the earth element, water element, fire element, and wind element. These are called the four great elements. The five people holding swords and staves refer to the five aggregates. What are the five? Namely, the form aggregate, feeling aggregate, perception aggregate, formation aggregate, and consciousness aggregate. The six enemies refer to desire and attachment. The empty village refers to the six internal sense bases. What are the six? Namely, the six sense bases are the eye sense base, ear sense base, nose sense base, tongue sense base, body sense base, and mind sense base.

「诸比丘当知，我今作喻，当念解之。说此义时，为有何义？言四毒蛇者，即四大是也。云何为四大？所谓地种、水种、火种、风种，是谓四大。五人持刀剑者，此是五盛阴也。云何为五？所谓色阴、痛阴、想阴、行阴、识阴是也。六怨家者，欲爱是也。空村者，内六入是也。云何为六？所谓六入者，眼入、耳入、鼻入、口入、身入、意入。

If there are those with wisdom who observe the eyes, they are empty and nothing, and also not solid. If they further observe the ears, nose, mouth, body, and mind, they are all empty and nothing, all empty and still, and also not solid. The water refers to the four streams. What are the four? Namely, the stream of desire, the stream of existence, the stream of ignorance, and the stream of views. The great raft refers to the eightfold noble path. What are the eight? Namely, right view, right thought, right speech, right effort, right action, right livelihood, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called the eightfold noble path. Seeking to cross in the water refers to the power of skillful means and diligent effort. This shore refers to the body of evil; the other shore refers to the extinction of the body of evil. This shore refers to the realm of King Ajātaśatru; the other shore refers to the realm of King Viśva. This shore refers to the realm of Māra; the other shore refers to the realm of the Tathāgata.

「若有智慧者而观眼时，尽空无所有，亦不牢固；若复观耳、鼻、口、身、意时，尽空无所有，皆虚、皆寂，亦不牢固。云水者，四流是也。云何为四？所谓欲流、有流、无明流、见流。大栰者，贤圣八品道是也。云何为八？正见、正治、正语、正方便、正业、正命、正念、正定，是谓贤圣八品道也。水中求度者，善权方便精进之力也。此岸者，身邪也；彼岸者，灭身邪也。此岸者，阿阇世国界也：彼岸者，毘沙王国界也。此岸者，波旬国界也；彼岸者，如来之境界也。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

是时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
