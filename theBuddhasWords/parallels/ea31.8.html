EĀ 31.8

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

31.8 （八）无息禅

Parallel to: MN 12 (~) / MN 36 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was in a forest outside the city of Vaiśālī.

一时，佛在毘舍离城外林中。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "When I had not yet attained Buddhahood, I relied on that Great Fear Mountain to live. At that time, if those with desire or without desire entered the mountain, their hair stood on end. If it was extremely hot, I would sit naked in the wild, exposed to the wind, and at night I would enter the deep forest. If it was extremely cold, with wind and rain pouring down, I would enter the forest during the day and sit naked at night.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我昔未成佛道时，尔时依彼大畏山而住。是时彼山，其有欲心、无欲心入中者衣毛皆竖。若复极盛热时，野马纵横，露其形体而坐，夜便入深林中。若复极寒之日，风雨交流，昼便入林中，夜便露坐。

At that time, I was able to recite one verse, which I had never heard or seen before.

「我尔时，正能诵一偈，昔所未闻，昔所未见也。

In the peaceful night, in the Great Fear
Mountain, revealing my body - this is my vow.

「澹淡夜安，　　大畏山中，露其形体，　　是我誓愿。

If I go to the charnel ground to take the clothes of the dead and cover my body, then if the people of Anathapindika village come to take the wood and put it in my ears, or in my nose, or spit on me, or urinate on me, or throw dirt on my body, I will not give rise to any intention towards those people. At that time, I will have this protective mind. At that time, there was a place where cows were kept. If I saw the feces of a calf, I would take it to eat; if there was no calf feces, I would take the feces of a large cow to eat. At that time, eating this food, I thought again: 'Now, what is the use of eating? I can go without eating all day.' At that time, I had this thought, and the gods came to me and said: 'You should not stop eating now. If you stop eating, we will nourish you with the essence of nectar to sustain your life.' At that time, I thought again: 'Now, why should I stop eating and have the gods send nectar to me? Now, this body will be empty and deceitful.' At that time, I thought again: 'Now, I can eat the leftovers of sesame and rice.

「若我至冢间，取彼死人之衣，而覆形体。尔时，若案咤村人来取木支，著我耳中，或著鼻中，或有唾者，或有溺者，或以土坌其身上；然我尔时，终不起意，向彼人民。尔时，有此护心。尔时，有［牛＊苗］牛之处，设见犊子屎，便取食之；若无犊子屎者，便取大牛屎食之。尔时，食此之食，我复作是念：『今用食为？乃可终日不食。』时我以生此念，诸天便来到我所，而作是言：『汝今勿复断食。若当断食者，我当以甘露精气相益，使存其命。』尔时，我复作是念：『今以断食，何缘复使诸天送甘露与我？今身将有虚诈。』是时，我复作是念：『今可食麻米之余。』

At that time, eating one sesame seed and one grain of rice a day, my body was weak and my bones were connected. A sore grew on the top of my head and the skin and flesh fell off by themselves, like a rotten gourd. It did not accomplish my head. At that time, it was also like this. A sore grew on the top of my head and the skin and flesh fell off by themselves, all because of not eating. It was also like the stars appearing in deep water. At that time, my eyes were also like this, all because of not eating. It was also like an old and broken cart. My body was also like this, all broken and not to be relied upon. It was also like the hoofprint of a camel. My two buttocks were also like this. When I massaged my belly with my hand, I touched my spine. When I pressed my spine, I touched my belly skin. My body was weak, all because of not eating.

「尔时，日食一麻一米，形体劣弱，骸骨相连，顶上生疮，皮肉自堕，犹如败坏瓠卢，亦不成就我头。尔时亦复如是，顶上生疮，皮肉自堕，皆由不食故也。亦如深水之中星宿现中，尔时，我眼亦复如是，皆由不食故。犹如故车败坏，我身亦复如是，皆悉败毁不可承顺。亦如骆驼脚迹，两尻亦复如是。若我以手按摩腹时，便值脊骨，若按脊时，复值腹皮，身体羸弱者，皆由不食故。

At that time, I again used one sesame seed and one grain of rice as food, but it was of no benefit, and I also did not attain the supreme Dharma. If I wanted to defecate or urinate, I would fall to the ground and be unable to get up. At that time, the gods saw this and said: 'This śramaṇa Gautama has attained parinirvāṇa.' Or some gods said: 'This śramaṇa has not yet died, but will now die.' Or some gods said: 'This śramaṇa is neither dead nor alive. This śramaṇa is truly an arhat. The Dharma of arhats has this kind of ascetic practice.'

「我尔时，复以一麻一米，以此为食，竟无所益，亦复不得上尊之法。若我意中欲大小便者，即便倒地不能自起居。是时，诸天见已，便作是说：『此沙门瞿昙以取灭度。』或复有诸天，而作是说：『此沙门未命终，今必命终。』或复有诸天，而作是说：『此沙门亦非命终，此沙门实是阿罗汉，夫罗汉之法有此苦行。』

At that time, I still had consciousness and knew the external conditions. At that time, I also had this thought: 'Now I can enter the meditation of no breath.' I then entered the meditation of no breath and counted the breaths. I now counted the breaths and realized that there was breath coming out of my ears. At that time, the sound of the wind was like thunder. At that time, I also had this thought: 'Now I close my mouth and cover my ears, preventing the breath from coming out.' The breath did not come out, and at that time the internal breath came out from my hands and feet. Even if the breath could not come out from the ears, nose, and mouth, at that time the internal sound was like thunder. At that time, I was also like this. At that time, the consciousness still followed the body.

「我尔时，犹有神识，知外来机趣。时，我复作是念：『今可入无息禅中。』便入无息禅中，数出入息，我今以数出入息，觉知有气从耳中出，是时风声如似雷鸣。尔时，复作是念：『我今闭口塞耳，使息不出。』息以不出，是时内气便从手脚中出，正使气不得从耳、鼻、口出，尔时内声如似雷吼，我尔时亦复如是。是时神识犹随身回。

At that time, I also had this thought: 'I should enter the meditation of no breath again.' At that time, I completely blocked the breath from all the orifices. When I blocked the in-and-out breaths, I then suffered from a headache, as if someone was drilling a hole in my head. I also had extreme pain in my head. At that time, I still had consciousness. At that time, I also had this thought: 'Now I should sit in meditation, and the breath should not come in or out.' At that time, I blocked the in-and-out breaths. At that time, all the breaths gathered in my abdomen. At that time, the breath circulation was extremely small, like a butcher killing a cow with a knife; I was also like that, suffering from extreme pain. It was also like two strong men holding a weak person over a fire, suffering from unbearable pain; I was also like that, the pain and suffering being indescribable. At that time, I still had consciousness. When I sat in meditation at that time, my body did not have the color of a human being; some people saw it and said: 'This śramaṇa's complexion is extremely dark.' Some people saw it and said: 'This śramaṇa's complexion is like death.' Bhikṣus should know, I practiced these austerities for six years, but did not attain the supreme Dharma.

「是时，复作是念：『我宜更入无息禅中。』是时，尽塞诸孔之息。我以塞诸出入息，是时便患头额痛，如似有人以钻钻头，我亦如是极苦头痛。尔时，我故有神识，尔时我复作是念：『我今更可坐禅，息气不得出入。』尔时，我便塞出入息，是时诸息尽集腹中。尔时，息转时极为少类，犹如屠牛之家，以刀杀牛；我亦如是，极患苦痛。亦如两健人共执一劣人于火上炙，极患疼痛不可堪忍；我亦如是，此苦疼痛不可具陈。尔时，我犹有神识存，当我尔时坐禅之日，形体不作人色；其中有人见已，而作是说：『此沙门颜色极黑。』有人见已，而作是说：『此沙门颜色似终。』比丘当知，我六年之中作此苦行，不得上尊之法。

At that time, I had this thought: 'Today I should eat one fruit.' At that time, I then ate one fruit. When I ate one fruit, my body was weak and could not get up on my own, like someone a hundred and twenty years old, with bones and joints scattered and unable to support each other. Bhikṣus should know, at that time, one fruit was like a small date today. At that time, I also had this thought: 'This is not the basis for my attaining the Way, so I should seek another path.' At that time, I also had this thought: 'I remember that in the past, under the tree of my father the king, without desire and lust, removing evil and unwholesome dharmas, I dwelled in the first dhyāna; without perception and contemplation, I dwelled in the second dhyāna; with pure mindfulness and no various thoughts, I dwelled in the third dhyāna; without further suffering or pleasure, with pure mental attention, I dwelled in the fourth dhyāna. This may be the path, I should now seek this path.'

「尔时，我作是念：『今日可食一果。』尔时我便食一果。当我食一果之日，身形萎弱不能自起居，如年百二十，骨节离散不能扶持。比丘当知，尔时一果者，如似今日小枣耳。尔时，我复作是念：『非我成道之本，故当更有余道。』尔时，我复作是念：『我自忆昔日，在父王树下无婬、无欲，除去恶不善法，游于初禅；无觉、无观，游于二禅；念清净无有众想，游于三禅；无复苦乐，意念清净，游于四禅。此或能是道，我今当求此道。』

For six years, I diligently sought the Way but did not attain it. I either lay on thorns or on boards with iron nails, or hung my body far from the ground with my two feet up and head down, or squatted with crossed legs, or let my beard and hair grow without ever trimming them, or exposed myself to the sun and fire, or sat in ice during the cold winter, submerging my body in water, or remained silent, or ate once a day, or twice a day, or three, four, up to seven times a day, or ate vegetables and fruits, or rice and hemp, or ate grass roots, or ate tree fruits, or ate flower fragrances, or ate various fruits and vegetables, or sometimes went naked, or wore tattered clothes, or wore clothes made of kusha grass, or wore clothes made of animal hair, or sometimes covered my body with human hair, or sometimes grew my hair, or sometimes took others' hair and added it to my own. In this way, bhikṣus! I practiced austerities in the past, but did not attain the basis of the four dharmas. What are the four? Namely, the noble precepts and discipline are difficult to understand and know; the noble wisdom is difficult to understand and know; the noble liberation is difficult to understand and know; the noble samādhi is difficult to understand and know. These, bhikṣus! are the four dharmas. In the past, I practiced austerities but did not attain this essential point.

「我六年之中勤苦求道而不克获；或卧荆棘之上；或卧板木铁钉之上，或悬鸟身体远地，两脚在上而头首向地；或交脚蹲踞；或养长须发未曾揃除；或日暴火炙；或盛冬坐冰；身体没水；或寂寞不语；或时一食；或时二食；或时三食、四食，乃至七食；或食菜果；或食稻麻；或食草根；或食木实；或食花香；或食种种果蓏；或时倮形；或时著弊坏之衣；或著莎草之衣；或著毛毳之衣；或时以人发覆形；或时养发；或时取他发益戴。如是，比丘！吾昔苦行乃至于斯，然不获四法之本。云何为四？所谓贤圣戒律难晓难知；贤圣智慧难晓难知；贤圣解脱难晓难知；贤圣三昧难晓难知。是谓，比丘！有此四法，吾昔苦行不获此要。

At that time, I also had this thought: 'I must now seek the unsurpassed Way.' What is the unsurpassed Way? Namely, the four dharmas: the noble precepts and discipline, the noble samādhi, the noble wisdom, and the noble liberation. At that time, I also had this thought: 'I cannot seek the supreme Way with this weak body. I should eat the essence of fine qi, nourish my body, and have vigorous energy, and then I can practice the Way.' When I was about to eat the essence of fine qi, the five bhikṣus abandoned me and returned, saying: 'This śramaṇa Gautama has a confused nature and practice. He abandons the true Dharma and turns to the wrong path.'

「尔时，我复作是念：『吾今要当求无上之道。』何者是无上之道？所谓向四法是也：贤圣戒律、贤圣三昧、贤圣智慧、贤圣解脱。尔时，我复作是念：『不可以此羸劣之体，求于上尊之道，多少食精微之气，长育身体，气力炽盛，然后得修行道。』当食精微之气，时五比丘舍我还退：『此沙门瞿昙性行错乱，以舍真法而就邪业。』

At that time, I immediately rose from my seat and walked eastward. At that time, I also had this thought: 'Where did the Buddhas of the past, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, attain enlightenment?' At that time, a god in the sky was dwelling above and said to me: 'Virtuous one, you should know that the Buddhas of the past, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, attained Buddhahood while sitting under the cool shade of the Bodhi tree.' At that time, I also had this thought: 'Where should I sit to attain Buddhahood? Sitting or standing?' At that time, the gods again came and told me, saying: 'The Buddhas of the past, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, attained Buddhahood while sitting on grass mats.'

「当我尔时，即从坐起，东向经行。是时，我复作是念：『过去久远恒沙诸佛，成道之处为在何所？』是时，虚空神天住在上，而语我曰：『贤士当知，过去恒沙诸佛世尊，坐于道树清凉荫下而得成佛。』时，我复作是念：『为坐何处得成佛道？坐耶？立耶？』是时，诸天复来告我，而作是说：『过去恒沙诸佛世尊，坐于草蓐，然后成佛。』

At that time, not far from me, there was a virtuous brāhman who was cutting grass nearby. I went to him and asked: 'Who are you? What is your name? Do you have a surname?' The brāhman replied: 'My name is Auspicious, and my surname is Fuxing.' At that time, I said to that person: 'Excellent! Excellent! Such a surname is rare in the world; the name and surname are not false, and will surely fulfill its meaning. May there be auspiciousness and no harm in this present life, and may birth, old age, sickness, and death be forever eliminated. Your surname Fuxing is the same as mine. I now wish to ask for some grass.' Auspicious asked: 'Gautama! What do you need this grass for today?' At that time, I replied to Auspicious: 'I wish to spread it under the Bodhi tree to seek the four dharmas. What are the four? Namely, the noble precepts and discipline, the noble samādhi, the noble wisdom, and the noble liberation.'

「是时，去我不远，有吉祥梵志在侧刈草，即往至彼，问：『汝是何人，为名何等，为有姓耶？』梵志报曰：『我名吉祥，其姓弗星。』我尔时语彼人曰：『善哉！善哉！如是姓字，世之希有；姓名不虚，必成其号，当使现世吉无不利，生、老、病、死，永使除尽。汝姓弗星，与我共同。吾今欲所求，见惠少草。』吉祥问曰：『瞿昙！今日用斯草为？』尔时，我报吉祥曰：『吾欲敷树王下求于四法。云何为四？所谓贤圣戒律、贤圣三昧、贤圣智慧、贤圣解脱。』

Bhikṣus, you should know that at that time, Auspicious personally took the grass and went to the Bodhi tree. I then sat on it, straightened my body and mind, crossed my legs, and focused my thoughts. At that time, my mind of desire was liberated, and I eliminated all evil dharmas. With perception and contemplation, I dwelled in the first dhyāna. With the elimination of perception and contemplation, I dwelled in the second, third, and fourth dhyānas. With pure mindfulness and the elimination of joy and sorrow, I dwelled in the fourth dhyāna. At that time, with a pure mind, I eliminated all afflictions, attained fearlessness, and self-cognized my innumerable past lives. I then remembered the affairs of innumerable lifetimes, either one, two, three, four, five, ten, twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, one hundred, one thousand, one hundred thousand lifetimes, lifetimes during the eon of formation, lifetimes during the eon of destruction, innumerable lifetimes during the eon of formation, innumerable lifetimes during the eon of destruction, and innumerable lifetimes during the eon of formation and destruction. I remembered that I had died here and been reborn there, and that I had come to be born here from passing away there, without knowing the beginning and end of the causes and conditions. I remembered such affairs of innumerable lifetimes.

「比丘当知，尔时，吉祥躬自执草诣树王所。吾即坐其上，正身正意，结加趺坐，计念在前。尔时，贪欲意解，除诸恶法，有觉、有观，游志初禅；有觉、有观除尽，游志二、三禅；护念清净，忧喜除尽，游志四禅。我尔时以清净之心，除诸结使，得无所畏，自识宿命无数来变。我便自忆无数世事，或一生、二生、三、四、五生、十生、二十、三十、四十、五十、百生、千生、百千万生、成劫、败劫、无数成劫、无数败劫、无数成败之劫，我曾死此生彼，从彼命终而来生此，无其本末因缘所从，忆如此无数世事。

I further used my pure and flawless divine eye to observe the classes of sentient beings who are born and die, those in good destinies and with good appearances, those in bad destinies and with bad appearances, whether good or ugly, according to their fundamental practices, I knew them all. There are sentient beings who practice evil deeds with the body, speech, and mind, slander the sages, create the basis of wrong deeds, and are associated with wrong views. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn in hell. There are also classes of sentient beings who practice good deeds with the body, speech, and mind, do not slander the sages, and are associated with right views. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among humans. This is called these sentient beings practicing good deeds with the body, speech, and mind, without any wrong deeds. I used the mind of samādhi that is pure and flawless, with the exhaustion of afflictions, attained the liberation of the mind without taints and the liberation of wisdom. The cycle of birth and death is exhausted, the practice of the Brahma way is established, what is to be done is accomplished, and I will not again receive a womb. I truly know this and attained the unsurpassed perfect true path.

「我复以天眼清净无瑕秽，观众生类生者、终者，善趣、善色，恶趣、恶色，若好、若丑，随其行本，皆悉知之。或有众生身修恶行，口修恶行，意修恶行，诽谤贤圣，造邪业本，与邪见相应，身坏命终，生地狱中。或有众生之类，身、口、意行善，不诽谤贤圣，与正见相应，身坏命终，生于人间，是谓此众生身、口、意行无有邪业。我以三昧之心清净无瑕秽，有漏尽，成无漏心解脱、智慧解脱；生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受胎，如实知之。即成无上正真之道。

If a bhikṣu knows the various destinies of śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, but this destiny has no basis, then I have not practiced it before, except for the Pure Abode Heaven, which does not come to this world; or if a śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa is to be reborn in a certain place, but I am not born there, then it is not appropriate. I have already been born in the Pure Abode Heaven and will not return to this world. You have attained the noble precepts, and I have also attained them; the noble samādhi, you have also attained it, and I have also attained it; the noble wisdom, you have also attained it, and I have also attained it; the noble liberation, you have also attained it, and I have also attained it; the noble knowledge and vision of liberation, you have also attained it, and I have also attained it; having severed the roots of rebirth, birth and death are forever exhausted, and I will not again receive a womb.

「若使比丘！或有沙门、婆罗门明了诸趣，然此趣无本，吾昔未始不行，除一净居天上不来此世；或复沙门、婆罗门当可所生之处，然我不生者，则非其宜，已生净居天，不复来此世间。卿等以得贤圣戒律，我亦得之；贤圣三昧，卿等亦得，我亦得之；贤圣智慧，卿等亦得，我亦得之；贤圣解脱，卿等亦得，我亦得之；贤圣解脱知见，卿等亦得，我亦得之；以断胞胎之根，生死永尽，更不复受胞胎。

"Therefore, bhikṣus! You should seek means to accomplish the four dharmas. The reason is this. If bhikṣus attain these four dharmas, it is not difficult to attain the Way, just as I have today attained the unsurpassed perfect true Way, all due to the four dharmas that have borne fruit. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「是故，诸比丘！当求方便，成就四法。所以然者。若比丘得此四法者，成道不难，如我今日成无上正真之道，皆由四法而得成果。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.
