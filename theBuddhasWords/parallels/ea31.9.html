EĀ 31.9

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

31.9 （九）四乐

Parallel to: SN 1.11 / SĀ 576 / SĀ² 2.161

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Long ago, Śakra Devānām-Indra of the Thirty-three Heavens, together with his celestial maidens, went to Nandana Grove to play.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「过去久远，三十三天释提桓因及将诸玉女，诣难檀槃那园游。

At that time, a certain god spoke this verse:

「是时，有一天人便说此偈：

Not seeing Nandana Grove, one does not know what happiness is
The abode of the gods, there is none that surpasses it.

「『不见难檀园，　　则不知有乐，诸天之所居，　　无有过是者。』

At that time, a certain god spoke to that god, saying: 'You are now unwise and cannot distinguish the right principles. You call suffering and painful things happy; you call impermanent things permanent; you call unstable things stable. Why is this so? Have you not heard the Buddha speak the verse:

「是时，有天语彼天言：『汝今无智不能分别正理，忧苦之物，反言是乐；无牢之物，而言是牢；无常之物，反言是常；不坚要之物，复言坚要。所以然者，汝竟不闻如来说偈乎？

All conditioned things are impermanent, those who are born must die. Those
who are not born will not die, this cessation is the most blissful.

「『一切行无常，　　生者必有死，不生必不死，　　此灭最为乐。』

'They have this meaning and also this verse, how can you say that this place is the most blissful? You should know that the Tathāgata also spoke of the four streams of dharmas. If all sentient beings are submerged in these streams, they will never attain the Way. What are the four? Namely, the stream of desire, the stream of existence, the stream of views, and the stream of ignorance.

「『彼有此义，又有此偈，云何方言此处最为乐耶？汝今当知，如来亦说有四流法。若一切众生没在此流者，终不得道。云何为四？所谓欲流、有流、见流、无明流。

'What is called the stream of desire? Namely, the five desires. What are the five? Namely, if the eyes see form and give rise to eye-form thoughts; if the ears hear sound and give rise to cognition thoughts; if the nose smells fragrance and gives rise to cognition thoughts; if the tongue knows taste and gives rise to cognition thoughts; if the body knows smoothness and gives rise to cognition thoughts, this is called the stream of desire.

「『云何名为欲流？所谓五欲是也。云何为五？所谓若眼见色起眼色想；若耳闻声起识想；若鼻嗅香起识想；若舌知味起识想；若身知细滑起识想，是谓名为欲流。

'What is called the stream of existence? Namely, existence refers to the three existences. What are the three? Namely, the existence of desire, the existence of form, and the existence of formlessness. This is called the stream of existence.

「『云何名为有流？所谓有者，三有是也。云何为三？所谓欲有、色有、无色有，是谓名为有流也。

'What is called the stream of views? Namely, the stream of views: the world is eternal or not eternal; the world has an end or no end; there is a self or no self; there is a Tathāgata after death or not; there is a Tathāgata after death or not; there is neither a Tathāgata after death nor not a Tathāgata after death. This is called the stream of views.

「『云何名为见流？所谓见流者：世有常、无常；世有边见、无边见；彼身彼命、非身非命；有如来死、无如来死、若有如来死无如来死、亦非有如来死亦非无如来死；是谓名为见流。

'What is called the stream of ignorance? Namely, ignorance refers to ignorance, disbelief, and wrong views, with a mind that is always greedy and hopeful, and the five hindrances: the hindrance of sensual desire, the hindrance of ill will, the hindrance of sloth and torpor, the hindrance of restlessness and remorse, and the hindrance of doubt. If one does not know suffering, does not know its origin, does not know its cessation, and does not know the path, this is called the stream of ignorance. Heavenly sons should know that this is the four streams spoken of by the Tathāgata. If a person is submerged in these, they will also not be able to attain the path.'

「『彼云何无明流？所谓无明者，无知、无信、无见，心意贪欲恒有希望，及其五盖：贪欲盖、瞋恚盖、睡眠盖、调戏盖、疑盖。若复不知苦、不知习、不知尽、不知道，是谓名为无明流。天子当知，如来说此四流。若有人没在此者，亦不能得道。』

"At that time, having heard these words, the heavenly being, as quickly as a strong man bends and stretches his arm, disappeared from the Heaven of the Thirty-three and came before me. He bowed his head at my feet and stood to one side. Then the heavenly being said to me, 'Excellent, World-Honored One! You have spoken these words well. The Tathāgata has spoken of the four streams. If ordinary people do not hear about these four streams, they will not attain the four kinds of happiness. What are the four? Namely, the happiness of rest, the happiness of right awakening, the happiness of the śramaṇa, and the happiness of nirvāṇa. If ordinary people do not know about these four streams, they will not attain these four kinds of happiness.' Having said this, I further told him, 'So it is, heavenly being! As you have said, if one does not awaken to these four streams, one will not awaken to these four kinds of happiness.'"

「是时，彼天闻此语已，犹如力士屈申臂顷，从三十三天没，来至我所，头面礼足，在一面立。尔时，彼天而白我言：『善哉！世尊！快说此语！如来乃说四流。若凡夫之人不闻此四流者，则不获四乐。云何为四？所谓休息乐、正觉乐、沙门乐、涅槃乐。若凡夫之人不知此四流者，不获此四乐。』作是语已，我复告曰：『如是，天子！如汝所言，若不觉此四流，则不觉此四乐。』

"At that time, I gradually discussed with that heavenly being. The discussion was about giving, precepts, and the idea of rebirth in heaven. I spoke about the impure nature of desire, the great suffering of defilements, and the happiness of liberation. At that time, the heavenly being gave rise to a joyful mind. Then I extensively explained the Dharma of the four streams and the four kinds of happiness. At that time, the heavenly being single-mindedly contemplated this Dharma, and all his defilements were exhausted, attaining the purity of the Dharma eye. I now also speak of these four dharmas and four kinds of happiness, and one can attain the Dharma of the four truths. Thus, bhikṣus! One should practice in this way."

「我时与彼天人渐渐共论，所谓论者：施论、戒论、生天之论，欲不净想，漏为大患，出要为乐。尔时，天人以发欢喜之心；是时，我便广演说四流之法，及说四乐。尔时，彼天专心一意，思惟此法已，诸尘垢尽，得法眼净。我今亦说此四法、四乐，便得四谛之法。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
