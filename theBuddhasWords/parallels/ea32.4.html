EĀ 32.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

32.4 （四）天使

Parallel to: MN 130 / AN 3.36 (~) / DĀ 30 (~) / MĀ 64

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "It is like a house with two doors facing each other. If someone is inside and someone else is above, they can see all the comings and goings of those below. I am also like this. With the divine eye, I see the various beings, those who are born and those who die, those in good destinies and those in bad destinies, those with good appearances and those with bad appearances, those who are good and those who are ugly, according to the seeds they have planted through their actions.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「犹如屋舍有两门相对，有人在中住，复有人在上住，观其下出入行来皆悉知见。我亦如是。以天眼观众生之类，生者、终者，善趣、恶趣，善色、恶色，若好、若丑，随行所种，皆悉知之。

If there are sentient beings who perform good actions with their body, speech, and mind, do not slander the sages, practice the Dharma of right views and are in accord with right views, then when their body perishes and their life ends, they will be reborn in the good realms of the heavens. This is called sentient beings practicing goodness. If there are sentient beings who practice these good dharmas and do not engage in unwholesome actions, then when their body perishes and their life ends, they will be reborn among humans. If there are sentient beings who engage in unwholesome actions with their body, speech, and mind, and do unwholesome deeds, then after their body perishes and their life ends, they will be reborn in the realm of hungry ghosts. If there are sentient beings who engage in unwholesome actions with their body, speech, and mind, slander the sages, and are in accord with wrong views, then after their body perishes and their life ends, they will be reborn in the animal realm. If there are sentient beings who engage in unwholesome actions with their body, speech, and mind, do unwholesome deeds, slander the sages, then after their body perishes and their life ends, they will be reborn in the hells.

「若复有众生，身行善，口行善，意行善，不诽谤贤圣，行正见法与等见相应，身坏命终，生善处天上，是谓名众生行善。若复有众生，行此善法不造恶行，身坏命终，来生人中。若复有众生，身、口、意行恶，造不善行，命终之后，生饿鬼中。或复有众生，身、口、意行恶，诽谤贤圣与邪见相应，命终之后，生畜生中。或复有众生，身、口、意行恶，造不善行，诽谤贤圣，命终之后，生地狱中。

At that time, the jailers will bring the criminal before King Yama and say, 'Great King, you should know that this person committed evil deeds with their body and mind in their previous life. After their body perishes and their life ends, they will be reborn in this hell. Great King, please examine this person's crimes and punish them accordingly.' Then King Yama will privately ask the person about their crimes and say, 'Tell me, man, didn't you see people being born as humans in your previous life? When they were in the womb, they were extremely distressed and in great pain. When they grew up, they were nurtured and breastfed, and their bodies were bathed.' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I did see it.' King Yama will say, 'Tell me, man, don't you know the essential practice of birth? The body, speech, and mind should cultivate good practices.' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, as you instruct. But I was foolish and deluded, and did not distinguish between good and bad practices.' King Yama will say, 'As you say, the matter is no different. I also know that you did not perform good deeds with your body, speech, and mind. But today, I will investigate your crimes of negligence. This is not done by your parents, nor by the king or ministers. You committed the crimes yourself, and now you will receive the retribution.

「是时，狱卒将此罪人示阎罗王，并作是说：『大王当知，此人前世身意行恶，作诸恶行已，生此地狱中。大王！当观此人以何罪治？』是时，阎罗王渐与彼人私问其罪，告彼人曰：『云何，男子！汝本前世为人身时，不见人有生者得作人身，处胎之时极为困厄，痛实难处，及其长大，将养乳哺，沐浴身体耶？』是时，罪人报曰：『实见，大王！』阎罗王曰：『云何，男子！汝自不知生法之要行耶？身、口、意法修诸善趣。』罪人报曰：『如是，大王！如大王教。但为愚惑，不别善行。』阎罗王曰：『如卿所说，其事不异，亦复知卿不作身、口、意行，但为今日，当究汝放逸罪行。非父母为，亦非国王、大臣之所为也，本自作罪，今自受报。』

"At that time, King Yama will first ask about the crimes and then order the punishment. Next, the second messenger will ask the person, 'When you were a human, didn't you see an old person whose body was extremely weak, walking with difficulty, their clothes dirty, their movements unsteady, breathing with groans, and no longer having the mind of youth?' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I have seen it.' King Yama will say, 'You should know for yourself: "I too have this law of aging. Being disgusted with old age, I should cultivate good practices."'" The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, at that time, I really did not believe it.' King Yama will say, 'I truly know that you did not perform good deeds with your body, speech, and mind. Now I will punish your crimes to prevent you from committing them again in the future. The evil you have done is not done by your parents, nor by the king, ministers, or people. You yourself have committed the crimes, and you will now receive the retribution.

「是时，阎罗王先问其罪，却勅治之。次复，第二天使问彼人曰：『汝本为人时，不见老人形体极劣，行步苦竭，衣裳垢坌，进止战掉，气息呻吟，无复少壮之心。』是时，罪人报曰：『如是，大王！我已见之。』阎罗王报曰：『汝当自知：「我今亦有此形老之法，为老所厌，当修其善行。」』罪人报曰：『如是，大王！尔时，实不信之。』阎罗王报曰：『我实知之，汝不作身、口、意行，今当治汝罪，使后不犯。汝所作恶，非父母为，亦非国王、大臣、人民所造，汝今自造其罪，当自受报。』

At that time, King Yama, having instructed with the second messenger, will again instruct with the third messenger, saying to the person, 'When you were a human, didn't you see a sick person lying in their own excrement and urine, unable to get up on their own?' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I have seen it.' King Yama will say, 'Tell me, man, don't you know for yourself: "I too will have this illness, and I cannot escape it"?' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I really did not see it.' King Yama will say, 'I also know that you are foolish and deluded, not understanding. Now I will punish your crimes to prevent you from committing them again in the future. This is not done by your parents, nor by the king, ministers, or people.

「是时，阎罗王以此第二天使约勅已，复以第三天使告彼人曰：『汝前身作人时，不见有病人乎？卧在屎尿之上，不能自起居。』罪人报曰：『如是。大王！我实见之。』阎罗王曰：『云何，男子！汝不自知：「我亦当有此病，不免此患？」』罪人报曰：『实尔，大王！我实不见之。』阎罗王曰：『我亦知之，愚惑不解，我今当处汝罪，使后不犯此之罪行。非父、非母为，亦非国王、大臣之所造作。』

At that time, King Yama, having instructed with the third messenger, will again instruct with the fourth messenger, saying to the person, 'Tell me, man, when the body is like a withered tree, the wind gone, the fire extinguished, and without any thought or feeling, surrounded by the five relatives crying out in grief, what do you think?' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I have seen it.' King Yama will say, 'Why didn't you think: "I too cannot escape this death"?' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I really did not realize it.' King Yama will say, 'I also believe that you did not realize this truth. Now I will punish your crimes to prevent you from committing them again in the future. This unwholesome crime is not done by your parents, nor by the king, ministers, or people.

「是时，阎罗王以此教勅已，复以第四天使告彼人曰：『云何，男子，身如枯木，风去火歇而无情想，五亲围遶而号哭？』罪人报曰：『如是。大王！我已见之。』阎罗王曰：『汝何故不作是念：「我亦当不免此死。」』罪人报曰：『实尔，大王！我实不觉。』阎罗王曰：『我亦信汝不觉此法，今当治汝使后不犯。此不善之罪，非父、非母为，亦非国王、大臣、人民所造，汝本自作，今自受罪。』

At that time, King Yama, having instructed with the fourth messenger, will again instruct with the fifth messenger, saying to the person, 'Tell me, when you were a human, didn't you see thieves breaking through walls and houses to steal others' wealth, or burning them with fire, or hiding them on roads? If you were caught by the king, they might cut off your hands and feet, or take you to be killed, or lock you in prison, or bind you in chains and take you to the marketplace, or make you carry sand and stones, or hang you upside down, or shoot you with arrows, or pour molten copper over your body, or roast you with fire, or peel your skin and make you eat it, or open your belly and stuff it with grass, or boil you in hot water, or cut you with a knife and crush your head with a wheel, or trample you to death with an elephant's foot, or impale your head until you die?' The criminal will reply, 'I have indeed seen it.' King Yama will say, 'Why did you privately steal others' things, knowing that there would be consequences? Why did you commit the crime?' The criminal will reply, 'Yes, Great King, I was indeed foolish and deluded.' King Yama will say, 'I also believe what you say. Now I will punish your crimes to prevent you from committing them again in the future. This crime is not done by your parents, nor by the king, ministers, or people.

「是时，阎罗王复以第五天使告彼人曰：『汝本为人时，不见有贼穿墙破舍，取他财宝，或以火烧，或道路隐藏。设当为国王所擒得者，或截手足，或取杀之，或闭著牢狱，或反缚诣市，或使负沙石，或取倒悬，或攒箭射，或以融铜而灌其身，或以火炙，或剥其皮还使食之，或开其腹以草擆之，或以汤中煮之，或以刀斫轮轹其头，或以象脚蹈杀，或著标头乃至于死？』罪人报曰：『我实见之。』阎罗王曰：『汝何故私盗他物？情知有事，何为犯之？』『如是，大王！我实愚惑。』阎罗王曰：『我亦信汝所言，今当治汝罪，使后不犯。此之罪者，非父母为，亦非国王、大臣、人民所为，自作其罪，还自受报。』

At that time, King Yama, having questioned the criminal, will order the jailers, 'Quickly take this person to the prison!' The jailers, having received the king's order, will take the criminal to the prison. The left side of the hell is extremely fiery, with iron walls, iron fences, and an iron floor. There are four city gates that are extremely foul-smelling, like excrement and urine that have stained the place. There are knife mountains and sword trees surrounding on all sides, and it is further covered with an iron net.

「是时，阎罗王以问罪已，便勅狱卒：『速将此人往著狱中！』是时，狱卒受王教令，将此罪人往著狱中。地狱左侧极为火然，铁城、铁廓，地亦铁作；有四城门极为臭处，如似屎尿所见染污，刀山、剑树围遶四面，复以铁疏笼而覆其上。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

The four walls and four city gates are wide and long
truly solid. Covered by an iron cage, there is no hope
of escape. At that time, on the iron ground, the fire
burns extremely fiercely. The walls are a hundred yojanas square, all
burning with one color. In the center are four pillars, truly
terrifying to behold. On the sword trees above, iron crows perch
The foul-smelling place is truly difficult to dwell in, seeing it
one's hair stands on end. The various frightening implements are divided
into sixteen sections.

「四壁四城门，　　广长实为牢，铁笼之所覆，　　求出无有期。彼时铁地上，　　火然极为炽，壁方百由旬，　　洞然一种色。中央有四柱，　　覩之实恐畏，及其剑树上，　　铁［口＊隽］乌所止。臭处实难居，　　覩之衣毛竖，种种之畏器，　　隔子有十六。

Bhikṣus, you should know that at that time, the jailers beat that person with various kinds of painful implements. When that criminal raised his foot to step into the prison, the flesh and blood were all gone, leaving only bones. At that time, the jailers took that criminal and again chopped up his body with sharp axes. The pain was immeasurable, and he could not die. He must wait until his crimes are exhausted before he can escape; he must make sure to eliminate all the evil karma he created in the human realm before he can be released.

「比丘当知，是时，狱卒以若干苦痛［歹＊支］打此人。若彼罪人举脚著狱中时，血肉斯尽，唯有骨在。是时，狱卒将此罪人，复以利斧斫其形体，苦痛难计，求死不得。要当罪灭之后，尔乃得脱；彼于人间所作罪业，要使除尽，后乃得出。

At that time, the jailers led that criminal up and down the sword tree. When the criminal was on the tree, he was eaten by the iron crows, who pecked at his head to eat the brain, or took his hands and feet to break the bones and eat the marrow. However, the crime was not yet finished. If the crime was finished, he could then be released.

「是时，彼狱卒将此罪人，缘刀剑树，或上或下。是时，罪人以在树上，便为此铁［口＊隽］乌所食，或啄其头，取脑食之；或取手脚，打骨取髓。然罪未毕，若罪毕者，然后乃出。

At that time, the jailers took that criminal and made him sit on a hot copper pillar. Because he enjoyed licentiousness in his previous life, he incurred this crime. The crime pursued him, and he could not escape. At that time, the jailers pulled the tendons from his heels to his neck and dragged him forward; or made him ride in a cart, advancing and retreating without control. The suffering he endured was immeasurable. He must wait until his crimes are exhausted before he can be released.

「是时，狱卒取彼罪人使抱热铜柱坐。前世时，喜婬泆故，故致此罪；为罪所追，终不得脱。是时，狱卒从脚跟拔筋，乃至项中而前挽之；或使车载，或进、或退不得自在，其中受苦不可称计。要当使罪灭，然后乃出。

At that time, the jailers took that criminal and placed him on a burning mountain, driving him up and down. At that time, he was completely burned to a crisp before he could be released. At that time, the criminal, for this reason, sought death but could not find it. He must wait until his crimes are exhausted before he can be released.

「是时，狱卒取彼罪人，著火山上驱使上下。是时，极为烂尽，然后乃出。是时，罪人由此因缘求死不得，要当使罪除尽，然后乃出。

At that time, the jailers again took the criminal, pulled out his tongue and threw it behind his back. The suffering he endured was immeasurable, and he sought death but could not find it. At that time, the jailers again took the criminal and placed him on a mountain of swords, either cutting off his legs, head, or hands. He must wait until his crimes are exhausted before he can be released.

「是时，狱卒复取罪人，拔其舌掷著背后，于中受苦不可称计，求死不得。是时，狱卒复取罪人著刀山上，或断其脚，或断其头，或断其手。要当使罪灭，然后乃出。

At that time, the jailers again covered the criminal's body with hot iron sheets, like wearing clothes when alive. At that time, the pain was unbearable, all due to greed and desire, which led to this crime. At that time, the jailers again made the criminal perform five kinds of labor, driving him to lie down, taking iron nails to nail his hands and feet, and then nailing his heart with a single nail. The suffering he endured was truly indescribable. He must wait until his crimes are exhausted before he can be released.

「是时，狱卒复以热大铁叶覆罪人身，如生时著衣，当时苦痛毒为难处，皆由贪欲之故，故致斯罪。是时，狱卒复使罪人，五种作役，驱令偃卧，取其铁钉钉其手足，复以一钉而钉其心，于中受斯苦痛，实不可言。要当使罪灭，然后乃出。

At that time, the jailers again took the criminal, turned him upside down, and lifted him into a cauldron. At that time, his body was completely rotten when it reached the bottom. When it returned to the top, it was also completely rotten. When it reached the four sides, it was also completely rotten. The pain was unbearable. It was like boiling small beans in a large pot, either up or down. Now this criminal was also like this, both visible and invisible, rotten. The suffering he endured was immeasurable. He must wait until his crimes are exhausted before he can be released.

「是时，狱卒复取罪人，颠倒其身，举著镬中。时，身至下皆悉烂尽。若还至上亦复烂尽。若至四边亦复烂尽，酸楚毒痛不可称计，现亦烂，不现亦烂，犹如大釜而煮小豆，或上或下。今此罪人亦复如是，现亦烂，不现亦烂，于中受苦不可称计，要当受罪毕，然后乃出。

"Bhikṣus, you should know that sometimes, after many years in that hell, the eastern gate opens. At that time, the criminals again go to the gate, and the gate naturally closes. At that time, those people all fall to the ground, suffering unbearable pain. Sometimes, they each blame each other, saying, 'It is because of you that we cannot leave the gate.'"

「比丘当知，或复有时彼地狱中，经历数年，东门乃开。是时，罪人复往趣门，门自然闭。是时，彼人皆悉倒地，于中受苦不可具称。或时，各各自称怨债：『我由汝等，不得出门。』」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

The foolish are always joyful, like the gods in the
Ābhāsvara Heaven; the wise are always sorrowful, like prisoners in jail.

「愚者常喜悦，　　亦如光音天，智者常怀忧，　　如似狱中囚。

At that time, the great hell had passed through hundreds of thousands of years, and the northern gate opened again. At that time, the sinners again went to the northern gate, and the gate closed again, requiring them to extinguish their sins before they could leave. At that time, those sinners again passed through hundreds of thousands of years before they could leave. The sins committed among humans must be completed. At that time, the jailers again took the sinners and chopped their bodies with iron axes, making them experience those sins again. Requiring them to exhaust the suffering of their sins, they could then leave.

「是时，大地狱中经历百千万岁，北门复开。是时，罪人复向北门，门便复闭，要当使罪灭，然后乃出。是时，彼罪人复经数百万岁，乃复得出，人中所作罪，要当使毕。是时，狱卒复取罪人，以铁斧斫罪人身，经尔许之罪，使令更之。要当使罪苦毕尽，然后乃出。

Bhikṣus should know that sometimes the eastern gate opens again. At that time, the sentient beings again go to the eastern gate, but the gate closes again and they cannot leave. Even if they can leave, there is a large mountain outside that they go to. When they enter the mountain, they are crushed between two mountains like sesame oil, suffering unbearably. They must exhaust their suffering before they can leave. At that time, those sinners can proceed further and encounter the hot ash hell, which is tens of thousands of yojanas long and wide, where they suffer unbearably. They must exhaust their karmic causes before they can leave. Proceeding further, there is the knife-stabbing hell. At that time, the sinners again enter this knife-stabbing hell, and a strong wind arises, destroying the sinners' bodies, sinews, and bones, causing them to suffer unbearably. They must exhaust their sins before they can leave.

「比丘当知，或复有时彼东门复更一开。是时，彼众生复诣东门，门复自闭而不得出；设复得出，外复有大山，而往趣之，彼入山中，为两山所压犹如压麻油，于中受苦不可称计。要当苦尽，然后乃出。尔时，彼罪人转得前进，复值热灰地狱，纵广数千万由旬，于中受苦不可称计。要当毕其罪原，然后乃出。转复前进，次有刀刺地狱。是时，罪人复入此刀刺地狱中，便有大风起，坏此罪人身体筋骨，于中受苦不可称计。要当罪灭，然后乃出。

Next, there is the great hot ash hell. At that time, the sinners again enter this great hot ash hell, their bodies melting and suffering immeasurably. They must exhaust their sins before they can leave. At that time, although the sinners can leave this hot ash hell, they again encounter the sword hell, which is tens of thousands of miles long and wide. At that time, the sinners enter this sword hell, suffering unbearably. They must exhaust their sins before they can leave.

「次复，有大热灰地狱。是时，罪人复入此大热灰地狱中，形体融烂，受苦无量。要当使罪灭，然后乃出。是时，罪人虽得出此热灰地狱，复值刀剑地狱，纵广数千万里。是时，罪人入此刀剑地狱中，于中受苦不可称计，要当使罪灭，然后乃出。

Next, there is the boiling excrement hell, where tiny worms enter the bones and marrow to eat the person. Although they can leave this hell, they encounter jailers ahead. At that time, the jailers ask the sinners, 'Where do you want to go? Where do you come from?' The sinners reply, 'We do not know where we come from, nor do we know where we should go. But we are extremely hungry and thirsty now, and want to eat.' The jailers say, 'We will provide for you.' At that time, the jailers make the sinners lie down and give them a large hot iron ball to swallow. The sinners suffer unbearably. When the hot iron ball passes through the mouth, the intestines and stomach are burned, causing immeasurable suffering. They must exhaust their sins before they can leave.

「次复，有沸屎地狱，中有细虫，入骨彻髓食此人，虽得出此地狱，前值狱卒。是时，狱卒问罪人曰：『卿等欲何所至？为从何来？』罪人报曰：『我等不知所从来处，亦复不知当何所至。但我等今日极为饥困，意欲须食。』狱卒报曰：『我等当相供给。』是时，狱卒取罪人仰卧，取大热铁丸，使罪人吞之，然罪人受苦不可称计。是时，热铁丸从口下过，肠胃烂尽，受苦难量。要当使罪灭，然后乃出。

However, the sinners cannot bear this pain and return to the boiling excrement hell, sword hell, and great hot ash hell, going through these hells again. At that time, those beings cannot bear the suffering and turn back to the boiling excrement hell. At that time, the jailers say to those beings, 'Where do you want to go? Where do you come from?' The sinners reply, 'We do not know where we come from, nor do we know where we should go.' The jailers ask, 'What do you need now?' The sinners reply, 'We are extremely thirsty and want to drink water.' At that time, the jailers make the sinners lie down and pour molten copper into their mouths, causing it to pass through. The suffering they experience is immeasurable. They must exhaust their sins before they can leave.

「然彼罪人不堪受此苦痛，还复入热屎地狱、刀剑地狱、大热灰地狱，还来经尔许地狱。是时，彼众生不堪受苦，还回头至热屎地狱中。是时，狱卒语彼众生曰：『卿等欲何所至？为从何来？』罪人报曰：『我等不能自知为从何所来，今复不知当何所至。』狱卒问曰：『今须何物？』罪人报曰：『我等极渴，欲须水饮。』是时，狱卒取罪人仰卧，融铜灌口，使令下过，于中受罪不可具计，要当使罪灭，然后乃出。是时，彼人不堪受此苦，还入沸屎地狱、剑树地狱、热灰地狱，还入大地狱中。

Bhikṣus should know that at that time, the suffering of the sinners was immeasurable. Even if the sinners saw forms with their eyes, their minds were not delighted. Even if they heard sounds, smelled fragrances, tasted flavors, or had their bodies touched, their minds were always angry. The reason for this was that they did not create the results of good conduct in the past, but always committed evil karma, thus incurring these sins.

「比丘当知，尔时，罪人苦痛，难可称计，设彼罪人眼见色者，心不爱乐，设复闻声、嗅香、知味、身更细滑、意知法，皆起瞋恚。所以然者，由本不作善行之报，恒作恶业，故致斯罪。

"At that time, King Yama instructed those sinners, 'You have not obtained good benefits. In the past, you received human blessings in the human realm, but your bodily, verbal, and mental conduct did not accord with it. You did not give generously, be kind and loving, benefit others, or benefit equally. For this reason, you now suffer this pain. This evil conduct was not done by your parents, nor was it done by the king or ministers. All beings whose bodies, speech, and minds are pure, without defilement, are like the Heaven of Brahmā's Assembly. All beings who commit evil deeds are like those in hell. Your bodies, speech, and minds are impure, thus causing these sins.' Bhikṣus should know that King Yama then thought, 'When will I be free from this suffering and be reborn as a human? Having obtained a human body, I will then be able to leave home, shave my beard and hair, wear the three Dharma robes, leave home, and study the path.' King Yama still had this thought. How much more so for you who have now obtained a human body and become monks! Therefore, bhikṣus, you should always be mindful of practicing bodily, verbal, and mental conduct, without any deficiency; you should eliminate the five fetters and cultivate the five faculties. Thus, bhikṣus, you should train in this way."

「是时，阎罗王勅彼罪人曰：『卿等不得善利，昔在人中受人中福，身、口、意行不与相应，亦不惠施、仁爱、利人、等利，以是之故，今受此苦。此之恶行，非父母为，亦非国王、大臣之所为也。诸有众生身、口、意清净，无有沾污，如似光音天；诸有众生作诸恶行，如似地狱中。卿等身、口、意不净，故致斯罪。』比丘当知，阎罗王便作是说：『我当何日脱此苦难，于人中生？已得人身，便得出家，剃除须发，著三法衣，出家学道。』阎罗王尚作是念，何况汝等今得人身，得作沙门！是故，诸比丘！常当念行身、口、意行，无令有缺；当灭五结，修行五根。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
