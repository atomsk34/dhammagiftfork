EĀ 32.7

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

32.7 （七）文荼

Parallel to: AN 5.50

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Venerable Narada was staying in the bamboo grove of a householder in the country of Vārāṇasī.

一时，尊者那罗陀在波罗梨国长者竹林中。

At that time, King Mandhātu's chief queen passed away. The king loved and cherished her deeply, never leaving her out of his mind. Then a man came to the king and said, "Great King, you should know that the chief queen has now passed away."

尔时，文茶王第一夫人而取命终，王甚爱敬，念未曾去怀。是时，有一人至王所，而白王言：「大王当知，第一夫人今已命终。」

When the king heard that his queen had passed away, he was immediately filled with sorrow. He told the man who had come, "Quickly carry the dead body of the queen to the sesame oil and let me see it."

是时，王闻夫人无常，即怀愁忧，告来人曰：「汝速舆夫人死尸，著麻油中，使我见之。」

At that time, the man received the king's order and went to place the queen's body in the sesame oil.

是时，彼人受王教命，即往持夫人身，著麻油中。

At that time, the king heard that his queen had passed away and was extremely sorrowful. He did not eat or drink, did not uphold the Dharma, and did not attend to royal affairs.

尔时，王闻夫人逝丧，极怀愁恼，不食不饮，复不持法，不理王事。

At that time, there was a person named Good Thought who always held a sword for the great king. He said to the great king, "Great King, you should know that there is a śramaṇa named Nārada in this country who has attained arahantship, has great supernatural powers, is knowledgeable and wise, is skilled in all matters, has eloquent courage and wisdom, and always speaks with a smile. I wish that the King would go there to listen to his Dharma teachings. If the King hears the Dharma, he will no longer be sorrowful, worried, or distressed."

是时，左右有一人名曰善念，恒与大王执剑，白大王曰：「大王当知，此国界中有沙门，名那罗陀，得阿罗汉，有大神足，博识多知，无事不练，辩才勇慧，语常含笑。愿王当往至彼，听其说法。若王闻法，无复愁忧苦恼。」

The king replied, "Excellent! Excellent! You have spoken well. Now, Good Thought, go first and speak to that śramaṇa. The reason is that when a wheel-turning sage king wishes to go somewhere, he should first send someone. It is not right to go without first sending a message."

王报之曰：「善哉！善哉！善说此语。汝今，善念！先往语彼沙门。所以然者，夫转轮圣王欲有所至，先当遣人，不先遣信而至者，此事不然。」

At that time, Good Thought replied, "As the great king commands." He then accepted the king's instructions and went to the elder's bamboo grove, where he approached Nārada, bowed his head at his feet, and stood to one side.

时，善念报曰：「如大王教。」即受王教，往至长者竹园中，至那罗陀所，头面礼足，在一面立。

At that time, Good Thought said to Venerable Nārada, "Venerable one, you should know that the king's queen has now passed away. Because of this suffering, she has not eaten or drunk, and she has also neglected the affairs of the kingdom. She now wishes to come and pay her respects to your countenance. I only pray that you will kindly teach the Dharma to the king, so that he may be free from further sorrow and suffering."

尔时，善念白尊者那罗陀言：「尊当知之，大王夫人今已命终，缘此苦恼，不食不饮，亦复不治王法、国事，今欲来觐省尊颜，唯愿善与说法，使王无复愁苦。」

Nārada replied, "If she wishes to come, now is the right time."

那罗陀报言：「欲来者，今正是时。」

At that time, Good Thought, having received the instruction, bowed her head at his feet and then withdrew. She went to the king and said, "I have already informed the śramaṇa. The king should know."

是时，善念已闻教令，即头面礼足，便退而去。往至王所，而白王言：「已语沙门！王宜知之。」

At that time, he immediately ordered Good Thought, "Quickly prepare the jeweled carriage with feathered wheels. I now wish to go and meet the śramaṇa."

是时，即勅善念：「汝速严驾宝羽之车，吾今欲往与沙门相见。」

At that time, Good Thought immediately prepared the jeweled carriage with feathered wheels and said to the king, "The carriage is ready. The king may know the time."

是时，善念即严驾宝羽之车，前白王言：「严驾已办，王知是时。」

At that time, the king rode in the jeweled carriage with feathered wheels, left the city and went to where Nārada was. He walked into the bamboo grove of the elder. According to the law of the king, he removed the five ornaments and put them aside. He went to where Nārada was, bowed his head at his feet, and sat to one side.

是时，王乘宝羽之车，出城诣那罗陀所，步入长者竹园中。夫人王法，除五威容，舍著一面，至那罗陀所，头面礼足，在一面坐。

At that time, Nārada told the king, "Great King, you should know that the Dharma of dreams and illusions arises from sorrow and worry. The Dharma of bubbles and snowballs also arises from sorrow and worry. One should not give rise to sorrow and worry because of the Dharma of flowers. Why is this so? Now there are five things that are most unattainable, as spoken by the Tathāgata. What are the five? First, things that should end, wanting them not to end - this is unattainable. Second, things that should cease, wanting them not to cease - this is unattainable. Third, the law of aging, wanting to avoid aging - this is unattainable. Fourth, the law of sickness, wanting to avoid sickness - this is unattainable. Fifth, the law of death, wanting to avoid death - this is unattainable. These are called, Great King, the five things that are most unattainable, as spoken by the Tathāgata."

是时，那罗陀告王曰：「大王当知，梦幻之法起于愁忧，泡沫之法及以雪揣而起愁忧，亦复不可以华法之想起于愁忧。所以然者，今有五事最不可得，是如来之所说也。云何为五？夫物应尽，欲使不尽者，此不可得；夫物应灭，欲使不灭者，此不可得；夫老之法，欲使不老者，此不可得；复次，病法，欲使不病者，此不可得；复次，死法，欲使不死者，此不可得。是谓，大王！此有五事最不可得，是如来之所说。」

At that time, Nārada spoke this verse:

尔时，那罗陀便说此偈：

One does not attain blessings through sorrow and distress. If
one has sorrow and distress, external conditions can take advantage
If the wise contemplate this, external enemies will have sorrow
but will not be able to take advantage. With proper
deportment and etiquette, one should be generous without stinginess. One
should seek this means to attain great benefits. If it
cannot be attained, I and the people will have no
sorrow and no suffering. How does one know the retribution of practice?

「不以愁忧恼，　　而获其福祐，设有怀愁忧，　　外境得其便。若使有智者，　　终不思惟是，外敌便有愁，　　而不得其便。威仪礼节具，　　好施无悋心，当求此方便，　　使获其大利。设使不可得，　　我及彼众人，无愁便无患，　　行报知如何。

Moreover, Your Majesty should know that when something that should be lost is lost, one is already sorrowful, worried, suffering, and distressed, with pain that is indescribable. What I love has been lost today. This is called losing what should be lost, and giving rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress, with pain that is indescribable. This is called, Your Majesty! the first thorn of sorrow, which stains the mind. Ordinary people have this, but do not know where birth, old age, sickness, and death come from.

「又大王当知，应失之物便失之，已失便愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。我所爱者今日已失，是谓失物便失之，于中起愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。是谓，大王！第一愁刺，染著心意，凡夫之人有此法，不知生、老、病、死之所来处。

Moreover, one hears that what the noble disciples should lose is lost. At that time, that person does not give rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress. One should study like this: What I have lost is not unique to me alone. Others also have this. If I give rise to sorrow and worry about it, this is not appropriate. It may cause my relatives to be sorrowful and worried, and my enemies to rejoice. Food will not be digested, and I will become ill. The body will be hot. Because of this, I will die. At that time, one can remove the thorn of sorrow and fear, and be free from birth, old age, sickness, and death. There will be no more calamities, suffering, and afflictions.

「又复闻贤圣弟子所应失物便失之，是时彼人不起愁、忧、苦、恼，当作是学：我今所失非独一已，余人亦有此法；设我于中起愁忧者，此非其宜，或能使亲族起愁忧，怨家欢喜，食不消化，即当成病，身体烦热，由此缘本，便致命终。尔时，便能除去忧畏之刺，便脱生、老、病、死，无复灾患苦恼之法。

Moreover, Your Majesty should know that when something that should be extinguished is extinguished, one is already sorrowful, worried, suffering, and distressed, with pain that is indescribable. What I love has been extinguished today. This is called extinguishing what should be extinguished, and giving rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress, with pain that is indescribable. This is called, Your Majesty! the second thorn of sorrow, which stains the mind. Ordinary people have this, but do not know where birth, old age, sickness, and death come from.

「复次，大王！应灭之物便灭之，已灭便愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。我所爱者今日已灭，是谓灭物便灭之，于中起愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。是谓，大王！第二愁刺，染著心意。凡夫之人有此法，不知生、老、病、死之所来处。

Moreover, one hears that what the noble disciples should extinguish is extinguished. At that time, that person does not give rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress. One should study like this: What I have extinguished is not unique to me alone. Others also have this. If I give rise to sorrow and worry about it, this is not appropriate. It may cause my relatives to be sorrowful and worried, and my enemies to rejoice. Food will not be digested, and I will become ill. The body will be hot. Because of this, I will die. At that time, one can remove the thorn of sorrow and fear, and be free from birth, old age, sickness, and death. There will be no more calamities, suffering, and afflictions.

「又复闻贤圣弟子所应灭物便灭之，是时彼人不起愁、忧、苦、恼，当作是学：我今所灭非独一已，余人亦有此法，设我于中起愁忧者，此非其宜。或能使亲族起忧，怨家欢喜，食不消化，即当成病，身体烦热，由此缘本，便致命终。尔时，便能除去忧畏之刺，便脱生、老、病、死，无复灾患苦恼之法。

Moreover, Your Majesty should know that when something that should grow old grows old, one is already sorrowful, worried, suffering, and distressed, with pain that is indescribable. What I love has grown old today. This is called growing old what should grow old, and giving rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress, with pain that is indescribable. This is called, Your Majesty! the third thorn of sorrow and worry, which stains the mind. Ordinary people have this, but do not know where birth, old age, sickness, and death come from.

「复次，大王！应老之物便老，已老便愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。我所爱者今日已老，是谓老物便老，于中起忧、愁、苦、恼，痛不可言。是谓，大王！第三愁忧之刺，染著心意，凡夫之人有此法，不知生、老、病、死之所来处。

Moreover, one hears that what the noble disciples should grow old is grown old. At that time, that person does not give rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress. One should study like this: What I have grown old is not unique to me alone. Others also have this. If I give rise to sorrow about it, this is not appropriate. It may cause my relatives to be sorrowful and worried, and my enemies to rejoice. Food will not be digested, and I will become ill. The body will be hot. Because of this, I will die. At that time, one can remove the thorn of sorrow and fear, and be free from birth, old age, sickness, and death. There will be no more calamities, suffering, and afflictions.

「又复闻贤圣弟子所应老物便老，是时彼人不起愁、忧、苦、恼，当作是学：我今所老非独一已，余人亦有此法，设我于中起愁者，此非其宜。或能亲族起忧，怨家欢喜，食不消化，即当成病，身体烦热，由此缘本，便致命终。尔时，便能除去忧畏之刺，脱生、老、病、死，无复灾患苦恼之法。

Next, Your Majesty should know that when something that should be sick is sick, one is already sorrowful, worried, suffering, and distressed, with pain that is indescribable. What I love is sick today. This is called being sick what should be sick, and giving rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress, with pain that is indescribable. This is called, Your Majesty! the fourth thorn of sorrow and worry, which stains the mind. Ordinary people have this, but do not know where birth, old age, sickness, and death come from.

「次复，大王！应病之物便病，已病便愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。我所爱者今日以病，是谓病物便病，于中起愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。是谓，大王！第四愁忧之刺，染著心意。凡夫之人有此法，不知生、老、病、死之所来处。

Moreover, one hears that what the noble disciples should be sick is sick. This is called that person not giving rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress. One should study like this: What I am sick is not unique to me alone. Others also have this. If I give rise to sorrow and worry about it, this is not appropriate. It may cause my relatives to be sorrowful and worried, and my enemies to rejoice. Food will not be digested, and I will become ill. The body will be hot. Because of this, I will die. At that time, one can remove the thorn of sorrow and fear, and be free from birth, old age, sickness, and death. There will be no more calamities, suffering, and afflictions.

「又复闻贤圣弟子所应病物便病，是谓彼人不起愁、忧、苦、恼，当作是学：我今所病非独一已，余人亦有此法，设我于中起愁忧者，此非其宜。或能使亲族起忧，怨家欢喜，食不消化，即当成病，身体烦热，由此缘本，便致命终。尔时，便能除去愁畏之刺，脱生、老、病、死，无复灾患苦恼之法。

Furthermore, Your Majesty should know that when something that should die dies, it is called a dead thing. One gives rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress, with pain that is indescribable. This is called, Your Majesty! the fifth thorn of sorrow and worry, which stains the mind. Ordinary people have this, but do not know where birth, old age, sickness, and death come from.

「复次，大王！应死之物便死，已死是谓死物，于中起愁、忧、苦、恼，痛不可言。是谓，大王！第五愁忧之刺，染著心意。凡夫之人有此法，不知生、老、病、死之所来处。

Moreover, one hears that what the noble disciples should die is dead. At that time, that person does not give rise to sorrow, worry, suffering, and distress. One should study like this: What I am dead is not unique to me alone. Others also have this. If I give rise to sorrow and worry about it, this is not appropriate. It may cause my relatives to be sorrowful and worried, and my enemies to rejoice. Food will not be digested, and I will become ill. The body will be hot. Because of this, I will die. At that time, one can remove the thorn of sorrow and fear, and be free from birth, old age, sickness, and death. There will be no more calamities, suffering, and afflictions.

「又复闻贤圣弟子所应死者便死，是时彼人不起忧愁苦恼，当作是学：我今死者非独一已，余人亦有此法，我设于中起愁忧者，此非其宜。或能使亲族起忧，怨家欢喜，食不消化，即当成病，身体烦热，由此缘本，便致命终。尔时，便能除去愁畏之刺，脱生、老、病、死，无复灾患苦恼之法。」

At that time, the great king said to Venerable Narada: "What is the name of this Dharma? How should it be practiced?"

是时，大王白尊者那罗陀曰：「此名何法？当云何奉行？」

Narada said: "This sutra is called 'Eliminating the Afflictions of Sorrow'. One should remember to practice it."

那罗陀言：「此经名曰除忧之患，当念奉行。」

The king replied: "Indeed, as you have said, it removes sorrow and worry. Because, having heard this Dharma, all my sorrows and sufferings are forever eliminated today. If the Venerable has any instructions, please come to the palace often. I will provide for you and let the people of the country enjoy endless blessings. I only wish that the Venerable will widely expound this Dharma, preserving it for the world, so that the four groups of followers will be at ease for a long time. I now take refuge in the Venerable Narada."

时王报言：「实如所说，除去愁忧。所以然者，我闻此法已，所有愁苦今日永除。若尊者有所教勅者，数至宫中，当相供给，使国土人民长受福无穷。唯愿尊者广演此法，永存于世，使四部之众长夜安隐，我今自归尊者那罗陀。」

Narada said: "Great King! Do not take refuge in me, but take refuge in the Buddha."

那罗陀曰：「大王！莫自归我，当自归于佛。」

The king asked: "Where is the Buddha now?"

时王问曰：「今佛在何处？」

Narada said: "Great King, you should know that in the great country of Kapilavastu, a prince of the Sakya clan was born to a wheel-turning sage king. His name is Siddhattha. He left home to study the path and has now attained Buddhahood. He is called Sakyamuni. You should take refuge in him."

那罗陀曰：「大王当知，迦毘罗卫大国，转轮圣王种出于释姓，彼王有子，名曰悉达，出家学道，今自致成佛，号释迦文，当自归彼。」

The king asked again: "Where is he now? How far is it from here?"

大王复问：「今在何方？去此几所？」

Narada said: "The Tathāgata has already entered nirvana."

那罗陀曰：「如来已取涅槃。」

The king said: "How quickly the Tathāgata entered nirvana! If he were still alive, I would travel many tens of thousands of yojanas to visit him."

大王曰：「如来取灭度何其速疾？若当在世者，经数千万由旬，当往觐省。」

At that time, he immediately rose from his seat, knelt on one knee with palms joined, and said: "I take refuge in the Tathāgata, the Dharma, and the Sangha of monks. For as long as I live, I will listen and be an upasaka. I will no longer take life. The affairs of the country are many. I now wish to return to the palace."

是时，即从坐起，长跪叉手，而作是说：「我自归如来、法及比丘僧，尽形寿听为优婆塞，不复杀生。国事猥多，今欲还宫。」

Narada said: "Now is the right time." At that time, the king rose from his seat, bowed at the feet, circumambulated three times, and departed.

那罗陀曰：「今正是时。」是时，王从坐起，礼足遶三匝而去。

At that time, King Mendhaka heard what Narada said and joyfully accepted it.

尔时，文茶王闻那罗陀所说，欢喜奉行。
