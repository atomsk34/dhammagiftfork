EĀ 33.1

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

Chapter 33 五王品第三十三

33.1 （一）五王品

Parallel to: SN 3.12 / SĀ 1149 / SĀ² 2.72

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the five great kings, with King Prasenajit at the head, were gathered in a garden pavilion, each discussing this matter. What are the five kings? Namely, King Prasenajit, King Virūḍhaka, King Udayana, King Ajātaśatru, and King Udayana.

尔时，五大国王波斯匿为首，集在园观之中，各作此论。云何为五王？所谓波斯匿王、毘沙王、优填王、恶生王、优陀延王。

At that time, the five kings gathered in one place and each discussed this matter: "Virtuous ones, you should know that the Tathāgata speaks of these five desires. What are the five? If the eyes see forms that are very lovable and cherished, hoped for by the world; if the ears hear sounds, the nose smells fragrances, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows smoothness, the Tathāgata speaks of these five desires. Among these five desires, which is the most wonderful? Is it wonderful to see forms with the eyes? Is it wonderful to hear sounds with the ears? Is it wonderful to smell fragrances with the nose? Is it wonderful to know tastes with the tongue? Is it wonderful to know smoothness with the body? Which of these five is the most wonderful?"

尔时，五王集在一处，各作此论：「诸贤当知，如来说此五欲。云何为五？若眼见色甚爱敬念，世人所希望；若耳闻声、鼻嗅香、舌知味、身知细滑，如来说此五欲。此五欲中何者最妙？为眼见色妙耶？为耳闻声妙耶？为鼻嗅香妙耶？为舌知味妙耶？为身知细滑妙耶？此五事何者为最妙？」

Among them, some kings said, "Form is the most wonderful." Some discussed, "Sound is the most wonderful." Some discussed, "Fragrance is the most excellent." Some discussed, "Taste is the most wonderful." Some discussed, "Smoothness is the most excellent." At that time, those who said form was wonderful were spoken by King Udayana. Those who said sound was wonderful were discussed by King Udayana. Those who said fragrance was wonderful were discussed by King Ajātaśatru. Those who said taste was wonderful were discussed by King Prasenajit. Those who said smoothness was wonderful were discussed by King Virūḍhaka. At that time, the five kings said to each other, "We have discussed these five desires together, but we still do not know which is the most wonderful?"

其中或有国王而作是说：「色最为妙。」或有作是论：「声最为妙。」或有作是论：「香最为胜。」或有作是论：「味最为妙。」或有作是论：「细滑为最胜。」是时，言色妙者，优陀延王之所说也。言声妙者，优填王之所论也。言香妙者，恶生王之所论也。言味妙者，波斯匿王之所论也。言细滑妙者，毘沙王之所论也。是时，五王各相谓言：「我等共论此五欲，然复不知何者为妙？」

At that time, King Prasenajit said to the four kings, "Now the Tathāgata is near the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. Let us all go together to where the World-Honored One is and ask about this meaning. If the World-Honored One gives any instructions, we shall all together uphold and practice them."

是时，波斯匿王语四王曰：「今如来近在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。我等尽共至世尊所，问斯义。若世尊有所教勅，当共奉行。」

At that time, the various kings, having heard King Prasenajit's words, then together led each other to where the World-Honored One was, bowed their heads at his feet, and sat to one side. At that time, King Prasenajit, with what they had discussed together about the five desires, fully informed the Tathāgata.

是时，诸王闻波斯匿王语已，便共相将至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。是时，波斯匿王以所共论五欲者，具白如来。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the five kings, saying, "The discussions of the various kings are each appropriate to the time. The reason is this: When a person's nature and conduct are deeply attached to form, they see it without being satisfied. This person considers form to be the most wonderful and supreme, with nothing surpassing it. At that time, that person is not attached to sound, smell, taste, or the dharmas of smoothness and softness. Among the five desires, form is the most wonderful. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to sound, when they hear sound, they are extremely joyful and never satisfied. This person considers sound to be the most wonderful and supreme. Among the five desires, sound is the most wonderful. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to smell, when they smell it, they are extremely joyful and never satisfied. This person considers smell to be the most wonderful and supreme. Among the five desires, smell is the most wonderful. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to taste, when they know the taste, they are extremely joyful and never satisfied. This person considers taste to be the most wonderful and supreme. Among the five desires, taste is the most wonderful. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to smoothness and softness, when they obtain smoothness and softness, they are extremely joyful and never satisfied. This person considers smoothness and softness to be the most wonderful and supreme. Among the five desires, smoothness and softness are the most wonderful.

尔时，世尊告诸五王曰：「诸王所论各随时宜。所以然者，夫人性行深著色者，覩无厌足，此人于色最妙、最上，无复过者。尔时，彼人不著声、香、味、细滑之法，五欲之中色为最妙。若复有人性行著声，彼闻声已，极怀欢喜而无厌足，此人于声最妙、最上，五欲之中声最为妙。若复有人性行著香，彼闻香已，极怀欢喜而无厌足，此人于香最妙、最上，五欲之中香最为妙。若复有人性行著味，彼知味已，极怀欢喜而无厌足，此人于味最妙、最上，五欲之中味最为妙。若复有人性行著细滑，彼得细滑已，极怀欢喜而无厌足，此人于细滑最上、最妙，五欲之中细滑最为妙。

If there is a person whose mind is attached to form, at that time that person is not attached to sound, smell, taste, or the dharmas of smoothness and softness. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to sound, at that time that person is not attached to form, smell, taste, or the dharmas of smoothness and softness. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to smell, at that time that person is not attached to form, sound, taste, or the dharmas of smoothness and softness. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to taste, at that time that person is not attached to form, sound, smell, or the dharmas of smoothness and softness. If there is a person whose nature and conduct are attached to smoothness and softness, at that time that person is not attached to form, sound, smell, or taste.

「若复彼人心以著色，尔时彼人不著声、香、味、细滑之法；若复彼人性行著声，尔时彼人不著色、香、味、细滑之法；若复彼人性行著香，尔时彼人不著色、声、味、细滑之法；若复彼人性行著味，尔时彼人不著色、声、香、细滑之法；若复彼人性行著细滑，尔时彼人不著色、声、香、味之法。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

是时，世尊便说此偈：

When the mind is intensely passionate, what is desired can be
accomplished. Having obtained it, one is doubly joyful, with no doubt
about one's wishes. They obtain this desire, but the mind of
greed is not released. Taking this as joy, it is considered
the most wonderful. When listening to sounds, what is desired can
be accomplished. Having heard it, one is doubly joyful, with no
doubt about one's wishes. They obtain this sound, but the mind
of greed is not released. Taking this as joy, it is
considered the most wonderful. When smelling fragrances, what is desired can
be accomplished. Having smelled it, one is doubly joyful, with no
doubt about one's wishes. They obtain this fragrance, but the mind
of greed is not released. Taking this as joy, it is
considered the most wonderful. When obtaining tastes, what is desired can
be accomplished. Having obtained it, one is doubly joyful, with no
doubt about one's wishes. They obtain this taste, but the mind
of greed is not released. Taking this as joy, it is
considered the most wonderful. When obtaining smoothness and softness, what is
desired can be accomplished. Having obtained it, one is doubly joyful
with no doubt about one's wishes. They obtain this smoothness and
softness, but the mind of greed is not released. Taking this
as joy, it is considered the most wonderful.

「欲意炽盛时，　　所欲必可克，得已倍欢喜，　　所愿无有疑。彼以得此欲，　　贪欲意不解，以此为欢喜，　　缘之最为妙。若复听声时，　　所欲必可克，闻已倍欢喜，　　所愿无有疑。彼以得此声，　　贪之意不解，以此为欢喜，　　从之最为妙。若复嗅香时，　　所欲必可克，嗅已倍欢喜，　　所欲无有疑。彼以得此香，　　贪之意不解，以此为欢喜，　　从之最为妙。若复得味时，　　所欲必可克，得已倍欢喜，　　所欲无有疑。彼以得此味，　　贪之意不解，以此为欢喜，　　从之最为妙。若得细滑时，　　所欲必可克，得已倍欢喜，　　所欲无疑难。彼以得细滑，　　贪之意不解，以此为欢喜，　　从之最为妙。

Therefore, great king! If you say that form is wonderful, we should discuss it equally. The reason is that form has a taste. If form had no taste, beings would not be attached to it. Because it has a taste, form is considered the most wonderful among the five desires. However, form has faults. If form had no faults, beings would not be disgusted with it. Because it has faults, beings are disgusted with it. However, form has a way out. If form had no way out, these beings would not be able to escape the ocean of birth and death; because it has a way out, beings can reach the city of fearlessness, nirvana. Form is considered the most wonderful among the five desires.

「是故，大王！若言色妙者，当平等论之。所以然者，于色有气味。若色无味者，众生终不染著，以其有味故，五欲之中色为最妙。然色有过失。若当色无过失，众生则无厌患，以其有过失故，众生厌患之。然色有出要。若当色无出要者，此众生类不得出生死之海；以其出要故，众生得至无畏涅槃城中。五欲之中色为最妙。

However, great king! If you say that sound is wonderful, we should discuss it equally. The reason is that sound has a taste. If sound had no taste, beings would not be attached to it. Because it has a taste, sound is considered the most wonderful among the five desires. However, sound has faults. If sound had no faults, beings would not be disgusted with it. Because it has faults, beings are disgusted with it. However, sound has a way out. If sound had no way out, these beings would not be able to escape the ocean of birth and death; because it has a way out, beings can reach the city of fearlessness, nirvana. Sound is considered the most wonderful among the five desires.

「然复，大王！若言声妙者，当平等论之。所以然者，于声有气味故。若声无味者，众生终不染著；以其有味故，五欲之中声为最妙。然声有过失。若当声无过失，众生则无厌患；以其有过失故，众生厌患之。然声有出要。若当声无出要者，此众生类不得出生死之海；以其出要故，众生得至无畏涅槃城中。五欲之中声为最妙。

Great King, you should know that if you say that scent is wonderful, we should discuss it equally. The reason is that scent has a taste. If scent had no taste, beings would not be attached to it. Because it has a taste, scent is considered the most wonderful among the five desires. However, scent has faults. If scent had no faults, beings would not be disgusted with it. Because it has faults, beings are disgusted with it. However, scent has a way out. If scent had no way out, these beings would not be able to escape the ocean of birth and death; because it has a way out, beings can reach the city of fearlessness, nirvana. Scent is considered the most wonderful among the five desires.

「大王当知，若言香妙者，当平等论之。所以然者，于香有气味故。若香无气味者，众生之类终不染著；以其有味故，五欲之中香为最妙。然香有过失。若香无过失者，众生则不厌患；以其有过失故，众生厌患之。然香有出要。若当香无出要者，此众生类不得出生死之海；以其出要故，众生得至无畏涅槃城中。五欲之中香为最妙。

However, great king! If you say that taste is wonderful, we should discuss it equally. The reason is that taste has a taste. If taste had no taste, beings would not be attached to it. Because it has a taste, taste is considered the most wonderful among the five desires. However, taste has faults. If taste had no faults, beings would not be disgusted with it. Because it has faults, beings are disgusted with it. However, taste has a way out. If taste had no way out, these beings would not be able to escape the ocean of birth and death; because it has a way out, beings can reach the city of fearlessness, nirvana. Taste is considered the most wonderful among the five desires.

「然复，大王！若言味妙者，当平等论之。所以然者，于味有气味故。若味无气味者，众生之类终不染著；以其有气味故，五欲之中味为最妙。然味有过失。若当味无过失者，众生则不厌患；以其有过失故，众生厌患之。然味有出要。若当味无出要者，此众生类不得出生死之海；以其出要故，众生得至无畏涅槃城中。味为最妙。

However, great king! You should know that if you say that touch is wonderful, we should discuss it equally. The reason is that touch has a taste. If touch had no taste, beings would not be attached to it. Because it has a taste, touch is considered the most wonderful among the five desires. However, touch has faults. If touch had no faults, beings would not be disgusted with it. Because it has faults, beings are disgusted with it. However, touch has a way out. If touch had no way out, these beings would not be able to escape the ocean of birth and death; because it has a way out, beings can reach the city of fearlessness, nirvana. Touch is considered the most wonderful among the five desires.

「然复，大王当知，言细滑妙者，当平等论之。所以然者，于细滑无气味者，众生终不染著，以其有味故，五欲之中细滑为最妙。然细滑有过失。若细滑无过失者，众生之类则不厌患之；以其有过失故，众生厌患之。然细滑有出要。若当细滑无出要者，此众生类不得出生死之海；以其出要故，众生得至无畏涅槃城中。五欲之中细滑为最妙。是故，大王！所乐之处，心即染著。如是，大王！当作是知。」

At that time, the five kings heard what the Buddha said and joyfully accepted it.

尔时，五王闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
