EĀ 33.10

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

33.10 （一〇）枯树

Parallel to: AN 7.72 / MĀ 5

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Bright Light Pond in the country of Magadha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus, traveling and teaching among the people.

一时，佛在摩竭国光明池侧，尔时世尊与大比丘众五百人俱，在人间游化。

At that time, the World-Honored One saw from afar a large tree burning in flames. Having seen it, the Tathāgata went to another tree. Having arrived, he sat down at the foot of the tree. At that time, the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus: "What do you think, bhikṣus? Would you rather throw your body into this fire, or would you rather associate with an attractive woman?"

尔时，世尊遥见大树为火所烧，见已，如来更诣一树下。到已，就树下坐。尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「云何，比丘！宁持身投此火中？为宁与端正女人而共交游？」

At that time, the bhikṣus said to the Buddha: "We would rather associate with a woman than throw ourselves into this fire. The reason is that this fire is extremely hot and unbearable, and it would sever our life roots, causing immeasurable suffering."

尔时，诸比丘白佛言：「宁与女人共相交游，不投身入此火中。所以然者，此火毒热不可称计，断其命根，受苦无量。」

The World-Honored One said: "I now tell you, those who do not practice the way of śramaṇas but claim to be śramaṇas, those who do not practice the way of purity but claim to be pure, those who have not heard the true Dharma but claim to have heard it, those who lack pure and white Dharma - such people would rather throw themselves into this fire than associate with women. The reason is that they would rather endure this suffering than enter hell due to this sin and suffer immeasurable pain. What do you think, bhikṣus? Would you rather receive people's worship and respect, or would you rather have people take sharp swords to cut off your hands and feet?"

世尊告曰：「我今告汝等，非沙门行，言是沙门，非梵行人，言是梵行；不闻正法，言我闻法。无清白法，如是之人，宁投入此火中不与女人共相交游。所以然者，彼人宁受此苦痛，不以此罪入地狱中受苦无量。云何，比丘！宁受人礼拜恭敬？为宁使人取利剑断其手足？」

The bhikṣus replied: "We would rather receive respect and worship than have people cut off our hands and feet with swords. The reason is that the pain of having our hands and feet cut off is unbearable."

诸比丘对曰：「宁受恭敬礼拜，不使人以剑断其手足。所以然者，断其手足，痛不可称计。」

The World-Honored One said: "I now tell you, those who do not practice the way of śramaṇas but claim to be śramaṇas, those who do not practice the way of purity but claim to be pure, those who have not heard the true Dharma but claim to have heard it, those who lack pure and white Dharma, those who have severed the roots of goodness - such people would rather throw themselves onto this sharp sword than accept the respect of others without precepts. The reason is that this pain is only for a moment, while the suffering in hell is immeasurable. What do you think, bhikṣus? Would you rather receive people's clothing, or would you rather have your body wrapped in hot iron sheets?"

世尊告曰：「我今告汝等，非沙门行，言是沙门；非梵行人，言是梵行；不闻正法，言闻正法。无清白行，断善根。如是之人，宁投身受此利剑，不以无戒受他恭敬。所以然者，此痛斯须间耳；地狱苦痛不可称计。云何，比丘！宁受人衣裳？为宁以热铁鍱用缠裹身？」

The bhikṣus replied: "We would rather receive people's clothing than suffer this pain. The reason is that this poison-like pain is immeasurable."

诸比丘对曰：「宁以受人衣裳，不受此苦痛。所以然者，此毒痛不可称计。」

The World-Honored One said: "I now tell you again, those without precepts would rather have their bodies wrapped in hot iron sheets than receive people's clothing. The reason is that this pain is only for a moment, while the suffering in hell is immeasurable. What do you think, bhikṣus? Would you rather receive people's offerings of food, or would you rather swallow hot iron balls?"

世尊告曰：「我今重告汝，无戒之人，宁以热铁鍱缠裹其身，不受人衣裳。所以然者，此痛须臾间耳；地狱苦痛不可称计。云何，比丘！宁受人信施之食？为宁以吞热铁丸乎？」

The bhikṣus replied: "We would rather receive people's offerings of food than swallow hot iron balls. The reason is that this pain is unbearable."

诸比丘对曰：「宁受人信施之食，不吞热铁丸。所以然者，此痛不可堪处。」

The World-Honored One said: "I now tell you, it is better to swallow hot iron balls than to receive people's offerings without precepts. The reason is that swallowing hot iron balls is painful for a moment, but not receiving people's offerings without precepts. What do you think, bhikṣus? Would you rather receive people's bedding and furnishings, or would you rather lie on a bed of hot iron?"

世尊告曰：「我今语汝，宁吞热铁丸，不以无戒受人信施。所以然者，吞热铁丸，痛斯须间，不以无戒受他信施。云何，比丘！宁受人床敷之具？为宁卧热铁床上？」

The bhikṣus replied: "We, World-Honored One! would rather receive people's bedding and furnishings than lie on an iron bed. The reason is that this poisonous pain is immeasurable."

诸比丘对曰：「我等，世尊！宁受人床卧之具，不卧铁床上。所以然者，此之毒痛不可称计。」

The World-Honored One said: "That foolish person has no precepts and practices, yet claims to be a śramaṇa; has no pure conduct, yet claims to cultivate pure conduct. It is better to lie on an iron bed than to receive others' offerings without precepts. The reason is that lying on an iron bed is painful for a moment, but not receiving others' offerings without precepts.

世尊告曰：「彼愚痴之人无有戒行，非沙门言是沙门；无有梵行，言修梵行，宁当卧铁床上，不以无戒受他信施。何以故，卧铁床上，痛斯须间，不以无戒受他信施。

Bhikṣus, you should know that according to my observation today, the direction in which those without precepts go, even if they were to be in the human realm, their bodies would be emaciated, boiling blood would flow from their facial orifices, and they would then take their lives. They would not associate with women, not receive others' respect and virtue, not receive others' clothing, food, bedding, bedding, or medicine for illness and weakness. Because those without precepts do not consider the sins of the past and future lives, they do not care about the life root and receive this suffering. Those without precepts will be born in the three evil destinies. The reason is that it is caused by their evil actions.

「比丘当知，如我今日观无戒之人所趣向处，设彼人间者，形体枯悴，沸血从面孔出，便取命终，不与女人共相交游，不受人礼敬之德，不受人衣被、饭食、床敷卧具、病瘦医药，以其无戒之人，不观后世、前世之罪，不顾命根受此苦痛，无戒之人当生三恶趣中。所以然者，以其造恶行之所致也。

The Tathāgata observes today the direction in which those who practice good go. Even if they are poisoned or injured by a knife, they will end their own lives. Why is this? They wish to abandon this body and receive the blessings of the heavens. They will be born in good places, all due to the retribution of practicing good in past lives.

「如来今日观察善行人之所趣向，正使中毒，为刀所伤，自断命根。何以故？欲舍此身受天之福，当生善处，皆由前世受善行报之所致也。

Therefore, bhikṣus, you should remember to cultivate the body of precepts, the body of concentration, the body of wisdom, the body of liberation, and the body of the view of liberation. You should wish to attain its fruits in this world and attain the path of immortality. Even if you receive others' clothing, food, bedding, bedding, and medicine for illness and weakness, there will be no fault. Moreover, you should cause the donors to receive endless blessings. Thus, bhikṣus, you should train in this way.

「是故，比丘，当念修行戒身、定身、慧身、解脱身、解脱所见身。欲使今世获其果报，得甘露道，正使受人衣被、饮食、床敷卧具、病瘦医药而无过失，又使檀越受福无穷。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, when this Dharma was being spoken, sixty bhikṣus attained the exhaustion of taints and liberation of the mind. Sixty bhikṣus returned their Dharma robes and became lay people.

尔时，说此法时，六十比丘漏尽意解，六十比丘还舍法服而作白衣。

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

Five kings and Moonlight, Śiṃha, two kinds of fighting, two sweepings, two
practices, going and staying have two kinds of withered trees at the end.

五王及月光　　尸婆、二种鬪二扫、二行法　　去住有二种枯树最在后
