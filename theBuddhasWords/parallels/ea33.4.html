EĀ 33.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

33.4 （四）斗

Parallel to: AN 5.76 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are five kinds of warriors who appear in the world. What are the five? There is one person who puts on armor and takes up arms to enter the army and fight. When he sees the dust raised by the wind, he becomes terrified and does not dare enter the midst of the great army. This is called the first kind of person.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有五战鬪之人出现于世。云何为五？或有一人著铠持仗，入军战鬪，彼见风尘，便怀恐怖，不敢入彼大阵之中，是谓第一之人。

Furthermore, the second kind of warrior puts on armor and takes up arms to enter the army and fight. When he sees the dust raised by the wind, he does not become afraid. But when he hears the sound of drums being beaten, he becomes terrified. This is called the second kind of person.

「复次，第二战鬪之人著铠持仗，入军战鬪，彼见风尘，不生畏惧；但闻击鼓之音，便怀恐怖，是谓第二之人。

Furthermore, the third kind of person puts on armor and takes up arms to enter the army and fight. When he sees the dust raised by the wind, he does not become afraid. Even if he hears the sound of drums and horns, he does not become afraid. But if he sees a tall banner, he becomes terrified and is not able to fight. This is called the third kind of person.

「复次，第三之人著铠持仗，入军战鬪，彼见风尘，不生畏惧，设闻鼓角之声，不起畏惧；彼若见高幢，便怀恐怖，不堪战鬪，是谓第三之人。

Furthermore, the fourth kind of warrior puts on armor and takes up arms to enter the army and fight. When he sees the dust raised by the wind, he does not become afraid. When he hears the sound of drums and horns, he is not afraid. When he sees a tall banner, he is not afraid. Even if he is captured by others or his life is cut off, this is called the fourth kind of person.

「复次，第四战鬪之人著铠持仗，入军战鬪中，若见风尘，不起畏惧，若闻鼓角之音，复非恐惧，若见高幢，亦非怖畏；设为他所捉，或断命根，是谓第四之人。

Furthermore, the fifth kind of person puts on armor and takes up arms to enter the army and fight. He is able to destroy everything and expand the borders of the country. This is called the fifth kind of person appearing in the world.

「复次，第五有人著铠持仗，入军共鬪，彼尽能有所坏广接国界，是谓第五之人出现于世。

Bhikkhus, you should know that there are also five kinds of people who appear in the world. What are the five? There is a bhikkhu who lives in a village. He hears that there is a woman who is exceptionally beautiful, like a peach blossom in color. When it is time, he puts on his robe, takes his bowl, enters the village to beg for food, does not guard the sense-doors, does not protect body, speech, and mind, and when he sees a woman, he gives rise to lustful thoughts, abandons the precepts, and practices the ways of a layperson. Like the first person who, hearing the sound of raised dust, is not able to fight and becomes terrified, I say that this person is like that.

「比丘当知，今比丘亦有五种之人，出现于世间。云何为五？或有一比丘住村落中，彼闻有女人，端正无双，如桃华色。彼比丘到时，著衣持钵，入村乞食，不守根门，不护身、口、意法，彼若见女人便起欲意，还舍禁戒，习白衣法。如彼初人闻扬尘之声，不堪战鬪，便怀恐怖，我由是故而说此人。

Furthermore, there is a bhikkhu who lives in a village. He hears that there is a woman in the village who is exceptionally beautiful, with a face like a peach blossom in color. He then abandons the precepts and practices the ways of a layperson. Like the second fighter who, hearing only the sound of drums and horns, is not able to fight, it is the same for this person.

「复次，有比丘住在村落，彼闻村中有女人，端正无比，面如桃华色，而便舍戒，习白衣法。如彼第二鬪人，但闻鼓角之声，不堪战鬪，此亦如是。

Furthermore, there is a bhikkhu who lives in a village. He hears that there is a woman in that village. Having heard, he gives rise to lustful thoughts, but when he sees a woman, he does not give rise to lustful thoughts. He only plays and teases with women, and then abandons the precepts and practices the ways of a layperson. Like the third person who, seeing the banner from afar, becomes terrified and is not able to fight, for this reason I now speak of this person. This is called the third kind of fighter.

「复次，有比丘住在村落，闻有女人在彼村落。彼闻已，便起欲意，若见女人不起欲想；但共女人共相调戏，于中便舍禁戒，习白衣法，如彼第三人遥见幢已，便怀恐怖，不堪战鬪。由是故，今说此人，是谓第三战鬪之人。

Furthermore, there is a bhikkhu who lives in a village. That bhikkhu hears that there is a woman in the village. Having heard, he puts on his robes and takes his alms bowl to enter the village to beg for food. He does not guard his body, speech, and mind. He sees a woman who is exceptionally beautiful and gives rise to lustful thoughts. He either touches or strikes her with his hands, and then abandons the precepts and returns to being a layperson. Like the fourth fighter who is captured by the enemy in a large army and loses his life, for this reason I now speak of this person.

「复次，有比丘住在村落，彼比丘闻村中有女人。闻已，著衣持钵，入村乞食，不守护身、口、意，彼见女人端正无双，于中便起欲意，或与女人共相捻挃或手拳相加，便舍禁戒，还为白衣。如彼第四战鬪之人，在大军中为他所捉，丧失命根，由是之故，今说此人。

Furthermore, there is a bhikkhu who hears that there is a woman in a village who is rare in the world. Although he hears this, he does not give rise to lustful thoughts. That bhikkhu, at the time, puts on his robes and takes his alms bowl to enter the village to beg for food, and guards his body, speech, and mind. Although he sees a woman, he does not give rise to lustful thoughts, and has no wrong thoughts. Even if he speaks and interacts with a woman, he does not give rise to lustful thoughts, and has no wrong thoughts. Even if he touches or strikes her with his hands, at that time he gives rise to lustful thoughts, and his body, speech, and mind become inflamed. The lustful thoughts having become inflamed, he returns to the grove, goes to the elder bhikkhus, and tells them the cause and condition, saying: 'Venerable ones, you should know that I now have inflamed lustful thoughts, and am unable to restrain myself. I only wish that you would teach the Dharma to free me from the evil and impure aspects of lust.' At that time, the elder bhikkhus said: 'You should now contemplate where this lust arises from, and where it ceases. The Tathāgata has said that the removal of lust is done through the contemplation of impurity, and the practice of the path of contemplating impurity.'

「复次，有比丘闻村落中有女人，世之希有。彼虽闻此不起欲想，彼比丘到时，著衣持钵，入村乞食，而守护身、口、意。彼虽见女人，不起欲想，无有邪念；设共女言语往返，亦不起欲想，亦无邪念：设共女人共相捻挃，手拳相加，尔时便起欲想，身、口、意便炽盛。欲意已炽盛，还诣园中，至长老比丘所，以此因缘，向长老比丘说之：『诸贤当知，我今欲意炽盛，不能自禁制，唯愿说法，使脱欲之恶露不净！』是时，长老比丘告曰：『汝今当观此欲为从何生？复从何灭？如来所说，夫去欲者，以不净观除之，及修行不净观之道。』

At that time, the elder bhikkhus then spoke this verse:

「是时，长老比丘便说此偈言：

If one knows the inverted, one adds to the mind and it becomes inflamed
One should remove the inflamed mind, and the intention of desire will cease and rest.

「『设知颠倒者，　　加心而炽盛，当去诸炽心，　　欲意止休息。

"The wise should know that desire arises from thought. By giving rise to thoughts, desire arises. It may lead to harming oneself and others, causing various calamities and changes. In this present life, one will experience suffering. In future lives, one will suffer immeasurably. By eliminating desire, one does not harm oneself or others. In this present life, one will not experience suffering. Therefore, one should now eliminate thoughts. Without thoughts, there will be no desire. Without desire, there will be no confused thoughts."

「『诸贤知之，欲从想生，以兴想念，便生欲意。或能自害，复害他人，起若干灾患之变，于现法中受其苦患，复于后世受苦无量。欲意以除，亦不自害，不害他人，于现法报不受其苦。是故，今当除想念。以无想念；便无欲心，以无欲心，便无乱想。』

At that time, the bhikṣu received this instruction and immediately contemplated the impure aspects. By contemplating the impure thoughts, at that time his defiled mind was liberated and reached the unconditioned state. It is like the fifth person who puts on armor, takes up weapons, and enters the army to fight. He sees the enemy without fear and is not moved if they come to harm him. He can defeat the external enemy and occupy their territory. Therefore, it is said that this person can defeat the army of Māra, remove confused thoughts, and reach the unconditioned state. This is called the fifth person appearing in the world.

「尔时，彼比丘受如此教勅，即思惟不净之相。以思惟不净之想，尔时有漏心得解脱，至无为处，如彼第五人著铠持仗入军战鬪，彼见众敌无有恐惧，设有来害者心不移动，能破外寇，居他界中。由是故，今说此人能破魔众，去诸乱想，至无为处，是谓第五人出现于世。

"Bhikṣus should know that these five people appear in the world. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should remember to practice the thought of impurity. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「比丘当知，世间有此五人出现世间。是故，诸比丘！当念修行欲不净想。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
