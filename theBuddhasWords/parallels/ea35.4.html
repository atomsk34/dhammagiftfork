EĀ 35.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

35.4 （四）

Parallel to: SN 37.27–28

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Women have five powers to despise their husbands. What are the five? First, the power of beauty, second, the power of relatives, third, the power of fields and businesses, fourth, the power of children, and fifth, the power of self-reliance. These are the five powers of women. Bhikṣus should know that women rely on these five powers to despise their husbands. Even if a husband has one power, he can completely cover up those women. What is the one power? It is the power of wealth and nobility. Women are inferior in beauty and nobility, and in relatives, fields and businesses, children, and self-reliance. All because of one power, it surpasses so many powers.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「女人有五力轻慢夫主。云何为五？一者色力，二者亲族之力，三者田业之力，四者儿力，五者自守力。是谓女人有此五力。比丘当知，女人依此五力已，便轻慢夫主。设复夫以一力，尽覆蔽彼女人。云何为一力？所谓富贵力也。夫人以贵色力不如，亲族、田业、儿、自守尽不如也。皆由一力，胜尔许力也。

"Now, the evil Māra Pāpīyas also has five powers. What are the five? Namely, the power of form, the power of sound, the power of smell, the power of taste, and the power of touch. Those foolish people who are attached to the dharmas of form, sound, smell, taste, and touch cannot transcend the realm of Māra Pāpīyas. If a noble disciple accomplishes one power, it surpasses those many powers. What is the one power? It is the power of non-distraction. If a noble disciple accomplishes non-distraction, he will not be bound by form, sound, smell, taste, or touch. Not being bound by the five desires, he can discern the dharmas of birth, old age, sickness, and death, surpass the five powers of Māra, not fall into the realm of Māra, transcend all fears and difficulties, and reach the state of non-conditioning."

「今弊魔波旬亦有五力。云何为五？所谓色力、声力、香力、味力、细滑力，夫愚痴之人著色、声、香、味、细滑之法者，不能得度波旬境界。若圣弟子成就一力，胜尔许力。云何为一力？所谓无放逸力。设贤圣弟子成就无放逸者，则不为色、声、香、味、细滑之所拘系。以不为五欲所系，则能分别生、老、病、死之法，胜魔五力，不堕魔境界，度诸畏难，至无为之处。」

At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

The precepts are the path to immortality, while distraction is the path to death. Those who
are not greedy will not die, but those who lose the path will perish on their own.

「戒为甘露道，　　放逸为死径，不贪则不死，　　失道为自丧。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "You should remember to practice and not be negligent. Thus, bhikṣus, you should train in this way."

佛告诸比丘：「当念修行而不放逸。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
