EĀ 37.10

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

37.10 （一〇）

Parallel to: MN 35 / SĀ 110

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was staying in a forest outside the city of Vaiśālī, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus.

一时，佛在毘舍离城外林中，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, Venerable Aśvajit, having put on his robe and taken his alms bowl, entered the city to beg for food. At that time, Sāṇḍaka saw Aśvajit coming from afar, and went to him, saying: "What kind of teachings does your teacher speak? What kind of instructions does he give? What kind of teachings does he give to his disciples?"

尔时，尊者马师到时，著衣持钵，入城乞食。是时，萨遮尼健子遥见马师来，即往语马师曰：「汝师说何等义？有何教训？以何教诫向弟子说法乎？」

Aśvajit replied: "Brahmin! Form is impermanent. Impermanence is suffering. Suffering is non-self. Non-self is emptiness. Emptiness is not mine. I am not theirs. This is what the wise should learn. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are impermanent. These five aggregates of clinging are impermanent, and therefore suffering. Suffering is non-self. Non-self is emptiness. Emptiness is not theirs. I am not theirs. If you wish to know, this is the meaning of my teacher's instructions. I teach this meaning to my disciples."

马师报曰：「梵志！色者无常，无常者即是苦，苦者即是无我，无我者即是空也，空者彼不我有，我非彼有。如是者智人之所学也。痛、想、行、识无常，此五盛阴无常者即是苦，苦者即是无我，无我者即是空，空者彼非我有，我非彼有。卿欲知者，我师教诫其义如是，与诸弟子说如是义。」

At that time, Sāṇḍaka covered his ears with both hands and said: "Stop! Stop! Aśvajit! I do not enjoy hearing this. Even if the Śramaṇa Gautama has such teachings, I truly do not enjoy hearing them. The reason is that according to my view, form is permanent, but according to the Śramaṇa's view, it is impermanent. When will I see the Śramaṇa Gautama to discuss and remove his inverted mind?"

是时，尼健子以两手掩耳，而作是言：「止！止！马师！我不乐闻此语。设瞿昙沙门有此教者，我实不乐闻。所以然者，如我义者色者是常，沙门义者无常。何日当见沙门瞿昙与共论议，当除沙门瞿昙颠倒之心。」

At that time, five hundred young men in the city of Vaiśālī gathered in one place to discuss something. Then, Sāṇḍaka went to where the five hundred young men were and said to them: "You all come with me to where the Śramaṇa Gautama is. The reason is that I want to discuss with that Śramaṇa Gautama so that he can see the right path of truth. The Śramaṇa says that form is impermanent, but according to my view, form is permanent. It is like a strong man holding a long-haired sheep, able to take it wherever he wants without any difficulty. I am also like this. When I debate with that Śramaṇa Gautama, I will be able to let go of him without any difficulty. It is like a fierce elephant with six tusks playing in the deep mountains, also without any difficulty. I am also like this. When I debate with him, there will be no difficulty. It is like two strong men holding a weak one over a fire, able to turn him around as they please without any difficulty. I will also have no difficulty in debating with him. In my debates, I can even kill an elephant, let alone a human. I can also make an elephant go east, west, south, or north, just like a human. Now, even the pillars and beams of this lecture hall, which are inanimate objects, I can make them move. How much more so can I defeat someone in debate? I will make his blood come out of his face and cause his death."

尔时，毘舍离城五百童子集在一处，欲有所论。是时，尼健子往至五百童子所，语童子曰：「汝等皆来，共至沙门瞿昙所。所以然者，意欲与彼沙门瞿昙共论，使彼沙门得见正谛之道。沙门所说者色者无常，如我义者色者是常。犹如力士手执长毛之羊，随意将东西，亦无疑难。我今亦复如是，与彼沙门瞿昙论议，随我捉舍而无疑难。犹如猛象凶暴而有六牙，在深山中戏，亦无所难。我今亦复如是，与彼论议亦无疑难。犹如两健丈夫而捉一劣者，在火上炙，随意转侧，亦无疑难。我今与彼论义亦无疑难。我论议中尚能害象，何况人乎？亦能使象东、西、南、北，岂不如人乎？今此讲堂梁柱无情之物，尚能使移转，何况与人共论能胜，我使彼血从面孔出而命终。」

Among them, some young men said: "Sāṇḍaka will never be able to debate with the Śramaṇa. It is only feared that the Śramaṇa Gautama will debate with Sāṇḍaka." Some said: "The Śramaṇa will not debate with Sāṇḍaka. Sāṇḍaka can debate with the Śramaṇa." At that time, Sāṇḍaka thought: "If what the Śramaṇa Gautama says is like what Aśvajit said, it is enough to discuss. If there is meaning, I will know when I hear it."

其中或有童子而作是言：「尼健子终不能与沙门论议，但恐沙门瞿昙与尼健子论议耳。」或有作是说：「沙门不与尼健子论议，尼健子能与沙门共论议。」是时，尼健子便作是念：「设令沙门瞿昙所说如马师比丘者，足得相畴。若有义者，闻已当知。」

At that time, Sāṇḍaka, surrounded by the five hundred young men, went to where the World-Honored One was. They exchanged greetings and sat to one side. Then, Sāṇḍaka said to the World-Honored One: "What, Gautama! What teachings and instructions do you give to your disciples?"

是时，尼健子将五百童子，前后围绕，往至世尊所，共相问讯，在一面坐。是时，尼健子白世尊言：「云何，瞿昙！有何教诫，以何教诫训诸弟子？」

The Buddha told Sāṇḍaka: "What I say is that form is impermanent, and what is impermanent is suffering. Suffering is without self, and what is without self is empty. What is empty is not mine, and I am not theirs. Feeling, perception, formation, consciousness, and the five aggregates of clinging are all impermanent. What is impermanent is suffering. Suffering is without self. What is without self is empty. What is empty is not mine, and I am not theirs. This is the meaning of my teachings and instructions."

佛告尼健子：「我之所说，色者无常，无常即是苦，苦者即是无我，无我者即是空，空者彼非我有，我非彼有；痛、想、行、识及五盛阴皆悉无常，无常即是苦，苦者无我，无我者是空，空者彼非我有，我非彼有。我之教诫其义如是。」

Sāṇḍaka replied: "I do not enjoy hearing this meaning. The reason is that according to my understanding of the meaning, form is permanent."

尼健子报曰：「我不乐闻此义。所以然者，如我所解义，色者是常。」

The World-Honored One said: "You should now concentrate your mind and contemplate the subtle truth, and then speak."

世尊告曰：「汝今且专心意，思惟妙理，然后说之。」

Sāṇḍaka replied: "What I am saying now is that form is permanent, and the meaning of these five hundred youths is also like this."

尼健子报曰：「我今所说色者是常，此五百童子其义亦尔。」

The World-Honored One said: "What you are saying now is that form is permanent, and the meaning of these five hundred youths is also like this." The World-Honored One said: "You should now speak with your own eloquence. Why bring up those five hundred people?"

世尊告曰：「汝今所说色者是常，此五百童子其义亦尔。」世尊告曰：「汝今以己之辩说之，何为引彼五百人乎？」

Sāṇḍaka replied: "What I am saying now is that form is permanent. What kind of discussion does the śramaṇa want to have?"

尼健子报曰：「我今说色是常，沙门欲何等言论？」

The World-Honored One said: "What I am saying now is that form is impermanent and also without self. The form that is artificially combined and numbered is also unreal, not solid, not firm, like a snow ball. It is a law of decay and change. You are now saying that form is permanent. I will ask you back, please answer me as you wish. What do you think, Sāṇḍaka? Can a wheel-turning sage king still have sovereignty over his own country? Can that great king release those who should not be released, and bind those who should not be bound?"

世尊告曰：「我今说色者无常，亦复无我。权诈合数有此色者，亦无真实，无固、无牢亦如雪搏，是等磨灭之法，是变易之法。汝今方说色者是常，我还问汝，随意报我。云何，尼健子！转轮圣王还于己国得自在不乎？又彼大王不应脱者而脱之，不应系者而系之，可得尔乎？」

Sāṇḍaka replied: "This sage king has this power of sovereignty. He can kill those who should not be killed, and bind those who should not be bound."

尼健子报曰：「此圣王有此自在之力，不应杀者能杀之，不应系者能系之。」

The World-Honored One said: "What do you think, Sāṇḍaka? Will the wheel-turning sage king also grow old? Will his hair turn white, his face wrinkled, and his clothes soiled?" At that time, Sāṇḍaka remained silent and did not reply. The World-Honored One asked him a second and a third time, but he remained silent and did not reply. At that time, the vajra-wielding deity Guhyapāda held a vajra scepter in the sky and said to him: "If you do not reply to the discussion now, I will break your head into seven pieces in front of the Tathāgata."

世尊告曰：「云何，尼健子！转轮圣王当复老乎？头白、面皱、衣裳垢坋？」是时，尼健子默然不报。世尊再三问之，彼亦再三默然不报。是时，密迹金刚力士手执金刚之杵，在虚空中而告之曰：「汝今不报论者，于如来前破汝头作七分。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Sāṇḍaka: "Now, look at the sky."

尔时，世尊告尼健子曰：「汝今观虚空中。」

At that time, Sāṇḍaka looked up into the sky and saw the vajra-wielding deity Guhyapāda. He also heard a voice in the sky saying: "If you do not reply to the Tathāgata's discussion, I will break your head into seven pieces." Seeing this, he was frightened and his hair stood on end. He said to the World-Honored One: "Please, Gautama, save me! Now, if I am asked about the discussion again, how should I respond?"

是时，尼健子仰观空中，见密迹金刚力士，又闻空中语：「设汝不报如来论者，当破汝头作七分。」见已惊恐，衣毛皆竖，白世尊言：「唯愿瞿昙当见救济，今更问论当畴对。」

The World-Honored One said: "What do you think, Sāṇḍaka! Will a wheel-turning sage king also grow old, with white hair, falling teeth, loose skin, and wrinkled face?"

世尊告曰：「云何，尼健子！转轮圣王当复老乎？亦当头白、齿落、皮缓、面皱耶？」

Sāṇḍaka replied: "śramaṇa Gautama! Although you say this, according to my meaning, form is permanent."

尼健子报曰：「沙门瞿昙！虽有此语，如我义者，色者是常。」

The World-Honored One said: "You should think carefully before answering. The previous and later meanings do not correspond. But let me ask again, will a sage king also grow old, with white hair, falling teeth, loose skin, and wrinkled face?"

世尊告曰：「汝善思惟而后报之，前之与后，义不相应。但具论圣王当复老乎？亦当头白、齿落、皮缓、面皱耶？」

Sāṇḍaka replied: "A wheel-turning sage king is allowed to grow old."

尼健子报曰：「转轮圣王许使老。」

The World-Honored One said: "A wheel-turning sage king can always be free in his own country. Why can't he avoid old age, sickness, and death? I do not want old age, sickness, and death. I am the one who should be permanent. Is it possible to make it so?"

世尊告曰：「转轮圣王常能于己国得自由，何以故不能却老、却病、却死？我不用老、病、死，我是常之应。欲使然者，其义可乎？」

At that time, Sāṇḍaka was silent and did not reply. He was sorrowful and unhappy, silent and speechless. At that time, Sāṇḍaka's body was sweating, and the sweat stained his clothes and even the seat he was sitting on, down to the ground.

是时，尼健子默然不对，愁忧不乐，寂然不语。是时，尼健子身体汗出，汗] cf.【丽】污衣裳，亦彻坐处，乃至于地。

The World-Honored One said: "Sāṇḍaka! You roared like a lion in the great assembly, saying: 'You young men, come with me to where Gautama is, and discuss with him. We will defeat him, just like catching a sheep with long wool, going east or west as we please, without any difficulty. It is also like a great elephant entering deep water, swimming freely as it pleases, without any fear. It is also like two strong men holding a weak one over a fire, turning him over as they please.' You also said: 'I can always debate and harm a great elephant. Such pillars, beams, and grass and trees are all without feeling. When discussing with them, I can make them bend, lower, and raise their heads, and also make them sweat under their armpits.'"

世尊告曰：「尼健子！汝在大众中而师子吼：『汝等童子共我至瞿昙所，与共论议，当降伏，如捉长毛之羊，随意东西，而无疑难。亦如大象入深水中，随意自游，亦无所畏。亦如两健丈夫捉一劣者，在火上炙，随意转侧。』又复汝说：『我常能论害大象，如此梁柱草木斯皆无情，与共论议能使屈申低仰，亦能使腋下流汗。』」

At that time, the World-Honored One lifted up his three Dharma robes and showed them to Sāṇḍaka, saying: "Look, the Tathāgata's armpits do not sweat. But you, today, have sweat that penetrates the ground." At that time, Sāṇḍaka again remained silent and did not reply.

尔时，世尊举三法衣，示尼健子曰：「汝观如来腋无流汗，然汝今日返更有汗，乃彻乎地。」是时，尼健子复默然不对。

At that time, there was a boy named Toumo in the assembly. The boy Toumo then addressed the World-Honored One, saying: "I am now able to perform and also wish to speak.

尔时，有童子名头摩，［仁－二＋（亡／大）］在彼众。是时，头摩童子白世尊言：「我今堪任有所施行，亦欲所说。」

The World-Honored One said: "Speak as you wish.

世尊告曰：「随意说之。」

The boy Toumo addressed the Buddha, saying: "It is like a good bathing pond not far from a village, but that bathing pond is full of worms with many legs. Then the people of the village, men and women, young and old, go to the bathing pond and take out the worms, each using tiles and stones to strike and injure the worms' hands and feet. Those worms want to return to the water, but it is impossible. This Nirgrantha Jñātiputra is also like this. Initially, his intention was fierce, wanting to debate with the Tathāgata, harboring jealousy and also embracing arrogance. The Tathāgata has completely eliminated it, leaving nothing remaining. This Nirgrantha Jñātiputra will never be able to return to the Tathāgata to debate again."

头摩童子白佛言：「犹如去村落不远有好浴池，然彼浴池有虫饶脚。然村落人民，男女大小往至浴池所，而出此虫，各各以瓦石取此虫，打之伤破手脚，彼虫意欲还入水者，终无此事。此尼健子亦复如是，初意猛盛，与如来共论，心怀姤意，兼抱憍慢。如来尽以除之，永无有余。此尼健子更终不能重至如来所而共论议。」

At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra said to the boy Toumo: "You are now foolish and deluded, not distinguishing true from false. I will not debate with you, but with the śramaṇa Gautama." At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra said to the Buddha: "Please ask about the meaning and principles, and I will explain them again."

是时，尼健子语头摩童子曰：「汝今愚惑，不别真伪，亦不与汝共论，乃与沙门瞿昙共论。」是时，尼健子白佛言：「唯问义理，当更说之。」

The World-Honored One said: "What do you think, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra! Can a wheel-turning sage king make old age, sickness, and death not come, and can that sage king fulfill this wish?"

世尊告曰：「云何，尼健子！转轮圣王欲使老、病、死不至，可得尔乎？彼圣大王果此愿耶？」

Nirgrantha Jñātiputra replied: "This wish cannot be fulfilled."

尼健子报曰：「不果此愿也。」

"Can one make this form exist, and can one make this form not exist?"

「欲使有此色，欲使无此色，可果乎？」

Nirgrantha Jñātiputra replied: "This cannot be done, Gautama!"

尼健子报曰：「不果也。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said: "Well then, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, is form permanent or impermanent?"

世尊告曰：「云何，尼健子！色者是常？为是无常？」

Nirgrantha Jñātiputra replied: "Form is impermanent."

尼健子报曰：「色者无常。」

"If it is impermanent, is it a changing phenomenon? Do you see this as 'me' and accept that 'I' is that?"

「设复无常，为变易法，汝复见此是我，许我是彼有乎？」

He replied: "No, Gautama!"

对曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

"Are feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness permanent or impermanent?"

「痛、想、行、识为是常？为是非常？」

He replied: "Impermanent."

对曰：「无常。」

The World Honored One said: "If they are impermanent, are they subject to change? Do you see this?"

世尊告曰：「设复无常，为变易之法，汝颇见有乎？」

He replied: "No."

对曰：「无也。」

The World Honored One said: "Are these five aggregates of clinging permanent or impermanent?"

世尊告曰：「此五盛阴是常、无常也？」

Nirgrantha Jñātiputra replied: "Impermanent."

尼健子报曰：「无常也。」

The Buddha said: "If they are impermanent, are they subject to change? Have you ever seen this?"

佛言：「设复无常，为变易法，汝颇见有乎？」

Nirgrantha Jñātiputra replied: "No."

对曰：「无也。」

"Then, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, do your words contradict the truth?"

「云何，尼健子！汝言是常，此理不与义相违乎？」

At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra said to the World-Honored One: "I am now foolish and ignorant, not discerning the truth. I have given rise to this thought to discuss with Gautama, saying that form is permanent. Is it like a fierce beast, a lion, seeing a person coming from afar, having a fearful mind? There is no such thing! Today the Tathāgata is also like this, without the slightest. I am now deluded and do not understand the profound meaning, yet I dare to disturb. The many teachings spoken by Śramaṇa Gautama are like a blind person obtaining eyes, a deaf person hearing clearly, a lost person seeing the road, and a blind person seeing forms. Śramaṇa Gautama is also like this, teaching the Dharma with countless skillful means. I now take refuge in Śramaṇa Gautama, the Dharma, and the bhikṣus. From now on, for as long as I live, I will listen and become an upāsaka. I will no longer kill living beings. I only wish that Gautama and the bhikṣus will accept my invitation to offer food to the Buddha and the bhikṣus." At that time, the World-Honored One silently accepted the invitation.

是时，尼健子白世尊言：「我今愚痴，不别真谛，乃兴此怀，与瞿昙共论，言色是常。犹如猛兽师子遥见人来，有恐怖心乎？终无此事！今日如来亦复如是，无有毫牦。我今狂惑，未明深义，乃敢触娆。沙门瞿昙所说过多，犹如盲者得眼，聋者彻听，迷者见路，无目见色。沙门瞿昙亦复如是，无数方便而为说法。我今自归沙门瞿昙、法、比丘僧，自今以后尽形寿，听为优婆塞，不复杀生，唯愿瞿昙及比丘僧！当受我请，欲饭佛及比丘僧。」尔时，世尊默然受请。

At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra saw that the World-Honored One silently accepted the invitation. He then rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha three times, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and departed. He went to where the young men of Vaiśālī were. Having arrived, he said to the young men: "The offerings you should make to me, give them to me now, not at the wrong time. I now invite Śramaṇa Gautama and the bhikṣus to have a meal tomorrow."

是时，尼健子见世尊默然受请，即从坐起，绕佛三匝，头面礼足而去。往诣毘舍离童子所。到已，语童子曰：「汝等所应供养我具，以当时给我，莫以非时。我今请沙门瞿昙及比丘僧，明当饭之。」

At that time, the young men each prepared food and drink offerings and gave them to him. At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra then prepared various delicious foods and drinks during the night, laid out fine seats, and said: "The time has come. It is now the right time. I only wish that you would deign to come."

是时，诸童子各办饮食之具，持用与之。是时，尼健子即以其夜，办种种甘馔饮食，敷好坐具，而白：「时到，今正是时，唯愿屈伸。」。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, at the right time, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered Vaiśālī with the bhikṣu saṃgha. He went to Nirgrantha Jñātiputra's house. Having arrived, he took his seat, and the bhikṣu saṃgha also took their seats in order. At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, seeing that the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha had taken their seats, personally served and offered various foods and drinks. Seeing that the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha had finished eating, he offered clean water. He then took a small seat and sat in front of the Tathāgata, wishing to hear the Dharma.

是时，世尊到时，著衣持钵，将诸比丘僧入毘舍离，往至尼健子家。到已就坐，及比丘僧各次第坐。是时，尼健子以见佛、比丘僧坐定，自手斟酌，行种种饮食，见佛、比丘僧食讫，行清净水，便取一小座，在如来前坐，欲得闻法。

At that time, the World-Honored One gradually spoke the wonderful teachings to him. The teachings were about giving, precepts, and the teachings on rebirth in the heavens. He spoke of the impurity and filthiness of desire, and the joy of liberation from sexual misconduct. At that time, the World-Honored One saw that Nirgrantha Jñātiputra's mind was open and his intentions were resolved. The teachings that the Buddhas of the past always spoke, namely, about suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path, the World-Honored One spoke them all to Nirgrantha Jñātiputra. At that time, Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, while sitting there, completely eliminated all defilements and attained the purity of the Dharma eye.

尔时，世尊渐与说妙论，所谓论者：施论、戒论、生天之论，欲为秽恶，婬不净行，出要为乐。尔时，世尊以见尼健子心开意解，诸佛世尊常所说法，苦、习、尽、道，尽与彼尼健子说之。是时，尼健子即于坐上，诸尘垢尽，得法眼净。

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

是时，世尊便说此偈：

Sacrificing to fire is supreme, poetry and books are foremost, among humans
the king is the best, among rivers, the ocean is the source
among stars, the moon is bright, among lights, the sun is most
radiant. Above, below, and in the four directions, all the things produced
on the earth, among the gods and people, the Buddha is the
unsurpassed honored one. If one seeks his virtues, the three Buddhas are the supreme.

「祠祀火为上，　　诗书颂为首，人中王为最，　　众流海为源，星中月为明，　　光明日最胜。上下及四方，　　诸地所出物，天及人民类，　　佛为无上尊，欲求其德者，　　三佛为最上。」

At that time, after the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he immediately rose from his seat and departed.

尔时，世尊说此偈已，即从坐起而去。

At that time, the five hundred disciples of Nirgrantha heard that their teacher had accepted the Buddha's teaching. Having heard this, they each said to each other, "How can our great teacher follow Gautama?" At that time, the disciples left the city of Vesali and stood in the middle of the road. At that time, Nirgrantha wanted to go to where the Buddha was to listen to the Dharma. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the Dharma to Nirgrantha, assisting him with joy. Having heard the Dharma, Nirgrantha immediately rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and then withdrew.

是时，尼健子五百弟子闻师受佛教化，闻已，各各自相谓言：「我等大师，云何师宗瞿昙？」是时，诸弟子出毘舍离城，在中道立。是时，尼健子欲至佛所听法。是时，世尊与尼健子说法，助令欢喜。尼健子闻法已，即从坐起，头面礼足，便退而去。

At that time, the disciples of Nirgrantha saw their teacher coming from afar. They each said to each other, "This disciple of the śramaṇa Gautama is now coming in the Dharma robe. Let us each take tiles and stones to beat and kill him."

是时，尼健子弟子遥见师来，各各自相谓言：「此沙门瞿昙弟子今著道来，各各取瓦石而打杀之。」

At that time, the young boys heard that Nirgrantha had been killed by his disciples. They went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed their heads to his feet, and sat to one side. At that time, the young boys said to the World-Honored One, "The Nirgrantha whom the Tathāgata had taught has now been killed by his disciples. Where has he been reborn after his death?"

时，诸童子闻尼健子为弟子所杀，往至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，诸童子白世尊言：「如来所可教化尼健子者，今为弟子所杀。今以命终，为生何处？」

The World-Honored One said, "He was a virtuous person, fully endowed with the Four Truths and the destruction of the three fetters. He attained the stage of stream-enterer and will definitely reach the end of suffering. He died today and was reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-three. Having seen Maitreya Buddha, he will reach the end of suffering. This is the meaning, you should remember and practice it."

世尊告曰：「彼是有德之人，四谛具足，三结使灭，成须陀洹，必尽苦际，今日命终生三十三天，彼见弥勒佛已，当尽苦际。此是其义，当念修行。」

At that time, the young boys said to the World-Honored One, "How wonderful! How extraordinary! This Nirgrantha came to where the World-Honored One was to debate, but he was bound by his own arguments. He came to receive the World-Honored One's teachings. Those who see the World-Honored One will never be in vain. It is like a person entering the ocean to obtain treasures, they will definitely obtain something and not return empty-handed. It is the same for those sentient beings who come to where the World-Honored One is. They will definitely obtain the Dharma treasures and not return empty-handed."

尔时，诸童子白世尊言：「甚奇！甚特！此尼健子至世尊所，捔论议，还以己论而自缚，来受如来化。夫见如来者终无虚妄，犹如有人入海取宝，必有所克获，终不空还。此亦如是，其有众生至如来所者，要得法宝，终不空还。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the subtle Dharma to the young boys, causing them to rejoice. At that time, the young boys, having heard the Dharma from the Buddha, immediately rose from their seats, circumambulated the Buddha three times, bowed their heads at his feet, and then withdrew.

尔时，世尊与诸童子说微妙法，使令欢喜。尔时，诸童子从佛闻法已，即从坐起，绕佛三匝，头面礼足，便退而去。

At that time, the young boys, having heard what the Buddha said, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸童子闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
