EĀ 37.6

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

37.6 （六）

Parallel to: AN 9.11 / MĀ 24

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. Then Śāriputra said to the World-Honored One, "I now wish to spend the summer retreat in Śrāvastī and wander and teach among the people."

尔时，尊者舍利弗往诣世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，舍利弗白世尊言：「我今以在舍卫城夏坐，意欲人间游化。」

The World-Honored One said, "Now is the right time." At that time, Śāriputra rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and then left.

世尊告曰：「今正是时。」时，舍利弗即从坐起，头面礼足，便退而去。

Not long after Śāriputra left, a bhikṣu with slanderous intentions said to the World-Honored One, "Śāriputra has been disputing with the bhikṣus and not repenting. Now he is wandering among the people.

时，舍利弗去未久，有一比丘怀诽谤意，白世尊言：「舍利弗与诸比丘共诤竞，不忏悔，今游行人间。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told a bhikṣu, "Go quickly and call Śāriputra in my name.

尔时，世尊告一比丘：「汝速往持吾声，唤舍利弗。」

The bhikṣu replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

比丘对曰：「如是。世尊！」

The Buddha ordered Maudgalyāyana and Ānanda, "You should have the monks in the various rooms summoned to the World-Honored One. The reason is that Śāriputra has entered samādhi and will now roar like a lion in front of the Tathāgata."

佛勅目连、阿难：「汝等使诸房中召诸比丘诣世尊所。所以然者，舍利弗所入三昧，今当在如来前作师子吼。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's instruction, gathered before the World-Honored One. They bowed their heads at his feet and sat to one side.

是时，诸比丘闻佛教已，各集世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。

At that time, the bhikṣu, having received the World-Honored One's instruction, went to where Śāriputra was and said to Śāriputra, "The Tathāgata wishes to see you."

是时，彼比丘受世尊教，即往至舍利弗所，语舍利弗言：「如来欲得相见。」

At that time, Śāriputra went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. The Buddha then said to Śāriputra, "Not long after you left, a bhikṣu of impure conduct came to me and said, 'Śāriputra bhikṣu has been disputing with other bhikṣus and has not repented. He is wandering and teaching among the people.' Is this true?"

尔时，舍利弗往至佛所，头面礼足，在一面坐。是时，佛告舍利弗言：「卿向者去未久，有秽行比丘来至我所，而白我言：『云舍利弗比丘与诸比丘共诤，亦不悔过，在人间游化。』审实尔乎？」

Śāriputra said to the Buddha, "The Tathāgata should know for himself."

舍利弗白佛言：「如来自当知之。」

The World-Honored One said, "I know myself. But now the great assembly each has doubts. You can now clarify yourself in the midst of the great assembly with your own eloquence."

世尊告曰：「我自知耳。但今大众各怀狐疑。汝今于大众中，可以己辩而自明净。」

Śāriputra said to the Buddha, "Since leaving the womb, I am now eighty years old. I have always thought to myself that I have never killed living beings, nor have I spoken falsely. Even in jest, I have never spoken falsely. I have also never caused disputes between others. If I am not focused, I may have done these things. But now, World-Honored One, my mind is pure. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct? It is like this earth, which can receive both pure and impure things. It receives all kinds of filth, pus, blood, and spittle without rejecting them. Yet this earth does not say it is bad or good. I am also like this, World-Honored One. My mind does not waver. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct and wander far away? Only when the mind is not focused can this happen. Now my mind is correct. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct and wander far away? It is also like water, which can cleanse both good and bad things. That water does not think, 'I cleanse this, I do not cleanse that.' It is the same here. There is no different thought. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct and wander far away? It is like a raging fire burning a mountain or field, not discriminating between good and bad, and never having any thought. I am also like this. How could I have any intention of arguing with a person of pure conduct? It is also like sweeping and mopping, which do not discriminate between good and bad, but can remove them all, never having any thought. It is like a cow without its horns, being extremely gentle and not fierce. It is easy to lead and can go wherever one wants, without any difficulty. Yes, World-Honored One! My mind is like this. It does not harm any thought. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct and wander far away? It is also like a caṇḍāla woman wearing tattered clothes begging for food in the human world, without any taboos. I am also like this, World-Honored One. I have no thought of causing disputes and wandering far away? It is also like a pot of fat leaking everywhere. A person with eyes can see the leaks everywhere. I am also like this, World-Honored One. The nine orifices leak out impurities. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct? It is like a young and beautiful woman with a dead body tied around her neck, which she detests. World-Honored One! I also detest this body, no different from that. How could I argue with a person of pure conduct and wander far away? This is not the case, World-Honored One! You should know for yourself, and that bhikṣu should also know. If there is such

舍利弗白佛言：「自出母胎年向八十，每自思惟，未曾杀生，亦不妄语，正使于调戏之中亦不妄语，亦复未曾鬪乱彼此；设不专意之时，或能有此行耳。我今，世尊！心意清净，岂当与梵行人共鬪诤乎？亦如此地，亦受净，亦受不净，屎尿秽恶皆悉受之，脓血涕唾终不逆之；然此地亦不言恶，亦不言善，我亦如是，世尊！心不移转，何得与梵行人共诤而远游行？心不专者能有此耳。我今心正，何得与梵行人共诤而远游乎？亦如水，亦能使好物净，亦能使不好物净；彼水不作是念：『我净是、置是。』此亦如是，无有异想，何得与梵行人共鬪而远游乎？犹如炽火焚烧山野，不择好丑，终无想念。我亦如是，岂当有意与梵行人共诤乎？亦如扫洒，不择好丑，皆能除之，终无想念。犹如牛无其双角，极自良善，亦不凶暴，善可将御，随意所至，终无疑难。唯然，世尊！我心如是，亦不与想有所伤害，岂当与梵行人共诤而远游乎？亦如旃陀罗女著弊坏衣，在人间乞食亦无禁忌。我亦如是，世尊！亦无想念，当兴诤讼而远游也？亦如脂釜，处处漏坏，有目之人皆悉观见处处漏出。我亦如是，世尊！九孔之中漏出不净，岂当与梵行人共诤？犹如女人年少端正，复以死尸系彼女颈，而厌患之。世尊！我亦如是，厌患此身如彼无异，岂当与梵行人共诤而远游乎？此事不然，世尊！自当知之，彼比丘亦当知之。设当有是者，愿彼比丘受我忏悔。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told that bhikṣu, "You should now repent yourself. If you do not repent, your head will break into seven pieces."

尔时，世尊告彼比丘：「汝今可自悔过。所以然者，若不悔者头便破为七分。」

At that time, that bhikṣu was terrified and his hair stood on end. He immediately rose from his seat, bowed at the feet of the Tathāgata, and said to the World-Honored One, "I now know that I have offended Śāriputra. I only pray that the World-Honored One will accept my repentance."

是时，彼比丘心怀恐怖，衣毛皆竖，即从坐起，礼如来足，白世尊言：「我今自知犯舍利弗，唯愿世尊受我忏悔。」

The World-Honored One said, "Bhikṣu! You should repent to Śāriputra yourself. If you do not, your head will break into seven pieces."

世尊告曰：「汝比丘！自向舍利弗忏悔，若不尔者头便为七分。」

At that time, that bhikṣu immediately bowed his head at Śāriputra's feet and said to Śāriputra, "I only pray that you will accept my repentance. I was foolish and did not distinguish the true."

是时，彼比丘即向舍利弗头面礼足，白舍利弗言：「唯愿受我忏悔，愚不别真。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra, "You should now accept this bhikṣu's repentance and also pat his head. The reason is that if you do not accept this bhikṣu's repentance, his head will break into seven pieces."

尔时，世尊告舍利弗：「汝今可受此比丘悔过，又以手摩头。所以然者，若当不受此比丘忏悔者，头破为七分。」

At that time, Śāriputra patted his head and said to the bhikṣu, "I accept your repentance. Like a fool, like a deluded one. This Buddha Dharma is extremely vast. If one can repent in a timely manner, it is excellent! I now accept your repentance. Do not commit offenses again." He said this three times.

尔时，舍利弗以手摩头，语比丘曰：「听汝忏悔！如愚如惑，此佛法中极为旷大，能随时悔过者，善哉！今受汝忏悔，后更莫犯。」如是再三。

At that time, Śāriputra told the bhikṣu, "Do not commit offenses again. The reason is that there are six dharmas that lead to hell, six dharmas that lead to rebirth in heaven, and six dharmas that lead to the place of nirvāṇa. What are the six? Wishing to harm others, I give rise to this mind of harm and am overjoyed, unable to contain myself; I will teach others to harm others, giving rise to a mind of harm in the process; having harmed others, giving rise to joy in the process; I will obtain this ill-repute; not having done this, I am already anxious and worried. These are called the six dharmas that cause one to fall into evil destinies.

是时，舍利弗告彼比丘曰：「汝更莫犯。所以然者，有六法入地狱，六法生天，六法至涅槃处。云何为六？欲害他人，我以起此害心，便欢喜踊跃，不能自胜；我当教人使害他，于中起害心；以得害人，于中起欢喜，我当得此不馨之问；未起此事便快愁忧。是谓有此六法，令人堕恶趣。

What are the six that lead to a good place? Namely, complete bodily precepts, complete verbal precepts, complete mental precepts, holy life force, a mind free from killing, and a mind free from jealousy. These are called the six dharmas that lead to a good place.

「云何有六，令人至善处？所谓身戒具足，口戒具足，意戒具足，命根清净，不杀害心，无妬嫉心。是谓有此六法，生于善处。

"What are the six dharmas that lead to nirvāṇa? Namely, the six dharmas of mindfulness. What are the six? Namely, practicing loving-kindness with the body without defilement; practicing loving-kindness with speech without defilement; practicing loving-kindness with the mind without defilement; if one obtains the requisites of livelihood, one is able to share them equally with others without stinginess; upholding the precepts without defilement, valued by the wise; such precepts are able to fully understand all wrong views, right views, and the noble ones' way out, and are able to end the root of suffering. Such views are all clearly understood. These are called the six dharmas that lead to nirvāṇa. You should now, bhikṣu, seek the means to practice these six dharmas. Thus, bhikṣu, you should train in this way."

「云何修六法至于涅槃？所谓六思念法。云何为六？所谓身行慈无瑕秽；口行慈无瑕秽；意行慈无瑕秽；若得利养之具，能与人等共分之而无悋想；奉持禁戒无瑕疵，智者所贵；如是之戒能具足诸有邪见、正见、贤圣出要，能得尽苦本。如是诸见皆悉分明。是谓六法得至涅槃。汝今，比丘！当求方便，行此六法。如是，比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣu rose from his seat again and bowed at Śāriputra's feet, saying: "I now confess again, like a fool and deluded, not distinguishing the true. I only pray, Śāriputra, accept my repentance and not commit it again in the future."

尔时，彼比丘重从坐起，礼舍利弗足：「我今重自忏，如愚如惑而不别真。唯愿，舍利弗！受我悔过，后不复犯。」

Śāriputra said: "I allow you to repent! The Dharma of the noble ones is extremely vast and great. If you can change your past and cultivate the future, do not commit it again."

舍利弗曰：「听汝悔过！贤圣法中极为旷大，能自改往修来，莫复更犯。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard what Śāriputra said, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

尔时，彼比丘闻舍利弗所说，欢喜奉行。
