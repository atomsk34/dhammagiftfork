EĀ 38.10

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

38.10 （一〇）

Parallel to: MN 89 / AN 10.30 / MĀ 213

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, King Prasenajit said to his charioteer, "Prepare a chariot with feathered jewels. I wish to go out and enjoy the scenery."

尔时，波斯匿王告御车人曰：「汝今办羽宝之车，吾欲出外游观。」

At that time, the man received the king's order, immediately prepared the chariot with feathered jewels, and said to the king, "The chariot with feathered jewels is ready. The king should know the time."

是时，彼人受王教勅，即办羽宝之车，前白王曰：「已严驾羽宝之车，王宜知是时。」

King Prasenajit took this man and left the city of Shravasti. He arrived at the garden and observed the trees. They were all silent and there were no people. It was quiet and empty. Having seen this, he remembered the Buddha's teachings on the origin of all phenomena. At that time, the man was behind the king, holding a fan to fan the king. "These fruit trees in the garden are all silent and there are no people. It is quiet and empty. I now wish to invite the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One to come here to teach. However, I do not know where the Tathāgata is now. I wish to go and pay homage to him."

波斯匿王将此人便出舍卫城，至彼园观，观诸树木，皆无声响，亦无人民，寂然空虚。见已，便忆如来说诸法之本。是时，彼人在王后，执扇而扇王。「此园果树木皆无声响，亦无人民，寂然空虚，我今欲请如来、至真、等正觉在此游化，然不知如来今为所在？我欲往觐。」

The attendant replied, "There is a village of the Sakya clan called Deer Park. The Tathāgata is teaching there."

侍人报曰：「释种有村名曰鹿堂，如来在彼游化。」

King Prasenajit asked, "How far is Deer Park from here?"

波斯匿王告曰：「此鹿堂去此近远？」

The attendant informed the king, "The Tathāgata's dwelling is not far from here. It is three yojanas away."

侍人白王：「如来住处去此不远，计其道里有三由旬。」

At that time, King Prasenajit said, "Quickly prepare a chariot adorned with feathers and jewels. I wish to see the Tathāgata now."

是时，波斯匿王告曰：「速办羽宝之车，我今欲见如来。」

At that time, the man received the king's order and immediately prepared the chariot. He then informed the king, "The chariot is now ready. The king may know the time." The king then rode in the chariot and went to that village.

是时，彼人受王教已，即办驾车，前白王曰：「车今已驾，王知是时。」王即乘车往诣彼村。

At that time, the group of bhikṣus were walking in the open air. The king then alighted from his carriage and went to where the many bhikṣus were. Having arrived, he bowed his head to their feet and stood to one side. The king then said to the bhikṣus, "Where is the Tathāgata? I wish to see him."

尔时，众比丘辈于露地而经行，是时王下车至众多比丘所。到已，头面礼足，在一面住。是时，王白比丘曰：「如来为所在？吾欲见之。」

The many bhikṣus replied, "The World-Honored One is staying in the lecture hall. You may go and see him, do not find it difficult. When the king wishes to leave, raise your foot slowly, do not make a sound." At that time, King Prasenajit looked back at his attendant. The attendant then thought, "The king is now alone with the World-Honored One. I should stay here."

众多比丘报曰：「世尊在此讲堂中住，可往见之，勿以为难。王欲去时，徐举其足，无令有声。」是时，波斯匿王还顾视彼侍人，是时侍人便作是念：「王今独与世尊相见，我应住此。」

At that time, the king went alone to where the World-Honored One was. At that time, the World-Honored One used his divine eye to see King Prasenajit standing outside the door. At that time, the World-Honored One immediately rose from his seat and opened the door for the king. The king saw the World-Honored One and bowed his head to his feet, proclaiming his name, "I am King Prasenajit." He proclaimed his name three times.

是时，王独往至世尊所。尔时，世尊以天眼观见波斯匿王在门外立。是时，世尊即从座起与王开门。王见世尊，头面礼足，自称姓名：「我是波斯匿王。」三自称号。

The World-Honored One said, "You are now a king, and I am a Śākya who has left home to study the path."

世尊告曰：「汝今是王，我今释种出家学道。」

At that time, the king said to the Buddha, "I only wish that the World-Honored One would prolong his life without end, so that the devas and humans may find peace."

时王白佛：「唯愿世尊延寿无穷，使天、人得安。」

The World-Honored One said, "May the great king prolong his life without end, govern with the Dharma, and not with unrighteousness. Those who govern with the Dharma will all be reborn in the heavens and good places. After their lives end, their reputations will not be forgotten. The people will say, 'In the past, there was a king who governed with the Dharma, and there was never any injustice.' If there are people living within this king's realm who praise the king's virtues and remember them without forgetting, the king's body in the heavens will increase six kinds of meritorious virtues. What are the six? First, heavenly lifespan, second, heavenly appearance, third, heavenly bliss, fourth, heavenly supernatural powers, fifth, heavenly nobility, and sixth, heavenly radiance. Therefore, great king! You should govern with the Dharma, and not with unrighteousness. I have these meritorious virtues in my body today, and I should be respected and worshipped by people."

世尊告曰：「使大王当延寿无穷，以法治化，莫以非法。诸有以法化者，皆生天上善处，正使命终之后，名称不朽。世人所传云：『昔有国王以法治化，未曾有枉。』设有人民住此王境界，叹王功德，思忆不忘者，王身在天上，增六事功德。云何为六？一者天寿，二者天色，三者天乐，四者天神足，五者天豪，六者天光。是故，大王！当以法治，莫以非法。我今日身中有此功德，应受人恭敬礼拜。」

The king said to the Buddha, "The Tathāgata's meritorious virtues should be respected and worshipped by people."

王白佛言：「如来功德应受人拜。」

The World-Honored One said, "How can you say that the Tathāgata should be respected and worshipped by people?"

世尊告曰：「汝今云何言如来应受人礼拜？」

The king said to the Buddha, "The Tathāgata has six meritorious virtues that should be respected and worshipped by people. What are the six? The Tathāgata's true Dharma is very harmonious and elegant, practiced by the wise. This is called the Tathāgata's first meritorious virtue, which is worthy of service and respect.

王白佛言：「如来有六功德，应得受人礼拜。云何为六？如来正法甚为和雅，智者所修行，是谓如来初功德，可事、可敬。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata's noble assembly is extremely harmonious, accomplished in the Dharma, accomplished in precepts, accomplished in wisdom, accomplished in liberation, and accomplished in the vision and wisdom of liberation. The noble assembly refers to the four pairs and eight groups. This is the Tathāgata's noble assembly, worthy of respect and reverence, the great field of merit in the world. This is called the Tathāgata's second meritorious virtue.

「复次，如来圣众极为和顺，法法成就；戒成就、智慧成就、解脱成就、解脱见慧成就。所谓圣众者四双八辈，此是如来圣众，可敬、可贵，世间之大福田，是谓如来第二功德。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata has four groups of followers who practice the Dharma taught by him, without further disturbing the Tathāgata. This is called the Tathāgata's third meritorious virtue.

「复次，如来有四部之众，所施行法皆习行之，更不重受触扰如来，是谓如来第三功德。

Furthermore, World-Honored One! I see that the kṣatriya, brāhmaṇa, householder, and śramaṇa classes, who are highly talented and renowned, all come together to discuss: 'We should go ask the Tathāgata about this matter. If that śramaṇa Gautama does not answer this matter, then he will be at fault; if he can answer, then we should praise him.' At that time, the four classes came to where the World-Honored One was and asked about this matter, but some were silent. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the Dharma for them. Having heard the Dharma, they no longer asked about matters, let alone wanted to discuss them. They all became disciples of the Tathāgata. This is the fourth meritorious virtue.

「复次，世尊！我见刹利之姓、婆罗门、居士、沙门，高才盖世，皆来集论议：『我等当以此论往问如来，设彼沙门瞿昙不报此论者，则有缺也；设当能报者，我等当称其善。』是时，四姓来至世尊所，而问此论，或有默然者。尔时，世尊与彼说法，彼闻法已，更不复问事，况复欲论，皆师事如来，是第四功德。

Furthermore, the sixty-two views deceive the world, not understanding the true Dharma, leading to ignorance. However, the Tathāgata is able to eliminate these wrong views and cultivate right views. This is called the Tathāgata's fifth meritorious virtue.

「复次，诸六十二见，欺诳世人，不解正法，由此致愚。然如来能除此诸邪见业，修其正见，是谓第五如来功德。

Furthermore, sentient beings who engage in evil actions of body, speech, and mind, if they recall the Tathāgata's merits at the time of death, will be freed from the three evil destinies and be reborn in the heavens. Even the most evil person can be reborn in the heavens. This is called the Tathāgata's sixth meritorious virtue.

「复次，众生身、口、意行恶，彼若命终，忆如来功德，离三恶趣，得生天上；正使极恶之人，得生天上，是谓第六如来功德。其有众生见如来者，皆起恭敬之心，而供养之。」

The World-Honored One said, "Excellent! Excellent! Great King! You are able to roar like a lion before the Tathāgata and expound the Tathāgata's merits. Therefore, Great King! You should always give rise to a mind directed towards the Tathāgata. Thus, Great King! You should train in this way."

世尊告曰：「善哉！善哉！大王！乃能如来前作师子吼，演如来功德。是故，大王！常当兴心向于如来。如是，大王！当作是学。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke subtle and wonderful Dharma to King Prasenajit, causing him to rejoice. When the great king had heard the Buddha's Dharma, he immediately rose from his seat, bowed at the feet of the World-Honored One, and then departed.

尔时，世尊与王波斯匿说微妙之法，使令欢喜。是时，大王闻佛说法已，即从座起，礼世尊足，便退而去。

Not long after, the Buddha told the bhikṣus, "You should uphold and venerate this Dharma, recite and contemplate it well. Because this was spoken by King Prasenajit. You should also widely expound its meaning to the fourfold assembly. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

未久，佛告比丘：「汝等当持此法供养，善讽诵念。所以然者，此波斯匿王之所说也。汝等亦当与四部众广演其义。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
