EĀ 38.8

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

38.8 （八）

Parallel to: SN 35.247 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "You should all concentrate your thoughts and cultivate yourselves. How should you concentrate your thoughts? Bhikṣus, you should know when to walk, when to move, when to bend, when to stretch, when to bow, when to put on your robes, when to sleep, when to wake, when to speak, and when to be silent. If a bhikṣu's mind is concentrated and upright, then the bhikṣu's desire-defilement that has not yet arisen will not arise, and that which has arisen will be extinguished; the existence-defilement that has not yet arisen will not arise, and that which has arisen will be extinguished; the ignorance-defilement that has not yet arisen will not arise, and that which has arisen will be extinguished. If one concentrates one's thoughts and discerns the six sense bases, one will never fall into evil destinies.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等专念而自修己。云何当专念？于是，比丘！可行知行，举动、进止、屈申、俯仰、著衣法则、睡眠、觉寤、或语、或默，皆悉知时。若复比丘心意专正，彼比丘欲漏未生便不生，已生便灭之；未生有漏使不生，已生令灭之；未生无明漏使不生，已生令灭之。若专念分别六入，终不堕恶道。

What are the six sense bases that lead to evil destinies? The eye sees forms, whether good or bad. Seeing good, one is pleased; seeing bad, one is displeased. The ear hears sounds, whether good or bad. Hearing good, one is pleased; hearing bad, one is displeased. The nose, mouth, body, and mind are also like this. It is like the six kinds of insects, each with different natures and behaviors. If someone were to take a rope and tie them together, taking a dog, a wild fox, a monkey, a shark, a poisonous snake, and a flying bird, and tying them all together in one place and releasing them, then the six kinds of insects would each have their own natures and behaviors. At that time, the dog would want to go to the village; the wild fox would want to go to the cemetery; the shark would want to go into the water; the monkey would want to go to the mountains and forests; the poisonous snake would want to enter its hole; the flying bird would want to fly in the sky. At that time, the six kinds of insects would each have their own natures and behaviors, not being the same.

「云何六入为恶道？眼观此色，若好、若丑，见好则喜，见恶不喜；若耳闻声，若好、若丑，闻好则喜，闻不好则不喜；鼻、口、身、意，亦复如是。犹如有六种之虫，性行各异，所行不同。若有人取绳缠缚之，取狗、野狐、猕猴、鳣鱼、蚖蛇、飞鸟，皆悉缚之，共系一处而放之。尔时，六种之虫各有性行。尔时，狗意中欲赴趣村中；野狐意中欲趣赴塜间；鳣鱼意中欲趣水中；猕猴意中欲向山林之间；毒蛇意中欲入穴中；飞鸟意中欲飞在空。尔时，六种之虫各各有性行而不共同。

Suppose someone were to take these six kinds of insects and tie them together in one place, not allowing them to go east, west, south, or north. At that time, although the six kinds of insects would move and turn, they would not leave their original place. The six internal sense faculties are also like this. Each has its own master, their affairs are different, and what they observe is distinct, whether good or bad.

「设复有人取此六种之虫，系著一处，而不得东、西、南、北。是时，六种之虫虽复动转，亦不离故处。此内六情亦复如是，各各有所主，其事不同，所观别异，若好、若丑。

"At that time, the bhikṣu binds these six sense faculties together in one place. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should be mindful and diligent, with your mind not confused. At that time, the evil Māra Pāpīyas will not be able to find an opportunity, and all wholesome virtues will be accomplished. Thus, bhikṣus! You should be mindful and perfect the eye faculty, and you will attain two fruits: in this present life, you will attain the fruit of an anāgāmin or the fruit of an arhat. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「尔时，比丘系此六情而著一处。是故，诸比丘！当念专精，意不错乱，是时弊魔波旬终不得其便，诸善功德皆悉成就。如是，诸比丘！当念具足眼根，便得二果，于现法中得阿那含果，若得阿罗汉果。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
