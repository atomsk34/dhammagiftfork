EĀ 39.3

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

39.3 （三）水

Parallel to: AN 7.15 / MĀ 4

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "I will now explain the seven similes of water, which are also like people. Listen carefully, listen carefully, and consider it well."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今当说七事水喻，人亦如是。谛听！谛听！善思念之。」

The bhikṣus replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

诸比丘对曰：「如是。世尊！」

The World-Honored One said, "What are the seven similes of water that resemble people? It is like a person who sinks to the bottom of the water, or like a person who briefly emerges from the water and then sinks again, or like a person who emerges from the water and looks around, or like a person who emerges from the water and stays there, or like a person who walks on the water, or like a person who emerges from the water and wants to reach the other shore, or like a person who has already reached the other shore. These are called, bhikṣus, the seven similes of water that appear in the world.

世尊告曰：「彼云何七事水喻而似人？犹如有人没在水底，如复有人暂出水还没，如复有人出水观看，如复有人出头而住，如复有人于水中行，如复有人出水而欲到彼岸，如复有人已到彼岸。是谓，比丘！七事水喻出现于世。

What is a person who sinks to the bottom of the water and cannot emerge? It is like a person whose body is filled with unwholesome dharmas, who will experience eons of suffering and cannot be cured. This is called a person who sinks to the bottom of the water.

「彼云何人没在水底而不得出？于是，或有一人，不善之法遍满其体，当经历劫数，不可疗治，是谓此人没在水底。

What is a person who emerges from the water and then sinks again? It is like a person whose faith gradually diminishes, who has wholesome dharmas but they are not firm. They practice wholesome actions with body, speech, and mind, but later they practice unwholesome actions with body, speech, and mind. When their body perishes and their life ends, they are reborn in hell. This is called a person who emerges from the water and then sinks again.

「彼何等人出水还没？或有一人信根渐薄，虽有善法而不牢固，彼身、口、意行善，后复身、口、意行不善法，身坏命终，生地狱中，是谓此人出水还没。

What is a person who emerges from the water and looks around? It is like a person who has faith and wholesome roots, and practices wholesome actions with body, speech, and mind, but does not further increase those dharmas. They abide by themselves. When their body perishes and their life ends, they are reborn among the asuras. This is called a person who emerges from the water and looks around.

「彼何等人出水观看？于是，或有人有信善根，身、口、意行，更不增益其法，自守而住，彼身坏命终，生阿须伦中，是谓此人出水而观。

What is a person who emerges from the water and stays? It is like a person who has faith and diligence, who cuts off the three fetters and does not regress, and will definitely attain the unsurpassed path. This is called a person who emerges from the water and stays.

「彼何等人出水住者？于是，或有人有信精进，断三结使，更不退转，必至究竟成无上道，是谓此人出水而住。

What is a person who wants to cross the water? It is like a person who has the faculty of faith and diligence, who is always ashamed and remorseful, who cuts off the three fetters of desire, anger, and delusion, who comes to this world and cuts off the edge of suffering. This is called a person who wants to cross the water.

「彼何等人欲渡水者？于是，或有人信根精进，恒怀惭愧，断三结使，婬、怒、痴薄，来至此世而断苦际，是谓此人欲渡水者。

What is a person who wants to reach the other shore? It is like a person who has the faculty of faith and diligence, who cuts off the five lower fetters, becomes a non-returner, and attains parinirvāṇa right there, never returning to this world. This is called a person who wants to reach the other shore.

「彼何等人欲至彼岸？或有人信根精进，断下五结，成阿那含，即彼般涅槃，更不来此世，是谓此人欲至彼岸者也。

What is a person who has already reached the other shore? It is like a person who has the faculty of faith and diligence, who is ashamed and remorseful, who exhausts the taints and attains the absence of taints, who delights in the present life, who has ended birth and death, established the holy life, accomplished what was to be done, and will not again take up a womb, who truly knows this, and attains parinirvāṇa in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder. This is called a person who has already reached the other shore.

「何等人已至彼岸者？于是，或有一人，信根精进而怀惭愧，尽有漏成无漏，于现法中而自娱乐；生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受胎，如实知之。于此无余涅槃界而般涅槃，是谓此人已渡彼岸者也。

This, monks, is what I have taught you about the seven people and the water analogy. The Buddhas of the past, present, and future have practiced and liberated people in this way. Now I have also practiced it. You should dwell in seclusion and quietude, under trees, and contemplate sitting meditation. Do not be lazy. This is my instruction.

「是谓，比丘！有此七人水喻向汝等说，诸佛世尊所应修行接度人民，今已施行。当在闲居静处，若在树下，当念坐禅，勿起懈怠。此是我之教诲。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
