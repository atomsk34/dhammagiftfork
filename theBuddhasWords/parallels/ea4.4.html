EĀ 4.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

4.4 （四）

"The foremost of my disciples who sit in meditation under trees without distraction is the monk Vīradatta. The one who endures hardships, sitting in the open without avoiding wind and rain, is the monk Bāhu. The one who delights in solitude and concentrates his thoughts is the monk Dhūta. The one who wears hempen robes and does not adorn himself is the monk Nivṛta. The one who always delights in charnel grounds and does not dwell among people is the monk Udayin. The one who always sits on a grass mat and daily benefits people is the monk Revata. The one who does not speak to people and walks looking at the ground is the monk Upavāna. The one who, when sitting, standing, or walking, always enters samādhi is the monk Saṃjīva. The one who delights in traveling to distant lands to teach the people is the monk Dharmarakṣa. The one who delights in gathering the noble assembly to discuss the flavor of the Dharma is the monk Kātyāyana."

「我声闻中第一比丘，树下坐禅，意不移转，所谓狐疑离曰比丘是。苦身露坐，不避风雨，所谓婆嗟比丘是。独乐空闲，专意思惟，所谓陀素比丘是。著五纳衣，不著荣饰，所谓尼婆比丘是。常乐冢间，不处人中，所谓优多罗比丘是。恒坐草蓐，日福度人，所谓卢醯宁比丘是。不与人语，视地而行，所谓优钳摩尼江比丘是。坐起行步，常入三昧，所谓删提比丘是。好游远国，教授人民，所谓昙摩留支比丘是。喜集圣众，论说法味，所谓迦泪比丘是。」

The foremost of my disciples who sit in meditation under trees without distraction is the monk Vīradatta. The one who endures hardships, sitting in the open without avoiding wind and rain, is the monk Bāhu
The one who delights in solitude and concentrates his thoughts is the monk Dhūta. The one who wears hempen robes and does not adorn himself is the monk Nivṛta. The one who always delights in charnel

狐疑、婆蹉离　　陀苏、婆、优多卢醯、优迦摩　　息、昙摩留、泪
