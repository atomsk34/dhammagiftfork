EĀ 40.2

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

40.2 （二）

Parallel to: AN 7.22 / MĀ 142

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Karanda Bamboo Grove in the city of Rajagriha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikshus.

一时，佛在罗阅城迦兰陀竹园所，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, King Ajatasatru of Magadha said to his ministers, "The country of Vatsa is very prosperous, with many people. I shall attack and annex that territory.

是时，摩竭国王阿阇世，在群臣中而作是说：「此拔祇国极为炽盛，人民众多，吾当攻伐，摄彼邦土。」

At that time, King Ajatasatru told the Brahmin Varakarama, "Now go to the World-Honored One and convey my name. Inquire about the World-Honored One's well-being and pay respects on my behalf. Say: 'King Ajatasatru informs the World-Honored One that he wishes to attack the country of Vatsa. Is this permissible?' If the Tathāgata says anything, you should contemplate it well and come back to tell me. Because the words of the Tathāgata are never contradictory.

是时，阿阇世王告婆利迦婆罗门曰：「汝今往至世尊所，持吾姓名，往问讯世尊，礼敬承事云：『王阿阇世白世尊言：「意欲攻伐拔祇国，为可尔不？」』设如来有所说者，汝善思惟，来向吾说。所以然者，如来语终不有二。」

At that time, the Brahmin received the king's instructions and went to where the World-Honored One was. They exchanged greetings and sat to one side. The Brahmin then said to the Buddha, "King Ajatasatru pays respects to the World-Honored One, serves and inquires about his well-being, and further asks: 'I wish to attack the great country of Vatsa. I first come to ask the Buddha if this is permissible?'"

是时，婆罗门受王教勅，往至世尊所，共相问讯，在一面坐。是时，婆罗门白佛言：「王阿阇世礼敬世尊，承事问讯，又复重白：『意欲往攻伐拔祇大国。』先来问佛为可尔不？」

At that time, the Brahmin covered his head and feet with his robe, wore ivory clogs, and fastened a sharp sword at his waist. It was not appropriate to teach the Dharma.

尔时，彼婆罗门以衣覆头脚，著象牙屐，腰带利剑，不应说法。

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "If the people of Vatsa practice seven dharmas, they will never be destroyed by external enemies. What are the seven? If the people of Vatsa gather together in one place and do not disperse, they will not be destroyed by other countries. This is called the first dharma that will not be defeated by external enemies.

是时，世尊告阿难曰：「若拔祇人民修七法者，终不为外寇所坏。云何为七？若当拔祇国人民，尽集一处而不散者，便不为他国所坏，是谓初法不为外寇所败。

Furthermore, Ānanda! The people of Vatsa are harmonious with each other. The people of Vatsa are not captured by outsiders. This is called the second dharma that will not be destroyed by external enemies.

「复次，阿难！拔祇国人上下和顺，拔祇人民不为外人所擒。是谓，阿难，第二之法不为外寇所坏。

Furthermore, Ānanda! If the people of Vatsa do not lust after others, and do not become attached to the appearance of other women, this is called the third dharma that will not be destroyed by external enemies.

「复次，阿难！若拔祇国人亦不婬他，著他女人色，是谓第三之法不为外寇所坏。

Furthermore, Ānanda! If the people of Vatsa do not spread from here to there, nor from there to here, this is called the fourth dharma that will not be destroyed by external enemies.

「复次，阿难！若拔祇国不从此间而传至彼，亦复不从彼间传来至此，是谓第四之法不为外寇所坏。

Furthermore, Ānanda! If the people of Vatsa honor śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, and serve and respect those who practice the holy life, this is called the fifth dharma, and at that time they will not be conquered by external enemies.

「复次，阿难！若拔祇国人供养沙门、婆罗门，承事礼敬梵行人者，是谓第五之法，是时便不为外寇所获。

Furthermore, Ānanda! If the people of Vatsa do not greedily cling to the wealth and treasures of others, this is called the sixth dharma that will not be destroyed by external enemies.

「复次，阿难！若拔祇国人民不贪著他财宝者，是谓第六法不为外寇所坏。

Furthermore, Ānanda! If the people of Vatsa all have the same mind, do not go to the temples of gods, and concentrate their minds, they will not be destroyed by external enemies. This is called the seventh dharma that will not be destroyed by external enemies. This is called, Ānanda! The seven dharmas practiced by the people of Vatsa will never be destroyed by outsiders.

「复次，阿难！若拔祇国人民皆同一心，不向神寺，专精其意，便不为外寇所坏也，是谓第七之法不为外寇所坏。是谓，阿难！彼拔祇人修此七法者，终不为外人所坏。」

At that time, the brāhman said to the Buddha, "If those people accomplish even one dharma, they cannot be destroyed, let alone seven dharmas. Stop, stop! World-Honored One! There are many affairs of state. I wish to return to my residence." At that time, the brāhman immediately rose from his seat and departed.

是时，梵志白佛言：「设当彼人成就一法，犹不可坏，何况七法而可坏乎？止！止！世尊！国事猥多，欲还所止。」尔时，梵志即从座起而去。

Not far from there, at that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "I will now explain the seven dharmas of non-retrogression. Listen carefully and contemplate them well!"

彼梵志不远，尔时世尊告诸比丘：「我今当说七不退转法，汝等谛听！善思念之。」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

诸比丘白佛言：「唯然，世尊！」

At that time, the bhikṣus received the Buddha's instruction. The World-Honored One said, "What are the seven dharmas of non-retrogression? Bhikṣus should know, if bhikṣus gather together in one place, all harmoniously and respectfully, advancing upwards, cultivating good dharmas without retrogression, and not being taken advantage of by Māra, this is called the first dharma of non-retrogression.

尔时，诸比丘从佛受教。世尊告曰：「云何为七不退转之法？比丘当知，若比丘共集一处，皆共和顺，上下相奉，转进于上，修诸善法而不退转，亦不为魔所得便，是谓初法不退转。

Furthermore, the harmonious Sangha follows his teachings, advancing upwards without retrogression, not being destroyed by Māra, this is called the second dharma of non-retrogression.

「复次，众僧和合顺从其教，转进于上而不退转，不为魔王所坏，是谓第二之法不退转也。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! Not attached to worldly affairs, not cultivating worldly glory, advancing upwards without retrogression, not being taken advantage of by Māra, this is called the third dharma of non-retrogression.

「复次，比丘！不著事务，不修世荣，转进于上，不为魔天所得其便，是谓第三不退转之法也。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! Not reciting miscellaneous books, all day long exerting their minds, advancing upwards without retrogression, not being taken advantage of by Māra, this is called the fourth dharma of non-retrogression.

「复次，比丘！不讽诵杂书，终日策役其情意，转进于上，不为魔王得其便，是谓第四不退转之法也。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! Diligent in cultivating the Dharma, removing sleepiness, always self-alert, advancing upwards without retrogression, not being taken advantage of by Māra, this is called the fifth dharma of non-retrogression.

「复次，比丘！勤修其法，除去睡眠，恒自警寤，转进于上，不为弊魔而得其便，是谓第五不退转之法。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! Not studying mathematics, nor teaching others, delighting in quiet places, cultivating the Dharma, advancing upwards without retrogression, not being taken advantage of by Māra, this is called the sixth dharma of non-retrogression.

「复次，比丘！不学算术，亦不使人习之，乐闲静之处，修习其法，转进于上，不为弊魔得其便也。是谓第六不退转之法。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! Arousing the thought of the impermanence of all worldly things, practicing dhyāna, enduring the teachings of the Dharma, advancing upwards without retrogression, not being taken advantage of by Māra, this is called the seventh dharma of non-retrogression. If a bhikṣu accomplishes these seven dharmas, and harmonizes with them, then Māra will not be able to take advantage of him.

「复次，比丘！起一切世间不可乐想，习于禅行，忍诸法教，转进于上，不为魔所得其便，是谓七不退转法也。若有比丘成就此七法，共和顺者，便不为魔得其便也。」

At that time, the World Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊说此偈言：

Eliminating worldly activities, not being disturbed by thoughts, if
one does not practice this, one will not attain
samādhi. Those who delight in the Dharma, discern its
meaning, bhikṣus who delight in this practice, will attain samādhi.

「除去于事业，　　又非思惟乱，设不行此者，　　亦不得三昧。能乐于法者，　　分别其法义，比丘乐此行，　　便致三昧定。

"Therefore, bhikṣus! You should seek skillful means to accomplish these seven dharmas. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「是故，比丘！当求方便，成此七法。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard the Buddha's teaching and joyfully upheld it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
