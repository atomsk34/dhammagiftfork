EĀ 40.6

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

40.6 （六）

Parallel to: MN 2 / AN 6.58 / MĀ 10

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I shall expound the most wonderful Dharma, which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with profound meaning and principles, fully endowed with the practice of the holy life. This sūtra is called 'The Dharma for Purifying All Defilements.' You should contemplate it well."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我当说极妙之法，初、中、竟善，义理深邃，具足得修梵行，此经名为净诸漏法，汝等善思念之。」

The bhikṣus replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!" At that time, the bhikṣus received the Buddha's instruction.

比丘对曰：「如是。世尊！」是时，诸比丘从佛受教。

The World-Honored One said, "What is meant by the Dharma for purifying all defilements? There are defilements that can be eliminated through seeing; there are defilements that can be eliminated through close association; there are defilements that can be eliminated through separation; there are defilements that can be eliminated through enjoyment; there are defilements that can be eliminated through conduct; there are defilements that can be eliminated through contemplation.

世尊告曰：「彼云何名为净诸漏法？或有有漏，缘见得断；或有有漏，亲近得断；或有有漏，远离得断；或有有漏，娱乐得断；或有有漏，威仪得断；或有有漏，思惟得断。

What are the defilements that can be eliminated through seeing? Here, ordinary people do not see the noble ones, do not follow the teachings of the Tathāgata, cannot uphold the Dharma of the noble ones, do not associate with good friends, and do not engage with good friends. They do not discern the Dharma that should be contemplated, but contemplate what should not be contemplated. They give rise to the defilement of desire that was not previously there, and increase the defilement of desire that was already there. They give rise to the defilement of existence that was not previously there, and increase the defilement of existence that was already there. They give rise to the defilement of ignorance that was not previously there, and increase the defilement of ignorance that was already there. This is the Dharma that should not be contemplated but is contemplated.

「彼云何有漏由见得断？于是，凡夫之人不覩圣人，不顺从如来之法，不能拥护贤圣之法，不亲近善知识，不与善知识从事。其闻法所应思惟法者亦不分别，不应思惟者而思惟之。未生欲漏而生，已生欲漏便增多；未生有漏而生，已生有漏便增多；未生无明漏而生，已生无明漏便增多。此法不应思惟而思惟之。

What is the Dharma that should be contemplated but is not contemplated? The Dharma that should be contemplated means that the defilement of desire that was not previously there does not arise, and the defilement of desire that was already there is eliminated. The defilement of existence that was not previously there does not arise, and the defilement of existence that was already there is eliminated. The defilement of ignorance that was not previously there does not arise, and the defilement of ignorance that was already there is eliminated. This is called the Dharma that should be contemplated but is not contemplated. What should not be contemplated is contemplated, and what should be contemplated is not contemplated. The defilement of desire that was not previously there arises, and the defilement of desire that was already there increases. The defilement of existence that was not previously there arises, and the defilement of existence that was already there increases. The defilement of ignorance that was not previously there arises, and the defilement of ignorance that was already there increases. That person thinks like this: 'How can there be a distant past? I will have a distant past.' Or they think: 'There is no distant past. How can there be a distant past? For whom is there a distant past?' They also think: 'How can there be a distant future? I will have a distant future.' Or they say: 'There is no distant future. How can there be a distant future? For whom is there a distant future?' They also think: 'How can there be these beings for a long time? Where do these beings come from? Where will they be reborn after death?' That person gives rise to these inauspicious thoughts, and then gives rise to the six views, thinking that there is a self. They think: 'There is a self, and this view is true.' 'There is no self, and this view is true.' 'There is a self and no self, and this view is true.' They also observe their own body and give rise to this view. They do not see themselves in themselves. They give rise to this view. They do not see non-self in non-self, and give rise to this view. At that time, that person further gives rise to this wrong view: 'The self is the present world, and also the future world. It is eternal, does not decay, does not change, and does not move.' This is called the heap of wrong views. Wrong views, calamities, worries, sorrows, suffering, and distress all arise from this and cannot be cured. They cannot be abandoned, and only increase the basis of suffering. Therefore, they do not practice the way of the śramaṇa and nirvāṇa.

「彼云何法应思惟，然不思惟此法？所言思惟法者，未生欲漏使不生，已生欲漏而灭之；未生有漏令不生，已生有漏而灭之；未生无明漏令不生，已生无明漏而灭之。是谓此法应可思惟而不思惟，所不应思惟者便思惟之。应思惟者复不思惟之，未生欲漏而生，已生欲漏而增多；未生有漏而生，已生有漏而增多；未生无明漏而生，已生无明漏而增多。彼人作如是思惟：『云何有过去久远？我今当有过去久远？』或复思惟：『无过去久远？云何当有过去久远？为谁有过去久远？云何复有当来久远？我今当有将来久远？』或复言：『无将来久远。云何当有将来久远？为谁有将来久远？云何有此众生久远？此众生久远为从何来？从此命终当生何处？』彼人起此不祥之念，便兴六见展转生耶想，有我见审有此见；无有我见审兴此见；有我见无我见于中起审见；又复自观身复兴此见；于己而不见己；复兴此见，于无我而不见无我，于中起此见。尔时，彼人复生此邪见：我者即是今世，亦是后世，常存于世而不朽败，亦不变易，复不移动。是谓名为邪见之聚。邪见、灾患、忧、悲、苦、恼，皆由此生而不可疗治，亦复不能舍，遂增苦本，由是不为沙门之行、涅槃之道。

Moreover, bhikṣus! The noble disciples cultivate the Dharma, do not lose the sequence, are able to protect it, and associate with good friends. They are able to discern the Dharma that should not be contemplated, and also know the Dharma that should be contemplated. They do not contemplate the Dharma that should not be contemplated, and contemplate the Dharma that should be contemplated.

「又复，比丘！贤圣弟子修其法，不失次叙，善能拥护，与善知识共从事，彼能分别；不可思惟法亦能知；所可思惟法亦能知之；彼所不应思惟法亦不思惟之；所应思惟法而思惟之。

What is the Dharma that should not be contemplated but is not contemplated? The Dharma that should not be contemplated means that the defilement of desire that was not previously there arises, and the defilement of desire that was already there increases. The defilement of existence that was not previously there arises, and the defilement of existence that was already there increases. The defilement of ignorance that was not previously there arises, and the defilement of ignorance that was already there increases. This is called the Dharma that should not be contemplated but is not contemplated.

「彼云何不应思惟法而不思惟之？于是，诸法未生欲漏而生，已生欲漏而增多；未生有漏而生，已生有漏而增多；未生无明漏而生，已生无明漏而增多。是谓此法不应思惟之。

What is the Dharma that should be contemplated and is contemplated? The Dharma that should be contemplated means that the defilement of desire that was not previously there does not arise, and the defilement of desire that was already there is extinguished. The defilement of existence that was not previously there does not arise, and the defilement of existence that was already there is extinguished. The defilement of ignorance that was not previously there does not arise, and the defilement of ignorance that was already there is extinguished. This is called the Dharma that should be contemplated and is contemplated.

「彼何等法应思惟者而思惟之？于是，诸法未生欲漏而不生，已生欲漏而灭之；未生有漏而不生，已生有漏而灭之；未生无明漏而不生，已生无明漏而灭之。是谓此法应可思惟之。

As for the Dharma that should not be contemplated, one does not contemplate it. As for the Dharma that should be contemplated, one contemplates it. By contemplating in this way, one extinguishes three things. What are the three? The defilement of desire, the defilement of existence, and the defilement of ignorance. If one does not know and see, then one increases the practice of defilement. If one sees, hears, remembers, and knows, then one does not increase the practice of defilement. Having known and seen, defilement does not arise. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by seeing.

「彼不应思惟者亦不思惟之，可思惟者便思惟之，彼作如是思惟，便灭三法。云何为三？身耶、戒盗、疑。设不知、见，则增有漏之行；设见、闻、念、知者，则不增有漏之行；已知、已见，有漏便不生。是谓此漏见所断也。

What is the defilement that is to be eliminated by respect? Here, a bhikṣu endures hunger and cold, wind and rain, mosquitoes and gadflies, harsh words and insults. The body produces pain and extreme headaches, and life is about to end, but he is able to endure it. If he cannot, then suffering arises. If he is able to endure it, then it does not arise. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by respect.

「彼云何漏恭敬所断？于是，比丘！堪忍饥寒，勤苦风雨、蚊虻、恶言、骂辱，身生痛脑，极为烦疼，命垂欲断，便能忍之。若不尔者，便起苦恼。设复能堪忍者，如是不生，是谓此漏恭敬所断。

What is the defilement that is to be eliminated by intimacy? Here, a bhikṣu holds the mind and accepts robes, not giving rise to adornment, but only wishing to support his body, to dispel cold and heat, to prevent wind and rain from affecting his body, and to cover his body so that it is not exposed. He also holds the mind and begs for food according to the time, not giving rise to an attached mind, but only wishing to support his body, to cure old pain and prevent new pain from arising; to protect the practices and not violate them, to cultivate the holy life for a long time, and to abide in the world for a long time. He also holds the mind and is intimate with bedding and seats, not attached to luxurious adornments, but only wishing to dispel hunger, cold, wind, rain, mosquitoes, and gadflies, to support his body, and to practice the Dharma. He also holds the mind and is intimate with medicine, not giving rise to an attached mind; regarding that medicine, he only wishes to cure illness and make the body peaceful. If he is not intimate with it, then there will be the affliction of defilement. If he is intimate with it, then there will be no affliction of defilement. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by intimacy.

「彼云何漏亲近所断？于是，比丘！持心受衣，不兴荣饰，但欲支其形体，欲除寒热，欲令风雨不加其身，又覆形体不令外露。又持心随时乞食，不起染著之心，但欲趣支形体，使故痛得差，新者不生；守护众行，无所触犯，长夜安隐而修梵行，久存于世。复持心意亲近床座，亦不著荣华服饰，但欲除饥寒、风雨、蚊虻之类，趣支其形，得行道法。又复持心亲近医药，不生染著之心；于彼医药，但欲使疾病除愈故，身体得安隐。设不亲近者，则生有漏之患。若亲近，则无有漏之患，是谓此漏亲近所断。

What is the defilement that is to be eliminated by distance? Here, a bhikṣu removes chaotic thoughts, like a vicious elephant, camel, cow, horse, tiger, wolf, dog, snake, deep pit, steep bank, thorny bush, steep cliff, or wet mud, all of which should be distanced from. Do not associate with bad friends, and also do not be close to bad people. If one is not able to carefully contemplate and keep it in mind, then defilement will arise. If one is able to guard it, then defilement will not arise. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by distance.

「彼云何有漏远离所断？于是，比丘！除去乱想，犹如恶象、骆驼、牛、马、虎、狼、狗、蛇蚖、深坑、危岸、柘棘、峻崖、湿泥，皆当远离之。莫与恶知识从事，亦复不与恶人相近，能熟思惟不去心首，设不将护者，则生有漏；设拥护者，则不生有漏，是谓有漏远离所断。

What is the defilement that is to be eliminated by entertainment? Here, a bhikṣu gives rise to thoughts of desire and does not abandon them, if he gives rise to thoughts of anger, he also does not abandon them, if he gives rise to thoughts of illness, he also does not abandon them. If he does not abandon them, then defilement will arise. If he is able to abandon them, then he will not give rise to defilement. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by entertainment.

「彼云何有漏娱乐所断？于是，比丘！生欲想而不舍离，设起瞋恚想，亦不舍离，设复起疾想，亦不舍离，设不舍离者，则生有漏；能舍离者，便能不起有漏。是谓此漏娱乐所断。

What is the defilement that is to be eliminated by deportment? Here, a bhikṣu, when the eye sees forms, does not give rise to thoughts of forms, nor does he give rise to a defiled mind. He is fully endowed with the eye faculty, without any deficiency or leakage, and guards the eye faculty. When the ear hears sounds, the nose smells fragrances, the tongue tastes flavors, the body experiences tactile objects, and the mind knows dharmas, he does not give rise to a defiled mind, nor does he give rise to thoughts of attachment and guards the mind faculty. If he does not restrain his deportment, then defilement will arise. If he restrains his deportment, then there will be no affliction of defilement. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by deportment.

「云何有漏威仪所断？于此，比丘！若眼见色不起色想，亦不起染污之心，具足眼根，亦无缺漏而护眼根。若耳闻声、鼻嗅香、舌知味、身知细滑、意知法，都不起染污之心，亦不起想著而护意根。若不摄其威仪，则生有漏；若摄其威仪者，则无有漏之患。是谓此漏威仪所断。

What is the defilement that is to be eliminated by contemplation? Here, a bhikṣu cultivates the mindfulness faculty, relying on non-desire, non-defilement, and cessation, seeking the essential. He cultivates the faculties of dharmas, vigor, joy, tranquility, concentration, and equanimity, relying on non-desire, non-defilement, and cessation, seeking the essential. If he does not cultivate these, then the affliction of defilement will arise. If he is able to cultivate them, then the affliction of defilement will not arise. This is called the defilement that is to be eliminated by contemplation.

「彼何等有漏思惟所断？于是，比丘！修念觉意，依无欲、依无污、依灭尽而求出要，修法觉意、精进觉意、喜觉意、猗觉意、定觉意、护觉意，依无欲、依无污、依灭尽而求出要。若不修此者，则生有漏之患；设能修者，则不生有漏之患。是谓此漏思惟所断。

"Moreover, bhikṣus, regarding all the defilements that exist among bhikṣus, those that are to be eliminated by insight should be eliminated by insight, those that are to be eliminated by respect should be eliminated by respect, those that are to be eliminated by intimacy should be eliminated by intimacy, those that are to be eliminated by distance should be eliminated by distance, those that are to be eliminated by deportment should be eliminated by deportment, and those that are to be eliminated by contemplation should be eliminated by contemplation. This is called, bhikṣus, being fully endowed with all deportments, being able to eliminate afflictions and remove desires, cross the four streams, and gradually transcend suffering. This is called, bhikṣus, eliminating the dharmas of defilement. The Buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, always practice compassion for all sentient beings. Now, I have practiced it. You should always enjoy dwelling in seclusion under trees, diligently apply yourselves, and do not be lazy. If you do not diligently apply yourselves now, it will be of no benefit to regret later. This is my instruction."

「若复，比丘！于比丘中诸所有漏，见断者便见断之，恭敬断者便恭敬断之，亲近断者亲近断之，远离断者远离断之，威仪断者威仪断之，思惟断者思惟断之。是谓，比丘！具足一切威仪，能断结、去爱，度于四流，渐渐越苦。是谓，比丘！除有漏之法。诸佛世尊常所施行慈念一切有形之类，今已施行。汝等常乐闲居树下，勤加精进，勿有懈怠。今不勤加者，后悔无益。此是我之训教。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard the Buddha's teaching and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
