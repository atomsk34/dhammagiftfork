EĀ 41.1

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

Chapter 41 增壹阿含经莫畏品第四十一

41.1 （一 ）

Parallel to: MN 14 (~) / SN 55.21 (~) / MĀ 100 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Śākya Kapilavastu in the Nyagrodha Garden.

一时，佛在释翅迦毘罗卫尼拘屡园中。

At that time, Mahānāman Śākya went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. Then Mahānāman Śākya addressed the World-Honored One, saying, "I personally received this teaching from the Tathāgata: 'Any good sons or good daughters who sever the three fetters and attain the stage of stream-entry are called irreversible, and will surely attain the fruit of the path. They will not seek other teachings from non-Buddhist teachers, nor will they examine the teachings of others.' If that is so, it is not so. If I see a wild bull, horse, or camel, I am immediately frightened and my hair stands on end. I also think, 'If I were to die today out of this fear, where would I be reborn?'"

是时，摩呵男释往至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，摩呵男释白世尊言：「躬从如来受此言教：『诸有善男子、善女人断三结使，成须陀洹，名不退转，必成道果，更不求诸外道异学，亦复不观察余人所说。』设当尔者，此事不然。我若见暴牛、马、骆驼，即时恐惧，衣毛皆竖，复作是念：『设我今日怀此恐惧，当取命终者，为生何处？』」

The World-Honored One told Mahānāman, "Do not give rise to fear. If you were to die now, you would not fall into the three evil destinies. There are three reasons for this. What are the three? If one is attached to sexual desire and gives rise to disturbance, and also gives rise to a mind of harm towards others, then if one is free from such desire, one will not give rise to a mind of killing and harming. In this present life, one will not give rise to suffering and distress. All the evil and unwholesome dharmas that one wishes to harm oneself - if one is free from them, one will be free from disturbance and will not have sorrow. This is called, Mahānāman, the three meanings. All evil and unwholesome dharmas will then fall below, while all wholesome dharmas will be above. It is like a butter pot breaking in water. At that time, the shards will sink below, while the butter will float above. It is the same here. All evil and unwholesome dharmas will be below, while all wholesome dharmas will float above. Mahānāman! You should know that in the past, before I attained Buddhahood, I practiced diligently for six years in Uruvilvā, not eating delicious food. My body became emaciated, like a hundred-year-old person, all due to not eating. When I wanted to get up, I would fall to the ground. At that time, I also thought, 'If I were to die here, where would I be reborn?' At that time, I also thought, 'If I were to die now, I would definitely not be reborn in the evil destinies.' However, the meaning is that one cannot go from pleasure to pleasure, but must go from suffering to pleasure.

世尊告摩呵男：「勿起恐畏之心，设当取命终，不堕三恶趣。所以然者，今有三消灭之义。云何为三？如有著于婬欲而起恼乱，复起害心向他人，已无此欲，则不起杀害之心，于现法中不起苦恼，诸有恶不善法欲自害己；设无此者，则无扰乱，便无愁忧。是谓，摩呵男，此三义，诸恶不善法便堕在下，诸善之法便在于上。亦如酥瓶在水中坏，是时瓦石便沈在下，酥便浮在上。此亦如是，诸恶不善之法便在于下，诸善之法便浮在上。摩呵男！当知我昔日未成佛道，在优留毘六年勤苦，不食美味，身体羸瘦，如似百年之人，皆由不食之所致。若我欲起时，便自堕地。时，我复作是念：『设我于中命终者，当生何处？』时，我复作是念：『我今命终者，必不生恶道中。』然复义趣不可从乐至乐，要当由苦然复至乐。

At that time, I also wandered in the cave of the sages. At that time, there were many Nirgrantha sons studying the path there. At that time, the Nirgrantha sons raised their fingers to the sun, exposed their bodies to study the path, or squatted to study the path. At that time, I went to where they were and said to the Nirgrantha sons, 'Why do you leave your seats, raise your hands, and stand on tiptoe?' Those Nirgrantha sons said, 'Gautama should know, our former teachers committed unwholesome actions. Now we suffer because we want to eliminate those sins. Now, although we expose our bodies, we have a sense of shame and disgrace, and we also have a way to eliminate this matter. Gautama should know, when the practice is exhausted, suffering is also exhausted. When suffering is exhausted, the practice is also exhausted. When the practice of suffering is exhausted, one attains nirvana.'

「我尔时复游在仙人窟中。尔时，有多尼揵子在彼学道。是时，尼揵子举手指日，曝体学道，或复有蹲而学道。我尔时往至彼所，语尼揵子：『汝等何故离坐举手翘足乎？』彼尼揵子曰：『瞿昙当知，昔我先师作不善行，今所以苦者，欲灭其罪；今虽露形体，有惭辱分，亦有消灭此事。瞿昙当知，行尽苦亦尽，苦尽行亦尽，苦行已尽，便至涅槃。』

At that time, I also said to the Nirgrantha sons, 'This is not so. It is not by exhausting the practice that suffering is also exhausted, nor is it by exhausting suffering that the practice is also exhausted, that one attains nirvana. But now, it is so that by exhausting the practice of suffering one attains nirvana. But it is not possible to go from pleasure to pleasure.' The Nirgrantha sons said, 'King Bimbisāra goes from pleasure to pleasure. What suffering is there?' At that time, I also said to the Nirgrantha sons, 'How does King Bimbisāra's pleasure compare to my pleasure?' The Nirgrantha sons replied to me, 'King Bimbisāra's pleasure is superior to your pleasure.' At that time, I also said to the Nirgrantha sons, 'Can King Bimbisāra make me sit cross-legged for seven days and seven nights without moving my body? Even if it is six, five, four, three, two, or even one day, can I sit cross-legged?' The Nirgrantha sons replied, 'No, Gautama!' The World-Honored One said, 'I am able to endure sitting cross-legged without moving my body. Now, Nirgrantha sons! Who is at ease? King Bimbisāra or me?' The Nirgrantha sons said, 'The śramaṇa Gautama is at ease.'

「我尔时复语尼揵子曰：『此事不然，亦无由行尽苦亦尽，亦不由苦尽行亦尽，得至涅槃；但今苦行尽得至涅槃者，此事然矣！但不可从乐至乐。』尼揵子曰：『频毘娑罗王从乐至乐，有何苦哉？』我尔时复语尼揵子曰：『频毘娑罗王乐，何如我乐？』尼揵子报我言：『频毘娑罗王乐，胜汝乐也。』我尔时复语尼揵子曰：『频毘娑罗王能使我七日七夜结跏趺坐，形体不移动乎？正使六、五、四、三、二，乃至一日结跏趺坐乎？』尼揵子报曰：『不也。瞿昙！』世尊告曰：『我能堪任结跏趺坐，形不移动。云何，尼揵子！谁者为乐？频毘娑罗王乐耶？为我乐耶？』尼揵子曰：『瞿昙沙门乐。』

"So it is, Mahanama! One should use this method to know that it is not possible to go from pleasure to pleasure, but one must go from suffering to pleasure. It is like, Mahanama! There is a large pond to the left and right of a large village, one yojana long and wide, filled with water. If someone comes to take a drop of that water, what do you think, Mahanama? Which is more, the drop of water or the pond water?"

「如是。摩呵男！当作此方便，知不可从乐至乐，要当从苦至乐。犹如，摩呵男！大村左右有大池水，纵广] cf.【丽】一由旬，水满其中。若复有人，来取彼一渧水。云何，摩呵男！水何者为多？一渧水多？为池水多乎？」

Mahanama said, "The pond water is more, not the drop of water."

摩呵男曰：「池水多，非一渧水多也。」

The World Honored One said, "It is like this. The noble disciples have completely exhausted all suffering, and there is no more remaining existence, like a drop of water. The lowest path among my disciples does not exceed seven deaths and seven births to reach the end of suffering. If one is vigorous and diligent, one will become a family-to-family practitioner and attain the path."

世尊告曰：「此亦如是。贤圣弟子诸苦已尽，永无复有，余存在者如一渧水耳。如我众中最下道者，不过七死七生而尽苦际。若复勇猛精进，便为家家，即得道迹。」

At that time, the World Honored One extensively taught the subtle Dharma to Mahanama. Having heard the Dharma, he immediately rose from his seat and departed.

尔时，世尊重与摩呵男说微妙之法，彼闻法已，即从座起而去。

At that time, Mahanama heard what the Buddha said and joyfully accepted it.

尔时，摩呵男闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
