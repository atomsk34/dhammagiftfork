EĀ 43.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

43.4 （四）提婆达

Parallel to: MN 29 / MN 30 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Karanda Bamboo Grove in the city of Rajagriha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikshus.

一时，佛在罗阅城迦兰陀竹园所，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, Devadatta, having lost his supernatural powers, was daily provided with five hundred cauldrons of food by Prince Ajatasatru. Then many bhikshus heard that Devadatta had lost his supernatural powers and was being supported by Ajatasatru. They went together to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, and sat to one side. Then the many bhikshus said to the Buddha, "Devadatta is extremely powerful and is now being supported by King Ajatasatru, who sends him five hundred cauldrons of food every day."

尔时，提婆达兜以失神足，阿阇世太子日遣五百釜食而供养之。是时，众多比丘闻提婆达兜以失神足，又为阿阇世所供养，共相将诣佛所，头面礼足，在一面坐。是时，众多比丘白佛言：「提婆达兜者极大威力，今为阿阇世王所供养，日遣五百釜食。」

At that time, the World Honored One, having heard these words, told the bhikshus, "Do not have this thought, coveting the offerings to the bhikshu Devadatta. That foolish person will perish because of those offerings. Because, bhikshus, Devadatta left home to study, but did not fulfill his wishes.

尔时，世尊闻此语已，告诸比丘：「汝等莫兴此意，贪提婆达兜比丘利养；彼愚人由此利养自当灭亡。所以然者，于是，比丘！提婆达兜所以出家学者，不果其愿。

Bhikshus, you should know that it is like a person leaving his village, holding a sharp axe, going to a big tree, with the intention of wanting the big tree, but when he arrives at the tree, he returns with branches and leaves. This bhikshu is also like this. He covets offerings and, because of those offerings, praises himself and disparages others. The practices of a bhikshu will not fulfill his wishes. Because of those offerings, he does not seek skillful means and arouse a courageous mind, like that person seeking treasures but not obtaining them, being abandoned by the wise.

「比丘当知，犹如有人出其村落，手执利斧，往诣大树，先意所望，欲望大树，及其到树，持枝叶而还。今此比丘亦复如是，贪著利养，由此利养，向他自誉，毁呰他人，比丘所行宜，则不果其愿。彼由此利养故，不求方便、起勇猛心，如彼人求宝不得，为智者所弃。

"If there is a bhikshu who, having obtained offerings, does not praise himself or disparage others, but sometimes speaks to others, saying, 'I am a person who upholds the precepts, while he is a person who violates the precepts.' The wishes of the bhikshu will not be fulfilled, like a person who abandons the root and returns home with a branch. The wise, having seen this, will say, 'Although this person returns home with a branch, he does not know the root.' This bhikshu is also like this. Having obtained offerings, he upholds the precepts and practices pure conduct, and cultivates samadhi. With this samadhi mind, he praises himself to others, saying, 'I have attained samadhi, while others do not.' The Dharma that the bhikshu should practice will also not be fulfilled. It is like a person seeking solid wood, going to a big tree, hoping for the solid wood, abandoning the branches and leaves, and taking the root to return home. The wise, having seen this, will say, 'This person distinguishes the root.' Now this bhikshu is also like this. He arouses offerings, upholds the precepts, does not praise himself, nor disparages others, and cultivates samadhi. It is also like this. Gradually practicing wisdom. Wisdom is the foremost in this Dharma. The bhikshu Devadatta did not attain wisdom and samadhi in this Dharma, nor did he fully possess the Dharma of precepts and discipline."

「设有比丘得利养已，亦不自誉，复不毁他人；或时复向他人自称说：『我是持戒之人，彼是犯戒之士。』比丘所愿者而不果获，如人舍根，持枝还家，智者见已：『此人虽持枝还家，然不识根。』此中比丘亦复如是，以得利养，奉持戒律，并修梵行，好修三昧。彼以此三昧心向他自誉：『我今得定，余人无定。』比丘所应行法亦不果获。犹如有人其求实木，往至大树，望其实，舍其枝叶，取其根持还。智者见已，便作是说：『此人别其根。』今此比丘亦复如是，兴起利养，奉持戒律，亦不自誉，复非毁他人，修行三昧，亦复如是，渐行智慧。夫智慧者，于此法中最为第一。提婆达兜比丘于此法中竟不获智慧、三昧，亦复不具戒律之法。」

A bhikshu addressed the World-Honored One, saying, "Why does Devadatta not understand the Dharma of precepts and discipline? He has accomplished spiritual powers and practices, and has this wisdom. Why does he not understand the Dharma of precepts and discipline? Wisdom leads to samadhi, and samadhi leads to precepts and discipline."

有一比丘白世尊言：「彼提婆达兜者，云何不解戒律之法？彼有神德成就诸行，有此智慧。云何不解戒律之法？有智慧则有三昧，有三昧则有戒律。」

The World-Honored One said, "The Dharma of precepts and discipline is a worldly constant; the accomplishment of samadhi is also a worldly constant; the spiritual powers of flying are also a worldly constant; the accomplishment of wisdom is the foremost meaning."

世尊告曰：「戒律之法者，世俗常数；三昧成就者，亦是世俗常数；神足飞行者，亦是世俗常数；智慧成就者，此是第一之义。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

是时，世尊便说此偈：

By attaining the spiritual powers through meditation, one may reach the highest, but it
is not ultimate. Without attaining the state of non-conditioning, one will fall back into
the five desires. Wisdom is the highest, without worries or concerns. After a long
time, one will attain the equal view and cut off the existence of birth and death.

「由禅得神足，　　至上不究竟，不获无为际，　　还堕五欲中。智慧最为上，　　无忧无所虑，久毕获等见，　　断于生死有。

"Bhikṣus, you should know that through this method, you can know that Devadatta does not understand the Dharma of precepts and discipline, nor does he understand wisdom and the practice of samādhi. You bhikṣus should not be like Devadatta, who is attached to material gain and honor. Material gain and honor lead one to an evil state, not to a good destination. If one is attached to material gain and honor, one will practice wrong views, leaving behind right views; practice wrong conduct, leaving behind right conduct; practice wrong speech, leaving behind right speech; practice wrong action, leaving behind right action; practice wrong livelihood, leaving behind right livelihood; practice wrong effort, leaving behind right effort; practice wrong mindfulness, leaving behind right mindfulness; practice wrong concentration, leaving behind right concentration. Therefore, bhikṣus! Do not give rise to the mind of material gain and honor, restrain it from arising; for the mind of material gain and honor that has already arisen, seek means to extinguish it. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「比丘当知，以此方便，知提婆达兜不解戒律之法，亦复不解智慧、三昧之行。汝等比丘，莫如提婆达兜贪著利养。夫利养者，堕人恶处，不至善趣。若著利养，便习邪见，离于正见；习于邪治，离于正治；习于邪语，离于正语；习于邪业，离于正业；习于邪命，离于正命；习邪方便，离正方便；习于邪念，离于正念；习于邪定，离于正定。是故，比丘！勿起利养之心，制令不起；已起利养之心，求方便而灭之。如是，比丘！当作是学。」

"I will now expound this subtle and wonderful Dharma." More than sixty bhikṣus abandoned their Dharma robes and returned to lay life, while more than sixty bhikṣus attained the exhaustion of taints, the purity of the Dharma eye, and the exhaustion of all defilements.

当说此微妙之法，六十余比丘舍除法服，习白衣行；复有六十余比丘，漏尽意解，诸尘垢尽，得法眼净。

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
