EĀ 43.6

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

43.6 （六）牧牛

Parallel to: MN 34 / SĀ 1248

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was by the Ganges River in the country of Magadha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus.

一时，佛在摩竭国神祇恒水侧，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "It is like a foolish and ignorant cowherd in Magadha who wants to lead his cows across the Ganges River from this shore to the other shore. Without considering the shallow and deep places on both shores, he drives the cows into the water. First, he crosses the thin ones, but the calves are still small and exhausted in the middle of the water, unable to reach the other shore. Next, the cows in the middle of the stream, neither fat nor thin, also cannot cross and suffer in the water. Finally, the strongest ones also suffer in the water.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「犹如摩竭牧牛人愚惑，少智慧，意欲从恒水此岸，渡牛至彼岸，亦复不观彼此之岸深浅之处，便駈牛入水，先渡瘦者，又犊尚小，在水中央，极为羸劣，不能得至彼岸。复次，渡中流之牛不肥不瘦，亦不得渡，于中受其苦恼。次复，渡极有力者，亦在水中，受其困厄。

Now, the bhikṣus in my assembly are also like this. Their minds are dull and without wisdom. They do not distinguish the stages of birth and death, nor do they distinguish the bridge and boat of Māra. They want to cross the stream of birth and death, but they do not practice the Dharma of precepts. They are then taken advantage of by Māra. Following the wrong path, they seek nirvāṇa, hoping to attain liberation, but they will never succeed. They create their own offenses and also cause others to fall into offenses. It is like the wise and intelligent cowherd in Magadha who wants to lead his cows across to the other shore. He first observes the shallow and deep places and first crosses the strongest cows to the other shore; next, the cows in the middle of the stream, neither fat nor thin, can also cross to the other shore; next, the weakest ones can also cross without difficulty, and the young calves follow behind and cross without hindrance.

「今我众中比丘亦复如是，心意暗钝，无有慧明，不别生死位，不别魔之桥船，意欲渡生死之流，不习于禁戒之法，便为波旬得其便也。从邪道求于涅槃，望得灭度，终不果获。自造罪业，复堕他人著罪中。犹摩竭牧牛人黠慧多智，意欲渡牛至彼此之岸，先观察深浅之处，前渡极盛力牛到彼岸；次渡中流之牛，不肥不瘦，亦得渡至彼岸；次渡极羸者，亦渡无他，小犊寻从其后而济渡无为。

Bhikṣus! The Tathāgata is also like this. He well observes this world and the next world, and observes the sea of birth and death and the path of Māra. He himself crosses the difficulties of birth and death by the Eightfold Right Path. He also uses this path to liberate those who are not liberated, just like the right path of the cows. When one is right, the others all follow. My disciples are also like this. They exhaust the taints and accomplish the absence of taints, liberation of mind and liberation through wisdom. In this present life, they personally realize and roam freely, liberating themselves from the realm of Māra and reaching the state of non-conditioning. It is like those strong cows crossing the Ganges River and reaching the other shore. My śrāvakas are also like this. They sever the five lower fetters and become anāgāmins. They attain parinirvāṇa there and do not return to this world. They liberate themselves from the realm of Māra and reach the state of non-conditioning. It is like those cows in the middle of the stream, neither fat nor thin, who can cross the Ganges River without any difficulty. My disciples are also like this. They sever the three fetters and weaken lust, anger, and delusion. They become sakṛdāgāmins and come to this world to end suffering. They sever the realm of Māra and reach the state of non-conditioning. It is like those thin cows leading their young calves across the Ganges River. My disciples are also like this. They sever the fetters and become srota-āpannas. They will definitely attain liberation, liberate themselves from the realm of Māra, and liberate themselves from the difficulties of birth and death. It is like those young calves following their mothers to cross.

「比丘！如来亦复如是，善察今世、后世，观生死之海，魔之径路，自以八正道度生死之难。复以此道度不度者，犹如道牛之正；一正，余者悉从。我弟子亦复如是，尽有漏成无漏，心解脱、智慧解脱，于现法中以身作证而自游化，度魔境界至无为处。亦如彼有力之牛渡彼恒水得至彼岸。我声闻亦复如是，断五下结，成阿那含，于彼般涅槃不还来此间，度魔境界至无为处。如彼中流之牛，不肥不瘦，得渡恒水而无疑难。我弟子亦复如是，断三结使，婬、怒、痴薄，成斯陀含，来至此世，尽于苦际，断魔境界至无为之处。如彼瘦牛将诸小犊得渡彼恒水，我弟子亦复如是，断结使，成须陀洹，必至得度，度魔境界，度生死之难。如彼小犊从母得渡。我弟子亦复如是，持信奉法，断魔诸缚，至无为处。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说斯偈：

What Māra should have obtained, the limits of birth and death, the
Tathāgata has now fully realized, illuminating the world with wisdom. What the
Buddhas have realized, the brāhmaṇas do not understand, still wandering on the
shore of birth and death, also liberating those who are not yet
liberated. Now these five kinds of people, and others innumerable, wishing to
liberate themselves from the difficulties of birth and death, exhaust the Buddha's
majestic spiritual power.

「魔王所应获，　　不究生死边，如来今究竟，　　世间现慧明。诸佛所觉了，　　梵志不明晓，犹涉生死岸，　　兼度未度者。今此五种人，　　及余不可计，欲度生死难，　　尽佛威神力。

"Therefore, bhikṣus! You should concentrate your minds, practice without negligence, and also seek means to accomplish the path of the eightfold noble path. Relying on the noble path, you will be able to liberate yourselves from the ocean of birth and death. The reason is this: just like that foolish cowherd, the non-Buddhist brāhmaṇas are like that, drowning themselves in the currents of birth and death, and also causing others to fall into the mire of offenses. That river is the ocean of birth and death. The wise cowherd is the Tathāgata; he has crossed the difficulties of birth and death by means of the noble eightfold path. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should seek means to accomplish the eightfold noble path. Thus, bhikṣus! You should train in this way."

「是故，比丘！当专其心，无放逸行，亦求方便，成贤圣八品之道；依贤圣道已，便能自度生死之海。所以然者，犹如彼愚牧牛之人，外道梵志是也，自溺生死之流，复堕他人著罪中。彼恒水者，即是生死之海也。彼黠慧牧牛者，如来是也；度生死之难，由圣八品道。是故，比丘！当求方便，成八圣道。如是，诸比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
