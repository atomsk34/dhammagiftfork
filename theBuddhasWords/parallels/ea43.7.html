EĀ 43.7

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

43.7 （七）无根信

Parallel to: DN 2 / DĀ 27

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was staying in the Jivaka Mango Grove in Rajagriha, together with a group of 1,250 disciples, all of whom were arhats, their defilements exhausted, their six supernormal powers clear and penetrating, except for one person, the bhiksu Ānanda.

一时，佛在罗阅城耆婆伽梨园中，与千二百五十弟子俱，尽是阿罗汉，诸漏已尽，六通清彻，唯除一人，阿难比丘也。

At that time, King Ajatasatru, on the fifteenth day of the seventh month, when the stars appeared at midnight, told Queen Candraprabha, "Today is the full moon, the night is very clear. What should we do?"

尔时，王阿阇世七月十五日受岁时，夜半明星出现，告月光夫人曰：「今十五日月盛满，极为清明，当应施行何事？」

The queen replied, "Today is the day of the precepts. We should have music and entertainment, enjoy the five desires."

夫人报曰：「今十五日说戒之日，应当作倡伎乐，五欲自娱乐。」

When the king heard this, he was not pleased. He then told Prince Udayana, "Tonight is very clear. What should we do?"

时，王闻此语已，不入其怀。王复告优陀耶太子曰：「今夜极清明，应作何事？」

Prince Udayana said to the king, "Now that it is midnight and very clear, we should assemble the four divisions of troops and attack those foreign enemies who have not submitted."

优陀耶太子白王言：「如今夜半极清明，应集四种之兵，诸外敌异国不靡伏者，当往攻伐。」

When King Ajātaśatru heard this, he was also not pleased. He then said to Prince Abhaya, "Now that it is a very clear night, what should we do?"

是时，王阿阇世闻此语已，亦复不入其意。复语无畏太子曰：「如今极清明之夜，应何所施行？」

Prince Abhaya replied, "Now Nirgrantha Jñātiputra is skilled in various calculations and knows astronomy and geography. He is revered by the people. We can go to him and ask about this doubt. He will surely explain the most wonderful principles to you, without any hesitation."

无畏王子报曰：「今不兰迦叶，明诸算数，兼知天文地理，众人所宗仰。可往至彼问此疑难，彼人当与尊说极妙之理，永无留滞。」

When the king heard this, he was also not pleased. He then said to the minister Sunītha, "Now that it is a very clear night, what should we do?"

时，王闻此语已，亦不入其意。复语须尼摩大臣曰：「如今之夜极为清明，应何所施行？」

Sunītha said to the king, "Now that it is the middle of the night and very clear, but Ājīvaka is not far away and is very knowledgeable. I only wish that the great king would go and ask him what is appropriate."

须尼摩白王言：「如今夜半极为清明，然阿夷耑近在不远，多所晓了，唯愿大王往问其宜。」

When the king heard this, he was also not pleased. He then said to the brahmin Vāśa, "Now that it is the middle of the night and very clear, what should we do?"

王闻此语已，亦复不入其意。复告婆沙婆罗门曰：「如今夜半极为清明，应何所施行？」

The brahmin replied, "Now that it is the fifteenth day and very clear, but there is a Jaina near who is not far away. I only wish that the great king would go and ask him for the meaning."

婆罗门报曰：「如今十五日极为清明，然有瞿耶楼近在不远，唯愿大王往问其义。」

When the king heard this, he was also not pleased. He then said to the brahmin Māṭhara, "Now that it is the middle of the night and very clear, what should we do?"

时，王闻此语已，复不合其意。复语摩特梵志曰：「如今夜半极为清明，应作何事？」

The brahmin replied, "Great King, you should know that the Nirgrantha Jñātiputra is not far away. I only wish that the great king would go and ask him about the matter."

梵志报曰：「大王当知，彼休迦旃近在不远，唯愿大王往问其情。」

When the king heard this, he was also not pleased. He then said to the general Soma, "Now that it is the middle of the night and very clear, what should we do?"

王闻此语已，复不合其意。复告索摩典兵师曰：「如今夜半极为清明，应何所施行？」

Soma replied, "The astrologer Pilindavatsa is not far away. He is well-versed in calculations. We can go and ask him about the meaning."

索摩报曰：「先毕卢持近在不远，明诸算术，可往问其义。」

When the king heard these words, he was also not pleased. He then said to the prime minister Jyotiska, "Now that it is the fifteenth day and very clear, what should we do?"

王闻此言已，亦不合其意。复告最胜大臣曰：「如今十五日极为清明，应何所施行？」

Jyotiska said to the king, "There is a Nirgrantha who has studied all the scriptures and is the foremost among teachers. I only wish that the great king would go and ask him about the meaning."

最胜白王言：「今有尼揵子，博览诸经，师中最上，唯愿大王往问其义。」

When the king heard these words, he was not pleased. He further thought, "These people are foolish and deluded, unable to distinguish true from false, and have no skillful means."

王闻此语已，不合其意。复作是思惟：「此诸人等，斯是愚惑，不别真伪，无有巧便。」

At that time, Prince Jivaka was on the king's left side. The king looked at Jivaka and said, "Now at midnight it is extremely clear. What should be done?"

尔时，耆婆伽王子在王左侧，王顾语耆婆伽曰：「如今夜半极为清明，应何所施行？」

At that time, Jivaka immediately knelt before the king and said, "Now the Tathāgata is not far away, staying in the Poor Grove with 1,250 disciples. I only wish that the great king would go and ask him about the meaning. That Tathāgata is illuminating and radiant, without any doubts or hindrances. He knows the affairs of the three times and is well-versed in all of them. He will surely explain the matter to the king, and the king's doubts will suddenly be awakened."

是时，耆婆伽即前长跪，而白王言：「今如来近在不远，游贫聚园中，将千二百五十弟子，唯愿大王往问其义。然彼如来为明为光，亦无疑滞，知三世事，靡不贯博，自当与王演说其事，王所有狐疑，［火＊霍］然自悟。」

At that time, King Ajātaśatru, having heard Jivaka's words, was overjoyed and a good mind arose in him. He immediately praised Jivaka, saying, "Excellent! Excellent! Prince! You have spoken well. The reason is that my body and mind are extremely burning now, and I have also killed my father for no reason. I have always thought at night, 'Who can awaken my mind?' Now what Jivaka has just said is exactly in line with my intention. It is wonderful! It is extraordinary! Hearing the sound of the Tathāgata, I am suddenly greatly awakened."

是时，王阿阇世闻耆婆伽语已，欢喜踊跃，善心生焉，即叹耆婆伽曰：「善哉！善哉！王子！快说斯言。所以然者，我今身心极为炽然，又复无故取父王杀，我恒长夜作是念：『谁堪任悟我心意者？』今耆婆伽向所说者，正入我意。甚奇！甚特！闻如来音响，［火＊霍］然大悟。」

At that time, the king spoke this verse to Jivaka:

时，王向耆婆伽便说此偈：

Today is extremely clear, but my mind cannot be awakened
You all should speak up and ask who the meaning
should be asked of. The non-Buddhist Ājīvikas, Nirgranthas, and Brahma's
disciples - these cannot be relied upon, they cannot provide
any benefit. Today is extremely clear, the full moon is
flawless. Now I ask Jivaka, who should the meaning be asked of?

「今日极清明，　　心意不得悟，汝等人人说，　　应往问谁义。不兰阿夷耑，　　尼揵梵弟子，斯等不可依，　　不能有所济。今日极清明，　　月满无瑕秽，今问耆婆伽，　　应往问谁义。」

At that time, Jivaka again replied to the king in verse:

是时，耆婆伽复以偈报王曰：

Hearing his gentle voice, I was freed from the makaras. I only wish
to go to the Buddha now, to dwell forever in the realm of fearlessness.

「闻其柔软音，　　得脱摩竭鱼，唯愿时诣佛，　　永处无畏境。」

At that time, the king again replied in verse:

时，王复以偈报曰：

The deeds I have done in the past have been of no benefit
to the Buddha. I harmed that true son of the Buddha, named Bimbisāra
Now I am extremely ashamed and have no face to see the World-Honored
One. How can you now speak so that I may go and see him?

「我昔所施行，　　于佛无益事，害彼真佛子，　　名曰频婆娑。今极怀羞耻，　　无颜见世尊，汝今云何说，　　使吾往见之？」

At that time, Jīvaka again replied to the king in verse:

是时，耆婆伽复以偈报王曰：

The Buddhas have no partiality, all afflictions are forever eliminated, with an equal
mind without duality - this is the meaning of the Buddha's teachings. Even
if one were to anoint one's right hand with sandalwood incense and cut
off one's left hand with a knife, one's mind would not increase or
decrease. Just as one would cherish one's own son, with no second thought
one should hold the mind towards Devadatta, with no difference between enemies and
friends. I only wish that the great king would bend and go to
pay homage to the Tathāgata's countenance. He will cut off your doubts and
should not linger any longer.

「诸佛无彼此，　　诸结永已除，平等无二心，　　此是佛法义。设以栴檀香，　　以涂右手者，执刀断左手，　　心不起增减。如愍罗云子，　　一息更无二，持心向提婆，　　怨亲无有异。唯愿大王屈，　　往觐如来颜，当断其狐疑，　　勿足有留滞。」

At that time, King Ajātaśatru told Prince Jīvaka, "You should quickly prepare five hundred tusked elephants, five hundred she-elephants, and light five hundred lamps."

是时，王阿阇世告耆婆伽王子曰：「汝今速严驾五百牙象，五百牸象，然五百灯。」

Jīvaka replied, "Yes, Your Majesty!" At that time, Prince Jīvaka immediately prepared a thousand elephants and lit five hundred lamps, and said to the king, "The preparations are complete. The king may know the time."

耆婆伽对曰：「如是，大王！」是时，耆婆伽王子即时严驾千象，及然五百灯，前白王言：「严驾已办，王知是时。」

At that time, King Ajātaśatru, together with his retinue, went to the grove. On the way, he suddenly became fearful and his hair stood on end. He turned back and said to Prince Jīvaka, "Am I not being deceived by you now? Are you not going to hand me over to my enemies?"

尔时，王阿阇世将诸营从，往诣梨园中，中路便怀恐怖，衣毛皆竖，还顾谓耆婆伽王子曰：「吾今将非为汝所误乎？将非持吾与怨家耶？」

Jīvaka said to the king, "It is not so. I only wish that the great king would advance a little further. The Tathāgata is not far from here."

耆婆伽白王：「实无此理，唯愿大王小复前进，今如来去此不远。」

At that time, King Ajātaśatru was still fearful in his mind. He again said to Jīvaka, "Are you not deceiving me? I have heard that the Tathāgata has 1,250 disciples. Why do I not hear their voices now?"

时，王阿阇世意犹怀恐，重告耆婆伽曰：「将非为汝所惑，又闻如来将千二百五十弟子，今不闻其声？」

Jīvaka replied, "The Tathāgata's disciples are always in samādhi, without any disordered thoughts. I only wish that the great king would advance a little further."

耆婆伽报曰：「如来弟子恒入三昧，无有乱想，唯愿大王小复前进。」

King Ajātaśatru then got off his carriage and walked to the entrance of the lecture hall. He stood there silently, observing the assembly of sages. He then turned to Jīvaka and said, "Where is the Tathāgata now?" At that time, the entire assembly of sages all entered the Flaming Light Samādhi, illuminating the lecture hall, reaching everywhere.

阿阇世王即下车，步入门至讲堂前，默然而立，观察诸圣众，还顾谓耆婆伽曰：「如来今在何处？」尔时，一切圣众尽入炎光三昧，照彼讲堂，靡不周遍。

At that time, Jīvaka immediately knelt down and pointed to the Tathāgata with his right hand, saying, "This is the Tathāgata, right in the center, like the sun breaking through the clouds."

是时，耆婆伽即时长跪，伸右手指示如来言：「此是如来，最在中央，如日披云。」

At that time, King Ajātaśatru said to Jīvaka, "How wonderful! How extraordinary! The minds of this assembly of sages are so concentrated. What is the cause of this light?"

是时，王阿阇世语耆婆伽曰：「甚奇！甚特！今此圣众心定乃尔。复以何缘有此光明？」

Jīvaka said to the king, "It is because of the power of samādhi that light is emitted."

耆婆伽白王：「三昧之力故放光明耳。」

The king said again, "As I observe the assembly of sages today, they are extremely tranquil, causing my Prince Udayana to also be tranquil and without activity." At that time, King Ajātaśatru joined his palms and said, "I only wish that the World-Honored One will observe and see me."

王复告曰：「如我今日观察圣众，极为寂然，使我优陀耶太子亦当如是寂然无为。」时，王阿阇世叉手，自称说曰：「唯愿世尊当见观察。」

The World-Honored One said, "Welcome, great king!" Hearing the Tathāgata's voice, the king was extremely joyful. The Tathāgata then saw and addressed the king by name.

世尊告曰：「善来，大王！」王闻如来音响，极怀欢喜，如来乃见称说王号。

At that time, King Ajātaśatru immediately went to where the Buddha was, prostrated with his five limbs touching the ground, placed his two hands on the Tathāgata's feet, and said, "I only wish that the World-Honored One will see and have compassion, accept my repentance. The father king was innocent, yet I took his life. I only wish to repent and not commit it again in the future, to reform and improve."

时，王阿阇世即至佛所，五体投地，以两手著如来足上，而自称说：「唯愿世尊当见垂愍，受其悔过。父王无罪而取害之，唯愿受悔，后更不犯，自改往修来。」

The World-Honored One said, "Now is the right time. Repent in time, lest there be loss. When a person in the world has faults and can repent, this is called a superior person. In my Dharma, this is extremely vast. It is appropriate to repent." At that time, the king bowed at the Tathāgata's feet and sat to one side.

世尊告曰：「今正是时，宜时悔过，无令有失。夫人处世，有过能自改者，斯名上人。于我法中，极为广大，宜时忏悔。」是时，王礼如来足已，住一面坐。

At that time, the king said to the Buddha, "I wish to ask something. If the Tathāgata allows it, I dare to ask."

时王白佛言：「唯愿欲有所问，如来听者，乃敢问耳。」

The Buddha told the king, "If you have doubts or difficulties, ask them now."

佛告王曰：「有疑难者，宜时问之。」

The king said to the Buddha, "Can one create merit in this present life and receive its rewards in this present life?"

王白佛言：「于现世造福，得受现报不乎？」

The Buddha told the king, "Have you ever asked anyone about this meaning in the past?"

佛告王曰：「古昔已来颇以此义，曾问人乎？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I have asked others about this meaning before. I once asked Nirgrantha Jnatiputra, 'How is it, Nirgrantha Jnatiputra! Can one create merit in this present life and receive its rewards in this present life?' Nirgrantha Jnatiputra replied to me, 'There is no merit, no giving, no good or bad rewards in this world or the next. There are no accomplished arhats in the world.' When I asked about the rewards of receiving fruits, he replied, 'No.' It is like asking about melons and being told about wood. This Jnatiputra is also like this. At that time, I thought, 'This brahman does not understand the meaning. The meaning asked by the noble king is answered by this person with other matters.' World-Honored One! I wanted to cut off his head, but I did not accept his words and immediately dismissed him.

王白佛言：「我昔曾以此义而问他人不兰迦叶：『云何，不兰迦叶！现世作福得受现报乎？』不兰迦叶报我言：『无福、无施，无今世、后世善恶之报，世无阿罗汉等成就者。』当我尔时，问此受果之报，彼报曰：『无也。』如有人问以瓜义，报以［木＊奈］理，今此迦叶亦复如是。时，我作是念：『此梵志已不解义，豪族王种所问之义，此人方便引余事报我。』世尊！我即欲断其头，即不受其语，寻发遣之。

Then I went to Ajivikas and asked about this meaning. Ajivikas replied to me, 'If one kills sentient beings on the left bank of the Ganges, one commits immeasurable crimes, but there is no sin, and no evil karmic retribution.' At that time, World-Honored One! I again thought, 'I am now asking about the meaning of receiving rewards in this present life, but this person uses killing to answer me. It is like asking about pears and being told about wood.' I then left.

「时，我复至阿夷耑所，而问此义，阿夷耑报我言：『若于江左杀害众生，作罪无量，亦无有罪，亦无恶果之报。』时我，世尊！复作是念：『我今问现世受报之义，此人乃持杀害报吾，犹如有人问梨之义，以［木＊奈］报之。』即舍之去。

I went to the place of the Jains and asked about this meaning. They replied to me, 'If one creates merits on the right bank of the Ganges, it is immeasurable, but there is no good karmic retribution in it.' At that time, I again thought, 'The meaning I am asking about is not answered at all.' I then left.

「复至瞿耶楼所，而问此义。彼人报我曰：『于江右边造诸功德，不可称计，于中亦无善之报。』我尔时复作是念：『吾今所问义者，竟不报其理。』复舍之去。

I went to the place of the Nirgranthas and asked about this meaning. They replied, 'Only one person appears in the world, one person dies, and one person goes back and forth to experience suffering and happiness.' At that time, I again thought, 'The present life retribution I am asking about is answered with birth and death.' I then left.

「复往至彼休迦栴所，而问斯义。彼人报曰：『唯有一人出世，一人死，一人往返受其苦乐。』时我复作是念：『我今所问现世之报，乃将生死来相答。』复舍之去。

I went to ask the former Vairatiyas about this meaning. They replied to me, 'The past has ceased and will not arise again; the future has not yet come and does not exist; the present does not abide, and what abides is subject to change.' At that time, I again thought, 'The present life retribution I am asking about is answered with the characteristics of the three time periods. This is not the right principle.' I then left.

「往问先毘卢持如此之义。彼人报我言：『过去者以灭，更不复生；当来未至，亦复不有；现在不住，住者即变易。』时我复作是念：『我今所问现世之报，乃持三世相酬，此非正理。』即复舍去。

I went to the Nirgranthas and asked about this meaning: 'How is it, Nirgranthas! Is there any present life merit that can be experienced in the present life?' They replied to me, 'There is no cause or condition for sentient beings' bondage; there is also no cause or condition for sentient beings' attachment and bondage; there is no cause or condition for sentient beings' purity.' At that time, I again thought, 'These Brahmins are foolish and deluded, not distinguishing the true. They are like the blind without eyes. The meaning I asked about is not answered at all, like playing with the wheel-turning sage king's seed.' I then left.

「至尼揵子所，而问此义：『云何，尼揵子！颇有现世作福得受现世报也？』彼报我言：『无因无缘，众生结缚；亦无有因，亦无有缘，众生著结缚；无因无缘，众生清净。』时我复作是念：『此梵志等斯是愚惑，不别真为，犹盲无目，所问之义，竟不相报，如似弄转轮圣王种。』寻复舍之去。今我，世尊！故问其义，现世作福现受报也？唯愿世尊演说其义。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said, "Great King! I now ask you about the meaning, please answer as you wish. Great King! Is there a wine steward, cook, or steward who rewards and protects the left and right attendants?"

尔时，世尊告曰：「大王！我今问汝义，随所乐报之。大王！颇有典酒、厨宰，赏护物左右使人乎？」

The king said to the Buddha, "Yes, there is."

王白佛言：「唯然，有之。」

"If those attendants work for a long time, will you also reward them?"

「设彼使人执劳经久，复当赏遗不乎？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I will reward them according to their work, so that there will be no resentment."

王白佛言：「随功叙用，不令有怨。」

The Buddha said to the king, "With this method, one knows that making merit in this life will receive its reward in this life. How is it, great king! Since you are in a high position, caring for the people with courtesy, will you also reward them?"

佛告王曰：「以此方便，知现世作福得受现报。云何，大王！既处高位，恤民以礼，当复赏遗不乎？」

The king said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One! Eating together is equally sweet, and life is not hated."

王白佛言：「唯然，世尊！食共同甘，并命不恨。」

The Buddha said to the king, "With this method, one knows that in the past, one was born in an extremely humble place, gradually accumulating merit, and now enjoying happiness with the king. For this reason, making merit in this life will receive its reward in this life."

佛告王曰：「当以此方便，知昔日出处极卑，渐渐积功，与王同欢，以是之故，现世作福得受现报。」

The Buddha said to the king, "Those who have worked for years come to the king and say, 'Our merit has been established, and we are friends with the king. We wish to ask for what we desire from the king.' Would the king grant it to them or not?"

佛告王曰：「彼有劳之人，经历年岁，来白王言：『我等功劳已立，王所朋知，欲从王求意所愿。』王当以与不乎？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I would not go against their wishes."

王白佛言：「随彼所愿而不违之。」

The Buddha said to the king, "Those who have worked for you wish to resign from the king, shave their heads and beards, wear the three Dharma robes, leave home to study the path, and cultivate holy life. Would the king allow it or not?"

佛告王曰：「彼有劳之人，欲得辞王，剃除须发，著三法衣，出家学道，修清净行。王听不乎？」

The king said to the Buddha, "Yes, I allow it."

王白佛言：「唯然，听之。」

The Buddha said to the king, "If the king sees them shaving their heads and beards, leaving home to study the path, and being by my side, what would the king do?"

佛告王曰：「设王见彼剃除须发，出家学道，在我左右；王欲何所施为？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I would serve and make offerings, paying homage according to the time."

王白佛言：「承事供养，随时礼拜。」

The Buddha said to the king, "By this method, know that making merit in the present life receives its reward in the present life. If there are those who are diligent and uphold the precepts completely, without any transgression; what would the king do to them?"

佛告王曰：「以此方便，知现作福得受现报。设彼有劳之人，持戒完具，无有所犯；王欲何所施行？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I would provide them with clothing, food and drink, bedding, bedding, and medicine for illness and weakness for the rest of their lives, not letting them be lacking."

王白佛言：「尽其形寿供给衣被、饮食、床敷卧具、病瘦医药，不使缺减。」

The Buddha said to the king, "By this method, know that making merit in this life receives its reward in this life. If there are those who have already become monks, exhausted their defilements, attained purity, liberated their minds and wisdom, realized it themselves, and roam freely, their births and deaths are exhausted, their holy life are established, their tasks are accomplished, and they will not be reborn again, knowing it as it is. What would the king do to them?"

佛告王曰：「以此方便，知现身作福得受现报。设复彼人已作沙门，尽有漏，成无漏，心解脱、智慧解脱，己身作证而自游化，生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受有，如实知之。王欲何为？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I would serve and provide them with clothing, food and drink, bedding, bedding, and medicine for illness and weakness for the rest of my life, not letting them be lacking."

王白佛言：「我当尽形寿，承事供养衣被、饮食，床卧具、病瘦医药，不令有乏。」

The Buddha said to the king, "By this method, know that making merit in this life receives its reward in this life. If there are those who exhaust their life span and attain parinirvāṇa in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder, what would the king do to them?"

佛告王曰：「当以此方便，知现世作福得受现报。设复彼人尽其形寿，于无余涅槃界而般涅槃者，王欲何所施设？」

The king said to the Buddha, "I will build great divine temples at the four crossroads, and also make offerings of incense and flowers, hang banners, flags, and canopies, and serve and pay respect to them. Because they are heavenly bodies, not human bodies."

王白佛言：「当于四道头起大神寺，兼以香华供养，悬缯、幡、盖，承事礼敬。所以然者，彼是天身，非为人身。」

The Buddha said to the king, "By this method, know that making merit in this life receives its reward in this life."

佛告王曰：「当以此方便，知现世作福得受现报。」

The king said to the Buddha, "I now understand this parable. From now on, I will believe and accept its meaning. I only wish that the World-Honored One would accept me as a disciple! I take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha. I now repent, like a fool, like one deluded, that I took the life of my father king without fault. I now take refuge in my body and life. I only wish that the World-Honored One would remove my offenses and expound the wonderful Dharma, so that I may be without activity for a long time. As I know the retribution for my own offenses, there is no good basis."

王白佛言：「我今以此譬喻于中受解，今日世尊重演其义。自今已后信受其义，唯愿世尊受为弟子！自归于佛、法、比丘僧。今复忏悔，如愚、如惑，父王无过而取害之，今以身命自归，唯愿世尊除其罪愆，演其妙法，长夜无为，如我自知所作罪报，无有善本。」

The Buddha said to the king, "There are two kinds of people in the world who die without guilt and are reborn in heaven in the time it takes to bend and stretch an arm. Who are the two? One is who does not create the basis for wrongdoing but cultivates goodness. The other is who, having committed wrongdoing, reforms what they have done. These are the two who die and are reborn in heaven, without obstruction."

佛告王曰：「世有二种人无罪而命终，如屈伸臂顷，得生天上。云何为二？一者不造罪本而修其善，二者为罪，改其所造。是谓二人而取命终生于天上，亦无流滞。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

If a person commits extremely evil deeds, but repents and turns away from them, reducing them to
a small fraction, and repents daily without ceasing, the roots of their sins will be forever eradicated.

「人作极恶行，　　悔过转微薄，日悔无懈息，　　罪根永已拔。

"Therefore, great king! You should govern with the Dharma, not with unrighteousness. Governing with the Dharma, when the body perishes and life ends, one is reborn in a good place, in heaven. When one's life ends there, one's fame spreads far and wide, heard in the four directions, and later people will commonly say: 'In the past, there was a king who governed righteously, without crookedness.' People will praise and transmit this. Wherever that person is reborn, their lifespan will be extended, without premature death. Therefore, great king! You should give rise to a joyful mind towards the Three Jewels, the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha. Thus, great king! You should train in this way."

「是故，大王！当以法治化，莫以非法。夫以法治化者，身坏命终，生善处天上。彼以命终，名誉远布，周闻四方，后人共传：『昔日有王，正法治化，无有阿曲。』人以称传，彼人所生之处，增寿益算，无有中夭。是故，大王！当发欢喜之心，向三尊，佛、法、圣众。如是，大王！当作是学。」

At that time, King Ajatasatru immediately rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and then withdrew. Not long after the king left, the Buddha told the bhikṣus: "If King Ajātaśatru had not killed his father, he would have attained the first fruit of the śramaṇa path today, and would have been among the four pairs and eight groups. He would have also attained the eightfold noble path, eliminated the eight kinds of love, and transcended the eight difficulties. Although it is so, he still has great fortune in obtaining rootless faith. Therefore, bhikṣus! For those who have committed offenses, they should seek means to attain rootless faith. Among my upāsakas, the one who has attained rootless faith is none other than Ajātaśatru."

尔时，阿阇世王即从座起，头面礼佛足，便退而去。王去不远，佛告诸比丘：「今此阿阇世王不取父王害者，今日应得初沙门果证，在四双八辈之中，亦复得贤圣八品道，除去八爱，超越八难，虽尔，今犹获大幸，得无根之信。是故，比丘！为罪之人，当求方便，成无根之信。我优婆塞中得无根信者，所谓阿阇世是也。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
