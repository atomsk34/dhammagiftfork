45.3 （三）

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

45.3 （三）

Parallel to: AN 9.6, MN 17

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World Honored One told the bhikṣus: "If a bhikṣu dwells in a village, the good dharmas will be destroyed and evil dharmas will increase. That bhikṣu should train thus: 'Now I dwell in a village, and evil dharmas increase while good dharmas diminish. My mindfulness is not concentrated, I do not exhaust the outflows, and I do not reach the place of peace and tranquility without outflows. The robes, food and drink, bedding and mats, and medicine for illness that I obtain are obtained with great difficulty.' That bhikṣu should train thus: 'Now I dwell in this village, and evil dharmas increase while good dharmas diminish. I also did not come to be a śramaṇa for the sake of robes, food and drink, bedding and mats, and medicine. The wish I sought is not yet fulfilled.' Moreover, that bhikṣu should leave the village far behind.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有比丘依村落住，善法消灭，恶法遂增。彼比丘当作是学：『我今在村落居止，恶法遂增，善法渐减，念不专一，不得尽有漏，不至无为安隐之处。我所得衣被、饮食、床卧具、病瘦医药，劳苦乃获。』彼比丘当作是学：『吾今住此村落之中，恶法遂增，善法消灭，我亦不以衣被、饮食、床卧具、医药，故来作沙门！吾所求愿者，今不获果。』又彼比丘当远离村落去。

"If there is a bhikṣu who dwells near a village, and the wholesome dharmas increase while the unwholesome dharmas diminish, and he obtains robes, food, bedding, and medicine through diligent effort, that bhikṣu should train thus: 'Now I dwell near this village, and the wholesome dharmas increase while the unwholesome dharmas diminish. The requisites of offerings are obtained through diligent effort. I did not leave home to study the path and practice the holy life for the sake of robes. The path I study and the vow I seek will surely accomplish the Dharma. I should serve and make offerings to it for the rest of my life.'"

「若复有比丘依村落住，善法增益，恶法消灭，所得衣裳、饮食、床卧具，勤劳乃获，彼比丘当作是学：『我今依此村落住，善法增益，恶法消灭，所得供养之具，勤劳乃得。有我不以衣被故出家学道，修于梵行。我所学道求愿者必成其法，应当尽形寿承事供养。』」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊便说偈曰：

"Do not be attached to clothing, food and drink, beds, bedding, and dwelling places. Do not come to this world with such thoughts. Do not leave home to study the path because of clothing and bedding. The reason for studying the path is to surely fulfill one's wishes. The bhikṣu should immediately abide in that village for the rest of his life. He will attain parinirvāṇa there, at the root of his life."

「衣被及饮食， 床卧及所安，不应贪著想， 亦莫来此世。不以衣被故， 出家而学道，所以学道者， 必果其所愿。比丘寻应时， 尽形住彼村，于彼般涅槃， 尽其命根本。

"At that time, if the bhikṣu is wandering in a quiet place among the villages, the good dharmas increase and the evil dharmas naturally cease. The bhikṣu should stay in that village for the rest of his life and not wander far away."

「是时，彼比丘若在人间静处所游之村，善法增益，恶法自灭，彼比丘尽形寿住彼村中，不应远游。」

At that time, Ānanda addressed the World-Honored One, saying, "The Tathāgata has always said that the four great elements depend on food for sustenance, and also depend on the mind and the Dharma to be contemplated. All wholesome dharmas arise in dependence on the mind. Moreover, those bhikṣus who reside in villages and towns, exhausting their mental energy in seeking clothing and food, how can they give rise to wholesome dharmas while residing in those villages and towns without wandering far?"

是时，阿难白世尊言：「如来常不说四大依食得存，亦依于心所念法？诸善之法依心而生？又彼比丘依村落住，劳苦精神乃求衣食。彼云何生善法，住彼村落而不远游？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "There are three kinds of clothing, food, bedding, and medicine for the sick. If a bhikṣu single-mindedly attends to these four things but does not attain what he desires, this is suffering. If he is able to be content and not give rise to attachment, the devas and people will rejoice on his behalf. Moreover, bhikṣus should study this way. I speak this meaning because of this. Therefore, Ānanda, bhikṣus should be mindful of few desires and contentment."

佛告阿难：「衣被、饭食、床卧具、病瘦医药有三种。若复比丘专念四事供养，所欲不果，此依是苦。若复兴知足之心，不起想著，诸天、人民代其欢喜。又比丘！当作是学。我由此故而说此义。是故，阿难！比丘当念少欲知足。」

Thus, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and upheld it.

如是，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
