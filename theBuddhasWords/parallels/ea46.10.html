EĀ 46.10

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

46.10 （一〇）观想

Parallel to: AN 10.57

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a bhikṣu came to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. The bhikṣu then said to the World-Honored One, "The Tathāgata has just now spoken to the bhikṣus about the ten kinds of thoughts. If one can cultivate them, one can sever the contaminants and accomplish the practice of being without contaminants. But, World-Honored One, I am not able to practice these thoughts. The reason is that my desire is excessive, and my body and mind are agitated, unable to find peace."

尔时，有一比丘至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，彼比丘白世尊言：「如来今日与诸比丘说：『十想之法，其能修者，断诸有漏，成无漏行。』如我，世尊！不堪任行此想。所以然者，欲心多故，身意炽盛，不得宁息。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣu, "You should now abandon the thought of purity and contemplate the thought of impurity; abandon the thought of permanence and contemplate the thought of impermanence; abandon the thought of self and contemplate the thought of no-self; abandon the thought of pleasure and contemplate the thought of displeasure. The reason is that if a bhikṣu contemplates the thought of purity, his desire will become rampant; if he contemplates the thought of impurity, desire will be absent. Bhikṣus should know that desire is impure, like a pile of excrement; desire is like a cuckoo, with many sounds; desire is without return, like a poisonous snake; desire is like an illusion, like snow melting in the sun; one should contemplate abandoning desire, like discarding a corpse. Desire will harm oneself, like a snake carrying poison; desire is insatiable, like drinking salty water; desire is difficult to satisfy, like the ocean swallowing rivers; desire is full of fear, like a rākṣasa village; desire is like an enemy, always to be avoided. Desire is like a little taste, like honey smeared on a knife; desire is unlovable, like white bones on the road; desire appears externally, like flowers growing in a latrine; desire is not true, like a painted bottle, with ugly things inside and a special appearance outside; desire is not solid, also like foam. Therefore, bhikṣu! You should contemplate abandoning the thought of desire and contemplate the thought of impurity. You, bhikṣu! Should remember the ten thoughts practiced under the Buddha Kāśyapa in the past. Now contemplate the ten thoughts again, and the mind with contaminants will be liberated."

尔时，世尊告彼比丘：「汝今当舍净想，思惟不净想；舍有常想，思惟无常想；舍有我想，思惟无我想；舍可乐想，思惟不可乐想。所以然者，若比丘思惟净想，欲心便炽盛；若思惟不净想，便无欲心。比丘当知，欲为不净，如彼屎聚；欲如鸜鹆，饶诸音响；欲无返复，如彼毒蛇；欲如幻化，如日消雪；当念舍欲，如弃塜间。欲还自害，如蛇怀毒；欲无厌患，如饮咸水；欲难可满，如海吞流；欲多可畏，如罗刹村；欲犹怨家，恒当远离。欲犹少味，如蜜涂刀；欲不可爱，如路白骨；欲现外形，如厕生华；欲为不真，如彼画瓶，内盛丑物，外见殊特；欲无牢固，亦如聚沫。是故，比丘！当念远离贪欲之想，思惟不净之想。汝今，比丘！当忆昔迦叶佛所奉行十想，今当重思惟十想，有漏心便解脱。」

At that time, the bhikṣu wept and shed tears, unable to stop himself. He immediately bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and said to the World-Honored One, "Only, World-Honored One! I have been foolish and deluded for a long time. The Tathāgata himself has just now spoken of the ten thoughts, and I am just about to distance myself from them. Now I confess and repent, and will not commit them again in the future! I only pray that the Tathāgata will accept my grave faults and forgive my inadequacies!"

尔时，彼比丘悲泣堕泪不能自止，即时头面礼佛，白世尊言：「唯，世尊！愚惑积久，如来躬自说十想，方欲远离。今自忏悔，后更不犯！唯愿如来受其重过，原恕不及。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "I allow you to change your faults and not commit them again. Also, the Tathāgata has spoken to you about the ten thoughts, but you are unwilling to uphold them."

佛告比丘：「听汝改过，勿复更犯，又如来与汝说十想而不肯奉持。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard the World-Honored One's instruction, in a secluded place, disciplined himself and contemplated. The reason why a noble son shaves his beard and hair, puts on the three Dharma robes, and practices the unsurpassed Brahma-conduct is to ascend to his aspirations; to have exhausted birth and death, established the Brahma-conduct, accomplished what is to be done, and no longer to receive a womb, knowing it as it is. At that time, the bhikṣu then became an arhat.

是时，彼比丘闻世尊教诫已，在闲静之处，克己思惟，所以族姓子，剃除须发，著三法衣，修无上梵行者，欲升其所愿；生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受胎，如实知之。尔时，彼比丘便成阿罗汉。

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and upheld it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时。佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are ten kinds of mindfulness that, when practiced extensively and distinguished, can completely eliminate desire, form, and formless realms, pride, and ignorance. What are the ten? They are mindfulness of the Buddha, mindfulness of the Dharma, mindfulness of the bhikṣu saṃgha, mindfulness of precepts, mindfulness of giving, mindfulness of heaven, mindfulness of tranquility and insight, mindfulness of counting breaths, mindfulness of the body, and mindfulness of death. These are called, bhikṣus, the ten kinds of mindfulness that, when practiced by sentient beings, can completely eliminate desire, form, and formless realms, all ignorance and pride. Thus, bhikṣus, you should train in this way."

尔时。世尊告诸比丘：「有十念广分别修行，尽断欲爱、色爱、无色爱、憍慢、无明。云何为十？所谓念佛、念法、念比丘僧、念戒、念施、念天、念止观、念安般、念身、念死。是谓，比丘！有众生修行此十念者，尽断欲爱，色爱，无色爱，一切无明、憍慢、皆悉除尽。如是，比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
