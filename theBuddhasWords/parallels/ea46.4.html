EĀ 46.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

46.4 （四）力

Parallel to: MN 12 (~) / AN 6.64 (~) / AN 10.21 / SĀ 684 / SĀ 701

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "The Tathāgata, having attained the ten powers and the four fearlessnesses, is able to roar the lion's roar in the great assembly. What are the ten powers? Here, the Tathāgata truly knows what is possible and what is not possible.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「如来成十种力，得四无所畏，在大众中能师子吼。云何为十力？于是，如来是处如实知之，非处如实知之。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the causes and conditions of other sentient beings in their places of rebirth.

「复次，如来处所，知他众生因缘处所受其果报。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the various kinds of realms, the various kinds of abodes, and the various kinds of entrances.

「复次，如来知若干种界、若干种持、若干种入，如实知之。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the various kinds of liberation and immeasurable liberation.

「复次，如来若干种解脱、无量解脱，如实知之。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the wisdom of other sentient beings, whether it is much or little.

「复次，如来知他众生智慧多少，如实知之。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the thoughts in the minds of others: he knows a mind with desire as having desire, and a mind without desire as without desire; he knows a mind with anger as having anger, and a mind without anger as without anger; he knows a mind with delusion as having delusion, and a mind without delusion as without delusion; he knows a mind with love as having love, and a mind without love as without love; he knows a mind with acceptance as having acceptance, and a mind without acceptance as without acceptance; he knows a distracted mind as having distraction, and an undistracted mind as without distraction; he knows a scattered mind as having scattering, and an unscattered mind as without scattering; he knows a small mind as having smallness, and a mind without smallness as without smallness; he knows a broad mind as having breadth, and a mind without breadth as without breadth; he knows an immeasurable mind as immeasurable, and a limited mind as limited, as he truly knows them. He knows a concentrated mind as having concentration, and a mind without concentration as without concentration; he knows a liberated mind as liberated, and a mind without liberation as without liberation.

「复次，如来知他众生心中所念，如实知之：有欲心知有欲心，无欲心知无欲心；有瞋恚心知有瞋恚心，无瞋恚心知无瞋恚心；有愚痴心知有愚痴心，无愚痴心知无愚痴心；有爱心知有爱心，无爱心知无爱心；有受心知有受心，无受心知无受心；乱心知有乱心，无乱心知无乱心；散心知有散心，无散心知无散心；少心知有少心，无少心知无少心；广心知有广心，无广心知无广心；无量心知无量心，有量心知有量心，如实知之。定心知有定心，无定心知无定心；解脱心知解脱心，无解脱心知无解脱心。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata completely knows the path of all minds that go to various destinations, whether it is one or two lives, three, four, five, ten, fifty, a hundred, a thousand, a hundred thousand, or countless lives, or whether it is an eon of formation, an eon of destruction, or countless eons of formation and destruction. He knows, 'I was born in such and such a place in the past, with such and such a name, eating such and such food, experiencing such and such suffering and happiness, with such and such a lifespan, dying here and being born there, dying there and being born here.' He remembers such countless past lives.

「复次，如来尽知一切所趣心之道，或一二生、三生、四生、五生、十生、五十生、百生、千生、亿百千生、无量生、成劫、败劫、无数成败劫中，我昔生彼处，名是，字是，食如此之食，受其苦乐，寿命长短，死此生彼，彼死生此。自忆如是无数宿命之事。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata knows the destinations of sentient beings' birth and death. With his divine eye, he sees the various types of sentient beings, whether their forms are good or bad, and whether their destinations are good or bad. He knows according to the seeds they have planted through their actions. He knows that some sentient beings, with their physical, verbal, and mental actions, slander the sages, create the karma of wrong views, and when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are born in hell. He also knows that some sentient beings, with their physical, verbal, and mental actions, do not slander the sages, always practice right views, and when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are born in the good places of the heavens. This is called the purity of the divine eye, seeing the destinations of the various types of sentient beings.

「复次，如来知众生生死之趣，以天眼观众生之类，善色、恶色、善趣、恶趣，随行所种，皆悉知之。或复众生身、口、意行恶，诽谤贤圣，造邪见业，身坏命终，生地狱中；或复众生身、口、意行善，不诽谤贤圣，恒行正见，身坏命终，生善处天上，是谓名为天眼清净，观众生类所趣之行。

Furthermore, the Tathāgata has exhausted all taints, attained the liberation of the mind free from taints and the liberation of wisdom, and has ended birth and death, established the practice of purity, accomplished what was to be done, and will not again experience existence. He truly knows this. This is called the Tathāgata's possession of these ten powers, which are called 'unattached'. He has attained the four fearlessnesses and roars the lion's roar in the great assembly, turning the wheel of the Dharma.

「复次，如来有漏尽，成无漏心解脱、智慧解脱；生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受有，如实知之。是谓如来有此十力，名为无著，得四无所畏，在大众中作师子吼，转于梵轮。

What are the Tathāgata's four fearlessnesses? If someone says that the Tathāgata has attained perfect enlightenment, and if there are sentient beings who want to know this, there is no such place. If there are any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who want to come and slander the Buddha, saying that he has not attained perfect enlightenment, there is no such place. Because there is no such place, he attains peace.

「云何如来得四无所畏？欲言如来成等正觉，若有众生，欲言知者，则无此处；若复有沙门、婆罗门欲来诽谤佛，不成等正觉者，则无此处；以无此处，则获安隐。

But today, I want to say that I have exhausted all taints. If there are any śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, gods, or Māra gods who come and say that I have not exhausted all taints, there is no such place. Because there is no such place, I attain peace.

「然我今日欲言已尽有漏，设复有沙门、婆罗门、天、若魔天来，欲言未尽有漏者，则无此处；以无此处，则获安隐。

Furthermore, the Dharma I teach leads the noble ones to attain the ultimate cessation of suffering. If there are any śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, gods, or Māra gods who come and say that the ultimate cessation of suffering has not been attained, there is no such place. Because there is no such place, I attain peace.

「复次，我所说法，贤圣得出要者，如实尽于苦际。设有沙门、婆罗门、天、若魔天来，欲言未尽苦际者，无此处；以无此处，则获安隐。

Furthermore, I teach the inner Dharma that leads to the lower realms. If there are any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who come and say that it is not so, there is no such place. This is called, bhikṣus, the Tathāgata's four kinds of fearlessness.

「复次，我所说内法者、堕恶趣者，设复有沙门、婆罗门来，欲言非者，则无此处。是谓，比丘！如来有四无所畏。

"If there are non-Buddhist ascetics who say, 'What powers and fearlessnesses does that śramaṇa Gautama have that he claims to be unsurpassed?' You should hold these ten powers and reply to them. If those non-Buddhist ascetics repeat their statement, 'We also accomplish the ten powers,' you bhikṣus should then ask, 'What are your ten powers?' At that time, those non-Buddhist ascetics will be unable to reply and will only increase their confusion. Because I have never seen any śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who claims to have attained the four kinds of fearlessness except for the Tathāgata. Therefore, bhikṣus, you should seek the means to accomplish the ten powers and four kinds of fearlessness. Thus, bhikṣus, you should train in this way."

「设有外道异学言：『彼沙门瞿昙！有何等之力，有何无畏，自称无著最尊？』汝等当持此十力往报之。设复外道异学重作是说：『我等亦成就十力。』汝等比丘复当问曰：『汝有何十力？』是时，外道异学则不能报也，遂增其惑。所以然者，我终不见沙门、婆罗门自称言得四无所畏，除如来者。是故，比丘！当求方便，成十力、四无所畏。如是，比丘！当作是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and upheld it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行！
