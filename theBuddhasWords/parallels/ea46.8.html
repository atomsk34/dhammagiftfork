EĀ 46.8

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

46.8 （八）十论

Parallel to: AN 10.27 / Kp 4 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Karanda Bamboo Grove in the city of Rajagriha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikshus.

一时，佛在罗阅城迦兰陀竹园所，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, many bhikshus put on their robes, took their alms bowls, and entered the city of Rajagriha to beg for food. At that time, the many bhikshus thought: "It is still early in the day when we enter the city to beg for food. We should go to the non-Buddhist ascetics to discuss the Dharma with them." At that time, the many bhikshus went to the place of the non-Buddhist ascetics. When the non-Buddhist ascetics saw the bhikshus coming from afar, they each said to themselves: "Be silent and do not speak loudly. The disciples of the Shakyamuni Buddha are coming here. The Dharma of the shamans praises those who are silent. Let them know that our true Dharma is not chaotic."

是时，众多比丘时到，著衣持钵，入罗阅城乞食。是时，众多比丘便作是念：「我等入城乞食，日犹故早，我等可至外道异学，与共论议。」是时，众多比丘便至外道异学所。时，诸外道遥见诸沙门来，各各自谓言：「各各寂寞，勿有高声语言，沙门瞿昙弟子今来此间；然沙门之法，称誉寂寞之人，令知我等正法，不乱有乱。」

At that time, the many bhikshus went to the place of the non-Buddhist ascetics, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side.

尔时，众多比丘便至外道异学所，共相问讯，在一面坐。

At that time, the non-Buddhist ascetics asked the bhikshus: "The Shakyamuni Buddha teaches the Dharma to his disciples, and those bhikshus all understand all dharmas and play freely without interference. We also teach the Dharma to our disciples and play freely. What is the difference between what we say and what you say? What is the difference in the Dharma and precepts?" At that time, the many bhikshus heard what the non-Buddhist ascetics said, neither praising it as good nor condemning it as bad. They then rose from their seats and left.

尔时，外道问诸比丘：「汝等，沙门瞿昙与诸弟子说此妙法，是诸比丘尽解一切诸法而自游戏不干？我等亦复与诸弟子说此妙法而自游戏。我之所说，与汝有何等异？有何差别？说法戒教一类无异。」是时，众多比丘闻外道异学所说，亦不称善，复非言恶，即从坐起，各退而去。

At that time, the many bhikshus said to each other: "We should take this meaning and report it to the World-Honored One. If the Tathāgata says something, we will remember and practice it."

是时，众多比丘自相谓言：「我等当持此义，往白世尊。若如来有所说者，我当念奉行。」

At that time, the many bhikshus entered Rajagriha to beg for alms. After returning to their rooms, they put away their robes and alms bowls, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed their heads at his feet, and stood to one side. At that time, the many bhikshus told the Tathāgata about this matter in detail. At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikshus: "When those non-Buddhist ascetics ask about this meaning, you should use these words to reply: 'One discourse, one meaning, one explanation, up to ten discourses, ten meanings, and ten explanations. What is the meaning of speaking these words?' If you use these words to ask them, they will not be able to reply. Those non-Buddhist ascetics will then increase their ignorance and confusion. The reason is that this is not within their realm.

尔时，众多比丘入罗阅城乞食已，还至房中，收摄衣钵，往至世尊所，头面礼足，住在一面。尔时，众多比丘以此缘本，尽向如来说之。尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「彼外道异学问此义已，汝等应持此语报之：『一论、一义、一演，乃至十论、十义] cf.【丽】、十演，说此语时有何等义？』设汝持此语往问者，彼人则不能报之，彼外道异学遂增愚惑。所以然者，非彼所有境界。

Therefore, bhikshus! I do not see any gods, people, Mara, Mara's heavenly beings, Sakra, or Brahma kings who can reply to these words, except for the Tathāgata and the Tathāgata's disciples who have heard from me. This is not discussed. One discourse, one meaning, one explanation. Although I have explained this meaning, why do I explain it? All sentient beings exist by eating. Without food, they die. That bhikshu equally despises, equally liberates, equally observes, equally analyzes the meaning, and equally ends suffering. They have the same meaning and are not two. What I say is precisely this.

「是故，比丘！我不见天及人民、魔、若魔天、释、梵天王能报此语者，除如来及如来弟子从吾闻者，此则不论。一论、一义、一演，我虽说此义，由何故而说乎？一切众生由食而存，无食则死，彼比丘平等厌患，平等解脱，平等观察，平等分别其义，平等尽苦际，同一义而不二。我所说者，正谓此耳。

One meaning, one discourse, one explanation, up to ten discourses, ten meanings, and ten explanations. Although I have explained this meaning, why do I explain it? What is meant by name and form? Namely, feeling, perception, memory, and thought. This is called name. What is called form? Namely, the four great elements and form derived from the four great elements. This is called form. For this reason, it is called form. The second discourse, second meaning, and second explanation are explained for this reason. If a bhikshu equally despises, equally liberates, equally observes, equally analyzes the meaning, and equally ends suffering.

「一义、一论、一演，乃至十论、十义、十演，我虽说此义，由何说乎？名与色，彼何等谓名？所谓痛、想、念、更、思惟，是谓名也。彼云何名为色耶？四大及四大所造色，是谓名为色。以此缘本，故名为色也。二论、二义、二演者，由此因缘故，我今说之。若比丘平等厌患，平等解脱，平等观察，平等分别其义，平等尽其苦际。

Three discourses, three meanings, and three explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the three feelings. What are the three? Namely, painful feeling, pleasant feeling, and neither painful nor pleasant feeling. What is called pleasant feeling? Namely, a pleasant thought in the mind, not scattered. This is called pleasant feeling. What is called painful feeling? Namely, a confused and unsettled mind, thinking various thoughts. This is called painful feeling. What is called neither painful nor pleasant feeling? Namely, a feeling in the mind that is neither painful nor pleasant, not fixed, not scattered, not thinking about dharmas or non-dharmas, always silent, with no mental record. Therefore, it is called neither painful nor pleasant feeling. These are the three feelings. If a bhikshu equally despises, equally liberates, equally observes, equally analyzes the meaning, and equally ends suffering.

「三论、三义、三演，由何等故而说此义乎？所谓三痛。云何为三？所谓苦痛、乐痛、不苦不乐痛。彼云何名为乐痛？所谓心中乐想，亦不分散，是谓名为乐痛。彼云何名为苦痛？所谓心中愦乱而不定一，思惟若干想，是谓名苦痛。彼云何名为不苦不乐痛？所谓心中无苦无乐想，复非一定，复非乱想，亦不思惟法与非法，恒自寂默，心无有记，是故名为不苦不乐痛。是谓三痛。若比丘平等厌患，平等解脱，平等观察，平等分别其义，平等尽其苦际。我所说三论、三义、三演者，正谓此耳。

Four meanings, four discourses, and four explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning again? Namely, the four truths. What are the four? Namely, the noble truths of suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. What is the truth of suffering? Namely, the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress, the suffering of being with what is disliked, the suffering of separation from what is loved, and the suffering of not getting what one desires. What is the truth of the origin of suffering? Namely, the root of craving associated with desire. This is called the truth of the origin of suffering. What is the truth of the cessation of suffering? Namely, the complete and utter extinction of that craving, never to arise again. This is called the truth of the cessation of suffering. What is the truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering? Namely, the noble eightfold path: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called the noble eightfold path. If a bhikshu equally despises, equally liberates, equally analyzes the meaning, equally observes, and equally ends suffering,

「四义、四论、四演，由何等故复说此义乎？所谓四谛。云何为四？所谓苦、习、尽、道圣谛。彼云何为苦谛？所谓生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、忧悲恼苦、怨憎会苦、恩爱别苦、所欲不得苦。彼云何名为习谛？所谓爱本与欲相应者，是谓名为习谛。彼云何名为苦尽谛？所谓彼爱永尽无余，更不复生，是谓名苦尽谛。彼云何名为苦要谛？所谓贤圣八品道：正见、正治、正语、正命、正业、正方便、正念、正三昧，是名为八品之道也。若比丘平等厌患，平等解脱，平等分别其义，平等观察，平等尽其苦际，是谓四论、四义、四演。我所说者，正谓此耳。

Five discourses, five meanings, and five explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the five faculties. What are the five? Namely, the faculties of faith, diligence, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom. What is called the faculty of faith? Namely, the noble disciples have faith in the Tathāgata's path. The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the One of Perfect Wisdom and Conduct, the Well-Gone One, the Knower of the World, the Unsurpassed One, the Trainer of those to be tamed, the Teacher of gods and men, the Buddha, the Blessed One, appears in the world. This is called the faculty of faith. What is called the faculty of diligence? Namely, the diligent practice of body, mind, and intention without weariness, eliminating unwholesome dharmas and increasing wholesome dharmas, following the mind and upholding it. This is called the faculty of diligence. What is called the faculty of mindfulness? Namely, the faculty of mindfulness means not forgetting what is recited, always keeping it in mind, upholding it without losing it, the conditioned and unconditioned dharmas, never forgetting them. This is called the faculty of mindfulness. What is called the faculty of concentration? Namely, the faculty of concentration means the mind being without confusion, without various thoughts, always single-mindedly focused. This is called the faculty of concentration. What is called the faculty of wisdom? Namely, knowing suffering, knowing its origin, knowing its cessation, and knowing the path. This is called the faculty of wisdom. These are called the five faculties. If a bhikshu equally liberates, equally analyzes the meaning, equally ends suffering,

「五论、五义、五演，我今所说，由何等故说？所谓五根。云何为五？信根、精进根、念根、定根、慧根。云何名为信根？所谓贤圣弟子，信如来道法，彼如来、至真、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐，出现于世，是谓名为信根。彼云何名为精进根？所谓身心意并勤劳不倦，灭不善法，使善增益，顺心执持，是谓名为精进根。彼云何名为念根？所谓念根者，所诵不忘，恒在心怀，总持不失，有为、无漏之法，终不忘失，是谓名为念根。彼云何名为定根。所谓定根者，心中无错乱，无若干想，恒专精一意，是谓名为三昧根。彼云何名智慧根？所谓知苦、知习、知尽、知道，是谓名智慧之根。此名五根也。比丘于中平等解脱，平等分别其义，平等尽其苦际。五论、五义、五演，我所说者，正谓此耳。

Six discourses, six meanings, and six explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the sixfold Dharma. What are the six? Namely, a bhikshu always practices loving-kindness with the body, whether in a quiet and pure room, always single-mindedly, worthy of respect and precious, always in harmony with others. This is called the first weighty Dharma of a bhikshu. Furthermore, practicing loving-kindness with the mouth, never speaking falsely, worthy of respect and precious, this is called the second weighty Dharma. Furthermore, practicing loving-kindness with the mind, without giving rise to hatred and jealousy, worthy of respect and precious, this is called the third weighty Dharma. Furthermore, if one obtains Dharma offerings, one should share the leftovers in one's bowl equally with fellow practitioners. This is called the fourth weighty Dharma, worthy of respect and precious. Furthermore, upholding the precepts without transgression, worthy of the praise of the wise, this is called the fifth weighty Dharma, worthy of respect and precious. Furthermore, having right view, one can attain the noble path, end suffering, and have an unconfused mind. Practicing equally with fellow practitioners, this is called the sixth Dharma, worthy of respect and precious. At that time, the bhikshu equally despises, equally liberates, equally analyzes the meaning, equally ends suffering,

「六论、六义、六演，我所说者，由何等故乎？所谓六重之法也。云何为六？于是，比丘恒身行慈心，若在闲净室中，常若一心，可尊可贵，恒与和合，是谓比丘第一重法。复次，口行慈心，终无虚妄，可敬可贵，是谓第二重法。复次，意行慈，不起憎嫉，可敬可贵，是谓第三重法。复次。若得法利之养，钵中遗余，与诸梵行之人等心施与，是谓第四重法可敬可贵。复次，奉持禁戒，无所脱失，贤人之所贵，是谓第五重法可敬可贵。复次，正见贤圣得出要，得尽苦际，意不错乱，与诸梵行之人等修其行，是谓第六之法可敬可贵。尔时，比丘平等厌患，平等解脱，平等分别其义，平等尽于苦际。六论、六义、六演，我所说者，正谓此耳。

Seven discourses, seven meanings, and seven explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the seven abodes of consciousness. What are the seven? Namely, some beings have various perceptions and various bodies, namely gods and humans. Some beings have various bodies and one perception, namely the Brahma-kāyika gods when they first emerge. Some beings have one perception and one body, namely the Ābhāsvara gods. Some beings have one body and various perceptions, namely the Śubhakṛtsna gods. Some beings have immeasurable space, namely the Ākāśānantyāyatana gods. Some beings have immeasurable consciousness, namely the Vijñānānantyāyatana gods. Some beings have immeasurable nothingness, namely the Ākiñcanyāyatana gods. Some beings have immeasurable neither perception nor non-perception, namely the Naivasaṃjñānāsaṃjñāyatana gods. These are called, bhikshu, the seven abodes of consciousness. Herein, the bhikshu equally liberates, up to equally ending suffering,

「七论、七义、七演，由何等故而说此乎？所谓七神识止处。云何为七？或有众生，若干想，若干种身，所谓天及人也。或有众生，若干种身一想，所谓梵迦夷天最初出时。或有众生，一想一身，所谓光音天是也。或有众生，一身若干想，所谓遍净天是也。或有众生，空处无量，所谓空处天是也。或有众生，识处无量，所谓识处天是也。或有众生，无所有处无量，所谓不用处天是也。或有众生，有想无想处无量，所谓有想无想天是也。是谓，比丘！七神止处。于是，比丘平等解脱，乃至平等尽于苦际。七论、七义、七演，我所说者，正谓此耳。

Eight discourses, eight meanings, and eight explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the eight worldly dharmas that revolve with the world. What are the eight? Gain, loss, slander, praise, fame, disgrace, suffering, and happiness. These are called the eight worldly dharmas that revolve with the world. If a bhikshu is equally liberated from these, up to ending suffering,

「八论、八义、八演，我所说者，由何等故而说此乎？所谓世间八法是随世回转。云何为八？利、衰、毁、誉、称、讥、苦、乐，是谓世间八法随世回转。若比丘于中平等解脱，乃至尽于苦际。八论、八义、八演，我所说者，正谓此耳。

Nine discourses, nine meanings, and nine explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the nine abodes of sentient beings. What are the nine? Namely, some beings have various bodies, namely gods and humans. Some beings have various bodies and one perception, namely the Brahma-kāyika gods when they first emerge. Some beings have one perception and one body, namely the Ābhāsvara gods. Some beings have one body and various perceptions, namely the Śubhakṛtsna gods. Some beings have immeasurable space, namely the Ākāśānantyāyatana gods. Some beings have immeasurable consciousness, namely the Vijñānānantyāyatana gods. Some beings have immeasurable nothingness, namely the Ākiñcanyāyatana gods. Some beings have immeasurable neither perception nor non-perception, namely the Naivasaṃjñānāsaṃjñāyatana gods. The formless beings and all that is born are the nine abodes of consciousness. Herein, the bhikshu is equally liberated, up to ending suffering.

「九论、九义、九演，我所说者，由何故而说此乎？所谓九众生居处。云何为九？若有众生。若干种身，所谓天及人。或有众生，若干种身一想，谓梵迦夷天最初出时是也。或有众生，一想一身，所谓光音天是也。或有众生，一身若干想，所谓遍净天也。或有众生，空处无量，所谓空处天是也。或有众生，识处无量，所谓识天也。或有众生，无有处无量，所谓不用处天是也。或有众生，有想无想处无量，所谓有想无想天是也。无想众生及诸所生之类，为九神止处。于是，比丘平等解脱，乃至尽于苦际。九论、九义、九演，我所说者，正谓此耳。

Ten discourses, ten meanings, and ten explanations. For what reason do I explain this meaning? Namely, the ten recollections: recollecting the Buddha, recollecting the Dharma, recollecting the Sangha, recollecting the precepts, recollecting generosity, recollecting the heavens, recollecting tranquility, recollecting mindfulness of breathing, recollecting the body, recollecting death. These are called the ten recollections. If a bhikshu is equally liberated from these, up to ending suffering.

「十论、十义、十演，由何等说乎？所谓十念：念佛、念法、念比丘僧、念戒、念施、念天、念休息、念安般、念身、念死，是谓十念。若比丘平等解脱，乃至尽于苦际。十论、十义、十演。如是，比丘！从一至十。

Bhikshus should know that if non-Buddhist ascetics hear this teaching, they will not even be able to look at the speaker's face, let alone respond. If a bhikshu understands this meaning, he is the foremost person in the present life. If a bhikshu or bhikshuni contemplates this meaning for ten years, they will definitely attain one of two fruits: arahantship or non-return. Bhikshu, let alone ten years. If they contemplate this meaning for one year, they will definitely attain one of two fruits, without any doubt. Bhikshu, let alone one year. If the four groups of followers contemplate this meaning for ten months, or even one month, they will definitely attain one of two fruits, without any doubt. Let alone one month. If the four groups of followers contemplate this meaning for seven days, they will definitely attain one of two fruits, without any doubt.

「比丘当知，若外道异学闻此语者，犹不能熟视颜色，况欲报之！其有比丘解此义者，于现法中最尊第一之人。若复比丘、比丘尼思惟此义，乃至十岁，必成二果、若阿罗汉、若阿那含。比丘！且舍十岁。若一年之中思惟此义者，必成二果，终无中退。比丘！且舍一年，其四部之众十月，若至一月，思惟此义者，必成二果，亦不中退。且舍一月。若四部之众七日之中思惟此义，必成二果，终不有疑。」

At that time, Ānanda was behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha. Ānanda said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! This Dharma is extremely profound. Wherever this Dharma is, it should be known that the Tathāgata is present. Yes, World-Honored One! What is the name of this Dharma, and how should it be upheld?"

尔时，阿难在世尊后，执扇扇佛。尔时，阿难白佛言：「世尊！此法极为甚深。若所在方面有此法者，当知便遇如来。唯然，世尊！此法名何等，当云何奉行？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "This sūtra is called the Meaning of the Ten Dharmas. You should remember and uphold it."

佛告阿难：「此经名为十法之义，当念奉行。」

At that time, Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，阿难及诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
