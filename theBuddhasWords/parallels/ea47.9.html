EĀ 47.9

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

47.9 （九）

Parallel to: MN 8 (~) / MĀ 91 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at the Karanda Bamboo Grove in the city of Rajagriha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikshus.

一时，佛在罗阅城迦兰陀竹园所，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, Venerable Mahākappina was in a quiet place, giving rise to this thought: "How can I know the views of the past, present, and future?" Then, at the proper time, Mahākappina put on his robe, took his bowl, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. At that time, Mahākappina addressed the World-Honored One, saying: "Now, these views are connected with the past, present, and future. How can I extinguish these views and prevent others from arising?"

时，尊者大均头在静寂之处，兴此念想：「诸前后中央之见，云何得知？」尔时，大均头到时，著衣持钵，到世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。尔时，均头白世尊言：「今此诸见，前后相应。云何得灭此见，又使余者不生？」

The World-Honored One said: "In this way, Mahākappina! The origin and cessation of these views are all impermanent, suffering, and empty. Mahākappina should know and establish this intention. The Dharma of views has sixty-two kinds. One must abide in the ten wholesome states to remove these views. What are the ten? Mahākappina! If others like to kill, we should not kill; if others like to steal, we should not steal; if others violate the precepts, we should practice the precepts; if others lie, we should not lie; if others engage in divisive speech, causing discord between others, or in frivolous speech, harsh speech, jealousy, anger, or wrong views, we should practice right views.

世尊告曰：「于是，均头！此见所出与所灭之处，皆是无常、苦、空。均头知之，当建此意。夫见之法六十二种，要当住十善之地，除去此见。云何为十？于是，均头！他好杀生，我等应当不杀；他好盗，我不盗；他犯梵行，我行梵行；他妄语，我不行妄语；他行两舌鬪乱彼此、绮语、恶口、嫉妬、恚、邪见，我行正见。

Mahākappina should know that it is like going from a bad path to encountering the right path, or going from wrong views to attaining right views. Turning away from wrong and going to the right is like a person who is drowning themselves but wants to save others - this is impossible. If one has not attained nirvana themselves but wants to cause others to attain nirvana, this is not possible. It is like a person who is not drowning themselves being able to save others - this is possible. It is the same now. If one attains nirvana themselves and can cause others to attain nirvana, this is possible. Therefore, Mahākappina should remember to be free from killing, not killing, and attaining nirvana; to be free from stealing, not stealing, and attaining nirvana; to be free from sexual misconduct, not engaging in sexual misconduct, and attaining nirvana; to be free from lying, not lying, and attaining nirvana; to be free from frivolous speech, not engaging in frivolous speech, and attaining nirvana; to be free from harsh speech, not engaging in harsh speech, and attaining nirvana; to be free from divisive speech, not engaging in divisive speech, and attaining nirvana; to be free from jealousy, not being jealous, and attaining nirvana; to be free from anger, not being angry, and attaining nirvana; to be free from wrong views, attaining right views, and attaining nirvana.

「均头当知，如从恶道得值正道，如从邪见得至正见，回邪就正，犹如有人自己没溺，复欲渡人者，终无此理。己未灭度，欲使他人灭度者，此事不然。如有人自不没溺，便能渡人，可有此理。今亦如是，自般涅槃，复使他人取灭度者，可有此理。是故，均头当念，离杀，不杀灭度；离盗，不盗灭度；离淫，不淫灭度；离妄语，不妄语灭度；离绮语，不绮语灭度；离麁言，不麁言灭度；离鬪乱彼此，不鬪乱彼此灭度；离嫉妬，不嫉妬灭度；离恚，不恚灭度；离邪见，得正见灭度。

Mahākappina should know that if ordinary people give rise to the thought: 'Is there a self? Is there no self? Is there both self and no self? Is the world permanent? Is the world impermanent? Is the world finite? Is the world infinite? Is the life the body? Is the life different from the body? Is the Tathāgata dead? Is the Tathāgata not dead? Is there death? Is there no death? Who created this world?' and give rise to various wrong views: 'Is this world created by Brahmā? Is this world established by the Lord of the Earth? Moreover, Brahmā created this world and beings, and the Lord of the Earth created this world and beings; beings were originally non-existent, now exist, and having existed, will cease.' Ordinary people who are ignorant and have no vision give rise to these thoughts.

「均头当知，若凡夫之人便生此念：『为有我耶？为无我耶？有我无我耶？世有常耶？世无常耶？世有边耶？世无边耶？命是身耶？为命异身异耶？如来死耶？如来不死耶？为有死耶？为无死耶？为谁造此世？』生诸邪见：『为是梵天造此世？为是地主施设此世？又梵天此众生，地主造此世间；众生本无今有，已有便灭。』凡夫之人无闻、无见，便生此念。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

There is naturally Brahmā, this is the speech of the Brahmins
this view is not genuine, as they see it. The Lord
is born from a lotus, Brahmā emerges from it, the Lord
of the Earth gives birth to Brahmā, self-birth is not appropriate
The Lord of the Earth is of the Kṣatriya caste, the
parents of the Brahmins, how can the son of a Kṣatriya
be born from a Brahmin again? Seeking their place of birth
it is said by the gods, this is a praiseworthy statement
but it is still difficult to be attached to. Brahmā gives
birth to people, the Lord of the Earth creates the world
or it is said that others create it, whose words are
true? Confused by anger and desire, the three things gather together
the mind is not free, and they claim themselves to be
supreme in the world. The gods create the world, it is
not born from Brahmā, even if Brahmā creates it, is this
not false? Seeking the traces, there are many, the words of
truth are false, their practices are each different, this practice is
not truly real.

「自然有梵天，　　此是梵志语，此见不真正，　　如彼之所见。我主生莲华，　　梵天于中出，地主生梵天，　　自生不相应。地主刹利种，　　梵志之父母，云何刹利子，　　梵志还相生？寻其所生处，　　诸天之所说，此是叹誉言，　　还自著羇难。梵天生人民，　　地主造世间，或言余者造，　　此语谁者审？恚欲之所惑，　　三事共合集，心不得自在，　　自称我世胜。天神造世间，　　亦非梵天生，设复梵天造，　　此非虚妄耶？寻迹遂复多，　　审谛方言虚，其行各各异，　　此行不审实。

"Gautama should know that the views of sentient beings are not the same, and their thoughts are different. These views are all impermanent. Those who hold these views are impermanent and changeable. If others kill, we should avoid killing; if others steal, we should stay away from it and not practice it. Focus the mind and do not let it be confused. Contemplate and analyze. The arising of wrong views, up to the ten evil dharmas, should all be avoided and not practiced. If others are angry, we should learn patience. If others are jealous, we should let go of it. If others are arrogant, we should let go of it. If others praise themselves and slander others, we should not praise ourselves or slander others. If others have few desires, we should learn to have few desires. If others violate the precepts, we should cultivate the precepts. If others are lazy, we should be diligent. If others do not practice samādhi, we should practice samādhi. We should learn in this way. If others are ignorant, we should practice wisdom. Those who can observe and analyze the dharmas will extinguish wrong views, and the rest will not arise."

「均头当知，众生之类所见不同，其念各异。此诸见者皆是无常，其有怀抱此见，则是无常变易之法。若他人杀生，我等当离杀生；设他盗者，当远离之，不习其行，专其心意，不使错乱，思惟挍计，邪见所兴，乃至十恶之法皆当去离，不习其行。若他瞋恚，我等学于忍辱，他人怀嫉妬，我当舍离；他兴憍慢，我念舍离；若他自称、毁余人，我等不自称、不毁他人；他不少欲，我等当学少欲；他人犯戒，我修其戒；他人有懈怠，我当精进；他人不行三昧，我行三昧。当作是学。他人愚惑，我行智慧。其能观察分别其法者，邪见消灭，余者不生。」

At that time, when Mūrdhagata received the Tathāgata's instruction, he stayed in a quiet place, contemplating and analyzing the reason why the sons of noble families left home to study the Way, put on the three Dharma robes, and practiced the unsurpassed pure conduct: the end of birth and death, the establishment of the pure conduct, the completion of what should be done, and no longer accepting existence, knowing it as it is. At that time, Mūrdhagata then attained arahantship.

是时，均头受如来教已，在闲静之处，思惟挍计，所以族姓子，出家学道，著三法衣，修无上梵行：生死已尽，梵行已立，所造已办，更不复受有，如实知之。是时，均头便成阿罗汉。

At that time, Mūrdhagata, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时，均头闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
