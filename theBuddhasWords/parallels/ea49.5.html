EĀ 49.5

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

49.5 （五）

Parallel to: SN 12.1–2 (~) / SĀ 298 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Now I will explain the Dharma of causes and conditions. Consider it well and cultivate its practice."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「今当说因缘之法，善思念之，修习其行。」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One!" At that time, the bhikṣus received the Buddha's instruction.

诸比丘白佛言：「唯然，世尊！」尔时，诸比丘从佛受教。

The World-Honored One said, "What is meant by the Dharma of causes and conditions? Namely, ignorance conditions formations, formations condition consciousness, consciousness conditions name-and-form, name-and-form conditions the six sense bases, the six sense bases condition contact, contact conditions feeling, feeling conditions craving, craving conditions grasping, grasping conditions existence, existence conditions birth, birth conditions aging and death, aging and death condition sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus, this five-aggregates body is formed.

世尊告曰：「彼云何名为因缘之法？所谓无明缘行，行缘识，识缘名色，名色缘六入，六入缘更乐，更乐缘痛，痛缘爱，爱缘受，受缘有，有缘生，生缘死，死缘忧、悲、苦、恼、不可称计。如是成此五阴之身。

What is meant by ignorance? Namely, not knowing suffering, not knowing the origin of suffering, not knowing the cessation of suffering, and not knowing the path. This is called ignorance.

「彼云何名为无明？所谓不知苦，不知习，不知尽，不知道，此名为无明。

What is meant by formations? There are three kinds of formations. What are the three? Namely, bodily formations, verbal formations, and mental formations. These are called formations.

「彼云何名为行？所谓行者有三种。云何为三？所谓身行、口行、意行，是谓为行。

What is meant by consciousness? Namely, the six sense bases of consciousness. What are the six? Namely, eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind consciousness. This is called consciousness.

「彼云何名为识？所谓六识身是也。云何为六？所谓眼、耳、鼻、舌、身、意识，是谓为识。

What is meant by name and form? Name refers to feeling, perception, intention, contact, and thought. Form refers to the four great elements and the form derived from the four great elements. This is called form. Form is different from name, therefore it is called name and form.

「云何名为名？所谓名者，痛、想、念、更乐、思惟，是为名。彼云何为色？所谓四大身及四大身所造色，是谓名为色。色异、名异、故曰名色。

What are the six sense bases? The six internal sense bases. What are the six? Namely, the sense bases of eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. These are called the six sense bases.

「彼云何六入？内六入。云何为六？所谓眼、耳、鼻、舌、身、意入，是谓六入。

What is meant by contact? The six kinds of contact with the body. What are the six? Namely, contact with the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is called contact.

「彼云何名为更乐？所谓六更乐身。云何为六？所谓眼、耳、鼻、舌、身、意更乐，是谓名为更乐。

What is meant by feeling? The three kinds of feeling. What are the three? Namely, pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-pleasant-nor-painful feeling. This is called feeling.

「彼云何为痛？所谓三痛。云何为三？所谓乐痛、苦痛、不苦不乐痛，是谓名为痛。

What is meant by craving? The three kinds of craving. Namely, craving for sensual pleasures, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence. What is meant by grasping? The four kinds of grasping. What are the four? Namely, grasping at sensual pleasures, grasping at views, grasping at precepts and practices, and grasping at a self. These are the four kinds of grasping.

「彼云何名为爱？所谓三爱身是也。欲爱、有爱、无有爱。云何为受？所谓四受是。云何为四？所谓欲受、见受、戒受、我受，是谓四受。

What is meant by existence? The three kinds of existence. What are the three? Namely, existence in the realm of desire, existence in the realm of form, and existence in the formless realm. This is called existence.

「彼云何为有？所谓三有。云何为三？欲有、色有、无色有，是名为有。

What is meant by birth? Birth means to be born, to be fully ordained, to receive all kinds of existence, to obtain the five aggregates, to receive the six sense bases. This is called birth.

「彼云何为生？所谓生者，等具出家，受诸有，得五阴，受诸入，是谓为生。

What is meant by old age? It means that for each and every being, their teeth fall out, their hair turns white, their strength is exhausted, their faculties become mature, their lifespan declines day by day, and they lose their original awareness. This is called old age.

「彼云何为老？所谓彼彼众生，于此身分，齿落发白，气力劣竭，诸根纯熟，寿命日衰，无复本识，是谓为老。

What is meant by death? It means that for each and every being, their bodies gradually change, they become lifeless, impermanent, and changeable. The five close relatives are scattered, and they abandon the body of the five aggregates. The life faculty is cut off and destroyed. This is called death. Bhikṣus should know that this is called old age, sickness, and death. This is called the Dharma of dependent origination, extensively distinguishing its meaning. The Tathāgatas, Arhats, Perfectly Enlightened Ones should practice with great compassion and pity. I have now accomplished it. One should contemplate sitting under a tree in the open, or in a charnel ground, and contemplate sitting in meditation, without fear or difficulty. If one does not diligently practice now, it will be of no benefit to regret later.

「云何为死？所谓彼彼众生，展转受形，身体无煴，无常变易，五亲分张，舍五阴身，命根断坏，是谓为死。比丘当知，故名为老、病、死。此名为因缘之法，广分别其义。诸佛如来所应施行起大慈哀，吾今已办。当念在树下露坐，若在冢间，当念坐禅，勿怀恐难。今不精勤，后悔无益。」

At that time, Ānanda addressed the World-Honored One, saying: "The Tathāgata has spoken to the bhikṣus about the profound dependent origination. However, I observe that there is no profound meaning.

尔时，阿难白世尊言：「如来与诸比丘说甚深缘本，然我观察无甚深之义。」

The World-Honored One said: "Stop, stop! Ānanda, do not give rise to this thought. The reason is that the twelve links of dependent origination are extremely profound, not something that ordinary people can understand. In the past, when I had not yet realized this Dharma of dependent origination, I wandered in birth and death without a way out. Moreover, Ānanda, it is not only today that you say dependent origination is not profound. In the past, you have also said it is not profound. The reason is that in the past, there was a king of the Syama Asuras who secretly gave rise to this thought, wanting to seize the sun and moon, emerge from the great ocean, and transform his body to be extremely large, with the seawater reaching his waist.

世尊告曰：「止！止！阿难！勿兴此意。所以然者，十二因缘者极为甚深，非是常人所能明晓。我昔未觉此因缘法时，流浪生死，无有出期。又复，阿难！不但今日汝言因缘不甚深，昔日已来言不甚深也。所以然者，乃昔过去世时，有须焰阿须伦王窃生此念，欲捉日月，出大海水，化身极大，海水齐腰。

"At that time, the Asura king had a son named Kunala, who said to his father: 'I now want to bathe in the seawater.' The Asura king replied: 'Do not enjoy bathing in the seawater. The reason is that the seawater is extremely deep and vast, and it cannot bear bathing.' At that time, Kunala said: 'I now see the water reaching the king's waist. Why do you still say it is very deep?' At that time, the Asura king took his son and placed him in the great ocean. At that time, the Asura son's feet did not reach the bottom of the water, and he was extremely terrified. At that time, the Asura king told his son: 'I previously instructed you that the seawater is very deep, but you said it was not painful. Only I can bathe in the great ocean, not you.'"

「尔时，彼阿须伦王有儿名拘那罗，自白其父：『我今欲于海水沐浴。』须焰阿须伦报曰：『莫乐海水中浴。所以然者，海水极深且广，终不堪任] cf.【丽】海水中浴。』时，拘那罗白言：『我今观水齐大王腰，何以故复言甚深？』是时，阿须伦王即取儿著大海水中。尔时，阿须伦儿足不至水底，极怀恐怖。尔时，须焰告其子曰：『我先勅汝，海水甚深，汝言无苦。唯我能在大海水洗浴，非汝所能欲洗。』

"At that time, the Asura king Syama, was he not someone else? Do not view it like that. The reason is that Syama was none other than myself. At that time, the Asura son was none other than you. At that time, you said the seawater was not painful, although it was very deep. Now, you again say the profound Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination is not profound. Those sentient beings who do not understand the Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination wander in birth and death without a way out, all deluded, not recognizing the basis of their actions. From this world to the next world, from the next world to this world, they are forever in the midst of the five afflictions, and it is extremely difficult to seek liberation. When I first attained Buddhahood, I contemplated the twelve links of dependent origination, subdued the demon's officials and attendants, eliminated ignorance and attained the illumination of wisdom, forever removing all darkness and defilements. Moreover, Ānanda! When I turned the wheel of the twelve links of dependent origination three times, I attained the path of enlightenment. By this means, I know that the Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination is extremely profound, not something that ordinary people can proclaim. Thus, Ānanda! You should remember the profundity and uphold this Dharma of the twelve links of dependent origination. You should remember to study in this way."

「尔时须焰阿须伦者，岂异人乎？莫作是观。所以然者，须焰者即我身是也。尔时阿须伦儿，即汝身是也。尔时海水甚深，汝言无苦；今复言十二因缘甚深之法，汝复言无是甚深。其有众生不解十二缘法，流转生死，无有出期，皆悉迷惑，不识行本，于今世至后世，从后世至今世，永在五恼之中，求出甚难。我初成佛道，思惟十二因缘，降伏魔官属，以除无明而得慧明，诸暗永除，无尘垢，又我，阿难！三转十二说此缘本时，即成觉道。以此方便，知十二缘法极为甚深，非常人所能宣畅。如是，阿难！当念甚深，奉持此十二因缘之法，当念作是学。」

At that time, Ānanda heard what the Buddha said and joyfully upheld it.

尔时，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
