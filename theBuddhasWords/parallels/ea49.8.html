EĀ 49.8

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

49.8 （八）

Parallel to: MN 39 / MĀ 182

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in Anga Village with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus.

一时，佛在鸯艺村中，与大比丘众五百人俱。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "All the people call you śramaṇas. If they ask you, 'Are you śramaṇas?' you also say, 'We are śramaṇas.' I now tell you, the practice of śramaṇas and the practice of brāhmaṇas, you should remember to cultivate and practice, later you will surely attain results, as it is, not different. The reason is this, there are two kinds of śramaṇas: there are śramaṇas who practice, and there are śramaṇas who vow.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「诸人民皆称汝等为沙门。设复问：『汝等是沙门乎？』汝等亦言：『是沙门。』吾今告汝，沙门之行、婆罗门之行，汝等当念修习，后必成果，如实不异。所以然者，有二种沙门：有习行沙门，有誓愿沙门。

What is meant by practicing śramaṇas? Here, bhikṣus, in their walking, coming, going, looking, appearance, wearing robes, and carrying alms bowls, all are in accordance with the Dharma, not attached to greed, anger, or delusion, but upholding precepts and diligently practicing, not violating what is not the Dharma, equally studying all precepts. This is called practicing śramaṇas.

「彼云何名为习行沙门？于是，比丘行来、进止、视瞻、容貌、著衣、持钵，皆悉如法，不著贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴，但持戒精进，不犯非法，等学诸戒，是谓名为习行沙门。

What is meant by vowing śramaṇas? Here, some bhikṣus, in their deportment, precepts, entering and leaving, going and stopping, walking, appearance, looking, and movements, all are in accordance with the Dharma, exhausting the taints and attaining the outflow-free, attaining realization in this very life and wandering freely: the cycle of birth and death is exhausted, the practice of purity is established, what is to be done is accomplished, no more rebirth will be taken, knowing it as it is. This is called vowing śramaṇas. This is called, bhikṣus, the two kinds of śramaṇas.

「彼云何名誓愿沙门？于是，或有比丘威仪、戒律、出入、进止、行步、容貌、视瞻、举动，皆悉如法，尽有漏成无漏，于现法中身得证而自游化：生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受有，如实知之。是谓名誓愿沙门。是谓，比丘！二种沙门。」

At that time, Ānanda addressed the World-Honored One, saying: "What is meant by the practice of śramaṇas and the practice of brāhmaṇas?"

尔时，阿难白世尊言：「彼云何名为沙门法行，婆罗门法行？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Here, bhikṣus know moderation in food and drink, practice walking meditation day and night without missing the proper time, and practice the various aspects of the path.

佛告阿难：「于是，比丘饮食知足，昼夜经行，不失时节，行诸道品。

How do bhikṣus have tranquil faculties? Here, bhikṣus, when the eye sees forms, they do not give rise to attachment and thoughts of disturbance. The eye faculty becomes pure, eliminating evil thoughts and not thinking of unwholesome dharmas. When the ear hears sounds, the nose smells fragrances, the tongue tastes flavors, the body experiences tactile sensations, and the mind knows dharmas, they do not give rise to attachment and thoughts of disturbance. The mind faculty becomes pure. In this way, bhikṣus have faculties that become pure.

「云何比丘诸根寂静？于是，比丘若眼见色，不起想著，兴诸乱念，于中眼根而得清净，除诸恶念，不念不善之法。若耳闻声、鼻臭香、舌知味、身知细滑、意知法，不起想著，兴诸乱念，于意根而得清净。如是，比丘根得清净。

How do bhikṣus know moderation in food and drink? Here, bhikṣus eat in moderation, not seeking to be fat or white, but only to sustain this body. To remove old pain and prevent new pain, they can practice the holy life. It is like a man or woman with a wound on the body. They apply ointment to the wound in a timely manner, always wanting the wound to heal. Now, bhikṣus are also like this. The reason for applying ointment to a cart is to go a long distance. Bhikṣus eat in moderation, for the sake of sustaining life. In this way, bhikṣus know moderation in food and drink.

「云何比丘饮食知足？于是，比丘量腹而食，不求肥白，但欲使此身趣存而已，除去故痛，新者不生，得修梵行。犹如男女身生疮痍，随时以膏涂疮，常欲使疮愈故。今此比丘亦复如是，量腹而食，所以以膏膏车者，欲致远故，比丘量腹而食者，欲趣存命故也。如是，比丘饮食知足。

How do bhikṣus always know the time of day? Here, bhikṣus always know the time of day in the early and late watches, contemplating the thirty-seven factors of the path. During the day, they walk back and forth, removing evil thoughts and the notion of afflictions. Again, in the early and late watches, they walk back and forth, removing the notion of evil afflictions and unwholesome dharmas. Again, in the middle of the night, they lie on their right side, with one foot on top of the other, only thinking of the light. Again, in the late night, they walk back and forth, removing unwholesome thoughts. In this way, bhikṣus know the time of day. In this way, Ānanda, this is the essential practice of a śramaṇa.

「云何比丘恒知景寤？于是，比丘初夜、后夜恒知景寤，思惟三十七道之法。若昼日经行，除去恶念诸结之想，复于初夜、后夜经行，除去恶结不善之想，复于中夜右脇著地，以脚相累，唯向明之想，复于后夜，出入经行，除去不善之念。如是，比丘知时景寤。如是，阿难！此是沙门要行。

What is called the essential practice of a brāhmaṇa? Here, a bhikṣu truly knows suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering. Later, he attains liberation from the mind's defilements of desire, existence, and ignorance. Having attained liberation, he attains the knowledge of liberation. Birth and death are exhausted, the holy life is established, what was to be done is done, and he will not again take up a womb. This is called the essential practice of a brāhmaṇa. Ānanda should know that this is the meaning of essential practice.

「彼云何名婆罗门要行？于是，比丘苦谛如实知之，苦习、苦尽、苦出要如实而知之，后以解此欲漏心、有漏心、无明漏心而得解脱，已得解脱，便得解脱智，生死已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不复受胎，如实知之。此名为婆罗门要行之法。阿难当知，此名为要行之义也。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时，世尊便说此偈：

The śramaṇa is called one who has extinguished the mind, who has forever
eliminated all evils. The brāhmaṇa is called pure, who has removed all deluded thoughts.

「沙门名息心，　　诸恶永已尽，梵志名清净，　　除去诸乱想。

"Therefore, Ānanda! The practice of the śramaṇa and the practice of the brāhmaṇa should be remembered and cultivated. Those sentient beings who practice these dharmas are then called śramaṇas. Why are they called śramaṇas? Because all afflictions are permanently extinguished, they are called śramaṇas. Why are they called brāhmaṇas? Because they have completely removed the dharmas of ignorance and delusion, they are called brāhmaṇas; they are also called kṣatriyas. Why are they called kṣatriyas? Because they have cut off desire, anger, and ignorance, they are called kṣatriyas; they are also called bathed. Why are they called bathed? Because they have washed away the twenty-one afflictions, they are called bathed. They are also called awakened. Why are they called awakened? Because they have awakened to the dharmas of ignorance and wisdom, they are called awakened. They are also called the other shore. Why are they called the other shore? Because they have gone from this shore to the other shore, they are called the other shore. Ānanda! Those who can practice these dharmas are then called śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas. This is the meaning, remember to uphold and practice it."

「是故，阿难！沙门法行、婆罗门法行，当念修行。其有众生行此诸法，然后乃称为沙门。复以何故名为沙门？诸结永息故名为沙门。复以何故名为婆罗门？尽除愚惑之法故名为梵志；亦名为刹利。复以何故名刹利？以其断淫、怒、痴故名为刹利；亦名为沐浴。以何故名为沐浴？以其洗二十一结故名为沐浴。亦名为觉。以何故名为觉？以其觉了愚法、慧法故名为觉。亦名为彼岸。以何等故名为彼岸？以其从此岸至彼岸故名为彼岸。阿难！能行此法者，然后乃名为沙门、婆罗门。此是其义，当念奉行。」

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and upheld it.

尔时，阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
