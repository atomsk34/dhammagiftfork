EĀ 50.4

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

50.4 （四）

Parallel to: MN 83 / MĀ 67 / EĀ 1.1 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Blessed One was staying in the great city of Rājagṛha in the eastern garden of Ambapālī, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus.

一时，婆伽婆在摩竭国蜜［口＊提］罗城东大天园中止，与大比丘僧千二百五十人俱。

At that time, after the meal, the World-Honored One arose and was walking in the garden with Ānanda. The Buddha then smiled. Ānanda thought, "The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One does not smile without reason. Why did he smile now? There must be a reason. I shall ask him." Ānanda adjusted his robes, touched his right knee to the ground, joined his palms, and asked the Buddha, "The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One does not smile without reason. Why did you smile now? There must be a reason. I wish to hear the reason for your smile."

尔时，世尊食后起，与阿难共于树园中经行。佛便笑，阿难心念：「如来、无所著、等正觉不妄笑，今何以笑？必当有意，我当问之。」阿难整衣服，右膝著地，叉手问佛：「如来、无所著、等正觉不妄笑，今何以笑？必当有意，愿闻笑意。」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "I will tell you. In the past, at the beginning of the Bhadrakalpa, there was a wheel-turning sage king who ruled over the four continents. His name was Mahādeva. He lived a long life without illness, was handsome and courageous, governed with the right Dharma, did not oppress the people, and had the seven natural treasures. What were the seven? First, the wheel treasure, second, the elephant treasure, third, the horse treasure, fourth, the jewel treasure, fifth, the woman treasure, sixth, the treasure of the treasury, and seventh, the treasure of the military commander.

佛语阿难：「我当为汝说。过去贤劫初，于此中间有转轮圣王主四天下，名曰大天，长寿无病，端正勇猛，治以正法，不枉人民，有自然七宝。何等为七？一者轮宝，二者象宝，三者马宝，四者珠宝，五者女宝，六者主藏宝，七者典兵宝。」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "When this great heavenly king was a prince, he was a prince for 84,000 years, and when he ascended the throne of a sage king, he reigned for 84,000 years."

佛语阿难：「此大天王为童子时八万四千岁，作太子时八万四千岁，登圣王位八万四千岁。」

Ānanda asked the Buddha, "What is the wheel treasure?"

阿难问佛：「云何为轮宝？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "On the fifteenth day of the month, when the moon is full, the king bathes and purifies himself, ascends to the eastern tower with his concubines, and looks east. There is a golden wheel with a thousand spokes. The wheel is seven tala trees high. A tala tree is a solitary tree used as a standard. It is seven tala trees high, made entirely of purple gold. When the king sees the wheel, he thinks, 'This is a good wheel. I wish to take it up.' As soon as he thinks this, the wheel comes to the king's left hand, and he lifts it into his right hand. The king says to the wheel, 'Those who are not subdued, subdue them for me; those who are not my subjects, take them for me; according to the Dharma, not contrary to the Dharma.' Having spoken, the wheel returns to stand in the sky, with the rim facing east and the hub facing north. The king orders his attendants to prepare the four kinds of troops. Having prepared the troops, he immediately leads the troops to follow the wheel standing in the sky. It leads them east to the eastern border. In the evening, the king and his troops stay under the wheel.

佛语阿难：「月十五日，月盛满时，王沐浴清净，与婇女上东楼上，东向视，有千辐金轮。轮高七仞为一多罗；多罗者，独挺树，以树为限，高七多罗，纯以紫磨金为轮。王见轮已，心念：『此轮好轮，愿得提之耶？』念已，轮即就王左手，便举移右手中。王语此轮曰：『诸不伏者，为我伏之；非我地者，为我取之；如法，非不如法。』语竟，轮还住空，辋东向，毂北向。王勅左右具四种兵；具兵已，即将兵众，逐轮立空，随轮东引，巡行尽东界；暮则王与兵众，宿于轮下。

The kings of the eastern border all come to pay homage, offering golden bowls filled with silver grains and silver bowls filled with golden grains. 'Welcome, great king! This land, treasures, and people of the eastern border are all yours. I wish you would stay here and rule over us.' The great heavenly king replies to the minor kings, 'If you wish to follow my teachings, return to your own countries and teach the people with the ten wholesome ways, do not act unjustly.' Having given this instruction, the wheel then turns back in the ocean, riding on the clouds. A natural path opens up in the ocean, one yojana wide. The king and the four kinds of troops follow the wheel as before, patrolling the southern border. The kings of the southern border also come to pay homage, offering golden bowls filled with silver grains and silver bowls filled with golden grains, presenting them and saying, 'Welcome, heavenly king! This land, treasures, and people of the southern border are all yours. I wish you would stay here and rule over us.' The great heavenly king replies to the kings, 'If you wish to follow my command, return to your own countries and teach the people with the ten wholesome ways, do not act unjustly.' Having given this instruction, the wheel then turns west and patrols the western border. The kings of the western border offer tribute and request, just like the kings of the south. The wheel then turns north and patrols the northern border. The kings of the northern border also all come to pay homage, offer tribute and request, all just like the previous method. After four days of touring the four seas of Jambudvīpa, it returns to the city of Mithilā, where it stands in the sky in front of the palace gate, seven tala trees high, with the rim facing east. The king then enters the palace.

「东界诸小王皆来朝觐，所贡皆以金钵盛银粟，银钵盛金粟：『善来！大王！此东界土地、珍宝、人民尽是王有，愿当停驾住此，我等当禀承天教！』大天王答诸小王曰：『汝等欲承我教者，各还本国以十善教民，勿行枉横。』诫勅已讫，轮即于海上，回转乘云而行。海中自然开道，广一由延，王与四种兵随轮如前巡行南界。南界诸小王亦来朝觐，皆以金钵盛银粟，银钵盛金粟，贡上曰：『善来！天王，此南界土地、珍宝、人民尽是王有，愿停驾住此，我等当禀承天命！』大天答诸王曰：『汝等欲承我命者，各还本土，以十善教民，勿行枉横。』诫勅已讫，轮则西回按行西界。西界诸王贡献劝请，如南方比讫，轮复北回巡行北界。北界诸王亦皆朝觐，贡献劝请，尽如前法。周游四日，遍阎浮提四海，还本蜜［口＊提］罗城，于宫门前虚空中住，高七多罗，辋东向，王便入宫。」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king obtained the wheel treasure like this."

佛语阿难：「大天得轮宝如此。」

Ānanda further asked the Buddha, "How did the great heavenly king obtain the elephant treasure?"

阿难复问佛：「大天得象宝复云何？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king, on the fifteenth day after the full moon, bathed and purified himself, ascended to the eastern tower with his concubines, and looked eastward into the sky. He saw a white elephant king named Manhu flying through the air, with its seven limbs standing upright, six tusks in its mouth, a golden crown on its head, golden necklaces, and its body adorned with strings of pearls and golden bells hanging on its sides. The elephant had supernatural powers and could transform its form at will. The great heavenly king, seeing it, thought to himself, 'Can I obtain this elephant and make it do something?' As soon as he thought this, the elephant immediately stood in the air before the king. The king then taught it five things. The king further thought, 'I should test this elephant to see if it can.' The next day, at sunrise, the king rode this elephant. In a short while, he circled the four seas and returned to his original place, standing to the east of the palace gate, facing east. Ānanda! The elephant treasure obtained by the great heavenly king was like this."

佛语阿难：「大天以后十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，从诸婇女上东楼上，东向观见空中，有白象王名满呼，乘虚而来，七肢平跱，口有六牙，头上金冠，金为璎珞，以真珠交络其体，左右佩金铃。象有神力，变形自在。大天见之，心自念曰：『我得此象可耶？当使有所为。』念讫，象便立空中于王前，王即教以五事。王复念曰：『当试此象为能以不？』至明日日出，王乘此象，须臾之间，周遍四海，还到本处，于宫门东，东向立。阿难！大天所得象宝如此。」

Ānanda further asked the Buddha, "How was the horse treasure obtained by the great heavenly king?"

阿难复问佛：「大天所得马宝复云何？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king, on the fifteenth day after the full moon, bathed and purified himself, ascended to the western tower with his concubines, and looked westward. He saw a purple horse king named Varāha, flying through the air, walking without moving its body, with a golden crown on its head, jeweled necklaces, and strings of pearls draped on its sides, with golden bells hanging on the sides. The horse had supernatural powers and could transform its form at will. The great heavenly king, seeing it, thought to himself, 'I can obtain this mount.' As soon as he thought this, it came before the king. The king then rode it to test it. The next day, at sunrise, the king rode east. In a short while, he circled the four seas and returned to his own country, standing to the west of the palace gate, facing west. Ānanda! The horse treasure obtained by the great heavenly king was like this."

佛语阿难：「大天到后十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，从诸婇女上西楼上，西向视，见有绀马王，名婆罗含，乘空而来，行不动身，头上金冠，宝为璎珞，披珠交络，左右垂铃。马有神力，变形自在。大天见之，自念言：『得此乘之可也。』念讫，至王前，王便乘欲试之，至明日日出，王乘东行，须臾之顷，周遍四海，还至本国，住宫门西，西向而立。阿难！大天所获马宝如此。」

Ānanda asked the Buddha, "How was the jewel treasure obtained by the great heavenly king?"

阿难问佛：「大天所得珠宝复云何？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king, on the fifteenth day after the full moon, bathed and purified himself, ascended to the eastern tower with his concubines, and looked east. He saw a divine jewel, one foot and six inches long, with eight facets, the color of dark blue lapis lazuli, flying through the air, seven tāla trees high. The great heavenly king, seeing it, thought to himself, 'I can obtain this jewel to observe it.' As soon as he thought this, he obtained it. The king wanted to test it. At midnight, he led the four divisions of the army, hung the jewel on the top of a banner, left the city and roamed. The jewel illuminated an area of twelve yojanas. The soldiers saw each other as if it were daytime. Wherever the light of the jewel reached, the people were startled and thought it was dawn. The king then returned to the palace, and erected the banner inside the palace. Inside and outside were always as bright as daytime. Ānanda! The jewel treasure obtained by the great heavenly king was like this."

佛语阿难：「大天至后十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，将诸婇女上东楼上，东向视，见有神珠，珠长一尺六寸，有八楞，作绀琉璃色，乘空而来，高七多罗。大天见之，心念曰：『得此珠观之可也？』如念获之。王欲试之，至夜半，合四种兵，以珠悬于幢头，出城而游，珠照辟方十二由延，兵众相见，如昼无异，珠光所及，人民惊起，皆言：『天明。』王即还宫，以幢竖之宫内，内外常明与昼不异。阿难！大天所获珠宝如此。」

Ānanda asked the Buddha, "How was the jade-like female treasure obtained by the great heavenly king?"

阿难问佛：「大天所得玉女宝复云何？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king, on the fifteenth day after the full moon, bathed and purified himself, ascended to the eastern tower with his concubines, and looked east. He saw a female treasure of the kṣatriya class, named Manodharā, incomparably upright, beautiful, dignified, and pure. She was neither tall nor short, neither coarse nor fine, neither white nor black. In winter she was warm and in summer she was cool. The pores of her body emitted the fragrance of sandalwood, and her mouth emitted the fragrance of blue lotus flowers. She was also free from the many evil postures of women. Her nature was gentle and harmonious, and she anticipated the king's wishes. She came flying through the air and went directly to the king. Ānanda! The female treasure obtained by the great heavenly king was like this."

佛语阿难：「大天至十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，从诸婇女上东楼上，东向视，见有刹帝利女宝，名曼那呵利，端正无比，姝妙严净，不长、不短、不麁、不细、不白、不黑，冬则温煖，夏则清凉，身毛孔中出栴檀香，口出忧钵莲花香，亦无女人众恶姿态，情性调和，先意承旨，乘虚而来，迳至王所。阿难！大天所获玉女宝如是。」

Ānanda asked the Buddha, "How was the treasure of the chief minister obtained by the great heavenly king?"

阿难问佛：「大天所得主藏宝者复云何？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king, on the fifteenth day after the full moon, bathed and purified himself, took his concubines to the northern tower, and looked north. He saw a chief minister of the treasury, named Āraṭṭa, who was upright and beautiful, neither tall nor short, neither fat nor thin, with a golden complexion, hair of a dark blue color, and eyes with clear whites and blacks. He could also see the treasures buried in the ground. Those with owners were protected, and those without owners were taken to be used by the king. He was intelligent and wise, good at planning. He came flying through the air and went directly to the king, saying to the king, 'From now on, the king can enjoy himself happily and not worry anymore. I will provide the king with treasures and not let him be in want.' The king then tested the chief minister of the treasury and took him on a boat alone to the sea. The king said to the chief minister of the treasury, 'I want to obtain gold, silver, and treasures.' The chief minister of the treasury said to the king, 'When we return to the shore, I will provide the treasures.' The king said, 'I want the treasures in the water, not those on the shore.' The chief minister of the treasury then rose from his seat, adjusted his clothes, knelt on his right knee, joined his palms, and paid homage to the water. Treasures then naturally emerged from the water, as large as chariot hubs, filling the boat in an instant. The king said, 'Stop! No more gold should be put on board, the boat is about to sink.' Ānanda! The chief minister of the treasury obtained by the great heavenly king was like this."

佛语阿难：「大天至十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，将诸婇女上北楼上，北向观，见主藏臣，名阿罗咃吱，端正姝妙，不长、不短，不肥、不瘦，身黄金色，发绀青色，眼白黑分明，又能视见地伏藏七宝，有主者为护之，无主者取共王用，聪明智慧，善有方谋，乘虚而来，迳诣王前，而谓王曰：『自今已往，王快可自乐，勿复忧愁，我当给王宝，不令有乏。』王便试藏臣，与之乘船独共入海。王谓藏臣者言：『吾欲得金银财宝。』藏臣者白王：『还至岸边，当给财宝。』王曰：『吾欲得水中宝，不用岸上者。』主藏臣者便从坐起，整衣服，跪右膝，叉手礼水，水中即自然出金顶，大如车毂，须臾满船。王曰：『可止！勿复上金，船将欲没。』阿难！大天所获典宝臣如此。」

Ānanda again asked the Buddha, "How was the general in charge of the army obtained by the great heavenly king?"

阿难复问佛：「大天所得典兵将军者复云何？」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king, on the fifteenth day after the full moon, bathed and purified himself, took his concubines to the southern tower, and looked south. He saw a great general in the south named Vibhīṣaṇa, who was upright and beautiful, with hair the color of pearls, a body the color of green, neither tall nor short, neither fat nor thin, with eyes that could see through and know the thoughts of others. He was skilled in military strategies and knew when to advance and retreat. He came flying through the air and went directly to the king, saying to the king, 'May the king enjoy himself happily and not worry about the world. I will handle the conquests in the four directions.' The king wanted to test him. In the middle of the night, he thought, 'I want to assemble the four kinds of troops.' As soon as he thought it, the four troops all gathered. The king further thought, 'I want to lead them east.' The army immediately led them east. The king was in the center, with the general in front, surrounded by the four troops. The king thought of going and went, and thought of returning and returned. Ānanda! The general in charge of the army obtained by the great heavenly king was like this." The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The seven treasures obtained by the great heavenly king were like this."

佛语阿难：「大天至十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，将诸婇女上南楼上，南向视，见南方有大将军比毘那，端正姝好，发如真珠色，身犹绿色，不长、不短，不肥、不瘦，眼能彻视，知他人心念，军策变谋，进退知时，乘虚而来，迳诣王所，谓王曰：『愿王自恣快乐，莫忧天下，征伐四方，臣自办之！』王欲试之，半夜思惟：『欲合四种兵。』念讫，四兵尽集。王复念：『欲使东引。』军即东引，王在中央，将军在前，四兵围遶。王念欲往即往，王念欲还即还。阿难！大天所获典兵将军宝如此。」佛语阿难：「大天所获七宝如此。」

The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king ruled the world for a long time. He then said to his barber named Kapila, 'If there is a white hair, pluck it out and show it to me.' Kapila looked at the hair for a long time and saw a white hair. He then said to the king, 'As previously instructed, I now see a white hair.' The king said, 'Pull it out and show it to me.' Kapila then used golden tweezers to pluck out the white hair and placed it in the king's hand. The king took the white hair and spoke this verse:

佛语阿难：「大天王治天下遂久，便语梳头侍者，名劫北：『若有白发者，便拔示我。』劫北视发遂久，见有一白发，便白王曰：『前所勅者，今已白见。』王曰：『拔来示我。』劫北即以金镊拔取白发，置王手中。王捉白发，便说斯偈：

This blemish on my head has arisen. It is time
for me to summon the body and enter the path.

「『我身首上，　　生此毁庄，　　身使来召，入道时到。』

The king thought, 'I have already indulged in the five desires of humans. Now I shall renounce the householder life, shave my beard and hair, and wear the Dharma robes.' He summoned Prince Changsheng and said, 'Young man! White hair has already appeared on my head. I am weary of the five desires of the world. Now I wish to seek the desires of the heavens. I now wish to shave my beard and hair, wear the Dharma robes, and renounce the householder life to practice the path. You should take charge of the affairs of the state and establish Chang as crown prince. Treat Kapila well and have him watch for white hair. When white hair appears, hand over the kingdom to the crown prince, just as I renounce the householder life, shave my beard and hair, and wear the Dharma robes.' The king told the crown prince, 'I now earnestly entrust this sacred position to you. You should ensure that the sacred position is passed down from generation to generation, and do not allow the lineage to be cut off. If the lineage is cut off, you will become a person of the borderlands. If you cut off good conduct, you will be reborn in a place without the Dharma.' After the great heavenly king had given these instructions, he handed over the kingdom to Prince Changsheng and provided Kapila with fields and property." The Buddha said to Ānanda, "The great heavenly king shaved his beard and hair, wore the Dharma robes, and entered the path in this city, in this garden, on this ground. For eighty-four thousand years, he practiced the four immeasurables: loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity. At the end of his life, he was reborn in the Heaven of Brahmā. Seven days after the great heavenly king renounced the householder life, the precious queen passed away.

「王心念曰：『我已极人五欲，今当出家，下须发，被法服。』召太子长生告曰：『童子！吾头已有白发出，世间五欲吾已厌之，今欲求天所欲，我今欲剃须发，著法服，出家为道。汝当领国政，立长为太子；好养劫北，使伺白发；白发出者，以国付太子，如我出家，下须发，被法服。』王告太子：『我今以此圣位，慇懃累汝，当使圣位世世相绍，莫使种断；种断者，便为边地人也。若断善行者，便生无法处。』大天王诫勅已，便以国付太子长生，给劫北田业。」佛语阿难：「大天王于此城，于此园，于此地，下须发，著法服入道，于此处八万四千岁，行四梵行：慈、悲、喜、护，于是寿终得生梵天。大天出家七日后，女宝命终。

Prince Changsheng ascended the throne. On the fifteenth day, when the moon was full, he took his concubines to the eastern tower and looked east. He saw a jade maiden as before, coming through the air. Changsheng returned to wearing the seven treasures. King Changsheng had already taken charge of the government and ruled the four continents. Changsheng again told Kapila, 'From now on, when you comb my hair, if you see white hair, come and tell me.' After ascending the throne of the sage king, he reigned for eighty-four thousand years. White hair grew again, and Kapila told the king, 'The white hair has grown.' The king said, 'Pull it out and place it in my palm.' Kapila then used golden tweezers to pull it out and place it in the king's hand. The king held the white hair and spoke a verse:

「长生登位已，至十五日，月盛满时，将诸婇女上东楼上，东向视，见有玉女端正如前，乘虚而来。长生还服七宝。王长生已领国政，统四天下。长生复语劫北：『从今而去，为我梳头时，见白发，辄来白我。』登圣王位，遂经八万四千岁，白发复生，劫北白王：『素发已生。』王曰：『拔来著吾掌中。』劫北即以金镊拔置王手中。王执白发，即说偈曰：

This blemish on my head was born. It is time
for me to summon the body and enter the path.

「『我身首上，　　生此毁庄，　　身使来召，入道时到。』

The king thought to himself, 'I have already indulged in the five desires of humans. Now I shall renounce the householder's life, shave my beard and hair, and wear the Dharma robes.' He then summoned Prince Guanying and said, 'Young man! My head has turned white. I am weary of the five desires of the world and seek the desires of the heavens. I now wish to renounce the householder's life, shave my beard and hair, and wear the Dharma robes. You should take charge of the government and establish Chang as crown prince. Treat Kapila well and have him watch for the white hair. When the white hair appears, hand over the country to the crown prince, just as I renounce the householder's life, shave my beard and hair, and wear the Dharma robes.' The king told the crown prince, 'I earnestly entrust this position of sage king to you. You should ensure that the position of sage king is passed down from generation to generation, and do not allow the lineage to be cut off. If the lineage is cut off, you will become a person of the borderlands. If you cut off good conduct, you will be reborn in a place without the Dharma.' After King Changsheng had given these instructions, he handed over the country to Prince Guanying and provided Kapila with fields and property.

「王心念曰：『我已极人五欲，今当出家，下须发，被法服。』即召太子冠髻告曰：『童子！吾已头白，世间五欲吾已厌之，当求天欲，我今欲出家为道，剃除须发，被著法服。汝当领国政，立长为太子；好养劫北，使伺白发] cf.【丽】；白发出者，以国付太子，如我出家，下须发，被法服。』王告太子：『我今以此圣王位，慇懃累汝，当使圣王位，世世相绍，莫使种断；种断者，便为边地人也。若断善行者，便生无法处。』长生王诫勅已，即以国付太子冠髻，给劫北田业。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "King Changsheng also shaved his beard and hair, put on the Dharma robes, and entered the path in this city, this garden, and this place. For eighty-four thousand years, he practiced the four immeasurables: loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity. Upon his death, he was reborn in the Heaven of Brahmā."

佛语阿难：「长生王亦于此城、此园、此地，下须发，著法服入道，于此处八万四千岁，行四梵行：慈、悲、喜、护也，于是寿终得生梵天。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Seven days after King Changsheng renounced the householder's life, the seven treasures naturally disappeared. King Guanying was sorrowful and unhappy. The ministers, seeing the king's displeasure, asked him, 'Why is the Heavenly King unhappy?' The king replied to the ministers, 'Because the seven treasures have disappeared!' The ministers said to the king, 'Do not be worried about it.' The king said, 'Why should I not be worried?' The ministers said, 'The father king is practicing the precepts nearby in the garden. You should go and consult with him. He will surely teach the king the method of obtaining the treasures.' The king then ordered, 'Prepare the carriage!' The ministers immediately prepared the carriage and reported to the king. The king, together with his ministers, rode in a carriage made of the seven treasures, with five objects as banners: a jeweled crown, a feathered parasol, a sword, a fan, and jeweled shoes. The ministers on the left and right followed as the king proceeded to the garden. Upon arrival, he alighted from the carriage, removed the five objects, and entered the garden on foot. He approached his father king, bowed at his feet, stepped back to one side, joined his palms, and said, 'The seven treasures that the king possessed have now all disappeared.' The father king, sitting in meditation, heard the report and looked up, replying, 'Young man, the Dharma of the sage king is not to rely on what the father possesses. You should practice the Dharma and inherit it.'

佛语阿难：「王长生出家之后七日之中，七宝自然化去。冠髻王忧愁不乐。诸臣见王不悦，便问王曰：『天王何以不悦？』王答诸臣曰：『以七宝化去故也！』诸臣白王：『王勿以] cf.【丽】为忧。』王曰：『何得不忧也？』臣等启曰：『父王梵行，近在园中，可往咨承，必当诲王致宝之法。』王即勅：『严驾！』诸臣便严驾讫，便白王。王与群臣乘七宝车，以五物为帜：宝冠、羽盖、剑、扇、宝屐。左右臣从，进诣园所。到则下车，废] cf.【丽】却五物，步入园门，前至父王，稽首礼足，却住一面，叉手白言：『王所有七宝，今皆化去。』父先定坐，闻所启白，仰头答曰：『童子，夫圣王法，不恃父之所有也。汝自行法承之。』

The king asked again, 'How does a wheel-turning sage king rule?' The father replied, 'By respecting the Dharma, valuing the Dharma, contemplating the Dharma, nurturing the Dharma, growing the Dharma, flourishing the Dharma, and upholding the great Dharma. Practicing these seven Dharmas is the way to rule as a sage king and attain the treasures.' The king asked again, 'What is meant by respecting the Dharma, up to upholding the great Dharma?' The father replied, 'One should learn to give to the poor, teach the people to be filial to their parents, make offerings at the four seasons and eight festivals, teach patience, eliminate the practice of desire, jealousy, and ignorance. These seven Dharmas are the way of a sage king.' The king then accepted the teachings, took leave, bowed, circumambulated seven times, and returned. Thereupon, the king immediately followed his father's instructions, practiced the seven Dharmas, and proclaimed them far and wide. The people all upheld the king's teachings. The king then opened the treasury, gave to the poor, and served the elderly. The people from all directions did not fail to follow. Then, on the fifteenth day, when the moon was full, the king bathed and purified himself, took his concubines, and went up to the eastern tower. Facing east, he saw a purple gold wheel with a thousand spokes, seven tāla trees high, and seven tāla trees above the ground, coming through the air and stopping in the sky.

「王复问曰：『转轮圣王以何法化？』父便答曰：『敬法、重法、念法、养法、长法、炽法、大法，行此七法者，便应圣王治也，可以致宝也。』王复问曰：『云何敬法，乃至大法也？』父答曰：『当学给赐贫穷，教民孝养二亲，四时八节以时祭祠，诲以忍辱，除婬、嫉、痴行，此七法者，乃应圣王法也。』王即受教，辞退却礼，绕七匝已，便引还归。于是，王辄承父命，奉行七法，宣令远近，祇崇王教，王便开藏，给赐贫穷，侍养孤老，四方之民，莫不奉承。于是，王以十五日，月盛满时，沐浴清净，将诸婇女上东楼上，东向视，见有千辐紫磨金轮，轮高七多罗，去地亦七多罗，乘虚而来，住于空中。

"The king thought to himself, 'May I obtain this wheel!' The wheel then descended to the king's left hand, and then moved to his right hand. The king spoke to the wheel, 'May those who are not subdued be subdued for me; may those who are not my territory be taken for me; may it be according to the Dharma, not contrary to the Dharma.' The king then handed the wheel back to the sky with his hand. At the eastern gate of the palace, the rim faced east, and the hub faced the king. The wheel stopped in the sky. After the wheel, there was a white elephant, then a dark horse, then a divine jewel, then a jade woman, then a treasure-keeper, then a general. These seven treasures appeared, just like a great heavenly king. After eight thousand four hundred years, the king gave the northern part of the country to the crown prince, and entrusted him with the affairs of the country. He renounced the householder's life and entered the path, just as the previous kings had done."

「王心念曰：『愿得此轮可乎？』轮即下至王左手，复移之于右手。王语此轮，『诸不伏者，为我伏之；非我地者，为我取之；如法，非不如法。』王便以手投轮还之虚空，于宫门东，辋东向，毂比向而住空中。轮后次有白象，次有绀马，次有神珠，次有玉女，次有主藏，次有将军，此七宝出，如大天王，比试亦如是。迳八万四千岁竟，王赐劫北，并勅太子及付国事，出家入道，皆如前王法。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "This Crown-Topknot King shaved his beard and hair and donned the Dharma robes in this city park. For eighty-four thousand years, he practiced the four immeasurables: loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity. When his life ended, he was reborn in the Heaven of Brahmā."

佛语阿难：「此冠髻王于此城园地，下须发，被法服，八万四千岁，修四梵行：慈、悲、喜、护，于是寿尽亦生梵天。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "The great prince's descendants succeeded each other, up to eighty-four thousand years, as wheel-turning sage kings. The position of the wheel-turning sage king was not cut off. The last sage king was named Jeng, who ruled with the right Dharma. He was intelligent, discerning, and did not forget. He had the thirty-two marks, and his complexion was like a red lotus flower. He liked to give generously, making offerings to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, serving the elderly and orphaned, and providing for the poor. At the four city gates and in the center of the city, he set up treasuries filled with gold, silver, various treasures, elephants, horses, carriages, clothing, bedding, medicine for the sick, incense, flowers, and food. He provided wives for the orphaned and gave various kinds of charity according to people's wishes. On the six days of fasting, the king ordered everyone inside and outside to observe the eight precepts. On these days, the gods of the Heaven of Pure Abode would all come down to receive the eight precepts. Śakra and the thirty-three gods all praised the people of the country, saying, 'How wonderful! What a great benefit! To encounter such a Dharma king! He gives various kinds of charity, satisfying people's wishes, and he also observes the fasts purely without omission.' Śakra told the gods, 'Do you wish to see King Jeng?' They all said, 'We wish to see him. Please have him come here.' Śakra then ordered the celestial maiden Qiongbi to go to the city of Milati and tell King Jeng, 'You have gained great benefits! The gods here all praise your lofty virtues. They ask after you with sincerity. These gods greatly desire to see you. Please temporarily come here.'"

佛告阿难：「大王子孙相绍，乃至八万四千岁转轮圣王位善种不断。最后圣王名荏，治以正法，为人聪明，审谛不忘，相有三十二，色犹红莲花，好喜布施，供养沙门、婆罗门，侍养孤老，赈给贫穷。于四城门及城中央，置设库藏，金银、杂宝、象、马、车乘、衣服、床褥、病瘦医药、香华、饮食，诸孤独者皆给妻妇，种种惠施随人所欲。王于六斋之日，具勅内外皆持八关；于斯之日，首陀会天当悉来下，受其八戒；帝释、三十三天皆叹其国人民：『快哉，善利！乃值斯法王耶！种种惠施，恣民所欲，又能清洁斋戒无阙。』天帝释告诸天子：『欲得见荏王不？』咸言：『欲见，可使来至此。』释提桓因即勅穷鼻尼天女：『汝诣蜜［口＊提］罗城，告荏王曰：「卿！大得善利也，此间诸天皆叹卿功德巍巍，为吾致问慇懃。此诸天子甚思相见，暂可屈意来至于此？」』

Qiongbi accepted the order and descended, as quickly as a person bending and stretching an arm. She suddenly appeared in front of the king's palace, standing in the air. The king's attendant, a concubine, was sitting in the palace, thinking of the world, wishing for everyone to attain peace and be free from suffering. Qiongbi snapped her fingers in the air to wake her. The king raised his head and saw the bright light in the palace. He heard the voice saying, 'I am a servant of Śakra. I was sent to visit the king.' The king replied, 'I do not know what the Heavenly Emperor has to order?' The celestial maiden answered, 'The Heavenly Emperor is sincere in his greetings. These gods praise your virtues and wish to see you in advance. Please temporarily come.' The king silently agreed. The celestial maiden then returned and reported to the Heavenly Emperor, 'I have delivered the message. He has agreed to come.' The Heavenly Emperor then ordered the attendant to prepare a chariot drawn by seven-treasure flying horses and descend to the city of Milati to welcome King Jeng. The attendant accepted the order and immediately drove the chariot, suddenly descending.

「穷鼻尼受教便下，如人屈伸臂顷，忽然在王殿前，虚空中立。王侍一婇女，于殿上坐，思念世间，欲使一切皆得安隐，无众苦患。穷鼻尼于空中，弹指觉之，王举头见殿上光明，闻其声曰：『我是释提桓因侍者，故遣我诣王』王答曰：『不审天帝何所约勅？』天女答曰：『天帝致意慇懃，此诸天子赞卿功德，预思相见，可暂屈意？』王默然许之。天女便还白天帝曰：『已达宣命，许当驰诣。』天帝即勅侍御严驾七宝飞行马车，下到蜜［口＊提］罗城，迎于荏王。侍御受教，即驾马车，忽然便下。

"The king and his ministers gathered in the capital. The chariot stopped in front of the king in the air. The charioteer said, 'The Heavenly Emperor has now sent a chariot to welcome you. The gods are all waiting. You may board the chariot and do not look back.' When the ministers, young and old, heard that the king was leaving, they were all displeased. They all stood up, joined their palms, and said, 'After the king leaves, whom shall we follow?' The king replied, 'Do not worry, my ministers. After I leave, distribute kindness, observe the fasts, nourish the people, and govern the country as if I were here. I will return soon.' After the king gave his instructions, the chariot descended to the ground, and the king boarded it. The charioteer asked the king, 'Which path should we take?' The king said, 'What do you mean by this?' The charioteer replied, 'There are two paths for traveling: one is the path of evil, and the other is the path of good. Those who practice evil follow the path of evil to a place of suffering; those who cultivate goodness travel the path of good to a place of happiness.' The king said, 'Today, I wish to follow both the path of good and the path of evil.' Hearing this, the charioteer was enlightened for a long time and said, 'Very good, great king!' The charioteer then led the chariot in the middle of the two paths, showing both good and evil, and went straight to the Heaven of the Thirty-three Gods. The Heavenly Emperor and the gods saw the king coming from afar. Śakra said, 'Welcome, great king!' and ordered him to sit together."

「王与群臣会于都坐，车当王前，于空中止。御者告曰：『天帝今遣车相迎，诸天子俨然相待，便可上车，勿复顾恋。』诸臣大小，闻王当去，佥然不悦，皆起而立，叉手白言：『王去之后，臣等何所承命？』王答曰：『卿等勿忧，吾去之后，施惠、斋戒、养民、治国，如吾在时，我比还不久。』王诫勅讫，车即下地，王便上车。侍御问王：『当从何道？』王曰：『此言何谓？』侍御答曰：『夫行有两道，一者恶道，二者善道。行恶者，从恶道到苦处；修善者，游善道至乐处。』王曰：『今日行道，善恶皆欲从之。』御者闻之，久乃寤曰：『甚善，大王！』御者便引在两道之中，善恶悉覩，迳诣三十三天。天帝及诸天子遥见王来，释提桓因曰：『善来，大王！』命令共坐。」

"The Buddha told Ānanda: "The king then sat with the Heavenly Emperor. The king and Śakra were identical in appearance, attire, and voice. The gods in their minds thought, 'Which one is Śakra? Which one is the king?' They also thought, 'Human nature should blink, but they both do not blink.' They were all astonished and could not distinguish them. The Heavenly Emperor saw that the gods had doubts in their minds. He further thought, 'I should detain the king to stay, and then they will be awakened.' Śakra said to the gods, 'Do you want me to detain the king to stay here?' The gods said, 'Indeed we want him to stay.' The Heavenly Emperor said to Jeng, 'Great King! Would you like to stay here? I will provide you with the five desires.' Because of this, the gods then recognized.

佛语阿难：「王便就天帝坐，王与帝释貌相、被服、音声一揆。诸天子心中念言：『何者帝释？何者为王？』又复念曰：『人法当眴，而俱不眴？』各怀愕然，无以别之。天帝见诸天有疑心，复念言：『我当留王使住，然后乃寤耳。』帝释谓诸天子：『卿等欲使我留王住此不？』诸天子曰：『实欲使住。』天帝谓荏：『大王！可住此不，我当供给五欲。』因是诸天乃识。

The human king said to the Heavenly Emperor, 'Just now, you have already provided me with the gifts. I wish the gods have boundless longevity.' The host and guest politely declined each other like this three times. Śakra said to the king, 'Why don't you stay?' The king replied, 'I am going to renounce the householder life to cultivate the path. Now in heaven, there is no condition to study the path.' Śakra said, 'Why do you want to cultivate the path?' The king said, 'My father, the king, left an order that if white hair grows, I should renounce the householder life.' When Śakra heard the order to enter the path, he remained silent and did not respond. The king stayed in heaven for a short while, indulging in the five desires. Compared to the world, it was already twelve years. The king was about to part and spoke the Dharma of truth to the gods. Śakra ordered the charioteer, 'You should send King Jeng back to his country.' The charioteer received the order, immediately prepared the chariot, and when it was ready, said to the king, 'The king may board the chariot.' Then the king took leave of Śakra and the gods, boarded the chariot, followed the original path to return, and arrived at the palace of Mithila. The charioteer then returned to heaven. A few days after the king descended, he again ordered Kapi to inform him if he saw white hair. Within a few days, white hair grew on his head. Kapi plucked the white hair with golden tweezers and placed it in the king's hand. Seeing it, the king then spoke a verse:

「人王白天帝曰：『正尔，便为给赐已，愿诸天子寿命无极。』宾主请让。如是至三，帝释谓王：『何以不住？』王答曰：『我当出家修道，今在天上无缘学道。』天帝曰：『胡为作道？』王曰：『被父王遗令，若白发生，法当出家。』释闻遗令入道，默然不对。王于天上，须臾之间，五欲自恣，方之世间已十二年。王将欲别，与诸天子说审谛法。释勅侍御：『汝送荏王还于本国。』侍御受教，即严驾，驾讫，白王曰：『王可上车。』于是，王便与帝释及诸天子诰别，即上车，循本道而归，到蜜［口＊提］罗宫，侍御即还天上。王下数日，复勅劫北：『若见白发，便白我。』数日之中，头上白发，劫比以金镊拔白发，置王手中。王见已，便说偈曰：

「『我身头上，　　生此毁庄，　　身使来召，入道时到。』

"The king thought, 'I have already indulged in the five desires of humans. Now I should renounce the householder life, shave my beard and hair, and wear the Dharma robes.' The king then summoned Prince Sudhana and said, 'White hair has grown on my head. I am already weary of the five desires of the world. I should seek the desires of the heavens. I will now shave my beard and hair, wear the Dharma robes, renounce the householder life, and enter the path. Prince! I now entrust the affairs of the country to you. Take good care of Kapi. If white hair grows, hand over the country to the prince, renounce the householder life, and enter the path. Prince! I now entrust this position of a sage-king to you. Do not let the lineage be cut off. If the lineage is cut off, you will become a person of the borderlands.'"

「王心念：『我已极人五欲，今当出家，剃须发，被法服。』王即召太子善尽告曰：『吾白发已出，世间五欲吾已厌之，当求天欲，当下须发，被法服，出家入道。童子！今以国事付汝，好养劫北。若白发出者，以国付太子，出家入道，童子！今以此圣王位累汝，莫使种断，种断者，便为边地人也。』」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "King Rāma immediately entrusted the affairs of the country to Prince Sudhana, and gave Kapi fields and property. He then shaved his beard and hair, wore the Dharma robes, renounced the householder life, and practiced the path in the garden of the city. After practicing the path for seven days, the wheel, jewel, and the elephant, horse, jade woman, elder, and general all became impermanent. The king practiced the four brahmavihāras of loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity for eighty-four thousand years in the garden, and after his life ended, he was reborn in the Brahma Heaven. After that, King Sudhana did not succeed to his father's rule. The true Dharma was replaced and abolished. Therefore, the seven treasures no longer came to respond. The good practices were not continued. The five degenerations gradually arrived. The people had short lives, little color, little strength, many illnesses, and little wisdom. With the five degenerations having arrived, they became even more impoverished. The impoverished stole from each other and reported to the king, 'This person took what was not given.' The king then ordered the execution of the country's people. Hearing that taking what was not given would be punished by the king, everyone considered it evil. Each raised a sharp knife. The knife was first created from this. Thus, killing arose from this. Then two evils arose. Next, there was sexual misconduct with another's wife. The husband argued with her, saying, 'I did not do it.' This became four evils. Slander and fighting became five evils. Fighting then became mutual insults, which became six evils. Words that were not sincere became seven evils. Jealousy of others' harmony became eight evils. Bearing resentment and changing one's expression became nine evils. Harboring doubts and confusion became ten evils. With the ten evils complete, the five degenerations increased even more."

佛语阿难：「荏王即付太子国政，给劫北田业已，于此城园地，下须发，被法服，出家修道。修道之后于七日中，轮、珠化去，象、马、玉女、长者、将军，皆悉无常。王于园中八万四千岁，行四梵行：慈、悲、喜、护，命终生梵天。其后善尽王不承父业，正法替废，由是七宝不复来应，善行不继，五减遂至，人民短命、薄色、少力、多病、无智，五减以至，转复贫困，困穷窃盗相𫄙，诣王启曰：『此人不与取。』王即勅外行刑国人。闻不与取，王辄杀之，皆咸其恶，各兴利刀；刀自此始造，由是杀生从此起，便有两恶出。次复婬犯他妻；夫主共诤，自言我不，便成四恶；两舌遘鬪，是为五恶；鬪则相骂，是为六恶；言不至诚，是为七恶；嫉他和合，是为八恶；含忿色变，是为九恶；心怀疑乱，是为十恶。十恶已具，五减转增。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do you want to know who the great king of that time was at the beginning of the Bhadrakalpa? It was me. Ānanda! Do you want to know who the king named Rāma was, who ruled for eighty-four thousand years without injustice? It was you. Do you want to know who the last king named Sudhana was, who was violent, unrighteous, and cut off the lineage of the sage-kings? It was Devadatta. Ānanda! In the past, you succeeded to the good lineage of the great heavenly wheel-turning sage-king, causing it to be established without interruption. It was your merit. It was in accordance with the Dharma, not contrary to the Dharma. Ānanda! I am now the unsurpassed Dharma king. I now entrust the unsurpassed good Dharma to you with sincerity. You are the son of the Śākya clan. Do not become a person of the borderlands. Do not engage in conduct that cuts off the lineage.

佛语阿难：「欲知尔时大天王在贤劫初兴者不？则我是也。阿难！欲知尔时八万四千年王名荏，治政无枉者，则汝是也。欲知尔时最后名善尽王，暴逆不道，断圣王种者，调达是也。阿难！汝于往时，承继大天转轮圣王之善嗣，使其绍立不绝者，汝之功也。如法，非不如法。阿难！我今是无上法王，今我遗无上善法，慇懃嘱累汝。汝是释种子，莫作边地人，莫为断种行。」

Ānanda asked the Buddha, "Why should I engage in conduct that cuts off the lineage?"

阿难问佛：「何以故当作断种行？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Although the great heavenly wheel-turning sage-king practiced good Dharma, he did not attain the exhaustion of taints and transcend the world. He did not attain liberation, did not attain the cessation of desires, did not break the twenty koṭis of bonds, did not eliminate the sixty-two views, did not purify the three defilements, did not attain supernatural powers, did not attain the true path of liberation, and did not attain nirvāṇa. The good Dharma practiced by the great heavenly wheel-turning sage-king was no more than being reborn in the Brahma Heaven.

佛语阿难：「大天王虽行善法，未得漏尽出世间，未得度，未得断欲，未得破二十亿结，六十二见未除，三垢未净，未得神通，未得解脱真道，不得涅槃，大天所行善法，不过生梵天。

"Ānanda! I have clearly understood the ultimate Dharma without taints. My Dharma has reached the true limit. I am supreme among gods and humans. My Dharma is without taints, without desires, and leads to the cessation of existence. It is the path of liberation, the true śramaṇa, and leads to nirvāṇa. Ānanda! I now entrust this unsurpassed path and Dharma to you with sincerity. Do not increase or decrease my Dharma, do not become a person from the borderlands! If there are those who currently practice the śrāvaka path, Ānanda! If there are those who cut off this Dharma, they will become people from the borderlands. If they can uphold this Dharma, they will become the Buddha's eldest son and will accomplish the retinue. Ānanda! You should accomplish the retinue, do not engage in conduct that destroys the lineage. Ānanda! All the Dharma I have taught before and after, I entrust to you. You should learn this."

「阿难！我明法究竟无为，我法得到真际，天、人之上，我法无漏、无欲、减没度、通解脱、真沙门、至涅槃。阿难！我今以是无上道法，慇懃嘱累汝，莫增减我法，莫作边地人也！若有现行声闻，阿难！设有断此法者，便为边地人也。若能兴此法者，便为佛长子，即为眷属成就。阿难！汝当成就眷属，莫作灭族行。阿难！我前后所说法，尽嘱累汝。汝当学是。」

The Buddha having spoken thus, Ānanda rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说是已，阿难欢喜奉行。
