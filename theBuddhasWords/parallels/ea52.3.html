EĀ 52.3

Ekottarāgama 增壹阿含经

52.3 （三）

Parallel to: SN 15.6 / SĀ 948 / SĀ² 2.341

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a bhikṣu went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and after a while, withdrew his seat and said to the Buddha, "Is the eon long or short, and is it limited?"

尔时，有一比丘往至世尊所，头面礼足，在一面坐。须臾退坐，前白佛言：「劫为长短、为有限乎？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "The eon is extremely long. I will now give you an analogy. Listen attentively. I will now explain." At that time, the bhikṣu received the Buddha's instruction.

佛告比丘：「劫极长远，我今与汝引譬，专意听之，吾今当说。」尔时，比丘从佛受教。

The World-Honored One said, "Bhikṣu, you should know that it is like an iron city one yojana long and wide, filled with mustard seeds without any empty spaces. Suppose someone comes every hundred years to take one mustard seed. The iron city and mustard seeds will still be depleted, and then it will take an incalculable eon. The reason is that birth and death are long and far-reaching, without any limit. Sentient beings are bound by affection and attachment, transmigrating through birth and death, dying here and being born there, without end. I am disgusted with birth and death among them. Thus, bhikṣu! You should seek skillful means to escape this thought of attachment to love."

世尊告曰：「比丘当知，犹如铁城纵广一由旬，芥子满其中，无空缺处，设有人来百岁取一芥子，其铁城芥子犹有减尽，然后乃至为一劫，不可称计。所以然者，生死长远无有边际，众生恩爱缚著，流转生死，死此生彼，无有穷已，我于其中厌患生死。如是，比丘！当求巧便，免此爱著之想。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
