MĀ 100

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 9 因品

100. （一〇〇）苦阴经

Parallel to: MN 14 / EĀ 41.1 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of the Śākyas, staying in the garden of the Nyagrodha tree in Kapilavastu.

一时，佛游释羁瘦，在加维罗卫尼拘类园。

At that time, Śrī Mahānāman was strolling in the middle of the day and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said, "World-Honored One! I understand the World-Honored One's teachings in this way, which allows me to eliminate the three defilements in my mind: the defilement of a defiled mind, the defilement of an angry mind, and the defilement of a deluded mind. World-Honored One! I understand this teaching in this way, but defiled, angry, and deluded dharmas still arise in my mind. World-Honored One! I think to myself, 'What dharmas have not been eliminated, causing defiled, angry, and deluded dharmas to still arise in my mind?'"

尔时，释摩诃男中后仿佯往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！我如是知世尊法，令我心中得灭三秽：染心秽、恚心秽、痴心秽。世尊！我如是知此法，然我心中复生染法、恚法、痴法。世尊！我作是念：『我有何法不灭？令我心中复生染法、恚法、痴法耶？』」

The World-Honored One said, "Mahanama! You have one dharma that has not been eliminated, namely, you dwell at home, without faith, not leaving home, without a family, and not studying the path. Mahanama! If you eliminate this one dharma, you will definitely not dwell at home, you will have faith, leave home, be without a family, and study the path. Because of one dharma that has not been eliminated, you dwell at home, without faith, not leaving home, without a family, and not studying the path."

世尊告曰：「摩诃男！汝有一法不灭，谓汝住在家，不至信、舍家、无家、学道。摩诃男！若汝灭此一法者，汝必不住在家，必至信、舍家、无家，学道。汝因一法不灭故，住在家，不至信、舍家、无家、学道。」

Then Śrī Mahānāman rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms towards the Buddha, and said to the World-Honored One, "I only wish that the World-Honored One would teach the Dharma for me, so that my mind may be purified, doubts removed, and the path attained."

于是，释摩诃男即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白世尊曰：「唯愿世尊为我说法，令我心净，除疑得道。」

The World-Honored One said, "Mahānāman! There are five kinds of sensual pleasures that are delightful, memorable, joyful, and associated with desire, causing people to be happy. What are the five? They are the eyes knowing forms, the ears knowing sounds, the nose knowing smells, the tongue knowing tastes, and the body knowing tactile objects. These cause the king and his retinue to attain peace, happiness, and joy. Mahānāman! This is the utmost taste of desire, nothing surpasses it, but the suffering is very great.

世尊告曰：「摩诃男！有五欲功德可爱、可念、欢喜、欲相应而使人乐。云何为五？谓眼知色、耳知声、鼻知香、舌知味、身知触，由此令王及王眷属得安乐欢喜。摩诃男！极是欲味无复过是，所患甚多。

Mahānāman! What are the sufferings of desire? Mahānāman! A clansman, according to his skills, makes a living. He may farm, engage in commerce, study writing, understand mathematics, know arithmetic, be skilled in carving seals, compose literature, create calligraphy, understand scriptures, become a brave general, or serve the king. When cold, he is cold; when hot, he is hot; hungry, thirsty, tired, bitten by mosquitoes and flies, he engages in such work to seek wealth. Mahānāman! This clansman, with such methods, practices in such ways, and seeks in such ways, if he does not obtain wealth, he will give rise to suffering, sorrow, distress, and delusion, saying, 'In vain I worked hard, but my seeking has no result.' Mahānāman! This clansman, with such methods, practices in such ways, and seeks in such ways, if he obtains wealth, he will then cherish, guard, and hide it. Why? 'I do not want this wealth to be taken by the king, robbed by thieves, burned by fire, rot, or lost. If I spend it, there will be no profit, or if I engage in various activities, they will not be accomplished.' He guards and hides it like this. If it is taken by the king, robbed by thieves, burned by fire, rot, or lost, he will then give rise to suffering, sorrow, distress, and delusion, saying, 'What I have cherished and remembered for a long time is now lost.' Mahānāman! Such present-life suffering aggregates arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！云何欲患？摩诃男！族姓子者，随其技术，以自存活，或作田业、或行治生、或以学书、或明算术、或知工数、或巧刻印、或作文章、或造手笔、或晓经书、或作勇将、或奉事王。彼寒时则寒，热时则热，饥渴、疲劳、蚊虻所蜇，作如是业，求图钱财。摩诃男！此族姓子如是方便作如是行，作如是求，若不得钱财者，便生忧苦、愁戚、懊恼，心则生痴，作如是说：『唐作唐苦，所求无果。』摩诃男！彼族姓子如是方便作如是行，作如是求，若得钱财者，彼便爱惜守护密藏。所以者何？『我此财物莫令王夺、贼劫、火烧、腐坏、亡失，出财无利，或作诸业，而不成就。』彼作如是守护密藏，若使王夺、贼劫、火烧、腐坏、亡失，彼便生忧苦、愁戚、懊恼，心则生痴，作如是说：『若有长夜所可爱念者，彼则亡失。』摩诃男！如是现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, mothers quarrel with their children, children quarrel with their mothers, fathers, brothers, sisters, and relatives quarrel with each other. Having quarreled like this, mothers speak ill of their children, children speak ill of their mothers, fathers, brothers, sisters, and relatives speak ill of each other, let alone others. Mahānāman! This is called the present-life suffering aggregates, arising from desire, conditioned by desire, and having desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，母共子诤，子共母诤，父子、兄弟、姉妹、亲族展转共诤。彼既如是共鬪诤已，母说子恶，子说母恶，父子、兄弟、姉妹、亲族更相说恶，况复他人？摩诃男！是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, kings quarrel with other kings, brahmins quarrel with other brahmins, householders quarrel with other householders, commoners quarrel with other commoners, countries quarrel with other countries. Because of this mutual hatred, they harm each other with various weapons, either by punching, throwing stones, beating with sticks, or cutting with swords. When they fight, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. Mahānāman! This is called the present-life suffering aggregates, arising from desire, conditioned by desire, and having desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，王王共诤，梵志梵志共诤，居士居士共诤，民民共诤，国国共诤，彼因鬪诤共相憎故，以种种器仗，转相加害，或以拳扠石掷，或以杖打刀斫，彼当鬪时，或死、或怖，受极重苦。摩诃男！是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, they put on armor, wear robes, carry spears and bows and arrows, or hold swords and shields, and enter into battle formations. They fight with elephants, horses, chariots, or infantry, or with men and women. When they fight, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. Mahānāman! This is called the present-life suffering aggregates, arising from desire, conditioned by desire, and having desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，著铠被袍，持矟弓箭，或执刀楯入在军阵，或以象鬪，或马、或车，或以步军，或以男女鬪，彼当鬪时，或死、或怖，受极重苦。摩诃男！是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, they put on armor, wear robes, carry spears and bows and arrows, or hold swords and shields, and go to conquer other countries, attacking cities and breaking through walls. They fight each other, beating drums and blowing horns, shouting loudly, or hitting with hammers, spears, sharp wheels, or arrows, or throwing stones, or using large crossbows, or sprinkling molten copper beads. When they fight, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. Mahānāman! This is called the present-life suffering aggregates, arising from desire, conditioned by desire, and having desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，著铠被袍，持矟弓箭，或执刀楯往夺他国，攻城破坞，共相格战，打鼓吹角，高声唤呼，或以槌打，或以𫓴戟，或以利轮，或以箭射，或乱下石，或以大弩，或以融铜珠子洒之，彼当鬪时，或死、或怖，受极重苦。摩诃男！是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, they put on armor, wear robes, carry spears and bows and arrows, or hold swords and shields, and enter villages, towns, countries, and cities, digging through walls and opening up hidden treasures to rob and steal. They cut off royal roads, or go to other lanes, destroying villages, harming towns, annihilating countries, and breaking through cities. There, they may be caught by the king's men and tortured in various ways, having their hands and feet cut off, or their hands and feet cut off, their ears and noses cut off, or their ears and noses cut off, or being cut and torn apart, their beards and hair pulled out, or their beards and hair pulled out, or being wrapped in cloth and burned in a fire, or being wrapped in grass and burned in a fire, or being placed in the belly of an iron donkey, or being put in the mouth of an iron pig, or being placed in the mouth of an iron tiger and burned, or being put in an iron cauldron, or being put in an iron pot and boiled, or being cut into pieces, or being pierced with sharp stakes, or being hooked with iron hooks, or being laid on an iron bed and poured with boiling oil, or being seated on an iron mortar and pounded with an iron pestle, or being bitten by dragons and snakes, or being whipped with whips, or being beaten with sticks, or being struck with clubs, or being impaled alive on a high pole, or having their heads cut off. There, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. Mahānāman! This is called the present-life suffering aggregates, arising from desire, conditioned by desire, and having desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，著铠被袍，持矟弓箭，或执刀楯入村、入邑、入国、入城，穿墙发藏，劫夺财物，断截王路，或至他巷，坏村、害邑、灭国、破城。于中或为王人所捉，种种拷治，截手、截足或截手足，截耳、截鼻或截耳鼻，或脔脔割，拔须、拔发或拔须发，或著槛中衣裹火烧，或以沙壅草缠火𦶟，或内铁驴腹中，或著铁猪口中，或置铁虎口中烧，或安铜釜中，或著铁釜中煮，或段段截，或利叉刺，或铁钩钩，或卧铁床以沸油浇，或坐铁臼以铁杵捣，或龙蛇蜇，或以鞭鞭，或以杖挝，或以棒打，或生贯高标上，或枭其首。彼在其中，或死、或怖，受极重苦。摩诃男！是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, they engage in bad conduct of body, speech, and mind. Later, when they are sick and lying in bed, or sitting or lying on the ground, suffering afflicts their bodies, causing them to experience extremely painful and unpleasant feelings. If they have engaged in bad conduct of body, speech, and mind, then at the time of death, those things will come before them and cover them, just as when the sun is about to set, the shadow of a large mountain covers the ground. In the same way, if they have engaged in bad conduct of body, speech, and mind, then at the time of death, those things will come before them and cover them. They will think: 'My past bad conduct is coming before me and covering me. I did not do any meritorious deeds, but only bad deeds. If there were a place where people who do evil and are violent only do evil and not meritorious deeds, do not practice good, have no fear, no refuge, and no place to go, then I will definitely be born there.' From this, they will have regret. Those who regret will die unwholesomely and without merit.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼于后时疾病著床，或坐、卧地，以苦逼身，受极重苦，不可爱乐。彼若有身恶行，口、意恶行，彼临终时在前覆障，犹日将没大山岗侧，影障覆地。如是，彼若有身恶行，口、意恶行，在前覆障，彼作是念：『我本恶行在前覆我，我本不作福业，多作恶业，若使有人作恶凶暴唯为罪，不作福、不行善，无所畏、无所依、无所归，随生处者，我必生彼。』从是有悔，悔者不善死，无福命终。摩诃男！是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Furthermore, because beings arise from desire, are conditioned by desire, and have desire as their basis, they engage in bad conduct of body, speech, and mind. Due to their bad conduct of body, speech, and mind, when their bodies disintegrate and their lives end, they will definitely go to a bad place and be born in hell. Mahānāman! This is called the suffering aggregates of the next life, arising from desire, conditioned by desire, and having desire as their basis.

「摩诃男！复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，行身恶行，行口、意恶行。彼因身、口、意恶行故，因此、缘此，身坏命终，必至恶处，生地狱中。摩诃男！是谓后世苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Mahānāman! Therefore, know that desire is entirely without pleasure and has immeasurable suffering and harm. If the learned noble disciples do not see it as it truly is, they will be covered by desire and will not attain the pleasure of renunciation and the unsurpassed peace. Mahānāman! Thus, the learned noble disciples regress due to desire. Mahānāman! I know that desire is without pleasure and has immeasurable suffering and harm. Having known it as it truly is, Mahānāman! I am not covered by desire, nor am I bound by evil. I have attained the pleasure of renunciation and the unsurpassed peace. Mahānāman! Therefore, I do not regress due to desire.

「摩诃男！是故当知欲一向无乐，无量苦患。多闻圣弟子不见如真者，彼为欲所覆，不得舍乐及无上息。摩诃男！如是彼多闻圣弟子因欲退转。摩诃男！我知欲无乐，无量苦患。我知如真已，摩诃男！不为欲所覆，亦不为恶所缠，便得舍乐及无上息。摩诃男！是故我不因欲退转。

Mahānāman! Once, while wandering in Rājagṛha, I stayed at the Seven-Leaf Hut of the Rishis on Mount Vipula. Mahānāman! In the afternoon, I arose from meditation and went to the Broad Mountain, where I saw many Nirgranthas standing and not sitting, undergoing extreme suffering. I asked them, 'Nirgranthas! Why do you practice this standing and not sitting, undergoing such suffering?' They replied, 'Gautama! We have a teacher named Nirgrantha, who teaches us thus: "Nirgranthas! If you have unwholesome karma from past lives, by practicing this suffering, it will surely be exhausted. If you now guard the wholesome conduct of body, speech, and mind, because of this, you will not create any more unwholesome karma."'

「摩诃男！一时，我游王舍城，住鞞哆逻山仙人七叶屋。摩诃男！我于晡时，从宴坐起，往至广山，则于彼中见众多尼揵，行不坐行，常立不坐，受极重苦。我往问曰：『诸尼揵！汝等何故行此不坐行，常立不坐，受如是苦？』彼如是说：『瞿昙！我有尊师尼揵，名曰亲子，彼则教我，作如是说：「诸尼揵等！汝若宿命有不善业，因此苦行故，必当得尽；若今身妙行护，口、意妙行护，因缘此故，不复作恶不善之业。」』

Mahānāman! I further asked, 'Nirgranthas! Do you have faith in your teacher without doubt?' They again replied, 'Yes, Gautama! We have faith in our teacher without doubt.' Mahānāman! I further asked, 'Nirgrantha! If so, your teacher Nirgrantha previously committed heavy unwholesome karma. He was born as a Nirgrantha in his past life, and now he has renounced the householder life and become a Nirgrantha, practicing standing and not sitting, undergoing such suffering, just like you and your disciples.' They again said to me, 'Gautama! Happiness does not arise from happiness but from suffering. The happiness of King Bimbisāra is not as good as that of the śramaṇa Gautama.'

「摩诃男！我复问曰：『诸尼揵！汝等信尊师无有疑耶？』彼复答我：『如是。瞿昙！我等信尊师无有疑惑。』摩诃男！我复问曰：『尼揵！若尔者，汝等尊师尼揵本重作恶不善之业，彼本作尼揵死，今生人间出家作尼揵，行不坐行，常立不坐，受如是苦，如汝等辈及弟子也。』彼复语我曰：『瞿昙！乐不因乐要因苦得，如频鞞娑罗王乐，沙门瞿昙不如也。』

I further said, 'You are foolish and deluded, speaking without meaning. Why is that? You are unwholesome, ignorant, and do not know the right time. You say that the happiness of King Bimbisāra is not as good as that of the śramaṇa Gautama. Nirgrantha! You should have asked thus: Who is happier, King Bimbisāra or the śramaṇa Gautama? Nirgrantha! If I say that my happiness is superior and that of King Bimbisāra is not as good, then you can say that the happiness of King Bimbisāra is not as good as that of the śramaṇa Gautama.' Those Nirgranthas then said, 'Gautama! We now ask the śramaṇa Gautama: Who is happier, King Bimbisāra or the śramaṇa Gautama?' I further said, 'Nirgrantha! I now ask you, answer as you understand. Nirgranthas! What do you think? Can King Bimbisāra attain the joy and happiness of silent meditation for seven days and seven nights?' The Nirgranthas replied, 'No, Gautama!' 'For six, five, four, three, two, or one day and night?' The Nirgranthas replied, 'No, Gautama!' I further asked, 'Nirgrantha! Can I attain the joy and happiness of silent meditation for one day and night?' The Nirgranthas replied, 'Yes, Gautama!' 'For two, three, four, five, six, or seven days and seven nights?' The Nirgranthas replied, 'Yes, Gautama!' I further asked, 'Nirgranthas! What do you think? Who is happier, King Bimbisāra or I?' The Nirgranthas replied, 'Gautama! According to our understanding of what the śramaṇa Gautama has said, the śramaṇa Gautama's happiness is superior and that of King Bimbisāra is not as good.

「我复语曰：『汝等痴狂，所说无义。所以者何？汝等不善，无所晓了，而不知时，谓汝作是说，如频鞞娑罗王乐，沙门瞿昙不如也。尼揵！汝等本应如是问：谁乐胜？为频鞞娑罗王？为沙门瞿昙耶？尼揵！若我如是说我乐胜，频鞞娑罗王不如者。尼揵！汝等可得作是语，如频鞞娑罗王乐，沙门瞿昙不如也？』彼诸尼揵即如是说：『瞿昙！我等今问沙门瞿昙：谁乐胜？为频鞞娑罗王？为沙门瞿昙耶？』我复语曰：『尼揵！我今问汝，随所解答。诸尼揵等，于意云何？频鞞娑罗王可得如意静默无言，因是七日七夜得欢喜快乐耶？』尼揵答曰：『不也。瞿昙！』『六五四三二，一日一夜得欢喜快乐耶？』尼揵答曰：『不也。瞿昙！』复问曰：『尼揵！我可得如意静默无言，因是一日一夜得欢喜快乐耶？』尼揵答曰：『如是。瞿昙！』『二三四五六，七日七夜得欢喜快乐耶？』尼揵答曰：『如是。瞿昙！』我复问曰：『诸尼揵等！于意云何？谁乐胜？为频鞞娑罗王？为是我耶？』尼揵答曰：『瞿昙！如我等受解沙门瞿昙所说，瞿昙乐胜，频鞞娑罗王不如也。』

"Mahānāman! For this reason, I know that desire is without happiness and has immeasurable suffering. If the learned noble disciples do not see it as it truly is, they will be covered by desire, bound by evil and unwholesome dharmas, and will not attain the happiness of renunciation and the unsurpassed tranquility. Mahānāman! Thus, the learned noble disciples will regress due to desire. Mahānāman! I know that desire is without happiness and has immeasurable suffering. Having known it as it truly is, I am not covered by desire, nor bound by evil and unwholesome dharmas, and I have attained the happiness of renunciation and the unsurpassed tranquility. Mahānāman! Therefore, I do not regress due to desire."

「摩诃男！因此故知，欲无乐，有无量苦患。若多闻圣弟子不见如真者，彼为欲所覆，恶、不善所缠，不得舍乐及无上息。摩诃男！如是彼多闻圣弟子为欲退转。摩诃男！我知欲无乐，有无量苦患。我知如真已，不为欲所覆，亦不为恶不善法所缠，便得舍乐及无上息。摩诃男！是故我不为欲退转。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Mahānāman and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。释摩诃男及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
