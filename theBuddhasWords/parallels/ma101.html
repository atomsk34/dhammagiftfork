MĀ 101

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 9 因品

101. （一〇一）增上心经

Parallel to: MN 20 / MN 26 (~) / MN 36 (~) / MN 100 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If a bhikṣu wishes to attain supreme concentration, he should repeatedly contemplate the five characteristics. Having contemplated the five characteristics repeatedly, if unwholesome thoughts arise, they will be extinguished. After the evil thoughts are extinguished, the mind will always abide, resting internally, and attain concentration with one-pointedness.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念于五相。数念五相已，生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。

What are the five? A bhikṣu contemplates the characteristics of good dharmas. If unwholesome thoughts arise, he contemplates other characteristics of good dharmas to prevent the arising of unwholesome thoughts. By contemplating other characteristics of good dharmas, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. It is like a carpenter or carpenter's apprentice using a string to mark the wood, then using a sharp axe to cut and straighten it. In the same way, a bhikṣu contemplates other characteristics of good dharmas to prevent the arising of unwholesome thoughts. By contemplating other characteristics of good dharmas, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. If a bhikṣu wishes to attain supreme concentration, he should repeatedly contemplate this first characteristic. Having contemplated this characteristic, unwholesome thoughts that arise are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration.

「云何为五？比丘者，念相善相应，若生不善念者，彼因此相复更念异相善相应，令不生恶不善之念。彼因此相更念异相善相应，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内、止息、一意、得定。犹木工师、木工弟子，彼持墨绳，用拼于木，则以利斧，斫治令直。如是，比丘！因此相复更念异相善相应，令不生恶不善之念。彼因此相更念异相善相应，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念此第一相，念此相已，生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu contemplates the characteristics of good dharmas. If unwholesome thoughts arise, he contemplates the faults of those thoughts, thinking, 'These thoughts are unwholesome, these thoughts are evil, these thoughts are despised by the wise. If these thoughts are fully developed, one will not attain the supernormal powers, the path of awakening, or nirvāṇa. They give rise to unwholesome thoughts.' By contemplating the faults in this way, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. It is like a young person who is handsome and attractive, who bathes and washes, puts on bright and clean clothes, anoints the body with fragrance, and trims the beard and hair, making them extremely clean and pure. Then, a dead snake, dead dog, or dead person, half green, bloated, stinking, and oozing with filth, is tied around their neck. They become disgusted, unhappy, and displeased. In the same way, a bhikṣu contemplates the faults of those thoughts, thinking, 'These thoughts are unwholesome, these thoughts are evil, these thoughts are despised by the wise. If these thoughts are fully developed, one will not attain the supernormal powers, the path of awakening, or nirvāṇa. They give rise to unwholesome thoughts.' By contemplating the faults in this way, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. If a bhikṣu wishes to attain supreme concentration, he should repeatedly contemplate this second characteristic. Having contemplated this characteristic, unwholesome thoughts that arise are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration.

「复次，比丘！念相善相应，若生不善念者，彼观此念恶有灾患，此念不善，此念是恶，此念智者所恶，此念若满具者，则不得通、不得觉道、不得涅槃，令生恶不善念故。彼如是观恶，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。犹人年少，端政可爱，沐浴澡洗，著明净衣，以香涂身，修治须发，极令净洁，或以死蛇、死狗、死人食半青色，膖胀臭烂，不净流出，系著彼颈，彼便恶秽，不喜不乐。如是，比丘！彼观此念，恶有灾患，此念不善，此念是恶，此念智者所恶，此念若满具者，则不得通、不得觉道、不得涅槃，令生恶不善念故。彼如是观恶，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念此第二相，念此相，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。

"Furthermore, a bhikṣu contemplates the characteristics of good dharmas. If unwholesome thoughts arise, he contemplates the faults of those thoughts. If unwholesome thoughts arise again, that bhikṣu should not contemplate those thoughts, because they give rise to unwholesome thoughts. By not contemplating those thoughts, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. It is like a person with eyes who, when forms are in the light, does not use them to see. They may close their eyes or move their body away. What do you think? Can that person still perceive the forms in the light?"

「复次，比丘！念相善相应时，生不善念，观念恶患时，复生不善念者，彼比丘不应念此念，令生恶不善念故。彼不念此念，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。犹有目人，色在光明，而不用见，彼或闭目，或身避去。于汝等意云何？色在光明，彼人可得受色相耶？」

"No, they cannot."

答曰：「不也。」

Thus, a bhikṣu should not contemplate those thoughts, because they give rise to unwholesome thoughts. By not contemplating those thoughts, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. If a bhikṣu wishes to attain superior concentration, he should repeatedly contemplate this third characteristic. After contemplating this characteristic, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen are extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration.

「如是，比丘不应念此念，令生恶不善念故，彼不念此念，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念此第三相，念此相已，生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。

Furthermore, bhikṣus, when contemplating the characteristics of good, unwholesome thoughts arise. When contemplating the faults of unwholesome thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also arise. When not contemplating thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also arise. For those bhikṣus, in order to gradually reduce those thoughts and prevent unwholesome thoughts from arising, they should use contemplation to gradually reduce their thoughts. Unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will then be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. It is like a person walking along a path, quickly advancing. They think, 'Why am I walking so fast? I should walk slowly now.' They then walk slowly. They think again, 'Why am I walking so slowly? I should stop now.' They then stop. They think again, 'Why am I standing still? I should sit down now.' They then sit down. They think again, 'Why am I sitting? I should lie down now.' They then lie down. In this way, that person gradually stops their coarse physical activities. Know that bhikṣus are also like this. For those bhikṣus, in order to gradually reduce those thoughts and prevent unwholesome thoughts from arising, they should use contemplation to gradually reduce their thoughts. Unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will then be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. If bhikṣus wish to attain superior concentration, they should repeatedly contemplate this fourth characteristic. After contemplating this characteristic, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration.

「复次，比丘！念相善相应时生不善念，观念恶患时亦生不善念，不念念时复生不善念者，彼比丘为此念，当以思行渐减其念，令不生恶不善之念。彼为此念，当以思行渐减念，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。犹人行道，进路急速，彼作是念：『我何为速？我今宁可徐徐行耶。』彼即徐行。复作是念：『我何为徐行？宁可住耶。』彼即便住。复作是念：『我何为住？宁可坐耶。』彼即便坐。复作是念：『我何为坐？宁可卧耶。』彼即便卧。如是，彼人渐渐息身麤行。当知比丘亦复如是，彼为此念，当以思行渐减其念，令不生恶不善之念。彼为此念，当以思行渐减念，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念此第四相，念此相，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。

Furthermore, bhikṣus, when contemplating the characteristics of good, unwholesome thoughts arise. When contemplating the faults of unwholesome thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also arise. When not contemplating thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also arise. When using contemplation to gradually reduce thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also arise. For those bhikṣus, they should contemplate in this way. Bhikṣus, because of these thoughts, unwholesome thoughts arise. Those bhikṣus should then clench their teeth and press their tongues against the palate, using their minds to cultivate their minds, to hold and subdue them, preventing unwholesome thoughts from arising. They use their minds to cultivate their minds, to hold and subdue them. Unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will then be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. It is like two strong men holding down a weak person. In this way, bhikṣus, clench your teeth and press your tongues against the palate, using your minds to cultivate your minds, to hold and subdue them, preventing unwholesome thoughts from arising. They use their minds to cultivate their minds, to hold and subdue them. Unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will then be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. If bhikṣus wish to attain superior concentration, they should repeatedly contemplate this fifth characteristic. After contemplating this characteristic, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration.

「复次，比丘！念相善相应时生不善念，观念恶患时亦生不善念，不念念时亦生不善念，当以思行渐减念时复生不善念者，彼比丘应如是观。比丘者，因此念故，生不善念，彼比丘便齿齿相著，舌逼上腭，以心修心，受持降伏，令不生恶不善之念。彼以心修心，受持降伏，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。犹二力士捉一羸人，受持降伏。如是，比丘！齿齿相著，舌逼上腭，以心修心，受持降伏，令不生恶不善之念。彼以心修心，受持降伏，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念此第五相，念此相，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。

"If bhikṣus wish to attain superior concentration, they should repeatedly contemplate these five characteristics. After contemplating these five characteristics, unwholesome thoughts that have already arisen will be extinguished. After the unwholesome thoughts are extinguished, the mind abides internally, resting, with one-pointedness, attaining concentration. If bhikṣus contemplate the characteristics of good, unwholesome thoughts do not arise. When contemplating the faults of unwholesome thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also do not arise. When not contemplating thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also do not arise. When using contemplation to gradually reduce thoughts, unwholesome thoughts also do not arise. When using the mind to cultivate the mind, to hold and subdue them, unwholesome thoughts also do not arise. They then attain mastery. When they wish to contemplate, they contemplate. When they do not wish to contemplate, they do not contemplate. If bhikṣus wish to contemplate when they wish to contemplate, and not to contemplate when they do not wish to contemplate, this is called bhikṣus' mastery over all contemplations, mastery over all traces of contemplation."

「若比丘欲得增上心者，当以数数念此五相。数念五相，已生不善念，即便得灭，恶念灭已，心便常住，在内止息，一意得定。若比丘念相善相应时不生恶念，观念恶患时亦不生恶念，不念念时亦不生恶念，若以思行渐减念时亦不生恶念，以心修心、受持降伏时亦不生恶念者，便得自在，欲念则念，不念则不念。若比丘欲念则念，不欲念则不念者，是谓比丘随意诸念，自在诸念迹。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
