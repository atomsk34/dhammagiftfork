MĀ 102

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 9 因品

102. （一〇二）念经

Parallel to: MN 19

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "When I had not yet attained supreme perfect enlightenment, I thought like this: 'I should separate all thoughts into two parts, making one part of the thoughts of desire, anger, and harm, and another part of the thoughts of non-desire, non-anger, and non-harm.' Later, I indeed separated all thoughts into two parts, making one part of the thoughts of desire, anger, and harm, and another part of the thoughts of non-desire, non-anger, and non-harm. Practicing in this way, I dwelt in seclusion, alone, with an undistracted mind, diligently cultivating and practicing. When a thought of desire arose, I immediately recognized the arising of the thought of desire, which would harm myself, harm others, harm both, destroy wisdom, cause much trouble, and not attain nirvāṇa. Recognizing that it would harm myself, harm others, harm both, destroy wisdom, cause much trouble, and not attain nirvāṇa, I quickly extinguished it. Again, thoughts of anger and harm arose. I immediately recognized the arising of the thoughts of anger and harm, which would harm myself, harm others, harm both, destroy wisdom, cause much trouble, and not attain nirvāṇa. Recognizing that it would harm myself, harm others, harm both, destroy wisdom, cause much trouble, and not attain nirvāṇa, I quickly extinguished it.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我本未觉无上正尽觉时，作如是念：『我宁可别诸念作二分，欲念、恚念、害念作一分，无欲念、无恚念、无害念复作一分。』我于后时，便别诸念作二分，欲念、恚念、害念作一分，无欲念、无恚念、无害念复作一分。我如是行，在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤。生欲念，我即觉生欲念，自害、害他、二俱害，灭慧、多烦劳、不得涅槃。觉自害、害他、二俱害，灭慧、多烦劳、不得涅槃，便速灭。复生恚念、害念，我即觉生恚念、害念，自害、害他、二俱害，灭慧、多烦劳、不得涅槃。觉自害、害他、二俱害，灭慧、多烦劳、不得涅槃，便速灭。

When I give rise to thoughts of desire, I do not accept, cut off, eliminate, and expel them. When thoughts of anger and harm arise, I do not accept, cut off, eliminate, and expel them. Why is that? Because I see that this will definitely give rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. It is like in the spring and summer months, when farmers plow the fields and graze cattle. The grazing land is not vast. When the cattle enter other people's fields, the cattle herders immediately go to stop them. Why is that? Because the cattle herders know that this will definitely lead to scolding, beating, binding, and faults. Therefore, the cattle herders go to stop them. I am also like this. When thoughts of desire arise, I do not accept, cut off, eliminate, and expel them. When thoughts of anger and harm arise, I do not accept, cut off, eliminate, and expel them. Why is that? Because I see that this will definitely give rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas.

「我生欲念，不受、断、除、吐，生恚念、害念，不受、断、除、吐。所以者何？我见因此故，必生无量恶不善之法。犹如春后月，以种田故，放牧地，则不广，牧牛儿放牛野泽，牛入他田，牧牛儿即执杖往遮。所以者何？牧牛儿知因此故，必当有骂、有打、有缚、有过失也。是故牧牛儿执杖往遮。我亦如是，生欲念，不受、断、除、吐，生恚念、害念，不受、断、除、吐。所以者何？我见因此故，必生无量恶不善之法。

"Bhikṣus, one follows one's thoughts and intentions, and the mind then delights in them. If a bhikṣu has many thoughts of desire, he abandons thoughts of non-desire. Because of having many thoughts of desire, the mind then delights in them. If a bhikṣu has many thoughts of anger and harm, he abandons thoughts of non-anger and non-harm. Because of having many thoughts of anger and harm, the mind then delights in them. Thus, bhikṣus who do not abandon thoughts of desire, anger, and harm, cannot escape birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, and cannot escape all suffering. I practice in this way, dwelling in seclusion, alone, with an undistracted mind, diligently cultivating and practicing. When thoughts of non-desire arise, I immediately recognize the arising of thoughts of non-desire, which do not harm myself, harm others, or harm both, cultivate wisdom, cause no trouble, and attain nirvāṇa. Recognizing that they do not harm myself, harm others, or harm both, cultivate wisdom, cause no trouble, and attain nirvāṇa, I quickly cultivate and spread them. Again, thoughts of non-anger and non-harm arise. I immediately recognize the arising of thoughts of non-anger and non-harm, which do not harm myself, harm others, or harm both, cultivate wisdom, cause no trouble, and attain nirvāṇa. Recognizing that they do not harm myself, harm others, or harm both, cultivate wisdom, cause no trouble, and attain nirvāṇa, I quickly cultivate and spread them."

「比丘者，随所思、随所念，心便乐中。若比丘多念欲念者，则舍无欲念，以多念欲念故，心便乐中。若比丘多念恚念、害念者，则舍无恚念、无害念，以多念恚念、害念故，心便乐中。如是，比丘不离欲念，不离恚念，不离害念者，则不能脱生、老、病、死、愁忧、啼哭，亦复不能离一切苦。我如是行，在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤。生无欲念，我即觉生无欲念，不自害、不害他，亦不俱害，修慧、不烦劳而得涅槃。觉不自害、不害他、亦不俱害，修慧、不烦劳而得涅槃，便速修习广布。复生无恚念、无害念，我即觉生无恚念、无害念，不自害、不害他、亦不俱害，修慧、不烦劳而得涅槃。觉不自害、不害他、亦不俱害，修慧、不烦劳而得涅槃，便速修习广布。」

I give rise to thoughts of non-desire and think about them a lot. I also give rise to thoughts of non-anger and non-harm and think about them a lot. I further think like this: 'Those who think about things a lot will have a distracted body and mind, which will then harm the mind. I should rather regulate my inner mind, always dwell in inner tranquility, attain concentration with one-pointedness, and not harm the mind.' Later, I then regulated my inner mind, always dwelt in inner tranquility, attained concentration with one-pointedness, and did not harm the mind. After giving rise to thoughts of non-desire, I further gave rise to thoughts directed towards the Dharma and its principles. After giving rise to thoughts of non-anger and non-harm, I further gave rise to thoughts directed towards the Dharma and its principles. Why is that? Because I do not see that this will give rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. It is like in the autumn and winter months, when all the crops have been harvested. When cattle herders let their cattle graze in the wild fields, they think like this: 'My cattle are in the herd.' Why is that? Because the cattle herders do not see that this will lead to scolding, beating, binding, and faults. Therefore, they think like this: 'My cattle are in the herd.' I am also like this. After giving rise to thoughts of non-desire, I further give rise to thoughts directed towards the Dharma and its principles. After giving rise to thoughts of non-anger and non-harm, I further give rise to thoughts directed towards the Dharma and its principles. Why is that? Because I do not see that this will give rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas.

「我生无欲念、多思念，生无恚念、无害念、多思念。我复作是念：『多思念者，身定憙忘，则便损心，我宁可治内心，常住在内止息，一意得定，令不损心。』我于后时便治内心，常住在内止息，一意得定，而不损心。我生无欲念已，复生念向法次法；生无恚念、无害念已，复生念向法次法。所以者何？我不见因此生无量恶不善之法。犹如秋后月收一切谷讫，牧牛儿放牛野田时作是念：『我牛在群中。』所以者何？牧牛儿不见因此故当得骂詈，得打、得缚，有过失也，是故彼作是念：『我牛在群中。』我亦如是，生无欲念已，复生念向法次法；生无恚念、无害念已，复生念向法次法。所以者何？我不见因此生无量恶不善之法。

Bhikṣus, one follows one's thoughts and intentions, and the mind then delights in them. If a bhikṣu has many thoughts of non-desire, he abandons thoughts of desire. Because of having many thoughts of non-desire, the mind then delights in them. If a bhikṣu has many thoughts of non-anger and non-harm, he abandons thoughts of anger and harm. Because of having many thoughts of non-anger and non-harm, the mind then delights in them. He then stops thinking and contemplating, internally tranquil, with single-minded concentration, without thinking or contemplating, giving rise to joy and bliss, attaining the second dhyāna and dwelling in it. He then abandons desire for joy, dwells in equanimity, mindful and aware, experiencing bliss in the body, as described by the sages, attaining the third dhyāna and dwelling in it. He then abandons bliss, abandons suffering, and previously abandoned joy and sorrow, neither suffering nor blissful, equanimous, mindful, and pure, attaining the fourth dhyāna and dwelling in it.

「比丘者，随所思、随所念，心便乐中。若比丘多念无欲念者，则舍欲念，以多念无欲念故，心便乐中。若比丘多念无恚念、无害念者，则舍恚念、害念，以多念无恚念、无害念故，心便乐中。彼觉、观已息，内静、一心、无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，得第二禅成就游。彼离喜欲，舍无求游，正念正智而身觉乐，谓圣所说，圣所舍、念、乐住、空，得第三禅成就游。彼乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，得第四禅成就游。

His mind is thus concentrated, pure, without defilement or vexation, supple, well-established, immovable, and directed towards the knowledge and vision of the destruction of the contaminants. He then knows this suffering as it really is, knows the arising of this suffering, knows the cessation of this suffering, and knows the path to the cessation of this suffering as it really is. He also knows these contaminants as they really are, knows the arising of these contaminants, knows the cessation of these contaminants, and knows the path to the cessation of these contaminants as it really is. Having thus known and seen, his mind is liberated from the contaminants of desire, existence, and ignorance. Having been liberated, he then knows he is liberated. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there will not be another existence.

「彼如是定心清净，无秽、无烦，柔软善住，得不动心，趣向漏尽通智作证，便知此苦如真，知此苦习、知此苦灭、知此苦灭道如真；亦知此漏如真，知此漏习、知此漏灭、知此漏灭道如真。彼如是知，如是见已，则欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱，解脱已便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。此比丘离欲念、离恚念、离害念，则得解脱生、老、病、死、愁忧、啼哭，离一切苦。犹如一无事处有大泉水，彼有群鹿游住其中。有一人来，不为彼群鹿求义及饶益、求安隐快乐，塞平正路，开一恶道，作大坑壍，使人守视。如是群鹿一切死尽。复有一人来，为彼群鹿求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，开平正路，闭塞恶道，却守视人，如是群鹿普得安济。

Bhikṣus, you should know that I tell this parable to make you understand the meaning. The wise, having heard the parable, will understand its purport. This parable has meaning. The large pond means the five desires, love, joy, and happiness. What are the five? The eyes know forms, the ears know sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile objects. The large pond means the five desires. The large herd of deer means the śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas. The person who comes and does not seek benefit and welfare for them, seeking peace and happiness, means Māra Pāpīyas. Blocking the level path and opening a bad path means the three unwholesome thoughts: thoughts of desire, anger, and harm. The bad path means the three unwholesome thoughts. There are also other bad paths, namely the eight wrong paths: wrong view up to wrong concentration. These are the eight. Making a large pitfall means ignorance. The person who guards it means Māra Pāpīyas' retinue. The person who comes and seeks benefit and welfare for them, seeking peace and happiness, means the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Closing the bad path and opening the level path means the three wholesome thoughts: thoughts of non-desire, non-anger, and non-harm. The path means the three wholesome thoughts. There are also other paths, namely the eightfold right path: right view up to right concentration. These are the eight.

「比丘！当知我说此喻，欲令知义，慧者闻喻，则解其趣。此说有义，大泉水者，谓是五欲爱念欢乐。云何为五？眼知色、耳知声、鼻知香、舌知味、身知触，大泉水者，当知是五欲也。大群鹿者，当知是沙门、梵志也。有一人来，不为彼求义及饶益、求安隐快乐者，当知是魔波旬也。塞平正路，开一恶道者，是三恶不善念：欲念、恚念、害念也。恶道者，当知是三恶不善念。复更有恶道，谓八邪道，邪见乃至邪定是为八。作大坑壍者，当知是无明也。使人守者，当知是魔波旬眷属也。复有一人来，为彼求义及饶益、求安隐快乐者，当知是如来、无所著、等正觉也。闭塞恶道，开平正路者，是三善念：无欲念、无恚念、无害念也。道者，当知是三善念。复更有道，谓八正道，正见乃至正定是为八。

Bhikṣus, I have opened the level path for you, closed the bad path, filled in the pitfall, and removed the guards. As a teacher does for his disciples, I have given rise to great compassion, pity, and sympathy, seeking benefit and welfare, seeking peace and happiness. I have done this for you. You should also do it for yourselves. Go to secluded places, under trees, in empty and quiet places, and meditate in seclusion. Do not be negligent, but strive diligently, so as not to regret it later. This is my instruction and admonition.

「比丘！我为汝等开平正路，闭塞恶道，填平坑壍，除却守人。如尊师所为弟子起大慈，哀怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐者，我今已作。汝等亦当复自作，至无事处山林树下空安静处，宴坐思惟，勿得放逸，勤加精进，无令后悔。此是我之教勅，是我训诲。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说如是，彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
