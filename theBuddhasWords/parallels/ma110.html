MĀ 110

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 10 林品

110. （一一〇）自观心经

Parallel to: AN 10.51

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "If a bhikṣu is unable to observe the minds of others well, he should observe his own mind well. He should learn in this way. How does a bhikṣu observe his own mind well? A bhikṣu who has this observation will greatly benefit: Do I have much mental agitation or not? Do I have much ill-will or not? Do I have much sleepiness or not? Do I have much conceit or not? Do I have much doubt or not? Do I have much bodily conflict or not? Do I have much defilement or not? Do I have much faith or not? Do I have much diligence or not? Do I have much mindfulness or not? Do I have much concentration or not? Do I have much evil wisdom or not?

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有比丘不能善观于他心者，当自善观察于己心，应学如是。云何比丘善自观心？比丘者，若有此观，必多所饶益：我为多行增伺，为多行无增伺？我为多行瞋恚心，为多行无瞋恚心？我为多行睡眠缠，为多行无睡眠缠？我为多行调贡高，为多行无调贡高？我为多行疑惑，为多行无疑惑？我为多行身诤，为多行无身诤？我为多行秽污心，为多行无秽污心？我为多行信，为多行不信？我为多行精进，为多行懈怠？我为多行念，为多行无念？我为多行定，为多行无定？我为多行恶慧，为多行无恶慧？

If a bhikṣu observes and knows that he has much mental agitation, ill-will, sleepiness, conceit, doubt, bodily conflict, defilement, lack of faith, laziness, lack of mindfulness, lack of concentration, and evil wisdom, then that bhikṣu, wishing to eliminate these evil and unwholesome dharmas, should quickly seek means, study with utmost diligence, with right mindfulness and right knowledge, and endure without retreating. It is like a person whose head or clothes are on fire, urgently seeking means to save their head or clothes. In the same way, a bhikṣu, wishing to eliminate these evil and unwholesome dharmas, should quickly seek means, study with utmost diligence, with right mindfulness and right knowledge, and endure without retreating. If a bhikṣu observes and knows that he has little mental agitation, ill-will, sleepiness, conceit, doubt, bodily conflict, defilement, faith, diligence, mindfulness, concentration, and evil wisdom, then that bhikṣu, having abided in these wholesome dharmas, should seek to realize the knowledge and insight of the exhaustion of the taints. Why is this? I say that one should not keep all kinds of robes, and I also say that one should keep all kinds of robes. Why do I say that one should not keep robes? If keeping robes increases evil and unwholesome dharmas and diminishes wholesome dharmas, then I say that one should not keep such robes. Why do I say that one should keep robes? If keeping robes increases wholesome dharmas and diminishes evil and unwholesome dharmas, then I say that one should keep such robes. As with robes, so it is with food, drink, bedding, and dwelling places. I say that one should not associate with all kinds of people, and I also say that one should associate with all kinds of people. Why do I say that one should not associate with people? If associating with people increases evil and unwholesome dharmas and diminishes wholesome dharmas, then I say that one should not associate with such people. Why do I say that one should associate with people? If associating with people increases wholesome dharmas and diminishes evil and unwholesome dharmas, then I say that one should associate with such people.

「若比丘观时，则知我多行增伺、瞋恚心、睡眠缠、调贡高、疑惑、身诤、秽污心、不信、懈怠、无念、无定、多行恶慧者，彼比丘欲灭此恶不善法故，便以速求方便，学极精勤，正念正智，忍不令退。犹人为火烧头、烧衣、急求方便救头、救衣。如是，比丘欲灭此恶不善法故，便以速求方便，学极精勤，正念正智，忍不令退。若比丘观时，则知我多行无增伺、无瞋恚心、无睡眠缠、无调贡高、无疑惑、无身诤、无秽污心、有信、有进、有念、有定、多行无恶慧者。彼比丘住此善法已，当求漏尽智通作证。所以者何？我说不得畜一切衣，亦说得畜一切衣。云何衣我说不得畜？若畜衣便增长恶不善法、衰退善法者，如是衣我说不得畜。云何衣我说得畜？若畜衣便增长善法、衰退恶不善法者，如是衣我说得畜。如衣，饮食、床榻村邑亦复如是，我说不得狎习一切人，亦说得狎习一切人。云何人我说不得狎习？若狎习人便增长恶不善法、衰退善法者，如是人我说不得狎习。云何人我说得与狎习？若狎习人便增长善法、衰退恶不善法者，如是人我说得与狎习。

"They know the dharmas that should be practiced as they truly are, and they also know the dharmas that should not be practiced as they truly are. Having known the dharmas that should be practiced and the dharmas that should not be practiced as they truly are, they do not practice the dharmas that should not be practiced, and they practice the dharmas that should be practiced. Having not practiced the dharmas that should not be practiced and having practiced the dharmas that should be practiced, they increase wholesome dharmas and diminish unwholesome dharmas. This is called a bhikṣu who observes his own mind well, knows his own mind well, and takes and abandons well."

「彼可习法知如真，不可习法亦知如真。彼可习法、不可习法知如真已，不可习法便不习，可习法便习，彼不可习法不习，可习法习已，便增长善法，衰退恶不善法。是谓比丘善自观心，善自知心，善取善舍。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
