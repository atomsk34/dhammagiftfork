MĀ 113

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 10 林品

113. （一一三）诸法本经

Parallel to: AN 8.83 / AN 10.58

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "If non-Buddhist practitioners come and ask you, 'What is the basis of all dharmas?' you should answer them like this: 'All dharmas are based on desire.' If they further ask, 'What is the harmony?' you should answer like this: 'The harmony is based on contact.' If they further ask, 'What is the coming?' you should answer like this: 'The coming is based on perception.' If they further ask, 'What is the existence?' you should answer like this: 'The existence is based on conceptualization.' If they further ask, 'What is the master?' you should answer like this: 'The master is based on mindfulness.' If they further ask, 'What is the front?' you should answer like this: 'The front is based on concentration.' If they further ask, 'What is the highest?' you should answer like this: 'The highest is based on wisdom.' If they further ask, 'What is the truth?' you should answer like this: 'The truth is based on liberation.' If they further ask, 'What is the completion?' you should answer like this: 'The completion is based on nirvāṇa.' This is what is meant, bhikṣus! Desire is the basis of all dharmas, contact is the harmony of all dharmas, perception is the coming of all dharmas, conceptualization is the existence of all dharmas, mindfulness is the master of all dharmas, concentration is the front of all dharmas, wisdom is the highest of all dharmas, liberation is the truth of all dharmas, and nirvāṇa is the completion of all dharmas. Therefore, bhikṣus, you should train like this.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若诸异学来问汝等：『一切诸法以何为本？』汝等应当如是答彼：『一切诸法以欲为本。』彼若复问：『以何为和？』当如是答：『以更乐为和。』彼若复问：『以何为来？』当如是答：『以觉为来。』彼若复问：『以何为有？』当如是答：『以思想为有。』彼若复问：『以何为上主？』当如是答：『以念为上主。』彼若复问：『以何为前？』当如是答：『以定为前。』彼若复问：『以何为上？』当如是答：『以慧为上。』彼若复问：『以何为真？』当如是答：『以解脱为真。』彼若复问：『以何为讫？』当如是答：『以涅槃为讫。』是为，比丘！欲为诸法本，更乐为诸法和，觉为诸法来，思想为诸法有，念为诸法上主，定为诸法前，慧为诸法上，解脱为诸法真，涅槃为诸法讫。是故比丘当如是学。

"Cultivate the mind of renouncing the world and studying the path, cultivate the thought of impermanence, cultivate the thought of impermanence and suffering, cultivate the thought of suffering and non-self, cultivate the thought of impurity, cultivate the thought of bad food, cultivate the thought of the impermanence of the entire world, cultivate the thought of death. Know the good and bad of the world, cultivate the mind of such thoughts. Know the existence of the world, cultivate the mind of such thoughts. Know the arising, cessation, taste, danger, and exit of the world as it truly is, cultivate the mind of such thoughts. If a bhikṣu cultivates the mind of renouncing the world and studying the path, cultivates the thought of impermanence, cultivates the thought of impermanence and suffering, cultivates the thought of suffering and non-self, cultivates the thought of impurity, cultivates the thought of bad food, cultivates the thought of the impermanence of the entire world, cultivates the thought of death. Knowing the good and bad of the world, cultivates the mind of such thoughts. Knowing the existence of the world, cultivates the mind of such thoughts. Knowing the arising, cessation, taste, danger, and exit of the world as it truly is, cultivates the mind of such thoughts. This is called a bhikṣu who cuts off craving, removes afflictions, and, having rightly known and seen all dharmas, attains the end of suffering."

「习出家学道心，习无常想，习无常苦想，习苦无我想，习不净想，习恶食想，习一切世间不可乐想，习死想。知世间好恶，习如是想心。知世间习有，习如是想心。知世间习、灭、味、患、出要如真，习如是想心。若比丘得习出家学道心者，得习无常想，得习无常苦想，得习苦无我想，得习不净想，得习恶食想，得习一切世间不可乐想，得习死想。知世间好恶，得习如是想心。知世间习有，得习如是想心。知世间习、灭、味、患、出要如真，得习如是想心者，是谓比丘断爱除结，正知正观诸法已，便得苦边。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
