MĀ 115

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 10 林品

115. （一一五）蜜丸喻经

Parallel to: MN 18 / EĀ 40.10

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the Śākya country, staying in Kapilavastu.

一时，佛游释羇瘦，在迦维罗卫。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having passed the night, put on his robe and took his alms bowl in the morning to enter Kapilavastu for alms. After eating, in the middle of the day, he put away his robe and bowl, washed his hands and feet, put his sitting mat on his shoulder, went to the Śākya monastery in the bamboo grove, entered the great forest, went to the foot of a tree, spread out his sitting mat, and sat cross-legged.

尔时，世尊过夜平旦，著衣持钵，为乞食故，入迦维罗卫。食讫，中后收举衣钵，澡洗手足，以尼师坛著于肩上，往诣竹林释迦寺中，入彼大林，至一树下敷尼师檀，结加趺坐。

Then, the Śākya with the staff walked with his staff in the middle of the day, leisurely approached the Buddha, exchanged greetings, stood with his staff in front of the Buddha, and asked the World-Honored One, "Śramaṇa Gautama! What do you take as the basis? What kind of Dharma do you teach?"

于是，执杖释拄杖而行，中后彷徉，往诣佛所，共相问讯，拄杖立佛前，问世尊曰：「沙门瞿昙！以何为宗本？说何等法？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Śākya! If the entire world, including gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, from humans to gods, were to be made to be without disputes, to cultivate detachment and purity, to be free from deceit and hypocrisy, to eliminate regret, to be without attachment to existence or non-existence, and to be without thought, that would be my basis; the teaching would be the same as this."

世尊答曰：「释！若一切世间，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，使不鬪诤，修习离欲清净梵志，舍离谄曲，除悔，不著有、非有，亦无想，是我宗本；说亦如是。」

Then, the Śākya with the staff, having heard what the Buddha said, did not approve or disapprove, and the Śākya with the staff left with his head bowed.

于是，执杖释闻佛所说，不是不非，执杖释奋头而去。

Then, not long after the Śākya with the staff left, in the afternoon, the World-Honored One rose from his seat, went to the lecture hall, sat down on the prepared seat in front of the assembly of monks, and said to the monks, "This morning, I put on my robe, took my bowl, and entered Kapilavastu for alms. After eating, in the middle of the day, I put away my robe and bowl, washed my hands and feet, put my sitting mat on my shoulder, went to the Śākya monastery in the bamboo grove, entered the great forest, went to the foot of a tree, spread out my sitting mat, and sat down cross-legged. Then, the Śākya with the staff, walking with his staff, came to me in the middle of the day, paid his respects, stood in front of me with his staff, and asked me, 'śramaṇa Gautama! What is your basis? What do you teach?' I replied, 'Śākya! If the entire world, including gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, from humans to gods, were to be made to be without disputes, to cultivate detachment and purity, to be free from deceit and hypocrisy, to eliminate regret, to be without attachment to existence or non-existence, and to be without thought, that would be my basis; the teaching would be the same as this.' The Śākya with the staff, having heard what I said, did not approve or disapprove, and the Śākya with the staff left with his head bowed."

于是，世尊执杖释去后不久，则于晡时，从燕坐起，往诣讲堂，比丘众前敷座而坐，告诸比丘：「我今平旦著衣持钵，为乞食故，入迦维罗卫。食讫，中后收举衣钵，澡洗手足，以尼师檀著于肩上，往诣竹林释迦寺中，入彼大林，至一树下敷尼师檀，结跏趺坐。于是，执杖释拄杖而行，中后彷徉，来诣我所，共相问讯，拄杖立我前，问我曰：『沙门瞿昙！以何为宗本？说何等法？』我答曰：『释！若一切世间，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，使不鬪诤，修习离欲清净梵志，舍离谄曲，除悔，不著有、非有，亦无想，是我宗本；说亦如是。』彼执杖释闻我所说，不是不非，执杖释奋头而去。」

Then, a certain monk rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, put his palms together toward the Buddha, and said, "World-Honored One! How can the entire world, including gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, from humans to gods, be made to be without disputes? How does one cultivate detachment and attain pure brāhmanhood? How does one abandon deceit and hypocrisy, eliminate regret, and be without attachment to existence or non-existence, and also without thought?"

于是，有一比丘即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！云何一切世间，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，使不鬪诤？云何修习离欲，得清净梵志？云何舍离谄曲，除悔，不著有、非有，亦无想耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Monk! If one relies on thoughts to renounce the world and study the path, and cultivates thoughts of past, future, and present dharmas, without loving, delighting in, being attached to, or dwelling in them, this is said to be the edge of suffering. The afflictions of desire, anger, existence, pride, ignorance, views, and doubt, as well as disputes, hatred, flattery, deceit, false speech, divisive speech, and countless evil and unwholesome dharmas, are said to be the edge of suffering."

世尊告曰：「比丘！若人所因念，出家学道，思想修习，及过去、未来、今现在法，不爱、不乐、不著、不住，是说苦边欲使，恚使、有使、慢使、无明使、见使、疑使、鬪诤、憎嫉、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌及无量恶不善之法，是说苦边。」

The Buddha spoke thus, then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation.

佛说如是，即从坐起，入室燕坐。

Then the monks thought, "Venerable friends! Know that the World-Honored One has briefly explained this meaning without elaborating, and then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation. 'If one relies on thoughts to renounce the world and study the path, and cultivates thoughts of past, future, and present dharmas, without loving, delighting in, being attached to, or dwelling in them, this is said to be the edge of suffering. The afflictions of desire, anger, existence, pride, ignorance, views, and doubt, as well as disputes, hatred, flattery, deceit, false speech, divisive speech, and countless evil and unwholesome dharmas, are said to be the edge of suffering.'" They further thought, "Venerable friends! Who can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One?" They further thought, "Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise practitioners of the holy life. Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One. Venerable friends! Let us go together to Venerable Mahākātyāyana and request him to explain this meaning. If Venerable Mahākātyāyana explains it, we shall uphold it well."

于是，诸比丘便作是念：「诸贤！当知世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室燕坐。『若人所因念，出家学道，思想修习，及过去、未来、今现在法，不爱、不乐、不著、不住，是说苦边。欲使、恚使、有使、慢使、无明使、见使、疑使、鬪诤、憎嫉、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌及无量恶不善之法，是说苦边。』」彼复作是念：「诸贤！谁能广分别世尊向所略说义？」彼复作是念：「尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。诸贤！共往诣尊者大迦旃延所，请说此义；若尊者大迦旃延为分别者，我等当善受持。」

Then the monks went to Venerable Mahākātyāyana, exchanged greetings, sat down to one side, and said, "Venerable Mahākātyāyana! Know that the World-Honored One has briefly explained this meaning without elaborating, and then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation. 'Monks! If one relies on thoughts to renounce the world and study the path, and cultivates thoughts of past, future, and present dharmas, without loving, delighting in, being attached to, or dwelling in them, this is said to be the edge of suffering. The afflictions of desire, anger, existence, pride, ignorance, views, and doubt, as well as disputes, hatred, flattery, deceit, false speech, divisive speech, and countless evil and unwholesome dharmas, are said to be the edge of suffering.' We then thought, 'Venerable friends! Who can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One?' We further thought, 'Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise practitioners of the holy life. Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One.' We only wish that Venerable Mahākātyāyana would explain it out of compassion."

于是，诸比丘往诣尊者大迦旃延所，共相问讯，却坐一面，白曰：「尊者大迦旃延！当知世尊略说此义，不广分别。即从坐起，入室燕坐。『比丘！若人所因念，出家学道，思想修习，及过去、未来、今现在法，不爱、不乐、不著、不住，是说苦边。欲使、恚使、有使、慢使、无明使、见使、疑使、鬪诤、憎嫉、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌及无量恶不善之法，是说苦边。』我等便作是念：『诸贤！谁能广分别世尊向所略说义？』我等复作是念：『尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。』唯愿尊者大迦旃延为慈愍故而广说之。」

Then Venerable Mahākātyāyana said, "Venerable friends! Listen to my explanation of a parable. The wise understand the meaning through parables. Venerable friends! It is like a person who wants to obtain the fruit. In order to obtain the fruit, he carries an axe into the forest. He sees a big tree with roots, trunk, joints, branches, leaves, flowers, and fruit. He does not touch the roots, trunk, joints, and fruit, but only touches the branches and leaves. The meaning you speak of is also like this. The World-Honored One is present. You should go to him and ask about this meaning. Why is that? Venerable friends! Know that the World-Honored One is the eye, the wisdom, the meaning, the Dharma, the lord of the Dharma, the leader of the Dharma, who speaks the true meaning and manifests all meanings. Venerable friends! You should go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning: 'World-Honored One! What is this? What is the meaning of this?' As the World-Honored One explains it, you should uphold it well."

尔时，尊者大迦旃延告曰：「诸贤！听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。诸贤！犹如有人欲得求实，为求实故，持斧入林，彼见大树成根、茎、节、枝、叶、华、实，彼人不触根、茎、节、实，但触枝、叶。诸贤所说亦复如是，世尊现在，舍来就我而问此义。所以者何？诸贤！当知世尊是眼、是智、是义、是法、法主、法将，说真谛义，现一切义由彼世尊。诸贤应往诣世尊所而问此义：『世尊！此云何？此何义？』如世尊说者，诸贤等当善受持。」

The monks replied, "Yes, Venerable Mahākātyāyana! The World-Honored One is the eye, the wisdom, the meaning, the Dharma, the lord of the Dharma, the leader of the Dharma, who speaks the true meaning and manifests all meanings. We should go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning: 'World-Honored One! What is this? What is the meaning of this?' As the World-Honored One explains it, we will uphold it well. However, Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise practitioners of the holy life. Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One. We only wish that Venerable Mahākātyāyana would explain it out of compassion."

时，诸比丘白曰：「唯然。尊者大迦旃延！世尊是眼、是智、是义、是法、法主、法将，说真谛义，现一切义由彼世尊，我等应往诣世尊所而问此义：『世尊！此云何？此何义？』如世尊说者，我等当善受持。然，尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义，唯愿尊者大迦旃延为慈愍故而广说之。」

Venerable Mahākātyāyana said to the monks, "Venerable friends! Listen to what I say. Venerable friends! Due to the eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises. The coming together of these three things is contact. Due to contact, there is feeling. If there is feeling, there is perception. If there is perception, there is intention. If there is intention, there is memory. If there is memory, there is discrimination. A monk, due to this memory, leaves home to study the path, thinking and practicing. In this, past, future, and present dharmas are not loved, not enjoyed, not attached to, and not dwelled upon. This is said to be the end of suffering. The defilements of desire, anger, existence, pride, ignorance, views, doubt, disputes, hatred, flattery, deceit, lying, divisive speech, and countless other evil and unwholesome dharmas are said to be the end of suffering. In this way, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, due to dharmas, give rise to mental consciousness. The coming together of these three things is contact. Due to contact, there is feeling. If there is feeling, there is perception. If there is perception, there is intention. If there is intention, there is memory. If there is memory, there is discrimination. A monk, due to this memory, leaves home to study the path, thinking and practicing. In this, past, future, and present dharmas are not loved, not enjoyed, not attached to, and not dwelled upon. This is said to be the end of suffering. The defilements of desire, anger, existence, pride, ignorance, views, doubt, disputes, hatred, flattery, deceit, lying, divisive speech, and countless other evil and unwholesome dharmas are said to be the end of suffering.

尊者大迦旃延告诸比丘：「诸贤等共听我所说。诸贤！缘眼及色，生眼识，三事共会，便有更触，缘更触便有所觉，若所觉便想，若所想便思，若所思便念，若所念便分别。比丘者因是念出家学道，思想修习，此中过去、未来、今现在法，不爱、不乐、不著、不住，是说苦边。欲使、恚使、有使、慢使、无明使、见使、疑使、鬪诤、憎嫉、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌及无量恶不善之法，是说苦边。如是耳、鼻、舌、身缘意及法，生意识，三事共会，便有更触，缘更触便有所觉，若所觉便想，若所想便思，若所思便念，若所念便分别。比丘者因是念出家学道，思想修习，此中过去、未来、今现在法，不爱、不乐、不著、不住，是说苦边。欲使、恚使、有使、慢使、无明使、见使、疑使、鬪诤、憎嫉、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌及无量恶不善之法，是说苦边。

Venerable friends! A monk, having eliminated the eye, forms, and eye-consciousness, if there is contact and the designation of contact, that is not so. If contact is not designated, if there is feeling and the designation of feeling, that is not so. If feeling is not designated, if there is memory, leaving home to study the path, thinking, and practicing, that is not so. In this way, the ear, nose, tongue, body, having eliminated the mind, dharmas, and mental consciousness, if there is contact and the designation of contact, that is not so. If contact is not designated, if there is feeling and the designation of feeling, that is not so. If feeling is not designated, if there is memory, leaving home to study the path, thinking, and practicing, that is not so.

「诸贤！比丘者，除眼、除色、除眼识，有更触施设更触者，是处不然。若不施设更触，有觉施设觉者，是处不然。若不施设觉，有施设念、出家学道、思想修习者，是处不然。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，除意、除法、除意识，有更触施设更触者，是处不然。若不施设更触，有觉施设觉者，是处不然。若不施设觉，有施设念、出家学道、思想修习者，是处不然。

"Venerable friends! A monk, due to the eye, forms, and eye-consciousness, if there is contact and the designation of contact, there is certainly such a place. Due to the designation of contact, if there is feeling and the designation of feeling, there is certainly such a place. Due to the designation of feeling, if there is memory, leaving home to study the path, thinking, and practicing, there is certainly such a place. In this way, the ear, nose, tongue, body, due to the mind, dharmas, and mental consciousness, if there is contact and the designation of contact, there is certainly such a place. Due to the designation of contact, if there is feeling and the designation of feeling, there is certainly such a place. Due to the designation of feeling, if there is memory, leaving home to study the path, thinking, and practicing, there is certainly such a place. Venerable friends! The World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning, without elaborating. He then rose from his seat, entered the room, and sat in meditation. 'Monks! If a person, due to memory, leaves home to study the path, thinks and practices, and past, future, and present dharmas are not loved, not enjoyed, not attached to, and not dwelled upon, this is said to be the end of suffering. The defilements of desire, anger, existence, pride, ignorance, views, doubt, disputes, hatred, flattery, deceit, lying, divisive speech, and countless other evil and unwholesome dharmas are said to be the end of suffering.' The World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning, without elaborating. I have explained it in this way using these phrases and these words. Venerable friends! You may go to the Buddha and ask him about it. If it is as the World-Honored One has said, you may then accept and uphold it."

「诸贤！比丘者，因眼、因色、因眼识，有更触施设更触者，必有此处。因施设更触，有觉施设觉者，必有此处。因施设觉，有施设念、出家学道、思想修习者，必有此处。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，因意、因法、因意识，有更触施设更触者，必有此处。因施设更触，有觉施设觉者，必有此处。因施设觉，有施设念、出家学道、思想修习者，必有此处。诸贤！谓世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室燕坐。『比丘！若人所因念，出家学道，思想修习，及过去、未来、今现在法，不爱、不乐、不著、不住，是说苦边。欲使、恚使、有使、慢使、无明使、见使、疑使、鬪诤、憎嫉、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌及无量恶不善之法，是说苦边。』此世尊略说不广分别义，我以此句、以此文广说如是。诸贤！可往向佛具陈，若如世尊所说义者，诸贤等便可受持。」

Then the monks, having heard what Venerable Mahākātyāyana said, accepted and recited it well. They rose from their seats, circumambulated Venerable Mahākātyāyana three times to the right, and departed. They went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, sat to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! The World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning, without elaborating, and then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation. Venerable Mahākātyāyana explained it in this way using these phrases and these words.

于是，诸比丘闻尊者大迦旃延所说，善受持诵，即从坐起，遶尊者大迦旃延三匝而去，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！向世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室宴坐；尊者大迦旃延以此句、以此文而广说之。」

The World-Honored One, having heard this, praised: "Excellent! Excellent! Among my disciples, there are those with eyes, wisdom, the Dharma, and meaning. Why is this? Because the teacher briefly explains this meaning to the disciples, without elaborating, and the disciples explain it in this way using these phrases and these words. As the monk Mahākātyāyana has said, you should accept and uphold it in this way. Why is this? Because the meaning of contemplation should be explained in this way. Monk! It is like a person who, while walking in a place without people, in a mountain forest, suddenly obtains a honey pill, and according to what they eat, they obtain its taste. In this way, a noble son in my true Dharma and Vinaya, according to what they contemplate, obtains its taste. Contemplating the eyes, they obtain the taste. Contemplating the ears, nose, tongue, body, and contemplating the mind, they obtain the taste."

世尊闻已，叹曰：「善哉！善哉！我弟子中有眼、有智、有法、有义。所以者何？谓师为弟子略说此义，不广分别，彼弟子以此句、以此文而广说之。如迦旃延比丘所说，汝等应当如是受持。所以者何？以说观义，应如是也。比丘！犹如有人因行无事处、山林树间，忽得蜜丸，随彼所食而得其味，如是族姓子于我此正法、律，随彼所观而得其味，观眼得味，观耳、鼻、舌、身，观意得味。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was holding a whisk and attending to the Buddha. Then Venerable Ānanda joined his palms towards the Buddha and said: "World-Honored One! What is the name of this teaching? How should I uphold it?"

尔时，尊者阿难执拂侍佛。于是，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！此法名何等？我当云何奉持？」

The World-Honored One said: "Ānanda! This teaching is called the Honey Pill Parable. You should uphold it."

世尊告曰：「阿难！此法名为蜜丸喻，汝当受持。」

Then the World-Honored One told the monks: "You should receive and recite this Honey Pill Parable teaching. Why is that? Monks! This Honey Pill Parable has the Dharma and meaning, is the basis of the holy life, leads to the path, leads to enlightenment, and leads to nirvāṇa. If a noble son shaves off his hair and beard, puts on the monastic robe, with true faith leaves home for homelessness, and studies the path, he should uphold this Honey Pill Parable well."

于是，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等受此蜜丸喻法，当讽诵读。所以者何？比丘！此蜜丸喻有法有义，梵行之本，趣通趣觉，趣于涅槃。若族姓子剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，当善受持此蜜丸喻。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the monks, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them respectfully.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
