MĀ 119

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 11 大品

119. （一一九）说处经

Parallel to: AN 3.67

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "There are three ways of speaking, not four or five. If a bhikṣu sees and then speaks about what he has seen, heard, cognized, or known, and says, 'I know this,' then he is speaking about what he knows. What are the three? Bhikṣus, speaking about the past, saying, 'In the past, there was such and such'; bhikṣus, speaking about the future, saying, 'In the future, there will be such and such'; bhikṣus, speaking about the present, saying, 'In the present, there is such and such.' These are the three ways of speaking, not four or five. If a bhikṣu sees and then speaks about what he has seen, heard, cognized, or known, and says, 'I know this,' then he is speaking about what he knows. Speaking well leads to understanding, while not speaking leads to misunderstanding.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「此有三说处，无四无五。若比丘见已，因彼故说而说我见、闻、识、知，比丘说而说是我所知。云何为三？比丘，因过去世说，而说如是过去世时有；比丘！因未来世说，而说如是未来世时有；比丘！因现在世说，而说如是现在世时有；是谓三说处，无四无五。若比丘见已，因彼故说而说我见、闻、识、知，比丘说而说是我所知，因所说善习得义，因不说不善习得义。

The noble disciple listens to the Dharma with both ears and a single-pointed mind. Having listened to the Dharma with both ears and a single-pointed mind, he abandons one thing, cultivates one thing, and realizes one thing. Having abandoned one thing, cultivated one thing, and realized one thing, he attains right concentration. The noble disciple, having attained right concentration, then abandons all desire, anger, and delusion. The noble disciple thus attains mental liberation. Having attained liberation, he knows liberation: 'My births are ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this state.' There are four ways to observe people in accordance with their teachings: whether they are suitable to speak with or not. If a person speaks in a one-sided way and does not answer in a one-sided way, speaks in a discriminating way and does not answer in a discriminating way, speaks in a questioning way and does not answer in a questioning way, speaks in a stopping way and does not answer in a stopping way, then one should not speak with that person or discuss with them. If a person speaks in a one-sided way and answers in a one-sided way, speaks in a discriminating way and answers in a discriminating way, speaks in a questioning way and answers in a questioning way, speaks in a stopping way and answers in a stopping way, then one may speak with that person and discuss with them.

「贤圣弟子两耳一心听法，彼两耳一心听法已，断一法、修一法、一法作证，彼断一法、修一法、一法作证已，便得正定。贤圣弟子心得正定已，便断一切婬、怒、痴，贤圣弟子如是得心解脱。解脱已便知解脱，我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。因其所说有四处，当以观人，此贤者可共说、不可共说？若使此贤者一向论、不一向答者，分别论、不分别答者，诘论、不诘答者，止论、不止答者，如是此贤者不得共说，亦不得共论。若使此贤者一向论、使一向答者，分别论、分别答者，诘论、诘答者，止论、止答者，如是此贤者得共说，亦得共论。

"Furthermore, based on what they say, there are four more ways to observe people: whether they are suitable to speak with or not. If a person does not abide in the true and false, does not abide in what is to be known, does not abide in the use of similes, and does not abide in the path and its fruits, then one should not speak with that person or discuss with them. If a person abides in the true and false, abides in what is to be known, abides in the use of similes, and abides in the path and its fruits, then one may speak with that person and discuss with them. When speaking, one should stop verbal actions, abandon one's views, abandon enmity, abandon desire, anger, delusion, pride, silence, stinginess, jealousy, not seeking victory, not subduing others, not grasping what is lost, speaking the meaning and speaking the Dharma. Having spoken the meaning and the Dharma, one should teach and instruct again, rejoicing oneself and rejoicing others. Speaking like this, speaking about things like this, is the noble one's speaking of meaning, the noble one's speaking of things, namely, ultimately exhausting the taints."

「复次，因其所说，更有四处，当以观人，此贤者可共说、不可共说？若使此贤者于处、非处不住者，所知不住者，说喻不住者，道迹不住者，如是此贤者不可共说，亦不可共论。若此贤者于处、非处住者，所知住者，说喻住者，道迹住者，如是此贤者可得共说，亦可得共论。因所说时止息口行，舍已所见，舍怨结意，舍欲、舍恚、舍痴、舍慢、舍不语、舍悭嫉、不求胜、不伏他，莫取所失，说义说法。说义说法已，教复教止，自欢喜、令彼欢喜，如是说义，如是说事，是圣说义，是圣说事，谓至竟漏尽。」

Then the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

于是，世尊说此颂曰：

If there is debate and discussion, with mixed intentions and
arrogance, the non-noble ones slander the virtuous, each seeking an
opportunity. They only seek the faults of others, intending to
subdue them, competing with each other for victory. The noble
ones do not speak like this. If one wishes to
debate, the wise should know the time. There is Dharma
and also meaning. The noble ones debate like this. The
wise speak like this, without dispute, without arrogance, with inexhaustible
intention, without bonds, without taints. Following the non-inverted, speaking with
right knowledge, speaking well is acceptable, but never speaking evil
Not debating, also not accepting others' debates, knowing the place
and speaking the place, that is what they discuss. The
noble ones speak like this, the wise all attain the
meaning, for the present life to attain happiness, also for
peace in the future. One should know the wise ones
not speaking the inverted or the impermanent.

「若有诤论议，　　杂意怀贡高，非圣毁呰德，　　各各相求便。但求他过失，　　意欲降伏彼，更互而求胜，　　圣不如是说。若欲得论议，　　慧者当知时，有法亦有义，　　诸圣论如是。慧者如是说，　　无诤无贡高，意无有厌足，　　无结无有漏。随顺不颠倒，　　正知而为说，善说则然可，　　自终不说恶。不以诤论议，　　亦不受他诤，知处及说处，　　是彼之所论。如是圣人说，　　慧者俱得义，为现法得乐，　　亦为后世安。当知聪达者，　　非倒非常说。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
