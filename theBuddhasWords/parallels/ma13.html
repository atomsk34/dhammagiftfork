MĀ 13

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 2 业相应品

13. Transcended （一三）度经

Parallel to: AN 3.61

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "There are three kinds of people who are of different surnames, names, sects, and teachings. They are wise ones who well receive, uphold, and explain to others, but do not gain benefits. What are the three? Some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have such views and say that all that people do is caused by past karma. Again, some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have such views and say that all that people do is caused by the blessings of the noble ones. Again, some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have such views and say that all that people do is without cause and condition.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有三度处，异姓、异名、异宗、异说，谓有慧者善受、极持而为他说，然不获利。云何为三？或有沙门、梵志如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆因宿命造；复有沙门、梵志如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆因尊祐造；复有沙门、梵志如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆无因无缘。

Among them, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have such views and say that all that people do is caused by past karma, I go to them and ask: 'Virtuous ones, do you really have such views and say that all that people do is caused by past karma?' They reply: 'Yes.' I then tell them: 'If so, then all of you virtuous ones are killing living beings. Why is that? Because all of it is caused by past karma. Thus, all of you virtuous ones are taking what is not given, engaging in sexual misconduct, speaking falsehoods, and even holding wrong views. Why is that? Because all of it is caused by past karma. Virtuous ones, if all of it is caused by past karma, and one sees it as true, then one has no desire or means for what is done or not done internally. Virtuous ones, if one does not truly know what is done or not done, then one loses right mindfulness and right knowledge, and there is nothing to teach. If one speaks in this way, one can logically defeat those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas.'

「于中若有沙门、梵志如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆因宿命造者，我便往彼，到已，即问：『诸贤！实如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆因宿命造耶？』彼答言：『尔。』我复语彼：『若如是者，诸贤等皆是杀生。所以者何？以其一切皆因宿命造故。如是，诸贤皆是不与取、邪婬、妄言，乃至邪见。所以者何？以其一切皆因宿命造故。诸贤！若一切皆因宿命造，见如真者，于内因内，作以不作，都无欲、无方便。诸贤！若于作以不作，不知如真者，便失正念、无正智，则无可以教，如沙门法如是说者，乃可以理伏彼沙门、梵志。』

Among them, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have such views and say that all that people do is caused by the blessings of the noble ones, I go to them and ask: 'Virtuous ones, do you really have such views and say that all that people do is caused by the blessings of the noble ones?' They reply: 'Yes.' I then tell them: 'If so, then all of you virtuous ones are killing living beings. Why is that? Because all of it is caused by the blessings of the noble ones. Thus, all of you virtuous ones are taking what is not given, engaging in sexual misconduct, speaking falsehoods, and even holding wrong views. Why is that? Because all of it is caused by the blessings of the noble ones. Virtuous ones, if all of it is caused by the blessings of the noble ones, and one sees it as true, then one has no desire or means for what is done or not done internally. Virtuous ones, if one does not truly know what is done or not done, then one loses right mindfulness and right knowledge, and there is nothing to teach. If one speaks in this way, one can logically defeat those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas.'

「于中若有沙门、梵志如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆因尊祐造者，我便往彼，到已，即问：『诸贤！实如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆因尊祐造耶？』彼答言：『尔。』我复语彼：『若如是者，诸贤等皆是杀生。所以者何？以其一切皆因尊祐造故。如是，诸贤皆是不与取、邪婬、妄言，乃至邪见。所以者何？以其一切皆因尊祐造故。诸贤！若一切皆因尊祐造，见如真者，于内因内，作以不作，都无欲、无方便。诸贤！若于作以不作，不知如真者，便失正念、无正智，则无可以教，如沙门法如是说者，乃可以理伏彼沙门、梵志。』

Among them, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have such views and say that all that people do is without cause and condition, I go to them and ask: 'Virtuous ones, do you really have such views and say that all that people do is without cause and condition?' They reply: 'Yes.' I then tell them: 'If so, then all of you virtuous ones are killing living beings. Why is that? Because all of it is without cause and condition. Thus, all of you virtuous ones are taking what is not given, engaging in sexual misconduct, speaking falsehoods, and even holding wrong views. Why is that? Because all of it is without cause and condition. Virtuous ones, if all of it is without cause and condition, and one sees it as true, then one has no desire or means for what is done or not done internally. Virtuous ones, if one does not truly know what is done or not done, then one loses right mindfulness and right knowledge, and there is nothing to teach. If one speaks in this way, one can logically defeat those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas.'

「于中若有沙门、梵志如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆无因无缘者，我便往彼，到已，即问：『诸贤！实如是见、如是说，谓人所为一切皆无因无缘耶？』彼答言：『尔。』我复语彼：『若如是者，诸贤等皆是杀生。所以者何？以其一切皆无因无缘故。如是，诸贤皆是不与取、邪婬、妄言，乃至邪见。所以者何？以其一切皆无因无缘故。诸贤！若一切皆无因无缘，见如真者，于内因内，作以不作，都无欲、无方便。诸贤！若于作以不作，不知如真者，便失正念、无正智，则无可以教，如沙门法如是说者，乃可以理伏彼沙门、梵志。』

The Dharma that I have personally known and realized, which I teach to you, cannot be defeated, defiled, or controlled by any śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, Māra, Brahmā, or any other being in the world. How is it that the Dharma that I have personally known and realized, which I teach to you, cannot be defeated, defiled, or controlled by any śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, Māra, Brahmā, or any other being in the world? It is because there is the Dharma of the six sense bases, which I have personally known and realized and teach to you, and which cannot be defeated, defiled, or controlled by any śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, Māra, Brahmā, or any other being in the world. There is also the Dharma of the six elements, which I have personally known and realized and teach to you, and which cannot be defeated, defiled, or controlled by any śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, Māra, Brahmā, or any other being in the world.

「我所自知、自觉法，为汝说者，若沙门、梵志，若天、魔、梵及余世间皆无能伏，皆无能秽，皆无能制。云何我所自知、自觉法为汝说，非为沙门、梵志，若天、魔、梵及余世间所能伏、所能秽、所能制？谓有六处法，我所自知、自觉为汝说，非为沙门、梵志，若天、魔、梵及余世间所能伏、所能秽、所能制。复有六界法，我所自知、自觉为汝说，非为沙门、梵志，若天、魔、梵及余世间所能伏、所能秽、所能制。

What is the Dharma of the six sense bases, which I have personally known and realized and teach to you? It refers to the sense base of the eye, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is called the Dharma of the six sense bases, which I have personally known and realized and teach to you. What is the Dharma of the six elements, which I have personally known and realized and teach to you? It refers to the earth element, the water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness elements. This is called the Dharma of the six elements, which I have personally known and realized and teach to you.

「云何六处法，我所自知、自觉为汝说？谓眼处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意处，是谓六处法，我所自知、自觉为汝说也。云何六界法，我所自知、自觉为汝说？谓地界，水、火、风、空、识界，是谓六界法，我所自知、自觉为汝说也。

"Because of the combination of the six elements, one enters the mother's womb. Based on the six elements, there are the six sense bases. Based on the six sense bases, there is contact. Based on contact, there is feeling. Bhikṣus, if there is feeling, one can truly know suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path to the cessation of suffering. What is truly knowing suffering? It refers to the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, death, being with what is disliked, being separated from what is loved, not getting what one desires, and the suffering of the five aggregates. This is called truly knowing suffering. What is truly knowing the origin of suffering? It refers to this craving, which leads to future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, seeking various forms of existence. This is called truly knowing the origin of suffering. What is truly knowing the cessation of suffering? It refers to the complete cessation, abandonment, elimination, exhaustion, absence of desire, cessation, tranquility, and extinction of this craving, which leads to future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, seeking various forms of existence. This is called truly knowing the cessation of suffering. What is truly knowing the path to the cessation of suffering? It refers to the eightfold noble path, namely right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called truly knowing the path to the cessation of suffering. Bhikṣus, you should truly know suffering, abandon the origin of suffering, realize the cessation of suffering, and cultivate the path to the cessation of suffering. If bhikṣus truly know suffering, abandon the origin of suffering, realize the cessation of suffering, and cultivate the path to the cessation of suffering, then they are called bhikṣus who have exhausted all taints, untied all bonds, and attained the end of suffering through right knowledge."

「以六界合故，便生母胎，因六界便有六处，因六处便有更乐，因更乐便有觉。比丘！若有觉者便知苦如真，知苦习、知苦灭、知苦灭道如真。云何知苦如真？谓生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、怨憎会苦、爱别离苦、所求不得苦、略五盛阴苦，是谓知苦如真。云何知苦习如真？谓此爱受当来有，乐欲共俱，求彼彼有，是谓知苦习如真。云何知苦灭如真？谓此爱受当来有，乐欲共俱，求彼彼有断无余、舍、吐、尽、无欲、灭、止、没，是谓知苦灭如真。云何知苦灭道如真？谓八支圣道，正见乃至正定，是为八，是谓知苦灭道如真。比丘！当知苦如真，当断苦习，当苦灭作证，当修苦灭道。若比丘知苦如真，断苦习，苦灭作证，修苦灭道者，是谓比丘一切漏尽，诸结已解，能以正智而得苦际。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
