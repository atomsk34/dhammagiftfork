MĀ 141

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 11 大品

141. （一四一）喻经

Parallel to: SN 3.17 (~) / SN 45.141–148 / SĀ 1239 / SĀ² 2.66

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If there are immeasurable wholesome dharmas to be attained, they all take non-distraction as their basis, non-distraction as their practice, are born from non-distraction, and are led by non-distraction; non-distraction is the foremost among all wholesome dharmas. It is like farming, which all depends on the land, relies on the land, and is established on the land to be able to farm. In the same way, if there are immeasurable wholesome dharmas to be attained, they all take non-distraction as their basis, non-distraction as their practice, are born from non-distraction, and are led by non-distraction; non-distraction is the foremost among all wholesome dharmas. It is like seeds, villages, and ghost villages, where hundreds of grains, medicines, and trees can grow and be nourished, all depending on the land, relying on the land, and being established on the land to be able to grow and be nourished.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有无量善法可得，彼一切以不放逸为本，不放逸为习，因不放逸生，不放逸为首；不放逸者，于诸善法为最第一。犹作田业，彼一切因地、依地、立地，得作田业。如是若有无量善法可得，彼一切以不放逸为本，不放逸为习，因不放逸生，不放逸为首；不放逸者，于诸善法为最第一。犹种子，村及与鬼村，百谷药木得生长养，彼一切因地、依地、立地，得生长养。

In the same way, if there are immeasurable wholesome dharmas to be attained, they all take non-distraction as their basis, non-distraction as their practice, are born from non-distraction, and are led by non-distraction; non-distraction is the foremost among all wholesome dharmas. It is like the fragrance of various roots, with agarwood being the foremost. It is like the fragrance of various trees, with red sandalwood being the foremost. It is like the water flowers, with blue lotus flowers being the foremost. It is like the land flowers, with sumana flowers being the foremost. It is like the footprints of various animals, with all of them being able to fit within the footprint of an elephant, and the elephant's footprint being the foremost, because it is the largest. In the same way, if there are immeasurable wholesome dharmas to be attained, they all take non-distraction as their basis, non-distraction as their practice, are born from non-distraction, and are led by non-distraction; non-distraction is the foremost among all wholesome dharmas. It is like the various animals, with the lion king being the foremost. It is like when two armies are arrayed and fighting, with only the oath being the foremost. It is like the rafters of a tower, with all of them relying on the rafters and beams, and the rafters and beams being able to hold them all; the rafters and beams are the foremost, because they hold everything.

「如是，若有无量善法可得，彼一切以不放逸为本，不放逸为习，因不放逸生，不放逸为首；不放逸者，于诸善法为最第一。犹诸根香，沈香为第一。犹诸树香，赤栴檀为第一。犹诸水华，青莲华为第一。犹诸陆华，须摩那华为第一。犹诸兽迹，彼一切悉入象迹中，象迹尽摄，彼象迹者为最第一，谓广大故。如是，若有无量善法可得，彼一切以不放逸为本，不放逸为习，因不放逸生，不放逸为首；不放逸者，于诸善法为最第一。犹诸兽中，彼师子王为最第一。犹如列阵共鬪战时，唯要誓为第一。犹楼观椽，彼一切皆依承椽梁立，承椽梁、承椽梁皆摄持之，承椽梁者为最第一，谓尽摄故。

"In the same way, if there are immeasurable wholesome dharmas to be attained, they all take non-distraction as their basis, non-distraction as their practice, are born from non-distraction, and are led by non-distraction; non-distraction is the foremost among all wholesome dharmas. It is like the various mountains, with Mount Sumeru being the foremost. It is like the various springs, with the great ocean being the foremost. It is like the various great bodies, with the asura king being the foremost. It is like the various attendants, with the demon king being the foremost. It is like the various desires, with King Top-Born being the foremost. It is like the various minor kings, with the wheel-turning king being the foremost. It is like the various stars in the sky, with the moon palace being the foremost. It is like the various colored garments, with white silk being the foremost. It is like the various lights, with the light of wisdom being the foremost. It is like the various assemblies, with the Tathāgata's disciples being the foremost. It is like the various dharmas, with the cessation of craving, desirelessness, and the cessation of cessation, nirvāṇa, being the foremost. It is like the various beings, without feet, with two feet, with four feet, with many feet, with form, without form, with perception, without perception, up to neither with perception nor without perception, with the Tathāgata being the foremost among them, the greatest, the highest, the most excellent, the most honored, and the most wonderful. It is like how from cows there is milk, from milk there is curds, from curds there is fresh butter, from fresh butter there is clarified butter, and from clarified butter there is butter essence, with butter essence being the foremost, the greatest, the highest, the most excellent, the most honored, and the most wonderful. In the same way, if there are various beings, without feet, with two feet, with four feet, with many feet, with form, without form, with perception, without perception, up to neither with perception nor without perception, the Tathāgata is the foremost among them, the greatest, the highest, the most excellent, the most honored, and the most wonderful."

「如是，若有无量善法可得，彼一切以不放逸为本，不放逸为习，因不放逸生，不放逸为首；不放逸者，于诸善法为最第一。犹如诸山，须弥山王为第一。犹如诸泉，大泉摄水，大海为第一。犹诸大身，阿须罗王为第一。犹诸瞻侍，魔王为第一。犹诸行欲，顶生王为第一。犹如诸小王，转轮王为第一。犹如虚空诸星，宿月殿为第一。犹诸彩衣，白练为第一。犹诸光明，慧光明为第一。犹如诸众，如来弟子众第一。犹如诸法，有为及无为，爱尽、无欲、灭尽、涅槃为第一。犹诸众生，无足、二足、四足、多足，色、无色、有想、无想，乃至非有想非无想，如来于彼为极第一，为大为上，为最为胜，为尊为妙。犹如因牛有乳，因乳有酪，因酪有生酥，因生酥有熟酥，因熟酥有酥精，酥精为第一，为大为上，为最为胜，为尊为妙。如是若有诸众生，无足、二足、四足、多足，色、无色、有想、无想，乃至非有想非无想，如来于彼为极第一，为大为上，为最为胜，为尊为妙。」

Then the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

于是，世尊说此颂曰：

If one seeks wealth, it will increase greatly. Praising non-distraction
speaking about what is and is not. Those who are
non-distracted will surely attain both meanings, attaining them in this
life and also in the next life. The heroic ones
observe all meanings, the wise ones will surely be liberated.

「若有求财物，　　极好转增多，称誉不放逸，　　事无事慧说。有不放逸者，　　必取二俱义，即此世能获，　　后世亦复得，雄猛观诸义，　　慧者必解脱。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The twenty-fifth sūtra on parables is completed.

喻经第二十五竟
