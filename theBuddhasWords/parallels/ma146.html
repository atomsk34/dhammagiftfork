MĀ 146

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 12 梵志品

146. （一四六）象迹喻经

Parallel to: MN 27

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the non-Buddhist teacher Bhīru left Śrāvastī in the morning and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head in reverence, sat to one side, and the Buddha taught him the Dharma, inspiring and uplifting him, bringing about joy and delight. Having used immeasurable skillful means to teach him the Dharma, inspiring and uplifting him, bringing about joy and delight, he remained silent. The non-Buddhist teacher Bhīru, having been taught the Dharma by the Buddha, inspired and uplifted, brought to joy and delight, rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times, and departed.

尔时，卑卢异学平旦则从舍卫国出，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面。佛为彼说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜，无量方便为彼说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜已，默然而住。卑卢异学，佛为说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜已，即从坐起，稽首佛足，绕三匝而去。

At that time, the Brāhman student Jīvaka was riding in an excellent white carriage, together with five hundred disciples, leaving Śrāvastī in the morning to go to a place without affairs, to teach his disciples to recite the scriptures. Jīvaka saw the non-Buddhist teacher Bhīru coming from afar and asked, "Bhīru, where have you come from this morning?"

尔时，生闻梵志乘极好白乘，与五百弟子俱，以平旦时从舍卫出，至无事处，欲教弟子讽读经书。生闻梵志遥见卑卢异学来，便问：「婆蹉！晨起从何处来？」

Bhīru replied, "Brāhman, I have come to pay homage and make offerings to the World-Honored One."

卑卢异学答曰：「梵志！我见世尊礼事、供养来。」

Jīvaka asked, "Bhīru, do you know that the Śramaṇa Gautama studies wisdom in an empty and quiet place?"

生闻梵志问曰：「婆蹉！颇知沙门瞿昙空安静处学智慧耶？」

Bhīru replied, "Brāhman, who can know that the World-Honored One studies wisdom in an empty and quiet place? Brāhman, if one knows that the World-Honored One studies wisdom in an empty and quiet place, one should be like that. But, Brāhman, I have read four phrases in the scriptures, and based on these four phrases, I must believe that the World-Honored One is a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, that the Dharma he teaches is good, and that the noble assembly of disciples of the Tathāgata is good. Brāhman, it is like a skilled elephant trainer traveling to an uninhabited place, where he sees the tracks of a large elephant in the forest. Having seen them, he must believe that the elephant is extremely large and has left those tracks. Brāhman, it is the same for me. I have read four phrases in the scriptures, and based on these four phrases, I must believe that the World-Honored One is a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, that the Dharma he teaches is good, and that the noble assembly of disciples of the Tathāgata is good.

卑卢异学答曰：「梵志！何等人可知世尊空安静处学智慧耶？梵志！若知世尊空安静处学智慧者，亦当如彼。但，梵志！我所读书有四句义，因四句义，我必信世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。梵志！譬善象师游无事处，于树林间见大象迹，见已，必信彼象极大而有此迹。梵志！我亦如是，我所读书有四句义，因四句义，我必信世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。

What are the four phrases? Brāhman, a wise kṣatriya scholar who is well-versed and decisive can subdue worldly people and knows everything. He composes treatises based on various views and circulates them in the world. He thinks, 'I will go to the Śramaṇa Gautama and ask him about such and such matters. If he can answer, I will ask further. If he cannot answer, I will subdue him and leave.' He hears that the World-Honored One is traveling to a certain village or town, so he goes there. Having seen the World-Honored One, he does not dare to ask, let alone subdue him. Brāhman, I have read the scriptures and obtained this first phrase. Based on this phrase, I must believe that the World-Honored One is a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, that the Dharma he teaches is good, and that the noble assembly of disciples of the Tathāgata is good.

「云何四句义？梵志！智慧刹利论士多闻决定，能伏世人，无所不知，则以诸见造作文章，行于世间。彼作是念：『我往沙门瞿昙所，问如是如是事，若能答者，当复重问，若不能答，便伏舍去。』彼闻世尊游某村邑，便往彼所，见世尊已，尚不敢问，况复能伏？梵志！我所读书，用得如此第一句义，我因此义，必信世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。

In the same way, a wise brāhman, a wise householder, a wise śramaṇa scholar who is well-versed and decisive can subdue worldly people and knows everything. He composes treatises based on various views and circulates them in the world. He thinks, 'I will go to the Śramaṇa Gautama and ask him about such and such matters. If he can answer, I will ask further. If he cannot answer, I will subdue him and leave.' He hears that the World-Honored One is traveling to a certain village or town, so he goes there. Having seen the World-Honored One, he does not dare to ask, let alone subdue him. Brāhman, I have read the scriptures and obtained this fourth phrase. Based on this phrase, I must believe that the World-Honored One is a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, that the Dharma he teaches is good, and that the noble assembly of disciples of the Tathāgata is good.

「如是智慧梵志、智慧居士、智慧沙门论士多闻决定，能伏世人，无所不知，则以诸见造作文章，行于世间。彼作是念：『我往沙门瞿昙所，问如是如是事，若能答者，当复重问，若不能答，便伏舍去。』彼闻世尊游某村邑，便往彼所，见世尊已，尚不敢问，况复能伏？梵志！我所读书，用得如此第四句义，我因此义，必信世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。梵志！我所读书有此四句义，我因此四句义故，必信世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。」

The brāhman Śiṃśapā said, "So it is, brāhman. The Tathāgata is a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, the Dharma he teaches is good, and the noble assembly of disciples of the Tathāgata is good." The brāhman Śiṃśapā said, "So it is, brāhman. The Tathāgata is a

生闻梵志语曰：「婆蹉！汝大供养沙门瞿昙，所因所缘，欢喜奉行。」

The brāhman Śiṃśapā said, "So it is, brāhman. The Tathāgata is a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, the Dharma he teaches is good, and the noble assembly of disciples of the Tathāgata is good." The brāhman Śiṃśapā said, "So it is, brāhman. The Tathāgata is a

卑卢异学答曰：「梵志！如是，如是。我极供养于彼世尊，亦极称誉，一切世间亦应供养。」

At that time, the brāhman Śiṃśapā, having heard this meaning, immediately descended from his carriage, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms towards the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma, and bowed three times, saying, "Homage to the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One." Having done so three times, he returned to his excellent white carriage and went to the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma. Upon arriving, he descended from the carriage and walked to where the Buddha was. They exchanged greetings and then sat to one side.

彼时，生闻梵志闻此义已，即从乘下，右膝著地，叉手向于胜林给孤独园，再三作礼：「南无如来、无所著、等正觉。」如是至三已，还乘极好白乘，往诣胜林给孤独园。到彼乘地，即便下乘，步进诣佛，共相问讯，却坐一面。

The brāhman Śiṃśapā told the Buddha in detail about the discussion he had had with the brāhman Śiṃśapā. The World-Honored One, having heard it, said, "Brāhman, the brāhman Śiṃśapā's simile of the elephant's footprint is not well-made and not complete. I will now explain to you the well-made and complete simile of the elephant's footprint. Listen well. Brāhman, it is like a skilled elephant trainer who, while traveling in an uninhabited place, sees a large elephant footprint in a forest. Having seen it, he is certain that there is a very large elephant with such a footprint. Brāhman, if that skilled elephant trainer is not convinced, he will further search in that forest for the footprint of a female elephant named Karīṣa, which is very large. Having found it, he will then see a large elephant footprint.

生闻梵志向与卑卢异学所共论事尽向佛说，世尊闻已，告曰：「梵志！卑卢异学说象迹喻，犹不善作，亦不具足，如象迹喻善作具足者，今为汝说，当善听之。梵志！譬善象师游无事处，于树林间见大象迹，见已，必信彼象极大而有此迹。梵志！彼善象师或不信者，于此林中复有母象，名加梨［少／兔］，身极高大，彼有此迹。即寻此迹，复见大象迹，见已，必信彼象极大而有此迹。

Brāhman, if that skilled elephant trainer is still not convinced, he will further search in that forest for the footprint of a female elephant named Karīṣa, which is very large. Having found it, he will then see a large elephant footprint. Having seen it, he is certain that there is a very large elephant with such a footprint. Brāhman, if that skilled elephant trainer is still not convinced, he will further search in that forest for the footprint of a female elephant named Karīṣa, which is very large. Having found it, he will then see a large elephant footprint. Having seen it, he is certain that there is a very large elephant with such a footprint. Having searched for the footprints, he sees a large elephant footprint, which is very long and wide, completely touching the ground, deeply sunk into the ground. He also sees that elephant going, coming, standing, running, standing, or lying down. Having seen that elephant, he thinks, 'If there are such footprints, there must be a very large elephant.'

「梵志！彼善象师或复不信，于此林中更有母象，名加罗梨，身极高大，彼有此迹。即寻此迹，复见大象迹，见已，必信彼象极大而有此迹。梵志！彼善象师或复不信，于此林中更有母象，名婆惒［少／兔］，身极高大，彼有此迹。即寻此迹，复见大象迹，见已，必信彼象极大而有此迹。彼寻此迹已，见大象迹，大象迹方极长极广，周匝遍著，正深入地，及见彼象，或去或来，或住或走，或立或卧，见彼象已，便作是念：『若有此迹，必是大象。』

Brāhman, it is like this. If a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, a Knower of the World, a Leader of Tamed People, a Teacher of Gods and Men, a Buddha, a Blessed One appears in the world, he knows and sees for himself, awakens to, and accomplishes the state of a Buddha in this world with its gods, Māra, Brahmā, recluses, brahmins, and gods and men. Having done so, he declares it. His teaching is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing, revealing a holy life that is perfectly complete and pure. When some householder or householder's son hears it, he gains faith in the Tathāgata's true teaching and discipline. Having gained faith, he thinks, 'The household life is cramped and dusty, and the going forth to practice the path is wide open. If I were to stay in the household life, I would be bound by fetters and unable to practice the holy life to the full extent of my life. Why don't I give up a little or a lot of wealth, a little or a lot of relatives, shave off my hair and beard, put on the yellow robe, leave home out of faith, and go forth to practice the path?'

「梵志！如是。若世中出如来、无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐，彼于此世，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，乃至天、人，自知自觉，自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。彼说法初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行。彼所说法，或居士、居士子，闻已得信，于如来正法、律彼得信已，便作是念：『在家至狭，尘劳之处，出家学道，发露旷大。我今在家，为鏁所鏁，不得尽形寿净修梵行，我宁可舍于少财物及多财物，舍少亲族及多亲族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。』

Later, he gives up a little or a lot of wealth, a little or a lot of relatives, shaves off his hair and beard, puts on the yellow robe, leaves home out of faith, and goes forth to practice the path. Having gone forth, he abandons the characteristics of a householder, accepts the precepts of a bhikkhu, practices and guards the precepts, and protects the precepts. He also well restrains his deportment and etiquette, sees even the slightest fault as always fearful, and upholds the precepts of training.

「彼于后时，舍少财物及多财物，舍少亲族及多亲族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信舍家、无家、学道。彼出家已，舍亲族相，受比丘要，修习禁戒，守护从解脱。又复善摄威仪礼节，见纤芥罪，常怀畏怖，受持学戒。

He abandons killing, cuts off killing, abandons weapons, has shame and conscience, has compassion and kindness, benefits all beings, even insects. He purifies his mind from killing. He abandons stealing, cuts off stealing, takes only what is given, delights in taking only what is given, always likes to give, rejoices in giving, is not stingy, and does not expect anything in return. He purifies his mind from stealing. He abandons sexual misconduct, cuts off sexual misconduct, diligently practices celibacy, diligently practices the sublime practice, is pure and undefiled, free from desire, and cuts off sexual desire. He purifies his mind from sexual misconduct.

「彼离杀、断杀，弃舍刀杖，有惭有愧，有慈悲心，饶益一切，乃至蜫虫，彼于杀生净除其心。彼离不与取、断不与取，与而后取，乐于与取，常好布施，欢喜无恡，不望其报，彼于不与取净除其心。彼离非梵行、断非梵行，懃修梵行，精懃妙行，清净无秽，离欲断婬，彼于非梵行净除其心。

He abandons false speech, cuts off false speech, speaks the truth, delights in the truth, abides in the truth, is not moved, is trustworthy, does not deceive the world. He purifies his mind from false speech. He abandons divisive speech, cuts off divisive speech, practices non-divisive speech, does not break others apart, does not hear this and tell that to break them apart, does not hear that and tell this to break them apart. He wants to reconcile those who are divided, delights in those who are united, does not form factions, does not delight in factions, does not praise faction-related matters. He purifies his mind from divisive speech. He abandons harsh speech, cuts off harsh speech. Whatever he says, his words are coarse, harsh, unpleasant to the ear, disagreeable, hurtful, causing others to be disturbed, unable to attain concentration. He cuts off such speech. Whatever he says, it is gentle, smooth, pleasant to the ear, agreeable, causing others to be at ease, his voice is clear, not causing others to fear, enabling others to attain concentration. He speaks such words. He purifies his mind from harsh speech. He abandons idle chatter, cuts off idle chatter. He speaks at the right time, speaks the truth, speaks the Dharma, speaks the meaning, speaks cessation, speaks delight in cessation, speaks matters according to the occasion, teaches well, admonishes well. He purifies his mind from idle chatter.

「彼离妄言、断妄言，真谛言，乐真谛，住真谛，不移动，一切可信，不欺世间，彼于妄言净除其心。彼离两舌、断于两舌，行不两舌，不破坏他，不闻此语彼，欲破坏此，不闻彼语此，欲破坏彼，离者欲合，合者欢喜，不作群党，不乐群党，不称说群党事，彼于两舌净除其心。彼离麤言、断于麤言。若有所言，辞气麤［麸－夫＋黄］，恶声逆耳，众所不憙，众所不爱，使他苦恼，令不得定，断如是言。若有所言，清和柔润，顺耳入心，可憙可爱，使他安隐，言声具了，不使人畏，令他得定，说如是言，彼于麤言净除其心。彼离绮语、断于绮语，时说、真说、法说、义说、止息说、乐止息说，事随时得宜，善教善诃，彼于绮语净除其心。

He abandons commerce, cuts off commerce, abandons weighing and measuring, also does not accept goods, does not bind people, does not hope for a discount, does not deceive others with small profits. He purifies his mind from commerce. He abandons accepting widows and girls, cuts off accepting widows and girls. He purifies his mind from accepting widows and girls. He abandons accepting slaves and servants, cuts off accepting slaves and servants. He purifies his mind from accepting slaves and servants. He abandons accepting elephants, horses, cattle, and sheep, cuts off accepting elephants, horses, cattle, and sheep. He purifies his mind from accepting elephants, horses, cattle, and sheep. He abandons accepting chickens and pigs, cuts off accepting chickens and pigs. He purifies his mind from accepting chickens and pigs. He abandons accepting fields, businesses, and shops, cuts off accepting fields, businesses, and shops. He purifies his mind from accepting fields, businesses, and shops. He abandons accepting raw rice, wheat, and beans, cuts off accepting raw rice, wheat, and beans. He purifies his mind from accepting raw rice, wheat, and beans.

「彼离治生、断于治生、弃舍称量及斗斛，亦不受货，不缚束人，不望折斗量，不以小利侵欺于人，彼于治生净除其心。彼离受寡妇、童女，断受寡妇、童女。彼于受寡妇、童女净除其心。彼离受奴婢、断受奴婢，彼于受奴婢净除其心。彼离受象、马、牛、羊，断受象、马、牛、羊，彼于受象、马、牛、羊净除其心。彼离受鸡、猪，断受鸡、猪，彼于受鸡、猪净除其心。彼离受田业、店肆，断受田业、店肆，彼于受田业、店肆净除其心。彼离受生稻、麦、豆，断受生稻、麦、豆，彼于受生稻、麦、豆净除其心。

He abandons alcohol, cuts off alcohol. He purifies his mind from drinking alcohol. He abandons high and wide beds, cuts off high and wide beds. He purifies his mind from high and wide beds. He abandons flowers, garlands, necklaces, perfumes, and cosmetics, cuts off flowers, garlands, necklaces, perfumes, and cosmetics. He purifies his mind from flowers, garlands, necklaces, perfumes, and cosmetics. He abandons singing, dancing, acting, and going to see them, cuts off singing, dancing, acting, and going to see them. He purifies his mind from singing, dancing, acting, and going to see them. He abandons accepting precious things, cuts off accepting precious things. He purifies his mind from accepting precious things. He abandons eating after the proper time, cuts off eating after the proper time, eats one meal a day, does not eat at night, and eats at the proper time. He purifies his mind from eating after the proper time.

「彼离酒、断酒，彼于饮酒净除其心。彼离高广大床、断高广大床，彼于高广大床净除其心。彼离华鬘、璎珞、涂香、脂粉，断花鬘、璎珞、涂香、脂粉，彼于华鬘、璎珞、涂香、脂粉净除其心。彼离歌舞、倡妓及往观听，断歌舞、倡妓及往观听，彼于歌舞、倡妓及往观听净除其心。彼离受生色、像宝，断受生色、像宝，彼于受生色、像宝净除其心。彼离过中食，断过中食，一食、不夜食、学时食，彼于过中食净除其心。

Having accomplished this aggregate of noble morality, he also practices extreme contentment, taking robes to cover his body and food to fill his stomach. Wherever he goes, he carries his robes and bowl, walking without attachment, like a bird with its two wings flying through the air. Having accomplished this aggregate of noble morality and extreme contentment, he also guards the sense faculties, always mindful of closing them off, with mindfulness and clear comprehension, guarding the mind with mindfulness, and always giving rise to right knowledge. If the eye sees forms, he does not grasp at them, nor does he savor the forms, because of anger and conflict, he guards the eye faculty. In the mind, he does not give rise to greed, sorrow, evil and unwholesome dharmas, because of heading towards them, he guards the eye faculty. In the same way, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and if the mind knows dharmas, he does not grasp at them, nor does he savor the dharmas, because of anger and conflict, he guards the mind faculty. In the mind, he does not give rise to greed, sorrow, evil and unwholesome dharmas, because of heading towards them, he guards the mind faculty.

「彼已成就此圣戒聚，复行极知足，衣取覆形，食取充躯，随所游至，与衣钵俱，行无顾恋，犹如鹰鸟，与两翅俱，飞翔空中。彼已成就此圣戒聚，及极知足，复守护诸根，常念闭塞，念欲明达，守护念心而得成就，恒起正知。若眼见色，然不受想，亦不味色，谓忿诤故，守护眼根。心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，趣向彼故，守护眼根。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，若意知法，然不受想，亦不味法，谓忿诤故，守护意根。心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，趣向彼故，守护意根。

Having accomplished this aggregate of noble morality and extreme contentment, and guarding the sense faculties, he also knows correctly when entering and leaving, and observes and distinguishes bending, stretching, looking up and down, deportment, putting on the saṃghāṭī robe and other robes and bowls, walking, standing, sitting, lying down, sleeping, waking, speaking, and being silent. Having accomplished this aggregate of noble morality and extreme contentment, and guarding the sense faculties, and knowing correctly when entering and leaving, he also dwells alone in seclusion, in a place free from affairs, either under a tree, in an empty, peaceful place, a mountain cave, a stone chamber, an open space, a pile of straw, or in a forest, or in a charnel ground. Having gone to a place free from affairs, or under a tree, in an empty, peaceful place, he spreads out his sitting mat, sits cross-legged, straightens his body, sets his mind straight, turns his mind back, eliminates greed and craving, has no disputes in his mind, and when seeing the wealth and living requisites of others, does not give rise to greed and craving, wishing to obtain them. He purifies his mind from greed and craving.

「彼已成就此圣戒聚，及极知足，守护诸根，复正知出入，善观分别，屈伸低仰，仪容庠序，善著僧伽梨及诸衣钵，行住坐卧，眠寤语默，皆正知之。彼已成就此圣戒聚，及极知足，守护诸根，正知出入，复独住远离，在无事处，或至树下空安靖处、山岩石室、露地穰积，或至林中，或在冢间。彼已在无事处，或至树下空安静处，敷尼师檀，结加趺坐，正身正愿，返念不向，断除贪伺，心无有诤，见他财物诸生活具，不起贪伺，欲令我得，彼于贪伺净除其心。

In the same way, he eliminates anger, sloth and torpor, restlessness and remorse, doubt, and transcends perplexity. Regarding wholesome dharmas, he has no hesitation. He purifies his mind from perplexity. He eliminates these five hindrances, which defile the mind and weaken wisdom. He is free from desire, free from evil and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, and attains the joy and bliss born of seclusion, and dwells in the first dhyāna. This, brāhmaṇa, is called the Tathāgata's bending, the Tathāgata's practice, the Tathāgata's robe. But he does not consider that to be enough. The World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma is good, and the Tathāgata's disciples, the noble assembly, are good. He then stops initial and sustained thought, internally pacifies, unifies the mind, without initial and sustained thought, with concentration born of joy and bliss, attains the second dhyāna, and dwells in it. This, brāhmaṇa, is called the Tathāgata's bending, the Tathāgata's practice, the Tathāgata's robe. But he does not consider that to be enough. The World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One,

「如是瞋恚、睡眠、调悔、断疑、度惑，于诸善法，无有犹豫，彼于疑惑净除其心。彼断此五盖、心秽、慧羸，离欲、离恶不善之法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，逮初禅成就游。梵志！是谓如来所屈，如来所行，如来所服。然彼不以此为讫，世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。彼觉观已息，内靖、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，逮第二禅成就游。梵志！是谓如来所屈，如来所行，如来所服。然彼不以此为讫，世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。」

He then, free from desire, free from evil and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, and with joy and bliss born of seclusion, attains the first dhyāna, and dwells in it. This, brāhmaṇa, is called the Tathāgata's bending, the Tathāgata's practice, the Tathāgata's robe. But he does not consider that to be enough. The World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma is good, and the Tathāgata's disciples, the noble assembly, are good. He then, free from desire, free from evil and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, and with joy and bliss born of seclusion, attains the first dhyāna, and dwells in it. This, brāhmaṇa, is called the Tathāgata's bending, the Tathāgata's practice, the Tathāgata's robe. But he does not consider that to be enough. The World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One,

「彼离喜欲，舍无求游，正念正智而身觉乐，谓圣所说、圣所舍、念、乐住、空，逮第三禅成就游。梵志！是谓如来所屈，如来所行，如来所服。然彼不以此为讫，世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。彼乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍念、清净，逮第四禅成就游。梵志！是谓如来所屈，如来所行，如来所服。然彼不以此为讫，世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。」

"He then attains the knowledge and insight into the destruction of the taints. He knows as it really is: 'This is suffering.' He knows as it really is: 'This is the arising of suffering.' He knows as it really is: 'This is the cessation of suffering.' He knows as it really is: 'This is the path to the cessation of suffering.' He knows as it really is: 'These are the taints.' He knows as it really is: 'This is the arising of the taints.' He knows as it really is: 'This is the cessation of the taints.' He knows as it really is: 'This is the path to the cessation of the taints.' Knowing and seeing thus, his mind is liberated from the taint of sensual desire, from the taint of existence, and from the taint of ignorance. When it is liberated, he knows it is liberated. He knows: 'Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this to come.' This, brāhmaṇa, is called the Tathāgata's bending, the Tathāgata's practice, the Tathāgata's robe. He considers that to be enough. The World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma is good, and the Tathāgata's disciples, the noble assembly, are good. Brāhmaṇa, what do you think? Is this simile of the elephant's footprint well-made and complete?"

「彼已得如是定心，清净无秽，无烦柔软，善住得不动心，趣向漏尽智通作证。彼知此苦如真，知此苦习，知此苦灭，知此苦灭道如真。知此漏如真，知此漏习，知此漏灭，知此漏灭道如真。彼如是知、如是见，欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱，解脱已，便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。梵志！是谓如来所屈，如来所行，如来所服。彼以此为讫，世尊。如来。无所著。等正觉，世尊所说法善，如来弟子圣众善趣。梵志！于意云何？如是象迹喻善作具足耶？」

"Yes, Gotama. This simile of the elephant's footprint is well-made and complete."

生闻梵志答曰：「唯然。瞿昙！如是象迹喻善作具足。」

"World-Honored One, I have understood. Well-Gone One, I have understood. World-Honored One, I now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the community of monks. May the World-Honored One accept me as a lay follower. From this day forward, for as long as I live, I take refuge and go for protection to the Buddha, the Dharma, and the community of monks."

生闻梵志白曰：「世尊！我已知。善逝！我已解。世尊！我今自归于佛、法及比丘众，唯愿世尊受我为优婆塞，从今日始，终身自归，乃至命尽。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The brahmin Janavasabha and the wanderer Pūraṇa heard what the Buddha said, and they rejoiced and practiced it.

佛说如是。生闻梵志及卑卢异学，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
