MĀ 152

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 12 梵志品

152. （一五二）鹦鹉经

Parallel to: MN 99 / MN 135 (~) / MĀ 170 (~)

parrot, sukhāvati, brahma-vihāra
sudatta, brahma-pāla, yellow
reeds, head-na, agalavahana garden-na, brahma

鹦鹉、须闲提　　婆罗婆游堂须达、梵波罗　　黄芦园、头那阿伽罗诃那　　阿园那、梵摩

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time the Buddha was staying in the Bamboo Grove, in the Kalandaka Park, near Rājagṛha.

一时，佛游王舍城，在竹林加兰哆园。

At that time, the young Parrots, the son of the householder, was traveling to Rājagṛha and stayed overnight at a householder's house. Then the young Parrots asked the householder where he was staying, "Is there a śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who is the leader of the assembly, the teacher of the great assembly, respected by people, whom I can visit from time to time to pay homage, perhaps I can be joyful by paying homage to him?"

尔时，鹦鹉摩纳都题子少有所为，往至王舍城，寄宿居士家。于是，鹦鹉摩纳都题子问所寄宿居士曰：「颇有沙门、梵志宗主众师，统领大众，为人所尊，令我随时往见奉敬，傥能因此敬奉之时，得欢喜耶？」

The householder replied, "Yes, there is. Beloved of Heaven! The śramaṇa Gautama, the son of the Śākya clan, abandoned his Śākya family, shaved his beard and hair, put on the dyed robe, with utmost faith left home for homelessness, and practiced the path. He has realized supreme perfect enlightenment. Beloved of Heaven! You can visit him from time to time, go to pay homage to him, perhaps you can be joyful by paying homage to him."

居士答曰：「有也。天爱！沙门瞿昙释种子舍释宗族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道，觉无上正尽觉。天爱！自可随时往见，诣彼奉敬，或能因此奉敬之时，心得欢喜。」

The young Parrots then asked again, "Where is the śramaṇa Gautama now? I wish to see him."

鹦鹉摩纳即复问曰：「沙门瞿昙今在何处？我欲见之。」

The householder replied, "The śramaṇa Gautama is now in the Bamboo Grove Kalandakanivāpa in the city of Rājagṛha. You can go and see him."

居士答曰：「沙门瞿昙在此王舍城竹林加兰哆园，便可往见。」

Then the young Parrots left the householder's house where he was staying and went to the Bamboo Grove Kalandakanivāpa. The young Parrots saw from afar the World-Honored One in the grove between the trees, upright and beautiful, like the moon among the stars, shining brightly, dazzling like a golden mountain, with perfect marks and majestic spiritual power, with calm faculties and no obstructions, accomplished in taming and pacifying the mind, and silent. Having seen him, he went forward to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings, sat to one side, and said, "Gautama! I wish to ask something. Please listen before I speak."

于是，鹦鹉摩纳从所寄宿居士家出，往诣竹林加兰哆园。鹦鹉摩纳遥见世尊在树林间，端政姝好，犹星中月，光耀炜晔，晃若金山，相好具足，威神巍巍，诸根寂定，无有蔽碍，成就调御，息心静默。见已，便前往诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面，白曰：「瞿昙！欲有所问，听乃敢陈？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ask as you please."

世尊告曰：「恣汝所问。」

The young Parrots asked, "Gautama! As I have heard, if one is a householder, one can attain good understanding and know the Dharma. It is not so for those who leave home to study the path. I ask Gautama about this matter."

鹦鹉摩纳问曰：「瞿昙！如我所闻，若在家者，便得善解，则知如法，出家学道者，则不然也。我问瞿昙此事云何？」

The World-Honored One said, "This matter is not fixed."

世尊告曰：「此事不定。」

The young Parrots said, "Gautama! Please now explain this matter to me."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！愿今为我分别此事。」

The World-Honored One said, "Young man! Listen carefully and think well about it, I will explain it to you in detail." The young Parrots listened to the instruction.

世尊告曰：「摩纳！谛听，善思念之，我当为汝具分别说。」鹦鹉摩纳受教而听。

The Buddha said, "Young man! If there are those who practice wrongly, whether lay or renunciant, I do not praise them. Why is that? If there are those who practice wrongly, whether lay or renunciant, they will not attain good understanding and will not know the Dharma. Therefore, young man, if there are those who practice wrongly, whether lay or renunciant, I do not praise them. Young man, if there are those who practice rightly, whether lay or renunciant, I praise them. Why is that? If there are those who practice rightly, whether lay or renunciant, they will attain good understanding and will know the Dharma. Therefore, young man, if there are those who practice rightly, whether lay or renunciant, I praise them. Young man, I speak thus, speaking of these two dharmas, distinguishing them thus, revealing them thus. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who are powerful, firm, and deeply committed, and who speak of this as true and the rest as false,

佛言：「摩纳！若有在家及出家学道行邪行者，我不称彼。所以者何？若有在家及出家学道行邪行者，不得善解，不知如法。是故，摩纳！若有在家及出家学道行邪行者，我不称彼。摩纳！若有在家及出家学道行正行者，我称说彼。所以者何？若有在家及出家学道行正行者，必得善解，则知如法。是故，摩纳！若有在家及出家学道行正行者，我称说彼。摩纳！我如是说，说此二法，如是分别，如是显示。若有沙门梵志有力坚固深入，一向专著，而说此为真谛，余者虚妄。」

The young Parrots said, "Gautama! As I understand it, if one is a householder, there are great benefits and great merits, but it is not so for those who renounce the householder life and study the path. I ask Gautama about this matter.

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！如我所闻，若在家者，便有大利，有大功德，出家学道者，则不然也。我问瞿昙此事云何？」

The World-Honored One said, "This is not certain.

世尊告曰：「此事不定。」

The young Parrots said, "Gautama! Please explain this matter to me again.

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！愿复为我分别此事。」

The World-Honored One said, "Young man! Listen carefully and think about it well, I will explain it to you in detail." The young Parrots listened.

世尊告曰：「摩纳！谛听，善思念之，我当为汝具分别说。」鹦鹉摩纳受教而听。

The Buddha said, "Young man! For those who live at home, there are great calamities, great disputes, and great hatred. Those who engage in wrong practices do not attain great fruits or great merits. It is like farming, where there are great calamities, great disputes, and great hatred. Those who engage in wrong practices do not attain great fruits or great merits. In the same way, young man, it is also like this for those who live at home. Young man! For those who renounce the householder life and practice the path, there are few calamities, few disputes, and few hatreds. Those who engage in wrong practices do not attain great fruits or great merits. It is like commerce, where there are few calamities, few disputes, and few hatreds. Those who engage in wrong practices do not attain great fruits or great merits. In the same way, young man, it is also like this for those who renounce the householder life and practice the path.

佛言：「摩纳！若在家者，有大灾患，有大鬪诤，有大怨憎，行邪行者，不得大果，无大功德。犹如田作，有大灾患，有大鬪诤，有大怨憎，行邪行者，不得大果，无大功德。如是，摩纳！若在家者亦复如是。摩纳！出家学道少有灾患，少有鬪诤，少有怨憎，行邪行者，不得大果，无大功德。犹如治生，少有灾患，少有鬪诤，少有怨憎，行邪行者，不得大果，无大功德。如是，摩纳！出家学道亦复如是。

Young man! For those who live at home, there are great calamities, great disputes, and great hatred. Those who engage in right practices attain great fruits and great merits. It is like farming, where there are great calamities, great disputes, and great hatred. Those who engage in right practices attain great fruits and great merits. In the same way, young man, it is also like this for those who live at home. Young man! For those who renounce the householder life and practice the path, there are few calamities, few disputes, and few hatreds. Those who engage in right practices attain great fruits and great merits. It is like commerce, where there are few calamities, few disputes, and few hatreds. Those who engage in right practices attain great fruits and great merits. In the same way, young man, it is also like this for those who renounce the householder life and practice the path.

「摩纳！若在家者，有大灾患，有大鬪诤，有大怨憎，行正行者，得大果报，有大功德。犹如田作，有大灾患，有大鬪诤，有大怨憎，行正行者，得大果报，有大功德。如是，摩纳！若在家者亦复如是。摩纳！出家学道少有灾患，少有鬪诤，少有怨憎，行正行者，得大果报，有大功德。犹如治生，少有灾患，少有鬪诤，少有怨憎，行正行者，得大果报，有大功德。如是，摩纳！出家学道亦复如是。摩纳！我如是说，说此二法，如是分别，如是显示。若有沙门、梵志有力坚固深入，一向专著，而说此为真谛，余者虚妄。」

The parrot-keeper said, "Gautama! Those brāhmaṇas establish five dharmas that have great fruits and great merits, and create merit and goodness."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！彼诸梵志施设五法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善。」

The World-Honored One said, "If those brāhmaṇas establish five dharmas that have great fruits and great merits, and create merit and goodness, can you speak about them here in this assembly?"

世尊告曰：「若诸梵志施设五法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善。汝在此众，今可说耶？」

The parrot-keeper said, "Gautama! I have nothing to hide. Why is that? Gautama! I am now sitting in this assembly."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！我无不可。所以者何？瞿昙！于今现坐此众。」

The World-Honored One said, "You may speak."

世尊告曰：「汝便可说。」

The parrot-keeper said, "Gautama! Listen well. Gautama! The Brahmins establish the first true Dharma, which has great fruit and great merit, and by doing good deeds one attains goodness; the second is recitation and practice, the third is intense practice, and the fourth is ascetic practice. Gautama! The Brahmins establish the fifth Brahma practice, which has great fruit and great merit, and by doing good deeds one attains goodness."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！善听。瞿昙！梵志施设第一真谛法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善；第二诵习，第三热行，第四苦行。瞿昙！梵志施设第五梵行，有大果报，有大功德作福得善。」

The World-Honored One said, "If there are Brahmins who establish the five dharmas, which have great fruit and great merit, and by doing good deeds one attains goodness, is there any Brahmin who says, 'I know and realize these five dharmas by myself in this present life, and having realized them myself, I establish the fruit'?"

世尊告曰：「若有梵志施设五法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善。彼梵志中颇有一梵志作如是说：『我此五法，于现法中自知自觉，自作证已，施设果耶？』」

The parrot-keeper said to the World-Honored One, "No, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳白世尊曰：「无也。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said, "Is there any teacher or ancestral teacher, up to the seventh generation of parents, who says, 'I know and realize these five dharmas by myself in this present life, and having realized them myself, I establish the fruit'?"

世尊告曰：「颇有师及祖师，至七世父母，作如是说：『我此五法，于现法中自知自觉，自作证已，施设果耶？』」

The parrot-keeper said to the World-Honored One, "No, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳白世尊曰：「无也。瞿昙！」

At that time, the World-Honored One asked, "Monastic, if in the past there were Brahmins who died, reciting and upholding the scriptures, spreading the scriptures, reciting and studying the sutras, namely, Yatha, Bhama, Bhamadeva, Visamitra, Yabhatagani, Angarava, Vasishtha, Kashyapa, Brahmana, and Bhara. The present Brahmins are those who fully recite, study, and uphold the scriptures. Do they say, 'I know and realize these five dharmas by myself in this present life, and having realized them myself, I establish the fruit'?"

尔时，世尊问曰：「摩纳！若昔有梵志寿终命过，诵持经书，流布经书，诵习典经：一曰夜咤，二曰婆摩，三曰婆摩提婆，四曰毗奢蜜哆罗，五曰夜婆陀揵尼，六曰应疑罗婆，七曰婆私咤，八曰迦叶，九曰婆罗婆，十曰婆惒。谓今诸梵志即彼具经诵习持学，彼颇作是说：『我此五法，于现法中自知自觉，自作证已，施设果耶？』」

The parrot-keeper said to the World-Honored One, "No, Gautama! But the Brahmins uphold it out of faith."

鹦鹉摩纳白世尊曰：「无也。瞿昙！但诸梵志因信受持。」

The World-Honored One said, "If among the Brahmins, there is not a single Brahmin who says, 'I know and realize these five dharmas by myself in this present life, and having realized them myself, I establish the fruit.' There is also no teacher or ancestral teacher, up to the parents of the seventh generation, who says, 'I know and realize these five dharmas by myself in this present life, and having realized them myself, I establish the fruit.' If in the past there were Brahmins who died, reciting and upholding the scriptures, spreading the scriptures, reciting and studying the sutras, namely, Yatha, Bhama, Bhamadeva, Visamitra, Yabhatagani, Angarava, Vasishtha, Kashyapa, Brahmana, and Bhara. The present Brahmins are those who fully recite, study, and uphold the scriptures. They do not say, 'I know and realize these five dharmas by myself in this present life, and having realized them myself, I establish the fruit.' Monastic! Do the Brahmins not have a basis for their faith in this?"

世尊告曰：「若于诸梵志，无一梵志而作是说：『我此五法，于现法中自知自觉，自作证已，施设果报。』亦无师及祖师，乃至七世父母，而作是说：『我此五法，于现法中自知自觉，自作证已，施设果报。』若昔有梵志寿终命过，诵持经书，流布经书，诵习典经：一曰夜咤，二曰婆摩，三曰婆摩提婆，四曰毗奢蜜哆逻，五曰夜婆陀揵尼，六曰应疑罗婆，七曰婆私咤，八曰迦叶，九曰婆罗婆，十曰婆惒。谓今诸梵志即彼具经诵习持学，彼无作是说：『我此五法，于现法中自知自觉，自作证已，施设果报。』摩纳！彼诸梵志不以此故，于信向中无根本耶？」

The parrot-keeper said, "Gautama! There is indeed no basis, but the Brahmins have heard and upheld it."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！实无根本，但诸梵志闻已受持。」

The World-Honored One said, "It is like a group of blind people supporting each other. The one in front does not see the one behind, nor does he see the one in the middle. The one in the middle does not see the one in front, nor does he see the one behind. The one behind does not see the one in the middle, nor does he see the one in front. Monastic! The Brahmins you speak of are also like this. Monastic! You first said to believe, and then you said to hear."

世尊告曰：「犹众盲儿各相扶持，彼在前者，不见于后，亦不见中。彼在中者，不见于前，亦不见后。彼在后者，不见于中，亦不见前。摩纳！所说诸梵志辈亦复如是。摩纳！前说信而后复说闻。」

The parrot-keeper was angry with the World-Honored One, resentful and displeased, slandering the World-Honored One, pointing at the World-Honored One, cursing the World-Honored One, saying, "You should slander Gautama! You should point at Gautama! You should fall down on Gautama!" He said to the World-Honored One, "There is a Brahmin named Phrakkasapa, of pure and upright lineage. He says, 'If there are any Shakyamuni Brahmins who claim to know and see the Dharma above humans, I have heard this and laugh at it. I do not agree. It is false and not true, and not in accordance with the Dharma. How can a person born among humans claim to have attained the Dharma above humans? If one says that one knows and sees the Dharma above humans, this is not so.'"

鹦鹉摩纳瞋恚世尊，憎嫉不悦，诽谤世尊，指擿世尊，骂詈世尊：「应诽谤瞿昙！应指瞿昙！应堕瞿昙！」语世尊曰：「有一梵志，名弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，彼作是说：『若有沙门、梵志，于人上法有知有见，现我得者。我闻是已，便大笑之，意不相可，虚妄不真，亦不如法。云何人生人中，自说得人上法？若于人上法言我知我见者，此事不然。』」

Then the World-Honored One thought, "The parrot-keeper Doutui is angry with me, resentful and displeased, slandering me, pointing at me, and cursing me, saying, 'You should slander Gautama! You should point at Gautama! You should fall down on Gautama!' He said to me, 'Gautama! There is a Brahmin named Phrakkasapa, of pure and upright lineage. He says, "If there are any Shakyamuni Brahmins who claim to know and see the Dharma above humans, I have heard this and laugh at it. I do not agree. It is false and not true, and not in accordance with the Dharma. How can a person born among humans claim to have attained the Dharma above humans? If one says that one knows and sees the Dharma above humans, this is not so."'

于是，世尊便作是念：「鹦鹉摩纳都题子瞋恚于我，憎嫉不悦，诽谤于我，指擿于我，骂詈于我：『应诽谤，瞿昙！应指，瞿昙！应堕，瞿昙！』而语我曰：『瞿昙！有梵志名弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，彼作是说：「若有沙门、梵志，于人上法有知有见，现我得者。我闻是已，便大笑之，意不相可，虚妄不真，亦不如法。云何人生人中，自说得人上法？若于人上法言我知我见者，此事不然。」』」

The World-Honored One, knowing this, said, "Monastics! The Brahmin Phrakkasapa, of pure and upright lineage, knows the thoughts of all Shakyamuni Brahmins. Then he says, 'If there are any Shakyamuni Brahmins who claim to know and see the Dharma above humans, I have heard this and laugh at it. I do not agree. It is false and not true, and not in accordance with the Dharma. How can a person born among humans claim to have attained the Dharma above humans? If one says that one knows and sees the Dharma above humans, this is not so.'"

世尊知已，告曰：「摩纳！梵志弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，彼知一切沙门、梵志心之所念，然后作是说：『若有沙门、梵志，于人上法有知有见，现我得者。我闻是已，便大笑之，意不相可，虚妄不真，亦不如法。云何人生人中，自说得人上法？若于人上法言我知我见者，此事不然耶？』」

The parrot-keeper replied, "Gautama! The Brahmin Phrakkasapa, of pure and upright lineage, has a maid named Buni. He cannot even know the thoughts in her mind, let alone the thoughts of all Shakyamuni Brahmins. If he could know them, it would be impossible."

鹦鹉摩纳答曰：「瞿昙！梵志弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，自有一婢，名曰不尼，尚不能知心之所念，况复欲知一切沙门、梵志心之所念耶？若使知者，终无是处。」

The World-Honored One said, "It is like a person born blind saying, 'There is no black or white, nor one who sees black or white. There is no good or bad color, nor one who sees good or bad color. There is no long or short color, nor one who sees long or short color. There is no near or far color, nor one who sees near or far color. There is no coarse or fine color, nor one who sees coarse or fine color. I have never seen or known it, therefore there is no color.' Does the blind person's statement hold true?"

世尊告曰：「犹人生盲，彼作是说：『无黑白色，亦无见黑白色者。无好恶色，亦无见好恶色。无长短色，亦无见长短色。无近远色，亦无见近远色。无麤细色，亦无见麤细色。我初不见不知，是故无色。』彼生盲人作如是说，为真实耶？」

The parrot-keeper replied to the World-Honored One, "No, Gautama! Why is that? There is black and white, and one who sees black and white. There is good and bad color, and one who sees good and bad color. There is long and short color, and one who sees long and short color. There is near and far color, and one who sees near and far color. There is coarse and fine color, and one who sees coarse and fine color. If one says, 'I have never seen or known it, therefore there is no color,' then the blind person's statement is not true."

鹦鹉摩纳答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！所以者何？有黑白色，亦有见黑白色者。有好恶色，亦有见好恶色。有长短色，亦有见长短色。有近远色，亦有见近远色。有麤细色，亦有见麤细色。若言我初不见不知，是故无色。彼生盲人作是说者，为不真实。」

"Monks, is the brahmin Faju's statement not like that of a blind person without eyes?"

「摩纳！梵志弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，彼所说者，非如生盲无目人耶？」

The parrot-keeper replied to the World-Honored One, "Like a blind person, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳答世尊曰：「如盲。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said, "Monks, what do you think? If there were brahmins in the past who passed away, reciting and upholding the scriptures, spreading the scriptures, reciting and studying the sacred texts, such as the brahmin Sangakya, the brahmin Shengwen, the brahmin Faju, and your father Dutui, is what they said true or false, is there truth or falsehood, is there high or low?"

世尊告曰：「摩纳！于意云何？若昔有梵志寿终命过，诵持经书，流布经书，诵习典经，谓商伽梵志、生闻梵志、弗袈裟裟罗梵志及汝父都题，若彼所说，可不可、有真无真、有高有下耶？」

The parrot-keeper replied to the World-Honored One, "If there were brahmins in the past who passed away, reciting and upholding the scriptures, spreading the scriptures, reciting and studying the sacred texts, such as the brahmin Sangakya, the brahmin Shengwen, the brahmin Faju, and my father Dutui, as for what they said, in my opinion, I want it to be acceptable, not unacceptable, I want it to be true, not untrue, I want it to be high, not low."

鹦鹉摩纳答世尊曰：「若昔有梵志寿终命过，诵持经书，流布经书，诵习典经，谓商伽梵志、生闻梵志、弗袈裟裟罗梵志及我父都题。彼所说者，于我意者，欲令可，莫令不可，欲令真，莫令不真，欲令高，莫令下。」

At that time, the World-Honored One asked, "Monks, what do you think? The brahmin Faju, whose family is pure and upright, is he not acceptable, is there no acceptability? Is he not true, is there no truth? Is he not high, is there no highness?"

彼时，世尊问曰：「摩纳！梵志弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，彼所说者，非为不可、无有可耶？非为不真、无有真耶？非为至下、无有高耶？」

The parrot-keeper replied to the World-Honored One, "Indeed, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳答世尊曰：「实尔。瞿昙！」

Furthermore, monks, there are five dharmas that create obstacles, create coverings, create blindness and lack of eyes, can extinguish wisdom, exhaust oneself in vain, and not attain nirvana. What are the five? Monks, the first dharma of desire creates obstacles, creates coverings, creates blindness and lack of eyes, can extinguish wisdom, exhaust oneself in vain, and not attain nirvana. Monks, anger, the view of the body, the grasping of precepts, and the fifth dharma of doubt create obstacles, create coverings, create blindness and lack of eyes, can extinguish wisdom, exhaust oneself in vain, and not attain nirvana. Monks, what do you think? If one is obstructed, covered, and entangled by these five dharmas, if one wishes to contemplate one's own benefit, the benefit of others, the benefit of both, and know the thoughts of all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, it is impossible. Monks, the brahmin Faju, whose family is pure and upright, is defiled by desire, defiled by desire, touched by desire, leaning on desire, attached to desire, and immersed in desire. He does not see the calamities and does not know the way out. He practices desire. He is obstructed, covered, and entangled by these five dharmas. If he wishes to contemplate his own benefit, the benefit of others, the benefit of both, and know the thoughts of all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, it is impossible.

「复次，摩纳！有五法作障碍，作覆盖，作盲无目，能灭智慧，唐自疲劳，不得涅槃。云何为五？摩纳！欲第一法作障碍，作覆盖，作盲无目，能灭智慧，唐自疲劳，不得涅槃。摩纳！恚、身见、戒取，疑第五法作障碍，作覆盖，作盲无目，能灭智慧，唐自疲劳，不得涅槃。摩纳！于意云何？为此五法之所障碍、覆盖、阴缠，彼若欲观自义，观他义，观俱义，及知一切沙门、梵志心之所念者，终无是处。摩纳！梵志弗袈裟裟罗，姓直清净化，为欲所染，欲所秽染，欲触欲猗，著于欲，入于欲中，不见灾患，不知出要而行于欲。彼为此五法之所障碍、覆盖、阴缠，彼若欲观自义，观他义，观俱义，及知一切沙门、梵志心之所念者，终无是处。

"Furthermore, monks, there are five kinds of sensual pleasures that are loved and cherished, associated with desire for form. What are the five? The eyes know forms, the ears know sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile objects. Monks, what do you think? Do sentient beings, because of these five kinds of sensual pleasures, give rise to joy and delight, and not go beyond them?"

「复次，摩纳！有五欲功德，爱念意乐，彼有爱色欲相应，甚可于乐。云何为五？目知色，耳知声，鼻知香，舌知味，身知触。摩纳！于意云何？众生因此五欲功德故，生乐生喜，不复是过耶？」

The parrot-like Mānava said to the World-Honored One, "Yes, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳白世尊曰：「如是。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One asked, "What do you think, Mānava? If one lights a fire using grass and wood, and if one lights a fire without using grass and wood, which light is the best, most excellent, and most supreme?"

世尊问曰：「摩纳！于意云何？若因草木而然火，及离草木而然火，何者光［火＊佥］最上、最妙、最胜耶？」

The parrot-like Mānava said, "Gautama! If one lights a fire without using grass and wood, it is impossible. Only with the power of the bases of spiritual power. Gautama! If one lights a fire without using grass and wood, that light is the best, most excellent, and most supreme."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！若离草木而然火者，终无是处，唯有如意足力。瞿昙！若离草木而然火者，彼光［火＊佥］最上、最妙、最胜。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is, so it is! Mānava! If one lights a fire without using grass and wood, it is impossible. Only with the power of the bases of spiritual power. If one lights a fire without using grass and wood, that light is the best, most excellent, and most supreme. I now speak hypothetically. Mānava! Just as one lights a fire using grass and wood, so the joy and pleasure that beings experience is due to desire and unwholesome factors, and they do not attain the pleasure of renunciation and tranquility. Mānava! Just as one lights a fire without using grass and wood, so the pleasure of renunciation that beings experience is due to the renunciation of desire and the attainment of wholesome factors, and they attain the pleasure of renunciation and tranquility."

世尊告曰：「如是，如是。摩纳！若离草木而然火者，终无是处，唯有如意足力。若离草木而然火者，彼光［火＊佥］最上、最妙、最胜，我今假说。摩纳！如因草木而然火者，如是众生所生喜乐，谓因欲、恶不善之法，不得舍乐及于止息。摩纳！如离草木而然火者，如是众生所生舍乐，谓因离欲，从诸善法而得舍乐及于止息。」

The World-Honored One said, "Mānava! What do you think? If a brahmin holds a ritual feast and makes offerings, and a kṣatriya youth comes from the east, he says, 'I should get the best seat, the best water, and the best food.' If he does not get the best seat, the best water, and the best food, he becomes resentful and jealous. If a brahmin youth comes from the south, he says, 'I should get the best food.' If he does not get the best food, he becomes resentful and jealous. If a householder youth comes from the west, he says, 'I should get the most abundant food.' If he does not get the most abundant food, he becomes resentful and jealous. If a craftsman youth comes from the north, he says, 'I should get the most satisfying food.' If he does not get the most satisfying food, he becomes resentful and jealous. Mānava! What kind of result do those brahmins get from making such offerings?"

世尊告曰：「摩纳！于意云何？有一梵志作斋行施，或从东方有刹利童子来，彼作是说：『我于其中得第一座、第一澡水、第一饮食。』彼于其中不得第一座、第一澡水、第一饮食，便生怨恨而怀憎嫉。或从南方有梵志童子来，彼作是说：『我于其中得净妙食。』彼于其中不得净妙食，便生怨恨而怀憎嫉。或从西方有居士童子来，彼作是说：『我于其中得丰饶食。』彼于其中不得丰饶食，便生怨恨而怀憎嫉。或从北方有工师童子来，彼作是说：『我于其中得丰足食。』彼于其中不得丰足食，便生怨恨而怀憎嫉。摩纳！彼诸梵志行如是施，施设何等报耶？」

The parrot Mānava said, "Gautama! Brahmins do not make offerings with such a mind, causing others to become resentful and jealous. Gautama! You should know that brahmins make offerings with a compassionate mind. Having made offerings with a compassionate mind, they attain great merit."

鹦鹉摩纳白曰：「瞿昙！梵志不如是心行于布施，使他生怨恨而怀憎嫉。瞿昙！当知梵志以愍伤心而行于施，以愍伤心而行施已，便得大福。」

The World-Honored One said, "Mānava! Do brahmins not establish a sixth teaching that has great results, great merit, and brings about merit and goodness?"

世尊告曰：「摩纳！梵志非为施设第六法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善耶？」

The parrot Mānava replied to the World-Honored One, "Yes, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳答世尊曰：「如是。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One asked, "Mānava! If there are five teachings established by brahmins that have great results, great merit, and bring about merit and goodness, in which of these do you see more, those who are householders or those who have gone forth to practice the path?"

世尊问曰：「摩纳！若有梵志施设五法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善，汝见此法多在何处，为在家耶？为出家学道耶？」

The parrot Mānava replied, "Gautama! If there are five teachings established by brahmins that have great results, great merit, and bring about merit and goodness, I see more of these in those who have gone forth to practice the path, not in householders. Why is that? Household life is full of many affairs, many things to do, many grudges, and many disputes. They cannot uphold sincerity and truthfulness. Gautama! The life of one who has gone forth to practice the path is free from many affairs, many things to do, many grudges, and many disputes. They can definitely uphold sincerity and truthfulness. Gautama! As for sincerity and truthfulness, I see more of this in those who have gone forth to practice the path, not in householders. Why is that? Household life is full of many affairs, many things to do, many grudges, and many disputes. They cannot practice giving, recitation, ascetic practices, or the holy life. Gautama! The life of one who has gone forth to practice the path is free from many affairs, many things to do, many grudges, and many disputes. They can practice giving, recitation, ascetic practices, and the holy life. Gautama! As for the holy life, I see more of this in those who have gone forth to practice the path, not in householders."

鹦鹉摩纳答曰：「瞿昙！若有梵志施设五法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善，我见此法多在出家学道，非在家也。所以者何？在家者多事，多有所作，多有结恨，多有憎诤，彼不能得守护诚谛。瞿昙！出家学道者少事，少有所作，少有结恨，少有憎诤，彼必能得守护诚谛。瞿昙！彼诚谛者，我见多在出家学道，非在家也。所以者何？在家者多事，多有所作，多有结恨，多有憎诤，彼不得行施，不得诵习，不得行苦行，不得行梵行。瞿昙！出家学道者少事，少有所作，少有结恨，少有憎诤，彼得行施，彼得诵习，得行苦行，得行梵行。瞿昙！行梵行者，我见此法多在出家学道，非在家也。」

The World-Honored One said, "Mānava! If there are five teachings established by brahmins that have great results, great merit, and bring about merit and goodness, I say that they arise from the mind. What is the mind? If the mind is free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, it is for the sake of cultivating that. Mānava! What do you think? If there is a bhikṣu who upholds sincerity and truthfulness, because of upholding sincerity and truthfulness, he attains joy and delight. Mānava! If there is joy and delight, associated with goodness and goodness, I say that it arises from the mind. What is the mind? If the mind is free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, it is for the sake of cultivating that. In this way, he can practice giving, recitation, ascetic practices, and the holy life. Because of practicing the holy life, he attains joy and delight. Mānava! If there is joy and delight, associated with goodness and goodness, I say that it arises from the mind. What is the mind? If the mind is free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, that mind is accompanied by loving-kindness, and pervades one direction, accomplishing and dwelling. Thus, two, three, four directions, the four intermediate directions, above and below, everywhere, the mind is accompanied by loving-kindness, free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, extremely vast and great, immeasurable, well-cultivated, pervading the entire world, accomplishing and dwelling. Thus, compassion and joy, the mind is accompanied by equanimity, free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, extremely vast and great, immeasurable, well-cultivated, pervading the entire world, accomplishing and dwelling.

世尊告曰：「摩纳！若有梵志施设五法，有大果报，有大功德，作福得善者，我说是从心起。云何为心？若心无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，为修彼故。摩纳！于意云何？若有比丘守护诚谛者，彼因守护诚谛故，得喜得悦。摩纳！若有喜及悦，善善相应，我说是从心起。云何为心？若心无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，为修彼故，如是彼得行施，彼得诵习，得行苦行，得行梵行。彼因行梵行故，得喜得悦。摩纳！若有喜及悦，善善相应，我说是从心起。云何为心？若心无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，彼心与慈俱，遍满一方成就游。如是二三四方、四维上下，普周一切，心与慈俱，无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，极广甚大，无量善修，遍满一切世间成就游。如是悲、喜心与舍俱，无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，极广甚大，无量善修，遍满一切世间成就游。

"Mānava! It is like a person who is skilled in blowing a conch. If there is a place where it has never been heard before, at midnight he climbs a high mountain and blows the conch with utmost strength, producing a subtle sound that fills the four directions. In this way, a bhikṣu, with a mind accompanied by loving-kindness, pervades one direction and accomplishes dwelling. Thus, two, three, four directions, the four intermediate directions, above and below, everywhere, with a mind accompanied by loving-kindness, free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, extremely vast and great, immeasurable, well-cultivated, pervading the entire world, accomplishing and dwelling. Thus, compassion and joy, the mind is accompanied by equanimity, free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, extremely vast and great, immeasurable, well-cultivated, pervading the entire world, accomplishing and dwelling. Mānava! What do you think? If there is someone who seeks the heavens and desires to be a heavenly being, they then engage in a mind associated with greed and desire, thinking, 'May I become a heavenly being and the others.' If there is someone who seeks the heavens and desires to be a heavenly being, they then become free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, immeasurable, extremely vast, well-cultivated, with a mind of concentration and understanding, pervading and accomplishing dwelling, thinking, 'May I become a heavenly being and the others.' Which of them do you think will become a heavenly being and the others?"

「摩纳！犹如有人善吹于螺，彼若有方未曾闻者，彼于夜半而登高山，极力吹螺，出微妙声，遍满四方。如是，比丘心与慈俱，遍满一方成就游。如是二三四方、四维上下，普周一切，心与慈俱，无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，极广甚大，无量善修，遍满一切世间成就游。如是悲、喜心与舍俱，无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，极广甚大，无量善修，遍满一切世间成就游。摩纳！于意云何？若有求天，要求天上故，便行贪伺相应心，令我作天及余天。若有求天，要求天上故，便无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，无量极广善修，心定意解，遍满成就游，令我作天及余天。汝观于彼，谁得作天及余天耶？」

The parrot Mānava replied, "Gautama! If this one who seeks the heavens and desires to be a heavenly being then becomes free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, immeasurable, extremely vast, well-cultivated, with a mind of concentration and understanding, pervading and accomplishing dwelling, thinking, 'May I become a heavenly being and the others,' I see that they will definitely become a heavenly being or another heavenly being.

鹦鹉摩纳答曰：「瞿昙！若此求天，要求天上故，便无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，无量极广善修，心定意解，遍满成就游者，我观于彼必得作天或余天也。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Mānava! What do you think? If there is someone who seeks the Brahma Heaven and desires to be a Brahma heavenly being, they then engage in a mind associated with greed and desire, thinking, 'May I become a Brahma heavenly being and the others.' If there is someone who seeks the Brahma Heaven and desires to be a Brahma heavenly being, they then become free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, immeasurable, extremely vast, well-cultivated, with a mind of concentration and understanding, pervading and accomplishing dwelling, thinking, 'May I become a Brahma heavenly being and the others.' Which of them do you think will become a Brahma heavenly being and the others?"

世尊问曰：「摩纳！于意云何？若有求梵天，要求梵天上故，便行贪伺相应心，令我作梵天及余梵天。若有求梵天，要求梵天上故，便无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，无量极广善修，心定意解，遍满成就游，令我作梵天及余梵天。汝观于彼，谁得作梵天及余梵天耶？」

The parrot Mānava replied, "Gautama! If this one who seeks the Brahma Heaven and desires to be a Brahma heavenly being then becomes free from attachment, resentment, anger, and disputes, immeasurable, extremely vast, well-cultivated, with a mind of concentration and understanding, pervading and accomplishing dwelling, thinking, 'May I become a Brahma heavenly being and the others,' I see that they will become a Brahma heavenly being or another Brahma heavenly being.

鹦鹉摩纳答曰：「瞿昙！若此求梵天，要求梵天上故，便无结、无怨、无恚、无诤，无量极广善修，心定意解，遍满成就游者，我观于彼得作梵天或余梵天。」

The parrot Mānava asked, "Gautama! Do you know the path to the Brahma Heaven?"

鹦鹉摩纳问曰：「瞿昙！知梵道迹耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Mānava! I will ask you, answer as you see fit. Mānava! What do you think? Is the village of Nalakagala not far from this assembly?"

世尊告曰：「摩纳！我今问汝，随所解答。摩纳！于意云何？那罗歌逻村去此众不远耶？」

The parrot Mānava replied, "Not far."

鹦鹉摩纳答曰：「不远。」

The World-Honored One said, "Mānava! What do you think? If you were to tell someone in this assembly, 'Go to that village of Nalakagala and return immediately upon arrival,' and they were to quickly go to the village of Nalakagala, arrive, and return immediately. After they had gone and returned, you asked about the road, referring to the going and coming in the village of Nalakagala. Would that person be unable to answer?"

世尊告曰：「摩纳！于意云何？汝于此众告一人曰：『汝往至彼那罗歌逻村，到便即还。』彼受汝教，速疾往至那罗歌逻村，到便即还。彼往返已，汝问道路，谓于那罗歌逻村往返出入事。彼人宁住不能答耶？」

The parrot Mānava replied to the World-Honored One, "No, Gautama!"

鹦鹉摩纳答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said, "Mānava! That person can stay and not be able to answer when asked about the road in the village of Nalakagala after going and returning. But if one asks the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One about the path of the Brahmā way, he will never stay and not be able to answer."

世尊告曰：「摩纳！彼人往返于那罗歌逻村，问道路事，乃可得住而不能答。若问如来、无所著、等正觉梵道迹者，终不暂住而不能答。」

The parrot Mānava said to the World-Honored One, "Śramaṇa Gautama! The shrine of the god Anāthapiṇḍada is fully endowed with this, namely, being able to quickly answer when asked about the path of the Brahmā way. World-Honored One! I have understood. Well-Gone One! I have understood. World-Honored One! I now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the community of monks. May the World-Honored One accept me as a lay follower, from this day forth until the end of my life, taking refuge in him with my whole being.

鹦鹉摩纳白世尊曰：「沙门瞿昙！无著天祠，此事具足，谓问梵道迹能速答故。世尊！我已知。善逝！我已解。世尊！我今自归于佛、法及比丘众，唯愿世尊受我为优婆塞，从今日始，终身自归，乃至命尽。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The parrot Mānava, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。鹦鹉摩纳闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
