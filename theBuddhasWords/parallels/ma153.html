MĀ 153

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 12 梵志品

153. （一五三）须闲提经

Parallel to: MN 75

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in Kuru and stayed in the first quiet room of the Brahmans, sitting on a grass seat.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦，在婆罗婆第一静室，坐于草座。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having passed the night, put on his robe and took his alms bowl in the morning, and entered the city of Kanyakubja to beg for food in order. After eating, he returned in the middle of the day, put away his robe and bowl, washed his hands and feet, put his sitting mat on his shoulder, and went to a certain forest to the place for daytime walking. At that time, the World-Honored One entered that forest, went to the foot of a tree, spread out his sitting mat, and sat cross-legged.

尔时，世尊过夜平旦，著衣持钵，入釰摩瑟昙次第乞食。食讫，中后还举衣钵，澡洗手足，以尼师檀著于肩上，往诣一林，至昼行处。尔时，世尊入于彼林，至一树下，敷尼师檀，结加趺坐。

Then, the non-Buddhist ascetic Udraka, wandering about in the middle of the day, went to the first quiet room of the Brahmans. The non-Buddhist ascetic Udraka saw from afar in the first quiet room of the Brahmans a grass seat with a place to lie on one side, like a lion's posture, like a śramaṇa's posture, like a brahman's posture. Having seen it, he asked: "In the first quiet room of the Brahmans, who has this grass seat with a place to lie on one side, like a lion's posture, like a śramaṇa's posture, like a brahman's posture?"

于是，须闲提异学中后彷徉，往诣婆罗婆第一静室，须闲提异学遥见婆罗婆第一静室有布草座一脇卧处，似师子卧，似沙门卧，似梵行卧，须闲提异学见已，问曰：「婆罗婆第一静室，谁有此草座一脇卧处，似师子卧，似沙门卧，似梵行卧？」

The Brahman Udraka replied: "Udraka, there is a śramaṇa Gautama, a son of the Śākya clan, who has renounced his Śākya family, shaved off his hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, with true faith, left home for homelessness, and is practicing the path. He has attained supreme perfect enlightenment. In his first quiet room, there is this grass seat with a place to lie on one side, like a lion's posture, like a śramaṇa's posture, like a brahman's posture."

婆罗婆梵志答曰：「须闲提！有沙门瞿昙释种子，舍释宗族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信，舍家、无家、学道，觉无上正尽觉。彼第一静室有此草座一脇卧处，似师子卧，似沙门卧，似梵行卧。」

The non-Buddhist ascetic Udraka said: "Udraka, I cannot see what I have not seen, hear what I have not heard, namely that I have seen the place where the śramaṇa Gautama lies down. Why is that? The śramaṇa Gautama has destroyed the earth. One who has destroyed the earth is of no use."

须闲提异学语曰：「婆罗婆！我今不可见见，不可闻闻，谓我见沙门瞿昙卧处。所以者何？彼沙门瞿昙坏败地，坏败地者，无可用也。」

The Brahman Udraka said: "Udraka, you should not insult the śramaṇa Gautama for this matter. Why is that? The śramaṇa Gautama has much wisdom, the wisdom of a kṣatriya, a brāhman, a householder, and a śramaṇa. If one speaks of wisdom, all attain holy knowledge. Udraka, I want to speak to the śramaṇa Gautama about this matter. Is it possible?"

婆罗婆语曰：「须闲提！汝不应以此事骂彼沙门瞿昙。所以者何？彼沙门瞿昙多有慧，刹利慧、梵志慧、居士慧、沙门慧，若说慧者皆得圣智。须闲提！我欲以此义向彼沙门瞿昙说，为可尔不？」

Udraka said: "Brahman Udraka, if you want to speak, then do as you please. I have no objection. Brahman Udraka, if you see the śramaṇa Gautama, I will also speak this meaning to him. Why is that? The śramaṇa Gautama has destroyed the earth. One who has destroyed the earth is of no use."

须闲提语曰：「婆罗婆！若欲说者，则随汝意，我无所违。婆罗婆！若见沙门瞿昙者，我亦说此义。所以者何？彼沙门瞿昙败坏地，败坏地者，无可用也。」

At that time, the World-Honored One was in the daytime walking place. With his pure divine ear surpassing that of humans, he heard the Brahman Udraka and the non-Buddhist Udraka discussing this matter together. Having heard it, the World-Honored One then rose from his afternoon meditation and went to the Brahman Udraka's first quiet room. He spread out his sitting mat on the grass seat, crossed his legs, and sat down. The Brahman Udraka saw the World-Honored One from afar in the grove, upright and beautiful like the moon among the stars, shining brightly like a golden mountain, with all the marks and characteristics complete, majestic and majestic, with his faculties calm and collected, without any obstruction, accomplished in taming and subduing, with his mind at peace and silent. Having seen him, he approached the Buddha, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side.

尔时，世尊在昼行处，以净天耳出过于人，闻婆罗婆梵志与须闲提异学共论此事。世尊闻已，则于晡时从燕坐起，往诣婆罗婆梵志第一静室，于草座上敷尼师檀，结加趺坐。婆罗婆梵志遥见世尊在树林间，端政姝好，犹星中月，光耀炜晔，晃若金山，相好具足，威神巍巍，诸根寂定，无有蔽碍，成就调御，息心静默。见已，进前往诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面。

The World-Honored One asked: "Brahman Udraka, did you discuss this grass seat with the non-Buddhist Udraka?"

世尊问曰：「婆罗婆！与须闲提异学共论此草坐处耶？」

The Brahman Udraka replied to the World-Honored One: "Yes, Gautama! I also wanted to tell the Shakyamuni about this matter, but the Shakyamuni knew it himself before I could say it. Why is that? Because the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One knows it.

婆罗婆梵志答世尊曰：「如是。瞿昙！我亦欲以此事向沙门瞿昙说，然沙门瞿昙未说已自知。所以者何？以如来、无所著、等正觉故。」

The World-Honored One discussed this matter with the Brahman Udraka. Later, the non-Buddhist Udraka wandered and went to Udraka's first quiet room. The World-Honored One saw the non-Buddhist Udraka coming from afar and said: "Udraka! If one does not tame the eye faculty, does not guard it closely, and does not cultivate it, one will surely receive suffering as a result. If one tames the eye faculty well, guards it closely, and cultivates it well, one will surely receive happiness as a result. Udraka! Is this not the reason you say that the Shakyamuni has lost ground? To say that the Shakyamuni has lost ground means that he is not useful.

世尊与婆罗婆梵志共论此事。须闲提异学于后彷徉，往诣婆罗婆第一静室。世尊遥见须闲提异学来已，而作是说：「须闲提！不调御眼根，不密守护而不修者，必受苦报。彼于沙门瞿昙善自调御，善密守护而善修者，必得乐报。须闲提！汝因此故，说沙门瞿昙败坏地，败坏地者，无可用耶？」

The non-Buddhist Udraka replied to the World-Honored One: "Yes, Gautama!"

须闲提异学答世尊曰：「如是。瞿昙！」

Udraka! It is the same with the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind faculties. If one does not tame the mind faculty, does not guard it closely, and does not cultivate it, one will surely receive suffering as a result. If one tames the mind faculty well, guards it closely, and cultivates it well, one will surely receive happiness as a result. Udraka! Is this not the reason you say that the Shakyamuni has lost ground? To say that the Shakyamuni has lost ground means that he is not useful.

「须闲提！如是耳、鼻、舌、身根，不调御意根，不密守护而不修者，必受苦报。彼于沙门瞿昙善自调御，善密守护而善修者，必得乐报。须闲提！汝因此故，说沙门瞿昙败坏地，败坏地者，无可用耶？」

The non-Buddhist Udraka replied to the World-Honored One: "Yes, Gautama!"

须闲提异学答世尊曰：「如是。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One asked: "Udraka, what do you think? If a person has not yet renounced the householder life to practice the path, and their eyes perceive forms, they may have a mind of affection and delight, associated with desire. Later, they shave their hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, leave home out of faith, and practice the path as a homeless one. They perceive forms with their eyes, and see them as they truly are in terms of their arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and exit. Their minds dwell internally and they roam. If they see people who have not yet transcended desire for forms, who are consumed by desire for forms, and who are tormented by passion for forms, and if their eyes perceive forms, they may have a mind of affection and delight, associated with desire. When they see this, they do not approve of it, nor do they delight in it. Udraka, what do you think? If there is such pleasure, arising from affection and forms, and when experiencing that pleasure, one does not approve of it and does not delight in it because of its thinness, can one say anything about it?"

世尊问曰：「须闲提！于意云何？若人本未出家学道，彼眼知色，爱念意乐，可欲相应。彼于后时舍眼知色，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。彼眼知色，习、灭、味、患、出要见如真，内息心游行。彼若见人未离色欲，为色爱所食，为色热所热。彼眼知色，爱念意乐，可欲相应，行时见已，不称彼、不乐彼。须闲提！于意云何？若有此乐，因爱因色，乐此乐时，薄贱故不称彼，薄贱故不乐彼。须闲提！宁可于彼有所说耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama!"

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

"Udraka, what do you think? If a person has not yet renounced the householder life to practice the path, and their ears perceive sounds, nose perceives smells, tongue perceives tastes, and body perceives tactile objects, they may have a mind of affection and delight, associated with desire. Later, they shave their hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, leave home out of faith, and practice the path as a homeless one. They perceive tactile objects with their body, and see them as they truly are in terms of their arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and exit. Their minds dwell internally and they roam. If they see people who have not yet transcended desire for tactile objects, who are consumed by desire for tactile objects, and who are tormented by passion for tactile objects, and if their body perceives tactile objects, they may have a mind of affection and delight, associated with desire. When they see this, they do not approve of it, nor do they delight in it. Udraka, what do you think? If there is such pleasure, arising from affection and tactile objects, and when experiencing that pleasure, one does not approve of it and does not delight in it because of its thinness, can one say anything about it?"

「须闲提！于意云何？若人本未出家学道，如是耳知声，鼻知香，舌知味，身知触，爱念意乐，可欲相应。彼于后时，舍身知触，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。彼身知触，习、灭、味、患、出要，见如真，内息心游行。彼若见人未离触欲，为触爱所食，为触热所热。彼身知触，爱念意乐，可欲相应，行时见已，不称彼、不乐彼。须闲提！于意云何？若有见此乐，因爱因触，乐此乐时，薄贱故不称彼，薄贱故不乐彼。须闲提！宁可于彼有所说耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama!"

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

"Udraka, what do you think? If a person has not yet renounced the householder life to practice the path, and they have a mind of affection and delight associated with the five sense pleasures, later they shave their hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, leave home out of faith, and practice the path as a homeless one. They perceive the five sense pleasures with their body, and see them as they truly are in terms of their arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and exit. Their minds dwell internally and they roam. If they see people who have not yet transcended desire, who are consumed by desire, and who are tormented by passion for the five sense pleasures, and if they perceive the five sense pleasures with their body, they may have a mind of affection and delight, associated with desire. When they see this, they do not approve of it, nor do they delight in it. Udraka, what do you think? If there is such pleasure, arising from desire and desire for the five sense pleasures, and when experiencing that pleasure, one does not approve of it and does not delight in it because of its thinness, can one say anything about it?"

世尊问曰：「须闲提！于意云何？若人本未出家学道，五欲功德爱念意乐，可欲相应。彼于后时，舍五欲功德，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。彼五欲功德，习、灭、味、患、出要，见如真，内息心游行。彼若见人未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热，五欲功德爱念意乐，可欲相应，行时见已，不称彼、不乐彼。须闲提！于意云何？若有此乐，因欲因欲爱，乐此乐时，薄贱故不称彼，薄贱故不乐彼。须闲提！宁可于彼有所说耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama!"

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

"Udraka, when I had not yet renounced the householder life to practice the path, I obtained the five sense pleasures, which were easy to obtain and not difficult. I had a mind of affection and delight associated with them. Later, I shaved my hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, left home out of faith, and practiced the path as a homeless one. I perceived the five sense pleasures with my body, and saw them as they truly are in terms of their arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and exit. My mind dwelt internally and I roamed. When I saw people who had not yet transcended desire, who were consumed by desire, and who were tormented by passion for the five sense pleasures, and when I perceived the five sense pleasures with my body, I may have had a mind of affection and delight associated with them. When I saw this, I did not approve of it, nor did I delight in it. Udraka, what do you think? If there is such pleasure, arising from desire and desire for the five sense pleasures, and when experiencing that pleasure, one does not approve of it and does not delight in it because of its thinness, can one say anything about me?"

「须闲提！我本未出家学道时，得五欲功德，易不难得，爱念意乐，可欲相应。我于后时，舍五欲功德，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。彼五欲功德，习、灭、味、患、出要，见如真，内息心游行。我见人未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热，五欲功德，爱念意乐，可欲相应，行时见已，我不称彼，我不乐彼。须闲提！于意云何？若有此乐，因欲因欲爱，乐此乐时，薄贱故我不称彼，薄贱故我不乐彼。须闲提！宁可于我有所说耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama!"

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said: "Udraka, it is like a householder or householder's son who is extremely wealthy and happy, with immeasurable wealth, many livestock, fields, and food, and abundant provisions for living. He obtains the five sense pleasures easily and without difficulty. He practices good conduct with the body, speech, and mind. At the time of death, he does not give up the five sense pleasures with regret. When his body perishes and his life ends, he ascends to a good realm and is reborn in heaven, fully enjoying the five sense pleasures. Udraka, would these gods and god-sons give up the heavenly five sense pleasures and delight in human desires with joy and thoughtfulness?"

世尊告曰：「须闲提！犹如居士、居士子，极大富乐，资财无量，多诸畜牧、封户、食邑，诸生活具种种丰饶，彼得五欲，易不难得。彼成就身妙行，口、意妙行，临死之时，不乐舍五欲功德，身坏命终，后升善处，得生天上，具足行五欲功德。须闲提！此天及天子宁当舍天五欲功德，乐人间欲，欢喜念耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama! Why is that? Human desires are foul and impure, with a very filthy and evil mind that cannot be directed towards them. They are hateful, contentious, and extremely painful. Gautama, among human desires, heavenly desires are the best, most wonderful, and most supreme. If those gods and god-sons were to give up the heavenly five sense pleasures and delight in human desires with joy and thoughtfulness, it would never happen."

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！所以者何？人间欲者，臭处不净，意甚秽恶而不可向，憎诤极苦。瞿昙！于人间欲，天欲最上、最妙、最胜。若彼天及天子舍于天上五欲功德，乐人间欲，欢喜念者，终无是处。」

"So it is, Udraka! I have cut off human desires and transcended heavenly desires. I have shaved my head and beard, put on the saffron robes, left home out of true faith, and become a homeless ascetic. I have seen the arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and escape of those five sense pleasures as they truly are, and my mind has been internally pacified. I see people who are not free from desire, who are consumed by desire, who are tormented by desire, who love and delight in the five sense pleasures, who are associated with what is desirable. When I see them, I do not praise them, I do not delight in them. Udraka, what do you think? If there is such pleasure, caused by desire and desire-love, when one enjoys that pleasure, because it is inferior I do not praise it, because it is inferior I do not delight in it. Udraka, is there anything I can say about this?"

「如是。须闲提！我断人间欲，度于天欲，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。彼五欲功德，习、灭、味、患、出要，见如真，内息心游行。我见人未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热，五欲功德爱念意乐，可欲相应，行时见已，我不称彼，我不乐彼。须闲提！于意云何？若有此乐，因欲因欲爱，乐此乐时，薄贱故我不称彼，薄贱故我不乐彼。须闲提！宁可于我有所说耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama!"

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said: "Udraka, suppose a person has leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, scratching open the sores and holding them over a pit of fire. What do you think, Udraka? If a person with leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, scratches open the sores and holds them over a pit of fire, could they be cured of the disease, be strong, have all their faculties intact, be free from leprosy, and have a healthy body, returning to its original state?"

世尊告曰：「须闲提！犹人病癞，身体烂熟，为虫所食，爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。须闲提！于意云何？若病癞人身体烂熟，为虫所食，爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。如是宁得除病有力，不坏诸根，为脱癞病，身体完健，平复如故，还本所耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama! Why is that? If a person with leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, scratches open the sores and holds them over a pit of fire, the sores will grow more and more, and the original sores will become larger. They would rather take the leprosy as pleasure."

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！所以者何？若病癞人身体烂熟，为虫所食，爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。如是更生疮转增多，本疮转大，然彼反以癞疮为乐。」

"Udraka, it is like a person with leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, scratching open the sores and holding them over a pit of fire. In this way, the sores will grow more and more, and the original sores will become larger. They would rather take the leprosy as pleasure. Udraka, in this way, sentient beings who have not yet transcended desire are eaten by the desire-love, burned by the desire-heat, and engage in desire. Udraka, in this way, sentient beings who have not yet transcended desire are eaten by the desire-love, burned by the desire-heat, and engage in desire. In this way, desire will grow more and more, desire-love will become more extensive. They would rather take desire-love as pleasure. If they do not cut off desire, do not transcend desire-love, and engage in desire with an internal mind, it is impossible. Why is that? It is not reasonable to cut off desire, transcend desire-love, and engage in desire."

「须闲提！如病癞人身体烂熟，为虫所食爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。如是更生疮转增多，本疮转大，然彼反以癞疮为乐。须闲提！如是众生未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热而行于欲。须闲提！如是众生未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热而行于欲，如是欲转增多，欲爱转广，然彼反以欲爱为乐。彼若不断欲，不离欲爱，内息心，已行、当行、今行者，终无是处。所以者何？此非道理，断欲、离欲爱，谓行于欲。」

The World-Honored One said: "Udraka, it is like a king or a minister who obtains the five desires, which are not difficult to obtain. If they do not cut off desire, do not transcend desire-love, and engage in desire with an internal mind, it is impossible. Why is that? It is not reasonable to cut off desire, transcend desire-love, and engage in desire. In this way, Udraka, sentient beings who have not yet transcended desire are eaten by the desire-love, burned by the desire-heat, and engage in desire. Udraka, if sentient beings who have not yet transcended desire are eaten by the desire-love, burned by the desire-heat, and engage in desire, in this way, desire will grow more and more, desire-love will become more extensive. They would rather take desire-love as pleasure. If they do not cut off desire, do not transcend desire-love, and engage in desire with an internal mind, it is impossible. Why is that? It is not reasonable to cut off desire, transcend desire-love, and engage in desire.

世尊告曰：「须闲提！犹王及大臣，得五所欲，易不难得。彼若不断欲，不离欲爱，内息心，已行、当行、今行者，终无是处。所以者何？此非道理，断欲、离欲爱，谓行于欲。如是，须闲提！众生未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热而行于欲。须闲提！若众生未离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热而行欲者，如是欲转增多，欲爱转广，然彼反以欲爱为乐。彼若不断欲，不离欲爱，内息心，已行、当行、今行者，终无是处。所以者何？此非道理，断欲、离欲爱，谓行于欲。

"Udraka, it is like a person with leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, scratching open the sores and holding them over a pit of fire. There is a person who, out of compassion and pity, seeks benefit and well-being, seeks peace and happiness, and gives them appropriate good medicine. After giving them appropriate good medicine, they are cured of their illness, gain strength, and do not lose their faculties. They are freed from leprosy and their bodies are healed, returning to their original state. If they see a person with leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, scratching open the sores and holding them over a pit of fire. Udraka, what do you think? Would that person, upon seeing this, still be delighted and praise it?"

「须闲提！犹病癞人身体烂熟，为虫所食，爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。有人为彼怜念愍伤，求利及饶益，求安隐快乐，与如其像好药，与如其像好药已，除病得力，不坏诸根，已脱癞病，身体完健，平复如故，更还本所。彼若见人有癞病者，身体烂熟，为虫所食，以爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。须闲提！彼人见已，宁复意乐称誉喜耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama! Why is that? For one who is sick, medicine is needed, but for one who is not sick, it is not needed."

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！所以者何？有病须药，无病不须。」

"Udraka, what do you think? If that leper is cured of his illness, gains strength, does not lose his faculties, is freed from leprosy, and his body is healed, returning to its original state, and then two strong wrestlers grab him and hold him over a pit of fire. He panics and dodges, and his body becomes very hot. Udraka, what do you think? Is that pit of fire now hotter, more painful, and more unbearable than before?"

「须闲提！于意云何？若彼癞人除病得力，不坏诸根，已脱癞病，身体完健，平复如故，更还本所，有二力士，强捉彼人临火坑炙。彼于其中慞惶回避，身生重热。须闲提！于意云何？此火坑者，于今更热，大苦可患，甚于本耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One: "No, Gautama! The original leprosy, with the body rotting and being eaten by worms, with the sores being scratched open, and being held over a pit of fire, he had a great feeling of pleasure in suffering, his mind was confused, and he had inverted thoughts. Gautama! Now that person is cured of his illness, gains strength, does not lose his faculties, is freed from leprosy, his body is healed, returning to its original state, and he returns to his original place. He has a great feeling of suffering in suffering, his mind is at ease, and he has no inverted thoughts."

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！其本病癞，身体烂熟，为虫所食，爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。彼于苦大乐更乐想，其心迷乱，有颠倒想。瞿昙！彼人于今除病得力，不坏诸根，已脱癞病，身体完健，平复如故，更还本所，彼于苦大苦更乐想，其心泰然，无颠倒想。」

"Udraka, it is like a leper whose body is rotting, being eaten by worms, with the sores being scratched open, and being held over a pit of fire. He has a great feeling of pleasure in suffering, his mind is confused, and he has inverted thoughts. In this way, Udraka! Sentient beings are not free from desire, being eaten by the craving for desire, being heated by the heat of desire and engaging in desire. They have a feeling of pleasure in the desire for suffering, their minds are confused, and they have inverted thoughts. Udraka! It is like that person who is cured of his illness, gains strength, does not lose his faculties, is freed from leprosy, his body is healed, returning to its original state, and he returns to his original place. He has a great feeling of suffering in suffering, his mind is at ease, and he has no inverted thoughts. In this way, Udraka! I have a feeling of suffering in the desire for suffering, attaining the truth, and no inverted thoughts. Why is that? Udraka! Past desire is impure, stinking, and disgusting, with a very filthy mind that cannot be directed towards it, with the suffering of hatred and conflict. Future and present desire is also impure, stinking, and disgusting, with a very filthy mind that cannot be directed towards it, with the suffering of hatred and conflict. Udraka! The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One says that the first benefit is freedom from illness, and the first bliss is nirvāṇa."

「须闲提！如病癞人身体烂熟，为虫所食，爪擿疮开，临火坑炙。彼于苦大乐更乐想，其心迷乱，有颠倒想。如是，须闲提！众生不离欲，为欲爱所食，为欲热所热而行于欲。彼于苦欲有乐欲想，其心迷乱，有颠倒想。须闲提！犹如彼人除病得力，不坏诸根，已脱癞病，身体完健，平复如故，更还本所。彼于苦大苦更乐想，其心泰然，无颠倒想。如是，须闲提！我于苦欲有苦欲想，得如真实，无颠倒想。所以者何？须闲提！过去时欲不净臭处，意甚秽恶而不可向，憎诤苦更触，未来、现在欲亦不净臭处，意甚秽恶而不可向，憎诤苦更触。须闲提！如来、无所著、等正觉说无病第一利，涅槃第一乐。」

The non-Buddhist Udraka said to the World-Honored One: "Gautama! I have also heard from the elders, the venerable ones, the long-practiced ones, and those who have practiced the holy life for a long time, that freedom from illness is the first benefit, and nirvāṇa is the first bliss."

须闲提异学白世尊曰：「瞿昙！我亦曾从耆旧尊德长老久学梵行所，闻无病第一利，涅槃第一乐。」

The World-Honored One asked: "Udraka! If you have heard from the elders, the venerable ones, the long-practiced ones, and those who have practiced the holy life for a long time, that freedom from illness is the first benefit, and nirvāṇa is the first bliss. Udraka! What is freedom from illness? What is nirvāṇa?"

世尊问曰：「须闲提！若汝曾从耆旧尊德长老久学梵行所，闻无病第一利，涅槃第一乐。须闲提！何者无病？何者涅槃耶？」

Then the non-Buddhist Udraka, whose body was itself the illness, the boil, the arrow, the snake, the impermanent, the suffering, the empty, and the non-self, wiped his body with both hands and said: "Gautama! This is freedom from illness, this is nirvāṇa."

于是，须闲提异学身即是病、是痈、是箭、是蛇、是无常、是苦、是空、是非神，以两手抆摸而作是说：「瞿昙！此是无病，此是涅槃。」

The World-Honored One said: "Udraka! It is like a blind person who hears from a sighted person: 'White and pure, white and pure.' Having heard this, he seeks white and pure. A deceitful person, not seeking benefit and well-being for him, seeking peace and happiness, gives him a dirty and impure garment and says: 'You should know that this is a white and pure garment. You should respectfully receive it with both hands and wear it.' The blind person is delighted and respectfully receives it with both hands, wearing it and saying: 'White and pure, white and pure.' Udraka! Does that person speak from knowing himself, or from not knowing? Does he speak from seeing himself, or from not seeing?"

世尊语曰：「须闲提！犹如生盲，从有目人闻其所说：『白净无垢，白净无垢。』彼闻此已，便求白净。有谄诳人而不为彼求利及饶益，求安隐快乐，则以垢腻不净之衣，持往语曰：『汝当知之，此是白净无垢之衣，汝以两手敬受被身。』彼盲子喜，即以两手敬受被身而作是说：『白净无垢，白净无垢。』须闲提！彼人为自知说，为不知说？为自见说，为不见说？」

The non-Buddhist Udraka replied: "Gautama! If it is said like this, it is truly not knowing and seeing."

须闲提异学答曰：「瞿昙！如是说者，实不知见。」

The World-Honored One said: "So it is, Udraka! It is like a blind person, whose body is sick, a boil, an arrow, a snake, impermanent, suffering, empty, and not a self. Rubbing it with both hands, he says: 'Gautama! This is sickness-free, this is nirvana.' Udraka! You do not even know about sickness-free, how much less can you know and see nirvana? To say that you know and see is utterly impossible. Udraka! The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One says:

世尊语曰：「如是。须闲提！如盲无目，身即是病、是痈、是箭、是蛇、是无常、是苦、是空、是非神，以两手抆摸而作是说：『瞿昙！此是无病，此是涅槃。』须闲提！汝尚不识于无病，何况知见于涅槃耶？言知见者，终无是处。须闲提！如来、无所著、等正觉说：

The best benefit is being sickness-free, nirvana is the greatest bliss, the eightfold
path is the path, abiding in the sweet dew of peace and tranquility.

「无病第一利，　　涅槃第一乐，诸道八正道，　　住安隐甘露。

"Those many people all heard it together. When the many non-Buddhists heard this verse, they passed it on to each other but could not understand the meaning. Having heard it, they wanted to seek instruction, but they were all ignorant and deceived each other. They manifested their own bodies, the four great elements, born from parents, nourished by food and drink, always covered, massaged, bathed, endured, destroyed, ground down, and dispersed. Yet they saw the spirit receiving the spirit, conditioned by receiving, there is existence, conditioned by existence, there is birth, conditioned by birth, there is old age and death, conditioned by old age and death, there is sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus, this life is entirely a mass of great suffering."

「彼众多人并共闻之，众多异学闻此偈已，展转相传，不能知义。彼既闻已，而欲求教，彼并愚痴，还相欺诳。彼自现身四大之种，从父母生，饮食所长，常复按摩澡浴，强忍、破坏、磨灭，离散之法。然见神受神，缘受则有，缘有则生，缘生则老死，缘老死则愁戚啼哭、忧苦懊恼，如是此生纯大苦阴。」

Then the non-Buddhist student Sūkaraṇīda rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, put his palms together towards the Buddha, and said, "Gautama! I now have utmost faith in the śramaṇa Gautama! I only wish that Gautama would skillfully teach the Dharma, enabling me to know what is sickness-free and what is nirvana."

于是，须闲提异学即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白曰：「瞿昙！我今极信沙门瞿昙！唯愿瞿昙善为说法，令我得知此是无病，此是涅槃。」

The World-Honored One said, "Sūkaraṇīda! If your holy wisdom-eye is not yet pure, I will explain to you about sickness-free and nirvana, but you will not be able to understand, and it will only tire me. Sūkaraṇīda! It is like a blind person who is told by another, 'You should know that this is blue, yellow, red, and white.' Sūkaraṇīda! Can that blind person know blue, yellow, red, and white colors based on what the other person says?"

世尊告曰：「须闲提！若汝圣慧眼未净者，我为汝说无病、涅槃，终不能知，唐烦劳我。须闲提！犹生盲人，因他往语：『汝当知之，此是青色，黄、赤、白色。』须闲提！彼生盲人颇因他说，知是青色、黄、赤、白色耶？」

He replied to the World-Honored One, "No, Gautama!"

答世尊曰：「不也。瞿昙！」

So it is, Sūkaraṇīda! If your holy wisdom-eye is not yet pure, I will explain to you about sickness-free and nirvana, but you will not be able to understand, and it will only tire me. Sūkaraṇīda! I will explain to you the wonderful medicine as it is, so that your impure holy wisdom-eye may become pure. Sūkaraṇīda! If your holy wisdom-eye becomes pure, you will then know for yourself what is sickness-free and what is nirvana. Sūkaraṇīda! It is like a blind person who has relatives who, out of compassion, seek to benefit and bring peace and happiness to them, and seek a doctor for their eyes. That eye doctor gives them various treatments, such as inducing vomiting, purging, nasal irrigation, or enemas, or pricking their veins, or causing tears to flow. Sūkaraṇīda! If there is a way to cleanse their two eyes, Sūkaraṇīda! If their two eyes become cleansed, they will then see for themselves what is blue, yellow, red, and white, and see their dirty and impure clothes, and think, 'They are enemies who have long deceived me with dirty clothes,' and then have feelings of hatred. Sūkaraṇīda! That person may then be able to kill them. In this way, Sūkaraṇīda! I will explain to you the wonderful medicine as it is, so that your impure holy wisdom-eye may become pure. Sūkaraṇīda! If your holy wisdom-eye becomes pure, you will then know for yourself what is sickness-free and what is nirvana.

「如是。须闲提！若汝圣慧眼未净者，我为汝说无病、涅槃，终不能知，唐烦劳我。须闲提！我为汝说如其像妙药，令未净圣慧眼而得清净。须闲提！若汝圣慧眼得清净者，汝便自知此是无病，此是涅槃。须闲提！犹生盲人，有诸亲亲为彼慈愍，求利及饶益，求安隐快乐故，为求眼医，彼眼医者与种种治，或吐或下，或灌于鼻，或复灌下，或刺其脉，或令泪出。须闲提！傥有此处，得净两眼。须闲提！若彼两眼得清净者，则便自见此是青色，黄、赤、白色，见彼垢腻不净之衣，便作是念：『彼即怨家，长夜则以垢腻之衣欺诳于我。』便有憎心。须闲提！此人傥能杀害于彼。如是，须闲提！我为汝说如其像妙药，令未净圣慧眼而得清净。须闲提！若汝圣慧眼得净者，汝便自知此是无病，此是涅槃。

Sūkaraṇīda! There are four kinds of dharmas that can purify the impure holy wisdom-eye. What are the four? Associating with good friends, respecting and serving them, hearing the good Dharma, contemplating it well, and practicing according to the Dharma. Sūkaraṇīda! You should study like this: associating with good friends, respecting and serving them, hearing the good Dharma, contemplating it well, and practicing according to the Dharma. Sūkaraṇīda! You should study like this. Sūkaraṇīda! After associating with good friends, respecting and serving them, you will then hear the good Dharma. After hearing the good Dharma, you will then contemplate it well. After contemplating it well, you will then practice according to the Dharma. After practicing according to the Dharma, you will then know this suffering as it truly is, know the origin of this suffering, know the cessation of this suffering, and know the path to the cessation of this suffering as it truly is.

「须闲提！有四种法，未净圣慧眼而得清净。云何为四？亲近善知识，恭敬、承事，闻善法，善思惟，趣向法次法。须闲提！汝当如是学，亲近善知识，恭敬、承事，闻善法，善思惟，趣向法次法。须闲提！当学如是。须闲提！汝亲近善知识，恭敬、承事已，便闻善法，闻善法已，便善思惟，善思惟已便趣向法次法，趣向法次法已，便知此苦如真，知此苦习、知此苦灭、知此苦灭道如真。

"How does one know suffering as it truly is? It means knowing the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, death, being with what is disliked, being separated from what is loved, not getting what one desires, and the suffering of the five aggregates. Knowing suffering like this is knowing suffering as it truly is. How does one know the origin of suffering as it truly is? It means this craving, which leads to future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, wandering here and there, namely: craving for sensual pleasures, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence. Knowing the origin of suffering like this is knowing the origin of suffering as it truly is. How does one know the cessation of suffering as it truly is? It means the cessation of this craving, which leads to future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, wandering here and there, namely: the cessation of craving for sensual pleasures, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence, without remainder, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, made unable to arise again in the future. Knowing the cessation of suffering like this is knowing the cessation of suffering as it truly is. How does one know the path leading to the cessation of suffering as it truly is? It means the eightfold noble path, namely: right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These are the eight. Knowing the path leading to the cessation of suffering like this is knowing the path leading to the cessation of suffering as it truly is."

「云何知苦如真？谓生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、怨憎会苦、爱别离苦、所求不得苦、略五盛阴苦，如是知苦如真。云何知苦习如真？谓此爱当受未来有，与喜欲俱，愿彼彼有，如是知苦习如真。云何知苦灭如真？谓此爱当受未来有，与喜欲俱，愿彼彼有灭、无余、断、舍、吐、尽、无欲、没、息止，如是知苦灭如真。云何知苦灭道如真？谓八支圣道，正见乃至正定，是谓为八，如是知苦灭道如真。」

Having spoken this Dharma, the non-Buddhist Sūkaraṇīda was far removed from dust and defilements, and the Dharma-eye of the Dharma arose for him. Then the non-Buddhist Sūkaraṇīda saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, realized the pure Dharma, cut off doubts, crossed over perplexity, had no other masters, did not follow others, was without hesitation, and abided in the realization of the fruits. He said to the World-Honored One, "World-Honored One! Please allow me to go forth and practice the path, to receive full ordination and become a bhikṣu."

说此法已，须闲提异学远尘离垢，诸法法眼生。于是，须闲提异学见法得法，觉白净法，断疑度惑，更无余尊，不复从他，无有犹豫，已住果证，于世尊法得无所畏。即从座起，稽首佛足，白曰：「世尊！愿令我得出家学道，受具足，得比丘。」

The World-Honored One said, "Welcome, bhikṣu! Practice the holy life."

世尊告曰：「善来比丘！修行梵行。」

The non-Buddhist Sūkaraṇīda went forth and practiced the path, received full ordination, and became a bhikṣu. Having gone forth and practiced the path, received full ordination, and become a bhikṣu, Sūkaraṇīda knew the Dharma and attained arahantship.

须闲提异学即是出家学道，受具足，得比丘。须闲提出家学道，受具足，知法已，至得阿罗诃。

The Buddha spoke thus. The Venerable Sūkaraṇīda, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者须闲提闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
