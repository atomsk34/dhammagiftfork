MĀ 161

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 12 梵志品

161. （一六一）梵摩经

Parallel to: MN 91

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Vidéha, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus.

一时，佛游鞞陀提国，与大比丘众俱。

At that time, in the city of Vesālī, there was a brāhman named Brahmadatta who was extremely wealthy and happy, with immeasurable wealth and property, livestock, and industries beyond measure. He had a fief and a granary, and was fully endowed with various necessities. The food was abundant, and even the water, grass, and trees in Vesālī were given to him by King Ajātaśatru of Magadha, the son of Vaidehī. The brāhman Brahmadatta had a young brāhman named Udraka who was raised by his parents. He was born pure, and his parents' lineage was unbroken for seven generations. He was well-versed and had a vast memory, reciting more than the four Vedas. He deeply understood the five kinds of statements: cause, condition, truth, text, and play.

尔时，弥萨罗有梵志，名曰梵摩，极大富乐，资财无量，畜牧产业不可称计，封户食邑种种具足食丰，弥萨罗乃至水草木，谓摩竭陀王未生怨鞞陀提子特与梵封。梵志梵摩有一摩纳，名优多罗，为父母所举，受生清净，乃至七世父母不绝种族，生生无恶，博闻总持，诵过四典经，深达因、缘、正、文、戏五句说。

The brāhman Brahmadatta heard that the śramaṇa Gautama, the son of the Śākya clan, had renounced his Śākya relatives, shaved his beard and hair, donned the saffron robes, left home out of true faith, and gone forth to practice the path. He was traveling in the country of Videha, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus. The śramaṇa Gautama had a great reputation that was heard in the ten directions. He was a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, a Sugata, an unsurpassed knower of the world, a driver of tameable people, a teacher of gods and men, a Buddha, a Bhagavān. In this world, including the gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas, from humans to gods, he himself knew, himself realized, himself accomplished, and himself dwelt. He taught the Dharma, which was excellent in the beginning, excellent in the middle, and excellent in the end, with meaning and expression, complete and pure, revealing the holy life.

梵志梵摩闻有沙门瞿昙释种子舍释宗族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道，游鞞陀提国，与大比丘众俱。彼沙门瞿昙有大名称，周闻十方，彼沙门瞿昙如来、无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐。彼于此世，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，自知自觉，自作证成就游，彼说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行。

Furthermore, it is said that the śramaṇa Gautama possesses the thirty-two marks of a great person. If one possesses the marks of a great person, there are necessarily two true and non-deceptive destinies. If one remains at home, one will certainly become a wheel-turning king, wise and intelligent, with a fourfold army, ruling the world, self-controlled. As a righteous Dharma king, one will accomplish the seven treasures. Those seven treasures are the wheel treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, jewel treasure, woman treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure. These are called the seven. One will have a thousand sons, with handsome features, brave and fearless, able to subdue other armies. One will certainly rule over all this land up to the great ocean, not by means of weapons, but by teaching with the Dharma, bringing peace and happiness. If one shaves off one's beard and hair, dons the saffron robes, leaves home out of true faith, and goes forth to practice the path, one will certainly attain the state of a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, with a renowned name spreading throughout the ten directions.

复次，闻彼沙门瞿昙成就三十二大人之相，若成就大人相者，必有二处真谛不虚。若在家者，必为转轮王，聪明智慧，有四种军，整御天下，由己自在。如法法王成就七宝，彼七宝者，轮宝、象宝、马宝、珠宝、女宝、居士宝、主兵臣宝，是谓为七。千子具足，颜貌端政，勇猛无畏，能伏他众。彼必统领此一切地乃至大海，不以刀杖，以法教令，令得安隐。若剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，必得如来、无所著、等正觉，名称流布，周闻十方。

The brāhman Brahmadatta heard this and said, "Udraka, I have heard that the śramaṇa Gautama, the son of the Śākya clan, renounced his Śākya relatives, shaved his beard and hair, donned the saffron robes, left home out of true faith, and went forth to practice the path. He is traveling in the country of Videha, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus. Udraka, it is said that the śramaṇa Gautama has a great reputation that is heard in the ten directions. He is a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, a Sugata, an unsurpassed knower of the world, a driver of tameable people, a teacher of gods and men, a Buddha, a Bhagavān. In this world, including the gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas, from humans to gods, he himself knows, himself realizes, himself accomplishes, and himself dwells. He teaches the Dharma, which is excellent in the beginning, excellent in the middle, and excellent in the end, with meaning and expression, complete and pure, revealing the holy life.

梵志梵摩闻已，告曰：「优多罗！我闻如是：『彼沙门瞿昙释种子舍释宗族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道，游鞞陀提国，与大比丘众俱。』优多罗！『彼沙门瞿昙有大名称，周闻十方，彼沙门瞿昙如来、无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐。彼于此世，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，自知自觉，自作证成就游，彼说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行。』

Furthermore, Udraka, it is said that the śramaṇa Gautama possesses the thirty-two marks of a great person. If one possesses the marks of a great person, there are necessarily two true and non-deceptive destinies. If one remains at home, one will certainly become a wheel-turning king, wise and intelligent, with a fourfold army, ruling the world, self-controlled. As a righteous Dharma king, one will accomplish the seven treasures. Those seven treasures are the wheel treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, jewel treasure, woman treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure. These are called the seven. One will have a thousand sons, with handsome features, brave and fearless, able to subdue other armies. One will certainly rule over all this land up to the great ocean, not by means of weapons, but by teaching with the Dharma, bringing peace and happiness. If one shaves off one's beard and hair, dons the saffron robes, leaves home out of true faith, and goes forth to practice the path, one will certainly attain the state of a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, with a renowned name spreading throughout the ten directions.

「复次，优多罗！『彼沙门瞿昙成就三十二大人之相，若成就大人相者，必有二处真谛不虚，若在家者，必为转轮王，聪明智慧，有四种军，整御天下，由己自在。如法法王成就七宝，彼七宝者，轮宝、象宝、马宝、珠宝、女宝、居士宝、主兵臣宝，是谓为七。千子具足，颜貌端政，勇猛无畏，能伏他众，彼必统领此一切地乃至大海，不以刀杖，以法教令，令得安隐。若剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，必得如来、无所著、等正觉，名称流布，周闻十方。』

Udraka, you uphold the teachings and possess the thirty-two marks of a great person. If one possesses the marks of a great person, there are necessarily two true and non-deceptive destinies. If one remains at home, one will certainly become a wheel-turning king, wise and intelligent, with a fourfold army, ruling the world, self-controlled. As a righteous Dharma king, one will accomplish the seven treasures. Those seven treasures are the wheel treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, jewel treasure, woman treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure. These are called the seven. One will have a thousand sons, with handsome features, brave and fearless, able to subdue other armies. One will certainly rule over all this land up to the great ocean, not by means of weapons, but by teaching with the Dharma, bringing peace and happiness. If one shaves off one's beard and hair, dons the saffron robes, leaves home out of true faith, and goes forth to practice the path, one will certainly attain the state of a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, with a renowned name spreading throughout the ten directions.

「优多罗！汝受持诸经，有三十二大人之相，若成就大人相者，必有二处真谛不虚。若在家者，必为转轮王，聪明智慧，有四种军，整御天下，由己自在。如法法王成就七宝，彼七宝者，轮宝、象宝、马宝、珠宝、女宝、居士宝、主兵臣宝，是谓为七。千子具足，颜貌端政，勇猛无畏，能伏他众，彼必统领此一切地乃至大海，不以刀杖，以法教令，令得安隐。若剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，必得如来、无所著、等正觉，名称流布，周闻十方。」

"Udraka replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! I uphold the teachings and possess the thirty-two marks of a great person. If one possesses the marks of a great person, there are necessarily two true and non-deceptive destinies. If one remains at home, one will certainly become a wheel-turning king, wise and intelligent, with a fourfold army, ruling the world, self-controlled. As a righteous Dharma king, one will accomplish the seven treasures. Those seven treasures are the wheel treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, jewel treasure, woman treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure. These are called the seven. One will have a thousand sons, with handsome features, brave and fearless, able to subdue other armies. One will certainly rule over all this land up to the great ocean, not by means of weapons, but by teaching with the Dharma, bringing peace and happiness. If one shaves off one's beard and hair, dons the saffron robes, leaves home out of true faith, and goes forth to practice the path, one will certainly attain the state of a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, with a renowned name spreading throughout the ten directions.

优多罗答曰：「唯然。世尊！我受持诸经，有三十二大人之相，若成就大人相者，必有二处真谛不虚。若在家者，必为转轮王，聪明智慧，有四种军，整御天下，由己自在。如法法王成就七宝，彼七宝者，轮宝、象宝、马宝、珠宝、女宝、居士宝、主兵臣宝，是谓为七。千子具足，颜貌端政，勇猛无畏，能伏他众，彼必统领此一切地乃至大海，不以刀杖，以法教令，令得安隐。若剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，必得如来、无所著、等正觉，名称流布，周闻十方。」

"Brahman Varuna said, "Udraka, go to that śramaṇa Gautama and see if he is like this or not. Does he really have the thirty-two marks of a great person?

梵志梵摩告曰：「优多罗！汝往诣彼沙门瞿昙所，观彼沙门瞿昙为如是，为不如是？实有三十二大人相耶？」

Udraka heard this and bowed at the feet of Brahman Varuna, circumambulated him three times, and left. He went to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. He observed the World-Honored One's body for the thirty-two marks. He saw that the World-Honored One had thirty marks, but he was uncertain about two marks: the hidden male organ and the broad and long tongue. The World-Honored One thought, "This Udraka is observing my body for the thirty-two marks. He sees thirty marks, but is uncertain about two marks: the hidden male organ and the broad and long tongue. I should now resolve his doubts." Knowing this, the World-Honored One immediately manifested his supernormal powers as appropriate. Having manifested his supernormal powers as appropriate, he caused Udraka to see my hidden male organ and broad and long tongue.

优多罗摩纳闻已，稽首梵志梵摩足，绕三匝而去，往诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面，观世尊身三十二相。彼见世尊身有三十相，于二相疑惑，阴马藏及广长舌。世尊念曰：「此优多罗于我身观三十二相，彼见有三十相，于二相疑惑，阴马藏及广长舌。我今宁可断其疑惑。」世尊知已，即如其像作如意足，如其像作如意足已，令优多罗摩纳见我身阴马藏及广长舌。

Then the World-Honored One manifested his supernormal powers as appropriate. Having manifested his supernormal powers as appropriate, Udraka saw the World-Honored One's hidden male organ and broad and long tongue. The broad and long tongue is that the tongue comes out of the mouth and covers the face. Having seen this, Udraka thought, "The śramaṇa Gautama has accomplished the thirty-two marks of a great person. If one has accomplished the marks of a great person, there are definitely two truths that are not false. If one stays at home, one will definitely become a wheel-turning king, wise and intelligent, with the four types of armies, ruling the world, self-controlled, a righteous Dharma king who has accomplished the seven treasures. Those seven treasures are the wheel treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, jewel treasure, woman treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure. These are called the seven. With a thousand sons, handsome and brave, fearless and able to subdue others, he will definitely rule over all this land up to the great ocean, not by sword or staff, but by teaching with the Dharma, bringing peace and happiness. If he shaves his head and beard, puts on the monastic robes, leaves home out of true faith, becomes a homeless monk, and studies the path, he will definitely attain the state of a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, with his fame spreading and being heard in the ten directions."

于是，世尊即如其像作如意足，如其像作如意足已，优多罗摩纳见世尊身阴马藏及广长舌。广长舌者，从口出舌，尽覆其面。优多罗摩纳见已，而作是念：「沙门瞿昙成就三十二大人之相，若成就大人相者，必有二处真谛不虚。若在家者，必为转轮王，聪明智慧，有四种军，整御天下，由己自在，如法法王成就七宝。彼七宝者，轮宝、象宝、马宝、珠宝、女宝、居士宝、主兵臣宝，是谓为七。千子具足，颜貌端政，勇猛无畏，能伏他众，彼必统领此一切地乃至大海，不以刀杖，以法教令，令得安隐。若剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，必得如来、无所著、等正觉，名称流布，周闻十方。」

Udraka further thought, "I should observe his demeanor and conduct, and observe where he goes." Then Udraka followed the Buddha and observed his demeanor and conduct, and observed where he went, for four summer months. After four summer months, Udraka was pleased with the World-Honored One's demeanor and conduct, and with observing where he went. He said, "Gautama! I have something to do now and wish to take leave and return."

优多罗摩纳复作是念：「我宁可极观威仪礼节，及观游行所趣。」于是，优多罗摩纳寻随佛行，于夏四月观威仪礼节，及观游行所趣。优多罗摩纳过夏四月，悦可世尊威仪礼节，及观游行所趣，白曰：「瞿昙！我今有事，欲还请辞。」

The World-Honored One said, "Udraka! Go as you please."

世尊告曰：「优多罗！汝去随意。」

Having heard what the World-Honored One said, Udraka Ramaputta accepted it well and remembered it well. He then rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and left. He went to the brahmin Brahmadatta, bowed his head at the brahmin Brahmadatta's feet, and sat to one side.

优多罗摩纳闻世尊所说，善受善持，即从坐起，绕三匝而去，往诣梵志梵摩所。稽首梵志梵摩足，却坐一面。

The brahmin Brahmadatta asked, "Udraka! Is it really as I have heard, that the śramaṇa Gautama has a great reputation that is heard in the ten directions, and that he really has the thirty-two marks of a great man?"

梵志梵摩问曰：「优多罗！实如所闻，沙门瞿昙有大名称，周闻十方，为如是，为不如是？实有三十二大人相耶？」

Udraka Ramaputta replied, "Yes, sir! It is really as I have heard, that the śramaṇa Gautama has a great reputation that is heard in the ten directions. The śramaṇa Gautama is like this, not unlike this. He really has the thirty-two marks. Sir, the śramaṇa Gautama stands with his feet firmly planted, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has a wheel with a thousand spokes growing on the soles of his feet, all complete, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has long toes, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has straight and upright feet, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has full and even ankles on both sides of his feet, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has well-rounded ankles, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has upward-pointing body hair, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has webbed fingers and toes like a goose king, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama.

优多罗摩纳答曰：「唯然。尊！实如所闻，沙门瞿昙有大名称，周闻十方，沙门瞿昙如是，非不如是，实有三十二相。尊！沙门瞿昙足安平立，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊！沙门瞿昙足下生轮，轮有千辐，一切具足，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊！沙门瞿昙足指纤长，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊！沙门瞿昙足周正直，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊！沙门瞿昙足跟踝后两边平满，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊，沙门瞿昙足两踝佣，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊！沙门瞿昙身毛上向，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。复次，尊！沙门瞿昙手足网缦，犹如鴈王，是谓，尊！沙门瞿昙大人大人之相。

Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has extremely wonderful and soft hands and feet, soft and tender like a cotton flower, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has soft and fine skin, dust and water do not stick to it, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has one hair per pore, and each hair grows in a single pore, the color is like dark blue, curling to the right like a conch, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has intestines like a deer king, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has a hidden male organ like a good horse king, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has a round and beautiful body like a nyagrodha tree, the upper and lower parts are perfectly proportioned, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama. Furthermore, sir, the śramaṇa Gautama has a straight body, by standing upright and stretching his arms he can touch his knees, this is called, sir, the great man's mark of a great man of the śramaṇa Gautama.

He had gradually come to the country of Vesali and was staying in the Great Grove of the Gods. That śramaṇa Gautama had a great reputation that was heard in the ten directions. That śramaṇa Gautama was a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, a Sugata, an unsurpassed knower of the world, a driver of tameable people, a teacher of gods and men, a Buddha, a Bhagavān. He had realized for himself and accomplished the supreme perfect enlightenment in this world, among gods, Māras, Brahmās, śramaṇas, and brahmins, from humans to gods. He taught the Dharma that was good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing, complete and pure, revealing the holy life. If one saw the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and honored, paid respect, and made offerings to him, it would be a great gain. I should go to see that śramaṇa Gautama and pay homage and make offerings.

梵志梵摩闻沙门瞿昙释种子舍释宗族，出家学道，游行鞞陀提国，与大比丘众俱，展转来至此弥萨罗国，住大天［木＊奈］林。彼沙门瞿昙有大名称，周闻十方，彼沙门瞿昙如来、无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐。彼于此世，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，自知自觉，自作证成就游。彼说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行。若有见如来、无所著、等正觉，敬重礼拜，供养承事者，快得善利，我宁可往见沙门瞿昙！礼拜供养。

The brahmin Brahmadatta said to the charioteer, "Quickly prepare the carriage! I wish to go to see the śramaṇa Gautama!"

梵志梵摩告御者曰：「汝速严驾！我今欲往诣沙门瞿昙！」

The charioteer received the order and quickly prepared the carriage. He then returned and said, "The carriage is ready. Please know the time." Thereupon, Brahmadatta mounted the most excellent and wonderful carriage, left Vesali, and went north to the Great Grove of the Gods, wishing to see the World-Honored One, pay homage, and make offerings.

御者受教，即速严驾讫，还白曰：「严驾已毕，尊自知时。」于是，梵摩乘极贤妙车，从弥萨罗出，北行至大天［木＊奈］林，欲见世尊礼拜供养。

At that time, the World-Honored One was surrounded by an immeasurable assembly in front and behind, teaching the Dharma. The brahmin Brahmadatta saw from afar the World-Honored One surrounded by an immeasurable assembly in front and behind, teaching the Dharma. Having seen this, he was terrified. Thereupon, Brahmadatta immediately moved to the side of the road, went to the foot of a tree, and said to a mendicant, "Go to that śramaṇa Gautama and ask on my behalf, 'Are you in good health, free from illness, strong, and living in comfort?' Say this: 'Gautama! My teacher Brahmadatta asks if you are in good health, free from illness, strong, and living in comfort. Gautama! My teacher Brahmadatta wishes to come and see the śramaṇa Gautama!'"

尔时，世尊在无量众前后围绕而为说法，梵志梵摩遥见世尊在无量众前后围绕而为说法，见已，恐怖。于是，梵摩即避在道侧，至树下住，告一摩纳：「汝往诣彼沙门瞿昙，为我问讯圣体康强，安快无病，起居轻便，气力如常耶？作如是语：『瞿昙！我师梵摩问讯圣体康强，安快无病，起居轻便，气力如常耶？瞿昙！我师梵摩欲来见沙门瞿昙！』」

Thereupon, the mendicant accepted the instruction and went to where the Buddha was. They exchanged greetings and sat to one side. He said, "Gautama! My teacher Brahmadatta asks if you are in good health, free from illness, strong, and living in comfort. Gautama! My teacher Brahmadatta wishes to come and see the śramaṇa Gautama!"

于是，摩纳即受教行，往诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面，白曰：「瞿昙！我师梵摩问讯圣体康强，安快无病，起居轻便，气力如常耶？瞿昙！我师梵摩欲来见沙门瞿昙！」

The World-Honored One said, "Mendicant! May the brahmin Brahmadatta be at ease and happy, and may the gods, humans, asuras, gandharvas, rākṣasas, and other various kinds of beings be at ease and happy. Mendicant! Let the brahmin Brahmadatta come as he pleases."

世尊告曰：「摩纳！令梵志梵摩安隐快乐，令天及人、阿修罗、揵沓惒、罗刹及余种种身安隐快乐。摩纳！梵志梵摩欲来随意。」

Thereupon, the mendicant, having heard what the Buddha said, accepted it well and remembered it well. He then rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and departed. He returned to where the brahmin Brahmadatta was and said, "Sir! I have already informed the śramaṇa Gautama. The śramaṇa Gautama is now waiting for you, sir. You should know the time."

于是，摩纳闻佛所说，善受善持，即从坐起，绕佛三匝而去，还诣梵志梵摩所，白曰：「尊！我已通沙门瞿昙，彼沙门瞿昙今住待尊，唯尊知时。」

The brahmin Brahmadatta then descended from his carriage and walked to where the Buddha was. The assembly saw from afar the brahmin Brahmadatta coming and immediately rose from their seats, making way for him. Why was this? Because of his fame, virtue, and wide knowledge.

梵志梵摩即从车下，步诣佛所。彼众遥见梵志梵摩来，即从座起，开道避之。所以者何？以有名德及多识故。

The brahmin Brahmadatta said to the assembly, "Virtuous ones! All of you, please sit down again. I wish to go straight to see the śramaṇa Gautama." Thereupon, Brahmadatta went to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side.

梵志梵摩告彼众曰：「诸贤！各各复坐，我欲直往见沙门瞿昙。」于是，梵摩往诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面。

At that time, the brahmin Brahmadatta was not lacking in the two faculties of the eyes and ears. Having sat down, Brahmadatta carefully observed the Buddha's body with the thirty-two marks. He saw thirty of them, but had doubts about two: the sheathed penis and the broad and long tongue. Brahmadatta then asked the World-Honored One in verse:

尔时，梵摩不坏二根，眼根及耳根。梵志梵摩坐已，谛观佛身三十二相，彼见三十相，于二相有疑，阴马藏及广长舌。梵志梵摩即时以偈问世尊曰：

As I have heard before, the thirty-two marks of a great person, I seek
but do not see two on the body of the honored śramaṇa Gautama. Does
he have a sheathed penis? The most honored among all people is deeply hidden
How can he be the most honored among people, not showing his subtle tongue?
If the honored one has a broad and long tongue, I only wish to
see it. Now I truly have doubts in my mind, I pray that the
Tamer will resolve my doubts.

「如我昔曾所闻，　　三十二大人相，于中求不见二，　　尊沙门瞿昙身。为有阴马藏不？　　一切人尊深密，云何为人最尊，　　不现视微妙舌？若尊有广长舌，　　唯愿令我得见，今实有疑惑心，　　愿调御决我疑。」

The World-Honored One thought: "This brahmin Brahmadatta seeks the thirty-two marks on my body. He sees thirty, but has doubts about two: the sheathed penis and the broad and long tongue. I should now remove his doubts." The World-Honored One, knowing this, performed the supernormal power of manifesting the image. Having performed the supernormal power of manifesting the image, the brahmin Brahmadatta saw the World-Honored One's sheathed penis and broad and long tongue. As for the broad and long tongue, the tongue came out of the mouth and completely covered his face. The World-Honored One stopped the supernormal power and spoke this verse to the brahmin Brahmadatta:

世尊作是念：「此梵志梵摩求我身三十二相，彼见三十，于二有疑，阴马藏及广长舌，我今宁可除彼疑惑。」世尊知已，作如其像如意足，作如其像如意足已，梵志梵摩见世尊身阴马藏及广长舌，于中广长舌者，舌从口出，尽覆其面。世尊止如意足已，为梵志梵摩说此颂曰：

What you have heard before, the thirty-two marks of a great person, they
are all complete in my body, the supreme and true. The Tamer has
cut off my doubts, the brahmin has given rise to subtle faith. It
is difficult to see and hear, the supreme and true perfect enlightenment. The
appearance in the world is extremely difficult, the supreme and true perfect enlightenment
Brahmins, I am the perfectly enlightened one, the supreme and true Dharma King.

「谓汝昔曾所闻，　　三十二大人相，彼一切在我身，　　满具足最上正。调御断于我疑，　　梵志发微妙信。至难得见闻，　　最上正尽觉。出世为极难，　　最上正尽觉，梵志我正觉，　　无上正法王。」

Having heard this, the brahmin Brahmadatta thought: "This Śramaṇa Gautama has accomplished the thirty-two marks of a great person. If one who has accomplished the marks of a great person, there are necessarily two true and non-false places. If he stays at home, he will surely become a wheel-turning king, wise and intelligent, with a fourfold army, ruling the world. As a Dharma king, he will accomplish the seven treasures. Those seven treasures are the wheel treasure, elephant treasure, horse treasure, jewel treasure, woman treasure, householder treasure, and general treasure. These are called the seven. He will have a thousand sons, with handsome faces, brave and fearless, able to subdue other armies. He will surely rule over all this land up to the great ocean, not by sword or staff, but by teaching with the Dharma, bringing peace and happiness. If he shaves his beard and hair, puts on the saffron robes, leaves home out of true faith, becomes a homeless one, and studies the path, he will surely attain the state of Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, with his fame spreading and being heard in the ten directions."

梵志梵摩闻已，而作是念：「此沙门瞿昙成就三十二大人之相，谓成就大人相者，必有二处真谛不虚。若在家者，必为转轮王，聪明智慧，有四种军，整御天下。如法法王成就七宝，彼七宝者，轮宝、象宝、马宝、珠宝、女宝、居士宝、主兵臣宝，是谓为七。千子具足，颜貌端政，勇猛无畏，能伏他众，彼必统领此一切地乃至大海，不以刀杖，以法教令，令得安隐。若剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，必得如来、无所著、等正觉，名称流布，周闻十方。」

Then the World-Honored One thought: "This brahmin Brahmadatta has been without flattery and deceit for a long time. Whatever he wants to ask is to understand, not to trouble. He is also like this. I should explain the profound Abhidharma to him." Knowing this, the World-Honored One spoke a verse to the brahmin Brahmadatta:

于是，世尊而作是念：「此梵志梵摩长夜无谀谄、无欺诳，所欲所问者，一切欲知非为触娆，彼亦如是，我宁可说彼甚深阿毘昙。」世尊知已，为梵志梵摩即说颂曰：

Because of the Dharma of present happiness, benefiting for the
future. Brahmin, you ask about matters, according to your original
intention. I will resolve your doubts about those various matters."
The World-Honored One having already permitted the brahmin Brahmadatta to
ask, he then asked the World-Honored One about matters according
to his original intention: "What is a brahmin? What is
the meaning of the three knowledges? What is called non-attachment?
What is perfect enlightenment?

「现世乐法故，　　饶益为后世，梵志汝问事，　　随本意所思。彼彼诸问事，　　我为汝断疑。」世尊已许问，　　梵志梵摩故，便问世尊事，　　随本意所思：「云何为梵志，　　三达有何义？以何说无著，　　何等正尽觉？」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied in verse:

尔时，世尊以颂答曰：

Destroying unwholesome dharmas, establishing and abiding in the practice of
the brahman. Cultivating the brahman's practice, this is called a
brahman. Clearly understanding the past, seeing the pleasant and the
evil paths, attaining the exhaustion of ignorance, this is called
the sage. Well knowing the pure mind, completely liberating from
desire, anger, and ignorance, accomplishing the three knowledges, this is
called the three knowledges. Far from unwholesome dharmas, rightly abiding
in the ultimate truth, most respected in the world, this
is called non-attachment. Benefiting devas and humans, giving the eye
destroying disputes, universally knowing and seeing the exhaustion, this is
called perfect enlightenment.

「灭恶不善法，　　立住择梵行，修习梵志正，　　以此为梵志。明达于过去，　　见乐及恶道，得无明尽说，　　知是立牟尼。善知清净心，　　尽脱婬怒痴，成就于三明，　　以此为三达。远离不善法，　　正住第一义，第一世所敬，　　以此为无著。饶益天及人，　　与眼灭坏诤，普知现视尽，　　以此正尽觉。」

At that time, Brahma immediately rose from his seat, wanting to bow his head at the Buddha's feet. At that time, the great assembly simultaneously let out a loud and lofty voice, saying: "The Śramaṇa Gautama is truly amazing! He has great supernatural powers, great authority, great merit, and great spiritual power. Why is this? Among the brāhmaṇas and householders of this country of Mithilā, Brahma is the foremost, because of his birth. Brahma is raised by his parents, born pure, and his parents' lineage is pure for seven generations. He is born without evil. He bows his head in utmost respect to the Śramaṇa Gautama, making offerings and serving him. The Śramaṇa Gautama is truly amazing! He has great supernatural powers, great authority, great merit, and great spiritual power. Why is this? Among the brāhmaṇas and householders of this country of Mithilā, Brahma is the foremost, because of his learning. Brahma is well-versed and has memorized the four Vedas, deeply understanding the five kinds of statements: cause, condition, right, text, and play. He bows his head in utmost respect to the Śramaṇa Gautama, making offerings and serving him.

于是，梵摩即从座起，欲稽首佛足，彼时大众同时俱发高大音声：「沙门瞿昙甚奇！甚特！有大如意足，有大威德，有大福祐，有大威神。所以者何？此弥萨罗国所有梵志、居士者，梵志梵摩于彼最第一，谓出生故。梵志梵摩为父母所举，受生清净，乃至七世父母不绝种族，生生无恶，彼为沙门瞿昙极下意尊敬作礼，供养奉事。沙门瞿昙甚奇！甚特！有大如意足，有大威德，有大福祐，有大威神。所以者何？此弥萨罗国所有梵志、居士者，梵志梵摩于彼最第一，谓学书故。梵志梵摩博闻总持，诵过四典经，深达因、缘、正、文、戏五句说。彼为沙门瞿昙极下意尊敬作礼，供养奉事。

"The Śramaṇa Gautama is truly amazing! He has great supernatural powers, great authority, great merit, and great spiritual power. Why is this? Among the brāhmaṇas and householders of this country of Mithilā, Brahma is the foremost, because of his wealth. Brahma is extremely wealthy and prosperous, with immeasurable wealth, livestock, and industries. His fiefs and estates are abundant, with plenty of food and water, grass, and wood. The king of Magadha, Ajātaśatru, has specially granted him a fief. He bows his head in utmost respect to the Śramaṇa Gautama, making offerings and serving him. The Śramaṇa Gautama is truly amazing! He has great supernatural powers, great authority, great merit, and great spiritual power. Why is this? Among the brāhmaṇas and householders of this country of Mithilā, Brahma is the foremost, because of his longevity. Brahma is extremely old, with a full lifespan of one hundred and twenty-six years. He bows his head in utmost respect to the Śramaṇa Gautama, making offerings and serving him."

「沙门瞿昙甚奇！甚特！有大如意足，有大威德，有大福祐，有大威神。所以者何？此弥萨罗国所有梵志、居士者，梵志梵摩于彼最第一，谓财物故。梵志梵摩极大富乐，资财无量，畜牧产业不可称计，封户食邑种种具足食丰，弥萨罗乃至水草木，谓王摩竭陀未生怨鞞陀提子特与梵封，彼为沙门瞿昙极下意尊敬作礼，供养奉事。沙门瞿昙甚奇！甚特！有大如意足，有大威德，有大福祐，有大威神。所以者何？此弥萨罗国所有梵志、居士者，梵志梵摩于彼最第一，谓寿命故。梵志梵摩极大长老，寿命具足，年百二十六，彼为沙门瞿昙极下意尊敬作礼，供养奉事。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing the thoughts of the great assembly with his mind-reading knowledge, said to the brāhmaṇa Brahma: "Stop, stop! Brāhmaṇa, just be content in your mind. Return to your seat, and I will teach the Dharma for you."

是时，世尊以他心智知彼大众心之所念，世尊知已，告梵志梵摩：「止！止！梵志！但心喜足，可还复坐，为汝说法。」

The brāhmaṇa Brahma bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, then sat to one side. The World-Honored One taught the Dharma for him, inspiring him with yearning and bringing about joy. With immeasurable skillful means, he taught the Dharma for him, inspiring him with yearning and bringing about joy. Having done so, according to the way of the Buddhas, he first spoke the Dharma of beauty, which delights the listener, namely, speaking of giving, speaking of precepts, and speaking of the Dharma of rebirth in heaven, criticizing desire as a calamity and saṃsāra as impure, praising the wonderful state of desirelessness and the white purity of the path. Having spoken this, the Buddha knew that he had a joyful mind, a complete mind, a pliant mind, a patient mind, an elevated mind, a one-pointed mind, a mind without doubt, and a mind without afflictions. He had the ability to receive the true Dharma of the Buddha, namely, the true essentials spoken by all Buddhas. The World-Honored One fully spoke for him the teachings on suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. The brāhmaṇa Brahma then saw the four noble truths on that very seat, namely, suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path, just as white cloth is easily dyed. In this way, the brāhmaṇa Brahma saw the four noble truths on that very seat, namely, suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path.

梵志梵摩稽首佛足，却坐一面，世尊为彼说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜。无量方便为彼说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜已，如诸佛法，先说端政法，闻者欢悦，谓说施、说戒、说生天法，毁呰欲为灾患，生死为秽，称叹无欲为妙，道品白净。为说是已，佛知彼有欢喜心、具足心、柔软心、堪耐心、升上心、一向心、无疑心、无盖心，有能有力受佛正法，谓如诸佛所说正要，世尊具为彼说苦、习、灭、道。梵志梵摩即于座上见四圣谛，苦、习、灭、道，犹如白素，易染为色。如是梵摩即于座上见四圣谛，苦、习、灭、道。

Then Brahma saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, realized the pure Dharma, cut off doubts, crossed over perplexity, had no other masters, was no longer dependent on others, had no hesitation, and abided in the realization of the fruits. He attained fearlessness in the World-Honored One's teachings. He then rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and said: "World-Honored One! I now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the community of bhikṣus. I only wish that the World-Honored One would accept me as a lay devotee. From this day forth, I take refuge for life, until the end of my life."

于是，梵摩见法得法，觉白净法，断疑度惑，更无余尊，不复由他，无有犹豫，已住果证，于世尊法得无所畏，即从座起，稽首佛足：「世尊！我今自归于佛、法及比丘众，唯愿世尊受我为优婆塞，从今日始，终身自归，乃至命尽。」

At that time, the brāhmaṇa Brahma joined his palms towards the Buddha and said, "World-Honored One! I only wish that you would deign to accept my invitation tomorrow, together with the community of bhikṣus." The World-Honored One accepted for the sake of the brāhmaṇa Brahma.

时，梵志梵摩叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！唯愿明日垂顾受请，及比丘众。」世尊为梵志梵摩故，默然而受。

The brāhmaṇa Brahma, knowing that the World-Honored One had silently accepted, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times, and departed. He returned to his home and prepared a feast of the finest and most delicious food, with abundant and digestible food. When the meal was ready, he laid out the seats and announced the time, saying, "The World-Honored One! The meal is ready. The Sage knows the time."

梵志梵摩知世尊默然受已，稽首佛足，绕三匝而去，还归其家。即于其夜施设肴馔极妙上味、种种丰饶食噉含消，施设已讫，平旦敷床，至时唱曰：「世尊！饭食已办，唯圣知时。」

Then, the World-Honored One, passing the night, at dawn, put on his robe, took his bowl, with the community of bhikṣus following, with the World-Honored One leading, went to the house of the brāhmaṇa Brahma. In front of the community of bhikṣus, he spread out a seat and sat down. The brāhmaṇa Brahma, knowing that the World-Honored One and the community of bhikṣus had settled in their seats, personally served the water for washing, with the finest and most delicious food, with abundant and digestible food, personally serving and pouring, until they were fully satisfied. After the meal, he put away the dishes, served the water for washing, and took a small seat to sit and receive the blessings.

于是，世尊过夜平旦，著衣持钵，比丘翼从，世尊在前，往诣梵志梵摩家，于比丘众前敷座而坐。梵志梵摩知世尊及比丘众众坐已定，自行澡水，以上味肴馔、种种丰饶食噉含消，自手酙酌，令极饱满，食讫收器，行澡水竟，取一小床，坐受呪愿。

After Brahma sat down, the World-Honored One spoke the blessing for him, saying:

梵志梵摩坐已，世尊为彼说呪愿曰：

The fire is the best of fasts, the sound of all sounds
the king is the most honored among humans, the ocean is the
longest of rivers, the moon is the brightest among the stars, the
light of the sun is unsurpassed, above, below, in all directions, and
in all worlds, from humans to gods, the Buddha is the most supreme.

「呪火第一斋，　　通音诸音本，王为人中尊，　　海为江河长，月为星中明，　　明照无过日，上下维诸方，　　及一切世间，从人乃至天，　　唯佛最第一。」

Then, after the World-Honored One had spoken the blessing for the brahmin Brahma, he rose from his seat and left. He stayed in the country of Misala for several days, then put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and traveled to the country of Savatthi. He continued on and arrived at Savatthi, where he stayed at the Jeta Grove Anathapindika's Park. Then, when many bhikkhus were begging for alms in Savatthi, they heard that the brahmin Brahma of Misala had asked the Buddha a question in verse and then passed away. Having heard this, the bhikkhus finished their meal, and after midday, they put away their robes and bowls, washed their hands and feet, put their sitting mats over their shoulders, went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, stood to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! We many bhikkhus put on our robes in the morning, took our alms bowls, and entered Savatthi to beg for alms. We heard that the brahmin Brahma of Misala had asked the Buddha a question in verse and then passed away. World-Honored One! Where has he gone? Where has he been reborn? What will be his future state?"

于是，世尊为梵志梵摩说呪愿已，从座起去，弥萨罗国住经数日，摄衣持钵，则便游行至舍卫国，展转前进，到舍卫国，住胜林给孤独园。于是，众多比丘舍卫乞食时，闻彼弥萨罗梵志梵摩以偈问佛事，彼便命终。诸比丘闻已，食讫，中后收举衣钵，澡洗手足，以尼师檀著于肩上，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却住一面，白曰：「世尊！我等众多比丘平旦著衣，持钵入舍卫乞食时，闻彼弥萨罗梵志梵摩以偈问佛事，彼便命终。世尊！彼至何处，为生何许，后世云何？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikkhus! The brahmin Brahma has gained great benefit. He understood the Dharma at the last moment and did not trouble me for the sake of the Dharma. Bhikkhus! The brahmin Brahma has exhausted the five lower fetters, and he has been reborn in a place where he will attain parinirvana. He has attained the non-retrogressive state and will not return to this world." At that time, the World-Honored One prophesied that Brahma would attain the state of a non-returner.

世尊答曰：「比丘！梵志梵摩极有大利，最后知法，为法故不烦劳我。比丘！梵志梵摩五下分结尽，生彼得般涅槃，得不退法，不还此世。」尔时，世尊记说梵摩得阿那含。

The Buddha spoke thus. The brahmin Brahma and the bhikkhus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说如是。梵志梵摩及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The tenth chapter on the Brahma Sutra is completed.
