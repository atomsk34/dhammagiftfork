Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 13 根本分别品

162. （一六二）分别六界经

Parallel to: MN 140

distinguishing the six elements and sense bases observing dharmas
hot springs, and forests Venerable Ānanda, speaking in the
meditation chamber meaningful conduct, Kūluśa, non-disputation parrots, distinguishing karma

分别六界、处　　观法、温泉林释中禅室尊　　阿难说、意行拘楼瘦无诤　　鹦鹉、分别业

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Magadha, and he went to the city of Rajagriha to spend the night. Then the World-Honored One went to a potter's house and said, "Potter! I wish to stay in your house for one night. Do you allow it?"

一时，佛游摩竭陀国，往诣王舍城宿。于是，世尊往至陶家，语曰：「陶师！我今欲寄陶屋一宿，汝见听耶？」

The potter replied, "I have no objection. But there is already a monk staying there. If he allows it, you may stay as you wish."

陶师答曰：「我无所违。然有一比丘先已住中，若彼听者，欲住随意。」

At that time, Venerable Pukkasati was already staying in the potter's house. Then the World-Honored One left the potter's house and entered the potter's shed, and said to Venerable Pukkasati, "Monk! I wish to stay in the potter's house for one night. Do you allow it?"

尔时，尊者弗迦逻娑利先已在彼住陶屋中。于是，世尊出陶师家入彼陶屋，语尊者弗迦逻娑利曰：「比丘！我今欲寄陶屋一宿，汝见听耶？」

Venerable Pukkasati replied, "Sir! I have no objection. But the grass seat in the potter's shed is already spread. If you wish to stay, you may do as you please."

尊者弗迦逻娑利答曰：「君！我无所违。且此陶屋草座已敷，君欲住者，自可随意。」

At that time, the World-Honored One came out of the potter's house to wash his feet, then returned inside, spread his sitting mat on the grass seat, and sat cross-legged in meditation, silently and peacefully, for the whole night. Venerable Pukkasati also sat cross-legged in meditation, silently and peacefully, for the whole night. Then the World-Honored One thought, "This monk's dwelling is very peaceful and wonderful! I should now ask him: 'Who is your teacher? Who did you go forth with to study the path and receive the Dharma?'"

尔时，世尊从彼陶屋出外洗足讫，还入内，于草座上敷尼师檀，结跏趺坐，竟夜默然靖坐定意，尊者弗迦逻娑利亦竟夜默然靖坐定意。彼时，世尊而作是念：「此比丘住止寂靖，甚奇！甚特！我今宁可问彼比丘：『汝师是谁？依谁出家学道受法？』」

Having thought this, the World-Honored One asked, "Monk! Who is your teacher? Who did you go forth with to study the path and receive the Dharma?"

世尊念已，问曰：「比丘！汝师是谁？依谁出家学道受法？」

Venerable Pukkasati replied, "Virtuous one! There is a śramaṇa Gautama, a son of the Śākya clan, who has abandoned his Śākya family, shaved his beard and hair, donned the saffron robes, gone forth from home to homelessness, and awakened to unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. He is my teacher, and I have gone forth to study the path and receive the Dharma under him."

尊者弗迦逻娑利答曰：「贤者！有沙门瞿昙释种子，舍释宗族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道，觉无上正尽觉，彼是我师，依彼出家学道受法。」

The World-Honored One then asked again, "Monk! Have you ever seen your teacher?"

世尊即复问曰：「比丘！曾见师耶？」

Venerable Pukkasati replied, "I have not seen him."

尊者弗迦逻娑利答曰：「不见。」

The World-Honored One asked, "If you had seen your teacher, would you recognize him?"

世尊问曰：「若见师者，为识不耶？」

Venerable Pukkasati replied, "I do not recognize him. However, sir, I have heard that the World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the One with Perfect Wisdom and Conduct, the Well-Gone One, the Knower of the World, the Unsurpassed One, the Trainer of Those to Be Tamed, the Teacher of Gods and Humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One, is my teacher. I have gone forth and studied the Dharma under him."

尊者弗迦逻娑利答曰：「不识。然，贤者！我闻世尊、如来、无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐，彼是我师，依彼出家学道受法。」

At that time, the World-Honored One also had this thought: "This noble son has gone forth and studied the Dharma under me. Should I now teach the Dharma to him?"

彼时，世尊复作是念：「此族姓子依我出家学道受法，我今宁可为说法耶？」

Having thought this, the World-Honored One said to Venerable Pukkasati, "Bhikṣu! I will teach you the Dharma, which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with meaning and expression, complete and pure, revealing the holy life. It is called the analysis of the six elements. Listen carefully and keep it in mind."

世尊念已，语尊者弗迦逻娑利曰：「比丘！我为汝说法，初善、中善、竟亦善，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行，谓分别六界，汝当谛听，善思念之。」

Venerable Pukkasati replied, "Yes."

尊者弗迦逻娑利答曰：「唯然。」

The Buddha told him, "Bhikṣu! There are six aggregates, six sense bases, eighteen mental activities, and four abodes. If one abides in them, one does not hear of distressing matters. Having not heard of distressing matters, one's mind is not hateful, sorrowful, or weary, and one is not terrified. There is such teaching, non-negligent wisdom, guarding the truth, and nurturing generosity. Bhikṣu! One should study the supreme, study the ultimate, and analyze the six elements. Thus, bhikṣu! There are six aggregates. What is meant by this? Namely, the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. Bhikṣu! There are six aggregates. This is why it is said.

佛告彼曰：「比丘！人有六界聚、六触处、十八意行、四住处。若有住彼，不闻忧戚事，不闻忧戚事已，意便不憎、不忧、不劳，亦不恐怖。如是有教，不放逸慧，守护真谛，长养惠施。比丘！当学最上，当学至寂，分别六界。如是，比丘！人有六界聚，此说何因？谓地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。比丘！人有六界聚者，因此故说。

Bhikṣu! There are six sense bases. What is meant by this? Namely, bhikṣu, the eye sense base sees forms, the ear sense base hears sounds, the nose sense base smells odors, the tongue sense base tastes flavors, the body sense base feels tangibles, and the mind sense base cognizes dharmas. Bhikṣu! There are six sense bases. This is why it is said.

「比丘！人有六触处，此说何因？谓比丘眼触见色，耳触闻声，鼻触嗅香，舌触尝味，身触觉触，意触知法。比丘！人有六触处者，因此故说。比丘！人有十八意行，此说何因？谓比丘眼见色，观色喜住，观色忧住，观色舍住，如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法，观法喜住，观法忧住，观法舍住。比丘！此六喜观、六忧观、六舍观，合已十八行。比丘！人有十八意行者，因此故说。

Bhikṣu! There are four abodes. What is meant by this? Namely, the abode of truth, the abode of wisdom, the abode of generosity, and the abode of cessation. Bhikṣu! There are four abodes. This is why it is said.

「比丘！人有四住处，此说何因？谓真谛住处、慧住处、施住处、息住处。比丘！人有四住处者，因此故说。云何比丘不放逸慧？若有比丘分别身界，今我此身有内地界而受于生，此为云何？谓发、毛、爪、齿、麤细肤、皮、肉、骨、筋、肾、心、肝、肺、脾、大肠、胃、粪。如斯之比，此身中余在内，内所摄坚，坚性住内，于生所受，是谓比丘内地界也。比丘！若有内地界及外地界者，彼一切总说地界，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非神也。如是慧观，知其如真，心不染著于此地界，是谓比丘不放逸慧。

Furthermore, the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu. If a bhikṣu analyzes the body element, he thinks, 'Now, this body of mine has an internal water element that is born. What is it? Namely, brain, membranes, tears, sweat, snot, saliva, pus, blood, fat, marrow, mucus, lymph, and urine. Such and so forth are the remaining internal parts of this body, which are internal and included within. The water nature moistens internally, born and received. This is called the internal water element, bhikṣu. If there is an internal water element and an external water element, they are all collectively called the water element. They are all not mine, I am not theirs, and they are not the spirit either. Knowing this through such wisdom and seeing it as it truly is, the mind is not attached to this water element. This is called the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu.

「复次，比丘不放逸慧。若有比丘分别身界，今我此身有内水界而受于生，此为云何？谓脑膜、眼泪、汗、涕、唾、脓、血、肪、髓、涎、淡、小便，如斯之比，此身中余在内，内所摄水，水性润内，于生所受，是谓比丘内水界也。比丘！若有内水界及外水界者，彼一切总说水界，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非神也。如是慧观，知其如真，心不染著于此水界，是谓比丘不放逸慧。

Furthermore, the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu. If a bhikṣu analyzes the body element, he thinks, 'Now, this body of mine has an internal fire element that is born. What is it? Namely, the heat of the body, the warmth of the body, the fever of the body, and the cooking of the body, which digests food and drink. Such and so forth are the remaining internal parts of this body, which are internal and included within. The fire nature is hot internally, born and received. This is called the internal fire element, bhikṣu. If there is an internal fire element and an external fire element, they are all collectively called the fire element. They are all not mine, I am not theirs, and they are not the spirit either. Knowing this through such wisdom and seeing it as it truly is, the mind is not attached to this fire element. This is called the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu.

「复次，比丘不放逸慧。若有比丘分别此身界，今我此身有内火界而受于生，此为云何？谓热身、暖身、烦闷身、温庄身，谓消饮食，如斯之比，此身中余在内，内所摄火，火性热内，于生所受，是谓比丘内火界也。比丘！若有内火界及外火界者，彼一切总说火界，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非神也。如是慧观，知其如真，心不染著于此火界，是谓比丘不放逸慧。

Furthermore, the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu. If a bhikṣu analyzes the body element, he thinks, 'Now, this body of mine has an internal wind element that is born. What is it? Namely, upper wind, lower wind, side wind, pulling wind, kicking wind, non-path wind, joint wind, exhalation wind, and inhalation wind. Such and so forth are the remaining internal parts of this body, which are internal and included within. The wind nature moves internally, born and received. This is called the internal wind element, bhikṣu. If there is an internal wind element and an external wind element, they are all collectively called the wind element. They are all not mine, I am not theirs, and they are not the spirit either. Knowing this through such wisdom and seeing it as it truly is, the mind is not attached to this wind element. This is called the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu.

「复次，比丘不放逸慧。若有比丘分别身界，今我此身有内风界而受于生，此为云何？谓上风、下风、脇风、掣缩风、蹴风、非道风、节节风、息出风、息入风。如斯之比，此身中余在内，内所摄风，风性动内，于生所受，是谓比丘内风界也。比丘！若有内风界及外风界者，彼一切总说风界，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非神也。如是慧观，知其如真，心不染著于此风界，是谓比丘不放逸慧。

Furthermore, the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu. If a bhikṣu analyzes the body element, he thinks, 'Now, this body of mine has an internal space element that is born. What is it? Namely, the space of the eyes, the space of the ears, the space of the nose, the space of the mouth, the movement of the throat, which refers to eating, swallowing, and digesting food, and the slow swallowing and staying. If it goes down and comes out. Such and so forth are the remaining internal parts of this body, which are internal and included within. The space is not covered by flesh, skin, bones, and tendons. This is called the internal space element, bhikṣu. If there is an internal space element and an external space element, they are all collectively called the space element. They are all not mine, I am not theirs, and they are not the spirit either. Knowing this through such wisdom and seeing it as it truly is, the mind is not attached to this space element. This is called the non-negligent wisdom of a bhikṣu.

「复次，比丘不放逸慧。若有比丘分别身界，今我此身有内空界而受于生，此为云何？谓眼空、耳空、鼻空、口空、咽喉动摇，谓食噉含消，安徐咽住，若下过出。如斯之比，此身中余在内，内所摄空，在空不为肉、皮、骨、筋所覆，是谓比丘内空界也。比丘！若有内空界及外空界者，彼一切总说空界，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非神也。如是慧观，知其如真，心不染著于此空界，是谓比丘不放逸慧。

Bhikṣu! If a bhikṣu knows these five elements as they truly are, and having known them as they truly are, the mind is not attached to them and is liberated, then there is only remaining consciousness. What kind of consciousness is this? The consciousness of pleasure, the consciousness of pain, the consciousness of joy, the consciousness of sorrow, and the consciousness of equanimity. Bhikṣu! Because of the contact of pleasure, there arises the perception of pleasure. That perception is the perception of pleasure. Having perceived the perception of pleasure, one knows the perception of pleasure. If a bhikṣu extinguishes this contact of pleasure, having extinguished this contact of pleasure, if there is a perception of pleasure arising from the contact of pleasure, that also ceases, stops, and is known as coolness. Bhikṣu! Because of the contact of pain, there arises the perception of pain. That perception is the perception of pain. Having perceived the perception of pain, one knows the perception of pain. If a bhikṣu extinguishes this contact of pain, having extinguished this contact of pain, if there is a perception of pain arising from the contact of pain, that also ceases, stops, and is known as coolness. Bhikṣu! Because of the contact of joy, there arises the perception of joy. That perception is the perception of joy. Having perceived the perception of joy, one knows the perception of joy. If a bhikṣu extinguishes this contact of joy, having extinguished this contact of joy, if there is a perception of joy arising from the contact of joy, that also ceases, stops, and is known as coolness.

「比丘！若有比丘于此五界知其如真，知如真已，心不染彼而解脱者，唯有余识，此何等识？乐识、苦识、喜识、忧识、舍识。比丘！因乐更乐故生乐觉，彼觉乐觉，觉乐觉已，即知觉乐觉。若有比丘灭此乐更乐，灭此乐更乐已，若有从乐更乐生乐觉者，彼亦灭息止，知已冷也。比丘！因苦更乐故生苦觉，彼觉苦觉，觉苦觉已，即知觉苦觉。若有比丘灭此苦更乐，灭此苦更乐已，若有从苦更乐生苦觉者，彼亦灭息止，知已冷也。比丘！因喜更乐故生喜觉，彼觉喜觉，觉喜觉已，即知觉喜觉。若有比丘灭此喜更乐，灭此喜更乐已，若有从喜更乐生喜觉者，彼亦灭息止，知已冷也。

Bhikṣu! Because of the contact of sorrow, there arises the perception of sorrow. That perception is the perception of sorrow. Having perceived the perception of sorrow, one knows the perception of sorrow. If a bhikṣu extinguishes this contact of sorrow, having extinguished this contact of sorrow, if there is a perception of sorrow arising from the contact of sorrow, that also ceases, stops, and is known as coolness. Bhikṣu! Because of the contact of equanimity, there arises the perception of equanimity. That perception is the perception of equanimity. Having perceived the perception of equanimity, one knows the perception of equanimity. If a bhikṣu extinguishes this contact of equanimity, having extinguished this contact of equanimity, if there is a perception of equanimity arising from the contact of equanimity, that also ceases, stops, and is known as coolness. Bhikṣu! Because of various contacts, there arise various perceptions. Having extinguished various contacts, various perceptions also cease. He knows that this perception arises from contact, that contact is the root of contact, the practice of contact, arising from contact, with contact as the foremost, and based on contact.

「比丘！因忧更乐故生忧觉，彼觉忧觉，觉忧觉已，即知觉忧觉。若有比丘灭此忧更乐，灭此忧更乐已，若有从忧更乐生忧觉者，彼亦灭息止，知已冷也。比丘！因舍更乐故生舍觉，彼觉舍觉，觉舍觉已，即知觉舍觉。若有比丘灭此舍更乐，灭此舍更乐已，若有从舍更乐生舍觉者，彼亦灭息止，知已冷也。比丘！彼彼更乐故生彼彼觉，灭彼彼更乐已，彼彼觉亦灭，彼知此觉从更乐，更乐本，更乐习，从更乐生，以更乐为首，依更乐行。

Bhikṣu! It is like a fire-drill, because of the friction of the drill and the person's effort, fire is produced. Bhikṣu! Various forests and trees are separated and scattered. If fire is produced from them, the fire is hotter than the wood. When it completely ceases and stops, the wood becomes cool. In the same way, bhikṣu! Because of various contacts, there arise various perceptions. When various contacts cease, various perceptions also cease. He knows that this perception arises from contact, that contact is the root of contact, the practice of contact, arising from contact, with contact as the foremost, and based on contact. If a bhikṣu is not attached to these three perceptions and is liberated, then that bhikṣu only remains in equanimity, which is extremely pure. Bhikṣu! That bhikṣu thinks: 'I move this pure equanimity into the immeasurable space, cultivate this mind, rely on it, abide in it, establish it, take it as an object, and bind it to it. I move this pure equanimity into the immeasurable consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, cultivate this mind, rely on it, abide in it, establish it, take it as an object, and bind it to it.'

「比丘！犹如火母，因钻及人方便热相故，而生火也。比丘！彼彼众多林木相离分散，若从彼生火，火数热于生数受，彼都灭止息，则冷樵木也。如是，比丘！彼彼更乐故生彼彼觉，灭彼彼更乐故彼彼觉亦灭，彼知此觉从更乐，更乐本，更乐习，从更乐生，以更乐为首，依更乐行。若比丘不染此三觉而解脱者，彼比丘唯存于舍，极清净也。比丘！彼比丘作是念：『我此清净舍，移入无量空处，修如是心，依彼、住彼、立彼、缘彼、系缚于彼。我此清净舍，移入无量识处、无所有处、非有想非无想处，修如是心，依彼、住彼、立彼、缘彼、系缚于彼。』

Bhikṣu! It is like a skilled goldsmith who burns gold in a fire, forges it to be extremely thin, and repeatedly adds fire to refine it, making it extremely soft and radiant. Bhikṣu! This gold, when repeatedly refined by the goldsmith in a fire, is made extremely soft and radiant. Then the goldsmith can make whatever he wishes, such as silk brocade, new clothes, finger rings, bracelets, necklaces, and jeweled garlands. In the same way, bhikṣu! That bhikṣu thinks: 'I move this pure equanimity into the immeasurable space, cultivate this mind, rely on it, abide in it, establish it, take it as an object, and bind it to it. I move this pure equanimity into the immeasurable consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, cultivate this mind, rely on it, abide in it, establish it, take it as an object, and bind it to it.'

「比丘！犹工炼金上妙之师，以火烧金，锻令极薄，又以火燷，数数足火熟炼令净，极使柔软而有光明。比丘！此金者，于金师以数数足火熟炼令净，极使柔软而有光明已，彼金师者，随所施设，或𦈐缯彩，严饰新衣，指镮、臂钏、璎珞、宝鬘，随意所作。如是，比丘！彼比丘作是念：『我此清净舍移入无量空处，修如是心，依彼、住彼、立彼、缘彼、系缚于彼。我此清净舍移入无量识处、无所有处、非有想非无想处，修如是心，依彼、住彼、立彼、缘彼、系缚于彼。』

That bhikṣu further thinks: 'My pure equanimity relies on the immeasurable space, therefore it is conditioned. If it is conditioned, then it is impermanent. If it is impermanent, then it is suffering. If it is suffering, then I know suffering. Knowing suffering, I will not move from here to enter the immeasurable space. My pure equanimity relies on the immeasurable consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, therefore it is conditioned. If it is conditioned, then it is impermanent. If it is impermanent, then it is suffering. If it is suffering, then I know suffering. Knowing suffering, I will not move from here to enter the immeasurable consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception.' Bhikṣu! If a bhikṣu observes these four places with wisdom, knows them as they truly are, and does not accomplish them, does not move into them, then at that time he is not conditioned, does not think of existence or non-existence. He knows the last perception of the body, knows the last perception of the body, knows the last perception of life, knows the last perception of life, and when the body perishes and life ends, the end of life, all perceptions cease and stop, and he knows the end of cold.

「彼比丘复作是念：『我此清净舍，依无量空处者，故是有为。若有为者，则是无常，若无常者，即是苦也。若是苦者，便知苦，知苦已，彼此舍不复移入无量空处。我此清净舍，依无量识处、无所有处、非有想非无想处者，故是有为。若有为者，则是无常，若无常者，即是苦也。若是苦者，便知苦，知苦已，彼此舍不复移入无量识处、无所有处、非有想非无想处。』比丘！若有比丘于此四处以慧观之，知其如真，心不成就，不移入者。彼于尔时不复有为，亦无所思，谓有及无，彼受身最后觉，则知受身最后觉，受命最后觉，则知受命最后觉，身坏命终，寿命已讫，彼所觉一切灭息止，知至冷也。

Bhikṣu! It is like a burning lamp, which depends on oil and wick. If no one adds more oil and continues the wick, then the previous one will be extinguished and not continue. In the same way, bhikṣu! The last perception of the body knows the last perception of the body, the last perception of life knows the last perception of life, when the body perishes and life ends, the end of life, all perceptions cease and stop, and he knows the end of cold. Bhikṣu! This is called the first true wisdom of a bhikṣu, namely, the ultimate cessation, the bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints accomplishes it, and accomplishes the first true wisdom. Bhikṣu! This liberation abides in true reality, and does not move. True reality means according to the Dharma. False speech means false Dharma. Bhikṣu! Accomplishes that first true reality.

「比丘！譬如燃灯，因油因炷，彼若无人更增益油，亦不续炷，是为前已灭讫，后不相续，无所复受。如是，比丘！受身最后觉，则知受身最后觉，受命最后觉，则知受命最后觉，身坏命终，寿命已讫，彼所觉一切灭息止，知至冷也。比丘！是谓比丘第一正慧，谓至究竟灭讫，漏尽比丘成就于彼，成就第一正慧处。比丘！此解脱住真谛，得不移动，真谛者，谓如法也，妄言者，谓虚妄法。比丘！成就彼第一真谛处。

Bhikṣu! That bhikṣu, if he has enemies, at that time lets go, spits out, liberates, and extinguishes them. Bhikṣu! This is called the first true wisdom of a bhikṣu, namely, the ultimate cessation of all worldly things, without desire, extinction, cessation, and stopping. Bhikṣu! Accomplishes it, and accomplishes the first true wisdom. Bhikṣu! That bhikṣu's mind is defiled by desire, anger, and delusion, and cannot be liberated. Bhikṣu! All desire, anger, and delusion are exhausted, without desire, extinction, cessation, and stopping, and attains the first cessation. Bhikṣu! Accomplishes it, and accomplishes the first cessation.

「比丘！彼比丘施说施若本必有怨家，彼于尔时放舍、吐离、解脱、灭讫。比丘！是谓比丘第一正惠施，谓舍离一切世尽，无欲、灭、息、止，比丘成就于彼，成就第一惠施处。比丘！彼比丘心为欲、恚、痴所秽，不得解脱。比丘！此一切婬、怒、痴尽，无欲、灭、息、止，得第一息。比丘！成就彼者成就第一息处。

"Bhikṣu! I is self-elevation, I will be is also self-elevation, I will not be is also self-elevation, I will have form is also self-elevation, I will have formless is also self-elevation, I will not have form and not formless is also self-elevation, I will have perception is also self-elevation, I will have no perception is also self-elevation, I will not have perception and not no perception is also self-elevation. This is arrogance, this is pride, this is indulgence. Bhikṣu! If there is no all this self-elevation, arrogance, pride, and indulgence, the mind is called cessation. Bhikṣu! If the mind ceases, then there is no hatred, no sorrow, no toil, no fear. Why? Because that bhikṣu accomplishes the Dharma, and there is no more hatred to be spoken of. If there is no hatred, then there is no sorrow, no sorrow, then no toil, no toil, then no fear. Because of no fear, one should attain parinirvāṇa, with birth ended, the holy life established, what was to be done is done, no more future lives, knowing it as it really is."

「比丘！我者是自举，我当有是亦自举，我当非有非无是亦自举，我当色有是亦自举，我当无色有是亦自举，我当非有色非无色是亦自举，我当有想是亦自举，我当无想是亦自举，我当非有想非无想是亦自举。是贡高、是憍慠、是放逸，比丘！若无此一切自举、贡高、憍慠、放逸者，意谓之息。比丘！若意息者，便不憎、不忧、不劳、不怖。所以者何？彼比丘成就法故，不复有可说憎者。若不憎则不忧，不忧则不愁，不愁则不劳，不劳则不怖，因不怖便当般涅槃，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。」

Having spoken this Dharma, the Venerable Phālguna Sālī was far from defilements and impurities, and the Dharma eye was born. Then the Venerable Phālguna Sālī saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, realized the pure Dharma, cut off doubts, crossed over perplexity, had no other masters, did not depend on others, was without hesitation, had attained the fruit of realization, and attained fearlessness in the World-Honored One's Dharma. He then rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and said: "World-Honored One! I repent. Well-Gone One! I confess. Like a fool, like a fool, like one who is not settled, like one who does not understand well, not recognizing good fields, I was unable to know myself. Why is that? Because I called the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One my lord. May the World-Honored One please allow me to repent. Having repented, I will not do it again in the future."

说此法已，尊者弗迦逻娑利远尘离垢，诸法法眼生。于是，尊者弗迦逻娑利见法得法，觉白净法，断疑度惑，更无余尊，不复由他，无有犹豫，已住果证，于世尊法得无所畏，即从坐起，稽首佛足，白曰：「世尊！我悔过。善逝！我自首。如愚如痴，如不定，如不善解，不识良田，不能自知。所以者何？以我称如来、无所著、等正觉为君也。唯愿世尊听我悔过，我悔过已，后不更作。」

The World-Honored One said: "Bhikṣu! You are indeed foolish, you are indeed unsettled, you do not understand well, to call the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One your lord. Bhikṣu! If you can repent, see, confess, and guard against doing it again, then in the noble Dharma and Vinaya, it will be beneficial and not harmful. That is, to be able to repent, see, confess, and guard against doing it again."

世尊告曰：「比丘！汝实愚痴，汝实不定，汝不善解，谓称如来、无所著、等正觉为君也。比丘！若汝能自悔过，见已发露，护不更作者，比丘！如是则于圣法、律中益而不损，谓能自悔过，见已发露，护不更作。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The Venerable Phālguna Sālī, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者弗迦逻娑利闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
