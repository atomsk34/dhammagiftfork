MĀ 163

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 13 根本分别品

163. （一六三）分别六处经

Parallel to: MN 137

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I shall teach you the Dharma, which is wonderful in the beginning, wonderful in the middle, and wonderful in the end, with meaning and phrasing, complete and pure, revealing the holy life. It is called the Discourse on the Analysis of the Six Sense Bases. Listen well, listen well, and consider it carefully."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我当为汝说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行，谓分别六处经。谛听，谛听，善思念之。」

At that time, the bhikṣus said, "World-Honored One! We shall receive the teaching."

时，诸比丘白曰：「世尊！唯当受教。」

The Buddha said, "You should know the six sense bases as internal. You should know the six sense objects as internal. You should know the eighteen mental activities as internal. You should know the thirty-six kinds of suffering as internal. Within that, you should cut them off and accomplish them. You should know the immeasurable teachings as internal. The three foundations of mindfulness are what the noble ones practice. Having practiced what the noble ones practice, the assembly can be taught. The unsurpassed tamer of beings, the tamer of beings, goes to all directions. This is called the matter of the Discourse on the Analysis of the Six Sense Bases.

佛言：「汝等六处当知内也，六更乐处当知内，十八意行当知内，三十六刀当知内，于中断彼成就是，无量说法当知内，三意止，谓圣人所习。圣人所习已，众可教，无上调御士者，调御士趣一切方，是谓分别六处经事。

What is the reason for saying 'the six sense bases should be known as internal'? It means the sense base of the eye, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. The reason for saying 'the six sense bases should be known as internal' is for this reason.

「六处当知内者，此何因说？谓眼处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意处，六处当知内者，因此故说。

What is the reason for saying 'the six sense objects should be known as internal'? It means the sense object of the eye for seeing forms, the sense object of the ear for hearing sounds, the sense object of the nose for smelling fragrances, the sense object of the tongue for tasting flavors, the sense object of the body for feeling tactile objects, and the sense object of the mind for knowing phenomena. The reason for saying 'the six sense objects should be known as internal' is for this reason.

「六更乐处当知内者，此何因说？谓眼更乐为见色，耳更乐为闻声，鼻更乐为嗅香，舌更乐为尝味，身更乐为觉触，意更乐为知法，六更乐处当知内者，因此故说。

What is the reason for saying 'the eighteen mental activities should be known as internal'? It means that when a monk sees forms with the eye, he dwells in joy, sorrow, or equanimity in regard to forms. Similarly, with regard to sounds, smells, tastes, tactile objects, and phenomena known by the mind, he dwells in joy, sorrow, or equanimity. These are called the eighteen mental activities: six joys, six sorrows, and six equanimities. The reason for saying 'the eighteen mental activities should be known as internal' is for this reason.

「十八意行当知内者，此何因说？比丘者，眼见色已，分别色喜住，分别色忧住，分别色舍住，如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法已，分别法喜住，分别法忧住，分别法舍住，是谓分别六喜、分别六忧、分别六舍，总说十八意行，十八意行当知内者，因此故说。

What is the reason for saying 'the thirty-six knives should be known as internal'? It means there are six joys based on attachment, six joys based on non-attachment, six sorrows based on attachment, six sorrows based on non-attachment, six equanimities based on attachment, and six equanimities based on non-attachment.

「三十六刀当知内者，此何因说？有六喜依著，有六喜依无欲，有六忧依著，有六忧依无欲，有六舍依著，有六舍依无欲。

What are the six joys based on attachment? What are the six joys based on non-attachment? When one sees forms with the eye and gives rise to joy, it should be known as twofold: based on attachment or based on non-attachment. What is joy based on attachment? When one knows forms as desirable, thinks of them with affection, and is associated with the pleasure of desire, one desires to obtain what has not been obtained, and upon obtaining it, one recollects and gives rise to joy. This kind of joy is called joy based on attachment. What is joy based on non-attachment? Knowing that forms are impermanent, subject to change, and will cease, one desires to be free from them, and one wishes to extinguish and pacify them. Recollecting that all forms in the past and present are impermanent, suffering, and subject to cessation, one gives rise to joy. This kind of joy is called joy based on non-attachment. Similarly, when one knows sounds, smells, tastes, tactile objects, and phenomena with the mind and gives rise to joy, it should be known as twofold: based on attachment or based on non-attachment. What is joy based on attachment? When one knows phenomena as desirable, thinks of them with affection, and is associated with the pleasure of desire, one desires to obtain what has not been obtained, and upon obtaining it, one recollects and gives rise to joy. This kind of joy is called joy based on attachment. What is joy based on non-attachment? Knowing that phenomena are impermanent, subject to change, and will cease, one desires to be free from them, and one wishes to extinguish and pacify them. Recollecting that all phenomena in the past and present are impermanent, suffering, and subject to cessation, one gives rise to joy. This kind of joy is called joy based on non-attachment.

「云何六喜依著？云何六喜依无欲？眼见色已生喜，当知二种，或依著，或依无欲。云何喜依著？眼知色可喜、意念、爱色、欲相应乐，未得者欲得，已得者忆已生喜，如是喜，是谓喜依著。云何喜依无欲？知色无常、变易，尽、无欲、灭、息，前及今一切色无常、苦、灭法，忆已生喜，如是喜，是谓喜依无欲。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法已生喜，当知二种，或依著，或依无欲。云何喜依著？意知法可喜、意念、爱法、欲相应乐，未得者欲得，已得者忆已生喜，如是喜，是谓喜依著。云何喜依无欲？知法无常、变易，尽、无欲、灭、息，前及今一切法无常、苦、灭法，忆已生喜，如是喜，是谓喜依无欲。

What are the six sorrows based on attachment? What are the six sorrows based on non-attachment? When one sees forms with the eye and gives rise to sorrow, it should be known as twofold: based on attachment or based on non-attachment. What is sorrow based on attachment? When one knows forms as desirable, thinks of them with affection, and is associated with the pleasure of desire, one desires to obtain what has not been obtained, and upon obtaining it, one recollects and gives rise to sorrow when it is past, scattered, destroyed, changed, or lost. This kind of sorrow is called sorrow based on attachment. What is sorrow based on non-attachment? Knowing that forms are impermanent, subject to change, and will cease, one desires to be free from them, and one wishes to extinguish and pacify them. Recollecting that all forms in the past and present are impermanent, suffering, and subject to cessation, one thinks, 'When will I attain the state of practice of the noble ones?' This is the supreme touch of the aspiration for fear, and the knowledge of suffering gives rise to sorrow. This kind of sorrow is called sorrow based on non-attachment. Similarly, when one knows sounds, smells, tastes, tactile objects, and phenomena with the mind and gives rise to sorrow, it should be known as twofold: based on attachment or based on non-attachment. What is sorrow based on attachment? When one knows phenomena as desirable, thinks of them with affection, and is associated with the pleasure of desire, one desires to obtain what has not been obtained, and upon obtaining it, one recollects and gives rise to sorrow when it is past, scattered, destroyed, changed, or lost. This kind of sorrow is called sorrow based on attachment. What is sorrow based on non-attachment? Knowing that phenomena are impermanent, subject to change, and will cease, one desires to be free from them, and one wishes to extinguish and pacify them. Recollecting that all phenomena in the past and present are impermanent, suffering, and subject to cessation, one thinks, 'When will I attain the state of practice of the noble ones?' This is the supreme touch of the aspiration for fear, and the knowledge of suffering gives rise to sorrow. This kind of sorrow is called sorrow based on non-attachment.

「云何六忧依著？云何六忧依无欲？眼见色已生忧，当知二种，或依著，或依无欲。云何忧依著？眼知色可喜、意念、爱色、欲相应乐，未得者不得，已得者过去、散坏、灭、变易、生忧，如是忧，是谓忧依著。云何忧依无欲？知色无常、变易，尽、无欲、灭、息，前及今一切色无常、苦、灭法，忆已作是念：『我何时彼处成就游，谓：处诸圣人成就游。』是为上具触愿恐怖，知苦忧生忧。如是忧，是谓忧依无欲。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法已生忧，当知二种，或依著，或依无欲。云何忧依著？意知法可喜、意念、爱法、欲相应乐，未得者不得，已得者过去、散坏、灭、变易、生忧，如是忧，是谓忧依著。云何忧依无欲？知法无常、变易，尽、无欲、灭、息，前及今一切法无常、苦、灭法，忆已作是念：『我何时彼处成就游？谓：处诸圣人成就游。』是为上具触愿恐怖，知苦忧生忧。如是忧，是谓忧依无欲。

What are the six equanimities based on attachment? What are the six equanimities based on non-attachment? When one sees forms with the eye and gives rise to equanimity, it should be known as twofold: based on attachment or based on non-attachment. What is equanimity based on attachment? When one knows forms as giving rise to equanimity, one is equal-minded, not well-versed, without wisdom, ignorant, and an ordinary person. One has equanimity towards forms, and is not free from forms. This is called equanimity based on attachment. What is equanimity based on non-attachment? Knowing that forms are impermanent, subject to change, and will cease, one desires to be free from them, and one wishes to extinguish and pacify them. Recollecting that all forms in the past and present are impermanent, suffering, and subject to cessation, one abides in equanimity. If one cultivates equanimity with utmost sincerity, this is called equanimity based on non-attachment. Similarly, when one knows sounds, smells, tastes, tactile objects, and phenomena with the mind and gives rise to equanimity, it should be known as twofold: based on attachment or based on non-attachment. What is equanimity based on attachment? When one knows phenomena as giving rise to equanimity, one is equal-minded, not well-versed, without wisdom, ignorant, and an ordinary person. One has equanimity towards phenomena, and is not free from phenomena. This is called equanimity based on attachment. What is equanimity based on non-attachment? Knowing that phenomena are impermanent, subject to change, and will cease, one desires to be free from them, and one wishes to extinguish and pacify them. Recollecting that all phenomena in the past and present are impermanent, suffering, and subject to cessation, one abides in equanimity. If one cultivates equanimity with utmost sincerity, this is called equanimity based on non-attachment. These are the six joys based on attachment, the six joys based on non-attachment, the six sorrows based on attachment, the six sorrows based on non-attachment, the six equanimities based on attachment, and the six equanimities based on non-attachment. In total, there are thirty-six kinds of cutting. It should be known that these are internal. Therefore, it is said.

「云何六舍依著？云何六舍依无欲？眼见色已生舍，当知二种，或依著，或依无欲。云何舍依著？眼知色生舍，彼平等、不多闻、无智慧、愚、痴、凡夫，为色有舍，不离色，是谓舍依著。云何舍依无欲？知色无常、变易，尽、无欲、灭、息，前及今一切色无常、苦、灭法，忆已舍住。若有至意修习舍，是谓舍依无欲，如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法已生舍，当知二种，或依著，或依无欲。云何舍依著？意知法生舍，平等、不多闻、无智慧、愚、痴、凡夫，为法有舍，不离法，是谓舍依著。云何舍依无欲？意知法无常、变易，尽、无欲、灭、息，前及今一切法无常、苦、灭法，忆已舍住。若有至意修习舍，是谓舍依无欲。是为六喜依著、六喜依无欲、六忧依著、六忧依无欲、六舍依著、六舍依无欲，总说三十六刀，当知内者，因此故说。

Among them, what is meant by 'attaining the cessation of those'? It is said that these six joys are based on non-attachment, taken, relied upon, and abided in. These six joys based on attachment are extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated. These six sorrows are based on non-attachment, taken, relied upon, and abided in. These six sorrows based on attachment are extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated. These six equanimities are based on non-attachment, taken, relied upon, and abided in. These six equanimities based on attachment are extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated. These six sorrows are based on non-attachment, taken, relied upon, and abided in. These six joys based on non-attachment are extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated. These six equanimities are based on non-attachment, taken, relied upon, and abided in. These six sorrows based on non-attachment are extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated.

「于中断彼成就是者，此何因说？谓此六喜依无欲，取是、依是、住是也。谓此六喜依著，灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也。谓此六忧依无欲，取是、依是、住是也。谓此六忧依著，灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也。谓此六舍依无欲，取是、依是、住是也。谓此六舍依著，灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也。谓此六忧依无欲，取是、依是、住是也。谓此六喜依无欲，灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也。谓此六舍依无欲，取是、依是、住是也。谓此六忧依无欲，灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也。

There is equanimity with immeasurable pleasure, with various pleasures, and there is equanimity with one pleasure, without various pleasures. What is equanimity with immeasurable pleasure, with various pleasures? If one gives rise to equanimity towards forms, sounds, smells, tastes, and tactile objects, this equanimity has immeasurable pleasure, with various pleasures. What is equanimity with one pleasure, without various pleasures? Equanimity based on the immeasurable space, the immeasurable consciousness, the nothingness, and the neither perception nor non-perception. This equanimity has one pleasure, without various pleasures. It is taken, relied upon, and abided in. Equanimity with immeasurable pleasure, with various pleasures is extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated. Equanimity with one pleasure, without various pleasures is taken, relied upon, and abided in. Equanimity with immeasurable pleasure, with various pleasures is extinguished, eliminated, and eliminated. Among them, what is meant by 'attaining the cessation of those'? It is said that this is the reason.

「有舍无量更乐，若干更乐，有舍一更乐，不若干更乐。云何有舍无量更乐，若干更乐？若舍为色、为声、为香、为味、为触，此舍无量更乐，若干更乐。云何舍一更乐，不若干更乐？谓舍或依无量空处，或依无量识处，或依无所有处，或依非有想非无想处，此舍一更乐，不若干更乐，谓此舍有一更乐，不若干更乐。取是、依是、住是也，谓此舍有无量更乐，若干更乐。灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也。取无量、依无量、住无量，谓此舍有一更乐，不若干更乐。取是、依是、住是也，谓此舍有无量更乐，若干更乐。灭彼、除彼、吐彼，如是断彼也，于中断彼成就是者，因此故说。

As for the immeasurable teachings of the Tathāgata, what is meant by 'should be known internally'? There are four disciples of the Tathāgata, who have superior practice, superior intention, superior mindfulness, and superior wisdom. They have eloquence and the foremost eloquence. They live for a hundred years. The Tathāgata teaches them the Dharma for a hundred years, except during times of eating, defecating, urinating, sleeping, resting, and gatherings. The Dharma taught by the Tathāgata, the words and phrases of the Dharma, and the contemplation of meaning, they quickly contemplate the meaning with wisdom, and do not ask the Tathāgata about the Dharma again. Why is that? Because the Tathāgata's teachings are inexhaustible and immeasurable. The words and phrases of the Dharma, and the contemplation of meaning, even if the four disciples live for a hundred years, it is like four skilled archers shooting arrows at the same time. They are well-trained, well-informed, and skillful. They quickly penetrate the past. In the same way, the World-Honored One has four disciples, who have superior practice, superior intention, superior mindfulness, and superior wisdom. They have eloquence and the foremost eloquence. They live for a hundred years. The Tathāgata teaches them the Dharma for a hundred years, except during times of eating, defecating, urinating, sleeping, resting, and gatherings. The Dharma taught by the Tathāgata, the words and phrases of the Dharma, and the contemplation of meaning, they quickly contemplate the meaning with wisdom, and do not ask the Tathāgata about the Dharma again. Why is that? Because the Tathāgata is inexhaustible and immeasurable. As for the immeasurable teachings of the Tathāgata, what is meant by 'should be known internally'? It is said that this is the reason.

「无量说法当知内者，此何因说？如来有四弟子，有增上行、有增上意、有增上念、有增上慧，有辩才成就第一辩才，寿活百岁，如来为彼说法满百年，除饮食时、大小便时、睡眠息时及聚会时，彼如来所说法，文句法句观义，以慧而速观义，不复更问于如来法。所以者何？如来说法无有极不可尽法，文句法句观义，乃至四弟子命终，犹如四种善射之人，挽彊俱发，善学善知，而有方便，速彻过去。如是，世尊有四弟子，有增上行、有增上意、有增上念、有增上慧，有辩才成就第一辩才，寿活百岁，如来为彼说法满百年，除饮食时、大小便时、睡眠息时及聚会时。彼如来所说法，文句法句观义，以慧而速观义，不复更问于如来法。所以者何？如来无极不可尽，无量说法当知内者，因此故说。

As for the three foundations of mindfulness, which are practiced by the noble ones and which, when practiced by the noble ones, can be taught to the assembly. What is meant by this? If the Tathāgata teaches the Dharma to his disciples out of compassion, seeking meaning and benefit, seeking peace and happiness, with a mind of loving-kindness and compassion, this is for their benefit, this is for their happiness, this is for their benefit and happiness. If those disciples are not respectful, do not practice accordingly, do not establish wisdom, their minds do not turn towards the Dharma and the next Dharma, they do not accept the true Dharma, they go against the teachings of the World-Honored One, and are unable to attain concentration, the World-Honored One does not worry or grieve about this. The World-Honored One simply abandons it without doing anything, always mindful and always wise. This is called the first foundation of mindfulness, which is practiced by the noble ones and which, when practiced by the noble ones, can be taught to the assembly.

「三意止，谓圣人所习，圣人所习已，众可教者。此何因说？若如来为弟子说法，怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，发慈悲心，是为饶益，是为快乐，是为饶益乐。若彼弟子而不恭敬，亦不顺行，不立于智，其心不趣向法、次法，不受正法，违世尊教，不能得定者，世尊不以此为忧戚也。但世尊舍无所为，常念常智，是谓第一意止，谓圣人所习，圣人所习已，众可教也。

Furthermore, if the Tathāgata teaches the Dharma to his disciples out of compassion, seeking meaning and benefit, seeking peace and happiness, with a mind of loving-kindness and compassion, this is for their benefit, this is for their happiness, this is for their benefit and happiness. If those disciples are respectful, practice accordingly, establish wisdom, their minds turn towards the Dharma and the next Dharma, they uphold the true Dharma, do not go against the teachings of the World-Honored One, and are able to attain concentration, the World-Honored One does not rejoice about this. The World-Honored One simply abandons it without doing anything, always mindful and always wise. This is called the second foundation of mindfulness, which is practiced by the noble ones and which, when practiced by the noble ones, can be taught to the assembly.

「复次，如来为弟子说法，怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，发慈悲心，是为饶益，是为快乐，是为饶益乐。若彼弟子恭敬顺行而立于智，其心归趣向法、次法，受持正法，不违世尊教，能得定者，世尊不以此为欢喜也。但世尊舍无所为，常念常智，是谓第二意止，谓圣人所习，圣人所习已，众可教也。

Furthermore, if the Tathāgata teaches the Dharma to his disciples out of compassion, seeking meaning and benefit, seeking peace and happiness, with a mind of loving-kindness and compassion, this is for their benefit, this is for their happiness, this is for their benefit and happiness. Some disciples may not be respectful, do not practice accordingly, do not establish wisdom, their minds do not turn towards the Dharma and the next Dharma, they do not uphold the true Dharma, go against the teachings of the World-Honored One, and are unable to attain concentration. Some disciples may be respectful, practice accordingly, establish wisdom, their minds turn towards the Dharma and the next Dharma, they uphold the true Dharma, do not go against the teachings of the World-Honored One, and are able to attain concentration. The World-Honored One does not worry or rejoice about this. The World-Honored One simply abandons it without doing anything, always mindful and always wise. This is called the third foundation of mindfulness. Which is practiced by the noble ones and which, when practiced by the noble ones, can be taught to the assembly.

「复次，如来为弟子说法，怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，发慈悲心，是为饶益，是为快乐，是为饶益乐。或有弟子而不恭敬，亦不顺行，不立于智，其心不趣向法、次法，不受正法，违世尊教，不能得定者。或有弟子恭敬顺行而立于智，其心归趣向法、次法，受持正法，不违世尊教，能得定者。世尊不以此为忧戚，亦不欢喜，但世尊舍无所为，常念常智，是谓第三意止。谓圣人所习，圣人所习已，众可教也，三意止，谓圣人所习。圣人所习已，众可教者，因此故说。

The Unsurpassed Tamer of Men means the tamer of men who goes to all directions. Why is this said? The tamer of men means the tamer of men who goes to one direction, either the east, south, west, or north; the tamer of elephants means the tamer of elephants who goes to one direction, either the east, south, west, or north; the tamer of horses means the tamer of horses who goes to one direction, either the east, south, west, or north; the tamer of oxen means the tamer of oxen who goes to one direction, either the east, south, west, or north. The Unsurpassed Tamer of Men means the tamer of men who goes to all directions. Among them, the one who internally has no form perception but externally perceives form is called the first direction. The one who internally has no form perception but externally perceives form is called the second direction. The one who dwells in the attainment of the body touch of the pure liberation is called the third direction. The one who transcends all perceptions of form, eliminates perceptions of resistance, does not think of various perceptions, and dwells in the attainment of the sphere of infinite space is called the fourth direction. The one who transcends the entire sphere of infinite space, the infinite consciousness, and dwells in the attainment of the sphere of infinite consciousness is called the fifth direction. The one who transcends the entire sphere of infinite consciousness, the nothingness, and dwells in the attainment of the sphere of nothingness is called the sixth direction. The one who transcends the entire sphere of nothingness, the neither perception nor non-perception, and dwells in the attainment of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception is called the seventh direction. The one who transcends the entire sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, the cessation of perception and feeling, and dwells in the attainment of the body touch of the cessation of perception and feeling, with wisdom observing the knowledge of the exhaustion of taints, is called the eighth direction. The Unsurpassed Tamer of Men means the tamer of men who goes to all directions. For this reason it is said.

「无上调御士者，调御士趣一切方者。此何因说？调御士者，此说调御士趣一方，或东方、或南方、或西方、或北方；调御象者，调御象趣一方，或东方，或南、西、北方；调御马者，调御马趣一方，或东方，或南、西、北方；调御牛者，调御牛趣一方，或东方，或南、西、北方也。无上调御士者，调御士趣一切方，于中方者色观色，是谓第一方。内无色想，外观色，是谓第二方。净解脱身触成就游，是谓第三方。度一切色想，灭有对想，不念若干想，无量空，是无量空处成就游，是谓第四方。度一切无量空处，无量识，是无量识处成就游，是谓第五方。度一切无量识处，无所有，是无所有处成就游，是谓第六方。度一切无所有处，非有想非无想，是非有想非无想处成就游，是谓第七方。度一切非有想非无想处，想知灭尽身触成就游，慧观漏尽断智，是谓第八方。无上调御士者，调御士趣一切方者，因此故说。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
