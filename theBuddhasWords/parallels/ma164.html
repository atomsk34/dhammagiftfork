MĀ 164

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 13 根本分别品

164. （一六四）分别观法经

Parallel to: MN 138

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I shall teach you the Dharma, which is wonderful in the beginning, wonderful in the middle, and wonderful in the end, with meaning and phrasing, complete and pure, revealing the holy life. Listen well and pay attention, I shall explain it for you."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我当为汝说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行，谓分别观法经。谛听，谛听，善思念之。」

At that time, the bhikṣus said, "World-Honored One! We shall listen attentively."

时，诸比丘白曰：「世尊！唯当受教。」

The Buddha said, "Bhikṣu! When you observe in this way, your mind will be scattered externally, not dwelling within, and you will be terrified without accepting it. Bhikṣu! When you observe in this way, your mind will not be scattered externally, dwelling within, not accepting and not being terrified. In this way, there will be no more birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is said to be the end of suffering." Having spoken thus, the Buddha rose from his seat, entered the room, and sat in meditation.

佛言：「比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心出外洒散，心不住内，不受而恐怖。比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心不出外不洒散，心住内，不受不恐怖，如是不复生、老、病、死，是说苦边。」佛说如是已即从坐起，入室燕坐。

Then the bhikṣus thought, "Venerable friends! Know that the World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning without elaborating, and then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation: 'Bhikṣu! When you observe in this way, your mind will be scattered externally, not dwelling within, and you will be terrified without accepting it. Bhikṣu! When you observe in this way, your mind will not be scattered externally, dwelling within, not accepting and not being terrified. In this way, there will be no more birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is said to be the end of suffering.'" They further thought, "Venerable friends! Who can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One?" They further thought, "Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise and virtuous ones. Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One. Venerable friends! Let us go together to Venerable Mahākātyāyana and request him to explain this meaning. If Venerable Mahākātyāyana explains it, we shall uphold it well."

于是，诸比丘便作是念：「诸贤！当知世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室燕坐：『比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心出外洒散，心不住内，不受而恐怖。比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心不出外不洒散，心住内，不受不恐怖，如是不复生、老、病、死，是说苦边。』」彼复作是念：「诸贤！谁能广分别世尊向所略说义？」彼复作是念：「尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。诸贤！共往诣尊者大迦旃延所，请说此义，若尊者大迦旃延为分别者，我等当善受持。」

Then the bhikṣus went to Venerable Mahākātyāyana, exchanged greetings, sat to one side, and said, "Venerable Mahākātyāyana! Know that the World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning without elaborating, and then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation: 'Bhikṣu! When you observe in this way, your mind will be scattered externally, not dwelling within, and you will be terrified without accepting it. Bhikṣu! When you observe in this way, your mind will not be scattered externally, dwelling within, not accepting and not being terrified. In this way, there will be no more birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is said to be the end of suffering.' We then thought, 'Venerable friends! Who can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One?' We further thought, 'Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise and virtuous ones. Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One.' We only wish that Venerable Mahākātyāyana would explain it out of compassion."

于是，诸比丘往诣尊者大迦旃延所，共相问讯，却坐一面，白曰：「尊者大迦旃延！当知世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室燕坐：『比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心出外洒散，心不住内，不受而恐怖。比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心不出外不洒散，心住内，不受不恐怖，如是不复生、老、病、死，是说苦边。』我等便作是念：『诸贤！谁能广分别世尊向所略说义？』我等复作是念：『尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。』唯愿尊者大迦旃延为慈愍故而广说之。」

Then Venerable Mahākātyāyana said, "Venerable friends! Listen to my explanation of a parable. The wise understand the meaning through parables. Venerable friends! It is like a person who wants to obtain the fruit. In order to obtain the fruit, he carries an axe into the forest. He sees a large tree with roots, trunk, joints, branches, leaves, flowers, and fruit. He does not touch the roots, trunk, joints, and fruit, but only touches the branches and leaves. The meaning you speak of is also like this. The World-Honored One is present. You should go to him and ask about this meaning. Why? Venerable friends! Know that the World-Honored One is the eye, the wisdom, the meaning, the Dharma, the lord of the Dharma, the leader of the Dharma, who speaks the true meaning, manifests all meanings, and is the World-Honored One. Venerable friends! You should go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning: 'World-Honored One! What is this? What is the meaning of this?' As the World-Honored One explains it, you should uphold it well."

尔时，尊者大迦旃延告曰：「诸贤！听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。诸贤！犹如有人欲得求实，为求实故，持斧入林，彼见大树成根、茎、节、枝、叶、花、实，彼人不触根、茎、节、实，但触枝叶。诸贤所说亦复如是，世尊现在，舍来就我而问此义。所以者何？诸贤！当知世尊是眼、是智、是义、是法、法主、法将，说真谛义，现一切义，由彼世尊。诸贤！应往诣世尊所而问此义：『世尊！此云何？此何义？』如世尊说者，诸贤等当善受持。」

The bhikṣus replied, "Yes, Venerable Mahākātyāyana! The World-Honored One is the eye, the wisdom, the meaning, the Dharma, the lord of the Dharma, the leader of the Dharma, who speaks the true meaning, manifests all meanings, and is the World-Honored One. We should go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning: 'World-Honored One! What is this? What is the meaning of this?' As the World-Honored One explains it, we will uphold it well. However, Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise and virtuous ones. Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning briefly explained by the World-Honored One. We only wish that Venerable Mahākātyāyana would explain it out of compassion."

时，诸比丘白曰：「唯然。尊者大迦旃延！世尊是眼、是智、是义、是法、法主、法将，说真谛义，现一切义，由彼世尊，我等应往诣世尊所而问此义：『世尊！此云何？此何义？』如世尊说者，我等当善受持。然尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。唯愿尊者大迦旃延为慈愍故而广说之。」

Venerable Mahākātyāyana said to the bhikṣus, "Venerable friends! Listen to what I say. Venerable friends! How does a bhikṣu's mind scatter outside? Venerable friends! When a bhikṣu sees forms with the eye, he recognizes the characteristics of forms, is attached to the characteristics of forms, and is bound by the characteristics of forms. His mind is scattered outside, scattered and scattered. Similarly, with regard to sounds, smells, tastes, tangibles, and dharmas known by the mind, he recognizes the characteristics of dharmas, is attached to the characteristics of dharmas, and is bound by the characteristics of dharmas. His mind is scattered outside, scattered and scattered. Venerable friends! In this way, a bhikṣu's mind scatters outside. Venerable friends! How does a bhikṣu's mind not scatter outside? Venerable friends! When a bhikṣu sees forms with the eye, he does not recognize the characteristics of forms, is not attached to the characteristics of forms, and is not bound by the characteristics of forms. The characteristics of forms do not bind his mind, and it does not scatter outside, scatter and scatter. Similarly, with regard to sounds, smells, tastes, tangibles, and dharmas known by the mind, he does not recognize the characteristics of dharmas, is not attached to the characteristics of dharmas, and is not bound by the characteristics of dharmas. The characteristics of dharmas do not bind his mind, and it does not scatter outside, scatter and scatter. Venerable friends! In this way, a bhikṣu's mind does not scatter outside.

尊者大迦旃延告诸比丘：「诸贤等！共听我所说。诸贤！云何比丘心出外洒散？诸贤！比丘眼见色，识食色相，识著色乐相，识缚色乐相，彼色相味结缚心出外洒散；如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法，识食法相，识著法乐相，识缚法乐相，彼法相味结缚心出外洒散。诸贤！如是比丘心出外洒散。诸贤！云何比丘心不出外洒散？诸贤！比丘眼见色，识不食色相，识不著色乐相，识不缚色乐相，彼色相味不结缚心，不出外洒散；如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意知法，识不食法相，识不著法乐相，识不缚法乐相，彼法相味不结缚心，不出外洒散。诸贤！如是比丘心不出外洒散。

Venerable friends! How does a bhikṣu's mind not dwell internally? Venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, detached from sensual desires, detached from unwholesome states, with directed thought and evaluation, dwells in the joy and bliss born of detachment, having attained the first absorption, his consciousness is attached to the taste of detachment, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, with the subsiding of directed thought and evaluation, with internal clarity and one-pointedness of mind, without directed thought, without evaluation, born of concentration, with joy and bliss, dwells having attained the second absorption, his consciousness is attached to the taste of concentration, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, detached from joy, detached from suffering, dwells in equanimity, mindfulness, and purity, having attained the third absorption, his consciousness is attached to the taste of equanimity and purity, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, with the cessation of pleasure and pain, and the earlier cessation of joy and displeasure, dwells in equanimity, mindfulness, and purity, having attained the fourth absorption, his consciousness is attached to the taste of equanimity and purity, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally.

「诸贤！云何比丘心不住内？诸贤！比丘离欲，离恶不善之法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，得初禅成就游。彼识著离味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。复次，诸贤！比丘觉、观已息，内静、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，得第二禅成就游。彼识著定味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。复次，诸贤！比丘离于喜欲，舍无求游，正念正智而身觉乐，谓圣所说，圣所舍、念、乐住、空，得第三禅成就游。彼识著无喜味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。复次，诸贤！比丘乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，得第四禅成就游。彼识著舍及念清净味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。

Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending all perceptions of form, perceptions of resistance, and perceptions of variety, with the perception of infinite space, dwells having attained the sphere of infinite space, his consciousness is attached to the taste of the knowledge of space, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending the sphere of infinite space, with the perception of infinite consciousness, dwells having attained the sphere of infinite consciousness, his consciousness is attached to the taste of the knowledge of consciousness, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending the sphere of infinite consciousness, with the perception of nothingness, dwells having attained the sphere of nothingness, his consciousness is attached to the taste of the knowledge of nothingness, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending the sphere of nothingness, with the perception of neither perception nor non-perception, dwells having attained the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, his consciousness is attached to the taste of the knowledge of neither perception nor non-perception, abides in it, is bound to it, and his consciousness does not dwell internally. Venerable friends! In this way, a bhikṣu's mind does not dwell internally.

「复次，诸贤！比丘度一切色想，灭有对想，不念若干想，无量空，是无量空处成就游，彼识著空智味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。复次，诸贤！比丘度一切无量空处，无量识，是无量识处成就游，彼识著识智味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。复次，诸贤！比丘度一切无量识处，无所有，是无所有处成就游，彼识著无所有智味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。复次，诸贤！比丘度一切无所有处，非有想非无想，是非有想非无想处成就游，彼识著无想智味，依彼住彼，缘彼缚彼，识不住内。诸贤！如是比丘心不住内。

Venerable friends! How does a bhikṣu's mind dwell internally? Venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, detached from sensual desires, detached from unwholesome states, with directed thought and evaluation, dwells in the joy and bliss born of detachment, having attained the first absorption, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of detachment, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, with the subsiding of directed thought and evaluation, with internal clarity and one-pointedness of mind, without directed thought, without evaluation, born of concentration, with joy and bliss, dwells having attained the second absorption, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of concentration, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, detached from joy, detached from suffering, dwells in equanimity, mindfulness, and purity, having attained the third absorption, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of equanimity and purity, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, with the cessation of pleasure and pain, and the earlier cessation of joy and displeasure, dwells in equanimity, mindfulness, and purity, having attained the fourth absorption, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of equanimity and purity, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally.

「诸贤！云何比丘心住内？诸贤！比丘离欲，离恶不善之法，有觉有观，离生喜乐，得初禅成就游。彼识不著离味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。复次，诸贤！比丘觉、观已息，内静、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，得第二禅成就游。彼识不著定味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。复次，诸贤！比丘离于喜欲，舍无求游，正念正智而身觉乐，谓圣所说，圣所舍、念、乐住、空，得第三禅成就游。彼识不著无喜味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。复次，诸贤！比丘乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，得第四禅成就游。识不著舍及念、清净味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。

Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending all perceptions of form, perceptions of resistance, and perceptions of variety, with the perception of infinite space, dwells having attained the sphere of infinite space, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of the knowledge of space, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending the sphere of infinite space, with the perception of infinite consciousness, dwells having attained the sphere of infinite consciousness, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of the knowledge of consciousness, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending the sphere of infinite consciousness, with the perception of nothingness, dwells having attained the sphere of nothingness, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of the knowledge of nothingness, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Furthermore, venerable friends! When a bhikṣu, transcending the sphere of nothingness, with the perception of neither perception nor non-perception, dwells having attained the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, his consciousness is not attached to the taste of the knowledge of neither perception nor non-perception, does not abide in it, is not bound to it, and his consciousness dwells internally. Venerable friends! In this way, a bhikṣu's mind dwells internally.

「复次，诸贤！比丘度一切色想，灭有对想，不念若干想，无量空，是无量空处成就游。彼识不著空智味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。复次，诸贤！比丘度一切无量空处，无量识，是无量识处成就游，彼识不著识智味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。复次，诸贤！比丘度一切无量识处，无所有，是无所有处成就游。彼识不著无所有智味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。复次，诸贤！比丘度一切无所有处，非有想非无想，是非有想非无想处成就游，彼识不著无想智味，不依彼不住彼，不缘彼不缚彼，识住内也。诸贤！如是比丘心住内也。

Venerable friends! How does a bhikṣu not accept and yet be terrified? Venerable friends! When a bhikṣu is not detached from form, not detached from desire for form, not detached from love for form, not detached from thirst for form, venerable friends! If a bhikṣu is not detached from form, not detached from desire for form, not detached from love for form, not detached from thirst for form, he desires form, seeks form, clings to form, abides in form, form is me, form is mine, he desires form, clings to form, abides in form, form is me, form is mine, his consciousness touches form, touches form, changes that form, consciousness turns to form, turns to form, he generates the Dharma of terror, the mind abides in it, because of not knowing the mind, he is frightened, troubled, and terrified without accepting it; so for perception, conception, and volitional formations. When a bhikṣu is not detached from consciousness, not detached from desire for consciousness, not detached from love for consciousness, not detached from thirst for consciousness, venerable friends! If a bhikṣu is not detached from consciousness, not detached from desire for consciousness, not detached from love for consciousness, not detached from thirst for consciousness, he desires consciousness, seeks consciousness, clings to consciousness, abides in consciousness, consciousness is me, consciousness is mine, he desires consciousness, seeks consciousness, clings to consciousness, abides in consciousness, consciousness is me, consciousness is mine, his consciousness touches consciousness, touches consciousness, changes that consciousness, consciousness turns to consciousness, turns to consciousness, he generates the Dharma of terror, the mind abides in it, because of not knowing the mind, he is frightened, troubled, and terrified without accepting it. Venerable friends! In this way, a bhikṣu does not accept terror.

「诸贤！云何比丘不受而恐怖？诸贤！比丘不离色染，不离色欲，不离色爱，不离色渴。诸贤！若有比丘不离色染，不离色欲，不离色爱，不离色渴者，彼欲得色、求色、著色、住色，色即是我，色是我有，彼] cf.［丽】欲得色、著色、住色，色即是我，色是我有已，识扪摸色，识扪摸色已，变易彼色时，识转于色，识转于色已，彼生恐怖法，心住于中，因心不知故，便怖惧烦劳，不受而恐怖；如是觉、想、行。比丘不离识染，不离识欲，不离识爱，不离识渴。诸贤！若有比丘不离识染，不离识欲，不离识爱，不离识渴者，彼欲得识、求识、著识、住识，识即是我，识是我有，彼欲得识、求识、著识、住识，识即是我，识是我有已，识扪摸识，识扪摸识已，变易彼识时，识转于识，识转于识已，彼生恐怖法，心住于中，因心不知故，便怖惧烦劳，不受而恐怖。诸贤！如是比丘不受恐怖。

Venerable friends! How does a bhikṣu not accept and not be terrified? Venerable friends! When a bhikṣu is detached from form, detached from desire for form, detached from love for form, detached from thirst for form, venerable friends! If a bhikṣu is detached from form, detached from desire for form, detached from love for form, detached from thirst for form, he does not desire form, does not seek form, does not cling to form, does not abide in form, form is not me, form is not mine. 「诸贤！云何比丘不受不恐怖。诸贤！比丘离色染，离色欲，离色爱，离色渴。诸贤！若有比丘离色染、离色欲、离色爱、离色渴者，彼不欲得色，不求色、不著色、不住色，色非是我，色非我有。彼不欲得色，不求色，不著色，不住色，色非是我，色非我有已，识不扪摸色，识不扪摸色已，变易彼色时，识不转于色，识不转于色已。彼不生恐怖法，心不住中，因心知故，便不怖惧，不烦劳，不受，不恐怖；如是觉、想、行。比丘离识染，离识欲，离识爱，离识渴。诸贤！若有比丘离识染，离识欲，离识爱，离识渴者，彼不欲得识，不求识，不著识，不住识，识非是我，识非我有，彼不欲得识，不求识，不著识，不住识，识] cf.［丽】非是我，识非我有已，识不扪摸识，识不扪摸识已，变易彼识时，识不转于识，识不转于识已，彼不生恐怖法，心不住中，因心知故，便不怖惧，不烦劳，不受，不恐怖。诸贤！如是比丘不受不恐怖。

"Venerable friends! The World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning, but did not elaborate on it. He then rose from his seat, entered the room, and sat in meditation. Bhikṣus! If you contemplate in this way, as you contemplate, your mind will be scattered externally, not dwelling internally, and you will be terrified without accepting it. Bhikṣus! If you contemplate in this way, as you contemplate, your mind will not be scattered externally, it will dwell internally, and you will not be terrified without accepting it. In this way, there will be no more birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is said to be the end of suffering. The World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning, but did not elaborate on it. I have explained it in this way with these words and phrases. Venerable friends! You may go to the Buddha and explain it in detail. If it is as the World-Honored One has said, you may then accept and uphold it."

「诸贤！谓世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室燕坐。比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心出外洒散，心不住内，不受而恐怖。比丘！如是如是观，如汝观已，比丘！心不出外不洒散，心住内，不受不恐怖，如是不复生、老、病、死，是说苦边。此世尊略说，不广分别义，我以此句、以此文广说如是。诸贤！可往向佛具陈，若如世尊所说义者，诸贤等便可受持。」

Then the bhikṣus, having heard what Venerable Mahākātyāyana said, accepted and recited it well. They rose from their seats, circumambulated Venerable Mahākātyāyana three times to the right, and departed. They went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, sat to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! The World-Honored One briefly explained this meaning, but did not elaborate on it. He then rose from his seat, entered the room, and sat in meditation. Venerable Mahākātyāyana explained it in this way with these words and phrases."

于是，诸比丘闻尊者大迦旃延所说，善受持诵，即从坐起，绕尊者大迦旃延三匝而去，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！向世尊略说此义，不广分别，即从坐起，入室燕坐。尊者大迦旃延以此句、以此文而广说之。」

The World-Honored One, having heard this, praised: "Excellent! Excellent! Among my disciples, there are those with eyes, wisdom, the Dharma, and meaning. Why is this? Because the teacher briefly explains this meaning to the disciples, but does not elaborate on it. The disciples explain it with these words and phrases, just as the bhikṣu Mahākātyāyana has said. You should accept and uphold it in this way. Why is this? Because the meaning of contemplation should be explained in this way."

世尊闻已，叹曰：「善哉！善哉！我弟子中有眼、有智、有法、有义。所以者何？谓师为弟子略说此义，不广分别，彼弟子以此句、以此文而广说之，如迦旃延比丘所说，汝等应当如是受持。所以者何？以说观义应如是也。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, joyfully received and practiced them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
