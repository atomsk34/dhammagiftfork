MĀ 165

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 13 根本分别品

165. （一六五）温泉林天经

Parallel to: MN 133

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the city of Rājagṛha, staying in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove.

一时，佛游王舍城，在竹林迦兰哆园。

At that time, Venerable Samīti was also wandering in the city of Rājagṛha, staying in the Hot Spring Grove. Then, Venerable Samīti, as the night was about to turn to dawn, left his dwelling and went to the hot springs. He took off his clothes on the bank, entered the hot springs to bathe, and after bathing, he came out, wiped his body, and put on his clothes.

尔时，尊者三弥提亦游王舍城，住温泉林。于是，尊者三弥提夜将向旦，从房而出，往诣温泉，脱衣岸上，入温泉浴，浴已还出，拭体著衣。

At that time, a god of supreme form and appearance, majestic and radiant, came to Venerable Samīti as the night was about to turn to dawn. He bowed his head in reverence and stood to one side. The god's form and appearance were extremely wonderful, and his radiant light illuminated the bank of the hot springs. Having stood to one side, he said to Venerable Samīti, "Monk! Do you uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra?"

尔时，有一天形体极妙，色像巍巍，夜将向旦，往诣尊者三弥提所，稽首作礼，却住一面。彼天色像威神极妙，光明普照于温泉岸，彼天却住于一面已，白尊者三弥提曰：「比丘！受持跋地罗帝偈耶？」

Venerable Samīti replied to the god, "I do not uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra." He then asked the god, "Do you uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra?"

尊者三弥提答彼天曰：「我不受持跋地罗帝偈也。」寻问彼天：「汝受持跋地罗帝偈耶？」

The god replied, "I also do not uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra."

彼天答曰：「我亦不受持跋地罗帝偈也。」

Venerable Samīti then asked the god, "Who upholds the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra?"

尊者三弥提复问彼天：「谁受持跋地罗帝偈耶？」

The god replied, "The World-Honored One is staying in this Rājagṛha, in the Bamboo Grove, at the Squirrels' Feeding Ground. He upholds the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra. Monk! You should go and personally receive and recite the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra from the World-Honored One. Why is that? The verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra have the Dharma and meaning, are the basis for the holy life, lead to knowledge, lead to awakening, and lead to nirvāṇa. Those of noble birth who have perfect faith, leave home, become homeless, and study the path, should well receive and recite the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra." The god spoke thus, bowed his head at the feet of Venerable Samīti, circumambulated him three times, and then disappeared on the spot.

彼天答曰：「世尊游此王舍城，在竹林迦兰哆园，彼受持跋地罗帝偈也。比丘！可往面从世尊，善受持诵跋地罗帝偈。所以者何？跋地罗帝偈者，有法有义，为梵行本，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃，族姓者至信、舍家、无家、学道，当以跋地罗帝偈善受持诵。」彼天说如是，稽首尊者三弥提足，绕三匝已，即彼处没。

Then, not long after the god disappeared, Venerable Samīti went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at his feet, sat to one side, and said, "World-Honored One! Today, at the beginning of the night, I left my room and went to the hot springs. I took off my clothes on the shore and entered the hot springs to bathe. After bathing, I came out and stood on the shore wiping my body. At that time, a god with an extremely wonderful form and majestic appearance came to me at the beginning of the night, bowed his head at my feet, and stood to one side. The god's appearance and spiritual power were extremely wonderful, and his radiance illuminated the shore of the hot springs. After the god stood to one side, he said to me, 'Monk! Do you uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra?' I replied to the god, 'I do not uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra.' I asked the god, 'Do you uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra?' The god replied, 'I also do not uphold the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra.' I further asked, 'Who upholds the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra?' The god replied, 'The World-Honored One is staying in this Rājagṛha, in the Bamboo Grove, at the Squirrels' Feeding Ground. He upholds the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra. Monk! You should go and personally receive and recite the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra from the World-Honored One. Why is that? The verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra have the Dharma and meaning, are the basis for the holy life, lead to knowledge, lead to awakening, and lead to nirvāṇa. Those of noble birth who have perfect faith, leave home, become homeless, and study the path, should well receive and recite the verses of the Vāḍīra-sūtra.' The god spoke thus, bowed his head at my feet, circumambulated me three times, and then disappeared on the spot."

于是，尊者三弥提，天没不久往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！我于今日夜将向旦出房往诣彼温泉所，脱衣岸上，入温泉浴，浴已便出，住岸拭身。尔时，有一天形体极妙，色像巍巍，夜将向旦，来诣我所，稽首作礼，却住一面。彼天色像威神极妙，光明普照于温泉岸。彼天却住于一面已，而白我曰：『比丘！受持跋地罗帝偈耶？』我答彼天：『不受持跋地罗帝偈也。』我问彼天：『汝受持跋地罗帝偈耶？』彼天答曰：『我亦不受持跋地罗帝偈也。』我复问：『谁受持跋地罗帝偈耶？』彼天答曰：『世尊游此王舍城，住竹林迦兰哆园，彼受持跋地罗帝偈也。比丘！可往面从世尊善受持诵跋地罗帝偈。所以者何？跋地罗帝偈者，有义有法，为梵行本，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃，族姓者至信、舍家、无家、学道，当以跋地罗帝偈善受持诵。』彼天说如是，稽首我足，绕三匝已，即彼处没。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Samīti! Do you know where that god came from? What is the name of that god?"

世尊问曰：「三弥提！汝知彼天从何处来？彼天名何耶？」

Venerable Samīti replied, "World-Honored One! I do not know where that god came from, nor do I know his name."

尊者三弥提答曰：「世尊！我不知彼天从何所来，亦不知名也。」

The World-Honored One said, "Samīti! That god's name is Sakka, the general of the Thirty-three Gods."

世尊告曰：「三弥提！彼天子名正殿，为三十三天军将。」

Then Venerable Samīti said, "World-Honored One! Now is the time. Well-Gone One! Now is the time. If the World-Honored One would speak the verses of the Vāgīśa for the monks, the monks would listen well and remember them."

于是，尊者三弥提白曰：「世尊！今正是时。善逝！今正是时。若世尊为诸比丘说跋地罗帝偈者，诸比丘从世尊闻已，当善受持。」

The World-Honored One said, "Samīti! Listen well, listen well, and pay close attention. I shall speak for you."

世尊告曰：「三弥提！谛听，谛听，善思念之，我当为汝说。」

Venerable Samīti said, "Yes, sir." The monks listened as instructed.

尊者三弥提白曰：「唯然。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said:

佛言：

Do not think of the past, nor wish for
the future. The past is already extinguished, and the
future has not yet arrived. The present phenomena are
also impermanent. The wise perceive this. If one practices
the path of the sages, who knows about sorrow
at death? I will not meet with that great
suffering and calamity. Thus, one should practice diligently day
and night without laziness. Therefore, I always recite the Vāḍiladhara verse.

「慎莫念过去，　　亦勿愿未来，过去事已灭，　　未来复未至。现在所有法，　　彼亦当为思，念无有坚强，　　慧者觉如是。若作圣人行，　　孰知愁于死，我要不会彼，　　大苦灾患终。如是行精勤，　　昼夜无懈怠，是故常当说，　　跋地罗帝偈。」

The Buddha spoke thus, then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation. At that time, the bhikṣus thought: "Venerable friends! You should know that the World-Honored One briefly taught this instruction without elaborating, then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation.

佛说如是，即从座起，入室宴坐。于是，诸比丘便作是念：「诸贤！当知世尊略说此教，不广分别，即从座起，入室宴坐。

Do not think of the past, nor wish for
the future. The past is already extinguished, and the
future has not yet arrived. The present phenomena are
also impermanent. The wise perceive this. If one practices
the path of the sages, who knows about sorrow
at death? I will not meet with that great
suffering and calamity. Thus, one should practice diligently day
and night without laziness. Therefore, I always recite the Vāḍiladhara verse.

「『慎莫念过去，　　亦勿愿未来，过去事已灭，　　未来复未至。现在所有法，　　彼亦当为思，念无有坚强，　　慧者觉如是。若作圣人行，　　孰知愁于死，我要不会彼，　　大苦灾患终。如是行精勤，　　昼夜无懈怠，是故常当说，　　跋地罗帝偈。』」

They further thought: "Venerable friends! Who can elaborate on the meaning of what the World-Honored One has just briefly taught?" They further thought: "The Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise practitioners of the holy life. The Venerable Mahākātyāyana can elaborate on the meaning of what the World-Honored One has just briefly taught. Venerable friends! Let us all go to the Venerable Mahākātyāyana and request him to explain this meaning. If the Venerable Mahākātyāyana explains it, we shall uphold it well."

彼复作是念：「诸贤！谁能广分别世尊向所略说义？」彼复作是念：「尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义，诸贤共往诣尊者大迦旃延所，请说此义。若尊者大迦旃延为分别者，我等当善受持。」

Then the monks went to the Venerable Mahākātyāyana, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. They said: "Venerable Mahākātyāyana! Know that the World-Honored One briefly taught this instruction without elaborating, then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation.

于是，诸比丘往诣尊者大迦旃延所，共相问讯，却坐一面，白曰：「尊者大迦旃延！当知世尊略说此教，不广分别，即从座起，入室宴坐。

Do not think of the past, nor wish for the
future. The past has already ceased, and the future has
not yet come. The present phenomena, they too should be
contemplated. Thoughts have no substance; the wise perceive thus. If
one practices the path of the noble ones, who knows
sorrow from death? I will not meet them, the end
of great suffering and calamity. Thus practicing diligently, day and
night without laziness, therefore always recite the verse of the Buddha.

「『慎莫念过去，　　亦勿愿未来，过去事已灭，　　未来复未至。现在所有法，　　彼亦当为思，念无有坚强，　　慧者觉如是。若学圣人行，　　孰知愁于死，我要不会彼，　　大苦灾患终。如是行精勤，　　昼夜无懈怠，是故常当说，　　跋地罗帝偈。』

"We then thought: 'Venerable ones! Who can extensively explain the meaning of what the World-Honored One has just briefly taught?' We further thought: 'The Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise and virtuous ones. The Venerable Mahākātyāyana can extensively explain the meaning of what the World-Honored One has just briefly taught.' We only wish that the Venerable Mahākātyāyana would explain it out of compassion."

「我等便作是念：『诸贤！谁能广分别世尊向所略说义？』我等复作是念：『尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。』唯愿尊者大迦旃延为慈愍故而广说之。」

The Venerable Mahākātyāyana said, "Venerable ones! Listen to my parable. The wise understand the meaning through parables. Venerable ones! It is like a person who wants to obtain the fruit. In order to obtain the fruit, he carries an axe into the forest. He sees a large tree with roots, trunk, joints, branches, leaves, flowers, and fruit. He does not touch the roots, trunk, joints, and fruit, but only touches the branches and leaves. What you have said is also like this. The World-Honored One is present. You should abandon coming to me and ask about this meaning. Why is that? Venerable ones! You should know that the World-Honored One is the eye, the knowledge, the meaning, the Dharma, the master of the Dharma, the leader of the Dharma, who speaks the true meaning. All meanings are present due to the World-Honored One. Venerable ones! You should go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning: 'World-Honored One! What is this? What is the meaning of this?' As the World-Honored One explains, you should uphold it well."

尊者大迦旃延告曰：「诸贤！听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。诸贤！犹如有人欲得求实，为求实故，持斧入林，彼见大树成根、茎、节、枝、叶、华、实，彼人不触根、茎、节、实，但触枝、叶。诸贤所说亦复如是，世尊现在，舍来就我而问此义。所以者何？诸贤！当知世尊是眼、是智、是义、是法，法主、法将，说真谛义，现一切义由彼世尊，诸贤应往诣世尊所而问此义：『世尊！此云何？此何义？』如世尊说者，诸贤等当善受持。」

At that time, the bhikṣus said, "Yes, Venerable Mahākātyāyana! The World-Honored One is the eye, the knowledge, the meaning, the Dharma, the master of the Dharma, the leader of the Dharma, who speaks the true meaning. All meanings are present due to the World-Honored One. We will go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning: 'World-Honored One! What is this? What is the meaning of this?' As the World-Honored One explains, we will uphold it well. However, the Venerable Mahākātyāyana is always praised by the World-Honored One and the wise and virtuous ones. The Venerable Mahākātyāyana can extensively explain the meaning of what the World-Honored One has just briefly taught. We only wish that the Venerable Mahākātyāyana would explain it out of compassion."

时，诸比丘白曰：「唯然。尊者大迦旃延！世尊是眼、是智、是义、是法，法主、法将，说真谛义，现一切义由彼世尊，我等往诣世尊所而问此义：『世尊！此云何？此何义？』如世尊说者，我等当善受持。然尊者大迦旃延常为世尊之所称誉，及诸智梵行人，尊者大迦旃延能广分别世尊向所略说义。唯愿尊者大迦旃延为慈愍故而广说之。」

The Venerable Mahākātyāyana said to the bhikṣus, "Venerable ones! Listen to what I say. Venerable ones! How does a bhikṣu recollect the past? Venerable ones! A bhikṣu truly has eyes that know forms that are pleasing, mentally contemplated, loved, associated with desire, and joyfully touched. The basis is the past. He is attached to the desire of past consciousness. Having been attached to the desire of past consciousness, he then delights in it. Having delighted in it, he then recollects the past. Similarly, the ear, nose, tongue, and body truly have minds that know dharmas that are pleasing, mentally contemplated, loved, associated with desire, and joyfully touched. The basis is the past. He is attached to the desire of past consciousness. Having been attached to the desire of past consciousness, he then delights in it. Having delighted in it, he then recollects the past. Venerable ones! In this way, a bhikṣu recollects the past.

尊者大迦旃延告诸比丘：「诸贤等共听我所说。诸贤！云何比丘念过去耶？诸贤！比丘实有眼知色可喜、意所念，爱色，欲相应，心乐，扪摸本，本即过去也。彼为过去识欲染著，因识欲染著已，则便乐彼，因乐彼已，便念过去。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，实有意知法可喜、意所念，爱法，欲相应，心乐，扪摸本，本即过去也。彼为过去识欲染著，因识欲染著已，则便乐彼，因乐彼已，便念过去。诸贤！如是比丘念过去也。

Venerable ones! How does a bhikṣu not recollect the past? Venerable ones! A bhikṣu truly has eyes that know forms that are pleasing, mentally contemplated, loved, associated with desire, and joyfully touched. The basis is the past. He is not attached to the desire of past consciousness. Having not been attached to the desire of past consciousness, he then does not delight in it. Having not delighted in it, he then does not recollect the past. Similarly, the ear, nose, tongue, and body truly have minds that know dharmas that are pleasing, mentally contemplated, loved, associated with desire, and joyfully touched. The basis is the past. He is not attached to the desire of past consciousness. Having not been attached to the desire of past consciousness, he then does not delight in it. Having not delighted in it, he then does not recollect the past. Venerable ones! In this way, a bhikṣu does not recollect the past.

「诸贤！云何比丘不念过去？诸贤！比丘实有眼知色可喜、意所念，爱色，欲相应，心乐，扪摸本，本即过去也。彼为过去识不欲染著，因识不欲染著已，则便不乐彼，因不乐彼已，便不念过去。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，实有意知法可喜、意所念，爱法，欲相应，心乐，扪摸本，本即过去也。彼为过去识不欲染著，因识不欲染著已，则便不乐彼，因不乐彼已，便不念过去。诸贤！如是比丘不念过去也。

Venerable ones! How does a bhikṣu wish for the future? Venerable ones! If a bhikṣu has eye, form, and eye consciousness in the future, he wishes to obtain what he has not yet obtained, and his mind wishes for what he has already obtained. Because of his mental wish, he then delights in it. Having delighted in it, he then wishes for the future. Similarly, the ear, nose, tongue, and body. If he has mind, dharma, and mind consciousness in the future, he wishes to obtain what he has not yet obtained, and his mind wishes for what he has already obtained. Because of his mental wish, he then delights in it. Having delighted in it, he then wishes for the future. Venerable ones! In this way, a bhikṣu wishes for the future.

「诸贤！云何比丘愿未来耶？诸贤！比丘若有眼、色、眼识未来者，彼未得欲得，已得心愿，因心愿已，则便乐彼，因乐彼已，便愿未来；如是耳、鼻、舌、身。若有意、法、意识未来者，未得欲得，已得心愿，因心愿已，则便乐彼，因乐彼已，便愿未来。诸贤！如是比丘愿未来也。

Venerable ones! How does a bhikṣu not wish for the future? Venerable ones! If a bhikṣu has eye, form, and eye consciousness in the future, he does not wish to obtain what he has not yet obtained, and his mind does not wish for what he has already obtained. Because his mind does not wish, he then does not delight in it. Having not delighted in it, he then does not wish for the future. Similarly, the ear, nose, tongue, and body. If he has mind, dharma, and mind consciousness in the future, he does not wish to obtain what he has not yet obtained, and his mind does not wish for what he has already obtained. Because his mind does not wish, he then does not delight in it. Having not delighted in it, he then does not wish for the future. Venerable ones! In this way, a bhikṣu does not wish for the future.

「诸贤！云何比丘不愿未来？诸贤！比丘若有眼、色、眼识未来者，未得不欲得，已得心不愿，因心不愿已，则便不乐彼，因不乐彼已，便不愿未来；如是耳、鼻、舌、身。若有意、法、意识未来者，未得不欲得，已得心不愿，因心不愿已，则便不乐彼，因不乐彼已，便不愿未来。诸贤！如是比丘不愿未来也。

Venerable ones! How does a bhikṣu experience present dharmas? Venerable ones! If a bhikṣu has eye, form, and eye consciousness in the present, he is attached to the desire for consciousness in the present. Because of being attached to the desire for consciousness, he then delights in it. Having delighted in it, he then experiences present dharmas. Similarly, the ear, nose, tongue, and body. If he has mind, dharma, and mind consciousness in the present, he is attached to the desire for consciousness in the present. Because of being attached to the desire for consciousness, he then delights in it. Having delighted in it, he then experiences present dharmas. Venerable ones! In this way, a bhikṣu experiences present dharmas.

「诸贤！云何比丘受现在法？诸贤！比丘若有眼、色、眼识现在者，彼于现在识欲染著，因识欲染著已，则便乐彼，因乐彼已，便受现在法；如是耳、鼻、舌、身。若有意、法、意识现在者，彼于现在识欲染著，因识欲染著已，则便乐彼，因乐彼已，便受现在法。诸贤！如是比丘受现在法也。

Venerable ones! How does a bhikṣu not experience present dharmas? Venerable ones! If a bhikṣu has eye, form, and eye consciousness in the present, he is not attached to the desire for consciousness in the present. Because of not being attached to the desire for consciousness, he then does not delight in it. Having not delighted in it, he then does not experience present dharmas. Similarly, the ear, nose, tongue, and body. If he has mind, dharma, and mind consciousness in the present, he is not attached to the desire for consciousness in the present. Because of not being attached to the desire for consciousness, he then does not delight in it. Having not delighted in it, he then does not experience present dharmas. Venerable ones! In this way, a bhikṣu does not experience present dharmas.

「诸贤！云何比丘不受现在法。诸贤！比丘若有眼、色、眼识现在者，彼于现在识不欲染著，因识不欲染著已，则便不乐彼，因不乐彼已，便不受现在法；如是耳、鼻、舌、身。若有意、法、意识现在者，彼于现在识不欲染著，因识不欲染著已，则便不乐彼，因不乐彼已，便不受现在法。诸贤！如是比丘不受现在法。

Venerable ones! The World-Honored One briefly taught this instruction without elaborating on it, then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation.

「诸贤！谓世尊略说此教，不广分别，即从座起，入室宴坐。

Do not think of the past, nor wish for the
future. The past has already ceased, and the future has
not yet arrived. The present dharmas should also be contemplated
They are impermanent and not strong. The wise perceive it
thus. If one practices the path of the noble ones
who knows sorrow from death? I will not meet them
The great suffering and calamity will end. Thus practicing diligently
day and night without laziness, therefore always recite the Vāḍiśa verse.

「『慎莫念过去，　　亦勿愿未来，过去事已灭，　　未来复未至。现在所有法，　　彼亦当为思，念无有坚强，　　慧者觉如是。若学圣人行，　　孰知愁于死，我要不会彼，　　大苦灾患终。如是行精勤，　　昼夜无懈怠，是故常当说，　　跋地罗帝偈。』

"The World-Honored One briefly taught this without elaborating. I will explain it in detail using these words and phrases. Venerable ones! You may go to the Buddha and report it. If it is as the World-Honored One has said, you may all accept and uphold it."

「此世尊略说，不广分别，我以此句、以此文广说如是。诸贤！可往向佛具陈，若如世尊所说义者，诸贤等便可共受持。」

Then the monks, having heard what Venerable Mahākātyāyana said, accepted and recited it well. They rose from their seats, circumambulated Venerable Mahākātyāyana three times, and departed. They went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, sat to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! The World-Honored One briefly taught this teaching without elaborating. He then rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation. Venerable Mahākātyāyana explained it in detail using these words and phrases."

于是，诸比丘闻尊者大迦旃延所说，善受持诵，即从座起，绕尊者大迦旃延三匝而去，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！向世尊略说此教，不广分别，即从座起，入室燕坐。尊者大迦旃延以此句、以此文而广说之。」

The World-Honored One, having heard this, praised: "Excellent! Excellent! Among my disciples, there are those with eyes, wisdom, the Dharma, and meaning. Why is this? It is because the teacher briefly teaches this teaching to the disciples without elaborating. The disciples explain it in detail using these words and phrases, just as the monk Mahākātyāyana has explained. You should accept and uphold it in this way. Why is this? Because the meaning of contemplation should be explained in this way."

世尊闻已，叹曰：「善哉！善哉！我弟子中有眼、有智、有法、有义。所以者何？谓师为弟子略说此教，不广分别，彼弟子以此句、以此文而广说之，如大迦旃延比丘所说，汝等应当如是受持。所以者何？以说观义应如是也。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those monks, having heard the Buddha's words, joyfully received and practiced them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
