MĀ 168

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 13 根本分别品

168. （一六八）意行经

Parallel to: MN 120 (~) / AN 4.123–124

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I will now teach you the Dharma. The beginning is wonderful, the middle is wonderful, and the end is also wonderful. It has meaning and text, is complete and pure, and fully reveals the holy life. It is called the Sūtra on Distinguishing Mental Activities, which arises from mental activities. Listen attentively, listen attentively, and consider it well." At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今为汝说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行，谓分别意行经，如意行生。谛听，谛听，善思念之。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "How does mental activity arise? If a bhikṣu, having separated from desires, separated from evil and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, attains the joy and bliss born of separation, and abides in the first dhyāna, having accomplished it, he wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the Brahmakāyika gods. Those Brahmakāyika gods, having been born there and abiding there, experience the joy and bliss born of separation. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, enters the first dhyāna and experiences the joy and bliss born of separation. These two joys and bliss born of separation are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the Brahmakāyika gods. This is how mental activity arises.

佛言：「云何意行生？若有比丘离欲，离恶不善之法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，得初禅成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生梵身天中。诸梵身天者，生彼住彼，受离生喜、乐，及比丘住此，入初禅，受离生喜、乐，此二离生喜、乐无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生梵身天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having stilled initial and sustained thought, with inner tranquility and one-pointedness of mind, without initial thought and sustained thought, gives rise to concentration, experiences joy and bliss, and abides in the second dhyāna, having accomplished it. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the Ābhāsvara gods. Those Ābhāsvara gods, having been born there and abiding there, experience the joy and bliss born of concentration. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, enters the second dhyāna and experiences the joy and bliss born of concentration. These two joys and bliss born of concentration are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the Ābhāsvara gods. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘觉、观已息，内靖、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，得第二禅成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生晃昱天中。诸晃昱天者，生彼住彼，受定生喜、乐，及比丘住此，入第二禅，受定生喜、乐，此二定生喜、乐无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生晃昱天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having separated from the desire for joy, dwells without desire, with equanimity, mindful, and fully aware, experiencing bliss with the body, and attains the third dhyāna, which the noble ones proclaim, dwells in, and enjoys. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the Śubhakṛtsna gods. Those Śubhakṛtsna gods, having been born there and abiding there, experience the bliss without joy. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, enters the third dhyāna and experiences the bliss without joy. These two blisses without joy are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the Śubhakṛtsna gods. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘离于喜欲，舍无求游，正念正智而身觉乐，谓圣所说、圣所舍、念、乐住、空，得第三禅成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生遍净天中。诸遍净天者，生彼住彼，受无喜乐，及比丘住此，入第三禅，受无喜乐，此二无喜乐无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生遍净天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having stilled pleasure and pain, and having previously removed joy and sorrow, without pleasure or pain, and with equanimity and mindfulness, attains the fourth dhyāna, which the noble ones proclaim, dwell in, and enjoy. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the Bṛhatphala gods. Those Bṛhatphala gods, having been born there and abiding there, experience the bliss of equanimity and mindfulness. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, enters the fourth dhyāna and experiences the bliss of equanimity and mindfulness. These two blisses of equanimity and mindfulness are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the Bṛhatphala gods. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，得第四禅成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生果实天中。诸果实天者，生彼住彼，受舍、念、清净乐，及比丘住此，入第四禅，受舍、念、清净乐，此二舍、念、清净乐无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生果实天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having transcended all perceptions of form, having abandoned perceptions of obstruction, not paying attention to perceptions of discrimination, attains the sphere of infinite space, and dwells having accomplished and abided in it. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the gods of the sphere of infinite space. Those gods of the sphere of infinite space, having been born there and abiding there, experience the perception of the sphere of infinite space. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, experiences the perception of the sphere of infinite space. These two perceptions of the sphere of infinite space are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the gods of the sphere of infinite space. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘度一切色想，灭有对想，不念若干想，无量空，是无量空处成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生无量空处天中、诸无量空处天者，生彼住彼，受无量空处想，及比丘住此，受无量空处想，此二无量空处想无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生无量空处天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having transcended the sphere of infinite space, attains the sphere of infinite consciousness, and dwells having accomplished and abided in it. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the gods of the sphere of infinite consciousness. Those gods of the sphere of infinite consciousness, having been born there and abiding there, experience the perception of the sphere of infinite consciousness. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, experiences the perception of the sphere of infinite consciousness. These two perceptions of the sphere of infinite consciousness are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the gods of the sphere of infinite consciousness. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘度无量空处，无量识，是无量识处成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生无量识处天中。诸无量识处天者，生彼住彼，受无量识处想，及比丘住此，受无量识处想，此二无量识处想无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生无量识处天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having transcended the sphere of infinite consciousness, attains the sphere of nothingness, and dwells having accomplished and abided in it. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the gods of the sphere of nothingness. Those gods of the sphere of nothingness, having been born there and abiding there, experience the perception of the sphere of nothingness. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, experiences the perception of the sphere of nothingness. These two perceptions of the sphere of nothingness are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the gods of the sphere of nothingness. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘度无量识处，无所有，是无所有处成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生无所有处天中。诸无所有处天者，生彼住彼，受无所有处想，及比丘住此，受无所有处想，此二无所有处想无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生无所有处天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having transcended the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, attains the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, and dwells having accomplished and abided in it. He wishes to abide in that samādhi bliss. Having wished to abide in that samādhi bliss, there is a place where he abides in that bliss and, having died there, is reborn among the gods of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. Those gods of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, having been born there and abiding there, experience the perception of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. And the bhikṣu, abiding here, experiences the perception of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. These two perceptions of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception are without distinction, both being equal. Why is that? Because he first practiced samādhi here and then was reborn there. That samādhi is practiced, cultivated, and widely practiced like this, and one is reborn among the gods of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. This is how mental activity arises.

「复次，比丘度一切无所有处想，非有想非无想，是非有想非无想处成就游。彼此定乐欲住，彼此定乐欲住已，必有是处，住彼乐彼，命终生非有想非无想处天中。诸非有想非无想处天者，生彼住彼，受非有想非无想处想，及比丘住此，受非有想非无想处想，此二想无有差别，二俱等等。所以者何？先此行定，然后生彼，彼此定如是修、如是习、如是广布，生非有想非无想处天中，如是意行生。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having transcended the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, attains the cessation of perception and feeling, and dwells having accomplished and abided in it. With wisdom, he sees the exhaustion of the taints, and attains the knowledge of their exhaustion. Among those samādhis, this samādhi is said to be the foremost, the greatest, the highest, the most excellent, and the most sublime. Just as from a cow comes milk, from milk comes curd, from curd comes butter, from butter comes ghee, and from ghee comes the essence of ghee, and the essence of ghee is said to be the foremost, the greatest, the highest, the most excellent, and the most sublime. In the same way, among those samādhis, this samādhi is said to be the foremost, the greatest, the highest, the most excellent, and the most sublime. Having attained this samādhi, relying on this samādhi, abiding in this samādhi, one no longer experiences the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is said to be the end of suffering.

「复次，比丘度一切非有想非无想处，想知灭身触成就游。慧见诸漏尽断智，彼诸定中，此定说最第一、最大、最上、最胜、最妙。犹如因牛有乳，因乳有酪，因酪有生酥，因生酥有熟酥，因熟酥有酥精，酥精者说最第一、最大、最上最胜、最妙。如是彼诸定中，此定说最第一、最大、最上、最胜、最妙，得此定、依此定、住此定已，不复受生老病死苦，是说苦边。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
