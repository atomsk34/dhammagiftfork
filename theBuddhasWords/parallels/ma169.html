MĀ 169

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 13 根本分别品

169. （一六九）拘楼瘦无诤经

Parallel to: MN 139

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the city of Kumbhahattha in the country of Kasi.

一时，佛游婆奇瘦剑磨瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the monks, saying, "I will teach you the Dharma, which is wonderful in the beginning, wonderful in the middle, and wonderful in the end, with meaning and phrasing, complete and pure, revealing the holy life. Listen well and pay close attention, I will explain." The monks accepted the teaching and listened.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我当为汝说法，初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行，名分别无诤经。谛听，谛听，善思念之。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "Do not seek sensual pleasures and extremely base actions, which are the practices of ordinary people. Also, do not seek self-mortification, which leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning. Avoiding these two extremes, there is the middle way, which leads to the attainment of the eye, wisdom, mastery, and concentration, and to the realization of wisdom, enlightenment, and nirvana. Whether praised or criticized, whether praised or not, I teach the Dharma. Being determined in moderation, knowing this, I always seek the inner joy. Do not lead others to speak, do not praise in front of others, speak within limits, do not seek limits, follow the customs of the country, do not be this or that. This is the matter of the Sutra on the Discrimination of the Absence of Dispute. Do not seek sensual pleasures and extremely base actions, which are the practices of ordinary people, and also do not seek self-mortification, which leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning. Why is this said? Not seeking sensual pleasures and extremely base actions, which are the practices of ordinary people, is said to be one extreme. Also, not seeking self-mortification, which leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning, is said to be the second extreme. Not seeking sensual pleasures and extremely base actions, which are the practices of ordinary people, and also not seeking self-mortification, which leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning, is said for this reason.

佛言：「莫求欲乐、极下贱业，为凡夫行。亦莫求自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应，离此二边，则有中道，成眼成智，自在成定，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃。有称、有讥，有无称、无讥而为说法。决定于齐，决定知已，所有内乐常求彼也。莫相导说，亦莫面前称誉，齐限说，莫求齐限，随国俗法，莫是莫非，此分别无诤经事。莫求欲乐、极下贱业，为凡夫行，亦莫求自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应者。此何因说？莫求欲乐、极下贱业，为凡夫行，是说一边，亦莫求自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应者，是说二边。莫求欲乐、极下贱业，为凡夫行，亦莫求自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应者，因此故说。

"Avoiding these two extremes, there is the middle way, which leads to the attainment of the eye, wisdom, mastery, and concentration, and to the realization of wisdom, enlightenment, and nirvana." Why is this said? There are eight branches of the noble path: right view, and so forth, up to right concentration. This is called the eight. "Avoiding these two extremes, there is the middle way, which leads to the attainment of the eye, wisdom, mastery, and concentration, and to the realization of wisdom, enlightenment, and nirvana." This is said for this reason.

「离此二边，则有中道，成眼成智，自在成定，趣智、趣觉、趣涅槃者。此何因说？有圣道八支，正见乃至正定，是谓为八。离此二边，则有中道，成眼成智，自在成定，趣智、趣觉、趣涅槃者，因此故说。有称、有讥，有无称、无讥而为说法者。此何因说？云何为称？云何为讥，而不说法。若有欲相应与喜乐俱，极下贱业，为凡夫行，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行。彼知此已，则便自讥。所以者何？欲者，无常、苦、磨灭法，彼知欲无常已，是故彼一切有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，是故便自讥。

Self-mortification, which leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then criticize themselves. Why is this? The ascetics and brahmins who shave their beards and hair, wear the saffron robes, leave home out of true faith, and practice the homeless life, they also embrace this suffering. Therefore, they all have suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then criticize themselves. Having not exhausted the fetters, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then criticize themselves. Why is this? If the fetters are not exhausted, then their existence is also not exhausted. Therefore, they all have vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then criticize themselves. Having exhausted the fetters, this method has no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, they then praise themselves. Why is this? If the fetters are exhausted, then their existence is also exhausted. Therefore, they all have no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, they then praise themselves.

「自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便自讥。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志所可畏苦，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，彼沙门、梵志复抱此苦，是故彼一切有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行。彼知此已，是故便自讥。有结不尽，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便自讥。所以者何？若有结不尽者，彼有亦不尽。是故彼一切有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，是故便自讥也。有结尽者，此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，彼知此已，则便自称。所以者何？若有结尽者，彼有亦尽，是故彼一切无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，彼知此已，是故便自称也。

Not seeking inner happiness, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then criticize themselves. Why is this? If one does not seek inner happiness, then one also does not seek the inner. Therefore, they all have suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then criticize themselves. Seeking inner happiness, this method has no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, they then praise themselves. Why is this? If one seeks inner happiness, then one also seeks the inner. Therefore, they all have no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, they then praise themselves. Thus, there is praise and blame, but one does not teach the Dharma. There is neither praise nor blame, but one teaches the Dharma.

「不求内乐，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便自讥。所以者何？若有不求内乐者，彼亦不求内，是故彼一切有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，是故便自讥也。求于内乐，此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，彼知此已，则便自称。所以者何？若有求内乐者，彼亦求内，是故彼一切无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行。彼知此已，是故便自称。如是有称有讥而不说法也，不称不讥而为说法。

How does one teach the Dharma without praise or blame? If one is associated with desire and joy, and engages in the most base and lowly actions, which are the practices of ordinary people, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, one then teaches the Dharma. Why is this? They do not say that desire is impermanent, suffering, and subject to destruction. Knowing that desire is impermanent, they all have suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Not understanding this method, which is only the truth of suffering, they have vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma. Self-mortification, which leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma. Why is this? They do not say that self-mortification leads to suffering and is not the practice of the noble ones, and is not conducive to meaning. This method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Not understanding this method, which is only the truth of suffering, they have vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma.

「云何不称不讥而为说法？若欲相应与喜乐俱，极下贱业，为凡夫行，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便说法。所以者何？彼不如是说，欲无常、苦、磨灭法，彼知欲无常已，是故彼一切有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，不达此法，唯有苦法，有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行。彼知此已，是故便说法。自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便说法。所以者何？彼不如是说，自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，不达此法，唯有苦法，有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，是故便说法也。

If there are bonds that are not exhausted, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, one then teaches the Dharma. Why is this? They do not say that if there are bonds that are not exhausted, then what they have is also not exhausted. Therefore, they all have suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Not understanding this method, which is only the truth of suffering, they have vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma. If there are bonds that are exhausted, this method has no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, one then teaches the Dharma. Why is this? They do not say that if there are bonds that are exhausted, then what they have is also exhausted. Therefore, they all have no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Not understanding this method, which is only the truth of no suffering, they have no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma.

「有结不尽，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便说法。所以者何？彼不如是说，若有结不尽者，彼有亦不尽，是故彼一切有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，不达此法，唯有苦法，有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行。彼知此已，是故便说法也。有结尽者，此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行。彼知此已，则便说法。所以者何？彼不如是说，若有结尽者，彼有亦尽，是故彼一切无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，不达此法，唯无苦法，无烦、无热、无忧戚正行。彼知此已，是故便说法也。

If one does not seek inner joy, this method has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, one then teaches the Dharma. Why is this? They do not say that if one does not seek inner joy, then one also does not seek inner. Therefore, they all have suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Not understanding this method, which is only the truth of suffering, they have vexation, heat, and sorrow and dejection in wrong practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma. If one seeks inner joy, this method has no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, one then teaches the Dharma. Why is this? They do not say that if one seeks inner joy, then one also seeks inner. Therefore, they all have no suffering, no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Not understanding this method, which is only the truth of no suffering, they have no vexation, no heat, and no sorrow and dejection in right practice. Knowing this, they then teach the Dharma. Thus, one teaches the Dharma without praise or blame. There are those who teach the Dharma with praise and blame, and those who teach the Dharma without praise or blame. For this reason, it is said.

「不求内乐，此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，彼知此已，则便说法。所以者何？彼不如是说，若不求内乐者，彼亦不求内。是故彼一切有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，不达此法，唯有苦法，有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行。彼知此已，是故便说法也。求于内乐，此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行。彼知此已，则便说法。所以者何？彼不如是说，若有求内乐者，彼亦求内，是故彼一切无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，不达此法，唯无苦法，无烦、无热、无忧戚正行。彼知此已，是故便说法，如是不称、不讥而为说法，有称有讥、有无称无讥而为说法者，因此故说也。

If one is determined in moderation and knows what is determined, then one seeks inner joy. Why is this said? There is joy, which is not the joy of the noble ones but the joy of ordinary beings, the root of illness, the root of boils, the root of arrow wounds. It has food and has birth and death. It cannot be cultivated, cannot be practiced, cannot be widely spread. I say that it should not be cultivated. There is joy, which is the joy of the noble ones, the joy of being without desire, the joy of detachment, the joy of cessation, the joy of right awakening. It has no food and has no birth and death. It can be cultivated, can be practiced, can be widely spread. I say that it should be cultivated.

「决定于齐，决定知已，所有内乐当求彼者，此何因说？有乐，非圣乐是凡夫乐，病本、痈本、箭刺之本，有食有生死，不可修、不可习、不可广布，我说于彼则不可修也。有乐，是圣乐、无欲乐、离乐、息乐、正觉之乐，无食无生死，可修、可习、可广布，我说于彼则可修也。

What is the joy that is not the joy of the noble ones but the joy of ordinary beings, the root of illness, the root of boils, the root of arrow wounds, has food and has birth and death, cannot be cultivated, cannot be practiced, cannot be widely spread, and I say that it should not be cultivated? If joy and pleasure arise from the five sense pleasures, this joy is not the joy of the noble ones but the joy of ordinary beings, the root of illness, the root of boils, the root of arrow wounds. It has food and has birth and death. It cannot be cultivated, cannot be practiced, cannot be widely spread. I say that it should not be cultivated.

「云何有乐，非圣乐是凡夫乐，病本、痈本、箭刺之本，有食有生死，不可修、不可习、不可广布，我说于彼不可修也？彼若因五欲功德生喜生乐，此乐非圣乐，是凡夫乐，病本、痈本、箭刺之本，有食有生死，不可修、不可习、不可广布，我说于彼则不可修。

What is the joy that is the joy of the noble ones, the joy of being without desire, the joy of detachment, the joy of cessation, the joy of right awakening, has no food and has no birth and death, can be cultivated, can be practiced, can be widely spread, and I say that it should be cultivated? If a monk, having abandoned desire, abandoned evil and unwholesome states, attains and abides in the fourth dhyāna, this joy is the joy of the noble ones, the joy of being without desire, the joy of detachment, the joy of cessation, the joy of right awakening. It has no food and has no birth and death. It can be cultivated, can be practiced, can be widely spread. I say that it should be cultivated.

「云何有乐，是圣乐、无欲乐、离乐、息乐、正觉之乐，无食无生死，可修、可习、可广布，我说于彼则可修耶？若有比丘离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游。此乐是圣乐、无欲乐、离乐、息乐、正觉之乐，无食无生死，可修、可习、可广布，我说于彼则可修也。决定于齐，决定知已，所有内乐当求彼者，因此故说。

Do not speak of each other, and do not praise each other in front of each other. Why is this said? There is speaking of each other that is untrue, false, and meaningless. There is speaking of each other that is true, not false, but meaningless. There is speaking of each other that is true, not false, and meaningful. Among them, if there is speaking of each other that is untrue, false, and meaningless, this should never be spoken. Among them, if there is speaking of each other that is true, not false, but meaningless, one should also learn not to speak of it. Among them, if there is speaking of each other that is true, not false, and meaningful, then one should know the time, with right knowledge and right mindfulness, to accomplish that. Thus, do not speak of each other, and do not praise each other in front of each other. This is why it is said.

「莫相导说，亦莫面前称誉者，此何因说？有相导说不真实、虚妄、无义相应，有相导说真实、不虚妄、无义相应，有相导说真实、不虚妄、与义相应。于中若有导说不真实、虚妄、无义相应者，此终不可说。于中若有导说真实、不虚妄、无义相应者，彼亦当学不说是也。于中若有导说真实、不虚妄、义相应者，彼为知时，正智正念，令成就彼，如是面前称誉，莫相导说，亦莫面前称誉者，因此故说。

Speak within limits, do not speak beyond limits. Why is this said? Speaking beyond limits tires the body, makes the mind forgetful, causes mental exhaustion, damages the voice, and makes one not at ease in the presence of the wise. Speaking within limits does not tire the body, makes the mind not forgetful, does not cause mental exhaustion, does not damage the voice, and makes one at ease in the presence of the wise. Thus, speak within limits, do not speak beyond limits. This is why it is said.

「齐限说，莫不齐限者，此何因说？不齐限说者，烦身，念憙忘，心疲极，声坏，向智者不自在也。齐限说者，不烦身，念不憙忘，心不疲极，声不坏，向智者得自在也。齐限说，莫不齐限者，因此故说。

Follow the customs of the country, do not say what is or is not. Why is this said? How does one follow the customs of the country, saying what is or is not? In different places, among different people, for different things, one may say 'bowl', 'wooden cup', 'water bottle', 'cup', or 'vessel'. In different places, among different people, for different things, one may say 'bowl', 'wooden cup', 'water bottle', 'cup', or 'vessel'. For those different things, one may speak of them as true, and the others as false, according to one's ability. Thus, one follows the customs of the country, saying what is or is not. How does one follow the customs of the country, saying what is not or is not? In different places, among different people, for different things, one may say 'bowl', 'wooden cup', 'water bottle', 'cup', or 'vessel'. In different places, among different people, for different things, one may say 'bowl', 'wooden cup', 'water bottle', 'cup', or 'vessel'. For those different things, one does not speak of them as true, and the others as false, according to one's ability. Thus, one follows the customs of the country, saying what is not or is not. Thus, follow the customs of the country, do not say what is or is not. This is why it is said.

「随国俗法，莫是莫非者，此何因说？云何随国俗法？是及非耶？彼彼方、彼彼人间、彼彼事，或说瓯，或说［木＊堕］，或说杅，或说椀，或说器。如彼彼方、彼彼人间、彼彼事，或说瓯，或说［木＊堕］，或说杅，或说椀，或说器，彼彼事随其力，一向说此是真谛，余者虚妄。如是随国俗法，是及非也。云何随国俗法？不是不非耶？彼彼方、彼彼人间、彼彼事，或说瓯，或说［木＊堕］，或说杅，或说椀，或说器。如彼彼方、彼彼人间、彼彼事，或说瓯，或说［木＊堕］，或说杅，或说椀，或说器。彼彼事不随其力，不一向说此是真谛，余者虚妄。如是随国俗法，不是不非也。随国俗法，莫是莫非者，因此故说。

There are dharmas with conflict and dharmas without conflict. What are the dharmas with conflict? What are the dharmas without conflict? If associated with desire, accompanied by joy and pleasure, it is the most inferior practice, the practice of ordinary people. This dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. If one practices suffering for oneself, it is not the practice of the noble ones, and is associated with meaninglessness. This dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. Apart from these two extremes, there is the middle way, which leads to the attainment of the eye, the attainment of wisdom, the attainment of mastery, the attainment of concentration, the attainment of wisdom, the attainment of enlightenment, and the attainment of nirvāṇa. This dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「有诤法、无诤法。云何有诤法？云何无诤法？若欲相应与喜乐俱，极下贱业，为凡夫行，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。若自身苦行，至苦非圣行，无义相应，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。离此二边，则有中道，成眼成智，自在成定，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。

There are bonds that are not completely eliminated, and this dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. There are bonds that are completely eliminated, and this dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「有结不尽，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。有结灭尽，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。

Not seeking inner joy, this dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. Seeking inner joy, this dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「不求内乐，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。求于内乐，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。

If there is joy in the midst of this, it is the joy of ordinary beings, the root of illness, the root of boils, the root of arrow wounds, with food and rebirth, not to be cultivated, not to be practiced, not to be widely spread. I say that it should not be cultivated. This dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. If there is joy in the midst of this, it is the joy of the noble ones, the joy of detachment, the joy of cessation, the joy of right awakening, without food and rebirth, to be cultivated, to be practiced, to be widely spread. I say that it should be cultivated. This dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「于中若有乐，非圣乐是凡夫乐，病本、痈本、箭刺之本，有食有生死，不可修、不可习、不可广布，我说于彼则不可修，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。于中若有乐，是圣乐、无欲乐、离乐、息乐、正觉之乐，无食无生死，可修、可习、可广布，我说于彼则可修也，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。

If there is guidance and teaching in the midst of this that is untrue, false, and meaningless, this dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. If there is guidance and teaching in the midst of this that is true, not false, and meaningless, this dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. If there is guidance and teaching in the midst of this that is true, not false, and meaningful, this dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「于中若有导说不真实、虚妄、无义相应，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。于中若有导说真实、不虚妄、无义相应，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。于中若有导说真实、不虚妄、与义相应，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。

If there is unlimited teaching in the midst of this, this dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. If there is limited teaching in the midst of this, this dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「无齐限说者，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。齐限说者，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。

If there is conformity with the customs of the country, this dharma has conflict. Why does this dharma have conflict? This dharma has suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced wrongly. Therefore, this dharma has conflict. If there is non-conformity with the customs of the country, this dharma is without conflict. Why is this dharma without conflict? This dharma is without suffering, vexation, heat, and sorrow and lamentation, and is practiced rightly. Therefore, this dharma is without conflict.

「随国俗法，是及非，此法有诤。以何等故此法有诤？此法有苦、有烦、有热、有忧戚邪行，是故此法则有诤也。随国俗法，不是不非，此法无诤。以何等故此法无诤？此法无苦、无烦、无热、无忧戚正行，是故此法则无诤也。是谓诤法。汝等当知诤法及无诤法，知诤法及无诤法已，弃舍诤法，修习无诤法，汝等当学。」

Thus, Subhūti, the clansman knows the Dharma as it is by the path of non-conflict.

如是须菩提族姓子以无诤道，于后知法如法。

Knowing the Dharma as it truly is, Subhūti spoke this verse:
This practice is truly empty, abandoning it, one abides in tranquility.

「知法如真实，　　须菩提说偈，此行真实空，　　舍此住止息。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
