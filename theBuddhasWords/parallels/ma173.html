MĀ 173

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 14 心品

173. （一七三）浮弥经

Parallel to: MN 126

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the city of Rājagṛha, staying in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove.

一时，佛游王舍城，在竹林迦兰哆园。

At that time, Venerable Upāli was also in the Meditation Chamber in Rājagṛha. Then, Venerable Upāli, having passed the night, put on his robe, took his bowl, and was about to enter Rājagṛha to beg for alms. Venerable Upāli then thought: "Let me put aside entering Rājagṛha to beg for alms. I shall go to the house of Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca." Then, Venerable Upāli went to the house of Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca.

尔时，尊者浮弥，亦在王舍城无事禅室中。于是，尊者浮弥过夜平旦，著衣持钵，欲入王舍城而行乞食，尊者浮弥复作是念：「且置入王舍城乞食，我宁可往至王子耆婆先那童子家。」于是，尊者浮弥便往至王子耆婆先那童子家。

Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca saw Venerable Upāli coming from afar, and immediately rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms towards Venerable Upāli, and said: "Welcome, Venerable Upāli! It has been a long time since Venerable Upāli came here. Please sit on this seat." Venerable Upāli then took his seat.

王子耆婆先那童子遥见尊者浮弥来，即从座起，偏袒著衣，叉手向尊者浮弥，作如是说：「善来，尊者浮弥！尊者浮弥久不来此，可坐此床。」尊者浮弥即便就坐。

Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca bowed his head at Venerable Upāli's feet, then sat to one side and said: "Venerable Upāli, I wish to ask something. May I ask?"

王子耆婆先那童子稽首尊者浮弥足，却坐一面，白曰：「尊者浮弥！我欲有所问，听我问耶？」

Venerable Upāli replied: "Prince, ask what you wish. I will think about it after hearing it."

尊者浮弥答曰：「王童子！欲问便问，我闻已当思。」

Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca then asked Venerable Upāli: "There are some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who come to me and say: 'Prince, there are those who make vows and practice pure conduct. They will definitely attain the fruit, or without vows, or with and without vows, or neither with nor without vows. They will definitely attain the fruit.' Venerable Upāli, what is your teacher's intention and how does he explain this?"

王童子便问尊者浮弥：「或有沙门梵志来诣我所，而语我曰：『王童子！有人作愿行正梵行，彼必得果，或无愿，或愿无愿，或非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。』尊者浮弥！尊师何意、云何说？」

Venerable Upāli said: "Prince, I have not heard this directly from the World-Honored One, nor from the virtuous ones. Prince, the World-Honored One may have said: 'There are those who make vows and practice pure conduct. They will definitely attain the fruit, or without vows, or with and without vows, or neither with nor without vows. They will definitely attain the fruit.'"

尊者浮弥告曰：「王童子！我不面从世尊闻，亦不从诸梵行闻。王童子！世尊或如是说：『或有人作愿行正梵行，彼必得果，或无愿，或愿无愿，或非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。』」

Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca said: "If Venerable Upāli's teacher has such an intention and speaks like this, then this is the highest in the world, among gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas, from humans to gods. Venerable Upāli, please eat here." Venerable Upāli silently accepted. Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca, knowing that Venerable Upāli had silently accepted, immediately rose from his seat, personally served water for washing, and offered various abundant and delicious food and drink, personally serving and pouring until he was satisfied. After finishing the meal and putting away the utensils, he personally served water for washing. He then took a small seat and sat separately to listen to the Dharma. Venerable Upāli spoke the Dharma for him, inspiring him with yearning and bringing about joy. With immeasurable skillful means, he spoke the Dharma for him, inspiring him with yearning and bringing about joy. Having done so, he rose from his seat and departed, going to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and told the Buddha in detail about what he had discussed with Prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca.

王童子白曰：「若尊者浮弥尊师如是意、如是说者，此于世间，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，最在其上。尊者浮弥！可在此食。」尊者浮弥默然而受。王童子知尊者浮弥默然受已，即从坐起，自行澡水，以极美净妙种种丰饶食噉含消，自手酙酌，令得饱满，食讫收器，行澡水已，取一小床别坐听法。尊者浮弥为彼说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜，无量方便为彼说法，劝发渴仰。成就欢喜已，从坐起去，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却坐一面，与王童子所共论者尽向佛说。

The World-Honored One, having heard this, said: "Upāli, why didn't you tell Prince Jīvaka the four parables?"

世尊闻已，告曰：「浮弥！何意不为王童子说四喻耶？」

Venerable Upāli asked: "World-Honored One, what are the four parables?"

尊者浮弥问曰：「世尊！何谓四喻？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, but they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path. Upāli, it is like a person who wants to obtain milk but milks the horns of a cow. They will definitely not obtain milk. Those who want to obtain milk but milk the horns of a cow without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not obtain milk. Why is that? Because they seek milk wrongly, meaning they milk the horns of a cow. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, but they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path.

世尊答曰：「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得乳者而［（壳－一）／牛］牛角，必不得乳，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得乳而［（壳－一）／牛］牛角，必不得乳。所以者何？以邪求乳，谓［（壳－一）／牛］牛角也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path. Upāli, it is like a person who wants to obtain milk. They feed the cow well and then milk it. They will definitely obtain milk. Those who want to obtain milk and feed the cow well and then milk it without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely obtain milk. Why is that? Because they seek milk rightly, meaning they milk the cow. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得乳者，饱饮饲牛而［（壳－一）／牛］牛乳，彼必得乳，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得乳，饱饮饲牛而［（壳－一）／牛］牛乳，彼必得乳。所以者何？以正求乳，谓［（壳－一）／牛］牛乳也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path. Upāli, it is like a person who wants to obtain butter. They take a vessel, fill it with water, and churn it. They will definitely not obtain butter. Those who want to obtain butter and take a vessel, fill it with water, and churn it without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not obtain butter. Why is that? Because they seek butter wrongly, meaning they churn water. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得酥者，以器盛水，以抨抨之，必不得酥，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得酥，以器盛水，以抨抨之，必不得酥。所以者何？以邪求酥，谓抨水也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path. Upāli, it is like a person who wants to obtain butter. They take a vessel, fill it with curds, and churn it. They will definitely obtain butter. Those who want to obtain butter and take a vessel, fill it with curds, and churn it without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely obtain butter. Why is that? Because they seek butter rightly, meaning they churn curds. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得酥者，以器盛酪，以抨抨之，彼必得酥，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得酥，以器盛酪，以抨抨之，彼必得酥。所以者何？以正求酥，谓抨酪] cf.［丽】也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path. Upāli, it is like a person who wants to obtain oil. They take a press, fill it with sand, soak it with cold water, and press it. They will definitely not obtain oil. Those who want to obtain oil and take a press, fill it with sand, soak it with cold water, and press it without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not obtain oil. Why is that? Because they seek oil wrongly, meaning they press sand. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得油者，以笮具盛沙，以冷水渍而取压之，必不得油，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得油，以笮具盛沙，以冷水渍而取压之，必不得油。所以者何？以邪求油，谓压沙也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path. It is like a person who wants to obtain oil. They take a press, fill it with sesame seeds, soak it with hot water, and press it. They will definitely obtain oil. Those who want to obtain oil and take a press, fill it with sesame seeds, soak it with hot water, and press it without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely obtain oil. Why is that? Because they seek oil rightly, meaning they press sesame seeds. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。犹如有人欲得油者，以笮具盛麻子，以煖汤渍而取压之，彼必得油，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得油，以笮具盛麻子，以煖汤渍而取压之，彼必得油。所以者何？以正求油，谓压麻子也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path. It is like a person who wants to obtain fire. They take wet wood to make a fire drill and drill with wet drill sticks. They will definitely not obtain fire. Those who want to obtain fire and take wet wood to make a fire drill and drill with wet drill sticks without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not obtain fire. Why is that? Because they seek fire wrongly, meaning they drill wet wood. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with wrong views and fixed wrong views, they make vows and practice wrong pure conduct, they will definitely not attain the fruit. Those who practice wrong pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely not attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit wrongly, meaning there is no path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得火者，以湿木作火母，以湿钻钻，必不得火，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得火，以湿木作火母，以湿钻钻，必不得火。所以者何？以邪求火，谓钻湿木也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，邪见、邪见定，彼作愿行行邪梵行，必不得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行邪梵行，必不得果。所以者何？以邪求果，谓无道也。

Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path. It is like a person who wants to obtain fire. They take dry wood to make a fire drill and drill with dry drill sticks. They will definitely obtain fire. Those who want to obtain fire and take dry wood to make a fire drill and drill with dry drill sticks without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely obtain fire. Why is that? Because they seek fire rightly, meaning they drill dry wood. In the same way, Upāli, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with right views and fixed right views, they make vows and practice right pure conduct, they will definitely attain the fruit. Those who practice right pure conduct without vows, with both vows and non-vows, and neither with vows nor without vows will definitely attain the fruit. Why is that? Because they seek the fruit rightly, meaning there is a path.

「浮弥！若有沙门、梵志，正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。浮弥！犹如有人欲得火者，以燥木作火母，以燥钻钻，彼必得火，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿人欲得火，以燥木作火母，以燥钻钻，彼必得火。所以者何？以正求火，谓钻燥木也。如是，浮弥！若有沙门、梵志正见、正见定，彼作愿行行正梵行，彼必得果，无愿、愿无愿、非有愿非无愿行正梵行，彼必得果。所以者何？以正求果，谓有道也。

Upāli, if you tell these four parables to Prince Ajātaśatru, he will surely be greatly delighted upon hearing them, and will make offerings to you for the rest of his life, including clothing, food and drink, bedding, medicine, and various other necessities for living.

「浮弥！若汝为王童子说此四喻者，王童子闻已必大欢喜，供养于汝，尽其形寿，谓衣被、饮食、卧具、汤药及余种种诸生活具。」

Venerable Upāli said, "World-Honored One! I have never heard these four parables before, how could I have explained them? I have only just now heard them from the World-Honored One.

尊者浮弥白曰：「世尊！我本未曾闻此四喻，何由得说？唯今始从世尊闻之。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Upāli and the other bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者浮弥及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
