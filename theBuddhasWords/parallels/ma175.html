MĀ 175

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 14 心品

175. （一七五）受法经

Parallel to: MN 46

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the city of Kūlu, in the country of Kūlu.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦剑磨瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "In this world, there are such desires, such hopes, such loves, such pleasures, and such intentions that lead to the cessation of what is disliked, unloved, and undesirable, and the arising of what is liked, loved, and desirable. If one has such desires, such hopes, such loves, such pleasures, and such intentions, but what is disliked, unloved, and undesirable arises, and what is liked, loved, and desirable ceases, this is a deluded state. My teachings are profound, difficult to see, difficult to realize, and difficult to understand. If my teachings are profound, difficult to see, difficult to realize, and difficult to understand, and what is disliked, unloved, and undesirable ceases, and what is liked, loved, and desirable arises, this is a non-deluded state.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「此世间如是欲、如是望、如是爱、如是乐、如是意，令不喜、不爱、不可法灭，喜、爱、可法生。彼如是欲、如是望、如是爱、如是乐、如是意，然不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭，此是痴法。我法甚深，难见、难觉、难达。如是我法甚深，难见、难觉、难达，不喜、不爱、不可法灭，喜、爱、可法生，是不痴法。

There are four kinds of feeling in the world. What are the four? There is feeling that is pleasant in the present but has a painful result in the future; there is feeling that is painful in the present but has a pleasant result in the future; there is feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future; there is feeling that is pleasant in the present and has a pleasant result in the future.

「世间真实有四种受法。云何为四？或有受法现乐、当来受苦报，或有受法现苦、当来受乐报，或有受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，或有受法现乐、当来亦受乐报。

What is feeling that is pleasant in the present but has a painful result in the future? There is one who takes pleasure and joy in killing, and because of killing, gives rise to pleasure and joy. They take pleasure and joy in taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, false speech, up to wrong view, and because of wrong view, gives rise to pleasure and joy. Such bodily and mental pleasure arises from unwholesomeness, is born from unwholesomeness, does not lead to knowledge, does not lead to awakening, and does not lead to nirvāṇa. This is called feeling that is pleasant in the present but has a painful result in the future.

「云何受法现乐、当来受苦报？或有一自乐自喜杀生，因杀生，生乐生喜，彼自乐自喜不与取、邪婬、妄言乃至邪见，因邪见，生乐生喜。如是身乐、心乐，不善从、不善生，不趣智、不趣觉、不趣涅槃，是谓受法现乐、当来受苦报。

What is feeling that is painful in the present but has a pleasant result in the future? There is one who torments and grieves themselves by abandoning killing, and because of abandoning killing, gives rise to suffering and grief. They torment and grieve themselves by abandoning taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, false speech, up to abandoning wrong view, and because of abandoning wrong view, gives rise to suffering and grief. Such bodily and mental suffering arises from wholesomeness, is born from wholesomeness, leads to knowledge, leads to awakening, and leads to nirvāṇa. This is called feeling that is painful in the present but has a pleasant result in the future.

「云何受法现苦、当来受乐报？或有一自苦自忧断杀，因断杀，生苦生忧，彼自苦自忧断不与取、邪婬、妄言乃至断邪见，因断邪见，生苦生忧。如是身苦、心苦，善从、善生，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃，是谓受法现苦、当来受乐报。

What is feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future? There is one who torments and grieves themselves by killing, and because of killing, gives rise to suffering and grief. They torment and grieve themselves by taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, false speech, up to wrong view, and because of wrong view, gives rise to suffering and grief. Such bodily and mental suffering arises from unwholesomeness, is born from unwholesomeness, does not lead to knowledge, does not lead to awakening, and does not lead to nirvāṇa. This is called feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future.

「云何受法现苦、当来亦受苦报？或有一自苦自忧杀生，因杀生，生苦生忧，彼自苦自忧不与取、邪婬、妄言乃至邪见，因邪见生苦、生忧。如是身苦、心苦，不善从、不善生，不趣智、不趣觉、不趣涅槃，是谓受法现苦、当来亦受苦报。

What is feeling that is pleasant in the present and has a pleasant result in the future? There is one who rejoices and delights themselves by abandoning killing, and because of abandoning killing, gives rise to joy and delight. They rejoice and delight themselves by abandoning taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, false speech, up to abandoning wrong view, and because of abandoning wrong view, gives rise to joy and delight. Such bodily and mental joy arises from wholesomeness, is born from wholesomeness, leads to knowledge, leads to awakening, and leads to nirvāṇa. This is called feeling that is pleasant in the present and has a pleasant result in the future.

「云何受法现乐、当来亦受乐报？或有一自乐自喜断杀，因断杀，生乐生喜，彼自乐自喜断不与取、邪婬、妄言乃至断邪见，因断邪见，生乐生喜。如是身乐、心乐，善从、善生，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃，是谓受法现乐当来亦受乐报。

If there is feeling that is pleasant in the present but has a painful result in the future, the foolish do not know it as it really is. Not knowing it as it really is, they practice it without stopping. Practicing it without stopping, they give rise to what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable, and the pleasing, lovable, and desirable is extinguished. It is like a medicine called Amani, which has good color, aroma, and taste, but is mixed with poison. If someone takes it for illness, when taken, it has good color, aroma, and taste, and is palatable without harming the throat. But after taking it, it does not become medicine in the stomach. This feeling that is pleasant in the present but has a painful result in the future, the foolish do not know it as it really is. Not knowing it as it really is, they practice it without stopping. Practicing it without stopping, they give rise to what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable, and the pleasing, lovable, and desirable is extinguished. This is called the Dharma of foolishness.

「若有受法现乐、当来受苦报，彼痴者不知如真，此受法现乐、当来受苦报，不知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭。犹如阿摩尼药，一分好色香味，然杂以毒，或有人为病故服，服时好色香味，可口而不伤咽，服已在腹，便不成药。如是此受法现乐、当来受苦报，彼痴者不知如真，此受法现乐、当来受苦报，不知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭，是谓痴法。

If there is feeling that is painful in the present but has a pleasant result in the future, the foolish do not know it as it really is. Not knowing it as it really is, they do not practice it and stop it. Not practicing it and stopping it, they give rise to what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable, and the pleasing, lovable, and desirable is extinguished. This is called the Dharma of foolishness.

「若有受法现苦、当来受乐报，彼痴者不知如真，此受法现苦、当来受乐报，不知如真已，便不习行而断之，不习行断已，便不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭，是谓痴法。

If there is feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future, the foolish do not know it as it really is. Not knowing it as it really is, they practice it without stopping. Practicing it without stopping, they give rise to what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable, and the pleasing, lovable, and desirable is extinguished. It is like excrement and urine, mixed with poison. If someone takes it for illness, when taken, it has bad color, smell, and taste, is unpalatable and harms the throat. But after taking it, it does not become medicine in the stomach. This feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future, the foolish do not know it as it really is. Not knowing it as it really is, they practice it without stopping. Practicing it without stopping, they give rise to what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable, and the pleasing, lovable, and desirable is extinguished. This is called the Dharma of foolishness.

「若有受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，彼痴者不知如真，此受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，不知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭。犹如大小便，复杂以毒，或有人为病故服，服时恶色臭无味，不可口而伤咽，服已在腹便不成药。如是此受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，彼痴者不知如真，此受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，不知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭，是谓痴法。

If there is feeling that is pleasant in the present and has a pleasant result in the future, the foolish do not know it as it really is. Not knowing it as it really is, they do not practice it and stop it. Not practicing it and stopping it, they give rise to what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable, and the pleasing, lovable, and desirable is extinguished. This is called the Dharma of foolishness.

「若有受法现乐、当来亦受乐报，彼痴者不知如真，此受法现乐、当来亦受乐报，不知如真已，便不习行而断之，不习行断已，便不喜、不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭，是谓痴法。彼习行法不知如真，不习行法不知如真，习行法不知如真，不习行法不知如真已，不习行法习，习行法不习，不习行法习，习行法不习已，便不喜，不爱、不可法生，喜、爱、可法灭，是谓痴法。

If there is feeling that is pleasant in the present and has a painful result in the future, the wise know it as it really is. This feeling that is pleasant in the present and has a painful result in the future, knowing it as it really is, they do not practice it and stop it. Not practicing it and stopping it, they give rise to what is pleasing, lovable, and desirable, and what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable is extinguished. This is called the Dharma of wisdom.

「若有受法现乐、当来受苦报，彼慧者知如真，此受法现乐、当来受苦报，知如真已，便不习行而断之，不习行断已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭，是谓慧法。若有受法现苦、当来受乐报，彼慧者知如真，此受法现苦、当来受乐报，知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭。犹如大小便和若干种药，或有人为病故服，服时恶色臭无味，不可口而伤咽，服已在腹便成药。如是此受法现苦、当来受乐报，彼慧者知如真，此受法现苦、当来受乐报，知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭，是谓慧法。

If there is feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future, the wise know it as it really is. This feeling that is painful in the present and has a painful result in the future, knowing it as it really is, they do not practice it and stop it. Not practicing it and stopping it, they give rise to what is pleasing, lovable, and desirable, and what is not pleasing, not lovable, and undesirable is extinguished. This is called the Dharma of wisdom.

「若有受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，彼慧者知如真，此受法现苦、当来亦受苦报，知如真已，便不习行而断之，不习行断已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭，是谓慧法。若有受法现乐、当来亦受乐报，彼慧者知如真，此受法现乐、当来亦受乐报，知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭。犹如酥、蜜和若干种药，或有人为病故服，服时好色香味，可口而不伤咽，服已在腹便成药。如是此受法现乐、当来亦受乐报，彼慧者知如真，此受法现乐、当来亦受乐报，知如真已，便习行不断，习行不断已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭，是谓慧法。彼习行法知如真，不习行法知如真，习行法知如真，不习行法知如真已，便习行法习，不习行法不习，习行法习，不习行法不习已，便喜、爱、可法生，不喜、不爱、不可法灭，是谓慧法。世间真实有是四种受法者，因此故说。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
