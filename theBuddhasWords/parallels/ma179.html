MĀ 179

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 14 心品

179. （一七九）五支物主经

Parallel to: MN 78

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the householder Pañcaśikha left Śrāvastī in the morning and went to where the Buddha was, wishing to see the World-Honored One and make offerings and pay respects. Pañcaśikha then thought: "Let me put aside going to see the World-Honored Buddha for now. Perhaps he is in meditation or with the venerable bhikṣus. I should now go to a Sāṇḍāmaṇḍika heretic's garden." Thereupon, Pañcaśikha went to a nearby place for recreation and enjoyment, near the Kumbhīra River, and went to a Sāṇḍāmaṇḍika heretic's garden.

尔时，五支物主平旦出舍卫国，往诣佛所，欲见世尊供养礼事，五支物主便作是念：「且置往见佛世尊，或能宴坐及诸尊比丘，我今宁可诣一娑逻末利异学园。」于是，五支物主便至比道游戏欢乐，近巾头阿梨，往诣一娑逻末利异学园。

At that time, in the Sāṇḍāmaṇḍika heretic's garden, there was a heretic śramaṇa named Vairāṭiputra, who was the leader there, the teacher of the masses, respected by the masses, and the leader of a large assembly of five hundred heretic teachers. He was making a great commotion in the assembly, speaking in various animalistic ways, such as discussing kings, thieves, fighting, food and drink, clothing, women, girls, prostitutes, the world, wrong paths, and the ocean. He was gathering to discuss various animalistic ways. The heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra saw Pañcaśikha coming from afar and admonished his own assembly to be silent, saying: "You all be silent and do not speak. Gather yourselves. This is the disciple of the śramaṇa Gautama, Pañcaśikha. Among the disciples of the śramaṇa Gautama who live in Śrāvastī, there is none greater than Pañcaśikha. Why is that? He delights in silence and praises silence. If he sees this assembly being silent, he may come forward." At that time, the heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra, having admonished his own assembly, remained silent himself.

彼时，娑逻末利异学园中，有一异学沙门文祁子，在于彼中为大宗主，众人之师，众所敬重，统领大众五百异学师。彼在扰乱众发高大音声，其声喧閙，说若干种畜生之论，谓论王、论贼、论鬪诤、论饮食、论衣被、论妇人、论童女、论婬女、论世间、论邪道、论海中，如是比聚集，论若干种畜生之论。异学沙门文祁子遥见五支物主来，便自勅己众，令默然住：「汝等默然，莫复语言，宜自收敛，此沙门瞿昙弟子五支物主来。若有沙门瞿昙在家弟子居舍卫国者，无过于五支物主。所以者何？彼爱乐默然，称说默然，若彼见此众默然者，或能来前。」彼时，异学沙门文祁子止己众已，自默然住。

Then Pañcaśikha went to where the heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. The heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra said: "Pañcaśikha, if there are four things, I establish that one has accomplished goodness, supreme goodness, the unsurpassed person, attained the ultimate truth, and is an upright śramaṇa. What are the four? Not committing evil deeds with the body, not speaking evil words, not engaging in wrong livelihood, and not thinking evil thoughts. Pañcaśikha, if one has these four things, I establish that one has accomplished goodness, supreme goodness, the unsurpassed person, attained the ultimate truth, and is an upright śramaṇa."

于是，五支物主往诣异学沙门文祁子所，共相问讯，却坐一面，异学沙门文祁子语曰：「物主！若有四事，我施设彼成就善、第一善、无上士，得第一义、质直沙门。云何为四？身不作恶业、口不恶言，不行邪命、不念恶念。物主！若有此四事者，我施设彼成就善、第一善、无上士，得第一义、质直沙门。」

Pañcaśikha heard what the heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra said, and did not refute it. He rose from his seat and left, thinking: "I will go to the Buddha myself and ask about this meaning." He then went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and told the Buddha everything that had been discussed with the heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra.

五支物主闻异学沙门文祁子所说，不是不非，从坐起去。「如此所说，我自诣佛，当问此义。」便往诣佛，稽首作礼，却坐一面，与异学沙门文祁子所共论者，尽向佛说。

The World-Honored One, having heard this, said: "Pañcaśikha, if it is as the heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra says, then even an infant child with soft limbs, lying on its back and sleeping, should have accomplished goodness, supreme goodness, the unsurpassed person, attained the ultimate truth, and be an upright śramaṇa. Pañcaśikha, an infant child does not even have the notion of a body, let alone commit evil deeds with the body. It can only move its body. Pañcaśikha, an infant child does not even have the notion of speech, let alone speak evil words. It can only cry. Pañcaśikha, an infant child does not even have the notion of life, let alone engage in wrong livelihood. It can only groan. Pañcaśikha, an infant child does not even have the notion of thought, let alone have evil thoughts. It only thinks of its mother's milk. Pañcaśikha, if it is as the heretic śramaṇa Vairāṭiputra says, then such an infant child has accomplished goodness, supreme goodness, the unsurpassed person, attained the ultimate truth, and is an upright śramaṇa.

世尊闻已，告曰：「物主！如异学沙门文祁子所说，若当尔者，婴孩童子支节柔软，仰向卧眠，亦当成就善、第一善、无上士，得第一义，质直沙门。物主！婴孩童子尚无身想，况复作身恶业耶？唯能动身。物主！婴孩童子，尚无口想，况复恶言耶？唯能得啼。物主！婴孩童子，尚无命想，况复行邪命耶？唯有呻吟。物主！婴孩童子，尚无念想，况复恶念耶？唯念母乳。物主！若如异学沙门文祁子说者，如是婴孩童子成就善、第一善、无上士，得第一义、质直沙门。

Pañcaśikha, if there are four things, I establish that they have accomplished goodness and supreme goodness, but they are not the unsurpassed person, have not attained the ultimate truth, and are not upright śramaṇas. What are the four? Not committing evil deeds with the body, not speaking evil words, not engaging in wrong livelihood, and not having evil thoughts. Pañcaśikha, if there are these four things, I establish that they have accomplished goodness and supreme goodness, but they are not the unsurpassed person, have not attained the ultimate truth, and are not upright śramaṇas. Pañcaśikha, I establish that bodily and verbal karma is morality. Pañcaśikha, I establish that thought is a mental factor that accompanies the mind. Pañcaśikha, I say that one should know unwholesome morality, know from where unwholesome morality arises, know where unwholesome morality is completely extinguished, where it is completely destroyed, and how the noble disciples practice to extinguish unwholesome morality. Pañcaśikha, I say that one should know wholesome morality, know from where wholesome morality arises, know where wholesome morality is completely extinguished, where it is completely destroyed, and how the noble disciples practice to extinguish wholesome morality. Pañcaśikha, I say that one should know unwholesome thoughts, know from where unwholesome thoughts arise, know where unwholesome thoughts are completely extinguished, where they are completely destroyed, and how the noble disciples practice to extinguish unwholesome thoughts. Pañcaśikha, I say that one should know wholesome thoughts, know from where wholesome thoughts arise, know where wholesome thoughts are completely extinguished, where they are completely destroyed, and how the noble disciples practice to extinguish wholesome thoughts.

「物主！若有四事，我施设彼成就善、第一善，然非无上士，不得第一义，亦非质直沙门。云何为四？身不作恶业，口不恶言，不行邪命，不念恶念。物主！若有此四事，我施设彼成就善、第一善，然非无上士，不得第一义，亦非质直沙门。物主！身业、口业者，我施设是戒；物主！念者，我施设是心所有与心相随。物主！我说当知不善戒，当知不善戒从何而生？当知不善戒何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？当知贤圣弟子云何行灭不善戒耶？物主！我说当知善戒，当知善戒从何而生？当知善戒何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？当知贤圣弟子云何行灭善戒耶？物主！我说当知不善念，当知不善念从何而生，当知不善念何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？当知贤圣弟子云何行灭不善念耶？物主！我说当知善念，当知善念从何而生？当知善念何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？当知贤圣弟子云何行灭善念耶？

Pañcaśikha, what is unwholesome morality? Unwholesome bodily action, unwholesome verbal and mental action - this is called unwholesome morality. Pañcaśikha, from where does this unwholesome morality arise? I say that it arises from the mind. What is the mind? If the mind has desire, anger, and delusion, know that unwholesome morality arises from this mind. Pañcaśikha, where is unwholesome morality completely extinguished, where is it completely destroyed? The well-learned noble disciple abandons unwholesome bodily action and cultivates wholesome bodily action, abandons unwholesome verbal and mental action and cultivates wholesome verbal and mental action. This unwholesome morality is completely extinguished and completely destroyed. Pañcaśikha, how do the noble disciples practice to extinguish unwholesome morality? If the well-learned noble disciple observes the internal body as the body, up to observing feelings, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, the noble disciples practice in this way to extinguish unwholesome morality.

「物主！云何不善戒耶？不善身行，不善口、意行，是谓不善戒。物主！此不善戒从何而生？我说彼所从生，当知从心生。云何为心？若心有欲、有恚、有痴，当知不善戒从是心生。物主！不善戒何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？多闻圣弟子舍身不善业，修身善业，舍口、意不善业，修口、意善业，此不善戒灭无余，败坏无余。物主！贤圣弟子云何行灭不善戒？若多闻圣弟子观内身如身，至观觉、心、法如法，贤圣弟子如是行者，灭不善戒也。

Pañcaśikha, what is wholesome morality? Wholesome bodily action, wholesome verbal and mental action - this is called wholesome morality. Pañcaśikha, from where does this wholesome morality arise? I say that it arises from the mind. What is the mind? If the mind is without desire, anger, and delusion, know that wholesome morality arises from this mind. Pañcaśikha, where is wholesome morality completely extinguished, where is it completely destroyed? If the well-learned noble disciple practices morality without attachment to morality, this wholesome morality is completely extinguished and completely destroyed. Pañcaśikha, how do the noble disciples practice to extinguish wholesome morality? If the well-learned noble disciple observes the internal body as the body, up to observing feelings, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, the noble disciples practice in this way to extinguish wholesome morality.

「物主！云何善戒耶？善身业，善口、意业，是谓善戒。物主！此善戒从何而生？我说彼所从生，当知从心生。云何为心？若心无欲、无恚、无痴，当知善戒从是心生。物主！善戒何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？若多闻圣弟子行戒不著戒，此善戒灭无余，败坏无余。物主！贤圣弟子云何行灭善戒？若多闻圣弟子观内身如身，至观觉、心、法如法，贤圣弟子如是行者，灭善戒也。

Pañcaśikha, what is unwholesome thought? Thought of desire, thought of anger, thought of harm - this is called unwholesome thought. Pañcaśikha, from where does this unwholesome thought arise? I say that it arises from perception. What is perception? I say that perception is of many kinds, immeasurable kinds, and various kinds of practice, such as thought of desire, thought of anger, and thought of harm. Pañcaśikha, because beings have perceptions of the desire realm, unwholesome thoughts arise, associated with the desire realm. If there is perception, unwholesome thoughts arise because of that perception, associated with the desire realm. Pañcaśikha, because beings have perceptions of the anger and harm realms, unwholesome thoughts arise, associated with the anger and harm realms. If there is perception, unwholesome thoughts arise because of that perception, associated with the anger and harm realms. This unwholesome thought arises from that perception. Pañcaśikha, where is unwholesome thought completely extinguished, where is it completely destroyed? If the well-learned noble disciple, detached from desires, detached from evil and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, has joy and happiness, and attains the first concentration and abides in it, this unwholesome thought is completely extinguished and completely destroyed. Pañcaśikha, how do the noble disciples practice to extinguish unwholesome thought? If the well-learned noble disciple observes the internal body as the body, up to observing feelings, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, the noble disciples practice in this way to extinguish unwholesome thought.

「物主！云何不善念耶？欲念、恚念、害念，是谓不善念。物主！不善念从何而生？我说彼所从生，当知从想生。云何为想？我说想多种、无量种、若干种行，或欲想，或恚想，或害想。物主！众生因欲界想故，生不善念，欲界相应。若有想者，因彼想故，生不善念，欲界相应。物主！众生因恚、害界想故，生不善念，恚、害界相应。若有想者，因彼想故，生不善念，恚、害界相应，此不善念从是想生。物主！不善念何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？若多闻圣弟子离欲、离恶不善之法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，得初禅成就游。此不善念灭无余，败坏无余。物主！贤圣弟子云何行灭不善念？若多闻圣弟子观内身如身，至观觉、心、法如法，贤圣弟子如是行者，灭不善念也。

Pañcaśikha, what is wholesome thought? Thought of non-desire, thought of non-anger, thought of non-harm - this is called wholesome thought. Pañcaśikha, from where does this wholesome thought arise? I say that it arises from perception. What is perception? I say that perception is of many kinds, immeasurable kinds, and various kinds of practice, such as thought of non-desire, thought of non-anger, and thought of non-harm. Pañcaśikha, because beings have perceptions of the non-desire realm, wholesome thoughts arise, associated with the non-desire realm. If there is perception, wholesome thoughts arise because of that perception, associated with the non-desire realm. Pañcaśikha, because beings have perceptions of the non-anger and non-harm realms, wholesome thoughts arise, associated with the non-anger and non-harm realms. If there is perception, wholesome thoughts arise because of that perception, associated with the non-anger and non-harm realms. This wholesome thought arises from that perception. Pañcaśikha, where is wholesome thought completely extinguished, where is it completely destroyed? If the well-learned noble disciple, with the cessation of joy, with the cessation of suffering, with the cessation of previous joy and sorrow, without pain, without pleasure, with the purity of equanimity and mindfulness, attains the fourth concentration and abides in it, this wholesome thought is completely extinguished and completely destroyed. Pañcaśikha, how do the noble disciples practice to extinguish wholesome thought? If the well-learned noble disciple observes the internal body as the body, up to observing feelings, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, the noble disciples practice in this way to extinguish wholesome thought.

「物主！云何善念耶？无欲念、无恚念、无害念，是谓善念。物主！善念从何而生？我说彼所从生，当知从想生。云何为想？我说想多种、无量种、若干种行，或无欲想，或无恚想，或无害想。物主！众生因无欲界想故，生善念，无欲界相应。若有想者，因彼想故，生善念，无欲界相应。物主！众生因无恚、无害界故，生善念无恚、无害界相应。若有想者，因彼想故生善念，无恚、无害界相应，此善念从是想生。物主！善念何处灭无余？何处败坏无余？若多闻圣弟子乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，得第四禅成就游。此善念灭无余，败坏无余。物主！贤圣弟子云何行灭善念？若多闻圣弟子观内身如身，至观觉、心、法如法，贤圣弟子如是行者，灭善念也。

Pañcaśikha, if the well-learned noble disciple, with wisdom, knows unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows the arising of unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows the cessation of unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows the path leading to the cessation of unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows the arising of unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows the cessation of unwholesome precepts as they really are, knows the path leading to the cessation of unwholesome precepts as they really are, then unwholesome precepts are completely extinguished and completely destroyed. The noble disciple practices in this way to know unwholesome precepts as they really are. If the well-learned noble disciple, with wisdom, knows wholesome precepts as they really are, knows the arising of wholesome precepts as they really are, knows the cessation of wholesome precepts as they really are, knows the path leading to the cessation of wholesome precepts as they really are, knows wholesome precepts as they really are, knows the arising of wholesome precepts as they really are, knows the cessation of wholesome precepts as they really are, knows the path leading to the cessation of wholesome precepts as they really are, then wholesome precepts are completely extinguished and completely destroyed. The noble disciple practices in this way to know wholesome precepts as they really are. If the well-learned noble disciple, with wisdom, knows unwholesome thought as it really is, knows the arising of unwholesome thought as it really is, knows the cessation of unwholesome thought as it really is, knows the path leading to the cessation of unwholesome thought as it really is, knows unwholesome thought as it really is, knows the arising of unwholesome thought as it really is, knows the cessation of unwholesome thought as it really is, knows the path leading to the cessation of unwholesome thought as it really is, then unwholesome thought is completely extinguished and completely destroyed. The noble disciple practices in this way to know unwholesome thought as it really is. If the well-learned noble disciple, with wisdom, knows wholesome thought as it really is, knows the arising of wholesome thought as it really is, knows the cessation of wholesome thought as it really is, knows the path leading to the cessation of wholesome thought as it really is, knows wholesome thought as it really is, knows the arising of wholesome thought as it really is, knows the cessation of wholesome thought as it really is, knows the path leading to the cessation of wholesome thought as it really is, then wholesome thought is completely extinguished and completely destroyed. The noble disciple practices in this way to know wholesome thought as it really is. Why is that? 「物主！若多闻圣弟子以慧观不善戒知如真，从生不善戒知如真，此不善戒灭无余，败坏无余，知如真，以慧观。贤圣弟子如是行者，灭不善戒知如真。以慧观善戒知如真，从生善戒知如真，此善戒灭无余，败坏无余，知如真，以慧观。贤圣弟子如是行者，灭善戒知如真。以慧观不善念知如真，从生不善念知如真，此不善念灭无余，败坏无余，知如真，以慧观。贤圣弟子如是行者，灭不善念知如真。以慧观善念知如真，从生善念知如真，此善念灭无余，败坏无余，知如真，以慧观。贤圣弟子如是行者，灭善念知如真。所以者何？因正见故生正志，因正志故生正语，因正语故生正业，因正业故生正命，因正命故生正方便，因正方便故生正念，因正念故生正定。贤圣弟子心如是定已，便解脱一切婬、怒、痴。

Pañcaśikha, when the noble disciple is thus liberated in mind, he knows that all births are exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this mundane state - he knows it as it really is. This is called the noble disciple who has accomplished the eightfold path of the noble ones and has exhausted the contaminants. He has accomplished ten things. What are the ten? They are right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration, right knowledge, and right liberation. Pañcaśikha, what is the noble disciple who has accomplished the eightfold path of the noble ones? It is right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. Pañcaśikha, what is the one who has exhausted the contaminants and has accomplished ten things? It is right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration, right knowledge, and right liberation. Pañcaśikha, if there are ten things, I declare that he has accomplished the good, the supreme good, the supreme person, and has attained the supreme meaning, the straight path.

「物主！贤圣弟子如是正心解脱已，便知一切生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。是谓学见迹成就八支，漏尽阿罗诃成就十支。物主！云何学见迹成就八支？谓学正见至学正定，是谓学见迹成就八支。物主！云何漏尽阿罗诃成就十支？谓无学正见至无学正智，是谓漏尽阿罗诃成就十支。物主！若有十支，我施设彼成就善、第一善、无上士，得第一义、质直沙门。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The five-headed Pañcaśikha and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced them.
