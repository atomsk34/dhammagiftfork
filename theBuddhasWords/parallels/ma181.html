MĀ 181

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 14 心品

181. （一八一）多界经

Parallel to: MN 115 / AN 1.268–277 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was alone in a quiet place, sitting in meditation and reflecting. He thought to himself: "All fears arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows arise from ignorance, not from wisdom." Then, in the afternoon, Venerable Ānanda rose from his meditation and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! I was alone in a quiet place, sitting in meditation and reflecting. I thought to myself: 'All fears arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows arise from ignorance, not from wisdom.'"

尔时，尊者阿难独安靖处宴坐思惟，心作是念：「诸有恐怖，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。」于是，尊者阿难则于晡时，从宴坐起，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却住一面，白曰：「世尊！我今独安靖处宴坐思惟，心作是念：『诸有恐怖，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。』」

The World-Honored One said: "So it is, Ānanda! So it is, Ānanda! All fears arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. Ānanda, it is like a fire burning a house or building from a pile of reeds or grass. Ānanda, in the same way, all fears arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. Ānanda, in the past, all fears arose from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows arose from ignorance, not from wisdom. Ānanda, in the future, all fears will arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows will arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. Ānanda, in the present, all fears arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. All calamities, disasters, and sorrows arise from ignorance, not from wisdom. Ānanda, this is called ignorance having fears, while wisdom has no fears. Ignorance has calamities, disasters, and sorrows, while wisdom has no calamities, disasters, and sorrows. Ānanda, all fears, calamities, disasters, and sorrows can be obtained from ignorance, not from wisdom."

世尊告曰：「如是。阿难！如是。阿难！诸有恐怖，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。阿难！犹如从苇［卄／积］草［卄／积］生火，烧楼阁堂屋。阿难！如是诸有恐怖，从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。阿难！昔过去时若有恐怖，彼一切亦从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。阿难！当来时诸有恐怖，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。阿难！今现在诸有恐怖，从愚痴生，不从智慧。诸有遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴生，不从智慧。阿难！是为愚痴有恐怖，智慧无恐怖。愚痴有遭事、灾患、忧戚，智慧无遭事、灾患、忧戚。阿难！诸有恐怖、遭事、灾患、忧戚，彼一切从愚痴可得，不从智慧。」

Then Venerable Ānanda wept with tears streaming down, joined his palms towards the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! How is a bhikṣu ignorant and not wise?"

于是，尊者阿难悲泣泪出，叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！云何比丘愚痴非智慧？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu does not know the realms, does not know the abodes, does not know the causes and conditions, and does not know what is possible and what is not possible, Ānanda! Such a bhikṣu is ignorant and not wise."

世尊答曰：「阿难！若有比丘不知界，不知处，不知因缘，不知是处、非处者，阿难！如是比丘愚痴非智慧。」

Venerable Ānanda said: "World-Honored One! Such a bhikṣu is ignorant and not wise. World-Honored One! How is a bhikṣu wise and not ignorant?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！如是比丘愚痴非智慧。世尊！云何比丘智慧非愚痴？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu knows the realms, knows the abodes, knows the causes and conditions, and knows what is possible and what is not possible, Ānanda! Such a bhikṣu is wise and not ignorant."

世尊答曰：「阿难！若有比丘知界、知处、知因缘，知是处、非处者。阿难！如是比丘智慧非愚痴。」

Venerable Ānanda said: "World-Honored One! Such a bhikṣu is wise and not ignorant. World-Honored One! How does a bhikṣu know the realms?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！如是比丘智慧非愚痴。世尊！云何比丘知界？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu sees the eighteen realms and knows them as they truly are: the eye realm, form realm, and eye consciousness realm; the ear realm, sound realm, and ear consciousness realm; the nose realm, smell realm, and nose consciousness realm; the tongue realm, taste realm, and tongue consciousness realm; the body realm, tactile object realm, and body consciousness realm; the mind realm, mental object realm, and mind consciousness realm. Ānanda! Seeing these eighteen realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the six realms and knowing them as they truly are: the earth realm, water realm, fire realm, wind realm, space realm, and consciousness realm. Ānanda! Seeing these six realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the six realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm of desire, the realm of anger, the realm of harm, the realm without desire, the realm without anger, and the realm without harm. Ānanda! Seeing these six realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the six realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm of pleasure, the realm of pain, the realm of joy, the realm of sorrow, the realm of equanimity, and the realm of ignorance. Ānanda! Seeing these six realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the four realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm of perception, the realm of conception, the realm of volition, and the realm of consciousness. Ānanda! Seeing these four realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the three realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm of desire, the realm of form, and the formless realm. Ānanda! Seeing these three realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the three realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm of form, the formless realm, and the realm of cessation. Ānanda! Seeing these three realms and knowing them as they truly are.

世尊答曰：「阿难！若有比丘见十八界知如真，眼界、色界、眼识界，耳界、声界、耳识界、鼻界、香界、鼻识界，舌界、味界、舌识界，身界、触界、身识界，意界、法界、意识界。阿难！见此十八界知如真。复次，阿难！见六界知如真，地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。阿难！见此六界知如真。复次，阿难！见六界知如真，欲界、恚界、害界，无欲界、无恚界、无害界。阿难！见此六界知如真。复次，阿难！见六界知如真，乐界、苦界、喜界、忧界、舍界、无明界。阿难！见此六界知如真。复次，阿难！见四界知如真，觉界、想界、行界、识界。阿难！见此四界知如真。复次，阿难！见三界知如真，欲界、色界、无色界。阿难！见此三界知如真。复次，阿难！见三界知如真，色界、无色界、灭界。阿难！见此三界知如真。

"Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the three realms and knowing them as they truly are: the past realm, the future realm, and the present realm. Ānanda! Seeing these three realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the three realms and knowing them as they truly are: the excellent realm, the not excellent realm, and the middle realm. Ānanda! Seeing these three realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the three realms and knowing them as they truly are: the wholesome realm, the unwholesome realm, and the indeterminate realm. Ānanda! Seeing these three realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the three realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm of training, the realm of no-more-training, and the realm of neither training nor no-more-training. Ānanda! Seeing these three realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the two realms and knowing them as they truly are: the realm with taints and the realm without taints. Ānanda! Seeing these two realms and knowing them as they truly are. Furthermore, Ānanda! Seeing the two realms and knowing them as they truly are: the conditioned realm and the unconditioned realm. Ānanda! Seeing these two realms and knowing them as they truly are. Ānanda! Seeing these sixty-two realms and knowing them as they truly are. Ānanda! Such a bhikṣu knows the realms."

「复次，阿难！见三界知如真，过去界、未来界、现在界。阿难！见此三界知如真。复次，阿难！见三界知如真，妙界、不妙界、中界。阿难！见此三界知如真。复次，阿难！见三界知如真，善界、不善界、无记界。阿难！见此三界知如真。复次，阿难！见三界知如真，学界、无学界、非学非无学界。阿难！见此三界知如真。复次，阿难！见二界知如真，有漏界、无漏界。阿难！见此二界知如真。复次，阿难！见二界知如真，有为界、无为界、阿难！见此二界知如真。阿难！见此六十二界知如真。阿难！如是比丘知界。」

Venerable Ānanda said: "World-Honored One! Such a bhikṣu knows the realms. World-Honored One! How does a bhikṣu know the sense bases?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！如是比丘知界。世尊！云何比丘知处？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu sees the twelve sense bases and knows them as they truly are: the eye sense base and form sense base, the ear sense base and sound sense base, the nose sense base and smell sense base, the tongue sense base and taste sense base, the body sense base and touch sense base, the mind sense base and mental phenomena sense base. Ānanda! Seeing these twelve sense bases and knowing them as they truly are. Ānanda! Such a bhikṣu knows the sense bases."

世尊答曰：「阿难！若有比丘见十二处知如真，眼处、色处，耳处、声处，鼻处、香处，舌处、味处，身处、触处，意处、法处。阿难！见此十二处知如真。阿难！如是比丘知处。」

Venerable Ānanda said: "World-Honored One! Such a bhikṣu knows the sense bases. How does a bhikṣu know the causes and conditions?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！如是比丘知处。云何比丘知因缘？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu sees the causes and conditions and knows them as they truly are: because of this, there is that; without this, there is no that; this arises, that arises; this ceases, that ceases. That is to say, conditioned by ignorance, there are volitional formations, up to conditioned by birth, there is old age and death. If ignorance ceases, then volitional formations cease, up to if birth ceases, then old age and death cease. Ānanda! Such a bhikṣu knows the causes and conditions."

世尊答曰：「阿难！若有比丘见因缘及从因缘起知如真，因此有彼，无此无彼，此生彼生，此灭彼灭，谓缘无明有行乃至缘生有老死；若无明灭则行灭，乃至生灭则老死灭。阿难！如是比丘知因缘。」

Venerable Ānanda said: "World-Honored One! Such a bhikṣu knows the causes and conditions. How does a bhikṣu know what is possible and what is impossible?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！如是比丘知因缘。云何比丘知是处、非处？」

The World-Honored One replied: "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu sees what is possible as possible and knows it as it truly is, and sees what is impossible as impossible and knows it as it truly is. Ānanda! If there were two wheel-turning kings ruling the world at the same time, it would never be possible. If there is one wheel-turning king ruling the world, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If there were two Tathāgatas in the world, it would never be possible. If there is one Tathāgata in the world, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would intentionally kill his parents, kill an arhat, destroy the community of monks, have evil thoughts towards the Buddha, or draw blood from the Tathāgata, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would intentionally kill his parents, kill an arhat, destroy the community of monks, have evil thoughts towards the Buddha, or draw blood from the Tathāgata, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would intentionally break the precepts, abandon the precepts, and leave the monastic life, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would intentionally break the precepts, abandon the precepts, and leave the monastic life, it is certainly possible. If a person who has seen the truth would abandon this inner [teaching] and seek respect and merit from outside, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would abandon this inner [teaching] and seek respect and merit from outside, it is certainly possible.

世尊答曰：「阿难！若有比丘见处是处知如真，见非处是非处知如真。阿难！若世中有二转轮王并治者，终无是处，若世中有一转轮王治者，必有是处。阿难！若世中有二如来者，终无是处，若世中有一如来者，必有是处。阿难！若见谛人故害父母，杀阿罗诃，破坏圣众，恶心向佛，出如来血者，终无是处。若凡夫人故害父母，杀阿罗诃，破坏圣众，恶心向佛，出如来血者，必有是处。阿难！若见谛人故犯戒，舍戒罢道者，终无是处。若凡夫人故犯戒，舍戒罢道者，必有是处。若见谛人舍离此内，从外求尊、求福田者，终无是处。若凡夫人舍离此内，从外求尊、求福田者，必有是处。

Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would say to other śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas: 'Venerables! What can be seen is seen, what can be known is known,' it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would say to other śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas: 'Venerables! What can be seen is seen, what can be known is known,' it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would believe in divination and ask about auspiciousness and inauspiciousness, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would believe in divination and ask about auspiciousness and inauspiciousness, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would see suffering and trouble in divination and asking about auspiciousness and inauspiciousness in accordance with other śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, and see it as true, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would see suffering and trouble in divination and asking about auspiciousness and inauspiciousness in accordance with other śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, and see it as true, it is certainly possible.

「阿难！若见谛人从余沙门、梵志作是说：『诸尊！可见则见，可知则知。』者，终无是处。若凡夫人从余沙门、梵志作是说：『诸尊！可见则见，可知则知。』者，必有是处。阿难！若见谛人信卜问吉凶者，终无是处。若凡夫人信卜问吉凶者，必有是处。阿难！若见谛人从余沙门、梵志卜问吉凶相应，见有苦有烦，见是真者，终无是处。若凡夫人从余沙门、梵志卜问吉凶相应，见有苦有烦，见是真者，必有是处。

Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would experience extreme suffering and heavy suffering, which is unlovable, unenjoyable, unthinkable, and unmindful, and would abandon this inner [teaching] and seek it from outside, and if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who hold one, two, three, four, or many mantras, up to hundreds of thousands of mantras, to free him from suffering, seeking suffering, the practice of suffering, the course of suffering, and the end of suffering, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person abandons this inner [teaching] and seeks it from outside, and if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who hold one, two, three, four, or many mantras, up to hundreds of thousands of mantras, to free him from suffering, seeking suffering, the practice of suffering, the course of suffering, and the end of suffering, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If a person who has seen the truth would experience the eight kinds of existence, it would never be possible. If an ordinary person would experience the eight kinds of existence, it is certainly possible.

「阿难！若见谛人生极苦甚重苦，不可爱、不可乐、不可思、不可念乃至断命，舍离此内，更从外求。或有沙门、梵志，或持一句呪，二句、三句、四句、多句、百千句呪，令脱我苦，是求苦、习苦、趣苦、苦尽者，终无是处。若凡夫人舍离此内，更从外求，或有沙门、梵志持一句呪，二句、三句、四句、多句、百千句呪，令脱我苦，是求苦、习苦、趣苦、苦尽者，必有是处。阿难！若见谛人受八有者，终无是处，若凡夫人受八有者，必有是处。

Ānanda! If one engages in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and as a result of this, after the body breaks up and life ends, one goes to a good place and is reborn in heaven, it would never be possible. If one engages in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and as a result of this, after the body breaks up and life ends, one goes to an evil place and is reborn in hell, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If one engages in good bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and as a result of this, after the body breaks up and life ends, one goes to an evil place and is reborn in hell, it would never be possible. If one engages in good bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and as a result of this, after the body breaks up and life ends, one goes to a good place and is reborn in heaven, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If one engages in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and experiences a pleasant result, it would never be possible. Ānanda! If one engages in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and experiences a painful result, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If one engages in good bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and experiences a painful result, it would never be possible. If one engages in good bodily, verbal, and mental actions, and experiences a pleasant result, it is certainly possible.

「阿难！若身恶行，口、意恶行，因此缘此，身坏命终，趣至善处，生于天中者，终无是处。若身恶行，口、意恶行，因此缘此，身坏命终，趣至恶处，生地狱中者，必有是处。阿难！若身妙行，口、意妙行，因此缘此，身坏命终，趣至恶处，生地狱中者，终无是处。若身妙行，口、意妙行，因此缘此，身坏命终，趣至善处，生天中者，必有是处。阿难！若身恶行，口、意恶行，受乐报者，终无是处。阿难！若身恶行，口、意恶行，受苦报者，必有是处。阿难！若身妙行，口、意妙行，受苦报者，终无是处。若身妙行，口、意妙行，受乐报者，必有是处。

Ānanda! If one does not abandon the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one tries to establish the four foundations of mindfulness, it would never be possible. If one abandons the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one tries to establish the four foundations of mindfulness, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If one does not abandon the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one's mind is not established in the four foundations of mindfulness, and one tries to cultivate the seven factors of awakening, it would never be possible. If one abandons the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one's mind is established in the four foundations of mindfulness, and one cultivates the seven factors of awakening, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If one does not abandon the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one's mind is not established in the four foundations of mindfulness, and one does not cultivate the seven factors of awakening, and one tries to attain unsurpassed perfect awakening, it would never be possible. If one abandons the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one's mind is established in the four foundations of mindfulness, and one cultivates the seven factors of awakening, and one attains unsurpassed perfect awakening, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! If one does not abandon the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one's mind is not established in the four foundations of mindfulness, and one does not cultivate the seven factors of awakening, and one does not attain unsurpassed perfect awakening, and does not reach the end of suffering, it would never be possible. If one abandons the five hindrances, one's mind is defiled and wisdom is weak, and one's mind is established in the four foundations of mindfulness, and one cultivates the seven factors of awakening, and one attains unsurpassed perfect awakening, and reaches the end of suffering, it is certainly possible. Ānanda! Thus a bhikṣu knows what is possible and what is not possible.

「阿难！若不断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心正立四念处者，终无是处。若断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心正立四念处者，必有是处。阿难！若不断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心不正立四念处，欲修七觉意者，终无是处。若断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心正立四念处，修七觉意者，必有是处。阿难！若不断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心不正立四念处，不修七觉意，欲得无上正尽觉者，终无是处。若断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心正立四念处，修七觉意，得无上正尽觉者，必有是处。阿难！若不断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心不正立四念处，不修七觉意，不得无上正尽觉，尽苦边者，终无是处。若断五盖、心秽、慧羸，心正立四念处，修七觉意，得无上正尽觉，尽苦边者，必有是处。阿难！如是比丘知是处、非处。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! Such a bhikṣu knows what is possible and what is not possible."

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！如是比丘知是处、非处。」

Then Venerable Ānanda, with palms joined towards the Buddha, said, "World-Honored One! What is the name of this sūtra? How should we uphold it?"

于是，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！此经名何？云何奉持？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! You should uphold this as the 'Sūtra on the Many Realms, the Dharma Realms, and the Ambrosia Realms,' the 'Many Drums, the Dharma Drums, and the Ambrosia Drums,' the 'Dharma Mirror,' and the 'Four Chapters.' Therefore, this sūtra is called the 'Sūtra on the Many Realms.'"

世尊告曰：「阿难！当受持此多界、法界、甘露界、多鼓、法鼓、甘露鼓、法镜、四品。是故称此经名曰多界。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teachings, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The tenth chapter of the Sūtra on the Many Realms is completed.
