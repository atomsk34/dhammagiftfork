MĀ 186

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 15 双品

186. （一八六）求解经

Parallel to: MN 47

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Kaurava, in the city of Mathura.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦劒摩瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If one does not know the minds of others through the mind, how can one seek understanding from the Tathāgata, who is the perfectly enlightened one, the inconceivable one?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「缘于彼意，不知他心如真者，彼世尊正尽觉不可知，云何求解于如来乎？」

At that time, the bhikṣus said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. Please explain it. Having heard it, we will extensively understand its meaning."

时，诸比丘白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Bhikṣus! Listen carefully and contemplate it well. I will explain it in detail for you." At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

佛便告曰：「比丘！谛听，善思念之，我当为汝具分别说。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The World-Honored One said, "Bhikṣus! If one does not know the minds of others, one should seek to understand the Tathāgata in two ways: one, knowing forms with the eye; two, hearing sounds with the ear. If there are defiled eye and ear knowing dharmas, does that venerable one have them or not? When seeking, one will know that the defiled eye and ear knowing dharmas that venerable one does not have. If not, one should seek further. If there are mixed eye and ear knowing dharmas, does that venerable one have them or not? When seeking, one will know that the mixed eye and ear knowing dharmas that venerable one does not have. If not, one should seek further. If there are pure eye and ear knowing dharmas, does that venerable one have them or not? When seeking, one will know that the pure eye and ear knowing dharmas that venerable one has. If so, one should seek further. Does that venerable one practice these dharmas for a long time or temporarily? When seeking, one will know that that venerable one practices these dharmas for a long time, not temporarily. If so, one should seek further. Does that venerable one enter this samādhi for fame and gain, or not for fame and gain? When seeking, one will know that that venerable one does not enter this samādhi for fame and gain.

世尊告曰：「缘于彼意，不知他心如真者，当以二事求解如来，一者眼知色，二者耳闻声。若有秽污眼、耳知法，是彼尊者为有、为无耶？若求时，则知所有秽污眼、耳知法，彼尊者无。若无此者，当复更求，若有杂眼、耳知法，是彼尊者为有、为无耶？若求时，则知所有杂眼、耳知法，彼尊者无。若无此者，当复更求，若有白净眼、耳知法，是彼尊者为有、为无耶？若求时，则知所有白净眼、耳知法，彼尊者有。若有此者，当复更求，彼尊者为长夜行此法、为暂行耶？若求时，则知彼尊者长夜行此法，不暂行也。若常行者，当复更求，彼尊者为为名誉，为为利义入此禅耶？不为名誉，不为利义入此禅耶？若求时，则知彼尊者非为灾患故入此禅也。

If someone says, 'That venerable one practices happily, not out of fear, free from desire, not engaging in desire, desire is exhausted,' then one should ask them, 'Virtuous one! What practices, what powers, what wisdom do you have that you can observe and say that that venerable one practices happily, not out of fear, free from desire, not engaging in desire, desire is exhausted?' If they answer, 'Virtuous one! I do not know their mind, nor do I know it through other means. However, that venerable one, whether alone, in a group, or in an assembly, if there is a well-gone one, or one who is guided by a well-gone one, and if one can see that virtuous one through food, I do not know it myself. I have heard it directly from that venerable one, and I have received it face-to-face. I practice happily, not out of fear, free from desire, not engaging in desire, desire is exhausted. Virtuous one! I have these practices, these powers, this wisdom, which allow me to observe and say that that venerable one practices happily, not out of fear, free from desire, not engaging in desire, desire is exhausted.'

「若有作是说：『彼尊者乐行非恐怖，离欲不行欲，欲已尽也。』便应问彼：『贤者！有何行、有何力、有何智，令贤者自正观如是说，彼尊者乐行非恐怖，离欲不行欲，欲已尽耶？』彼若作是答：『贤者！我不知彼心，亦非余事知，然彼尊者或独住，或在众，或在集会。若有善逝，若为善逝所化为宗主，因食可见彼贤者，我不自知，我从彼尊者闻，面前咨受，我乐行非恐怖，离欲不行欲，欲已尽也。贤者！我有是行、有是力、有是智，令我自正观如是说，彼尊者乐行不恐怖，离欲不行欲，欲已尽也。』

In this regard, one should further ask about the Tathāgata's teachings. If there are defiled eye and ear knowing dharmas, there is a place where these dharmas are completely extinguished without remainder. If there are mixed eye and ear knowing dharmas, there is a place where these dharmas are completely extinguished without remainder. If there are pure dharmas, there is a place where these dharmas are completely extinguished without remainder. The Tathāgata will answer accordingly. If there are defiled eye and ear knowing dharmas, there is a place where these dharmas are completely extinguished without remainder. If there are mixed eye and ear knowing dharmas, there is a place where these dharmas are completely extinguished without remainder. If there are defiled eye and ear knowing dharmas, the Tathāgata has completely extinguished, uprooted, and eradicated them, so they will not arise again. If there are mixed eye and ear knowing dharmas, the Tathāgata has completely extinguished, uprooted, and eradicated them, so they will not arise again. If there are pure dharmas, I have such purity, such realm, such śramaṇa, I have accomplished this true Dharma and Vinaya. A faithful disciple goes to see the Tathāgata, attends the Tathāgata, hears the Dharma from the Tathāgata, and the Tathāgata teaches the Dharma, higher and higher, more and more wonderful, well removing the black and white. The Tathāgata teaches the Dharma, higher and higher, more and more wonderful, well removing the black and white, having heard it thus, one knows to cut off one dharma, to attain the ultimate in all dharmas, to have tranquil faith in the World-Honored One, that World-Honored One is truly and completely enlightened.

「于中当复问彼如来法。若有秽污眼、耳知法，有彼处此法灭尽无余。若有杂眼、耳知法，有彼处此法灭尽无余。若有白净法，有彼处此法灭尽无余，如来为彼答。若有秽污眼、耳知法，有彼处此法灭尽无余。若有杂眼、耳知法，有彼处此法灭尽无余。若有秽污眼、耳知法，如来灭断拔绝根本，终不复生。若有杂眼、耳知法，如来灭断拔绝根本，终不复生。若有白净法，如是我白净，如是境界，如是沙门，我如是成就此正法、律。有信弟子往见如来，奉侍如来，从如来闻法，如来为说法，上复上，妙复妙，善除黑白。如来为说法，上复上，妙复妙，善除黑白者，如是如是闻已，知断一法，于诸法得究竟，靖信世尊，彼世尊正尽觉也。

"One should further ask them, 'Virtuous one! What practices, what powers, what wisdom do you have that you know to cut off one dharma, to attain the ultimate in all dharmas, to have tranquil faith in the World-Honored One, that World-Honored One is truly and completely enlightened?' They may answer, 'Virtuous one! I do not know the World-Honored One's mind, nor do I know it through other means. I have such tranquil faith in the World-Honored One because the World-Honored One has taught the Dharma to me, higher and higher, more and more wonderful, well removing the black and white. Virtuous one! As the World-Honored One has taught the Dharma to me, so I have heard it. The Tathāgata has taught the Dharma to me, higher and higher, more and more wonderful, well removing the black and white. Having heard it thus, I know to cut off one dharma, to attain the ultimate in all dharmas, to have tranquil faith in the World-Honored One, that World-Honored One is truly and completely enlightened. Virtuous one! I have these practices, these powers, this wisdom, which allow me to know to cut off one dharma, to attain the ultimate in all dharmas, to have tranquil faith in the World-Honored One, that World-Honored One is truly and completely enlightened. If one has these practices, these powers, deeply attached to the Tathāgata's faith, having established the faculty of faith, this is called the basis of faith, the view, the unbreakable wisdom, associated with the śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, gods, Māra, Brahmā, and others in the world, none can take it away. Seeking to understand the Tathāgata in this way, knowing the Tathāgata in this way.'"

「复应问彼：『贤者！有何行、有何力、有何智，令贤者知断一法，于诸法得究竟？靖信世尊，彼世尊正尽觉耶？』彼如是答：『贤者！我不知世尊心，亦非余事知，我因世尊有如是靖信，世尊为我说法，上复上，妙复妙，善除黑白。贤者！如如世尊为我说法者，如是如是我闻，如来为我说法，上复上，妙复妙，善除黑白。如是如是我闻已，知断一法，于诸法得究竟，靖信世尊，彼世尊正尽觉也。贤者！我有是行、有是力、有是智，令我知断一法，于诸法得究竟，靖信世尊，彼世尊正尽觉也。若有此行、有此力，深著如来信根已立者，是谓信见本不坏智相应，沙门、梵志、天及魔、梵及余世间无有能夺，如是求解如来，如是正知如来。』」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The fifth sūtra on seeking understanding is completed.

求解经第五竟
