MĀ 187

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 15 双品

187. （一八七）说智经

Parallel to: MN 112

The wise Aiyana, the bright Kloumin, the holy path, the small
emptiness in the eastern garden, the great emptiness is at the end.

说智阿夷那　　拘楼明圣道东园论小空　　大空最在后

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in the Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in the Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in the Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in the Anāthapiṇḍada

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "If a bhikṣu comes to you and says that he has attained the knowledge of the exhaustion of birth, the establishment of the holy life, the completion of what was to be done, and the non-arising of future existence, knowing it as it really is, when you hear it, you should approve of it, rejoice in it, and practice it accordingly. Having approved of it, rejoiced in it, and practiced it accordingly, you should further ask that bhikṣu: 'Noble one! The World-Honored One has spoken of the five aggregates of clinging: the form aggregate of clinging, the feeling aggregate of clinging, the perception aggregate of clinging, the volitional formations aggregate of clinging, and the consciousness aggregate of clinging. Noble one! How does one know and see these five aggregates of clinging so as to know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging?'

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有比丘来向汝说已所得智，我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真者，汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是问彼比丘：『贤者！世尊说五盛阴，色盛阴，觉、想、行、识盛阴。贤者！云何知、云何见此五盛阴，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱耶？』

A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this: 'Venerable ones! The form aggregate of clinging is not a fruit, empty, undesirable, impermanent, unreliable, and subject to change. I know it thus. If there is desire, attachment, grasping, bondage, and the fetters of bondage and attachment towards the form aggregate of clinging, then they are exhausted, desireless, extinguished, ceased, and stopped. One knows without clinging and attains the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints. Similarly, the feeling aggregate of clinging, the perception aggregate of clinging, the volitional formations aggregate of clinging, and the consciousness aggregate of clinging are not fruits, empty, undesirable, impermanent, unreliable, and subject to change. I know it thus. If there is desire, attachment, grasping, bondage, and the fetters of bondage and attachment towards the consciousness aggregate of clinging, then they are exhausted, desireless, extinguished, ceased, and stopped. One knows without clinging and attains the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints. Venerable ones! I know and see these five aggregates of clinging thus, and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints without clinging.' A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this.

「漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答：『诸贤！色盛阴非果、空虚、不可欲、不恒有、不可倚、变易法，我知如是。若于色盛阴有欲、有染、有著、有缚、缚著使者，彼尽、无欲、灭、息、止，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱；如是觉、想、行、识盛阴，非果、空虚、不可欲、不恒有、不可倚、变易法，我知如是。若于识盛阴有欲、有染、有著、有缚、缚著使者，彼尽、无欲、灭、息、止，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。诸贤！我如是知、如是见此五盛阴，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。』漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答。

When you hear it, you should approve of it, rejoice in it, and practice it accordingly. Having approved of it, rejoiced in it, and practiced it accordingly, you should further ask that bhikṣu: 'Noble one! The World-Honored One has spoken of four nutriments by which beings subsist and grow. What are the four? They are: coarse and fine solid food, contact, intention, and consciousness. Noble one! How does one know and see these four nutriments so as to know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging?'

「汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是问彼比丘：『贤者！世尊说四食，众生以此得存长养。云何为四？一曰抟食麤细，二曰更乐，三曰意念，四曰识也。贤者！云何知、云何见此四食，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱耶？』

A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this: 'Venerable ones! Regarding solid food, I am neither elated nor depressed, not attached, not bound, not defiled, not clinging, liberated, fully liberated, with the mind free from delusion, with birth ended, the holy life established, what was to be done is done, there is no more coming to any state of existence, known as it really is. Similarly, regarding contact, intention, and consciousness as nutriment, I am neither elated nor depressed, not attached, not bound, not defiled, not clinging, liberated, fully liberated, with the mind free from delusion, with birth ended, the holy life established, what was to be done is done, there is no more coming to any state of existence, known as it really is. Venerable ones! I know and see these four nutriments thus, and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging.' A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this.

「漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答：『诸贤！我于抟食，意不高不下，不倚不缚，不染不著，得解得脱，尽得解脱，心离颠倒，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。如是更乐、意念、识食，不高不下，不倚不缚，不染不著，得解得脱，尽得解脱，心离颠倒，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。诸贤！我如是知，如是见此四食，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。』漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答。

When you hear it, you should approve of it, rejoice in it, and practice it accordingly. Having approved of it, rejoiced in it, and practiced it accordingly, you should further ask that bhikṣu: 'Noble one! The World-Honored One has spoken of four statements. What are the four? They are: seeing as seeing, hearing as hearing, knowing as knowing, and knowing as knowing. Noble one! How does one know and see these four statements so as to know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging?'

「汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是问彼比丘：『贤者！世尊说四说。云何为四？一曰见见说，二曰闻闻说，三曰识识说，四曰知知说。贤者！云何知、云何见此四说，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱耶？』

A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this: 'Venerable ones! Regarding seeing as seeing, I am neither elated nor depressed, not attached, not bound, not defiled, not clinging, liberated, fully liberated, with the mind free from delusion, with birth ended, the holy life established, what was to be done is done, there is no more coming to any state of existence, known as it really is. Similarly, regarding hearing as hearing, knowing as knowing, and knowing as knowing, I am neither elated nor depressed, not attached, not bound, not defiled, not clinging, liberated, fully liberated, with the mind free from delusion, with birth ended, the holy life established, what was to be done is done, there is no more coming to any state of existence, known as it really is. Venerable ones! I know and see these four statements thus, and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging.' A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this.

「漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答：『诸贤！我于见见说，不高不下，不倚不缚，不染不著，得解得脱，尽得解脱，心离颠倒，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。如是闻闻、识识、知知说，不高不下，不倚不缚，不染不著，得解得脱，尽得解脱，心离颠倒，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。诸贤！我如是知、如是见此四说，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。』漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答。

When you hear it, you should approve of it, rejoice in it, and practice it accordingly. Having approved of it, rejoiced in it, and practiced it accordingly, you should further ask that bhikṣu: 'Noble one! The World-Honored One has spoken of the six internal sense bases: the eye sense base, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind sense bases. Noble one! How does one know and see these six internal sense bases so as to know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging?'

「汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是问彼比丘：『贤者！世尊说内六处，眼处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意处。贤者！云何知、云何见此内六处，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱耶？』

A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this: 'Venerable ones! Regarding the eye and eye consciousness, the eye consciousness and the dharmas cognized by eye consciousness, I know both of these dharmas. Venerable ones! If the eye and eye consciousness, the eye consciousness and the dharmas cognized by eye consciousness, are exhausted, they are exhausted, desireless, extinguished, ceased, and stopped. I know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging. Similarly, regarding the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, and the mental consciousness, the mental consciousness and the dharmas cognized by mental consciousness, I know both of these dharmas. Venerable ones! If the mind and mental consciousness, the mental consciousness and the dharmas cognized by mental consciousness, are exhausted, they are exhausted, desireless, extinguished, ceased, and stopped. I know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging. Venerable ones! I know and see these six internal sense bases thus, and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging.' A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this.

「漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答：『诸贤！我于眼及眼识，眼识知法俱知，二法知已。诸贤！若眼及眼识，眼识知法，乐已尽，彼尽、无欲、灭、息、止，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱，如是耳、鼻、舌、身、意及意识，意识知法俱知，二法知已。诸贤！若意及意识，意识知法，乐已尽，彼尽、无欲、灭、息、止，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。诸贤！我如是知、如是见此内六处，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。』漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答。

When you hear it, you should approve of it, rejoice in it, and practice it accordingly. Having approved of it, rejoiced in it, and practiced it accordingly, you should further ask that bhikṣu: 'Noble one! The World-Honored One has spoken of the six elements: the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. Noble one! How does one know and see these six elements so as to know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging?'

「汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是问彼比丘：『贤者！世尊说六界，地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。贤者！云何知、云何见此六界，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱耶？』

A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this: 'Venerable ones! I do not see the earth element as mine, nor am I possessed by the earth element. The earth element is not the self, but rather the three feelings abide in dependence on the earth element, and consciousness is attached to it. When it is exhausted, desireless, extinguished, ceased, and stopped, I know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging. Similarly, regarding the water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness elements, they are not mine, nor am I possessed by the consciousness element. The consciousness element is not the self, but rather the three feelings abide in dependence on the consciousness element, and consciousness is attached to it. When it is exhausted, desireless, extinguished, ceased, and stopped, I know and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging. Venerable ones! I know and see these six elements thus, and attain the liberation of the mind through the exhaustion of the taints, without clinging.' A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this.

「漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答：『诸贤！我不见地界是我所，我非地界所，地界非是神，然谓三受依地界住，识使所著，彼尽、无欲、灭、息、止，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。如是水、火、风、空、识界，非是我所，我非识界所，识界非是神，然谓三受依识界住，识使所著，彼尽、无欲、灭、息、止，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。诸贤！我如是知、如是见此六界，得知无所受，漏尽心解脱。』漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答。

When you hear it, you should approve of it, rejoice in it, and practice it accordingly. Having approved of it, rejoiced in it, and practiced it accordingly, you should further ask that bhikṣu: 'Noble one! How does one know and see, regarding this internal body with its consciousness and external characteristics, the complete cessation of the afflictions of 'I', 'mine', and conceit, the uprooting of their foundation, and the prevention of their future arising?'

「汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是问彼比丘：『贤者！云何知、云何见，此内身共有识及外诸相，一切我、我作及慢使断知，拔绝根本，终不复生？』

A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints and knows that the holy life has been established should answer like this: 'Venerable ones! Before I went forth to practice the path, I was weary of birth, old age, sickness, death, crying, distress, sorrow, and lamentation, and wished to completely eliminate this great mass of suffering. Venerable ones! Having become weary and disgusted, I contemplated thus: "Living at home is narrow and confined, a place of defilements, while going forth to practice the path is open and vast." I thought: "Now, living at home, I am bound by fetters and cannot practice the holy life to the end of my life. Why don't I give up little or much wealth, little or many relatives, shave off my hair and beard, put on the yellow robe, leave home out of faith, and go forth to practice the path?" Venerable ones! Later, I gave up little or much wealth, little or many relatives, shaved off my hair and beard, put on the yellow robe, left home out of faith, and went forth to practice the path. Venerable ones! Having gone forth to practice the path, I abandoned the characteristics of a householder, accepted the precepts of a bhikṣu, practiced and guarded the precepts, and attained liberation through discernment. I also well restrained my deportment and conduct, and seeing even the slightest fault, I always felt fear and guarded the precepts.

「漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答：『诸贤！我本未出家学道时，厌生老病死、啼泣困苦、愁戚忧悲，欲断此大苦阴。诸贤！我厌患已而作是观，在家至狭，尘劳之处，出家学道，发露旷大。我今在家，为锁所锁，不得尽形寿净修梵行，我宁可舍少财物及多财物，舍少亲族及多亲族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。诸贤！我于后时舍少财物及多财物，舍少亲族及多亲族，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。诸贤！我出家学道，舍族相已，受比丘要，修习禁戒，守护从解脱，又复善摄威仪礼节，见纤介罪，常怀畏怖，受持学要。

Venerable ones! I have abandoned killing, cut off killing, laid aside weapons, have a sense of shame and conscience, have a compassionate mind, and benefit all beings, even insects. I have purified my mind from killing. I have abandoned taking what is not given, cut off taking what is not given, take only what is given, delight in taking what is given, am always fond of giving, rejoice in giving without stinginess, and do not expect anything in return. I have purified my mind from taking what is not given. Venerable ones! I have abandoned sexual misconduct, cut off sexual misconduct, diligently practiced celibacy, diligently practiced the sublime practice, was pure and undefiled, free from desire, and cut off sexual desire. I have purified my mind from sexual misconduct. Venerable ones! I have abandoned false speech, cut off false speech, spoken the truth, delighted in the truth, abided in the truth, and was not moved. I was trustworthy, did not deceive the world, and was not deceitful. I have purified my mind from false speech. Venerable ones! I have abandoned divisive speech, cut off divisive speech, practiced non-divisive speech, did not break others apart, did not listen to this in order to break apart that, did not listen to that in order to break apart this, wanted to reconcile those who were divided, and delighted in those who were united. I did not form factions, did not delight in factions, and did not praise factions. I have purified my mind from divisive speech.

「『诸贤！我离杀，断杀，弃舍刀杖，有惭有愧，有慈悲心，饶益一切乃至蜫虫，我于杀生净除其心。我离不与取，断不与取，与而后取，乐于与取，常好布施，欢喜无恡，不望其报，我于不与取净除其心。诸贤！我离非梵行，断非梵行，勤修梵行，精勤妙行，清净无秽，离欲断婬，我于非梵行净除其心。诸贤！我离妄言，断于妄言，真谛言，乐真谛，住真谛，不移动，一切可信，不欺世间，我于妄言净除其心。诸贤！我离两舌，断于两舌，行不两舌，不破坏他，不此闻语彼，欲破坏此，不彼闻语此，欲破坏彼，离者欲合，合者欢喜，不作群党，不乐群党，不称群党，我于两舌净除其心。

Venerable ones! I have abandoned harsh speech, cut off harsh speech. Whatever I say, my words are harsh, coarse, unpleasant to the ear, disagreeable, disliked by many, and cause others suffering and distress, making it difficult for them to concentrate. I have cut off such speech. Whatever I say is gentle, smooth, pleasant to the ear, agreeable, liked by many, and causes others happiness and joy, with a clear and distinct voice, not causing others to fear, and enabling them to concentrate. I have purified my mind from harsh speech. Venerable ones! I have abandoned idle chatter, cut off idle chatter, speak at the right time, speak the truth, speak the Dharma, speak the meaning, speak to stop disputes, delight in stopping disputes, speak in a timely manner, speak appropriately, give good instruction, and give good admonition. I have purified my mind from idle chatter. Venerable ones! I have abandoned commerce, cut off commerce, abandoned weighing and measuring, and also not engaged in buying and selling. I did not bind others, did not hope for a discount, did not deceive others with small profits, and did not harm others. I have purified my mind from commerce.

「『诸贤！我离麤言，断于麤言。若有所言，辞气麤犷，恶声逆耳，众所不喜，众所不爱，使他苦恼，令不得定，断如是言。若有所说，清和柔润，顺耳入心，可喜可爱，使他安乐，言声具了，不使人畏，令他得定，说如是言，我于麤言净除其心。诸贤！我离绮语，断绮语，时说、真说、法说、义说、止息说，乐止息诤事，顺时得宜，善教善呵，我于绮语净除其心。诸贤！我离治生，断于治生，弃舍称量及斗斛，亦不受货，不缚束人，不望折斗量，不以小利侵欺于人，我于治生净除其心。

Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking a widow or a virgin, cut off taking a widow or a virgin. I have purified my mind from taking a widow or a virgin. Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking a slave or a servant, cut off taking a slave or a servant. I have purified my mind from taking a slave or a servant. Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking an elephant, horse, cow, or sheep, cut off taking an elephant, horse, cow, or sheep. I have purified my mind from taking an elephant, horse, cow, or sheep. Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking a chicken or a pig, cut off taking a chicken or a pig. I have purified my mind from taking a chicken or a pig. Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking a field, shop, or storehouse, cut off taking a field, shop, or storehouse. I have purified my mind from taking a field, shop, or storehouse. Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking raw rice, wheat, or beans, cut off taking raw rice, wheat, or beans. I have purified my mind from taking raw rice, wheat, or beans. Venerable ones! I have abandoned alcohol, cut off alcohol. I have purified my mind from drinking alcohol. Venerable ones! I have abandoned high and wide beds, cut off high and wide beds. I have purified my mind from high and wide beds. Venerable ones! I have abandoned garlands, necklaces, perfumes, and cosmetics, cut off garlands, necklaces, perfumes, and cosmetics. I have purified my mind from garlands, necklaces, perfumes, and cosmetics. Venerable ones! I have abandoned singing, dancing, and acting, and going to see them, cut off singing, dancing, and acting, and going to see them. I have purified my mind from singing, dancing, and acting, and going to see them. Venerable ones! I have abandoned taking precious things that are not given, cut off taking precious things that are not given. I have purified my mind from taking precious things that are not given. Venerable ones! I have abandoned eating after the midday meal, cut off eating after the midday meal, eat one meal a day, do not eat at night, and eat at the proper time. I have purified my mind from eating after the midday meal.

「『诸贤！我离受寡妇、童女，断受寡妇、童女，我于受寡妇、童女净除其心。诸贤！我离受奴婢，断受奴婢，我于受奴婢净除其心。诸贤！我离受象、马、牛、羊，断受象、马、牛、羊，我于受象、马、牛、羊净除其心。诸贤！我离受鸡、猪，断受鸡、猪，我于受鸡、猪净除其心。诸贤！我离受田业、店肆，断受田业、店肆，我于受田业、店肆净除其心。诸贤！我离受生稻、麦、豆，断受生稻、麦、豆，我于受生稻、麦、豆净除其心。诸贤！我离酒，断酒，我于饮酒净除其心。诸贤！我离高广大床，断高广大床，我于高广大床净除其心。诸贤！我离华鬘、璎珞、涂香、脂粉，断华鬘、璎珞、涂香、脂粉，我于花鬘、璎珞、涂香、脂粉净除其心。诸贤！我离歌舞倡伎及往观听，断歌舞倡伎及往观听，我于歌舞倡伎及往观听净除其心。诸贤！我离受生色像宝，断受生色像宝，我于受生色像宝净除其心。诸贤！我离过中食，断过中食，一食、不夜食、学时食，我于过中食净除其心。

Venerable ones! I have already accomplished this holy body of precepts, and further practice contentment. I wear clothes to cover my body and eat to sustain my life. Wherever I go, my robes and alms bowl follow me, without any attachment. Just as a goose flies through the air with its two wings, so am I. Venerable ones! I have already accomplished this holy body of precepts and extreme contentment, and further guard the sense faculties, always mindful of closing them off, being mindful of desire and understanding, guarding the mind with mindfulness, and accomplishing it, always wishing to arouse the mind. If the eyes see forms, they do not grasp characteristics, nor do they savor forms, because of anger and disputes, guarding the eye faculty. In the mind, greed, sorrow, and evil and unwholesome dharmas do not arise, heading towards them, guarding the eye faculty. In this way, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and if the mind knows dharmas, they do not grasp characteristics, nor do they savor dharmas, because of anger and disputes, guarding the mind faculty. In the mind, greed, sorrow, and evil and unwholesome dharmas do not arise, heading towards them, guarding the mind faculty. Venerable ones! I have already accomplished this holy body of precepts and extreme contentment, the holy guarding of the sense faculties, right knowledge in coming and going, well observing and distinguishing, bending, stretching, bowing, looking up, dignified deportment, well wearing the saṃghāṭī robe and other robes and alms bowl, walking, standing, sitting, lying down, sleeping, waking, speaking, and being silent, all with right knowledge.

「『诸贤！我已成就此圣戒身，复行知足，衣取覆形，食取充躯，我所往处，衣钵自随，无有顾恋。犹如鴈鸟与两翅俱飞翔空中，我亦如是。诸贤！我已成就此圣戒身及极知足，复守诸根，常念闭塞，念欲明达，守护念心，而得成就，恒欲起意。若眼见色，然不受相，亦不味色，谓忿诤故，守护眼根。心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，趣向彼故，守护眼根。如是耳、鼻、舌、身，若意知法，然不受相，亦不味法，谓忿诤故，守护意根。心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，趣向彼故，守护意根。诸贤！我已成就此圣戒身及极知足，圣护诸根，正知出入，善观分别，屈伸低仰，仪容庠序，善著僧伽梨及诸衣钵，行住坐卧、眠寤语默，皆正知之。

Venerable ones! I have already accomplished this holy body of precepts and extreme contentment, and further accomplished the holy guarding of the sense faculties, attained right knowledge in coming and going, dwelling alone in seclusion in a place free from affairs, or going to the foot of a tree, an empty and peaceful place, a mountain cave, a stone chamber, an open space, a pile of straw, or to a forest, or to a charnel ground. Venerable ones! I have already dwelt in a place free from affairs, or gone to the foot of a tree, an empty and peaceful place, spread out a sitting mat, sat cross-legged, straightened the body and mind, and focused the mind, eliminating greed and desire, and having no disputes. Seeing others' wealth and various necessities of life, I do not give rise to greed and desire, wishing to obtain them. I have purified my mind from greed and desire. In this way, I have eliminated anger, sloth and torpor, restlessness and remorse, and doubt and confusion. Regarding wholesome dharmas, I have no hesitation. I have purified my mind from doubt and confusion. Venerable ones! I have already eliminated these five hindrances, defilements of the mind, and weakness of wisdom, and attained the fourth dhyāna, accomplished and dwelt in it. Venerable ones! I have already attained such a mind of concentration, pure, undefiled, pliant, well-established, attained an immovable mind, and directed it towards the knowledge and realization of the destruction of the taints.

「『诸贤！我已成就此圣戒身及极知足，亦成就圣] cf.［丽】护诸根，得正知出入，独住远离在无事处，或至树下空安靖处，山岩石室、露地穰［卄／积］，或至林中，或在塜间。诸贤！我已在无事处，或至树下空安靖处，敷尼师檀，结跏趺坐，正身正愿，及念不向，断除贪伺，心无有诤。见他财物、诸生活具，不起贪伺，欲令我得，我于贪伺净除其心。如是瞋恚、睡眠、调悔，断疑度惑，于诸善法无有犹豫，我于疑惑净除其心。诸贤！我已断此五盖、心秽、慧羸，离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游。诸贤！我已得如是定心清净，无秽无烦，柔软善住，得不动心，趣向漏尽通智作证。

"Venerable ones! I know this suffering as it truly is, I know this origin of suffering, I know this cessation of suffering, I know this path to the cessation of suffering as it truly is, I know these taints, I know the origin of these taints, I know the cessation of these taints, I know the path to the cessation of these taints as it truly is. Knowing and seeing thus, my mind is liberated from the outflow of desire, from the outflow of existence, and from the outflow of ignorance. Being liberated, I know I am liberated. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there will be no more of this state of existence. Venerable ones! Knowing and seeing thus, I have cut off the conceit of 'I' and 'mine' regarding internal and external forms, and the conceit of 'I' and 'mine' regarding all forms. I know that the root is eradicated, and it will not arise again." A bhikṣu who has exhausted the taints should answer thus when asked about the establishment of the holy life.

「『诸贤！我知此苦如真，知此苦习、知此苦灭、知此苦灭道如真，知此漏、知此漏习、知此漏灭、知此漏灭道如真。彼如是知、如是见，欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱，解脱已便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。诸贤！我如是知、如是见，内身有识及外诸相，一切我、我行及慢使断知，拔绝根本，终不复生。』漏尽比丘得知梵行已立，法者应如是答。

"Having heard this, you should all agree, rejoice, and practice it well. Having agreed, rejoiced, and practiced it well, you should further say to that bhikṣu: 'Venerable one! We have already agreed and rejoiced in your initial explanation, but we wish to further seek wisdom, skillful answers, and eloquence from you. Therefore, we ask you further questions.'"

「汝等闻之，当善然可，欢喜奉行。善然可彼，欢喜奉行已，当复如是语彼比丘：『贤者！初说我等已可意欢喜，然我等欲从贤者上复上，求智慧应答辩才，以是故我等从贤者问复问耳。』」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
