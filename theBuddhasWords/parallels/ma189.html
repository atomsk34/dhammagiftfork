MĀ 189

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 15 双品

189. （一八九）圣道经

Parallel to: MN 117 / SĀ 785 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the city of Kūlu, in the country of Kūlu.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦劒磨瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "There is one path that enables sentient beings to attain purity, to be free from sorrow, lamentation, and crying, to eliminate suffering, distress, and torment, and to attain the Dharma. It is called the noble right concentration. There is practice, there is assistance, and there is fulfillment. It has seven branches. In the noble right concentration, practice is spoken of, assistance is spoken of, and fulfillment is also spoken of. What are the seven? Right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, and right mindfulness. If one practices, assists, and fulfills with these seven branches, and if one attains right concentration with a well-directed mind, this is called the noble right concentration, with practice, assistance, and fulfillment. Why is that? Right view gives rise to right intention, right intention gives rise to right speech, right speech gives rise to right action, right action gives rise to right livelihood, right livelihood gives rise to right effort, right effort gives rise to right mindfulness, and right mindfulness gives rise to right concentration. The noble disciples, with their minds thus rightly concentrated, completely exhaust desire, anger, and delusion. The noble disciples, with their minds thus rightly liberated, completely know that birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and there is no more of this mundane state, knowing it as it really is. Among them, right view is foremost.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有一道令众生得清净，离愁戚啼哭，灭忧苦懊恼，便得如法，谓圣正定。有习、有助，亦复有具，而有七支，于圣正定说习、说助，亦复说具。云何为七？正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念。若有以此七支习、助、具，善趣向心得一者，是谓圣正定，有习、有助，亦复有具。所以者何？正见生正志，正志生正语，正语生正业，正业生正命，正命生正方便，正方便生正念，正念生正定，贤圣弟子如是心正定，顿尽婬、怒、痴，贤圣弟子如是正心解脱，顿知生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真，彼中正见最在其前。

If one sees wrong view as wrong view, that is called right view. If one sees right view as right view, that is also called right view. What is wrong view? It means this view: there is no giving, no offering, no recitation of spells, no good or bad deeds, no retribution for good or bad deeds, no this world or the next world, no father or mother, no noble ones in the world who go to a good place, go well, go in the right direction, and in this world and the next world, know for themselves, awaken for themselves, realize for themselves, and abide in accomplishment. This is called wrong view. What is right view? It means this view: there is giving, there is offering, there are recitations of spells, there are good and bad deeds, there is retribution for good and bad deeds, there is this world and the next world, there is father and mother, there are noble ones in the world who go to a good place, go well, go in the right direction, and in this world and the next world, know for themselves, awaken for themselves, realize for themselves, and abide in accomplishment. This is called right view. This is called seeing wrong view as wrong view, which is called right view. Seeing right view as right view is also called right view. Having understood this, one then seeks and practices to abandon wrong view and accomplish right view. This is called right effort. The bhikṣu abandons wrong view with mindfulness and accomplishes right view. This is called right mindfulness. These three branches follow right view and arise from view. Therefore, right view is foremost.

「若见邪见是邪见者，是谓正见，若见正见是正见者，亦谓正见。云何邪见？谓此见无施、无斋、无有呪说，无善恶业，无善恶业报，无此世彼世，无父无母，世无真人往至善处、善去善向、此世彼世自知自觉自作证成就游，是谓邪见。云何正见？谓此见有施、有斋，亦有呪说，有善恶业，有善恶业报，有此世彼世，有父有母，世有真人往至善处、善去善向，此世彼世自知自觉自作证成就游，是谓正见。是为见邪见是邪见者，是谓正见。见正见是正见者，亦谓正见。彼如是知已，则便求学，欲断邪见成就正见，是谓正方便。比丘以念断于邪见，成就正见，是谓正念。此三支随正见从见方便，是故正见最在前也。

If one sees wrong intention as wrong intention, that is called right intention. If one sees right intention as right intention, that is also called right intention. What is wrong intention? It means the intention of desire, anger, and harm. This is called wrong intention. What is right intention? It means the intention of non-desire, non-anger, and non-harm. This is called right intention. This is called seeing wrong intention as wrong intention, which is called right intention. Seeing right intention as right intention is also called right intention. Having understood this, one then seeks and practices to abandon wrong intention and accomplish right intention. This is called right effort. The bhikṣu abandons wrong intention with mindfulness and accomplishes right intention. This is called right mindfulness. These three branches follow right intention and arise from view. Therefore, right view is foremost.

「若见邪志是邪志者，是谓正志。若见正志是正志者，亦谓正志。云何邪志？欲念、恚念、害念，是谓邪志。云何正志，无欲念、无恚念、无害念，是谓正志。是为见邪志是邪志者，是谓正志。见正志是正志者，亦谓正志。彼如是知已，则便求学，欲断邪志成就正志，是谓正方便。比丘以念断于邪志，成就正志，是谓正念。此三支随正志从见方便，是故正见最在前也。

If one sees wrong speech as wrong speech, that is called right speech. If one sees right speech as right speech, that is also called right speech. What is wrong speech? It means false speech, divisive speech, harsh speech, and frivolous speech. This is called wrong speech. What is right speech? It means to be free from false speech, divisive speech, harsh speech, and frivolous speech. This is called right speech. This is called seeing wrong speech as wrong speech, which is called right speech. Seeing right speech as right speech is also called right speech. Having understood this, one then seeks and practices to abandon wrong speech and accomplish right speech. This is called right effort. The bhikṣu abandons wrong speech with mindfulness and accomplishes right speech. This is called right mindfulness. These three branches follow right speech and arise from view. Therefore, right view is foremost.

「若见邪语是邪语者，是谓正语。若见正语是正语者，亦谓正语。云何邪语？妄言、两舌、麤言、绮语，是谓邪语。云何正语？离妄言、两舌、麤言、绮语，是谓正语。是为见邪语是邪语者，是谓正语。见正语是正语者，亦谓正语。彼如是知已，则便求学，欲断邪语成就正语，是谓正方便。比丘以念断于邪语，成就正语，是谓正念。此三支随正语从见方便，是故正见最在前也。

If one sees wrong action as wrong action, that is called right action. If one sees right action as right action, that is also called right action. What is wrong action? It means killing, taking what is not given, and sexual misconduct. This is called wrong action. What is right action? It means to be free from killing, taking what is not given, and sexual misconduct. This is called right action. This is called seeing wrong action as wrong action, which is called right action. Seeing right action as right action is also called right action. Having understood this, one then seeks and practices to abandon wrong action and accomplish right action. This is called right effort. The bhikṣu abandons wrong action with mindfulness and accomplishes right action. This is called right mindfulness. These three branches follow right action and arise from view. Therefore, right view is foremost.

「若见邪业是邪业者，是谓正业。若见正业是正业者，亦谓正业。云何邪业？杀生、不与取、邪婬，是谓邪业。云何正业？离杀、不与取、邪婬，是谓正业。是为见邪业是邪业者，是谓正业。见正业是正业者，亦谓正业。彼如是知已，则便求学，欲断邪业成就正业，是谓正方便。比丘以念断于邪业，成就正业，是谓正念。此三支随正业从见方便，是故正见最在前也。

If one sees wrong livelihood as wrong livelihood, that is called right livelihood. If one sees right livelihood as right livelihood, that is also called right livelihood. What is wrong livelihood? It means seeking without fulfillment, using various animal spells, and maintaining livelihood through wrong means. One does not seek robes in accordance with the Dharma, but by unwholesome means. One does not seek food and drink, bedding and seats, medicine and supplies for daily use in accordance with the Dharma, but by unwholesome means. This is called wrong livelihood. What is right livelihood? It means not seeking without fulfillment, not using various animal spells, and not maintaining livelihood through wrong means. One seeks robes in accordance with the Dharma, and by wholesome means. One seeks food and drink, bedding and seats, medicine and supplies for daily use in accordance with the Dharma, and by wholesome means. This is called right livelihood. This is called seeing wrong livelihood as wrong livelihood, which is called right livelihood. Seeing right livelihood as right livelihood is also called right livelihood. Having understood this, one then seeks and practices to abandon wrong livelihood and accomplish right livelihood. This is called right effort. The bhikṣu abandons wrong livelihood with mindfulness and accomplishes right livelihood. This is called right mindfulness. These three branches follow right livelihood and arise from view. Therefore, right view is foremost.

「若见邪命是邪命者，是谓正命。若见正命是正命者，亦谓正命。云何邪命？若有求无满意，以若干种畜生之呪，邪命存命，彼不如法求衣被，以非法也。不如法求饮食、床榻、汤药、诸生活具，以非法也，是谓邪命。云何正命？若不求无满意，不以若干种畜生之呪，不邪命存命，彼如法求衣被，则以法也。如法求饮食、床榻、汤药、诸生活具，则以法也，是谓正命。是为见邪命是邪命者，是谓正命。见正命是正命者，亦谓正命。彼如是知已，则便求学，欲断邪命，成就正命，是谓正方便。比丘以念断于邪命，成就正命，是谓正念。此三支随正命从见方便，是故正见最在前也。

What is right effort? The bhikṣu, in order to abandon arisen evil and unwholesome dharmas, generates desire, strives, arouses energy, applies his mind, and exerts his mind. In order to prevent the arising of unarisen evil and unwholesome dharmas, he generates desire... In order to give rise to unarisen wholesome dharmas, he generates desire... In order to maintain, not forget, not regress, increase, expand, cultivate, and perfect arisen wholesome dharmas, he generates desire... This is called right effort.

「云何正方便？比丘者，已生恶法为断故，发欲求方便，精勤举心灭。未生恶法为不生故，发欲求方便，精勤举心灭。未生善法为生故，发欲求方便，精勤举心灭。已生善法为住不忘不退，转增广布，修习满具故，发欲求方便，精勤举心灭，是谓正方便。

What is right mindfulness? The bhikṣu contemplates the internal body as body, contemplates feelings as feelings, contemplates mind as mind, contemplates dharmas as dharmas. This is called right mindfulness.

「云何正念？比丘者，观内身如身，观至觉、心、法如法，是谓正念。

What is right concentration? The bhikṣu, having abandoned desire, abandoned evil and unwholesome dharmas, attains and abides in the fourth dhyāna, which is accompanied by equanimity and pure and bright mindfulness. This is called right concentration.

「云何正定？比丘者，离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游，是谓正定。

What is right liberation? The bhikṣu is liberated from the mind of desire, hatred, and delusion. This is called right liberation.

「云何正解脱？比丘者，欲心解脱，恚、痴心解脱，是谓正解脱。

What is right knowledge? The bhikṣu knows the liberation of the mind from desire, hatred, and delusion. This is called right knowledge.

「云何正智？比丘者，知欲心解脱，知恚、痴心解脱，是谓正智也。是为学者成就八支，漏尽阿罗诃成就十支。

What are the eight factors accomplished by the learner? The learner's right view up to right concentration. These are the eight factors accomplished by the learner.

「云何学者成就八支？学正见至学正定，是为学者成就八支。云何漏尽阿罗诃成就十支？无学正见至无学正智，是谓漏尽阿罗诃成就十支。所以者何？正见者，断于邪见，若因邪见生无量恶不善法者，彼亦断之。若因正见生无量善法者，彼则修习，令满具足。至正智者断于邪智，若因邪智生无量恶不善法者，彼亦断之。若因正智生无量善法者，彼则修习，令满具足。

These are the twenty wholesome and twenty unwholesome categories. This is called turning the wheel of the Dharma in the great Dharma categories of forty, which no śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, Māra, Brahmā, or anyone else in the world can stop or dispute. If there are any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who say that I turn the wheel of the Dharma in the great Dharma categories of forty, which no śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, Māra, Brahmā, or anyone else in the world can stop or dispute, then they can be questioned on ten points. What are the ten? If they disparage right view and praise wrong view, and if they make offerings to and praise those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have wrong view, then that is the first point of questioning.

「是为二十善品、二十不善品，是为说四十大法品转于梵轮，沙门、梵志、天及魔、梵及余世间，无有能制而言非者。若有沙门、梵志者，我所说四十大法品转于梵轮，沙门、梵志、天及魔、梵及余世间，无有能制而言非者。彼于如法有十诘责。云何为十？若毁呰正见，称誉邪见。若有邪见沙门、梵志，若供养彼而称誉彼。若有沙门、梵志者，我所说四十大法品转于梵轮，沙门、梵志、天及魔、梵及余世间，无有能制而言非者，彼于如法是谓一诘责。

If they disparage right knowledge and praise wrong knowledge, and if they make offerings to and praise those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have wrong knowledge, then that is the tenth point of questioning.

「若毁呰，至正智，称誉邪智。若有邪智沙门、梵志，若供养彼而称誉彼。若有沙门、梵志，我所说四十大法品转于梵轮，沙门、梵志、天及魔，梵及余世间，无有能制而言非者，彼于如法是谓第十诘责。若有沙门、梵志，我所说四十大法品转于梵轮，沙门、梵志、天及魔、梵及余世间，无有能制而言非者，是谓于如法有十诘责。若更有余沙门、梵志，蹲踞说蹲踞，无所有说无所有，说无因、说无作、说无业，谓彼彼所作善恶施设，断绝破坏彼此，我所说四十大法品转于梵轮，沙门、梵志、天及魔、梵及余世间，无有能制而言非者，彼亦有诘责、愁忧恐怖。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
