MĀ 19

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 2 业相应品

To the Nigaṇṭhas （一九）尼干经

Parallel to: MN 101

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Śrāvastī, staying in the city of the gods.

一时，佛游释羇瘦，在天邑中。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "The Nirgranthas have such views and make such statements: 'What one experiences is all due to past actions. If one's past actions are extinguished through ascetic practices, and one does not create new ones, then all actions will be exhausted. When all actions are exhausted, one will attain the exhaustion of suffering. Having attained the exhaustion of suffering, one will attain the end of suffering.' I then went to them and, upon arrival, asked, 'Nirgranthas! Do you truly have such views and make such statements: "What one experiences is all due to past actions. If one's past actions are extinguished through ascetic practices, and one does not create new ones, then all actions will be exhausted. When all actions are exhausted, one will attain the exhaustion of suffering. Having attained the exhaustion of suffering, one will attain the end of suffering."'" They replied, "Yes, Gautama!" I further asked the Nirgranthas, 'Do you have pure knowledge yourself, that you were originally existent, originally non-existent, that you have committed evil, not committed evil, that you have exhausted so much suffering, not exhausted so much suffering, that having exhausted it, you have attained exhaustion, and that you have in this very life cut off all unwholesome states, attained wholesome states, and cultivated and realized them?' They replied, "No, Gautama!"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「诸尼干等如是见、如是说，谓人所受皆因本作，若其故业因苦行灭，不造新者，则诸业尽，诸业尽已，则得苦尽，得苦尽已，则得苦边。我便往彼，到已，即问：『尼干！汝等实如是见、如是说，谓人所受皆因本作，若其故业因苦行灭，不造新者，则诸业尽，诸业尽已，则得苦尽，得苦尽已，则得苦边耶？』彼答我言：『如是。瞿昙！』我复问彼尼干：『汝等自有净智，我为本有，我为本无，我为本作恶，为不作恶，我为尔所苦尽，为尔所苦不尽，若尽已，便得尽，即于现世断诸不善，得众善法，修习作证耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』

I further said to the Nirgranthas, 'You do not have pure knowledge yourself, that you were originally existent, originally non-existent, that you have committed evil, not committed evil, that you have exhausted so much suffering, not exhausted so much suffering, that having exhausted it, you have attained exhaustion, and that you have in this very life cut off all unwholesome states, attained wholesome states, and cultivated and realized them. Yet you make such statements: "What one experiences is all due to past actions. If one's past actions are extinguished through ascetic practices, and one does not create new ones, then all actions will be exhausted. When all actions are exhausted, one will attain the exhaustion of suffering. Having attained the exhaustion of suffering, one will attain the end of suffering." Nirgranthas! If you had pure knowledge yourself, that you were originally existent, originally non-existent, that you have committed evil, not committed evil, that you have exhausted so much suffering, not exhausted so much suffering, that having exhausted it, you have attained exhaustion, and that you have in this very life cut off all unwholesome states, attained wholesome states, and cultivated and realized them, then Nirgranthas! you could make such statements: "What one experiences is all due to past actions. If one's past actions are extinguished through ascetic practices, and one does not create new ones, then all actions will be exhausted. When all actions are exhausted, one will attain the exhaustion of suffering. Having attained the exhaustion of suffering, one will attain the end of suffering."'

「我复语彼尼干：『汝等自无净智，我为本有，我为本无，我为本作恶，为不作恶，我为尔所苦尽，为尔所苦不尽，若尽已，便得尽，即于现世断诸不善，得众善法，修习作证，而作是说，谓人所受皆因本作，若其故业因苦行灭，不造新者，则诸业尽，诸业尽已，则得苦尽，得苦尽已，则得苦边。尼干！若汝等自有净智，我为本有，我为本无，我为本作恶，为不作恶，我为尔所苦尽，为尔所苦不尽，若尽已，便得尽，即于现世断诸不善，得众善法，修习作证。尼干！汝等可得作是说，谓人所受皆因本作，若其故业因苦行灭，不造新者，则诸业尽，诸业尽已，则得苦尽，得苦尽已，则得苦边。』

"Nirgranthas! It is like a person who is struck by a poisoned arrow. Because of being struck by the poisoned arrow, extreme suffering arises. His relatives, out of compassion and pity, wishing to benefit and bring peace to him, call a skilled surgeon. When the skilled surgeon arrives, he uses a sharp knife to open the wound. When opening the wound, further extreme suffering arises. After opening the wound, he seeks the poisoned arrow. When seeking the poisoned arrow, further extreme suffering arises. After finding the poisoned arrow, he then pulls it out. When pulling it out, further extreme suffering arises. After pulling out the poisoned arrow, he wraps the wound. When wrapping the wound, further extreme suffering arises. After pulling out the poisoned arrow, he regains strength, is free from illness, and his faculties are restored to their original state. Nirgranthas! That person has pure knowledge and thinks like this: 'I was originally struck by a poisoned arrow. Because of being struck by the poisoned arrow, extreme suffering arose. My relatives, out of compassion and pity, wishing to benefit and bring peace to me, called a skilled surgeon. When the skilled surgeon arrived, he used a sharp knife to open my wound. When opening the wound, further extreme suffering arose. After opening the wound, he sought the poisoned arrow. When seeking the poisoned arrow, further extreme suffering arose. After finding the poisoned arrow, he then pulled it out. When pulling it out, further extreme suffering arose. After pulling out the poisoned arrow, he wrapped my wound. When wrapping the wound, further extreme suffering arose. After pulling out the poisoned arrow, I regained strength, am free from illness, and my faculties are restored to their original state.'"

「尼干！犹如有人身被毒箭，因被毒箭则生极苦，彼为亲属怜念愍伤，欲饶益安隐故，即呼拔箭金医。箭金医来，便以利刀而为开疮，因开疮时，复生极苦，既开疮已，而求箭金，求箭金时，复生极苦，求得金已，即便拔出，因拔出时，复生极苦，拔金出已，薄疮缠裹，因裹疮时，复生极苦，彼于拔箭金后，得力无患，不坏诸根，平复如故。尼干！彼人自有净智，便作是念：『我本被毒箭，因被毒箭，则生极苦，我诸亲属见怜念愍伤，欲饶益安隐我故，即呼拔箭金医，箭金医来，便以利刀为我开疮，因开疮时，复生极苦，既开疮已，而求箭金，求箭金时，复生极苦，求得金已，即便拔出，因拔出时，复生极苦，拔金出已，薄疮缠裹，因裹疮时，复生极苦，我于拔箭金后，得力无患，不坏诸根，平复如故。』

"In this way, Nirgranthas! If you have pure knowledge yourself, that you were originally existent, originally non-existent, that you have committed evil, not committed evil, that you have exhausted so much suffering, not exhausted so much suffering, that having exhausted it, you have attained exhaustion, and that you have in this very life cut off all unwholesome states, attained wholesome states, and cultivated and realized them, then Nirgranthas! you could make such statements: "What one experiences is all due to past actions. If one's past actions are extinguished through ascetic practices, and one does not create new ones, then all actions will be exhausted. When all actions are exhausted, one will attain the exhaustion of suffering. Having attained the exhaustion of suffering, one will attain the end of suffering." I ask this, but I do not see any Nirgranthas who can answer me, saying: 'Gautama! It is so, it is not so.'"

「如是，尼干！若汝等自有净智，我为本有，我为本无，我为本作恶，为不作恶，我为尔所苦尽，为尔所苦不尽，若尽已，便得尽，即于现世断诸不善，得众善法，修习作证。尼干！汝等可得作是说，谓人所受皆因本作，若其故业因苦行灭，不造新者，则诸业尽，诸业尽已，则得苦尽，得苦尽已，则得苦边，我问如是，不见诸尼干能答我言：『瞿昙！如是，不如是。』

"Furthermore, I asked the Nirgranthas: 'If the Nirgranthas have superior cessation and superior ascetic practices, do the Nirgranthas then experience superior suffering?' They answered me: 'So it is, Gautama!' 'If they have middling cessation and middling ascetic practices, do the Nirgranthas then experience middling suffering?' They answered me: 'So it is, Gautama!' 'If they have inferior cessation and inferior ascetic practices, do the Nirgranthas then experience inferior suffering?' They answered me: 'So it is, Gautama!' This means that when the Nirgranthas have superior cessation and superior ascetic practices, the Nirgranthas then experience superior suffering; when they have middling cessation and middling ascetic practices, the Nirgranthas then experience middling suffering; when they have inferior cessation and inferior ascetic practices, the Nirgranthas then experience inferior suffering. If the Nirgranthas have superior cessation and superior ascetic practices, the Nirgranthas then cease superior suffering; when they have middling cessation and middling ascetic practices, the Nirgranthas then cease middling suffering; when they have inferior cessation and inferior ascetic practices, the Nirgranthas then cease inferior suffering. If they act like this or not like this, to cease extreme suffering and heavy suffering, then one should know that the Nirgranthas experience suffering in this very life. But the Nirgranthas, being covered and bound by delusion, make such statements: "What one experiences is all due to past actions. If one's past actions are extinguished through ascetic practices, and one does not create new ones, then all actions will be exhausted. When all actions are exhausted, one will attain the exhaustion of suffering. Having attained the exhaustion of suffering, one will attain the end of suffering." I ask this, but I do not see any Nirgranthas who can answer me, saying: 'Gautama! It is so, it is not so.'"

「复次，我问诸尼干曰：『若诸尼干有上断、上苦行，尔时诸尼干生上苦耶？』彼答我言：『如是。瞿昙！』『若有中断、中苦行，尔时诸尼干生中苦耶？』彼答我言：『如是，瞿昙！』『若有下断、下苦行，尔时诸尼干生下苦耶？』彼答我言：『如是，瞿昙！』是为诸尼干有上断、上苦行，尔时诸尼干则生上苦；有中断、中苦行，尔时诸尼干则生中苦；有下断、下苦行，尔时诸尼干则生下苦。若使诸尼干有上断、上苦行，尔时诸尼干止息上苦；有中断、中苦行，尔时诸尼干止息中苦；有下断、下苦行，尔时诸尼干止息下苦。若如是作、不如是作，止息极苦甚重苦者，当知诸尼干即于现世作苦。但诸尼干为痴所覆，为痴所缠，而作是说：『谓人所受皆因本作，若其故业因苦行灭，不造新者，则诸业尽，诸业尽已，则得苦尽，得苦尽已，则得苦边。』我问如是，不见诸尼干能答我言：『瞿昙！如是，不如是。』

Furthermore, I asked the Nirgranthas: 'Nirgranthas! If there is an action with a pleasant result, can that action be transformed into an action with a painful result through cessation and ascetic practices?' They answered me: 'No, Gautama!' 'Nirgranthas! If there is an action with a painful result, can that action be transformed into an action with a pleasant result through cessation and ascetic practices?' They answered me: 'No, Gautama!' 'Nirgranthas! If there is an action with a result in this life, can that action be transformed into an action with a result in the next life through cessation and ascetic practices?' They answered me: 'No, Gautama!' 'Nirgranthas! If there is an action with a result in the next life, can that action be transformed into an action with a result in this life through cessation and ascetic practices?' They answered me: 'No, Gautama!' 'Nirgranthas! If there is an action with an unripe result, can that action be transformed into an action with a ripe result through cessation and ascetic practices?' They answered me: 'No, Gautama!' 'Nirgranthas! If there is an action with a ripe result, can that action be transformed into a different action through cessation and ascetic practices?' They answered me: 'No, Gautama!' 'Nirgranthas! This means that an action with a pleasant result cannot be transformed into an action with a painful result through cessation and ascetic practices. Nirgranthas! An action with a painful result cannot be transformed into an action with a pleasant result through cessation and ascetic practices. Nirgranthas! An action with a result in this life cannot be transformed into an action with a result in the next life through cessation and ascetic practices. Nirgranthas! An action with a result in the next life cannot be transformed into an action with a result in this life through cessation and ascetic practices. Nirgranthas! An action with an unripe result cannot be transformed into an action with a ripe result through cessation and ascetic practices. Nirgranthas! An action with a ripe result cannot be transformed into a different action through cessation and ascetic practices. Therefore, Nirgranthas! it is a false expedient, empty cessation without attainment.

「复次，我问诸尼干曰：『诸尼干！若有乐报业，彼业宁可因断、因苦行，转作苦报耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』『诸尼干！若有苦报业，彼业宁可因断、因苦行，转作乐报耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』『诸尼干！若有现法报业，彼业宁可因断、因苦行，转作后生报耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』『诸尼干！若有后生报业，彼业宁可因断、因苦行，转作现法报耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』『诸尼干！若有不熟报业，彼业宁可因断、因苦行，转作熟报耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』『诸尼干！若有熟报业，彼业宁可因断、因苦行，转作异耶？』彼答我言：『不也。瞿昙！』『诸尼干！是为乐报业，彼业不可因断、因苦行，转作苦报。诸尼干！苦报业，彼业不可因断、因苦行，转作乐报。诸尼干！现法报业，彼业不可因断、因苦行，转作后生报。诸尼干！后生报业，彼业不可因断、因苦行，转作现法报。诸尼干！不熟业，彼业不可因断、因苦行，转作熟报。诸尼干！熟报业，彼业不可因断、因苦行，转作异者。以是故，诸尼干！虚妄方便，空断无获。』

"Those Nirgranthas then replied to me: 'Gautama! We have a revered teacher named Pārśva Nirgrantha, who says: "Nirgranthas! If you have previously committed evil actions, those actions can all be extinguished through these ascetic practices. If you now protect your body, speech, and mind, you will not commit any more evil actions."'" I further asked those Nirgranthas: 'Do you believe in your revered teacher Pārśva Nirgrantha, without any doubt?' They replied to me: 'Gautama! We believe in our revered teacher Pārśva Nirgrantha, without any doubt.' I further said to those Nirgranthas: 'There are five dharmas that have two results in this life: faith, joy, hearing, mindfulness, and seeing the good. Nirgranthas! Can a person's own false speech be believed, enjoyed, heard, remembered, and seen as good?' They replied to me: 'So it is, Gautama!' I further said to those Nirgranthas: 'How can false speech be believed, enjoyed, heard, remembered, and seen as good? It means that a person's own false speech can be believed, enjoyed, heard, remembered, and seen as good.

「彼诸尼干便报我言：『瞿昙！我有尊师，名亲子尼干，作如是说：「诸尼干！汝等若本作恶业，彼业皆可因此苦行而得灭尽，若今护身、口、意，因此不复更作恶业也。」』我复问彼诸尼干曰：『汝等信尊师亲子尼干，不疑惑耶？』彼答我言：『瞿昙！我信尊师亲子尼干，无有疑惑。』我复语彼诸尼干曰：『有五种法现世二报，信、乐、闻、念、见善观，诸尼干！人自有虚妄言，是可信、可乐、可闻、可念、可见善观耶？』彼答我言：『如是。瞿昙！』我复语彼诸尼干曰：『是虚妄言，何可信，何可乐，何可闻，何可念，何可善观？谓人自有虚妄言，有信、有乐、有闻、有念、有善观。』

If the Nirgranthas say this, they can be refuted in five ways according to the Dharma, and it is detestable. What are the five? Now, the suffering and happiness experienced by sentient beings are all caused by past actions. In that case, the Nirgranthas and others committed evil actions in the past. Why is that? Because of that, the Nirgranthas now experience extremely heavy suffering. This is the first thing that is detestable about the Nirgranthas. Furthermore, the suffering and happiness experienced by sentient beings are all caused by coming together. In that case, the Nirgranthas and others came together with evil in the past. Why is that? Because of that, the Nirgranthas now experience extremely heavy suffering. This is the second thing that is detestable about the Nirgranthas. Furthermore, the suffering and happiness experienced by sentient beings are all caused by life. In that case, the Nirgranthas and others had evil life in the past. Why is that? Because of that, the Nirgranthas now experience extremely heavy suffering. This is the third thing that is detestable about the Nirgranthas. Furthermore, the suffering and happiness experienced by sentient beings are all caused by views. In that case, the Nirgranthas and others had evil views in the past. Why is that? Because of that, the Nirgranthas now experience extremely heavy suffering. This is the fourth thing that is detestable about the Nirgranthas. Furthermore, the suffering and happiness experienced by sentient beings are all caused by the creation of the honored ones. In that case, the Nirgranthas and others had evil creation of the honored ones in the past. Why is that? Because of that, the Nirgranthas now experience extremely heavy suffering. This is the fifth thing that is detestable about the Nirgranthas. If the Nirgranthas are caused by past evil actions, evil coming together, evil life, evil views, and evil creation of the honored ones, and are created by evil creation of the honored ones, because of that, the Nirgranthas now experience extremely heavy suffering. This is called the cause of that event, for which the Nirgranthas and others are detestable.

「若诸尼干作是说者，于如法中得五诘责，为可憎恶。云何为五？今此众生所受苦乐皆因本作，若尔者，诸尼干等本作恶业。所以者何？因彼故，诸尼干于今受极重苦，是谓尼干第一可憎恶。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因合会，若尔者，诸尼干等本恶合会。所以者何？因彼故，诸尼干于今受极重苦，是谓尼干第二可憎恶。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因为命，若尔者，诸尼揵等本恶为命。所以者何？因彼故，诸尼干于今受极重苦，是谓尼干第三可憎恶。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因见也。若尔者，诸尼干等本有恶见。所以者何？因彼故，诸尼干于今受极重苦，是谓尼干第四可憎恶。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因尊祐造，若尔者，诸尼干等本恶尊祐。所以者何？因彼故，诸尼干于今受极重苦，是谓尼干第五可憎恶。若诸尼干因本所作恶业、恶合会、恶为命、恶见、恶尊祐，为恶尊祐所造，因彼故，诸尼干于今受极重苦，是谓因彼事故，诸尼干等为可憎恶。

The Dharma that I know and realize myself and teach to you cannot be subdued, defiled, or controlled by any śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, demon, Brahmā, or any other being in the world. How is it that the Dharma that I know and realize myself and teach to you cannot be subdued, defiled, or controlled by any śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, god, demon, Brahmā, or any other being in the world? If a bhikṣu abandons unwholesome bodily actions and cultivates wholesome bodily actions, abandons unwholesome verbal and mental actions and cultivates wholesome verbal and mental actions, he will know for himself in the future that he will have no suffering. He will attain happiness according to the Dharma and not abandon it. He may wish to eliminate the cause of suffering by practicing desire, or he may wish to eliminate the cause of suffering by practicing equanimity. If he wishes to eliminate the cause of suffering by practicing desire, he will cultivate that practice of desire, and once it is eliminated, suffering will be extinguished. If he wishes to eliminate the cause of suffering by practicing equanimity, he will cultivate that practice of equanimity, and once it is eliminated, suffering will be extinguished. If that bhikṣu then thinks: 'Whatever I do and whatever I practice, unwholesome dharmas arise and wholesome dharmas cease. If I eliminate suffering myself, unwholesome dharmas cease and wholesome dharmas arise. I should now eliminate my suffering myself.' He then eliminates his suffering. Once he has eliminated his suffering, unwholesome dharmas cease and wholesome dharmas arise. He no longer eliminates suffering. Why is that? Bhikṣu! The meaning of what he has done is already accomplished. If he eliminates suffering again, that is not the case.

「我所自知、自觉法为汝说者，若沙门、梵志，若天、魔、梵及余世间皆无能伏，皆无能秽，皆无能制。云何我所自知、自觉法为汝说者，非为沙门、梵志，若天、魔、梵及余世间所能伏、所能秽、所能制？若有比丘舍身不善业，修身善业，舍口、意不善业，修口、意善业，彼于未来苦，便自知我无未来苦，如法得乐而不弃舍。彼或欲断苦因，行欲，或欲断苦因，行舍欲，彼若欲断苦因，行欲者，即修其行欲，已断者，苦便得尽。彼若欲断苦因，行舍欲者，即修其行舍欲，已断者，苦便得尽。若彼比丘便作是念：『随所为、随所行，不善法生而善法灭，若自断苦，不善法灭而善法生，我今宁可自断其苦。』便自断苦。自断苦已，不善法灭而善法生，不复断苦。所以者何？比丘！本所为者，其义已成，若复断苦，是处不然。

Bhikṣu! It is like a fletcher straightening an arrow. Once the arrow is straight, it is no longer straightened. Why is that? The person's original purpose is already accomplished. If it is straightened again, that is not the case. In the same way, the bhikṣu thinks: 'Whatever I do and whatever I practice, unwholesome dharmas arise and wholesome dharmas cease. If I eliminate suffering myself, unwholesome dharmas cease and wholesome dharmas arise. I should now eliminate my suffering myself.' He then eliminates his suffering. Once he has eliminated his suffering, unwholesome dharmas cease and wholesome dharmas arise. He no longer eliminates suffering. Why is that? The original purpose is already accomplished. If he eliminates suffering again, that is not the case.

「比丘！犹如箭工用检挠箭，其箭已直，不复用检。所以者何？彼人本所为者，其事已成，若复用检，是处不然。如是，比丘便作是念：『随所为、随所行，不善法生而善法灭，若自断苦，不善法灭而善法生，我今宁可自断其苦。』便自断苦。自断苦已，不善法灭而善法生，不复断苦。所以者何？本所为者，其义已成，若复断苦，是处不然。比丘！犹如有人爱念、染著、敬待彼女，然彼女人更与他语，共相问讯，往来止宿，其人因是身心生苦恼，极忧戚耶？」

"The bhikṣu replied: "Yes, World-Honored One! Why is that? The person loves, is attached to, and respects the woman. If that woman speaks with another man, greets him, comes and goes, and stays with him, how could the person not experience mental and physical suffering and distress?

比丘答曰：「如是。世尊！所以者何？其人于女爱念、染著，极相敬待，而彼女人更与他语，共相问讯，往来止宿，其人身心何得不生苦恼忧戚？」

"Bhikṣu! If that person thinks: 'I have loved and respected that woman in vain. If that woman speaks with another man, greets him, comes and goes, and stays with him, I should now eliminate my love and attachment to her because of my own suffering and distress.' If that person later eliminates his love and attachment to her because of his own suffering and distress. If that woman still speaks with another man, greets him, comes and goes, and stays with him, will that person later experience mental and physical suffering and distress?"

「比丘！若使其人而作是念：『我唐爱念、敬待彼女，然彼女人更与他语，共相问讯，往来止宿，我今宁可因自苦自忧故，断为彼女爱念、染著耶？』其人于后因自苦自忧故，便断为彼女爱念、染著。若彼女人故与他语，共相问讯，往来止宿，其人于后，身心宁当复生苦恼，极忧戚耶？」

"The bhikṣu replied: "No, World-Honored One! Why is that? The person no longer has any feelings of love and attachment to the woman. If that woman still speaks with another man, greets him, comes and goes, and stays with him, if that person were to experience mental and physical suffering and distress because of that, it would not be so.

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！所以者何？其人于女无复爱念、染著之情，若彼女人故与他语，共相问讯，往来止宿，若使其人因此身心复生苦恼极忧戚者，是处不然。」

In the same way, the bhikṣu thinks: 'Whatever I do and whatever I practice, unwholesome dharmas arise and wholesome dharmas cease. If I eliminate my suffering, unwholesome dharmas cease and wholesome dharmas arise. I should now eliminate my suffering.' He then eliminates his suffering. After eliminating his suffering, unwholesome dharmas cease and wholesome dharmas arise. He no longer eliminates his suffering. Why is that? The meaning of what he did before has already been accomplished. If he were to eliminate his suffering again, it would not be so.

「如是，比丘便作是念：『随所为、随所行，不善法生而善法灭，若自断其苦，不善法灭而善法生，我今宁可自断其苦。』便自断苦。自断苦已，不善法灭而善法生，不复断苦。所以者何？本所为者，其义已成，若复断苦，是处不然。

He further thinks: 'If there is a cause for eliminating suffering, I have already eliminated it. However, I have not yet eliminated desire. I should now seek to eliminate desire.' He then seeks to eliminate desire. In order to eliminate desire, he dwells alone in seclusion, in a place without affairs, either under a tree or in an empty, quiet place, a mountain cave, a stone chamber, in the open, or in a straw pile. He goes to a forest or to a charnel ground. Having already gone to a place without affairs, or under a tree in an empty, quiet place, he spreads out his sitting mat, sits cross-legged, straightens his body and mind, and reflects inwardly, eliminating greed and desire, with no disputes in his mind. Seeing the wealth and livelihood of others, he does not give rise to greed and desire, wishing to obtain it for himself. He purifies his mind of greed and desire. In the same way, he eliminates anger, sloth and torpor, restlessness and remorse, eliminates doubt, and transcends confusion. Regarding wholesome dharmas, he has no hesitation. He purifies his mind of doubt.

「彼复作是念：『若有所因，断其苦者，我便已断，然我于欲犹故未断，我今宁可求断于欲。』便求断欲。彼为断欲故，独住远离，在无事处，或至树下空安静处，山岩石室、露地穰积，或至林中，或在冢间，彼已在无事处，或至树下空安静处，敷尼师檀，结加趺坐，正身正愿，反念不向，断除贪伺，心无有诤，见他财物诸生活具不起贪伺，欲令我得，彼于贪伺净除其心。如是，瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔，断疑、度惑，于诸善法无有犹豫，彼于疑惑净除其心。

Having already eliminated these five hindrances, defilements of the mind, and weakness of wisdom, he is detached from desire, detached from evil and unwholesome dharmas, and attains the fourth dhyāna, accomplishing and dwelling in it. Having attained such concentration, his mind is pure, undefiled, free from afflictions, pliant, well-established, and immovable. He directs his mind towards the knowledge and vision of the exhaustion of the taints, and realizes it. He then truly knows this suffering, truly knows the origin of this suffering, truly knows the cessation of this suffering, and truly knows the path to the cessation of this suffering. He also truly knows these taints, truly knows the origin of these taints, truly knows the cessation of these taints, and truly knows the path to the cessation of these taints. Having thus known and seen, his mind is liberated from the taints of desire, from the taints of existence, and from the taints of ignorance. Having been liberated, he knows he is liberated. He knows that birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and there is no more of this mundane state.

「彼已断此五盖、心秽、慧羸，离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游。彼得如是定，心清净，无秽无烦，柔软善住，得不动心，趣向漏尽智通作证，彼便知此苦如真，知此苦习、知此苦灭、知此苦灭道如真，亦知此漏如真，知此漏习、知此漏灭、知此漏灭道如真，彼如是知、如是见已，则欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱，解脱已，便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。

The Tathāgata is thus liberated in mind, and attains five praises in accordance with the Dharma, being free from disputes and lovable and respectable. What are the five? Those sentient beings experience suffering and happiness due to their past actions. In that case, the Tathāgata had wonderful actions in the past. Because of that, the Tathāgata now attains the holy, undefiled bliss, peaceful and tranquil, and experiences the bliss of cessation. This is called the Tathāgata's attainment of the first praise. Furthermore, sentient beings experience suffering and happiness due to their past associations. In that case, the Tathāgata had wonderful associations in the past. Because of that, the Tathāgata now attains the holy, undefiled bliss, peaceful and tranquil, and experiences the bliss of cessation. This is called the Tathāgata's attainment of the second praise. Furthermore, sentient beings experience suffering and happiness due to their past lives. In that case, the Tathāgata had wonderful lives in the past. Because of that, the Tathāgata now attains the holy, undefiled bliss, peaceful and tranquil, and experiences the bliss of cessation. This is called the Tathāgata's attainment of the third praise. Furthermore, sentient beings experience suffering and happiness due to their past views. In that case, the Tathāgata had wonderful views in the past. Because of that, the Tathāgata now attains the holy, undefiled bliss, peaceful and tranquil, and experiences the bliss of cessation. This is called the Tathāgata's attainment of the fourth praise. Furthermore, sentient beings experience suffering and happiness due to their past noble blessings. In that case, the Tathāgata had wonderful noble blessings in the past. Because of that, the Tathāgata now attains the holy, undefiled bliss, peaceful and tranquil, and experiences the bliss of cessation. This is called the Tathāgata's attainment of the fifth praise. These are the Tathāgata's wonderful actions, wonderful associations, wonderful lives, wonderful views, and wonderful noble blessings, which are created by wonderful noble blessings. Because of that, the Tathāgata now attains the holy, undefiled bliss, peaceful and tranquil, and experiences the bliss of cessation. For this reason, the Tathāgata now attains the five praises.

「如来如是正心解脱，得五称誉，如法无诤，可爱可敬。云何为五？彼众生者，所受苦乐皆因本作，若尔者，如来本有妙业，因彼故，如来于今圣无漏乐，寂静止息而得乐觉，是谓如来得第一称誉。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因合会，若尔者，如来本妙合会，因彼故，如来于今圣无漏乐，寂静止息而得乐觉，是谓如来得第二称誉。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因为命，若尔者，如来本妙为命，因彼故，如来于今圣无漏乐，寂静止息而得乐觉，是谓如来得第三称誉。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因见也，若尔者，如来本妙见，因彼故，如来于今圣无漏乐，寂静止息而得乐觉，是谓如来得第四称誉。复次，众生所受苦乐皆因尊祐造，若尔者，如来本妙尊祐，因彼故，如来于今圣无漏乐，寂静止息而得乐觉，是谓如来得第五称誉。是为如来本妙业、妙合会、妙为命、妙见、妙尊祐，为妙尊祐所造，因彼故，如来于今圣无漏乐，寂静止息而得乐觉，以此事故，如来于今得五称誉。

There are five causes and conditions that give rise to mental sorrow and suffering. What are the five? Those who are bound by lust give rise to mental sorrow and suffering due to being bound by lust. Similarly, those who are bound by anger, sloth, restlessness, and doubt give rise to mental sorrow and suffering due to being bound by doubt. These are the five causes and conditions that give rise to mental sorrow and suffering. There are five causes and conditions that eliminate mental sorrow and suffering. What are the five? For those who are bound by lust, the mental sorrow and suffering that arises due to being bound by lust ceases when the bonds of lust are removed. The mental sorrow and suffering that arises due to being bound by lust is completely eliminated in this very life, free from afflictions and heat, eternally abiding and unchanging. This is known and seen by the noble ones. Similarly, for those who are bound by anger, sloth, restlessness, and doubt, the mental sorrow and suffering that arises due to being bound by doubt ceases when the bonds of doubt are removed. The mental sorrow and suffering that arises due to being bound by doubt is completely eliminated in this very life, free from afflictions and heat, eternally abiding and unchanging. This is known and seen by the noble ones. These are the five causes and conditions that eliminate mental sorrow and suffering.

「有五因缘，心生忧苦。云何为五？婬欲缠者，因婬欲缠故，心生忧苦。如是，瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑惑缠者，因疑惑缠故，心生忧苦。是谓五因缘，心生忧苦。有五因缘，心灭忧苦。云何为五？若婬欲缠者，因婬欲缠故，心生忧苦，除婬欲缠已，忧苦便灭，因婬欲缠，心生忧苦，于现法中而得究竟，无烦无热，常住不变，是圣所知、圣所见。如是，瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔，若疑惑缠者，因疑惑缠故，心生忧苦，除疑惑缠已，忧苦便灭，因疑惑缠，心生忧苦，于现法中而得究竟，无烦无热，常住不变，是圣所知、圣所见。是谓五因缘，心灭忧苦。

Furthermore, there is another way to completely eliminate in this very life, free from afflictions and heat, eternally abiding and unchanging, which is known and seen by the noble ones. What is that way? It is the noble eightfold path, namely right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called another way to completely eliminate in this very life, free from afflictions and heat, eternally abiding and unchanging, which is known and seen by the noble ones.

「复次，更有现法而得究竟，无烦无热，常住不变，是圣所知、圣所见。云何更有现法而得究竟，无烦无热，常住不变，是圣所知、圣所见？谓八支圣道，正见乃至正定，是为八。是谓更有现法而得究竟，无烦无热，常住不变，是圣所知、圣所见。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
