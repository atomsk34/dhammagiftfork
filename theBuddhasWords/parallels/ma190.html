MĀ 190

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 15 双品

190. （一九〇）小空经

Parallel to: MN 121

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Shravasti, staying at the Deer Mother's Hall in the Eastern Garden.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在于东园鹿子母堂。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda rose from his afternoon meditation, went to where the Buddha was, bowed at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said, "World-Honored One, once when you were traveling among the Shakyas, in the city called Shakyapura, I heard you say, 'Ānanda, I often practice emptiness.' Did I understand and accept well what the World-Honored One said, and did I uphold it well? "

尔时，尊者阿难则于晡时从燕坐起，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却住一面，白曰：「世尊一时游行释中，城名释都邑，我于尔时从世尊闻说如是义。『阿难！我多行空。』彼世尊所说，我善知、善受，为善持耶？」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda, what I said, you indeed understood well, accepted well, and upheld well. Why is that? Since then until now, I have often practiced emptiness. Ānanda, this Deer Mother's Hall is empty of elephants, horses, cattle, sheep, wealth, grain, and slaves, but it is not empty of the assembly of monks. This, Ānanda, is what I see as empty. If there is anything else here, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted. Ānanda, if a monk wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that monk should not think of villages, should not think of people, but should often think of the thought of one thing being without affairs. If he knows emptiness in the thought of villages, emptiness in the thought of people, but there is not emptiness in the thought of one thing being without affairs, if there is weariness due to the thought of villages, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the thought of people, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to the thought of one thing being without affairs. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

尔时，世尊答曰：「阿难！彼我所说，汝实善知、善受、善持。所以者何？我从尔时及至于今，多行空也。阿难！如此鹿子母堂，空无象、马、牛、羊、财物、谷米、奴婢，然有不空，唯比丘众。是为，阿难！若此中无者，以此故我见是空，若此有余者，我见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。阿难！比丘若欲多行空者，彼比丘莫念村想，莫念人想，当数念一无事想。彼如是知空于村想，空于人想，然有不空，唯一无事想。若有疲劳，因村想故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因人想故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因一无事想故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空，若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。

Furthermore, Ānanda, if a monk wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that monk should not think of people, should not think of the thought of one thing being without affairs, but should often think of the thought of one thing being the earth. If that monk sees this earth as having highs and lows, as having snake mounds, as having thorn bushes, as having sand and stones, as having steep mountains and deep rivers, he should not think of them. If he sees this earth as flat like the palm of a hand, as a good place to look out over, he should often think of it. Ānanda, it is like a cowhide stretched with a hundred nails, stretched tightly, without wrinkles or shrinking. If he sees this earth as having highs and lows, as having snake mounds, as having thorn bushes, as having sand and stones, as having steep mountains and deep rivers, he should not think of them. If he sees this earth as flat like the palm of a hand, as a good place to look out over, he should often think of it. If he knows emptiness in the thought of people, emptiness in the thought of one thing being without affairs, but there is not emptiness in the thought of one thing being the earth, if there is weariness due to the thought of people, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being without affairs, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to the thought of one thing being the earth. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

「复次，阿难！比丘若欲多行空者，彼比丘莫念人想，莫念无事想，当数念一地想。彼比丘若见此地有高下，有蛇聚，有棘刺丛，有沙有石，山崄深河，莫念彼也。若见此地平正如掌，观望处好，当数念彼。阿难！犹如牛皮，以百钉张，极张挓] cf.［丽】已，无皱无缩。若见此地有高下，有蛇聚，有棘刺丛，有沙有石，山崄深河，莫念彼也。若见此地平正如掌，观望处好，当数念彼。彼如是知，空于人想，空无事想，然有不空，唯一地想。若有疲劳，因人想故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因无事想故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因一地想故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空，若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。

Furthermore, Ānanda, if a monk wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that monk should not think of the thought of one thing being without affairs, should not think of the thought of one thing being the earth, but should often think of the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space. If he knows emptiness in the thought of one thing being without affairs, emptiness in the thought of one thing being the earth, but there is not emptiness in the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, if there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being without affairs, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the earth, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

「复次，阿难！比丘若欲多行空者，彼比丘莫念无事想，莫念地想，当数念一无量空处想。彼如是知，空无事想，空于地想，然有不空，唯一无量空处想。若有疲劳，因无事想故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因地想故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因一无量空处想故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空，若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。

Furthermore, Ānanda, if a monk wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that monk should not think of the thought of one thing being the earth, should not think of the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, but should often think of the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness. If he knows emptiness in the thought of one thing being the earth, emptiness in the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, but there is not emptiness in the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, if there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the earth, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

「复次，阿难！比丘若欲多行空者，彼比丘莫念地想，莫念无量空处想，当数念一无量识处想。彼如是知，空于地想，空无量空处想，然有不空，唯一无量识处想。若有疲劳，因地想故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因无量空处想故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因一无量识处想故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空，若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。

Furthermore, Ānanda, if a monk wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that monk should not think of the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, should not think of the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, but should often think of the thought of one thing being the sphere of nothingness. If he knows emptiness in the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, emptiness in the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, but there is not emptiness in the thought of one thing being the sphere of nothingness, if there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the immeasurable space, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to the thought of one thing being the sphere of nothingness. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

「复次，阿难！比丘若欲多行空者，彼比丘莫念无量空处想，莫念无量识处想，当数念一无所有处想。彼如是知，空无量空处想，空无量识处想，然有不空，唯一无所有处想。若有疲劳，因无量空处想故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因无量识处想故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因一无所有处想故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空，若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。

Furthermore, Ānanda, if a monk wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that monk should not think of the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, should not think of the thought of one thing being the sphere of nothingness, but should often think of the thought of one thing being the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. If he knows emptiness in the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, emptiness in the thought of one thing being the sphere of nothingness, but there is not emptiness in the thought of one thing being the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, if there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the immeasurable consciousness, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the thought of one thing being the sphere of nothingness, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to the thought of one thing being the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

「复次，阿难！比丘若欲多行空者，彼比丘莫念无量识处想，莫念无所有处想，当数念一无想心定。彼如是知，空无量识处想，空无所有处想，然有不空，唯一无想心定。若有疲劳，因无量识处想故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因无所有处想故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因一无想心定故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空，若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。

He thinks: 'I had the thought of one thing being the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception before, what I practiced and thought about before, if what I practiced and thought about before, I do not enjoy it, do not seek it, do not abide in it. Knowing and seeing like this, the mind is liberated from the leakage of desire, the leakage of existence, and the leakage of ignorance. Having been liberated, one knows liberation. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there will be no more continuation of existence in the future, knowing it as it really is.' He knows emptiness in the leakage of desire, emptiness in the leakage of existence, emptiness in the leakage of ignorance, but there is not emptiness in this body of mine, the six sense bases, and life. If there is weariness due to the leakage of desire, I do not have that. If there is weariness due to the leakage of existence and ignorance, I also do not have that. There is only weariness due to this body of mine, the six sense bases, and life. If there is nothing in that, then I see it as empty. If there is anything else in that, I see it as truly existent. Ānanda, this is called practicing the true, the empty, and the non-inverted.

「彼作是念：『我本无想心定，本所行、本所思，若本所行、本所思者，我不乐彼，不求彼，不应住彼，如是知、如是见，欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱，解脱已，便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。』彼如是知，空欲漏、空有漏、空无明漏，然有不空，唯此我身六处、命存。若有疲劳，因欲漏故，我无是也。若有疲劳，因有漏、无明漏故，我亦无是。唯有疲劳，因此我身六处、命存故。若彼中无者，以此故，彼见是空；若彼有余者，彼见真实有。阿难！是谓行真实、空、不颠倒也。谓漏尽、无漏、无为、心解脱。

"Ānanda, if the past Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Perfectly Enlightened Ones, they all practiced this truth, emptiness, and non-inverted, namely the exhaustion of the taints, the uncontaminated, the unconditioned, and the mind liberation. Ānanda, if the future Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Perfectly Enlightened Ones, they all will practice this truth, emptiness, and non-inverted, namely the exhaustion of the taints, the uncontaminated, the unconditioned, and the mind liberation. Ānanda, if the present Tathāgata, Arhat, and Perfectly Enlightened One, I also practice this truth, emptiness, and non-inverted, namely the exhaustion of the taints, the uncontaminated, the unconditioned, and the mind liberation. Ānanda, you should study like this, I also practice this truth, emptiness, and non-inverted, namely the exhaustion of the taints, the uncontaminated, the unconditioned, and the mind liberation. Therefore, Ānanda, you should study like this."

「阿难！若过去诸如来、无所著、等正觉，彼一切行此真实、空、不颠倒，谓漏尽、无漏、无为、心解脱。阿难！若当来诸如来、无所著、等正觉，彼一切行此真实、空、不颠倒，谓漏尽、无漏、无为、心解脱。阿难！若今现在我如来、无所著、等正觉，我亦行此真实、空、不颠倒，谓漏尽、无漏、无为、心解脱。阿难！汝当如是学，我亦行此真实、空、不颠倒，谓漏尽、无漏、无为、心解脱。是故，阿难！当学如是。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and they joyfully practiced it.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
