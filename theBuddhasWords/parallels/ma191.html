MĀ 191

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 15 双品

191. （一九一）大空经

Parallel to: MN 122

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the Śākya country of Kapilavastu, staying in the Nyagrodha Park.

一时，佛游释中迦维罗卫，在尼拘类园。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having passed the night, put on his robe, took his bowl, entered Kapilavastu to beg for alms, finished eating in the middle of the day, and went to the Śākya monastery of Karacara. At that time, the Śākya monastery of Karacara had many beds and seats prepared, and many bhikṣus were staying there. Then the World-Honored One left the Śākya monastery of Karacara and went to the Śākya monastery of Karala. At that time, Venerable Ānanda was with many bhikṣus in the Śākya monastery of Karala, engaged in the work of making robes. Venerable Ānanda saw the Buddha coming from afar, went out to greet him, took the Buddha's robe and bowl, prepared a seat, and drew water to wash the Buddha's feet. After the Buddha washed his feet, he sat on the seat prepared by Venerable Ānanda in the Śākya monastery of Karala and said, "Ānanda! The Śākya monastery of Karacara has many beds and seats prepared, and many bhikṣus are staying there."

尔时，世尊过夜平旦，著衣持钵，入迦维罗卫而行乞食，食讫中后，往诣加罗差摩释精舍。尔时，加罗差摩释精舍敷众多床座，众多比丘于中住止。彼时，世尊从加罗差摩释精舍出，往诣加罗释精舍。尔时，尊者阿难与众多比丘在加罗释精舍中集作衣业，尊者阿难遥见佛来，见已出迎，取佛衣钵，还敷床座，汲水洗足。佛洗足已，于加罗释精舍坐尊者阿难所敷之座，告曰：「阿难！加罗差摩释精舍敷众多床座，众多比丘于中住止。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "Yes, World-Honored One! The Śākya monastery of Karacara has many beds and seats prepared, and many bhikṣus are staying there. Why is that? I am now engaged in the work of making robes."

尊者阿难白曰：「唯然。世尊！加罗差摩释精舍敷众多床座，众多比丘于中住止。所以者何？我今作衣业。」

At that time, the World-Honored One again said to Ānanda, "A bhikṣu should not desire frivolous talk, enjoy frivolous talk, or gather for frivolous talk. He should not desire the assembly, enjoy the assembly, or gather with the assembly. He should not wish to leave the assembly, nor enjoy dwelling alone in a secluded place. If a bhikṣu desires frivolous talk, enjoys frivolous talk, gathers for frivolous talk, desires the assembly, enjoys the assembly, gathers with the assembly, does not wish to leave the assembly, and does not enjoy dwelling alone in a secluded place, then he will not attain the joy, the noble joy, the joy of being without desire, the joy of being free, the joy of cessation, the joy of right awakening, the joy of being without food, and the joy that is not of birth and death. If it were easy and not difficult to attain such joy, it would never happen. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu does not desire frivolous talk, does not enjoy frivolous talk, does not gather for frivolous talk, does not desire the assembly, does not enjoy the assembly, does not gather with the assembly, wishes to leave the assembly, and always enjoys dwelling alone in a secluded place, then he will attain the joy, the noble joy, the joy of being without desire, the joy of being free, the joy of cessation, the joy of right awakening, the joy of being without food, and the joy that is not of birth and death. If it were easy and not difficult to attain such joy, it would certainly happen.

时，世尊复告阿难曰：「比丘不可欲哗说、乐于哗说、合会哗说、欲众、乐众、合会于众、不欲离众、不乐独住远离之处。若有比丘欲哗说、乐于哗说、合会哗说、欲众、乐众、合会于众、不欲离众、不乐独住远离处者，谓有乐、圣乐、无欲之乐、离乐、息乐、正觉之乐、无食之乐、非生死乐。若得如是乐，易不难得者，终无是处。阿难！若有比丘不欲哗说，不乐哗说，不合会哗说，不欲于众，不乐于众，不合会众，欲离于众，常乐独住远离处者，谓有乐、圣乐、无欲之乐、离乐、息乐、正觉之乐、无食之乐、非生死乐。若得如是乐，易不难得者，必有是处。

Ānanda! A bhikṣu should not desire frivolous talk, enjoy frivolous talk, or gather for frivolous talk. He should not desire the assembly, enjoy the assembly, or gather with the assembly. He should not wish to leave the assembly, nor enjoy dwelling alone in a secluded place. If a bhikṣu desires frivolous talk, enjoys frivolous talk, gathers for frivolous talk, desires the assembly, enjoys the assembly, gathers with the assembly, does not wish to leave the assembly, and does not enjoy dwelling alone in a secluded place, then he will not attain the joy of liberation of the mind through loving-kindness at the right time, nor the liberation of the mind that is not moved by the wrong time. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu does not desire frivolous talk, does not enjoy frivolous talk, does not gather for frivolous talk, does not desire the assembly, does not enjoy the assembly, does not gather with the assembly, wishes to leave the assembly, and always enjoys dwelling alone in a secluded place, then he will attain the joy of liberation of the mind through loving-kindness at the right time, and the liberation of the mind that is not moved by the wrong time. Why is that? I do not see any form that I desire and enjoy. When that form decays, changes, and becomes different, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and mental anguish at a later time. Therefore, I have fully awakened to this different state of dwelling, which is the transcendence of all forms and the practice of external emptiness.

「阿难！比丘不可欲哗说、乐于哗说、合会哗说、欲众、乐众、合会于众、不欲离众、不乐独住远离之处。若有比丘欲哗说、乐于哗说、合会哗说、欲众、乐众、合会于众、不欲离众、不乐独住远离处者，得时爱乐心解脱，及不时不移动心解脱者，终无是处。阿难！若有比丘不欲哗说，不乐哗说，不合会哗说，不欲于众，不乐于众，不合会众，欲离于众，常乐独住远离处者，得时爱乐心解脱，及不时不移动心解脱者，必有是处。所以者何？我不见有一色令我欲乐，彼色败坏变易，异时生愁戚啼哭、忧苦、懊恼，以是故我此异住处正觉尽觉，谓度一切色想行于外空。

Ānanda! Having practiced this state of dwelling, I have given rise to joy. This joy of mine is fully experienced by the body, with right mindfulness and right knowledge. It gives rise to joy, tranquility, bliss, and concentration. This concentration of mine is fully experienced by the body, with right mindfulness and right knowledge. Ānanda! If there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās who come to me, I will then practice such and such a mind for them, distancing them, delighting them in being without desire. I will also teach the Dharma for them, encouraging them. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu wishes to practice emptiness extensively, that bhikṣu should hold his inner mind and dwell in it, making it stable. Having held his inner mind and dwelt in it, making it stable, he should contemplate inner emptiness. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu says, 'I do not hold my inner mind and dwell in it, making it stable, contemplating inner emptiness,' then know that bhikṣu is greatly exhausting himself.

「阿难！我行此住处已，生欢悦，我此欢悦，一切身觉正念正智，生喜、生止、生乐、生定，如我此定，一切身觉正念正智。阿难！或有比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆私共来诣我，我便为彼行如是如是心，远离，乐无欲，我亦复为彼说法，劝助于彼。阿难！若比丘欲多行空者，彼比丘当持内心住止令一定，彼持内心住止令一定已，当念内空。阿难！若比丘作如是说我不持内心住止，不令一定，念内空者，当知彼比丘大自疲劳。

Ānanda! How does a bhikṣu hold his inner mind and dwell in it, making it stable? A bhikṣu, with the joy and bliss born of separation, soaks, permeates, fills, and pervades his body with the joy and bliss born of separation, without any part of his body that is not pervaded. Ānanda! It is like a person bathing, taking a ball of soap powder, mixing it with water, and making it into a ball. It soaks, permeates, fills, and pervades, inside and out, without any leakage. In the same way, Ānanda! A bhikṣu, with the joy and bliss born of separation, soaks, permeates, fills, and pervades his body with the joy and bliss born of separation, without any part of his body that is not pervaded. Ānanda! In this way, a bhikṣu holds his inner mind and dwells in it, making it stable. Having held his inner mind and dwelt in it, making it stable, he should contemplate inner emptiness. Having contemplated inner emptiness, his mind is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand inner emptiness. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, contemplating inner emptiness, is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand inner emptiness, then that bhikṣu should contemplate outer emptiness. Having contemplated outer emptiness, his mind is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand outer emptiness.

「阿难！云何比丘持内心住止令一定耶？比丘者，此身离生喜、乐，渍、尽润渍，普遍充满，离生喜、乐，无处不遍。阿难！犹人沐浴，器盛澡豆，以水浇和，和令作丸，渍尽润渍，普遍充满，内外周密，无处有漏。如是，阿难！比丘此身离生喜、乐，渍尽润渍，普遍充满，离生喜、乐，无处不遍。阿难！如是比丘持内心住止令得一定，彼持内心住止令一定已，当念内空。彼念内空已，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于内空也。阿难！若比丘观时，则知念内空，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于内空者，彼比丘当念外空，彼念外空已，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于外空也。

Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, contemplating outer emptiness, is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand outer emptiness, then that bhikṣu should contemplate inner and outer emptiness. Having contemplated inner and outer emptiness, his mind is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand inner and outer emptiness. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, contemplating inner and outer emptiness, is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand inner and outer emptiness, then that bhikṣu should contemplate the unmoving. Having contemplated the unmoving, his mind is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand the unmoving.

「阿难！若比丘观时，则知念外空，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于外空者，彼比丘当念内外空。彼念内外空已，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于内外空也。阿难！若比丘观时，则知念内外空，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于内外空者，彼比丘当念不移动。彼念不移动已，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于不移动也。

Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, contemplating the unmoving, is distracted, does not approach, is not clear, and does not dwell or understand the unmoving, then that bhikṣu should control his mind in that state, cultivate it, soften it, make it good, bright, pliant, and concentrated, and enjoy the bliss of seclusion. If his mind is controlled in that state, cultivated, softened, made good, bright, pliant, and concentrated, and enjoys the bliss of seclusion, then he should practice inner emptiness. Having practiced inner emptiness, his mind is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands inner emptiness. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, practicing inner emptiness, is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands inner emptiness, this is called right knowledge.

「阿难！若比丘观时，则知念不移动，其心移动，不趣向近，不得清澄，不住不解于不移动者，彼比丘彼彼心于彼彼定，御复御，习复习，软复软，善快柔和，摄乐远离。若彼心于彼彼定，御复御，习复习，软复软，善快柔和，摄乐远离已，当以内空成就游。彼内空成就游已，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于内空。阿难！如是比丘观时，则知内空成就游，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于内空者，是谓正知。

Ānanda! A bhikṣu should practice outer emptiness. Having practiced outer emptiness, his mind is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands outer emptiness. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, practicing outer emptiness, is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands outer emptiness, this is called right knowledge. Ānanda! A bhikṣu should practice inner and outer emptiness. Having practiced inner and outer emptiness, his mind is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands inner and outer emptiness. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, practicing inner and outer emptiness, is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands inner and outer emptiness, this is called right knowledge. Ānanda! A bhikṣu should practice the unmoving. Having practiced the unmoving, his mind is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands the unmoving. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu observes and knows that his mind, practicing the unmoving, is not distracted, approaches, is clear, dwells, and understands the unmoving, this is called right knowledge.

「阿难！比丘当以外空成就游，彼外空成就游已，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于外空。阿难！如是比丘观时，则知外空成就游，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于外空者，是谓正知。阿难！比丘当以内外空成就游，彼内外空成就游已，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于内外空。阿难！如是比丘观时，则知内外空成就游，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于内外空者，是谓正知。阿难！当以不移动成就游，彼不移动成就游已，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于不移动。阿难！如是比丘观时，则知不移动成就游，心不移动，趣向于近，得清澄住，解于不移动者，是谓正知。

Ānanda! If a bhikṣu practices this state of mind and wants to walk, he should leave the meditation hut, walk in the shade of the hut, and not let his senses wander outside. Thinking of the past, he should walk like this. If his mind does not give rise to greed, sorrow, or evil and unwholesome dharmas, this is called right knowledge. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu practices this state of mind and wants to sit in meditation, he should leave the walking path, go to the end of the walking path, spread out his sitting mat, and sit cross-legged. If he sits like this, his mind will not give rise to greed, sorrow, or evil and unwholesome dharmas. This is called right knowledge. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu practices this state of mind and wants to think about something, he should not think about the three kinds of evil and unwholesome thoughts: thoughts of desire, anger, and harm. He should think about the three kinds of wholesome thoughts: thoughts of non-desire, non-anger, and non-harm. If he thinks like this, his mind will not give rise to greed, sorrow, or evil and unwholesome dharmas. This is called right knowledge.

「阿难！彼比丘行此住处心，若欲经行者，彼比丘从禅室出，在室影中露地经行，诸根在内，心不向外，后作前想，如是经行已，心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，是谓正知。阿难！彼比丘行此住处心，若欲坐定者，彼比丘从离经行，至经行头，敷尼师檀，结跏趺坐，如是坐定已，心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，是谓正知。阿难！彼比丘行此住处心，若欲有所念者，彼比丘若此三恶不善之念：欲念、恚念、害念，莫念此三恶不善之念。若此三善念：无欲念、无恚念、无害念，当念此三善念。如是念已，心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，是谓正知。

Ānanda! If a bhikṣu practices this state of mind and wants to speak, he should not speak about the following kinds of non-holy discussions that are meaningless: discussions about kings, thieves, fighting, food and drink, clothing, women, girls, prostitutes, the world, wrong paths, and the ocean. He should not discuss such animal-like topics. He should discuss the following kinds of holy discussions that are meaningful and can soften the mind, free it from the hindrances, namely: discussions about giving, precepts, concentration, wisdom, liberation, the knowledge and vision of liberation, gradual reduction, non-meeting, few desires, contentment, non-desire, cutting off, extinction, sitting in meditation, and dependent origination. These are the discussions of śramaṇas. If he discusses like this, his mind will not give rise to greed, sorrow, or evil and unwholesome dharmas. This is called right knowledge.

「阿难！彼比丘行此住处心，若欲有所说者，彼比丘若此论非圣论，无义相应，谓论王论、贼论、鬪诤论、饮食论、衣被论、妇人论、童女论、婬女论、世间论、邪道论、海中论，不论如是种种畜生论。若论圣论与义相应，令心柔和，无诸阴盖，谓论施论、戒论、定论、慧论、解脱论、解脱知见论、渐损论、不会论、少欲论、知足论、无欲论、断论、灭论、燕坐论、缘起论，如是沙门所论。如是论已，心中不生贪伺、忧戚、恶不善法，是谓正知。

Furthermore, Ānanda! There are five kinds of sensual pleasures that are delightful, pleasing to the mind, and associated with desire for form. The eyes know forms, the ears know sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile sensations. If a bhikṣu's mind reaches and contemplates these five kinds of sensual pleasures, he follows the sensual pleasures in his mind. Why is that? Because there is no before or after, these five kinds of sensual pleasures follow the sensual pleasures in the mind. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know that these five kinds of sensual pleasures follow the sensual pleasures in the mind. That bhikṣu contemplates those sensual pleasures as impermanent, as decaying, as without desire, as cut off, as extinguished, as cut off and let go. If there is desire and defilement for these five kinds of sensual pleasures, they will be extinguished. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu contemplates like this, he will know that there is desire and defilement for these five kinds of sensual pleasures, and they have been cut off. This is called right knowledge.

「复次，阿难！有五欲功德，可乐、意所念，爱色欲相应，眼知色，耳知声，鼻知香，舌知味，身知触。若比丘心至到，观此五欲功德，随其欲功德，若心中行者。所以者何？无前无后，此五欲功德，随其欲功德，心中行者。阿难！若比丘观时，则知此五欲功德，随其欲功德，心中行者。彼比丘彼彼欲功德，观无常、观衰耗、观无欲、观断、观灭、观断舍离，若此五欲功德有欲有染者，彼即灭也。阿难！若如是比丘观时，则知者此五欲功德有欲有染，彼已断也，是谓正知。

"Furthermore, Ānanda! There are five aggregates of clinging: the form aggregate of clinging, the feeling aggregate of clinging, the perception aggregate of clinging, the volitional formations aggregate of clinging, and the consciousness aggregate of clinging. A bhikṣu contemplates their arising and ceasing in this way: 'This is form, this is the origin of form, this is the cessation of form.' 'This is feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness. This is the origin of consciousness, this is the cessation of consciousness.' If there is a sense of self in these five aggregates of clinging, they will be extinguished. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu contemplates like this, he will know that the sense of self in the five aggregates has been extinguished. This is called right knowledge. Ānanda! This Dharma is entirely good, entirely pleasant, entirely delightful, without taints, without clinging, not reached by Māra, not reached by evil, not reached by all evil and unwholesome dharmas, defiled, leading to future existence, hot, with the result of suffering, and the cause of birth, old age, sickness, and death. It is not reached. It is because of this non-distraction that all Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Perfectly Awakened Ones attain awakening. Because of the root of non-distraction, all immeasurable wholesome dharmas arise. If one follows the path, Ānanda! Therefore, you should train like this. I also accomplish non-distraction. I should train like this. Ānanda! Why do the faithful disciples follow the World-Honored One and serve him until the end of their lives?"

「复次，阿难！有五盛阴，色盛阴，觉、想、行、识盛阴，谓比丘如是观兴衰，是色、是色习、是色灭，是觉、想、行、识，是识、是识习、是识灭。若此五盛阴有我慢者，彼即灭也。阿难！若有比丘如是观时，则知五阴中我慢已灭，是谓正知。阿难！是法一向可、一向乐、一向意念，无漏无受，魔所不及，恶所不及，诸恶不善法、秽污、当来有本、烦热苦报生老病死因亦所不及，谓成就此不放逸也。所以者何？因不放逸，诸如来、无所著、等正觉得觉，因不放逸根，生诸无量善法。若有随道品，阿难！是故汝当如是学。我亦成就于不放逸，当学如是。阿难！以何义故，信弟子随世尊行奉事至命尽耶？」

Venerable Ānanda said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. Please explain it. Having heard it, I will be able to know its meaning in detail."

尊者阿难白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我今闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! Listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you in detail." Venerable Ānanda accepted the teaching and listened.

佛便告曰：「阿难！谛听，善思念之，我当为汝具分别说。」尊者阿难受教而听。

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! It is not because of reciting the true scriptures, chanting, and narrating that the faithful disciples follow the World-Honored One in service until the end of their lives. Rather, Ānanda! It is because they have heard this Dharma many times, recited and practiced it a thousand times, contemplated it with their minds, and clearly seen and deeply understood it. If this discussion of the holy teachings accords with the meaning, it will make the mind gentle and free from the five hindrances. That is, discussing giving, discussing precepts, discussing concentration, discussing wisdom, discussing liberation, discussing the knowledge and vision of liberation, discussing gradual reduction, discussing non-meeting, discussing little desire, discussing contentment, discussing desirelessness, discussing cutting off, discussing extinction, discussing sitting in meditation, discussing dependent origination. Such discussions by monastics are easy to obtain and not difficult to obtain. For this reason, the faithful disciples follow the World-Honored One in service until the end of their lives. Ānanda! This is troublesome for the teacher, troublesome for the disciples, and troublesome for the practice of the holy life.

佛言：「阿难！不其正经、歌咏、记说故，信弟子随世尊行奉事至命尽也。但，阿难！或彼长夜数闻此法，诵习至千，意所惟观，明见深达，若此论圣论与义相应，令心柔和，无诸阴盖，谓论施论、戒论、定论、慧论、解脱论、解脱知见论、渐损论、不会论、少] cf.［丽】欲论、知足论、无欲论、断论、灭论、燕坐论、缘起论。如是沙门所论，得、易不难得，因此义故，信弟子随世尊行奉事至命尽也。阿难！如是为烦师，为烦弟子，为烦梵行。

Ānanda! What is troublesome for the teacher? If the teacher appears in the world, he has thoughts and contemplations, and goes to a place of contemplation. He has mixed thoughts and contemplations. He has eloquence. He dwells in a place free from affairs, under a tree in a forest, or on a high cliff, in a quiet place without sound, far away, without evil, without people, suitable for sitting in meditation. He dwells there, practicing diligently in seclusion, attaining supreme concentration, and dwelling in happiness in the present life. He practices diligently in seclusion, happily wandering, and then returns to the disciples, the brahmins, householders, villages, and people of the country. Having returned to the disciples, the brahmins, householders, villages, and people of the country, he then returns home with great merit. This is troublesome for the teacher. It is also unwholesome, defiled, leading to future existence, causing afflictions and suffering, and the cause of birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is called troublesome for the teacher.

「阿难！云何为烦师？若师出世，有策虑思惟，往策虑地，有思惟观杂，凡人有辩才，彼住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺宴坐，或住彼处，学远离精勤，得增上心，现法乐居。彼学远离，精勤安隐，快乐游行已，随弟子还梵志、居士、村邑、国人。彼随弟子还梵志、居士、村邑、国人已，便功高还家。如是为烦师，是亦为恶不善法、秽污，当来有本，烦热苦报、生老病死因所烦，是谓烦师。

Ānanda! What is troublesome for the disciples? The teacher's disciples practice seclusion. They dwell in a place free from affairs, under a tree in a forest, or on a high cliff, in a quiet place without sound, far away, without evil, without people, suitable for sitting in meditation. They dwell there, practicing diligently in seclusion, attaining supreme concentration, and dwelling in happiness in the present life. They practice diligently in seclusion, happily wandering, and then return to the disciples, the brahmins, householders, villages, and people of the country. Having returned to the disciples, the brahmins, householders, villages, and people of the country, they then return home with great merit. This is troublesome for the disciples. It is also unwholesome, defiled, leading to future existence, causing afflictions and suffering, and the cause of birth, old age, sickness, and death. This is called troublesome for the disciples.

「阿难！云何为烦弟子？彼师弟子学彼远离，彼住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐，或住彼处，学远离精勤，得增上心，现法乐居，彼学远离，精勤安隐，快乐游行已，随弟子还梵志、居士、村邑、国人。彼随弟子还梵志、居士、村邑、国人已，便功高还家。如是为烦弟子，是亦为恶不善法，秽污，当来有本，烦热苦报、生老病死因所烦，是谓烦弟子。

"Ānanda! What is troublesome for the holy life? When the Tathāgata appears in the world, the one without attachment, the fully enlightened one, the accomplished one in knowledge and conduct, the Sugata, the knower of the world, the unsurpassed guide for those who can be trained, the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One, he dwells in a place free from affairs, under a tree in a forest, or on a high cliff, in a quiet place without sound, far away, without evil, without people, suitable for sitting in meditation. Ānanda! For what reason does the Tathāgata dwell in a place free from affairs, under a tree in a forest, or on a high cliff, in a quiet place without sound, far away, without evil, without people, suitable for sitting in meditation?"

「阿难！云何为烦梵行？若如来出世，无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐，彼住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。阿难！如来以何义故，住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐耶？」

Venerable Ānanda said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. Please explain it. Having heard it, I will be able to understand it extensively."

尊者阿难白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我今闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! Listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you in detail." Venerable Ānanda accepted the teaching and listened.

佛便告曰：「阿难！谛听，善思念之，我当为汝具分别说。」尊者阿难受教而听。

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! The Tathāgata does not dwell in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, in quiet and secluded places, far from noise, away from people, in order to attain what has not yet been attained, to obtain what has not yet been obtained, or to realize what has not yet been realized. Ānanda! The Tathāgata dwells in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, in quiet and secluded places, far from noise, away from people, in order to practice in accordance with the Dharma, and out of compassion for future generations. There may be future generations who will emulate the Tathāgata in dwelling in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, in quiet and secluded places, far from noise, away from people, in order to practice in accordance with the Dharma. Ānanda! The Tathāgata dwells in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, in quiet and secluded places, far from noise, away from people, in order to practice in accordance with the Dharma, and out of compassion for future generations. Having practiced in accordance with the Dharma, diligently and energetically, he attains the supreme mind and dwells in the bliss of the present Dharma. Having practiced in accordance with the Dharma, diligently and energetically, he dwells in peace and happiness. Having returned to the monks, nuns, male lay followers, and female lay followers who practice in accordance with the Dharma, he does not return home in vain. Ānanda! If he attains the liberation of the mind that is unshakable, I do not say that he will be obstructed. If he attains the supreme mind and dwells in the bliss of the present Dharma, it is because he has practiced diligently and energetically. This may be lost due to the gathering of many disciples.

佛言：「阿难！如来非为未得欲得、未获欲获、未证欲证故，住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。阿难！如来但以二义故，住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐，一者为自现法乐居故，二者慈愍后生人故。或有后生人效如来住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。阿难！如来以此义故，住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐，或住彼处，学远离精勤，得增上心，现法乐居。彼学远离，精勤安隐，快乐游行已，随梵行还比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆私。彼随梵行还比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆私已，便不功高而不还家。阿难！若彼不移动心解脱作证，我不说彼有障碍也。若彼得四增上心现法乐居，本为精勤，无放逸游行故，此或可有失以弟子多集会故。

Furthermore, Ānanda! Those teachers and disciples who emulate dwelling in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, in quiet and secluded places, far from noise, away from people, in order to practice in accordance with the Dharma, or who dwell in those places, practice in accordance with the Dharma, diligently and energetically, attain the supreme mind, and dwell in the bliss of the present Dharma, having practiced in accordance with the Dharma, diligently and energetically, dwell in peace and happiness. Having returned to the monks, nuns, male lay followers, and female lay followers who practice in accordance with the Dharma, they return home in vain. This is called the disturbance of the practice of the Dharma. This is also an unwholesome, defiled, and future-oriented practice, leading to the suffering of heat, old age, sickness, and death. This is called the disturbance of the practice of the Dharma. Ānanda! Among the disturbance of teachers and the disturbance of disciples, this disturbance of the practice of the Dharma is the most undesirable, unpleasant, and unloved, and is the least thought of. Ānanda! Therefore, you should practice loving deeds towards me, and not hateful deeds.

「复次，阿难！彼师弟子效住无事处山林树下，或居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。或住彼处，学远离精勤，得增上心，现法乐居，彼学远离，精勤安隐，快乐游行已，随梵行还比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆私。彼随梵行还比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆私已，便功高还家。如是为烦梵行，是亦为恶不善法、秽污，当来有本，烦热苦报、生老病死因所烦，是谓烦梵行。阿难！于烦师、烦弟子，此烦梵行最为不可、不乐、不爱，最意不念。阿难！是故汝等于我行慈事，莫行怨事。

Ānanda! How do disciples practice hateful deeds towards their teachers, and not loving deeds? If the teacher teaches the Dharma to the disciples out of compassion, seeking their benefit and well-being, seeking their peace and happiness, with a mind of loving-kindness, this is for their benefit, this is for their happiness, this is for their benefit and happiness. If the disciples are not respectful, do not follow, do not establish wisdom, their minds do not turn towards the Dharma, they do not accept the true Dharma, they violate the teacher's instructions, and are unable to attain concentration, then such disciples practice hateful deeds towards their teachers, and not loving deeds.

「阿难！云何弟子于师行怨事，不行慈事？若尊师为弟子说法，怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，发慈悲心，是为饶益，是为快乐，是为饶益乐。若彼弟子而不恭敬，亦不顺行，不立于智，其心不趣向法次法，不受正法，违犯师教，不能得定者，如是弟子于师行怨事，不行慈事。

Ānanda! How do disciples practice loving deeds towards their teachers, and not hateful deeds? If the teacher teaches the Dharma to the disciples out of compassion, seeking their benefit and well-being, seeking their peace and happiness, with a mind of loving-kindness, this is for their benefit, this is for their happiness, this is for their benefit and happiness. If the disciples are respectful, follow, establish wisdom, their minds turn towards the Dharma, they accept the true Dharma, do not violate the teacher's instructions, and are able to attain concentration, then such disciples practice loving deeds towards their teachers, and not hateful deeds. Ānanda! Therefore, you should practice loving deeds towards me, and not hateful deeds. Why is that? I do not teach like a potter making pots. Ānanda! I teach that it is difficult to reach, but if there is truth, one can surely go there.

「阿难！云何弟子于师行慈事，不行怨事？若尊师为弟子说法，怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，发慈悲心，是为饶益，是为快乐，是为饶益乐。若彼弟子恭敬顺行而立于智，其心归趣向法次法，受持正法，不违师教，能得定者，如是弟子于师行慈事，不行怨事。阿难！是故汝等于我行慈事，莫行怨事。所以者何？我不如是说，如陶师作瓦。阿难！我说严急至苦，若有真实者，必能往也。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The fifth chapter on great emptiness is finished.
