MĀ 195

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 16 大品

195. （一九五）阿湿贝经

Parallel to: MN 70

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Kāśi, together with a large assembly of bhikṣus. He was staying in a certain place and addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, I am free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, my body is light and at ease, my strength is strong and healthy, and I am at peace and happy. You should also eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, you will be free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, your body will be light and at ease, your strength will be strong and healthy, and you will be at peace and happy."

一时，佛游迦尸国，与大比丘众俱，游在一处，告诸比丘：「我日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。」

At that time, the World-Honored One established the precept of eating only one meal a day for the bhikṣu assembly. The bhikṣu assembly all upheld the precepts and the subtle Dharma of the World-Honored One's realm. Then, the World-Honored One gradually arrived at Karala and stayed in the Śiṣyahavana Grove north of Karala village.

尔时，世尊为比丘众施设日一食戒，诸比丘众皆奉学戒及世尊境界诸微妙法。于是，世尊展转到迦罗赖，住迦罗赖北村尸摄和林。

At that time, there were two bhikṣus in Karala, named Aśvajit and Punarvasu. They were the former landowners, temple owners, and clan leaders. They ate breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon. After eating breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon, they were free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, their bodies were light and at ease, their strength was strong and healthy, and they were at peace and happy. Many bhikṣus heard this and went to the bhikṣus Aśvajit and Punarvasu, saying to them, "Aśvajit! Punarvasu! The World-Honored One was traveling in the country of Kāśi, together with a large assembly of bhikṣus. He was staying in a certain place and addressed the bhikṣus, saying, 'I eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, I am free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, my body is light and at ease, my strength is strong and healthy, and I am at peace and happy. You should also eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, you will be free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, your body will be light and at ease, your strength will be strong and healthy, and you will be at peace and happy.' At that time, the World-Honored One established the precept of eating only one meal a day for the bhikṣu assembly. The bhikṣu assembly all upheld the precepts and the subtle Dharma of the World-Honored One's realm. Aśvajit! Punarvasu! You should also eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, you will be free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, your body will be light and at ease, your strength will be strong and healthy, and you will be at peace and happy. Do not go against the World-Honored One and the bhikṣu assembly."

尔时，迦罗赖中有二比丘，一名阿湿贝，二名弗那婆修，旧土地主、寺主、宗主，彼朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食。彼朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。众多比丘闻已，往诣阿湿贝及弗那婆修比丘所，而语彼曰：「阿湿贝！弗那婆修！世尊游迦尸国，与大比丘众俱，游在一处，告诸比丘：『我日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。』尔时，世尊为比丘众施设日一食戒，诸比丘众皆奉学戒及世尊境界诸微妙法。阿湿贝！弗那婆修！汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐；汝等莫违世尊及比丘众。」

Aśvajit and Punarvasu heard this and replied, "Venerable friends! We eat breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon. After eating breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon, we are free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, our bodies are light and at ease, our strength is strong and healthy, and we are at peace and happy. Why should we abandon the present and wait for the future?" They said this a second and a third time.

阿湿贝、弗那婆修闻已，报曰：「诸贤！我等朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食。朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐，我等何缘舍现而须待后？」如是再三。

Those many bhikṣus were unable to make Aśvajit and Punarvasu abandon their wrong views. They then rose from their seats, left them, and went to where the Buddha was. They bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said, "World-Honored One! In Karala there are two bhikṣus, named Aśvajit and Punarvasu. They are the former landowners, temple owners, and clan leaders. They eat breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon. After eating breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon, they are free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, their bodies are light and at ease, their strength is strong and healthy, and they are at peace and happy. World-Honored One! Having heard this, we went to the bhikṣus Aśvajit and Punarvasu and said to them, 'Aśvajit! Punarvasu! The World-Honored One was traveling in the country of Kāśi, together with a large assembly of bhikṣus. He was staying in a certain place and addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, I am free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, my body is light and at ease, my strength is strong and healthy, and I am at peace and happy. You should also eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, you will be free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, your body will be light and at ease, your strength will be strong and healthy, and you will be at peace and happy." At that time, the World-Honored One established the precept of eating only one meal a day for the bhikṣu assembly. The bhikṣu assembly all upheld the precepts and the subtle Dharma of the World-Honored One's realm. Aśvajit! Punarvasu! You should also eat only one meal a day. After eating only one meal a day, you will be free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, your body will be light and at ease, your strength will be strong and healthy, and you will be at peace and happy. Do not go against the World-Honored One and the bhikṣu assembly."

彼众多比丘不能令阿湿贝及弗那婆修除恶邪见，即从坐起，舍之而去，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却住一面，白曰：「世尊！此迦罗赖中有二比丘，一名阿湿贝，二名弗那婆修，旧土地主、寺主、宗主，彼朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食。彼朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。世尊！我等闻已，便往至阿湿贝及弗那婆修比丘所，而语彼曰：『阿湿贝！弗那婆修！世尊游迦尸国，与大比丘众俱，游在一处，告诸比丘：「我日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。」尔时，世尊为比丘众施设日一食戒，诸比丘众皆奉学戒及世尊境界诸微妙法。阿湿贝！弗那婆修！汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐；汝等莫违世尊及比丘众。』

"Aśvajit and Punarvasu heard this and replied to us, 'Venerable friends! We eat breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon. After eating breakfast, dinner, lunch, and meals after noon, we are free from desires, free from seeking, free from illness and pain, our bodies are light and at ease, our strength is strong and healthy, and we are at peace and happy. Why should we abandon the present and wait for the future?' They said this a second and a third time. World-Honored One! As we were unable to make Aśvajit and Punarvasu abandon their wrong views, we rose from our seats and left them."

「阿湿贝、弗那婆修闻已，报我等曰：『诸贤！我等朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食，朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐，我等何缘舍现而须待后？』如是再三。世尊！如我等不能令阿湿贝、弗那婆修除恶邪见，即从坐起，舍之而去。」

The World-Honored One heard this and told a bhikṣu, "Go to the bhikṣus Aśvajit and Punarvasu and say, 'Aśvajit! Punarvasu! The World-Honored One calls you.'"

世尊闻已，告一比丘：「汝往至阿湿贝、弗那婆修比丘所，语如是曰：『阿湿贝！弗那婆修！世尊呼汝等。』」

The bhikṣu heard this and said, "Yes, World-Honored One!" He then rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times, and left. He went to the bhikṣus Aśvajit and Punarvasu and said, "Aśvajit! Punarvasu! The World-Honored One calls you, venerable ones."

一比丘闻已：「唯然世尊！」即从坐起，稽首佛足，绕三匝而去，至阿湿贝及弗那婆修比丘所，语如是曰：「阿湿贝！弗那婆修！世尊呼贤者等。」

Aśvajit and Punarvasu heard this and immediately went to where the Buddha was. They bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side.

阿湿贝、弗那婆修闻已，即诣佛所，稽首佛足，却坐一面。

The World-Honored One asked, "Aśvajit! Punarvasu! Did many bhikṣus really tell you, 'Bhikṣus Aśvajit and Punarvasu, when the World-Honored One was traveling in the country of Kāśi with a large assembly of bhikṣus, he said to the bhikṣus, "I eat one meal a day. After eating one meal a day, I am without activity or seeking, without illness or pain, with a light body, strong energy, and peaceful happiness. You should also eat one meal a day. After eating one meal a day, you will be without activity or seeking, without illness or pain, with a light body, strong energy, and peaceful happiness." At that time, the World-Honored One established the precept of eating one meal a day for the assembly of bhikṣus. The assembly of bhikṣus all practiced the precepts and the subtle Dharma of the World-Honored One's realm. Aśvajit! Punarvasu! You should also eat one meal a day. After eating one meal a day, you will be without activity or seeking, without illness or pain, with a light body, strong energy, and peaceful happiness. Do not go against the World-Honored One and the assembly of bhikṣus.'

世尊问曰：「阿湿贝！弗那婆修！众多比丘实语汝等：『阿湿贝、弗那婆修比丘，世尊游迦尸国，与大比丘众俱，游在一处，告诸比丘：「我日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐。」尔时，世尊为比丘众施设日一食戒，诸比丘众皆奉学戒及世尊境界诸微妙法。阿湿贝！弗那婆修！汝等亦应日一食，日一食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐；汝等莫违世尊及比丘众。』

"Aśvajit! Punarvasu! Having heard this, you told the bhikṣus, 'Venerable ones! We eat breakfast, dinner, lunch, and post-noon meals. After eating breakfast, dinner, lunch, and post-noon meals, we are without activity or seeking, without illness or pain, with a light body, strong energy, and peaceful happiness. Why should we abandon the present and wait for the future?' In this way, you said it twice more. Aśvajit! Punarvasu! Were the bhikṣus unable to make you abandon your evil views, so they got up from their seats and left?"

「阿湿贝！弗那婆修！汝等闻已，语诸比丘曰：『诸贤！我等朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食，朝食、暮食、昼食、过中食已，无为无求，无有病痛，身体轻便，气力康强，安隐快乐，我等何缘舍现而须待后？』如是再三。阿湿贝！弗那婆修！诸比丘不能令汝舍恶邪见，即从坐起，舍之而去耶？」

Aśvajit and Punarvasu replied, "Indeed."

阿湿贝、弗那婆修答曰：「实尔。」

The World-Honored One said, "Aśvajit! Punarvasu! You know and speak of such a teaching. If one experiences pleasant feelings, having experienced pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. If one experiences painful feelings, having experienced painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase, is that so?"

世尊告曰：「阿湿贝！弗那婆修！汝等知说如是法。若有觉乐觉者，彼觉乐觉已，恶不善法转增，善法转减。若有觉苦觉者，彼觉苦觉已，恶不善法转减，善法转增耶？」

Aśvajit and Punarvasu replied, "Yes. We know the World-Honored One's teaching in this way. If one experiences pleasant feelings, having experienced pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. If one experiences painful feelings, having experienced painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase."

阿湿贝、弗那婆修答曰：「唯然。我等如是知世尊说法。若有觉乐觉者，彼觉乐觉已，不善法转增，善法转减。若有觉苦觉者，彼觉苦觉已，不善法转减，善法转增。」

The World-Honored One rebuked the monks Aśvajit and Punarvasu, saying, "You foolish ones! How do you know that I teach the Dharma in this way? You foolish ones! From which mouth did you hear and know this teaching? You foolish ones! I did not teach it unilaterally, yet you unilaterally uphold it. You foolish ones! When speaking to many monks, you should answer like this according to the Dharma: 'We do not know. We should ask the monks!'"

世尊呵阿湿贝、弗那婆修比丘：「汝等痴人！何由知我如是说法？汝等痴人！从何口闻知如是说法？汝等痴人！我不一向说，汝等一向受持。汝等痴人！为众多比丘语时，应如是如法答：『我等未知，当问诸比丘！』」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the monks, "You also know my teaching of the Dharma in this way. If one experiences pleasant feelings, having experienced pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. If one experiences painful feelings, having experienced painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase, right?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等亦如是知我说法。若有觉乐觉者，彼觉乐觉已，不善法转增，善法转减。若有觉苦觉者，彼觉苦觉已，不善法转减，善法转增耶？」

The many monks replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

众多比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One further asked, "How do you know my teaching of the Dharma?"

世尊复问曰：「汝等云何知我说法？」

The many monks replied, "World-Honored One! We know the World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma like this: for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. For some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase. For some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. For some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase. World-Honored One! We know the World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma like this."

众多比丘答曰：「世尊！我等如是知世尊说法，或有觉乐觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减。或有觉乐觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增。或有觉苦觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减。或有觉苦觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增。世尊！我等如是知世尊所说法。」

Having heard this, the World-Honored One praised the monks, saying, "Excellent! Excellent! If you speak like this, then for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. For some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase. For some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. For some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase. Why is this? Because I also teach like this: for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. For some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase. For some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease. For some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase.

世尊闻已，叹诸比丘曰：「善哉！善哉！若汝如是说，或有觉乐觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减。或有觉乐觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增。或有觉苦觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减。或有觉苦觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增。所以者何？我亦如是说，或有觉乐觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减。或有觉乐觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增。或有觉苦觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减。或有觉苦觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增。

If I did not truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease, I would not say to abandon pleasant feelings. If I did not truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase, I would not say to cultivate pleasant feelings. If I did not truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease, I would not say to abandon painful feelings. If I did not truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase, I would not say to cultivate painful feelings.

「若我不知如真，不见、不解、不得、不正尽觉者，或有乐觉者，不善法转增，善法转减，我不应说断乐觉。若我不知如真，不见、不解、不得、不正尽觉者，或有乐觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增，我不应说修乐觉。若我不知如真，不见、不解、不得、不正尽觉者，或有苦觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减，我不应说断苦觉。若我不知如真，不见、不解、不得、不正尽觉者，或有苦觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增，我不应说修苦觉。

If I truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease, therefore I say to abandon pleasant feelings. If I truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience pleasant feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase, therefore I say to cultivate pleasant feelings. If I truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas decrease, therefore I say to abandon painful feelings. If I truly know, see, understand, attain, and fully awaken to this, then for some, when they experience painful feelings, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas increase, therefore I say to cultivate painful feelings.

「若我知如真，见、解、得、正尽觉者，或有乐觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减，是故我说断乐觉。若我知如真，见、解、得、正尽觉者，或有乐觉，恶不善法转减，善法转增，是故我说修乐觉。若我知如真，见、解、得、正尽觉者，或有苦觉者，恶不善法转增，善法转减，是故我说断苦觉。若我知如真，见、解、得、正尽觉者，或有苦觉者，恶不善法转减，善法转增，是故我说修苦觉。所以者何？我不说修一切身乐，亦不说莫修一切身乐。我不说修一切身苦，亦不说莫修一切身苦。我不说修一切心乐，亦不说莫修一切心乐。我不说修一切心苦，亦不说莫修一切心苦。

How do I say not to cultivate bodily pleasure? If cultivating bodily pleasure increases unwholesome dharmas and decreases wholesome dharmas, I say not to cultivate such bodily pleasure. How do I say to cultivate bodily pleasure? If cultivating bodily pleasure decreases unwholesome dharmas and increases wholesome dharmas, I say to cultivate such bodily pleasure. How do I say not to cultivate bodily pain? If cultivating bodily pain increases unwholesome dharmas and decreases wholesome dharmas, I say not to cultivate such bodily pain. How do I say to cultivate bodily pain? If cultivating bodily pain decreases unwholesome dharmas and increases wholesome dharmas, I say to cultivate such bodily pain. How do I say not to cultivate mental pleasure? If cultivating mental pleasure increases unwholesome dharmas and decreases wholesome dharmas, I say not to cultivate such mental pleasure. How do I say to cultivate mental pleasure? If cultivating mental pleasure decreases unwholesome dharmas and increases wholesome dharmas, I say to cultivate such mental pleasure. How do I say not to cultivate mental pain? If cultivating mental pain increases unwholesome dharmas and decreases wholesome dharmas, I say not to cultivate such mental pain. How do I say to cultivate mental pain? If cultivating mental pain decreases unwholesome dharmas and increases wholesome dharmas, I say to cultivate such mental pain.

「云何身乐我说不修？若修身乐，恶不善法转增，善法转减者，如是身乐我说不修。云何身乐我说修耶？若修身乐，恶不善法转减，善法转增者，如是身乐我说修也。云何身苦我说不修？若修身苦，恶不善法转增，善法转减者，如是身苦我说不修。云何身苦我说修耶？若修身苦，恶不善法转减，善法转增者，如是身苦我说修也。云何心乐我说不修？若修心乐，恶不善法转增，善法转减者，如是心乐我说不修。云何心乐我说修耶？若修心乐，恶不善法转减，善法转增者，如是心乐我说修也。云何心苦我说不修？若修心苦，恶不善法转增，善法转减者，如是心苦我说不修。云何心苦我说修耶？若修心苦，恶不善法转减，善法转增者，如是心苦我说修也。彼可修法知如真，不可修法亦知如真，彼可修法知如真，不可修法亦知如真已，不可修法便不修，可修法便修，不可修法便不修，可修法便修已，便恶不善法转减，善法转增。

I do not say that all monks practice heedfulness, nor do I say that all monks do not practice heedfulness. How do I say that a monk does not practice heedfulness? If there is a monk who is liberated through both means. How is a monk liberated through both means? If there is a monk who dwells touching with his body the eight liberations, and with wisdom sees the end of the contaminants, such a monk is liberated through both means. I say that such a monk does not practice heedfulness. Why is that? Because this wise one has already practiced heedfulness. If this wise one had practiced heedlessness, it would not be possible. Therefore I say that such a monk does not practice heedfulness. If there is a monk who is liberated through wisdom, not liberated through both means. How is a monk liberated through wisdom? If there is a monk who does not dwell touching with his body the eight liberations, but with wisdom sees the end of the contaminants, such a monk is liberated through wisdom. I say that such a monk does not practice heedfulness. Why is that? Because this wise one has already practiced heedfulness. If this wise one had practiced heedlessness, it would not be possible. Therefore I say that such a monk does not practice heedfulness. I say that these two monks do not practice heedfulness.

「我不说一切比丘行无放逸，亦复不说一切比丘不行无放逸。云何比丘我说不行无放逸？若有比丘俱解脱者。云何比丘有俱解脱？若有比丘八解脱身触成就游，已慧见诸漏已尽已知，如是比丘有俱解脱，此比丘我说不行无放逸。所以者何？此贤者本已行无放逸，若此贤者本有放逸者，终无是处，是故我说此比丘不行无放逸。若有比丘非俱解脱，有慧解脱者，云何比丘有慧解脱？若有比丘八解脱身不触成就游，以慧见诸漏已尽已知，如是比丘有慧解脱，此比丘我说不行无放逸。所以者何？此贤者本已行无放逸，若此贤者本有放逸者，终无是处，是故我说此比丘不行无放逸。此二比丘我说不行无放逸。

How do I say that a monk practices heedfulness? If there is a monk who is neither liberated through both means nor liberated through wisdom, but has attained the body-witness. How is a monk a body-witness? If there is a monk who dwells touching with his body the eight liberations, but does not see the end of the contaminants with wisdom, such a monk is a body-witness. I say that such a monk practices heedfulness. I see that this monk practices heedfulness. What fruit does it have that I say this monk practices heedfulness? It is that this monk seeks the faculties, cultivates good friends, practices in accordance with the way of dwelling, and with the contaminants exhausted attains the undefiled liberation of mind and liberation through wisdom. In this very life he knows and realizes for himself, accomplishes and dwells in, the end of birth, the practice of the holy life, the task accomplished, and the future life without further becoming. I see that this monk practicing heedfulness has such fruit. Therefore I say that this monk practices heedfulness.

「云何比丘我为说行无放逸？若有比丘非俱解脱亦非慧解脱，而有身证。云何比丘而有身证？若有比丘八解脱身触成就游，不以慧见诸漏已尽已知，如是比丘而有身证。此比丘我为说行无放逸，我见此比丘行无放逸，为有何果，令我为此比丘说行无放逸耶？或此比丘求于诸根，习善知识，行随顺住止，诸漏已尽得无漏，心解脱、慧解脱，于现法中自知自觉自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。谓我见此比丘行无放逸，有如是果。是故我为此比丘说行无放逸。

If there is a monk who is neither liberated through both means nor liberated through wisdom, nor a body-witness, but has attained the view of the Dharma. How is a monk a Dharma-witness? If there is a monk who is single-mindedly determined in faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and with wisdom he increases his superior understanding and superior patience according to the teachings he hears, such a monk is a Dharma-witness. I say that such a monk practices heedfulness. I see that this monk practices heedfulness. What fruit does it have that I say this monk practices heedfulness? It is that this monk seeks the faculties, cultivates good friends, practices in accordance with the way of dwelling, and with the contaminants exhausted attains the undefiled liberation of mind and liberation through wisdom. In this very life he knows and realizes for himself, accomplishes and dwells in, the end of birth, the practice of the holy life, the task accomplished, and the future life without further becoming. I see that this monk practicing heedfulness has such fruit. Therefore I say that this monk practices heedfulness.

「若有比丘非俱解脱非慧解脱，亦非身证而有见到。云何比丘而有见到？若有比丘一向决定信佛、法、众，随所闻法，便以慧增上观、增上忍，如是比丘而有见到。此比丘我说行无放逸，我见此比丘行无放逸，为有何果，令我为此比丘说行无放逸耶？或此比丘求于诸根，习善知识，行随顺住止，诸漏已尽得无漏，心解脱、慧解脱，于现法中自知自觉自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。谓我见此比丘行无放逸，有如是果。是故我为此比丘说行无放逸。

If there is a monk who is neither liberated through both means nor liberated through wisdom, nor a body-witness, nor a Dharma-witness, but has attained the faith-liberation. How is a monk a faith-liberation? If there is a monk who is single-mindedly determined in faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and with wisdom he increases his superior understanding and superior patience according to the teachings he hears, but not like the Dharma-witness, such a monk is a faith-liberation. I say that such a monk practices heedfulness. I see that this monk practices heedfulness. What fruit does it have that I say this monk practices heedfulness? It is that this monk seeks the faculties, cultivates good friends, practices in accordance with the way of dwelling, and with the contaminants exhausted attains the undefiled liberation of mind and liberation through wisdom. In this very life he knows and realizes for himself, accomplishes and dwells in, the end of birth, the practice of the holy life, the task accomplished, and the future life without further becoming. I see that this monk practicing heedfulness has such fruit. Therefore I say that this monk practices heedfulness.

「若有比丘非俱解脱非慧解脱，又非身证亦非见到，而有信解脱。云何比丘有信解脱？若有比丘一向决定信佛、法、众，随所闻法，以慧观忍，不如见到，如是比丘有信解脱。此比丘我为说行无放逸，我见此比丘行无放逸，为有何果，令我为此比丘说行无放逸耶？或此比丘求于诸根，习善知识，行随顺住止，诸漏已尽得无漏，心解脱、慧解脱，于现法中自知自觉自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。谓我见此比丘行无放逸，有如是果。是故我为此比丘说行无放逸。

If there is a monk who is neither liberated through both means nor liberated through wisdom, nor a body-witness, nor a Dharma-witness, nor a faith-liberation, but has attained the Dharma-follower. How is a monk a Dharma-follower? If there is a monk who is single-mindedly determined in faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and with wisdom he increases his superior understanding and superior patience according to the teachings he hears, but not like the faith-liberation, such a monk is a Dharma-follower. I say that such a monk practices heedfulness. I see that this monk practices heedfulness. What fruit does it have that I say this monk practices heedfulness? It is that this monk seeks the faculties, cultivates good friends, practices in accordance with the way of dwelling, and among the two fruits he will certainly attain one, either the knowledge of the end of defilements in this very life, or if there is something remaining he will attain the state of a non-returner. I see that this monk practicing heedfulness has such fruit. Therefore I say that this monk practices heedfulness.

「若有比丘非俱解脱非慧解脱，又非身证复非见到，亦非信解脱，而有法行。云何比丘而有法行？若有比丘一向决定信佛、法、众，随所闻法，便以慧增上观、增上忍，如是比丘而有法行。此比丘我为说行无放逸，我见此比丘行无放逸，为有何果，令我为此比丘说行无放逸耶？或此比丘求于诸根，习善知识，行随顺住止，于二果中必得一也，或于现法得究竟智，若有余者得阿那含。谓我见此比丘行无放逸，有如是果。是故我为此比丘说行无放逸。

If there is a monk who is neither liberated through both means nor liberated through wisdom, nor a body-witness, nor a Dharma-witness, nor a faith-liberation, nor a Dharma-follower, but has attained the faith-follower. How is a monk a faith-follower? If there is a monk who is single-mindedly determined in faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and with wisdom he increases his superior understanding and superior patience according to the teachings he hears, but not like the Dharma-follower, such a monk is a faith-follower. I say that such a monk practices heedfulness. I see that this monk practices heedfulness. What fruit does it have that I say this monk practices heedfulness? It is that this monk seeks the faculties, cultivates good friends, practices in accordance with the way of dwelling, and among the two fruits he will certainly attain one, either the knowledge of the end of defilements in this very life, or if there is something remaining he will attain the state of a non-returner. I see that this monk practicing heedfulness has such fruit. Therefore I say that this monk practices heedfulness.

「若有比丘非俱解脱非慧解脱，又非身证复非见到，非信解脱亦非法行，而有信行。云何比丘而有信行？若有比丘一向决定信佛、法、众，随所闻法，以慧观忍，不如法行，如是比丘而有信行。此比丘我为说行无放逸，我见此比丘行无放逸，为有何果，令我为此比丘说行无放逸耶？或此比丘求于诸根，习善知识，行随顺住止，于二果中必得一也，或于现法得究竟智，若有余者得阿那含。谓我见此比丘行无放逸，有如是果。是故我为此比丘说行无放逸。此诸比丘我说行无放逸。

"I do not say that all monks attain the knowledge of the end of defilements, nor do I say that all monks attain it for the first time, but gradually they study, follow the path, receive instruction and admonition, and then the monks attain the knowledge of the end of defilements. What is the knowledge of the end of defilements that these monks attain? It is that some, having faith, go to see, having gone to see, they practice, having practiced, they listen to the Dharma with single-minded attention, having listened to the Dharma with single-minded attention, they uphold the Dharma, having upheld the Dharma, they contemplate, having contemplated, they measure, having measured, they observe. The noble disciples, having observed, realize the truth with wisdom, and they think: 'This truth I have not realized with the body before, nor observed with wisdom, this truth I now realize with the body, and observe with wisdom.' In this way, gradually studying, following the path, receiving instruction and admonition, then the monks attain the knowledge of the end of defilements, this is the knowledge of the end of defilements that these monks attain."

「我不说一切诸比丘得究竟智，亦复不说一切诸比丘初得究竟智，然渐渐习学趣迹，受教受诃，然后诸比丘得究竟智。此诸比丘所得究竟智，云何渐渐习学趣迹，受教受诃，然后诸比丘得究竟智。此诸比丘所得究竟智耶？或有信者便往诣，往诣已便奉习，奉习已便一心听法，一心听法已便持法，持法已便思惟，思惟已便平量，平量已便观察，贤圣弟子观察已，身谛作证，慧增上观，彼作是念：『此谛我未曾身作证，亦非慧增上观，此谛今身作证，以慧增上观。』如是渐渐习学趣迹，受教受诃，然后诸比丘得究竟智，此诸比丘所得究竟智。」

Then the World-Honored One said, "Ashibei! Punabbasu! There is a teaching called the Four Statements. I wish to explain it to you. Do you wish to know it?"

于是，世尊告曰：「阿湿贝！弗那婆修！有法名四句，我欲为汝说，汝等欲知耶？」

Ashibei and Punabbasu replied, "World-Honored One! Who are we? How can we know the Dharma?"

阿湿贝及弗那婆修白曰：「世尊！我等是谁？何由知法？」

Then the World-Honored One thought, "These foolish people have gone far beyond this true Dharma and Vinaya. If there were a Dharma teacher who was attached to food and did not transcend food, then his disciples should not quickly engage in negligence, let alone me who am not attached to food and have transcended food? A disciple with faith should say, 'The World-Honored One is my teacher, I am a disciple of the World-Honored One. The World-Honored One teaches the Dharma to me, the Well-Gone One teaches the Dharma to me, causing me to attain meaning, benefit, peace, and happiness for a long time.'

于是，世尊便作是念：「此愚痴人！越过于我此正法、律极大久远。若有法、律师贪著食、不离食者，彼弟子不应速行放逸，况复我不贪著食、远离于食？信弟子者应如是说：『世尊是我师，我是世尊弟子。世尊为我说法，善逝为我说法，令我长夜得义、得饶益安隐快乐。』

"Those faithful disciples have done much for the World-Honored One's realm, benefited much in the World-Honored One's realm, practiced much in the World-Honored One's realm, entered the World-Honored One's realm, and abided in the World-Honored One's realm. If they travel to the east, they will surely attain peace and be free from suffering. If they travel to the south, west, or north, they will surely attain peace and be free from suffering. If faithful disciples have done much for the World-Honored One's realm, benefited much in the World-Honored One's realm, practiced much in the World-Honored One's realm, entered the World-Honored One's realm, and abided in the World-Honored One's realm, I do not even speak of their abiding in good dharmas, let alone their decline. They should only increase good dharmas day and night without decline. If faithful disciples have done much for the World-Honored One's realm, benefited much in the World-Honored One's realm, practiced much in the World-Honored One's realm, entered the World-Honored One's realm, and abided in the World-Honored One's realm, they will surely attain one of the two fruits, either final knowledge in this life or else becoming a non-returner."

「彼信弟子于世尊境界多有所作，于世尊境界多所饶益，于世尊境界多有所行，入世尊境界，止世尊境界者，若游东方，必得安乐，无众苦患；若游南方、西方、北方者，必得安乐，无众苦患。若信弟子于世尊境界多有所作，于世尊境界多所饶益，于世尊境界多有所行，入世尊境界，止世尊境界者，我尚不说诸善法住，况说衰退？但当昼夜增长善法而不衰退。若信弟子于世尊境界多有所作，于世尊境界多所饶益，于世尊境界多有所行，入世尊境界，止世尊境界者，于二果中必得一也，或于现世得究竟智，或复有余得阿那含。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
