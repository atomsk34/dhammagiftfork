MĀ 198

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 16 大品

198. （一九八）调御地经

Parallel to: MN 125

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the city of Rājagṛha, staying in the Kalandakanivāpa in the Bamboo Grove.

一时，佛游王舍城，在竹林迦兰陀园。

At that time, the novice monk Aiyanahedi was also wandering in the city of Rājagṛha, staying in a meditation hut in an unoccupied place. At that time, the prince Jīvaka Komārabhacca was strolling around and came to where the novice monk Aiyanahedi was. They greeted each other and sat down to one side. Jīvaka said, "Noble one Achiśana, I wish to ask something. May I ask?"

尔时，沙弥阿夷那和提亦游王舍城，在无事处，住禅屋中。彼时王童子耆婆先那中后仿佯，至沙弥阿夷那和提所，共相问讯，却坐一面，语曰：「贤者阿奇舍那！欲有所问，听我问耶？」

The novice monk Aiyanahedi said, "Noble prince! Ask what you wish. I will think about it when I hear it."

沙弥阿夷那和提告曰：「贤王童子！欲问便问，我闻当思。」

The prince asked, "Achiśana! Is it true that in this Dharma and Vinaya, a monk who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness?"

王童子问曰：「阿奇舍那！实比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心耶？」

The novice monk replied, "Noble prince! It is true that in this Dharma and Vinaya, a monk who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness."

沙弥答曰：「贤王童子！实比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心。」

The prince further asked, "Noble Achiśana! Please tell me everything you have heard and memorized, about how a monk in this Dharma and Vinaya, who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness."

王童子复问曰：「贤者阿奇舍那！汝当随所闻，汝随所诵习者，尽向我说，如比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心。」

The novice monk replied, "Noble prince! I am not capable of explaining in detail, according to what I have heard and memorized, about how a monk in this Dharma and Vinaya, who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness. Noble prince! If I were to explain to you, according to what I have heard and memorized, about how a monk in this Dharma and Vinaya, who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness, you may not understand, and it would be a waste of my effort."

沙弥答曰：「贤王童子！我不堪任随所闻法，随所诵习，广向汝说，如比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心也。贤王童子！若我随所闻法，随所诵习，向贤王童子说，如比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心者，或贤王童子不知也，如是我唐烦劳。」

The prince said to the novice monk, "Noble Achiśana! You have not been defeated by others, so why are you retreating? Noble Achiśana! Please explain to me, according to what you have heard and memorized, about how a monk in this Dharma and Vinaya, who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness. If I understand, that is good. If I do not understand, I will not ask about the teachings again."

王童子语沙弥曰：「贤者阿奇舍那！汝未为他所伏，以何意故而自退耶？贤者阿奇舍那！如随所闻法，随所诵习，可向我说，如比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心。若我知者为善，若我不知者，我便不复更问诸法。」

Then the novice monk Aiyanahedi explained to the prince Jibo Xianna, according to what he had heard and memorized, about how a monk in this Dharma and Vinaya, who is not negligent, practices diligently, and attains single-mindedness.

于是，沙弥阿夷那和提随所闻法，随所诵习，向王童子耆婆先那说，如比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心。

Then the prince Jibo Xianna said, "Noble Achiśana! It is impossible for a monk in this Dharma and Vinaya, who is not negligent, to practice diligently and attain single-mindedness." Having said this, he immediately got up from his seat and left without taking leave.

于是，王童子耆婆先那语曰：「贤者阿奇舍那！若比丘此法、律中不放逸，行精勤，得一心者，终无是处。」说无是处已，即从坐起，不辞而去。

Not long after the prince Jibo Xianna left, the novice monk Aiyanahedi went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head in reverence, sat to one side, and told the Buddha everything that had been discussed with the prince Jibo Xianna.

王童子耆婆先那去后不久。于是，沙弥阿夷那和提往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，与王童子耆婆先那所共论者，尽向佛说。

The World-Honored One, having heard this, told the novice monk, "Achiśana! Stop! How can the prince Jibo Xianna know? He is attached to desire, consumed by desire, and burned by desire. If one cuts off desire, desire-love, and the burning of desire, and has no desire-knowledge, no desire-view, and no desire-perception, it is impossible for the prince to know or see this. Why is that? Achiśana! The prince Jibo Xianna always indulges in desire.

世尊闻已，告沙弥曰：「阿奇舍那！止！王童子耆婆先那云何得？行欲著欲，为欲爱所食，为欲所烧。若地断欲、断欲爱、断欲烧热，无欲知、无欲见、无欲觉，此地王童子知者、见者，终无是处。所以者何？阿奇舍那！王童子耆婆先那常行欲也。

Achiśana! It is like the four kinds of taming: taming elephants, taming horses, taming oxen, and taming humans. Among them, two kinds of taming are untamable, while two kinds of taming are tamable. What do you think, Achiśana? If these two untamable tamings are untamed, untamed grounds, and untamed objects of taming, it is impossible. If these two tamable tamings are well tamed, tamed grounds, and objects of taming, it is certain. In this way, Achiśana! Stop! How can the prince Jibo Xianna know? He is attached to desire, consumed by desire, and burned by desire. If one cuts off desire, desire-love, and the burning of desire, and has no desire-knowledge, no desire-view, and no desire-perception, it is impossible for the prince to know or see this. Why is that? Achiśana! The prince Jibo Xianna always indulges in desire.

「阿奇舍那！犹四调御，象调御、马调御、牛调御、人调御，于中二调御不可调御，二调御可调御。阿奇舍那！于意云何？若此二调御不可调御，此未调、未调地、未调御受御事者，终无是处。若此二调御可调御，善调御，此调、未调地，御受御事者，必有是处。如是，此阿奇舍那！止！王童子耆婆先那云何得？行欲著欲，为欲爱所食，为欲所烧，若地断欲、断欲爱、断欲烦热，无欲知、无欲见、无欲觉，此地王童子知者、见者，终无是处。所以者何？阿奇舍那！王童子耆婆先那常行欲也。

Achiśana! It is like a large rock mountain not far from a village, without gaps or holes, solid and not hollow, firm and immovable, all combined into one. Suppose there are two people who want to see it. One person quickly climbs the mountain, while the other stays below. The person on the mountain sees good flat ground, gardens, forests, springs, flower ponds, long streams, and rivers on the side of the mountain. Having seen this, the person on the mountain asks the person below, 'Do you see the good flat ground, gardens, forests, springs, flower ponds, long streams, and rivers on the side of the mountain?' The person below replies, 'If I see the mountain, there is no way I would see the good flat ground, gardens, forests, springs, flower ponds, long streams, and rivers on the side of the mountain.' Then the person on the mountain quickly comes down and grabs the person below, quickly taking them up to the top of the rock mountain. Having arrived, the person asks, 'Do you see the good flat ground, gardens, forests, springs, flower ponds, long streams, and rivers on the side of the mountain?' The person replies, 'Now I see it.' The person further asks, 'You said before that there is no way you would see it. Now you say you see it. What do you mean?' The person replies, 'Before, I could not see because I was obstructed by the mountain.' In this way, Achiśana! Stop! How can the prince Jibo Xianna know? He is attached to desire, consumed by desire, and burned by desire. If one cuts off desire, desire-love, and the burning of desire, and has no desire-knowledge, no desire-view, and no desire-perception, it is impossible for the prince to know or see this.

「阿奇舍那！犹去村不远，有大石山，无缺无穿，实而不虚，坚固不动，都合为一。或有二人正欲见者，彼中一人速疾上山，第二人者依住山下。石山上人见石山边有好平地、园观、林木、清泉、华池、长流、河水，山上人见已，语山下人：『汝见山边有好平地、园观、林木、清泉、华池、长流、河水耶？』山下人答曰：『若我见山，彼边有好平地、园观、林木、清泉、华池、长流、河水者，终无是处。』于是，石山上人疾疾来下，捉山下人速疾将上，于石山上，到已问曰：『汝见山边有好平地、园观、林木、清泉、华池、长流、河水耶？』彼人答曰：『今始见也。』复问彼人曰：『汝本言见者，终无是处。今复言见，为何谓耶？』彼人答曰：『我本为山之所障碍，故不见耳。』如是，阿奇舍那！止！王童子耆婆先那云何得？行欲著欲，为欲爱所食，为欲所烧，若地断欲、断欲爱、断欲烦热，无欲知、无欲见、无欲觉，此地王童子知者、见者，终无是处。

Achiśana! In the past, King Mahādeva had an elephant hunter. The king told him, 'Elephant hunter, for me, capture a wild elephant and bring it here. When you have captured it, inform me.' At that time, the elephant hunter received the king's order and rode the king's elephant into the wild forest. The elephant hunter saw a wild elephant in the wild forest, and having seen it, he caught it and tied it to the neck of the king's elephant. At that time, the king's elephant led the wild elephant out into the open. The elephant hunter then returned to King Mahādeva and said, 'Heavenly King! I have captured a wild elephant and tied it in the open. It is at your disposal.' Having heard this, King Mahādeva said, 'Good elephant trainer! You should now quickly tame this wild elephant, subdue it, and tame it well. When it is well tamed, return and inform me.' Thereupon, the good elephant trainer received the king's order, took a very large staff, placed it on his right shoulder, went to the wild elephant, placed the staff on the ground, tied the wild elephant's neck, restrained its desire for the wild, removed its thoughts of desire for the wild, stopped its fatigue from the wild, made it enjoy the village, and habituated it to love the human realm. The good elephant trainer first gave it food and drink.

「阿奇舍那！昔者刹利顶生王有捕象师，王告之曰：『汝捕象师，为我捕取野象将来，得已白我。』时，捕象师受王教已，即乘王象往野林中。彼捕象师在野林中见大野象，见已捉系，著王象项。彼时王象将野象出在于露地，彼捕象师还诣刹利顶生王所，白曰：『天王！已得野象，系在露地，随天王意。』刹利顶生王闻已告曰：『善调象师！汝今可速调此野象，伏令善调象，善调已，还来白我。』于是善调象师受王教已，持极大杖，著右肩上，往野象所，以杖著地，系野象项，制乐野意，除野欲念，止野疲劳，令乐村邑，习爱人间，善调象师先与饮食。

Achiśana! If the wild elephant first accepts food and drink from the elephant trainer, the good elephant trainer will think, 'This wild elephant will surely survive. Why is that? This great wild elephant first accepts food and drink.' If the wild elephant first accepts food and drink from the elephant trainer, the good elephant trainer will then speak gentle and pleasant words to it, regarding lying down, rising, going, coming, taking, letting go, bending, and stretching. If the wild elephant follows the good elephant trainer's gentle and pleasant words, regarding lying down, rising, going, coming, taking, letting go, bending, and stretching, then the wild elephant will follow the elephant trainer's instructions. Achiśana! If the wild elephant follows the elephant trainer's instructions, the good elephant trainer will then tie its two front legs, two hind legs, two thighs, two sides, tail, spine, head, forehead, ears, tusks, and nose, and have people hold hooks and stand on its head, with many people holding swords, shields, spears, halberds, axes, and battle-axes standing in front. The good elephant trainer will hold a sharp halberd in hand and say to the wild elephant, 'I will now discipline you, not allowing you to move. Discipline you, do not move.' If the wild elephant follows the elephant trainer's discipline and does not move, not lifting its front legs, not moving its hind legs, not moving its two thighs, two sides, tail, spine, head, forehead, ears, tusks, and nose, then the wild elephant will follow the elephant trainer's instructions and remain still.

「阿奇舍那！若彼野象从调象师初受饮食，善调象师便作是念：『今此野象必得生活。所以者何？此野大象初受饮食。』若彼野象从调象师初受饮食者，善调象师则以柔软可爱言向，卧起、去来、取舍、屈申。若彼野象从调象师，则以柔软可爱言向，卧起、去来、取舍、屈申者，如是野象随调象师教。阿奇舍那！若彼野象从调象师随受教者，善调象师则缚前两脚、后脚、两髀、两脇、尾脊、头额、耳、牙，及缚其鼻，使人捉钩，骑其头上，令众多人持刀、楯、矟、𫓴、戟、斧、钺而在前立，善调象师手执锋𫓴，在野象前而作是语：『我今治汝，令不移动，治汝勿动摇。』若彼野象从调象师治不移动时，不举前脚，亦不动后脚，两髀、两脇、尾脊、头额、耳、牙及鼻皆不动摇，如是野象随调象师住不移动。

Achiśana! If the wild elephant follows the elephant trainer's instructions and remains still, then at that time it will endure swords, shields, spears, halberds, axes, battle-axes, loud calls, and the blowing of conches, the beating of drums, and the striking of bells. If the wild elephant can endure, then at that time it will be disciplined, well disciplined, attain the highest discipline, attain the supreme discipline, the highest speed, the supreme speed, suitable for the king to ride, receive the king's provisions, and be praised as the king's elephant.

「阿奇舍那！若彼野象随调象师不移动者，彼于尔时忍刀、楯、矟、𫓴、戟、斧、钺、唤呼高声，若啸吹螺、击鼓、椎钟，皆能堪忍。若彼野象能堪忍者，彼于尔时调御、善调御，得上调御、得最上调御，上速疾、无上速疾，可中王乘，受食王廪，称说王象。

Thus it is, Achiśana! When a Tathāgata appears in the world, an Arhat, a fully Enlightened One, perfect in knowledge and conduct, a Sugata, an unsurpassed knower of the world, a supreme charioteer of men to be tamed, a teacher of gods and men, a Buddha, a Blessed One, he knows and sees for himself, and attains and dwells in, the supreme right self-awakening in this world with its gods, Māra, Brahmā, and the generation of recluses and brahmins, and among the classes of gods and men. He teaches the Dharma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing, revealing a holy life that is perfectly complete and purified. When the householder's son hears the Dharma taught by the Tathāgata, he gains faith in the Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma. Having gained faith, he shaves off his hair and beard, puts on the yellow robe, leaves home out of faith, and goes forth to practice the path. Achiśana! At that time, the noble disciple goes out into the open, like a wild elephant. Such a wild elephant delights in and enjoys being in the forest. Achiśana! Such gods and men delight in and enjoy the five sense pleasures: forms, sounds, smells, tastes, and tangibles. The Tathāgata first instructs those monks: 'You should guard the body and life, and guard the speech and mind and life, and be pure.'

「如是。阿奇舍那！若时如来出世，无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐。彼于此世，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志，从人至天，自知自觉，自作证成就游。彼说法初妙、中妙、竟亦妙，有义有文，具足清净，显现梵行。彼所说法，居士子闻，居士子闻已，得信如来所说法。彼得信已，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道。阿奇舍那！尔时圣弟子出在露地，犹王野象，如是野象贪欲乐著者，谓在林中。阿奇舍那！如是天及人贪欲乐著，谓在五欲，色、声、香、味、触。如来初始御彼比丘：『汝当护身及命清净，当护口、意及命清净。』

If the noble disciple guards the body and life and is pure, and guards the speech and mind and life and is pure, then the Tathāgata further instructs the monks: 'You should contemplate the internal body as body, and so on up to contemplating perception, mind, and dharmas as dharmas.' If the noble disciple contemplates the internal body as body, and so on up to contemplating perception, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, then these four foundations of mindfulness are in the mind of the noble disciple, binding his mind, restraining his desire for the household life, removing his thoughts of desire for the household life, stopping his weariness with the household life, causing him to delight in the true Dharma, and practicing the noble precepts. Achiśana! Just as the elephant trainer, having received King Kṣatriya's instructions, takes a large staff, puts it on his right shoulder, goes to the wild elephant, puts the staff on the ground, ties the wild elephant's neck, restrains its desire for the wilderness, removes its thoughts of desire for the wilderness, stops its weariness with the wilderness, causing it to delight in villages and towns, and practice loving the human world. In the same way, Achiśana! These four foundations of mindfulness are in the mind of the noble disciple, binding his mind, restraining his desire for the household life, removing his thoughts of desire for the household life, stopping his weariness with the household life, causing him to delight in the true Dharma, and practicing the noble precepts.

「若圣弟子护身及命清净，护口、意及命清净者，如来复调御比丘：『汝当观内身如身，乃至观觉、心、法如法。』若圣弟子观内身如身，乃至观觉、心、法如法者，此四念处，谓在贤圣弟子心中，系缚其心，制乐家意，除家欲念，止家疲劳，令乐正法，修习圣戒。阿奇舍那！犹调象师受刹利顶生王教已，持极大杖，著右肩上，往野象所，以杖著地，系野象颈，制乐野意，除野欲念，止野疲劳，令乐村邑，习爱人间。如是，阿奇舍那！此四念处，谓在贤圣弟子心中，系缚其心，制乐家意，除家欲念，止家疲劳，令乐正法，修习圣戒。

If the noble disciple contemplates the internal body as body, and so on up to contemplating perception, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, then the Tathāgata further instructs the monks: 'You should contemplate the internal body as body, and do not think thoughts associated with desire, and so on up to contemplating perception, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, and do not think thoughts associated with unwholesome dharmas.' If the noble disciple contemplates the internal body as body, and does not think thoughts associated with desire, and so on up to contemplating perception, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, and does not think thoughts associated with unwholesome dharmas, then such a noble disciple follows the Tathāgata's instructions. Achiśana! Just as a wild elephant, from the elephant trainer, then with soft and pleasant words, lying down, rising, going, coming, taking, putting down, bending, and stretching, such a wild elephant follows the elephant trainer's instructions. In the same way, Achiśana! If the noble disciple contemplates the internal body as body, and does not think thoughts associated with desire, and so on up to contemplating perception, mind, and dharmas as dharmas, and does not think thoughts associated with unwholesome dharmas, then such a noble disciple follows the Tathāgata's instructions.

「若圣弟子观内身如身，乃至观觉、心、法如法。彼如来复更调御比丘：『汝当观内身如身，莫念欲相应念，乃至观觉、心、法如法，莫念非法相应念。』若圣弟子观内身如身，不念欲相应念，乃至观觉、心、法如法，不念非法相应念者，如是圣弟子随如来教。阿奇舍那！犹如野象从调象师，则以柔软可爱言向，卧起、去来、取舍、屈伸者，如是野象随调象师教。如是，阿奇舍那！若圣弟子观内身如身，不念欲相应念，乃至观觉、心、法如法，不念非法相应念，如是圣弟子随如来教。

If the noble disciple follows the Tathāgata's instructions, then the Tathāgata further instructs the monks: 'You should be free from desire, free from evil and unwholesome dharmas, and attain the fourth dhyāna and abide in it.' If the noble disciple is free from desire, free from evil and unwholesome dharmas, attains the fourth dhyāna and abides in it, then such a noble disciple abides in accordance with the Tathāgata and does not move. Achiśana! Just as a wild elephant, when being tamed by the elephant trainer, does not lift its front foot, nor move its hind foot, nor move its two thighs, two sides, tail, back, head, forehead, ears, tusks, or trunk, such a wild elephant abides in accordance with the elephant trainer and does not move. In the same way, Achiśana! If the noble disciple is free from desire, free from evil and unwholesome dharmas, attains the fourth dhyāna and abides in it, then such a noble disciple abides in accordance with the Tathāgata and does not move.

「若圣弟子随如来教者，如来复更调御比丘：『汝当离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游。』若圣弟子离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游者，如是圣弟子则随如来住不移动。阿奇舍那！犹如野象从调象师治不移动时，不举前脚，亦不动后脚，两髀、两脇、尾脊、头额、耳、牙及鼻皆不动摇，如是野象随调象师住不移动。如是，阿奇舍那！若圣弟子离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游者，如是圣弟子则随如来住不移动。

If the noble disciple abides in accordance with the Tathāgata and does not move, then at that time he is able to endure hunger, thirst, cold, heat, mosquitoes, flies, wind, sun, harsh words, beatings, and pain in the body, even to the point of death. Achiśana! Just as a wild elephant, when abiding in accordance with the elephant trainer and not moving, is able to endure swords, shields, spears, halberds, axes, and high-pitched sounds, as well as the blowing of conches, the beating of drums, and the striking of bells, in the same way, Achiśana! If the noble disciple abides in accordance with the Tathāgata and does not move, then at that time he is able to endure hunger, thirst, cold, heat, mosquitoes, flies, wind, sun, harsh words, beatings, and pain in the body, even to the point of death.

「若圣弟子随如来住不移动者，彼于尔时则能堪忍饥渴、寒热、蚊虻、蝇蚤、风日所逼，恶声、捶杖亦能忍之，身遇诸疾极为苦痛，至命欲绝，诸不可乐皆能堪耐。阿奇舍那！犹如野象随调象师住不移动，彼于尔时忍刀、楯、矟、𫓴、戟、斧、钺、唤呼高声，若啸吹螺、击鼓、椎钟，皆能堪忍。如是，阿奇舍那！若圣弟子随如来住不移动者，彼于尔时则能堪忍饥渴、寒热、蚊虻、蝇蚤、风日所逼，恶声、捶杖亦能忍之，身遇诸疾极为苦痛，至命欲绝，诸不可乐皆能堪耐。

Achiśana! If the noble disciple is able to endure in accordance with the Tathāgata, then at that time he is well-tamed, excellently tamed, supremely tamed, supremely pacified, free from crookedness, evil, fear, ignorance, and flattery, pure, free from defilement, stainless, and without impurities. He is worthy to be called, invited, respected, honored, and truly worshipped, and is a good field of merit for all gods and humans. Achiśana! Just as a wild elephant, when able to endure, is well-tamed, excellently tamed, supremely tamed, supremely swift, worthy to ride for the king, to eat the king's grain, and to be praised as the king's elephant, in the same way, Achiśana! If the noble disciple is able to endure in accordance with the Tathāgata, then at that time he is well-tamed, excellently tamed, supremely tamed, supremely pacified, free from crookedness, evil, fear, ignorance, and flattery, pure, free from defilement, stainless, and without impurities. He is worthy to be called, invited, respected, honored, and truly worshipped, and is a good field of merit for all gods and humans.

「阿奇舍那！若圣弟子随如来能堪忍者，彼于尔时调御、善调御，得上调御、最上调御，得上息、最上息，除诸曲恶、恐怖、愚痴及谀谄，清净止尘，无垢无秽，可呼可请，可敬可重，实可供养，为一切天人良福田也。阿奇舍那！犹如野象能堪忍者，彼于尔时调御、善调御，得上调御、得最上调御，上速疾、无上速疾，可中王乘，受食王廪，称说王象。如是，阿奇舍那！若圣弟子随如来能堪忍者，彼于尔时调御、善调御，得上调御、最上调御，得上息，最上息，除诸曲恶、恐怖、愚痴及谀谄，清净止尘，无垢无秽，可呼可请，可敬可重，实可供养，为一切天人良福田也。

"Achiśana! A young wild elephant that dies undisciplined is said to die undisciplined, and a middle-aged or old wild elephant that dies undisciplined is also said to die undisciplined. Achiśana! A young noble disciple who dies undisciplined is said to die undisciplined, and a middle-aged or old noble disciple who dies undisciplined is also said to die undisciplined. Achiśana! A young wild elephant that dies well-disciplined is said to die well-disciplined, and a middle-aged or old wild elephant that dies well-disciplined is also said to die well-disciplined. Achiśana! A young noble disciple who dies well-disciplined is said to die well-disciplined, and a middle-aged or old noble disciple who dies well-disciplined is also said to die well-disciplined."

「阿奇舍那！少野象不调御死者，说不调御死，中、老野象不调御死者，说不调御死。阿奇舍那！少圣弟子不调御命终者，说不调御命终，中、老圣弟子不调御命终者，说不调御命终。阿奇舍那！少野象善调御死者，说善调御死，中、老野象善调死者，说善调御死。阿奇舍那！少圣弟子善调御命终者，说善调御命终，中、老圣弟子善调御命终者，说善调御命终。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The novice monk Aiyanaheqi and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。沙弥阿夷那和提及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
