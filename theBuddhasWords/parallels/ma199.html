MĀ 199

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 16 大品

199. （一九九）痴慧地经

Parallel to: MN 129

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "I will now explain to you the Dharma of foolishness and the Dharma of wisdom. Listen carefully, listen carefully, and consider it well." The bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今为汝说愚痴法、智慧法，谛听，谛听，善思念之。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "What is the Dharma of foolishness? A foolish person has three characteristics of foolishness, marks of foolishness, and appearances of foolishness, which are said to be accomplished by a foolish person. What are the three? A foolish person thinks evil thoughts, speaks evil words, and does evil deeds. Therefore, a foolish person is said to be foolish. If a foolish person does not think evil thoughts, does not speak evil words, and does not do evil deeds, then he should not be said to be foolish. Because a foolish person thinks evil thoughts, speaks evil words, and does evil deeds, therefore a foolish person is said to be foolish. That foolish person in this present life experiences three kinds of mental and physical suffering. How does a foolish person experience three kinds of mental and physical suffering? A foolish person, whether walking, gathering, sitting, in the streets, in the marketplace, or at a crossroads, speaks about things related to foolishness. A foolish person kills, takes what is not given, engages in sexual misconduct, lies, and has wrong views, as well as accomplishing other immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. If one accomplishes immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, others, having seen it, will speak of one's evil. That foolish person, having heard it, will think, 'If others, having seen that one accomplishes immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, speak of one's evil, then I also have these immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. If others know, they will also speak of my evil.' This is called the first kind of mental and physical suffering experienced by a foolish person in this present life.

佛言：「云何愚痴法？愚痴人有三相愚痴标、愚痴像，谓成就愚痴人说愚痴也。云何为三？愚痴人思恶思、说恶说、作恶作，是以愚痴人说愚痴也。若愚痴人不思恶思，不说恶说，不作恶作者，不应愚痴人说愚痴也。以愚痴人思恶思、说恶说、作恶作故，是以愚痴人说愚痴也。彼愚痴人于现法中，身心则受三种忧苦。云何愚痴人身心则受三种忧苦耶？愚痴人者，或有所行，或聚会坐，或在道巷，或在市中，或四衢头，说愚痴人相应事也。愚痴人者，杀生、不与取、行邪婬、妄言，乃至邪见，及成就余无量恶不善之法。若成就无量恶不善法者，他人见已，便说其恶。彼愚痴人闻已，便作是念：『若成就无量恶不善之法，他人见已，说其恶者，我亦有是无量恶不善之法，若他知者，亦当说我恶。』是谓愚痴人于现法中，身心则受第一忧苦。

Furthermore, that foolish person sees the king's men arrest criminals and torture them in various ways, such as cutting off their hands, feet, both hands and feet, ears, noses, both ears and noses, or cutting them into pieces, pulling out their beards and hair, or pulling out their beards and hair, or putting them in a cage and burning them with fire-wrapped cloth, or burning them with grass-wrapped sand, or putting them in an iron donkey's belly, or putting them in an iron pig's mouth, or burning them in an iron tiger's mouth, or putting them in an iron cauldron, or boiling them in an iron pot, or cutting them into pieces, or piercing them with sharp spears, or hooking them with hooks, or burning them with boiling oil while lying on an iron bed, or pounding them with an iron pestle while sitting in an iron mortar, or biting them with poisonous dragons, or whipping them with whips, or beating them with sticks, or hitting them with clubs, or impaling them alive, or beheading them. Having seen this, that foolish person thinks, 'If one who accomplishes immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas is caught by the king and tortured like this, then I also have these immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. If the king knows, he will also torture me.' Thus, this is called the second kind of mental and physical suffering experienced by a foolish person in this present life.

「复次，彼愚痴人又见王人收捉罪人，种种苦治，谓截手、截足，并截手足，截耳、截鼻，并截耳鼻，或脔脔割，拔须、拔发，或拔须发，或著槛中衣裹火烧，或以沙壅草缠火𦶟，或内铁驴腹中，或著铁猪口中，或置铁虎口中烧，或安铜釜中，或著铁釜中煑，或段段截，或利叉刺，或以钩钩，或卧铁床以沸油浇，或坐铁臼以铁杵捣，或毒龙蜇，或以鞭鞭，或以杖挝，或以棒打，或活贯标头，或枭其首。彼愚痴人见已，便作是念：『若成就无量恶不善法者，王知捉已，如是考治，我亦有是无量恶不善之法，若王知者，亦当苦治考我。』如是，是谓愚痴人于现法中，身心则受第二忧苦。

Furthermore, that foolish person practices evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. When he is sick and suffering, whether sitting or lying on a bed, a couch, or the ground, his body experiences extreme pain and suffering, and his life is about to end. All his evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions then hang above him, like the shadow of the setting sun on a high mountain hanging down on the ground. Thus, all his evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions hang above him. He thinks, 'This is my evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions hanging above me. I did not do good deeds in the past, but did many evil things. If there is a place for those who do evil, who are violent and do unreasonable things, who do not do good deeds, who do not do good, who do not do what is frightening, who are the refuge and the protector, I will go to that evil place.' From this arises regret. Having regret, he dies unvirtuous and passes away unskillfully. This is called the third kind of mental and physical suffering experienced by a foolish person in this present life.

「复次，彼愚痴人行身恶行，行口] cf.［丽】、意恶行，彼若时疾病受苦，或坐卧床，或坐卧榻，或坐卧地，身生极苦甚苦，乃至命欲断。彼所有身恶行，口、意恶行，彼于尔时悬向在上，犹如晡时，日下高山，影悬向在地，如是彼所有身恶行，口、意恶行，彼于尔时悬向在上。彼作是念：『此是我身恶行，口、意恶行，悬向在上。我于本时不作福、多作恶，若有处作恶者，凶暴作无理事，不作福、不作善、不作恐怖，所归命、所依怙，我至彼恶处。』从是生悔，生悔已，不贤死，不善命终。是谓愚痴人于现法中，身心则受第三忧苦。

"Furthermore, that foolish person practices evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Having practiced evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when the body perishes and life ends, he will definitely go to an evil place and be born in hell. Having been born there, he will experience the retribution of suffering, which is entirely unlovable, unenjoyable, and undesirable. If it is said that 'what is entirely unlovable, unenjoyable, and undesirable is said to be hell. Why is that? Because hell is entirely unlovable, unenjoyable, and undesirable.'"

「复次，彼愚痴人行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，必至恶处，生地狱中。既生彼已，受于苦报，一向不可爱、不可乐、意不可念，若作是说：『一向不可爱、不可乐、意不可念者，是说地狱。所以者何？彼地狱者，一向不可爱、不可乐、意不可念。』」

At that time, a certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms towards the Buddha, and said, "World-Honored One! What is the suffering of hell like?"

尔时，有一比丘即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！地狱苦云何？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikṣu! The suffering of hell is indescribable. Bhikṣu! The suffering of hell is only suffering.

世尊答曰：「比丘！地狱不可尽说，所谓地狱苦。比丘！但地狱苦唯有苦。」

The bhikṣu asked again, "World-Honored One! Can you illustrate the meaning with a parable?"

比丘复问曰：「世尊！可得以喻现其义也。」

The World-Honored One replied, "It can be illustrated with a parable. Bhikṣu! It is like a king's men capturing a thief and bringing him before King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa, saying, 'Your Majesty! This criminal is guilty. Please punish him, Your Majesty!' King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa orders, 'Take him away and punish him. Pierce him with a hundred spears in the morning.' The king's men receive the order and take him away to be punished. They pierce him with a hundred spears in the morning, but he still lives. King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa asks, 'How is he?' The king's men reply, 'Your Majesty! He is still alive.' King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa then orders, 'Take him away! Pierce him with a hundred spears at noon.' The king's men receive the order and pierce him with a hundred spears at noon, but he still lives. King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa then asks again, 'How is he?' The king's men reply, 'Your Majesty! He is still alive.' King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa then orders, 'Take him away! Pierce him with a hundred spears in the evening.' The king's men receive the order and pierce him with a hundred spears in the evening, but he still lives. His body is completely pierced, broken, and destroyed, with no part intact, like a coin hole. King Kṣatriya Uṣṇīṣa then asks again, 'How is he?' The king's men reply, 'Your Majesty! He is still alive. His body is completely pierced, broken, and destroyed, with no part intact, like a coin hole.' Bhikṣu! What do you think? If that man is pierced with three hundred spears in a day, would he suffer mental and physical torment and extreme pain because of that?"

世尊答曰：「亦可以喻现其义也。比丘！犹如王人收贼，送诣刹利顶生王所，白曰：『天王！此贼人有罪，愿天王治。』刹利顶生王告曰：『汝等将去治此人罪，朝以百矛刺。』王人受教，便将去治，朝以百矛刺，彼人故活。刹利顶生王问曰：『彼人云何？』王人答曰：『天王！彼人故活。』刹利顶生王复告曰：『汝等去！日中复以百矛刺。』王人受教，日中复以百矛刺，彼人故活。刹利顶生王复问曰：『彼人云何？』王人答曰：『天王！彼人故活。』刹利顶生王复告曰：『汝等去！日西复以百矛刺。』王人受教，日西复以百矛刺，彼人故活，然彼人身一切穿决破碎坏尽，无一处完，至如钱孔。刹利顶生王复问曰：『彼人云何？』王人答曰：『天王！彼人故活，然彼身一切穿决破碎坏尽，无一处完，至如钱孔。』比丘！于意云何？若彼人一日被三百矛刺，彼人因是身心受恼极忧苦耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "World-Honored One! Even being pierced by one spear would still cause extreme suffering. How much more so being pierced with three hundred spears in a day? Would that person not suffer mental and physical torment and extreme pain?"

比丘答曰：「世尊！被一矛刺，尚受极苦，况复一日受三百矛刺，彼人身心岂不受恼极忧苦也？」

Then the World-Honored One took a small stone, the size of a bean, and said, "Bhikṣu! Do you see me taking this small stone?"

于是，世尊手取石子，犹如小豆，告曰：「比丘！汝见我手取此石子，如小豆耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "I see it, World-Honored One!"

比丘答曰：「见也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One further asked, "Bhikṣu! What do you think? Compared to the snow mountain king, which is larger, my taking a small stone the size of a bean?"

世尊复问曰：「比丘！于意云何？我取石子，犹如小豆，比雪山王，何者为大？」

The bhikṣu replied, "World-Honored One! Compared to the snow mountain king, the small stone taken by the World-Honored One's hand is a hundred times, a thousand times, a hundred thousand times, and cannot be compared, cannot be counted, cannot be calculated, cannot be compared, and cannot be compared. Only the snow mountain king is extremely large and very large."

比丘答曰：「世尊手取石子，犹如小豆，比雪山王，百倍、千倍、百千万倍，终不相及，不可数、不可算，不可譬喻、不可比方，但雪山王极大甚大。」

The World-Honored One said, "Bhikṣu! If I take a small stone the size of a bean and compare it to the snow mountain king, it is a hundred times, a thousand times, a hundred thousand times, and cannot be compared, cannot be counted, cannot be calculated, cannot be compared, and cannot be compared. Only the snow mountain king is extremely large and very large. In this way, bhikṣu! If this person is pierced by three hundred spears in a day, due to this cause and condition, the mental and physical suffering and extreme sorrow and pain are a hundred times, a thousand times, a hundred thousand times, and cannot be compared, cannot be counted, cannot be calculated, cannot be compared, and cannot be compared to the suffering in hell. Only the suffering in hell is extremely painful and very painful.

世尊告曰：「比丘！若我取石子，犹如小豆，比雪山王，百倍、千倍、百千万倍，终不相及，不可数、不可算，不可譬喻、不可比方，但雪山王极大甚大。如是，比丘！若此人一日被三百矛刺，彼因缘此，身心受恼极重忧苦，比地狱苦，百倍、千倍、百千万倍，终不相及，不可数、不可算，不可譬喻、不可比方，但地狱中极苦甚苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron axes to cut and shape their bodies, making them into octagons, hexagons, squares, or circles, either high or low, good or bad. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁斧洞然俱炽，斫治其身，或作八楞，或为六楞，或为四方，或令团圆，或高或下，或好或恶。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron axes to cut and shape their bodies, making them into octagons, hexagons, squares, or circles, either high or low, good or bad. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁𬬱洞然俱炽，斫治其身，或作八楞，或为六楞，或为四方，或令团圆，或高或下，或好或恶。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron spears to force them to sit on them. Then they use iron tongs to pry open their mouths and put blazing iron balls into their mouths, burning their lips, tongues, gums, throats, hearts, and stomachs, coming out from their bodies. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁枪洞然俱炽，强令坐上，便以铁钳钳开其口，则以铁丸洞然俱炽，著其口中，烧唇烧舌，烧龂烧咽，烧心烧胃，从身下出。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron spikes to force them to sit on them. Then they use iron tongs to pry open their mouths and pour molten copper into their mouths, burning their lips, tongues, gums, throats, hearts, and stomachs, coming out from their bodies. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁锵洞然俱炽，令强坐上，便以铁钳钳开其口，则以融铜灌其口中，烧唇烧舌，烧龂烧咽，烧心烧胃，从身下出。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron ground to force them to lie face up, tie them with five ropes, and nail their two hands and two feet with iron nails, and nail their abdomen with a separate iron nail. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁地洞然俱炽，令仰向卧，挓五缚治，两手两足以铁钉钉，以一铁钉别钉其腹。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron ground to force them to lie face down, pull out their tongues from their mouths, and nail them with a hundred nails without wrinkles or shrinkage, like a cowhide nailed with a hundred nails without wrinkles or shrinkage. In this way, when sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them and use blazing iron ground to force them to lie face down, pull out their tongues from their mouths, and nail them with a hundred nails without wrinkles or shrinkage. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁地洞然俱炽，令其伏地，从口出舌，以百钉张无皱无缩，犹如牛皮以百钉张无皱无缩。如是众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，则以铁地洞然俱炽，令其伏地，从口出舌，以百钉张无皱无缩。彼如是考治者苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers seize them by the scalp and peel it down to the feet, and peel the skin from the feet up to the head. They then use a blazing iron cart to bind them to the cart, and drag them back and forth on the blazing iron ground. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒以手捉其头皮剥下至足，从足剥皮上至其头，则以铁车洞然俱炽，以缚著车，便于铁地洞然俱炽，牵挽往来。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers use blazing fire to make them roll on the ground, and then make them pick it up with their hands and pour it over their own bodies. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒以火洞然俱炽，使扬扑地，复使手取，自灌其身。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers use a blazing volcano to make them go up and down. When they put their feet down, their skin, flesh, and blood are immediately burned away. When they lift their feet, their skin, flesh, and blood grow back as before. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒以火山洞然俱炽，令其上下，彼若下足，其皮肉血即便烧尽，若举足时，其皮肉血还生如故。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? When sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers grab them with their hands and put them upside down in a large iron cauldron that is blazing and burning. They are cooked in it, either floating up or down, or reaching the sides. Their own body produces foam, which then cooks their body. It is like putting soybeans, small beans, mung beans, bitter beans, or mustard seeds into a cauldron full of water, and then lighting a fire underneath. Those beans will either float up or down, or reach the sides, and their own foam will wrap around and cook them. In the same way, when sentient beings are born in hell, after being born there, the jailers grab them with their hands and put them upside down in a large iron cauldron that is blazing and burning. They are cooked in it, either floating up or down, or reaching the sides, and their own body produces foam, which then cooks their body. They are tortured in this way, suffering for many years, even hundreds of thousands of years, experiencing immeasurable suffering, extremely heavy and severe, but they cannot die. They must endure until their evil and unwholesome karma is exhausted. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，以大铁釜洞然俱炽，倒举其身，足上头下，以著釜中，彼于其中，或上或下，或至方维，自体沫出，还煑其身，犹如大豆、小豆、蕴豆、苦豆、芥子著多水釜中，下极然火，彼豆于中，或上或下，或至方维，自沫缠煮。如是众生生地狱中，既生彼已，狱卒手捉，以大铁釜洞然俱炽，倒举其身，足上头下，以著釜中，彼于其中，或上或下，或至方维，自体沫出，还煮其身。彼如是考治苦痛逼迫，岁数甚多，乃至百千，受无量苦，极重甚苦，终不得死，要当至恶不善业尽，是谓地狱苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering in hell? There is a hell in hell called 'Six More Pleasures'. When sentient beings are born there, after being born there, if their eyes see forms, they are not pleased and cannot be pleased, their minds are not moistened and loved, and cannot be moistened and loved, their minds are not well pleased, and cannot be well pleased. The sounds heard by the ears, the smells smelled by the nose, the tastes tasted by the tongue, the tangibles felt by the body, and the dharmas cognized by the mind are not pleased and cannot be pleased, their minds are not moistened and loved, and cannot be moistened and loved, their minds are not well pleased, and cannot be well pleased. This is called the suffering in hell.

「比丘！云何地狱苦？彼地狱中有狱，名六更乐，若众生生彼中，既生彼已，若眼见色，不憙不可，非是憙可，意不润爱，非是润爱，意不善乐，非是善乐，耳所闻声、鼻所齅香、舌所尝味、身所觉触、意所知法，不憙不可，非是喜可，意不润爱，非是润爱，意不善乐，非是善乐，是谓地狱苦。

"Bhikṣu! I have explained the hells and the affairs of the hells to you in many ways, but the suffering in hell cannot be fully explained. There is only suffering in hell. Bhikṣu! If a foolish person sometimes escapes from hell and is reborn as an animal, the suffering of animals is also great."

「比丘！我为汝等无量方便说彼地狱，说地狱事，然此地狱苦不可具说，但地狱唯有苦。比丘！若愚痴人或时从地狱出，生畜生者，畜生亦甚苦。」

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering of animals? If sentient beings are born among animals, they are born in darkness, grow in darkness, and die in darkness. What are they? They are worms born in the earth. Foolish people, because of their attachment to the taste of food in the past, engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among animals, born in darkness, growing in darkness, and dying in darkness. This is called the suffering of animals.

「比丘！云何畜生苦？若众生生畜生中，谓彼暗冥中生，暗冥中长，暗冥中死，彼为云何？谓地生虫。愚痴人者，以本时贪著食味，行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，生畜生中，谓暗冥中生，暗冥中长，暗冥中死，是谓畜生苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering of animals? If sentient beings are born among animals, they are born in the body, grow in the body, and die in the body. What are they? They are worms named 'wound worms'. Foolish people, because of their attachment to the taste of food in the past, engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among animals, born in the body, growing in the body, and dying in the body. This is called the suffering of animals.

「比丘！云何畜生苦？若众生生畜生中，谓身中生，身中长，身中死。彼为云何？谓名疮虫。愚痴人者，以本时贪著食味，行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，生畜生中，谓身中生，身中长，身中死，是谓畜生苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering of animals? If sentient beings are born among animals, they are born in the water, grow in the water, and die in the water. What are they? They are fish, makaras, turtles, alligators, varāha, tibhi, tibhagara, and tibhagara. Foolish people, because of their attachment to the taste of food in the past, engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among animals, born in the water, growing in the water, and dying in the water. This is called the suffering of animals.

「比丘！云何畜生苦？若众生生畜生中，谓水中生，水中长，水中死。彼为云何？谓鱼、摩竭鱼、龟、鼍、婆留尼、提鼻、提鼻伽罗、提鼻伽罗。愚痴人者，以本时贪著食味，行身恶行，行口、意恶行。彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，生畜生中，谓水中生，水中长，水中死，是谓畜生苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering of animals? If sentient beings are born among animals, they are born as those who eat grass and trees with their teeth. What are they? They are elephants, horses, camels, cows, donkeys, deer, buffaloes, and pigs. Foolish people, because of their attachment to the taste of food in the past, engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among animals, born as those who eat grass and trees with their teeth. This is called the suffering of animals.

「比丘！云何畜生苦？若众生生畜生中，谓齿啮者生草树木食。彼为云何？谓象、马、骆驼、牛、驴、鹿、水牛及猪。愚痴人者，以本时贪著食味，行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，生畜生中，谓齿啮生草树木食，是谓畜生苦。

Bhikṣu! What is the suffering of animals? If sentient beings are born among animals, they are born as those who run towards the smell of human feces and urine and eat it, just as men and women run towards the smell of food and drink. Thus, it is said that they eat what they eat. Thus, bhikṣu! If sentient beings are born among animals, they are born as those who run towards the smell of human feces and urine and eat it. What are they? They are chickens, pigs, dogs, jackals, crows, kaurava, and kaurava. Foolish people, because of their attachment to the taste of food in the past, engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when their bodies perish and their lives end, they are reborn among animals, born as those who eat feces and impurities. This is called the suffering of animals.

「比丘！云何畜生苦？若众生生畜生中，谓彼闻人大小便气，即走往趣彼，食彼食，犹如男女闻饮食香，即便往趣彼，如是说彼食彼食。如是，比丘！若众生生畜生中，谓彼闻人大小便气，即走往趣彼，食彼食。彼为云何？谓鸡、猪、狗、犲、乌、拘楼罗、拘棱迦。愚痴人者，以本时贪著食味，行身恶行，行口、意恶行。彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，生畜生中，谓食屎不净，是谓畜生苦。

Bhikṣu! I have explained the animals to you in countless ways, and the affairs of animals, but the suffering of animals cannot be fully explained. Animals only have suffering.

「比丘！我为汝等无量方便说彼畜生，说畜生事，然此畜生苦不可具说，但畜生唯有苦。

"Bhikṣu! It is extremely difficult for a foolish person to be reborn as a human after leaving the animal realm. Why is that? Because in the animal realm, they do not practice benevolence and righteousness, do not practice propriety and ritual, and do not practice the sublime and good. Animals eat each other, the strong eat the weak, and the large eat the small. Bhikṣu! Suppose this land were filled with water, and there was a blind turtle with a lifespan of countless hundreds of thousands of years. There was a small, light wooden board with only one hole, blown by the wind. Bhikṣu! What do you think? Would the blind turtle's head be able to enter this small, light wooden board through the one hole?"

「比丘！若愚痴人从畜生出，还生为人，极大甚难。所以者何？彼畜生中不行仁义，不行礼法，不行妙善，彼畜生者更相食噉，强者食弱，大者食小。比丘！犹如此地，满其中水，有一瞎龟，寿命无量百千之岁，彼水上有小轻木板，唯有一孔，为风所吹。比丘！于意云何？彼瞎龟头宁得入此小轻木板一孔中耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "World-Honored One! It may be possible to enter, but it would be very difficult after a long time."

比丘答曰：「世尊！或可得入，但久久甚难。」

The World-Honored One said, "Bhikṣu! Suppose the blind turtle, after a hundred years, comes from the east and raises its head once. The small wooden board with only one hole is blown by the east wind and moves to the south. Suppose the blind turtle, after a hundred years, comes from the south and raises its head once. The wooden board with one hole is blown by the south wind and moves to the west. Suppose the blind turtle, after a hundred years, comes from the west and raises its head once. The wooden board with one hole is blown by the west wind and moves to the north. Suppose the blind turtle comes from the north and raises its head once. The wooden board with one hole is blown by the north wind and moves to various directions. Bhikṣu! What do you think? Would the blind turtle's head be able to enter this wooden board with one hole?"

世尊告曰：「比丘！或时瞎龟过百年已，从东方来而一举头，彼小木板唯有一孔，为东风吹移至南方。或时瞎龟过百年已，从南方来而一举头，彼一孔板为南风吹移至西方。或时瞎龟过百年已，从西方来而一举头，彼一孔板为西风吹移至北方。或时瞎龟从北方来而一举头，彼一孔板为北风吹随至诸方。比丘！于意云何？彼瞎龟头宁得入此一孔板耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "World-Honored One! It may be possible to enter, but it would be very difficult after a long time."

比丘答曰：「世尊！或可得入，但久久甚难。」

Bhikṣu! It is like this. It is also very difficult for that foolish person to be reborn as a human after being born as an animal. Why is that? Because in the animal realm, they do not practice benevolence and righteousness, they do not practice propriety and ritual, they do not practice the sublime and good. Those animals eat each other, the strong eat the weak, the large eat the small. Bhikṣu! If that foolish person is sometimes reborn as a human after being born as an animal, if they have a family, it will be of low status, inferior, poor, and lacking in food and drink. They will think it very difficult to obtain food. What do they mean? They mean the families of jailers, craftsmen, skilled workers, potters, and other such inferior families, which are poor, lacking in food and drink, and think it very difficult to obtain food. Being born into such a family, once born there, they may be blind, lame, with short arms and elbows, or with a hunched back, or using the left hand, with a sheep-like face, ugly, short-lived, and enslaved by others. They engage in evil bodily actions, evil verbal and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily actions, evil verbal and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when their body breaks up and their life ends, they go to a bad place, are born in hell.

「比丘！如是彼愚痴人从畜生出，还生为人，亦复甚难。所以者何？彼畜生中不行仁义，不行礼法，不行妙善，彼畜生者更相食噉，强者食弱，大者食小。比丘！若愚痴人或时从畜生出，还生为人，彼若有家，小姓下贱，弊恶贫穷，少有饮食，谓得食甚难。彼为云何？谓狱卒家、工师家、巧手家、陶师家，如是比余下贱家，弊恶贫穷，少有饮食，谓得食甚难。生如是家，既生彼已，或瞎或跛，或臂肘短，或身伛曲，或用左手，恶色羊面，丑陋短寿，为他所使，彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行。彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，还至恶处，生地狱中。

"It is like two people playing a game. One of them starts to take such actions, and then loses his slaves and wife, and also takes his own body to hang upside down in a smoky house. He thinks, 'I will not eat or drink, but I will start to take such actions.' Then he loses his slaves and wife, and also takes his own body to hang upside down in a smoky house. Bhikṣu! This action is very little, losing slaves and wife, and also taking one's own body to hang upside down in a smoky house. Bhikṣu! It is said that this action is to be practiced, to engage in evil bodily actions, evil verbal and mental actions. Having engaged in evil bodily actions, evil verbal and mental actions, due to this and on account of this, when the body breaks up and life ends, one goes to a bad place, is born in hell. Bhikṣu! These actions are the most unlovable, truly unpleasant, and not what is intended. Bhikṣu! Is it not to fully explain the foolish Dharma?"

「犹如二人而共博戏，彼有一人始取如是行，便失奴婢及失妻子，复取己身倒悬烟屋中，彼作是念：『我不食不饮，然我始取如是行。』便失奴婢及失妻子，复取己身倒悬烟屋中。比丘！此行甚少，失奴婢、失妻子，复取己身倒悬烟屋中。比丘！谓此行所可行，行身恶行，行口、意恶行。彼行身恶行，行口、意恶行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，还至恶处，生地狱中。比丘！此诸行最不可爱，实不可乐，非意所念。比丘！非为具足说愚痴法耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! It is to fully explain the foolish Dharma."

比丘答曰：「唯然。世尊！为具足说愚痴法也。」

The World-Honored One said, "What is the Dharma of wisdom? The wise person has three characteristics, marks, and appearances of wisdom, which are said to be accomplished by the wise person. What are the three? The wise person thinks good thoughts, speaks good words, and does good deeds, and therefore the wise person speaks of wisdom. If the wise person does not think good thoughts, speak good words, or do good deeds, it is not appropriate for the wise person to speak of wisdom. Because the wise person thinks good thoughts, speaks good words, and does good deeds, the wise person speaks of wisdom. The wise person, in this present life, experiences three kinds of joy and happiness in body and mind. How does the wise person experience three kinds of joy and happiness in body and mind in this present life? The wise person, whether walking, gathering, sitting, on the road, in the market, or at the crossroads, speaks of things that accord with the wise person. The wise person abandons killing, abstains from killing, does not take what is not given, does not engage in sexual misconduct, does not speak falsehoods, and so on, up to abandoning wrong views and attaining right views, and accomplishing other immeasurable good dharmas. If one accomplishes immeasurable good dharmas, others, having seen them, will praise them. The wise person, having heard this, will think, 'If others, having seen those who accomplish immeasurable good dharmas, praise them, I also have these immeasurable good dharmas. If others know, they will also praise me.' This is called the first joy and happiness experienced by the wise person in body and mind in this present life.

世尊告曰：「云何智慧法？彼智慧人有三相智慧标智慧像，谓成就智慧人说智慧也。云何为三？智慧人者，思善思、说善说、作善作，是以智慧人说智慧也。若智慧人不思善思，不说善说，不作善作者，不应智慧人说智慧也。以智慧人思善思、说善说、作善作故，是智慧人说智慧也。智慧人者，于现法中，身心则受三种喜乐。云何智慧人于现法中身心则受三种喜乐也？智慧人者，或有所行，或聚会坐，或在道巷，或在市中，或四衢头，说智慧人相应事也。智慧人者，断杀、离杀、不与取、邪婬、妄言，乃至断邪见得正见，及成就余无量善法。若成就无量善法者，他人见已，便称誉之。彼智慧人闻已，便作是念：『若成就无量善法，他人见已称誉者，我亦有是无量善法，若他知者，亦当称誉我。』是谓智慧人于现法中，身心则受第一喜乐。

Furthermore, the wise person sees various ways that the king punishes thieves, such as cutting off hands, feet, both hands and feet, ears, noses, both ears and noses, or cutting them up, pulling out beards and hair, or pulling out beards and hair, putting them in cages, wrapping them in cloth and burning them, or burying them in sand and burning them with grass, putting them in the belly of an iron donkey, or in the mouth of an iron pig, or in the mouth of an iron tiger, burning them, or putting them in a copper cauldron, or in an iron cauldron to boil them, or cutting them into pieces, or piercing them with sharp spears, or hooking them with hooks, or lying them on an iron bed and pouring boiling oil on them, or sitting them in an iron mortar and pounding them with an iron pestle, or having them bitten by poisonous dragons, or whipping them with whips, or beating them with sticks, or hitting them with clubs, or impaling them alive, or beheading them. Having seen this, the wise person thinks, 'If one accomplishes immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, the king, having known, will punish them in this way. I do not have these immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. If the king knows, he will not punish me in this way.' This is called the second joy and happiness experienced by the wise person in body and mind in this present life.

「复次，彼智慧人又见王人种种治贼，谓截手、截足，并截手足，截耳、截鼻，并截耳鼻，或脔脔割，拔须、拔发，或拔须发，或著槛中衣裹火烧，或以沙壅草缠火𦶟，或内铁驴腹中，或著铁猪口中，或置铁虎口中烧，或安铜釜中，或著铁釜中煑，或段段截，或利叉刺，或以钩钩，或卧铁床以沸油浇，或坐铁臼以铁杵捣，或毒龙蜇，或以鞭鞭，或以杖挝，或以棒打，或活贯摽头，或枭其首。彼智慧人见已，便作是念：『若成就无量恶不善法者，王知捉已，如是考治。我无是无量恶不善之法，若王知者，终不如是苦治于我。』是谓智慧人于现法中，身心则受第二喜乐。

Furthermore, the wise person practices good conduct with the body, speech, and mind. If they become ill, whether lying on a bed, couch, or the ground, or experiencing extreme pain and suffering in the body, even to the point of death, their good conduct with the body, speech, and mind will then be directed upwards, like the shadow of the sun on a high mountain in the evening, cast downwards on the ground. In the same way, their good conduct with the body, speech, and mind will then be directed upwards. They think, 'This is my good conduct with the body, speech, and mind, directed upwards. I did not do evil in the past, but did much good. If there is a place where one does not do evil, is not violent, does not do unreasonable things, does good, does what is wholesome and fearful, where one takes refuge and finds support, I will go to that good place. Without regret, I will die well and pass away.' This is called the third joy and happiness experienced by the wise person in body and mind in this present life.

「复次，彼智慧人行身妙行，行口、意妙行，彼若时疾病，或坐卧床，或坐卧榻，或坐卧地，或身生极苦甚重苦，乃至命欲断。彼所有身妙行，口、意妙行，彼于尔时悬向在上，犹如晡时，日下高山，影悬向在地，如是彼所有身妙行，口、意妙行，彼于尔时悬向在上。彼作是念：『此是我身妙行，口、意妙行，悬向在上，我于本时不作恶、多作福。若有处不作恶者，不凶暴，不作无理事，作福、作善、作恐怖，所归命、所依怙，我至彼善处。而不生悔，不生悔已，贤死善命终。』是谓智慧人于现法中，身心则受第三憙乐。

"Furthermore, the wise person practices good conduct with the body, speech, and mind. Having practiced good conduct with the body, speech, and mind, they will, on account of this and because of this, when their body breaks up and their life ends, definitely ascend to a good place and be reborn in heaven. Having been reborn there, they will experience pleasant results, which are entirely lovable, entirely enjoyable, and agreeable to the mind. If they think, 'What is entirely lovable, entirely enjoyable, and agreeable to the mind is called a good place. Why is that? Because that good place is entirely lovable, entirely enjoyable, and agreeable to the mind,'"

「复次，彼智慧人行身妙行，行口、意妙行，彼行身妙行，行口、意妙行已，因此缘此，身坏命终，必升善处，上生天中。既生彼已，受于乐报，一向可爱、一向可乐而意可念，若作是念：『一向可爱、一向可乐而意可念者，是说善处。所以者何？彼善处者，一向可爱，一向可乐而意可念。』」

At that time, a certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms towards the Buddha, and said, "World-Honored One! What is the happiness of a good place like?"

尔时，有一比丘即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！善处乐云何？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikṣu! The happiness of a good place cannot be fully described. The happiness of a good place is simply the happiness of a good place."

世尊答曰：「比丘！善处不可尽说，所谓善处乐，但善处唯有乐。」

The bhikṣu further asked, "World-Honored One! Can you illustrate the meaning with a parable?"

比丘复问曰：「世尊！可得以喻现其义耶？」

The World-Honored One replied, "It can also be illustrated with a parable. It is like a wheel-turning king who possesses the seven treasures and the four kinds of human supernormal powers. Bhikṣu! What do you think? Does that wheel-turning king, possessing the seven treasures and the four kinds of human supernormal powers, experience supreme joy and happiness in body and mind because of that?"

世尊答曰：「亦可以喻现其义也。犹如转轮王成就七宝、四种人如意足。比丘！于意云何？彼转轮王成就七宝、四种人如意足，彼因是身心受极喜乐耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "World-Honored One! If one possesses even one treasure and one kind of human supernormal power, one still experiences supreme joy and happiness, let alone a wheel-turning king who possesses the seven treasures and the four kinds of human supernormal powers. Would one not experience supreme joy and happiness?"

比丘答曰：「世尊！成就一宝、一人如意足，尚受极喜乐，况复转轮王成就七宝、四种人如意足，非为受极喜乐耶？」

Then the World-Honored One took a small stone, about the size of a small bean, and said, "Bhikṣu! Do you see me taking this small stone?"

于是，世尊手取石子，犹如小豆，告曰：「比丘！汝见我手取此石子如小豆耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "I see it, World-Honored One!"

比丘答曰：「见也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One asked again, "Bhikṣu! What do you think? Compared to the snow mountain king, which is larger, my taking a small stone the size of a small bean?"

世尊复问曰：「比丘！于意云何？我取石子，犹如小豆，比雪山王，何者为大？」

The bhikṣu replied, "World-Honored One! Only the snow mountain king is extremely large and very large."

比丘答曰：「世尊手取石子，犹如小豆，比雪山王，百倍、千倍、百千万倍，终不相及，不可数、不可算，不可譬喻、不可比方，但雪山王极大甚大。」

If I take a small stone the size of a small bean and compare it to the snow mountain king, it is a hundred times, a thousand times, a hundred thousand times, and cannot be compared, cannot be counted, cannot be calculated, cannot be compared, and cannot be compared. Only the snow mountain king is extremely large and very large. In the same way, bhikṣu! If a wheel-turning king possesses the seven treasures and the four kinds of human supernormal powers, the joy and happiness experienced by that person's body and mind, compared to the happiness of the gods, is a hundred times, a thousand times, a hundred thousand times, and cannot be compared, cannot be counted, cannot be calculated, cannot be compared, and cannot be compared. The bhikṣu said, "Yes, World-Honored One! Please explain in full the Dharma of a wise person."

比丘白曰：「唯然。世尊！为具足说智慧人法。」

The World-Honored One said, "This is called the Dharma of a foolish person and the Dharma of a wise person. You should know the Dharma of a foolish person and the Dharma of a wise person. Having known the Dharma of a foolish person and the Dharma of a wise person, abandon the Dharma of a foolish person and take the Dharma of a wise person. You should train like this."

世尊告曰：「是谓愚痴人法、智慧人法，汝等应当知愚痴人法、智慧人法，知愚痴人法、智慧人法已，舍愚痴人法，取智慧人法，当如是学。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
