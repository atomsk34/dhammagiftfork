MĀ 200

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 16 大品

200. （二〇〇）阿梨咤经

Parallel to: MN 22 / EĀ 43.5 (~) / EĀ 50.8 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma had such an evil view: "I know that the World-Honored One teaches that there is no hindrance to engaging in desire." When the bhikṣus heard this, they went to the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma and asked: "Ārādakālāma, is it true that you said: 'I know that the World-Honored One teaches that there is no hindrance to engaging in desire'?"

尔时，阿梨咤比丘本伽陀婆梨，生如是恶见：「我知世尊如是说法，行欲者无障碍。」诸比丘闻已，往至阿梨咤比丘所，问曰：「阿梨咤！汝实如是说：『我知世尊如是说法，行欲者无障碍耶？』」

Ārādakālāma replied: "Venerable ones! I really know that the World-Honored One teaches that there is no hindrance to engaging in desire."

时，阿梨咤答曰：「诸贤！我实知世尊如是说法，行欲者无障碍。」

The bhikṣus rebuked Ārādakālāma, saying: "Do not say that! Do not slander the World-Honored One! Slandering the World-Honored One is not good. The World-Honored One did not say that. Ārādakālāma! Desire is obstructed. The World-Honored One has said in countless ways that desire is obstructed. Ārādakālāma! You should quickly abandon this evil view!"

诸比丘诃阿梨咤曰：「汝莫作是说，莫诬谤世尊，诬谤世尊者不善，世尊亦不如是说。阿梨咤！欲有障碍，世尊无量方便说欲有障碍。阿梨咤！汝可速舍此恶见也。」

After being rebuked by the bhikṣus, the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma held onto this evil view with great force, saying: "This is true, the rest is false." He said this three times.

阿梨咤比丘为诸比丘所诃已，如此恶见其强力执，而一向说：「此是真实，余者虚妄。」如是再三。

The many bhikṣus were unable to make the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma abandon this evil view. They rose from their seats and went to where the Buddha was. They bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, then sat to one side and said: "World-Honored One! The bhikṣu Ārādakālāma has given rise to this evil view: 'I know that the World-Honored One has taught the Dharma like this: "There is no obstruction in engaging in desire." World-Honored One! Having heard this, we went to where the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma was and asked him: 'Ārādakālāma! Do you really say: "I know that the World-Honored One has taught the Dharma like this: 'There is no obstruction in engaging in desire'?" The bhikṣu Ārādakālāma answered us: 'Virtuous ones! I really know that the World-Honored One has taught the Dharma like this: "There is no obstruction in engaging in desire." World-Honored One! We rebuked him, saying: 'Ārādakālāma! Do not say that! Do not slander the World-Honored One! Slandering the World-Honored One is not good. The World-Honored One did not say that. Ārādakālāma! Desire is obstructed. The World-Honored One has said in countless ways that desire is obstructed. Ārādakālāma! You should quickly abandon this evil view!' Having rebuked him, this evil view was held by him with great force, and he said only: 'This is true, the rest is false.' He said this three times. World-Honored One! We were unable to make the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma abandon this evil view. We rose from our seats and left."

众多比丘不能令阿梨咤比丘舍此恶见，从坐起去，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！阿梨咤比丘生如是恶见：『我知世尊如是说法：「行欲者无障碍。」』世尊！我等闻已，往诣阿梨咤比丘所，问曰：『阿梨咤！汝实如是说：「我知世尊如是说法：『行欲者无障碍耶？』」』阿梨咤比丘答我等曰：『诸贤！我实知世尊如是说法：「行欲者无障碍。」』世尊！我等诃曰：『阿梨咤！汝莫作是说，莫诬谤世尊，诬谤世尊者不善，世尊亦不如是说。阿梨咤！欲有障碍，世尊无量方便说欲有障碍。阿梨咤！汝可速舍此恶见。』我等诃已，如此恶见其强力执，而一向说：『此是真实，余者虚妄。』如是再三。世尊！如我等不能令阿梨咤比丘舍此恶见，从坐起去。」

The World-Honored One, having heard this, told a bhikṣu: "Go to the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma and say: 'The World-Honored One calls you!'"

世尊闻已，告一比丘：「汝往阿梨咤比丘所，作如是语：『世尊呼汝！』」

Then a bhikṣu accepted the World-Honored One's instruction, rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times, and left. He went to where the bhikṣu Ārādakālāma was and said to him: "The World-Honored One calls you!" The bhikṣu Ārādakālāma then went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

于是，一比丘受世尊教，即从坐起，稽首佛足，遶三匝而去，至阿梨咤比丘所，即语彼曰：「世尊呼汝！」阿梨咤比丘即诣佛所，稽首佛足，却坐一面。

The World-Honored One asked: "Ārādakālāma, is it true that you said: 'I know that the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma like this: "There is no obstruction to engaging in desire."'?"

世尊问曰：「阿梨咤！实如是说：『我知世尊如是说法：「行欲者无障碍。」耶？』」

Ārādakālāma replied: "World-Honored One! I truly know that the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma like this: 'There is no obstruction to engaging in desire.'"

阿梨咤答曰：「世尊！我实知世尊如是说法：『行欲者无障碍。』」

The World-Honored One rebuked: "Ārādakālāma! How do you know that I teach the Dharma like this? From which mouth did you hear me teach the Dharma like this? You foolish person! I do not teach one-sidedly, yet you teach one-sidedly! You foolish person! When you hear the bhikṣus criticizing you, you should reply according to the Dharma: 'I will now ask the bhikṣus.'"

世尊诃曰：「阿梨咤！汝云何知我如是说法？汝从何口闻我如是说法？汝愚痴人！我不一向说，汝一向说耶？汝愚痴人！闻诸比丘共诃，汝时应如法答：『我今当问诸比丘也。』」

Then the World-Honored One asked the bhikṣus: "Do you also know that I teach the Dharma like this: 'There is no obstruction to engaging in desire'?"

于是，世尊问诸比丘：「汝等亦如是知我如是说法：『行欲者无障碍。』耶？」

The bhikṣus replied: "No, we do not."

时，诸比丘答曰：「不也。」

The World-Honored One asked: "How do you know that I teach the Dharma?"

世尊问曰：「汝等云何知我说法？」

The bhikṣus replied: "We know that the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma thus: 'Desire has obstacles.' The World-Honored One says that desire has obstacles. Desire is like a bone shackle, the World-Honored One says that desire is like a bone shackle. Desire is like a piece of meat, the World-Honored One says that desire is like a piece of meat. Desire is like holding a torch, the World-Honored One says that desire is like holding a torch. Desire is like a pit of fire, the World-Honored One says that desire is like a pit of fire. Desire is like a poisonous snake, the World-Honored One says that desire is like a poisonous snake. Desire is like a dream, the World-Honored One says that desire is like a dream. Desire is like borrowing, the World-Honored One says that desire is like borrowing. Desire is like a tree fruit, the World-Honored One says that desire is like a tree fruit. We know that the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma thus."

诸比丘答曰：「我等知世尊如是说法：『欲有障碍。』世尊说欲有障碍也。欲如骨鏁，世尊说欲如骨鏁也。欲如肉脔，世尊说欲如肉脔也。欲如把炬，世尊说欲如把炬也。欲如火坑，世尊说欲如火坑也。欲如毒蛇，世尊说欲如毒蛇也。欲如梦，世尊说欲如梦也。欲如假借，世尊说欲如假借也。欲如树果，世尊说欲如树果也。我等知世尊如是说法。」

The World-Honored One praised: "Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣus, you know that I teach the Dharma thus. Why is that? Because I also teach thus: 'Desire has obstacles.' I say that desire has obstacles. Desire is like a bone shackle, I say that desire is like a bone shackle. Desire is like a piece of meat, I say that desire is like a piece of meat. Desire is like holding a torch, I say that desire is like holding a torch. Desire is like a pit of fire, I say that desire is like a pit of fire. Desire is like a poisonous snake, I say that desire is like a poisonous snake. Desire is like a dream, I say that desire is like a dream. Desire is like borrowing, I say that desire is like borrowing. Desire is like a tree fruit, I say that desire is like a tree fruit."

世尊叹曰：「善哉！善哉！诸比丘！汝等知我如是说法。所以者何？我亦如是说：『欲有障碍。』我说欲有障碍。欲如骨鏁，我说欲如骨鏁。欲如肉脔，我说欲如肉脔。欲如把炬，我说欲如把炬。欲如火坑，我说欲如火坑。欲如毒蛇，我说欲如毒蛇。欲如梦，我说欲如梦。欲如假借，我说欲如假借。欲如树果，我说欲如树果。」

The World-Honored One praised: "Excellent! Excellent! You know that I teach the Dharma thus. However, this foolish person Āḷavaka has distorted understanding of the meaning and the words. Because of his own distorted understanding, he falsely slanders me, harming himself, committing an offense and fault, displeasing to the wise and virtuous practitioners, and incurring great blame. You foolish person! Do you know that there is such an evil and unwholesome state?"

世尊叹曰：「善哉！善哉！汝等知我如是说法。然此阿梨咤愚痴之人，颠倒受解义及文也。彼因自颠倒受解故，诬谤于我，为自伤害，有犯有罪，诸智梵行者所不憙也，而得大罪。汝愚痴人！知有此恶不善处也。」

Then, after being directly rebuked by the World-Honored One, the bhikṣu Āḷavaka was inwardly distressed, lowered his head in silence, lost his eloquence, and was speechless, as if waiting for something.

于是，阿梨咤比丘为世尊面诃责已，内怀忧戚，低头默然，失辩无言，如有所伺。

Then, after directly rebuking the bhikṣu Āḷavaka, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "If you fully understand the meaning of what I teach, you should uphold it thus. If you do not fully understand the meaning of what I teach, you should ask me and the wise and virtuous practitioners. Why is this? There may be foolish people who have distorted understanding of the meaning and the words. Because of their own distorted understanding, they know the Dharma thus, namely, the sūtras, gāthās, vyākaraṇas, verses, causes and conditions, compilations, jātakas, avadānas, birth stories, extensive explanations, and marvelous teachings, and they argue about understanding the meaning. They do not accept the liberation of understanding the meaning. What they know about the Dharma does not attain the meaning. They only experience extreme suffering and exhaust themselves in vain. Why is this? Because they have distorted understanding of the Dharma.

于是，世尊面诃责数阿梨咤比丘已，告诸比丘：「若我所说法尽具解义者，当如是受持。若我所说法不尽具解义者，便当问我及诸智梵行者。所以者何？或有痴人，颠倒受解义及文也，彼因自颠倒受解故，如是如是知彼法，谓正经、歌咏、记说、偈他、因缘、撰录、本起、此说、生处、广解、未曾有法及说义，彼诤知此义，不受解脱知此义，彼所为知此法，不得此义，但受极苦，唐自疲劳。所以者何？彼以颠倒受解法故。

It is like a person who wants to catch a snake. They go in search of a snake. While searching for a snake, they come across a very large snake in the wild forest. They then approach and grab the snake by the middle. The snake turns its head and bites their hands, feet, or other limbs. The person who sought to catch the snake does not attain the meaning, but only experiences extreme suffering and exhausts themselves in vain. Why is this? Because they do not skillfully know the method of catching snakes. In the same way, there may be foolish people who have distorted understanding of the meaning and the words. Because of their own distorted understanding, they know the Dharma thus, namely, the sūtras, gāthās, vyākaraṇas, verses, causes and conditions, compilations, jātakas, avadānas, birth stories, extensive explanations, and marvelous teachings, and they argue about understanding the meaning. They do not accept the liberation of understanding the meaning. What they know about the Dharma does not attain the meaning. They only experience extreme suffering and exhaust themselves in vain. Why is this? Because they have distorted understanding of the Dharma.

「譬若如人，欲得捉蛇，便行求蛇。彼求蛇时，行野林间，见极大蛇，便前以手捉其腰中，蛇回举头，或蜇手足及余支节。彼人所为求取捉蛇，不得此义，但受极苦，唐自疲劳。所以者何？以不善解取蛇法故。如是或有痴人，颠倒受解义及文也，彼因自颠倒受解故，如是如是知彼法，谓正经、歌咏、记说、偈他、因缘、撰录、本起、此说、生处、广解、未曾有法及说义。彼诤知此义，不受解脱知此义，彼所为知此法，不得此义，但受极苦，唐自疲劳。所以者何？彼以颠倒受解法故。

There may be sons of noble families who have an undistorted good understanding of the meaning and the words. Because of their undistorted good understanding, they know the Dharma thus, namely, the sūtras, gāthās, vyākaraṇas, verses, causes and conditions, compilations, jātakas, avadānas, birth stories, extensive explanations, and marvelous teachings, and they do not argue about understanding the meaning. They only accept the liberation of understanding the meaning. What they know about the Dharma attains the meaning. They do not experience extreme suffering and are not exhausted. Why is this? Because they have an undistorted understanding of the Dharma.

「或有族姓子，不颠倒善受解义及文，彼因不颠倒善受解故，如是如是知彼法，谓正经、歌咏、记说、偈他、因缘、撰录、本起、此说、生处、广解、未曾有法及说义。彼不诤知此义，唯受解脱知此义，彼所为知此法，得此义，不受极苦，亦不疲劳。所以者何？以不颠倒受解法故。

It is like a person who wants to catch a snake. They go in search of a snake. While searching for a snake, they take an iron rod in hand and come across a very large snake in the wild forest. They first press down the snake's head with the iron rod and then grab its head. Although the snake may turn its tail and wrap around their hands, feet, or other limbs, it cannot bite them. The person who sought to catch the snake attains the meaning and does not experience extreme suffering or become exhausted. Why is this? Because they skillfully know the method of catching snakes. In the same way, there may be sons of noble families who have an undistorted good understanding of the meaning and the words. Because of their undistorted good understanding, they know the Dharma thus, namely, the sūtras, gāthās, vyākaraṇas, verses, causes and conditions, compilations, jātakas, avadānas, birth stories, extensive explanations, and marvelous teachings, and they do not argue about understanding the meaning. They only accept the liberation of understanding the meaning. What they know about the Dharma attains the meaning. They do not experience extreme suffering and are not exhausted. Why is this? Because they have an undistorted understanding of the Dharma. I have taught the Dharma using the analogy of a raft for a long time, wishing for you to abandon it, not wishing for you to grasp it.

「譬若如人，欲得捉蛇，便行求蛇。彼求蛇时，手执铁杖，行野林间，见极大蛇，先以铁杖押彼蛇顶，手捉其头，彼蛇虽反尾回，或缠手足及余支节，然不能蜇。彼人所为求取捉蛇，而得此义，不受极苦，亦不疲劳。所以者何？彼以善解取蛇法故。如是或有族姓子，不颠倒善受解义及文，彼因不颠倒善受解故，如是如是知彼法，谓正经、歌咏、记说、偈他、因缘、撰录、本起、此说、生处、广解、未曾有法及说义，彼不诤知此义，唯受解脱知此义。彼所为知此法，得此义，不受极苦，亦不疲劳。所以者何？以不颠倒受解法故。我为汝等长夜说筏喻法，欲令弃舍，不欲令受故。

"How do I teach the Dharma using the analogy of a raft for a long time, wishing for you to abandon it, not wishing for you to grasp it? It is like a river with deep, vast, long, and swift currents, carrying many things along. There are no boats or bridges. A person comes and wants to cross to the other shore. When seeking to cross, they think: 'This river is deep, vast, long, and swift, carrying many things along. There are no boats or bridges to cross. I have something to do on the other shore. How can I safely reach the other shore?' They further think: 'I should gather grass and wood on this shore, bind them into a raft, and ride it across.' They then gather grass and wood on the shore, bind them into a raft, and ride it across, safely reaching the other shore. They then think: 'This raft has been of great benefit to me. Riding on this raft, I have safely come from the other shore to this shore. I should now carry it on my right shoulder or wear it on my head.' They then carry the raft on their right shoulder or wear it on their head. What do you think? Would they be of any benefit to the raft by doing so?"

「云何我为汝等长夜说筏喻法，欲令弃舍，不欲令受？犹如山水甚深极广，长流驶疾，多有所漂，其中无舡，亦无桥梁。或有人来，而于彼岸有事欲度，彼求度时，而作是念：『今此山水甚深极广，长流驶疾，多有所漂，其中无舡亦无桥梁而可度者，我于彼岸有事欲度，当以何方便，令我安隐至彼岸耶？』复作是念：『我今宁可于此岸边收聚草木，缚作椑栰，乘之而度。』彼便岸边收聚草木，缚作椑栰，乘之而度，安隐至彼。便作是念：『今我此栰多有所益，乘此栰已，令我安隐，从彼岸来，度至此岸，我今宁可以著右肩或头戴去。』彼便以栰著右肩上或头戴去。于意云何？彼作如是竟，能为栰有所益耶？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, they would not."

时，诸比丘答曰：「不也。」

The World-Honored One said, "How could that person be of any benefit to the raft by doing so? That person thinks: 'This raft has been of great benefit to me. Riding on this raft, I have safely come from the other shore to this shore. I should now return this raft to the water or place it on the shore and leave.' That person then returns the raft to the water or places it on the shore and leaves. What do you think? Would they be of any benefit to the raft by doing so?"

世尊告曰：「彼人云何为栰所作能有益耶？彼人作是念：『今我此栰多有所益，乘此栰已，令我安隐，从彼岸来，度至此岸。我今宁可更以此栰还著水中，或著岸边而舍去耶？』彼人便以此栰还著水中，或著岸边舍之而去。于意云何？彼作如是，为栰所作能有益耶？」

The bhikṣus replied, "It would be of benefit."

时，诸比丘答曰：「益也。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is. I have long taught you the raft simile, wishing for you to abandon it, not to accept it. If you know that I have long taught you the raft simile, you should abandon it, let alone what is not the Dharma.

世尊告曰：「如是。我为汝等长夜说栰喻法，欲令弃舍，不欲令受。若汝等知我长夜说栰喻法者，当以舍是法，况非法耶？

"Furthermore, there are six views. What are the six? A bhikṣu, with regard to all forms, past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or fine, beautiful or ugly, near or far, knows them all as not mine, not belonging to me, and not the self. He knows them as they truly are through wisdom. With regard to all feelings, perceptions, and views, he knows them all as not mine, not belonging to me, and not the self. He knows them as they truly are through wisdom. With regard to all views, whether seen, heard, known, or realized, obtained, or contemplated, and all thoughts of the mind, from this world to the other world, from the other world to this world, he knows them all as not mine, not belonging to me, and not the self. He knows them as they truly are through wisdom. With regard to all views, such as 'this is the self, this is the world, this is me, I will exist in the future world, eternally unchanging, always indestructible,' he knows them all as not mine, not belonging to me, and not the self. He knows them as they truly are through wisdom."

「复次，有六见处。云何为六？比丘者，所有色，过去、未来、现在，或内或外，或精或麤，或妙或不妙，或近或远，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神，如是慧观，知其如真。所有觉、所有想、所有此见非我有，我非彼有，我当无，我当不有，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神，如是慧观，知其如真。所有此见，若见闻识知，所得所观，意所思念，从此世至彼世，从彼世至此世，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神，如是慧观，知其如真。所有此见，此是神，此是世，此是我，我当后世有，常不变易，恒不磨灭法，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神，如是慧观，知其如真。」

Then a certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, arranged his robe over one shoulder, bowed with his palms together toward the Buddha, and said, "World-Honored One! Is there any internal cause for fear?"

于是，有一比丘从坐而起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！颇有因内有恐怖耶？」

The World-Honored One replied, "There is."

世尊答曰：「有也。」

The bhikṣu asked again, "World-Honored One! What is the internal cause for fear?"

比丘复问曰：「世尊！云何因内有恐怖耶？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikṣu! If one sees and speaks like this, one may have had it before but now cannot obtain it. If one sees and speaks like this, one will be anxious, troubled, cry, beat one's chest, and go mad. Bhikṣu! This is the internal cause for fear."

世尊答曰：「比丘者，如是见、如是说，彼或昔时无，设有我不得。彼如是见、如是说，忧戚烦劳，啼哭椎胸而发狂痴。比丘！如是因内有恐怖也。」

The bhikṣu praised the World-Honored One and then asked again, "World-Honored One! Is there an internal cause for fearlessness?"

比丘叹世尊已，复问曰：「世尊！颇有因内无恐怖也。」

The World-Honored One replied, "There is."

世尊答曰：「有也。」

The bhikṣu asked again, "World-Honored One! What is the internal cause for fearlessness?"

比丘复问曰：「世尊！云何因内无恐怖也？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikṣus do not see or speak like this. They may have had it in the past, but they do not obtain it. They do not see or speak like this, and are not anxious, troubled, crying, beating their chests, or going mad. Bhikṣus, this is the internal cause for fearlessness."

世尊答曰：「比丘者，不如是见、不如是说，彼或昔时无，设有我不得。彼不如是见、不如是说，不忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴。比丘！如是因内无恐怖也。」

The bhikṣu praised the World-Honored One and asked again, "World-Honored One! Is there an external cause for fear?"

比丘叹世尊已，复问曰：「世尊！颇有因外有恐怖也？」

The World-Honored One replied, "There is."

世尊答曰：「有也。」

The bhikṣu asked again, "World-Honored One! What is the external cause for fear?"

比丘复问曰：「世尊！云何因外有恐怖也？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikṣus, if they see and say, 'This is the self, this is the world, this is me, and I will exist in the next life,' then they may encounter a Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata who is wise, intelligent, and eloquent, and who has attained wisdom. That Tathāgata or disciple of the Tathāgata will teach the Dharma to eliminate all self, to abandon all afflictions, all 'I' and 'mine,' and to eliminate the afflictions of pride. When that Tathāgata or disciple of the Tathāgata teaches the Dharma to eliminate all self, to abandon all afflictions, all 'I' and 'mine,' and to eliminate the afflictions of pride, they will be distressed, troubled, and agitated, weeping and beating their breasts in madness and delusion, saying, 'I am cut off and will no longer exist.' Why is that? Because for those bhikṣus, it is not something they have long desired, enjoyed, or thought about. Bhikṣus, if they engage in that extensively, they will be distressed, troubled, and agitated, weeping and beating their breasts in madness and delusion. Bhikṣus, this is the external cause for fear.

世尊答曰：「比丘者，如是见、如是说，此是神，此是世，此是我，我当后世有。彼如是见、如是说，或遇如来，或遇如来弟子，聪明智慧而善言语，成就智慧。彼或如来，或如来弟子，灭一切自身故说法，舍离一切漏、一切我、我所作，灭慢使故说法，彼或如来，或如来弟子，灭一切自身故说法，舍离一切漏、一切我、我所作，灭慢使故说法时，忧戚烦劳，啼哭椎胸而发狂痴，如是说，我断坏不复有。所以者何？彼比丘所谓长夜不可爱、不可乐、不可意念，比丘多行彼便忧戚烦劳，啼哭椎胸而发狂痴。比丘！如是因外有恐怖也。」

The bhikṣu praised the World-Honored One and then asked again, "World-Honored One! Is there an external cause for fearlessness?"

比丘叹世尊已，复问曰：「世尊！颇有因外无恐怖耶？」

The World-Honored One replied, "There is."

世尊答曰：「有也。」

The bhikṣu asked again, "World-Honored One! What is the external cause for fearlessness?"

比丘复问曰：「世尊！云何因外无恐怖耶？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Bhikṣus do not see or speak like this: 'This is the self, this is the world, this is me, and I will exist in the next life.' They do not see or speak like this. They may encounter the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's disciples, who are intelligent, wise, and good at speaking, and who have accomplished wisdom. The Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's disciples teach the Dharma to eliminate all self, to abandon all afflictions, all 'I' and 'mine,' and to eliminate the afflictions of pride. When the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's disciples teach the Dharma to eliminate all self, to abandon all afflictions, all 'I' and 'mine,' and to eliminate the afflictions of pride, they do not become distressed, troubled, crying, beating their chests, or insane. They do not say, 'I am cut off and will no longer exist.' Why is that? Because those bhikṣus have long cultivated what is lovable, enjoyable, and pleasing to the mind. Bhikṣus who practice much in this way do not become distressed, troubled, crying, beating their chests, or insane. Bhikṣus! This is the external cause for fearlessness.

世尊答曰：「比丘者，不如是见、不如是说，此是神，此是世，此是我，我当后世有。彼不如是见、不如是说，或遇如来，或遇如来弟子，聪明智慧而善言语，成就智慧。彼或如来，或如来弟子，灭一切自身故说法，舍离一切漏、一切我、我所作，灭慢使故说法，彼或如来，或如来弟子，灭一切自身故说法，舍离一切漏、一切我、我所作，灭慢使故说法时，不忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴，不如是说，我断坏不复有。所以者何？彼比丘所谓长夜可爱、可乐、可意念，比丘多行彼便不忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴，比丘！如是因外无恐怖也。」

At that time, the bhikṣu praised the World-Honored One, saying, "Excellent! Excellent!" Having praised him, he listened to the Buddha's teachings, received and recited them well, and then remained silent.

尔时，比丘叹世尊曰：「善哉！善哉！」叹善哉已，闻佛所说，善受持诵，则便默然。

Then the World-Honored One praised the bhikṣus, saying, "Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣus, you accept what is to be accepted. Having accepted it, you do not become distressed, troubled, crying, beating your chests, or insane. Do you see what is to be accepted and what is to be accepted, and do you not become distressed, troubled, crying, beating your chests, or insane?"

于是，世尊叹诸比丘曰：「善哉！善哉！比丘受如是所可受，受已，不生忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴。汝等见所受所可受，不生忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴耶？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One praised, "Excellent! Excellent! You rely on such views to be relied upon. Having seen them, you do not become distressed, troubled, crying, beating your chests, or insane. Do you see such views to be relied upon, and having seen them, do you not become distressed, troubled, crying, beating your chests, or insane?"

世尊叹曰：「善哉！善哉！汝等依如是见所可依，见已，不生忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴。汝等见依如是见所可依，见已，不生忧戚，不烦劳，不啼哭，不椎胸，不发狂痴耶？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One praised, "Excellent! Excellent! You accept such a body, and the body is permanent, unchanging, and indestructible. Do you see the body that is accepted as such, and having seen it, is it permanent, unchanging, and indestructible?"

世尊叹曰：「善哉！善哉！汝等受如是身，所有身常住不变易、不磨灭法。汝等见受如是身所可受身已，常住不变易、不磨灭法耶？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One praised, "Excellent! Excellent! It is said that because of the spirit there is a self, and without the spirit there is no self. This spirit and the possessions of the spirit cannot be obtained or established, and the views, attachments, and afflictions in the mind cannot be obtained or established. Bhikṣu! It is not a complete explanation of views and the continuity of views, just like the bhikṣu Āḷavaka's former life as the brahmin Gaṇakaputta."

世尊叹曰：「善哉！善哉！所谓因神故有我，无神见无我，是为神、神所有，不可得、不可施设，及心中有见处、结著、诸使亦不可得，不可施设。比丘！非为具足说见及见所相续，犹如阿梨咤比丘本为伽陀婆利耶？」

The bhikṣu replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! It is a complete explanation of views and the continuity of views, just like the bhikṣu Āḷavaka's former life as the brahmin Gaṇakaputta."

比丘答曰：「如是。世尊！为具足说见及见所相续，犹如阿梨咤比丘本为伽陀婆梨。」

Furthermore, there are six views. What are the six? A bhikṣu, all forms, whether past, future, or present, whether internal or external, whether gross or subtle, whether excellent or inferior, whether near or far, are not mine, I do not belong to them, and they are not the spirit. Thus, with wisdom, one knows them as they truly are. All feelings, perceptions, volitional formations, and consciousness, all these views are not mine, I do not belong to them, they will be without me, and will not exist. All of them are not mine, I do not belong to them, and they are not the spirit. Thus, with wisdom, one knows them as they truly are. All these views, whether seen, heard, recognized, or known, obtained, observed, or contemplated by the mind, from this world to the other world, from the other world to this world, are not mine, I do not belong to them, and they are not the spirit. Thus, with wisdom, one knows them as they truly are. All these views, this is the spirit, this is the world, this is the self, I will exist in the future world, eternally unchanging, always indestructible, all of them are not mine, I do not belong to them, and they are not the spirit. Thus, with wisdom, one knows them as they truly are.

「复次，有六见处。云何为六？比丘者，所有色，过去、未来、现在，或内或外，或精或麤，或妙或不妙，或近或远，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神。如是慧观，知其如真。所有觉行、有想、所有此见，非我有，我非彼有，彼当无我，当不有，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神。如是慧观，知其如真。所有此见，若见闻识知，所得所观，意所思念，从此世至彼世，从彼世至此世，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神。如是慧观，知其如真。所有此见，此是神，此是世，此是我，我当后世有，常不变易，恒不磨灭法，彼一切非我有，我非彼有，亦非是神。如是慧观，知其如真。

All bhikṣus, these six views are not the spirit, nor do they belong to the spirit. Having not seen this, one does not accept this world. Having not accepted this world, one is without fear. Having not been afraid, one attains parinirvāṇa, with birth ended, the holy life established, what was to be done is done, and one knows as it truly is. This is called a bhikṣu who has crossed the moat, gone beyond the moat, broken through the wall, without a door, and the mirror of holy wisdom. How does a bhikṣu cross the moat? The moat of ignorance is exhausted and known, its root is uprooted, broken, and will not arise again. Thus, a bhikṣu has crossed the moat. How does a bhikṣu go beyond the moat? The moat of craving for existence is exhausted and known, its root is uprooted, broken, and will not arise again. Thus, a bhikṣu has gone beyond the moat. How does a bhikṣu break through the wall? The endless cycle of birth and death is exhausted and known, its root is uprooted, broken, and will not arise again. Thus, a bhikṣu has broken through the wall. How does a bhikṣu have no door? The five lower fetters are exhausted and known, their root is uprooted, broken, and will not arise again. Thus, a bhikṣu has no door. How does a bhikṣu have the mirror of holy wisdom? The conceit of 'I' is exhausted and known, its root is uprooted, broken, and will not arise again. Thus, a bhikṣu has the mirror of holy wisdom. This is called a bhikṣu who has crossed the moat, gone beyond the moat, broken through the wall, without a door, and the mirror of holy wisdom.

「所有比丘，此六见处不见是神，亦不见神所有，彼如是不见已，便不受此世，不受此世已，便无恐怖，因不恐怖已，便得般涅槃，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。是谓比丘度壍、过壍、破墎、无门、圣智慧镜。云何比丘度壍耶？无明壍已尽已知，拔绝根本，打破不复当生，如是比丘得度壍也。云何比丘过壍耶？有爱已尽已知，拔绝根本，打破不复当生，如是比丘得过壍也。云何比丘破墎耶？无穷生死已尽已知，拔绝根本，打破不复当生，如是比丘得破墎也。云何比丘无门耶？五下分结已尽已知，拔绝根本，打破不复当生，如是比丘得无门也。云何比丘圣智慧镜？我慢已尽已知，拔绝根本，打破不复当生，如是比丘圣智慧镜。是谓比丘度壍、过壍、破墎、无门、圣智慧镜。

"Thus, the Tathāgata, who is truly liberated, has the cause of the Tīrthikas and the gods of the Heaven of the Thirty-three, the Brahmās and their retinues. They seek but cannot find the Tathāgata's dependent consciousness. The Tathāgata is the Brahmā, the Tathāgata is the cool one, the Tathāgata is not agitated, the Tathāgata is not different. I say thus: 'The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas slander me, speaking falsely and not truthfully. The śramaṇa Gautama teaches without establishing anything. They truly have sentient beings, and establish their annihilation and destruction. If there is no self in this, I do not say that the Tathāgata is without sorrow in this present life. If others curse, beat, and angrily rebuke the Tathāgata, the Tathāgata does not become angry, hateful, or harmful because of that.' If others curse, beat, and angrily rebuke the Tathāgata, what does the Tathāgata think? The Tathāgata thinks: 'If I have done and created in the past, it leads to this.' If others curse, beat, and angrily rebuke the Tathāgata, the Tathāgata thinks thus. If others pay respect, make offerings, honor, and revere the Tathāgata, the Tathāgata does not rejoice in it, does not delight in it, and does not find it pleasant. If others pay respect, make offerings, honor, and revere the Tathāgata, what does the Tathāgata think? The Tathāgata thinks: 'If I now know and cut off, it leads to this.' If others pay respect, make offerings, honor, and revere the Tathāgata, the Tathāgata thinks thus."

「如是正解脱如来，有因提罗及天伊沙那，有梵及眷属，彼求不能得如来所依识，如来是梵，如来是冷，如来不烦热，如来是不异。我如是说：『诸沙门、梵志诬谤我，虚妄言、不真实，沙门瞿昙御无所施设，彼实有众生，施设断灭坏，若此中无我不说，彼如来于现法中说无忧。若有他人骂詈如来，挝打如来，瞋恚责数者，如来因彼处不瞋恚、不憎嫉，终无害心。』若人骂詈如来，挝打、瞋恚责数时，如来意云何？如来作是念：『若我本所作、本所造者，因彼致此。』言然骂詈如来，挝打、瞋恚责数者，如来作是意。若有他人恭敬如来，供养礼事尊重者，如来因此不以为悦，不以为欢喜，心不以为乐。若他人恭敬如来，供养礼事尊重者，如来意云何？如来作是念：『若我今所知所断，因彼致此。』若有他人恭敬如来，供养礼事尊重者，如来作是意。」

"Then the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus: "If others curse, beat, and angrily rebuke you, or if others pay respect, make offerings, honor, and revere you, you should not become angry, hateful, or harmful because of that, nor should you rejoice, delight, or find pleasure in it. Why is that? Because we have no self, no possession of self. It is like the dry grass and withered wood outside the gate of this Jeta Grove. If others take it away to burn it and use it as they please, what do you think? Does that dry grass and withered wood think: 'Others are taking me away to burn me and use me as they please'?

于是，世尊告诸比丘：「若有他人骂詈汝等，挝打、瞋恚责数者，若有恭敬供养、礼事尊重者，汝等因此亦当莫瞋恚憎嫉，莫起害心，亦莫欢悦欢喜，亦莫心乐。所以者何？我等无神、无神所有，犹如今此胜林门外燥草枯木，或有他人持去火烧，随意所用，于意云何？彼燥草枯木颇作是念：『他人持我去火烧，随意所用耶？』」

The bhikṣus replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

诸比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！」

"So it is, if others curse, beat, and angrily rebuke you, or if others pay respect, make offerings, honor, and revere you, you should not become angry, hateful, or harmful because of that, nor should you rejoice, delight, or find pleasure in it. Why is that? Because we have no self, no possession of self. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. Those who are liberated by right wisdom and die, they do not establish the endless. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. Those who have exhausted the five lower fetters and die, are born there and attain parinirvāṇa, attain the irreversible state, and do not return to this world. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. Those who have exhausted the three fetters, are thin in lust, anger, and delusion, attain once-returning, go to the heavens and humans once, and attain the end of suffering after that. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. Those who have exhausted the three fetters, attain the stream-enterer, do not fall into evil ways, are certain to attain right awakening, go to the heavens and humans seven times, and attain the end of suffering after seven times. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. The Dharma I have well-proclaimed is revealed, widely spread, without gaps, and circulated, even to the devas and humans. Those who have faith and joy in me and die, all are born in good places, as above, with remainder."

「如是，若有他人骂詈汝等，挝打、瞋恚责数者，若有恭敬供养、礼事尊重者，汝因此亦当莫瞋恚憎嫉，莫起害心，亦莫欢悦欢喜，亦莫心乐。所以者何？我等无神、无神所有，有我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。如是我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。若正智慧解脱命终者，彼不施设有无穷，我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。如是我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。若有五下分结尽而命终者，生于彼间，便般涅槃，得不退法，不还此世。我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。如是我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。彼三结已尽，婬怒痴薄，得一往来天上人间，一往来已，便得苦边。我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。如是我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。彼三结已尽，得须陀洹，不堕恶法，定趣正觉，极七往来天上人间，七往来已，便得苦边。我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。如是我法善说，发露广布，无有空缺，流布宣传，乃至天人。若有信乐于我而命终者，皆生善处，如上有余。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
