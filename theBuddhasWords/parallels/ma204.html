MĀ 204

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 17 晡利多品

204. （二〇四）罗摩经

Parallel to: DN 14 (~) / MN 26 / MN 85 (~) / SN 6.1 (~) / SN 56.11 (~) / EĀ 19.1 (~) / EĀ 24.5 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Shravasti, staying at the Deer Mother's Hall in the Eastern Garden.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在于东园鹿子母堂。

At that time, the World-Honored One rose from his afternoon meditation, came down from the hall, and said to Venerable Ānanda, "I will now go with you to the Airavana River to bathe."

尔时，世尊则于晡时从燕坐起，堂上来下，告尊者阿难：「我今共汝至阿夷罗婆提河浴。」

Venerable Ānanda replied, "Yes, World-Honored One."

尊者阿难白曰：「唯然。」

Venerable Ānanda took the key and went to all the rooms, walking around. Seeing the monks, he said, "Virtuous ones! Let us go together to the house of the Brahmin Rama." The monks, having heard, then went together to the house of the Brahmin Rama. The World-Honored One, together with Venerable Ānanda, went to the Airavana River, took off their robes on the bank, and entered the water to bathe. After bathing, they came out, dried their bodies, and put on their robes.

尊者阿难执持户钥，遍诣诸屋而彷徉，见诸比丘，便作是说：「诸贤！可共诣梵志罗摩家。」诸比丘闻已，便共往诣梵志罗摩家。世尊将尊者阿难往至阿夷罗婆提河，脱衣岸上，便入水浴，浴已还出，拭体著衣。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was standing behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha. Then Venerable Ānanda joined his palms towards the Buddha and said, "World-Honored One! The house of the Brahmin Rama is extremely well-arranged and very delightful. I only wish that the World-Honored One, out of compassion, would go to the house of the Brahmin Rama." The World-Honored One silently accepted Venerable Ānanda's request. Then the World-Honored One, together with Venerable Ānanda, went to the house of the Brahmin Rama.

尔时，尊者阿难立世尊后，执扇扇佛。于是，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！梵志罗摩家极好整顿，甚可爱乐，唯愿世尊以慈愍故，往至梵志罗摩家。」世尊为尊者阿难默然而受。于是世尊将尊者阿难往至梵志罗摩家。

At that time, many monks were gathered in the house of the Brahmin Rama, discussing the Dharma. The Buddha was standing outside the door, waiting for the monks to finish their discussion. The many monks soon finished their discussion and fell silent. The World-Honored One, knowing this, cleared his throat and knocked on the door. The monks, hearing this, went to open the door. The World-Honored One then entered the house of the Brahmin Rama, spread out a seat in front of the assembly of monks, and sat down. He asked, "What were the monks discussing just now? What was the matter that brought them together here? "

尔时，梵志罗摩家，众多比丘集坐说法，佛住门外，待诸比丘说法讫竟。众多比丘寻说法讫，默然而住。世尊知已，謦欬敲门，诸比丘闻，即往开门，世尊便入梵志罗摩家，于比丘众前敷座而坐，问曰：「诸比丘向说何等？以何事故集坐在此？」

The monks replied, "World-Honored One! We were discussing the Dharma just now. That is why we gathered here for this matter."

时，诸比丘答曰：「世尊！向者说法，以此法事集坐在此。」

The World-Honored One praised, "Excellent! Excellent! When monks gather and sit, they should do two things: discuss the Dharma or remain silent. Why is this? I will also discuss the Dharma for you. Listen carefully, listen carefully, and contemplate it well."

世尊叹曰：「善哉！善哉！比丘集坐当行二事：一曰说法，二曰默然。所以者何？我亦为汝说法，谛听，谛听，善思念之。」

The monks said, "Yes, we will listen to the teachings."

时，诸比丘白曰：「唯然。当受教听。」

The Buddha said, "There are two kinds of seeking: the noble seeking and the non-noble seeking. What is the non-noble seeking? There are real sick dharmas and seeking sick dharmas, real old dharmas and death dharmas, real sorrow and lamentation dharmas, and real defiled dharmas and seeking defiled dharmas. What are the real sick dharmas and seeking sick dharmas? What are the sick dharmas? Sons and brothers are sick dharmas. Elephants, horses, cattle, sheep, slaves, money, treasures, and rice are sick and harmful dharmas. Sentient beings are attached to them, arrogantly accept them, do not see their calamities, and do not see the way out, yet take and use them. What are the old dharmas, death dharmas, sorrow and lamentation dharmas, and defiled dharmas? Sons and brothers are defiled dharmas. Elephants, horses, cattle, sheep, slaves, money, treasures, and rice are defiled and harmful dharmas. Sentient beings are attached to them, arrogantly accept them, do not see their calamities, and do not see the way out, yet take and use them. If those people seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, it is impossible for them to attain the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness. Seeking the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement, it is impossible for them to attain the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement. This is called the non-noble seeking.

佛言：「有二种求，一曰圣求，二曰非圣求。云何非圣求？有一实病法、求病法，实老法、死法、愁忧戚法，实秽污法、求秽污法。云何实病法求病法？云何病法耶？儿子、兄弟是病法也。象马、牛羊、奴婢、钱财、珍宝、米谷是病害法，众生于中触染贪著，憍慠受入，不见灾患，不见出要，而取用之。云何老法、死法、愁忧戚法，秽污法耶？儿子、兄弟是秽污法，象马、牛羊、奴婢、钱财、珍宝、米谷是秽法害法，众生于中染触贪著，憍慠受入，不见灾患，不见出要，而取用之。彼人欲求无病无上安隐涅槃，得无病无上安隐涅槃者，终无是处。求无老、无死、无愁忧戚，无秽污无上安隐涅槃，得无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃者，终无是处。是谓非圣求。

What is the noble seeking? There is one who thinks like this: 'I myself have real sick dharmas, and I seek sick dharmas without guilt. I myself have real old dharmas, death dharmas, sorrow and lamentation dharmas, and defiled dharmas, and I seek defiled dharmas without guilt. I should now seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement.' That person then seeks the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, and it is certain that they will attain the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness. Seeking the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement, it is certain that they will attain the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement.

「云何圣求耶？有一作是念：『我自实病法，无辜求病法，我自实老法、死法，愁忧戚法，秽污法，无辜求秽污法。我今宁可求无病无上安隐涅槃，求无老、无死，无愁忧戚，无秽污法无上安隐涅槃。』彼人便求无病无上安隐涅槃，得无病无上安隐涅槃者，必有是处。求无老、无死，无愁忧戚，无秽污无上安隐涅槃，得无老、无死，无愁忧戚，无秽污无上安隐涅槃者，必有是处。

When I had not yet realized the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment, I also thought like this: 'I myself have real sick dharmas, and I seek sick dharmas without guilt. I myself have real old dharmas, death dharmas, sorrow and lamentation dharmas, and defiled dharmas, and I seek defiled dharmas without guilt. Should I now seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement?' At that time, I was a young boy with pure blue hair, in the prime of my youth at the age of twenty-nine. At that time, I was extremely fond of play, adornment, and wandering. At that time, my parents wept and my relatives were unhappy. I shaved my beard and hair, put on the monastic robes, left home with utmost faith, became a homeless ascetic, and practiced the path. I protected the life of the body and made it pure, and protected the life of speech and mind and made them pure. Having accomplished this body of precepts, I wished to seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and without defilement. Therefore, I went to Ārāḍa Kālāma and asked, 'Ārāḍa! I wish to practice the holy life in your Dharma. Is that possible?' Ārāḍa replied to me, 'Virtuous one! I have no objection. If you wish to practice, then practice.' I further asked, 'Ārāḍa! How do you know, awaken, and realize this Dharma for yourself?' Ārāḍa replied to me, 'Virtuous one! I have transcended all consciousness and attained the sphere of nothingness. I dwell in accomplishment. Therefore, I know, awaken, and realize this Dharma for myself.'

「我本未觉无上正尽觉时，亦如是念：『我自实病法，无辜求病法，我自实老法、死法，愁忧戚法，秽污法，无辜求秽污法，我今宁可求无病无上安隐涅槃，求无老、无死、无愁忧戚，无秽污无上安隐涅槃耶？』我时年少童子，清净青发，盛年年二十九，尔时极多乐戏，庄饰游行。我于尔时，父母啼哭，诸亲不乐，我剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道，护身命清净，护口、意命清净。我成就此戒身已，欲求无病无上安隐涅槃，无老、无死、无愁忧戚，无秽污无上安隐涅槃故，便] cf.［丽】往阿罗罗伽罗摩所，问曰：『阿罗罗！我欲于汝法行梵行，为可尔不？』阿罗罗答我曰：『贤者！我无不可，汝欲行便行。』我复问曰：『阿罗罗！云何汝此法自知自觉自作证耶？』阿罗罗答我曰：『贤者！我度一切识处，得无所有处成就游。是故我法自知自觉自作证。』

I further thought like this: 'It is not only Ārāḍa who has this faith, I also have this faith. It is not only Ārāḍa who has this diligence, I also have this diligence. It is not only Ārāḍa who has this wisdom, I also have this wisdom. Ārāḍa knows, awakens, and realizes this Dharma for himself.' I wished to realize this Dharma, so I dwelt alone in seclusion, in an empty and peaceful place, with a mind free from negligence, practicing diligently. Having dwelt alone in seclusion, in an empty and peaceful place, with a mind free from negligence, practicing diligently, I soon attained realization of that Dharma. Having attained realization of that Dharma, I went back to Ārāḍa Kālāma and asked, 'Ārāḍa! Is this Dharma known, awakened, and realized by oneself, namely transcending the immeasurable sphere of consciousness and attaining the sphere of nothingness, dwelling in accomplishment?' Ārāḍa Kālāma replied to me, 'Virtuous one! This Dharma is known, awakened, and realized by myself, namely transcending the immeasurable sphere of consciousness and attaining the sphere of nothingness, dwelling in accomplishment.' Ārāḍa Kālāma further said to me, 'Virtuous one! This is how I realize this Dharma, and so do you. This is how you realize this Dharma, and so do I. Virtuous one! Come and lead this assembly together.' This is where Ārāḍa Kālāma was my teacher, and I was equal to him, with utmost respect, utmost offerings, and utmost joy.

「我复作是念：『不但阿罗罗独有此信，我亦有此信，不但阿罗罗独有此精进，我亦有此精进，不但阿罗罗独有此慧，我亦有此慧。阿罗罗于此法自知自觉自作证。』我欲证此法故，便独住远离、空安靖处，心无放逸，修行精勤。我独住远离、空安靖处，心无放逸，修行精勤已，不久得证彼法。证彼法已，复往诣阿罗罗加罗摩所，问曰：『阿罗罗！此法自知自觉自作证，谓度一切无量识处，得无所有处成就游耶？』阿罗罗伽罗摩答我曰：『贤者！我是法自知自觉自作证，谓度无量识处，得无所有处成就游。』阿罗罗伽罗摩复语我曰：『贤者！是为如我此法作证，汝亦然，如汝此法作证，我亦然。贤者！汝来共领此众。』是为阿罗罗伽罗摩师处，我与同等，最上恭敬、最上供养、最上欢喜。

"I further thought like this: 'This Dharma does not lead to wisdom, does not lead to awakening, and does not lead to nirvāṇa. Should I now abandon this Dharma and seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement?' I then abandoned this Dharma and sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement. Having sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement, I went to Udraka Rāmaputra and asked, 'Udraka! I wish to study in your Dharma. Is that possible?' Udraka Rāmaputra replied to me, 'Virtuous one! I have no objection. If you wish to study, then study.' I further asked, 'Udraka! Rāmaputra, what Dharma do you know, awaken, and realize for yourself?' Udraka Rāmaputra replied to me, 'Virtuous one! Transcending the sphere of nothingness, I attain the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception and dwell in accomplishment. Virtuous one! My father Rāma knows, awakens, and realizes this Dharma for himself.'"

「我复作是念：『此法不趣智，不趣觉，不趣涅槃，我今宁可舍此法，更求无病无上安隐涅槃，求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃。』我即舍此法，便求无病无上安隐涅槃，求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃已，往诣欝陀罗罗摩子所，问曰：『欝陀罗！我欲于汝法中学，为可尔不？』欝陀罗罗摩子答我曰：『贤者！我无不可，汝欲学便学。』我复问曰：『欝陀罗！汝罗摩子，自知自觉自作证何等法耶？』欝陀罗罗摩子答我曰：『贤者！度一切无所有处，得非有想非无想处成就游。贤者！我父罗摩自知自觉自作证，谓此法也。』」

I further thought like this: 'It is not only Rāma who has this faith, I also have this faith. It is not only Rāma who has this diligence, I also have this diligence. It is not only Rāma who has this wisdom, I also have this wisdom. Rāma knows, awakens, and realizes this Dharma for himself. Why should I not know, awaken, and realize this Dharma for myself?' Wishing to realize this Dharma, I dwelt alone in seclusion, in an empty and peaceful place, with a mind free from negligence, practicing diligently. Having dwelt alone in seclusion, in an empty and peaceful place, with a mind free from negligence, practicing diligently, I soon attained realization of that Dharma. Having attained realization of that Dharma, I went back to Udraka Rāmaputra and asked, 'Udraka! Does your father Rāma know, awaken, and realize this Dharma for himself, namely transcending the sphere of nothingness and attaining the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, dwelling in accomplishment?' Udraka Rāmaputra replied to me, 'Virtuous one! My father Rāma knows, awakens, and realizes this Dharma for himself, namely transcending the sphere of nothingness and attaining the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, dwelling in accomplishment.' Udraka further said to me, 'As my father Rāma realizes this Dharma, so do you. As you realize this Dharma, so does my father. Virtuous one! Come and lead this assembly together.' Udraka Rāmaputra was my teacher, and I was like a teacher to him, with utmost respect, utmost offerings, and utmost joy.

「我复作是念：『不但罗摩独有此信，我亦有此信。不但罗摩独有此精进，我亦有此精进。不但罗摩独有此慧，我亦有此慧。罗摩自知自觉自作证此法，我何故不得自知自觉自作证此法耶？』我欲证此法故，便独住远离、空安靖处，心无放逸，修行精勤。我独住远离、空安靖处，心无放逸，修行精勤已，不久得证彼法。证彼法已，复往欝陀罗罗摩子所，问曰：『欝陀罗！汝父罗摩，是法自知自觉自作证，谓度一切无所有处，得非有想非无想处成就游耶？』欝陀罗罗摩子答我曰：『贤者！我父罗摩，是法自知自觉自作证，谓度一切无所有处，得非有想非无想处成就游。』欝陀罗复语我曰：『如我父罗摩此法作证，汝亦然，如汝此法作证，我父亦然。贤者！汝来共领此众。』欝陀罗罗摩子同师处，我亦如师，最上恭敬、最上供养、最上欢喜。

I further thought like this: 'This Dharma does not lead to wisdom, does not lead to awakening, and does not lead to nirvāṇa. Should I now abandon this Dharma and seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, seek the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement?' I then abandoned this Dharma and sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement. Having sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, sought the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement, I went to the southern Elephant Peak Mountain, to the village of the Udra Brahmins, called Sīna. In the middle of that place, the mountains and forests were lush, and the Nairañjanā River was full of clear water. Having seen it, I thought like this: 'This place is very pleasant, with lush mountains and forests, and the Nairañjanā River is full of clear water. If a noble son wishes to study, he can study here. I shall also study here. Should I now study here?' I then took some grass and went to the Bodhi tree. Having arrived, I spread out a sitting mat, crossed my legs, and sat down, vowing not to get up until I attained the exhaustion of defilements. I then did not get up until I attained the exhaustion of defilements. Seeking the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness, I attained the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without sickness. Seeking the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement, I attained the supreme peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa without old age, death, sorrow and lamentation, and defilement. With birth knowledge and birth vision, the factors of the path of certainty arose. With the arising, my birth is exhausted, the holy life is established, what was to be done is done, and I will not be subject to rebirth again, knowing it as it really is.

「我复作是念：『此法不趣智，不趣觉，不趣涅槃，我今宁可舍此法，更求无病无上安隐涅槃，求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃。』我即舍此法，便求无病无上安隐涅槃，求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃已，往象顶山南，欝鞞罗梵志村，名曰斯那。于彼中地至可爱乐，山林欝茂，尼连禅河清流盈岸。我见彼已，便作是念：『此地至可爱乐，山林欝茂，尼连禅河清流盈岸，若族姓子欲有学者，可于中学，我亦当学，我今宁可于此中学。』即便持草往诣觉树，到已布下敷尼师檀，结跏趺坐，要不解坐，至得漏尽，我便不解坐，至得漏尽。我求无病无上安隐涅槃，便得无病无上安隐涅槃。求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃，便得无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃。生知生见，定道品法，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。

When I first attained supreme perfect enlightenment, I thought like this: 'For whom should I first teach the Dharma?' I further thought like this: 'Should I now first teach the Dharma to Ārāḍa Kālāma?' At that time, a deity dwelling in the sky said to me, 'Great Sage! Know that Ārāḍa Kālāma passed away seven days ago.' I also knew for myself that Ārāḍa Kālāma passed away seven days ago. I further thought like this: 'Ārāḍa Kālāma! That person has long declined and will not hear this Dharma. If he heard it, he would quickly know the Dharma and the next Dharma.

「我初觉无上正尽觉已，便作是念：『我当为谁先说法耶？』我复作是念：『我今宁可为阿罗罗加摩先说法耶？』尔时有天，住虚空中，而语我曰：『大仙人！当知阿罗罗加摩彼命终来至今七日。』我亦自知阿罗罗加摩其命终来得今七日。我复作是念：『阿罗罗加摩！彼人长衰不闻此法，若闻此者，速知法次法。』

When I first attained supreme perfect enlightenment, I thought like this: 'For whom should I first teach the Dharma?' I further thought like this: 'Should I now first teach the Dharma to Udraka Rāmaputra?' The deity again said to me, 'Great Sage! Know that Udraka Rāmaputra passed away two weeks ago.' I also knew for myself that Udraka Rāmaputra passed away two weeks ago. I further thought like this: 'Udraka Rāmaputra! That person has long declined and will not hear this Dharma. If he heard the Dharma, he would quickly know the Dharma and the next Dharma.

「我初觉无上正尽觉已，作如是念：『我当为谁先说法耶？』我复作是念：『我今宁可为欝陀罗罗摩子先说法耶？』天复住空，而语我曰：『大仙人！当知欝陀罗罗摩子命终已来二七日也。』我亦自知欝陀罗罗摩子命终已来二七日也。我复作是念：『欝陀罗罗摩子！彼人长衰不闻此法，若闻法者，速知法次法。』

When I first attained supreme perfect enlightenment, I thought like this: 'For whom should I first teach the Dharma?' I further thought like this: 'The five monks who served me in the past were very helpful. When I was practicing austerities, those five monks served me. Should I now first teach the Dharma to the five monks?' I further thought like this: 'Where are the five monks now?' Using my pure divine eye that surpasses that of humans, I saw the five monks in the Deer Park at the place where the sages dwell in Benares. I then went to the city of Kāśi in Benares, following the Bodhi tree, wearing my robe and carrying my alms bowl. At that time, the non-Buddhist Udra saw me coming from afar and said to me, 'Virtuous Gautama! Your faculties are pure, your appearance is extremely wonderful, and your facial radiance shines brightly. Virtuous Gautama! Who is your teacher? From whom did you learn the path? In whom do you have faith?' At that time, I then spoke a verse in reply to Udra:

「我初觉无上正尽觉已，作如是念：『我当为谁先说法耶？』我复作是念：『昔五比丘为我执劳，多所饶益，我苦行时，彼五比丘承事于我，我今宁可为五比丘先说法耶？』我复作是念：『昔五比丘今在何处？』我以清净天眼出过于人，见五比丘在波罗［木＊奈］仙人住处鹿野园中，我随住觉树下，摄衣持钵，往波罗［木＊奈］加尸都邑。尔时，异学优陀遥见我来，而语我曰：『贤者瞿昙！诸根清净，形色极妙，面光照耀。贤者瞿昙！师为是谁？从谁学道？为信谁法？』我于尔时即为优陀说偈答曰：

I am the most supreme and excellent one, not attached to any dharmas. With
the exhaustion of all desires, I am liberated. Who can be my teacher? There
is none equal or superior to me. I have self-awakened to the unsurpassed awakening
The Tathāgata, the teacher of gods and humans, has fully accomplished the power of universal knowledge.

「『我最上最胜，　　不著一切法，诸爱尽解脱，　　自觉谁称师。无等无有胜，　　自觉无上觉，如来天人师，　　普知成就力。』

Udra asked me, 'Virtuous Gautama! Do you claim to be superior?' I then replied to him in verse:

「优陀问我曰：『贤者瞿昙！自称胜耶？』我复以偈而答彼曰：

The superior one is like this, having attained the exhaustion of all
taints. I have destroyed all evil dharmas. Therefore, Udra, I am superior.

「『胜者如是有，　　谓得诸漏尽，我害诸恶法，　　优陀故我胜。』

Udra further asked me, 'Virtuous Gautama! Where do you wish to go?' I then replied to him in verse:

「优陀复问我曰：『贤者瞿昙！欲至何处？』我时以偈而答彼曰：

I will go to Varanasi to beat the wonderful drum of ambrosia and turn
the unsurpassed wheel of the Dharma, which has never been turned in the world.

「『我至波罗［木＊奈］，　　击妙甘露鼓，转无上法轮，　　世所未曾转。』

"Udra said to me, 'Virtuous Gautama! It may be so.' Having said this, he then left along his own wrong path. I went to the Deer Park, the abode of the sages. When the five monks saw me coming from afar, they made an agreement among themselves, saying, 'Virtuous ones! You should know that this Shakyamuni monk is coming. He is greedy and has many desires. He eats fine food and drinks, such as rice gruel, flour, butter, honey, and sesame oil. He now comes here again. You should just sit down and do not rise to welcome him. Do not pay homage to him. Leave a seat for him and do not invite him to sit. When he arrives, say to him, "Sir! If you wish to sit, you may do as you please."'" I then went to where the five monks were. At that time, the five monks were unable to bear my supreme majestic virtue. They rose from their seats. Some held their robes and bowls, some spread out a bed, some took water, and some wanted to wash my feet. I thought, 'These foolish people! How can they be so fickle? They make their own rules and then go against their original agreement.' I knew what they had done and sat on the seat spread out by the five monks.

「优陀语我曰：『贤者瞿昙！或可有是。』如是语已，即彼邪道经便还去，我自往至仙人住处鹿野园中。时，五比丘遥见我来，各相约勅而立制曰：『诸贤！当知此沙门瞿昙来，多欲多求，食妙饮食，好粳粮饭，及𪎊酥蜜，麻油涂体，今复来至，汝等但坐，慎莫起迎，亦莫作礼，豫留一座，莫请令坐。到已语曰：「卿！欲坐者，自随所欲。」』我时往至五比丘所。时，五比丘于我不堪极妙威德，即从坐起，有持衣钵者，有敷床者，有取水者，欲洗足者，我作是念：『此愚痴人！何无牢固？自立制度还违本要。』我知彼已，坐五比丘所敷之座。

At that time, the five monks called me by my surname and addressed me as 'sir'. I said to them, 'Five monks! I am the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. You should not call me by my original surname or address me as "sir". Why is that? I sought the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from illness, and I have attained the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from illness. I sought the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and defilement, and I have attained the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and defilement. With birth, knowledge, and insight, I have attained the fixed path of the factors of the path. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and there will be no more future lives, known as it truly is.' They said to me, 'Sir Gautama! You practiced and followed the path like this, and you practiced austerities like this, but you still could not attain the superior Dharma, the distinction of the holy knowledge and holy vision. How much less so now, with your many desires and many demands, eating fine food and drink, such as rice gruel, flour, butter, honey, and sesame oil applied to the body?' I said to them again, 'Five monks! Did you see my faculties purified like this, with radiant light shining?' At that time, the five monks again replied to me, 'We did not see your faculties purified with radiant light shining. Sir Gautama! Now your faculties are purified, your form is extremely wonderful, and your facial radiance shines.

「时，五比丘呼我姓字，及卿于我。我语彼曰：『五比丘！我如来、无所著、正尽觉，汝等莫称我本姓字，亦莫卿我。所以者何？我求无病无上安隐涅槃，得无病无上安隐涅槃。我求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃，得无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃。生知生见，定道品法，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。』彼语我曰：『卿瞿昙！本如是行，如是道迹，如是苦行，尚不能得人上法差降圣知圣见，况复今日多欲多求，食妙饮食，好粳粮饭，及𪎊酥蜜，麻油涂体耶？』我复语曰：『五比丘！汝等本时见我如是诸根清净，光明照耀耶？』时，五比丘复答我曰：『本不见卿诸根清净，光明照耀。卿瞿昙！今诸根清净，形色极妙，面光照耀。』

At that time, I immediately said to them, 'Five monks! You should know that there are two extremes in practice that those who follow the path should not learn: one is to indulge in sensual pleasures and lowly deeds, which are practiced by ordinary people; the other is to torment oneself and suffer, which is not the way of the sages seeking the Dharma, and is not conducive to meaning. Five monks! Abandoning these two extremes, there is the middle way that leads to illumination, wisdom, and attainment of concentration, and to mastery, leading to wisdom, enlightenment, and nirvāṇa. It is the eightfold noble path, namely right view, and so on up to right concentration. This is called the eight.' I intended to follow the teachings and guide the five monks, with two of them begging for alms and three bringing back the food, enough for six people to eat; or with three begging for alms and two bringing back the food, enough for six people to eat. I taught and guided them in this way, seeking the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from illness, and I attained the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from illness. Seeking the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and defilement, I attained the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa free from old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and defilement. With birth, knowledge, and insight, I attained the fixed path of the factors of the path. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and there will be no more future lives, known as it truly is.

「我于尔时即告彼曰：『五比丘！当知有二边行，诸为道者所不当学：一曰著欲乐下贱业，凡人所行；二曰自烦自苦，非贤圣求法，无义相应。五比丘！舍此二边，有取中道，成明成智，成就于定，而得自在，趣智趣觉，趣于涅槃，谓八正道，正见乃至正定，是谓为八。』意欲随顺教五比丘，教化二人，三人乞食，三人持食来，足六人食；教化三人，二人乞食，二人持食来，足六人食。我如是教，如是化彼，求无病无上安隐涅槃，得无病无上安隐涅槃。求无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃，得无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃。生知生见，定道品法，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。

Then the World-Honored One again said to them, 'Five monks! There are five kinds of sensual pleasures that are desirable, enjoyable, pleasing, and worth remembering, associated with good desires. What are the five? The eye knows forms, the ear knows sounds, the nose knows smells, the tongue knows tastes, and the body knows tactile objects. Five monks! The ignorant ordinary person, who is not well-versed, does not see good friends, does not know the holy Dharma, and does not practice the holy Dharma, is touched by defilement, greedily attached, arrogantly accepts, does not see the dangers, does not see the way out, and uses them. One should know that they follow Mara, make themselves Mara, fall into Mara's hands, are bound by Mara's net, bound by Mara's snare, and do not escape Mara's bondage. Five monks! It is like a wild deer bound by a snare. One should know that they follow the hunter, make themselves the hunter, fall into the hunter's hands, are bound by the hunter's net, and when the hunter comes, they cannot escape. So it is, five monks! The ignorant ordinary person, who is not well-versed, does not see good friends, does not know the holy Dharma, and does not practice the holy Dharma. They are touched by defilement, greedily attached, arrogantly accept, do not see the dangers, do not see the way out, and use these five kinds of sensual pleasures. One should know that they follow Mara, make themselves Mara, fall into Mara's hands, are bound by Mara's net, bound by Mara's bondage, and do not escape Mara's bondage.

「于是，世尊复告彼曰：『五比丘！有五欲功德可爱、可乐、可意所念，善欲相应。云何为五？眼知色，耳知声，鼻知香，舌知味，身知触。五比丘！愚痴凡夫而不多闻，不见善友，不知圣法，不御圣法，彼触染贪著，憍慠受入，不见灾患，不见出要，而取用之。当知彼随弊魔，自作弊魔，堕弊魔手，为魔网缠，魔羂所缠，不脱魔缠。五比丘！犹如野鹿，为缠所缠，当知彼随猎师，自作猎师，堕猎师手，为猎师网缠，猎师来已，不能得脱。如是，五比丘！愚痴凡夫而不多闻，不见善友，不知圣法，不御圣法。彼于此五欲功德触染贪著，憍慠受入，不见灾患，不见出要，而取用之。当知彼随弊魔，自作弊魔，堕弊魔手，为魔网缠，魔缠所缠，不脱魔缠。

Five monks! The well-versed noble disciple sees good friends, knows the holy Dharma, and practices the holy Dharma. They are not touched by, defiled by, greedy for, or attached to these five kinds of sensual pleasures. They are not arrogant, do not accept them, see the dangers, see the way out, and use them. One should know that they do not follow Mara, do not make themselves Mara, do not fall into Mara's hands, are not bound by Mara's net, are not bound by Mara's bondage, and are liberated from Mara's bondage. Five monks! It is like a wild deer that is freed from a snare. One should know that they do not follow the hunter, do not make themselves the hunter, do not fall into the hunter's hands, are not bound by the hunter's net, and when the hunter comes, they are able to escape. So it is, five monks! The well-versed noble disciple sees good friends, knows the holy Dharma, and practices the holy Dharma. They are not touched by, defiled by, greedy for, or attached to these five kinds of sensual pleasures. They do not see arrogance, do not accept them, see the dangers, see the way out, and use them. One should know that they do not follow Mara, do not make themselves Mara, do not fall into Mara's hands, are not bound by Mara's net, are not bound by Mara's bondage, and are liberated from Mara's bondage.

「『五比丘！多闻圣弟子见善知识，而知圣法，又御圣法，彼于此五欲功德，不触、不染、不贪、不著，亦不憍慠、不受入，见灾患，见出要，而取用之。当知彼不随弊魔，不自作魔，不堕魔手，不为魔网所缠，不为魔缠所缠，便解脱魔缠。五比丘！犹如野鹿得脱于缠，当知彼不随猎师，不自在猎师，不堕猎师手，不为猎师网所缠，猎师来已，则能得脱。如是，五比丘！多闻圣弟子见善知识而知圣法，又御圣法，彼于此五欲功德，不触、不染、不贪、不著，亦不见憍慠、不受入，见灾患，见出要，而取用之。当知彼不随弊魔，不自在魔，不堕魔手，不为魔网所缠，不为魔缠所缠，便解脱魔缠。

Five monks! When a Tathāgata appears in the world, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, a one who is perfect in knowledge and conduct, a Sugata, a knower of the world, an unsurpassed trainer of those who can be trained, a teacher of gods and men, a Buddha, a Blessed One, he abandons, up to the five hindrances, defilements of the mind that weaken wisdom. He is detached, having abandoned evil and unwholesome states, and attains the fourth meditation, which is peaceful and sublime, and dwells in it. His mind is thus concentrated, purified, bright, unblemished, free from defilement, malleable, workable, steady, and attained to imperturbability. He dwells contemplating arising phenomena in the body, and he dwells contemplating vanishing phenomena in the body. He dwells contemplating both arising and vanishing phenomena in the body. Or, mindfulness that 'there is a body' is established in him just to the extent necessary for knowledge and awareness. He dwells detached, and does not cling to anything in the world. He understands thus: 'Such is form, such is the arising of form, such is the passing away of form. Such is feeling... Such is perception... Such are volitional formations... Such is consciousness, such is the arising of consciousness, such is the passing away of consciousness.' He understands thus: 'Such is this whole mass of suffering, such is the arising of this whole mass of suffering, such is the passing away of this whole mass of suffering.' His desire and passion for form is abandoned, his desire and passion for feeling... perception... volitional formations... consciousness is abandoned. He is detached, and does not cling to anything in the world. He understands thus: 'This is peaceful, this is sublime, that is, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all attachments, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nirvāṇa.' His mind is thus liberated from the pollutants, without attachment, detached, liberated from the pollutants, without attachment, detached.

「『五比丘！若时如来出兴于世，无所著、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、众祐，彼断，乃至五盖、心秽、慧羸，离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游。彼如是定心清净，无秽无烦，柔软善住，得不动心，修学漏尽智通作证。彼知此苦如真，知此苦习、知此苦灭、知此苦灭道如真。知此漏如真，知此漏习、知此漏灭、知此漏灭道如真。彼如是知、如是见，欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱。解脱已便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。彼于尔时自在行，自在住、自在坐、自在卧。所以者何？彼自见无量恶不善法尽。是故彼自在行、自在住、自在坐、自在卧。

Five monks! It is like a place without people or affairs, where wild deer roam freely, live freely, lie down freely, and sleep freely. Why is that? Because those wild deer are not within the hunter's domain. Therefore they roam freely, live freely, lie down freely, and sleep freely. So it is, five monks! A monk, having exhausted the pollutants, attained the unpolluted liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, personally realized it, and abides in it. He knows as it really is: 'Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this mundane state.' At that time he roams freely, lives freely, sits freely, and lies down freely. Why is that? Because he has seen the exhaustion of immeasurable evil and unwholesome states. Therefore he roams freely, lives freely, sits freely, and lies down freely. Five monks! This is called the unsurpassed liberation without remainder. This is called the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of the absence of disease. This is called the unsurpassed peace and tranquility of the absence of old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and defilement.

「『五比丘！犹如无事无人民处，彼有野鹿，自在行、自在住、自在伏、自在卧。所以者何？彼野鹿不在猎师境界。是故自在行、自在住、自在伏、自在卧。如是，五比丘！比丘漏尽得无漏，心解脱、慧解脱，自知自觉自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。彼于尔时自在行、自在住、自在坐、自在卧。所以者何？彼自见无量恶不善法尽。是故彼自在行、自在住、自在坐、自在卧。五比丘！是说无余解脱，是说无病无上安隐涅槃，是说无老、无死、无愁忧戚、无秽污无上安隐涅槃。』」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced them.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
