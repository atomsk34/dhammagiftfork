MĀ 205

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 17 晡利多品

205. （二〇五）五下分结经

Parallel to: MN 64

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus, "I have previously spoken of the five lower fetters. Do you remember them?" The bhikṣus remained silent and did not reply.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我曾说五下分结，汝等受持耶？」诸比丘默然不答。

The World-Honored One again asked the bhikṣus three times, "I have previously spoken of the five lower fetters. Do you remember them?" The bhikṣus also remained silent and did not reply three times.

世尊复再三告诸比丘：「我曾说五下分结，汝等受持耶？」诸比丘亦再三默然不答。

At that time, Venerable Mālatīputra was in the assembly. Then Venerable Mālatīputra rose from his seat, arranged his robe over one shoulder, joined his palms towards the Buddha, and said, "World-Honored One, you have previously spoken of the five lower fetters, and I remember them.

尔时，尊者鬘童子在彼众中。于是，尊者鬘童子即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊曾说五下分结，我受持之。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Mālatīputra! I have previously spoken of the five lower fetters. Do you remember them?"

世尊问曰：「鬘童子！我曾说五下分结，汝受持耶？」

Venerable Mālatīputra replied, "The World-Honored One has previously spoken of the first lower fetter, which I remember. Desire, anger, holding wrong views, holding wrong precepts, and doubt - the World-Honored One has spoken of the fifth lower fetter, which I remember."

尊者鬘童子答曰：「世尊曾说初下分结，是我受持，欲、恚、身见、戒取、疑，世尊说第五下分结，是我受持。」

The World-Honored One rebuked him, saying, "Mālatīputra! How do you remember the five lower fetters I spoke of? Mālatīputra! From which mouth did you remember the five lower fetters I spoke of? Mālatīputra! Are you not being criticized by many non-Buddhist teachers using the analogy of an infant? Mālatīputra! An infant is soft and lying on its back, without any desire, let alone being entangled by desire. Yet, because of its nature, it is said to have the desire affliction. Mālatīputra! An infant is soft and lying on its back, without any sentient being, let alone being entangled by anger. Yet, because of its nature, it is said to have the anger affliction. Mālatīputra! An infant is soft and lying on its back, without any self, let alone being entangled by the view of self. Yet, because of its nature, it is said to have the view of self affliction. Mālatīputra! An infant is soft and lying on its back, without any precepts, let alone being entangled by the view of precepts. Yet, because of its nature, it is said to have the view of precepts affliction. Mālatīputra! An infant is soft and lying on its back, without any Dharma, let alone being entangled by doubt. Yet, because of its nature, it is said to have the doubt affliction. Mālatīputra! Are you not being criticized by many non-Buddhist teachers using this analogy of an infant?" Thereupon, Venerable Mālatīputra, having been rebuked by the World-Honored One face to face, was inwardly distressed, lowered his head in silence, lost his eloquence, and was as if waiting for something.

世尊诃曰：「鬘童子！汝云何受持我说五下分结？鬘童子！汝从何口受持我说五下分结耶？鬘童子！非为众多异学来，以婴孩童子责数喻诘责汝耶？鬘童子！婴孩幼小，柔软仰眠，意无欲想，况复欲心缠住耶？然彼性使故，说欲使。鬘童子！婴孩幼小，柔软仰眠，无众生想，况复恚心缠住耶？然彼性使故，说恚使。鬘童子！婴孩幼小，柔软仰眠，无自身想，况复身见心缠住耶？然彼性使故，说身见使。鬘童子！婴孩幼小，柔软仰眠，无有戒想，况复戒取心缠住耶？然彼性使故，说戒取使。鬘童子！婴孩幼小，柔软仰眠，无有法想，况复疑心缠住耶？然彼性使故，说疑使。鬘童子！非为众多异学来，以此婴孩童子责数喻诘责汝耶？」于是，尊者鬘童子为世尊面诃责已，内怀忧戚，低头默然，失辩无言，如有所伺。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having rebuked Venerable Mālatīputra face to face, remained silent. Then Venerable Ānanda, standing behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha, joined his palms towards the Buddha and said, "World-Honored One! Now is the time. Well-Gone One! Now is the time. If the World-Honored One would speak to the monks about the five lower fetters, the monks would listen well and remember them well from the World-Honored One.

彼时，世尊面前诃责鬘童子已，默然而住。尔时，尊者阿难立世尊后，执扇扇佛。于是，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！今正是时。善逝！今正是时。若世尊为诸比丘说五下分结者，诸比丘从世尊闻已，善受善持。」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! Listen carefully and keep it in mind."

世尊告曰：「阿难！谛听，善思念之。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "Yes, I will listen to the teaching."

尊者阿难白曰：「唯然。当受教听。」

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! There are some who are bound by desire. When a desire arises, they do not know how to let go of it as it truly is. Not knowing how to let go of it as it truly is, their desire becomes more intense and cannot be controlled or eliminated. This is a lower fetter. Ānanda! There are some who are bound by anger. When an angry thought arises, they do not know how to let go of it as it truly is. Not knowing how to let go of it as it truly is, their anger becomes more intense and cannot be controlled or eliminated. This is a lower fetter. Ānanda! There are some who are bound by the view of the self. When a thought of the view of the self arises, they do not know how to let go of it as it truly is. Not knowing how to let go of it as it truly is, their view of the self becomes more intense and cannot be controlled or eliminated. This is a lower fetter. Ānanda! There are some who are bound by the grasping of rules and rituals. When a thought of grasping rules and rituals arises, they do not know how to let go of it as it truly is. Not knowing how to let go of it as it truly is, their grasping of rules and rituals becomes more intense and cannot be controlled or eliminated. This is a lower fetter. Ānanda! There are some who are bound by doubt. When a doubtful thought arises, they do not know how to let go of it as it truly is. Not knowing how to let go of it as it truly is, their doubt becomes more intense and cannot be controlled or eliminated. This is a lower fetter.

佛言：「阿难！或有一为欲所缠，欲心生已，不知舍如真，彼不知舍如真已，欲转炽盛，不可制除，是下分结。阿难！或有一为恚所缠，恚心生已，不知舍如真，彼不知舍如真已，恚转炽盛，不可制除，是下分结。阿难！或有一为身见所缠，身见心生已，不知舍如真，彼不知舍如真已，身见转盛，不可制除，是下分结。阿难！或有一为戒取所缠，戒取心生已，不知舍如真，彼不知舍如真已，戒取转盛，不可制除，是下分结。阿难！或有一为疑所缠，疑心生已，不知舍如真，彼不知舍如真已，疑转炽盛，不可制除，是下分结。

Ānanda! If one relies on the path and traces to sever the five lower fetters, it is impossible for one to sever the five lower fetters without relying on this path and traces. Ānanda! It is like a person who wants to obtain fruit. In order to obtain fruit, he enters the forest with an axe. He sees a tree with roots, trunk, branches, leaves, and fruit. It is impossible for him to obtain fruit by not cutting the roots and trunk. Similarly, Ānanda! If one relies on the path and traces to sever the five lower fetters, it is impossible for one to sever the five lower fetters without relying on this path and traces. Ānanda! If one relies on the path and traces to sever the five lower fetters, it is possible for one to sever the five lower fetters by relying on this path and traces. Ānanda! It is like a person who wants to obtain fruit. In order to obtain fruit, he enters the forest with an axe. He sees a tree with roots, trunk, branches, leaves, and fruit. It is possible for him to obtain fruit by cutting the roots and trunk. Similarly, Ānanda! If one relies on the path and traces to sever the five lower fetters, it is possible for one to sever the five lower fetters by relying on this path and traces.

「阿难！若依道、依迹，断五下分结；彼不依此道、不依此迹，断五下分结者，终无是处。阿难！犹如有人欲得求实，为求实故，持斧入林，彼人见树成就根、茎、枝、叶及实，彼人不截根、茎，得实归者，终无是处。如是。阿难！若依道、依迹，断五下分结；不依此道、不依此迹，断五下分结者，终无是处。阿难！若依道、依迹，断五下分结，彼依此道、依此迹，断五下分结者，必有是处。阿难！犹如有人欲得求实，为求实故，持斧入林，彼人见树成就根、茎、枝、叶及实，彼人截根、茎，得实归者，必有是处。如是。阿难！若依道、依迹，断五下分结，依此道、依此迹，断五下分结者，必有是处。

Ānanda! What path and traces are relied upon to sever the five lower fetters? Ānanda! There is one who is not bound by desire. If a desire arises, he knows how to let go of it as it truly is. Having known how to let go of it as it truly is, that desire is extinguished. Ānanda! There is one who is not bound by anger. If an angry thought arises, he knows how to let go of it as it truly is. Having known how to let go of it as it truly is, that angry thought is extinguished. Ānanda! There is one who is not bound by the view of the self. If a thought of the view of the self arises, he knows how to let go of it as it truly is. Having known how to let go of it as it truly is, that view of the self is extinguished. Ānanda! There is one who is not bound by the grasping of rules and rituals. If a thought of grasping rules and rituals arises, he knows how to let go of it as it truly is. Having known how to let go of it as it truly is, that grasping of rules and rituals is extinguished. Ānanda! There is one who is not bound by doubt. If a doubtful thought arises, he knows how to let go of it as it truly is. Having known how to let go of it as it truly is, that doubt is extinguished. Ānanda! This is the path and traces relied upon to sever the five lower fetters.

「阿难！依何道、依何迹，断五下分结？阿难！或有一不为欲所缠，若生欲缠，即知舍如真，彼知舍如真已，彼欲缠便灭。阿难！或有一不为恚所缠，若生恚缠，即知舍如真，彼知舍如真已，彼恚缠便灭。阿难！或有一不为身见所缠，若生身见缠，即知舍如真，彼知舍如真已，彼身见缠便灭。阿难！或有一不为戒取所缠，若生戒取缠，即知舍如真，彼知舍如真已，彼戒取便灭。阿难！或有一不为疑所缠，若生疑缠，即知舍如真，彼知舍如真已，彼疑缠便灭。阿难！依此道、依此迹，断五下分结。

Ānanda! It is like the Ganges River, whose waters overflow its banks. If someone comes to the other shore and wants to cross the river, they think, 'The Ganges River is overflowing its banks. I have something to do on the other shore and want to cross. But my body is not strong enough to safely float me to the other shore.' Ānanda! Know that this person is weak. Similarly, Ānanda! If someone is aware of the cessation and nirvāṇa, but their mind is not inclined towards it and is not pure, and they do not abide in liberation, Ānanda! Know that this person is like the weak person who is not strong. Ānanda! It is like the Ganges River, whose waters overflow its banks. If someone comes to the other shore and wants to cross the river, they think, 'The Ganges River is overflowing its banks. I have something to do on the other shore and want to cross. My body is now strong enough to safely float me to the other shore.' Ānanda! Know that this person is strong. Similarly, Ānanda! If someone is aware of the cessation and nirvāṇa, their mind is inclined towards it and is pure, and they abide in liberation. Ānanda! Know that this person is like the strong person.

「阿难！犹恒伽河，其水溢岸。若有人来彼岸，有事欲得度河，彼作是念：『此恒伽河，其水溢岸，我于彼岸有事欲度，身无有力令我安隐浮至彼岸。』阿难！当知彼人无力。如是，阿难！若有人觉、灭、涅槃，其心不向而不清净，不住解脱。阿难！当知此人如彼羸人无有力也。阿难！犹恒伽河，其水溢岸。若有人来彼岸，有事欲得度河，彼作是念：『此恒伽河，其水溢岸，我于彼岸有事欲度，身今有力令我安隐浮至彼岸。』阿难！当知彼人有力。如是，阿难！若有人觉、灭、涅槃，心向清净而住解脱。阿难！当知此人如彼力人。

Ānanda! It is like a mountain stream, very deep and wide, with a long, swift current, carrying much driftwood. There is no boat or bridge there. If someone comes to the other shore and has something to do, they want to cross. When they want to cross, they think, 'This mountain stream is very deep and wide, with a long, swift current, carrying much driftwood. There is no boat or bridge there to cross. I have something to do on the other shore and want to cross. What expedient means can I use to safely reach the other shore?' They further think, 'I should gather grass and wood on this shore, bind them into a raft, and ride it across.' They then gather grass and wood on the shore, bind them into a raft, ride it across, and safely reach the other shore. Similarly, Ānanda! If a bhikṣu clings to and relies on disenchantment, abides in disenchantment, and stops evil bodily actions, his mind enters into detachment and samādhi, being free from desire and evil and unwholesome dharmas, with initial and sustained thought, joy and happiness, he attains the first dhyāna and abides in it. Relying on this, he observes the arising and perishing of thought. Having observed the arising and perishing of thought, he abides there, necessarily attaining the exhaustion of taints. If he abides there and does not attain the exhaustion of taints, he will necessarily advance to attain a place of rest.

「阿难！犹如山水，甚深极广，长流驶疾，多有所漂，其中无船，亦无桥梁。或有人来彼岸，有事则便求度，彼求度时而作是念：『今此山水甚深极广，长流驶疾，多有所漂，其中无船，亦无桥梁而可度者。我于彼岸有事欲度，当以何方便令我安隐至彼岸耶？』复作是念：『我今宁可于此岸边收聚草木，缚作椑栰，乘之而度。』彼便岸边收聚草木，缚作椑栰，乘之而度，安隐至彼。如是，阿难！若有比丘攀缘厌离，依于厌离，住于厌离，止息身恶故，心入离、定故，离欲、离恶不善之法，有觉、有观，离生喜、乐，得初禅成就游。彼依此处，观觉兴衰，彼依此处，观觉兴衰已，住彼必得漏尽，设住彼不得漏尽者，必当升进得止息处。

How does one advance to attain a place of rest? He stops thinking and reflection, internally pacifies and unifies his mind, without thinking or reflection, gives rise to samādhi, and experiences joy and happiness, attaining the second dhyāna and abiding in it. Relying on this, he observes the arising and perishing of thought. Having observed the arising and perishing of thought, he abides there, necessarily attaining the exhaustion of taints. If he abides there and does not attain the exhaustion of taints, he will necessarily advance to attain a place of rest. How does one advance to attain a place of rest? He is free from the desire for joy, abandons it, and dwells without seeking, with right mindfulness and right knowledge, experiencing bodily happiness. That is said by the noble ones, the noble ones' abandonment, mindfulness, happiness, and dwelling in emptiness, attaining the third dhyāna and abiding in it. Relying on this, he observes the arising and perishing of thought. Having observed the arising and perishing of thought, he abides there, necessarily attaining the exhaustion of taints. If he abides there and does not attain the exhaustion of taints, he will necessarily advance to attain a place of rest. How does one advance to attain a place of rest? He stops pain and pleasure, and the disappearance of joy and sorrow, neither painful nor pleasant, with equanimity, mindfulness, and purity, attaining the fourth dhyāna and abiding in it. Relying on this, he observes the arising and perishing of thought. Having observed the arising and perishing of thought, he abides there, necessarily attaining the exhaustion of taints. If he abides there and does not attain the exhaustion of taints, he will necessarily advance to attain a place of rest.

「云何升进得止息处？彼觉、观已息，内靖、一心，无觉、无观，定生喜、乐，得第二禅成就游。彼依此处，观觉兴衰，彼依此处，观觉兴衰已，住彼必得漏尽，设住彼不得漏尽者，必当升进得止息处。云何升进得止息处？彼离于喜欲，舍无求游，正念正智而身觉乐。谓圣所说，圣所舍、念、乐住、空，得第三禅成就游。彼依此处，观觉兴衰，彼依此处，观觉兴衰已，住彼必得漏尽，设住彼不得漏尽者，必当升进得止息处。云何升进得止息处？彼乐灭、苦灭，喜、忧本已灭，不苦不乐、舍、念、清净，得第四禅成就游。彼依此处，观觉兴衰，彼依此处，观觉兴衰已，住彼必得漏尽，设住彼不得漏尽者，必当升进得止息处。

How does one advance to attain a place of rest? He transcends all perceptions of form, eliminates perceptions of obstruction, does not think of various perceptions, and abides in the sphere of infinite space. He relies on this to observe the arising and perishing of thought. Having observed the arising and perishing of thought, he abides there, necessarily attaining the exhaustion of taints. If he abides there and does not attain the exhaustion of taints, he will necessarily advance to attain a place of rest. How does one advance to attain a place of rest? He transcends the entire sphere of infinite space, and abides in the sphere of infinite consciousness. He relies on this to observe the arising and perishing of thought. Having observed the arising and perishing of thought, he abides there, necessarily attaining the exhaustion of taints. If he abides there and does not attain the exhaustion of taints, he will necessarily advance to attain a place of rest. How does one advance to attain a place of rest? He transcends the entire sphere of infinite consciousness, and abides in the sphere of nothingness. If he has any perception, it may be pleasant, painful, or neither painful nor pleasant. He observes that this perception is impermanent, observes its arising and perishing, observes detachment from it, observes its cessation, observes its abandonment, and observes its relinquishment. Having observed this perception as impermanent, observed its arising and perishing, observed detachment from it, observed its cessation, observed its abandonment, and observed its relinquishment, he no longer accepts this world. Having no longer accepted this world, he is no longer afraid. Because he is no longer afraid, he attains parinirvāṇa. His births are exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and he will not be reborn again, knowing it as it really is.

「云何升进得止息处？彼度一切色想，灭有碍想，不念若干想，无量空，是无量处成就游，彼依此处，观觉兴衰，彼依此处、观觉兴衰已，住彼必得漏尽，设住彼不得漏尽者，必当升进得止息处。云何升进得止息处？彼度一切无量空处，无量识，是无量识处成就游。彼依此处，观觉兴衰，彼依此处，观觉兴衰已，住彼必得漏尽，设住彼不得漏尽者，必当升进得止息处。云何升进得止息处？彼度一切无量识处，无所有，是] cf.［丽】无所有处成就游。彼若有所觉，或乐或苦，或不苦不乐。彼观此觉无常，观兴衰、观无欲、观灭、观断、观舍。彼如是观此觉无常，观兴衰、观无欲、观灭、观断、观舍已，便不受此世，不受此世已，便不恐怖，因不恐怖，便般涅槃，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。

"Not far from the village, there is a large plantain tree. If a person takes an axe to break the plantain tree, breaking it into pieces, breaking it into ten parts, or a hundred parts. Breaking it into ten parts, or a hundred parts, then tearing off the leaves, one cannot see the joints, let alone the fruit. Ānanda! In this way, if a bhikṣu has any perception, whether pleasant, painful, or neither painful nor pleasant, he observes that this perception is impermanent, observes its arising and perishing, observes detachment from it, observes its cessation, observes its abandonment, and observes its relinquishment. Having observed this perception as impermanent, observed its arising and perishing, observed detachment from it, observed its cessation, observed its abandonment, and observed its relinquishment, he no longer accepts this world. Having no longer accepted this world, he is no longer afraid. Because he is no longer afraid, he attains parinirvāṇa. His births are exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and he will not be reborn again, knowing it as it really is."

「犹去村不远，有大芭蕉，若人持斧破芭蕉树，破作片，破为十分，或作百分。破为十分，或作百分已，便擗叶叶，不见彼节，况复实耶？阿难！如是比丘若有所觉，或乐或苦，或不苦不乐，彼观此觉无常，观兴衰、观无欲、观灭、观断、观舍。彼如是观此觉无常，观兴衰、观无欲、观灭、观断、观舍已，便不受此世，不受此世已，便不恐怖，因不恐怖已，便般涅槃，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。」

Then Venerable Ānanda, with palms joined towards the Buddha, said: "World-Honored One! It is truly amazing! The World-Honored One has established the basis for the bhikṣus to rely on, speaking of abandoning the taints and transcending the taints. However, the bhikṣus have not quickly attained the unsurpassed, namely the complete end of suffering."

于是，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！甚奇！甚特！世尊为诸比丘依依立依，说舍离漏，说过度漏，然诸比丘不速得无上，谓毕究竟尽。」

The World-Honored One said: "So it is, Ānanda! So it is, Ānanda! It is truly amazing! I have established the basis for the bhikṣus to rely on, speaking of abandoning the taints and transcending the taints. However, the bhikṣus have not quickly attained the unsurpassed, namely the complete end of suffering. Why is that? Because there are differences among people, the cultivation of the path has distinctions of refinement and coarseness. Because the cultivation of the path has distinctions of refinement and coarseness, there are differences among people. Ānanda! Therefore, I say there are differences among people."

世尊告曰：「如是。阿难！如是。阿难！甚奇！甚特！我为诸比丘依依立依，说舍离漏，说过度漏，然诸比丘不速得无上，谓毕究竟尽。所以者何？人有胜如故，修道便有精麤，修道有精麤故，人便有胜如。阿难！是故我说人有胜如。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
