MĀ 215

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 18 例品

215. （二一五）第一得经

Parallel to: AN 10.29

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "If there is a realm of King Prasenajit of Kośala, where his teachings reach, King Prasenajit of Kośala is the foremost there. King Prasenajit of Kośala undergoes changes and differences. The well-versed noble disciple, observing thus, becomes disenchanted with that. Having become disenchanted, he does not even desire the foremost, let alone the inferior. Namely, the realm of the sun and moon, where their light shines. The directions illuminated are the thousand worlds. This thousand worlds has a thousand suns, a thousand moons, a thousand Uttarakurus, a thousand Jambudvīpas, a thousand Pūrvavidehas, a thousand Avaragodānīyas, a thousand Sumerus, a thousand Cāturmahārājakāyikas, a thousand Cāturmahārājakāyika princes, a thousand Trāyastriṃśas, a thousand Śakras, a thousand Yāmas, a thousand Yāma princes, a thousand Tuṣitas, a thousand Tuṣita princes, a thousand Nirmāṇaratis, a thousand Nirmāṇarati princes, a thousand Paranirmitavaśavartins, a thousand Paranirmitavaśavartin princes, a thousand Brahmā worlds and a thousand separate Brahmas. Among them, there is one great Brahmā, the honored creator, the father of beings, who has been, will be, and is. That great Brahmā undergoes changes and differences. The well-versed noble disciple, observing thus, becomes disenchanted with that. Having become disenchanted, he does not even desire the foremost, let alone the inferior. Later, this world decays and is destroyed. When this world decays and is destroyed, beings are born in the Ābhāsvara heaven. There, they have form, are born from the mind, are complete in all limbs and joints, with no deficiencies in the faculties. They subsist on joy, have pure physical appearance, and their own light illuminates. They fly through the sky, abiding there for a long time. The Ābhāsvara heaven undergoes changes and differences. The well-versed noble disciple, observing thus, becomes disenchanted with that. Having become disenchanted, he does not even desire the foremost, let alone the inferior.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若拘萨罗王波斯匿所有境界，教令所及，彼中拘萨罗王波斯匿最为第一。拘萨罗王波斯匿者，变易有异，多闻圣弟子如是观则厌彼，厌彼已，尚不欲第一，况复下贱？所谓日月境界，光明所照，所照诸方，谓千世界。此千世界，有千日、千月、千弗于逮、千阎浮洲、千拘陀尼洲、千欝单越洲、千须弥山、千四大王天、千四天王子、千三十三天、千释天因陀罗、千焰摩天、千须焰摩天子，千兜率哆天、千兜率哆天子，千化乐天、千善化乐天子、千他化乐天、千自在天子，千梵世界及千别梵。彼中有一梵大梵，富祐作化尊，造众生父，已有当有。彼大梵者，变易有异。多闻圣弟子如是观则厌彼，厌彼已，尚不欲第一，况复下贱？后时此世败坏，此世败坏时，众生生晃昱天中。彼中有色乘意生，具足一切，支节不减，诸根不坏，以喜为食，形色清净，自身光照，飞乘虚空，住彼久远。晃昱天者，变易有异。多闻圣弟子如是观则厌彼，厌彼已，尚不欲第一，况复下贱？

"Furthermore, there are four kinds of thoughts. A bhikṣu thinks small, thinks large, thinks immeasurable, and thinks of nothing. Sentient beings delight in such thoughts and understanding. Those who undergo changes and differences, the well-versed noble disciple, observing thus, becomes disenchanted with that. Having become disenchanted, he does not even desire the foremost, let alone the inferior. "Furthermore, there are eight kinds of elimination. What are the eight? A bhikṣu, having internal form-perception, externally observes form, both good and bad form, in small quantity. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the first kind of elimination.' Furthermore, a bhikṣu, having internal form-perception, externally observes form, both good and bad form, in immeasurable quantity. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the second kind of elimination.' Furthermore, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, both good and bad form, in small quantity. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the third kind of elimination.' Furthermore, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, both good and bad form, in immeasurable quantity. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the fourth kind of elimination.'

「复次，有四想，有比丘想小、想大、想无量、想无所有，众生如是乐想意解者，变易有异。多闻圣弟子如是观则厌彼，厌彼已，尚不欲第一，况复下贱？复次有八除处。云何为八？比丘，内有色想，外观色，少善色恶色，彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第一除处。』复次，比丘！内有色想，外观色，无量善色恶色，彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第二除处。』复次，比丘！内无色想，外观色，少善色恶色，彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第三除处。』复次，比丘！内无色想，外观色，无量善色恶色，彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第四除处。』

"Furthermore, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, blue in color, blue in appearance, and blue in radiance. Like blue lotus flowers, blue in color, blue in appearance, and blue in radiance. Like well-polished and lustrous cloth made of cotton, blue in color, blue in appearance, and blue in radiance. In this way, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, blue in color, blue in appearance, and blue in radiance, immeasurable and immeasurable, with a pure mind, a moist mind, delighting in it and not disliking it. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the fifth kind of elimination.' "Furthermore, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, yellow in color, yellow in appearance, and yellow in radiance. Like the flowers of the pinda-gālava tree, yellow in color, yellow in appearance, and yellow in radiance. Like well-polished and lustrous cloth made of cotton, yellow in color, yellow in appearance, and yellow in radiance. In this way, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, yellow in color, yellow in appearance, and yellow in radiance, immeasurable and immeasurable, with a pure mind, a moist mind, delighting in it and not disliking it. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the sixth kind of elimination.'

「复次，比丘！内无色想，外观色，青青色，青见青光。犹如青水华，青青色，青见青光。犹如成就波罗［木＊奈］衣，熟捣磨碾，光色悦泽，青青色，青见青光。如是，比丘！内无色想，外观色，青青色，青见青光，无量无量，净意润意，乐不憎恶。彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第五除处。』复次，比丘！内无色想，外观色，黄黄色，黄见黄光。犹如频头歌罗华，黄黄色，黄见黄光。犹如成就波罗［木＊奈］衣，熟捣磨碾，光色悦泽，黄黄色，黄见黄光。如是，比丘！内无色想，外观色，黄黄色，黄见黄光，无量无量，净意润意，乐不憎恶。彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第六除处。』

Furthermore, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, red in color, red in appearance, and red in radiance. Like the flowers of the kanikara tree, red in color, red in appearance, and red in radiance. Like well-polished and lustrous cloth made of cotton, red in color, red in appearance, and red in radiance. In this way, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, red in color, red in appearance, and red in radiance, immeasurable and immeasurable, with a pure mind, a moist mind, delighting in it and not disliking it. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the seventh kind of elimination.' "Furthermore, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, white in color, white in appearance, and white in radiance. Like a great white cloud, white in color, white in appearance, and white in radiance. Like well-polished and lustrous cloth made of cotton, white in color, white in appearance, and white in radiance. In this way, a bhikṣu, without internal form-perception, externally observes form, white in color, white in appearance, and white in radiance, immeasurable and immeasurable, with a pure mind, a moist mind, delighting in it and not disliking it. Having eliminated them, he knows and sees. He thinks like this: 'This is called the eighth kind of elimination.' "Sentient beings delight in such elimination and understanding. Those who undergo changes and differences, the well-versed noble disciple, observing thus, becomes disenchanted with that. Having become disenchanted, he does not even desire the foremost, let alone the inferior.

「复次，比丘！内无色想，外观色，赤赤色，赤见赤光。犹如加尼歌罗华，赤赤色，赤见赤光。犹如成就波罗［木＊奈］衣，熟捣磨碾，光色悦泽，赤赤色，赤见赤光。如是，比丘！内无色想，外观色，赤赤色，赤见赤光，无量无量，净意润意，乐不憎恶。彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第七除处。』复次，比丘！内无色想，外观色白，白色、白见、白光；犹如大白，白色、白见、白光；犹如成就波罗［木＊奈］衣熟捣磨碾、光色悦泽白，白色、白见、白光。如是，比丘！内无色想，外观色白，白色、白见、白光，无量无量净意、润意，乐不憎恶。彼色除已知、除已见，作如是想：『是谓第八除处。』众生如是乐除处意解者，变易有异。多闻圣弟子如是观则厌彼，厌彼已，尚不欲第一，况复下贱？

Furthermore, there are ten all-encompassing realms. What are the ten? A bhikṣu cultivates one of the immeasurable earth realms, contemplating above, below, and in all directions without duality. The same for the immeasurable water realms, fire realms, wind realms, blue realms, yellow realms, red realms, white realms, space realms, and the tenth, the consciousness realms. He cultivates one, contemplating above, below, and in all directions without duality. Sentient beings delight in such all-encompassing realms and understanding. Those who undergo changes and differences, the well-versed noble disciple, observing thus, becomes disenchanted with that. Having become disenchanted, he does not even desire the foremost, let alone the inferior. This is called the foremost pure teaching, the foremost establishment, namely, the non-existence of self, the non-existence of mine, and the establishment of the path for the sake of realizing that. This is called the foremost external basis for views, the foremost basis for views, namely, transcending all perceptions of form, and attaining the realm of neither perception nor non-perception, dwelling in accomplishment. This is called the foremost in the present life, seeking to reach nirvāṇa, the foremost establishment of nirvāṇa in the present life, namely, the arising, cessation, taste, drawback, and purification of the six sense bases, and the true insight and knowledge of them, and the establishment of the path for the sake of realizing that.

「复次，有十一切处。云何为十？有比丘无量地处修一，思惟上下诸方不二，无量水处，无量火处，无量风处，无量青处，无量黄处，无量赤处，无量白处，无量空处，无量识处第十修一，思惟上下诸方不二。众生如是乐一切处意解者，变易有异。多闻圣弟子如是观则厌彼，厌彼已，尚不欲第一，况复下贱？是谓第一清净说，施设最第一，谓我无、我不有，及为彼证故，施设于道，是谓第一外依见处、最依见处，谓度一切色想，乃至得非有想非无想处成就游。是谓于现法中第一，求趣至涅槃，于现法中最施设涅槃，谓六更乐处生、灭、味、离、慧见如真，及为彼证故，施设于道。

"Furthermore, there are four kinds of cessation. What are the four? There is cessation with slow pleasure, cessation with quick pleasure, cessation with slow pain, and cessation with quick pain. Among them, if there is cessation with slow pleasure, it is called inferior because of the slowness of that pleasure. Among them, if there is cessation with the desire for pleasure, if a person practices this Dharma without ever being satisfied, if a person practices drinking alcohol without ever being satisfied, if a person practices sleeping without ever being satisfied, this is called a bhikṣu who, if a person practices these three dharmas, is never satisfied, and is also unable to reach the place of cessation. Therefore, bhikṣus should always abandon and distance themselves from these three dharmas. Thus should bhikṣus train." At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

「复次，有四断。云何为四？有断乐迟，有断乐速，有断苦迟，有断苦速，于中若有断乐迟者，是乐迟故，说下贱。于中若有断乐欲，若有人习此法初无厌足，若复有人习饮酒者初无厌足，若复有人修习睡眠初无厌足，是谓比丘若人有习此三法者初无厌足，亦复不能至灭尽处。是故诸比丘常当舍离此三法不亲近之，如是诸比丘当作是学。」尔时诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

worship, three wholesome roots, three kinds of pain, three kinds of open and
exposed, characteristics of dharmas, three kinds of ignorance, love and respect, being insatiable

供养、三善根　　三痛、三复露相法、三不觉　　爱敬、无厌足

The first part, the fourth chapter, is completed.

第一得经第四竟

"If there is one who quickly abandons pleasure, because of quickly abandoning pleasure, that abandonment is also said to be inferior. Among them, if there is one who slowly abandons pain, because of slowly abandoning pain, that abandonment is also said to be inferior. Among them, if there is one who quickly abandons pain, because of quickly abandoning pain, that abandonment is not widely known, not spread, and even the gods and humans do not praise it as widely known. I say that my abandonment is widely known, spread, and even the gods and humans praise it as widely known. How is it that my abandonment is widely known, spread, and even the gods and humans praise it as widely known? It is the eightfold noble path, namely right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called my abandonment that is widely known, spread, and even the gods and humans praise it as widely known. I say this, but those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas falsely speak, unskillfully and untruthfully, falsely defaming me. They say that there are sentient beings who are annihilated and destroyed, but the śramaṇa Gautama does not say so. They say that there are sentient beings who are annihilated and destroyed. If this non-self were not taught like this, then the Tathāgata would have completely abandoned and known all in this very life, and attained cessation, tranquility, extinction, and nirvāṇa."

「若有断乐速者，此断乐速故，此断亦说下贱。于中若有断苦迟者，此断苦迟故，此断亦说下贱。于中若有断苦速者，此断苦速故，此断非广布、不流布，乃至天人亦不称广布，我断广布流布，乃至天人亦称广布。云何我断广布流布，乃至天人亦称广布？谓八支正道，正见乃至正定为八，是谓我断广布流布，乃至天人亦称广布。我如是，诸沙门、梵志虚伪妄言，不善不真，实诬谤于我，彼实有众生施设断坏，沙门瞿昙无所施设，彼实有众生施设断坏。若此无我不如是说，彼如来于现法中断知一切，得息、止、灭、涅槃。」
