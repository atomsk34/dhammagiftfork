MĀ 216

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 18 例品

216. （二一六）爱生经

Parallel to: MN 87 / EĀ 13.3

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, there was a brāhman who had only one son. He loved him deeply, with a gentle and affectionate mind, never tiring of looking at him. Suddenly, the son passed away. After the son's death, the brāhman was so grief-stricken that he could not eat or drink, did not wear clothes or apply perfume. He only went to the charnel ground to cry, remembering where his son used to sleep. Then, the brāhman wandered around and went to where the Buddha was. They exchanged greetings and sat to one side.

尔时，有一梵志，唯有一儿，心极爱念，忍意温润，视之无厌，忽便命终。命终之后，梵志愁忧，不能饮食，不著衣裳，亦不涂香，但至冢哭，忆儿卧处。于是，梵志周遍彷徉，往诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面。

The World-Honored One asked, "Brāhman! Now, do your faculties not resemble your own mind?"

世尊问曰：「梵志！今汝诸根不似自心住耶？」

The brāhman replied, "How can I now have my faculties dwell in my own mind? Why is that? I had only one son, whom I loved deeply, with a gentle and affectionate mind, never tiring of looking at him. Suddenly, he passed away. After his death, I became so grief-stricken that I could not eat or drink, did not wear clothes or apply perfume. I only went to the charnel ground to cry, remembering where my son used to sleep."

梵志答曰：「今我诸根何由当得自心住耶？所以者何？唯有一儿，心极爱念，忍意温润，视之无厌，忽便命终。彼命终已，我便愁忧，不能饮食，不著衣裳，亦不涂香，但至塜哭，忆儿卧处。」

The World-Honored One said, "So it is, brāhman! So it is, brāhman! When love arises, then sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress arise."

世尊告曰：「如是，梵志！如是，梵志！若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

The brāhman said, "Gautama! How can you say that when love arises, then sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress arise? Gautama! You should know that when love arises, there is joy and happiness."

梵志语曰：「瞿昙！何言若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼耶？瞿昙！当知若爱生时，生喜心乐。」

The World-Honored One said this up to the third time: "So it is, brāhman! So it is, brāhman! When love arises, then sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress arise."

世尊如是至再三告曰：「如是，梵志！如是，梵志！若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

The brāhman also said this up to the third time: "Gautama! How can you say that when love arises, then sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress arise? Gautama! You should know that when love arises, there is joy and happiness."

梵志亦至再三语曰：「瞿昙！何言若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼耶？瞿昙！当知若爱生时，生喜心乐。」

At that time, the brāhman, having heard the Buddha's words, did not say it was so, but only said it was not so. He then rose from his seat, shook his head, and left.

时，彼梵志闻佛所说，不说言是，但说非已，即从坐起，奋头而去。

At that time, in front of the gate of the Jeta Grove, there were many street children playing dice. The brāhman saw this from afar and thought: "In this world, there is none more intelligent and wise than those who play dice. I should now go there and tell them everything that was discussed with Gautama." So the brāhman went to where the many street children were playing dice and told them everything that was discussed with the World-Honored One.

尔时，胜林于其门前有众多市郭儿而共博戏，梵志遥见已，便作是念：「世中若有聪明智慧者，无过博戏人，我今宁可往彼，若与瞿昙所共论者，尽向彼说。」于是，梵志往至众多市郭儿共博戏所，若与世尊所共论者，尽向彼说。

The many street children playing dice heard this and said: "Brāhman! How can you say that when love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress? Brāhman! You should know that when love arises, it gives rise to joy and happiness.

众多市郭博戏儿闻已，语曰：「梵志！何言若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼耶？梵志！当知若爱生时，生喜心乐。」

Having heard this, the brāhman thought: "What the dice players say is exactly the same as mine." He nodded his head and left.

梵志闻已，便作是念：「博戏儿所说，正与我同。」颔头而去。

Thus, this discussion spread widely and entered the royal palace. King Prasenajit of Kosala heard that the śramaṇa Gautama said: "When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress.

于是，此论展转广布，乃入王宫，拘萨罗王波斯匿闻沙门瞿昙作如是说：「若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦，烦惋、懊恼。」

He said to Queen Māyā: "I heard that the Gautama said: 'When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress.

语末利皇后曰：「我闻瞿昙作如是说：『若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。』」

Queen Māyā, having heard this, said: "Yes, great king! Yes, great king! When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress.

末利皇后闻已，白曰：「如是，大王！如是，大王！若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

King Prasenajit of Kosala said to Queen Māyā: "The teacher's teachings are necessarily the same as the disciple's. The śramaṇa Gautama is your teacher, so he speaks like this. You are his disciple, so you speak like this: 'When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress.'"

拘萨罗王波斯匿语末利皇后曰：「闻师宗说，弟子必同，沙门瞿昙是汝师，故作如是说，汝是彼弟子，故作如是说：『若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。』」

Queen Māyā said: "Great king! If you do not believe, you can go ask yourself, or you can send someone.

末利皇后白曰：「大王！若不信者，可自往问，亦可遣使。」

Then King Prasenajit of Kosala said to the brāhman Nālakaṅga: "Go to the śramaṇa Gautama and ask on my behalf if the śramaṇa Gautama is in good health, strong, without illness, and of light and comfortable living. Say this: 'King Prasenajit of Kosala asks if you are in good health, strong, without illness, and of light and comfortable living.' Does the śramaṇa Gautama really say this: 'When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress'? Nālakaṅga, if the śramaṇa Gautama says anything, you should remember and recite it well. Why is that? Such a person will never speak falsely.

于是，拘萨罗王波斯匿即告那利鸯伽梵志曰：「汝往沙门瞿昙所，为我问讯沙门瞿昙圣体康强，安快无病，起居轻便，气力如常耶？作如是语：『拘萨罗王波斯匿问讯圣体康强，安快无病，起居轻便，气力如常耶？』沙门瞿昙实如是说：『若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼耶？』那利鸯伽！若沙门瞿昙有所说者，汝当善受持诵。所以者何？如是之人，终不妄言。」

The brāhman Nālakaṅga received the king's instructions and went to where the Buddha was. They exchanged greetings and sat to one side. He said: "Gautama! King Prasenajit of Kosala asks if you are in good health, strong, without illness, and of light and comfortable living. Does the śramaṇa Gautama really say this: 'When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress'?"

那利鸯伽梵志受王教已，即诣佛所，共相问讯，却坐一面，白曰：「瞿昙！拘萨罗王波斯匿问讯圣体康强，安快无病，起居轻便，气力如常耶？沙门瞿昙实如是说：『若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼耶？』」

The World-Honored One said: "Nālakaṅga! I now ask you, answer as you see fit. Nālakaṅga! What do you think? If someone's mother were to die, would that person go mad, their mind greatly confused, take off their clothes and run naked along the road, saying: 'Good people! Have you seen my mother? Have you seen my mother?' Nālakaṅga! From this we can know that when love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress. It is the same for father, brother, sister, and wife. If their wife were to die, that person would go mad, their mind greatly confused, take off their clothes and run naked along the road, saying: 'Good people! Have you seen my wife? Have you seen my wife?' Nālakaṅga! From this we can know that when love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress. Nālakaṅga! Once there was a man whose wife had returned home temporarily. Her relatives wanted to force her to remarry. When she heard about it, she quickly returned to her husband's home and said to him: 'Sir! You should know that my relatives are trying to force you to remarry. What should we do?' Then that man grabbed his wife's arm, took her into the house, and said: 'Let us go to the next world together! Let us go to the next world together!' He then took a sharp knife and killed his wife and himself. Nālakaṅga! From this we can know that when love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress."

世尊告曰：「那利鸯伽！我今问汝，随所解答。那利鸯伽！于意云何？若使有人，母命终者，彼人发狂，心大错乱，脱衣裸形，随路遍走，作如是说：『诸贤！见我母耶？诸贤！见我母耶？』那利鸯伽！以此事故可知，若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。如是父、兄、姊、妹也，儿妇命终，彼人发狂，心大错乱，脱衣裸形，随路遍走，作如是说：『诸贤！见我儿妇耶？诸贤！见我儿妇耶？』那利鸯伽！以此事故可知，若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。那利鸯伽！昔有一人，妇暂归家，彼诸亲族欲夺更嫁。彼女闻之，即便速疾还至夫家，语其夫曰：『君！今当知我亲族强欲夺君妇嫁与他人，欲作何计？』于是，彼人即执妇臂，将入屋中，作如是语：『俱至后世，俱至后世，便以利刀斫杀其妇，并自害己。』那利鸯伽！以此事故可知，若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

The brāhman Nālakaṅga heard what the Buddha said, accepted it well, and recited it. He then rose from his seat, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and left. He returned to King Prasenajit of Kosala and said: "Your Majesty! The śramaṇa Gautama really says this: 'When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress.'"

那利鸯伽梵志闻佛所说，善受持诵，即从坐起，绕三匝而去，还至拘萨罗王波斯匿所，白曰：「天王！沙门瞿昙实如是说：『若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。』」

King Prasenajit of Kosala heard this and said to Queen Mallikā: "The śramaṇa Gautama really says this: 'When love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and distress.'"

拘萨罗王波斯匿闻已，语末利皇后曰：「沙门瞿昙实如是说：『若爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。』」

Queen Mallikā said: "Great King! I ask the Great King, please answer as you see fit. What do you think? Do you love the general Vīra?"

末利皇后白曰：「大王！我问大王，随所解答，于意云何？王爱鞞留罗大将耶？」

He replied: "I truly love him."

答曰：「实爱。」

Mallikā asked again: "If the general Vīra were to change and become different, what would the king do?"

末利复问：「若鞞留罗大将变易异者，王当云何？」

He replied: "Mallikā, if the general Vīra were to change and become different, I would certainly feel sorrow, weeping, grief, distress, and anguish."

答曰：「末利！若鞞留罗大将变易异者，我必生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

Mallikā said: "From this matter, we know that when love arises, it gives rise to sorrow, weeping, grief, distress, and anguish."

末利白曰：「以此事故，知爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

Mallikā further asked: "Does the king love the minister Śrīdatta, the elephant Pāṇḍava, the girl Vārili, the rain-day umbrella, and the countries of Kāśi and Kosala?"

末利复问：「王爱尸利阿荼大臣，爱一奔陀利象，爱婆夷利童女，爱雨日盖，爱加尸及拘萨罗国耶？」

He replied: "Indeed, I love them."

答曰：「实爱。」

Mallikā further asked: "If the countries of Kāśi and Kosala were to change and become different, what would the king do?"

末利复问：「若加尸及拘萨罗国变易异者，王当云何？」

He replied: "Mallikā! The five sense pleasures and merits that I enjoy are due to those two countries. If the countries of Kāśi and Kosala were to change and become different, I would not even be alive, let alone not give rise to sorrow, weeping, grief, distress, and anguish."

答曰：「末利！我所具足五欲功德自娱乐者，由彼二国，若加尸及拘萨罗国当变易异者，我乃至无命，况复不生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼耶？」

Mallikā said: "From this matter, it is known that when love arises, then sorrow, weeping, grief, distress, and anguish arise."

末利白曰：「以此事故，知爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

Mallikā asked the king: "What do you think? Do you love me?"

末利问王：「于意云何？为爱我耶？」

The king replied: "I truly love you."

王复答曰：「我实爱汝。」

Mallikā asked again: "If I were to change and become different one day, what would you do?"

末利复问：「若我一旦变易异者，王当云何？」

The king replied: "Mallikā! If you were to change and become different one day, I would certainly feel sorrow, cry, be distressed, be troubled, and be tormented."

答曰：「末利！若汝一旦变易异者，我必生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、烦惋、懊恼。」

Mallikā said: "From this, we know that when love arises, it brings about sorrow, crying, distress, trouble, and torment."

末利白曰：「以此事故，知爱生时，便生愁戚、啼哭、忧苦，烦惋、懊恼。」

King Prasenajit of Kosala said: "Mallikā! From today onwards, the Śramaṇa Gautama is my teacher in this matter, and I am his disciple. Mallikā! I now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha of monks. I only wish that the World-Honored One would accept me as a lay devotee. From today onwards, I take refuge for life, until the end of my life."

拘萨罗王波斯匿语曰：「末利！从今日去，沙门瞿昙因此事是我师，我是彼弟子。末利！我今自归于佛、法及比丘众，唯愿世尊受我为优婆塞，从今日始，终身自归，乃至命尽。」

The Buddha spoke thus. King Prasenajit of Kosala and Queen Mallikā, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。拘萨罗王波斯匿及末利皇后，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
