MĀ 221

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 18 例品

221. （二二一）箭喻经

Parallel to: MN 63

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the Venerable Mālatīputra was alone in a quiet place, sitting in meditation, and his mind gave rise to this thought: "As for this view, the World-Honored One has set it aside and not fully explained it, namely, whether the world is eternal, whether the world is not eternal, whether the world has an end, whether the world has no end, whether life is the same as the body, whether life is different from the body, whether the Tathāgata exists after death, whether the Tathāgata does not exist after death, whether the Tathāgata both exists and does not exist after death, or whether the Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death. I do not desire this, I cannot bear this, I cannot accept this. If the World-Honored One were to fully explain to me that the world is eternal, I would study the holy life under him. If the World-Honored One does not fully explain to me that the world is eternal, I will question him and leave. Similarly, whether the world is not eternal, whether the world has an end, whether the world has no end, whether life is the same as the body, whether life is different from the body, whether the Tathāgata exists after death, whether the Tathāgata does not exist after death, whether the Tathāgata both exists and does not exist after death, or whether the Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death. If the World-Honored One were to fully explain to me that this is true and the rest is false, I would study the holy life under him. If the World-Honored One does not fully explain to me that this is true and the rest is false, I will question him and leave."

尔时，尊者鬘童子独安靖处，燕坐思惟，心作是念：「所谓此见，世尊舍置除却，不尽通说，谓世有常，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？我不欲此，我不忍此，我不可此。若世尊为我一向说世有常者，我从彼学梵行。若世尊不为我一向说世有常者，我当难诘彼，舍之而去。如是世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？若世尊为我一向说此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，我从彼学梵行，若世尊不为我一向说此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，我当难诘彼，舍之而去。」

Then the Venerable Mālatīputra rose from his solitary meditation in the afternoon, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at his feet, sat to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! I was alone in a quiet place, sitting in meditation, and my mind gave rise to this thought: 'As for this view, the World-Honored One has set it aside and not fully explained it, namely, whether the world is eternal, whether the world is not eternal, whether the world has an end, whether the world has no end, whether life is the same as the body, whether life is different from the body, whether the Tathāgata exists after death, whether the Tathāgata does not exist after death, whether the Tathāgata both exists and does not exist after death, or whether the Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death. I do not desire this, I cannot bear this, I cannot accept this. If the World-Honored One fully knew that the world is eternal, World-Honored One! You should explain it to me. If the World-Honored One does not fully know that the world is eternal, you should say that you do not know. Similarly, whether the world is not eternal, whether the world has an end, whether the world has no end, whether life is the same as the body, whether life is different from the body, whether the Tathāgata exists after death, whether the Tathāgata does not exist after death, whether the Tathāgata both exists and does not exist after death, or whether the Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death. If the World-Honored One fully knew that this is true and the rest is false, World-Honored One! You should explain it to me. If the World-Honored One does not fully know that this is true and the rest is false, you should say that you do not know.'"

于是，尊者鬘童子则于晡时，从燕坐起，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！我今独安靖处，燕坐思惟，心作是念：『所谓此见，世尊舍置除却，不尽通说，谓世有常，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？我不欲此，我不忍此，我不可此。若世尊一向知世有常者，世尊！当为我说。若世尊不一向知世有常者，当直言不知也。如是世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？若世尊一向知此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，世尊！当为我说。若世尊不一向知此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，当直言不知也。』」

The World-Honored One asked: "Mālatīputra! Have I ever told you that the world is eternal, and you came to study the holy life under me?"

世尊问曰：「鬘童子！我本颇为汝如是说世有常，汝来从我学梵行耶？」

Mālatīputra replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

鬘童子答曰：「不也。世尊！」

"Is it like this: the world is without permanence, the world has a bottom, the world has no bottom, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished? Have I ever told you that this is true and the rest is false, and you came to study the holy life under me?"

「如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？我本颇为汝如是说此是真谛，余皆虚妄言，汝来从我学梵行耶？」

Mālatīputra replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

鬘童子答曰：「不也。世尊！」

"Mālatīputra, have you ever said to me: 'If the World-Honored One would teach me that the world is permanent, I would study the holy life under the World-Honored One'?"

「鬘童子！汝本颇向我说，若世尊为我一向说世有常者，我当从世尊学梵行耶？」

Mālatīputra replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

鬘童子答曰：「不也。世尊！」

"Is it like this: 'The world is permanent, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata perishes, the Tathāgata does not perish, the Tathāgata both perishes and does not perish, the Tathāgata neither perishes nor does not perish'? Mālatīputra, have you ever said to me: 'If the World-Honored One would teach me that this alone is true and the rest is false, I would study the holy life under the World-Honored One'?"

「如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？鬘童子！汝本颇向我说，若世尊为我一向说此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，我当从世尊学梵行耶？」

Mālatīputra replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

鬘童子答曰：「不也。世尊！」

The World-Honored One said: "Mālatīputra, I have never said anything to you, and you have never said anything to me. You foolish man! Why do you falsely slander me?"

世尊告曰：「鬘童子，我本不向汝有所说，汝本亦不向我有所说，汝愚痴人！何故虚妄诬谤我耶？」

Then Venerable Mālatīputra was rebuked by the World-Honored One face to face, feeling ashamed and distressed, he lowered his head in silence, lost his eloquence, and was speechless, as if waiting for something.

于是，尊者鬘童子为世尊面诃责数，内怀忧戚，低头默然，失辩无言，如有所伺。

Then the World-Honored One, having rebuked Mālatīputra face to face, addressed the monks: "If there are foolish people who think like this: 'If the World-Honored One does not speak to me exclusively about the eternity of the world, I will not study the holy life under the World-Honored One.' Those foolish people will not know this in the meantime before they die. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. If there are foolish people who think like this: 'If the World-Honored One does not speak to me exclusively about this being true and the rest being false, I will not study the holy life under the World-Honored One.' Those foolish people will not know this in the meantime before they die.

于是，世尊面诃鬘童子已，告诸比丘：「若有愚痴人作如是念：『若世尊不为我一向说世有常者，我不从世尊学梵行。』彼愚痴人竟不得知，于其中间而命终也。如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？若有愚痴人作如是念：『若世尊不为我一向说此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，我不从世尊学梵行。』彼愚痴人竟不得知，于其中间而命终也。

It is like a person who is shot by a poisoned arrow and suffers extreme pain because of the poisoned arrow. His relatives, out of compassion and pity, seek to benefit and bring peace to him, and seek a doctor for the arrow. But that person thinks like this: 'I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the person's family name, given name, and birthplace, whether he is tall or short, coarse or fine, black or white, or neither black nor white, whether he is from the kṣatriya, brahmin, vaiśya, or śūdra caste, and from which of the four directions. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the bow, whether it is made of mulberry, zelkova, or horn. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the bowstring, whether it is made of ox sinew, deer sinew, or silk. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the color of the bow, whether it is black, white, red, or yellow. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the bowstring, whether it is sinew, silk, hemp, or flax. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the arrow shaft, whether it is wood or bamboo. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the arrow wrapping, whether it is ox sinew, deer sinew, or silk. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the arrow feathers, whether they are from a vulture, hawk, peacock, or crane. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the arrowhead, whether it is a knife, spear, or awl. I should not pull out the arrow yet. I should first know the arrowhead maker's family name, given name, and birthplace, whether he is tall or short, coarse or fine, black or white, or neither black nor white, and from which of the four directions.' That person will not know this in the meantime before he dies.

「犹如有人身被毒箭，因毒箭故，受极重苦。彼见亲族怜念愍伤，为求利义饶益安隐，便求箭医。然彼人者方作是念：『未可拔箭，我应先知彼人如是姓、如是名、如是生，为长、短、麤、细，为黑、白、不黑不白，为刹利族，梵志、居士、工师族，为东方、南方、西方、比方耶？未可拔箭，我应先知彼弓为柘、为桑、为槻、为角耶？未可拔箭，我应先知弓扎，彼为是牛筋、为麞鹿筋、为是丝耶？未可拔箭，我应先知弓色为黑、为白、为赤、为黄耶？未可拔箭，我应先知弓弦为筋、为丝、为纻、为麻耶？未可拔箭，我应先知箭簳为木、为竹耶？未可拔箭，我应先知箭缠为是牛筋、为麞鹿筋、为是丝耶？未可拔箭，我应先知箭羽为飘𫛯毛、为雕鹫毛、为鹍鸡毛、为鹤毛耶？未可拔箭，我应先知箭［金＊适］为錍、为矛、为铍刀耶？未可拔箭，我应先知作箭［金＊适］师如是姓、如是名、如是生，为长、短、麤、细，为黑、白、不黑不白，为东方、西方、南方、北方耶？』彼人竟不得知，于其中间而命终也。

If there are foolish people who think like this: 'If the World-Honored One does not speak to me exclusively about the eternity of the world, I will not study the holy life under the World-Honored One.' Those foolish people will not know this in the meantime before they die. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. If there are foolish people who think like this: 'If the World-Honored One does not speak to me exclusively about this being true and the rest being false, I will not study the holy life under the World-Honored One.' Those foolish people will not know this in the meantime before they die.

「若有愚痴人作如是念：『若世尊不为我一向说世有常者，我不从世尊学梵行。』彼愚痴人竟不得知，于其中间而命终也。如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？若有愚痴人作如是念：『若世尊不为我一向说此是真谛，余皆虚妄言者，我不从世尊学梵行。』彼愚痴人竟不得知，于其中间而命终也。

The world is eternal. Because of this view, those who study the holy life under me are not right. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. Because of this view, those who study the holy life under me are not right. The world is eternal. Because of this view, those who do not study the holy life under me are not right. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. Because of this view, those who do not study the holy life under me are not right.

「世有常，因此见故，从我学梵行者，此事不然。如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？因此见故，从我学梵行者，此事不然。世有常，有此见故，不从我学梵行者，此事不然。如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？有此见故，不从我学梵行者，此事不然。

The world is eternal. Because of not having this view, those who study the holy life under me are not right. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. Because of not having this view, those who study the holy life under me are not right. The world is eternal. Because of not having this view, those who study the holy life under me are not right. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. Because of not having this view, those who do not study the holy life under me are not right.

「世有常，无此见故，从我学梵行者，此事不然。如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？无此见故，从我学梵行者，此事不然。世有常，无此见故，从我学梵行者，此事不然。如是，世无有常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终耶？无此见故，不从我学梵行者，此事不然。

The world is eternal. There is birth, aging, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus, this mass of suffering arises. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. There is birth, aging, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus, this mass of suffering arises.

「世有常者，有生、有老、有病、有死，愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、懊恼，如是此淳大苦阴生。如是，世无常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终者，有生、有老、有病、有死，愁戚、啼哭、忧苦、懊恼，如是此淳大苦阴生。

The world is eternal. I do not speak of this unilaterally. Why do I not speak of this unilaterally? Because it is not in accord with meaning, not in accord with the Dharma, not the basis of the holy life, not leading to knowledge, not leading to awakening, not leading to nirvāṇa. Therefore, I do not speak of this unilaterally. Thus, the world is not eternal, the world has an end, the world has no end, life is the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata is extinguished, the Tathāgata is not extinguished, the Tathāgata is both extinguished and not extinguished, and the Tathāgata is neither extinguished nor not extinguished. I do not speak of this unilaterally. Why do I not speak of this unilaterally? Because it is not in accord with meaning, not in accord with the Dharma, not the basis of the holy life, not leading to knowledge, not leading to awakening, not leading to nirvāṇa. Therefore, I do not speak of this unilaterally. What Dharma do I speak of unilaterally? I speak of this unilaterally: suffering, the arising of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering. Why do I speak of this unilaterally? Because it is in accord with meaning, in accord with the Dharma, the basis of the holy life, leading to knowledge, leading to awakening, leading to nirvāṇa. Therefore, I speak of this unilaterally. This is how it should be upheld and learned.

「世有常，我不一向说此。以何等故，我不一向说此？此非义相应，非法相应，非梵行本，不趣智、不趣觉、不趣涅槃，是故我不一向说此。如是，世无常，世有底，世无底，命即是身，为命异身异，如来终，如来不终，如来终不终，如来亦非终亦非不终，我不一向说此。以何等故，我不一向说此？此非义相应，非法相应，非梵行本，不趣智、不趣觉、不趣涅槃，是故我不一向说此也。何等法我一向说耶？此义我一向说，苦、苦习、苦灭、苦灭道迹，我一向说。以何等故，我一向说此？此是义相应，是法相应，是梵行本，趣智、趣觉、趣于涅槃，是故我一向说此。是为不可说者则不说，可说者则说，当如是持，当如是学。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
