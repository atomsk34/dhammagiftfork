MĀ 25

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 3 舍梨子相应品

25. Parables Relating to Water （二五）水喻经

Parallel to: AN 5.162

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "Virtuous ones! I will now explain to you the five methods for dispelling distress. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Consider it well and keep it in mind." Those bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

尔时，尊者舍梨子告诸比丘：「诸贤！我今为汝说五除恼法。谛听，谛听！善思念之。」彼诸比丘受教而听。

Venerable Śāriputra said, "What are the five? Virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily conduct but pure verbal conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. Furthermore, virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure verbal conduct but pure bodily conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. Furthermore, virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily conduct and verbal conduct but has a slightly pure mind, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. Furthermore, virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily conduct, verbal conduct, and mental conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. Furthermore, virtuous ones! If someone engages in pure bodily conduct, verbal conduct, and mental conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress.

尊者舍梨子言：「云何为五？诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应当除之。复次。诸贤！或有一人口不净行，身净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应当除之。复次。诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口不净行，心少有净，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应当除之。复次。诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口、意不净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应当除之。复次。诸贤！或有一人身净行，口、意净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应当除之。

Virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily conduct but pure verbal conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. How should this be dispelled? Virtuous ones! It is like a forest-dwelling bhikṣu who wears discarded rags. Seeing a pile of excrement with discarded rags, either soiled with feces, urine, snot, spittle, or other impurities, upon seeing this, he takes it with his left hand and stretches it out with his right hand. If it is not soiled with feces, urine, snot, spittle, or other impurities, and is not torn, he then tears it apart and takes it. In this way, virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily conduct but pure verbal conduct, do not think of their impure bodily conduct, but only think of their pure verbal conduct. If a wise person sees this and any anger or distress arises, it should be dispelled in this way.

「诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，当云何除？诸贤！犹如阿练若比丘持粪扫衣，见粪聚中所弃弊衣，或大便污，或小便、涕、唾及余不净之所染污，见已，左手执之，右手舒张，若非大便、小便、涕、唾及余不净之所污处，又不穿者，便裂取之。如是，诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口净行，莫念彼身不净行也，但当念彼口之净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应如是除。

Virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure verbal conduct but pure bodily conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. How should this be dispelled? Virtuous ones! It is like a pond outside a village, covered with algae. If someone comes, exhausted by heat, hungry, thirsty, and oppressed by wind and heat, upon reaching the pond, they take off their clothes and place them on the shore, then enter the pond and part the algae with both hands, bathing freely to relieve the exhaustion, hunger, thirst, and oppression.

「诸贤！或有一人口不净行，身净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，当云何除？诸贤！犹村外不远，有深水池，藳草所覆，若有人来，热极烦闷，饥渴顿乏，风热所逼，彼至池已，脱衣置岸，便入池中，两手披藳，恣意快浴，除热烦闷，饥渴顿乏。如是，诸贤！或有一人口不净行，身有净行，莫念彼口不净行，但当念彼身之净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应如是除。

Virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily and verbal conduct but has a little purity in their mind, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. How should this be dispelled? Virtuous ones! It is like a small amount of water in a cow's hoofprint at a four-way intersection. If someone comes, exhausted by heat, hungry, thirsty, and oppressed by wind and heat, they think: 'There is only a little water in this cow's hoofprint at the four-way intersection. If I try to take it with my hand or a leaf, it will become turbid and will not relieve my exhaustion, hunger, thirst, and oppression. I should rather kneel, pat the ground with my hands and knees, and drink the water with my mouth.' They then kneel, pat the ground with their hands and knees, and drink the water with their mouth. They then relieve their exhaustion, hunger, thirst, and oppression.

「诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口不净行，心少有净，若慧者见，设生恚恼，当云何除？诸贤！犹四衢道，有牛迹水。若有人来，热极烦闷，饥渴顿乏，风热所逼，彼作是念：『此四衢道牛迹少水，我若以手、以叶取者，则扰浑浊，不得除我热极烦闷、饥渴顿乏。我宁可跪，手膝拍地，以口饮水。』彼即长跪，手膝拍地，以口饮水，彼即得除热极烦闷、饥渴顿乏。如是，诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口不净行，心少有净，莫得念彼身不净行，口不净行，但当念彼心少有净。诸贤！若慧者见，设生恚恼，应如是除。

Virtuous ones! If someone engages in impure bodily, verbal, and mental conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. How should this be dispelled? Virtuous ones! It is like a person traveling a long road who becomes sick along the way, extremely exhausted and helpless, without companions, with the next village still far away and the previous village not yet reached. If someone comes and stands to one side, seeing this traveler who has become sick along the way, extremely exhausted and helpless, without companions, with the next village still far away and the previous village not yet reached, if they can get a servant to take them from the wilderness to a village, provide them with fine soup and medicine, and nourish them with good food and care, then this person's illness will surely be cured. This is because that person has a compassionate and caring mind towards this sick person.

「诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口、意不净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，当云何除？诸贤！犹如有人远涉长路，中道得病，极困委顿，独无伴侣，后村转远，而前村未至。若有人来住一面，见此行人远涉长路，中道得病，极困委顿，独无伴侣，后村转远，而前村未至，彼若得侍人，从逈野中，将至村邑，与妙汤药，𫗦养美食，好瞻视者，如是此人病必得差，谓彼人于此病人，极有哀愍慈念之心。如是，诸贤！或有一人身不净行，口、意不净行，若慧者见，便作是念：『此贤身不净行，口、意不净行，莫令此贤因身不净行，口、意不净行，身坏命终，趣至恶处，生地狱中。若此贤得善知识者，舍身不净行，修身净行，舍口、意不净行，修口、意净行。如是，此贤因身净行，口、意净行，身坏命终，必至善处，乃生天上。』谓彼贤为此贤极有哀愍慈念之心，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应如是除。

Virtuous ones! If someone engages in pure bodily, verbal, and mental conduct, a wise person, upon seeing this, should dispel any arising anger or distress. How should this be dispelled? Virtuous ones! It is like a good pond outside a village, clear and beautiful, its depths evenly full, with green grass covering the banks, and flowering trees all around. If someone comes, exhausted by heat, hungry, thirsty, and oppressed by wind and heat, upon reaching the pond, they take off their clothes and place them on the bank, then enter the pond and bathe to their heart's content, dispelling their exhaustion, hunger, thirst, and oppression.

「诸贤！或有一人身净行，口、意净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，当云何除？诸贤！犹村外不远，有好池水，既清且美，其渊平满，翠草被岸，华树四周，若有人来，热极烦闷，饥渴顿乏，风热所逼，彼至池已，脱衣置岸，便入池中，恣意快浴，除热烦闷、饥渴顿乏。如是，诸贤！或有一人身净行，口、意净行，常当念彼身之净行，口、意净行，若慧者见，设生恚恼，应如是除。诸贤！我向所说五除恼法者，因此故说。」

The Venerable Sāriputta spoke thus. The monks, having heard it, rejoiced and approved.

尊者舍梨子所说如是。诸比丘闻已，欢喜奉行。
