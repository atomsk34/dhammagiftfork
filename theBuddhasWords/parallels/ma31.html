MĀ 31

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 3 舍梨子相应品

31. Discerning the Noble Truths （三一）分别圣谛经

Parallel to: DN 22 (~) / MN 10 (~) / MN 141 / MĀ 98 (~) / EĀ 12.1 (~) / EĀ 27.1

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "This is the correct practice of teaching the Dharma, namely, the four noble truths, which are extensively included, extensively observed, distinguished, revealed, opened up, established, shown, and directed towards. The past Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Perfectly Enlightened Ones also had this correct practice of teaching the Dharma, namely, the four noble truths, which are extensively included, extensively observed, distinguished, revealed, opened up, established, shown, and directed towards. The future Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Perfectly Enlightened Ones will also have this correct practice of teaching the Dharma, namely, the four noble truths, which are extensively included, extensively observed, distinguished, revealed, opened up, established, shown, and directed towards. I, the present Tathāgata, Arhat, and Perfectly Enlightened One, also have this correct practice of teaching the Dharma, namely, the four noble truths, which are extensively included, extensively observed, distinguished, revealed, opened up, established, shown, and directed towards.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「此是正行说法，谓四圣谛广摄、广观、分别、发露、开仰、施设、显示、趣向。过去诸如来、无所著、等正觉，彼亦有此正行说法，谓四圣谛广摄、广观、分别、发露、开仰、施设、显示、趣向。未来诸如来、无所著、等正觉，彼亦有此正行说法，谓四圣谛广摄、广观、分别、发露、开仰、施设、显示、趣向。我今现如来、无所著、等正觉，亦有此正行说法，谓四圣谛广摄、广观、分别、发露、开仰、施设、显示、趣向。

"Śāriputra Bhikṣu is wise, quick-witted, sharp-witted, intelligent, broad-minded, deep-minded, able to transcend, penetratingly wise, eloquent. Śāriputra Bhikṣu has accomplished true wisdom. Why is that? It is because I have briefly explained these four noble truths, and Śāriputra Bhikṣu is then able to extensively teach, extensively observe, distinguish, reveal, open up, establish, show, and direct others towards them. When Śāriputra Bhikṣu extensively teaches and shows these four noble truths, distinguishing, revealing, opening up, establishing, showing, and directing others towards them, he enables countless people to attain insight. Śāriputra Bhikṣu is able to lead with right view. Maudgalyāyana Bhikṣu is able to establish them in the supreme true reality, namely, the ultimate exhaustion of defilements. Śāriputra Bhikṣu gives birth to all virtuous practices, like a birth mother, while Maudgalyāyana Bhikṣu nurtures all virtuous practices, like a nursing mother. Therefore, all virtuous practitioners should serve, make offerings, respect, and pay homage to Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana Bhikṣus. Why is that? Because Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana Bhikṣus seek the meaning and benefit for all virtuous practitioners, seeking peace and happiness." At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken thus, immediately rose from his seat and entered the room to sit in meditation.

「舍梨子比丘聪慧、速慧、捷慧、利慧、广慧、深慧、出要慧、明达慧、辩才慧，舍梨子比丘成就实慧。所以者何？谓我略说此四圣谛，舍梨子比丘则能为他广教、广观、分别、发露、开仰、施设、显现、趣向。舍梨子比丘广教、广示此四圣谛，分别、发露、开仰、施设、显现、趣向时，令无量人而得于观，舍梨子比丘能以正见为导御也。目干连比丘能令立于最上真际，谓究竟漏尽。舍梨子比丘生诸梵行，犹如生母，目连比丘长养诸梵行，犹如养母，是以诸梵行者，应奉事供养恭敬礼拜舍梨子、目干连比丘。所以者何？舍梨子、目干连比丘为诸梵行者求义及饶益，求安隐快乐。」尔时，世尊说如是已，即从坐起，入室燕坐。

Then Venerable Śāriputra addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "Virtuous ones! The World-Honored One has appeared in the world for us, namely, to extensively teach and show these four noble truths to others, distinguishing, revealing, opening up, establishing, showing, and directing towards them. What are the four? They are the noble truth of suffering, the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering. Virtuous ones! What is the noble truth of suffering? It means the suffering of birth, the suffering of old age, the suffering of sickness, the suffering of death, the suffering of being with what is disliked, the suffering of separation from what is loved, the suffering of not obtaining what one seeks, and the suffering of the five aggregates. Virtuous ones! When it is said that birth is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! Birth means that sentient beings of various kinds are born, emerge, come into existence, and arise with the five aggregates, and obtain the life faculty. This is called birth. Virtuous ones! The suffering of birth means that when sentient beings are born, they experience bodily suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily and mental distress, pervasive distress, awareness, and pervasive awareness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that birth is said to be suffering.

于是，尊者舍梨子告诸比丘：「诸贤！世尊为我等出世，谓为他广教、广示此四圣谛，分别、发露、开仰、施设、显现、趣向。云何为四？谓苦圣谛，苦习、苦灭、苦灭道圣谛。诸贤！云何苦圣谛？谓生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、怨憎会苦、爱别离苦、所求不得苦、略五盛阴苦。诸贤！说生苦者，此说何因？诸贤！生者，谓彼众生、彼彼众生种类，生则生，出则出，成则成，兴起五阴，已得命根，是名为生。诸贤！生苦者，谓众生生时，身受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心受苦受、遍受，觉、遍觉，身热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受，觉、遍觉，心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉。诸贤！说生苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that old age is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! Old age means that sentient beings of various kinds become aged, with gray hair, falling teeth, declining vigor, bent bodies, limping gait, heavy bodies, labored breathing, walking with a cane, sagging skin, wrinkles like hemp seeds, decaying faculties, and ugly complexion. This is called old age. Virtuous ones! The suffering of old age means that when sentient beings are old, they experience bodily suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily and mental distress, pervasive distress, awareness, and pervasive awareness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that old age is said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说老苦者，此说何因？诸贤！老者，谓彼众生、彼彼众生种类，彼为老耄，头白齿落，盛壮日衰，身曲脚戾，体重气上，拄杖而行，肌缩皮缓，皱如麻子，诸根毁熟，颜色丑恶，是名为老。诸贤！老苦者，谓众生老时，身受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉。诸贤！说老苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that sickness is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! Sickness means headaches, eye pain, ear pain, nose pain, face pain, lip pain, tooth pain, tongue pain, palate pain, throat pain, wind, panting, coughing, vomiting, hiccups, epilepsy, goiters, diarrhea, red gall, fever, emaciation, hemorrhoids, diarrhea, and other various illnesses that arise from contact with pleasure and are not separate from the mind, established in the body. This is called sickness. Virtuous ones! The suffering of sickness means that when sentient beings are sick, they experience bodily suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily and mental distress, pervasive distress, awareness, and pervasive awareness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that sickness is said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说病苦者，此说何因？诸贤！病者，谓头痛、眼痛、耳痛、鼻痛、面痛、唇痛、齿痛、舌痛、腭痛、咽痛、风喘、咳嗽、喝吐、喉啤、癫癎、癕瘿、经溢、赤胆、壮热、枯槁、痔［病－丙＋匿］、下利，若有如是比余种种病，从更乐触生，不离心，立在身中，是名为病。诸贤！病苦者，谓众生病时，身受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉。诸贤！说病苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that death is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! Death means that sentient beings of various kinds die, are impermanent, die, perish, are scattered, their lifespan ends, they are destroyed, their life force is blocked. This is called death. Virtuous ones! The suffering of death means that when sentient beings die, they experience bodily suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental heat, pervasive heat, awareness, and pervasive awareness. They experience bodily and mental distress, pervasive distress, awareness, and pervasive awareness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that death is said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说死苦者，此说何因？诸贤！死者，谓彼众生、彼彼众生种类，命终无常，死丧散灭，寿尽破坏，命根闭塞，是名为死。诸贤！死苦者，谓众生死时，身受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心热受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心壮热烦恼忧戚受、遍受、觉、遍觉。诸贤！说死苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that meeting with what is disliked is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! Meeting with what is disliked means that sentient beings actually have the six internal sense bases, which are not loved. The sense bases of the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, when they meet together, are included, combined, and associated, and are collectively called suffering. The same is true for the external sense bases, contact, feeling, perception, intention, and craving. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings actually have the six elements, which are not loved. The elements of earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness, when they meet together, are included, combined, and associated, and are collectively called suffering. This is called meeting with what is disliked. Virtuous ones! The suffering of meeting with what is disliked means that when sentient beings meet with what is disliked, they experience bodily suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that meeting with what is disliked is said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说怨憎会苦者，此说何因？诸贤！怨憎会者，谓众生实有内六处，不爱眼处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意处，彼同会一，有摄、和、习，共合为苦，如是外处，更乐、觉、想、思、爱、亦复如是。诸贤！众生实有六界，不爱地界，水、火、风、空、识界，彼同会一，有摄、和、习，共合为苦，是名怨憎会。诸贤！怨憎会苦者，谓众生怨憎会时，身受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉。诸贤！说怨憎会苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that separation from what is loved is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! The suffering of separation from what is loved means that sentient beings actually have the six internal sense bases, which are loved. The sense bases of the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, when they are separated, cannot be united, are separated, not united, not included, not practiced, and not combined, and are collectively called suffering. The same is true for the external sense bases, contact, feeling, perception, intention, and craving. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings actually have the six elements, which are loved. The elements of earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness, when they are separated, cannot be united, are separated, not united, not included, not practiced, and not combined, and are collectively called suffering. This is called separation from what is loved. Virtuous ones! The suffering of separation from what is loved means that when sentient beings are separated from what is loved, they experience bodily suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness; they experience bodily and mental suffering, pervasive suffering, awareness, and pervasive awareness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that separation from what is loved is said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说爱别离苦者，此说何因？诸贤！爱别离苦者，谓众生实有内六处，爱眼处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意处，彼异分散，不得相应，别离不会，不摄、不习、不和合为苦，如是外处，更乐、觉、想、思、爱，亦复如是。诸贤！众生实有六界，爱地界，水、火、风、空、识界，彼异分散，不得相应，别离不会，不摄、不习、不和合为苦，是名爱别离。诸贤！爱别离苦者，谓众生别离时，身受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉，身心受苦受、遍受、觉、遍觉。诸贤！说爱别离苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that not obtaining what one seeks is suffering, what is meant by that? Virtuous ones! It means that sentient beings are born with the nature of birth, and cannot be separated from the nature of birth. They wish to be born without being born, but this cannot be obtained by desire. They are born with the nature of aging, death, sorrow, and distress, and cannot be separated from the nature of sorrow and distress. They wish to be born without sorrow and distress, but this also cannot be obtained by desire. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings are actually born with suffering, which cannot be enjoyed or loved. They think, 'If my birth is suffering, which cannot be enjoyed or loved, I wish to change it to be lovable.' But this also cannot be obtained by desire. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings are actually born with pleasure, which is lovable. They think, 'If my birth is pleasure, which is lovable, I wish to make it permanent, eternal, and unchanging.' But this also cannot be obtained by desire. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings are actually born with thoughts, which cannot be enjoyed or loved. They think, 'If my birth is thoughts, which cannot be enjoyed or loved, I wish to change it to be lovable.' But this also cannot be obtained by desire. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings are actually born with thoughts, which are lovable. They think, 'If my birth is thoughts, which are lovable, I wish to make them permanent, eternal, and unchanging.' But this also cannot be obtained by desire. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that not obtaining what one seeks is said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说所求不得苦者，此说何因？诸贤！谓众生生法，不离生法，欲得令我而不生者，此实不可以欲而得，老法、死法、愁忧戚法，不离忧戚法，欲得令我不忧戚者，此亦不可以欲而得。诸贤！众生实生苦而不可乐、不可爱念，彼作是念：『若我生苦而不可乐、不可爱念者，欲得转是，令可爱念。』此亦不可以欲而得。诸贤！众生实生乐而可爱念，彼作是念：『若我生乐可爱念者，欲得令是常恒久住不变易法。』此亦不可以欲而得。诸贤！众生实生思想而不可乐、不可爱念，彼作是念：『若我生思想而不可乐、不可爱念者，欲得转是，令可爱念。』此亦不可以欲而得。诸贤！众生实生思想而可爱念，彼作是念：『若我生思想可爱念者，欲得令是常恒久住不变易法。』此亦不可以欲而得。诸贤！说所求不得苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! When it is said that the five aggregates of clinging are suffering, what is meant by that? It means the aggregate of form, the aggregates of feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness. Virtuous ones! It is for this reason that the five aggregates of clinging are said to be suffering.

「诸贤！说略五盛阴苦者，此说何因？谓色盛阴，觉、想、行、识盛阴。诸贤！说略五盛阴苦者，因此故说。

Virtuous ones! The past time is the noble truth of suffering, the future and present time is the noble truth of suffering, the true truth is not false, not apart from suchness, also not inverted, the true truth is truly real, in accordance with this truth, it is possessed by the noble ones, known by the noble ones, seen by the noble ones, understood by the noble ones, attained by the noble ones, and fully awakened to by the noble ones. Therefore, it is said to be the noble truth of suffering.

「诸贤！过去时是苦圣谛，未来、现在时是苦圣谛，真谛不虚，不离于如，亦非颠倒，真谛审实，合如是谛，圣所有，圣所知，圣所见，圣所了，圣所得，圣所等正觉，是故说苦圣谛。

Virtuous ones! What is the noble truth of the origin of suffering as craving? It means that sentient beings truly have craving for the six internal sense bases, the sense bases of the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. If there is craving, attachment, defilement, and clinging in them, this is called the origin. Virtuous ones! The noble disciple of the learned knows that I know this teaching in this way, sees it in this way, understands it in this way, views it in this way, and awakens to it in this way. This is called the noble truth of the origin of suffering as craving. How does one know it? If there is craving for wife, children, servants, attendants, relatives, fields, houses, shops, and wealth, and if there is craving, attachment, defilement, and clinging in the work done for them, this is called the origin. They know this craving as the noble truth of the origin of suffering. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings truly have craving for the six elements, the elements of earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness. If there is craving, attachment, defilement, and clinging in them, this is called the origin. Virtuous ones! The noble disciple of the learned knows that I know this teaching in this way, sees it in this way, understands it in this way, views it in this way, and awakens to it in this way. This is called the noble truth of the origin of suffering as craving. How does one know it? If there is craving for wife, children, servants, attendants, relatives, fields, houses, shops, and wealth, and if there is craving, attachment, defilement, and clinging in the work done for them, this is called the origin. They know this craving as the noble truth of the origin of suffering. Virtuous ones! The past time is the noble truth of the origin of suffering as craving, the future and present time is the noble truth of the origin of suffering as craving, the true truth is not false, not apart from suchness, also not inverted, the true truth is truly real, in accordance with this truth, it is possessed by the noble ones, known by the noble ones, seen by the noble ones, understood by the noble ones, attained by the noble ones, and fully awakened to by the noble ones. Therefore, it is said to be the noble truth of the origin of suffering as craving.

「诸贤！云何爱习苦习圣谛？谓众生实有爱内六处，眼处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意处，于中若有爱、有腻、有染、有著者，是名为习。诸贤！多闻圣弟子知我如是知此法，如是见，如是了，如是视，如是觉，是谓爱习苦习圣谛，如是知之。云何知耶？若有爱妻、子、奴婢、给使、眷属、田地、屋宅、店肆、出息财物，为所作业，有爱、有腻、有染、有著者，是名为习；彼知此爱习，苦习圣谛，如是外处，更乐、觉、想、思、爱，亦复如是。诸贤！众生实有爱六界，地界，水、火、风、空、识界，于中若有爱、有腻、有染、有著者，是名为习。诸贤！多闻圣弟子知我如是知此法，如是见，如是了，如是视，如是觉，是谓爱习苦习圣谛，如是知之。云何知耶？若有爱妻、子、奴婢、给使、眷属、田地、屋宅、店肆、出息财物，为所作业，有爱、有腻、有染、有著者，是名为习，彼知是爱习苦习圣谛。诸贤！过去时是爱习苦习圣谛，未来、现在时是爱习苦习圣谛，真谛不虚，不离于如，亦非颠倒，真谛审实，合如是谛，圣所有，圣所知，圣所见，圣所了，圣所得，圣所等正觉，是故说爱习苦习圣谛。

Virtuous ones! What is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering as the cessation of craving? It means that sentient beings truly have craving for the six internal sense bases, the sense bases of the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. If they are liberated from them, not defiled, not attached, cut off, abandoned, exhausted, without desire, ceased, pacified, and extinguished, this is called the cessation of suffering. Virtuous ones! The noble disciple of the learned knows that I know this teaching in this way, sees it in this way, understands it in this way, views it in this way, and awakens to it in this way. This is called the noble truth of the cessation of suffering as the cessation of craving. How does one know it? If there is no craving for wife, children, servants, attendants, relatives, fields, houses, shops, and wealth, and if one is not engaged in work for them, and if one is liberated from them, not defiled, not attached, cut off, abandoned, exhausted, without desire, ceased, pacified, and extinguished, this is called the cessation of suffering. They know this cessation of craving as the noble truth of the cessation of suffering. Virtuous ones! Sentient beings truly have craving for the six elements, the elements of earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness. If they are liberated from them, not defiled, not attached, cut off, abandoned, exhausted, without desire, ceased, pacified, and extinguished, this is called the cessation of suffering. Virtuous ones! The noble disciple of the learned knows that I know this teaching in this way, sees it in this way, understands it in this way, views it in this way, and awakens to it in this way. This is called the noble truth of the cessation of suffering as the cessation of craving. How does one know it? If there is no craving for wife, children, servants, attendants, relatives, fields, houses, shops, and wealth, and if one is not engaged in work for them, and if one is liberated from them, not defiled, not attached, cut off, abandoned, exhausted, without desire, ceased, pacified, and extinguished, this is called the cessation of suffering. They know this cessation of craving as the noble truth of the cessation of suffering. Virtuous ones! The past time is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering as the cessation of craving, the future and present time is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering as the cessation of craving, the true truth is not false, not apart from suchness, also not inverted, the true truth is truly real, in accordance with this truth, it is possessed by the noble ones, known by the noble ones, seen by the noble ones, understood by the noble ones, attained by the noble ones, and fully awakened to by the noble

「诸贤！云何爱灭苦灭圣谛？谓众生实有爱内六处，眼处、耳、鼻、舌、身、意处，彼若解脱，不染不著、断舍吐尽、无欲、灭、止没者，是名苦灭。诸贤！多闻圣弟子知我如是知此法，如是见，如是了，如是视，如是觉，是谓爱灭苦灭圣谛，如是知之。云何知耶？若有不爱妻、子、奴婢、给使、眷属、田地、屋宅、店肆、出息财物，不为所作业，彼若解脱，不染不著、断舍吐尽、无欲、灭、止没者，是名苦灭，彼知是爱灭苦灭圣谛，如是外处，更乐、觉、想、思、爱亦复如是。诸贤！众生实有爱六界，地界，水、火、风、空、识界，彼若解脱，不染不著、断舍吐尽、无欲、灭、止没者，是名苦灭。诸贤！多闻圣弟子知我如是知此法，如是见，如是了，如是视，如是觉，是谓爱灭苦灭圣谛，如是知之。云何知耶？若有不爱妻、子、奴婢、给使、眷属、田地、屋宅、店肆、出息财物，不为所作业，彼若解脱，不染不著，断舍吐尽、无欲、灭、止没者，是名苦灭，彼知是爱灭苦灭圣谛。诸贤！过去时是爱灭苦灭圣谛，未来、现在时是爱灭苦灭圣谛，真谛不虚，不离于如，亦非颠倒，真谛审实，合如是谛，圣所有，圣所知，圣所见，圣所了，圣所得，圣所等正觉，是故说爱灭苦灭圣谛。

Virtuous ones! What is the noble truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering? It means right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. Virtuous ones! What is right view? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, the discernment, thorough discernment, subsequent discernment, discernment of phenomena, seeing, thorough seeing, contemplation, and clear comprehension, this is called right view.

「诸贤！云何苦灭道圣谛？谓正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定。诸贤！云何正见？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中择、遍择、次择，择法、视、遍视，观察明达，是名正见。

Virtuous ones! What is right intention? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, the mental investigation, thorough investigation, and following investigation, the recollection of what is to be remembered, and the hope of what is to be hoped for, this is called right intention.

「诸贤！云何正志？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中心伺、遍伺、随顺伺，可念则念，可望则望，是名正志。

Virtuous ones! What is right speech? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, the abandonment, elimination, non-practice, non-performance, non-association, and non-encounter of the four kinds of good verbal action, and the avoidance, elimination, non-practice, non-performance, non-association, and non-encounter of all other verbal evil actions, this is called right speech.

「诸贤！云何正语？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中除口四妙行，诸余口恶行远离除断，不行不作，不合不会，是名正语。

Virtuous ones! What is right action? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, the abandonment, elimination, non-practice, non-performance, non-association, and non-encounter of the three kinds of good bodily action, and the avoidance, elimination, non-practice, non-performance, non-association, and non-encounter of all other bodily evil actions, this is called right action.

「诸贤！云何正业？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中除身三妙行，诸余身恶行远离除断，不行不作，不合不会，是名正业。

Virtuous ones! What is right livelihood? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, the non-pursuit of wrong livelihood, the non-pursuit of livelihood with excessive desire and dissatisfaction, the non-pursuit of livelihood by various arts and spells, but the pursuit of livelihood with the Dharma, not with the non-Dharma, the pursuit of livelihood with the Dharma, not with the non-Dharma, in regard to robes, food, bedding, and seats, this is called right livelihood.

「诸贤！云何正命？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中非无理求，不以多欲无厌足，不为种种伎术呪说邪命活，但以法求衣，不以非法，亦以法求食、床座，不以非法，是名正命。

Virtuous ones! What is right effort? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, the effort, expedient means, single-minded diligence, striving, and determination, without slackening, not retreating, and properly subduing the mind, this is called right effort.

「诸贤！云何正方便？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中若有精进方便，一向精勤求，有力趣向，专著不舍，亦不衰退，正伏其心，是名正方便。

Virtuous ones! What is right mindfulness? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, if the mind is mindful, turns away from unmindfulness, is mindful everywhere, remembers, remembers again, is mindful, and does not forget what should be remembered, this is called right mindfulness.

「诸贤！云何正念？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中若心顺念、背不向念、念遍、念忆、复忆、心心、不忘心之所应，是名正念。

Virtuous ones! What is right concentration? It means when a noble disciple recollects suffering as suffering, origin as origin, cessation as cessation, and path as path, or contemplates past deeds, or learns to recollect all conditioned phenomena, or sees the danger in all conditioned phenomena, or sees the cessation of nirvana, or contemplates the mind liberated from attachment with a good mind, in that, if the mind abides, abides in concentration, abides in accordance, is not distracted, not scattered, collected, restrained, and rightly concentrated, this is called right concentration.

「诸贤！云何正定？谓圣弟子念苦是苦时，习是习、灭是灭，念道是道时，或观本所作，或学念诸行，或见诸行灾患，或见涅槃止息，或无著念观善心解脱时，于中若心住、禅住、顺住，不乱不散，摄止正定，是名正定。

Virtuous ones! The past time is the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering, the future and present time is the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering. It is true, not false, not apart from suchness, not inverted, truly real, in accordance with suchness, possessed by the noble ones, known by the noble ones, seen by the noble ones, realized by the noble ones, attained by the noble ones, fully awakened to by the noble ones. Therefore, it is said to be the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering.

「诸贤！过去时是苦灭道圣谛，未来、现在时是苦灭道圣谛，真谛不虚，不离于如，亦非颠倒，真谛审实，合如是谛，圣所有，圣所知，圣所见，圣所了，圣所得，圣所等正觉，是故说苦灭道圣谛。」

Then there is a verse:

于是颂曰：

The Buddha clearly understands all dharmas, seeing immeasurable good qualities. The
truths of suffering, origin, cessation, and path are well revealed and distinguished.

「佛明达诸法，　　见无量善德，苦习灭道谛，　　善显现分别。」

Thus spoke Venerable Sariputra. The bhikṣus, having heard Venerable Sariputra's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

尊者舍梨子所说如是。彼诸比丘闻尊者舍梨子所说，欢喜奉行。

The eleventh chapter on the analysis of the noble truths is completed.
