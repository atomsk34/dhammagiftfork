MĀ 4

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 1 七法品

4. The Water Parable （四）水喻经（初一日诵）

Parallel to: AN 7.15 / EĀ 39.3

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Shravasti, staying at the Jeta Grove Anathapindika Monastery. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the monks, saying, "I shall now explain to you the seven types of people who are like water. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Consider it well and keep it in mind." At that time, the monks accepted the teaching and listened.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我当为汝说七水人。谛听，谛听！善思念之。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "What are the seven? There is someone who always lies in the water; there is someone who emerges from the water and sinks back in; there is someone who emerges from the water and stays; there is someone who emerges from the water and stays, and after staying, observes; there is someone who emerges from the water and stays, and after staying and observing, crosses over; there is someone who emerges from the water and stays, and after staying and observing, crosses over, and after crossing over, reaches the other shore; there is someone who emerges from the water and stays, and after staying and observing, crosses over, and after crossing over, reaches the other shore, and after reaching the other shore, tells those on the shore. Thus, I shall now explain to you the seven types of people who are like water. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Consider it well and keep it in mind." At that time, the monks accepted the teaching and listened.

佛言：「云何为七？或有一人常卧水中；或复有人出水还没；或复有人出水而住；或复有人出水而住，住已而观；或复有人出水而住，住已而观，观已而渡；或复有人出水而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸；或复有人出水而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸，至彼岸已，谓住岸人。如是，我当复为汝说七水喻人。谛听，谛听！善思念之。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "What are the seven? There is someone who always lies down; there is someone who emerges and sinks back in; there is someone who emerges and stays; there is someone who emerges and stays, and after staying, observes; there is someone who emerges and stays, and after staying and observing, crosses over; there is someone who emerges and stays, and after staying and observing, crosses over, and after crossing over, reaches the other shore; there is someone who emerges and stays, and after staying and observing, crosses over, and after crossing over, reaches the other shore, and after reaching the other shore, tells those on the shore. These seven types of people who are like water, I have explained briefly. As I have explained above, as I have established above, what do you understand? What do you distinguish? What are the causes and conditions?"

佛言：「云何为七？或有人常卧；或复有人出已还没；或复有人出已而住；或复有人出已而住，住已而观；或复有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡；或复有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸；或复有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸，至彼岸已，谓住岸梵志。此七水喻人，我略说也。如上所] cf.［丽】说，如上施设。汝知何义？何所分别？有何因缘？」

At that time, the monks said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma, the Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. Please explain it. After hearing it, we will be able to know the meaning extensively."

时，诸比丘白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you." The monks accepted the teaching and listened.

佛便告曰：「汝等谛听！善思念之。我当为汝分别其义。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "What kind of person is always lying down? It means that some people are covered by unwholesome dharmas, defiled by defilements, experiencing the retribution of unwholesome dharmas, and creating the basis for birth and death. This is called a person who is always lying down. It is like a person drowning and lying in the water. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the first person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

佛言：「云何有人常卧？谓或有人为不善法之所覆盖，染污所染，受恶法报，造生死本，是谓有人常卧。犹人没溺，卧于水中，我说彼人亦复如是，是谓初水喻人，世间谛如有也。

What kind of person emerges but sinks again? It means that a person, having emerged, attains wholesome dharmas of faith, upholds precepts, gives generously, is learned, and cultivates wisdom. But later, they lose their faith and it is not firm, and they lose their precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom and they are not firm. This is called a person who emerges but sinks again. It is like a person drowning who emerges but sinks again. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the second person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

「云何有人出已还没？谓人既出，得信善法，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，修习善法，彼于后时失信不固，失持戒、布施、多闻、智慧而不坚固，是谓有人出已还没。犹人溺水，既出还没，我说彼人亦复如是，是谓第二水喻人，世间谛如有也。

What kind of person emerges and abides? It means that a person, having emerged, attains wholesome dharmas of faith, upholds precepts, gives generously, is learned, and cultivates wisdom. Later, their faith is firm and not lost, and their precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom are firm and not lost. This is called a person who emerges and abides. It is like a person drowning who emerges and abides. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the third person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

「云何有人出已而住？谓人既出，得信善法，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，修习善法，彼于后时信固不失，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，坚固不失，是谓有人出已而住，犹人溺水，出已而住，我说彼人亦复如是，是谓第三水喻人，世间谛如有也。

What kind of person emerges, abides, and observes? It means that a person, having emerged, attains wholesome dharmas of faith, upholds precepts, gives generously, is learned, and cultivates wisdom. Later, their faith is firm and not lost, and their precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom are firm and not lost. Abiding in wholesome dharmas, they truly know suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path to the cessation of suffering. Knowing and seeing thus, their three fetters are exhausted: the view of a self, adherence to precepts and practices, and doubt. With the three fetters exhausted, they attain the state of a stream-enterer, not falling into evil destinies, definitely progressing toward right awakening, experiencing up to seven existences, going and coming between the heavens and the human realm seven times, and then attaining the end of suffering. This is called a person who emerges, abides, and observes. It is like a person drowning who emerges, abides, and observes. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the fourth person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

「云何有人出已而住，住已而观？谓人既出，得信善法，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，修习善法，彼于后时信固不失，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，坚固不失，住善法中，知苦如真，知苦习、知苦灭、知苦灭道如真。彼如是知、如是见，三结便尽，谓身见、戒取、疑，三结已尽，得须陀洹，不堕恶法，定趣正觉，极受七有，天上、人间七往来已，便得苦际，是谓有人出已而住，住已而观。犹人溺水，出已而住，住已而观，我说彼人亦复如是，是谓第四水喻人，世间谛如有也。

What kind of person emerges, abides, observes, and crosses over? It means that a person, having emerged, attains wholesome dharmas of faith, upholds precepts, gives generously, is learned, and cultivates wisdom. Later, their faith is firm and not lost, and their precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom are firm and not lost. Abiding in wholesome dharmas, they truly know suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path to the cessation of suffering. Knowing and seeing thus, their three fetters are exhausted: the view of a self, adherence to precepts and practices, and doubt. With the three fetters exhausted, their desire, anger, and delusion are weakened, and they attain the state of a once-returner, going and coming between the heavens and the human realm once, and then attaining the end of suffering. This is called a person who emerges, abides, observes, and crosses over. It is like a person drowning who emerges, abides, observes, and crosses over. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the fifth person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

「云何有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡？谓人既出，得信善法，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，修习善法。彼于后时信固不失，持戒，布施、多闻、智慧，坚固不失，住善法中，知苦如真，知苦习、知苦灭、知苦灭道如真，如是知、如是见，三结便尽，谓身见、戒取、疑，三结已尽，婬、怒、痴薄，得一往来天上、人间，一往来已，便得苦际，是谓有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡。犹人溺水，出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，我说彼人亦复如是，是谓第五水喻人，世间谛如有也。

What kind of person emerges, abides, observes, crosses over, and reaches the other shore? It means that a person, having emerged, attains wholesome dharmas of faith, upholds precepts, gives generously, is learned, and cultivates wisdom. Later, their faith is firm and not lost, and their precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom are firm and not lost. Abiding in wholesome dharmas, they truly know suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path to the cessation of suffering. Knowing and seeing thus, their five lower fetters are exhausted: desire, anger, the view of a self, adherence to precepts and practices, and doubt. With the five lower fetters exhausted, they are born in that realm and attain parinirvāṇa, attaining the state of non-retrogression and not returning to this world. This is called a person who emerges, abides, observes, crosses over, and reaches the other shore. It is like a person drowning who emerges, abides, observes, crosses over, and reaches the other shore. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the sixth person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

「云何有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸？谓人既出，得信善法，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，修习善法，彼于后时信固不失，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，坚固不失，住善法中，知苦如真，知苦习、知苦灭、知苦灭道如真，如是知、如是见，五下分结尽，谓贪欲、瞋恚、身见、戒取、疑，五下分结尽已，生于彼间，便般涅槃，得不退法，不还此世，是谓有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸。犹人溺水，出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸，我说彼人亦复如是，是谓第六水喻人，世间谛如有也。

What kind of person emerges, abides, observes, crosses over, reaches the other shore, and abides on the other shore as a brahman? It means that a person, having emerged, attains wholesome dharmas of faith, upholds precepts, gives generously, is learned, and cultivates wisdom. Later, their faith is firm and not lost, and their precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom are firm and not lost. Abiding in wholesome dharmas, they truly know suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path to the cessation of suffering. Knowing and seeing thus, their mind is liberated from the taints of desire, existence, and ignorance. Being liberated, they know liberation. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this mundane state - they know it as it really is. This is called a person who emerges, abides, observes, crosses over, reaches the other shore, and abides on the other shore as a brahman. It is like a person drowning who emerges, abides, observes, crosses over, reaches the other shore, and abides on the other shore as a person. I say that such a person is also like that. This is called the seventh person compared to water. It exists in the worldly truth.

「云何有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸，至彼岸已谓住岸梵志？谓人既出，得信善法，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，修习善法，彼于后时信固不失，持戒、布施、多闻、智慧，坚固不失，住善法中，知苦如真，知苦习、知苦灭、知苦灭道如真，如是知、如是见，欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱，解脱已，便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真，是谓有人出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸，至彼岸已，谓住岸梵志。犹人溺水，出已而住，住已而观，观已而渡，渡已至彼岸，至彼岸已，谓住岸人。我说彼人亦复如是，是谓第七水喻人，世间谛如有也。我向所言，当为汝说七水人者，因此故说。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
