MĀ 42

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 5 习相应品

“What is the Purpose?” （四二）何义经

Parallel to: AN 10.1 (~) / AN 11.1

What is the meaning of "not thinking," "recollection," "conscience," and "shame"? Two each of "morality" and
"respect," and two of "original limit." Two of "food," "exhaustion-knowledge," and "nirvana." Mīhī, for the bhikṣu spoke.

何义、不思、念、惭二　　戒敬各二、及本际二食、尽智、说涅槃　　弥醯、即为比丘说

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda rose from his afternoon meditation, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of upholding the precepts?"

尔时，尊者阿难则于晡时从燕坐起，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，却住一面，白曰：「世尊！持戒为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Upholding the precepts means not having regrets. Ānanda! If one upholds the precepts, one will not have regrets."

世尊答曰：「阿难！持戒者，令不悔义。阿难！若有持戒者，便得不悔。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of not having regrets?"

复问：「世尊！不悔为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Not having regrets means being joyful. Ānanda! If one does not have regrets, one will be joyful."

世尊答曰：「阿难！不悔者，令欢悦义。阿难！若有不悔者，便得欢悦。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of being joyful?"

复问：「世尊！欢悦为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Being joyful means being delighted. Ānanda! If one is joyful, one will be delighted."

世尊答曰：「阿难！欢悦者，令喜义。阿难！若有欢悦者，便得喜。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of being delighted?"

复问：「世尊！喜为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Being delighted means being calm. Ānanda! If one is delighted, one will be calm in body."

世尊答曰：「阿难！喜者，令止义。阿难！若有喜者，便得止身。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of being calm?"

复问：「世尊！止为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Being calm means being joyful. Ānanda! If one is calm, one will experience the joy of awakening."

世尊答曰：「阿难！止者，令乐义。阿难！若有止者，便得觉乐。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of joy?"

复问：「世尊！乐为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Joy means being focused. Ānanda! If one is joyful, one will attain a focused mind."

世尊答曰：「阿难！乐者，令定义。阿难！若有乐者，便得定心。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of concentration?"

复问：「世尊！定为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Concentration means seeing things as they truly are and knowing them as they truly are. Ānanda! If one is concentrated, one will see things as they truly are and know them as they truly are."

世尊答曰：「阿难！定者，令见如实、知如真义。阿难！若有定者，便得见如实、知如真。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of seeing things as they truly are and knowing them as they truly are?"

复问：「世尊！见如实、知如真为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Seeing things as they truly are and knowing them as they truly are means the meaning of disenchantment. Ānanda! If one sees things as they truly are and knows them as they truly are, one will become disenchanted."

世尊答曰：「阿难！见如实、知如真者，令厌义。阿难！若有见如实、知如真者，便得厌。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of disenchantment?"

复问：「世尊！厌为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Disenchantment means the meaning of being without desire. Ānanda! If one is disenchanted, one will become without desire."

世尊答曰：「阿难！厌者，令无欲义。阿难！若有厌者，便得无欲。」

He further asked, "World-Honored One! What is the meaning of being without desire?"

复问：「世尊！无欲为何义？」

The World-Honored One replied, "Ānanda! Being without desire means the meaning of liberation. Ānanda! If one is without desire, one will attain liberation from all desire, anger, and delusion. This is how, Ānanda, one attains non-regret through upholding the precepts, joy through non-regret, delight through joy, tranquility through delight, bliss through tranquility, and concentration through bliss. Ānanda, the noble disciple of much learning, through concentration, attains true insight and true knowledge. Through true insight and true knowledge, one attains disenchantment, through disenchantment, one attains being without desire, through being without desire, one attains liberation, through liberation, one knows liberation, and one knows as it truly is that birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and there is no more of this mundane state. Ānanda, this is how the Dharma benefits the Dharma, and the Dharma is the cause of the Dharma. In this way, these precepts lead to the foremost, namely, crossing over from this shore to the other shore."

世尊答曰：「阿难！无欲者，令解脱义。阿难！若有无欲者，便得解脱一切婬、怒、痴。是为，阿难！因持戒便得不悔，因不悔便得欢悦，因欢悦便得喜，因喜便得止，因止便得乐，因乐便得定。阿难！多闻圣弟子因定便得见如实、知如真，因见如实、知如真，便得厌，因厌便得无欲，因无欲便得解脱，因解脱便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。阿难！是为法法相益，法法相因，如是此戒趣至第一，谓度此岸，得至彼岸。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
