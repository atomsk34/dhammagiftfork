MĀ 57

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 5 习相应品

57. Spoken for the Monks （五七）即为比丘说经

Parallel to: AN 9.1

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "For those whose mind-liberation is not yet ripe, wishing to ripen it, there are five practices. What are the five? Bhikṣus! Associating with good friends, being together with good friends, and harmonizing with good friends, for those whose mind-liberation is not yet ripe, wishing to ripen it, this is called the first practice. Furthermore, bhikṣus! Cultivating the precepts, guarding the precepts, and well-restraining deportment and etiquette, seeing even the slightest fault as always fearful, upholding the precepts of learning, for those whose mind-liberation is not yet ripe, wishing to ripen it, this is called the second practice. Furthermore, bhikṣus! Speaking of the meaning of the noble teachings, causing the mind to be soft and free from hindrances, namely speaking of precepts, concentration, wisdom, liberation, knowledge and insight of liberation, gradual reduction, not enjoying gatherings, speaking of few desires, contentment, cutting off, non-attachment, extinction, sitting in meditation, and dependent origination, obtaining such teachings of the śramaṇa, easily obtaining them without difficulty, for those whose mind-liberation is not yet ripe, wishing to ripen it, this is called the third practice. Furthermore, bhikṣus! Always practicing diligence, cutting off unwholesome dharmas, cultivating wholesome dharmas, always arousing one's own mind, single-mindedly firm, for the sake of all wholesome roots, not abandoning skillful means, for those whose mind-liberation is not yet ripe, wishing to ripen it, this is called the fourth practice. Furthermore, bhikṣus! Cultivating wisdom, observing the arising and ceasing of dharmas, obtaining such wisdom, the illumination of noble wisdom, discerning and understanding, with right knowledge exhausting suffering, for those whose mind-liberation is not yet ripe, wishing to ripen it, this is called the fifth practice.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「心解脱未熟，欲令熟者，有五习法。云何为五？比丘！自善知识与善知识俱，善知识共和合，心解脱未熟，欲令熟者，是谓第一习法。复次，比丘！修习禁戒，守护从解脱，又复善摄威仪礼节，见纤芥罪，常怀畏怖，受持学戒，心解脱未熟，欲令熟者，是谓第二习法。复次，比丘！谓所可说圣有义，令心柔软，使心无盖，谓说戒、说定、说慧、说解脱、说解脱知见、说渐损、说不乐聚会、说少欲、说知足、说断、说无欲、说灭、说燕坐、说缘起，得如是比沙门所说，具得易，不难得，心解脱未熟，欲令熟者，是谓第三习法。复次，比丘！常行精进，断恶不善，修诸善法，恒自起意，专一坚固，为诸善本，不舍方便，心解脱未熟，欲令熟者，是谓第四习法。复次，比丘！修行智慧，观兴衰法，得如此智，圣慧明达，分别晓了，以正尽苦，心解脱未熟，欲令熟者，是谓第五习法。

"Having practiced these five practices, they further cultivate four dharmas. What are the four? Cultivating impurity to eliminate desire; cultivating loving-kindness to eliminate anger; cultivating inhalation and exhalation to eliminate distracted thoughts; cultivating the perception of impermanence to eliminate the conceit of self. If a bhikṣu associates with good friends, is together with good friends, and harmonizes with good friends, it should be known that he will definitely cultivate the precepts, guard the precepts, and well-restrain deportment and etiquette, seeing even the slightest fault as always fearful, upholding the precepts of learning. If a bhikṣu associates with good friends, is together with good friends, and harmonizes with good friends, it should be known that he will definitely obtain the meaning of the noble teachings, causing the mind to be soft and free from hindrances, namely speaking of precepts, concentration, wisdom, liberation, knowledge and insight of liberation, gradual reduction, not enjoying gatherings, speaking of few desires, contentment, cutting off, non-attachment, extinction, sitting in meditation, and dependent origination, obtaining such teachings of the śramaṇa, easily obtaining them without difficulty. If a bhikṣu associates with good friends, is together with good friends, and harmonizes with good friends, it should be known that he will definitely practice diligence, cutting off unwholesome dharmas, cultivating wholesome dharmas, always arousing his own mind, single-mindedly firm, for the sake of all wholesome roots, not abandoning skillful means. If a bhikṣu associates with good friends, is together with good friends, and harmonizes with good friends, it should be known that he will definitely practice wisdom, observing the arising and ceasing of dharmas, obtaining such wisdom, the illumination of noble wisdom, discerning and understanding, with right knowledge exhausting suffering. If a bhikṣu associates with good friends, is together with good friends, and harmonizes with good friends, it should be known that he will definitely cultivate impurity to eliminate desire; cultivate loving-kindness to eliminate anger; cultivate inhalation and exhalation to eliminate distracted thoughts; cultivate the perception of impermanence to eliminate the conceit of self. If a bhikṣu obtains the perception of impermanence, he will definitely obtain the perception of no-self. If a bhikṣu obtains the perception of no-self, he will then in this very life cut off all conceit of self, and attain peace, cessation, extinction, the unconditioned, nirvāṇa."

「彼有此五习法已，复修四法。云何为四？修恶露，令断欲；修慈，令断恚；修息出息入，令断乱念；修无常想，令断我慢。若比丘自善知识与善知识俱，善知识共和合，当知必修习禁戒，守护从解脱，又复善摄威仪礼节，见纤芥罪，常怀畏怖，受持学戒。若比丘自善知识与善知识俱，善知识共和合，当知必得所可说圣有义，令心柔软，使心无盖，谓说戒、说定、说慧、说解脱、说解脱知见、说渐损、说不乐聚会、说少欲、说知足、说断、说无欲、说灭、说燕坐、说缘起，得如是比沙门所说，具得易，不难得。若比丘自善知识与善知识俱，善知识共和合，当知必行精进，断恶不善，修诸善法，恒自起意，专一坚固，为诸善本，不舍方便。若比丘自善知识与善知识俱，善知识共和合，当知必行智慧，观兴衰法，得如此智，圣慧明达，分别晓了，以正尽苦。若比丘自善知识与善知识俱，善知识共和合，当知必修恶露，令断欲；修慈，令断恚；修息出息入，令断乱念；修无常想，令断我慢。若比丘得无常想者，必得无我想，若比丘得无我想者，便于现法断一切我慢，得息、灭、尽、无为、涅槃。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
