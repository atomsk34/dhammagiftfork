MĀ 61

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 6 王相应品

61. The Cow Dung Parable （六一）牛粪喻经

Parallel to: SN 22.96 / SĀ 264

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, a bhikṣu was sitting in meditation in a quiet place, and he thought like this: "Are there any forms that are permanent, unchanging, and always blissful, that exist for a long time? Are there any feelings, perceptions, mental formations, and consciousness that are permanent, unchanging, and always blissful, that exist for a long time?"

尔时，有一比丘在安静处，燕坐思惟而作是念：「颇复有色常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存耶？颇有觉、想、行、识常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存耶？」

That bhikṣu rose from his meditation in the afternoon, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head in reverence, sat to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! I was sitting in meditation in a quiet place, and I thought like this: 'Are there any forms that are permanent, unchanging, and always blissful, that exist for a long time? Are there any feelings, perceptions, mental formations, and consciousness that are permanent, unchanging, and always blissful, that exist for a long time?'"

彼一比丘则于晡时从燕坐起，往诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，白曰：「世尊！我今在安静处燕坐思惟而作是念：『颇复有色常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存耶？颇有觉、想、行、识常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存耶？』」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu: "There are no forms that are permanent, unchanging, and always blissful, that exist for a long time. There are no feelings, perceptions, mental formations, and consciousness that are permanent, unchanging, and always blissful, that exist for a long time."

佛告比丘：「无有一色常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存者，无有觉、想、行、识常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存者。」

Then the World-Honored One took a small amount of cow dung with his fingernail and said: "Bhikṣu! Do you see me taking a small amount of cow dung with my fingernail?"

于是，世尊以手指爪抄少牛粪，告曰：「比丘！汝今见我以手指爪抄少牛粪耶？」

The bhikṣu replied: "I see, World-Honored One!"

比丘白曰：「见也。世尊！」

The Buddha said again: "Bhikṣu! There is no form at all that is permanent, unchanging, and always blissful. There is no feeling, perception, formation, or consciousness at all that is permanent, unchanging, and always blissful. Why is this? Bhikṣu! I remember in the past, for a long time I practiced merit, and having practiced merit for a long time, I experienced the reward of long-lasting happiness. Bhikṣu! In the past, I practiced loving-kindness for seven years, and seven times I was reborn and destroyed, not coming to this world. When the world was destroyed, I was reborn in the Ābhāsvara Heaven. When the world was formed, I descended and was born in the empty palace of Brahmā. Among the Brahmas, I became the Great Brahmā, and in other places, I was the Lord of the Heaven of the Thirty-three for a thousand times, and thirty-six times I was Śakra Devānām-Indra. I was also a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head for countless times.

佛复告曰比丘：「如是，无有少色常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存也。如是，无有少觉、想、行、识常住不变，而一向乐，恒久存也。所以者何？比丘！我忆昔时长夜作福，长作福已，长受乐报。比丘！我在昔时七年行慈，七反成败，不来此世，世败坏时，生晃昱天，世成立时，来下生空梵宫殿中，于彼梵中作大梵天，余处千反，作自在天王，三十六反，作天帝释，复无量反，作刹利顶生王。

Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand great elephants, adorned with fine riding equipment and various treasures, covered with white pearl ornaments, with the elephant king Saha at the head. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand horses, adorned with fine riding equipment and various treasures, adorned with gold and silver, with the horse king Māra at the head. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand chariots, adorned with four kinds of decorations, adorned with various treasures, with the lion, tiger, leopard, and tiger-striped skins woven into various colors, with various decorations, with the famous and swift chariot called 'Pleasure Sound' at the head. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand great cities, extremely rich and happy, with many people, with the city of King Kuśa at the head. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand towers, with four kinds of precious towers made of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and crystal, with the Dharma Hall at the head.

「比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千大象，被好乘具，众宝校饰，白珠珞覆，于娑贺象王为首。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千马，被好乘具，众宝庄饰，金银珓珞，［马＊毛］马王为首。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千车，四种校饰，庄以众好、师子、虎豹斑文之皮，织成杂色，种种庄饰，极利疾名乐声车为首。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千大城，极大富乐，多有人民，拘舍惒堤王城为首。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千楼，四种宝楼，金、银、琉璃及水精，正法殿为首。

Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand thrones, with four kinds of precious thrones made of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and crystal, covered with woolen blankets, covered with brocade, gauze, and fine silk, with a body-covering cloth, with pillows at both ends, with the Kaṇḍa-pāṭala and Pañcālika robes. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand pairs of garments, with the first-class garments, brocade garments, silk garments, cotton garments, and Kaṇḍa-pāṭala garments. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand women, with radiant bodies, pure and bright, with beauty surpassing humans, slightly inferior to the gods, with dignified appearance, pleasing to behold, fully adorned with various treasures, all from the kṣatriya caste, with countless other clans. Bhikṣu! When I was a kṣatriya king born from the crown of the head, I had eighty-four thousand kinds of food, constantly offered day and night, prepared for me, wishing for me to eat.

「比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千御座，四种宝座，金、银、琉璃及水精，敷以氍氀、毾［毯－炎＋登］，覆以锦绮罗縠，有衬体被，两头安枕，加陵伽波惒逻、波遮悉多罗那。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千双衣，初摩衣、锦衣、缯衣、劫贝衣、加陵伽波惒逻衣。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千女，身体光泽，晈洁明净，美色过人，小不及天，姿容端正，覩者欢悦，众宝璎珞严饰具足，尽刹利种女，余族无量。比丘！我作刹利顶生王时，有八万四千种食，昼夜常供，为我故设，欲令我食。

Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand kinds of food, there is one kind of food that is extremely delicious and pure, with countless flavors, which I always eat. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand women, there is one kṣatriya woman who is the most dignified and beautiful, who always attends to me. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand pairs of garments, there is one pair of garments, either first-class garments, brocade garments, silk garments, cotton garments, or Kaṇḍa-pāṭala garments, which I always wear. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand thrones, there is one throne, either made of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, or crystal, covered with woolen blankets, covered with brocade, gauze, and fine silk, with a body-covering cloth, with pillows at both ends, with the Kaṇḍa-pāṭala and Pañcālika robes, which I always sit on. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand towers, there is one tower, either made of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, or crystal, called the Dharma Hall, which I always reside in.

「比丘！彼八万四千种食中，有一种食，极美净洁，无量种味，是我常所食。比丘！彼八万四千女中，有一刹利女，最端正姝好，常奉侍我。比丘！彼八万四千双衣中，有一双衣，或初摩衣、或锦衣、或缯衣、或劫贝衣、或加陵伽波惒逻衣，是我常所著。比丘！彼八万四千御座中，有一御座，或金、或银、或琉璃、或水精，敷以氍氀、毾［毯－炎＋登］，覆以锦绮罗縠，有衬体被，两头安枕，加陵伽波惒逻、波遮悉多罗那，是我常所卧。比丘！彼八万四千楼观中，有一楼观，或金、或银、或琉璃、或水精，名正法殿，是我常所住。

Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand great cities, there is one city that is extremely rich and happy, with many people, called Kuśāvatī, which I always live in. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand chariots, there is one chariot adorned with various good things, with tiger and leopard-spotted skins woven into various colors, with various decorations, extremely sharp and fast, called the Rati-svara chariot, which I always ride to the pleasure garden to view. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand horses, there is one horse with a dark blue body and a head like a crow, called the Horse King, which I always ride to the pleasure garden to view. Bhikṣu! Among those eighty-four thousand great elephants, there is one elephant with a completely white body and seven limbs that are all correct, called the Uṣaṇa elephant king, which I always ride to the pleasure garden to view.

「比丘！彼八万四千大城中，而有一城，极大富乐，多有人民，名拘舍惒堤，是我常所居。比丘！彼八万四千车中，而有一车，庄以众好、师子、虎豹斑文之皮，织成杂色，种种庄饰，极利疾名乐声车，是我常所载，至观望园观。比丘！彼八万四千马中，而有一马，体绀青色，头像如乌，名［马＊毛］马王，是我常所骑，至观望园观。比丘！彼八万四千大象中，而有一象，举体极白，七支尽正，名于娑贺象王，是我常所乘，至观望园观。

"Bhikṣu! I thought like this: 'What are the fruits of karma, what are the results of karma, that cause me to have great supernatural powers, great authority, great blessings, and great spiritual power today?' Bhikṣu! I further thought like this: 'These are the fruits of the three karmas, the results of the three karmas, that cause me to have great supernatural powers, great authority, great blessings, and great spiritual power today. One is giving, two is taming, and three is guarding.' Bhikṣu! You see that all possessions are exhausted and destroyed, and supernatural powers are also lost. Bhikṣu! What do you think? Is form permanent or impermanent?"

「比丘！我作此念：『是何业果，为何业报，令我今日有大如意足，有大威德，有大福祐，有大威神？』比丘！我复作此念：『是三业果，为三业报，令我今日有大如意足，有大威德，有大福祐，有大威神。一者布施，二者调御，三者守护。』比丘！汝观彼一切所有尽灭，如意足亦失。比丘！于意云何？色为有常，为无常耶？」

"He replied: "Impermanent, World-Honored One!

答曰：「无常也。世尊！」

He further asked: "If it is impermanent, is it suffering or not suffering?"

复问曰：「若无常者，是苦，非苦耶？」

He replied: "Suffering, changing, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「苦、变易也。世尊！」

He further asked: "If it is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, can the learned noble disciples regard it as 'I', 'mine', or 'I am that'?"

复问曰：「若无常、苦、变易法者，是多闻圣弟子颇受是我，是我所，我是彼所耶？」

He replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「不也。世尊！」

He further asked: "Bhikkhu, what do you think? Are consciousness, perception, volition, and cognition permanent or impermanent?"

复问曰：「比丘！于意云何？觉、想、行、识为有常，为无常耶？」

He replied: "Impermanent, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「无常也。世尊！」

He further asked: "If it is impermanent, is it suffering or not suffering?"

复问曰：「若无常者，是苦，非苦耶？」

He replied: "Suffering, changing, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「苦、变易也。世尊！」

He further asked: "If it is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, can the learned noble disciples regard it as 'I', 'mine', or 'I am that'?"

复问曰：「若无常、苦、变易法者，是多闻圣弟子颇受是我，是我所，我是彼所耶？」

He replied: "No, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「不也。世尊！」

"Therefore, bhikkhus, you should train thus: 'Whatever form there is, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, all that form is not mine, not I, not my self.' One should see it as it really is with right wisdom. Whatever feeling there is, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, all that feeling is not mine, not I, not my self. One should see it as it really is with right wisdom. Whatever perception there is, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, all that perception is not mine, not I, not my self. One should see it as it really is with right wisdom. Whatever volitional formations there are, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, all those volitional formations are not mine, not I, not my self. One should see them as they really are with right wisdom. Whatever consciousness there is, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, all that consciousness is not mine, not I, not my self. One should see it as it really is with right wisdom. If a learned noble disciple sees this thus, he becomes disenchanted with form, disenchanted with feeling, disenchanted with perception, disenchanted with volitional formations, disenchanted with consciousness. Being disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate. Through dispassion, he is liberated. When liberated, he knows he is liberated. He understands: 'Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is no return to any state of existence.'"

「是故，比丘！汝应如是学。若有色，或过去、或未来、或现在，或内、或外，或麤、或细，或好、或恶，或近、或远，彼一切非我，非我所，我非彼所，当以慧观知如真。若有觉、想、行、识，或过去、或未来、或现在，或内、或外，或麤、或细，或好、或恶，或近、或远，彼一切非我，非我所，我非彼所，当以慧观知如真。比丘！若多闻圣弟子如是观者，彼便厌色，厌觉、想、行、识，厌已便无欲，无欲已便解脱，解脱已便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。」

Then the bhikkhu, having delighted and rejoiced in the Blessed One’s statement, rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him keeping the right side toward him, and departed.

于是，彼比丘闻佛所说，善受善持，即从坐起，稽首佛足，绕三匝而去。

Having been thus instructed by the Blessed One, the bhikkhu dwelled alone, withdrawn, diligent, ardent, and resolute. Dwelling alone, withdrawn, diligent, ardent, and resolute, he by his own super-knowledge discovered the supreme goal of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from home into homelessness, and having entered upon it, dwelled in it. He directly knew: ‘Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being.’

彼比丘受佛化已，独住远离，心无放逸，修行精勤，彼独住远离，心无放逸，修行精勤已，族姓子所为，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，唯无上梵行讫，于现法中自知自觉，自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。如是彼比丘知法已，乃至得阿罗诃。

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikkhus, having heard what the Buddha said, were delighted and remembered it well.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
