MĀ 71

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 6 王相应品

71. To Pāyāsi （七一）蜱肆经

Parallel to: DN 23 / DĀ 7

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa was traveling in the country of Kosala with a large assembly of bhikkhus. He went to Sāvatthī and stayed in the Sītavana near the village.

一时，尊者鸠摩罗迦叶游拘萨罗国，与大比丘众俱，往诣斯惒提，住彼村北尸摄惒林。

At that time, there was a king named Pāyāsi in Sāvatthī. He was extremely prosperous and happy, with immeasurable wealth and livestock. His industries were innumerable, and he was fully endowed with fiefs, food, and estates. The springs, ponds, grass, and trees of Sāvatthī all belonged to the king, granted to him by King Pasenadi of Kosala.

尔时，斯惒提中有王名蜱肆，极大丰乐，资财无量，畜牧产业不可称计，封户、食邑种种具足，斯惒提邑泉池草木一切属王，从拘萨罗王波斯匿之所封授。

Then the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī heard that the śramaṇa named Kumara Kāśyapa was traveling in the country of Kosala with a large assembly of bhikkhus. He had come to Sāvatthī and was staying in the Sītavana near the village. It was said that this śramaṇa Kumara Kāśyapa had a great reputation that was heard in the ten directions. Kumara Kāśyapa was unimpeded in his eloquence and his words were subtle. He was a learned arhat. If one could see this arhat and pay respect and serve him, it would be a great gain. We should go and see this śramaṇa Kumara Kāśyapa. The brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī each went with their own kind and left Sāvatthī together, going north to the Sītavana.

于是，斯惒提梵志、居士，闻有沙门名鸠摩罗迦叶，游拘萨罗国，与大比丘众俱，来至此斯惒提，住彼村北尸摄惒林。彼沙门鸠摩罗迦叶有大名称，周闻十方，鸠摩罗迦叶才辩无碍，所说微妙，彼是多闻阿罗诃也。若有见此阿罗诃恭敬礼事者，快得善利，我等可往见彼沙门鸠摩罗迦叶。斯惒提梵志、居士各与等类相随而行，从斯惒提并共北出，至尸摄惒林。

At that time, King Pāyāsi was on the main hall. He saw from afar the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī each going with their own kind, leaving Sāvatthī together and going north to the Sītavana. Having seen this, he asked his attendant, "Why are the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī each going with their own kind today, leaving Sāvatthī together and going north to the Sītavana?"

是时，蜱肆王在正殿上，遥见斯惒提梵志、居士，各与等类相随而行，从斯惒提并共北出，至尸摄惒林。蜱肆王见已，告侍人曰：「此斯惒提梵志、居士，今日何故各与等类相随而行，从斯惒提并共北出，至尸摄惒林？」

The attendant replied, "Your Majesty! The brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī heard that the śramaṇa named Kumara Kāśyapa was traveling in the country of Kosala with a large assembly of bhikkhus. He had come to Sāvatthī and was staying in the Sītavana near the village. It was said that this śramaṇa Kumara Kāśyapa had a great reputation that was heard in the ten directions. Kumara Kāśyapa was unimpeded in his eloquence and his words were subtle. He was a learned arhat. If one could see this arhat and pay respect and serve him, it would be a great gain. We should go and see this śramaṇa Kumara Kāśyapa. Your Majesty! That is why the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī each went with their own kind and left Sāvatthī together, going north to the Sītavana.

侍人白曰：「天王！彼斯惒提梵志、居士，闻有沙门鸠摩罗迦叶游拘萨罗国，与大比丘众俱，来至此斯惒提，住彼村北尸摄惒林。天王！彼沙门鸠摩罗迦叶有大名称，周闻十方，鸠摩罗迦叶才辩无碍，所说微妙，彼是多闻阿罗诃也。若有见此阿罗诃恭敬礼事者，快得善利，我等可往见彼沙门鸠摩罗迦叶。天王！是故斯惒提梵志、居士，各与等类相随而行，从斯惒提并共北出，至尸摄惒林。」

King Pāyāsi, having heard this, told his attendant, "Go to those brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī and tell them, 'King Pāyāsi says to the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī, "Wait, I will go with you to see that śramaṇa Kumara Kāśyapa. You are foolish! Do not be deceived by him. There is no afterlife, no beings are born. I see and say that there is no afterlife, no beings are born."'"

蜱肆王闻已，告侍人曰：「汝往诣彼斯惒提梵志、居士所，而语之曰：『蜱肆王告斯惒提梵志、居士：「诸贤可住，我与汝等共往见彼沙门鸠摩罗迦叶。汝等愚痴！勿为彼所欺，为有后世，有众生生。我如是见，如是说，无有后世，无众生生。」』」

The attendant, having received the order, went to those brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī and said to them, "King Pāyāsi says to the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī, "Wait, I will go with you to see that śramaṇa Kumara Kāśyapa. You are foolish! Do not be deceived by him. There is no afterlife, no beings are born. I see and say that there is no afterlife, no beings are born."'"

侍人受教，即往诣彼斯惒提梵志、居士所，而语之曰：「蜱肆王告斯惒提梵志、居士：『诸贤可住，我与汝等共往见彼沙门鸠摩罗迦叶。汝等愚痴！勿为彼所欺，为有后世，有众生生。我如是见，如是说：无有后世，无众生生。』」

Having heard this order, the brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī replied to the attendant, "We will do as the king commands."

斯惒提梵志、居士闻此教已，答侍人曰：「輙如来勅。」

The attendant returned and reported, "The order of the king has been announced. Those brāhmaṇas and householders of Sāvatthī are waiting for the king. May the king know the time."

侍人还启，已宣王命：「彼斯惒提梵志、居士住待天王，唯愿天王宜知是时。」

Then King Pasenadi of Kosala ordered his charioteer, "Quickly prepare the chariot. I wish to go now."

时，蜱肆王即勅御者：「汝速严驾，我今欲行。」

The charioteer received the order, quickly prepared the chariot, and returned to report, "The chariot is ready. Please do as the king wishes."

御者受教，即速严驾讫，还白王：「严驾已办，随天王意。」

Then King Pasenadi of Kosala mounted the chariot and went to where the brahmin householders of Sāketa were. They went together to the Śiṣṭa forest. When King Pasenadi saw Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa in the grove, he alighted from the chariot and approached Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa on foot. They exchanged greetings and sat to one side. The king asked, "Kāśyapa, I wish to ask something. May I?"

时，蜱肆王即乘车出，往诣斯惒提梵志、居士所，与共北] cf.［丽】行至尸摄惒林。时，蜱肆王遥见尊者鸠摩罗迦叶在树林间，即下车步进，往诣尊者鸠摩罗迦叶所，共相问讯，却坐一面，问曰：「迦叶！我今欲问，宁见听耶？」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, if you wish to ask, ask. I will think about it after hearing."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！若欲问者，便可问之，我闻已当思。」

Then King Pasenadi asked, "Kāśyapa, I see and say like this: there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings. What do you think, monk Kumāra Kāśyapa?"

时，蜱肆王即便问曰：「迦叶！我如是见，如是说，无有后世，无众生生。沙门鸠摩罗迦叶！于意云何？」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, I ask you, answer as you see fit. What do you think, king? Are the sun and moon of this world or the next world?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！我今问王，随所解答。于王意云何？今此日月，为是今世，为后世耶？」

Pasenadi replied, "Though the monk Kumāra Kāśyapa says this, I still see and say like this: there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings."

蜱肆答曰：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我如是见，如是说，无有后世，无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, is there any greater evil than this?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复更有恶而过此耶？」

Pasenadi replied, "Yes, Kāśyapa, there is. Kāśyapa, I have relatives who are sick and suffering. I went to them and said, 'You should know that I see and say like this: there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings. Kāśyapa, there are some ascetics and brahmins who see and say like this: there is an afterlife, there is rebirth of beings. I have never believed what they say. They also say, "If there are sons or daughters who engage in evil deeds, are not diligent, lazy, indolent, jealous, stingy, do not open their hands, are not generous, and are extremely attached to wealth, because of this, when their body perishes and their life ends, they will definitely go to a bad place and be reborn in hell." If what those ascetics and brahmins say is true, then you, my relatives, engage in evil deeds, are not diligent, lazy, indolent, jealous, stingy, do not open your hands, are not generous, and are extremely attached to wealth. If your body perishes and your life ends, you will definitely go to a bad place and be reborn in hell. Then you can come back and tell me, "Pasenadi, the suffering in hell is like this and like that." If that happens, I will see it for myself.' They heard my words and accepted my instructions, but none of them came back to tell me, 'Pasenadi, the suffering in hell is like this and like that.' Kāśyapa, because of this, I think there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings."

蜱肆答曰：「如是。迦叶！复更有恶。迦叶！我有亲亲疾病困笃，我往彼所，到已，谓言：『汝等当知我如是见，如是说：无有后世，无众生生。亲亲！有沙门、梵志如是见，如是说言：有后世，有众生生。我常不信彼之所说。彼复作是语：「若有男女作恶行，不精进，事惰、懈怠、嫉妬、悭贪，不舒手，不庶几，极著财物，彼因缘此，身坏命终，必至恶处，生地狱中。」若彼沙门、梵志所说是真实者，汝等是我亲亲，作恶行，不精进事惰、懈怠、嫉妬、悭贪，不舒手，不庶几，极著财物，若汝等身坏命终，必至恶处，生地狱中者，可还语我：「蜱肆！彼地狱中如是如是苦。」若当尔者，我便现见。』彼闻我语，受我教已，都无有来语我言：『蜱肆！彼地狱中如是如是苦。』迦叶！因此事故，我作是念：无有后世，无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, I will ask the king again, and he will answer according to his understanding. If a king's men arrest a criminal and bring him before the king, saying, 'Your Majesty, this person has committed a crime, please punish him,' the king tells them, 'Take him away, tie his hands behind his back, make him ride a donkey, beat a broken drum that sounds like a donkey braying, proclaim it all over the city, take him out through the southern gate, place him under a tall pole, and behead him.' They follow the king's instructions, tie the criminal's hands behind his back, make him ride a donkey, beat a broken drum that sounds like a donkey braying, proclaim it all over the city, take him out through the southern gate, place him under a tall pole, and are about to behead him. As he is about to die, the criminal tells the executioner, 'Please wait a moment, I want to see my parents, wife, children, slaves, and servants, please let me go for a while.' What do you think, Pasenadi, would the executioner release the criminal and let him go for a while?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！我复问王，随所解答。若有王人收缚罪者，送至王所，白曰：『天王！此人有罪，王当治之。』王告彼曰：『汝等将去反缚两手，令彼骑驴，打破败鼓，声如驴鸣，遍宣令已，从城南门出，坐高标下，斩断其头。』彼受教已，即反缚罪人，令其骑驴，打破败鼓，声如驴鸣，遍宣令已，从城南门出，坐高标下，欲斩其头。此人临死语彼卒曰：『汝且小住，我欲得见父母、妻子、奴婢、使人，听我暂去。』于王意云何？彼卒宁当放斯罪人，听暂去耶？」

Pasenadi replied, "No, Kāśyapa."

蜱肆答曰：「不也。迦叶！」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, it is the same with the king's relatives. If they engage in evil conduct, are lazy, indolent, jealous, stingy, do not open their hands, are not generous, are extremely attached to wealth, and because of that, when their body breaks up and their life ends, they certainly go to a bad place, to a hell realm. When the hell wardens seize them and torment them severely, they say to the wardens, 'Hell wardens, please wait a moment, do not torment me, I want to go to King Pasenadi for a while and tell him, "It is like this and like that in hell," so that he can see it for himself.' What do you think, Pasenadi, would the hell wardens release the king's relatives and let them come for a while?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！王亲亲者亦复如是，作恶行，不精进，事惰、懈怠、嫉妬、悭贪、不舒手，不庶几，极著财物，彼因缘此，身坏命终，必至恶处，生地狱中。地狱卒捉极苦治时，彼语卒曰：『诸地狱卒！汝等小住，莫苦治我，我欲暂去诣蜱肆王，告语之曰：「彼地狱中如是如是苦。」令彼现见。』于王意云何？彼地狱卒宁当放王亲亲令暂来耶？」

Pasenadi replied, "No, Kāśyapa."

蜱肆答曰：「不也。迦叶！」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, you should view the afterlife like this, not as seen by the fleshly eye. Pasenadi, if there are monastics and brahmins who have cut off desire, are heading towards the cessation of desire, have cut off anger, are heading towards the cessation of anger, have cut off delusion, are heading towards the cessation of delusion, they see with their purified divine eye, surpassing that of humans, the passing away and rebirth of beings, the beautiful and ugly, the good and bad, going to a good or bad place, according to the deeds of beings, seeing it as it really is."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！汝应如是观于后世，莫如肉眼之所见也。蜱肆！若有沙门、梵志断绝离欲，趣向离欲，断绝离恚，趣向离恚，断绝离痴，趣向离痴，彼以清净天眼出过于人，见此众生死时、生时，好色、恶色，或妙、不妙，往来善处及不善处，随此众生之所作业，见其如真。」

King Pasenadi said again, "Although the monk Kumāra Kāśyapa says this, I still see and say that there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings."

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我如是见，如是说：无有后世，无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, is there any other evil greater than this?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复更有恶而过此耶？」

Pasenadi replied, "Yes, Kāśyapa, there is another evil. Kāśyapa, I have relatives who are sick and suffering. I went to them and said, 'You should know that I see and say this: there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings. Relatives, there are some ascetics and brahmins who see and say this: there is an afterlife, there is rebirth of beings. I have never believed what they say. They also say this: "If there are men and women who practice well, diligently, without laziness, without jealousy, without stinginess, with open hands, generous, open-minded, giving to orphans and the poor, always delighting in giving, not attached to wealth, because of this, when their body breaks up and their life ends, they will surely ascend to a good place and be reborn in heaven." If what those ascetics and brahmins say is true, then you, my relatives, practice well, diligently, without laziness, without jealousy, without stinginess, with open hands, generous, open-minded, giving to orphans and the poor, always delighting in giving, not attached to wealth. If when your body breaks up and your life ends, you will surely ascend to a good place and be reborn in heaven, then you can come back and tell me, "Pasenadi, heaven is like this and like that." If that is so, then I will see it for myself." They heard my words and accepted my instructions, but none of them came back to tell me, "Pasenadi, heaven is like this and like that." Kāśyapa, because of this, I think there is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings.

蜱肆答曰：「如是。迦叶！复更有恶。迦叶！我有亲亲疾病困笃，我往彼所，到已谓言：『汝等当知我如是见，如是说：无有后世，无众生生。亲亲！有沙门、梵志如是见，如是说言：有后世，有众生生。我常不信彼之所说。彼复作是语：「若有男女妙行精进，精勤不懈，无有嫉妬，亦不悭贪，舒手，庶几，开意放舍，给诸孤穷，常乐施与，不著财物，彼因缘此，身坏命终，必升善处，乃生天上。」若彼沙门、梵志所说是真实者，汝等是我亲亲，妙行精进，精勤不懈，无有嫉妬，亦不悭贪，舒手，庶几，开意放舍，给诸孤穷，常乐施与，不著财物，若汝等身坏命终，必升善处，生天上者，可还语我：「蜱肆！天上如是如是乐。」若当尔者，我便现见。』彼闻我语，受我教已，都无有来语我言：『蜱肆！天上如是如是乐。』迦叶！因此事故，我作是念：无有后世，无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pasenadi, let me give you an analogy. The wise understand the meaning through an analogy. Pasenadi, it's like a cesspit outside a village, deep enough to cover a person's head, full of excrement. And a person falls into it and sinks to the bottom. If someone out of compassion for that person, seeking their welfare and benefit, seeking their peace and happiness, were to carefully pull them out of the cesspit, scrape them with bamboo strips, wipe them with tree leaves, and wash them with warm water. And later, after they have bathed and cleansed themselves, anointed their body with perfume, ascended to the royal palace, and enjoyed the five sense pleasures. What do you think, Pasenadi? Would that person remember the cesspit fondly, praise it, and want to see it again?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹村邑外有都圊厕，深没人头，粪满其中，而有一人堕没厕底。若复有人为慈愍彼，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐，便从厕上徐徐挽出，刮以竹片，拭以树叶，洗以暖汤。彼于后时净澡浴已，以香涂身，升正殿上，以五所欲而娱乐之。于王意云何？彼人宁复忆念先厕，欢喜称誉，复欲见耶？」

Pasenadi replied, "No, Kāśyapa. If someone else were to remember that cesspit fondly, praise it, and want to see it, I would not love that person. How much less would I remember the cesspit fondly, praise it, and want to see it myself? That is impossible."

蜱肆答曰：「不也。迦叶！若更有人忆念彼厕，欢喜称誉而欲见者，便不爱此人，况复自忆念先厕，欢喜称誉？复欲见者，是处不然。」

"Pasenadi, if a king has a relative who practices well, is diligent, energetic, without jealousy, not stingy or greedy, open-handed, generous, open-minded, giving to orphans and the poor, always delighting in giving, not attached to wealth, and because of that, when their body breaks up and their life ends, they certainly ascend to a good place and are reborn in heaven. Having been reborn in heaven, they enjoy the five heavenly sense pleasures. What do you think, Pasenadi? Would that heavenly prince give up those heavenly five sense pleasures and remember the human five sense pleasures fondly, praise them, and want to see them again?"

蜱肆答曰：「如是。迦叶！复更有恶。迦叶！我有右伺，收捕罪人送诣我所，到已，白曰：『天王！此人有罪，愿王治之。』我语彼曰：『取此罪人可生称之，生称之已，还下著地，以绳绞杀，杀已复称。我欲得知此人为何时极轻柔软，色悦泽好，为死时耶？为活时耶？』彼受我教，取此罪人，活称之已，还下著地，以绳绞杀，杀已复称。彼罪人活时极轻柔软，色悦泽好；彼人死已，皮转厚重，坚不柔软，色不悦泽。迦叶！因此事故，我作是念：无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pāyāsi! Listen as I give you another analogy. The wise understand the meaning through analogies. Pāyāsi! It's like an iron ball or iron plowshare that is burned all day. At that time, it is extremely light and supple, with a pleasant complexion. But when the fire goes out and it gradually cools, it becomes thicker, hard, and not supple, with an unpleasant complexion. In the same way, Pāyāsi! When a person is alive, their body is extremely light and supple, with a pleasant complexion. But when they die, it becomes thicker, hard, and not supple, with an unpleasant complexion. Pāyāsi! You should view the rebirth of beings like this, not as seen by the flesh eye. Pāyāsi! If there are monastics and brahmins who have cut off desire, are heading towards the cessation of desire, have cut off anger, are heading towards the cessation of anger, and have cut off delusion, are heading towards the cessation of delusion, then with their purified divine eye, surpassing that of humans, they see beings passing away and being reborn, good-looking or ugly, excellent or not, going to a good place or a bad place, according to their deeds. They see this as it really is."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹如铁丸或铁犁镵，竟日火烧，彼当尔时，极轻柔软，色悦泽好，若火灭已，渐渐就冷，转凝厚重，坚不柔软，色不悦泽。如是，蜱肆！若人活时，身体极轻柔软，色悦泽好；若彼死已，便转厚重，坚不柔软，色不悦泽。蜱肆！汝应如是观众生生，莫如肉眼之所见也。蜱肆！若有沙门、梵志断绝离欲，趣向离欲，断绝离恚，趣向离恚，断绝离痴，趣向离痴，彼以清净天眼出过于人，见众生死时、生时，好色、恶色，或妙、不妙，往来善处及不善处，随此众生之所作业，见其如真。」

King Pāyāsi said again, "Although the monk Kumāra Kāśyapa says this, I still see and say that there is no rebirth of beings."

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我如是见，如是说：无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pāyāsi! Is there any greater evil than this?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复更有恶而过此耶？」

Pāyāsi replied, "Yes, Kāśyapa! There is another evil. Kāśyapa! I have a right-hand man who arrests criminals and brings them to me. When they arrive, they say, 'Your Majesty! This person has committed a crime, please punish them.' I tell them, 'Take this criminal and turn them upside down in an iron pot or a copper pot, cover their mouth tightly, and light a fire underneath. After lighting the fire, observe the beings entering and exiting, coming and going.' They follow my instructions, take the criminal, turn them upside down in an iron pot or a copper pot, cover their mouth tightly, light a fire underneath, and observe the beings entering and exiting, coming and going. Kāśyapa! I do this in this way, but I do not see beings being born. Kāśyapa! For this reason, I think: There is no rebirth of beings."

蜱肆答曰：「如是。迦叶！复更有恶。迦叶！我有右伺收捕罪人送诣我所，到已，白曰：『天王！此人有罪，愿王治之。』我语彼曰：『取此罪人倒著铁釜中，或著铜釜中，密盖其口，于下燃火，下燃火已，观视众生入时出时，往来周旋。』彼受我教，取此罪人，倒著铁釜中，或著铜釜中，密盖其口，于下燃火，下燃火已，观视众生入时出时，往来周旋。迦叶！我作如是方便，不见众生生。迦叶！因此事故，我作是念：无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pāyāsi! I will ask you something, answer as you see fit. What do you think? If you eat delicious food and take a nap on a bed, have you ever remembered seeing a garden, pond, forest, flowers, fruits, clear springs, and streams in your dreams, and playing and coming and going with all your heart?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！我今问汝，随所解答。于意云何？若汝食好极美上馔，昼寝于床，汝颇曾忆于梦中见园观浴池、林木华果、清泉长流，极意游戏周旋往来耶？」

Pāyāsi replied, "I remember it."

蜱肆答曰：「曾忆有之。」

Kāśyapa asked again, "When you eat delicious food and take a nap on a bed, do you have any attendants at that time?"

迦叶复问：「若汝食好极美上馔，昼寝于床，尔时颇有直侍人不？」

Pāyāsi replied, "Yes, there are."

答曰：「有也。」

Kāśyapa asked again, "When you eat delicious food and take a nap on a bed, do those who attend you see you coming and going at that time?"

迦叶复问：「若汝食好极美上馔，昼寝于床，当尔之时，左右直侍颇有见汝出入周旋往来时耶？」

Pāyāsi replied, "Even a stranger cannot see it, let alone those who attend me."

蜱肆答曰：「正使异人亦不能见，况复左右直侍人耶？」

Pāyāsi, you should view the birth of beings like this, not as seen by the flesh eye. Pāyāsi, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have cut off desire, are heading towards the cessation of desire, have cut off anger, are heading towards the cessation of anger, have cut off delusion, are heading towards the cessation of delusion, they use their pure divine eye, surpassing that of humans, to see the death and rebirth of beings, their good and bad forms, whether fine or not, and their coming and going to good and bad places, according to the deeds of those beings, seeing them as they truly are.

「蜱肆！汝应如是观众生生，莫如肉眼之所见也。蜱肆！若有沙门、梵志断绝离欲，趣向离欲，断绝离恚，趣向离恚，断绝离痴，趣向离痴，彼以清净天眼出过于人，见此众生死时、生时，好色、恶色，或妙、不妙，往来善处及不善处，随此众生之所作业，见其如真。」

King Pāyāsi said again, "Although the śramaṇa Kumāra Kāśyapa speaks thus, I still see and say that there are no beings born."

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我如是见，如是说：无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pāyāsi, is there any other evil that surpasses this?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复更有恶而过此耶？」

Pāyāsi replied, "Yes, Kāśyapa, there is another evil. Kāśyapa, I have a right-hand man who arrests criminals and brings them to me. When they arrive, he says, 'Your Majesty, this person is guilty. Please punish them.' I tell him, 'Take this criminal and peel their skin, cut their flesh, sever their tendons, break their bones, and even reach their marrow, seeking the birth of beings.' He follows my instructions, taking this criminal and peeling their skin, cutting their flesh, severing their tendons, breaking their bones, and even reaching their marrow, seeking the birth of beings. Kāśyapa, I use such means to seek the birth of beings, but I still do not see the birth of beings. Kāśyapa, for this reason, I think: there is no birth of beings."

蜱肆答曰：「如是。迦叶！复更有恶。迦叶！我有右伺，收捕罪人送诣我所，到已，白曰：『天王！此人有罪，愿王治之。』我语彼曰：『取此罪人剥皮剔肉，截筋破骨，乃至于髓，求众生生。』彼受我教，取此罪人剥皮剔肉，截筋破骨，乃至于髓，求众生生。迦叶！我作如是方便求众生生，而竟不见众生生。迦叶！因此事故，我作是念：无众生生。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Pāyāsi, listen as I give you another analogy. The wise, on hearing an analogy, understand its meaning. Pāyāsi, it is like a fire-worshipping ascetic who lives near a road. Not far from him, some merchants spend the night. When the merchants leave early in the morning, they forget a small child. Then the fire-worshipping ascetic gets up early and walks around the place where the merchants stayed. He sees a small child alone and without a guardian. Seeing this, he thinks, 'This child has no one to rely on. If I do not raise him, he will surely die.' He then takes the child back to his place and raises him. As the child grows up, his faculties become complete. At that time, the fire-worshipping ascetic has some minor affairs to attend to in the human realm. The fire-worshipping ascetic then instructs the youth, 'I have some minor affairs to attend to in the human realm. You should tend the fire and be careful not to let it go out. If the fire goes out, you should take this fire drill and seek it out.'

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹如事火编发梵志居近道边，去彼不远，有商人宿。时，诸商人过夜平旦怱怱发去，忘一小儿。于是，事火编发梵志早起案行商人宿处，见一小儿独住失主，见已，念曰：『今此小儿无所依怙，我不养者，必死无疑。』便抱持去，还至本处而养长之。此儿转大，诸根成就。尔时，事火编发梵志彼于人间有小事缘，于是，事火编发梵志勅年少曰：『我有小事暂出人间，汝当种火，慎莫令灭；若火灭者，汝可取此火钻求之。』

At that time, the fire-worshipping ascetic, having given good instructions, went to the human realm. Later, the youth went out to play, and the fire went out. He returned to seek fire, took the fire drill, and struck the ground, saying, 'Fire, come out! Fire, come out!' But the fire did not come out. He then struck it harder on a stone, saying, 'Fire, come out! Fire, come out!' But the fire still did not come out. When the fire did not come out, he broke the fire drill into ten or a hundred pieces, threw it away, sat on the ground, and said in distress, 'Without fire, what should I do?'

「尔时，事火编发梵志善教勅已，即至人间，于后年少便出游戏，火遂灭尽。彼还求火，即取火钻以用打地，而作是语：『火出，火出。』火竟不出，复于石上加力打之：『火出，火出。』火亦不出。火既不出，便破火钻十片、百片，弃去坐地，愁恼而言：『不能得火，当如之何？』

At that time, the fire-worshipping ascetic, having finished his affairs in the human realm, returned to his place. Upon arriving, he asked, 'Young man! Didn't you play around and tend the fire, not letting it go out?' The youth replied, 'Venerable! I went out to play, and the fire went out later. I returned to seek fire. I took the fire drill and struck the ground, saying, "Fire, come out! Fire, come out!" But the fire did not come out. I then struck it harder on a stone, saying, "Fire, come out! Fire, come out!" But the fire still did not come out. When the fire did not come out, I broke the fire drill into ten or a hundred pieces, threw it away, and sat on the ground. Venerable! I sought it like this, but could not get fire. What should I do?'

「是时，事火编发梵志彼于人间所作已讫，还归本处，到已，问曰：『年少！汝不游戏随视种火，不令灭耶？』年少白曰：『尊者！我出游戏，火后遂灭，我还求火。即取火钻以用打地而作是语：「火出，火出。」火竟不出。复于石上加力打之：「火出，火出。」火亦不出。火既不出，便破火钻十片、百片，弃去坐地。尊者！我如是求，不能得火，当如之何？』

"At that time, the fire-worshipping ascetic thought, 'This young man is very foolish and ignorant, not understanding well, and lacking wisdom. Why is that? He seeks fire from an ignorant fire drill with such an intention.' Then, the fire-worshipping ascetic took a dry fire drill and fire board, placed them on the ground, and drilled them, and fire immediately came out, becoming more and more intense. He said to the young man, 'Young man! The method of seeking fire should be like this, not like your foolishness and ignorance, lacking wisdom, seeking fire from an ignorant fire drill with such an intention.' Know that Piśuna is also like this, foolish and ignorant, not understanding well, lacking wisdom, seeking the birth of beings in ignorant dead flesh and even bone marrow. Piśuna! You should view the birth of beings like this, not as seen by the flesh eye. Piśuna! If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have cut off and are free from desire, heading towards freedom from desire, cut off and are free from anger, heading towards freedom from anger, cut off and are free from delusion, heading towards freedom from delusion, they use their pure divine eye, surpassing that of humans, to see the birth and death of beings, their good and bad appearances, whether fine or not, and their coming and going to good and bad places, according to the deeds of those beings, seeing them as they truly are."

「尔时，事火编发梵志便作是念：『今此年少甚痴不达，不善晓解，无有智慧。所以者何？从无知火钻作如是意求索火耶？』于是，事火编发梵志取燥火钻火母，著地而以钻之，即便火出，转转炽盛，语年少曰：『年少！求火法应如是，不应如汝愚痴不达，无有智慧，从无知火钻作如是意求索于火。』当知蜱肆亦复如是，愚痴不达，不善晓解，无有智慧，于无知死肉乃至骨髓求众生生。蜱肆！汝应如是观众生生，莫如肉眼之所见也。蜱肆！若有沙门、梵志断绝离欲，趣向离欲，断绝离恚，趣向离恚，断绝离痴，趣向离痴，彼以清净天眼出过于人，见此众生死时、生时，好色、恶色，或妙、不妙，往来善处及不善处，随此众生之所作业，见其如真。」

King Piśuna said again, "Although the śramaṇa Kumāra Kāśyapa speaks thus, I cannot give up this view, the grasping of desire, anger, fear, and delusion. Why is that? If there are people from other countries who hear this, they will say, 'King Piśuna has held this view for a long time, and it has been defeated, eliminated, and abandoned by the śramaṇa Kumāra Kāśyapa.' Kāśyapa! Therefore, I cannot give up this view, the grasping of desire, anger, fear, and delusion."

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。所以者何？若有他国异人闻之，便作是说：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』迦叶！是故我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Piśuna! Listen to my simile. The wise understand the meaning through similes. Piśuna! It is like two friends who leave home to do business. While traveling, they first see a lot of hemp that belongs to no one. One sees it and says to his companion, 'You should know that there is a lot of hemp here that belongs to no one. I want to take it with you, carry it back, and use it for our livelihood.' He then takes a heavy load. On the road, they see a lot of silk thread and silk garments that belong to no one. They also see a lot of silver that belongs to no one. The one who sees it abandons the hemp load and takes the silver to carry himself. On the road, they see a lot of gold that belongs to no one. The one who carries the silver tells the one who carries the hemp, 'You should know that there is a lot of gold here that belongs to no one. You should abandon the hemp, and I will abandon the silver load. I want to take this gold with you, carry it back, and use it for our livelihood.' The one who carries the hemp tells the one who carries the silver, 'This hemp load of mine is already well-prepared and tightly bound. I have carried it from far away. I cannot abandon it. You should know for yourself. Do not worry about me.' Then the one who carries the silver forcibly snatches the hemp load and throws it on the ground, pulling it apart. The one who carries the hemp tells the one who carries the silver, 'You have already pulled apart my load like this. This hemp load of mine is tightly bound and I have carried it from far away. I want to carry this hemp back myself. I will not abandon it. You should know for yourself. Do not worry about me.'

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹如朋友二人舍家治生，彼行道时，初见有麻甚多无主，一人见已，便语伴曰：『汝当知之，今此有麻甚多无主，我欲与汝共取，自重而担，还归可得资用。』便取重担。彼于道路复见多有劫贝纱缕及劫贝衣甚多无主。复见多银亦无有主。一人见已，便弃麻担取银自重。复于道路见多金聚而无有主。时，担银人语担麻者：『汝今当知，此金极多而无有主，汝可舍麻，我舍银担，我欲与汝共取此金，重担而归，可得供用。』彼担麻者语担银人：『我此麻担已好，装治缚束已坚，从远担来，我不能舍，汝且自知，勿忧我也。』于是，担银人强夺麻担，扑著于地而挽坏之。彼担麻者语担银人：『汝已如是挽坏我担，我此麻担缚束已坚，所来处远，我要自欲担此麻归，终不舍之，汝且自知，勿忧我也。』

The one who carries the silver then abandons the silver load and takes the gold to carry back. When the one who carries the gold returns, his parents see him carrying gold from afar. Seeing him, they exclaim, 'Welcome, good son! Come quickly, good son! Because of this gold, you will be able to make a living, support your parents, provide for your wife and children, servants, and workers, and also be able to make offerings to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, create merit and ascend to the higher realms, with good fruits and good rewards, and be reborn in heaven for a long time.' The one who carries the hemp returns to his home. His parents see him carrying hemp from afar. Seeing him, they scold, 'You criminal comes! You worthless one comes! Because of this hemp, you will not be able to make a living, support your parents, provide for your wife and children, servants, and workers, and also be unable to make offerings to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, create merit and ascend to the higher realms, with good fruits and good rewards, and be reborn in heaven for a long time.' Piśuna! You should know that it is the same for you. If you do not abandon this view, the grasping of desire, anger, fear, and delusion, you will then experience immeasurable evil and also be despised by many people.

「彼担银人即舍银担，便自取金重担而还。担金人归，父母遥见担金来归，见已，叹曰：『善来，贤子！快来，贤子！汝因是金快得生活，供养父母，供给妻子、奴婢、使人，复可布施沙门、梵志，作福升上，善果善报，生天长寿。』彼担麻者还归其家，父母遥见担麻来归，见已，骂曰：『汝罪人来，无德人来，汝因此麻，不得生活、供养父母、供给妻子奴婢使人，又亦不得布施沙门及诸梵志，作福升上，善果善报，生天长寿。』当知蜱肆亦复如是，若汝此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不舍者，汝便当受无量之恶，亦为众人之所增恶。」

King Piśuna said again, "Although the śramaṇa Kumāra Kāśyapa speaks like this, I cannot abandon this view, the grasping of desire, anger, fear, and delusion. Why is that? If people from other countries hear about it, they will say, 'King Piśuna has held this view for a long time. He has been defeated, subdued, and abandoned by the śramaṇa Kumāra Kāśyapa.' Kāśyapa! Therefore, I cannot abandon this view, the grasping of desire, anger, fear, and delusion."

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。所以者何？若有他国异人闻之，便作是说：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』迦叶！是故我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。」

Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa said, "Piśuna! Listen as I give you another analogy. The wise understand the meaning through analogies. Piśuna! It is like merchants traveling with a large group of a thousand carts on a road of famine. Among this large group, there are two leaders. They think, 'How can we escape this danger?' They further think, 'We should divide this large group into two parts, each with five hundred.' The merchants then divide into two parts, each with five hundred. Then one merchant leader leads five hundred carts to the road of famine. This merchant leader always leads the way and sees a person coming from a side road, with wet clothes, a black body, yellow head, and extremely red eyes, wearing a garland of angelica flowers, riding a donkey cart with mud on both wheels. The merchant leader sees him and asks, 'Is there rain on the road of famine? Are there fresh water, firewood, and grass there?' The person answers, 'It has rained heavily on the road of famine. There is plenty of fresh water, firewood, and grass. Sirs! You should abandon the old water, firewood, and grass, lest your carts be exhausted. You will soon find fresh water and good firewood and grass.'

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆，复听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹如商人与其大众，有千乘车，行饥俭道。此大众中而有两主，彼作是念：『我等何因得脱此难？』复作是念：『我此大众应分为两部，部各五百。』彼商人众便分为两部，部各五百。于是，一商人主将五百乘至饥俭道，彼商人主常在前导，见有一人从傍道来，衣服尽湿，身黑头黄，两眼极赤，著蘅华鬘而乘驴车，泥著两［𫐘－咸＋匆］，彼商人主见，便问曰：『饥俭道中有天雨不？彼有新水、樵及草耶？』彼人答曰：『饥俭道中天降大雨，极有新水乃饶樵、草。诸贤！汝等可舍故水、樵、草，莫令乘乏，汝等不久当得新水及好樵、草。』

The merchant leader, having heard this, immediately returned to the merchants and told them, 'While I was leading the way, I saw a person coming from a side road, with wet clothes, a black body, yellow head, and extremely red eyes, wearing a garland of angelica flowers, riding a donkey cart with mud on both wheels. I asked him, "Is there rain on the road of famine? Are there fresh water, firewood, and grass there?" He answered me, "It has rained heavily on the road of famine. There is plenty of fresh water, firewood, and grass. Sirs! You should abandon the old water, firewood, and grass, lest your carts be exhausted. You will soon find fresh water and good firewood and grass." Merchants! We should abandon the old water, firewood, and grass. In this way, we will soon find fresh water, firewood, and grass, lest our carts be exhausted.' The merchants then immediately abandoned the old water, firewood, and grass. They traveled for one day but did not find fresh water, firewood, and grass. For two, three, up to seven days, they still did not find fresh water, firewood, and grass. After seven days, they were killed by man-eating ghosts.

「彼商人主闻已即还，诣诸商人而告之曰：『我在前行，见有一人从傍道来，衣服尽湿，身黑头黄，两眼极赤，著蘅华鬘而乘驴车，泥著两［𫐘－咸＋匆］。我问彼曰：「饥俭道中有天雨不？彼有新水、樵及草耶？」彼答我曰：「饥俭道中天降大雨，极有新水乃饶樵、草。诸贤！汝等可舍故水、樵、草，莫令乘乏，汝等不久当得新水及好樵、草。」诸商人！我等可舍故水、樵、草，如是不久当得新水、樵、草，莫令乘乏。』彼商人等即便弃舍故水、樵、草，一日行道不得新水、樵、草，二日、三日，乃至七日行道，犹故不得新水、樵、草，过七日已，为食人鬼之所杀害。

The second merchant leader then thought, 'The first merchant leader has already passed through the danger. How can we now escape the danger?' Having thought this, the second merchant leader, with five hundred carts, immediately advanced to the road of famine. The second merchant leader led the way and saw a person coming from a side road, with wet clothes, a black body, yellow head, and extremely red eyes, wearing a garland of angelica flowers, riding a donkey cart with mud on both wheels. The second merchant leader saw him and asked, 'Is there rain on the road of famine? Are there fresh water, firewood, and grass there?' The person answered, 'It has rained heavily on the road of famine. There is plenty of fresh water, firewood, and grass. Sirs! You should abandon the old water, firewood, and grass, lest your carts be exhausted. You will soon find fresh water and good firewood and grass.'

Having seen this, the second merchant leader told the merchants, 'You should look at the first merchant leader and the merchants. They are foolish, ignorant, unwise, and lacking in understanding. If you want to take this view out of desire, anger, fear, or delusion, and do not let go, you will certainly experience immeasurable evil and be hated by many, just like the first merchant leader and the merchants."

「第二商人主闻已即还，诣诸商人而告之曰：『我在前行见有一人从傍道来，衣服尽湿，身黑头黄，两眼极赤，著蘅华鬘而乘驴车，泥著两［𫐘－咸＋匆］。我问彼曰：「饥俭道中有天雨不？彼有新水、樵及草耶？」彼答我曰：「饥俭道中天适大雨，极有新水乃饶樵、草。诸贤！汝等可舍故水、樵、草，莫令乘乏，汝等不久当得新水及好樵、草。」诸商人！我等未可舍故水、樵、草，若得新水、樵、草，然后当弃。』彼不舍故水、樵、草，一日行道不得新水、樵、草，二日、三日，乃至七日行道，犹故不得新水、樵、草。第二商人主在前行时，见前第一商人主及诸商人为食人鬼之所杀害，第二商人主见已，语诸商人：『汝等看前商人主愚痴不达，不善晓解，无有智慧，既自杀身，复杀诸人。汝等商人若欲取前诸商人物，自恣取之。』当知蜱肆亦复如是。若汝此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不舍者，汝便当受无量之恶，亦为众人之所憎恶，犹前第一商人之主及诸商人。」

King Piyadassi said again, "Although the monk Kumara Kāśyapa speaks thus, I cannot give up this view, which I have held for a long time. If people from other countries hear about this, they will say, 'King Piyadassi had a view that he held for a long time, but it was defeated, refuted, and abandoned by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa.' Kāśyapa! Therefore, I cannot give up this view, which I have taken out of desire, anger, fear, and delusion.

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。所以者何？若有他国异人闻之，便作是说：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』迦叶！是故我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Piyadassi, listen as I give another analogy. The wise understand the meaning through analogies. Piyadassi, it is like two people playing a game for a bet of cakes. The first player secretly eats them, one, two, three, or many. The second player thinks, 'Playing with this person, he often deceives me and steals the cakes, one, two, three, or many.' Seeing this, he says to his companion, 'I want to rest now, we can play again later.' Then the second player leaves that place and uses poison to smear the cakes. After smearing them, he returns and says to his companion, 'Come and play.' They play again. The first player secretly eats the cakes again, one, two, three, or many. After eating the cakes, he immediately rubs his eyes, foams at the mouth, and is about to die. Then the second player speaks a verse to the first player:

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹如二人许戏赌饼，第一戏者并窃食之，食一、二、三，或至众多，第二戏者便作是念：『共此人戏，数数欺我而偷饼食，或一、二、三，或至众多。』见如是已，语彼伴曰：『我今欲息，后当更戏。』于是，第二戏者离于彼处，便以毒药用涂其饼，涂已即还，语其伴曰：『可来共戏。』即来共戏。第一戏者复窃饼食，或一、二、三，或至众多，既食饼已，即便戴眼吐沫欲死。于是，第二戏者向第一戏人即说颂曰：

These cakes are smeared with poison, you greedily eat them without noticing
Sitting here, you are deceived by the cakes, later you will surely suffer.

「『此饼毒药涂，　　汝贪食不觉，坐为饼欺我，　　后必致苦患。』

Piyadassi, you should know that you are also like this. If you do not give up your views of desire, anger, fear, and delusion, you will surely suffer immeasurable evil and be hated by many people, just like the gambler who deceives others with cakes and brings misfortune upon himself.

「当知蜱肆亦复如是，若汝此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不舍者，汝便当受无量之恶，亦为众人之所憎恶，犹如戏人为饼欺他，还自得殃。」

King Piyadassi said again, "Although the monk Kumara Kāśyapa speaks like this, I cannot give up my views of desire, anger, fear, and delusion. Why? If people from other countries hear about this, they will say, 'King Piyadassi has long held these views, but he has been defeated, subdued, and eliminated by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa.' Kāśyapa, that is why I cannot give up my views of desire, anger, fear, and delusion.

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。所以者何？若有他国异人闻之，便作是说：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』迦叶！是故我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Piyadassi, listen as I give you another analogy. The wise understand the meaning through analogies. Piyadassi, it is like a pig farmer who, while traveling, sees a pile of manure with no owner. He thinks, 'This manure can feed many pigs. I should take it and carry it back.' He takes it and carries it, but on the way he encounters heavy rain, and the liquid manure spills and stains his body. He still carries it back, never letting go. He will suffer immeasurable evil and be hated by many people. You should know that you are also like this. If you do not give up your views of desire, anger, fear, and delusion, you will surely suffer immeasurable evil and be hated by many people, just like the pig farmer.

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复听我说喻，慧者闻喻则解其义。蜱肆！犹养猪人，彼行路时，见有熇粪甚多无主，便作是念：『此粪可以养饱多猪，我宁可取自重而去。』即取负去，彼于中道遇天大雨，粪液流漫，浇污其身，故负持去，终不弃舍，彼则自受无量之恶，亦为众人之所憎恶。当知蜱肆亦复如是，若汝此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不舍者，汝便当受无量之恶，亦为众人之所憎恶，犹养猪人。」

King Piyadassi said again, "Although the monk Kumara Kāśyapa speaks like this, I cannot give up my views of desire, anger, fear, and delusion. Why? If people from other countries hear about this, they will say, 'King Piyadassi has long held these views, but he has been defeated, subdued, and eliminated by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa.' Kāśyapa, that is why I cannot give up my views of desire, anger, fear, and delusion.

蜱肆王复言：「沙门鸠摩罗迦叶虽作是说，但我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。所以者何？若有他国异人闻之，便作是说：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』迦叶！是故我此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不能舍。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Piyadassi, listen as I give you one last analogy. If you understand, that is good. If you do not understand, I will not teach the Dharma any further. Piyadassi, it is like a large pig, the king of five hundred pigs, traveling on a dangerous path. On the way, it encounters a tiger. Seeing the tiger, the pig thinks, 'If I fight it, the tiger will surely kill me. If I run in fear, my relatives will despise me. I do not know what expedient means I can use to escape this danger?' Having thought this, it says to the tiger, 'If you want to fight, then fight. If not, let me pass.' The tiger, hearing this, says to the pig, 'I will let you fight, but I will not let you pass.' The pig then says, 'Tiger, wait a moment while I put on the armor of my ancestors. Then I will fight you.' The tiger, hearing this, thinks, 'He is not my match, let alone the armor of his ancestors?' It then says to the pig, 'Do as you wish.' The pig then returns to its original toilet, rolls around in the filth, smearing its body up to its eyes. It then goes to the tiger and says, 'If you want to fight, then fight. If not, let me pass.' The tiger, seeing the pig, again thinks, 'I have always refrained from eating small insects, out of concern for my teeth. How much less would I approach this stinking pig?' Having thought this, the tiger says to the pig, 'I will let you pass, but I will not fight you.' The pig, having passed, then returns to the tiger and speaks a verse:

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！复听我说最后譬喻，若汝知者，善；若不知者，我不复说法。蜱肆！犹如大猪为五百猪王，行崄难道。彼于中路遇见一虎，猪见虎已，便作是念：『若与鬪者，虎必杀我；若畏走者，然诸亲族便轻慢我。不知今当以何方便得脱此难？』作是念已，而语虎曰：『若欲鬪者，便可共鬪；若不尔者，借我道过。』彼虎闻已，便语猪曰：『听汝共鬪，不借汝道。』猪复语曰：『虎！汝小住，待我被著祖父时铠，还当共战。』彼虎闻已，而作是念：『彼非我敌，况祖父铠耶？』便语猪曰：『随汝所欲。』猪即还至本厕处所，婉转粪中，涂身至眼已，便往至虎所，语曰：『汝欲鬪者便可共鬪；若不尔者，借我道过。』虎见猪已，复作是念：『我常不食杂小虫者，以惜牙故，况复当近此臭猪耶？』虎念是已，便语猪曰：『我借汝道，不与汝鬪。』猪得过已，则还向虎而说颂曰：

Tiger, you have four legs, and so do I. Come
and fight with me. Why are you running away in fear?

「『虎汝有四足，　　我亦有四足，汝来共我鬪，　　何意怖而走？』

At that time, the tiger, having heard this, also spoke a verse in reply to the pig:

「时，虎闻已，亦复说颂而答猪曰：

Your hair stands on end, you are the lowest of
all animals. Pig, you should quickly leave. Your feces stink unbearably.

「『汝毛竖森森，　　诸畜中下极，猪汝可速去，　　粪臭不可堪。』

At that time, the pig, boasting, spoke another verse:

「时，猪自夸复说颂曰：

The countries of Magadha and Anga have heard that I am fighting with
you. Come and fight with me. Why are you running away in fear?

「『摩竭鸯二国，　　闻我共汝鬪，汝来共我战，　　何以怖而走？』

The tiger, having heard this, spoke another verse:

「虎闻此已，复说颂曰：

Your whole body is covered with dirt. You, a pig, stink and make
me stink. You want to fight to win. I now give you the victory.

「『举身毛皆污，　　猪汝臭熏我，汝鬪欲求胜，　　我今与汝胜。』」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Pesi, it is the same for me. If you do not give up these views, which are grasping at desire, grasping at anger, grasping at fear, and grasping at delusion, you will bring upon yourself immeasurable evil and be hated by many people, just like that tiger and the pig."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！我亦如是，若汝此见欲取、恚取、怖取、痴取，终不舍者，汝便自受无量之恶，亦为众人之所憎恶，犹如彼虎与猪胜也。」

King Pesi heard this and said, "Venerable One! When you first spoke the analogy of the sun and moon, I understood and joyfully accepted it. But I wanted to ask the Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa for even more profound teachings. That is why I asked again and again. I now take refuge in the Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa."

蜱肆王闻已，白曰：「尊者！初说日月喻时，我闻即解，欢喜奉受，然我欲从尊者鸠摩罗迦叶求上复上妙智所说，是故我向问复问耳。我今自归尊者鸠摩罗迦叶。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Pesi! Do not take refuge in me. I take refuge in the Buddha, and you should also take refuge in him."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！汝莫归我，我所归佛，汝亦应归。」

King Pesi said, "Venerable One! I now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha of monks. I wish for the Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa to accept me as a lay follower on behalf of the Buddha. From this day forth, I take refuge until the end of my life. Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa! From this day forth, I will practice giving and cultivate merit."

蜱肆王白曰：「尊者！我今自归佛、法及比丘众，愿尊者鸠摩罗迦叶为佛受我为优婆塞，从今日始，终身自归，乃至命尽。尊者鸠摩罗迦叶！我从今日始行布施修福。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa asked, "Pesi! When you wish to practice giving and cultivate merit, how many people will you give to? For how long can you do it?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶问曰：「蜱肆！汝欲行施修福，施与几人？能至几时？」

King Pesi said, "I will give to a hundred or a thousand people, for one day, two days, or up to seven days."

蜱肆王白曰：「布施百人，或至千人，一日、二日，或至七日。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "If the king practices giving and cultivating merit, giving to a hundred or a thousand people, for one day, two days, or up to seven days, then all the monks and brahmins from all directions will hear that King Pesi has long held a view, which has now been defeated, refuted, and abandoned by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa. Having heard this, they will all come from afar. Within seven days, they will not be able to receive the king's offerings. If they do not receive the king's offerings, the king will have no merit and will not experience long-lasting peace and happiness. King Pesi! It is like a seed that is not broken, not damaged, not split, not cracked, not harmed by wind or sun, and not damaged by water. If it is well stored in the autumn, and then a householder deeply plows good fields, thoroughly prepares the ground, and sows the seeds at the right time, but the rain and moisture are not suitable, what do you think, King Pesi, can that seed grow and increase?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「若王行施修福，布施百人，或至千人，一日、二日，或至七日者，诸方沙门、梵志尽闻：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』诸方闻已，尽当远来，七日之中不及王施；若不得食王信施者，王便无福，不得长夜受其安乐。蜱肆王！犹如种子不碎不坏，不剖不坼，非风非日，非水中伤，秋时好藏。若彼居士深耕良田，极治地已，随时下种，然雨泽不适者，于蜱肆意云何，彼种可得生增长不？」

He replied, "No, it cannot."

答曰：「不也。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Pesi, it is the same for you. If you practice giving and cultivating merit, giving to a hundred or a thousand people, for one day, two days, or up to seven days, then all the monks and brahmins from all directions will hear that King Pesi has long held a view, which has now been defeated, refuted, and abandoned by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa. Having heard this, they will all come from afar. Within seven days, they will not be able to receive the king's offerings. If they do not receive the king's offerings, the king will have no merit and will not experience long-lasting peace and happiness."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！汝亦如是。若行施修福，布施百人或至千人，一日、二日，或至七日者，诸方沙门、梵志尽闻：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』诸方闻已，尽当远来，七日之中不及王施；若不得食王信施者，王便无福，不得长夜受其安乐。」

King Pesi asked again, "Venerable One, what should I do?"

蜱肆王复问曰：「尊者！我当云何？」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa replied, "Pesi, you should practice giving and cultivating merit, always observing the long fast. If King Pesi practices giving and cultivating merit, always observing the long fast, then monks and brahmins from all directions will hear that King Pesi has long held a view, which has now been defeated, refuted, and abandoned by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa. Having heard this, they will all come from afar. They will all be able to receive the king's offerings. The king will then have merit and will experience long-lasting peace and happiness. Pesi, it is like a seed that is not broken, not damaged, not split, not cracked, not harmed by wind or sun, not damaged by water. If it is well stored in the autumn, and if the householder deeply plows the good field, thoroughly prepares the ground, and sows the seeds at the right time, with timely rain and moisture, what do you think, Pesi, can that seed grow and increase?"

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶答曰：「蜱肆！汝当行施修福，常供长斋。若蜱肆王行施修福，常供长斋者，诸方沙门、梵志闻：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』诸方闻已，尽当远来，彼皆可得及王信施，王便有福，而得长夜受其安乐。蜱肆！犹如种子不碎不坏，不剖不坼，非风非日，非水中伤，秋时好藏。若彼居士深耕良田，极治地已，随时下种，雨泽适者，于蜱肆意云何，彼种可得生增长不？」

He replied, "It can grow."

答曰：「生也。」

Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa said, "Pesi, it is the same for you. If you practice giving and cultivating merit, always observing the long fast, then monks and brahmins from all directions will hear that King Pesi has long held a view, which has now been defeated, refuted, and abandoned by the monk Kumara Kāśyapa. Having heard this, they will all come from afar. They will all be able to receive the king's offerings. The king will then have merit and will experience long-lasting peace and happiness."

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶告曰：「蜱肆！汝亦如是，若当行施修福，常供长斋者。诸方沙门、梵志闻：『蜱肆王有见长夜受持，彼为沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。』诸方闻已，尽当远来，彼皆可得及王信施，王便有福，而得长夜受其安乐。」

Then King Pesi said, "Venerable! From now on, I will practice giving and cultivating merit, always observing the long fast."

于是，蜱肆王白曰：「尊者！我从今始行施修福，常供长斋。」

At that time, Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa spoke the Dharma for King Pesi, the Sāṃkāśya brahmins and householders, inspiring and uplifting them, bringing them to a state of joy. Having used immeasurable skillful means to speak the Dharma for them, inspiring and uplifting them, bringing them to a state of joy, he remained silent. Then King Pesi, the Sāṃkāśya brahmins and householders, having had the Dharma spoken for them by Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa, inspired and uplifted, brought to a state of joy, rose from their seats, bowed their heads at the feet of Venerable Kumara Kāśyapa, circumambulated him three times, and departed.

尔时，尊者鸠摩罗迦叶为蜱肆王及斯惒提梵志、居士说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜，无量方便，为彼说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜已，默然而住。于是，蜱肆王及斯惒提梵志、居士，尊者鸠摩罗迦叶为其说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜已，即从坐起，稽首尊者鸠摩罗迦叶足，绕三匝而去。

Although King Pesi practiced giving and cultivating merit, he was extremely evil and coarse. He gave only a single piece of ginger with bean soup and vegetables, and also gave coarse and shabby cloth. At that time, the kitchen supervisor named Utta was entrusted by King Pesi to make a vow of merit when practicing giving and cultivating merit: "If this giving has any karmic retribution, may King Pesi not receive it in this life or the next." King Pesi heard that Utta, when practicing giving and cultivating merit, was always entrusted to the senior monk to make a vow: "If this giving has any karmic retribution, may King Pesi not receive it in this life or the next." Having heard this, he immediately called and asked: "Utta! Is it true that when you practice giving and cultivating merit, you entrust the senior monk to make a vow: 'If this giving has any karmic retribution, may King Pesi not receive it in this life or the next'? Is it like this?"

彼蜱肆王虽行施修福，然极恶麤弊，豆羹菜茹，唯一片姜，又复施以麤弊布衣。时，监厨者名优多罗，彼行施修福时，为蜱肆王嘱语上座呪愿：「此施若有福报者，莫令蜱肆王今世后世受。」蜱肆王闻优多罗行施修福时，常为嘱上座呪愿：「此施若有福报者，莫令蜱肆王今世后世受。」闻已，即呼问曰：「优多罗！汝实行施修福时，为我嘱上座呪愿：『此施若有福报者，莫令蜱肆王今世后世受。』为如是耶？」

Utta replied: "It is true, Your Majesty! Why is that? Your Majesty, although you practice giving and cultivating merit, you are extremely evil and coarse. You give only a single piece of ginger with bean soup and vegetables. Your Majesty, this food is not even suitable to be touched with the hand, let alone eaten by yourself. Your Majesty gives coarse and shabby cloth. Your Majesty, this cloth is not even suitable to be stepped on with the feet, let alone worn by yourself. I respect Your Majesty, but do not value the gifts. Therefore, Your Majesty, I do not wish for the retribution of this shabby giving to be received by the king."

优多罗白曰：「实尔。天王！所以者何？天王虽行施修福，然极恶麤弊，豆羹菜茹，唯一片姜。天王！此食尚不可以手触，况复自食耶？天王施以麤弊布衣。天王！此衣尚不可以脚蹑，况复自著耶？我敬天王，不重所施，是故，天王！我不愿此弊布施报令王受也。」

Having heard this, King Pesi said: "Utta! From now on, whatever I eat, you should give me rice to eat, and whatever I wear, you should give me cloth to wear." Thereupon, Utta, from then on, gave the king rice to eat according to what the king ate, and gave him cloth to wear according to what the king wore.

蜱肆王闻已，告曰：「优多罗！汝从今始，如我所食，当以饭食，如我著衣，当以布施。」于是，优多罗从是已后，如王所食，便以饭食，如王所衣，便以布施。

At that time, because Utta was in charge of King Pesi's giving, when his body was destroyed and his life ended, he was reborn in the Heaven of the Four Heavenly Kings. Because King Pesi gave without sincerity, when his body was destroyed and his life ended, he was reborn in the empty palace of the Cotton Tree Forest.

尔时，优多罗因为蜱肆王监行布施故，身坏命终，生四王天中。彼蜱肆王以不至心行布施故，身坏命终，生榵树林空宫殿中。

Venerable Upavāna often went to roam in the empty palace of the Cotton Tree Forest. Venerable Upavāna saw King Pesi from afar and asked, "Who are you?"

尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝数往游行彼榵树林空宫殿中，尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝遥见蜱肆王，即便问曰：「汝是谁耶？」

King Pesi replied, "Venerable Upavāna! Have you heard of King Pesi of Jambudvīpa?"

蜱肆王答曰：「尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝！颇闻阎浮洲中有斯惒提王，名蜱肆耶？」

Venerable Upavāna replied, "I have heard that there is a king named Pesi in Jambudvīpa."

尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝答曰：「我闻阎浮洲中斯惒提有王，名蜱肆。」

King Pesi said, "Venerable Upavāna, I am the very one, formerly known as King Pesi."

蜱肆王白曰：「尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝，我即是也，本名蜱肆王。」

Venerable Upavāna further asked, "King Pesi, you have such views and say, 'There is no afterlife, no rebirth of beings.' How did you come to be born in the empty palace of the small cotton tree forest in the Heaven of the Four Kings?"

尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝复问曰：「蜱肆王如是见，如是说：无有后世，无众生生。彼何由生此，依四王天小榵树林空宫殿中？」

King Pesi said, "Venerable Upavāna, I indeed had such views, but they were subdued, eliminated, and abandoned by the venerable monk Kumāra Kāśyapa. If Venerable Upavāna returns to Jambudvīpa, please inform the people of Jambudvīpa: 'When practicing generosity and cultivating merit, one should give with utmost sincerity, give with one's own hands, give in person, give with utmost faith, and give with the knowledge of karma and its results.' Why is this? Do not let the fruits of generosity be like those of King Pesi of Hāstinapura. King Pesi was a generous donor, but because he practiced generosity without utmost sincerity, he was born in the empty palace of the small cotton tree forest in the Heaven of the Four Kings." At that time, Venerable Upavāna silently accepted.

蜱肆王白曰：「尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝！我本实有是见，然为尊者沙门鸠摩罗迦叶之所降伏、所治、断舍。若尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝还下阎浮洲者，愿遍告语阎浮洲人：『若行施修福时，当至心与，自手与，自往与，至信与，知有业、有业报与。所以者何？莫令以是受布施报如斯惒提蜱肆王也。蜱肆王者是布施主，以不至心行施与故，生依四王天小榵树林空宫殿中。』」尔时，尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝默然而受。

Then, Venerable Upavāna sometimes came down to Jambudvīpa and informed the people of Jambudvīpa: "Give with utmost sincerity, give with your own hands, give in person, give with utmost faith, and give with the knowledge of karma and its results. Why is this? Do not let the fruits of generosity be like those of King Pesi of Hāstinapura. King Pesi was a generous donor, but because he practiced generosity without utmost sincerity, he was born in the empty palace of the small cotton tree forest in the Heaven of the Four Kings."

于是，尊者桥［火＊佥］钵帝有时来下至阎浮洲，则遍告诸阎浮洲人：「至心施与，自手与，自往与，至信与，知有业、有业报与。所以者何？莫令以是受布施报如斯惒提蜱肆王也。蜱肆王者是布施主，以不至心行施与故，生依四王天小榵树林空宫殿中。」

The Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa spoke thus. King Pesi, the brahmins and householders of Hastināpura, and the monks, having heard the Venerable Kumāra Kāśyapa's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

尊者鸠摩罗迦叶所说如是。蜱肆王，斯惒提梵志、居士及诸比丘，闻尊者鸠摩罗迦叶所说，欢喜奉行。

The seventh chapter on King Pesi is completed.
