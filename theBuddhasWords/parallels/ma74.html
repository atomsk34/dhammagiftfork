MĀ 74

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 7 长寿王品

74. （七四）八念经

Parallel to: AN 8.30 / EĀ 42.6

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Vārāṇasī, staying in the Deer Park at the Sītavana near the Rṣipatana.

一时，佛游婆奇瘦，在鼍山怖林鹿野园中。

At that time, Venerable Aniruddha was staying in the forest on the island of Jītavana in Vārāṇasī. At that time, Venerable Aniruddha was sitting in meditation in a quiet place, and this thought arose in his mind: "The path comes from being desireless, not from having desires. The path comes from contentment, not from being insatiable. The path comes from seclusion, not from enjoying gatherings, not from dwelling in gatherings, not from joining gatherings. The path comes from diligence, not from laziness. The path comes from right mindfulness, not from wrong mindfulness. The path comes from concentration, not from distraction. The path comes from wisdom, not from foolishness."

尔时，尊者阿那律陀在枝提瘦水渚林中。彼时，尊者阿那律陀在安静处燕坐思惟，心作是念：「道从无欲，非有欲得。道从知足，非无厌得。道从远离，非乐聚会，非住聚会，非合聚会得。道从精勤，非懈怠得。道从正念，非邪念得。道从定意，非乱意得。道从智慧，非愚痴得。」

Then the World-Honored One, knowing with his mind the thoughts, intentions, and actions in Venerable Aniruddha's mind, entered the samādhi of suchness. In the samādhi of suchness, like a strong man bending and stretching his arm, the World-Honored One suddenly disappeared from the Deer Park at the Sītavana near the Rṣipatana in Vārāṇasī and appeared in front of Venerable Aniruddha in the forest on the island of Jītavana in Vārāṇasī. At that time, the World-Honored One then awakened from samādhi and praised Venerable Aniruddha, saying: "Excellent, excellent! Aniruddha! You were sitting in meditation in a quiet place, and this thought arose in your mind: 'The path comes from being desireless, not from having desires. The path comes from contentment, not from being insatiable. The path comes from seclusion, not from enjoying gatherings, not from dwelling in gatherings, not from joining gatherings. The path comes from diligence, not from laziness. The path comes from right mindfulness, not from wrong mindfulness. The path comes from concentration, not from distraction. The path comes from wisdom, not from foolishness.'

于是，世尊以他心智知尊者阿那律陀心中所念、所思、所行。世尊知已，即入如其像定，以如其像定犹若力士屈申臂顷，如是世尊从婆奇瘦鼍山怖林鹿野园中忽没不现，住枝提瘦水渚林中尊者阿那律陀前。是时，世尊便从定觉，叹尊者阿那律陀曰：「善哉，善哉！阿那律陀！谓汝在安静处燕坐思惟，心作是念：『道从无欲，非有欲得。道从知足，非无厌得。道从远离，非乐聚会，非住聚会，非合聚会得。道从精勤，非懈怠得。道从正念，非邪念得。道从定意，非乱意得。道从智慧，非愚痴得。』

Aniruddha! You should receive the eighth great thought from the Tathāgata. Having received it, you should think that the path comes from not playing, enjoying not playing, and practicing not playing, not from playing, not enjoying playing, and not practicing playing. Aniruddha! If you accomplish these eight great thoughts, you will surely be able to be free from desire, free from unwholesome states, and attain the fourth dhyāna. Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. It is like a king or a king's minister who has a well-sealed chest filled with various garments. If he wants to wear them in the morning, he can take them out and wear them. If he wants to wear them at noon or in the evening, he can take them out and wear them, as he pleases. Aniruddha! It is the same for you. You can obtain a rag robe as your first garment. Your mind is free from desire, practicing and dwelling in this way.

「阿那律陀！汝从如来更受第八大人之念，受已，便思道从不戏、乐不戏、行不戏，非戏、非乐戏、非行戏得。阿那律陀！若汝成就此大人八念者，汝必能离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游。阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，如王、王臣有好缄簏，盛满种种衣，中前欲著，便取著之，中时、中后若欲著衣，便取著之，随意自在。阿那律陀！汝亦如是，得粪扫衣为第一服，汝心无欲，行此住止行。

Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. It is like a king or a king's minister who has a good cook who prepares various pure, wonderful, and delicious foods. Aniruddha! It is the same for you. You always practice alms-begging as your first meal. Your mind is free from desire, practicing and dwelling in this way.

「阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，如王、王臣有好厨宰，种种净妙甘美肴饍。阿那律陀！汝亦如是，常行乞食为第一馔，汝心无欲，行此住止行。

Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. It is like a king or a king's minister who has a good house, or a pavilion or palace. Aniruddha! It is the same for you. You rely on dwelling under a tree as your first dwelling. Your mind is free from desire, practicing and dwelling in this way.

「阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，如王、王臣有好屋舍，或楼阁宫殿。阿那律陀！汝亦如是，依树下止为第一舍，汝心无欲，行此住止行。

Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. It is like a king or a king's minister who has a good bed and seat, spread with woolen blankets and woolen carpets, covered with brocade and gauze, with a body-covering quilt, two pillows at both ends, and a woolen blanket and a woolen coverlet. Aniruddha! It is the same for you. You use grass seats and leaf seats as your first seat. Your mind is free from desire, practicing and dwelling in this way.

「阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，如王、王臣有好床座，敷以氍氀、毾［毯－炎＋登］，覆以锦绮、罗縠，有衬体被，两头安枕，加陵伽波惒逻、波遮悉哆罗那。阿那律陀！汝亦如是，草座叶座为第一座，汝心无欲，行此住止行。

Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. In this way, if you travel to the east, you will surely find peace and happiness, without any suffering or affliction; if you travel to the south, west, or north, you will surely find peace and happiness, without any suffering or affliction.

「阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，如是汝若游东方，必得安乐，无众苦患；若游南方、西方、北方者，必得安乐，无众苦患。

Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. I do not even say that your wholesome dharmas will remain, let alone decline. You should only increase wholesome dharmas day and night without decline.

「阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，我尚不说汝诸善法住，况说衰退？但当昼夜增长善法而不衰退。

Aniruddha! If you accomplish the eight great thoughts and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. You will certainly attain one of the two fruits, either attaining ultimate knowledge in this present life, or else attaining the state of a non-returner.

「阿那律陀！若汝成就大人八念，而复得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得者，汝于二果必得其一，或于现世得究竟智，或复有余得阿那含。

Aniruddha! You should accomplish these eight great thoughts of the great person, and also attain these four immeasurable minds, you will be able to dwell in ease in this present life, which is not difficult to attain. Then you should spend the summer retreat in the forest on the island of Jīvaka.

「阿那律陀！汝当成就此大人八念，亦应得此四增上心，现法乐居，易不难得已，然后于枝提瘦水渚林中受夏坐也。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the Dharma for Venerable Aniruddha, inspiring and uplifting him, causing him to attain joy and delight. With immeasurable skillful means, he spoke the Dharma for him, inspiring and uplifting him, causing him to attain joy and delight. Having done so, he entered the samādhi of suchness. With the samādhi of suchness, in the time it takes a strong man to bend and stretch his arm, the World-Honored One suddenly disappeared from the forest on the island of Jīvaka and appeared in the Deer Park at the Fearsome Forest on the mountain of Bālakhilla.

尔时，世尊为尊者阿那律陀说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜，无量方便为彼说法，劝发渴仰。成就欢喜已，入如其像定，以如其像定，犹若力士屈申臂顷，如是世尊从枝提瘦水渚林中忽没不见，住婆奇瘦鼍山怖林鹿野园中。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was holding a whisk and attending to the Buddha. Then the World-Honored One suddenly awoke from samādhi and looked back, saying, "Ānanda! If there are any bhikṣus staying in the Deer Park at the Fearsome Forest on the mountain of Bālakhilla, have them all gather in the Dharma hall. After they have gathered, come and inform me."

彼时，尊者阿难执拂侍佛。于是，世尊便从定觉，回顾告曰：「阿难！若有比丘游鼍山怖林鹿野园中者，令彼一切皆集讲堂，集讲堂已，还来白我。」

Venerable Ānanda, having received the Buddha's instruction, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and said, "Yes, World-Honored One!" He then went to proclaim, "All bhikṣus staying in the Deer Park at the Fearsome Forest on the mountain of Bālakhilla should gather in the Dharma hall." After they had gathered, he returned to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said, "World-Honored One! All bhikṣus staying in the Deer Park at the Fearsome Forest on the mountain of Bālakhilla have been gathered in the Dharma hall. May the World-Honored One know the time."

尊者阿难受佛教已，稽首礼足，即行宣勅：「诸有比丘游鼍山怖林鹿野园中者，令彼一切皆集讲堂。」集讲堂已，还诣佛所，头面礼足，却住一面，白曰：「世尊！诸有比丘游鼍山怖林鹿野园中者，已令一切皆集讲堂，唯愿世尊自当知时。」

Then the World-Honored One, together with Venerable Ānanda, went to the Dharma hall, took his seat in front of the assembly of bhikṣus, and said, "Bhikṣus! I will now explain the eight contemplations of great persons for you. Listen carefully and keep them in mind." At that time, the bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

于是，世尊将尊者阿难往诣讲堂，于比丘众前敷座而坐，坐已，告曰：「诸比丘！我今为汝说大人八念，汝等谛听，善思念之。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "The eight contemplations of great persons are as follows: The path comes from being free from desire, not from obtaining desire. The path comes from contentment, not from being without disgust. The path comes from seclusion, not from enjoying gatherings, not from dwelling in gatherings, not from uniting in gatherings. The path comes from diligence, not from laziness. The path comes from right mindfulness, not from wrong mindfulness. The path comes from concentration, not from distracted mind. The path comes from wisdom, not from foolishness. The path comes from not playing, not enjoying playing, and not practicing playing, not from playing, not enjoying playing, and not practicing playing.

佛言：「大人八念者，谓道从无欲，非有欲得。道从知足，非无厌得。道从远离，非乐聚会，非住聚会，非合聚会得。道从精勤，非懈怠得。道从正念，非邪念得。道从定意，非乱意得。道从智慧，非愚痴得。道从不戏、乐不戏、行不戏，非戏、非乐戏、非行戏得。

How does the path come from being free from desire, not from obtaining desire? It means that a bhikṣu attains being free from desire, knows for himself that he has attained being free from desire, and does not let others know that he is free from desire. He attains contentment, seclusion, diligence, right mindfulness, concentration, wisdom, and not playing, and knows for himself that he does not play, and does not want others to know that he is free from desire. This is called the path coming from being free from desire, not from obtaining desire.

「云何道从无欲，非有欲得？谓比丘得无欲，自知得无欲，不令他人知我无欲，得知足、得远离、得精勤、得正念、得定意、得智慧、得不戏；自知得不戏，不欲令他知我无欲，是谓道从无欲，非有欲得。

How does the path come from contentment, not from being without disgust? It means that a bhikṣu practices contentment, taking robes to cover the body and food to fill the stomach. This is called the path coming from contentment, not from being without disgust.

「云何道从知足，非无厌得？谓比丘行知足，衣取覆形，食取充躯，是谓道从知足，非无厌得。

How does the path come from seclusion, not from enjoying gatherings, not from dwelling in gatherings, not from uniting in gatherings? It means that a bhikṣu practices seclusion, accomplishing two kinds of seclusion, both physical and mental seclusion. This is called the path coming from seclusion, not from enjoying gatherings, not from dwelling in gatherings, not from uniting in gatherings.

「云何道从远离，非乐聚会，非住聚会，非合聚会得？谓比丘行远离，成就二远离，身及心俱远离，是谓道从远离，非乐聚会，非住聚会，非合聚会得。

How does the path come from diligence, not from laziness? It means that a bhikṣu always practices diligence, abandoning evil and unwholesome dharmas, cultivating wholesome dharmas, always generating his own intention, being single-minded and firm, and not abandoning skillful means for the sake of wholesome roots. This is called the path coming from diligence, not from laziness.

「云何道从精勤] cf.［丽】，非懈怠得？谓比丘常行精进，断恶不善，修诸善法，恒自起意，专一坚固，为诸善本不舍方便，是谓道从精勤，非懈怠得。

How does the path come from right mindfulness, not from wrong mindfulness? It means that a bhikṣu observes the internal body as the body, observes internal feelings, mind, and dharmas as dharmas. This is called the path coming from right mindfulness, not from wrong mindfulness.

「云何道从正念，非邪念得？谓比丘观内身如身，观内觉、心、法如法，是谓道从正念，非邪念得。

How does the path come from concentration, not from distraction? It means that a bhikṣu, having abandoned desire and evil and unwholesome dharmas, attains and abides in the fourth dhyāna. This is called the path coming from concentration, not from distraction.

「云何道从定意，非乱意得？谓比丘离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游，是谓道从定意，非乱意得。

How does the path come from wisdom, not from ignorance? It means that a bhikṣu cultivates wisdom, observes the dharmas of arising and perishing, attains such wisdom, with the bright and penetrating wisdom of the sages, discerning and understanding, and with right knowledge, completely ends suffering. This is called the path coming from wisdom, not from ignorance.

「云何道从智慧，非愚痴得？谓比丘修行智慧，观兴衰法，得如是智，圣慧明达，分别晓了，以正尽苦，是谓道从智慧，非愚痴得。

How does the path come from non-play, delight in non-play, and practice of non-play, not from play, delight in play, or practice of play? It means that a bhikṣu always extinguishes play, delights in abiding in the remainderless nirvāṇa, and always delights in abiding with a joyful mind and understanding. This is called the path coming from non-play, delight in non-play, and practice of non-play, not from play, delight in play, or practice of play.

「云何道从不戏、乐不戏、行不戏，非戏、非乐戏、非行戏得？谓比丘意常灭戏，乐住无余涅槃，心恒乐住，欢喜意解，是谓道从不戏、乐不戏、行不戏，非戏、非乐戏、非行戏得。

"Bhikṣus! Aniruddha Bhikṣu, having accomplished these eight adult recollections, then spent the summer retreat in the forest grove on the bank of the Jīta River. I taught him to dwell in seclusion, alone, without negligence, practicing diligently. Having dwelt in seclusion, alone, without negligence, practicing diligently, the clansman, having shaved off his hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, left home out of faith, became a homeless ascetic, and practiced the path, only to complete the unsurpassed holy life in this very life, knowing and realizing it for himself, accomplishing it by his own super-knowledge, ending birth, establishing the holy life, doing what should be done, not experiencing another existence, knowing it as it really is."

「诸比丘！阿那律陀比丘成就此大人八念已，然后枝提瘦水渚林中受夏坐也。我以此教彼在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤。彼在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤已，族姓子所为，剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道者，唯无上梵行讫，于现法中自知自觉，自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。」

At that time, Venerable Aniruddha attained arahantship, with his mind liberated and fully enlightened. He then became an elder of the highest rank and spoke this verse:

是时，尊者阿那律陀得阿罗呵，心正解脱，得长老上尊，则于尔时而说颂曰：

I knew from afar that you were thinking of the
unsurpassed World-Honored Teacher. With body and mind in concentration, you
suddenly came through the air. What you spoke was beyond
my thoughts. The Buddhas delight in non-play, far removed from
all play. Having understood the Dharma from them, I delight
in abiding in the true Dharma. Attaining the samadhi, I
have accomplished the Buddha's teachings. I do not delight in
death, nor do I wish for birth. Adapting to circumstances
I establish right mindfulness and right wisdom. In the bamboo
grove of Vairambhya, my life will end there. I will
attain parinirvana without remainder under the bamboo trees.

「遥知我思念，　　无上世间师，正身心入定，　　乘虚忽来到。如我心所念，　　为说而复过，诸佛乐不戏，　　远离一切戏。既从彼知法，　　乐住正法中，逮得三昧达，　　佛法作已办。我不乐于死，　　亦不愿于生，随时任所适，　　立正念正智。鞞耶离竹林，　　我寿在彼尽，当在竹林下，　　无余般涅槃。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Aniruddha and the other bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是，尊者阿那律陀及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
