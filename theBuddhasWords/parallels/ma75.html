MĀ 75

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 7 长寿王品

75. （七五）净不动道经

Parallel to: MN 106

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Kuru, staying at the city of Kumbhahattha.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦，在劒磨瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the monks, saying, "Desire is impermanent, false, and deceptive. This deceptive Dharma is illusory, deceptive, and foolish. Whether desire in this world or desire in the next world, whether form in this world or form in the next world, all of that is the realm of Mara, the bait of Mara. It causes the mind to generate immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, such as craving, anger, and disputes, which obstruct the learning of noble disciples. The well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: The World-Honored One has said that desire is impermanent, false, and deceptive. This deceptive Dharma is illusory, deceptive, and foolish. Whether desire in this world or desire in the next world, whether form in this world or form in the next world, all of that is the realm of Mara, the bait of Mara. It causes the mind to generate immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, such as craving, anger, and disputes, which obstruct the learning of noble disciples. They think: 'I can attain the great mind, subdue the world, and control my mind. If I can attain the great mind, subdue the world, and control my mind, then such a mind will not generate immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, such as craving, anger, and disputes, which obstruct the learning of noble disciples.' They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the unshakable state in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the unshakable state. This is called the first teaching of the path to the unshakable state of purity.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「欲者无常、虚伪、妄言，是妄言法，则是幻化、欺诳、愚痴。若现世欲及后世欲，若现世色及后世色，彼一切是魔境界，则是魔饵，因此令心生无量恶不善之法，增伺、瞋恚及鬪诤等，谓圣弟子学时为作障碍。多闻圣弟子作如是观，世尊所说，欲者无常、虚伪、妄言，是妄言法则是幻化、欺诳、愚痴。若现世欲及后世欲，若现世色及后世色，彼一切是魔境界，则是魔饵，因此令心生无量恶不善之法，增伺、瞋恚及鬪诤等，谓圣弟子学时为作障碍。彼作是念：『我可得大心成就游，掩伏世间，摄持其心。若我得大心成就游，掩伏世间，摄持其心者，如是心便不生无量恶不善之法，增伺、瞋恚及鬪诤等，谓圣弟子学时为作障碍。』彼以是行、以是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净。于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入不动，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至不动。是谓第一说净不动道。

Furthermore, the well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: If there is form, all of it is the four great elements and what is derived from the four great elements. The four great elements are impermanent, suffering, and perishable. They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the unshakable state in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the unshakable state. This is called the second teaching of the path to the unshakable state of purity.

「复次，多闻圣弟子作如是观，若有色者，彼一切四大及四大造，四大者是无常法，是苦、是灭。彼如是行、如是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净。于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入不动，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至不动。是谓第二说净不动道。

Furthermore, the well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: Whether desire in this world or desire in the next world, whether form in this world or form in the next world, whether desire-thought in this world or desire-thought in the next world, whether form-thought in this world or form-thought in the next world, all of those thoughts are impermanent, suffering, and perishable. They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the unshakable state in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the unshakable state. This is called the third teaching of the path to the unshakable state of purity.

「复次，多闻圣弟子作如是观，若现世欲及后世欲，若现世色及后世色，若现世欲想、后世欲想，若现世色想、后世色想，彼一切想是无常法、是苦、是灭，彼于尔时必得不动想。彼如是行、如是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净，于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入不动，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至不动。是谓第三说净不动道。

Furthermore, the well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: Whether desire-thought in this world or desire-thought in the next world, whether form-thought in this world or form-thought in the next world, and the thought of the unshakable state; all of those thoughts are impermanent, suffering, and perishable. They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the unshakable state in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the unshakable state. This is called the first teaching of the path to the state of purity without anything.

「复次，多闻圣弟子作如是观，若现世欲想、后世欲想，若现世色想、后世色想及不动想；彼一切想是无常法、是苦、是灭，彼于尔时得无所有处想。彼如是行、如是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净，于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入不动，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至不动。是谓第一说净无所有处道。

Furthermore, the well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: This world is empty, empty of self and what belongs to self, empty of permanence, empty of constancy, empty of enduring, empty of not changing. They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the state without anything in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the state without anything. This is called the second teaching of the path to the state of purity without anything.

「复次，多闻圣弟子作如是观，此世空，空于神、神所有，空有常，空有恒，空长存，空不变易。彼如是行、如是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净，于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入无所有处，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至无所有处。是谓第二说净无所有处道。

Furthermore, the well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: I do not act for others, nor do I act for myself. They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the state without anything in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the state without anything. This is called the third teaching of the path to the state of purity without anything.

「复次，多闻圣弟子作如是观，我非为他而有所为，亦非自为而有所为。彼如是行、如是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净，于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入无所有处，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至无所有处。是谓第三说净无所有处道。

Furthermore, the well-learned noble disciples contemplate thus: Whether it is desire in the present world or desire in the next world, whether it is form in the present world or form in the next world, whether it is the thought of desire in the present world or the thought of desire in the next world, whether it is the thought of form in the present world or the thought of form in the next world, as well as the thought of the immovable and the thought of the state of nothing, all those thoughts are impermanent phenomena, they are suffering, they are cessation. At that time, they attain the state without thought. They practice and study in this way, cultivating and spreading it, and then attain mental purity in that place. Having attained mental purity in that place, the monk either attains the state without thought in that place or attains liberation through wisdom. Later, when their body perishes and life ends, due to their original intention, they will definitely reach the state without thought. This is called the teaching of the path to the state of purity without thought.

「复次，多闻圣弟子作如是观，若现世欲及后世欲，若现世色及后世色，若现世欲想、后世欲想，若现世色想、后世色想及不动想、无所有处想，彼一切想是无常法、是苦、是灭，彼于尔时而得无想。彼如是行、如是学，如是修习而广布，便于处得心净，于处得心净已，比丘者或于此得入无想，或以慧为解。彼于后时，身坏命终，因本意故，必至无想处。是谓说净无想道。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was holding a whisk and attending to the Buddha. Then Venerable Ānanda joined his palms towards the Buddha and said, "World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu practices in this way, without self and without possessions, I will not have self and I will not have possessions. If I have had them, I will completely let go of them. World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu practices in this way, will he completely attain parinirvāṇa?"

是时，尊者阿难执拂侍佛。于是，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊！若有比丘如是行，无我、无我所，我当不有、我所当不有，若本有者，便尽得舍。世尊！比丘行如是，彼为尽，得般涅槃耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! This matter is uncertain, some may attain it, some may not attain it."

世尊告曰：「阿难！此事不定，或有得者，或有不得。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! How does a bhikṣu practice and not attain parinirvāṇa?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！比丘云何行不得般涅槃？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu practices thus, without self, without possessions, I will not exist, my possessions will not exist. If they existed before, they will be completely abandoned. Ānanda! If a bhikṣu delights in that abandonment, clings to that abandonment, abides in that abandonment, Ānanda! A bhikṣu practicing thus will definitely not attain parinirvāṇa."

世尊告曰：「阿难！若比丘如是行，无我、无我所，我当不有、我所当不有，若本有者，便尽得舍。阿难！若比丘乐彼舍、著彼舍、住彼舍者，阿难！比丘行如是，必不得般涅槃。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! Does a bhikṣu not attain parinirvāṇa if he has something to accept?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！比丘若有所受，不得般涅槃耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu has something to accept, he will definitely not attain parinirvāṇa."

世尊告曰：「阿难！若比丘有所受者，彼必不得般涅槃也。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! What does that bhikṣu accept?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！彼比丘为何所受？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! There are other things in existence, namely the realms of neither perception nor non-perception. Among existences, it is the best. That bhikṣu accepts it."

世尊告曰：「阿难！行中有余，谓有想、无想处，于有中第一，彼比丘受。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! Does that bhikṣu accept other things in existence?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！彼比丘受余行耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! That bhikṣu accepts other things in existence."

世尊告曰：「阿难！如是比丘受余行也。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! How does a bhikṣu practice to attain parinirvāṇa?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！比丘云何行必得般涅槃？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu practices thus, without self, without what belongs to self, without future self, without future what belongs to self, if what was previously had is completely abandoned, Ānanda! If a bhikṣu does not delight in that abandonment, does not cling to that abandonment, does not abide in that abandonment, Ānanda! A bhikṣu practicing thus will definitely attain parinirvāṇa."

世尊告曰：「阿难！若比丘如是行，无我、无我所，我当不有、我所当不有，若本有者，便尽得舍。阿难！若比丘不乐彼舍、不著彼舍、不住彼舍者，阿难！比丘行如是，必得般涅槃。」

Venerable Ānanda said, "World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu has nothing to receive, will he definitely attain parinirvāṇa?"

尊者阿难白曰：「世尊！比丘若无所受，必得般涅槃耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! If a bhikṣu has nothing to receive, he will definitely attain parinirvāṇa."

世尊告曰：「阿难！若比丘无所受，必得般涅槃。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda joined his palms towards the Buddha and said, "The World-Honored One has already spoken of the path of purity and immovability, the path of purity and the sphere of nothingness, the path of purity and the state of no-thought, and the nirvāṇa without remainder. World-Honored One! What is the noble liberation?"

尔时，尊者阿难叉手向佛，白曰：「世尊已说净不动道，已说净无所有处道，已说净无想道，已说无余涅槃。世尊！云何圣解脱耶？」

The World-Honored One said, "Ānanda! The well-versed noble disciple contemplates thus: 'Whether it is desire in the present world or desire in the future world, whether it is form in the present world or form in the future world, whether it is the thought of desire in the present world or the thought of desire in the future world, whether it is the thought of form in the present world or the thought of form in the future world, as well as the thought of the immovable, the thought of the sphere of nothingness, and the thought of the state of no-thought, all those thoughts are impermanent phenomena, they are suffering, they are cessation.' This is called one's own existence. If there is one's own existence, then there is birth, aging, sickness, and death. Ānanda! If there are these phenomena, and they all completely cease without remainder, then there is no birth, no aging, sickness, and death. The noble ones contemplate thus: 'If there is existence, it must be the Dharma of liberation. If there is nirvāṇa without remainder, that is called immortality.' They contemplate and see thus, and they will definitely attain liberation of the mind from the taints of desire, existence, and ignorance. Having attained liberation, they will know liberation, the end of birth, the establishment of the holy life, the completion of what was to be done, and the cessation of future existence, knowing it as it really is.

世尊告曰：「阿难！多闻圣弟子作如是观，若现世欲及后世欲，若现世色及后世色，若现世欲想、后世欲想，若现世色想、后世色想及不动想、无所有处想、无想想，彼一切想是无常法、是苦、是灭，是谓自己有。若自己有者，是生、是老、是病、是死。阿难！若有此法，一切尽灭无余不复有者，彼则无生，无老、病、死。圣如是观，若有者必是解脱法，若有无余涅槃者是名甘露，彼如是观、如是见，必得欲漏心解脱，有漏、无明漏心解脱。解脱已，便知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。

"Ānanda! I have now spoken to you about the path of purity and immovability, the path of purity and the sphere of nothingness, the path of purity and the state of no-thought, the nirvāṇa without remainder, and the noble liberation. As a teacher does for his disciples, I have given rise to great compassion, pity, and concern, seeking what is beneficial and advantageous, seeking peace and happiness. I have now done this for you. You should also do it yourselves. Go to places free from affairs, to the foot of trees, to empty and quiet places, and sit in meditation. Do not be negligent, but strive with diligence, so as not to regret it later. This is my instruction and admonition."

「阿难！我今为汝已说净不动道，已说净无所有处道，已说净无想道，已说无余涅槃，已说圣解脱。如尊师所为弟子起大慈哀，怜念愍伤，求义及饶益，求安隐快乐者，我今已作；汝等当复自作，至无事处，至林树下，空安静处，燕坐思惟，勿得放逸，勤加精进，莫令后悔。此是我之教勅，是我训诲。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
