MĀ 77

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 7 长寿王品

77. （七七）中阿含长寿王品娑鸡帝三族姓子经第六（第二小土城诵）

Parallel to: MN 68

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Sāketa, staying in the Blue Grove.

一时，佛游娑鸡帝，在青林中。

At that time, there were three young noble sons in Sāketa, Venerable Anuruddha, Venerable Nandiya, and Venerable Kimbila, who had all newly gone forth and entered this true teaching not long ago. At that time, the World-Honored One asked the monks, "These three young noble sons have all newly gone forth and entered this true teaching not long ago. Do these three young noble sons enjoy practicing the holy life in this true teaching and discipline?" At that time, the monks remained silent and did not reply.

尔时，娑鸡帝有三族姓子，尊者阿那律陀、尊者难提、尊者金毘罗，并皆年少新出家学，共来入此正法不久。尔时，世尊问诸比丘：「此三族姓子并皆年少新出家学，共来入此正法不久，此三族姓子颇乐于此正法、律中行梵行耶？」时，诸比丘嘿然不答。

The World-Honored One asked the monks a second and a third time, "These three young noble sons have all newly gone forth and entered this true teaching not long ago. Do these three young noble sons enjoy practicing the holy life in this true teaching and discipline?" The monks also remained silent and did not reply a second and a third time.

世尊复再三问诸比丘：「此三族姓子并皆年少新出家学，共来入此正法不久，此三族姓子颇乐于此正法、律中行梵行耶？」时，诸比丘亦复再三嘿然不答。

Then the World-Honored One himself asked the three noble sons, addressing Venerable Anuruddha, "You three noble sons are all young and have newly gone forth, and have entered this true teaching not long ago. Anuruddha, do you enjoy practicing the holy life in this true teaching and discipline?"

于是，世尊自问三族姓子，告尊者阿那律陀：「汝等三族姓子并皆年少新出家学，共来入此正法不久，阿那律陀！汝等颇乐此正法、律中行梵行耶？」

Venerable Anuruddha replied, "Yes, World-Honored One, we enjoy this true teaching and practice the holy life."

尊者阿那律陀白曰：「世尊！如是。我等乐此正法，修行梵行。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Anuruddha, when you were young, a child with pure black hair, your body was strong and healthy, and you enjoyed playing, bathing, and taking care of your body. Later, your relatives and parents all loved you and wept and cried, not wanting you to go forth to study the path. Yet you were able to shave your beard and hair, wear the monastic robes, leave home out of true faith, become a homeless one, and study the path. Anuruddha, you did not study the path out of fear of the king, nor out of fear of thieves, debt, terror, or poverty. You did not study the path to escape birth, old age, sickness, death, crying, sorrow, or suffering, or to reach the end of the great mass of suffering. Anuruddha, did you not go forth to study the path with such a mind?"

世尊问曰：「阿那律陀！汝等小时年幼童子，清净黑发，身体盛壮，乐于游戏，乐数澡浴，严爱其身，于后亲亲及其父母皆相爱恋，悲泣啼哭，不欲令汝出家学道。汝等故能剃除须发，著袈裟衣，至信、舍家、无家、学道，阿那律陀！汝等不畏王而行学道，亦不畏贼，不畏负债，不畏恐怖，不畏贫穷不得活故而行学道，但厌生、老、病、死、啼哭、忧苦，或复欲得大苦聚边。阿那律陀！汝等不以如是心故出家学道耶？」

He replied, "Yes."

答曰：「如是。」

"Anuruddha, if a noble son goes forth to study the path with such a mind, does he know the cause and attain immeasurable good dharmas?"

「阿那律陀！若族姓子以如是心出家学道者，为知所由，得无量善法耶？」

Venerable Anuruddha said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. I only wish that the World-Honored One would explain it. Having heard it, we will be able to know the meaning extensively."

尊者阿那律陀白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Anuruddha, listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you." Anuruddha and the others accepted the teaching and listened.

佛便告曰：「阿那律陀！汝等谛听，善思念之，我当为汝分别其义。」阿那律陀等受教而听。

The World-Honored One said, "Anuruddha, if one is covered by desire and entangled by evil dharmas, one cannot attain the bliss of renunciation and supreme tranquility. One's mind will give rise to restlessness, ill-will, and drowsiness, and one's mind will not be at ease. One's body will stretch frequently, and one will eat a lot and be worried. Such a bhiksu will not be able to endure hunger and thirst, cold and heat, mosquitoes, flies, fleas, wind and sun, harsh words, and beatings. When the body encounters various illnesses and extreme pain, even to the point of death, all that is unpleasant will be unbearable. Why is this so? Because one is covered by desire and entangled by evil dharmas, one cannot attain the bliss of renunciation and supreme tranquility. If one is free from desire and not entangled by evil dharmas, one will certainly attain the bliss of renunciation and supreme tranquility. One's mind will not give rise to restlessness, ill-will, and drowsiness, and one's mind will not be unhappy. One's body will not stretch frequently, and one will not eat too much, nor will one be worried. Such a bhiksu will be able to endure hunger and thirst, cold and heat, mosquitoes, flies, fleas, wind and sun, harsh words, and beatings. When the body encounters various illnesses and extreme pain, even to the point of death, all that is unpleasant will be endured. Why is this so? Because one is not covered by desire, and not entangled by evil dharmas. Moreover, one attains the bliss of renunciation and supreme tranquility."

世尊告曰：「阿那律陀！若为欲所覆、恶法所缠者，不得舍乐、无上止息，彼心生增伺、瞋恚、睡眠，心生不乐，身生频伸，多食心忧；彼比丘便不能忍饥渴、寒热、蚊虻、蝇蚤、风日所逼，恶声、捶杖亦不能忍，身遇诸疾极为苦痛，至命欲绝，诸不可乐，皆不堪耐。所以者何？以为欲所覆、恶法所缠，不得舍乐、无上止息故。若有离欲，非为恶法之所缠者，必得舍乐及无上止息，彼心不生增伺、瞋恚、睡眠、心不生不乐，身不生频伸，亦不多食，心不愁忧；彼比丘便能忍饥渴、寒热、蚊虻、蝇蚤、风日所逼，恶声、捶杖亦能忍之，身遇诸疾极为苦痛，至命欲绝，诸不可乐，皆能堪耐。所以者何？以非为欲所覆故，不为恶法之所缠故，又得舍乐、无上止息故。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Anuruddha, for what reasons does the Tathāgata sometimes eliminate, sometimes employ, sometimes endure, sometimes stop, and sometimes vomit?"

世尊问曰：「阿那律陀！如来以何义故？或有所除，或有所用，或有所堪，或有所止，或有所吐耶？」

Anuruddha said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. I only wish that the World-Honored One would explain it. After hearing it, we will be able to know its meaning in detail."

阿那律陀白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Anuruddha, listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you." Anuruddha and the others listened to the teaching.

佛便告曰：「阿那律陀！汝等谛听，善思念之，我当为汝分别其义。」阿那律陀等受教而听。

The World-Honored One said, "Anuruddha, the defilements of the various taints are the basis for future existence, the cause of suffering and distress, birth, old age, sickness, and death. The Tathāgata does not not completely eliminate them, nor does he not know them. Therefore, he sometimes eliminates them, sometimes uses them, sometimes tolerates them, sometimes stops them, and sometimes expels them. Anuruddha, the Tathāgata only does so because of this body, because of the six sense bases, and because of life. Therefore, he sometimes eliminates them, sometimes uses them, sometimes tolerates them, sometimes stops them, and sometimes expels them. Anuruddha, for this reason, the Tathāgata sometimes eliminates them, sometimes uses them, sometimes tolerates them, sometimes stops them, and sometimes expels them."

世尊告曰：「阿那律陀！诸漏秽污为当来有本，烦热苦报，生、老、病、死因，如来非不尽，非不知故，或有所除，或有所用，或有所堪，或有所止，或有所吐。阿那律陀！如来但因此身故，因六处故，因寿命故，或有所除，或有所用，或有所堪，或有所止，或有所吐。阿那律陀！如来以此义故，或有所除，或有所用，或有所堪，或有所止，或有所吐。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Anuruddha, for what reason does the Tathāgata dwell in places without affairs, under trees in mountain forests, enjoying high cliffs with no sound, far away, without evil, without people, and in accordance with solitary sitting?"

世尊问曰：「阿那律陀！如来以何义故，住无事处、山林树下，乐居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐耶？」

Venerable Anuruddha said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. I only wish that the World-Honored One would explain it. Having heard it, we will know the meaning in detail."

尊者阿那律陀白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Anuruddha, listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you." Anuruddha and the others listened to the teaching.

佛便告曰：「阿那律陀！汝等谛听，善思念之，我当为汝分别其义。」阿那律陀等受教而听。

The World-Honored One said, "Anuruddha, the Tathāgata does not dwell in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, enjoying the solitude of high cliffs, far from noise, secluded, without evil, and without people, in order to practice meditation, because he has not yet attained what he wants to attain, has not yet obtained what he wants to obtain, and has not yet realized what he wants to realize. Anuruddha, the Tathāgata dwells in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, enjoying the solitude of high cliffs, far from noise, secluded, without evil, and without people, in order to practice meditation, for two reasons: first, to demonstrate the joy of practicing the Dharma himself; second, to have compassion for future generations. There may be future generations who will emulate the Tathāgata in dwelling in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, enjoying the solitude of high cliffs, far from noise, secluded, without evil, and without people, in order to practice meditation. Anuruddha, it is for these reasons that the Tathāgata dwells in the wilderness, under trees, or in remote places, enjoying the solitude of high cliffs, far from noise, secluded, without evil, and without people, in order to practice meditation."

世尊告曰：「阿那律陀！如来非为未得欲得、未获欲获、未证欲证故，住无事处、山林树下，乐居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。阿那律陀！如来但以二义故，住无事处、山林树下，乐居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐：一者为自现法乐居故，二者为慈愍后生人故。或有后生人效如来住无事处、山林树下，乐居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。阿那律陀！如来以此义故，住无事处、山林树下，乐居高岩，寂无音声，远离，无恶，无有人民，随顺燕坐。」

The World-Honored One asked, "Anuruddha, for what reason does the Tathāgata, when a disciple dies, predict where they will be reborn?"

世尊问曰：「阿那律陀！如来以何义故，弟子命终，记说某生某处、某生某处？」

Venerable Anuruddha said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. I only wish that the World-Honored One would explain it. Having heard it, we will understand it extensively."

尊者阿那律陀白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，唯愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Anuruddha, listen carefully and think about it well. I will explain it to you." Anuruddha and the others listened to the teaching.

佛便告曰：「阿那律陀！汝等谛听，善思念之，我当为汝分别其义。」阿那律陀等受教而听。

The World-Honored One said, "Anuruddha, the Tathāgata does not speak for the sake of leading people, nor does he deceive people, nor does he wish to please people. When a disciple passes away, he does not predict their rebirth in such and such a place. Anuruddha, the Tathāgata only does so for faithful male and female lay followers who have great faith, great love, and great joy. Having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, they may aspire to be like this and that. Therefore, when a disciple passes away, he predicts their rebirth in such and such a place. If a bhikṣu hears that a certain venerable one has passed away in such and such a place, and that he was predicted by the Buddha to attain ultimate wisdom, with the end of birth, the establishment of the holy life, the completion of what was to be done, and the knowledge that this is the end of saṃsāra, or if he sees that venerable one himself, or hears about him from others many times, and knows that that venerable one had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, he aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣu will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

世尊告曰：「阿那律陀！如来非为趣为人说，亦不欺诳人，亦不欲得人欢乐故，弟子命终，记说某生某处、某生某处。阿那律陀！如来但为清信族姓男、族姓女，极信极爱，极生喜悦，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是，故弟子命终，记说某生某处、某生某处。若比丘闻某尊者于某处命终，彼为佛所记，得究竟智，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。或自见彼尊者，或复从他数数闻之，彼尊者如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是惠施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼尊者有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, furthermore, if a bhikṣu hears that a certain venerable one has passed away in such and such a place, and that he was predicted by the Buddha to have exhausted the five lower fetters, to be born there and attain parinirvāṇa, to attain the non-retrogressive state, and not to return to this world, or if he sees that venerable one himself, or hears about him from others many times, and knows that that venerable one had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, he aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣu will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

「阿那律陀！复次，比丘闻某尊者于某处命终，彼为佛所记，五下分结已尽，生于彼间而般涅槃，得不退法，不还此世。或自见彼尊者，或复从他数数闻之，彼尊者如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是慧施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼尊者有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, furthermore, if a bhikṣu hears that a certain venerable one has passed away in such and such a place, and that he was predicted by the Buddha to have exhausted the three fetters, to have weakened lust, anger, and delusion, to be reborn once in the heavens and once in the human realm, and to attain the end of suffering after that one rebirth, or if he sees that venerable one himself, or hears about him from others many times, and knows that that venerable one had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, he aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣu will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

「阿那律陀！复次，比丘闻某尊者于某处命终，彼为佛所记，三结已尽，婬怒痴薄，得一往来天上人间，一往来已而得苦际。或自见彼尊者，或复从他数数闻之，彼尊者如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是惠施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼尊者有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, furthermore, if a bhikṣu hears that a certain venerable one has passed away in such and such a place, and that he was predicted by the Buddha to have exhausted the three fetters, to have attained the state of a stream-enterer, not to fall into evil ways, to be certain to attain right awakening, to be reborn at most seven times in the heavens and the human realm, and to attain the end of suffering after that seven rebirths, or if he sees that venerable one himself, or hears about him from others many times, and knows that that venerable one had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, he aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣu will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

「阿那律陀！复次，比丘闻某尊者于某处命终，彼为佛所记，三结已尽，得须陀洹，不堕恶法，定趣正觉，极受七有，天上人间七往来已而得苦际。或自见彼尊者，或复从他数数闻之，彼尊者如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是惠施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼尊者有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, if a bhikṣuṇī hears that a certain bhikṣuṇī has passed away in such and such a place, and that she was predicted by the Buddha to have attained final knowledge, to have exhausted birth, to have established the holy life, to have done what was to be done, to have transcended all future existence, and to know it as it really is, or if she sees that bhikṣuṇī herself, or hears about her from others many times, and knows that that bhikṣuṇī had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, she aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣuṇī will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

「阿那律陀！若比丘尼闻某比丘尼于某处命终，彼为佛所记，得究竟智，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。或自见彼比丘尼，或复从他数数闻之，彼比丘尼如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是惠施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼比丘尼有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘尼必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, furthermore, if a bhikṣuṇī hears that a certain bhikṣuṇī has passed away in such and such a place, and that she was predicted by the Buddha to have exhausted the five lower fetters, to be born there and attain parinirvāṇa, to attain the state of non-retrogression, and not to be reborn in this world again, or if she sees that bhikṣuṇī herself, or hears about her from others many times, and knows that that bhikṣuṇī had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, she aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣuṇī will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

「阿那律陀！复次，比丘尼闻某比丘尼于某处命终，彼为佛所记，五下分结已尽，生于彼间而般涅槃，得不退法，不还此生。或自见彼比丘尼，或复从他数数闻之，彼比丘尼如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是惠施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼比丘尼有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘尼必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, furthermore, if a bhikṣuṇī hears that a certain bhikṣuṇī has passed away in such and such a place, and that she was predicted by the Buddha to have exhausted the three fetters, to have weakened lust, anger, and delusion, to be a once-returner, to be born in heaven once more, and to attain the end of suffering there, or if she sees that bhikṣuṇī herself, or hears about her from others many times, and knows that that bhikṣuṇī had such faith, such discipline, such learning, such generosity, and such wisdom, and that having heard this true Dharma and Vinaya, she aspired to be like this and that, then that bhikṣuṇī will certainly attain a state of peace and happiness.

「阿那律陀！复次，比丘尼闻某比丘尼于某处命终，彼为佛所记，三结已尽，婬怒痴薄，得一往来天上人间，一往来已而得苦际。或自见彼比丘尼，或复从他数数闻之，彼比丘尼如是有信、如是持戒、如是博闻、如是惠施、如是智慧。其人闻已，忆彼比丘尼有信、持戒、博闻、惠施、智慧，闻此正法、律已，或心愿效如是如是。阿那律陀！如是比丘尼必得差降安乐住止。

Anuruddha, for this reason the Tathāgata predicts the rebirth of his disciples after death, saying that they will be reborn in such and such a place.

「阿那律陀！如来以此义故，弟子命终，记说某生某处、某生某处。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The Venerable Anuruddha and the other monks, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them respectfully.

佛说如是。尊者阿那律陀及诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
