MĀ 81

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 7 长寿王品

81. （八一）念身经

Parallel to: DN 22 (~) / MN 10 (~) / MN 119 / MĀ 98 (~) / EĀ 12.1 (~)

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Anga, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, and went to the residence of the Ajivakas.

一时，佛游鸯祇国中，与大比丘众俱，往诣阿惒那揵尼住处。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having passed the night, put on his robe and took his alms bowl in the morning, entered Ajivaka to beg for alms. After eating, in the middle of the day, he put away his robe and bowl, washed his hands and feet, put his sitting mat on his shoulder, went to a certain forest, entered the forest, went to the foot of a tree, spread out his sitting mat, and sat cross-legged.

尔时，世尊过夜平旦，著衣持钵，入阿惒那而行乞食。食讫，中后收举衣钵，澡洗手足，以尼师檀著于肩上，往诣一林，入彼林中，至一树下，敷尼师檀，结跏趺坐。

At that time, many bhikṣus gathered in the Dharma hall after the midday meal and discussed this matter. "Virtuous ones! The World-Honored One is very wonderful and extraordinary! He cultivates mindfulness of the body, analyzes and spreads it widely, knows it thoroughly, observes it thoroughly, cultivates it thoroughly, protects it thoroughly, is well-equipped, and practices well in one mind. The Buddha says that mindfulness of the body has great fruit and retribution, and one obtains the eye, has the eyes to see the ultimate truth."

尔时，众多比丘于中食后，集坐讲堂，共论此事。「诸贤！世尊甚奇！甚特！修习念身，分别广布，极知极观，极修习，极护治，善具善行，在一心中，佛说念身有大果报，得眼、有目见第一义。」

At that time, the World-Honored One was sitting in meditation. With his pure divine ear surpassing that of humans, he heard the bhikṣus gathered in the Dharma hall after the midday meal discussing this matter. "Virtuous ones! The World-Honored One is very wonderful and extraordinary! He cultivates mindfulness of the body, analyzes and spreads it widely, knows it thoroughly, observes it thoroughly, cultivates it thoroughly, protects it thoroughly, is well-equipped, and practices well in one mind. The Buddha says that mindfulness of the body has great fruit and retribution, and one obtains the eye, has the eyes to see the ultimate truth." Having heard this, the World-Honored One then rose from his meditation in the afternoon, went to the Dharma hall in front of the assembly of bhikṣus, spread out his seat, and sat down.

尔时，世尊在于宴坐，以净天耳出过于人，闻诸比丘于中食后，集坐讲堂，共论此事。「诸贤！世尊甚奇！甚特！修习念身，分别广布，极知极观，极修习，极护治，善具善行，在一心中，佛说念身有大果报，得眼、有目见第一义。」世尊闻已，则于晡时从宴坐起，往诣讲堂比丘众前，敷座而坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus, "What were you discussing just now? Why did you gather in the Dharma hall?"

尔时世尊告诸比丘：「汝等向共论何事耶？以何事故集坐讲堂？」

At that time, the bhikṣus said, "World-Honored One! After the midday meal, we bhikṣus gathered in the Dharma hall and discussed this matter. Virtuous ones! The World-Honored One is very wonderful and extraordinary! He cultivates mindfulness of the body, analyzes and spreads it widely, knows it thoroughly, observes it thoroughly, cultivates it thoroughly, protects it thoroughly, is well-equipped, and practices well in one mind. The Buddha says that mindfulness of the body has great fruit and retribution, and one obtains the eye, has the eyes to see the ultimate truth. World-Honored One! We were discussing this matter just now, and for this reason, we gathered in the Dharma hall."

时，诸比丘白曰：「世尊！我等诸比丘于中食后，集坐讲堂，共论此事。诸贤！世尊甚奇！甚特！修习念身，分别广布，极知极观，极修习，极护治，善具善行，在一心中，佛说念身有大果报，得眼、有目见第一义。世尊！我等向共论如此事，以此事故，集坐讲堂。」

The World-Honored One again said to the bhikṣus, "How do I say that cultivating mindfulness of the body, analyzing and spreading it widely, attains great fruit and retribution?"

世尊复告诸比丘曰：「云何我说修习念身，分别广布，得大果报？」

At that time, the bhikṣus said to the World-Honored One, "The World-Honored One is the basis of the Dharma, the World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma. The Dharma depends on the World-Honored One. Please explain it. Having heard it, we will extensively understand its meaning."

时，诸比丘白世尊曰：「世尊为法本，世尊为法主，法由世尊，惟愿说之，我等闻已，得广知义。」

The Buddha then said, "Listen attentively and think well about it. I will explain it to you." The bhikṣus accepted the teaching and listened.

佛便告曰：「汝等谛听，善思念之，我当为汝分别其义。」时，诸比丘受教而听。

The Buddha said, "How does a bhikṣu cultivate mindfulness of the body? A bhikṣu, when walking, knows he is walking; when standing, knows he is standing; when sitting, knows he is sitting; when lying down, knows he is lying down; when sleeping, knows he is sleeping; when waking, knows he is waking; when sleeping and waking, knows he is sleeping and waking. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

佛言：「云何比丘修习念身？比丘者，行则知行，住则知住，坐则知坐，卧则知卧，眠则知眠，寤则知寤，眠寤则知眠寤；如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu knows when entering and exiting, observes and distinguishes bending, stretching, bowing, and raising, dignified deportment, well-wearing the saṃghāṭī robe and other robes and alms bowl, walking, standing, sitting, lying down, sleeping, waking, speaking, and being silent. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者正知出入，善观分别，屈伸低仰，仪容庠序，善著僧伽梨及诸衣钵，行住坐卧、眠寤语默皆正知之；如是比丘随其身行便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, when evil and unwholesome thoughts arise, eliminates them with wholesome thoughts, just as a carpenter or carpenter's apprentice uses a chalk line to mark the wood and then cuts it straight with a sharp axe. Thus, a bhikṣu, when evil and unwholesome thoughts arise, eliminates them with wholesome thoughts. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者生恶不善念，以善法念治断灭止，犹木工师、木工弟子，彼持墨绳，用拼于木，则以利斧斫治令直。如是比丘生恶不善念，以善法念治断灭止。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, with teeth touching teeth and tongue pressing against the palate, uses the mind to subdue the mind, eliminating and stopping it. It is like two strong men grabbing a weak person and twisting and beating them as they please. Thus, a bhikṣu, with teeth touching teeth and tongue pressing against the palate, uses the mind to subdue the mind, eliminating and stopping it. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者齿齿相著，舌逼上腭，以心治心，治断灭止。犹二力士捉一羸人，处处旋捉，自在打锻，如是比丘齿齿相著，舌逼上腭，以心治心，治断灭止。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, when mindful of inhalation, knows he is mindful of inhalation, and when mindful of exhalation, knows he is mindful of exhalation. When inhaling long, he knows he is inhaling long, and when exhaling long, he knows he is exhaling long. When inhaling short, he knows he is inhaling short, and when exhaling short, he knows he is exhaling short. He trains in inhaling with the whole body, trains in exhaling with the whole body, trains in stopping bodily activities while inhaling, and trains in stopping verbal activities while exhaling. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者念入息即知念入息，念出息即知念出息，入息长即知入息长，出息长即知出息长，入息短即知入息短，出息短即知出息短。学一切身息入，学一切身息出，学止身行息入，学止口行息出。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, with the joy and pleasure born of separation, saturates and moistens the body, pervading and filling it without exception. It is like a skilled bath attendant kneading bath powder with water into a ball, saturating and moistening it, pervading and filling it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu, with the joy and pleasure born of separation, saturates and moistens the body, pervading and filling it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者，离生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，离生喜乐无处不遍。犹工浴人器盛澡豆，水和成抟，水渍润泽，普遍充满无处不周。如是比丘离生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，离生喜乐无处不遍。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真，是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, with the joy and pleasure born of concentration, saturates and moistens the body, pervading and filling it without exception. It is like a mountain spring, extremely pure and clear, filling and overflowing, with water coming from the four directions, unable to enter without a cause. Then, from the bottom of the spring, water wells up, overflowing and saturating the mountain, pervading and filling it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu, with the joy and pleasure born of concentration, saturates and moistens the body, pervading and filling it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者定生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，定生喜乐无处不遍。犹如山泉，极净澄清，充满盈流，四方水来，无缘得入，即彼泉底，水自涌出，盈流于外，渍山润泽，普遍充满无处不周。如是比丘定生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，定生喜乐无处不遍。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, with the pleasure born of the absence of joy, saturates and moistens the body, pervading and filling it without exception. It is like blue, red, and white lotuses, born and grown in water, under the water, with roots, stems, and leaves all saturated and moistened, pervading and filling it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu, with the pleasure born of the absence of joy, saturates and moistens the body, pervading and filling it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者无喜生乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，无喜生乐无处不遍。犹青莲华，红、赤、白莲，水生水长，在于水底，根茎华叶，悉渍润泽，普遍充满无处不周。如是比丘无喜生乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，无喜生乐无处不遍。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, with a pure mind, pervades and fills this body, accomplishing and abiding in it, with a pure mind pervading it without exception. It is like a person wearing a seven-cubit or eight-cubit robe, from head to foot, covering this body without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu, with a pure mind, pervades and fills this body, accomplishing and abiding in it, with a pure mind pervading it without exception. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者于此身中，以清净心意解遍满成就游，于此身中，以清净心无处不遍。犹有一人，被七肘衣或八肘衣，从头至足，于此身体无处不覆。如是比丘于此身中，以清净心意解遍满成就游，于此身中，以清净心无处不遍。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu, with the thought of light, well receives and well holds it, with a good mind, as it was before, so it is now; as it was now, so it will be later; as it was during the day, so it is at night; as it was at night, so it is during the day; as it was below, so it is above; as it was above, so it is below. Thus, without being inverted, the mind is not bound, cultivating a luminous mind, the mind is never obscured by darkness. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者念光明想，善受善持，善意所念，如前后亦然，如后前亦然，如昼夜亦然，如夜昼亦然，如下上亦然，如上下亦然，如是不颠倒，心无有缠，修光明心，心终不为暗之所覆。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu observes the characteristics, well receiving and well holding them, with a good mind. It is like a person sitting observing a person lying down, or a person lying down observing a person sitting. Thus, a bhikṣu observes the characteristics, well receiving and well holding them, with a good mind. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者观相善受、善持、善意所念。犹如有人，坐观卧人，卧观坐人。如是比丘观相善受、善持、善意所念。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu observes this body as it abides, according to its good and bad aspects, from head to toe, seeing it filled with various impurities, namely, in this body there are hair, body hair, nails, teeth, coarse and fine skin, flesh, tendons, bones, heart, kidneys, liver, lungs, large intestine, small intestine, spleen, stomach, feces, brain and brain matter, tears, sweat, snot, saliva, pus, blood, fat, marrow, mucus, bile, and urine. It is like a vessel filled with various seeds, which a person with eyes can clearly see, namely, rice, millet, wheat, barley, large and small hemp seeds, cabbage, and mustard seeds. Thus, a bhikṣu observes this body as it abides, according to its good and bad aspects, from head to toe, seeing it filled with various impurities, namely, in this body there are hair, body hair, nails, teeth, coarse and fine skin, flesh, tendons, bones, heart, kidneys, liver, lungs, large intestine, small intestine, spleen, stomach, feces, brain and brain matter, tears, sweat, snot, saliva, pus, blood, fat, marrow, mucus, bile, and urine. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者此身随住，随其好恶，从头至足，观见种种不净充满，谓此身中有发、毛、爪、齿、麁细薄肤、皮、肉、筋、骨、心、肾、肝、肺、大肠、小肠、脾、胃、抟粪、脑及脑根、泪、汗、涕、唾、脓、血、肪、髓、涎、胆、小便。犹以器盛若干种子，有目之士，悉见分明，谓稻、粟种、大麦、小麦、大小麻豆、菘菁芥子。如是比丘此身随住，随其好恶，从头至足，观见种种不净充满，谓此身中有发、毛、爪、齿、麁细薄肤、皮、肉、筋、骨、心、肾、肝、肺、大肠、小肠、脾、胃、抟粪、脑及脑根、泪、汗、涕、唾、脓、血肪、髓、涎、胆、小便。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu observes the various elements of the body, namely, in this body there are the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. It is like a butcher killing a cow, spreading its skin on the ground, and dividing it into six parts. Thus, a bhikṣu observes the various elements of the body, namely, in this body there are the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者观身诸界，我此身中有地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。犹如屠儿杀牛，剥皮布于地上，分作六段。如是比丘观身诸界，我此身中，地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu observes a dead body, whether for one or two days, up to six or seven days, pecked by crows and kites, eaten by jackals and dogs, burned by fire, or buried in the ground, all rotten and decayed. Having seen this, he compares it to his own body, thinking, 'My body is also like this, sharing in this nature, and will never be exempt from it.' Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者观彼死尸，或一、二日，至六、七日，乌鵄所啄，犲狗所食，火烧埋地，悉腐烂坏，见已自比，今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu sees a skeleton, blue in color, half-eaten and decayed, with bones scattered on the ground. Having seen this, he compares it to his own body, thinking, 'My body is also like this, sharing in this nature, and will never be exempt from it.' Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者如本见息道骸骨青色，腐烂食半，骨锁在地，见已自比，今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu sees a skeleton, separated from flesh and blood, only connected by tendons. Having seen this, he compares it to his own body, thinking, 'My body is also like this, sharing in this nature, and will never be exempt from it.' Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者如本见息道离皮肉血，唯筋相连，见已自比，今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu sees a skeleton, with the bones scattered in all directions - foot bones, shin bones, thigh bones, hip bones, spine bones, shoulder bones, neck bones, and skull bones, each in a different place. Having seen this, he compares it to his own body, thinking, 'My body is also like this, sharing in this nature, and will never be exempt from it.' Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者如本见息道骨节解散，散在诸方，足骨、膊骨、髀骨、𣎑骨、脊骨、肩骨、颈骨、髑髅骨，各在异处，见已自比，今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of the body. A bhikṣu sees a skeleton, white like a conch, blue like a pigeon's color, red like blood-smeared, rotten and broken into pieces. Having seen this, he compares it to his own body, thinking, 'My body is also like this, sharing in this nature, and will never be exempt from it.' Thus, a bhikṣu knows his bodily activities as they are. If he abides in seclusion, dwells alone, without negligence, practices diligently, eliminates mental afflictions, and attains a concentrated mind, then, having attained a concentrated mind, he knows it as it is. This is called a bhikṣu cultivating mindfulness of the body.

「复次，比丘修习念身，比丘者如本见息道骨白如螺，青犹鸽色，赤若血涂，腐坏碎末，见已自比，今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。如是比丘随其身行，便知上如真。彼若如是在远离独住，心无放逸，修行精勤，断心诸患而得定心，得定心已，则知上如真。是谓比丘修习念身。

If one cultivates mindfulness of the body in this way and spreads it in this way, all wholesome dharmas are contained therein, namely the factors of the path. If one's mind and understanding are fully pervaded, like the great ocean, all the small rivers are contained within the ocean. If one cultivates mindfulness of the body in this way and spreads it in this way, all wholesome dharmas are contained therein, namely the factors of the path.

「若有如是修习念身、如是广布者，彼诸善法尽在其中，谓道品法也。若彼有心意解遍满，犹如大海，彼诸小河尽在海中。若有如是修习念身、如是广布者，彼诸善法尽在其中，谓道品法也。

"If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not establish mindfulness of the body properly and wander with a small mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity and will surely find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are empty and without mindfulness of the body. It is like an empty jar placed on the ground. If someone were to pour water into it, what do you think, bhikṣus? Would that jar be able to receive the water?"

「若有沙门、梵志不正立念身、游行少心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，必能得也。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志空无念身故，犹如有瓶，中空无水，正安著地，若人持水来泻瓶中，于比丘意云何？彼瓶如是当受水不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "It will receive it, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because it is empty and without water, and is properly placed on the ground, therefore it will definitely receive it."

比丘答曰：「受也。世尊！所以者何？彼空无水，正安著地，是故必受。」

"In the same way, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not establish mindfulness of the body properly and wander with a small mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity and will surely find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are empty and without mindfulness of the body. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who establish mindfulness of the body properly and wander with an immeasurable mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity but will not be able to find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are not empty and have mindfulness of the body. It is like a jar filled with water, properly placed on the ground. If someone were to pour water into it, what do you think, bhikṣus? Would that jar be able to receive more water?"

「如是若有沙门、梵志不正立念身、游行少心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，必能得也。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志空无念身故。若有沙门、梵志正立念身、游行无量心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，终不能得。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志不空有念身故，犹如有瓶，水满其中，正安著地，若人持水来泻瓶中，于比丘意云何？彼瓶如是复受水不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because that jar is full of water and is properly placed on the ground, therefore it will not receive any more."

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！所以者何？彼瓶水满，正安著地，是故不受。」

"In the same way, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have established mindfulness of the body properly and wander with an immeasurable mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity but will not be able to find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are not empty and have mindfulness of the body. It is like a strong man throwing a heavy stone into soft mud. What do you think, bhikṣus? Will the mud receive it?"

「如是若有沙门、梵志，有正立念身、游行无量心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，终不能得。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志不空有念身故。若有沙门、梵志不正立念身、游行少心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，必能得也。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志空无念身故，犹如力士，以大重石掷淖泥中，于比丘意云何？泥为受不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "It will receive it, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because the mud is soft and the stone is heavy, therefore it will definitely receive it."

比丘答曰：「受也。世尊！所以者何？泥淖石重，是故必受。」

"In the same way, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have not established mindfulness of the body properly and wander with a limited mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity and will definitely be able to find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are empty and have no mindfulness of the body. It is like a strong man throwing a light tuft of cotton wool at a flat door panel. What do you think, bhikṣus? Will it receive it?"

「如是若有沙门、梵志不正立念身、游行少心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，必能得也。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志空无念身故。若有沙门、梵志正立念身、游行无量心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，终不能得。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志不空有念身故，犹如力士，以轻毛毱掷平户扇，于比丘意云何？彼为受不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because the tuft of cotton wool is light and the door panel is flat, therefore it will not receive it."

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！所以者何？毛毱轻阐，户扇平立，是故不受。」

"In the same way, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have established mindfulness of the body properly and wander with an immeasurable mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity but will not be able to find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are not empty and have mindfulness of the body. It is like a person seeking fire, using dry wood as the mother and drilling with a dry drill. What do you think, bhikṣus? Will that person be able to obtain fire in this way?"

「如是若有沙门、梵志正立念身、游行无量心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，终不能得。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志不空有念身故。若有沙门、梵志不正立念身、游行少心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，必能得也。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志空无念身故，犹人求火，以槁木为母，以燥钻钻，于比丘意云何？彼人如是，为得火不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "Yes, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because he drills with a dry drill into dry wood, therefore he will definitely obtain it."

比丘答曰：「得也。世尊！所以者何？彼以燥钻钻于槁木，是故必得。」

"In the same way, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have not established mindfulness of the body properly and wander with a limited mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity and will definitely be able to find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are empty and lack mindfulness of the body. It is like a person seeking fire, using wet wood as the mother and drilling with a wet drill. What do you think, bhikṣus? Will that person be able to obtain fire in this way?"

「如是若有沙门、梵志不正立念身、游行少心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，必能得也。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志空无念身故。若有沙门、梵志正立念身、游行无量心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，终不能得。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志不空有念身故，犹人求火，以湿木为母，以湿钻钻，于比丘意云何？彼人如是，为得火不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because he drills with a wet drill into wet wood, therefore he will not obtain it."

比丘答曰：「不也。世尊！所以者何？彼以湿钻钻于湿木，是故不得。」

"In the same way, if there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have established mindfulness of the body properly and wander with an immeasurable mind, then Māra Pāpīyān will seek an opportunity but will never be able to find it. Why is that? Because those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are not empty and have mindfulness of the body."

「如是若有沙门、梵志正立念身、游行无量心者，彼为魔波旬伺求其便，终不能得。所以者何？彼沙门、梵志不空有念身故。」

If one cultivates mindfulness of the body in this way and spreads it in this way, it should be known that there are eighteen virtues. What are the eighteen? The bhikṣu is able to endure hunger, thirst, cold, heat, mosquitoes, flies, wind, and sun, as well as bad sounds and beatings. The body encounters various illnesses, extreme pain, and even the brink of death. All that is unpleasant, he is able to endure. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the first virtue.

「如是修习念身、如是广布者，当知有十八德。云何十八？比丘者，能忍饥渴、寒热、蚊虻、蝇蚤、风日所逼，恶声、捶杖亦能忍之，身遇诸疾，极为苦痛，至命欲绝，诸不可乐，皆能堪耐，如是修习念身，如是广布者，是谓第一德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu is able to endure what is unpleasant. If unpleasant things arise, the mind will never be attached. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the second virtue.

「复次，比丘堪耐不乐，若生不乐，心终不著，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第二德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu is able to endure fear. If fear arises, the mind will never be attached. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the third virtue.

「复次，比丘堪耐恐怖，若生恐怖，心终不著，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第三德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu gives rise to three evil thoughts: thoughts of desire, anger, and harm. If these three evil thoughts arise, the mind will never be attached. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the fourth, fifth, sixth, and seventh virtues.

「复次，比丘生三恶念欲念、恚念、害念，若生三恶念，心终不著，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第四、五、六、七德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having abandoned desire and evil and unwholesome dharmas, attains and abides in the fourth dhyāna. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the eighth virtue.

「复次，比丘离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第八德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having exhausted the three fetters, attains the state of a srota-āpanna, does not fall into evil destinies, is certain to attain right enlightenment, will experience a maximum of seven existences, and after one more birth in the heavens or among humans, will attain the end of suffering. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the ninth virtue.

「复次，比丘三结已尽，得须陀洹，不堕恶法，定趣正觉，极受七有，天上人间一往来已而得苦际，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第九德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having exhausted the three fetters, with weakened lust, anger, and delusion, attains the state of a once-returner, who will be reborn once more in the heavens or among humans, and after that will attain the end of suffering. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the tenth virtue.

「复次，比丘三结已尽，婬怒痴薄，得一往来天上人间，一往来已而得苦际，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第十德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having exhausted the five lower fetters, is reborn there and attains parinirvāṇa, attains the state of non-retrogression, and will not return to this world. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the eleventh virtue.

「复次，比丘五下分结尽，生于彼间，便般涅槃，得不退法，不还此世，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第十一德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having attained the liberation of the mind through concentration, is free from form and attains the formless, and dwells in the meditative state of suchness, having realized it and attained mastery over it. With wisdom, he knows and severs the taints. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the twelfth, thirteenth, fourteenth, fifteenth, sixteenth, and seventeenth virtues.

「复次，比丘若有息解脱，离色得无色，如其像定身作证成就游，而以慧观知漏、断漏，如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第十二、十三、十四、十五、十六、十七德。

Furthermore, the bhikṣu, having attained the supernormal powers, divine hearing, knowledge of others' minds, knowledge of past lives, and knowledge of the end of birth and death, has exhausted all taints, attained the liberation of the mind through the absence of taints, and the liberation of wisdom. In this very life, he knows for himself, awakens to, and fully realizes it. He dwells having accomplished it. Birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there will be no more of this state of existence. He knows as it really is. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, this is called the eighteenth virtue. Cultivating mindfulness of the body in this way and spreading it in this way, one should know that there are these eighteen virtues.

「复次，比丘如意足、天耳、他心智、宿命智、生死智，诸漏已尽，得无漏心解脱、慧解脱，于现法中自知自觉，自作证成就游，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。如是修习念身、如是广布者，是谓第十八德。如是修习念身、如是广布者，当知有此十八功德。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The tenth chapter on mindfulness of the body is completed.
