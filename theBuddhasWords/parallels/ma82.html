MĀ 82

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 7 长寿王品

82. （八二）支离弥梨经

Parallel to: AN 6.60

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Bamboo Grove, in the Kalandaka Park, in the city of Rājagṛha.

一时，佛游王舍城，在竹林加兰哆园。

At that time, many bhikṣus, after the midday meal, gathered in the Dharma hall to discuss and resolve a dispute, namely, discussing this Dharma and Vinaya, this teaching of the Buddha. At that time, the bhikṣu Cīvaradhara was also in the assembly. Then, while the many bhikṣus were discussing this Dharma and Vinaya, this teaching of the Buddha, Cīvaradhara spoke in the middle of their discussion without waiting for the bhikṣus to finish speaking, and he did not ask the senior bhikṣus with respect or good observation.

尔时，众多比丘于中食后少有所为，集坐讲堂，欲断诤事，谓论此法、律，此佛之教。彼时，质多罗象子比丘亦在众中。于是，质多罗象子比丘，众多比丘论此法、律，此佛教时，于其中间竞有所说，不待诸比丘说法讫竟，又不以恭敬、不以善观问诸上尊长老比丘。

At that time, the Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila was in the assembly. Then, the Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila said to the bhikṣu Cīvaradhara, "Noble one! You should know that when the many bhikṣus are discussing this Dharma and Vinaya, this teaching of the Buddha, do not speak in the middle of their discussion. When the bhikṣus have finished speaking, then you may speak. You should ask the senior bhikṣus with respect and good observation, do not ask the senior bhikṣus without respect or good observation.

是时，尊者大拘𫄨罗在彼众中。于是，尊者大拘𫄨罗告质多罗象子比丘曰：「贤者！当知众多比丘说此法、律，此佛教时，汝莫于中竞有所说，若诸比丘所说讫已，然后可说。汝当以恭敬，当以善观问诸上尊长老比丘，莫不恭敬，莫不善观问诸上尊长老比丘。」

At that time, the bhikṣu Cīvaradhara's close friends were also in the assembly. Then, the bhikṣu Cīvaradhara's close friends said to the Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila, "Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila! Do not criticize the bhikṣu Cīvaradhara too much. Why? The bhikṣu Cīvaradhara has many virtuous precepts and much learning, but he seems lazy and not arrogant. Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila! The bhikṣu Cīvaradhara is able to assist the bhikṣus in whatever they do.

尔时，质多罗象子比丘诸亲朋友悉在众中。于是，质多罗象子比丘诸亲朋友语尊者大拘𫄨罗曰：「贤者大拘𫄨罗！汝莫大责数质多罗象子比丘。所以者何？质多罗象子比丘戒德多闻，似如懈怠，然不贡高。贤者大拘𫄨罗！质多罗象子比丘，诸比丘随所为时而能佐助。」

Then, the Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila said to the bhikṣu Cīvaradhara's close friends, "Noble ones! Those who do not know others' minds should not speak rashly, whether it is true or not. Why? There may be someone who is well-guarded and well-protected in the presence of the World-Honored One and the senior bhikṣus who are worthy of respect and reverence, but later, when away from the World-Honored One and the senior bhikṣus who are worthy of respect and reverence, they associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! It is like a cow that enters someone else's field. The field-keeper catches it, either tying it with a rope or putting it in a pen. Noble ones! If someone says that this cow will no longer enter someone else's field, is that a correct statement?"

于是，尊者大拘𫄨罗语质多罗象子比丘诸亲朋友曰：「诸贤！不知他心者，不得妄说称不称。所以者何？或有一人在世尊前时，及诸上尊长老梵行可慙可愧、可爱可敬前，彼便善守善护；若于后时离世尊前，及离诸上尊长老梵行可慙可愧、可爱可敬前，彼便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！犹若如牛，入他田中，守田人捉，或以绳系，或著栏中。诸贤！若有作是说，此牛不复入他田中，如是彼为正说耶？」

"No, why? Because that cow, being tied with a rope, may break or untie it, or being blocked by a fence, may break through or jump out, and again enter someone else's field, no different from before."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？谓彼牛者，为绳所系，或断或解，为栏所遮，或破或跳出，复入他田，如前无异。」

Noble ones! There may be someone who is well-guarded and well-protected in the presence of the World-Honored One and the senior bhikṣus who are worthy of respect and reverence, but later, when away from the World-Honored One and the senior bhikṣus who are worthy of respect and reverence, they associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! This is called one kind of person.

「诸贤！或有一人在世尊前时，及诸上尊长老梵行可慙可愧、可爱可敬前，彼便善守善护；若于后时离世尊前，及离诸梵行可慙可愧、可爱可敬前，彼便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！是谓有一人。

"Furthermore, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the first dhyāna. Having attained the first dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! It is like when it rains heavily, the ponds and lakes between villages are filled with water. If one had previously seen the sand, stones, grass, trees, insects, fish, turtles, frogs, and other aquatic creatures coming and going, moving and stopping, but later when the water is full, one no longer sees them. Noble ones! If someone were to say that in that pond or lake one would never again see the sand, stones, grass, trees, insects, fish, turtles, frogs, and other aquatic creatures coming and going, moving and stopping, would that be a correct statement?"

「复次，诸贤！或有一人逮得初禅，彼得初禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！犹大雨时，村间湖池水满其中，彼若本时所见沙石、草木、甲虫、鱼鼈、虾蟆及诸水性，去时来时，走时住时，后水满已，尽不复见。诸贤！若有作是说，彼湖池中终不复见沙石、草木、甲虫、鱼鼈、虾蟆及诸水性，去时来时，走时住时，如是彼为正说耶？」

"No, why is that? The water in that pond or lake may be drunk by elephants, horses, camels, cows, donkeys, pigs, deer, buffaloes, or taken by people, blown by the wind and dried by the sun. If one had not seen the sand, stones, grass, trees, insects, fish, turtles, frogs, and other aquatic creatures coming and going, moving and stopping, but later when the water recedes, one sees them as before."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？彼湖池水，或象饮马饮，骆驼、牛、驴、猪、鹿、水牛饮，或人取用，风吹日炙；彼若本时不见沙石、草木、甲虫、鱼鼈、虾蟆及诸水性，去时来时，走时住时，后水减已，还见如故。」

So it is, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the first dhyāna. Having attained the first dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! This is called one person.

「如是，贤者！或有一人逮得初禅，彼得初禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证；彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！是谓有一人。

"Furthermore, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the second dhyāna. Having attained the second dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! It is like when it rains heavily, the dust in the crossroads becomes mud. Noble ones! If someone were to say that the mud in the crossroads will never dry up and become dust again, would that be a correct statement?"

「复次，诸贤！或有一人得第二禅，彼得第二禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！犹大雨时，四衢道中尘灭作泥。诸贤！若有作是说，此四衢道泥终不燥，不复作尘，如是彼为正说耶？」

"No, why? Because in the crossroads, elephants, horses, camels, oxen, donkeys, pigs, deer, buffaloes, and people walk. The wind blows and the sun shines. The mud in the crossroads dries up and becomes dust again."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？此四衢道，或象行马行，骆驼、牛、驴、猪、鹿、水牛及人民行，风吹日炙，彼四衢道泥干燥已，还复作尘。」

So it is, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the second dhyāna. Having attained the second dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! This is called one person.

「如是，诸贤！或有一人得第二禅，彼得第二禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！是谓有一人。

"Furthermore, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the third dhyāna. Having attained the third dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! It is like a mountain spring or lake, clear and calm, not moving or agitated, without waves. Noble ones! If someone were to say that the mountain spring or lake will never move again, nor have waves, would that be a correct statement?"

「复次，诸贤！或有一人得第三禅，彼得第三禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！犹山泉湖水，澄清平岸，定不动摇，亦无波浪。诸贤！若有作是说，彼山泉湖水终不复动，亦无波浪，如是彼为正说耶？」

"No, why is that? Because a strong wind may suddenly blow from the east, stirring up the lake water, causing waves. Similarly, a strong wind may suddenly blow from the south, west, or north, stirring up the lake water, causing waves."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？或于东方大风卒来，吹彼湖水，动涌波浪；如是南方、西方、北方大风卒来，吹彼湖水，动涌波浪。」

So it is, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the third dhyāna. Having attained the third dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! This is called one person.

「如是，诸贤！或有一人得第三禅，彼得第三禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！是谓有一人。

"Furthermore, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the fourth dhyāna. Having attained the fourth dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! It is like a householder or householder's son who has eaten exquisite food and is fully satisfied. If they were to say that they would never again wish to eat, would that be a correct statement?"

「复次，诸贤！或有一人得第四禅，彼得第四禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！犹如居士、居士子，食微妙食，充足饱满已，本欲食者，则不复欲。诸贤！若有作是说，彼居士、居士子，终不复欲得食，如是彼为正说耶？」

"No, why? That householder or householder's son, after a night of fasting, if they were to eat what they had not eaten before, they would still wish to eat it again."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？彼居士、居士子，过夜饥已，彼若本所不用食者，还复欲得。」

So it is, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the fourth dhyāna. Having attained the fourth dhyāna, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! This is called one person.

「如是，诸贤！或有一人得第四禅，彼得第四禅已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！是谓有一人。

"Furthermore, noble ones! There may be someone who attains the mind of the formless realm. Having attained the mind of the formless realm, they are content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, they often associate with laypeople, jest and boast, and engage in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, they give rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, their bodies and minds become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, they abandon the precepts and return to lay life. Noble ones! It is like a certain place where the sound of the chilioli-mīli insect is heard. In that place, either a king or a royal minister stays overnight. There are the sounds of elephants, horses, chariots, infantry, conches, drums, small drums, cymbals, dancing, singing, lutes, and eating. If they have previously heard the sound of the chilioli-mīli insect, they will no longer hear it. Noble ones! If someone says that in that place, one will never again hear the sound of the chilioli-mīli insect, is that a correct statement?"

「复次，诸贤！或有一人得无想心定，彼得无想心定已，便自安住，不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，心便生欲。彼心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！犹如一无事处，闻支离弥梨虫声，彼无事处，或王或王大臣夜止宿，彼象声、马声、车声、步声、螺声、鼓声、细腰鼓声、妓鼓声、舞声、歌声、琴声、饮食声，彼若本闻支离弥梨虫声便不复闻。诸贤！若有作是说，彼无事处，终不复闻支离弥梨虫声，如是彼为正说耶？」

"No, why? Because the king and the royal minister have passed the night and in the morning each returns to their own place. Because of hearing the sounds of elephants, horses, chariots, infantry, conches, drums, small drums, cymbals, dancing, singing, lutes, and eating, they do not hear the sound of the chilioli-mīli insect. When they have left, they hear it as before."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？彼王及王大臣过夜平旦，各自还归，彼若闻象声、马声、车声、步声、螺声、鼓声、细腰鼓声、妓鼓声、舞声、歌声、瑟声、饮食声故，不闻支离弥梨虫声，彼既去已，还闻如故。」

"So it is, noble ones! Having attained the mind of the formless realm, one is content to abide in it, no longer seeking what is not yet attained, not obtaining what is to be obtained, and not realizing what is to be realized. Later, one often associates with laypeople, jest and boast, and engages in various frivolous talk. Having associated with laypeople, jested and boasted, and engaged in various frivolous talk, one gives rise to desire. Having given rise to desire, one's body and mind become hot. Having become hot in body and mind, one abandons the precepts and returns to lay life. Noble ones! This is called one person."

「如是。诸贤！得无想心定，得无想心定已，便自安住、不复更求未得欲得、不获欲获、不作证欲作证。彼于后时，便数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗；彼数与白衣共会，调笑贡高，种种谈哗已，便心生欲。心生欲已，便身热心热。彼身心热已，便舍戒罢道。诸贤！是谓有一人。」

At that time, the monk Chida Xiangzi soon afterwards abandoned the precepts and returned to lay life. The relatives and friends of the monk Chida Xiangzi, having heard that the monk Chida Xiangzi had abandoned the precepts and returned to lay life, went to Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila and said, "Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila! Do you know the mind of the monk Chida Xiangzi, or do you know it for other reasons? Why is that? Now the monk Chida Xiangzi has abandoned the precepts and returned to lay life."

尔时，质多罗象子比丘寻于其后舍戒罢道，质多罗象子比丘诸亲朋友闻质多罗象子比丘舍戒罢道已，往诣尊者大拘𫄨罗所，到已，白曰：「尊者大拘𫄨罗！为知质多罗象子比丘心，为因余事知。所以者何？今质多罗象子比丘已舍戒罢道。」

Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila said to his relatives and friends, "Noble ones! This is exactly how it should be. Why is that? Because of not knowing and seeing the truth as it is. Why is that? Because of not knowing and seeing the truth as it is."

尊者大拘𫄨罗告彼亲朋友曰：「诸贤！此事正应尔。所以者何？以不知如真，不见如真。所以者何？因不知如真，不见如真故。」

The Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila spoke thus. The monks, having heard the Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

尊者大拘𫄨罗所说如是。彼诸比丘闻尊者大拘𫄨罗所说，欢喜奉行。
