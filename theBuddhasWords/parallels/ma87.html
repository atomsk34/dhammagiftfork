MĀ 87

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 8 秽品

87. （八七）经

Parallel to: MN 5 / EĀ 25.6

defilement, request, bhikṣu's invitation, wisdom, Ānanda's question, seeing the
flower simile, water purity, brahman, black, abiding, not present

秽、求、比丘请　　智、周那问见华喻、水净梵　　黑、住、无在后

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Vārāṇasī, staying in the Deer Park at the foot of the Bṛhadratha Hill.

一时，佛游婆奇瘦，在鼍山怖林鹿野园中。

At that time, Venerable Sāriputta addressed the monks, saying: "Virtuous ones! There are four kinds of people in the world. What are the four? There is someone who internally has defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having internal defilements; there is someone who internally has defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having internal defilements; there is someone who internally has no defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having no internal defilements; there is someone who internally has no defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having no internal defilements.

尔时，尊者舍梨子告诸比丘：「诸贤！世有四种人。云何为四？或有一人内实有秽不自知，内有秽不知如真；或有一人内实有秽自知，内有秽知如真；或有一人内实无秽不自知，内无秽不知如真；或有一人内实无秽自知，内无秽知如真。

Virtuous ones! If there is someone who internally has defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having internal defilements, this person is the most inferior among people. If there is someone who internally has defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having internal defilements, this person is the most superior among people. If there is someone who internally has no defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having no internal defilements, this person is the most inferior among people. If there is someone who internally has no defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having no internal defilements, this person is the most superior among people.

「诸贤！若有一人内实有秽不自知，内有秽不知如真者，此人于诸人中为最下贱。若有一人内实有秽自知，内有秽知如真者，此人于诸人中为最胜也。若有一人内实无秽不自知，内无秽不知如真者，此人于诸人中为最下贱。若有一人内实无秽自知，内无秽知如真者，此人于诸人中为最胜也。」

Then a certain monk rose from his seat, arranged his robe over one shoulder, joined his palms towards Venerable Sāriputta, and said: "Venerable Sāriputta! For what reason and on what basis do you say that the first two people both have defilements, with defiled minds, one inferior and one superior? And for what reason and on what basis do you say that the last two people both have no defilements, with undefiled minds, one inferior and one superior?"

于是，有一比丘即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向尊者舍梨子，白曰：「尊者舍梨子！何因、何缘说前二人俱有秽，秽污心，一者下贱，一者最胜？复何因缘说后二人俱无秽，不秽污心，一者下贱，一者最胜？」

Then Venerable Sāriputta replied to that monk: "Virtuous one! If there is someone who internally has defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having internal defilements, know that they do not wish to eliminate defilements, do not seek means, and do not diligently study. They then have defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death. Because they have defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death, they die unvirtuously and are reborn in an unwholesome state. Why is that? Because they have defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death. Virtuous one! It is like a person who buys a copper bowl from a shop or a copper smith, and it is soiled with dust. After buying it, they do not wash it often, rub it often, or expose it to the sun, and they put it in a dusty place. In this way, the copper bowl accumulates more dust and dirt. Virtuous one! In the same way, if there is someone who internally has defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having internal defilements, know that they do not wish to eliminate defilements, do not seek means, and do not diligently study. They then have defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death. Because they have defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death, they die unvirtuously and are reborn in an unwholesome state. Why is that? Because they have defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death.

于是，尊者舍梨子答彼比丘曰：「贤者！若有一人内实有秽不自知，内有秽不知如真者，当知彼人不欲断秽，不求方便，不精勤学，彼便有秽，秽污心命终；彼因有秽，秽污心命终故，便不贤死，生不善处。所以者何？彼因有秽，秽污心命终故。贤者！犹如有人或从市肆，或从铜作家，买铜槃来，尘垢所污，彼持来已，不数洗尘，不数揩拭，亦不日炙，又著饶尘处，如是铜槃增受尘垢。贤者！如是若有一人内实有秽不自知，内有秽不知如真者，当知彼人不欲断秽，不求方便，不精勤学，彼便有秽，秽污心命终；彼因有秽，秽污心命终故，便不贤死，生不善处。所以者何？彼因有秽，秽污心命终故。

Virtuous one! If there is someone who internally has defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having internal defilements, know that they wish to eliminate defilements, seek means, and diligently study. They then have no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death. Because they have no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death, they die virtuously and are reborn in a wholesome state. Why is that? Because they have no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death. Virtuous one! It is like a person who buys a copper bowl from a shop or a copper smith, and it is soiled with dust. After buying it, they wash it often, rub it often, expose it to the sun often, and do not put it in a dusty place. In this way, the copper bowl becomes extremely clean. Virtuous one! In the same way, if there is someone who internally has defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having internal defilements, know that they wish to eliminate defilements, seek means, and diligently study. They then have no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death. Because they have no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death, they die virtuously and are reborn in a wholesome state. Why is that? Because they have no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death.

「贤者！若有一人我内有秽，我内实有此秽知如真者，当知彼人欲断此秽，求方便，精勤学，彼便无秽，不秽污心命终；彼因无秽，不秽污心命终故，便贤死，生善处。所以者何？彼因无秽，不秽污心命终故。贤者！犹如有人或从市肆，或从铜作家，买铜槃来，尘垢所污，彼持来已，数数洗尘，数数揩拭，数数日炙，不著饶尘处，如是铜槃便极净洁。贤者！如是若有一人我内有秽，我内实有此秽知如真者，当知彼人欲断此秽，求方便，精勤学，彼便无秽，不秽污心命终；彼因无秽，不秽污心命终故，便贤死，生善处。所以者何？彼因无秽，不秽污心命终故。

Virtuous one! If there is someone who internally has no defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having no internal defilements, know that they do not guard against what is known by the eyes and ears. Because they do not guard against what is known by the eyes and ears, they are bound by desire. They then have desire and defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death. Because they have desire and defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death, they die unvirtuously and are reborn in an unwholesome state. Why is that? Because they have desire and defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death. Virtuous one! It is like a person who buys a copper bowl from a shop or a copper smith, and it is clean and free from dust. After buying it, they do not wash it often, rub it often, expose it to the sun often, and put it in a dusty place. In this way, the copper bowl will definitely accumulate dust and dirt. Virtuous one! In the same way, if there is someone who internally has no defilements but does not know it, and does not know the truth of having no internal defilements, know that they do not guard against what is known by the eyes and ears. Because they do not guard against what is known by the eyes and ears, they are bound by desire. They then have desire and defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death. Because they have desire and defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death, they die unvirtuously and are reborn in an unwholesome state. Why is that? Because they have desire and defilements, with a defiled mind at the time of death.

「贤者！若有一人我内无秽，我内实无此秽不知如真者，当知彼人不护由眼耳所知法，彼因不护由眼耳所知法故，则为欲心缠，彼便有欲有秽，秽污心命终；彼因有欲有秽，秽污心命终故，便不贤死，生不善处。所以者何？彼因有欲有秽，秽污心命终故。贤者！犹如有人或从市肆，或从铜作家，买铜槃来，无垢净洁，彼持来已，不数洗尘，不数揩拭，不数日炙，著饶尘处，如是铜槃必受尘垢。贤者！如是若有一人我内无秽，我内实无此秽不知如真者，当知彼人不护由眼耳所知法，彼因不护由眼耳所知法故，则为欲心缠，彼便有欲有秽，秽污心命终；彼因有欲有秽，秽污心命终故，便不贤死，生不善处。所以者何？彼因有欲有秽，秽污心命终故。

Virtuous one! If there is someone who internally has no defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having no internal defilements, know that they guard against what is known by the eyes and ears. Because they guard against what is known by the eyes and ears, they are not bound by desire. They then have no desire and no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death. Because they have no desire and no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death, they die virtuously and are reborn in a wholesome state. Why is that? Because they have no desire and no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death. Virtuous one! It is like a person who buys a copper bowl from a shop or a copper smith, and it is clean and free from dust. After buying it, they wash it often, rub it often, expose it to the sun often, and do not put it in a dusty place. In this way, the copper bowl becomes extremely clean. Virtuous one! In the same way, if there is someone who internally has no defilements and knows it, and knows the truth of having no internal defilements, know that they guard against what is known by the eyes and ears. Because they guard against what is known by the eyes and ears, they are not bound by desire. They then have no desire and no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death. Because they have no desire and no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death, they die virtuously and are reborn in a wholesome state. Why is that? Because they have no desire and no defilements, with an undefiled mind at the time of death.

「贤者！若有一人我内无秽，我内实无此秽知如真者，当知彼人护由眼耳所知法，彼因护由眼耳所知法故，则不为欲心缠，彼便无欲无秽，不秽污心命终；彼因无欲无秽，不秽污心命终故，便贤死，生善处。所以者何？彼因无欲无秽，不秽污心命终故。贤者！犹如有人或从市肆，或从铜作家，买铜槃来，无垢净洁，彼持来已，数数洗磨，数数揩拭，数数日炙，不著饶尘处，如是铜槃便极净洁。贤者！如是若有一人我内无秽，我内实无此秽知如真者，当知彼人护由眼耳所知法，彼因护由眼耳所知法故，则不为欲心缠，彼便无欲无秽，不秽污心命终；彼因无欲无秽，不秽污心命终故，便贤死，生善处。所以者何？彼因无欲无秽，不秽污心命终故。

"Virtuous one! Because of this and that, I say that the two people mentioned earlier both have defilements, with defiled minds, one is inferior and the other is supreme. Because of this and that, I say that the two people mentioned later both have no defilements, with undefiled minds, one is inferior and the other is supreme."

「贤者！因是缘是，说前二人俱有秽，秽污心，一者下贱，一者最胜。因是缘是，说后二人俱无秽秽污心，一者下贱，一者最胜。」

Then another bhikkhu rose from his seat, arranged his robe over one shoulder, paid homage to the Venerable Sāriputta with his hands placed palm-to-palm over his heart, and said to him:

于是，复有比丘即从坐起，偏袒著衣，叉手向尊者舍梨子，白曰：「尊者舍梨子！所说秽者，何等为秽？」

The Venerable Sāriputta replied to the bhikkhu: "Virtuous one! Countless evil and unwholesome things arise from desire, which is called defilement. Why is that? Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'May others not know that I have committed an offense.' Virtuous one! Suppose others do know that they have committed an offense. Because others know that they have committed an offense, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome. Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'May others rebuke me in private for my offense, not in public.' Virtuous one! Suppose others rebuke them in public, not in private. Because others rebuke them in public, not in private, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome. Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'May the superior ones rebuke me for my offense, not the inferior ones.' Virtuous one! Suppose the inferior ones rebuke them for their offense, not the superior ones. Because the inferior ones rebuke them for their offense, not the superior ones, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

尊者舍梨子答比丘曰：「贤者！无量恶不善法从欲生，谓之秽。所以者何？或有一人心生如是欲：『我所犯戒，莫令他人知我犯戒。』贤者！或有他人知彼犯戒，彼因他人知犯戒故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『我所犯戒，当令他人于屏处诃，莫令在众诃我犯戒。』贤者！或有他人于众中诃，不在屏处，彼因他人在众中诃，不在屏处故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『我所犯戒，令胜人诃，莫令不如人诃我犯戒。』贤者！或有不如人诃彼犯戒，非是胜人，彼因不如人诃，非胜人故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。

Virtuous one! Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'May I sit in the presence of the Buddha and ask the Blessed One about the Dhamma for the sake of the bhikkhus, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu sits in the presence of the Buddha and asks the Blessed One about the Dhamma for the sake of the bhikkhus. Because another bhikkhu sits in the presence of the Buddha and asks the Blessed One about the Dhamma for the sake of the bhikkhus, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome. Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'When the bhikkhus enter the inner quarters, may I be the foremost, with the bhikkhus attending me, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu is the foremost, with the bhikkhus attending him, when the bhikkhus enter the inner quarters. Because another bhikkhu is the foremost, with the bhikkhus attending him, when the bhikkhus enter the inner quarters, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

「贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『令我在佛前坐，问世尊法，为诸比丘说，莫令余比丘在佛前坐，问世尊法，为诸比丘说。』贤者！或有余比丘在佛前坐，问世尊法，为诸比丘说，彼因余比丘在佛前坐，问世尊法，为诸比丘说故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『诸比丘入内时，令我最在其前，诸比丘侍从我将入内，莫令余比丘诸比丘入内时，最在其前，诸比丘侍从彼将入内。』贤者！或有余比丘诸比丘入内时，最在其前，诸比丘侍从彼将入内，彼因余比丘诸比丘入内时，最在其前，诸比丘侍从彼将入内故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。

Virtuous one! Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'When the bhikkhus have entered the inner quarters, may I be the foremost, obtaining the foremost seat, the foremost water for washing, and the foremost food, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu is the foremost, obtaining the foremost seat, the foremost water for washing, and the foremost food, when the bhikkhus have entered the inner quarters. Because another bhikkhu is the foremost, obtaining the foremost seat, the foremost water for washing, and the foremost food, when the bhikkhus have entered the inner quarters, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

「贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『诸比丘已入内时，令我最在上坐，得第一座，第一澡水，得第一食，莫令余比丘诸比丘已入内时，最在上坐，得第一座，第一澡水，得第一食。』贤者！或有余比丘诸比丘已入内时，最在上坐，得第一座，第一澡水，得第一食；彼因余比丘诸比丘已入内时，最在上坐，得第一座，第一澡水，得第一食故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。

Virtuous one! Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'When the bhikkhus have finished their meal, put away their bowls and washed their hands, may I give a Dhamma talk to the householders, arousing their desire, inspiring them, and gladdening them, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu gives a Dhamma talk to the householders, arousing their desire, inspiring them, and gladdening them, when the bhikkhus have finished their meal, put away their bowls and washed their hands. Because another bhikkhu gives a Dhamma talk to the householders, arousing their desire, inspiring them, and gladdening them, when the bhikkhus have finished their meal, put away their bowls and washed their hands, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

「贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『诸比丘食竟，收摄食器，行澡水已，令我为诸居士说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜；莫令余比丘诸比丘食竟，收摄食器，行澡水已，为诸居士说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜。』贤者！或有余比丘诸比丘食竟，收摄食器，行澡水已，为诸居士说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜；彼因余比丘诸比丘食竟，收摄食器，行澡水已，为诸居士说法，劝发渴仰，成就欢喜故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。

Virtuous one! Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'When the householders go to the monastery, may I associate with them, gather with them, sit with them, and converse with them, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu associates with them, gathers with them, sits with them, and converses with them, when the householders go to the monastery. Because another bhikkhu associates with them, gathers with them, sits with them, and converses with them, when the householders go to the monastery, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

「贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『诸居士往诣众园时，令我与共会共集，共坐共论，莫令余比丘诸居士往诣众园时，与共会共集，共坐共论。』贤者！或有余比丘诸居士往诣众园时，与共会共集，共坐共论，彼因余比丘诸居士往诣众园时，与共会共集，共坐共论故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。

Virtuous one! Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'May I be known to the king, and to the king's ministers, brahmins, householders, and people of the country, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu is known to the king, and to the king's ministers, brahmins, householders, and people of the country. Because another bhikkhu is known to the king, and to the king's ministers, brahmins, householders, and people of the country, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

「贤者！或有一人心生如是欲：『令我为王者所识，及王大臣、梵志、居士、国中人民所知重，莫令余比丘为王者所识，及王大臣、梵志、居士、国中人民所知重。』贤者！或有余比丘为王者所识，及王大臣、梵志、居士、国中人民所知重，彼因余比丘为王者所识，及王大臣、梵志、居士、国中人民所知重故，心便生恶，若彼心生恶及心生欲者，俱是不善。

Virtuous one! Suppose someone has this kind of desire: 'May I be respected by the four assemblies of bhikkhus, bhikkhunīs, male lay followers, and female lay followers, not another bhikkhu.' Virtuous one! Suppose another bhikkhu is respected by the four assemblies of bhikkhus, bhikkhunīs, male lay followers, and female lay followers. Because another bhikkhu is respected by the four assemblies of bhikkhus, bhikkhunīs, male lay followers, and female lay followers, their mind gives rise to evil. If their mind gives rise to evil and desire, both are unwholesome.

At that time, there was another chariot-maker cutting and shaping a chariot axle. The old chariot-maker Nirgrantha went to that house. Then the old chariot-maker Nirgrantha saw him shaping the axle and thought: 'If that chariot-maker holds an axe and cuts out the bad parts of the axle, then the axle will be very good.' Then that chariot-maker did just as the old chariot-maker Nirgrantha had thought in his mind. He took an axe and cut out the bad parts. Then the old chariot-maker Nirgrantha was extremely delighted and said: 'Chariot-maker! Your mind is like this, so you know my mind. Why is that? Because you took an axe and cut out the bad parts of the axle, just as I intended.' In this way, Venerable Śāriputra! If there are flattery, deceit, jealousy, lack of faith, laziness, no right mindfulness and right knowledge, no concentration and wisdom, a deluded mind, not guarding the faculties, not practicing as a śramaṇa, and no discrimination, Venerable Śāriputra! The mind is said to know that mind. Venerable Śāriputra! If there are people who are not flattering, not deceitful, without jealousy, have faith, are diligent without laziness, have right mindfulness and right knowledge, practice concentration and wisdom, have an undeluded mind, guard the faculties, widely practice as a śramaṇa, and are well-discriminating, then when they hear the Dharma taught by Venerable Śāriputra, it is like a hungry person wanting to eat, or a thirsty person wanting to drink, with their mouth and mind.

尊者大目揵连则便白曰：「尊者舍梨子！我忆一时游王舍城，在岩山中，我于尔时过夜平旦，著衣持钵，入王舍城而行乞食，诣旧车师无衣满子家。时，彼比舍更有车师斫治车轴，是时，旧车师无衣满子往至彼家。于是，旧车师无衣满子见彼治轴，心生是念：『若彼车师执斧治轴，斫彼彼恶处者，如是彼轴便当极好。』时，彼车师即如旧车师无衣满子心中所念，便持斧斫彼彼恶处。于是，旧车师无衣满子极大欢喜，而作是说：『车师子！汝心如是，则知我心。所以者何？以汝持斧斫治车轴彼彼恶处，如我意故。』如是，尊者舍梨子！若有谀谄、欺诳、嫉妬、无信、懈怠，无正念正智，无定无慧，其心狂惑，不护诸根，不修沙门，无所分别。尊者舍梨子！心为知彼心故，而说此法。尊者舍梨子！若有人不谀谄，不欺诳，无嫉妬，有信，精进而无懈怠，有正念正智，修定修慧，心不狂惑，守护诸根，广修沙门而善分别。彼闻尊者舍梨子所说法者，犹饥欲得食，渴欲得饮，口及意也。

"Venerable Śāriputra! It is like a kṣatriya girl, a brahmin girl, a householder girl, or a worker girl, who is beautiful, well-bathed, with fragrant body, wearing bright and clean clothes, adorned with various ornaments. If someone thinks of that girl, seeking benefit and advantage, seeking peace and happiness, and gives her a blue lotus garland, or a jasmine garland, or a sumana flower garland, or a pāṭala flower garland, or an ati-mūrdha flower garland, that girl is delighted and receives it with both hands to adorn her head. Venerable Śāriputra! In this way, if there are people who are not flattering, not deceitful, without jealousy, have faith, are diligent without laziness, have right mindfulness and right knowledge, practice concentration and wisdom, have an undeluded mind, guard the faculties, widely practice as a śramaṇa, and are well-discriminating, then when they hear the Dharma taught by Venerable Śāriputra, it is like a hungry person wanting to eat, or a thirsty person wanting to drink, with their mouth and mind. Venerable Śāriputra is very wonderful! Very special! Venerable Śāriputra always rescues all practitioners, making them leave unwholesomeness and establish them in wholesomeness."

「尊者舍梨子！犹刹利女，梵志、居士、工师女，端正姝好，极净沐浴，以香涂身，著明净衣，种种璎珞严饰其容；或复有人为念彼女，求利及饶益，求安隐快乐，以青莲华鬘，或薝卜华鬘；或修摩那华鬘，或婆师华鬘；或阿提牟哆华鬘，持与彼女。彼女欢喜，两手受之，以严其头。尊者舍梨子！如是，若有人不谀谄，不欺诳，无嫉妬] cf.［丽】，有信，精进而无懈怠，有正念正智，修定修慧，心不狂惑，守护诸根，广修沙门而善分别，彼闻尊者舍梨子所说法者，犹饥欲食，渴欲得饮，口及意也。尊者舍梨子甚奇！甚特！尊者舍梨子常拔济诸梵行者，令离不善，安立善处。」

The two Venerables spoke to each other in this way, then rose from their seats and left.

如是二尊更相称说，从座起去。

The Venerable Śāriputra spoke thus. The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana and the other bhikṣus, having heard the Venerable Śāriputra's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

尊者舍梨子所说如是。尊者大目揵连及诸比丘，闻尊者舍梨子所说，欢喜奉行。
