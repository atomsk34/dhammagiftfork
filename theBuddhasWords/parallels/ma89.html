MĀ 89

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 8 秽品

89. （八九）比丘请经

Parallel to: MN 15

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Bamboo Grove, in the Kalandaka Park, near Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, having entered the rainy season retreat.

一时，佛游王舍城，在竹林迦兰哆园，与大比丘众俱，受夏坐。

At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu invites other bhikṣus, saying, 'Venerables, please speak to me, teach me, admonish me, and do not criticize me.' Why is that? Virtuous ones! There may be a person who speaks perversely, who has the Dharma of speaking perversely. Because of having the Dharma of speaking perversely, the virtuous practitioners will not speak to him, will not teach or admonish him, but will criticize him. Virtuous ones! What is the Dharma of speaking perversely? If there is someone who has the Dharma of speaking perversely, the virtuous practitioners will not speak to him, will not teach or admonish him, but will criticize him. Virtuous ones! There may be a person who has evil desires and thoughts of desire. Virtuous ones! If there is a person who has evil desires and thoughts of desire, this is called the Dharma of speaking perversely. Thus, one is defiled in conduct, defiled in thoughts, bound by non-speaking, deceitful, hypocritical, stingy, jealous, without shame or conscience, angry, with an evil mind, speaks angrily, criticizes bhikṣus, criticizes bhikṣus with contempt, criticizes bhikṣus by revealing, avoids each other and speaks of external matters, does not speak, is angry, and has intense hatred and jealousy. One has evil friends and companions, is ungrateful, and does not know gratitude. Virtuous ones! If there is a person who is ungrateful and does not know gratitude, this is called the Dharma of speaking perversely. Virtuous ones! These are called the Dharmas of speaking perversely. If there is someone who has the Dharma of speaking perversely, the virtuous practitioners will not speak to him, will not teach or admonish him, but will criticize him. Virtuous ones! A bhikṣu should reflect on this.

尔时，尊者大目揵连告诸比丘：「诸贤！若有比丘请诸比丘，诸尊，语我、教我、诃我，莫难于我。所以者何？诸贤！或有一人戾语，成就戾语法，成就戾语法故，令诸梵行者不语彼，不教、不诃而难彼人。诸贤！何者戾语法？若有成就戾语法者，诸梵行者不语彼，不教、不诃而难彼人。诸贤！或有一人恶欲、念欲。诸贤！若有人恶欲、念欲者，是谓戾语法。如是染行染、不语结住，欺诳谀谄，悭贪嫉妬，无慙无愧，瞋弊恶意，瞋恚语言，诃比丘诃，诃比丘轻慢，诃比丘发露，更互相避而说外事，不语、瞋恚、憎嫉炽盛，恶朋友、恶伴侣，无恩、不知恩。诸贤！若有人无恩、不知恩者，是谓戾语法。诸贤！是谓诸戾语法。若有成就戾语法者，诸梵行者不语彼，不教、不诃而难彼人，诸贤！比丘者，当自思量。

Virtuous ones! If there is a person who has evil desires and thoughts of desire, I do not love him; if I have evil desires and thoughts of desire, he also does not love me. A bhikṣu should contemplate in this way and not engage in evil desires or thoughts of desire. One should learn in this way. Thus, one is defiled in conduct, defiled in thoughts, bound by non-speaking, deceitful, hypocritical, stingy, jealous, without shame or conscience, angry, with an evil mind, speaks angrily, criticizes bhikṣus, criticizes bhikṣus with contempt, criticizes bhikṣus by revealing, avoids each other and speaks of external matters, does not speak, is angry, and has intense hatred and jealousy. One has evil friends and companions, is ungrateful, and does not know gratitude. Virtuous ones! If there is a person who is ungrateful and does not know gratitude, I do not love him; if I am ungrateful and do not know gratitude, he also does not love me. A bhikṣu should contemplate in this way and not engage in ingratitude or ignorance of gratitude. One should learn in this way.

「诸贤！若有人恶欲、念欲者，我不爱彼；若我恶欲、念欲者，彼亦不爱我。比丘如是观，不行恶欲、不念欲者，当学如是。如是染行染、不语结住，欺诳谀谄，悭贪嫉妬，无慙无愧，瞋弊恶意，瞋瞋语言，诃比丘，诃比丘轻慢，诃比丘发露，更互相避而说外事，不语、瞋恚、憎嫉炽盛，恶朋友、恶伴侣，无恩、不知恩。诸贤！若有人无恩、不知恩者，我不爱彼；若我无恩、不知恩者，彼亦不爱我。比丘如是观，不行无恩、不知恩者，当学如是。

Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu does not invite other bhikṣus, saying, 'Venerables, please speak to me, teach me, admonish me, and do not criticize me.' Why is that? Virtuous ones! There may be a person who speaks well, who has the Dharma of speaking well. Because of having the Dharma of speaking well, the virtuous practitioners will speak well to him, teach and admonish him well, and not criticize him. Virtuous ones! What is the Dharma of speaking well? If there is someone who has the Dharma of speaking well, the virtuous practitioners will speak well to him, teach and admonish him well, and not criticize him. Virtuous ones! There may be a person who does not have evil desires and thoughts of desire. Virtuous ones! If there is a person who does not have evil desires and thoughts of desire, this is called the Dharma of speaking well. Thus, one is not defiled in conduct, not defiled in thoughts, not bound by non-speaking, not deceitful, not hypocritical, not stingy, not jealous, not without shame or conscience, not angry, with a good mind, does not speak angrily, does not criticize bhikṣus, does not criticize bhikṣus with contempt, does not criticize bhikṣus by revealing, does not avoid each other and speak of external matters, does not not speak, is not angry, and does not have intense hatred and jealousy. One has good friends and companions, is grateful, and knows gratitude. Virtuous ones! If there is a person who is grateful and knows gratitude, I love him; if I am grateful and know gratitude, he also loves me. A bhikṣu should contemplate in this way and engage in gratitude and knowledge of gratitude. One should learn in this way.

「诸贤！若比丘不请诸比丘，诸尊！语我、教我、诃我，莫难于我。所以者何？诸贤！或有一人善语，成就善语法，成就善语法故，诸梵行者善语彼，善教、善诃，不难彼人。诸贤！何者善语法？若有成就善语法者，诸梵行者善语彼，善教、善诃，不难彼人。诸贤！或有一人不恶欲、不念欲。诸贤！若有人不恶欲、不念欲者，是谓善语法。如是不染行染、不语结住，不欺诳谀谄，不悭贪嫉妬，不无慙无愧，不瞋弊恶意，不瞋瞋语言，不诃比丘诃，不诃比丘轻慢，不诃比丘发露，不更互相避而说外事，不不语、瞋恚、憎嫉炽盛，不恶朋友、恶伴侣，不无恩、不知恩。诸贤！若有人不无恩、不知恩者，是谓善语法。诸贤！是谓诸善语法。若有成就善语法者，诸梵行者善语彼，善教、善诃，不难彼人。诸贤！比丘者当自思量。

Virtuous ones! If there is a person who does not have evil desires and thoughts of desire, I love him; if I do not have evil desires and thoughts of desire, he also loves me. A bhikṣu should contemplate in this way and not engage in evil desires or thoughts of desire. One should learn in this way. Thus, one is not defiled in conduct, not defiled in thoughts, not bound by non-speaking, not deceitful, not hypocritical, not stingy, not jealous, not without shame or conscience, not angry, with a good mind, does not speak angrily, does not criticize bhikṣus, does not criticize bhikṣus with contempt, does not criticize bhikṣus by revealing, does not avoid each other and speak of external matters, does not not speak, is not angry, and does not have intense hatred and jealousy. One has good friends and companions, is grateful, and knows gratitude. Virtuous ones! If there is a person who is grateful and knows gratitude, I love him; if I am grateful and know gratitude, he also loves me. A bhikṣu should contemplate in this way and engage in gratitude and knowledge of gratitude. One should learn in this way.

「诸贤！若有人不恶欲、不念欲者，我爱彼人；若我不恶欲、不念欲者，彼亦爱我。比丘如是观，不行恶欲、不念欲者，当学如是。如是不染行染、不不语结住，不欺诳谀谄，不悭贪嫉妬，不无慙无愧，不瞋弊恶意，不瞋瞋语言，不诃比丘诃，不诃比丘轻慢，不诃比丘发露，不更互相避而说外事，不不语、瞋恚、憎嫉炽盛，不恶朋友、恶伴侣，不无恩、不知恩。诸贤！若有人不无恩、不知恩者，我爱彼人；若我不无恩、不知恩者，彼亦爱我。比丘如是观，不无恩、不知恩者，当学如是。

Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates in this way, he will benefit greatly. Do I have evil desires and thoughts of desire, or do I not have evil desires and thoughts of desire? Virtuous ones! When a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he has evil desires and thoughts of desire or not. If he knows he has evil desires and thoughts of desire, he will not be joyful and will seek to eliminate them. Virtuous ones! When a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he does not have evil desires and thoughts of desire. He will then be joyful, thinking, 'I am pure.' He seeks to learn the noble Dharma, and therefore is joyful. Virtuous ones! It is like a person with eyes using a mirror to look at their face, seeing whether it is clean or unclean. Virtuous ones! If a person with eyes sees dirt on their face, they will not be joyful and will seek to wash it. Virtuous ones! If a person with eyes sees their face is clean, they will be joyful, thinking, 'My face is clean.' Therefore, they are joyful.

「诸贤！若比丘如是观者，必多所饶益，我为恶欲、念欲，为不恶欲、念欲耶？诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我是恶欲、念欲者，则不欢悦，便求欲断。诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我无恶欲、不念欲者，即便欢悦，我自清净，求学尊法，是故欢悦。诸贤！犹有目人以镜自照，则见其面净及不净。诸贤！若有目人见面有垢者，则不欢悦，便求欲洗。诸贤！若有目人见面无垢者，即便欢悦我面清净，是故欢悦。

Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he engages in defiled conduct and thoughts or not. If he knows he engages in defiled conduct and thoughts, he will not be joyful and will seek to eliminate them. Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he does not engage in defiled conduct and thoughts. He will then be joyful, thinking, 'I am pure.' He seeks to learn the noble Dharma, and therefore is joyful. Thus, I am defiled in conduct and thoughts, or not defiled in conduct and thoughts; bound by non-speaking, or not bound by non-speaking; deceitful and hypocritical, or not deceitful and hypocritical; stingy, greedy, and jealous, or not stingy, greedy, and jealous; without shame or conscience, or not without shame or conscience; angry, with an evil mind, or not angry, with an evil mind; speaking angrily, or not speaking angrily; criticizing bhikṣus, or not criticizing bhikṣus; criticizing bhikṣus with contempt, or not criticizing bhikṣus with contempt; criticizing bhikṣus by revealing, or not criticizing bhikṣus by revealing; avoiding each other, or not avoiding each other; speaking of external matters, or not speaking of external matters; not speaking, being angry, and having intense hatred and jealousy, or not not speaking, not being angry, and not having intense hatred and jealousy; having bad friends and companions, or not having bad friends and companions; being ungrateful and not knowing gratitude, or not being ungrateful and knowing gratitude? Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he is ungrateful and does not know gratitude. He will then not be joyful and will seek to eliminate them. Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he is not ungrateful and knows gratitude. He will then be joyful, thinking, 'I am pure.' He seeks to learn the noble Dharma, and therefore is joyful. Virtuous ones! It is like a person with eyes using a mirror to look at their face, seeing whether it is clean or unclean. Virtuous ones! If a person with eyes sees dirt on their face, they will not be joyful and will seek to wash it. Virtuous ones! If a person with eyes sees their face is clean, they will be joyful, thinking, 'My face is clean.' Therefore, they are joyful.

「诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我行恶欲、念欲者，则不欢悦，便求欲断。诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我不行恶欲、不念欲者，即便欢悦，我自清净，求学尊法，是故欢悦。如是我为染行染，为不染行染；为不语结住，为不不语结住；为欺诳谀谄，为不欺诳谀谄；为悭贪嫉妬，为不悭贪嫉妬；为无慙无愧，为不无慙无愧；为瞋弊恶意，为不瞋弊恶意；为瞋瞋语言，为不瞋瞋语言；为诃比丘诃，为不诃比丘诃；为诃比丘轻慢，为不诃比丘轻慢；为诃比丘发露，为不诃比丘发露；为更互相避，为不更互相避；为说外事，为不说外事；为不语、瞋恚、憎嫉炽盛，为不不语、瞋恚、憎嫉炽盛；为恶朋友、恶伴侣，为不恶朋友、恶伴侣；为无恩、不知恩，为不无恩，不知恩耶？诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我无恩、不知恩者，则不欢悦，便求欲断。诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我不无恩、不知恩者，即便欢悦，我自清净，求学尊法，是故欢悦。诸贤！犹有目人以镜自照，则见其面净及不净。诸贤！若有目人见面有垢者，则不欢悦，便求欲洗。诸贤！若有目人见面无垢者，即便欢悦我面清净，是故欢悦。

"Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he is ungrateful and does not know gratitude. He will then not be joyful and will seek to eliminate them. Virtuous ones! If a bhikṣu contemplates, he will know whether he is not ungrateful and knows gratitude. He will then be joyful, thinking, 'I am pure.' He seeks to learn the noble Dharma, and therefore is joyful. Because of joy, he attains joy. Because of joy, he attains physical tranquility. Because of physical tranquility, he attains the bliss of awakening. Because of the bliss of awakening, he attains a concentrated mind. Virtuous ones! The noble disciple of much learning, because of the concentrated mind, then sees as it really is and knows as it really is. Because of seeing as it really is and knowing as it really is, he then attains disgust. Because of disgust, he then attains dispassion. Because of dispassion, he then attains liberation. Because of liberation, he then attains the knowledge and vision of liberation, knowing that birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and there is no more of this mundane state, knowing it as it really is."

「诸贤！如是若比丘观时，则知我无恩、不知恩者，则不欢悦，便求欲断。诸贤！若比丘观时，则知我不无恩、不知恩者，即便欢悦，我自清净，求学尊法，是故欢悦。因欢悦故，便得欢喜。因欢喜故，便得止身。因止身故，便得觉乐。因觉乐故，便得定心。诸贤！多闻圣弟子因定心故，便见如实、知如真。因见如实、知如真故，便得厌。因厌故，便得无欲。因无欲故，便得解脱。因解脱故，便得知解脱，生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，不更受有，知如真。」

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana's words, rejoiced and practiced them.

尊者大目揵连所说如是。彼诸比丘闻尊者大目揵连所说，欢喜奉行。
