MĀ 90

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 8 秽品

90. （九〇）知法经

Parallel to: AN 10.24

Thus have I heard. Once, the Buddha was traveling in Kosambi, staying at the Ghosita Monastery.

我闻如是：一时，佛游拘舍弥在瞿师罗园。

At that time, Venerable Cunda said to the monks, "If a monk says, 'I know the knowable dharmas without any further investigation,' but that wise one's mind dwells in evil further investigation, then there is dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view; but that wise one's mind dwells in evil desire and evil view. The virtuous practitioners know that that wise one does not know the knowable dharmas without any further investigation. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind dwells in further investigation, and there is dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind dwells in evil desire and evil view.

尔时，尊者周那告诸比丘：「若有比丘作如是说：『我知诸法所可知法而无增伺。』然彼贤者心生恶增伺而住，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见；然彼贤者心生恶欲、恶见而住。诸梵行人知彼贤者不知诸法所可知法而无增伺。所以者何？以彼贤者心生增伺而住，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见。所以者何？以彼贤者心生恶欲、恶见而住。

Virtuous ones! It is like a person who is not rich but claims to be rich, and also has no fief but claims to have a fief, and also has no livestock but claims to have livestock. When they want to use it, they have no gold, silver, pearls, lapis lazuli, crystal, amber, no livestock, rice, and also no slaves. Their relatives and friends go to them and say, 'You are really not rich but claim to be rich, and also have no fief but claim to have a fief, and also have no livestock but claim to have livestock. But when you want to use it, you have no gold, silver, pearls, lapis lazuli, crystal, amber, no livestock, rice, and also no slaves.' So it is, virtuous ones! If a monk says, 'I know the knowable dharmas without any further investigation,' but that wise one's mind dwells in evil further investigation, then there is dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view; but that wise one's mind dwells in evil desire and evil view. The virtuous practitioners know that that wise one does not know the knowable dharmas without any further investigation. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind does not incline towards the remainderless Nirvana of the exhaustion of further investigation, and there is dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind does not incline towards the remainderless Nirvana of the exhaustion of evil views.

「诸贤！犹人不富自称说富，亦无国封说有国封，又无畜牧说有畜牧，若欲用时，则无金、银、真珠、琉璃、水精、琥珀，无畜牧、米谷，亦无奴婢。诸亲朋友往诣彼所，而作是说：『汝实不富自称说富，亦无国封说有国封，又无畜牧说有畜牧，然欲用时，则无金、银、真珠、琉璃、水精、琥珀，无畜牧、米谷，亦无奴婢。』如是。诸贤！若有比丘作如是说：『我知诸法所可知法而无增伺。』然彼贤者心生恶增伺而住，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见；然彼贤者心生恶欲、恶见而住。诸梵行人知彼贤者不知诸法所可知法而无增伺。所以者何？以彼贤者心不向增伺尽、无余涅槃，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见。所以者何？以彼贤者心不向恶见法尽、无余涅槃。

Virtuous ones! There is a monk who does not say, 'I know the knowable dharmas without any further investigation,' but that wise one's mind does not dwell in evil further investigation, and there is no dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view; but that wise one's mind does not dwell in evil desire and evil view. The virtuous practitioners know that that wise one truly knows the knowable dharmas without any further investigation. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind does not dwell in evil further investigation, and there is no dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind does not dwell in evil desire and evil view.

「诸贤！或有比丘不作是说：『我知诸法所可知法而无增伺。』然彼贤者心不生恶增伺而住，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见，然彼贤者心不生恶欲、恶见而住。诸梵行人知彼贤者实知诸法所可知法而无增伺。所以者何？以彼贤者心不生恶增伺而住，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见。所以者何？以彼贤者心不生恶欲、恶见而住。

Virtuous ones! It is like a very rich person who says they are not rich, and also has a fief but says they do not have a fief, and also has livestock but says they do not have livestock. When they want to use it, they have gold, silver, pearls, lapis lazuli, crystal, amber, livestock, rice, and also slaves. Their relatives and friends go to them and say, 'You are really very rich but say you are not rich, and also have a fief but say you do not have a fief, and also have livestock but say you do not have livestock. But when you want to use it, you have gold, silver, pearls, lapis lazuli, crystal, amber, livestock, rice, and also slaves.' So it is, virtuous ones! If a monk does not say, 'I know the knowable dharmas without any further investigation,' but that wise one's mind does not dwell in evil further investigation, and there is no dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view; but that wise one's mind does not dwell in evil desire and evil view. The virtuous practitioners know that that wise one truly knows the knowable dharmas without any further investigation. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind inclines towards the remainderless Nirvana of the exhaustion of further investigation, and there is no dispute, resentment, anger, resentment, stinginess, jealousy, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and non-embarrassment. There is no evil desire or evil view. Why is that? Because that wise one's mind inclines towards the remainderless Nirvana of the exhaustion of evil views.

「诸贤！犹人大富自说不富，亦有国封说无国封，又有畜牧说无畜牧，若欲用时，则有金、银、真珠、琉璃、水精、琥珀，有畜牧、米谷，亦有奴婢。诸亲朋友往诣彼所，作如是说：『汝实大富自说不富，亦有国封说无国封，又有畜牧说无畜牧，然欲用时，则有金、银、真珠、琉璃、水精、琥珀，有畜牧、米谷，亦有奴婢。』如是。诸贤！若有比丘不作是说：『我知诸法所可知法而无增伺。』然彼贤者心不生恶增伺而住，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见，然彼贤者心不生恶欲、恶见而住。诸梵行人知彼贤者知诸法所可知法而无增伺。所以者何？以彼贤者心向增伺尽、无余涅槃，如是诤讼、恚恨、瞋缠、不语结、悭、嫉、欺诳、谀谄、无慙、无愧，无恶欲、恶见。所以者何？以彼贤者心向恶见法尽、无余涅槃。」

The Venerable Cūḷa-panthaka spoke thus. The monks, having heard the Venerable Cūḷa-panthaka's words, were delighted and remembered them well.

尊者周那所说如是。彼诸比丘闻尊者周那所说，欢喜奉行。
