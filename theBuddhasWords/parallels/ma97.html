MĀ 97

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 9 因品

97. （九七）大因经

Parallel to: DN 15 / DĀ 13

Cause, condition, and the two aggregates of suffering Superior mind
and mindfulness Lion's roar, udumbara Vow, and thought are the last.

因、处、二苦阴　　增上心、及念师子吼、优昙　　愿、想最在后

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in Kuru, staying at the city of Kumbhahattha in Kuru.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦，在剑磨瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was in seclusion, sitting in meditation, and this thought arose in his mind: "This dependent origination is indeed wondrous, extremely profound, and illuminating, yet my understanding is shallow." Then, in the afternoon, Venerable Ānanda rose from his meditation, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! I was in seclusion, sitting in meditation, and this thought arose in my mind: 'This dependent origination is indeed wondrous, extremely profound, and illuminating, yet my understanding is shallow.'"

尔时，尊者阿难闲居独处，宴坐思惟，心作是念：「此缘起甚奇，极甚深，明亦甚深，然我观见至浅至浅。」于是，尊者阿难则于晡时从宴坐起，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，却住一面，白曰：「世尊！我今闲居独处，宴坐思惟，心作是念：『此缘起甚奇，极甚深，明亦甚深，然我观见至浅至浅。』」

The World-Honored One said: "Ānanda! Do not think that this dependent origination is shallow. Why is that? This dependent origination is extremely profound, and the illumination is also profound. Ānanda! It is because one does not truly know and see this dependent origination, does not awaken to and penetrate it, that one thinks of those beings as being bound together like the threads of a loom, like entangled vines, with much disturbance and commotion, going from this world to that world, from that world to this world, coming and going without being able to transcend birth and death. Ānanda! Therefore, know that this dependent origination is extremely profound, and the illumination is also profound.

世尊告曰：「阿难！汝莫作是念，此缘起至浅至浅。所以者何？此缘起极甚深，明亦甚深。阿难！于此缘起不知如真，不见如实，不觉不达故。念彼众生如织机相锁，如蕴蔓草，多有调乱，怱怱喧閙，从此世至彼世，从彼世至此世，往来不能出过生死。阿难！是故知此缘起极甚深，明亦甚深。

Ānanda! If someone asks: 'Does old age and death have conditions?' you should answer: 'Old age and death have conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions for old age and death?' you should answer: 'They are conditioned by birth.' Ānanda! If someone asks: 'Does birth have conditions?' you should answer: 'Birth also has conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions for birth?' you should answer: 'They are conditioned by existence.' Ānanda! If someone asks: 'Does existence have conditions?' you should answer: 'Existence also has conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions for existence?' you should answer: 'They are conditioned by grasping.' Ānanda! If someone asks: 'Does grasping have conditions?' you should answer: 'Grasping also has conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions for grasping?' you should answer: 'They are conditioned by craving.' Ānanda! This is called conditioned by craving there is grasping, conditioned by grasping there is existence, conditioned by existence there is birth, conditioned by birth there is old age and death, conditioned by old age and death there is sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair. Thus, this complete mass of suffering arises.

「阿难！若有问者：『老死有缘耶？』当如是答：『老死有缘。』若有问者：『老死有何缘？』当如是答：『缘于生也。』阿难！若有问者：『生有缘耶？』当如是答：『生亦有缘。』若有问者：『生有何缘？』当如是答：『缘于有也。』阿难！若有问者：『有有缘耶？』当如是答：『有亦有缘。』若有问者：『有有何缘？』当如是答：『缘于受也。』阿难！若有问者：『受有缘耶？』当如是答：『受亦有缘。』若有问者：『受有何缘？』当如是答：『缘于爱也。』阿难！是为缘爱有受，缘受有有，缘有有生，缘生有老死，缘老死有愁戚，啼哭、忧苦、懊恼皆缘老死有，如此具足纯生大苦阴。

"Ānanda! When it is said that old age and death are conditioned by birth, it means that old age and death are conditioned by birth. Ānanda! If there were no birth, there would be no fish, no fish species, no birds, no bird species, no mosquitoes, no mosquito species, no dragons, no dragon species, no spirits, no spirit species, no ghosts, no ghost species, no gods, no god species, no humans, no human species. Ānanda! Those beings in those places, if there were no birth, if there were no individual births, if one were to be apart from birth, would there be old age and death?"

「阿难！缘生有老死者，此说缘生有老死，当知所谓缘生有老死。阿难！若无生，鱼、鱼种，鸟、鸟种，蚊、蚊种，龙、龙种，神、神种，鬼、鬼种，天、天种，人、人种，阿难！彼彼众生随彼彼处，若无生，各各无生者，设使离生，当有老死耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda! Therefore, one should know that the cause, source, origin, and condition of old age and death is birth. Why is that? Because of birth, there is old age and death.

「阿难！是故当知是老死因、老死习、老死本、老死缘者，谓此生也。所以者何？缘生故则有老死。

"Ānanda! The statement 'because of existence, there is birth' means that because of existence, there is birth. One should know that this is the meaning of 'because of existence, there is birth'. Ānanda! If there were no fish, no fish species, no birds, no bird species, no mosquitoes, no mosquito species, no dragons, no dragon species, no spirits, no spirit species, no ghosts, no ghost species, no gods, no god species, no humans, no human species. Ānanda! If those beings were not in those places, and if each one were not, then if one were to remove existence, would there be birth?"

「阿难！缘有有生者，此说缘有有生，当知所谓缘有有生。阿难！若无有，鱼、鱼种，鸟，鸟种，蚊、蚊种，龙、龙种，神、神种，鬼、鬼种，天、天种，人、人种。阿难！彼彼众生随彼彼处无有，各各无有者，设使离有，当有生耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, one should know that the cause, condition, root, and condition of birth is existence. Why is that? Because of existence, there is birth.

「阿难！是故当知是生因、生习、生本、生缘者，谓此有也。所以者何？缘有故则有生。

"Ānanda, the existence that arises from clinging is said to arise from clinging. One should know that it is said to arise from clinging. Ānanda, if there were no clinging, and no one who clings, would there still be existence apart from clinging, and could existence be established?"

「阿难！缘受有有者，此说缘受有有，当知所谓缘受有有。阿难！若无受，各各无受者，设使离受，当复有有，施设有有耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, one should know that there is a cause, a condition, a basis, and a condition for this existence. Because of clinging, there is existence.

「阿难！是故当知是有因、有习、有本、有缘者，谓此受也。所以者何？缘受故则有有。

"Ānanda, clinging is a condition for existence. This is said to be clinging as a condition for existence. One should know that clinging is a condition for existence. Ānanda, if there were no clinging, and no one clung, if one were to be separated from clinging, would there still be existence established as existence?"

「阿难！缘爱有受者，此说缘爱有受，当知所谓缘爱有受。阿难！若无爱，各各无爱者，设使离爱，当复有受立于受耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, one should know that the cause, accumulation, origin, and condition of feeling is this craving. Why is that? Because of craving, there is feeling.

「阿难！是故当知是受因、受习、受本、受缘者，谓此爱也。所以者何？缘爱故则有受。

"Ānanda! This is how craving leads to seeking, seeking leads to gain, gain leads to possession, possession leads to defilement, defilement leads to attachment, attachment leads to stinginess, stinginess leads to household, and household leads to guarding. Ānanda! Because of guarding, there are then weapons, fighting, flattery, deceit, false speech, and divisive speech, giving rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. There is such a complete and pure birth of the great mass of suffering. Ānanda! If there were no guarding, and each one had no guarding, even if one were to abandon guarding, would there be weapons, fighting, flattery, deceit, false speech, and divisive speech, giving rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas?"

「阿难！是为缘爱有求，缘求有利，缘利有分，缘分有染欲，缘染欲有著，缘著有悭，缘悭有家，缘家有守。阿难！缘守故便有刀杖，鬪诤、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌，起无量恶不善之法，有如此具足纯生大苦阴。阿难！若无守，各各无守者，设使离守，当有刀杖、鬪诤、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌，起无量恶不善之法耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda! Therefore, one should know that these weapons, fighting, flattery, deceit, false speech, and divisive speech, giving rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas, are based on this guarding. Why is that? Because of guarding, there are then weapons, fighting, flattery, deceit, false speech, and divisive speech, giving rise to immeasurable evil and unwholesome dharmas. There is such a complete and pure birth of the great mass of suffering.

「阿难！是故当知是刀杖、鬪诤、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌，起无量恶不善之法。因是习、是本、是缘者，谓此守也。所以者何？缘守故则有刀杖、鬪诤、谀谄、欺诳、妄言、两舌，起无量恶不善之法，有如此具足纯生大苦阴。

"Ānanda! When there is guarding due to having a family, this is said to be guarding due to having a family. One should know that this is called guarding due to having a family. Ānanda! If there were no family, and each had no family, if one were to leave the family, would there be guarding?"

「阿难！缘家有守者，此说缘家有守，当知所谓缘家有守。阿难！若无家，各各无家者，设使离家，当有守耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda! Therefore, one should know that the cause, practice, basis, and condition of guarding is this family. Why is that? Because of the family, there is guarding.

「阿难！是故当知是守因、守习、守本、守缘者，谓此家也。所以者何？缘家故则有守。

"Ānanda! The family that arises from stinginess is said to arise from stinginess. One should know that the family that arises from stinginess. Ānanda! If there is no stinginess, and each one is without stinginess, if one is separated from stinginess, would there be a family?"

「阿难！缘悭有家者，此说缘悭有家，当知所谓缘悭有家。阿难！若无悭，各各无悭者，设使离悭，当有家耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda! Therefore, one should know that the cause, practice, basis, and condition of the family is stinginess. Why is that? Because of stinginess, there is a family.

「阿难！是故当知是家因、家习、家本、家缘者，谓此悭也。所以者何？缘悭故则有家。

"Ānanda! When there is attachment, there is stinginess. This is said to be attachment to stinginess. One should know that it is called attachment to stinginess. Ānanda! If there is no attachment, and each one has no attachment, if one is detached, will there be stinginess?"

「阿难！缘著有悭者，此说缘著有悭，当知所谓缘著有悭。阿难！若无著，各各无著者，设使离著，当有悭耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, one should know that the cause, habit, root, and condition of stinginess is attachment. Why is that? Because of attachment, there is stinginess.

「阿难！是故当知是悭因、悭习、悭本、悭缘者，谓此著也。所以者何？缘著故则有悭。

"Ānanda, if there is attachment due to desire, this is called attachment due to desire. One should know that attachment is due to desire. Ānanda, if there is no desire, and each one has no desire, if one is separated from desire, would there be attachment?"

「阿难！缘欲有著者，此说缘欲有著，当知所谓缘欲有著。阿难！若无欲，各各无欲者，设使离欲，当有著耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, one should know that the cause, condition, basis, and condition of attachment is desire. Why is that? Because of desire, there is attachment.

「阿难！是故当知是著因、著习、著本、著缘者，谓此欲也。所以者何？缘欲故则有著。

"Ānanda, for those who are attached to the aggregates, it is said that they are attached to the aggregates. One should know that they are attached to the aggregates. Ānanda, if there were no aggregates, and each one were without aggregates, would there be attachment even if the aggregates were separated?"

「阿难！缘分有染欲者，此说缘分有染欲，当知所谓缘分有染欲。阿难！若无分，各各无分者，设使离分，当有染欲耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, one should know that the cause of attachment, the source of attachment, the basis of attachment, and the condition of attachment are the aggregates. Why is that? Because of the aggregates, there is attachment.

「阿难！是故当知是染欲因、染欲习、染欲本、染欲缘者，谓此分也。所以者何？缘分故则有染欲。

"Ānanda, the aggregates that are the basis of gain, this is said to be the basis of gain. Ānanda, if there were no gain, and no one had gain, would there still be the aggregates if gain were eliminated?"

「阿难！缘利有分者，此说缘利有分，当知所谓缘利有分。阿难！若无利，各各无利者，设使离利，当有分耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, you should know that the basis, the practice, the origin, and the condition of the aggregates are gain. Why is that? Because of gain, there are the aggregates.

「阿难！是故当知是分因、分习、分本、分缘者，谓此利也。所以者何？缘利故则有分。

"Ānanda, the condition of gain is said to be the condition of seeking gain. You should know that the condition of seeking gain is the condition of gain. Ānanda, if there is no seeking and no one who seeks, and if one is separated from seeking, would there be gain?"

「阿难！缘求有利者，此说缘求有利，当知所谓缘求有利。阿难！若无求，各各无求者，设使离求，当有利耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, you should know that the cause, practice, basis, and condition of gain is seeking. Why is that? Because of seeking, there is gain.

「阿难！是故当知是利因、利习、利本、利缘者，谓此求也。所以者何？缘求故别有利。

"Ānanda, the statement "seeking arises from craving" means that seeking arises from craving. You should know that seeking arises from craving. Ānanda, if there were no craving, and no one had craving, would seeking arise if one were to be separated from craving?"

「阿难！缘爱有求者，此说缘爱有求，当知所谓缘爱有求。阿难！若无爱，各各无爱者，设使离爱，当有求耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, you should know that the cause, source, origin, and condition of seeking is craving. Why is that? Because seeking arises from craving.

「阿难！是故当知是求因、求习、求本、求缘者，谓此爱也。所以者何？缘爱故则有求。

"Ānanda, desire and existence, these two dharmas, are caused by and conditioned by perception. Ānanda, if someone asks: 'Does perception have conditions?' you should answer: 'Perception also has conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions of perception?' you should answer: 'It is conditioned by contact.' You should know that perception is conditioned by contact. Ānanda, if there is no eye contact, and each one has no eye contact, if one is separated from eye contact, can there arise the perception of pleasure, pain, or neither pleasure nor pain conditioned by eye contact?"

「阿难！欲爱及有爱，此二法因觉、缘觉致来。阿难！若有问者：『觉有缘耶？』当如是答：『觉亦有缘。』若有问者：『觉有何缘？』当如是答：『缘更乐也。』当知所谓缘更乐有觉。阿难！若无有眼更乐，各各无眼更乐者，设使离眼更乐，当有缘眼更乐生乐觉、苦觉、不苦不乐觉耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda, if there is no ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind contact, and each one has no mind contact, if one is separated from mind contact, can there arise the perception of pleasure, pain, or neither pleasure nor pain conditioned by mind contact?"

「阿难！若无耳、鼻、舌、身、意更乐，各各无意更乐者。设使离意更乐，当有缘意更乐生乐觉、苦觉、不苦不乐觉耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda, therefore you should know that the cause, condition, origin, and condition of perception is contact. Why is that? Because of contact as a condition, there is perception.

「阿难！是故当知是觉因、觉习、觉本、觉缘者，谓此更乐也。所以者何？缘更乐故则有觉。

"Ānanda, if someone asks: 'Does contact have conditions?' you should answer: 'Contact has conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions of contact?' you should answer: 'It is conditioned by name-and-form.' You should know that contact is conditioned by name-and-form. Ānanda, is there any contact that is not conditioned by the body of names and the objects of names?"

「阿难！若有问者：『更乐有缘耶？』当如是答：『更乐有缘。』若有问者：『更乐有何缘？』当如是答：『缘名色也。』当知所谓缘名色有更乐。阿难！所行、所缘有名身，离此行、离此缘有有对更乐耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda, is there any contact that is not conditioned by the body of forms and the objects of forms?"

「阿难！所行、所缘有色身，离此行、离此缘有增语更乐耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"If one were to be apart from the body of names and the body of forms, would there be any contact that could be designated as contact?"

「设使离名身及色身，当有更乐施设更乐耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

Ānanda! Therefore, one should know that the cause, condition, origin, and basis of contact is this name and form. Why is that? Because of depending on name and form, there is contact.

「阿难！是故当知是更乐因、更乐习、更乐本、更乐缘者，谓此名色也。所以者何？缘名色故则有更乐。

"Ānanda! If someone asks: 'Does name and form have conditions?' you should answer: 'Name and form have conditions.' If someone asks: 'What are the conditions of name and form?' you should answer: 'They are conditioned by consciousness.' One should know that consciousness conditions name and form. Ānanda! If consciousness does not enter the mother's womb, can name and form form this body?"

「阿难！若有问者：『名色有缘耶？』当如是答：『名色有缘。』若有问者：『名色有何缘？』当如是答：『缘识也。』当知所谓缘识有名色。阿难！若识不入母胎者，有名色成此身耶？」

He replied: "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Ānanda! If consciousness enters the womb and then leaves, does name and form meet with the essence?"

「阿难！若识入胎即出者，名色会精耶？」

He replied: "It does not meet."

答曰：「不会。」

"Ānanda! If the consciousness of a young boy or girl is not destroyed at the beginning, does name and form increase and grow?"

「阿难！若幼童男童女、识初断坏不有者，名色转增长耶？」

He replied: "No, it does not."

答曰：「不也。」

Therefore, Ānanda, you should know that the cause, condition, origin, and basis of name and form is this consciousness. Why is that? Because of consciousness, there is name and form.

「阿难！是故当知是名色因、名色习、名色本、名色缘者，谓此识也。所以者何？缘识故则有名色。

"If someone asks, 'Does consciousness have conditions?' you should answer, 'Consciousness also has conditions.' If someone asks, 'What are the conditions of consciousness?' you should answer, 'It has name and form as conditions.' You should know that consciousness has name and form as conditions. Ānanda, if consciousness did not obtain name and form, if consciousness did not establish or rely on name and form, would consciousness have birth, aging, sickness, death, and suffering?"

「阿难！若有问者：『识有缘耶？』当如是答：『识亦有缘。』若有问者：『识有何缘？』当如是答：『缘名色也。』当知所谓缘名色有识。阿难！若识不得名色，若识不立、不倚名色者，识宁有生、有老、有病、有死、有苦耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「无也。」

"Therefore, Ānanda, you should know that the cause, source, origin, and condition of consciousness is name and form. Why is that? Because of having name and form as conditions, there is consciousness. Ānanda, this is called having name and form as conditions for consciousness, and having consciousness as conditions for name and form. This is the increase of language, the increase of language transmission, the transmission of transmission, and the establishment of existence, namely, consciousness and name and form together. Ānanda, how is there one view of the existence of a soul?"

"Ānanda! The third abode of consciousness is that sentient beings with form have one body and various perceptions, such as the Heaven of Luminous Light. If a bhikṣu knows that abode of consciousness, knows the practice of that abode of consciousness, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode of consciousness and cling to it?"

「阿难！第三识住者，有色众生一身、若干想，谓晃昱天。若有比丘知彼识住、知识住习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼识住，计著住彼识住耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Ānanda! The fourth abode of consciousness is that sentient beings with form have one body and one perception, such as the Heaven of Universal Purity. If a bhikṣu knows that abode of consciousness, knows the practice of that abode of consciousness, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode of consciousness and cling to it?"

「阿难！第四识住者，有色众生一身、一想，谓遍净天。若有比丘知彼识住、知识住习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼识住，计著住彼识住耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Ānanda! The fifth abode of consciousness is that formless sentient beings transcend all perceptions of form, eliminate perceptions of resistance, do not think of various perceptions, and abide in the accomplishment of the limitless space of the limitless space heaven. If a bhikṣu knows that abode of consciousness, knows the practice of that abode of consciousness, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode of consciousness and cling to it?"

「阿难！第五识住者，无色众生度一切色想，灭有对想，不念若干想，无量空处，是空处成就游，谓无量空处天。若有比丘知彼识住、知识住习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼识住，计著住彼识住耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Ānanda! The sixth abode of consciousness is that formless sentient beings transcend the limitless space and abide in the accomplishment of the limitless consciousness of the limitless consciousness heaven. If a bhikṣu knows that abode of consciousness, knows the practice of that abode of consciousness, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode of consciousness and cling to it?"

「阿难！第六识住者，无色众生度一切无量空处，无量识处，是识处成就游，谓无量识处天。若有比丘知彼识住、知识住习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼识住，计著住彼识住耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Ānanda! The seventh abode of consciousness is that formless sentient beings transcend the limitless consciousness and abide in the accomplishment of the limitless nothingness of the nothingness heaven. If a bhikṣu knows that abode of consciousness, knows the practice of that abode of consciousness, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode of consciousness and cling to it?"

「阿难！第七识住者，无色众生度一切无量识处，无所有处，是无所有处成就游，谓无所有处天。若有比丘知彼识住、知识住习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼识住，计著住彼识住耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Ānanda! The first abode is that sentient beings with form have no thought or perception, namely the non-thought heaven. If a bhikṣu knows that abode, knows the practice of that abode, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode and cling to it?"

「阿难！第一处者，有色众生无想无觉，谓无想天。若有比丘知彼处、知彼处习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼处，计著住彼处耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

"Ānanda! The second abode is that formless sentient beings who have transcended the abode of nothing at all, the abode of neither perception nor non-perception, and who abide in the accomplishment of the abode of neither perception nor non-perception, namely the gods of the abode of neither perception nor non-perception. If a bhikṣu knows that abode, knows the practice of that abode, knows its cessation, knows its taste, knows its suffering, and knows its true nature, can that bhikṣu delight in that abode and cling to it?"

「阿难！第二处者，无色众生度一切无所有处，非有想非无想处，是非有想非无想处成就游，谓非有想非无想处天。若有比丘知彼处、知彼处习，知灭、知味、知患、知出要如真。阿难！此比丘宁可乐彼处，计著住彼处耶？」

He replied, "No."

答曰：「不也。」

Ānanda! If a bhikṣu truly knows those seven abodes of consciousness and two abodes, and his mind is not attached, and he attains liberation, then that bhikṣu is called an arhat, a liberated one through wisdom.

「阿难！若有比丘彼七识住及二处知如真，心不染著，得解脱者，是谓比丘阿罗诃，名慧解脱。

Furthermore, Ānanda, there are eight liberations. What are the eight? Observing forms internally, one sees forms externally. This is called the first liberation. Furthermore, internally without form-perception, one sees forms externally. This is called the second liberation. Furthermore, one dwells in the attainment of the purification liberation. This is called the third liberation. Furthermore, transcending all perceptions of form, putting an end to perceptions of multiplicity, not paying attention to perceptions of form, one dwells in the sphere of infinite space, having attained the sphere of infinite space. This is called the fourth liberation. Furthermore, transcending the sphere of infinite space, one dwells in the sphere of infinite consciousness, having attained the sphere of infinite consciousness. This is called the fifth liberation. Furthermore, transcending the sphere of infinite consciousness, one dwells in the sphere of nothingness, having attained the sphere of nothingness. This is called the sixth liberation. Furthermore, transcending the sphere of nothingness, one dwells in the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, having attained the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. This is called the seventh liberation. Furthermore, transcending the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, one dwells in the attainment of the cessation of perception and feeling, having attained the cessation of perception and feeling. This is called the eighth liberation. Ānanda, if a bhikṣu truly knows those seven abodes of consciousness and two abodes, his mind is not attached, and he attains liberation, and also attains these eight liberations, dwelling in the attainment of them in forward and reverse order, and also attains the knowledge of the exhaustion of the taints through wisdom, then that bhikṣu is called an arhat, a liberated one through both wisdom and concentration.

「复次，阿难！有八解脱。云何为八？色观色，是谓第一解脱。复次，内无色想外观色，是谓第二解脱。复次，净解脱身作证成就游，是谓第三解脱。复次，度一切色想，灭有对想，不念若干想，无量空处，是无量空处成就游，是谓第四解脱。复次，度一切无量空处，无量识处，是无量识处成就游，是谓第五解脱。复次，度一切无量识处，无所有处，是无所有处成就游，是谓第六解脱。复次，度一切无所有处，非有想非无想处，是非有想非无想处成就游，是谓第七解脱。复次，度一切非有想非无想处，想知灭解脱身作证成就游，及慧观诸漏尽知，是谓第八解脱。阿难！若有比丘彼七识住及二处知如真，心不染著，得解脱，及此八解脱，顺逆身作证成就游，亦慧观诸漏尽者，是谓比丘阿罗诃，名俱解脱。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Venerable Ānanda and the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said, and they were delighted and carried it out.

佛说如是。尊者阿难及诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
