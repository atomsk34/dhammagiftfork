MĀ 98

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 9 因品

98. （九八）念处经

Parallel to: DN 22 / MN 10 / MN 119 (~) / MĀ 31 (~) / MĀ 81 (~) / EĀ 12.1

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Kuru, staying at the city of Kumbhahattha.

一时，佛游拘楼瘦，在劒磨瑟昙拘楼都邑。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the monks, saying, "There is one path that purifies beings, liberates them from sorrow and fear, extinguishes suffering and distress, cuts off weeping and lamentation, and leads to the attainment of the true Dharma. It is called the four foundations of mindfulness. If there were any past Tathāgatas, Arhats, Perfectly Enlightened Ones, they all eliminated the five hindrances, mental defilements, and weakness of wisdom, and established their minds in the right abiding of the four foundations of mindfulness, cultivating the seven factors of awakening, and attained the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment of awakening. If there will be any future Tathāgatas, Arhats, Perfectly Enlightened Ones, they will all eliminate the five hindrances, mental defilements, and weakness of wisdom, and establish their minds in the right abiding of the four foundations of mindfulness, cultivating the seven factors of awakening, and attain the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment of awakening. As for myself, the present Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, I have also eliminated the five hindrances, mental defilements, and weakness of wisdom, established my mind in the right abiding of the four foundations of mindfulness, cultivated the seven factors of awakening, and attained the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment of awakening.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有一道净众生，度忧畏，灭苦恼，断啼哭，得正法，谓四念处。若有过去诸如来、无所著、等正觉，悉断五盖、心秽、慧羸，立心正住于四念处，修七觉支，得觉无上正尽之觉。若有未来诸如来、无所著、等正觉，悉断五盖、心秽、慧羸，立心正住于四念处，修七觉支，得觉无上正尽之觉。我今现在如来、无所著、等正觉，我亦断五盖、心秽、慧羸，立心正住于四念处，修七觉支，得觉无上正尽之觉。

What are the four? Observing the body as the body foundation of mindfulness, observing feelings as the feelings foundation of mindfulness, observing the mind as the mind foundation of mindfulness, observing dharmas as the dharmas foundation of mindfulness. How does one observe the body as the body foundation of mindfulness? A monk, when walking, knows he is walking; when standing, he knows he is standing; when sitting, he knows he is sitting; when lying down, he knows he is lying down; when sleeping, he knows he is sleeping; when awake, he knows he is awake; when awake or sleeping, he knows he is awake or sleeping. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body. Furthermore, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, when entering or leaving, knows it well; when bending or stretching, lowering or raising, deportment, or attire, he knows it well; when wearing the saṃghāṭī robe and other robes, and carrying the alms bowl, he knows it well; when walking, standing, sitting, lying down, sleeping, waking, speaking, or being silent, he knows it well. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「云何为四？观身如身念处，如是观觉、心、法如法念处。云何观身如身念处？比丘者，行则知行，住则知住，坐则知坐，卧则知卧，眠则知眠，寤则知寤，眠寤则知眠寤。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，正知出入，善观分别，屈伸低昂，仪容庠序，善著僧伽梨及诸衣钵，行住坐卧，眠寤语默皆正知之。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, when evil and unwholesome thoughts arise, eliminates them with wholesome thoughts, just as a carpenter or carpenter's apprentice uses a string to mark the wood and then cuts it straight with a sharp axe. Similarly, a monk, when evil and unwholesome thoughts arise, eliminates them with wholesome thoughts. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，生恶不善念，以善法念治断灭止，犹木工师、木工弟子，彼持墨绳，用拼于木，则以利斧斫治令直。如是比丘生恶不善念，以善法念治断灭止。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，齿齿相著，舌逼上腭，以心治心，治断灭止。犹二力士捉一羸人，处处旋捉] cf.［丽】，自在打锻。如是比丘齿齿相著，舌逼上腭，以心治心，治断灭止。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, when inhaling, knows he is inhaling; when exhaling, knows he is exhaling; when inhaling long, knows he is inhaling long; when exhaling long, knows he is exhaling long; when inhaling short, knows he is inhaling short; when exhaling short, knows he is exhaling short; trains in inhaling while knowing the whole body, trains in exhaling while knowing the whole body, trains in stopping bodily activities while inhaling, trains in stopping verbal activities while exhaling. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，念入息即知念入息，念出息即知念出息，入息长即知入息长，出息长即知出息长，入息短即知入息短，出息短即知出息短；学一切身息入，学一切身息出，学止身行息入，学止口行息出。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, with the joy and happiness born of separation, drenches, moistens, and pervades this body, with the joy and happiness born of separation nowhere unpervaded. Just as a skilled bath attendant or bath attendant's apprentice takes a ball of bath powder, mixes it with water to form a paste, and drenches, moistens, and pervades it, with the paste nowhere unpervaded. Similarly, a monk, with the joy and happiness born of separation, drenches, moistens, and pervades this body, with the joy and happiness born of separation nowhere unpervaded. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，离生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，离生喜乐无处不遍。犹工浴人器盛澡豆，水和成抟，水渍润泽，普遍充满无处不周。如是比丘离生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，离生喜乐无处不遍。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有] cf.［丽】达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, with the joy and happiness born of concentration, drenches, moistens, and pervades this body, with the joy and happiness born of concentration nowhere unpervaded. Just as a mountain spring, clear and unpolluted, fills and overflows, with water coming from the four directions having no chance to enter, but from the bottom of the spring, water wells up and overflows outside, drenching and moistening the mountain, pervading and filling it everywhere without exception. Similarly, a monk, with the joy and happiness born of concentration, drenches, moistens, and pervades this body, with the joy and happiness born of concentration nowhere unpervaded. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，定生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，定生喜乐无处不遍。犹如山泉，清净不浊，充满流溢，四方水来，无缘得入，即彼泉底，水自涌出，流溢于外，渍山润泽，普遍充满无处不周。如是比丘定生喜乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，定生喜乐无处不遍。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, with the joy and happiness born of the absence of joy, drenches, moistens, and pervades this body, with the joy and happiness born of the absence of joy nowhere unpervaded. Just as blue, red, and white lotuses, born in water and growing in water, with their roots, stems, flowers, and leaves all drenched, moistened, and pervading, with nowhere unpervaded. Similarly, a monk, with the joy and happiness born of the absence of joy, drenches, moistens, and pervades this body, with the joy and happiness born of the absence of joy nowhere unpervaded. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，无喜生乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，无喜生乐无处不遍。犹青莲华，红、赤、白莲，水生水长，在于水底，彼根茎华叶悉渍润泽，普遍充满无处不周。如是比丘无喜生乐，渍身润泽，普遍充满于此身中，无喜生乐无处不遍。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, with a pure mind, pervades, fills, and accomplishes this body, with a pure mind nowhere unpervaded. Just as a person wearing a seven- or eight-cubit robe from head to foot has nowhere on their body that is not covered. Similarly, a monk, with a pure mind, has nowhere in this body that is not pervaded. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，于此身中，以清净心意解遍满成就游，于此身中，以清净心无处不遍。犹有一人，被七肘衣或八肘衣，从头至足，于其身体无处不覆。如是比丘于此身中，以清净心无处不遍。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, with the thought of mindfulness and light, well receives, well holds, well remembers what is to be remembered, as before so after, as after so before, as day so night, as night so day, as below so above, as above so below. Thus, without delusion, the mind is not bound, cultivating the mind of light, the mind is never obscured by darkness. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，念光明想，善受善持，善忆所念，如前后亦然，如后前亦然，如昼夜亦然，如夜昼亦然，如下上亦然，如上下亦然。如是不颠倒，心无有缠，修光明心，心终不为暗之所覆。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, well receiving the characteristics of observation, well remembering what is to be remembered, is like a person sitting observing a lying person, or lying observing a sitting person. Thus, a monk well receives the characteristics of observation, well remembers what is to be remembered. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，善受观相，善忆所念，犹如有人，坐观卧人，卧观坐人。如是比丘善受观相，善忆所念。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, according to the body's dwelling, according to its good or bad, from head to foot, sees various impurities filling it. In this body of mine there are hair, body hair, nails, teeth, coarse and fine skin, flesh, tendons, bones, heart, kidneys, liver, lungs, large intestine, small intestine, spleen, stomach, feces, brain and brain roots, tears, sweat, snot, saliva, pus, blood, fat, marrow, saliva, bile, and urine. It is like a vessel holding various seeds, which a person with eyes sees clearly, namely rice, millet seeds, rape seeds, and mustard seeds. Thus, a monk, according to the body's dwelling, according to its good or bad, from head to foot, sees various impurities filling it. In this body of mine there are hair, body hair, nails, teeth, coarse and fine skin, flesh, tendons, bones, heart, kidneys, liver, lungs, large intestine, small intestine, spleen, stomach, feces, brain and brain roots, tears, sweat, snot, saliva, pus, blood, fat, marrow, saliva, bile, and urine. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，此身随住，随其好恶，从头至足，观见种种不净充满，我此身中有发、髦、爪、齿、麁细薄肤、皮、肉、筋、骨、心、肾、肝、肺、大肠、小肠、脾、胃、抟粪、脑及脑根、泪、汗、涕、唾、脓、血、肪、髓、涎、胆、小便。犹如器盛若干种子，有目之士，悉见分明，谓稻、粟种、蔓菁、芥子。如是比丘此身随住，随其好恶，从头至足，观见种种不净充满，我此身中有发、髦、爪、齿、麤细薄肤、皮、肉、筋、骨、心、肾、肝、肺、大肠、小肠、脾、胃、抟粪、脑及脑根、泪、汗、涕、唾、脓、血、肪、髓、涎、胆、小便。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, observing the body's elements, sees in this body the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. It is like a butcher killing a cow, spreading its skin on the ground and cutting it into six pieces. Thus, a monk observes the body's elements, seeing in this body the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，观身诸界，我此身中有地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。犹如屠儿杀牛，剥皮布地于上，分作六段。如是比丘观身诸界，我此身中，地界、水界、火界、风界、空界、识界。如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, observing a dead body, whether one, two, up to six or seven days old, pecked by crows and kites, eaten by jackals and wolves, burned by fire, or buried in the ground, all rotten and decayed, compares it to himself, thinking: 'Now this body of mine is also like this, sharing in this nature, and will never escape.' Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，观彼死尸，或一、二日，至六、七日，乌鵄所啄，犲狼所食，火烧埋地，悉腐烂坏，见已自比：『今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。』如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, observing the body's elements, sees in this body the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. It is like a butcher killing a cow, spreading its skin on the ground and cutting it into six pieces. Thus, a monk observes the body's elements, seeing in this body the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，如本见息道骸骨青色，烂腐食半，骨璅在地，见已自比：『今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。』如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，如本见息道离皮肉血，唯筋相连，见已自比：『今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。』如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。

Again, a monk observes the body as the body. A monk, observing the body's elements, sees in this body the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. It is like a butcher killing a cow, spreading its skin on the ground and cutting it into six pieces. Thus, a monk observes the body's elements, seeing in this body the earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. Thus, a monk observes the internal body as the body, observes the external body as the body, establishes mindfulness in the body, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the body as the body.

「复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，如本见息道骨节解散，散在诸方，足骨、䏝骨、髀骨、髋骨、脊骨、肩骨、颈骨、髑髅骨，各在异处，见已自比：『今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。』如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。复次，比丘观身如身，比丘者，如本见息道骨白如螺，青犹鸽色，赤若血涂，腐坏碎粖，见已自比：『今我此身亦复如是，俱有此法，终不得离。』如是比丘观内身如身，观外身如身，立念在身，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观身如身。若比丘、比丘尼，如是少少观身如身者，是谓观身如身念处。

What is observing feelings as feelings? A monk, when experiencing a pleasant feeling, knows he is experiencing a pleasant feeling. When experiencing a painful feeling, he knows he is experiencing a painful feeling. When experiencing a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, he knows he is experiencing a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. He knows a pleasant body, a painful body, and a neither-painful-nor-pleasant body. A pleasant mind, a painful mind, and a neither-painful-nor-pleasant mind. A pleasant feeling, a painful feeling, and a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. A pleasant feeling without feeling, a painful feeling without feeling, and a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling without feeling. A pleasant desire, a painful desire, and a neither-painful-nor-pleasant desire. A pleasant desire without desire, a painful desire without desire, and a neither-painful-nor-pleasant desire without desire. When experiencing a neither-painful-nor-pleasant desire without desire, he knows he is experiencing a neither-painful-nor-pleasant desire without desire. Thus, a monk observes internal feelings as feelings, observes external feelings as feelings, establishes mindfulness in feelings, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing feelings as feelings.

「云何观觉如觉念处？比丘者，觉乐觉时，便知觉乐觉。觉苦觉时，便知觉苦觉。觉不苦不乐觉时，便知觉不苦不乐觉。觉乐身、苦身、不苦不乐身；乐心、苦心、不苦不乐心；乐食、苦食、不苦不乐食；乐无食、苦无食、不苦不乐无食；乐欲、苦欲、不苦不乐欲。乐无欲、苦无欲觉、不苦不乐无欲觉时，便知觉不苦不乐无欲觉。如是比丘观内觉如觉，观外觉如觉，立念在觉，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观觉如觉。若比丘、比丘尼如是少少观觉如觉者，是谓观觉如觉念处。

What is observing the mind as the mind? A monk, with a mind of desire, knows he has a mind of desire. With a mind without desire, he knows he has a mind without desire. With a mind of anger, without anger, with a mind of delusion, without delusion, with a defiled mind, without defilement, with a concentrated mind, with a distracted mind, with a low mind, with an elevated mind, with a small mind, with a great mind, with a mind of cultivation, with a mind of non-cultivation, with a mind of concentration, with a mind of non-concentration, with an unliberated mind, he knows he has an unliberated mind. With a liberated mind, he knows he has a liberated mind. Thus, a monk observes the internal mind as the mind, observes the external mind as the mind, establishes mindfulness in the mind, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing the mind as the mind.

「云何观心如心念处？比丘者，有欲心知有欲心如真，无欲心知无欲心如真，有恚，无恚，有痴，无痴，有秽污，无秽污。有合，有散，有下，有高，有小，有大，修，不修，定，不定，有不解脱心知不解脱心如真，有解脱心知解脱心如真。如是比丘观内心如心，观外心如心，立念在心，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观心如心。若有比丘、比丘尼如是少少观心如心者，是谓观心如心念处。

What is observing phenomena as phenomena? A monk, with the eye as condition, there arises internal binding. He knows that internally there is binding. He knows that internally there is no binding. He knows that internal binding has not yet arisen but will arise. He knows that internal binding has arisen and will cease without further arising. Thus, with the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind as conditions, there arises internal binding. He knows that internally there is binding. He knows that internally there is no binding. He knows that internal binding has not yet arisen but will arise. He knows that internal binding has arisen and will cease without further arising. Thus, a monk observes internal phenomena as phenomena, observes external phenomena as phenomena, establishes mindfulness in phenomena, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing phenomena as phenomena.

「云何观法如法念处？眼缘色生内结，比丘者，内实有结知内有结如真，内实无结知内无结如真，若未生内结而生者知如真，若已生内结灭不复生者知如真；如是耳、鼻、舌、身，意缘法生内结，比丘者，内实有结知内有结如真，内实无结知内无结如真，若未生内结而生者知如真，若已生内结灭不复生者知如真。如是比丘观内法如法，观外法如法，立念在法，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观法如法，谓内六处。

Furthermore, a monk observes phenomena as phenomena. A monk, with internal desire, knows he has internal desire. With internal non-desire, he knows he has internal non-desire. He knows that internal desire has not yet arisen but will arise. He knows that internal desire has arisen and will cease without further arising. Thus, with internal anger, sloth, restlessness, and doubt, he knows he has internal doubt. He knows he has internal non-doubt. He knows that internal doubt has not yet arisen but will arise. He knows that internal doubt has arisen and will cease without further arising. Thus, a monk observes internal phenomena as phenomena, observes external phenomena as phenomena, establishes mindfulness in phenomena, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing phenomena as phenomena.

「复次，比丘观法如法。比丘者，内实有欲知有欲如真，内实无欲知无欲如真，若未生欲而生者知如真，若已生欲灭不复生者知如真，如是瞋恚、睡眠、调悔。内实有疑知有疑如真，内实无疑知无疑如真，若未生疑而生者知如真，若已生疑灭不复生者知如真。如是比丘观内法如法，观外法如法，立念在法，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观法如法，谓五盖也。

Furthermore, a monk observes phenomena as phenomena. A monk, with internal mindfulness as a factor of awakening, knows he has internal mindfulness as a factor of awakening. With internal non-mindfulness as a factor of awakening, he knows he has internal non-mindfulness as a factor of awakening. He knows that internal mindfulness as a factor of awakening has not yet arisen but will arise. He knows that internal mindfulness as a factor of awakening has arisen and abides without forgetting, not declining, but cultivating and increasing it. Thus, with internal investigation of phenomena, energy, joy, tranquility, and concentration as factors of awakening, he knows he has internal concentration as a factor of awakening. He knows he has internal non-concentration as a factor of awakening. He knows that internal concentration as a factor of awakening has not yet arisen but will arise. He knows that internal concentration as a factor of awakening has arisen and abides without forgetting, not declining, but cultivating and increasing it. Thus, a monk observes internal phenomena as phenomena, observes external phenomena as phenomena, establishes mindfulness in phenomena, with knowledge, vision, clarity, and insight. This is called a monk observing phenomena as phenomena.

「复次，比丘观法如法。比丘者，内实有念觉支知有念觉支如真，内实无念觉支知无念觉支如真，若未生念觉支而生者知如真，若已生念觉支便住不忘而不衰退，转修增广者知如真，如是法、精进、喜、息、定。比丘者，内实有舍觉支知有舍觉支如真，内实无舍觉支知无舍觉支如真，若未生舍觉支而生者知如真，若已生舍觉支便住不忘而不衰退，转修增广者知如真。如是比丘观内法如法，观外法如法，立念在法，有知有见，有明有达，是谓比丘观法如法，谓七觉支。若有比丘、比丘尼如是少少观法如法者，是谓观法如法念处。

If a monk or nun establishes their mind in the four foundations of mindfulness for seven years, they will certainly attain two fruits: either final knowledge in this life or the state of a non-returner. Setting aside seven years, six, five, four, three, two, or one year. If a monk or nun establishes their mind in the four foundations of mindfulness for seven months, they will certainly attain two fruits: either final knowledge in this life or the state of a non-returner. Setting aside seven months, six, five, four, three, two, or one month. If a monk or nun establishes their mind in the four foundations of mindfulness for seven days and nights, they will certainly attain two fruits: either final knowledge in this life or the state of a non-returner. Setting aside seven days and nights, six, five, four, three, two, or one day and night. If a monk or nun establishes their mind in the four foundations of mindfulness for a short while, they will certainly progress in the morning or evening.

「若有比丘、比丘尼七年立心正住四念处者，彼必得二果，或现法得究竟智，或有余得阿那含，置七年，六五四三二一年。若有比丘、比丘尼七月立心正住四念处者，彼必得二果，或现法得究竟智，或有余得阿那含，置七月，六五四三二一月。若有比丘、比丘尼七日七夜立心正住四念处者，彼必得二果，或现法得究竟智，或有余得阿那含，置七日七夜，六五四三二，置一日一夜。若有比丘、比丘尼少少须臾顷立心正住四念处者，彼朝行如是，暮必得升进，暮行如是，朝必得升进。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those monks, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
