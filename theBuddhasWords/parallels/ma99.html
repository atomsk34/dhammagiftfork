MĀ 99

Madhyamāgama 中阿含经

Chapter 9 因品

99. （九九）苦阴经

Parallel to: MN 13 / EĀ 21.9

I have heard thus:

我闻如是：

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, staying at the Jeta Grove Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

一时，佛游舍卫国，在胜林给孤独园。

At that time, after the midday meal, the bhikṣus gathered in the Dharma hall to discuss various matters. Then, in the middle of the day, many non-Buddhist practitioners leisurely approached the bhikṣus, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. They said to the bhikṣus, "Virtuous ones! The Śramaṇa Gautama teaches the knowledge and elimination of desire, the knowledge and elimination of form, and the knowledge and elimination of perception. We also teach the knowledge and elimination of desire, the knowledge and elimination of form, and the knowledge and elimination of perception. What is the superiority and difference between the two knowledges and eliminations of the Śramaṇa Gautama and us?"

尔时，诸比丘于中食后，少有所为，集坐讲堂。于是众多异学，中后仿佯往诣诸比丘所，共相问讯，却坐一面，语诸比丘：「诸贤！沙门瞿昙施设知断欲，施设知断色，施设知断觉。诸贤！我等亦施设知断欲，施设知断色，施设知断觉。沙门瞿昙及我等此二知二断，为有何胜？有何差别？」

Then, the bhikṣus heard what the many non-Buddhist practitioners said. They neither agreed nor disagreed, but rose silently and left, thinking, "We shall learn about this from the World-Honored One." They then went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, sat to one side, and told the Buddha everything they had discussed with the many non-Buddhist practitioners.

于是，诸比丘闻彼众多异学所说，不是亦不非，默然起去，并作是念：「如此所说，我等当从世尊得知。」便诣佛所，稽首作礼，却坐一面，谓与众多异学所可共论，尽向佛说。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "You should have asked the many non-Buddhist practitioners like this: 'Virtuous ones! What is the taste of desire? What are the afflictions of desire? What is the way out of desire? What is the taste of form? What are the afflictions of form? What is the way out of form? What is the taste of perception? What are the afflictions of perception? What is the way out of perception?' Bhikṣus! If you had asked like this, they would have become even more angry and contentious upon hearing it, and would have certainly risen from their seats in silence and left. Why is that? I do not see in this world, among the gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, or any other assembly, anyone who can understand and explain this meaning. Only the Tathāgata, the Tathāgata's disciples, or those who have heard it from them can do so."

彼时，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等即时应如是问众多异学：『诸贤！云何欲味？云何欲患？云何欲出要？云何色味？云何色患？云何色出要？云何觉味？云何觉患？云何觉出要？』诸比丘！若汝等作如是问者，彼等闻已，便更互相难说外余事，瞋诤转增，必从座起，默然而退。所以者何？我不见此世，天及魔、梵、沙门、梵志、一切余众，能知此义而发遣者，唯有如来、如来弟子或从此闻。」

The Buddha said, "What is the taste of desire? It means the pleasure and joy arising from the five sense pleasures. This is the ultimate taste of desire, nothing surpasses it. But the afflictions are very many.

佛言：「云何欲味？谓因五欲功德，生乐生喜，极是欲味，无复过是，所患甚多。

What are the afflictions of desire? Noble sons, they live by their skills, such as farming, commerce, writing, mathematics, counting, carving seals, composing, calligraphy, studying scriptures, being brave generals, or serving the king. When cold, they are cold; when hot, they are hot. They are hungry, thirsty, tired, bitten by mosquitoes and flies. They work like this to seek wealth. If those noble sons do not obtain wealth through such means, they will give rise to suffering, sorrow, distress, and delusion, saying, 'It was all in vain, the effort was fruitless.' If those noble sons obtain wealth through such means, they will cherish, guard, and hide it. Why is that? 'I do not want this wealth to be taken by the king, robbed by thieves, burned by fire, rot, lost, or not yield any profit. Or if I engage in various activities, they may not succeed.' If they guard and hide it like this, and it is taken by the king, robbed by thieves, burned by fire, rot, or lost, they will give rise to suffering, sorrow, distress, and delusion, saying, 'What was long cherished is lost.' This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「云何欲患？族姓子者，随其伎术以自存活，或作田业、或行治生、或以学书、或明算术、或知工数、或巧刻印、或作文章、或造手笔、或晓经书、或作勇将、或奉事王。彼寒时则寒，热时则热，饥渴、疲劳、蚊虻所蜇，作如是业，求图钱财。彼族姓子如是方便，作如是行，作如是求，若不得钱财者，便生忧苦、愁戚、懊恼，心则生痴，作如是说：『唐作唐苦，所求无果。』彼族姓子如是方便作如是行，作如是求，若得钱财者，彼便爱惜，守护密藏。所以者何？『我此财物，莫令王夺、贼劫、火烧、腐坏、亡失，出财无利，或作诸业，而不成就。』彼作如是守护密藏，若有王夺、贼劫、火烧、腐坏、亡失，便生忧苦、愁戚、懊恼，心则生痴，作如是说：『若有长夜所可爱念者，彼则亡失。』是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, mothers quarrel with their children, children quarrel with their mothers, fathers with sons, brothers with brothers, sisters with sisters, and relatives with relatives. After quarreling like this, mothers speak ill of their children, children speak ill of their mothers, fathers speak ill of their sons, brothers speak ill of their brothers, sisters speak ill of their sisters, and relatives speak ill of each other. How much more so with others? This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，母共子诤，子共母诤，父子、兄弟、姉妹、亲族展转共诤。彼既如是共鬪诤已，母说子恶，子说母恶，父子、兄弟、姉妹、亲族更相说恶，况复他人？是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, kings quarrel with other kings, brahmins quarrel with other brahmins, householders quarrel with other householders, commoners quarrel with other commoners, countries quarrel with other countries. Because of this mutual hatred, they harm each other with various weapons, either by punching, throwing stones, beating with sticks, or cutting with swords. When they fight, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，王王共诤，梵志梵志共诤，居士居士共诤，民民共诤，国国共诤，彼因鬪诤共相憎故，以种种器仗，转相加害，或以拳扠石掷，或以杖打刀斫，彼当鬪时，或死、或怖，受极重苦，是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, they put on armor, wear robes, carry spears and bows and arrows, or hold swords and shields, and enter into battle formations. They fight with elephants, horses, chariots, or infantry. They fight with men or women. When they fight, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，著铠被袍，持矟弓箭，或执刀楯入在军阵，或以象鬪，或马、或车，或以步军，或以男女鬪。彼当鬪时，或死、或怖，受极重苦，是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, they put on armor, wear robes, carry spears and bows and arrows, or hold swords and shields, and go to conquer other countries, attacking cities and breaking through strongholds. They fight with each other, beating drums and blowing horns, shouting loudly, or hitting with hammers, spears, sharp wheels, or arrows, or throwing stones, or using large crossbows, or sprinkling molten copper beads. When they fight, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，著铠被袍，持矟弓箭，或执刀楯往夺他国，攻城破坞，共相格战，打鼓吹角，高声唤呼，或以槌打，或以𫓴戟，或以利轮，或以箭射，或乱下石，或以大弩，或以融铜珠子洒之。彼当鬪时，或死、或怖，受极重苦，是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, they put on armor, wear robes, carry spears and bows and arrows, or hold swords and shields, and enter villages, towns, countries, or cities, digging through walls and opening up hidden treasures, robbing and stealing, cutting off royal roads, or going to other lanes, destroying villages, harming towns, annihilating countries, and breaking through cities. There, they may be caught by the king's men and subjected to various tortures, having their hands cut off, feet cut off, or both hands and feet cut off, ears cut off, noses cut off, or both ears and noses cut off, or being cut and sliced, having their beards and hair pulled out, or being wrapped in cloth and burned, or being wrapped in grass and burned, or being placed in an iron donkey's belly, or being put in an iron pig's mouth, or being placed in an iron tiger's mouth and burned, or being put in an iron cauldron or a copper cauldron and boiled, or being cut into pieces, or being pierced with sharp stakes, or being hooked with iron hooks, or being laid on an iron bed and poured with boiling oil, or being seated on an iron mortar and pounded with an iron pestle, or being bitten by dragons and snakes, or being whipped with whips, or being beaten with sticks, or being struck with clubs, or being impaled alive on a high pole, or having their heads cut off. There, they may die or be terrified, suffering extreme pain. This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，著铠被袍，持矟弓箭，或执刀楯入村、入邑、入国、入城，穿墙发藏，劫夺财物，断截王路，或至他巷，坏村、害邑、灭国、破城。于中或为王人所捉，种种考治，截手、截足或截手足，截耳、截鼻或截耳鼻，或脔脔割，拔须、拔发或拔须发，或著槛中衣裹火烧，或以沙壅草缠火𦶟，或内铁驴腹中，或著铁猪口中，或置铁虎口中烧，或安铜釜中，或著铁釜中煮，或段段截，或利叉刺，或铁钩钩，或卧铁床以沸油浇，或坐铁臼以铁杵捣，或龙蛇蜇，或以鞭鞭，或以杖挝，或以棒打，或生贯高标上，或枭其首。彼在其中，或死或怖，受极重苦，是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, they engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Later, when they become ill and lie down on a bed or sit or lie on the ground, suffering afflicts their bodies, and they experience extreme, unbearable suffering. If they have engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, those actions will cover them at the time of death, like the shadow of a mountain blocking the ground as the sun is about to set. In the same way, if they have engaged in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, those actions will cover them. They will think: 'My past evil actions are covering me. I did not create any meritorious deeds, but only evil ones. If there were a place where people who do evil and violent acts only for the sake of sin, who do not create merit or practice goodness, have no fear, no refuge, and no place to go, I would definitely be born there.' From this, they will have regret. Those who regret will die unwholesomely and pass away without merit. This is the present suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼于后时，疾病著床，或坐、卧地，以苦逼身，] cf.［丽】受极重，苦不可爱乐。彼若有身恶行，口、意恶行，彼临终时在前覆障，犹日将没大山岗侧，影障覆地。如是，彼若有身恶行，口、意恶行，在前覆障，彼作是念：『我本恶行，在前覆我，我本不作福业，多作恶业。若使有人作恶凶暴唯为罪，不作福、不行善，无所畏、无所依、无所归，随生处者，我必生彼。』从是有悔，悔者不善死，无福命终，是谓现法苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本。

Furthermore, because of desire, for the sake of desire, and based on desire, they engage in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Because of those evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, when their bodies disintegrate and their lives end, they will definitely go to a bad place and be born in hell. This is the future suffering aggregate, caused by desire, conditioned by desire, and based on desire. This is called the danger of desire.

「复次，众生因欲缘欲，以欲为本故，行身恶行，行口、意恶行，彼因身、口、意恶行故，因此、缘此，身坏命终，必至恶处，生地狱中，是谓后世苦阴，因欲缘欲，以欲为本，是谓欲患。

What is the way out of desire? It is to cut off desire, to abandon desire, to extinguish desire, to completely eliminate desire, to cross over desire, and to escape from desire. This is called the way out of desire. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not know the taste of desire, the danger of desire, and the way out of desire as they truly are, they will not be able to eliminate their own desires, let alone be able to eliminate the desires of others. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who know the taste of desire, the danger of desire, and the way out of desire as they truly are, they will be able to eliminate their own desires and also be able to eliminate the desires of others.

「云何欲出要？若断除欲，舍离于欲，灭欲欲尽，度欲出要，是谓欲出要。若有沙门、梵志，欲味、欲患、欲出要不知如真者，彼终不能自断其欲，况复能断于他欲耶？若有沙门、梵志，欲味、欲患、欲出要知如真者，彼既自能除，亦能断他欲。

What is the taste of form? When a kṣatriya, brāhmaṇa, householder, or artisan woman is fourteen or fifteen years old, her beauty is at its peak. If, because of that beauty, one gives rise to pleasure and joy, this is the ultimate taste of form, nothing surpasses it. But the dangers are very many.

「云何色味？若刹利女、梵志、居士、工师女，年十四五，彼于尔时，美色最妙。若因彼美色、缘彼美色故，生乐生喜，极是色味无复过是，所患甚多。

"What are the dangers of form? If one sees that beautiful person later on, when they are extremely old, with white hair and falling teeth, hunched back and crooked legs, walking with a cane, their youthful vigor declining, their life nearing its end, their body trembling, and their faculties decaying, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?"

「云何色患？若见彼姝而于后时极大衰老，头白齿落，背偻脚戾，拄杖而行，盛壮日衰，寿命垂尽，身体震动，诸根毁熟，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」

"Yes, it is."

答曰：「如是。」

"Furthermore, if one sees that beautiful person sick, lying in bed, or sitting or lying on the ground, with suffering pressing on their body, experiencing extreme suffering, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?" "Yes, it is."

「复次，若见彼姝疾病著床，或坐、卧地，以苦逼身，受极重苦，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」答曰：「如是。」

"Furthermore, if one sees that beautiful person dead, from one to six or seven days, pecked by crows and kites, eaten by jackals and wolves, burned by fire, or buried in the ground, completely rotten and decayed, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?" "Yes, it is."

「复次，若见彼姝死，或一二日至六七日，乌鵄所啄，犲狼所食，火烧埋地，悉烂腐坏，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」

"Yes, it is."

答曰：「如是。」

"Furthermore, if one sees that beautiful person's bones, blue in color, rotten and decayed, half of the bones and joints lying on the ground, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?" "Yes, it is."

「复次，若见彼姝息道，骸骨青色烂腐，食半骨璅在地，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」

"Yes, it is."

答曰：「如是。」

"Furthermore, if one sees that beautiful person's bones, separated from skin, flesh, and blood, only connected by tendons, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?" "Yes, it is."

「复次，若见彼姝息道，离皮肉血，唯筋相连，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」

"Yes, it is."

答曰：「如是。」

"Furthermore, if one sees that beautiful person's bones, the joints separated and scattered in all directions, the foot bones, shin bones, thigh bones, hip bones, spine bones, shoulder bones, neck bones, and skull bones each in different places, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?" "Yes, it is."

「复次，若见彼姝息道，骨节解散，散在诸方，足骨、［跳－兆＋专］骨、髀骨、髋骨、脊骨、肩骨、颈骨、髑髅骨各在异处，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」

"Yes, it is."

答曰：「如是。」

"Furthermore, if one sees that beautiful person's bones, white like a conch, blue like a pigeon's color, red like blood smeared, rotten and broken into powder, what do you think? If there was beauty in them before, is it not a danger when it disappears?" "Yes, it is."

「复次，若见彼姝息道，骨白如螺，青犹鸽色，赤若血涂，腐坏碎末，于汝等意云何？若本有美色，彼灭生患耶？」

"Yes, it is."

答曰：「如是。」

"This is called the danger of form."

「是谓色患。」

What is the way out of form? If one abandons form, discards form, extinguishes form, and transcends form, that is called the way out of form. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not truly know the taste, danger, and way out of form, they will not be able to eliminate their own form, let alone be able to eliminate the form of others. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who truly know the taste, danger, and way out of form, they will be able to eliminate their own form and also be able to eliminate the form of others.

「云何色出要？若断除色，舍离于色，灭色色尽，度色出要，是谓色出要。若有沙门、梵志，色味、色患、色出要不知如真者，彼终不能自断其色，况复能断于他色耶？若有沙门、梵志，色味、色患、色出要知如真者，彼既自能除，亦能断他色。

What is the taste of awakening? A bhikṣu, having abandoned desire and evil and unwholesome dharmas, attains and abides in the fourth dhyāna. At that time, he does not think of harming himself, nor does he think of harming others. If he does not think of harming, that is called the taste of the bliss of awakening. Why is that? Because not thinking of harming leads to the attainment of bliss. This is called the taste of awakening.

「云何觉味？比丘者，离欲、离恶不善之法，至得第四禅成就游，彼于尔时不念自害，亦不念害他，若不念害者，是谓觉乐味。所以者何？不念害者，成就是乐，是谓觉味。

What are the dangers of awakening? Awakening is a dharmic state of impermanence, suffering, and cessation. This is called the dangers of awakening.

「云何觉患？觉者是无常法、苦法、灭法，是谓觉患。

"What is the way out of awakening? To cut off awakening, to abandon awakening, to extinguish awakening, to exhaust awakening, to transcend awakening, this is called the way out of awakening. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who do not truly know the taste, dangers, and way out of awakening, they will not be able to cut off their own awakening, let alone be able to cut off the awakening of others. If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who truly know the taste, dangers, and way out of awakening, they will be able to cut it off for themselves and also be able to cut it off for others."

「云何觉出要？若断除觉，舍离于觉，灭觉觉尽，度觉出要，是谓觉出要。若有沙门、梵志，觉味、觉患、觉出要不知如真者，彼终不能自断其觉，况复能断于他觉耶？若有沙门、梵志，觉味、觉患、觉出要知如真者，彼既自能除，亦能断他觉。」

The Buddha spoke thus. Those bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说如是。彼诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
