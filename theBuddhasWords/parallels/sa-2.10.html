SĀ² 2.10

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 10（一〇）

Parallel to: SN 21.10 / SĀ 1071

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, there was an elder bhikṣu who stayed alone in a room, praising living alone. The bhikṣus went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! This elder bhikṣu praises living alone, walking alone, and sitting alone."

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。时，有一比丘名曰长老，独止一房，赞叹独住。时，诸比丘往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐。白佛言：「世尊！此长老比丘赞叹独住、独行、独坐。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "You may call that elder bhikṣu." At that time, a bhikṣu went to him and said to the elder, "The World-Honored One calls you." The elder bhikṣu, having received the instruction, came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side.

佛告比丘：「汝可唤彼长老比丘。」时，一比丘往至其所，语长老言：「世尊唤汝。」长老比丘受教勅已，来诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面立。

The Buddha told the elder, "Is it true that you live alone and praise the practice of living alone?" The elder said to the Buddha, "It is true, World-Honored One." The Buddha again said, "Why do you now enjoy living alone and praise living alone?" The elder said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I truly enter the village alone, leave alone, and sit alone." The Buddha again said, "There is another kind of living alone that surpasses your living alone. What is it? The root of desire is dried up, future desires do not arise, and present desires do not arise. This is called a brāhmaṇa. Without self and mine, one cuts off doubts and bonds, is far removed from the sense-fields, and extinguishes afflictions.

佛告长老：「汝实独住，赞叹独坐行法耶？」长老白佛言：「实尔，世尊。」佛复告言：「汝今云何乐于独住，赞叹独住？」长老白佛言：「世尊！我实独入聚落，独出独坐。」佛复告言：「更有独住，胜汝独住。何等是耶？欲本干竭，来欲不起，现欲不生，是名婆罗门。无我、我所，断于疑结，远离诸入，灭于烦恼。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

I know all the
worlds, and have abandoned
all, ending all attachments
Such supreme Dharma is
called living alone.

「一切世间，　　我悉知之。　　舍弃一切，尽诸爱结。　　如此胜法，　　名为独住。」

Having spoken thus, the Buddha finished. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them respectfully.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
