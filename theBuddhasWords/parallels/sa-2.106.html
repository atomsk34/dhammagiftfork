SĀ² 2.106

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 106（一〇六）

Parallel to: SN 6.7–9 / SĀ 1193 / SĀ³ 3.5

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, the Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes, with great radiance, came to where the Buddha was. At that time, the World-Honored One entered the samadhi of the fire-light. The Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes had this thought: "Now the Tathāgata has entered samadhi. It is not the right time for me to come here." At that time, Devadatta's friend, the bhikṣu Kāśyapa, slandered Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana. The Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes then went to where he was, knocked on Kāśyapa's door, and called out: "Kāśyapa! Kāśyapa! You should give rise to pure faith towards Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana. Those two Venerables have pure and gentle minds, and are fully endowed with the practice of the Brahma-way. If you slander them, you will suffer greatly for a long time." Kāśyapa then asked: "Who are you?" He replied: "I am the Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes." Kāśyapa said: "The Buddha has prophesied that you will attain the state of an anāgāmin, right?" The Brahmā king replied: "Indeed." Kāśyapa said: "An anāgāmin is called one who does not return. How can you return?" The Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes then had this thought: "Such a person is not worthy of speaking with." He then spoke a verse:

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。时，梵主天于其中夜，威光甚明，来至佛所。尔时，世尊入火光三昧。时，梵主天作是心念：「今者如来入于三昧，我来至此，甚为非时。」当尔之时，提婆达多亲友瞿迦梨比丘，谤舍利弗及大目连。此梵主天即诣其所，扣瞿迦梨门唤言：「瞿迦梨！瞿迦梨！汝于舍利弗、目连当生净信，彼二尊者，心净柔软，梵行具足。汝作是谤，后于长夜，受诸衰苦。」瞿迦梨即问之言：「汝为是谁？」答曰：「我是梵主天。」瞿迦梨言：「佛记汝得阿那含耶？」梵主答言：「实尔。」瞿迦梨言：「阿那含名为不还，汝云何还？」梵主天复作是念：「如此等人，不应与语。」而说偈言：

Desiring to fathom the immeasurable Dharma, the wise should not do so. If
one tries to fathom the immeasurable Dharma, one will surely be burned and harmed.

「欲测无量法，　　智者所不应，若测无量法，　　必为所烧害。」

At that time, the Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes, having spoken this verse, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. He told the World-Honored One in detail about the cause and condition of what Kāśyapa had said. The Buddha said to the Brahmā king: "Indeed, indeed. Desiring to fathom the immeasurable Dharma, it can burn ordinary beings." At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke a verse:

时，梵主天说是偈已，即往佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐。以瞿迦梨所说因缘，具白世尊。佛告梵言：「实尔，实尔。欲测无量法，能烧凡夫。」尔时，世尊即说偈言：

The axe of a person's life is in
their mouth, because of their evil speech, they
cut their own body. Those who should be
praised are criticized, and those who should be
criticized are praised. Such evil people will never
see happiness. Kāśyapa falsely slandered the Buddha and
the sages, and Kāśyapa's sin is heavier than
that of a hundred thousand hells. At that
time, Āfutu slandered the sages, because of the
evil of his mouth and mind, he entered this hell.

「夫人生世，　　斧在口中，　　由其恶口，自斩其身。　　应赞者毁，　　应毁者赞，如斯恶人，　　终不见乐。　　迦梨伪谤，于佛贤圣，　　迦梨为重，　　百千地狱。时阿浮陀　　毁谤贤圣，　　口意恶故，入此地狱。」

At that time, the Brahmā king of the heaven of the Pure Abodes, having heard this verse, bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.

时，梵主天闻是偈已，礼佛而退。

The last five sutras in this scroll and the first five sutras in the sixth scroll are not found in the Guo and Song editions but are unique to the Dan edition. The ninth is the "Brahma's Questions Sutra" and the tenth is the "Sutra on Delivering Subhadra". Now, examining the "Brahma's Questions Sutra", it is the same text with a different translation as the beginning of the forty-fourth scroll of the great version of the "Miscellaneous Collection of the Avatamsaka Sutra". The "Sutra on Delivering Subhadra" is the same text with a different translation as the beginning of the sixteenth scroll of the thirty-fifth scroll of the great version. Then, the Guo and Song editions do not have these sutras, which are omitted. Now, following the Dan edition, they are added and divided into two scrolls.

此卷末五经及第六卷初五经，皆国、宋本所无而丹本独有者。第九是《梵问经》，第十是《度须跋经》。今捡《梵问经》者，与彼大本《杂阿含经》第四十四卷之初，同本异译。《度须跋经》与大本第三十五卷十六幅已下，同本异译。则国、宋二本无此经者，脱之耳。今依丹藏加之，分入二卷焉。
