SĀ² 2.123

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 123（一二三）

Parallel to: SN 42.3 / SĀ 908

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, there was a skilled general who was the head of a village. He went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and after exchanging greetings, sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I have heard from the elders of old that when going into battle, one must adorn one's weapons, protect oneself well, be brave and advance straight ahead without cowardice, be able to defeat the enemy, injure and kill living beings, and cause the other armies to all retreat and scatter. After doing this, one will be reborn in the Heaven Adorned with Arrows upon death." At that time, the general asked this question. The Buddha said to him, "Stop! Do not speak. What you are asking about now is very unwholesome." The second and third times, he asked the same question.

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。时，彼城中有善鬪将，为聚落主，往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，问讯已讫，在一面坐。白佛言：「世尊！我于昔者，曾从宿旧耆老边闻：『若欲战时，要当庄严所持器仗，牢自防护，勇猛直进，无有怯弱，能破前敌，伤杀物命，使余军众皆悉退散。作是事已，命终得生箭庄严天。』」彼时鬪将作如是问，佛告之曰：「止！不须说。汝于今者，所问义趣，甚为不善。」第二第三，亦如是问。

The Buddha again said, "You have earnestly asked me three times. If you can accept it, I will tell you. Those who fight must firmly arm themselves, be skilled in battle tactics, and be at the forefront of the formation, advancing bravely. Such a general must surely intend to harm the other armies, thinking, 'How can I bind them, injure them, and destroy them?' Would he not have such thoughts? General, you have committed three kinds of evil deeds towards sentient beings. What are the three kinds of evil deeds? They are body, speech, and mind. If one is reborn in heaven due to such three unwholesome deeds when the body perishes and life ends, it is impossible. General, if you see it this way, it is wrong view. The karma of wrong view will definitely lead to two places, either in hell or falling into the animal realm." At that time, the general, having heard the Buddha's words, wept with tears. The Buddha again said, "This is why I did not speak when asked three times. Now I tell you, why are you weeping?"

佛复告言：「汝已慇懃三问于我，汝若能受，当为汝说。诸有战者，牢自庄严，善知鬪术，最为阵首，勇猛前进。如是战将，岂不作意方便，欲得伤害彼诸军众，作是念言：『云何当系缚于彼、伤害于彼，令其坏尽。』宁可不生如是念耶？战将！汝于众生所起三邪恶业。何等名为三邪恶业？所谓即是身口意也。若以如是三不善业，身坏命终，得生天者，无有是处。战将！汝今若如是见者，即是邪见。邪见之业必生二处，或在地狱，或堕畜生。」尔时，鬪将闻佛语已，悲泣流泪。佛复告曰：「我以是故，三请不说。今为汝说，何故涕泣？」

At that time, the general said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I do not weep because of hearing this teaching; I feel compassion for all those who fight, who are long deluded, childish, and ignorant. They do unwholesome deeds and always do this. In the future, they will suffer greatly. Such evil karma will not actually lead to rebirth in the heavens. If one is reborn in the Heaven Adorned with Arrows due to such karma, it is truly impossible. World-Honored One! From now on, I will never have such wrong views." The Buddha praised him, saying, "Excellent! Excellent! What you have said is truly rare.

时，彼鬪将白佛言：「世尊！我不为闻是说故，而生悲恼；怜愍诸鬪战者，长夜愚暗，𪧀孩无智，所作不善，常为此事，于未来世，当受大苦。如是恶业，而实不得生于天中。若以此业，而生彼箭庄严天者，实无是处。世尊！我从今更不作于如是邪见。」佛即赞言：「善哉！善哉！汝所说者，甚为希有。」

At that time, the general, having heard the Buddha's teaching, bowed his head and left.

尔时，鬪将闻佛所说，顶礼还去。
