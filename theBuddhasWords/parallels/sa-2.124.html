SĀ² 2.124

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 124（一二四）

Parallel to: SN 42.5 / SĀ 909

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, the village headman Suṇā, the horse trainer, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. The Buddha said to Suṇā, the horse trainer, "For how many reasons can a horse be trained?"

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。尔时，善调马师聚落主往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐。佛告调马师曰：「以几因缘，令马得调？」

The horse trainer said, "Gautama! There are three reasons that can make a horse well-trained: first, always gentle; second, always rough; third, both gentle and rough."

马师言：「瞿昙！以三事故，能令马调：一者、一向须濡。二者、一向须麁。三者、亦濡亦麁。」

The Buddha said to him, "If these three things cannot tame it, what else should be done?"

佛语之言：「若斯三事不能调者，复当云何？」

The horse trainer replied, "Beat it until it dies." The horse trainer then said, "Gautama! You are the unsurpassed teacher of taming, when taming a man, how many things do you use to tame?"

马师对曰：「打令命终。」马师即言：「瞿昙！汝为无上调御之师，调丈夫时，以几事调？」

The Buddha said, "I also tame with three things: first, gentle words; second, harsh words; third, neither gentle nor harsh, and then they are tamed. What is meant by being tamed by gentle words alone? It is like the Buddha telling the bhikshus, 'If you cultivate the three wholesome karmas, you will attain the good karmic rewards. This is heaven, this is human.' This is called being tamed by gentle words alone. What is meant by harsh? It is like speaking of the three evil paths, saying that these are the karmic results of the body, speech, and mind creating evil. What is meant by both harsh and gentle? It is saying that the body, speech, and mind have wholesome karmas, which lead to rebirth as humans and gods. This is the karmic result of the wholesome body, speech, and mind. It also says that the body, speech, and mind have unwholesome karmas, which will lead to falling into the three evil paths. This is also the karmic result of the body, speech, and mind. This is called both harsh and gentle, taming sentient beings."

佛言：「我亦以三事调御：一、须濡语。二者、麁语。三者、不濡不麁，而得调伏。云何名为一向须濡？如佛告比丘：『汝若修三业善者，获善果报，此是天，此是人。』是名一向以濡而得调伏。云何名麁？如说三恶道，此是身口意业造恶果报。云何名为麁而濡？说身口意有诸善业，得生人天，此是身口意善所获果报；亦说身口意有诸恶业，当堕三涂，斯亦身口意所获果报。是则名为亦麁亦濡，而调众生。」

The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "If they are not tamed by these three, how should they be tamed?"

马师白佛：「若以此三不调伏者，当云何调？」

The Buddha told him, "If they are not tamed by gentle words, then use harsh words. If they are still not tamed, then severely harm them."

佛告之曰：「与其切言，若不调者，深加毁害。」

The horse trainer said, "Now you, a śramaṇa, always speak of not killing. How can you speak of harming?"

马师对曰：「今汝沙门常说不杀，云何言害？」

The Buddha said, "Horse trainer! It is so! It is so! The Tathāgata truly should not kill, and should not do what should not be done. The Tathāgata World-Honored One uses these three things to tame sentient beings. If they are not tamed, he will never speak to them, teach them, or instruct them." The Buddha told the horse trainer, "What do you think? If the Tathāgata does not instruct, does not speak, and does not instruct, then this is called harming, this is true harming."

佛言：「马师！如是！如是！言如来者，实不应杀，所不应作。如来世尊以此三事用调众生，若不调者，终不与语，亦不教诏，亦不指授。」佛告马师：「于汝意云何？如来若不教授，设不与语，设不指授，如是者则名毁害，是真毁害。」

The horse trainer said, "Indeed, Gautama! If the Tathāgata does not speak to them, does not teach the Dharma, it truly becomes harming, worse than worldly harm." The horse trainer said again, "Gautama! From now on, I will stop harming and will not create evil again."

马师对曰：「实尔！瞿昙。如来若当不与其语，不教授法，实成毁害，甚于世害。」马师复言：「瞿昙！我自今已后，当断毁害，更不造恶。」

The Buddha praised, "Horse trainer! What you say is truly correct."

佛即赞言：「马师！如汝所说，实为真正。」

At that time, the horse trainer, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully bowed his head and departed.

尔时，马师闻佛所说，欢喜顶礼而去。
