SĀ² 2.125

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 125（一二五）

Parallel to: SN 42.1 / SĀ 910

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, a village headman named Evil Nature went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! There are people in the world who do not cultivate anything, who trouble and touch others, and speak words that trouble and touch. Therefore, all people call such a person extremely evil."

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。时，聚落主名曰恶性，往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐。即白佛言：「世尊！如世有人无所修集，恼触于他，作恼触语。是故诸人，咸称其人，名为极恶。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "If there is a person who troubles and harms others, speaks words of trouble and harm, and because of those words, makes others angry and resentful, that is why he is called evil nature. Not cultivating right view, right action, right speech, right livelihood, right mindfulness, right effort, right intention, and right concentration, not cultivating right concentration, troubles and harms others. Because of the trouble and harm, extreme anger and resentment arise. Because of the anger and resentment, words of anger and resentment are spoken. Because of the words of anger, he is called evil nature."

佛告聚落主：「设有一人，恼触于彼，作恼害语，出恼语故，令他瞋忿，以是之故，名为恶性。不修正见、正业、正语、正命、正念、正方便、正志、正定，不修正定故，恼触于彼。以恼触故，极生瞋忿。以瞋忿故，出瞋忿语。出瞋语故，名为恶性。」

At that time, the village headman said, "Rare, Gautama! It is truly as you say. Because of the trouble and harm, it is indeed called evil nature. Because I did not cultivate right view, I was touched and troubled by him. Because of the trouble and harm, I am called evil nature. All the people in the world call me evil nature. From then on, I am called evil nature." The village headman further said, "Gautama! How can one not be touched and troubled?"

时，村主言：「希有瞿昙！实如所言，以恼触故，实名恶性。我以不修正见故，为彼触恼。以恼触故，称我恶性。一切世人咸皆称我，以为恶性，从是已来，名为恶性。」村主复言：「瞿昙！云何得不恼触？」

"Because of not being touched and troubled, one obtains words without trouble and harm. Although one may be touched and troubled by others, one does not trouble others. Although one may be spoken to with words of trouble and harm, one does not speak words of trouble and harm to others. Although one may be troubled by others, one does not give rise to mental trouble. Because of not being troubled, people all call one a patient and good person, and also give rise to the thought of patience and goodness towards them. If it is like this, one can cultivate right view. Cultivating right view, one has right action, right speech, right livelihood, right intention, right effort, right concentration, and right mindfulness. Cultivating right concentration, one is touched by others, but does not give rise to trouble. Because of not giving rise to trouble, one is called patient and good."

「无恼触故，得无恼语。虽复为彼之所恼触，不恼于他。虽复为彼之所恼语，而不恼语以恼于彼。虽为他恼，不生心恼。以不恼故，世人咸称能忍善者，亦复于彼生忍善想。若如是者，能修正见。修正见故，正业、正语、正命、正志、正方便、正定、正念。修正定故，为他所触，而不生恼。不生恼故，名为忍善。」

The village headman said, "Rare, Gautama! What you have said is very good. It is truly as you say. Because I did not cultivate right view, I was troubled by others. Because I was troubled by others, I spoke words of trouble and harm. Therefore, people in the world call me evil nature, and all give rise to evil thoughts, thus calling me evil nature. Because I did not cultivate the eightfold right path, I was touched by others and spoke words of trouble and harm, and finally became angry and resentful. Because of having such thoughts, people in the world call me evil nature."

村主言：「希有瞿昙！所说甚善。实如所言，我以不修正见故，为他所恼。为他恼故，出恼触言，是以世人，称我恶性，皆生恶想，故名恶性。不修八正道故，为他所触，出恼触语，遂至瞋忿。有是想故，世人称我，以为恶性。」

The village headman said, "Gautama! From now on, I will abandon such evil nature, violent and arrogant fierceness."

村主言：「瞿昙！我从今已后，如是恶性，卒暴、我慢、凶险，我当舍弃。」

The Buddha praised him, saying, "If you can do so, it is truly very good."

佛赞之曰：「汝若如是，实为甚善。」

At that time, the village headman, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully bowed his head in respect and departed.

时，彼村主闻佛所说，欢喜顶礼而去。
