SĀ² 2.18

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 18（一八）

Parallel to: MN 23 / SĀ 1079 / EĀ 39.9

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, a bhikṣu went to the riverbank in the early morning, took off his clothes, bathed, and came out on the shore to dry his body. A deity emitted light, illuminating the riverbank, and asked the bhikṣu, "Bhikṣu! This is a nest and den, at night smoke comes out, and during the day fire burns. A brāhman saw this and destroyed the nest and den, and dug the ground. Then a wise person told the brāhman, 'Dig the ground with a knife.' He saw a turtle and said, 'Take this turtle.' He further told him to dig the ground, and saw a poisonous snake, and told him to catch it. He further told him to dig the ground, and saw a piece of meat, and told him to pull it out. He further told him to dig the ground, and saw a knife sheath, and the brāhman said, 'This is a knife sheath.' He told him to dig it out. He further told him to dig the ground, and saw a poisonous insect with a sharp thorn, and told him to dig it out. He further told him to dig the ground, and saw two paths, and further told him to dig them out. He told him to dig the ground again, and saw a pile of stones, and told him to take out the stones. He further told him to dig the ground, and saw a dragon, and the brāhman said, 'Do not disturb the dragon.' He then knelt before the dragon."

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。尔时，有一比丘，于清晨朝往趣河边，脱衣洗浴，还出岸上，晞干其身。有天放光，照于河岸，问比丘言：「比丘！此是巢窟，夜则烟出，昼则火燃。有婆罗门见是事已，破彼巢窟，并掘其地。时有智人语婆罗门言：『以刀掘地。』见有一龟，婆罗门言：『取是龟来。』复语掘地，见一蝮蛇，语令捉取。复语掘地，见一肉段，语令挽取。复语掘地，见一刀舍，婆罗门言：『此是刀舍。』语令掘取。复语掘地，见楞祇芒毒虫，语令掘取。复语掘地，见有二道，复语掘出。语更掘地，见有石聚] cf.【丽】，语令出石。复语掘地，见有一龙，婆罗门言：『莫恼于龙。』即跪彼龙。」

The deity said to the bhikṣu, "Do not forget my words. You can ask the Buddha. Remember what the Buddha says. Why is that? I do not see any god, demon, or Brahma who can explain it, except for the Buddha and his disciple bhikṣus. No one can understand such a question."

天语比丘言：「莫忘我语，可以问佛，佛有所说，至心忆持。所以者何？我不见若天、若魔、若梵，有能分别者，除佛及以声闻弟子比丘，无能得解如是问者。」

At that time, the bhikṣu went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. He told the Buddha in detail what the deity had said, saying, "World-Honored One! How is it that the nest and den smoke at night and burn during the day? Who is the brāhman? Who is the wise person? What is the knife? What is the digging? What is the turtle? What is the poisonous snake? What is the piece of meat? What is the knife sheath? What is the poisonous insect with a sharp thorn? What are the two paths? What is the pile of stones? What is called a dragon?"

尔时比丘往至佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面立。所闻天语，具向佛说：「世尊！云何巢窟夜则烟出，昼则火然？谁是婆罗门？谁是智人？云何是刀？云何是掘？云何为龟？云何蝮蛇？云何肉段？云何刀舍？云何楞祇芒毒虫？云何二道？云何石聚？云何名龙？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "Listen carefully, I will explain it to you. The nest and den refer to this body. It is formed from the essence of the parents, the combination of the four great elements, and nourished by clothing and food. But this body will eventually disintegrate, bloat, become infested with worms, and decay. The night smoke refers to various thoughts and contemplations. The daytime fire refers to the extensive actions of body and speech. The brāhman refers to the Tathāgata. The wise person refers to the śrāvakas. The knife is a metaphor for wisdom. Digging the ground is a metaphor for diligence. The turtle is a metaphor for the five hindrances. The poisonous snake is a metaphor for anger and harm. The piece of meat is a metaphor for stinginess, greed, and jealousy. The knife sheath is a metaphor for the five desires. The poisonous insect with a sharp thorn is a metaphor for ignorance. The two paths are a metaphor for doubt. The pile of stones is a metaphor for arrogance. The dragon is a metaphor for the arhat who has exhausted all afflictions."

佛告比丘：「谛听谛听，当为汝说。巢窟者，所谓是身。受于父母精气，四大和合，衣食长养，乃得成身。而此身者，会至散败，膖胀虫烂乃至碎坏。夜烟出者，种种觉观。昼火然者，从身口业，广有所作。婆罗门者，即是如来。有智人者，即诸声闻。刀喻智慧。掘地者，喻于精进。龟者，喻于五盖。蝮蛇者，喻瞋恼害。肉段者，喻悭贪嫉妬。刀舍者，喻五欲。楞祇芒毒虫，喻如愚痴。二道者，喻于疑。诸石聚者，喻于我慢。龙者，喻于罗汉，尽诸有结。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

The nest and den are called the body, the thoughts and contemplations are like
the smoke, the creation is like the burning fire, the brāhman is like the
Buddha, the wise person is the śrāvaka, the knife is the wisdom, digging the
ground is a metaphor for diligence, the five hindrances are like a turtle, anger
is like a poisonous snake, greed and jealousy are like a piece of meat
the five desires are like a knife sheath, ignorance is like a poisonous insect
with a sharp thorn, doubt is like the two paths, the view of self
is like a pile of stones, you should not disturb the dragon now, the
dragon is a true arhat. Only the Buddha, the World-Honored One, can answer questions well.

「巢窟名为身，　　觉观如彼烟，造作如火然，　　婆罗门如佛，智人是声闻，　　刀即是智慧，掘地喻精进，　　五盖犹如龟，瞋恚如蝮蛇，　　贪嫉如肉段，五欲如刀舍，　　愚痴如楞祇，疑者如二道，　　我见如石聚，汝今莫恼龙，　　龙是真罗汉。善答问难者，　　唯有佛世尊。」

The Buddha having spoken thus, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
