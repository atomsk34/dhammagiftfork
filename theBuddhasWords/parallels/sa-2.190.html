SĀ² 2.190

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 190（一九〇）

Parallel to: SN 44.9 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, the Brāhman Kaṇāda went to where the Buddha was, inquired about the Tathāgata's well-being, and sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I have a few doubts that I wish to ask. If you are learned, please listen and consider them."

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹林。尔时，犊子梵志往诣佛所，慰问如来，在一面坐。白佛言：「世尊！我有少疑，将欲请问。汝若多闻，愿垂听察。」

The Buddha told Kaṇāda, "If you have any doubts, ask as you wish."

佛告犊子：「若有所疑，随汝所问。」

Kaṇāda asked, "Is the body the same as the self?"

犊子问曰：「身之与我为是一耶？」

The Buddha said, "I do not answer such questions."

佛言：「如此之事，我所不答。」

He asked again, "Is the body different from the self?"

又问：「身我异耶？」

The Buddha said, "I also do not answer such questions."

佛言：「如此之事，我亦不答。」

The calf said again, "Now I ask you, is my body one? You do not answer. Is the body different from the self? You do not answer either. Such questions, you do not answer. How can you then predict for your disciples that they will die here and be reborn in the heavens or among humans? If you predict that they will die here and be reborn there, wouldn't it be that the body stays here while the self goes to the five paths? If so, then the body and the self would be separate."

犊子复言：「今我问汝，我身一耶？汝不见答。身我异耶？汝复不答。如斯等问，尚不见答，云何而能记诸弟子死此生彼天人之中？汝若记彼死此生彼，宁可不是身留于此，我往于彼五道之中？若如斯者，身之与我则为别异。」

The Buddha told the brahman, "I say that with grasping, one will be reborn. Without grasping, there will be no rebirth. Furthermore, calf, it is like fire. With fuel, it burns. Without fuel, it does not burn."

佛告梵志：「我说有取，记彼受生；若无取者，则无受生。复次] cf.【丽】犊子，譬如彼火，有取则然；若无取者，火则不然。」

The calf said, "Gautama! I also see fire burning without fuel."

犊子言：「瞿昙！我亦见火无取而然。」

The Buddha told the calf, "What fire do you see burning without fuel?"

佛告犊子：「汝见何火无取而然？」

The calf said again, "It is like seeing a great fire, very blazing, with a fierce wind extinguishing the flames, seeing the burning apart from the fire."

犊子复言：「譬如见大火，甚为炽盛，猛风绝炎，离火见然。」

The Buddha told the calf, "This extinguished flame also has fuel."

佛告犊子：「如此绝炎，亦复有取。」

The calf said, "Seeing the burning apart from the fire, what is the fuel?"

犊子言：「离火见然，以何为取？」

The Buddha said, "This extinguished flame is burning due to the wind. Because of the fuel of the wind, the flame can temporarily stop. Because of the power of the wind, the extinguished flame can be seen."

佛言：「如斯绝炎，因风而然，以风取故，炎得暂停。以风力故，绝炎可见。」

The calf said, "Gautama! Fire may be like that, but people are not. Why is that? When the body dies here, the mind is born there. Who is the fuel in between?"

犊子言：「瞿昙！火尚可尔，人则不然。所以者何？身死于此，意生于彼，于其中间，谁为其取？」

The Buddha said, "At that time, craving is the fuel. Because of the condition of craving as fuel, sentient beings receive birth. All worlds delight in fuel. All are delighted by fuel. All are caused by fuel. When sentient beings see fuel, they give rise to joy. All sentient beings enter into fuel. The Tathāgata, the Arhat, attains unsurpassed perfect enlightenment because of being without fuel."

佛言：「当于尔时，以爱为取，爱取因缘，众生受生。一切世间，皆乐于取，一切皆为取所爱乐，一切悉皆以取为因。众生见取，则生欢喜，一切众生皆入于取。如来阿罗呵以无取故，而得成于无上正觉。」

The calf said, "I have much to do now. I want to return to my dwelling place."

犊子言：「我于今者，大有所作，欲还所止。」

The Buddha said, "Brahmin! It is time for you to know."

佛言：「梵志！宜知是时。」

At that time, the calf heard what the Buddha said and joyfully accepted it.

尔时，犊子闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
