SĀ² 2.195

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 195（一九五）

Parallel to: SN 44.10 / SĀ 961

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Vulture Peak in Rajagriha, in the bamboo grove of Kalanda. At that time, the Brahmin calf went to where the Buddha was, greeted the Buddha, and sat to one side. He asked the Buddha, "Gautama! Do all sentient beings have a self?" The Buddha remained silent and did not answer. He further asked, "Are they without a self?" The Buddha also did not answer. At that time, the calf thought, "I have asked the śramaṇa Gautama about this meaning many times, but he remains silent and does not answer."

一时，佛在王舍城灵鹫山迦兰陀竹林。彼时犊子梵志往诣佛所，问讯佛已，在一面坐。白佛言：「瞿昙！一切众生为有我不？」佛默然不答。又问：「为无我耶？」佛亦不答。尔时，犊子作是念：「我曾数问沙门瞿昙如是之义，默不见答。」

At that time, Ānanda was attending to the Tathāgata, fanning him with a fan. When Ānanda heard this, he said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Why did you remain silent and not answer the questions of the calf? If you do not answer, the calf will say, 'I asked the Tathāgata, but he did not answer at all. Is this increasing wrong views?'"

尔时，阿难侍如来侧，以扇扇佛。彼时阿难闻其语已，即白佛言：「世尊！何故犊子所问默然不答？若不答者，犊子当言：『我问如来都不见答，增邪见耶？』」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "In the past, he asked about all dharmas. If there were a self, I could have answered the questions of the calf. Have I not already said in all the sutras that there is no self? Because there is no self, if I answered his questions, it would go against reason. Why is that? Because all dharmas are without self. How could I answer him with a self? If I did, it would only increase his ignorance and confusion from the past. Furthermore, Ānanda! If one speaks of a self, one falls into the view of eternalism; if one speaks of no self, one falls into the view of annihilationism. The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma abandons the two extremes and unifies the middle way. Because these dharmas are impermanent due to disintegration, and continuous due to non-disintegration, they are neither permanent nor discontinuous. Because of this, they arise from causes and conditions. If the causes and conditions do not arise, they will not arise. Therefore, from ignorance, there is the arising of formations. From formations, there is consciousness. From consciousness, there is name and form. From name and form, there are the six sense bases. From the six sense bases, there is contact. From contact, there is feeling. From feeling, there is craving. From craving, there is grasping. From grasping, there is becoming. From becoming, there is birth. From birth, there is old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. All these great masses of suffering arise from this. When the cause ceases, the fruit ceases. When ignorance ceases, formations cease. When formations cease, consciousness ceases. When consciousness ceases, name and form cease. When name and form cease, the six sense bases cease. When the six sense bases cease, contact ceases. When contact ceases, feeling ceases. When feeling ceases, craving ceases. When craving ceases, grasping ceases. When grasping ceases, becoming ceases. When becoming ceases, birth ceases. When birth ceases, old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress cease. The great mass of suffering ceases.

佛告阿难：「于先昔，彼问一切诸法，若有我者，吾可答彼犊子所问。吾于昔时，宁可不于一切经说无我耶？以无我故，答彼所问，则违道理。所以者何？一切诸法，皆无我故。云何以我，而答于彼？若然者，将更增彼昔来愚惑。复次，阿难！若说有我，即堕常见；若说无我，即堕断见。如来说法，舍离二边，会于中道，以此诸法坏故不常，续故不断，不常不断，因是有是，因是生故，彼则得生，若因不生，则彼不生，是故因于无明，则有行生，因行故有识，因识故有名色，因名色故有六入，因六入故有触，因触故有受，因受故有爱，因爱故有取，因取故有有，因有故有生，因生故有老死，忧悲苦恼，众苦聚集。因是故有果灭，无明灭则行灭，行灭则识灭，识灭则名色灭，名色灭则六入灭，六入灭则触灭，触灭则受灭，受灭则爱灭，爱灭则取灭，取灭则有灭，有灭则生灭，生灭则老死，忧悲苦恼，众苦聚集灭尽，则大苦聚灭。」

Having spoken thus, the Buddha's disciples rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
