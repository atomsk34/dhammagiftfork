SĀ² 2.196

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 196（一九六）

Parallel to: MN 72 / SĀ 962

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, the Brāhmaṇa Kaulita went to where the Buddha was, greeted the Buddha, and sat to one side. He asked the Buddha, "Gautama, do you ever have this view and make this statement: 'The world is permanent, only I understand it, others do not know it'? Do you say this?"

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。尔时，犊子梵志往诣佛所，问讯佛已，在一面坐。问佛言：「瞿昙！汝颇作是见，作是论：『世界是常，唯我解了，余人不知。』作是说不？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "I do not have this view, nor do I say, 'Only I can know, others do not understand.'"

佛告犊子：「我不作是见，不作是说：『唯我能知，余人不解。』」

Kaulita asked again, "If you do not make such statements, then is the entire world impermanent?"

犊子又问：「汝若不作如是说者，一切世界悉无常耶？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "I also do not make such statements, saying, 'The world is impermanent, only I can know, others do not understand.'"

佛告犊子：「我亦不作如是说言：『世界无常，唯我能知，余人不解。』」

Kaulita asked again, "Do you also make such statements, saying, 'The world is both permanent and impermanent, only I can know, others do not understand'?"

犊子又问：「汝颇复作如是论言：『世界亦常无常，唯我能知，余人不解。』作是说耶？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "I also do not make such statements, saying, 'The entire world is neither permanent nor impermanent, neither permanent nor impermanent, only I can understand, others do not understand.'"

佛告犊子：「我亦不作如是说言：『一切世界，亦常无常，唯我独了，余人不知。』」犊子又问：「汝颇复作如是说言：『一切世界非常非无常，非非常非非无常，唯我能解，余人不了。』作是说耶？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "I also do not make such statements, saying, 'The entire world is neither permanent nor impermanent, neither permanent nor impermanent, only I can understand, others do not understand.'"

佛告犊子：「我亦不作如是说言：『一切世界，非常非无常，非非常非非无常，唯我能知，余人不解。』」

Kaulita asked again, "The world has boundaries, the world has no boundaries, both has boundaries and has no boundaries, neither has boundaries nor has no boundaries, neither has boundaries nor has no boundaries. The body is the same as the life, the life is the same as the body, the body is different from the life, the life is different from the body. The sentient being's spirit and self, dying here and being born there, is it existent, is it non-existent, is it both existent and non-existent, is it neither existent nor non-existent, is it neither existent nor non-existent? Gautama, do you now make such statements?"

犊子复问：「世界有边，世界无边，亦有边亦无边，非有边非无边，非非有边非非无边。身即是命，命即是身，身异命异。众生神我，死此生彼，为有为无，亦有亦无，非有非无，非非有非非无。瞿昙] cf.【丽】！汝今作是说耶？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "I do not have such views, and I do not make such statements, saying that the world has boundaries or no boundaries, up to neither existent nor non-existent."

佛告犊子：「我不作是见，不作是论说言，世界有边无边，乃至非非有非非无。」

Kaulita said again, "Gautama! Now, in this Dharma, what faults do you see that you do not take any view?"

犊子复言：「瞿昙！汝今于斯法中，见何过患，不取一见？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "I also do not say that the world is permanent, only this is true, the rest is foolish darkness. Those views are obstructed by afflictions, those views are what is practiced and observed, those views are dust, dirt, and impurity. Views are afflictions that can harm, can cause sorrow, can cause practitioners to experience distress, and can give rise to various afflictions. If one is associated with views, one is foolish, also called ignorant, also called an ordinary person, able to cause birth and death to flow and increase." He further told Kaulita, "The world is permanent, impermanent, both permanent and impermanent, neither permanent nor impermanent. The world has boundaries, no boundaries, both boundaries and no boundaries, neither boundaries nor no boundaries. The sentient being's spirit and self, dying here and being born there, either exists, does not exist, both exists and does not exist, neither exists nor does not exist, neither does not exist nor does not not exist. If someone holds these views, they are called foolish, also called ignorant, also called an ordinary person, able to increase birth and death, afflictions, and defilements, able to cause practitioners to experience distress, and give rise to various afflictions, without peace and happiness. For this reason, I do not cling to these views."

佛告犊子：「我亦不言世界是常，唯此事实，余皆愚暗。彼见结障，彼见所行及所观处，彼见尘埃垢秽不净，见结与苦俱能为害，能与忧恼，能令行人受欝［火＊烝］热，生诸忧患。若与见结相应，即是𪧀愚，亦名无闻，亦名凡夫，能令生死回流增长。」复告犊子：「世间常无常，亦常无常，非常非无常。世界有边，及以无边，亦有边亦无边，非有边非无边。众生神我，死此生彼，若有若无，亦有亦无，非有非无，非非有非非无。若有人计斯见者，名为𪧀愚，亦名无闻，亦名凡夫，增长生死烦恼垢污，能令行人受欝［火＊烝］热，生诸忧患，无有安乐。以是义故，我于此见，无所执著。」

Kaulita again asked, "If you do not hold such views, what views do you hold now?"

犊子又问：「汝若不计如是见者，汝今所计，为是何见？」

The Buddha told Kaulita, "The Tathāgata, the World-Honored One, has long since abandoned all views and is completely free from views. Although he sees, his mind is not attached. That is, he sees the noble truth of suffering, the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the path to the cessation of suffering. He clearly understands and sees this. Having seen this, he regards all dharmas as being the afflictions of craving and attachment, as being mine and mine. This is called grasping views and is also called pride. Such dharmas are to be hated and despised. Therefore, they should all be eliminated. Having eliminated them, one attains nirvāṇa, tranquility, purity, and true liberation. It is impossible for bhikṣus who have attained such true liberation to take rebirth in the three realms of existence."

佛告犊子：「如来世尊，于久远来诸有见者，悉皆除舍，都无诸见，虽有所见，心无取著。所谓见苦圣谛，见苦集谛，见苦灭谛，见至苦灭道谛，我悉明了，知见是已，视一切法皆是贪爱诸烦恼结，是我我所，名见取著，亦名憍慢。如斯之法，是可患厌，是故皆应当断除之。既断除已，获得涅槃，寂灭清净，如是正解脱。诸比丘等，若更受身于三有者，无有是处。」

Kaulita said, "Gautama! What causes and conditions do you see now that you say there is no place of rebirth?"

犊子言：「瞿昙！汝于今者，见何因缘，说无生处？」

The Buddha said to Kaulita, "I will ask you a question. Answer as you wish. Suppose someone lights a large fire in front of you. You know the fire is burning. Do you know that the fire in front of you has gone out? If someone asks you, 'Where did the fire go after it went out? Did it go to the east, south, west, north, or even the lower direction?' How would you answer?"

佛告犊子：「我还问汝，随汝意答。譬如有人，于汝目前然大火聚，汝知是火然，不知此火聚，在汝前灭，汝知灭不？若复有人，来问汝言：『此火灭已，为至东方，南西北方，乃至下方，亦复如是。斯诸方中，为至何处？』若如是者，当云何答？」

Kaulita said, "Gautama! If someone asks me, I will answer truthfully. If there is grass, wood, or cow dung, the fire will burn with the fuel and not go out. If the grass, wood, and cow dung are all exhausted, the fire will go out and not go to any direction."

犊子言：「瞿昙！若人问我，当如实答，若有草木及牛马粪，此火与薪相得，便然不灭。草木牛粪，若都尽者，此火则灭，不至方所。」

The Buddha said to Kaulita, "It is so! It is so! If one says that form is the Tathāgata, and feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are the Tathāgata, it is not so. Why? Because the Tathāgata has already severed such form, and feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are also like this, all having been severed. It is like a person cutting down a palm tree. Once cut, it will not grow again. The Tathāgata is also like this. Having severed the five aggregates, he will not be reborn again. The cessation of thought and conception is the unborn Dharma."

佛告犊子：「如是！如是。若言色是如来，受想行识是如来者，无有是处。何以故？如来已断如斯色故，受想行识，亦复如是，皆悉已断。譬如有人断多罗树，断已不生，如来亦尔，断五阴已，不复受生。寂灭无想，是无生法。」

Kaulita said, "Gautama! I now wish to speak a parable. Please listen."

犊子言：「瞿昙！我于今者，乐说譬喻，唯愿听说。」

The Buddha said, "Speak as you wish."

佛告之曰：「随汝意说。」

Kaulita said, "It is like a śāla forest not far from a city or village, where the branches and leaves have fallen for a hundred thousand years, leaving only the trunk. You, Gautama, are also like this. You have already severed all afflictions and bonds, and the four inverted delusions are all completely extinguished. Only the firm and true Dharma body remains. Gautama! You should know that I now have affairs to attend to and wish to return."

犊子即言：「譬如去于城邑聚落不远，平博之处有娑罗林，是娑罗林已百千年，枝叶悉堕，唯贞实在。汝今瞿昙，亦复如是，已断一切烦恼结缚，四倒邪惑，皆悉灭尽，唯有坚固真法身在。瞿昙！当知我今缘务，将欲还归。」

The Buddha said, "It is time for you to know."

佛言：「宜知是时。」

The Kaulita Brahmana, having heard the Buddha's teaching, was delighted and left.

犊子梵志闻佛所说，欢喜而去。
