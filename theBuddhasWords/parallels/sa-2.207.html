SĀ² 2.207

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 207（二〇七）

Parallel to: AN 3.71 (~)

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was in the Kuśīnagara Grove in the country of Kauśāmbī. At that time, there was a brāhman named Cunda who went to where Ānanda was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. He said, "For what reason did you leave home and study the path in the teachings of that śramaṇa Gautama?"

尔时，尊者阿难在拘睒弥国瞿师罗园。时有梵志名曰阐陀，诣阿难所，问讯已讫，在一面坐，而作是言：「汝以何事，于彼沙门瞿昙法中，出家学道？」

Ānanda replied, "I now wish to cut off evil and give rise to goodness. For this reason, I have left home and study the path in the Buddha's teachings."

阿难答言：「我今为欲断恶生善，以是义故，于佛法中，出家学道。」

The brāhman further said, "What kind of evil is to be cut off?"

梵志复言：「断何等恶？」

Ānanda replied, "I now wish to cut off greed, anger, and ignorance."

阿难答言：「我今欲为断除贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。」

The brāhman further said, "Do you also know how to cut off greed, anger, and ignorance?"

梵志复言：「汝等亦知断除贪欲、瞋恚、痴耶？」

Ānanda replied, "Only in the Buddha's teachings is there a way to cut off such greed, anger, and ignorance, to restrain body and mind."

阿难答曰：「唯佛法中，有断如是贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴之法，禁制身心。」

The brāhman further said, "What are the faults of such greed, anger, and ignorance? Why do you restrain them in your teachings?"

梵志又言：「如此贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴，有何过患？汝等法中禁制之耶？」

Ānanda replied, "Attachment to desire can give rise to vexation. In the present life, it increases evil dharmas, and from it arise sorrow, grief, and suffering. In the future life, it is also like this. Attachment to anger and ignorance can destroy one's own mind and also destroy the minds of others. Both self and others are troubled. In the present life, it increases all evils; in the future life, it also increases all evils. Furthermore, if one is attached to this desire, it can make sentient beings blind without the eye of wisdom. The causes and conditions of desire can weaken wisdom, diminish all goodness, not lead to nirvāṇa, not attain the three insights and six supernormal powers, and depart from the path of bodhi. As with desire, anger and ignorance are also like this. We see that desire, anger, and ignorance have such faults. For this reason, we restrain desire, anger, and ignorance."

阿难对曰：「欲爱染著能生恼乱，于现在世，增长恶法，忧悲苦恼，由之而生；未来世中，亦复如是。瞋恚所著，愚痴所著，能坏己心，亦坏他心，自他俱恼，于现在世，增长诸恶；未来世中，亦复如是，增长诸恶。复次，若有染著此贪欲者，能令众生盲无慧眼。贪欲因缘能令智慧微弱，损减诸善，不趣涅槃，不得三明及六神通，离菩提道。如贪欲，瞋恚、愚痴亦复如是。我等见斯贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴，有如是过患，以是义故，禁断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。」

The brāhman further asked, "Is there a path to cultivate and increase that can cut off desire, anger, and ignorance?"

梵志又问：「颇复有道修集增广，能断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴耶？」

Ānanda replied, "There is the Eightfold Noble Path, namely right view, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right concentration, right mindfulness, and right intention, which can cut off desire, anger, and ignorance and lead to nirvāṇa."

阿难答言：「有八圣道，所谓正见、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正定、正念、正志，能断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴，趣向涅槃。」

The brāhman further said, "This path is extremely good. Cultivating and increasing it can cut off desire, anger, and ignorance. Ānanda should know that I now have many affairs and wish to return."

梵志复言：「如斯之道，极为甚善，修集增广，能断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。阿难当知，我今缘务，极为猥多，今欲还归。」

Ānanda said, "It is time to know."

阿难告曰：「宜知是时。」

The brāhman, having heard what Ānanda said, was delighted and left.

梵志闻阿难所说，欢喜而去。
