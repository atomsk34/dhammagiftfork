SĀ² 2.214

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 214（二一四）

Parallel to: SN 5.1 / SĀ 1198

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, there was a bhikṣuṇī named Āṭānāṭī. In the morning, she put on her robe, took her alms bowl, and entered the city to beg for food. After eating, she washed her bowl and was about to go to the Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. At that time, Māra the Evil One thought like this: "The Śramaṇa Gautama is now in the Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. His disciple, the bhikṣuṇī Āṭānāṭī, has entered the city to beg for food. After eating, she has washed her bowl and is about to go to that park. I shall create disturbances for her." At that time, Māra transformed himself into a young brahmin and asked Āṭānāṭī on the side of the road, "Where are you going?"

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。时有比丘尼名曰旷野，于其晨朝，著衣持钵，入城乞食，食讫洗钵，将欲向彼得眼林中。时魔王波旬作是心念：「瞿昙沙门今在舍卫得眼林中，其弟子旷野比丘尼入城乞食，食讫洗钵，收摄坐具，将欲往诣于彼林间，我当为其而作扰乱。」尔时，波旬化作摩纳，于彼路侧，问旷野言：「欲何所诣？」

The bhikṣuṇī replied, "I now wish to go to a quiet place."

比丘尼答言：「我今欲诣闲静之处。」

At that time, the young brahmin, having heard these words, spoke a verse:

尔时，摩纳闻是语已，即说偈言：

In all the worlds, there is no one who is liberated
You go to an empty and quiet place, what do you
intend to do? You are young and beautiful now, not enjoying
the five desires. Once old age comes, do not later give
rise to regret.

「一切世间中，　　无有解脱者，汝诣空静处，　　将欲何所作？汝今年盛美，　　不受于五欲，一旦衰老至，　　后莫生忧悔。」

At that time, the bhikṣuṇī had this thought: "Who is this, wanting to disturb me, very deceitful. Is it a person or a non-person?" Having had this thought, she entered concentration to observe. Knowing that it was Pāpīyān wanting to come and disturb, she spoke a verse:

时比丘尼而作是念：「此为是谁？欲恼乱我，甚为欺诈。为是人耶？是非人乎？」作是念已，入定观察，知是波旬欲来恼乱，即说偈言：

In the world, there is liberation, which I now know for myself
Pāpīyān, you are foolish and base, not understanding such a trace. Desire
is like a sharp spear, the hidden enemy follows with a drawn
sword. You say to enjoy the five desires, but desire is fearful
suffering. Desire can give rise to sorrow and distress, desire can give
rise to longing, desire can give rise to a hundred sufferings, desire
is the root of all suffering. Cutting off all craving, extinguishing the
darkness of ignorance, attaining the ultimate cessation, abiding in the undefiled Dharma.

「世间有解脱，　　我今自证知，波旬汝愚鄙，　　不解如斯迹。欲如摽利戟，　　阴贼拔刀逐，汝言受五欲，　　欲苦可怖畏。欲能生忧恼，　　欲能生追念，欲能生百苦，　　欲是众苦本。断除一切爱，　　灭诸无明暗，逮证于尽灭，　　住于无漏法。」

At that time, Pāpīyān had this thought: "The bhikṣuṇī in the wilderness knows my mind well." Disappointed and regretful, ashamed and embarrassed, he returned to his palace.

尔时，波旬而作是念：「旷野比丘尼，善知我心。」懊恼悔恨，惭愧还宫。
