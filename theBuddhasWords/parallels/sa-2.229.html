SĀ² 2.229

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 229（二二九）

Parallel to: SN 8.2 / SĀ 1213

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, Venerable Vāgīśa was in a secluded place, about to examine his mind, focusing his thoughts and contemplating, when suddenly a strange thought arose, giving rise to displeasure. He immediately realized, "I have now lost a good opportunity. The life of renunciation is called difficult to attain; if one has this mind, it is not difficult to attain. I have now regressed from the good mind and obtained an evil mind. I should now speak of the many faults of the mind and recite verses of disenchantment." At that time, the Venerable One spoke a verse, saying:

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，尊者婆耆奢在空静处，时欲捡心，系念思惟，卒起异想，生不憙乐，即自觉知：「我于今者，便失善利。夫出家者，名为难得；若有是心不名难得，我今便为退失善心，得于恶心，今当说心，多诸过恶，说厌患偈。」时彼尊者即说偈言：

Abandoning the pleasures of attachment, and those who are not attached to
pleasure, abandoning the greed for robes and the perception of pleasure, not
creating the forest of afflictions. The branches of desire hang down and
spread, sentient beings delight in the conditions of attachment. Being able to
cut off the forest of desire, this is called a bhikṣu. Not
letting desire hang down and attach, without the name of a forest
a bhikṣu. The sixth mind arises from perception. This desire is what
the world delights in attaching to. If one can arise from perception
one can be free from non-attachment. Not delighting in superior desires. Delighting
in coarse and evil speech, not called a bhikṣu. Delighting in the
perception of the body, due to seeing, hearing, and mental perception. Attaching
to the five sense faculties, being able to be free from the
attachment of desire, not accepting the defilement of humiliation, this is called
liberation. The great earth and empty space, the world with form, all
return to disintegration, everything is the same in extinction. Knowing and seeing
this, practicing the Dharma has already been determined. Not giving rise to
perception in the various realms, being straightforward and not deceitful. Although seeking
to preserve the body, it is for the benefit of something. If
one can be like this, it is the same as entering nirvāṇa.

「弃舍乐诸著，　　及不乐著者，舍衣贪嗜觉，　　不造烦恼林。欲枝下垂布，　　众生乐缘著，能断于欲林，　　是名为比丘。不垂下著欲，　　无林名比丘，第六意出觉。　　然此欲觉者，世间所乐著，　　若得出觉意，能离非结著，　　不乐于胜欲。乐出麁恶言，　　不名为比丘，乐嗜于受身，　　因见闻意识，想著生五根，　　能离欲想著，不受涂污辱，　　是名得解脱。大地及虚空，　　世间有色处，悉皆归散坏，　　一切同尽灭。知见是事已，　　行法已决定，诸处不生受，　　质直不谄伪。虽求念存身，　　为有所利益，若能如是者，　　同彼入涅槃。」
