SĀ² 2.25

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 25（二五）

Parallel to: SN 4.15 / SĀ 1086

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, the World-Honored One was walking in the grove in the latter part of the night. In the early morning, after washing his feet, he sat upright with his body straight, focusing his attention in front of him. At that time, Māra the Evil One thought, "Śramaṇa Gautama is in Rājagṛha, walking in the grove in the latter part of the night. In the early morning, after washing his feet, he has entered a quiet room, sitting upright with his body straight, focusing his attention in front of him. I shall now go and disturb him." Having thought this, he immediately transformed himself into the form of a young brahmin, stood in front of the Buddha, and spoke a verse:

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹林。尔时，世尊于夜后分，经行林中。于其晨朝，洗足已，正身端坐，系念在前。尔时，魔王作是念言：「沙门瞿昙在王舍城，于夜后分，林中经行。于其晨朝，洗足已，入于静室，正身端坐，系念在前。我今当往，而作娆乱。」作是念已，即便化为摩纳之形，在佛前立，而说偈言：

My mind can transform and create a net that covers the
sky. The śramaṇa will never be able to escape from me.

「我心能化作，　　罗网遍虚空，沙门于我所，　　终不得解脱。」

The Buddha thought, "Māra has come to disturb me." He then spoke a verse:

佛作是念：「魔来娆乱。」即说偈言：

In the world, there are five desires that bind the foolish. One
who can cut off these desires will completely end all suffering. I
have already cut off all desires and my mind is also not
attached. Māra, you should know that I have long destroyed the net of desires.

「世间有五欲，　　愚者为所缚，能断此诸欲，　　永尽一切苦。我已断诸欲，　　意亦不染著，波旬应当知，　　我久坏欲网。」

At that time, when Māra heard the verse, his wishes were not fulfilled. He was sorrowful, distressed, and disappeared, returning to his heavenly palace.

尔时，魔王闻说偈已，不果所愿，忧愁苦恼，隐形而去，还于天宫。
