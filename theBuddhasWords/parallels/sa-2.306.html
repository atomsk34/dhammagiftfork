SĀ² 2.306

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 306（三〇六）

Parallel to: SN 2.26 / AN 4.45 / AN 4.46 / SĀ 1307 / EĀ 43.1 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, a god named Red Horse, with radiance and splendor surpassing the usual, came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Where can one find a place where there is no birth, aging, death, no sinking or emerging, the end of all beings? Is it possible to know such a limit? "

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。时有天子名曰赤马，光色倍常，来诣佛所，顶礼佛足，却坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！当于何处而能得有不生老死，不没不出，众生尽处？如是边际，为可知不？」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the god Red Horse, "As for not being born, not aging, not dying, not perishing, not being reborn, no one can reach the limit, and no one can go to the end of it."

尔时，世尊告赤马天子言：「不生老死，既不终没，亦不出生，无有人能行至边际，亦无有能往诣于彼，尽其崖限。」

At that time, the god Red Horse said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! The World-Honored One's words are very good and rare. As for not being born, not aging, not dying, and not being able to reach the limit, why is that? I remember in the past, I was once a sage named Red Horse. I cut off the bonds of desire and attained the five supernatural powers of the world. My divine power was swift, surpassing the sun and moon. With a single leap, I could cross the great ocean. I thought, 'My divine power is so swift now. I shall go to the limit of all beings.' At that time, I was single-mindedly seeking the limit of all beings. My mind was so focused that I had no leisure, except for washing my hands, eating, and going to the toilet. In a hundred years, I still could not reach the limit of all beings, and then I died. Therefore, I know that the Tathāgata speaks well. As for not being born, not aging, not dying, not perishing, not being reborn, wanting to go there and know its limit, there is no such place."

时赤马天子白佛言：「世尊！世尊所说，甚善希有，不生老死，乃至无能得其边际。所以者何？念我过去，曾为仙人，号名赤马，断于欲结，得世五通，神力骏疾，过于日月，举足一踔，能渡大海。而作是念：『我今神力骏疾如是，我当行尽众生边际。』我于尔时，志欲专求众生边故，心意［怡－台＋（公／心）］［怡－台＋（公／心）］都无闲暇，唯除洗手，并饮食时及大小便，于百年中，竟不能得众生边际，而便命终。以是故知，如来善说，不生老死，不出不没，欲往于彼，知其边际，都无是处。」

The Buddha told the god Red Horse, "So it is! So it is! If there is no birth, aging, death, no perishing, no rebirth, the limit of beings is indeed not a place. If one wants to know, the limit of beings is the same as nirvāṇa. If one ends suffering, that is called reaching the limit."

佛告赤马天子曰：「如是！如是！若有不生老死，不出不没，众生边际，实无是处。若欲知者，众生边际即是涅槃，若尽苦际，是即名为得其边际。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

Although there is this spiritual power, in the end, one cannot
attain the limit of beings. If one cannot reach the limit
how can one end suffering? Therefore, I, the sage, am called
one who knows the world well. Only those with superior wisdom
can understand the limit. The practice of purity has been established
with right wisdom reaching the limit of beings, crossing to the
other shore of the limit.

「虽有是神力，　　终无有能得，行尽众生边。　　若不得边际，何能尽苦际？　　是故我牟尼，得名善知世，　　唯有胜智人，能晓了边际，　　梵行已得立，正智众生边，　　度边之彼岸。」

At that time, the Heavenly Son of the Red Horse heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and returned to his heavenly palace.

时赤马天子闻佛所说，欢喜顶礼，还于天宫。
