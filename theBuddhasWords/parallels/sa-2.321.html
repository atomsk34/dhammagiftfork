SĀ² 2.321

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 321（三二一）

Parallel to: SN 10.7 / SĀ 1322

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was staying in the palace of the yakṣa mother Punyavati in the country of Magadha. That night, the Buddha stayed in her palace. The yakṣa son Punyavati and his wife Utarā cried out in the night. The mother then consoled her children, wanting them to stop crying, and spoke a verse:

一时，佛在摩竭提国富那婆修夜叉母宫。佛于其夜，在彼宫宿，其子夜叉婆修，及女优怛罗，夜中啼泣。其母尔时慰抚男女，欲令不啼，即说偈言：

Punyavati and Utarā, you should stop
crying now. The Buddha, the hero
of the world, has taught the
Dharma, which I have heard. Neither
father nor mother can free one
from suffering and distress. Only the
World-Honored One, skillfully teaching the Dharma
can cause the listener to forever
be free from suffering. All beings
follow the current of desire, sinking
in the ocean of birth and
death. I wish to listen to
the Dharma to cut off this
current of desire. Punyavati and Utarā
therefore you should be silent.

「富那婆修，　　及优怛罗，　　汝等今者，宜止啼声。　　佛之世雄，　　所说法要，使我得闻，　　非父非母，　　能脱苦恼。唯有世尊，　　善巧说法，　　能令闻者，永离诸苦。　　一切众生，　　随于欲流，没生死海，　　我欲听法，　　断斯欲流。富那婆修，　　及优怛罗，　　是故汝等，宜应默然。」

At that time, Punyavati spoke a verse:

时富那婆修，即说偈言：

I will now follow my mother's instruction and no longer make a
sound. My younger sister Utarā, you should also be silent now. I
wish to listen to the subtle Dharma spoken by that śramaṇa. The
Buddha, the foremost among men in Magadha, extensively expounds the Dharma to
all beings, teaching the means to end suffering. He speaks of suffering
and the arising of suffering, and the path to escape suffering. He
speaks of the eightfold noble path, which leads to peace and nirvāṇa
It is excellent to hear the essential Dharma spoken by the śramaṇa.

「我今随母教，　　更不生音声，小妹优怛罗，　　尔今亦默然，愿听彼沙门，　　说于微妙法。佛于摩竭提，　　人中最为上，广为诸众生，　　演说断苦法。说苦能生苦，　　说苦出要道，说贤圣八道，　　安隐趣涅槃。善哉闻沙门，　　所说法之要。」

The mother replied in verse:

母以偈答：

You are one who knows and sees, and what you say
accords with my mind. You praise well that guide of the
world. Because of your silence, I have seen the four noble
truths. Utarā will also see the four noble truths in the future.

「汝是知见者，　　所说称我心，汝善赞叹彼，　　世间之导师。以汝等默故，　　令我见四谛，优怛罗后时，　　亦当见四谛。」
