SĀ² 2.330

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 330（三三〇）

Parallel to: SN 15.13 / SĀ 937 / EĀ 51.2

At that time, the World-Honored One was at the great lecture hall on the bank of the Monkey Pond in Vaiśālī. At that time, there were forty bhikṣus who were all practicing in the wilderness, wearing rag robes, and begging for food. They were all on the path of learning and had not yet transcended the Dharma of desire. They all came to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

尔时，世尊在毘舍离猕猴陂岸大讲堂中。时有四十波利蛇迦比丘皆阿练若，著粪扫衣，尽行乞食，悉在学地，未离欲法，咸至佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One had this thought: "These bhikṣus are all practicing in the wilderness, wearing rag robes, and begging for food. They are all learners who have not yet severed the bonds. I shall teach the Dharma to them as appropriate, so that the bhikṣus, without rising from their seats, will attain understanding and exhaust all afflictions and taints."

尔时，世尊作是念：「此诸比丘皆阿练若，著粪扫衣，尽行乞食，悉是学人，未断诸结，吾当为彼，如应说法，令诸比丘不起于坐，心得解悟，尽诸结漏。」

The Buddha told them, "Bhikṣus, you should know that birth and death are long and far-reaching, without boundaries. No one can know their source. All sentient beings are covered by ignorance, bound by the fetters of craving, and their necks are bound. The long road of birth and death is endless. The suffering of the past eons is immeasurable. It is like the Ganges River flowing into the four oceans. I now ask you: Which is greater, the blood you have shed in your births and deaths, or the water of the Ganges River?"

佛告之曰：「比丘当知，生死长远，无有边际，无有能知其根源者，一切众生皆为无明之所覆盖，爱结所使，缠系其颈，生死长途，流转无穷，过去亿苦无能知者，譬如恒河流入四海。我今问汝：汝处生死，所出血多？为恒河多？」

At that time, the bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! According to my understanding of the meaning of the Buddha's words, the blood shed by my body in my births and deaths is greater than the four great oceans of the Ganges River."

时诸比丘白佛言：「世尊！如我解佛所说义者，我处生死，身所出血，多彼恒河四大海水。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Excellent! Excellent! From your past lives, you have taken the form of an elephant. Others have cut off your nose, ears, or feet. You have been hooked in the head and had your neck cut. The blood you have shed is immeasurable and boundless. You have also taken the forms of cows, horses, mules, donkeys, camels, pigs, chickens, dogs, and other animals. As a chicken, your wings, neck, and feet have been cut off. The blood shed by these animals, each having been cut, is incalculable." He further asked the bhikṣus, "Is form permanent or impermanent?"

佛告诸比丘：「善哉！善哉！汝从往世所受象身，为他截鼻、截耳，或时截足，铁钩［邱－丘＋（卯／亚）］头，及以斩项，所出之血，无量无边。又受牛、马、骡、驴、骆驼、猪、鸡、犬、豕，种种禽兽，如受鸡形，截其羽翼，及其项足，身所出血，是诸禽兽，各被割截，所出之血，不可计量。」复告诸比丘：「色为是常？是无常乎？」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Form is impermanent."

诸比丘白佛言：「世尊！色是无常。」

The Buddha further asked, "If form is impermanent, is it suffering or not suffering?"

佛复问言：「色若无常，为当是苦？为非苦乎？」

The bhikṣus replied, "Because it is impermanent, it is suffering."

比丘对曰：「无常故苦。」

The Buddha further said, "If the impermanent and suffering are the dharmas of decay and destruction, do the noble disciples consider them to be the self or what belongs to the self?"

佛复告言：「若无常苦是败坏法，于此法中，贤圣弟子计有我，及我所不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

比丘对曰：「不也。世尊！」

The Buddha further said, "Are feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness permanent or impermanent?"

佛复告曰：「受、想、行、识为是常耶？为无常乎？」

The bhikṣus replied, "They are all impermanent."

比丘对曰：「斯皆无常。」

The Buddha further asked, "If they are impermanent, are they suffering or not suffering?"

佛复问言：「若是无常，为是苦耶？为非苦耶？」

The bhikṣus replied, "Because they are impermanent, they are suffering."

比丘对曰：「无常故苦。」

The Buddha further asked, "If the impermanent and suffering are subject to destruction, would the noble disciples consider them as self or what belongs to self?"

佛又问言：「若无常苦是败坏法，贤圣弟子宁计是中我、我所不？」

The bhikṣus replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

比丘对曰：「不也。世尊！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Excellent! Excellent! Form is impermanent. Because it is impermanent, it is without self. If there is no self, there is no what belongs to self. Knowing this as it is, one observes with right wisdom. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are also like this. Therefore, bhikṣus, if there is form, even for a short while, past, future, or present, internal or external, near or far, it is all without self and what belongs to self. This is seen as it is by right view. If feeling, perception, volition, or consciousness, whether much or little, internal or external, near or far, past, future, or present, are all without self and what belongs to self, one knows and sees it as it is. When the noble disciples see this, they are called learned. They become disgusted with form, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. Because of disgust, they become detached. Because of detachment, they are liberated. Because of liberation, they attain liberation-knowledge and liberation-vision. If they attain liberation-knowledge and liberation-vision, they know that birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and they will not experience another existence.

佛告比丘：「善哉！善哉！色是无常，无常故即无我，若无有我，则无我所，如是知实正慧观察，受、想、行、识亦复如是。是故比丘，若有是色，乃至少时，过去、未来、现在，若内、若外，若近、若远，此尽无我，及以我所，如是称实正见所见。若受、想，若行、若识，若多、若少，若内、若外，若近、若远，过去、未来、现在，都无有我，亦无我所，如实知见。贤圣弟子见是事已，即名多闻。于色厌恶，受、想、行、识亦生厌恶，以厌恶故得离欲，得离欲故，则解脱。得解脱故，则解脱知见。若得解脱知见，即知我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已办，更不受有。」

When the Buddha spoke this, forty bhikṣus of the Pārāyaṇika clan attained liberation from future rebirth.

佛说是时，四十波利蛇迦比丘不受后有，心得解脱。

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

时诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
