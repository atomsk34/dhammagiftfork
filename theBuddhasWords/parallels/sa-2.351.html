SĀ² 2.351

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 351（三五一）

Parallel to: SN 9.4 / SĀ 1331

At that time, many bhikṣus were staying in the bamboo grove in the Kalandakanivāpa for the summer retreat. There was a deity living in that grove who was worried and said, "Now the saṃgha is about to dismiss the summer retreat on the fifteenth day of the month and leave again."

尔时，众多比丘在俱萨罗园竹林中夏坐安居。彼园林中，有天神住，天神愁念，而作是言：「今僧自恣，月十五日已复欲去。」

Another deity asked him, "Why are you so worried?" and spoke a verse:

更有天神即问之言：「汝今何故愁忧如是？」即说偈言：

Deity, why are you so worried now? The bhikṣus
with pure precepts are about to dismiss the summer
retreat today. Having encountered such an event, you should rejoice.

「天神汝今者，　　何以怀愁忧？净戒诸比丘，　　今日当自恣，得遇如是事，　　宜应自欣悦。」

The deity of the forest replied in verse:

彼林天神以偈答曰：

I also know that they will dismiss the summer retreat today. They are
not without shame and remorse, like the non-Buddhist practitioners. They are all diligent
and have a sense of shame. After putting away their robes and alms
bowls, they will dismiss the summer retreat and disperse. When the bhikṣus have
dispersed, this forest will be empty and without people. There will be nothing
more to hear or see. That is why I am worried and sad.

「我亦知彼等，　　今日当自恣，非是无惭愧，　　同诸外道等。斯等皆精懃，　　具有惭愧者，收敛衣钵已，　　自恣各散去。比丘既散已，　　此林空无人，更无所闻见，　　是故我愁忧。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having dismissed the summer retreat, each dispersed from the forest and returned to their abodes. Then the deity, seeing them disperse, was worried and sad, and spoke a verse:

时诸比丘既自恣已，各散出林，还其所止。尔时，天神见其四散，心怀忧惨，即说偈言：

After the bhikṣus have left, I only see their dwelling
places. The disciples of the Muni are learned and have
knowledge and insight. They are well able to distinguish and
explain various pure teachings. Where have those who uphold the
Dharma gone now?

「诸比丘去已，　　但见游居处，牟尼诸弟子，　　多闻有知见，善能具分别，　　种种清净说。如斯持法人，　　今者安所诣？」

Then another deity spoke a verse:

时余天神复说偈言：

These bhikṣus have scattered in different directions. Some
go to Magadha, some to the Vṛji country
and some also go to Vaiśālī. The bhikṣus
who gather in this forest dwelling are like
wild birds and deer, dwelling without a fixed
place. These bhikṣus abandon worldly affairs and always
seek secluded places, sitting in tranquility and attaining
peace and happiness.

「此诸比丘等，　　四散道不同，有向摩竭提，　　或有诣跋耆，亦复有向彼，　　毘舍离国者。此阿练若处，　　集会诸比丘，譬如野鸟鹿，　　栖止无恒所。此诸比丘等，　　舍弃] cf.【丽】于缘务，常求空闲处，　　静坐得安乐。」
