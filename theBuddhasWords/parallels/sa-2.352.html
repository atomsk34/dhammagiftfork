SĀ² 2.352

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 352（三五二）

Parallel to: SN 9.2 / SĀ 1332

A certain monk, from the country of Kusumapura, went to Kusumapura Forest and stayed there during the day, sleeping. At that time, a deity in the forest thought, "This monk is sleeping in the forest, which is very inappropriate. It is not the way of a monk, and it defiles this forest. I should now awaken him." Having thought this, he went to where the monk was, coughed, snapped his fingers, and spoke a verse:

有一比丘，从俱萨罗国，诣俱萨罗林，于中止住，昼日睡眠。时彼林中，有天神作是念言：「今此比丘处林而睡，甚非所宜，非沙门法，污辱此林。我于今者，当觉悟之。」作是念已，即往其所，［口＊磬］咳弹指，而说偈言：

Hey, monk, get up! Do not indulge in sleep. What is
the point of such sleep? When the body is afflicted with
a serious illness, how can one sleep peacefully? A poisonous arrow
has pierced your heart. How can you sleep while seeking to
pull it out? Since you have already renounced the householder's life
and left behind all worldly affairs, you should fulfill your original
aspiration and not be covered by sleep. Drowsy and unaware, you
lose your former aspirations. The nature of desire is impermanent, agitated
and unstable. The blinking of the eyes cannot be guaranteed. Fools
are deluded and attached to it. Now that you have renounced
the householder's life and left behind the bonds of the household
how can you, having left the bonds, still enjoy sleeping? If
you have not yet cut off desire, your mind is not
yet liberated, and you have not attained the highest wisdom, and
you do not possess these things, you are not called a
renunciant. How can you sleep peacefully, claiming to be a renunciant?
You should diligently practice, day and night without slacking off, firmly
seeking nirvana. If what you seek has not yet been attained
why sleep as a renunciant? Only when you have eliminated ignorance
with wisdom, exhausted all the bonds of defilements, well-tamed your mental
activities, attained the final body, and are able to possess the
above things, can you sleep peacefully.

「咄比丘汝起，　　无得嗜睡眠，如是睡眠者，　　竟有何义利？身遭极重病，　　云何而安眠？毒箭中汝心，　　求拔云何眠？汝既能出家，　　舍离众缘务，当满本愿求，　　勿为睡所覆。［梦－夕＋登］瞢无觉了，　　失于昔所愿，欲体性无常，　　掉动不停住，眴息不可保，　　凡夫愚惑著。汝今已出家，　　离于在家缚，云何离缚已，　　而复乐眠睡？若未断爱欲，　　其心未解脱，未得最上智，　　不具斯事者，不名为出家。　　云何安睡眠，欲称出家法？　　应当勤精进，昼夜不懈惓，　　坚固求涅槃。所求既未获，　　出家为何眠？慧识却无明，　　尽于诸漏结，善调于心行，　　获最后边身，能具如上事，　　乃可安眠睡。」
