SĀ² 2.358

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 358（三五八）

Parallel to: SN 9.14 / SĀ 1338

At that time, there was also a bhikṣu who was staying in the country of Kuśāvatī, relying on that forest. His eyes were unclear, so he asked a doctor to examine him. The doctor told him, "Bhikṣu! If you can smell the fragrance of lotus flowers, your eyes will become clear again." The bhikṣu then believed his words and asked, "Where can I obtain these lotus flowers?" The doctor then replied, "If you want to obtain the fragrance of lotus flowers, you should go to a lotus pond." At that time, the bhikṣu used his words and went to that pond, sitting upright and smelling the fragrance. At that time, a deity saw him doing this and spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，复有一比丘，在俱萨罗国，依止彼林，眼视不明，请医占之。医语之言：「比丘！若能嗅莲华香，眼还得明。」彼比丘即信其言，又语之曰：「我于何处得斯莲花？」医即答言：「汝若欲得莲花香者，当诣莲花池所。」时彼比丘即用其言，至彼池所，端坐嗅香。尔时，天神见其如是，即说偈言：

The flowers born in the pond have a very fragrant scent
You do not see the owner at all. How can you
steal the fragrance of flowers? Now you truly deserve the name
of a thief. Why do you, O great sage, steal that fragrance?

「池中所生华，　　香气甚馝馥，汝都不见主，　　云何偷花香？而汝于今者，　　真实得名盗，大仙汝何故，　　而盗于彼香？」

The bhikṣu spoke a verse in reply,

比丘说偈答言：

O heavenly spirit, you should know that the lotus flowers are born
in the pond. I do not harm the roots and stems, nor
do I steal them. I only smell the fragrance from afar. For
what reason is it called stealing fragrance? I do not accept this statement.

「天神汝当知，　　莲华生池中，我不伤根茎，　　亦不偷盗取，但远嗅香气。　　以何因缘故，名为偷香者？　　我不受此语。」

The heavenly spirit again spoke a verse,

天神复说偈言：

There are fragrant flowers in the pond. Taking them without asking the
owner, the benefactor does not give them. The world calls it stealing
Great sage, you steal the fragrance. It is entirely a crime of stealing.

「池中有香花，　　不问其主取，檀越不施与，　　世人名为盗。大仙汝偷香，　　一向成盗罪。」

At that time, a person came and entered the pond, cutting off the lotus roots and leaves with a sickle, and carrying them away heavily. The bhikṣu, having seen this, again spoke a verse:

时有一人来入此池，以镰芟截莲花根叶，重负而去。比丘见已，复说偈言：

This person enters the pond, cutting and pulling out the lotus
roots and seeds, trampling them in disorder, and carrying them back
heavily. Why do you not stop him and say, 'You are stealing'?

「斯人入池中，　　斩拔花根子，狼籍而践蹈，　　重担而赍归。何故不遮彼，　　语言汝盗取？」

The heavenly spirit spoke a verse in reply:

天神说偈答言：

The person who enters the pond always does evil deeds
It is like a mother with a baby in black
clothes. Although there is drooling and spitting, it is not
seen as defiled. You are like a white and pure
garment, easily accepting stains. Therefore, you are restrained, but you
cannot stop him. Evil people are like black-clad garments, doing
evil without criticism. A small white spot is like a
fly's foot, seen by all people in the world. Even
wise and intelligent people have minor faults, which are also
like this. A black spot on a white shell is
seen from afar by people. If one cuts off afflictions
all karmas are pure. Even a small evil is seen
by people as a mountain.

「彼人入池者，　　恒作于恶业，譬如乳儿母，　　而著于黑衣，虽有诸唌唾，　　都不见污辱。汝如白净衣，　　易受其点污，是故止制汝，　　不能遮于彼。恶人如衣黑，　　造恶不讥呵；鲜白上有点，　　犹如蝇脚等，世人皆共见。　　设诸贤智人，有少微细过，　　其喻亦如是，珂贝上黑点，　　人皆远见之。若断结使者，　　诸业皆洁净，有如毛发恶，　　人见如丘山。」

The bhikṣu also spoke a verse:

比丘复说偈言：

The gods now benefit me, wishing to save and
liberate me. Wherever they see me, they repeatedly awaken me.

「天今利益我，　　为欲拔济故，随所见我处，　　数数觉悟我。」

The heavenly spirit replied in verse:

天神说偈答言：

You did not buy me with money and wealth, nor did
you conquer another country and capture me as a prisoner. You
know the benefits and harm yourself. Who is chasing you and
awakening you? You should now consider yourself, all the matters of
benefit and harm.

「汝不以钱财，　　而用市我得，又不破他国，　　虏掠见擒获。损益汝自知，　　谁逐汝觉悟？汝今应自忖，　　诸有损益事。」
