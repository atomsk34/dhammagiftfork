SĀ² 2.36

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 36（三六）

Parallel to: SN 11.22 / SĀ 1107 / EĀ 45.5

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "In the past, there was a yakṣa whose appearance was very small, his complexion was ugly, his body was black, and people did not like to see him. He sat on the seat of Śakra, the Lord of the Devas. At that time, the thirty-three devas saw this yakṣa sitting in Śakra's place and were all greatly angry and reviled him in various ways. At that time, the evil appearance of the yakṣa gradually disappeared and good features appeared, gradually growing larger. The devas reviled and became more and more angry, and the yakṣa's body became even larger and his complexion became more radiant. The devas went together to Śakra and said to him, 'There is a yakṣa who is extremely ugly, his body is very small, and he sits in Śakra's place. We devas all revile him together, but the yakṣa's complexion becomes better and his body gradually grows larger.' Śakra said, 'There is this yakṣa who receives revilement and his appearance becomes better. It is called 'assisting others in anger.' At that time, Śakra returned to his seat, bared his right shoulder, held an incense burner in his hand, and said to the yakṣa, 'Great Sage! I am Śakra, the Lord of the Devas! I am Śakra, the Lord of the Devas!' He proclaimed his name three times, and the yakṣa became smaller and his appearance became more evil, and then disappeared. Śakra returned to his seat and told the devas, 'From now on, do not give rise to anger. If there is an evil opponent, do not add anger.' He then spoke a verse:

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「昔所有一夜叉，形状甚小，颜色鄙恶，身形又黑，人不喜见，坐帝释座上。尔时，三十三天见是夜叉坐于释处，皆大瞋忿，种种毁骂。尔时，夜叉恶相渐灭，善色转生，渐渐长大。诸天骂詈，瞋恚转多，夜叉遂复身形长大，颜色鲜盛。诸天相将至帝释所，白帝释言：『有一夜叉，极为丑陋，身形甚小，坐帝释处。我等诸天，尽共骂詈，而夜叉子颜色转好，身形渐大。』帝释语言：『有是夜叉，得诸骂詈，形色转好，名助人瞋。』尔时，帝释还向坐所，偏袒右肩，手擎香炉，语夜又言：『大仙！我是帝释，我是帝释。』三自称名，夜叉转小，形色转恶，于是消灭。帝释还复帝释坐，告诸天言：『自今以往，莫生瞋恚。若有恶对，慎莫加瞋。』即说偈言：

If others come to deceive and oppress, do not return the
deception and oppression to them. Toward those who come to harm
give rise to a compassionate mind. Those who are without anger
and harm should always be befriended, for they are the sages
and disciples of the sages. Those who are angry are obstructed
by the mountain of anger. If one is able to restrain
anger when it arises, this is called the good Dharma, like
a bridle controlling a bad horse.

「『若他来侵欺，　　莫还侵欺彼，于来侵害者，　　皆生于慈心。无瞋无害者，　　常应亲近之，彼即是贤圣，　　亦贤圣弟子。诸有瞋恚者，　　为瞋山所障。若有瞋恚时，　　能少禁制者，是名为善法，　　如辔制恶马。』」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Śakra, the Lord of the Devas, dwells in the heavenly realm and enjoys the pleasures of the senses, yet he is still able to control anger. He also always praises those who control anger. How much more so for you bhikṣus who have faith in leaving home life and entering the path, shaving your beards and hair, and donning the Dharma robes? You should not give rise to anger, but praise those who are free from anger. Therefore, bhikṣus, you should train in this way."

佛告诸比丘：「帝释居天王位，受诸欲乐，犹能制瞋，又常赞叹禁制瞋者，况汝比丘，信家非家，出家入道，剃除须发，被服法衣，而不制瞋，赞离瞋者？是故比丘，当如是学。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
