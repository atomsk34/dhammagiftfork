SĀ² 2.364

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 364（三六四）

Parallel to: SN 9.8 / SĀ 1344

At that time, there was a bhikṣu in the country of Kosala who was staying in a forest, and he was a close friend of a certain elder. At that time, the elder had a daughter-in-law who was young and beautiful. The bhikṣu had spoken a few words with her, and many people slandered him, saying it was improper. When the bhikṣu heard these words, he was distressed in his mind and wanted to go to the forest to kill himself. A deity thought, "This bhikṣu is truly without fault. If he were to kill himself in this forest, it would be very improper. I should cause him to gain awakening."

尔时，憍萨罗国有一比丘，林中止住，与一长者共为亲友。是时长者有一儿妇，年少端正，时，此比丘少共语言，众人皆谤，谓为非法。是时比丘闻是语已，心中懊恼，欲向林中而自刑戮。天神念言：「彼比丘实无过患，于此林中，若自刑害，甚为非理。我当令其使得觉悟。」

At that time, this deity immediately transformed into the appearance of the daughter-in-law and went to where the bhikṣu was. When the bhikṣu saw her, he spoke a verse to the transformed daughter-in-law:

时此天神即便化作彼儿妇形，至比丘所。比丘见已，即向化妇而说偈言：

Just like a market at a four-way intersection, a very spacious
place, there are only defiled words, among a group of three
or four people, associating with them gives rise to slander. Having
known this matter, you should quickly leave, do not stay here.

「如市在四衢，　　甚为宽博处，唯有染污语，　　三四人众中，亲近生诽谤。　　汝知是事已，宜应速疾去，　　勿得此间住。」

At that time, the transformed deity again spoke a verse:

时化天神复说偈言：

One who has gone forth should endure slanderous words, and should
not give rise to sorrow and distress. Empty sounds do not
attach to oneself, they are just false words. Examining oneself, if
there is no fault, one should not give rise to distress
Hearing slander and being afraid, how can one dwell in the
deep forest? It is like a wild deer, walking all its
life without standing still. Being able to endure all sounds, good
bad, superior, and inferior, a person of discernment, accomplishing the correct
practice, should not be called a thief-like sage because of the
words of others. You should now examine yourself, if there are
no faults, the sages and gods also know that you are faultless.

「出家应忍受，　　讥毁诽谤言，谤语是不实，　　不宜生愁恼。空声不著己，　　但是虚妄语，自省无过咎，　　不应生恼苦。闻谤而恐畏，　　云何处深林？譬如彼野鹿，　　终身行不立，能忍诸音声，　　善恶上中下，有识之佳人，　　成就具正行，不以他语故，　　得名贼牟尼。汝今自审己，　　既无诸过咎，贤圣及诸天，　　亦知汝无过。」

At that time, the transformed deity, having spoken this verse, immediately disappeared at that place. At that time, the bhikṣu diligently practiced day and night, without slacking off, eliminating afflictions, and attaining arahantship.

时化天神说是偈已，即于其处隐没不现。彼时比丘昼夜精勤，心不懈息，断除烦恼，得成罗汉。
