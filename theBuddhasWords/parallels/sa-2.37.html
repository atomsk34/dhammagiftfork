SĀ² 2.37

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 37（三七）

Parallel to: SN 11.24 / SĀ 1108

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At dawn, the World-Honored One put on his robes, took his alms bowl, and entered the city to beg for food. After eating, he washed his feet, put away his sitting mat, went to the forest of the blind, surveyed it, and sat cross-legged under a tree in a secluded place, abiding in the divine abode. At that time, in the Jītavana Monastery, two monks were arguing during a monastic assembly. One was silent and forbearing, while the other was fiercely angry. The angry one saw his own faults and came to the monk who was silent and forbearing, seeking to repent. But the monk who was silent and forbearing did not accept his repentance. In this way, the monks discussed it among themselves, making a great noise.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，世尊晨朝时到，著衣持钵，入城乞食。食已，洗足，摄坐具，诣得眼林中，遍观察已，于闲静处，在树下，结加趺坐，住于天住。尔时，耆陀精舍中，有二比丘于僧断事时，共生忿诤，一小默然忍，一瞋炽盛。彼炽盛者，自见己过，而来归向默忍比丘，求欲忏悔。默忍比丘，不受其忏。如是展转，诸比丘等共相论说，出大音声。

At that time, the Tathāgata was abiding in the divine abode. With his pure divine ear surpassing that of humans, he heard this sound from afar. He then rose from his seat, went to the assembly of monks, sat in front of the assembly, and addressed the monks, saying, "This morning, I put on my robes, took my alms bowl, entered the city to beg for food, and came to this forest to sit in tranquility. I heard the monks shouting loudly. What are you doing?"

如来尔时住于天住，以净天耳过于人耳，遥闻是声，即从坐起，至于僧中，在于僧前，敷座而坐。佛告诸比丘：「我于今朝著衣持钵，入城乞食，乃至来入林中静坐，闻诸比丘高声大唤，为作何事？」

At that time, the monks then addressed the Buddha, saying, "World-Honored One! In the Jītavana Monastery, two monks were arguing during a monastic assembly. One monk was silent and forbearing, while the other was fiercely angry. The angry monk saw his own faults and came to repent to the monk who was silent and forbearing, but the monk who was silent and forbearing did not accept his repentance. They discussed it among themselves, making a great noise.

尔时，比丘即白佛言：「世尊！耆陀精舍有二比丘僧断事时，共生忿诤。一比丘者，小自默忍；其一比丘，炽盛多语。炽盛比丘，自知己过，归诚忏悔，默忍比丘，不受其忏。展转共道，出大音声。」

The Buddha told the monks, "How foolish! Why do you not accept his repentance? Monks! You should know that in the past, Śakra Devānām-Indra spoke a verse in the assembly of gods in the Sudharma Hall,

佛告比丘：「云何愚痴，不受他忏？诸比丘！当知昔日释提桓因在善法堂诸天众中，而说偈言：

It is like using a ladle to pour ghee to increase
the lamp's flame. The fire burns more fiercely and burns the
ladle itself. The mind of anger is also like this, it
burns one's own wholesome roots. I will never harbor anger, anger
arises and is then dispersed. It is not like the swirling
water, which returns and is endless. Although angry, I do not
speak ill, I do not touch your taboo. Taboo is like
a vital artery, I will never harm it. Having disciplined the
body, there is benefit for oneself. Those without anger and harm
they are the sages. They are also the disciples of the
sages, who should always be close to them. Those who are
angry, the heavy obstacles are like mountains. If one can restrain
anger a little when angry, this is called good karma, like
a bridle controlling a bad horse.

「『譬如用瓢器，　　斟酥以益灯，火然转炽盛，　　反更烧瓢器。瞋心亦如是，　　还自烧善根，我终不含怒，　　瞋已寻复散。不如水漩流，　　回复无穷已，虽瞋不恶口，　　不触汝所讳。所讳如要脉，　　我终不伤害，调伏于身已，　　于己即有利。无瞋无害者，　　彼即是贤圣，亦贤圣弟子，　　常应亲近之。诸有瞋恚者，　　重障犹如山。若有瞋恚时，　　能少禁制者，是名为善业，　　如辔制恶马。』」

The Buddha told the monks, "Śakra Devānām-Indra, dwelling in the position of heavenly king, with self-mastery among the gods, is still able to cultivate patience and praise patience. How much more so for you monks, who have left home, destroyed your appearance, and should not be impatient, but praise patience?"

佛告诸比丘：「释提桓因处天王位，天中自在，尚能修忍，赞叹忍者，况汝比丘，出家毁形，而当不忍赞叹于忍？」

The Buddha having spoken thus, the monks, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them respectfully.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
