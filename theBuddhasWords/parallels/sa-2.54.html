SĀ² 2.54

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 54（五四）

Parallel to: SN 3.22 / SĀ 1227 / EĀ 26.7

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, King Prasenajit was naturally kind and filial. When his mother passed away, he grieved and missed her so much that he could not bear it. After cremating his mother, he bathed himself, with his hair still wet. At noon, he went to where the Buddha was, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. The Buddha said to the king, "Where are you coming from? Your hair and clothes are still wet." King Prasenajit said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! My kind mother was especially respected by me. One day she passed away. I sent my mother's remains far into the wilderness for burial. After finishing the burial, I have just bathed, so my hair and clothes are still wet."

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，波斯匿王禀性仁孝，母初崩背，哀号恋慕，不自堪胜。烧葬母已，便自沐浴，衣发故湿，于日中时，往诣佛所，礼佛足已，在一面坐。佛告王曰：「王从何来？衣发故湿。」波斯匿王白佛言：「世尊！我之慈母情特尊敬，一旦崩背。我送母丧，远至旷野，殡葬已讫，新浴洗故，衣发犹湿。」

The Buddha said to the great king, "Do you love and respect your mother very much?" The king replied, "Indeed I love and respect her. If there is someone who can bring my mother back to life, I would give them my elephant troops, chariot troops, cavalry, and infantry, and would not regret it to continue my mother's life. If I were to give half the country as a reward, I would not regret it." The king said again, "The Buddha's words are true. All who are born will eventually return to death."

佛告大王：「汝于母所极爱敬不？」王即答言：「实为爱敬。设令有人能使我母还得活者，我以象军、车军、马军、步军悉以与之，续我母命，心无悔恨。设以半国赏之，亦不生恨。」王复言曰：「佛语诚实，一切生者，会必归死。」

The Buddha said, "Indeed! Indeed! The born will certainly die. The five destinies and four births all end. Kings, ministers, and brāhmanas will all return to death. The anointed king, with supreme power, ruling the country, will also return to death. The wheel-turning sage king, ruling the four continents, with the seven treasures complete, will also return to death. The five-supernatural-power immortals, dwelling in the mountains and forests, drinking water and eating fruits, will also return to death. The thirty-three celestials, experiencing supreme bliss, with radiant appearance, dwelling in heavenly palaces, with long lifespans, will also return to death. The arhats, having abandoned the heavy burden, having attained their own benefit, having exhausted all bonds, having attained mental freedom, having liberated right wisdom, with their final bodies, will also return to disintegration. The pratyekabuddhas, solitary and without companions, always dwelling in solitude, will also disintegrate. The buddhas, the perfectly enlightened ones, endowed with the ten powers, having the four fearlessnesses, having attained the four unobstructed knowledges, able to roar like lions, their bodies are also impermanent and will return to disintegration." The Buddha said, "Great King! I have explained to the great king in various ways that the born will certainly die. In brief, there is no birth that does not end."

佛言：「实尔！实尔！生必有死，五趣四生，无不终者。王者、臣民、婆罗门众，会归当死。灌顶人王，威力自在，统领国土，会归终没。转轮圣王，王四天下，七宝具足，亦会当死。五通神仙，在于山薮，饮水食果，亦归于死。三十三天，极受快乐，光色炽盛，处天宫殿，寿命延长，亦归终没。诸罗汉等，舍于重担，逮得己利，尽诸有结，心得自在，正智解脱，后边之身，亦归散灭。诸辟支佛，独一无侣，常在闲静，亦当散灭。诸佛正觉，具于十力，有四无畏，得四无碍，能师子吼，身亦无常，会归散灭。」佛言：「大王！我为大王，种种分别，生必有死。略而言之，无生不终。」

The Buddha then spoke a verse, saying:

佛即说偈言：

All that is born will die, life must return to
its end, following karma to receive retribution, good and evil
each reaping their fruits. Cultivating merit one ascends to heaven
doing evil one enters hell. Cultivating the path one cuts
off birth and death, forever entering nirvana. Not in the
sky, not in the ocean, not entering mountains or rocks
there is no place where one can escape death. Even
the buddhas, pratyekabuddhas, bodhisattvas, and sravakas still abandon their impermanent
bodies, how much more so ordinary people.

「一切生皆死，　　寿命必归终，随业受缘报，　　善恶各获果。修福上升天，　　为恶入地狱，修道断生死，　　永入于涅槃。非空非海中，　　非入山石间，无有地方所，　　脱之不受死。诸佛与缘觉，　　菩萨及声闻，犹舍无常身，　　何况诸凡夫。」

At that time, King Prasenajit heard the Buddha's words, his mind opened and his thoughts were resolved. He was no longer sorrowful, and departed joyfully.

时，波斯匿王闻佛所说，心开意解，更不忧愁，欢喜而去。

The Buddha having spoken thus, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
