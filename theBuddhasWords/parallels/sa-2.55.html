SĀ² 2.55

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 55（五五）

Parallel to: SN 3.4 / SĀ 1228

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, King Prasenajit was alone in a quiet place, contemplating: "What is it to love oneself? What is it to hate oneself?" He further thought: "If one acts with good deeds of body, speech, and mind, and avoids all evil, that is called loving oneself. If one acts with unwholesome deeds of body, speech, and mind, and does many evil things, that is called not loving oneself." Having contemplated thus, King Prasenajit rose from his quiet place and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I was alone in a quiet place, contemplating: 'What is it to love oneself? What is it to hate oneself? If one acts with good deeds of body, speech, and mind, that is called loving oneself. If one acts with unwholesome deeds of body, speech, and mind, that is called not loving oneself.'"

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，波斯匿王在空闲处独静思惟：「夫为人者，云何爱己？云何恶己？」复作是念：「若身口意行于善业，远离诸恶，是名] cf.【丽】爱己。若身口意行不善业，作众恶行，名不爱己。」波斯匿王思惟是已，从静处起，往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐，即白佛言：「世尊！我独静处，作是思惟：『云何名爱己？云何不爱己？若能于身口意行善，是名爱己；若身口意行不善业，名不爱己。』」

The Buddha said, "Great King! It is indeed so. If a person acts with evil deeds of body, speech, and mind, that is called not loving oneself. Why is that? For those who do evil, even if they have enemies, they may not be able to harm them quickly; but the self-created evil karma is very deep in harm. Therefore, creating evil karma oneself is called not loving oneself. Moreover, for the sake of oneself, one commits killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct, which is harming oneself. If a person acts with good deeds of body, speech, and mind, even if one thinks, 'I abandon my beloved home, wife, and children,' that is called not loving oneself, but it is truly loving oneself. Why is that? For such a person, even if they have close friends, parents, and siblings, whose kindness penetrates to the bone marrow, when they become old and feeble, they cannot be saved. One must practice good with one's own body, speech, and mind, and be able to save oneself. This is called loving oneself."

佛言：「大王！实尔。若人身口意行恶者，是名不爱己。何以故？彼为恶者，虽有怨雠，不必速能有所伤害；自造恶业，毁害甚深，是以自作恶业，名为不爱己。又有为己故，作杀盗婬，是为损己。若人身口意行善者，设作是念：『我舍所爱居家妻子。』名不爱己，实是爱己。何以故？如此之人，虽有亲友、父母、兄弟，恩彻骨髓，至其衰老，不能得救。要自身口意修行善，能自济度，是名爱己。」

The Buddha then spoke a verse, saying:

佛即说偈言：

If a person loves themselves, they will not harm others with
evil. There is no one who creates evil and attains happiness
If a person loves themselves, they should cultivate good deeds and
quickly attain various kinds of happiness. Those who wish to love
themselves should protect themselves. It is like a border city in
a wilderness with many robbers. When one encounters a time of
no danger, one should hide oneself. If one loses the time
of no danger, the suffering of encountering danger will be endless.

「若人自爱己，　　不以恶加彼，无有造作恶，　　得于快乐者。若人自爱己，　　应修诸善业，速疾能获得，　　种种诸快乐。夫欲爱己者，　　应当自拥护，譬如边表城，　　旷野多贼盗。得值无难时，　　应当自隐藏，若其失无难，　　值难苦无穷。」

The Buddha having spoken this, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
