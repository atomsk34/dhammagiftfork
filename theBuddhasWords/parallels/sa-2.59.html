SĀ² 2.59

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 59（五九）

Parallel to: SN 3.19 / SĀ 1232 / EĀ 23.4

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, King Prasenajit went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! There is an elder named Mahānāman, whose family is very wealthy and has abundant wealth and treasures."

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，波斯匿王往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐，即白佛言：「世尊！有一长者名摩诃南，其家巨富，多饶财宝。」

The Buddha asked the king, "How is he considered wealthy?"

佛问王曰：「云何大富？」

The king said to the Buddha, "That elder's family has tens of millions of gold, silver, and precious treasures, which cannot be measured. How much more so for the rest of their wealth? Although they have wealth, they cannot eat and drink. What they eat is mixed with coarse chaff. When making soup, they boil ginger and stop. After cooking, they take it out to sell for financial use. What they wear is only coarse cloth, loosely and roughly made, as their inner garment. They ride an old and broken cart, and use a tree leaf as an umbrella. They have never seen giving to śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, or poor beggars. When they want to eat, they must first close the door, fearing that śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas will come to beg from them."

王白佛言：「彼长者家，金银珍宝，数千万亿，不可称量，况复余财。虽有财富，不能饮食，所可食者，杂糠麁澁。若作羹时，浑煮姜罢，煮已还取，卖为财用。所可衣者，唯著麁布，五总踈弊，以为内衣。乘朽故车，连缀树叶，以为繖盖。未曾见其施沙门、婆罗门、贫穷乞儿。若欲食时，要先闭门，恐诸沙门、婆罗门等来从其乞。」

The Buddha said, "Great King! Such a person is not a good man. Why? Having obtained such wealth, he cannot open his mind to enjoy it righteously, nor can he support and provide for his wife and children, nor give to his servants and maids, nor regularly give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, nor seek the reward of rebirth in the heavens. It is like a saline field with a little pond of water. Because of its saltiness and bitterness, no one can drink it, and it will eventually dry up. Worldly fools are also like this. Having obtained much wealth and property, they cannot use it to enjoy themselves, nor can they support their parents and wife and children, nor their relatives, servants, maids, friends, and acquaintances. They do not give to them at all. Although they have abundant wealth and treasures, it is of no benefit at all."

佛言：「大王！如此之人，非善丈夫。何以故？得斯财富，不能开意正直受乐，又复不能孝养、供给妻子，亦不赐与奴婢仆使，又不时时施诸沙门、婆罗门，亦复不求上业生天之报。譬如卤地，有少汪水，以咸苦故，无能饮者，乃至竭尽。世间愚夫，亦复如是。大得财业，不能施用身自受乐，亦复末能供养父母及与妻子，并其眷属、奴婢、仆使、亲友知识，悉不惠与，虽丰财宝，都无利益。」

The Buddha said, "Great King! A good man, having obtained wealth and property, can use it himself to enjoy it righteously, and can also support and provide for his teachers, elders, parents, wife and children, and their relatives, servants, maids, friends, and acquaintances, and even give to śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, the poor, and beggars. Such a good person's wealth and treasures are called the highest deeds. They are the cause of happiness and the condition for rebirth in heaven. This person's accumulation of wealth accomplishes great goodness. It is like a clear and cool pond near a city, village, or town, flowing with good water, with level banks on all sides, abundant with various trees, flowers, and fruits, and soft grass covering the ground. All people can bathe and drink there, and flying birds and running beasts can soar and play happily. A good and strong man is also like this, up to rebirth in heaven, accomplishing great goodness."

佛言：「大王！善丈夫者，得于财业，能自施用正直受乐，亦能供养师长父母及与妻子，并其眷属、奴婢仆使、亲友知识，乃至供养沙门、婆罗门、贫穷乞匃，悉能惠施。如斯善人，所得财宝，名为上业。作快乐因生天之缘，此人聚财，成就大善。譬如近城村邑聚落，有清冷池，流出好水，四边平正，多饶林树，种种华果，有柔软草，遍布其地，一切众人，皆得洗浴，并获好饮，飞禽走兽，翱翔嬉乐。善健丈夫，亦复如是，乃至生天，成就大善。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

It is like a salty and barren land, with a cold and stagnant
pond in the middle. The salty and bitter water is undrinkable, and will
eventually dry up. A lazy person is also like this, even if they
have many treasures, they cannot clothe and feed themselves, nor can they give
to others. This is called a lazy person. Having wealth and being able
to give is like a flat and open land, with a good and
clear pond, and the forests are also very lush. People and animals enjoy
happiness together. This is called a wise person. It is like a great
bull king. When born, it experiences happiness, and when it dies, it is
reborn in heaven.

「譬如咸卤土，　　中有冷汪水，咸苦不可饮，　　后自煎涸尽。儜夫亦复尔，　　虽有多财宝，不能自衣食，　　亦不施他人，是名为儜者。　　有财能布施，譬如平博地，　　有好清流池，林亦甚蔚茂，　　人兽同快乐，是名为智者。　　如似大牛王，生则受快乐，　　死则生天上。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and upheld it.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
