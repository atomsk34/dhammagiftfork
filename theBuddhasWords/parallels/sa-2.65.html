SĀ² 2.65

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 65（六五）

Parallel to: SN 3.18 / SĀ 1238 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Thus have I heard:

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，波斯匿王于闲静处，作是思惟：「佛所教法，极有义利，能得现报，无有热恼，不待时节，能将于人到于善处。语诸人言：『汝等来！善示汝妙法。夫为智者，自身取证，深得解达。须善友，须善同伴，恒应亲友如是善友，不向恶友并恶知识，远离恶伴。』」思惟是已，从坐处起，往诣佛所，在一面坐。白佛言：「世尊！我于闲处，作是思惟：『佛所教法，有大义利，能招现报，无诸热恼，不待时节，乃至不与恶友交游。』」

The Buddha said to the king, "It is true, it is true. The teachings of the Buddha are greatly meaningful and beneficial, able to bring immediate results, and so on, up to not associating with bad friends. I was once in the Jīrṇavṛkṣa Forest in Rājagṛha. At that time, the bhikṣu Ānanda was alone in a quiet place, thinking like this: 'A good friend is half of the practice of the Dharma.' Ānanda then came to me, bowed his head to my feet, and said, 'A good friend is half of the practice of the Dharma, not a bad friend, bad companion, or bad acquaintance.' I told Ānanda, 'Stop, stop, do not say that. Why? A good friend, good companion, and good acquaintance are the whole of the practice of the Dharma. Moreover, a good friend and companion does not associate with bad friends, bad acquaintances, or bad companions. Because I was able to escape from birth and death due to good friends. Therefore, know that a good friend is the whole of the practice of the Dharma. Such matters should be distinguished and understood.' The Dharma taught by the Buddha is greatly meaningful and beneficial, able to bring immediate results, and so on, up to not associating with bad friends, bad companions, or bad acquaintances."

佛告王曰：「实尔，实尔。佛所教法，有大义利，能招现报，乃至不与恶伴交游。我于往时，在王舍城耆梨跋提林。尔时，阿难比丘独在静处，作是思惟：『善知识者，梵行半体。』阿难起已，来至我所，顶礼我已，而作是言：『善知识者，梵行半体，非恶知识、恶伴、恶友。』我告阿难：『止！止！莫作是语。所以者何？夫善知识、善友、善伴，乃是梵行全体。又善友伴者，不与恶知识、恶友、恶伴而为徒党。何以故？我以善知识故，脱于生死。是故当知，善知识者，梵行全体。如是之事，应分别知。』佛所说法，有大义利，能招现报，乃至不与恶友恶伴恶知识等而为伴党。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

Among all good dharmas, non-distraction is supreme. If one
is distracted, one is criticized by the sages. If
one is not distracted, one attains the position of
Indra, supreme among the gods. Among all actions and
non-actions, non-distraction is supreme. If one is not distracted
one sits in meditation, exhausts all taints, and attains
the supreme fruit.

「于诸善法中，　　不放逸最胜。若当放逸者，　　贤圣所讥嫌。若不放逸者，　　获于天帝位，于诸天中胜。　　于作无作中，不放逸最胜。　　若不放逸者，坐禅尽诸漏，　　逮得于胜果。」

Having spoken thus, the Buddha finished. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
