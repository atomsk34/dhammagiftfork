SĀ² 2.68

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 68（六八）

Parallel to: SN 3.24 / SĀ 1145

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, King Prasenajit went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! When making offerings, to whom should they be given?" The Buddha replied to the king, "Those who practice giving should give to whomever they wish."

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，波斯匿王往诣佛所，顶礼佛足，在一面坐。白佛言：「世尊！布施之时，应与何处？」佛答王曰：「修布施者，随意所乐，布施于彼。」

The king further asked the Buddha, "Where should offerings be made to obtain great rewards?"

王复问佛：「布施何处，得大果报？」

The Buddha replied to the king, "Your question is different from the previous one. I will now ask you, and you can answer as you wish. When going into battle, if there is a kṣatriya in the east who is strong and capable, but does not know the art of war, cannot skillfully control his body, and is afraid of the enemy, always retreating first and unable to stay where he is, his arrows do not go far, and even if he shoots, he never hits the target, and is not fit to be in the midst of the great army - if the king has such a person in battle, will the king comfort him, saying, 'You are close to me, I will heavily reward you'? Can the king do this?"

佛答王曰：「汝问异前。我今问汝，随汝意答。如出军阵，欲鬪战时，如东方有刹利，盛壮有力，然其不能善解兵法，亦复不知善调其身，及以射术，畏惮前敌，生于惊惧，每常先退，不能住其所止之处；射箭不远，设复射箭，终无所中，不堪处彼大阵之中。大王若鬪战时，有如此人，王当安慰，汝亲近我，当重赏赐。王能尔不？」

The king said, "World-Honored One! I truly do not need such a person. Why? Because he is not needed in battle."

王言：「世尊！我实不用如是之人。何以故？鬪战之时，所不须故。」

"If there is a brahmin in the south, a vaiśya in the west, and a śūdra in the north, who are also like this, will the king use such people?"

「南方有婆罗门，西方有毘舍，北方有首陀，亦复如是。如此人等，王当用不？」

The king said, "They are all not needed in battle."

王言：「若鬪战时，皆所不用。」

The Buddha said, "When fighting, if a kṣatriya from the east comes, in the prime of his youth, with a strong and healthy body, brave and powerful, skilled in military arts, and also knowledgeable in archery and various other techniques, with many manual skills, well able to control his body, brave in facing the enemy, greatly bold and fearless, with no fear or fright in his mind, not retreating when seeing the enemy, staying where he is, bending his bow and shooting from afar, able to hit objects, his arrows never missing their mark, bravely advancing straight ahead, able to break through large formations. Great King, when fighting, which one would you use?"

佛言：「若鬪战时，东方有刹利来，年在盛壮，身体丁大，骁勇有力，善解兵法，兼知射术，种种诸术，多诸手伎，善能调身，勇于向敌，大胆不惧，心无惊畏，见敌不退，住所住处；弯弓远射，能中于物，箭不虚发，勇捍直进，能坏大阵。大王若鬪战时，当用何者？」

The king replied to the Buddha, "I would use the brave and strong one. Why? The art of fighting requires bravery and strength. The same is true for the south, west, and north."

王答佛言：「用勇健者。何以故？鬪战之法，须勇健故。南西北方，亦复如是。」

The Buddha told the great king, "So it is, great king! If there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who lack the five branches, they are not worthy of being fields of merit. If there are those who are fully endowed with the five branches, they are worthy of being fields of merit. Giving to them yields great fruits, great benefits, and extreme prosperity, with the rewards increasing and expanding. What are the five branches? Eliminating the five hindrances. How does one eliminate the five hindrances? Eliminating the hindrances of desire, anger, sloth and torpor, restlessness and remorse, and doubt. Knowing oneself to have eliminated the five desires is called eliminating the five hindrances. How does one fulfill the five branches? Fulfilling the five branches of the non-learner: morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation. If one can fulfill these five branches, the śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas will receive great fruits from giving, which is called greatly blazing, with profound and vast rewards.

佛告大王：「如是。大王！若有沙门、婆罗门，五支不具，不任福田。复有五支满足，堪任福田，施得大果，得大利益，极为炽盛，果报增广。云何名为具于五支？断除五盖。云何] cf.【丽】断除五盖] cf.【丽】？断除欲盖、瞋恚、睡眠、调悔及疑。自知除五欲，名断除五盖。云何满足五支？满足无学戒、定、慧、解脱、解脱知见。若能满足如是五支，沙门、婆罗门，施得大果，名大炽然，果报深广。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

It is like a person who is brave and strong, and
also skilled in archery, proficient in all arts. In battle, such
a person is needed, and should be richly rewarded with wealth
and rank, without regard to his social class, but only for
his merit. The great king should be like this. If one
can practice good deeds, be gentle and cultivate patience, and be
able to see the Four Noble Truths, one can enter the
noble state. One should not choose the social class of the
wise and learned when making offerings to them. There should be
food and drink, as well as bedding and bedding, for all
who dwell there. Such offerings should be made to those who
uphold the precepts. In the great flood, one should make rafts
and boats, and build bridges, to ferry oneself and others across
The wise and learned are like dense clouds that cover the
world, with lightning flashing brightly, thunder rumbling in the distance, and
heavy rain pouring down, moistening the land everywhere, so that all
the grasses, trees, and forests are not left unmoistened. When the
crops grow lush, the farmers rejoice. In the same way, the
faithful benefactors, who are learned and able to give, are without
stinginess or jealousy. The moistening is likened to food and drink
and the encouragement and benefit are like the distant rumbling of
thunder, which is like the pouring rain, resulting in a great
harvest of fruits. Those who are able to practice giving will
greatly gain merit and later attain the bliss of nirvāṇa.

「譬如有一人，　　骁勇有大力，兼善解射术，　　众技悉备知。鬪战须此人，　　当厚赐财宝，并与其爵赏，　　不择其种姓，但录其功勋，　　大王应如是。若能行善者，　　柔和修忍辱，能见四真谛，　　得入于圣位。供养黠慧者，　　不应择种姓，住处悉应有，　　饮食并卧具，如此之供养，　　应有具戒者。于大洪流中，　　应作浮囊栰，并造作桥船，　　自渡亦济他。安直多闻者，　　譬如有密云，遍覆于世界，　　电光甚赫曜，雷音声远震，　　降注于大雨，土地普沾洽，　　众卉木丛林，无不蒙润者。　　禾稼既滋茂，农夫生悦庆，　　如是信施主，多闻能惠施，　　无有悭嫉者。润泽喻饮食，　　劝让益进与，如雷音远震，　　譬如降注雨，大获于子实。　　能修布施者，大获于功德，　　后得涅槃乐。」

The Buddha spoke this, and the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
