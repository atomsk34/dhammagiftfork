SĀ² 2.70

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 70（七〇）

Parallel to: SN 3.25 / SĀ 1147

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. King Prasenajit, in the middle of the day, came to where the Buddha was in his carriage, his body covered in dust. At that time, the World-Honored One asked the king, "Great King! Why have you come here in the middle of the day, your body covered in dust?"

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。波斯匿王于日中时，乘驾辇舆，往诣佛所，身体尘坌。尔时，世尊而问王言：「大王！何故以日中来至于此，身体尘坌？」

The king said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! The affairs of the country are vast and numerous. After finishing the affairs of the people, I came to where the Buddha is. That is why my body is covered in dust."

王白佛言：「世尊！国事广大，众务猥多，断理庶讫，来诣佛所，以是之故，身体坌尘。」

The Buddha said, "Great King! I will now ask you, please answer as you wish. Great King! Suppose someone comes from the east, with an honest nature, never deceitful, trusted by the people. If he tells the king, 'Now in the east there is a huge stone mountain, reaching up to the heavens, down to the earth. Coming from the east, wherever it passes, all forests, plants, and living beings will be crushed.' The south, west, and north are also like this. All the people deeply believe him and say to each other, 'Now the four directions have huge stone mountains, all coming at once. There are no holes to escape through. All the living beings of the heavens, dragons, humans, and ghosts will be crushed. It is very frightening.'" The Buddha told the king, "At that time, what means would you use to escape the danger?"

佛言：「大王！我今问汝，随汝意答。大王！譬如有人从东方来，禀性正直，未曾虚欺，为众所信。设语王言：『今者东方有大石山，上连于天，下连于地。从东方来，其所历处所，有林卉有生之类，悉皆摧碎。』南西北方，亦复如是，皆为众人，深生信心，亦相谓言：『今者四方有大石山，一时俱至，又无孔穴可逃避处，天龙人鬼，有生之类，咸皆碎灭，甚可怖畏。』」佛告王曰：「当于尔时，设何方计而得免难？」

The king said, "World-Honored One! At that time, there would be no other means. Only by believing in the Buddha's teachings and practicing true practices would there be no other way."

王言：「世尊！当尔之时，更无方计，唯信佛法，修行真行，更无余方。」

The Buddha said, "Great King! As you have said, except for believing in the Buddha's teachings, there is no other means. Great King! Why do you say this?"

佛言：「大王！如王所说，乃至除信佛法，更无余计。大王！何故作如是语？」

King Prasenajit said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! If it is like the coronation and assuming the throne, with elephant troops, cavalry, chariot troops, and infantry, each fully armed, such a great mountain would be invincible. Swords, arrows, bows, and spears would be of no use. If using spells, money, and offerings, such things would be of no avail. There would also be no place to seek fame, compete in strength, and dispute victory. Therefore, World-Honored One! I say one should cultivate good dharmas, avoid falsehood, and except for believing in the Buddha's teachings, there is no other means."

波斯匿王白佛言：「世尊！设如灌顶受王位者，象兵、马兵、车兵、步兵，各严战具，如此大山，无可共鬪，刀箭弓矟，无可用处。若以呪术，钱财贡献，如此之事，无如之何，亦复无有求名捔力诤胜之处。是故世尊！我言应修善法，远离虚妄，除信佛法，更无余计。」

The Buddha said, "Great King! Great King! The mountain of old age can destroy the youthful appearance, the mountain of sickness can destroy all strength and health, the mountain of death can destroy all life, the mountain of decline can destroy all glory and wealth, the loss of wife and children, the separation of relatives, and the loss of money and wealth. Great King! There are these four mountains that destroy the world, following after people. It is indeed as you have said. Only by practicing true Dharma, except for the Buddha's teachings, there is no other means."

佛言：「如是大王！如是大王！老山能坏壮年盛色，病山能坏一切强健，死山能坏一切寿命，衰耗之山能坏一切荣华富贵，妻子丧没，眷属分离，钱财亡失。大王！有如是四方碎坏世间，随逐于人，实如汝言，唯有修行真法，除于佛法，更无余计。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说颂曰：

It is like the four great mountains from the four directions, vast and deep, with no
boundaries. Coming from the four directions at the same time, there is no place to flee
Elephant chariots and horse troops cannot resist them, and spells, treasures, and wealth cannot turn them
away. Great King! The mountain of impermanence, the mountain of old age, sickness, and death, and
the mountain of decline and destruction, destroy all living beings. Kshatriyas, Shudras, Brahmins, and even the
lowest Chandalas, those at home, those who have left home to practice purity, and those who
uphold the precepts to those who violate them, all are destroyed without remainder. Therefore, the wise
should cultivate goodness, honor the Three Jewels, and perform various merits. The body, speech, and mind
should always be pure, so that one can gain fame in the present life and be
reborn in heaven in the future.

「譬如四方有大山，　　广大深厚无涯际，从四面来一时至，　　慞惶奔走无避处，象车马兵不能拒，　　呪术财宝不能却，如是大王无常山，　　老病死山衰灭山，残灭一切有生类，　　刹利首陀婆罗门，乃至下贱真陀罗，　　在家出家修梵行，及以全戒至毁禁，　　悉皆残灭无遗余。是以智人应修善，　　尊崇三宝行众福，身口及意常清净，　　现得名誉后生天。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
