SĀ² 2.71

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 71（七一）

Parallel to: SN 3.11 / Ud 6.2 / SĀ 1148

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, King Prasenajit went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. At that time, there were seven long-haired Brahmins, seven naked Nirgranthas, and seven one-clothed ascetics in the Jeta Grove, all of them tall and large. King Prasenajit saw the non-Buddhist ascetics walking back and forth in the Jeta Grove. At that time, King Prasenajit rose from his seat, joined his palms respectfully, faced the non-Buddhist ascetics, and said, "I am King Prasenajit." He said this three times. The Buddha said to King Prasenajit, "Why do you see these long-haired, naked, and one-clothed people with such respect?"

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，波斯匿王往诣佛所，稽首佛足，在一面坐。时，祇洹中有长发梵志七人，复有裸形尼干七人，复有一衣外道七人，身皆长大。波斯匿王见诸外道在祇洹中，经行彷徉。时，波斯匿王从坐而起，合掌敬心，向诸外道，自说：「我是波斯匿王。」如是三说。佛告波斯匿王：「何故见此长发裸形一衣之人如此恭敬？」

The king said, "World-Honored One! These three people in my country are the most supreme among arhats."

王言：「世尊！我国有此三人，阿罗汉中，名为最胜。」

The Buddha said to the king, "You do not understand the intentions of others. How can you know who is an arhat and who is not? Only by living with them for a long time and observing them can you know. Even if you live with them for a long time, the wise can know whether they keep the precepts or break them, but the foolish cannot. If their parents, relatives, and family members die, you can distinguish them. If there is no such thing, it is difficult to know. If they encounter difficulties and are forced by others to commit murder, or if they are forced by a woman in private and do not break the precepts, you can know their firmness. Only by following and observing them can you know their purity or impurity. If you want to test their wisdom, listen to what they say. Only the wise can distinguish. Only the wise can know after living with them for a long time."

佛告王言：「汝不善知他心所趣，云何知是罗汉非罗汉也？如共久处，用意观察，尔乃可知。持戒破戒，虽复久处，聪智能知，愚者不知。若其父母，亲里眷属，有死亡者，可分别知。若无此事，难可了知。若遭厄难，为人强逼，令行杀害。或为女人私处逼迫，而不犯戒。可知坚实。随逐观察，乃能可知，净行不净，欲试其智，听其所说，唯有智者，善能分别。唯有智人，久处共住，尔乃可知。」

The king praised the Buddha, saying, "Excellent! World-Honored One! As the Buddha has said, only by living with them for a long time can one know whether they keep the precepts or break them. When faced with difficulties, one can distinguish them by observing their conduct and knowing their purity or impurity. Only by discussing and debating can one distinguish their wisdom and ignorance. The Buddha has said that only the wise can know after living with them for a long time, not immediately. Why is this? The people I send are also made to wear such attire. I send them far to other countries to observe the people there. They may stay for eight months or ten months, doing various things, before returning to the country. They indulge in the five desires and do everything as before, without any difference. Therefore, I know that what the Buddha has said is truly well-said." The king said again, "World-Honored One! I also knew about this before, but I did not observe it and immediately gave rise to respect.

王即赞佛言：「善哉！世尊！如佛所说，久处共住，尔乃可知。持戒破戒，临难别人，观察其行，知净不净，讲说议论，乃别其智凡，此上说有智能知，愚者不知。久处乃知，非可卒知。何以故？我所使人，亦使著如是形服，使远至他国，察彼国中，或经八月，或至十月，作种种事已，还来归国。五欲自恣，一切所作，如前无异。是以知佛所说实是善说。」王又白言：「世尊！我亦先知有如是事，但卒不观察，便起恭敬。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

One cannot observe and know by seeing their appearance. If one
suddenly sees a person, one cannot immediately trust them. Their appearance
may resemble an arhat, but they do not truly control their
faculties. Their various physical appearances and conduct cannot be distinguished. It
is like painting an ear pot or painting a coin. The
ignorant think it is gold, but inside it is actually copper
Such people are ignorant and know nothing. Externally they resemble the
virtuous, but internally their minds are truly poisonous and evil. When
walking, they often bring many followers, showing themselves to be virtuous and superior.

「不以见色貌，　　而可观察知，若卒见人时，　　不可即便信。相貌似罗汉，　　实不摄诸根，形貌种种行，　　都不可分别。如似涂耳铛，　　亦复如涂钱，愚者谓是金，　　其内实是铜。如是诸人等，　　痴暗无所知，外相似贤善，　　内心实毒恶。行时多将从，　　表于贤胜者。」

The Buddha having spoken thus, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and upheld it.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
