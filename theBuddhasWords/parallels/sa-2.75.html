SĀ² 2.75

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 75（七五）

Parallel to: SN 7.2

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, there was a young brahmin named Nirgrantha who went to where the Buddha was and, in the presence of the Buddha, spoke ill of the World-Honored One with his mouth and mind, slandering and harassing him in various ways. At that time, the Tathāgata, having seen and heard this, said to Nirgrantha, "It is like in the world, on the day of the great festival, when the moon is full. On that night, did you give clothes, jewelry, and various delicacies to your relatives and friends?"

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。时有摩纳名曰卑嶷，往诣佛所，面于佛前，不善口意，骂詈世尊，加诸诽谤种种触恼。尔时，如来见闻是已，语卑嶷言：「譬如世间，于大节会，锯无提日。当于其夜，汝于彼时，颇以衣服、璎珞、种种肴饍，饷亲戚不？」

Nirgrantha replied, "Indeed, I did give them."

卑嶷答言：「实尔！饷与。」

The Buddha said to Nirgrantha, "If they did not accept your gifts, to whom do these gifts belong?"

佛告卑嶷：「若彼不受汝之所饷，此饷属谁？」

Nirgrantha replied, "If they did not accept, I would take them back myself."

卑嶷答言：「若彼不受，我还自取。」

The Buddha said, "So it is, Nirgrantha! You have directly reviled and slandered the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and caused various troubles. Although you have given to me, I do not accept it. It is like when people give something to someone else, and the recipient accepts it, this is called giving and receiving. If someone gives, but the recipient does not accept, this is called giving, but not receiving. If someone curses, angers, beats, or slanders, and then retaliates, this is called giving and receiving. If someone curses, angers, beats, or slanders, and endures it without retaliating, this is called giving, but not receiving."

佛言：「如是，如是。卑嶷！汝于如来、至真、等正觉所，面加骂辱，作诸谤毁，种种触恼。汝虽与我，我不受取。譬如世人，有所舍与，前者受取，是名舍与，亦名受取。有人虽施，前人不受，是名为舍，不名为受。若人骂詈、瞋打、毁呰，更还报者，是名为舍，是名为受。若人骂詈、瞋打、毁呰，忍不加报，是名为舍，不名为受。」

Nirgrantha said, "Gautama! I have heard the elders say that if there is a Buddha, a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Perfectly Enlightened One, then even if one curses in front of him, he will not be annoyed. Now I curse you, and you are annoyed."

卑嶷言：「瞿昙！我闻先旧长老宿德咸作是言：『世若有佛、无上正真、等正觉，面前骂詈，终不生恼。』我今骂汝，汝便生恼。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

For one who is free from anger, where can anger arise?
For one who is disciplined and lives righteously, know that there
is no anger. If anger is not repaid with anger, it
is difficult to conquer in battle; if one does not retaliate
that is called supreme. Non-anger is superior to anger, practicing goodness
is superior to unwholesomeness; giving is superior to stinginess, truthful speech
is superior to falsehood; those who are non-angry and non-harming are
always with the sages. Those who associate with evil people accumulate
anger like a mountain. Anger is like a wild horse, controlled
by reins; the reins are not yet firm, controlling the mind
is called firm. Therefore, I am now called a well-trained mount.

「无有瞋恚者，　　于何而得瞋？调顺正命者，　　无瞋汝当知。若瞋不报瞋，　　鬪战难为胜；若不加报者，　　是则名为上。不瞋胜于瞋，　　行善胜不善；布施胜悭贪，　　实言胜妄语；不瞋不害者，　　常与贤圣俱。近诸恶人者，　　积瞋如丘山。瞋恚如狂马，　　制之由辔勒；辔勒未为坚，　　制心乃名坚。是故我今者，　　名为善调乘。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and upheld it.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
