SĀ² 2.86

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 86（八六）

Parallel to: AN 3.51 / AN 3.52 (~) / SĀ² 2.87 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, there was an old brāhman who was aged and had long been committing various evils, extremely coarse and rude, violating the prohibitions, not believing in the merit of goodness, not first creating merit, and having nothing to rely on at the time of death. He went to where the Buddha was, greeted the Buddha, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I have long been committing various evils, extremely coarse and rude, violating the prohibitions, unable to cultivate merit, and also unable to cultivate goodness. I have also been unable to first create merit and virtue, and at the time of death, I have nothing to rely on." The Buddha said, "It is indeed as you say." The old brāhman said, "Excellent! Gautama! Please explain it to me, so that I may attain peace and happiness for a long time, attain meaning and benefit."

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，有一老婆罗门年耆根熟，先于往日，多造众恶，极为麁弊，毁犯所禁，不信福善，不先作福，临终之时，无所依止，往诣佛所，问讯佛已，在一面坐，而白佛言：「世尊！我于往日，多造众恶，极为麁弊，毁犯所禁，不能修福，又不修善，亦复不能先作福德，临终之时，无所依止。」佛言：「实如汝语。」老婆罗门言：「善哉！瞿昙！当为我说，使我长夜获于安乐，得义得利。」

The Buddha said, "It is indeed as you say. You have long been committing various evils, extremely coarse and rude, violating the prohibitions, unable to cultivate merit, and also unable to cultivate goodness. You have also been unable to first create merit and virtue, and at the time of death, you have nothing to rely on. You are now truly old and decrepit, having committed many crimes, doing coarse and evil deeds, not creating meritorious karma, not cultivating good practices, and unable to create them in advance. At the time of fear, you have no place to return to. It is like a person who is about to die, thinking of escaping, entering a good house to protect himself. Such a thing is completely impossible. Therefore, you should now cultivate good practices with your body, and the same with your speech and mind. If the three karmas are good, at the time of death, they are the house to escape to."

佛言：「实如汝说，汝于往日，身口意业不作善行，毁犯禁戒，不修福德，不能先造，临命终时，作所怙恃。汝于今者，实为衰老，先造众罪，所作麁恶，不造福业，不修善行，不能先造，可畏之时，所归依处。譬如有人，将欲死时，思愿逃避，入善舍宅，以自救护，如是之事，都不可得。是故今当身修善行，意口亦然，若三业善，临命之时，即是舍宅，可逃避处。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

The lifespan of a person is short, and they will surely be handed over to
death. When invaded by old age, there is no one who can save them. Therefore
one should fear death. Only by entering the Buddha's teachings can one have a place
to return to. If one cultivates good dharmas, that is the place to return to.

「人生寿命促，　　必将付于死。衰老之所侵，　　无有能救者。是以应畏死，　　唯有入佛法。若修善法者，　　是则归依处。」

Having spoken this, the Buddha continued, "The second sutra is without distinction. One should seek a place to take refuge. The great ones should cultivate goodness." The third section has a different prose and verse. The verse says:

佛说是已，第二经无差别，应求归依处，大人宜修善。第三长行别偈则不同，偈言：

Youth and old age, the three periods have all passed. The
remaining life is short, always afflicted by old age. Approaching the
realm of Yama, the Brahmin wishes to be reborn. There is
no dwelling place between the two. You have no provisions at
all. You should make a small bright lamp, relying on diligence
Remove the afflictions in front, and do not be reborn with
old age and death again.

「壮盛及衰老，　　三时皆过去，余命既无几，　　常为老所患。近到阎王际，　　婆罗门欲生，二间无住处，　　汝都无资粮。应作小明灯，　　依凭于精勤，前除于诸使，　　不复生老死。」

This is the classical Chinese root text of Saṃyuktāgama (2nd), a parallel to the Pali sutta(s) listed above. Source: CBETA (Taishō edition).
