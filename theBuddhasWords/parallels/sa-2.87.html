SĀ² 2.87

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 87（八七）

Parallel to: AN 3.51 (~) / AN 3.52 / SĀ² 2.86 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī. At that time, an old brāhman went to where the Buddha was, greeted the Buddha, and sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I am now old and decrepit. Since the past, I have committed many evils, never doing any merit, never cultivating goodness. Moreover, I have not practiced the Dharma of avoiding fear and seeking protection. Excellent, Gautama! Please teach the Dharma for me, so that when I die, I will have someone to protect me, a place to return to, a refuge, and a place to escape to."

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，有一老婆罗门往至佛所，问讯佛已，在一面坐，白佛言：「世尊！吾今朽迈，往昔已来，造作众恶，未曾作福，未更修善，又所不行离于怖畏救护之法。善哉！瞿昙！为我说法，使我命终有所救护、屋宅、归依、逃避之处。」

The Buddha told the brāhman, "The world is burning. What is burning? It is old age, sickness, and death. For this reason, one should cultivate goodness with the body, speech, and mind. You have not cultivated goodness with body, speech, and mind. If you can now cultivate goodness with body, speech, and mind, that will be your boat and ferry, and even at the time of death, it will be able to protect you, be your house, your refuge, and your place of escape."

佛告婆罗门：「世间炽然。何谓炽然？谓老病死。以是之故，应身修善，口意亦然。汝都不修身口意善，汝今若能于身口意修于善者，即是汝之船济，乃至死时，能为汝救护，为汝屋宅，为汝归依、逃避之处。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

It is like a house on fire, burning the house. One
should quickly take out the valuables and place them in a
place without fire. The fire of birth, old age, sickness, and
death burns sentient beings. One should cultivate giving and relieve the
poor. The gold and treasures of the world are encroached upon
by kings, thieves, water, and fire. At the time of death
they are all abandoned and do not follow one. Giving follows
one and does not abandon one, like a strong and secure
treasure. Kings, thieves, and water and fire cannot encroach upon it
Stinginess and not giving is called constant sleep. Cultivating giving to
relieve the needy is called awakening.

「譬如失火家，　　焚烧于屋宅，宜急出财宝，　　以置无火处。生老病死火，　　焚烧于众生，宜应修惠施，　　赈众于贫穷。世间金宝等，　　王贼水火侵，死时悉舍离，　　无有随人者。施逐人不舍，　　犹如坚牢藏，王贼及水火，　　无能侵夺者。悭贪不布施，　　是名常睡眠；修施济匮乏，　　是名为觉悟。」

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说是已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
