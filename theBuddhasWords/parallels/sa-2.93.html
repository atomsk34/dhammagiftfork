SĀ² 2.93

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 93（九三）

Parallel to: SN 7.10 / SĀ 1179

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was in the great forest of the country of Vaishali. At that time, the Tathāgata put on his robe, took his alms bowl, entered the city to beg for food, finished eating, put away his robe and bowl, washed his feet, sat under a tree, and abided in the divine abode. At that time, there was a brahmin named Uccala Turagha who had lost a lactating cow. He searched everywhere for six days, not knowing where the cow was. He continued to search and came to the great forest. He saw from afar the Tathāgata sitting under a tree, with a distinguished appearance, his faculties tranquil, his mind and thoughts serene, having attained the supreme state of tranquility, like a golden tower, his majestic radiance shining brightly. Having seen this, he went to where the Buddha was and spoke a verse in front of the Buddha:

一时，佛在毘舍离国大林之中。尔时，如来著衣持钵，入城乞食。食讫，摄其衣钵，并复洗足，坐一树下，住于天住。时，有一婆罗门名欝凑罗突逻阇，失产乳牛，遍处推求，经于六日，不知牛处，次第求觅，趣大林中，遥见如来在树下坐，容貌殊特，诸根寂定，心意恬静，获于最上调伏之意，如似金楼，威光赫然。见是事已，往诣佛所，即于佛前而说偈言：

How does a monk enjoy solitude and tranquility, and what does he gain from such contemplation?

「云何比丘乐独静，　　如是思惟何所得？」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，世尊说偈答言：

I have no sorrow or worry about gain or loss
Do not think that I am the same as you.

「我于诸得失，　　都无有愁忧，汝莫谓于我，　　与汝等无异。」

At that time, the brāhman again spoke a verse:

时，婆罗门复说偈言：

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke various teachings to the brahmin, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting him, as extensively described above, until he exhausted all afflictions and did not accept future existence. At that time, the Venerable Uttara Toraṇādatta attained arahantship, attained the joy of liberation, and joyfully spoke a verse:

尔时，世尊为婆罗门种种说法，示教利喜，广说如上，乃至尽诸有结，不受后有。时，尊者欝凑罗突逻阇得阿罗汉，得解脱乐，踊跃欢喜，而说偈言：

Now I am extremely happy and joyful, with the Dharma taught by the Great Sage. Hearing the Dharma
I have attained understanding, and have completely let go of all grasping and rejecting. It is not in
vain to see the World-Honored One, and to encounter the Buddha and attain the fruit of the path.

「今我极憘乐，　　大仙所说法，闻法得解悟，　　都无诸取舍，不虚见世尊，　　遇佛获道果。」
