SĀ² 2.94

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 94（九四）

Parallel to: SN 7.22 / SĀ 1180

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was in the village of the Śālavana brahmins. At dawn, the World-Honored One put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and was about to enter the Śālavana village to beg for alms. There was an untimely cloud that brought rain, and the Tathāgata took shelter from the rain and went to the village. At that time, the brahmins and elders of the village were gathered together discussing something. Seeing the Buddha coming from afar, they said, "What kind of Dharma does the shaven-headed monk know?" Hearing their words, the Buddha told them, "Among you brahmins, there are those who know the Dharma and those who do not. The same is true for the kṣatriyas and householders.

一时，佛在娑罗婆罗门聚落。尔时，世尊于其晨朝，著衣持钵，欲入娑罗聚落乞食。有非时云起，天降于雨，如来避雨至彼聚落。时，聚落中，婆罗门长者共集论处，遥见佛来，咸作是言：「剃发道人知何等法？」佛闻其言，即告之曰：「汝婆罗门有知法者，不知法者。刹利居士，亦复如是。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

One should not make one's friends and relatives feel humiliated. The king
should not force those who should not be forced to submit. A
wife should not force her husband to submit. When parents grow old
their children should respect and support them, and should not be rebellious
There is no place where there are no wise and good people
There is no good man who does not speak the Dharma. Cutting
off greed, anger, and delusion, what is spoken is all in accordance
with the Dharma.

「终不于亲友，　　令其生屈伏，王者亦不取，　　不应伏者伏。妻不求夫伏，　　父母衰老至，子应致敬养，　　不宜生勃逆。无有众聚处，　　而无贤良人，无有善丈夫，　　而不说法语。断于贪瞋痴，　　所说皆如法。」

At that time, the brāhmaṇas said, "You are well-versed in the brāhmaṇa Dharma. Come and enter this assembly." They immediately prepared a seat and invited the Buddha to take his seat, saying, "Please teach the Dharma for us. We wish to listen." At that time, the World-Honored One entered the assembly, took his seat, and for them expounded various teachings, showing, teaching, benefiting, and delighting them. He spoke a verse, saying:

时，诸婆罗门言：「汝善知婆罗门法，来入此众。」即时敷座，请佛就座：「为我说法，我等乐听。」尔时，世尊入其众中，坐其座上，为说种种法，示教利喜，而说偈言：

If one is silent and says nothing, one cannot know whether
one is foolish or wise. It is only through speech that
one can then distinguish. If one speaks the wonderful Dharma, speaking
the Dharma leads to nirvāṇa. Therefore, one should speak, blazing the
lamp of the Dharma. The banner of sages and saints is
established through speech. Speech is the banner of the sages. Therefore
one should not remain silent.

「若默无所说，　　莫知其愚智，要因于言说，　　然后乃别知。若说妙法者，　　说法趣涅槃，是以应言说，　　炽然于法灯。已立仙圣幢，　　皆由于言说，言说即圣幢，　　是以不应嘿。」

Having spoken thus, the Buddha rose from his seat and departed.

佛说是已，从坐而去。
