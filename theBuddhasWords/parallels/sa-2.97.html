SĀ² 2.97

Saṃyuktāgama (2nd)别译杂阿含经

SA-2 97（九七）

Parallel to: SN 7.18 / SĀ 1183

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was wandering in the country of Kosala, staying in a shala forest. At that time, there was a brahmin not far from the forest, with five hundred young brahmins studying under him. The brahmin often thought, "When will the World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, come to this forest? I will go and ask him to resolve my doubts." The brahmin then sent the young brahmins to the forest to gather firewood for making fire offerings. When the young brahmins arrived at the forest, they saw the Tathāgata sitting under a tree, with an upright and extraordinary appearance, a peaceful and harmonious countenance, like a golden tower shining brightly. When the young brahmins saw the Tathāgata, they carried the firewood back and said to their teacher, "Teacher, you have always wanted to see the Buddha. Now the Tathāgata is near this forest. If you want to see him, you should know the time." When the brahmin heard these words, he went to where the Buddha was, inquired about his well-being, and sat to one side. He then spoke a verse, saying:

一时，佛游拘萨罗国，在娑罗林。时有一婆罗门去林不远，五百摩纳，从其受学。时彼婆罗门每念世尊如来何时来游此林，当往咨问，释我疑滞。时婆罗门遣诸摩纳，诣林采薪，欲以祀火。时诸摩纳既至林已，见于如来在树下坐，端正殊特，容貌和奕，如真金楼，晖光赫然。时诸摩纳覩如来已，寻负薪归，白其师言：「和上昔日，每思见佛，今者如来近在此林，若欲见者，宜知是时。」时婆罗门闻是语已，即诣佛所，问讯起居，在一面坐。即说偈言：

In the deep forest, so lush and dense, so very
frightening, why do you sit alone in meditation, cultivating concentration
without fear? And there are no musical instruments to entertain
yourself, why do you enjoy solitary dwelling? It is truly
unprecedented. Do you seek to be the great Brahma, the
lord of the world? Do you seek to be Sakra
the honored one of the thirty-three heavens? Why do you
enjoy being alone in the frightening deep forest? Always practicing
austerities, what do you seek to attain?

「深林极茂盛，　　其中甚可畏，何故独宴坐，　　修禅无惧心？又无众音乐，　　可以娱自身，云何乐闲居？　　实为未曾有。汝为求大梵，　　世界自在主？为求于帝释，　　三十三天尊？何故乐独处，　　可畏深林中？常修于苦行，　　将欲何所求？」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply,

尔时，世尊说偈答言：

Those who are attached to desires have many doubts and
uncertainties. In countless realms, each gives rise to attachment. All
afflictions arise from ignorance. I have cut off the root
of ignorance, spit out the mud of desire and dried
it up. I have completely cut off all seeking and
have no deceit. In all wholesome dharmas, I have realized
and attained purity. Having attained the unsurpassed path, I cultivate
concentration and detachment from desire.

「著有所欲者，　　多怀诸疑惑，于无数境界，　　各各生染著。一切诸结使，　　皆因无智起，我断无智根，　　吐结欲埿干。悉断于志求，　　亦无诸谄曲，于诸善法中，　　证知得清净，正得无上道，　　修禅离欲者。」

At that time, the brāhman again spoke a verse,

尔时，婆罗门复说偈言：

I now bow my head in homage and take refuge in the
Venerable Sage, who is self-mastery in all dhyānas and has realized immeasurable
enlightenment. Among devas and humans, he is the most honored, endowed with
the thirty-two marks, upright and without equal, like the king of snow
mountains. He attained liberation in the forest but is not attached to
the forest. The one who is pure and liberated, without birth, has
pulled out the poisonous arrow. The Dharma spoken by the Tathāgata is
supreme among all treatises. His words are the foremost. The lion's roar
among humans expounds the four noble truths, widely liberating all. He himself
is free from great suffering and also liberates all beings, causing them
all to attain peace and happiness. Please teach this Dharma for me
I now take refuge and pay homage, attaining liberation on the other
shore. The one who is free from all fears, please come and
dwell in this forest. Now I have encountered the great guide of
devas and humans, who can eliminate all the suffering and distress of sentient beings.

「我今稽首礼，　　归依牟尼尊，于诸禅自在，　　解悟无量觉。于天人中尊，　　具三十二相，端正无与等，　　犹彼雪山王。于林得解脱，　　而不著于林，清净解脱者，　　无生拔毒箭。如来所说法，　　于诸论中上，言说最第一。　　人中师子吼，敷演四真谛，　　广度于一切，自离于大苦，　　亦度诸群生，咸令] cf.【丽】得安乐，　　愿为说此法，我今归命礼，　　得度于彼岸。离诸怖畏者，　　善来住此林。今我得值遇，　　天人大导师，能除灭众生，　　一切诸苦恼。」

At that time, after the brāhman had spoken this verse, he departed joyfully.

尔时，婆罗门说是偈已，欢喜而去。
