SĀ 101

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 101（一〇一） 人间

Parallel to: AN 4.36 / SĀ² 2.267 / EĀ 38.3 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was traveling among the people of Kosala, and he sat under a tree between the two villages of Kāti and Dāruvana, entering the right concentration during the day.

一时，佛在拘萨罗人间游行，有从迦帝聚落、堕鸠罗聚落二村中间，一树下坐，入昼正受。

At that time, a Brahmin of the bean-grinding caste was traveling along that road. He saw the Buddha's footprints with the thousand-spoked wheel marks, complete with spokes, rims, and all the auspicious signs. Seeing this, he thought, "I have never seen such footprints in the human realm. I shall now follow the footprints to find the person." He then followed the footprints to where the Buddha was. He saw the World-Honored One sitting under a tree, entering the right concentration during the day. His dignified appearance was extraordinary, his faculties were clear and still, his mind was peacefully concentrated, he was the foremost in discipline, his right contemplation was accomplished, and his radiant appearance was majestic, like a golden mountain. Seeing this, he said, "Is this a god?"

时，有豆磨种姓婆罗门随彼道行，寻佛后来，见佛脚迹千辐轮相，印文显现，齐辐圆辋，众好满足。见已，作是念：「我未曾见人间有如是足迹，今当随迹以求其人。」即寻脚迹至于佛所，来见世尊坐一树下，入昼正受，严容绝世，诸根澄静，其心寂定，第一调伏，正观成就，光相巍巍，犹若金山。见已，白言：「为是天耶？」

The Buddha told the Brahmin, "I am not a god."

佛告婆罗门：「我非天也。」

"Are you a dragon, yaksha, gandharva, asura, garuda, kinnara, mahoraga, human, or non-human?"

「为龙、夜叉、干闼婆、阿修罗、迦楼罗、紧那罗、摩睺罗伽、人、非人等？」

The Buddha told the Brahmin, "I am not a dragon, nor a human or non-human."

佛告婆罗门：「我非龙乃至人、非人也。」

The Brahmin said to the Buddha, "If you are not a god, dragon, human, or non-human, then what are you?"

婆罗门白佛：「若言非天、非龙，乃至非人、非非人，为是何等？」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，世尊说偈答言：

Gods, dragons, gandharvas, kinnaras, yakshas, asuras, mahoragas, humans
non-humans, and so forth, all arise from afflictions
Such afflictions and defilements, I have all abandoned
destroyed, and extinguished, like a lotus born. Although
born in water, it is never stained by
water. Although I am born in the world
I am not stained by the world. Throughout
eons, I have always chosen pure suffering without
temporary happiness. All conditioned activities are all born
and extinguished. Free from defilements, unshakable, having already
pulled out all sword wounds, having ultimately eliminated
birth and death, therefore called Buddha.

「天龙干闼婆，　　紧那罗夜叉，无善阿修罗，　　诸摩睺罗伽，人与非人等，　　悉由烦恼生，如是烦恼漏，　　一切我已舍，已破已磨灭，　　如芬陀利生，虽生于水中，　　而未曾著水，我虽生世间，　　不为世间著，历劫常选择，　　纯苦无暂乐，一切有为行，　　悉皆生灭故，离垢不倾动，　　已拔诸剑刺，究竟生死除，　　故名为佛陀。」

Having spoken this sutra, the Buddha said to the Brahmin of the Douma clan, "Listen well, listen well, and contemplate it carefully." The Brahmin replied, "Yes, I will listen."
