SĀ 1028

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1028（一〇二八） 疾病

Parallel to: SN 36.7

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Thus have I heard:

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。时，有众多比丘集于伽梨隶讲堂。时，多有比丘疾病。

At that time, the World-Honored One, in the afternoon, arose from meditation and went to the Gārikā assembly hall. In front of the great assembly, he spread out his seat and sat down. Having sat down, he addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "You should wait for the time with right mindfulness and right wisdom. This is my instruction for you to follow. Bhikṣus! What is right mindfulness? It means that a bhikṣu contemplates the body internally with mindfulness of the body as a basis for spiritual practice, diligently applying himself with right mindfulness and right wisdom to subdue worldly greed and sorrow; contemplating the body externally, contemplating the body both internally and externally, contemplating feelings internally, externally, and both internally and externally, contemplating the mind internally, externally, and both internally and externally, contemplating phenomena internally, externally, and both internally and externally with mindfulness of phenomena as a basis for spiritual practice, diligently applying himself with right mindfulness and right wisdom to subdue worldly greed and sorrow. This is called right mindfulness for a bhikṣu.

尔时，世尊晡时从禅觉，往至伽梨隶讲堂，于大众前敷座而坐。坐已，告诸比丘：「当正念正智以待时，是则为我随顺之教。比丘！云何为正念？谓比丘内身身观念处，精勤方便，正念正智，调伏世间贪忧；外身身观念处、内外身身观念处，内受、外受、内外受，内心、外心、内外心，内法、外法、内外法法观念处，精勤方便，正念正智，调伏世间贪忧，是名比丘正忆念。

What is right wisdom? It means that a bhikṣu, when walking or standing, is aware of it, and when looking or observing, bending or stretching, bowing or raising the head, holding robes or alms bowl, walking, standing, sitting, lying down, sleeping, waking, up to fifty or sixty, is aware of it in accordance with speech or silence. This is called right wisdom for a bhikṣu.

「云何正智？谓比丘若来若去，正知而住，瞻视观察，屈申俯仰，执持衣钵，行、住、坐、卧、眠、觉，乃至五十、六十，依语默正智行。比丘！是名正智。

Thus, bhikṣus, those who abide in right mindfulness and right wisdom can give rise to pleasant feelings, which have causes and conditions, not without causes and conditions. What are the causes and conditions? It means contemplating this body as conditioned and arising from mental causes and conditions; pleasant feelings are also conditioned and arising from mental causes and conditions. Contemplating the body and pleasant feelings as impermanent, contemplating their arising and ceasing, contemplating their detachment, contemplating their cessation, contemplating their relinquishment, contemplating the impermanence of the body and pleasant feelings, up to their relinquishment, if there are any afflictions of craving and desire for the body and pleasant feelings, they will be forever eliminated.

「如是，比丘！正念正智住者能起乐受，有因缘，非无因缘。云何因缘？谓缘于身，作是思惟：『我此身无常、有为、心因缘生；乐受亦无常、有为、心因缘生。』身及乐受观察无常，观察生灭，观察离欲，观察灭尽，观察舍彼，观察身及乐受无常，乃至舍已，若于身及乐受贪欲使者永不复使。

Thus, bhikṣus, right mindfulness and right wisdom give rise to feelings of suffering, which have causes and conditions, not without causes and conditions. What are the causes and conditions? It means contemplating this body as conditioned and arising from mental causes and conditions; feelings of suffering are also conditioned and arising from mental causes and conditions. Contemplating the body and feelings of suffering as impermanent, up to their relinquishment, if there are any afflictions of anger and resentment towards the body and feelings of suffering, they will be forever eliminated.

「如是，正念正智生苦受因缘，非不因缘。云何为因缘？如是缘身，作是思惟：『我此身无常、有为、心因缘生；苦受亦无常、有为、心因缘生。』身及苦受观察无常，乃至舍，于此及苦受瞋恚所使，永不复使。

Thus, bhikṣus, right mindfulness and right wisdom give rise to feelings that are neither painful nor pleasant, which have causes and conditions, not without causes and conditions. What are the causes and conditions? It means contemplating this body as conditioned and arising from mental causes and conditions; feelings that are neither painful nor pleasant are also conditioned and arising from mental causes and conditions. Contemplating the body and feelings that are neither painful nor pleasant as impermanent, up to their relinquishment, if there are any afflictions of ignorance and delusion towards the body and feelings that are neither painful nor pleasant, they will be forever eliminated.

「如是，正念正智生不苦不乐受因缘，非不因缘。云何因缘？谓身因缘，作是思惟：『我此身无常、有为、心因缘生；彼不苦不乐受亦无常、有为、心因缘生。』彼身及不苦不乐受观察无常，乃至舍，若所有身及不苦不乐受无明使使，使永不复使。多闻圣弟子如是观者，于色厌离，于受、想、行、识厌离，厌离已离欲，离欲已解脱，解脱知见：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

When experiencing pleasant feelings, one cannot know pleasant feelings, being bound
by the fetter of craving, one does not see liberation. When
experiencing painful feelings, one cannot know painful feelings, being bound by
the fetter of anger, one does not see the path of
liberation. The Buddha has taught that neither painful nor pleasant feelings
are also like this, they cannot be known, and one will
not cross over to the other shore. If a bhikṣu diligently
cultivates right wisdom without wavering, he will be able to know
all feelings. Having known all feelings, he will exhaust all taints
in this very life, and will attain nirvāṇa without falling into
the category of existence.

「乐觉所觉时，　　莫能知乐觉，贪欲使所使，　　不见于出离。苦受所觉时，　　莫能知苦受，瞋恚使所使，　　不见出离道。不苦不乐受，　　等正觉所说，彼亦不能知，　　终不度彼岸。若比丘精勤，　　正智不倾动，于彼一切受，　　黠慧能悉知。能知诸受已，　　现法尽诸漏，依慧而命终，　　涅槃不堕数。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and upheld it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
