SĀ 103

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 103（一〇三） 差摩

Parallel to: SN 22.89

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, a group of senior monks were staying at the Ghosita Monastery in the country of Kosambi.

一时，有众多上座比丘住拘舍弥国瞿师罗园。

At that time, the monk Cāma was staying at the Bhadrāvana Monastery in the country of Kosambi, suffering from a serious illness.

时，有差摩比丘住拘舍弥国跋陀梨园，身得重病。

At that time, the monk Tāsā was attending to the sick monk. He went to the senior monks, bowed at their feet, and stood to one side.

时，有陀娑比丘为瞻病者。时，陀娑比丘诣诸上座比丘，礼诸上座比丘足，于一面住。

The senior monks told the monk Tāsā, "Go to the monk Cāma and say, 'The senior monks ask if you are feeling better, if your illness is not getting worse.'"

诸上座比丘告陀娑比丘言：「汝往诣差摩比丘所，语言：『诸上座问汝，身小差安隐，苦患不增剧耶？』」

The monk Tāsā received the instructions of the senior monks and went to the monk Cāma, saying, "The senior monks ask if your illness is gradually improving, if the suffering is not increasing."

时，陀娑比丘受诸上座比丘教，至差摩比丘所，语差摩比丘言：「诸上座比丘问讯汝，苦患渐差不？众苦不至增耶？」

The monk Cāma said to the monk Tāsā, "My illness is not improving, my body is not at ease, and the suffering is increasing without relief. It is like a strong man taking a weak person, tying their head with a rope, and tightly strangling their hands. The pain is extremely great. My pain now is worse than that. It is like a butcher cutting open the belly of a cow with a sharp knife and taking out its internal organs. How can the cow's belly bear the pain? My belly pain now is much worse than that cow's. It is like two strong men holding a weak person and hanging them over a fire, burning their two feet. My two feet are now hotter than that."

差摩比丘语陀娑比丘言：「我病不差，不安隐身，诸苦转增无救。譬如多力士夫，取羸劣人，以绳继头，两手急绞，极大苦痛，我今苦痛有过于彼。譬如屠牛，以利刀生割其腹，取其内藏，其牛腹痛当何可堪！我今腹痛甚于彼牛。如二力士捉一劣夫，悬著火上，烧其两足，我今两足热过于彼。」

At that time, the monk Tāsā returned to the senior monks and fully informed them of the condition of the monk Cāma's illness.

时，陀娑比丘还至诸上座所，以差摩比丘所说病状，具白诸上座。

At that time, the senior monks sent the monk Tāsā back to the monk Cāma and said to the monk Cāma, "The World-Honored One has said that there are five aggregates of grasping. What are the five? The form aggregate of grasping, the feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness aggregates of grasping. Can you, Cāma, observe these five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine?"

时，诸上座还遣陀娑比丘至差摩比丘所，语差摩比丘言：「世尊所说，有五受阴。何等为五？色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴，汝差摩能少观察此五受阴非我、非我所耶？」

At that time, the monk Tāsā, having received the instructions of the senior monks, went to inform the monk Cāma, saying, "The senior monks tell you that the World-Honored One has spoken of the five aggregates of grasping. Can you observe them as not-self and not mine?"

时，陀娑比丘受诸上座比丘教已，往语差摩比丘言：「诸上座语汝，世尊说五受阴，汝少能观察非我、非我所耶？」

The monk Cāma said to the monk Tāsā, "I can observe those five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine."

差摩比丘语陀娑言：「我于彼五受阴能观察非我、非我所。」

The monk Tāsā returned and reported to the senior monks, "The monk Cāma said, 'I can observe those five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine.'"

陀娑比丘还白诸上座：「差摩比丘言：『我于五受阴能观察非我、非我所。』」

The senior monks again sent the monk Tāsā to tell the monk Cāma, "Can you observe those five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, like an arhat who has exhausted the taints?"

诸上座比丘复遣陀娑比丘语差摩比丘言：「汝能于五受阴观察非我、非我所，如漏尽阿罗汉耶？」

At that time, the monk Tāsā received the instructions of the senior monks and went to where the monk Cāma was, and said to Cāma, "Can a monk who can observe the five aggregates of grasping in this way be like an arhat who has exhausted the taints?"

时，陀娑比丘受诸上座比丘教，往诣差摩比丘所，语差摩言：「比丘能如是观五受阴者，如漏尽阿罗汉耶？」

The monk Cāma said to the monk Tāsā, "I observe the five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, but I am not an arhat who has exhausted the taints."

差摩比丘语陀娑比丘言：「我观五受阴非我、非我所，非漏尽阿罗汉也。」

At that time, the monk Tāsā returned to where the senior monks were and said to the senior monks, "The monk Cāma said, 'I observe the five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, but I am not an arhat who has exhausted the taints.'"

时，陀娑比丘还至诸上座所，白诸上座：「差摩比丘言：『我观五受阴非我、非我所，而非漏尽阿罗汉也。』」

At that time, the senior monks said to the monk Tāsā, "You should go back and tell the monk Cāma, 'What you said, "I observe the five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, but I am not an arhat who has exhausted the taints," contradicts your previous statement.'"

时，诸上座语陀娑比丘：「汝复还语差摩比丘：『汝言：「我观五受阴非我、非我所，而非漏尽阿罗汉。」前后相违。』」

The monk Tāsā accepted the instructions of the senior monks and went to tell the monk Cāma, "What you said, 'I observe the five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, but I am not an arhat who has exhausted the taints,' contradicts your previous statement."

陀娑比丘受诸上座比丘教，往语差摩比丘：「汝言：『我观五受阴非我、非我所，而非漏尽阿罗汉。』前后相违。」

The monk Cāma said to the monk Tāsā, "As for my observation of the five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, but not being an arhat, it is because I have not yet cut off, known, separated from, and eliminated the conceit of 'I,' the desire for 'I,' and the defilement of 'I'."

差摩比丘语陀娑比丘言：「我于五受阴观察非我、非我所，而非阿罗汉者，我于我慢、我欲、我使，未断、未知、未离、未吐。」

The monk Tāsā returned to the senior monks and said to them, "The monk Cāma said, 'As for my observation of the five aggregates of grasping as not-self and not mine, but not being an arhat who has exhausted the taints, it is because I have not yet cut off, known, separated from, and eliminated the conceit of 'I,' the desire for 'I,' and the defilement of 'I' regarding the five aggregates of grasping'."

陀娑比丘还至诸上座所，白诸上座：「差摩比丘言：『我于五受阴观察非我、非我所，而非漏尽阿罗汉者，于五受阴我慢、我欲、我使，未断、未知、未离、未吐。』」

The senior monks again sent the monk Tāsā to tell the monk Cāma, "You say there is a self. Where is this self? Is form the self, or is the self different from form? Are feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness the self, or is the self different from consciousness?"

诸上座复遣陀娑比丘语差摩比丘言：「汝言有我，于何所有我？为色是我？为我异色？受、想、行、识是我？为我异识耶？」

The monk Cāma said to the monk Tāsā, "I do not say that form is the self, or that the self is different from form; that feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are the self, or that the self is different from consciousness. However, regarding the five aggregates of grasping, I have not yet cut off, known, separated from, and eliminated the conceit of 'I,' the desire for 'I,' and the defilement of 'I'."

差摩比丘语陀娑比丘言：「我不言色是我，我异色；受、想、行、识是我，我异识。然于五受阴我慢、我欲、我使，未断、未知、未离、未吐。」

The monk Cāma said to the monk Tāsā, "Why bother sending you back and forth? Take a staff and I will support it myself. I will go to the senior monks and ask for the staff." The monk Cāma then supported the staff himself and went to the senior monks.

差摩比丘语陀娑比丘言：「何烦令汝駈駈往反？汝取杖来，我自扶杖，诣彼上座，愿授以杖。」差摩比丘即自扶杖，诣诸上座。

At that time, the senior monks saw from afar that the monk Cāma was coming with a staff, and they themselves spread out seats, set up footstools, went out to greet him, took his robes and bowl, and ordered him to take a seat. After exchanging greetings, they said to the monk Cāma, "You say you have conceit of 'I.' What do you see as the self? Is form the self, or is the self different from form? Are feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness the self, or is the self different from consciousness?"

时，诸上座遥见差摩比丘扶杖而来，自为敷座，安停脚机，自往迎接，为持衣钵，命令就座，共相慰劳。慰劳已，语差摩比丘言：「汝言我慢，何所见我？色是我耶？我异色耶？受、想、行、识是我耶？我异识耶？」

The monk Cāma replied, "Form is not the self, nor is the self different from form. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are not the self, nor is the self different from consciousness. I have not yet cut off, known, separated from, or eliminated the conceit of 'I,' the desire for 'I,' and the defilement of 'I' in regard to the five aggregates of clinging. It is like the fragrance of blue lotus, red lotus, white lotus, and jasmine flowers. Is the fragrance the root, or is the fragrance different from the root? Is the fragrance the stem, leaves, anthers, or the fine and coarse parts, or is the fragrance different from the fine and coarse parts? Can you explain this equally?"

差摩比丘白言：「非色是我，非我异色；非受、想、行、识是我，非我异识。能于五受阴我慢、我欲、我使，未断、未知、未离、未吐。譬如优钵罗、钵昙摩、拘牟头、分陀利华香，为即根香耶？为香异根耶？为茎叶须精麤香耶？为香异精麤耶？为等说不？」

The senior monks replied, "No, monk Cāma! The root of the blue lotus, red lotus, white lotus, and jasmine flowers is not the fragrance, nor is the fragrance different from the root. The stem, leaves, anthers, and fine and coarse parts are not the fragrance, nor is the fragrance different from the fine and coarse parts."

诸上座答言：「不也，差摩比丘！非优钵罗、钵昙摩、拘牟头、分陀利根即是香、非香异根，亦非茎叶须精麤是香，亦非香异精麤也。」

Monk Cāma asked again, "What kind of fragrance is it?"

差摩比丘复问：「彼何等香？」

The senior monks replied, "It is the fragrance of the flowers."

上座答言：「是华香。」

Monk Cāma said again, "It is the same for me. I am not form, yet I am not apart from form; I am not feeling, perception, formation, or consciousness, yet I am not apart from consciousness. However, I see the five aggregates of clinging as not being me or mine, yet I have not yet abandoned, known, separated from, or eliminated the conceit of 'I', the desire for 'I', and the defilements of 'I'. Senior monks, please allow me to give an analogy. The wise understand through analogies. It is like a wet nurse's garment given to a laundryman. It is washed with various ash and lye, but still has a lingering smell. It must be scented with various fragrances to eliminate the smell. In the same way, the learned noble disciple, having abandoned the five aggregates of clinging, correctly observes them as not being me or mine, yet is unable to abandon, know, separate from, or eliminate the conceit of 'I', the desire for 'I', and the defilements of 'I'. Only then, with regard to the five aggregates of clinging, does he progress in contemplation, observing their arising and ceasing. This is form, this is the arising of form, this is the cessation of form. This is feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. This is the arising of consciousness, this is the cessation of consciousness. Having observed in this way the arising and ceasing of the five aggregates of clinging, he completely eliminates the conceit of 'I', the desire for 'I', and the defilements of 'I'. This is called true correct observation."

差摩比丘复言：「我亦如是。非色即我，我不离色；非受、想、行识即我，我不离识。然我于五受阴见非我、非我所，而于我慢、我欲、我使，未断、未知、未离、未吐。诸上座听我说譬，凡智者，因譬类得解。譬如乳母衣，付浣衣者，以种种灰汤，浣濯尘垢，犹有余气，要以种种杂香，薰令消灭。如是，多闻圣弟子离于五受阴，正观非我、非我所，能于五受阴我慢、我欲、我使，未断、未知、未离、未吐，然后于五受阴增进思惟，观察生灭，此色、此色集、此色灭，此受、想、行、识，此识集、此识灭。于五受阴如是观生灭已，我慢、我欲、我使，一切悉除，是名真实正观。」

When monk Cāma spoke this Dharma, those senior monks became free from defilements and attained the purity of the Dharma eye. Monk Cāma, without any further defilements, attained liberation of the mind. Due to the joy and benefit of the Dharma, his physical illness was completely cured.

差摩比丘说此法时，彼诸上座远尘离垢，得法眼净。差摩比丘不起诸漏，心得解脱，法喜利故，身病悉除。

At that time, the senior monks said to monk Cāma, "We have already understood and delighted in what you said earlier. The reason we asked is to elicit your subtle eloquence, not to disturb you. You are indeed capable of expounding the Dharma of the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One.

时，诸上座比丘语差摩比丘言：「我闻仁者初所说，已解已乐，况复重闻！所以问者，欲发仁者微妙辩才，非为娆乱汝，便堪能广说如来、应、等正觉法。」

At that time, the senior monks, having heard what monk Cāma said, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

时，诸上座闻差摩比丘所说，欢喜奉行。
