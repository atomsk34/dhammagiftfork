SĀ 1064

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1064（一〇六四） 提婆

Parallel to: SN 17.35–36 / AN 4.68 / SĀ² 2.3 / EĀ 12.7 (~) / EĀ 23.7

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, Devadatta had gained support and offerings. King Ajātaśatru of Magadha, son of Vaidehī, would visit Devadatta every day with five hundred carriages, and would bring five hundred cauldrons of food every day to offer to Devadatta. Devadatta would accept these offerings with a separate group of five hundred people.

尔时，提婆达多有利养起，摩竭陀王阿阇世毘提希子日日侍从五百乘车，来诣提婆达多所，日日持五百釜食，供养提婆达多，提婆达多将五百人别众受其供养。

At that time, many bhikṣus put on their robes in the morning, took their alms bowls, entered Rājagṛha to beg for food, and heard that Devadatta had gained such support and offerings, and that he accepted them with a separate group of five hundred people. After begging for food, they returned to the monastery, put away their robes and bowls, washed their feet, went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! We put on our robes in the morning, took our alms bowls, entered Rājagṛha to beg for food, and heard that Devadatta had gained such support and offerings, and that he accepted them with a separate group of five hundred people."

时，有众多比丘晨朝著衣持钵，入王舍城乞食，闻提婆达多有如是利养起，乃至五百人别众受其供养。乞食已，还精舍，举衣钵，洗足毕，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我等晨朝著衣持钵，入王舍城乞食，闻提婆达多有如是利养起，乃至五百人别众受其供养。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Do not praise the support and offerings that Devadatta has gained. Why is that? Devadatta accepts separate support and offerings, which will now destroy him and also destroy him in the next world. It is like a banana tree, bamboo, or reed, which dies after bearing fruit and is also destroyed the following year; Devadatta is also like this, accepting support and offerings, which will destroy him in this world and also destroy him in the next world. It is like a mule, which dies after giving birth; Devadatta is also like this, accepting various support and offerings, which will destroy him in this world and also destroy him in the next world. That foolish Devadatta will gain long-lasting harm and suffering for as long as he accepts support and offerings. Therefore, bhikṣus, you should train like this: 'Even if we gain support and offerings, do not give rise to defilement and attachment.'" At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke a verse, saying:

佛告诸比丘：「汝等莫称是提婆达多所得利养。所以者何？彼提婆达多别受利养，今则自坏，他世亦坏。譬如芭蕉、竹、芦，生果即死，来年亦坏；提婆达多亦复如是，受其利养，今世则坏，他世亦坏。譬如駏驉，受胎必死；提婆达多亦复如是，受诸利养，今世亦坏。他世亦坏，彼愚痴提婆达多随几时受其利养，当得长夜不饶益苦。是故，诸比丘！当如是学：『我设有利养起，莫生染著。』」尔时，世尊即说偈言：

Banana trees bear fruit and die, bamboo and reeds also wither after
fruiting; mules die from pregnancy, and scholars are ruined by greed. Always
practicing unrighteousness, much knowledge does not avoid foolishness. The good Dharma is
daily diminished, and when the stem withers, the root is also injured.

「芭蕉生果死，　　竹芦实亦然，駏驉坐妊死，　　士以贪自丧。常行非义行，　　多知不免愚，善法日损减，　　茎枯根亦伤。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "Good, good!" The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
