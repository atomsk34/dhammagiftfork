SĀ 1076

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1076（一〇七六） 陀骠

Parallel to: Ud 8.9

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, Venerable Dīghanakha went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stepped back to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I wish to attain parinirvāṇa in front of the Buddha."

尔时，尊者陀骠摩罗子诣佛所，稽首佛足，退住一面，白佛言：「世尊！我愿于佛前取般涅槃。」

The World-Honored One remained silent. He asked this three times, and the Buddha told Dīghanakha, "This is the way of conditioned phenomena.

世尊默然。如是三启，佛告陀骠摩罗子：「此有为诸行，法应如是！」

At that time, Venerable Dīghanakha immediately entered samādhi in front of the Buddha. As he entered samādhi, he rose into the sky in the eastern direction and manifested the four dignified deportments of walking, standing, sitting, and lying down. He entered the fire samādhi, emitting fire from his body, with the entire body blazing with light and flames in blue, yellow, red, white, and crystal-like colors. Fire emitted from the lower part of his body burned his body, while water emitted from the upper part of his body sprinkled his body. Sometimes fire emitted from the upper part of his body burned the lower part of his body, while water emitted from the lower part of his body sprinkled the upper part of his body. He manifested various transformations in the ten directions. Then, in the sky, he emitted fire from his body and burned himself to parinirvāṇa, without remainder, extinguishing and pacifying, leaving no trace. It was like a lamp in the sky, with the oil and wick both extinguished. Dīghanakha's parinirvāṇa in the sky, with body and mind both extinguished, was also like this. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，尊者陀骠摩罗子即于佛前，入于三昧，如其正受，向于东方，升虚空行，现四威仪，行、住、坐、卧，入火三昧，身下出火，举身洞然，光焰四布，青、黄、赤、白、颇梨红色。身下出火，还烧其身，身上出水，以洒其身，或身上出火，下烧其身，身下出水，上洒其身。周向十方，种种现化已，即于空中，内身出火，还自焚其身，取无余涅槃，消尽寂灭，令无遗尘。譬如空中然灯，油炷俱尽，陀骠摩罗子空中涅槃，身心俱尽，亦复如是。尔时，世尊即说偈言：

It is like a burning iron ball, its flames blazing
brightly, the heat gradually subsiding and extinguishing, one does not
know where it goes. Such is liberation, crossing the mire
of afflictions, the various flows forever severed, one does not
know where it goes. Attaining the immovable trace, entering nirvāṇa without remainder.

「譬如烧铁丸，　　其焰洞炽然，热势渐息灭，　　莫知其所归。如是等解脱，　　度烦恼淤泥，诸流永已断，　　莫知其所之。逮得不动迹，　　入无余涅槃。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "The bhikṣus should practice in this way." The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
