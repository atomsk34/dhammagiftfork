SĀ 1079

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1079（一〇七九） 丘冢

Parallel to: MN 23 / SĀ² 2.18 / EĀ 39.9

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu went to the bank of the Toraṇavatī River in the latter part of the night, took off his robe and placed it on the bank, entered the water to bathe, and after bathing, returned to the bank, put on one robe, and waited for his body to dry.

时，有异比丘于后夜时至搨补河边，脱衣置岸边，入水洗浴。浴已，还上岸，著一衣，待身干。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu went to the bank of the Toraṇavatī River in the latter part of the night, took off his robe and placed it on the bank, entered the water to bathe, and after bathing, returned to the bank, put on one robe, and waited for his body to dry.

时，有一天子放身光明，普照搨补河侧，问比丘言：「比丘！比丘！此是丘冢，夜则起烟，昼则火然，彼婆罗门见已而作是言：『坏此丘冢，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』又见大龟，婆罗门见已，作是言：『除此大龟，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有氍氀，婆罗门见已，作此言：『却此氍氀，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有肉段，彼婆罗门见已，作是言：『除此肉段，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有屠杀，婆罗门见已，作是言：『坏是屠杀处，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有楞耆，彼婆罗门见已，作是言：『却此楞耆，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有二道，彼婆罗门见已，作是言：『除此二道，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有门扇，婆罗门见已，作是言：『却此门扇，发掘者智，持以刀剑。』见有大龙，婆罗门见已，作是言：『止！勿却大龙，应当恭敬。』比丘！汝来受此论，往问世尊，如佛所说，汝随受持。所以者何？除如来，我不见世间诸天、魔、梵、沙门、婆罗门于此论心悦乐者，若诸弟子从我所闻，然后能说。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard this treatise from that god, went to the World-Honored One, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. He asked the World-Honored One in detail about the treatises that had been asked by the god: "What is a burial mound for monks? What is meant by 'at night it emits smoke'? What is meant by 'during the day it burns with fire'? What is meant by 'brāhmaṇas'? What is meant by 'digging it up'? What is meant by 'wise'? What is meant by 'sword'? What is meant by 'large turtle'? What is meant by 'woolen blanket'? What is meant by 'piece of meat'? What is meant by 'slaughterhouse'? What is meant by 'large snake'? What is meant by 'two paths'? What is meant by 'door panel'? What is meant by 'large dragon'?"

尔时，比丘从彼天所闻此论已，往诣世尊，稽首礼足，退坐一面。以彼天子所问诸论广问世尊：「云何为丘冢？云何为夜则起烟？云何为昼则火然？云何是婆罗门？云何发掘？云何智者？云何刀剑？云何为大龟？云何氍氀？云何为肉段？云何为屠杀处？云何为楞耆？云何为二道？云何为门扇？云何为大龙？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "The burial mound for monks refers to the body of sentient beings, the coarse four great elements, the body inherited from parents, food, clothing, covering, bathing, massaging, and nourishing. All of these are subject to change, decay, and destruction. 'At night it emits smoke' means that when a person arises at night, they follow their awareness and contemplation. During the day, they practice their teachings, with bodily and verbal actions. 'Brāhmaṇas' refers to the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. 'Digging it up' means diligently applying skillful means. 'Wise' refers to the learned noble disciples. 'Sword' refers to the sword of wisdom. 'Large turtle' refers to the five hindrances. 'Woolen blanket' refers to anger and resentment. 'Piece of meat' refers to stinginess and jealousy. 'Slaughterhouse' refers to the five sense pleasures. 'Large snake' refers to ignorance. 'Two paths' refers to doubt. 'Door panel' refers to the conceit of self. 'Large dragon' refers to the arhat who has exhausted the taints. Thus, bhikṣu! If the great teacher has done what is to be done for the śrāvakas, out of compassion and pity, with meaningful consolation, then you should do what is to be done. You should spread grass and tree leaves in the open, in the forest, in empty houses, in mountains, fields, and caves, and contemplate and meditate, without being negligent, so as not to regret it later. This is following my teachings.' He then spoke a verse:

佛告比丘：「丘冢者，谓众生身，麤四大色父母遗体，抟食、衣服、覆盖、澡浴、摩饰、长养，皆是变坏磨灭之法。夜起烟者，谓有人于夜时起，随觉随观。昼行其教，身业、口业。婆罗门者，谓如来、应、等正觉。发掘者，谓精勤方便。智士者，谓多闻圣弟子。刀剑者，谓智慧刀剑。大龟者，谓五盖。氍氀者，谓忿恨。肉段者，谓悭愱。屠杀者，谓五欲功德。楞耆者，谓无明。二道，谓疑惑。门扇者，谓我慢。大龙者，谓漏尽罗汉。如是，比丘！若大师为声闻所作，哀愍悲念，以义安慰，于汝已作，汝等当作所作，当于曝露、林中、空舍、山泽、岩窟，敷草树叶，思惟禅思，不起放逸，莫令后悔，是则为我随顺之教。」即说偈言：

The body is said to be a burial mound for monks, and awareness and contemplation
are like smoke rising at night. During the day, actions are like fire burning. The
brāhmaṇas are the true and correct enlightenment. Diligent effort is like digging it up. The
wise and intelligent ones are those who are clearly wise. The sharp sword of wisdom
is used to cut off the five hindrances, which are like a large turtle. Anger
and resentment are like a woolen blanket. Stinginess and jealousy are like a piece of
meat. The five sense pleasures are like a slaughterhouse. Ignorance is like a large snake
Doubt is like the two paths. The conceit of self is like a door panel
The arhat who has exhausted the taints is like a large dragon. Having thoroughly cut
off all discussions, I speak thus.

「说身为丘冢，　　觉观夜起烟，昼业为火然，　　婆罗门正觉。精进勤发掘，　　黠慧明智士，以智慧利剑，　　厌离胜进者。五盖为巨龟，　　忿恨为氍氀，悭愱为肉段，　　五欲屠杀处。无明为楞耆，　　疑惑于二道，门扇现我慢，　　漏尽罗汉龙。究竟断诸论，　　故我如是说。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished. The bhikṣu, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，彼比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
