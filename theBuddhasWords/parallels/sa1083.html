SĀ 1083

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1083（一〇八三） 食藕根

Parallel to: SN 20.9 / SĀ² 2.22

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was staying at the Monkey Pond in the city of Vaishali, in a two-storied lecture hall. At that time, many bhikṣus put on their robes in the morning, took their alms bowls, and entered Vaishali to beg for food.

一时，佛住毘舍离国猕猴池侧重阁讲堂。时，有众多比丘晨朝著衣持钵，入毘舍离乞食。

At that time, there was a young bhikṣu who had just left home and was not familiar with the Dharma and Vinaya. When begging for food, he did not know the proper sequence. The other bhikṣus saw this and told him, "You are young, have just left home, and do not know the Dharma and Vinaya. Do not jump over! Do not step on! Go begging for food in the wrong sequence, and you will suffer for a long time.

时，有年少比丘出家未久，不闲法、律，当乞食时，不知先后次第。余比丘见已而告之言：「汝是年少，出家未久，未知法、律，莫越！莫重！前后失次而行乞食，长夜当得不饶益苦。」

The young bhikṣu said, "The senior bhikṣus also jump over the sequence and do not follow the proper order. It is not just me." This was repeated three times, but they could not make him stop.

年少比丘言：「诸上座亦复越次，不随前后，非独我也。」如是再三，不能令止。

The many bhikṣus, having begged for food, returned to the monastery, put away their robes and bowls, washed their feet, went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! We, in the morning, put on our robes, took our bowls, entered Vaiśālī to beg for food. There was a young bhikṣu who had just left home in this Dharma and Vinaya. When begging for food, he did not follow the proper sequence, jumping over and stepping on. The bhikṣus admonished him twice, but he would not accept it, saying, 'The senior bhikṣus also do not follow the proper sequence. Why do you rebuke me?' We bhikṣus admonished him three times, but he would not accept it. Therefore, we come to inform the World-Honored One. We only pray that the World-Honored One will remove this unwholesome practice out of compassion!"

众多比丘乞食已，还精舍，举衣钵，洗足已，诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我等晨朝著衣持钵，入毘舍离乞食，有一年少比丘！于此法、律出家未久，行乞食时不以次第，前后复重。诸比丘等再三谏不受，而作是言：『诸上座亦不次第，何故呵我？』我等诸比丘三呵不受，故来白世尊。唯愿世尊为除非法，哀愍故！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "In a great marsh there is a large lake where a great dragon elephant lives. It plucks lotus roots, washes away the mud, and then eats them. After eating, its body becomes plump, strong, and joyful. For this reason, it always likes to stay there. There is another elephant of a different species, small in body, who imitates the dragon elephant, plucking its lotus roots. It cannot wash them clean, and eats them together with the mud. The food does not digest, and the body does not become plump and joyful. It becomes weaker and weaker, and dies or suffers like death.

佛告诸比丘：「如空泽中有大湖水，有大龙象而居其中，拔诸藕根，洗去泥土，然后食之。食已，身体肥悦，多力多乐，以是因缘，常喜乐住。有异种族象，形体羸小，効彼龙象，拔其藕根，洗不能净，合泥土食。食之不消，体不肥悦，转转羸弱，缘斯致死，或同死苦。

Thus, the virtuous bhikṣus have practiced the Way for a long time, not delighting in play. They have practiced the Brahma-conduct for a long time, praised by the Great Teacher, and also praised by other wise ones who practice the Brahma-conduct. These bhikṣus rely on cities and villages. In the morning, they put on their robes, take their bowls, enter the city to beg for food, well-guarding their body and speech, well-restraining their faculties, and concentrating their minds. They can make those who do not believe believe, and those who believe not change. If they obtain material benefits, clothing, food and drink, bedding, and medicine, they are not stained, attached, greedy, fond, deluded, or pursued. They see their faults and see their liberation. However, they still eat. After eating, their body and mind are nourished, and they obtain complexion and strength. For these reasons, they always attain peace and joy.

「如是，宿德比丘学道日久，不乐嬉戏，久修梵行，大师所叹，诸余明智修梵行者亦复加叹。是等比丘依止城邑聚落，晨朝著衣持钵，入城乞食，善护身口，善摄诸根，专心系念，能令彼人不信者信、信者不异，若得财利、衣被、饮食、床卧、汤药，不染、不著、不贪、不嗜、不迷、不逐，见其过患，见其出离，然复食之。食已，身心悦泽，得色得力，以是因缘，常得安乐。

"The young bhikṣus have just left home and are not yet familiar with the Dharma and Vinaya. They rely on the elders and rely on villages. They put on their robes, take their bowls, enter the village to beg for food, not well-guarding their bodies, not guarding the sense-doors, not concentrating their minds. They cannot make those who do not believe believe, and those who believe not change. If they obtain material benefits, clothing, food and drink, bedding, and medicine, they are stained, attached, greedy, and pursued. They do not see the faults and do not see the liberation. They eat with a mind of craving and cannot nourish their bodies, make them peaceful and joyful. Because of this food, they turn towards death, or suffer like death. What is meant by death is to abandon the precepts and return to lay life, losing the true Dharma and true Vinaya. Suffering like death means to violate the true Dharma and Vinaya, not knowing the characteristics of offenses, and not knowing how to remove offenses." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

「彼年少比丘出家未久，未闲法、律，依诸长老，依止聚落，著衣持钵，入村乞食，不善护身，不守根门，不专系念，不能令彼不信者信、信者不变。若得财利、衣被、饮食、卧具、汤药，染著贪逐，不见过患，不见出离，以嗜欲心食，不能令身悦泽，安隐快乐。缘斯食故，转向于死，或同死苦。所言死者，谓舍戒还俗，失正法、正律。同死苦者，谓犯正法、律，不识罪相，不知除罪。」尔时，世尊即说偈言：

The dragon-like elephant pulls up lotus roots, washes them in water, and
eats them. The elephants of other tribes imitate it, mixing mud and
eating. Because of eating mixed mud, they become weak and sick, and eventually die.

「龙象拔藕根，　　水洗而食之，异族象効彼，　　合泥而取食。因杂泥食故，　　羸病遂至死。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully upheld it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
