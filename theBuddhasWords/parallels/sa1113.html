SĀ 1113

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1113（一一一三） 敬僧

Parallel to: SN 11.20 / SĀ² 2.42

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "In the past, when the Heavenly King Śakra wanted to enter the garden, he ordered the charioteer to prepare a thousand-horse chariot. The charioteer received the order, immediately prepared it, and then returned to inform Śakra, 'The chariot is ready, Your Majesty may know the time.'

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「过去世时，有天帝释欲入园观，王勅御者，令严驾千马之车。御者受教，即严驾已，还白帝释：『乘已严驾，唯王知时。』

At that time, Śakra descended from the Hall of Constant Victory, circumambulated in all directions, and paid homage with palms joined.

「尔时，帝释从常胜殿来下，周向诸方，合掌恭敬。

At that time, the charioteer saw Śakra descending from the hall, standing in the courtyard, circumambulating in all directions, and paying homage with palms joined. Seeing this, he was frightened, dropped his horsewhip, and spoke a verse:

「时，彼御者见天帝释从殿来下，住于中庭，周向诸方，合掌恭敬。见已惊怖，马鞭落地，而说偈言：

The charioteer said: 'In all directions, there are only humans, born from
stinking wombs. The spirit dwells in a filthy corpse, always burning with
hunger and thirst. Why do you, Kauśika, still cherish the non-home? Please
tell me the meaning of this, I am hungry and thirsty to hear it.

「『诸方唯有人，　　臭秽胞胎生，神处秽死尸，　　饥渴常燋然。何故憍尸迦，　　故重于非家？为我说其义，　　饥渴愿欲闻。』

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, spoke a verse in reply:

「时，天帝释说偈答言：

I am truly respectful towards those who can leave the household
life, freely wandering in all directions, not counting their comings and
goings. The colors of cities, towns, and countries cannot burden their
minds. They do not accumulate provisions for living, going forth without
any fixed desires. Going forth, they have nothing to seek, only
the unconditioned as happiness. When speaking, their words are always good
when silent, they are in tranquil concentration. The devas and asuras
each oppose each other, and humans themselves quarrel with each other
also like this. Only those who have left the household life
are free from disputes in all disputes, and towards all beings
they lay down their weapons. Towards wealth and possessions, they are
detached, not intoxicated or confused. They are far removed from all
evil, therefore I pay homage to them.

「『我正恭敬彼，　　能出非家者，自在游诸方，　　不计其行止。城邑国土色，　　不能累其心，不畜资生具，　　一往无欲定。往则无所求，　　唯无为为乐，言则定善言，　　不言则寂定。诸天阿修罗，　　各各共相违，人间自共诤，　　相违亦如是。唯有出家者，　　于诸诤无诤，于一切众生，　　放舍于刀杖。于财离财色，　　不醉亦不荒，远离一切恶，　　是故敬礼彼。』

At that time, the charioteer spoke a verse in reply:

「是时，御者复说偈言：

The king of devas pays respect to them, they must be the best in the world
Therefore, from today onwards, I shall pay homage to those who have left the household life.

「『天王之所敬，　　是必世间胜，故我从今日，　　当礼出家人。』

"Having spoken thus, Sakra, Lord of the Devas, paid homage to the Sangha in all directions, and then ascended his horse-drawn carriage to tour the gardens."

「如是说已，天帝释敬礼诸方一切僧毕，升于马车，游观园林。」

The Buddha told the monks, "Sakra, Lord of the Devas, is the sovereign king of the Thirty-three Heavens, yet he always pays respect to the Sangha and praises the merits of respecting the Sangha. You monks, having renounced the household life with right faith, should also respect the Sangha and praise the merits of respecting the Sangha."

佛告比丘：「彼天帝释于三十三天为自在王，而常恭敬众僧，亦常赞叹恭敬僧功德。汝等比丘正信非家，出家学道，亦当如是恭敬众僧，亦当赞叹敬僧功德。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished. The monks, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
