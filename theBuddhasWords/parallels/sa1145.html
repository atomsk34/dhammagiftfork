SĀ 1145

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1145（一一四五） 可厌患

Parallel to: SN 3.24 / SĀ² 2.68

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time King Prasenajit came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha’s feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, “World-Honored One! To whom should one make offerings?”

时，波斯匿王来诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！应施何等人？」

The Buddha said, “Great King! Wherever one’s mind delights.”

佛言：「大王！随心所乐处。」

King Prasenajit again said to the Buddha, “Where should one make offerings to obtain great rewards?”

波斯匿王复白佛言：「应施何处，得大果报？」

The Buddha said, “Great King! This is a different question. The question ‘Where should one make offerings?’ is different. The question ‘Where should one make offerings to obtain great rewards?’ is also different. I now ask you, answer me as you wish. Great King! It is like this country gathering warriors for battle. There is a young brahmin who comes from the east, young, tender, and handsome, with fair skin and black hair. He is not skilled in martial arts, has not learned strategies, is timid and weak, cannot be at ease, cannot bear to look at the enemy, cannot shoot or stab, has no skill, and cannot harm the enemy. What do you think, Great King? Would the king reward such a warrior?”

佛言：「大王！此是异问。所问『应施何处？』此问则异。复问『施何处，应得大果？』此问复异。我今问汝，随意答我。大王！譬如此国临阵战鬪，集诸战士，而有一婆罗门子，从东方来，年少幼稚，柔弱端正，肤白发黑，不习武艺，不学术策，恐怖退弱，不能自安，不忍敌观，若刺若射，无有方便，不能伤彼。云何？大王，如此士夫，王当赏不？」

The king said to the Buddha, “No, World-Honored One!”

王白佛言：「不赏。世尊！」

“In the same way, Great King! There is a kṣatriya youth coming from the south, a vaiśya youth coming from the west, and a śūdra youth coming from the north, all without skills, just like the brahmin youth from the east. Would the king reward them?”

「如是，大王！有刹利童子从南方来，鞞舍童子从西方来，首陀罗童子从北方来，无有伎术，皆如东方婆罗门子，王当赏不？」

The king said to the Buddha, “No, World-Honored One!”

王白佛言：「不赏。世尊！」

The Buddha said to the great king, “When this country gathers troops to engage in battle, there is a brahmin youth coming from the east, young and handsome, with fair skin and black hair, skilled in martial arts, knowing the methods of warfare, brave and fearless, fighting hard without retreating, calmly observing, able to injure with weapons, and able to defeat great enemies. What do you think, great king? Would you give such a warrior heavy rewards?”

「佛告大王，此国集军临战鬪时，有婆罗门童子从东方来，年少端正，肤白发黑，善学武艺，知鬪术法，勇健无畏，苦战不退，安住谛观，运戈能伤，能破巨敌。云何？大王！如此战士，加重赏不？」

The king said to the Buddha, “Heavy rewards, World-Honored One!”

王白佛言：「重赏，世尊！」

“In the same way, a kṣatriya youth comes from the south, a vaiśya youth from the west, and a śūdra youth from the north, young and handsome, skilled in various arts, brave and capable, fighting hard to repel the enemy, all like the brahmin youth from the east. Would the king reward such warriors?”

「如是，刹利童子从南方来，鞞舍童子从西方来，首陀罗童子从北方来，年少端正，善诸术艺，勇健堪能，苦战却敌，皆如东方婆罗门子。如是战士，王当赏不？」

The king said to the Buddha, “Heavy rewards, World-Honored One!”

王白佛言：「重赏，世尊！」

The Buddha said, "Great King! Thus, the śramaṇa and brāhmaṇa abandon the five branches and accomplish the five branches, establishing the field of merit. Those who give to this field will attain great blessings and great rewards. What are the five branches to be abandoned? Namely, the hindrances of desire, anger, sloth, agitation, and doubt, which have been cut off and known. This is called abandoning the five branches. What are the five branches to be accomplished? Namely, accomplishing the body of precepts, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation without learning. This is called accomplishing the five branches. Great King! Thus, abandoning the five branches and accomplishing the five branches, establishing the field of merit, those who give to this field will attain great rewards." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

佛言：「大王！如是沙门、婆罗门远离五支，成就五支，建立福田。施此田者，得大福利，得大果报。何等为舍离五支？所谓贪欲盖，瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑盖，已断已知，是名舍离五支。何等为成就五支？谓无学戒身成就，无学定身、慧身、解脱身、解脱知见身，是名成就五支。大王！如是舍离五支，成就五支，建立福田，施此田者，得大果报。」尔时，世尊复说偈言：

Those who wage fierce battles, capable warriors, for their battles, are rewarded
according to their merits. Those of noble lineage, the timid and cowardly
the patient and virtuous, who see the truth and establish the field
of merit, the sages and saints, with their disciplined conduct, who accomplish
profound and wondrous wisdom, though of humble lineage, are worthy of being
fields of merit for giving. Clothing, food, money, wealth, treasures, beds, bedding
and various necessities, all should be respectfully given to them, for their
upholding of pure precepts. At the edge of the forest and wilderness
wells are dug to provide for travelers. Bridges are built across streams
and ravines, and houses are constructed along the way. The assembly of
those with virtuous precepts and much learning, travelers can find rest. It
is like heavy clouds gathering, with the sound of thunder and lightning
flashing. Raining down on the soil, all the hundred plants grow lush
birds and beasts are all delighted, and farmers are all joyful. In
the same way, with pure faith, hearing the teachings and abandoning the
defilements of stinginess, with abundant money, wealth, food, and drink, always giving
to the excellent field of merit, proclaiming loudly, increasing joy and love
like thunder and rain on good fields, the stream of merit and
virtue flows, moistening the minds of the givers. Wealth, fame, and the
great fruit of nirvāṇa flow.

「运戈猛战鬪，　　堪能勇士夫，为其战鬪故，　　随功重加赏。不赏名族胄，　　怯劣无勇者，忍辱修贤良，　　见谛建福田。贤圣律仪备，　　成就深妙智，族胄虽卑微，　　堪为施福田。衣食钱财宝，　　床卧等众具，悉应以敬施，　　为持净戒故。人表林野际，　　穿井给行人，溪涧施桥梁，　　逈路造房舍。戒德多闻众，　　行路得止息，譬如重云起，　　雷电声振耀。普雨于壤土，　　百卉悉扶踈，禽兽皆欢喜，　　田夫并欣乐。如是净信心，　　闻慧舍悭垢，钱财丰饮食，　　常施良福田。高唱增欢爱，　　如雷雨良田，功德注流泽，　　沾洽施主心。财富名称流，　　及涅槃大果。」

Having spoken this sūtra, King Prasenajit, having heard the Buddha's words, was delighted and joyful. He bowed and departed.

佛说此经已，波斯匿王闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
