SĀ 1147

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1147（一一四七） 石山

Parallel to: SN 3.25 / SĀ² 2.70

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time King Prasenajit was covered in dust and dirt every day. He came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha’s feet, and sat to one side.

时，波斯匿王日日身蒙尘土，来诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面。

The Buddha said, “Great King! Where have you come from?”

佛言：「大王！从何所来？」

King Prasenajit said to the Buddha, “World-Honored One! The Dharma of the anointed king is supreme among humans. Diligent and skillful, the king rules the great earth and manages royal affairs. He travels around to inspect.

波斯匿王白佛言：「世尊！彼灌顶王法，人中自在，精勤方便。王领大地，统理王事，周行观察，而来至此。」

The Buddha said to the great king, "Now I ask the great king, please answer me as you wish. Suppose someone comes from the east, trustworthy and reliable, never false, and says to the king, 'I came from the east and saw a stone mountain, extremely square and wide, unbroken, undamaged, and without any cracks or flaws, grinding along the ground, crushing all sentient beings and plants to pieces.' Someone from the south, west, and north also comes, trustworthy and reliable, never false, and says to the king, 'I saw a stone mountain, square, wide, and tall, unbroken, undamaged, and without any cracks or flaws, grinding along the ground, crushing all sentient beings and plants to pieces.' What do you think, great king? Such a terrifying sight, with dangerous and evil killing, the end of sentient beings, and the difficulty of attaining the human path, what should be done?"

佛告大王：「今问大王，随意答我。譬如有人从东方来，有信有缘，未曾虚妄，而白王言：『我东方来，见一石山，极方广大，不穿不坏，亦无缺坏，磨地而来，一切众生草木之类悉磨令碎。』南、西、北方亦有人来，有信有缘，亦不虚妄，而白王言：『我见石山，方广高大，不断不坏，亦不缺坏，磨地而来，众生草木悉皆磨碎。』大王！于意云何？如是像貌大恐怖事，崄恶相杀，众生运尽，人道难得，当作何计？」

The king said to the Buddha, "If it is like this, there is no other plan. We should only cultivate goodness and focus our efforts on the Buddha's teachings and precepts."

王白佛言：「若如是者，更无余计，唯当修善，于佛法、律专心方便。」

The Buddha said to the great king, "Why do you say: 'The dangerous and evil terrors suddenly arise in the world, the end of sentient beings, and the difficulty of attaining the human body. We should only practice the Dharma, practice righteousness, and practice merit, focusing our efforts on the Buddha's teachings.' Why don't you say: 'The crown prince, the foremost among people, is capable of freely ruling the kingdom. The king should manage the affairs of the people on the great earth'?"

佛告大王：「何故说言：『崄恶恐怖于世卒起，众生运尽，人身难得，唯当行法、行义、行福，于佛法教专精方便。』何以不言：『灌顶王位众人人首，堪能自在。』王于大地，事务众人，当须营理耶？」

The king said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Do you also say at leisure times: 'The crown prince is the foremost among people. The king should manage the affairs of the people on the great earth. There are disputes of words, disputes of wealth, disputes of elephants, disputes of chariots, and disputes of infantry. At that time, there is no freedom, whether victorious or defeated.' Therefore, I say: 'When dangerous and evil terrors suddenly arise, when the end of sentient beings, and the difficulty of attaining the human body, there is no other plan. We should only practice righteousness, practice the Dharma, and practice merit, focusing our efforts on the Buddha's teachings and taking refuge in them.'"

王白佛言：「世尊！为复闲时言：『灌顶王位为众人首，王于大地，多所经营，以言鬪言，以财鬪财，以象鬪象，以车鬪车，以步鬪步。当于尔时，无有自在，若胜若伏。』是故我说：『崄恶恐怖卒起之时，众生运尽，人身难得，无有余计，唯有行义、行法、行福，于佛法教专心归依。』」

The Buddha said to the great king, "It is so! It is so! The constant grinding and pressing refers to the evil eon, old age, sickness, and suffering, grinding and pressing sentient beings. What should be done? One should cultivate righteousness, cultivate the Dharma, cultivate merit, cultivate goodness, cultivate kindness, and diligently practice in the Buddha's teachings." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse:

佛告大王：「如是！如是！经常磨迮，谓恶劫、老、病、苦，磨迮众生。当作何计？正当修义、修法、修福、修善、修慈，于佛法中精勤方便。」尔时，世尊而说偈言：

If there were a great rocky mountain, high and wide, without any
gaps, coming from all four directions, grinding and pressing this great earth
it could not be prevented by soldiers, horses, spells, or magical powers
The evil eon, old age, sickness, and death constantly grind and press
sentient beings. The four great clans, the candalas, hunters, those at home
and those who have left home, those who uphold precepts and those
who violate precepts - all are ground and pressed, with no one
able to protect them. Therefore, the wise should observe their own benefit
and establish pure faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha. With pure
body, speech, and mind, they should follow the true Dharma. In this
life, their fame will spread, and in the end, they will be
reborn in the heavens.

「如有大石山，　　高广无缺坏，周遍四方来，　　磨迮此大地。非兵马呪术，　　力所能防御，恶劫老病死，　　常磨迮众生。四种大族姓，　　栴陀罗猎师，在家及出家，　　持戒犯戒者。一切皆磨迮，　　无能救护者，是故慧士夫，　　观察自己利。建立清净信，　　信佛法僧宝，身口心清净，　　随顺于正法。现世名称流，　　终则生天上。」

Having spoken this sutra, King Prasenajit, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and followed the teachings. He bowed and departed.

佛说此经已，波斯匿王闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
