SĀ 1149

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1149（一一四九） 诸王

Parallel to: SN 3.12 / SĀ² 2.72 / EĀ 33.1

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time King Prasenajit was the leader, and together with the kings of the seven countries and the ministers, they all gathered for a discussion, saying, "Among the five desires, which is the best?" One person said, "Form is the best." Others praised sound, smell, taste, and touch as the best. Someone in the middle said, "We each speak of the best, but there is no definite judgment. We should go to the World-Honored One and ask about this meaning. As the World-Honored One says, we should all remember it together."

时，波斯匿王为首，并七国王及诸大臣悉共集会，作如是论议：「五欲之中，何者第一？」有一人言：「色最第一。」又复有称声、香、味、触为第一者。中有人言：「我等人人各说第一，竟无定判，当诣世尊！问如此义。如世尊说，当共忆持。」

At that time, King Prasenajit was the leader, and together with the kings of the seven countries, ministers, and attendants, he came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! We, the seven kings and the ministers, had this discussion: 'Among the five desires and merits, which is the best?' Some said form is best, some said sound is best, some said smell is best, some said taste is best, and some said touch is best. In the end, there was no definite conclusion. We came to ask the World-Honored One, which is really the best?"

尔时，波斯匿王为首，与七国王、大臣、眷属来诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我等七王与诸大臣如是论议：『五欲功德，何者为胜？』其中有言色胜、有言声胜、有言香胜、有言味胜、有言触胜，竟无决定。来问世尊，竟何者胜？」

The Buddha told the kings, "Each according to your own preference, I have all the remaining explanations. For this reason, I speak of the merits of the five desires. However, there are those who are satisfied with a certain form, and they stop loving just that one form, fulfilling their wishes. Even if there are superior forms above, which they do not love, they do not touch or see them, saying that what they love is the best, with nothing above it. It is like those who love form. Sound, smell, taste, and touch are also like this. When they love something, they immediately say it is the best, joyfully and happily attached to it. Even if there are superior things above, which they do not desire, they do not touch or see them, only saying that what they love is the best and most wonderful, incomparable and unsurpassed."

佛告诸王：「各随意适，我悉有余说，以是因缘，我说五欲功德。然自有人于色适意，止爱一色，满其志愿，正使过上有诸胜色，非其所爱，不触不视，言己所爱最为第一，无过其上。如爱色者。声、香、味、触亦皆如是。当其所爱，辄言最胜，欢喜乐著，虽更有胜过其上者，非其所欲，不触不视，唯我爱者最胜最妙，无比无上。」

At that time, in the assembly, there was an upāsaka named Sandalwood. He rose from his seat, adjusted his robes, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "Well spoken, World-Honored One! Well spoken, Well-Gone One!"

尔时，座中有一优婆塞，名曰栴檀，从座起，整衣服，偏袒右肩，合掌白佛：「善说！世尊！善说！善逝！」

The Buddha told the upāsaka, "Well spoken, Sandalwood! Well spoken, Sandalwood!"

佛告优婆塞：「善说！栴檀！快说！栴檀！」

At that time, the upāsaka Sandalwood spoke a verse, saying:

时，栴檀优婆塞即说偈言：

The king of the Anga clan, wearing pearl necklaces and
armor, the assembly of Magadha rejoiced, the Tathāgata appeared in
his country. His fame spread everywhere, like the king of
the snow mountains, like a pure lotus in water, pure
and spotless. Blooming with the sunlight, fragrantly perfuming his country
the Anga country was bright, like the sun in the
sky. Observing the Tathāgata's wisdom power, like a torch burning
at night, being the eye, being the great light, coming
to resolve doubts.

「央伽族姓王，　　服珠璎珞铠，摩竭众庆集，　　如来出其国。名闻普流布，　　犹如雪山王，如净水莲华，　　清净无瑕秽。随日光开敷，　　芬香熏其国，央耆国明显，　　犹如空中日。观如来慧力，　　如夜然炬火，为眼为大明，　　来者为决疑。」

At that time, the kings of various countries praised, saying: "Well spoken, upāsaka Sandalwood!"

时，诸国王叹言：「善说！栴檀优婆塞！」

At that time, the seven kings took off their seven-jeweled upper garments and offered them to the upāsaka.

尔时，七王脱七宝上衣，奉优婆塞。

At that time, the seven kings, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully rejoiced and rose from their seats to depart.

时，彼七王闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，从座起去。

At that time, the sandalwood upāsaka, knowing that the kings had left, rose from his seat, adjusted his robes, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "Now the seven kings have left behind seven upper garments for me. I only wish that the World-Honored One would accept these seven garments out of compassion!"

尔时，栴檀优婆塞知诸王去已，从座起，整衣服，偏袒右肩，合掌白佛：「今七国王遗我七领上衣，唯愿世尊受此七衣，以哀愍故！」

At that time, the World-Honored One, out of compassion, accepted the seven garments. The sandalwood upāsaka joyfully rejoiced, paid homage, and departed.

尔时，世尊为哀愍故，受其七衣，栴檀优婆塞欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
