SĀ 1152

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1152（一一五二） 卑嶷

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a young Brāhman named Piṅgiya came to where the Buddha was. In front of the World-Honored One, he spoke harsh, evil, and unwholesome words, angrily scolding and criticizing.

时，有年少宾耆迦婆罗门来诣佛所，于世尊面前作麁恶不善语，瞋骂呵责。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the young Piṅgiya, "At one time, on an auspicious day, will you gather your relatives and family members?"

尔时，世尊告年少宾耆迦：「若于一时吉星之日，汝当会诸宗亲眷属耶？」

Piṅgiya said to the Buddha, "Yes, Gautama!"

宾耆白佛：「如是，瞿昙！」

The Buddha said to Piṅgiya, "If your relatives do not accept food, what should be done?"

佛告宾耆：「若汝宗亲不受食者，当如之何？」

Piṅgiya said to the Buddha, "If they do not accept food, the food will still belong to me."

宾耆白佛：「不受食者，食还属我。」

The Buddha said to Piṅgiya, "It is the same for you. You spoke harshly and unwholesome words in front of the Tathāgata, reviling and scolding, but I did not accept it. To whom should such scolding belong?"

佛告宾耆：「汝亦如是，如来面前作麁恶不善语，骂辱呵责，我竟不受，如此骂者，应当属谁？」

Piṅgiya said to the Buddha, "So it is, Gautama! Although he does not accept it, it is still given as a gift."

宾耆白佛：「如是，瞿昙！彼虽不受，且以相赠，则便是与。」

The Buddha said to Piṅgiya, "This is not called giving each other gifts. How can it be considered giving?"

佛告宾耆：「如是不名更相赠遗，何得便为相与？」

Piṅgiya said to the Buddha, "What is called giving each other gifts? What is called not accepting gifts?"

宾耆白佛：「云何名为更相赠遗，名为相与？云何名不受相赠遗，不名相与？」

The Buddha said to Piṅgiya, "If one curses and then curses back, gets angry and then gets angry back, hits and then hits back, fights and then fights back, this is called giving each other gifts. If one curses and does not curse back, gets angry and does not get angry back, hits and does not hit back, fights and does not fight back, this is not called giving each other gifts.

佛告宾耆：「若当如是骂则报骂，瞋则报瞋，打则报打，鬪则报鬪，名相赠遗，名为相与，若复，宾耆！骂不报骂，瞋不报瞋，打不报打，鬪不报鬪，若如是者，非相赠遗，不名相与。」

Piṅgiya said to the Buddha, "Gautama, I have heard the ancient elders of the Brahmins say, 'The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, when cursed and reviled, does not get angry or upset.' But now, does Gautama get angry?"

宾耆白佛：「瞿昙！我闻古昔婆罗门长老宿重行道大师所说：『如来、应、等正觉，面前骂辱，瞋恚诃责，不瞋不怒。』而今瞿昙有瞋恚耶？」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

Without anger, how can there be anger? The right livelihood is to subdue
With right wisdom, the mind is liberated. The wise have no anger. To
repay anger with anger is to be a bad person. Not repaying anger
with anger, one subdues the enemy that is hard to subdue. Not being
angry is superior to being angry. The three verses are as stated above.

「无瞋何有瞋，　　正命以调伏，正智心解脱，　　慧者无有瞋。以瞋报瞋者，　　是则为恶人，不以瞋报瞋，　　临敌伏难伏。不瞋胜于瞋，　　三偈如前说。」

At that time, the young man Bhinqi said to the Buddha, "I repent, Gautama! Like a fool, like a fool, I was unable to distinguish what was unwholesome, and spoke harshly and unwholesome words in front of the śramaṇa Gautama, angrily scolding and criticizing." Having heard the Buddha's words, he rejoiced, paid homage, and departed.

尔时，年少宾耆白佛言：「悔过！瞿昙！如愚如痴，不辩不善，而于沙门瞿昙面前麁恶不善语，瞋骂呵责。」闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
