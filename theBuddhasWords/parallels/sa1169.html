SĀ 1169

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1169（一一六九） 琴

Parallel to: SN 35.246

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Ghosita Monastery in the country of Kusinara.

一时，佛住拘睒弥国瞿师罗园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If there are bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs who, due to the conditions of eye and form, give rise to desire, greed, attachment, thought, or determination, they should guard their minds well in those places. Why is that? Because all these are paths of fear, obstacles, and difficulties. They are relied upon by evil people, not by good people. Therefore, one should guard oneself. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. It is like a farmer who has good crops in his field, but the one who guards the field is lazy and negligent, allowing cattle to eat them. The same is true for ordinary foolish people. The same is true for the six sense bases, up to negligence.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有比丘、比丘尼，眼色识因缘生，若欲、若贪、若昵、若念、若决定著处，于彼诸心善自防护。所以者何？此等皆是恐畏之道，有碍、有难，此恶人所依，非善人所依，是故应自防护。耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦复如是。譬如田夫有好田苗，其守田者懒惰放逸，栏牛噉食；愚痴凡夫亦复如是。六触入处，乃至放逸亦复如是。

"If the crops in a good field are well guarded, the cattle will not rampage. Even if they enter the field, they will be driven out. The same is true for the mind, intention, and consciousness. The noble disciple of much learning well guards the five sense pleasures and desires, and completely extinguishes them. If the crops in a good field are well guarded, the cattle will not rampage. If they enter the field, they will be driven out with the left hand holding the nose and the right hand holding a staff, beating all over the body. What do you think, bhikṣus? After that cow has suffered, will it go from village to house, from house to village, and again do as before, overeating the crops in the field?"

「若好田苗，其守田者心不放逸，栏牛不暴，设复入田，尽驱令出；所谓若心、若意、若识，多闻圣弟子于五欲功德善自摄护，尽心令灭。若好田苗，其守护田者不自放逸，栏牛入境，左手牵鼻，右手执杖，遍身搥打，驱出其田。诸比丘！于意云何？彼牛遭苦痛已，从村至宅，从宅至村，复当如前过食田苗不？」

"No, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because of remembering the suffering of being beaten in the field before."

答言：「不也，世尊！所以者何？忆先入田遭捶杖苦故。」

Thus, bhikṣus, the noble disciple of much learning, with regard to the six sense bases, generates extreme disgust and fear, and internally settles the mind, controlling it to be one-pointed. Bhikṣus, in the past, a king heard an unprecedentedly beautiful sound of a plucked string instrument and became extremely fond of it, addicted and attached to it. He asked his ministers, 'What is this sound? It is so lovely!' The ministers replied, 'This is the sound of a plucked string instrument.' The king said to the ministers, 'Bring me that sound!' The ministers took the order and went to fetch the plucked string instrument. They said, 'Great King! This is the plucked string instrument that makes the beautiful sound.' The king said to the ministers, 'I do not need the plucked string instrument. Bring me the sound that I previously heard and loved.' The ministers replied, 'This kind of plucked string instrument has many parts, namely a handle, a body, a fretboard, strings, and a skin. When a skilled person plucks it, the sound is produced through the conditions of these parts. Without the parts, there is no sound. The sound that you heard before has long since passed and is completely extinguished, and cannot be brought here.'

「如是，比丘！若心、若意、若识，多闻圣弟子于六触入处极生厌离、恐怖，内心安住，制令一意。诸比丘！过去世时，有王闻未曾有好弹琴声，极生爱乐，耽湎染著，问诸大臣：『此何等声？甚可爱乐！』大臣答言：『此是琴声。』语大臣：『取彼声来。』大臣受教，即往取琴来，白言：『大王！此是琴作好声者。』王语大臣：『我不用琴，取其先闻可爱乐声来。』大臣答言：『如此之琴，有众多种具，谓有柄、有槽、有丽、有弦、有皮，巧方便人弹之，得众具因缘乃成音声，非不得众具而有音声，前所闻声，久已过去，转亦尽灭，不可持来。』

"At that time, the great king thought, 'Oh! What use are these false things? The plucked string instruments of the world are false things, yet they cause worldly people to be addicted and attached. Now take them away, break them into pieces, and discard them in the ten directions.' The ministers took the order, broke them into a hundred pieces, and discarded them everywhere. Thus, bhikṣus, if it is form, feeling, perception, volition, or desire, knowing that these dharmas are impermanent, conditioned, and arise from the causes and conditions of the mind, yet one still says, 'This is mine, this is me.' Then at another time, all of them will be gone. Bhikṣus, one should cultivate such equality and right knowledge, and observe it as it really is."

「尔时，大王作是念言：『咄！何用此虚伪物为？世间琴者是虚伪物，而令世人耽湎染著；汝今持去，片片析破，弃于十方。』大臣受教，析为百分，弃于处处。如是，比丘！若色、受、想、思、欲，知此诸法无常、有为、心因缘生，而便说言：『是我、我所。』彼于异时，一切悉无。诸比丘！应作如是平等正智，如实观察。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha had said and joyfully upheld it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
