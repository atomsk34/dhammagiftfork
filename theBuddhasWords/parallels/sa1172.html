SĀ 1172

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1172（一一七二） 毒蛇

Parallel to: SN 35.238 / EĀ 31.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Ghosita Monastery in the country of Kusinara.

一时，佛住拘睒弥国瞿师罗园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "It is like having four venomous snakes, fierce and poisonous, placed in a basket. At that time, there was a wise and intelligent person who sought happiness and disliked suffering, who sought life and disliked death. Then a certain person said to that person, 'You should now take this basket of venomous snakes, rub and bathe them, treat them with kindness and feed them, and take them out and in at the right time. If the four venomous snakes should harm you, they may kill you or bring you close to death. You should guard against them.' At that time, that person was terrified and fled. 'Suddenly there are five enemies with drawn swords chasing after you, seeking to kill you. You should guard against them.' At that time, that person was afraid of the four venomous snakes and the five enemies with drawn swords, and fled in terror.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「譬如有四蚖蛇，凶恶毒虐，盛一箧中。时，有士夫聪明不愚，有智慧，求乐厌苦，求生厌死。时，有一士夫语向士夫言：『汝今取此箧盛毒蛇，摩拭洗浴，恩亲养食，出内以时。若四毒蛇脱有恼者，或能杀汝，或令近死，汝当防护。』尔时，士夫恐怖驰走。『忽有五怨，拔刀随逐，要求欲杀，汝当防护。』尔时，士夫畏四毒蛇及五拔刀怨，驱驰而走。

Another person said to that person, 'Sir! There are six internal thieves who follow and watch for you. If they get the chance, they will kill you. You should guard against them.' At that time, that person was afraid of the four venomous snakes, the five enemies with drawn swords, and the six internal thieves, and fled in terror. Returning to an empty village, he saw an empty house, dilapidated and rotten, with all kinds of evil things. When grasped, they were all fragile and not solid.

「人复语言：『士夫！内有六贼，随逐伺汝，得便当杀，汝当防护。』尔时，士夫畏四毒蛇、五拔刀怨及内六贼，恐怖驰走，还入空村，见彼空舍，危朽腐毁，有诸恶物，捉皆危脆，无有坚固。

Another person said to that person, 'Sir! There will be a group of bandits in this empty village who will certainly harm you.' At that time, that person was afraid of the four venomous snakes, the five enemies with drawn swords, the six internal thieves, the bandits in the empty village, and fled in terror. Suddenly on the road he came to a great river with swift and strong currents. He only saw that this shore had all kinds of fears, while the other shore was peaceful and happy, cool and fearless. There were no bridges or boats to cross to the other shore. He thought, 'I will gather grass and wood, bind them into a raft, and use my hands and feet to cross to the other shore.' Having thought this, he immediately gathered grass and wood, bound them into a raft on the shore, and used his hands and feet to cross the current.

「人复语言：『士夫！是空聚落当有群贼，来必奄害汝。』尔时，士夫畏四毒蛇、五拔刀贼、内六恶贼、空村群贼，而复驰走。忽尔道路临一大河，其水浚急，但见此岸有诸怖畏，而见彼岸安隐快乐，清凉无畏，无桥船可渡得至彼岸，作是思惟：『我取诸草木，缚束成栰，手足方便，渡至彼岸。』作是念已，即拾草木，依于岸傍，缚束成栰，手足方便，截流横渡。

Thus, that person escaped the four venomous snakes, the five enemies with drawn swords, the six internal thieves, and also escaped the bandits in the empty village. He crossed the swift current, leaving behind the various fears on this shore, and reached the other shore, which was peaceful and happy. I speak this parable to explain its meaning. Bhikṣu! The chest is a metaphor for this body, which is coarse and composed of the four great elements, formed from the four great elements, the essence of blood and semen, nourished by impure food, bathed and clothed, impermanent, changing, fragile, and perishable. The venomous snakes are a metaphor for the four great elements - the earth element, water element, fire element, and wind element. If the earth element disputes, it can cause death or near death, and the same is true for the water, fire, and wind elements. The five enemies with drawn swords are a metaphor for the five aggregates of grasping. The six internal thieves are a metaphor for the six kinds of love and joy. The empty village is a metaphor for the six internal sense bases. Good man! Observing the sense base of the eye is impermanent and perishable. The holder of the eye is also an impermanent and false dharma. The same is true for the sense bases of the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. The bandits in the empty village are a metaphor for the six external sense bases. The eye is harmed by desirable and undesirable forms. The ear by sounds, the nose by smells, the tongue by tastes, the body by tactile sensations, and the mind by desirable and undesirable dharmas. The swift current is a metaphor for the four streams - the stream of desire, the stream of existence, the stream of views, and the stream of ignorance. The river is a metaphor for the three kinds of craving - craving for desire, craving for form, and craving for formlessness. The many fears on this shore are a metaphor for having a body. The coolness and peace on the other shore is a metaphor for nirvāṇa without remainder. The raft is a metaphor for the eightfold noble path. The use of hands and feet to cross the current is a metaphor for the vigorous effort to reach the other shore. The abode of the brāhmaṇa is a metaphor for the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One.

「如是士夫免四毒蛇、五拔刀怨、六内恶贼，复得脱于空村群贼，渡于浚流，离于此岸种种怖畏，得至彼岸安隐快乐。我说此譬，当解其义。比丘！箧者，譬此身色麁四大，四大所造精血之体，秽食长养，沐浴衣服，无常变坏危脆之法。毒蛇者，譬四大——地界、水界、火界、风界。地界若诤，能令身死，及以近死；水、火、风诤亦复如是。五拔刀怨者，譬五受阴。六内贼者，譬六爱喜。空村者，譬六内入。善男子！观察眼入处，是无常变坏，执持眼者，亦是无常虚伪之法；耳、鼻、舌、身、意入处亦复如是。空村群贼者，譬外六入处。眼为可意、不可意色所害；耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意，为可意、不可意法所害。浚流者，譬四流——欲流、有流、见流、无明流。河者，譬三爱——欲爱、色爱、无色爱。此岸多恐怖者，譬有身。彼岸清凉安乐者，譬无余涅槃。栰者，譬八正道。手足方便截流渡者，譬精进勇猛到彼岸。婆罗门住处者，譬如来、应、等正觉。

"Thus, bhikṣu! The great teacher compassionately comforts the disciples and does what is to be done for them. I have now done what is to be done. You should also do what is to be done. In an empty and quiet place, under a tree, in a clean dwelling, spreading grass for a seat, in the open air, near a charnel ground, sitting far away, diligently practicing meditation. Be careful not to be negligent, causing regret later! This is my teaching method."

「如是，比丘！大师慈悲安慰弟子，为其所作；我今已作，汝今亦当作其所作，于空闲树下，房舍清净，敷草为座，露地、冢间，远离边坐，精勤禅思，慎莫放逸，令后悔恨！此则是我教授之法。」

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha said rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行！
