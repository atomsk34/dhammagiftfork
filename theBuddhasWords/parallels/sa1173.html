SĀ 1173

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1173（一一七三） 苦法

Parallel to: SN 35.244

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Ghosita Monastery in the country of Kusinara.

一时，佛住拘睒弥国瞿师罗园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "The noble disciple of much learning truly knows and sees the origin, cessation, taste, danger, and escape of all suffering dharmas. He sees the five desires as if they were a pit of fire. Having observed the five desires in this way, he is not permanently covered by the desire, desire-love, desire-thought, and desire-attachment for the five desires. He knows the place of desire-thought and the place of desire-attachment, and guards against them. Having guarded against the place of desire-thought and the place of desire-attachment, worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas do not leak into his mind according to the place of desire-thought and the place of desire-attachment.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「多闻圣弟子于一切苦法集、灭、味、患、离如实知见，见五欲犹如火坑。如是观察五欲已，于五欲贪、欲爱、欲念、欲著不永覆心，知其欲心行处、住处，而自防闭；行处、住处逆防闭已，随其行处、住处，世间贪、忧、恶不善法不漏其心。

What is meant by the noble disciple of much learning truly knowing and seeing the origin, cessation, taste, danger, and escape of all suffering dharmas? The noble disciple of much learning truly knows this noble truth of suffering, this origin of suffering, this cessation of suffering, and this noble truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering. This is called the noble disciple of much learning truly knowing and seeing the origin, cessation, taste, danger, and escape of all suffering dharmas.

「云何名为多闻圣弟子于一切苦法集、灭、味、患、离如实知见？多闻圣弟子于此苦圣谛如实知，此苦集、此苦灭、此苦灭道迹圣谛如实知，是名多闻圣弟子于一切苦法集、灭、味、患、离如实知见。

How does the noble disciple of much learning see the five desires as if they were a pit of fire, and how are worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas not permanently covered by his mind? Suppose there is a deep pit full of fire near a village, without smoke or flames. At that time, there is a man who is not foolish or deluded, but intelligent and wise, who enjoys happiness and dislikes suffering, and who likes to be born and dislikes death. He thinks: 'There is a pit of fire full of fire here. If I fall into it, I will surely die without a doubt.' He feels distant from it, thinks of it as distant, and wishes to be distant from it. In this way, the noble disciple of much learning sees the five desires as if they were a pit of fire, and worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas are not permanently covered by his mind. If he guards against the place of desire-thought and the place of desire-attachment, and knows them as they are, then worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas do not leak into his mind according to the place of desire-thought and the place of desire-attachment.

「云何多闻圣弟子见五欲如火坑，乃至世间贪、忧、恶不善法不永覆心？譬如近一聚落，边有深坑，满中盛火，无有烟焰。时，有士夫不愚不痴，聪明黠慧，乐乐厌苦，乐生恶死。彼作是念：『此有火坑，满中盛火，我若堕中，必死无疑。』于彼生远、思远、欲远。如是多闻圣弟子见五欲如火坑，乃至世间贪、忧、恶不善法不永覆心；若行处、住处逆防逆知，乃至世间贪、忧、恶不善法不漏其心。

It is like a forest of thorny trees near a village, full of thorns. At that time, a man enters the forest to do some work. After entering the forest, there are thorns all around, in front, behind, to the left, to the right, above, and below. At that time, the man walks with right mindfulness, comes and goes with right mindfulness, with right mindfulness and clear eyes, with right mindfulness and upright gaze, with right mindfulness and bending the body. Why is that? So as not to be injured by sharp thorns. The noble disciple of much learning is also like this. If he dwells in a village or town, in the morning he puts on his robe, takes his alms bowl, enters the village to beg for food, well protects his body, well holds his mind, abides in right mindfulness, walks with right mindfulness, with right mindfulness and clear eyes, observes with right mindfulness. Why is that? So as not to injure the noble Dharma and Vinaya with sharp thorns.

「譬如聚落边有［木＊奈］林，多诸棘刺，时，有士夫入于林中，有所营作。入林中已，前后左右上下尽有棘刺。尔时，士夫正念而行、正念来去、正念明目、正念端视、正念屈身。所以者何？莫令利刺伤坏身故。多闻圣弟子亦复如是，若依聚落城邑而住，晨朝著衣持钵，入村乞食，善护其身，善执其心，正念安住、正念而行、正念明目、正念观察。所以者何？莫令利刺伤圣法、律。

What are the sharp thorns that injure the noble Dharma and Vinaya? They are the desirable and lovable forms, which are called the sharp thorns that injure the noble Dharma and Vinaya. What are the desirable and lovable forms that injure the noble Dharma and Vinaya? They are the five sense pleasures. When eye consciousness arises from forms, it gives rise to love and nourishes sensual pleasure. When ear consciousness arises from sounds, nose consciousness arises from smells, tongue consciousness arises from tastes, and body consciousness arises from tangibles, it gives rise to love and nourishes sensual pleasure. These are called the desirable and lovable forms that injure the noble Dharma and Vinaya. These are called the places of practice and dwelling of the noble disciple of much learning, who guards against them and knows them as they are, so that worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas do not leak into his mind according to the places of desire-thought and desire-attachment.

「云何利刺伤圣法、律？谓可意爱念之色，是名利刺伤圣法、律。云何是可意爱念之色伤圣法、律？谓五欲功德，眼识色生爱念，长养欲乐；耳识声、鼻识香、舌识味、身识触生爱念，长养欲乐，是名可爱念色伤圣法、律；是名多闻圣弟子所行处、所住处逆防逆知，乃至不令世间贪、忧、不善法以漏其心。

"Sometimes the noble disciple of much learning gives rise to right mindfulness, but gives rise to evil and unwholesome thoughts, nourishing desire, nourishing anger, and nourishing delusion. This is a dull faculty. Although the noble disciple of much learning gives rise to the arising and cessation of desire, his mind is covered by desire. It is like a red-hot iron ball, sprinkled with a little water, which quickly dries up and disappears. In this way, the noble disciple of much learning with dull faculties gives rise to mindfulness, which quickly ceases in this way. The noble disciple of much learning practices and dwells like this. If a king, a minister, or a relative goes to him and invites him with a salary, saying, 'Man, why shave your head, hold a pottery vessel, wear a kasaya robe, and beg for food from house to house? It is better to enjoy the pleasures of the five desires, practice giving, and make merit.' What do you think, bhikṣu? If the noble disciple of much learning is invited with a salary by a king, a minister, or a relative, will he return to the precepts and reduce them?"

「或时多闻圣弟子生于正念，生恶不善觉，长养欲、长养恚、长养痴，是钝根；多闻圣弟子虽起集灭，以欲覆心。譬如铁丸，烧令极热，以少水洒，寻即干消，如是多闻圣弟子钝根生念，寻灭如是。多闻圣弟子如是行，如是住。若王、大臣若亲往诣其所，请以俸禄，语言：『男子！何用剃发，执持瓦器，身著袈裟，家家乞食为？不如安慰服五欲乐，行施作福。』云何？比丘！多闻圣弟子，国王、大臣、诸亲檀越请以俸禄，彼当还戒退减以不？」

"No, World-Honored One. Why is that? Because the noble disciple of much learning truly knows and sees the arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and abandonment of all suffering dharmas. He sees the pit of fire as an analogy for the five desires, and worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas do not permanently cover his mind. He guards against the places of practice and dwelling, and knows them as they are, so that worldly greed, sorrow, and unwholesome dharmas do not leak into his mind according to the places of desire-thought and desire-attachment. If he is again invited with a salary by a king, a minister, or a relative, it is impossible for him to return to the precepts and reduce them."

答曰：「不也。所以者何？多闻圣弟子于一切苦法集、灭、味、患、离如实知见故，见火坑，譬五欲，乃至世间贪、忧、恶不善法不永覆心，行处、住处逆防逆知，乃至世间贪、忧、恶不善法不漏其心。若复为国王、大臣、亲族请以俸禄，还戒退减，无有是处！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Excellent! Excellent! That noble disciple of much learning has for a long time been inclined, flowing, and rushing towards seclusion, towards detachment, towards the peace and tranquility of nirvāṇa, and delights in nirvāṇa. If he is invited with a salary by a king, a minister, or a relative, it is impossible for him to return to the precepts and reduce them. Otherwise, he will suffer greatly. It is like the Ganges River, which has for a long time been inclined, flowing, and rushing towards the east. If many people try to cut it off and make it flow and rush towards the west, can it be done?"

佛告诸比丘：「善哉！善哉！彼多闻圣弟子其心长夜临趣、流注、浚输，向于远离，向于离欲，而于涅槃，寂静舍离，乐于涅槃；于有漏处，寂灭清凉。若为国王、长者、亲族所请，还戒退减者，无有是处！余得大苦。譬如恒河，长夜临趣、流注、浚输东方，多众断截，欲令临趣、流注、浚输西方，宁能得不？」

"No, World-Honored One. Why is that? The Ganges River has for a long time been inclined, flowing, and rushing towards the east. If many people try to cut it off and make it flow and rush towards the west, it cannot be done. Those great masses will only suffer in vain! In the same way, that noble disciple of much learning has for a long time been inclined, flowing, and rushing towards seclusion, and so on. If one tries to make him retreat and reduce it, it cannot be done. It will only be in vain and cause suffering!"

答言：「不能。世尊！所以者何？恒水长夜流注东方，欲令西流，未而可得，彼诸大众徒辛苦耳！如是多闻圣弟子长夜临趣、流注、浚输，向于远离，乃至欲令退减，无有是处，徒辛苦耳！」

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行！
