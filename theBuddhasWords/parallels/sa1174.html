SĀ 1174

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1174（一一七四） 树

Parallel to: SN 35.241 / EĀ 43.3

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was staying by the banks of the Aciravatī River. At that time, a bhikṣu came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "Excellent! World-Honored One! Please teach the Dharma for me. After I have heard the Dharma, I will dwell alone in a quiet place, diligently contemplating and not being negligent. The reason why a noble son shaves his beard and hair, with right faith leaves home, goes forth to practice the path, and further cultivates the holy life, is to see the Dharma, know for himself, and realize: 'My births are exhausted, the holy life are established, what was to be done is done, and I know for myself that I will not undergo further rebirth.'"

一时，佛住阿毘阇恒水边。时，有比丘来诣佛所，稽首佛足，退住一面，白佛言：「善哉！世尊！为我说法，我闻法已，独一静处，专精思惟，不放逸住：『所以族姓子剃除须发，正信非家，出家学道，于上增修梵行，见法自知作证：「我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。」』」

At that time, the World-Honored One observed the water and saw a large tree in the river flowing downstream. He said to the bhikṣu, "Do you see the large tree in the river flowing downstream?"

尔时，世尊观察水，见恒水中有一大树，随流而下，语彼比丘：「汝见此恒水中大树流不？」

He replied, "I see it, World-Honored One!"

答言：「已见。世尊！」

The Buddha said to the bhikṣu, "This large tree does not cling to this shore, does not cling to the other shore, does not sink to the bottom of the water, does not obstruct the islands, does not enter the whirlpools, is not taken by people, is not taken by non-humans, and does not rot. Will it flow along with the water, flowing, flowing, and flowing into the great ocean?"

佛告比丘：「此大树不著此岸，不著彼岸，不沈水底，不阂洲渚，不入洄澓，人亦不取，非人不取，又不腐败，当随水流，顺趣、流注、浚输大海不？」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「如是。世尊！」

The Buddha said, "The bhikṣu is also like this. He does not cling to this shore, does not cling to the other shore, does not sink to the bottom of the water, does not obstruct the islands, does not enter the whirlpools, is not taken by people, is not taken by non-humans, and does not rot. He flows, flows, and flows into nirvāṇa."

佛言：「比丘亦复如是。亦不著此岸，不著彼岸，不沈水底，不阂洲渚，不入洄澓，人亦不取，非人不取，又不腐败，临趣、流注、浚输涅槃。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "What is this shore? What is the other shore? What is sinking? What are the islands? What are the whirlpools? What is taken by people? What is taken by non-humans? What is rotting? Excellent, World-Honored One! Please explain it to me in detail. After I hear the Dharma, I will dwell alone in a quiet place, diligently contemplating and not being negligent, and I will know for myself that I will not be reborn again."

比丘白佛：「云何此岸？云何彼岸？云何沉没？云何洲渚？云何洄澓？云何人取？云何非人取？云何腐败？善哉世尊！为我广说，我闻法已，当独一静处，专精思惟，不放逸住，乃至自知不受后有。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "This shore refers to the six sense bases. The other shore refers to the six external sense bases. Being taken by people is like someone who associates with laypeople and renunciants, sharing their joys, sorrows, sufferings, and pleasures, and doing things together with them. They start and end together. This is being taken by people. Being taken by non-humans is like someone who wishes to practice the holy life, thinking, 'I now uphold the precepts, practice austerities, and cultivate various holy life. I will be reborn in such and such a place, in the heavens.' This is being taken by non-humans. Whirlpools are like someone who regresses from the precepts. Rotting refers to breaking the precepts, practicing evil and unwholesome dharmas, being ignorant, like weeds and chaff, the sound of a conch, not being a śramaṇa but appearing to be a śramaṇa, not practicing the holy life but appearing to practice the holy life. Thus, bhikṣu! This is called not clinging to this shore or the other shore, and flowing, flowing, and flowing into nirvāṇa."

佛告比丘：「此岸者，谓六入处。彼岸者，谓六外入处。人取者，犹如有一习近俗人及出家者，若喜、若忧、若苦、若乐，彼彼所作，悉与共同，始终相随，是名人取。非人取者，犹如有人愿修梵行，我今持戒、苦行、修诸梵行，当生在处，在处天上，是非人取。洄澓者，犹如有一还戒退转。腐败者，犯戒行恶不善法，腐败寡闻，犹莠稗、吹贝之声，非沙门为沙门像，非梵行为梵行像。如是，比丘！是名不著此彼岸，乃至浚输涅槃。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully rejoiced, paid homage, and departed.

时，彼比丘闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。

At that time, the bhikṣu, alone in a quiet place, contemplated the teachings of the Water Flowing and Great Tree Sutra spoken by the Buddha, and attained arahantship, knowing for himself that he would not be reborn again.

时，彼比丘独一静处，思惟佛所说水流大树经教，乃至自知不受后有，得阿罗汉。

At that time, a cowherd named Nandu was not far from the Buddha, holding a staff to herd the cows. After the bhikṣu left, he went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Can I now leave behind this shore, leave behind the other shore, not sink, not be obstructed by islands, not be taken by non-humans, not be taken by non-humans, not enter whirlpools, and not decay? Can I go forth and practice the holy life in the World-Honored One's true Dharma and Vinaya?"

时，有牧牛人，名难屠，去佛不远，执杖牧牛。比丘去已，诣世尊所，稽首礼足，于一面住，白佛言：「世尊！我今堪能不著此岸，不著彼岸，不沉没，不阂洲渚，非人所取，不非人取，不入洄澓，亦不腐败，我得于世尊正法、律中出家修梵行不？」

The Buddha said to the cowherd, "Did you return the cows to their owner?"

佛告牧牛者：「汝送牛还主不？」

The cowherd said, "All the cows have calves and can return by themselves. There is no need to send them back. I only ask to be allowed to go forth and study the path."

牧牛者言：「诸牛中悉有犊牛，自能还归，不须送也，但当听我出家学道。」

The Buddha said to the cowherd, "Although the cows can return home, you have already received the food and clothing of others. You must repay the owner."

佛告牧牛者：「牛虽能还家，汝今已受食人衣食，要当还报其家主。」

At that time, the cowherd, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully followed and paid homage before departing.

时，牧牛者闻佛教已，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra was in this assembly. Not long after the cowherd left, he addressed the Buddha, saying, "World-Honored One! The cowherd who was difficult to slaughter wished to go forth. Why did the World-Honored One send him back home?"

时，尊者舍利弗在此会中，牧牛者去不久，白佛言：「世尊！难屠牧牛者求欲出家，世尊何故遣还归家？」

The Buddha told Śāriputra, "It is impossible for the cowherd who is difficult to slaughter to return home and indulge in the five desires! After entrusting the cattle to the owner, he will certainly return to go forth in this Dharma and Vinaya, practice the holy life, and even know for himself that he will not be reborn again, attaining arahantship."

佛告舍利弗：「难屠牧牛者若还住家受五欲者，无有是处！牛付主人已，辄自当还，于此法，律出家学道，净修梵行，乃至自知不受后有，得阿罗汉。」

At that time, the cowherd who was difficult to slaughter entrusted the cattle to the owner, returned to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha, saying, "World-Honored One! The cattle have been entrusted to the owner. Please allow me to go forth in the true Dharma and Vinaya to practice the path."

时，难屠牧牛者以牛付主人已，还至佛所，稽首礼足，退住一面，白佛言：「世尊！牛已付主，听我于正法、律出家学道。」

The Buddha told the cowherd who was difficult to slaughter, "You may go forth in this Dharma and Vinaya, receive full ordination, and attain the status of a bhikṣu."

佛告难屠牧牛者：「汝得于此法、律出家、受具足，得比丘分。」

After going forth, he contemplated, "The reason why a clansman shaves his beard and hair, puts on the monastic robe, with right faith leaves home to study the path, and cultivates the holy life, is to know for himself that he will not be reborn again." He attained arahantship.

出家已，思惟：「所以族姓子剃除须发，著袈裟衣，正信非家，出家学道，增修梵行，乃至自知不受后有。」成阿罗汉。
