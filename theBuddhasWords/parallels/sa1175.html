SĀ 1175

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1175（一一七五） 紧兽

Parallel to: SN 35.245

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu was sitting alone in meditation, thinking, "How should a bhikṣu know and see in order to attain pure vision?" Having thought this, he went to the bhikṣus and said to them, "Venerable bhikṣus! How should one know and see in order to attain pure vision?"

时，有异比丘独处坐禅，作是思惟：「比丘云何知、云何见，得见清净？」作是念已，诣诸比丘，语诸比丘言：「诸尊比丘！云何知、云何见，令见清净？」

The bhikṣus replied, "Venerable one! It is by knowing and seeing the arising, cessation, taste, danger, and escape of the six sense bases as they really are. Bhikṣu! One who knows and sees in this way attains pure vision."

比丘答言：「尊者！于六触入处集灭、味、患、离如实正知。比丘！作如是知、如是见者，得见清净。」

Having heard the bhikṣu's explanation, the bhikṣu was not pleased. He then went to other bhikṣus and asked them, "Venerable bhikṣus! How should one know and see in order to attain pure vision?"

是比丘闻彼比丘记说，心不欢喜，复诣余比丘所，问彼比丘言：「诸尊比丘！云何知、云何见，得见清净？」

The bhikṣus replied, "It is by knowing and seeing the arising, cessation, taste, danger, and escape of the six elements as they really are. Bhikṣu! One who knows and sees in this way attains pure vision."

彼比丘答言：「于六界集、灭、味、患、离如实正知。如是，比丘！如是知、如是见，得见清净。」

The bhikṣu, having heard the bhikṣu's explanation, was not pleased. He then went to other bhikṣus and asked them, "Bhikṣus! How should one know and see in order to attain pure vision?"

时，比丘闻其记说，心亦不喜，复诣余比丘，作是问言：「比丘！云何知、云何见，得见清净？」

The bhikṣus replied, "It is by contemplating the five aggregates as being like a disease, a boil, a thorn, and a killer, as impermanent, suffering, empty, and non-self. By knowing and seeing in this way, one attains pure vision."

彼比丘答言：「于五受阴观察，如病、如痈、如刺、如杀，无常、苦、空、非我；作如是知、如是见，得见清净。」

The bhikṣu, having heard the explanations of the bhikṣus, was not pleased. He went to the Buddha, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I was alone, in quiet contemplation, thinking, 'How do bhikṣus know and see in order to attain pure vision?' Having had this thought, I went to the bhikṣus and asked them about it. I have now told the World-Honored One what they said. When I heard their explanations, I was not pleased. I have come to the World-Honored One to ask about this meaning. How do bhikṣus know and see in order to attain pure vision?"

是比丘闻诸比丘记说，心亦不喜，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我独静思惟：『比丘云何知、云何见，得见清净？』作是念已，诣诸比丘，三处所说，具白世尊，我闻彼说，心不欢喜，来诣世尊。故以此义请问世尊：『比丘云何知、云何见，得见清净？』」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "In the past, there was a man who had never seen a tiger. He went to someone who had seen a tiger and asked, 'Do you know what a tiger looks like?' The other replied, 'Yes, I know.' He then asked, 'What does it look like?' The other replied, 'Its color is black, like a pillar burned by fire.' At that time, the tiger he saw was indeed black, like a pillar burned by fire.

佛告比丘：「过去世时，有一士夫，未曾见紧兽，往诣曾见紧兽者，问曾见紧兽士夫言：『汝知紧兽不？』答言：『知。』复问：『其状云何？』答言：『其色黑如火烧柱。』当彼见时，紧兽黑色，如火烧柱。

At that time, the man, having heard that the tiger was black like a pillar burned by fire, was not very pleased. He went to another man who had seen a tiger and asked, 'Do you know what a tiger looks like?' The other replied, 'Yes, I know.' He then asked, 'What does it look like?' The man who had seen a tiger replied, 'Its color is red and open, like a piece of meat.' At that time, the tiger he saw was indeed open, like a piece of meat.

「时，彼士夫闻紧兽黑色如火烧柱，不大欢喜，复更诣一曾见紧兽士夫，复问彼言：『汝知紧兽不？』彼答言：『知。』复问：『其状云何？』彼曾见紧兽士夫答言：『其色赤而开敷，状似肉段。』彼人见时，紧兽开敷，实似肉段。

The man, having heard what was said, was still not pleased. He went to another man who had seen a tiger and asked, 'Do you know what a tiger looks like?' The other replied, 'Yes, I know.' He then asked, 'What does it look like?' The man who had seen a tiger replied, 'It is shaggy and drooping, like a śirīṣa fruit.'

「是士夫闻彼所说，犹复不喜，复更诣余曾见紧兽士夫，问：『汝知紧兽不？』答言：『知。』复问：『其状云何？』答言：『毵毵下垂，如尸利沙果。』

The man, having heard this, was still not pleased. He went to ask another person who knew about tigers. He asked, 'Do you know what a tiger looks like?' The other replied, 'Yes, I know.' He then asked, 'What does it look like?' The other replied, 'Its leaves are green, its leaves are smooth, and its leaves are long and wide, like a nyagrodha tree.' Just as that man asked about the tiger and was not pleased upon hearing it, he went to seek it elsewhere. The people who had seen the tiger described it according to what they had seen, and therefore it was not the same.

「是人闻已，心复不喜，复行问余知紧兽者，问：『汝知紧兽不？』彼答言：『知。』又问：『其状云何？』彼复答言：『其叶青、其叶滑、其叶长广，如尼拘娄陀树。』如彼士夫问其紧兽，闻则不喜，处处更求，而彼诸人见紧兽者，随时所见，而为记说，是故不同。

Thus, monks, if one dwells in solitude, diligently contemplating, not being negligent, and the dharmas that arise from contemplation do not give rise to the taints, the mind is liberated, and one describes according to what one has seen. Now listen again as I speak a parable. The wise understand through parables.

「如是，诸比丘！若于独处专精思惟，不放逸住，所因思惟法不起诸漏，心得解脱，随彼所见，而为记说。汝今复听我说譬，其智者以譬喻得解。

It is like a border king who well governs the city walls, the gates are strong, the crossroads are level, and at the four city gates he places four guards, all of them wise, knowing who comes and goes. In the middle of the city, at the four crossroads, he places a bed and the city lord sits on it. If an envoy from the east comes and asks the gatekeeper, 'Where is the city lord?' he will reply, 'The lord is sitting on a bed at the crossroads in the middle of the city.' Having heard this, the envoy goes to the city lord, receives his instructions, and returns by the same route. When envoys from the south, west, and north come, they ask the gatekeeper, 'Where is the city lord?' He also replies, 'He is at the crossroads in the middle of the city.' Having heard this, the envoys all go to the city lord, receive his instructions, and return to their respective places.

「譬如有边国王，善治城壁，门下坚固，交道平正，于四城门置四守护，悉皆聪慧，知其来去。当其城中，有四交道，安置床榻，城主坐上。若东方使来，问守门者：『城主何在？』彼即答言：『主在城中四交道头，床上而坐。』彼使闻已，往诣城主，受其教令，复道而还。南、西、北方远使来人，问守门者：『城主何在？』彼亦答言：『在其城中四交道头。』彼使闻已，悉诣城主，受其教令，各还本处。」

The Buddha told the monks, "I have spoken this parable. Now I will explain the meaning. The city is likened to the coarse form of the human body, like the poisonous snake in the basket as described in the sutra. 'Well-governed city walls' refers to right view. 'Level crossroads' refers to the six internal sense bases. 'Four gates' refers to the four abodes of consciousness. 'Four gatekeepers' refers to the four foundations of mindfulness. 'City lord' refers to the consciousness aggregate of feeling. 'Envoys' refers to tranquility and insight. 'Speaking the truth' refers to the four noble truths. 'Returning by the same route' refers to the eightfold noble path.

佛告比丘：「我说斯譬，今当说义。所谓城者，以譬人身麁色，如箧毒蛇譬经说。善治城壁者，谓之正见。交道平正者，谓内六入处。四门者，谓四识住。四守门者，谓四念处。城主者，谓识受阴。使者，谓止、观。如实言者，谓四真谛。复道还者，以八圣道。」

The Buddha told the monks, "If a great teacher does something for his disciples, I have now done it out of compassion, as described in the sutra about the poisonous snake in the basket.

佛告比丘：「若大师为弟子所作，我今已作，以哀愍故，如箧毒蛇譬经说。」

At that time, the monks, having heard the Buddha's teaching, diligently contemplated and abided without negligence, increasing their practice of the holy life, and ultimately not accepting future existence, becoming arhats.

尔时，比丘闻佛说已，专精思惟，不放逸住，增修梵行，乃至不受后有，成阿罗汉。
