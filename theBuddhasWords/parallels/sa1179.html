SĀ 1179

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1179（一一七九） 失牛

Parallel to: SN 7.10 / SĀ² 2.93

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Great Forest Monastery in the country of Vaiśālī.

一时，佛住毘舍离国大林精舍。

At that time, there was a brahmin named Vīrya who bought a cow in the morning. Before he paid for it, he lost the cow on the same day and did not see it for six days.

时，有毘梨耶婆罗豆婆遮婆罗门，晨朝买牛，未偿其价，即日失牛，六日不见。

At that time, the brahmin, in search of the cow, went to the Great Forest Monastery. From afar, he saw the World-Honored One sitting under a tree, with an outstanding appearance, pure faculties, a tranquil mind, accomplished in tranquility and insight, with a golden body radiating brilliant light. Having seen this, he approached and spoke a verse:

时，婆罗门为觅牛故，至大林精舍，遥见世尊坐一树下，仪容挺特，诸根清净，其心寂默，成就止观，其身金色，光明焰照。见已，即诣其前，而说偈言：

How can one be without seeking, dwelling in emptiness and tranquility
alone in an empty and quiet place, and attain mental joy?

「云何无所求，　　空寂在于此，独一处空闲，　　而得心所乐？」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，世尊说偈答曰：

Whether lost or gained, my mind is not disturbed
Brahmins should know, do not think that I am
like them, calculating gain and loss, their minds not at ease.

「若失若复得，　　于我心不乱。婆罗门当知，　　莫谓彼如我，心计于得失，　　其心不自在。」

At that time, the brahmin again spoke a verse:

时，婆罗门复说偈言：

The supreme brahman is as the bhikshu has said; I will
now speak the truth, listen attentively. The shamana is certainly not
the one who lost the cows in the morning, seeking for
six days without finding them, therefore he dwells at ease. The
shamana is certainly not the one who planted sesame fields, fearing
they will be overgrown with weeds, therefore he dwells at ease
The shamana is certainly not the one who planted rice fields
lacking water, fearing the leaves will wither and die, therefore he
dwells at ease. The shamana is certainly not the one with
seven widows, all raising orphaned children, therefore he dwells at ease
The shamana is certainly not the one with seven unloved children
who are negligent and have many debts, therefore he dwells at
ease. The shamana is certainly not the one with creditors guarding
his door, seeking to collect interest on his wealth, therefore he
dwells at ease. The shamana is certainly not the one with
seven heavy blankets, worrying about choosing out the insects, therefore he
dwells at ease. The shamana is certainly not the one with
a wife with red eyes and yellow hair, hearing evil sounds
day and night, therefore he dwells at ease. The shamana is
certainly not the one with empty granaries where rats play, always
worrying about their weakness, therefore he dwells at ease.

「最胜梵志处，　　如比丘所说；我今当自说，　　真实语谛听。沙门今定非，　　晨朝失牛者，六日求不得，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定非，　　种殖胡麻田，虑其草荒没，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定非，　　种稻田乏水，畏叶枯便死，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定无，　　寡女有七人，悉养孤遗子，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定无，　　七不爱念子，放逸多负债，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定无，　　债主守其门，求索长息财，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定无，　　七领重卧具，忧勤择诸虫，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定无，　　赤眼黄发妇，昼夜闻恶声，　　是故安乐住。沙门今定无，　　空仓群鼠戏，常忧其羸乏，　　是故安乐住。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，世尊说偈答言：

I am certainly not the one who lost the cows in
the morning, seeking for six days without finding them, therefore I
dwell at ease. I am certainly not the one who planted
sesame fields, fearing they will be overgrown with weeds, therefore I
dwell at ease. I am certainly not the one who planted
rice fields lacking water, fearing the leaves will wither and die
therefore I dwell at ease. I am certainly not the one
with seven widows, all raising orphaned children, therefore I dwell at
ease. I am certainly not the one with seven unloved children
who are negligent and have many debts, therefore I dwell at
ease. I am certainly not the one with creditors guarding my
door, seeking to collect interest on my wealth, therefore I dwell
at ease. I am certainly not the one with seven heavy
blankets, worrying about choosing out the insects, therefore I dwell at
ease. I am certainly not the one with a wife with
red eyes and yellow hair, hearing evil sounds day and night
therefore I dwell at ease. I am certainly not the one
with empty granaries where rats play, always worrying about their weakness
therefore I dwell at ease. Not abandoning mindfulness and non-mindfulness, sentient
beings dwell at ease. Cutting off desires and leaving behind affection
one attains ease and happiness.

「我今日定不，　　晨朝失其牛，六日求不得，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　种殖胡麻田，常恐其荒没，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　种稻田乏水，畏叶便枯死，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　寡女有七人，悉养孤遗子，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　七不爱念子，放逸多负债，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　债主守其门，求索长息财，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　七领重卧具，忧勤择诸虫，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　黄头赤眼妇，昼夜闻恶声，　　是故安乐住。我今日定无，　　空仓群鼠戏，常忧其羸乏，　　是故安乐住。不舍念不念，　　众生安乐住，断欲离恩爱，　　而得安乐住。」

At that time, the World-Honored One expounded the Dharma for the Brāhman Pāyāsi in various ways, teaching, illuminating, and delighting him, as is the constant practice of the Buddha. He expounded the Dharma in stages, speaking of giving, upholding precepts, and so forth, up to the true Dharma, where the mind attains fearlessness. He then rose from his seat, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "May I now go forth and practice the path in the true Dharma and Vinaya, attain the status of a bhikṣu, and cultivate the holy life?"

尔时，世尊为精进婆罗豆婆遮婆罗门种种说法，示、教、照、喜，如佛常法，次第说法，布施、持戒，乃至于正法中，心得无畏。即从座起，合掌白佛：「我今得于正法、律出家学道，成比丘分，修梵行不？」

The Buddha told the Brāhman, "You may now go forth and practice the path in the true Dharma and Vinaya, attain the status of a bhikṣu, cultivate the holy life, and even attain arahantship with the mind well liberated."

佛告婆罗门：「汝今可得于正法、律出家、受具足，修诸梵行，乃至得阿罗汉，心善解脱。」

At that time, the Brāhman Pāyāsi attained arahantship and personally realized and understood the bliss of liberation, and spoke a verse:

尔时，精进婆罗豆婆遮婆罗门得阿罗汉，缘自觉知，得解脱乐，而说偈言：

I am now very joyful and delighted in the great sage's supreme Dharma
having attained the bliss of freedom from desire and not seeing the Buddha.

「我今甚欣乐，　　大仙法之上，得离贪欲乐，　　不生见于佛。」
