SĀ 1190

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1190（一一九〇） 梵主

Parallel to: SN 6.11 / SĀ² 2.103

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was staying at the Nairañjanā River near the Bodhi Tree in the village of Uruvilvā, not long after his enlightenment.

一时，佛住欝毘罗聚落尼连禅河侧菩提树下，成佛未久。

At that time, the Lord of the Sahā World, the Brahmā King, with his wondrous form, came to where the Buddha was in the latter part of the night, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, withdrew and sat to one side, and spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，娑婆世界主梵天王绝妙色身，于后夜时来诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面，而说偈言：

Among all the clans, the Śākya clan is the most noble. The Buddha, the
most honored among humans and gods, is the most excellent one with wisdom and practice.

「于诸种姓中，　　刹利两足尊，明行具足者，　　天人中最胜！」

The Buddha told the Brahmā King: "So it is, Brahmā King! So it is, Brahmā King!

佛告梵天王：「如是，梵天！如是，梵天！

Among all the clans, the Śākya clan is the most noble. The Buddha, the
most honored among humans and gods, is the most excellent one with wisdom and practice.

「于诸种姓中，　　刹利两足尊，明行具足者，　　天人中最胜。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Brahmā King, the Lord of the Sahā World, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and followed along. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and then disappeared.

佛说是经已，娑婆世界主梵天王闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，稽首佛足，即没不现。
