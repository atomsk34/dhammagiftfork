SĀ 1213

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1213（一二一三） 不乐

Parallel to: SN 8.2 / SĀ² 2.229

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, Venerable Nigrodha was staying in a wilderness inhabited by birds and beasts. Venerable Vāgīśa, who had recently gone forth, had such deportment. He stayed in villages and towns, putting on his robes and taking his alms bowl in the morning, begging for alms in those villages and towns. He well protected his body, guarded the sense-doors, and concentrated his mind. After eating, he returned to his dwelling place, put away his robes and bowl, washed his feet, entered the room, and sat in meditation. He quickly emerged from meditation, not attached to begging for alms. He had no one to instruct him in a timely manner, no one to admonish him, and his mind was not at ease, hidden all around. He deeply abided in this way.

尔时，尊者尼拘律相住于旷野禽兽住处。尊者婆耆舍出家未久，有如是威仪，依聚落城邑住，晨朝著衣持钵，于彼聚落城邑乞食，善护其身，守诸根门，摄心系念。食已，还住处，举衣钵，洗足毕，入室坐禅。速从禅觉，不著乞食，而彼无有随时教授、无有教诫者，心不安乐，周圆隐覆。如是深住。

At that time, Venerable Vāgīśa thought: "I have not gained any benefit, which is difficult to obtain and not easy to obtain. I do not obtain timely instruction and admonition, and I do not obtain a mind that is pleased, all-around hidden, and deeply abiding. I shall now praise and lament with verses." He then spoke the verses:

时，尊者婆耆舍作是念：「我不得利，难得非易得。我不随时得教授、教诫，不得欣乐周圆隐覆心住。我今当赞叹自厌之偈。」即说偈言：

Abandon pleasure and displeasure, and all greedy thoughts. In the neighborhood, do
nothing. The one who is free from defilement is called a bhikṣu
The mind of the six kinds of thoughts runs wild in the
world. Evil and unwholesome things are hidden and covered, unable to remove
the skin and flesh. The defilement delights in the mind, which is
not called a bhikṣu. There are other bonds that bind, seeing, hearing
and knowing together. For one who is awakened to the thought of
desire, that place is no longer defiled. One who is not defiled
in this way is then called a sage. The great earth and
empty space, the various forms in the world, all are subject to
destruction. They are naturally determined to be still. The Dharma vessel is
cultivated for a long time, and then attains samādhi. Not touching, not
deceitful, the mind is extremely focused. The noble one has long entered
nirvāṇa, keeping mindfulness, waiting for the time of cessation.

「当舍乐不乐，　　及一切贪觉，于隣无所作，　　离染名比丘。于六觉心想，　　驰骋于世间，恶不善隐覆，　　不能去皮肤。秽污乐于心，　　是不名比丘，有余缚所缚，　　见闻觉识俱。于欲觉悟者，　　彼处不复染，如是不染者，　　是则为牟尼。大地及虚空，　　世间诸色像，斯皆磨灭法，　　寂然自决定。法器久修习，　　而得三摩提，不触不谄伪，　　其心极专至。彼圣久涅槃，　　系念待时灭。」

At that time, Venerable Vāgīśa spoke the verses of self-reproach and detachment, and his mind was self-awakened. Having awakened to displeasure and so forth, his mind was pleased and abiding.

时，尊者婆耆舍说自厌离偈，心自开觉，于不乐等开觉已，欣乐心住。
