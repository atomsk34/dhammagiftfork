SĀ 1228

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1228（一二二八） 爱己

Parallel to: SN 3.4 / SĀ² 2.55

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At one time King Prasenajit was alone in a quiet place, absorbed in meditation, and he thought, “What is self-reflection? What is not self-reflection?” He further thought, “If one engages in evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that this is not self-reflection. If one engages in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that this is self-reflection.” Having emerged from meditation, he went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha’s feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, “World-Honored One! While I was alone in a quiet place, absorbed in meditation, I thought, ‘What is self-reflection? What is not self-reflection?’ I further thought, ‘If one engages in evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that this is not self-reflection. If one engages in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that this is self-reflection.’”

时，波斯匿王独一静处，禅思思惟，作是念：「云何为自念？云何为不自念？」复作是念：「若有行身恶行、行口恶行、行意恶行者，当知斯等为不自念。若复行身善行、行口善行、行意善行者，当知斯等则为自念。」从禅觉已，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，退住一面，白佛言：「世尊！我于静处独一思惟，作是念：『云何为自念？云何为不自念？』复作是念：『若有行身恶行、行口恶行、行意恶行者，当知斯等为不自念。若复行身善行、行口善行、行意善行者，当知斯等则为自念。』」

The Buddha told the great king, “Yes, great king! Yes, great king! If one engages in evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that this is not self-reflection. Although they may think, ‘I love and care for myself,’ it is not truly self-reflection. Why is that? There is no one who does evil deeds with evil friends and does not reflect on them. There is no one who does not love what they do not reflect on. There is no one who does not love what they do not love. There is no one who does what they do for themselves. Therefore, this is not self-reflection.

佛告大王：「如是，大王！如是，大王！若有行身恶行、行口恶行、行意恶行者，当知斯等为不自念。彼虽自谓：『为自爱念。』而实非自念。所以者何？无有恶知识所作恶不念者，所不念不爱者，所不爱所作如其自为自己所作者，是故斯等为不自念。

“Great king! If one engages in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that this is self-reflection. They may think, ‘I do not love and care for myself,’ but in fact they do love and care for themselves. Why is that? There is no one who has good friends and does not reflect on them. There is no one who does not love what they reflect on. There is no one who does not love what they love. There is no one who does what they do for themselves. Therefore, this is self-reflection.” At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

「若复，大王！行身善行、行口善行、行意善行者，当知斯等则为自念，斯等自谓：『不自爱惜己身。』然其斯等实为自念。所以者何？无有善友，于善友所作念者，念作爱者，爱作如自为己所作者，是故斯等则为自念。」尔时，世尊复说偈言：

Those who reflect on themselves should not engage in evil conduct
Ultimately, evil conduct will not bring one peace and happiness. Those
who reflect on themselves should not engage in evil conduct. Those
who engage in good conduct will bring themselves peace and happiness
If one loves and cares for oneself, one should protect oneself
well, like a king who guards the border cities. If one
loves and cares for oneself, one should protect oneself well, like
a king who guards the inner border cities. Such a treasure
is uninterrupted and flawless. If it is flawed for a moment
one will suffer in the evil realms for a long time.

「谓为自念者，　　不应造恶行，终不因恶行，　　令己得安乐。谓为自念者，　　终不造恶行，造诸善业者，　　令己得安乐。若自爱念者，　　善护而自护，如善护国王，　　外防边境城。若自爱念者，　　极善自宝藏，如善守之王，　　内防边境城。如是自宝藏，　　刹那无间缺，刹那缺致忧，　　恶道长受苦。」

Having spoken this sūtra, King Prasenajit, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and paid homage before departing.

佛说此经已，波斯匿王闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
