SĀ 1229

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1229（一二二九） 护己

Parallel to: SN 3.5 / SĀ² 2.56

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, King Prasenajit was alone in quiet contemplation, thinking to himself, "How does one protect oneself? How does one not protect oneself?" He further thought, "If one engages in evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that one does not protect oneself. If one engages in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that one protects oneself." Having arisen from meditation, he went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I was alone in quiet contemplation, thinking to myself, 'How does one protect oneself? How does one not protect oneself?' I further thought, 'If one engages in evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that one does not protect oneself. If one engages in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that one protects oneself.'"

尔时，波斯匿王独静思惟，作如是念：「云何自护？云何不自护？」复作是念：「若有行身恶行、行口恶行、行意恶行者，当知斯等为不自护。若复行身善行、行口善行、行意善行者，当知斯等则为自护。」从禅觉已，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我独静思惟，而作是念：『云何为自护？云何为不自护？』复作是念：『若有行身恶行、行口恶行、行意恶行者，当知斯等为不自护。若复行身善行、行口善行、行意善行者，当知斯等则为自护。』」

The Buddha told the great king, "So it is, great king! So it is, great king! If one engages in evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that one does not protect oneself, yet they claim to be able to protect themselves. Protecting oneself with an army of elephants, horses, chariots, and infantry, although claiming to protect oneself, is not true self-protection. Why is that? Although protecting the outside, one does not protect the inside. Therefore, great king, it is called not protecting oneself. Great king! If one engages in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, one should know that one protects oneself. Although they do not protect themselves with an army of elephants, horses, chariots, and infantry, they truly protect themselves. Why is that? Protecting the inside is called good self-protection, not protecting the outside." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

佛告大王：「如是，大王！如是，大王！若有行身恶行、行口恶行、行意恶行者，当知斯等为不自护，而彼自谓能自防护。象军、马军、车军、步军以自防护，虽谓自护，实非自护。所以者何？虽护于外，不护于内。是故，大王！名不自护。大王！若复有行身善行、行口善行、行意善行者，当知斯等则为自护。彼虽不以象、马、车、步四军自防，而实自护。所以者何？护其内者，名善自护，非谓防外。」尔时，世尊复说偈言：

Well guarding body, speech, and mind, and all karmic actions, with
a sense of shame and self-restraint, this is called good protection.

「善护于身口，　　及意一切业，惭愧而自防，　　是名善守护。」

At that time, King Prasenajit, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully rejoiced, paid homage, and departed.

时，波斯匿王闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
