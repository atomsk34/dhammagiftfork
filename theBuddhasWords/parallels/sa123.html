SĀ 123

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 123（一二三） 爱喜贪

Parallel to: SN 23.9–10

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying on Mount Makura.

一时，佛住摩拘罗山。

At that time, there was an attendant bhikṣu named Rathodana who went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. He addressed the Buddha, saying, "Excellent! World-Honored One! Please briefly teach me the essentials of the Dharma. Having heard the Dharma, I will dwell alone in a quiet place, contemplating with focused mind, and not be negligent. The reason why a noble son shaves his head and face, puts on dyed robes, leaves home with right faith, and goes forth to practice the path is to increase his diligence, cultivate the practices of purity, see the Dharma, and know for himself that he has attained the realization: 'My births are exhausted, the holy life are established, what was to be done is done, and I know for myself that I will not undergo further rebirth.'"

时，有侍者比丘名曰罗陀，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白佛言：「善哉！世尊！为我略说法要，我闻法已，我当独一静处，专心思惟，不放逸住：『所以族姓子剃除须发，身著染衣，正信非家，出家学道，增加精进，修诸梵行，见法自知作证：「我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。」』」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Rathodana, "Excellent! Rathodana! You are able to ask the Buddha about such a matter in front of the Buddha. Listen carefully and contemplate well, I will explain it to you. Rathodana! You should know that there is existence, the origin of existence, the cessation of existence, and the path leading to the cessation of existence. What is existence? It refers to the five aggregates of clinging - the form aggregate of clinging, the feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness aggregates of clinging. What is the origin of existence? It refers to the craving for future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, taking pleasure in this and that. This is called the origin of existence. What is the cessation of existence? It refers to the complete abandonment, elimination, and cessation of the craving for future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, taking pleasure in this and that. This is called the cessation of existence. What is the path leading to the cessation of existence? It refers to the eightfold noble path - right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called the path leading to the cessation of existence. One should know existence, abandon the origin of existence, realize the cessation of existence, and cultivate the path leading to the cessation of existence. Rathodana! If a learned noble disciple knows and abandons existence, knows and abandons the origin of existence, knows and realizes the cessation of existence, and knows and cultivates the path leading to the cessation of existence, Rathodana! It is called cutting off craving, being free from craving, turning away from attachment, stopping conceit, and ultimately reaching the end of suffering."

尔时，世尊告罗陀曰：「善哉！罗陀！能于佛前问如是义。谛听，善思，当为汝说。罗陀！当知有身、有身集、有身灭、有身灭道迹。何等为有身？谓五受阴——色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴。云何有身集？谓当来有爱，贪、喜俱，于彼彼爱乐，是名有身集。云何有身灭？谓当有爱，喜、贪俱，彼彼爱乐无余断、舍、吐、尽、离欲、寂、没，是名有身尽。云何有身灭道迹？谓八正道——正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定，是名有身灭道迹。有身当知，有身集当断，有身灭当证，有身灭道迹当修。罗陀！若多闻圣弟子于有身若知、若断，有身集若知、若断，有身灭若知、若证，有身灭道迹若知、若修已，罗陀！名断爱、离爱、转结，止慢无间等，究竟苦边。」

Bhikṣu Rathodana, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully accepted and practiced it. He rose from his seat, bowed, and departed. After the World-Honored One had instructed him thus, Bhikṣu Rathodana dwelled alone in a quiet place, contemplating with focused mind. The reason why a noble son shaves his head and face, puts on dyed robes, leaves home with right faith, and goes forth to practice the path is to increase his diligence, cultivate the practices of purity, see the Dharma, and know for himself that he has attained the realization: 'My births are exhausted, the holy life are established, what was to be done is done, and I know for myself that I will not undergo further rebirth.' He became an arhat, with a well-liberated mind.

罗陀比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。从坐起，作礼而去。世尊如是教授已，罗陀比丘独一静处，专精思惟。所以善男子剃除须发，著染色衣，正信非家，出家学道，增益精进，修诸梵行，见法自知作证：「我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。」成阿罗汉，心善解脱。

Having spoken this sūtra, Bhikṣu Rathodana, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully accepted and practiced it.

佛说此经已，罗陀比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
