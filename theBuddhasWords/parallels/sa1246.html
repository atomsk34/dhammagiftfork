SĀ 1246

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1246（一二四六） 铸金者

Parallel to: SN 46.33 (~) / AN 3.101 / AN 5.23 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the residence of a goldsmith in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城金师住处。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Just as a goldsmith collects sand and earth, places it in a trough, and then pours water over it, the coarse and hard stones and lumps are carried away by the water, leaving only coarse sand entangled. He then pours water over it again, and the coarse sand is carried away by the water. Then gold appears, but it is still entangled with fine sand and black soil. He then pours water over it again, and the fine sand and black soil are carried away by the water. Then pure gold appears, free from impurities, but it still has a slight resemblance to gold. He then places it in a furnace, increases the fire, and blows on it, causing it to melt. The impurities are all removed, but the gold that has been produced is still not light, not soft, and does not emit light. If it is bent, it breaks. The goldsmith and his apprentice then place it in a furnace again, increase the fire, and blow on it, turning and refining it. Then the gold that has been produced becomes light, soft, lustrous, and can be bent without breaking. It can be used to make various ornaments such as hairpins, pots, bracelets, and anklets.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「如铸金者，积聚沙土，置于槽中，然后以水灌之，麁上烦恼、刚石坚块随水而去，犹有麁沙缠结。复以水灌，麁沙随水流出，然后生金，犹为细沙、黑土之所缠结。复以水灌，细沙、黑土随水流出，然后真金纯净无杂，犹有似金微垢。然后金师置于炉中，增火鼓𫖔，令其融液，垢秽悉除，然其生金犹故，不轻、不软、光明不发，屈伸则断。彼炼金师、炼金弟子复置炉中，增火鼓𫖔，转侧陶炼，然后生金轻软光泽，屈伸不断，随意所作钗、铛、镮、钏诸庄严具。

In the same way, a bhikṣu with a pure mind gradually eliminates the coarse afflictions, unwholesome actions, and wrong views, just as the goldsmith removes the hard stones and lumps from the gold.

「如是，净心进向比丘麁烦恼缠、恶不善业、诸恶邪见渐断令灭，如彼生金，淘去刚石坚块。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu with a pure mind eliminates the next coarse defilements, such as the desire-thought, anger-thought, and harm-thought, just as the goldsmith removes the coarse sand and gravel from the gold.

「复次，净心进向比丘除次麁垢，欲觉、恚觉、害觉，如彼生金除麁沙砾。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu with a pure mind next eliminates the subtle defilements, such as the thought of relatives, the thought of crowds, and the thought of rebirth in heaven, contemplating their elimination, just as the goldsmith removes the dust, fine sand, and black soil from the gold.

「复次，净心进向比丘次除细垢，谓亲里觉、人众觉、生天觉，思惟除灭，如彼生金除去尘垢、细沙、黑土。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu with a pure mind has the thought of good dharmas, contemplating their elimination, purifying the mind, just as the goldsmith removes the impurities similar in color to the gold, making it completely pure.

「复次，净心进向比丘有善法觉，思惟除灭，令心清净，犹如生金除去金色相似之垢，令其纯净。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu, in the various samādhis, is supported by practice, like a pond supported by surrounding banks, supported by the Dharma, not attaining tranquility and excellence, not attaining the bliss of cessation, not exhausting all taints. It is like a goldsmith or his apprentice refining raw gold, removing all impurities, but it is not light, not soft, not radiant, not flexible, not able to be used as desired to make ornaments.

「复次，比丘于诸三昧有行所持，犹如池水周匝岸持，为法所持，不得寂静胜妙，不得息乐，尽诸有漏。如彼金师、金师弟子陶炼生金，除诸垢秽，不轻、不软、不发光泽，屈伸断绝，不得随意成庄严具。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu attains various samādhis, not being supported by practice, attaining tranquility and excellence, attaining the bliss of the path of cessation, with one mind and one intention, exhausting all taints. It is like a goldsmith or his apprentice refining raw gold, making it light, soft, unbroken, radiant, flexible, and able to be used as desired to make ornaments.

「复次，比丘得诸三昧，不为有行所持，得寂静胜妙，得息乐道，一心一意，尽诸有漏。如炼金师、炼金师弟子陶炼生金，令其轻软、不断、光泽，屈伸随意。

Furthermore, a bhikṣu, having abandoned all discursive thoughts, attains the second, third, and fourth dhyānas. Such samādhi is pure and free from afflictions, soft, true, and immovable. He can attain realization in all the sense bases as he wishes. It is like a goldsmith refining raw gold, making it very light, soft, radiant, unbroken, and able to be used to make any vessel as desired. In this way, a bhikṣu attains samādhi and can attain realization in all the sense bases.

「复次，比丘离诸觉观，乃至得第二、第三、第四禅。如是正受，纯一清净，离诸烦恼，柔软真实不动。于彼彼入处，欲求作证悉能得证。如彼金师陶炼生金，极令轻软、光泽、不断，任作何器，随意所欲。如是，比丘三昧正受，乃至于诸入处悉能得证。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha then said to the bhikṣus, "The bhikṣus should practice in this way." When the bhikṣus heard the Buddha's teachings, they rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，时诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
