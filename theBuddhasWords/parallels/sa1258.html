SĀ 1258

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1258（一二五八） 鼓

Parallel to: SN 20.7

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the Deer Park at the Sage's Abode in the country of Varanasi.

一时，佛住波罗［木＊奈］国仙人住处鹿野苑中。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "In the past, there was a man named Tathāgata. Tathāgata had a drum named Anāhata, with a good sound, a beautiful sound, and a deep sound, reaching forty li. After a long time, the drum was torn and broken in many places. At that time, the drummer cut cowhide and wrapped it around. Although it was wrapped, the drum still did not have a loud sound, a beautiful sound, or a deep sound. Later, it became even more decayed, with the hide peeling off in large pieces, leaving only a pile of wood.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「过去世时，有一人名陀舍罗诃，彼陀舍罗诃有鼓名阿能诃，好声、美声、深声，彻四十里。彼鼓既久，处处裂坏。尔时，鼓士裁割牛皮，周匝缠缚；虽复缠缚，鼓犹无复高声、美声、深声。彼于后时，转复朽坏，皮大剥落，唯有聚木。

"Thus, bhikṣus! Cultivate the body, cultivate the precepts, cultivate the mind, and cultivate wisdom. Because of cultivating the body, cultivating the precepts, cultivating the mind, and cultivating wisdom, regarding the sūtras spoken by the Tathāgata that are profound and illuminating, difficult to see and difficult to realize, inconceivable, subtle, and definitive, known by the wise, they immediately accept and fully accept them. Hearing what is said, they joyfully cultivate and practice, attaining liberation and benefit. Future bhikṣus who do not cultivate the body, do not cultivate the precepts, do not cultivate the mind, and do not cultivate wisdom, hearing the sūtras spoken by the Tathāgata that are profound and illuminating, empty, associated with characteristics, and in accordance with dependent origination, they do not immediately accept and uphold them, nor do they fully accept them. Hearing what is said, they do not joyfully cultivate and practice, but instead focus on accepting the various worldly discourses, ornate literary expressions, and worldly mixed phrases. Hearing what is said, they joyfully cultivate and practice, but do not attain liberation and benefit. Regarding the essential teachings of emptiness and characteristics spoken by the Tathāgata that are profound and illuminating and in accordance with dependent origination, they will be extinguished, just like the drum that is decayed, broken, and only has a pile of wood left. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should diligently cultivate the body, cultivate the precepts, cultivate the mind, and cultivate wisdom. Regarding the essential teachings of emptiness and characteristics spoken by the Tathāgata that are profound and illuminating and in accordance with dependent origination, immediately accept and fully accept them. Hearing what is said, joyfully cultivate and practice, attaining liberation and benefit."

「如是，比丘！修身、修戒、修心、修慧，以彼修身、修戒、修心、修慧故，于如来所说修多罗甚深明照，难见难觉，不可思量，微密决定，明智所知，彼则顿受、周备受，闻其所说，欢喜崇习，出离饶益。当来比丘不修身、不修戒、不修心、不修慧，闻如来所说修多罗甚深明照空相应随顺缘起法，彼不顿受持，不至到受。闻彼说者，不欢喜崇习，而于世间众杂异论、文辞绮饰、世俗杂句，专心顶受，闻彼说者，欢喜崇习，不得出离饶益。于彼如来所说甚深明照空相要法随顺缘起者，于此则灭，犹如彼鼓，朽故坏裂，唯有聚木。是故，诸比丘！当勤方便修身、修戒、修心、修慧，于如来所说甚深明照空相要法随顺缘起，顿受、遍受。闻彼说者，欢喜崇习，出离饶益。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "Good, good! The bhikṣus have spoken well." The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
