SĀ 1324

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1324（一三二四） 箭毛

Parallel to: SN 10.3 / Snp 2.5 / SĀ 1314 (~) / SĀ² 2.313 / SĀ² 2.323

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was traveling in the human realm of Magadha and spent the night in a place inhabited by the Needle-Hair Ghost. At that time, the Needle-Hair Ghost had gathered all the ghosts and spirits in one place.

一时，佛在摩竭提国人间游行，到针毛鬼住处夜宿。尔时，针毛鬼会诸鬼神，集在一处。

At that time, a flaming ghost saw the World-Honored One spending the night in the dwelling of the Needle-Hair Ghost. Having seen this, he went to the Needle-Hair Ghost and said, "Village Chief! You have now gained great benefits. The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One is spending the night in your house."

时，有炎鬼见世尊在针毛鬼住处夜宿。见已，往诣针毛鬼所，语针毛鬼言：「聚落主！汝今大得善利，今如来、应、等正觉于汝室宿。」

The Needle-Hair Ghost said, "Let us see if it is the Tathāgata or not."

针毛鬼言：「今当试看，为是如来、为非？」

At that time, the Needle-Hair Ghost and the ghosts and spirits gathered together, returned to his own house, and rushed towards the Buddha. At that time, the World-Honored One stepped aside to avoid him. This happened three times, and the Buddha also stepped aside to avoid him three times.

时，针毛鬼与诸鬼神集会已，还归自舍，束身冲佛。尔时，世尊却身避之。如是再三，束身冲佛，佛亦再三却身避之。

At that time, the Needle-Hair Ghost said, "Are you afraid, Śramaṇa?"

尔时，针毛鬼言：「沙门怖耶？」

The Buddha said, "Headman, I am not afraid, but your touch is evil."

佛言：「聚落主！我不怖也，但汝触恶。」

The Needle-Hair Ghost said, "I have a question to ask. If you can answer it to my satisfaction, that's good. If you cannot, I will destroy your heart, tear open your chest, make hot blood gush from your face, grab your two hands, and throw you to the other shore of the Ganges River."

针毛鬼言：「今有所问，当为我说，能令我喜者善，不能令我喜者，当坏汝心，裂汝胸，令汝热血从其面出，捉汝两手掷恒水彼岸。」

The Buddha said to the Needle-Hair Ghost, "Headman, I do not see any gods, demons, Brahmas, śramaṇas, brahmins, devas, or worldly people who can destroy the Tathāgata's mind, tear open his chest, make hot blood gush from his face, grab his two arms, and throw him to the other shore of the Ganges River. Just ask me now, and I will explain it to you, to your satisfaction."

佛告针毛鬼：「聚落主！我不见诸天、魔、梵、沙门、婆罗门、天神、世人能坏如来、应、等正觉心者，能裂其胸者，能令热血从面出者，执其两臂掷著恒水彼岸者。汝今但问，当为汝说。令汝欢喜。」

At that time, the Needle-Hair Ghost spoke a verse to ask the Buddha:

时，针毛鬼说偈问佛：

All greedy and angry minds, what is their cause? Why do
I not enjoy it, my body hair standing on end? From
where does fear arise? The thoughts and perceptions of the mind
from where do they arise? Like a newborn child, relying on
a wet nurse.

「一切贪恚心，　　以何为其因，不乐身毛竖，　　恐怖从何起？意念诸觉想，　　为从何所起，犹如新生儿，　　依倚于乳母。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，世尊说偈答言：

Love gives rise to one's own growth, like a banyan
tree, mutually binding and pulling, like a tangled thicket. If
one knows their cause, one should awaken the ghosts, to
cross the flowing sea of birth and death, and not
increase existence again.

「爱生自身长，　　如尼拘律树，展转相拘引，　　如籐绵丛林，若知彼所因，　　当令鬼觉悟，度生死海流，　　不复重增有。」

At that time, the Needle-Hair Ghost heard the World-Honored One speak the verse, and his mind was delighted. He repented towards the Buddha and upheld the Three Refuges.

尔时，针毛鬼闻世尊说偈，心得欢喜，向佛悔过，受持三归。

After the Buddha spoke this sutra, the Needle-Hair Ghost heard what the Buddha said and joyfully upheld it.

佛说此经已，针毛鬼闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
