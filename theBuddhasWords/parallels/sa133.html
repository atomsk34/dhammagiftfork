SĀ 133

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 133（一三三） 何所有故

Parallel to: SN 22.151 (~) / SN 24.2 (~) / SĀ 142 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus, "Because of the existence of what, does what arise? What are you attached to, and what do you view as the self, that causes sentient beings to be covered by ignorance, bound by craving, driven along the path, and revolving and wandering in birth and death, not leaving the original state?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「何所有故，何所起？何所系著，何所见我？令众生无明所盖，爱系其] cf.【丽】首，长道驱驰，生死轮回，生死流转，不去本际？」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "The World-Honored One is the root of the Dharma, the eye of the Dharma, and the reliance of the Dharma. Excellent! World-Honored One! Please compassionately explain the meaning in detail. The bhikṣus will listen and accept it with reverence."

诸比丘白佛言：「世尊是法根、法眼、法依。善哉！世尊！唯愿哀愍，广说其义，诸比丘闻已，当受奉行。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Listen carefully and think well, I will explain it for you. Bhikṣus! Because of the existence of form, the affairs of form arise, one is bound by form, and one views the self in form, causing sentient beings to be covered by ignorance, bound by craving, driven along the path, revolving and wandering in birth and death. The same is true for feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. Bhikṣus! Is form permanent or impermanent?"

佛告比丘：「谛听，善思，当为汝说。诸比丘！色有故，色事起，色系著，色见我，令众生无明所盖，爱系其首，长道驱驰，生死轮回，生死流转。受、想、行、识亦复如是。诸比丘！色为常耶？为非常耶？」

He replied, "Impermanent, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「无常。世尊！」

He further asked, "If it is impermanent, is it suffering?"

复问：「若无常者，是苦耶？」

He replied, "It is suffering, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「是苦。世尊！」

Thus, bhikkhus, if it is impermanent, it is suffering. Because of this suffering, this arises, is bound, and is seen as the self, causing beings to be covered by ignorance, bound by craving, driven along the long path, revolving in birth and death, and wandering in the cycle of birth and death. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are also like this. Therefore, bhikkhus, all forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, are not the self, not different from the self, and not in the self. This is called right wisdom. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are also like this.

「如是，比丘！若无常者是苦，是苦有故，是事起、系著、见我，令众生无明所盖，爱系其头，长道驱驰，生死轮回，生死流转。受、想、行、识亦复如是。是故，诸比丘！诸所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外、若麤、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、非异我、不相在，是名正慧。受、想、行、识亦复如是。

Thus, seeing, hearing, cognition, and perception, seeking, obtaining, remembering, cognizing, and contemplating, all of them are not the self, not different from the self, and not in the self. This is called right wisdom.

「如是见、闻、觉、识，求得随忆、随觉、随观，彼一切非我、非异我、不相在，是名正慧。

If there is a view that says there is a self, a world, this world, that is permanent, constant, and unchanging, all of them are not the self, not different from the self, and not in the self. This is called right wisdom.

「若有见言有我、有世间、有此世、常、恒、不变易法，彼一切非我、非异我、不相在，是名正慧。

If there is a view that says this is not the self, this is not the self's possessions, this is not the future self, this is not the future self's possessions, all of them are not the self, not different from the self, and not in the self. This is called right wisdom.

「若复有见非此我、非此我所、非当来我、非当来我所，彼一切非我、非异我、不相在，是名正慧。

If a learned noble disciple observes these six views as not the self and not the self's possessions, one who observes in this way will have doubts about the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha cut off. This is called, bhikkhus! a learned noble disciple who is no longer able to create bodily, verbal, and mental actions that lead to the three lower realms; even if they are negligent, a noble disciple is definitely heading towards the three kinds of enlightenment, going and coming among the gods and humans for seven existences, making an end to suffering.

「若多闻圣弟子于此六见处观察非我、非我所，如是观者，于佛所狐疑断，于法、于僧狐疑断。是名，比丘！多闻圣弟子不复堪任作身、口、意业，趣三恶道；正使放逸，圣弟子决定向三菩提，七有天人往来，作苦边。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
