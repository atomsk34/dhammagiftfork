SĀ 1333

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1333（一三三三） 远离

Parallel to: SN 9.1 / SĀ² 2.353

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu was staying in a forest in Kośala. He entered the right concentration during the day, but gave rise to unwholesome thoughts based on evil desires.

时，有异比丘在拘萨罗住林中，入昼正受，心起不善觉，依于恶贪。

At that time, the deity who was dwelling in that forest had this thought: "It is not the way of a bhikṣu to stay in the forest, enter right concentration during the day, and give rise to unwholesome thoughts based on evil desires. I should go and enlighten him."

时，彼林中住止天神作是念：「非比丘法，止住林中，入昼正受，心生不善觉，依于恶贪，我今当往开悟之。」

Then the deity spoke a verse, saying:

时，彼天神即说偈言：

The mind desires to be far away, in the empty and
quiet forest, but the mind wanders after external conditions, with scattered
thoughts running wild. Subduing the worldly mind, always delighting in mental
liberation, one should abandon the unhappy mind, and abide in peace
and happiness. Thinking is not right mindfulness, do not be attached
to self and mine, like being stained by dust, this attachment
is extremely difficult to remove. Do not allow the mind to
be stained and attached, disturbed by desire, like the king of
Śakra's running elephant, shaking off dust and defilements. The bhikṣu, in
his own body, rightly mindful, removes dust and defilements. Dust refers
to greed and desire, not to worldly dust and dirt. The
wise and intelligent ones should awaken to those defilements. In the
Tathāgata's teachings and discipline, uphold the mind, do not be negligent
Defilements refer to anger and hatred, not to worldly dust and
dirt. The wise and intelligent ones should awaken to those defilements
In the Tathāgata's teachings and discipline, uphold the mind, do not
be negligent. Defilements refer to ignorance and foolishness, not to worldly
dust and dirt. The wise and intelligent ones should abandon those
defilements. In the Tathāgata's teachings and discipline, uphold the mind, do
not be negligent.

「其心欲远离，　　正于空闲林，放心随外缘，　　乱想而流驰。调伏乐世心，　　常乐心解脱，当舍不乐心，　　执受安乐住。思非于正念，　　莫著我我所，如以尘头染，　　是著极难遣。莫令染乐著，　　欲心所浊乱，如释君驰象，　　奋迅去尘秽。比丘于自身，　　正念除尘垢，尘者谓贪欲，　　非世间尘土。黠慧明智者，　　当悟彼诸尘，于如来法律，　　持心莫放逸。尘垢谓瞋恚，　　非世间尘土，黠慧明智者，　　当悟彼诸尘。于如来法律，　　持心莫放逸，尘垢谓愚痴，　　非世间尘土。明智黠慧者，　　当舍彼诸尘，于如来法律，　　持心莫放逸。」

When the deity had spoken this verse, the bhikṣu heard what was said, concentrated his thoughts, eliminated all afflictions, and attained arahantship.

时，彼天神说是偈已，彼比丘闻其所说，专精思惟，断诸烦恼心，得阿罗汉。
