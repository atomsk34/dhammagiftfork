SĀ 1338

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1338（一三三八） 花

Parallel to: SN 9.14 / SĀ² 2.358

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu was staying in a forest in the country of Kosala. The bhikṣu had an eye ailment and was instructed by his teacher to smell a pāṭhīna flower. The bhikṣu, having received his teacher's instruction, went to the side of a pāṭhīna pond, sat facing the wind on the bank of the pond, and smelled the fragrance as it came.

时，有异比丘在拘萨罗人间，止一林中。时，彼比丘有眼患，受师教，应嗅钵昙摩花。时，彼比丘受师教已，往至钵昙摩池侧，于池岸边，迎风而坐，随风嗅香。

At that time, a deity who was the guardian of the pond said to the bhikṣu, "Why are you stealing the flowers? You are now a thief of fragrance!"

时，有天神，主此池者，语比丘言：「何以盗华？汝今便是盗香贼也！」

At that time, the bhikṣu spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，比丘说偈答言：

Not destroying or taking away, staying far away and following the scent
why do you now say that I am a thief of fragrance?

「不坏亦不夺，　　远住随嗅香，汝今何故言，　　我是盗香贼？」

At that time, the heavenly spirit again spoke a verse:

尔时，天神复说偈言：

Not seeking but not abandoning, the world calls it a
thief. Now you, without being given, take it all by
yourself. This is truly called a thief of fragrance in the world.

「不求而不舍，　　世间名为贼，汝今人不与，　　而自一向取，是则名世间，　　真实盗香贼。」

At that time, a man took the lotus roots, loaded them heavily, and left.

时，有一士夫取彼藕根，重负而去。

At that time, the bhikṣu spoke a verse to the deity:

尔时，比丘为彼天神而说偈言：

Now, like that man who cut and divided the lotus roots, pulled
them up and carried them away heavily, he is a cunning person
Why did you not stop him and say that I stole the incense?

「如今彼士夫，　　断截分陀利，拔根重负去，　　便是奸狡人，汝何故不遮，　　而言我盗香。」

At that time, the deity spoke a verse in reply:

时，彼天神说偈答言：

The mad, cunning person is like a nurse's robe. Why bother to
speak more? It is appropriate to speak with you. The saffron robe
is stained and not visible, the black robe is not stained by
ink. The cunning and vicious person is not spoken to in the
world. The fly's foot stains the white silk, the wise see even
a small fault. Like ink on a pearl, even a small fault
is all seen. Always seek purity from them, free from afflictions and
troubles. Even a hair's worth of evil is seen by people as Mount Tai.

「狂乱奸狡人，　　犹如乳母衣，何足加其言，　　宜堪与汝语。袈裟污不现，　　黑衣墨不污，奸狡凶恶人，　　世间不与语。蝇脚污素帛，　　明者小过现，如墨点珂贝，　　虽小悉皆现。常从彼求净，　　无结离烦恼，如毛发之恶，　　人见如泰山。」

At that time, the bhikṣu spoke a verse:

时，彼比丘复说偈言：

Excellent, excellent! You speak with meaning and comfort
me. You should often speak these verses for me.

「善哉善哉说，　　以义安慰我，汝可常为我，　　数数说斯偈。」

At that time, the god spoke a verse:

时，彼天神复说偈言：

I am not your bought slave, nor am I given to you by
others. Why do you constantly follow me and speak to me again and
again? You should now know for yourself the beneficial matters in each case.

「我非汝买奴，　　亦非人与汝，何为常随汝，　　数数相告语，汝今自当知，　　彼彼饶益事。」

At that time, after the god had spoken this verse, the bhikṣu heard what he said, rejoiced, and rose from his seat. He went to a solitary place, concentrated his mind, contemplated, eliminated all afflictions, and attained arahantship.

时，彼天子说是偈已，彼比丘闻其所说，欢喜随喜，从座起去，独一静处，专精思惟，断诸烦恼，得阿罗汉。
