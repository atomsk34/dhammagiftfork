SĀ 1344

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 1344（一三四四） 嬉戏

Parallel to: SN 9.8 / SĀ² 2.364

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was at Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu was staying in a forest in the country of Kosala. He played with the wife of an elder, and a bad reputation arose.

时，有异比丘在拘萨罗人间，住一林中。时，彼比丘与长者妇女嬉戏，起恶名声。

The bhikṣu thought, "I am not like others. I have caused a bad reputation by playing with another's wife. I now want to kill myself in this forest."

时，彼比丘作是念：「我今不类，共他妇女起恶名声，我今欲于此林中自杀。」

At that time, a deity dwelling in that forest thought, "This bhikṣu is not bad or wrong. He is not corrupt or at fault, yet he wants to kill himself in the forest. I should now devise a means to enlighten him."

时，彼林中止住天神作是思念：「不善不类，此比丘不坏、无过，而于林中欲自杀身，我今当作方便开悟。」

The deity then transformed into the body of a merchant's daughter and said to the bhikṣu, "In the streets and crossroads of the world, people have given rise to a bad reputation for me and you, saying that I have been intimate with you and done improper things. Since there is already a bad reputation, you should now return to lay life and enjoy each other's company."

时，彼天神化作长者女身，语比丘言：「于诸巷路四衢道中，世间诸人为我及汝起恶名声，言我与汝共相习近，作不正事。已有恶名，今可还俗，共相娱乐。」

The bhikṣu replied, "Because in the streets and crossroads of the world, they have given rise to a bad reputation for me and you, saying that we have been intimate and done improper things. I will now kill myself!"

比丘答言：「以彼里巷四衢道中，为我与汝起恶名声，共相习近，为不正事。我今且自杀身！」

At that time, the deity returned to his heavenly body and spoke a verse, saying:

时，彼天神还复天身，而说偈言：

Though one hears much bad reputation, the ascetic endures it. One should
not suffer oneself, nor should one give rise to vexation. One who
is terrified upon hearing a sound is a forest beast. Such an
impetuous being does not accomplish the Dharma of leaving home. The compassionate
one should be able to endure it. One should not dwell on
bad reputation. One who firmly abides in the mind is then the
Dharma of leaving home. It is not because of the words of
others that you become a bandit. It is also not because of
the words of others that you attain arahantship. As you know for
yourself, the devas also know it.

「虽闻多恶名，　　苦行者忍之，不应苦自苦，　　亦不应起恼。闻声恐怖者，　　是则林中兽，是轻躁众生，　　不成出家法。仁者当堪耐，　　不中住恶声，执心坚住者，　　是则出家法。不由他人语，　　令汝成劫贼，亦不由他语，　　令汝得罗汉。如汝自知已，　　诸天亦复知。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having been enlightened by the deity, diligently contemplated and eliminated afflictions, attaining arahantship.

尔时，比丘为彼天神所开悟已，专精思惟，断除烦恼，得阿罗汉。
