SĀ 15

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 15（一五） 使

Parallel to: SN 22.36 (~) / SN 22.63–65 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha’s feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, “Excellent! World-Honored One! Now please briefly teach me the essentials of the Dharma. Having heard the Dharma, I will dwell in solitude and cultivate non-negligence. Having cultivated non-negligence, I will further contemplate: ‘The reason good sons leave home, shave their heads and beards, wear the Dharma robes, and leave home for the non-home, is to ultimately realize the unsurpassed holy life, to realize in this very life: “My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and I know that I will not be subject to any future lives.”’

尔时，有异比丘来诣佛所，稽首佛足，却住一面，白佛言：「善哉！世尊！今当为我略说法要，我闻法已，当独一静处，修不放逸。修不放逸已，当复思惟：『所以善男子出家，剃除须发，身著法服，信家非家出家，为究竟无上梵行，现法作证：「我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。」』」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to that bhikṣu, "Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu, you have spoken well. You said, 'Please briefly teach me the essentials of the Dharma. Having heard the Dharma, I will dwell in solitude and cultivate non-negligence, and so on, up to knowing that I will not be subject to any future lives.' Is that what you said?"

尔时，世尊告彼比丘：「善哉！善哉！比丘快说此言，云：『当为我略说法要，我闻法已，独一静处，修不放逸，乃至自知不受后有。』如是说耶？」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「如是，世尊！」

The Buddha said to the bhikṣu, "Listen carefully! Listen carefully! Consider it well, and I will explain it to you. Bhikṣu! If one is driven by the afflictions, one will die driven by the afflictions; if one dies driven by the afflictions, one will be bound by grasping. Bhikṣu! If one is not driven by the afflictions, one will not die driven by the afflictions; if one does not die driven by the afflictions, one will be liberated from grasping."

佛告比丘：「谛听！谛听！善思念之，当为汝说。比丘！若随使使者，即随使死；若随死者，为取所缚。比丘！若不随使使，则不随使死；不随使死者，则于取解脱。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "I understand, World-Honored One! I understand, Well-Gone One!"

比丘白佛：「知已，世尊！知已，善逝！」

The Buddha said to the bhikṣu, "How do you understand the meaning of my brief teaching?"

佛告比丘：「汝云何于我略说法中，广解其义？」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Form is bound by the fetters, form is bound by the fetters and death; those who are bound by the fetters and die are bound by grasping. Thus, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are bound by the fetters, bound by the fetters and death; those who are bound by the fetters and die are bound by grasping.

比丘白佛言：「世尊！色随使使、色随使死；随使使、随使死者，则为取所缚。如是受、想、行、识，随使使、随使死；随使使、随使死者，为取所缚。

World-Honored One! If form is not bound by the fetters, not bound by the fetters and death; not bound by the fetters and not bound by the fetters and death, then one is liberated from grasping. Thus, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are not bound by the fetters, not bound by the fetters and death; not bound by the fetters and not bound by the fetters and death, then one is liberated from grasping. Thus, World-Honored One! I have broadly explained the meaning of the brief teaching.

「世尊！若色不随使使、不随使死；不随使使、不随使死者，则于取解脱。如是受、想、行、识，不随使使、不随使死；不随使使、不随使死者，则于取解脱。如是，世尊！略说法中，广解其义。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu! You have broadly explained the meaning of my brief teaching. Why is that? Form is bound by the fetters, bound by the fetters and death; those who are bound by the fetters and die are bound by grasping. Thus, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are bound by the fetters, bound by the fetters and death; those who are bound by the fetters and die are bound by grasping.

佛告比丘：「善哉！善哉！比丘！于我略说法中，广解其义。所以者何？色随使使、随使死；随使使、随使死者，则为取所缚。如是受、想、行、识，随使使、随使死；随使使、随使死者，则为取所缚。

Bhikṣu! Form is not bound by the fetters, not bound by the fetters and death; those who are not bound by the fetters and do not die are liberated from grasping. Thus, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are not bound by the fetters, not bound by the fetters and death; those who are not bound by the fetters and do not die are liberated from grasping.

「比丘！色不随使使、不随使死；不随使使、不随使死者，则于取解脱。如是受、想、行、识，不随使使、不随使死；不随使使、不随使死者，则于取解脱。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard the Buddha's teaching, was greatly delighted. He bowed to the Buddha and left. Alone in a quiet place, he diligently practiced and abided in non-negligence. Having diligently practiced and abided in non-negligence, he contemplated: "The reason good sons leave home, shave their beards and hair, wear the Dharma robes, leave home for homelessness, and so forth, is to know for themselves that they will not be reborn again.

时，彼比丘闻佛所说，心大欢喜，礼佛而退。独在静处，精勤修习，住不放逸。精勤修习，住不放逸已，思惟：「所以善男子出家，剃除须发，身著法服，信家非家出家，乃至自知不受后有。」

At that time, the bhikṣu immediately attained arahantship and attained liberation of the mind.

时，彼比丘即成罗汉，心得解脱。
