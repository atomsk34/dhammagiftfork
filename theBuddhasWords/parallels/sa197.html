SĀ 197

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 197（一九七） 燃烧

Parallel to: SN 22.61 / SN 35.28

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Kajaśirīśa Shrine, together with a thousand monks, all of whom were former matted-hair ascetics.

一时，佛住迦阇尸利沙支提，与千比丘俱，皆是旧萦发婆罗门。

At that time, the World-Honored One used three kinds of manifestations to teach and transform the thousand monks. What were the three? The manifestation of supernatural powers, the manifestation of knowing others' minds, and the manifestation of instruction.

尔时，世尊为千比丘作三种示现教化。云何为三？神足变化示现、他心示现、教诫示现。

As for the manifestation of supernatural powers, the World-Honored One manifested entering into various meditative absorptions according to their needs, flying through the air to the east, performing the four dignified postures of walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, entering the fire samadhi, emitting various colored firelight - blue, yellow, red, white, pink, and crystal-like - water and fire appearing together, fire coming out from below the body and water from above, fire from above and water from below, and the same in all directions. At that time, after the World-Honored One had performed various supernatural transformations, he sat among the assembly. This is called the manifestation of supernatural powers.

神足示现者，世尊随其所应，而示现入禅定正受，陵虚至东方，作四威仪，行、住、坐、卧，入火三昧，出种种火光，青、黄、赤、白、红、颇梨色，水火俱现、或身下出火，身上出水，身上出火，身下出水，周圆四方亦复如是。尔时，世尊作种种神变已，于众中坐，是名神足示现。

As for the manifestation of knowing others' minds, it is like knowing their minds, intentions, and consciousness, knowing what they should think and what they should not think, what they should abandon, and what they should realize and abide in with the body. This is called the manifestation of knowing others' minds.

他心示现者，如彼心、如彼意、如彼识，彼应作如是念、不应作如是念、彼应作如是舍、彼应作如是身证住，是名他心示现。

As for the manifestation of instruction, it is as the World-Honored One said: "Bhikshus! Everything is burning. What is burning? The eyes are burning, and so are forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact, whether pleasant, unpleasant, or neither. The same goes for the ears, nose, tongue, body, and mind. The dharmas, mind consciousness, mind contact, and the feelings that arise from mind contact, whether pleasant, unpleasant, or neither, are also burning. What burns? The fire of craving burns, the fire of anger burns, the fire of delusion burns, and the fire of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress burns.

教诫示现者，如世尊说：「诸比丘！一切烧然。云何一切烧然？谓眼烧然，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，彼亦烧然。如是耳、鼻、舌、身、意烧然，若法、意识、意触、意触因缘生受，若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，彼亦烧然，以何烧然，贪火烧然、恚火烧然、痴火烧然，生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦火烧然。」

At that time, the thousand bhikshus heard what the Buddha said, and their defilements ceased, their minds were liberated. After the Buddha spoke this sutra, the bhikshus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

尔时，千比丘闻佛所说，不起诸漏，心得解脱，佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
