SĀ 24

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 24（二四） 罗睺罗所问

Parallel to: SN 18.22 / SN 22.92 / SĀ 199

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Bamboo Grove Monastery in the city of Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城伽兰陀竹园。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Rāhula, "Bhikṣu! How do you know and see this body of consciousness and all the characteristics of external objects, without the view of 'I' and 'mine,' the conceit of 'I,' and the bondage of afflictions?"

尔时，世尊告罗睺罗：「比丘！云何知、云何见我此识身及外境界一切相，无有我、我所见、我慢使系著？」

Rāhula said to the Buddha, "The World-Honored One is the master of the Dharma, the guide, and the protector. Excellent! May the World-Honored One explain this meaning to the bhikṣus. Having heard it from the Buddha, the bhikṣus will uphold and practice it."

罗睺罗白佛言：「世尊为法主、为导、为覆。善哉！世尊当为诸比丘演说此义，诸比丘从佛闻已，当受持奉行。」

The Buddha told Rāhula, "Listen attentively! Listen attentively! Consider it well, and I shall explain it to you."

佛告罗睺罗：「谛听！谛听！善思念之，当为汝说。」

Rāhula said to the Buddha, "Yes, I shall listen to the teaching."

罗睺罗白佛：「唯然，受教。」

The Buddha told Rāhula, "Contemplate all existing forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, they are all not-self, not different from self, and not in possession of self. Contemplate them with this equanimity and wisdom. In the same way, contemplate feelings, perceptions, mental formations, and consciousness, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, they are all not-self, not different from self, and not in possession of self. Contemplate them with this equanimity and wisdom.

佛告罗睺罗：「当观诸所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、不异我、不相在。如是平等慧如实观。如是受、想、行、识，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、不异我、不相在。如是平等慧如实观。

Bhikṣu! Knowing and seeing in this way, you will have no view of self, self-possession, or self-pride and attachment to this body of consciousness and all external objects.

「比丘！如是知、如是见我此识身及外境界一切相，无有我、我所见、我慢使系著。

Rāhula! Bhikṣus who have no view of self, self-possession, or self-pride and attachment to this body of consciousness and all external objects, who transcend doubt, are far removed from all characteristics, and are peacefully liberated, are said to have cut off desire and turned away from all bonds, to be rightly without interruption, and to have reached the end of suffering.

「罗睺罗！比丘如是识身及外境界一切相，无有我、我所见、我慢使系著者，超越疑心，远离诸相，寂静解脱，是名比丘断除爱欲，转去诸结，正无间等，究竟苦边。」

At that time, Rāhula, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

时，罗睺罗闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The aggregates, sense bases, and sense fields are not mine, not of another, not to be grasped, not to be
attached to, not to be defiled, not to be agitated, not to be disturbed, not to be agitated, not to
be disturbed, not to be agitated, not to be disturbed, not to be agitated, not to be disturbed, not to
be agitated, not to be disturbed, not to be agitated, not to be disturbed, not to be agitated, not to
be disturbed, not

使、增诸数　　非我、非彼　　结系、动摇劫波所问　　亦罗睺罗　　所问二经
