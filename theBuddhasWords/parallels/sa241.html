SĀ 241

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 241（二四一） 烧热

Parallel to: SN 35.235

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Monkey Pond in the two-storied lecture hall in Vaiśālī.

一时，佛住毘舍离猕猴池侧重阁讲堂。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people, bhikṣus! It is better to burn one's eyes with a red-hot copper rod than to perceive forms with the eye consciousness and grasp at their characteristics and secondary characteristics. Why is that? Because grasping at the characteristics of forms and secondary characteristics leads to falling into evil destinies like a ball of iron sinking.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「愚痴无闻凡夫，比丘！宁以火烧热铜筹以烧其目，令其炽然，不以眼识取于色相、取随形好。所以者何？取于色相、取随形好故，堕恶趣中如沈铁丸。

Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people, it is better to drill one's ears with an iron awl than to perceive sounds with the ear consciousness and grasp at their characteristics and secondary characteristics. Why is that? Because grasping at the characteristics and secondary characteristics of sounds leads to falling into evil destinies like a ball of iron sinking.

「愚痴无闻凡夫，宁烧铁锥以钻其耳，不以耳识取其声相、取随声好。所以者何？耳识取声相、取随声好者，身坏命终堕恶趣中如沈铁丸。

Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people, it is better to cut off one's nose with a sharp knife than to perceive smells with the nose consciousness and grasp at their characteristics and secondary characteristics. Why is that? Because grasping at the characteristics and secondary characteristics of smells leads to falling into evil destinies like a ball of iron sinking.

「愚痴无闻凡夫，宁以利刀断截其鼻，不以鼻识取于香相、取随香好。所以者何？以取香相、取随香好故，身坏命终堕恶趣中如沈铁丸。

Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people, it is better to cut off one's tongue with a sharp knife than to perceive tastes with the tongue consciousness and grasp at their characteristics and secondary characteristics. Why is that? Because grasping at the characteristics and secondary characteristics of tastes leads to falling into evil destinies like a ball of iron sinking.

「愚痴无闻凡夫，宁以利刀断截其舌，不以舌识取于味相、取随味好。所以者何？以取味相、随味好故，身坏命终堕恶趣中如沈铁丸。

Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people, it is better to pierce one's body with a sharp iron spear than to perceive tactile sensations with the body consciousness and grasp at their characteristics and secondary characteristics. Why is that? Because grasping at the characteristics and secondary characteristics of tactile sensations leads to falling into evil destinies like a ball of iron sinking.

「愚痴无闻凡夫，宁以刚铁利枪以刺其身，不以身识取于触相及随触好。所以者何？以取触相及随触好故，身坏命终堕恶趣中如沈铁丸。

Bhikṣus! Sleep is the life of fools, it is the life of delusion, it is without benefit and merit. However, bhikṣus, it is better to sleep than to give rise to conceptual thoughts about those forms. If one gives rise to conceptual thoughts, it will certainly give rise to bondage and disputes, causing many people to do what is not beneficial, and not benefiting or bringing peace and happiness to gods and humans.

「诸比丘！睡眠者是愚痴活、是痴命，无利、无福，然诸比丘宁当睡眠，不于彼色而起觉想；若起觉想者，必生缠缚诤讼，能令多众起于非义，不能饶益安乐天人。

"The well-learned noble disciples should train thus: 'It is better for me to pierce my eyes with a burning iron spear than to perceive forms with the eye consciousness and fall into the three evil destinies, suffering for a long time. From today onwards, I should contemplate thus: "The eye is impermanent, conditioned, and arises from mental conditions. If forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact as conditions are experienced internally as painful, pleasant, or neither painful nor pleasant, they are also impermanent, conditioned, and arise from mental conditions." They should train thus about the ear, nose, tongue, and body sense bases: 'It is better to pierce my body with an iron spear than to perceive tactile sensations with the body consciousness and fall into the three evil destinies because of grasping at their characteristics and secondary characteristics. From today onwards, I should contemplate thus: "The body is impermanent, conditioned, and arises from mental conditions. If tactile sensations, body consciousness, body contact, and the feelings that arise from body contact as conditions are experienced internally as painful, pleasant, or neither painful nor pleasant, they are also impermanent, conditioned, and arise from mental conditions.

「彼多闻圣弟子作如是学：『我今宁以炽然铁枪以贯其目，不以眼识取于色相，堕三恶趣，长夜受苦。我从今日当正思惟：「观眼无常、有为、心缘生法，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，彼亦无常、有为、心缘生法。」』耳、鼻、舌、身入处当如是学：『宁以铁枪贯其身体，不以身识取于触相及随触好故，堕三恶道。我从今日当正思惟：「观身无常、有为、心缘生法，若触、身识、身触、身触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，彼亦无常、有为、心缘生法。」』

The well-learned noble disciples should train thus: 'Sleep is the life of fools, it is the life of delusion, it is without fruit, benefit, or merit. I should not sleep, nor give rise to conceptual thoughts. Giving rise to conceptual thoughts will give rise to bondage and disputes, causing many people to do what is not beneficial and not bringing peace and happiness.'

「多闻圣弟子作如是学：『睡眠者是愚痴活、痴命，无果、无利、无福，我当不眠，亦不起觉想，起想者生于缠缚诤讼，令多人非义饶益，不得安乐。』

The well-learned noble disciples who contemplate thus will become disenchanted with the eye, forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact as conditions, whether experienced internally as painful, pleasant, or neither painful nor pleasant. They will become disenchanted, not delight in them, and be liberated. With the knowledge and vision of liberation, they will know: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this state.' The same applies to the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind.

「多闻圣弟子如是观者，于眼生厌，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，彼亦生厌，厌故不乐，不乐故解脱，解脱知见：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦复如是。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
