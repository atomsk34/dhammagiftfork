SĀ 246

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 246（二四六） 七年

Parallel to: SN 4.24

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Vulture Peak in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城耆阇崛山。

At that time, the World-Honored One, in the morning, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered Rājagṛha to beg for food.

尔时，世尊晨朝著衣持钵，入王舍城乞食。

At that time, the heavenly demon Pāpīyān had this thought: "The śramaṇa Gautama, in the morning, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered Rājagṛha to beg for food. I should go now and disturb his mind."

尔时，天魔波旬作是念：「沙门瞿昙晨朝著衣持钵，入王舍城乞食，我今当往乱其道意。」

At that time, the demon Pāpīyān transformed into a charioteer, holding a staff to look for cattle, wearing tattered clothes, with disheveled hair, hands and feet torn apart, holding a cattle prod in hand, he came before the World-Honored One and asked: "Gautama! Have you seen my cattle?"

时，魔波旬化作御车象类，执杖觅牛，著弊衣，蓬头乱发，手脚剥裂，手执牛杖，至世尊前问言：「瞿昙！见我牛不？」

The World-Honored One had this thought: "This is the evil demon, wanting to come and disturb me." He then told the demon: "Evil demon! Where are the cattle? What use are cattle for?"

世尊作是念：「此是恶魔，欲来乱我。」即告魔言：「恶魔！何处有牛？何用牛为？」

The demon had this thought: "The śramaṇa Gautama knows that I am the demon." He then said to the Buddha: "Gautama! The sense-field of eye-contact is what I ride. The sense-fields of ear-, nose-, tongue-, body-, and mind-contact are what I ride."

魔作是念：「沙门瞿昙知我是魔。」而白佛言：「瞿昙！眼触入处，是我所乘。耳、鼻、舌、身、意触入处，是我所乘。」

He further asked: "Gautama! Where are you going?"

复问：「瞿昙！欲何所之？」

The Buddha told the evil demon: "You have the sense-field of eye-contact, the sense-fields of ear-, nose-, tongue-, body-, and mind-contact. If there is no sense-field of eye-contact, no sense-fields of ear-, nose-, tongue-, body-, and mind-contact, you cannot reach there, but I can go there."

佛告恶魔：「汝有眼触入处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意触入处。若彼无眼触入处，无耳、鼻、舌、身、意触入处，汝所不到，我往到彼。」

At that time, the evil demon Mara spoke a verse:

尔时，天魔波旬即说偈言：

If there is always a self, then all that
is mine, everything belongs to me. Where is Gautama going?

「若常有我者，　　彼悉是我所，一切悉属我，　　瞿昙何所之。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse in reply:

尔时，世尊说偈答言：

If one says there is a self, then what is said is
not the self. Therefore, know that Māra himself has fallen into defeat.

「若言有我者，　　彼说我则非，是故知波旬，　　即自堕负处。」

Māra again spoke a verse:

魔复说偈言：

If one says one knows the path, peacefully heading towards
nirvāṇa, you alone wander and go, why bother teaching others?

「若说言知道，　　安隐向涅槃，汝自独游往，　　何烦教他为？」

The World Honored One again spoke a verse in reply:

世尊复说偈答言：

If there is one who has left Māra, asking about the path
to the other shore, I will equally explain for them the true
and ultimate, without remainder. At that time, practice non-laxity and forever leave Māra's control.

「若有离魔者，　　问度彼岸道，为彼平等说，　　真实永无余，时习不放逸，　　永离魔自在。」

Māra again spoke a verse, saying:

魔复说偈言：

There is a stone resembling a piece of meat, to which a hungry
crow comes to eat. It thinks it is soft and delicious, wanting to
use it to fill its hunger. In the end, it does not get
the taste, breaks its beak, and soars into the sky. I am now
like the crow, Gautama is like a stone. Not entering shame, I depart
like the crow soaring into the sky. With a sorrowful and poisonous mind
I disappear right there.

「有石似段肉，　　饿乌来欲食，彼作软美想，　　欲以补饥虚，竟不得其味，　　折觜而腾虚，我今犹如乌，　　瞿昙如石生，不入愧而去，　　犹乌陵虚逝，内心怀愁毒，　　即彼没不现。」

This is the classical Chinese root text of Saṃyuktāgama, a parallel to the Pali sutta(s) listed above. Source: CBETA (Taishō edition).
