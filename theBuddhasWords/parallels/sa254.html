SĀ 254

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 254（二五四） 二十亿耳

Parallel to: AN 6.55 / MĀ 123 / EĀ 23.3

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, Venerable Vīradatta was staying on Vulture Peak Mountain, always diligently cultivating the factors of enlightenment.

尔时，尊者二十亿耳住耆阇崛山，常精勤修习菩提分法。

At that time, Venerable Vīradatta was alone in quiet meditation, and he had this thought: "Among the disciples of the World-Honored One who diligently practice the teachings, I am one of them. However, I have not yet exhausted all afflictions. I am a noble son from a wealthy family with abundant wealth and treasures. I should now return to enjoy the five desires and widely practice giving and making merit."

时，尊者二十亿耳独静禅思，而作是念：「于世尊弟子精勤声闻中，我在其数，然我今日未尽诸漏，我是名族姓子，多饶财宝，我今宁可还受五欲，广行施作福。」

At that time, the World-Honored One knew the thoughts in Vīradatta's mind and told a bhikṣu, "You should now go to Vīradatta and tell him, 'The World-Honored One calls you.'"

尔时，世尊知二十亿耳心之所念，告一比丘，汝等今往二十亿耳所，告言：「世尊呼汝。」

That bhikṣu, having received the Buddha's instruction, went to Vīradatta and said, "The World-Honored One calls you."

是一比丘受佛教已，往诣二十亿耳所，语言：「世尊呼汝。」

Vīradatta, hearing the bhikṣu call the World-Honored One, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side.

二十亿耳闻彼比丘称大师命，即诣世尊所，稽首礼足，退住一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Vīradatta, "You truly were alone in quiet meditation, thinking, 'The World-Honored One diligently studies and practices among the śrāvakas, and I am among them, but now I have not yet attained the liberation of exhausting the taints. I am a noble son of a famous clan, and I also have much wealth. Should I return to lay life, enjoy the five desires, and widely give and make merit?'"

尔时，世尊告二十亿耳，汝实独静禅思作是念：「世尊精勤修学声闻中，我在其数，而今未得漏尽解脱。我是名族姓子，又多钱财，我宁可还俗，受五欲乐，广施作福耶？」

At that time, Vīradatta thought, "The World-Honored One already knows my mind." He was startled and his hair stood on end. He said to the Buddha, "It is true, World-Honored One!"

时，二十亿耳作是念：「世尊已知我心。」惊怖毛竖，白佛言：「实尔。世尊！」

The Buddha said to Vīradatta, "I now ask you, answer me as you wish. Vīradatta, when you were a layperson, were you good at playing the zither?"

佛告二十亿耳：「我今问汝，随意答我。二十亿耳，汝在俗时，善弹琴不？」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

答言：「如是，世尊！」

He further asked, "What do you think? When you play the zither, if you tighten the strings, can you produce a subtle and harmonious sound?"

复问：「于意云何？汝弹琴时，若急其弦，得作微妙和雅音不？」

He replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

答言：「不也，世尊！」

He further asked, "What if you loosen the strings, can you produce a subtle and harmonious sound?"

复问：「云何？若缓其弦，宁发微妙和雅音不？」

He replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

答言：「不也，世尊！」

He further asked, "How can one tune the strings of a zither neither too loose nor too tight, and then produce a wonderful and harmonious sound?"

复问：「云何善调琴弦，不缓不急，然后发妙和雅音不？」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

答言：「如是，世尊！」

The Buddha told Vīradatta, "If one exerts too much, it increases agitation and remorse. If one exerts too little, it causes laziness. Therefore, you should practice equally, without attachment, carelessness, or grasping at characteristics."

佛告二十亿耳：「精进太急，增其掉悔，精进太缓，令人懈怠，是故汝当平等修习摄受，莫著、莫放逸、莫取相。」

At that time, Venerable Vīradatta, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and followed it. He bowed and departed.

时，尊者二十亿耳闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。

At that time, Venerable Vīradatta always remembered the Buddha's teaching on the analogy of playing the lute. He meditated in solitude and tranquility, as explained above, until his defilements were exhausted, his mind was liberated, and he attained arahantship.

时，尊者二十亿耳常念世尊说弹琴譬，独静禅思。如上所说，乃至漏尽心得解脱，成阿罗汉。

At that time, Venerable Vīradatta attained arahantship and experienced the joy and bliss of inner liberation. He thought, "I should now go to pay my respects to the World-Honored One."

尔时，尊者二十亿耳得阿罗汉，内觉解脱喜乐，作是念：「我今应往问讯世尊。」

At that time, Venerable Vīradatta went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! In the World-Honored One's Dharma, I attained arahantship, exhausted all afflictions, accomplished what was to be done, cast off the heavy burden, attained my own benefit, exhausted all bonds, and attained liberation of mind through right wisdom. At that time, I was liberated from six things. What are the six? Liberation from desire, liberation from anger, liberation from distance, liberation from the exhaustion of craving, liberation from grasping, and liberation from undistracted mindfulness.

尔时，尊者二十亿耳往诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！于世尊法中得阿罗汉，尽诸有漏，所作已作，舍离重担，逮得己利，尽诸有结，正智心解脱，当于尔时解脱六处。云何为六？离欲解脱、离恚解脱、远离解脱、爱尽解脱、诸取解脱、心不忘念解脱。

World-Honored One! If one relies on a small amount of faith and says one is liberated from desire, this is not appropriate; the exhaustion of greed, anger, and delusion is truly liberation from desire.

「世尊！若有依少信心而言离欲解脱，此非所应；贪、恚、痴尽，是名真实离欲解脱。

If someone relies on a small amount of upholding precepts and says they have attained liberation from anger, this is also not appropriate; the exhaustion of greed, anger, and delusion is truly liberation from anger.

「若复有人依少持戒而言我得离恚解脱，此亦不应；贪、恚、痴尽，是名真实离恚解脱。

If someone relies on practicing for the sake of offerings and distance and says they have attained liberation through distance, this is also not appropriate; the exhaustion of greed, anger, and delusion is truly liberation through distance.

「若复有人依于修习利养远离而言远离解脱，是亦不应；贪、恚、痴尽，是真实远离解脱。

The exhaustion of greed, anger, and delusion is also called liberation from desire, liberation from grasping, and liberation from forgetfulness and distraction. Thus, World-Honored One! If bhikṣus have not attained arahantship and have not exhausted all afflictions, they will not attain liberation from these six sense bases.

「贪、恚、痴尽，亦名离爱，亦名离取，亦名离忘念解脱。如是，世尊！若诸比丘未得罗汉，未尽诸漏，于此六处不得解脱。

If a bhikṣu is still in training, has not attained the supreme bliss of nirvāṇa, and his mind is directed towards practice, at that time he accomplishes the precepts of training, accomplishes the faculties of training, and later attains the exhaustion of afflictions, the liberation of the mind free from taints, and knows for himself that he will not be reborn again. At that time he attains the precepts of no more training and the faculties of no more training. It is like a young child lying on his back, at that time he accomplishes the faculties of a child. Later, as he gradually grows, he accomplishes the faculties of an adult. It is the same for one who is still in training. He has not attained the supreme bliss, and so on, up to accomplishing the precepts of no more training and the faculties of no more training.

「若复比丘在于学地，未得增上乐，涅槃习向心住，尔时成就学戒，成就学根，后时当得漏尽、无漏心解脱，乃至自知不受后有。当于尔时得无学戒，得无学诸根。譬如婴童愚小仰卧，尔时成就童子诸根，彼于后时渐渐增长，诸根成就，当于尔时成就长者诸根。在学地者亦复如是，未得增上安乐，乃至成就无学戒、无学诸根。

If the eye consciousness cognizes form, it cannot obstruct the liberation of the mind and wisdom. Because the mind is firmly established, one internally cultivates immeasurable good liberation, observing arising and ceasing, up to impermanence. The ear consciousness and sounds, the nose consciousness and smells, the tongue consciousness and tastes, the body consciousness and tactile objects, and the mind consciousness and dharmas cannot obstruct the liberation of the mind and wisdom. Because the mind is firmly established, one internally cultivates immeasurable good liberation, observing arising and ceasing. It is like a village near a large stone mountain, uncut, unbroken, and unpierced, completely solid. Even if winds from the four directions blow, they cannot move it or penetrate it. The one without taints is also like this. The eye consciousness cognizes form, up to the mind consciousness cognizes dharmas, they cannot obstruct the liberation of the mind and wisdom. Because the mind is firmly established, one internally cultivates immeasurable good liberation, observing arising and ceasing.

「若眼常识色，终不能妨心解脱、慧解脱，意坚住故，内修无量善解脱，观察生灭，乃至无常。耳识声、鼻识香、舌识味、身识触、意识法，不能妨心解脱、慧解脱，意坚住故，内修无量善解脱，观察生灭。譬如村邑近大石山，不断、不坏、不穿，一向厚密，假使四方风吹，不能动摇、不能穿过。彼无学者亦复如是，眼常识色，乃至意常识法，不能妨心解脱、慧解脱，意坚住故，内修无量善解脱，观察生灭。」

Then Vīradatta spoke a verse again:

尔时，二十亿耳重说偈言：

The mind is liberated from desire, and also from anger, and is far
removed from the mind. The mind is liberated from desire, and there is
no remainder of craving. The mind is liberated from grasping, and the mind
does not forget mindfulness. Understanding the arising of the sense bases, the mind
is liberated there. The mind of the one whose mind is liberated, the
bhikṣu rests his mind. What is to be done is done, and what
is to be done is not done. Like a great stone mountain, the
four winds cannot move it. Form, sound, smell, taste, touch, and the good
and bad of dharmas, the six sense bases always confront, but cannot move
the mind. The mind always abides firmly, contemplating the arising and ceasing of dharmas.

「离欲心解脱，　　无恚脱亦然，远离心解脱，　　贪爱永无余。诸取心解脱，　　及意不忘念，晓了入处生，　　于彼心解脱。彼心解脱者，　　比丘意止息，诸所作已作，　　更不作所作。犹如大石山，　　四风不能动。色声香味触，　　及法之好恶，六入处常对，　　不能动其心。心常住坚固，　　谛观法生灭。」

When Venerable Vīradatta spoke this Dharma, the great teacher was delighted. The many learned Brahmins and practitioners, hearing what Venerable Vīradatta said, were all greatly delighted. At that time, Venerable Vīradatta heard the Buddha speak the Dharma, was delighted and rejoiced, paid homage and departed.

尊者二十亿耳说是法时，大师心悦，诸多闻梵行者闻尊者二十亿耳所说，皆大欢喜。尔时，尊者二十亿耳闻佛说法，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing that Vīradatta had left not long ago, told the bhikṣus: "One who is liberated by the good mind should be recorded and spoken of like this, just as Vīradatta recorded and spoke of with wisdom, not elevating oneself or belittling others, but speaking of the meaning correctly; not like one with excessive pride, who does not understand the meaning but praises oneself as having attained the Dharma beyond human beings, bringing about one's own harm and diminution."

尔时，世尊知二十亿耳去不久，告诸比丘：「善心解脱者，应如是记说，如二十亿耳以智记说，亦不自举，亦不下他，正说其义；非如增上慢者，不得其义，而自称叹得过人法，自取损减。」
