SĀ 256

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 256（二五六） 无明

Parallel to: SN 22.126 (~) / SN 22.127–128

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, Venerable Śāriputra and Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila were staying on Vulture Peak.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。尔时，尊者舍利弗、尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗在耆阇崛山。

At that time, Venerable Kauṣṭhila rose from his meditation in the afternoon and went to where Venerable Śāriputra was. They greeted each other and joyfully conversed with each other in various ways. After that, they sat to one side.

时，尊者拘𫄨罗晡时从禅起，诣尊者舍利弗所，共相问讯，种种相娱悦已，却坐一面。

At that time, Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila said to Śāriputra, "I wish to ask something. Is there time for you to explain it to me?"

时，尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗语舍利弗言：「欲有所问，宁有闲暇为我说不？」

Śāriputra said, "Ask as you wish. I will explain what I know."

舍利弗言：「随仁所问，知者当说。」

Mahākauṣṭhila asked Śāriputra, "What is called ignorance? Who has this ignorance?"

摩诃拘𫄨罗问舍利弗言：「所谓无明。云何是无明？谁有此无明？」

Śāriputra replied, "Ignorance means not knowing. Not knowing is ignorance."

舍利弗答言：「无明者谓不知，不知者是无明。」

"What is not known?"

「何所不知？」

"It means not knowing the impermanence of form, not knowing the truth of the impermanence of form, not knowing the truth of the destruction of form, not knowing the truth of the arising and ceasing of form. Not knowing the impermanence of feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, not knowing the truth of the destruction of feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, not knowing the truth of the arising and ceasing of feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. Mahākauṣṭhila! Not truly knowing, seeing, understanding, or being ignorant of these five aggregates is called ignorance. One who possesses this is said to have ignorance."

「谓色无常，色无常如实不知，色磨灭法，色磨灭法如实不知，色生灭法，色生灭法如实不知。受、想、行、识，受、想、行、识无常如实不知，识磨灭法，识磨灭法如实不知，识生灭法，识生灭法如实不知。摩诃拘𫄨罗！于此五受阴如实不知、不见、无无间等、愚、暗、不明，是名无明，成就此者，名有无明。」

He asked again, "Śāriputra! What is called 'enlightenment'? Who has this enlightenment?"

又问：「舍利弗！所谓明者，云何为明？谁有此明？」

Śāriputra said, "Mahākauṣṭhila! What is called 'enlightenment' is knowledge. Knowledge is called 'enlightenment'."

舍利弗言：「摩诃拘𫄨罗！所谓明者是知，知者是名为明。」

He asked again, "What is known?"

又问：「何所知？」

"It is knowing that form is impermanent, knowing that form is impermanent as it really is; form is a law of decay, form is a law of decay as it really is; form is a law of arising and ceasing, form is a law of arising and ceasing as it really is. Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness - knowing that feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are impermanent as they really are; consciousness is a law of decay, consciousness is a law of decay as it really is; consciousness is a law of arising and ceasing, consciousness is a law of arising and ceasing as it really is. Kauṣṭhila! Truly knowing, seeing, illuminating, awakening, discerning, and penetrating these five aggregates of grasping is called 'enlightenment'. One who accomplishes this is called 'enlightened'."

「谓知色无常，知色无常如实知；色磨灭法，色磨灭法如实知；色生灭法，色生灭法如实知。受、想、行、识，受、想、行、识无常如实知，识磨灭法，识磨灭法如实知，识生灭法，识生灭法如实知。拘𫄨罗！于此五受阴如实知、见、明、觉、慧、无间等，是名为明；成就此法者，是名有明。」

These two noble ones each heard what was said, and they rejoiced in turn. They rose from their seats and returned to their respective places.

是二正士各闻所说，展转随喜，从坐而起，各还本处。
