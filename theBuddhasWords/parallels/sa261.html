SĀ 261

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 261（二六一） 富留那

Parallel to: SN 22.83

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Venerable Ānanda was staying in the Kuśīnagara Bamboo Grove.

一时，尊者阿难住拘睒弥国瞿师罗园。

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda addressed the monks, saying: "When the Venerable Pūrṇa Maitrāyaniputra was young and first ordained, he often spoke of the profound Dharma, saying: 'Ānanda! The arising of form is considered to be the self, not the non-arising. Ānanda! How is the arising of form considered to be the self, not the non-arising? The arising of form is the arising of the self, not the non-arising. The arising of feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness is the arising of the self, not the non-arising. It is like a man holding a bright mirror or a clear water mirror, and seeing his own face arising. Because of arising, he sees, not because of non-arising. Therefore, Ānanda! The arising of form is considered to be the self because of arising, not because of non-arising. Similarly, the arising of feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness is considered to be the self because of arising, not because of non-arising. Ānanda! Is form permanent or impermanent?' He replied: 'Impermanent.'

时，尊者阿难告诸比丘：「尊者富留那弥多罗尼子年少初出家时，常说深法，作如是言：『阿难！生法计是我，非不生。阿难！云何于生法计是我，非不生？色生，生是我，非不生。受、想、行、识生，生是我，非不生。譬如士夫手执明镜及净水镜，自见面生，生故见，非不生。是故，阿难！色生，生故计是我，非不生。如是受、想、行、识生，生故计是我，非不生。云何？阿难！色是常耶？为无常耶？』答曰：『无常。』

He further asked: 'If it is impermanent, is it suffering?' He replied: 'It is suffering.' He further asked: 'If it is impermanent and suffering, is it a changeable and transformable phenomenon? Do the noble disciples still consider it to be the self, different from the self, or to have characteristics?' He replied: 'No.' 'Is feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness permanent or impermanent?' He replied: 'Impermanent.' 'If it is impermanent, is it suffering?' He replied: 'It is suffering.' He further asked: 'If it is impermanent and suffering, is it a changeable and transformable phenomenon? Do the learned noble disciples still consider it to be the self, different from the self, or to have characteristics?' He replied: 'No.'

「又问：『无常者，是苦耶？』答曰：『是苦。』又问：『若无常、苦者，是变易法，圣弟子于中复计我、异我、相在不？』答曰：『不也。』『如是受、想、行、识为是常耶？为无常耶？』答曰：『无常。』『若无常，是苦耶？』答曰：『是苦。』又问：『若无常、苦者，是变易法，多闻圣弟子于中宁复计我、异我、相在不？』答曰：『不也。』

Ānanda! Therefore, form, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, is all not the self, not different from the self, nor does it exist in the self. Similarly, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, is all not the self, not different from the self, nor does it exist in the self. Knowing and observing this as it truly is, the noble disciples generate disgust, detachment, and liberation towards form, thinking: "My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and I know that I will not be subject to any future existence." Similarly, they generate disgust, detachment, and liberation towards feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness, thinking: "My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and I know that I will not be subject to any future existence."'

「『阿难！是故，色若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、不异我、不相在。如是受、想、行、识，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、不异我、不相在如实知，如实观察。如是观者，圣弟子于色生厌、离欲、解脱：「我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。」如是受、想、行、识，生厌、离欲、解脱：「我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。」』

"Bhikkhus, know that that venerable one was of great benefit to me. Having heard the Dhamma from that venerable one, I became free from dust and defilements, and attained the pure Dhamma-eye. From that time on, I have always taught this Dhamma to the four assemblies, not the teachings of other non-Buddhist ascetics and brahmins who have gone forth."

「诸比丘当知，彼尊者于我有大饶益，我从彼尊者所闻法已，远尘离垢，得法眼净。我从是来，常以此法为四众说，非余外道沙门、婆罗门出家者说。」
