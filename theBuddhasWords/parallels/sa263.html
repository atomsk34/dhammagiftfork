SĀ 263

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 263（二六三） 应说

Parallel to: SN 22.101 / AN 7.71 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying in the village of Mixed-Colored Cattle Herders in the country of Kuru.

一时，佛住拘留国杂色牧牛聚落。

At that time, the Buddha told the bhikṣus, "I attained the exhaustion of the taints through knowledge and vision, not without knowledge and vision. How is it that I attained the exhaustion of the taints through knowledge and vision, not without knowledge and vision? It is this: form, the arising of form, the cessation of form; feeling, perception, volition, consciousness, the arising of consciousness, the cessation of consciousness. If one does not cultivate the means to accord with and accomplish this, and uses the mind to seek the exhaustion of the taints and the liberation of the mind, one should know that such a bhikṣu will never attain the exhaustion of the taints and liberation. Why is that? Because of not cultivating. What is not cultivated? It is not cultivating the foundations of mindfulness, right effort, the bases of spiritual power, the faculties, the powers, the limbs of awakening, and the path. It is like a hen that has many chicks but is unable to provide timely warmth and coolness, and wants the chicks to peck at the eggs with their beaks and claws to hatch and safely emerge from the shells. One should know that those chicks are incapable of using their beaks and claws to safely emerge from the shells. Why is that? Because that hen mother is unable to provide timely warmth and coolness to raise the chicks.

尔时，佛告诸比丘：「我以知见故，得诸漏尽，非不知见。云何以知见故，得诸漏尽，非不知见？谓此色、此色集、此色灭；此受、想、行、识，此识集、此识灭。不修方便随顺成就，而用心求：『令我诸漏尽，心得解脱。』当知彼比丘终不能得漏尽解脱。所以者何？不修习故，不修习何等？谓不修习念处、正勤、如意足、根、力、觉、道。譬如伏鸡，生子众多，不能随时荫馏，消息冷暖，而欲令子以觜、以爪啄卵自生，安隐出［谷-禾＋卵］，当知彼子无有自力，堪能方便以觜、以爪安隐出［谷-禾＋卵］。所以者何？以彼鸡母不能随时荫馏，冷暖长养子故。

Thus, bhikṣus, if one does not diligently cultivate and accomplish this, and wants to attain the exhaustion of the taints and liberation, it is impossible. Why is that? Because of not cultivating. What is not cultivated? It is not cultivating the foundations of mindfulness, right effort, the bases of spiritual power, the faculties, the powers, the limbs of awakening, and the path. If a bhikṣu diligently cultivates and accomplishes this, even if he does not want to attain the exhaustion of the taints and liberation, that bhikṣu will naturally exhaust the taints and attain liberation of the mind. Why is that? Because of cultivation. What is cultivated? It is cultivating the foundations of mindfulness, right effort, the bases of spiritual power, the faculties, the powers, the limbs of awakening, and the path. It is like that hen that raises her chicks well, providing timely warmth and coolness, and although she does not want the chicks to use their beaks and claws to peck at the eggs and hatch, those chicks are able to use their beaks and claws to safely emerge from the shells. Why is that? Because that hen provides timely warmth and coolness.

「如是，比丘不勤修习随顺成就，而欲令得漏尽解脱，无有是处。所以者何？不修习故。不修何等？谓不修念处、正勤、如意足、根、力、觉、道，若比丘修习随顺成就者，虽不欲令漏尽解脱，而彼比丘自然漏尽，心得解脱。所以者何？以修习故。何所修习？谓修念处、正勤、如意足、根、力、觉、道，如彼伏鸡善养其子，随时荫馏，冷暖得所，正复不欲令子方便自啄卵出，然其诸子自能方便安隐出［谷-禾＋卵］。所以者何？以彼伏鸡随时荫馏，冷暖得所故。

Thus, bhikṣus, if a bhikṣu diligently cultivates the means, and although he does not want to attain the exhaustion of the taints and liberation, that bhikṣu will naturally exhaust the taints and attain liberation of the mind. Why is that? Because of diligent cultivation. What is cultivated? It is cultivating the foundations of mindfulness, right effort, the bases of spiritual power, the faculties, the powers, the limbs of awakening, and the path. It is like a skilled craftsman or his apprentice holding an axe handle. If he does not let go, the handle gradually becomes smaller and the fingers become visible, but he does not notice that the handle is becoming smaller and the fingers are becoming visible.

「如是，比丘善修方便，正复不欲漏尽解脱，而彼比丘自然漏尽，心得解脱。所以者何？以勤修习故。何所修习？谓修念处、正勤、如意足、根、力、觉、道。譬如巧师、巧师弟子，手执斧柯，捉之不已，渐渐微尽手指处现，然彼不觉斧柯微尽而尽处现。

Thus, bhikṣus, if a bhikṣu diligently cultivates and accomplishes this, he does not know or see today how many taints are exhausted, or tomorrow how many taints are exhausted, but that bhikṣu knows that the taints are exhausted. Why is that? Because of cultivation. What is cultivated? It is cultivating the foundations of mindfulness, right effort, the bases of spiritual power, the faculties, the powers, the limbs of awakening, and the path. It is like a large ship on the shore of the ocean, which has been exposed to the wind and sun for six months in the summer, and the ropes that bind it gradually break.

「如是，比丘精勤修习随顺成就，不自知见今日尔所漏尽，明日尔所漏尽，然彼比丘知有漏尽。所以者何？以修习故，何所修习？谓修习念处、正勤、如意足、根、力、觉、道。譬如大舶，在于海边，经夏六月，风飘日暴，藤缀渐断。

"Thus, bhikṣus, if a bhikṣu diligently cultivates and accomplishes this, he gradually attains liberation from all fetters, bonds, afflictions, and entanglements. Why is that? Because of good cultivation. What is cultivated? It is cultivating the foundations of mindfulness, right effort, the bases of spiritual power, the faculties, the powers, the limbs of awakening, and the path."

「如是，比丘精勤修习随顺成就，一切结缚、使、烦恼、缠，渐得解脱。所以者何？善修习故，何所修习？谓修习念处、正勤、如意足、根、力、觉、道。」

When this teaching was spoken, sixty bhikṣus were free from all taints and attained liberation of the mind.

说是法时，六十比丘不起诸漏，心得解脱。

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
