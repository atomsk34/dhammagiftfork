SĀ 265

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 265（二六五） 泡沫

Parallel to: SN 22.95 / SĀ 953 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the bank of the Ganges River in the country of Apidu.

一时，佛住阿毘陀处恒河侧。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "It is like the great floodwaters of the Ganges River, which carry along foam. A person with clear eyes carefully observes and distinguishes it. When carefully observing and distinguishing it, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness. Why is that? Because there is no firmness in that foam. In the same way, all forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, bhikṣus! Carefully contemplate and distinguish them, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness, like a disease, like a boil, like a thorn, like a killer, impermanent, suffering, empty, and not-self. Why is that? Because form is not solid.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「譬如恒河大水暴起，随流聚沫，明目士夫谛观分别；谛观分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固。所以者何？彼聚沫中无坚实故。如是诸所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近。比丘！谛观思惟分别，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固，如病、如痈、如刺、如杀，无常、苦、空、非我。所以者何？色无坚实故。

Bhikṣus! It is like the water bubbles in a heavy rainstorm, appearing and disappearing. A person with clear eyes carefully contemplates and distinguishes them. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing them, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness. Why is that? Because those water bubbles are not solid. In the same way, bhikṣus! All feelings, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near. Bhikṣus! Carefully contemplate and distinguish them. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing them, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness, like a disease, like a boil, like a thorn, like a killer, impermanent, suffering, empty, and not-self. Why is that? Because feelings are not solid.

「诸比丘！譬如大雨水泡，一起一灭，明目士夫谛观思惟分别；谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固。所以者何？以彼水泡无坚实故。如是，比丘！诸所有受，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近。比丘！谛观思惟分别。谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固，如病、如痈、如刺、如杀，无常、苦、空、非我。所以者何？以受无坚实故。

Bhikṣus! It is like the mirage in the hot season, without clouds or rain, in the middle of the day, when the sun is shining. A person with clear eyes carefully contemplates and distinguishes it. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing it, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness. Why is that? Because that mirage is not solid. In the same way, bhikṣus! All perceptions, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near. Bhikṣus! Carefully contemplate and distinguish them. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing them, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness, like a disease, like a boil, like a thorn, like a killer, impermanent, suffering, empty, and not-self. Why is that? Because perceptions are not solid.

「诸比丘！譬如春末夏初，无云、无雨，日盛中时，野马流动，明目士夫谛观思惟分别；谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固。所以者何？以彼野马无坚实故。如是，比丘！诸所有想，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麤、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近。比丘！谛观思惟分别。谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固，如病、如痈、如刺、如杀，无常、苦、空、非我。所以者何？以想无坚实故。

Bhikṣus! It is like a person with clear eyes seeking solid wood. Holding a sharp axe, they enter a forest and see a large plantain tree, straight and tall. They immediately cut its roots and chop off its top, peeling off the leaves one by one, finding nothing solid. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing it, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness. Why is that? Because that plantain has no solidity. In the same way, bhikṣus! All formations, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near. Bhikṣus! Carefully contemplate and distinguish them. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing them, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness, like a disease, like a boil, like a thorn, like a killer, impermanent, suffering, empty, and not-self. Why is that? Because those formations have no solidity.

「诸比丘！譬如明目士夫求坚固材，执持利斧，入于山林，见大芭蕉树，佣直长大，即伐其根，斩截其峰，叶叶次剥，都无坚实，谛观思惟分别。谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固。所以者何？以彼芭蕉无坚实故。如是，比丘！诸所有行，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近。比丘！谛观思惟分别。谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固，如病、如痈、如刺、如杀，无常、苦、空、非我。所以者何？以彼诸行无坚实故。

Bhikṣus! It is like a magician or a magician's disciple, at the crossroads, conjuring up an army of elephants, horses, chariots, and infantry. A person with clear eyes and wisdom carefully contemplates and distinguishes it. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing it, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness. Why is that? Because that illusion has no solidity. In the same way, bhikṣus! All consciousness, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near. Bhikṣus! Carefully contemplate and distinguish it. When carefully contemplating and distinguishing it, there is nothing, no solidity, no reality, no firmness, like a disease, like a boil, like a thorn, like a killer, impermanent, suffering, empty, and not-self. Why is that? Because consciousness has no solidity.

「诸比丘！譬如幻师、若幻师弟子，于四衢道头，幻作象兵、马兵、车兵、步兵，有智明目士夫谛观思惟分别；谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固。所以者何？以彼幻无坚实故。如是，比丘！诸所有识，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近。比丘！谛观思惟分别，谛观思惟分别时，无所有、无牢、无实、无有坚固，如病、如痈、如刺、如杀，无常、苦、空、非我。所以者何？以识无坚实故。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, wanting to elaborate on this meaning, spoke a verse:

尔时，世尊欲重宣此义，而说偈言：

Seeing form as like foam, feeling as like bubbles on water, perception
as like a spring mirage, conditioning as like a plantain tree, and
consciousness as like an illusion, the Honored One of the Solar Clan
has said. Carefully contemplating all around, with right mindfulness and good observation
there is no reality, no solidity, no firmness, no self or mine
Regarding this body of suffering, the greatly wise one has explained it
as being separate from the three dharmas. The body becomes discarded, with
life, warmth, and consciousness leaving it. The remaining parts of the body
are forever abandoned in the charnel ground, like a log without the
thought of consciousness. This body is always like this, an illusion to
deceive the foolish. Like killing, like a poisonous thorn, there is no
solidity. The bhikṣu diligently cultivates and observes this body of aggregates, always
diligently and single-mindedly during day and night, with right wisdom and focused
attention. The conditioned activities cease, and one forever attains the place of coolness.

「观色如聚沫，　　受如水上泡，想如春时燄，　　诸行如芭蕉，诸识法如幻，　　日种姓尊说。周匝谛思惟，　　正念善观察，无实不坚固，　　无有我我所。于此苦阴身，　　大智分别说，离于三法者，　　身为成弃物，寿暖及诸识，　　离此余身分，永弃丘冢间，　　如木无识想。此身常如是，　　幻为诱愚夫。如杀如毒刺，　　无有坚固者。比丘勤修习，　　观察此阴身，昼夜常专精，　　正智系念住，有为行长息，　　永得清凉处。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
