SĀ 270

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 270（二七〇） 树

Parallel to: SN 22.102

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance. It is like a farmer deeply plowing the field in late summer and early autumn, uprooting weeds. In the same way, bhikṣus, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。譬如田夫，于夏末秋初深耕其地，发荄断草。如是，比丘！无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like a person cutting grass, grasping the tip with the hand, lifting it and shaking it, the withered and dead leaves fall off, taking the long ones. In the same way, bhikṣus, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如，比丘！如人刈草，手揽其端，举而抖擞，萎枯悉落，取其长者。如是，比丘！无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like an āmra fruit on a tree, a strong wind shakes the branches, and the fruit all falls down. In the same way, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如庵罗果著树，猛风摇条，果悉堕落。如是，无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like a pavilion, with a solid center, supported by many materials, embracing and not scattering. In the same way, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如楼阁，中心坚固，众材所依，摄受不散。如是，无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like the footprints of all beings, with the elephant's footprint being the largest, because it can embrace. In the same way, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如一切众生迹，象迹为大，能摄受故。如是，无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like all the rivers of Jambudvipa flowing into the great ocean, with the great ocean being the foremost, because it can embrace. In the same way, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如阎浮提一切诸河，悉赴大海，其大海者，最为第一，悉摄受故。如是，无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like the rising sun, which can dispel all the darkness in the world. In the same way, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如日出，能除一切世间暗冥。如是，无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

It is like the Wheel-Turning Sage King, who is the foremost and most supreme among all minor kings. In the same way, cultivating the perception of impermanence extensively and repeatedly can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance.

「譬如转轮圣王，于诸小王最上、最胜。如是，无常想修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。

"Monks! How does cultivating the perception of impermanence, cultivating it extensively and repeatedly, eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance? If a monk, in an open and exposed place or between trees, correctly contemplates and observes that form is impermanent, and that feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are impermanent, then by contemplating in this way, he can eliminate all desire, form, and formless realms, agitation, pride, and ignorance. Why is this? The perception of impermanence can establish the perception of no-self. When noble disciples abide in the perception of no-self, their minds are free from the conceit of self, and they attain nirvana in accordance with it."

「诸比丘！云何修无常想，修习多修习，能断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明？若比丘于空露地、若林树间，善正思惟，观察色无常，受、想、行、识无常。如是思惟，断一切欲爱、色爱、无色爱、掉、慢、无明。所以者何？无常想者，能建立无我想，圣弟子住无我想，心离我慢，顺得涅槃。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha taught rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说是经已，时，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
