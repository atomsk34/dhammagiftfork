SĀ 271

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 271（二七一） 低舍

Parallel to: SN 22.84

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, there was a bhikṣu named Dīrghāyu who, together with many other bhikṣus, gathered in the dining hall and said to the other bhikṣus, "Venerable ones, I do not discern the Dharma, do not delight in practicing the holy life, am fond of sleeping a lot, and am doubtful about the Dharma."

尔时，有比丘名曰低舍，与众多比丘集于食堂，语诸比丘言：「诸尊，我不分别于法，不乐修梵行，多乐睡眠，疑惑于法。」

At that time, a bhikṣu in the assembly went to where the Buddha was, bowed at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! The bhikṣu Dīrghāyu, together with many other bhikṣus, gathered in the dining hall and said this, proclaiming, 'I cannot discern the Dharma, do not delight in practicing the holy life, am fond of sleeping a lot, and am doubtful about the Dharma.'"

尔时，众中有一比丘，往诣佛所，礼佛足，却住一面，白佛言：「世尊！低舍比丘以众多比丘集于食堂，作如是说，唱言：『我不能分别于法，不乐修梵行，多乐睡眠，疑惑于法。』」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "This bhikṣu Dīrghāyu is a foolish person. He does not guard the sense-doors, eats without moderation, is not mindful in the early and late watches of the night, is lazy and indolent, does not diligently practice, and does not observe and contemplate the good Dharma. It is impossible for him to discern the Dharma, delight in practicing the holy life, be free from sleep, and be free from doubt in the true Dharma. If a bhikṣu guards the sense-doors, eats in moderation, is mindful in the early and late watches of the night, diligently practices, observes the good Dharma, delights in discerning the Dharma, delights in practicing the holy life, is free from sleep, and has no doubts about the Dharma, this is possible."

佛告比丘：「是低舍比丘是愚痴人，不守根门，饮食不知量，初夜、后夜，心不觉悟，懈怠嬾惰，不勤精进，不善观察思惟善法；彼于分别法，心乐修梵行，离诸睡眠，于正法中离诸疑惑，无有是处。若当比丘守护根门，饮食知量，初夜、后夜，觉悟精进，观察善法，乐分别法，乐修梵行，离于睡眠，心不疑法，斯有是处。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told a bhikṣu, "Go and tell the bhikṣu Dīrghāyu that the Master is calling for him."

尔时，世尊告一比丘，汝往语低舍比丘言：「大师呼汝。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Yes, I accept your instruction." He then went forward, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and said to Dīrghāyu, "Elder Dīrghāyu, the World-Honored One is calling for you." Upon hearing the message, Dīrghāyu went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side.

比丘白佛：「唯然，受教。」前礼佛足，诣低舍所，而作是言：「长老低舍！世尊呼汝！」低舍闻命，诣世尊所，稽首礼足，却住一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣu Dīrghāyu, "Dīrghāyu, is it true that you gathered with many bhikṣus in the dining hall and proclaimed, 'Elders, I am unable to discern the Dharma, have no joy in the holy life, am fond of sleep, and am doubtful about the Dharma'?" Dīrghāyu said to the Buddha, "It is true, World-Honored One!"

尔时，世尊语低舍比丘言：「汝低舍！实与众多比丘集于食堂，作是唱言：『诸长老！我不能分别于法，不乐梵行，多乐睡眠，疑惑于法』耶？」低舍白佛：「实尔。世尊！」

The Buddha asked Dīrghāyu, "I will ask you, and you may answer as you wish. What do you think? If one is not free from desire, passion, attachment, thought, and thirst towards form, and that form changes or becomes different, what do you think? Will one then give rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress?"

佛问低舍：「我今问汝，随汝意答。于意云何？若于色不离贪、不离欲、不离爱、不离念、不离渴，彼色若变、若异。于汝意云何？当起忧、悲、恼、苦为不耶？」

Dīrghāyu said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One! If one is not free from desire, passion, attachment, thought, and thirst towards form, and that form changes or becomes different, one will indeed give rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. World-Honored One, it is indeed so, not otherwise."

低舍白佛：「如是，世尊！若于色不离贪、不离欲、不离爱、不离念、不离渴，彼色若变、若异，实起忧、悲、恼、苦。世尊！实尔不异。」

The Buddha told Dīrghāyu, "Excellent, excellent! Dīrghāyu, it should indeed be so, speaking the Dharma without being free from desire. Dīrghāyu, if one is not free from desire, passion, attachment, thought, and thirst towards feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, and that consciousness changes or becomes different, what do you think? Will one then give rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress?"

佛告低舍：「善哉！善哉！低舍！正应如是，不离贪欲说法。低舍！于受、想、行、识，不离贪、不离欲、不离爱、不离念、不离渴，彼识若变、若异，于汝意云何？当起忧、悲、恼、苦为不耶？」

Dīrghāyu said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One! If one is not free from desire, passion, attachment, thought, and thirst towards consciousness, and that consciousness changes or becomes different, one will indeed give rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. World-Honored One, it is indeed so, not otherwise."

低舍白佛：「如是，世尊！于识不离贪、不离欲、不离爱、不离念、不离渴，彼识若变、若异，实起忧、悲、恼、苦。世尊！实尔不异。」

The Buddha told Dīrghāyu, "Excellent, excellent! It should indeed be so, speaking the Dharma without being free from desire towards consciousness."

佛告低舍：「善哉！善哉！正应如是，识不离贪欲说法。」

The Buddha asked Dīrghāyu, "What do you think? If one is free from desire, passion, attachment, thought, and thirst towards form, and that form changes or becomes different, will one give rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress?"

佛告低舍：「于意云何？若于色离贪、离欲、离爱、离念、离渴，彼色若变、若异时，当生忧、悲、恼、苦耶？」

Dīrghāyu said to the Buddha, "No, World-Honored One!"

低舍白佛：「不也，世尊！」

"It is not different. What do you think? If one is free from desire, passion, attachment, thought, and thirst towards feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, and that consciousness changes or becomes different, will one give rise to sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress?"

「如是不异。于意云何？受、想、行、识，离贪、离欲、离爱、离念、离渴，彼识若变、若异，当生忧、悲、恼、苦耶？」

Dīrghāyu replied, "No, World-Honored One! It is not different."

低舍答曰：「不也，世尊！如是不异。」

The Buddha told Dīrghāyu, "Excellent! Excellent! Dīrghāyu! Now I will give an analogy. The greatly wise understand through analogies. It is like two men traveling together on the same road, one knows the road well, the other does not. The one who does not know asks the one who knows the road, 'I wish to go to such and such a city, village, or town. Please show me the way.' The one who knows the road then shows him the way and says, 'Sir, go from here. Ahead you will see two roads. Leave the left and take the right. There will be ditches and streams. Again, leave the left and take the right. There will be thickets. Again, leave the left and take the right. You should proceed in this way and gradually reach such and such a city.'"

佛告低舍：「善哉！善哉！低舍！今当说譬，大智慧者，以譬得解。如二士夫共伴行一路，一善知路，一不知路，其不知者语知路者，作如是言：『我欲诣某城、某村、某聚落，当示我路。』时，知路者即示彼路，语言：『士夫，从此道去，前见二道，舍左从右前行，复有坑涧渠流，复当舍左从右，复有丛林，复当舍左从右。汝当如是渐渐前行，得至某城。』」

The Buddha told Dīrghāyu, "The analogy is like this: The one who does not know the road is like an ignorant ordinary person. The one who knows the road is like the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One. The two roads ahead refer to the doubts of sentient beings. The left road refers to the three unwholesome mental factors - greed, anger, and harmfulness. The right road refers to the three wholesome mental factors - the awareness of detachment from desire, non-anger, and non-harmfulness. Going on the left road ahead refers to wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, and wrong concentration. Going on the right road ahead refers to right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. The ditches, streams, and canals refer to anger, obstruction, sorrow, and grief. The thickets refer to the five sense pleasures. The city refers to parinirvāṇa."

佛告低舍：「其譬如是：不知路者，譬愚痴凡夫。其知路者，譬如来、应、等正觉。前二路者，谓众生狐疑。左路者，三不善法——贪、恚、害觉。其右路者，谓三善觉——出要离欲觉、不瞋觉、不害觉。前行左路者，谓邪见、邪志、邪语、邪业、邪命、邪方便、邪念、邪定。前行右路者，谓正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定。坑涧渠流者，谓瞋恚、覆障、忧、悲。丛林者，谓五欲功德也。城者，谓般涅槃。」

The Buddha told Dīrghāyu, "The Buddha is the great teacher, and has done what he should do for the śrāvakas. Now he should do what he should do with compassion and pity, and bring peace and happiness to all. You should do what you should do today. You should sit under a tree, or in an open space, a mountain cave, or a house, spreading grass for a seat. Think well, be mindful, and cultivate non-distraction. Do not let there be regret in your mind later. I now instruct you."

佛告低舍：「佛为大师，为诸声闻所作已作，如今当作哀愍悲念，以义安乐，皆悉已作。汝等今日，当作所作，当于树下，或空露地、山岩窟宅，敷草为座，善思正念，修不放逸，莫令久后心有悔恨，我今教汝。」

At that time, Dīrghāyu heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully accepted it.

尔时，低舍闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
