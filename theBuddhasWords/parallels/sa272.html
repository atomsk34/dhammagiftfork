SĀ 272

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 272（二七二） 诸想

Parallel to: SN 22.80 / MĀ 140 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, there was a minor dispute in the assembly. The World-Honored One reproached the bhikṣus. In the morning, he put on his robe, took his bowl, and entered the city to beg for food. After eating, he left, put away his robe and bowl, washed his feet, entered the Andha forest, sat under a tree, and contemplated in solitude, thinking: "There was a minor dispute in the assembly. I reproached the bhikṣus. However, there are many young bhikṣus in that assembly who have been ordained for a short time and have not seen the Great Teacher. They may feel regretful and be unhappy. I have long had a compassionate mind towards the bhikṣus. Now I should return to gather them in with compassion."

尔时，众中有少诤事，世尊责诸比丘故，晨朝著衣持钵，入城乞食。食已，出，摄举衣钵，洗足，入安陀林，坐一树下，独静思惟，作是念：「众中有少诤事，我责诸比丘，然彼众中多年少比丘，出家未久，不见大师，或起悔心，愁忧不乐。我已长夜于诸比丘生哀愍心，今当复还，摄取彼众，以哀愍故。」

At that time, the Great Brahmā King knew the Buddha's thoughts. In the time it takes for a strong man to bend and stretch his arm, he disappeared from the Brahmā Heaven and appeared before the Buddha. He said to the Buddha: "Yes, World-Honored One! Yes, Well-Gone One! You reproach the bhikṣus because of a minor dispute. In that assembly, there are many young bhikṣus who have been ordained for a short time and have not seen the Great Teacher. They may feel regretful and be unhappy. The World-Honored One has long had compassion and gathered the saṃgha. Excellent, World-Honored One! Please now return to gather the bhikṣus."

时，大梵王知佛心念，如力士屈伸臂顷，从梵天没，住于佛前，而白佛言：「如是，世尊！如是，善逝！责诸比丘，以少诤事故。于彼众中多有年少比丘，出家未久，不见大师，或起悔心，愁忧不乐。世尊长夜哀愍，摄受众僧。善哉！世尊！愿今当还摄诸比丘。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having already had compassion for the Brahmā Heaven, silently accepted. The Great Brahmā Heaven knew that the Buddha had silently accepted. He bowed to the Buddha, circumambulated him three times to the right, and suddenly disappeared.

尔时，世尊心已垂愍梵天故，默然而许。时，大梵天知佛世尊默然已许，为佛作礼，右遶三匝，忽然不现。

At that time, not long after the World-Honored One and the Great Brahmā King had parted, he returned to Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma. He spread out his sitting mat, straightened his body, and sat upright, expressing subtle signs, allowing the bhikṣus to come and pay homage. At that time, the bhikṣus came to where the Buddha was, with a look of shame and embarrassment. They bowed at the Buddha's feet, then sat to one side.

尔时世尊，大梵天王还去未久，即还祇树给孤独园。敷尼师檀，歛身正坐，表现微相，令诸比丘敢来奉见。时，诸比丘来诣佛所，怀惭愧色，前礼佛足，却坐一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Those who have left home live humbly, shaving their heads and carrying alms bowls, begging for food from household to household, as if under a spell. The reason for this is to seek the supreme truth, to liberate from birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress, and to reach the end of suffering. Good men! You have not left home to serve the king or thieves, nor are you debtors, nor are you fleeing in fear, nor are you leaving to avoid losing your life. You have left home to be liberated from birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. Have you not left home for these reasons?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「出家之人，卑下活命，剃发持钵，家家乞食，如被噤呪。所以然者，为求胜义故，为度生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦、究竟苦边故。诸善男子！汝不为王贼所使、非负债人、不为恐怖、不为失命而出家，正为解脱生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。汝等不为此而出家耶？」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「实尔。世尊！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "You bhikṣus have left home for such supreme truth. How can there still be foolish ordinary people among you who give rise to greed and attachment, extreme defilement, anger and violence, laziness and inferiority, forgetfulness and instability, and confusion of the faculties? It is like a person going from darkness into darkness, from gloom into gloom, from a latrine into another latrine, washing blood with blood, abandoning evil and then taking up evil again. I use this analogy to describe such foolish bhikṣus. It is also like a funeral pyre, discarded in a charnel ground, not picked up for firewood. I use this analogy to describe foolish ordinary bhikṣus who give rise to greed and attachment, extreme defilement, anger and violence, laziness and inferiority, forgetfulness and instability, and confusion of the faculties.

佛告比丘：「汝等比丘为如是胜义而出家。云何于中犹复有一愚痴凡夫，而起贪欲，极生染著，瞋恚凶暴，懈怠下劣，失念不定，诸根迷乱？譬如士夫从暗而入暗，从冥入冥，从粪厕出复堕粪厕，以血洗血，舍离诸恶还复取恶。我说此譬，凡愚比丘亦复如是。又复譬如焚尸火［木＊（暴-（日／共）＋夭）］，捐弃塜间，不为樵伐之所采拾。我说此譬，愚痴凡夫比丘而起贪欲，极生染著，瞋恚凶暴，懈怠下劣，失念不定，诸根散乱，亦复如是。

Bhikṣus! There are three unwholesome states of consciousness. What are the three? The states of consciousness of greed, anger, and harm. These three states of consciousness arise from conceptualization. What is conceptualization? Conceptualization has countless kinds, such as the conceptualization of greed, anger, and harm. All unwholesome states of consciousness arise from this. Bhikṣus! The conceptualization of greed, anger, and harm, the states of consciousness of greed, anger, and harm, and the countless kinds of unwholesomeness - how can they be completely extinguished? By focusing the mind on the four foundations of mindfulness, abiding in the formless samādhi, cultivating and practicing it extensively, the unwholesome and unskillful dharmas will be extinguished from this, completely and forever. It is precisely by this method.

「比丘！有三不善觉法。何等为三？贪觉、恚觉、害觉，此三觉由想而起。云何想？想有无量种种，贪想、恚想、害想，诸不善觉从此而生。比丘！贪想、恚想、害想、贪觉、恚觉、害觉，及无量种种不善。云何究竟灭尽？于四念处系心，住无相三昧，修习多修习，恶不善法从是而灭，无余永尽，正以此法。

"Good sons and daughters who believe and delight in leaving home to cultivate the formless samādhi, having cultivated and practiced it extensively, abide in the gate of immortality, and ultimately attain the nirvāṇa of immortality. I do not say that this nirvāṇa of immortality is based on the three views. What are the three? There is one kind of view that says like this: 'Life is the body.' There is also this kind of view: 'Life is different from the body.' It is also said: 'Form is me, without duality or difference, eternal and unchanging.' The well-versed noble disciples contemplate like this: 'Is there any one dharma in the world that can be grasped without fault?' Having contemplated, they do not see any one dharma that can be grasped without fault. If I grasp form, there will be fault; if I grasp feeling, perception, volition, or consciousness, there will be fault. Having understood this, they have nothing to grasp in the world. Having nothing to grasp, they realize nirvāṇa for themselves: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, I know for myself that I will not be subject to future lives.'"

「善男子、善女人信乐出家，修习无相三昧，修习多修习已，住甘露门，乃至究竟甘露涅槃。我不说此甘露涅槃，依三见者。何等为三？有一种见如是如是说：『命则是身。』复有如是见：『命异身异。』又作是说：『色是我，无二无异，长存不变。』多闻圣弟子作是思惟：『世间颇有一法可取而无罪过者？』思惟已，都不见一法可取而无罪过者。我若取色，即有罪过；若取受、想、行、识，则有罪过。作是知已，于诸世间，则无所取，无所取者，自觉涅槃：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha then said to the bhikṣus: "The bhikṣus should know that the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, has attained the supreme, perfect enlightenment, and has realized the supreme, perfect enlightenment. The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, has attained the supreme,
