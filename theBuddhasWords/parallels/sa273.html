SĀ 273

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 273（二七三） 手声喻

Parallel to: SN 35.92–93 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu was alone in quiet contemplation, thinking, "What is the self? What is the purpose of the self? What is the self? Where does the self abide?" Having awakened from meditation, he went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! When I was alone in a quiet place, I had this thought: 'What is the self? What is the purpose of the self? What is the self? Where does the self abide?'"

时，有异比丘独静思惟：「云何为我？我何所为？何等是我？我何所住？」从禅觉已，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，退住一面，白佛言：「世尊！我独一静处，作是思惟：『云何为我？我何所为？何法是我？我于何住？』」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "Now I will explain two dharmas to you. Listen carefully and consider well. What are the two? The eye and form are two. The ear and sound, the nose and smell, the tongue and taste, the body and touch, and the mind and dharmas are two. These are called the two dharmas. Bhikṣu! If someone says, 'The two dharmas spoken by Śramaṇa Gautama are not two. I now abandon this and establish two dharmas,' they only have words. When asked, they do not know, increasing their confusion, because it is not their realm. Why is that? Because the eye and form give rise to eye consciousness.

佛告比丘：「今当为汝说于二法。谛听，善思，云何为二？眼色为二。耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触、意法为二，是名二法。比丘！若有说言：『沙门瞿昙所说二法，此非为二，我今舍此，更立二法。』彼但有言数，问已不知，增其疑惑，以非境界故。所以者何？缘眼、色，生眼识。

Bhikṣu! That eye is fleshly form, internal, conditioned, solid, and felt. This is called the internal earth element of the fleshly form of the eye. Bhikṣu! If the fleshly form of the eye is internal, conditioned, moist, and felt, this is called the internal water element of the fleshly form of the eye. Bhikṣu! If that fleshly form of the eye is internal, conditioned, bright and warm, and felt, this is called the internal fire element of the fleshly form of the eye. Bhikṣu! If that fleshly form of the eye is internal, conditioned, light, floating, and moving, and felt, this is called the internal wind element of the fleshly form of the eye.

「比丘！彼眼者，是肉形、是内、是因缘、是坚、是受，是名眼肉形内地界。比丘！若眼肉形，若内、若因缘、津泽、是受，是名眼肉形内水界。比丘！若彼眼肉形，若内、若因缘、明暖、是受，是名眼肉形内火界。比丘！若彼眼肉形，若内、若因缘、轻飘动摇、是受，是名眼肉形内风界。

Bhikṣu! It is like the sound made by the palms of two hands clapping together. In the same way, when the eye and form are conditions, eye consciousness arises. The three things coming together is contact. Along with contact, there is feeling, perception, and volition. These dharmas are not the self, not permanent, and impermanent. This is the impermanent self, not constant, not peaceful, and changing. Why is that? Bhikṣu! It is because of the dharmas of birth, aging, death, passing away, and rebirth.

「比丘！譬如两手和合相对作声。如是缘眼、色，生眼识，三事和合触，触俱生受、想、思。此等诸法非我、非常，是无常之我，非恒、非安隐、变易之我。所以者何？比丘！谓生、老、死、没、受生之法。

Bhikṣu! All conditioned things are like illusions and mirages, momentary, perishable, and unreal. They come and go. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should know, rejoice, and remember the emptiness of conditioned things: 'The emptiness of conditioned things is permanent, constant, abiding, and unchanging. It is empty, without self or anything belonging to a self.' It is like a person with clear eyes holding a bright lamp and entering an empty room. They observe the empty room.

「比丘！诸行如幻、如炎，刹那时顷尽朽，不实来实去。是故，比丘！于空诸行当知、当喜、当念：『空诸行常、恒、住、不变易法空，无我、我所。』譬如明目士夫，手执明灯，入于空室，彼空室观察。

"In the same way, bhikṣus! You should observe and rejoice in the emptiness of all conditioned things and the emptiness of the mind. In the practice of the empty dharmas, it is permanent, constant, abiding, and unchanging. It is empty, without self or anything belonging to a self. Like the eye, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind are conditions for the arising of mental consciousness. The three things coming together is contact. Along with contact, there is feeling, perception, and volition. These dharmas are without self, impermanent, and so forth, up to empty, without self or anything belonging to a self. Bhikṣus! What do you think? Is the eye permanent or impermanent?"

「如是，比丘！于一切空行、空心观察欢喜，于空法行，常、恒、住、不变易法，空我、我所。如眼，耳、鼻、舌、身、意法因缘生意识，三事和合触，触俱生受、想、思，此诸法无我、无常，乃至空我、我所。比丘！于意云何？眼是常、为非常耶？」

He replied, "Impermanent, World-Honored One!"

答言：「非常。世尊！」

He further asked, "If it is impermanent, is it suffering?"

复问：「若无常者，是苦耶？」

He replied, "It is suffering, World-Honored One!"

答言：「是苦。世尊！」

He further asked, "If it is impermanent and suffering, a changeable and unstable phenomenon, would the learned noble disciples see self, other, or characteristics in it?"

复问：「若无常、苦，是变易法，多闻圣弟子宁于中见我、异我、相在不？」

He replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

答言：「不也，世尊！」

The same applies to the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. Thus, the learned noble disciples generate disgust towards the eye, due to disgust they do not delight, due to non-delight they are liberated, and through liberated knowledge and vision they know: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, I know that I will not experience another existence.' The same applies to the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind.

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦复如是。如是多闻圣弟子于眼生厌，厌故不乐，不乐故解脱，解脱知见：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦复如是。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard the World-Honored One's teaching on the sūtra of the parable of the sound of clapping hands, dwelt alone in seclusion, diligently contemplating and not being negligent, and attained the knowledge that he would not experience another existence, becoming an arhat.

时，彼比丘闻世尊说合手声譬经教已，独一静处，专精思惟，不放逸住，乃至自知不受后有。成阿罗汉。
