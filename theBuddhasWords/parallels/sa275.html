SĀ 275

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 275（二七五） 难陀

Parallel to: AN 8.9 / SĀ 1066 / SĀ² 2.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Those who say that he has great strength, that is indeed correct. Those who say that he is the most handsome, that is indeed correct. Those who say that he is deeply attached to desire, that is indeed correct.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「其有说言大力者，其唯难陀！此是正说。其有说言最端政者，其唯难陀！是则正说。其有说言爱欲重者，其唯难陀！是则正说。

Bhikṣus! Now Nanda closes the sense doors, knows moderation in eating, diligently practices in the early and late watches of the night, accomplishes right knowledge, and is able to fully and purely practice the holy life for the rest of his life. Because Nanda closes the sense doors, when he sees forms with the eye, he does not grasp the characteristics of forms, nor does he grasp their secondary characteristics. If the eye faculty increases unruliness, ignorance, darkness, worldly craving, love, evil, unwholesome dharmas, and does not leak into his mind, it produces restraint. Protecting the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind faculties, it produces restraint. This is called Nanda bhikṣu closing the sense doors.

「诸比丘！而今难陀关闭根门，饮食知量，初夜、后夜精勤修习，正智成就，堪能尽寿纯一满净，梵行清白。彼难陀比丘关闭根门故，若眼见色，不取色相，不取随形好。若诸眼根增不律仪、无明暗障、世间贪、爱、恶不善法不漏其心，生诸律仪；防护于眼、耳、鼻、舌、身、意根，生诸律仪，是名难陀比丘关闭根门。

"Knowing moderation in eating" means that bhikṣu Nanda is mindful of food: "Not for self-aggrandizement, not for indulgence, not for attachment to form, not for attachment to adornment, but only to support the body. Accepting what is obtained, to stop hunger and thirst, for the sake of practicing the holy life. Therefore, to stop the arising of painful feelings and prevent the arising of unarisen painful feelings, to accomplish their aspiration. For the sake of peaceful and blissful, non-offensive touch. Just as a person rides a cart, anointing it with oil, not for self-aggrandizement, up to adornment, but for the sake of carrying. Also, like applying medicine to a wound, not for the taste, but to stop the pain." Thus, the good man Nanda knows moderation in eating, up to peaceful and blissful, non-offensive touch. This is called Nanda knowing moderation in eating.

「饮食知量者，难陀比丘于食系数：『不自高、不放逸、不著色、不著庄严，支身而已。任其所得，为止饥渴，修梵行故。故起苦觉令息灭，未起苦觉令不起故，成其崇向故。气力安乐，无罪触住故。如人乘车，涂以膏油，不为自高，乃至庄严，为载运故。又如涂疮，不贪其味，为息苦故。』如是，善男子难陀知量而食，乃至无罪触住，是名难陀知量而食。

"The good man Nanda diligently practices in the early and late watches of the night" means that Nanda during the day walks and sits in meditation, removing the obscurations and purifying his body. In the early part of the night, he walks and sits in meditation, removing the obscurations and purifying his body. In the middle of the night, he washes his feet outside the room, enters the room, lies on his right side, bends his knees, and places his feet one on top of the other, with mindfulness and clear perception, thinking of rising. In the later part of the night, he slowly wakes up, slowly gets up, and walks and sits in meditation. This is called the good man Nanda diligently practicing in the early and late watches of the night.

「彼善男子难陀初夜、后夜精勤修业者，彼难陀昼则经行、坐禅，除去阴障，以净其身。于初夜时，经行、坐禅，除去阴障，以净其身。于中夜时，房外洗足，入于室中，右脇而卧，屈膝累足，系念明想，作起觉想。于后夜时，徐觉徐起，经行、坐禅，是名善男子难陀初夜、后夜精勤修集。

"The good man Nanda has superior mindfulness and right knowledge" means that this good man Nanda observes the eastern direction, with single-minded right mindfulness, dwelling in observation; observing the southern, western, and northern directions is also like this. Dwelling in observation with single-minded right mindfulness. Observing in this way, worldly greed, love, and evil and unwholesome dharmas do not leak into his mind. This good man Nanda is aware of the arising of all feelings, is aware of the abiding of all feelings, is aware of the cessation of all feelings, and abides with right mindfulness, not allowing distraction; is aware of the arising of all thoughts, is aware of the abiding of all thoughts, is aware of the cessation of all thoughts, is aware of the arising of all perceptions, is aware of the abiding of all perceptions, is aware of the cessation of all perceptions, and abides with right mindfulness, not allowing distraction. This is called the good man Nanda accomplishing superior mindfulness and right knowledge.

「彼善男子难陀胜念正知者，是善男子难陀观察东方，一心正念，安住观察；观察南、西、北方，亦复如是。一心正念，安住观察。如是观者，世间贪、爱、恶不善法不漏其心。彼善男子难陀觉诸受起，觉诸受住，觉诸受灭，正念而住，不令散乱；觉诸想起，觉诸想住，觉诸想灭，觉诸觉起，觉诸觉住，觉诸觉灭，正念心住，不令散乱，是名善男子难陀正念正智成就。

Therefore, bhikṣus! You should train like this: 'Closing the sense-doors like the good man Nanda; eating in moderation like the good man Nanda; diligently practicing in the early and late watches of the night like the good man Nanda; accomplishing right mindfulness and right knowledge like the good man Nanda.' As instructed, the method for Nanda should also be upheld and taught to others.

「是故，诸比丘！当作是学：『关闭根门，如善男子难陀；饮食知量，如善男子难陀；初夜、后夜精勤修业，如善男子难陀；正念正智成就，如善男子难陀。』如教授难陀法，亦当持是为其余人说。」

At that time, another bhikṣu spoke a verse:

时，有异比丘而说偈言：

Well closing the sense-doors, with right mindfulness, the mind
abides. Eating in moderation, knowing the characteristics of the
mind, the good man Nanda, praised by the World-Honored One.

「善关闭根门，　　正念摄心住，饮食知节量，　　觉知诸心相，善男子难陀，　　世尊之所叹。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha had said and joyfully upheld it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
