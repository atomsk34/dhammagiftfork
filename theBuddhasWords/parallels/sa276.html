SĀ 276

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 276（二七六） 难陀说法

Parallel to: MN 146

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, there was a great assembly of bhikṣuṇīs staying in the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. The bhikṣuṇīs were named Cundā, Mṛdā, Māravatī, Pārāśara, Ālavī, Cāma, Nandi, Gijjhakūṭa-gotamī, Utpala-varṇa, Mahā-prajāpatī, and others. They were staying in the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma.

尔时，有如是像类大声闻尼众住舍卫国王园中。比丘尼众其名曰：纯陀比丘尼、民陀比丘尼、摩罗婆比丘尼、波罗遮罗比丘尼、阿罗毘迦比丘尼、差摩比丘尼、难摩比丘尼、吉离舍瞿昙弥比丘尼、优钵罗色比丘尼、摩诃波阇波提比丘尼，此等及余比丘尼住王园中。

At that time, Mahā-prajāpatī, surrounded by five hundred bhikṣuṇīs, came to where the Buddha was, bowed her head at his feet, and sat to one side. The World-Honored One then taught the Dharma to Mahā-prajāpatī, showing, teaching, illuminating, and delighting her. Having taught the Dharma in various ways, showing, teaching, illuminating, and delighting her, he dismissed her, saying, "Bhikṣuṇī! It is time for you to go."

尔时，摩诃波阇波提比丘尼与五百比丘尼前后围遶，来诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面。尔时，世尊为摩诃波阇波提比丘尼说法，示、教、照、喜；种种说法，示、教、照、喜已，发遣令还。言：「比丘尼！应时宜去。」

Mahā-prajāpatī, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and followed it. She bowed and departed.

摩诃波阇波提比丘尼闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing that Mahā-prajāpatī had left, told the bhikṣus, "I am now old and no longer able to teach the bhikṣuṇīs. You bhikṣu saṃgha, today the elders and senior ones should instruct the bhikṣuṇīs."

尔时，世尊知摩诃波阇波提比丘尼去已，告诸比丘：「我年已老迈，不复堪能为诸比丘尼说法，汝等诸比丘僧，今日诸宿德上座，当教授诸比丘尼。」

At that time, the bhikṣus, receiving the World-Honored One's instruction, instructed the bhikṣuṇīs in turn. When it was Nanda's turn, he did not wish to instruct.

时，诸比丘受世尊教，次第教授比丘尼，次至难陀。尔时，难陀次第应至而不欲教授。

At that time, Mahā-prajāpatī, surrounded by five hundred bhikṣuṇīs, went to where the World-Honored One was. She bowed her head at his feet, and after hearing the Dharma, she rejoiced and paid homage before leaving.

尔时，摩诃波阇波提比丘尼与五百比丘尼前后围遶，诣世尊所，稽首礼足，乃至闻法，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing that Mahā-prajāpatī had left, asked Venerable Ānanda, "Who should be next to instruct the bhikṣuṇīs?"

尔时，世尊知摩诃波阇波提比丘尼去已，问尊者阿难：「谁应次至教授诸比丘尼？」

Venerable Ānanda said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! The senior bhikṣus take turns instructing the bhikṣuṇīs. It is now Nanda's turn, but he does not wish to instruct them."

尊者阿难白佛言：「世尊！诸上座次第教授比丘尼，次至难陀，而难陀不欲教授。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Nanda, "You should instruct the bhikṣuṇīs and teach the Dharma to them. Why is that? I myself instruct the bhikṣuṇīs, and you should be the same; I teach the Dharma to the bhikṣuṇīs, and you should be the same."

尔时，世尊告难陀言：「汝当教授诸比丘尼，为诸比丘尼说法。所以者何？我自教授比丘尼，汝亦应尔；我为比丘尼说法，汝亦应尔。」

At that time, Nanda silently accepted the instruction.

尔时，难陀默然受教。

At that time, Nanda, after the night had passed, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, entered the city of Śrāvastī to beg for food. After eating, he returned to the monastery, put away his robe and bowl, washed his feet, entered the room, and sat in meditation. Having arisen from meditation, he put on his saṃghāṭī robe, took a bhikṣu with him, and went to the Jetavana. The bhikṣuṇīs saw Venerable Nanda coming from afar, quickly prepared a seat, and invited him to sit. Venerable Nanda sat down. The bhikṣuṇīs bowed their heads in respect, then sat to one side. Venerable Nanda said to the bhikṣuṇīs, "Sisters! You should ask me. Now I will teach the Dharma for you. If you understand, you should say you understand; if you do not understand, you should say you do not understand. In my explanation of the meaning, if you understand, you should accept and uphold it well; if you do not understand, you should ask again, and I will explain for you."

时，难陀夜过晨朝，著衣持钵，入舍卫城乞食。食已，还精舍，举衣钵，洗足已，入室坐禅。从禅觉，著僧伽梨将一比丘往诣王园，诸比丘尼遥见尊者难陀来，疾敷床座，请令就坐，尊者难陀坐已，诸比丘尼稽首敬礼，退坐一面。尊者难陀语诸比丘尼：「诸姊妹！汝等当问我，今当为汝等说法。汝等解者，当说言解；若不解者，当说不解。于我所说义，若当解者，当善受持；若不解者，汝当更问，当为汝说。」

The bhikṣuṇīs said to Venerable Nanda, "Today we heard the Venerable's instruction, which told us to ask. He said, 'If you do not understand, you should ask now; if you understand, you should say you understand; if you do not understand, you should say you do not understand. In my explanation of the meaning, if you understand, you should accept and uphold it well; if you do not understand, you should ask again, and I will explain for you.' Hearing this, our minds are greatly joyful. We will ask about the meaning we do not understand today."

诸比丘尼白尊者难陀言：「我等今日闻尊者教，令我等问，告我等言：『汝等若未解者，今悉当问；已解者当言解，未解者当言不解。于我所说义，已解者当奉持，未解者当复更问。』我等闻此，心大欢喜，未解义者，今日当问。」

At that time, Venerable Nanda said to the bhikṣuṇīs, "Sisters! Do you observe the internal sense-field of the eye as being the same as, different from, or in a relationship with the self?"

尔时，尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「云何？姊妹！于眼内入处观察，是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

"Do you observe the internal sense-fields of the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind as being the same as, different from, or in a relationship with the self?"

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意内入处观察，是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why is that? Venerable Nanda! We have already truly known and seen this Dharma. We observe the absence of self in the six internal sense-fields. We have already made this understanding: 'The six internal sense-fields are without self.'"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？尊者难陀！我等已曾于此法如实知见，于六内入处观察无我，我等已曾作如是意解：『六内入处无我。』」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent, excellent, sisters! You should understand thus: 'Observe the absence of self in the six internal sense-fields.' Nuns, is the external sense-field of form the same as, different from, or in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！姊妹！应如是解：『六内入处观察无我。』诸比丘尼！色外入处是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

"Are the external sense-fields of sound, smell, taste, touch, and dharmas the same as, different from, or in a relationship with the self?"

「声、香、味、触、法外入处，是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why is that? Venerable Nanda! I have already thoroughly observed the six external sense-fields as selfless. I always understand in this way: the six external sense-fields are truly selfless."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？尊者难陀！我已曾于六外入处如实观察无我，我常作此意解，六外入处如实无我。」

Venerable Nanda praised the nuns, saying, "Excellent! Excellent! You should view this meaning in this way: 'The six external sense-fields are selfless.' If eye and form give rise to eye consciousness, is that eye consciousness the same as, different from, or in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀赞诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！汝于此义应如是观：『六外入处无我。』若缘眼、色，生眼识，彼眼识是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

"Are ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind dharmas the same as, different from, or in a relationship with the self?"

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，彼意识是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? I have already thoroughly observed the six sense bases as selfless, and I always understand them to be truly selfless."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我已曾于此六识身如实观察无我，我亦常作是意解，六识身如实无我。」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent, excellent, sisters! You should observe the meaning in this way: 'The six sense bases are truly selfless.' Do the eye, form, and eye consciousness that arise from these three things being in contact with each other belong to the self, are they different from the self, or are they in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！姊妹！汝于此义应如是观察：『六识身如实无我。』缘眼、色，生眼识，三事和合生触，彼触是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

"Do the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, and the dharmas that arise from these, belong to the self, are they different from the self, or are they in a relationship with the self?"

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，三事和合生触，彼触是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why is that? I have already observed the six contacts as they truly are, without a self. I also always understand in this way, that the six contacts are truly without a self."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我已曾于此六触观察如实无我，我亦常如是意解，六触如实无我。」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent! Excellent! You should observe in reality that the six contact bodies are truly without a self. The eye and forms give rise to eye contact, and the three things coming together is contact. Contact gives rise to feeling. Is that contact that gives rise to feeling the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！当如实观察：『于六触身如实无我。』缘眼、色，生眼触，三事和合触，触缘受，彼触缘受是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

"The ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind and dharmas give rise to mental consciousness. The three things coming together is contact. Contact gives rise to feeling. Is that feeling the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?"

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，三事和合触，触缘受，彼受是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? We have previously truly observed the six sense bodies as selfless. We also always understand that the six sense bodies are truly selfless."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我等曾于此六受身如实观察无我，我亦常作此意解，六受身如实无我。」

Venerable Nanda told the nuns, "Excellent! Excellent! You should observe this meaning like this: 'The six sense bodies are truly selfless.' The eye and form give rise to eye consciousness. The three things coming together give rise to contact. Contact gives rise to thought. Is that thought the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！汝于此义应如是观察：『此六受身如实无我。』缘眼、色，生眼识，三事和合生触，触缘想，彼想是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

'The ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind and dharmas give rise to mental consciousness. The three things coming together give rise to contact. Contact gives rise to thought. Is that thought the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?'

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，三事和合生触，触缘想，彼想是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why is that? I have truly observed the six sense bases as being without self. I also always understand that the six sense bases are truly without self."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我曾于此六想身如实观察无我，我亦常作此意解，六想身如实无我。」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent! Excellent! Nuns, you should observe this meaning like this: 'The six sense bases are truly without self.' The eye and forms give rise to eye consciousness. The three things coming together give rise to contact. Contact gives rise to thought. Is that thought the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！比丘尼！汝于此义应如是观察：『此六想身如实无我。』缘眼、色，生眼识，三事和合触，触缘思，彼思是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

'The ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind and dharmas give rise to mental consciousness. The three things coming together give rise to contact. Contact gives rise to thought. Is that thought the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?'

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，三事和合触，触缘思，彼思是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? I have truly observed the six kinds of thought as without self. I always understand this: 'The six kinds of thought are truly without self.'"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我曾于此六思身如实观察无我，我常作此意解：『此六思身如实无我。』」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent! Excellent! Nuns! You should observe this meaning like this: 'The six kinds of thought are truly without self.' The eye and forms give rise to eye consciousness. The three things coming together give rise to contact. Contact gives rise to craving. Is that craving the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！比丘尼！汝于此义应如是观察：『此六思身如实无我。』缘眼、色，生眼识，三事和合触，触缘爱，彼爱是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda!"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！」

'The ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind and dharmas give rise to mental consciousness. The three things coming together give rise to contact. Contact gives rise to craving. Is that craving the self, different from the self, or in a relationship with the self?'

「耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，三事和合触，触缘爱，彼爱是我、异我、相在不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? I have truly observed the six sense bases of craving as being without self. I always understand this: 'The six sense bases of craving are truly without self.'"

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我曾于此六爱身如实观察无我，我常作此意解：『此六爱身如实无我。』」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "You should observe the meaning of this thus: 'The six sense bases of craving are truly without self.' Sisters! It is like a lamp that burns due to oil, a wick, and a flame. The oil is impermanent, the wick is impermanent, the flame is impermanent, and the vessel is impermanent. If someone were to say, 'Without oil, wick, flame, or vessel, the light of the lamp that arises depends on something that is permanent, constant, abiding, and unchanging,' would that be an equal statement?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「汝于此义应如是观察：『此六爱身如实无我。』姊妹！譬因膏油、因炷，灯明得然；彼油无常，炷亦无常，火亦无常，器亦无常。若有作是言：『无油、无炷、无火、无器，而所依起灯光，常、恒、住、不变易。』作是说者，为等说不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? The lamp burns depending on oil, wick, and vessel. The oil, wick, and vessel are all impermanent. If there is no oil, no wick, and no vessel, the light of the lamp that arises also ceases, subsides, disappears, cools, and becomes true.

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？缘油、炷、器然灯，彼油、炷、器悉无常；若无油、无炷、无器，所依灯光亦复随灭、息、没、清凉、真实。」

"In the same way, sisters! These six internal sense bases are impermanent. If someone were to say, 'The joy and pleasure that arise from these six internal sense bases are permanent, constant, abiding, unchanging, and peaceful,' would that be an equal statement?"

「如是，姊妹！此六内入处无常。若有说言：『此六内入处因缘生喜乐，常、恒、住、不变易、安隐。』是为等说不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? We have directly observed that such and such phenomena arise from such and such conditions. When the conditions for those phenomena cease, those phenomena also cease, subside, disappear, cool, and become true.

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我等曾如实观察，彼彼法缘生彼彼法；彼彼缘法灭，彼彼生法亦复随灭、息、没、清凉、真实。」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent! Excellent! Nuns! You should observe this meaning like this: 'Such and such phenomena arise from such and such conditions. When the conditions for those phenomena cease, those phenomena also cease, subside, disappear, become peaceful, cool, and true.' Sisters! It is like a large tree with roots, trunk, branches, and leaves. The roots are also impermanent, the trunk, branches, and leaves are all impermanent. If someone were to say, 'There are no roots, trunk, branches, or leaves of that tree, but only its shadow is permanent, constant, abiding, unchanging, and peaceful,' would that be an equal statement?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！比丘尼！汝于此义应如是观察：『彼彼法缘生彼彼法，彼彼法缘灭，彼彼生法亦复随灭、息、没、寂静、清凉、真实。』诸姊妹！譬如大树根、茎、枝、叶，根亦无常，茎、枝、叶皆悉无常。若有说言：『无彼树根、茎、枝、叶，唯有其影常、恒、住、不变易、安隐者，为等说不？』」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? Just as that large tree with roots, trunk, branches, and leaves, its roots are also impermanent, the trunk, branches, and leaves are also impermanent. Without roots, trunk, branches, or leaves, the tree shadow that is relied upon is completely nonexistent."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？如彼大树根、茎、枝、叶，彼根亦无常，茎、枝、叶亦复无常，无根、无茎、无枝、无叶，所依树影，一切悉无。」

"Sisters! If the external six sense bases are impermanent, and if it is said that the external six sense bases give rise to joy and pleasure, are permanent, abiding, unchanging, and peaceful, is this an equal statement?"

「诸姊妹！若缘外六入处无常，若言外六入处因缘生喜乐，恒、住、不变易、安隐者，此为等说不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? I have directly observed the meaning of this: those dharmas arise in dependence on those dharmas, and those dharmas cease in dependence on those dharmas. Those dharmas that arise in dependence also cease, subside, disappear, become tranquil, cool, and true."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？我曾于此义如实观察，彼彼法缘生彼彼法，彼彼法缘灭；彼彼生法亦复随灭、息、没、寂静、清凉、真实。」

Venerable Nanda said to the nuns, "Excellent! Excellent! Sisters! You should directly observe the meaning of this: 'Those dharmas arise in dependence on those dharmas, and those dharmas cease in dependence on those dharmas. Those dharmas that arise in dependence also cease, subside, disappear, become tranquil, cool, and true.' Sisters! Listen to my analogy, for the wise understand through analogies. It is like a skilled butcher or butcher's apprentice holding a sharp knife, skillfully flaying a cow, flaying it in between, not injuring the inner flesh or the outer skin, separating the joints, tendons, and bones, and then covering it back with the skin. If someone were to say, 'This cow's skin and flesh are complete and not separated,' would that be an equal statement?"

尊者难陀告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！姊妹！汝于此义当如实观察：『彼彼法缘生彼彼法，彼彼法缘灭，彼彼生法亦复随灭、息、没、寂灭、清凉、真实。』诸姊妹！听我说譬，夫智者因譬得解。譬如善屠牛师、屠牛弟子手执利刀，解剥其牛，乘间而剥，不伤内肉、不伤外皮，解其枝节筋骨，然后还以皮覆其上。若有人言：『此牛皮肉全而不离。』为等说不？」

They replied, "No, Venerable Nanda! Why? That skilled butcher or butcher's apprentice, holding a sharp knife, skillfully flayed the cow in between, not injuring the skin or flesh, and completely severed the joints, tendons, and bones, and then covered it back with the skin. The skin and flesh are already separated, not not separated."

答言：「不也，尊者难陀！所以者何？彼善屠牛师、屠牛弟子手执利刀，乘间而剥，不伤皮肉，枝节筋骨悉皆断截，还以皮复上，皮肉已离，非不离也。」

"Sisters! I have given the analogy, now I will explain the meaning. The cow is an analogy for the coarse form of the human body..." It is explained in detail in the Basket of Poisonous Snakes Sūtra.

「姊妹！我说所譬，今当说义。牛者譬人身麁色……」如箧毒蛇经广说。

"The flesh refers to the six internal sense bases, the outer skin refers to the six external sense bases, the butcher refers to those who study the traces of the teachings, the tendons and bones between the skin and flesh refer to the joy and greed together, the sharp knife refers to sharp wisdom. The well-versed noble disciples use the sharp knife of wisdom to sever all bonds, fetters, afflictions, upper afflictions, and entanglements. Therefore, sisters! You should train like this: 'In regard to the objects of pleasure, the mind should not be attached, in order to eliminate greed; in regard to the objects of anger, one should not give rise to anger, in order to eliminate anger; in regard to the objects of delusion, one should not give rise to delusion, in order to eliminate delusion. One should observe the arising and ceasing of the five aggregates of clinging; one should observe the arising and ceasing of the six sense bases; one should well focus the mind on the four foundations of mindfulness. Abiding in the seven factors of awakening, having cultivated the seven factors of awakening, the mind does not become attached to the desire-based impurities, and the mind is liberated; the mind does not become attached to the existence-based impurities, and the mind is liberated; the mind does not become attached to the ignorance-based impurities, and the mind is liberated.' Sisters! You should train like this."

「肉者谓内六入处，外皮者谓外六入处，屠牛者谓学见迹，皮肉中间筋骨者谓贪喜俱，利刀者谓利智慧。多闻圣弟子以智慧利刀断截一切结、缚、使、烦恼、上烦恼、缠。是故，诸姊妹！当如是学：『于所可乐法，心不应著，断除贪故；所可瞋法，不应生瞋，断除瞋故；所可痴法，不应生痴，断除痴故。于五受阴，当观生灭；于六触入处，当观集灭；于四念处，当善系心。住七觉分，修七觉分已，于其欲漏，心不缘著，心得解脱；于其有漏，心不缘著，心得解脱；于无明漏，心不缘著，心得解脱。』诸姊妹！当如是学。」

At that time, Venerable Nanda gave a Dharma teaching to the bhikṣuṇīs, showing, teaching, illuminating, and delighting them. Having shown, taught, illuminated, and delighted them, he rose from his seat and departed. At that time, Mahāprajāpatī bhikṣuṇī, surrounded by a retinue of five hundred bhikṣuṇīs, went to where the Buddha was, bowed her head at his feet, and stood to one side. She then paid homage to the Buddha and departed.

尔时，尊者难陀为诸比丘尼说法，示、教、照、喜；示、教、照、喜已，从座起去。时，摩诃波阇波提比丘尼与五百比丘尼眷属围遶，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，退住一面，乃至为佛作礼而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing that Mahāprajāpatī bhikṣuṇī had left, told the bhikṣus: "It is like the moon on the night of the fourteenth day. Many people look at the moon, wondering if it is full or not yet full. Know that the moon is not yet fully complete. In the same way, the good man Nanda is giving proper instruction and teaching the Dharma to the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs, but their liberation is not yet complete. However, when these bhikṣuṇīs die, they will not see a single fetter that is not severed, causing them to be reborn in this world again."

尔时，世尊知摩诃波阇波提比丘尼去已，告诸比丘：「譬如明月十四日夜，多众观月，为是满耶？为未满耶？当知彼月未究竟满。如是，善男子难陀为五百比丘尼正教授、正说法，于其解脱犹未究竟。然此等比丘尼命终之时，不见一结不断，能使彼还生于此世。」

At that time, the World-Honored One again told Nanda: "Speak the Dharma for the bhikṣuṇīs again."

尔时，世尊复告难陀：「更为诸比丘尼说法。」

At that time, Venerable Nanda silently accepted the instruction. After the night passed, he took his bowl and entered the city to beg for food. After eating, he went to the royal garden, took his seat, and spoke the Dharma for the bhikṣuṇīs, showing, teaching, illuminating, and delighting them. Having shown, taught, illuminated, and delighted them, he rose from his seat and left. Mahāprajāpatī bhikṣuṇī, at another time, surrounded by five hundred bhikṣuṇīs, went to where the Buddha was, bowed her head at his feet, and departed after making prostrations.

尔时，尊者难陀默然奉教，夜过晨朝，持钵入城乞食，食已，乃至往诣王园，就座而坐，为诸比丘尼说法，示、教、照、喜；示、教、照、喜已，从座起去。摩诃波阇波提比丘尼复于异时与五百比丘尼前后围遶，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，乃至作礼而去。

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing that Mahāprajāpatī bhikṣuṇī had left, told the bhikṣus: "It is like the fifteenth night of the bright moon. No one doubts whether the moon is full or not. It is completely full. In the same way, the good man Nanda spoke such correct instructions for the bhikṣuṇīs, leading to ultimate liberation. If at the time of death, there is no one who says where their path leads, this should be known as the end of suffering." This is the World-Honored One's prediction of the first fruit for the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs.

尔时，世尊知摩诃波阇波提比丘尼去已，告诸比丘：「譬如明月十五日夜，无有人疑月满不满者，然其彼月究竟圆满。如是，善男子难陀为诸比丘尼说如是正教授，究竟解脱；若命终时，无有说彼道路所趣，此当知即是苦边。」是为世尊为五百比丘尼受第一果记。

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
