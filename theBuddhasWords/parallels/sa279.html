SĀ 279

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 279（二七九） 调伏

Parallel to: SN 35.94

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "If these six faculties are not disciplined, not closed, not guarded, not held, and not cultivated, one will definitely experience suffering in the future.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「于此六根不调伏、不关闭、不守护、不执持、不修习，于未来世必受苦报。

What are the six faculties? If the eye faculty is not disciplined, not closed, not guarded, not cultivated, and not held, one will definitely experience suffering in the future.

「何等为六根？眼根不调伏、不关闭、不守护、不修习、不执持，于未来世必受苦报；耳、鼻、舌、身、意根亦复如是。愚痴无闻凡夫眼根见色，执受相，执受随形好，任彼眼根趣向，不律仪执受，住世间贪、爱、恶不善法，以漏其心，此等不能执持律仪，防护眼根。耳、鼻、舌、身、意根，亦复如是。如是于六根不调伏、不关闭、不守护、不执持、不修习，于未来世必受苦报。

How are the six faculties well disciplined, well closed, well guarded, well held, and well cultivated, so that one will definitely experience happiness in the future? The noble disciple with much learning, when the eye faculty sees forms, does not grasp their characteristics or details, but allows the eye faculty to pursue them. They always abide in the precepts, and worldly greed, love, and unwholesome dharmas do not defile their minds. They are able to generate the precepts and well guard the eye faculty. The same is true for the faculties of the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. Thus, if the six faculties are well disciplined, well closed, well guarded, well held, and well cultivated, one will definitely experience happiness in the future.

「云何六根善调伏、善关闭、善守护、善执持、善修习，于未来世必受乐报？多闻圣弟子眼见色，不取色相，不取随形好，任其眼根之所趣向，常住律仪，世间贪、爱、恶不善法不漏其心，能生律仪，善护眼根。耳、鼻、舌、身、意根，亦复如是。如是六根善调伏、善关闭、善守护、善执持、善修习，于未来世必受乐报。」

Then he spoke a verse, saying:

即说偈言：

Those who dwell in the unwholesome discipline of the six sense
bases, those bhikṣus will experience great suffering for a long time
They should always diligently cultivate the wholesome discipline, with right faith
and non-duality, with their taints not leaking out. When the eye
sees forms, whether agreeable or disagreeable, they do not give rise
to desire for the agreeable, nor aversion for the disagreeable. When
the ear hears various sounds, whether remembered or not, they do
not delight in the remembered, nor give rise to evil in
the un-remembered. When the nose smells fragrant or foul objects, they
have an equal mind towards fragrance and foulness, without desire or
aversion. When tasting various flavors, they also have the agreeable and
disagreeable, but do not give rise to greed for the agreeable
nor reject the disagreeable. When touched by pleasant touch, they do
not give rise to carelessness. When touched by painful touch, they
do not give rise to evil thoughts. Equanimously abandoning pleasure and
pain, they extinguish the unextinguished. The mind observes those various characteristics
falsely discriminating and causing desire and greed to increase. Awakening to
those evils, they abide in a mind free from desire. Well-restraining
these six faculties, the six sense objects do not move. Subduing
the hosts of Māra, they cross to the other shore of
birth and death.

「于六触入处，　　住于不律仪，是等诸比丘，　　长夜受大苦。斯等于律仪，　　常当勤修习，正信心不二，　　诸漏不漏心。眼见于彼色，　　可意不可意，可意不生欲，　　不可不憎恶。耳闻彼诸声，　　亦有念不念，于念不乐著，　　不念不起恶。鼻根之所嚊，　　若香若臭物，等心于香臭，　　无欲亦无违。所食于众味，　　彼亦有美恶，美味不起贪，　　恶味亦不择。乐触以触身，　　不生于放逸，为苦触所触，　　不生过恶想。平等舍苦乐，　　不灭者令灭，心意所观察，　　彼种彼种相。虚伪而分别，　　欲贪转增广，觉悟彼诸恶，　　安住离欲心。善摄此六根，　　六境触不动，摧伏众魔怨，　　度生死彼岸。」

The Buddha spoke this sūtra. The bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them with respect.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
