SĀ 280

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 280（二八〇） 频头城

Parallel to: MN 150

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the World-Honored One was traveling among the people of Kosala and arrived at the north of the city of Benares, at the Deer Park in the Sītavana.

一时，世尊在拘萨罗国人间游行，到频头城北申恕林中。

At that time, the brahmins and elders of the city of Benares all heard that the World-Honored One was traveling among the people of Kosala and staying at the Deer Park in the Sītavana. Having heard this, they all came out of the city, went to the Deer Park, approached the World-Honored One, bowed their heads at his feet, and sat to one side.

尔时，频头城中，婆罗门长者皆闻世尊于拘萨罗国人间游行，住频头城申恕林中。闻已，悉共出城，至申恕林，诣世尊所，稽首礼足，退坐一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the brahmins and elders of the city of Benares: "If someone asks you, 'What kind of śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas should not be respected, revered, honored, and offered to?' you should answer, 'If a śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa sees forms with the eye, is not free from desire, not free from passion, not free from attachment, not free from thirst, not free from recollection, and the mind is not internally tranquil, their practice is unwholesome, their practice is rough and harsh. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas. Such a bhikṣu should not be respected, revered, honored, and offered to.'

尔时，世尊告频头城婆罗门长者：「若人问汝言：『何等像类沙门、婆罗门不应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养？』汝当答言：『若沙门、婆罗门眼见色，未离贪、未离欲、未离爱、未离渴、未离念，内心不寂静，所行非法，所行踈澁行。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦复如是。如是像类比丘，不应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养。』

Having spoken thus, you should further ask, 'Why should such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas not be respected, revered, honored, and offered to?' You should answer, 'We see forms with the eye, and are not free from desire, not free from passion, not free from attachment, not free from thirst, not free from recollection, and the mind is not internally tranquil. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas. Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas see forms with the eye, and are not free from desire, not free from passion, not free from attachment, not free from thirst, not free from recollection, and the mind is not internally tranquil. Their practice is unwholesome, their practice is rough and harsh. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas. I seek their distinctions, but do not see any distinct practices. Therefore, I should not respect, revere, honor, and offer to such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas.'

「作是说已，当复问言：『何故如此像类沙门、婆罗门，不应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养？』汝应答言：『我等眼见色，不离欲、不离爱、不离渴、不离念，内心不寂静。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦复如是。彼沙门、婆罗门眼见色，亦不离贪、不离欲、不离爱、不离渴、不离念，内心不寂静，行非法，行踈澁行。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦复如是。我于斯等求其差别，不见差别之行。是故我于斯等像类沙门、婆罗门不应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养。』

If they further ask, 'What kind of śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas should be respected, revered, honored, and offered to?' you should answer, 'If they see forms with the eye, are free from desire, free from passion, free from attachment, free from thirst, free from recollection, and the mind is internally tranquil. Their practice is wholesome, their practice is equal, not rough and harsh. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas. Such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas should be respected, revered, honored, and offered to.'

「若复问言：『何等像类沙门、婆罗门所应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养？』汝应答言：『若彼眼见色，离贪、离欲、离爱、离渴、离念，内心寂静，不行非法行，行等行，不踈澁行。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦复如是。如是像类沙门、婆罗门所应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养。』

If they further ask, 'Why should such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas be respected, revered, honored, and offered to?' you should answer, 'We see forms with the eye, and are not free from desire, not free from passion, not free from attachment, not free from thirst, not free from recollection, and the mind is not internally tranquil. Our practice is unwholesome, our practice is rough and harsh. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas.' Such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are free from desire, free from passion, free from attachment, free from thirst, free from recollection, and the mind is internally tranquil. Their practice is wholesome, their practice is equal, not rough and harsh. The same is true for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas. We seek their distinctions, and see their distinct practices. Therefore, we should respect, revere, honor, and offer to such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas.

「若复问言：『何故于此像类沙门、婆罗门恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养？』汝应答言：『我等眼见色，不离贪、不离欲、不离爱、不离渴、不离念，内心不寂静，行非法行，行踈澁行。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦复如是。』斯等像类沙门、婆罗门离贪、离欲、离渴、离念，内心寂静，行如法行，不行踈澁行。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦复如是。我等于彼，求其差别，见差别故，于彼像类沙门、婆罗门所应恭敬、尊重、礼事、供养。

"Having spoken thus, if they further ask, 'What are the practices, appearances, and characteristics of those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas? Do you know that those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are free from desire, heading towards taming desire, free from anger, heading towards taming anger, free from delusion, heading towards taming delusion?' you should answer, 'We see those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas with such appearances and characteristics, dwelling in secluded places, forests, under trees, on low beds and straw mats, practicing seclusion, away from women, enjoying solitude, together with those who practice meditation. If there are no forms seen by the eye that can give rise to attachment, no sounds heard by the ear, smells smelled by the nose, tastes tasted by the tongue, or tangibles felt by the body that can give rise to attachment, then those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have such practices, appearances, and characteristics, which enable us to know that those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are free from desire, heading towards taming desire, free from anger, heading towards taming anger, free from delusion, heading towards taming delusion.'"

「如是说已，若复问言：『彼沙门、婆罗门有何行] cf.【丽】？有何形貌？有何相？汝等知是沙门、婆罗门离贪、向调伏贪，离恚、向调伏恚，离痴、向调伏痴？』汝应答言：『我见彼沙门、婆罗门有如是像类，在空闲处、林中树下、卑床草蓐，修行远离，离诸女人，近乐独人，同禅思者；若于彼处，无眼见色可生乐著，无耳声、鼻香、舌味、身触可生乐著，若彼沙门、婆罗门有如是行、如是形貌、如是相，令我等知是沙门，婆罗门离贪、向调伏贪，离恚、向调伏恚，离痴、向调伏痴。』」

At that time, the śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and elders addressed the Buddha, saying, "How wonderful, World-Honored One! You do not praise yourself or disparage others, but speak the truth. Each one can discern the defilement and purity of the sense bases, and extensively explain dependent origination, just as it is taught by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. It is like a person who can rescue those who are drowning, open the doors for those who are closed in, show the way for those who are lost, and light a lamp in the dark. The World-Honored One is also like this. You do not praise yourself or disparage others, but speak the truth, up to just as it is taught by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One."

时，诸沙门、婆罗门长者白佛言：「奇哉！世尊！不自誉、不毁他，正说其义，各各自于诸入处，分别染污清净，广说缘起，如如来、应、等正觉说。譬如士夫，溺者能救，闭者能开，迷者示路，暗处然灯。世尊亦复如是。不自誉、不毁他，正说其义，乃至如如来、应、等正觉说。」

At that time, the brāhmaṇas and elders of Pāṭaliputra, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and made prostrations before departing.

尔时，频头城婆罗门长者闻佛所说，欢喜作礼而去。
