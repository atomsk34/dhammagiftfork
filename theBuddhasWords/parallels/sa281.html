SĀ 281

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 281（二八一） 萦 发目犍连

Parallel to: SN 46.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, the ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa, having gone forth, came to where the Buddha was. They exchanged greetings and, after the greetings and polite conversation were over, he sat to one side.

时，有萦发目揵连出家来诣佛所，共相问讯，问讯已，退坐一面。

Then the World-Honored One asked the ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa, "Where are you coming from?"

尔时，世尊告萦发目揵连：「汝从何来？」

The ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "I have come from the assembly of many different non-Buddhist ascetics, brahmins, and Shakyas who have gone forth, from listening to the Dharma in the assembly hall, from that forest."

萦发目揵连白佛言：「我从彼众多种种异道沙门、婆罗门、遮罗迦出家，集会未曾讲堂听法，从彼林来。」

The Buddha said to the ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa, "For what merit and power have you come to listen to the Dharma from the assembly of many different non-Buddhist ascetics, brahmins, and Shakyas who have gone forth?"

佛告萦发目揵连：「汝为何等福力故，从彼众多种种异道沙门、婆罗门、遮罗迦出家所听其说法？」

The ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa said, "I have come to listen to their debates and arguments for the sake of their benefits."

萦发目揵连言：「我试听其竞胜论义福利，听其相违反论议福利故。」

The Buddha said to Kāśyapa, "For a long time, the various non-Buddhist ascetics, brahmins, and Shakyas who have gone forth have debated and argued for the sake of their benefits, destroying each other."

佛告目揵连：「长夜久远，种种异道沙门、婆罗门、遮罗迦出家竞胜论议，相违反论议福利，迭相破坏。」

The ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "Gautama, what kind of Dharma do you teach your disciples for their benefits, so that they can teach others without slandering the Tathāgata, without increasing or decreasing, speaking truthfully, speaking the Dharma, speaking the Dharma in accordance with the Dharma, without others being able to compare, question, or rebuke?"

萦发目揵连白佛言：「瞿昙！为诸弟子说何等法福利，令彼转为人说，不谤如来，不增不减，诚说、法说、法次法说，无有余人能来比挍、难诘、诃责？」

The Buddha said to Kāśyapa, "The benefits of the fruit of wisdom and liberation, when taught to others, do not slander the Tathāgata, do not contradict the truth, are in accordance with the Dharma, and cannot be compared, questioned, or criticized by others."

佛告目连：「明、解脱果报福利，为人转说者，不谤如来，不乖其理，法次法说，无有能来比挍、难诘、嫌责。」

The ascetic Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "Gautama, do your disciples have any Dharma that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the benefits of wisdom and liberation?"

萦发目揵连白佛言：「瞿昙！诸弟子有法，修习多修习，令明、解脱福利满足者不？」

The Buddha said to Uruvela-Kāśyapa, "There are seven factors of awakening that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the benefits of wisdom and liberation."

佛告萦发目犍连：「有七觉分，修习多修习，明、解脱福利满足。」

Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "Is there any Dharma that, when cultivated, can fulfill the seven factors of awakening?"

萦发目揵连白佛言：「有法修习，能令七觉分满足不？」

The Buddha said to Uruvela-Kāśyapa, "There are four foundations of mindfulness that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the seven factors of awakening."

佛告萦发目揵连：「有四念处，修习多修习，能令七觉分满足。」

Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "Is there any Dharma that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the four foundations of mindfulness?"

萦发目揵连白佛：「复有法修习多修习，令四念处满足不？」

The Buddha said to Uruvela-Kāśyapa, "There are three kinds of excellent conduct that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the four foundations of mindfulness."

佛告萦发目揵连：「有三妙行，修习多修习，能令四念处满足。」

Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "Is there any Dharma that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the three kinds of excellent conduct?"

萦发目揵连白佛言：「复有法修习多修习，令三妙行满足不？」

The Buddha said to Kāśyapa, "There is the discipline of the six sense bases that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the three kinds of excellent conduct."

佛告目揵连：「有六触入处律仪，修习多修习，令三妙行满足。」

Uruvela-Kāśyapa said to the Buddha, "What is the discipline of the six sense bases that, when cultivated and practiced extensively, can fulfill the three kinds of excellent conduct?"

萦发目揵连白佛言：「云何六触入处律仪，修习多修习，令三妙行满足？」

The Buddha said to Kāśyapa, "If a monk sees a form that is pleasing, lovable, and can arouse desire and attachment, he should not be delighted, praise it, be attached to it, or dwell on it. If he sees a form that is displeasing, unlovable, and leads to painful feelings, he should not be afraid, dislike it, hate it, or be angry. He should not be attached to pleasant forms when he sees them, nor be attached to unpleasant forms when he sees them. His mind should be well-established, immovable, well-cultivated in liberation, and not weary. The same applies to the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. By cultivating and practicing extensively in this way, one can fulfill the three kinds of excellent conduct."

佛告目揵连：「若眼见适意、可爱念、能长养欲乐、令人缘著之色，彼比丘见已，不喜、不赞叹、不缘、不著、不住；若眼见不适意、不可爱念、顺于苦觉之色，诸比丘见已，不畏、不恶、不嫌、不恚。于彼好色，起眼见已，永不缘著；不好色，起眼见已，永不缘著；内心安住不动，善修解脱，心不懈勌。耳、鼻、舌、身、意识法亦复如是。如是于六触入修习多修习，满足三妙行。

How does cultivating the three kinds of excellent conduct lead to the fulfillment of the four foundations of mindfulness? The learned noble disciples, in secluded places, forests, and under trees, study and contemplate thus: 'This body of unwholesome conduct will definitely bring unwholesome consequences in this life and the next. If I engage in unwholesome bodily conduct, I will surely give rise to self-reproach, others will also despise me, the teacher will also rebuke me, and the virtuous practitioners will also reproach me according to the Dharma; a bad reputation will spread in all directions, and when the body perishes and life ends, I will fall into hell. Seeing the consequences of unwholesome bodily conduct in this life and the next, I will therefore abandon unwholesome bodily conduct and cultivate wholesome bodily conduct. The same applies to unwholesome verbal and mental conduct.' This is called the fulfillment of the four foundations of mindfulness through cultivating the three kinds of excellent conduct.

「云何修三妙行，满足四念处？多闻圣弟子于空闲处、林中、树下，作如是学、如是思惟：『此身恶行，现世、后世必得恶报。我若行身恶行者，必当自生厌悔，他亦嫌薄，大师亦责，诸梵行者亦复以法而嫌我；恶名流布，遍于诸方，身坏命终，当堕地狱。于身恶行，见现世、后世如是果报，是故除身恶行，修身妙行，口、意恶行亦复如是。』是名修习三妙行已，得四念处清净满足。

How does cultivating the four foundations of mindfulness lead to the fulfillment of the seven factors of awakening? Moggallāna, when a bhikṣu abides in the contemplation of the body in accordance with the body, while abiding in the contemplation of the body in accordance with the body, he recollects and abides in peace without forgetting. At that time, he practices the factor of awakening of mindfulness, and having practiced the factor of awakening of mindfulness, he fulfills the factor of awakening of mindfulness. He discerns and contemplates the dharmas in his mind. At that time, he practices the factor of awakening of investigation of dharmas, and having practiced the factor of awakening of investigation of dharmas, he fulfills the factor of awakening of investigation of dharmas. He discerns those dharmas and contemplates them. He practices the factor of awakening of energy, and having practiced the factor of awakening of energy, he fulfills the factor of awakening of energy. Having exerted energy, he gives rise to a joyful mind. At that time, he practices the factor of awakening of joy, and having practiced the factor of awakening of joy, he fulfills the factor of awakening of joy. Having a joyful mind, his body and mind rest. At that time, he practices the factor of awakening of tranquility, and having practiced the factor of awakening of tranquility, he fulfills the factor of awakening of tranquility. Having tranquility of body and mind, he attains samādhi. At that time, he practices the factor of awakening of concentration, and having practiced the factor of awakening of concentration, he fulfills the factor of awakening of concentration. He unifies his mind, and the afflictions of greed and worry cease. He practices the factor of awakening of equanimity, and having practiced the factor of awakening of equanimity, he fulfills the factor of awakening of equanimity. The same applies to the foundations of mindfulness of feeling, mind, and dharmas. Thus, cultivating the four foundations of mindfulness leads to the fulfillment of the seven factors of awakening.

「云何修四念处？得七觉分满足？目揵连比丘！如是顺身身观住，彼顺身身观住时，摄念安住不忘。尔时，方便修习念觉分，方便修习念觉分已，得念觉分满足，于彼心念选择于法，觉想思量。尔时，方便修习择法觉分，方便修习择法觉分已，逮得择法觉分满足，选择彼法，觉想思量。方便修习精进觉分，方便修习精进觉分已，逮得精进觉分满足，勤精进已，生欢喜心。尔时，修习方便欢喜觉分，修习欢喜觉分已，逮得欢喜觉分满足，心欢喜已，身心止息。尔时，修习猗息觉分，修习猗息觉分已，逮得猗息觉分满足，身心息已，得三摩提。尔时，修习定觉分，修习定觉分已，定觉分满足，谓一其心，贪忧灭息，内身行舍，方便修习舍觉分，方便修习舍觉分已，逮得舍觉分清净满足。受、心、法念处，亦如是说。如是修习四念处、七觉分满足。

How does cultivating the seven factors of awakening lead to the fulfillment of wisdom and liberation? Moggallāna, if a bhikṣu cultivates the factor of awakening of mindfulness, relying on seclusion, relying on detachment, relying on cessation, and relinquishment, he cultivates the factor of awakening of mindfulness, and attains the fulfillment of wisdom and liberation through purity. The same applies to cultivating the factors of awakening of investigation of dharmas, energy, joy, tranquility, concentration, and equanimity. This is called the fulfillment of wisdom and liberation through purity through cultivating the seven factors of awakening. Thus, Moggallāna, the Dharma is the ferry, from this shore to the other shore.

「云何修习七觉分？明、解脱满足？目揵连！若比丘修念觉分，依远离、依离欲、依灭舍，于进趣修念觉分，逮得明、解脱清净满足。乃至修习舍觉分，亦如是说，是名修习七觉分已，明、解脱清净满足。如是，目揵连！法法相律，从此岸而到彼岸。」

When this teaching was spoken, the matted-hair ascetic Moggallāna was free from defilements and attained the purity of the Dharma-eye. At that time, the matted-hair ascetic Moggallāna saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, understood the Dharma, entered the Dharma, transcended all doubts, and was not dependent on others. He attained fearlessness in the teachings and discipline, and from his seat he rose, arranged his robes, bowed to the Buddha, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "May I now be allowed to go forth in the true Dharma and Vinaya and receive the full ordination of a bhikṣu?"

说是法时，萦发目揵连远尘离垢，得法眼净。时，萦发目揵连见法、得法、知法、入法，度诸疑惑，不由于他，于诸法、律得无所畏。从座起，整衣服，为佛作礼，合掌白佛言：「我今宁得于正法、律出家，得具足比丘分不？」

The Buddha told Moggallāna, "You are now allowed to go forth in the true Dharma and Vinaya, and receive the full ordination of a bhikṣu. Having gone forth, you should diligently contemplate and abide without negligence, until you attain arahantship.

佛告目揵连：「汝今已得于正法、律出家，具足得比丘分，得出家已，专精思惟，不放逸住，乃至成阿罗汉。」
