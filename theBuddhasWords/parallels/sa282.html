SĀ 282

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 282（二八二） 诸根修

Parallel to: MN 152

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying in the Kalingavana near Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住迦微伽罗牟真隣陀林中。

At that time there was a young man named Uttara, a disciple of Parāśara, who came to where the Buddha was, paid his respects, and then sat to one side.

时，有年少名欝多罗，是波罗奢那弟子，来诣佛所，恭敬问讯已，退坐一面。

At that time the World-Honored One said to Uttara, "Does your teacher Parāśara teach you about cultivating the faculties?"

尔时，世尊告欝多罗：「汝师波罗奢那为汝等说修诸根不？」

Uttara said, "He has taught me, Gautama!"

欝多罗言：「说已，瞿昙！」

The Buddha said to Uttara, "How does your teacher Parāśara teach about cultivating the faculties?"

佛告欝多罗：「汝师波罗奢那云何说修诸根？」

Uttara said to the Buddha, "My teacher Parāśara says that not seeing forms with the eyes and not hearing sounds with the ears is called cultivating the faculties."

欝多罗白佛言：「我师波罗奢那说，眼不见色，耳不听声，是名修根。」

The Buddha said to Uttara, "If it is as your teacher Parāśara says, are the blind cultivating the faculties? Why is that? It is like only the blind do not see forms with the eyes."

佛告欝多罗：「若如汝波罗奢那说，盲者是修根不？所以者何？如唯盲者眼不见色。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was behind the World-Honored One, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha. Venerable Ānanda said to Uttara, "As Parāśara says, are the deaf cultivating the faculties? Why is that? It is like only the deaf do not hear sounds with the ears."

尔时，尊者阿难在世尊后，执扇扇佛，尊者阿难语欝多言：「如波罗奢那所说，聋者是修根不？所以者何？如唯聋者耳不闻声。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Venerable Ānanda, "There is no supreme cultivation of the faculties apart from the noble Dharma and Vinaya."

尔时，世尊告尊者阿难：「异于贤圣法、律无上修诸根。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "May the World-Honored One please explain the supreme cultivation of the faculties in the noble Dharma and Vinaya for the bhikṣus. Having heard it, the bhikṣus will accept and uphold it."

阿难白佛言：「唯愿世尊为诸比丘说贤圣法、律无上修根，诸比丘闻已，当受奉行。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Listen carefully and consider it well, I will explain it to you. Through the conditions of the eye and form, eye consciousness arises. Seeing desirable forms, one cultivates the Tathāgata's detachment, right mindfulness, and right wisdom. Through the conditions of the eye and form, eye consciousness arises. Because of the undesirable, one cultivates the Tathāgata's non-detachment, right mindfulness, and right wisdom. Through the conditions of the eye and form, eye consciousness arises. Desirable and undesirable, one cultivates the Tathāgata's detachment and non-detachment, right mindfulness, and right wisdom. Through the conditions of the eye and form, eye consciousness arises. Undesirable and desirable, one cultivates the Tathāgata's non-detachment and detachment, right mindfulness, and right wisdom. Through the conditions of the eye and form, eye consciousness arises. Desirable, undesirable, and neither desirable nor undesirable, one cultivates the Tathāgata's detachment, non-detachment, and equanimous mind, right mindfulness, and right wisdom.

佛告阿难：「谛听，善思，当为汝说。缘眼、色，生眼识，见可意色，欲修如来厌离正念正智。眼、色缘生眼识，不可意故，修如来不厌离正念正智，眼、色缘生眼识，可意不可意，欲修如来厌离、不厌离正念正智。眼、色缘生眼识，不可意可意，欲修如来不厌离、厌离正念正智。眼、色缘生眼识，可意不可意，可不可意，欲修如来厌、不厌、俱离舍心住正念正智。

Thus, Ānanda! If one can well tame, well close, well guard, well hold, and well cultivate the mind towards these five phrases, then one has cultivated the supreme faculties with regard to the eye and form. The same is said for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind with regard to dharmas. Ānanda! This is called the supreme cultivation of the faculties in the noble Dharma and Vinaya.

「如是，阿难！若有于此五句，心善调伏、善关闭、善守护、善摄持、善修习，是则于眼、色无上修根。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法亦如是说。阿难！是名贤圣法，律无上修根。」

Venerable Ānanda said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! How does the noble Dharma and Vinaya cultivate the faculties of the noble ones?"

尊者阿难白佛言：「世尊！云何贤圣法、律为贤圣修根？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "The eye and form give rise to eye consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. The noble disciple truly knows this: 'My eye and form give rise to eye consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. This is quiescence, this is supreme, namely, the relinquishment of both. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion. It is like the time it takes for a strong man to snap his fingers. Thus, the eye and form give rise to eye consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. In an instant, it ceases and is extinguished, and one is free from both aversion and non-aversion, and relinquishes.'

佛告阿难：「眼、色缘生眼识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意。彼圣弟子如是如实知：『我眼、色缘生眼识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，此则寂灭，此则胜妙，所谓俱舍；得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。譬如力士顷。如是眼、色缘生眼识生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，俄尔尽灭，得离厌、不厌，舍。』

Thus, the ear and sound give rise to ear consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. The noble disciple truly knows this: 'My ear consciousness hears sounds, which give rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. This is quiescence, this is supreme, namely, the relinquishment of both. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion. It is like the time it takes for a strong man to snap his fingers, the sound of which ceases immediately. Thus, the ear and sound give rise to ear consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is relinquishment. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion.'

「如是耳、声缘生耳识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意。圣弟子如是如实知：『我耳识闻声，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，此则寂灭、胜妙，所谓为舍；得舍已，离厌、不厌。譬如大力士夫弹指，发声即灭。如是耳、声缘生耳识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭，是则为舍，得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。』

The nose and smell give rise to nose consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. The noble disciple truly knows this: 'The nose and smell give rise to nose consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. This is quiescence, this is supreme, namely, the relinquishment of both. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion. It is like a lotus flower, which is not stained by water. Thus, the nose and smell give rise to nose consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is relinquishment. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion.'

「鼻、香缘生鼻识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意。圣弟子如是如实知：『鼻、香缘生鼻识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，此则寂灭，此则胜妙，所谓为舍；得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。譬如莲荷，水所不染。如是鼻、香缘生鼻识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭，所谓为舍；得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。』

The tongue and taste give rise to tongue consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. The noble disciple truly knows this: 'The tongue and taste give rise to tongue consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is quiescence, this is supreme, namely, the relinquishment of both. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion. It is like the time it takes for a strong man to spit saliva from the tip of his tongue and it is extinguished. Thus, the tongue and taste give rise to tongue consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is relinquishment. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion.'

「舌、味缘生舌识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意。彼圣弟子如是如实知：『舌、味缘生舌识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭、寂灭、胜妙，所谓为舍；得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。譬如力士舌端唾沫，尽唾令灭。如是舌、味缘生舌识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭，所谓为舍，得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。』

The body and touch give rise to body consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. The noble disciple truly knows this: 'The body and touch give rise to body consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is quiescence, this is supreme, namely, the relinquishment of both. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion. It is like a red-hot iron ball. If a little water is sprinkled on it, it immediately extinguishes. Thus, the body and touch give rise to body consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is relinquishment. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion.'

「身、触缘生身识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭。圣弟子如是如实知：『身、触缘生身识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭、寂灭、胜妙，所谓为舍；得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。譬如铁丸烧令极热，小渧水洒，寻即消灭。如是身、触缘生身识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭，所谓为舍，得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。』

The mind and dharmas give rise to mind consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it quickly ceases and is extinguished. The noble disciple truly knows this: 'The mind and dharmas give rise to mind consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is quiescence, this is supreme, namely, the relinquishment of both. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion. It is like a strong man cutting off a palm tree. Thus, the mind and dharmas give rise to mind consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. Having arisen, it ceases and is extinguished. This is relinquishment. Having attained that relinquishment, one is free from both aversion and non-aversion.' Ānanda! This is the noble Dharma and Vinaya, practiced by the noble disciples to cultivate the faculties.

「意、法缘生意识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已速灭。圣弟子如是如实知：『意、法缘生意识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭，是则寂灭，是则胜妙，所谓为舍；得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。譬如力士断多罗树头。如是意、法缘生意识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，生已尽灭，所谓为舍，得彼舍已，离厌、不厌。』阿难！是为贤圣法、律，为圣弟子修诸根。」

「云何为圣法、律觉见迹？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "The eye and form give rise to eye consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. The noble disciple feels shame and disgust towards them. The ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind and dharmas give rise to mind consciousness, which gives rise to the agreeable, the disagreeable, and the agreeable and disagreeable. The noble disciple feels shame and disgust towards them. Ānanda! This is called the noble Dharma and Vinaya, the noble disciples' practice of the faculties, the noble Dharma and Vinaya, the noble disciples' practice of the faculties, the noble Dharma and Vinaya, the noble disciples' practice of the faculties, the noble Dharma and Vinaya, the noble disciples' practice of the faculties, the noble Dharma and Vinaya, the noble disciples' practice of

佛告阿难：「眼、色缘生眼识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，彼圣弟子惭耻厌恶。耳、鼻、舌、身、意法缘生意识，生可意，生不可意，生可意不可意，彼圣弟子惭耻厌恶。阿难！是名贤圣法、律觉见迹。阿难！是名贤圣法、律无上修诸根。已说贤圣修诸根，已说觉见迹。阿难！我为诸声闻所作，所作已作，汝等当作所作……」广说如箧毒蛇经。

Having spoken this sūtra, the World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, having spoken this sūtra, the World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, having spoken this sūtra, the World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat

佛说此经已，尊者阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
