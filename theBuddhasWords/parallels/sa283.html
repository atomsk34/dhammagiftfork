SĀ 283

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 283（二八三） 种树

Parallel to: SN 12.57

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If one gives rise to taste and attachment, looks back with longing, and is bound by the mind to the dharmas that are bound by the fetters, then craving arises. Craving conditions grasping, grasping conditions becoming, becoming conditions birth, and birth conditions old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. In this way, this great mass of suffering comes into being. It is like a person planting a tree. At first it is small and tender. They protect it and make it secure. They cover it with manure and earth. They water it at the right time. They adjust the temperature. Because of these causes and conditions, the tree then grows and becomes large. In the same way, bhikṣus! If one gives rise to taste and attachment, and nurtures the dharmas that are bound by the fetters, then affection arises. Affection conditions grasping, grasping conditions becoming, becoming conditions birth, and birth conditions old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. In this way, this great mass of suffering comes into being.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若于结所系法随生味著、顾念、心缚，则爱生；爱缘取，取缘有，有缘生，生缘老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。如是如是纯大苦聚集，如人种树，初小软弱，爱护令安，壅以粪土，随时溉灌，冷暖调适，以是因缘，然后彼树得增长大。如是，比丘！结所系法味著将养，则生恩爱，爱缘取，取缘有，有缘生，生缘老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。如是如是纯大苦聚集。

"If one observes the dharmas that are bound by the fetters as impermanent, abides in the observation of arising and ceasing, the observation of desirelessness, the observation of cessation, and the observation of relinquishment, and does not give rise to longing, the mind is not bound and attached, then craving ceases. With the cessation of craving, grasping ceases. With the cessation of grasping, becoming ceases. With the cessation of becoming, birth ceases. With the cessation of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress cease. In this way, this great mass of suffering ceases. It is like a tree. At first it is small and tender. If one does not protect it, make it secure, cover it with manure and earth, water it at the right time, and adjust the temperature, it will not grow. If one further cuts its roots, chops its branches, cuts it into pieces, analyzes it into parts, exposes it to wind and sun, burns it with fire, burns it to make fertilizer, or carries it away with a strong wind or throws it into a stream, what do you think, bhikṣus? Is it not because the tree's roots are cut off, and so on, up to being burned, that it is destroyed and will not arise in the future?"

「若于结所系法随顺无常观，住生灭观、无欲观、灭观、舍观，不生顾念，心不缚著，则爱灭；爱灭则取灭，取灭则有灭，有灭则生灭，生灭则老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是如是纯大苦聚灭。犹如种树，初小软弱，不爱护，不令安隐，不壅粪土，不随时溉灌，冷暖不适，不得增长；若复断根、截枝，段段斩截，分分解析，风飘日炙，以火焚烧，烧以成粪，或飏以疾风，或投之流水。比丘！于意云何？非为彼树断截其根，乃至焚烧，令其磨灭，于未来世成不生法耶？」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

答言：「如是，世尊！」

"Thus, bhikṣus, if one observes the dharmas that are bound by the fetters as impermanent, abides in the observation of arising and ceasing, the observation of desirelessness, the observation of cessation, and the observation of relinquishment, and does not give rise to longing, the mind is not bound and attached, then craving ceases. With the cessation of craving, grasping ceases. With the cessation of grasping, becoming ceases. With the cessation of becoming, birth ceases. With the cessation of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress cease. In this way, this great mass of suffering ceases."

「如是，比丘！于结所系法随顺无常观，住生灭观，无欲观、灭观、舍观，不生顾念，心不缚著，则爱灭，爱灭则取灭，取灭则有灭，有灭则生灭，生灭则老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是如是纯大苦聚灭。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha had said and joyfully upheld it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
