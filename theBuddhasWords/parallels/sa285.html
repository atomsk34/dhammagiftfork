SĀ 285

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 285（二八五） 佛缚

Parallel to: SN 12.10 (~) / SN 12.53–54 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "I remember from my past lives before I attained perfect enlightenment, when I was alone in a quiet place, diligently practicing meditation, I had this thought: 'The world is difficult to enter, namely birth, old age, sickness, death, change, and rebirth. However, sentient beings do not truly understand birth, old age, and death, and what they rely on.'

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我忆宿命未成正觉时，独一静处，专精禅思，生如是念：『世间难入，所谓若生、若老、若病、若死、若迁、若受生，然诸众生，生、老、死上及所依不如实知。』

I thought, 'What exists causes birth to exist? What conditions cause birth to exist?' Then I contemplated and immediately knew that existence causes birth to exist, and existence conditions birth to exist.

「我作是念：『何法有故生有？何法缘故生有？』即正思惟，起无间等知，有有故生有，有缘故生有。

I further contemplated, 'What exists causes existence to exist? What conditions cause existence to exist?' Then I contemplated and immediately knew that grasping causes existence to exist, and grasping conditions existence to exist.

「复思惟：『何法有故有有？何法缘故有有？』即正思惟，如实无间等起知，取有故有有，取缘故有有。

I further thought, 'What conditions grasping, and what exists causes grasping to exist? What conditions cause grasping to exist?' Then I contemplated and immediately knew that the taste of grasping, the recollection, the mental bondage, and the increase of craving cause grasping to exist. Because of craving, grasping exists. Grasping conditions existence, existence conditions birth, birth conditions old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. Thus, thus, there is the great mass of suffering.

「又作是念：『取复何缘、何法有故取有？何法缘故取有？』即正思惟，如实无间等起知，取法味著、顾念、心缚，爱欲增长；彼爱有故取有，爱故缘取，取缘有，有缘生，生缘老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。如是如是纯大苦聚集。

"Monks, what do you think? For example, if a lamp is lit with oil and a wick, can the light burn for a long time if the oil and wick are replenished?"

「诸比丘！于意云何？譬如缘膏油及炷，灯明得烧，数增油、炷，彼灯明得久住不？」

"Yes, World-Honored One!"

答言：「如是，世尊！」

So it is, monks! If one takes pleasure in form, clings to it, is attached to it, and is bound by it, and increases the conditions for craving, then craving gives rise to grasping, grasping gives rise to existence, existence gives rise to birth, and birth gives rise to old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. Thus there is the arising of this great mass of suffering.

「如是，诸比丘！于色取味著、顾念、爱缚，增长爱缘故取，取缘有，有缘生，生缘老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。如是如是纯大苦聚集。

At that time, I further thought: 'What is the absence of which causes the absence of old age, sickness, and death? What is the cessation of which causes the cessation of old age, sickness, and death?' Then I correctly contemplated and gave rise to the true and uninterrupted understanding that without birth, there is no old age, sickness, and death. The cessation of birth causes the cessation of old age, sickness, and death.

「我时复作是念：『何法无故无此老、病、死？何法灭故老、病、死灭？』即正思惟，起如实无间等，无生则无老、病、死，生灭故，则老、病、死灭。

I further thought: 'What is the absence of which causes the absence of birth? What is the cessation of which causes the cessation of birth?' Then I correctly contemplated and gave rise to the true and uninterrupted understanding that without existence, there is no birth. The cessation of existence causes the cessation of birth.

「复作是念：『何法无故无生？何法灭故生灭？』即正思惟，起如实无间等，有无故生无，有灭故生灭。

I further contemplated: 'What is the absence of which causes the absence of existence? What is the cessation of which causes the cessation of existence?' Then I correctly contemplated and gave rise to the true and uninterrupted understanding that without grasping, there is no existence. The cessation of grasping causes the cessation of existence.

「又复思惟：『何法无故有无？何法灭故有灭？』即正思惟，生如实无间等观，取无故有无，取灭故有灭。

I further thought: 'What is the absence of which causes the absence of grasping? What is the cessation of which causes the cessation of grasping?' Then I correctly contemplated and gave rise to the true and uninterrupted understanding that the grasped dharmas are impermanent, subject to birth and cessation, to be abandoned, to be extinguished, and to be let go. The mind does not dwell on them, is not bound to them, and love ceases. Due to the cessation of love, grasping ceases. Due to the cessation of grasping, existence ceases. Due to the cessation of existence, birth ceases. Due to the cessation of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and anguish cease. Thus, thus, the great mass of suffering ceases.

「又作是念：『何法无故取无？何法灭故取灭？』即正思惟，生如实无间等观，所取法无常、生灭、离欲、灭尽、舍离，心不顾念，心不缚著，爱则灭，彼爱灭故取灭，取灭故有灭，有灭故生灭，生灭故老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是如是纯大苦聚灭。

"Bhikṣus! What do you think? For example, if the oil and wick of a lamp are not replenished, will the light of that lamp not arise, not be extinguished, and not be worn out in the future?"

「诸比丘！于意云何？譬如油、炷然灯，若不增油治炷，非彼灯明未来不生、尽、磨灭耶？」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「如是，世尊！」

"So it is, bhikṣus! Observe impermanence, arising and ceasing, dispassion, cessation, and letting go in the things that are grasped. Do not be mindful of the mind, do not be attached to the mind. Then craving will cease, with the cessation of craving, grasping will cease, and so on up to the cessation of the great mass of suffering."

「如是，诸比丘！于所取法观察无常、生灭、离欲、灭尽、舍离，心不顾念，心不缚著，爱则灭，爱灭则取灭，乃至纯大苦聚灭。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha had said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
