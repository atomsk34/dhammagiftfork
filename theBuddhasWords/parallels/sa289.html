SĀ 289

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 289（二八九） 无闻

Parallel to: SN 12.61

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people are disgusted, detached, and turn away from the four great elements of the body, but not from consciousness. Why is that? They see that the four great elements of the body increase, decrease, are grasped, and are abandoned, but ignorant and unlearned ordinary people are unable to generate disgust, detachment, and liberation from mind, intention, and consciousness. Why is that? They have long cherished and attached to this, grasping it as 'mine,' and say, 'This is me, mine, and what belongs to me.' Therefore, ignorant and unlearned ordinary people are unable to generate disgust, detachment, and turning away from it. Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people would rather attach to the four great elements of the body as 'me' and 'mine,' but not to consciousness as 'me' and 'mine.' Why is that? The four great elements of the physical body may be seen to last for ten years, twenty, thirty, up to a hundred years, or if well measured, even a little longer. But their mind, intention, and consciousness change moment by moment, day and night, with different arising and ceasing. It is like a monkey roaming among the trees in a forest, grasping at branches here and there, letting go of one and taking another. Their mind, intention, and consciousness are also like this, with different arising and ceasing.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「愚痴无闻凡夫于四大身厌患、离欲、背舍而非识。所以者何？见四大身有增、有减、有取、有舍，而于心、意、识，愚痴无闻凡夫不能生厌、离欲、解脱。所以者何？彼长夜于此保惜系我，若得、若取，言：『是我、我所、相在。』是故愚痴无闻凡夫不能于彼生厌、离欲、背舍。愚痴无闻凡夫宁于四大身系我、我所，不可于识系我、我所。所以者何？四大色身或见十年住，二十、三十，乃至百年，若善消息或复小过。彼心、意、识日夜时刻，须臾转变，异生、异灭。犹如猕猴游林树间，须臾处处，攀捉枝条，放一取一。彼心、意、识亦复如是，异生、异灭。

"The well-versed noble disciple contemplates and observes the dependent origination of feelings, namely, pleasant feeling arises from pleasant contact, and when pleasant feeling arises, he knows it as it really is. When that pleasant contact ceases, the feeling that arises from the cause of pleasant contact also ceases, stops, cools down, subsides, and disappears. As with pleasant feeling, so with painful feeling, joyful feeling, sorrowful feeling, and neutral feeling that arises from neutral contact. When neutral feeling arises, he knows it as it really is. When that neutral contact ceases, the feeling that arises from the cause of neutral contact also ceases, stops, cools down, subsides, and disappears. He contemplates thus: 'This feeling arises from contact, is pleasant, and is bound by contact. Because of the pleasant contact, there is pleasant feeling. When the pleasant contact ceases, the pleasant feeling also ceases, stops, cools down, subsides, and disappears.' Thus, the well-versed noble disciple becomes disenchanted with form, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. Being disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate. Being dispassionate, he is liberated. With liberation, he knows and sees as it really is: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this state.'"

「多闻圣弟子于诸缘起善思惟观察，所谓乐触缘生乐受，乐受觉时，如实知乐受觉；彼乐触灭，乐触因缘生受亦灭、止、清凉、息、没。如乐受，苦触、喜触、忧触、舍触因缘生舍受，舍受觉时，如实知舍受觉；彼舍触灭，彼舍触因缘生舍受亦灭、止、清凉、息、没。彼如是思惟：『此受触生、触乐、触缚，彼彼触乐故，彼彼受乐，彼彼触乐灭，彼彼受乐亦灭、止、清凉、息、没。』如是，多闻圣弟子于色生厌，于受、想、行、识生厌，厌故不乐，不乐故解脱，解脱知见：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
