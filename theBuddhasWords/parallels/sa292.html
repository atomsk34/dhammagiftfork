SĀ 292

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 292（二九二） 思量观察

Parallel to: SN 12.51

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "How does one contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering? At that time, one contemplates the various kinds of suffering experienced by sentient beings, and wonders what causes, what conditions, what origins, and what contacts give rise to this suffering. One contemplates the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of grasping. If grasping ceases without remainder, then suffering also ceases. One truly knows the path of practice that leads to the cessation of suffering. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering, namely the cessation of grasping.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「云何思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边？时，思量众生所有众苦，种种差别，此诸苦何因、何集、何生、何触？思量取因、取集、取生、取触。若彼取灭无余，众苦则灭，彼所乘苦灭道迹如实知，修行彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓取灭。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. At that time, they contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of grasping. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of craving. If craving ceases without remainder, then grasping also ceases. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of grasping. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering, namely the cessation of craving.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量彼取何因、何集、何生、何触？思量彼取爱因、爱集、爱生、爱触。彼爱永灭无余，取亦随灭，彼所乘取灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓爱灭。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of craving. They know that craving has feeling as its cause, feeling as its condition, feeling as its origin, and feeling as its contact. If feeling ceases without remainder, then craving also ceases. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of craving. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering, namely the cessation of feeling.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。则思量彼爱何因、何集、何生、何触？知彼爱受因、受集、受生、受触。彼受永灭无余，则爱灭，彼所乘爱灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓受灭。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of feeling. They know that feeling has contact as its cause, contact as its condition, contact as its origin, and contact as its condition. If contact ceases without remainder, then feeling also ceases. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of contact. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量彼受何因、何集、何生、何触？知彼受触因、触集、触生、触缘。彼触永灭无余，则受灭，彼所乘触灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of contact. They know that contact has the six sense bases as its cause, the six sense bases as its condition, the six sense bases as its origin, and the six sense bases as its contact. If the six sense bases cease without remainder, then contact also ceases. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of the six sense bases. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量彼触何因、何集、何生、何触？当知彼触六入处因、六入处集、六入处生、六入处触。彼六入处欲灭无余，则触灭，彼所乘六入处灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of the six sense bases. They know that the six sense bases have name-and-form as their cause, name-and-form as their condition, name-and-form as their origin, and name-and-form as their contact. If name-and-form ceases without remainder, then the six sense bases also cease. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of name-and-form. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量彼六入处何因、何集、何生、何触？知彼六入处名色因、名色集、名色生、名色触。名色永灭无余，则六入处灭，彼所乘名色灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓名色灭。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of name-and-form. They know that name-and-form has consciousness as its cause, consciousness as its condition, consciousness as its origin, and consciousness as its contact. If consciousness ceases without remainder, then name-and-form also ceases. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of consciousness. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering.

「复次，比丘思量正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量名色何因、何集、何生、何触，知彼名色识因、识集、识生、识触。彼识欲灭无余，则名色灭，彼所乘识灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓识灭。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of consciousness. They know that consciousness has volitional formations as its cause, volitional formations as its condition, volitional formations as its origin, and volitional formations as its contact. If volitional formations cease without remainder, then consciousness also ceases. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of volitional formations. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量彼识何因、何集、何生、何触？知彼识行因、行集、行生、行触，作诸福行，善识生，作诸不福不善行，不善识生；作无所有行，无所有识生，是为彼识行因、行集、行生、行触。彼行欲灭无余，则识灭，彼所乘行灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓行灭。

Furthermore, bhikṣus contemplate and observe the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering. They contemplate the causes, conditions, origins, and contacts of volitional formations. They know that volitional formations have ignorance as their cause, ignorance as their condition, ignorance as their origin, and ignorance as their contact. Volitional formations that are meritorious are conditioned by ignorance, volitional formations that are demeritorious are also conditioned by ignorance, and volitional formations that are neither meritorious nor demeritorious are also conditioned by ignorance.

「复次，比丘思量观察正尽苦，究竟苦边。时，思量彼行何因、何集、何生、何触？知彼行无明因、无明集、无明生、无明触。彼福行无明缘，非福行亦无明缘，非福不福行亦无明缘。

Therefore, know that volitional formations have ignorance as their cause, ignorance as their condition, ignorance as their origin, and ignorance as their contact. If ignorance ceases without remainder, then volitional formations also cease. They truly know the path of practice that leads to the cessation of ignorance. This is called the bhikṣu's approach to the complete cessation of suffering, the end of suffering.

「是故当知，彼行无明因、无明集、无明生、无明触。彼无明永灭无余，则行灭，彼所乘无明灭道迹如实知，修习彼向次法，是名比丘向正尽苦，究竟苦边，所谓无明灭。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "What do you think? If one does not delight in ignorance and gives rise to understanding, would one then give rise to meritorious, demeritorious, or neither meritorious nor demeritorious volitional formations based on that ignorance?"

佛告比丘：「于意云何？若不乐无明而生明，复缘彼无明作福行、非福行、无所有行不？」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha: "No, World-Honored One! Why is that? Because the learned noble disciples do not delight in ignorance and give rise to understanding. When ignorance ceases, then volitional formations cease. When volitional formations cease, then consciousness ceases. In this way, up to birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress cease. In this way, the great mass of suffering ceases.

比丘白佛：「不也，世尊！所以者何？多闻圣弟子不乐无明而生明，无明灭则行灭，行灭则识灭，如是乃至生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是如是纯大苦聚灭。」

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu! I also say this, and you also understand this. In those dharmas, those dharmas arise, those dharmas are born, those dharmas cease, cease, stop, cool, rest, and disappear. If the learned noble disciples are free from desire for ignorance and give rise to understanding, they know as it is the sensations experienced within the limits of the body. When they know as it is the sensations experienced within the limits of the body, they also know as it is the sensations experienced within the limits of life. When the body perishes and life is about to end, all sensations experienced are completely extinguished without remainder. It is like a strong man taking a new, well-fired pot, placing it on the ground while still hot, and it will soon shatter, with the heat completely extinguished. In this way, bhikṣus, being free from desire for ignorance and giving rise to understanding, they know as it is the sensations experienced within the limits of the body, they know as it is the sensations experienced within the limits of life, and when the body perishes and life ends, all sensations experienced are completely extinguished without remainder.

佛言：「善哉！善哉！比丘！我亦如是说，汝亦知此。于彼彼法起彼彼法，生彼彼法，灭彼彼法，灭、止、清凉、息、没。若多闻圣弟子无明离欲而生明，身分齐受所觉，身分齐受所觉时如实知；若寿分齐受所觉，寿分齐受所觉时如实知；身坏时寿命欲尽，于此诸受一切所觉灭尽无余。譬如力士取新熟瓦器，乘热置地，须臾散坏，热势悉灭。如是，比丘无明离欲而生明，身分齐受所觉如实知，寿分齐受所觉如实知，身坏命终，一切受所觉悉灭无余。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus were delighted and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
