SĀ 296

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 296（二九六） 因缘法

Parallel to: SN 12.20

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I will now explain the Dharma of causes and conditions, and the Dharma of dependent origination.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今当说因缘法及缘生法。

What is the Dharma of causes and conditions? It means that because this exists, that exists. It means that due to ignorance there are karmic formations, due to karmic formations there is consciousness, and so on, up to the complete aggregation of suffering.

「云何为因缘法？谓此有故彼有，谓缘无明行，缘行识，乃至如是如是纯大苦聚集。

What is the Dharma of dependent origination? It means ignorance and karmic formations. Whether the Buddha appears in the world or not, this Dharma abides permanently, abiding in the realm of reality. The Tathāgata himself realizes and attains perfect enlightenment, and explains it to others, revealing and illuminating it, saying that due to ignorance there are karmic formations, and so on, up to due to birth there is old age and death. Whether the Buddha appears in the world or not, this Dharma abides permanently, abiding in the realm of reality. The Tathāgata himself realizes and attains perfect enlightenment, and explains it to others, revealing and illuminating it, saying that due to birth there is old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. These dharmas abide in the Dharma, are empty in the Dharma, are as they are in the Dharma, are as they are in the Dharma, are not apart from the Dharma as it is, and are not different from the Dharma as it is. They are true, real, and non-erroneous. This is called the Dharma of dependent origination. It means ignorance, karmic formations, consciousness, name and form, six sense bases, contact, feeling, craving, grasping, becoming, birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. This is called the Dharma of dependent origination.

「云何缘生法？谓无明、行。若佛出世，若未出世，此法常住，法住法界，彼如来自所觉知，成等正觉，为人演说，开示显发，谓缘无明有行，乃至缘生有老死。若佛出世，若未出世，此法常住，法住法界，彼如来自觉知，成等正觉，为人演说，开示显发，谓缘生故，有老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。此等诸法，法住、法空、法如、法尔，法不离如，法不异如，审谛真实、不颠倒。如是随顺缘起，是名缘生法。谓无明、行、识、名色、六入处、触、受、爱、取、有、生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦，是名缘生法。

The noble disciple of much learning correctly knows and sees these dharmas of causes and conditions and dependent origination. He does not seek the past, saying: 'Was I in the past? Was I not in the past? What kind was I in the past? What was I like in the past?' He does not seek the future: 'Will I be in the future? Will I not be in the future? What kind will I be? What will I be like?' He does not hesitate within: 'What is this? How is this? What is before this? Who will end? How will this be? Where do these beings come from? Where will they go when they pass away here?' If any śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa arises with worldly views and is bound by them, namely bound by the view of self, bound by the view of sentient beings, bound by the view of life, bound by the view of avoiding and promoting, then at that time he completely cuts them off, completely knows them, cuts off their root, like cutting off the top of a palm tree, and in the future they will not arise. This is called the noble disciple of much learning who correctly knows and sees the dharmas of causes and conditions and dependent origination, who sees well, knows well, cultivates well, and enters well.

「多闻圣弟子于此因缘法、缘生法正知善见，不求前际，言：『我过去世若有、若无？我过去世何等类？我过去世何如？』不求后际：『我于当来世为有、为无？云何类？何如？』内不犹豫：『此是何等？云何有此为前？谁终当云何之？此众生从何来？于此没当何之？』若沙门、婆罗门起凡俗见所系，谓说我见所系、说众生见所系、说寿命见所系、忌讳吉庆见所系，尔时悉断、悉知，断其根本，如截多罗树头，于未来世，成不生法。是名多闻圣弟子于因缘法、缘生法如实正知，善见、善觉、善修、善入。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
