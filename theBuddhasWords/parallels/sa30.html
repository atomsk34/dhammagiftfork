SĀ 30

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 30（三〇） 输屡那

Parallel to: SN 22.49

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra was staying on Vulture Peak.

尔时，尊者舍利弗在耆阇崛山中。

At that time, there was a young householder named Sunīdha who would go to Vulture Peak every day to visit Venerable Śāriputra. After exchanging greetings, he sat to one side and said to Śāriputra, "If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say about impermanent form, changing form, and unstable form, 'I am superior, I am equal, I am inferior,' why do śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have such thoughts and not see the truth? If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say about impermanent, changing, and unstable feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, 'I am superior, I am equal, I am inferior,' why do śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas have such thoughts and not see the truth?

时，有长者子名输屡那。日日游行，到耆阇崛山，诣尊者舍利弗，问讯起居已，却坐一面，语舍利弗言：「若诸沙门、婆罗门于无常色、变易、不安隐色言：『我胜、我等、我劣。』何故沙门、婆罗门作如是想，而不见真实？若沙门、婆罗门于无常、变易、不安隐受、想、行、识而言：『我胜、我等、我劣。』何故沙门、婆罗门作如是想，而不见真实？

"If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say about impermanent form, unstable form, and changing form, 'I am superior, I am equal, I am inferior,' what do they think and not see the truth? If they say about impermanent, changing, and unstable feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, 'I am superior, I am equal, I am inferior,' what do they think and not see the truth? "

「若沙门、婆罗门于无常色、不安隐色、变易言：『我胜、我等、我劣。』何所计而不见真实？于无常、变易、不安隐受、想、行、识言：『我胜、我等、我劣。』何所计而不见真实？」

"Sunīdha, what do you think? Is form permanent or impermanent?"

「输屡那！于汝意云何？色为常、为无常耶？」

He replied, "Impermanent."

答言：「无常。」

"Sunīdha, if it is impermanent, is it suffering?"

「输屡那！若无常，为是苦耶？」

He replied, "It is suffering."

答言：「是苦。」

"Sunīdha, if it is impermanent and suffering, and subject to change, what do you think? Do the noble disciples see form as 'me,' 'mine,' or 'in me'?"

「输屡那！若无常、苦，是变易法，于意云何？圣弟子于中见色是我、异我、相在不？」

He replied, "No."

答言：「不也。」

"Sunīdha, what do you think? Is feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness permanent or impermanent?"

「输屡那！于意云何？受、想、行、识为常、为无常？」

He replied, "Impermanent."

答言：「无常。」

"If it is impermanent, is it suffering?"

「若无常，是苦耶？」

He replied, "It is suffering."

答言：「是苦。」

"Śāriputra, if consciousness is impermanent and suffering, and subject to change, what do you think? Can a noble disciple see consciousness as 'I,' 'mine,' or 'something I am'?"

「输屡那！识若无常、苦，是变易法，于意云何？圣弟子于中见识是我、异我、相在不？」

He replied, "No."

答言：「不也。」

"Śāriputra, you should know that all forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, are not the self, not different from the self, nor do they coexist with the self. This is called true knowledge. Similarly, all feelings, perceptions, mental formations, and consciousness, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, gross or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, are not the self, not different from the self, nor do they coexist with the self. This is called true knowledge. Śāriputra, in this way, one becomes disenchanted with form, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness, and becomes detached, liberated, and with the knowledge and vision of liberation, one knows: 'Birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this state.'"

「输屡那！当知色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切色不是我、不异我、不相在，是名如实知。如是受、想、行、识，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麁、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切识不是我、不异我、不相在，是名如实知。输屡那！如是于色、受、想、行、识生厌，离欲、解脱，解脱知见：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

When Shariputra had finished speaking this sutra, the elder's son, Sumanas, was free from defilements and attained the pure Dharma eye. At that time, the elder's son, Sumanas, having seen the Dharma, attained the Dharma, not relying on others, and attained fearlessness in the true Dharma. He rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, knelt on his right knee, joined his palms, and said to Shariputra: "I am now liberated. From today, I take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, and take refuge in the Sangha as a lay devotee. From today, for as long as I live, I take pure refuge in the Three Jewels."

时，舍利弗说是经已，长者子输屡那远尘离垢，得法眼净。时，长者子输屡那见法得法，不由于他，于正法中，得无所畏。从坐起，偏袒右肩，胡跪合掌，白舍利弗言：「我今已度，我从今日归依佛、归依法、归依僧，为优婆塞。我从今日已，尽寿命，清净归依三宝。」

At that time, the elder's son, Sumanas, having heard what Shariputra said, was joyful and delighted. He bowed and departed.

时，长者子输屡那闻舍利弗所说，欢喜踊跃，作礼而去。
