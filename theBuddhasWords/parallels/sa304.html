SĀ 304

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 304（三〇四） 六六

Parallel to: MN 18 (~) / MN 38 (~) / MN 148 / SN 35.60 / SĀ 330 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the village of Gotiṇṇaka in the country of Kuru.

一时，佛住拘留搜调牛聚落。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I will now teach you the Dharma, which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with excellent meaning and excellent taste, pure and complete, the pure and bright practice of the Brahmins. Listen carefully and consider it well. There are six groups of six dharmas. What are the six groups of six dharmas? They are the six internal sense bases, the six external sense bases, the six groups of consciousness, the six groups of contact, the six groups of feeling, and the six groups of craving.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今当为汝等说法，初、中、后善，善义善味，纯一满净，梵行清白。谛听，善思，有六六法。何等为六六法？谓六内入处、六外入处、六识身、六触身、六受身、六爱身。

What are the six internal sense bases? They are the sense base of the eye, the sense base of the ear, the sense base of the nose, the sense base of the tongue, the sense base of the body, and the sense base of the mind. What are the six external sense bases? They are the sense base of form, the sense base of sound, the sense base of smell, the sense base of taste, the sense base of touch, and the sense base of dharmas. What are the six groups of consciousness? They are the group of eye consciousness, the group of ear consciousness, the group of nose consciousness, the group of tongue consciousness, the group of body consciousness, and the group of mind consciousness. What are the six groups of contact? They are eye contact, ear contact, nose contact, tongue contact, body contact, and mind contact. What are the six groups of feeling? They are feeling arising from eye contact, feeling arising from ear contact, feeling arising from nose contact, feeling arising from tongue contact, feeling arising from body contact, and feeling arising from mind contact. What are the six groups of craving? They are craving arising from eye contact, craving arising from ear contact, craving arising from nose contact, craving arising from tongue contact, craving arising from body contact, and craving arising from mind contact.

「何等为六内入处？谓眼入处、耳入处、鼻入处、舌入处、身入处、意入处。何等为六外入处，色入处、声入处、香入处、味入处、触入处、法入处。云何六识身？谓眼识身、耳识身、鼻识身、舌识身、身识身、意识身。云何六触身？谓眼触、耳触、鼻触、舌触、身触、意触。云何六受身？谓眼触生受、耳触生受、鼻触生受、舌触生受、身触生受、意触生受。云何六爱身？谓眼触生爱、耳触生爱、鼻触生爱、舌触生爱、身触生爱、意触生爱。

If one says that the eye is the self, that is not so. Why? Because the eye is born and perishes. If the eye were the self, the self would have to undergo birth and death. Therefore, to say that the eye is the self is not so. Similarly, if form, eye consciousness, eye contact, or feeling arising from eye contact were the self, that is not so. Why? Because feeling arising from eye contact is a phenomenon of birth and cessation. If feeling arising from eye contact were the self, the self would have to undergo birth and death. Therefore, to say that feeling arising from eye contact is the self is not so. Therefore, feeling arising from eye contact is not the self. Similarly, feeling arising from ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind contact is not the self. Why? Because feeling arising from mind contact is a phenomenon of birth and cessation. If it were the self, the self would have to undergo birth and death. Therefore, to say that feeling arising from mind contact is the self is not so. Therefore, feeling arising from mind contact is not the self.

「若有说言眼是我，是则不然。所以者何？眼生灭故，若眼是我者，我应受生死，是故说眼是我者，是则不然。如是若色、若眼识、眼触、眼触生受若是我者，是则不然。所以者何？眼触生受是生灭法，若眼触生受是我者，我复应受生死，是故说眼触生受是我者，是则不然，是故眼触生受非我。如是耳、鼻、舌、身、意触生受非我。所以者何？意触生受是生灭法，若是我者，我复应受生死，是故意触生受是我者，是则不然，是故意触生受非我。

Thus, bhikkhus, one should truly know the function of the eye, the function of knowledge, and the function of cessation, and develop supernormal power, and proceed directly to nibbana. How does one truly know and see the function of the eye, and proceed directly to nibbana? Thus, bhikkhus, the eye is not the self, and form, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feeling that arises from eye contact as a condition, whether pleasant, painful, or neither-pleasant-nor-painful, one should also observe as not the self. The same should be said of the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is called truly knowing and seeing the function of the eye, and proceeding directly to nibbana. This is called the Discourse on the Six Sixes.

「如是，比丘！当如实知眼所作、智所作、寂灭所作，开发神通，正向涅槃。云何如实知见眼所作，乃至正向涅槃？如是。比丘！眼非我，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，彼亦观察非我。耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦如是说，是名如实知见眼所作，乃至正向涅槃，是名六六法经。」

Having spoken this discourse, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "The bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
