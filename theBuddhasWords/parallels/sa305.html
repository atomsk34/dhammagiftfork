SĀ 305

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 305（三〇五） 六入处

Parallel to: MN 149

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the village of Gotiṇṇaka in the country of Kuru.

一时，佛住拘留搜调牛聚落。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying, "I will now teach you the Dharma, which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with good meaning and good taste, pure and complete, the pure and bright practice of the Brahmins. Listen carefully and contemplate it well, I will explain it to you.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今当为汝等说法，初、中、后善，善义善味，纯一满净，梵行清白，所谓六分别六入处经。谛听，善思，当为汝说。

What is the sutra on the six divisions of the six sense bases? It means not truly knowing and seeing the eye sense base, forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant. Not truly knowing and seeing, one becomes attached to the eye, forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant. In the same way, one does not truly know and see the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, phenomena, mind consciousness, mind contact, and the feelings that arise from mind contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant. Not truly knowing and seeing, one becomes attached. In this way, one is bound by attachment, ignorance, and clinging, and one's mind is bound. One nurtures the five aggregates of clinging, and future existence, clinging, and delight all increase. One's body and mind become weary and evil, and one's body and mind are burned and inflamed. One's body and mind are burning and one's mind is deranged. One experiences bodily pain. Because one experiences bodily pain, in the future one will increase birth, aging, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. This is called the accumulation of a great mass of suffering.

「何等为六分别六入处经？谓于眼入处不如实知见者，色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，不如实知见；不如实知见故，于眼染著，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，皆生染著。如是耳、鼻、舌、身、意，若法、意识、意触、意触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，不如实知见；不如实知见故，生染著。如是染著相应、愚暗、顾念、结缚其心，长养五受阴，及当来有受、贪、喜悉皆增长，身心疲恶，身心坏烧然，身心炽然，身心狂乱，身生苦觉；彼身生苦觉故，于未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦悉皆增长，是名纯一大苦阴聚集。

Monks, if one truly knows and sees the eye sense base, forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant, and sees them as they are, one will not become attached to the eye, forms, eye consciousness, eye contact, and the feelings that arise from eye contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant. In the same way, one truly knows and sees the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind, phenomena, mind consciousness, mind contact, and the feelings that arise from mind contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant. Truly knowing and seeing, one will not become attached to the mind, phenomena, mind consciousness, mind contact, and the feelings that arise from mind contact as a condition, whether they are pleasant, unpleasant, or neither pleasant nor unpleasant. Not being attached, one is not mixed up, not ignorant, not clinging, not bound. One reduces the five aggregates of clinging, future existence, clinging, and delight. All attachments are extinguished. One's body and mind are not weary and suffering. One's body and mind are not burned and inflamed. One's body and mind are not burning. One experiences bodily and mental pleasure. Because one experiences bodily and mental pleasure, in the future one will extinguish birth, aging, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus the great mass of suffering is extinguished.

「诸比丘！若于眼如实知见，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，如实知见；见已于眼不染著，若色、眼识、眼触、眼触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，不染著。如是耳、鼻、舌、身、意法如实知见，若法、意识、意触、意触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，如实知见；如实知见故，于意不染著。若法、意识、意触、意触因缘生受，内觉若苦、若乐、不苦不乐，不染；不染著故，不相杂、不愚暗、不顾念、不系缚，损减五受阴，当来有爱，贪、喜，彼彼染著悉皆消灭，身不疲苦，心不疲苦，身不烧，心不烧，身不炽然，心不炽然，身觉乐，心觉乐；身心觉乐故，于未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦悉皆消灭，如是纯大苦聚阴灭。

Those who know and see in this way are said to have perfected the cultivation of right view, right intention, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. The previously mentioned right speech, right action, and right livelihood are also said to be perfected. This is called the perfection of cultivating the eightfold noble path. Having perfected the cultivation of the eightfold noble path, one perfects the cultivation of the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernormal power, the five faculties, the five powers, and the seven factors of awakening.

「作如是知、如是见者，名为正见修习满足，正志、正方便、正念、正定，前说正语、正业、正命清净修习满足，是名修习八圣道清净满足。八圣道修习满足已，四念处修习满足，四正勤、四如意足、五根、五力、七觉分修习满足。

If there are dharmas to be known and understood, they are all known and understood. If there are dharmas to be known and abandoned, they are all known and abandoned. If there are dharmas to be known and realized, they are all realized. If there are dharmas to be known and cultivated, they are all cultivated. What dharmas should be known and understood? Namely, name and form. What dharmas should be known and abandoned? Namely, ignorance and craving for existence. What dharmas should be known and realized? Namely, wisdom and liberation. What dharmas should be known and cultivated? Namely, tranquility and insight. If a bhikṣu knows and understands these dharmas, he knows and understands them all. If there are dharmas to be known and abandoned, he knows and abandons them all. If there are dharmas to be known and realized, he knows and realizes them all. If there are dharmas to be known and cultivated, he knows and cultivates them all. This is called a bhikṣu who has cut off the fetters and bonds of craving, is rightly without obstruction, and has reached the end of suffering. Bhikṣus! This is called the Sūtra on the Sixfold Analysis of the Six Sense Bases.

「若法应知、应了者，悉知、悉了。若法应知、应断者，悉知、悉断。若法应知、应作证者，悉皆作证。若法应知、应修习者，悉已修习。何等法应知、应了，悉知、悉了？所谓名色。何等法应知、应断？所谓无明及有爱。何等法应知、应证？所谓明、解脱。何等法应知、应修？所谓止、观。若比丘于此法应知、应了，悉知、悉了。若法应知、应断者，悉知、悉断。若法应知、应作证者，悉知、悉证。若法应知、应修者，悉知、悉修，是名比丘断爱结缚，正无间等，究竟苦边。诸比丘！是名六分别六入处经。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus were delighted and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
