SĀ 309

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 309（三〇九） 鹿纽

Parallel to: SN 35.63

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Jētavana Monastery in Sàvatthi.

一时，佛住瞻婆国揭伽池侧。

At that time, Venerable Ruru came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! As the World-Honored One has said, there is a second abode and a single abode. What is the second abode? What is the single abode?"

尔时，尊者鹿纽来诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！如世尊说，有第二住，有一一住。彼云何第二住？云何一一住？」

The Buddha told Ruru, "Excellent! Excellent! Ruru! You are able to ask the Tathāgata about this meaning."

佛告鹿纽：「善哉！善哉！鹿纽！能问如来如是之义。」

The Buddha told Ruru, "If the eye perceives forms that are desirable, pleasant, and agreeable, and nourishes desire, and the bhikṣu, having seen them, delights in them, praises them, and dwells attached to them, having delighted in them, praised them, and dwelt attached to them, his mind becomes joyful. Having become joyful, he deeply delights. Having deeply delighted, he becomes greedy and attached. Having become greedy and attached, he becomes obstructed. Joy, deep delight, greed, and attachment, and obstruction, this is called the second abode. The same is said for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. Ruru! There are bhikṣus of this kind and appearance who, even if they dwell in solitude, are still called the second abode. Why is that? Because the joy and love are not cut off or extinguished. The Buddha Tathāgatas say that the second abode is where desire and love are not cut off and where ignorance is not known.

佛告鹿纽：「若眼识色可爱、乐、念、可意，长养于欲；彼比丘见已，喜乐、赞叹、系著住，爱乐、赞叹、系著住已，心转欢喜，欢喜已深乐，深乐已贪爱，贪爱已阨碍。欢喜、深乐、贪爱、阨碍者，是名第二住。耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦如是说。鹿纽！有如是像类比丘，正使空闲独处，犹名第二住。所以者何？爱喜不断不灭故，爱欲不断、不知者，诸佛如来说第二住。

"If a bhikṣu perceives forms that are desirable, pleasant, and agreeable, and nourish desire, and the bhikṣu, having seen them, does not delight in them, does not praise them, and does not dwell attached to them, having not delighted in them, not praised them, and not dwelt attached to them, he does not rejoice. Because he does not rejoice, he does not deeply delight. Because he does not deeply delight, he does not become greedy and attached. Because he does not become greedy and attached, he does not become obstructed. Not rejoicing, deeply delighting, greed, and attachment, and obstruction, this is called the single abode. The same is said for the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. Ruru! Bhikṣus of this kind and appearance, even if they dwell in a high building or a multi-storied pavilion, are still called those who dwell in the single abode. Why is that? Because desire and love are already exhausted and already known. The Buddha Tathāgatas say that those who have exhausted desire and love and have already known them are called those who dwell in the single abode."

「若有比丘，于可爱、乐、念、可意，长养于欲色；彼比丘见已不喜乐、不赞叹、不系著住，不喜乐、不赞叹、不系著住已，不欢喜，不欢喜故不深乐，不深乐故不贪爱，不贪爱故不阨碍。不欢喜、深乐、贪爱、阨碍者，是名为一一住。耳、鼻、舌、身、意亦如是说。鹿纽！如是像类比丘，正使处于高楼重阁，犹是一一住者。所以者何？贪爱已尽、已知故，贪爱已尽、已知者，诸佛如来说名一一住。」

At that time, Venerable Ruru, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully rejoiced, paid homage, and departed.

尔时，尊者鹿纽闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
