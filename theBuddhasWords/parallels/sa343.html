SĀ 343

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 343（三四三） 浮弥

Parallel to: SN 12.24–25

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, Venerable Fu Mi was staying at Vulture Peak.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。尔时，尊者浮弥比丘住耆阇崛山。

At that time, many non-Buddhist ascetics went to Venerable Fu Mi, exchanged greetings and pleasantries, and after exchanging greetings and pleasantries, they sat to one side and said to Venerable Fu Mi, "We wish to ask something. Is there time to answer us?"

时，有众多外道出家诣尊者浮弥所，共相问讯庆慰，共相问讯庆慰已，退坐一面，语尊者浮弥言：「欲有所问，宁有闲暇见答与不？」

Venerable Fu Mi said to the non-Buddhist ascetics, "Ask as you wish, and I will explain for you."

尊者浮弥语诸外道出家：「随汝所问，当为汝说。」

At that time, the non-Buddhist ascetics asked Venerable Fu Mi, "Is suffering and happiness self-made?"

时，诸外道出家问尊者浮弥：「苦乐自作耶？」

Venerable Fu Mi replied, "The non-Buddhist ascetics say that suffering and happiness are self-made. The World-Honored One said that this is indeterminate."

尊者浮弥答言：「诸外道出家说苦乐自作者，世尊说言：『此是无记。』」

He further asked, "Is suffering and happiness made by others?"

复问：「苦乐他作耶？」

He replied, "The World-Honored One said that this is indeterminate."

答言：「苦乐他作者，世尊说言：『此是无记。』」

He further asked, "Is suffering and happiness made by oneself and others?"

复问：「苦乐自他作耶？」

He replied, "The World-Honored One said that this is indeterminate."

答言：「苦乐自他作者，世尊说言：『此是无记。』」

He further asked, "Is suffering and happiness not made by oneself, not made by others, and not made without a cause?"

复问：「苦乐非自非他无因作耶？」

He replied, "The World-Honored One said that this is indeterminate."

答言：「苦乐非自非他无因作者，世尊说言：『此是无记。』」

The non-Buddhist renunciants further asked, "Venerable Fu Mi, is suffering and happiness self-made? You say it is indeterminate. Is suffering and happiness made by others? You say it is indeterminate. Is suffering and happiness made by oneself and others? You say it is indeterminate. Is suffering and happiness not made by oneself or others, but without a cause? You say it is indeterminate. Now, how does the Śramaṇa Gautama say suffering and happiness arise?"

诸外道出家复问：「云何？尊者浮弥苦乐自作耶？说言无记。苦乐他作耶？说言无记。苦乐自他作耶？说言无记。苦乐非自非他无因作耶？说言无记。今沙门瞿昙说苦乐云何生？」

Venerable Fu Mi replied, "Non-Buddhist renunciants, the World-Honored One says that suffering and happiness arise from conditions."

尊者浮弥答言：「诸外道出家！世尊说苦乐从缘起生。」

At that time, the non-Buddhist renunciants, having heard Venerable Fu Mi's words, were displeased and scolded him, leaving.

时，诸外道出家闻尊者浮弥所说，心不欢喜，呵责而去。

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra was sitting under a tree not far from Venerable Fu Mi.

尔时，尊者舍利弗去尊者浮弥不远，坐一树下。

At that time, Venerable Fu Mi, knowing that the non-Buddhist renunciants had left, went to where Venerable Śāriputra was. Having arrived, he exchanged greetings with Śāriputra. After the greetings, he told Venerable Śāriputra in detail about the questions asked by the non-Buddhist renunciants: "Having answered like this, have I not slandered the World-Honored One? Have I spoken as it is said, spoken according to the Dharma, and practiced in accordance with the Dharma? Will there be no one who comes to debate, criticize, and rebuke me for being a heretic?"

尔时，尊者浮弥知诸外道出家去已，往诣尊者舍利弗所，到已，与舍利弗面相庆慰。庆慰已，以彼诸外道出家所问事，具白尊者舍利弗：「我作此答，得不谤毁世尊！如说说、不如法说、不为是随顺法行法，得无为余因法论者来难诘呵责不？」

Venerable Śāriputra said, "Venerable Fu Mi! What you have said is indeed as the Buddha has said. You have not slandered the Tathāgata. You have spoken as it is said, spoken according to the Dharma, and practiced in accordance with the Dharma. There will be no one who comes to debate, criticize, and rebuke you for being a heretic. Why is that? Because the World-Honored One has said that suffering and happiness arise from conditions. Venerable Fu Mi! Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who ask about suffering and happiness being self-created, they also arise from causes. To say that they do not arise from conditions is impossible. Suffering and happiness being created by others, by self and others, or without cause, they also arise from conditions. To say that they do not arise from conditions is impossible. Venerable Fu Mi! Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who say that suffering and happiness are self-created, they also arise from contact. To say that they do not arise from contact is impossible. Suffering and happiness being created by others, by self and others, or without cause, they also arise from contact. To say that they do not arise from contact is impossible."

尊者舍利弗言：「尊者浮弥！汝之所说，实如佛说，不谤如来，如说说、如法说、法行法说，不为余因论义者来难诘呵责。所以者何？世尊说苦乐从缘起生故。尊者浮弥！彼诸沙门、婆罗门所问苦乐自作者，彼亦从因起生；言不从缘起生者，无有是处。苦乐他作、自他作、非自非他无因] cf.【丽】作说者，彼亦从缘起生；若言不从缘生者，无有是处。尊者浮弥！彼沙门、婆罗门所说苦乐自作者，亦缘触生；若言不从触生者，无有是处。苦乐他作、自他作、非自非他无因作者，彼亦缘触生，若言不缘触生者，无有是处。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda was not far from Śāriputra, sitting under a tree. He heard the discussion between Śāriputra and Fu Mi. Having heard it, he rose from his seat, went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. He told the World-Honored One in detail about the discussion between Fu Mi and Śāriputra.

尔时，尊者阿难去舍利弗不远，坐一树下，闻尊者舍利弗与尊者浮弥所论说事。闻已，从座起，往诣佛所，稽首佛足，退住一面。以尊者浮弥与尊者舍利弗共论说一一具白世尊。

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Excellent! Excellent! Ānanda! Venerable Śāriputra is able to answer questions according to the occasion. Excellent! Śāriputra! Because of having timely wisdom, he is able to answer questions according to the occasion. If my disciples have timely questions, they should answer according to the occasion, just as Śāriputra has said.

佛告阿难：「善哉！善哉！阿难！尊者舍利弗有来问者，能随时答。善哉！舍利弗！有应时智故，有来问者，能随时答。若我声闻，有随时问者，应随时答，如舍利弗所说。

Ānanda! When I was dwelling in the mountain hermitage of Rājagṛha, some non-Buddhist renunciants came to ask me about this meaning, these phrases, and this flavor. I answered them with this meaning, these phrases, and this flavor, just as Venerable Śāriputra has said.

「阿难！我昔时住王舍城山中仙人住处，有诸外道出家以如是义、如是句、如是味来问于我，我为斯等以如是义、如是句、如是味而为记说，如尊者舍利弗所说。

"Ānanda! If any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas say that suffering and happiness are self-made, I would go to them and ask, 'Do you really say that suffering and happiness are self-made?' If they answer, 'Yes,' I would ask further, 'Can you firmly uphold this meaning, saying it is true and the rest is foolish? I do not agree. Why? Because I say the arising of suffering and happiness is different from this.' If they ask me, 'How does Gautama say the arising of suffering and happiness is different?' I would answer, 'Suffering and happiness arise from their causes. If they say suffering is made by others, by self and others, or neither self nor others, without a cause, I would go to them and explain as above."

「阿难！若诸沙门、婆罗门苦乐自作，我即往彼问言：『汝实作是说苦乐自作耶？』彼答我言：『如是。』我即问言：『汝能坚执持此义，言是真实，余则愚者，我所不许。所以者何？我说苦乐所起异于此。』彼若问我：『云何瞿昙所说，苦乐所起异者？』我当答言：『从其缘起而生苦乐。如是说苦他作、自他作、非自非他无因作者，我亦往彼所说如上。』」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "As the World-Honored One has explained, I understand that because there is birth, there is old age and death, not otherwise. Because there is birth, there is old age and death, up to because there is ignorance, there are volitional formations, not otherwise. Because there is ignorance, there are volitional formations. When ignorance ceases, volitional formations cease, up to when birth ceases, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and anguish cease. Thus, this great mass of suffering ceases.

阿难白佛：「如世尊所说义，我已解知，有生故有老死，非缘余；有生故有老死，乃至无明故有行，非缘余；有无明故有行，无明灭则行灭，乃至生灭则老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是纯大苦聚灭。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the World-Honored One, the Venerable Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and followed them, paid homage and departed.

佛说此经已，尊者阿难闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
