SĀ 344

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 344（三四四） 拘𫄨罗

Parallel to: MN 9 / AN 9.13 (~) / MĀ 29 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, Venerable Śāriputra and Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila were staying at Vulture Peak.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。尔时，尊者舍利弗、尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗住耆阇崛山。

At that time, Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila arose from his meditation in the afternoon and went to where Venerable Śāriputra was. They greeted each other with joy and, after exchanging greetings, sat to one side. Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila said to Venerable Śāriputra, "I have a question to ask. Is there time to answer it?"

时，尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗晡时从禅定起，诣舍利弗所，共相庆慰，共相庆慰已，退坐一面，语尊者舍利弗：「欲有所问，宁有闲暇见答与不？」

Venerable Śāriputra said to Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila, "Ask your question, Venerable One. I will answer what I know."

尊者舍利弗语尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗：「仁者但问，知者当答。」

Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila said to Venerable Śāriputra, "In this Dharma and Vinaya, what qualities do the learned noble disciples possess, so that they are called 'complete in view,' 'with right view accomplished,' 'with accomplishment in the purity of faith in the Buddha,' 'having entered the true Dharma,' 'having attained this true Dharma,' 'having awakened to this true Dharma'?"

尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗语尊者舍利弗言：「多闻圣弟子于此法、律，成就何法，名为见具足，直见成就，成就于佛不坏净，来入正法，得此正法，悟此正法？」

Venerable Śāriputra said to Venerable Mahākauṣṭhila, "The learned noble disciples truly know unwholesome dharmas, the roots of unwholesome dharmas, wholesome dharmas, and the roots of wholesome dharmas.

尊者舍利弗语尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗：「多闻圣弟子于不善法如实知、不善根如实知、善法如实知、善根如实知。

How do they truly know unwholesome dharmas? Unwholesome bodily, verbal, and mental actions are called unwholesome dharmas. They truly know unwholesome dharmas in this way. How do they truly know the roots of unwholesome dharmas? The three unwholesome roots: the unwholesome root of greed, the unwholesome root of anger, and the unwholesome root of delusion. These are called the roots of unwholesome dharmas. They truly know the roots of unwholesome dharmas in this way.

「云何不善法如实知？不善身业、口业、意业，是名不善法，如是不善法如实知。云何不善根如实知？三不善根——贪不善根、恚不善根、痴不善根——是名不善根，如是不善根如实知。

How do they truly know wholesome dharmas? Wholesome bodily, verbal, and mental actions are called wholesome dharmas. They truly know wholesome dharmas in this way. How do they truly know the roots of wholesome dharmas? The three wholesome roots: non-greed, non-anger, and non-delusion. These are called the three wholesome roots. They truly know the roots of wholesome dharmas in this way.

「云何善法如实知？善身业、口业、意业，是名善法，如是善法如实知。云何善根如实知？谓三善根——无贪、无恚、无痴——是名三善根，如是善根如实知。

Venerable Mahā-Koṭṭhita, the well-versed noble disciple truly knows unwholesome dharmas, the roots of unwholesome dharmas, wholesome dharmas, and the roots of wholesome dharmas. Therefore, in this Dharma and Vinaya, they have perfect right view, upright view, and accomplish the unshakable faith in the Buddha. They enter the true Dharma, attain the true Dharma, and realize the true Dharma.

「尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗！如是多闻圣弟子不善法如实知、不善根如实知、善法如实知、善根如实知，故于此法、律正见具足，直见成就，于佛不坏净成就，来入正法，得此正法，悟此正法。」

Venerable Mahā-Koṭṭhita said to Venerable Śāriputra, "Are there any more?"

尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗语尊者舍利弗：「正有此等，更有余耶？」

Venerable Śāriputra said, "There are. If the well-versed noble disciple truly knows food, the origin of food, the cessation of food, and the path leading to the cessation of food,

尊者舍利弗言：「有。若多闻圣弟子于食如实知，食集、食灭、食灭道迹如实知。

How does one truly know food? It refers to the four kinds of food. What are the four? First, coarse solid food; second, subtle contact food; third, mental intention food; fourth, consciousness food. These are called food, and one truly knows them as such. How does one truly know the origin of food? It refers to the craving for future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, taking pleasure in various forms of existence. This is called the origin of food, and one truly knows the origin of food as such. How does one truly know the cessation of food? It refers to the complete cessation, relinquishment, elimination, detachment, cessation, pacification, and disappearance of the craving for future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, taking pleasure in various forms of existence. This is called the cessation of food, and one truly knows the cessation of food as such. How does one truly know the path leading to the cessation of food? It refers to the eightfold noble path - right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called the path leading to the cessation of food, and one truly knows the path leading to the cessation of food as such.

「云何于食如实知？谓四食。何等为四？一者麁抟食，二者细触食，三者意思食，四者识食，是名为食，如是食如实知。云何食集如实知？谓当来有爱，喜、贪俱，彼彼乐著，是名食集，如是食集如实知。云何食灭如实知？若当来有爱，喜、贪俱，彼彼乐著无余断、舍、吐、尽、离欲、灭、息、没，是名食灭，如是食灭如实知。云何食灭道迹如实知？谓八圣道——正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定，是名食灭道迹，如是食灭道迹如实知。

"If the well-versed noble disciple truly knows food, the origin of food, the cessation of food, and the path leading to the cessation of food, then the well-versed noble disciple will be endowed with right view in the true Dharma and Vinaya, will attain upright view, will attain unshakable faith in the Buddha, will enter the true Dharma, will attain this true Dharma, and will realize this true Dharma."

「若多闻圣弟子于此食如实知、食集如实知、食灭如实知、食灭道迹如实知，是故多闻圣弟子于正法、律正见具足，直见成就，于佛不坏净成就，来入正法，得此正法，悟此正法。」

Venerable Mahā-Koṭṭhita further asked Venerable Śāriputra, "Are there any other kinds of food?"

尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗复问尊者舍利弗：「正有此等，更有余耶？」

Venerable Śāriputra said, "Venerable Mahā-Koṭṭhita! There are also other kinds of food. The well-versed noble disciple truly knows illness, the origin of illness, the cessation of illness, and the path leading to the cessation of illness.

尊者舍利弗言：「尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗！复更有余，多闻圣弟子于病如实知、病集如实知、病灭如实知、病灭道迹如实知。

What is truly knowing illness? There are three kinds of illness: the illness of desire, the illness of existence, and the illness of ignorance. This is called illness, and one truly knows it as such. What is truly knowing the origin of illness? The origin of ignorance is the origin of illness. This is called the origin of illness, and one truly knows it as such. What is truly knowing the cessation of illness? The cessation of ignorance is the cessation of illness. This is called the cessation of illness, and one truly knows it as such. What is truly knowing the path leading to the cessation of illness? It is the eightfold noble path, as explained before. This is called the path leading to the cessation of illness, and one truly knows it as such.

「云何有病如实知？谓三病——欲病、有病、无明病——是名病，如是病如实知。云何病集如实知？无明集是病集，是名病集如实知。云何病灭如实知？无明灭是病灭，如是病灭如实知。云何病灭道迹如实知？谓八正道，如前说，如是病灭道迹如实知。

"If the well-versed noble disciple truly knows illness, the origin of illness, the cessation of illness, and the path leading to the cessation of illness, then the well-versed noble disciple will be endowed with right view in this Dharma and Vinaya, and will awaken to this true Dharma."

「若多闻圣弟子于病如实知、病集如实知、病灭如实知、病灭道迹如实知，故多闻圣弟子于此法、律正见具足，乃至悟此正法。」

Venerable Mahā-Koṭṭhita asked Venerable Śāriputra, "Are there only these, or are there others?"

尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗问尊者舍利弗：「正有此等，更有余耶？」

Venerable Śāriputra said to Venerable Mahā-Koṭṭhita, "There are also others. The well-versed noble disciple truly knows suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering.

尊者舍利弗语尊者摩诃拘𫄨罗：「亦更有余，多闻圣弟子于苦如实知、苦集如实知、苦灭如实知、苦灭道迹如实知。

How does one truly know suffering? It refers to the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, death, separation from what is loved, encountering what is hated, and not obtaining what one desires. In brief, it refers to the suffering of the five aggregates. This is called suffering, and one truly knows it as such. How does one truly know the origin of suffering? It refers to the craving for future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, seeking delight in this and that. This is called the origin of suffering, and one truly knows it as such. How does one truly know the cessation of suffering? It refers to the complete cessation, pacification, and disappearance of the craving for future existence, accompanied by delight and desire, seeking delight in this and that. This is called the cessation of suffering, and one truly knows it as such. How does one truly know the path leading to the cessation of suffering? It refers to the eightfold noble path as explained above. This is called the path leading to the cessation of suffering, and one truly knows it as such.

「云何苦如实知？谓生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、恩爱别苦、怨憎会苦、所欲不得苦，如是略说五受阴苦，是名为苦，如是苦如实知。云何苦集如实知？当来有爱，喜、贪俱，彼彼集著，是名苦集，如是苦集如实知。云何苦灭如实知？若当来有爱，喜、贪俱，彼彼染著无余断，乃至息、没，是名苦灭，如是苦灭如实知。云何苦灭道迹如实知？谓八圣道，如上说，是名苦灭道迹，如是苦灭道迹如实知。

"The well-versed noble disciple truly knows suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering. Such a noble disciple has perfect right view in my teachings and discipline, has attained right view, has attained unshakable faith in the Buddha, has entered the right path, has attained this right Dharma, and has realized this right Dharma."

「多闻圣弟子如是苦如实知，苦集、苦灭、苦灭道迹如实知。如是圣弟子于我法、律具足正见，直见成就，于佛不坏净成就，来入正道，得此正法，悟此正法。」

He further asked Venerable Śāriputra, "Are there only these teachings, or are there others?"

复问尊者舍利弗：「正有此法，复有余耶？」

Venerable Śāriputra replied, "There are others, namely, the well-versed noble disciple truly knows old age and death, the origin of old age and death, the cessation of old age and death, and the path leading to the cessation of old age and death. As explained in the previous sūtra. How does one truly know the origin of old age and death? The origin of birth is the origin of old age and death. The cessation of birth is the cessation of old age and death. The path leading to the cessation of old age and death is the eightfold noble path, as explained before. The well-versed noble disciple truly knows old age and death in this way, up to the path leading to the cessation of old age and death. Such a noble disciple has perfect right view in my teachings and discipline, has attained right view, has attained unshakable faith in the Buddha, has entered the right Dharma, has attained this right Dharma, and has realized this right Dharma. In this way, birth, existence, grasping, craving, feeling, contact, the six sense bases, name and form, consciousness, and formations - the noble disciple truly knows formations, the origin of formations, the cessation of formations, and the path leading to the cessation of formations.

尊者舍利弗答言：「更有余，谓多闻圣弟子老死如实知、老死集如实知、老死灭如实知、老死灭道迹如实知，如前分别经说。云何老死集如实知？生集是老死集，生灭是老死灭，老死灭道迹，谓八正道，如前说，多闻圣弟子于此老死如实知，乃至老死灭道迹如实知。如是圣弟子于我法、律正见具足，直见成就，于佛不坏净成就，来入正法，得此正法，悟此正法。如是生、有、取、爱、受、触、六入处、名色、识、行，圣弟子于行如实知，行集、行灭、行灭道迹如实知。

"How does one truly know formations? There are three kinds of formations - bodily formations, verbal formations, and mental formations. One truly knows these formations in this way. How does one truly know the origin of formations? The origin of ignorance is the origin of formations. One truly knows the origin of formations in this way. How does one truly know the cessation of formations? The cessation of ignorance is the cessation of formations. One truly knows the cessation of formations in this way. How does one truly know the path leading to the cessation of formations? It is the eightfold noble path, as explained before. Mahākauṣṭhila! This is called the noble disciple truly knowing formations, the origin of formations, the cessation of formations, and the path leading to the cessation of formations. They have perfect right view in my teachings and discipline, have attained right view, have attained unshakable faith in the Buddha, have entered the right Dharma, have attained this right Dharma, and have realized this right Dharma."

「云何行如实知？行有三种——身行、口行、意行，如是行如实知。云何行集如实知？无明集是行集，如是行集如实知。云何行灭如实知？无明灭是行灭，如是行灭如实知。云何行灭道迹如实知？谓八圣道，如前说。摩诃拘𫄨罗！是名圣弟子行如实知，行集、行灭、行灭道迹如实知，于我法、律正见具足，直见成就，于佛不坏净成就，来入正法，得此正法，悟此正法。」

Mahākauṣṭhila further asked Venerable Śāriputra, "Are there only these teachings, or are there others?"

摩诃拘𫄨罗复问尊者舍利弗：「唯有此法，更有余耶？」

Śāriputra replied, "Mahākauṣṭhila! Why do you pursue? You will never be able to exhaust all the teachings and reach their limits. If a noble disciple has eliminated ignorance and given rise to wisdom, what more need is there to seek?"

舍利弗答言：「摩诃拘𫄨罗！汝何为逐，汝终不能究竟诸论，得其边际。若圣弟子断除无明而生明，何须更求？」

At that time, the two noble ones had finished discussing the teachings and each returned to their respective places.

时，二正士共论义已，各还本处。
