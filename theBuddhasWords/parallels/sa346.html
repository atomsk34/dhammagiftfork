SĀ 346

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 346（三四六） 三法

Parallel to: AN 10.76 / SĀ 760

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "There are three things that are not loved, not remembered, and not agreeable in the world. What are the three? They are old age, sickness, and death. If these three things that are not loved, not remembered, and not agreeable did not exist in the world, the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One would not appear in the world, and the world would not know that the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One knows and sees, and teaches the true Dharma and Vinaya. Because in the world there are these three things that are not loved, not remembered, and not agreeable, therefore the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One appears in the world, and the world knows that the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One knows and sees, and teaches the true Dharma and Vinaya.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有三法，世间所不爱、不念、不可意。何等为三？谓老、病、死。世间若无此三法不可爱、不可念、不可意者，如来、应、等正觉不出于世间，世间亦不知有如来、应、等正觉知见，说正法、律。以世间有老、病、死三法不可爱、不可念、不可意故，是故如来、应、等正觉出于世间，世间知有如来、应、等正觉所知、所见，说正法、律。

Because these three things are not cut off, one is unable to be free from old age, sickness, and death. What are the three? They are greed, anger, and delusion. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from greed, anger, and delusion. What are the three? They are the view of a self, holding precepts as supreme, and doubt. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from the view of a self, holding precepts as supreme, and doubt. What are the three? They are incorrect contemplation, following wrong paths, and a lazy mind. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from incorrect contemplation, following wrong paths, and a lazy mind. What are the three? They are forgetfulness, incorrect knowledge, and a distracted mind. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from forgetfulness, incorrect knowledge, and a distracted mind. What are the three? They are agitation, lack of discipline, and not learning precepts. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from agitation, lack of discipline, and not learning precepts. What are the three? They are lack of faith, being difficult to teach, and laziness. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from lack of faith, being difficult to teach, and laziness. What are the three? They are not wanting to see the noble ones, not wanting to hear the Dharma, and always seeking others' faults. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from not wanting to see the noble ones, not wanting to hear the Dharma, and always seeking others' faults. What are the three? They are lack of respect, harsh speech, and associating with bad friends. There are also three things that are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from lack of respect, harsh speech, and associating with bad friends. What are the three? They are shamelessness, lack of conscience, and heedlessness. These three things are not cut off, and one is unable to be free from lack of respect, harsh speech, and associating with bad friends.

「以三法不断故，不堪能离老、病、死。何等为三？谓贪、恚、痴，复有三法不断故，不堪能离贪、恚、痴。何等为三？谓身见、戒取、疑，复有三法不断故，不堪能离身见、戒取、疑。何等为三？谓不正思惟、习近邪道，及懈怠心，复有三法不断故，不堪能离不正思惟、习近邪道及懈怠心。何等为三？谓失念、不正知、乱心，复有三法不断故，不堪能离失念、不正知、乱心。何等为三？谓掉、不律仪、不学戒，复有三法不断故，不堪能离掉、不律仪、不学戒。何等为三？谓不信、难教、懈怠，复有三法不断故，不堪能离不信、难教、嬾堕。何等为三？谓不欲见圣、不欲闻法、常求人短，复有三法不断故，不堪能离不欲见圣、不欲闻法、常求人短。何等为三？谓不恭敬、戾语、习恶知识，复有三法不断故，不堪能离不恭敬、戾语、习恶知识。何等为三？谓无惭、无愧、放逸，此三法不断故，不堪能离不恭敬、戾语、习恶知识。

Why is that? Because of shamelessness and lack of conscience, one becomes heedless. Because of heedlessness, one lacks respect. Because of lacking respect, one associates with bad friends. Because of associating with bad friends, one does not want to see the noble ones, does not want to hear the Dharma, and always seeks others' faults. Because of seeking others' faults, one lacks faith, is difficult to teach, speaks harshly, and is lazy. Because of laziness, one becomes agitated, undisciplined, and does not learn precepts. Because of not learning precepts, one becomes forgetful, has incorrect knowledge, and has a distracted mind. Because of a distracted mind, one has incorrect contemplation, follows wrong paths, and has a lazy mind. Because of a lazy mind, one has the view of a self, holds precepts as supreme, and has doubt. Because of doubt, one is unable to be free from greed, anger, and delusion. Because one is unable to be free from greed, anger, and delusion, one is unable to be free from old age, sickness, and death.

「所以者何？以无惭、无愧故放逸，放逸故不恭敬，不恭敬故习恶知识，习恶知识故不欲见圣、不欲闻法、常求人短，求人短故不信、难教、戾语、嬾堕，嬾堕故掉、不律仪、不学戒，不学戒故失念、不正知、乱心，乱心故不正思惟、习近邪道、懈怠心，懈怠心故身见、戒取、疑，疑故不离贪、恚、痴，不离贪、恚、痴故不堪能离老、病、死。

Because these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from old age, sickness, and death. What are the three? They are greed, anger, and delusion. When these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from old age, sickness, and death. There are also three things that are cut off, and one is able to be free from greed, anger, and delusion. What are the three? They are the view of a self, holding precepts as supreme, and doubt. When these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from greed, anger, and delusion. There are also three things that are cut off, and one is able to be free from the view of a self, holding precepts as supreme, and doubt. What are the three? They are incorrect contemplation, following wrong paths, and a lazy mind. When these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from the view of a self, holding precepts as supreme, and doubt. There are also three things that are cut off, and one is able to be free from incorrect contemplation, following wrong paths, and a lazy mind. What are the three? They are forgetfulness, incorrect knowledge, and a distracted mind. When these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from incorrect contemplation, following wrong paths, and a lazy mind. There are also three things that are cut off, and one is able to be free from forgetfulness, incorrect knowledge, and a distracted mind. What are the three? They are agitation, lack of discipline, and breaking precepts. When these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from forgetfulness, incorrect knowledge, and a distracted mind. There are also three things that are cut off, and one is able to be free from agitation, lack of discipline, and breaking precepts. What are the three? They are lack of faith, being difficult to teach, and laziness. When these three things are cut off, one is able to be free from agitation, lack of discipline, and breaking precepts. There are also three things that are cut off, and one is able to be free from lack of faith, being difficult to teach, and laziness. What are the three? 「断三法故，堪能离老、病、死。云何三？谓贪、恚、痴，此三法断已，堪能离老、病、死，复三法断故，堪能离贪、恚、痴。云何三？谓身见、戒取、疑，此三法断故，堪能离贪、恚、痴，复三法断故，堪能离身见、戒取、疑。云何为三？谓不正思惟、习近邪道、起懈怠心，此三法断故，堪能离身见、戒取、疑，复三法断故，堪能离不正思惟、习近邪道及懈怠心。云何为三？谓失念心、不正知、乱心，此三法断故，堪能离不正思惟、习近邪道及心懈怠，复三法断故，堪能离失念心、不正知、乱心。何等为三？谓掉、不律仪、犯戒，此三法断故，堪能离失念心、不正知、乱心，复有三法断故，堪能离掉、不律仪、犯戒。云何三？谓不信、难教、嬾堕，此三法断故，堪能离掉、不律仪、犯戒，复有三法断故，堪能离不信、难教、嬾堕。云何为三？谓不欲见圣、不乐闻法、好求人短，此三法断故，堪能离不信、难教、嬾堕，复三法断故，堪能离不欲见圣、不欲闻法、好求人短。云何为三？谓不恭敬、戾语、习恶知识，此三法断故，离不欲见圣、不欲闻法、好求人短，复有三法断故，堪能离不恭敬、戾语、习恶知识。云何三？谓无惭、无愧、放逸。

Why is that? Because of shame and conscience, one becomes heedful. Because of heedfulness, one is respectful and obedient, and has good friends. Because of having good friends, one enjoys seeing the noble ones, enjoys hearing the true Dharma, and does not seek others' faults. Because of not seeking others' faults, one gives rise to faith, is obedient, and is diligent. Because of diligence, one is not agitated, abides in discipline, and learns precepts. Because of learning precepts, one does not lose mindfulness, has correct knowledge, and abides in an unconfused mind. Because of an unconfused mind, one has correct contemplation, practices the right path, and has an undistracted mind. Because of an undistracted mind, one does not cling to the view of a self, does not cling to holding precepts as supreme, and crosses over doubt. Because of being free from doubt, one does not give rise to greed, anger, or delusion. Because one is free from greed, anger, and delusion, one is able to cut off old age, sickness, and death.

「所以者何？以惭愧故不放逸，不放逸故恭敬顺语、为善知识，为善知识故乐见贤圣、乐闻正法、不求人短，不求人短故生信、顺语、精进，精进故不掉、住律仪、学戒，学戒故不失念、正知、住不乱心，不乱心故正思惟、习近正道、心不懈怠，心不懈怠故不著身见、不著戒取、度疑惑，不疑故不起贪、恚、痴，离贪、恚、痴故堪能断老、病、死。」

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha taught rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
