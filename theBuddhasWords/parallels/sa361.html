SĀ 361

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 361（三六一） 思量

Parallel to: SN 12.40

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If there is thought and imagination, then there are the fetters and clinging to the abode of consciousness. Because there is clinging to the abode of consciousness, one enters into name and form. Because one enters into name and form, there is coming and going. Because there is coming and going, there is birth and death. Because there is birth and death, there is future birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. Thus, this great mass of suffering arises. If there is no thought and no imagination, there are no fetters and no clinging to the abode of consciousness. Because there is no clinging to the abode of consciousness, one does not enter into name and form. Because one does not enter into name and form, there is no coming and going. Because there is no coming and going, there is no birth and death. Because there is no birth and death, future birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress cease. Thus, this great mass of suffering ceases."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「若有思量、有妄想，则有使攀缘识住；有攀缘识住故，入于名色；入名色故，则有往来；有往来故，则有生死；有生死故，则有未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦。如是纯大苦聚集，若不思量、无妄想，无使无攀缘识住；无攀缘识住故，不入名色；不入名色故，则无往来；无往来故，则无生死；无生死故，于未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是纯大苦聚灭。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha had said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
