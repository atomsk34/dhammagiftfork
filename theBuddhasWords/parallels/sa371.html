SĀ 371

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 371（三七一） 食

Parallel to: SN 12.11

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are four kinds of food that nourish beings, allowing them to abide in the world, being accepted and nurtured. What are the four? They are: first, coarse solid food, second, subtle contact food, third, intention food, and fourth, consciousness food.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有四食资益众生，令得住世摄受长养。何等为四？谓一、麤抟食，二、细触食，三、意思食，四、识食。

What are the causes, accumulations, origins, and conditions of these four foods? They are caused by craving, accumulated by craving, originated by craving, and conditioned by craving. What are the causes, accumulations, origins, and conditions of this craving? It is caused by feeling, accumulated by feeling, originated by feeling, and conditioned by feeling. What are the causes, accumulations, origins, and conditions of this feeling? It is caused by contact, accumulated by contact, originated by contact, and conditioned by contact. What are the causes, accumulations, origins, and conditions of this contact? It is caused by the six sense bases, accumulated by the six sense bases, originated by the six sense bases, and conditioned by the six sense bases. The accumulation of the six sense bases is the accumulation of contact, the accumulation of contact is the accumulation of feeling, the accumulation of feeling is the accumulation of craving, the accumulation of craving is the accumulation of food, and the accumulation of food leads to the accumulation of future birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus there is the accumulation of this great mass of suffering.

「此四食何因、何集、何生、何触？谓此诸食爱因、爱集、爱生、爱触。此爱何因、何集、何生、何触？谓爱受因、受集、受生、受触。此受何因、何集、何生、何触？谓受触因、触集、触生、触触，此触何因、何集、何生、何触？谓触六入处因、六入处集、六入处生、六入处触。六入处集是触集，触集是受集，受集是爱集，爱集是食集，食集故未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦集，如是纯大苦聚集。

Thus, with the cessation of the six sense bases, contact ceases; with the cessation of contact, feeling ceases; with the cessation of feeling, craving ceases; with the cessation of craving, food ceases; with the cessation of food, in the future there is the cessation of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus there is the cessation of this great mass of suffering.

「如是六入处灭则触灭，触灭则受灭，受灭则爱灭，爱灭则食灭，食灭故于未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦灭，如是纯大苦聚灭。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus were delighted and practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
