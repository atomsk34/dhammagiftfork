SĀ 373

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 373（三七三） 子肉

Parallel to: SN 12.63

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are four kinds of food that nourish beings, allowing them to abide in the world, being accepted and nurtured. What are the four? They are: first, coarse solid food, second, subtle contact food, third, intention food, and fourth, consciousness food.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有四食资益众生，令得住世摄受长养。云何为四？谓一、麤抟食，二、细触食，三、意思食，四、识食。

"How do bhikṣus contemplate solid food? Suppose there is a married couple with only one child, whom they love and cherish. They want to cross a wilderness with dangerous paths, but their provisions are exhausted and they are starving and exhausted, with no hope of survival. They discuss, 'We have only one child, whom we love dearly. If we eat his flesh, we can cross the danger. Do not let all three of us die here.' Having made this plan, they kill their child, shed tears of sorrow, and force themselves to eat his flesh to cross the wilderness. What do you think, bhikṣus? Would that couple, eating their child's flesh, take pleasure in its taste and savor its deliciousness?"

「云何比丘观察抟食？譬如有夫妇二人，唯有一子，爱念将养，欲度旷野崄道难处，粮食乏尽，饥饿困极，计无济理，作是议言：『正有一子，极所爱念，若食其肉，可得度难，莫令在此三人俱死。』作是计已，即杀其子，含悲垂泪，强食其肉，得度旷野。云何？比丘！彼人夫妇共食子肉，宁取其味，贪嗜美乐与不？」

He replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

答曰：「不也，世尊！」

He further asked, "Bhikṣu! Did they force themselves to eat the flesh to cross the wilderness and dangerous paths?"

复问：「比丘！彼强食其肉，为度旷野崄道与不？」

He replied, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

答言：「如是，世尊！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "When eating solid food, one should contemplate in this way. If one contemplates in this way, one will know the cessation of solid food. Having known the cessation of solid food, one will then cut off craving and attachment to the five sense pleasures. For those who have cut off craving and attachment to the five sense pleasures, I do not see any remaining afflictions or fetters that are not severed for those learned and noble disciples. Because of the remaining afflictions and fetters, one will be reborn in this world.

佛告比丘：「凡食抟食，当如是观。如是观者，抟食断知，抟食断知已，于五欲功德贪爱则断，五欲功德贪爱断者，我不见彼多闻圣弟子于五欲功德上有一结使而不断者，有一结系故，则还生此世。

How does a bhikṣu contemplate the food of contact? It is like a cow that is skinned alive. Wherever there are insects, they will feed on it. Sand and dust will cover it. Grass, wood, and thorns will pierce it. If it relies on the ground, it will be eaten by ground insects. If it relies on water, it will be eaten by water insects. If it relies on the air, it will be eaten by flying insects. Lying down or getting up, there is always suffering for this body. In this way, bhikṣu! One should contemplate the food of contact in this way. If one contemplates in this way, one will know the cessation of the food of contact. Knowing the cessation of the food of contact, the three feelings will cease. For those who have ceased the three feelings, the learned and noble disciples have nothing more to do, because what was to be done has already been done.

「云何比丘观察触食？譬如有牛，生剥其皮，在在处处，诸虫唼食，沙土坌尘，草木针刺，若依于地，地虫所食，若依于水，水虫所食，若依空中，飞虫所食，卧起常有苦毒此身。如是，比丘！于彼触食，当如是观。如是观者，触食断知，触食断知者，三受则断，三受断者，多闻圣弟子于上无所复作，所作已作故。

How does a bhikṣu contemplate the food of intention? It is like a fire that starts near a village or town, without smoke or flames. At that time, a man who is intelligent and wise, turning away from suffering and towards happiness, weary of death and desiring life, thinks like this: 'There is a great fire there, without smoke or flames. When walking or coming, I should avoid it, lest I fall into it and surely die.' Having this thought, he always gives rise to the wish to abandon and go far away. Contemplating the food of intention is also like this. If one contemplates in this way, the food of intention will cease. If the food of intention ceases, the three kinds of craving will cease. For those who have ceased the three kinds of craving, the learned and noble disciples have nothing more to do, because what was to be done has already been done.

「云何比丘观察意思食？譬如聚落城邑边有火起，无烟无炎，时有士夫聪明黠慧，背苦向乐，厌死乐生，作如是念：『彼有大火，无烟无炎，行来当避，莫令堕中，必死无疑。』作是思惟，常生思愿，舍远而去。观意思食，亦复如是。如是观者，意思食断；意思食断者，三爱则断；三爱断者，彼多闻圣弟子于上更无所作，所作已作故。

Bhikṣus! How does one contemplate the food of consciousness? It is like a king who has a guard who captures thieves and bandits, binds them, and sends them to the king..." As extensively explained in the Sūtra of Sūpātra. "Because of those causes and conditions, he experiences the suffering of being pierced by three hundred spears, suffering day and night. Contemplating the food of consciousness is also like this. If one contemplates in this way, one will know the cessation of the food of consciousness. Knowing the cessation of the food of consciousness, one will know the cessation of name and form. Knowing the cessation of name and form, the learned and noble disciples have nothing more to do, because what was to be done has already been done.

「诸比丘！云何观察识食？譬如国王，有防逻者，捉捕劫盗，缚送王所……」如前须深经广说。「以彼因缘，受三百矛苦觉，昼夜苦痛，观察识食，亦复如是。如是观者，识食断知，识食断知者，名色断知，名色断知者，多闻圣弟子于上更无所作，所作已作故。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
