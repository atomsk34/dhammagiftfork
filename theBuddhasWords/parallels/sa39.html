SĀ 39

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 39（三九） 种子

Parallel to: SN 22.54

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are five kinds of seeds. What are the five? They are root seeds, stem seeds, node seeds, self-falling seeds, and fruit seeds. These five kinds of seeds, if unbroken, undamaged, unrotten, not exposed to wind, fresh, ripe, firm, and have the earth element but lack the water element, will not grow, increase, or expand. If those seeds are fresh, ripe, firm, unbroken, undamaged, not exposed to wind, and have the water element but lack the earth element, they will also not grow, increase, or expand. If those seeds are fresh, ripe, firm, unbroken, undamaged, unrotten, not exposed to wind, and have both the earth and water elements, they will grow, increase, and expand.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有五种种子。何等为五？谓根种子、茎种子、节种子、自落种子、实种子。此五种子不断、不坏、不腐、不中风，新熟坚实，有地界而无水界，彼种子不生长增广。若彼种新熟坚实，不断、不坏、不中风，有水界而无地界，彼种子亦不生长增广。若彼种子新熟坚实，不断、不坏、不腐、不中风，有地、水界，彼种子生长增广。

Bhikṣus! Those five kinds of seeds are likened to the consciousness that takes the aggregates as its companion; the earth element is likened to the four abodes of consciousness; the water element is likened to the four grasping and clinging that are the conditions for the abode of consciousness. What are the four? Consciousness abides in form, clinging to form, moistened by the joy and craving of attachment, it grows, increases, and expands; consciousness abides in feeling, perception, and formations, clinging to feeling, perception, and formations, moistened by the joy and craving of attachment, it grows, increases, and expands. Bhikṣus! Consciousness comes, goes, abides, perishes, and grows, increases, and expands in these.

「比丘！彼五种子者，譬取阴俱识；地界者，譬四识住；水界者，譬贪喜四取攀缘识住。何等为四？于色中识住，攀缘色，喜贪润泽，生长增广；于受、想、行中识住，攀缘受、想、行，贪喜润泽，生长增广。比丘！识于中若来、若去、若住、若没、若生长增广。

"Bhikṣus! If consciousness comes, goes, abides, or is born apart from form, feeling, perception, and formations, then that is just a verbal count. When asked, one does not know, and it only increases delusion, because it is not the realm. When one is free from attachment to the form realm, one is free from attachment. Having been free from attachment, one cuts off the fetters of the mind that are bound to form. Having cut off the fetters of the mind that are bound to form, one cuts off clinging. Having cut off clinging, consciousness has no abode and does not grow, increase, or expand. When one is free from attachment to the realms of feeling, perception, and formations, one is free from attachment. Having been free from attachment, one cuts off the contact of the mind that is bound to formations. Having cut off the contact of the mind that is bound to formations, one cuts off clinging. Having cut off clinging, that consciousness has no abode and does not grow, increase, or expand. Because it does not grow, increase, or expand, one does not create formations. Having not created formations, one abides. Having abided, one is content. Having been content, one is liberated. Having been liberated, one does not grasp or cling to anything in the world. Having not grasped or clung to anything, one awakens to nirvāṇa by oneself: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and I know that I will not be subject to future lives.' I say that consciousness does not go to the east, west, south, north, the four intermediate directions, above, or below. It does not go anywhere. It only sees the Dharma, and wishes to enter nirvāṇa, peace, coolness, purity, and truth."

「比丘！若离色、受、想、行，识有若来、若去、若住、若生者，彼但有言数，问已不知，增益生痴，以非境界故。色界离贪，离贪已，于色封滞意生缚断；于色封滞意生缚断已，攀缘断；攀缘断已，识无住处，不复生长增广。受、想、行界离贪，离贪已，于行封滞意生触断；于行封滞意生触断已，攀缘断，攀缘断已，彼识无所住，不复生长增广。不生长故，不作行；不作行已住，住已知足，知足已解脱；解脱已，于诸世间都无所取、无所著；无所取、无所著已，自觉涅槃：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』我说彼识不至东、西、南、北、四维、上、下，无所至趣，唯见法，欲入涅槃、寂灭、清凉、清净、真实。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
