SĀ 41

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 41（四一） 五转

Parallel to: SN 22.56

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "There are five aggregates of clinging - the form aggregate of clinging, the feeling aggregate of clinging, the perception aggregate of clinging, the volitional formations aggregate of clinging, and the consciousness aggregate of clinging. I have five kinds of true knowledge regarding these five aggregates of clinging - true knowledge of form, true knowledge of the origin of form, true knowledge of the satisfaction in form, true knowledge of the danger in form, and true knowledge of the escape from form. Thus, true knowledge of feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness, true knowledge of the origin of consciousness, true knowledge of the satisfaction in consciousness, true knowledge of the danger in consciousness, and true knowledge of the escape from consciousness.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有五受阴——色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴。我于此五受阴，五种如实知——色如实知，色集、色味、色患、色离如实知。如是受、想、行、识如实知，识集、识味、识患、识离如实知。

What is true knowledge of form? All forms, whether the four great elements or forms derived from the four great elements, are called form. This is true knowledge of form. What is true knowledge of the origin of form? Desire and attachment to form is called the origin of form. This is true knowledge of the origin of form. What is true knowledge of the satisfaction in form? The joy and pleasure arising from form is called the satisfaction in form. This is true knowledge of the satisfaction in form. What is true knowledge of the danger in form? If form is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the danger in form. This is true knowledge of the danger in form. What is true knowledge of the escape from form? Subduing desire and attachment to form, cutting off desire and attachment, transcending desire and attachment, this is called the escape from form. This is true knowledge of the escape from form.

「云何色如实知？诸所有色，一切四大及四大造色，是名色，如是色如实知。云何色集如实知？于色喜爱，是名色集，如是色集如实知。云何色味如实知？谓色因缘生喜乐，是名色味，如是色味如实知。云何色患如实知？若色无常、苦、变易法，是名色患，如是色患如实知。云何色离如实知？若于色调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名色离，如是色离如实知。

What is true knowledge of feeling? There are six kinds of feeling: feeling arising from contact with the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is called feeling. This is true knowledge of feeling. What is true knowledge of the origin of feeling? The origin of contact is the origin of feeling. This is true knowledge of the origin of feeling. What is true knowledge of the satisfaction in feeling? The joy and pleasure arising from the six kinds of feeling is called the satisfaction in feeling. This is true knowledge of the satisfaction in feeling. What is true knowledge of the danger in feeling? If feeling is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the danger in feeling. This is true knowledge of the danger in feeling. What is true knowledge of the escape from feeling? Subduing desire and attachment to feeling, cutting off desire and attachment, transcending desire and attachment, this is called the escape from feeling. This is true knowledge of the escape from feeling.

「云何受如实知？有六受身——眼触生受，耳、鼻、舌、身、意触生受，是名受，如是受如实知。云何受集如实知？触集是受集，如是受集如实知。云何受味如实知？缘六受生喜乐，是名受味，如是受味如实知。云何受患如实知？若受无常、苦、变易法，是名受患，如是受患如实知。云何受离如实知？于受调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名受离，如是受离如实知。

What is true knowledge of perception? There are six kinds of perception: perception arising from contact with the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is called perception. This is true knowledge of perception. What is true knowledge of the origin of perception? The origin of contact is the origin of perception. This is true knowledge of the origin of perception. What is true knowledge of the satisfaction in perception? The joy and pleasure arising from the six kinds of perception is called the satisfaction in perception. This is true knowledge of the satisfaction in perception. What is true knowledge of the danger in perception? If perception is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the danger in perception. This is true knowledge of the danger in perception. What is true knowledge of the escape from perception? Subduing desire and attachment to perception, cutting off desire and attachment, transcending desire and attachment, this is called the escape from perception. This is true knowledge of the escape from perception.

「云何想如实知？谓六想身。云何为六？谓眼触生想，耳、鼻、舌、身、意触生想，是名想，如是想如实知。云何想集如实知？谓触集是想集，如是想集如实知。云何想味如实知？想因缘生喜乐，是名想味，如是想味如实知。云何想患如实知？谓想无常、苦、变易法，是名想患，如是想患如实知。云何想离如实知？若于想调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名想离，如是想离如实知。

What is true knowledge of volitional formations? There are six kinds of volitional formations: volitional formations arising from contact with the eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is called volitional formations. This is true knowledge of volitional formations. What is true knowledge of the origin of volitional formations? The origin of contact is the origin of volitional formations. This is true knowledge of the origin of volitional formations. What is true knowledge of the satisfaction in volitional formations? The joy and pleasure arising from the six kinds of volitional formations is called the satisfaction in volitional formations. This is true knowledge of the satisfaction in volitional formations. What is true knowledge of the danger in volitional formations? If volitional formations are impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the danger in volitional formations. This is true knowledge of the danger in volitional formations. What is true knowledge of the escape from volitional formations? Subduing desire and attachment to volitional formations, cutting off desire and attachment, transcending desire and attachment, this is called the escape from volitional formations. This is true knowledge of the escape from volitional formations.

「云何行如实知？谓六思身——眼触生思，耳、鼻、舌、身、意触生思，是名为行，如是行如实知。云何行集如实知？触集是行集，如是行集如实知。云何行味如实知？谓行因缘生喜乐，是名行味，如是行味如实知。云何行患如实知？若行无常、苦、变易法，是名行患，如是行患如实知。云何行离如实知？若行调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名行离，如是行离如实知。

What is true knowledge of consciousness? There are six kinds of consciousness: eye consciousness, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind consciousness. This is called consciousness. This is true knowledge of consciousness. What is true knowledge of the origin of consciousness? The origin of name and form is the origin of consciousness. This is true knowledge of the origin of consciousness. What is true knowledge of the satisfaction in consciousness? The joy and pleasure arising from the six kinds of consciousness is called the satisfaction in consciousness. This is true knowledge of the satisfaction in consciousness. What is true knowledge of the danger in consciousness? If consciousness is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the danger in consciousness. This is true knowledge of the danger in consciousness. What is true knowledge of the escape from consciousness? Subduing desire and attachment to consciousness, cutting off desire and attachment, transcending desire and attachment, this is called the escape from consciousness. This is true knowledge of the escape from consciousness.

「云何识如实知？谓六识身——眼识身，耳、鼻、舌、身、意识身，是名为识身，如是识身如实知。云何识集如实知？谓名色集，是名识集，如是识集如实知。云何识味如实知？识因缘生喜乐，是名识味，如是识味如实知。云何识患如实知？若识无常、苦、变易法，是名识患，如是识患如实知。云何识离如实知？谓于识调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名识离，如是识离如实知。

Bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu knows and sees form thus, he is said to be one who has gone forth to the right. If he has gone forth to the right, I declare that he has entered. The same applies to feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness. If a bhikkhu knows and sees form as it really is, he becomes disenchanted with form, becomes dispassionate toward form, and is liberated from form. If he is liberated, he is said to be one who abides in purity. If he abides in purity, he is said to have established the holy life. If he has established the holy life, he is said to have gone beyond the control of others. This is the end of suffering.

「比丘！若沙门、婆罗门于色如是知、如是见；如是知、如是见，离欲向，是名正向。若正向者，我说彼入。受、想、行、识亦复如是。若沙门、婆罗门于色如实知、如实见，于色生厌、离欲，不起诸漏，心得解脱；若心得解脱者，则为纯一；纯一者，则梵行立；梵行立者，离他自在，是名苦边。受、想、行、识亦复如是。」

Having spoken this discourse, the Buddha said to the monks, "The monks heard what the Buddha said and joyfully put it into practice."

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
