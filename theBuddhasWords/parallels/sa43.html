SĀ 43

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 43（四三） 取著

Parallel to: MN 138 / SN 22.7 / SĀ 66

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Because of grasping, there is attachment. If one does not grasp, there is no attachment. Listen carefully and consider it well, I will explain it to you."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「取故生著，不取则不著。谛听，善思，当为汝说。」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "Yes, we are willing to receive the teaching!"

比丘白佛：「唯然，受教！」

The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "How does grasping lead to attachment? Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people see form as 'me,' 'other,' or 'in between,' and they see form as 'me' or 'mine' and grasp it. When that form changes or becomes different, their minds also follow and change. When their minds follow and change, they also give rise to grasping and clinging, and their minds abide in grasping. Because their minds abide in grasping, they give rise to fear, obstruction, and mental confusion due to grasping. Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people see feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness as 'me,' 'other,' or 'in between,' and they see consciousness as 'me' or 'mine' and grasp it. When that consciousness changes or becomes different, their minds follow and change. Because their minds follow and change, they give rise to grasping and clinging, and their minds abide in grasping. Abiding in grasping, they give rise to fear, obstruction, and mental confusion due to grasping. This is called grasping and clinging.

佛告比丘：「云何取故生著？愚痴无闻凡夫于色见是我、异我、相在，见色是我、我所而取；取已，彼色若变、若异，心亦随转；心随转已，亦生取著摄受心住；摄受心住故，则生恐怖、障碍、心乱，以取著故。愚痴无闻凡夫于受、想、行、识，见我、异我、相在，见识是我、我所而取；取已，彼识若变、若异，彼心随转；心随转故，则生取著摄受心住；住已，则生恐怖、障碍、心乱，以取著故，是名取著。

What is meant by not grasping and not being attached? The well-versed noble disciples do not see form as 'me,' 'other,' or 'in between.' They do not see form as 'me' or 'mine' and grasp it. Not seeing 'me' or 'mine' and grasping form, when that form changes or becomes different, their minds do not follow and change. Because their minds do not follow and change, they do not give rise to grasping and clinging, and their minds abide in non-grasping. Not abiding in grasping, they do not give rise to fear, obstruction, or mental confusion due to non-grasping. This is called not grasping and not being attached.

「云何名不取不著？多闻圣弟子于色不见我、异我、相在，于色不见我、我所而取；不见我、我所而取色，彼色若变、若异，心不随转；心不随转故，不生取著摄受心住；不摄受住故，则不生恐怖、障碍、心乱，不取著故。如是受、想、行、识，不见我、异我、相在，不见我、我所而取。彼识若变、若异，心不随转；心不随转故，不取著摄受心住；不摄受心住故，心不恐怖、障碍、心乱，以不取著故，是名不取著。是名取著、不取著。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus were delighted and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
