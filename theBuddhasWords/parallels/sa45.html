SĀ 45

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 45（四五） 觉

Parallel to: SN 22.47 / SĀ 63

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There are five aggregates of grasping. What are the five? The form aggregate of grasping, the feeling aggregate, the perception aggregate, the volitional formations aggregate, and the consciousness aggregate. If any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas see a self, they all see it in these five aggregates of grasping. The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas see form as the self, the self as different from form, the self as in form, or form as in the self; they see feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness as the self, the self as different from consciousness, the self as in consciousness, or consciousness as in the self. Because of ignorance, foolish and ignorant ordinary people see form as the self, different from the self, or as having the characteristics of the self, and say, 'This is true, I will not abandon it.' Because they do not abandon it, their faculties grow; when their faculties grow, they increase their contacts; because they are touched by the six sense bases, foolish and ignorant ordinary people give rise to the perception of suffering and happiness. What are the six? They are the sense base of eye contact, the sense base of ear contact, the sense base of nose contact, the sense base of tongue contact, the sense base of body contact, and the sense base of mind contact.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有五受阴。云何为五？色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴。若诸沙门、婆罗门见有我者，一切皆于此五受阴见我。诸沙门、婆罗门见色是我，色异我，我在色，色在我；见受、想、行、识是我，识异我，我在识，识在我。愚痴无闻凡夫以无明故，见色是我、异我、相在，言我真实不舍；以不舍故，诸根增长；诸根长已，增诸触；六触入处所触故，愚痴无闻凡夫起苦乐觉，从触入处起。何等为六？谓眼触入处，耳、鼻、舌、身、意触入处。

"Thus, bhikṣus, there are the realms of intention, phenomena, and ignorance. Because of ignorant contact, foolish and ignorant ordinary people give rise to the perceptions of existence, non-existence, both existence and non-existence, superiority, equality, inferiority, knowledge, and views. These perceptions and views arise from the six sense bases. The well-versed noble disciples abandon ignorance and give rise to wisdom regarding these six sense bases. They do not give rise to the perceptions of existence, non-existence, both existence and non-existence, superiority, equality, inferiority, knowledge, and views. Having known and seen thus, the ignorant contact that arose previously ceases, and the contact of wisdom arises."

「如是，比丘！有意界、法界、无明界，愚痴无闻凡夫无明触故，起有觉、无觉、有无觉、我胜觉、我等觉、我卑觉、我知我见觉。如是知、如是见觉，皆由六触入故。多闻圣弟子于此六触入处，舍离无明而生明，不生有觉、无觉、有无觉、胜觉、等觉、卑觉、我知我见觉。如是知、如是见已，先所起无明触灭，后明触觉起。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
