SĀ 46

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 46（四六） 三世阴世食

Parallel to: SN 22.79

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "There are five aggregates of grasping. What are the five? They are the aggregates of grasping form, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas use their knowledge of past lives to recognize various past lives, whether they have recognized, will recognize, or are recognizing them, it is all within these five aggregates of grasping. They recognize, will recognize, or are recognizing that I have gone through such forms, such feelings, such perceptions, such mental formations, and such consciousness in the past. If they can obstruct and divide them, this is called the aggregate of grasping form. What can obstruct it? It can be obstructed by the hand, a stone, a stick, a knife, cold, warmth, thirst, hunger, mosquitoes, gadflies, poisonous insects, wind, and rain. This is called obstruction by contact. Therefore, obstruction is the aggregate of grasping form. Furthermore, this aggregate of grasping form is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change. The characteristics of feeling are the aggregate of grasping feeling. What does it feel? It feels suffering, pleasure, and neither suffering nor pleasure. Therefore, the characteristics of feeling are the aggregate of grasping feeling. Furthermore, this aggregate of grasping feeling is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change. The characteristics of perception are the aggregate of grasping perception. What does it perceive? It perceives little, much, immeasurable, and completely non-existent. Therefore, the characteristics of perception are the aggregate of grasping perception. Furthermore, this aggregate of grasping perception is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change. The characteristics of mental formations are the aggregate of grasping mental formations. What does it form? It forms form, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness. Therefore, the characteristics of mental formations are the aggregate of grasping mental formations. Furthermore, this aggregate of grasping mental formations is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change. The characteristics of consciousness are the aggregate of grasping consciousness. What does it cognize? It cognizes form, sound, smell, taste, touch, and dharmas. Therefore, the characteristics of consciousness are the aggregate of grasping consciousness. Furthermore, this aggregate of grasping consciousness is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有五受阴。云何为五？色、受、想、行、识受阴。若沙门、婆罗门以宿命智自识种种宿命，已识、当识、今识，皆于此五受阴；已识、当识、今识，我过去所经。如是色、如是受、如是想、如是行、如是识。若可阂可分，是名色受阴。指所阂，若手、若石、若杖、若刀、若冷、若暖、若渴、若饥、若蚊、虻、诸毒虫、风、雨触，是名触阂，是故阂是色受阴。复以此色受阴无常、苦、变易。诸觉相是受受阴，何所觉？觉苦、觉乐、觉不苦不乐，是故名觉相是受受阴。复以此受受阴是无常、苦、变易。诸想是想受阴，何所想？少想、多想、无量想、都无所有作无所有想，是故名想受阴。复以此想受阴是无常、苦、变易法。为作相是行受阴，何所为作？于色为作，于受、想、行、识为作，是故为作相是行受阴。复以此行受阴是无常、苦、变易法。别知相是识受阴，何所识？识色，识声、香、味、触、法，是故名识受阴。复以此识受阴是无常、苦、变易法。

Bhikṣus, those well-versed noble disciples study the aggregate of grasping form in this way: 'I am now being consumed by present form, just as I was consumed by past form.' They further think: 'I am now being consumed by present form. If I were to delight in and cling to future form, I would also be consumed by that form, just as I am now.' Having understood this, they do not concern themselves with past form, do not delight in future form, and give rise to disgust, detachment, cessation, and inclination towards cessation with regard to present form. The well-versed noble disciples study the aggregates of grasping feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness in this way: 'I am now being consumed by present consciousness, just as I was consumed by past consciousness. I am now being consumed by present consciousness. If I were to delight in and cling to future consciousness, I would also be consumed by that consciousness, just as I am now.' Having understood this, they do not concern themselves with past consciousness, do not delight in future consciousness, and give rise to disgust, detachment, cessation, and inclination towards cessation with regard to present consciousness. They cease and do not increase, retreat and do not advance, cease and do not arise, abandon and do not take.

「诸比丘！彼多闻圣弟子于此色受阴作如是学：『我今为现在色所食，过去世已曾为彼色所食，如今现在。』复作是念：『我今为现在色所食，我若复乐著未来色者，当复为彼色所食，如今现在。』作如是知已，不顾过去色，不乐著未来色，于现在色生厌、离欲、灭患、向灭。多闻圣弟子于此受、想、行、识受阴学：『我今现在为现在识所食，于过去世已曾为识所食，如今现在。我今已为现在识所食，若复乐著未来识者，亦当复为彼识所食，如今现在。』如是知已，不顾过去识，不乐未来识，于现在识生厌、离欲、灭患、向灭，灭而不增，退而不进，灭而不起，舍而不取。

What ceases and does not increase? Form ceases and does not increase, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness cease and do not increase. What retreats and does not advance? Form retreats and does not advance, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness retreat and do not advance. What ceases and does not arise? Form ceases and does not arise, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness cease and do not arise. What is abandoned and not taken? Form is abandoned and not taken, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness are abandoned and not taken.

「于何灭而不增？色灭而不增，受、想、行、识灭而不增。于何退而不进？色退而不进，受、想、行、识退而不进。于何灭而不起？色灭而不起，受、想、行、识灭而不起。于何舍而不取？色舍而不取，受、想、行、识舍而不取。

"Ceased and not increasing, abiding in quiescent cessation; retreated and not advancing, abiding in quiescent retreat; ceased and not arising, abiding in quiescent cessation; abandoned and not taken, not giving rise to attachment. Having no attachment, one realizes nirvāṇa by oneself: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, I know no more of any future existence.'"

「灭而不增，寂灭而住；退而不进，寂退而住；灭而不起，寂灭而住；舍而不取，不生系著；不系著已，自觉涅槃：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

When the Buddha spoke this sūtra, many bhikṣus were free from defilements and attained liberation.

佛说此经时，众多比丘不起诸漏，心得解脱。

Having spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

I, low, seed, obstruction, five turns, seven, two bonds
attachment, and awakening, three periods of time, aggregates, world food,

我、卑下、种子　　封滞、五转、七二系著及觉　　三世阴世食
