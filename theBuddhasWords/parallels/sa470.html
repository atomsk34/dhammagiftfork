SĀ 470

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 470（四七〇） 箭

Parallel to: SN 36.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the monks, "Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people experience the feelings of suffering, happiness, and neither suffering nor happiness. Well-learned noble disciples also experience the feelings of suffering, happiness, and neither suffering nor happiness. Monks, what is the difference between ordinary people and noble ones?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「愚痴无闻凡夫生苦乐受、不苦不乐受，多闻圣弟子亦生苦乐受、不苦不乐受。诸比丘！凡夫、圣人有何差别？」

The monks said to the Buddha, "The World-Honored One is the root of the Dharma, the eye of the Dharma, and the reliance of the Dharma. Excellent! World-Honored One! Please explain in detail. The monks will listen and accept it with respect."

诸比丘白佛：「世尊是法根、法眼、法依。善哉！世尊！唯愿广说，诸比丘闻已，当受奉行。」

The Buddha told the monks, "Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people experience various feelings arising from bodily contact, which are painful and oppressive, even life-threatening, causing sorrow, crying, and lamenting."

佛告诸比丘：「愚痴无闻凡夫身触生诸受，苦痛逼迫，乃至夺命，忧愁啼哭，称怨号呼。」

The Buddha told the monks, "Listen carefully and think well, I will explain for you. Monks! Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people experience various feelings arising from bodily contact, which increase suffering and pain, even life-threatening, causing sorrow, crying, and lamenting. At that time, they increase two kinds of feelings, bodily feelings and mental feelings.

佛告诸比丘：「谛听，善思，当为汝说。诸比丘！愚痴无闻凡夫身触生诸受，增诸苦痛，乃至夺命，愁忧称怨，啼哭号呼，心生狂乱，当于尔时，增长二受，若身受、若心受。

It is like a person who is struck by two poisonous arrows, experiencing extreme pain. Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people are also like this. They increase two kinds of feelings, bodily feelings and mental feelings, and experience extreme pain. Why is this? Because those ignorant and unlearned ordinary people do not understand. They experience pleasant feelings arising from the five desires, and enjoy the pleasures of the five desires. Because of enjoying the pleasures of the five desires, they are bound by the fetter of craving. Due to experiencing painful feelings, they give rise to anger. Because of giving rise to anger, they are bound by the fetter of hatred. They do not truly understand the arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and detachment from these two kinds of feelings. Because they do not truly understand, they experience neither-pleasant-nor-unpleasant feelings, and are bound by the fetter of delusion. They are bound by pleasant feelings and will never be free from them, bound by painful feelings and will never be free from them, and bound by neither-pleasant-nor-unpleasant feelings and will never be free from them. How are they bound? They are bound by craving, hatred, and delusion, and bound by birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress.

「譬如士夫身被双毒箭，极生苦痛，愚痴无闻凡夫亦复如是。增长二受，身受、心受，极生苦痛。所以者何？以彼愚痴无闻凡夫不了知故，于诸五欲生乐受触，受五欲乐，受五欲乐故，为贪使所使；苦受触故，则生瞋恚，生瞋恚故，为恚使所使。于此二受，若集、若灭、若味、若患、若离不如实知；不如实知故，生不苦不乐受，为痴使所使。为乐受所系终不离，苦受所系终不离，不苦不乐受所系终不离。云何系？谓为贪、恚、痴所系，为生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦所系。

The well-taught noble disciple experiences painful feelings arising from bodily contact, great suffering and oppression, even life-threatening, but does not give rise to sorrow, lamentation, resentment, or crying. At that time, they only experience one kind of feeling, namely bodily feeling, and do not experience mental feeling.

「多闻圣弟子身触生苦受，大苦逼迫，乃至夺命，不起忧悲称怨、啼哭号呼、心乱发狂，当于尔时，唯生一受，所谓身受，不生心受。

It is like a person who is struck by one poisonous arrow, and not by a second. At that time, they only experience one kind of feeling, namely bodily feeling, and do not experience mental feeling. They experience pleasant feelings arising from contact, but are not attached to pleasant feelings, and are not attached to pleasant feelings, they are not bound by the fetter of craving for those pleasant feelings. They do not give rise to anger towards painful feelings arising from contact, and do not give rise to anger, they are not bound by the fetter of hatred. They truly understand the arising, cessation, taste, suffering, and detachment from those two fetters. Because they truly understand, they are not bound by the fetter of delusion towards neither-pleasant-nor-unpleasant feelings. They are liberated from pleasant feelings and are not bound, liberated from painful feelings and are not bound, and liberated from neither-pleasant-nor-unpleasant feelings and are not bound. From what are they liberated and not bound? They are liberated from craving, hatred, and delusion and are not bound, liberated from birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress and are not bound.

「譬如士夫被一毒箭，不被第二毒箭，当于尔时，唯生一受，所谓身受，不生心受。为乐受触，不染欲乐，不染欲乐故，于彼乐受，贪使不使。于苦触受不生瞋恚，不生瞋恚故，恚使不使。于彼二使，集、灭、味、患、离如实知，如实知故，不苦不乐受痴使不使，于彼乐受解脱不系，苦受、不苦不乐受解脱不系。于何不系？谓贪、恚、痴不系，生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦不系。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

The learned one experiences both pleasure and pain, but does not experience
the perception of feeling. For the ordinary person, this is indeed great
learning. Not being negligent towards pleasant feelings, and not increasing sorrow towards
painful contacts, abandoning both pleasure and pain, not following or opposing. The
bhikṣu diligently applies himself, with right knowledge unshakable. Towards all these feelings
the wise one is able to understand. By understanding all feelings, in
this life all taints are exhausted. At death, one does not fall
into the cycle of birth and death, and abides forever in parinirvāṇa.

「多闻于苦乐，　　非不受觉知，彼于凡夫人，　　其实大有闻。乐受不放逸，　　苦触不增忧，苦乐二俱舍，　　不顺亦不违。比丘勤方便，　　正智不倾动，于此一切受，　　黠慧能了知。了知诸受故，　　现法尽诸漏，身死不堕数，　　永处般涅槃。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
