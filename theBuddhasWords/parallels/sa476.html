SĀ 476

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 476（四七六） 禅思

Parallel to: SN 36.15 (~) / SN 36.16 (~) / SN 36.17–18 (~) / SN 36.23

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, a certain bhikṣu was alone in a quiet place, contemplating and observing feelings. He wondered, "What are feelings? What is the origin of feelings? What is the cessation of feelings? What is the path leading to the cessation of feelings? What is the taste of feelings? What are the drawbacks of feelings? What is the escape from feelings?"

尔时，有异比丘独一静处禅思。如是观察诸受。云何受？云何受集？云何受灭？云何受集道迹？云何受灭道迹？云何受味？云何受患？云何受离？

Then the bhikṣu, having emerged from meditation, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I was alone in a quiet place, contemplating and observing feelings. What are feelings? What is the origin of feelings? What is the cessation of feelings? What is the path leading to the cessation of feelings? What is the taste of feelings? What are the drawbacks of feelings? What is the escape from feelings?"

时，彼比丘从禅觉已，诣世尊所，稽首礼足，退住一面，白佛言：「世尊！我独一静处禅思，观察诸受。云何为受？云何受集？云何受灭？云何受集道迹？云何受灭道迹？云何受味？云何受患？云何受离？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "There are three kinds of feelings: pleasant feelings, painful feelings, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feelings. The origin of feelings is the origin of contact. The cessation of feelings is the cessation of contact. If one delights in, praises, is attached to, and clings to feelings, this is called the path leading to the origin of feelings. If one does not delight in, praise, be attached to, and cling to feelings, this is called the path leading to the cessation of feelings. If feelings give rise to joy and happiness, this is called the taste of feelings. If feelings are impermanent and subject to change, this is called the drawbacks of feelings. If one cuts off desire and craving for feelings, and goes beyond desire and craving, this is called the escape from feelings."

佛告比丘：「有三受——乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受。触集是受集，触灭是受灭。若于受爱乐、赞叹、染著、坚住，是名受集道迹。若于受不爱乐、赞叹、染著、坚住，是名受灭道迹。若受因缘生乐喜，是名受味，若受无常变易法，是名受患。若于受断欲贪、越欲贪，是名受离。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "Good, good! The bhikṣus have spoken well. The bhikṣus should all practice in this way." Having heard the Buddha's words, the bhikṣus rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
