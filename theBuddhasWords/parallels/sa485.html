SĀ 485

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 485（四八五） 优陀夷

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, King Bimbisāra went to Venerable Udayin, bowed his head in respect, and sat to one side. King Bimbisāra then asked Venerable Udayin, "What are the various feelings spoken of by the World-Honored One?"

尔时，瓶沙王诣尊者优陀夷所，稽首作礼，退坐一面。时，瓶沙王白尊者优陀夷言：「云何世尊所说诸受？」

Udayin said, "Great King! The World-Honored One speaks of three feelings - pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling."

优陀夷言：「大王！世尊说三受——乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受。」

King Bimbisāra said to Venerable Udayin, "Do not say that the World-Honored One speaks of three feelings - pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. There should only be two feelings - pleasant feeling and painful feeling. If there is neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, then it is cessation." Thus he spoke three times, but Udayin could not establish the three feelings for the king, and the king could not establish the two feelings. They both went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads in respect, and withdrew to one side.

瓶沙王白尊者优陀夷：「莫作是言：『世尊说三受——乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受。』正应有二受——乐受、苦受。若不苦不乐受，是则寂灭。」如是三说，优陀夷不能为王立三受，王亦不能立二受，俱共诣佛所，稽首礼足，退住一面。

At that time, Venerable Udayin extensively informed the World-Honored One with what he had said earlier, "I also cannot establish the three feelings, and the king also cannot establish the two feelings. Now we have come together to ask the World-Honored One about this meaning. How many feelings are there, for certain?"

时，尊者优陀夷以先所说，广白世尊：「我亦不能立三受，王亦不能立二受，今故共来具问世尊如是之义，定有几受？」

The Buddha told Udayin, "Sometimes I speak of one feeling, sometimes I speak of two feelings, sometimes three, four, five, six, eighteen, thirty-six, up to one hundred and eight feelings, sometimes I speak of immeasurable feelings.

佛告优陀夷：「我有时说一受，或时说二受，或说三、四、五、六、十八、三十六，乃至百八受，或时说无量受。

How do I speak of one feeling? It is like saying that all feelings are suffering. This is called speaking of one feeling. How do I speak of two feelings? Speaking of physical feelings and mental feelings, this is called two feelings. How do I speak of three feelings? Pleasant feelings, painful feelings, and neither-pleasant-nor-painful feelings. How do I speak of four feelings? Namely, feelings bound to the desire realm, feelings bound to the form realm, feelings bound to the formless realm, and unattached feelings. How do I speak of five feelings? Namely, the faculty of pleasure, the faculty of joy, the faculty of pain, the faculty of distress, and the faculty of equanimity. This is called speaking of five feelings. How do I speak of six feelings? Namely, feelings arising from eye contact, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind contact. How do I speak of eighteen feelings? Namely, feelings following six joyful activities, six sorrowful activities, and six equanimous activities. This is called speaking of eighteen feelings. How do I speak of thirty-six feelings? Namely, six attachments to joy, six detachments from joy, six attachments to sorrow, six detachments from sorrow, six attachments to equanimity, and six detachments from equanimity. This is called speaking of thirty-six feelings. How do I speak of one hundred and eight feelings? Namely, thirty-six past feelings, thirty-six future feelings, and thirty-six present feelings. This is called speaking of one hundred and eight feelings. How do I speak of immeasurable feelings? It is like saying 'this feeling, that feeling,' and so on. Such immeasurable names are called speaking of immeasurable feelings.

「云何我说一受？如说所有受，皆悉是苦，是名我说一受。云何说二受？说身受、心受，是名二受。云何三受？乐受、苦受、不苦不乐受。云何四受？谓欲界系受、色界系受、无色界系受，及不系受。云何说五受？谓乐根、喜根、苦根、忧根、舍根，是名说五受。云何说六受？谓眼触生受。耳、鼻、舌、身、意触生受。云何说十八受？谓随六喜行、随六忧行、随六舍行受，是名说十八受。云何三十六受，依六贪著喜、依六离贪著喜、依六贪著忧、依六离贪著忧、依六贪著舍、依六离贪著舍，是名说三十六受。云何说百八受？谓三十六受，过去三十六、未来三十六、现在三十六，是名说百八受。云何说无量受？如说此受彼受等，比如是无量名说，是名说无量受。

Udayin, I speak of feelings in various ways according to the truth. But the world does not understand, and so they argue and contradict each other, never reaching the true meaning of my Dharma and Vinaya to bring themselves to rest. Udayin, if one truly understands the meaning of my various teachings on feelings, one will not give rise to arguments and contradictions. For arising and unarisen disputes, one can use the Dharma and Vinaya to stop them and bring them to rest.

「优陀夷！我如是种种说受如实义，世间不解，故而共诤论，共相违反，终竟不得我法、律中真实之义以自止息。优陀夷！若于我此所说种种受义，如实解知者，不起诤论、共相违反，起、未起诤能以法、律止令休息。

Udayin, there are two kinds of feelings: the feeling of desire and the feeling of detachment. What is the feeling of desire? It is the feeling that arises from the five sense pleasures. This is called the feeling of desire. What is the feeling of detachment? It is when a monk, detached from desire and evil and unwholesome states, with initial and sustained thought, dwells in the joy and bliss born of detachment, fully endowed with the first absorption. This is called the feeling of detachment.

「然，优陀夷！有二受，欲受、离欲受。云何欲受，五欲功德因缘生受，是名欲受。云何离欲受？谓比丘离欲、恶不善法，有觉有观，离生喜乐，初禅具足住，是名离欲受。

If someone says that sentient beings rely on this first absorption, it is only for pleasure and not for anything else, that is not so. Why? Because there is a superior pleasure that surpasses this. What is it? It is when a monk, detached from initial and sustained thought, with inner purity, gives rise to joy and bliss, fully endowed with the second absorption. This is called superior pleasure. In this way, up to the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, it is said to be increasingly superior.

「若有说言众生依此初禅，唯是为乐非余者，此则不然。所以者何？更有胜乐过于此故，何者是？谓比丘离有觉有观，内净，定生喜、乐，第二禅具足住，是名胜乐。如是，乃至非想非非想入处，转转胜说。

If someone says that only this place, up to the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, is supremely blissful and not the others, that is also not so. Why? Because there is a superior bliss that surpasses this. What is it? It is when a monk, transcending the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, with the cessation of perception and feeling, fully abides in the body's realization of it. This is called the superior bliss that surpasses it.

「若有说言：『唯有此处，乃至非想非非想极乐非余。』亦复不然。所以者何？更有胜乐过于此故，何者是？谓比丘度一切非想非非想入处，想受灭，身作证具足住，是名胜乐过于彼者。

If a non-Buddhist renunciant says, 'The Śākya monk only speaks of the cessation of perception and feeling as supreme bliss,' that is not so. Why? One should say to them, 'This is not the number of blissful feelings spoken by the World-Honored One. The number of blissful feelings spoken by the World-Honored One is as follows':

「若有异学出家作是说言：『沙门释种子唯说想受灭，名为至乐。』此所不应。所以者何？应当语言：『此非世尊所说受乐数，世尊说受乐数者，如说。』

"Udayin, there are four kinds of bliss. What are the four? They are the bliss of detachment, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of tranquility, and the bliss of enlightenment."

「优陀夷！有四种乐。何等为四？谓离欲乐、远离乐、寂灭乐、菩提乐。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha, the Venerable Udayin, and King Bimbisāra, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说此经已，尊者优陀夷及瓶沙王闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
