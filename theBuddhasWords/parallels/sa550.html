SĀ 550

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 550（五五〇） 迦旃延

Parallel to: AN 6.26

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Thus have I heard:

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时，尊者摩诃迦旃延在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At one time, the Buddha was staying at Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. At that time, Venerable Mahākātyāyana was also staying at Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. Venerable Mahākātyāyana said to the bhikṣus, "The World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, knows and sees the six dharmas that lead out of the realm of suffering and ascend to the realm of excellence, and he teaches the one vehicle path to purify all beings, to be free from all afflictions, sorrows, and laments, and to attain the true Dharma. What are the six? They are: the noble disciple recollects the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, who practices the pure Dharma. The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the one who has accomplished the goal, the one who is well gone, the knower of the world, the supreme charioteer, the unsurpassed trainer of those who can be trained, the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One. Because the noble disciple recollects the Tathāgata's practice of the pure Dharma, he is free from the perception of desire, free from the perception of anger, and free from the perception of harm. Thus, the noble disciple is free from a defiled mind. What is a defiled mind? It refers to the five sense pleasures. Being free from desire, anger, and delusion towards these five sense pleasures, one abides in right mindfulness and right knowledge, rides the straight path, cultivates mindfulness of the Buddha, and heads straight for nirvāṇa. This is called the first teaching known and seen by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, which leads out of the realm of suffering and ascends to the realm of excellence, and which purifies beings through the one vehicle path, frees them from suffering and affliction, and extinguishes sorrows and laments, enabling them to attain the true Dharma.

尊者摩诃迦旃延语诸比丘：「佛世尊、如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说六法出苦处升于胜处，说一乘道净诸众生，离诸恼苦，忧悲悉灭，得真如法。何等为六？谓圣弟子念如来、应、等正觉所行法净，如来、应、等正觉、明行足、善逝、世间解、无上士、调御丈夫、天人师、佛世尊。圣弟子念如来、应所行法故，离贪欲觉、离瞋恚觉、离害觉。如是，圣弟子出染著心。何等为染著心？谓五欲功德，于此五欲功德离贪、恚、痴，安住正念正智，乘于直道，修习念佛，正向涅槃，是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说第一出苦处升于胜处，一乘道净于众生，离苦恼，灭忧悲，得如实法。

Furthermore, the noble disciple recollects the true Dharma and the World-Honored One's present Dharma and Vinaya, free from all afflictions, not in vain, and directly realized in this very life. At that time, when the noble disciple recollects the true Dharma, he does not give rise to the perception of desire, the perception of anger, or the perception of harm. Thus, the noble disciple is free from a defiled mind. What is a defiled mind? It refers to the five sense pleasures. Being free from desire, anger, and delusion towards these five sense pleasures, one abides in right mindfulness and right knowledge, rides the straight path, cultivates mindfulness of the Dharma, and heads straight for nirvāṇa. This is called the second teaching known and seen by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, which leads out of the realm of suffering and ascends to the realm of excellence, and which purifies beings through the one vehicle path, frees them from suffering and affliction, and extinguishes sorrows and laments, enabling them to attain the true Dharma.

「复次，圣弟子念于正法，念于世尊现法、律，离诸热恼，非时，通达，即于现法，缘自觉悟。尔时，圣弟子念此正法时，不起欲觉、瞋恚、害觉。如是，圣弟子出染著心。何等为染著心？谓五欲功德。于此五欲功德离贪、恚、痴，安住正念正知，乘于直道，修习念法，正向涅槃，是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说第二出苦处升于胜处，一乘道净于众生，离苦恼，灭忧悲，得如实法。

Furthermore, the noble disciple recollects the Saṅgha of the Dharma, which is well-directed, rightly-directed, straight-directed, and equally-directed, practicing in accordance with it, namely, those who are directed towards the stream-enterer and attain the fruit of stream-entry, those who are directed towards the once-returner and attain the fruit of once-return, those who are directed towards the non-returner and attain the fruit of non-return, and those who are directed towards the arhat and attain the fruit of arahantship. These are the eight persons in four pairs. This is called the Saṅgha of the World-Honored One's disciples, which is endowed with morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation, worthy of offerings, respect, and homage, the unsurpassed field of merit in the world. When the noble disciple recollects the Saṅgha in this way, at that time the noble disciple does not give rise to the perception of desire, the perception of anger, or the perception of harm. Thus, the noble disciple is free from a defiled mind. What is a defiled mind? It refers to the five sense pleasures. Being free from desire, anger, and delusion towards these five sense pleasures, one abides in right mindfulness and right knowledge, rides the straight path, cultivates mindfulness of the Saṅgha, and heads straight for nirvāṇa. This is called the third teaching known and seen by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, which leads out of the realm of suffering and ascends to the realm of excellence, and which purifies beings through the one vehicle path, frees them from suffering and affliction, and extinguishes sorrows and laments, enabling them to attain the true Dharma.

「复次，圣弟子念于僧法，善向、正向、直向、等向，修随顺行，谓向须陀洹、得须陀洹果，向斯陀含、得斯陀含，向阿那含、得阿那含，向阿罗汉、得阿罗汉。如是四双八士，是名世尊弟子僧戒具足、定具足、慧具足、解脱具足、解脱知见具足，供养、恭敬、礼拜处，世间无上福田。圣弟子如是念僧时，尔时圣弟子不起欲觉、瞋恚、害觉。如是，圣弟子出染著心。何等为染著心？谓五欲功德。于此五欲功德离贪、恚、痴，安住正念正知，乘于直道，修习念僧，正向涅槃，是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说第三出苦处升于胜处，一乘道净于众生，离苦恼，灭忧悲，得如实法。

Furthermore, the noble disciple recollects the virtue of morality, recollecting morality that is unbroken, uninterrupted, pure, not apart from, not stolen, well-completed, praiseworthy, and not detested by the practice of purity. When the noble disciple recollects this morality, he recollects the morality accomplished within himself. At that time, he does not give rise to the perception of desire, the perception of anger, or the perception of harm. Thus, the noble disciple is free from a defiled mind. What is a defiled mind? It refers to the five sense pleasures. Being free from desire, anger, and delusion towards these five sense pleasures, one abides in right mindfulness and right knowledge, rides the straight path, cultivates mindfulness of morality, and heads straight for nirvāṇa. This is called the fourth teaching known and seen by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, which leads out of the realm of suffering and ascends to the realm of excellence, and which purifies beings through the one vehicle path, frees them from suffering and affliction, and extinguishes sorrows and laments, enabling them to attain the true Dharma.

「复次，圣弟子念于戒德，念不缺戒、不断戒、纯厚戒、不离戒、非盗取戒、善究竟戒、可赞叹戒、梵行不憎恶戒。若圣弟子念此戒时，自念身中所成就戒，当于尔时不起欲觉、瞋恚、害觉。如是，圣弟子出染著心。何等为染著心？谓五欲功德。于此五欲功德离贪、恚、痴，安住正念正知，乘于直道，修戒念，正向涅槃，是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说第四出苦处升于胜处，一乘道净于众生，离苦恼，灭忧悲，得如实法。

Furthermore, the noble disciple recollects the Dharma of generosity, rejoicing in his own generosity. I am now free from the defilement of stinginess and greed, free from living in a household, and I give with a liberated mind, always give, give up, give with joy, give fully, and give equally. When the noble disciple recollects the Dharma of generosity accomplished by himself, he does not give rise to the perception of desire, the perception of anger, or the perception of harm. Thus, the noble disciple is free from a defiled mind. What is a defiled mind? It refers to the five sense pleasures. Being free from desire, anger, and delusion towards these five sense pleasures, one abides in right mindfulness and right knowledge, rides the straight path, cultivates mindfulness of generosity, and heads straight for nirvāṇa. This is called the fifth teaching known and seen by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, which leads out of the realm of suffering and ascends to the realm of excellence, and which purifies beings through the one vehicle path, frees them from suffering and affliction, and extinguishes sorrows and laments, enabling them to attain the true Dharma.

「复次，圣弟子自念施法，心自欣庆。我今离悭贪垢、离在居家，解脱心施、常施、舍施、乐施、具足施、平等施。若圣弟子念于自所施法时，不起欲觉、瞋恚、害觉。如是，圣弟子出染著心。于何染著？谓五欲功德。于此五欲功德离贪、恚、痴，安住正念正知，乘于直道，修施念，正向涅槃，是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说第五出苦处升于胜处，一乘道净于众生，离苦恼，灭忧悲，得如实法。

Furthermore, the noble disciple recollects the virtues of the heavens, recollecting the gods of the Four Heavenly Kings, the Thirty-three Heavens, the Yāma Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, and the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven. With pure faith, they are reborn in those heavens upon the end of their lives. I am also like this, with faith, morality, generosity, learning, and wisdom. Upon the end of my life, I will be reborn in those heavens. Thus, when the noble disciple recollects the virtues of the heavens, he does not give rise to the perception of desire, the perception of anger, or the perception of harm. Thus, the noble disciple is free from a defiled mind. What is a defiled mind? It refers to the five sense pleasures. Being free from desire, anger, and delusion towards these five sense pleasures, one abides in right mindfulness and right knowledge, rides the straight path, cultivates mindfulness of the heavens, and heads straight for nirvāṇa. This is called the sixth teaching known and seen by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, which leads out of the realm of suffering and ascends to the realm of excellence, and which purifies beings through the one vehicle path, frees them from suffering and affliction, and extinguishes sorrows and laments, enabling them to attain the true Dharma.

「复次，圣弟子念于天德，念四王天、三十三天，炎摩天、兜率陀天、化乐天、他化自在天，清净信心，于此命终，生彼诸天。我亦如是，信、戒、施、闻、慧，于此命终，生彼天中。如是，圣弟子念天功德时，不起欲觉、瞋恚、害觉。如是，圣弟子出染著心。于何染著？谓五欲功德。于此五欲功德离贪、恚、痴，安住正念正知，乘于直道，修天念，正向涅槃，是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说第六出苦处升于胜处，一乘道净于众生，离苦恼，灭忧悲，得如实法。」

Having spoken this sūtra, Venerable Mahākātyāyana, the bhikṣus who heard what was said rejoiced and received it respectfully.

尊者摩诃迦旃延说此经已，诸比丘闻其所说，欢喜奉行。
