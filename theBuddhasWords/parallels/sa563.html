SĀ 563

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 563（五六三） 尼犍

Parallel to: AN 3.74

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Monkey Pond in the upper chamber of the lecture hall in Vaiśālī, together with Venerable Ānanda.

一时，佛住毘舍离猕猴池侧重阁讲堂，尊者阿难亦在彼住。

At that time, the Licchavi Anāthapiṇḍada, a disciple of the Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, and the Licchavi Kumāra, a disciple of the Ājīvika Āsāḍha, went together to Venerable Ānanda. After exchanging greetings and pleasantries, they sat to one side. Then the Licchavi Anāthapiṇḍada said to Venerable Ānanda, "My teacher, the Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, has extinguished the burning, purified, and transcended. He teaches his disciples the path thus: 'By practicing austerities in past lives, one can vomit them all out. By not creating physical karma, cutting off bridges, and having no more taints in the future, all karma will be exhausted. Because karma is exhausted, all suffering is exhausted. Because suffering is exhausted, the end of suffering is reached.' Venerable Ānanda, what is the meaning of this?"

尔时，无畏离车，是尼犍弟子、聪明童子离车，是阿耆毘弟子，俱往尊者阿难所，共相问讯慰劳已，于一面坐。时，无畏离车语尊者阿难：「我师尼犍子灭炽然法，清净超出，为诸弟子说如是道：『宿命之业，行苦行故，悉能吐之。身业不作，断截桥梁，于未来世无复诸漏，诸业永尽；业永尽故，众苦永尽；苦永尽故，究竟苦边。』尊者阿难！此义云何？」

Venerable Ānanda said to the Licchavi, "The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, knows and sees three kinds of paths that are free from burning, pure, and transcend. With the path of one vehicle, he purifies sentient beings, frees them from sorrow and lamentation, transcends suffering and distress, and attains the true nature of the Dharma. What are the three? Thus, the noble disciple abides in pure precepts, upholds the prātimokṣa, is fully endowed with deportment, and has faith in all faults, giving rise to the notion of fear. Upholding such complete pure precepts, past karma gradually comes out, attaining the present liberation from burning, not waiting for the right time, being able to attain the true Dharma, penetrating and directly seeing through observation, and awakening by oneself through wisdom. Licchavi elder! This is called the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, speaking of what is known and seen, speaking of liberation from burning, purification, and transcendence, using the path of one vehicle to purify sentient beings, extinguish suffering and distress, transcend sorrow and lamentation, and attain the true nature of the Dharma.

尊者阿难语离车言：「如来、应、等正觉所知所见，说三种离炽然清净超出道，以一乘道净众生、离忧悲、越苦恼，得真如法。何等为三？如是，圣弟子住于净戒，受波罗提木叉，威仪具足，信于诸罪过，生怖畏想。受持如是具足净戒，宿业渐吐，得现法离炽然，不待时节，能得正法，通达现见观察，智慧自觉。离车长者！是名如来、应、等正觉说所知所见说离炽然，清净超出，以一乘道净众生、灭苦恼、越忧悲，得真如法。

"Furthermore, Licchavi! Abiding in such complete pure precepts, being free from desire and evil unwholesome dharmas, up to fully abiding in the fourth dhyāna, this is called the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, speaking of liberation from burning, up to attaining the true Dharma. There is also the samādhi absorption, knowing the noble truth of suffering as it really is, knowing the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering as they really are. Fully endowed with such wisdom and mind, one no longer creates karma, and past karma gradually ceases. One attains the present true Dharma, is free from burning, does not wait for the right time, penetrates and directly sees, and awakens by oneself through wisdom. Licchavi! This is called the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, speaking of what is known and seen, speaking of the third liberation from burning, purification, and transcendence, using the path of one vehicle to purify sentient beings, free them from suffering and distress, transcend sorrow and lamentation, and attain the true nature of the Dharma."

「复次，离车！如是净戒具足，离欲、恶不善法，乃至第四禅具足住，是名如来、应、等正觉说离炽然，乃至得如实法。复有三昧正受，于此苦圣谛如实知，此苦集圣谛、苦灭圣谛、苦灭道迹圣谛如实知。具足如是智慧心，业更不造，宿业渐已断，得现正法，离诸炽然，不待时节，通达现见，生自觉智。离车！是名如来、应、等正觉所知所见说第三离炽然，清净超出，以一乘道净众生、离苦恼、灭忧悲，得如实法。」

At that time, the Licchavi disciple Nirgrantha Ajita remained silent.

尔时，尼犍弟子离车无畏默然住。

At that time, the Licchavi disciple of the Ājīvika Ajita spoke again to the Licchavi Nirgrantha Ajita, saying: "Strange, Ajita! Why do you remain silent? Do you not rejoice in the well-spoken Dharma that is known, seen, and spoken by the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One?"

尔时，阿耆毘弟子离车聪慧重语离车无畏言：「怪哉！无畏！何默然住？于如来、应、等正觉所说、所知、所见、善说法，闻不随喜耶？」

The Licchavi Ajita replied: "I was contemplating its meaning, that is why I remained silent. Who would not rejoice in the Dharma spoken by the World-Honored One, the Śramaṇa Gautama? If there are those who hear the Dharma spoken by the Śramaṇa Gautama and do not rejoice in it, they are fools who will long suffer from unbeneficial suffering."

离车无畏答言：「我思惟其义，故默然住耳。谁闻世尊沙门瞿昙所说法不随喜者？若有闻沙门瞿昙说法而不随喜者，此则愚夫，长夜当受非义不饶益苦。」

At that time, the Licchavi Ajita, the disciple of the Nirgrantha, and the Licchavi Ajita, the disciple of the Ājīvika, having heard the Dharma spoken by the Buddha and Venerable Ānanda, rejoiced and followed them, then rose from their seats and departed.

时，尼犍弟子离车无畏、阿耆毘弟子聪慧重闻佛所说法，尊者阿难陀所说，欢喜随喜，从座起去。
