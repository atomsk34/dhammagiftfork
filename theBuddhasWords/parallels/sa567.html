SĀ 567

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 567（五六七） 那迦达多

Parallel to: SN 41.7

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the mango grove monastery in the mango village, together with a large assembly of elder monks.

一时，佛住庵罗聚落庵罗林精舍，与众多上座比丘俱。

At that time, the elder Citta went to the elder monks, bowed his head at their feet, and sat to one side. The elder monks then taught the Dharma to the elder Citta in various ways, instructing, illuminating, and delighting him. Having instructed, illuminated, and delighted him, they remained silent.

时，有质多罗长者诣诸上座比丘所，稽首礼足，退坐一面。时，诸上座比丘为质多罗长者种种说法，示、教、照、喜；示、教、照、喜已，默然住。

At that time, the elder Citta went to Venerable Nāgadatta, bowed his head at his feet, and sat to one side. Venerable Nāgadatta said to the elder Citta, "There are immeasurable mind samādhi, signless mind samādhi, wishless mind samādhi, and emptiness mind samādhi. What do you think, elder? Are these dharmas given various names for various meanings, or are they given various names for one meaning?"

时，质多罗长者诣尊者那伽达多比丘所，稽首礼足，退坐一面。尊者那伽达多告质多罗长者：「有无量心三昧、无相心三昧、无所有心三昧、空心三昧。云何？长者！此法为种种义故种种名？为一义有种种名？」

The elder Citta asked Venerable Nāgadatta, "Are these various samādhis spoken by the World-Honored One, or are they spoken by you yourself?"

质多罗长者问尊者那伽达多：「此诸三昧为世尊所说？为尊者自意说耶？」

Venerable Nāgadatta replied, "These are spoken by the World-Honored One."

尊者那伽达多答言：「此世尊所说。」

The elder Citta said to Venerable Nāgadatta, "Please allow me to contemplate this meaning a little before I answer."

质多罗长者语尊者那伽达多：「听我小思惟此义，然后当答。」

After a moment of contemplation, he said to Venerable Nāgadatta, "There are teachings with various meanings, various phrases, and various flavors. There are teachings with one meaning and various flavors."

须臾思惟已，语尊者那伽达多：「有法种种义、种种句、种种味，有法一义种种味。」

He further asked the elder, "What are the teachings with various meanings, various phrases, and various flavors?"

复问长者：「云何有法种种义、种种句、种种味？」

The elder replied, "The immeasurable concentration refers to when a noble disciple's mind is filled with loving-kindness, without enmity, hatred, or anger, with a broad and heavy mind, practicing and cultivating it extensively, pervading one direction. Thus, in two directions, three directions, four directions, above, below, and all worlds, the mind is filled with loving-kindness, without enmity, hatred, or anger, with a broad and heavy mind, practicing and cultivating it extensively, pervading all directions, abiding in the contemplation of all worlds. This is called the immeasurable concentration. What is the signless concentration? It means that a noble disciple does not contemplate any signs, and abides in the signless concentration with a mind that has attained it. This is called the signless concentration. What is the concentration of the formless? It means that a noble disciple transcends all the sense sphere and the sphere of infinite consciousness, and abides in the formless with a mind that has attained it. This is called the concentration of the formless. What is the empty concentration? It means that a noble disciple observes the emptiness of the world as it truly is, as permanent, unchanging, not self, and not belonging to self. This is called the empty concentration. These are called the teachings with various meanings, various phrases, and various flavors."

长者答言：「无量三昧者，谓圣弟子心与慈俱，无怨、无憎、无恚，宽弘重心，无量修习普缘，一方充满。如是二方、三方、四方上下，一切世间心与慈俱，无怨、无憎、无恚，宽弘重心，无量修习，充满诸方，一切世间普缘住，是名无量三昧。云何为无相三昧？谓圣弟子于一切相不念，无相心三昧，身作证，是名无相心三昧。云何无所有心三昧？谓圣弟子度一切无量识入处，无所有，无所有心住，是名无所有心三昧。云何空三昧？谓圣弟子世间空，世间空如实观察，常住不变易，非我、非我所，是名空心三昧，是名为法种种义、种种句、种种味。」

He further asked the elder, "What are the teachings with one meaning and various flavors?"

复问长者：「云何法一义种种味？」

He replied, "Venerable one! It means that craving is limited, but the foremost immeasurable is the absence of conflict. Craving is characterized, anger and delusion are characterized, but the absence of conflict is without characteristics. Craving is with something, anger and delusion are with something, but the absence of conflict is without something. Furthermore, the absence of conflict is empty, craving is empty, anger and delusion are empty, it is permanent, unchanging, empty, not self, and not belonging to self. This is called the teaching with one meaning and various flavors."

答言：「尊者！谓贪有量，若无诤者第一无量，谓贪者是有相，恚、痴者是有相，无诤者是无相。贪者是所有，恚、痴者是所有，无诤者是无所有。复次，无诤者空，于贪空，于恚、痴空，常住不变易空，非我、非我所，是名法一义种种味。」

Venerable Nāgadatta asked, "How is it, elder? Did you hear this meaning before?"

尊者那伽达多问言：「云何？长者！此义汝先所闻耶？」

He replied, "Venerable one! I did not hear it."

答言：「尊者！不闻。」

He further told the elder, "You have gained great benefit. In the profound Buddha Dharma, you have now attained the eye of noble wisdom and entered it."

复告长者：「汝得大利，于甚深佛法，现贤圣慧眼得入。」

The elder Jīvaka heard what Venerable Nāgârjuna said, rejoiced, paid homage, and departed.

质多罗长者闻尊者那伽达多所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
