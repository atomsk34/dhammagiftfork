SĀ 568

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 568（五六八） 迦摩

Parallel to: MN 44 / SN 41.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying in the mango grove of Ambapālī's grove, together with a group of senior bhikṣus.

一时，佛住庵罗聚落庵罗林中，与诸上座比丘俱。

At that time, the elder Citta went to the senior bhikṣus, paid homage to the senior bhikṣus, and then went to Venerable Gamā Bhikṣu. He bowed his head at Gamā Bhikṣu's feet, withdrew to sit to one side, and said to Venerable Gamā Bhikṣu, "What is meant by 'practicing'?"

时，有质多罗长者诣诸上座比丘所，礼诸上座已，诣尊者伽摩比丘所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白尊者伽摩比丘：「所谓行者，云何名行？」

Gamā Bhikṣu said, "Practicing means the three practices: bodily practice, verbal practice, and mental practice."

伽摩比丘言：「行者，谓三行，身行、口行、意行。」

He further asked, "What is bodily practice? What is verbal practice? What is mental practice?"

复问：「云何身行？云何口行？云何意行？」

He replied, "Householder! Exhalation and inhalation are called bodily practice. Perception and contemplation are called verbal practice. Conception and intention are called mental practice."

答言：「长者！出息、入息名为身行。有觉、有观名为口行。想、思名为意行。」

He further asked, "Why are exhalation and inhalation called bodily practice? Why are perception and contemplation called verbal practice? Why are conception and intention called mental practice?"

复问：「何故出息、入息名为身行？有觉、有观名为口行？想、思名为意行？」

He replied, "Householder! Exhalation and inhalation are bodily dharmas, based on the body, belonging to the body, and turning based on the body. Therefore, exhalation and inhalation are called bodily practice. Because there is perception and contemplation, there is verbal expression. Therefore, perception and contemplation are verbal practice. Conception and intention are mental practice, based on the mind, belonging to the mind, and turning based on the mind. Therefore, conception and intention are mental practice."

答：「长者！出息、入息是身法，依于身、属于身、依身转，是故出息、入息名为身行。有觉、有观故则口语，是故有觉、有观是口行。想、思是意行，依于心、属于心、依心转，是故想、思是意行。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! After perception and contemplation, one utters words. Are perception and contemplation called verbal practice? Conception and intention are mental factors, based on the mind, belonging to the mind, and turning based on the mind. Therefore, conception and intention are called mental practice."

复问：「尊者！觉、观已，发口语，是觉、观名为口行。想、思是心数法，依于心、属于心想转，是故想、思名为意行。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! How many dharmas are there?"

复问：「尊者！有几法？

When a person abandons the body, the corpse lies on the
ground, discarded in the charnel ground, without mind, like wood or stone.

「若人舍身时，　　彼身尸卧地，弃于丘塜间，　　无心如木石。」

He replied, "Householder! "

答言：「长者！

Life, warmth, and consciousness, when abandoning the body, are abandoned together. That body is discarded in the charnel ground, without mind, like wood or stone.

「寿暖及与识，舍身时俱舍，彼身弃塜间，无心如木石。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! Is there a difference between death and entering the cessation of perception and feeling?"

复问：「尊者！若死、若入灭尽正受，有差别不？」

He replied, "Abandoning life and warmth, all faculties are destroyed, and body and life are separated. This is called death. In the cessation of perception and feeling, bodily, verbal, and mental activities cease, but life is not abandoned, warmth is not separated, faculties are not destroyed, and body and life are connected. This is the difference between death and entering the cessation of perception and feeling."

答：「舍于寿暖，诸根悉坏，身命分离，是名为死。灭尽定者，身、口、意行灭，不舍寿命，不离于暖，诸根不坏，身命相属，此则命终、入灭正受差别之相。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! How does one enter the cessation of perception and feeling?"

复问：「尊者！云何入灭正受？」

He replied, "Householder! Entering the cessation of perception and feeling is not saying, 'I enter the cessation of perception and feeling, I will enter the cessation of perception and feeling.' Rather, one first makes gradual preparations, as one did before, and then enters concentration.

答言：「长者！入灭正受，不言：『我入灭正受，我当入灭正受。』然先作如是渐息方便，如先方便，向入正受。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! When entering the cessation of perception and feeling, which dharmas are extinguished first? Are they bodily actions, verbal actions, or mental actions?"

复问：「尊者！入灭正受时，先灭何法？为身行、为口行、为意行耶？」

He replied, "Householder! One who enters the cessation of perception and feeling first extinguishes verbal actions, then bodily actions, and then mental actions."

答言：「长者！入灭正受者，先灭口行，次身行、次意行。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! How does one emerge from the cessation of perception and feeling?"

复问：「尊者！云何为出灭正受？」

He replied, "Householder! One who emerges from the cessation of perception and feeling also does not think, 'I am now emerging from the cessation of perception and feeling, I shall emerge from the cessation of perception and feeling.' Rather, one first makes a preparatory mental resolve, and then arises according to that previous resolve."

答言：「长者！出灭正受者亦不念言：『我今出正受，我当出正受。』然先已作方便心，如其先心而起。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! When one arises from the cessation of perception and feeling, which comes first - bodily action, verbal action, or mental action?"

复问：「尊者！起灭正受者，何法先起，为身行、为口行、为意行耶？」

He replied, "Householder! When one arises from the cessation of perception and feeling, mental action arises first, then bodily action, and lastly verbal action."

答言：「长者！从灭正受起者，意行先起，次身行，后口行。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! When entering the cessation of perception and feeling, how does it flow, stream, and pour?"

复问：「尊者！入灭正受者。云何顺趣、流注、浚输？」

He replied, "Householder! When entering the cessation of perception and feeling, it flows towards detachment, streams towards detachment, and pours towards detachment. It flows towards emergence, streams towards emergence, and pours towards emergence. It flows towards nirvāṇa, streams towards nirvāṇa, and pours towards nirvāṇa."

答言：「长者！入灭正受者，顺趣于离、流注于离、浚输于离，顺趣于出、流注于出、浚输于出，顺趣涅槃、流注涅槃、浚输涅槃。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! When abiding in the cessation of perception and feeling, how many contacts are experienced?"

复问：「尊者！住灭正受时，为触几触？」

He replied, "Householder! The contact is unmoving, the contact is signless, the contact is insubstantial."

答言：「长者！触不动、触无相、触无所有。」

He further asked, "Venerable One! When entering the cessation of perception and feeling, how many dharmas are practiced?"

复问：「尊者！入灭正受时，为作几法？」

He replied, "Householder! This should have been asked first, why are you asking now? But I will explain it to you. A bhikṣu who enters the cessation of perception and feeling practices two dharmas: tranquility and insight."

答言：「长者！此应先问，何故今问，然当为汝说。比丘入灭正受者，作于二法，止以观。」

At that time, the householder Jīvaka heard what Venerable Kāma said, and joyfully followed his words, paid homage, and departed.

时，质多罗长者闻尊者迦摩所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
