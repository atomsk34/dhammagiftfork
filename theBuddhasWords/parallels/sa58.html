SĀ 58

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 58（五八） 阴即受

Parallel to: MN 109 / SN 22.82

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time, the Buddha was staying at the Deer Park Lecture Hall in the Eastern Garden of Shravasti.

一时，佛住舍卫国东园鹿母讲堂。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having emerged from meditation in the afternoon, sat down in front of the bhikṣus, arranged his seat, and said to the bhikṣus, "There are five aggregates of grasping. What are the five? They are the form aggregate of grasping, the feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness aggregates of grasping."

尔时，世尊于晡时从禅觉，于诸比丘前敷座而坐，告诸比丘：「有五受阴。云何为五？谓色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴。」

At that time, a bhikṣu rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bared his right shoulder, touched his right knee to the ground, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Are these five aggregates of grasping the form aggregate of grasping, the feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness aggregates of grasping?"

时，有一比丘从坐起，整衣服，偏袒右肩，右膝著地，合掌白佛言：「世尊！此五受阴，色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴耶？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "Sit down and ask. I will explain it for you."

佛告比丘：「还坐而问，当为汝说。」

At that time, the bhikṣu bowed to the Buddha, returned to his original seat, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! What is the root of these five aggregates of grasping? What is their accumulation? What is their arising? What is their contact?"

时，彼比丘为佛作礼，还复本坐，白佛言：「世尊！此五受阴，以何为根？以何集？以何生？以何触？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "These five aggregates of grasping have desire as their root, desire as their accumulation, desire as their arising, and desire as their contact."

佛告比丘：「此五受阴，欲为根，欲集、欲生、欲触。」

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! You have explained that the five aggregates are grasping. Excellent! Now I will ask further. World-Honored One! Are the aggregates grasping, or are the aggregates different from grasping?"

时，彼比丘闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，而白佛言：「世尊！为说五阴即受，善哉所说！今当更问。世尊！阴即受，为五阴异受耶？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "The five aggregates are not grasping, nor are the five aggregates different from grasping. Those who have desire and greed for them are the five aggregates of grasping."

佛告比丘：「非五阴即受，亦非五阴异受；能于彼有欲贪者，是五受阴。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! World-Honored One! I rejoice in this. Now I will ask further. World-Honored One! Are there two aggregates that are related?"

比丘白佛：「善哉！世尊！欢喜随喜，今复更问。世尊！有二阴相关耶？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "It is so, it is so. It is like a person who thinks like this: 'In the future, I will attain such form, such feeling, such perception, such volition, and such consciousness.' This is called, bhikṣu, the relatedness of the aggregates."

佛告比丘：「如是，如是。犹若有一人如是思惟：『我于未来得如是色、如是受、如是想、如是行、如是识。』是名比丘阴阴相关也。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! I rejoice in what you have said."

比丘白佛：「善哉所说，欢喜随喜。」

He asked further, "World-Honored One! What is meant by 'aggregates'?" The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "All forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, good or bad, far or near, are collectively called aggregates. This is what is meant by 'aggregates.' Feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are also like this. Thus, bhikṣu, these are called the aggregates."

更有所问：「世尊！云何名阴？」佛告比丘：「诸所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麤、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切总说阴，是名为阴。受、想、行、识亦复如是。如是，比丘！是名为阴。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! I rejoice in what you have said."

比丘白佛：「善哉所说，欢喜随喜。」

He asked further, "World-Honored One! What are the causes and conditions for the aggregate of form? What are the causes and conditions for the aggregates of feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness?"

更有所问：「世尊！何因何缘名为色阴？何因何缘名受、想、行、识阴？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "The four great elements are the causes and conditions for the aggregate of form. Why is that? All aggregates of form are all the four great elements, because they are produced from the four great elements. Contact is the cause and condition for the arising of feeling, perception, and volition, therefore they are called the aggregates of feeling, perception, and volition. Why is that? All feeling, perception, and volition arise from contact. Name and form are the causes and conditions, therefore they are called the aggregate of consciousness. Why is that? All consciousness arises from name and form."

佛告比丘：「四大因、四大缘，是名色阴。所以者何？诸所有色阴，彼一切悉皆四大，缘四大造故。触因、触缘，生受、想、行，是故名受、想、行阴。所以者何？若所有受、想、行，彼一切触缘故，名色因、名色缘，是故名为识阴。所以者何？若所有识，彼一切名色缘故。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! I rejoice in what you have said."

比丘白佛：「善哉所说，欢喜随喜。」

He asked further, "What is the taste of form? What is the suffering of form? What is the transcendence of form? What is the taste of feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness? What is the suffering of consciousness? What is the transcendence of consciousness?"

更有所问：「云何色味？云何色患？云何色离？云何受、想、行、识味？云何识患？云何识离？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "The joy and pleasure arising from form is called the taste of form; if form is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the suffering of form; if one subdues desire and greed for form, cuts off desire and greed, and transcends desire and greed, this is called the transcendence of form. The joy and pleasure arising from feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness is called the taste of consciousness; feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, this is called the suffering of consciousness; subduing desire and greed for feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness, cutting off desire and greed, and transcending desire and greed, this is called the transcendence of consciousness."

佛告比丘：「缘色生喜乐，是名色味；若色无常、苦、变易法，是名色患；若于色调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名色离。若缘受、想、行、识生喜乐，是名识味；受、想、行、识，无常、苦、变易法，是名识患；于受、想、行、识，调伏欲贪、断欲贪、越欲贪，是名识离。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! I rejoice in what you have said."

比丘白佛：「善哉所说，欢喜随喜。」

He asked further, "World-Honored One! How does one give rise to the conceit of self?"

更有所问：「世尊！云何生我慢？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "A foolish, ignorant ordinary person sees the self in form, sees the self as different from form, and sees the self as existing in form. They see the self in feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness, see the self as different from consciousness, and see the self as existing in consciousness. This gives rise to the conceit of self."

佛告比丘：「愚痴无闻凡夫于色见我、异我、相在，于受、想、行、识见我、异我、相在，于此生我慢。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! I rejoice in what you have said."

比丘白佛：「善哉所说，欢喜随喜。」

He asked further, "World-Honored One! How can one be free from the conceit of self?"

更有所问：「世尊！云何得无我慢？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "The learned noble disciple does not see me, other than me, or the characteristics of me in form, nor does he see me, other than me, or the characteristics of me in feeling, perception, formation, or consciousness."

佛告比丘：「多闻圣弟子不于色见我、异我、相在，不于受、想、行、识，见我、异我、相在。」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Excellent! I have another question. What is known and seen to completely end the taints?"

比丘白佛：「善哉所说，更有所问，何所知、何所见，尽得漏尽？」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "All forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, are not the self, not different from the self, and not present in the self. Feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are also like this. Bhikṣu! Knowing and seeing in this way, one will quickly end the taints."

佛告比丘：「诸所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麤、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、不异我、不相在；受、想、行、识亦复如是。比丘！如是知，如是见，疾得漏尽。」

At that time, there was another bhikṣu in the assembly who was dull-witted and ignorant, giving rise to evil and wrong views in the shell of ignorance, thinking: "If there is no self, and one performs actions without a self, who will receive the retribution in the future?" At that time, the World-Honored One knew the thoughts in that bhikṣu's mind and told the bhikṣus: "In this assembly, if there are foolish people who are ignorant and unclear, and they think: 'If form is without self, and feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are without self, and one performs actions without a self, who will receive the retribution?' Such doubts should be explained first. How is it, bhikṣus! Is form permanent or impermanent?"

尔时，会中复有异比丘，钝根无知，在无明［谷-禾＋卵］起恶邪见，而作是念：「若无我者，作无我业，于未来世，谁当受报？」尔时，世尊知彼比丘心之所念，告诸比丘：「于此众中，若有愚痴人，无智明，而作是念：『若色无我，受、想、行、识无我，作无我业，谁当受报？』如是所疑，先以解释彼。云何比丘！色为常耶？为非常耶？」

He replied, "Impermanent, World-Honored One!"

答言：「无常。世尊！」

"If it is impermanent, is it suffering?"

「若无常者，是苦耶？」

He replied, "It is suffering, World-Honored One!"

答言：「是苦。世尊！」

"If it is impermanent and suffering, and subject to change, would the learned noble disciples see it as self, different from self, or as existing in self?"

「若无常、苦，是变易法，多闻圣弟子于中宁见是我、异我、相在不？」

He replied, "No, World-Honored One!"

答言：「不也，世尊！」

"So it is with feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. Therefore, monks, all forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or fine, beautiful or ugly, far or near, are not self nor do they belong to self. Seeing this is right view. So it is with feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. The learned noble disciples, contemplating in this way, cultivate disenchantment. Having become disenchanted, they become detached. Having become detached, they are liberated. With liberation, they know and see: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this state.'"

「受、想、行、识亦复如是。是故，比丘！若所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麤、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、非我所。如是见者，是为正见；受、想、行、识亦复如是。多闻圣弟子如是观者便修厌，厌已离欲，离欲已解脱，解脱知见：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

When the Buddha spoke this sūtra, many bhikṣus were free from defilements and attained liberation of the mind. After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what he said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经时，众多比丘不起诸漏，心得解脱。佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The aggregates, sense bases, and aggregates are the same as the aggregates. The two aggregates
are mutually related. Names, causes, and two flavors. Arrogance, and the swift exhaustion of taints.

阴、根、阴即受　　二阴共相关名字、因、二味　　我慢、疾漏尽
