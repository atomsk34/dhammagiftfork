SĀ 615

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 615（六一五） 比丘尼

Parallel to: SN 47.10

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda put on his robe in the morning, took his alms bowl, and entered Śrāvastī to beg for food. On the way, he thought, “I should go to the nuns’ temple first.” He then went to the nuns’ temple.

尔时，尊者阿难晨朝著衣持钵，入舍卫城乞食，于路中思惟：「我今先至比丘尼寺。」即往比丘尼寺。

The nuns saw Venerable Ānanda coming from afar, quickly prepared a seat, and invited him to sit.

诸比丘尼遥见尊者阿难来，疾敷床座，请令就座。

The nuns bowed at Venerable Ānanda's feet, then sat to one side and said to Venerable Ānanda, "We nuns practice the four establishments of mindfulness, focusing our minds, and know our own progress and decline.

时，诸比丘尼礼尊者阿难足，退坐一面，白尊者阿难：「我等诸比丘尼修四念处系心住，自知前后升降。」

Venerable Ānanda said to the nuns, "Excellent, excellent, sisters! You should learn as you have said. Those who practice the four establishments of mindfulness and focus their minds well should know their progress and decline in this way.

尊者阿难告诸比丘尼：「善哉！善哉！姊妹！当如汝等所说而学，凡修习四念处善系心住者，应如是知前后升降。」

At that time, Venerable Ānanda spoke various teachings to the nuns. Having spoken various teachings, he rose from his seat and left.

时，尊者阿难为诸比丘尼种种说法，种种说法已，从座起去。

At that time, Venerable Ānanda went to Śrāvastī to beg for alms. After returning, he put away his robe and bowl, washed his feet, went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. He told the World-Honored One in detail what the nuns had said.

尔时，尊者阿难于舍卫城中乞食还，举衣钵，洗足已，诣世尊所，稽首佛足，退坐一面。以比丘尼所说具白世尊。

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Excellent! Excellent! One should learn in this way to dwell with the mind well-restrained on the four foundations of mindfulness, knowing the rise and fall of the mind. Why is that? The mind seeks externally, and then one restrains it to seek its own mind. A distracted mind and an unliberated mind are all known as they are. If a bhikṣu dwells in mindfulness of the body observing the body, having dwelt in mindfulness of the body observing the body, if the body is lazy and the mind is indolent, that bhikṣu should arouse pure faith and take up the pure characteristics, arousing pure faith and mindfulness of the pure characteristics. Then the mind will be delighted, and from delight, joy arises. When the mind is joyful, the body becomes tranquil. When the body is tranquil, one experiences bodily bliss. When one experiences bodily bliss, the mind becomes concentrated. When the mind is concentrated, the noble disciple should train thus: 'I shall restrain the mind that is externally distracted, and not arouse thoughts of perception and contemplation. Without perception and contemplation, I shall dwell in joy with equanimity and mindfulness. Having dwelt in joy, I shall know it as it really is.' The same is said for mindfulness of feeling, mind, and dharmas.

佛告阿难：「善哉！善哉！应如是学四念处善系心住，知前后升降。所以者何？心于外求，然后制令求其心，散乱心、不解脱皆如实知。若比丘于身身观念住，于彼身身观念住已，若身躭睡，心法懈怠，彼比丘当起净信，取于净相，起净信心；忆念净相已，其心则悦，悦已生喜；其心喜已，身则猗息；身猗息已，则受身乐；受身乐已，其心则定。心定者，圣弟子当作是学：『我于此义，外散之心摄令休息，不起觉想及已观想，无觉无观，舍念乐住；乐住已，如实知。』受、心、法念亦如是说。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to Venerable Ānanda, "Excellent! Excellent!" Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and practiced them.

佛说此经已，尊者阿难闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
