SĀ 616

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 616（六一六） 厨士

Parallel to: SN 47.8

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "You should take hold of your own mind and not let it scatter externally. Why is that? If a bhikṣu is foolish, unskillful, and not good, and does not take hold of his own mind but takes hold of external characteristics, then he will later regress and create obstacles for himself. It is like a cook who is foolish, unskillful, and not good at skillfully blending various flavors to serve the honored master. If the flavors are sour, salty, bitter, or bland, they will not please the master. He is not able to skillfully take hold of the master's preferences for sour, salty, bitter, or bland flavors, and the blending of various flavors. He is not able to closely attend to the master's left and right, attend to his needs, listen to his desires, and skillfully take hold of his mind. Instead, he uses his own mind to blend various flavors and offer them to the master. If they do not please the master, the master will not be delighted. Because of not being delighted, he will not receive rewards or be loved. A foolish bhikṣu is also like this. If he is unskillful and not good, and dwells in contemplation of the body in the body, he is not able to eliminate and cut off the upper afflictions, and is not able to take hold of his mind. He also does not attain inner mental tranquility, does not attain supreme right mindfulness and right knowledge, and also does not attain the four kinds of superior mental states and the present happiness of dwelling. He does not attain the peaceful nirvāṇa that he has not attained before. This is called a bhikṣu who is foolish, unskillful, and not good. He is not able to skillfully take hold of the characteristics of his inner mind but takes hold of external characteristics, creating obstacles for himself.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「当取自心相，莫令外散。所以者何？若彼比丘愚痴、不辨、不善，不取自心相而取外相，然后退减，自生障阂。譬如厨士愚痴、不辨，不善巧便调和众味，奉养尊主，酸醎酢淡，不适其意。不能善取尊主所嗜，酸醎酢淡，众味之和。不能亲侍尊主左右，司其所须，听其所欲，善取其心，而自用意调和众味，以奉尊主。若不适其意，尊主不悦，不悦故不蒙爵赏，亦不爱念。愚痴比丘亦复如是，不辨、不善，于身身观住，不能除断上烦恼，不能摄取其心，亦复不得内心寂静，不得胜妙正念正知，亦复不得四种增上心法、现法乐住、本所未得安隐涅槃，是名比丘愚痴、不辨、不善，不能善摄内心之相而取外相，自生障阂。

If a bhikṣu is wise, skillful, and good, and dwells in contemplation of the body in the body, he is able to eliminate and cut off the upper afflictions, and is able to take hold of his mind. He is able to attain inner mental tranquility, attain supreme right mindfulness

「若有比丘黠慧才辩，善巧方便，取内心已，然后取于外相，彼于后时终不退减，自生障阂。譬如厨士黠慧聪辩，善巧方便，供养尊主，能调众味，酸醎酢淡，善取尊主所嗜之相，而和众味，以应其心，听其尊主所欲之味，数以奉之，尊主悦已，必得爵禄，爱念倍重。如是黠慧厨士善取尊主之心，比丘亦复如是。身身观念住，断上烦恼，善摄其心，内心寂止，正念正知，得四增心法，现法乐住，得所未得安隐涅槃，是名比丘黠慧辩才，善巧方便，取内心相，摄持外相，终无退减，自生障阂，受、心、法观亦复如是。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "Good, good! The bhikṣus should all practice in this way." Having heard the Buddha's words, the bhikṣus rejoiced and practiced accordingly.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
