SĀ 617

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 617（六一七） 鸟

Parallel to: SN 47.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "In the past there was a bird called Rāvaṇa. It was caught by a hawk and soared into the sky, crying out, 'I did not realize that I would suddenly encounter this difficulty. It is because I left my parents' territory and wandered elsewhere that I encountered this difficulty. How can I be so constrained by others today and not be free?' The hawk said to Rāvaṇa, 'Where is your own territory where you can be free?' Rāvaṇa replied, 'In the furrows of a plowed field, I have my own territory, enough to avoid all difficulties. That is my parents' territory.'

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「过去世时有一鸟，名曰罗婆，为鹰所捉，飞腾虚空，于空鸣唤言：『我不自觉，忽遭此难，我坐舍离父母境界而游他处，故遭此难。如何今日为他所困，不得自在。』鹰语罗婆：『汝当何处自有境界而得自在？』罗婆答言：『我于田耕垅中自有境界，足免诸难，是为我家父母境界。』

The hawk arrogantly said to Rāvaṇa, 'I will let you go. Can you escape back to the furrows?' Then Rāvaṇa escaped from the hawk's claws and returned to the large mound in the plowed field. It settled down in a safe place and then wanted to fight with the hawk on the mound.

「鹰于罗婆起憍慢言：『放汝令去，还耕垅中，能得脱以不？』于是罗婆得脱鹰爪，还到耕垅大块之下，安住止处，然后于块上欲与鹰鬪。

The hawk was greatly enraged. 'This is a small bird, daring to fight with me!' Its anger was extreme. It flew straight and struck. Then Rāvaṇa entered under the mound. The hawk flew with force and its chest struck the hard mound, shattering its body and killing it.

「鹰则大怒，彼是小鸟，敢与我鬪，瞋恚极盛，骏飞直搏，于是罗婆入于块下，鹰鸟飞势，臆冲坚块，碎身即死。

At that time, the Rāvaṇa bird deeply hid under the mound and spoke a verse from above:

「时，罗婆鸟深伏块下，仰说偈言：

The hawk bird came with force, Rāvaṇa relied on its own
realm. Riding on the power of fierce anger, it brought disaster
and shattered its body. I fully understand and rely on my
own realm. Subduing the enemy, the mind rejoices. Observing myself, I
rejoice in its power. Even if you have the strength of
a hundred thousand dragons and elephants, it is not as good
as one-sixteenth of my wisdom. Observing my superior wisdom, it destroys the hawk.

「『鹰鸟用力来，　　罗婆依自界，乘瞋猛盛力，　　致祸碎其身。我具足通达，　　依于自境界，伏怨心随喜，　　自观欣其力。设汝有凶愚，　　百千龙象力，不如我智慧，　　十六分之一。观我智殊胜，　　摧灭于苍鹰。』

Thus, bhikkhus! Like that hawk bird, foolishly abandoning its own parents' realm and wandering elsewhere, it brings about this calamity. You bhikkhus should also be like this. In your own realm, where you practice, you should guard it well and avoid others' realms. You should learn this.

「如是，比丘！如彼鹰鸟，愚痴自舍所亲父母境界，游于他处，致斯灾患。汝等比丘亦应如是，于自境界所行之处，应善守持，离他境界，应当学。

Bhikkhus! The other place, the other realm, refers to the five sense objects. Seeing desirable, agreeable, and delightful forms with the eye, one is lustfully attached. Hearing sounds with the ear, smelling scents with the nose, tasting flavors with the tongue, and touching tangibles with the body, desirable, agreeable, and delightful tangibles, one is lustfully attached. This is called the other place, the other realm, bhikkhus. Bhikkhus! The self-place, the parents' realm, refers to the four foundations of mindfulness. What are the four? They are mindfulness of the body contemplating the body, mindfulness of feelings, mind, and phenomena contemplating phenomena. Therefore, bhikkhus! You should wander in your own place, the parents' realm, and avoid the other place, the other realm. You should learn this.

「比丘！他处他境界者，谓五欲境界，眼见可意、受、念妙色，欲心染著；耳识声、鼻识香、舌识味、身识触，可意、受、念妙触，欲心染著。是名比丘他处他境界。比丘！自处父母境界者，谓四念处。云何为四？谓身身观念处，受、心、法法观念处。是故，比丘！于自行处父母境界而自游行，远离他处他境界，应当学。」

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
