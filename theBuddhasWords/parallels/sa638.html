SĀ 638

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 638（六三八） 纯陀

Parallel to: SN 47.13 / EĀ 26.9 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was at the Karanda Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, Venerable Śāriputra was staying in the village of Nārāyaṇa in Magadha, where he was about to enter parinirvāṇa due to illness. The novice monk Cunda was attending to and serving him.

一时，佛在王舍城迦兰陀竹园。尔时，尊者舍利弗住摩竭提那罗聚落，疾病涅槃，纯陀沙弥瞻视供养。

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra was about to enter parinirvāṇa due to illness.

尔时，尊者舍利弗因病涅槃。

At that time, the novice monk Cunda, having attended to Venerable Śāriputra, took the remaining relics, put on his robe and bowl, went to Rājagṛha, put away his robe and bowl, washed his feet, and went to Venerable Ānanda. He bowed at Venerable Ānanda's feet, stepped back to one side, and said to Venerable Ānanda, "Venerable Ānanda should know that my teacher, Venerable Śāriputra, has entered parinirvāṇa. I bring his relics and robe and bowl.

时，纯陀沙弥供养尊者舍利弗已，取余舍利，担持衣钵，到王舍城，举衣钵，洗足已，诣尊者阿难所。礼尊者阿难足已，却住一面，白尊者阿难：「尊者当知，我和上尊者舍利弗已涅槃，我持舍利及衣钵来。」

Then Venerable Ānanda, having heard the novice monk Cunda's words, went to the Buddha and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I now feel my whole body is numb, my eloquence is blocked, and the novice monk Cunda came to tell me, 'My teacher, Śāriputra, has entered parinirvāṇa. I bring his remaining relics and robe and bowl.'"

于是尊者阿难闻纯陀沙弥语已，往诣佛所，白佛言：「世尊！我今举体离解，四方易韵，持辩闭塞，纯陀沙弥来语我言：『和上舍利弗已涅槃，持余舍利及衣钵来。』」

The Buddha said, "Ānanda, how is it? Did Śāriputra enter parinirvāṇa with the body of precepts he received? Or with the body of concentration, wisdom, liberation, or the knowledge and vision of liberation?"

佛言：「云何？阿难！彼舍利弗持所受戒身涅槃耶？定身、慧身、解脱身、解脱知见身涅槃耶？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "No, World-Honored One!"

阿难白佛言：「不也，世尊！」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If the Dharma I myself know and speak of, having attained perfect enlightenment, refers to the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernormal power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, and the eightfold path, does it lead to parinirvāṇa?"

佛告阿难：「若法我自知，成等正觉所说，谓四念处、四正断、四如意足、五根、五力、七觉支、八道支涅槃耶？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "No, World-Honored One! Although one does not attain parinirvāṇa by upholding the precepts, the body, and even the factors of the path, still the Venerable Śāriputra upholds the precepts, has much learning, is content with few desires, always practices seclusion, diligently applies skillful means, gathers and abides in mindfulness, and attains one-pointed concentration. He has swift wisdom, deep and sharp wisdom, surpassing wisdom, discerning wisdom, great wisdom, vast wisdom, profound wisdom, incomparable wisdom, and has accomplished the jewel of wisdom. He is able to show, teach, illuminate, and delight, and is good at praising. He preaches the Dharma for the sake of the assembly. Therefore, World-Honored One, I am sorrowful, distressed, and afflicted for the sake of the Dharma and for the sake of those who uphold the Dharma."

阿难白佛：「不也，世尊！虽不持所受戒身乃至道品法而涅槃，然尊者舍利弗持戒多闻，少欲知足，常行远离，精勤方便，摄念安住，一心正受捷疾智慧、深利智慧、超出智慧、分别智慧、大智慧、广智慧、甚深智慧、无等智慧，智宝成就，能示、能教、能照、能喜，善能赞叹，为众说法。是故，世尊！我为法故，为受法者故，愁忧苦恼。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not be sorrowful, distressed, and afflicted. Why is that? Whether sitting, standing, or acting, conditioned things are subject to decay. How could they not decay? Wishing for them not to decay is impossible. I have already said that all beloved things, various objects of enjoyment, and pleasant experiences are all subject to separation and cannot be permanently preserved. It is like a large tree with luxuriant roots, trunk, branches, leaves, flowers, and fruits. The large branches will break first. It is like a great treasure mountain, where the large rocks will collapse first. In the same way, the great Saṅgha of the Tathāgata, the great disciples, will pass into parinirvāṇa first. If there were Śāriputra in that place, I would have nothing to do there. But that place would not be empty, because there would be Śāriputra. I have already said this before. Ānanda! As I have said before, all beloved things and various objects of enjoyment are subject to separation. Therefore, do not be greatly sorrowful now. Ānanda! Know that the Tathāgata will not remain for long. Therefore, Ānanda! You should rely on yourself as your island, rely on the Dharma as your island, and rely on the unchanging as your unchanging island."

佛告阿难：「汝莫愁忧苦恼。所以者何？若坐、若起、若作，有为败坏之法，何得不坏，欲令不坏者，无有是处，我先已说，一切所爱念种种诸物、适意之事，一切皆是乖离之法，不可常保。譬如大树，根、茎、枝、叶、华、果茂盛，大枝先折，如大宝山，大岩先崩。如是，如来大众眷属，其大声闻先般涅槃，若彼方有舍利弗住者，于彼方我则无事，然其彼方，我则不空，以有舍利弗故，我先已说故。汝今，阿难！如我先说，所可爱念种种适意之事，皆是别离之法，是故汝今莫大愁毒。阿难！当知，如来不久亦当过去。是故，阿难！当作自洲而自依，当作法洲而法依，当作不异洲不异依。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! How does one rely on oneself as an island? How does one rely on the Dharma as an island? How does one rely on the unchanging as an unchanging island?"

阿难白佛：「世尊！云何自洲以自依？云何法洲以法依？云何不异洲不异依？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If a bhikṣu contemplates the body with mindfulness of the body, diligently applying skillful means, with right knowledge and right mindfulness, to subdue worldly greed and sorrow. The same is true for the external body, internal and external body, feelings, mind, and dharmas. Contemplating the dharmas with mindfulness of the dharmas is also explained in the same way. Ānanda! This is called relying on oneself as an island, relying on the Dharma as an island, and relying on the unchanging as an unchanging island."

佛告阿难：「若比丘身身观念处，精勤方便，正智正念，调伏世间贪忧。如是外身、内外身，受、心、法法观念处，亦如是说。阿难！是名自洲以自依、法洲以法依、不异洲不异洲依。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha had said rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
