SĀ 69

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 69（六九） 其道

Parallel to: SN 22.44

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "I will now explain the path leading to the origination of existence and the path leading to the cessation of the origination of existence. What is the path leading to the origination of existence? Ignorant and unlearned ordinary people see and do not know the origination, cessation, taste, danger, and escape from form as they truly are. Because they do not know this as it truly is, they delight in form, praise form, are attached to form, and dwell in form. Because they delight in form, praise form, are attached to form, and dwell in form, they take up craving. Craving gives rise to existence, and existence gives rise to birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. Thus arises this mass of suffering. The same is said extensively for feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. This is called the path leading to the origination of existence. Bhikṣus, the path leading to the origination of existence should be known as the path leading to the origination of suffering.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今当说有身集趣道及有身集灭道。云何有身集趣道？愚痴无闻凡夫，见不如实知色集、色灭、色味、色患、色离。不如实知故，乐色、叹色、著色、住色；乐色、叹色、著色、住色故，爱乐取；缘取有，缘有生，缘生、老、病、死、忧、悲、苦、恼。如是纯大苦聚生。如是受、想、行、识广说，是名有身集趣道。比丘！有身集趣道，当知即是苦集趣道。

"What is the path leading to the cessation of the origination of existence? The well-learned noble disciple truly knows form, the origination of form, the cessation of form, the taste of form, the danger of form, and the escape from form. Because they truly know this, they do not delight in form, do not praise form, are not attached to form, and do not dwell in form. Because they do not delight in form, do not praise form, are not attached to form, and do not dwell in form, their craving for form ceases. When craving ceases, grasping ceases. When grasping ceases, existence ceases. When existence ceases, birth ceases. When birth ceases, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress cease. Thus ceases this mass of suffering. The same is said extensively for feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. This is called the path leading to the cessation of the origination of existence. The path leading to the cessation of the origination of existence is the path leading to the cessation of suffering. Therefore, it is said to be the path leading to the cessation of the origination of existence."

「云何有身集灭道？多闻圣弟子如实知色、色集、色灭、色味、色患、色离。如实知故，于色不乐、不叹、不著、不住；不乐、不叹、不著、不住故，彼色爱乐灭；爱乐灭则取灭，取灭则有灭，有灭则生灭，生灭则老、病、死、忧、悲、苦、恼，纯大苦聚灭。如色，受、想、行、识亦如是，是名有身灭道迹。有身灭道迹，则是苦灭道迹，是故说有身灭道迹。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

As will be explained, the same is said for existence and what is to be known.

如当说，有及当知，亦如是说。
