SĀ 71

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 71（七一） 有身

Parallel to: SN 22.105 / AN 10.20 (~) / EĀ 46.2 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "I will now explain the existence of the body, the origin of the existence of the body, the cessation of the existence of the body, and the path leading to the cessation of the existence of the body. Listen carefully and consider it well, I will explain it to you. What is the existence of the body? It refers to the five aggregates of clinging. What are the five? They are the form aggregate of clinging, the feeling aggregate of clinging, the perception aggregate of clinging, the volitional formations aggregate of clinging, and the consciousness aggregate of clinging. This is called the existence of the body. What is the origin of the existence of the body? It is future existence, together with craving and delight, clinging to various things. This is called the origin of the existence of the body. What is the cessation of the existence of the body? It is the remainderless fading away and cessation of future existence, together with craving and delight, clinging to various things. This is called the cessation of the existence of the body.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「我今当说有身、有身集、有身灭、有身灭道迹。谛听，善思，当为汝说。云何有身？谓五受阴。云何为五？色受阴，受、想、行、识受阴，是名有身。云何有身集？当来有爱，贪、喜俱，彼彼染著，是名有身集。云何有身灭？当来有爱，贪、喜俱，彼彼乐著无余断、吐、尽、离欲、灭，是名有身灭。

What is the path leading to the cessation of the existence of the body? It is the Noble Eightfold Path, namely right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. This is called the path leading to the cessation of the existence of the body. This is called the explanation of the existence of the body, the origin of the existence of the body, the cessation of the existence of the body, and the path leading to the cessation of the existence of the body.

「云何有身灭道迹？谓八圣道，正见、正志、正语、正业、正命、正方便、正念、正定，是名有身灭道迹。是名当说有身、有身集、有身灭、有身灭道迹。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha had said rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说是经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

The rest is explained in the same way. The difference is: "One should know the existence of the body, one should know the cessation of the origin of the existence of the body, one should know the realization of the cessation of the existence of the body, and one should know the cultivation of the path leading to the cessation of the existence of the body."

余如是说。差别者：「当知有身，当知断有身集，当知证有身灭，当知修断有身道迹。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha had said rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

As will be explained, the existence and the knowledge of it are also explained in the same way. Moreover, the difference is: "Bhikṣus, knowing the existence of the body, cutting off the origin of the existence of the body, realizing the cessation of the existence of the body, cultivating the path leading to the cessation of the existence of the body, this is called a bhikṣu cutting off the fetters of desire and love, cultivating the uninterrupted path, and ultimately reaching the end of suffering."

如当说，有及当知，亦如是说。又复差别者：「比丘知有身，断有身集，证有身灭，修断有身道，是名比丘断爱欲缚诸结等法，修无间等，究竟苦边。」

Moreover, the difference is: "This is called a bhikṣu reaching the ultimate limit, ultimately free from defilements, ultimately practicing the pure brahmacarya, and being a pure and excellent person."

又复差别者：「是名比丘究竟边际，究竟离垢，究竟梵行，纯净上士。」

Moreover, the difference is: "This is called a bhikṣu who is an arhat, having exhausted all taints, having done what was to be done, having laid down the heavy burden, having attained his own benefit, having exhausted all bonds, and having attained liberation of mind through right knowledge."

又复差别者：「是名比丘阿罗汉尽诸有漏，所作已作，已舍重担，逮得己利，尽诸有结，正智心解脱。」

Moreover, the difference is: "This is called a bhikṣu who has broken through the barriers, crossed the moats, transcended the boundaries, escaped the guards, and raised the banner of the holy Dharma."

又复差别者：「是名比丘断关、度堑，超越境界，脱诸防逻，建圣法幢。」

Moreover, the difference is: "What is meant by 'breaking through the barriers'? It means severing the five lower fetters. What is meant by 'crossing the moats'? It means crossing the deep moat of ignorance. What is meant by 'transcending the boundaries'? It means ultimately ending beginningless birth and death. What is meant by 'escaping the guards'? It means the end of craving for existence. What is meant by 'raising the banner of the holy Dharma'? It means the end of conceit."

又复差别者：「云何断关？谓断五下分结。云何度堑？谓度无明深堑。云何超越境界？谓究竟无始生死。云何脱诸防逻？谓有爱尽。云何建圣法幢？谓我慢尽。」

Moreover, the difference is: "This is called a bhikṣu who has severed the five branches, accomplished the six branches, guarded the one, relied on the four kinds, abandoned the truths, separated from all seeking, purified the awakenings, ceased bodily activities, well liberated in mind, well liberated in wisdom, established the pure practice of the brahman, and is the unsurpassed person."

又复差别者：「是名比丘断五枝，成六枝，守护一，依四种，弃舍诸谛，离诸求，净诸觉，身行息，心善解脱，慧善解脱，纯一立梵行，无上士。」

The path has three kinds, true awakening also has three kinds, the
body is said to have four kinds, the arhat has six kinds.

其道有三种　　实觉亦三种有身四种说　　罗汉有六种
