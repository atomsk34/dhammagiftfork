SĀ 711

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 711（七一一） 无畏

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Vulture Peak in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城耆阇崛山中。

At that time, Prince Abhaya would walk and stroll every day, coming to where the Buddha was. After exchanging greetings and pleasantries with the World-Honored One, he sat to one side and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One, there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who hold this view and make this statement: 'Sentient beings are afflicted without cause or condition, and sentient beings are purified without cause or condition.' How does the World-Honored One explain this? "

时，有无畏王子，日日步涉，仿佯游行，来诣佛所，与世尊面相问讯慰劳已，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！有沙门、婆罗门作如是见，作如是说：『无因、无缘众生烦恼，无因、无缘众生清净。』世尊复云何？」

The Buddha told Abhaya, "The śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas speak without thinking, foolishly, without discernment, unskillfully, not knowing, not measuring, and say, 'Sentient beings are afflicted without cause or condition, and sentient beings are purified without cause or condition.' Why is that? Because sentient beings are afflicted with causes and conditions, and sentient beings are purified with causes and conditions. What are the causes and conditions for sentient beings to be afflicted? What are the causes and conditions for sentient beings to be purified? It is because sentient beings are excessively greedy, and they give rise to greedy thoughts about others' wealth and possessions, thinking, 'If I had these things, I would like them, not be separated from them, and enjoy them.' They give rise to hateful and violent thoughts towards other sentient beings, plotting to beat, bind, subdue, and do all kinds of evil to them. They create all kinds of difficulties and do not abandon their anger. Their bodies are sleepy and their minds are lazy and agitated, not inwardly tranquil. Their minds are always doubtful, doubting the past, future, and present. Abhaya, these are the causes and conditions for sentient beings to be afflicted. These are the causes and conditions for sentient beings to be purified."

佛告无畏：「沙门、婆罗门为其说，不思而说，愚痴、不辨、不善，非知思，不知量，作如是说：『无因、无缘众生烦恼，无因、无缘众生清净。』所以者何？有因、有缘众生烦恼，有因、有缘众生清净故。何因、何缘众生烦恼？何因、何缘众生清净？谓众生贪欲增上，于他财物、他众具而起贪言：『此物于我有者，好、不离、爱乐。』于他众生而起恨心、凶心，计挍、欲打、欲缚、欲伏，加诸不道。为造众难，不舍瞋恚，身睡眠、心懈怠、心掉动，内不寂静，心常疑惑，过去疑、未来疑、现在疑。无畏。如是因、如是缘众生烦恼。如是因、如是缘众生清净。」

Abhaya said to the Buddha, "Gautama, even one of these covers is enough to afflict the mind, let alone all of them." Abhaya said to the Buddha, "Gautama, what are the causes and conditions for sentient beings to be purified?"

无畏白佛：「瞿昙！一分之盖，足烦恼心，况复一切？」无畏白佛：「瞿昙！何因、何缘众生清净？」

The Buddha told Abhaya, "If a brahmin has one superior thought, which is determined, accomplished, done for a long time, and spoken for a long time, and can follow the recollection, then at that time he cultivates the factor of awakening of recollection. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of recollection, the factor of awakening of recollection is fulfilled. Having fulfilled the factor of awakening of recollection, he then chooses, discerns, and contemplates. At that time he cultivates the factor of awakening of the investigation of phenomena. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of the investigation of phenomena, the factor of awakening of the investigation of phenomena is fulfilled. Having chosen, discerned, and contemplated the phenomena, he then makes effort and applies skillful means. The factor of awakening of effort is cultivated here. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of effort, the factor of awakening of effort is fulfilled. Having made effort and applied skillful means, then joy arises, free from thoughts of food. He cultivates the factor of awakening of joy. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of joy, the factor of awakening of joy is fulfilled. Having fulfilled the factor of awakening of joy, body and mind rest in tranquility, then he cultivates the factor of awakening of tranquility. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of tranquility, the factor of awakening of tranquility is fulfilled. Having rested the body, then he delights. Having delighted, the mind is concentrated, then he cultivates the factor of awakening of concentration. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of concentration, the factor of awakening of concentration is fulfilled. Having fulfilled the factor of awakening of concentration, greed and sorrow cease, then the mind of equanimity arises, and he cultivates the factor of awakening of equanimity. Having cultivated the factor of awakening of equanimity, the factor of awakening of equanimity is fulfilled. Thus, Abhaya, these are the causes and conditions for sentient beings to be purified."

佛告无畏：「若婆罗门有一胜念，决定成就，久时所作，久时所说，能随忆念，当于尔时习念觉支。修念觉已，念觉满足，念觉满足已，则于选择分别思惟，尔时择法觉支修习。修择法觉支已，择法觉支满足，彼选择分别思量法已，则精进方便。精进觉支于此修习，修精进觉支已，精进觉支满足，彼精进方便已，则欢喜生，离诸食想。修喜觉支，修喜觉支已，则喜觉支满足，喜觉支满足已，身心猗息，则修猗觉支。修猗觉支已，猗觉满足，身猗息已，则爱乐，爱乐已心定，则修定觉支。修定觉支已，定觉满足，定觉满足已，贪忧灭，则舍心生，修舍觉支。修舍觉支已，舍觉支满足。如是，无畏！此因、此缘众生清净。」

Abhaya said to Gautama, "If one factor of awakening is fulfilled, it can purify sentient beings, let alone all of them." Abhaya said to the Buddha, "Gautama, what should this sutra be named? How should it be upheld?"

无畏白瞿昙：「若一分满足，令众生清净，况复一切？」无畏白佛：「瞿昙！当何名此经？云何奉持？」

The Buddha told Prince Abhaya, "This sutra should be named the 'Awakening Factors Sutra'."

佛告无畏王子：「当名此为觉支经。」

Abhaya said to the Buddha, "Gautama! This is the supreme awakening factor. Gautama! I am a prince, always seeking comfort and happiness, but now coming up the mountain, my four limbs are exhausted. Hearing the Buddha's teaching on the awakening factors, I have forgotten my fatigue."

无畏白佛：「瞿昙！此为最胜觉分。瞿昙！我是王子，安乐亦常求安乐，而希出入，今来上山，四体疲极，得闻瞿昙说觉支经，悉忘疲劳。」

Having spoken thus, Prince Abhaya, hearing the Buddha's words, rejoiced and followed the Buddha's teachings. He rose from his seat, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and departed.

佛说此已，王子无畏闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，从座起，稽首礼佛足而去。
