SĀ 803

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 803（八〇三） 一明

Parallel to: MN 62 / MN 118 / SN 54.1 / SĀ 815 (~) / SĀ³ 3.15 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "Cultivate mindfulness of inhalation and exhalation. If a bhikṣu cultivates mindfulness of inhalation and exhalation, and cultivates it extensively, he will attain mental and physical tranquility, with perception and contemplation, pacified, pure, and with clear discernment. What is meant by cultivating mindfulness of inhalation and exhalation extensively, attaining mental and physical tranquility, with perception and contemplation, pacified, pure, and with clear discernment? This bhikṣu, whether dwelling in a village or town, in the morning puts on his robe, takes his alms bowl, enters the village to beg for food, well-protecting his body, guarding the sense-doors, and well-restraining his mind. After begging for food, he returns to his dwelling place, puts away his robe and bowl, washes his feet, and then either enters a forest, an empty room, the shade of a tree, or an open space, sits upright with a straight body, focuses his mind on the front, cuts off worldly greed and love, is free from desire and pure, and is free from anger, sleepiness, agitation, regret, and doubt. He has transcended all doubts and has a determined mind towards all wholesome dharmas. He is far removed from the five hindrances and afflictions that weaken wisdom and obstruct the attainment of nirvāṇa. He focuses his mind on internal breathing, well-trains in focusing his mind on external breathing. He is aware of long and short breaths, and is well-trained in being aware of all bodily inhalations. He is aware of all bodily exhalations, and is well-trained in being aware of all bodily movements during inhalation. He is aware of all bodily movements during exhalation, and is well-trained in being aware of joy, bliss, and mental activity. He is aware of mental activity during inhalation, and is well-trained in being aware of mental activity during exhalation. He is aware of the mind, mental delight, mental concentration, and mental liberation during inhalation, and is well-trained in being aware of mental liberation during inhalation. He is aware of mental liberation during exhalation, and is well-trained in being aware of mental liberation during exhalation. He observes impermanence, observes cessation, observes dispassion, and observes cessation during inhalation, and is well-trained in observing cessation during inhalation. He observes cessation during exhalation, and is well-trained in observing cessation during exhalation. This is called cultivating mindfulness of inhalation and exhalation, attaining mental and physical tranquility, with perception and contemplation, pacified, pure, and with clear discernment."

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「修习安那般那念。若比丘修习安那般那念，多修习者，得身心止息，有觉有观，寂灭、纯一，明分想修习满足。何等为修习安那般那念多修习已，身心止息，有觉有观，寂灭、纯一，明分想修习满足？是比丘若依聚落、城邑止住，晨朝著衣持钵，入村乞食，善护其身，守诸根门，善系心住。乞食已，还住处，举衣钵，洗足已，或入林中、闲房、树下，或空露地，端身正坐，系念面前，断世贪爱，离欲清净，瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑断，度诸疑惑，于诸善法心得决定。远离五盖烦恼于心，令慧力羸，为障碍分，不趣涅槃。念于内息，系念善学，念于外息，系念善学。息长息短，觉知一切身入息，于一切身入息善学，觉知一切身出息，于一切身出息善学。觉知一切身行息入息，于一切身行息入息善学，觉知一切身行息出息，于一切身行息出息善学。觉知喜，觉知乐，觉知心] cf.【丽】行，觉知心行息入息，于觉知心行息入息善学；觉知心行息出息，于觉知心行息出息善学。觉知心，觉知心悦，觉知心定，觉知心解脱入息，于觉知心解脱入息善学，觉知心解脱出息，于觉知心解脱出息善学，观察无常，观察断，观察无欲，观察灭入息，于观察灭入息善学；观察灭出息，于观察灭出息善学，是名修安那般那念，身止息、心止息，有觉有观，寂灭、纯一，明分想修习满足。」
