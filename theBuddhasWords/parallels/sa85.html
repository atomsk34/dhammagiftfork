SĀ 85

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 85（八五） 正观察

Parallel to: SN 22.46

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Bhikṣus! Where do you not see me, something different from me, or a combination of us?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「比丘！于何所不见我、异我、相在？」

The bhikṣus said to the Buddha, "The World-Honored One is the root of the Dharma, the eye of the Dharma, and the reliance of the Dharma. Please explain it for us! When we have heard it, we will uphold and practice it as taught."

比丘白佛：「世尊为法根、法眼、法依，唯愿为说！诸比丘闻已，如说奉行。」

The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "Listen carefully and consider it well, I will explain it for you. Do you not see any self, anything different from the self, or a combination of them in form? The same is true for feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. Bhikṣu! Is form permanent or impermanent?"

佛告比丘：「谛听，善思，当为汝说。于色不见有我、异我、相在不？于受、想、行、识亦复如是。比丘！色为是常、无常耶？」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "Impermanent, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「无常。世尊！」

The Buddha said, "Bhikṣu! If it is impermanent, is it suffering?"

佛言：「比丘！若无常者，是苦不？」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "It is suffering, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「是苦。世尊！」

"Bhikṣu! If it is impermanent and suffering, a changing and transforming phenomenon, would a well-versed noble disciple see self, other, or characteristics in it?"

「比丘！若无常、苦，是变易法，多闻圣弟子宁于中见我、异我、相在不？」

The bhikṣu said to the Buddha, "No, World-Honored One!"

比丘白佛：「不也，世尊！」

"So it is with feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. Therefore, bhikṣu! All forms, whether past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse or subtle, beautiful or ugly, far or near, are not self, not different from self, nor do they coexist with self. So it is with feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness. Bhikṣu! The well-versed noble disciple observes the five aggregates of grasping as not self and not belonging to self. Observing in this way, they do not grasp at anything in the world. Not grasping, they are not attached. Not attached, they realize Nirvāṇa for themselves: 'My birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, I will not experience another existence after this one.'"

「受、想、行、识亦复如是。是故，比丘！诸所有色，若过去、若未来、若现在，若内、若外，若麤、若细，若好、若丑，若远、若近，彼一切非我、不异我、不相在。受、想、行、识亦复如是。比丘！多闻圣弟子观察五受阴非我、非我所。如是观察者，于诸世间都无所取，无所取者无所著，无所著故自觉涅槃：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished what he had to say. The bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
