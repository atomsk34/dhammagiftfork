SĀ 860

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 860（八六〇） 田业

Parallel to: SN 55.6

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Thus have I heard:

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。前三月结夏安居竟，众多比丘集于食堂，为世尊缝衣。

At that time, there were two brothers, the elder Liśīdatta and Pūrṇa, who were farming in the Deer Park. Hearing that many bhikṣus were in the dining hall sewing robes for the Tathāgata, they said, "The Tathāgata will soon finish sewing his robes, put on his robe and alms bowl, and travel through the human world." Having heard this, they told a man, "You should now go to the World-Honored One to pay respects to him. If he is definitely leaving, quickly come and tell me."

时，有长者梨师达多及富兰那兄弟二人，于鹿径泽中修治田业，闻众多比丘在于食堂，为世尊缝衣，言：「如来不久作衣竟，著衣持钵，人间游行。」闻已，语一士夫言：「汝今当往诣世尊所，瞻视世尊；若必去者，速来语我。」

At that time, the man immediately accepted the order, went to a place, saw the World-Honored One leave, and quickly returned to report to Liśīdatta and Pūrṇa, "The World-Honored One has come, together with the great assembly."

时，彼士夫即受教勅，往到一处，见世尊出，即速来还白梨师达多及富兰那：「世尊已来，及诸大众。」

At that time, Liśīdatta and Pūrṇa went to welcome the World-Honored One. The World-Honored One saw from afar that Liśīdatta and Pūrṇa were coming along the road, so he immediately went to the side of the road, spread out his sitting mat, and sat there with his body upright.

时，梨师达多及富兰那往迎世尊。世尊遥见梨师达多及富兰那随路而来，即出路边，敷尼师坛，正身端坐。

Liśīdatta and Pūrṇa bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, then sat to one side and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Now my four limbs and joints are dislocated, and my memory is confused. When will I be able to see the World-Honored One and the bhikshus I know again? The World-Honored One has now gone from Kośala to Kāśi, from Kāśi to Magadha, from Magadha to Aṅga, from Aṅga to Sumā, from Sumā to Pundravardhana, from Pundravardhana to Kāliṅga. Therefore, I am now extremely distressed. When will I be able to see the World-Honored One and the bhikshus I know again?"

梨师达多及富兰那稽首佛足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我今四体支解，四方易韵，所忆念事，今悉迷忘，何时当复得见世尊及诸知识比丘？世尊今出至拘萨罗，从拘萨罗至伽尸，从伽尸至摩罗，从摩罗至摩竭陀，从摩竭陀至殃伽，从殃伽至修摩，从修摩至分陀罗，从分陀罗至迦陵伽。是故我今极生忧苦，何时当复得见世尊及诸知识比丘？」

The Buddha told Liśīdatta and Pūrṇa, "Whether you see the Tathāgata or not, whether you see the bhikshus you know or not, you should practice the six recollections according to the time. What are the six? You should recollect the Tathāgata..." (The rest is explained in detail.) "...and the gods. However, householder! The home is noisy and the home is defiled. The renunciate life is empty and secluded. It is difficult for a layperson to dwell in the non-home, to be single-mindedly pure, completely and perfectly pure, and to practice the pure brahmacarya."

佛告梨师达多及富兰那：「汝见如来及不见如来，见诸知识比丘及不见，汝且随时修习六念。何等为六？汝当念如来事……」广说乃至「念天。然其长者！在家愦闹，在家染著；出家空闲，难可俗人处于非家，一向鲜洁，纯一满净，梵行清白。」

The householder said to the Buddha, "Amazing! World-Honored One! You have well spoken this Dharma: 'The home is noisy and the home is defiled. The renunciate life is empty and secluded. It is difficult for a layperson to dwell in the non-home, to be single-mindedly pure, completely and perfectly pure, and to practice the pure brahmacarya.' I am a minister of King Prasenajit. When King Prasenajit wants to enter the garden, he has me ride a large elephant, with the king's foremost concubine in front of me and another behind me, and I sit in between. When the elephant goes downhill, the one in front embraces my neck and the one behind clings to my back; when the elephant goes uphill, the one behind embraces my neck and the one in front clings to my collar. Those concubines, in order to entertain the king, wear silk and brocade clothes, apply various wonderful fragrances, and adorn themselves with necklaces. When I go out with them, I always protect three things: first, I control the elephant, fearing it will lose its proper path. Second, I protect my own mind, fearing it will give rise to defilement. Third, I protect myself, fearing it will fall. World-Honored One! At that time, I did not have even a single moment of improper thought towards the king's concubines."

长者白佛：「奇哉！世尊！善说此法：『在家愦闹，在家染著，出家空闲，难可俗人处于非家，一向鲜洁，纯一满净，梵行清白。』我是波斯匿王大臣，波斯匿王欲入园观，令我乘于大象，载王第一宫女，一在我前，一在我后，我坐其中。象下坂时，前者抱我项，后者攀我背；象上坂时，后者抱我颈，前者攀我衿。彼诸婇女为娱乐王故，衣缯彩] cf.【丽】衣，著众妙香，璎珞庄严。我与同游，常护三事：一者御象，恐失正道。二自护心，恐生染著。三自护持，恐其颠坠。世尊！我于尔时，于王婇女，无一刹那不正思惟。」

The Buddha told the householder, "Excellent! Excellent! You are able to protect your mind well."

佛告长者：「善哉！善哉！能善护心。」

The householder said to the Buddha, "All the wealth I have in my home, I always share with the World-Honored One, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and others. I do not consider it as mine."

长者白佛：「我在家中所有财物，常与世尊及诸比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷、等共受用，不计我所。」

The Buddha told the householder, "Excellent! Excellent! You are the richest man in the country of Kosala, and yet you are able to not consider your wealth as yours."

佛告长者：「善哉！善哉！汝拘萨罗国钱财巨富，无有与汝等者，而能于财，不计我所。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke various teachings for the sake of the householder, showing, teaching, illuminating, and delighting him. Having shown, taught, illuminated, and delighted him, he rose from his seat and departed.

尔时，世尊为彼长者种种说法，示、教、照、喜，示、教、照、喜已，从座而去。
