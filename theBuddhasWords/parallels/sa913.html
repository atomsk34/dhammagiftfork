SĀ 913

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 913（九一三） 驴姓

Parallel to: SN 42.11 / SĀ² 2.128

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Once, the Buddha was traveling among the human realms of the wrestlers, and arrived at the place of Uḍḍiyāna, staying in the Parrot Jambu Grove.

一时，佛在力士人间游行，到欝鞞罗住处鹦鹉阎浮林。

At that time, the village headman Khadā was told that the śramaṇa Gautama was traveling among the human realms of the wrestlers, and had arrived at the village of Uḍḍiyāna, in the Parrot Jambu Grove, teaching the present-life suffering, its origin, and its cessation. He thought, "I shall go to that śramaṇa Gautama. If I go to that śramaṇa Gautama, he will surely teach me the present-life suffering, its origin, and its cessation." He then went to that village of Uḍḍiyāna, approached the World-Honored One, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I have heard that the World-Honored One always teaches people the present-life suffering, its origin, and its cessation. Excellent! World-Honored One! Please teach me the present-life suffering, its origin, and its cessation."

时，有竭昙聚落主闻沙门瞿昙在力士人间游行，至欝鞞罗聚落鹦鹉阎浮林，说现法苦集、苦没。我当往诣彼沙门瞿昙，若我诣沙门瞿昙者，彼必为我说现法苦集、苦没。即往彼欝鞞罗聚落，诣世尊所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我闻世尊常为人说现法苦集、苦没。善哉！世尊！为我说现法苦集、苦没。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "If I were to speak of the past suffering, its origin, and its cessation, I would know whether you would believe or not, desire or not, remember or not, and be happy or not. Are you suffering now? If I were to speak of the future suffering, its origin, and its cessation, I would know whether you would believe or not, desire or not, remember or not, and be happy or not. Are you suffering now? I now speak of the present suffering, its origin, and its cessation here. Village headman! All suffering that sentient beings experience arises from desire. Desire gives rise to suffering, is the origin of suffering, is the cause of suffering, and is the condition for suffering.

佛告聚落主：「我若说过去法苦集、苦没者，知汝于彼为信、为不信，为欲、不欲，为念、不念，为乐、不乐，汝今苦不？我若说未来苦集、苦没者，知汝于彼为信、不信，为欲、不欲，为念、不念，为乐、不乐，汝今苦不？我今于此说现法苦集、苦没，聚落主！若众生所有苦生，彼一切皆以欲为本，欲生、欲集、欲起、欲因、欲缘而苦生。」

The village headman said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! You have spoken the Dharma in brief, without explaining it in detail. I do not understand. Excellent! World-Honored One! Please explain it in detail so that I may understand."

聚落主白佛言：「世尊！极略说法，不广分别，我所不解。善哉！世尊！唯愿广说，令我等解。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "I will ask you a question. Please answer as you see fit. Village headman! What do you think? If sentient beings were to be bound, beaten, blamed, or killed in this Ujjenī village, would you feel sorrow, grief, distress, and suffering?"

佛告聚落主：「我今问汝，随汝意说，聚落主！于意云何？若众生于此欝鞞罗聚落住者，是若缚、若打、若责、若杀，汝心当起忧、悲、恼、苦不？」

The village headman said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! It is not always so. If sentient beings who live in this Ujjenī village have desire, greed, love, and attachment towards me, and are close to me, then if they are bound, beaten, blamed, or killed, I will feel sorrow, grief, distress, and suffering. But if those sentient beings have no desire, greed, love, or attachment towards me, and are not close to me, then why should I feel sorrow, grief, distress, and suffering when they are bound, beaten, blamed, or killed?"

聚落主白佛言：「世尊！亦不一向。若诸众生于此欝鞞罗聚落住者，于我有欲、有贪、有爱、有念、相习近者，彼遭若缚、若打、若责、若杀，我则生忧、悲、恼、苦。若彼众生所无欲、贪、爱、念、相习近者，彼遭缚、打、责、杀，我何为横生忧、悲、恼、苦？」

The Buddha told the village headman, "Therefore, you should know that the various sufferings of sentient beings all arise from desire. Desire gives rise to desire, cultivates desire, arouses desire, causes desire, and conditions desire, thus giving rise to various sufferings. Village headman! What do you think? If your parents were not to see each other, would you feel desire, greed, love, and longing?"

佛告聚落主：「是故当知，众生种种苦生，彼一切皆以欲为本，欲生、欲习、欲起、欲因、欲缘而生众苦。聚落主！于意云何？汝依父母不相见者，则生欲、贪、爱、念不？」

The village headman said, "No, World-Honored One!"

聚落主言：「不也，世尊！」

"Village headman! What do you think? If you see or hear about their parents, will you feel desire, love, and longing?"

「聚落主！于意云何？若见、若闻彼依父母，当起欲、爱、念不？」

The village headman said, "Yes, World-Honored One!"

聚落主言：「如是，世尊！」

He further asked, "Village headman! What do you think? If their parents are impermanent and change, will you feel sorrow, grief, distress, and suffering?"

复问：「聚落主！于意云何？彼依父母，若无常变异者，当起忧、悲、恼、苦不？」

The village headman said, "Yes, World-Honored One! If the parents I rely on are impermanent and change, I will be close to death. How could I not feel sorrow, grief, distress, and suffering?"

聚落主言：「如是，世尊！若依父母无常变异者，我或隣死，岂唯忧、悲、恼、苦？」

The Buddha told the village headman, "Therefore, you should know that all the suffering that sentient beings experience arises from desire. Desire gives rise to suffering, accumulates suffering, causes suffering, and conditions suffering."

佛告聚落主：「是故当知，若诸众生所有苦生，一切皆以爱欲为本，欲生、欲集、欲起、欲因、欲缘而生苦。」

The village headman said, "Amazing! World-Honored One! You have well spoken this analogy of relying on parents. I have parents to rely on, but they live elsewhere. I send messages to them every day to ask about their well-being. If the messenger does not return in time, I am already filled with worry and suffering. How much more so if they were to become impermanent, and I were not to feel worry and suffering?"

聚落主言：「奇哉！世尊！善说如此依父母譬。我有依父母，居在异处，我日日遣信问其安否；使未时还，我以忧苦，况复无常，而无忧苦？」

The Buddha told the village headman, "Therefore, I say that all the worry and suffering that sentient beings experience arise from desire. Desire gives rise to worry and suffering, accumulates worry and suffering, causes worry and suffering, and conditions worry and suffering."

佛告聚落主：「是故我说，其诸众生所有忧苦，一切皆以欲为根本，欲生、欲集、欲起、欲因、欲缘而生忧苦。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "If there are four things that are loved and cherished, and they become impermanent and change, then four kinds of worry and suffering arise. If there are three, two, or one thing that is loved and cherished, and it becomes impermanent and changes, then one kind of worry and suffering arises. Village headman! If there is nothing at all that is loved and cherished, then there is no worry and suffering or defilements." He then spoke a verse:

佛告聚落主：「若有四爱念无常变异者，则四忧苦生；若三、二，若一爱念无常变异者，则一忧苦生。聚落主！若都无爱念者，则无忧苦尘劳。」即说偈言：

If there is no worldly love and cherishing, then there is no worry, suffering, or
defilements. All worry and suffering are extinguished, like a lotus flower not being stained by water.

「若无世间爱念者，　　则无忧苦尘劳患，一切忧苦消灭尽，　　犹如莲花不著水。」

When the World-Honored One spoke this Dharma, the village headman Gayāśīrṣa was free from defilements, attained the purity of the Dharma eye, saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, deeply penetrated the Dharma, crossed over doubts, was not dependent on others, was not liberated by others, and attained fearlessness in the true Dharma and Vinaya. He rose from his seat, adjusted his robes, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "I am liberated, World-Honored One! I have transcended. From today onward, I take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha of bhikṣus. For as long as I live, I will be an upāsaka. Please remember me!"

当其世尊说是法时，揭昙聚落主远尘离垢，得法眼净，见法得法，深入于法，度诸狐疑不由于他、不由他度，于正法、律得无所畏。从坐起，整衣服，合掌白佛：「已度。世尊！我以超越。我从今日，归佛、归法、归比丘僧，尽其寿命为优婆塞，唯忆持我！」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished. The village headman Gayāśīrṣa, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and followed the teachings. He bowed and departed.

佛说此经已，揭昙聚落主闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
