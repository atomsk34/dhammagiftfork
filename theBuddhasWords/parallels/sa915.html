SĀ 915

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 915（九一五） 种田

Parallel to: SN 42.7 / SĀ² 2.130

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Good Clothing Mango Grove in the village of Nala.

一时，佛住那罗聚落好衣庵罗园中。

At that time, the village headman of the Kṣatriyas, who was formerly a disciple of the Nirgranthas, went to where the Nirgranthas were, bowed at their feet, and sat to one side.

时，有刀师氏聚落主，先是尼揵弟子，诣尼揵所，礼尼揵足，退坐一面。

At that time, the Nirgranthas said to the village headman, "Can you engage in a thorny debate with the śramaṇa Gautama, causing the śramaṇa Gautama to be unable to speak or not speak?"

尔时，尼揵语聚落主：「汝能共沙门瞿昙作蒺［卄／梨］论，令沙门瞿昙不得语、不得不语？」

The village headman said to the Nirgranthas, "Venerable ones! What is a thorny debate that causes the śramaṇa Gautama to be unable to speak or not speak?"

聚落主白尼揵：「阿梨！何等为蒺［卄／梨］论，令沙门瞿昙不得语、不得不语耶？」

The Nirgranthas said to the village headman, "You go to where the śramaṇa Gautama is and say this: 'Gautama, do you not always wish to comfort all beings and praise comforting all beings?' If he says no, you should say, 'What is the difference between Gautama and an ordinary fool?' If he says he always wishes to comfort all beings and praises comforting all beings, you should further ask, 'If you wish to comfort all beings, why do you sometimes teach the Dharma to one kind of person and not to another kind of person?' Asking in this way is called a thorny debate that causes that śramaṇa Gautama to be unable to speak or not speak."

尼揵语聚落主：「汝往沙门瞿昙所，作如是言：『瞿昙不常欲安慰一切众生、赞叹安慰一切众生耶？』若言不者，应语言：『瞿昙与凡愚夫有何等异？』若言常欲安慰一切众生，赞叹安慰一切众生者，复应问言：『若欲安慰一切众生者，以何等故，或为一种人说法？或不为一种人说法？』作如是问者，是名蒺［卄／梨］论，令彼沙门瞿昙不得语、不得不语。」

At that time, the village headman accepted the Nirgranthas' advice and went to where the Buddha was. After respectfully inquiring about his well-being, he sat to one side and said to the Buddha, "Gautama, do you not wish to always comfort all beings and praise comforting all beings?"

尔时，聚落主受尼揵劝进已，往诣佛所，恭敬问讯已，退坐一面，白佛言：「瞿昙岂不欲常安慰一切众生，叹说安慰一切众生？」

The Buddha told the village headman, "The Tathāgata has long been compassionate and comforting to all beings, and he also always praises comforting all beings."

佛告聚落主：「如来长夜慈愍安慰一切众生，亦常叹说安慰一切众生。」

The village headman said to the Buddha, "If that is so, why does the Tathāgata teach the Dharma for some people but not for others?"

聚落主白佛言：「若然者，如来何故为一种人说法？又复不为一种人说法？」

The Buddha said to the village headman, "I will ask you a question, please answer as you wish. Village headman! Suppose there are three kinds of fields, one fertile and rich, the second medium, and the third barren. Which field would the farmer plow and sow first?"

佛告聚落主：「我今问汝，随意答我。聚落主！譬如有三种田，有一种田沃壤肥泽，第二田中，第三田［塉-月＋目］薄，云何？聚落主！彼田主先于何田耕治下种？」

The village headman said, "Gautama! The best fertile and rich field would be plowed and sown first."

聚落主言：「瞿昙！于最沃壤肥泽者，先耕下种。」

"Village headman! Which field would be plowed and sown next?"

「聚落主！复于何田次耕下种？」

The village headman said, "Gautama! The middle field would be plowed and sown next."

聚落主言：「瞿昙！当于中田次耕下种。」

The Buddha said to the village headman, "Which field would be plowed and sown next?"

佛告聚落主：「复于何田次耕下种？」

The village headman said, "The lowest and thinnest field would be plowed and sown next."

聚落主言：「当于最下［塉-月＋目］薄之田，次耕下种。」

The Buddha said to the village headman, "Why is it so?"

佛告聚落主：「何故如是？」

The village headman said, "I do not want to abandon the field and preserve the seeds."

聚落主言：「不欲废田存种而已。」

The Buddha said to the village headman, "I am also like this. The fertile and moist field is also like my bhikshus and bhikshunis. I always expound the true Dharma for them, which is good in the beginning, middle, and end, with good meaning and good taste, pure and perfect, pure and bright in the practice of Brahma. They hear the Dharma and rely on my house, my continent, my shelter, my shade, and my destination. They always look at me with pure eyes and abide, thinking, 'The Dharma spoken by the Buddha, I will all uphold, so that I may benefit for a long time, peacefully and happily abiding.'

佛告聚落主：「我亦如是，如彼沃壤肥泽田者，我诸比丘、比丘尼亦复如是。我常为彼演说正法，初、中、后善，善义善味，纯一满净，梵行清白，开示显现。彼闻法已，依于我舍、我洲、我覆、我荫、我趣，常以净眼，观我而住，作如是念：『佛所说法，我悉受持，令我长夜以义饶益，安隐乐住。』

Village headman! The middle field is also like my lay male and female disciples. I also expound the true Dharma for them, which is good in the beginning, middle, and end, with good meaning and good taste, pure and perfect, pure and bright in the practice of Brahma. They hear the Dharma and rely on my house, my continent, my shelter, my shade, and my destination. They always look at me with pure eyes and abide, thinking, 'The World-Honored One's teachings, I will all uphold, so that I may benefit for a long time, peacefully and happily abiding.'

「聚落主！如彼中田者，我弟子优婆塞、优婆夷亦复如是。我亦为彼演说正法，初、中、后善，善义善味，纯一满净，梵行清白，开发显示。彼闻法已，依于我舍、我洲、我覆、我荫、我趣，常以净眼，观察我住，作如是念：『世尊说法，我悉受持，令我长夜以义饶益，安隐乐住。』

"Village headman! The lowest field of the farmer is also like my teachings for the non-Buddhist practitioners, the Nirgrantha ascetics. I also expound the Dharma for them, which is good in the beginning, middle, and end, with good meaning and good taste, pure and perfect, pure and bright in the practice of Brahma. However, among them, those who hear little of the Dharma, I also teach them, and those who hear much of the Dharma, I also teach them. However, among that assembly, those who attain a single phrase of the Dharma from my well-spoken teachings and understand its meaning, they will also benefit for a long time, peacefully and happily abiding."

「聚落主！如彼田家最下田者，如是我为诸外道异学尼揵子辈，亦为说法，初、中、后善，善义善味，纯一满净，梵行清白，开示显现，然于彼等少闻法者，亦为其说，多闻法者，亦为其说。然其彼众于我善说法中，得一句法，知其义者，亦复长夜以义饶益，安隐乐住。」

At that time, the village headman said to the Buddha, "How wonderful! World-Honored One! You have well spoken this parable of the three fields."

时，聚落主白佛：「甚奇！世尊！善说如是三种田譬。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "Listen to me as I further explain with a parable. It is like a man having three water vessels that are not pierced, not broken, and also not leaking. The second vessel is not pierced or broken, but it leaks. The third vessel is pierced, broken, and leaks. What do you think, village headman? Among these three kinds of vessels, where would that man always keep clean water?"

佛告聚落主：「汝听我更说譬类。譬如士夫有三水器，不穿不坏，亦不津漏。第二器不穿不坏，而有津漏。第三器者，穿坏津漏。云何？聚落主！彼士夫三种器中，常持净水著何等器中？」

The village headman said, "Gautama! One should first put water in the one that is not pierced, not broken, and not leaking."

聚落主言：「瞿昙！当以不穿不坏、不津漏] cf.【丽】者，先以盛水。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "Then, which vessel should one put water in next?"

佛告聚落主：「次复应以何器盛水？」

The village headman said, "Gautama! One should next put water in the vessel that is not pierced, not broken, but leaking."

聚落主言：「瞿昙！当持彼器不穿不坏而津漏者，次以盛水。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "When that vessel is full, in which vessel should one put water next?"

佛告聚落主：「彼器满已，复以何器为后盛水？」

The village headman said, "One should put water in the last vessel that is pierced, broken, and leaking. Why? Because it is needed for small use in a short time."

聚落主言：「以穿坏津漏之器最后盛水。所以者何？须臾之间，供小用故。」

The Buddha told the village headman, "The man who has an undamaged, unbroken, and non-leaking vessel is like my disciples, the monks and nuns. I always teach them the true Dharma, and they benefit and dwell in peace and happiness for a long time. The second vessel that is undamaged and unbroken but leaking is like my disciples, the male and female lay followers. I always teach them the true Dharma, and they benefit and dwell in peace and happiness for a long time. The third vessel that is damaged, broken, and leaking is like the non-Buddhist ascetics and Nirgranthas. I also teach them the true Dharma, which is good in the beginning, middle, and end, with good meaning and good taste, pure and complete, the pure and bright practice of the Brahma-way. I teach them much and also teach them little. If they understand the meaning of even a single phrase I teach, they will also dwell in peace and happiness for a long time."

佛告聚落主：「如彼士夫不穿不坏、不津漏器，诸弟子比丘、比丘尼亦复如是。我常为彼演说正法，乃至长夜以义饶益，安隐乐住。如第二器不穿不坏而津漏者，我诸弟子优婆塞、优婆夷亦复如是。我常为彼演说正法，乃至长夜以义饶益，安隐乐住，如第三器穿坏津漏者，外道异学诸尼揵辈亦复如是。我亦为彼演说正法，初、中、后善，善义善味，纯一满净，梵行清白，开示显现。多亦为说，少亦为说。彼若于我说一句法，知其义者，亦得长夜安隐乐住。」

At that time, the village headman of the Kṣatriyas heard what the Buddha said, and his heart was greatly terrified, his body hair stood on end. He went forward, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and repented, saying, "World-Honored One! I am foolish, ignorant, unskillful, and unable to discern. I did not speak the truth and made false and deceptive statements to the World-Honored One!"

时，刀师氏聚落主闻佛所说，心大恐怖，身毛皆竖，前礼佛足悔过：「世尊！如愚如痴，不善不辩，于世尊所不谛真实，虚伪妄说！」

Hearing what the Buddha said, he joyfully rejoiced, bowed at the feet [of the Buddha], and departed.

闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，礼足而去。
