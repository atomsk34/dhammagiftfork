SĀ 922

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 922（九二二） 鞭影

Parallel to: AN 4.113 / SĀ² 2.148

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "In the world, there are four kinds of good horses. There is a good horse that, when harnessed to a carriage, gallops swiftly upon seeing the shadow of the whip, skillfully observing the driver's demeanor, and going at a slow or fast pace to the left or right according to the driver's mind. This is called, bhikṣus, the first virtue of a good horse in the world. Furthermore, bhikṣus, a good horse in the world cannot observe itself in its own shadow, but when a whip or stick touches its hair or tail, it can then be startled and swift, observing the driver's mind, and going at a slow or fast pace to the left or right. This is called the second good horse in the world. Furthermore, bhikṣus, if a good horse in the world cannot observe its own shadow, and when its skin and fur are touched, it can follow the mind of the driver, but when the whip or stick slightly injures its skin and flesh, it can then be startled and observe, following the driver's mind, going at a slow or fast pace to the left or right. This is called, bhikṣus, the third good horse. Furthermore, bhikṣus, a good horse in the world cannot observe its own shadow, and when its skin and fur are touched, and when its skin and flesh are slightly injured, it is then pierced with an iron awl, penetrating the skin and injuring the bones, and only then is it startled, bringing the carriage to the road, following the driver's mind, going at a slow or fast pace to the left or right. This is called the fourth good horse in the world.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「世有四种良马，有良马驾以平乘，顾其鞭影驰𫘝，善能观察御者形势，迟速左右，随御者心。是名，比丘！世间良马第一之德。复次，比丘！世间良马不能顾影而自惊察，然以鞭杖触其毛尾则能惊速察御者心，迟速左右，是名世间第二良马。复次，比丘！若世间良马不能顾影，及触皮毛能随人心，而以鞭杖小侵皮肉则能惊察，随御者心，迟速左右。是名，比丘！第三良马。复次，比丘！世间良马不能顾其鞭影，及触皮毛，小侵肤肉，乃以铁锥刺身，彻肤伤骨，然后方惊，牵车著路，随御者心，迟速左右，是名世间第四良马。

"So it is with the four kinds of good men in the true Dharma and Vinaya. What are the four? It means that when a good man hears that there are men and women in other villages who are sick and suffering, and even dying, having heard it, he is able to give rise to fear and rely on right contemplation, like that good horse who is tamed upon seeing its own shadow. This is called the first good man who is able to tame himself in the true Dharma and Vinaya. Furthermore, a good man is unable to hear or see the suffering of old age, sickness, and death of men and women in other villages, and give rise to fear and rely on right contemplation; but when he sees the suffering of old age, sickness, and death of men and women in other villages, he then gives rise to fear and relies on right contemplation, like that good horse who is quickly tamed upon touching its hair and tail, and follows the driver's mind. This is called the second good man who is able to tame himself in the true Dharma and Vinaya. Furthermore, a good man is unable to hear or see the suffering of old age, sickness, and death of men and women in other villages, and give rise to a fearful mind and rely on right contemplation; but when he sees the suffering of old age, sickness, and death of good friends and those he is close to in villages and towns, he then gives rise to fear and relies on right contemplation, like that good horse who is tamed upon touching its skin and flesh, and then follows the driver's mind. This is called a good man who is able to tame himself in the true Dharma and Vinaya. Furthermore, a good man is unable to hear or see the suffering of old age, sickness, and death of men and women in other villages, and those he is close to, and give rise to a fearful mind and rely on right contemplation; but when he is able to give rise to disgust and fear towards the suffering of old age, sickness, and death in his own body, and rely on right contemplation, like that good horse who is tamed upon penetrating the flesh and bones, and then follows the driver's mind. This is called the fourth good man who is able to tame himself in the true Dharma and Vinaya."

「如是于正法、律有四种善男子。何等为四？谓善男子闻他聚落有男子、女人疾病困苦，乃至死，闻已，能生恐怖，依正思惟，如彼良马顾影则调，是名第一善男子于正法、律能自调伏。复次，善男子不能闻他聚落若男、若女老、病、死苦，能生怖畏，依正思惟；见他聚落若男、若女老、病、死苦，则生怖畏，依正思惟，如彼良马触其毛尾，能速调伏，随御者心，是名第二善男子于正法、律能自调伏。复次，善男子不能闻、见他聚落中男子、女人老、病、死苦，生怖畏心，依正思惟，然见聚落、城邑有善知识及所亲近老、病、死苦，则生怖畏，依正思惟，如彼良马，触其肤肉，然后调伏，随御者心，是名善男子于圣法、律而自调伏。复次，善男子不能闻、见他聚落中男子、女人及所亲近老、病、死苦，生怖畏心，依正思惟；然于自身老、病、死苦能生厌怖，依正思惟，如彼良马侵肌彻骨，然后乃调，随御者心，是名第四善男子于圣法、律能自调伏。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "Good men, you should all learn this." The bhikṣus said, "Yes, World-Honored One." The Buddha said, "Good men, you should all learn this." The bhikṣus said, "Yes, World-Honored One." The Buddha said, "Good men,

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
