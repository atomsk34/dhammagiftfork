SĀ 923

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 923（九二三） 乘调

Parallel to: AN 4.111

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, there was a horse trainer named Kīrti who came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! I see that the world is very contemptible, like a flock of sheep. World-Honored One! Only I am capable of training horses. For wild and vicious horses, I use skillful means to make their faults and illnesses manifest in an instant, and then tame them according to their faults and illnesses."

时，有调马师名曰只尸，来诣佛所，稽首佛足，退坐一面，白佛言：「世尊！我观世间甚为轻贱，犹如群羊。世尊！唯我堪能调马；狂逸恶马，我作方便，须臾令彼态病悉现，随其态病，方便调伏。」

The Buddha said to the horse trainer village head, "With how many kinds of skillful means do you tame horses?"

佛告调马师聚落主：「汝以几种方便调伏于马？」

The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "There are three kinds of methods for taming vicious horses. What are the three? First, gentleness, second, roughness, and third, both gentleness and roughness."

马师白佛：「有三种法调伏恶马。何等为三？一者柔软，二者麁澁，三者柔软麁澁。」

The Buddha said to the village head, "If you use these three skillful means to tame horses, but they still cannot be tamed, what should you do?"

佛告聚落主：「汝以三种方便调马，犹不调者，当如之何？」

The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "If they still cannot be tamed, I will kill them. Why? To avoid disgracing me." The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One, you are the unsurpassed tamer of men. How many skillful means do you use to tame men?"

马师白佛：「遂不调者，便当杀之。所以者何？莫令辱我。」调马师白佛：「世尊是无上调御丈夫，为以几种方便调御丈夫？」

The Buddha said to the village head, "I also use three skillful means to tame men. What are the three? First, being completely gentle, second, being completely harsh, and third, being both gentle and harsh."

佛告聚落主：「我亦以三种方便调御丈夫。何等为三？一者一向柔软] cf.【丽】，二者一向麁澁，三者柔软麁澁。」

The Buddha said to the village head, "What is meant by being completely gentle? It is said: 'This is good conduct of the body, this is the result of good conduct of the body, this is good conduct of speech and mind, this is the result of good conduct of speech and mind, this is called heaven, this is called human, this is called a good rebirth, this is called nirvana, this is called gentleness.'

佛告聚落主：「所谓一向柔软者，如所说：『此是身善行，此是身善行报，此是口、意善行，此是口、意善行报，是名天、是名人、是名善趣化生、是名涅槃，是为柔软。』

What is meant by being harsh? It is said: 'This is bad conduct of the body, this is the result of bad conduct of the body, this is bad conduct of speech and mind, this is the result of bad conduct of speech and mind, this is called hell, this is called animal realm, this is called hungry ghost, this is called evil realm, this is called falling into evil realm, this is called the Tathāgata's harsh teaching.'

「麁澁者，如所说：『是身恶行，是身恶行报，是口、意恶行，是口、意恶行报，是名地狱、是名畜生、是名饿鬼、是名恶趣、是名堕恶趣，是名如来麁澁教也。』

"What is meant by being both gentle and harsh? It means that sometimes the Tathāgata speaks of good conduct of the body, sometimes he speaks of the result of good conduct of the body, sometimes he speaks of good conduct of speech and mind, sometimes he speaks of the result of good conduct of speech and mind; sometimes he speaks of bad conduct of the body, sometimes he speaks of the result of bad conduct of the body, sometimes he speaks of bad conduct of speech and mind, sometimes he speaks of the result of bad conduct of speech and mind. Thus he speaks of heaven, thus he speaks of human, thus he speaks of good realms, thus he speaks of nirvana. Thus he speaks of hell, thus he speaks of animal realm, hungry ghosts, thus he speaks of evil realms, thus he speaks of falling into evil realms. This is called the Tathāgata's teaching of gentleness and harshness."

「彼柔软麁澁俱者，谓如来有时说身善行，有时说身善行报，有时说口、意善行，有时说口、意善行报；有时说身恶行，有时说身恶行报，有时说口、意恶行，有时说口、意恶行报。如是名天、如是名人、如是名善趣、如是名涅槃。如是名地狱、如是名畜生、饿鬼、如是名恶趣、如是堕恶趣，是名如来柔软麁澁教。」

The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! If one uses these three methods to tame sentient beings, but there are those who cannot be tamed, what should be done?"

调马师白佛：「世尊！若以三种方便调伏众生，有不调者，当如之何？」

The Buddha told the village head, "You should kill it. Why? Don't let it disgrace me."

佛告聚落主：「亦当杀之。所以者何？莫令辱我。」

The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "If killing is impure in the World-Honored One's teachings, and the World-Honored One's teachings do not condone killing, then why do you now speak of killing? What is the meaning of this?"

调马师白佛言：「若杀生者，于世尊法为不清净，世尊法中亦不杀生，而今言杀，其义云何？」

The Buddha told the village head, "It is so! It is so! In the Tathāgata's teachings, killing is impure, and in the Tathāgata's teachings, one does not kill. However, in the Tathāgata's teachings, those who are not disciplined by the three kinds of instruction are no longer spoken to, taught, or admonished. Village head! What do you think? In the Tathāgata's teachings, those who are no longer spoken to, taught, or admonished, is this not death?"

佛告聚落主：「如是！如是！如来法中杀生不清净，如来法中亦不杀生；然如来法中以三种教授不调伏者，不复与语、不教、不诫。聚落主！于意云何？如来法中不复与语、不教、不诫，岂非死耶？」

The horse trainer said to the Buddha, "Indeed, World-Honored One! Not being spoken to, not being taught, and not being admonished is truly death. World-Honored One! For this reason, I will from today on be free from all evil and unwholesome actions."

调马师白佛：「实尔。世尊！不复与语，永不教、诫，真为死也。世尊！以是之故，我从今日离诸恶不善业。」

The Buddha told the village head, "Excellent!"

佛告聚落主：「善哉所说！」

At that time, the village headman Kisāgotama, the horse trainer, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and followed the teaching. He paid homage at the Buddha's feet and departed.

时，调马师聚落主只尸闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，礼足而去。
