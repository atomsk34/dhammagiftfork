SĀ 924

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 924（九二四） 有过

Parallel to: AN 8.13 (~) / AN 8.14 / SĀ 925 (~) / SĀ² 2.149 / SĀ² 2.150 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "There are eight faults in worldly horses. What are the eight? When a bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it kicks people with its hind legs, kneels on the ground with its front legs, and rears up to bite people. This is called the first fault of a worldly horse. Furthermore, when a bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it lowers its head and shakes the yoke. This is called the second fault of a worldly bad horse. Furthermore, when a worldly bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it goes off the road or sways the carriage to make it overturn. This is called the third fault. Furthermore, when a worldly bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it rears up and goes backward. This is called the fourth fault of a worldly bad horse. Furthermore, when a worldly bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it gets a little whip or breaks the reins and halters, and runs wildly. This is called the fifth fault. Furthermore, when a worldly bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it raises its two front legs and stands like a person. This is called the sixth fault. Furthermore, when a worldly bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it is whipped and remains still. This is called the seventh fault. Furthermore, when a worldly bad horse is about to be harnessed to a carriage, it crouches down with all four legs and does not get up. This is called the eighth fault.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「世间马有八态。何等为八？谓恶马临驾车时，后脚蹹人，前脚跪地，奋头啮人，是名世间马第一态。复次，恶马就驾车时，低头振轭，是名世间恶马第二之态。复次，世间恶马就驾车时，下道而去，或复偏厉车，令其翻覆，是名第三之态。复次，世间恶马就驾车时，仰头却行，是名世间恶马第四之态。复次，世间恶马就驾车时，小得鞭杖，或断缰折勒，纵横驰走，是名第五之态。复次，世间恶马就驾车时，举前两足，而作人立，是名第六之态。复次，世间恶马就驾之时，加之鞭杖，安住不动，是名第七之态。复次，世间恶马就驾之时，丛聚四脚，伏地不起，是名第八之态。

So it is, bhikṣus, with worldly people who have eight faults. What are the eight? When a worldly person is about to be harnessed to the carriage of the Dharma, it is like a bad horse that kicks people with its hind legs.

「如是，世间恶丈夫于正法、律有八种过。何等为八？若比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑罪而发举时，彼则瞋恚，反呵责彼言：『汝愚痴，不辩不善，他立举汝，汝云何举我？』如彼恶马，后脚双蹹，前脚跪地，断鞅折轭，是名丈夫于正法、律第一之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he becomes angry and accuses them in return, saying: 'You are foolish, ignorant, and incompetent. You are being accused by others, yet you accuse me.' This is like a bad horse that rears up and breaks the yoke with its neck. This is called the second fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举，反出他罪，犹如恶马怒项折轭，是名丈夫于正法、律第二之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he does not answer correctly, but speaks of other matters instead. He is angry, arrogant, and hides his resentment and hatred. He is stubborn and does not know when to stop. This is like a bad horse that does not follow the right path and causes the carriage to overturn. This is called the third fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举，不以正答，横说余事，瞋恚憍慢，隐覆嫌恨，不忍，无所由作，如彼恶马不由正路，令车翻覆，是名丈夫于正法、律第三之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he makes them remember and then says: 'I do not remember!' He is stubborn and does not submit. This is like a bad horse that rears up and retreats. This is called the fourth fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举，令其忆念，而作是言：『我不忆念！』觝突不伏，如彼恶马却缩转退，是名丈夫于正法、律第四之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he despises them and does not count them as people, nor does he count the Sangha. He packs up his robes and bowl and leaves as he pleases. This is like a bad horse that rears up and runs wild when struck with a whip. This is called the fifth fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举时，轻蔑不数其人，亦不数僧，摄持衣钵，随意而去，如彼恶马加以鞭杖，纵横驰走，是名丈夫于正法、律第五之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he sits on a high bed and argues with the elders about right and wrong. This is like a bad horse standing on two legs. This is called the sixth fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举时，自处高床、与诸上座共诤曲直，如彼恶马双脚人立，是名丈夫于正法、律第六之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he remains silent and does not respond, causing trouble for the assembly. This is like a bad horse that stands still when struck with a whip. This is called the seventh fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举时，默然不应，以恼大众，如彼恶马加其鞭杖，兀然不动，是名丈夫于正法、律第七之过。

Furthermore, when the virtuous practitioners accuse him with the charges of seeing, hearing, or suspecting, he then abandons the precepts, becomes discouraged, and goes to the temple gate, saying: 'You can be happy and at ease in silence, but I myself abandon the precepts and become discouraged.' This is like a bad horse that crouches down with all four legs and does not move. This is called the eighth fault of a worldly person in the true Dharma and Vinaya.

「复次，比丘！诸梵行者以见闻疑举时，则便舍戒，自生退没，到于寺门，而作是言：『汝默然快喜安住，我自舍戒退没。』如彼恶马丛聚四足，伏地不动，是名丈夫于正法、律第八之过。是名比丘于正法、律有八种丈夫过恶。」

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣus heard what the Buddha said and joyfully practiced it.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
