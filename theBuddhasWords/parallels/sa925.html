SĀ 925

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 925（九二五） 八种

Parallel to: AN 8.13 / AN 8.14 (~) / SĀ 924 (~) / SĀ² 2.149 (~) / SĀ² 2.150

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the monks, saying, "There are eight qualities of a good horse in the world that, when accomplished, allow it to go as far as the person desires. What are the eight? Being born in a good horse lineage is called the first quality of a good horse. Furthermore, having a gentle nature and not frightening people is called the second quality of a good horse. Furthermore, a good horse does not discriminate in its food and drink, which is called the third quality of a good horse. Furthermore, a good horse dislikes impurity and chooses a clean place to lie down, which is called the fourth quality of a good horse. Furthermore, a good horse quickly shows its emotions and attitudes to the horse trainer, and when trained, it quickly abandons those attitudes, which is called the fifth quality of a good horse. Furthermore, a good horse is at ease when harnessed and does not look at other horses. It is able to exert its strength according to the lightness or heaviness of the load, which is called the sixth quality of a good horse. Furthermore, a good horse always follows the right path and does not follow the wrong path, which is called the seventh quality of a good horse. Furthermore, a good horse, whether sick or old, exerts its strength when harnessed and does not become weary or tired, which is called the eighth quality of a good horse.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「世间良马有八种德成就者，随人所欲，取道多少。何等为八？生于良马乡，是名良马第一之德。复次，体性温良，不惊恐人，是名良马第二之德。复次，良马不择饮食，是名良马第三之德。复次，良马厌恶不净，择地而卧，是名良马第四之德。复次，良马诸情态速为调马者现，马师调习，速舍其态，是名良马第五之德。复次，良马安于驾乘，不顾余马，随其轻重，能尽其力，是名良马第六之德。复次，良马常随正路，不随非道，是名良马第七之德。复次，良马若病、若老，勉力驾乘，不厌不倦，是名良马第八之德。

So it is, monks, when a good person in the true Dharma and Vinaya accomplishes eight qualities, they are to be known as a good person. What are the eight? It means that a good person abides in the precepts of the Prātimokṣa discipline, with perfect deportment and conduct, seeing even the slightest fault as something to be feared, and upholding the precepts of training. This is called the first quality of a good person in the true Dharma and Vinaya. Furthermore, a good person is naturally virtuous, well-disciplined, and well-abiding, not troubling or frightening those who practice the holy life. This is called the second quality of a good person. Furthermore, a good person next practices alms-begging, and whatever they obtain, whether coarse or fine, their mind is equal, without dislike or attachment. This is called the third quality of a good person. Furthermore, a good person gives rise to disgust for bad physical, verbal, and mental actions, bad and unwholesome dharmas, and afflictions, and for the heavy suffering of rebirth in the future, as well as birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress. This is called the fourth quality of a good person. Furthermore, if a good person has any faults of a śramaṇa, such as deceit and falsity, they quickly inform their teacher and good friends. When the teacher speaks the Dharma, they promptly eliminate them. This is called the fifth quality of a good person. Furthermore, a good person fully trains their mind, thinking, 'Whether others train or not, I shall train.' This is called the sixth quality of a good person. Furthermore, a good person practices the eightfold right path and does not practice the wrong path. This is called the seventh quality of a good person. Furthermore, a good person diligently practices with effort until the end of their life, without weariness or fatigue. This is called the eighth quality of a good person.

「如是，丈夫于正法、律八德成就，当知是贤士夫。何等为八？谓贤士夫住于正戒波罗提木叉律仪，威仪行处具足，见微细罪，能生怖畏，受持学戒，是名丈夫于正法、律第一之德。复次，丈夫性自贤善，善调善住，不恼、不怖诸梵行者，是名丈夫第二之德。复次，丈夫次行乞食，随其所得，若麁、若细，其心平等，不嫌、不著，是名丈夫第三之德。复次，丈夫心生厌离于身恶业，口、意恶业，恶不善法及诸烦恼，重受诸有炽然苦报，于未来世生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦，增其厌离，是名丈夫第四之德。复次，丈夫若有沙门过，谄曲不实，速告大师及善知识，大师说法则时除断，是名丈夫第五之德。复次，丈夫学心具足，作如是念：『设使余人学以不学，我悉当学。』是名丈夫第六之德。复次，丈夫行八正道，不行非道，是名丈夫第七之德。复次，丈夫乃至尽寿，精勤方便，不厌不倦，是名丈夫第八之德。如是丈夫八德成就，随其行地，能速升进。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha addressed the monks, saying, "Monks, there are eight qualities of a good person in the world that, when accomplished, allow them to go as far as they desire. What are the eight? Being born in a good family is called the first quality of a good person. Furthermore, having a gentle nature and not frightening people is called the second quality

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
