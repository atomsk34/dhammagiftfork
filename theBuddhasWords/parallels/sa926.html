SĀ 926

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 926（九二六） 迦旃延

Parallel to: AN 11.9 / SĀ² 2.151

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Deep Valley Monastery in the village of Nālaka.

一时，佛住那梨聚落深谷精舍。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Sañjaya Kātyāyana: "One should cultivate true meditation, not strong and excellent meditation. Like a strong and excellent horse tied to a manger, it does not think about what it should or should not do, but only thinks about grain and grass. In the same way, a person who cultivates the fetter of greed and desire a lot does not truly know the path of liberation, but always runs after the fetter of greed and desire, seeking to attain it. Because of cultivating the fetters of anger, sleep, agitation, remorse, and doubt a lot, one does not truly know the path of liberation, but seeks to attain it by contemplating with the fetter of doubt.

尔时，世尊告诜陀迦旃延：「当修真实禅，莫习强良禅，如强良马，系槽枥上，彼马不念：『我所应作、所不应作。』但念谷草。如是，丈夫于贪欲缠多所修习故，彼以贪欲心思惟，于出离道不如实知，心常驰骋，随贪欲缠而求正受；瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑多修习故，于出离道不如实知，以疑盖心思惟，以求正受。

Sañjaya! If a real horse is tied to a manger, it does not think about water and grass, but only thinks about being ridden. In the same way, a person does not think about the fetter of greed and desire, abides in liberation and truly knows it, and does not seek to attain it through the fetter of greed and desire. He also does not have the fetters of anger, sleep, agitation, remorse, and doubt, but abides in liberation a lot. He truly knows the fetters of anger, sleep, agitation, remorse, and doubt, and does not seek to attain it through the fetter of doubt.

「诜陀！若真生马系槽枥上，不念水草，但作是念：『驾乘之事。』如是，丈夫不念贪欲缠，住于出离如实知，不以贪欲缠而求正受，亦不瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑缠，多住于出离；瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑缠如实知，不以疑缠而求正受。

In this way, Sañjaya! A bhikṣu who meditates like this does not cultivate meditation based on earth, water, fire, wind, space, consciousness, nothingness, or neither perception nor non-perception. He does not cultivate meditation based on this world or the other world, not on the sun or moon, not on seeing, hearing, cognition, or perception, not on attainment or seeking, not on following perception, or following contemplation.

「如是，诜陀！比丘如是禅者，不依地修禅，不依水、火、风、空、识、无所有、非想非非想而修禅。不依此世、不依他世，非日、月，非见、闻、觉、识，非得非求，非随觉，非随观而修禅。

Sañjaya! A bhikṣu who cultivates meditation like this is respected, bowed to, and praised in verse by the lords of the gods, the asuras, and the gandharvas:

「诜陀！比丘如是修禅者，诸天主、伊湿波罗、波阇波提恭敬合掌，稽首作礼而说偈言：

Homage to the great man, homage to the supreme man. I
am unable to know on what he relies for his meditation.

「『南无大士夫，　　南无士之上，以我不能知，　　依何而禅定？』」

At that time, Venerable Vakkali was standing behind the Buddha, holding a fan and fanning him. Vakkali said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! How can a bhikṣu enter meditation without relying on earth, water, fire, wind, up to discernment and investigation, and cultivate meditative concentration? How can a bhikṣu's meditation be respected and bowed to with joined palms by the lords of the gods, the asuras, and the gandharvas, who then praise him in verse:

尔时，有尊者跋迦利住于佛后，执扇扇佛。时，跋迦利白佛言：「世尊！若比丘云何入禅，而不依地、水、火、风，乃至觉观，而修禅定？云何比丘禅，诸天主、伊湿波罗、波阇波提合掌恭敬，稽首作礼而说偈言：

Homage to the great man, homage to the supreme man. I
am unable to know on what he relies for his meditation.

「『南无大士夫，　　南无士之上，以我不能知，　　依何而禅定？』」

The Buddha told Vakkali, "A bhikṣu can subdue the notion of earth, water, fire, wind, the notion of the sphere of infinite space, the notion of the sphere of consciousness, the notion of the sphere of nothingness, and the notion of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. This world and the other world, the sun and moon, seeing, hearing, knowing, and perceiving, whether attained or sought, whether known or observed, all those notions are subdued. Vakkali! A bhikṣu's meditation like this does not rely on earth, water, fire, wind, up to not relying on discernment and investigation to cultivate meditation. Vakkali! A bhikṣu's meditation like this is respected and bowed to with joined palms by the lords of the gods, the asuras, and the gandharvas, who then praise him in verse:

佛告跋迦利：「比丘于地想能伏地想，于水、火、风想、无量空入处想、识入处想、无所有入处、非想非非想入处想。此世他世，日、月、见、闻、觉、识，若得若求，若觉若观，悉伏彼想。跋迦利！比丘如是禅者，不依地、水、火、风，乃至不依觉、观而修禅。跋迦利！比丘如是禅者，诸天主、伊湿波罗、波阇波提恭敬合掌，稽首作礼而说偈言：

Homage to the great man, homage to the supreme man. I
am unable to know on what he relies for his meditation.

「『南无大士夫，　　南无士之上，以我不能知，　　何所依而禅？』」

When the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣu Saṃgharakṣa was far removed from defilements and attained the purity of the Dharma eye. The bhikṣu Vakkali was free from all taints and attained liberation of the mind.

佛说此经时，诜陀迦旃延比丘远尘离垢，得法眼净。跋迦利比丘不起诸漏，心得解脱。

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikṣu Vakkali, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced and upheld it.

佛说此经已，跋迦利比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
