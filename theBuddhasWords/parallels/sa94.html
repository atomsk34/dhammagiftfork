SĀ 94

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 94（九四） 僧迦罗

Parallel to: AN 5.31 (~) / SĀ² 2.260

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, a young brahmin named Saṅkarāyana came to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings and pleasantries with the World-Honored One, and then sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "Gautama, how can one know an unwholesome person?"

时，有年少婆罗门名僧迦罗，来诣佛所，与世尊面相问讯慰劳已，退坐一面，白佛言：「瞿昙！不善男子云何可知？」

The Buddha told the brahmin, "It is like the moon."

佛告婆罗门：「譬犹如月。」

The brahmin asked again, "How can one know a good person?"

婆罗门复问：「善男子云何可知？」

The Buddha told the brahmin, "It is like the moon."

佛告婆罗门：「譬犹如月。」

The brahmin said to the Buddha, "How is a bad person like the moon?"

婆罗门白佛：「云何不善男子如月？」

The Buddha told the brahmin, "It is like the dark fortnight of the moon, when its light, color, and connection are all lost. It diminishes day and night until it disappears. In the same way, there are people who, in the presence of the Tathāgata, develop faith, uphold pure precepts, diligently study and listen, give away their possessions, and have right view and true insight. After developing faith, upholding precepts, giving, studying, and having right view in the presence of the Tathāgata, they then lose it all. They forget their precepts, learning, giving, and right view, diminishing day and night until they lose it all in an instant.

佛告婆罗门：「如月黑分，光明亦失，色亦失，所系亦失，日夜消灭，乃至不现。如是，有人于如来所，得信家心，受持净戒，善学多闻，损己布施，正见真实。于如来所净信、持戒、惠施、多闻、正见真直已，然后退失，于戒、闻、施、正见悉皆忘失，日夜消灭，乃至须臾，一切忘失。

"Furthermore, brahmin, if a good man does not associate with good friends, does not often hear the Dharma, does not contemplate correctly, and engages in evil bodily, verbal, and mental actions, due to the causes and conditions of evil actions, when their body perishes and their life ends, they will fall into the hells of evil destinies. In this way, brahmin, a bad man is like the moon."

「复次，婆罗门！若善男子不习近善知识，不数闻法，不正思惟，身行恶行，口行恶行，意行恶行。行恶因缘故，身坏命终，堕恶趣泥梨中。如是，婆罗门！不善男子其譬如月。」

The brahmin asked the Buddha, "What is a good man like the moon?"

婆罗门白佛：「云何善男子其譬如月？」

The Buddha told the brahmin, "It is like the bright and pure light of the full moon, its color and luster increasing day and night until it is completely full and perfectly pure. In the same way, a good man gains pure faith in the Tathāgata's Dharma and Vinaya, and his right view becomes truly pure and bright. His precepts, generosity, learning, and wisdom increase day and night. At another time, he associates with good friends, hears the true Dharma, contemplates it internally, and practices good bodily, verbal, and mental actions. Due to these causes and conditions, when his body perishes and his life ends, he is reborn in the heavens. Brahmin, that is why a good man is like the moon."

佛告婆罗门：「譬如明月净分光明，色泽日夜增明，乃至月满，一切圆净。如是，善男子于如来法、律得净信心，乃至正见真净增明，戒增、施增、闻增、慧增，日夜增长；复于余时亲近善知识，闻说正法，内正思惟，行身善行，行口善行，行意善行故，以是因缘，身坏命终，化生天上。婆罗门！是故善男子譬如月。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊而说偈言：

It is like the moon, free from defilements, traveling through
the sky, its light the brightest among all the small
stars. Pure faith is also like this, with precepts, learning
and generosity free from stinginess, shining brightly in the stingy world.

「譬如月无垢，　　周行于虚空，一切小星中，　　其光最盛明。净信亦如是，　　戒闻离悭施，于诸悭世间，　　其施特明显。」

Having spoken this sūtra, the Buddha finished. The brāhman Saṅkaravarman, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and followed the teaching. He rose from his seat and departed.

佛说此经已，僧迦罗婆罗门闻佛所说，欢喜随喜，从座起而去。
