SĀ 968

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 968（九六八） 须达

Parallel to: AN 10.93 / SĀ² 2.202

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time, the elder Anāthapiṇḍada went out to see the World-Honored One every day to pay respects and make offerings. The elder Anāthapiṇḍada thought, "I go out too early now. The World-Honored One and the bhikṣus have not yet arisen from their meditation. I should go to the abodes of the non-Buddhist practitioners." He then entered the non-Buddhist practitioners' monastery, exchanged greetings and pleasantries with the non-Buddhist practitioners, and sat to one side.

时，给孤独长者日日出见世尊，礼事供养，给孤独长者作是念：「我今出太早，世尊及诸比丘禅思未起，我宁可过诸外道住处。」即入外道精舍，与诸外道共相问讯慰劳已，退坐一面。

At that time, the non-Buddhist practitioners asked, "Elder, what do you see in the śramaṇa Gautama? What do you see?"

时，彼外道问言：「长者！汝见沙门瞿昙，云何见？何所见？」

The elder replied, "I also do not know how to see the World-Honored One. What does the World-Honored One see?"

长者答言：「我亦不知云何见世尊，世尊何所见。」

The non-Buddhist practitioners said, "You say you see the Saṃgha. How do you see the Saṃgha? What does the Saṃgha see?"

诸外道言：「汝言见众僧，云何见众僧？众僧何所见？」

The elder replied, "I also do not know how to see the Saṃgha. What does the Saṃgha see?"

长者答言：「我亦不知云何见僧，僧何所见。」

The non-Buddhist practitioners further asked, "Elder! How do you now see yourself? Where do you see yourself?"

外道复问：「长者！汝今云何自见？自何所见？」

The elder replied, "You all should each speak about what you see, and then I will speak about what I see. It is not difficult."

长者答言：「汝等各各自说所见，然后我说所见，亦不难。」

At that time, a non-Buddhist practitioner said, "Elder! I see that the entire world is permanent. This is true, the rest is false."

时，有一外道作如是言：「长者！我见一切世间常，是则真实，余者虚妄。」

Another said, "Elder! I see that the entire world is impermanent. This is true, the rest is false."

复有说言：「长者！我见一切世间无常，此是真实，余则虚妄。」

Another said, "Elder! The world is both permanent and impermanent. This is true, the rest is false."

复有说言：「长者！世间常无常，此是真实，余则虚妄。」

Another said, "The world is neither permanent nor impermanent. This is true, the rest is false."

复有说言：「世间非常非无常，此是真实，余则虚妄。」

Another said, "The world has an end. This is true, the rest is false."

复有说言：「世有边，此是真实，余则虚妄。」

Another said, "The world has no end. This is true, the rest is false."

复有说言：「世无边，此是真实，余则虚妄。」

Another said, "The world has both an end and no end."

复有说言：「世有边无边。」

Another said, "The world is neither finite nor infinite."

复有说言：「世非有边非无边。」

Another said, "Life is the same as the body."

复有说言：「命即是身。」

Another said, "Life is different from the body."

复有说言：「命异身异。」

Another said, "After the Tathāgata's death, he exists."

复有说言：「如来死后有。」

Another said, "After the Tathāgata's death, he does not exist."

复有说言：「如来死后无。」

Another said, "After the Tathāgata's death, he both exists and does not exist."

复有说言：「如来死后有无。」

Another said, "After the Tathāgata's death, he is neither existent nor nonexistent. This is true, the rest is false."

复有说言：「如来死后非有非无，此是真实，余则虚妄。」

The non-Buddhist teachers said to the elder, "We have each spoken our views. Now you should speak your view."

诸外道语长者言：「我等各各已说所见，汝复应说汝所见。」

The elder replied, "What I see is true, conditioned, conceptual, and dependent. If it is true, conditioned, conceptual, and dependent, then it is impermanent. What is impermanent is suffering. Knowing this, I do not attain any view at all. As you see it: 'The world is permanent, this is true, the rest is false.' This view is true, conditioned, conceptual, and dependent. If it is true, conditioned, conceptual, and dependent, then it is impermanent. What is impermanent is suffering. Therefore you practice near suffering, attain only suffering, firmly abide in suffering, and deeply enter suffering. Thus your words, 'The world is impermanent, this is true, the rest is false,' have such faults."

长者答言：「我之所见真实、有为、思量、缘起，若复真实、有为、思量、缘起者，彼则无常，无常者是苦。如是知已，于一切见都无所得。如汝所见：『世间常，此是真实，余则虚妄。』者，此见真实、有为、思量、缘起；若真实、有为、思量、缘起者，是则无常，无常者是苦。是故汝等习近于苦，唯得于苦，坚住于苦，深入于苦。如是汝言世间无常，此是真实，余则虚妄。有如是咎。」

The world is permanent, impermanent, neither permanent nor impermanent, the world has an end, the world is endless, the world has an end and is endless, the world is neither finite nor infinite, life is the same as the body, life is different from the body, the Tathāgata exists after death, the Tathāgata does not exist after death, the Tathāgata both exists and does not exist after death, the Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death, this is true, the rest is false, all as explained above.

世间常、无常、非常非无常，世有边、世无边、世有边无边、世非有边非无边，命即是身、命异身异，如来死后有、如来死后无、如来死后有无、如来死后非有非无，此是真实，余则虚妄，皆如上说。

An outsider said to the elder Anāthapiṇḍada, "As you say, if there is a view that is true, conditioned, conceptual, and dependent, then it is impermanent. If it is impermanent, then it is suffering. Therefore, elder, the view you see also practices near suffering, attains suffering, abides in suffering, and deeply enters suffering."

有一外道语给孤独长者言：「如汝所说，若有见彼，则真实、有为、思量、缘起者，是无常法，若无常者是苦。是故，长者所见亦习近苦，得苦，住苦，深入于苦？」

The elder replied, "I did not say that what is seen is true, conditioned, conceptual, and dependent, and all are impermanent. If it is impermanent, then it is suffering. Knowing suffering, do I not attain anything in what is seen?"

长者答言：「我先不言所见者，是真实、有为、思量、缘起法，悉皆无常，无常者是苦，知苦已，我于所见无所得耶？」

The outsider said, "So it is, elder!"

彼外道言：「如是，长者！」

At that time, the elder Anāthapiṇḍada subdued the heterodox theories in the heretic's monastery, established the correct theories, and having roared the lion's roar among the heterodox assembly, he went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and told the Buddha in detail about the discussion he had with the heretics.

尔时，给孤独长者于外道精舍伏彼异论，建立正论，于异学众中作师子吼已，往诣佛所，稽首礼足，退坐一面，以向与诸外道共论事向佛广说。

The Buddha told the elder Anāthapiṇḍada, "Excellent! Excellent! You should occasionally subdue the ignorant heretics and establish the correct theories."

佛告给孤独长者：「善哉！善哉！宜应时时摧伏愚痴外道，建立正论。」

Having spoken these words, the Buddha finished. The elder Anāthapiṇḍada rejoiced and followed the teachings, bowed, and left.

佛说是语已，给孤独长者欢喜随喜，作礼而去。
