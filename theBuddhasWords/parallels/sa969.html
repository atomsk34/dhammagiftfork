SĀ 969

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 969（九六九） 长爪

Parallel to: MN 74 / SĀ² 2.203

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, a long-nailed ascetic came to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings and pleasantries with the World-Honored One, and then sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "Gautama! I do not tolerate all views."

时，有长爪外道出家来诣佛所，与世尊面相问讯慰劳已，退坐一面，白佛言：「瞿昙！我一切见不忍。」

The Buddha said to the fire-kindling ascetic, "You say you do not tolerate all views. Do you also not tolerate this view?"

佛告火种：「汝言一切见不忍者，此见亦不忍耶？」

The long-nailed ascetic said, "The view that I do not tolerate all views also does not tolerate this view."

长爪外道言：「向言一切见不忍者，此见亦不忍。」

The Buddha said to the fire-kindling ascetic, "Knowing and seeing like this, this view is already cut off, abandoned, and detached. Other views do not continue, do not arise, and do not occur. Fire-kindling ascetic! Many people share your view. Many people have this view and speak like this. You are also similar to them. Fire-kindling ascetic! If all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas abandon these views and other views do not arise, then there are few such śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the world.

佛告火种：「如是知、如是见，此见则已断、已舍、已离，余见更不相续、不起、不生。火种！多人与汝所见同，多人作如是见、如是说，汝亦与彼相似，火种！若诸沙门、婆罗门舍斯等见，余见不起，是等沙门、婆罗门世间亦少少耳。

Fire-kindling ascetic! Based on three kinds of views. What are the three? Some have this view and say: 'I tolerate everything.' Again, some have this view and say: 'I do not tolerate anything.' Again, some have this view and say: 'I tolerate one thing and do not tolerate one thing.' Fire-kindling ascetic! If one says one tolerates everything, this view arises together with craving, not without craving; arises together with anger, not without anger; arises together with delusion, not without delusion; is bound, not unbound; is afflictive, not pure; is grasped with pleasure, and arises with attachment. If one has this view: 'I do not tolerate anything,' this view is not together with craving, not together with anger, not together with delusion; is pure, not afflictive; is unbound, not bound; is not grasped with pleasure, and does not arise with attachment. Fire-kindling ascetic! If one has this view: 'I tolerate one thing and do not tolerate one thing,' then if one tolerates, there is craving, up to arising with attachment. If one has this view and does not tolerate, then one is free from craving, up to not arising with attachment.

「火种！依三种见。何等为三？有一如是见、如是说：『我一切忍。』复次，有一如是见、如是说：『我一切不忍。』复次，有一如是见、如是说：『我于一忍、一不忍』。火种！若言一切忍者，此见与贪俱生，非不贪；与恚俱生，非不恚，与痴俱生，非不痴；系，不离系，烦恼，非清净，乐取，染著生。若如是见：『我一切不忍。』此见非贪俱、非恚俱、非痴俱，清净非烦恼，离系非系，不乐不取，不著生。火种！若如是见：『我一忍、一不忍。』彼若忍者，则有贪，乃至染著生，若如是见不忍者，则离贪，乃至不染著生。

The learned noble disciples learn to say: 'If I have this view and speak like this: "I tolerate everything," then I will be criticized and questioned by two kinds. What are the two? Namely, those who tolerate nothing and those who tolerate one thing and do not tolerate one thing. I will be criticized by these. Because of criticism, there is questioning. Because of questioning, there is harm. Because of seeing criticism, seeing questioning, and seeing harm, I will abandon that view. Other views will not arise again. In this way, the view of annihilation, the view of abandonment, and the view of separation will be cut off. Other views will not continue, will not arise, and will not occur.'

「彼多闻圣弟子所学言：『我若作如是见、如是说：「我一切忍。」则为二者所责、所诘。何等二种？谓一切不忍，及一忍、一不忍，则为此等所责。责故诘，诘故害，彼见责、见诘、见害故，则舍所见，余见则不复生。如是断见、舍见、离见，余见不复相续，不起不生。』

The learned noble disciples learn to say: 'If I have this view and speak like this: "I tolerate nothing," then I will be criticized and questioned by two kinds. What are the two? Namely, those who tolerate everything and those who tolerate one thing and do not tolerate one thing. In this way, there will be two criticisms and two questions, up to not continuing, not arising, and not occurring.'

「彼多闻圣弟子作如是学：『我若如是见、如是说：「我一切不忍。」者，则有二种二诘。何等为二？谓我一切忍，及一忍、一不忍。如是二责二诘，乃至不相续，不起不生。』

The learned noble disciples learn to say: 'If I have this view and speak like this: "I tolerate one thing and do not tolerate one thing," then I will be criticized and questioned by two kinds. What are the two? Namely, those who have this view and speak like this: "I tolerate everything and tolerate nothing." In this way, there will be two criticisms, up to not continuing, not arising, and not occurring.'

「彼多闻圣弟子作如是学：『我若作如是见、如是说：「一忍、一不忍。」则有二责二诘。何等二？谓如是见、如是说：「我一切忍，及一切不忍。」如是二责，乃至不相续，不起不生。』

Furthermore, Fire-seed! The body's form is coarse and composed of the four great elements. The noble disciple should contemplate impermanence, contemplate arising and ceasing, contemplate detachment, contemplate cessation, and contemplate letting go. If the noble disciple contemplates impermanence, contemplates cessation, contemplates detachment, contemplates cessation, and contemplates letting go, then in that body, bodily desires, bodily mindfulness, bodily love, bodily defilement, and bodily attachment will be forever extinguished and not dwell.

「复次，火种！如是身色麁四大，圣弟子当观无常、观生灭、观离欲、观灭尽、观舍。若圣弟子观无常、观灭、观离欲、观灭尽、观舍住者，于彼身、身欲、身念、身爱、身染、身著，永灭不住。

"Fire-seed! There are three kinds of feelings: painful feelings, pleasant feelings, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feelings. What is the cause of these three kinds of feelings? What is their origin? What is their arising? What is their transformation? Namely, these three feelings arise from contact, originate from contact, arise from contact, and transform from contact. When contact ceases, feelings cease; when contact ceases, feelings cease, and become tranquil, cool, and completely extinguished. They perceive painful feelings, pleasant feelings, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feelings. They know the origin, cessation, taste, danger, and exit of those feelings as they really are. Knowing them as they really are, they observe those feelings as impermanent, contemplate their arising and ceasing, contemplate detachment, contemplate cessation, and contemplate letting go. They know the extent of their bodily feelings as they really are, and they know the extent of their life feelings as they really are. When their body breaks up and their life ends, at that time all feelings are completely extinguished without remainder. They think: 'When pleasant feelings are perceived, the body is also destroyed; when painful feelings are perceived, the body is also destroyed; when neither-painful-nor-pleasant feelings are perceived, the body is also destroyed, and all are on the side of suffering. They are detached from pleasant feelings and not attached; they are detached from painful feelings and not attached; they are detached from neither-painful-nor-pleasant feelings and not attached. From what are they detached? They are detached from greed, hatred, and delusion, and detached from birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. I say that these are called detachment from suffering.'"

「火种！有三种受，谓苦受、乐受、不苦不乐受。此三种受，何因？何集？何生？何转？谓此三受触因、触集、触生、触转。彼彼触集，则受集；彼彼触灭，则受灭，寂静、清凉、永尽。彼于此三受，觉苦、觉乐、觉不苦不乐。彼彼受若集、若灭、若味、若患、若出如实知；如实知已，即于彼受观察无常、观生灭、观离欲、观灭尽、观舍。彼于身分齐受觉如实知，于命分齐受觉如实知，若彼身坏命终后，即于尔时一切受永灭、无余永灭。彼作是念：『乐受觉时，其身亦坏；苦受觉时，其身亦坏；不苦不乐受觉时，其身亦坏，悉为苦边。于彼乐觉，离系不系；于彼苦觉，离系不系；于不苦不乐觉，离系不系。于何离系？离于贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴，离于生、老、病、死、忧、悲、恼、苦，我说斯等，名为离苦。』」

At that time, Venerable Śāriputra had just received full ordination for half a month. At that time, Venerable Śāriputra stood behind the Buddha, holding a fan and fanning the Buddha. At that time, Venerable Śāriputra thought: "The World-Honored One praises and explains the cutting off of desires, detachment from desires, the cessation of desires, and the letting go of desires." At that time, Venerable Śāriputra observed those dharmas as impermanent, contemplated their arising and ceasing, contemplated detachment, contemplated cessation, and contemplated letting go. He did not arouse any taints and attained liberation of the mind.

当于尔时，尊者舍利弗受具足始经半月。时，尊者舍利弗住于佛后，执扇扇佛。时，尊者舍利弗作是念：「世尊叹说于彼彼法，断欲、离欲，欲灭尽、欲舍。」尔时，尊者舍利弗即于彼彼法观察无常，观生灭、观离欲、观灭尽、观舍，不起诸漏，心得解脱。

At that time, the long-nailed non-Buddhist ascetic renounced dust and defilements, attained the purity of the Dharma eye. The long-nailed non-Buddhist ascetic saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, realized the Dharma, entered the Dharma, crossed over doubts, and was not led by others. He entered the true Dharma and Vinaya, attained fearlessness, and immediately rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bowed to the Buddha, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha: "I wish to renounce the householder life in the true Dharma and Vinaya, receive full ordination, and practice pure conduct in the Buddha's teachings."

尔时，长爪外道出家远尘离垢，得法眼净。长爪外道出家见法、得法、觉法、入法、度诸疑惑，不由他度，入正法、律，得无所畏，即从坐起，整衣服，为佛作礼，合掌白佛：「愿得于正法、律出家、受具足，于佛法中修诸梵行。」

The Buddha told the long-nailed non-Buddhist ascetic: "You may renounce the householder life in the true Dharma and Vinaya, receive full ordination, and become a bhikṣu."

佛告长爪外道出家：「汝得于正法、律出家、受具足，成比丘分。」

He immediately attained the renunciation of the householder life as a bhikṣu. He thought: "The reason good sons shave their heads, wear the monastic robe, and with right faith renounce the householder life to study the path..." and so forth, up to his mind being well liberated, attaining arahantship.

即得善来比丘出家，彼思惟：「所以善男子剃除须发，著袈裟衣，正信非家，出家学道……。」乃至心善解脱，得阿罗汉。

After the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Venerable Śāriputra and Venerable Long-Claw, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and upheld them.

佛说是经已，尊者舍利弗、尊者长爪闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
