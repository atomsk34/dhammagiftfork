SĀ 973

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 973（九七三） 旃陀

Parallel to: AN 3.71 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Ghosita Monastery in the country of Kusinara, together with Venerable Ānanda.

一时，佛住拘睒弥国瞿师罗园，尊者阿难亦住于彼。

At that time, there was an ascetic named Canda who went to Venerable Ānanda and exchanged greetings and pleasantries with him. After sitting down to one side, he asked Venerable Ānanda, "Why did you leave home to practice the holy life under the śramaṇa Gautama?"

时，有外道出家名曰栴陀，诣尊者阿难所，与尊者阿难共相问讯慰劳已，退坐一面，问尊者阿难言：「何故于沙门瞿昙所出家修梵行？」

Ānanda replied, "It is to cut off greed, anger, and delusion that I left home to practice the holy life under him."

阿难答言：「为断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴故，于彼出家修梵行。」

Canda further asked, "Can he teach the cutting off of greed, anger, and delusion?"

栴陀复问：「彼能说断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴耶？」

Ānanda replied, "I can also teach the cutting off of greed, anger, and delusion."

阿难答言：「我亦能说断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。」

Canda further asked, "What faults do you see in greed, anger, and delusion that you teach the cutting off of greed, anger, and delusion?"

栴陀复问：「汝见贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴有何过患，说断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴耶？」

Ānanda replied, "Because of being attached to greed, it obscures the mind, leading to self-harm, harm to others, or harm to both. One commits offenses in the present life, offenses in the next life, and offenses in both the present and next lives. One constantly harbors sorrow and experiences painful feelings. The same applies to anger and delusion, leading to self-harm, harm to others, harm to both, and so on, up to constantly harboring sorrow and experiencing painful feelings. Moreover, greed is blind, eyeless, ignorant, with weak wisdom, and obstructive. It is not illumination, not equanimity, and does not turn towards nirvana. The same applies to anger and delusion. Seeing these faults in greed, anger, and delusion, I teach the cutting off of greed, anger, and delusion."

阿难答言：「染著贪欲映障心故，或自害，或复害他，或复俱害，现法得罪、后世得罪、现法后世二俱得罪，彼心常怀忧、苦受觉。若瞋恚映障、愚痴映障，自害、害他、自他俱害，乃至常怀忧、苦受觉。又复，贪欲为盲、为无目、为无智、为慧力羸、为障阂，非明、非等觉，不转向涅槃。瞋恚、愚痴亦复如是。我见贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴有如是过患，故说断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。」

Canda further asked, "What benefits do you see in cutting off greed, anger, and delusion that you teach the cutting off of greed, anger, and delusion?"

栴陀复问：「汝见断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴有何福利，而说断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴？」

Ānanda replied, "Having cut off greed, one does not harm oneself, harm others, or harm both. One does not commit offenses in the present life, offenses in the next life, or offenses in both the present and next lives. The mind is always joyful and experiences pleasant feelings. The same applies to anger and delusion. In the present life, one is always free from burning, not waiting for the right time, and attains self-realization through present conditions. Seeing these merits and benefits, I teach the cutting off of greed, anger, and delusion."

阿难答言：「断贪欲已，不自害，又不害他，亦不俱害；又复不现法得罪、后世得罪、现法后世得罪，心法常怀喜乐、受觉。瞋恚、愚痴亦复如是。于现法中常离炽然，不待时节，有得余现法缘自觉知。见有如是功德利益故，说断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。」

Canda further asked, "Venerable Ānanda, is there a path and a way to cultivate and extensively cultivate, which can cut off greed, anger, and delusion?"

栴陀复问：「尊者阿难！有道有迹，修习多修习，能断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴不？」

Ānanda replied, "There is. It is the eightfold noble path - right view, and so forth, up to right concentration."

阿难答言：「有。谓八正道——正见乃至正定。」

The Canda heretic said to Venerable Ānanda, "This is the path of the wise, the way of the wise, cultivating and extensively cultivating, which can cut off greed, anger, and delusion."

栴陀外道白尊者阿难：「此是贤哉之道、贤哉之迹，修习多修习，能断贪欲、瞋恚、愚痴。」

At that time, the Canda heretic, having heard what Venerable Ānanda said, joyfully rejoiced and rose from his seat to depart.

时，栴陀外道闻尊者阿难所说，欢喜随喜，从坐起而去。
