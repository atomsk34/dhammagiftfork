SĀ 977

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 977（九七七） 尸婆

Parallel to: SN 36.21 / SĀ² 2.211

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha.

一时，佛住王舍城迦兰陀竹园。

At that time, the ascetic Śiṣya came to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings with the World-Honored One, and then sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "Gautama! There are some śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have this view and say, 'If a person has knowledge and perception, it is all due to past causes and conditions. By practicing austerities, past karma is exhausted, no new karma is created, and causes and conditions are cut off. In the future, there will be no more afflictions. Because the afflictions are exhausted, karma is exhausted. Because karma is exhausted, suffering is exhausted. For those who have exhausted suffering, they reach the end of suffering.' What does Gautama say about this now?"

尔时，尸婆外道出家来诣佛所，与世尊面相问讯慰劳已，退坐一面，白佛言：「瞿昙！有一沙门婆罗门作如是见、如是说：『若人有所知觉，彼一切本所作因，修诸苦行，令过去业尽，更不造新业，断于因缘，于未来世无复诸漏。诸漏尽故业尽，业尽故苦尽，苦尽者究竟苦边。』今瞿昙所说云何？」

The Buddha told Śiṣya, "Those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas really speak like that, but they are not correct, not precise, foolish, unskilled, and not discerning. Why is that? Sometimes suffering arises from wind, and sentient beings are aware of it. Sometimes it arises from phlegm, sometimes from saliva, sometimes from a combination of factors, sometimes from self-harm, sometimes from harm by others, sometimes from seasonal changes. As for self-harm, it means pulling out hair, pulling out beards, always standing with raised hands, squatting on the ground, lying in ashes and dirt, lying on thorns, lying on pestles or boards, smearing cow dung on the ground and lying on it, lying in water, bathing three times a day, or standing on one foot, turning the body with the sun. Such various sufferings are diligently practiced. Śiṣya! This is called self-harm. As for harm by others, it means being harmed by others' hands, stones, knives, sticks, and other things. This is called harm by others. Śiṣya! If it is harmed by the seasons, then in winter it is very cold, in spring it is very hot, and in summer it is both cold and hot. This is called harm by seasonal changes. This is the truth of the world, not false. Śiṣya! There is this truth in the world, that one is harmed by wind, up to seasonal changes. Sentient beings are truly aware of this. You also have these afflictions, such as wind, phlegm, saliva, up to seasonal changes. You are truly aware of this. Śiṣya! If those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas say that all that is known and perceived by people is due to past causes and conditions, and they abandon the true affairs of the world and follow their own views, speaking falsely,

佛告尸婆：「彼沙门婆罗门实尔洛漠说耳，不审不数，愚痴不善不辩。所以者何？或从风起苦，众生觉知，或从痰起，或从唌唾起，或等分起，或自害，或他害，或因节气。彼自害者，或拔发、或拔须，或常立举手，或蹲地，或卧灰土中，或卧棘刺上，或卧杵上、或板上，或牛屎涂地而卧其上，或卧水中，或日三洗浴，或一足而立，身随日转。如是众苦精勤有行，尸婆！是名自害。他害者，或为他手石、刀、杖等，种种害身，是名他害。尸婆！若复时节所害，冬则大寒，春则大热，夏寒暑俱，是名节气所害。世间真实，非为虚妄。尸婆！世间有此真实，为风所害，乃至节气所害，彼众生如实觉知。汝亦自有此患，风痰唌唾，乃至节气所害觉，如是如实觉知。尸婆！若彼沙门婆罗门言一切人所知觉者皆是本所造因，舍世间真实事而随自见，作虚妄说。

"Śiṣya! There are five causes and five conditions that give rise to mental suffering and distress. What are the five? They are: due to being entangled by craving, being entangled by craving gives rise to mental suffering and distress; due to being entangled by anger, sleep, agitation, remorse, and doubt, being entangled by anger, sleep, agitation, remorse, and doubt gives rise to mental suffering and distress. Śiṣya! These are called the five causes and five conditions that give rise to mental suffering and distress. Śiṣya! There are five causes and five conditions that do not give rise to mental suffering and distress. What are the five? They are: due to being entangled by craving, being entangled by craving gives rise to mental suffering and distress. By being free from that entanglement by craving, mental suffering and distress do not arise. This is called the five causes and five conditions that do not give rise to mental suffering and distress. One can be free from burning in this very life, not waiting for the right time, penetrating and seeing for oneself, realizing it through one's own wisdom. Śiṣya! There is also freedom from burning in this very life, not waiting for the right time, penetrating and seeing for oneself, realizing it through one's own wisdom, namely the eightfold noble path - right view up to right concentration."

「尸婆！有五因五缘生心法忧苦。何等为五？谓因贪欲缠，缘贪欲缠，生心法忧苦；因瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑缠，缘瞋恚、睡眠、掉悔、疑缠，生彼心法忧苦，尸婆！是名五因五缘生心法忧苦。尸婆！有五因五缘不生心法忧苦。何等为五？谓因贪欲缠，缘贪欲缠，生彼心法忧苦者，离彼贪欲缠，不起心法忧苦，是名五因五缘不起心法忧苦。现法得离炽然，不待时节，通达现见，缘自觉知。尸婆！复有现法离炽然，不待时节，通达现见，缘自觉知，谓八正道——正见乃至正定。」

When this teaching was spoken, the ascetic Śiṣya, far from dust and defilements, attained the purity of the Dharma-eye. At that time, the ascetic Śiṣya, having seen the Dharma, attained the Dharma, understood the Dharma, entered the Dharma, was free from doubt, not relying on others, entered the true Dharma and Vinaya, and attained fearlessness. He then rose from his seat, arranged his robes, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! May I now be able to go forth in the true Dharma and Vinaya, receive full ordination, and attain the status of a bhikṣu?"

说是法时，尸婆外道出家远尘离垢，得法眼净。时，尸婆外道出家见法、得法、知法、入法，离诸狐疑，不由于他，入正法、律，得无所畏。即从坐起，整衣服，合掌白佛：「世尊！我今可得于正法、律出家、受具足，得比丘分耶？」

The Buddha told Śiṣya, "You may now go forth..." as above, up to "your mind is well liberated, and you have attained arahantship."

佛告尸婆：「汝今得出家……」如上说，乃至「心善解脱，得阿罗汉。」
