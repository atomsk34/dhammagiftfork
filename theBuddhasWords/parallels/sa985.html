SĀ 985

Saṃyuktāgama杂阿含经

SA 985（九八五） 爱

Parallel to: AN 4.200

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

At one time the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in the Jeta Grove near Śrāvastī.

一时，佛住舍卫国祇树给孤独园。

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "There is love arising from love, and anger arising from love; anger arising from anger, and love arising from anger.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「有从爱生爱，从爱生恚；从恚生爱，从恚生恚。

What is love arising from love? It means that one has joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, and others also have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Following this, one thinks, 'I have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards those sentient beings, and others also have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Because of this, I also give rise to love towards others.' This is called love arising from love.

「云何为从爱生爱？谓有一于众生有喜、有爱、有念、有可意，他于彼有喜、有爱、有念、有可意随行此，作是念：『我于彼众生有喜、有爱、有念、有可意，他复于彼有喜、有爱、有念、有可意随行故，我于他人复生于爱。』是名从爱生爱。

What is anger arising from love? It means that one has joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, but others do not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Following this, one thinks, 'I have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, but others do not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Because of this, I give rise to anger towards others.' This is called anger arising from love.

「云何从爱生恚？谓有一于众生有喜、有爱、有念、有可意，而他于彼不喜、不爱、不念、不可意随行此，作是念：『我于众生有喜、有爱、有念、有可意，而他于彼不喜、不爱、不念、不可意随行故，我于他而生瞋恚。』是名从爱生恚。

What is love arising from anger? It means that one does not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, but others also do not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Following this, one thinks, 'I do not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, but others also do not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Because of this, I also give rise to love towards others.' This is called love arising from anger.

「云何为从恚生爱？谓有一于众生不喜、不爱、不念、不可意，他复于彼不喜、不爱、不念、不可意随行故，我于他而生爱念，是名从恚生爱。

What is anger arising from anger? It means that one does not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, but others have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Following this, one thinks, 'I do not have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards sentient beings, but others have joy, love, recollection, and pleasant thoughts towards them. Because of this, I also give rise to anger towards others.' This is called anger arising from anger.

「云何从恚生恚？谓有一于众生不喜、不爱、不念、不可意，而他于彼有喜、有爱、有念、有可意随行此，作是念：『我于彼众生不喜、不爱、不念、不可意，而他于彼有喜、有爱、有念、有可意随行，我于他所问起瞋恚。』是名从恚生恚。

If a bhikṣu, having abandoned sensual desires and unwholesome states, with initial and sustained thought, dwells in the first, second, third, and fourth dhyānas, then love arising from love, anger arising from anger, anger arising from love, and love arising from anger are already abandoned, already known, their root cut off, like the stump of a palm tree, made incapable of further existence, not coming to be in the future.

「若比丘离欲、恶不善法，有觉有观，乃至初禅、第二、第三、第四禅具足住者，从爱生爱、从恚生恚、从恚生爱、从爱生恚已断已知，断其根本，如截多罗树头，无复生分，于未来世成不生法。

If that bhikṣu has exhausted all taints, with mind liberated from taints and liberated by wisdom, he knows and realizes in this very life: 'My birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, there is no more of this mundane state.' At that time, he does not exalt himself, does not raise dust, does not burn, does not scorch others.

「若彼比丘尽诸有漏，无漏心解脱、慧解脱，现法自知作证：『我生已尽，梵行已立，所作已作，自知不受后有。』当于尔时，不自举，不起尘，不炽然，不熑彼。

What is self-exaltation? It means seeing form as 'I,' 'other than I,' 'I in form,' or 'form in me.' The same applies to feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. This is called self-exaltation. What is not self-exaltation? It means not seeing form as 'I,' 'other than I,' 'I in form,' or 'form in me.' The same applies to feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness. This is called not self-exaltation. What is counter-exaltation? It means counter-abusing an abuser, counter-angering an angerer, counter-beating a beater, counter-touching a toucher. This is called counter-exaltation. What is not counter-exaltation? It means not counter-abusing an abuser, not counter-angering an angerer, not counter-beating a beater, not counter-touching a toucher. This is called not counter-exaltation.

「云何自举？谓见色是我、色异我、我中色、色中我。受、想、行、识亦复如是，是名自举。云何不自举？谓不见色是我、色异我、我中色、色中我。受、想、行、识亦复如是，是名不自举。云何还举？谓于骂者还骂，瞋者还瞋，打者还打，触者还触，是名还举。云何不还举？谓骂者不还骂，瞋者不还瞋，打者不还打，触者不还触，是名不还举。

What is raising dust? It means having a self, self-desire, up to the eighteen kinds of craving. This is called raising dust. What is not raising dust? It means having no self, no self-desire, up to the non-arising of the eighteen kinds of craving. This is called not raising dust.

「云何起尘？谓有我、我欲，乃至十八种爱，是名起尘。云何不起尘？谓无我、无我欲，乃至十八爱不起，是名不起尘。

What is burning? It means having possessions, possession-desire, up to the eighteen kinds of external craving-practices. This is called burning. What is not burning? It means having no possessions, no possession-desire, up to the absence of the eighteen kinds of external craving-practices. This is called not burning.

「云何炽然？谓有我所、我所欲，乃至外十八爱行，是名炽然。云何不炽然？谓无我所、无我所欲，乃至无外十八爱行，是名不炽然。

What is burning them? It means seeing the arising of a true self, and not cutting off or knowing the afflictions of self-pride, self-desire, and self-delusion. This is called burning them. What is not burning them? It means not seeing a true self, and having already cut off and known self-pride, self-desire, and self-delusion. This is called not burning them.

「云何熑彼？谓见我真实起，于我慢、我欲、我使不断不知，是名熑彼。云何不熑彼？谓不见我真实，我慢、我欲、我使已断已知，是名不熑彼。」

After the Buddha had spoken this sutra, the bhikṣus who heard what the Buddha had said rejoiced and received it respectfully.

佛说此经已，诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。
