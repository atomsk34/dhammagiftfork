Dharmapada-with-parables (T 211) 法句譬喻经

T 211.39 法句譬喻经吉祥品第三十九

Parallel to: Kp 5 / Snp 2.4

Formerly, the Buddha was on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa in Rājagṛha, turning the wheel of the three vehicles for devas, humans, dragons, and spirits. At that time, on the bank of the Ganges River south of the mountain, there was a Nīgan brahman who was an elder who had emerged earlier, erudite and knowledgeable, with virtue reaching the five supernatural powers, clearly knowing the past and present. He had five hundred disciples whom he taught and instructed, all of whom had mastered astronomy, geography, the stars, and human affairs, and were able to observe and survey the internal and external, the auspicious and inauspicious, the prosperous and declining, all of which he encompassed and knew. The brahman's disciples, who had previously practiced under the Buddha and should have attained the Way, suddenly went together to the bank of the water, sat in seclusion, and discussed among themselves, asking each other, "What things are considered auspicious in the world of all countries and people?" The disciples did not understand, so they went to their teacher and bowed to him, joining their palms and saying, "We disciples have studied for a long time and have already mastered what we have learned, but we have not heard what is considered auspicious in all countries." The Nīgan said, "Excellent question! There are sixteen great countries and eighty-four thousand small countries on the continent of Jambudvīpa. Each country has its own auspicious things, such as gold, silver, crystal, lapis lazuli, bright divine pearls, elephants, horses, carriages, jade women, coral, mother-of-pearl, music, phoenixes, peacocks, or the sun, moon, stars, jeweled vases, four flowers, and brahman ascetics. These are what the various countries like and consider auspicious omens. If one sees these, it is said to be immeasurable goodness. This is the auspiciousness of the country." The disciples said, "Could there be any more extraordinary auspiciousness that would benefit the body and lead to rebirth in heaven?" The Nīgan replied, "Since my teacher's time, there has been nothing that surpasses this, and it is not recorded in the scriptures." The disciples said, "We have recently heard that the Śākya clan has renounced the householder life to practice the Way, sitting in meditation for six years, subduing Māra, attaining Buddhahood, and having unobstructed knowledge of the three times. Let us go together and ask him how much he knows, compared to our teacher?" The teacher and his disciples, more than five hundred in number, traveled through the mountains and arrived at the Buddha's place. They bowed to the Buddha and sat in the position of brahman ascetics. 昔佛在罗阅祇耆阇崛山中为天人龙鬼转三乘法轮。时山南恒水岸边有尼揵梵志，先出耆旧博达多知，德向五通明识古今，所养门徒有五百人，教化指授，皆悉通达天文地理星宿人情，无不瞻察，观略内外，吉凶祸福丰俭出没，皆包知之。梵志弟子先佛所行应当得道，欻自相将至水岸边，屏坐论语自共相问：「世间诸国人民所行，以何等事为世吉祥？」徒等不了，往到师所为师作礼，叉手白言：「弟子等学久所学已达，不闻诸国以何为吉祥？」尼揵告曰：「善哉问也！阎浮利地有十六大国，八万四千小国，诸国各有吉祥，或金或银，水精琉璃、明月神珠、象马车舆、玉女珊瑚、珂贝妓乐、凤凰孔雀，或以日月星辰、宝瓶四华、梵志道士，此是诸国之所好喜吉祥瑞应，若当见是称善无量，此是瑞应国之吉祥。」诸弟子曰：「宁可更有殊特吉祥，于身有益、终生天上？」尼揵答曰：「先师以来未有过此，书籍不载。」诸弟子曰：「近闻释种出家为道，端坐六年降魔得佛，三达无碍，试共往问，所知博采何如大师？」师徒弟子五百余人，经涉山路往到佛所，为佛作礼坐梵志位，叉手长跪，白佛世尊曰：「诸国吉祥所好如此，不审更有胜是者不？」佛告梵志：「如卿所论世间之事，顺则吉祥反则凶祸，不能令人济神度苦。如我所闻吉祥之法，行者得福永离三界，自致泥洹。」

Formerly, the Buddha was on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa in Rājagṛha, turning the wheel of the three vehicles for devas, humans, dragons, and spirits. At that time, on the bank of the Ganges River south of the mountain, there was a Nīgan brahman who was an elder who had emerged earlier, erudite and knowledgeable, with virtue

于是世尊而作颂曰：

The Buddha is superior to all devas, the Tathāgata always manifests
the meaning. There are brahman ascetics who come to ask about auspiciousness.

「佛尊过诸天，　　如来常现义，有梵志道士，　　来问何吉祥？

Then the Buddha, out of compassion, spoke to them the true essentials. Having
already believed and delighted in the true Dharma, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

于是佛愍伤，　　为说真有要，已信乐正法，　　是为最吉祥。

Also not from devas or humans, hoping for good fortune
Also not praying to shrines, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

亦不从天人，　　希望求侥幸，亦不祷神祠，　　是为最吉祥。

Associating with virtuous friends, choosing good places to live, always taking the lead in
merit and virtue, commanding the body to uphold uprightness, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

友贤择善居，　　常先为福德，勅身承贞正，　　是为最吉祥。

Abandoning evil and following what is good, avoiding alcohol and knowing
self-restraint, not indulging in female beauty, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

去恶从就善，　　避酒知自节，不婬于女色，　　是为最吉祥。

Learning much like one practices precepts, diligently studying the Dharma
and Vinaya, cultivating oneself without contention, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

多闻如戒行，　　法律精进学，修己无所争，　　是为最吉祥。

Dwelling with filial piety, serving one's parents, managing the household and supporting one's wife
and children, not engaging in empty and destitute practices, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

居孝事父母，　　治家养妻子，不为空乏行，　　是为最吉祥。

Not being arrogant or conceited, knowing contentment and reflecting, reciting and
studying the sutras at the right time, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

不慢不自大，　　知足念反复，以时诵习经，　　是为最吉祥。

Always wishing to endure what is heard, delighting in seeing śramaṇas
always listening and receiving whenever they lecture, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

所闻常欲忍，　　乐欲见沙门，每讲辄听受，　　是为最吉祥。

Observing the precepts and cultivating pure conduct, always wishing to see the wise
and intelligent, relying on the wise and intelligent, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

持斋修梵行，　　常欲见贤明，依附明智者，　　是为最吉祥。

Having already believed in morality and virtue, with a correct mind, free from doubt
wishing to be liberated from the three evil paths, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

已信有道德，　　正意向无疑，欲脱三恶道，　　是为最吉祥。

Equanimous in practicing giving, offering to those who have attained the path
and also respecting all gods and humans, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

等心行布施，　　奉诸得道者，亦敬诸天人，　　是为最吉祥。

Always wishing to be free from greed, delusion, and anger, able to practice and realize the view of the path, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

常欲离贪婬，愚] cf.【丽】痴瞋恚意，能习成道见，　　是为最吉祥。

If one can abandon non-essential tasks and diligently cultivate the use of the path
always attending to what is to be attended to, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

若以弃非务，　　能勤修道用，常事于可事，　　是为最吉祥。

For the sake of all under heaven, establishing the mind of great compassion
cultivating the peace and well-being of sentient beings, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

一切为天下，　　建立大慈意，修人安众生，　　是为最吉祥。

The wise dwell in the world, always practicing auspicious
conduct, attaining the wisdom-view themselves, this is the utmost auspiciousness.

智者居世间，　　常习吉祥行，自致成慧见，　　是为最吉祥。」

The brahman master and his disciples, upon hearing the Buddha's verses, were delighted and understood. They were greatly joyful and said to the Buddha, "It is wonderful, World-Honored One! It is rare in the world. We have been deluded and have not yet seen the light. We only wish that the World-Honored One would have compassion and save us. We wish to take refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Three Jewels, and become monks to practice the lower path." The Buddha said, "Excellent! Welcome, monks!" They immediately became monks, contemplated the breath, and attained arahantship. The countless listeners all attained the Dharma eye.

梵志师徒闻佛说偈，欣然意解，甚大欢喜，前白佛言：「甚妙世尊！世所希有，由来迷惑未及［门@视］明。唯愿世尊！矜愍济度，愿身自归佛法三尊，得作沙门冀在下行。」佛言：「大善！善来比丘！」即成沙门，内思安般逮得应真。听者无数，皆得法眼。
