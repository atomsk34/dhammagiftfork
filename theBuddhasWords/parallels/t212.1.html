T 212.1

Chuyao jing / Udānavarga (T 212) 出曜经

T 212.1 无常品第一之一

Parallel to: SN 3.3 / SN 48.41 / Dhp 33–43 / Dhp 60–75 / Dhp 116–128 / Dhp 129–145 / Dhp 146–156 / Dhp 273–289 / Dhp 334–359 / Snp 3.8 / Iti / T 210.17 / T 210.37 / T 213.1

The Sutra of the Light of the World was compiled by the bodhisattva Dharmatrāta, the maternal uncle of Vasumitra. It collects one thousand chapters and establishes them into thirty-three chapters, named the Dharma Verses. It records their origins and connects them with explanations, named the Light of the World. The term "light of the world" was formerly called "parables", which is the sixth division of the twelve divisions of the sutras. There was a Kashmiri monk named Saṃghabhadra, who in the nineteenth year of the Jianyuan era of the Former Qin dynasty, climbed the Pamir Mountains and crossed the Taklamakan Desert, traveling thousands of miles to reach Chang'an. His profound knowledge and vast learning were unparalleled. His previous teacher valued him. Having valued the person, his sincerity was also deep. For several years, he heard many extraordinary and miscellaneous things. Then the three Qin states collapsed, and he fled to the east to Zhou. In the fourth year of the initial year of the Later Qin emperor, he returned to the Yi and Luo rivers, intending to return to his old homeland. Stopping at the capital, he looked at the road with regret, lamenting the incompleteness of the Dharma Verses and the lack of the Light of the World. He had long cherished these thoughts and kept them in his heart. Yao Min, the Grand Commandant, was deeply devoted and joyful. Hearing this, he did not wait for the carriage. In the autumn of the fifth year, he requested to translate it. In the spring of the sixth year, it was completed. Saṃghabhadra held the Sanskrit text, Buddhanāman translated, Dao Yi took notes, and the two masters He and Bi followed the Dharma and corrected it. At that time, there was no one to rely on, so it was done according to the original. There were previously four fascicles, which had already benefited many. Obtaining this complete explanation, one can understand it clearly. I myself, from Mount Wudang, with my sleeves rolled up and my collar turned up, consulted and observed the transformation, participating in the examination and correction. I will briefly preface it. Composed by Sengrui on the twelfth day of the eighth month of the first year of Hongshi.

《出曜经》者，婆须密舅法救菩萨之所撰也，集比一千章，立为三十三品，名曰法句，录其本起，系而为释，名曰出曜。出曜之言，旧名譬喻，即十二部经第六部也。有罽宾沙门僧伽跋澄，以前秦建元十九年，陟葱岭、涉流沙，不远万里来至长安。其所暗识富博绝伦，先师器之。既重其人，吐诚亦深，数四年中，上闻异要，奇杂盈耳。俄而三秦覆坠，避地东周。后秦皇初四年，还辕伊洛，将返旧乡，伫驾京师，望路致慨，恨《法句》之不全、《出曜》之未具，缅邈长怀，蕴情盈抱。太尉姚旻，笃诚深乐，闻不俟驾，五年秋请令出之，六年春讫。澄执梵本，佛念宣译，道嶷笔受，和碧二师师法括而正之。时不有怙，从本而已。旧有四卷，所益已多，得此具解，览之画然矣。予自武当，轩衿华领，咨询观化，预参检校，聊复序之。弘始元年八月十二日僧叡造首。

In the past, the Buddha was in the country of Varanasi. The Buddha told the monks, "In the future, the lifespan of sentient beings will be eighty-four thousand years. At that time, the lifespan of sentient beings will be eighty-four thousand years. They will live together in Jambudvipa, with abundant crops and a flourishing population. The cries of chickens and dogs will be heard from each other." The Buddha told the monks, "You should know that at that time, women will marry at the age of five hundred. There will be a king named Sagara, who will be guided by the seven treasures and rule righteously without any crookedness. There will be a natural jeweled chariot, a thousand cubits high and sixteen cubits wide, adorned with various jeweled necklaces. In the great assembly, he will distribute alms without any stingy or regretful mind. He will establish merit and virtue as the leader for the masses, and will provide for the monks, Brahmins, and those who have attained the Way, whether they are far away, staying, or passing by. He will give without any stinginess. At that time, the lifespan of sentient beings will be eighty-four thousand years. There will be a Tathāgata who appears in the world, named Maitreya, a Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone, Knower of the Secular World, Unsurpassed Person, Tamer of Men, Teacher of Gods and Men, called the Buddha, the World-Honored One; just as I am today, having attained supreme perfect enlightenment, endowed with the ten titles. He will always protect countless hundreds of thousands of monks; just as I am today, protecting countless hundreds of thousands of monks. Together with the great assembly, he will extensively expound the profound Dharma of the upper, middle, and lower grades, with subtle and wonderful meaning, fully endowed with pure practice of the Brahma way; just as I am today, together with the great assembly, extensively expounding the profound Dharma of the upper, middle, and lower grades, with subtle and wonderful meaning, fully endowed with pure practice of the Brahma way. The extensive explanation is like Maitreya's descent. As the Buddha said, there is a sutra called the Six Recollections and Pleasures. If sentient beings are born in it, if they see forms with the eyes, they will see only good forms and not bad forms, see what is lovable and not what is unlovable, see what is respectable and not what is not respectable, see what is memorable and not what is not memorable, see beautiful forms and not ugly forms. All sentient beings who hear sounds with the ears, smell fragrances with the nose, know tastes with the tongue, know textures with the body, and know dharmas with the mind, up to the Heavenly Emperor, are also like this.

昔佛在波罗［木＊奈］国。佛告诸比丘：「当来之世，众生之类寿八万四千岁。尔时寿八万四千岁众生辈，于此阎浮利内，众生共居一处，谷米丰熟人民炽盛，鸡狗鸣唤共相闻声。」佛告比丘：「汝等当知，尔时人民，女年五百岁，便外适娶。尔时有王，名曰蠰佉，七宝导从，以法治化无有阿曲。有自然羽宝之车，高千肘广十六肘，竖立修治众宝璎珞，在大众中，分檀布施无悋悔心，造立功德为众导首，与诸沙门婆罗门诸得道者，远行住止经过居宿皆悉给施，有所求索无所悋惜。尔时众生寿八万四千岁，有如来出世，名曰弥勒，至真、等正觉、明行成为、善逝、世间解、无上士、道法御、天人师，号佛、世尊；如我今日，成无上正真等正觉，十号具足。常当将护无数百千诸比丘僧；如我今日，将护无数百千诸比丘僧。与诸大众，广说深法上中下善，义味微妙，具足清净修于梵行；如我今日，与诸大众广说深法上中下善，义味微妙，具足清净修于梵行。广说如弥勒下生。如佛所说，有经名曰六更乐道，若有众生生其中者，若眼见色，尽见善色不见恶色，见爱不见非爱，见可敬不见非可敬，见可念不见非可念，见美色不见非美色。诸有众生耳闻声者，鼻嗅香、舌知味、身知细滑、意知法，乃至天帝亦复如是。」

At that time, the World-Honored One was staying at the great lecture hall by the Monkey Pond in Vaiśālī. At that time, many young boys from Vaiśālī had this thought: "We should all gather together to pay respects and visit the World-Honored One." Among them, some rode on blue horses with blue canopies and wore blue robes, while others rode on blue, yellow, red, and white horses, and wore white robes. They beat drums and played music, leading the way in front and following behind to where the World-Honored One was. At that time, the World-Honored One told the monks, "You should know that if there are those who have not seen the gods roaming and visiting the bathing pond in the back garden, now you should observe these young boys, wearing Dharma robes and riding in carriages, which are no different from the gods. The reason is that the gods' robes are no different from these.

尔时世尊游毘舍离猕猴池水大讲堂上，尔时众多毘舍离诸童子等，各生此念：「我等宜可共相率合至世尊所问讯礼觐。」其中童子，或有乘载青马青盖被服皆青，或有乘载青黄赤白，被服皆白，搥钟鸣鼓作倡伎乐，前后导从至世尊所。尔时世尊告诸比丘：「汝等当知，若有不见诸天游观至后园浴池者，今当观此诸童子等，所著法服乘载舆辇，与彼诸天亦无差别。所以然者，诸天被服与此无异。」

At that time, hundreds of thousands of sentient beings in the assembly each had this thought: "We should make a sincere vow to be reborn in heaven or among humans, always wearing these Dharma robes and never being apart from them, and to hear the profound Dharma when the Buddha appears in the future, forever leaving suffering and entering the realm of nirvana." The Tathāgata knew the thoughts of the sentient beings, seeking to be reborn in the three realms without leaving suffering, and then spoke this verse to the great assembly:

尔时座上数百千众生之类，各作是念：「我等宜可发真诚誓，使我等后生生天上人中，恒著此法服永已不离，使当来世有佛兴出，闻甚深法，永离苦恼入泥洹界。」如来以知众生心念，求生三有不离苦恼，便与大众而说此偈：

What is practiced is impermanent, subject to the law of decay, cannot be relied upon, and changes without abiding.

「所行非常，　　为磨灭法，不可恃怙，变易不住。」

At that time, countless hundreds of thousands of sentient beings, upon hearing this single verse, attained the fruit of the path with their defilements exhausted in the present life.

尔时众生闻此一句偈，不可称计百千众生，于现法中漏尽意解，皆得道果。

In the past, there were four brahmins who all attained supernatural powers and could fly freely with unimpeded divine feet. These four brahmins said to each other, "If there are people who offer food and drink to the śramaṇa Gautama, they will be reborn in heaven and not leave the hall of blessings. Those who hear the Dharma will enter the gate of liberation. We do not wish for liberation today, nor do we need to hear the Dharma." At that time, the four of them each held four pieces of delicious rock candy. The first person went to where the Tathāgata was and offered it to the World-Honored One. The Tathāgata accepted it and spoke this verse to the brahmin: "What is practiced is impermanent." When the brahmin heard it, he covered his ears with his hands. The second person went to where the Tathāgata was and offered the rock candy. The Tathāgata again spoke this verse, saying: "It is the law of rise and fall." When the brahmin heard it, he covered his ears with his hands. The third person went to where the Tathāgata was and offered the rock candy. The Tathāgata accepted it and again spoke this verse: "What is born will inevitably die." When the brahmin heard it, he covered his ears with his hands. The fourth person went to where the Tathāgata was and offered the rock candy. The Tathāgata accepted it and again spoke this verse: "This cessation is bliss." When the brahmin heard it, he covered his ears with his hands and left.

昔有婆罗门四人皆得神通，身能飞行神足无碍。此四梵志自相谓言：「其有人民以肴馔食，施瞿昙沙门者，便得生天不离福堂，有闻法者入解脱门，我等今日意贪天福，不愿解脱不须闻法。」是时，四人各执四枚甘美石蜜，一人先至如来所，奉上世尊。如来受已，告彼梵志而说此偈，所行非常；梵志闻已以手掩耳。次第二人至如来所，贡上石蜜。如来复说此偈，谓兴衰法；梵志闻已以手掩耳。次第三人至如来所，贡上石蜜。如来受已，复说此偈，夫生辄死；梵志闻已以手掩耳。次第四人至如来所，贡上石蜜；如来受已，复说此偈，此灭为乐；梵志闻已以手掩耳，各舍之去。

The Tathāgata observed their minds and intentions, knowing that they were to be saved. He then used skillful means to hide his form and disappear. The four of them gathered together and said to each other, "Although we offered to the śramaṇa Gautama, our minds were not determined. What teachings did the śramaṇa Gautama give?" They first asked the first person, "When you offered the rock candy, what teachings did you receive? Did you also hear the Dharma?" He replied, "I heard one meaning from the Tathāgata: 'What is practiced is impermanent.' When I heard this meaning, I immediately covered my ears with my hands and did not accept it." They then asked the second person, "When you went to where the Tathāgata was, what teachings did you receive?" That person also spoke for himself, "When I went to where the Tathāgata was and offered the rock candy, the Tathāgata gave me this verse: 'It is the law of rise and fall.' When I heard this, I covered my ears with my hands and did not accept it." They then asked the third person, "When you went to where the Tathāgata was, what teachings did you receive?" That person also spoke for himself, "When I went to where the Tathāgata was and offered the rock candy, the Tathāgata gave me this verse: 'What is born will inevitably die.' When I heard this, I covered my ears with my hands and did not accept it." They then asked the fourth person, "When you went to where the Tathāgata was, what teachings did you receive?" That person replied, "When I went to where the Tathāgata was and offered the rock candy, the Tathāgata gave me this verse: 'This cessation is bliss.'" After the four of them spoke this verse, their minds opened up and they attained the path of the anāgāmin.

如来观彼心意念，知应得度，便以权便隐形不现，四人各聚一处自相谓言：「我等虽施瞿昙沙门，意不决了，瞿昙沙门有何言教？」先问前者：「奉上石蜜得何言教？亦不闻法乎？」对曰：「我从如来闻一句义，所行非常；闻此义已即以手掩耳亦不承受。」次问第二人：「至如来所得何言教？」其人复自陈说：「吾至如来所贡上石蜜，如来与我而说此偈，谓兴衰法；吾闻此已以手掩耳亦不承受。」次问第三人：「汝至如来所得何言教？」其人复自陈说：「至如来所贡上石蜜，如来与我而说此偈，夫生辄死；吾闻此已以手掩耳亦不承受。」次问第四人：「汝至如来所得何言教？」其人对曰：「至如来所贡上石蜜，如来与我而说此偈，此灭为乐。」四人说此偈已心开意解，得阿] cf.【丽】那含道。

At that time, the four of them knew for themselves that they had each attained the path. They then reproached themselves and went to where the Tathāgata was. They bowed their heads at his feet, stood to one side, and after a while withdrew to sit. They said to the World-Honored One, "We only wish that the Tathāgata would allow us to be in the ranks of the path and become śramaṇas." The World-Honored One said, "Welcome, bhikṣus! Practice the holy life well." At that time, the four of them's hair fell out by itself, and the clothes they were wearing turned into monastic robes. They then attained arahantship in front of the Buddha.

尔时四人自知各得道证，还自恳责，至如来所，头面礼足在一面立，须臾退坐白世尊曰：「唯愿如来！听在道次得为沙门。」世尊告曰：「善来比丘！快修梵行。」尔时四人头发自堕，身所衣服变为袈裟，寻于佛前得罗汉道。

When the Buddha was about to enter parinirvāṇa, he told Mahākāśyapa and Aniruddha, "You bhikṣus should uphold my teachings and respect the Buddha's words. The two of you should not attain parinirvāṇa first. Collect the sūtras, precepts, abhidharma, and miscellaneous treasures first, and then you can attain parinirvāṇa. Spread this widely, and make offerings to the relics." After a full year, they gathered all these sūtras together. The five hundred arhats all attained this liberation, with sharp faculties and complete virtues. They gathered in one place and prepared a high lion seat for Ānanda, urging him to ascend the high seat. Having ascended the high seat, he asked Ānanda, "Where did the Tathāgata first teach the Dharma?" At that time, Ānanda said, "Thus have I heard at one time." Having said this, the five hundred arhats all rose from their rope beds and knelt on the ground, saying, "We personally saw the Buddha teaching the Dharma. Today we say 'thus have I heard at one time.'" They all raised their voices and wept in unison. At that time, Mahākāśyapa told Ānanda, "From today onwards, when expounding the profound Dharma, all say 'thus have I heard,' and do not say 'I have seen.'" "The Buddha was in the Deer Park of the Rājagṛha Kalandakanivāpa. At that time, the World-Honored One told the five bhikṣus, 'This is the original suffering, which has never been heard before and never seen before. Explain it extensively as in the sūtras.'" At that time, the assembly had already collected the sūtras. At that time, Venerable Kāśyapa again asked Ānanda, "Where did the Tathāgata first teach the precepts?" At that time, Ānanda replied to Mahākāśyapa, "I heard from the Buddha thus. At one time, the Buddha was in the Kalandakanivāpa in the city of Rājagṛha. At that time, the son of Kalandaka named Sucandra renounced the householder life and became a bhikṣu. He was the first to commit a precept violation, reaching the point of being beyond redemption. Explain it extensively as in the precepts." At that time, Kāśyapa again asked Ānanda, "Where did the Tathāgata first teach the abhidharma?"

佛临欲般泥洹时，告大迦叶及阿那律：「汝等比丘当承受我教、敬事佛语，汝等二人莫取灭度，先集契经、戒律、阿毘昙及宝杂藏，然后当取灭度，广说乃至供养舍利。」尽耶旬竟，便共普会集此诸经，五百罗汉皆得此解脱，捷疾利根众德备具普集一处，便与阿难敷师子高座，劝请阿难使升高座。已升高座便问阿难：「如来最初何处说法？」时阿难便说：「闻如是一时。」说此语已，时五百罗汉皆从绳床上起，在地长跪：「我等躬自见如来说法，今日乃称闻如是一时。」普皆举声相对悲泣。时，大迦叶即告阿难曰：「从今日始出法深藏，皆称闻如是，勿言见也。」「佛在波罗［木＊奈］仙人鹿野苑中，尔时世尊告五比丘：『此苦原本，本所未闻、本所未见，广说如经本。』」是时，众人已集契经。是时，尊者迦叶复问阿难：「如来最初何处说戒律？」时阿难报大迦叶：「吾从佛闻如是。一时佛在罗阅城迦] cf.【丽】兰陀竹园，时迦兰陀子名曰须陈那，出家学道在比丘境，最初犯律，至不度法，广说如戒律。」是时，迦叶复问阿难：「如来最初何处说阿毘昙？」

Ānanda said, "I heard from the Buddha thus. At one time, the Buddha was at the hall by the Monkey Pond in Vaiśālī. At that time, the World-Honored One saw the causes and conditions of Bākula and told the bhikṣus, 'Those who are without the five fears and hateful minds will not fall into evil destinies, nor will they be reborn in hell. Explain it extensively as in the abhidharma.' At the beginning of the night, they finished collecting the abhidharma. In the latter part of the night, they then explained the Abhidharma-mahāvibhāṣā-śāstra, and spoke this verse: 'Why speak of sleep and awakening? As the World-Honored One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, said: "Sleep destroys life, causes delusion, and brings harm. It does not lead to fruition, and one sinks without rescue, not reaching the place of illumination." Because when one is awake, one can cultivate virtue and establish good roots. Dwelling in sleep, one loses this Dharma. Therefore, it is called delusion.'"

阿难曰：「吾从佛闻如是。一时佛在毘舍离猕猴池侧普集讲堂所。尔时世尊见拔耆子因缘本末，告诸比丘：『诸无五畏恚恨之心者，便不堕恶趣，亦复不生入地狱中，广说如阿毘昙。』」初夜集阿毘昙竟，后夜便说出曜，而说此偈：「睡眠觉寤，何以故说，睡眠觉寤？如世尊等，正觉所说：『夫睡眠者，损命愚惑，有所伤坏，不成果证，没命无救，不至明处。所以然者，如人觉寤，便能修德，造立善本，躭著睡眠，便失此法，故谓愚惑。』」

At that time, there was another speaker in the assembly who said, "As the Buddha said, 'If sentient beings are mindful of various matters while awake, they are completely without thought while sleeping.' The World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, 'How can this be understood?' Therefore, the Buddha said to remove sleep and always be mindful of awakening. As the Buddha said in the verse, 'Sleep and awakening should be joyfully contemplated.' Joy means the mind leaping within, with a joyful and happy good mind arising. Therefore, it is called joyfully contemplating. Those who listen to my words should focus their minds without any distracted thoughts. The mind is concentrated and without error, capable of receiving and upholding. Therefore, it is said to listen to my words and record the Abhidharma-mahāvibhāṣā-śāstra. The Abhidharma-mahāvibhāṣā-śāstra means that the past Buddhas, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges River, all praised the Dharma and meaning of the Abhidharma-mahāvibhāṣā-śāstra. The Tathāgata, the World-Honored One, is also called the most supreme. Why is he called the most supreme? He surpasses all the afflictions and unwholesome dharmas, surpasses lust, anger, and delusion, surpasses all the bonds of birth and death, and surpasses the ninety-six kinds of arts of the non-Buddhist practitioners such as the Nirgranthas. He stands out among them, therefore called the most supreme. He expounds and clarifies without any obstruction, revealing and expounding for the devas and humans. The meaning is accomplished. Therefore, it is said to record the Abhidharma-mahāvibhāṣā-śāstra. As the World-Honored One said, expounding and clarifying without any obstruction,

What is practiced is impermanent, namely the law of rise and fall. Birth inevitably leads to death, and this cessation is bliss.

所行非常，　　谓兴衰法，夫生辄死，此灭为乐。

In the past, the Brahmins each recited their teacher's teachings, dividing into two groups. They saw all things differently. One group claimed that all things exist, while the other claimed that all things do not exist. The Tathāgata distinguishes and removes their doubts and cuts off their hopes, and then explains to them that what is practiced is impermanent.

If harming one's parents has no retribution for good or

It is like a bird in a bottle. If someone hits the bottle, the bird will fly away. Harming sentient beings, their lives depart far away, without any harm. The Tathāgata, the World-Honored One, wants to remove the wrong views of sentient beings, so he says that what is practiced is impermanent and cannot be relied upon, that it changes and does not abide, is a law of decay, and that life is like morning dew, temporarily existing and then disappearing. Therefore, he says that what is practiced is impermanent.

犹如以瓶盛雀，有人打瓶，雀便飞逝；伤害众生，命自远逝，无所伤损。如来世尊，欲去彼邪见众生，故曰所行非常不可恃怙，迁转不住为磨灭法，命如朝露暂有便灭，故曰所行非常。

One group, calling themselves 'those who say all things do not exist,' rejoiced together, saying, 'This proves our point.' The Tathāgata saw what they were thinking and told them, 'What is meant by the law of rise and fall? Rise and fall means that what is prosperous has decline, what is united will be separated. If there were no body, that would be it. But since there is a body, what can be avoided?' The brāhmaṇa again thought, 'If the law of decline and exhaustion does not arise again, then it proves our point.' Therefore, the World-Honored One continued to explain the meaning to them: 'What is meant by birth immediately dying, revolving and not abiding, the various aggregates of perception together receiving and entering each other, observing with the eye of wisdom, one is then able to distinguish them, just as the dusts of sunlight flowing and racing are difficult to count?' These five aggregates of form and sensation are oppressed by various practices, revolving in birth and death without rest. Therefore, it is said, 'What is meant by birth immediately dying.' 'This cessation is bliss' means the complete and final cessation without remainder, the mind without desire and attachment, always at peace and tranquility, the supreme bliss. The thought of non-arising and non-cessation accomplishes the supreme meaning. The bliss of non-desire, the bliss of non-conditioning, the bliss of non-outflow, the bliss of exhaustion, the bliss of cessation. Therefore, it is said, 'This cessation is bliss.'

What is there to rejoice in and laugh about? Thinking of the constant burning, deeply obscured in the dark, why not seek the lamp?

Once the Buddha was in the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. After the meal in the afternoon, there were many bhikṣus, devas, ministers, and disciples of the four groups who wanted to listen to the Buddha's teaching on the nectar-like Dharma. There were seven non-Buddhist practitioners, non-Buddhist brāhmaṇas with white beards and hair, who came to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads, joined their palms, and said to the Buddha, "We are from afar and have long been following the holy teachings. We should have taken refuge in the Way long ago, but now we have finally come to see the holy countenance. We wish to become disciples and put an end to all suffering." The Buddha immediately accepted them and made them all śramaṇas. He instructed the seven bhikṣus to stay in the same room. However, these seven people saw the World-Honored One and soon attained the Way. They did not consider the impermanent and changing nature of things. They sat together in the room, contemplating worldly matters, whispering and laughing without thinking of success or failure. Life is short and fleeting, not to be expected. They only indulged in play and laughter, unrestrained and heedless, not thinking of impermanence. At that time, the World-Honored One arose and went to the room, and said to them, "You have become monastics, you should seek the Way of transcending the world and non-conditioning. Why are you laughing? All sentient beings rely on five things. What are the five? 何喜何笑者？尔时世尊告七人曰：「汝等七人来在道境，亦复不在须陀洹、斯陀含、阿那含、阿罗汉。」复告比丘：「我先有教，未能尽漏不可有所恃怙，汝等受形未脱结缚，蛇蚖共居成五盛阴，云何于中小语大笑？当念此苦永劫不除，方兴戏笑以成尘垢，苦哉难悟，卿等是也。」故曰，何喜何笑，是世尊教勅之言。念常炽然者，云何为炽然？以无常火而为炽然，亦以苦火而为炽然，愁忧苦恼而为炽然。又以何等而见炽然？爱欲瞋恚愚痴憍慢，嫉妬恚疑所见炽然，故曰念常炽然。深蔽幽冥者，犹人夜行不覩颜色，生盲无目不见玄黄，如此幽冥盖不足言。所谓大幽冥者，无明缠络遍人形体无空缺处，是谓大冥覆蔽众生，不别善恶趣要之本，不别白黑缚解之要道俗之法，亦复不知善趣恶趣出要灭尽，故曰深蔽幽冥。而不求锭者，云何为锭？所谓智慧之锭，以智慧锭为照何等？答曰：知结所兴以道灭之，分别善趣恶趣出要之本，能别白黑缚解之要道俗之法，善能分别善趣恶趣出要灭尽，普曜诸法无不明照，而更舍之乃趣冥道故，曰而不求锭。

诸有形器，　　散在诸方，骨色如鸽，斯有何乐？

He passed through someone else's field to get there. The owner saw him and became angry: "What kind of mendicant goes back and forth here every day without practicing the Way?" He asked the bhikṣu, "What kind of beggar are you, wandering back and forth in my field, leaving your footprints?" The bhikṣu replied, "I have a dispute and am seeking witnesses." At that time, the owner of the field, due to past karmic connections, was destined to be liberated. He secretly followed the bhikṣu. In the wilderness cemetery, he saw the corpses scattered about, bloated and stinking, being eaten by birds and beasts, scattered in different places, some eaten completely, some not. It was like a roasted pigeon, with maggots crawling and oozing, foul-smelling and hard to approach. Crows, foxes, dogs, old vultures, and owls were eating the dead human corpses. The bhikṣu raised his hand and said to the man, "These birds and beasts are my witnesses." The man asked, "Can these birds and beasts be witnesses? With whom are you disputing now, bhikṣu?" The bhikṣu replied, "The mind is sick with many afflictions. I observe these corpses and distinguish the impurity of the body. Then I return to my room and observe my own body, from head to toe, no different from that. However, this mind wanders in myriad ways, chasing after the illusory dharmas of form, sound, smell, taste, and touch. I now want to admonish the original mind. You should know, mind, do not give rise to such thoughts, lest you lead me into hell or the realm of hungry ghosts. I am now an ordinary person, not yet free from the bonds. However, this mind-thief does not obey my orders. For this reason, I stay in the wilderness every day, contemplating the impurity of the body. I also speak to the mind, but the mind is impetuous, confused, and unstable. Now, mind, you should change and not create causes for evil." At that time, the owner of the field, hearing the bhikṣu's teaching, wiped away tears with his hand and sighed. That owner of the field, for ten thousand years under Kāśyapa Buddha, cultivated the contemplation of impurity, and immediately distinguished the thirty-six impure substances. At that time, the bhikṣu and the owner of the field went to the greatly frightening wilderness cemetery and attained the path of srota-āpanna.

若如初夜，　　识降母胎，日涉迁变，逝而不还。

As the Buddha, the World Honored One, expounds the teachings, there are three conditioned things, and the characteristics of conditioned things are arising, ceasing, and changing. Question: "Therefore, if all things are eternally existent, will corpses and bones not last long? One hundred and twenty hours is called one day and one night. If the body is to last long in the world, the body of one person will fill the entire world." Answer: "Because sentient beings are born together with the faculties and perish together with the faculties. For this reason, the bones do not last long. If sentient beings were to perish together with the faculties and be born together with the faculties, then the bones would last long in the world. Furthermore, they are born together with consciousness and perish together with consciousness. At that time, the body does not last long in the world. If sentient beings were to perish together with consciousness and be born together with consciousness, then the body would last long in the world." Question: "If old age were to last long in the world, would the hair of a newborn baby always be white?" Answer: "The whiteness of the hair does not mean the meaning of old age." "What is the meaning of this?" Answer: "When one takes on a form, there is a change in the color of decay, and white hair grows. Just as wine, butter, and sesame oil must have sediment, when one takes on a form, it is also like this, and there is a change in the color of decay and white hair grows." Therefore, it is said: "If, like the first watch of the night, consciousness enters the mother's womb." It is like male consciousness and female consciousness entering the mother's womb. Based on a period of time, it is either born or perishes. It goes through hundreds of thousands of changes, arising and ceasing without stopping, like a wheel turning, innumerable. Only the heavenly eye can see it. The consciousness that has passed and returned is also not something that can be controlled by divine spells or techniques. It goes forever and does not return. The consciousness in the womb that arises and ceases without stopping is also like this. It is like a river flowing east and never looking west. The consciousness in the womb that goes and does not return is also like this. Only the heavenly eye can see the consciousness in the womb returning and the consciousness in the womb going.

These are the five things, great king, that cannot be achieved. They do not make an appointment with people. All things are impermanent and difficult to dwell in for a long time. One day passes, and so does a person's life. It is like the flow of the five rivers day and night without stopping. A person's life is also like this, swiftly passing by.

昔佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园，为天人龙鬼众生之类广演法教。时，国王波斯匿母年过百二十，卒得重病，非医药所疗，神祇不能救，不经日夜遂便命终。王及大臣如法葬送，油酥华香事事供养，安措神庙给人瞻守。葬送已讫，还过佛所，如故王法除去五饰前礼佛足，佛命令坐而问之曰：「王所从来，衣服尘土、形变色异，何所施设乃至于斯？」王白佛言：「国大夫人年过百二十，间得重病奄忽无常。向送灵柩殡葬始讫，今还城池过觐世尊。」佛三达智知而问曰：「云何大王！夫人生世有不死者乎？」王白佛言：「人生于世无有不死。」佛告王曰：「自古迄今，大畏有五不可得避：应老之法欲使不老者，此不可得；应病之法欲使不病，此不可得；应死之法欲使不死，此不可得；应磨灭之法欲使不磨灭，此不可得；应尽之法欲使不尽，此不可得。是谓大王！此五不可得法，不与人期，万物无常难得久居，一日过去，人命亦然，如五江流昼夜不息，人命驶疾亦复如是。」

"At that time, the World-Honored One gradually spoke subtle and wonderful Dharma with King Prasenajit, discussing the essential points of non-retrogression. The discussion was about giving, precepts, and being born in heaven. He spoke of the impurity of desire and the great suffering of defilements. The king should know: Birth leads to aging, illness has no radiance, union will inevitably separate - this is the constant law of the world. Like lightning flashing across the eyes, or striking a stone to produce fire, human life is even more fleeting. How can there be any joy in the law of decay and change? Wishing to make it last long is not possible." At that time, the World-Honored One, based on this cause and condition, investigated the beginning and end, showing great illumination for future beings, also causing the true Dharma to remain in the world for a long time. At that time, the World-Honored One, in the midst of the great assembly, spoke this verse to King Prasenajit:

尔时世尊渐与波斯匿王说微妙法，论讲不退转要，所谓论者，施论戒论生天之论，欲不净想，漏为大患。大王当知：「生则老至，病无光泽，合会必离，是世常法，如电历目击石现火，人命剧是，有何可乐？衰变之法，欲使久存者，此事不然。」尔时世尊以此因缘寻究本末，为后众生示现大明，亦使正法久存于世。尔时世尊在大众中，与波斯匿王而说斯偈：

Although one may live to be a hundred years old, one will still die and pass away, oppressed by old age, with illness at the end.

「虽寿百岁，　　亦死过去，为老所压，病条至际。」

The Buddha told the king: "This is the way of the world. There is nothing that lasts forever. Everyone will return to death without exception. Past kings, buddhas, sages, and immortals with the five supernatural powers have all passed away and none can remain. It is in vain to grieve for the deceased. To accumulate merit tirelessly, merit will follow the departed spirit like sending food to a farmer. By this cause and condition, the king should widely establish meritorious deeds. Merit will assist people like relying on a strong staff." After the Buddha spoke this, the king and the four groups of people who came to the assembly all rejoiced, forgetting their worries and removing their afflictions. They suddenly awakened, rose from their seats, circumambulated the Buddha three times, paid homage, and departed.

佛告大王：「世皆有是，无长存者，皆当归死无有脱者。古者国王、诸佛真人、五通仙士，亦皆过去无能住者；空为悲恋亡者，为福不惓，福追魂灵如饷田夫。王由此缘广设福业，福祜助人如凭强杖。」佛说此已，王及四辈诸来会者，莫不欢喜，忘忧除患［火＊霍］然启悟，寻从坐起遶佛三匝，作礼而去。

This day has already passed, and life follows in its reduction. Like a fish with little water, what joy is there?

是日已过，　　命则随减，如少水鱼，斯有何乐？

Once, the Buddha was at Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. At that time, the southern ocean suddenly surged and overflowed the sea boundaries. There were three large fish that followed the rising current and were stranded in shallow water. They said to each other: "We three fish are in a perilous place. The water has not receded yet. We should go upstream and return to the great ocean." There was a boat stuck in the water that they could not pass. The first fish used all its strength to jump over the boat. The second fish was able to cross by clinging to grass. The third fish was exhausted and was caught by a hunter. The hunter then spoke this verse:

昔佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。时，南大海卒涌大洮，越海境界有三大鱼，随上流处在浅水，自相谓言：「我等三鱼处在厄地漫水未减，宜可逆上还归大海。」有碍水舟不得越过，第一鱼者尽其力势跳舟越过，第二鱼者复得凭草越度，第三鱼者气力消竭为猎者所得。时猎者便说此偈：

The first one worried about the future and was surely to be harmed
Relying on grass for the present, that life was able to escape death.

「第一虑未然，　　必当被伤害，凭草计现在，　　彼命得脱死。

The two fish were both saved, rescuing their fragile lives. The
foolish one guarded a small pond and was trapped by the hunter.

二鱼俱得免，　　以济危脆命，愚守少水池，　　受困于猎者。」

At that time, the World Honored One used his pure and flawless heavenly eye to observe and saw the three fish being chased by the Tapo. Two fish were saved, while one fish was trapped. He also saw the hunter speaking this verse. Because of this cause and condition, he sought the root cause in order to show great illumination for future beings and also to make the true Dharma last long in the world. He then gathered the assembly and spoke this verse:

尔时世尊以天眼观清净无瑕秽，见彼三鱼逐洮波，二鱼得济、一鱼受困，复见猎者而作斯颂。因此缘本寻究根原，为后众生示现大明，亦使正法久存于世，即集大众说斯颂曰：

The day has already passed, and life follows in its reduction. Like a fish with little water, what joy is there in this?

「是日已过，　　命则随减，如少水鱼，斯有何乐？」

"The so-called 'the day has already passed' refers to whether one is a kṣatriya, brāhman, elder, or householder, or whether one is young, middle-aged, or old, all share in the passing away of this day, with the passing of day and night without stopping. Life changes, the body becomes emaciated, the breath weakens, and strength is exhausted, faster than that. The 'fish with little water' refers to whether one is a flying bird in the sky, a river heron, a crane, a blue crane, a water crow, or a black chicken, or whether one is a hunter who nets and catches men and women, or sets up a hook and a snare. Being in shallow water, one's life is in the hands of many. Having a body in the water, one's life is lost in the water. With many sufferings difficult to find, what joy is there? Therefore, it is said, 'like a fish with little water, what joy is there?'"

所谓是日已过者，或刹利婆罗门长者居士，若复少壮盛年老迈，俱同此日共有损减之逝，昼夜不停，命变形羸气衰力竭，速迅于彼。如少水鱼者，或为虚空飞鸟［寿＊鸟］河白鹘鹳雀青鹤水乌黑鸡，亦为世人男女猎师，罗网捕取钩饵悬弶，处在浅水一命万虑，受形于水丧命在水，众苦难寻有何可乐？是故说曰，如少水鱼，斯有何乐？

The departed do not return. Day and night, one diligently exerts oneself. The fish is burned. The suffering of birth and the calamity of death.

逝者不还，　　昼夜懃力，鱼被炽然，生苦死厄。

Formerly, the Buddha was in the realm of Magadha, at the place of good victory, gathering together those who practiced the Way. Those who were in high mountains or deep caves, hiding their forms and not coming out, still observed the signs of the times and the weather. When spring arrived, they saw that all the trees were budding, their colors like crystal, gradually unfolding into flowers. They also saw that the streams and ditches were flowing calmly, without any sound. At that time, the practitioners thought to themselves, "The time is not to be borrowed. All things are born together." At that time, the practitioners went down the mountain to the villages to beg for food. They saw men and women feasting and enjoying themselves together. The practitioners asked, "Who are these people?" The others replied, "They are from such and such a village, such and such a family, with such and such a surname. The son of such and such a family was born to such and such a father." The practitioners then thought to themselves, "Now, the inner things are all giving birth." Knowing that all things are growing day by day, they returned to the deep mountains to practice in silence. When autumn arrived, they went down the mountain to the villages to beg for food. They saw that the trees were gradually withering, covered with frost and snow, and the leaves falling and freezing. They also saw that the streams and ditches had dried up, not even enough water to wet a finger. At that time, the practitioners contemplated within themselves, "Now, all external things are withering." Knowing that the inner and outer are decaying, they returned to the deep mountains, sat cross-legged, or sat on a rope bed or under a tree, concentrating their minds without giving rise to chaotic thoughts. They observed the nature of the inner and outer to be truly impermanent, and gave rise to the thought that the days pass by without stopping. What is meant by the passing of the days? They calculated from spring to autumn, from years to months, from months to half months, from half months to days, from days to hours, from hours to day and night, from day and night to movement and stillness, from movement and stillness to inhalation and exhalation, from inhalation and exhalation to exhaustion and non-existence, until exhaustion, only then did they know that all things are empty and non-existent. Knowing non-existence, they knew that there is no origin of arising, and no trace of cessation. Sometimes practitioners observe the traces of arising and the absence of traces of cessation, and only then do they awaken to themselves and remember the Tathāgata's teaching that all is impermanent and without a nest. Sometimes practitioners observe the past, and after accumulating time and days, they are able to eliminate it. They also remember the Buddha's words, that the Tathāgata also said that impermanence is suffering.

昔佛在摩竭国界善胜道场，集诸修行之士。处高山者，或在深窟隐形不出，然彼行人随时行道瞻相时气，春节以至观诸树木悉皆蓓蕾，色如水精渐转敷花，复见沟涧水流澄清静无声响。时彼行人心则念言：「时不假借，万物并生。」尔时行人下山诣村家家乞食，见诸男女饮食欢醼共相娱乐，行人问曰：「斯是何人？」前人对曰：「某村某家姓号如是，某家子者某父所生。」时修行人复自念曰：「今此内物悉皆孚乳。」知其万物日滋日长，还入深山静默自修。复至秋节，下山诣村人间乞食，见诸树木渐皆凋落，霜雪加被叶落凝冻，复见沟涧水竭枯涸指刺不［而／火］。时，修行人内自忖度：「今外万物皆悉凋落，时不再鲜华不重茂，诚哉斯言！」复见人间村落城郭，男女大小共相携抱，散头垂发椎胸自掴，高声啼哭不能自止。时修行人问彼人曰：「此是何人，哀号啼哭乃至于斯？」「某村某家，儿亡女死或父母终，是故村落号悲如是。」行人闻已而自思惟：「今此内物亦复凋落。」已知内外衰耗法至，即还深山，内自校计结跏趺坐，或坐绳床或坐树下，专心定意不兴乱想，观内外性实皆无常，便兴日转不停住想。云何为日转？计春至秋至岁，计岁至月，计月至半月，计半月至日数，计日数至时，计时至昼夜，计昼夜至动转，计动转至出入息，计出入息至尽无余，以至于尽，方知尽空万物无有。已知无有则知何起本、灭亦无迹。或时行人行起有踪、灭无有迹，方自觉悟，忆如来一切无常亦无窠窟。有时行人观察前行，积时累日乃得消灭，复忆佛语，如来亦说无常者苦也。时，修行人内自忖度：「生死如是，谁肯乐者？」心已厌患不染四流，愿于泥洹速取灭度，或于中间得须陀洹、斯陀含、阿那含果、阿罗汉果。尔时世尊以天眼观清净无瑕秽，见彼行人处在深山，精懃学道不断圣族，因此缘本寻究根源，为后众生示现大明，亦使正法久存于世，即集大众而说斯偈：

The departed do not return. One diligently exerts oneself to the utmost. The fish is burned. The suffering of birth and the calamity of death.

「逝者不还，　　尽欲懃力，鱼被炽然，生苦死厄。」

Human life is like the sun and moon, sometimes still, sometimes
moving in a circle, like a swiftly flowing river, going without returning.

人命] cf.【丽】如日夜，　　或住或周行，犹如驶流河，　　往而不复反。

Formerly, the Buddha was in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in Śrāvastī. The World-Honored Buddhas always teach the Dharma, observing the minds of people at the right time. Some sentient beings consider the body to be permanent, thinking that the body of the five aggregates today is no different from yesterday. The ignorant are deluded and think that the aggregates do not decay, like a swiftly flowing river. The past is not the present flow, and the present is not the past flow. The departed are gone forever, and the incoming does not stop. People are also like this. The past actions are not the present actions, and the present actions are not the past actions. Those who create merit, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, observe the four great elements of the body as an analogy to a flowing river, and contemplate and discern up to the realm of impurity. The upāsakas and upāsikās attain the fruits of srota-āpanna, sakṛd-āgāmin, and anāgāmin, while the bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs attain arahantship. At that time, the World-Honored One used his pure and flawless divine eye to see the four groups of the assembly discerning the formation and destruction of the five aggregates. At that time, the World-Honored One investigated the beginning and end, showing great illumination for future generations, and also causing the true Dharma to remain in the world for a long time. At that time, the World-Honored One gathered the great assembly and spoke a verse, saying:

昔佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。诸佛世尊凡常说法，相时相宜观察人意，或有众生计身是常，昨五阴身今日不异，愚者意迷，谓阴不衰犹河逝驶，前非后流、后非前流，去者永逝来者不停。人亦如是，前行非后行、后行非前行，造功德人，比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷，观四大身以譬流河，分别思惟至无漏境，优婆塞优婆夷得须陀洹、斯陀含、阿那含果，比丘比丘尼得阿罗汉。尔时世尊以天眼观清净无瑕秽，见彼四部众分别五阴成败所趣。尔时世尊寻究本末，为后众生示现大明，亦使正法久存于世。尔时世尊集于大众，而说颂曰：

Human life is like the sun and moon, sometimes still, sometimes
moving in a circle, like a swiftly flowing river, going without returning.

「人命] cf.【丽】如日夜，　　或住或周行，犹如驶流河，　　往而不复反。」

The four groups of the assembly heard what the Buddha said and departed joyfully.

四部之众闻佛所说，欢喜而去。

Old age causes the complexion to fade, and diseases arise on their own. The body decays and rots, and life ends in the same way.

老则色衰，　　所病自坏，形败腐朽，命终其然。

Once, the Buddha was at the Monkey Pond Assembly Hall in the city of Vaishali. He told the monks, "There are sentient beings who rely on their youthful vigor and strength, or on their good health, or on their wealth and boundless livelihood, or on their noble status and accomplished family lineage. There are also the elderly gathered in the assembly, who are disparaged by others and called 'worthless.' At that time, the World-Honored One observed the minds of the people, knowing their right and wrong, and wished to make them change their past and cultivate the future. Therefore, he spoke this verse. At that time, the young men of Vaishali, upon hearing this teaching, gave rise to the thought of impermanence and no-self, or the mind of contemplation on impurity, or mindfulness of breathing, or the dharmas of the peak, warmth, patience, and the supreme worldly dharmas, or attained the fruits of stream-entry, once-returning, or some gave rise to the aspiration for the unsurpassed path, or the path of pratyekabuddha and arhat. At that time, the World-Honored One, with this single verse, transformed countless young men of Vaishali. For this cause and condition, investigating the beginning and end, he manifested great illumination for the people of future generations, and also caused the true Dharma to remain in the world for a long time. He then spoke this verse to the great assembly:

昔佛在毘舍离城弥猴池侧普集讲堂所。佛告诸比丘：「或有众生自怙盛壮力无俦匹，或恃无病自保康宁，或恃财富生业无量，或恃豪贵宗族成就，或有老者集在众中，为人所毁称为弃物。」尔时世尊观察其人心有是非，欲使众人改往修来故说斯颂。时，毘舍离诸童子等闻此教诫，或起无常无我之想，或起不净止观之心，或念安般守意、顶法煖法忍法世间第一法，或得须陀洹果、斯陀含果，或有兴发求无上道，或有求辟支佛、阿罗汉道。尔时世尊以此一偈，化毘舍离无数童子，以此因缘寻究本末，为后世人现其大明，亦使正法久存于世，即于大众而说颂曰：

Old age causes the complexion to fade, and diseases arise on their own. The body decays and rots, and life ends in the same way.

「老则色衰，　　所病自坏，形败腐朽，命终其然。」

At that time, the young men, having heard the Buddha's teachings, bowed in respect and departed.

时诸童子闻佛所说，作礼而去。

This body will not last long, and will return to the earth. The consciousness has already departed, leaving only the skeletal frame.

是身不久，　　还归于地，神识已离，骨干独存。

Once, the Buddha was staying in the Amrapali Garden in Vaishali. At that time, Ambapali, relying on her unparalleled beauty in the world, surpassing human appearance and matching heavenly form, with a dignified and perfect appearance that one never tired of seeing, and with immeasurable wealth and treasures, abundant in the seven precious things, adorned herself with a jeweled carriage, bathed and perfumed herself with fragrant flowers, and went to where the Buddha was. She bowed her head at his feet and stood to one side. At that time, many bhikshus, whose desire had not yet been cut off and who were still on the level of ordinary beings, saw Ambapali coming to where the Buddha was and all gave rise to desire and impure thoughts. The Buddha knew their thoughts and immediately told them, "What do you think, bhikshus? Ambapali has a body composed of the four great elements, which is foul and impure, with nothing to be greedy for. Bhikshus, you should know that this Ambapali will not be long before she lies on a high and wide bed, wearing clothes worth a hundred million, and suddenly dies on that bed, to be carried to the charnel ground for cremation." At that time, the bhikshus, upon hearing this, were startled. "Impermanence is swift and does not avoid the old and young. This person's appearance is rare in the world, yet such a form will be discarded and left in the charnel ground." At that time, the World Honored One, for the sake of the people of later generations, sought the causes and conditions of this matter, to show great illumination and cause the true Dharma to remain in the world for a long time. He then spoke a verse in the assembly. The four groups of the assembly, upon hearing this teaching, gave rise to the thought of impermanence and no-self, or the mind of impurity and contemplation, or the mindfulness of breathing, the contemplation of the head, the contemplation of warmth, the contemplation of patience, the supreme worldly dharmas, or attained the fruit of stream-entry, the fruit of once-returning, or gave rise to the aspiration for the unsurpassed path, or sought the path of pratyekabuddha and arhat. At that time, the World Honored One, with this single verse, transformed countless young men in Vaishali. For this cause and condition, he spoke a verse:

昔佛在毘舍离甘梨园中。尔时阿梵和利自怙色貌与世无双，进过人貌退及天形，形范端严视无厌足，然家里财富不可称限，饶财多宝七珍备足。时，阿梵和利严饰羽宝之车，自严庄校饰沐浴澡洗香华芬薰，往至佛所，头面礼足在一面住。是时，众多比丘欲爱未断在凡夫地，见阿梵和利来至佛所，皆兴爱欲起不净想，佛知其意即告之曰：「云何，比丘！阿梵和利者受四大形，臭处秽污无一可贪。比丘当知！此阿梵和利如是不久，当卧好高广床上，衣裳芬薰价直一亿，于彼床上忽然命终，舁诣冢圹取耶旬之。」时诸比丘闻之愕然。「无常迅急不避老少，此人形貌世之希有，受如是形便当弃捐在于冢间。」尔时世尊以此因缘寻究本末，为后世人示现大明，亦使正法久存于世，即于众中而说颂曰。其中四部众闻此教诫，或起无常无我之想，或起不净止观之心，或念安般守意、顶法煖法忍法世间第一法，或得须陀洹果、斯陀含果，或有兴发求无上道，或有求辟支佛、阿罗汉道。尔时世尊以此一偈，化毘舍离无数童子。以此因缘而说颂曰：

This body will not last long, and will return to the earth. The consciousness has already departed, leaving only the skeletal frame.

「是身不久，　　还归于地，神识已离，骨干独存。」

At that time, the great assembly, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and departed.

时诸大众闻佛所说，欢喜而去。

What use is this body? It is a constantly leaking and stinking place, afflicted by illness and suffering from old age and death.

是身何用？　　恒漏臭处，为病所困，有老死患。

Once, the Buddha was in the Nyagrodha Park in the country of Kapilavastu. The people of that country always relied on their noble lineage, wealth, and strong bodies, acting freely and boasting of their matchlessness. Among them, there was a person whose family lineage was the strongest, but he was born with sores and wounds, pus and blood flowing day and night, stinking and impure, causing everyone to cover their noses. The Buddha knew his mind and told him, "When a person receives a body, the four elements are the same. The six sense gates flow out, no different from him. The body of all people is a house of suffering, dwelling in myriad sorrows and fears." At that time, the Śākya clan members, having heard the Buddha's teachings, gave rise to the thought of impermanence and no-self, or the mind of contemplation on impurity, or mindfulness of breathing, or the dharmas of the crown, warmth, patience, and the supreme worldly dharmas, or attained the fruits of stream-entry, once-returning, or some gave rise to the aspiration for the unsurpassed path, or the path of pratyekabuddha or arhat. Once, the Buddha was in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in Śrāvastī. A certain elder invited the Buddha and the bhikṣus. When the bhikṣus went to his house, the Tathāgata did not go but sent a message to receive the food. There are two reasons why the Tathāgata sends a message to receive food. What are the two? First, to give the devas the Dharma. Second, to visit the sick. At that time, the World-Honored One looked around and saw that all the bhikṣus had accepted the invitation. He then took the key and opened a room, where he saw a bhikṣu lying in bed, gravely ill, unable to turn over in his own excrement and urine. The World-Honored One knew and asked, "What illness do you have that you lie in bed, unable to turn over in your own excrement and urine?" The bhikṣu, being of honest nature and without deceit, replied to the World-Honored One, "By nature, I am dull and always lazy. I never helped to care for others. Therefore, no one cares for me now. I am truly destitute and have no one to rely on." At that time, the World-Honored One personally carried him outside, removed his filth, washed his sitting mat, and then washed his body with clean water. He then dressed him, spread a new sitting mat, and returned him to the room. The Tathāgata himself stretched out his hand as a pillow and told the bhikṣu, "If you do not diligently seek the supreme Dharma, you will not attain what you have not attained, obtain what you have not obtained, and realize the fruit of the path. If you do not apply yourself to this Dharma, you will suffer even more." At that time, the World-Honored One gradually taught him the most wonderful Dharma, using countless skillful means to encourage him to be diligent in contemplating the Way. He then rose from his seat, closed the door, went to the assembly hall, and ordered his attendant, "Now, quickly assemble all the bhikṣus present in Śrāvastī and go to the assembly hall." When the bhikṣus had gathered, the World-Honored One said, "You bhikṣus! Without father, mother, brothers, sisters, or relatives, you do not look after each other and abandon each other. 昔佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。有一长者请佛及比丘僧。时比丘僧往彼家，如来不往遣信迎食。所以如来遣信迎食，有二因缘。云何为二？一者欲与诸天说法，二者瞻视病人。是时，世尊遍观比丘皆悉受请，即取钥母开一房门，见一比丘抱患顿笃，卧大小便不能转侧。尔时世尊知而问曰：「汝有何患，卧著床褥大小便利，不能转侧？」时彼比丘受性质直内无奸宄，报世尊曰：「受性暗钝恒怀懈慢，初不劝佐瞻视余人，是故今日无看我者，今实孤穷所怙无处。」尔时世尊躬抱出在门外，除去不净湔浣坐具，复取净水用洗其身，便与著衣敷新坐具还卧房中，如来躬自舒手为枕，告比丘曰：「汝不加懃求增上法，未获者获、未得者得，未受果证令受果证，设不用意受此法者，便当更受剧是苦恼。」尔时世尊渐与说极妙法，无数方便劝使勇猛思惟道德，即从座起还闭房门，诣普会讲堂，勅语侍者：「汝今速集舍卫城中诸现在比丘即诣普会讲堂。」比丘已集，世尊告曰：「汝等比丘！无父无母无弟无兄，亦无姊妹，亦复无有宗族五亲，不相瞻视各相捐弃，此非其宜，便为外道异学梵志所见嗤笑：『瞿昙沙门乃无毫厘慈心，视人形命如视瓦石，死者孤穷无瞻养者。』我法齐整上下和顺，汝设尔者便屈于彼。自今已始，弟子侍师事如父母至死不舍，师看弟子视如己息，随时将息至死不舍，师徒相慈恩流永劫，所有什物平等分布，设无什物当诣广施之家劝令修福。若少知识当诣贾家分卫乞食，好者给病、恶者自食。其瞻病者则瞻我身，所获功德亦无差降。」

At that time, the sick bhikṣu, not long after the World-Honored One left, thought to himself: "This body is composed of the four elements, with various sufferings gathered together. What use is this body? It is full of impurities and is afflicted by illness, unable to escape old age and death? I should be diligent and practice the Buddha's words." He then abandoned his body and entered the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder, and attained parinirvāṇa. At that time, many bhikṣus took the key and opened the door. Seeing that the bhikṣu had already abandoned his body, they immediately informed the World-Honored One: "The sick bhikṣu has now passed away. I wonder where his spirit has been reborn? In what kind of path?" The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "That sick bhikṣu had accumulated good practices. I taught him the Dharma, and his mind was enlightened. Later, his mind became fierce, following the Dharma and distinguishing the profound Dharma. This noble son has already attained nirvāṇa. You should pay homage to his relics." At that time, the World-Honored One, having observed this meaning, wished to make the true Dharma last long in the world, and to show great illumination to future sentient beings, he spoke a verse in the great assembly:

时病比丘，世尊去不久便自思惟：「受此四大众苦凑集，是身何用？漏诸不净，为病所困不脱老死？宜可自谨承修佛语。」即舍形寿入无余泥洹境而般泥洹。尔时众多比丘持钥母开门，见彼比丘已舍形寿，即白世尊：「抱患比丘今已命终，不审魂神为生何处？在何道种？」佛告比丘：「彼病比丘素积善行，吾与说法意寻开悟，后便意猛向法次法分别深法。此族姓子已取泥洹，汝等宜可供养舍利。」尔时世尊观察此义已，欲使正法久存于世，为将来众生示现大明，于大众中而说颂曰：

What use is this body? It is always leaking and stinking, afflicted by illness, and has the suffering of old age and death.

「是身何用？　　恒漏臭处，为病所困，有老死患。」

Sentient beings who hear the Dharma rejoice and practice it.

众生闻法，欢喜奉行。

This body is a leaking and stinking place, where all illnesses
gather. The supreme cessation of suffering is peaceful and eternally at rest.

是身漏臭处，　　众疾集普会，无患第一灭，　　安隐永休息。

"This body is a leaking and stinking place" means that it is defiled by many afflictions and is disliked by people. It is the foremost of all impure things, subject to impermanence and change, and the suffering within suffering is no greater than the aggregates. One should seek skillful means to leave the body of the four great elements, and with skillful wisdom seek the way out. All afflictions will cease forever without remainder, hence it is called "extinction." Having extinguished emptiness, it is empty and tranquil, and there is neither birth nor cessation, nor the duality of attachment and cutting off. "The supreme cessation of suffering is peaceful and eternally at rest" means peaceful and free from thoughts of joy and sorrow, peaceful and eternally at rest, the supreme cessation without remainder of nirvana. At that time, those in the assembly, upon hearing this meaning, joyfully accepted it, bowed, and departed.

是身漏臭处者，众患秽污人所恶见，疮痍苦漏诸不净众刺之首，无常变易法应磨灭，苦中之苦莫甚于阴，当求巧便离四大身，善谋权慧求于出要，一切众恼永息无余，故曰尽也。已尽虚者空寂，亦无生灭著断之二。无患第一灭者，安隐无忧喜想，安隐永休息，第一灭尽无余泥洹。时诸会者闻说此义，欢喜而受，作礼而去。

In the heat, one can stop here; in the cold and snow, one can stop here. The foolish have many worries, not knowing what changes will come.

暑当止此，　　寒雪止此，愚多豫虑，莫知来变。

For the three months of the year, he did not observe the restrictions. He was wealthy but stingy and did not give to anyone. He did not believe in this world or the next, and was self-indulgent and difficult to transform. He did not recognize morality and did not consider impermanence. He built more good rooms, with a front hall and a rear hall, a cool pavilion and a warm room in winter. He built dozens of houses to the east and west. The wind of the knife separated his form, and suddenly he became impermanent. The Buddha, with his pure and flawless heavenly eye, saw that this elder had died. While he was alive, he had no kindness or compassion to bestow upon sentient beings, but only caused trouble for other people. His intentions and plans were not fulfilled. At that time, the World-Honored One, among the great assembly, wished to manifest its meaning and proclaim its origin, also to enable future sentient beings to understand it well without doubt. He also manifested the seal of the divine mouth of the past Buddhas, which was sealed. He also wished to make the true Dharma last long in the world. He then gathered the great assembly and spoke a verse:"

昔有长者造立屋舍，春秋冬夏各立堂室，任情自用不奉禁戒，岁三月六初不防制，财富无数悭贪不施，亦不给与沙门婆罗门；亦不信有今世后世，放逸自恣悭贪难化，不识道德不计无常，更作好室前庌后堂，清凉之台冬温之室，东西起舍数十余间，刀风解形忽然无常。佛以天眼清净无瑕秽，见此长者卒便命终，存在之日无有慈恩加被众生，但有劳役于余人民，意所规郭竟不充愿。尔时世尊在诸大众中，欲现其义宣畅本原，亦使将来众生善解无疑，复现过去诸佛世尊神口印封之所封印，亦使正法久存于世，寻集大众而说颂曰：

In the heat, one can stop here; in the cold and snow, one can stop here. The foolish have many worries, not knowing what changes will come.

At that time, those who were present heard what the Buddha said, and joyfully accepted and practiced it.

The joy of giving birth to a child, the attachment that does not part, like being drunk and encountering a raging river, drowning the body and life.

In the past, there was a householder who was wealthy beyond measure. His house was filled with treasures, with gold, silver, jewels, beryl, agate, pearls, tiger-eye, and the seven treasures all complete. He had servants, elephants, horses, carriages, and granaries, all without shortage. He only lacked a son to continue his lineage. For the sake of a son, he prayed to various gods, kneeling before the gods of the house, the city gods, the gods of the steps and streets, or kneeling before various gods, ancestors, parents, mountain gods, tree gods, gods of heaven and earth, down to the gods of the tombs and filth. He bowed to them all, but his wish was not fulfilled and he still did not have a son. Day and night he was sorrowful and gradually became ill. "Now my house is filled with countless treasures, and rare treasures are all in my house. Moreover, I have no son to succeed me. If I die, all my wealth will be confiscated by the government. Thinking of this, I am heartbroken and do not know what to do." This man was fortunate and his illness gradually subsided. Before a few weeks had passed, he gave birth to a son who was upright and unparalleled, rare in the world, with a face like a peach blossom and all the marks complete. His parents saw him and were overjoyed, unable to contain themselves. They again invited the neighbors to a feast, with food and drink, singing and dancing, enjoying themselves all day, or indulging in sleep and not knowing anything. At that time, there was a great flood that swept away everything, drowning and killing all without any survivors. At that time, the World-Honored One, with his pure and flawless divine eye, saw the success and failure of this elder. Based on this, he sought the root cause, wishing to make sentient beings of future generations understand without doubt. He also manifested the seal of the divine mouth of the past Buddha World-Honored Ones, which was sealed, in order to make the true Dharma last in the world. 尔时诸比丘，闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

Having children and wealth, the foolish are only anxious. Life is not mine, so how can there be children and wealth?

有子有财，　　愚惟汲汲，命非我有，何有子财？

The ignorant and deluded consider themselves wise. Being

愚蒙愚极，　　自谓我智，愚而称智，　　是谓极愚。

Once the Buddha was at the Monkey Pond on the high lecture hall in Vaiśālī. At that time, many young boys were skilled in archery, with arrows touching each other, and they were the best in archery. They relied on their noble family and considered themselves unmatched in the world. "There is no one in Jambudvīpa who can match us. Even if there are worries, our sons are now unmatched in the world. How could there be thieves and robbers to deceive us?" The sons also thought to themselves, "Our father has skills and will surely surpass others." They relied on each other and did not save themselves. When impermanence arrived, they were scattered in different places. Therefore, the verse says:

昔佛在毘舍离猕猴池侧高讲堂上。尔时众多童子等，善知射术筈筈相拄，于射术上彼最为第一，自恃高族与世无双。「处阎浮利内无及我等，正使有忧虑者，子今与世无双，岂有奸贼侵欺我等？」儿复自惟：「吾父有伎必胜众人。」各相凭俟竟不自济，无常对至迸在异处。是故颂曰：

「无常力势，　　不可恃怙，知死命终，然不久住。

At that time, the Śākya clansmen of the country of Kapilavastu heard the Buddha's words, knowing that archery had no obstacles, and fought with King Virūḍhaka, shooting arrows at each other, either cutting off eyebrows or cutting off beards and hair, without any harm. King Virūḍhaka then wanted to retreat, but his ministers advised him, saying, "The Śākya clansmen all uphold the precepts and have attained the fruit of the path. Although they have archery skills, they do no harm." At that time, King Virūḍhaka gradually advanced, while the Śākyas retreated and firmly guarded the city gates. They sent a messenger to the Buddha, saying, "Today we are in dire straits, attacked by King Virūḍhaka." The World-Honored One said, "If you open the gates, there will be harm. If you do not open the gates, there will be no harm." The messenger then returned to the Śākyas and said to them, "The Tathāgata has instructed that if you open the gates, you will be harmed by the king. If you do not open the gates, you will not be harmed by the king." When the Śākyas heard this, they closed the gates even more firmly and guarded them. At that time, King Virūḍhaka camped at the city gates and said to the Śākyas, "Quickly open the gates. The two families can live in harmony without any harm." Among the Śākyas, those with past karmic connections all said to open the gates, while those without past karmic connections, following the Tathāgata's instruction, refused to open the gates. The Śākya Śāma had previously been outside the city fighting with King Virūḍhaka, killing 70,000 beings and injuring countless beings by pulling out their elephant tusks. Śāma Śākya said to Virūḍhaka, "The son of a slave girl must not retreat. I must enter the city to prepare for battle again." At that time, the Śākyas in the city heard that Śāma Śākya had caused immeasurable harm to the people, and immediately sent for Śāma Śākya to reprimand him, saying, "You are not a Śākya claiming to be a Śākya, polluting our Śākya clan. At that time, Śāma Śākya left the city. King Virūḍhaka then opened the gates again. The Śākyas with past karmic connections said to the other Śākyas, "Just quickly open the gates. What can this son of a slave girl accomplish?" The Śākyas without past karmic connections said to the other Śākyas, "The Tathāgata has instructed that if you open the gates, there will be killing. If you do not open the gates, there will be no killing." At that time, King Virūḍhaka again pressed the city gates. At that time, Mahānāma Śākya said to King Virūḍhaka, "Please listen to my one wish. If you agree, I will state it." The king replied to the Śākya, "Speak freely. I will not go against you."

时舍马释即出城去。时流离王复开门，宿缘释种语诸释言：「但速开门，此婢生子何所堪办？」无缘释种等语诸释言：「如来有教，若开门者有所杀，不开门者无所杀。」时流离王复逼城门。时摩诃男释语流离王曰：「听我一愿，若见许者便当自陈。」王报释言：「恣汝所说，吾不相违。」

At that time, Mahānāma Śākya said to the king, "Please allow me to enter the water for a while, so that the Śākyas may all escape." At that time, Mahānāma Śākya entered the water, tied his hair to the root of a tree, and drowned at the bottom of the water. At that time, the Śākyas all escaped. At that time, King Virūḍhaka sent someone into the water to look. Why is my brother-in-law Śākya taking so long in the water?" He then entered the water and said, "Mahānāma Śākya has already died in the water." Sentient beings harm each other and argue with each other. The practices they follow are not in accordance with the true Dharma. It is like the story of a couple, both of whom were handsome and had dignified appearances, with all the characteristics complete and their faculties tranquil. They treated each other with respect all day long without ever tiring of it. As the days passed, the couple suddenly went blind and could not see anything. They loved each other and feared being deceived by others. The husband feared losing his wife, and the wife feared losing her husband. They sat together, guarding each other, not leaving each other's side for a moment. At that time, their five relatives sought a doctor from afar and brought him to the blind couple. They applied medicine to their eyes and they soon regained their sight. The husband saw his wife's changed appearance and said in a raised voice, "Who has replaced my wife?" The wife saw her husband's changed appearance and said in a raised voice, "Who has replaced my husband?" The five relatives explained, "The youthful appearance changes with the passing days. As the breath weakens and the strength is exhausted, the skin sags and the face wrinkles, changing from day to day. Comparing the old and decrepit appearance to the youthful one is like drilling ice to seek fire. How can you cry and not recognize each other?" They looked at themselves in a mirror and saw their changed appearances. Alas, old age comes and beauty does not last long. The dignified appearance changes in an instant. The Tathāgata proclaims the impermanence and transience of the past. In the morning, at noon, in the evening, in the early night, in the middle of the night, and in the late night, the Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'Whether walking, sitting, coming, going, sleeping, or waking, you should contemplate and practice this samādhi so that it is not lost. The exhaustion of birth and the absence of heat and distress means that body, speech, and mind are not distressed, not distressed by the fire of afflictions. Be diligent and energetic.' The Buddha told the bhikṣus: 'How can you prevent burning your head and burning your clothes?' The bhikṣus said to the Buddha: 'Only by saving the head and protecting the clothes can we prevent it.'
