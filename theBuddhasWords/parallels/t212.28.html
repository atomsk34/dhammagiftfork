T 212.28

Chuyao jing / Udānavarga (T 212) 出曜经

T 212.28 观品第二十八

Parallel to: MN 82 / SN 15.10 / SN 41.5 / Dhp 146–156 / Dhp 167–178 / Dhp 179–196 / Dhp 235–255 / Dhp 334–359 / Ud 6.6 / Ud 6.8 (~) / Ud 7.3 / Ud 7.5 / Snp 5.16 (~) / Iti / Thag 16 / Thag 20 / Thag 21 / Thig 7 / Thig 13 / T 210.22 / T 213.27

Look well at your own faults, and do not let them show
outside. Each one has his own faults, like the light dust that flies.

善观己瑕隙，　　使己不露外，彼彼自有隙，　　如彼飞轻尘。

Look well at your own faults, and do not let them show outside. Each one has his own faults, like the light dust that flies.

善观己瑕隙者，人但见彼恶不见己愆，互相是非共相诽谤，由如典场之人抄谷高扬，轻者在远重者在近，是故说曰，善观己瑕隙，使己不露外，彼彼自有隙，如彼飞轻尘。

If you claim to be faultless, both things will come to you. You only see
the faults of others, and always harbor harmful thoughts. You see far but not near.

若己称无瑕，　　二事俱并至，但见外人隙，　　恒怀危害心，远观不见近。

People in the world often deceive themselves, claiming that their virtues are unparalleled. They say, "My practice of precepts, hearing, giving, and wisdom is supreme, unique, and incomparable." Therefore, it is said, "If you claim to be faultless, both things will come to you." This refers to those who cover their own faults. Those who oppose them win, while those who comply are always defeated. Those who practice and cultivate virtues are also like this. They know their own faults and do not reveal them to others. Therefore, it is said, "Both things will come to you." "You only see the faults of others, and always harbor harmful thoughts" means that people do not examine themselves but only see the unwholesome dharmas and troubles of others. They fall into evil destinies and do not reach good places. They experience the sufferings of hell, animals, and hungry ghosts. Therefore, it is said, "You only see the faults of others, and always harbor harmful thoughts." "You see far but not near" means that space and the earth are each separate, not seeing the true dharmas and not seeing the non-true dharmas.

夫人在世多自矫誉，自称功德与世无双，我之所行戒闻施慧，为尊为特为无俦匹，是故说曰，若己称无瑕。二事俱并至者，此自博掩之人，逆者得胜顺者恒负，执行之人修德亦尔，自知己愆不露见彼，是故说曰，二事俱并至。但见外人隙，恒怀危害心者，人不自审但见外事诸不善法弊恶之患，堕入恶趣不至善处，种地狱畜生饿鬼之苦，是故说曰，但见外人隙，恒怀危害心也。虚空与地各各离别，不见真法不见非真法，是故说曰，远观不见近也。

Knowing shame is the best in life, but hawks greedily seize and
tear. Strong men are fearless and cautious, but their lives are short.

知惭寿中上，　　鸢以贪掣搏，力士无畏忌，　　斯等命促短。

"Knowing shame is the best in life" means that when people are in the world, they do not know shame and have nothing to fear. It is like a wild bull that has nothing to fear. Those ignorant people are also like this. They act without restraint and have nothing to fear. Therefore, it is said, "Knowing shame is the best in life." "Hawks greedily seize and tear" means that hawks are greedy and insatiable, seizing and tearing at people without any sense of moderation. Sentient beings are also like this. They are attached to wealth and desires without ever being satisfied. Therefore, it is said, "Hawks greedily seize and tear." "Strong men are fearless and cautious" means that those strong men have nothing to fear. In the midst of the crowd, they do as they please without anyone being able to stop them. If someone comes to admonish and persuade them, they become angry and cut off their lives. Therefore, it is said, "Strong men are fearless and cautious." "Their lives are short" means that people in the world belittle others and value themselves. They only hold onto inverted views and are deluded and unaware. They encroach on the property of the Three Treasures and arrogantly rely on themselves. Such people will not have long lives. Therefore, it is said, "Their lives are short."

知惭寿中上者，人之处世不知惭愧无所畏难，犹如暴逸之牛无所畏难，彼愚𫘤人亦复如是，出意造行无所畏忌，是故说曰，知惭寿中上也。鸢以贪掣搏者，犹如飞鸢贪餮无厌，掣搏人物无有忌度，众生之类亦复如是，贪著财色无有厌足，是故说曰，鸢以贪掣搏。力士无畏忌者，如彼力人无所畏难，在大众中恣意所作无有及者，其有呵谏来劝喻者，寻怀瞋恚断其命根，是故说曰，力士无畏忌也。斯等命促短者，夫人处世轻人贵己，但执颠倒迷惑不寤，侵三尊物强梁自恃，如斯之类命不久停，是故说曰，斯等命促短也。

Knowing shame does not exhaust one's life, always seeking holy life
with dignified conduct without leakage, one should observe the true holy life.

知惭不尽寿，　　恒求清净行，威仪不缺漏，　　当观真净寿。

"Knowing shame does not exhaust one's life" means that those who are ashamed and have a sense of shame are not very diligent in seeking clothing and food. They distribute their wealth to others, wear coarse clothes and eat poorly, without adornment. They only maintain their lives in the world without any glory or hope. Therefore, it is said, "Knowing shame does not exhaust one's life." "Always seeking holy life" means that their practices are pure and they do not create evil. Their physical, verbal, and mental purity should be the supreme practice. They also know external purity, speaking appropriately without harming others. Therefore, it is said, "Always seeking holy life." "Dignified conduct without leakage" means that they restrain their faculties and do not let them wander. Therefore, it is said, "Dignified conduct without leakage." "Should observe the true holy life" means that in their coming and going, speaking and eating, they take food to nourish their life. Therefore, it is said, "Should observe the true holy life."

知惭不尽寿者，彼惭愧之人，于诸衣食不大慇懃，所得财货分布与人，麤衣恶食不著庄饰，唯存命于世无所荣冀，是故说曰，知惭不尽寿也。恒求清净行者，所行清净不造邪部，身口意净应无上行，亦知外净出言适前无所伤害，是故说曰，恒求清净行也。威仪不缺漏者，收摄诸根不使流逸，是故说曰，威仪不缺漏。当观真净寿者，进止行来出口言语，饮食取以养其寿，是故说曰，当观真净寿也。

The world is universally blind and deluded, with few having eyes
Many birds fall into nets, but few are born in heaven.

世间普盲冥，　　有目尠尠耳，群鸟堕罗网，　　生天不足言。

The world is universally blind and deluded" means that it is like a blind person who does not see good or bad colors, flat ground or high cliffs. This kind of sentient being is also like this, covered by desire, anger, and delusion, not seeing the practices of good and evil, not knowing what is good or bad, and also not knowing the laws of white and black. The mind is self-deluded and does not seek good places. Therefore, it is said, "The world is universally blind and deluded." "Those with eyes are few and far between" is like what is said in the Chang Ahan Sutra. The Buddha told the Brahman with long fingernails, "In the world, those who cultivate goodness are very few. To put it simply, those with inverted views are more numerous than the earth. They do not recognize the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha, or their parents. They also do not distinguish between superiors and inferiors. Those with right views are like the soil on a fingernail. Although their views are not wrong, their aspirations are not the same. It is like the non-Buddhist Brahmins and Nirgranthas who leave home to study the path, each considering themselves superior. Their scriptures are different, seeking liberation. They hold onto ignorance, their minds deluded, not reaching the great path. Those with right views are indeed few." Therefore, it is said, "Those with eyes are few and far between." "Birds fall into nets" is like a hunter setting up nets and snares to catch birds. He catches countless birds and beasts, but only one or two escape. The number of those born in heaven is also like this, only one or two who can enjoy heavenly blessings. As said in the Za Ahan Sutra, the Buddha told the bhikshus, "The number of sentient beings entering hell is greater than the earth. From hell, they return to hell. The same is true for hungry ghosts and animals. Those born in heaven are like the soil on a fingernail." Therefore, it is said, "Birds in nets, the same for those born in heaven.

世间普盲冥者，犹如盲人，不见善色恶色平地高岸，此众生类亦复如是，为婬怒痴所覆，不见善恶之行，不知好丑，亦复不知白黑之法，意自迷惑不求善处，是故说曰，世间普盲冥也。有目尠尠耳者，犹若《长阿鋡》契经所说，佛告长爪梵志：「世皆修善甚少少。取要言之，怀倒见众生多于大地之土，不识佛不识法不识比丘僧不识父母，亦复不别尊卑高下。怀正见众生者如爪上土，见虽不错愿求不同；犹如外道梵志尼揵子等，出家学道各自谓尊，书籍别异求于解脱，执愚意迷不达大道，正见之人盖不足言。」是故说曰，有目尠尠耳。群鸟堕罗网者，犹如猎者施张罗网悬弶捕鸟，克获无数鸟兽之属，其得脱者若一若两；生天之众亦复如是，若一若两得受天福。如《杂阿鋡》契经所说，佛告比丘：「众生入地狱者多于地土，从地狱终还生地狱，饿鬼畜生亦复如是；生天众生如爪上土。」是故说曰，群鸟在罗网，生天亦复尔。

Observing the world's decline, only seeing the changes in
various forms, the foolish bind themselves, entangled in darkness.

观世衰耗法，　　但见众色变，愚者自系缚，　　为暗所缠裹。

Observing the world's decline, only seeing the changes in various forms means that people in the world have a thousand twists and turns in their actions. There are three things in the world: the world of vessels, the world of aggregates, and the world of sentient beings. The world of vessels refers to the trichiliocosm. The world of sentient beings refers to the sentient beings of the three realms, the four modes of birth, and the five destinies. The world of aggregates refers to the form aggregate and the formless aggregate. Among the three worlds, the realm of sentient beings is taken. Why is it said to be the Dharma of decline? The so-called Dharma of decline means being declined by desire, anger, and delusion. It is like merchants traveling far on the road, encountering thieves and losing their acquired wealth, being robbed by thieves. This kind of sentient being is also like this, being robbed by desire, anger, and delusion, cutting off the roots of goodness and wealth. Many people all see and know their decline. Among the billions and millions, only one or two escape. Therefore, it is said, "Observing the world's decline, only seeing the changes in various forms." The foolish bind themselves, entangled in darkness means that there are many people in the world with different ways of acting. They are always bound by two things: afflictions and aggregate bonds. Bound by these two things, they are covered by ignorance. They are also unable to attain liberation by skipping stages and exhausting defilements to accomplish purity. It is like a person with a crime being imprisoned, not seeing the light of the sun and moon. This kind of sentient being is also like this, bound by desire, anger, and delusion, and covered by the darkness of ignorance. If they want to seek liberation, it is difficult to attain. Therefore, it is said, "The foolish bind themselves, entangled in darkness." Also not seeing any practice, observing and finding nothing means that the sages observe sentient beings, wondering if there is any tiny root of goodness that can be treated. They observe everywhere but find no root of goodness that can be treated. The sages think to themselves, "Alas, the declining masses, their crimes are so grave!" Therefore, it is said, "Also not seeing any practice, observing and finding nothing."

观世衰耗法，但见众色变者，夫人处世千转万端，所行不同。世有三事：一者器世，二者阴世，三者众生世。所谓器世者，三千大千刹是也。众生世者，三界众生四生五趣是。阴世者，色阴无色阴。于三世中取众生界。何以故说衰耗之法？所谓衰耗法者，为婬怒痴所衰耗，犹如商贾远涉涂路，遇贼亡失所获财宝，为贼所劫；此众生类亦复如是，为婬怒痴所劫，劫断善根财货，众人皆见知其衰耗，亿千万众时有脱者，是故说曰，观世衰耗法，但见众色变也。愚者自系缚，为暗所缠裹者，世多有人行迹不同，恒为二缚所系，一者结使，二者阴缚，为此二事所缚无明所阴，盖亦不堪任越次取证尽有漏成无漏；犹若有罪之人，闭在牢狱不覩日月光明，此众生类亦复如是，以无明暗室所见缠裹，夫为欲怒痴所系缚，欲求解脱难可得也。是故说曰，愚者自系缚，为暗所缠裹也。亦不见于行，观而无所有，以性观察都不见功德之本，复以知他人心智，欲免此难者，无一善根可济免也；犹若有人没溺深厕粪除所污，复有慈愍之人，欲得免济彼难，求觅净处欲往手捉，遍悉观之无一净处，便舍而去。无漏之人观察众生，颇有毫厘善本可疗治乎？遍观察之无有善本可疗治者，圣人自念：「咄嗟衰耗群徒，罪重乃至于斯。」是故说曰，亦不见于行，观而无所有。

Sentient beings all have self, giving rise to suffering for them. Each
does not see the other, not seeing the thorn of wrong views.

众生皆有我，　　为彼而生患，一一不相见，　　不覩邪见刺。

Sentient beings all have self, giving rise to suffering for them means that there are many people in the world with inverted natures. Sentient beings think that they are created by self and arise from self. Some say they arise from others and exist due to others. Therefore, it is said, "Sentient beings all have self, giving rise to suffering for them." Each does not see the other, not seeing the thorn of wrong views means that each refers to non-Buddhist Brahmins. They do not contemplate right views but believe in wrong views and inversions. Therefore, it is said, "Each does not see the other, not seeing the thorn of wrong views."

众生皆有我，为彼而生患者，世多有人性怀颠倒，众生之类我所造、为从我而生；复有说者，从他而生、从他而有。是故说曰，众生皆有我，为彼而生患也。一一不相见，不覩邪见刺者，一一者，所谓外道梵志是，不思惟正见信邪颠倒，是故说曰，一一不相见，不覩邪见刺。

Observing the causes and conditions of this thorn, sentient beings are attached to it. I
create it, but it does not exist. It creates, but I do not have it.

观此刺因缘，　　众生所染著，我造彼非有，　　彼造非我有。

Observing the causes and conditions of this thorn means that the thorn refers to the thorn of wrong views. The causes and conditions refer to the different kinds of sentient beings in the hells, hungry ghosts, animals, humans, and heavens, each with different seeds. Therefore, it is said, "Observing the causes and conditions of this thorn." Sentient beings are attached to it means that non-Buddhist practitioners and students diligently and eagerly believe in wrong views and inversions day and night, unable to let go and follow the right path. Therefore, it is said, "Sentient beings are attached to it." I create it, but it does not exist means that each thinks they are right and interfere with each other. Sentient beings think that I create and make it, but it does not belong to them. They also think, "It creates and makes it, but it does not belong to me." Therefore, it is said, "I create it, but it does not exist. It creates, but I do not have it.

观此刺因缘者，所谓刺者，邪见之刺也，因缘者，地狱饿鬼畜生人道人天，各各别异所种不同，是故说曰，观此刺因缘也。众生所染著，外道异学昼夜孜孜汲汲，各自谓真信邪倒见，不能舍离就于正路，是故说曰，众生所染著。我造彼非有，彼造我非有者，各自谓正共相干错，众生之类，我作我造非彼所有，复自思惟：「彼造彼作非我所有。」是故说曰，我造彼非有，彼造我非有也。

Sentient beings are bound by arrogance and attachment to arrogance, deluded
by views, unable to escape the brink of birth and death.

众生为慢缠，　　染著于憍慢，为见所迷惑，　　不免生死际。

Sentient beings are bound by arrogance and attachment to arrogance means that they think their minds are arrogant and superior, thinking, "I am the most honored and supreme among the masses. My family lineage, reputation, house, fields, property, servants, and family wealth are unmatched." Their minds are firm and cannot let go. Therefore, it is said, "Sentient beings are bound by arrogance and attachment to arrogance." Deluded by views, unable to escape the brink of birth and death means that the view of eternalism is not associated with the view of annihilation, and the view of annihilation is not associated with the view of eternalism. They cannot escape this birth and death to reach the shore of the unconditioned. Therefore, it is said, "Deluded by views, unable to escape the brink of birth and death."

众生为慢缠，染著于憍慢者，彼人自念意性憍豪：「我今在众最尊最上，宗族姓望屋宅田业，仆从家产无及我者。」心意坚固不能舍离，是故说曰，众生为慢缠，染著于憍慢也。为见所迷惑，不免生死际者，计常见不与断灭见相应，断灭见不与计常见相应，不能免此生死至无为岸，是故说曰，为见所迷惑，不免生死际也。

Those who have attained and will attain are both subject to defilement
Cultivating the root of illness and learning all that is to be learned,

以逮及当逮，　　二俱受尘垢，习于病根本，　　及学诸所学，

Observing those who uphold precepts, those with pure conduct, and those who
care for the sick and weak - this is called reaching the limit.

观诸持戒者，　　梵行清净人，瞻视病瘦者，　　是谓至边际。

Those who have wrong views and are attached to desires, unable to let go, consider desire to be pure and indulge in it, giving rise to arrogance and not reforming - this is called the second limit, this is called the wise increasing attachments. Those who have attained and will attain obtain the aggregates and sense bases, while some do not obtain the aggregates and sense bases. These two are both subject to defilement: one is the defilement of wrong views, the other is the defilement of desire and attachment, bound by afflictions and unable to let go. Therefore it is said: both are subject to defilement. Those who cultivate the root of illness are the non-Buddhist practitioners who cultivate their skills and glorify themselves. Those who learn all that is to be learned are those sentient beings who learn their skills, riding horses, driving chariots, and accomplishing all kinds of things. Those who practice this attain liberation. Therefore it is said: those who learn all that is to be learned. Observing those who uphold precepts, some Brahmins uphold precepts, some uphold the crow precepts and make sounds like crows, some uphold the owl precepts and kneel and bow at times imitating the owl's cry, some uphold the deer precepts and make sounds like deer. Therefore it is said: observing those who uphold precepts. The pure Brahmins, those non-Buddhist practitioners say to each other: "Those who practice purity in pairs attain liberation and reach the pure place. If they serve fire, the sun, moon, divine jewels, medicine, herbs, clothing, palaces, and houses, then they will reach the unconditioned state." This is called the limit. There are sentient beings with strong wrong views who are attached to desires and cannot let go. They consider desire to be pure and practice it without fault. This is called the wise increasing attachments. Those who can know this do not follow the cycle of birth and death. Those with eyes observe. Those with eyes are the Buddhas and World Honored Ones. Faith can observe the cycle of birth and death and its cessation. Therefore it is said: those with eyes see. Those who understand these two limits are not attached and do not give rise to afflictions. This is called the limit.

世有众生邪见心盛，贪著爱欲不能舍离，洁欲清净玩而习之，于中兴起憍慢不自改更，是谓第二边际，是谓诸贤增益诸著。以逮及当逮者，得阴持入，或有不得阴持入者，此二俱受尘，一者邪见尘，二者爱欲尘，为结所使不能舍离，是故说曰，二俱受尘垢也。习于病根本者，外道异学是，习彼技术而自荣己，及学诸所学者，诸有众生学其技术，乘马御车造作无端皆能备悉，具此行者乃得解脱，是故说曰，及学诸所学也。观诸持戒者，或有梵志奉持禁戒，或持乌戒举声似乌，或持秃枭戒随时跪拜効秃枭鸣，或持鹿戒声响似鹿，是故说曰，观诸持戒者也。梵志清净人者，彼外道异学自相谓言：「其有两两行净行者，便得解脱至清净处，若复事火日月神珠药草衣服宫殿屋舍，然后乃至无为之处。」是谓名曰，一边际也。世有众生邪见心盛，贪著爱欲不能舍离，计欲清净外人习之犯欲无隙，是谓诸贤增益诸著，能得知此者亦不随流转。有目者观，所谓有目者，诸佛世尊是，信能观察流转停息，是故说曰，有目者所见。解此二边者，无所染著不兴尘劳，此名边际。

Observe the bubbles on water, also observe the mirage, in this way do
not observe the body, and also do not see the King of Death.

当观水上泡，　　亦观幻野马，如是不观身，　　亦不见死王。

Observe the bubbles on water, also observe the mirage. Just as those water bubbles cannot last long. Once there was a king's daughter who was loved by the king and never left his sight. When it rained, there were bubbles on the water. The daughter saw the water bubbles and was very fond of them. She said to the king, "I want to get the water bubbles to make a flower garland for my head." The king told the daughter, "Now the water bubbles cannot be obtained. How can you get them to make a flower garland?" The daughter said to the king, "If I cannot get them, I will kill myself." Hearing the daughter's words, the king summoned a skilled craftsman and told him, "You are skilled in all crafts and understand everything. Quickly get the water bubbles and make a garland for my daughter. If you do not, I will have you executed!" The craftsman said to the king, "We are not capable of getting the bubbles to make a garland." Among them was an old craftsman who volunteered to be able to get the bubbles. He then said to the king, "I am able to get the bubbles and make a garland for the king." The king was very happy and told the daughter, "Now there is someone who is able to make a garland. You can go yourself and watch over it." The daughter followed the king's words and watched outside. At that time, the old craftsman said to the king's daughter, "I am not familiar with the good and bad of water bubbles. I beg the king's daughter to personally get the bubbles. I will make a garland." The daughter then got the bubbles, but they burst as soon as she touched them. She could not get them. Like this, she tried all day but could not get the bubbles. The daughter became tired and gave up. She said to the king, "The water bubbles are false and cannot last long. I beg the king to make me a purple gold garland. It will not wither day and night." The water bubbles deceive the eyes. Although they have form, they are born and perish. The mirage is also like this. Thirst and fatigue lead to the loss of life. The human body is false, with little happiness and much suffering. It is subject to destruction and cannot last long. It changes and is not in the world for long. It is not seen by the King of Death. Therefore it is said: observe the bubbles on water, also observe the mirage. In this way do not observe the body, and also do not see the King of Death.

"Thus observe the body as a chariot of various colors" means that although the king's chariot is adorned with various colors, it is not solid and cannot bear heavy loads. Therefore, it is said, "Thus observe the body as a chariot of various colors." "Fools are attached to it, seeking to be far away from it" means that fools are greedy for and practice it, while the wise abandon it like throwing away excrement. Therefore, it is said, "Fools are attached to it, seeking to be far away from it."

The wise know it is unstable and do not delight in it. They always wish to be far away from it, avoiding it like a fire disaster. Therefore, it is said, "The wise are far away from it."

智人知动摇，心不愿乐，常意欲远离如避火灾，是故说曰，智者远离之。

People are born from the womb due to past causes and conditions. Many illnesses, few illnesses, good and bad appearances. Therefore, it is said, "Thus observe the body as the cause of many illnesses. Illness combines with ignorance. How can it be relied upon?"

The so-called other shore is the extinction of nirvana. Therefore, it is said, "The foolish take it as a cause, not seeking to cross to the other shore."

智慧之人，分别妙观思惟挍计，不兴想著，是故说曰，智者所厌患。

In the past, there was a wealthy family with abundant wealth and the seven treasures. The elder thought, "Now the young monks have not yet cut off their desires. The women were adorned and dressed in new clothes. They all came out to pay respects and give rise to respectful intentions. At that time, a six-supernatural-power arhat immediately realized and transformed a dead person's skeleton with the flesh and blood gone, the skull and limbs each in a different place. The arhat then told the monks, "You should concentrate your minds to seek liberation, do not look at women's forms and give rise to defiled and polluted minds." At that time, the elder saw that auspicious response and marveled at it. He reproached himself and knew it was not right. He prostrated with his five limbs and sought repentance, saying, "I now know the subtlety of the Dharma." The women were each ashamed and returned to their homes. At that time, the arhat told the elder, "The Buddha's teachings are vast and boundless. You now measure the sages with the wisdom of an ordinary person. This is not the right principle. 昔有豪族之家，饶财多宝七珍具足，长者自念：「今时年少道人情欲未断，我今宜请来在家，使诸妇女擎食供养，设有欲情者我当知之。」即往在寺请，诸年少道人诣长者家，庄严妇女更著新衣，尽出礼拜兴恭敬意。时有六通罗汉寻而觉知，即化死人骸骨血肉消尽，髑髅手脚各自一处。尔时罗汉告诸比丘：「当自专意以求度世，莫视女色兴秽污心。」时彼长者覩彼瑞应叹未曾有，内自克责知为不是，五体投地自求悔过：「我今乃知法之微妙。」诸妇女各各惭愧即还入舍。是时，罗汉告长者曰：「佛法宽博汪洋无涯，卿今以凡夫之智量度圣人，斯非正理，犹若拳许土凷仰比须弥，升合之器欲量海水。」尔时比丘便说此偈：

Attached to desire, defiled by desire, not investigating the conditions of afflictions, not
giving rise to afflictions, one should cross over the flow of desire and existence.

著欲染于欲，　　不究结使缘，不以生结使，　　当度欲有流。

"Attached to desire, defiled by desire" means that the aspirations of groups in the world are not the same. Some have little desire, while others have excessive desire. Those with excessive desire do not understand the Dharma of the sages. Therefore, it is said, "Attached to desire, defiled by desire." "Not investigating the conditions of afflictions" means that greed, jealousy, and stinginess are the most serious illnesses, penetrating to the bones and marrow, incurable by doctors. Accumulating wealth in the billions, one is unwilling to give in charity. At the end of one's life, one cannot take even a single coin with oneself. Those sentient beings who practice greed and jealousy have no spiritual power in their bodies and thus become impoverished. Their relatives are not harmonious and they are looked down upon by others. Therefore, it is said, "Not investigating the conditions of afflictions." "Not giving rise to afflictions, one should cross over the flow of desire and existence" means that there are four kinds of flows, which are not the same. What are the four? First, the flow of desire; second, the flow of existence; third, the flow of ignorance; fourth, the flow of views. Sentient beings sink and drown in birth and death due to these four. They wander in the four afflictions and cannot free themselves. They will traverse the five paths of rebirth. Therefore, it is said, "Not giving rise to afflictions, one should cross over the flow of desire and existence."

著欲染于欲者，群徒在世志趣不同，或有少欲或欲意偏多，欲偏多者不达贤之法，是故说曰，著欲染于欲也。不究结使缘者，贪嫉悭结病中之重者，入骨彻髓医所不疗，积财亿万不肯惠施，至其寿终不能持一钱自随；其有众生修行贪嫉者，身无威神遂致贫穷，宗亲不和为人所轻，是故说曰，不究结使缘也。不以生结使，当度欲有流者，流有四品，其事不同。云何为四？一者欲流，二者有流，三者无明流，四者见流。众生之类沈溺生死皆由此四，流浪四使不能自免，方当涉历流转五道，是故说曰，不以生结使，当度欲有流也。

Those who are free from all desires should contemplate this
great view. Such liberation is what has not been crossed before.

上一切无欲，　　当察此大观，如是有解脱，　　本所未度者。

"Those who are free from all desires" means those in the form and formless realms, while "desires" refers to the desire realm. In these three realms, they are completely free from the three poisons. Therefore, it is said, "Those who are free from all desires." "Should contemplate this great view" means that those free from desires are the Buddha's foremost disciples. The Buddha has four disciples, with the arhats being the most excellent, honorable, noble, and unsurpassed. Therefore, it is said, "Should contemplate this great view." "Such liberation" means that the sages practice for the sake of others, not for themselves. They are completely liberated from the four great floods and are no longer attached to existence in body and speech. Therefore, it is said, "Such liberation." "What has not been crossed before" refers to the difficulties of birth and death that one has experienced in the past. One has never been liberated. One should seek means to liberate oneself from these three existences and not create a body of the four great elements again. Therefore, it is said, "What has not been crossed before."

上一切无欲者，上者色界无色界，欲者欲界也，于此三界无复三毒，于中永得解脱，是故说曰，上一切无欲也。当察此大观者，无欲之人是佛第一弟子，佛有四弟子，罗汉为胜为尊为贵为无有上，是故说曰，当察此大观也。如是有解脱者，圣人执行不自为己，于诸四𫘝永得自在，更不著有在身口行，是故说曰，如是有解脱也。本所未度者，昔所经历生死之难，未曾为度，当求方便度此三有，更不受有造四大身，是故说曰，本所未度者。

Once, the Buddha was in the Niagroup Garden in Kapilavastu. At that time, the World-Honored One, having put on his robe and taken his alms bowl, went into the city of Kapilavastu with his attendant Ānanda to beg for alms. At that time, the young Nanda saw from a high building that the World-Honored One had entered the city to beg for alms. He quickly descended from the high building to where the World-Honored One was and bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, saying to the World-Honored One, "The Buddha's clan is a noble one in the country, a wheel-turning sage king. Why do you debase yourself by holding a bowl to beg for alms?" At that time, Nanda took the Buddha's bowl, went inside to fill it with delicious food and drink. Seeing Nanda enter the house, the Buddha told Ānanda, "I am now going to the Niagroup Garden. If Nanda comes out, do not take the bowl again. Tell Nanda to personally return the bowl to the Tathāgata." Nanda accepted the instruction and followed behind, delivering the bowl. His wife followed behind and said to Nanda, "Return quickly, do not stay long, wait until I come and then eat." He went forward a little, and his wife sent a message again, "Return in time, do not delay." The reason for being so insistent was because she feared he would leave home to study the path. Nanda brought the bowl to where the Tathāgata was and personally handed it to the Tathāgata, saying, "Please accept it now. I wish to return home." The Buddha told Nanda, "You have come this far. Now you should leave home, shave your head and beard, and wear the three Dharma robes. Why do you want to return home again?" At that time, the Tathāgata used his spiritual power to force Nanda to leave home and study the path, locking him in a quiet room and not allowing him to return home. In this way, days and months passed, and it was Nanda's turn to be in charge. Nanda heard this and was delighted inside, thinking, "I can now take advantage of this opportunity to escape and return home." At that time, Nanda took charge of preparing water and sweeping the ground, not neglecting anything. At that time, the heavenly spirits guarding Nanda filled the water and naturally overturned it in the clean ground, and the grass and soil grew again. The door was closed, but it naturally opened. Nanda thought, "My family is of royal descent, with abundant wealth and treasures, lacking nothing. I will now escape and return home. If there is any loss, I will make it up with my possessions. 「我今将汝天上游观，宜当自专勿怀恐怖。」是时，世尊以神足力，手接难陀将至天上。见一宫殿七宝所作，金银刻镂玉女营从不可称计，纯女无男亦无夫主。是时，难陀前白佛言：「是何天宫殿快乐无比？七宝殿堂弹琴鼓瑟，作倡伎乐共相娱乐昔所未闻。然此天女无有夫主，唯愿世尊解我狐疑。」尔时世尊告难陀曰：「汝自往彼问其情实？天女自当与汝说之。」难陀受教至彼天宫，以其情实问天女曰：「汝等天女自然受福，七宝殿堂五乐自娱，汝等夫主竟为所在？」天女报曰：「汝不知乎？阎浮利地迦维罗竭国，释迦文佛并父弟名曰难陀，命终之后当来生此处在天宫，彼人即我等夫主。」难陀闻之密自欢喜：「今所论者正是我也。」即还佛所具以此情白世尊言：「此诸宫殿玉女营从尽是我许。」佛告难陀：「快修梵行，如是不久当来至此受福自然。」是时，世尊以神足力，手接难陀将至地狱，路经铁围山表，见一猕猴瞎无一目。佛语难陀：「汝孙陀利妇何如是瞎猕猴乎？」难陀白佛：「止止世尊勿复说此！岂当以此方之彼人？孙陀利者女中英妙，六十四术无事不闲。」尔时世尊告难陀曰：「瞎猕猴比孙陀利，复以孙陀利比诸天女，亿千万倍不以譬喻为比。」是时，世尊即接难陀将至地狱，示彼苦痛考掠搒笞酸毒难计，八大地狱汤煑罪人，一大地狱十六隔子围绕其狱，刀山剑树火车炉炭，烧炙缹煮苦痛难陈。有一大镬狱卒围遶，汤沸火炽不见罪人。难陀白佛：「不审，世尊！斯诸地狱皆有罪囚，斯是何镬不见罪人？」佛告难陀：「汝躬自往问彼狱卒，自当为汝说其本末。」是时，难陀受佛教诫，往问狱卒：「斯是何镬空无罪人？」狱卒报曰：「阎浮利地，真净王家儿得成道，并父弟甘露王儿，名曰难陀，为人放逸婬欲情多，自恃豪族轻忽万民，彼命终之后当来入此镬中，经历劫数乃得免脱。卿欲知者其事如是。」难陀闻已衣毛皆竖，形体战栗颜色变异，往趣世尊前白佛言：「唯然天师二界大护，今覩此变倍怀恐惧。」寻于佛前而说此偈：

I now take you to tour the heavens. You should concentrate and not be afraid." At that time, the World-Honored One used his supernatural powers to take Nanda by the hand and bring him to the
heavens. He saw a palace made of the seven treasures, with countless jade maidens attending to it. There were only women, no men, and no husbands. Nanda then addressed the Buddha, saying, "What is this heavenly palace?

「今舍天上位，　　不造生死本，求离地狱苦，　　愿说泥洹灭。」

At that time, the World-Honored One gradually spoke the subtle Dharma to Nanda, establishing him in the formless and leading him to the place of the Way.

尔时世尊渐与难陀说微妙法，安处无为令至道场。

The blue-robed and white-canopied one, the charioteer driving a single wheel, observing
those who are covered in dust and dirt, forever severing the bonds.

青衣白盖覆，　　御者御一轮，观彼末尘垢，　　永便断缚者，

Many people seek refuge in the spirits of mountains, rivers
trees, groves, and shrines, hoping to escape suffering and difficulties.

人多求自归，　　山川树木神，园观及神祠，　　望免苦患难。

People are fearful and confused, not realizing the truth. They rely on past prayers and sacrifices without distinguishing between true and false. In the past, there was a king named Aksobhya in the country of Yuezhi. He ruled over the world without resistance. His mother instructed him, saying, "If you encounter the danger of death, do not turn left towards the Buddha's temple. Remember to turn right. Do not disobey this instruction of mine." At that time, King Aksobhya led a large army to attack the city of Jinshi. He personally wielded a sword to kill three hundred million people, not quite reaching four hundred million, aiming to reach five hundred million. Later, in battle, he was defeated and fled on an elephant. Looking back, he saw the Buddha's temple and remembered his mother's instruction. He then turned the elephant to the right. The enemy saw this and all surrendered and returned to their country. The king saw the enemy retreat and pursued them. He then remembered the Buddha's words: "Those who take refuge in the Buddha are supreme and unsurpassed. If I had not turned right, how could I have defeated this enemy?" Therefore, it is said: "Many people seek refuge in the spirits of mountains, rivers, trees, groves, and shrines, hoping to escape suffering and difficulties."

人怀恐惧意迷不寤，值前祷祀不别真伪。昔月支国有王名恶少，王此天下莫不靡伏。母教勅王：「设卿有临死之难，慎莫左旋佛寺，当念右旋，慎莫违吾此教。」是时，恶少王大出兵众攻纯西城，手自执剑杀三亿人，不满四亿，规满五亿，后战不如，乘象奔走，顾见佛图忆母教诫，便回象右旋，敌国见之皆伏还国。王见贼退寻后追摄，即还坏贼擒获王身，便忆佛语：「自归佛者，为尊为上无有及者。设我不右旋者，岂能坏此贼乎？」是故说曰，人多求自归，山川树木神，园观及神祠，望免苦患难也。

This is not the supreme refuge, nor is it auspicious. If
one takes refuge in this way, one will not escape all suffering.

此非自归上，　　亦非有吉利，如有自归者，　　不脱一切苦。

If one takes refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the
Sangha, and cultivates the noble four truths as seen by wisdom,

若有自归佛，　　归法比丘僧，修习圣四谛，　　如慧之所见。

The causes and conditions of suffering arise, one should transcend the root of
suffering. The eightfold path of the sages, the end of the sweet dew.

苦因苦缘生，　　当越此苦本，贤圣八品道，　　灭尽甘露际。

This is the highest refuge, not without auspicious benefits. If there
are those who take refuge, they will be liberated from all suffering.

是为自归上，　　非不有吉利，如有自归者，　　得脱一切苦。

The cultivation of the path by humans only has faith and righteousness. With the root of faith complete, precepts are not violated. All sentient beings who can take refuge in these Three Jewels will have no wishes unfulfilled, will be revered by gods and humans, will attain the path on their own, and will also receive blessings for eternal eons. Those who have no refuge are like a tree without roots. If there is something to rely on, what is not accomplished?

人之修道唯有信义，信根以全戒则不毁。诸有众生能自归此三宝者，无愿不成，为天人所供养，自致得道，亦复受永劫之福。人之无怙犹树之无根，若有所凭何事不果也。

Observe what should be observed, and also observe what should not
be observed. Observe again and again, and observe without further observation.

观以观当观，　　不观亦当观，观而复重观，　　观而不复观。

What is meant by "observation"? It refers to observing the four noble truths of suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Those who practice the teachings observe the four noble truths of suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. They observe the present, observe the past, and will observe the future. The arising of afflictions is all due to the three time periods. Falling into birth and death, one does not reach the path. Therefore, it is said, "Observe what should be observed, and also observe what should not be observed." "Not observing, one should still observe" means not seeing suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. One should deeply observe and discern clearly, knowing that it is because one does not see the four noble truths of suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Therefore, it is said, "Not observing, one should still observe." "Observe again and again" means having faith and being able to distinguish suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. One contemplates each one, thoroughly understanding its meaning. "Observe without further observation" means having already observed and known, one does not contemplate further. Therefore, it is said, "Not observing, one should still observe, and observe again and again, but not observe further."

所谓观者，苦习尽道真如四谛，彼执行人，以观苦习尽道真如四谛，观者现在以观过去当观未来，兴于尘劳皆由三世，坠堕生死不至于道，是故说曰，观以观当观也。不观亦当观者，所谓不观者，不见苦习尽道，如是当观深察分明，知为不见苦习尽道真如四谛，是故说曰，不观亦当观也。观而复重观者，信能分别苦习尽道，一一思惟究畅其义。观而不复观者，已观已知不复思惟，是故说曰，不观亦当观，观而复不观也。

Observe again and again, and discern their inherent nature. Consider
day as night, for the precious body will soon be destroyed.

观而复重观，　　分别彼性本，计昼以为夜，　　宝身坏不久。

"Observe again and again" means there are two kinds of observation: first, observation of wealth, and second, observation of the ultimate truth. As for the observation of wealth, it increases afflictions, while the observation of the ultimate truth exhausts afflictions and perfects the practice of the formless. Therefore, it is said, "Observe again and again." "Discern their inherent nature" means that the natures and practices of people are not the same. The Dharma teachings in different countries are not uniform. The sages discern them one by one. Some have the intention to awaken, some do not, and some both awaken and do not. Sentient beings have different natures and awakenings. Therefore, the sages teach them the Way, diligently practicing day and night without slacking. Therefore, it is said, "Discern their inherent nature." "Consider day as night" means that the natures and practices of sentient beings are not the same. Some contemplate the roots of goodness, while others do not. This is called considering day as night. "The precious body will soon be destroyed" means that worldly wealth is a common phenomenon in the world. It is accumulated all day but will eventually be exhausted. The wealth of wholesome roots never decays. Therefore, the Vinaya scriptures say, "Exchange your precious body for an ordinary body, your precious wealth for ordinary wealth, and your precious life for an ordinary life." Therefore, it is said, "The precious body will soon be destroyed."

观而复重观者，观有二种：一者财观，二者第一义观。夫财观者增益结使，第一义者尽有漏成无漏行，是故说曰，观而复重观也。分别彼性本者，或有人性造行不同，国界若干法教非一，圣人在中一一分别；或有意开寤者，或有意不开寤者，或有开寤不开寤者，众生受性寤有迟疾，是以圣人训之以道，懃加修行昼夜匪懈，是故说曰，分别彼性本也。计昼以为夜者，众生之类性行不同，或思善本，或不思善本，是谓计昼以为夜也。宝身坏不久者，世间财货世之常法，终日聚集要当消坏，善根财货者终不腐败，是故律本说曰：「当以不宝之身易宝身，不宝之财易宝财，不宝之命易宝命。」是故说曰，宝身坏不久也。

Observe but not repeatedly observe" means that although one sees, one does not truly
see. It is like seeing but not truly seeing. Observing but not truly seeing.

观而不重观，　　虽见亦不见，如见而不见，　　观而亦不见。

"Observe but not repeatedly observe" means that the practitioner contemplates subtle observation, but the sage observes and knows that the practitioner also lacks subtle observation. There are two kinds of people who attain meditative concentration: one attains observation, and one does not. There is also a guide who observes the practitioner, wondering if there is anyone who responds to the truth of the noble ones. After thorough observation, it is not suitable for the truth of the noble ones. Therefore, it is said, "Observe but not repeatedly observe." "Observe but not truly see" means that there are many who contemplate and cultivate the practice of the path, and also observe the affairs of the distant past. Some understand, and some do not. Each is distinguished without confusion. Therefore, it is said, "Observe but not truly see"

观而不重观者，彼修行人思惟妙观，道者观察知彼行人亦无妙观。得思惟定者有二种人：一人得观，一者不得观。复更有导师观察行人，颇有应于圣谛者不？遍思观之不应圣谛，是故说曰，观而不重观也。观而亦不见者，多有思惟修集道行，复观久远过去世事，或有达者，或有不达者，一一分别亦不错乱，是故说曰，观而亦不观也。

How does one see and not see? What is meant by seeing and not seeing?
What causes seeing and not seeing? What is the cause of seeing and not seeing?

云何见不见？　　何说见不见？因何见不见？　　因为出何见。

"How does one see and not see?" means that the practitioner cultivates the Dharma, considering it to be an eternally pure Dharma. "What is meant by not seeing" means not seeing suffering, origination, cessation, and the path. Therefore, it is said, "How does one see and not see?" "What causes seeing and not seeing" means that two people practice together and are fully endowed with merit and virtue. Although they are in saṃsāra, they are not timid. They intend to sever the bonds without hesitation. One person's intention is biased and does not reach the ultimate, while the other does not see the cessation of all taints. One does not see being in saṃsāra. Therefore, it is said, "What causes seeing and not seeing?" "What is the cause of seeing and not seeing" means that due to the Dharma of the sages, one sees the meaning of liberation for oneself. One's wishes are certainly accomplished without fear or avoidance. Therefore, it is said, "What is the cause of seeing and not seeing?"

云何见不见者，行人修法计有是常清净之法，所谓不见者，不见苦习尽道，是故说曰，云何见不见也。何说见不见者，行人唯见一缘，或缘色或缘色声香味，或有思惟或不思惟，是故说曰，何说见不见也。因何见不见者，犹如二人众行以具功德备悉，虽在生死不怀怯弱，意求断结亦无疑滞，一人意偏不达究竟，一者不见断诸有漏，一者不见在诸生死，是故说曰，因何见不见也。因为出何见者，由贤圣法自见出要义，所愿必克无所畏忌，是故说曰，因何出何见也。

Just as one does not contemplate suffering, one should always deeply contemplate oneself, in
order to understand the source of suffering. This is called the subtle contemplation of wisdom.

犹若不观苦，　　常当深自观，以解苦根源，　　是谓明妙观。

"Just as one does not contemplate suffering" means that the learner does not see suffering, emptiness, non-self, and the absence of a self. He also does not discern the aggregates of conditioned phenomena. He then falls into error. He contemplates the impurities and uncleanliness within his own body, from head to toe, without anything to be desired. He thinks, "I have form, I have form." He also does not discern the beginning and end of form. Therefore, it is said, "Just as one does not contemplate suffering, one should always deeply contemplate oneself." "In order to understand the source of suffering, this is called the subtle contemplation of wisdom" means that one understands the meaning of suffering, emptiness, impermanence, and non-self. The body is afflicted with myriad illnesses. The practitioner contemplates without confusion. He deeply understands the source of suffering. The body is entrusted to the world, composed of the four great elements. Since countless eons ago, it has not seen the great illumination. This is due to being bound by delusion. I now escape and do not create those conditions. Therefore, it is said, "In order to understand the source of suffering, this is called the subtle contemplation of wisdom."

犹若不观苦者，如彼学人不见苦空非身无我，亦不分别于诸行阴，便为堕落；自观身中污秽不净，从头至足无一可贪，我自我有色自我色，亦不分别色之本末，是故说曰，犹若不观苦，常当深自观也。以解苦根源，是谓明妙观者，所解苦空无常非身之义，身之为患流溢万病，行人思惟意不乱错，深知病之根源，身寄于世四大合成，从无数劫以来不覩大明，斯由痴惑所缠裹故，我今以脱不造彼缘。是故说曰，以解苦根源，是谓明妙观也。

Who causes ordinary people not to see the origin of various practices?
Because of that, they observe and remove darkness to see great illumination.

谁令凡夫人，　　不覩众行本，因彼而观察，　　去冥见大明。

"Who causes ordinary people not to see the origin of various practices?" means that the blind and deluded in the world do not see the great illumination. Who created it? Sentient beings wander without recognizing the right path. There are currently the four great elements, the aggregates, and the bases of suffering. The ignorant are attached and do not believe in the affliction. They give rise to various wrong views and further increase afflictions. Because of this, practitioners observe themselves and contemplate day and night to sever afflictions. "Removing darkness to see great illumination" means that the origin of great illumination is without the root of darkness. They do not recognize the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Sangha. They also do not recognize the true suchness of the four noble truths of suffering, origin, cessation, and path. They do not cultivate the practice of pure realms. Therefore, it is said, "Who causes ordinary people not to see the origin of various practices?"

谁令凡夫人，不覩众行本者，世间盲冥不覩大明谁之所造，众生游游不识正路，现有四大阴持入苦，愚者染著不信为患，兴诸邪见遂增尘劳；因彼行人而自观察，昼夜思惟断结为业，去冥见大明，大明之本无冥根是，不识佛不识法不识比丘僧，亦复不识真如四谛苦习尽道，不修境界清净之行，是故说曰，谁令凡夫人，不覩众行本也。
