T 212.29

Chuyao jing / Udānavarga (T 212) 出曜经

T 212.29 ◎恶行品第二十九

Parallel to: DN 14 / DN 16 / SN 1.22 / SN 2.22 / SN 3.15 (~) / SN 7.4 / AN 5.174 (~) / Dhp 1–20 / Dhp 33–43 / Dhp 60–75 / Dhp 76–89 / Dhp 116–128 / Dhp 129–145 / Dhp 157–166 / Dhp 179–196 / Dhp 197–208 / Ud 5.3 / Ud 5.6 / Ud 5.8 / Ud 8.5 / Ud 8.7 / Snp 2.3 / Snp 3.10 / Iti (~) / Thag 17 / T 210.17 / T 210.18 / T 210.22 / T 213.28

Do not do evil, practice all that is good, purify your own mind: this is the teaching of all the Buddhas.

诸恶莫作，　　诸善奉行，自净其意，是诸佛教。

"Do not do evil" means that the Buddhas and World-Honored Ones instruct later generations on the path of the Three Vehicles. One does not attain the Way by cultivating evil, but by cultivating goodness and attaining the Way on one's own. Therefore, it is said, "Do not do evil." "Practice all that is good" means that practitioners universally cultivate all goodness, adorning themselves with all virtues. Seeing evil, they avoid it and always cultivate goodness. What is called goodness is the wonderful medicine of stopping and contemplating, burning away deluded thoughts. Therefore, it is said, "Practice all that is good." "Purify one's own mind" means that the mind is the basis of actions, attracting the roots of sin. The hundred and eight heavy roots are difficult to untie, binding the mind. Desire, anger, and delusion are rampant, with pride, stinginess, and jealousy giving rise to various defilements. Those with this illness have impure minds. Practitioners hold their intentions and refine their minds to prevent deluded thoughts. If they do not cease like this, they will form the roots of the Way. Therefore, it is said, "Purify one's own mind." "This is the teaching of all the Buddhas" means that the Tathāgata's teachings on precepts are not the same. Precepts restrain the form, and meaning subdues the mind. The Buddha's appearance in the world is extremely rare, like the udumbara flower, which only appears once in many millions of eons. Therefore, the Tathāgata's bequeathed teachings and edification have been passed down from sage to sage until today. The precepts are not to be neglected, and giving is not to be left undone. The reason I have attained Buddhahood and rule over the Three Thousand is all due to the precepts and giving. Therefore, it is said, "This is the teaching of all the Buddhas."

诸恶莫作者，诸佛世尊教诫后人三乘道者，不以修恶而得至道，皆习于善自致道迹，是故说曰，诸恶莫作也。诸善奉行者，彼修行人普修众善，唯自璎珞具足众德，见恶则避恒修其善，所谓善者，止观妙药烧灭乱想。是故说曰，诸善奉行。自净其意者，心为行本招致罪根，百八重根难解之结缠裹其心，欲怒痴盛憍慢悭嫉种诸尘垢，有此病者则心不净，行人执志自练心意使不乱想，如是不息便成道根，是故说曰，自净其意也。是诸佛教者，如来演教禁戒不同，戒以检形义以摄心，佛出世间甚不可遇，犹如优昙钵花亿千万劫时时乃有；是故如来遗诫教化，圣圣相承以至今日，禁诫不可不修，惠施不可不行，吾所成佛王三千者，皆由禁诫惠施所致也。是故说曰，是诸佛教。

Giving brings about the reward of blessings, not harboring anger and resentment
using goodness to eliminate evil, with no trace of desire, anger, or delusion.

惠施获福报，　　不藏恚怒怀，以善灭其恶，　　欲怒痴无余。

Giving brings about the reward of blessings, not harboring anger and resentment, using goodness to eliminate evil, with no trace of desire, anger, or delusion.

昔日大目揵连同产弟，饶财多宝七珍具足，金银珍宝、车璩马瑙、真珠虎珀，库藏盈溢仆从奴婢不可称计。是时，目连往到弟家而告弟曰：「闻卿悭嫉不好惠施。佛常演说，夫人惠施获报无数。卿今施者得福无量。」弟闻兄教开藏惠施，更新立库藏欲受其报。未经旬日财宝竭尽，故藏悉空新藏无报，甚至懊恼向兄说曰：「前见告勅施获大报，不敢违教竭藏惠施，当来过去诸贫穷者靡不周遍；然财宝货尽旧藏空竭新藏无报，将无为兄所疑误乎？」目连告曰：「止止族姓子！莫陈此语。无使异学邪见之士闻此麤言。若使福德当有形者，虚空境界所不容受，吾今权且示汝微报，若欲见者随从我来。」尔时目连以神足力，手接其弟至于六天。彼有宫殿七宝合成，前后浴池香风远布，库藏盈溢不可称计，玉女营从数千万众，纯女无男亦无夫主。弟白目连：「是何宫殿巍巍乃尔？不见有男，纯是女人。」目连告弟：「汝今往问，自当知之。」即往问之：「天女当知！我有所问愿见发遣。」天女问曰：「有何狐疑而欲见问？」其人报曰：「是何宫殿七宝合成，巍巍堂堂悬处虚空？谁有斯德于中受福？愿解我疑永无犹豫。」天女报曰：「汝不知乎？我等在此积有年岁，食福自然无复是过，欲知我夫主者，施在心怀今当与说。阎浮利内迦比国界释迦文佛神力弟子，名曰目连，彼有贤者弟大富长者，好憙惠施周穷济乏，彼命终之后当来生此，与我等作夫主，七宝宫殿及我等身惠施之报。」其人闻憙善心生焉，还至兄所具白其情。目连告曰：「云何族姓子？夫人惠施当有报耶？为无报耶？」弟怀惭愧头面忏悔，还至世间广施不惓。是故说曰，惠施获福报。不藏恚怒怀者，夫人怀毒藏匿在内，伺人之恶恼人之善，如斯之类不可与亲；如灰覆火，目虽不覩，蹈则烧脚，身无防备唐突禁戒，当时意勇不觉伤损。人之伤害自古有之，或先怀嫌，或卒兴怒，卒兴怒者犹尚可恕，先怀嫌者斯意难亲。所以然者？夫人阴谋必有伤克，群愚相逐遂致恶灾，外扬不密内共情通，共相称誉成恶朋友，事与愿违遂致丧没，家属财产斯皆入官，人所憎嫉恶闻其声。是故说曰，不藏恚怒怀也。以善灭其恶，欲怒痴无余者，所谓善者贤圣道品是也，乘此道品，犹四渎水断流而无所畏难，灭渡诸恶部使不复生，有灾吐毒欲怒痴生，拔三根栽种其三业，仰修道观进趣四道有何难受？是故说曰，以善灭其恶，欲怒痴无余也。

Walk alone, do not follow fools; if you want to associate, follow
the wise. The wise eliminate evil, like a crane selecting milk to drink.

只行勿逐愚，　　欲群当逐智，智者灭其恶，　　如鹤择乳饮。

Walk alone, do not follow fools; if you want to associate, follow the wise. The wise eliminate evil, like a crane selecting milk to drink.

只行勿逐愚者，所谓只行者，在闲静之处意不分散，思惟善本系念在前，设欲同处当与善知识从事，莫与恶知识从事，是故说曰，只行勿逐愚也。欲群当逐智者，世多有人慕及上贤，追逐有智持戒精进辩才深邃，堪说道教不怀] cf.【丽】疲劳，是故说曰，欲群当逐智也。智者灭其恶者，智慧之人明古达今，出言所说必有所济，昼夜孜孜思惟道术，承受明智所吐言教，以善功德消灭众恶，是故说曰，智者灭其恶也。如鹤择乳饮者，如昔有人多捕群鹤孚乳滋长，展转相生其数无限，养鹤之法，以水和乳乃得食之，鹤之常法当食之时，鼻孔出气吹水两辟纯食其乳，鸟之顽卤由能分别去水食乳。今之比丘能不尔乎？当选其善蠲除其恶，如彼鸟鹤深知好恶也。是故说曰，如鹤择乳饮也。

Observing the world's various changes, knowing the traces of the arising and ceasing of dharmas
the sages do not delight in the world, the foolish do not dwell with the wise.

观世若干变，　　知法起灭迹，贤圣不乐世，　　愚者不处贤。

Observing the world's various changes, knowing the traces of the arising and ceasing of dharmas, the sages do not delight in the world, the foolish do not dwell with the wise.

观世若干变者，所谓世者世有三品：一者器世，二者阴世，三者众生世。此三世者贮病之牢室，内外坚固非医所疗治，内者四百四病同时俱作，外者含毒之类，蚖蛇百足蝮蝎虎狼所见噬螫，众变若干其事不同，水火盗贼怨雠之类窃来伤害。是故说曰，观世若干变也。知法起灭迹，知迹起灭其事有二：一者结迹，二者阴迹。能灭其事乃应无为，是故名曰，知法起灭迹。贤圣不乐世，愚者不处贤者，贤圣永灭诸恶不处群俗，鹤飞则高不乐丘冢，猩猩好净不处厕溷，贤圣之人亦复如是，不处群俗与共同光；愚者好恶不处贤众。是故说曰，贤圣不乐世，愚者不处贤也。

Understanding the taste of waiting for thoughts, contemplating the meaning of rest
without heat or hunger, one should take the taste of the Dharma.

解知念待味，　　思惟休息义，无热无饥想，　　当服于法味。

Understanding the taste of waiting for thoughts, contemplating the meaning of rest, without heat or hunger, one should take the taste of the Dharma.

解知念待味者，经历无数生死已来，未曾得此念待之味，世多甘美殊] cf.【丽】胜之味，苷蔗葡萄如此之比不可称数，昼夜享之无有厌足；然不从此得至无为，念待味者未曾经口，设当一遇永无饥渴，其余味者展转生死坠堕三涂，欲求出期实为难矣。是故说曰，解知念待味也。思惟休息义者，彼修行人专精一已思惟禅定，心所念法终不错乱，从初至竟不识其绪，是故说曰，思惟休息义也。无热无饥想者，贪欲是热、瞋恚是热、愚痴是热、饥渴是热，能断此饥渴热者，其事甚难；正使饮此四大海水欲消其渴者，未始见也。欲除其渴永使不生，唯有八解澄净之味，乃得消此众渴之本。是故说曰，无热无渴想。当服于法味者，所谓法味，众施法施胜，众味法味胜，得此味者法身不离苦本，断诸世俗饥渴之患，人欲修学求其解脱，不得甘露至要之味者，安坐无为？不自慇懃欲求得道迹者甚为难矣。是故说曰，当服于法味也。

People do not harm their minds, nor do they destroy their intentions. With
goodness, they forever eliminate evil, and do not worry about falling into evil paths.

人不损其心，　　亦不毁其意，以善永灭恶，　　不忧堕恶道。

People do not harm their minds, nor do they destroy their intentions. With goodness, they forever eliminate evil, and do not worry about falling into evil paths.

人不损其心，亦不毁其意者，人初立行先习善法，初意犹豫乍信乍不信，其意勇者闻辄信解，意狐疑者不达于法，此人必当经历生死，亿佛超过不蒙得度。设损其心不毁其意，欲得至道取之甚易。人欲修学专意乃获，如匹夫闻彼有法，中路多难无由经过，一意念彼形意以达。何以故知？如彼得通之人，心念形以随，是故说曰，人不损其心，亦不毁其意也。以善永灭恶，不忧堕恶道者，夫人习行敦崇道业，世俗见根而现在前，虽有善根，斯是世俗有漏之行，不兴想著求于上及，斯人终不忧堕恶趣。是故说曰，以善永灭恶，不忧堕恶道也。

People who wish to refine their spirits must repeatedly practice polishing. The wise are
easy to carve and adorn, and are called heroes of the world. Those who
can be close to them are at ease and have no worries or troubles.

人欲练其神，　　要当数修琢，智者易雕饰，　　乃名世之雄，能亲近彼者，　　安隐无忧恼。

People who wish to refine their spirits must repeatedly practice polishing. The wise are easy to carve and adorn, and are called heroes of the world. Those who can be close to them are at ease and have no worries or troubles.

人欲练其神，要当数修琢者，旧学之人外虚内实，或有潜隐山薮，或有佯狂游世，行虽不同所济等一，此不取形器，此纯练精神定意不错，行人权现千转百化，要设方便导引众生至百练室；所谓室者，泥洹虚寂无为城是。是故说曰，人欲练其神，要当数雕琢也。智者易雕饰，乃名世之雄者，捷疾利根之人出言成律，必欲所度得四辩才，义辩法辩辞辩应辩，义辩法辩者此二摄内法，辞辩应辩者此二摄外法。是故说曰，智者易雕饰，乃名世之雄也。能亲近彼者，安隐无忧恼者，人执威仪进止去来周旋往返，皆执威仪不失其节，犹如众花竞敷香气远布；履行之人亦复如是，戒闻施德诸总持门定意不散者，能亲近此无所违失，便能成就无漏圣行。是故说曰，能亲近彼者，安隐无忧恼。

Those who permanently cease without error are gentle and not hasty
They blow away all evil dharmas like wind blowing away leaves.

永息无过者，　　柔和不卒暴，吹弃诸恶法，　　如风落其叶。

Those who permanently cease without error are gentle and not hasty. They blow away all evil dharmas like wind blowing away leaves.

永息无过者，柔和不卒暴者，诸根具足无所流溢，所说专正言不卒暴，威仪礼节无所漏失，如斯之人，无有俦匹亦无过者，是故说曰，永息无过者，柔和不卒暴也。吹弃诸恶法，如风去其叶者，行人执意铿然不动，执信坚固毫厘不犯，去诸恶法日进其善，昼夜挍饰不令有尘，如铁生垢莹治乃明，人心重垢须慧乃照，是故说曰：吹弃诸恶法，如风去其叶也。

Without reason, they fear those who slander the pure ones. They soon
experience the power of evil, like smoke and clouds blown by the wind.

无故畏彼人，　　谤毁清净者，寻恶获其力，　　烟云风所吹。

Without reason, they fear those who slander the pure ones. The highest practice of people is to remove defilements. Practitioners remove defilements and cultivate purity. When merit is fulfilled, why fear not reaching it? Those without stinginess and jealousy cultivate the roots of the path and suddenly awaken. This is because they thoroughly understand the profound essentials. Pure people have no afflictions. Fools slander them as impure. Slandering sages brings the sin of indiscriminate retribution. This is due to the accumulation of merit and practice. Therefore, it is said: "Without reason, they fear those who slander the pure ones." People are deluded and take evil as wonderful. Therefore, calamities gradually enter Mount Tai, creating the sins of hell, hungry ghosts, and various animal realms. Therefore, it is said: "Soon experiencing the power of evil, like smoke and clouds blown by the wind."

无故畏彼人，谤毁清净者，人之修学除秽为上，行人除垢唯修清净，功德充满何惧不达，心无悭嫉者，崇其道根豁然自寤，斯由通达了深要故。清净之人无有结使，愚者谤毁谓为不净，谤毁圣者受无择罪。斯由福报积行所致。是故说曰，无故畏彼人，谤毁清净者也。寻恶获其力，烟云风所吹者，世人执迷以恶为妙，由是殃祸渐入泰山，造地狱饿鬼杂畜生之罪，是故说曰，寻恶获其力，烟云风所吹也。

People's practices are each known by themselves. Goodness is good, and evil is evil.

人之为行，　　各各自知，善之为善，恶之为恶。

People's practices are each known by themselves. Evil ones know evil, and good ones know good. Even if one does good or evil without knowing it, the retribution is doubled. The good receive blessings endlessly, and the evil receive double punishment. The pure receive holy life, and the impure receive impure practices. At the time of death, good and evil are clearly distinguished. If the spirit is welcomed, one sees palaces, houses, gardens, ponds, and gods who are not confused. Robes and clothing naturally cover the body, and heavenly maidens surround and entertain together. One sees the light shining unobstructed. Those who accumulate evil see great fires, swords, and halberds when they are about to die. They see vultures, foxes, rākṣasas, demons, tigers, wolves, and vicious beasts. They also see sword mountains, sword trees, thorns, pits, and evil ghosts surrounding them.

人之为行，各各自知者，人之修行志趣若干，恶者自知恶、善者自知善，虽为善恶不自知者受报一倍，善者受福无穷，恶者受罪一倍，净者受净行，不净者受不净行；临终之时善恶然别，若神来迎，见宫殿屋舍园观浴池神不错乱，衣被服饰自然著体，天女围遶共相娱乐，还自见光所照无碍。积恶之人临死之日神识倒错，但见大火剑戟，见蹲鵄野狐罗刹妖魅虎狼恶兽，复见刀山剑树荆棘坑坎恶鬼围遶。是故说曰，善之为善，恶之为恶也。

People's evil deeds will later bring their own retribution. Having done no evil, there is nothing to worry about later.

人之为恶，　　后自受报，已不为恶，后无所忧。

People's evil deeds will later bring their own retribution. People's evil deeds bring about their own calamities and troubles. It is not their parents, siblings, or relatives who suffer the consequences on their behalf. If one does not do evil, one will not receive retribution later. Such a person, when born, will encounter the sages and receive their blessings. It is not their parents or siblings who receive the blessings on their behalf. Their minds are naturally pure and do not burden others. They act in purity and receive their own retribution. Therefore, it is said, "People's evil deeds will later bring their own retribution. Having done no evil, there is nothing to worry about later."

人之为恶，后自受报者，夫人为恶自招祸患，非有父母兄弟宗族代受其罪；自不为恶后不受报，如此之人，生则遇圣当受其福，非父母兄弟代获其庆，意自清洁不累于人，自行清净自受其报。是故说曰，人之为恶，后自受报，已不为恶，后无所忧也。

Understanding one's own purity and impurity, how can one worry about others'
purity? The foolish do not refine themselves, like iron drilling pure steel.

达己净不净，　　何虑他人净？愚者不自练，　　如铁钻纯钢。

Understanding one's own purity and impurity, how can one worry about others' purity? If one is pure oneself, one can also make others practice purity. If one's own practice is uneven, how can one make others attain pure practice? Therefore, it is said, "Understanding one's own purity and impurity, how can one worry about others' purity?" The foolish do not refine themselves, like iron drilling pure steel. The foolish practice endlessly. What they create in one day will cause them to fall for eons. Even if they encounter sages, they will not be saved. It is like drilling pure steel with iron. The effort is futile. Therefore, it is said, "The foolish do not refine themselves, like iron drilling pure steel."

达己净不净，何虑他人净者，己自清净亦能使彼行清净，己行不均焉能使彼得清净行？是故说曰，达己净不净，何虑他人净也。愚者不自练，如铁钻纯钢者，愚人所习终日不穷，一日所造坠堕永劫，虽遇贤圣不蒙济度，犹铁钻纯钢功至不可获。是故说曰，愚者不自练，如铁钻纯钢也。

If the eyes see what is not wrong, the wise seek means
The wise live well in the world, and do not do many evils.

若眼见非邪，　　黠人求方便，智者善寿世，　　亦不为众恶。

If the eyes see what is not wrong, the wise seek means. The wise live well in the world, and do not do many evils.

若眼见非邪者，夫人习行专精为恶，若眼见色不起眼识，若好若丑意悉平等，设见好色不兴染著，设见恶色亦不怀戚，是故说曰，若眼见非邪。黠人求方便者，见彼眼色知为非真，为磨灭法迁转不住，生者有尽常者亦灭，愚者玩习智者所嗤，是故说曰，黠人求方便也。智者善寿世，亦不为众恶者，智人所施教权化非一，防恶于无形，养福于自然，执行不累于世，言教不损于形质；在世周旋，未几彼寿，见短如有耻，见长不自称，在世讫其寿，终不为恶行。是故说曰，智者善寿世，不为众恶也。

Merchants on the road are afraid, with few companions and much merchandise
passing through dangerous places, but there is the worry of broken axles.

商人在路惧，　　伴少而货多，经过崄难处，　　然有折轴忧。

Merchants on the road are afraid, with few companions and much merchandise. In the past, there were many merchants who ventured on the road, passing through the wilderness and dangerous places, with many robbers and no way to escape. The wealth and treasures they carried had no provisions, and the companions they traveled with had no weapons to defend themselves. The travelers were few and the wealth was extremely abundant, causing them to be fearful and agitated. A wise one told his companions, "Do not be afraid. I will devise a plan to escape this danger." The group then became focused and were able to avoid harm. Therefore it is said, "Merchants on the road are afraid, with few companions and much merchandise." Passing through dangerous places, but with the worry of broken axles. On dangerous roads, not encountering good companions, abandoning the main road to follow narrow paths, not reaching the destination but encountering a broken cart, the previous companions not looking back, the later companions abandoning each other. Therefore the World-Honored One used this as an analogy, wishing to make later generations deeply understand the karmic retribution of sin and merit. Those who receive the teaching have no obstruction even in the slightest, and those who expound the teaching do not abandon their efforts. Therefore it is said, "Passing through dangerous places, but with the worry of broken axles."

商人在路惧，伴少而货多者，昔有众贾商人冒涉途路，经过旷野崄难之中，路多盗贼无由自免，所赍财宝无有资粮，同伴行人无有器仗用自防备，行人既少财宝极多，心怀恐惧神识炽然。有一黠者告其同伴：「勿生恐惧，吾当设计得免此难。」众人意正便得无他。是故说曰，商人在路惧，伴少而货多也。经过崄难处，然有折轴忧者，道路崄难不遇良伴，舍其大道随其细径，不达所至中遇车坏，前伴不顾后伴共相捐弃。是以世尊借此为喻，欲使后生深识罪福，受化者无毫厘之碍，演教者不捐其功。是故说曰，经过崄难处，然有折轴忧。

Having a body without wounds or sores, one is not harmed by poison
Poison cannot do anything to a wound. Without evil, there is nothing to create.

有身无疮痏，　　不为毒所害，毒无奈疮何，　　无恶无所造。

Just like Devadatta in Rājagṛha, who had the intention to harm. Later, the matter was revealed and the whole country heard about it. At that time, King Ajātaśatru said to Devadatta, "You should leave the country and not stay here. The sixteen great countries all know about it. How can there be Devadatta here, creating various evils and having the intention to harm the Tathāgata?" Upon hearing this, Devadatta was inwardly worried and his mind was not at ease. He then returned to his own country. The enmity from the past was not exhausted, and he was entangled by anger and resentment. He rashly entered the palace and said to Yaśodharā, "I now take you as my first wife. I wonder if the noble lady would agree?" Yaśodharā heard this and said to Devadatta, "I want to hold your right hand." Devadatta then extended his hand for her to hold. He grasped the wrist and the bones were crushed, with blood flowing from the five fingers. He immediately fainted and regained consciousness after a long while. Yaśodhara said, "Except for the power of Siddhārtha, no one can surpass me. If I were to hold you, your body would be crushed and scattered like dust, just like a strong man crushing a thousand trees with his fingers. What difficulty would there be?" At that time, Devadatta entered the palace and sat on the Bodhisattva's bed. The palace ladies all disliked him and came forward to grab him, throwing him under the bed. He injured his left hip and was unable to walk. His family carried him back to his own house. All the Śākyas disliked him and came to tell him, "You should now repent to the Buddha." Devadatta secretly devised a scheme and secretly made iron claws and smeared poison on them, outwardly appearing gentle but inwardly harboring anger. At that time, Devadatta remembered what the Buddha said: "The Śramaṇa Gautama always says this: 'Having a body without wounds or sores, one is not harmed by poison. Poison cannot do anything to a wound. Without evil, there is nothing to create.' I will now go and pretend to repent, and scratch his feet with my claws. The poison will spread and he will die on his own." The people carried him to the World-Honored One, and three or seven paces away from the World-Honored One, he said to those around him, "Put me down on the ground. I want to walk." He then got down on the ground. At that moment, brave fire boiled up from the ground, wrapped around his body and dragged him into hell. Therefore it is said, 'Having a body without wounds or sores, one is not harmed by poison. Poison cannot do anything to a wound.'

犹如调达在罗阅城，兴谋害心，后事彰露举国闻知，时王阿阇世语调达曰：「汝宜出国，不须住此，十六大国莫不闻知。云何此有调达造作众恶，起伤害心向于如来？」调达闻已内怀忧戚，心不自宁便还本国，宿怨不尽为恚结所缠，唐突菩萨宫内，语瞿夷曰：「我今取汝拜为第一夫人，不审圣女为可尔不？」瞿夷闻之语调达曰：「前汝右手吾欲把之。」调达寻舒手使把，扼腕骨碎五指血出，当时迷闷良久乃稣。瞿夷语曰：「除悉达力，更无有人出我上者，设当与汝相把持者，身体碎烂剧于尘雾，犹如力人指坏千树，随意碎之有何难乎？」是时调达，转进入宫殿坐菩萨床，宫人见之悉共嫌恨，即前竞捉掷于床下，即伤左𣎑不堪行来，家人辇舆还归本舍。诸释皆嫌皆来告语：「汝今调达宜可改更向佛忏悔。」调达闻之私设巧诈，密作铁爪害毒涂之，外形柔和内怀瞋恚。尔时调达忆佛所说：「瞿昙沙门恒陈此言：『有身无疮痏，不为毒所害，毒无奈疮何，无恶无所造。』我今当往佯如忏悔，以爪掴坏其脚，毒气流溢自当取死。」诸人辇舆往诣世尊，去世尊三七仞，语左右人：「下我在地，吾欲步往。」寻下在地。寻时地中勇火沸出，缠裹其身将入地狱。是故说曰，有身无疮痏，不为毒所害，毒无奈疮何也。

There are many who commit evil deeds, which will surely become a burden for
their bodies. To practice giving and spread kindness and virtue is indeed very difficult.

多有行众恶，　　必为身作累，施善布恩德，　　此事甚为难。

There are many who commit evil deeds, which will surely become a burden for their bodies. There are many people in the world who spread evil and harm themselves, not in accordance with the truth. They slaughter, fish, hunt, raise pigs and chickens, set up traps to catch deer, become thieves to kill thieves, and are bound to prison guards. The kind of evil deeds committed by such beings are innumerable. Such actions will surely become a burden for their bodies. When they die, they will enter hell and suffer immeasurable pain. Therefore it is said, "There are many who commit evil deeds, which will surely become a burden for their bodies." To practice giving and spread kindness and virtue is indeed very difficult. People can reflect on the good and evil karmic consequences of past and future lives, and widely give to the needy, even at the expense of their own flesh. This is indeed very difficult. Therefore it is said, "To practice giving and spread kindness and virtue is indeed very difficult."

多有行众恶，必为身作累者，世多有人布恶自侵不合圣谛，屠割鱼猎养猪畜鸡，张施悬弶以捕群鹿，为贼杀贼缚就狱卒，真陀罗种弦索飞纶，如是恶行众生不可称说，如斯之类必为身作患，死入地狱受痛难量，是故说曰，多有行众恶，必为身作累也。施善布恩德，此事甚为难者，人能自察前世后世善恶报应，广施周穷，侵肌之货] cf.【丽】【碛砂】，以施于人，此事甚难。是故说曰，施善布恩德，此事甚为难也。

It is good to cultivate goodness, and it is good to do great evil. Evil is
easy to do for oneself, but it is difficult for an evil person to do good.

善哉修善者，　　善哉为甚恶，恶恶自为易，　　恶人为善难。

It is good to cultivate goodness. Good people naturally practice good deeds. Those who do evil should not be associated with. Good people are protected by the Buddhas and respected by the gods and people of the world. Wherever they go, they will never be apart from good friends. Therefore it is said, "It is good to cultivate goodness." It is good to do great evil. People's evil increases without diminishing, like creeping grass that grows without being planted. Even if the ground is cleared and purified, it still grows unceasingly. Therefore it is said, "It is good to do great evil." Evil is easy to do for oneself. It is difficult for an evil person to do good. It is like the kind of people who always carry dead bodies to be discarded in the charnel ground. Their minds are always delighted and fearless. Their minds are doubly joyful and they amuse themselves. It is like the jailers who guard the shackles. They do evil day and night and consider themselves honorable. The wise and holy ones view these changes as great suffering. Those who are to be executed are taken to the city. With every step they take, they are closer to the place of death. How can one crave and yearn for the bitterness of the three realms? Therefore it is said, "Evil is easy to do for oneself. It is difficult for an evil person to do good."

善哉修善者，善人修善行应自然，为恶之徒不可亲近，为善之人诸佛卫护，诸天世人所可爱敬，所至之方终不离善知识，是故说曰，善哉修善者。善哉为甚恶者，人之为恶日增无损，犹如蔓草不种自滋，正使铲其地净故处，犹生不息，是故说曰，善哉为甚恶也。恶恶自为易，恶人为善难者，犹如真陀罗种，恒担死人捐弃冢间，心恒喜欢无所畏忌，心倍欢喜以自娱乐；犹若典狱之人守护杻械，昼夜行恶自谓为尊。贤圣之人观此众变以为大患，应死之人将诣都市，举足下足以近死地，三界酸楚何可贪慕？是故说曰，恶恶自为易，恶人为善难也。

The foolish call themselves upright, but their evil is not yet
ripe. When evil ripens and is fulfilled, all suffering also ripens.

愚者自谓正，　　犹恶未成熟，恶以成熟满，　　诸苦亦复熟。

The foolish call themselves upright, but their evil is not yet ripe. The foolish think that their actions are upright, while others' actions are all unlawful. They see those who do good and hate them together. When the roots of sin are complete and the foolish mind is ripe, they then know that what they have done is wrong. Now the evil they have created is not done by their parents, nor is it done by their brothers and relatives. They bear the consequences of their own sins and regret is of no avail. It is not done by gods, ghosts, śramaṇas, or brāhmaṇas. They now know for themselves the fundamental cause of their sins. They do not blame heaven above or the earth below. They willingly accept the punishment and know what to do. Therefore it is said, "The foolish call themselves upright, but their evil is not yet ripe." When evil ripens and is fulfilled, all suffering also ripens. Those who accumulate sins enter hell to receive retribution. Thirteen kinds of flames wrap around their bodies. They die and revive, seeking death but not being able to. They must repay their past sins until they are exhausted, then they may leave. If they are born as animals, they are obscured by foolishness and do not recognize the true path. They lead with swollen backs, broken noses, and heads shackled with hands and feet in chains. If they are born as hungry ghosts, they are hungry and thirsty day and night, with bellies as big as Mount Tai and throats as fine as needles. Their bodies are forty li long, with a thousand pores per inch. If they are born as humans, they are poor, destitute, with clothes that do not cover their bodies and food that does not fill their mouths. Therefore it is said, "When evil ripens and is fulfilled, all suffering also ripens."

愚者自谓正，犹恶未成熟者，愚人自念所行专正，余者所作皆为非法，见行善者共憎嫉之，罪根以具痴心纯熟，然后乃知我所作非。今我造恶非父母为，亦非兄弟宗亲所造，分受其罪悔无所及，非天非鬼非沙门梵志之所造，我今自知罪之根本，上不怨天下不尤地，甘心受罪知复奈何。是故说曰，愚者自谓正，犹恶未成熟也。恶以成熟满，诸苦亦复受者，积罪之人入狱受报，十三种焰缠裹其身，死而复稣求死不得，要偿故罪以尽无余，然后乃出；若在畜生，愚痴所蔽不识真道，领肿脊坏穿鼻羁头枷锁手脚；若生饿鬼，昼夜饥渴腹若泰山咽细若针，身长四十里，一寸千隔；若在人中，贫贱困悴衣不盖形食不充口。是故说曰，恶以成熟满，诸苦亦复熟也。

The wise see evil and are not ripened by evil, just
as evil is not ripened. The wise observe their own evil.

贤者见于恶，　　不为恶所熟，如恶以不熟，　　恶者观其恶。

The wise see evil and are not ripened by evil, just as evil is not ripened. The wise observe their own evil.

贤者见于恶，不为恶所熟者，彼执行人见其行恶随时诃谏，此非妙行轮转生死求出甚难，于三恶道造罪根本，是故说曰，贤者见于恶，不为恶所熟也。如恶以不熟，恶者观其恶者，如人作恶后寻怀悔：「咄我所作，将非其宜，人之所嫉，我今习之，将非是我执意误乎？自今改悔，观恶秽污。」是故说曰，如恶以不熟，恶者观其恶也。

The wise observe their own evil, even the wise are not
ripened. If the wise are ripened, the wise observe each other.

贤者观其恶，　　乃至贤不熟，设以贤熟者，　　贤贤自相观。

If the wise are ripened, the wise observe each other.

贤者观其恶，乃至贤不熟者，贤人守戒众德具足，多闻辩慧言无缺漏，出言柔和常行真诚，行四等心慈愍一切，见小过隙便怀恐惧，况当造无择之罪？是故说曰，贤者观其恶，乃至贤不熟也。设以贤熟者，贤贤自相观者，贤者自察自观性行：「我今所致供养者，皆由前身积学所致，宿不种福布恩施德，今日何缘得此福报？今不谨慎重行其德者，后更受形无福可凭，复当流浪经历生死，方便积行久乃成就，其间艰难非度所知非算所筹，过佛恒沙不覩不闻，由行自坠至今不度。」是故说曰，设以贤熟者，贤贤自相观也。

Even though a person commits evil deeds, they do not do them
repeatedly. They are not pleased with them, knowing the suffering of evil.

人虽为恶行，　　亦不数数作，于彼意不乐，　　知恶之为苦。

Even though a person commits evil deeds, they do not do them repeatedly. They are not pleased with them, knowing the suffering of evil.

人虽为恶行，亦不数数作者，人为恶行当自改更，备受三涂八难之苦，于中求出亦甚难得。是故智者，制以禁法防以未然，设受其报犹轻，若在地狱汤冷水微受苦无几，斯由悔过知罪根本；若作畜生负担不重，食以随时不加苦痛；若为饿鬼，鬼有四种，生作豪尊饿鬼，衣食自然；若处人间，豪富大族无所渴乏；若生于天，微福之报，食以覆口自耻福少。是故说曰，人虽为恶行，亦不数数作也。于彼意不乐，知恶之为苦者，学人见恶意不愿乐，自摄其意不使分散，罪虽微细报如泰山，猛火虽小焚烧山野，是以智者常当防虑，知恶根源众苦之首。是故说曰，于彼意不乐，知恶之为苦也。

If a person can create merit, they should also do it
repeatedly. They are pleased with it, receiving the good rewards of merit.

人能作其福，　　亦当数数造，于彼意愿乐，　　善受其福报。

If a person can create merit, they should also do it repeatedly. They are pleased with it, receiving the good rewards of merit.

人能作其福，亦当数数造者，人生一世所以致贫穷者，皆由前身悭结所误。是以圣人触类所说，先以施惠为首，虽复贫穷，要当少多减损以补曩愆，虽无财货当自役己出力作使，修补神祠佐助众事，不使日夜阙其福业，弹指之顷念善亦是，况复躬自行功德乎？是故说曰，人能作其福，亦当数数造也。于彼意愿乐，善受其福报者，人之修福多所润及，见行善者代其欢喜，辄自出财劝助为福，见身获祐善名流布，见者心欢靡不致敬，生辄遇圣不堕八无闲处，是故说曰，于彼意愿乐，善受其福报。

First, one should control the good mind and hold the root of evil
Because of this, one creates meritorious deeds. The mind is happy with evil.

先当制善心，　　摄持恶根本，由是兴福业，　　心由乐于恶。

First, one should control the good mind and hold the root of evil. Because of this, one creates meritorious deeds. The mind is happy with evil.

先当制善心，摄持恶根本者，善心具足勿令分散，执意在前如擎油钵，战战兢兢] cf.【丽】如避劫烧，当以无常苦空非身，除心秽垢沐浴使净，是故说曰，当先制善心，摄持恶根本也。由是兴福业，心由乐于恶者，人不行善作后世资粮者，命终烧身之患，日夜为恶不能自改，是故说曰，由是兴福业，心由乐于恶也。

Although one's evil may be small, the suffering in the next life is
deep. One will receive boundless retribution, like poison in the heart and abdomen.

为恶虽复少，　　后世受苦深，当获无边报，　　如毒在心腹。

Although one's evil may be small, the suffering in the next life is deep. Because of this, one creates meritorious deeds. The mind is happy with evil.

为恶虽复少，后世受苦深者，人意不固所行无记，少多为罪或觉不觉，要当受报不免其对，无惭无愧不求出要求度世道，是故说曰，为恶虽复少，后世受苦深也。当获无边报，如毒在心腹者，少多有隙尘垢染意，便当受于无边之罪，或触娆人使兴恶行，由是自致堕无边罪，或离别眷属鬪乱家室，如此之苦众恼无数。是故说曰，当获无边报，如毒在心腹也。

Although one's merit may be small, the later great merit received is like planting and reaping fruit.

为福虽少，　　后受大福，当获大报，如种获实。

Although one's merit may be small, the later great merit received is like planting and reaping fruit.

为福虽少，后受大福者，人之为福唯存在心，不在财物有多有少，设施物多内心悋惜，后获其福亦不足言；施物虽少心意普等，广及一切不自为己，后获其福不可称限。是故说曰，为福虽少，后受大福也。当获大报，如种获实者，后受天人自然之福，颜色从容，恒处中国不在边境，言从语用不伤人意，饶财多宝不怀憎嫉，在家修德宗族和穆，设当出家捐弃恩爱，剃除须发著三法衣，苦形学道除荣冀心，越次取证尽其有漏成无漏行，众德普备功福具满，犹如田夫多种获报，仓库盈满意志欢喜，内自庆贺功不唐举。是故说曰，当获大报，如种获实也。

Those who unjustly oppress others, who unjustly harm others
will go to the ten kinds of evil realms.

无过而强轻，　　无恚而强侵，当于十品处，　　便当趣于彼。

Those who unjustly oppress others, who unjustly harm others, will go to the ten kinds of evil realms.

无过而强轻，无恚而强侵者，如彼有人无有恚嫉憍慢之心，然愚𫘤之人，兴意向彼起谋害心，诸佛世尊慈愍一切，见有哀苦拔济其难，兴念生类如母爱子。是故说曰，无过而强轻，无恚而强侵也。当于十品处，便当趣于彼者，所谓十品者，一名无救，二名焰，三名大焰，四名黑绳，五名啼哭，六名大啼哭，七名等害，八名等命，九者畜生，十者饿鬼。其有众生恶心炽盛，寿终之后不离此十处。是故说曰，当于十品处，便当趣于彼也。

Painful feelings, harsh speech, this body will surely decay
tormented by various diseases, the mind is disturbed and unsettled,

痛痒语麤犷，　　此形必坏败，众病所酷切，　　心乱而不定，

Family and relatives are separated, wealth and goods are
exhausted, robbed by the king, wishes are not fulfilled,

宗族别离散，　　财货费耗尽，王者所劫略，　　所愿不从意，

Or else, countless transformations, burned by fire, with a destroyed
body and no wisdom, also proceed to the ten stages.

或复无数变，　　为火所焚烧，身坏无智慧，　　亦趣于十品。

All these verses above were spoken by the Tathāgata's divine mouth. Devadatta foolishly instructed Ajātaśatru to drink wine and let a drunken elephant charge towards the Tathāgata. At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke this verse towards the elephant.

此上诸偈，尽是如来神口所说，调达愚教阿阇世酒饮暴象醉向如来。是时，世尊寻向彼象而说斯偈。

Do not say that evil is nonexistent, for doing it for a long time is
said to be without fault. Saying it is without fault in private is also all verified.

作恶勿言无，　　久作言无罪，屏猥言无罪，　　斯皆有证验。

When a person commits evil deeds, there are degrees of seriousness. If the intention is strong and not abandoned, one is unable to let go and leave, and does not seek the means of escape. Concealing and hiding oneself, one is also unable to confess to others. Therefore, the World-Honored One teaches later generations that new and old deeds, down to private places, good and evil karmic retribution cannot be concealed. Therefore, it is said, "Do not say that evil is nonexistent, for doing it for a long time is said to be without fault." "Saying it is without fault in private is also all verified" means that when a person intends to commit various roots of evil in private, although at that time one may avoid being slandered by the masses, one still cannot avoid the retribution of the next life. Therefore, it is said, "Saying it is without fault in private is also all verified"

夫人作恶事有轻重，意盛不舍不能去离不求出要，藏隐自匿，亦复不能向人陈说；是以世尊教诲后人，新作旧造下至屏猥之处，善恶冥报不可藏匿。是故说曰，作恶勿言无，久作言无罪也。屏猥言无罪，斯皆有证验者，人欲设意在屏猥处造诸罪根，当时虽可免萌类谤，然复不免后世报对。是故说曰，屏猥言无罪，斯皆有证验也。

Saying that evil is with worry, for doing it for a long time is also said to be with
worry. Saying it is with worry in private is also all with worry. The retribution is also with worry.

作恶言有忧，　　久作亦言忧，屏猥亦言忧，　　彼报亦有忧。

When a person commits evil, the initial intention is fierce and one is not aware of it. At that time, the mind is brave and considers it to be appropriate. At that time, the World-Honored One then used a metaphor, like when the sun first sets, the shadows of mountains, rivers, and trees all cast shadows and eventually become dark. Now, this group of deluded people who are attached to delusion are also like this. They commit unwholesome deeds of body and speech. At the time of death, the heavy shadows of various evils each follow and gradually lead to the dark room to receive retribution. Therefore, it is said, "Saying that evil is with worry, for doing it for a long time is also said to be with worry. Saying it is with worry in private is also all with worry. The retribution is also with worry"

人之造恶初意赫炽不自觉知，当时心勇谓为应尔。尔时世尊便引其喻，如日初没之际，山川树影皆各垂阴遂至于冥，今此群惑之徒执迷亦尔，造身口行不善之本，临终之日诸恶重阴，各各自随渐渐将至冥室受报。是故说曰，作恶言有忧，久作亦言忧，屏猥亦言忧，彼报亦有忧也。

This worry is also worried by others, evil conduct is worried by both, that
worry is worried by the retribution, seeing the conduct one knows it is true.

此忧彼亦忧，　　恶行二俱忧，彼忧彼受报，　　见行乃知审。

The so-called this worry is the worry of the present life, the so-called that worry is the worry of the next life. The so-called this worry is not dying and not passing away, the so-called that worry is already dead and already passed away. Therefore, it is said, "This worry is also worried by others." Evil conduct is worried by both. That worry is worried by the retribution. Seeing the conduct one knows it is true.

所谓此忧者今现世忧，所谓彼忧者后世之忧，所谓此忧，不死不命终，所谓彼忧者已死已命终，是故说曰，此忧彼亦忧也。恶行二俱忧者，彼忧彼受报，见行乃知审。

This joy is also joyful by others, meritorious conduct is joyful by
both, that joy is joyful by the retribution, seeing the conduct is self-purified.

此喜彼亦喜，　　福行二俱喜，彼喜彼受报，　　见行自清净。

Formerly King Prasenajit raised troops to attack the country of Kapilavastu, defeated the people and captured seven thousand. Those who had seen the path of the sages were all buried up to their feet and trampled to death by wild elephants. In brief, the meaning is this. The Buddha told the monks: "The king of Kosala is now ungrateful, going against the sages and betraying the truth, committing the sin of indiscriminate killing. These kinds of people will receive retribution in seven days. The royal lineage of the country of Kosala will be cut off, with no heir. The flames of the indiscriminate hell will emerge and envelop the king's body and his attendants, all entering the indiscriminate hell." When Prasenajit heard this, he immediately prepared his four kinds of troops, left the city with his palace ladies and concubines to avoid the disaster, and went to the Ganges River to set sail and ride a boat, thinking he would escape the danger. At that time, the flames of the Avīci hell came to reach him and his followers, and all of them entered the hell without escape. Before Prasenajit had avoided the disaster, he came to the city of Śrāvastī and heard the sound of singing, dancing, and laughter from afar. The king asked his attendants: "Whose is this sound of singing and laughter that reaches here?" The ministers said: "This is the sound of music from Prince Jeta's house." The king then sent a message to summon him quickly. "I am now fighting with the enemy, worrying about the affairs of the country, but Jeta is now enjoying himself with the five kinds of pleasures. If I lose the battle, he will surely hope to obtain the royal position." When Prince Jeta heard the king's summons, he came out to greet him. The king told the prince: "I am fighting with the enemy, worried about the ten thousand countries, but you are now enjoying yourself with the five kinds of pleasures." He then drew his sharp sword and beheaded him, leaving him there. Jeta's body was reborn in heaven. The palace ladies and concubines enjoyed themselves with the five kinds of pleasures, not realizing they had lost their master. The heavenly maidens surrounded him in front and behind, also singing and playing music to entertain themselves. At that time, the World-Honored One, with his heavenly eye, saw Jeta Prince receiving blessings in two places. He spoke this verse in the great assembly:

昔琉璃王兴兵，攻伐迦维罗竭国，摧破人民擒获七千，圣人见道迹者悉埋其足，使暴象蹋蹈杀之。略说其义。佛告比丘：「拘萨罗王现无反复，违圣叛真，兴无择罪，斯等之类却后七日自当受报。拘萨罗国，王种当绝，无复继嗣，无择地狱火焰当出，缠裹王身及诸侍从，悉入无择地狱之中。」琉璃闻之即日严驾四种之兵，宫人婇女出城避灾，寻诣恒水张帆乘船谓为免难。时，阿鼻地狱火焰来接及诸群众，翼从多少悉入地狱无得脱者。琉璃王先未避灾之时，来至舍卫城内，遥闻作倡伎乐歌舞戏笑五乐自娱，王问左右：「斯是谁家戏笑之声乃彻于此？」诸臣白曰：「此是祇头太子家中音乐之声。」王寻遣信速唤使来。「我今征伐与贼战鬪忧虑国事，祇头今日方更欢乐，以五乐自娱，设我战鬪不如贼者，此人必望得王尊位。」祇头太子闻王召唤，寻出奉迎。王告太子：「吾与贼战心忧万国，汝今方更五乐自娱。」即拔利剑斩而舍去。祇头舍身即生天上，内宫妓女五乐自娱不觉失主，天上婇女前后围遶，亦复作倡伎乐共相娱乐。尔时世尊以天眼，观见祇头王子二处受福，在大众中而说斯偈：

This joy is also joyful by others, meritorious conduct is joyful by
both, that joy is joyful by the retribution, seeing the conduct is self-purified.

「此喜彼亦喜，　　福行二俱喜，彼喜彼受报，　　见行自清净。」

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke this verse to King Prasenajit:

尔时世尊，复与琉璃王而说斯偈：

This cooking is also cooked by others, sinful conduct is cooked by both
that cooking is cooked by the retribution, seeing the conduct has its own evidence.

「此煮彼亦煮，　　罪行二俱煮，彼煮彼受罪，　　见行自有验。」

At that time, the World-Honored One used his divine eye to see King Prasenajit in hell, being tortured and whipped with the five kinds of severe pain. Therefore, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时世尊，以天眼观见琉璃王处在地狱，拷掠榜笞五毒酸楚，是故世尊而说斯偈：

Doing merit and not doing evil, all due to the Dharma of past actions, will
never fear the path of death, like a boat cutting through the current to cross.

「作福不作恶，　　皆由宿行法，终不畏死径，　　如船截流渡。」

In the past, before the Buddha attained perfect enlightenment, when he was a bodhisattva, he was called Sarva-dā. For the sake of a brahmin, he bound himself and went to the palace. The king of the enemy country said, "Are you afraid of me now?" At that time, Sarva-dā spoke this verse:

昔佛先世未成等正觉时，为菩萨身，号曰一切施，为婆罗门故，自缚诣阙。敌国王曰：「汝今畏吾？」尔时一切施而说斯偈：

Doing merit and not doing evil, all due to the Dharma of past actions, will
never fear the path of death, like a boat cutting through the current to cross.

「作福不作恶，　　皆由宿行法，终不畏死径，　　如船截流渡。」

"Doing merit and not doing evil, all due to the Dharma of past actions, will never fear the path of death, like a boat cutting through the current to cross."

昔有噉人鬼，在人中作王，恒食人肉以为厨宰，隣国征伐得九十九王，二十一人以为常则，九十九王白罗刹王曰：「隣国有王名曰善宿，好行施惠修菩萨德，有所求索不逆人意，大王设能擒获彼者，我等甘心受死万无一恨。」尔时罗刹人王即起鬼兵往伺其便，正值善宿大王在外园观浴池游戏。有一梵志，辞家外学，夫梵志之法，临辞去时白父母言：「我今离家追伴学问，计还之日且未有期，设财货穷乏从王举贷我还当偿。」其人学问以得成就，来至家中，但见空屋不见人众，即问隣比：「我今父母兄弟姊妹竟为所在？」隣比报曰：「汝学之后举王财贿无以当偿，为王所系今在牢狱，欲往看者宜知是时。」其人自念：「家穷事狭无有财宝，设我诣狱亲觐父母，复当拘执同受其苦不免王法，宜令在外改形易服窃行求索，毕偿官物乃得出身耳。」其人复念：「隣国有王号善宿，修行道德施心不绝，当往至彼至诚告情，必不见违，足偿王物。」寻往至彼随王乞索。王言：「大佳！当相供给，须吾沐浴讫当惠施，小停勿忧不负言信。」王诣浴池为鬼兵所擒，王寻还顾悲戚涕零，鬼王问曰：「我等闻王仁和博爱靡不周济，虽遭厄困何为悲戚？」王报鬼曰：「我生惠施未曾有悔，向有梵志在外乞索，许而未与，是以忧戚耳。」鬼王白王：「王守诚信由来不改，如今放王施讫时还。」乃知王心不失诚信，王得还宫开藏惠施恣彼人意，寻还就信诣鬼王所。鬼王告曰：「汝不畏吾乎？何为受死而来？」尔时善宿大王向彼鬼王，而说斯偈：

Doing merit and not doing evil, all due to the Dharma of past actions, will
never fear the path of death, like a boat cutting through the current to cross.

「作福不作恶，　　皆由宿行法，终不畏死径，　　如船截流渡。」

"Doing merit and not doing evil, all due to the Dharma of past actions, will never fear the path of death, like a boat cutting through the current to cross."

鬼王闻之内怀惭愧，改心易行思修善本，即告善宿王曰：「今闻所说人中难有，今放九十九王。我舍此位，愿王统领以法治化，我领鬼众还归本居住，若俱健者自当数觐。」即共离别各还所在。万民称庆国界清泰，共行十善不修恶业，善宿积行不息，后得成佛于树王下，复说斯偈：

Doing merit and not doing evil, all due to the Dharma of past actions, will
never fear the path of death, like a boat cutting through the current to cross.

「作福不作恶，　　皆由宿行法，终不畏死径，　　如船截流渡。」
