T 212.31

Chuyao jing / Udānavarga (T 212) 出曜经

T 212.31 ◎乐品第三十一

Parallel to: SN 3.14 / SN 4.18 / SN 10.8 / AN 3.35 / Dhp 76–89 / Dhp 129–145 / Dhp 167–178 / Dhp 179–196 / Dhp 197–208 / Dhp 290–305 / Dhp 320–333 / Ud 2.1 / Ud 2.2 / Ud 2.3 / Ud 2.4 / Ud 2.5 / Ud 2.9 / Ud 2.10 / Ud 8.10 / Snp 1.1 / T 210.18 / T 210.22 / T 210.31 / T 210.38 / T 213.30

If you win, you destroy your enemies; if you lose, you despise yourself. If you stop, you are happy. There is no mind of victory or defeat.

胜则怨灭，　　负则自鄙，息则快乐，无胜负心。

If you win, you destroy your enemies; if you lose, you despise yourself. If you stop, you are happy. There is no mind of victory or defeat.

胜则怨灭，负则自鄙者，如彼怨家昼夜伺察彼人，于彼有大怨嫌，从世至世不舍罪怨，如是经历数百千身，报怨乃息负者自鄙，是故说曰，胜则怨灭，负者自鄙也。息则快乐，无胜负心，一切结使永尽无余，更不复起想著之念，亦复无胜负之心，我胜彼不如、彼胜我不如，都无彼此之心，是故说曰，息则快乐，无胜负心也。

If one disturbs others, seeking peace and happiness for
oneself, one will only create enmity and never escape suffering.

若人娆乱彼，　　自求安乐世，遂成其怨憎，　　终不脱苦患。

If one disturbs others, seeking peace and happiness for oneself, one will only create enmity and never escape suffering.

若人娆乱彼，自求安乐世者，世多有人执迷惑意，怨雠心深触娆于，人自望快乐宗族蒙庆，如种苦栽冀望甘菓，唐丧功夫无益于时。是故说曰，若人娆乱彼，自求安乐世也。遂成其怨憎，终不脱苦患者，卒鬪杀人犹尚可恕，怀毒阴谋乃不可亲，如斯之类必趣恶道，所以然者，由其执愚不舍故也。是故说曰，遂成其怨憎，终不脱苦患也。

Those who delight in sensual pleasures and use weapons to harm beings
seeking peace and happiness for themselves, will not find happiness in future lives.

善乐于爱欲，　　以杖加群生，于中自求安，　　后世不得乐。

Those who delight in sensual pleasures and use weapons to harm beings, seeking peace and happiness for themselves, will not find happiness in future lives.

善乐于爱欲者，一切众生皆贪乐乐不乐苦恼，见苦则群心不愿乐，己自行杀教人杀生，己自婬泆教人婬泆，己自妄言绮语复教人妄言绮语，己自不与取复教他人窃盗他物，是故说曰，善乐于爱欲也。以杖加群生者，所行非法滥抂百姓，意之所存以伤为本，是故说曰，以杖加群生也。于中自求安，后世不得乐，人作恶行皆自为己，舍身受形遭诸苦恼，经历生死沈漂五道，所生之处罪苦自随，是故说曰，于中自求安，后世不得乐也。

Those who wish for happiness and do not harm beings with
weapons, seeking happiness for themselves, will also find happiness in future lives.

人欲得欢乐，　　杖不加群生，于中自求乐，　　后世亦得乐。

Those who wish for happiness and do not harm beings with weapons, seeking happiness for themselves, will also find happiness in future lives.

人欲得欢乐，杖不加群生者，一切众生皆贪于乐不乐于苦，见彼苦者兴慈愍心，四等平均视彼如赤子，初不起怨捶打众生，处世皆求安身。「设我今日触娆彼者，后世之中受对无数。」是故说曰，人欲得欢乐，杖不加群生，于中自求乐，后世亦得乐也。

Those who delight in the Dharma and delight in practicing it, carefully avoiding unwholesome dharmas, are
able to practice the Dharma well. They will find happiness in this world and in future lives.

乐法乐学行，　　慎莫行恶法，能善行法者，　　今世后世乐。

When people are in the world, they should practice the Dharma, selecting good dharmas and discarding evil ones. They should associate with good friends and take up good teachings. Wherever they go, they should promote Dharma activities. Therefore, it is said: Those who delight in the Dharma and delight in practicing it, carefully avoiding unwholesome dharmas, are able to practice the Dharma well. They will find happiness in this world and in future lives.

夫人在世务行于法，选择善法去其恶者，周旋往来追善知识采取善教，所至到处兴有法事，是故说曰，乐法乐学行，慎莫行恶法，能善行法者，今世后世乐也。

Those who protect the Dharma and practice the Dharma, practicing the Dharma and receiving good
rewards, should follow the teachings of the Dharma. Practicing the Dharma does not lead to evil.

护法行法者，　　行法获善报，此应法律教，　　行法不趣恶。

Those who protect the Dharma and practice the Dharma, practicing the Dharma and receiving good rewards, are able to protect the Dharma themselves and prevent it from being lost. They will later receive its blessings. Therefore, it is said: Those who protect the Dharma and practice the Dharma, practicing the Dharma and receiving good rewards. Those who practice the Dharma protect themselves with the Dharma. Wherever they are born, they will not encounter evil calamities. From childhood to adulthood, they will receive their due. When their blessings in heaven are exhausted, they will be reborn as humans and again receive blessings. Therefore, it is said: This should follow the teachings of the Dharma. Practicing the Dharma does not lead to evil.

护法行法者，行法获善报者，能自拥护法不使漏失，后获其福，是故说曰，护法行法者，行法获善报也。此应法律教，行法不趣恶者，彼执行人以法自护，所生之中不遇恶灾，从小至大悉受其对，天受福尽下生人间复重受福，是故说曰，此应法律教，行法不趣恶也。

Those who protect the Dharma and practice the Dharma, practicing the Dharma and receiving good rewards, are like a
canopy covering their form. This should follow the teachings of the Dharma. Practicing the Dharma does not lead to evil.

护法行法者，　　如盖覆其形，此应法律教，　　行法不趣恶。

Those who practice the Dharma protect themselves with the Dharma. Wherever they are born, they will not encounter evil calamities. From childhood to adulthood, they will receive their due. When their blessings in heaven are exhausted, they will be reborn as humans and again receive blessings. Therefore, it is said: This should follow the teachings of the Dharma. Practicing the Dharma does not lead to evil.

彼修行人拥护深法微妙之教，去诸阴盖，如猛赫热而获好盖得蒙济度，是故说曰，护法行法者，如盖覆其形，此应法律教，行法不趣恶也。

Those who practice evil enter hell and fall into evil paths
Unlawful acts lead to self-destruction, like grasping a snake with one's hand.

恶行入地狱，　　所至堕恶道，非法自陷溺，　　如手把蛇蚖。

Those who practice evil enter hell and fall into evil paths. This means that when one commits evil deeds, it is not done by one's parents, siblings, or relatives. It is all caused by oneself. When one commits a crime, one must bear the consequences oneself. No one can take one's place. The views of non-Buddhist schools are different. Non-Buddhists believe that one commits the crime but others receive the retribution. Therefore, it is said: Those who practice evil enter hell and fall into evil paths.

恶行入地狱，所至堕恶道者，人为恶行，非父母兄弟宗亲所为，皆由己身为罪所致，作罪自受其殃无能代者；外道异学所见不同，外道所见己身作罪他人受报。是故说曰，恶行入地狱，所至堕恶道也。非法自陷溺，如手把蛇蚖者，犹如彼人手把蛇蚖，或以呪术而取者，或以药草而取者，或被师教而手玩弄恶蛇，呪罢之后为蛇所啮，死入地狱饿鬼畜生，经历生死无有休已。是故说曰，非法自陷溺，如手把蛇蚖也。

Neither by the law nor by the unlawful, both are equally rewarded. By
the unlawful, one enters hell; by the right law, one is born in heaven.

不以法非法，　　二事俱同报，非法入地狱，　　正法生于天。

Those who practice good and evil do not know the retribution of blessings and misfortunes. Those who do good do not know the reward of good, and those who do evil do not know the retribution of evil. It is like a person who gets poisoned food. When they get it and enjoy it, they do not know that there is poison in the food. The poison spreads and harms their body. People who do evil are also like this. At the time, it is sweet in the mouth, but later they suffer the consequences and lose their lives, not going to a good place. Those with eyes observe the food and know it is pure and free from poison, so they take it and eat it without suffering.

不以法非法，二事俱同报，此众生类造善恶行，不自觉知殃福之报，为善者不知善之有报，为恶者不知恶之有报。如彼有人得杂毒之食，得而享之，不知食中有毒，毒气流炽不便其身。行恶之人亦复如是，当时甘口后受其殃，遂丧其命不至善处。有目之士观食知之，斯是清净其中无毒，便取食之后无苦患。是故说曰，不以法非法，二事俱同报，非法入地狱，正法生于天也。

Giving and fighting are the same, this virtue and wisdom are not
praised. When giving, it is also like fighting, these two things are equal.

施与战同处，　　此德智不誉，施时亦战时，　　此事二俱等。

In the past, in the city of Śrāvastī, there was an elder named Uttama, and another elder named Durjaya. These two were the most stingy and greedy in the country, with abundant wealth and treasures, seven kinds of precious things, elephants, horses, carriages, servants, slaves, and grain and fields beyond measure. Each of the two had seven gates, and they ordered the gatekeepers not to let beggars enter their gates. The courtyard was covered with iron cages to prevent flying birds from pecking at the grain. The walls of the houses were cast with iron fences to prevent rats from gnawing on utensils. At that time, the five great śrāvakas each went there in turn to teach and transform them, emerging from the ground to teach them the Dharma of giving. The two elders heard it but did not accept the teaching. Later, the Buddha himself went there, sitting and lying in the sky, emitting a great light. The Buddha spoke the subtle Dharma to the elders, but although they heard it, their minds were still not enlightened. They thought to themselves, "The Buddha has come to our house. It would not be right to let him return to the monastery without anything. I should enter the treasury and take a white cloth to offer to the Tathāgata." They then got up and entered the treasury to select a bad one, but they got an even better one. They discarded it and took another, which was even better. Their minds were in conflict and they could not decide for themselves. On that day, the asuras fought with the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. Sometimes the gods won and the asuras lost, and sometimes the asuras won and the gods lost. At that time, the World-Honored One used his heavenly eye to see the minds of the elders, sometimes the stingy mind won and the giving mind lost, and sometimes the giving mind won and the stingy mind lost. At that time, the World-Honored One then spoke this verse:

昔舍卫城内有一长者，名曰最胜，更有长者名曰难降，二人悭贪国中第一，饶财多宝七珍具足，象马车乘仆从奴婢，谷食田业不可称计。二人门户各有七重，勅守门者，无令乞儿入我门户中庭之中，铁笼复上恐有飞鸟啄拾谷食，屋舍四壁铸铁垣墙，恐鼠穿凿啮坏器物也。是时，五大声闻各以次第诣彼教化，从地踊出教以法施，长者二人闻之各不受化。后佛自往，坐卧虚空放大光明，佛与长者说微妙法，长者虽闻心犹不达，内自思惟：「佛来至舍，不可虚尔使还精舍，宜入藏里取一白［叠＊毛］布施如来。」即起入藏选一恶者反更得好，舍而更取倍得好者，心意共诤不能自决。当于其日，阿须伦与忉利天共鬪，或天得胜、阿须伦不如，或阿须伦得胜、诸天不如。尔时世尊以天眼观见长者心，或时悭心得胜、施心不如，或时施心得胜、悭心不如。尔时世尊便说斯偈：

Giving and fighting are the same, this virtue and wisdom are not
praised. When giving, it is also like fighting, these two things are equal.

「施与战同处，　　此德智不誉，施时亦战时，　　此事二俱等。」

The elder heard this from afar and felt ashamed inside. "What the Tathāgata said is indeed about me." He then took out a good cloth to give as a gift. Elder Durjaya took out five hundred taels of gold to give as a gift. Their minds opened up and they each saw the traces of the path.

长者遥闻内怀惭愧：「如来所说正谓我身。」即出好［叠＊毛］持用为施，难降长者出五百两金持用惠施，心开意解各见道迹也。

People may undergo hundreds of thousands of changes, but they are all equal in eliminating the enemy
of arrogance. At that time, giving with a pure mind is the most excellent among the strong.

人遭百千变，　　等除憍慢怨，时施清净心，　　健夫最为胜。

"People may undergo hundreds of thousands of changes, but they are all equal in eliminating the enemy of arrogance" means that when a student is at home, they are attached to wealth and affairs, and their minds are not fixed due to the busyness of various matters. If one wishes to cultivate the Way, one should leave home and business, remove arrogance and not give rise to attachment, then one can give without expecting a reward. Being humble and respectful is the basis for cultivating virtue. Despising others and valuing oneself is a disaster. Therefore, the teaching is to be in a quiet place, and then one can cultivate the true Way. Therefore, it is said, "People may undergo hundreds of thousands of changes, but they are all equal in eliminating the enemy of arrogance." "Giving with a pure mind at the right time is the most excellent among the strong" means that giving at the right time brings five merits and removes the mind of arrogance and self-importance. The mind is always pure and free from defilement. Therefore, it is said, "Giving with a pure mind at the right time is the most excellent among the strong."

人遭百千变，等除憍慢怨者，学人在家恋著财业，众事愦乱心不一定，人欲修道当离家业，除去憍慢不兴想著，乃得惠施不望其报，谦恭卑下修德之本，轻人贵己殃祸之灾，是以教人闲静之处，然后乃得修于道真。是故说曰，人遭百千变，等除憍慢怨也。时施清净心，健夫最为胜者，施有五时获五功德，除去憍慢自大之心，意常清净不怀秽浊，是故说曰，时施清净心，健夫最为胜也。

Forbearance, though small, can overcome much; precepts, though few, can overcome much laziness
Those who have faith and give will receive good rewards in their next lives.

忍少得胜多，　　戒胜懈怠多，有信惠施者，　　后身受善报。

"Forbearance, though small, can overcome much; precepts, though few, can overcome much laziness" means that there are many sentient beings with extremely little faith and rampant anger. Those who uphold precepts and practice forbearance are also few. By practicing forbearance, one can overcome enemies. Those who uphold precepts are superior to the lazy. It is like Aniruddha, who made a single offering to a pratyekabuddha and did not fall into evil paths for ninety kalpas. Later, he was born in the Śākya clan, was the Buddha's paternal cousin, left home to study the Way, and attained the fruit of the Way. Therefore, it is said, "Forbearance, though small, can overcome much; precepts, though few, can overcome much laziness. Those who have faith and give will receive good rewards in their next lives"

忍少得胜多，戒胜懈怠多者，多有众生信心极少，瞋恚隆炽，持戒忍辱亦复少少耳，以能行忍则胜怨雠，持戒之人胜懈怠者，犹如阿那律一有施德与辟支佛，九十劫中未曾趣恶道，后生释种家，佛并父弟，出家学道成其道果。是故说曰，忍少得胜多，戒胜懈怠多，有信惠施者，后身受善报也。

How wonderful! Great blessings and rewards, all wishes fulfilled, quickly
attain the ultimate cessation, gradually entering the realm of non-action.

快哉大福报，　　所愿皆全成，速得第一灭，　　渐入无为际。

"How wonderful! Great blessings and rewards, all wishes fulfilled" means that the cultivation of blessings by people is due to the practice established in previous lives. Even though the seeds sown in good fields of merit are few, the rewards received are immeasurable. If one has previously harmed sages and had impure intentions in giving, without equality, even if one receives a human form, one's appearance will be ugly and one will be despised by others. Doing evil receives evil, doing blessings receives blessings. Therefore, it is said, "How wonderful! Great blessings and rewards, all wishes fulfilled." "Quickly attain the ultimate cessation, gradually entering the realm of non-action" means that when all afflictions are eliminated and all virtues are fully possessed, one is pure like light, clear inside and outside. If one wishes to seek the ultimate truth, one will immediately attain it. If one wishes to permanently enter the realm of emptiness, one will immediately attain it without any doubt or obstruction. Even if the heretical and evil demons come to destroy those who have merit, they will immediately be destroyed, with no way to do anything about it. It is like when the demon king led an army of 180 million with a hundred heads and one body, a terrifying appearance with tigers, wolves, lions, poisonous snakes, and venomous reptiles to frighten the Tathāgata. The Tathāgata's power of blessings caused the demons to be destroyed. The demon king retreated, and at that time the World Honored One spoke this verse:

快哉大福报，所愿皆全成者，人之修福皆由前身立行所致，值良福田种子虽少获报无量，若复前身触娆贤圣，施心不纯无平等意，设受人形形状丑陋为人所轻，作恶受恶作福受福，是故说曰，快哉大福报，所愿皆全成也。速得第一灭，渐入无为际者，众结除尽诸德普具，净如光明内外清彻，意欲所求第一义者寻时即获，欲得永入虚无之处，寻时即得无有疑滞；正使外邪弊魔之徒] cf.【丽】，欲来毁坏为福之人，寻时自坏，无奈之何。犹昔魔王将十八亿众，百头一身，形像可畏，虎狼师子毒蛇恶蚖来恐如来，如来福力使魔断坏。魔王退后，尔时世尊便说斯偈：

How wonderful! Great blessings and rewards, all wishes fulfilled" means that the cultivation of blessings by people is due to the practice established in previous lives. Even though the seeds sown in good fields of merit are few
the rewards received are immeasurable. If one has previously harmed sages and had impure intentions in giving, without equality, even if one receives a human form, one's appearance will be ugly and one will be despised by others Doing

「快哉大福报，　　所愿皆全成，速得第一灭，　　渐入无为际。」

If they seek skillful means, the wisdom of the sages and saints, and exhaust
the original source of suffering, they should know that they will attain great fortune.

若彼求方便，　　贤圣智慧施，尽其苦原本，　　当知获大幸。

"If they seek skillful means, the wisdom of the sages and saints, and exhaust the original source of suffering, they should know that they will attain great fortune."

若彼求方便，贤圣智慧施者，学人欲习贤圣法者，勇猛精进意不分散，然后乃应贤圣之法，是故说曰，若彼求方便，贤圣智慧施也。尽其苦原本，当知获大幸者，所谓苦者五盛阴是，能灭此者乃应道教，是故说曰，尽其苦原本，当知获大幸也。

Loving the Dharma, sleeping and waking well, with a pure and clean mind
the Dharma spoken by the sages and saints is enjoyed by the wise.

爱法善眠寤，　　心意洁清净，贤圣所说法，　　智者所娱乐。

"Learners practice and understand the profound Dharma, discern the meaning and direction of the phrases, with a tranquil mind without other thoughts, entering concentration with a single mind, not swayed by various delusions. The teachings spoken by the sages and saints are enjoyed and practiced without being able to let go. What the wise practice is not discussed by fools."

学人习行达了深法，晓了分别义句所趣，心意澹然无余异想，入定一意，不为众邪之所倾动，贤圣所言教，玩而习之不能舍离，智者所习非愚所论，是故说曰，爱法善眠寤，心意洁清净，贤圣所说法，智者所娱乐也。

If one's mind delights in meditation, it also delights in non-arising, and delights
in the four foundations of mindfulness, as well as the seven factors of
awakening, and the four bases of supernormal power, and the eightfold noble path.

若人心乐禅，　　亦复乐不起，亦乐四意止，　　并及七觉意，及彼四神足，　　贤圣八品道。

"If one's mind delights in meditation, it also delights in non-arising." The practitioner delights in meditation because they wish to attain nirvana in the realm of no remainder. It does not arise or cease. Therefore, it is said, "If one's mind delights in meditation, it also delights in non-arising." "It also delights in the four foundations of mindfulness, as well as the seven factors of awakening." Stopping afflictions and not arising is called mindfulness. Being awakened to something is called the factors of awakening. Therefore, it is said, "It also delights in the four foundations of mindfulness, as well as the seven factors of awakening." "And the four bases of supernormal power, and the eightfold noble path." The Dharma of the bases of supernormal power also cuts off afflictions and brings happiness without conditioning in the present life. The eightfold noble path also cuts off afflictions and brings happiness and good benefits in the present life. Therefore, it is said, "And the four bases of supernormal power, and the eightfold noble path."

At that time, the World-Honored One told his disciples, "You should each go to the four directions to teach and liberate the people of Jambudvipa. I wish to go alone to the river." At that time, the elder Anathapindada had some worldly affairs to attend to and came to Rajagriha. He went to a great elder's house to ask for lodging. Just then, the elder's family members and servants were all busy with various tasks, such as chopping firewood to start a fire, blowing on cooked and raw food, and arranging seats with woolen blankets and felt. The elder himself spread out a high seat, hung silk banners and canopies, and sprinkled scented water on the ground. Anathapindada asked the elder, "The preparations you are making today are not small. Are you inviting the king to your house? Or is it that your son is getting married or your daughter is getting married? Please tell me the reason." The elder replied, "The food and drink I am preparing cannot be measured by gods or humans, nor is it for the king, ministers, or officials. My son is not getting married and my daughter is not leaving the house. The reason I am preparing delicious food and drink is to invite the Buddha and the Sangha of monks to my house for a meal in the morning." When Anathapindada heard the name of the Buddha and the Sangha of monks, his hair stood on end with joy and sorrow. He then went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and after a while, withdrew and said to the Buddha, "I humbly wish that the World-Honored One is in good health and has been traveling in comfort. I heard that you are staying here. Have you been sleeping well?" At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse to Anathapindada. Therefore, it is said:

昔佛成道未久，初度五人，次后五人、江村十三人、贤士众中三十七人，通佛六十一人。尔时世尊告诸弟子：「汝等各各四面教化，度阎浮利地人。吾欲独往诣江水侧。」度三迦叶师徒千人，次度舍利弗、目揵连，次度洴沙王，在罗阅城迦兰陀竹园所。尔时阿那邠低长者，有少俗缘来至罗阅城中，造大长者欲得寄住，正值彼家男女仆从各各作役，或破薪然火，或吹生熟食，或有布置坐具氍毹毾［登＊毛］。是时，长者躬敷高座悬缯幡盖香汁洒地。是时，阿那邠低长者问彼长者：「贵家今日办具待宾之调亦非小节，为欲请国王过舍？为是贵家男欲娶妇？女欲嫁乎？愿闻其意。」其主报曰：「我今所办肴馔之具，亦非天及世人所能测度，亦非国王群臣百僚，男不娶妇女不出门。我所以办具甘馔饮食者，清旦请佛及比丘僧在家供养。」阿那邠低闻佛名号及比丘僧，衣毛悚竖悲而且喜，寻往佛所头面礼足在一面坐，斯须退坐前白佛言：「伏惟天尊兴居轻利游步康彊，闻侨在此得善眠乎？」尔时世尊与阿那邠低而说斯偈，是故说曰：

All attain good sleep, the Brahmins attain nirvana
not defiled by desire, completely liberated from all places.

「一切得善眠，　　梵志取灭度，不为欲所染，　　尽脱于诸处。

Completely severing inauspicious bonds, subduing inner afflictions, eternally ceasing to
attain sleep, with mind and consciousness all clear and penetrating.

尽断不祥结，　　降伏内烦热，永息得睡眠，　　心识悉清彻。」

Be careful not to be attached to pleasure, you should follow the
practice of protection, you should contemplate abandoning the world, and observe pleasant things.

慎莫著于乐，　　当就护来行，当念舍于世，　　观于快乐事。

"Be careful not to be attached to pleasure, you should follow the practice of protection. For a person who studies the path, without hardship it cannot be accomplished. It is necessary to endure hardship and then it can be accomplished. Abandoning mundane dhyāna and mundane liberation, cultivate immaculate dhyāna and immaculate liberation. Therefore it is said: Be careful not to be attached to pleasure, you should follow the practice of protection. Contemplate abandoning the world and observe pleasant things. When people encounter small pleasures, they should seek more to increase the basis of their pleasure. Therefore it is said: Contemplate abandoning the world and observe pleasant things."

慎莫著于乐，当就护来行者，夫人学道不苦不成，要当须苦然后乃成，舍世俗禅及俗解脱，修无漏禅无漏解脱，是故说曰，慎莫著于乐，当就护来行。当念舍于世，观于快乐事者，人遇小乐当更求索增其乐本，是故说曰，当念舍于世，观于快乐事也。

The worldly pleasures and the pleasures of the heavens, this
is called the exhaustion of craving, not even one-sixteenth is obtained.

如世俗欢乐，　　及彼天上乐，此名为爱尽，　　十六未获一。

"The worldly pleasures and the pleasures of the heavens, the worldly pleasures are the pleasures of the desire realm, and the heavenly pleasures are the pleasures of the form realm. For a long time, sentient beings are deluded in the five destinies and do not know how to embrace the truth. They greedily attach to the rewards of mundane dhyāna and blessings, transmigrating through the five destinies in a cycle. They think that they have attained the path and will be eternally extinguished without arising again. Therefore it is said: The worldly pleasures and the pleasures of the heavens. This is called the exhaustion of craving, not even one-sixteenth is obtained. For practitioners who first cut off the root of craving and forever remove the branches and leaves, holding the intention with fear to prevent future evils, later they attain the pleasure of the immaculate and roam freely in the natural. Among the sixteen parts, they have not yet obtained one. Therefore it is said: This is called the exhaustion of craving, not even one-sixteenth is obtained."

如世俗欢乐，及彼天上乐者，世俗乐者欲界之乐，及彼天乐者色界之乐，众生之类长夜之中，迷惑五趣不知禀真，贪著世俗禅福之报，流转五趣周而复始，谓为得道永灭不起，是故说曰，如世俗欢乐，及彼天上乐也。此名为爱尽，十六不获一者，其有行人先断爱根永去枝叶，执意怀惧防恶未然，后得无漏之乐游心自然，于十六分中未得其一。是故说曰，此名为爱尽，十六不获一也。

Able to abandon the heavy burden, no longer creating a heavy burden, the heavy
burden is the suffering of the world, able to abandon it is the fastest happiness.

能舍于重担，　　更不造重担，重担世之苦，　　能舍最快乐。

"Able to abandon the heavy burden, no longer creating a heavy burden, etc." means that when a person carries a heavy burden through a dangerous place, what they are carrying is not something worldly that is not urgent, nor is it gold, silver, precious stones, agate, coral, pearls, amber, etc., but rather worldly goods that are not wanted. A bystander advises them, "Look at what you are carrying. It is not a true treasure. Why don't you abandon it and seek the true one?" The person then abandons it and seeks the true one. The same is true for these sentient beings. They carry the body of the five aggregates and wander in the desire realm, revolving in birth and death without being able to escape. The sage tells them, "What you are carrying now is the body of the five aggregates, a filthy, leaking, and stinking place. Why do you carry it? You should quickly abandon it and seek a lighter one." At that time, the sentient beings then devised a means to abandon the form of the desire realm and take on the body of the form realm. Having already taken on the form of the form realm, the sage again went to them to teach and transform them, causing them to abandon the body and take up the five-part Dharma nature of the immaculate wisdom. Therefore it is said: "Able to abandon the heavy burden, no longer creating a heavy burden. The heavy burden is the suffering of the world. The fastest happiness is being able to abandon it."

能舍于重担，更不造重担等者，如人负重担经过崄难处，所负既不要世俗不急货，亦非金银珍宝、车璩马瑙、真珠琥珀，乃是世俗不要之货，傍人谏语：「观君所负，非是真宝，何不舍之，更求真者？」其人即舍，更求真者。观此众生亦复如是，负五阴身游处欲界，宛转生死不能得出。圣人告曰：「汝今所负五阴之形，秽漏臭处荷负是为？宜可速舍更求轻者。」尔时众生即设方便，舍欲界形受色界身。已受色界之形，圣人复往就彼教化，使令舍身就无漏智五分法性。是故说曰，能舍于重担，更不造重担，重担世之苦，能舍最快乐也。

Extinction of all desires and love, and cessation of all activities, and extinction of
the root of the five aggregates, no longer accepting the three realms of existence.

尽断诸爱欲，　　及灭一切行，并灭五阴本，　　更不受三有。

"Like that practitioner who observes with immaculate wisdom, extinguishing desire, form, and formless love, body, speech, and mind activities, eliminating the three of the body, four of the speech, and three of the mind, completely extinguishing without remainder, understanding the rise and fall of the five aggregates, no longer attached to the activities of the three realms of existence." Therefore it is said: "Extinction of all desires and love, and cessation of all activities, and extinction of the root of the five aggregates, no longer accepting the three realms of existence."

如彼行人以无漏慧观，灭欲爱色爱无色爱，身行口行意行，除身三口四意三永尽无余，解知五阴兴起本末，更不复著三有之行。是故说曰，尽断诸爱欲，及灭一切行，并灭五阴本，更不受三有也。

When righteousness flourishes, there is joy; friends and food bring happiness. The bliss
of cessation and tranquility spreads to others. Suffering is the basis for happiness.

义兴则有乐，　　朋友食福乐，彼灭寂然乐，　　展转普及人，苦为乐为本。

"When righteousness flourishes, there is joy; friends and food bring happiness." This means that it is like merchants who toil and suffer, braving dangers to gather great treasures. When they return home safely, their clans rejoice, and their children and grandchildren are all delighted. Friends and companions are all benefited. If one opens one's mind and gives widely to all, there will be no more suffering. Happiness is the basis. The clans enjoy themselves and cannot bear to part. Therefore it is said: "When righteousness flourishes, there is joy; friends and food bring happiness. The bliss of cessation and tranquility spreads to others. Suffering is the basis for happiness."

义兴则有乐，朋友食福乐等者，犹若商贾之人劳形苦体，冒涉危崄采致重宝，安隐还家宗族庆贺，男女大小靡不欢喜，朋友同伴悉皆蒙恩；若使开意惠施普及一切，无复众苦以乐为本，宗族娱乐不能舍离。是故说曰，义兴则有乐，朋友食福乐，彼灭寂然乐，展转普及人，苦为乐为本也。

It is like a fire pit, blazing and burning, gradually going out, not knowing where it has gone.

犹彼火炉，　　赫焰炽然，渐渐还灭，不知所凑。

Those who see in this way are free from the mire of desire and love. Even
when they leave, there is no place to go. They attain the bliss of the immovable.

如是等见人，　　免于爱欲泥，去亦无处所，　　以获无动乐。

"It is like a fire pit, blazing and burning, like a blacksmith burning an iron ball, extremely blazing and burning, very difficult to approach. Therefore, the sage observes the class of sentient beings burning themselves with the fire of desire, anger, and ignorance, yet they are not aware of it. Therefore, it is said: 'It is like a fire pit, blazing and burning.' Gradually going out, not knowing where it has gone, is like that hot iron ball gradually becoming cold, not knowing where the heat has gone, and also not knowing where the coolness is. Therefore, it is said: 'Gradually going out, not knowing where it has gone.' Those who see in this way are free from the mire of desire and love. Those practitioners attain equal liberation, without any obstruction, free from the deep mire of desire and love, and then attain liberation from the shore of birth and death. Therefore, it is said: 'Those who see in this way are free from the mire of desire and love.' Even when they leave, there is no place to go. They attain the bliss of the immovable. Such people unite the spirit with the void and the consciousness with the empty essence. They also do not know the east, west, south, north, the four directions, or above and below. When they come, they do not know where they come from. When they leave, they do not know where they go. It is like a hot iron ball gradually cooling down, not knowing where the heat has gone, and also not knowing where the coolness is. Therefore, it is said: 'Even when they leave, there is no place to go. They attain the bliss of the immovable.'"

犹彼火炉，赫焰炽然者，犹若彼匠火烧铁丸，极自炽然甚难可近，是以圣人观众生类婬怒痴火，而自烧炙不自觉知。是故说曰，犹彼火炉，赫焰炽然也。渐渐还灭，不知所凑者，如彼热铁丸渐渐至冷，不知热之所凑，亦复不知冷之所在，是故说曰，渐渐还灭，不知所凑也。如是等见人，免于爱欲泥者，彼修行人得等解脱无复罣碍，免于爱欲之深泥，便得离于生死之岸，是故说曰，如是等见人，免于爱欲泥也。去亦无处所，以获无动乐者，如是之类神与冥合识与空体，亦复不知东西南北四维上下，来亦不知所从来，去亦不知所从去，犹如热铁丸渐渐欲冷，不知热之所凑，亦复不知冷之所在，是故说曰，去亦无处所，以获无动乐也。

In the middle, there is no anger. There is change and impermanence
Eliminating sorrow, there is no worry. Tranquil, observing the existence of the world.

中间无有恚，　　有变易不停，除忧无有愁，　　寂然观世有。

"In the middle, there is no anger. This refers to anger, which defiles the human mind and does not lead to the path. Only those without defilements can avoid this angry mind. Therefore, it is said: 'In the middle, there is no anger.' There is change and impermanence. The world has many practices, with varying degrees of lightness and heaviness. Some are in accord with the void and reach without creating afflictions, while some know and deliberately violate, giving rise to defilements. Therefore, the sage admonishes later generations, wanting those who practice to change their past mistakes and cut off future calamities. Those who study greedily treasure it, and before it falls into the mind, they can advance to the room of sages. Only then do they know the sanctity of the holy Dharma and the unapproachability of the defiled Dharma. Therefore, it is said: 'There is change and impermanence.' Eliminating sorrow, there is no worry. It is like that practitioner who forever uproots the root of sorrow and is in accord with the faculty of joy. Tranquil, observing the changes of the world, like an illusion or a mirage. Therefore, it is said: 'Eliminating sorrow, there is no worry. Tranquil, observing the existence of the world.'"

中间无有恚者，所谓恚者，染污人心不至于道，唯有无垢之人，乃能免此恚怒之心，是故说曰，中间无有恚也。有变易不停者，世多有行，行有轻重举操不同，或有冥契运至不造结使，或有知而故犯以兴尘劳。是以圣人布诫后生，欲令执行之人改既往之失、绝将来之祸，贪学之人玩之宝之，未坠于心便能进适贤圣之室，然后方知圣法之可崇，秽法之叵近。是故说曰，有变易不停也。除忧无有愁者，如彼修行人，永拔愁忧之本，与乐根共相应，寂然观世变，如彼幻野马也。是故说曰，除忧无有愁，寂然观世有也。

There is joy and no affliction. There is much hearing of the
true Dharma. Even if one sees harm, everyone is greedy for form.

有乐无有恼，　　正法而多闻，设见有所损，　　人人贪于色。

"There is joy and no affliction. There is much hearing of the true Dharma. Even if one sees harm, everyone is greedy for form."

有乐无有恼，正法而多闻者，如彼入定人，昼夜禅寂不离定意，空无相愿以为游观，当时虽复身遭苦行，神寂无为无所伤损，如彼行人无瞋怒心，慈愍群萌与己无异，是故说曰，有乐无有恼，正法而多闻也。设见有所损，人人贪于色者，如彼学者观彼根原，婬怒痴病众祸之首，皆起欲怒心意，共相染污以成大患，便不能脱生老病死愁忧苦恼众患之原。是故说曰，设见有所损，人人贪于色。

There is no bondage in the world. The good life is long. The great Dharma knows the origin of bondage. People
should understand the flaws of bondage. People's minds are bound and attached. They are also bound to the origin of form.

无结世善寿，　　大法知结原，人当明结瑕，　　人人心缚著，亦缚于色本。

"Those without bondage have exhausted desire, anger, and ignorance. They no longer delight in the worldly roots of bondage. They also do not give rise to resentment and anger. The wise understand and can eliminate this illness. Having eliminated their own illness, they also treat others to eliminate illness. They also do not think of being attached to various forms. Their minds are unmoved by gain, loss, slander, or praise. Therefore it is said: 'There is no bondage in the world. The good life is long. The great Dharma knows the origin of bondage. People should understand the flaws of bondage. People's minds are bound and attached. They are also bound to the origin of form.'"

无结之人婬怒痴尽，不复乐俗众结之本，怨雠恚心亦复不兴，明人所鉴能断斯病，既自去病复治他人使无有病，亦复不念著于众色，利衰毁誉其心不动，是故说曰，无结世善寿，大法知结原，人当明结瑕，人人贪缚著，亦缚于色本。

All suffering from humiliation, all happiness from accepting oneself, victory and
defeat naturally arise, yet in the end there is nothing gained.

一切受辱苦，　　一切任己乐，胜负自然兴，　　竟不有所获。

"All suffering from humiliation, all happiness from accepting oneself. When people encounter difficulties, their minds cannot be at ease. They always fear losing favor by looking at others' faces. Those who indulge themselves get what they want at will, like a thought immediately becoming real, responding to sound like an echo. Therefore it is said: 'All suffering from humiliation, all happiness from accepting oneself.' Victory and defeat naturally arise, yet in the end there is nothing gained. It is like people in the world, nobility and humbleness are impermanent. One may be a wheel-turning sage king, and then become a king of scattered grains. One may be honored or humble, high or low. Only the path of sages has no honor or dishonor, high or low. Therefore it is said: 'Victory and defeat naturally arise, yet in the end there is nothing gained.'"

一切受辱苦，一切任己乐者，人遭困厄意不得舒，瞻人颜色恒恐失意，自恣之人随意所欲，如念即至如响应声，是故说曰，一切受辱苦，一切任己乐也。胜负自然兴，竟不有所获者，如人处世贵贱无常，或为转轮圣王，后便为粟散诸王，一尊一卑或高或下，唯有贤圣之道无有尊卑高下，是故说曰，胜负自然兴，竟不有所获也。

Those who wish for happiness and longevity can endure the light
retribution. The patient one endures others, the impatient one dwells in existence.

诸欲得乐寿，　　能忍彼轻报，忍者忍于人，　　不忍处诸有。

"To summarize the meaning, it is said: 'To harm without harm, to trouble without trouble, to anger without anger, to resent without resentment, as above, without difference.'"

取要言之略说其义，无害而生害，无恼而生恼，无恚而生恚，无怨而生怨，如上无异。

Those who wish for happiness and longevity are not deluded about
afflictions. The deluded one is deluded about others, I am without delusion.

诸欲得乐寿，　　于惑而无惑，惑者惑于人，　　我斯无有惑。

Those who wish for happiness and longevity should have no attachment to themselves. They
should eat the food of mindfulness, like the gods of the Light Sound Heaven.

诸欲得乐寿，　　终己无结著，当食于念食，　　如彼光音天，

Always using mindfulness as food, the mind and body have nothing to rely
on. In villages and fields, seeing suffering and happiness, each other is not
burned. Although encountering the traces of pleasure, without traces, how can there be traces?

恒] cf.【丽】以念为食，　　意身无所猗。村野见苦乐，　　彼此无所烧，虽值更乐迹，　　无迹焉有更？

"In villages and fields, seeing suffering and happiness, each other is not burned. When people practice the Way, they may live near a city or rely on a village, or in a desolate place without people. Sometimes they encounter suffering and many people feel pain, and sometimes they encounter happiness and do not rejoice. They do not give rise to the traces of pleasure and give rise to twelve kinds of illness. 'There' refers to the six sense objects, and 'here' refers to the six sense faculties. Therefore it is said, 'In villages and fields, seeing suffering and happiness, each other is not burned.' Although encountering the traces of pleasure, without traces, how can there be traces? When people live in the world, their minds are always negligent. First pleasure and then happiness increases the roots of sin. Sometimes they are born in hell and experience pleasure, but without traces, there is no hellish pleasure. Therefore it is said, 'Although encountering the traces of pleasure, without traces, how can there be traces?'"

村野见苦乐，彼此无所烧者，人之修道或在城傍依村而住，或在旷野无人之处，或时遇苦众人痛心，时复遭乐不以为欢，不兴更乐起十二种病。彼者彼六尘，此者此六情。是故说曰，村野见苦乐，彼此无所烧也。虽值更乐迹，无迹焉有更者，人之处世心恒放逸，先更后乐遂增罪根，或时生彼地狱更乐，无更则无迹，亦复无有地狱更乐，是故说曰，虽值更乐迹，无迹焉有更也。

Wherever there are wise people, they are not attached to the defilements of desire
Even if they encounter suffering and happiness, they do not give rise to harmful thoughts.

所在有贤人，　　不著欲垢秽，正使遭苦乐，　　不兴于害心。

"Wherever there are wise people, they are not attached to the defilements of desire. Sages live in the world, often hiding themselves, not attached to the thought of desire, not giving rise to the defilements of desire. The so-called wise people are those who have attained the stages of non-returner and arhat. Therefore it is said, 'Wherever there are wise people, they are not attached to the defilements of desire.' Even if they encounter suffering and happiness, they do not give rise to harmful thoughts. Even though they encounter suffering and happiness, they do not give rise to attachment. Therefore it is said, 'Even if they encounter suffering and happiness, they do not give rise to harmful thoughts.'"

所在有贤人，不著欲秽垢者，圣人处世多自隐遁，不著欲想不兴欲垢，所谓贤人，阿那含、阿罗汉是。故说曰，所在有贤人，不著欲秽垢也。正使遭苦乐，不兴于害意者，虽遭苦乐不兴想著，是故说曰，正使遭苦乐，不兴于害意也。
