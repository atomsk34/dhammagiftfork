T 212.33

Chuyao jing / Udānavarga (T 212) 出曜经

T 212.33 ◎沙门品第三十三

Parallel to: SN 2.8 / SN 7.20 / SN 9.11 / Dhp 256–272 / Dhp 306–319 / Dhp 360–382 / Ud 3.1 / Ud 3.3 / Ud 3.4 / Ud 3.7 (~) / Ud 3.9 / Ud 3.10 / Ud 4.8 / Ud 4.9 / Ud 4.10 / Snp 3.12 / Snp 4.15 / Iti / Thag 1 (~) / Thag 4 / Thag 14 / Thag 17 / T 210.30 / T 210.34 / T 213.32

A monk who begs for alms, having nothing accumulated, is praised by gods and humans, and is born pure and undefiled.

比丘乞求，　　以得无积，天人所誉，生净无秽。

A monk who begs for alms, having nothing accumulated, is praised by gods and humans, and is born pure and undefiled.

比丘乞求，以得无积者，乞食比丘恒作是念：「我今所求索者自足而已，不留遗余计为财货，设有遗余寻施与人不留遗长。如佛律禁所说，父母年迈老病著床，及同学比丘久抱重患不堪行来，听使乞索，不问多少供养老病。」是故说曰，比丘乞食，以得无积也。天人所誉，生净无秽者，比丘执行少欲知足，到时乞求无所藏积，诸天卫护称叹其德，名闻四远靡不闻知。论此比丘生净无秽，所以诸天称叹其德者，持戒之人，死必生天增益诸天众，减损阿须伦众，是故说曰，天人所誉，生净无秽也。

A monk who is compassionate, loving, and respectful of the Buddha's teachings, deeply enters tranquility and insight, and extinguishes afflictions to attain peace.

比丘为慈，　　爱敬佛教，深入止观，灭行乃安。

A monk who practices the four immeasurables with a compassionate, loving, and protective mind, cherishing all beings, and who loves and respects the Three Jewels with unwavering faith, deeply enters the tranquility and insight of the path, leaving traces wherever he goes begging for alms; the reason for eliminating greed and controlling the mind is to eliminate worldly glory and not be attached to material gain, to thoroughly exhaust birth and death and extinguish all evil practices, to transcend existence and reach non-existence, which is called eternal peace.

比丘执意行四等心，慈悲喜护愍念一切，爱敬三宝信心不断，深入分别止观所趣，在在乞求处处留化；所以除贪制意者，欲除世荣不贪利养，究尽生死灭诸恶行，度有至无乃谓永安。是故说曰，比丘为慈，爱敬佛教，深入止观，灭行乃安也。

A monk who has exhausted all attachments, abandoned attachment and removed
arrogance, without self or ego, who is this truth not close to?

比丘尽诸爱，　　舍爱去贡高，无我去吾我，　　此义孰不亲。

A monk who has exhausted all attachments, abandoned attachment and removed arrogance, without self or ego, who is this truth not close to?

比丘尽诸爱，舍爱去贡高者，彼苦行比丘灭诸想著，欲色色色无色色、欲爱色爱无色爱，三界憍慢众邪颠倒泓然除尽，是故说曰，比丘尽诸爱，舍爱去贡高也。无我去吾我，此义孰不亲者，苦行比丘不滞三界，解知内外悉无有主，计我之人撗来求福，虽得从愿后必堕落；在凡夫地不见吾我之人者，解知内外万物虚寂，孰者吾我？吾我是谁？为人所系及得骂詈，悉虚悉寂都无所有，为人所骂音声来往，中间内外悉无所有。是故说曰，无我去吾我，此义孰不亲也。

Know that this Dharma is the way out of the body, like an elephant commanding the enemy, a monk practices.

当知是法，　　身之出要，如象御敌，比丘习行。

Know that this Dharma is the way out of the body, like an elephant commanding the enemy, a monk practices.

当知是法，身之出要者，习行比丘得博采众要，择修善德以补不及，如人欲所至必由其径，求道窠窟必有其路，出要路者四谛真如是。是故说曰，当知是法，身之出要也。如象御敌，比丘习行者，如彼暴象饮以𬪩酒奔逸向敌，虽被刀射至死不退，要有所擒乃] cf.【丽】还本营。所以然者，畏上御者不畏外寇：习行比丘亦复如是，要从导师承受苦教，隐在心怀反复思惟不失义迹。是故说曰，如象御敌，比丘习行也。

People do not live for an eon, but struggle within with their minds. Guarding the body and contemplating the truth, the monk is at ease.

人不寿劫，　　内与心诤，护身念谛，比丘惟安。

As for those who cultivate and study, they attain the four bases of spiritual power and practice day and night. If they wish to live for an eon or more than an eon, they can do so as they wish without difficulty. They are free from all bonds and attachments, always struggling with their minds, not allowing them to wander, cutting off all hopes and desires, removing the intention of right and wrong, forever separating from desires, and not seeing the nest of the three realms. Only then can they be in accord with the practice of no loss. Therefore it is said: People do not live for an eon, but struggle within with their minds. Guarding the body and contemplating the truth, the monk is at ease.

夫修学之人得四神足昼夜修习，意欲住寿一劫若过一劫，随意所念则无有难，离诸缚著常与心诤，不使流驰断诸悕望，去是非意与欲永别，亦复不见三界窠窟，然后乃应无亏损行，是故说曰，人不寿劫，内与心诤，护身念谛，比丘惟安。

Remembering relatives and friends, with right livelihood free from mixing, giving
what should be given, also making the deportment complete, the monk
equipped with all practices, then able to end the edge of suffering.

念亲同朋友，　　正命无杂糅，施知应所施，　　亦令威仪具，比丘备众行，　　乃能尽苦际。

The accomplishment of practitioners is all due to friends. When merit and virtue are accomplished, it is praised beyond the four directions. Those who receive it are renewed day by day. The practice is genuine and not attached to external things. The giving is to the Buddha, bhikṣus, and the Sangha, as well as teachers and elders. The reason is that these people all have deportment, uphold various rules and regulations, and know the causes of suffering. Therefore it is said: Remembering relatives and friends, with right livelihood free from mixing, giving what should be given, also making the deportment complete, the monk equipped with all practices, then able to end the edge of suffering.

行人成就皆由朋友，功成德满称过四远，禀受之人日有其新，所行真正不著外部，所出惠施，施佛比丘僧与师及诸尊长。所以然者？斯等诸人皆有威仪，执诸礼节知苦之所由。是故说曰，念亲同朋友，正命无杂糅，施知应所施，亦令威仪具，比丘备众行，乃能尽苦际。

Hands and feet should not be rashly violated, speech should be restrained and actions
should be cautious. Always delighting in concentration within, guarding the practice is called a bhikṣu.

手足莫妄犯，　　节言慎所行，常内乐定意，　　守行谓比丘。

In the world, there are many people who are violent and do evil, rolling up their sleeves and attacking each other, causing injury. Indulging the six sense faculties, they are attached to the dharmas of form, sound, smell, taste, and touch. Such people, although they may practice the Way, do not practice according to the Dharma. They do not progress in the methods of cultivating the Way, and they lose the demeanor of the sages. They are like carrying a dead person, with nothing straight in the middle. These bhikṣus are also like this. If they can concentrate their minds and practice accordingly, sitting in meditation and concentrating their minds, practicing the Way six times a day, not losing their original practice, then it is said: Hands and feet should not be rashly violated, speech should be restrained and actions should be cautious. Always delighting in concentration within, guarding the practice is called a bhikṣu.

世多有人凶暴为恶，手卷相加遂致伤害，内恣六情，著色声香味细滑之法。如斯之人虽得为道，不应法行，进无修道之法，退失贤圣之仪，如担死人种，无所复中直。此比丘等亦复如是，能自专意所行随顺，坐禅定意六时行道，不失本行，是故说曰，手足莫妄犯，节言慎所行，常内乐定意，守行谓比丘也。

Delighting in the Dharma, desiring the Dharma, contemplating the peaceful Dharma, the bhikṣu relies on the Dharma, being upright and not wasting.

乐法欲法，　　思惟安法，比丘依法，正而不费。

The learner practices and distinguishes the dharmas, sees the dharmas, attains the dharmas, deeply contemplates the dharmas. Whether sitting or lying down, the multitudes of spirits come and go, contemplating the peaceful Dharma. The bhikṣu relies on the Dharma and attains nirvāṇa. It benefits the holy path without wasting. There is increase every day, never decreasing. It also causes the true Dharma to remain in the world for a long time. Therefore it is said: Delighting in the Dharma, desiring the Dharma, contemplating the peaceful Dharma, the bhikṣu relies on the Dharma, being upright and not wasting.

学人修行分别诸法，见法得法深入观法，若坐若卧众神往来思惟安法，比丘依法乃得灭度，于诸圣道益而无费，日有增益终无减损，亦使正法久存于世，是故说曰，乐法欲法，思惟安法，比丘依法，正而不费也。

One should study entering emptiness, the bhikṣu dwelling in tranquility, delighting in non-human places, observing the equal dharmas.

当学入空，　　比丘静居，乐非人处，观察等法。

The practitioner observes these five aggregates, considering them to be permanent, solid, and indestructible, unable to let go and give rise to afflictions. However, the practitioner distinguishes the five aggregates as empty inside and outside. Even if one is in the wilderness, under a tree, or in a charnel ground, contemplating the fundamental Dharma and seeking the fruit of the path, one should first cultivate emptiness and then accord with the true Dharma. In the past, some practitioners sat in meditation in an empty room. Subhūti was outside seeking to open the door. The person inside said, "Who are you?" Subhūti replied, "I am the one who is falsely named Subhūti in the world." What people enjoy is playing the zither, drums, and music. This is what people enjoy. What non-humans enjoy is meditation, counting the breath, and focusing the mind on one thing. This is what non-humans think about. Therefore it is said: One should study entering emptiness, the bhikṣu dwelling in tranquility, delighting in non-human places, observing the equal dharmas.

执行之人观此五阴，计为是常牢固不败，不能舍离兴于尘劳，然执行之人，分别五阴内外悉空，正使在于旷野之中树下冢间，思惟法本求于道果，先当习空乃应道真。昔诸道人室内坐禅空行，须菩提在外求索开门，内人应曰：「汝是谁乎？」须菩提对曰：「世人假名须菩提者也。」人所乐者，弹琴皷瑟作倡伎乐，此是人所乐，非人所乐者，禅定数息系意在一，非人所念。是故说曰，当学入空，比丘静居，乐非人处，观察等法也。

One should control the five aggregates,
subduing the mind like water,
pure and harmonious,
the taste of sweet dew.

当制五阴，　　服意如水，清净和悦，为甘露味。

Beginners observe these five aggregates, all of which will decay and perish, nothing to be desired. Distinguishing all attachments as not solid and firm, the mind is equal and peaceful, with a harmonious countenance. Pure and flawless, ending all suffering. Therefore it is said: One should control the five aggregates, subduing the mind like water, pure and harmonious, the taste of sweet dew.

初学之人观此五阴，皆当坏败无一可贪，分别诸持悉不牢固，意均平等颜色和悦，清净无瑕尽诸苦际，是故说曰，当制五阴，服意如水，清净和悦，为甘露味。

Just as an extremely steep mountain is not moved by the
wind, a bhikṣu who has eliminated ignorance is unshakable wherever he is.

如彼极峻山，　　不为风所动，比丘尽愚痴，　　所在不倾动。

Just as Mount Sumeru is not swayed by the four winds, a bhikṣu who has eliminated ignorance is likewise unshakable by the dharmas of form, sound, smell, taste, and touch. Therefore it is said: Just as an extremely steep mountain is not moved by the wind, a bhikṣu who has eliminated ignorance is unshakable wherever he is.

犹若安明山不为四种风所倾动，尽痴比丘亦复如是，不为色声香味细滑之法所动，是故说曰，如彼极峻山，不为风所动，比丘尽愚痴，所在不倾动也。

All names and forms are unreal and deceptive. Do not be deluded. Do not associate with them or love them. Only then can one be a bhikṣu.

一切名色，　　非有莫惑，不近不爱，乃为比丘。

The six sense bases of name and form are what practitioners abandon. There is no self or what belongs to the self. Do not associate with dangerous dharmas. There are various dharmas, some true and some dangerous. The true ones are the perfections. The dangerous ones are the worldly norms. A bhikṣu who is fully endowed with this is called a true person. Therefore it is said: All names and forms are unreal and deceptive. Do not be deluded. Do not associate with them or love them. Only then can one be a bhikṣu.

名色六入，行者之所弃，我所非我所都无所有，不近于危崄之法。法有种种，或有真实或有危崄，所谓真正者诸度无极，所谓危崄者世俗常则。比丘具足此者乃谓应真，是故说曰，一切名色，非有莫惑，不近不爱，乃为比丘。

A bhikṣu is not different. Arrogant and without precepts, abandoning greed and contemplating the path, only then can one be a bhikṣu.

比丘非别，　　慢诞无戒，舍贪思道，乃应比丘。

Tranquilizing the mind is not different. Careless and without faith, able to extinguish all suffering, one becomes the supreme śramaṇa.

息心非别，　　放逸无信，能灭众苦，为上沙门。

At that time, the World Honored One, at the proper time, holding his alms bowl and adjusting his robes, went directly to the place of the brāhman Brahmādatta. At that time, the brāhman saw the World Honored One from afar and praised himself, saying: "I am also an ascetic, and you are also an ascetic. Which of the two ascetics is superior?" At that time, the World Honored One spoke this verse:

尔时世尊，到时持钵整顿衣服，径向乞求婆罗堕者婆罗门所。尔时梵志遥见世尊，梵志自叹说曰：「我亦乞士君亦乞士，二乞士中何者为胜？」尔时世尊便说此偈：

A bhikṣu is not different. Arrogant and without precepts, abandoning greed and contemplating the path, then one is a true bhikṣu.

「比丘非别，　　慢诞无戒，舍贪思道，乃应比丘。

Tranquilizing the mind is not different. Careless and without faith, able to extinguish all suffering, is the supreme śramaṇa.

「息心非别，　　放逸无信，能灭众苦，为上沙门。」

At that time, the brāhman, having heard these verses, immediately offered all his wealth to the World-Honored One. At that time, the Tathāgata did not accept it, and said to the brāhman: "What I have spoken is not praised in songs. Why would I accept what you offer?" The brāhman said to the Buddha: "I do not know, to whom should I give this offering now?" The World-Honored One said: "You should now take this offering and place it in a clean place, either on grassless ground or in pure water." At that time, the brāhman accepted the Tathāgata's instruction and wrote the offering in the water. At that time, the water naturally surged up, making various sounds, and gradually emitted great light from within. The brāhman, having seen this, was overjoyed and could not contain himself. The Tathāgata then spoke the true four noble truths, and while sitting there, all his dust and defilements were exhausted and he attained the purity of the Dharma eye.

尔时梵志闻斯偈已，即以所有财货施于世尊。尔时如来寻不受之，语梵志曰：「我今所说非歌颂所赞，何缘取汝所施之物？」梵志白佛：「不审今者以此所施为付何人？」世尊告曰：「汝今持此所施持著净处，若著无草之地，若著清净水中。」尔时梵志受如来教，即以所施写著水中。是时，水中自然涌出，作若干种声，渐渐于中出大光明。梵志见已踊跃欢喜不能自胜，如来即说真如四谛，寻于坐上诸尘垢尽得法眼净。

The bhikṣu attained the samādhi of loving-kindness, accepted the Buddha's teachings, and attained
the traces of complete cessation, without relatives, one should be careful not to see.

比丘得慈定，　　承受诸佛教，极得灭尽迹，　　无亲慎莫覩。

The bhikṣu who attains the liberation of loving-kindness, distinguishes the myriad practices and understands all without exception. If there are people who see the various types of beings, infantry, elephant troops, cavalry, chariot troops, fighting and quarreling with each other, those who enter loving-kindness will pity those who are not yet able, and will liberate sentient beings to reach the shore of non-conditioning, just like a balanced scale, equal and without duality. In the Tathāgata, one attains the four unshakable minds that cannot be swayed, just like the supreme elder and the bhikṣu who see the Buddha without ever being satisfied. Even if a transformed Buddha were before them, it would not be able to cause their minds to be swayed. The practitioner who attains the traces of complete cessation is without further afflictions. Knowing what is approachable, knowing what is approachable, knowing what is to be followed, knowing what is to be followed, such is the trace of practice. The cessation of practice is the basis. In brief, the essence of such afflictions is burned by fire. In this way, gradually cutting off the source of all afflictions, is there perhaps a brāhman who has attained nirvāṇa?

比丘得慈所在解脱，分别万行无事不达。设复有人见众生之类，步兵象兵马兵车兵共相鬪讼，入慈之人愍彼不及，拔济众生至无为岸，犹如平称平等无二。于如来所得四坚固之心不可倾动，犹如最胜长者及以比丘覩佛无厌足，正使化佛在其前者，亦不能使心有所倾动。行人得灭尽之迹无复众恼，知可近知近可从知从，如是行踪迹，灭行则为本。略说其要，如是结使本为火之所烧，如是渐以次断诸结使源，如是颇有梵志无乃至于泥洹。

The mind is joyful and extremely delighted, and those who love and cherish it are bhikṣus who are often joyful and at ease, empty and without root or source.

心喜极欢悦，加] cf.【丽】以爱念者，比丘多熙怡，　　尽空无根源。

The practitioner, joyful and leaping, without laziness, hears joy without being joyful, hears evil without being distressed. The bhikṣu enters concentration without confusion, always reflecting that from countless eons ago, cultivating various virtues without losing the basis of practice, exhausting the source of emptiness without end or limit. Therefore it is said: The mind is joyful and extremely delighted, and those who love and cherish it are bhikṣus who are often joyful and at ease, empty and without root or source.

彼修行之人，欢喜踊跃无有懈怠，闻喜不以为欢，闻恶不以为戚，比丘入定无有错乱，恒自思念，从无数劫以来，修行众德不失行本，究尽空源无边无崖，是故说曰，心喜极欢悦，加以爱念者，比丘多熙怡，尽空无根源。

Resting the body and resting the mind, restraining the mouth is also
good. Abandoning the world is called a bhikṣu, crossing the abyss without obstruction.

息身而息意，　　摄口亦其善，舍世谓比丘，　　渡渊无有碍。

The practitioner upholds dignified conduct without losing the principle, guarding the four faults of speech without any violation, not allowing the mind to wander, the teachings spoken without coarseness, first smiling and then speaking, suitable for people's feelings. Abandoning the world is called a bhikṣu. What is a bhikṣu? A bhikṣu is one who is apart from form, sound, smell, taste, and tactile objects, and who has abandoned desire, anger, and delusion. Therefore it is said: Resting the body and resting the mind, restraining the mouth is also good. Abandoning the world is called a bhikṣu, crossing the abyss without obstruction.

彼修行人执持威仪不失其则，护口四过无所违失，不使其心有所流驰，所说言教无有麤犷，先笑后言适可人情。舍世谓比丘，何者为比丘？所谓比丘者，离色声香味细滑法，去婬怒痴，是故说曰，息身而息意，摄口亦其善，舍世谓比丘，渡渊无有碍也。

Without meditation there is no wisdom, without wisdom there is no meditation. The path comes from meditation and wisdom, approaching nirvāṇa.

无禅不智，　　无智不禅，道从禅智，得近泥洹。

When a person studies, they first recite the four āgamas and the three baskets in their entirety, and then they are called "meditative concentration." This is worldly wisdom. "Without wisdom there is no meditation" means that the observation of pure wisdom must lead to a destination without obstruction. If one possesses both of these two things, one will be close to nirvāṇa. Therefore it is said: "Without meditation there is no wisdom, without wisdom there is no meditation. The path comes from meditation and wisdom, approaching nirvāṇa.

夫人学问，先从诵四阿含三藏具足，然后乃名称为禅定，此是世俗之智。无智不禅者，无漏慧观必有所至无有罣碍，设有二事具足者，便近于泥洹，是故说曰，无禅不智，无智不禅，道从禅智，得近泥洹。

Meditation is without carelessness, do not be disturbed by desire. Do not swallow molten copper, tormenting your own body.

禅无放逸，　　莫为欲乱，无吞洋铜，自恼燋形。

Just as those who practice gather their body, speech, and mind, with few desires and contentment, not being too diligent. Although they obtain clothing, food and drink, bedding, and medicine for illness, they seek only to sustain their bodies and do not pursue worldly glory. Their deportment and etiquette do not lose their proper measure. They are always content with their bedding and sitting mats, not accepting the pouring of molten copper into their mouths in future lives. Therefore it is said: "Meditation is without carelessness, do not be disturbed by desire. Do not swallow molten copper, tormenting your own body.

如彼修行之人摄身口意，少欲知足不大慇懃，虽得衣被饮食床卧具病瘦医药，趣自支形不慕世荣，威仪礼节不失其度，床卧坐具恒知止足，莫受后世洋铜灌口，是故说曰，禅无放逸，莫为欲乱，无吞洋铜，自恼燋形。

Able to protect one's own body, speech, and mind, protecting the mind without
evil, later attaining the Dharma of precepts and discipline, therefore called a bhikṣu.

能自护身口，　　护意无有恶，后获禁戒法，　　故号为比丘。

When a person practices, the body does not do evil, the mouth does not curse, and the mind is not jealous. Possessing these three is being a bhikṣu. Therefore it is said: "Able to protect one's own body, speech, and mind, protecting the mind without evil, later attaining the Dharma of precepts and discipline, therefore called a bhikṣu.

夫人习行，身不行恶、口不骂詈、意不妬嫉，具此三者乃为比丘。是故说曰，能自护身口，护意无有恶，后获禁戒法，故号为比丘也。

All who cultivate good dharmas, the seven factors of awakening as the
basis, this is called the wonderful Dharma, therefore called a determined bhikṣu.

诸有修善法，　　七觉意为本，此名为妙法，　　故曰定比丘。

Just as that practitioner well cultivates the Dharma, first attaining the cessation of the unconditioned and the source of suffering, then attaining the flowers of the seven factors of awakening, gradually reaching the unconditioned and approaching nirvāṇa. Therefore it is said: "All who cultivate good dharmas, the seven factors of awakening as the basis, this is called the wonderful Dharma, therefore called a determined bhikṣu.

如彼行人善修其法，先得无漏尽苦之源，便得七觉意华，渐至无为得近泥洹。是故说曰，诸有修善法，七觉意为本，此名为妙法，故曰定比丘也。

Now what is being spoken of, knowing for oneself the source of the end
of suffering, this is called the good basis, this is the bhikṣu without taints.

如今现所说，　　自知苦尽源，此名为善本，　　是无漏比丘。

In the present life, one observes and understands for oneself, seeking skillful means to end suffering. What is meant by ending suffering is the extinction of nirvāṇa. Therefore, with complete faculties, one accomplishes the practice without taints, acting as one wishes without any loss. Therefore it is said: "Now what is being spoken of, knowing for oneself the source of the end of suffering, this is called the good Dharma, this is the bhikṣu without taints.

于现法中而自观了，求其巧便尽于苦际，所谓尽苦际者灭尽泥洹，是故诸根具足成就无漏行，所行如意无所违失。是故说曰，如今现所说，自知尽苦源，此名为善法，是无漏比丘也。

Not by the power of upholding precepts, nor by the meaning of
much learning, but by rightly attaining concentration and wisdom, not attached to
embellishment, the bhikṣu upholds what is to be upheld, ending the practice without taints.

不以持戒力，　　及以多闻义，正使得定慧，　　不著于文饰，比丘有所持，　　尽于无漏行。

Moreover, one does not seek liberation by diligently practicing forbearance, concentration, and wisdom, nor does one attain the unconditioned by knowing the internal and external dharmas through much learning and wisdom. One must attain worldly concentration before reaching the subtle state. Then, either in the mountains, wilderness, or secluded places, one encounters good friends who speak the right path and not the wrong path. Bhikṣus should know that this practice is the practice of the Dharma without taints. The reason for ending suffering is that all arhats who have exhausted the taints, as well as those who are stream-enterers, once-returners, and non-returners, still experience various sufferings and afflictions. Therefore it is said: "Not by the power of upholding precepts, nor by the meaning of much learning, but by rightly attaining concentration, not attached to embellishment, the bhikṣu upholds what is to be upheld, ending the practice without taints.

夫人习行，不但精进忍辱一心智慧求于解脱，亦复不以多闻解慧知内外法至于无为，要得世俗定意，然后至于妙际，或在山野空闲之处，与善知识相遇，说其正径不说邪路。比丘当知此行习无漏法，所以尽苦际者，皆是漏尽罗汉，须陀洹、斯陀含、阿那含犹尚涉诸苦恼。是故说曰，不以持戒力，及以多闻义，正使得定意，不著于文饰，比丘有所持，尽于无漏行也。

One should contemplate the bliss of right enlightenment and not associate with ordinary
people. Observing the affairs of this present world, one should discern the five aggregates.

当观正觉乐，　　勿近于凡夫，观此现世事，　　分别于五阴。

Just as those learners contemplate the bliss of right enlightenment for their own enjoyment and do not associate with ordinary people. Why is that? Because it is not within their realm of experience. "Observing the affairs of this present world" means knowing the birth and death, progress and retreat of sentient beings, knowing the causes of suffering, and discerning the rise and fall of the five aggregates.

如彼学人观正觉乐，以自娱乐不近于凡夫。所以然者？非彼境界所有。观此世事者，知众生之类生者灭者进退所趣，知苦所由，分别五阴成败所趣，是故说曰，当观正觉乐，勿近于凡夫，观此现世事，分别于五阴。

For the sake of the sake of, one must forcefully restrain oneself. Even after leaving home and resolving, the mind is still stained.

为之为之，　　必强自制，舍家而解，意犹复染。

Those who practice with laxity and do not eliminate mental afflictions, are not
pure in the practice of austerity, how can they attain the great treasure?

行懈缓者，　　劳意弗除，非净梵行，　　焉致大宝？

Those who practice with laxity and do not eliminate mental afflictions, are not pure in the practice of austerity, how can they attain the great treasure?

执行之人兴诸想著起结使本，或有分别计有今世后世之累，于苦而不自免，比丘莫著此，自谓清净之行。诸有沙门婆罗门不知出要之法，我不说此人应得度也。所以然者？不离缚著之所致。比丘当知，非有而言有，此习邪见非真谛法。何以故？皆由五阴身本而兴此病，以有此病复生恶行，由此诸病不得尽苦际。比丘当知究尽其源，解知无常为变易法。

Those who practice with laxity and do not eliminate mental afflictions, are not pure in the practice of austerity, how can they attain the great treasure?

夫学之人观此法者，无坚无牢为无有要，解知无身则知生死，不以为死魔之所沮坏，以得胜彼更不造有，尽一切之有，此名苦际更无有上。

Those who practice with laxity and do not eliminate mental afflictions, are not
pure in the practice of austerity, how can they attain the great treasure?

心得永休息，　　比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，　　便脱魔缚著。

Those who practice with laxity and do not eliminate mental afflictions, are not pure in the practice of austerity, how can they attain the great treasure?

如彼行人永尽诸结，意所染著不复造行，色声香味细滑之法不复在怀，自知罪毕更不受胎，永离魔界亦不与欲尘相应，是故说曰，心得永休息，比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，便脱魔缚著。

Those who practice with laxity and do not eliminate mental afflictions, are not
pure in the practice of austerity, how can they attain the great treasure?

心以得永寂，　　比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，　　更不复受有。

Existence is the burden of birth and death. The reason one sinks and drifts, revolving through the five paths, is all because the mind is deluded and does not exhaust its source. Therefore, it is said: The mind attains permanent tranquility. The bhikṣu subdues the mind and practices, exhausting old age, sickness, and death, and no longer accepts existence. The practitioner holds the mind and has much to accomplish, always seeking means to save himself.

有者生死之累，所以沈漂周旋五道者，皆由意惑不尽其源故，是故说曰，心以得永寂，比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，更不复受有。行人执意多有所济，常求方便以自济渡。

By severing the root of craving, the bhikṣu subdues the mind and
practices, exhausting old age, sickness, and death, and no longer accepts existence.

以断于爱根，　　比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，　　更不复受有。

Craving is a disease that causes much harm. There are two kinds of craving in the desire realm: craving for food and craving for desire. The craving for the taste of meditation in the form and formless realms. Therefore, it is said: By severing the root of craving.

爱之为病多所危害，欲界爱者其事有二：一者食爱，二者欲爱。色界无色界禅味爱。是故说曰，以断于爱根。

The bhikṣu subdues the mind and practices, with no more afflictions
exhausting old age, sickness, and death, and no longer accepting existence.

无有结使心，　　比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，　　更不复受有。

What are called afflictions are the root of various practices and the defilements of all leaks. Therefore, it is said: The bhikṣu subdues the mind and practices, with no more afflictions, exhausting old age, sickness, and death, and no longer accepting existence.

所谓结使者，众行之本漏诸秽浊，是故说曰，无有结使心，比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，更不复受有。

The bhikṣu subdues the mind and practices, not by cutting off the root
of existence, exhausting old age, sickness, and death, and no longer accepting existence.

不以断有根，　　比丘摄意行，以尽老病死，　　更不复受有。

By crossing over birth and death, one no longer accepts existence.

以度生死，更不受有。

The bhikṣu subdues the mind and practices, exhausting old age, sickness, and
death, no longer accepting existence, and being liberated from the realm of Māra.

比丘摄意行，　　以尽老病死，更不复受有，　　以脱于魔界。

Forever leaving the realm of Māra, no longer dwelling in the realm of desire, being liberated and eternally liberated, no longer accepting existence.

永离于魔界，更不处于欲界，以脱永脱更不受有。

Outshining the thorns of the forest, and dispelling those who revile
it is like relying on Mount Tai, the bhikṣu does not suffer.

以胜丛林刺，　　及除骂詈者，犹如凭泰山，　　比丘不受苦。

"Outshining the thorns of the forest" refers to form, sound, smell, taste, touch, and dharmas. What are the thorns of the forest? The thorns of the forest are the root of desire, anger, and ignorance. Only the Buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, can eliminate them. Even if they revile me, I understand that they have no form. Reflecting within, the body is a vessel of suffering, neither inside nor outside having a master. Analyzing this body, what is there to be greedy for and joyful about? When one illness arises, the four hundred and four illnesses occur simultaneously. This is called the internal affliction of the body. The external affliction is the thorns of the forest, slander, defamation, and humiliation of the body, or being beaten. These kinds of things come from the outside. Or being harmed by venomous snakes and centipedes, these are all external matters that oppress the body. It is like Mount Tai, which cannot be moved by illusory spells and techniques. Therefore, if a bhikṣu wants to be free from the root of all suffering, there is only the true suchness of the Four Noble Truths. That bhikṣu does not know suffering and happiness. What is meant by not knowing suffering and happiness? When suffering comes, he does not consider it painful; when happiness arrives, he does not take joy in it. Therefore, it is said, 'Outshining the thorns of the forest, and dispelling those who revile, it is like relying on Mount Tai, the bhikṣu does not suffer.'

以胜丛林刺者，此名为色声香味细滑法，更复有者，何者为林刺？所谓林刺者，婬怒痴病最为根本，唯有诸佛世尊乃能除耳。设彼骂我，解知无形，内自思惟，身为苦器内外无主，分别此身何可贪乐？一病以发，四百四病同时俱作，此名身之内患。所谓外患者，荆棘丛林诽谤之名毁形污辱，或被挝打，如斯之类从外而至，或被蚖蛇毒害百足之虫，此皆外事来逼其身。犹若泰山，不用幻呪奇术之法所可移动，是以比丘欲得离众苦之本，唯有真如四谛。彼比丘不知苦乐，所谓不知苦乐者，苦至不以为酸楚，乐到不以欢娱。是故说曰，以胜丛林刺，及除骂詈者，犹如凭泰山，比丘不受苦。

Not thinking of the future world, viewing the world as an illusion and
a dream, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

不念今后世，　　观世如幻梦，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

It is like a person of bright conduct, knowing in the mind that the changes and transformations of this world and the next world are not constant. Therefore, it is said, 'Not thinking of the future world, viewing the world as an illusion and a dream, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.'

犹若明行人，意知今世后世变易不停，是故说曰，不念今后世，观世如幻梦，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。

Able to cut off the root of craving, exhausting the deep spring
of desire, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

能断爱根本，　　尽竭欲深泉，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

Therefore, the Buddha spoke this parable to make practitioners understand the depths and shallows, and to measure the correct practice that all accords with the Dharma. At that time, the World-Honored One instructed with morality and virtue, fearing that later sentient beings would not distinguish the root of craving. Therefore, he expounded to know its source. Therefore, it is said, 'Able to cut off the root of craving, exhausting the deep spring of desire, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.' In brief, the essentials are as follows: craving, anger, delusion, and arrogance are also like this. For those attached to craving, it is said to be craving; for those attached to anger, it is said to be anger; for those attached to delusion, it is said to be delusion.

所以说此喻者，欲使行人知其深浅，料量正行皆顺于法。尔时世尊训以道德，恐后众生不别爱本，是故演说知其出源。是故说曰，能断爱根本，尽竭欲深泉，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。略说其要，欲怒痴憍慢亦复如是，著欲者说其欲，著瞋者说其瞋，著𫘤者说其𫘤。

Able to cut off the five desires, cutting off the root of
desire, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

能断于五欲，　　断于欲根本，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

It is like a person bound with five fetters, sorrowful and distressed, with no more thoughts. Later, they are pardoned and escape from danger. Therefore, the Tathāgata uses this as a parable to make later generations clearly understand. Therefore, it is said, 'Able to cut off the five desires, cutting off the root of desire, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.'

犹如有人身被五系，愁忧苦恼无复情意，后得蒙赦得免危厄。是以如来为喻，欲使后生审知明白，是故说曰，能断于五欲，断于欲根本，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。

Able to cut off the five fetters, pulling out the thorns of
desire, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

能断于五结，　　拔于爱欲刺，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

The so-called five fetters are: fetters of greed, fetters of anger, fetters of sloth, fetters of restlessness, and fetters of doubt. They cover the human mind, preventing the illumination of wisdom, causing people to be blind and not seeing the light, extinguishing wisdom and forever severing all paths, not reaching nirvāṇa. Therefore, it is said, 'Able to cut off the five fetters, pulling out the thorns of desire, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.' 'Pulling out the thorns of desire' means the thorns have three meanings: the thorns of desire, the thorns of anger, and the thorns of ignorance. Completely cutting them off without remainder, they will not arise again. The Dharma of non-arising and non-ceasing sees the five hindrances being cut off. Therefore, it is said, 'Pulling out the thorns of desire.'

所谓五结者，贪欲结、瞋恚结、睡眠结、调戏结、疑结，覆盖人心使不覩慧明，使人盲冥不覩光明，灭于智慧永断诸趣，不得至于泥洹。是故说曰，能断于五结，拔于爱欲刺，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。拔于爱欲刺者，刺有三义：欲刺、恚刺、无明刺，尽断无余更不复生，无起灭法见断五盖。是故说曰，拔于爱欲刺也。

Those who have no worldly affairs, and also cut off unwholesome
roots, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

诸有无家业，　　又断不善根，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

That practitioner has been enduring suffering for a long time, cultivating bodhisattva virtues without ever abandoning them. Renouncing the household life and studying, they do not crave worldly glory. Therefore, it is said, 'Those who have no worldly affairs, and also cut off unwholesome roots, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.'

彼修行人执苦来久，修菩萨德终日不舍，舍家出学不贪世荣，是故说曰，诸有无家业，又断不善根，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。

Those who are not troubled, and also cut off unwholesome roots
the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

诸不有热恼，　　又断不善根，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

The so-called troubles are: first, the trouble of desire, second, the trouble of anger, and third, the trouble of ignorance. Among the three troubles, anger is the worst. The burning by fire goes from the desire realm up to the first dhyāna stage. The blazing fire of the three poisons burns from the desire realm up to the formless realm. The one who can extinguish these three poisons is the foremost one who attains the bliss of the unconditioned. Therefore, it is said, 'Those who are not troubled, and also cut off unwholesome roots, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.'

所谓热恼者，一者欲热恼，二者瞋恚热恼，三者愚痴热恼，三热恼中恚最为上，火所焚烧从欲界乃至初禅地；三毒炽火烧欲界至无色界，能灭此三毒界者，乃为第一无为之乐。是故说曰，诸不有热恼，又断不善根，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。

Cutting off desire without leaving any remainder, like uprooting what is not
firmly established, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

断欲不遗余，　　如拔不牢固，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

People who are attached to desire all lose their lives. The reason for this is that it is caused by the mind being deluded by the mind. Therefore, the sages first restrain sexual desire. Therefore, it is said, 'Cutting off desire without leaving any remainder, like uprooting what is not firmly established, the bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.' In brief, the essentials are: greed, anger, ignorance, and arrogance, are also like this.

人之著欲无不丧命。所以然者？皆由意断心惑之所致。是以圣人先制婬欲。是故说曰，断欲不遗余，如拔不牢固，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。略说其要，贪欲瞋恚愚痴憍慢，亦复如是。

Love arises and overflows, like a snake containing poison, the
bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

爱生而流溢，　　犹蛇含毒药，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

People follow their loving thoughts and do not restrain themselves. Gradually, from the desire realm up to the three realms of existence, they transmigrate through the five destinies and are not free from the four modes of birth. The reason for discussing the bhikṣu's superiority over others is that others refer to the six sense objects and this refers to the six sense faculties. The bhikṣu is able to extinguish this and that, like a snake shedding its old skin.

人随爱意不自禁制，渐从欲界乃至三有，流转五趣不离四生。所以论比丘胜彼此者，彼者六尘此者六情，比丘能灭彼此者，如蛇脱故皮。

Those who have cut off thoughts and contemplation do not create their
minds internally. The bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

诸有断想观，　　内不造其心，比丘胜彼此，　　如蛇脱故皮。

There are three kinds of contemplation: contemplation of desire, contemplation of anger, and contemplation of ignorance. Being able to extinguish these is called the way of a Daoist practitioner. Therefore, it is said: Those who have cut off thoughts and contemplation do not create their minds internally. The bhikṣu surpasses others, like a snake shedding its old skin.

观有三观：欲观、恚观、无明观，能灭此者乃谓为道士。是故说曰，诸有断想观，内不造其心，比丘胜彼此，如蛇脱故皮。

Upholding the precepts is called a bhikṣu. Having emptiness, one then practices
meditation. The practitioner thoroughly investigates its source. Non-conditioning is the most blissful.

持戒谓比丘，　　有空乃行禅，行者究其源，　　无为最为乐。

Bhikṣus practice with deportment as the basis. Precepts are used to restrain the body and robes are used as Dharma robes. Their practices accord with the sages of the past. Having emptiness and concentration, then it is called meditation. Not abandoning the provisional name. Like those practitioners who receive and then believe and understand, discerning its meaning, seeking the place of non-conditioning and happiness, without the suffering of hunger, cold, and affliction. Therefore, it is said: Upholding the precepts is called a bhikṣu. Having emptiness, one then practices meditation. The practitioner thoroughly investigates its source. Non-conditioning is the most blissful.

比丘执行以威仪为本，戒以检形服以法衣，所行法则不违先圣，有空定意然后名为禅，不舍假号。如彼行人受则信解分别其义，求于无为快乐之处，无有饥寒苦恼之患。是故说曰，持戒谓比丘，有空乃行禅，行者究其源，无为最为乐也。

Bhikṣus endure and discern suffering, and distinguish bedding and utensils. They
should be mindful of non-negligence, cutting off all attachment without remainder.

比丘忧忍忧，　　分别床卧具，当念无放逸，　　断有爱无余。

Bhikṣus practice, not rejoicing in pleasure, not suffering in hardship, with no increase or decrease in mind towards benefit, decline, defamation, or praise. In a quiet place, single-mindedly sitting upright, the mind does not wander. Cutting off all afflictions, mindful without attachment. Therefore, it is said: Bhikṣus endure and discern suffering, and distinguish bedding and utensils. They should be mindful of non-negligence, cutting off all attachment without remainder.

比丘修行，处乐不以为欢，遭难不以为苦，利衰毁誉无增减心，在闲静处一意端坐心不流驰，断诸结使念无想著，是故说曰，比丘忧忍忧，分别床卧具，当念无放逸，断有爱无余也。◎
