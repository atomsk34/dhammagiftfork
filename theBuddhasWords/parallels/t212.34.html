A brahman is not just one who goes naked, lives in the wilds, or sleeps on thorns.

所谓梵志，　　不但倮形，居崄卧棘，名为梵志。

At that time, a bhikṣu came to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and said to the World-Honored One, "Yes, World-Honored One! From now on, I allow all disciples to go naked and not wear clothes." The World-Honored One said, "Oh! The foolish are stubborn and do not follow the Dharma and Vinaya. This is the method of the Brahmins, not the practice of the inner treasury. People have a sense of shame, and there are then distinctions of high and low. They know there are parents, brothers, and sisters. Why speak of going naked and wandering in the world?" At that time, another bhikṣu came to where the Buddha was, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and said to the World-Honored One, "Yes, World-Honored One! From now on, I allow all practitioners to keep their hair." The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "Oh! The foolish are stubborn and do not follow the Dharma and Vinaya. This is the method of the Brahmins, not the practice of the inner treasury." Another bhikṣu came to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and said to the World-Honored One, "Yes, World-Honored One! I allow all practitioners to smear their bodies with white ash." Another bhikṣu said to the World-Honored One, "From now on, I allow all practitioners to eat air and not eat food." Another bhikṣu said to the World-Honored One, "From now on, I allow all practitioners to go naked and sleep in the open air." The World-Honored One said, "Oh! The foolish are stubborn." Another bhikṣu bowed his head to the Buddha's feet and said to the World-Honored One, "Yes, World-Honored One! From now on, I allow all practitioners to bathe in a bathing pool to purify themselves." The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "This method does not lead to the Way.

尔时有一比丘至世尊所，头面礼足白世尊言：「唯然世尊！自今以后，听诸弟子皆悉倮形不著衣服。」世尊告曰：「咄！愚所戾不应法律，此梵志之法，非是内藏所修行也。人怀惭愧，便有尊卑高下，知有父母兄弟，何为复说倮形行世？」尔时复有一异比丘诣佛所，头面礼足白世尊言：「唯然世尊！自今以后，听诸道人各留头发。」佛告比丘：「咄！愚所戾不应法律，此梵志之法，非是内藏所修行也。」复有异比丘诣世尊所，头面礼足前白世尊言：「唯然世尊！听诸道人皆白灰涂身。」复有异比丘白世尊言：「自今以后听诸道人服气不食。」复有比丘白世尊言：「自今以后听诸道人倮形露地卧。」世尊告曰：「咄！愚所戾。」复有异比丘头面礼足白世尊言：「唯然世尊！自今以后，听诸道人在浴池沐浴清净。」佛告比丘：「不以此法得至于道。」

Abandoning the body without support, not reciting different words, eliminating the two extremes, this is called a brahman.

弃身无猗，　　不诵异言，两行以除，是谓梵志。

Formerly, the Buddha was in the Deer Park of the Sage in the country of Varanasi. At that time, the World-Honored One had just ordained the five bhikshus. At that time, there was an elder in the country of Varanasi named Yishu. His family was of noble lineage, wealthy, and had abundant treasures. His appearance was upright and handsome, unparalleled in the world. One day, he suddenly had a profound insight. He looked at his family members and servants, thinking they were like dead bodies, not worth thinking about. He viewed his own body as no different from a corpse in a charnel ground. He immediately rose from his seat and said, "The delusions are deep and profound, not distinguishing between illusions and transformations." At that time, the elder then abandoned his family and fled the city, taking off his lapis lazuli shoes worth ten thousand, and crossed the river to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha, "The affairs of the world are changeable and not one. All things are illusory and cannot be relied upon. I now take refuge and seek the place of peace and happiness without taints." The Buddha told the elder, "Excellent, excellent! Noble son! The Dharma of the sages is very vast and inclusive. It is exactly what you wish for." At that time, the elder heard the teaching of the Tathāgata, and was overjoyed, unable to contain himself. At that time, the World-Honored One gradually taught him the Dharma, which included discussions on giving, precepts, rebirth in heaven, the impurity of desire, and the great suffering of taints. When the elder heard this Dharma, all his dust and defilements were extinguished, and he attained the pure Dharma eye. He saw the Dharma, attained the Dharma, and accomplished all dharmas. He then rose from his seat, again took refuge, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and said to the World-Honored One, "Yes, Heavenly among the Heavenly! Please allow me to leave the household life and practice the path." The Buddha told the elder, "Welcome, bhikshu!" His beard and hair fell off by themselves, and he naturally donned the Dharma robes. He again heard the teaching of the Dharma and attained arahantship. At that time, the elder's family, parents, brothers, children, and attendants, with a solemn procession of elephants and horses, pursued and searched for Elder Yishu. They reached the river and saw his lapis lazuli shoes. The father thought to himself, "My son must have crossed the river. There is no doubt about it. The reason I know this is because he took off these lapis lazuli shoes worth billions. I will now cross the river to search for him." He then crossed the river and saw the World-Honored One from afar

昔佛在波罗奈国仙人鹿野苑中。尔时世尊度五比丘未经数日。尔时波罗奈国有一长者，名曰夜输，种姓豪族饶财多宝，颜貌端正世之无双。欻一日之中得非常观，自观家里男女之属，斯如死身无一可念，视己形体冢间无异，即从坐起并作是说：「惑愚至深不别幻化。」尔时长者即自舍家逃走出城，脱琉璃履屣价直一万，即渡江水奔趣世尊，头面礼足在一面立寻白佛言：「世事多故变易非一，万物幻化不可恃怙，我今自归，欲求无为安乐之处。」佛告长者：「善哉善哉！族姓子！贤圣法中甚大宽弘，正是汝身之所愿乐。」尔时长者闻如来教，欢喜踊跃不能自胜。尔时世尊渐与说法，所谓论者，施论戒论生天之论，欲不净想，漏为大患。尔时长者闻斯法已，即于坐上诸尘垢尽得法眼净，彼以见法得法成就诸法，即从坐起重自归命，头面礼足白世尊言：「唯然天中天！听在道次出家学道。」佛告长者：「善来比丘！」须发自落自然法服，重闻说法得罗汉道。尔时长者家中父母兄弟男女，仪从严驾象马追迹，求觅夜输长者，到江水侧见琉璃履。父自思惟：「我子将渡江水必然不疑，所以知其然，今脱此琉璃履价直亿万，吾今渡江所在求觅。」即渡江水，遥见世尊光相炳然，至世尊所头面礼足，白世尊言：「唯然世尊！颇见夜输童子游此过乎？」佛以神足隐彼夜输比丘使父不见，佛告长者：「汝今求子不如自求，汝但速坐吾与汝说法。」长者寻坐，佛为说法，即于坐上诸尘垢尽得法眼净。尔时世尊即舍三昧使父见子，父告子曰：「汝速还家，汝母愁苦恐汝不还。」佛告长者：「止止长者！勿作斯语。云何长者！如有修行之人，本在学地爱欲未尽，后得无学离于学地，欲使无学之人习于学法，于长者意云何！为可尔乎？」长者对曰：「不也。世尊！」佛告长者：「汝子今日以得无著住无学地，长者当知以得无著，焉得还家习于五欲？」长者闻之欢喜踊跃，即起礼子五体投地，自归真人永无所著。尔时世尊即与长者而说斯偈：

Abandoning the body without support, not reciting different words, eliminating the two extremes, this is called a brahman.

「弃身无猗，　　不诵异言，两行以除，是谓梵志。

The purity of the present world, the impurity of the future
world, without practice and without abandonment, this is called a brahman.

今世行净，　　后世无秽，无习无舍，　　是谓梵志。」

People hold wrong views until death without changing, those who hold the view of permanence are not associated with the view of annihilation, the view of annihilation is not associated with the view of permanence, being able to abandon these views and not clinging to the three times, therefore it is said: "The purity of the present world, the impurity of the future world, without practice and without abandonment, this is called a brahman.

人执邪见至死不改，计常之人不与断灭见相应，断灭见不与计常见相应，能舍此见不著三世，是故说曰：「今世行净，后世无秽，无习无舍，是谓梵志。」

If one relies on and loves, the mind has no attachment, having already abandoned and being correct, this is the cessation of suffering.

若猗与爱，　　心无所著，已舍已正，是灭终苦。

Although those who are initially practicing may be in the stage of learning, they are not yet able to discern and contemplate the path and its fruits, clearly understanding each one without losing the thread. They have not yet attained what is to be attained, and have not yet obtained what is to be obtained. Therefore it is said: "If one relies on and loves, the mind has no attachment, having already abandoned and being correct, this is the cessation of suffering.

初习行之人虽在学次，未能分别思惟道果一一明了不失其绪，未获者获、未得者得，是故说曰，若猗与爱，心无所著，已舍已正，是灭终苦。

Those who have nothing to rely on, always practice right
view, and constantly contemplate the exhaustion of defilements, are called Brahmins.

诸有无所猗，　　恒习于正见，常念尽有漏，　　是谓为梵志。

It is like a large elephant emerging from a small hole. It wants to exit the city gate but cannot fit through. The people who see it are all surprised and say to the elephant, "You can now come and go without difficulty through this small hole, but you cannot fit through the city gate." Therefore, the sage uses this as a metaphor. Although sentient beings may leave home and practice the Dharma, they cannot exhaust defilements and attain the undefiled, the liberation of the mind and the liberation of wisdom. Therefore it is said: "Those who have nothing to rely on, always practice right view, and constantly contemplate the exhaustion of defilements, are called Brahmins.

犹如大象从寸孔出，欲得出城门不容象，众人见之各各惊愕，谓彼象曰：「汝今出于寸孔往来无难，然欲出城反更不受。」是以圣人借以为喻，众生之类虽得出家修习道法，不能尽有漏成无漏，心解脱智慧解脱，是故说曰，诸有无所猗，恒习于正见，常念尽有漏，是谓为梵志。

The foolish ones accept the hair and beard, as well as bedding and utensils
but harbor greedy and defiled thoughts within. What do they seek in external adornment?

愚者受猥发，　　并及床卧具，内怀贪浊意，　　文饰外何求？

The foolish ones do not realize that they are nurturing their hair. The reason for shaving the hair is to shave off afflictions, not just to shave the hair. Foolish people are deluded and nurture their hair as an adornment. The Dharma of the Buddhas of the past, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, was transmitted to each other. Shaving the beard and hair and wearing the Dharma robes properly has been so since ancient times, not just today. Today, foolish people are attached to bedding and utensils. However, in my Dharma, it is prescribed to have only three robes and not keep any extra. Living under trees and in charnel grounds is considered normal. The detailed explanation is as in the original text. Harboring wrong views within and giving rise to greedy and defiled thoughts, while adorning oneself externally, claiming to be flawless. Abandoning delusion and following the path, the Dharma is not deluded. Therefore it is said: "The foolish ones accept the hair and beard, as well as bedding and utensils, but harbor greedy and defiled thoughts within. What do they seek in external adornment?"

愚者不自觉长养其发，所以剃发者剃其结使，非但剃发，愚人执迷长养其发以为文饰。过去恒沙诸佛之法，各各相授，剃除须发法服齐整，自古有之非适今日。今日愚人贪著卧具，然我法中制以三衣不畜遗余，树下冢间以此为常，广说如其本。内怀邪见兴贪浊意，外自文饰谓为无瑕，舍迷就道其法不惑，是故说曰，愚者受猥发，并及床卧具，内怀贪浊意，文饰外何求也。

Wearing tattered and evil robes, personally upholding the practice of the Dharma, dwelling in seclusion and contemplating, this is called a brahman.

被服弊恶，　　躬承法行，闲居思惟，是谓梵志。

The practitioner wears coarse and evil robes, not attached to adornment. Contemplating the practice of the Dharma, without seeking greed. Restraining speech and being frugal, not causing disputes between oneself and others. Therefore it is said: "Wearing tattered and evil robes, personally upholding the practice of the Dharma, dwelling in seclusion and contemplating, this is called a brahman.

修行之人，被服麁恶不著文饰，思惟法行无所贪求，节言省语不鬪乱彼此，是故说曰，被服弊恶，躬承法行，闲居思惟，是谓梵志。

Seeing the foolish come and go, falling into the pit and suffering, wishing to single-handedly cross
the shore, not liking others' words, only extinguishing and not arising, this is called a brahman.

见痴往来，　　堕壍受苦，欲单渡岸，不好他语，唯灭不起，　　是谓梵志。

When a person holds an ignorant mind and is not enlightened, they are also unable to attain the fruit of the path in succession. They always dwell in a suspicious and impure place, which is not the person of pure practice. Cutting off all afflictions and completely extinguishing them without remainder, this is called a brahman.

夫人执痴意不开悟，亦复不能越次取证，恒在嫌疑不净之地，此则非净行之人。断诸有漏永尽无余，是谓梵志。是故说曰，见痴往来，堕壍受苦，欲单渡岸，不好他语，唯灭不起，是谓梵志。

Crossing the stream, without desire like Brahma, knowing the end of actions, this is called a brahman.

截流而渡，　　无欲如梵，知行以尽，是谓梵志。

If bathing the body with water could lead to the path, all water-natured beings would attain the path. But it is not through bathing that one attains the path. Distinguishing all dharmas, examining their meaning, pure and flawless, with the wisdom and practices of the aggregates completely extinguished without remainder, therefore it is said: Crossing the stream, without desire like Brahma, knowing the end of actions, this is called a brahman.

若使以水沐浴其身得至于道者，水性之类皆称于道，但非沐浴而至于道，分别诸法审谛其义，清净无瑕众结智行永尽无余，是故说曰，截流而渡，无欲如梵，知行以尽，是谓梵志。

Not by water's purity, many people bathe, but
can remove bad dharmas, this is called a brahman.

不以水清净，　　多有人沐浴，能除弊恶法，　　是谓为梵志。

People bathing cannot remove the dirt inside the belly, completely removing bad dharmas and not creating them again, is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: Not by water's purity, many people bathe, but can remove bad dharmas, this is called a brahman.

夫人沐浴不能去腹里垢，尽除恶法更亦不造，乃名为梵志。是故说曰，不以水清净，多有人沐浴，能除弊恶法，是谓为梵志。

Not by shaving to become a śramaṇa, or by calling oneself a brahman, but
by being able to extinguish all evils, then one is a person of the Way.

非剃为沙门，　　称吉为梵志，谓能灭众恶，　　是则为道人。

What is called a śramaṇa is not necessarily one who shaves off beard and hair. If one has right practice and accords with the Dharma, then one should be a śramaṇa. As for being a brahman, if one calls oneself auspicious all day and is reborn in the Brahmā Heaven, then one sees that all people will be reborn there. But those who call themselves auspicious and are reborn in the Brahmā Heaven are able to extinguish all evils and cultivate holy life. Therefore it is said: Not by shaving to become a śramaṇa, or by calling oneself a brahman, but by being able to extinguish all evils, then one is a person of the Way.

所谓沙门者，未必剃除须发，内有正行应于律法乃应为沙门。夫为梵志，终日称吉得生梵天者，见人尽当生于彼处，但彼称吉生于梵天，谓能灭众恶修清净行。是故说曰，非剃为沙门，称吉为梵志，谓能灭众恶，是则为道人。

They are without duality, pure and flawless, with the bonds of desire untied. This is called a brahman.

彼以无二，　　清净无瑕，诸欲结解，是谓梵志。

They completely abandon all evil dharmas. Wherever they go, their words do not reach killing, they do not harm anything, and there is no injury or damage. They are pure and flawless, forever free from all bonds. Therefore it is said: They are without duality, pure and flawless, with the bonds of desire untied. This is called a brahman.

尽舍一切弊恶之法，出入行来周旋之处，言不及杀、不害一切无所伤损，清净无瑕永无诸缚，是故说曰，彼以无二，清净无瑕，诸欲结解，是谓梵志。

To emerge from evil is to be a brahman, to enter the right is to be
a shramana. To abandon the impure practices of the self is then to renounce the household.

出恶为梵志，　　入正为沙门，弃我众秽行，　　是则为舍家。

The practice of a brahman is to eliminate all evil dharmas. Internally and externally, it is pure and clear, with all impurities completely exhausted. Without harboring hopes or arrogance towards others, the mind is stable and immovable, awakening to the fundamental nature of all dharmas. The practice of purity has been established, what is to be done has been accomplished, and one will not again accept existence. Cultivating pure practice without any loss, therefore it is said: To emerge from evil is to be a brahman, to enter the right is to be a shramana. To abandon the impure practices of the self is then to renounce the household.

梵志之行去诸恶法，内外清彻众秽永尽，不怀悕望贡高于人，意定不移觉寤一切诸法之本，梵行已立所作已办] cf.【丽】，更不复受有，修清净行无所遗失，是故说曰，出恶为梵志，入正为沙门，弃我众秽行，是则为舍家。

A person without delusion and confusion, without arrogance and ignorance
without greed and the notion of self, is called a brahman.

人无幻惑意，　　无慢无愚惑，无贪无我想，　　是谓为梵志。

While a person is in the world, they do not harbor delusion and confusion. The brahman says to himself: "After a hundred eons, passing through the great ocean, there is naturally delusion and confusion that devours the people of the world." Eliminating all arrogance and not giving rise to attachment, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, is free from the eight worldly dharmas and is not tainted by the world. He is also called a bhikṣu, a śramaṇa, and a buddha. Therefore it is said: A person without delusion and confusion, without arrogance and ignorance, without greed and the notion of self, is called a brahman.

人之在世不怀幻惑，梵志自谓言：「百劫一过大海之中，自然有幻惑食噉天下人。」去诸憍慢不兴著想，如来，至真，等正觉，离世八法不染于世，亦名为比丘亦名为沙门亦名佛。是故说曰，人无幻惑意，无慢无愚惑，无贪无我想，是谓为梵志。

I do not speak of a brahman who is born from parents
for they have many faults. When they are extinguished, they become a brahman.

我不说梵志，　　托父母生者，彼多众瑕秽，　　灭则为梵志。

The so-called brahman born from parents has many faults. Or, having renounced the world and left worldly affairs, they practice pure conduct, giving without discrimination, equally and without duality, without mixed thoughts. Or, when giving, they seek to become a king or be reborn in heaven. This is called giving with mixed thoughts. Giving without mixed thoughts is for the sake of all, not for oneself. Therefore it is said: I do not speak of a brahman who is born from parents, for they have many faults. When they are extinguished, they become a brahman.

所谓梵志从父母生多诸瑕秽，或复出家离诸世俗，修清净行无选择施，平等无二不杂想施，或复施时求作国王生天，此名杂想之施。无杂想施者，尽为一切不自为己。是故说曰，我不说梵志，托父母生者，彼多众瑕秽，灭则为梵志。

Body, speech, and mind are pure and without fault, able to encompass the three practices - this is called a brahman.

身口与意，　　净无过失，能摄三行，是谓梵志。

Their speech is gentle and free from scolding, discerning the meaning like seeing a pearl in the palm of the hand. Their voice is pure and pleasant to hear, accomplishing much. Pure and without fault, they do not disturb others. Therefore it is said: Body, speech, and mind are pure and without fault, able to encompass the three practices - this is called a brahman.

Being struck by others, being scolded by others, all due to not being patient, leading to this harm. The patient one is supreme in battle. Patience is the good medicine that can cure many illnesses. If there is scolding, silently do not respond. Therefore it is said: Seeing scolding and beating, silently enduring without anger, having the power of patience - this is called a brahman.

If one sees encroachment and deception, one should only think of guarding the precepts, upholding oneself, and regulating oneself. This is called a brahman.

If there are people who are encroached upon and deceived, they do not give rise to evil thoughts or have an angry mind. The good and evil of the world, the long and short, the large and small, without taking or giving, this is called a brahman.

世所善恶，　　修短巨细，无取无与，是谓梵志。

The worldly methods and affairs are various. If one wants to observe people's feelings, one should first collect their words. Speaking of good or evil, one should not keep it in mind. One does not see any length or shortness, width or narrowness, and also does not see any taking or giving. One who fully practices like this is called a brahman.

The body is the basis of action, with no offenses in speech and mind. Being able to accomplish the three aspects is called a brahman.

The body does not kill, the mouth does not speak ill, and the mind is not jealous. Being able to practice these three in the boiling world of the five turbidities is called a brahman.

When coming, there is no joy; when going, there is no sorrow. In the midst of the crowd or alone, this is called a brahman.

来不作欢，　　去亦不忧，于聚离聚，是谓梵志。

The practitioner holds the mind firmly, unmoved by praise or blame. Seeing someone coming, there is no joy; seeing someone leaving, there is no sorrow. Whether in a crowd or alone, the mind is always equal, without high or low. Therefore it is said: When coming, there is no joy; when going, there is no sorrow. In the midst of the crowd or alone, this is called a brahman.

来亦不欢，　　去亦不忧，无忧清净，是谓梵志。

Seeing those who love and those who do not love, there is no joy. Why is this? Fearing attachment and the arising of causes and conditions. Therefore it is said: When coming, there is no joy; when going, there is no sorrow. Free from sorrow and pure, this is called a brahman.

Cutting off affection and love, leaving home without desires, the end of affection and existence, this is called a brahman.

Like a practitioner cultivating the path, permanently cutting off affection and love, leaving home without desires, wandering freely without obstruction, exhausting all affection and existence, lacking the taints of the three realms. Only one who can fully possess this is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: Cutting off affection and love, leaving home without desires, the end of affection and existence, this is called a brahman.

Adapting to the absence of that, that and that are non-existent. Abandoning greed and desire, this is called a brahman.

适彼无彼，　　彼彼以无，舍离贪欲，是谓梵志。

What is called that refers to the external six sense bases, what is called non-existent refers to the internal six sense bases. The practitioner contemplates the internal and external sense faculties, all of which are empty and still. Abandoning greed and desire, not giving rise to the six sense faculties. One who fully possesses the foundation of these practices is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: Adapting to the absence of that, that and that are non-existent. Abandoning greed and desire, this is called a brahman.

Only those who practice meditation can transcend the difficulties of birth and death, eliminate wrong views and doubts, and be free from hesitation. They abandon the fetters of afflictions and receive the fetters of purity. One who can accomplish this is called a brahman.

生死久远涉苦无数，唯有禅定之人越此生死之难，去邪疑意无复犹豫，舍烦恼结使，受清净结使。能具此者故名为梵志。是故说曰，城以堑为固，往来受其苦，欲适渡彼岸，不肯受他语，唯能灭不起，是谓名梵志。

A person who can cut off craving in this and future lives, and whose craving has already been exhausted, is called a brahman.

人能断爱，　　今世后世，有爱已尽，是谓梵志。

As long as the root of craving is not exhausted, one will not attain the path. Only when the root of craving is exhausted can one attain the path. Those who seek the path but do not cut off the fetters of the three realms will not attain the path. Only by being able to cut off the root of craving can one attain the path. One who can accomplish this is called a brahman.

爱根未尽则不至道，爱根已尽者乃能为道，欲求道者不断三界结使则不至于道，能断爱根然后乃至于道，能具足此者故名为梵志。是故说曰，人能断爱，今世后世，有爱已尽，是谓梵志。

A person who has no hope in this and future lives, and who is without hope, is called a brahman.

人无希望，　　今世后世，以无希望，是谓梵志。

What is meant by hope? All things in the world are what people hope for. However, because of this hope, it is not yet cut off. As long as one's present body is not dead and exists in the world, even if one takes one's life in the next life, the body and spirit will depart without further hope. One who can accomplish such merit is called a brahman.

所谓希望者，天下万物皆人之所希望，然此希望故未断绝，如今现身未死见存于世，正使后世取其命终，身死神逝无复希望。能如此功德具足者，名曰梵志。是故说曰，人无希望，今世后世，以无希望，是谓梵志。

Not knowing the gods, gandharvas, and asuras, and knowing immeasurable contemplation, is called a brahman.

自不识知，　　天揵沓和，知无量观，是谓梵志。

When the Buddha Tathāgata was sitting in meditation, the gods and worldly people did not know where the Buddha was. There was a bhikṣu named Tārāpīḍa who went to where the World-Honored One was and praised the Tathāgata with this verse:

当佛如来坐禅之时，诸天世人竟不知佛今为所在。有一比丘名曰多耆奢，往至世尊所，便以此偈而赞如来曰：

I take refuge in the most honored among humans, I take refuge in
the best among humans. I do not know, World-Honored One, what kind of
meditation is this? I only pray, Heaven among Heavens, to expound its meaning.

「归命人中尊，　　归命人中上，不审今世尊，　　为因何等禅？唯愿天中天，　　敷演其教义。」

The Buddha himself said: "Among the practices of purity, there is none who surpasses me. The reason I know this is because the meditation, liberation, right concentration, and samādhi are still the common methods of the world. Even the gods, dragons, and spirits cannot know where I am, let alone the wondrous methods of the Buddha's activities and wisdom. Can the gods, dragons, and spirits know where I am?" Therefore it is said: "Not knowing oneself, the gods and Gandharvas know the immeasurable contemplation. This is called a brahman."

如来自说：「梵行之中无有出我者，所以知其然，禅解脱正受定意，犹是世之常法，诸天龙神不能知我之所在，况我当行佛事众智之妙门？天龙鬼神能知我处乎？」是故说曰，自不识知，天揵沓和，知无量观，是谓梵志。

Knowing one's own past lives, seeing the paths of gods and humans, knowing the source of suffering in birth, the mind of wisdom is eternally tranquil.

自识宿命，　　见天人道，知生苦源，智心永寂。

Knowing one's own past lives for countless eons, observing the affairs of hell and heaven, others cannot. Only the Buddha, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One, can observe the trichiliocosm as if seeing a pearl in the palm of one's hand. Knowing the source of suffering in birth and thoroughly understanding its origin, with swift wisdom one quickly attains the path of arahantship, with no obstruction in one's thoughts. Therefore it is said: "Knowing one's own past lives, seeing the paths of gods and humans, knowing the source of suffering in birth, the mind of wisdom is eternally tranquil."

自识宿命无数劫事，观知地狱天上之事，余者不能，唯有佛、如来、至真、等正觉，观三千大千世界如掌观珠，知生苦源究畅其本，捷疾之智速成罗汉道，随意所念而无流滞。是故说曰，自识宿命，见天人道，知生苦源，智心永寂。

Knowing the mind is liberated, free from desires and
unattached, accomplishing the three insights, this is called a brahman.

自知心解脱，　　脱欲无所著，三明以成就，　　是谓为梵志。

Just as a practitioner knows the thoughts in the mind, clearly knowing those who are liberated and those who are not, and forever attaining liberation from the thoughts and activities of desire. The so-called three insights are: knowing one's own past lives, the divine eye, and the exhaustion of taints. If one fully possesses such practice, one is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: "Knowing the mind is liberated, free from desires and unattached, accomplishing the three insights, this is called a brahman."

如彼行人知心所念，解脱者不解脱者皆悉明知，欲想诸行永得解脱。所谓三明者，自识宿命、天眼、漏尽，若具足如是行者名曰梵志。是故说曰，自知心解脱，脱欲无所著，三明以成就，是谓为梵志。

Knowing one's own past lives, understanding the causes and conditions of
sentient beings, the Tathāgata Buddha is unattached, this is called a brahman.

自识于宿命，　　知众生因缘，如来佛无著，　　是谓为梵志。

At that time, the Tathāgata knew countless matters, observing the nature and conduct of sentient beings, each and every one clearly, knowing the birth and death of all, just like rain from the heavens universally moistening the world. At that time, the World-Honored One observed the categories of birth and death in the same way, without not observing and training those who are born and die.

是时，如来知无数事，观众生性行一一分明，生者死者皆悉了知，犹如天雨普润世界，是时，世尊观生死之类亦复如是，生者死者无不观练。

At that time, the World-Honored One was alone with Śāriputra in a quiet room. At that time, there was someone who had already taken rebirth and was in the intermediate state, with the spirit not changing. The Buddha told Śāriputra, "Now you observe this intermediate state consciousness-spirit. From where did it come? If it moves again, where will it be?" At that time, Śāriputra immediately entered the four dhyāna concentrations and observed from where this person's spirit came and where it would go. At that time, Śāriputra did not know from where this person came or where it would go. At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra, "What you see now does not reach the realm of the Buddhas. This spirit came from this countless world, which is not something you can see with your spiritual power." The Buddha told Śāriputra, "You should further observe where this spirit will be reborn." At that time, Śāriputra again entered samādhi, but did not know where the spirit would go. Śāriputra then arose from samādhi and said to the Buddha, "Today, entering samādhi and observing the world, I do not know where the spirit will go." The Buddha told Śāriputra, "This spirit will today pass through one hundred million worlds and be reborn in such-and-such a family with such-and-such a name. What the Tathāgata sees is not something that śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas can know. Only the Tathāgata, the Perfectly Enlightened One, attains this knowledge of past lives." Therefore it is said: Knowing one's own past lives, understanding the causes and conditions of sentient beings, the Tathāgata Buddha is unattached, this is called a brahman.

尔时世尊与舍利弗，在闲静室独共游处。尔时有人已取命终，处在中阴精神不移。佛告舍利弗：「汝今观此中阴中识神，为从何许中来？设复迁转为处何所？」是时，舍利弗即入四禅定意，观此人神为从何来？设当迁转为处何处？时，舍利弗不知此人为从何来？为趣何处？尔时世尊告舍利弗曰：「汝今所见不及诸佛境界，此神所从来处此无数世界，非汝神力之所能见。」佛告舍利弗：「汝复观此精神当生何处？」时舍利弗复入三昧，而不知精神所凑，舍利弗即从三昧起，前白佛言：「今日入定遍观世界不知神之所凑。」佛告舍利弗：「此神今日复当过一亿世界，当生某甲家姓某字某。如来所见，非是声闻辟支佛所及知，宿命通唯有如来等正觉得此宿命通。」是故说曰，自识于宿命，知众生因缘，如来佛无著，是谓为梵志。

Having completely severed all bonds, yet not being troubled
the Tathāgata Buddha is unattached, this is called a brahman.

尽断一切结，　　亦不有热恼，如来佛无著，　　是谓为梵志。

All sentient beings who have severed all bonds and afflictions, although Arhats and Pratyekabuddhas have severed bonds and afflictions, they still have similar bonds. The World-Honored Buddhas have no similarity. Therefore the Tathāgata Buddha is unattached. Therefore it is said: Having completely severed all bonds, yet not being troubled, the Tathāgata Buddha is unattached, this is called a brahman.

诸有众生断一切结使，罗汉辟支虽断结使，由有相似结在，诸佛世尊无有相似，是故如来佛无所著。是故说曰。尽断一切结。亦不有热恼。如来佛无著。是谓为梵志。

The sage is supreme among dragons, the great sage is
most honored, bathed by countless Buddhas, this is called a brahman.

仙人龙中上，　　大仙最为尊，无数佛沐浴，　　是谓为梵志。

As for the so-called sages, they attain the five supernatural powers and are the most honored among the masses, with nothing surpassing them. Internally and externally clear and pure, without any flaws. Sages are also called elephants, nurturing the physical form and being the largest among animals, with a firm intention and able to repel all enemies. Bathed by countless ones. As for the so-called bathing, it refers to the eight liberations and the pond of right bathing, removing all dust and defilements without any bonds and afflictions. The Tathāgata extends his hand, and wherever his hand reaches, dust and defilements do not adhere. Evil people cannot take advantage of him. Therefore it is said: The sage is supreme among dragons, the great sage is most honored, bathed by countless Buddhas, this is called a brahman.

所谓仙人者，得五通道，在群最尊无有出上，内外清彻无有众瑕。仙者亦名为象，长育形体兽中最大，执意刚强能却众敌。无数沐浴，所谓沐浴者八解正浴池，去诸尘垢无有结使。如来舒手，手所及处尘垢不著，伺察恶人不得其便。是故说曰，仙人龙中上，大仙最为尊，无数佛沐浴，是谓为梵志。

Having exhausted all, crossed the stream of the undefiled, and gone beyond the shore, this is called a brahman.

所有尽无，　　渡流无漏，从此越岸，是谓梵志。

That practitioner completely transcends all dharmas, discerning and clearly understanding that all worldly things are ultimately nonexistent. As for the so-called "stream," there are four names for it: first, the stream of desire, second, the stream of existence, third, the stream of ignorance, and fourth, the stream of views. Crossing over these four streams is then called the practice of the undefiled. Even Arhats and Pratyekabuddhas still contemplate emptiness, signlessness, and wishlessness, as well as the dharmas of patience, warmth, and the peak. Although they can contemplate the defiled worldly dharmas, the mind is still bound. Sometimes, they may first contemplate the undefiled and then the defiled. Therefore, the Tathāgata's deep concealment is greatly lacking. The Tathāgata, the Great Sage, focuses his mind on concentration, going from existence to nonexistence. In the undefiled dharmas, he contemplates without ever being lacking. He attains the dhāraṇīs, strong memory without forgetting, the ten powers, the four fearlessnesses, great compassion, the three unobstructed paths, and the practices of the supernormal powers. These are called the practices of the Tathāgata, not the practices of Arhats and Pratyekabuddhas. Therefore it is said: Having exhausted all, crossed the stream of the undefiled, gone beyond the shore, this is called a brahman.

彼修行人，都越一切诸法，审谛分明解世所有悉无所有。所谓流者，流有四名：一名欲流，二名有流，三名无明流，四名见流。渡此四流者，然后乃得无漏之行。罗汉辟支犹尚思惟空无想愿忍煖顶法，虽可思惟有漏俗法意结所在，或有是时，欲念无漏先念有漏，是以如来深藏则有大阙。如来大圣系意禅定从有至无，于无漏法观未始有阙，得诸总持强记不忘，十力、四无畏、大慈大悲、三无碍道及神足行，是谓如来所修之法，非罗汉辟支佛所修之法。是故说曰，所有尽无，渡流无漏，从此越岸，是谓梵志。

Without meditation, without speaking, and not contemplating evil, meditation and wisdom are pure. This is called a brahman.

无禅无说，　　亦不念恶，禅智清净，是谓梵志。

That practitioner does not contemplate evil meditation. As for those who enter meditation, they are without words or speech, always contemplating good dharmas. Even if they see scolding, they only guard the Dharma. If they attain the meditation associated with taste and the intermediate meditation, they hold to it and guard it without any disturbance or annoyance. Being able to fully practice this is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: Without meditation, without speaking, and not contemplating evil, meditation and wisdom are pure. This is called a brahman.

彼修行人不念恶禅，夫入禅之人，无言无说常思善法。设见骂詈但守其法，若得味相应禅及中间禅，执意守之无所娆恼，能具足此行者故名为梵志。是故说曰，无禅无说，亦不念恶，禅智清净，是谓梵志。

The monk's cemetery robe, contemplating desire as unreal, sitting under a
tree in an empty and secluded place, is called a brahman.

比丘塜间衣，　　观于欲非真，坐树空闲处，　　是谓为梵志。

There are four kinds of cemetery robes: first, the robe worn by those who leave home to study; second, the robe given by lay donors, which is accepted and guarded; third, the robe made from scraps of discarded cloth; fourth, the robe from the cemetery, which is defiled and impure. The Tathāgata first studied by wearing the robe of leaving home. He contemplated desire as unreal and abandoned sixty thousand wives. He renounced the position of a wheel-turning king, left home to study the path, sat under a tree in a secluded place, subdued Māra, the evil one, and defeated eighteen koṭis of his followers. One who is able to fully possess these many virtues is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: The monk's cemetery robe, contemplating desire as unreal, sitting under a tree in an empty and secluded place, is called a brahman.

塜间衣有四种：一者发家著衣出家学者，二者檀越施衣受而守护，三者百纳拾诸遗余，四者塜间污秽不净。如来初学发家著衣，观欲非真舍六万夫人，弃转轮王位，出家学道在闲静处，坐树王下，降伏魔王破十八亿众。能具此众德者故名为梵志。是故说曰，比丘塜间衣，观于欲非真，坐树空闲处，是谓为梵志。

A person without knowledge, without speech or expression, with a cold body and no warmth, is called a brahman.

人无识知，　　无语无说，体冷无煖，是谓梵志。

The Tathāgata appeared in the world, knowing nothing that was not known and encompassing nothing that was not encompassed. "Without speech or expression" means forever eliminating doubts and not harboring hesitation. All afflictions and fetters are completely extinguished without remainder, attaining the cessation of the sweet dew. Being able to fully possess these many practices is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: A person without knowledge, without speech or expression, with a cold body and no warmth, is called a brahman.

如来出世，无事不知无事不包，无语无说者，永除狐疑不怀犹豫，诸烦恼结使永尽无余，逮甘露灭，能具此众行故名为梵志。是故说曰，人无识知，无语无说，体冷无煖，是谓梵志。

Abandoning the household life, without the fear of a household, attaining the cessation of the sweet dew, is called a brahman.

弃捐家居，　　无家之畏，逮甘露灭，是谓梵志。

Therefore, those who live in a household provide a place for people to live and support themselves. The body is the house of various afflictions. Therefore, the sages teach people to leave home, dwell in tranquility, and seek the cessation of the sweet dew. Being able to fully possess these many virtues is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: Abandoning the household life, without the fear of a household, attaining the cessation of the sweet dew, is called a brahman.

所以居家者，安处人民得自生活，身者众结之屋室，是以圣人教人离家，在于闲静求甘露灭，能具如是众德者故曰梵志。是故说曰，弃捐家居，无家之畏，逮甘露灭，是谓梵志。

Cutting off worldly affairs, with no harsh words, examining the eightfold path, is called a brahman.

断绝世事，　　口无麤言，八道审谛，是谓梵志。

The Tathāgata, the World-Honored One, with his radiant appearance, first turned the wheel of the Dharma. Eighty thousand gods, two kings, seven brāhmaṇas, King Bimbisāra of Magadha and twelve thousand people, in the stone chamber in the realm of Magadha, Śakra Devānām-Indra and twelve thousand gods, in the country of Kuśinagara, the last to be liberated was Subhadra. After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, there will appear an arhat named Upāli, who will liberate countless beings in the meantime, expounding the unobstructed Dharma of the eightfold path. Therefore it is said: Cutting off worldly affairs, with no harsh words, examining the eightfold path, is called a brahman.

如来世尊光相炳著，初转法轮，八万诸天及二王人、梵志七人，摩竭国王洴沙万二千人，摩竭国界石室之中，释提桓因万二千天，拘尸那竭国，最后度须拔。佛灭度后当有罗汉出世，名曰优波掘，于其中间济度众生不可称计，演说八道无碍之法。是故说曰，断绝世事，口无麤言，八道审谛，是谓梵志。

Going far away, wandering alone, hiding without form, being able to subdue what is difficult to subdue, is called a brahman.

远逝独游，　　隐藏无形，难降能降，是谓梵志。

Just as a practitioner gives rise to boundless thoughts and scatters boundless thoughts, the physical body is here while the mind is on the other side of the ocean. It is very difficult to know the shape of the mind by observing it. In the time it takes to snap one's fingers, the mind can travel over countless myriads of koṭis of rivers and mountains. Therefore it is said: going far away, wandering alone. Someone asked: The mind has ten great earth dharmas, and the mind is the eleventh. Why is it said: going far away, wandering alone? The reply was: The mind always follows conditions and follows the previous activities. When the mind is in form and sound, there is no smell, taste, touch, or dharmas. When the mind is in smell, there is no form, taste, touch, or dharmas. When the mind is in taste, there is no form, smell, touch, or dharmas. When the mind is in touch, there is no form, sound, smell, or taste. When the mind is in dharmas, there is no above five things. When the mind is in form, the mind is the basis of dharmas, just as when a king goes out, he is accompanied by a retinue of attendants. But he is only called the king. This is also like this. The mind is formless and has no nest or cave. It is not seen by the flesh eyes of worldly people. It relies on the five aggregates. When the aggregates disperse, it is separated and has no form. The mind is difficult to tame, like drilling wood to make fire. Therefore the sages left teachings for later generations. Those who want to subdue the mind should use a hundred medicines in the morning, a hundred medicines at noon, and a hundred medicines in the evening. Emptiness, signlessness, wishlessness, stopping, contemplating, and extinguishing are used to treat the mind's illness and make it cured. Those who can do this are called brahman.

如彼行人兴无涯之想、散无边之念，身形在此心在海表，人欲观意知其形状者甚为难克。心意流驰弹指之顷，过数千万亿江河山表，是以故说远逝独游。复有问者，心有十大地法，心为十一，何以故说远逝独游乎？报曰：「心者恒逐因缘随前任行，当心在色声，尔时无有香味细滑法；当心在香，尔时无有色味细滑法；心在味，无色香细滑法；心在细滑，尔时无色声香味法；心在法，无上五事。当在色时心为法本，犹如王行羽仪宾从无不备有。但以王为名。此亦如是，心造因缘十法备有但不受名，亦如飞鸟飞行空中依其六翮，然但以鸟为名。此亦如是，心之无形亦无窠窟，非是世人肉眼所见，依止五阴，阴散则离非有形质，心之难化犹木钻钢。是以圣人遗教后生，欲降伏心者，晨用百药中用百药暮用百药，空无想愿止观灭尽，用疗心病使得除愈，能具此者故曰梵志。」是故说曰，远逝独游，隐藏无形，难降能降，是谓梵志。

Formless and invisible, this is also invisible. Understanding
this phrase, thoughts have a basis. Knowing the
exhaustion of afflictions, this is the world's foremost brahman.

无色不可见，　　此亦不可见，解知此句者，　　念则有所由，觉知结使尽，　　是世最梵志。

What is formless and invisible? The mind! The mind gives rise to afflictions and brings about calamities for the body, just like an elephant or horse that is unruly and difficult to tame. Those with eyes use whips and sticks to make them know pain, and then they become docile. The mind gives rise to afflictions, leading to hell, hungry ghosts, and animals. Even if one becomes a human, one is born in a lowly and ugly state, hated by others. Therefore it is said: formless and invisible, this is also invisible. Understanding this phrase, thoughts have a basis. Knowing the exhaustion of afflictions, this is the world's foremost brahman. The reason the Buddhas appear in the world is precisely to subdue this evil mind. The Buddhas have compassion for all, their vast compassion universally covers and illuminates without exception. Although dwelling in the world, they are not stained or attached.

无色不可见者，何者？心也？夫心兴患与身招殃，犹若象马刚强［怡-台＋龙］戾不调，有目之士加于捶杖，使知楚痛，然后调良？人心为患，牵致地狱饿鬼畜生，虽得为人处在卑贱，颜色丑陋为人所嫉，是故说曰？无色不可见，此亦不可见？解知此句者？念则有所由？觉知结使尽？是世最梵志。诸佛世尊所以出世者，正欲降此弊恶之心，诸佛世尊慈慜一切，弘慈普盖靡所不照，虽处于世无所染著。

Cutting off the river of birth and death, able to endure and transcend, self-awakening and emerging from the moat, this is called a brahman.

断生死河，　　能忍超度，自觉出壍，是谓梵志。

Just as practitioners are bound by the five desires, wandering in the river of birth and death, they must rely on the great sage's guidance to reach the other shore from this shore. The Tathāgata's appearance in the world is not without purpose. He must connect with those with karmic affinities before entering parinirvāṇa. The moat refers to the moat of arrogance. Being able to cross this moat is not being bound by arrogance. Being able to possess this is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: cutting off the river of birth and death, able to endure and transcend, self-awakening and emerging from the moat, this is called a brahman.

如彼行人为五欲所系，流转生死之河，要须大圣指授权宜，从此岸得至彼岸。如来降形非事不豫，要接有缘后乃灭度。壍者憍慢之壍，能度此壍不为憍慢所系。能具此者故名为梵志。是故说曰，断生死河，能忍超度，自觉出壍，是谓梵志。

One should seek to cut off the flow and cross over. A brahman has no desires. Observing one's
own faculties internally, this is called a brahman. Being able to know this is also being a brahman.

当求截流渡，　　梵志无有欲，内自观诸情，是] cf.【丽】谓为梵志，能知如是者，　　乃复为梵志。

Just as practitioners who do not cut off the four currents and four abysses of desire will find it difficult to progress on the path, just as a flooded river will inevitably cause harm, a brahman who is greedy and lustful will die and go to evil paths. Therefore the Tathāgata admonishes them to eliminate greed and explains the origin of desire as being defiled and impure, to be cut off, so that one does not run wild. Being able to possess these practices is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: one should seek to cut off the flow and cross over. A brahman has no desires. Observing one's own faculties internally, this is called a brahman. Being able to know this is also called a brahman.

如彼行人不断爱流四驶四渊者，进趣于道不亦难乎？如河暴溢必有所伤，梵志贪欲死趣恶道。是以如来诫以除贪，与说欲本污秽不净，当断诸邪使不流驰。能具此众行者故名为梵志。是故说曰，当求截流渡，梵志无有欲，内自观诸情，是谓为梵志，能知如是者，乃名为梵志。

First removing his mother, the king and the two ministers, conquering all the realms, this is called a brahman.

先去其母，　　王及二臣，尽胜境界，是谓梵志。

"First removing his mother" means that the mind flowing with desire is the source. The undefiled consciousness can remove this illness completely without remainder. "King" refers to arrogance, and the two ministers refer to the stealing of precepts and the view of the body. "Conquering all the realms" means being able to remove all the afflictions of the various bonds. Therefore it is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: first removing his mother, the king and the two ministers, conquering all the realms, this is called a brahman.

先去其母者，爱心流驰以为源本，无漏意识能去斯病使尽无余。王者我慢也，二臣戒盗身见。尽胜境界者，一切诸结使，能去众结之患。故曰为梵志。是故说曰，先去其母，王及二臣，尽胜境界，是谓梵志。

Not beating the brahman, not releasing the brahman, scolding the beating brahman, scolding the releasing one as well.

不捶梵志，　　不放梵志，咄捶梵志，放者亦咄。

As for the so-called brahman, one who attains the path of arahantship should not use hands, swords, or sticks to harm such a true person. "Not releasing the brahman" means that this is a true person who should always be provided with clothing, food, bedding, and medicine for illness, with the four things of offering not being allowed to be lacking. "Scolding the beating brahman" refers to those who commit evil, and "scolding the releasing one as well" refers to those who are also evil people. Not providing them with food, drink, bedding, and medicine for illness, but being able to fulfill these practices is called a brahman. Therefore it is said: not beating the brahman, not releasing the brahman, scolding the beating brahman, scolding the releasing one as well.

所谓梵志者，得阿罗汉道，不得以手卷刀杖加彼真人，不放梵志者，此是真人，恒当供养衣被服饭食床卧具病瘦医药，四事供养令不减少。咄捶梵志，行恶之人，放者亦咄，复是恶人，不留供养饮食床卧具病瘦医药，能具此行故名为梵志。是故说曰，不捶梵志，不放梵志，咄捶梵志，放者亦咄。

Those who know the profound Dharma, whether old or young
carefully uphold precepts and faith, like the brahman who worships fire.

诸有知深法，　　不问老以少，审谛守戒信，　　犹祀火梵志。

In the past, when the Buddha was in the world, he traveled around teaching and transforming. At that time, the bhikṣus were not widely learned. The World-Honored One then had this thought: "Now the bhikṣus are mostly lazy and not diligent. I will also observe the past and future of the three time periods." He knew that in the future there would be bhikṣus who were jealous, angry, and ignorant, not following the teachings of the Way. They would then give rise to slander and harm the Dharma of the Tathāgata, disrespecting their teachers and also not respecting those who expound the Dharma." Therefore the World-Honored One observed the Dharma of the later world and left it in the middle, fearing that there would be old and young who would treat each other with superiority and inferiority without distinction. The old relied on their age, and the young relied on their intelligence. The old would say: "What I have seen with my own eyes is not what you know. What you see now is like a firefly." The young would say: "The old are stubborn and dull, with their minds lost and blocked. I don't know where to turn." The Tathāgata taught: "You should uphold the precepts yourself, just like the brahman who serves fire and burns fire in five places. Serving day and night without missing the proper time, offering incense, flowers, silk, and various other things." Therefore it is said: Those who know the profound Dharma do not ask about old or young. Carefully upholding precepts and faith, they are like the brahman who worships fire. Taking refuge in the Honored One among humans is also like serving the fire god.

昔佛在世周旋教化。时诸比丘不广多闻，尔时世尊便作是念：「今诸比丘多有懈怠意不精懃，复自观察当来过去三世之事，知当来世当有比丘，嫉妬恚痴不顺道教，便兴诽谤损如来法轻慢于师，亦复不敬说法之人。」是以世尊观察后世遗法中间，恐有老少共相上下尊卑不别，老恃耆艾少恃聪叡。老者自陈：「吾所目覩，非卿所知，汝今所见如萤火虫。」少者自陈：「老顿嚚卤，情丧心塞，知何可归？」如来教曰：「当自守戒，犹若事火梵志五处然火，昼夜承事不失时节，香华缯彩事事供养。」是故说曰，诸有知深法，不问老以少，审谛守戒信，犹祀火梵志，归命人中尊，亦如事火神。

Those who know the profound Dharma, the teachings of the equally enlightened
ones, carefully uphold precepts and faith, like the brahman who worships fire.

诸有知深法，　　等觉之所说，审谛守戒信，　　犹祀火梵志。

The Tathāgata appears in the world, and after many millions of eons, he appears from time to time. It is truly impossible to encounter a sage. If one can uphold the precepts and faith without losing propriety, it is like the brahman who worships fire. In the past, when the Buddha was in the world, he instructed the bhikṣus: "From now on, you should not recite the texts of non-Buddhist teachings or what is recited and practiced by non-Buddhist practitioners. Why? Because what they present is not the true meaning, nor is it the basis of the ultimate Way." Therefore it is said: Those who know the profound Dharma, the teachings of the equally enlightened ones, carefully uphold precepts and faith, like the brahman who worships fire. Truly taking refuge in the Buddha.

如来出现，亿千万劫时时乃出，遭贤遇圣实不可得，人能守戒信不失仪，如祀火梵志。昔佛在世诫诸比丘：「自今以后不得诵外书外道异学所诵习者。何以故？彼所陈说非真正义，亦复不是至道之本。」是故说曰，诸有知深法，等觉之所说，审谛守戒信，犹祀火梵志，真诚归命佛。

Among their own teachings, the brahman is supreme
All the contaminants are completely exhausted, without remainder.

于己法在外，　　梵志为最上，一切诸有漏，　　皆尽皆无余。

Or they contemplate feelings, completely exhausted, without remainder.

或复观于痛，　　皆尽皆无余。

Or they contemplate the coming together of conditions, completely exhausted, without remainder.

或复观合会，　　皆尽皆无余。

Or they contemplate causes and conditions, completely exhausted, without remainder.

或复观因缘，　　皆尽皆无余。

As for those who are outside their own Dharma, those practitioners contemplate and understand all phenomena, without anything not being connected or unknown. It is like how Brahmins know astronomy, geography, the changes in the stars, and all calamities, and contemplate and understand them all. All their taints are completely exhausted without remainder. They contemplate all suffering, whether good or bad, all returning to exhaustion. They contemplate their coming together, which must have the causes and conditions of separation. What is temporary also returns to extinction.

于己法在外者，彼修行人观了一切众法，无事不关无事不知，犹若梵志知天文地理星宿灾变皆悉观了，一切诸漏皆尽无余。观诸苦痛若好若丑，皆归于尽，观其合会必有离别因缘，暂有亦复归灭。

It is like the inner Dharma, the Brahmins are outside. If
they share a bed and bedding, it is like the Po-kou-lo.

犹若内法本，　　梵志为在表，若使共床褥，　　如彼婆钩卢。

As for the so-called inner Dharma, the four truths are truly thus, each distinguished without losing the sequence. The Brahmins consider the inner to be the outer. Therefore it is said: "It is like the inner Dharma, the Brahmins are outside. If they share a bed and bedding, it is like the Po-kou-lo.

所谓内法者，四谛真如一一分别不失次绪，梵志于内则谓为表。是故说曰，犹若内法本，梵志为在表，若使共床褥，如婆钩卢者。此婆钩卢比丘出家以来，未曾与人说四句之义，正使与共同坐不闻说其正法，从生至老八十一钵和蓝，未曾畜沙弥弟子及余使人，若为人鲜洁托志虚无系意玄寂。是故说曰，若使共床褥，如彼婆钩卢。

It is like the inner Dharma, the Brahmins are outside. Knowing birth, knowing old age, turning to death.

犹如内法，　　梵志在表，知生知老，转当至死。

As for the so-called inner Dharma, it does not deceive people, it is one-sided and without inclination, one-sided and without perversity. Only the Tathāgata can transcend this realm, to end his life and no longer accept existence, knowing it as it truly is. Therefore it is said: "It is like the inner Dharma, the Brahmins are outside. Knowing birth, knowing old age, turning to death.

所谓内法者不诳惑人，一向而无倾，一向而无邪，唯有如来能越此境界，以尽其生更不受有，如实知之。是故说曰，犹若内法，梵志在表，知生知老，转当至死。

The sun shines during the day, the moon shines at night, armor and weapons shine on the army
meditation shines on the practitioners of the Way, the Buddha appears in the world, shining on all darkness.

日照于昼，　　月照于夜，甲兵照军，禅照道人，佛出天下，　　照一切冥。

"The sun shines during the day" means that when the sun first rises, it emits billions of light rays, causing the light of the stars and moon to no longer shine. If the sun sets, the moon and stars all compete in brightness, illuminating together but their brightness is not the same. It is like the soldiers of a great general facing each other in battle, displaying their might and martial prowess to decide victory or defeat, with the clash of sharp weapons sounding like thunder. The person in meditative concentration can move mountains, fly over mountains, raise dust in the ocean, and touch the sun and moon with their hands, possessing such supernatural powers without boasting. Although these people have such virtues, they are not equal to the Tathāgata. When the Buddha appears in the world, fully endowed with all characteristics, he emits great light that illuminates everywhere. The light reaches everywhere, day and night without interruption. Those who see the light, the deaf, blind, mute, and those suffering from torture, naturally find relief. Therefore it is said: "The sun shines during the day, the moon shines at night, armor and weapons shine on the army, meditation shines on the practitioners of the Way, the Buddha appears in the world, shining on all darkness"

日照于昼者，当日天子初出之时，放亿百千万光明，使星宿月光无复光明；若复日没之时，月及星宿皆共竞明，俱有所照其明不同。犹若大将之士两敌相向，扬威奋武决战胜负，震赫精刃钟皷雷鸣。禅定之人移山飞岳海水扬尘手扪日月，有此神力不自称誉。方此诸人虽有此德，不及如来。佛出世间众相具足，放大光明靡所不照，光明所及昼夜不绝，其见光者聋盲瘖痖考掠苦痛自然休息。是故说曰，如日照于昼，月照于夜，甲兵照军，禅照道人，佛出天下，照一切冥。

The Brahmins do not have this, they have worries and thoughts of
worries, like turning according to one's wishes, they eliminate doubts here and there.

梵志无有是，　　有忧无忧念，如如意所转，　　彼彼灭狐疑。

The Brahmins do not have this, they are attached to the Dharma of excellence, seeing happiness they do not rejoice, seeing suffering they do not grieve, like turning according to one's wishes, they always contemplate goodness and eliminate evil by themselves, attaining the noble truths and distinguishing the afflictions. Therefore it is said: "The Brahmins do not have this, they have worries and thoughts of worries, like turning according to one's wishes, they eliminate doubts here and there"

梵志无有是，意著于殊妙之法，见乐不以为喜，见忧不以为戚，如如意所转恒自念善，彼彼自灭恶，得习圣谛分别诸使。是故说曰，梵志无有是，有忧无忧念，如如意所转，彼彼灭狐疑。

Giving rise to various profound dharmas, the Brahmins practice and enter into meditation
able to resolve the net of doubts, knowing the suffering of the body.

出生诸深法，　　梵志习入禅，能解狐疑网，　　身知其苦痛。

When the Tathāgata, the Perfectly Enlightened One, first attained Buddhahood, for seven days he was absorbed in meditative concentration, contemplating the twelve links of dependent origination, discerning their arising and cessation one by one. At that time, the Tathāgata arose from samādhi and spoke this verse:

如来等正觉初成佛时，七日之中禅定正受思惟十二因缘，一一分别知起知灭。尔时如来即从三昧起而说斯偈：

Giving rise to various profound dharmas, the Brahmins practice and enter into meditation
able to resolve the net of doubts, knowing the suffering of the body.

「出生诸深法，　　梵志习入禅，能解狐疑网，　　身知其苦痛。

"As I have practiced and accumulated, I have now attained perfect enlightenment, which is true and not false." The Brahmins practice and enter into meditation, eliminating all evil dharmas and destroying the net of doubts. They attain unobstructed wisdom in the profound dharmas, have mastery in their thoughts, deeply know the end of suffering, and deeply know the Dharma of the combination of causes and conditions, which is deceptive and not real. Summarizing the essentials, one should contemplate the Dharma of causes and conditions, and also contemplate the Dharma of cessation. All dharmas arise from the combination of causes and conditions, and all dharmas arise from feelings. One should know that by extinguishing and ceasing, one does not create any taints.

「如我所习积行所致，今日成等正觉实而不虚。」梵志习入禅，去诸恶法悉坏狐疑网，于诸深法得无碍智，所念自在深知苦际，深知因缘合数之法权诈非实。略诵其要，当观因缘法，复当观尽法，一切诸法皆由合数，一切诸法皆由于痛，当知尽灭不造有漏。

Giving rise to various profound dharmas, the Brahmins practice and enter
into meditation, illuminating all worlds, like the sun in the sky.

出生诸深法，　　梵志习入禅，遍照一切世，　　犹日在虚空。

The Dharma can perfect a person, while non-Dharma cannot. Day and night, one contemplates without letting go of it from the mind. The actions of body, speech, and mind do not falsely violate it. Being able to accomplish such a Dharma, one can illuminate all dharmas. One gives all that one has attained to sentient beings, just as the sun in the sky universally illuminates. Those who see it are not without being illuminated by its light. Therefore, it is said: Giving rise to various profound dharmas, the Brahmins practice and enter into meditation, illuminating all worlds, like the sun in the sky.

法能成人非法不就，昼夜思惟不去胸怀，身口意行不妄有犯，能成就如此法，便能照一切法。以己所得尽施众生，犹若明日处在虚空普有所照，其有覩者莫不蒙光。是故说曰，出生诸深法，梵志习入禅，遍照一切世，犹日在虚空。

Giving rise to various profound dharmas, the Brahmins practice and enter into meditation, able
to repel the armies of Mara, just as the Buddha is free from all defilements.

出生诸深法，　　梵志习入禅，能却魔众敌，　　如佛脱众垢。

"Giving rise to various profound dharmas" means that the Tathāgata, having attained perfect enlightenment, is fully endowed with the thirty-seven factors of the path. The actions of body, speech, and mind are in accord with the undefiled. Subduing the armies of Mara, advancing and retreating at the right time, the Tathāgata, the Perfectly Enlightened One, is free from all afflictions.

出生诸深法者，如来成等正觉，具足三十七道品之法，身口意行与无漏相应，降伏魔怨进却时宜，如来等正觉脱一切结使。
