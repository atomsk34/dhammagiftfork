T 212.9

Chuyao jing / Udānavarga (T 212) 出曜经

T 212.9 诽谤品第九

Parallel to: SN 6.9 / SN 6.10 / SN 8.5 / AN 10.89 / Dhp 157–166 / Dhp 360–382 / Snp 3.3 / Snp 3.10 / Thag 21 / T 210.8 / T 210.34 / T 210.38 / T 213.8

The Buddha was at Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī. At that time, the World-Honored One was surrounded by countless great assemblies, expounding the Dharma for them. At that time, there was a Canda-māṇava in the city of Śrāvastī. The Canda-māṇava had deep-seated hatred and enmity, and his sinful consciousness was not removed. He carried a wooden bladder covered with clothes, left the city of Śrāvastī, and went to Jetavana Monastery. From afar, he saw the World-Honored One expounding the Dharma for countless great assemblies. He was overjoyed and could not contain himself. "Today, I must disgrace the Śramaṇa Gautama in the great assembly, so that my teacher can receive offerings." In the great assembly, he spoke this verse:

佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园。尔时世尊，无数大众前后围绕而为说法。时，有栴摩那祇在舍卫城里，时摩那祇宿旧怨深，罪识不除，身带木杅以衣覆之，出舍卫城至祇桓精舍，遥见世尊与无数大众而为说法，欢喜踊跃不能自胜。「今日要当在大众中毁辱瞿昙沙门，令我等师得致供养。」在于大众而说此偈：

The Dharma is sweet and delights people, and from his mouth comes immeasurable meaning
It makes me, who am pregnant, not ashamed of this proper and suitable thing.

「说法甘悦人，　　口出无量义，使我怀姙身，　　不羞此宜适。」

At that time, the World-Honored One emitted a soft and gentle sound, like the voice of a kalaviṅka bird and also like the sound of Brahmā, and spoke this verse:

尔时世尊出柔软响，声如羯脾鸟亦如梵音，而说斯偈：

"Lying is close to hell" means saying that something exists when it does not, and saying that something is true when it is not. Not having experienced something, to say that one has experienced it many times - such people fall into the eight great hells and enter the sixteen cauldrons. Therefore, it is said, "Lying is close to hell." "Saying one did not do what one did" means that there are two kinds of offenses: one is actually doing it and committing it; the other is lying and saying one did not do it. This offense is the most serious and its retribution is immeasurable. Therefore, it is said, "Saying one did not do what one did." "Both offenses are received later" means that these two heavy offenses are the root of calamity and disaster, leading to the loss of one's body and life. The wise do not do them. Therefore, it is said, "Both offenses are received later." "This practice itself drags one there" means that worldly people create defiled practices that burden themselves. Later, they receive their retribution, and even their relatives cannot save them. They experience it for eons before being liberated. Therefore, it is said, "This practice itself drags one there."

妄语地狱近者，本无而言有，实非而言是。曾不经历自言数涉，如此之类堕八大地狱，入十六鬲子，是故说曰，妄语地狱近。作之言不作者，其罪有二：一者实作而犯之；二者妄语，作而言非。此罪最重其报无量，是故说曰，作之言不作也。二罪后俱受者，其二重罪，殃祸之本亡身丧命，智者所不为，是故说曰，二罪后俱受也。是行自牵往者，世人造秽行与身作累，后受其报亲族不救，经历劫数乃得解脱，是故说曰，是行自牵往也。

The axe is in the mouth of a person" means that the axe of evil speech is in the mouth of a person. It is used to cut off one's own body.

夫士之生，　　斧在口中，所以斩身，由其恶言。

"The axe is in the mouth of a person" means that the axe of evil speech is in the mouth of a person. It is used to cut off one's own body.

昔佛在罗阅祇城耆阇崛山。时，尊者舍利弗、大目犍连，食时著衣持钵正其威仪，下灵鹫山顶入城乞食。食后还出罗阅祇城，未至其所，道逢暴雨，雷电霹雳，道侧有神寺，房舍深邃，先有放牛女人于此止住。时，舍利弗、目犍连入寺便住，不见女人；女人遥见舍利弗等，即便失精堕地。时，瞿波利比丘复从后来，舍利弗遥见来，语目连曰：「不与愚从事，得离恶人快。今此比丘是恶知识，宜可出避。」即与目连出彼寺庙涉道而去。瞿波利后至入于庙内，见有女人颜貌端正，作弄女姿像如犯婬，有不净在地，咄曰：「祸灾未曾所见，云何舍利弗、目连等，自称智慧神足夸世独步，神通智达谓为第一，今乃与此放牛女人犯婬交接，斯现事如是。世岂有圣人耶？我今躬自见，不从人闻。」得是欢喜即出寺庙，徒跣涉雨至世尊所，头面礼足在一面立。时，瞿波利比丘前白佛言：「舍利弗、目连等，纂行极弊造凡夫业。适从城出，道遇暴雨入寺避之，见目连等与牧牛女人交接，我躬见之实不虚诳，女人今故在寺，现可验之。」尔时世尊三称瞿波利字而告之曰：「止止比丘！勿吐斯言！可发善心向舍利弗、目连等。所以然者？此二贤人，梵行已立所作已办。」时瞿波利复重白佛：「我今实信如来，如所教勅，然舍利弗、目连所行秽恶，实见婬妷犯于梵行。」佛复告瞿波利：「止止比丘！无令于如来前自坠命根。舍利弗、目连者，行过三界净如天金，岂当有此婬欲意乎？」瞿波利白佛：「诚如来教，但弟子躬见犯欲，若今如来不见信者，知复如何？」说此语已即前礼足涉路而去，还至静室复自思惟：「舍利弗、目连身犯秽行我实见之，然今世尊特不见信。」时，四天王夜非人时，往至瞿波利所，谓瞿波利曰：「舍利弗、目连等贤善之人，三界福田无有过者，何为兴念诽谤，自坠罪苦？」比丘问曰：「卿是何人？」报曰：「护世四天王。」比丘报曰：「汝等诸天，何为舍自然伎乐来至此山？」四王报言：「故为比丘来至此耳，可发善心于彼二贤。」比丘告王：「但还宫殿，不相豫事。」天即还宫。去未经时，释提桓因复从后至，谓比丘曰：「可发善心于彼二贤。」比丘报释提桓因曰：「且守汝天福，不豫汝事。」时拘翼复道而还。富梵天复来到比丘所，语比丘曰：「可发善心于彼二贤。」比丘答曰：「汝是何人？」报曰：「吾是富梵天。」比丘告曰：「世尊不记汝得阿那含耶？」报曰：「如是。」「汝设得阿那含者，何由至此乎？」富梵天即便还去。即其夜，瞿波利举身生疱大如芥子，转如胡豆，渐如桃杏，亦如鼻罗菓等，疮遂坏败，脓血流出臭秽难近，身坏命终入阿浮度地狱中，千具犁牛而耕其舌。

"The axe is in the mouth of a person" means that the axe of evil speech is in the mouth of a person. It is used to cut off one's own body.

尔时世尊告诸比丘：「守护口过慎勿诽谤，夫诽谤之生皆由贪嫉，昨瞿波利比丘来至我所云：『舍利弗、目连等，习近恶智与女人交接。』吾寻呵制：『止止比丘！勿出此言！可发善心于舍利弗目连所。』如是再三。波利比丘谤心遂炽，释提桓因、富梵天及四天王，皆来到波利比丘所，慇懃谏晓曰：『可发善心于彼舍利弗、目连等。』波利比丘即复报曰：『各还汝天宫，吾躬自覩彼二贤秽行，如是何为干豫其事？且还速归，勿复重宣。』寻即各还其所。于夜波利比丘身生疱疮即取命终，脓血流出，入阿浮度地狱中，千具犁牛而耕其舌。是故比丘！当兴勇猛摄护身口意过，见诸精进比丘共相敬待如视我无异。」是时，世尊在大众中，而说斯颂：

The axe is in the mouth of a person" means that the axe of evil speech is in the mouth of a person. It is used to cut off one's own body.

「夫士之生，　　斧在口中，所以斩身，由其恶言。

"The axe is in the mouth of a person" means that the axe of evil speech is in the mouth of a person. It is used to cut off one's own body.

「夫士之生者，出母胞胎宿行不同意性殊异，犹如有人手执利斧，入山斩伐林木华菓药草毁坏成功。今此人间亦复如是，人生于世不能守护口过，为心所使，造不善本，皆由于舌，端正丑陋长短好恶，亦由心念口发致此重罪。是故比丘！心秉牢固守护其口。如是比丘！当作是学。」尔时诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

Praise and blame, both are evil. Those who like to stir up
disputes with their mouths will all be without peace in the end.

誉恶恶所誉，　　是二俱为恶，好以口会鬪，　　是后皆无安。

"Praise and blame, both are evil" means that praising and blaming are both evil.

誉恶恶所誉者，彼瞿波利比丘叹誉调达功德，体性柔软行业真正，多闻广慧，慈悲四等育养众生。如此之类应当毁呰不应叹誉，是故说誉恶恶所誉也。是二俱为恶者，舍利弗、目连比丘，修清净梵行众德具足，永离尘埃不与欲会，广博多智明达三世。然瞿波利比丘，愚心不革执意牢固，兴致诽谤云：「舍利弗、目连比丘造诸恶业。」如此之类应当叹德不应兴谤。何以故？舍利弗、目连者，持戒多闻为人导师，处在大众转无上法轮，逮得罗汉诸漏已尽；然彼恶人兴致诽谤，不叹其德。是故说曰，是二俱为恶也。好以口会鬪者，夫人处世罪苦万端，或因婬嫉致悋，或因博戏致恚，罪心已固不虑后缘，出言招祸以灭身本，渐当入泰山地狱饿鬼畜生，涉诸苦难无有穷已；虽得为人诸根不具，聋盲瘖痖为人所轻；或在边地佛后，皆由口过身受殃罪。犹木生火还自焚烧，口为祸门，舌为殃本，二事机发，败毁形命。是故说，好以口会鬪。是后皆无安者，众生处在欲界，为颠倒所惑，愚无慧明，为爱缚所系，憍慢缠身悭嫉心深，不达五识身乐，不知苦之所兴，永处暗冥不求灯明，是故说，是后皆无安也。

Strife for little gain is like concealing lost wealth. Arising from
that, it leads to strife, causing the mind to turn towards evil.

诤为少利，　　如掩失财，从彼致诤，　　令意向恶。

Strife for little gain is like concealing lost wealth. Arising from that, it leads to strife, causing the mind to turn towards evil.

诤为少利者，昔有众多比丘，时到著衣持钵入舍卫城乞食。见数十博戏之人对坐共戏，一人得胜一人不如，彼得胜者捉不如者剥脱衣裳，五毒加形痛不可忍。众多比丘见已入舍卫城乞食，乞食已收摄衣钵，更正衣服至世尊所，头面礼足在一面坐。时诸比丘具以此事白世尊曰：「向入城乞食，见二戏人对面博戏，一人得胜一人不如，其不如者既失衣裳，身被毒手，痛不可忍。」佛因此本为诸比丘而说偈曰：「诤为少利也，言少利者，犹如大海取其一渧，减须弥山如芥子许，损大地土如米许，复损虚空如蚊许，比丘！极为少不？」白佛言：「甚少。」「博戏虽小亦复如是，百倍千倍万倍巨亿万倍不可譬喻为比，是故比丘！由小致大遂及诤讼，亲变为疎骨肉离索。若复有人诽谤贤圣持戒比丘及谤毁如来，虚而无实，其后受罪，亿佛不救。」是故说，诤为少利，如掩失财，从彼致诤，令意向恶也。

Hundreds of thousands of niraya hells, thirty-six and five, those who slander
the sages rely on the mouth to give rise to evil vows.

百千尼罗浮，　　三十六五狱，诽谤贤圣者，　　依口发愿恶。

At that time the World Honored One told the bhikṣus: 'The bhikṣu Guānpó himself invited disaster and entered the niraya hell. At that time the jailer Ābhāsvara pulled out his tongue with an iron clamp, which was several hundred zhang long, stretched it out flat, and nailed it with iron nails. He plowed his tongue with a thousand oxen and a blazing plowshare, dying and reviving hundreds of thousands of times in a day. The reason for this is that his evil karma has not yet been eliminated. He will have to go through thirty-six and five hells, suffering immeasurable and indescribable pain. After leaving that hell, he will be reborn as an animal or hungry ghost, and will have to go through hundreds of thousands of kalpas to finish his karmic suffering. If he is reborn as a human, he will be deaf, blind, mute, and have incomplete faculties. His words will not be genuine and he will be slandered a lot. If he walks on the road, he will be beaten by others. Therefore, bhikṣus should diligently cultivate the Way and be cautious not to slander sages and bhikṣus. Those who slander sages will suffer like this. Thus, bhikṣus should practice accordingly.' At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received and practiced it.

尔时世尊告诸比丘：「瞿波利比丘者，自招祸患入尼罗浮地狱中。尔时狱卒阿傍以铁刚钳拔出其舌长数百丈，舒展平正以铁钉钉之，以千犁牛犁铧火然用耕其舌，一日之中百千万终死而复生。所以然者？以其恶罪未除故，当复经三十六五地狱，受苦无量不可称记，从彼狱出当受畜生饿鬼，复当经历数千万劫毕其罪苦。若其为人，聋盲瘖痖六根不具，语不真正多被诽谤，若行道路横为人打。是故比丘当勤修道德，慎莫诽谤贤圣比丘。诽谤贤圣者其受如是。如是比丘！当作是学。」尔时诸比丘闻佛所说，欢喜奉行。

Without the Way, one falls into evil paths, increasing the suffering of hell. Far from
fools, cultivate the mind of patience, contemplate the truth, and there will be no transgression.

无道堕恶道，　　自增地狱苦，远愚修忍意，　　念谛则无犯。

Without the Way, one falls into evil paths, empty and unreal, not seeing the right path to rely on, deceiving and causing disaster. It is like the Channa and Guānpó bhikṣus, who were empty and unreal, slandering sages and Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana bhikṣus, suffering the consequences for countless kalpas without ceasing. Therefore, it is said: 'Without the Way, one falls into evil paths.' 'Increasing the suffering of hell' means entering hundreds of thousands of niraya hells and also entering thirty-six and five a-fu hells, suffering immeasurable pain in the body. After leaving the hells, one will again take on a hellish body, increasing the suffering of hell. Therefore, it is said: 'Increasing the suffering of hell.' 'Cultivating the mind of patience far from fools' means always being fearful, hearing about violating precepts and prohibitions, the hair on one's body standing on end. One should distance oneself from ignorance and endure what is difficult to endure. Therefore, it is said: 'Cultivating the mind of patience far from fools.' 'There will be no transgression by contemplating the truth' means that in removing defilements and stopping illness, nothing is better than the Four Noble Truths. If one can concentrate the mind without distraction and contemplate the form and vessel inside and outside without a self, one can then be free from the sounds of slander. Therefore, it is said: 'There will be no transgression by contemplating the truth.'

无道堕恶道者，虚而不实亦复不见，正事可依诳生非祸，犹如旃摩那者、瞿波利比丘等，虚而不实诽谤贤圣及舍利弗、目连比丘，身受其报亿劫不息。是故说曰，无道堕恶道也。自增地狱苦者，当入百千尼罗浮地狱，当复入三十六五阿浮地狱，身入其中毒痛无量，舍地狱身受地狱身增地狱苦，是故说曰，自增地狱苦也。远愚修忍意者，恒怀恐惧，闻犯禁制衣毛为竖，远离愚暗忍诸不忍，是故说曰，远愚修忍意也。念谛则无犯者，去垢止病莫若四谛，能专意不乱思惟形器内外无主，乃得离此诽谤之声，是故说曰，念谛则无犯也。

Chapter 9: Slandering, Part 2

诽谤品第九之余

If one relies on the inner treasury, relying on the sages
for livelihood, fools fall into evil, still wishing for wrong views.

◎若猗内藏，　　依贤圣活，愚者堕恶，　　犹愿邪见。

In the past, the Buddha was in the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍada-ārāma in Śrāvastī, surrounded by tens of millions of people in front and behind, teaching the Dharma. A certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, bared his right arm, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha, "Do not speak of the cessation of nirvāṇa, Tathāgata. The Tathāgata's essential teachings of the sages, the Tathāgata's present dharmas, are tranquil and unconditioned, and he delights in them himself." At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Look at this foolish person, who will fall into evil destinies and forever destroy his life. Not only does he rebuke the Tathāgata today, but also in the past, countless eons ago, he also rebuked the Tathāgata Vipaśyin, causing him not to teach the Dharma. Not only did he rebuke the Tathāgata Vipaśyin, but he also rebuked the Tathāgata Śikhin, causing him not to teach the Dharma. He also rebuked the Tathāgata Viśvabhū, the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, and the Tathāgata Kanakamuni. Now, the seventh Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One has appeared in the world, and he has come to rebuke me, causing me not to teach the Dharma. If this foolish person had focused his mind and intentions to not rebuke the Tathāgata, he would have been able to sit in this seat, eliminate all defilements, and attain the purity of the Dharma eye." At that time, the World-Honored One, in the midst of the great assembly, spoke this verse:

昔佛在舍卫国祇树给孤独园，时与数千万众前后围绕而为说法。有异比丘即从坐起，偏露右臂叉手合掌前白佛言：「如来莫说休息泥洹，如来出要贤圣妙法，如来于现法中闲静无为而自娱乐。」尔时世尊告诸比丘：「汝等观此愚人，自坠恶趣永灭形寿。不但今日呵制如来，乃往久远无数世时，于毘波尸如来所，亦复呵制使不说法。不但呵制毘波尸如来，乃复呵制尸弃如来使不说法，次复呵制毘舍毘如来，次复呵制拘楼如来，复次呵制拘那含牟尼佛，次复呵制迦叶如来。我今第七如来、至真、等正觉出现于世，复来呵制使不说法。如此愚人，端心正意不呵制如来者，即应此座上坐，诸尘垢尽得法眼净。」是时，世尊在大众中，说此偈曰：

Having missed this excellent opportunity, I vow to seek
again, never seeing the truth, let alone seeing the ultimate.

「以失今良会，　　更立誓愿求，终不见圣谛，　　况欲见究竟？

Bhikṣus, you should know that this foolish ordinary person, by criticizing the Tathāgata, will destroy his own life and be separated from the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha." If one relies on the inner treasury, the Tathāgata's profound Dharma treasury is without attachment. There are three aspects to this: First, the afflictions of the three realms are completely exhausted without remainder, also called an arhat. Second, there is no further birth and death, with no further germination or growth. Third, offerings from gods and humans are all able to be digested, causing the previous givers to receive blessings without end. Therefore, it is said, "If one relies on the inner treasury." Relying on the sages for livelihood means there are two kinds of sages: First, those who accomplish wholesome roots are called sages. Second, those who accomplish the undefiled are called sages. Such sages do not rely on wrong views to seek livelihood, but rely on the profound Dharma and wondrous wisdom to seek livelihood. Therefore, it is said, "Relying on the sages for livelihood." Foolish ones fall into evil means that, like that bhikṣu who criticized the Tathāgata, causing him not to teach the Dharma, saying, "The Tathāgata should rest in nirvāṇa, the Tathāgata's essential teachings of the sages, the Tathāgata's present dharmas, are tranquil and unconditioned, and he delights in them himself. Why teach the Dharma to the great assembly?" Such comparisons show that fools do not recognize the truth and bring upon themselves misfortune and evil. They wish for boiling water and fire to be their dwelling. This person should not be allowed to sit, rise, or speak. Therefore, it is said, "Foolish ones fall into evil." Still wishing for wrong views means having doubts and views that are accustomed to upside-down thinking for a long time, associated with extreme views. As the sūtras say, "The Buddha told the elder, 'Those with wrong views commit bodily, verbal, and mental actions as they say. They give rise to thoughts and intentions that run in myriad ways, all giving rise to wrong views that cannot be associated with. They destroy wholesome roots and increase evil seeds. Why is this? Elder, you should know that wrong views are a sickness that deludes the true Dharma. It is like the seeds of Ikshvāku, Emperor Tārā, and Śiśupāla scattered on the ground, gradually growing with timely watering, fire warming, wind moving, and earth holding, later growing large, but being extremely bitter, rough, foul-smelling, and inedible.' Those with wrong views are also like this, committing bodily, verbal, and mental actions.

「比丘当知！此愚痴凡夫之人，于贤圣道捡自丧形命，离于佛法圣众。」若猗内藏者，如来深法之藏无所染著，其事有三：一者三界缘对结使永尽无余亦名阿罗汉。二者不复生灭更无萌兆趣生熟藏。三者于天人世兴致供养悉能消化，使前施者受福无穷。是故说曰，若猗] cf.【丽】内藏也。依贤圣活者，贤圣有二：一者善根成就名曰贤圣，二者无漏成就名曰贤圣。如此贤圣不猗邪见而求活，乃依深法妙智而求活也。是故说曰，依贤圣活也。愚者堕恶者，犹如彼比丘呵制如来使不说法。「休息泥洹、如来出要、贤圣妙法，如来于现法中但当闲静无为而自娱乐，何为与大众说法？」如此比类，愚不识真自招祸恶，愿乐汤火以为屋室，此人不当与坐起言语。是故说曰，愚者堕恶也。犹愿邪见者，怀犹豫见习颠倒久与边见相应，如彼契经所说：「佛告长者：『夫邪见之人自犯身行如所说，犯口所行如所说，犯意所行如所说，兴意想念流驰万端，尽兴邪见不可亲近，消灭善本增益恶种。所以然者？长者当知！邪见为病迷于正法，犹伊叉桓子、帝多罗子、尸婆犁子种散于地，随时长养渐得水润，火温风动地持，后得长大，食之极苦麤澁秽臭不可食噉。』」邪见之人亦复如是，自犯身行口行意行，是故说曰，犹愿邪见也。

Like bamboo and reeds growing nodes, they harm their
own bodies. Speaking should be good, not teaching evil.

竹芦生节，　　还害其躯，吐言当善，　　不演恶教。

Like bamboo and reeds growing nodes, bamboo and reeds grow nodes that are rough and long. If they encounter a violent wind, they lean against each other and enter their own nodes. The nodes wither by themselves and do not grow again. The words spoken by that bhikṣu are also like this, bringing upon themselves their own faults, not for others. Therefore, it is said, "Like bamboo and reeds growing nodes, they harm their own bodies." Speaking should be kind and compassionate towards all. Therefore, it is said, "Speaking should be good." There are teachings of speech that are not good dharmas, leading to evil paths in the future. Even a human body is still possible to practice, with eloquence and understanding only occasionally.

竹芦生节者，竹芦生节麤而且长，若遇暴风倚互相撑各入其节，节自枯朽更不复生。彼比丘所说亦复如是，自招其罪不为他人，是故说曰，竹芦生节，还害其躯也。吐言当善者，言当慈仁哀愍一切，是故说曰，吐言当善也。不演恶教者，有吐言教不善之法后趣恶道，人身犹尚可行，言辩通达时乃有耳。是故说曰，不演恶教也。

From goodness one attains liberation, from evil one does not attain liberation. Those who
are liberated through goodness are wise, and this is liberation from evil afflictions. The
wise and holy do not liberate in this way, like the foolish who attain liberation.

从善得解脱，　　为恶不得解，善解者为贤，　　是为脱恶恼，贤圣解不然，　　如彼愚得解。

"Liberation from goodness means that the ultimate place of liberation is without taints. Speaking should be good. Those who speak well and speak kindly are respected by others, and wherever they go they benefit many. Therefore, it is said, "Liberation from goodness." Not liberated from evil means always harboring worries and mental afflictions, constantly fearing. Therefore, it is said, "Not liberated from evil." Those who are liberated through goodness are wise. Liberation does not necessarily mean liberation. One may be bound by the king. What is meant by liberation is liberation of both mind and body. Therefore, it is said, "Those who are liberated through goodness are wise." This is called liberation from evil afflictions. There are two causes of bondage: the present body and the future body. The present body is bound by shackles, beatings, and the five kinds of torture, and is also bound by the fetters and afflictions, wandering through the five destinies. Only those who can liberate themselves are truly liberated. Therefore, it is said, "Liberation from evil afflictions." The wise and holy do not liberate in this way. The wise and holy have two kinds of practices: first, accomplishing the wholesome roots; second, accomplishing the undefiled. The words and teachings spoken by those wise and holy ones do not harm themselves, and there is no suffering in the future. Therefore, it is said, "The wise and holy do not liberate in this way." Like the foolish who attain liberation means that foolish people practice and are attached to the wrong path, not following the right path, and thus dragging themselves towards the three evil destinies. Therefore, it is said, "Like the foolish who attain liberation.

从善得解脱者，夫至解脱无为之处，言当用善，巧言善语者，为人所敬，所至到方多所饶益，是故说曰，从善得解脱也。为恶不得解者，常怀愁忧心意烦恼恒怀恐惧，是故说，为恶不得解也。善解者为贤者，言解脱未必是解脱，或能被系王者所拘，所谓解脱者心身俱解脱，是故说，善解者为贤。是谓脱恶恼者，缚有二缘，今身易后身，今身者桁械考掠及诸五毒之痛，复被结使缚流转五趣，于中能自拔济乃为善解，是故说，解脱恶恼也。贤圣解不然者，贤圣有二业：一者善本成就，二者无漏成就。彼贤圣人所吐言教不自伤损，后无苦恼，是故说贤圣解不然也。如彼愚得解者，愚人习行好著邪道不随正路，以自牵往趣三恶道，是故说如彼愚得解也。

Monks who hold harmful intentions, not speaking rashly, attain loyalty. Speaking
righteously and according to the Dharma, such words are gentle and sweet.

比丘抱损意，　　不躁言得忠，义说如法说，　　是言柔软甘。

"Monks who hold harmful intentions means that monks guard their speech and mind, able to restrain themselves. Their speech is not verbose. Whether among monastics or laypeople, they speak in a balanced way, not disturbing either side. Therefore, it is said, "Monks who hold harmful intentions." Not speaking rashly, attaining loyalty means speaking little but being mostly correct, not deviating from the Buddha's words. The worldly is noisy, but the mind is always far removed. Therefore, it is said, "Not speaking rashly, attaining loyalty." Speaking righteously and according to the Dharma means speaking fully, without any omissions in the body of phrases or the body of flavors. Therefore, it is said, "Speaking righteously and according to the Dharma." Such words are gentle and sweet, expressing immeasurable meanings, delighting the minds of people. The teachings are inexhaustible, like the sweet dew of the heavens, with a good mind and no anger. They do not discriminate between noble and humble families, but speak equally without any increase or decrease in mind. Therefore, it is said, "Such words are gentle and sweet.

比丘抱损意者，比丘护口意，自能收摄，语不烦重，若处道俗，处中而说不娆彼此，是故说曰，比丘抱损意也。不躁言得忠者，少言多中不离佛语，世俗烦閙意恒远离，是故说曰，不躁言得忠也。义说如法说者，具足而说，句身味身无所缺漏，是故说曰，义说如法说也。是言柔软甘，出无量义解悦人意，所说无厌足，如天甘露善心无怒，亦不选择彼豪族种、此卑家种，平等而说无增减心，是故说曰，是言柔软甘也。

Well-spoken teachings of the sages and worthies, Dharma teachings and teachings according to the Dharma, are the second. Mindful
teachings and teachings according to mindfulness, are the third. Truthful teachings and teachings according to truth, are the fourth.

善说贤圣教，　　法说如法二，念说如念三，　　谛说如谛四。

Well-spoken teachings of the sages and worthies means that the teachings of sages and worthies must have a basis for speaking, and they are able to save sentient beings without fail. Therefore, it is said, "Well-spoken teachings of the sages and worthies." Dharma teachings and teachings according to the Dharma are the second. In the teachings of sages and worthies, the Dharma is the abode, and the non-Dharma is the abode. Why is this? When teaching the Dharma, it widely spreads the path of sages and worthies, increasing wholesome roots. When teaching the non-Dharma, it increases unwholesome roots and wholesome dharmas are diminished. Well-spoken teachings of the sages and worthies is the first sentence. Dharma teachings and teachings according to the Dharma are therefore said to be the second. Mindful teachings and teachings according to mindfulness are the third. Speech that is gentle is loved by people, while coarse speech is disliked by people. Those who wish to be remembered by others should reflect on their own goodness. Even animals, upon hearing harsh words, feel sorrowful. Once there were two men who each owned a strong ox. One bragged and wanted to compete with the other's ox. If he lost, he would have to pay five hundred coins. The two oxen were plowing together, and the one ox was indeed stronger. The owner scolded, "You lazy ox, work harder!" The ox heard the scolding and immediately lay down on the ground, losing the five hundred coins. The next day, the owner scolded the ox, "You are called a good ox, yet you made me lose money and embarrassed me!" The ox replied to its owner, "Try again, and I will do my best. I will double your money!" It then plowed again, and as the ox said, it doubled the money. Even animals can recognize good and bad words, so how can humans not recognize good and bad words? Therefore, it is said, "Mindful teachings and teachings according to mindfulness are the third." Truthful teachings and teachings according to truth are the fourth. Those who delight in learning fear the suffering and difficulties of the five paths of rebirth. If they harm others without benefiting them, they block the path to nirvana and open the gates of hell. The Tathāgata wishes to reveal the true and wonderful meaning, and therefore speaks truthfully and according to truth, which is the fourth.

善说贤圣教者，贤圣出教必有所由，接度众生无不有济，是故说贤圣教也。法说如法二者，贤圣教中正法所居、非法所居。何以故？说法之时，广布贤圣道长益善根，说非法时，增益不善根善法有损。善说贤圣教，第一句；法说如法说，是故说法说如法二也。念说如念三者，出言柔和为人所爱念，出言麤犷为人所憎恶，欲为人所念者当自念其善，虽处畜生，闻其恶言皆怀愁戚。昔有二人各畜力牛，一人自夸欲得与彼牛捔力，若不如者，要金钱五百，试牛并耕一牛力实胜之。其牛主骂曰：「弊牛努力。」牛闻其骂，即便卧地，输金钱五百枚。复至后日主责牛曰：「名汝为快牛，乃使我失钱，令我惭愧。」牛报其主曰：「更可试之，当尽我力，当还倍得彼钱。」即复更耕，如牛所言倍得彼钱。畜生尚识善恶之言，况人当不识善恶言乎？是故说，念说如念三也。谛说如谛四者，好学之士畏惧生死五道患难，既损耗不益前人，如斯之类塞泥洹路开地狱门，如来欲现真妙之义，谛说如谛四也。

Therefore, when speaking, one must ensure that oneself is without
trouble and not harm others. This is called speaking well.

是以言语者，　　必使己无患，亦不克众人，　　是为能善言。

Therefore, when speaking, one must ensure that oneself is without trouble and not harm others. This is called speaking well.

是以言语者，言先自惟不自克伐，使今世后世必无苦患，若今身为恶犯王者法，或财产田业没入于宫，或为盗贼劫掠，居业日耗所愿必乖。若至后身，死入地狱畜生饿鬼，地狱五毒不可堪忍，畜生刍蒿负重为役，饿鬼饥馑形［恋-心＋足］极苦。若欲吐言当念谨慎，使今世后世永无苦患，为无数众生见辄爱念，死生天上受福自然，是故说曰，是以言语者，必使己无患也。亦不克众人者，或有狂夫妄谗良善，使彼兴恚以致丧身。言说真诚不讥彼短则无所克，是故说曰，亦不克众人也。是为能善言者，人修善行言必有验，或说泥洹趣要正路，顺从佛教种天之福，是故说，是为能善言也。

When speaking, one's words should be agreeable and bring joy, not
leading to ill-will. When speaking, one's words should be acceptable to all.

言使投意可，　　亦令得欢喜，不使至恶意，　　出言众悉可。

When speaking, one's words should be agreeable and bring joy, not leading to ill-will. When speaking, one's words should be acceptable to all.

言使投意可者，人之处世当习方俗，或相颜而出语，或听彼进趣而后报，恒适彼人意良宜得所，或现威怒怯怕时人，或现羸弱伏从于人，将护其意令彼得所。是故说曰，言使投意可也。亦令得欢喜者，出言向人必使有益，前人闻者倍用欢喜，不被骂詈来彼骂辱，是故说曰，亦令得欢喜也。不使至恶意者，不施怨于人造不善行，亦不种地狱饿鬼畜生之行。造恶业者，当受三报，是故说曰，不使至恶意也。出言众悉可者，与人从事恒当谦恭卑下，正使言论得胜，当自鄙不如，是故说曰，出言众悉可也。

The truth is like nectar, the teaching is supreme. Speaking the truth
and in accordance with the Dharma is the foundation of the path.

至诚甘露说，　　说法无有上，谛说义如法，　　是为立道根。

The truth is like nectar, the teaching is supreme. Speaking the truth and in accordance with the Dharma is the foundation of the path.

至诚甘露说者，与人说法当修诸功德，如来法者，甚深微妙善住安隐，一切行无常、一切法无我，泥洹为灭尽，能意御乱，与人说法不怀懈怠，是故说曰，至诚甘露说也。说法无有上者，出言真正辄有所度，趣道之要由是通达，是故说曰，说法无有上也。谛说义如法者，如念真实本际清净，亦不颠倒不怀虚诈，如法者名句身句味句，真如法性亦不变易，是故说曰，谛说义如法也。是为立道根者，无漏善法永离尘垢，至解脱门逮无碍道，是为立道根也。

Speaking like the Buddha's words is auspicious and attains liberation. It can
cut off the edge of suffering. This is called the best among words.

说如佛言者，　　是吉得灭度，为能断苦际，　　是谓言中上。

Speaking like the Buddha's words is auspicious and attains liberation. It can cut off the edge of suffering. This is called the best among words.

说如佛言者，说四意止究生死原系念专意，或说意断精进不懈，或说神足兼逮定，或说根义于中逮慧根，或说力义成就于力，或说觉意令达觉法，或说八直道分别八道，亦复说若干众法名身句身味身。如来或说八十千诸度，使众生类乘此度而度彼岸，是故说曰，说如佛言者。是吉得泥洹者，永寂之处吉无不利，脱众苦患离诸结使，亦复脱于生老病死忧戚难祸，是故说曰，是吉得泥洹也。为能断苦际者，所谓苦者五盛阴身，牵致冥室不覩慧明。苦有八相，生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、怨憎会苦、爱别离苦、所求不得苦，取要言之，五盛阴苦。阿僧祇众生涉历苦涂至永寂处，如今众生履行妙观，越苦境界至无为岸者，皆由佛恩。是故说曰，为能断苦际也。是谓言中上者，所出言教无能过上，亦非二乘所能逮及，旷济无量亦无边福，是故说曰，是谓言中上也。
