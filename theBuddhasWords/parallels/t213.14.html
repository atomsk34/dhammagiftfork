T 213.14

Faji yaosong jing / Udānavarga (T 213) 法集要颂经

T 213.14 法集要颂经怨家品第十四

Parallel to: MN 128 / Dhp 1–20 / Dhp 60–75 / Dhp 320–333 / Snp 1.3 / Iti / Thag 4 / Thag 8 / MĀ 72 / T 210.17 / T 210.31 / T 212.15

Not blaming, yet giving rise to blame, not slandering, yet creating karma
the ignorant and deluded undergo rebirth, in this world and in future lives.

不怨而兴怨，　　不谤而造业，愚迷受轮回，　　今世及后世。

First creating one's own karmic leaks, then harming others, each
harming the other, like a bird caught in a net.

先自作漏业，　　然后害他人，彼此相兴害，　　如鸟堕罗网。

Destroying others, one destroys oneself; enemies meet enemies. Defaming
others, one defames oneself; hating others, one hates oneself.

破他还自破，　　冤家遇冤家，毁他还自毁，　　瞋他还自瞋。

What kind of practice is this? Not knowing the true Dharma
one's life is short, abandoning hatred only to form more hatred.

斯何沙门行，　　不知正法本，寿既获短促，　　舍冤复结冤。

All the characteristics together slander, each giving rise to
angry voices. Joyfully and equally enduring, this patience is unsurpassed.

众相共毁谤，　　各发恚怒声，欢心平等忍，　　此忍最无比。

Cutting bones and dying, cattle and horses dying, losing
wealth, the country then becomes chaotic, gathering again and regaining.

断骨而命终，　　牛马死财失，国界则丧乱，　　聚集还复得。

You all do not give rise to evil, this Dharma attains freedom
from enmity. Being able to endure others' enmity, this is called wisdom.

汝等不兴恶，　　此法得离怨，他怨能忍受，　　说之名为智。

If one knows this teaching is supreme, the deluded seek happiness. In the
present, there is no enmity, and in the future, there will be no resentment.

若知此说胜，　　愚迷求快乐，现在无怨意，　　未来亦无恨。

One should not repay enmity with enmity, only then will one attain happiness
Practicing patience, enmity naturally ceases, this is called the Dharma of the Tathāgata.

不可怨以怨，　　终已得快乐，行忍怨自息，　　此名如来法。

If someone insults me, they are superior, I am inferior. If
one is happy according to one's wishes, enmity will eventually cease.

若人致毁骂，　　彼胜我不胜，快乐从意者，　　怨终得休息。

If one befriends good friends and roams together in the world, one does
not accumulate any remaining enmity, but single-mindedly thinks in accordance with their intentions.

若人亲善友，　　共游于世间，不积有冤余，　　专念同其意。

If one does not find good friends and roams alone without companions
one should observe all countries and practice goodness alone without doing evil.

设不得善友，　　独游无伴侣，应观诸国土，　　独善不造恶。

If one has no companions in learning and also no close friends
it is better to alone uphold good conduct, not to be with fools.

学无同伴侣，　　又不得亲友，宁独守善行，　　不与愚人偕。

If one delights in learning precepts and practicing the Dharma, what use are companions?
Like a dragon who likes deep pools, like an elephant who enjoys the wilderness.

乐戒学法行，　　奚用伴侣为？如龙好深渊，　　如象乐旷野。
