T 213.18

Faji yaosong jing / Udānavarga (T 213) 法集要颂经

T 213.18 法集要颂经华喻品第十八

Parallel to: Dhp 44–59 / Dhp 273–289 / Dhp 360–382 / Snp 1.1 / Thag 5 / T 210.34

Who can choose the ground? To abandon hell and take heaven
only speak the good Dharma, like picking the best and wonderful flowers.

何人能择地？　　舍地狱取天，惟说善法句，　　如采善妙华。

The learner can choose the ground, abandoning hell and taking heaven
speaking the wonderful Dharma well, able to pick the various wonderful flowers.

学人能择地，　　舍地狱取天，善说妙法句，　　能采众妙华。

Cut the forest, not the tree, because fear arises from the forest
Having cut the forest and extinguished it, the bhikṣu attains perfect peace.

截林勿截树，　　因林生怖畏，截林而灭已，　　苾刍得圆寂。

Cutting the forest without uprooting it, because fear arises from the forest
not cutting even a tiny bit, causing the mind to be bound.

截林不断根，　　因林生怖畏，未断分毫间，　　令意生缠缚。

Cutting the forest without uprooting it, because fear arises from the forest, the
mind's attachment is hardest to leave, like a calf's love for its mother.

截林勿断根，　　因林生怖畏，心缠最难离，　　如犊恋爱母。

One should cut off one's own attachment, like a dried-up lotus pond, cease
one's traces and receive the right teachings, the Buddha says the bliss of nirvāṇa.

当自断爱恋，　　犹如枯莲池，息迹受正教，　　佛说圆寂乐。

Like a desirable flower, beautiful in color but without fragrance
skillful words are like this, without fruit and no reward.

犹如可意华，　　色好而无香，巧言华如是，　　无果不获报。

Like a desirable flower, beautiful in color and fragrant, skillful
words are like this, they will definitely receive good rewards.

犹如可意华，　　色好而香洁，巧言善如是，　　必获其好报。

Like a bee collecting flowers, not destroying the color and fragrance, but taking
the taste and flying away, a bhikṣu enters the village in the same way.

犹如蜂采华，　　不坏色与香，但取味飞去，　　苾刍入聚然。

Not going against others' likes and dislikes, not observing what is done or
not done, but only observing one's own bodily actions, whether correct or incorrect.

不违他好恶，　　勿观作不作，但自观身行，　　若正若不正。

Like a ditch filled with filth near a great road
a lotus flower grows in it, fragrant and very pleasing.

如田粪秽沟，　　而近于大道，其中生莲华，　　香洁甚可悦。

There is birth, there is death. Ordinary people enjoy being on the
edge. The wise love to leave. They are true followers of the Buddha.

有生必有终，　　凡夫乐处边，慧人爱出离，　　真是佛声闻。

Gathering many wonderful flowers, they make garlands and adornments. Sentient beings
accumulate wholesome roots, and in future lives they become even more excellent.

多集众妙华，　　结鬘为步摇，有情积善根，　　后世转殊胜。

Like the beautiful flowers of the mālati and the pure flowers of the
makuṭa, if greed and anger are eliminated, the bhikṣu is pure and fragrant.

如末哩妙华，　　末拘罗清净，贪欲瞋若除，　　苾刍净香洁。

Like a person who gathers beautiful flowers, concentrating without distraction, due to sleep
they encounter a flood and are suddenly brought down by the Lord of Death.

如人采妙华，　　专意不散乱，因眠遇水漂，　　俄被死王降。

Like a person who gathers beautiful flowers, concentrating without distraction
the mind of desire is insatiable and always afflicted by poverty.

如人采妙华，　　专意不散乱，欲意无厌足，　　常为穷所困。

Like a person who gathers beautiful flowers, concentrating without distraction, not
yet obtaining true wealth and treasures, one is long afflicted by poverty.

如人采妙华，　　专意不散乱，未获真财宝，　　长为穷所困。

If one does not see the king of death, with wisdom illuminating like a
pure flower, the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

若不见死王，　　慧照如净华，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If greed, anger, and delusion are cut off, like discarding the roots of poisonous
flowers, the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

贪瞋痴若断，　　如弃毒华根，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If the root of greed is eradicated, like a flower floating on water
the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

贪根若除断，　　如华水上浮，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If the root of anger is eradicated, like a flower floating on water
the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

恚根若除断，　　如华水上浮，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If the root of delusion is eradicated, like a flower floating on water
the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

痴根若除断，　　如华水上浮，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

Just as a person making a flower garland, the mind is greedy and
insatiable, not exhausting the poison of this world, the three roots constantly bind.

如人结花鬘，　　意乐贪无足，不尽现世毒，　　三根常缠缚。

Contemplating the body as a fragile vessel, illusory phenomena as a mirage, cutting off
the blooming flowers of Māra, not seeing the path of the Lord of Death.

观身如坏器，　　幻法如野马，断魔华开敷，　　不覩死王路。

This body is like a mass of foam, knowing this illusory and magical Dharma, cutting
off the blooming flowers of Māra, not seeing the path of the Lord of Death.

是身如聚沫，　　知此幻化法，断魔华开敷，　　不覩死王路。

The root of arrogance is eradicated, like a flower floating on water
the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

我慢根除断，　　如华水上浮，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If the root of stinginess is eradicated, like a flower floating on water
the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

悭悋根若断，　　如华水上浮，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If the root of the branch of craving is eradicated, like a flower floating
on water, the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

爱支根若断，　　如华水上浮，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。

If there are no roots of afflictions, and one attains the good causes and effects
of retribution, the bhikṣu reaches the other shore, like a snake shedding its old skin.

若无烦恼根，　　获报善因果，苾刍到彼岸，　　如蛇脱故皮。
