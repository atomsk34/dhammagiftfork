T 5

T5 佛般泥洹经

Parallel to: DN 16 (~) / DN 17 (~) / SN 47.9 (~) / Snp 1.5 / DĀ 2 / MĀ 68 (~) / T 6 / T 7 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was staying on Vulture Peak in the country of Rājagṛha, together with a community of 1,250 bhikṣus. At that time, King Ajātaśatru of Magadha was at odds with the country of Vaiśālī and wanted to attack it. He discussed with his ministers, saying, "The country of Vaiśālī is rich, with a thriving population and abundant precious treasures. If they do not submit to me, should I raise an army to attack their country?" There was a wise minister named Varṣākāra. Varṣākāra was of the Śākyan clan. The minister said, "Yes, Your Majesty." The king told Minister Varṣākāra, "The Buddha is not far from here. If you go to where the Buddha is in my name, bow your head to his feet, and inquire about his well-being, whether his body is at ease and his meals are as usual, and after paying homage to the Buddha, then speak your own thoughts to the Buddha, saying, 'The country of Vaiśālī greatly disrespects the king. The king wishes to attack them. Will he be able to conquer them?'"

一时，佛在王舍国鹞山中，与千二百五十比丘俱。时，摩竭国王，号名阿阇世，与越祇国不相得，欲往伐之。自与群臣共议：「越祇国富，人民炽盛，多出珍宝，不首伏于我，宁可起兵伐其国？」国有贤公，公名雨舍，雨舍公者，逝心种也，公言：「唯命。」王告雨舍公言：「佛去是不远，若持王声，往至佛所，头面著足，问佛消息，身体平安不？飡食如常不？问佛礼竟，自持若意白佛言：『越祇国大轻易王，王欲往伐之，宁能得胜不？』」

The minister accepted the king's instructions and immediately prepared five hundred carriages, two thousand mounted riders, and two thousand infantry. He went to the country of Rājagṛha and, upon reaching the outskirts, stopped the carriages and dismounted. He went to where the Buddha was and saw the Buddha in front of him. He bowed his head to the Buddha's feet. The Buddha offered him a seat and asked the prime minister of the country, "Where have you come from?" The minister said, "The king sent me to pay homage to the Buddha's feet and inquire about the Buddha's well-being, whether his body is at ease and his meals are as usual." The Buddha then asked, "Is the king and the people of the country at peace? Are grain and goods affordable?" The minister said, "By the Buddha's grace, all are at peace. The weather is timely and the country is prosperous." The Buddha said, "Are the people and horses on your journey all safe?" The minister said, "By the Buddha's grace, the journey is all safe and without incident."

公受王教，即严车五百，乘骑二千，步人二千，往到王舍国，得步径止车下，到佛所见佛前。头面著佛足，佛与机使坐，问国丞相：「从何所来？」公言：「王使臣来，稽首佛足，问佛消息，身体平安不？飡食如常不？」佛即问：「王及国人民，宁安和不？谷籴平贱不？」公言：「得佛恩，皆自安和，风雨时节，国中丰熟。」佛言：「公行道人马，皆平安不？」公言：「得佛恩，行道皆平安无他。」

The minister addressed the Buddha, saying, "The king has a grudge against the country of Vaiśālī and wishes to attack and destroy it. What do you think, Buddha? Can he be victorious?" The Buddha said, "The people of the country of Vaiśālī uphold the seven principles. The king cannot defeat them. Those who do not uphold the seven principles can be defeated."

公白佛言：「王与越祇国有嫌，欲往伐坏之，于佛意何如？可得胜不？」佛言：「是越祇国人民，持七法者，王不能得胜，不持七法者可胜。」

The Buddha said, "I once went to the country of Vaiśālī. There was an emergency shrine in the country. I stayed there for a while. The elders of the country of Vaiśālī all came to me and said, 'King Ajātaśatru wishes to attack our country. We are strictly ordered to defend our country.'"

佛言：「我昔尝往到越祇国，国有急疾神舍，我止顿其中。越祇国中诸长老，皆来语我言：『阿阇世王欲来伐我国，我曹谨勅自守国。』」

The Buddha said, 'I then told the elders not to worry or be afraid. If you uphold the seven principles, even if King Ajātaśatru comes, he will not be able to defeat you.'

佛言：「我即告诸长老，莫愁莫恐；若曹持七法，阿阇世王来者，不能胜汝。」

Vaiśālī asked the Buddha, 'What are the seven principles?'

雨舍问佛：「七法者何等？」

At that time, the Buddha was sitting, and Ānanda was fanning him from behind. The Buddha said to Ānanda, 'Have you heard that the people of Vaiśālī often gather together to discuss state affairs and prepare for self-defense?'

时，佛坐，阿难从后扇佛。佛告阿难：「汝宁闻越祇国人，数相聚会，讲议政事，修备自守不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that they often gather together to discuss state affairs and prepare for self-defense.'

对曰：「闻其数相聚会，讲议政事，修备自守。」

The Buddha said, 'So it is, they will not decline. Have you heard that in Vaiśālī, the ruler and ministers are always harmonious, and those appointed are loyal and good, passing on their positions to each other?'

佛言：「如是，彼为不衰。汝闻越祇，君臣常和，所任忠良，转相承用不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that the ruler and ministers are always harmonious, and those appointed are loyal and good, passing on their positions to each other.'

对曰：「闻其君臣常和，所任忠良，转相承用。」

'Have you heard that in Vaiśālī, they uphold the Dharma and follow each other, without taking or desiring, not daring to transgress?'

「汝闻越祇国，奉法相率，无取无愿，不敢有过不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that they uphold the Dharma and follow each other, without taking or desiring, not daring to transgress.'

曰：「闻其奉法相率，无取无愿，不敢有过。」

'Have you heard that in Vaiśālī, they pay respect and are careful and reverent, with distinctions between men and women, and the young serving the elders?'

「汝闻越祇，礼化谨敬，男女有别，长幼相事不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that their rituals and customs are respectful, with distinctions between men and women, and the young serving the elders.'

曰：「闻其礼化谨敬，男女有别，长幼相事。」

'Have you heard that in Vaiśālī, they are filial to their parents, deferential to their younger siblings and elders, and accept admonitions and teachings?'

「汝闻越祇，孝于父母，逊弟师长，受诫教诲不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that they are filial to their parents, deferential to their younger siblings and elders, and accept admonitions and teachings.'

曰：「闻其孝于父母，逊弟师长，受诫教诲。」

'Have you heard that in Vaiśālī, they follow the will of Heaven, respect the earth, revere the ancestral temple and state altars, and serve the four seasons?'

「汝闻越祇，承天则地，敬畏社稷，奉事四时不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that they follow the will of Heaven, respect the earth, revere the ancestral temple and state altars, and serve the four seasons.'

曰：「闻其] cf.【丽】承天则地，敬畏社稷，奉顺四时。」

'Have you heard that in Vaiśālī, they honor and revere the Way and virtue, and provide for the śramaṇas, pratyekabuddhas, and those who come from afar with robes, bedding, and medicine?'

「汝闻越祇，尊奉道德，国有沙门应真及方远来者，供养衣被、床卧、医药不？」

He replied, 'I have heard that they honor and revere the Way and virtue, and provide for the śramaṇas, pratyekabuddhas, and those who come from afar with robes, bedding, and medicine.'

曰：「闻其尊奉道德，国有沙门应真及方远来者，供养衣被、床卧、医药。」

The Buddha said, 'For a country to practice these seven principles is difficult to endanger.'

佛言：「夫有国者，行此七法，难可得危。」

Yueshe Gong replied, 'If Yuegi holds just one of these principles, it is still unassailable, let alone seven principles.' The official said, 'There are many affairs of state, I must return and take leave.' The Buddha said, 'You should know the right time!' He then rose from his seat, bowed to the Buddha, and left.

雨舍公对言：「使越祇持一法者，尚不可攻，何况七法？」公曰：「国事多故，当还请辞。」佛言：「可宜知时！」即从坐起礼佛而去。

Not long after he left, the Buddha called Ānanda and instructed him to go to Yaoshan Mountain to invite all the bhikṣus to gather in the lecture hall. Ānanda immediately accepted the order and went to Yaoshan Mountain, instructing the bhikṣus, 'The Buddha invites the bhikṣus.' The bhikṣus all came and paid homage to the Buddha. The Buddha then went to the lecture hall, set up seats, and they all sat down. The Buddha told the bhikṣus, 'You should uphold the seven principles of the Dharma. What are the seven? Bhikṣus should often gather together to recite the sūtras and the Dharma will last long. The upper and lower ranks should follow each other in sitting and rising, and the Dharma will last long. When sitting and rising, do not think of one's family, wife, and children, and the Dharma will last long. In the mountains and forests, or under deep trees or mounds, one should contemplate the five cessations, and the Dharma will last long. Young people who practice the Way should first ask the elder bhikṣus, respectfully following and accepting their teachings without weariness, and the Dharma will last long. The mind should uphold the Dharma, respectfully following the sūtras and precepts, and the Dharma will last long. Upholding the two hundred and fifty precepts, one will attain the path of arahantship. Those who come to study should not be turned away. Those who enter should follow each other. The robes, food, and drink of those who come should be shared. The sick should be cared for. Bhikṣus who uphold these seven principles will make the Dharma last long.

去未久，佛呼阿难勅之，往至鹞山中，请诸比丘僧皆聚会，著讲堂中。阿难即受教诏，至鹞山中，勅诸比丘僧：「佛请诸比丘。」比丘悉来，皆为佛作礼，佛即在前，至讲堂中，设座已皆坐。佛告诸比丘：「若曹当持七戒法。何等为七？比丘当数相聚会诵经，法可久。上下相承用坐起，法可久。坐起不得念家室妻子，法可久。在山阻间，若在深林树下冢间，当自思惟五灭，法可久。少年奉道，当先问长老比丘，敬畏承用，受教莫厌，法可久。心当奉法，敬畏经戒，法可久。持二百五十戒，具以得阿罗汉道，欲来学者莫却，入者相承用，来者所有衣被饮食当共用，病瘦当相瞻视。比丘持是七法，法可久。

"There are also seven methods that are all permitted."

「复有七法，皆听。」

The bhikṣus agreed, saying, "We will follow your instructions."

比丘诺：「受教。」

Bhikṣus should not be greedy for sleep. Those who sleep should not think of other matters. The Dharma can endure for a long time. Enjoying the purity of guarding, not enjoying conditioned things, the Dharma can endure for a long time. Enjoying sitting together with the virtuous, guarding the practice of patience, being careful not to quarrel, the Dharma can endure for a long time. Not expecting others to pay respect, teaching the sūtras for others without expecting any kindness in return, the Dharma can endure for a long time. Having attained a little of the Way, do not be arrogant and indulgent. The Dharma can endure for a long time. Not thinking of various desires, the mind not being inclined towards other practices, this is how the Dharma can endure for a long time. Not being greedy for material gain, always enjoying secluded places, using grass mats as bedding. Bhikṣus upholding this, the Dharma can endure for a long time.

「比丘不得贪卧，卧者、不得思他事，法可久。乐守清净，不乐有为，法可久。乐贤共坐，守忍辱行，慎无诤讼，法可久。不得责望人礼敬，为人说经不用作恩德，法可久。小得道，头角莫自憍恣，法可久。不思诸情欲，心不投余行，如此者法可久。不贪利养，常乐隐处。草蓐为床。比丘持是，法可久。

"There are also seven dharmas that are all permitted."

「复有七法，皆听。」

The bhikṣus said, "We accept the teaching."

诸比丘言：「受教。」

If a person gives something to another, the others should not be resentful. The Dharma can endure. One should know shame and modesty. The Dharma can endure. Not being lax in the sutras and precepts. The Dharma can endure. Sitting and standing, not forgetting the sutras and Dharma. The Dharma can endure. Sitting and standing, not being weary of suffering. The Dharma can endure. Sitting and standing, illuminating the sutras and Dharma. The Dharma can endure. Studying and reciting the sutras, chanting and contemplating their profound meaning. Bhikṣus who uphold these seven dharmas, the Dharma can endure.

「人有惠彼物，余人不得有恨意，法可久。当知羞惭，法可久。不懈于经戒，法可久。坐起心不忘经法，法可久。坐起不相厌苦，法可久。坐起当明经法，法可久。学读经当讽诵惟其深义。比丘持是七法，法可久。

There are also seven dharmas. The Buddha, while in the world, was the teacher of the bhikṣus. The bhikṣus respected the precepts and instructions spoken by the Buddha, upheld the Dharma of precepts, and did not disrespectfully forget the kindness of the teacher. Upholding the precepts of the teacher, the Dharma can endure. Not following the inferior path, but following the Buddha's Dharma and discipline, the Dharma can endure. Respecting the bhikṣu saṃgha and receiving their teachings and precepts, being able to accept and use them without weariness, the Dharma can endure. Valuing those who uphold the precepts and are able to endure, the Dharma can endure. Following the sutras and precepts, the mind is without greed or attachment, always contemplating that human life is impermanent, the Dharma can endure. During the day, not being greedy for food, and at night, not being greedy for a good bed, the Dharma can endure. Self-restraint and contemplation of the disturbances of the world, not being lax in one's thoughts, not following evil thoughts, and not following deviant thoughts. When deviant thoughts arise, self-restraint and not following them, one should be upright in mind. The people of the world are deceived by the mind. Bhikṣus should not follow the deluded minds of the people of the world. Upholding these seven dharmas, the Dharma can endure.

「复有七法。佛在世间，为比丘作师。比丘敬佛所说戒勅，持受戒法，不慢念师恩，持师戒法，法可久。不得下道，当随佛法约束，法可久。敬比丘僧，受其教戒，得当承用无厌，法可久。当重持戒能忍辱者，法可久。随经戒心无所贪爱，常念人命非常，法可久。昼日不得贪饭食，夜卧不得贪好床，法可久。自勅顿思惟世间扰扰，所念莫懈，莫随恶心，莫随邪心，邪心来至，自戒莫随，当端心，世间人为心所欺，比丘莫随天下愚人心。持是七法，法可久。

"There are also seven dharmas."

「复有七法。」

The bhikṣu saṃgha said, "We accept the teaching."

比丘僧言：「受教。」

The bhikṣus said, "We accept the teaching."

「比丘当重经如愚人重珍宝，持经当父母，当用经生活，父母活人一世耳，经度人无数世，令人得泥洹道，用是故，法可久。不得贪食嗜味，食不得多，多者病人，少者复饥，趣可而已，不得味饭，法可久。当持身比土，日当忧死，不乐在生死中，生者多忧，忧父母、兄弟、妻子、亲属、奴婢、知识、畜生、田宅，是曹忧者，皆愚痴忧耳。如人有罪，为吏所取，虽有宗亲，不得前附，用是秽故；身当以比土，独来独去，当与身竞，法可久。勤修精进，端身口意行无过失，取道不难，法可久。惧降心意，不听六情，抑婬怒痴，无有邪行，法可久。坐众人中，不羞众人，为人所敬，心净端故，不恐不畏；取道不邪，如人为人所谗，为吏所捕，吏虽执之，其人不恐，用无所犯故；清净持戒，畏佛戒语，坐众人中不恐，心净故，法可久。敬慎不自憍慢，从慧者受经戒，见痴者当教经戒。比丘持是七法，法可久。

"There are also seven dharmas."

「复有七法。」

The bhikṣus said, "We accept the teaching."

比丘言：「受教。」

The bhikṣus said, "We accept the teaching."

「比丘常当念经，弃贪婬之态，常当念度世之道，自思惟身体，法可久。常持佛所说经，用著心中，既著心中，当端其心，弃恶心受好心，如人衣多垢，以淳灰浣之再三，遍垢便去；念佛语当持戒，去恶就善，法可久。当与心诤，不当随心，心欲婬怒痴不得听，常自戒于心，不得随心，如人从军，健者众人共将踧在军前锋，难得复还，意欲悔却，羞其后人，以受净戒，但当端心正意，在众人前，莫得在后，可先得道，法可久。当知所入法行多少深浅，熟与初头志，当日胜乐经，不厌苦、不择食、不择床卧，以道自劝乐，法可久。当敬同学，当持同学作兄弟，当端外内，外者身口过，内者心过，当思惟是二事，法可久。坐自思惟，九孔恶露无所不有，一孔主内，九孔皆出不净；饥饱寒热，皆为苦极，身体难得宜适，皆不净洁；内怀不净，风寒热见外，有不净反自覆，鼻见吐寒热，心皆不喜，有臭者，亦不恶不喜，比丘当端心内外，法可久。视天下人，帝王亦死，贫富贵贱，无有离死者，同死生之道，如人梦见好舍好园豪贵快乐，寤则不见，世间所有贫富贵贱，如人梦耳，自思惟世间，譬如人梦。比丘持是七法，思惟莫失，法可久。

The bhikṣus said, "We accept the teaching."

「复有七法。比丘僧当有慈心于天下，有慈心于佛，人骂不得应，不得恨，持慈心向天下，如狱中有系囚，常慈心相向。人处世间，亦当慈心转相愍念，比丘执心人骂无怒，将踧无喜生有是心，可以无忧。所以不与世人诤者，譬如牸牛食刍草，出乳乳出酪，酪出酥酥成醍醐，持心当如醍醐，奉佛戒，法可久。端舌莫妄语，语莫伤人意，舌当端，舌不端，使人不得道；舌致刀杖或致灭门，为道常当端舌，法可久。端心莫念恶，莫思婬，有婬心者，不成阿罗汉道。夜卧婬欲态欲来者，当念女人恶露，婬意即解；恨怒心来，当念生在地上不久，法可久。若有将请比丘饮食，余人不得念言：『是比丘独得，我不得。』不得有是。曹念比丘病人，傥有义持医药来与之，余人不得念言：『独视彼，不视我。』不得念是。人持衣物遗比丘，余人不得念言：『我独不得。』何以所行乞匃得者？以在钵中，不得言多少，心如是者，法可久。持戒法慎戒法，不知者当问知戒比丘。念佛念法念比丘僧，莫得休息，展转相承用；于衣中得虱，当有慈心向之，法可久。见死人言：『此人既死，不知经道，举家啼哭，及知识亲属不知，此人独如去。』比丘以得道，能知死人魂神所趣向，佛经不可不读，道不可不学，天下径道众多王道最大，佛道亦尔，最上道也。如数十人，各持弓箭射埻中，有前中者，有后中者，射不休息，必复中埻，行佛经道如此，莫懈莫念。前以得道，今我不得道，不得有是恨，如人射不休息会中埻，为比丘不止会得道，法可久。坐起当相承用，佛经当读讽诵，思惟其义，除馑清信士、清信女，如此七，法可久。奉是七七四十九法，如天下水，小溪水流入大溪，大溪水流入江，江流入海。比丘！当如水流入海，为道不止会，当得阿罗汉道。」

The Buddha rose from Rājagṛha and said to Ānanda, "Let us go to Pāṭaligāma." Ānanda replied, "Yes." As they were traveling from Magadha to Pāṭaligāma, they passed through a place called Rāḍhagāma. The Buddha stopped and called the bhikṣus, saying, "Listen." The bhikṣus responded, "We accept the teaching."

佛从王舍国起，呼阿难：「去至巴隣陬。」阿难言：「诺。」即从摩竭国行未至巴隣陬。中间有为罗致陬，佛至呼比丘僧：「皆听。」比丘诺：「受教。」

The Buddha said, "There are four kinds of suffering in the world that the Buddha knows but people do not. Because people do not know them, birth and death do not cease, and there is no time for rest. What are the four? The suffering of birth, old age, sickness, and death. People do not contemplate these four sufferings, and because they strongly endure them, birth and death do not cease and there is no time for rest. Therefore, the Buddha reveals these four sufferings to people. Although there are parents, wives, and children, they must all be separated, causing each other to worry and cry endlessly. All evil views are present before one's eyes. Therefore, the Buddha expounds the sutras to leave these four sufferings and uphold the eight precepts, so that the body may also be disgusted with them."

佛言：「天下有四痛，佛所知，人皆不知。用人不知故，生死不止，无休息时。何等为四？生痛、老痛、病痛、死痛，人不思惟是四痛，强力忍之故生死不绝无休止时，佛故发是四痛以告人。虽有父母妻子，皆当别离，转相忧思啼哭不止。诸所恶见日在目前，用是故佛出经，当离是四痛，奉八戒，身亦可厌。」

The Buddha said, "First, accept the Buddha's words. Second, distance oneself from desire and attachment, and follow the path without greed or contention. Third, do not speak falsely, frivolously, divisively, or harshly. Fourth, do not kill, steal, or violate others' wives. Fifth, do not be jealous, angry, or foolish. Sixth, sit and contemplate the four sufferings, focusing on them. Seventh, reflect on the body as impure. Eighth, view birth and death as the body that will become earth. The past Buddha also contemplated these four sufferings, and the future Buddha will also contemplate them. The past Buddha also practiced these eight precepts, and the future Buddha will also practice them. One should contemplate the profound meaning of the Buddha's sutras. Bhikṣus! Those who think of parents, wives, and children, and think of worldly livelihood, cannot attain the path of transcendence. Those who delight in the world and do not delight in the path, the path arises from the mind. Those with a correct mind can attain the path, those with a slightly upright mind can attain rebirth in heaven, and those who study the sutras can become human. One should cut off the paths of hell, animal realms, and hungry ghosts. The Buddha is the one who correctly explains the path of birth and death for the world. Bhikṣus! You should contemplate this."

佛言：「一者受佛语。二者当远离爱欲就道，无所贪诤。三者不妄言、绮语、两舌、恶口，四者不得杀盗犯人妇女。五者不得嫉妬瞋恚愚痴。六者坐自思惟四痛，著意中。七者思念身体皆不净。八者视生死身体皆当作土。去佛亦念是四痛，来佛亦念是四痛，去佛亦出是八戒，来佛亦出是八戒，当念佛经深义。诸比丘！有念于父母妻子，念世间生活者，不得度世道。乐世间心不乐道，道从心起，心正者可得道，心小端可得上天，明经者可得作人，当断地狱、畜生、饿鬼道。佛为天下正生死道，诸比丘当思惟之。」

The Buddha left from the village of Rājagṛha and called Ānanda, saying, "Go to the village of Bārāṇasī." Ānanda replied, "Yes," and followed the Buddha. At that time, there were 1,250 bhikṣus. When the Buddha arrived at the village of Bārāṇasī, he sat under a tree. The spirits of the village of Bārāṇasī immediately went to inform the Śākya family, who all came out, some holding mats, some holding blankets, and some holding lamps. They all went to where the Buddha was, bowed to the Buddha, and sat to one side.

佛从罗致聚，呼阿难：「去至巴隣聚。」阿难言：「诺。」即随佛去。时比丘僧，有千二百五十人，佛至巴隣聚，树下坐。巴隣聚鬼神即往告逝心理家，皆出有持席者，持毾［毯－炎＋登］者，持灯者，皆往至佛所，前为佛作礼，却在一面坐。

The Buddha told the Śākya family, "When a person is in the world, if they are greedy and indulge themselves, there are five evils. What are the five? First, wealth and possessions will decrease day by day. Second, they will not understand the meaning of the Way. Third, they will not be respected by the masses, and will have regrets at the time of death. Fourth, their bad reputation and evil name will be heard far and wide throughout the world. Fifth, after death, they will enter the hells and the three evil paths. If a person can subdue their mind and not indulge themselves, there are five benefits. What are the five? First, wealth and possessions will increase day by day. Second, they will have the practice of the Way. Third, they will be respected by the masses, and will have no regrets at the time of death. Fourth, their good reputation and virtuous name will be heard far and wide throughout the world. Fifth, after death, they will be reborn in a place of upper blessings and virtues. Not indulging oneself has these five benefits. You should reflect on this." After the Buddha finished speaking the sūtra for the Śākya family, they all rejoiced, bowed to the Buddha, and left.

佛告逝心理家：「人在世间，其有贪欲，自放恣者，即有五恶。何等为五？一者财产日耗减。二者不知道意。三者众人所不敬，死时有悔。四者丑名恶声，远闻天下。五者死入地狱三恶道中。人能伏心，不自放恣者，即有五善。何等为五？一者财产日增。二者有道行。三者众人所敬，至死无悔。四者好名善誉，远闻天下。五者死生上福德之处。不自放恣，有是五善，汝等自思惟之。」佛为逝心理家说经竟，皆欢喜，为佛作礼而去。

The Buddha went to the village of Āveṇī and sat under a tree. Using his divine eye, he saw the gods above, who were causing the virtuous spirits to protect this place. The Buddha rose from his meditation and left the village of Āveṇī to sit elsewhere. The Venerable Ānanda rose from his seat, adjusted his robe, bowed, and stood to one side. The Buddha asked Ānanda, "Who planned to build this city of Bārāṇasī?" He replied, "The Magadha minister Varsakāra planned to build this city to block the Vajjis."

佛起到阿卫聚，坐一树下，持道眼见上诸天，使贤善神营护此地。佛从宴坐起，出阿卫聚，更坐一处。贤者阿难正服从坐起，稽首毕一面住。佛问阿难：「谁图此巴隣聚，起城郭者？」对曰：「摩竭大臣雨舍公，图起此城，欲以遏绝越祇。」

The Buddha said, "Excellent, Ānanda! Varsakāra is wise to plan this. I see the gods of the Heaven of the Thirty-three and the gods of the Heaven of the Thirty-three, who are all protecting this place. Wherever the gods of the heavens protect the land, that land will surely be peaceful and prosperous. Moreover, this land is in the middle of the heavens. The god who rules this area is named Kind Intention. Wherever Kind Intention protects, that country will become more and more prosperous, and there will surely be many sages, wise and intelligent people, which other countries cannot match, and no one will be able to destroy it. When this city of Bārāṇasī is about to be destroyed, it will be destroyed by three things: first, a great fire, second, a great flood, and third, the people in the middle conspiring with outsiders."

佛言：「善哉！阿难！雨舍公之贤，乃知图此。吾见忉利天上诸神妙天，共护此地，其有土地，为天上诸神所护持者，其地必安且贵。又此地者，天之中也。主此四分野之天，名曰仁意，仁意所护者，其国久而益胜，必多圣贤智谋之人，余国不及，亦无有能坏者。是巴隣城，欲坏时，当以三事：一者大火，二者大水，三者中人与外人谋；乃坏此城。」

Minister Varsakāra heard that the Buddha and his assembly of monks were traveling from Magadha to here, so he immediately rode in a majestic carriage with five hundred carriages, left the city of Bārāṇasī, and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed to the Buddha and sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "Can you and the monks come to my house tomorrow for a small meal?" The Buddha silently did not respond. Varsakāra said this three times. The Buddha's silent acceptance of the Dharma was like saying, "Yes." Varsakāra then left and prepared his house for the Buddha and the assembly of monks. He set up seats, lit lamps, and prepared food.

大臣雨舍闻佛与比丘众，从摩竭国转游到此，即乘王威严车五百乘，出巴隣聚往到佛所，前为佛作礼，却坐一面，前白佛：「明日宁可与诸比丘俱于舍小食？」佛默然不应。雨舍公言如是者三。佛法默然者，如言：「可。」雨舍公即去，严舍中，为佛及诸比丘僧，施设床座，然灯火，饭食具。

The next day, Varsakāra went to invite the Buddha. At that time, the Buddha went with the assembly of monks of 1,250 people. After the meal was finished, the Buddha then recited the following vow: "May you attain the Way and not delight in the position of a prince. Although you may not be able to escape from officialdom in this life, if you now offer food to the Buddha and the assembly of monks, it will enable you to be free from officialdom in your future lives. In this world, those who are wise should offer food to virtuous monks. The monks will recite the vow, not abandoning officialdom. Seeking office should not involve greed, cruelty, advancement, joy, or encouragement. Those who serve in officialdom while avoiding these five mental states can attain freedom from harm. After death, they can be freed from the sins of hell. Varsakāra! You should contemplate this." The minister said, "Yes, I accept the teaching." The Buddha and the assembly of monks all rose and left. When the Buddha left the city gate, the minister immediately followed behind the Buddha, watching from which city gate the Buddha left, wishing to name the gate where the Buddha left as the Buddha Gate; the small stream that the Buddha crossed, he named the Buddha Stream.

明日，雨舍公往请佛，佛时与比丘僧千二百五十人俱往。饭食讫竟，佛即呪愿言：「使若得道，莫乐国公位，虽今世不得离于县官者，若今饭佛及比丘僧，使若后世脱于县官。世有明者，当饭食贤善道人，道人呪愿，不弃仕官，求官不可有贪心、酷心、进心、乐心、劝心，去是五心事县官者，可得无他，死后可得除地狱之罪。雨舍公！若自思惟。」公言：「诺，受教。」佛及比丘僧皆起去。佛出城门，公即随佛后，视佛从何城门出，欲名佛所出门为佛城门；所度小溪水，名为佛溪。

When the Buddha reached the riverbank, at that time, the great assembly of people wanted to cross. Some rode on large boats, some on small boats, and some used bamboo rafts to cross. The Buddha sat and thought, "When I was not yet a Buddha, I crossed this river by riding on a raft. Now, my body no longer rides on a raft to cross the water." The Buddha thought to himself, "I am the teacher who liberates people, enabling them to attain liberation from the worldly path. I will no longer be liberated by others." As soon as he thought this, all the bhikṣus had already crossed. The Buddha called Ānanda, "Let us go to the village of Kuṇḍaliya." Ānanda said, "Yes." The Buddha then went with the assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus to the village of Kuṇḍaliya. The Buddha said to the bhikṣus, "All listen. Hold the good mind and be without dispute with the world. Reflect on yourself and know impermanence. With wisdom, be mindful of the body. Hold the good mind and be without dispute with the world. Reflect on yourself and be enlightened. The enlightened one will then eliminate the tendencies of greed, anger, and ignorance. When these three tendencies are eliminated, one will attain liberation from the worldly path, no longer be reborn, and the mind will no longer wander. With a single-minded mind, one will have nothing to which to cling. Just as a king enjoys contemplating alone, among the many people, I alone am the master. Those who attain the Way and are liberated from the world also contemplate their minds, which have many tens of thousands of aspects. Now, I am the master of this mind, just as a king is the ruler of the people."

佛至江水边，时，人民大众多欲度，中有乘舫舡者、小舡者、竹桴度者，佛坐思念：「我未作佛时，度此曹水，乘桴船度，今我身不复乘桴船度水。」佛自念言：「我是度人师，使人得度世道，不复从人受度。」念适已，诸比丘皆已度。佛呼阿难：「俱至拘隣聚。」阿难言：「诺。」佛即与千二百五十比丘，悉俱至拘隣聚。佛言诸比丘：「皆听。持善心与天下无诤，自思惟当知无常，以慧忧身。持善心与天下无诤，自思惟以即明，明者即去贪婬、瞋恚、愚痴之态，三态去，即得度世道，不复生死，心不复走，一心无所著，如国王乐独思，若干人众中我独主。得道者，度世者，亦自思心，有若干千万端，今皆主是心，如国王典主人民。」

The Buddha then left the village of Kuṇḍaliya and called Ānanda, "Let us go to the country of Kanyakubja." Ānanda said, "Yes." The Buddha went with the assembly of bhikṣus to the country of Kanyakubja and sat under a Jambu tree. The Buddha sent the assembly of bhikṣus to beg for alms in the country of Kanyakubja. Upon returning, they reported to the Buddha, "In the country of Kanyakubja, there are many sick people and many deaths. Among them are ten upāsakas named Suparṇa, Kāla, Prāpta, Śikṣa, Śubha, Śubhaśīla, Śubhaśīla, Bṛhaspati, Saṃmata, and Śuddhodana. They all uphold the five precepts. Now they have all died." The bhikṣus all asked the Buddha, "Where will these upāsakas go after death?" The Buddha said, "Suparṇa and the other ten people, after death, will all be in the stage of non-returners.

佛复从拘隣聚，呼阿难：「俱至喜豫国。」阿难言：「诺。」佛与诸比丘俱至喜豫国揵提树下坐。佛遣诸比丘僧于喜豫国分卫，以还，白佛言：「喜豫国多病者，人民多死者，中有优婆塞，名玄鸟、时仙、初动、式贤、淑贤、快贤、伯宗、兼尊、德举、上净，等十人，皆优婆塞，持五戒，今皆死。」诸比丘俱问佛：「是诸优婆塞，死者皆趣何道？」佛言：「玄鸟等十人，死皆在不还道中。」

The Buddha told the assembly of bhikṣus, "You only see the deaths of ten people. With my divine eye, I see that five hundred upāsakas have died and will all be reborn in the stage of non-returners. There are also two hundred upāsakas, such as Nanda, who were free from lust, anger, and ignorance when they were alive. After death, they will all be reborn in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, attain the stage of srota-āpanna, and after seven more deaths and rebirths, they will attain the stage of arhat. Suparṇa and the other five hundred people will all attain the stage of non-returners and attain the stage of arhat in the heavens." The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "When you go out for alms and return, why do you only mention these ten upāsakas? You are trying to disturb the Buddha, thinking that the Buddha does not want to hear about this evil. What is there to fear? All who are born will die. Past, future, and present Buddhas all enter parinirvāṇa. Now that I have become a Buddha, I will also enter parinirvāṇa. It is for this body that I became a Buddha. For countless kalpas, I sought Buddhahood, ending the path of birth and death. Becoming a Buddha cuts off the root of birth and death. I know that the origin of this person is due to delusion. From delusion comes karmic formations. From karmic formations comes consciousness. From consciousness comes name and form. From name and form come the six sense bases. From the six sense bases come contact. From contact comes feeling. From feeling comes craving. From craving comes grasping. From grasping comes becoming. From becoming comes birth. From birth comes old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, dejection, and agitation. Thus, the great mass of suffering arises from the accumulation of karma. The Buddha thus contemplates the origin of birth and death, like a chariot with wheels, which moves without rest. People obtain birth and death due to delusion. Those with wisdom uphold the path and no longer undergo birth and death."

佛告诸比丘僧：「若曹但见十人死，佛持天眼见，见优婆塞死者五百人，皆生不还道中。复有二百优婆塞，如难提等，生时无婬态、无怒态、无痴态，死皆生忉利天上，得沟港道，当复七死七生便得阿罗汉道。玄鸟等五百人，皆得不还道，自于天上得应真道。」佛告诸比丘：「若行分卫来还，何为道是十优婆塞？若曹故欲扰佛，谓佛不欲闻是恶，佛亦当何所畏难，其有生者皆当死，过去、当来、现在诸佛，皆般泥洹。今我作佛，亦当般泥洹，用是身故作佛，若干劫求佛，止生死之道，作佛绝生死之本，知是人本从痴故，从痴为行，从行为识，从识为字色，从字色为六入，从六入为栽，从栽为痛，从痛为爱，从爱为求，从求为有，从有为生，从生为老死，忧悲苦不如意恼，如是合大苦阴堕习。佛故思惟生死本，如车有轮，车行无休息时，人从痴故得生死，以去痴便痴灭，以痴灭便行灭，以行灭便识灭，以识灭便字色灭，以字色灭便六入灭，以六入灭便栽灭，以栽灭便痛灭，以痛灭便爱灭，以爱灭便求灭，以求灭便有灭，以有灭便生灭，以生灭便老死灭，以老死灭便忧悲苦不如意恼灭，如是合大阴堕习为尽。佛故先为若曹说，痴故有生死，慧者持道，不复生死。」

The Buddha said, "You should remember to uphold the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha, and the pure precepts. Follow the teachings of the Buddha's sutras. You should contemplate and focus your minds, so that you will no longer undergo birth and death, and be free from the suffering of sorrow and lamentation.

佛言：「若曹当念奉佛法圣众净戒，相承用教佛经，当思惟端心，不复更生死，无忧哭之患。」

The Buddha, from the Joyful Grove, called Ānanda, saying, "Go to the country of Vaiśālī." Ānanda replied, "Yes." The Buddha, from the Joyful Grove, went to the country of Vaiśālī. Before reaching seven li, the Buddha stopped in the Venuvana Grove. There was a courtesan named Venu, with five hundred courtesan disciples. Hearing that the Buddha had come to the Venuvana Grove, she ordered her five hundred courtesan disciples to adorn themselves and their carriages, and went out of the city to see the Buddha, kneeling and paying homage to him. At that time, the Buddha was in the Venuvana Grove, together with a thousand bhikṣus, expounding the sūtras for them. Seeing Venu and her five hundred courtesan disciples all well-adorned, the Buddha instructed the bhikṣus, "You see Venu and her five hundred courtesan disciples all bowing their heads, composed and focused. Although they are well-adorned, they are like painted jars. On the outside, they are beautifully painted, but inside, they are only impure, sealed and unopenable. If opened, the foul stench will immediately reach. Venu and her disciples are all like these jars. Any bhikṣu should be able to see their strength. What is the strength of seeing? To abandon evil and embrace goodness, not to indulge in lustful thoughts. It is better to break one's own bones and heart and burn one's body than to follow one's mind and do evil. Not only are strong men strong, but those who can compose their own minds are superior to strong men. I have been struggling with my mind for countless kalpas, not allowing myself to follow my mind. With diligent effort, I have attained Buddhahood. Bhikṣus should be able to compose their own minds. The mind has long been in impurity. Now it can also be pulled out. Contemplate the five viscera of the body. It is also possible to stop the methods of birth and death. Looking outside is also suffering, looking inside is also suffering. Compose and focus the mind."

佛从喜豫聚，呼阿难：「至维耶梨国。」阿难言：「诺。」佛从喜豫聚，至维耶梨国，未至七里，佛止［木＊奈］园中。有婬女人，字［木＊奈］女，有五百婬女弟子，于城中闻佛以来在［木＊奈］园中，皆勅五百婬女弟子，令好庄衣严车，从城中出，至佛所欲见佛，为佛跪拜。时，佛在［木＊奈］园中，与千比丘俱，为诸比丘说经。佛见［木＊奈］女与五百婬弟子俱，皆好庄衣，佛勅诸比丘：「汝曹见［木＊奈］女与五百婬弟子俱，皆低头，端若心，虽好庄衣来，譬如画瓶，外有好画，中但有不净，封结不可发解，解者不净臭即至。［木＊奈］女皆是瓶辈，其有比丘当见力。何等为见力？去恶就善，不听婬态，宁自破骨破心燔烧身体，终不随心作恶，不但力士为多力，能自端心，胜于力士。佛与心诤以来，其劫无数，不听随心，勤力精进，自致作佛。比丘可自齐端其心，心久在不净中，今亦可自拔擢，自思惟身体五藏，亦可齐止生死之法，视外亦苦，视中亦苦，端若心。」

Venu arrived, got off the carriage, went to where the Buddha was, paid homage to the Buddha, and sat to one side. The bhikṣus all bowed their heads. The Buddha said, "Why have you come?" Venu said, "I have often heard that the Buddha is honored by all the devas, so I have come to pay homage." The Buddha said, "Venu! Do you enjoy being a woman?" Venu said, "The devas have made me a woman. I do not enjoy it." The Buddha said, "If you do not enjoy being a woman, who made you keep five hundred courtesan disciples?" Venu said, "They are all poor people. I support them." The Buddha said, "No. If you do not dislike the suffering of being a woman, the impurity of menstruation, being bound and beaten, and not being free, and do not dislike your own body, you should follow five hundred men." Venu said, "It is because of my foolishness. The wise would not do this." The Buddha said, "If it is really so, it is good." Venu then knelt and said to the Buddha, "Please invite the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha for tomorrow's meal." The Buddha silently did not respond. Venu was greatly delighted, immediately rose, paid homage to the Buddha, and left.

［木＊奈］女到，下车至佛所，为佛作礼却坐一面，诸比丘皆低头。佛言：「若何缘来？」［木＊奈］女言：「我数闻佛尊于诸天，故来跪拜。」佛言：「［木＊奈］女！若乐作女人耶？」［木＊奈］女言：「天使我作女人耳，我不乐也。」佛言：「汝不乐作女人者，谁使汝畜五百婬弟子者？」［木＊奈］女言：「是皆贫民，我养护之。」佛言：「不然。若不厌女人之病，月期不净，拘绊捶杖，不得自在，不厌汝身，反更从五百人。」［木＊奈］女言：「我痴所致，慧者不为是。」佛言：「审如是者善。」［木＊奈］女即长跪白佛：「明日请佛及比丘僧。」佛默然不应。［木＊奈］女大喜，即起为佛作礼而去。

Not long after she left, the wealthy and noble families of Vaiśālī heard that the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha had come, seven li away, in the Venuvana Grove. They immediately rode in royal carriages, all adorned and mounted, and went out to pay homage and make offerings to the Buddha. Some rode blue horses and blue carriages, with blue clothes, blue canopies, blue banners, and blue flags, and officials all in blue; some rode yellow horses and yellow carriages, with yellow clothes, yellow canopies, yellow banners, and yellow flags, and officials all in yellow; some rode red horses and red carriages, with red clothes, red canopies, red banners, and red flags, and officials all in red; some rode white horses and white carriages, with white clothes, white canopies, white banners, and white flags, and officials all in white; some rode black horses and black carriages, with black clothes, black canopies, black banners, and black flags, and officials all in black. The Buddha saw from afar the tens of thousands of carriages and riders coming, and said to the bhikṣus, "If you want to see the attendants and followers of Śakra Devānām-Indra in the Nandana Grove, these wealthy and noble families are no different." When the wealthy and noble families arrived at the entrance of the road, they all got off their carriages and went to where the Buddha was. Those in front knelt before the Buddha, those in the middle all bowed their heads, and those at the end all sat with their hands clasped. The Buddha asked, "Where have you come from?" The wealthy and noble families said, "We heard that the Buddha is here, so we have come out of the city to pay homage." Among them was a man named Bindu, who rose and went before the Buddha, looking at the Buddha intently. The Buddha asked, "What are you looking at?" Bindu said, "Looking up to heaven and down to earth, all are moved by the Buddha. I look at the Buddha without ever tiring of it." The Buddha said, "Bindu! Look at the Buddha carefully. It has been a long time since there was a Buddha again. When there is a Buddha, one should accept the Buddha's teachings." Among them, four or five hundred wealthy and noble families said, "Bindu has great virtue and can discuss the Dharma with the Buddha." Bindu said, "I have long heard the Buddha's sūtras. I have been thinking about this sūtra for a long time. I have only seen it today. I have a compassionate and filial mind towards the Buddha." The Buddha said, "In the world, few are like Bindu, who is compassionate and filial towards the teacher.

去未久，维耶离豪姓诸理家，闻佛比丘僧俱来，去城七里，在［木＊奈］园中。即乘王威，皆严驾乘而出，欲觐见供养佛。中有乘青马青车，青衣青盖，青幢青幡，官属皆青；中有乘黄马黄车，黄衣黄盖，黄幢黄幡，官属皆黄；中有乘赤马赤车，赤衣赤盖，赤幢赤幡，官属皆赤；中有乘白马白车，白衣白盖，白幢白幡，官属皆白；中有乘黑马黑车，黑衣黑盖，黑幢黑幡，官属皆黑。佛遥见车骑数十万人来，即告诸比丘：「汝欲见忉利天上帝释苑中侍从出入者，如此诸理家无有异也。」诸理家行到道口，皆下车至佛所，前者为佛跪，中央者皆低头，最后者但叉手皆坐。佛问：「若曹所从来？」诸理家言：「闻佛在是故，出城跪拜。」中有一人字宾自，起至佛前，熟视佛。佛问：「若何等视？」宾自言：「举天上天下，皆为佛倾动，我视佛无厌极。」佛言：「宾自！庄当熟视佛，久远乃复有佛耳，曼有佛时，当受佛教命。」中有四五百理家言：「宾自有大德，与佛共谈。」宾自言：「我遥闻佛经，我念作是经久，我适今日乃得见耳，我有慈孝心于佛。」佛言：「天下人少有如宾自辈，慈孝于师。」

The Buddha told Bindu, "When the Buddha appears in the world, he knows the way of birth and death in the world. He preaches the sūtras to enlighten the devas, humans, and dragons in heaven and earth, and none are not moved. This is the Buddha's first majestic spiritual power. Those who read the Buddha's sūtras and attain the Way through self-discipline, this is the Buddha's second majestic spiritual power. When the Buddha preaches the sūtras in the world, the wise are all delighted, and those who hear are all delighted. Those who study pass it on to each other, teach and guide each other, and discipline their minds. This is the Buddha's third majestic spiritual power. Those who study the Buddha's sūtras are all delighted, like fools who obtain gold. The most intelligent attain the state of arhat, the second can attain the state of non-returner, the third can attain the state of once-returner, the fourth can attain the state of stream-enterer, and the fifth who uphold the five precepts of an upāsaka can attain heaven. Those who uphold the three precepts can become humans. When the Buddha appears in the world, he manifests this path. This is the Buddha's fourth majestic spiritual power." The Buddha told Bindu, "If you come and look at the Buddha carefully, if you say that you have heard the name of the Buddha many times but rarely see the Buddha. At that time, there were hundreds of thousands of people sitting there, all not asking the Buddha. If you alone ask, this is the Buddha's fifth majestic spiritual power." The Buddha told Bindu, "In the world, there are few who are wise. Those who are unfaithful are many. Those who receive the Buddha's teachings and the good words of the teacher, uphold the precepts and Dharma of the teacher, are protected by all the ghosts, spirits, and dragons. The officials dare not summon them wantonly. One should be compassionate and filial towards the teacher. The teacher does not seek anything from the disciples. In front of the teacher, one should respect the teacher. Behind the back, one should praise the teacher. After the teacher's death, one should always remember. Now Bindu is a hero among humans, who delights in the Dharma and pure precepts.

佛告宾自：「佛出于天下，知天下生死之道，说经开化天上天下及鬼龙，无不倾侧者，是佛第一威神。其有读佛经，自端心得道者，是佛第二威神。佛于天下说经，贤者无不喜，闻者无不喜，学者转相教，转相授导，转相端心，是佛第三威神。其学佛经者皆喜，如愚人得金，上智者得应真道，第二者可得不还，第三者可得频来，第四者可得沟港，第五持优婆塞五戒者，可得天上，持三戒者，可得作人。佛出在天下，因现此道，是佛第四威神。」佛告宾自：「若来熟视佛，若说数闻佛名希见佛。时坐中有数十万人，皆不问佛，若独问，是佛第五威神。」佛告宾自：「天下智慧者少，无反复者多，受佛经道，受师好语，持师戒法，诸鬼神龙，无不护视者，吏不敢妄召呼，当慈孝于师，师不从弟子有所求索，在师前当敬师，背后当称誉师，师死常当忆念，于今宾自者，人中雄善乐法清戒。」

The wealthy and noble families of Vaiśālī invited the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha to enter the city for a meal the next morning. The Buddha said, "The daughter of Venuvana will come in the morning to invite the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha." All the wealthy and noble families went together.

维耶梨逝心理家，请佛明日旦，宁可与诸比丘入城饭食？佛言：「［木＊奈］女朝旦来请佛及比丘僧。」诸逝心理家，皆俱去。

The next morning, the daughter of Venuvana came to where the Buddha was and said to the Buddha, "The seats have been arranged and the food and drink are all ready. I hope the World-Honored One will deign to come." The Buddha said, "If you go straight there, I will follow after you now." The Buddha got up, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered the city with the bhikṣus. Tens of thousands of people in the city watched. Among them were virtuous upāsakas who all said, "The Buddha is like the bright moon, and the disciples are like the bright stars, following the moon." At that time, the Buddha was indeed so.

［木＊奈］女明日旦来至佛所，白佛言：「已设座，饭食具皆已办，愿天尊屈威神。」佛言：「若径去，我今随后。」佛起著衣持钵，与比丘俱入城。城中观者数十万人，中有贤善优婆塞，皆言：「佛如明月，弟子如明星，与月相随。」时，佛好如是。

The Buddha arrived at the daughter of Venuvana's house, took his seat, and performed the water ritual. After the Buddha and the bhikṣu saṃgha had finished their meal and washed their hands, the daughter of Venuvana brought a small stool and sat in front of the Buddha. The Buddha told the daughter of Venuvana, "The sages and those who are noble and wealthy in the world only value the purity of precepts and the illumination of the Buddha's teachings. Their words in the assembly are all worth listening to. Wherever they go, they are respected and loved. Now, in this world, those who do not crave wealth and desire, and who uphold the Buddha's spiritual teachings, will not be reborn in heaven after death." The Buddha told the daughter of Venuvana, "Take good care of yourself and uphold the five precepts." The Buddha left with the bhikṣus.

佛至［木＊奈］女家，就坐行澡水，佛及诸比丘僧饭食竟，澡手已毕，［木＊奈］女持小机，于佛前坐。佛告［木＊奈］女：「圣人及天下尊豪富贵，唯尚戒净，明佛诸经。坐中语言，无不好听，其所行处，无不敬爱者，今在天下作人，不贪财色，奉佛神化，死无不生天上者。」佛告［木＊奈］女：「善自爱重，持五戒。」佛与比丘俱去。

The Buddha left the country of Vaiśālī and told Ānanda, "Let us go together to the Bamboo Grove." Ānanda said, "Yes." He also heard that in the Bamboo Grove, rice and grain were very expensive, and it was difficult for the bhikṣus to beg for alms. The Buddha sat in meditation and thought, "In the country of Vaiśālī, there is a famine and grain prices are soaring. The village is small and cannot provide alms for the bhikṣus." The Buddha thought of sending the bhikṣus to other countries where rice and grain were cheap to beg for alms. The Buddha told the bhikṣu saṃgha, "In the Bamboo Grove, rice and grain are soaring in price, and it is difficult to beg for alms. There is the country of Śrāvastī where there is plenty of grain. In the four directions around Vaiśālī, grain prices are all high. I will stay here in the Bamboo Grove with Ānanda alone." The bhikṣus accepted the Buddha's instruction and all went to the country of Śrāvastī.

佛从维耶梨国出，告阿难：「宁可俱至竹芳聚。」阿难言：「诺。」又闻竹芳聚，米谷大贵，诸比丘求分卫难得。佛坐思惟，维耶梨国饥馑，谷籴腾贵，其聚狭小，不能供诸比丘分卫，佛思念，欲遣诸比丘，分布余国贱米谷处行分卫。佛告诸比丘僧：「是竹芳聚，米谷腾贵，求分卫难得；彼间有沙罗提国丰熟。是维耶梨四界，米谷皆贵，我自与阿难，俱留此竹芳聚。」诸比丘受佛教，皆去至沙罗提国。

The Buddha went with Ānanda to the Bamboo Grove, and his body was in great pain, wishing to enter parinirvāṇa. The Buddha thought to himself, "The bhikṣus have all left. If I enter parinirvāṇa alone, there will be no one to uphold the precepts." Ānanda rose from under a tree and went to the Buddha, asking, "Is the Blessed One's body not well?" The Buddha said, "It is not well. It is very serious. I wish to enter parinirvāṇa." Ānanda said, "Please do not enter parinirvāṇa yet. Wait until the bhikṣu saṃgha gathers." The Buddha told Ānanda, "I have already given the sūtras and precepts. You should only uphold and practice them according to the sūtras and precepts. I am also among the bhikṣu saṃgha. The bhikṣu saṃgha has already understood the Buddha's teachings and instructions. The duties and methods of serving the teacher have all been entrusted to the disciples. The disciples should only uphold and practice them diligently. Now my body is in pain. I have used the Buddha's spiritual power to treat the illness, but I no longer use my mind to think about the illness. It is like a slight improvement in condition."

佛与阿难俱，至竹芳聚，身皆大痛，欲般泥洹。佛自念：「诸比丘皆去，我独般泥洹，不事无教戒？」阿难从一树下起至佛所，问佛：「圣体不和宁差不？」佛言：「未差大剧，欲般泥洹。」阿难言：「且莫般泥洹，须比丘僧聚会。」佛告阿难：「我已有经戒，若曹但当案经戒奉行之，我亦在比丘僧中，比丘僧皆已知佛所教勅，事师法皆以付诸弟子，弟子但当持行熟学。今我身皆痛，我持佛威神治病，不复持心思病，如小差状。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Now the Buddha is already eighty years old. It is like an old carriage without strength. My body is like this, without strength. I did not tell you before that there is no one who is born on this earth who does not die. There is a heaven above called the Unthinkable Realm, where the lifespan is eighty-four koṭi nayuta kalpas. Even there one will eventually die. Therefore, I have established the sūtras in the world to cut off the root of birth and death. After my parinirvāṇa, do not abandon these sūtras and precepts. Continue to uphold them and reflect on the internal and external. Uphold the Dharma of the precepts, and make the internal and external as usual. Those who uphold the Dharma of the precepts among the four groups of disciples are all the Buddha's disciples. Those who study the Buddha's teachings are all the Buddha's disciples. The Buddha abandoned the wheel-turning king, and the heavens and people of the world can also worry about their own illnesses. Abandon the tendencies of desire, anger, and ignorance."

佛语阿难：「今佛年已尊，且八十，如故车无坚强，我身体如此无坚强，我本不为若曹说，无有堕地不死者，最上有天，名不想入，寿八十亿四千万劫，会当复死，用是故起经于天下，断生死之根本。我般泥洹以后，无得弃是经戒，转相承用，自思中外，端心正行，当持戒法，中外令如常。其有四辈弟子，持戒法者，皆佛弟子，其有学佛经道者，皆是佛弟子。佛弃转轮王，忧天上天下人，亦可自忧疾，去婬态、怒态、痴态。」

The Buddha left the Bamboo Grove and called Ānanda, saying, "Let us return to the country of Vaiśālī." Ānanda said, "As instructed." The Buddha returned to the country of Vaiśālī, entered the city to beg for alms, and then sat in the open under the tree of the Swiftly Healing Spirit, contemplating the matter of birth and death. Ānanda sat under a tree not far away, contemplating the matter of the inner chamber. He rose and went to the Buddha, bowed to the Buddha, and said, "Why not enter parinirvāṇa?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "The country of Vaiśālī is greatly joyful, the country of Uruvela is greatly joyful, the land of the Swiftly Healing Spirit is greatly joyful, the gate of the city of Śāṭha is greatly joyful, the streets of the city are greatly joyful, the shrine named Puṣpa is greatly joyful, the world of Jambudvīpa is greatly joyful, the country of Vṛji is greatly joyful, the country of Śāpā is greatly joyful, the gate of the city of Sāṃkāśya is greatly joyful, the country of Magadha is greatly joyful, the country of Pūrvavideha is greatly joyful, the country of Uttarakuru is greatly joyful, the river of the Hīmavat Mountains is greatly joyful, and the land of Jambudvīpa, where the five colors are like paintings, is greatly joyful for those born there."

佛从竹芳聚，呼阿难：「且复还至维耶梨国。」阿难言：「受教。」佛还维耶梨国，入城持钵行分卫，还止急疾神树下露坐，思惟生死之事。阿难远在一树下，思惟阴房之事，起至佛所，为佛作礼已，住白佛言：「何以不般泥洹？」佛告阿难：「维耶梨国大乐，越耶国大乐，急疾神地大乐，沙达诤城门大乐，城中街曲大乐，社名浮沸大乐，阎浮利天下大乐，越祇大乐，遮波国大乐，萨城门大乐，摩竭国大乐，满沸大乐，郁提大乐，醯连溪出金山大乐，阎浮利内地，所生五色如画，人在] cf.【丽】其中生者大乐。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "If a bhikṣu or bhikṣuṇī upholds the four dharmas, contemplates the right mind, does not follow the mind, also contemplates goodness externally, also contemplates goodness internally, and the mind has no further greed or joy, the mind is not startled or frightened, and does not run again, if a bhikṣu or bhikṣuṇī upholds the mind with these four dharmas, it is called the four bases of supernormal power. One can live for an eon without dying." At that time, Māra entered Ānanda's abdomen. The Buddha again told Ānanda, "This is still possible.

佛告阿难：「其有比丘比丘尼，持四法熟思正心不随心，外亦思善，中亦思善，心亦无所复贪乐，心不惊恐不复走，比丘比丘尼，其有持志意如是四法，名四神足，欲不死一劫可得。」魔时入阿难腹中。佛复告阿难：「如是尚可。」

Ānanda again said, "Why not enter parinirvāṇa? The time is right to enter parinirvāṇa." The Buddha again said, "The world of Jambudvīpa is greatly joyful. Those who know these four bases of supernormal power can live in the world for an eon and more." The Buddha told Ānanda this three times, but Ānanda did not respond to the matter of the four bases of supernormal power. The Buddha told Ānanda, "Go back and contemplate under the tree by yourself."

阿难复言：「佛何以不般泥洹？时足可般泥洹。」佛复言：「阎浮利大乐，其有知是四神足者，当可在天地间一劫有余。」佛告阿难如是者再三，阿难不应四神足事。佛告阿难：「若却于树下自思惟。」

The Buddha arose and went to sit under a tree by the Helian River. Māra came to where the Buddha was and said, "Why not enter parinirvāṇa?" The Buddha said, "Bah! Wicked Māra! It is not yet time to enter parinirvāṇa. I must wait until my fourfold disciples are wise and attain the path. I must wait until the gods in the heavens and the people in the world, as well as the ghosts and spirits, attain wisdom and the path. I must wait until my sutras and teachings spread throughout the world. It is not yet time to enter parinirvāṇa." Māra knew that the Buddha would enter parinirvāṇa and joyfully departed.

佛起至醯连溪水边树下坐，魔来至佛所言：「何以不般泥洹？」佛言：「咄！弊魔！未可般泥洹，须我四辈弟子黠慧得道，须我天上诸天世间人民逮及鬼神智慧得道，须我经法遍布天下，未可般泥洹。」魔知佛当般泥洹，欢喜而去。

The Buddha sat and contemplated, "I can also abandon my lifespan. I wish to abandon my lifespan." At that time, the earth shook greatly, and all the ghosts and spirits were startled. Ānanda was startled while sitting under a tree and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head to the Buddha's feet and stood to one side. He said to the Buddha, "While I was sitting under a tree, the earth shook greatly. I was startled and my hair stood on end. I have never seen such an earthquake before.

佛坐自思惟：「亦可放弃寿命，意欲放弃寿命。」时天地大动，诸鬼神皆惊。阿难于树下，惊起至佛所，前以头面著佛足，却在一面住，白佛：「我于树下坐，天地大动，我惊衣毛为起，我生不更是曹地动。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "There are eight causes for the earth to shake. What are the eight? The earth rests on water, the water rests on wind, and the wind supports the water. It is like looking up at the sky from the ground. Sometimes the wind moves the water, the water moves the earth, and the earth shakes as a result. This is the first shaking. There are arhats who, out of their own dignity, wish to test their spiritual powers. They wish to make the earth shake, so they press down on the earth with two fingers. The earth and sky shake greatly. This is the second shaking. There are gods with great spiritual power who wish to shake the earth, and the earth shakes greatly. This is the third shaking. When the Buddha was a bodhisattva, he descended from the fourth dhyāna heaven and entered his mother's womb. At that time, the earth and sky shook greatly. When the bodhisattva was born from his mother's right side, the earth and sky shook greatly. When the bodhisattva attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, the earth and sky shook greatly. When the Buddha rose to preach the sutras, the earth and sky shook greatly. When the Buddha abandoned his lifespan, the earth and sky shook greatly." The Buddha told Ānanda, "In three months from now, the Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa. The earth and sky will shake greatly again. This is the eighth shaking.

佛为阿难说：「天地动有八事。何等为八？地在水上，水在风上，风持水，如从地上望天，或时风动水，水动地，地因动，是为一动。有阿罗汉尊贵，自欲试威神，意欲令地动，因以手两指案地，天地为大动，是为二动。中有天威神大，意欲动地，地即为大动，是为三动。佛为菩萨时，从第四兜术天来下，入母腹中，时，天地为大动。菩萨从右脇生时，天地为大动。菩萨得道为佛时，天地为大动。佛起本经时，天地为大动。佛放弃寿命，天地为大动。」佛告阿难：「今佛却后三月当般泥洹，天地为当复大动，是为八动。」

Ānanda heard the Buddha's self-determination of three months and immediately wept, asking, "Is it not to abandon your lifespan?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "It is to abandon your lifespan.

阿难闻佛自期三月，即啼而问：「得无以弃寿命？」佛告阿难：「是以弃寿命。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "I have heard from the Buddha that if a bhikṣu has these four dharmas, they are called the four bases of supernormal power, and they can live for an eon. The Buddha's virtues surpass the four bases of supernormal power. Why not live for more than an eon?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "It is because of this. It is what you have done. 佛复呼阿难：「去至醯连溪水边，我欲洗浴身体。」阿难言：「诺。」佛独与阿难俱，至醯连溪水边，佛解衣，自取水灌浴。佛告阿难：「朝华氏子淳家饭我，今日夜半，当般泥洹，若告淳言：『佛从若饭已，夜半当般泥洹，若当欢喜。』语淳：『莫啼哭，若一饭佛得五福，若饭佛，佛持若饭食气力用般泥洹，淳得长寿，得端正，得富贵尊豪，得生天上。佛可敬，一饭佛得五福。』」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "There is a bhikṣu named Candana. He is quick-tempered and often scolds and quarrels with other bhikṣus. After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, how should we deal with the Buddha's teachings and precepts?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "After my parinirvāṇa, do not speak to him. If other bhikṣus do not speak to him, the bhikṣu Candana will reflect and feel deeply ashamed for often quarreling with other bhikṣus."

阿难白佛：「有一比丘，名栴檀比丘，急性憙骂，数鬪诸比丘，佛般泥洹以后，我曹诸比丘，当云何共事佛经戒？」佛语阿难：「我般泥洹已，若曹莫复与语，诸比丘不与语，栴檀比丘，当思惟] cf.【丽】怀重惭愧悔数鬪诸比丘。」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Arrange a bed with the head to the north. I have a severe back pain and want to lie down." Ānanda immediately arranged a bed with a pillow. The Buddha lay on his right side, with his knees drawn up and his feet stacked, contemplating the path of non-action.

佛告阿难：「施床使北首，我背大痛欲卧。」阿难即施床著枕，佛偃右脇卧，屈膝累脚，卧思无为之道。

The Buddha lay down and called Ānanda, "Do you know the seven matters of the mind? What are the seven? First, having determination; second, being clear about the scriptures; third, not being lax in the scriptures; fourth, not being greedy for sleep but delighting in the scriptures; fifth, regulating the mind; sixth, purifying the mind; seventh, viewing the impurities within the body. If a bhikṣu has these seven dharmas, he can know for himself the path to liberation." Ānanda thought, "The Buddha is lying down lazily." The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do you think the Buddha is lying down lazily?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "If one is not lax in the scriptures, not lax in sitting and rising, and wishes to become a Buddha, one can attain the Buddha's words." Having spoken, he immediately rose and sat.

佛卧呼阿难：「若知七意之事不？何等为七？一者有志，二者明经，三者不懈于经，四者不贪卧当憙经，五者政心，六者净心，七者视身中恶露。比丘有是七法，以自知得度世道。」阿难意念佛懈卧，佛告阿难：「若意念佛懈卧耶？」佛告阿难：「人不懈于经，不懈于坐起欲作佛者，可得佛语已即起坐。」

At that time, there was a bhikṣu named Jiebin who came to Ānanda and said, "I want to ask a question." Ānanda said, "The Buddha's holy body is not well. Please wait." The Buddha immediately knew from within what the bhikṣu wanted to ask. The Buddha told Ānanda, "Call the bhikṣu to come in." Upon entering and seeing the Buddha, the Buddha said, "Ask what you want to ask." The bhikṣu said, "The Buddha is sick. Please put aside the sūtra. There is no need to speak further. The Buddha spoke of seven matters. We have heard them and will uphold them. Please stop, Buddha. Do not speak the sūtra." The Buddha told the bhikṣu, "When I lay down earlier, Ānanda thought that the Buddha had a lazy mind. Why lie down? I arose to speak of the seven matters for this reason." The bhikṣu said, "The Buddha is the most honored in heaven and on earth. Why not request medicine from the heavens to cure the illness?" The Buddha said, "Just as a house that has been standing for a long time will eventually collapse, the ground continues to be as it was before. The Buddha's mind is as stable as the ground. The body is like an old house. The mind is without illness, but only the body is ill." The Buddha said, "One should worry about seven matters. One should worry about upholding the precepts with the body." The bhikṣu said, "Now the Buddha is about to enter parinirvāṇa with a physical illness. How much more so for ordinary people?" The bhikṣu said, "A fledgling bird relies on its parents for food to survive. Now the Buddha abandons us and enters parinirvāṇa. Whom shall we rely on?" The World-Honored One said again, "My sūtras do not say that there is no birth and no death. Bhikṣus should remember to uphold the Buddha's weighty precepts." The bhikṣu turned and left.

时，有一比丘名劫宾，来语阿难言：「我欲问一事。」阿难言：「佛圣体不和且莫。」佛即从里知比丘欲问事，佛告阿难：「呼比丘来入。」入与佛相见，佛言：「所欲问者当问。」比丘言：「佛有疾且置经不须复说。佛说七事者，我曹以闻当持，佛且止莫说经。」佛告比丘：「我向卧，阿难念佛有懈堕之意，何以卧，我以是故起说七事。」比丘言：「佛是天上天下之尊，云何不从天请药，可使病愈？」佛言：「如人舍宅，久故皆当坏，地续安如故。佛心安如地，身如故舍，心无病，但身有病耳。」佛言：「忧七事，忧身持戒。」比丘言：「今佛当般泥洹有身病，何况凡人？」比丘言：「燕生子，怙父母得食以生活，今佛舍我曹般泥洹，我曹当依谁？」世尊又曰：「吾经不说，无生不死者，比丘当念持佛重戒。」比丘旋出。

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Quickly go to Śrāvastī and prepare a bed for the Buddha, with the head facing north. Tonight at midnight, the Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa." Ānanda obeyed and went to prepare the bed with the head facing north. After finishing, he returned and said, "The bed has been prepared." The Buddha arose and went to Śrāvastī. He lay down on the bed, leaning on his right side. There was a bhikṣu named Upavāna standing in front of the Buddha. The Buddha said, "Do not stand in front of me." Ānanda said, "In the twenty-five years I have served the Buddha, I have never seen a bhikṣu come forward without asking Ānanda." The Buddha said, "This bhikṣu has the greatest spiritual power among those devas. Hearing that the Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa, he came forward out of greed to see the Buddha." Ānanda asked, "Does only this deva know that the Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa? Are there other devas?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "Within a radius of four hundred and eighty li from the border of Kuśinagara, they are all devas who have heard that the Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa. They are crying and coming, some pulling their hair, some tearing their clothes, some blocking their hearts and fainting. They lament, 'Alas! The Buddha abandons us and enters parinirvāṇa forever. How swift! The Buddha is the great light, the eye of the three realms. Now entering parinirvāṇa, the eye of the three realms is extinguished.'"

佛告阿难：「疾去为佛于盐呵沙，施床使北首，今日夜半，佛当般泥洹。」阿难奉命，之彼施床床头北首。毕，还白言：「施床已竟。」佛起至盐呵沙，得床猗右脇卧。有一比丘，名优和洹，当佛前立。佛言：「无当吾前。」阿难白言：「自吾亲侍二十五年，未曾见比丘直自来进不问阿难。」佛言：「是比丘，于彼诸天，最有威神，闻佛灭度故，直自前，贪欲见佛。」阿难问言：「独是天知佛当灭度？复有余天？」佛告阿难：「从鸠夷那竭国境界，四百八十里中，头头相附，间不容针，皆是诸天，闻佛当灭度，悲哭且来，中有挽头发者，自裂衣者，塞心绝尸视者，哀云：『奈何！佛舍我曹，灭度永逝，何其疾乎！佛为大明，三界中眼，今般泥洹，三界眼灭。』」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "My sūtras do not say that there is no birth and no death. Heaven and earth are not indestructible. Foolish people take heaven and earth to be eternal, but the Buddha takes them to be empty. Heaven and earth have formation and destruction. No one can avoid abandoning their body. Goodness and evil follow the body. If the father has faults, the son does not receive retribution. If the son has faults, the father does not receive retribution. Each has their own birth and death. Goodness and evil retribution follow their own bodies."

佛告阿难：「吾本经不说，无生不死者，天地无不坏败者，愚人以天地为常，佛以为虚空，天地有成败，无不弃身者，善恶随身，父有过恶，子不获殃，子有过恶，父不获殃，各自生死，善恶殃咎，各随其身。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "After the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, how should we bury the Buddha's body?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not be silent and sorrowful. There will be Śrāvakas who will share the sorrow for my body." Ānanda said, "How will they sorrow for the Buddha's body?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "The burial method will be like that of a flying emperor. The Buddha's burial will surpass that." Ānanda said, "What is the burial method of a sage emperor?" The Buddha told Ānanda, "The burial method uses brocade and cotton to wrap the body, and a thousand layers of kapok cloth are wrapped on top. Place it in a false coffin, pour kapok oil, and place various good fragrances on top. At the four intersections, build a stupa and erect a pole, place a bowl on top, hang silk banners, and burn incense and lamps. The burial method of a flying emperor is like this; the Buddha's burial will surpass that." When the Buddha said this, Ānanda was behind him, sobbing and leaning his head against the corner of the bed. He then said, "The parinirvāṇa is too swift, the eyes of the world are gone.

阿难白佛言：「佛灭度后，吾等葬佛身体，法当云何？」佛告阿难：「汝默无忧，当有逝心理家，共忧吾身。」阿难言：「彼以何法，忧佛尊体？」佛告阿难：「葬法如飞行皇帝殡葬之法，佛复逾彼。」阿难言：「葬圣帝法云何？」佛告阿难：「葬法用锦亵以缠身，劫波育千] cf.【丽】张，交缠其上，著假棺中，以泽香膏，灌劫波育上，其有好香，皆以著上，以梓薪樟薪柟] cf.【丽】薪，以葢覆棺，以薪著上下，蛇维讫毕，歛舍利，于四交道，起塔立刹，以槃著上，悬缯鼓，华香燃灯，飞行皇帝葬法若斯；佛复胜之。」佛说此时，阿难在后，慷慨啼以头拄床角，从后白言：「灭度太疾，亡天下眼。」

The bhikṣus from the counties and kingdoms on all sides heard that the Buddha was about to enter parinirvāṇa. They wept and came, saying to each other, "We may not see the Buddha." When the bhikṣus arrived, the Buddha asked, "Where is Ānanda?" They replied, "Ānanda is near the corner of the bed, bowing his head and choking with sobs." The bhikṣus shed tears and said, "The World-Honored One's parinirvāṇa is too swift!" The Buddha said, "When I previously traveled to various villages and towns, I announced to you that I would enter parinirvāṇa in ninety days.

四面郡国，诸比丘僧，闻佛欲灭度，啼哭且来，自相谓恐不见佛。比丘僧到，佛问比丘：「阿难所在乎？」对曰：「阿难近在床后角，低头哽噎。」诸比丘流泪而言：「世尊灭度，何其太疾！」佛言：「吾本行诸墟聚，豫告若曹，却九十日当般泥洹。」

The four groups of disciples from thousands of miles away all came. The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not grieve. The reason is this: You have served the Buddha with utmost sincerity for over twenty years. Your kindness and benevolence towards the Buddha, your respect for the body and caution with the mouth, and your great filial piety towards the Buddha are like Ānanda. The attendants of past Buddhas were also like Ānanda, and the attendants of future Buddhas will also be like Ānanda. You know the Buddha's intentions. When you say that the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās can or cannot be seen at a certain time, and that the food and drink offered can or cannot be eaten, drunk, or slept on, you always accord with the Buddha's intentions and have never failed in propriety. You say that certain bhikṣus and certain Śrāvakas enjoy the sūtras or do not enjoy them. What you say is all true. You are the most filial towards the Buddha. Why do you weep?"

四辈弟子，在数千里外者悉至，佛告阿难：「若莫悲哀。所以然者？若尽心侍佛，二十余年，慈仁于佛，敬身慎口，大孝于佛，过去佛侍者亦如阿难，当来佛侍者亦如阿难。若知佛意，若云某时可见，比丘比丘尼优婆塞优婆夷，某时不可见；所供饮食，若言可食可饮可卧可起，常合佛意未甞失仪；某比丘某逝心，乐经不乐经，若所言皆诚，于佛最孝，啼哭何为？」

The Buddha said, "Listen, bhikṣus! The Flying Emperor has four virtues that are difficult to match. What are the four virtues? The kings of small countries, the Śrāvakas, and the common people all come to the palace of the Flying Emperor. The Flying Emperor sees them all; with a gentle and kind heart, he teaches the kings the Dharma of governing the country, knowing contentment and having no desires. The Śrāvakas and the common people go to the Buddha's temple to listen to and accept the true teachings of the Śramaṇas. Upon returning, they practice filial piety according to their determination. With a compassionate heart, he bestows them. The kings, Śrāvakas, and common people are all delighted and praise the sage emperor, moving the gods. The Flying Emperor has these four virtues. Ānanda bhikṣu also has four virtues: If the male and female lay followers, the male and female lay practitioners, come to Ānanda to ask about the sūtras and precepts, Ānanda explains them in detail. The four groups of disciples are all delighted, sit back and leave, praising along the way. This is called Ānanda's first four virtues; again, the four groups of disciples who do not understand the profound sūtras come to Ānanda to ask about their doubts. Ānanda resolves their doubts, and those who hear do not tire of it. This is called Ānanda's second four virtues; the four groups of noble ones see Ānanda attending the Buddha on the left side, and they all praise him. This is called Ānanda's third four virtues; the sūtras spoken by the Buddha, whether many or few, Ānanda hears them all and recites them, teaching the four groups without adding or subtracting. This is called Ānanda's fourth four virtues."

佛告：「诸比丘听！飞行皇帝有四难及之德。何谓四德？诸小国王，及诸逝心理家，并诸黎民，诣帝阙下，飞行皇帝皆见之；和心软教，为诸王说治国法，知足无求，逝心之行，清净为首；理家及民，出诣佛庙，听采沙门正真之化，归当修孝；随其所定，慈心赐之，诸王逝心理家庶民，靡不欣豫，称叹圣帝感动诸天；飞行皇帝有斯四德。阿难比丘亦有四德：其有除馑男除馑女清信士清信女，之阿难所从问经戒，阿难为具广陈演之，四辈弟子，靡不欣怿，退坐出去，寻涂称叹，斯谓阿难第一四德；复有四辈弟子，不解经奥，至阿难所启质所疑，阿难释结无不开解，闻者不厌出无不叹，斯谓阿难第二四德；四辈高德，覩阿难侍佛左侧，无不吟咏，斯谓阿难第三四德；佛所说经，言无多少，阿难所闻，皆识讽诵，宣授四辈，一无增减，是为阿难第四四德。」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "Not far from here are the kingdoms of Śrāvastī, Śāchi, Candrapura, Rājagṛha, Vārāṇasī, and Vaiśālī. These great kingdoms are well-versed in the teachings. Why not pass into nirvāṇa there? Why in this small county, moreover outside the city, in a poor and remote place?"

阿难白佛言：「去是不远有郡国，舍卫国、沙枝国、栴波国、王舍国、波罗［木＊奈］国、维耶梨国，斯诸大国，明义备悉，佛当灭度，何不于彼？既于小县，复处城外，薄聚鄙县，而般泥洹？」

The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do not say it is a small gathering. Why is that? In the past, in the kingdom of Kuśinagara, which was then called Kuśāvatī, the king was greatly joyful. At that time, there were no illnesses, the rice and grain were abundant, the people were prosperous, and every household had filial sons. The city was 480 li long from east to west and 280 li wide from south to north. The city had seven layers, all built with baked bricks. The city walls were made of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and crystal, and covered with tiles made of the four treasures. The city was six zhang and four chi tall, and two zhang and four chi wide. In the city were jeweled trees with flowers shining in five colors. There were three roads, both sides of which were covered with tiles made of the four treasures. On both sides were houses with carved and incised decorations, clothing like that of the heavens, and various musical instruments such as zithers and harps. Men and women did not mix, and the sound of singing was virtuous. The path of joy became even brighter. The people had no worries or fears, and their minds were always joyful. Their heads were not adorned, but the fragrance was far-reaching.

佛告阿难：「无云小聚。所以然者？惟昔往古鸠夷那竭，名鸠夷越，王国大乐时无疹病，米谷丰沃黎民炽盛，家有孝子。城东西长四百八十里，南北广二百八十里，其城七重，皆以焦墼累集作城，黄金白银琉璃水精，以著城壁，亦以四宝为瓦覆城。城高六丈四尺，上广二丈四尺，城中宝树，华光五色；行有三道，两边皆以四宝，瓦覆其上；两边居家舍宅，雕文刻镂，服如天上，琴瑟众乐。男女不杂，歌音以德，道乐益明，民无忧怖，心常欢喜，头上不饰，明香远达。

The sage king was named Mahāsukhadrști, also known as the flying emperor. His military might illuminated the world, and there was none to rival him. He led by the Way, without violating the king's laws. The people wished to fly, and upon thought, they would go. The king had seven treasures that naturally appeared: a golden flying wheel, a divine white elephant, a dark-blue divine horse, a moon-bright pearl, a heavenly jade female wife, a holy minister in charge of treasures, and a holy minister in charge of the army. The king had four virtues: when he was a child, when he was a crown prince, when he ascended the throne, and when he renounced the country and renounced desires to become a monk, each for 84,000 years. These were the virtues of King Mahāsukhadrști that were difficult to match. His food and drink transformed, his body was not long sick, and he was always at ease in body and mind. These were the second two virtues. His appearance was majestic, his complexion was extraordinary, and he was slightly inferior to Śakra, who considered him not as good. These were the third four virtues. All the people of the world, from the young to the old, loved the king with kindness, as a filial son wishes for his parents' peace. The king also loved all beings with a sincere heart, regarding them as his children. He gave wealth to the poor, fed the hungry, and gave drink to the thirsty. He made the poor, the old, the young, and the orphaned live together as relatives and children. He provided houses, carriages, and promptly provided medicine for the sick. These were called the four virtues.

「其圣王名曰大快见，号飞行皇帝，勒兵光世，都无齐双，相率以道，无违王法，民欲飞行，念即身往。王有七宝自然生：黄金飞轮，神力白象，绀色神马，明月珠，天玉女妻，主宝圣臣，典兵圣臣。王有四德，为小儿时，为太子时，即帝位时，捐国绝欲为沙门时，各八万四千岁，斯即大快见王一难及之德；饮食时化，体无长疾，寒温调适身意常安，斯二德；容观堂堂颜华绝世，微下帝释，以为不如，斯三德；普天率土民无巨细慈爱于王，犹至孝之子愿令亲安，王亦赤心慈爱众生，等之于子，贫给财宝，饥者饭之渴者饮之，穷老幼孤，令之合居为亲为子，屋舍车乘，疾济以药，斯谓四德。

The country always heard twelve kinds of sounds: the sounds of elephants, horses, oxen, carriages, conches, drums, dancing, singing, stringed instruments, reciting benevolence and righteousness, and praising the Buddha's supreme practices. The people's clothing was woven with various treasures, and the bright moon and mixed pearls shone on the path. Food, drink, and entertainment were like those in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. The residents were joyful, without a day of unhappiness. When the king wanted to go out, he called the chariot minister named Sudatta and ordered him, 'Drive the carriage slowly. I have not seen the Śramaṇas and Brāhmaṇas for a long time. I want to see them now.' The Śramaṇas and Brāhmaṇas heard that the king was going to come out and offered bright moon pearls, white pearls, green pearls, coral, and sandalwood incense to the sage king. The king did not want to accept them, but they all bowed and begged for mercy. The king then accepted them and ordered the treasure minister to double their value. The people, young and old, also scattered various treasures and flowers on the ground, praising the king's boundless longevity. The kings of the eighty-four thousand small countries heard that the flying emperor wanted to give alms and all came to follow him to the great hall. The emperor wanted to ascend the main hall with the kings, but the kings declined, saying, 'We all have jeweled halls in our countries.' The emperor said, 'Your small halls are not enough to be mentioned. Just look at the bright hall.' None dared to ascend. The kings each had jeweled carriages, ten zhang high, all with four wheels. From the bottom to the top, they were all made of the seven treasures. Above were banners and flags, brightly shining on each other. The carriages were drawn by six flying horses. 边境八国，闻佛灭度，舍利在鸠夷国中，皆发兵来，索舍利分。鸠夷国王曰：「佛在吾国，今者灭度，吾当供养，远苦抂顾，舍利不可得。」八王答曰：「吾等叉手，索舍利分，了不与我，必当以命抵取之耳。」天帝见八王共诤，欲得舍利还国供养，化为梵志，自名屯屈，叉手前晓八国王曰：「听吾一言，惟佛在时，诸王奉尊教常慈惠，夫为民主，无宜有诤，当行四等，分佛舍利，令诸国土，皆有宗庙，开民盲冥，令知有佛，以为宗绪使得景福。」天神鬼龙、诸王黎民佥曰：「善哉！屯屈！普施众生福田也。」共请屯屈作平八分。屯屈自以天上金甖，中以石蜜涂里，成量舍利，各与一甖。诸王得之，悲喜交集，皆以香华、悬缯、杂彩、烧香、燃灯、朝夕作乐。屯屈长跪乞甖中余著蜜舍利：「吾欲立庙。」诸王惠之遂入甖。道士名曰桓违，从王索舍利，王曰：「已分不可复得，唯有焦炭，便自往取。」道士取炭，香华供养。复有遮迦竭人，来索舍利。曰：「已分。唯有余灰，可自往取。」即复取灰，奉九十日。

Mahākāśyapa, Ānanda, and Kātyāyana discussed, "Ānanda has followed the Buddha the longest and is closest to the Buddha. The Buddha's teachings and practices, Ānanda has internalized without any omissions. He can receive Ānanda's teachings and record them on bamboo and silk." The bhikṣu saṃgha discussed, "Ānanda is a layperson. He may have greedy thoughts and hide the wonderful words, refusing to fully proclaim them." The bhikṣu saṃgha said, "We should trick him. Set up a high seat. The sages will gather there. The bhikṣu saṃgha will use compassion to question him up and down. By asking about the essentials of the sūtras, we can get the truth." The king of Kuśinagara built a temple for the Buddha, with meditation rooms, totaling three thousand. The bhikṣus lived there, reciting sūtras and sitting in meditation. The king sent a minister named Māndhātṛ with three thousand soldiers to guard the Buddha's temple. Mahākāśyapa and Ānanda together reported to the bhikṣu saṃgha that the Buddha's sūtras and vinaya were called the Four Āgamas. Ānanda had followed the Buddha and was uniquely close to him. The Buddha, seeing that sentient beings were unrestrained in their lust, composed one Āgama; in their violent anger and rebellion, he composed one Āgama; in their ignorance and distance from the right path, he composed one Āgama; in their lack of filial piety towards their parents and distance from the wise, not respecting and not considering repaying the Buddha's kindness, he composed one Āgama. The bhikṣus said, "Only Ānanda knows. The Four Āgamas should come from Ānanda." Mahākāśyapa said, "Ānanda is a layperson. He may have greedy thoughts and not fully proclaim the sūtras." The bhikṣus said, "We can use previous matters to question Ānanda. We should put Ānanda on a high bed and the wise assembly question him from below." They all said, "Excellent! It truly fits the great occasion." The bhikṣus then gathered the sages. They expelled Ānanda. The sages all sat down. They again ordered Ānanda to advance. He advanced and bowed to the sages. Those who had attained arahantship all sat as before. Those who had not attained it all stood up. The bhikṣus ordered him to ascend to the central high seat. Ānanda declined, saying, "This is not my seat." The sages all said, "Because of the Buddha's sūtras, we honor you there. We receive the highest Dharma from you." Ānanda then sat down.

大迦叶、阿那律、迦旃延共议：「阿难随佛最久于佛独亲，佛所教化，施为弘摸，阿难贯心无微不照，可受阿难法律，委曲载之竹帛。」比丘僧议：「阿难白衣，恐有贪心，隐藏妙语，不肯尽宣。」比丘僧曰：「当诡取之。设一高座处，诸圣上会，以比丘僧以慈诘问三上下，因问经要，可得诚实。」鸠夷国王，立佛宗庙，精房禅室，凡有三千，诸比丘处其中，诵经坐禅，王遣大臣，臣名摩南，将兵三千，宿卫佛庙，大迦叶与阿那律，共报比丘僧，佛经结律，名四阿含。阿难从佛，独为亲密，佛以众生婬泆无度，作一阿含；凶怒悖逆，作一阿含；愚冥远正作一阿含；不孝二亲，远贤不宗受佛恩，不惟上报，作一阿含。沙门众曰：「唯阿难知，夫四阿含，当由阿难出。」大迦叶曰：「阿难白衣，恐有贪意，不尽出经。」众比丘曰：「可以前事诘责阿难，当上阿难著于高床，诸贤者众自下问经。」佥曰：「善哉！诚合大宜。」直事沙门即会圣众，逐阿难出，圣众皆坐，复命阿难令疾进，进为圣众稽首作礼，得应真者，皆坐如旧，未得者皆起。直事沙门，令之升坐中央高座。阿难辞曰：「非吾座也。」圣众佥曰：「以佛经故尊尔于彼，从尔受佛之上法。」阿难乃坐。

The wise assembly asked him, "You have seven faults. Do you know them? When the World-Honored One was alive, he said, 'There is great joy in Jambudvīpa.' You were silent." The bhikṣu who was in charge called Ānanda. Ānanda immediately replied, "The Buddha is the unsurpassed, perfectly enlightened, and truly accomplished one. Can he not be free? Must he wait for my words? Even if the Buddha were to live for an eon, Maitreya will become the supreme Buddha." The sages were silent. Ānanda was fearless. The sages all said, "Return to your seat. We know that your proclamation of the Dharma is the same as the true Dharma we have heard." This happened three times. Ānanda descended three times. Ānanda again said, "I have heard from the Buddha that..." When the bhikṣu saṃgha heard Ānanda's words, "I have heard from the Buddha that...", they all choked with sobs, saying, "What shall we do? The Buddha has just been in the world, and now he says he has heard it from the Buddha." The heavenly spirits, ghosts, dragons, emperors, ministers, and people, the four groups of disciples, all lamented. Mahākāśyapa and the sages selected forty arhats and received the Four Āgamas from Ānanda. One Āgama was written on sixty rolls of silk. Before the sūtras were completed, four named trees naturally grew in the Buddha's temple. One tree was named Kaṇṭha, one was named Kaṭhina, one was named Āmra, and one was named Nyagrodha. The bhikṣu saṃgha said, "We wrote the Four Āgamas with compassionate minds. Four divine trees naturally grew. The Four Āgamas are the way tree of the Buddha." They then made a vow to receive the two hundred and fifty pure and bright precepts of the bhikṣu saṃgha, the five precepts of the bhikṣuṇī saṃgha, the five precepts of the upāsaka, and the ten precepts of the upāsikā. After writing the sūtras, the bhikṣu saṃgha each practiced the precepts and taught them to others for a thousand years. During those thousand years, those who upheld the precepts would be in the presence of the fourth Maitreya Buddha. Maitreya Buddha would teach the Dharma to the heavens. He said, "All those who are gathered here are those who upheld the precepts during the time of Śākyamuni Buddha. They have come to this assembly." Maitreya Buddha said, "You should diligently apply yourselves to diligence. The precepts are difficult to fulfill. Hold them to the best of your ability."
