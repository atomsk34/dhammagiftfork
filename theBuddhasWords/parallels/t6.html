T6 般泥洹经

Parallel to: DN 16 (~) / DN 17 (~) / SN 47.9 (~) / Snp 1.5 / DĀ 2 / MĀ 68 (~) / T 5 / T 7 (~)

Thus have I heard:

闻如是：

Once, the Buddha was staying at Vulture Peak in Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of monks, 1,250 monks in all. At that time, King Ajātaśatru of Magadha was at odds with the people of Vaiśālī. The ministers discussed, saying, "Vaiśālī relies on the wealth and abundance of their country, the fertile fields and abundance of precious treasures. They do not submit to us. We should go and attack them." There was a wise and great minister named Varṣākāra, of the brāhman caste. The king ordered him to go, bow at the Buddha's feet, respectfully inquire about his well-being, and say, "King Ajātaśatru is at odds with the people of Vaiśālī. The ministers have discussed going to attack them. I wish to hear the Buddha's teachings." The great minister received the order and immediately prepared five hundred carriages, two thousand cavalry, and two thousand infantry. He went to Vulture Peak and, upon reaching the entrance to the small path, got off the carriage and proceeded on foot. Seeing the Buddha, he was overjoyed. His facial expression was respectful and courteous. He bowed deeply and said, "King Ajātaśatru of Magadha bows at the Buddha's feet and respectfully inquires about your well-being, your light and strong constitution, your ease in walking, and the daily increase of your virtuous influence." The Buddha said, "Excellent! Is the king, the people of the country, and yourself all at peace?" Varṣākāra said, "The king is at odds with the people of Vaiśālī. The ministers have discussed going to attack them, because they rely on the wealth and abundance of their country, the fertile fields and abundance of precious treasures. They do not submit to us. I wish to hear the Buddha's teachings."

一时，佛游于王舍鹞山，从大众比丘，比丘千二百五十。时，摩竭王阿阇世，与越祇不相得，众臣议言：「越祇自恃国富民众，地沃野丰多出珍宝，不首伏我，当往攻伐。」国贤大臣名曰雨舍，梵志种也。王命使行，稽首佛足，敬问消息，兴居轻强气力游步，德化日升，言：「阿阇世与越祇有憾，众臣之议，欲往攻伐。愿闻众祐，有以教之。」大臣受命，即严车五百乘，骑二千步人二千，行诣鹞山，到小道口，下车步进，见佛欢喜，貌色恭辞，气重揖让毕长跪言：「摩竭王阿阇世，稽首佛足，敬问消息，兴居轻强气力游步，德化日升。」佛言：「甚善，王与国人及汝皆安不？」雨舍白言：「王与越祇有憾，众臣之议，以彼自恃国富民众，地沃野丰多出珍宝，不首伏我，欲往伐之。愿闻佛教。」

The Buddha replied to the great minister, "Once, I was traveling in Vaiśālī, in the Zhengzao Shrine. I saw that the people there were all very cautious. I then spoke to them about the seven methods for governing the country and the path to non-decline. If one can practice them, one will flourish daily and not decline. He then joined his palms and said, 'I wish to hear the seven methods. What should be practiced?' The Buddha said, 'Listen carefully.' He replied, 'I receive the teaching.'" At that time, Venerable Ānanda was standing behind the Buddha, fanning him. The Buddha said to Ānanda, "Have you not heard that the people of Vaiśālī often gather together to discuss state affairs and prepare for self-defense?" He replied, "I have heard that they often gather together to discuss state affairs and prepare for self-defense." The Buddha said, "In this way, they will not decline. Have you heard that the ruler and ministers of Vaiśālī are always harmonious, and those appointed are loyal and virtuous, passing on their positions to each other?" He replied, "I have heard that the ruler and ministers are always harmonious, and those appointed are loyal and virtuous, passing on their positions to each other." "Have you heard that the people of Vaiśālī follow the Dharma, are self-reliant, have no desires, and dare not transgress?" He replied, "I have heard that they follow the Dharma, are self-reliant, have no desires, and dare not transgress." "Have you heard that the people of Vaiśālī are respectful in their manners, with men and women distinguished, and the young serving the elders?" He replied, "I have heard that they are respectful in their manners, with men and women distinguished, and the young serving the elders." "Have you heard that the people of Vaiśālī are filial to their parents, deferential to their teachers and elders, and accept and understand their teachings?" He replied, "I have heard that they are filial to their parents, deferential to their teachers and elders, and accept and understand their teachings." "Have you heard that the people of Vaiśālī follow the ways of Heaven and Earth, respect the spirits, and respect the four seasons?" He replied, "I have heard that they follow the ways of Heaven and Earth, respect the spirits, and respect the four seasons." "Have you heard that the people of Vaiśālī honor morality and virtue, and provide clothing, food, bedding, and medicine to the śramaṇas and pratyekabuddhas in their country, as well as those who come from the four directions?" He replied, "I have heard that they honor morality and virtue, and provide clothing, food, bedding, and medicine to the śramaṇas and pratyekabuddhas in their country

佛报大臣：「昔吾一时曾游越祇正躁神舍，见其国人，皆多谨勅，我时为说治国七法不危之道，其能行者，日当兴盛未之衰也。即叉手言：『愿闻七法，盖何施行？』佛言：『谛听。』对曰：『受教。』」时，贤者阿难，住后扇佛。佛言阿难：「汝宁不闻越祇国人数相聚会，讲论政事，修备自守？」对曰：「闻其数相聚会，讲论政事，修备自守。」佛言：「如是，彼为不衰，汝闻越祇君臣常和，所任忠良，转相承用。」对曰：「闻其君臣常和，所任忠良，转相承用。汝闻越祇奉法相率，无取无愿，不敢有过？」对曰：「闻其奉法相率，无取无愿，不敢有过。」「汝闻越祇礼化谨敬，男女有别，长幼相事？」对曰：「闻其礼化谨敬，男女有别，长幼相事。」「汝闻越祇孝于父母，逊悌师长，受识教诲？」对曰：「闻其孝于父母，逊悌师长，受识教诲。」「汝闻越祇承天则地，敬畏鬼神，敬顺四时？」对曰：「闻其承天则地，敬畏鬼神，敬顺四时。」「汝闻越祇尊奉道德，国有沙门应真及四方来者，供养衣食卧床疾药？」对曰：「闻其尊奉道德，国有沙门应真及四方来者，供养衣食卧床疾药。」佛言：「夫有国者，行此七法，难可得危。」雨舍对曰：「使越祇人持一者，尚不可攻，何况有七？国事多故当还。」请辞。佛言：「可，置知是时。」即从座起，礼佛而去。

At that time, the Buddha instructed Venerable Ānanda to invite the bhikṣus who were practicing on Vulture Peak to gather in the lecture hall. After they had all gathered and bowed, they sat to one side. The Buddha said, "Bhikṣus, listen to what I say and practice it well." They all said, "We receive the teaching."

是时，佛勅贤者阿难，请鹞山中诸倚行比丘，令会讲堂。即请悉会，稽首毕，一面坐。佛告：「诸比丘！听我所言，善念行之。」皆曰：「受教。」

The Buddha said, 'Bhikṣus, there are seven teachings that can prevent the Dharma from declining. What are the seven teachings? First, one should often gather to discuss and recite the scriptures, without being lazy. Second, one should be harmonious, loyal, and upright in teaching each other, passing on one's position to the next. Third, one should not take or desire from others, but only enjoy the mountains and forests. Fourth, one should cut off hope for the elders and the young, serving each other with respect. Fifth, one should be filial and respectful in serving the elders, accepting and understanding their teachings. Sixth, one should uphold the Dharma, respect the precepts, and cultivate pure conduct. Seventh, one should follow the path and make offerings to the noble assembly, opening up and teaching the ignorant who come to study, providing them with clothing, food, bedding, and medicine. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

佛言：「比丘有七教，则法不衰。何等七教？一当数会讲诵经道，无有懈怠。二当和顺，忠正相教，转相承用。三当无取无愿于他，唯乐山泽。四当绝望长幼先后，相事以礼。五当慈孝承事师长，受识教诲。六当奉法，敬畏经戒，以修梵行。七当遵道供养圣众，开解童蒙，来学者受给施衣、食、卧床、疾药。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are seven ways of guarding the Dharma that can prevent it from declining. One should practice them well: First, guard purity and do not enjoy conditioned things. Second, guard against desire and do not be greedy for material gain. Third, guard against anger and do not quarrel. Fourth, guard the practice of emptiness and do not enter the crowd. Fifth, guard the Dharma mind and do not give rise to various thoughts. Sixth, guard single-minded concentration and sit in meditation. Seventh, guard against loss and be content with coarse and sparse clothing and food, and a grass mat as a bed. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

「又比丘有七守，则法不衰，当善念行：一守清净，不乐有为。二守无欲，不贪利养。三守忍辱，无所诤讼。四守空行，不入众聚。五守法意，不起众想。六守一心，坐禅定意。七守约损，衣食麁踈，草蓐为床。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are seven ways of respecting the Dharma that can prevent it from declining. One should practice them well: First, respect the Buddha with a good mind, pay homage, and do not rely on others. Second, respect the Dharma with the intention of the Way, and do not rely on others. Third, respect the Saṃgha by following their instructions, and do not rely on others. Fourth, respect learning by serving those who uphold the precepts, and do not rely on others. Fifth, respect listening by serving those who teach, and do not rely on others. Sixth, respect purity and non-desire, and do not rely on others. Seventh, respect concentration by serving those who sit in meditation, and do not rely on others. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

「又比丘有七敬，则法不衰，当善念行：一为敬佛，善心礼事，无他倚行。二为敬法，志在道意，无他倚行。三为敬众，依受教令，无他倚行。四为敬学，事持戒者，无他倚行。五为敬闻，事讲授者，无他倚行。六为敬净无欲，无他倚行。七为敬定，事坐禅寂，无他倚行。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are seven kinds of wealth that can prevent the Dharma from declining. One should practice them well: First, have faith and see the truth with joy and delight. Second, have precepts and be careful not to violate them. Third, have a sense of shame and repent of one's mistakes. Fourth, have a sense of modesty and act in accordance with one's words. Fifth, have much learning and recite without weariness. Sixth, have wisdom and deeply practice the subtle. Seventh, practice the Dharma and do not expect offerings. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

「又比丘有七财，则法不衰，当善念行：一当有信，见正喜乐。二当有戒，慎护不犯，三当有惭，改过自悔。四当有愧，顺所言行。五当多闻，讽诵无厌。六当智慧，深行微妙。七当法施，勿望礼贶。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are seven kinds of enlightenment that can prevent the Dharma from declining. One should practice them well: First, the enlightenment of mindfulness is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. Second, the enlightenment of Dharma understanding is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. Third, the enlightenment of diligence is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. Fourth, the enlightenment of joy and delight is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. Fifth, the enlightenment of one-pointedness is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. Sixth, the enlightenment of concentration is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. Seventh, the enlightenment of guarding practice is pure and free from desire, abandoning scattered thoughts. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

「又比丘有七觉意，则法不衰，当善念行：一志念觉倚净无淫，舍] cf.【丽】分散意。二法解觉倚净无淫，舍分散意。三精进觉倚净无淫，舍分散意。四爱喜觉倚净无淫，舍分散意。五一向觉倚净无淫，舍分散意。六惟定觉倚净无淫，舍分散意。七行护觉倚净无淫，舍分散意。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are seven kinds of knowledge that can prevent the Dharma from declining. One should practice them well: First, know the Dharma, the twelve divisions of the Buddha's teachings, and recite and discuss them. Second, know how to discuss, seek the wisdom of the Dharma, and thoroughly understand its essentials. Third, know the time, when to recite, walk, meditate, or lie down, without missing the appropriate time. Fourth, know oneself, the depth and shallowness of one's practice, whether it is mature or just beginning, and one's aspiration should be superior. Fifth, know moderation, not being greedy for delicious things, eating moderately to suit one's body, and not causing illness. Sixth, know the assembly, entering the assembly of bhikṣus, brāhmaṇas, sages, gentlemen, and commoners, distinguishing who is worthy of respect, who can be stayed with, who can be sat with, who can be silent with, and who can be spoken with. Seventh, know people, observing their preferences, examining their abilities, and guiding them according to their inclinations, causing them to know the transformation of the sages. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

「又比丘有七知，则法不衰，当善念行：一当知法，佛十二部经，谛受诵论。二当知议，求诸法慧，博解其要。三当知时，可诵可步可禅可卧，无失时宜。四当自知，所入法行，多少深浅，熟与初始，志当曰胜。五当知节，勿贪美妙，适身节食，无以自病。六当知众，入比丘众，梵志圣人，君子及士民众分别知可敬、可住、可坐、可默、可语。七当知人，观其所好，察其志能，随意劝导，令知圣化。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are seven kinds of contemplation that can prevent the Dharma from declining. One should practice them well: First, contemplate the path of the sūtras, as one would think of one's parents. Parents give birth to a child, thinking of them for one lifetime. Contemplating the Dharma saves people, liberating them from birth and death for countless lifetimes. Second, contemplate that all people have suffering. Worrying about one's wife, family, and possessions, when one dies, they all disperse and one does not know where they fall. If one has committed sins, one's relatives cannot free one from them. Knowing this impermanence, one should contemplate practicing the path. Third, contemplate diligence, rectifying one's body, speech, and mind, and attaining the path is not difficult. Fourth, contemplate humility, not being arrogant, serving the wise, and being taught what is not yet known, compassionately teaching others. Fifth, contemplate subduing the mind, not chasing after the six sense objects, suppressing the tendencies of desire, anger, and ignorance, and not engaging in deviant practices. Sixth, contemplate the body, which is only filled with foul odors, wind, cold, heat, and blood, and there is nothing to be greedy for. Seventh, contemplate oneself, the body being like filth, and contemplate death every day. Since the opening of heaven and earth, all living beings have been born and none have not died. The world is like a dream, what is seen and loved is impermanent. When one awakens, one realizes emptiness. One should know this is an illusion and not deceive oneself. With these seven teachings, one can abide for a long time.

「又比丘有七惟，则法不衰，当善念行：一惟经道，当如人念父母，父母生子，思极一世，惟法活人，无数世度人生死。二惟人生，无不有苦，忧念妻之家属所有，死各离散不知所堕，若身有罪，亲不能解，知此非常，当念行道。三惟精进，端身口意，取道不难。四惟谦虚，无自憍大，承事明哲，矜诲未闻，愍伤教之。五惟降意，不驰六情，抑淫怒痴态，无有邪行。六惟躯中，但盛臭秽，风寒热血，无可贪者。七惟自观，形如粪土，日当念死，天地开辟，生民以来，无不死者，世间如梦，所见欢爱，不知为化，悟乃觉空，当知是幻，勿以自] cf.【丽】欺。如是七法，可得久住。

Bhikṣus, there are also six kinds of dharmas that one should practice well in order to abide for a long time: First, cultivate the body and give rise to a compassionate mind. Rely on the pure and holy dharmas to carry out these heavy responsibilities, harmoniously loving and respecting fellow practitioners, without attachment or disputes, striving to uphold and practice together. Second, cultivate good conduct of speech and give rise to a compassionate mind. Third, cultivate good conduct of mind and give rise to a compassionate mind. Fourth, when one attains robes, food, alms bowls, and other things, one should not be attached to them. Fifth, uphold the precepts without violating them, and be able to use them to encourage others. Sixth, if one attains the right view and receives the right path, one will be able to know the end of suffering and attain liberation. One should carry out these heavy responsibilities, harmoniously loving and respecting fellow practitioners, without attachment or disputes, mutually establishing and upholding the practice of the path. Moreover, bhikṣus should have compassion for all crawling and moving creatures, even down to lice and fleas, and give rise to a compassionate mind. When people die, one should grieve for them. If they become human beings but do not hear the Dharma, their family members cry but do not know where their souls and spirits go. Only those who have attained the Way can know it. The Buddha therefore expounds the Dharma of the sūtras. The sūtras cannot be neglected, and the Way cannot be neglected. There are many ways in the world, but the Way of the king is the greatest. The Buddha's Way is like this, being the highest. It is like when many people shoot at a target together. Some hit the target first, some hit it later. As long as they keep shooting, they will eventually hit the target. It is also like all the rivers in the world, which flow ceaselessly and return to the ocean. Bhikṣus are like this. If they practice the Way without stopping, they will attain liberation. According to the teachings of the Buddha's Dharma, they should pass them on to each other, recite the Buddha's words, always teach them, and teach the four groups of disciples in turn. In this way, the Buddha's sūtras can abide for a long time.

「又比丘复有六重法，当善念行，可得久住：一为修身，以起慈心，依圣旬通，诸清净者，行此重任，和一爱敬，施于同学，无取无诤，勉共守行行。二为修口善行，以起慈心。三为修意善行，以起慈心。四为所见法际，若得衣食、应器余物，终不爱藏。五为持戒不犯，不以摸质，能用劝人。六为若从正见得出正要受道苦尽度知见了，行此重任，皆以圣旬通清净用和爱敬，施于同道，无取无诤，转相建立，共守道行。又复比丘，当为愍伤，一切蠕动，至于虮虱，必加慈心，人之死亡，当为悲哀，彼得为人，如不闻道，家室啼哭，亦不知死，魂神所趣；唯得道者，能知之耳。佛为是故敷陈经法，经不可不学，道不可不行，天下多道，王道为大，佛道如是，最为其上。譬数十人，俱共射准，有前中者，有后中者，要射不止，必复中准，又如天下众流不息皆归于海。比丘如此，行道不止，会得解脱，如佛法教，转相承用，讽咏佛语，常用时诲，四辈弟子，展转相教，如是佛经，可得久住。」

At that time, the Buddha invited the Venerable Ānanda to go together to the city of Bārāṇasī. He accepted the instruction and went. The Buddha put on his robe and alms bowl, passed through the city of Rājagṛha, and stopped halfway at the king's garden. The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "Listen all. For those who practice the Way, they should know the Four Noble Truths. Ordinary people do not know them, so they wander in the long path, transmigrating in birth and death without rest. I therefore open your minds. What are the Four? First, knowing suffering as suffering is called true. Second, suffering arises from craving, which is called true. Third, the extinction of suffering and craving is called true. Fourth, the acceptance of the path of the extinction of suffering and craving is called true. Not being wise about suffering and not knowing it, one wanders in the long path, transmigrating in birth and death without rest. One should know this truth of suffering. Suffering refers to the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, separation from what is loved, and not obtaining what one seeks; in short, the suffering of the five aggregates. Having realized this suffering, one can cut off craving and attachment. This is called attaining the eye, being the ultimate birth, and not having suffering again. Craving arises from attachment. When suffering and craving are completely extinguished, one attains the truth of the path, attains the eye of seeing, and is completely born, never having suffering again. Having seen the true truths and attained the eye of the path, one will no longer be reborn and the long path will be forever cut off. Thus, bhikṣus, one should also know the eight practices. What are the eight? First, with a concentrated mind, receive the Buddha's teachings. Second, abandon desires and have no disputes with the world. Third, never commit killing, stealing, or sexual misconduct. Fourth, do not deceive, flatter, or speak ill of others. Fifth, do not be jealous, greedy, or have disbelief. Sixth, contemplate impermanence, suffering, emptiness, and non-self. Seventh, observe the foul and impure nature of the body. Eighth, do not be attached to the body and know that it will return to the earth. The Buddhas of the past all saw these Four Noble Truths, and the Buddhas of the future will also see these Four Noble Truths. Those who crave the affection and love of family life and the glory and fame of the world will never attain the path of transcending the world. The path arises from the mind. Sometimes a Śramaṇa who has attained the Way, or a divine being, with their abundant virtue and precepts, wishes to test their power, and when they touch the earth with their hand, the entire earth shakes. This is the second cause. When a Bodhisattva first descends from the fourth heaven into his mother's womb, with his wise and compassionate intention, wishing to see the Dharma and teach, to enlighten the ignorant, he then emits a divine light, shaking heaven and earth, causing all Brahmā, Śakra, Māra, Śramaṇas, and Brahmins to see the light. This is the third cause. When a Bodhisattva is born from his mother's womb, his virtue moves the gods, and the sky is clear and cloudless, with the distant illumination of divine light, causing the entire earth to shake. This is the fourth cause. When a Bodhisattva attains the unsurpassed, true, and correct Buddhahood, the entire earth greatly shakes, and the heavenly gods spread out in the four directions, proclaiming the name of the Buddha. This is the fifth cause. When a Buddha has already become a Buddha, at the time of his first great assembly, when the Dharma wheel turns three times, the gods and humans understand, and the Bodhisattvas of this world and other worlds ascend to the great path, with the distant illumination of light, causing the entire earth to shake. This is the sixth cause. When the Buddha's teachings are about to end, and he wishes to abandon his life and not remain in existence, he then emits a great light, inspiring the gods and humans, causing the entire earth to shake. This is the seventh cause. When the Buddha, the Honored One, is about to abandon his body and enter parinirvāṇa, the light is bright and illuminating, and the gods gather, causing the earth to shake everywhere. This is the eighth cause."

佛语阿难：「凡世地动，有八因缘。何等八？天下地在水上，水止于风，风止于空，空中大风有时自起则大水扰，大水扰则普地动，是为一也。有时得道沙门，及神妙天，戒德隆盛，欲自试力，手按少地，则普地动，是为二也。若始菩萨，从第四天，下入母胎，明哲慈意，欲见道化，开发愚曚，乃放神光，震动天地，令梵释魔沙门梵志一切见明，是为三也。若菩萨生出母胎时，德感诸天，净无云曀，神光远照，则普地动，是为四也。至于菩萨得无上道正真佛时，普地大动，天神四布，称扬佛名，是为五也。及已作佛，初大会时，法轮三转，天人则解，此彼菩萨，升成大道，光明远照，时普地动，是为六也。佛教将毕，欲弃寿行，不住性命，乃大放光，劝发天人，则普地动，是为七也。如佛众祐，临当弃身般泥洹时，明无不照，天神参至则地普动，是为八也。」

Ānanda said, "Has the Buddha already abandoned his life?"

阿难言：「今佛已为舍性命耶？」

The Buddha said, "I have already abandoned it."

佛言：「已舍。」

Ānanda said, "In the past, I heard the Buddha say that if a disciple knows the four bases of supernormal power, cultivates them diligently, and focuses on them without forgetting, they can live as long as they wish. The Buddha's virtue and path surpass this, so he should not be able to stay for long either?"

阿难曰：「昔闻佛说：『若有弟子，知四神足，多修习行，专念不忘，在意所欲，可止不死一劫有余。』而佛道德，过殊于此，亦不可久止乎？」

The Buddha replied to Ānanda, "Is what you just said not excessive? I spoke to you about the four bases of supernormal power up to two or three times, but you were silent and immersed in obscurity, not giving rise to clear thoughts. You were obscured by Māra and then what? How can you say you possess the Buddha's teachings? Once words leave the mouth, how can they be contradicted?"

佛报阿难：「今汝言之，岂不过耶？吾与汝言四神足者，乃至再三；而若径默，没在暗昧，不发明想，为魔所蔽，而复何云具佛所说，言一出口宁自违乎？」

He replied, "No."

对曰：「不也。」

"So it is, Ānanda! The unwise, having spoken, then contradict themselves. I am not like that."

「如是阿难！夫不智者既自发言而追违之；我无是也。」

Ānanda, with tears streaming down, said, "How quickly! The Buddha enters nirvāṇa, how quickly! The eye of the world is extinguished."

阿难垂涕曰：「亦何驶哉！佛取泥洹，一何疾哉！世间眼灭。」

At that time, the Buddha instructed the wise Ānanda to invite the bhikṣus who were traveling in the country of Vaiśālī. Having received the instruction, he immediately invited them all to gather in the lecture hall. After paying homage, they stood to one side. The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "The world is impermanent, without solidity, and will all be separated. What remains is impermanent. The activities of the mind and consciousness are just self-deception. Who can have long-lasting reunions with loved ones? Even heaven and earth and Mount Sumeru will collapse and disintegrate, let alone human beings, who wish to have long existence? The suffering of birth and death is to be despised. The Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa in three months. Do not be surprised or worried. Moreover, all past, present, and future Buddhas attain it through the Dharma. The Dharma of the sūtras still exists. You should diligently study and practice, uphold pure thoughts, and strive for liberation. When the mind and consciousness are at peace, one will not die, be reborn, or wander in the five paths, abandoning one body and receiving another. When the five aggregates are severed, there will be no suffering of hunger, thirst, cold, heat, sorrow, or distress. When people know the right mind, all the gods in heaven will rejoice on their behalf. One should subdue the mind, be gentle and humble, and not follow the activities of the mind. The activities of the mind are endless. Those who attain the Way also have the mind. The mind creates heaven, the mind creates humans, the mind creates ghosts, spirits, animals, and hell. All are created by the mind. The arising of all dharmas is attained through the activities of the mind. The mind creates consciousness, consciousness creates intention, and intention turns into the mind. The mind is the longest. The mind's intention is the activity. The activity is the life. The wise and the foolish are in the activity. The longevity and brevity of life are in the life. The mind, intention, and life are mutually dependent. One's own good and evil deeds are to be borne by oneself. The father's unwholesome deeds are not borne by the son, and the son's unwholesome deeds are not borne by the father. Goodness brings its own blessings, and evil brings its own retribution. Now the Buddha is respected by all in heaven and on earth. All are due to the mind's intention. Therefore, one should practice the Dharma with the right mind. Only those who practice the Dharma can attain peace and tranquility in this life. One should diligently uphold and recite it, and contemplate it in stillness. Then my pure Dharma can remain for a long time, and can compassionately liberate the world from suffering. The Way benefits and pacifies all gods and humans.

彼时，佛勅贤者阿难，请维耶离国倚行比丘。受教即请，悉会] cf.【丽】讲堂，稽首毕一面住。佛告诸比丘：「世间无常，无有牢固，皆当离散，无常在者，心识所行，但为自欺，恩爱合会，其谁得久，天地须弥，尚有崩坏，况于人物，而欲长存？生死忧苦，可厌已矣！佛后三月，当般泥洹，勿怪勿忧。且夫一切去来现佛，皆从法得，经法且存，但当自勉勤学力行，持清净心，趣得度脱，心识情休，则不死不复生，亦不复走于五道舍一身受一身也。五阴已断，乃无饥渴寒热忧悲苦恼之患。人知正心，天上诸天，皆代人喜，当以降心柔弱自损，勿随心行，心之行无不为，得道者亦心也。心作天，心作人，心作鬼神畜生地狱，皆心所为也。从心行得起诸法，心作识，识作意，意转入心，心也者取为长，心志为行，行作为命，贤愚在行，寿夭在命。夫志行命三者相须，所作好恶，身自当之；父作不善，子不代受，子作不善，父亦不受，善自获福，恶自受殃。今佛为天上天下所尊敬者，皆志所为，是故当以正心行法，唯行法者，能现世得休，现世得安，宜善取持谛受讽诵，静意思惟，然则我清净法，可得久住，可以愍度世间众苦，道利绥宁诸天人民。

Bhikṣus, you should know! What is the Dharma? It refers to the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernormal power, the four dhyānas, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, and the eightfold noble path. By practicing and upholding it, one can attain liberation and prevent the Dharma from declining.

「比丘当知！何等为法？谓是四志惟、四意端、四神足、四禅行、五根、五力、七觉、八道谛，如受行可得解脱，令法不衰。

What are the four foundations of mindfulness? They are mindfulness of the body, mindfulness of feelings, mindfulness of the mind, and mindfulness of dharmas. By contemplating the body internally, contemplating the body externally, and contemplating both internally and externally, one discriminates and contemplates, cutting off deluded thoughts. The same applies to feelings, the mind, and dharmas.

「彼何谓四志惟？惟内身循身观，惟外身循身观，以内外观，分别思念，断痴惑意；惟痛之观，及意与法，皆如初说。

What are the four right efforts? They are the effort to eliminate unwholesome dharmas that have arisen, the effort to prevent unwholesome dharmas that have not yet arisen from arising, the effort to generate wholesome dharmas that have not yet arisen, and the effort to increase and not forget wholesome dharmas that have already arisen.

「何谓为四意端？已生恶法，能即断却，治性精进，自摄意端；未生恶法，制使不起，治性精进，自摄意端；未生善法，即能发生，治性精进，自摄意端；已生善法，志立不忘，能使增逾，精进意端。

What are the four bases of spiritual power? They are the base of spiritual power of desire, the base of spiritual power of energy, the base of spiritual power of mind, and the base of spiritual power of investigation. By contemplating desire, one eliminates various practices, and is endowed with the base of spiritual power of mindfulness. One's desire is not deluded, not grasped, and not abandoned, always guarding pure conduct. The same applies to energy, mind, and investigation.

「何谓为四神足？思惟欲定，以灭众行，具念神足，其欲不邪，不取无舍，常守清行；惟精进定，惟意志定，惟戒习定，皆同，文如初说。

What are the four dhyānas? Abandoning desire and evil dharmas, only contemplating and practicing, with the aspiration for non-action, one accomplishes the practice of the first dhyāna. Having extinguished contemplation and practice, internally guarding one's mind, aspiring for tranquility, one accomplishes the practice of the second dhyāna. Only observing the absence of desire, the mind is at ease and the body is upright, discerning and seeing the true, one accomplishes the practice of the third dhyāna. Having already eliminated suffering and happiness, without the thought of joy or sorrow, the mind is already pure, one accomplishes the practice of the fourth dhyāna.

「何谓四禅？弃欲恶法，但念但行，志乐无为成一禅行；念行已灭，内守一心，志在恬静，成二禅行；惟观无婬，心安体正，分别见真，成三禅行；已断苦乐，无忧喜想，意已清净，成四禅行。

What are the five faculties? The first is the faculty of faith, which directs the mind towards the four joys. The second is the faculty of energy, which cultivates the four right endeavors. The third is the faculty of mindfulness, which contemplates the four intentions. The fourth is the faculty of concentration, which contemplates the practice of the four dhyānas. The fifth is the faculty of wisdom, which sees the four noble truths.

「何谓五根？一为信根，意向四喜；二为精进根，治四意端；三为念根，念四志惟；四为定根，思四禅行；五为智根，见四真谛。

What are the five powers? The first is the power of faith, with a joyful mind that is indestructible. The second is the power of energy, always able to control oneself. The third is the power of mindfulness, attaining the contemplation of intentions. The fourth is the power of concentration, with a mind of dhyāna that is not disturbed. The fifth is the power of wisdom, realizing the path by oneself.

「何谓五力？一为信力，喜意不坏；二为精进力，常能健制；三为念力，得志惟观；四为定力，禅意不乱；五为智力，以道自证。

What are the seven factors of awakening? The factor of awakening of mindfulness, the factor of awakening of discernment of phenomena, the factor of awakening of energy, the factor of awakening of joy and happiness, the factor of awakening of one-pointedness, the factor of awakening of concentration, and the factor of awakening of equanimity.

「何谓七觉？志念觉意，法解觉意，精进觉意，爱喜觉意，一向觉意，惟定觉意，行护觉意。

What are the eightfold path? Right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These are the pure Dharma for transcending the world.

「何谓八道？正见、正思、正言、正行、正命、正治、正志、正定，是为度世清净之法」

At that time, the Buddha invited Venerable Ānanda to go together to the city of Kuśinagara. Having received the instruction, Ānanda followed. As the Buddha was passing through the country, he went out of the city gate and turned his body to the right, looking back at the gate and smiling. Ānanda immediately adjusted his robes, placed his right knee on the ground, knelt for a long time, and asked, "Since I have been attending to you for more than twenty years, I have never seen the Buddha act without a reason. Why did you turn your body to look back at the gate and smile? What is the reason for this?"

彼时，佛请贤者阿难，俱至拘利邑，即受教行。佛乐维耶，过国中出城门，回身右转视门而笑。贤者阿难即整衣服，右膝著地，长跪问曰：「自我得侍二十余年，未曾见佛行以无缘；如回身视门而笑，是何因缘？」

The Buddha said, "It is so, Ānanda! The Buddha's dignified manner does not turn the body in vain and smile without reason. It is because this is my last time seeing Vaiśālī, so I am looking back and smiling." Then the Buddha spoke a verse himself:

佛言：「如是如是。阿难！佛之仪法不妄回身虚而笑也。是我最后见维耶离，故视笑耳。」于是佛自颂曰：

This is my last time, viewing Vaiśālī. I
will go to nirvāṇa there, no longer taking rebirth.

「是吾之最后，　　游观维耶离，将游彼泥洹，　　不复受有身。」

Another bhikṣu also praised in verse:

有异比丘，亦赞颂曰：

The Buddha praised this last body, its practice reaching its utmost. If it
were to sink into emptiness, where would we see the coming of the Sage?

「佛称此末后，　　身行极于斯，若遂沦清虚，　　于何覩圣来？」

The Buddha went with Ānanda to the northern forest grove of the city of Kuśinagara and told the bhikṣus: "You should protect pure precepts, contemplate concentration, and understand wisdom. Those who uphold precepts, have concentration and wisdom, attain great virtue and abundant fame, permanently free from greed, anger, and ignorance, can attain arahantship. Those who wish to attain right liberation in this life should increase their self-understanding to end this life and enter the pure path. Having practiced accordingly, one can know for oneself that one will not receive another body in the future."

佛与阿难俱到拘利止城北林树下，告诸比丘：「当护净戒，当思定意，当解智慧。夫以守戒，有定慧者，成大德致丰誉，永离贪婬、瞋恚、愚痴，可得应真，欲以现世望正度者，当加自解，令尽是生，入清净道。已如应行，乃自知身，后不复受。」

The Buddha again invited the Venerable Ānanda to go together to the city of Kaniṣṭha, and sat under a tree north of the city. He told the bhikṣus: "You should protect pure precepts, contemplate concentration, and seek to understand wisdom. Those who protect pure precepts do not follow the three characteristics. Those who concentrate their minds do not let them wander. Those who understand wisdom are free from desire and have unobstructed practice. Those with precepts, concentration, and wisdom have great virtue and abundant fame. They are also free from the three defilements and can attain arahantship. Those who wish to attain right liberation with this body should diligently seek understanding to end this life and enter the pure path. Practicing accordingly, one can know for oneself that one will be extinguished and not receive another body in the future."

佛复请贤者阿难，俱之健持邑，止城北树下坐。告诸比丘：「当守净戒，思惟定意，求解智慧。守净戒者不随三态，惟定意者心不放散，已解慧者去离受欲，行无罣碍。有戒定慧，德大丰誉，又离三垢，终得应真。欲以是身望得正度，当勤求解，令尽是生，入清净道。作如应行，乃自知灭，后不复受。」

The Buddha again went with Ānanda through the city of Kaniṣṭha, and also through the cities of Suvarṇabhūmi, Hastināpura, and Puṣkarāvatī, to the city of Sudarśana. Everywhere he told the disciples about these three essentials: "You should protect precepts, contemplate concentration, and understand wisdom. Those who protect these three have great virtue and abundant fame. They can eliminate desire, anger, and ignorance, which is called right liberation. Having precepts and a concentrated mind, the mind of concentration is accomplished. With a concentrated mind accomplished, the mind of wisdom is clear. Just as dyed clean cloth receives clear colors, having these three minds makes the path easy to attain. You should just diligently seek understanding with a single mind, to end this life and enter purity. Practicing accordingly, one can know for oneself the end of this life and not receive another body. If one cannot fully practice precepts, concentration, and wisdom, it is difficult to attain liberation. Having these three, the mind is naturally enlightened. Sitting and contemplating, one can clearly see the five paths - heavenly beings, humans, hell beings, animals, and ghosts - and know the thoughts and intentions of all beings. It is like a clear stream, in which the sand, gravel, blue, yellow, white, and black are all seen. Those who attain the path only see clearly because their minds are clear. Those who wish to attain the path should purify their minds. Just as turbid water cannot be seen, an impure mind cannot attain liberation. The teachings of the teacher should be practiced by the disciples. The teacher is not in the disciple's mind, but by correcting their thoughts, those with upright thoughts will naturally attain the path."

佛又与阿难俱，过掩满邑，及出金邑、授手邑、华氏邑，至善净邑。处处为弟子说此三要曰：「当护戒，当思定，当解慧。守此三者，德丰誉大，消婬怒痴，是谓正度。已有戒心则定心成，定心已成则智心明，如染净洁受色明好，有此三心则道易得，但当一意勤身求解，令尽是生已入清净。行如应者，自知极此，不复受生。若不能具戒定慧行，欲度世难。有此三者意自开解，坐而思惟便见五道——天上、人中、地狱、畜生、鬼神——分明，悉知众生意志所念。譬如溪水清，其中沙砾，青黄白黑，所有皆见；得道之人，但心清故，所视悉见，欲得道者，当净其中心。如水浑浊，则无所见，持心不净，不得度世。师所见说，弟子当行，师同不入弟子心中，就正其念，念意端者道自得矣。」

The Buddha was already delighted with Sudarśana. He again invited the Venerable Ānanda to go together to the city of Vairambhya, and sat under a tree north of the city. In the evening, Ānanda rose from his meditation, went to the Buddha, bowed, and asked with his hands folded: "I suddenly want to know how many reasons there are for an earthquake."

佛已乐善净，又请贤者阿难，俱之夫延邑，止城北树下坐。晡] cf.【碛砂】时，阿难从燕坐起，到佛所稽首毕，叉手问曰：「仓卒欲知地动几事？」

The Buddha told Ānanda: "There are three reasons: First, the earth rests on water, the water rests on wind, and the wind rests on space. When a great wind arises, the water is disturbed, and when the water is disturbed, the earth moves. Second, it is because the enlightened śramaṇas and divine beings want to manifest responses, so they cause the earth to move. Third, it is because of the Buddha's power. Since I became a Buddha, the earth has moved before and after, and the sun, moon, and earth of the three thousand worlds are all moved. Many devas, humans, and ghosts have heard and understood."

佛语阿难：「有三因缘：一为地倚水上，水倚于风，风倚于空，大风起则水扰，水扰则地动。二为得道沙门及神妙天，欲现感应故以地动。三为佛力，自我作佛前后已动，三千日月万二千天地无不感发，天人鬼神多得闻解。」

Ānanda exclaimed: "Wonderful! The Buddha is unparalleled. With the natural Dharma, there is nothing that is not moved. The supreme virtue and supreme Way are majestic and so."

阿难叹曰：「妙哉！佛为无伦，以自然法无不感动，至德至道巍巍乃尔。」

The Buddha said: "It is so, Ānanda! The Buddha's virtue is not small. It comes from accumulating merit and practicing various good deeds for countless eons, and then becoming a Buddha on one's own. There is this wondrous and natural Dharma teaching. One knows everything and sees everything, and there is nothing that is not entered or transformed. I remember that in the past, with a compassionate mind, I transformed and taught hundreds of thousands of people, princes and nobles from all over the world, according to their appearance and demeanor. I traveled around teaching and transforming them, causing them to have good intentions. I manifested transformations like this throughout the eight directions, adapting to the customs, attire, and language of each country. I observed what methods people practiced and what scriptures they knew, and then taught them the right path. For those who enjoyed the meaning of the teachings, I established the teachings. For those who understood the principles, I explained the supreme essentials. After establishing their resolve, I would disappear. Even kings and nobles did not know who I was. Later, they all became immersed in and respected the Dharma teaching. This is the Buddha's pure and wondrous natural Dharma.

佛言：「如是如是。阿难！佛德不小，乃从无数劫积累功德，奉行诸善自致作佛，有是神妙自然法化，一切知一切见，无不入无不化。忆念我昔以慈悲心，若干百千人，天下诸王君子众，化住相见，随其像貌为安慰说经道，周流教化使得善意。如是现化，遍于八方，随其国俗服饰语言，相其人行何法、知何经，而为演说授以正道，乐义言者为设典教，解道理者为说上要，坚立其志已而舍没。设王君子莫知我谁，后皆耽味敬承法化，是为佛之清妙自然法也。

Moreover, Ānanda! I attained the Buddha's power and entered everywhere to manifest transformations with the Buddha's dignified Dharma. I entered the assembly of śramaṇas and became their teacher and guide. Later, I transformed and entered the assembly of brāhmaṇas. I also lived among the Confucian scholars and various schools of thought. According to their attire, voice, and language, I bestowed the scriptures and teachings, accomplishing everything for them. I taught the Dharma of confusion and then disappeared. All of them accepted my teachings but did not know who I was. This is the Buddha's rare and natural Dharma.

大迦叶、阿那律、众比丘会共议：「一日三十万众，及诸国豪姓群臣，得值佛时，敬意行福，终皆当生第四天上，与弥勒会而得解脱。拘夷国王，当生第十二水音天上，至弥勒作佛时，当下为佛造立精舍，胜今给孤独园。」阿难问大迦叶：「拘夷王何以不于弥勒佛求应真道？」答言：「是王未厌生死苦故，未厌] cf.【丽】苦者不得应真。」阿难言：「我已患厌身苦，不得离世间，奚不得道？」迦叶答言：「汝但持戒，不行身观，坐猗生死饭食想，而生死行未休故也。」

After ninety days, Mahākāśyapa, Aniruddha, and the assembly of bhikṣus discussed together, "The Buddha's twelve divisions of the canon have the four Agamas. Only Ānanda served the Buddha for a long time. What the Buddha spoke, Ānanda recited. We should receive it from the written texts. Fearing that he has not yet attained the Way and still has greedy thoughts, we want to hold him accountable for past events. We should set up a high seat for him, and he should ascend and descend three times. This way, we can get the truth." They all said, "Very good." When the assembly was seated, the upright bhikṣus expelled Ānanda. After a while, they invited him back. Ānanda entered and paid respects to the assembly. Those who had not attained the Way all stood up for him. The upright bhikṣus placed him on a high seat in the center, and he declined, saying, "This is not Ānanda's seat." The bhikṣus said, "For the sake of the Buddha's teachings, we place you on a high seat. We want to ask you something." Ānanda took his seat. The assembly asked, "You have a great fault. Do you know it? The Buddha once said, 'The happiness of Jambudvīpa.' Why didn't you respond?" The upright bhikṣus ordered Ānanda to descend. He descended and said, "Did the Buddha not attain freedom? Did he need me to speak for him?" The assembly was silent. The upright bhikṣus again ordered Ānanda to ascend. The assembly again asked, "The Buddha told you, 'One who has attained the four dhyānas can stay for one eon and more.' Why did you remain silent?" Ānanda descended and said, "The Buddha said that Maitreya will come down to become a Buddha. Those who have just entered the Dharma should attain it from him. If I stayed, what would be the difference from Maitreya?" The assembly was again silent. Ānanda was frightened. The bhikṣus said, "Virtuous one, you should fully explain the Buddha's teachings according to the Dharma." He replied, "Yes." In this way, he ascended three times. Ānanda said for the last time, "Thus have I heard at one time." Those in the assembly who had not attained the Way all wept, saying, "The Buddha just spoke the teachings. Why is it so soon?"

至九十日，大迦叶、阿那律、众比丘会共议：「佛十二部经，有四阿含，独阿难侍佛久，佛之所说，阿难志讽，当从书受，恐其未得道，尚有贪心，欲持旧事诘责阿难，与设高座，三上三下，如是者，可得诚实。」皆言大善。众会坐定，直事比丘，逐阿难出，须臾又请，阿难入礼众僧，未得道者，皆为之起。直事比丘，处著中央高座，于是让言：「此非阿难座。」众比丘言：「用佛经故，处汝高座，欲有所问。」阿难就座。众僧问曰：「汝有大过，宁自知不？昔者佛言：『阎浮提乐。』汝奚不对？」直事比丘，勅阿难下。即下对言：「佛为不得自在，当须我言耶？」众僧默然。直事比丘，又令阿难上。众复问曰：「佛为汝说：『得四禅足者，可止一劫有余。』汝何以嘿？」阿难下言：「佛说弥勒，当下作佛，始入法者，应从彼成；设自留者，如弥勒何？」僧又嘿然。阿难心怖，众比丘言：「贤者当如法意具说佛经。」对曰：「唯然。」如是三上，阿难最后上言：「闻如是一时」，座中未得道者，皆垂泣言：「佛适说经，今何以疾。」

Mahākāśyapa then selected forty arhats from the assembly to receive the four Agamas from Ānanda: the Madhyama Āgama, the Dīrghāgama, the Ekottara Āgama, and the Saṃyuktāgama. These four texts were written for those with greed and desire, anger and joy, ignorance and foolishness, and those who are unfilial and do not follow teachers. Each of the four Agamas was written on sixty rolls of silk. The bhikṣus said, "By writing these four texts, they will spread throughout the world." Therefore, at the place where the Buddha was cremated, four trees spontaneously grew, and they gathered together to classify and write down the Buddha's twelve divisions of the canon. The precepts and Dharma are complete. Those who uphold the Buddha's teachings and precepts for a thousand years will all gather to be born in Maitreya Buddha's presence and attain liberation from birth and death.

大迦叶即选众中四十应真，从阿难受得四阿含：一中阿含，二长阿含，三增一阿含，四杂阿含。此四文者，一为贪婬作，二为喜怒作，三为愚痴作，四为不孝不师作。四阿含文，各六十疋素。众比丘言：「用写四文，当兴行于天下。」故佛阇维处，自生四树，遂相捡敛，分别书佛十二部经。戒律法具，其在千岁中，持佛经戒者，后皆会生弥勒佛所，当从彼解度生死履。
