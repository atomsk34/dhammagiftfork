T7 大般涅槃经

Parallel to: DN 16 (~) / DN 17 (~) / Snp 1.5 (~) / DĀ 2 (~) / MĀ 68 (~) / T 5 (~) / T 6 (~)

Thus have I heard:

如是我闻：

Thus have I heard:

一时，佛在毘耶离大林中重阁讲堂，与大比丘众千二百五十人俱。尔时，世尊而与阿难，于晨朝时，著衣持钵，入城乞食，还归所止，食竟洗漱，收摄衣钵，告阿难言：「汝可取我尼师坛来，吾今当往遮波罗支提，入定思惟。」作此言已，即与阿难，俱往彼处。既至彼处，阿难即便敷尼师坛，于是世尊结跏趺坐，寂然思惟。阿难尔时去佛不远，亦于别处，端坐入定。

The Blessed One, after a while, arose from his concentration and said to Ānanda, "These stupas in Vaiśālī - the Udayana stupa, the Gautama stupa, the Mango stupa, the Many Sons stupa, the Sāla stupa, and the Cāpāla stupa - are all very delightful. Ānanda, a person with the four bases of supernormal power can live for a full eon or less. The Tathāgata now has great spiritual power, so how could he not be able to live for an eon or less?" At that time, the World-Honored One, having opened this door of requests, spoke to Ānanda. Ānanda was silent and did not realize it. The World-Honored One spoke earnestly three times, but Ānanda was still confused and did not understand. He did not request the Tathāgata to live for an eon or less, benefiting the devas and people of the world. Why was that? Because he was deluded by Māra, the evil one. At that time, the World-Honored One spoke these words three times, but still saw that Ānanda's mind was not enlightened. He then remained silent.

世尊须臾，从定而觉，告阿难言：「此毘耶离，优陀延支提、瞿昙支提、庵罗支提、多子支提、娑罗支提、遮波罗支提，此等支提，甚可爱乐。阿难！四神足人，尚能住寿满于一劫若减一劫，如来今者有大神力，岂当不能住寿一劫若减一劫？」尔时，世尊既开如是可请之门，以语阿难，阿难默然，而不觉知；世尊乃至慇懃三说，阿难茫然，犹不解悟，不请如来住寿一劫若减一劫，利益世间诸天人民。所以者何？其为魔王所迷惑故。尔时，世尊三说此语，犹见阿难心不开悟，即便默然。

Then Māra, the evil one, came to the Buddha and said, "World-Honored One! Now is the time for parinirvāṇa. Sugata! Now is the time for parinirvāṇa. Why is that? In the past, by the Nairañjanā River, I requested the World-Honored One to enter parinirvāṇa. The World-Honored One then replied, 'My fourfold assembly - bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās - is not yet complete. I have not yet subdued the other non-Buddhist paths. Therefore, I am not yet ready to enter parinirvāṇa.' World-Honored One! Now your fourfold assembly is complete. You have already subdued the other non-Buddhist paths. What you were to do is all done. Now is the time to enter parinirvāṇa." At that time, Māra, the evil one, made this request three times. The Tathāgata then replied, "Excellent! In the past, by the Nairañjanā River, I have already promised you. Because my fourfold assembly was not yet complete, I have waited until now. Now it is complete. In three months, I will enter parinirvāṇa." At that time, Māra, the evil one, heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and leaped for joy, and returned to his heavenly palace.

尔时，魔王来至佛所，而白佛言：「世尊！今者宜般涅槃，善逝！今者宜般涅槃。所以者何？我于往昔在尼连禅河侧，劝请世尊入般涅槃，世尊尔时而见答言：『我四部众——比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷——犹未具足，又未降伏诸余外道，所以未应入般涅槃。』世尊今者四部之众，无不具足，又已降伏诸余外道，所为之事皆悉已毕，今者宜应入般涅槃。」于时，魔王如是三请，如来即便答言：「善哉！我于往昔，在尼连禅河侧，已自许汝，以四部众未具足故，所以至今；今已具足，却后三月当般涅槃。」是时，魔王闻佛此语，欢喜踊跃，还归天宫。

At that time, the World-Honored One immediately gave up his life, but by his spiritual power, he remained alive for three months. At that time, the great earth moved in eighteen ways, and the heavenly drum sounded by itself. Due to the Buddha's power, a voice was heard in the sky, saying, "The Tathāgata will soon enter parinirvāṇa." The devas and human beings suddenly heard this voice, and their hearts were greatly saddened, with blood appearing all over their bodies.

尔时，世尊即便舍寿，而以神力住命三月。是时，大地十八相动，天鼓自鸣，以佛力故空中唱言：「如来不久当般涅槃。」诸天人众，忽闻此声，心大悲𢙐遍体血现。

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse at that place:

是时，世尊即于彼处，而说偈言：

All sentient beings follow existence and birth and death. I
now also have birth and death, but I do not
follow existence. All created activities, I now wish to abandon.

「一切诸众生，　　皆随有生死，我今亦生死，　　而不随于有，一切造作行，　　我今欲弃舍。」

At that time, after the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he remained silent. Then Ānanda saw the great earth shaking and was greatly frightened. He thought to himself, "Why has this suddenly happened? This kind of thing is not for a small reason. I should now go and ask the World-Honored One." Having thought this, he immediately rose from his seat, went before the Buddha, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and said, "World-Honored One! I was just contemplating in another place when I suddenly saw the great earth shake in eighteen ways, and I also heard the sound of heavenly drums in the sky. I was greatly frightened and did not know what this sign meant."

尔时，世尊说此偈已，默然而住。是时，阿难见大地动，心大惊怖，而自念言：「今者何故忽有是相？如此之事非为小缘，我今当往咨问世尊。」作此念已，即从座起，到于佛前，头面礼足白言：「世尊！我向于彼，别处思惟，忽见大地十八相动，又闻空中天鼓之声，心大怖惧，不知此相，是何因缘？」

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! The great earth shaking has eight causes and conditions: First, the great earth rests on water, and this great water rests on the wind wheel. This wind wheel rests on empty space. Sometimes a strong wind suddenly arises in empty space, blowing the wind wheel. When the wind wheel moves, the water also moves. When the water moves, the great earth shakes. Second, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās who cultivate spiritual powers and have just accomplished them want to test themselves, so the great earth shakes. Third, when a bodhisattva is in Tuṣita Heaven and is about to descend to be born from his mother's womb, the great earth shakes. Fourth, when a bodhisattva is first born, emerging from his right side, the great earth shakes. Fifth, when a bodhisattva abandons the royal palace, goes forth to study the path, and attains omniscience, the great earth shakes. Sixth, when a Tathāgata attains enlightenment and first turns the wonderful wheel of the Dharma for humans and gods, the great earth shakes. Seventh, when a Tathāgata abandons his life and abides by the power of spiritual powers, the great earth shakes. Eighth, when a Tathāgata enters parinirvāṇa, the great earth shakes. Ānanda! You should know that the causes and conditions of the earth shaking are these eight things.

佛言：「阿难！大地震动，有八因缘：一者大地依于水住，又此大水依风轮住，又此风轮依虚空住，空中有时猛风大起，吹彼风轮，风轮既动，彼水亦动，彼水既动，大地乃动；二者比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷，有修神通，始成就者，欲自试验，故大地动；三者菩萨在兜率天，将欲来下，降神母胎，故大地动；四者菩萨初生，从右胁出，故大地动；五者菩萨舍于王宫，出家学道，成一切种智，故大地动；六者如来成道，始为人天转妙法轮，故大地动；七者如来舍寿，以神通力住命而住，故大地动；八者如来般涅槃时，故大地动。阿难！当知地动因缘，有此八事。

Ānanda! There are eight groups of people: first, kṣatriyas, second, brāhmaṇas, third, elders and householders, fourth, śramaṇas, fifth, the Four Heavenly Kings, sixth, the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, seventh, the Māra King, and eighth, the Brahmā King. For these eight groups of people, I observe their faculties and those who are suitable to be liberated. I appear in various forms to teach the Dharma for them, but they do not know that it is taught by me. Ānanda! There are eight kinds of mastery: first, internally having the perception of form, externally observing few forms as the realm; second, internally having the perception of form, externally observing immeasurable forms as the realm; third, internally having no perception of form, externally observing few forms as the realm; fourth, internally having no perception of form, externally observing immeasurable forms as the realm; fifth, observing all forms as blue; sixth, observing all forms as yellow; seventh, observing all forms as red; eighth, observing all forms as white. These are the superior dharmas of the practitioner. Furthermore, Ānanda! There are eight kinds of liberation: first, internally having the perception of form, externally observing forms; second, internally having no perception of form, externally observing forms as impure contemplation; third, the liberation of purity; fourth, the liberation of the sphere of space; fifth, the liberation of the sphere of consciousness; sixth, the liberation of the sphere of nothingness; seventh, the liberation of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception; eighth, the liberation of the cessation of perception and feeling. These are also the superior dharmas of the practitioner. If one can thoroughly understand these dharmas, one will be free and unobstructed in all dharmas.

「阿难！有八部众，一者刹利、二者婆罗门、三者长者居士、四者沙门、五者四天王、六者忉利天、七者魔王、八者梵王，此八部众，我观其根应得度者，随所现形，而为说法，彼亦不知是我所说。阿难！有八胜处，一者内有色想外观色少境界、二者内有色想外观色无量境界、三者内无色想外观色少境界、四者内无色想外观色无量境界、五者观一切色青、六者观一切色黄、七者观一切色赤、八者观一切色白，此是行者上胜之法。复次，阿难！有八解脱，一者内有色想外观色、二者内无色想外观色不净思惟、三者净解脱、四者空处解脱、五者识处解脱、六者无所有处解脱、七者非想非非想处解脱、八者灭尽定解脱，此亦复是行者胜法。若能究竟此等法者，即于诸法，自在无碍。

"Ānanda! Do you know? When I first attained enlightenment in the past, I liberated Udraka Rāmaputra on the banks of the Nairañjanā River. At that time, Māra, the Evil One, came to me and requested, 'World-Honored One! Now is the time to enter parinirvāṇa. Well-Gone One! Now is the time to enter parinirvāṇa. Why is that? Those who are to be liberated are all liberated. Now is the time for parinirvāṇa.' He made this request three times, and I replied, 'Now is not the time for parinirvāṇa. Why is that? My fourfold assembly is not yet complete, and those who are to be liberated have not yet reached the end. The followers of other paths have not yet been subdued.' I answered like this three times. Māra, the Evil One, heard this and was filled with sorrow. He then returned to his heavenly palace. He came again and requested, 'World-Honored One! Now is the time to enter parinirvāṇa. Well-Gone One! Now is the time to enter parinirvāṇa. Why is that? In the past, on the banks of the Nairañjanā River, I requested the World-Honored One to enter parinirvāṇa. The World-Honored One then replied, "My fourfold assembly of bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās is not yet complete, and I have not yet subdued the followers of other paths. Therefore, it is not yet time to enter parinirvāṇa." World-Honored One! Now the fourfold assembly is complete, and the followers of other paths have been subdued. What was to be done has all been accomplished. Now is the time to enter parinirvāṇa." Māra, the Evil One, made this request three times. I then replied, 'In the past, on the banks of the Nairañjanā River, I already promised you. It is because my fourfold assembly was not yet complete. Now it is complete. After three months, I will enter parinirvāṇa.' When Māra, the Evil One, heard my words, he was overjoyed and returned to his heavenly palace. After I accepted Māra's request here, I then gave up my life and remained alive for three months. For this reason, the great earth shook.

「阿难！知不？我于往昔，初成道时，度优楼频螺迦叶，在尼连禅河侧。尔时，魔王来至我所，而请我言：『世尊！今者宜般涅槃。善逝！今者宜般涅槃。何以故？所应度者皆悉解脱，今者正是般涅槃时。』如是三请，我即答言：『今者未是般涅槃时。所以者何？我四部众未具足故，所应度者皆未究竟。诸外道众，又未降伏。』如是三答，魔王闻已，心怀愁𢙐，即还天宫。向者又来，而请我言：『世尊！今者宜般涅槃。善逝！今者宜般涅槃。所以者何？我于往昔，在尼连禅河侧，劝请世尊而般涅槃，世尊尔时即答我言：「我四部众——比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷——犹未具足，又未降伏诸余外道，是以未应入般涅槃。」世尊！今者四部之众，无不具足，又已降伏诸余外道，所为之事皆悉已毕，今者宜应入般涅槃。』魔王乃至如是三请，我即答言：『我于往昔，在尼连禅河侧，已自许汝，以四部众未具足故，所以至今。今已具足，却后三月，当般涅槃。』魔王闻我作此语已，欢喜踊跃还归天宫。我既于此受魔请已，即便舍寿，住命三月，以是因缘，大地震动。」

At that time, Ānanda heard the Buddha's words, and his heart was filled with great sorrow and blood appeared all over his body. He wept with tears streaming down and said to the Buddha, "I only pray that the World-Honored One will have compassion on us and remain alive for one kalpa or even less than one kalpa, benefiting the devas and people of the world." He made this request three times.

尔时，阿难闻佛此语，心大悲𢙐徧体血现，涕泣流泪，而白佛言：「唯愿世尊！哀愍我等，住寿一劫若减一劫，利益世间诸天人民。」如是三请。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Ānanda, "It is not the time for you to request the Tathāgata to stay. Why is that? I have already promised Māra that after three months, I will enter parinirvāṇa. How can you now request me to stay? Ānanda, since you have been serving me, have you ever heard me speak two words?"

尔时，世尊告阿难言：「汝今非是请如来时。所以者何？我已许魔，却后三月，当般涅槃。汝今云何而请住耶？阿难，汝侍我来，颇曾闻我说二言不？」

Ānanda said to the Buddha, "I have never heard the Teacher of gods and humans speak two words. I have heard the World-Honored One say to the four groups of followers that a person with the four bases of supernormal power can live for a full kalpa or less than a kalpa. How much more so for the Tathāgata, the king of immeasurable spiritual power and mastery! Now, if you cannot live for a kalpa or less than a kalpa, but will abandon your life and remain for only three months, I only pray that the World-Honored One will have compassion on us and live for a kalpa or less than a kalpa."

阿难白佛：「实不曾闻天人之师有二言也。我于往昔曾闻，世尊为四部众，而说法言，四神足人，则能住寿，满足一劫若减一劫，况复如来，无量神力自在之王！今更不能住寿一劫若减一劫，而便舍寿，住命三月。唯愿世尊！哀愍我等，住寿一劫若减一劫。」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied to Ānanda, "The reason I am now abandoning my life is because of you. Why is that? I previously told you here that a person with the four bases of supernormal power can live for a full kalpa or less than a kalpa. The Tathāgata now has great spiritual power. How could he not be able to live for a kalpa or less than a kalpa?" Up to this point, I earnestly spoke three times, opening the door for requests, but you remained silent and never asked me to live for a kalpa or less than a kalpa. Therefore, I now remain for three months. How can you now request me to stay?

尔时，世尊答阿难言：「我今所以便舍寿者，正由汝故。所以者何？我前于此向汝说言：『四神足人，尚能住寿，满足一劫若减一劫，如来今者有大神力，岂当不能住寿一劫若减一劫。』乃至如是慇懃三说，开劝请门，而汝默然，曾不请我，住寿一劫若减一劫。是故，我今住命三月，汝今云何方请我住！」

At that time, Ānanda heard the Buddha's words and definitively knew that the Buddha would enter parinirvāṇa and could not be persuaded. His heart was filled with pain, and he was distressed and overwhelmed, weeping uncontrollably. At that time, the World-Honored One, seeing that Ānanda was greatly distressed, comforted him with a voice like Brahma, saying, "Ānanda! Do not be so sorrowful now. All conditioned dharmas are like this. All gatherings will eventually separate."

尔时，阿难闻佛此语，决定知佛入般涅槃，不可劝请，心生苦痛，闷绝懊𢙐，泣涕流连，不能自胜。尔时，世尊既见阿难生大苦𢙐，而以梵音安慰之言：「阿难！汝今勿生忧悲，有为之法皆悉如是，一切合会无不别离。」

The World-Honored One then spoke a verse, saying:

世尊即便而说偈言：

All conditioned dharmas are impermanent. Those who are united through affection
and love will inevitably be separated. The nature of conditioned dharmas
is like this. One should not give rise to sorrow and distress.

「一切有为法，　　皆悉归无常，恩爱和合者，　　必归于别离，诸行法如是，　　不应生忧𢙐。」

Then Ānanda wept and said, "The teacher of gods and humans, the unsurpassed great honored one, will soon enter parinirvāṇa. How can I not be sorrowful?" He then beat his head and cried out, "Alas! How painful! The eye of the world is extinguished! Sentient beings will soon lose their compassionate father!"

于是，阿难流泪而言：「天人之师无上大尊，不久应当入般涅槃，我今云何而不忧𢙐？」即便拍头高声唱言：「呜呼！苦哉！世间眼灭！众生不久，失于慈父！」

At that time, the World-Honored One again told Ānanda, "You should not give rise to sorrow now. Even if I were to remain for a kalpa or less than a kalpa, I would still eventually pass away. The nature and characteristics of conditioned dharmas are like this. Do not be solely sorrowful for me. I now wish to return to the upper-story lecture hall. Please bring my sitting mat." Then the World-Honored One together with Ānanda returned to the upper-story lecture hall.

尔时，世尊又告阿难：「汝今不应生于忧𢙐，设住一劫若减一劫，会亦当灭，有为之法，性相如是，汝勿于我独生苦也。我今欲还重阁讲堂，汝可取我尼师坛来。」于是，世尊即与阿难俱，共还归重阁讲堂。

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "Now you should tell the bhikṣus in the great forest and the upper-story lecture hall to all go to the great assembly hall." Ānanda, upon receiving the order, immediately informed the bhikṣus that the World-Honored One wanted them all to go to the great assembly hall. When the bhikṣus had assembled, Ānanda said to the Buddha, "The bhikṣus have all assembled. May the Tathāgata know the time himself."

尔时，世尊告阿难言：「汝今可语，此大林中，重阁讲堂，诸比丘众，皆悉令往大集讲堂。」阿难奉勅，即便普语诸比丘众，世尊皆令往大集堂。比丘集已，阿难白佛：「诸比丘众悉皆已集。唯愿如来，自知其时。」

At that time, the Tathāgata went from the upper-story lecture hall to the great assembly hall, spread his seat, and sat down. He told the bhikṣus, "The dharmas I have taught you in the past, always contemplate them, recite and practice them without neglecting them. Cultivate pure conduct and uphold the precepts, benefiting the world, devas, and humans. Bhikṣus! What dharmas have I taught you in the past? Contemplate them, do not be lazy. The thirty-seven factors of the path, namely the four foundations of mindfulness, four right efforts, four bases of supernormal power, five faculties, five powers, seven factors of awakening, and eightfold noble path, you should cultivate and diligently contemplate. These dharmas can lead to the place of liberation. Furthermore, bhikṣus, all dharmas are impermanent. The body and life are fragile, like a flash of lightning. You should not give rise to carelessness. You should know that the Tathāgata will not remain for long. In three months, he will enter parinirvāṇa."

尔时，如来从重阁讲堂，往大集堂，敷座而坐，告诸比丘：「我昔为汝所说诸法，常思惟之，诵习勿废。净修梵行，护持禁戒，福利世间诸天人民。诸比丘！我昔为汝说何等法，汝思惟之，勿生懈怠。三十七道品法，所谓四念处、四正勤、四如意足、五根、五力、七觉支、八圣道分，汝应修习精勤思惟，此法能令到解脱处。复次，比丘，一切诸法皆悉无常，身命危脆犹如惊电，汝等不应生于放逸。汝等当知，如来不久，却后三月，当般涅槃。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

I wish to abandon this old and decrepit body. Now I
have given up my lifespan and will remain for three months
Those who should be taught and liberated are all completely finished
Therefore, I will not remain for long and will enter parinirvāṇa
The dharmas I have taught are your teachers. Uphold and protect
them, cultivate and practice them without neglecting them. You should diligently
practice them as if I were here, without any difference. Birth
and death are very fragile, and life and body are impermanent
Always seek liberation and do not engage in careless conduct. With
right mindfulness and pure contemplation, well protect and uphold the precepts
With concentrated mind and proper contemplation, restrain the senses from external
objects. If one can do this, then one protects the true
Dharma, reaches the place of liberation by oneself, and benefits devas and humans.

「我欲弃捐此，　　朽故之老身，今已舍于寿，　　住命留三月。所应化度者，　　皆悉已毕竟，是故我不久，　　当入般涅槃。我所说诸法，　　则是汝等师，顶戴加守护，　　修习勿废忘，汝等勤精进，　　如我在无异。生死甚危脆，　　身命悉无常，常求于解脱，　　勿造放逸行。正念清净观，　　善护持禁戒，定意端思惟，　　摄情于外境。若能如此者，　　是则护正法，自到解脱处，　　利益诸天人。」

At that time, the bhikṣus heard the Buddha's words, their hearts greatly pained, tears streaming down, blood appearing all over their bodies, confused and distressed, they said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! Please remain alive! Do not enter parinirvāṇa! Benefit sentient beings, increasing the devas and humans. Please remain alive! Do not enter parinirvāṇa! Open the wisdom eye for all sentient beings. All sentient beings fall into darkness. Please, Tathāgata, be their illumination! All sentient beings are all adrift in the great ocean of birth and death. Please, Tathāgata, be their boat and ferry!" Raising their hands, they beat their heads, pounded their chests, and cried out loudly, "Alas! How painful! The Tathāgata will soon enter parinirvāṇa. To whom will all sentient beings go for refuge?"

尔时，诸比丘闻佛此语，心大苦痛，涕泣交流，遍体血现，迷闷懊𢙐，而白佛言：「世尊！唯愿住寿！勿般涅槃！利益众生，增长人天。唯愿住寿！勿般涅槃！开诸众生智慧之眼。一切众生堕于黑暗，唯愿如来，为作明照！一切众生皆悉漂没生死大海，唯愿如来，为作舟航！」举手拍头，搥胸大叫：「呜呼！苦哉！如来不久当般涅槃，一切众生，何所归依？」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "All dharmas are impermanent. There is no separation from the union of affection and love. You should not request me to remain in the world. Why is that? Now is not the time to request me. I have briefly explained the essentials of the Dharma for you. You should uphold them well, just as I am." As the day was already late, the World-Honored One then returned with Ānanda to the lecture hall of the upper chamber.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「一切诸法，皆悉无常，恩爱合会，无不别离。汝等不应请我住世。何以故？今者非是劝请我时，向为汝等略说法要，当善奉持如我无异。」日既晚暮，世尊即与阿难俱共还归重阁讲堂。

At that time, the World-Honored One, having arrived at dawn, put on his robe, took his alms bowl, and entered the city with Ānanda to beg for food. Having obtained food, he returned to the lecture hall of the upper chamber. After eating and washing up, he went with the bhikṣus to the village of Kanda. Passing through the city of Vaiśālī, the World-Honored One looked back at the city and smiled. Ānanda immediately bowed his head to the feet and asked the Buddha, "Supreme Honored One! You do not smile without a cause." The Buddha replied, "Ānanda! The reason I am smiling at the city now is precisely because it is the last time I will see this city.

尔时，世尊既至明旦著衣持钵，而与阿难入城乞食；既得食已，即便还归重阁讲堂，食讫澡漱，与诸比丘，往干荼村。路经毘耶离城，世尊回顾，向城而笑。阿难即便头顶礼足，而问佛言：「无上大尊！非无因缘而妄笑也。」佛即答言：「阿难！我今所以向城笑者，正为最后见此城故。」

When the Tathāgata spoke these words, rain fell from the sky without clouds. Then Ānanda again said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! It is very extraordinary! The sky is clear and free from haze, yet suddenly such dense rain falls." The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do you know? The gods in the sky, hearing me say that it is the last time I will see the city of Vaiśālī, are greatly distressed and weep in sorrow. This is heavenly tears, not rain.

当于如来说此言时，虚空之中，无云而雨。于是阿难复白佛言：「世尊！甚为奇特！虚空清净，无有气翳，忽然而降如此密雨。」佛告阿难：「汝知之不？虚空诸天，闻我说言最后见于毘耶离城，心大懊𢙐，悲感涕泣，此是天泪，非为雨也。」

At that time, Ānanda and the bhikṣus, hearing the Buddha's words, were again distressed and fainted to the ground. They said to the Buddha, "Now the gods and humans are extremely pained. How can the World-Honored One wish to abandon and enter parinirvāṇa?" At that time, the Tathāgata comforted them with the Brahma voice, saying, "You should not give rise to sorrow and suffering." The bhikṣus said, "The World-Honored One is now seeing the city of Vaiśālī for the last time. Before long, he will enter parinirvāṇa. How can we not be sorrowful and pained?" In this way, it spread from one to another, and the sound reached the Licchavīs and others.

尔时，阿难及诸比丘，闻佛此语，心复悲𢙐，闷绝躃地，而白佛言：「今者天人，极大苦痛，世尊云何而欲委舍般涅槃耶？」尔时，如来即以梵音而安慰之：「汝等不应生于忧苦。」诸比丘言：「世尊今者，最后见于毘耶离城，不久便当入般涅槃，我等云何而不忧苦！」如是展转，人人相告，乃至声彻诸离车等。

At that time, the Licchavīs, hearing these words, were distressed in their hearts and blood appeared all over their bodies. They raised their hands to pat their heads, beat their chests, and cried out loudly, "Alas! How painful! The eye of the world is extinguished. Sentient beings now have no refuge." They spoke to each other, saying, "We should now go to where the Buddha is and entreat the World-Honored One to stay in Vaiśālī, to live for an eon or less than an eon, to benefit the devas and humans of the world." They then quickly went to where the Buddha was. Having left the city gate, they saw the Tathāgata from afar, and also saw Ānanda and the bhikṣus, weeping and lamenting, fainting and distressed. The Licchavīs were even more sorrowful. They went before the Buddha, bowed their heads at his feet, and said to the Buddha, "The World-Honored One now wishes to enter parinirvāṇa. All sentient beings will lose the eye of wisdom. They will be in darkness. How can they see where they should go? We only pray that the World-Honored One will live for an eon or less than an eon." They made this request three times. The Buddha then replied, "All conditioned dharmas are impermanent. Even if one lives for an eon or less than an eon, one still returns to impermanence."

时，诸离车，闻此语已，心怀悲𢙐，遍体血现，举手拍头，搥胷大叫：「呜呼！苦哉！世间眼灭，众生于今无所归依。」互相语言：「我等今者，应往佛所劝请世尊，住毘耶离，住寿一劫若减一劫，利益世间诸天人民。」即便严驾疾往佛所，既出城门遥覩如来，又见阿难及诸比丘，涕泣流连，闷绝懊𢙐，诸离车等，倍增悲恸，前诣佛所，头面礼足，而白佛言：「世尊今者欲般涅槃，一切众生，失智慧眼，方当在于黑暗之中，云何能见所应行处？唯愿世尊，住寿一劫若减一劫。」如是三请。佛即答言：「有为之法，皆悉无常，设住一劫若减一劫，亦归无常。」

At that time, the Tathāgata spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，如来即说偈言：

Although Mount Sumeru is high and wide, it will eventually be worn
away. Although the great ocean is deep and vast, it will also
dry up. Although the sun and moon are bright and clear, they
will soon set in the west. Although the earth is solid and
can bear everything, at the end of the eon, the fire of
karma will burn it and it will also return to impermanence. Those
who are united in affection and love will inevitably be separated. The
past Tathāgatas, with their indestructible vajra bodies, were also subject to impermanence
How can I be different now? This is the way of the
Buddhas. You should not request it. Do not give rise to sorrow
and distress only for me.

「须弥虽高广，　　终归于消磨，大海虽渊旷，　　会亦还枯竭。日月虽明朗，　　不久则西没，大地虽坚固，　　能负荷一切。劫尽业火然，　　亦复归无常，恩爱合会者，　　必归于别离。过去诸如来，　　金刚不坏身，亦为无常迁，　　今我岂独异。诸佛法如是，　　汝等不应请，勿偏于我上，　　而更生忧恼。」

At that time, after the World-Honored One spoke this verse, he told the Licchavis, "You should stop crying and listen to the Tathāgata's final words." The Licchavis said, "Excellent! World-Honored One! We wish to hear it." Then the Tathāgata spread out his sitting mat, sat cross-legged, and the bhikṣus and Licchavis, restraining themselves with difficulty, each sat to one side.

尔时，世尊说此偈已，告诸离车：「汝等可止啼泣之情，谛听如来最后所说。」诸离车言：「善哉！世尊！愿乐欲闻。」于是如来，敷尼师坛，结跏趺坐，诸比丘众，及以离车，强自抑忍，各坐一面。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the Licchavis, "You should know that there are seven dharmas that, if practiced, will increase and not decrease. First, being harmonious and without opposition; second, mutually understanding and discussing good deeds; third, upholding the precepts and observing propriety; fourth, respecting parents and other elders; fifth, relatives being harmonious and following each other; sixth, repairing and making offerings to the stupas in the country; seventh, upholding the Buddha's teachings, respecting bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs, and protecting upāsakas and upāsikās. If one practices these seven dharmas, it will cause one's dignity and virtue to increase, the country to flourish, and the people to be prosperous and happy. From now until the end of your life, you should uphold them without being lazy." The Licchavis then said to the Buddha, "If we practice even one of these seven dharmas, it will still cause our dignity and virtue to increase, let alone practicing all seven dharmas! Excellent! World-Honored One! We have now obtained blessings and benefits. We will uphold them until the end of our lives without forgetting them."

尔时，世尊告离车言：「汝等当知，有七种法，日就增进，而不减损。一者欢悦和同，无相违逆；二者共相晓悟，讲论善业；三者护持禁戒，及持礼仪；四者恭敬父母及余尊长；五者亲戚和睦，各相承顺；六者国内支提，修理供养；七者奉持佛法，亲敬比丘及比丘尼，爱护优婆塞及优婆夷。如是七法，若受行者，令人威德日就增进，国土炽盛，人民丰乐。汝等从今至尽形寿，当奉持之，无得懈怠。」时诸离车，即白佛言：「我等若于此七法中，修行一事，尚能令我威德增进，况复具足修行七法！善哉！世尊！我等今者，便得福利，当尽形寿奉持不忘。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "From now on, you should also practice the seven dharmas. First, being harmonious and harmonious like water and milk; second, always gathering together to discuss the Dharma; third, upholding the precepts and not giving rise to the thought of violating them; fourth, respecting teachers and senior bhikṣus; fifth, taking care of and respecting bhikṣus who practice in the wilderness; sixth, encouraging benefactors to build abodes for the Three Jewels; seventh, diligently guarding the Buddha's teachings. You should know that if a bhikṣu practices these seven dharmas, his merits and wisdom will increase day by day.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等从今亦当修习七法之行。一者欢悦和谐犹如水乳；二者常共集会讲论经法；三者护持禁戒，不生犯想；四者恭敬于师及以上座；五者料理爱敬，阿练比丘；六者劝化檀越，修营三宝所止住处；七者勤加精进，守护佛法。汝等当知，若有比丘行此七法，功德智慧，日就增进。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! There are seven more dharmas that you should practice. First, not engaging in worldly livelihood like householders; second, not engaging in frivolous and joking speech; third, not indulging in sleep and neglecting diligence; fourth, not discussing worldly matters that are of no benefit; fifth, distancing oneself from bad friends and associating with good friends; sixth, right mindfulness, not giving rise to wrong thoughts; seventh, if one attains something in the Buddha's teachings, one should further seek progress. If you can practice these seven dharmas, your merits and wisdom will increase day by day.

「复次，比丘！更有七法，汝等当修。一者不如白衣营资生业；二者不作戯论调谑之言；三者不乐睡眠废于精勤；四者不论世间无益之事；五者远恶知识，近于善友；六者正念，不生邪想；七者若于佛法有所得者，更求胜进。汝等若能行此七法，功德智慧日就增长。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! There are seven more dharmas that you should practice. First, generating firm faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha; second, having a sense of shame; third, having a sense of modesty; fourth, always delighting in learning; fifth, not being restless in mind; sixth, delighting in hearing the meaning of the sūtras; seventh, delighting in cultivating wisdom. If you can practice these seven dharmas, your merits and wisdom will increase day by day.

「复次，比丘！更有七法汝等当行。一者于佛法僧，生坚固信；二者有惭；三者有愧；四者心常乐于多闻；五者心不轻躁；六者乐闻经义；七者乐修智慧。汝等若能修此七法，功德智慧日就增长。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! There are seven more dharmas of the factors of awakening that you should practice. First, discerning dharmas; second, diligence; third, joy; fourth, mindfulness; fifth, concentration; sixth, tranquility; seventh, equanimity. If you can practice these seven dharmas, your merits and wisdom will increase day by day.

「复次，比丘！更有七觉意法，汝等当行。一者择法、二者精进、三者喜、四者念、五者定、六者猗、七者舍，汝等若能行此七法，功德智慧日就增长。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! There are seven more dharmas. First, contemplating impermanence; second, contemplating non-self; third, contemplating impurity; fourth, contemplating suffering; fifth, not delighting in the world; sixth, not being attached to the five desires; seventh, diligently cultivating tranquility. If you can practice these seven dharmas, your merits and wisdom will increase day by day.

「复次，比丘！更有七法。一者观于无常、二者观于无我、三者观于不净、四者观苦、五者不乐世间、六者不著五欲、七者勤修寂灭，汝等若能行此七法，功德智慧日就增长。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! There are seven more dharmas. First, always practicing loving-kindness with the body; second, always practicing loving-kindness with speech; third, always practicing loving-kindness with the mind; fourth, when benefactors make various offerings, distribute them equally without partiality; fifth, delight in explaining the profound and wonderful Dharma without weariness; sixth, do not teach others with worldly texts; seventh, do not give rise to hatred and jealousy towards those who are not fellow students. If you can practice these seven dharmas, your merits and wisdom will increase day by day.

「复次，比丘！更有七法，汝等当行。一者身常行慈；二者口常行慈；三者意常行慈；四者若有檀越种种布施，平等分与无使有偏；五者于深妙法，乐说不厌；六者不以世间典籍而教于人；七者见非同学，不生憎嫉。汝等若能修此七法，功德智慧日就增长。

Furthermore, bhikṣus! There are seven more dharmas. First, being able to skillfully distinguish the nine divisions of the Dharma; second, being able to understand their meanings; third, practicing and reciting the Dharma at the right times; fourth, being able to maintain proper conduct while walking, standing, sitting, and lying down; fifth, when teaching others, to teach according to one's own strengths; sixth, when brāhmaṇas, kṣatriyas, elders, and householders come to listen to the Dharma, to skillfully assess their capacities and teach accordingly; seventh, being able to distinguish between the wise and the foolish. If you can practice these seven dharmas, your merits and wisdom will increase day by day, and you will be able to protect my true Dharma.

「复次，比丘！更有七法，汝等当行。一者于九部法，善能分别；二者善解其义；三者行道诵习，皆得其时；四者行住坐卧，善得仪中；五者为人说法，并自量忖，以其所长，而以教人；六者若婆罗门、刹利、长者居士，来欲听法，当善筹量，随根为说；七者善别愚智。汝等若能行此七法，功德智慧日就增长，则能守护我之正法。」

At that time, the wives of the Licchavis in the country heard that the Buddha would soon enter parinirvāṇa and would be seeing Vaiśālī for the last time. Their hearts were filled with great sorrow and grief. Each of them, together with five hundred attendants, prepared five hundred carriages laden with offerings and various adornments. The white oxen and carriages had white banners and canopies. The black and yellow oxen and carriages were also like this, each according to the color of the oxen. They left the city in order and went to where the Buddha was. At that time, the World-Honored One saw them coming from afar and told the bhikṣus, "Do you see these wives of the Licchavis coming, with their attendants in front and behind, so beautifully adorned?" The bhikṣus replied, "Yes, we see them." The Buddha told the bhikṣus, "The Licchavi elders of Vaiśālī and their wives, their manner of coming and going, is very splendid, no different from the gods of Trāyastriṃśa Heaven.

尔时，国中诸离车妻，闻佛不久当般涅槃，今者最后见毘耶离，心大懊𢙐，悲泣流连，各与五百眷属，各办五百乘车，载供养具，种种庄严，车牛白者，悬素幡盖，如是玄黄，各随牛色，次第出城，往至佛所。尔时，世尊遥见彼来，告诸比丘：「汝等见此诸离车妻，前后导从，极严丽不？」比丘答言：「唯然见之。」佛告比丘：「此毘耶离离车长者，及以其妻，出入之仪，甚为光饰，与忉利天，等无异也。」

At that time, the wives of the Licchavis arrived where the Buddha was. They bowed their heads at his feet, wept sorrowfully, and were unable to control themselves. They offered various offerings to the Buddha and said, "World-Honored One! Please remain in the world to teach and transform sentient beings. If the World-Honored One now enters parinirvāṇa, we will be blind and forever without enlightenment. We have received birth with little merit and are in this female body, always limited and not free. We have no opportunity to often be close to the World-Honored One. If the World-Honored One is about to enter parinirvāṇa, our wholesome roots will diminish day by day.

时，离车妻既到佛所，头面礼足，悲泣流连，不能自胜，以诸供具，而供养佛白言：「世尊！唯愿住寿教化众生。世尊今若般涅槃者，我等盲瞑，永无开悟。受生薄福，为此女身，恒有限碍，不得自在，无缘而数亲近世尊。世尊便欲般涅槃者，我等善根日就减损。」

At that time, the Tathāgata replied, "From now until the end of your lives, diligently uphold the precepts, guarding them as one would protect one's eyes. Be upright in your thoughts and do not give rise to flattery or jealousy. This will be like always seeing me." When the wives of the Licchavis heard these words of the Buddha, their grief doubled and they were unable to control themselves. They sat to one side.

尔时如来而答之言：「汝等从今至尽形寿，精勤持戒，如人护眼，意念端直，勿生谄嫉，此便即是，常得见我。」诸离车妻，闻佛此语，倍增悲绝，不能自胜，却坐一面。

At that time, the woman Ambapālī, with an upright and beautiful appearance, the foremost in the world, heard that the Buddha would soon enter parinirvāṇa and would be seen for the last time in the city of Vaiśālī. She was filled with grief and tears flowed uncontrollably. She then left the city with her five hundred attendants, adorning five hundred carriages, and went to where the Buddha was. At that time, the World-Honored One, seeing them coming from afar, told the bhikṣus, "The woman Ambapālī is coming to see me now. Her appearance is extraordinary, unparalleled in the world. You should all be upright in mind and correct in thought, without giving rise to attachment. Bhikṣus! Observe this body, which is impure, filled with liver, gall, intestines, stomach, heart, lungs, spleen, kidneys, feces, urine, pus, and blood. There are eighty thousand kinds of worms dwelling within it. Hair, body hair, nails, teeth, thin skin covering the flesh, and the nine orifices constantly flow, with nothing to be enjoyed. Moreover, this body, from its beginning, arises from impurity. Wherever this body goes, it can cause impurity to overflow. Although adorned with carvings and colors, and perfumed with famous fragrances, it is like a jeweled vase containing foul stench. Moreover, when it dies, it swells and rots, with the limbs dismembered. The worms in the body then feed on it, and it is devoured by tigers, wolves, vultures, owls, and hawks. The foolish people of the world cannot observe correctly, but are attached to affection and cling to it until death. How can there be any wise person who would delight in this?"

尔时，庵婆罗女，颜容端正世界第一。闻佛不久当般涅槃，最后见于毘耶离城，心怀悲懊，涕泣交流，即与五百眷属，严五百乘车，次第出城，往诣佛所。尔时，世尊！遥见彼来，告诸比丘：「庵婆罗女今来诣我，形貌殊绝，举世无双，汝等皆当端心正念，勿生著意。比丘！当观此身，有诸不净，肝、胆、肠、胃、心、肺、脾肾、屎、尿、脓血，充满其中。八万户虫，居在其内。发毛爪齿，薄皮覆肉，九孔常流，无一可乐。又复此身，根本始生，由于不净。此身所可往来之处，皆悉能令不净流溢，虽复饰以雕彩，熏以名香，譬如宝瓶中藏臭秽。又其死时，膖胀腐烂，节节支解，身中有虫，而还食之，又为虎狼鵄枭雕鹫之所吞噬。世人愚痴，不能正观，恋著恩爱，保之至死，横于其中而生贪欲；何有智者，而乐此耶？」

尔时，庵婆罗女到于佛前，头面礼足，以诸供具，而供养佛，衔泪呜咽，而白佛言：「唯愿世尊！住寿住世，不般涅槃，利益世间诸天人民！世尊若定般涅槃者，一切众生，无复奖道，犹如婴儿失于慈母。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to her, "The nature and characteristics of all conditioned phenomena are like this. You should not give rise to sorrow." The World-Honored One then spoke the Dharma for the assembled: "From now on, you should uphold the precepts and not violate them. Those who break the precepts are despised by devas, nāgas, and spirits. Their bad reputation spreads, and people do not like to see them. If they are in a crowd, they alone have no dignity. The good spirits no longer protect them. When they are about to die, their minds are fearful. Even if they have slight goodness, they do not remember it. When they die, they follow their karma and experience the suffering of hell for eons. Only then can they be released, but they will again experience the bodies of hungry ghosts and animals. In this way, they will continue without any chance of liberation. Bhikṣus who uphold the precepts are respected by devas, nāgas, and spirits. Their good reputation spreads and is heard throughout the world. When they are in a large assembly, their dignity and brilliance are radiant. The good spirits always follow and protect them. When they are about to die, their right mindfulness is clear. When they die, they are reborn in a pure place." When the Tathāgata spoke this Dharma, 68,000 koṭis of devas, humans, and the eight classes of beings were free from defilements and attained the purity of the Dharma eye. Sixty bhikṣus exhausted their taints, their minds were liberated, and they attained arahantship.

尔时，世尊而告之言：「一切诸行，性相如是，汝今不应生于悲𢙐。」世尊即便普为来众而说法言：「汝等从今护持禁戒，勿得亏犯。破戒之人，天龙鬼神，所共憎厌！恶声流布，人不憙见，若在众中，独无威德。诸善鬼神，不复守护。临命终时，心识怖惧，设有微善，悉不忆念，死即随业受地狱苦，经历劫数，然后得出，复受饿鬼畜生之身，如是转转无解脱期。比丘持戒之人，天龙鬼神，所共恭敬。美声流布，闻彻世间。处大众中，威德明盛。诸善鬼神，常随守护。临命终时，正念分明，死即生于清净之处。」当于如来说此法时，六万八千那由他天人八部，远尘离垢，得法眼净。六十比丘，漏尽意解，成阿罗汉。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the Licchavis, their wives, and Ambapālī, "I now wish to proceed to the village of Gandhāra. You should each return to your abodes. Know that all conditioned phenomena are impermanent. You should only practice the Dharma I have taught, and do not weep and grieve like infants." The World-Honored One then rose from his seat.

尔时，世尊告诸离车及与其妻，并庵婆罗女：「我今欲进干荼村中，汝等可各还归所止。当知，诸行皆悉无常，但当修行我所说法，勿如婴儿涕泣悲𢙐。」世尊即便从座而起。

At that time, the Licchavis, their wives, and Ambapālī, hearing the Buddha's words, beat their chests and heads, wailing loudly, following the road after the Buddha, unwilling to turn back. Seeing their deep longing, the World-Honored One, unable to comfort them with words, used his spiritual power to transform the river into a deep and steep shore, with swift and turbulent waves. Seeing the Tathāgata and the bhikṣus on the other side of the river, they were even more grief-stricken, fainting and falling to the ground. They spoke to each other in a faint voice, "How did this great river suddenly appear, and yet it is so turbulent and frightening? It must be that the Tathāgata, seeing that we would not give up following him, deliberately created this to cut off our path!" The Licchavis, their wives, and Ambapālī, unable to cross, were even more agitated. They looked up, choked with sobs, and despaired before returning.

时，诸离车及与其妻、庵婆罗女，闻佛此言，搥胸拍头，号咷大叫，缘路随佛不肯旋返。世尊既见恋慕情深，非是言辞所可安慰，即以神力，化作河水，涯岸深绝，波流迅疾。时，诸离车及以眷属、庵婆罗女，既见如来与比丘众，在彼河岸，倍增悲恸，闷绝躃地，而以微声，共相谓言：「是处那忽有此大河，而复乃尔波湍惊急？当是如来见于我等随从不舍，而故作此，绝行道耳！」时，诸离车及以其妻、庵婆罗女，既不得度，心倍踊跃，俛仰哽咽，绝望乃还。

At that time, the Tathāgata arrived at the northern forest of the village of Gandhāra and said to the bhikṣus, "You should know that there are four kinds of dharmas: precepts, concentration, wisdom, and liberation. If one does not hear and know these four dharmas, one will wander in the ocean of birth and death for a long time. If I had not heard and known these four dharmas in the past, I would not have been able to quickly attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

尔时，如来至干荼村北林中住，告诸比丘：「汝等当知，有四种法：一戒、二定、三慧、四解脱，若不闻知此四法者，斯人长夜在生死海。我于往昔，若不闻知此四法者，不能疾得阿耨多罗三藐三菩提。」

Then the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

于是世尊即说偈曰：

If I had not heard these four dharmas for a long time, I
would not have been able to quickly realize the unsurpassed, perfect, true path
You should diligently cultivate these four dharmas, which can sever the suffering of
birth and death and be the supreme field of merit for gods and humans.

「戒定慧解脱，　　我若不久闻，不能疾得证，　　无上正真道。汝等宜精进，　　修习此四法，能断生死苦，　　天人上福田。」

At that time, after the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he extensively explained the meaning of these four dharmas for the bhikṣus. When the Tathāgata was expounding this teaching, 1,200 bhikṣus immediately attained the exhaustion of taints and realized arahantship.

尔时，世尊说此偈已，为诸比丘，分别广说此四法义。当于如来说此法时，千二百比丘，即于诸法，漏尽意解，成阿罗汉。

At that time, the World-Honored One, together with the bhikṣus, rose from his seat and went to the villages of Elephant, Āmra, and Jambū, and finally arrived at the city of Sugandha. Having arrived at the city, he sat down with the bhikṣus surrounding him in front and behind. Then the World-Honored One said to the bhikṣus: "There are four noble truths that should be diligently observed. They are: the truth of suffering, the truth of the origin of suffering, the truth of the cessation of suffering, and the truth of the path. Bhikṣus, the truth of suffering refers to the eight kinds of suffering: the suffering of birth, the suffering of old age, the suffering of sickness, the suffering of death, the suffering of not obtaining what one seeks, the suffering of being with what one dislikes, the suffering of separation from what one loves, and the suffering of the five aggregates. You should know that these eight kinds of suffering, as well as all conditioned phenomena, are truly suffering because they oppress. The truth of the origin of suffering refers to ignorance and craving, which are the fundamental causes of the eight kinds of suffering. You should know that this origin is truly the cause of suffering. The truth of the cessation of suffering refers to the cessation of ignorance and craving, which cuts off the causes of suffering. You should know that this cessation is truly cessation. The truth of the path refers to the eightfold noble path: right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These eight dharmas are truly the noble path. If a person diligently observes these four truths, they will quickly be free from birth and death and reach the place of liberation. You bhikṣus, if you have already realized these truths, you should also diligently explain them to others. After my parinirvāṇa, you should also diligently contemplate and cultivate them." When the Tathāgata was expounding this teaching, 500 bhikṣus attained the exhaustion of taints and realized arahantship. 40,000 gods in the sky attained the purity of the Dharma eye, free from dust and defilements.

尔时，世尊与诸比丘，即从座起，趣于象村、庵婆罗村、阎浮村乃至到于善伽城。到彼城已，与诸比丘，前后围绕] cf.【丽】，在一处坐。于是，世尊告诸比丘：「有四圣谛，当勤观察。一者苦谛、二者集谛、三者灭谛、四者道谛。比丘，苦谛者，所谓八苦：一生苦、二老苦、三病苦、四死苦、五所求不得苦、六怨憎会苦、七爱别离苦、八五受阴苦。汝等当知，此八种苦，及有漏法，以逼迫故，谛实是苦。集谛者，无明及爱，能为八苦而作因本。当知此集，谛是苦因。灭谛者，无明、爱灭，绝于苦因。当知此灭，谛实是灭。道谛者，八正道：一正见、二正念、三正思惟、四正业、五正精进、六正语、七正命、八正定。此八法者，谛是圣道。若人精勤，观此四法，速离生死，到解脱处。汝等比丘，若于此法，已究竟者，亦当精勤为他解说。我若灭后，汝等亦应勤思修习。」当于如来说此法时，五百比丘，漏尽意解，成阿罗汉。虚空诸天，其数四万，于诸法中，远尘离垢，得法眼净。

At that time, the World-Honored One, together with the bhikṣus, rose from his seat and went to the village of Kuśinagara. Having arrived at the village, he sat down with the bhikṣu assembly surrounding him in front and behind, under a tree. At that time, the brāhmaṇas, elders, and householders in the village, having heard that the Buddha had arrived, all rushed to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, and sat to one side. They said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Now you have come to this village with the bhikṣus. Do you have any other purpose?" Then the Tathāgata replied: "Three months from now, I will enter parinirvāṇa. I will travel through the villages and towns, passing through this place." At that time, when the people heard the Buddha's words, they wept sorrowfully, fainted and fell to the ground, raised their hands to beat their heads, and lamented loudly, saying: "Alas! How painful! The eye of the world is extinguished. We will soon lose our guide." They said with tears: "May the World-Honored One please remain for a kalpa or even less than a kalpa." At that time, the World-Honored One replied: "You should not give rise to such sorrow. Why is that? The nature and characteristics of conditioned phenomena are like this. You should abandon feelings of sorrow and listen quietly to my final teaching." Then the people forcibly restrained themselves, lowered their heads, and listened in silence.

尔时，世尊与诸比丘，从座而起，趣鸠娑村。到彼村已，与比丘众，前后围绕，坐一树下。时彼村中，诸婆罗门、长者居士，闻佛至已，皆悉驰竞，来诣佛所，头面礼足，却坐一面，而白佛言：「世尊！今者与诸比丘，故来此村，别有余趣？」于是，如来即答之言：「我却后三月，当般涅槃，从毘耶离城，遍历村邑，次第到此。」尔时，诸人，闻佛此语，悲泣懊𢙐，闷绝躃地，举手拍头，搥胸大叫，唱如是言：「呜呼！苦哉！世间眼灭，我等不久，失所归导。」垂涕白言：「唯愿世尊！住寿一劫，若减一劫。」尔时，世尊而答之言：「汝等不应生此悲𢙐，所以者何？有为之法，性相如是。汝等可舍忧𢙐之情，静心听我最后所说。」于是诸人，强自抑忍，低头默听。

At that time, there was a brāhmaṇa named Fupa Yudidi in the assembly. He was intelligent, wise, learned, and well-versed. The Tathāgata then said to him: "You should know that there are four dharmas that householders should cultivate. First, respectfully serving and supporting one's parents with utmost filial piety; second, always teaching one's wife and children with good Dharma; third, having compassion for servants and knowing their needs; fourth, befriending good friends and avoiding bad people. If you always practice these four dharmas, you will be loved and respected by people in this life, and in future lives, you will always be in good places. Furthermore, Fupa Yudidi, there are four joyful dharmas for householders. First, not owing others' wealth, without shame or embarrassment; second, being extremely wealthy, but stingy and not using it, not providing for one's parents, wife and children, relatives, and dependents, and not making offerings to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas; third, being extremely wealthy, wearing fine clothes, indulging in the best flavors, providing for one's parents, relatives, and dependents, and serving śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas; fourth, not committing evil deeds with body, speech, and mind, being intelligent and wise, and delighting in learning. You should know that although householders may have these four kinds of joy, if they do not owe others debts and are not stingy, this is called the lowest joy; being generous is called the middle joy; not committing evil deeds with body, speech, and mind, being intelligent and wise, and delighting in learning, this is called the supreme joy."

时，彼座中有一婆罗门，名弗波育帝，聪明智慧博闻强记。尔时如来，即告之言：「汝等当知，在家之人，有四种法，宜应修习。一者恭敬父母，尽心孝养；二者恒以善法，训导妻子；三者愍念僮仆，知其有无；四者近善知识，远离恶人。汝等若恒行此四法，现世为人之所爱敬，将来所生，常在善处。复次，弗波育帝，在家之人，有四乐法。一者不负他财，无惭愧色；二者极大巨富，自惜不用，父母、妻子、亲戚、眷属皆不给与，又不供养沙门婆罗门；三者极大巨富，身著丽服，口恣上味，供养父母，亲戚眷属皆悉给与，奉事沙门及婆罗门；四者身、口、意业，竝不为恶，聪明智慧，乐欲多闻。汝等当知，在家之人，虽复有此四种之乐，而不负他债及以悭贪，此法名为最下之乐；好行布施，名为中乐；身、口、意业不造于恶，聪明智慧，乐于多闻，此法名为上胜之乐。」

Fuboyu and the others said to the Buddha again, "We only wish that the World-Honored One and the bhikṣus would accept our offerings tomorrow." At that time, the Tathāgata silently accepted. Fuboyu and the others, knowing that the Buddha had accepted, immediately rose from their seats, paid homage to the Buddha, and withdrew with their retinue. Returning to their homes, they prepared fragrant and delicious food throughout the night. When the time for the meal arrived the next day, they sent a message saying, "We only wish that the World-Honored One would know the time." Then the Tathāgata, surrounded by the bhikṣus, went to their homes and took their seats in order. When Fuboyu saw that the Buddha and the saṃgha were all seated, he rose and served water, personally pouring the delicious food and drink. The other five hundred brāhmaṇas, elders, and householders each brought fine delicacies and also offered them to the Buddha.

尔时，弗波育帝等而白佛言：「世尊！我等从今以中上法，互相开导。」于是，弗波育帝等五百人，即于佛前，受三归依并及五戒。弗波育帝等，重白佛言：「唯愿世尊及比丘僧，明受我供。」于时，如来，默然许之。弗波育帝等，知佛许已，即从座起，与其来众，礼佛而退。还到其舍，通夕办好香美饮食，既至明日，食时将到，遣信白言：「唯愿世尊！自知其时。」于是，如来与比丘僧，前后围绕，往诣其舍，次第而坐。弗波育帝，见佛及僧悉安坐已，便起行水，手自斟酌，诸美饮食。余婆罗门长者居士，有五百人，各賷美馔，亦在其舍，共供养佛。

At that time, some bhikṣus were not well-composed in their bodily deportment during the meal. When the brāhmaṇas, elders, and householders saw this, they were displeased. At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing the thoughts of the assembly, universally addressed them, saying, "You should know that the Tathāgata's true Dharma is vast and deep like the ocean, immeasurable. Moreover, the great ocean has beings with extremely large bodies, some ten thousand six hundred yojanas long, some eight thousand yojanas long, some four thousand yojanas long, some one thousand yojanas long, some half an inch long, down to the tiniest particles. The Dharma ocean of the Tathāgata is also like this. Among them are those who have attained arahantship, perfected the three insights and six supernatural powers, and have great majestic virtue and blessings among gods and humans; among them are also those who have attained the state of non-returner, once-returner, and stream-enterer, and those who have attained the stages leading to the four fruits, down to those who are ordinary beings who have not yet attained the benefits of the Dharma. Therefore, do not give rise to obstructed minds in the Dharma ocean."

时，诸比丘当于食上，有不善摄身威仪者，诸婆罗门长者居士，既见之已，心不欢喜。尔时，世尊知众人心，而普告言：「汝等当知，如来正法，深旷如海，不可测量，又复大海，有诸众生，身体极大，长万六千逾阇那、或复身长八千逾阇那、或复身长四千逾阇那、或复身长千逾阇那、或复身长一寸半寸，乃至极微，如来法海，亦复如是。其中或有得阿罗汉，具足三明及以六通，有大威德福天人者；其中亦有得阿那含者、斯陀含者、须陀洹者，亦复有得四果向者，乃至亦有凡夫之人未得法利者；是故汝勿于法海中而生碍心。」

Then the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

于是世尊而说偈言：

All rivers and streams flow into the great ocean, and if one offers food
to the Buddha and the Sangha, the merit returns to oneself in the same way.

「一切众川流，　　皆悉归大海，若饭佛及僧，　　福归己亦然。」

At that time, after the Tathāgata had spoken this verse, he further expounded various teachings to the people. At that time, the five hundred people led by Fupo Yudidi attained the purity of the Dharma eye, free from dust and defilements, within the teachings. At that time, the World-Honored One rose from his seat with the assembly of bhikṣus and proceeded further to the city of Papā. The five hundred people led by Fupo Yudidi sorrowfully wept and saw the Tathāgata off. They looked back longingly and only returned when they lost sight of him.

尔时，如来说此偈已，又为众人，说种种法。于时，弗波育帝等五百人，于诸法中，远尘离垢] cf.【丽】，得法眼净。尔时，世尊与比丘僧，从座而起，更复前行，趣波波城。弗波育帝等五百人，悲号啼泣，奉送如来，徘徊顾慕，绝望乃返。

At that time, the World-Honored One arrived at that city. In that city, there was a skilled artisan named Cunda. His garden was extremely quiet. The Tathāgata then went with the bhikṣus surrounding him in front and back, and stayed in that garden. At that time, Cunda heard that the Buddha and the Sangha had come to his garden. He was overjoyed and could not contain himself. Together with his peers, he went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stepped back to one side, and said to the Buddha, "I do not know, World-Honored One! What is the reason for coming here? Is there some other destination?" At that time, the World-Honored One replied, "The reason I have come here now is that I will soon enter parinirvāṇa. That is why I have come to see you for the last time." At that time, Cunda and his peers, upon hearing the Buddha's words, were greatly saddened and fainted on the ground. After a long while, they spoke in a faint voice to the Buddha, saying, "World-Honored One! Now you are abandoning all sentient beings, are you not compassionate? How can you wish to enter parinirvāṇa? I only pray that the World-Honored One will remain alive for a kalpa, or even less than a kalpa!" They then beat their heads and chests, crying out, "Alas! How painful! The eye of the world is extinguished! From now on, all sentient beings will be submerged in the ocean of birth and death, without any hope of escape. Why is this so? Because the unsurpassed guide has entered parinirvāṇa."

尔时，世尊既至彼城。彼城之中，有工巧子，名曰淳陀，其人有园，极为闲静；如来即便与诸比丘，前后围绕，往住彼园。是时淳陀，闻佛及僧来其园中，欢喜踊跃，不能自胜，与其同类，俱诣佛所。头面礼足，却住一面，而白佛言：「不审世尊！何缘来此？有他趣耶？」尔时，世尊即答之言：「我今所以来至此者，不久应当入般涅槃，是以故来，最后相见。」是时，淳陀及其同类，闻佛此语，心大悲𢙐闷绝于地，良久微声而白佛言：「世尊今者舍诸众生，不慈念耶！云何便欲入般涅槃？唯愿世尊！住寿一劫！若减一劫！」即又拍头搥胸大叫，作如是言：「呜呼！苦哉！世间眼灭！一切众生，从今以后，没生死海，未有出期。所以者何？无上导师般涅槃故。」

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Cunda, "You should not give rise to suffering and distress now. All conditioned phenomena are like this, all subject to impermanence and change. The meeting and affection of loved ones will inevitably be separated. Therefore, you should not give rise to sorrow and distress now."

尔时，世尊告淳陀言：「汝今不应生苦𢙐也。一切诸行法皆如是，悉为无常之所迁变，合会恩爱，必有别离。是故汝今勿生忧𢙐。」

At that time, Cunda said to the Buddha, "I now also know that all conditioned phenomena are impermanent, and the meeting and affection of loved ones will all be separated. However, the unsurpassed Honored One is about to enter parinirvāṇa. How can I not be sorrowful and distressed now?"

尔时，淳陀即白佛言：「我今亦知诸行无常，合会恩爱，皆悉别离。然无上尊，当般涅槃，我今云何而不悲𢙐！」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke various teachings for Cunda. Having heard them, his sorrow and distress subsided a little. He then rose from his seat, adjusted his dignified demeanor, bared his right shoulder, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and said, "World-Honored One, please accept my meager offerings tomorrow." The World-Honored One then silently consented. At that time, Cunda, knowing the Buddha's consent, took leave and departed. Cunda returned home and prepared many delicious foods throughout the night. At the time of the meal the next day, he sent a message to the Buddha, saying, "May the World-Honored One know the time." Thereupon, the Tathāgata, surrounded by the bhikṣus, went to his house and took his seat in order. At that time, Cunda, seeing the Buddha seated, immediately served water and personally offered various fine dishes. The World-Honored One and the saṃgha, having finished their meal and washed their bowls, returned to their original seats, and Cunda also took his seat.

尔时，世尊即为淳陀，说种种法。淳陀闻已，忧悲小歇，便从座起，整身威仪，偏袒右肩，顶礼佛足白言：「世尊唯愿，明日受我薄供。」世尊即便默然许之。尔时，淳陀知佛许已，礼足而退淳陀还舍，通夕办于多美饮食。至明食时，遣信白佛：「唯愿世尊！自知其时。」于是，如来与诸比丘，前后围绕，往诣其舍，次第就坐。是时，淳陀见佛坐已，即便行水，手自斟酌，下诸精馔。世尊及僧，食竟洗钵，还归本坐，淳陀亦坐。

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Cunda, "You have now created an extraordinary merit. The last meal you offered to the Buddha and the bhikṣus will have inexhaustible rewards. No sentient being can match the merit you have planted. You should rejoice in your heart. I have now accepted your invitation for the last time and will not accept any other offerings."

尔时，世尊告淳陀言：「汝今已作希有之福，最后供饭佛比丘僧，如此果报，无有穷尽。一切众生，所种诸福，无有能得等于汝者。宜应自生欣庆之心，我今最后受汝请讫，更不复受他余供饭。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊即说偈言：

You have now established an extraordinary merit. The
last meal you offered to the Buddha and
the bhikṣus will have merit that increases daily
and will never be exhausted. You should now
deeply rejoice in your heart. No one's merit
can match yours.

「汝今已建立，　　希有之功德，最后得供饭，　　佛及比丘僧，功德日增长，　　永无穷竭时。汝今宜自应，　　深生欣庆心，一切所造福，　　无有等汝者。」

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, said to Ānanda: "My body is in pain now. I wish to go quickly to the city of Kuśinagara." At that time, Ānanda, together with the bhikṣus and Cunda, heard the Buddha's words and gave rise to great pain and sorrow, weeping and lamenting, unable to control themselves. Then the World-Honored One rose from his seat, surrounded by the bhikṣus in front and behind, and headed towards the city. At that time, Cunda also followed the Tathāgata with his retinue. The World-Honored One stopped under a tree on the way and said to Ānanda: "I am now suffering from severe abdominal pain." He then took Ānanda not far from the tree and bled. Returning under the tree, he ordered Ānanda: "You should fold my saṃghāṭī robe four times and spread it on the ground. I wish to sit and rest. I cannot go any further." Ānanda received the order. The World-Honored One then sat and rested under the tree. He also told Ānanda: "I am thirsty now. You should go to the Kakutstha River and fetch some clean water." Ānanda replied: "Just now, five hundred merchant carts passed by the river. The water must be turbid. I am afraid it is not drinkable." In this way, he ordered Ānanda three times. Ānanda then took his bowl and left. When he reached the river, he saw the water was clear. He was greatly frightened, his body hair standing on end. He thought to himself: "Just now, I saw five hundred merchant carts passing through this water. I thought it was still turbid and did not say it was clear. This caused me to repeatedly disobey the Tathāgata's order." He then took the water back and offered it, saying: "It is truly amazing, World-Honored One! Just now, I saw five hundred merchant carts passing through the river, obstructing the front and back. For ten days, it still did not become clear. The World-Honored One's spiritual power caused it to become clear in a short while." The World-Honored One then accepted the water and drank it.

尔时，世尊说此偈已，即语阿难：「我今身痛，欲疾往彼鸠尸那城。」尔时，阿难与诸比丘并及淳陀，闻佛此语，生大苦痛，号泣流连，不能自胜。于是，世尊即从座起，与诸比丘前后围绕，趣向彼城。尔时，淳陀亦与眷属随从如来，世尊中路止一树下，语阿难言：「我于今者，极患腹痛。」即将阿难，去树不远，而便下血。既还树下，而勅阿难：「汝可取我僧伽梨衣，四叠敷地，我欲坐息，不堪复前。」阿难受勅，世尊即便坐息树下，又告阿难：「我今患渴，汝可往至迦屈嗟河，取净水来。」阿难答言：「向有商人，五百乘车，从河而过，其水必浊，恐不堪饮。」如是再三，勅于阿难，阿难然后持钵而去。既到河上，见水澄清，心大怖惧，身毛皆竖，而自念言：「我于向者，见诸商人，五百乘车，经此水过，意谓犹浊，不言便清，致令屡逆如来之勅。」即持水归而以供奉，作如是言：「甚奇世尊！向见商人，五百乘车，从河而度妨于前后，十日之中犹未应清，世尊神力，俄尔之顷，而便澄洁。」世尊即便受水饮之。

At that time, there was a son of a full-fledged sage named Pukkasati, who was a disciple of the sage Karala. He went from Kuśinagara to the city of Pāpā. Suddenly, on the way, he saw the Tathāgata sitting and resting under a tree. He joined his palms, greeted him, and sat to one side. He said to the Buddha: "The practice of leaving home, the practice of sitting in meditation is the foremost. It tames the sense faculties and keeps the mind from being disturbed. It is concentrated and tranquil, and nothing can frighten it. Why is this so? I remember in the past, I followed my teacher, the sage Karala, on the road. We were tired and stopped to rest under a tree. My teacher then sat in meditation. At that time, there were merchants who passed by in fifty carts. My teacher was still silent, his body unmoving. After a long while, he emerged from meditation. I then went to him and said: 'When you were sitting in meditation just now, there were merchants who passed by in fifty carts, their sound like thunder. I wonder if you saw them?' He replied: 'I did not see anything.' I further asked: 'Did you hear their sound?' He also replied: 'I did not hear it.' I then said: 'The dust and dirt on your robe now is because of their passing by.' At that time, I deeply gave rise to wonder and knew that the practice of sitting in meditation is extremely worthy of respect. It well restrains the sense faculties and nothing can disturb it."

尔时，有一满罗仙人之子，名弗迦娑，是彼迦兰仙人弟子。从鸠尸那，诣波波城，忽于中路，而见如来坐息树下，合掌问讯却坐一面，而白佛言：「夫出家法，坐禅之业最为第一，调伏情根，使心不乱，专精寂静，莫能惊恐。所以者何？忆念往昔随从我师迦兰仙人，行于道路，既患疲乏，近于路侧，止息树下，我师即便坐禅思惟。当尔之时，有诸商人，乘五十乘车，从前而过，我师尔时犹故寂默，身不动摇，如是良久，方从禅起。我即便往而白师言：『尊向在此坐禅之时，有诸商人，五十乘车，经前而过，声如雷震，不审尊向为见之不？』师答我言：『都无所见。』又复问言：『闻其声不？』亦答：『不闻。』即复白言：『尊今衣上所以有此尘土污者，是彼车过故致尔耳。』我于尔时，深生奇特，知坐禅法极可敬重。善摄情根无能乱者。」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied to Pukkasati: "What you said just now is not extraordinary. Why is this so? If there is someone who is not deeply asleep and also not in the state of cessation, sitting in meditation with a focused mind, and five hundred carts pass by in front of him, if he does not perceive or hear it, then this can be called extraordinary. Furthermore, Pukkasati, this is not yet considered very extraordinary. If there is someone who is sitting in meditation with right mindfulness, and there is a heavenly thunderbolt, lightning, and thunder, at that time, there were two brothers who were plowing. They were frightened by the sound and died instantly. There were also four oxen that also collapsed. But the person sitting in meditation did not perceive or hear it. Can this be called extraordinary?" Pukkasati said: "Five hundred carts passing by in front without perceiving or hearing it is already extraordinary. How much more so if there is a thunderbolt, lightning, and thunder shaking the earth, and yet not hearing or perceiving it, it is extremely rare!"

尔时，世尊答弗迦娑：「汝向所说，非为奇特。所以者何？若复有人，非是熟眠，亦复不入于灭尽定，端心坐禅，五百乘车从其前过，此人于时不觉不闻，如是乃可名为奇特。复次，弗迦娑，斯亦未足为大奇特，若复有人，正念坐禅，遇天霹雳雷电震曜。时，有耕者兄弟二人，闻此惊怖，应声而死，又有四牛，亦皆顿绝；而坐禅者，不觉不闻。斯可得名为奇特不？」弗迦娑言：「五百乘车，从前而过，不觉不闻，已为奇特，况复霹雳震曜动地，而不闻觉，极为希有！」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Pukkasati: "In the past, I was in the village of Achimara, sitting in meditation under a tree. At that time, there were merchants with five hundred carts passing in front of me, but I did not perceive or hear it while in meditation. After the merchants had passed for a long time, I emerged from meditation. Then, the merchants saw me rising from afar, and they all came rushing over. Seeing that dust and dirt had soiled my robe, they immediately brushed it off and asked me: 'We just passed by here with five hundred carts. Did the World-Honored One see it?' I then replied: 'I did not see it.' They further asked: 'The World-Honored One may have closed his eyes and not seen, but did he hear the sound?' I again replied: 'I also did not hear the sound.' The merchants further asked: 'Was the World-Honored One asleep? Or were you in the state of cessation?' I again replied: 'I was not asleep just now, nor was I in cessation. I was just in meditation, so I did not hear or see.' When those merchants heard my words, they gave rise to great wonder and praised it as unprecedented. They said: 'The power of sitting in meditation can be like this.' I then spoke various teachings for them. At that time, the merchants all removed dust and defilements from the Dharma and attained the purity of the Dharma eye.

尔时，世尊告弗迦娑：「我于往昔，在阿车摩村，于一树下端坐思惟。时，有商人五百乘车，经我前过，而我禅思不觉不闻。诸商人等，经过良久，我方出定。时，彼商人遥见我起，皆悉竞来，见我身上尘坌污衣，即便拂之，而问我言：『我等向者，五百乘车从此而过，世尊见不？』即便答言：『我不见也。』彼复问言：『世尊自可闭目不视，为闻声不？』我又答言：『亦不闻声。』商人又问：『世尊为眠？为是入于灭尽定耶？』我又答言：『我向不眠，亦非入定。但在禅思，故无闻见。』彼诸商人闻我此言，极生奇特叹未曾有，而作是言：『坐禅之力，乃能如此。』我即为其说种种法。时，彼商众悉于诸法，远尘离垢得法眼净。

Furthermore, Pukkasati, in the past, I was sitting alone in a field near that village, silently in meditation. Before long, there was suddenly a great thunderclap, with lightning, wind, and rain shaking the earth. At that time, there were two brothers plowing. Suddenly hearing this sound, they died in fear together. There were also four oxen that also collapsed. Then, the villagers heard that the two brothers who were plowing had died in fear. Either their parents, wives, or acquaintances, the whole village came together, weeping to see them. At that time, I had just emerged from meditation and saw the muddy ground and the crowd of people gathered, crying. One person came and I asked: 'Why are the people gathered here, crying?' That person replied: 'World-Honored One, did you not hear the sound of thunder, lightning, and thunderclap just now? In our village, two brothers were plowing here together, and they were both killed by the thunderclap at the same time, along with the four oxen. They also died together. How is it that the World-Honored One did not perceive it? Was the Tathāgata asleep just now? Or were you in the state of cessation?' I then replied: 'I was not asleep just now, nor was I in cessation. I was just sitting in meditation, so I did not hear it.' At that time, that person heard the Buddha's words and gave rise to great wonder and praised it as unprecedented. He thought to himself: 'Sitting in meditation can have such power.' I then spoke various teachings for him. After hearing the teachings, he removed dust and defilements from the Dharma and attained the purity of the Dharma eye.

「复次，弗迦娑！我于往日，在彼村侧田间独坐，寂默禅思。不久忽然天大霹雳，雷电风雨震动天地。时，有耕者兄弟二人，忽闻此声，同共怖死；又有四牛，亦复顿绝。时，彼村人闻有耕者二人怖死，或是父母、妻子、知识，合村相随，涕泣来看。我于尔时，方从禅觉，见地泥水，又有众人，集聚号哭。有一人来，我即问言：『何故人众聚此悲泣。』彼人答言：『世尊！向者不觉雷电霹雳声耶？我村之中，兄弟二人在此而耕，同时为于霹雳所杀，及以四牛亦皆俱死，云何世尊而不觉知？如来向者为是得眠？为是入于灭尽定耶？』即答之言：『我向不眠，亦不入定，端寂坐禅，故不闻耳。』是时，彼人闻佛此语，深生奇特叹未曾有，心自念言：『坐禅乃有如此之力。』我即为其种种说法。既闻法已，于诸法中，远尘离垢得法眼净。」

At that time, Pukkasati heard the Buddha's words and gave rise to a rare mind. He said to the Buddha: "In the past, when I saw my teacher sitting in meditation, fifty carts passed by without him hearing. I considered it wonderful. Now, the Tathāgata speaks of these two things, which are hundreds of thousands of times incomparable. The power of the Tathāgata's meditation is inconceivable." He then took refuge in the Buddha. The Tathāgata spoke various wonderful teachings for him. After hearing the teachings, his mind opened and he understood, removing dust and defilements from the Dharma and attaining the purity of the Dharma eye. He then said to his attendant: "You should take my two pieces of golden cloth and bring them. I wish to offer them to the Buddha." The attendant obeyed the order and brought them. At that time, Pukkasati held the cloth in his hands, knelt before the Buddha, and said: "I now offer this to the World-Honored One. I only pray that you will accept it out of compassion."

时，弗迦娑闻佛此言，生希有心，而白佛言：「本见我师坐禅之时，五十车过，而不闻知，谓为奇特。今者如来说此二事，百千万倍不可为比，如来禅力不可思议。」即便从佛受三归依。如来为说种种妙法，其闻法已，心开意悟，远尘离垢得法眼净，即语侍人：「汝可取我金色劫贝二张持来，我欲上佛。」侍人奉勅，即取将来。时，弗迦娑手执劫贝，长跪佛前而作是言：「我今以此奉上世尊，唯愿哀愍，即赐纳受。」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied to Pukkasati: "I now accept one piece for you, and you can give one piece to Ānanda. Why is that? Ānanda has been serving by my side day and night, and today he has also looked after me while I was sick. If there are benefactors who give to the sick and those who look after the sick, this is called a great fulfillment of giving." At that time, Pukkasati heard the Buddha's words and joyfully leaped up. He then placed one piece at the Buddha's feet and took another piece to where Ānanda was. He knelt and said: "I now offer this to the Venerable One. I only pray that you will accept it." Ānanda replied: "Excellent! Excellent! You are now able to believe the words of the Teacher of gods and humans, which will cause you to be peacefully and happily for a long time. I accept it for you." Then, Pukkasati returned to where the Buddha was. The Tathāgata then spoke various teachings for him. After hearing the teachings, he attained the fruit of the non-returner. At that time, Pukkasati again said to the Buddha: "I now wish to go forth in the Buddha's teachings." The Buddha immediately called out: "Welcome, bhikṣu!" His beard and hair fell off by themselves, and the kasaya robe was put on his body, becoming a śramaṇa. He attained arahantship.

尔时，世尊答弗迦娑：「我今为汝受取一张，可以一张施于阿难。所以者何？阿难日夜，亲侍我侧，且又今日，看我疾病。若有施主，施于病人及看病者，斯则名为满足大施。」时，弗迦娑闻佛此语，欢喜踊跃，即以一张置佛足下，又持一张，至阿难所，长跪白言：「我今以此奉施尊者，唯愿纳受。」阿难答言：「善哉！善哉！汝今能信天人师言，令汝长夜永得安乐，我为汝受。」于是，弗迦娑还至佛所，如来即复为说诸法，其闻法已，得阿那含果。时，弗迦娑复白佛言：「我今欲于佛法出家。」佛即唤言：「善来比丘。」须发自落，袈裟著身，即成沙门，得阿罗汉。

At that time, the Tathāgata emitted various lights from his mouth, blue, yellow, red, white, and crystal-colored. Then Ānanda bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, knelt with his palms joined, and said to the Buddha: "I do not know, World-Honored One, what causes and conditions are there for this auspicious sign to appear?"

尔时，如来从其面门，放种种光，青黄赤白，颇梨红色。于是阿难顶礼佛足，长跪叉手，而白佛言：「不审世尊！有何因缘，而现此瑞？」

The Buddha replied: "Ānanda! You should know that I have two times when I emit great light. One is when I was about to attain Buddhahood under the Bodhi tree, and the other is when I was about to enter parinirvāṇa. Ānanda! Do you know? When I attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and entered parinirvāṇa at the end of the night, it was also like this. You should know that now, at the end of the night, between the twin śāla trees by the Hiraṇyavatī River in the city of Kuśinagara, I will enter parinirvāṇa." After saying this, the assembly of bhikṣus and the devas in the sky wept and cried out in grief.

佛即答言：「阿难！当知我有二时，放大光明。一者在菩提树欲成佛时放大光明，二者欲般涅槃放大光明。阿难！知不？我成阿耨多罗三藐三菩提，尽于夜分般涅槃时，亦复如是。汝今当知，我于今者，后夜分尽，在鸠尸那城力士生地熙连河侧娑罗双树间，入般涅槃。」说此语已，诸比丘众虚空诸天，悲号啼泣不能自胜。

At that time, the World-Honored One, together with the assembly of bhikṣus, arrived at the Kakutthā River. The World-Honored One then entered the river to bathe. After bathing, he sat down by the river with the assembly of bhikṣus. At that time, Cunda blamed himself, thinking: "Because the World-Honored One accepted my offering of food, he suffered from abdominal pain and wanted to enter parinirvāṇa." At that time, the World-Honored One knew Cunda's thoughts and told Ānanda: "You should know that all sentient beings should not blame themselves, saying: 'The Tathāgata accepted my offering of food, causing him to suffer from illness and enter parinirvāṇa.' Why is that? When the Tathāgata appears in the world, there are two kinds of people who attain the highest merit. One is when he comes to make offerings when about to attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi; the other is when he makes the last offering of food when about to enter parinirvāṇa. The merit of these two people is exactly the same, without any difference. The fruits they attain are immeasurable. Such two offerings are difficult to encounter, like the udumbara flower that appears only occasionally." At that time, the World-Honored One told Cunda: "Your mind is now thinking like this. You should not blame yourself in this way. You have already attained the unsurpassed and difficult-to-obtain treasure. You should be joyful. The name of the Buddha is difficult to hear even in hundreds of thousands of koṭis of kalpas. Although one may hear the name, it is difficult to see the Buddha. Although one may see the Buddha, it is difficult to make offerings. Although one may make offerings, it is also very difficult to be in these two offerings. You have now accomplished this. Before long, you will attain eloquence, wisdom, physical strength, and longevity." At that time, Cunda, hearing these words of the Buddha, was overjoyed and said to the Buddha: "Excellent! World-Honored One! I have now attained such great benefits.

尔时，世尊与比丘众，到迦屈蹉河，世尊即便入河洗浴。洗浴讫已，共比丘僧，坐于河侧。尔时，淳陀心自咎责：「世尊因受我之供饭，而患腹痛，欲般涅槃。」尔时，世尊知淳陀心，告阿难言：「汝今当知，一切众生，勿自责言：『如来因受我之供饭，致使身患而般涅槃。』所以者何？如来出世，有二种人，获福最上。一者欲成阿耨多罗三藐三菩提时，而来奉施；二者如来临欲般涅槃时，最后供饭。此二人福正等无异，所获果报不可称计，如此二施，难可值遇，如优昙钵花时时乃有。」尔时，世尊即告淳陀：「汝今心意正有此念，不应自生如此悔责，已获无上难得之宝，宜应自生庆幸之情。百千万劫，佛名难闻。虽得闻名，见佛又难。虽得见佛，供养又难。虽得供养，在此二施，亦又甚难。汝今已果，不久当获辩才智慧色力寿命。」尔时，淳陀闻佛此语，心生欢喜不能自胜，而白佛言：「快哉！世尊！我今已得如此大利。」

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，世尊而说偈言：

The one who gives obtains blessings, the compassionate have no
enemies, the virtuous eliminate evil, the detached have no afflictions
If one practices such practices, one will not be far from parinirvāṇa.

「布施者获福，　　慈心者无怨，为善者消恶，　　离欲者无恼。若行如此行，　　不久般涅槃。」

At that time, after the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told Cunda, "You should now widely proclaim the merit of your final offering to others, so that those who hear it will attain peace for a long time."

尔时，世尊说此偈已，告淳陀言：「汝今应以最后施福，广为人说，令得闻者长夜获安。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "I now wish to proceed to the city of Kuśinagara, the birthplace of the wrestlers, to the side of the Hiraṇyavatī River, between the twin śāla trees." Ānanda said, "Yes, World-Honored One." Then the Tathāgata, surrounded by the bhikṣus in front and behind, proceeded on the road. Having crossed the Hiraṇyavatī River, he stayed outside the śāla forest in the birthplace of the wrestlers in the city of Kuśinagara. He told Ānanda, "You should go to the śāla forest and see the twin trees standing alone. Sweep and clean the area beneath them, arrange the rope bed, with the head facing north. My body is extremely tired and exhausted." At that time, Ānanda and the bhikṣus, hearing these words of the Buddha, were even more overcome with grief. Ānanda wept and respectfully obeyed the order. He went to the tree, swept and arranged it according to the proper way, and returned to report, "The sweeping and arrangement are all done." At that time, the World-Honored One, together with the bhikṣus, entered the śāla forest, went to the twin trees, laid down on his right side, with his feet stacked, like a lion lying down, with a focused mind and right mindfulness.

尔时，世尊告阿难言：「我今欲进鸠尸那城力士生地熙连河侧娑罗双树间。」阿难白言：「唯然！世尊。」于是，如来与诸比丘，前后围绕，而便进路，渡熙连河，住鸠尸那城力士生地娑罗林外，语阿难言：「汝可往至娑罗林中见有双树，孤在一处洒扫其下，使令清净，安处绳床，令头北首，我今身体极苦疲极。」尔时阿难及诸比丘，闻佛此语，倍增悲绝。阿难流泪奉勅而去，至彼树下洒扫敷施，皆悉如法，还归白言：「洒扫敷施，皆悉已毕。」尔时，世尊与诸比丘，入娑罗林，至双树下，右脇著床，累足而卧，如师子眠，端心正念。

At that time, the twin trees suddenly produced flowers, which fell upon the Tathāgata. The World-Honored One then asked Ānanda, "Do you see those trees producing flowers out of season to make offerings to me?" Ānanda replied, "Yes, I see them." At that time, the devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings in the sky rained down various wonderful flowers, maṇḍala flowers, mahāmaṇḍala flowers, mañjūṣaka flowers, mahāmañjūṣaka flowers, and scattered them upon the Buddha. They also scattered the incense of ox-head sandalwood and other fragrances, and played heavenly music, singing praises. The Buddha told Ānanda, "Do you see the devas and the eight classes of beings in the sky making offerings to me?" Ānanda said, "Yes, I see them." The World-Honored One again told Ānanda, "Those who wish to make offerings to me and repay their kindness should not necessarily do so with incense, flowers, music, and so forth. Upholding the precepts purely, reading and reciting the scriptures, contemplating the profound and wonderful meaning of the Dharma - this is called making offerings to me.

尔时，双树忽然生花，堕如来上，世尊即便问阿难言：「汝见彼树非时生花供养我不？」阿难答言：「唯然，见之。」尔时，诸天龙神八部，于虚空中，雨众妙花、曼陀罗花、摩诃曼陀罗花、曼殊沙花、摩诃曼殊沙花，而散佛上，又散牛头栴檀等香，作天伎乐、歌呗赞叹。佛告阿难：「汝见虚空诸天八部供养我不？」阿难白言：「唯然，已见。」世尊又复告阿难言：「欲供养我报于恩者，不必以此香花伎乐。净持禁戒、读诵经典、思惟诸法深妙之义，斯则名为供养我也。」

At that time, there was a bhikṣu named Upamāna. Before the Tathāgata had taken Ānanda as his attendant, he had always served and attended to the Tathāgata. When Upamāna saw the Tathāgata lying down between the twin trees, he was greatly distressed and stood before the Buddha. At that time, the World-Honored One told him, "You should not stand before me now." Upamāna then stepped aside. At that time, Ānanda had a doubt in his mind: "I have served the Buddha for many years, but I have never seen the Buddha speak like this before. Why does he not allow me to stand before him now? The Tathāgata will soon enter parinirvāṇa, and yet he does not allow me to weep before him." Then Ānanda bowed at the Buddha's feet, knelt with his palms joined, and said, "World-Honored One! Since I have served the Buddha until now, I have often stood before the World-Honored One, but I have never heard you tell me to step back. Why do you now tell Upamāna to step aside?"

尔时，有一比丘，名优波摩那，如来昔日未取阿难为侍者时，其恒执事看视如来。时，优波摩那既见如来卧双树下，心大苦𢙐，在佛前立。尔时，世尊而告之言：「汝今不须当我前倚。」优波摩那即却一面。尔时，阿难心生疑念：「我侍佛来，经历年载，未曾见佛作如此语。今日何故不听前立？如来今者不久便当入般涅槃，而复不听在前悲泣。」于是阿难即礼佛足，长跪叉手白言：「世尊！我从昔来侍佛至今，数数在于世尊前立，而未曾闻令我却退，今者何故语优波摩那使避前耶？」

The Buddha said, "Ānanda! The devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings heard that I was lying on my right side between the twin śāla trees, and they all came to look at me. They piled up from the sky to the ground, filling the four directions, each thirty-two yojanas. This bhikṣu Upamāna stood before me. The devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings gave rise to an unhappy mind and thought like this: 'The Tathāgata is now between the twin trees, and he will soon enter parinirvāṇa. When we are looking at him for the last time, this bhikṣu is standing before the Buddha.' For this reason, I told him to step aside. Does Ānanda know? Now these eight classes of beings, some are weeping uncontrollably, some are distressed and confused, some are pulling out their hair with their hands, some are tearing at their adornments, all crying out in the same voice, 'The Tathāgata is entering parinirvāṇa so quickly! The Tathāgata's appearance in the world is difficult to encounter, like the udumbara flower appearing from time to time, and now he will soon enter parinirvāṇa. Alas! The eyes of the world are extinguished. From now on, who will be our guide?' The devas free from desire all lament, 'Alas! The world is extremely impermanent! There is no one born who does not return to extinction.' And those devas say to each other, 'In the past, the World-Honored One was sometimes in the city of Vaiśālī, sometimes in Rājagṛha, sometimes in Śrāvastī and other places. After the summer retreat, the bhikṣus came from all directions to pay their respects to the World-Honored One. Because of this, we were able to see the bhikṣus on the road, pay homage and make offerings, and listen to the Dharma, gaining long-lasting benefits. Now that the World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa, the bhikṣus will no longer come to pay their respects after the summer retreat. We will no longer be able to see the bhikṣus on the road, pay homage and make offerings, and listen to the Dharma. From now on, we will forever lose these benefits.'"

佛言：「阿难！诸天龙神八部之众，闻我在于娑罗双树右胁] cf.【丽】而卧，皆悉竞来瞻视于我。从虚空中，累至于地，四面充满，各三十二逾阇那。此优波摩那比丘，当我前立，天龙八部生不喜心，作如是念：『如来今者在双树间，不久便当入般涅槃，我等最后瞻视之时，而此比丘，当佛前立。』以是因缘故令之却。阿难知不？今此八部，或有悲泣不能自胜、或有懊𢙐迷闷欲绝、或有以手自拔头发、或有牵绝严身具者，悉皆同声唱如是言：『如来今者入般涅槃，何其速哉！如来出世难可值遇，如优昙钵花时时乃现，而今不久入般涅槃。呜呼！苦哉！世间眼灭，我等从今谁为归导。』离欲诸天皆悉叹言：『呜呼世间极为无常！无有受生不归灭者。』又彼诸天，共相谓言：『世尊昔日或在毘耶离城、或在王舍城、或在舍卫国并及余处，安居讫已，诸比丘众从四方来，问讯世尊。我等因此，得于路侧见诸比丘，礼拜供养，听受经法，长获福利。世尊今者既般涅槃，诸比丘僧，安居竟已，无复问讯。游行处所，我等不复得于路侧见诸比丘，礼拜供养，听受经法，从今永失如此福利。』」

At that time, the Tathāgata told Ānanda, "If bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās can deliberately give rise to the aspiration to go to my four places after my parinirvāṇa, the merit they will attain will be immeasurable. Wherever they are reborn, they will always be among humans and devas, experiencing the blissful karmic retribution without end. What are the four? First, the place where the Tathāgata was born as a bodhisattva in the country of Kapilavastu in the Lumbinī Garden; second, the place in the country of Magadha where I first sat under the bodhi tree and attained anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi; third, the place in the country of Vārāṇasī where I turned the wheel of the Dharma in the Deer Park where the sages dwell; fourth, the place between the twin śāla trees by the Nairañjanā River in the country of Kuśinagara where I entered parinirvāṇa. These are the four places. If bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and other people, including the followers of non-Buddhist paths, give rise to the aspiration to go there to pay homage, the merit they will attain is all as explained above."

尔时如来告阿难言：「若比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷，于我灭后，能故发心，往我四处，所获功德不可称计，所生之处，常在人天，受乐果报，无有穷尽。何等为四？一者如来为菩萨时，在迦比罗斾兜国蓝毘尼园所生之处；二者于摩竭提国，我初坐于菩提树下，得成阿耨多罗三藐三菩提处；三者波罗［木＊奈］国鹿野苑中仙人所住转法轮处；四者鸠尸那国力士生地熙连河侧娑罗林中双树之间般涅槃处，是为四处。若比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷，并及余人外道徒众，发心欲往到彼礼拜，所获功德，悉如上说。」

At that time, Ānanda heard the Buddha's words and said, "World-Honored One! From now on, I shall proclaim this to the four groups of followers, so that they may know these four places. If they go to pay homage, the merit will be like this." At that time, Ānanda further said to the Buddha, "If there are good-hearted upāsikās who uphold the precepts well, enjoy listening to the Dharma, and wish to see bhikṣus, what should we do from now on?" The Buddha said, "From now on, do not meet with them." Ānanda said, "If we happen to meet them, what should we do?" The Buddha said, "Do not speak with them." Ānanda said, "If we do not speak with them, what should we do if they ask questions and wish to hear the Dharma?" The Buddha said, "You should teach the Dharma, but you should well restrain your body, speech, and mind." At that time, Ānanda said to the Buddha, "We shall uphold and practice this from now on."

尔时，阿难闻佛此语，白言：「世尊！我从今者当普宣告诸四部众知此四处，若往礼拜，功德如是。」尔时，阿难复白佛言：「若有善心诸优婆夷，善持戒行，乐听经法，欲见比丘，我等从今当云何耶？」佛言：「汝等从今勿与相见。」阿难言：「若脱遇会与之相逢，当复云何？」佛言：「勿与共语。」阿难言：「若不共语，其脱咨请，欲闻经法，当复云何？」佛言：「应为说法，但当善摄汝身、口、意。」尔时，阿难而白佛言：「我等从今如是奉行。」

At that time, Ānanda said to the Buddha, "World-Honored One! After the Buddha enters parinirvāṇa, how should we make offerings?" The Buddha said, "You should not be so sad about this. Just think: 'After my parinirvāṇa, I will uphold the true Dharma and gladly teach others what I have heard. Why is this? The devas themselves will make offerings to my body. Also, the brāhmaṇas, kings, elders, and householders will themselves make offerings to my body.'"

尔时，阿难而白佛言：「世尊入于般涅槃后，供养之法，当云何耶？」佛言：「汝今不应逆忧此事。但自思惟：『于我灭后护持正法，以昔所闻，乐为人说。所以者何？诸天自当供养我身。又婆罗门及以诸王、长者居士，此等自当供养我身。』」

Ānanda said, "Although the devas and humans themselves will make offerings, I do not know what method to follow." The Buddha said, "Ānanda! Make offerings to my body according to the method of a wheel-turning sage king." Ānanda asked again, "What is the method for making offerings to a wheel-turning sage king?" The Buddha said, "Ānanda! The method for making offerings to a wheel-turning sage king is to wrap the body with new, clean cotton and fine wool, layer upon layer, until it is filled a thousand times. Place it inside a golden coffin, then a silver coffin, then a copper coffin, then an iron coffin. Then pour in various wonderful fragrant oils. Also, inside the coffin, use various fragrant flowers to smear and scatter, perform various music, sing and chant praises, and then lower the lid. Build a large jeweled palanquin, making it extremely tall and wide, with a canopy, railings, and various wonderful adornments. Place the coffin on top. Also, in the city, build a place for cremation, sweeping and sprinkling the four sides, making it extremely clean. Pile up various fine sandalwood and famous incense. Also, on top of the pile, spread out silk and wool, and set up a large jeweled tent to cover it. Then carry it to the cremation site, burn incense, scatter flowers, make offerings with music, circumambulate the incense pile seven times, and then place the coffin on the incense pile, pouring fragrant oil over it. The method of lighting the fire is to start from below. After the cremation is completed, collect the relics, place them in a golden bottle, and immediately build a caitya there, adorned with a victory banner and hanging silk banners and canopies. The people should always make offerings daily, burning incense, scattering flowers, and so on.

阿难言：「虽复天人自兴供养，然我不知应依何法？」佛言：「阿难！供养我身，依转轮圣王。」阿难又问：「供养转轮圣王，其法云何？」佛言：「阿难！供养转轮圣王之法，用新净绵及以细［叠＊毛］，合缠其身，如是乃至积满千重，内金棺中，又作银棺，盛于金棺，又作铜棺，盛于银棺，又作铁棺，盛于铜棺，然后灌以众妙香油；又复棺内，以诸香华而用涂散，作众伎乐，歌呗赞颂，然后下盖。造大宝舆，极令高广，轩盖栏楯，众妙庄严，以棺置上。又于城中作阇维处，扫洒四面极令清净，以好栴檀及诸名香，聚为大［卄／积］。又于［卄／积］上，敷舒缯［叠＊毛］，施大宝帐，以覆其上。然后舁举，至阇维处，烧香散华，伎乐供养，绕彼香［卄／积］，周回七匝，然后以棺置香［卄／积］上，而用香油，以浇洒之。然火之法，从下而起。阇维既竟，收取舍利，内金瓶中，即于彼处，而起兜婆，表刹庄严，悬缯幡盖。诸人民等，恒应日日烧香散华种种供养。

Ānanda should know that the method of making offerings to a wheel-turning sage king is like this. The cremation of my body should also be equal to the king, but the caitya built is different from the king's. The victory banner should be adorned with nine parasols. If sentient beings hang silk banners and canopies, burn incense, scatter flowers, light lamps and candles, pay homage, and praise my caitya, this person will obtain great blessings and benefits for a long time. In the future, not long from now, others will also build large caityas to make offerings to their bodies.

At that time, Ānanda heard the Buddha's words and was distressed. He wept and cried, hiding behind the Buddha not far away. At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "Ānanda should not grieve like this. Why is that? He will soon attain the place of liberation. Bhikṣus should know that the past buddhas all had attendants, just like Ānanda now. The future buddhas will also be like this. Bhikṣus should know that Ānanda now has profound and wonderful wisdom, is intelligent and sharp-witted. Whatever Dharma treasury I have spoken in the past, Ānanda has all remembered and not forgotten. Moreover, bhikṣus, Ānanda is good at knowing the timing of coming and going. If there are guests who wish to come and see me, Ānanda will first consider the time: 'The World-Honored One may see the bhikṣus at such and such a time, may see the bhikṣuṇīs at such and such a time, may see the upāsakas at such and such a time, may see the upāsikās at such and such a time, may see the brāhmaṇas at such and such a time, may see the kṣatriyas at such and such a time, may see the elders and householders at such and such a time, may see the non-Buddhist practitioners at such and such a time.' It is because Ānanda's communication and progress in seeing me has caused their wholesome roots to mature.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「阿难不应作此悲𢙐。所以者何？不久当得到解脱处。比丘当知，过去诸佛皆有侍者，如今阿难，未来诸佛亦复如是。比丘当知，今此阿难，智慧深妙、聪明利根，我从昔来，所说法藏，阿难皆悉忆持不忘。复次，比丘，阿难善知进止时节，若有人客，欲来见我，阿难即先思量其时：『世尊或应某时见诸比丘、或应某时见比丘尼、或应某时见优婆塞、或应某时见优婆夷、或应某时见婆罗门、或应某时见于刹利、或应某时见长者居士、或应某时见诸外道。』如是等众，若来见我及闻说法，皆悉多获功德福利。所以者何？悉是阿难通进见我，得其善根成熟时故。

"Moreover, bhikṣus, there are four extraordinary and rare qualities of a wheel-turning sage king. First, when a brāhmaṇa comes to the place of the wheel-turning sage king, having arrived and seen the king's dignified appearance and majestic virtue, he gives rise to joy; next, hearing the king's clear and melodious speech, he also gives rise to joy; even seeing the king silent and wordless, he is also filled with joy; and when taking leave of the king and returning to his abode, he looks back with longing, step by step, feeling regretful, like a hungry and thirsty person who cannot be satisfied. Second, the minor kṣatriyas; third, the vaiśyas; fourth, the śūdras are also like this. This is the wheel-turning sage king's four extraordinary qualities. You should know that Ānanda also has these four extraordinary qualities. Second, bhikṣuṇīs; third, upāsakas; fourth, upāsikās are also like this. You should know that Ānanda has these four extraordinary qualities."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "You should not grieve and say, 'The Teacher of gods and humans is about to enter parinirvāṇa. I now have no more hope of liberation.' Why is that? Whatever Dharma treasury I have spoken, after my parinirvāṇa, contemplate and uphold it, diligently practice and make progress, and you will soon attain liberation.

尔时，世尊告阿难言：「汝今不应自生苦𢙐而作是言：『天人之师将般涅槃，我今无复解脱之期。』所以者何？凡我所说，一切法藏，于我灭后，思惟奉持，勤行精进，不久自当得于解脱。」

At that time, Ānanda, having received the Tathāgata's comforting words in the Brahma-like voice, his grief was slightly relieved. He said to the Buddha, "My mind and thoughts are now slightly awakened. I wish to make a request. Please have compassion." The Buddha replied, "What do you wish to request?" Ānanda said, "This city of Kuśinagara, compared to other great countries, is extremely small and narrow, and the people are not prosperous. I only wish that the World-Honored One would go to other great countries: Rājagṛha, Vaiśālī, Śrāvastī, Vārāṇasī, Ayodhyā, Campā, Kauśāmbī, Takṣaśilā, and other such cities, which are centrally located. The people are prosperous, the land is rich and happy, and they all have faith. They are wise and intelligent. I only wish that the World-Honored One would go to those cities to enter parinirvāṇa, widely benefiting the sentient beings there.

尔时，阿难既得如来梵音安慰，忧𢙐小除，而白佛言：「我今心意，如小醒悟，欲有所请，唯愿哀愍。」佛即答言：「欲请何事？」阿难言：「此鸠尸那城，比余大国，极为边狭，人民又复不能炽盛。唯愿世尊！往余大国：王舍城、毘耶离城、舍卫国城、婆罗［木＊奈］城、阿逾阇城、瞻波城、俱睒弥城、德叉尸罗城，如是诸城，所处正中。人民炽盛，国土丰乐，皆多信心，智慧聪明，唯愿世尊！往彼诸城，而般涅槃，广利其中诸众生等。」

At that time, the World-Honored One replied to Ānanda, "You should not make such a request to me now, saying, 'This city of Kuśinagara is narrow.' Listen attentively now, I will tell you. Ānanda! In the distant past, there was a wheel-turning sage king named Mahāsudarśana in this city of Kuśinagara, who was endowed with the seven treasures and had a thousand sons. He subdued his enemies and taught the people with the right Dharma. At that time, this city was named Kuśāvatī, with two gates in the east and west, twelve yojanas apart, and two gates in the north and south, eight yojanas apart. The city was surrounded by seven layers. The first layer was pure gold, the second pure silver, the third pure lapis lazuli, the fourth pure crystal, the fifth pure agate, the sixth pure coral, and the seventh was a mixture of various treasures. The city towers were all seven stories high, with windows and railings adorned with the seven treasures, and hanging with various jeweled bells and jeweled nets. The distance between them was just one arrow's length. The four gates of the city were each ninefold, magnificently decorated and radiant, pleasing to the eye. The strong men, upon hearing these words of the Buddha, were not granted their request. They were sorrowful and distressed, weeping and lamenting, and remained silent.

尔时，如来告力士言：「汝今不应作此请我。所以者何？一切诸行皆悉无常，恩爱合会必归别离。设我住世，若满一劫，会亦当灭。我所说法但当忆持诵念勿忘，此则不异我在世也。」诸力士等闻佛此言，不果所请，心怀愁悴，悲泣懊恼，默然而住。

At that time, in the city of Kuśinagara, there was a non-Buddhist named Subhadra, who was 120 years old. He was intelligent and wise, reciting the four Vedas. He was well-versed in all writings and treatises, and was respected by all. Hearing that the Tathāgata was about to enter parinirvāṇa between the twin śāla trees in the Śāla Grove, he thought to himself, "In my writings and treatises, it is said that the appearance of a Buddha in the world is extremely rare, like the udumbara flower blooming only once in a while. Now he is in the Śāla Grove. I have some doubts. I should go and ask him. If Gautama can resolve my doubts, then he truly has attained omniscience." Having thought this, he went to where the Buddha was. Outside the grove, he encountered Ānanda and said to him, "In my writings and treatises, it is said that the appearance of a Buddha in the world is extremely difficult to encounter, only happening once in many millions of kalpas, like the udumbara flower that is rarely seen. Teaching and transforming the world, I have never seen it before. Now I hear that he is about to enter parinirvāṇa in the Śāla Grove. I have some doubts and wish to go and ask him. You should tell the World-Honored One on my behalf that I now wish to see him.

尔时，鸠尸那城有一外道，年百二十，名须跋陀罗，聪明多智，诵四毘陀经，一切书论无不通达，为一切人之所宗敬。其闻如来在娑罗林双树之间将般涅槃，心自思惟：「我诸书论，说佛出世极为难遇，如优昙钵花时一现耳。其今在于娑罗林中，我有所疑，试往请问，瞿昙若能决我疑者，便是实得一切种智。」作此念已，往到佛所，在于林外，逢见阿难，即语之言：「我书论中说佛兴世极难值遇，亿千万劫时时乃出，如优昙钵华不可数覩，在世教化，我初不见，今闻在此娑罗林中当般涅槃，我有所疑，欲往请问。汝可为我白世尊言，道我今者欲希相见。」

At that time, Ānanda heard his words and thought to himself, "The World-Honored One's four elements are now out of balance. If he meets with this non-Buddhist, there will certainly be discussion, which may cause further harm." He then replied, "The World-Honored One's four elements are now out of balance. He is lying in the forest, suffering greatly. You should not see the Tathāgata now. Do not create obstacles when the World-Honored One is about to enter parinirvāṇa." Subhadra made this request three times, and Ānanda also replied three times in the same way.

尔时，阿难闻其此语，心自思惟：「世尊今者四大不和，接对来久，已自增恶。若复与此外道相见，必有言论，容致损剧。」即答之言：「世尊今者四大不和，寝卧林中极苦身痛，汝今不须见如来也，莫临世尊般涅槃时而作障碍。」须跋陀罗如是三请，阿难亦复如是三答。

At that time, the World-Honored One, with his pure divine ear, heard Subhadra's request to Ānanda. He also observed his faculties and knew it was time to save him. He then spoke to Ānanda in a voice like Brahma's, saying, "Do not create obstacles for my last disciple alone. Allow Subhadra to come forward. I wish to see him. This person is straightforward, intelligent, and easy to understand. He seeks to resolve doubts, not to come and debate for the sake of victory." Then Ānanda accepted the Buddha's instruction and told Subhadra, "The World-Honored One has already allowed you to come forward." Hearing the Buddha's permission, Subhadra was overjoyed and could not contain himself. He thought to himself, "The śramaṇa Gautama is definitely one who has attained omniscience." He then went to where the Buddha was, exchanged greetings, and sat to one side. He said to the Buddha, "Gautama, I wish to ask something. Please allow me." The Buddha said, "Excellent! Excellent! Subhadra, ask as you please.

尔时，世尊以净天耳，闻须跋陀罗请阿难声，又观其根，是可度时，即以梵音告阿难言：「汝莫于我最后弟子独作留碍，听须跋陀罗前来，我欲见之。此人质直聪慧易悟，所以求] cf.【丽】进欲决疑难，非为故来论胜负也。」于是阿难即承佛教，语须跋陀罗：「世尊今已勅听汝前。」须跋陀罗闻佛许前，欢喜踊跃，不能自胜，而心念言：「沙门瞿昙，决定是得一切种智。」即前佛所，互相问讯坐于一面，而白佛言：「瞿昙，欲有所问，唯愿听许。」佛言：「善哉！善哉！须跋陀罗，恣汝所问。」

Subhadra then asked the Buddha, "Now in this world, the śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, non-Buddhist teachers, the six teachers such as Pūraṇa Kāśyapa, Maskarin Gośālīputra, Sañjayin Vairaṭṭīputra, Ajita Keśakambalin, Kātyāyana, and Nirgrantha Jñātiputra, each claim to be omniscient, while regarding others as having wrong views. They say that their practices are the path of liberation, and that the practices of others are the causes of birth and death. They argue with each other. How can we know their falsehood and truth? Which teacher deserves to be called a śramaṇa? Which teacher is definitely the cause of liberation?"

须跋陀罗即问佛言：「今者世间沙门婆罗门外道六师、富兰那迦叶、末伽利拘赊梨子，删阇夜毘罗眡子、阿耆多翅] cf.【丽】舍钦婆罗、迦罗鸠駄迦旃延、尼犍陀若提子等，各各自说，是一切智，以余学者，名为邪见。言其所行，是解脱道，说他行者，是生死因。互相是非，云何而得知其虚实？何师应得沙门之称？何师定是解脱之因？」

At that time, the Tathāgata replied, "Excellent! Excellent! Subhadra, you are able to ask me about such matters. Listen attentively, I will explain for you. Subhadra! If the eightfold noble path is not seen in all dharmas, know that there is no name of a śramaṇa, nor the second, third, or fourth. Without a śramaṇa, there is no liberation. Without liberation, there is no omniscience. Subhadra! If the eightfold noble path exists in all dharmas, there must be the names of the four śramaṇas. With the names of the śramaṇas, there is liberation. With liberation, there is omniscience. Subhadra! Only in my Dharma are there the eightfold noble path, the names of the four śramaṇas, the path of liberation, and omniscience. Those non-Buddhist teachers such as Pūraṇa Kāśyapa do not have the eightfold noble path in their teachings, nor the names of the śramaṇas, nor liberation, nor omniscience. If they say they do, know that it is definitely false. Subhadra! All sentient beings who hear my teachings and accept and contemplate them will definitely not hear in vain and attain liberation. Subhadra! When I was in the royal palace before leaving home, the entire world was deluded by the six teachers. I had not yet seen the reality of a śramaṇa. Subhadra! When I was twenty-nine years old, I left home to study the path. At thirty-six, under the bodhi tree, I contemplated the ultimate source of the eightfold noble path, attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and attained omniscience. I then went to the Deer Park in the country of Benares, where the sages dwelt, and turned the wheel of the four truths for the five people such as Ājñātakauṇḍinya. At that time, the name of a śramaṇa first appeared in the world, benefiting and liberating sentient beings. Subhadra! Know that my Dharma can attain liberation. The Tathāgata is truly omniscient."

尔时，如来即答之曰：「善哉！善哉！须跋陀罗，乃能问我如此之义。谛听谛听，吾为汝说。须跋陀罗！诸法之中若不见有八圣道法，当知无有一沙门名，二及三四，亦复不有；既无沙门，亦无解脱；解脱既无，非一切种智。须跋陀罗！若诸法中，有八圣道法，当知必有四沙门名；有沙门名，则有解脱；既有解脱，是一切种智。须跋陀罗！唯我法中，有八圣道，有四沙门名，是解脱道，是一切种智。彼诸外道，富兰那迦叶等，其说法中，无八圣道，无沙门名，非是解脱及一切种智。若言有者，当知必是虚诳之说。须跋陀罗！一切众生，闻我所说，信受思惟，当知其人必不空闻要得解脱。须跋陀罗！我在王宫未出家时，一切世间皆为六师之所迷醉，初未见有沙门之实。须跋陀罗！我年二十有九，出家学道，三十有六，于菩提] cf.【丽】树下，思八圣道究竟源底，成阿耨多罗三藐三菩提，得一切种智。即往波罗捺国鹿野苑中仙人住处，为阿若憍陈如等五人，转四谛法轮，其得道迹。尔时始有沙门之称，出于世间福利众生。须跋陀罗！当知我法能得解脱，如来实是一切种智。」

At that time, Subhadra, having heard the Tathāgata speak of the eightfold noble path, was filled with joy, his body hair standing on end. Thirsty to hear the meaning of the eightfold noble path, he said to the Buddha, "I only wish that the World-Honored One would explain the meaning of the eightfold noble path for me." The World-Honored One then explained it in detail for him. Having heard the Buddha's explanation of the meaning of the eightfold noble path, Subhadra's mind opened up, and he suddenly had a great realization. He was free from defilements and impurities in all dharmas, and attained the purity of the Dharma-eye. He then said to the Buddha, "I now wish to leave home and enter the Buddha's Dharma." The World-Honored One then called out to him, "Welcome, bhikṣu!" His beard and hair fell out by themselves, and the kāṣāya robe appeared on his body, making him a monk. The World-Honored One also explained the four truths to him in detail, and he immediately attained the exhaustion of taints and became an arhat.

尔时，须跋陀罗，既闻如来说八圣道，心生欢喜，举身毛竖，渴仰欲闻八圣道义，而白佛言：「唯愿世尊！为我分别八圣道义。」于是世尊即便为其分别广说。须跋陀罗既闻佛说八圣道义，心意开朗，豁然大悟，于诸法中远尘离垢得法眼净，即白佛言：「我今欲于佛法出家。」于是世尊即便唤之：「善来！比丘！」须发自落，袈裟著身，即成沙门。世尊又为广说四谛，即获漏尽，成阿罗汉。

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda, "You should know that when I attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi at the Bodhimanda, I first taught the Dharma and liberated the five people such as Ājñātakauṇḍinya. Now, at the Sāla Grove, on the verge of parinirvāṇa, I am giving my final teaching and liberating Subhadra. There are no more devas or humans who should hear my teachings and be liberated. Those with good roots who should attain liberation will all become my disciples in the future, teaching each other. Ānanda! Although Subhadra is a non-Buddhist, his good roots should have matured. Only the Tathāgata can discern this. After my parinirvāṇa, if any non-Buddhists wish to leave home and enter my Dharma, you should not immediately allow it. First, let them recite the scriptures for four months to observe their intentions and natures, whether they are false or true. If you see that their conduct is honest and gentle, and they truly have deep joy in my Dharma, then you may allow them to leave home. Ānanda! The reason is that you have little wisdom and cannot discern the faculties of sentient beings. Therefore, I am instructing you to observe them first.

尔时，世尊告阿难言：「汝今当知，我于道场，成阿耨多罗三藐三菩提。最初说法，度阿若憍陈如等五人。今日在于娑罗林中，临般涅槃，最后说法，度须跋陀罗。诸天及人，无复更应闻我说法而得度者；若有善根应得解脱，当来皆是我之弟子，展转相教。阿难！须跋陀罗虽是外道，而其善根，应成熟时，唯有如来，能分别知。我般涅槃后，若有外道，欲于我法求出家者，汝等不应便听许之。先令四月诵习经典，观其意性为虚为实，若见其行质直柔软，于我法中实有深乐，然后方可听其出家。阿难！所以然者，汝等小智，不能分别众生之根，是故令汝先观之耳。」

At that time, Subhadra said to the Buddha, "I wish to leave home and enter the monastic order. If the World-Honored One requires me to praise and recite the scriptures in the Buddha's Dharma for forty years before allowing me to leave home, I can do that! How much more so for four months?" The World-Honored One then said to him, "It is so! Subhadra! I see that your intention is earnest and eager in my Dharma. What you say now is not empty." Subhadra then said to the Buddha, "I cannot bear to see the Honored One of devas and humans enter parinirvāṇa. I wish to enter parinirvāṇa before the World-Honored One." The Buddha said, "Excellent!" At that time, Subhadra entered the samadhi of the fire element in front of the Buddha and entered parinirvāṇa.

尔时，须跋陀罗而白佛言：「我于向者，欲求出家。世尊若令先于佛法四十年中赞诵经典，然后听我而出家者，我亦能尔！岂况四月？」尔时，世尊即告之言：「如是，如是！须跋陀罗！我观汝意，于我法中慇懃渴仰，今作此言，非为虚设。」尔时，须跋陀罗前白佛言：「我今不] cf.【丽】忍见天人尊入般涅槃，我于今日，欲先世尊入般涅槃。」佛言：「善哉。」时，须跋陀罗，即于佛前，入火界三昧而般涅槃。

At that time, the Tathāgata told Ānanda, "Do not think that when you see me enter parinirvāṇa, the true Dharma will be forever cut off here. Why is that? I have previously established precepts and prātimokṣa for the bhikṣus, as well as other wonderful teachings. This is your great teacher, no different from when I am in the world. After my parinirvāṇa, the bhikṣus should respect each other according to seniority. Do not call each other by family name, but by given name. Watch over each other, and do not let there be any violation of the great precepts among the assembly. Do not seek out others' minor faults. The bhikṣu Channa should be severely punished." Ānanda asked the Buddha, "What is the severe punishment?" The Buddha said, "Ānanda! The severe punishment is that all bhikṣus should not speak to him." Thereupon, Ānanda followed the instruction.

尔时，如来告阿难言：「汝勿见我入般涅槃，便谓正法于此永绝。何以故？我昔为诸比丘，制戒波罗提木叉，及余所说种种妙法，此即便是汝等大师。如我在世，无有异也。阿难！我般涅槃后，诸比丘等，各依次第，大小相敬，不得呼姓，皆唤名字。互相伺察，无令众中有犯大戒，不应［门@视］求觅他细过。车匿比丘，应与重罚。」阿难问佛：「与何重罚？」佛言：「阿难！与重罚者，一切比丘勿与共语。」于是阿难，如教奉行。

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If you have any doubts or difficulties now, ask them freely. Do not regret after my parinirvāṇa, saying, 'The Tathāgata was recently in the Śāla Grove. At that time, I did not go to ask for clarification, causing me to have doubts now.' Although my body is now sick, I can still explain your doubts. If you wish to uphold the true Dharma and benefit devas and humans after my parinirvāṇa, you should come quickly now to resolve your doubts." The World-Honored One spoke like this three times, but the bhikṣus were silent and none asked to resolve their doubts. At that time, Ānanda said to the Buddha, "How strange, World-Honored One! You have exhorted like this three times, but there are no doubters in this assembly." The Buddha said, "It is so, it is so. Ānanda! In this assembly, there are five hundred bhikṣus who have not yet attained the Way. After my parinirvāṇa, in the future, they will all attain the exhaustion of taints. You should also be counted among them.

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等今者若有疑难，恣意请问，莫我灭后生悔恨言：『如来近在娑罗林中，我于尔时，不往咨决，致令今日情有所滞。』我今虽复身体有疾，犹堪为汝等解释疑惑。若欲于我般涅槃后奉持正法利益天人，今宜速来决所疑也。」世尊乃至如是三告，诸比丘等默然无有求决疑者。尔时，阿难即白佛言：「奇哉世尊！如是三诲，而此众中无有疑者。」佛言：「如是，如是。阿难！今此众中，五百比丘，未得道者，我般涅槃后，未来世中，当得尽漏。汝亦当在此中数也。」

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus, "If you see me committing any slight transgression with my body, speech, or mind, you should tell me." When the bhikṣus heard these words of the Buddha, they wept in distress and said to the Buddha, "How could the Tathāgata have any subtle faults with his body, speech, or mind?"

尔时，世尊告诸比丘：「汝等若见我身、口、意脱相犯触，汝当语我。」时，诸比丘闻佛此语，流泪懊恼而白佛言：「如来岂当有身口意微细过耶？」

Then the Tathāgata spoke a verse:

于是如来即便说偈：

All conditioned phenomena are impermanent, they are phenomena of arising and
ceasing. When arising and ceasing cease, that is peace and bliss.

「诸行无常，　　是生灭法，生灭灭已，　　寂灭为乐。」

At that time, after the Tathāgata had spoken this verse, he told the bhikṣus, "You should know that all conditioned phenomena are impermanent. Although I now have a vajra-like body, I am also not exempt from being moved by impermanence. Birth and death are extremely frightening. You should diligently practice and quickly seek to leave this pit of birth and death. This is my final instruction. The time for my parinirvāṇa has come." At that time, the bhikṣus and other devas and humans, hearing the Buddha's instruction, wept and lamented, fainting and falling to the ground. The Tathāgata then universally told them, "You should not give rise to this sorrow and distress. The nature and characteristics of conditioned phenomena are all like this." Thereupon, the Tathāgata entered the first dhyāna. Emerging from the first dhyāna, he entered the second dhyāna. Emerging from the second dhyāna, he entered the third dhyāna. Emerging from the third dhyāna, he entered the fourth dhyāna. Emerging from the fourth dhyāna, he entered the sphere of space. Emerging from the sphere of space, he entered the sphere of consciousness. Emerging from the sphere of consciousness, he entered the sphere of nothingness. Emerging from the sphere of nothingness, he entered the sphere of neither thought nor non-thought. Emerging from the sphere of neither thought nor non-thought, he entered the concentration of cessation.

尔时，如来说此偈已，告诸比丘：「汝等当知，一切诸行，皆悉无常。我今虽是金刚之体，亦复不免无常所迁。生死之中极为可畏，汝等宜应勤行精进，速求离此生死火坑。此则是我最后教也。我般涅槃，其时已至。」时，诸比丘及余天人，闻佛此诲，悲号涕泣，闷绝躃地。如来即便普告之言：「汝等不应生此悲恼，诸行性相，皆悉如是。」于是如来，即入初禅。出于初禅，入第二禅。出于二禅，入第三禅。出于三禅，入第四禅。出第四禅，入于空处。出于空处，入于识处。出于识处，入无所有处。出无所有处，入于非想非非想处。出于非想非非想处，入灭尽定。

At that time, Ānanda, seeing the Tathāgata silent and still, his body and limbs no longer moving, immediately shed tears and said, "The World-Honored One has now entered parinirvāṇa." At that time, Āryadeva said to Ānanda, "The Tathāgata has not yet entered parinirvāṇa. The reason he is silent and still is because he has entered the concentration of cessation.

尔时，阿难既见如来湛然不言，身体肢节不复动摇，即便流泪而作是言：「世尊今已入般涅槃。」尔时，阿［少／兔］楼駄语阿难言：「如来即时未般涅槃，所以湛然身不动者，正是入于灭尽定耳。」

At that time, the World-Honored One emerged from the concentration of cessation and again entered the sphere of neither thought nor non-thought, and so on, up to entering the first dhyāna. Emerging from the first dhyāna, he entered the second dhyāna. Emerging from the second dhyāna, he entered the third dhyāna. Emerging from the third dhyāna, he entered the fourth dhyāna, and then entered parinirvāṇa in that very place.

尔时，世尊出灭尽定，更还入于非想非非想处，乃至次第入于初禅。复出初禅，入第二禅。出于二禅，入第三禅。出于三禅，入第四禅，即于此地入般涅槃。

At that time, Āryadeva said to Ānanda, "The World-Honored One has entered parinirvāṇa in the fourth dhyāna ground." Then, Ānanda and the four groups of disciples, having heard Āryadeva's words, wept and wailed, fainting and falling to the ground. Some raised their hands, slapped their heads, beat their chests, and cried out, saying to each other, "The eye of the world has extinguished so quickly! From now on, who will guide all beings? The realms of humans and gods will diminish, while the evil paths will increase daily."

尔时，阿［少／兔］楼駄语阿难言：「世尊已于第四禅地入般涅槃。」于是，阿难及四部众，闻阿［少／兔］楼駄作此言已，悲号呜咽，闷绝躃地。其中或有举手拍头搥胸大叫，共相谓言：「世间眼灭，一何速哉！一切众生，从今已去，谁为导者？人天方减，恶道日增。」

At that time, the devas, nāgas, and eight groups in the sky wept profusely like heavy rain, saying to each other, "From now on, who will we take refuge in? It is like an infant losing its loving mother. The paths to the three evil realms will open up daily, while the gate of liberation will be tightly closed. All beings will sink in the sea of suffering. It is like a patient being far from a good doctor, or a blind person losing their guide. Since we have lost the unsurpassed Dharma King, the thieves of afflictions will increasingly encroach upon us." Having said this, they fainted and lamented uncontrollably.

时，虚空中天龙八部，涕泣滂沱犹如骤雨，互相谓言：「我等从今谁为归依？犹若婴儿失于慈母。三恶道迳日就开阔，解脱之门方巨重关。一切众生，沈沦苦海。亦如病人远于良医，又似盲者失所牵导。我等既去无上法王，烦恼之贼日见侵逼。」唱此言已，闷绝懊恼不能自禁。

At that time, the strong men of Kuśinagara were all as strong as fragrant elephants. Having seen the Tathāgata enter parinirvāṇa, their spirits were haggard, like a new illness.

时，鸠尸那城诸力士众，皆悉勇健，犹如香象。既见如来入般涅槃，神情憔悴，如病新起。

At that time, the great earth shook, the heavenly drum sounded by itself, the four great oceans' waves overturned, Mount Sumeru naturally swayed, a violent wind blew, the trees in the forest were uprooted and withered, and it was strange and unusual.

当于尔时大地震动天鼓自鸣，四大海水波浪翻倒，须弥山王自然倾摇，狂风奋发，林木摧折箫索枯悴，骇异于常。

At that time, the great Brahma King spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，大梵天王即说偈言：

In the past, future, and present, there are no sentient
beings who do not return to impermanence. Even the Tathāgata
the Honored One of gods and humans, with his vajra
body of solidity, still cannot escape impermanence, let alone other
people. All sentient beings cherish and protect their bodies, anointing
them with incense and flowers, not knowing that they will
be destroyed. The Tathāgata's golden body, adorned with excellent marks
will also be abandoned and enter parinirvāṇa. Having permanently severed
all afflictions and attained omniscience, he still cannot escape. How
much more so for those with other afflictions and entanglements.

「过去与未来，　　及以今现在，无有诸众生，　　不归无常者。如来天人尊，　　金刚身坚固，犹不免无常，　　而况于余人。一切诸众生，　　爱惜保其身，薰余以香华，　　不知当毁灭。如来金色身，　　相好以庄严，会亦当] cf.【丽】弃舍，　　应入般涅槃。永断诸烦恼，　　成一切种智，犹尚不得免，　　况余结累者。」

At that time, the Heavenly King Śakra spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，天帝释即说偈言：

All conditioned phenomena are indeed the nature of arising and ceasing
Even the most honored one with two feet will also return
to extinction. The raging fire of the three poisons constantly burns
all sentient beings. Without the great compassionate cloud, who can rain
to extinguish it?

「一切诸行性，　　实是生灭法，两足最胜尊，　　亦复归于尽。三毒炽然火，　　恒烧诸众生，无有大悲云，　　谁能雨令灭？」

At that time, Ārāḍa Kālāma spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，阿［少／兔］楼駄，即说偈言：

The Tathāgata today, his faculties are unshakable. His mind and intention
comprehend all dharmas, yet he abandons this body. Tranquil, cutting off
thoughts, also without any feelings. Like a lamp, when the oil
is exhausted, the light goes out. The Tathāgata's extinction is also like this.

「如来于今日，　　诸根不摇动，心意会诸法，　　而弃于此身。恬然绝思虑，　　亦复无诸受，如灯尽光灭，　　如来灭亦然。」

At that time, Ānanda spoke a verse, saying:

尔时，阿难即说偈言：

The great earth suddenly shook, and violent winds blew in the
four directions. The ocean waves overturned, and Mount Sumeru, the jeweled
mountain, shook. The minds of the gods were sorrowful, and their
tears fell like rain. They were all greatly terrified, as if
seized by non-humans. Because of the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, such things occurred.

「大地忽震动，　　狂风四激起，海水波翻倒，　　须弥宝山摇。天人心悲痛，　　泣泪犹如雨，皆悉大恐怖，　　如被非人执。由佛般涅槃，　　故有如此事。」

At that time, among the assembly, the bhikṣus and devas and humans who had not yet attained the path, having seen the Tathāgata already enter parinirvāṇa, their minds were distressed and they rolled on the ground. Those who had attained the path deeply lamented the impermanence and suffering of the world, and wept sorrowfully, unable to control themselves. At that time, Ānanda said to the bhikṣus and devas and humans, "You should not give rise to great distress. The Tathāgata has already spoken to you about the nature and characteristics of conditioned phenomena. How can you still weep?"

尔时，众中有未得道比丘、人天，既见如来已般涅槃，心生懊恼，宛转于地。已得道者，深叹世间无常之苦，悲号啼泣不能自胜。是时，阿［少／兔］楼駄语诸比丘及以天人：「汝等不应生大忧恼，如来前已为汝等说，诸行性相法皆如是。云何犹故而悲泣耶？」

At that time, Ānanda then universally addressed the assembly from the four directions who had come, saying, "The Tathāgata has now entered parinirvāṇa." At that time, the people, hearing Ānanda's words, wept sorrowfully and were distressed. They said in a faint voice to Ānanda, "Now this assembly of people is extremely crowded, with thirty-two yujana filled to the brim. I only pray that the Venerable One will allow us to approach the Tathāgata in turn to see him for the last time, to pay homage and make offerings. The Tathāgata's appearance in the world is difficult to encounter, like the udumbara flower appearing from time to time. Now he is here in person entering parinirvāṇa. I only pray that he will have compassion and allow us to see him."

尔时，阿难即便普语四远来众：「如来今已入般涅槃。」尔时，众人闻阿难言，悲号啼泣闷绝懊恼，而以微声语阿难言：「今此人众极为阗塞，三十二喻阇那，皆悉充满。唯愿尊者，各令我等次第得前亲见如来，最后瞻仰礼拜供养。如来出世难可值遇，如优昙钵华时时乃现，今者亲在此般涅槃，愿必哀愍，令我得见。」

At that time, Ānanda heard the people's words and thought to himself, "The Tathāgata's appearance in the world is extremely difficult to encounter, and the final offerings are also extremely difficult. Whom should I allow to make offerings to the Buddha first now? I should now allow the bhikṣuṇīs and upāsikās to come forward and make offerings to the Buddha's body. The reason is that these women are weak and may not have been able to reach where the Tathāgata is when they came before. For this reason, they should be allowed to go first." Having thought this, he then proclaimed, "All bhikṣuṇīs and upāsikās are allowed to come forward to where the Tathāgata's body is." The bhikṣuṇīs and countless upāsikās all came to where the Buddha was. Having seen that the Tathāgata had already entered parinirvāṇa, they wept sorrowfully and enwrapped him, paying homage and making various offerings.

尔时，阿难闻众人言，心自思惟：「如来出世，极为难值，最后供养亦复甚难，我今当令谁在于先供养佛者？今者宜使诸比丘尼及优婆夷，得在前来供养佛身。所以然者？斯等女弱，昔来之时，不必得到如来之所，以是因缘故宜在先。」作此念已，即便普唱：「诸比丘尼及优婆夷，皆听前于如来身所。」诸比丘尼及与无量优婆夷等，俱到佛所。既见如来已般涅槃，啼泣懊恼，围绕礼拜，种种供养。

At that time, there was a poor upāsikā who was one hundred years old. Seeing the brāhmaṇas, kṣatriyas, elders, householders, and the wives and children of the strong men, young and old, making offerings with wonderful incense and flowers, she lamented her own poverty and lack of such wishes. She thought to herself, "The Tathāgata's appearance in the world is extremely difficult to encounter, and the final offerings are also extremely difficult. Now I am destitute and have nothing to express myself." Having thought this, she was even more sorrowful and distressed. She wept bitterly, her tears staining the Tathāgata's feet, and said, "May I always see the Buddha in my future lives." The bhikṣuṇīs and upāsikās, having finished making offerings, then returned to their original places.

尔时，有一贫穷优婆夷，年一百岁。见诸婆罗门，并及刹利长者居士、力士、妻女、长幼、大小，以妙香华种种供养，自伤贫乏独绝此愿，心自思惟：「如来出世极为难值，最后供养复为甚难，而今穷罄无以自表。」作此念已，倍增悲恸，临佛足上心大懊恼，涕泣流连污如来足：「愿我将来所生之处，常得见佛。」诸比丘尼及优婆夷，供养毕已，即还本处。

At that time, Ānanda again universally announced to the other people, "The bhikṣuṇīs and upāsikās have finished making offerings. You may come forward in order to make offerings." At that time, the people came in order to where the Buddha's body was. Having seen that the Tathāgata had already entered parinirvāṇa, they wept and rolled on the ground, their hearts greatly saddened and distressed. They made offerings with various offerings. At that time, the assembly of strong men all gathered together and said to each other, "How should we cremate the Tathāgata's body? When the World-Honored One was about to enter parinirvāṇa, he should have left instructions." They then went together to ask Ānanda, "We now wish to cremate the Tathāgata's body. What is the proper way? When the World-Honored One was about to enter parinirvāṇa, he should have left instructions. Please tell us." At that time, Ānanda said to the strong men, "The Tathāgata left instructions for the cremation method, saying to treat it the same as a wheel-turning sage king." Ānanda then fully explained the matter that the Buddha had instructed before.

尔时，阿难又复普告诸余人言：「诸比丘尼及优婆夷，供养已毕，汝等可前次第供养。」时，诸人众以次而来，到佛身所，既见如来已般涅槃，号泣宛转心大悲恼，以诸供具而用供养。尔时，诸力士众皆悉集聚，共相谓言：「我等今者，云何阇维如来之身？世尊临欲般涅槃时，应有遗勅。」即便共往，问阿难言：「我等今者欲共阇维如来之身，其法云何？世尊临可般涅槃时，当有遗旨，唯愿见告。」尔时，阿难语力士言：「如来遗勅阇维之法，令与转轮圣王等无有异。」阿难即便具说佛向所勅之事。

The strong men, having heard Ānanda's words, all prepared the cremation equipment together. First, they built a jeweled palanquin, carved and adorned with exquisite craftsmanship. They placed the Tathāgata's body on the jeweled palanquin, burning incense and scattering flowers. They performed various music and sang praises. In the music, they spoke of the Dharma of suffering, emptiness, impermanence, non-self, and impurity. At that time, the strong men said to Ānanda, "The Tathāgata has now entered parinirvāṇa. The final offerings are extremely difficult to encounter. We request to keep the Tathāgata's body for seven days and seven nights to make offerings to our heart's content, so that all devas and humans may attain peace for a long time." Ānanda then asked Ānanda about the strong men's words. Ānanda replied to Ānanda, "Excellent! Do as you wish." At that time, Ānanda told the strong men, "Allow the Buddha's body to be kept for seven days and seven nights to make offerings to your heart's content."

诸力士等闻阿难言，皆共严办阇维之具。先造宝舆雕镂庄丽，以如来身置宝舆上，烧香散华。作众伎乐，歌颂赞叹。于音乐中，而说苦空无常无我不净之法。时，诸力士白阿难言：「如来今者既般涅槃，最后供养极为难遇，我等请留如来之身，七日七夜恣意供养，令诸天人长夜获安。」阿难即便以力士言问阿［少／兔］楼駄，阿［少／兔］楼駄答阿难言：「善哉！随意。」阿难尔时告诸力士：「听留佛身七日七夜恣意供养。」

At that time, the strong men, having heard Ānanda's words, were greatly saddened and delighted. They then made various offerings in the forest. After seven days, the strong men wrapped the Tathāgata's body with new, clean cotton and fine silk, and then placed it inside a golden coffin. Inside the golden coffin, they scattered ox-head sandalwood, fragrant powder, and various wonderful flowers. They then placed the golden coffin inside a silver coffin, and the silver coffin inside a copper coffin, and the copper coffin inside an iron coffin. They then placed the iron coffin on a jeweled palanquin, performing various music, singing praises, and chanting. The devas in the sky scattered maṇḍala flowers, mahā-maṇḍala flowers, mañjūṣa flowers, mahā-mañjūṣa flowers, and played heavenly music, making various offerings. They then successively placed the coffin lids. At that time, the strong men said to each other, "The seven-day period is now over. We should carry the Tathāgata's coffin around the city, allowing the people to make offerings to their heart's content, and then go to the south of the city for cremation." Having said this, they then carried the Tathāgata's coffin together, exhausting their strength but unable to lift it. They were all surprised and did not know why. They asked Ānanda, "We, the strong men, wish to carry the Buddha's coffin around the city. Returning to the south gate for offerings and cremation, we have exhausted our strength but cannot lift it. We do not know what this is a sign of. Please tell us, Venerable One."

时，诸力士闻阿难言，心大悲庆，即于林中种种供养，满七日已。时，诸力士以新净绵及以细［叠＊毛］缠如来身，然后内以金棺之中，其金棺内散以牛头栴檀、香屑及诸妙华，即以金棺内银棺中，又以银棺内铜棺中，又以铜棺内铁棺中，又以铁棺置宝舆上，作诸伎乐歌呗赞叹。诸天于空，散曼陀罗花、摩诃曼陀罗花、曼殊沙花、摩诃曼殊沙花，并作天乐，种种供养，然后次第下诸棺盖。时，力士等共相谓言：「七日之期今者已满，我等宜应舁如来棺，周匝绕城，令诸人民恣意供养，然后往于城南阇维。」作此言已，即便共舁如来之棺，尽其身力而不能起，各共惊怪不知何故？而以问于阿［少／兔］楼駄：「我等诸人欲舁佛棺，周匝绕城。还趣南门供养阇维，尽竭身力而不能举，不知此是何等事相？唯愿尊者为我说之。」

At that time, Ānanda said to the people, "The reason is that the devas in the sky wish to have the Buddha's coffin circumambulate the city. Enter through the north gate and stay in the city, allowing the devas and humans to make various offerings. Then, you should exit through the east gate and go to the place of the precious crown stūpa for cremation." Having heard these words, the strong men said to each other, "If that is the intention of the devas, we should comply." They then carried the Buddha's coffin, circumambulated the city once, entered through the north gate, and stayed in the city, allowing the devas and humans to make offerings to their heart's content. They performed wonderful music, burned incense, scattered flowers, and sang praises. The devas in the sky rained down maṇḍala flowers, mahā-maṇḍala flowers, mañjūṣa flowers, mahā-mañjūṣa flowers, and played heavenly music, making various offerings. After the offerings were completed, they then exited through the east gate of the city and went to the place of the precious crown stūpa. Having arrived there, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, devas, nāgas, and the eight groups of beings were overcome with grief and could not control themselves. They then piled up ox-head sandalwood and various incense. On top of the pile, they spread out silk and cotton, and set up a large jeweled canopy to cover it. They carried the jeweled coffin and circumambulated the incense pile seven times, burning incense, scattering flowers, and performing various music. They placed the jeweled coffin on the incense pile, and took wonderful fragrant oil to pour all over it.

时，阿［少／兔］楼駄语众人言：「所以然者，虚空诸天欲令佛棺周匝绕城。从北门入住于城中，听诸天人种种供养。然后应从东门而出，往于宝冠支提之所，而阇维之。」彼诸力士闻此语已，共相谓言：「诸天意尔，宜应顺从。」即舁佛棺，绕城一匝，从北门入，住城之中，听诸天人恣意供养，作妙伎乐，烧香散华，歌呗赞叹。诸天于空，雨曼陀罗花、摩诃曼陀罗华、曼殊沙花、摩诃曼殊沙花，并作天乐，种种供养。供养讫已，即便从城东门而出，往于宝冠支提之所。既到彼处，比丘、比丘尼、优婆塞、优婆夷、天龙八部感结悲哽不能自胜，而便聚积牛头栴檀及诸杂香。又于［卄／积］上，敷舒缯［叠＊毛］，施大宝帐以覆其上，舁举宝棺，绕彼香［卄／积］周回七匝，烧香散花，作众伎乐，而以宝棺置香［卄／积］上，取妙香油周浇洒之。

At that time, the four groups of the assembly and the devas and humans, longing and grieving, could not control themselves. They then used fire to burn from below. The fire would not burn, even after two or three attempts. The people did not know why and asked Ānanda about it, "Why won't the incense pile burn?" Ānanda said, "The reason is that Venerable Mahākāśyapa, having heard that the Tathāgata was about to enter parinirvāṇa in the country of Takṣaśilā, came with five hundred bhikṣus from that country to see the World-Honored One. That is why the Tathāgata did not allow the fire to burn." At that time, the great assembly, having heard these words, deeply sighed in wonder.

时，四部众并诸天人，恋慕懊𢙐不能自胜，即便以火从下烧之。火不肯然乃至再三，亦复不然。时，诸人众不知所以，即以此事问阿［少／兔］楼駄：「三烧香［卄／积］，何故不然？」阿［少／兔］楼駄言：「所以然者，尊者摩诃迦叶在铎叉那耆利国，闻于如来欲般涅槃，与五百比丘从彼国来，欲见世尊。是以如来不令火然。」尔时，大众闻此语已，深叹奇特。

At that time, Mahākāśyapa, in the country of Takṣaśilā, heard from afar that the Tathāgata was in the city of Kuśinagara, about to enter parinirvāṇa. He was greatly saddened and came with five hundred bhikṣus along the road. Not far from the city, he was exhausted and sat under a tree with the bhikṣus on the roadside. He saw a non-Buddhist holding a maṇḍala flower. Kāśyapa asked, "Where are you coming from?" He replied, "I am coming from the city of Kuśinagara." Kāśyapa asked again, "Do you know my teacher, the Perfectly Enlightened One?" He replied, "Yes. Your teacher is in the twin śāla trees in the śāla forest in the city of Kuśinagara. He has already entered parinirvāṇa and it has been seven days. He is now at the precious crown stūpa, about to be cremated. The devas and humans are filled with offerings. That is why I obtained these heavenly flowers." At that time, when Kāśyapa heard these words, he wept and choked with grief. The bhikṣu assembly fainted and fell to the ground. They said to each other in a faint voice, "Alas! How painful! The eye of the world has disappeared." Then Kāśyapa comforted them, "You should not be so distressed. The nature and characteristics of conditioned phenomena are all like this. Even the Tathāgata, the Honored One, could not escape. How much less so others who can be free? You should diligently seek to be free from worldly suffering. Now you should quickly rise and go to the precious crown stūpa to pay homage and gaze upon it." At that time, among the assembly, there were other bhikṣus who had renounced late in life. They were ignorant and unwise. They said to each other, "When the Buddha was in the world, he forbade us and did not allow us to indulge ourselves. Now that he has entered parinirvāṇa, how wonderful!"

尔时，摩诃迦叶在铎叉那耆利国，遥闻如来在鸠尸那城，欲般涅槃，心大悲恋，与五百比丘缘路而来。去城不远，身患疲极在于路边，与诸比丘坐于树下。见一外道，手执曼陀罗华，迦叶问言：「汝从何来？」答言：「我从鸠尸那城来。」迦叶又问：「汝知我师应正遍知不？」其即答言：「识。汝大师在鸠尸那城娑罗林中双树之间，已般涅槃，得今七日。即时正在宝冠支提，将欲阇维，天人充满，互竞供养故，我于彼得此天华。」尔时，迦叶闻此言已，悲号哽咽，诸比丘众闷绝躃地，而以微声共相谓言：「呜呼！苦哉！世间眼灭。」于是迦叶而安慰之：「汝等不应作此苦𢙐，诸行性相皆悉如是。如来天尊犹尚不免，况复余人而得脱耶？宜应精进求离世苦，今可速起前于宝冠支提之所礼拜瞻仰。」尔时，众中有余比丘，晚暮出家愚痴无智，共相谓言：「佛在世时，禁呵我等，不得纵意。既般涅槃，何其快哉！」

At that time, Kāśyapa and the bhikṣus went to the city of Kuśinagara and arrived at the precious crown stūpa. Seeing the Tathāgata's coffin on the incense pile, they wept and circled it seven times. They then climbed the incense pile and reached the precious coffin. At the foot of the coffin, they wailed and wept, bowing their heads in reverence. At that time, the Tathāgata extended his two feet from within the precious coffin. When Kāśyapa saw this, he was doubly saddened and shocked. The devas and humans, seeing this extraordinary and rare event, all sighed in wonder and deeply grieved. At that time, Kāśyapa saw a stain on the Buddha's foot and immediately turned to ask Ānanda, "Why is there this stain on the Tathāgata's foot?" Ānanda replied, "When the Tathāgata was about to enter parinirvāṇa, the four groups were filled. I then thought, 'If I allow the great assembly to advance at the same time, the women will be weak and may not be able to reach the front.' I then first allowed the bhikṣuṇīs and upāsikās to approach the Tathāgata to pay homage and make offerings. At that time, there was a poor upāsikā who was one hundred years old. Seeing the brahmins, kṣatriyas, elders, householders, and the wives and daughters of the strong men, young and old, making offerings with wonderful incense and flowers, she lamented her own poverty and lack of means to express her feelings. With this thought, she was doubly saddened and grieved. As she approached the Buddha's feet, her heart was greatly distressed, and tears flowed, staining the Tathāgata's foot." At that time, when Kāśyapa heard these words, he was deeply saddened and blamed Ānanda for not stopping it, causing this stain. He then offered incense and flowers to the Buddha's coffin, paid homage and praised it. When he was finished, the two feet naturally retracted. Kāśyapa then returned to the ground. By the power of the Buddha, the incense pile naturally caught fire on all four sides. After seven days, the precious coffin melted. At that time, the devas in the sky rained down various wonderful flowers and played music, singing and praising in offering to the relics. The assembled people and the strong men all made various offerings. The strong men then collected the relics in a golden urn and placed them on a jeweled carriage. Burning incense and scattering flowers, they played various music and returned to the city. They built a tall tower and placed the relics in it. 是时，迦叶与诸比丘，进鸠尸那城，到于宝冠支提之所，见如来棺，在香［卄／积］上，悲泣流泪围绕七匝，而登香［卄／积］。至宝棺所，在于足处号咷呜咽，头面作礼。尔时，如来于宝棺内，而出双足，迦叶见此，倍增悲惊。时，诸天人既覩奇特希有之事，莫不嗟叹，深生苦恋。尔时，迦叶见佛足上而有点污，即便回顾，问阿难言：「如来足上何缘有此？」阿难答言：「如来初可般涅槃时，四众充满，我时思惟：『若令大众同时进者，女人羸弱，不必得前。』即便先听诸比丘尼及优婆夷到如来所礼拜供养。尔时，有一贫穷优婆夷，年一百岁。见诸婆罗门，及以刹利长者居士，力士妻女，长幼大小，以妙香华种种供养，自伤贫乏无以表心，作此念已，倍增悲恸，临佛足上，心大懊𢙐，涕泣流连，污如来足。」尔时，迦叶既闻此语，心怀惆怅，怪责阿难曾不呵止致此点污，即以香华，供养佛棺，礼拜赞叹。皆悉毕已，于是双足自然还入，迦叶即便还下于地。以佛力故香［卄／积］自然，四面火起，经历七日，宝棺融尽。于时诸天，雨火令灭，诸力士众收取舍利。以千张［叠＊毛］缠佛身者，最里一张及外一重，如本不然，犹裹舍利。当尔之时，虚空诸天雨众妙华，并作伎乐，歌呗赞叹，供养舍利。时，诸来众及以力士，皆悉各设种种供养。诸力士众，即以金甖收取舍利，置宝舆上，烧香散华，作众伎乐，还归入城。起大高楼而以舍利置于楼上，即严四兵，防卫守护。唯听比丘及比丘尼，得入礼拜，种种供养。其余国王及婆罗门，长者居士，一切人民，皆不听前。

At that time, King Ajātaśatru, the son of Vaidehī, heard that the strong men had collected the Buddha's relics and placed them on a high tower, arranging the four divisions of troops to guard and protect them. He was greatly saddened and angry at the strong men. He then sent a message to the strong men, saying, "The World-Honored One was also my teacher while he was in the world. When he entered parinirvāṇa, I regret not being able to see him. My clan and the World-Honored One are both kṣatriyas. Why do you alone collect the relics, place them on a high tower, and arrange the four divisions of troops to guard and protect them, not sharing them with others? You should give me a portion. I wish to build a wonderful stūpa in my country and make various offerings. If you can agree, we will have eternal good relations; if you do not agree, I will raise troops to attack you." The seven other kings and the people of Vaiśālī and the Licchavis also sent messages in the same way. The brahmins, elders, and householders also each sent messages to the strong men, saying, "The World-Honored One was also our teacher. Please have compassion and give us a portion of the relics.

尔时，韦提希子阿阇世王，闻彼力士收佛舍利，置高楼上，而严四兵防卫守护，心大悲恼，又复忿怒诸力士辈，即便遣信，语力士言：「世尊在世，亦是我师。般涅槃时，恨不临见，我之族姓，及与世尊，皆是刹利，汝今云何，独收舍利，置高楼上，而严四兵防卫守护，不分余人？汝便可以一分与我，我欲于国起妙兜婆，兴诸供养。若能见许，永通国好；不见许者，兴兵伐汝。」余七国王及毗耶离、诸离车等，遣使之法，皆亦如是。时，婆罗门、长者、居士亦各遣信，白力士言：「世尊亦是我等之师，愿能哀愍，赐舍利分。」

At that time, the messengers of the various countries arrived at the place of the strong men and fully conveyed the king's intentions. When the strong men heard this, they deeply felt unfair and replied to the messengers, "The Buddha came to our country and entered parinirvāṇa. The relics naturally belong to us. We wish to build a stūpa in our country to make offerings and adorn it. This is not possible. If the stūpa is completed, you are welcome to come and make offerings and pay homage. If you wish to raise troops and attack, the military forces of this country are also sufficient to match you." At that time, the messengers each returned to their countries and told the kings about this matter. When the kings heard this, they became even more angry and each raised the four divisions of troops to attack. At that time, the strong men also prepared their weapons to meet the enemy.

时，诸国使到力士所，具宣王意，力士闻已，深怀不平，答诸使言：「佛来我国而般涅槃，舍利自然应属我等。欲于国界兴造兜婆庄严供养，此不可得。若兜婆成，恣汝等意，快共来此供养礼拜，若欲兴兵，而见向者，此国军众，亦足相拟。」时，彼诸使各归本国，人人向王说如此事。诸王闻已，益怀瞋忿，各严四兵而往攻伐。时，诸力士亦严战具，以拟来敌。

In the city of Kuśinagara, there was a brahmin named Tuluṇa, who was intelligent and wise, and deeply believed in the Three Jewels. He thought to himself, "The kings of the eight countries and the Licchavis are strong and their troops are sharp. Moreover, the strong men are brave and difficult to handle. If they fight, there will certainly be no two victors." He then said to the strong men, "Although you are brave and bold, the kings of the eight countries are united in their strength and their troops are gathered like clouds. If they fight, there will certainly be no two victors. Once weapons are drawn, there will certainly be injuries. While the Tathāgata was in the world, he taught people to practice kindness. But today, you suddenly want to kill each other. You should not be stingy with the relics. You should divide them among the various countries and the Licchavis, and each build stūpas in their territories. In accordance with the teachings of the World-Honored One in the past, and also so that you may all obtain blessings and benefits." When the strong men heard these words, their minds were enlightened, and they replied, "What you have said is indeed reasonable. We will listen to your words.

鸠尸那城中，有一婆罗门，名徒卢那，聪明多智，深信三宝。心自思惟：「彼八国王及诸离车，身力壮健，军众精锐。又且力士勇猛难当，若交战者必无两全。」而即便语诸力士言：「汝等虽复勇锐果敢，彼八国王，齐力同心，人众云集，军阵猛盛，若战鬪者，理无两全，锋刃既交，必有伤害。如来在世，教人行慈，而于今日，忽相杀戮，汝等不应悋惜舍利，宜分诸国及离车等，各于其界，造立兜婆。称于世尊往昔之训，又使汝等普获福利。」诸力士众，闻此语已，心意解悟，即答之言：「汝之所说，实得于理，听如汝言。」

The brahmin saw that the strong men had all put down their armor, and then left the city to tell the kings, "Why do you raise troops for the sake of the Dharma?" The kings replied, "We seek the relics for the sake of the Dharma, but we are refused and not given a share. Therefore, we raise troops and come together." The brahmin then said to the kings, "I have already mediated for the strong men. I will give you a share of the relics. You may take the jeweled bottles and I will divide them for you." The eight kings were delighted and handed over the golden bottles. The brahmin received the golden bottles and returned to the high tower to divide the relics and give them to the eight kings.

彼婆罗门，见力士众皆悉解甲，即便出城，语诸王言：「汝今为法何故兴兵？」诸王答言：「我为法故，远求舍利，而见拒逆，不肯分与，是以今者兴兵共来。」时，婆罗门复语王言：「我已相为和诸力士，皆悉与汝舍利之分，可取宝瓶，为汝分之。」八王欢喜，奉授金瓶。彼婆罗门，受诸金瓶，持以还归，于高楼上而分舍利，以与八王。

At that time, the eight kings, having obtained the relics, were overjoyed and returned to their own countries to build stūpas. The brahmin begged for a share of the relics from the strong men and built a stūpa himself. The strong men took a share and built a stūpa at the place of cremation, combining the remaining ashes and charcoal. In this way, they built ten stūpas in total.

于时，八王既得舍利，踊跃顶戴，还于本国各起兜婆。彼婆罗门从诸力士，乞分舍利瓶，自起兜婆。诸力士等取其一分，于阇维处，合余灰炭而起兜婆；如是凡起十处兜婆。
